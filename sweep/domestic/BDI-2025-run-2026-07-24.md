## The finding that matters — FY2025/26 breaks the OCR wall on the *execution* side

FY2024/25's entire budget chain was image-only; only the exposé and ministry PPMs were native
text. **FY2025/26 adds a genuinely new, machine-readable seam: the per-institution T3 canevas
execution reports (`CANEVAS-RAPPORT_<INST>_2025-2026_T3.xlsx`), published 2026-07-04 as native
XLSX.** Each is a full programme→action→PAP-activity spreadsheet (64 columns) carrying
**BUDGET ANNUEL REVISE**, quarterly revised budgets, and **ENG BUDGETAIRE / ENG JURIDIQUE /
LIQUIDATION / ORDONNANCEMENT / PAIEMENT / DECAISSEMENT at T3** with taux de réalisation, plus
a LITERA-equivalent `CODE NOMENCLATURE`. This is the **first machine-readable BDI budget/
execution artefact in the corpus** — a cross-vote *revised + executed* scan is now possible
**without OCR**, for the ~24 institutions that filed (whole set enumerable; digital-relevant
subset staged). It also substitutes for the missing standalone PTBA modifié (the revised
per-programme position lives in the `BUDGET ANNUEL REVISE` column).

The scanned volumes shrank sharply (loi 14.9 MB vs 462 MB, PTBA 53 MB vs 265 MB, modifiée
40 MB vs 246 MB) but are **still image-only** (200–450 bytes of text over 200–440 pp) — lower
DPI, no text layer. OCR remains the precondition for the appropriation act and the PTBA.
