## Track B — the ministry library works, behind the same DNS wall as Kenya/Senegal/Angola

`finances.gov.bi` **does not resolve on local DNS**. Workaround that worked (third
country running): DoH (`cloudflare-dns.com/dns-query`) → **41.79.224.90**, then
`curl --resolve finances.gov.bi:443:41.79.224.90`. Behind it is a clean WordPress
document library — `/index.php/category/lois/`, `/index.php/budget/`,
`/index.php/ptba/` — where each post carries one `wp-content/uploads/…pdf` link. One
fetch of the category page yields the whole per-year chain.

deactivated"); the live host is **`armp.gov.bi`**, which serves normally. Its
*Plan de Passation des Marchés 2024-2025* section paginates over 8 pages of
`/archives/<id>` posts, one per spending unit.

`courdescomptes.bi` and `armp.gov.bi` resolve and serve normally. `arct.bi` does not
resolve; the regulator is at **`arct.gov.bi`**.

## The finding that matters for Burundi

**Burundi's digital transformation is donor-financed and its fisc buys the furniture.**
The World Bank's **PAFEN** (P176396, IDA US$50m + US$42m additional financing = US$92m)
carries the IFMIS (SIGFP_BI, ~US$30m), the tax system (e-KORI), the e-government
strategy, the identity diagnostic and the SETIC site. Against that, the FY2024/25
fisc-funded digital lines visible in the procurement plans are computers, network
maintenance and a police biometrics package — hundreds of millions of BIF, not
billions. The CNI biométrique reaches the fisc only in the **FY2026/27** bill
(16 mds BIF).

Any BDI domestic-state total must state this, or it reads as a country doing almost
no digital spending when it is in fact doing it on someone else's balance sheet.
