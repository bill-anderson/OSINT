Angola’s Data Protection Agency has levied over 600 million kwanzas in fines against Crescer Tech and Fast Digital Center for serious breaches of personal data laws, marking a shift towards more aggressive compliance enforcement amid global regulatory tightening.

Angola’s Data Protection Agency has imposed fines totalling more than 600 million kwanzas on two technology companies over what it described as serious breaches of personal data rules, according to deliberations seen by Valor Económico. The regulator said Crescer Tech - Comércio e Prestação de Serviços, LDA and Fast Digital Center - Comércio e Serviços, LDA failed to comply with key legal duties covering notification, authorisation and the handling of customer information.

Crescer Tech received the larger penalty, equivalent to about $505,000 or roughly 549 million kwanzas. The APD said the company did not properly notify the authority or secure prior authorisation before processing clients’ data, and also transferred information abroad without permission by storing it on cloud servers outside Angola.

Fast Digital Center was fined $112,000, or about 110 million kwanzas. In its case, the regulator pointed to weak technical and organisational safeguards for personal data, alongside the absence of prior notification and approval for processing customer information.

The action is part of a broader tightening of enforcement by privacy regulators internationally. In the US, California’s privacy watchdog has recently penalised a data broker for unlawfully selling sensitive consumer information, while the Federal Communications Commission has also levied major fines on telecoms over the sharing of location data without consent. In Angola, the APD had already sanctioned TAAG and the Banco de Desenvolvimento de Angola last September in earlier data-protection cases, signalling a more assertive approach to compliance.
