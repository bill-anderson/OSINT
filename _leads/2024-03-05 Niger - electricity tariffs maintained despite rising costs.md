[Faithful English rendering. Agence Ecofin. Published 5 March 2024.]

(Agence Ecofin) - Since the coup of 26 July 2023, Niger, which faces major energy-supply challenges, has multiplied initiatives to limit the impact on the population.

The government announced at the Council of Ministers of Saturday 2 March [2024] that tariffs for supplying electricity to consumers will remain unchanged for one year from 1 January 2024. A decree "extending the validity period of the Methodology and structure of tariffs applicable to final users of the public electricity service supplied by the Societe Nigerienne d'Electricite (NIGELEC)" enacts this decision.

The measure aims to stabilise the electricity price for the population amid a surge in diesel prices - a fuel essential to electricity production in Niger, which the country recently decided to export to Burkina Faso, Mali and Chad.

The decision rests on the decree approving the tariff methodology and structure for NIGELEC final users, initially adopted in 2017 for five years and extended once in 2022 for one year. Under this text, the social electricity tariff for low-voltage consumption (0-50 kWh) is set at 59.45 CFA francs (USD 0.098) per kilowatt-hour, plus a fixed monthly charge of 250 CFA francs (USD 0.41).
