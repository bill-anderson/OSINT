## Description

On April 24, 2020, Bank of China and the Government of Côte d'Ivoire signed a CFA 73,931,502,506.49 loan agreement for Phase 2 of the Ministry of Interior and Security Unified Communication System Project. The borrowing terms of the loan are unknown. However, it is known that the borrower was expected to use the proceeds of the loan to finance an EUR 124 million commercial contract between Côte d'Ivoire’s Ministry of Interior and Security and China Railway Construction International Group, which was signed in December 2019.   According to the Government of Côte d'Ivoire’s Aid Management Platform (AMP), the Bank of China loan had achieved a 75% disbursement rate (CFA 55,335,025,216 out of CFA 73,931,502,506.49) as of December 31, 2022. Bank of China made four loan disbursements worth CFA 55,335,025,216 in 2021 and 2022: a CFA 15,297,225,415 disbursement on December 31, 2021, a CFA 23,445,307,879 disbursement as of January 31, 2022, a CFA 9,095,717,527 disbursement as of October 31, 2022, and a CFA 7,496,774,395 disbursement as of December 31, 2022.  Phase 2 involves the construction of a national emergency command center (with 2 floors and a total of 1,000 square meters) for the police, military, and fire department as well as the installation of video surveillance towers in 46 locations within 22 cities. Phase 2 also involves the construction of integrated military IT network, the unification and modernization of communication and command systems within the Ministry of Interior and Security.  China Railway Construction International Group is the contractor responsible for implementation. Phase 2 work commenced in late 2021/early 2022. Phase 2 was originally expected to reach completion on December 31, 2022.
