Opinion column by ICT practitioner Timo Neisho on institutional misalignment in Namibia's public-sector IT.

Namibia's frameworks - Vision 2030, the newly launched Sixth National Development Plan (NDP6) and the Digital Government Roadmap 2024-2026 - place digital transformation at the centre of governance and service delivery. But IT professionals in most ministries and parastatals still report to finance/administration departments with little strategic influence, and Namibia lacks formal CIO roles in most ministries and government-controlled entities.

Datable NDP6 targets cited: increase internet access from 53% to 90% by 2030; grow the ICT sector's contribution to GDP from 1.6% to 4%. NDP6 also includes plans for a national data centre, a modernised civil registration system, a digital curriculum for schools, and a cybercrime strategy.

Author argues these initiatives (e-health, digital civil registration, online tax, payment gateways, cybersecurity) need IT leadership integrated into decision-making; calls for official CIO roles with reporting lines to top leadership.
