Zimbabwe's Cyber and Data Protection Act clearly sets out how organisations must collect, use, and protect personal information. Alongside the Cyber and Data Protection (Licensing of Data Controllers and Appointment of Data Protection Officers) Regulations, it forms a comprehensive legal framework that applies to anyone who processes personal data, whether as an individual, private or public body.

## What you need to know about the Act

The Cyber and Data Protection Act, 2021, is Zimbabwe's main data protection law. It applies to all public and private organisations, regardless of size. In September 2024, the government introduced the Cyber and Data Protection (Licensing of Data Controllers and Appointment of Data Protection Officers) Regulations, which strengthen enforcement by requiring registration of data controllers and processors and the appointment of a data protection officer. The Act is enforced by the Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ).

## Who must comply?

The Act applies to anyone who processes or stores personal data, whether by automated means or partly by automated systems. This includes organisations established in Zimbabwe, those operating from outside Zimbabwe but using equipment/systems/services located in Zimbabwe, and those collecting or storing personal data electronically even if not permanently located in Zimbabwe. Foreign controllers not established in Zimbabwe must appoint a local representative.

## Key features

- Data subject rights: controllers must inform data subjects when they collect personal data, explaining who they are, why they are collecting the data, and how they will use it; direct-marketing collection requires informing individuals of the right to object.
- Security safeguards: organisations must protect data with physical, technical and policy measures matching the risks, including risk assessments, security policies and regular testing.
- Breach notification: report data breaches to POTRAZ within 24 hours; if the breach poses a high risk to individuals, notify affected people within 72 hours.
- Cross-border transfers: notify POTRAZ before transferring personal data outside Zimbabwe; transfers permitted only where the destination offers adequate protection or specific conditions (consent, legal necessity) are met. The Act does not regulate the sale of personal data.
- Registration and licensing: most data controllers must register with POTRAZ before processing; many must also apply for a licence, especially if they decide what data to collect or process data for commercial purposes. Fees for registration range depending on the number of people whose data is processed.
- Appointment of a DPO: all licensed data controllers must appoint a data protection officer. Registered-but-not-licensed controllers do not need one. The deadline to appoint a DPO was 12 December 2024.
- Records of processing activities (ROPAs): the Act does not require controllers to keep internal ROPAs; instead POTRAZ maintains a public register of automated data processing.
- Data privacy impact assessments (DPIAs): required when processing children's data, to be carried out regularly.

## Data processing principles

Lawfulness, fairness and transparency; purpose limitation; data minimisation; valid explanation for family/private-affairs data; accuracy; storage limitation; integrity and confidentiality; and accountability. The Act also requires data protection by design and by default when processing a minor's data.

## Special category personal data and children

Sensitive, genetic, biometric or health data may only be processed with the data subject's written consent, subject to exceptions (employment/national-security law, vital interests, public health/legal rights, research/medical treatment/public interest, or where the subject has made the information public). Health data may only be processed under the supervision of a health professional unless the subject consents or there is urgent risk of harm; the controller must issue a unique health ID when processing health data. A child is anyone under 18; a child's data may only be processed with parental/guardian consent.

## Timelines and next steps for compliance

The Regulations came into force on 12 September 2024. Organisations had to register with POTRAZ and, if required, apply for a licence before starting any processing. Licensed data controllers had until 12 December 2024 to appoint a data protection officer.
