# Safaricom Strengthens Market Leadership in Kenya's Broadband Sector

Kenya's telecommunications market continued to evolve during the third quarter of the 2025/2026 financial year, with the latest industry figures highlighting two defining trends: Safaricom further strengthened its leadership across mobile communications and digital financial services, while Starlink Internet Services Kenya continued to gain traction in the country's fixed broadband market.

The latest Sector Statistics Report from the Communications Authority of Kenya, covering January to March 2026, confirms Safaricom's overwhelming lead. The operator captured 68.9% of all active mobile subscriptions, equivalent to 57.9 million of Kenya's 84.1 million SIM cards. It also accounted for 62.7% of mobile broadband subscriptions.

The company also retained its leadership in fixed broadband, serving 941,501 subscribers and securing a 35.4% market share—well ahead of the second-largest provider, Jamii Telecommunications Ltd, at 19.5%.

Starlink ranked among the country's top 10 fixed internet service providers for the first time, ending the quarter with 24,999 subscriptions and a 0.9% market share. Satellite broadband recorded the fastest growth of any fixed access technology during the quarter, increasing by 11.4%.

Kenya's international internet capacity expanded by 16.4% to 28,130 Gbps. Kenya's fixed internet market subscriptions rose 7.9% to 2.66 million; fiber remained the dominant fixed access technology with 1.47 million subscriptions. Total mobile broadband consumption climbed 6% to 800 million gigabytes, while average monthly usage per subscriber increased to 15.1 GB; users connected to 5G networks consumed an average of 53.5 GB per month.
