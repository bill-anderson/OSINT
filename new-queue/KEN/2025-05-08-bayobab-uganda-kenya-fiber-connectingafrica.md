# Bayobab connects Uganda, Kenya with new fiber route

Bayobab, a digital connectivity solutions company, has completed a fiber optic network linking Mombasa in Kenya to Kampala in Uganda.
...
Bayobab, a digital connectivity solutions company has completed a fiber optic network linking Mombasa, Kenya, to Kampala, Uganda, marking a significant advancement in East Africa's digital connectivity.
...
Bayobab said the 260 km fiber route runs along the Uganda Railway corridor from Kampala to Tororo, connecting directly to Kenya's National Long Distance (NLD) fiber network that extends from Mombasa to Malaba via the Kenya Railways meter gauge route.
...
"Built between December 2024 and February 2025, this fiber cable delivers reliable, high-capacity connectivity from Uganda's capital, connecting urban and rural communities along the route," Bayobab said.
...
The company said the route provides Uganda with a low-latency, high-capacity connection exceeding 1 Tbit/s into Kenya's broader digital infrastructure, including subsea cable landing stations in Mombasa.
...
Bayobab Kenya MD Sylvia Anampiu said that by interconnecting with Uganda via this high-capacity route, Bayobab is enhancing regional digital resilience, creating alternative routes for traffic, and opening new opportunities for businesses and communities along the corridor.
...
Bayobab Uganda MD Juliet Nsubuga said the route connects major data centers across Uganda and Kenya and supports hyperscalers and enterprises looking to expand within the East African region.
...
"It caters to the needs of international and national technology players, telecoms, and Internet service providers (ISPs) serving communities, demonstrating our commitment to connecting Africa," she explained.
...
In April 2024, Bayobab announced that it had completed the structural separation and transfer of fiber assets from its sister company, MTN Zambia.
