# President Herminie officially launches Electronic Case Management System | 23 June 2026
State House Seychelles — Legal Affairs

President of the Republic, Dr Patrick Herminie, officially launched the Electronic Case Management System (ECMS) at a ceremony held at the Link Building, Île du Port. The launch marks a landmark advancement in the digitalisation of Seychelles' justice sector. Among those present were Chief Justice Rony Govinden, Attorney General Mr Vinsent Perera, and former Attorney General Mr Frank Ally.

The ECMS is an initiative of the Office of the Attorney General, developed with financial support from the United States Department of State and implemented through the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC) Global Maritime Crime Programme (GMCP), in partnership with the United Nations Office for Project Services (UNOPS). The system modernises and streamlines case management across the legal affairs sector, enabling registered users to access colleagues' calendars, generate links for virtual meetings, retrieve case data, and communicate through an integrated chat feature within a secure digital environment.

Remarks were delivered by AG Vinsent Perera; Mr Adham Loutfi, Chargé d'Affaires of the US Embassy in Seychelles; Mr Christophe Niyonkuru, UNOPS Head of Office for Madagascar, Comoros, Mauritius and Seychelles; and Mr Ali Elberier, UNODC Regional Representative for Eastern Africa. A technical presentation with a live demonstration of the file registration process was delivered by the AG's Office. The ECMS is now live as a fully operational online platform with immediate effect.
