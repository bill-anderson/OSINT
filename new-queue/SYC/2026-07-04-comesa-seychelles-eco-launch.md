# Seychelles National Launch of the e-CO — official statement by Guest of Honour PS for Trade, Ms Natalie Edmond | 4 July 2026
COMESA (author: Willis Osemo)

The Government of Seychelles remains focused on the digital expansion of services within the public sector, offering more speed and interoperability in service delivery through easily accessible ecosystems. The shift from paper-based services to digital solutions is a necessity for Seychelles not to be excluded from the digital evolution.

The Seychelles Revenue Commission (SRC), through its digital transformation plan, continues to evolve its digital pathway for maintaining Seychelles' supply chain, trade facilitation and border security against illicit trade. Today the country officially launches the implementation of the electronic certificate of origin (e-CO), an essential platform for Seychelles to foster connectivity and modernize trade facilitation, contributing towards the digital economic development agenda.

PS Edmond thanked the COMESA Secretariat for building and maintaining the engagement with the SRC in delivering the project, and the SRC project team, IT team and the Classification and Valuation of Origin Unit. Traders were urged to use the platform to benefit from reduced duties under applicable trade agreements. (Statement: LAUNCHING STATEMENT – CERTIFICATE OF ORIGIN.)
