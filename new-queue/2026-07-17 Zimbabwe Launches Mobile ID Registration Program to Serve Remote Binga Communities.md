Zimbabwe's government has launched a mobile identity document registration initiative in the Binga district, aimed at increasing birth registration and national identification coverage in remote communities. The program, running from July 7-11, 2025, serves areas like Siamusanga village, located approximately 160 kilometers from Binga Centre near the Zambian border.

The initiative addresses significant barriers faced by rural residents in obtaining birth certificates and national IDs, including long travel distances, poor road infrastructure with journey times up to seven hours, risks from wildlife, and limited connectivity to government services.

According to the Zimbabwe Demographic and Health Survey (ZDHS 2023-24), birth registration coverage in Zimbabwe currently stands at 57 percent. The mobile registration program represents part of broader efforts to increase this percentage by reaching underserved rural populations.

The Civil Registry Department is conducting the exercise in partnership with UNICEF and the Embassy of Sweden.

"Obtaining a birth certificate is an easy exercise for many people in urban areas, but not so for those in remote and hard-to-reach communities due to connectivity challenges," said Bongani Ndlovu, reporting from Binga.

The program has already impacted local communities, as exemplified by Emma Mwinde, a resident from Gokwe who traveled to Binga specifically for the mobile registration exercise. Her husband works in a fishing village approximately 20 kilometers from the registration site, illustrating the dispersed nature of the population being served.

The initiative is part of Zimbabwe's broader integrated efforts to improve legal identity access under the Second Republic's policy reforms, which focus on decentralization, digital innovation, and community empowerment.
