# TechCabal "Francophone Weekly" newsletter; core story = Semoa's BCEAO Level 3 licence (granted 29 Jan 2026).
---

(TechCabal, Francophone Weekly, 17 February 2026, by Lina Kacyem) — On 29 January 2026, Semoa Group, a Togolese fintech start-up founded a decade ago that develops digital payment and transaction-digitisation solutions, obtained Level 3 accreditation from the Central Bank of West African States (BCEAO). It is the most complete licence the BCEAO grants to a non-bank institution.

In the West African monetary union, there are around 248 million registered e-money accounts, over 11 billion electronic payment transactions were processed in 2024 alone, and the financial inclusion rate has risen from under 15% twenty years ago to about 74%. The payment infrastructure is being rebuilt not by innovation but by regulation.

Since January 2024, the BCEAO has applied Instruction No. 001-01-2024, a framework requiring all fintechs offering payment services in the UEMOA to obtain a licence or cease operations. The deadline was pushed back three times — from July 2024 to January 2025, then May, and finally 31 August 2025 — as most players were not ready. By September 2025, only 20 payment institutions had been licensed across the eight member states, in a region of ~140 million adults.

Semoa Group was founded in 2016 by Togolese engineer Edem Adjamagbo (Polytech Nantes), who returned to Lomé. Without the licence, a fintech can grow; with it, it can become systemic.
