# Grounding the cloud in Namibia (analysis)

Analysis by Peter Karon (infrastructure manager, Green Enterprise Solutions) arguing that Namibia's solar scale and Equiano landing position it as an "African digital sovereign" and ideal AI-data-centre host — a terrestrial answer to "orbital compute."

Key claims: Data centres are "energy-to-information converters," and AI workloads consume ~10x more energy than standard searches. Namibia has 3,000+ hours of sunshine annually and some of Earth's highest solar irradiation — a solar panel there generates nearly double the output of the same panel in Central Europe. It cites the 3GW solar-to-hydrogen facility near Walvis Bay as making renewable power a "2026 reality." Against space-based data centres: ground cooling (coastal air/liquid-to-air in Erongo) is far cheaper than radiative cooling in vacuum; maintenance is trivial vs multi-million-dollar orbital repair; and launching 1GW of space capacity could cost upwards of US$50 billion for cooling hardware alone — enough to build several world-class terrestrial hubs in Namibia and still connect the whole SADC region. Frames Africa as the emerging "digital sovereign" on latency and sovereignty grounds.
