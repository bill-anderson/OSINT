# Namibia Reports 37% Surge in Cyberattacks in 2025

Namibia recorded 1.7 million cyberattacks between January and December 2025, up ~37% year-on-year, per the "Namibia Cybersecurity Landscape Report 2025" released by the Namibia Cyber Security Incident Response Team (NAM-CSIRT, housed at CRAN) on 11 April 2026. Authorities identified 2,231,970 vulnerabilities during the year, enabling DDoS attacks, malware propagation and unauthorised remote access. Government allocated ~US$1 million to cybersecurity for FY2026/27.

The report notes a shift toward large-scale exploitation of trust, with attackers increasingly using social engineering and AI-assisted deception. In Q1, attackers deployed AI-generated deepfake campaigns promoting fraudulent investment schemes; by mid-year, phishing via Telegram remained widespread. It identifies the emergence of municipal-level ransomware using double-extortion, smishing campaigns mimicking delivery services, and deepfake videos of senior officials — a structured fraud ecosystem fuelled by AI-driven disinformation.

NAM-CSIRT stresses that the rollout of digital identity systems, expansion of digital services and infrastructure growth increase the urgency of integrating cybersecurity into national development, calling for a whole-of-society approach. (Context: Interpol "Cybercrime Africa Cyberthreat Assessment 2025.")
