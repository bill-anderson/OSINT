# Cran rejects Starlink licence

CRAN has officially declined Starlink's application to operate in Namibia, rejecting both its Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence (ECS and ECNS) and its Spectrum licence for satellite internet services. The decision was published in the Government Gazette on 23 March 2026.

"The authority resolved to decline the award of a Spectrum licence to Starlink Internet Services Namibia (Pty) Limited for the provision of satellite services…" The notice states the company had 0% of stock owned by Namibian citizens and requested frequencies: Downlink 10.7–12.7 GHz (2000 MHz bandwidth) and Uplink 14.0–14.5 GHz (500 MHz) for national coverage via fixed-satellite services. Under section 31 of the Communications Act No. 8 of 2009, CRAN may reconsider within 90 days.

The Gazette also notes CRAN recently approved the transfer of a licence and spectrum licence for Q-Kon Telecom Namibia (Pty) Ltd. Former parliamentarian Maximalliant Katjimune backed the decision: "Starlink is owned by Elon Musk, a man who has very problematic views on the current geopolitics... It would not be wise allowing such a man control over our personal data as a nation." CRAN spokesperson Mufaro Nesongano said a detailed statement would follow.
