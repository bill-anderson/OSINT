# Namibia expedites electronic IDs issuance

Namibia has made headway in launching electronic identity documents (EIDs). The launch is part of the 2026/27 budget (presented last month). An EID is a secure digital solution that uses an app or smart card to verify an individual online or in person.

Home Affairs Minister Lucia Iipumbu issued an update following the most recent cabinet meeting chaired by President Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah: "We are also progressing very well with our electronic identity documents, known as EID... We are expected to launch this in the course of the 2026/27 financial year. This will mark a significant step towards modernising identity management systems because this can be used within the government and at the private sector level."

Iipumbu revealed that in 2025 the government issued 117,175 national IDs and 126,737 birth certificates. During the period, 682 identity cards were issued to refugees the country classifies as "African guests." The IDs enable beneficiaries to access education, banking, employment and other essential services.
