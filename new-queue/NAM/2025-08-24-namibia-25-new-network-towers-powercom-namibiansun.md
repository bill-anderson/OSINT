# Namibia bolsters digital access with 25 new network towers

Twenty-five PowerCom network towers (project cost N$40 million) were inaugurated across nine regions, with a symbolic launch at Oikokola (Etayi constituency, Omusati). ICT Minister Emma Theofelus said infrastructure is key to driving digital transformation and access in underserved communities, in line with Vision 2030 and NDP6.

Targets restated: raise internet penetration from just over 50% to 90% of the population by 2030; grow digital technology access from 28% to 70%; and achieve 100% digitisation of services by 2029/30, "supported by secure data systems and a national digital ID." Theofelus called connectivity "the currency of modern development." Towers span Omusati, Erongo, Khomas, Otjozondjupa, Kavango East/West, //Karas, Oshana, Oshikoto and Zambezi. She commended PowerCom and Telecom Namibia for their investment and urged communities to protect the towers from vandalism and theft.
