# Namibia to launch groundbreaking electronic ID by 2026

The home affairs ministry (MHAISS) has announced the roll-out of Namibia's first electronic identity card (e-ID), scheduled for launch by July 2026. The move is expected to overhaul identity management and streamline access to both public and private services nationwide.

Developed in partnership with the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) and aligned with its model governance framework for digital legal identity, the e-ID is designed to modernise and redefine Namibia's civil registration system. The e-ID is a smart card embedded with a secure microchip (similar to a bank card) containing both biometric and demographic data; the contactless chip ensures quick, secure identity verification across healthcare, banking, education, elections and government services. The card is described as nearly impossible to forge, curbing identity fraud, eliminating duplicate records and strengthening national security.

The ministry launched a nationwide awareness campaign, starting in Khomas, centred on an Un-Conference on Digital Legal Identity set for 26 June in Windhoek (500+ community members expected). Executive director Etienne Maritz: "Every innovation brings questions and sometimes fears. That's why we're meeting people where they are, communicating openly and early to ensure no one is left behind."

Rural communities stand to benefit most: mobile verification units will bring services (birth registration, voter enrolment, social grants) to remote areas. The e-ID forms the backbone of Namibia's broader digital ambitions, laying groundwork for e-government services and enhancing cross-border mobility within SADC.
