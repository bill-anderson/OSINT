# Namibia's Digital Economy Needs Cybersecurity as Much as It Needs Connectivity (Opinion)

*Analysis by Theonista Baily. Documents a run of major Namibian breaches.*

On 6 March [2026] a breach hit the Namibia Airports Company; the full picture surfaced 19 March when the INC Ransomware Group announced it and Nam-CSIRT confirmed it. CRAN spokesperson Mufaro Nesongano said attackers claimed to have exfiltrated ~500GB (financial records, HR, customer data) — making the airports company the second Namibian INC victim after the Otjiwarongo municipality. Data (airport permit system files, engineering documents) was later dumped on the dark web.

Prior breaches: In December 2024, Telecom Namibia lost 626GB to the Hunters International ransomware group, exposing 490,000+ files affecting eight ministries, five regional councils and 10 municipalities. In October 2025, the Namibia Students Finance Assistance Fund published personal details of 7,000+ students on its own website. Paratus was hit in early 2025.

Meanwhile NDP6 targets internet use up from 53% to 90% by 2030 with fully digital government; the Bank of Namibia is preparing its Instant Payment System (starting with social grants to pensioners). The ITU 2024 Global Cybersecurity Index placed Namibia in Tier 4 ("evolving"), score 37.93/100; NDP6 admits cyber readiness of 37 vs a 2030 target of 65. Bank of Namibia's 2025 annual report put banking fraud losses at N$73.9 million for the year, up from ~N$8.7 million in 2020.
