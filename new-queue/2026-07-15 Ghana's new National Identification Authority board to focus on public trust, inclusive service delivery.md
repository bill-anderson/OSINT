The National Identification Authority of Ghana (NIA) has new appointees on its governing board. Minister of the Interior Muntaka Mohammed-Mubarak commissioned the new board, urging members to "deepen oversight, strengthen public trust, and accelerate the delivery of secure and inclusive identity services."

The minister praised recent NIA achievements under acting Executive Secretary Wisdom Kwaku Deku — decentralizing ID registration, expanding premium services, and introducing the online-only application system for premium services. New Board Chairman Moses Afetsi Positive pledged strategic leadership to enhance inclusive identification services, emphasizing the Ghana Card as a critical tool for citizens and aliens to access government and private-sector services.

The board is tasked with delivering legal and digital ID for all Ghanaians by 2030 (UN SDG 16.9). Under the National Identification Act 2009, the board handles policy direction, financial oversight, strategic leadership, appointment of executive management, approval of regulations, data governance/security, and stakeholder engagement.
