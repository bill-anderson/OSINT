# Congo sets 2-month deadline for SIM registration

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa. October 23, 2025.

The Republic of Congo's Postal and Electronic Communications Regulatory Agency (ARPCE) has given the country's telcos, MTN and Airtel, an additional two months to finalize SIM card identification, according to current regulations.

The decision follows a study by the regulator, which released the findings on October 21, 2025.

Conclusions from the survey, which was conducted from July 25 to August 29, 2025, in 18 localities of the country, were presented in a LinkedIn post.

Benjamin Mouandza, Congo's director of electronic communications networks and services (DRSCE), said only localities like Kinkala and Djambala complied 100% with the identification requirements.

"In other cities such as Brazzaville, Pointe-Noire, Dolisie, Ouesso, Pokola, Ngo, Tchamba-Nzassi, Madigou Kayes, Loudima, Bouansa, Loutété and Nkayi, all SIM cards are sold without presentation of identity documents and are often pre-activated," explained Mouandza.

The regulator said operators failed to request proper documentation during SIM card purchases, effectively enabling the sale of unregistered cards.

Operators, however, blamed the distribution network, noting that while they maintain formal agreements with distributors, those distributors rely on resellers who are allegedly responsible for selling pre-activated or unidentified SIM cards.

MTN Congo and Airtel Congo committed to sanctioning any resellers that fail to comply with subscriber identification regulations.

## African SIM registration movements

Many African countries are mandating SIM card registration to combat SIM fraud and requiring users to link their SIM cards to national ID cards.

From July 2025, Ghana upgraded its SIM card registration process to be fully online.

The Republic of the Congo is one of the countries that have implemented mandatory SIM card registration processes, including Lesotho, Nigeria, Namibia and Mauritania.
