Bo City, Saturday 29 November 2025 — President Dr Julius Maada Bio officially opened the new Orange Sierra Leone Data Centre in Bo, the company's disaster recovery data centre and the second such installation in the country following the first in Freetown. Orange Sierra Leone's Chief Technical Officer Sylvanus Redwood-Sawyer described it as the company's disaster recovery centre.

President Bio said the expansion into Bo aligns with his government's vision to advance digital connectivity nationwide. He emphasised responsible use of telecommunications and warned providers to protect citizens' information: "As we expand our digital space, service providers must guarantee robust cybersecurity for end users, eliminate cyberbullying, and confront cybercriminals."

Minister of Communication, Technology and Innovation Salima Monorma Bah recalled that since 2018 President Bio has placed technology at the centre of development priorities. Orange Sierra Leone CEO Sekou Amadou Bah described the centre as a bold investment to support the expansion of digital and financial services. Brelotte BA, CEO of Sonatel Group and Chairman of Orange Sierra Leone's Board, said: "This facility represents a major step forward for data sovereignty. Digital transformation must be inclusive."

Leader of Government Business in Parliament, Hon. Matthew Nyumah, noted that the country previously "had only one nerve centre for digital connectivity in Freetown," and the Bo centre ensures continuity if the Freetown hub is affected by disaster.
