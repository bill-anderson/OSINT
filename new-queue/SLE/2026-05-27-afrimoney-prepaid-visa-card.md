Government officials and financial industry leaders gathered in Freetown on Monday (25 May 2026) to launch the Afrimoney Prepaid Visa Card, a mobile money-integrated solution designed to expand digital financial access. The product, introduced by Africell Sierra Leone in collaboration with Ecobank and Visa, enables users to conduct domestic and international digital transactions directly through their Afrimoney wallets without necessarily holding a traditional bank account.

Chief Minister Dr. Moinina David Sengeh, who officially launched the card, described it as a step toward "techquity." He said the card would benefit small-scale traders ordering goods internationally, kekeh riders building credit history via "Trust Fuel" solutions, retired citizens receiving NACSA support through mobile money wallets, and students in remote areas.

Sengeh credited the Bank of Sierra Leone's regulatory sandbox for real-time testing of fintech products, and acknowledged the role of NatCA and the Ministry of Communication. He highlighted that financial inclusion in Sierra Leone has nearly doubled, rising from 18 percent in 2018 to nearly 40 percent in 2025, and that over one million transactions have been processed on the Instant Payment Switch. Users obtain the card by dialing 1615*4#; it is powered by Ecobank and accepted at Visa points globally.
