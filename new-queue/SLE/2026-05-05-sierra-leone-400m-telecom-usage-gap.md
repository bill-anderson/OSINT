Sierra Leone's Minister of Communication, Technology and Innovation Salima Monorma Bah said that despite investing $400 million in telecom infrastructure, the country still faces a 60% usage gap. She shared this at the Parliamentary Committee on Information and Communications' annual stakeholder meeting at the Atlantic Hotel in Freetown, attended by NatCA, Sierratel, SALPOST, the Felei Tech City project, mobile operators and civil society.

Despite the national fiber backbone (NFB) being expanded to 14 of the country's 16 districts, the usage gap remains high. Bah called for greater industry accountability and infrastructure sharing to reduce operating costs that hinder affordability. She cited a $15 million investment for the upcoming landing of a second subsea cable.

Digital divide figures: According to DataReportal statistics from end of October 2025, Sierra Leone had 1.85 million Internet users. With a population of 8.86 million, the internet penetration rate stood at 20.8%, meaning 7.02 million people (79.2%) remained offline at the end of 2025. In August 2025, Sierra Leone partnered with Liberia to roll out the ECOWAS free roaming initiative. Starlink launched in Sierra Leone in June 2024.
