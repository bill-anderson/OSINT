The National Communications Authority (NatCA) signed an agreement with the Republic of Guinea to introduce free mobile roaming between the two countries. The protocol was finalised during the 15th Session of the African Council of Regulators (CAR-15), held alongside the Transform Africa 2025 Summit and supported by the Smart Africa Alliance.

This marks the second major telecommunications partnership between Sierra Leone and Guinea in less than a year. In November 2024, both countries signed an agreement to interconnect their national internet backbone networks. The new roaming deal forms part of a wider regional effort; Ghana, Togo, Benin, Côte d'Ivoire, Burkina Faso, and Liberia have entered similar agreements.

Director-General Amara Brewah signed on behalf of Sierra Leone. Once operational, the agreement will allow mobile users to keep their home SIM and number while in the other country, enable more affordable calls/SMS/data, and reduce "bill shock" for traders, transport workers, students and families crossing the border. NatCA and its Guinean counterpart will work with licensed operators to finalise tariff structures and quality-of-service standards. Brewah linked the deal to "Smart Africa's vision of a Single Digital Market."
