# Senegal Moves New Technological Deal Into Operational Phase

We Are Tech Africa - 25 March 2026, by Ange J.A de Berry Quenum

The Senegalese government launched, on Tuesday 23 March, the flagship projects of the New Deal Technologique, marking the transition to the operational phase of a strategy designed to accelerate the digital transformation of public administration and the economy. One year after announcing the initiative, authorities are deploying digital platforms and infrastructure to modernise public services, strengthen digital sovereignty and improve access to government services.

Among the flagship initiatives, the e-Senegal platform acts as a one-stop shop for administrative procedures online; e-Consulat targets the diaspora; and SenTresor introduces a unified electronic payment system for public fees (taxes, customs duties, local government services). These rely on a national interoperability platform following the "once only" principle. Governance was restructured with the Digital Governance Committee (GouvNum), created March 2025, and the National Digital Council. The programme is part of Vision Senegal 2050, with a CFA1,100 billion ($1.95 billion) investment portfolio over 2025-2034 and a digital-inclusion component to connect more than one million people in underserved areas. The state is deploying data centres in Diamniadio and Orana, increasing national bandwidth from 600 Mbps to 20 Gbps, and developing a sovereign cloud to host sensitive public data locally.
