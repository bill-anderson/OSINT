# Senegal Advances Digital Governance with New Interoperability Platform

Tech Africa News - 19 September 2025, by Jennifer Onyeagoro

The Senegalese government is advancing its data exchange interoperability platform, a project officially launched on 22 April 2025, aimed at modernising public administration and improving service delivery. During a recent COCOOR (Coordination Committee) meeting, stakeholders reviewed the project's progress and validated the roadmap for next steps.

The platform is designed to enable ministries, public agencies and private organisations to exchange data securely and efficiently. Its first phase, scheduled for completion by end-December 2025, will allow vulnerable families to receive social assistance more quickly, strengthen state transparency, improve public revenue mobilisation, simplify administrative procedures, and foster innovation through secure data access. The initiative aligns with the New Deal for Technology and the Senegal 2050 agenda.
