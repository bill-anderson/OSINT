The Autorité de Régulation des Télécommunications/TIC de Côte d'Ivoire (ARTCI) has taken part in the 2nd Francophone Forum on Digital Governance and Artificial Intelligence, held under the auspices of the International Organisation of La Francophonie (OIF).

The Ivorian delegation, led by the Chairman of the Regulatory Council, Roger Adom, included members of the Council, the Director General and several senior directors of the regulatory authority.

The forum brought together policymakers, regulators and international stakeholders to discuss emerging frameworks for digital governance and artificial intelligence, with a focus on ensuring that technological development aligns with principles of trust, inclusivity and sustainable development.

ARTCI's participation reflects Côte d'Ivoire's broader engagement in global policy discussions on digital transformation and its commitment to strengthening cooperation with Francophone institutions on regulatory best practices.

Officials noted that the engagement provides an opportunity to exchange experiences with other regulators and contribute to shaping international approaches to artificial intelligence governance, particularly in areas such as data protection, digital trust and responsible innovation.

The authority reiterated its ambition to support the development of a secure and inclusive digital ecosystem that promotes innovation while safeguarding users and reinforcing confidence in digital services across Côte d'Ivoire.
