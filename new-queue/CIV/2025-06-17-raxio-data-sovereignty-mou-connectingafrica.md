By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa. June 17, 2025.

Data center operator Raxio Group and Côte d'Ivoire's Ministry of Digital Transition have signed a memorandum of understanding to strengthen the country's data sovereignty plan.

Raxio Group and Côte d'Ivoire's Ministry of Digital Transition have signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to strengthen the country's data sovereignty plan and accelerate the digital transformation of public services.

Under the partnership, the country's government will leverage Raxio Côte d'Ivoire (CIV1), a Tier 3-certified colocation facility in Abidjan that was inaugurated in September 2024.

The 2,000-square-meter facility is capable of housing up to 800 racks at full capacity and delivering 3MW of IT power to customer equipment.

In a LinkedIn post, Raxio Group said the CIV1 facility will provide the Ministry with 24/7 access, advanced monitoring and supervision, and redundant, scalable power infrastructure.

The data center operator added that the CIV1 will provide seamless carrier-neutral connectivity to national and international networks.

## Raxio's African footprint

Raxio's MoU with Côte d'Ivoire's Ministry of Digital Transition comes at a time when the company has a network of Tier 3 carrier-neutral colocation data centers on the African continent.

Currently, Raxio has Tier 3 carrier-neutral colocation data centers in Uganda, Ethiopia, Mozambique, the Democratic Republic of Congo and Côte d'Ivoire and has plans to build data centers in Tanzania and Angola.

The MoU also follows Raxio Group's appointment of Robert Skjødt as its new CEO, succeeding Robert Mullins.

In October 2023, Raxio secured US$46 million to support its expansion initiatives and facilitate the development of high-quality hyperscale-ready data center facilities in leading African metro areas.

## Data center growth in Africa

The MoU between Raxio Group and Côte d'Ivoire's Ministry of Digital Transition coincides with a period of significant expansion in data center infrastructure by a number of companies across the African continent.

In April 2025, Digital infrastructure firm Equinix announced the expansion of its data center in Lagos, Nigeria, to support the country's growing digital transformation efforts.

In January 2025, PAIX Data Centres announced the construction of a new data center in Dakar, Senegal, with the first phase of the project scheduled to be operational in 2026.

PAIX is already operational in Ghana and Kenya and has four other facilities under construction, including PAIX Dakar.

Africa Data Centres, a business of pan-African technology group Cassava Technologies, announced in July 2024 it was expanding its data center facility (CPT1) in Cape Town, South Africa, to add another 6MW of IT load, effectively doubling its capacity in the city.

Open Access Data Centres (OADC) and Texaf also launched an open-access carrier-neutral Tier 3 data center in the DRC in August 2024, a year after its scheduled launch.
