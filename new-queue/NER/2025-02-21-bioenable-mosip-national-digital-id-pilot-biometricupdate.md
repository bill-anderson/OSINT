# BioEnable powers Niger pilot for MOSIP-based national digital ID

The Republic of Niger has completed a national digital ID trial based on **MOSIP** (Modular Open Source Identity Platform), with Indian firm **BioEnable** enabling the process through its biometric technology. BioEnable says it deployed its IriUniverse-Two dual iris scanners for high-accuracy registration.

BioEnable worked on the pilot with **Niger's Ministry of Interior, Public Security and Territorial Administration, in collaboration with the West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion Project (WURI-Niger)**. WURI Niger launched in 2022 with the objective of issuing national digital ID to **at least 22 million people** in the country by 2026.

The registration was carried out in **four communes — Tanout, Mayahi, Doutchi and Tagazar — with over 3,000 persons biometrically enrolled**. The IriUniverse-Two scanners enabled ISO-compliant biometric authentication. BioEnable announced MOSIP compliance for its biometric enrollment kit at the close of 2024.

Stated objectives of the pilot: evaluate how a digital ID system can be implemented in Niger using MOSIP in real-world conditions; streamline biometric-data capture; encourage community participation; and assess cost efficiency for a large-scale programme. "This pilot marks an important step in Niger's digital transformation journey," said Mahesh Ghatge, CEO & CTO of BioEnable Technologies. As part of its digital-transformation agenda, Niger is looking to put in place a secure and inclusive national digital ID system to facilitate public-service delivery, drawing on open-source and interoperability.
