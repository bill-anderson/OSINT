# Entretien avec le président de la HAPDP (M. Iro Adamou)

*(Le Sahel / ONEP is the Niger state daily. Capture is not endorsement.)*

Interview with **Iro Adamou, President of the High Authority for the Protection of Personal Data (HAPDP)**, on the authority's mission and legal basis.

Key legal re-dating: the HAPDP was created in 2017 under **Loi n°2017-28 of 3 May 2017** (modified by **Loi n°2019-71 of 24 December 2019**), but "cet arsenal juridique … ont été abrogées et remplacés par la **loi n°2022-59 du 16 décembre 2022 et ses textes modificatifs subséquents**" — i.e. the 2017/2019 laws were **repealed and replaced by Loi n°2022-59 of 16 December 2022** (and subsequent modifying texts, incl. Loi n°2023-31), to keep pace with technological change and align with international standards. Related legislation cited: the cybercrime law and the civil-status (état civil) law.

Institutional basis: Niger **ratified the AU Convention on Cybersecurity and Personal Data Protection (Malabo Convention) on 23 May 2022**, whose Article 8 requires an independent administrative authority to oversee data-protection compliance. Under Article 15 of the law, HAPDP ensures processing of personal data does not harm public liberties or private life; its powers (Article-based) include issuing individual/regulatory decisions (délibérations), receiving declarations/authorisation requests and complaints, conducting controls via sworn agents, notifying judicial authorities of offences, and giving reasoned opinions on draft laws/regulations touching personal-data processing.
