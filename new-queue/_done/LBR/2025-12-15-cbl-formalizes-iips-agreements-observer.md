# CBL Formalizes IIPS Operations with Mobile Money, Orange Money

By David A. Yates — Liberian Observer, December 15, 2025

The Central Bank of Liberia (CBL) has taken a major step toward transforming the country's digital payments landscape with the signing of Participation and Service Level Agreements with Lonestar Cell/MTN Mobile Money and Orange Money, aimed at advancing the full operation of the Inclusive Instant Payment System (IIPS). The agreements, signed on Friday in Monrovia, establish the operational, technical, and performance framework for Liberia's national instant payment platform, allowing customers to send and receive money instantly across different mobile money networks.

Under the Participation Agreement, the CBL, MTN Mobile Money, Orange Money, and ThitsaWorks (the national switch operator) are formally bound to work together to ensure the smooth, secure, and transparent operation of the IIPS. The agreement runs for an initial period of three years, renewable upon mutual consent, and defines the roles and responsibilities of each institution. As part of its obligations, the CBL will hold and manage settlement/liquidity accounts in both US dollars and Liberian dollars, process daily settlements, provide daily settlement reports, and move funds between providers based on instructions issued by the switch operator. MTN Mobile Money and Orange Money must maintain designated settlement accounts at the CBL and keep minimum liquidity balances.

ThitsaWorks, as national switch operator, will operate the core switching technology, support daily transaction reconciliation using Mojaloop data, provide advance notice of maintenance, train participating institutions, and transmit all settlement instructions to the CBL. Complementing the Participation Agreement, the Service Level Agreement (SLA) sets strict performance and reliability standards — system availability requirements, response times, and operational benchmarks — while introducing penalties for late settlements, liquidity shortfalls, and system downtime. The two agreements underscore the CBL's commitment to building a safe, inclusive, and reliable national payment infrastructure ahead of the official launch of the IIPS on December 16.
