We Are Tech Africa (published in French by Samira Njoya; EN adaptation). Benin, Burkina Faso, Cote d'Ivoire, Guinea, Mali and Senegal adopted shared AI-governance guidelines to promote ethical, inclusive and responsible AI. Advocacy organisation Niyel led the initiative, announced 6 July 2026 on the sidelines of the UN's inaugural Global AI Dialogue on Governance (submitted as a written contribution). Developed by researchers, technical experts and senior digital officials of the six states; recommends transparent/safe/inclusive AI systems, stronger data protection, skills development, algorithmic-bias mitigation, support for local innovation and digital sovereignty. Complements (does not replace) national AI strategies. Aligns with UNESCO's 2021 AI-ethics recommendation and the AU's 2024 Continental AI Strategy. Warns uncontrolled adoption of externally-developed AI could deepen technological dependence. Mali is a signatory, its first substantive continental AI-governance commitment captured here.
