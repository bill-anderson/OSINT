# 21-nation COMESA bloc launches payment system to settle trade in local currencies

By Adonijah Ndege — TechCabal, 10 October 2025

The Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA), a 21-country regional trade bloc representing over 640 million people, has launched a new digital payments platform — the **Digital Retail Payments Platform (DRPP)** — that could make it easier and cheaper for African businesses to trade directly in local currencies instead of the US dollar. It is being tested between Malawi and Zambia, with plans to roll out across all 21 COMESA member states, including Kenya, Ethiopia, and Egypt.

The bloc hopes the system will keep costs below 3% of transaction value, down from the current average of around 8%. It is being implemented in partnership with two (unnamed) digital financial service providers and a foreign exchange firm. "For the first time, cross-border trade within COMESA can be settled directly in local currencies," Kenya's Trade Minister Lee Kinyanjui said at the Nairobi launch. "This is a game-changer."

COMESA members include Burundi, Comoros, DR Congo, Djibouti, Egypt, Eritrea, Eswatini, Ethiopia, Kenya, Libya, Madagascar, Malawi, Mauritius, Rwanda, Seychelles, Somalia, Sudan, Tunisia, Uganda, Zambia and Zimbabwe. Intra-COMESA trade remains small — less than 10% of the bloc's total trade — due to currency conversion costs, limited financial connectivity, and reliance on the US dollar. Kenya's President William Ruto, recently made COMESA chairman, said Kenya has increased investment in the Trade Development Bank ($100 million) and Afreximbank ($50 million) to support regional trade.
