# SCRYPT Expands Stablecoin Settlement Network Across Four East African Markets

TechAfrica News (Editorial Team), 16 July 2026

SCRYPT, a digital-assets infrastructure provider, announced the expansion of its licensed stablecoin settlement infrastructure across **four East African markets**, enabling banks, payment providers and corporate treasury teams to move value into and out of the continent in real time.

In most African markets, accessing US dollars remains the biggest friction in cross-border payments: local currencies can be volatile, bank dollar liquidity is often constrained, and correspondent banking remains slow and expensive. Businesses paying international suppliers frequently must convert local currency into USD before purchasing stablecoins for settlement, incurring FX conversions and spreads. SCRYPT says it eliminates this intermediate conversion by enabling direct settlement corridors for local African currencies into stablecoins — "local currency in, stablecoin out."

The expansion adds settlement support across four African currencies: the **Kenyan shilling (KES), Tanzanian shilling (TZS), Rwandan franc (RWF) and Ugandan shilling (UGX)**. "Across Africa, stablecoin adoption is driven by economic need, not speculation," said founder and CEO Norman Wooding. "Businesses here are not chasing yield, they are trying to pay suppliers and manage treasury without losing margin to a banking system that rations dollars." The article frames stablecoins as increasingly becoming settlement infrastructure rather than an investment product.
