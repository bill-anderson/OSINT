# How DPI can accelerate Africa's push for a single market

By Ayang Macdonald — Biometric Update, 26 December 2025

A conversation with Tech5 Co-founder/Chairman/CTO Rahul Parthe, and CEO of ID30, Jaume Dubois, on how Digital Public Infrastructure can turn the AfCFTA's single-market vision into reality. The AfCFTA vision is a single market of ~1.7 billion people with ~$6.7 trillion in spending by 2030. Yet only about 15.4 percent of trade in Africa currently takes place among African countries (UNCTAD), constrained by paperwork, payment delays, and identity verification friction.

Dubois argues DPI (digital identity, real-time payments, data-sharing/interoperability) requires a paradigm shift: countries "must stop competing over systems, and start collaborating on frameworks, standards, and trust." Cross-border interoperability is harder than within a single country, facing political, infrastructure and literacy barriers.

On standards, Dubois emphasises global decentralized standards such as W3C verifiable credentials, which are "sector-agnostic and country-agnostic." He notes he is working on two ECOWAS projects — an e-ID roadmap and a common ECOWAS framework for identity interoperability — but observes country positions vary widely, with differing progress and willingness to share data. Many countries are implementing MOSIP as a digital public good, helping create convergence. Regional payment frameworks (interoperability, digital wallets, verifiable credentials) are emerging alongside identity frameworks.

Both experts stress cross-border DPI in Africa will succeed "through alignment, not uniformity," being use-case-led (starting with cross-border payments, logistics, customs), with shared principles: common trust frameworks, interoperable standards, and mutual recognition while retaining sovereignty over national systems.
