# East African countries to launch regional satellite

By Matshepo Sehloho — Connecting Africa, 4 May 2026

Kenya, South Sudan, Uganda and Rwanda have agreed to move forward with plans to deploy a regional satellite to enhance connectivity across East Africa's northern corridor. Kenya's Cabinet Secretary for Information, Communications and the Digital Economy, William Kabogo Gitau, met government representatives from the other three countries on the sidelines of the Connected Africa Summit 2026 in Nairobi.

The project — the **Northern Corridor Regional Communication and Broadcasting Satellite Initiative (NCRCBSI)** — aims to bring connectivity to East Africa and reduce reliance on external satellite systems and international connectivity routes by investing in infrastructure that supports regional priorities. "We further resolved to work towards the design and launch of a jointly owned regional communication and broadcasting satellite to support digital services and broadcasting across our countries," Gitau said. As a first step, the four partner states will undertake a comprehensive feasibility study assessing technical, financial and legal requirements, supported through shared funding.

The commitment comes amid other cross-border connectivity moves: in February 2026 Ethio Telecom, Djibouti Telecom and the Sudatel Group turned a cross-border optical fibre MoU into a binding agreement; in July 2025 Tanzania's TTLC and Kenya's ICTA launched a terrestrial fibre link between Dar es Salaam and Mombasa; and in May 2025 Bayobab completed a fibre network linking Mombasa to Kampala.
