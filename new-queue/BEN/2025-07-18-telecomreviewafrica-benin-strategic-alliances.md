# Benin Advances Toward Digital Leadership with New Strategic Alliances

Benin's Information Systems and Digital Agency (ASIN) has signed several strategic partnerships with leading digital organizations to advance the country's digital transformation. These collaborations support the government's vision of using technology to drive inclusive growth, enhance competitiveness, and boost regional influence.

Key agreements include a partnership with the Institute for Inclusive Digital Africa (IIDiA), which will provide ASIN with expert support to modernize information systems and strengthen Benin's position in promoting sovereign and inclusive digital development across Africa.

ASIN also signed an agreement with QUALITY CORPORATE SARL to reinforce its role as the National Root Certification Authority. This will help expand the country's range of trusted digital services—such as electronic signatures and digital identities—while aligning with international cybersecurity standards.

Additionally, a memorandum of understanding (MoU) was signed with Orbus Digital Services to support Benin's public key infrastructure (PKI). The collaboration focuses on digitizing administrative services, building technical capacity, and promoting trusted digital tools across sectors like health, education, finance, and justice.

According to ASIN's Director General, these partnerships reflect Benin's ambition to become a regional leader in secure and sustainable digital service adoption.
