The Nigerian government launched the Virtual Privacy Academy, a digital training platform to deepen data protection and privacy in the private and public sectors. Dr Bosun Tijani (Minister of Communications, Innovation and Digital Economy) unveiled it during the 8th annual conference of the Network of African Data Protection Authorities (NADPA) in Abuja ("Balancing Innovation in Africa: Data Protection and Privacy in Emerging Technologies"), drawing attendees from over 30 African countries plus Europe, Asia, Middle East and the US. NDPC National Commissioner Dr Vincent Olatunji said the Commission had completed over 5,000 compliance assessments, opened 223 investigations, and assisted 12 organisations with rehabilitation, adding "strong data protection frameworks are not barriers to innovation, but enablers of a resilient and inclusive digital economy."
