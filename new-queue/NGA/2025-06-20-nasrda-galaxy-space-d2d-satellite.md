Nigeria's National Space Research and Development Agency (NASRDA) signed an MoU in Abuja with Chinese satellite company Galaxy Space to deploy direct-to-device (D2D) satellite communication technology, enabling Nigerians to access internet and voice services directly from satellites without cell towers. DG Dr Matthew Adepoju said it will let phones, laptops and IoT systems connect seamlessly to satellites, eliminating blind spots in rural and remote areas, projecting full D2D deployment before end-2025. The pact includes technology transfer and training Nigerian engineers: "We can't rely forever on imported technology... This collaboration is about building Nigerian capacity... and reducing external dependence in the space tech ecosystem." Context: the Internet Society estimates over 60% of Africa's population lacks broadband access.
