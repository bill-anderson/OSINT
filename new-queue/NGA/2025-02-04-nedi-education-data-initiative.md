# Nigeria Launches Ambitious Education Data Initiative (NEDI), Appoints Bode Pedro as Digital Advisor

Partner content, TechCabal, 4th Feb, 2025. Abuja, Nigeria

Nigeria has taken a significant stride towards transforming its education sector with the inauguration of the Nigerian Education Data Initiative (NEDI) committee. Dr. Tunji Alausa, The Honourable Minister of Education, unveiled the initiative, naming Bode Pedro, CEO of Casava, as Digital Advisor. This appointment underscores the government's commitment to leveraging technology and data-driven solutions to enhance access, quality, and management within the education system.

NEDI aims to establish a centralized, real-time data ecosystem, empowering policymakers, educators, and stakeholders with the insights to make informed decisions and drive improved educational outcomes nationwide.

Dr. Alausa emphasized the critical need for NEDI, stating, "For too long, the absence of a unified, comprehensive data framework has hindered our ability to allocate resources and make informed decisions effectively. NEDI will provide this vital foundation, consolidating all critical education data in Nigeria into a harmonized databank and unified platform. This is more than just information; it's a catalyst for development, enabling us to deliver quality education that meets the aspirations of every Nigerian child."

Pedro, a recognized expert in digital transformation and entrepreneurship, brings extensive experience to this role. His leadership at Casava, a leading microinsurance company, has demonstrated his ability to leverage technology for impactful solutions.

"This isn't just a data initiative—it's a movement," noted Pedro in a LinkedIn post announcing the appointment. "With reliable data, we can empower EdTech startups to compete globally, attract venture funding, and digitize education services, mirroring the transformative impact of BVN and payment switching on Nigeria's banking and fintech sectors. By making this data accessible to international institutions admitting Nigerian students and employers seeking credible education data, we are positioning Nigeria as a global leader in talent, innovation, and education technology. The integration of artificial intelligence (AI) and large language models (LLMs) into NEDI's framework will further amplify its impact. AI-driven tools can analyze vast amounts of education data to identify trends, predict outcomes, and personalize learning experiences, while LLMs can support educators and students with advanced language processing capabilities, such as automated grading, content generation, and virtual tutoring. Together, these technologies will not only enhance the efficiency of the education system but also foster innovation and inclusivity across Nigeria's learning landscape."

This initiative, Pedro further noted, "is a crucial step towards building a data-driven education ecosystem that will increase education outcomes, create jobs and impact our economy."

The launch of the NEDI committee signals a decisive step in Nigeria's journey to modernize its education sector and harness the power of data and technology for sustainable development.
