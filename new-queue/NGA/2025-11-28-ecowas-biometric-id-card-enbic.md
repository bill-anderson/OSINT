The Federal Government officially rolled out the ECOWAS National Biometric Identity Card (ENBIC), targeting transborder crimes, irregular migration and document fraud across West Africa. The launch makes Nigeria the seventh ECOWAS country to operationalise the card — a project conceived by the regional bloc 11 years ago, now revived under President Tinubu. Interior Minister Dr Olubunmi Tunji-Ojo said it heralds "a powerful new beginning" for secure mobility and regional integration: "You cannot protect who you do not know. Identification is the bedrock of security." The card will integrate with global public key directories like ICAO and relieve pressure on Nigeria's passport system — ECOWAS-bound travellers won't need a passport. Linked to the 2024 deployment of the UN-mandated Advanced Passenger Information System (APIS).
