The Federal Government disclosed plans to link citizens' credit scores to the National Identification Number (NIN), establishing a centralised credit bureau and a comprehensive database of Nigeria's credit profiles. CREDICORP (Nigerian Consumer Credit Corporation) MD Uzoma Nwagba said all lenders will be required to report loan repayments so every Nigerian maintains an accurate credit score, with structured (not predatory) consequences for defaulters — e.g. inability to renew a passport or driver's license, or rent a house. "This system ensures that whether your money is in a commercial bank, FinTech, or microfinance institution, loans taken and not repaid will be tracked and recoverable." Nwagba said CREDICORP's mission includes building credit infrastructure, combating corruption, and facilitating consumer credit to help Nigerians buy locally manufactured products (promoting the Nigeria First policy).
