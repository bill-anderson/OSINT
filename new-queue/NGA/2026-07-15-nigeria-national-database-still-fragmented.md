NIMC DG Engr Abisoye Coker-Odusote declared (at the ID4Africa AGM in Abidjan and in a Vanguard interview) that Nigeria has moved beyond fragmented identity databases, with the NIN as the country's master identity linking records across government and private sector; agencies for taxation, immigration, education, healthcare, security and aviation rely on the NIN, while banks, telcos and fintechs authenticate through NIMC's real-time verification. However, the report notes the reality contradicts the claim: many Nigerians still fill long forms to obtain/renew passports, driver's licenses or tax clearance, and banks, telcos and immigration still do siloed data registrations — a confirmation protocol with NIN is not the same as a unified database.
