Nigeria's internet (broadband) penetration crossed 50% in November 2025, reaching 50.58%, per NCC data — up from 45.61% in January but well short of the 70% target under the National Broadband Plan (2020-2025) expiring December. Broadband subscriptions stood at around 109 million by November 2025. The NBP targeted 90% coverage, minimum speeds of 25 Mbps urban / 10 Mbps rural, and data affordability of no more than ₦390 per 1GB. Nigeria missed its interim 2023 target of 50% and ended 2024 at 44.43%. Fibre vandalism (30-43 cuts daily), high Right-of-Way fees and declining subscriptions (over a million users lost in early 2025) have hampered expansion.
