The NCC revealed Nigeria records an average of 1,100 fibre optic cuts weekly, alongside 545 site access denials and nearly 100 thefts. Disclosed by EVC Dr Aminu Maida (represented by Mr Edoyemi Ogoh) at the Industry Sustainability and Critical National Information Infrastructure (CNII) Conference in Lagos. Despite telecom's contribution of over 14.4% to Nigeria's GDP, the sector remains under siege; telecom infrastructure (towers, fibre, base stations, data centres) is classified as CNII but increasingly targeted by vandals, thieves and uncooperative communities, disrupting banking, healthcare, education and security. Other challenges: poor power supply, Right-of-Way delays, cybersecurity threats.
