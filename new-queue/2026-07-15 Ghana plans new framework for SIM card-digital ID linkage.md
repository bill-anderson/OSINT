Ghana plans a new legislative framework to guide the linkage of SIM cards to the national digital ID (Ghana Card), to combat growing mobile money fraud. Minister of Communication, Digital Technology and Innovation Samuel Nartey George announced it at a stakeholders' meeting on digital security. Per the Bank of Ghana, mobile money transactions reached ~$88.3 billion in 2023; the Ghana Police cybercrime unit recorded ~7,250 mobile money fraud cases that year (+32% YoY).

The new SIM registration framework — to be presented as a bill (Legislative Instrument) when parliament reconvenes in October — will involve biometric SIM registration and device registration via a Centralized Equipment Identity Register (CEIR) linked to the NIA database. Participants included the NCA, telecom operators, the Mobile Money Agents Association of Ghana (MMAAG, 12,000+ members) and others; the MMAAG raised cyber fraud, cross-platform difficulties, SIM pricing and network challenges. NCA Director General Rev. Edmund Yirenkyi Fianko said measures will make the forthcoming SIM registration drive seamless.
