# Vodacom Lesotho at 30: From one portable antenna to a digital nation

*Public Eye, 17 June 2026. By 'Mathato Seboka.*

Vodacom Lesotho, which switched on its first portable base station in 1996, marks three decades of operations. As of 2026 it serves approximately **1.21 million customers**, commanding an **85 percent market share**. Its footprint has grown to **422 base stations, including 101 solar-powered sites**, delivering 2G coverage to 97% of the country, 3G to 98%, and 4G/LTE to 96% — across one of the world's most challenging terrains.

Vodacom Lesotho directly employs more than 300 Basotho and supports thousands more through its ecosystem, including a national network of **14,000 M-Pesa agents**. CEO Mohale Ralebitso said connectivity has "fundamentally altered life in Lesotho," pointing to benefits in healthcare, education and agriculture, and to social grants reaching vulnerable Basotho "efficiently and with dignity." The company reports a strong localisation profile and consistent tax, dividend and regulatory compliance.
