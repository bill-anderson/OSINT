# Lesotho engages Vodacom on digitisation

*ITWeb Africa, 17 February 2026. By Lezeth Khoza.*

Lesotho is set to bolster its digital transformation strategy by leveraging frameworks from Vodacom's broader African portfolio, following a visit by a Vodacom Group delegation. The public-private partnership is designed to fast-track the government's digitisation agenda, spearheaded by ICT Minister Nthati Moorosi.

The Vodacom delegation was led by Mohamed Abdallah, CEO of Vodafone Egypt and newly appointed head of International Markets, accompanied by Vodacom Group regulatory chief officer Ayman Essam and Vodacom Lesotho's leadership (CEO Mohale Ralebitso; head of regulatory affairs Tšepo Ntaopane). Moorosi reaffirmed commitment to building a "Digital Lesotho" with infrastructure supporting e-governance, financial inclusion and public service delivery. Vodacom said it would assist the government's digitisation "as it did in Kenya and Egypt," providing a technical blueprint to modernise government systems.
