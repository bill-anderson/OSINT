# Guinea and Sierra Leone Link National Fibre Networks for Regional Digital Integration

In a pivotal step toward regional digital integration, the Guinean Backbone Management Society (SOGEB) and Sierra Leone's Leoncom have signed an Interconnection Agreement to link their national fibre optic networks. The agreement, formalized in Conakry, builds on a broader telecommunications cooperation framework between the two governments.

The partnership is designed to enhance cross-border data exchange, cut internet and transit costs for telecom providers and end users, and strengthen the resilience of national networks through shared infrastructure and redundancy. It aligns with regional initiatives under ECOWAS and the African Union.

The interconnection point is located in Pamelap, a key border town between Guinea and Sierra Leone. The initial transmission capacity is 42.5 Gbps, with expansion plans to reach 100 Gbps by 2026. The infrastructure leverages fibre optic technology and secure IP/MPLS protocols.

"This agreement is a major milestone for Guinea, reflecting our commitment to building an open and interconnected national backbone," said Mohamed Kourouma, Director of SOGEB. Foday Rado Yokie, CEO of Leoncom, emphasized the bilateral value. Nouhan Traoré, Secretary General of Guinea's Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and the Digital Economy, said: "This project is a key component of the Guinea–Sierra Leone Digital Pact 2024, aimed at unifying digital markets and services."

Telecom operators like Orange and MTN stand to gain from lower data transit costs, while enterprises and public sector institutions will experience improved latency and network stability. Discussions are underway to extend the fibre network toward Liberia.
