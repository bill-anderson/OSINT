The Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ) handed over the country's inaugural Data Controller Licences to 570 companies, marking Zimbabwe's commitment to data protection and digital governance. The issuance aligns with Section 4 of the Cyber and Data Protection (Licensing of Data Controllers and Appointment of Data Protection Officers) Regulations, 2024. The licences, valid for 12 months, are categorised into four tiers (Tier 1 to Tier 4) reflecting the scope and scale of data processing.

Notable recipients included NetOne Cellular, POTRAZ, the Ministry of ICT, Dandemutande, Econet Wireless, EcoCash Holdings, RBZ, Innbucks, Mukuru, TelOne, Starlink Zimbabwe, Zimpost, NUST, Midlands State University and Africa University.

ICT Minister Tatenda Mavetera hailed the event as a testament to Zimbabwe's dedication to "safeguarding personal data, promoting accountability, and fostering trust in the digital economy," positioning the country among Africa's pioneers in formal data governance alongside Nigeria and Ghana. POTRAZ Director General Dr Gift Kalisto Machengete said the licences ensure compliance with data protection laws and shield citizens from breaches. "Compliance is not a nuisance but a necessity in protecting what matters," he said.

The training program for data controllers has attracted participants from SADC member states including Botswana, Eswatini and Malawi. Mavetera urged POTRAZ to decentralise training to Bulawayo, Mutare and Gweru. The licensing regime is expected to enhance Zimbabwe's appeal to foreign investors and align with regional frameworks. With 570 licences issued and over 500 data protection officers trained, the ceremony signals a step toward a secure digital economy.
