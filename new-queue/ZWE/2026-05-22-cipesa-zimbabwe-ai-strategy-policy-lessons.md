CIPESA analysis (by Edrine Wanyama) of Zimbabwe's National AI Strategy 2026-2030, assessing its lessons for other African countries. The strategy aims to accelerate development, industrialisation and service delivery in health, finance, agriculture, education and public administration, emphasising building local data infrastructure over reliance on foreign data storage, and an AI governance approach grounded in Ubuntu, human rights, accountability, transparency and inclusivity.

The strategy is organised around six pillars: (1) AI talent and capacity development; (2) AI infrastructure and computational sovereignty; (3) AI adoption and service transformation; (4) AI governance, ethics and regulation; (5) AI research, development and innovation; and (6) strategic international collaboration.

CIPESA finds the strategy aligns with the AU Continental AI Strategy on coordinated governance, digital sovereignty and sectoral innovation, and — if implemented effectively — could help address "digital neo-colonialism." It also aligns with Agenda 2063 and the SDGs, promoting AI for crop disease prevention and mineral development. However, Zimbabwe faces significant governance and implementation challenges: it scored 0 in the 2024 Global Index on Responsible AI Governance, highlighting the gap between policy ambition and institutional readiness, and requiring robust legal safeguards, accountability mechanisms and oversight institutions.
