The Uganda Peoples Defence Forces (UPDF) has warned against using Starlink. This follows the Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) restricting importation of Starlink gadgets, communication equipment and associated components without clearance from the UPDF Chief of Defence Forces (CDF). URA commissioner of customs Hajji Asadu Kigozi Kisitu, in a December 19, 2025 letter to all customs staff, said the restriction takes effect immediately.

Acting director of defence public information Col. Chris Magezi defended the CDF-clearance requirement, saying it had no connection with alleged internet restrictions ahead of general elections: "Clearance is part of security... Anything that needs security will ordinarily have to be cleared by [the CDF]. Has it even been licensed to operate in Uganda? It is illegal." UCC director of corporate affairs Fred Otunnu said Starlink was not licensed in Uganda.

As of mid-2025 Starlink operated in over 19 African nations but not officially in Uganda. In April 2025, UCC ED Nyombi Thembo confirmed engagement with Starlink over a licence application, after Starlink representatives met President Museveni signalling potential approval. (This restriction preceded the January 2026 election-period shutdown and Starlink's eventual May 2026 licensing.)
