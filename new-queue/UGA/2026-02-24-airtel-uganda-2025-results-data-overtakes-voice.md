Airtel Uganda reported financial results for the year ending December 31, 2025: a 41% increase in profit after tax to $124.3 million (UGX 446.8 billion), driven by rising data revenue and cost management. Overall revenue was $620 million (UGX 2.23 trillion), up 13.3% from $548 million in 2024, as smartphone penetration and digital adoption pushed data to replace voice as its main revenue source. Data income rose more than 22% to over $306 million (UGX 1.1 trillion), while voice revenue was $292 million.

MD Soumendra Sahu cited 24.5% EBITDA growth (margin 54.9%). Airtel rolled out 258 new 4G sites and 164 additional 5G sites; by year-end 100% of network sites were 4G-enabled with 5G coverage at 364 sites. Total subscribers rose ~14% to 19.2 million; data customers up 19.6% to 8.7 million; data traffic up 46%. Airtel, Uganda's second-largest operator after MTN, plans to accelerate fibre expansion, deepen 5G rollout and explore satellite partnerships for rural coverage.
