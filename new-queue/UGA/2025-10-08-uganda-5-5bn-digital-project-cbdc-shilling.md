Diacente Group, a Ugandan company that develops green industrial zones, has partnered with US-based Global Settlement Network (GSN) on a $5.5 billion plan to build national digital infrastructure linking Uganda's farms, mines, power projects and factories. The project also includes a pilot of Uganda's first central bank digital currency (CBDC), the "digital shilling," backed by treasury bonds, allowing Ugandans to send, receive and save money on their phones while tying financial activity to real economic activities.

The $5.5 billion investment will be financed through tokenised loans and investors from Korea and the Middle East. In a country where more than 80% of the workforce is in the informal economy, the system could make trade faster, cheaper and more transparent. GSN co-founder and CEO Ryan Kirkley said the project will link technology with real production — "a programmable economy grounded in real assets, regulatory collaboration and mass accessibility" — using advanced privacy tools, managed by government and local institutions and licensed locally to keep operations in Uganda and the wider East Africa region. The rollout starts in the Karamoja Green (industrial zone).
