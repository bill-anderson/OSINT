President Yoweri Museveni on Friday witnessed the signing of a Memorandum of Understanding and operational license agreement between the Uganda Communications Commission (UCC) and Starlink at State House, Entebbe, clearing the way for Starlink operations in Uganda. It was signed by UCC executive director George William Nyombi Thembo and Starlink/SpaceX representatives led by Ryan Goodnight.

Museveni said: "Our interest remains security, revenue assurance, and ensuring proper accountability within the telecommunications sector so that we clearly know what is happening with telecom companies and who the customers are." The licensing framework provides for a national gateway, a physical point of presence in Uganda, and an operational office with technical and legal staff; all devices activated in-country must be registered. UCC issued the certification to guide Starlink's rollout.

Goodnight said the partnership "will lower internet costs and enable more people to join the digital economy," and that Starlink intends to support education and health by donating connectivity devices. ICT permanent secretary Dr Aminah Zawedde said the provisional license followed extensive engagements to ensure compliance with Uganda's regulatory framework, "safeguarding national interests." (Follows Starlink's January 2026 suspension for operating without a local licence.)
