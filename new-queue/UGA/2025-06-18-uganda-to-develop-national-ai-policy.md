As Uganda prepares to roll out its Fourth National Development Plan (NDP4) in July 2025, the Ministry of ICT and National Guidance announced development of the country's first-ever Artificial Intelligence (AI) policy. Speaking at a two-day digital transformation review retreat at Mestil Hotel, Kampala, on June 17, 2025, permanent secretary Dr Amina Zawedde confirmed the AI policy will be a major priority in the upcoming financial year: "we are developing a national AI policy — not only for government but for the entire country."

The policy aims to establish a regulatory and enabling framework supporting innovation, data governance, cybersecurity and ethics. Zawedde noted the growing data volumes generated by national IDs and smart agriculture demand intelligent, ethical use. It will focus on workforce readiness, research investment and local innovation. She cited milestones under NDP3 (2020–2025): internet expansion to 53 districts, reduced data costs, completion of the ICT National Backbone Infrastructure, and e-government services (passport applications, business registration, digital ID renewals), plus the Parish Development Model MIS and digital tablets for parish chairpersons.
