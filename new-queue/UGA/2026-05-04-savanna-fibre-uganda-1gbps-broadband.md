Savanna Fibre Uganda has launched one gigabit per second (1Gbps) fixed broadband connectivity, described as a key milestone in the country's ICT development. The company said the launch reflects its aim of making high-speed internet available to all segments of Ugandan society, opening opportunities in business services, education, fintech, digital media, remote work, cloud adoption and e-government, and creating a stronger foundation for AI, advanced analytics and automation.

The company, which only began generating revenue two years prior, said it has rapidly expanded to deliver high-speed broadband supporting inclusive economic development and strengthening Uganda's position in the continent's digital economy. The milestone aligns with the Uganda Communications Commission's emphasis on next-generation broadband infrastructure. CEO Alex Wanyoike leads Savanna Fibre Uganda.
