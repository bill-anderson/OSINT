Zain Fintech, the financial services arm of Zain Group (operating in eight Middle East and Africa markets), has launched the 'Bede' Digital Wallet in Sudan. The platform lets Zain customers in Sudan carry out a wide range of financial transactions directly via mobile phones, without a traditional bank account or card, and is designed to work across both basic feature phones and smartphones.

The initial launch phase includes core transactions such as money transfers, airtime top-ups, bill payments, merchant purchases, and cash deposits/withdrawals through a broad network of certified agents across neighbourhoods and markets. The launch ceremony was held in Port Sudan, attended by representatives of the Sovereign Council, federal and state ministries, ambassadors, Zain management, banking partners and industry specialists.

Bede was first deployed in Bahrain. Its rollout in Sudan is planned in three phases: (1) initial service offer; (2) integration with additional banks plus electricity purchases and government transactions; (3) banking services, international remittances, savings/financing products, and full interoperability with all banks operating in Sudan. Bede operates in alignment with Central Bank of Sudan policies regulating digital-wallet activity, with several banks overseeing trust accounts, and complies with national AML, anti-corruption, customer-data-protection and financial-transparency standards.
