Minister of Technology and Science Felix Chipota Mutati announced new measures to accelerate Zambia's digital transformation following the Third Digital Economy Roundtable (DERT III) held at The Quorum in Lusaka. The roundtable brought together officials from the Ministry of Technology and Science, the Presidential Delivery Unit (PDU), ZICTA, and private sector stakeholders.

Among key achievements highlighted was the expansion of the country's fibre-optic network from 15,000 kilometres in 2021 to more than 45,000 kilometres. Mutati said financial inclusion has surpassed 80%, while national connectivity coverage has reached 94%. Addressing network congestion, he announced that government has approved K255 million to upgrade digital infrastructure and improve Quality of Service (QoS), particularly in rural areas. As part of efforts to strengthen accountability, ZICTA will begin publishing quarterly public reports on network performance.
