The Universal Access and Service Fund (UASF) Committee of the Zambia Information and Communications Technology Authority (ZICTA) Board of Directors conducted a field inspection of selected projects in Copperbelt and Northwestern Provinces from August 18 to 22, 2025, part of the Board's 2025 Work Plan to monitor initiatives implemented under the UASF to promote digital inclusion in underserved communities. The delegation was joined by Director General Eng. Collins Mbulo.

The inspection covered four major programmes. Under the Telecommunication Towers Programme, the Committee visited Machiya Tower in Mpongwe District, constructed in 2021 under the 24 Towers Project, providing broadband coverage to over 9,500 people and serving nearby schools, health posts and polling stations; and Lisomona Tower in Masaiti District, completed in 2025 under the 51 Towers Project, delivering 2G, 3G and 4G services within a 15 km radius. Through the Last Mile Optic Fibre Connectivity Programme, the team assessed the connection of the Gemstone Processing and Lapidary Training Centre in Ndola.
