# Somalia approves cybersecurity law to protect digital systems and critical infrastructure

Mogadishu (HOL), Tuesday January 27, 2026

Somalia's lower house of parliament on Saturday approved a new Cybersecurity Law to protect the country's digital infrastructure. The legislation establishes a national framework for managing cybersecurity, assigning policy responsibility to the Ministry of Communications and Technology, a technical oversight role to the National Communications Authority, and legal obligations to operators of critical infrastructure. A central feature is the creation of the Somalia Computer Incident Response Team (SOM-CIRT), which will coordinate national responses to cyberattacks.

Officials described the law as a major advance for a country that has rapidly expanded digital services while remaining vulnerable to cybercrime, data breaches and disruptions. NCA Director General Mustafa Yasin Sheikh said the approval marks "an important step toward building a coordinated national cybersecurity system." State Minister of Communications and Technology Ahmed Osman Dirie called it a critical pillar for safeguarding national security. The law comes as Somalia expands e-government services, mobile banking and digital identification systems.
