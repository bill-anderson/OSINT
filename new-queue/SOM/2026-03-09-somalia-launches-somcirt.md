# Somalia Strengthens Cyber Defenses With Launch of National Incident Response Center (SOMCIRT)

By Isaac K. Kassouwi — We Are Tech Africa, March 9, 2026

The Somali government launched the Somalia Computer Incident Response Team (SOMCIRT) on Saturday, March 7. The center coordinates the prevention, detection and response to cybersecurity incidents across the country, supports incident reporting, and issues cybersecurity alerts and warnings. The initiative follows the Cybersecurity Law approved by parliament in January 2026, which mandated the creation of the response center. (Prime Minister Hamza Abdi Barre officially opened the facility in Mogadishu; the Ministry said 45 officials from various government departments were undergoing the first phase of specialized training.)

The launch strengthens cooperation between government institutions, private-sector actors and international partners to protect Somalia's critical digital infrastructure. It adds to several recent regulatory reforms: a cybercrime bill approved in August 2025, and the Data Protection Law of March 2023 that created the Data Protection Authority. Somalia signed an MoU with Turkey on February 10, 2026 covering digital cooperation including cybersecurity, and has strengthened ties with Malaysia and UNODC. The ITU ranked Somalia Tier 4 of 5 in the Global Cybersecurity Index 2024 (37.39/100).
