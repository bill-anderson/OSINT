# Somalia launches national effort to build cybersecurity framework

Mogadishu (HOL), Saturday April 25, 2026

The National Communications Authority (NCA) convened a national consultation to develop Somalia's Cybersecurity Risk Management and Compliance Framework, bringing together government institutions, private-sector stakeholders, the education sector, civil society and technical experts. The consultation aimed to gather input and strengthen collaboration on a comprehensive framework to enhance the country's cybersecurity posture, safeguard critical digital infrastructure and ensure effective risk management and compliance across sectors.

NCA Director General Mustafa Yasin Sheikh urged stakeholders to review and provide input on the draft document. State Minister of Communications and Technology MP Ahmed Osman Dirie, who inaugurated the meeting, said establishing a robust cybersecurity risk management and compliance framework is essential, with the NCA mandated to lead the effort. Participants discussed risk identification, regulatory compliance mechanisms and institutional responsibilities. The outcomes will inform finalization of the Framework, intended as a key instrument in strengthening national cybersecurity governance — building on the Cybersecurity Law approved in January 2026 and the March 2026 launch of SOMCIRT.
