# Hassan Sheikh says 10 million Somali passports issued, vows overhaul of ID system

MOGADISHU (HOL), Friday September 19, 2025

President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud said more than 10 million people currently hold Somali passports — though the government does not know how many were issued legally — as his administration moves to overhaul the identification system. He ordered all government institutions and private businesses to link operations to the biometric national ID card issued by NIRA, which will replace the Somali passport as the primary form of identification. "Any government agency or business that bypasses the national ID is committing a national crime," he said, warning that Somalis who fail to obtain a NIRA card would lose access to public services.

So far about half a million Somalis have registered for the biometric card, against a goal of at least 20 million; the government targets 15 million biometric IDs by the 2026 elections, tied to IMF/World Bank debt relief and governance reforms. The NIRA system, branded eAqoonsi and Hubiye, is a donor-supported biometric platform required for passports, domestic travel, banking and driver's licenses. Questions remain about privacy and ownership of the biometric data; critics draw parallels to India's Aadhaar debates.

Regional opposition remains a hurdle: Puntland and Jubbaland have refused to implement the federal ID system, with Puntland creating its own identification authority and accusing Mogadishu of political overreach over data control, election influence and regional autonomy.
