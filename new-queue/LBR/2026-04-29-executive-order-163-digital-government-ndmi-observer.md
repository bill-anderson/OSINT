# Boakai's Executive Order 163 Signals Digital Government Shift

Liberian Observer, April 29, 2026

President Joseph Nyuma Boakai has signed Executive Order No. 163, setting in motion a government-wide digital reform agenda that establishes a new National Digitalization and Modernization Initiative (NDMI) and creates the Office of Technology, Digitalization and Innovation (OTDI) within the Office of the President. The order, signed on April 22, 2026, outlines a "whole-of-government" approach to modernizing public service delivery through secure, interoperable, and standards-based digital systems across ministries, agencies, and commissions (MACs).

At the center of the framework is the OTDI, which will serve as the Executive Branch's coordinating authority for digital governance — developing enterprise-wide technical standards, ensuring interoperability, and providing technical clearance for major ICT investments that affect cross-government operations, sensitive personal data, and core registries. The order mandates consolidation and reuse of shared digital capabilities, a comprehensive inventory of government digital systems, and introduces a Government Enterprise Architecture Framework and a Government Interoperability Framework.

A key feature is its emphasis on cybersecurity and data protection: OTDI is mandated to establish baseline cybersecurity requirements for all government digital systems, including identity and access management controls, data logging, backup protocols, vulnerability management, and incident response readiness, coordinated with the Ministries of Posts and Telecommunications and Justice. All data-sharing and interoperability initiatives must comply with constitutional privacy protections, including Article 16 of the Constitution, and be governed by formal agreements defining access controls, data usage, and accountability.

A Digital Government Steering Committee, chaired by the Minister of State for Presidential Affairs, will oversee implementation, with OTDI as technical secretariat submitting quarterly reports. Ministries must obtain "Technical Clearance" from OTDI before digital investments that impact interoperability, sensitive data, or financial management. The order does not diminish the statutory independence of autonomous institutions such as the Central Bank of Liberia and the Liberia Revenue Authority, and ICT procurement remains subject to the Public Procurement and Concessions Act. Within 60 days, MACs must submit an inventory of digital systems; within 180 days, OTDI must present a prioritized roadmap; and draft legislation to institutionalize the reforms is to be submitted within 12 months. Tech-sector figures — including Kpetermeni Siakor (Kwagei Group), Dwalu Khasu, Sekou Keita (Liberia Technology Summit), and Vivien Jones (Mwetana Consulting) — welcomed the order but cautioned that success depends on execution, leadership, and meaningful inclusion of Liberian expertise.
