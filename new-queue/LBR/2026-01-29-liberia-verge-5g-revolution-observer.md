# Liberia on the Verge of 5G Revolution

By Ishmael F. Menkor — Liberian Observer, January 29, 2026

The Liberia Telecommunication Authority (LTA) is moving to modernize the nation's telecommunications sector, with plans to introduce 5G network services across the country, LTA Chairman Clarence Massaquoi announced during an exclusive interview at the recent Infrastructure Conference in Ganta. Massaquoi emphasized that the government is committed to ensuring every Liberian has access to affordable and reliable telecommunication services.

"The time is here for every Liberian to have an affordable and adequate telecommunication network, and the government is ready to provide the service," Massaquoi said.

To achieve this, the LTA has begun upgrading digital infrastructure. A first phase of the fiber-optic cable has already landed, and a second is expected to land in Buchanan. "As a country, we have one submarine cable already in Monrovia, and we are looking for another cable landing in Buchanan. That is going to cost a lot of money, but the government has sourced some funding with help from partners — and we need investors to complete the fundraising aspects," he explained.

He said private firms are helping to extend the fiber-optic network from Buchanan to Rivercess, Sinoe, Maryland, Grand Gedeh, and Nimba counties. The line had already reached Loguatuo from Monrovia, but ongoing work aims to interconnect all regions to ensure universal access. "Modernizing and digitizing the telecommunication sector is a key priority in the rebranding and rebuilding of Liberia," he added.

The introduction of 5G technology is expected to offer faster internet speeds, improved connectivity, and more robust digital services. Massaquoi said the government is actively engaging investors and partners to ensure the project is fully funded and sustainable. "Our vision is clear: no Liberian should be left without telecommunication access. This is not just a policy; it is a commitment to every citizen," Massaquoi said.
