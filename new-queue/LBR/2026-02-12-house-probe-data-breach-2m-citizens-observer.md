# House to Probe Data Breach, $6.1M Foya Project

By Leroy M. Sonpon — Liberian Observer, February 12, 2026

(In-scope portion: the alleged data breach. The article also covers an unrelated Mano River Union Foya construction-financing probe, omitted here.)

The House of Representatives has launched an investigation into an alleged major cybersecurity breach reportedly affecting three key public institutions — the Liberia Revenue Authority (LRA), the Liberia Electricity Corporation (LEC), and the National Investment Commission (NIC).

According to a communication from Representative Taa Z. Wongbe of Nimba County District #9, sensitive personal data belonging to approximately two million citizens and residents of Liberia was allegedly accessed, stolen, and is being sold on the dark web. The reported breach has raised serious concerns about taxpayer privacy, investor confidence, and national security. However, the Liberia Revenue Authority has denied any breach, maintaining that its systems remain secure and that taxpayer data is protected.

In his communication, Rep. Wongbe called for transparency and accountability, stressing the urgent need for a coherent national cybersecurity strategy to address emerging threats. Following the reading of the communication during Tuesday's session, lawmakers unanimously voted to mandate the House Leadership to conduct a robust investigation and report its findings to the full Plenary.

The development comes amid ongoing government efforts to strengthen cybersecurity, including the passage of the Cybercrime Act of 2025, which aims to protect citizens' rights in cyberspace and establish a comprehensive framework for addressing cyber threats.
