Orange Côte d'Ivoire has launched Orange Sat, a satellite broadband service developed in partnership with Eutelsat, to extend internet connectivity to rural "white zones" that currently have no access to 4G or fiber networks.

The service offers a comprehensive solution for diverse users, including an alternative fiber option for agro-industrial enterprises and sensitive urban customers, providing unlimited high-speed data at up to 50 Mbps, as well as a prepaid 4G home broadband service, branded as 'Wi-Fi village,' which allows rural low-income users to access flexible data passes tailored to their needs.

Using Eutelsat's KONNECT satellite platform, the service extends high-speed internet access beyond the reach of fiber and mobile networks, including to rural and hard-to-access areas.

Orange Sat is part of Orange Côte d'Ivoire's broader commitment to digital inclusion, ensuring that all communities, regardless of location or economic situation, can benefit from reliable, high-performance internet connectivity.

Commenting on the launch, Philippe Baudrier, Vice President Connectivity, Africa at Eutelsat, said:

There is no clearer example of how satellite can help to bridge the digital divide than the work we are doing alongside our partners across the vast continent of Africa. By working with Orange Côte d'Ivoire, KONNECT is helping to improve national infrastructure and extend reliable internet access to communities that have historically been difficult to serve, supporting inclusion, resilience, and long-term digital development.

By bridging the gap in underserved areas, the initiative not only supports education, business, and social engagement but also reflects Orange's mission to provide solutions that match the geographical and economic realities of the country, enabling more Ivorians to participate in the digital economy.
