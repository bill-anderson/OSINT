# Starlink beams into Central African Republic

*SpaceX's satellite Internet service, Starlink, has launched in the Central African Republic.*

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa
March 17, 2026 · 3 Min Read

Starlink, the satellite Internet service operated by Elon Musk's SpaceX, has launched in the Central African Republic (CAR).

"Starlink's high-speed, low-latency Internet is now available in the Central African Republic," the company confirmed in a post on X, and the country is now showing as "available" on Starlink's connectivity map.

Starlink's launch follows the government's approval of its operating license in December 2025, marking a step in the country's journey toward bridging the digital divide.

The availability of the service is expected to support local businesses by enabling better communication and access to online markets.

Statistics from DataReportal indicate that there were 670,000 Internet users in the CAR at the end of 2025, with an Internet penetration rate of just 12%.

Kepios analysis indicates that the number of Internet users in the country increased by 51,000, or 8.3%, between October 2024 and October 2025. The CAR has a population of 5.56 million.

The data indicates that 4.89 million people in the CAR were not using the Internet at the end of 2025, leaving 88% of the population offline.

Starlink's entry into the country provides a new connectivity option for those who don't currently have Internet access.

## Starlink tariffs

According to the Starlink website, Central African users will have to pay 33,000 Central African francs (US$58) per month for a residential package.

Customers will also have to pay XAF240,000 ($420) for a standard Starlink kit and XAF123,000 ($215) for a Starlink Mini Kit.

Starlink's launch in the CAR follows a launch in Senegal in February 2026.

Starlink also offers a 30-day trial with no contracts or data caps and estimates shipping times to be two weeks in the country.

The company promises download speeds of up to 305 Mbit/s and upload speeds of up to 40 Mbit/s.

## Starlink's African movements

The satellite Internet service has been making moves on the African continent, especially since the beginning of this year.

The company has gone from pushing for a South African license through an advocacy campaign, to partnering with MTN Zambia to offer direct-to-cell services, to launching its services in Senegal, all in the first three months of 2026.

Since launching in Africa's most populous country Nigeria, in January 2023, the company has made huge inroads on the continent.

Starlink launched services in Somalia, in August 2025, three months after the National Communications Authority (NCA) granted it an operating license.

It launched in Chad in July 2025 and in the mountain kingdom of Lesotho in June 2025.

Starlink's entry into Lesotho followed other launches in the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) and Guinea-Bissau.

The company is now fully up and running in Lesotho, Botswana, Ghana, DRC, Zimbabwe, Zambia, Malawi, Mozambique, Eswatini, Madagascar, Nigeria, Niger, Liberia, Benin, Guinea-Bissau, Sierra Leone, Somalia, Senegal, Chad, Cape Verde, Kenya, South Sudan, Rwanda, Burundi, São Tomé and Príncipe.
