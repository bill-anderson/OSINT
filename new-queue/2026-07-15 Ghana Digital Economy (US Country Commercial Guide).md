US Country Commercial Guide — Ghana Digital Economy (last published 2024-09-20).

Ghana's Ministry of Communications and Digitalization (MoCD) directs communications-sector policy. The 2003 ICT for Accelerated Development (ICT4AD) Policy, the National Financial Inclusion and Development Strategy, and the National Cyber Security Policy and Strategy are key facets. The Ghana CARES ('Obaatan Pa') programme's phase two focuses on digital policies; government priorities include the National ID, digital address systems, land-records digitization, Ghana.gov, digitizing fiscal revenue collection, a cashless society, the national fibre backbone, and digital literacy.

Key agencies under MoCD: NITA (ICT security/standards), NCA (communications regulator/licensing), GIFEC (ICT access for underserved areas), Cyber Security Authority (CSA), and the Data Protection Commission (DPC). The Bank of Ghana oversees payment-system infrastructure, mobile money, interoperability, the National Switching and Processing System, payment sandboxes, and operates GhIPSS. The 2008 Electronic Transactions Act is the e-commerce/e-signature baseline; a draft Startup Law is under development.

Growth: Ghana ranks 15/47 African countries on the 2024 ICT Development Index; >135% mobile penetration; home to the only Tier IV data centre in West Africa plus several Tier III. ICT subsector values (analyst estimates): digital infrastructure $400m, software $200m, cloud $15m, cybersecurity $30m, fintech/health-tech/ed-tech $115m, training/services $150m. 100+ tech hubs (Accra/Tema, Kumasi, Takoradi).
