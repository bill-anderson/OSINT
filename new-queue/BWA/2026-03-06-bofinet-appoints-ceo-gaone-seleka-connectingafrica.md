# Botswana's BoFiNet appoints new CEO

By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa — March 6, 2026

Botswana Fibre Networks (BoFiNet) has appointed Dr. Gaone Seleka as its new CEO, taking over from Keabetswe Segole.

BoFiNet said Seleka is a seasoned telecoms and digital transformation leader with extensive experience spanning technical, executive and strategic roles across multiple sectors, including telecommunications, banking, mining and academia.

Seleka holds a Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Business Administration with a focus on fintech, a Master of Science (MSc) in Projects and Quality Management, an MSc in Computer Information Systems, and a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology/Computer Information Systems.

"I am deeply honored to step into the role of CEO at BoFiNet, an organization that carries such a strategic national mandate pivotal to Botswana's digital transformation," Seleka told Connecting Africa in an interview about his appointment.

"I look forward to working closely with the board, the executive team and the staff to drive innovation, expand digital infrastructure and deliver meaningful impact for Botswana," he added.

Seleka said his focus will be on shaping the next chapter of BoFiNet's growth following the introduction of a new carrier-neutral data center in November 2025. The Digital Delta Data Centre is located in the country's capital, Gaborone, and is run by BoFiNet, which is 100% owned by the government of Botswana.

BoFiNet Board Chairman Molimisi Sechaba said Seleka's appointment signals the board's commitment to positioning BoFiNet as a leader in the region's digital infrastructure landscape and accelerating Botswana's digital transformation journey.

"Dr. Seleka's expertise and vision will be instrumental as we build on our past achievements and chart an ambitious course for the future," Sechaba explained.

"We are confident that his leadership will ensure that the organization continues to deliver meaningful impact for Botswana while expanding its role as a regional partner in connectivity and innovation," Sechaba added.

## BoFiNet's recent projects

BoFiNet recently signed a memorandum of understanding with telecoms operator MTC Namibia aimed at strengthening cross-border fiber connectivity, enhancing network redundancy and improving regional Internet reliability between Botswana and Namibia.

The MoU followed the two companies completing cross-border fiber interconnections at Mamuno and Ngoma to unlock technical and commercial opportunities.

MTC Namibia's chief commercial officer, Octivius Kahiya, said the MoU is a launchpad for stronger regional digital cooperation.
