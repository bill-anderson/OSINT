# How Egypt's Fawry built a $1 billion fintech

Fawry, launched in 2008 as an e-payment switch, is now Egypt's largest mobile-money platform, processing about six million transactions daily and serving nearly half of Egypt's population. By 2025 revenue climbed to EGP 8.65 billion ($166.7m), net revenue EGP 3.1bn, and take-home profit EGP 2.89bn; market capitalisation stands at EGP 67.72 billion ($1.3bn), up 1,029% since its 2019 EGX listing. It is Egypt's second-largest listed fintech after state-owned e-finance.

Fawry has expanded via microfinance/consumer lending, securitisation (an EGP 8bn three-year programme), and equity stakes across logistics/e-commerce (Bosta, Waffarha, Roaderz), consolidating under Fawry Holding for Financial Investments (Nov 2025, FRA-approved).
