The Ministry of Gender, Labour and Social Development unveiled a new "Labour, Employment, Productivity and Reporting Programme" aimed at strengthening Uganda's labour market governance and evidence-based decision-making. The goal is to establish an integrated, reliable and timely administrative data ecosystem for labour, employment and productivity reporting, compliant with the Employment Act (Cap 226), national statistical requirements and ILO conventions.

Permanent secretary Aggrey David Kibenge (remarks delivered by Commissioner Alex Asiimwe) said Uganda's labour market governance faces persistent gaps in the generation, quality and use of administrative data. He cited that while the UBOS 2024 Census and Labour Force Survey 2021 provide nationally representative statistics (12.3% unemployment, 42% labour underutilisation, 88% informality), administrative data from labour inspections, employment records, enterprise productivity assessments and social security contributions remain fragmented, incomplete and inconsistently reported. The four-day consultative workshop (Admas Grand Hotel, Entebbe, June 15, 2026) drew employers', workers' and government representatives.
