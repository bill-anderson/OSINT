The government directed all mobile network operators (MNOs) and internet service providers (ISPs) to temporarily suspend public internet access and selected mobile services during the election period, citing national security. In a directive issued January 13, 2026, and signed by UCC Executive Director Nyombi Thembo, the suspension took effect at 6pm Tuesday and remained in force until further notice, following a "strong recommendation" from the Inter-Agency Security Committee to mitigate misinformation, prevent electoral fraud and curb incitement.

Operators were required to suspend public internet access, halt the sale and registration of new SIM cards, and disable outbound data roaming to One Network Area countries. The suspension applied across mobile broadband, fibre, leased lines, fixed wireless, microwave and satellite internet. All non-essential traffic (social media, web browsing, streaming, personal email, messaging) was blocked.

A strictly controlled exclusion list allowed continued access (via non-mobile connections, whitelisted IP ranges/VPNs/private circuits) for authorised personnel only: national referral hospital healthcare systems; core financial/payment infrastructure (banking networks, interbank clearing, ATMs, URA tax platforms, government payment gateways); critical government systems (immigration, Electoral Commission secure portals, voter verification, results tabulation); utilities; transport/aviation control; and network-management/cybersecurity tools. Mobile VPNs had to be disabled across all networks; operators were barred from extending the exclusion list or allowing public bypass, and had to submit whitelists, run 24-hour incident response teams, and keep traffic logs.
