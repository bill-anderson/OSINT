Bayobab Uganda (an MTN Group subsidiary) has launched a new fibre optic route along the railway line from Kampala to Malaba to enhance cross-border connectivity between Uganda and Kenya. The 260-kilometre route, extending to Malaba at the Kenyan border, is the shortest and fastest fibre link connecting Uganda's capital to Bayobab's subsea cable landing stations in Mombasa. Developed between December 2024 and February 2025, it completes East Africa's digital backbone from Kampala to Mombasa.

Speaking in Kampala on May 6, 2025, Juliet Nsubuga, MD of Bayobab Uganda, said the project was driven by the need for regional technological transformation. "In collaboration with the Uganda Railway, we leveraged the existing rail network to deploy fibre... connecting key routes between Kampala and Malaba at the Kenyan border — and beyond." The route caters to international and national technology players, telecoms and ISPs, supporting over 1 Tbit/s capacity and connecting major data centres in Kampala.
