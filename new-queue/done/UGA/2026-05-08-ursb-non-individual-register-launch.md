A centralised digital system designed to formalise all non-individual entities and eliminate bureaucratic bottlenecks in government service delivery has been rolled out. The Non-Individual Register (NIR) was unveiled by the Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB) during a stakeholders' engagement at the Source of the Nile Hotel in Jinja city on May 7, 2026, drawing local government leaders, private and civil society actors, and the business community from across Busoga region.

Representing URSB registrar general Mercy Kainobwisho, commissioner for finance and administration Ambrose Ekaju said the NIR establishes a single source of truth for every non-individual entity operating in Uganda — companies, partnerships, NGOs, co-operatives and community-based organisations. Under the new system, each entity is assigned a Unique Entity Registration Number (ERN) acting as a universal digital identity. (Context: in 2025 the government amended the Tax Procedures Code Act to provide for issuance of an Entity Registration Number by URSB after admission onto the NIR; the register is also intended as shared digital infrastructure for all MDAs, supporting entity authentication and verification.)
