Cybersecurity has become a defining issue for Uganda's financial sector as banks, insurers, fintechs and mobile money operators — handling large volumes of confidential data and money flows — remain attractive targets. The piece marks Ugandan cybersecurity firm Milima Security securing ISO/IEC 27001 certification; its CEO and founder Emmanuel Chagara pointed to skills shortages as one of the biggest challenges, while noting notable progress over the last five to six years and that COVID-19 accelerated digitisation (and cyberattacks).

Uganda-specific data: per the Uganda Police Crime Report 2024, 446 cybercrime cases were processed in 2024 with losses of Shs 72.1 billion (~USD 20 million), down slightly from 523 cases in 2023, with a backlog of 887 unresolved cases. Threats are especially worrying for "last mile" financial-inclusion users reliant on mobile money. IT expert Richard Irumba said government is focusing on shared infrastructure, governance and accountability for a coordinated response. Globally, cybercrime damages were ~USD 8 trillion in 2023 (projected USD 10.5 trillion by 2025); the average financial-sector data breach cost rose to USD 6.08 million.
