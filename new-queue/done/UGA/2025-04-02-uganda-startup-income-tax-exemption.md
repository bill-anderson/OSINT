Ugandan startups may soon receive a three-year income tax exemption aimed at accelerating the startup ecosystem. The measure, part of proposed amendments to the 2025 Income Tax Bill, seeks to spur entrepreneurship, support SMEs and bolster innovation. The government has previously launched the National ICT Initiatives Support Program and established the National ICT Innovation Hub (providing internet and workspace for tech entrepreneurs).

Uganda ranks third in East Africa and 95th globally (StartupBlink 2024 Global Startup Ecosystem Index); Kampala holds 368th of 1,000 cities and hosts startups such as Tugende, SafeBoda, Numida and Rocket Health. StartupBlink recommended diversifying beyond Kampala-centricity and stronger stakeholder collaboration. National ecosystem investment totalled $10.6 million in 2023, a 60.4% drop from $26.8 million in 2022.
