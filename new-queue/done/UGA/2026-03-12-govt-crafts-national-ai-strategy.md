The government is developing a comprehensive strategy to harness AI, focusing on strengthening digital infrastructure, building local skills and establishing policies for ethical use. At a stakeholder engagement on the AI strategy at Four Points By Sheraton, Kampala, on March 12, 2026, Ministry of ICT permanent secretary Dr Aminah Zawedde said AI "is one of the technologies that will define the future of economies across the world," with government ensuring Uganda harnesses benefits while putting safeguards in place.

Ambrose Ruyooka, head of the AI Secretariat at the ministry, said Uganda is laying the groundwork for a national AI ecosystem: "We are developing a national AI strategy that will guide how the country adopts and utilises this technology," aimed at economic growth, job creation and improved public services. Government institutions are exploring AI applications in agriculture (weather/soil analysis), healthcare (diagnostics), education and public administration. Uganda has undertaken readiness assessments and engaged international partners on governance structures protecting citizens' rights and privacy.
