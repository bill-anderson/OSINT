The National Information Technology Authority-Uganda (NITA-U) has launched its Strategic Plan for FY 2025/26 to 2029/30, a five-year agenda anchored in Vision 2040, the Fourth National Development Plan (NDP IV) and the National Digital Transformation Programme. The overarching goal is to grow the proportion of Ugandans actively using e-government services from a baseline of 9.2 percent to 40 percent by FY 2029/30, delivered through six objectives spanning ICT infrastructure, smart e-government access, cybersecurity and data protection, BPO/IT-enabled services, regulatory compliance, and institutional performance.

Specific targets: NITA-U's UGHub data integration platform, connecting 37% of MDAs, is set to reach 73% by FY 2029/30; National Data Centre utilisation to rise from 70% to 83%; public satisfaction with e-government services from 22.2% to 35%; and compliance with national IT laws from 67% to 81%. Officials framed the shift from measuring "infrastructure built" to measuring actual citizen utilisation. Two supporting documents — a Client Charter and Service Delivery Standards — were presented, with quarterly progress reporting promised.
