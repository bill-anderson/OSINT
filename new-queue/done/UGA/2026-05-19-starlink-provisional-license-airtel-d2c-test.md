Starlink has been granted a provisional license by the Uganda Communications Commission (UCC), while Airtel Uganda has begun testing Starlink's direct-to-cell (D2C) LEO satellite service. Ugandan President Museveni said on X that Starlink and UCC signed an MoU and operational license agreement — "an important step towards the commencement of their operations in Uganda." Uganda's interest: security, revenue assurance and accountability.

License conditions require Starlink to establish a national gateway with a physical point of presence, register all devices activated in-country, and maintain a staffed local office. ICT PS Dr Aminah Zawedde said the license followed engagements to ensure regulatory compliance; Starlink's Ryan Goodnight said the company will support education and health by donating connectivity devices.

On D2C: Airtel Uganda MD/CEO Soumendra Sahu said via LinkedIn the telco had entered the testing phase for D2C satellite services through its Starlink partnership — "testing a highly advanced technology that enhances smartphone network connectivity through Direct-to-Cell satellite services." This forms part of Airtel Africa's December 2025 agreement with SpaceX to roll out D2C across all 14 of its markets by end of 2026.
