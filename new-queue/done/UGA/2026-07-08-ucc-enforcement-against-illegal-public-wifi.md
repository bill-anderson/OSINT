The Uganda Communications Commission (UCC) has announced a nationwide enforcement campaign against illegal public Wi-Fi service providers to protect consumers, safeguard critical communications infrastructure, and ensure compliance with telecommunications regulations. In a public notice issued Tuesday, July 7, 2026, the Commission said the move comes amid rapid growth in internet access, which has driven demand for affordable connectivity and encouraged new internet service businesses.

Investigations revealed that unlicensed operators have been installing telecommunications equipment to provide unauthorised public Wi-Fi hotspot services, in violation of the Uganda Communications Act. UCC warned that illegal installations expose consumers to unreliable services, data privacy breaches, cybersecurity threats and limited complaint-resolution avenues, while compromising critical infrastructure. Licensed ISPs must meet strict quality standards, protect customer information and comply with cybersecurity regulations. UCC urged unlicensed providers to regularise or cease operations, and consumers to use only licensed providers (verifiable on the UCC website), with reports via toll-free hotline 0800 222 777.
