The Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) has launched its annual tax return filing season and unveiled a landmark reform: taxpayers will transition from the Tax Identification Number (TIN) to the National Identification Number (NIN). Under the #LearnTaxUg campaign, URA announced: "Soon and very soon, your NIN will be your TIN." The integration will allow Ugandans to use a single identification number for payments, registrations and license applications across government services.

The move, enacted under the Tax Procedures Amendment Bill, is part of the government's digital transformation agenda aimed at unifying public services, increasing efficiency, and curbing tax evasion. Citizens' NINs, issued by NIRA, will serve as their official tax identity; businesses registered with the Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB) will use their URSB registration numbers as TINs. Officials said the reform will eliminate duplicate records and allow seamless access to payments, licenses and registrations through a single digital channel.

URA also confirmed a new incentive: businesses registered after July 1, 2025, with capital not exceeding Shs 500 million will enjoy a three-year income tax exemption. The TIN-NIN merger is described as one of the most significant public administration reforms in recent history, a key component of Uganda's Vision 2040 push for a fully digital economy and a wider tax base.
