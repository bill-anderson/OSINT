Dennis Habu, finance manager at Vuka Africa Ltd, appeared before Grade One Magistrate Geoffrey Akena at Makindye Standards and Utilities Court over allegations of unlawfully obtaining and disclosing a client's personal data. The case, brought by PDPO director Baker Birikujja, follows an April 2025 incident: Vuka Africa (a Ntinda, Kampala courier firm contracted by Jumia to deliver an item) allegedly circulated client Aloyo Nyeko Mega's personal details among delivery agents without authorisation, violating Sections 35 and 36 of the Data Protection and Privacy Act. Vuka Africa was also operating as a data collector/processor without PDPO registration (Section 5). On December 3, 2025, the accused pleaded guilty to failing to register; the court imposed a lenient fine of five currency points (sh100,000) or two months' imprisonment in default.

The case comes amid a surge in cybercrime. NITA-U, with PDPO, launched a six-month nationwide cybersecurity and data-protection campaign, "Beera Ku Guard" ("stay guarded"), aiming to reach 70% of Ugandans and register at least 1,000 data-controlling organisations with the PDPO by 2026. NITA-U information security director Arnold Mangeni said digital transformation must be built on trust. Over 13 million Ugandans are now active online, but only 13.6% grasp data protection principles (48.8% have some cybersecurity understanding). Per the Uganda Police Crime Report 2024, cybercrime cases rose 93.5% in 2024 (474 incidents, sh72.1 billion losses, only sh420 million recovered). Government efforts are anchored in the National Cybersecurity Strategy (2022–2026).
