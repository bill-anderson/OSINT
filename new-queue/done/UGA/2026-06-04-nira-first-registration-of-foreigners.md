NIRA will begin the registration of all legally resident foreign nationals living in Uganda on June 10, 2026 — the first time it is officially documenting foreigners with a view to issuing registration cards. In a statement on June 3, NIRA said the move is in accordance with the Registration of Persons Act, CAP 332.

Registrar Clare Omalla said: "The Auditor General has on several occasions raised concern regarding this matter, and as NIRA, we had never officially registered them... We have been using the Immigration statistics where each foreigner is issued a permit, but now, we are going to issue them with cards." The Uganda Citizenship and Immigration Control Act (CAP 313) provides for compulsory registration of all alien residents; an alien is defined as a non-citizen except those visiting for a period not exceeding 90 days.

Applicants must pay a $100 (sh376,873) registration fee and present a valid passport/travel document, a valid Ugandan visa, and a valid immigration facility (work permit, student/pupil's pass, dependence pass, certificate of residence, special pass, etc.). Upon registration, individuals receive a unique identification number (Section 72) and an alien identification card (Section 73), valid for the period of their immigration facility. Registration is conducted at Plot 62 Lumumba Avenue (NIRA offices) and the Ministry of Internal Affairs work permit section.

Uganda hosts roughly 2 million registered foreigners, the overwhelming majority (~1.95 million) being refugees and asylum seekers (South Sudan, DRC, Somalia, Burundi, Sudan) registered by the Office of the Prime Minister and tracked by UNHCR. Conventional foreign residents (expatriates, students, investors, workers) number around 120,000–150,000.
