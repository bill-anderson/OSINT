MTN Uganda has called for stronger collaboration and increased adoption of home-grown innovations, arguing the biggest barrier to innovation in Uganda is not a lack of talent but the inability to scale promising solutions — many fail to move beyond pilot stage due to limited infrastructure, investment, partnerships and market adoption. The discussion took place at the Refactory Academy Digital Solutions Expo 2026 at the National ICT Innovation Hub in Nakawa.

Jemima Kariuki-Njuguna, Chief Product Officer at MoMo from MTN Uganda, said the key question is no longer whether Uganda can innovate but whether it can scale innovations for broader impact, calling for collaboration over competition and a strong ecosystem of businesses, startups, investors, government, academia and developers. MTN reaffirmed commitment to resilient digital infrastructure, expanded connectivity and opening MoMo platforms to developers.
