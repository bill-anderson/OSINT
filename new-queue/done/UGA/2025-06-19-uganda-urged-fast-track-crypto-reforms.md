Uganda has been urged to fast-track regulatory reforms for cryptocurrency, with experts warning that delays could cost economic opportunities. At a June 11, 2025 media briefing in Nairobi, Binance Africa legal counsel Larry Cooke said interest in digital assets is growing among Ugandan investors but the country's regulatory stance remains ambiguous and restrictive: "Uganda stands at a crossroads," while neighbouring Kenya forges ahead with a comprehensive framework.

Cryptocurrencies are not legal tender in Uganda; the Bank of Uganda has repeatedly warned of risks and licenses no crypto services. A 2023 High Court ruling (Justice Ssekaana Musa) declared cryptocurrencies illegal absent BOU recognition, barring institutions under the National Payment Systems Act 2020 from converting crypto to mobile money. Crypto trading continues informally via peer-to-peer mobile money and bank transfers. A private member's bill is under consideration by the Capital Markets Authority to regulate crypto as virtual assets. In January 2025, planning state minister Amos Lugoloobi revealed over 2.5 trillion shillings (~$700 million) in crypto transactions occurred in Uganda, underscoring the unregulated sector's size.
