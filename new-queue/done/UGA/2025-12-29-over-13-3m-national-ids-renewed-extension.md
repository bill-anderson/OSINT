NIRA says it has renewed over 13.3 million national identity cards and registered over 6.1 million first-time applicants in the ongoing nationwide mass enrolment and renewal exercise. Speaking at the joint security weekly media briefing at Police headquarters in Naguru, Kampala, on December 29, 2025, registrar Claire Ollama said the exercise, which commenced on April 1, 2025, was initially scheduled for six months but extended due to overwhelming demand. "The six months ended on November 8, 2025, but because of the demand... the board and management of NIRA, together with the Ministry of Internal Affairs, found it plausible to extend this project for a period of three months." The new deadline is February 8, 2026.

Figures given: 6,152,753 new registrations (first-time enrollees); 13,365,388 national IDs renewed; 636,942 first-time national IDs produced and available for collection; 32,351 changes of particulars processed; and 21,463,874 records migrated from the old identification system to the new one.

With schools closed for holidays, Ollama urged parents to register children (NIRA offices open Dec 29-31). A physical parent is not mandatory — "A blood relative is legally accepted." Change of particulars for children below 18 is free; Form 7 (on the NIRA website) is required. The commonly cited shillings 200,000 fee applies only to adult cardholders under specific circumstances, not children. Compliance with the exercise is a legal requirement.
