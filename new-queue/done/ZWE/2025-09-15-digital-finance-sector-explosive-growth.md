Zimbabwe is experiencing a digital finance boom. The RBZ reported that digital retail transactions climbed to $5.93 billion in Q2 2025, a 34% increase from $4.4 billion in Q1 2025. Wholesale digital transactions also rose 15% over the same period. Analysts attribute the growth to government-backed reforms, technological innovation and structural economic challenges.

Government initiatives were central: in Q2 alone, authorities deployed 152,000 point-of-sale (POS) machines. The rollout of real-time gross settlement (RTGS), electronic funds transfers (EFTs) and other payment technologies expanded access, while the national payment switch ZimSwitch improved interoperability. The gold-backed ZiG currency further boosted transaction volumes despite IMF criticism, and mobile money continues to thrive amid high mobile penetration and cash shortages.

Authorities are pushing for greater formalization, stepping up enforcement against unregulated forex dealers. The article situates the surge within a wider African trend (e.g. Tanzania's instant payment system processing $11.7 billion in H1 2025), noting projections that Africa's digital payments market could exceed $1.5 trillion by 2025.
