NewZimbabwe.com interviews Vengai Madzima, Senior Partner at Madzima Chidyausiku Museta Legal Practitioners (MCM Legal), on the Title Deed Validation and Reissuance Pilot Programme. Per a notice published Sunday 4 April 2026, MCM Legal was selected as one of 16 law firms on the pilot programme intended to test and refine the operational processes of the Digital Land Administration Platform (DLAP).

The Ministry of Justice, Legal and Parliamentary Affairs ran a two-week pilot programme to allow adjustments needed for an efficient and secure national deeds registry system on full implementation. The programme is part of the Deeds Registries Regulations of 2025, specifically Statutory Instrument 76 of 2025, described by Madzima as "a watershed law on property securitization in Zimbabwe," repealing the 2018 Regulations (S.I. 236 of 2018).

SI 76 of 2025 requires the holder of a paper-based title deed (issued before digitization) to submit original copies through conveyancers to the Deeds Registry for validation within 24 months from the publication date of the Statutory Instrument. The deadline for digitization of title deeds therefore falls on or around 18 July 2027. A parallel company re-registration deadline is set for 20 April 2026; failure to re-register would allow third parties to open new companies using the abandoned name, with consequences for title deeds held by those companies.

The regulations increase identification requirements for parties transacting, extending them to companies, trusts and other juristic persons; prescribe stricter rules for powers of attorney (now requiring an attestation clause signed by a Notary Public); and require applications for replacement of deeds to be accompanied by fingerprints and notarized copies of identification, as anti-fraud measures against identity theft and fraudulent property sales.
