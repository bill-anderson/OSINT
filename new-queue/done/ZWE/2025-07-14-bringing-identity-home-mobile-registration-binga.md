For the people of the Tshunga-lunga community in Binga, accessing essential documents was a daunting task, often requiring long journeys fraught with danger. A recent mobile registration exercise, conducted by the Civil Registry Department in partnership with UNICEF and funded by the government of Sweden, has brought new hope to this hard-to-reach area.

For Sophia Mtare, a widow raising seven children, the path to getting her children's birth certificates was a gruelling one, requiring her to walk for kilometres and stay overnight in a remote location to obtain cheaper transport. "Today I am happy that the civil registrar has brought mobile registration closer to my home, and today I collected birth certificates and IDs for my two children who had left without these documents," she said.

The mobile registration initiative aimed to provide vital documents such as IDs, birth certificates and death certificates to thousands of villagers who had previously been excluded from formal identification. Zimbabwe's birth registration coverage stands at 57%, as per the Zimbabwe Demographic and Health Survey 2023-24. The programme is expected to significantly increase this by providing services directly to marginalised communities.

Lukasi Sachurusi (37), who had lived for a year without an ID after it was burnt in a fire that destroyed 28 houses in his compound, said distance and lack of funds had prevented him reaching the Binga district office. "I got here at 10 and after an hour I had got my ID, it is fast and precise," he said.

Home Affairs and Cultural Heritage ministry secretary Raphael Faranisi said the ministry, through the Civil Registry Department, was intensifying efforts nationally to ensure every Zimbabwean has access to an identity regardless of their location. "Targeted mobile registration exercises have become a vital outreach mechanism to ensure those living in hard-to-reach areas are not left behind in terms of documentation," he said, describing it as in line with the government's policy of "leaving no one and no place behind."
