President Emmerson Mnangagwa launched Zimbabwe's five-year National AI Strategy (2026-2030) on Friday in Harare. As part of the strategy, Zimbabwe will establish an AI implementation agency and a regulatory sandbox. A National AI and Data Platform, introduced gradually, will provide countrywide datasets, infrastructure and computing power for AI development.

The AI Strategy Implementation Office, based in the Ministry of ICT, will drive AI projects. Zimbabwe will also establish the National Digital Regulatory Committee as regulatory custodian, reporting to POTRAZ. Mnangagwa said the "legal parameters being put in place" to control AI were for the "protection of national interests as well as the dignity" of Zimbabweans. The strategy aims to build high-performance computing, sovereign data platforms, data centres and secure inclusive connectivity.

UN resident coordinator Edward Kallon said the country can reach its AI potential only by investing in digital skills — pertinent given a serious IT-sector brain drain. Mnangagwa urged Zimbabwean businesses to recruit skilled diaspora Zimbabweans for AI development, and said the right investments could empower farmers, improve healthcare, optimise mining and transform public service delivery. The strategy also promotes an open data initiative to make government data accessible for AI development while safeguarding privacy and security, with common data standards for interoperability. Analysts noted persistent power and connectivity challenges (prompting reliance on solar and energy storage) but a strong skills base.
