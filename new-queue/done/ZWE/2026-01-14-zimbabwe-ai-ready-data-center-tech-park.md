The government of Zimbabwe has unveiled plans to establish a state-of-the-art data center and industrial technology park, a major step in the nation's digital transformation agenda. The announcement was made by ICT Minister Tatenda Mavetera following discussions with Roy Chimanikire, CEO of Econet Wireless.

The new data center will feature expanded power generation capacity to support energy-intensive computing — a critical requirement for advancing AI adoption across the country. Zimbabwe was also set to launch a national AI strategy that month (January 2026 announcement; the strategy was formally launched in March 2026), aimed at revolutionizing banking, finance and industrial production. The government simultaneously implemented measures to reduce the cost of doing business, expected to make data and voice services more accessible.

Mavetera said, "If successful, this initiative could position Zimbabwe as a key player in the regional digital economy," highlighting digital infrastructure's role in economic growth and the government's commitment to private-sector-led development. The initiative is part of Zimbabwe's broader push to leverage technology for economic competitiveness, innovation and regional integration.
