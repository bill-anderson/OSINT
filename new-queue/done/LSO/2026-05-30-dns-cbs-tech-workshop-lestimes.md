# Lesotho embraces digital transformation

*Lesotho Times, 30 May 2026. By Moroke Sekoboto. As CBS, DNS capacitate stakeholders through high-level technology workshop.*

IN a significant step towards accelerating digital transformation in Lesotho, DNS Technologies Ltd, in collaboration with Computer Business Solutions (CBS), recently hosted a three-day executive technology workshop in Maseru focusing on Artificial Intelligence (AI), cloud computing, cybersecurity, backup disaster recovery and managed services.

The workshop brought together decision-makers, IT leaders and professionals from institutions in education, healthcare, construction, manufacturing, communications and government. The event was supported by international partners including Palo Alto Networks, Softlabs Group, BelCloud and Keepit, contributing expertise in cybersecurity, AI, cloud infrastructure and SaaS data protection.

DNS Technologies' Africa business expansion official Jack Bismohun said the event focused on real-world challenges including ransomware attacks, cloud security risks, data loss, operational downtime, legacy infrastructure and the growing impact of AI. Cybersecurity was a central theme, with Palo Alto Networks sessions on proactive security, AI-driven threat detection and cloud-native protection. Keepit presented immutable SaaS backup strategies for Microsoft 365 and cloud data; BelCloud showcased cloud hosting designed for reliability, "sovereignty" and operational agility; Softlabs Group discussed Agentic AI and intelligent business automation.

DNS Technologies founder and CEO Pravin Swami said digital transformation was "no longer optional." CBS Manager Corporate Marketing Molemo Pefole described the event as an important milestone in Lesotho's digital transformation journey. DNS Technologies Ltd is headquartered in Mauritius; CBS is one of Lesotho's established ICT firms with more than 25 years of experience.
