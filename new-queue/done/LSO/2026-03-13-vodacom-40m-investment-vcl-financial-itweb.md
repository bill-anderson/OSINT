# Vodacom Lesotho invests $40m to supercharge connectivity

*ITWeb Africa, 13 March 2026. By Tawanda Karombo.*

Vodacom Lesotho has invested more than **$40 million (M700m)** into a phased network upgrade expanding coverage and improving service quality, and is deploying AI to enhance data analysis and internal processes, MD Mohale Ralebitso said.

The company has faced regulatory scrutiny: in 2025 the Lesotho Communications Authority ruled it violated consumer-protection laws over airtime restrictions on Top-Up Flexi packages, and it has faced licence-revocation threats over Universal Access Fund fee compliance. Vodacom launched its first regional 5G commercial service in Lesotho in 2018; revenues from the operation grew 10.8% to $23 million (R410m) over the quarter to December.

On mobile money, **M-PESA has evolved into a wholly owned entity called VCL Financial Services** — established for clearer regulatory alignment and operational focus — which has introduced savings and investment products beyond traditional mobile money. AI is being integrated across network management, customer engagement and HR.
