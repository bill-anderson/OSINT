Libya's General Authority for Communications and Informatics (GACI) participated in the **58th COM 2026 session** of the UN Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA), held in Tangier, Morocco (28 March–3 April), presenting a strategic vision for a "sovereign and integrated digital economy." It reviewed the National Communications Strategy's aim of **positioning Libya as a regional technical hub serving African digital interests**, based on advanced digital infrastructure and regional connectivity.

Libyan priorities included accelerating fibre-optic deployment, developing digital government services, and strengthening data-exchange systems; it also advocated a **unified African framework for data governance** while upholding digital-sovereignty principles.
