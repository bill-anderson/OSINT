The **CBL** apologised on behalf of commercial banks after a sudden disruption to electronic payment services. It said **two million purchase transactions were recorded through 67,000 points of sale in a single day** — a record for Libya's nascent e-payments system — causing "significant pressure" and disruptions at some banks. Commentators noted the CBL has raced to expand e-payments "at breakneck speed" without parallel investment in backbone infrastructure, warning the system needs stress-testing for long-term resilience.
