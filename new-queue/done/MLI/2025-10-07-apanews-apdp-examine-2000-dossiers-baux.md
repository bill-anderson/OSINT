APA News. On Monday 6 October 2025 Mali's APDP opened an extraordinary session to review ~2,000 declaratory-formality files over five days (checking legal conformity, information-system security, data-retention periods and data-subject rights). Among them: a draft interministerial decree (submitted by the Ministry of Urbanism, Housing, Domains, Territorial Planning and Population) instituting mandatory declaration of leases for residential, commercial, professional or mixed-use property, to better regulate rental contracts and improve traceability of real-estate transactions. Under Law No. 2013-015 of 21 May 2013, any state data processing requires a reasoned APDP opinion. Enforcement escalation: in 2025 the APDP fined a Bamako clinic 5 million FCFA for obstructing a verification mission tied to an undeclared video-surveillance system, and issued formal notices to 100+ companies for non-compliance. Establishes the APDP as an actively enforcing DPA and a new lease-registry data workstream.
