IFC press release (primary). Bamako, 17 Nov 2025. IFC announced a partnership with Orange Mali SA: an EUR80 million loan (~XOF 52.5 billion / ~$92.6-92.7m) from IFC and other investors to install 300 new 4G towers and expand fibre to reach ~300,000 households and small businesses; half the towers in rural areas. Package: EUR50m equivalent in XOF from IFC's own account + up to EUR30m mobilised from the West African Development Bank (BOAD), backed by the World Bank Group's IDA Private Sector Window. Structured as a Social Loan. Includes a provision for Orange Mali to raise women's participation in its digital-training programmes to 70% by 2032, and replacement of diesel generators with solar (cutting >8,000 tonnes CO2/year). First investment under the IFC-Orange Middle East and Africa (OMEA) partnership signed at the Africa CEO Forum, Abidjan, May 2025 (targeting 8 countries). Supports Mali Digital 2020 and the WBG Digital Economy for Africa initiative. Orange Mali (majority-owned by Sonatel) serves >12 million subscribers.
