# Airtel Congo, Wing Wah to deploy private telecom network

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa. August 18, 2025.

Airtel Congo and Wing Wah have partnered to deploy a private telecom network at the Banga Kayo oil field in Congo-Brazzaville.

Telecoms operator Airtel Congo and Wing Wah, a private Congolese oil company and subsidiary of the Chinese group Southernpec, have partnered to deploy a private telecom network at the Banga Kayo oil field in Kouilou in Congo-Brazzaville.

According to Airtel Congo, the five year contract marks a step in the development of industrial telecommunications in Central Africa.

"This private network, the first of its kind in the country, aims to strengthen the connectivity, security and efficiency of oil operations, while laying the foundations for a robust and sustainable digital infrastructure," Airtel Congo said in a LinkedIn post.

Ecofin Telecom reports that the private telecom network will be designed and implemented by Airtel Congo to meet the specific needs of Wing Wah in its oil operations.

Airtel Congo added that the project is a major asset for optimizing the management of this strategic site and supporting the company's growth ambitions.

The private network aims to strengthen the connectivity, security and efficiency of Wing Wah's oil operations.

"This partnership illustrates our constant commitment to innovation and to harnessing connectivity to advance the economic development of Congo-Brazzaville," said Airtel Congo Managing Director Djibril Tobe.

## Telecommunication deals in Africa

African telcos and companies have signed many deals aimed at connecting communities and industries on the continent recently.

Just last week, Airtel Congo's parent company Airtel Africa signed a strategic infrastructure sharing agreement with Vodacom Group in Mozambique, Tanzania and the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC).

Congo-Brazzaville's Electronic Communications Regulatory Agency (ARPCE) and the Angolan Institute of Communications (INACOM) signed a frequency coordination agreement on the common border, which is 231km long, also last week.
