# Guinea Introduces 3% Tax on Foreign Digital Services

Guinea has introduced a 3% tax on foreign digital services used in the country, under a decree signed on Thursday, May 21, by transitional president Mamadi Doumbouya. The measure establishes a Digital Compliance Fee, abbreviated as RCN, that applies to streaming platforms, cloud services, software-as-a-service subscriptions, digital advertising and e-commerce.

The decree sets a temporary flat rate of 3% for the first twelve months after the measure enters into force. After that period, a tiered rate structure ranging from 1.5% to 7% will apply depending on the category of service. Providers generating less than 250 million Guinean francs, roughly $28,600, in annual taxable revenue are exempt, along with certain educational and non-profit services.

The measure also creates a Digital Analysis and Regulation Platform, abbreviated as PARN, responsible for monitoring foreign digital services used in Guinea, and a Digital Sovereignty Fund, abbreviated as FSN, which will be financed primarily through revenue generated by the new fee. According to the authorities, the reform is intended to strengthen the country's digital sovereignty, improve oversight of digital activity and generate new revenue to fund technology infrastructure, cybersecurity, training and innovation.

To determine whether services are being used in Guinea, authorities will rely on users' IP addresses, payment methods, billing addresses and phone numbers. Companies affected will have 90 days to appoint a local representative. The fee may be collected through withholding mechanisms involving banks, mobile money operators and licensed payment gateways, or through direct payment to the Fund. Non-compliant companies face fines of up to twice the unpaid sums or temporary restrictions on access to services. A six-month transitional period is provided before enforcement begins.
