# Updated Whitepaper — Review and Reform of the Access to Information Act, 2018
Information Commission of Seychelles (InfoCom)

[Date proxy: derived from the document filename "ATIA-Updated-Whitepaper-10.11.2025.pdf" (10 Nov 2025); confirm the on-document date at ingest.]

In November 2024, InfoCom released an initial whitepaper for public consultation proposing a review and reform of the Access to Information Act (ATIA), enacted 20 July 2018. This updated whitepaper outlines current weaknesses of the ATIA and recommended changes, intended to facilitate stakeholder discussion before legislative amendments are drafted.

Key findings include: (Finding 1, urgent) absence of clear legal provisions to enforce InfoCom's orders/decisions/directives under Part VII s.63(1) — information holders have ignored directives since 2019 with no clear recourse; enforcement entails complex, lengthy, expensive proceedings and InfoCom lacks specialised legal/financial capacity. Recommendations include aligning s.33(4) third-party provisions with the newly enacted 2023 Data Protection Act; adding public-disclosure requirements and overrides for human-rights/corruption matters; a schedule-based override of secrecy provisions (Canada model); a centralized Access to Information Unit; and open-reuse licensing for public information. InfoCom notes it took on the Data Protection Act mandate at end-December 2023. Drafting of revisions was scheduled by the Attorney General's Office toward the end of the 2025 legislative session.
