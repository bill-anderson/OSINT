# Seychelles Commercial Bank Confirms Customer Data Breach | 15 July 2025
By Mathew J. Schwartz — BankInfoSecurity (ISMG)

A hacker claims to have stolen and sold personal data of clients of Seychelles Commercial Bank (SCB). The bank notified customers of a hack but said only personal information — not funds — was accessed, and temporarily suspended its internet banking services, directing customers to ATMs and branches.

The breach first came to light publicly on July 4 via cybercrime marketplace DarkForums, where user "ByteToBreach" advertised a "Seychelles main bank clients leak" including DOB, phones, names, addresses and emails. FCPB/FCDB refers to the Oracle Flexcube Private Banking application SCB uses. Researchers at Los Angeles-based firm Resecurity engaged the attacker and reviewed a sample: customer names, email addresses, phone numbers, account type and account balances, with multiple accounts labelled "current accounts - government."

ByteToBreach told ISMG they first breached the bank's network on Feb. 5, 2025, returned on July 3 and exfiltrated data through July 4, stealing ~2.2 GB. They claimed to have exploited an unspecified Oracle WebLogic Server vulnerability (with Fortinet also present), and to have decrypted some data using keys accessible in the bank's IT environment. The bank apologised, said it was working with police, and put "additional cybersecurity safeguards" in place. Seychelles ranks 45th on the Corporate Tax Haven Index (Tax Justice Network).
