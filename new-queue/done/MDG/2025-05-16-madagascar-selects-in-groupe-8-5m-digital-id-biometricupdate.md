# Madagascar selects IN Groupe for €8.5M digital ID contract

Madagascar has selected **IN Groupe** for a project to modernize the country's civil registry and create an integrated digital ID system for public service delivery. The contract has an estimated value of **8.5 million euros (approximately US$9.6 million)**.

The **PRODIGY** project was launched in 2019 and is supported by **$143 million from the World Bank**. It aims to provide a biometrics-backed legal identity to all Malagasy citizens, along with a digital ID usable despite relatively low digital literacy and mobile phone penetration.

Madagascar's government was considering suppliers for two PRODIGY contracts in September 2024, per Africa Intelligence. One was a $15 million contract to supply biometric registration equipment, with Augentic, Thales and Laxton all reportedly bidding. The other was a $9 million contract for a comprehensive database management system; bidders included Augentic, Thales, IN Groupe, Atos, Idemia and SELP.

IN Groupe won the database/identity management contract; **Laxton has won the contract for the biometric registration kits**. (An earlier version reported the identity management field narrowed to Idemia and Thales; the post was updated May 18, 2025 to clarify awards.)
