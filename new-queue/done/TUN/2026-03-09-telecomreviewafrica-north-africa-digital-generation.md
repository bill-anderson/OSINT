# What's Behind North Africa's Digital Generation? Tunisia.

March 9, 2026 — Telecom Review Africa (feature/overview)

Tunisia is establishing itself as a leader in North Africa's digital transformation, propelled by government initiatives, private innovation, and a rising generation of tech-savvy entrepreneurs.

At the core of this transformation is Tunisia's National Digital Strategy 2021-2025, a framework placing digitalization at the heart of economic and social development, focusing on expanding high-speed broadband, modernizing public administration, enhancing digital literacy, cybersecurity, data-driven governance, and adoption of AI and cloud computing. Under the leadership of Nizar Ben Neji, by 2025 nearly 80% of the population were internet users, and mobile subscriptions exceeded the total population. 4G coverage reached almost 95% of inhabitants, while commercial 5G services began rolling out in 2025 following licensing in late 2024.

Infrastructure milestones cited: 2018 plans for a blockchain-based smart city; 2021 preparations to launch a first satellite; in 2026, Tunisie Telecom's EUR 190 million EBRD digital-infrastructure partnership; and Orange Tunisie's Medusa submarine cable.

On fintech: in 2024, electronic transactions in Tunisia reached approximately USD 8.8 billion, a 10.6% increase from the previous year, with mobile payments and e-wallet use rising sharply. On the ICT sector: it represents roughly 11% of GDP and supports more than 2,200 companies, with around 40% of tech-sector employment comprising individuals under 30.
