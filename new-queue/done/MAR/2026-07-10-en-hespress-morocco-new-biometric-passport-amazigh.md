# Morocco to introduce new biometric passport featuring Amazigh, English starting August

Friday 10 July 2026 - 16:00

Morocco's new legal framework for biometric passports will introduce Amazigh as one of the official languages used in the document, while abolishing temporary passports and removing an exception that allowed minors applying for passports to obtain national identity cards.

The changes are contained in draft decree No. 2.26.551, which amends the 2008 decree establishing Morocco's biometric passport system. The text was approved by the government council on Thursday.

According to the explanatory memorandum accompanying the decree, the new provisions assign responsibility for determining passport issuance procedures to a joint decision by the ministries responsible for interior affairs and foreign affairs. The decree also defines the passport's physical and electronic components. The document will continue to take the form of a booklet containing a cover, data pages, a handwritten signature page and an embedded electronic chip.

Among the most significant changes is the adoption of four languages—Arabic, Amazigh, French and English—throughout the passport. The reform also introduces a secured photograph of the holder on the signature page and removes the passport holder's address from the personal data page. The new decree further specifies the information stored in the passport's electronic component.

The legislation repeals two provisions contained in the previous decree: one concerning temporary passports, which authorities say are no longer necessary, and another that allowed minors applying for passports to obtain a national electronic identity card through the General Directorate of National Security.

According to the memorandum, Moroccan authorities will continue issuing passports under the current format during a transitional period until the new generation of biometric passports is fully deployed. Existing passports will remain valid until their expiration dates. The reform forms part of Morocco's broader efforts to strengthen passport security against increasingly sophisticated forgery. The new biometric passport will be gradually introduced across provinces, as well as at Moroccan embassies and consulates abroad, with nationwide implementation expected by August 2026.
