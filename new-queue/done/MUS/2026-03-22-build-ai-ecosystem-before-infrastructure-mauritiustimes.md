# Why Mauritius must build an AI Ecosystem before an AI Infrastructure (opinion, Sameer Sharma)

*Mauritius Times, 22 March 2026.*

Opinion critiquing the sequencing of Mauritius's AI push. It notes **Mauritius Telecom's Rs 20 billion ($434 million) "Africa-Asia Digital Corridor" roadmap 2026-2029** (Tier IV data centres, 5.5G, T4 and SEACOM 2.0 subsea cables) and argues MT carries a "bet-the-company" risk and a **"Timing Trap"**: a year-long global waitlist for Nvidia B300 GPUs and years to commission Tier IV facilities mean the infrastructure could be obsolete on delivery.

Argument: Mauritius should adopt an **"Autopilot" strategy** — build end-to-end AI *services* today on **hybrid cloud** (AWS/Azure/Google) while solving domestic infrastructure later — rather than pouring billions into "plumbing" before it has "architects" (developers). True sovereignty lies in the **Logic Layer** (proprietary Multi-Agent Systems). Warns that Ebène's 1990s labour-outsourcing model (KYC, document verification, fund administration) is being displaced by autonomous AI. Proposes exporting **"Lending-as-a-Service"** and **RegTech for the FSC and Bank of Mauritius** across Africa. Frames the infrastructure-only focus as "financially suicidal."
