# "AI will not become an economic pillar because we declare it" — Veemal Gungadin (interview)

*Mauritius Times, 13 July 2026.*

Interview with **Veemal Gungadin, CEO of Mauritius Telecom (MT)**, on aligning the Government's 2026-2027 Budget ambition (AI as a new pillar of economic development) with MT's role as national operator.

Key points:
- MT's **2026-2029 strategy** makes the **AI & Compute Corridor** one of four pillars: expanding the **Tier IV-certified data centre**, deploying **dedicated GPU clusters**, and strengthening **sovereign cloud** capabilities.
- MT pioneered the country's first enterprise-grade **sovereign AI platform, mytGPT**, and **mytGPT Education** (evolving into **my.t Learn**), keeping data under Mauritian jurisdiction.
- Ambition to **democratise AI access** ("make AI accessible to everyone"; "an AI reserved for specialists is not an AI that truly serves a country").
- Previews the **AllMyT Summit (16-17 July 2026)** as moving AI "from ambition to visible execution."

Serves as the national-paper companion to the AllMyT Summit announcements (AWS MoU, NVIDIA H200, Mauritius AI Registry).
