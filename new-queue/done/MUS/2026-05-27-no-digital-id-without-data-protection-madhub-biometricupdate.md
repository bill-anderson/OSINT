# There's no digital ID without data protection: Mauritius privacy chief

*Biometric Update, 27 May 2026.*

Interview with **Drudeisha Madhub, Data Protection Commissioner of Mauritius** (also President of the Francophone Association of Data Protection Authorities), at the **ID4Africa 2026 AGM in Abidjan**. She argues privacy, cybersecurity and governance must be **foundational** to digital identity and DPI, not compliance afterthoughts — "two sides of the same coin" — and stresses embedding **privacy by design** into procured technologies, not merely registering controllers/processors.

Key development: Madhub retraced Mauritius's evolution (first data-protection law **2004**, partial implementation **2009**, new regime **2017**) and confirmed that **in 2026 Mauritius is going for the EU adequacy assessment, fully aligning its law with the EU GDPR** — a significant move toward GDPR adequacy. She cautions against digital-ID vendors "marketing trust" without genuine privacy-by-design foundations.
