# Starlink beams into Niger

SpaceX's satellite internet service Starlink has officially launched in Niger (announced on X). Niger's Ministry of Communications, Post and Digital Economy confirmed **commercial availability**, with residential and corporate kits purchasable online and through authorized sellers. Communications Minister **Sidi Mohamed Raliou** said access "remains regulated." The launch follows SpaceX's **November 2024 agreement with Niger's ruling military junta** to provide LEO satellite broadband.

The piece situates Niger (the 18th African Starlink market at the time) amid Starlink's uneven continental expansion — opposition in Lesotho (local-ownership concerns) and a 2024 cease-operations order in Namibia — and notes Chad signing a Starlink operating licence the same week.
