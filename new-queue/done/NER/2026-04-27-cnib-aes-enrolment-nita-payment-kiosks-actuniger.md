# Carte Nationale d'Identité Biométrique (CNIB-AES) : avec NITA, les démarches simplifiées pour régler ses frais et se faire enrôler

In the mass-enrolment drive for the new **CNIB-AES** biometric national ID card, national money-transfer leader **NITA Transfert** deployed dedicated kiosks at enrolment sites to let citizens pay the enrolment fee on the spot. The rollout reached the neighbourhood commissariats of Niamey, beginning with the **Yantala commissariat (ACNYI)** at an official ceremony on **Saturday 25 April 2026**; NITA installed kiosks so citizens no longer need to travel to pay their fees, issuing receipts needed for the enrolment file. The scheme will extend to other Niamey commissariats and the interior as enrolment progresses.

Per the authorities, the CNIB is "the key for each citizen to access their rights," a response to document fraud, identity theft and insecurity, guaranteeing uniqueness, authenticity and protection of each citizen's identity. It is **mandatory from age 15** and costs a **redevance of 4,500 FCFA**; required documents include a birth certificate or supplementary judgment, a nationality certificate, a residence certificate, proof of profession where applicable, and biometric-standard photographs. After Niamey the process will be progressively deployed to the interior.
