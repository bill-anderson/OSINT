# Government launches biometric registration exercise for organised forces

*One Citizen Daily, 24 June 2026*

The government has launched a headcount and biometric registration exercise for the organised forces. The initiative is being spearheaded by the Ministry of Public Service and Human Resource Development, the Ministry of Finance and Planning, and the Ministry of Interior. According to officials, the process is aimed at establishing a single verified biometric database for personnel serving in the country's organised forces.

"The exercise seeks to ensure that the right employee is paid in the right place and at the right time," Gatkuoth said, adding that the initiative would provide a reliable foundation for workforce planning, budgeting, human resource management, and the eventual establishment of a unified biometric payroll system.

The registration exercise is being implemented with support from the World Bank through the Public Financial Management and Institutional Strengthening Project (PFMIS). In recent years, concerns over payroll irregularities, including the presence of ghost workers and duplicate records, have prompted authorities to adopt digital systems to enhance personnel management. Biometric registration is widely regarded as an effective tool for verifying employee identities, improving payroll accuracy, and reducing opportunities for fraud.
