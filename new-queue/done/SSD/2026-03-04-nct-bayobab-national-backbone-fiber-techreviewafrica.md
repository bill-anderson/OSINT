# NCT South Sudan and Bayobab collaborate to roll out national backbone fiber network project

*Tech Review Africa, 4 March 2026 (Nana Appiah Acquaye)*

> Note: Tech Review Africa is a byline-thin PR-wire outlet (README lead-net) — treat as an announcement record; prefer a primary or better outlet if the same story appears elsewhere at ingest.

NCT South Sudan has announced that its collaboration with Bayobab has moved into the execution phase, marking the official kickoff of the National Backbone Fiber Network Project one month ago (early February 2026). The initiative is described as one of the largest ICT infrastructure developments in South Sudan's history, aiming to connect the eastern and western corridors of the country, linking key regions through Juba and extending to other strategic cities.

As part of the rollout, NCT South Sudan is collaborating on the eastern segment from Juba to Nadapal at the Kenya border. This section is expected to strengthen cross-border integration, provide backup international connectivity to Juba, and expand regional digital access. The development forms part of the broader East-to-West Connectivity Project (Bayobab Project E2W), with further phases expected as the network continues to expand.
