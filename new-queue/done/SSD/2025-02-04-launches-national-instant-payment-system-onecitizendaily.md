# South Sudan launches National Instant Payment System

*One Citizen Daily, 4 February 2025*

Bank of South Sudan (BOSS) has announced launching the country's first National Instant Payment System (NIPS). The project, in collaboration with AfricaNenda Foundation, is a significant step towards modernizing the nation's financial infrastructure. The Central Bank emphasized that NIPS will enable real-time, secure, and affordable transactions, connecting banks, mobile money providers, and other financial institutions.

It will support various payment types, including person-to-person (P2P), person-to-business (P2B), government-to-person (G2P), and person-to-government (P2G) transactions. The collaboration will focus on developing a detailed implementation roadmap, building capacity within BOSS and financial institutions, and ensuring integration and interoperability with banks, telecommunication companies, and other stakeholders.

The launch of NIPS aligns with broader efforts to implement other critical infrastructures, such as Automated Clearing House (ACH) and Real Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) systems. "Instant payment systems are transformative tools that redefine access to financial services and promote inclusivity," Governor Ohisa added.
