Dar es Salaam. The government has significantly reduced the cost of interoperability transfers — moving funds to mobile phone wallets from bank accounts or vice versa. Khamis Mussa Omar, the Finance minister, detailed these interventions in the National Assembly when responding to Kinondoni MP Tarimba Abbas, noting that transaction fees are capped at 5,000/- for any amount exceeding 500,000/-.

"As of March 2026, a total of 113 service providers have been licensed to operate payment system services across the country," he said, affirming that this influx of providers ensures consumers have variety and pushes companies to offer competitive pricing. The Bank of Tanzania (BoT) has been aggressively licensing new players to break monopolies in the electronic payment sector.

The minister pointed at achievements during 2024 and 2025, where the BoT moved to cap the maximum cost for single transactions in the Tanzania Automated Clearing House (TACH) and the instant payment system at 2,000/-. This move was designed to prevent exorbitant charges on high-value transfers, with the momentum pursued last year with a strategic focus on the Tanzania Instant Payment System (TIPS).
