Dar es Salaam. As Tanzania pushes ahead with its long-term ambition of building a $1 trillion economy by 2050, digital transformation is increasingly emerging as one of the strongest pillars to drive economic growth. This vision dominated discussions at the 9th Tanzania Annual ICT Conference (TAIC 2026), a five-day forum that opened on Monday, January 19, under the ICT Commission.

The conference comes as Tanzania begins implementing its 10-year Digital Economy Strategic Framework (2024-2034). Over the full period to 2034, the digital economy is expected to generate more than $1.1 billion in GDP. At the centre of the strategy is the goal of doubling the ICT sector's contribution to GDP to three percent by 2029, up from about 1.5 percent in 2022/23.

According to the ministry, by December 2025 a total of 758 communication towers had been constructed and switched on, while 304 towers were upgraded from 2G to 3G and 4G. About 660 public institutions have been connected to high-speed internet, with a target of at least 900 service points. These investments support the strategy's target of 80 percent broadband and internet user penetration by 2029. ICT Commission's Dr Mwasaga said Tanzania has already put in place close to 95 percent of the required digital infrastructure, shifting focus from building networks to driving innovation, skills and service delivery.

The framework also targets mobile money penetration exceeding 70 percent nationwide, and innovation hubs in all regions to support at least 1,000 new startups over five years. Minister Kairuki directed the commission to strengthen support frameworks so local digital products can compete regionally and globally, while safeguarding cybersecurity and the ethical use of AI.
