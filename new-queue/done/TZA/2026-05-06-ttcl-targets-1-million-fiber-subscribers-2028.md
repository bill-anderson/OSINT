Note: expansion covers Zanzibar among initial cities.

Tanzania's state-owned telecommunications company TTCL is targeting one million fibre optic subscribers over the next three years as part of a broader broadband expansion strategy. TTCL Managing Director Moremi Marwa announced the target during a ceremony to hand over vehicles to field teams. For the current fiscal year, the company aims to deliver 280,000 new connections, of which about 200,000 have already been completed. TTCL plans to deploy 400,000 additional connections in 2026/27, followed by around 300,000 in 2027/28.

Marwa said the current phase is focused on Dar es Salaam, Dodoma, Mwanza, Iringa, Mbeya, Zanzibar and Arusha, with the next phase gradually extending to the rest of the country. President Samia Suluhu Hassan urged TTCL in March 2023 to refocus on broadband services.

According to regulator data, TTCL held 2.78% of the mobile internet market at the end of March. In the fixed-line market, however, TTCL ranked third with 63,843 subscribers, behind Airtel with 213,010 and Vodacom with 114,109.
