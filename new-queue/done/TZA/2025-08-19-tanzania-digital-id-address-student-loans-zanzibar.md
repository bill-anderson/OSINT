Note: Zanzibar item, in-scope as TZA.

Hundreds of students in Zanzibar have been able to apply for student loans using a digital ID address system recently launched in the territory. Since the digital ID address system went operational in June by the Higher Education Students' Loans Board (HESLB), over 489,409 students have applied for loans using digital means, The Citizen reports.

The ID address system is part of the National Address Programme (NaP) launched in Zanzibar, with the objective of assigning a digital address to all citizens living in the region and elsewhere. In May, the Tanzanian government also made the national ID a requirement for obtaining student loans. Speaking during its formal launch, Tanzania's Communication and Information Minister Jerry Silaa explained the system will greatly contribute to the growth of the digital economy of the United Republic.

Construction, Communication and Transport Minister Dr Khalid Salum Mohamed announced that over 450,000 digital ID addresses have already been assigned to people and plots in Zanzibar. He mentioned the importance of linking the new system with other services such as postal, emergency, financial and transport services. Early in the year, the government also unveiled a digital address app (NaPA) for the transport sector. The national digital ID system, dubbed Jamii Number, is one of the foundations of Tanzania's digital transformation.
