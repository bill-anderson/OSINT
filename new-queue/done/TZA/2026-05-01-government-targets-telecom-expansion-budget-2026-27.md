Dar es Salaam. Tanzania is targeting a major expansion of its telecommunications infrastructure, particularly in rural and commercially less attractive areas, to improve access to mobile and radio communication services. The initiative is among key priorities in the government's Sh222.6 billion budget for the Ministry of Communication and Information Technology for the 2026/27 financial year.

Presenting the budget in Parliament, the minister for Communication and Information Technology, Angellah Kairuki, said 94.1 percent of the allocation will be directed towards development expenditure. "The government is prioritising strategic investments in ICT infrastructure to ensure inclusive connectivity and strengthen the digital economy," she told lawmakers.

TCRA data up to December 2025 shows population coverage of 98.6 percent for 2G, 93.9 percent for 3G, 94.2 percent for 4G, and 30.1 percent for 5G; geographical coverage stands at 79.5 percent (2G), 76.2 percent (3G), 77.3 percent (4G) and 10.5 percent (5G).

Ms Kairuki said the budget will support the development of a national digital addressing system, continue coordination of the cubesat educational satellite project and space technology programmes, expand e-commerce systems, strengthen cybersecurity frameworks, and support ICT start-ups and innovation ecosystems. Of the total, Sh209.4 billion is allocated for development expenditure and Sh13.1 billion for recurrent costs. The parliamentary infrastructure committee urged finalisation of the ICT Bill 2026 and proposed a Sh100 billion investment to upgrade the National Data Centre and establish new facilities in Dodoma and Zanzibar.
