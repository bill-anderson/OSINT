In a significant development for Zanzibar's digital future, Airtel Tanzania and the Zanzibar Communication and ICT Infrastructure Agency (ZICTIA) have signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to accelerate the islands' digital transformation. The agreement, witnessed by senior government officials, sets the stage for a long-term collaboration to enhance telecom services and expand access to modern communication technologies across the region.

The partnership focuses on improving broadband connectivity, boosting digital inclusion, and supporting government priorities, aiming to accelerate projects that benefit citizens through advancements in e-government, e-learning, e-health, and e-commerce. Zanzibar's Minister for Infrastructure, Communication, and Transport, Khalid Salum Mohammed, described the collaboration as a crucial step towards making digital services a reality, especially in underserved communities.

Airtel's Managing Director, Charles Kamoto, stated that the MoU reflects the company's commitment to being more than just a telecom operator, planning to invest in infrastructure and partnerships. ZICTIA's Director General, Shukuru Awadh Suleiman, highlighted the importance of leveraging private-sector partnerships to unlock the full potential of Zanzibar's ICT sector, including promoting financial inclusion.
