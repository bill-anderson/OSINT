According to data from the Tanzania Communications Regulatory Authority, the sector has expanded rapidly, with mobile subscriptions rising from 52.8 million in 2021 to 111.9 million in 2026, and mobile internet users increasing from 29 million to 58.9 million over the same period. The article argues that Tanzania should formally recognise telecommunications infrastructure as Critical Economic Infrastructure (CEI) because it now functions as the backbone of the modern economy, like roads, electricity, and water.

Despite this expansion, the physical network remains relatively limited, with just over 10,000 towers serving tens of millions of smartphone users, creating structural strain on quality and coverage, especially in rural and underserved regions. The report emphasises that telecom operators face financial constraints including low average revenue per user, high costs of rural expansion, and a combined tax and levy burden estimated at nearly half of sector revenues.

The article calls for a policy shift treating telecommunications less as a revenue-generating sector and more as foundational infrastructure. It recommends reducing regulatory friction, lowering deployment costs, and improving investment conditions to accelerate network expansion, arguing that ambitions for a trillion-dollar economy depend on strengthening and protecting the country's digital infrastructure.
