Dar es Salaam. The government has allocated Sh73 billion for the continued implementation of the National ICT Broadband Backbone (NICTBB) project in the 2025/26 financial year. Presenting the Sh291.533 billion budget for the Ministry of Information, Communication, and Information Technology in Parliament on 16 May 2025, Minister Jerry Silaa said the funds will support several key initiatives.

These include extending the NICTBB to the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC), installing Wi-Fi in 50 public spaces, and supporting the construction of two national data centres — one in Dodoma and another in Zanzibar.

The ministry will also supervise the rollout of Last Mile Optic Fibre to 100 institutions in the criminal justice, defence, and security sectors — including the Judiciary, PCCB, Police, Prisons, TPDF, and Tanzania Intelligence Service — alongside other government agencies. It will oversee the connection of 90,000 customers to fibre-to-the-doorstep services and complete 4,442 targeted kilometres of NICTBB construction.

Reflecting on 2024/25, Mr Silaa said 3,621 kilometres of fibre had been laid, representing 82 percent of the year's target. Since the project's inception, 13,820 kilometres have been completed, out of a total target of 16,280 kilometres. The backbone has now reached 109 out of 139 districts, equivalent to 78 percent coverage, with all 111 planned service delivery points finalised. "We connected the NICTBB to Mombasa, Kenya, through the Horohoro-Tanga route and improved mobile broadband infrastructure, which now covers 74 percent of the country," said the minister. He noted the ministry had completed feasibility studies for the two national data centres in Dodoma and Zanzibar.
