Tanzanian fintech NALA has secured a major funding package that highlights a shift in how global investors view African fintech firms — as payment systems builders rather than consumer apps. On 28 May 2026, NALA announced it had secured a US$25 million credit facility, which can rise to US$50 million, from Liquidity, a platform backed by Japan's MUFG through Mars Growth Capital.

The structure shows a wider trend: investors supporting debt-based growth funding instead of equity dilution, especially in fintech infrastructure businesses. The company said the facility will support "reliable and scalable payment infrastructure across international remittance corridors," and supports stablecoin-linked payment corridors to move money faster across borders.

The deal reflects NALA moving from a product model to a network model, and the growing use of stablecoins in global payments. Africa is becoming more important in global payments due to large and growing remittances, widespread mobile money, and increasing cross-border trade under AfCFTA. NALA's US$50 million expandable facility marks an important step in this transition, positioning the company as part of the infrastructure that moves money globally.
