# Namibia weighs biometrics options for foundation of instant payment system

Speaking at ID4Africa 2025 (Addis Ababa), Marsorry Ickua, chief operating officer of Instant Payments Namibia (seconded from the Bank of Namibia), described the instant payment system (IPS) initiative — launched in 2024 as part of Namibia's DPI drive. Namibia has a new Civil Registration and Identification law, a budget to implement it, and new digital ID cards "rolling out next month."

The project is at the stage of "deploying infrastructure and getting stakeholders on board." Ickua says the system will likely use biometrics for user authentication, because of concerns about the ease with which cards can be forged or cloned: "One of the underlying pillars of a successful instant payment product for a country is good authentication and understanding who people are." Cost-effectiveness has been a challenge — expensive hardware for KYC at commercial banks and points of entry.

Namibia aims to build a comprehensive system, citing the India Stack (high connectivity + digital ID coverage underpinning UPI's success) as a model, with objectives similar to M-Pesa: getting money moving in the remotest corners while lowering transaction costs and advancing financial inclusion.
