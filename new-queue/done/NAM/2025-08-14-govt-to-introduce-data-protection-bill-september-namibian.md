# Govt to introduce data protection bill in September

The government is set to introduce a data protection bill to be tabled in September 2025, per ICT Minister Emma Theofelus, speaking at the Standard Bank Anti-Financial Crime Conference. The bill will ensure protection of personal data, respect for privacy and security of digital transactions, aligning Namibia with international best practices.

In addition, the government is preparing to launch electronic signatures in February 2026 under the Electronic Transactions Act of 2019, carrying the same legal weight as handwritten signatures. "Imagine signing a contract from your phone with full legal recognition – no travel, no paper, no delay," Theofelus said. She noted e-signatures reduce forgery, enhance traceability and ensure accountability: "With biometric authentication, like facial recognition or voice signatures, fraud becomes harder to commit and easier to detect."
