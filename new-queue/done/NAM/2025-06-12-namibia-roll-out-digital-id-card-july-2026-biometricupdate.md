# Namibia to roll out digital ID card in July 2026

The Namibian government has named July 2026 for the rollout of a national digital ID system aimed at streamlining access to public and private services. The date follows Home Affairs Minister Lucia Iipumbu's remarks on the imminent launch, emphasising strong awareness and sensitisation campaigns.

Home Affairs Executive Director Etienne Maritz (quoted via Windhoek Observer/The Namibian) said the system will bring major changes to identity management, with impact expected in birth registration; the digital ID will be "almost impossible to forge," offering each citizen a uniquely verifiable identity, aiding the fight against identity fraud, improving national security and enhancing data protection.

An UnConference on Digital Legal Identity is scheduled for Windhoek on 26 June, rallying 500+ participants, organised in partnership with UNDP.

Notably: four months before the digital ID launch, the government will unveil an electronic signatures system. CRAN CEO Emilia Nghikembua said the e-signature system, to launch February 2026, will enable legally binding digital transactions without citizens having to travel to sign documents.
