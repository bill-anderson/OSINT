# Minister Iipumbu dismisses Namibia e-ID 'microchip tracking' allegations

Home Affairs Minister Lucia Iipumbu has dismissed public concerns and protests alleging that Namibia's planned electronic identity documents will be used to microchip and track citizens. Residents of the Omusati region recently protested the new e-IDs, claiming they are part of a government agenda to microchip citizens to track their movements.

Delivering a statement in the National Assembly, Iipumbu said the ministry noted information circulating on social media and concerns raised through petitions and demonstrations. "While public engagement and democratic participation are welcomed and respected, it is important that discussions on this matter are guided by facts and accurate information."

She said the e-ID is a secure smart card storing biometric and demographic information digitally (fingerprints, photographs and other identification details), enabling secure, real-time identity verification in physical and digital environments. "The electronic identity document does not involve the implantation of any device, chip, or technology into any individual. The technology is contained within the identity card itself, similar to that used in modern bank cards." She rejected assertions the e-ID could track citizens, and said implementation will fully comply with the Civil Registration and Identification Act of 2024.

Public awareness and consultation have begun nationwide and will continue ahead of the planned roll-out in September [2026].
