# AI abuse overshadows Namibia's e-signature drive

The abuse of AI is in the spotlight in Namibia after a deepfake video scam surfaced depicting government ministers endorsing a purported official investment scheme. The AI-generated video, "guaranteeing" income of N$35,000 (~US$2,000) per week, showed PM Elijah Ngurare, Deputy PM Natangue Ithete and ICT Minister Emma Theofelus. Theofelus: "the Government of the Republic of Namibia does not have such platforms nor schemes and the videos circulating are AI-generated and the messages shared are false." Windhoek-based Meinert Cybersecurity noted the video follows previous incidents targeting financial authorities and warned AI-generated videos are increasingly convincing.

The scam overshadowed the ICT ministry's drive to promote adoption of e-signatures. The government is preparing to implement legally recognised e-signatures by February 2026 under the Electronic Transactions Act (2019), and is working on accreditation of service providers. Interim ED Linda Aipinge: "As we prepare for the nationwide deployment of the electronic signature, we are taking a decisive step towards a future where secure, efficient and accessible digital services are a norm and not an exception."
