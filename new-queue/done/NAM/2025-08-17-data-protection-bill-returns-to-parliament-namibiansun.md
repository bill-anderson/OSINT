# Data protection bill returns to parliament

About 12 years after work on the draft data protection bill first began, it is expected to be tabled in parliament again next month (September 2025), per ICT Minister Emma Theofelus, speaking at a Standard Bank financial-crime conference in Windhoek.

Institute for Public Policy Research (IPPR) researcher Frederico Links said the public has not yet seen the latest version and hopes it addresses concerns the IPPR raised in 2023 that it fell short of best practice: "We have not yet seen whether any amendments have been made to the bill discussed in 2023. Until we have reviewed the latest version, our concerns remain the same as in 2023 – that it does not adequately address the rights of data subjects." A section addressing data-subject rights was removed in 2020 and, as far as he is aware, not substantially reintroduced. He warned a tabled bill "usually means it is on the fast track to coming into effect."

Theofelus called the bill "a cornerstone of Namibia's digital governance strategy," ensuring personal data protection, privacy and secure digital transactions, and aligning Namibia with international best practices. Separately she noted the Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC) froze more than N$96.9 million in the past year; the FIC, police and Anti-Corruption Commission investigated 148 cases (50 victims, 128 persons of interest). Government split the draft financial intelligence bill from the draft data protection bill in 2019.
