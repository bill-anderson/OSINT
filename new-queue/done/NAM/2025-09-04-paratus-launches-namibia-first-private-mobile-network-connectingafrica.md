# Paratus launches Namibia's first private mobile network

Paratus Group has launched the first privately-owned mobile network in Namibia, with both LTE and 5G capabilities, coinciding with Paratus Namibia's 20th anniversary. It comes in as a mobile challenger to state-owned MTC Namibia and Telecom Namibia. MTC is the biggest operator (2m+ mobile subscribers by mid-2025 in a country of ~3 million); Telecom Namibia had ~336,000 mobile subscribers by end-June 2025 (Omdia).

Paratus CEO Schalk Erasmus: "Connectivity is the backbone of economic growth. Launching Namibia's first private mobile network is a deliberate step in our mission to transform Africa's digital landscape." Paratus said Namibia is "the group's power base"; it has invested N$1.42 billion (US$79.8m) in network infrastructure since 2018, plus a further N$600 million (US$33.7m) in the last year on the new mobile network — one-third on a digital technology stack integrating all Paratus services into a single platform. Developed with Cerillion (digital) and Nokia (mobile network). Paratus Group operates in 15 African countries, runs an intercontinental fibre network and satellite infrastructure (including the Equiano landing), operates data centres and offers LEO services. Launch attended by ICT Minister Emma Theofelus.
