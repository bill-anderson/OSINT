# West Africa's Digital Backbone: Phase3 and Sonatel Launch 3,500km Fibre Route

Tech Africa News - 9 May 2025, Editorial Team

Phase3 and Sonatel have activated a new terrestrial fibre route stretching 3,500km from Lagos, Nigeria, to Dakar, Senegal, delivering scalable, low-latency connectivity and unlocking cloud and content capacity across the region. Extending Phase3's Lagos-Accra corridor to Dakar, the land-based route achieves latency as low as 32ms and provides resilience for a region heavily impacted by the 2024 subsea cable disruptions.

Bridging networks through Benin, Togo, Ghana and now Senegal, the route provides redundancy for hyperscalers, content networks, financial institutions, governments and cloud providers, expands the Djoliba network from Ghana into Nigeria, and lays groundwork for Ikasira, Sonatel's next-generation regional platform. It offers local access to cloud zones such as AWS Wavelength, hosted by Sonatel in Dakar, framed by the partners as being about data sovereignty and cloud transformation. El Hadji Maty Sene, MD of Sonatel Wholesale and International, said Dakar is emerging as a strategic connectivity hub for West Africa.
