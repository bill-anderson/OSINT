# Sonatel Commissions 2Africa Submarine Cable, Africa's Largest Connectivity Project

Tech Africa News - 23 February 2026, by Jennifer Onyeagoro

Sonatel has announced the operational commissioning of the 2Africa submarine cable, described as the largest and most efficient connectivity infrastructure deployed on the continent. Originally launched in 2020 and commissioned in December 2025, the 45,000 km system connects 33 countries across Africa, Europe and Asia. It carries 16 fibre pairs (versus 2-8 in previous systems) and a nominal capacity of 180 Tbits, roughly ten times prior cables, and is the first to connect Europe to all African coasts.

The project follows a consortium model uniting hyperscalers, international operators and ISPs, and features open access and carrier-neutral landing stations. As part of the Orange Group, Sonatel is the project owner of the Senegal landing station, overseeing construction, land backhaul operation and technical management. Sonatel says 2Africa positions Senegal as a critical hub for global internet traffic.
