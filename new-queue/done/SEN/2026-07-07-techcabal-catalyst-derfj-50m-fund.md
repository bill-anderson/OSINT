# Why Senegal launched a $50 million startup fund (Catalyst DER/FJ)

TechCabal - 7 July 2026, by Lina Kacyem

On 20 June, at VivaTech in Paris, Aida Mbodji, Delegue General of Senegal's Delegation a l'Entrepreneuriat Rapide des Femmes et des Jeunes (DER/FJ), announced the creation of the Catalyst DER/FJ fund: a $50 million vehicle dedicated to financing innovative Senegalese startups at pre-seed and seed stages. The announcement showcased Senegal's growing startup ecosystem, with 15 startups (including Lafricamobile, Andakia, Baamtu, SenITI) spanning fintech, healthtech and cybersecurity.

The event also produced a strategic partnership between DER/FJ and Wave Senegal to set up a local startup acceleration programme and decentralise support for digital innovation across Senegal. DER/FJ (created by presidential decree in 2017) already disburses smaller amounts digitally via Orange Money and Wave mobile-money wallets, reaching founders across Senegal's 552 communes. Larger tickets route through partner financial institutions (BNDE, Pamecas, CMS, La Banque Agricole). Tickets are described as roughly 50,000-60,000 euros, designed as leverage toward later rounds.
