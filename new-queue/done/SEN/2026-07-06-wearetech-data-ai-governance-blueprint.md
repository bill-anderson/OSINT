# Senegal Elevates Data and AI as Core Pillars of Public Governance Reform

We Are Tech Africa - 6 July 2026, by Samira Njoya

Senegal is developing a national blueprint to use data and artificial intelligence to modernise public administration and improve policy effectiveness, focusing on priority sectors including health, agriculture, transport and taxation. The Ministry of Telecommunications and Digital Economy convened public institutions, private sector actors, universities and technical partners on 1-2 July, in collaboration with Smart Africa and Germany's GIZ, to identify priority data and AI use cases.

The discussions feed into a national "Blueprint for Value Creation Through Data" supporting the New Deal Technologique. Working groups identified health, agriculture, transport, education, taxation, territorial administration, security and public services as key areas for AI deployment. Senegal also plans a governance framework enabling public administrations to share data securely while ensuring data quality, interoperability and protection, reducing fragmented databases. Data sovereignty remains a central pillar, alongside national infrastructure for data hosting, digital identity and public-service interoperability.
