# Senegal launches digital government portal to ease access to public services

Biometric Update - 27 March 2026, by Ayang Macdonald

Senegalese authorities say a digital government portal enabling access to a wide range of public services has gone operational. The presidency described the e-Senegal platform as a one-stop shop for administrative services, aimed at reducing the cost and time of physical transactions. Citizens can complete procedures including passport applications, business creation, police clearance certificates and certificates of nationality, and track their online applications.

The platform, launched on 24 March by Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko, is a major implementation phase of the New Deal Technologique. Alongside the portal, a national sovereignty cloud infrastructure for data storage was unveiled, together with a unified digital payment system for government payments (taxes, customs duties) and a platform for consular services. Strategy objectives include at least 95 percent internet connectivity and digitising 90 percent of administrative procedures by 2030.
