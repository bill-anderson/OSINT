# Senegal's ARTP Advances Digital Transformation to Strengthen Regulatory Ecosystem

Tech Africa News - 28 January 2026, by Jennifer Onyeagoro

The Authority for Regulation of Telecommunications and Posts (ARTP) of Senegal has accelerated its digital transformation, unveiling digital tools to modernise regulatory processes and enhance transparency across the telecommunications ecosystem. The announcement was made during a stakeholder meeting on 22 January 2026, bringing together government, operators, service providers, universities, startups and consumer associations. The initiative emphasises digital sovereignty as a key priority and aligns with the New Technological Deal.

Eight platforms have been deployed by ARTP's technical teams: SUNUCARTO (national map of digital infrastructure), RESEAU (quality-of-experience monitoring), CATALOGUE (operator offers), DMARKET (rate monitoring), NUMFLEX (national number portability), CLAIMS (unified complaints management), FSVA (value-added service providers), and VCN (ship checks). ARTP plans to strengthen data governance, integrate advanced analytics and automation, and deploy new interoperable platforms.
