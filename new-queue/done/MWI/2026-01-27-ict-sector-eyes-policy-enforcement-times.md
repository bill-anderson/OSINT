# ICT sector eyes policy enforcement

The ICT sector is expected to shift from pilot projects to policy enforcement in 2026, focused on clearing the **100,000-plus passport backlog** and fully operationalising government digital systems. Former ICTAM president Bram Fudzulani said the Department of e-Government faces immense pressure to onboard all major MDAs onto the **Enterprise Service Bus** for interoperability, and that "by mid-2026, the Digitalisation Policy must move from a document on a shelf to a lived reality where driver's licence renewals and national ID applications and payments are fully online."

He proposed zero-rating traffic to official government portals ("if it costs zero data to apply for a national ID online, uptake will happen naturally") and tying principal secretaries' appraisals to the Digitalisation Policy. Current ICTAM President Clarence Gama urged the two main operators, TNM and Airtel, to prioritise quality of service, and called for removing VAT/import duties on entry-level smartphones, finalising local-assembly incentives, and AI innovation hubs for voice-based local-language interfaces.

In 2025 the sector recorded moderate growth: over 13 million mobile connections (>60% of population), internet usage below 20%. Milestones noted: the Public Sector Reforms Information Management System (Dec 2025) and the **Malawi National Single Window** (May 2025) integrating trade agencies.
