# Airtel bets on Malawi's digital future with new STEM scholarships

Airtel Africa Foundation has launched the **Nzeru Fund**, a technology scholarship programme sponsoring **25 undergraduate students** from low-income households to study science, technology, engineering and mathematics (STEM) at the Malawi University of Science and Technology (MUST). High-performing first-year students in IT, computer science, software engineering, data science, artificial intelligence and cybersecurity will receive full financial support for the duration of their studies at MUST's Thyolo and Blantyre campuses.

Airtel Malawi Managing Director Aashish Dutt said the initiative reflects long-term investment in education: "The Nzeru Fund represents our belief that talent should never be limited by background." Beyond tuition, the package includes a monthly stipend, accommodation, data bundles and a one-off pocket Wi-Fi device. MUST Vice-Chancellor Professor Mauakowa Malata welcomed the partnership, noting financial constraints remain a major obstacle for capable students. The fund forms part of Airtel Africa Foundation's continental strategy to nurture digital skills.
