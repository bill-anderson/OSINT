# Chiyamwaka hails 5G rollout as key driver of Malawi 2063 vision

Principal Secretary in the Ministry of Information and Digitalisation, Baldwin Chiyamwaka, described **Telekom Networks Malawi's (TNM) launch of its 5G network** (at the Bingu International Convention Centre, Lilongwe) as a strategic leap toward the Malawi 2063 vision. He urged extending 5G to Blantyre, Mzuzu, Zomba and Liwonde as part of the Malawi Implementation Plan I (MIP-1), the first 10-year phase of the 2063 agenda.

TNM Chief Commercial Officer Amon Jere said the 5G network was "built and implemented by some of the brightest Malawian minds at TNM," crediting strategic partner **Huawei** for technical support. The rollout brings faster speeds for streaming, virtual meetings, online education and mobile transactions, with an upgraded TNM SmartApp and 5G eSIM support; TNM was the first operator to pilot 5G in Malawi. (Rival Airtel Malawi later launched its own 5G in November 2025.)
