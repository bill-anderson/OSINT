# Malawi devises operational rules for national data exchange platform

Malawi has proposed a **Government Data Sharing Regulation** to provide operational rules for the National Data Exchange Platform (also called **Boma Lathu**), currently under implementation. ICT Minister Shadric Namalomba (speaking in China during a meeting with Huawei) noted that while Malawi had already established the National Data Centre, enacted the Data Protection Act (2024) and developed Boma Lathu, government institutions still relied on informal methods — email, USB drives, paper — to share citizen information; a binding regulation was "the missing piece."

The regulation will entrench operational safeguards: all data requests routed securely through the **National Data Centre**; encryption for all transfers and storage; a common digital standard for seamless system-to-system communication; and approved exchanges governed by binding **Data Sharing Agreements** with usage limits and full audit trails. Alongside the World Bank-funded **DMAP**, the National Data Centre is being upgraded to an **AI-ready state**, and Namalomba said a **US$180 million ICT investment plan** discussed with Huawei would further strengthen the systems powering Boma Lathu.
