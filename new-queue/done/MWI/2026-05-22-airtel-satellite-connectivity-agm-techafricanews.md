# Airtel Malawi Eyes Satellite Connectivity as It Boosts Network Expansion Strategy

Airtel Malawi Plc has signaled growing interest in **satellite connectivity** to strengthen network resilience and expand digital access in hard-to-reach areas, alongside continued terrestrial network scaling. Speaking at the company's **27th AGM (Mzuzu, 15 May 2026)**, Chairperson Kayisi Sadala described 2025 as a year of disciplined execution; MD Aashish Dutt reaffirmed focus on network resilience and alignment with Vision 2063.

Financials: **34% rise in gross revenue**, EBITDA up 29.4%, profit before tax up 54.9%; shareholders approved a **MK38.5 billion dividend** (75% increase, MK3.50/share) for FY2025. Operationally, the company invested **MK50.8 billion** in network expansion, rolling out **58 new sites** and extending its **5G footprint to 111 sites across 10 districts**. Satellite connectivity was framed as a new dimension complementing terrestrial networks.
