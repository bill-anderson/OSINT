# Malawi unveils digital payments roadmap

The Reserve Bank of Malawi (RBM) has unveiled a new **National Payments Systems Vision and Strategy Framework (2026–2030)**, a roadmap to modernise the financial ecosystem, launched in Lilongwe. Deputy Governor (Operations) Kisu Simwaka framed it as a national commitment to a payments system supporting inclusive economic growth and resilience.

The strategy seeks to expand infrastructure and improve affordability for low-income users while strengthening resilience against power and connectivity failures. It recalls prior milestones — the Malawi Interbank Transfer and Settlement System (2002) and the **National Switch (2015)** linking banks, mobile money and ATMs — while acknowledging persistent gaps in rural areas. The framework prioritises enhanced **cybersecurity**, fintech innovation to accelerate the transition from cash, regulatory modernisation, and closer **regional integration** to align with continental payment systems. (Companion coverage reports a planned **CBDC Proof of Concept by 2030** and migration to ISO 20022.)
