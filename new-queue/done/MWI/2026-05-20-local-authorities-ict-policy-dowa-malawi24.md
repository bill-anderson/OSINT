# NLGFC, E-Government sensitizes Dowa Council on harmonized ICT policy

The National Local Government Finance Committee (NLGFC), in collaboration with the Department of E-Government under the Ministry of Information and Communications Technology (the policy custodian), is rolling out a new ICT policy for Local Authorities. There had been no dedicated ICT policy for Local Authorities — only the national National Digitalization Policy (2023) — despite significant issues persisting at council level that remained unaddressed. A Local Authorities ICT policy aligned with the 2023 Digitalization Policy was developed, accompanied by ICT standards to govern ICT use in councils.

Presenting the policy to the Dowa District Council, Ministry Economist Etakho Mitinda Massah said the sensitization follows document-validation sessions held on 4 May 2026, during which stakeholders reviewed and gave final input on the draft ICT Governance framework. The sensitization is intended to orient Local Authorities on the provisions and prepare them for implementation and adoption.

She said Local Authorities in Malawi operate without clear guiding principles or strategic direction in ICT. A 2022 situational analysis funded by the GESP project identified problems including poor support and innovation of ICT services, unavailability of ICT projects/programs in councils, an unconducive ICT establishment at council level, and exclusion of ICT expertise in procurement of ICT assets.
