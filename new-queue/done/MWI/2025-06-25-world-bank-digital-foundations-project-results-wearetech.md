# Malawi Sees Digital Gains as 8.5 Million People Benefit from High-Speed, Affordable Internet

The **Malawi Digital Foundations Project** (2017–2024, World Bank-supported) expanded high-speed, affordable internet access, benefiting **8.5 million citizens** per a World Bank results brief published 23 June 2025 ("Digitalizing Malawi to Improve Access to Education, Public Services, and Income Opportunities"). It brought nearly **7 million new users online** and improved speeds/affordability for 3 million existing users. Key achievements: a new **national data center**, the **Bomalathu data exchange platform**, connectivity to 600+ government sites, and the wholesale price of bandwidth dropping from **$460/Mbit/s to below $10/Mbit/s**.

Additionally, 83,000 students across 81 higher institutions gained internet via the research/education network (MAREN); 19,000 youths (including 10,000 women) received digital-literacy/entrepreneurship training. The second phase — the **Digital Malawi Acceleration Project** (launched November 2024, $72.4m World Bank IDA loan) — aims to connect 2,000 schools, expand digital ID coverage, and help low-income households acquire affordable devices. Internet users per 100 rose from 17.6 (2016) to 38.3 (mid-2022).
