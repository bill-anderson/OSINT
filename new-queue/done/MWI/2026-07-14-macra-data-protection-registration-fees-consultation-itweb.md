# Malawi advances data protection framework

MACRA is consulting stakeholders on **draft data protection regulations** proposing annual registration fees for certain data controllers and processors based on annual turnover. The consultation (Mount Soche Hotel, Blantyre, Monday) forms part of MACRA's mandate as Malawi's Data Protection Authority (DPA). Proposed fees range from approximately **$29 for small businesses to more than $4,000 for large organisations** (turnover-based).

Harold Msusa, Principal Secretary in the Ministry of ICT, said government is committed to a data protection regime that is "transparent, inclusive and responsive to the needs of both citizens and businesses," with larger organisations contributing proportionately while avoiding undue burden on smaller enterprises. DPA head of data protection Dan Chiwoni said the Authority has developed an implementation framework balancing data privacy, innovation and growth. The regulations come as Malawi expands online financial services, digital identity, e-government and AI applications.

(Same MACRA consultation event as the Malawi24 "MACRA targets stronger data protection compliance" (2026-07-16) report; the MWK figures there — K50,000 to K7m — correspond to these USD amounts.)
