# Connecting farmer data

An analytical piece on fragmentation of Malawian farmer data across siloed systems. Farmers in 14 districts interact with multiple platforms — the meteorological department's **PICSA** (climate services), **One Acre Fund**, the government **Farm Input Subsidy Programme (FISP)** (fertiliser at K10,000/bag via the Smallholder Farmers Fertiliser Revolving Fund), and the Agricultural Commodity Exchange's **Zaulimi** app (market info, trade facilitation, warehouse receipts). These require quality user data (farmer IDCs, field IDs) stored across providers whose databases are **not interoperable**, forcing farmers to log into different apps and leaving them without control of their data.

The piece argues the UNDP-led **National Registration and Identification System (NRIS)** offers a foundation for a sectoral **farmer ID** layer linking these systems, and that the **Data Protection Act 2024** (with MACRA as designated Data Protection Authority; registration required for anyone processing data on >10,000 people) gives farmers a say in data use. It highlights the Commonwealth-proposed **National Agricultural Data Infrastructure (NAGDI)** framework as an approach to delivering agricultural-sector DPI, harmonising individual data systems through an interoperable national data infrastructure.
