# Hundreds sign MACRA internet petition

Hundreds of Malawians have signed an online petition calling on the Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) to review internet prices and investigate mobile data billing practices. The petition, started by Stanley Mombera, attracted 885 verified signatures within about a day of its launch at the time of publication; organisers target at least 1,000 signatures. The campaign gained momentum after being shared on X: "Petition to the Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) – Sign the Petition!"

Addressed to MACRA's Director General and Board, the petition urges the regulator to conduct an independent and transparent review of internet pricing across all licensed telecommunications operators. It also calls on MACRA to investigate internet data billing systems and bundle consumption to ensure subscribers are billed fairly and accurately.

The organisers want the regulator to publish the findings of its investigations, explain how internet billing and data usage are measured, strengthen consumer protection regulations and promote policies that make internet services more affordable and accessible. The petition argues affordable and reliable internet is essential for education, entrepreneurship, innovation, remote work, healthcare, financial inclusion and access to information.

The petition argues that rising internet costs, concerns over how quickly data bundles are depleted (including on some unlimited packages), and what it describes as limited transparency in data consumption are making internet access increasingly difficult for many Malawians — slowing the country's digital transformation and creating barriers for students, software developers, researchers, businesses and other citizens. Efforts to obtain a comment from MACRA were unsuccessful by the time of publication. Organisers continue to collect signatures and intend to present the petition to the regulator.
