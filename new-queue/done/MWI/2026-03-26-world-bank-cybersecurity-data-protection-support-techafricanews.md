# Malawi Secures World Bank Backing to Strengthen Cybersecurity and Data Protection Frameworks

The Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) has secured renewed World Bank support to strengthen data protection and cybersecurity. Announced at a meeting in Blantyre, Tim Kelly (Team Leader of the World Bank delegation) outlined plans for technical assistance and capacity building focused on securing critical infrastructure, enhancing legal and regulatory frameworks, strengthening data protection systems, and improving regional cybersecurity capabilities.

MACRA highlighted ongoing efforts to establish the **Data Protection Authority (DPA)** and the **Malawi Computer Emergency Response Team (MwCERT)** as **independent institutions** — both currently operate under the regulator, with the transition aimed at improving governance, efficiency and national cybersecurity readiness. Director General Mayamiko Nkoloma welcomed the involvement and pointed to the **Connect-a-School project** under the Universal Service Fund, expanding digital access in schools through computer equipment and connectivity.
