The NCC issued a landing permit to Amazon-backed Kuiper Systems LLC for its Project Kuiper satellite constellation, allowing it to beam satellite signals over Nigerian territory beginning in 2026 — a step in the expansion of low Earth orbit satellite connectivity (challenging Starlink's dominance). Under the permit, Kuiper is authorised to deploy up to 3,236 non-geostationary LEO satellites using Ka-band frequencies, supporting fixed satellite services, mobile satellite services and earth stations. The NCC said the permit aligns with global best practices for regulating satellite communications.
