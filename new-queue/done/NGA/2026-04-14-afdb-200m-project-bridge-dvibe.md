The African Development Bank (AfDB) approved a $200 million loan towards Nigeria's Digital Value Chain Infrastructure for Boosting Employment (D-VIBE) project — also known as Project Bridge — to deploy 90,000km of open-access fibre nationwide, expand digital skills and drive job creation. It will extend Nigeria's national fibre backbone from around 30,000km to about 120,000km, connecting all 774 LGAs (schools, health facilities, agro-industrial zones, rural communities, commercial hubs) and establishing cross-border links with Benin, Cameroon, Niger and Chad. The AfDB loan is part of an $800 million sovereign financing package ($500m World Bank, $100m EBRD, $200m AfDB); total project financing is estimated at $2 billion, including a €22 million EU grant.
