Nigeria's proposed Artificial Intelligence (AI) Bill promises a regulatory foundation for AI in Africa's largest economy, but analysis reveals a law caught between protecting against risks (algorithmic bias, data misuse) and harnessing AI as a growth engine. The bill mirrors the EU AI Act's language for managing high-risk AI systems, but its structure tilts toward regulatory control with broad powers concentrated in government agencies that "lack proven capacity." Anda Usman (co-founder/CEO of Datum Africa): "Nigeria risks making the same mistake we made with fintech over-regulation... When you regulate too early and overdo it, you become a consumer of other people's technology rather than a producer." Comparisons drawn with the Alliance4AI framework.
