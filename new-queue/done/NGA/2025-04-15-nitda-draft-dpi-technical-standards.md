Nigeria's plans to reform public service delivery through digital public infrastructure reached a milestone, with NITDA launching draft technical standards for DPI. The draft sets out a roadmap for integration of digital identity and payment systems and data exchange frameworks into a wider DPI ecosystem. NITDA called for feedback through a public consultation (deadline May 8).

The framework is intended to provide a government-wide approach to public service delivery, including citizen-centric digital services by the private sector; open-source solution development will be supported. It would stand up a national implementation office, the Nigerian Digital Public Infrastructure Centre (Ng-DPIC). Other objectives: enabling interoperability, ensuring data security and digital privacy in line with international standards, defining performance benchmarks, and standardizing testing procedures. NITDA discussed potential sovereign digital infrastructure investment by Google, SGgrow and JICA, and has been working with NIMC to develop PKI and DPI.
