# Alausa, Kawu, Pedro, others lead charge for Nigerian Education Data Initiative

Vanguard News, 12 February 2025 — Emmanuel Okogba

Nigeria has taken a significant stride towards transforming its education sector with the inauguration of the Nigerian Education Data Initiative (NEDI) committee.

Dr. Tunji Alausa, the Honourable Minister of Education, will serve as chairman of the Implementation Committee, which includes 24 other members drawn from various ministries and industries.

Abubakar Isah of the Data and Digitalisation Unit (DDU) in the Ministry will serve as Pro-Coordinator, while Bode Pedro, a recognized expert in digital transformation and entrepreneurship, will serve as Digital Advisor.

Other notable members of the committee include Kwara State Commissioner of Education and Chairperson of the Nigeria Education Commissioners Forum, Hajia Sa'adatu Modibbo Kawu; UNESCO Representative, Oyebukola Tomori-Adeleye; Registrar of the Medical and Dental Council of Nigeria (MDCN), Dr. Fatima Kyari; Director of Education and Training at the Pharmaceutical Council of Nigeria, Dr. Idoko Anthony; Director of IT at NIMC, Lanre Yusuf; Head of Investments, NELFUND, Ms. Oluwatosin Sanusi; Assist Director of the Joint Admission and Matriculation Board (JAMB), Abdullahi Asha amongst others.

"For too long, the absence of a unified, comprehensive data framework has hindered our ability to allocate resources and make informed decisions effectively," said Dr. Alausa, emphasizing the critical need for the initiative.

"NEDI will provide this vital foundation, consolidating all critical education data in Nigeria into a harmonized databank and unified platform. This is more than just information; it's a catalyst for development, enabling us to deliver quality education that meets the aspirations of every Nigerian child."

Pedro, a recognized expert in digital transformation and founder of Casava, a leading microinsurance company, has demonstrated his ability to leverage technology for impactful solutions.

"I am deeply honored to contribute to this transformative project," Pedro commented in a LinkedIn post about his appointment. "Education is the cornerstone of national development, and by harnessing the power of data and technology, we can create a more inclusive and effective education system for all Nigerians. With reliable data, we can empower EdTech startups, digitize education services, and position Nigeria as a global leader in talent and innovation. This initiative is about ensuring that every Nigerian child can aspire, thrive, and prosper, building a nation that leads the emerging markets of the future." he added.

The launch of the NEDI committee signals a decisive step in Nigeria's journey to modernize its education sector and establish a centralized, real-time data ecosystem, empowering policymakers, educators, and stakeholders with the insights to make informed decisions and drive improved educational outcomes nationwide.

The framework will gather data on funding sources, university details, student records, Teacher and parent information, examination results, school locations, grading systems, and infrastructure, including essential facilities like ICT resources, libraries, laboratories, and classrooms. This comprehensive data will inform future investments, especially in areas that are currently underserved.
