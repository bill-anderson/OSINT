Nigeria began the financial advisory process to deploy 90,000km of fibre under the $2 billion Building Resilient Digital Infrastructure for Growth (BRIDGE) Project, supported by the World Bank ($500 million approved). Per a financing agreement (World Bank, dated January 20, 2026), the government will hold a 49% (minority) stake in a majority privately-owned and managed Project Company (SPV) that will oversee deployment across Nigeria's 774 local government areas. The SPV had not yet been formed; a transaction/financial advisory firm selection was near completion. The government aims to break ground in 2026. Signed under terms with the International Development Association (IDA).
