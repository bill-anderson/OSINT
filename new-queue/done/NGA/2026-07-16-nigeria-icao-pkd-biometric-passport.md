Nigeria completed integration with the International Civil Aviation Organization's (ICAO) Public Key Directory (PKD). Per the Nigeria Immigration Service (NIS), Nigeria imported all its Country Signing Certificate Authorities (CSCAs) into the ICAO PKD, addressing authentication challenges for passport holders. The ICAO PKD is a central global repository (111 members) where countries exchange digital certificates to authenticate e-passports. NIS took part in a ceremony at ICAO HQ in Montreal. The achievement establishes "a chain of trust" so Nigerian passports can be verified seamlessly by border control in all ICAO PKD member states — transitioning from "machine-readable" to cryptographically verifiable passports.
