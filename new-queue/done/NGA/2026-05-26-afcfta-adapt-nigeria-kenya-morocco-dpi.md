The African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) Secretariat chose Kenya, Morocco and Nigeria as the first countries to implement the Africa Digital Access and Public Infrastructure for Trade (ADAPT) initiative — one of the continent's most ambitious attempts to build interoperable digital public infrastructure across national borders. ADAPT seeks to create shared digital rails for identity, payments and trusted data exchange to support the AfCFTA. The three pilots were selected via a two-stage evaluation (political commitment, regulatory alignment, technical capacity, private-sector engagement, maturity of national digital systems). Launched November 2025; IOTA Foundation (chair Dominik Schiener) is a partner.
