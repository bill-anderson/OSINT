On September 20, 2025, on the sidelines of UNGA80, Minister Dr Bosun Tijani launched the Nigerian Atlas for Languages & AI at Scale (N-ATLAS), an open-source facility mapping Nigeria's linguistic diversity with AI. N-ATLAS v1 is a multilingual, multimodal large language model supporting Yoruba, Hausa, Igbo and Nigerian-accented English — built on Meta's Llama-3 8B architecture and fine-tuned with over 400 million tokens of multilingual instruction data. Powered by Awarri Technologies with NITDA and the National Centre for Artificial Intelligence and Robotics (NCAIR). Nigeria has more than 500 native languages; the model aims to make apps, chatbots and government services usable in local languages.
