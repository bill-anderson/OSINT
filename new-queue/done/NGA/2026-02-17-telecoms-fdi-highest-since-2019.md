FDI into Nigeria's telecommunications sector rebounded, driven by pricing reforms, naira stability and renewed investor confidence. In the first nine months of 2025, the sector attracted $392.91 million in foreign investment — its strongest since 2019 ($922.88 million over the same period), per National Bureau of Statistics data. Investment had fallen to an all-time low of $107.46 million in 2021 amid price controls and FX volatility. The rebound was driven by the January 2025 50% tariff hikes and improved naira stability. The sector is heavily dependent on FX for network equipment imports.
