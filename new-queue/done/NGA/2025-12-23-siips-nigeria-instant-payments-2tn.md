Instant payment systems across Africa processed transactions worth nearly $2 trillion in 2024, per the State of Inclusive Instant Payment Systems (SIIPS) 2025 Report by the AfricaNenda Foundation with the World Bank and UNECA. Nigeria's Instant Payments (NIP) is the first system in Africa to achieve "mature inclusivity," setting a benchmark. In total, 36 instant payment systems are now live across 31 African countries (five launched in the past year), collectively processing 64 billion transactions valued at nearly $2 trillion in 2024. AfricaNenda CEO Dr Robert Ochola cited clear progress; the World Bank's Niraj Verma noted significant gaps remain.
