The Government of Sierra Leone launched a Digital Skills Training Programme aimed at equipping civil servants with digital competencies. Launched on Friday (10 July 2026) by MoCTI in partnership with the Ministry of Public Administration and Political Affairs (MoPAPA), the Civil Service Training College (CSTC), and the Human Resource Management Office (HRMO), it will train 1,000 civil servants in its first year.

The initiative forms part of the broader Digital Skills Development and Gig Economy Pipeline (2025–2026), which targets 3,000 civil servants in the medium term and at least 5,000 over the longer term. Training for the first cohort was scheduled to begin on 17 July at the Public Service Training College in Freetown, over an eight-to-nine-month rollout.

The programme addresses gaps in MoCTI's Digital Skills Market Study Report (2025), which highlighted limited practical digital skills among public sector workers, particularly outside Freetown. The curriculum covers six priority areas: basic digital literacy and productivity tools, data management and reporting, digital communication platforms, AI and automation, cybersecurity and data protection, and digital leadership and innovation.

The programme supports two pillars of President Bio's second-term Big Five Game Changers — Technology and Infrastructure, and Transformation of the Public Service architecture (Medium-Term National Development Plan 2024–2030).

(A related partnership agreement to train the 1,000 civil servants across 29 MDAs via the Civil Service Training College was reported by TechAfrica News, 2026-06-26.)
