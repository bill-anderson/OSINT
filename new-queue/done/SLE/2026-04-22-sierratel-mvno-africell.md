The government of Sierra Leone has approved the appointment of private sector operator Africell Sierra Leone as its investment partner for the relaunch of troubled state-owned telecommunications company Sierratel. Africell will partner with Sierratel under an MVNO (Mobile Virtual Network Operator) model, allowing Sierratel to deliver services by leveraging existing network infrastructure rather than building its own full-scale network.

Previous privatisation attempts failed after investors pulled out, citing high upgrade costs, staff-related liabilities and strong competition. Technology and Innovation Minister Salima Bah stressed this should not be seen as a privatisation but as a strategic repositioning; Sierratel will keep its brand and target underserved segments including students and young professionals.

Sierratel owes about US$6.3 million in employee-related obligations and carries around US$35 million in external debt to two banks. The MVNO model operates on a revenue-sharing basis with Africell and allows Sierratel to avoid heavy capital expenditure, enabling relaunch of voice, data and mobile money services at an unspecified date.

The regulator says Africell's network covers just over 92% of the population for 2G, 77.48% for 3G and 61.72% for 4G; Africell held a 54.51% market share at the end of 2024.

(The same MVNO announcement was made at the MoICE weekly press conference of 2026-04-22.)
