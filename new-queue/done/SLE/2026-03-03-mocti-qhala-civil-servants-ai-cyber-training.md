MoCTI, in collaboration with the Directorate of Science, Technology and Innovation (DSTI), the National Cybersecurity Coordination Centre (NC3), and Qhala, launched a high-level leadership training on Cybersecurity, Artificial Intelligence, and Emerging Technologies. The training follows the strategic partnership signed between the Ministry and Qhala on September 30th 2025 on the sidelines of the 80th UN General Assembly (UNGA) during the Sierra Leone Innovation Showcase.

Held at the Miatta Conference Hall, over 50 senior leaders from government ministries, departments and agencies — Directors, senior managers, senior security officials and media — engaged with experts from DSTI and Qhala. The whole-of-government approach aims to ensure that as Sierra Leone digitizes, it does so with "strong digital armor to protect citizen data and institutional integrity."

Sessions covered Digital Literacy for public-sector leadership, emerging technologies and their national security implications (DSTI), and AI opportunities, risks and data foundations for government (Qhala). Objectives included applying AI to accelerate the Medium Term National Development Plan (MTNDP) Big Five Game Changers.
