President Dr. Julius Maada Bio officially commissioned the new headquarters of the National Telecommunications Authority (NatCA), at Southridge, Freetown. The modern facility accommodates both NatCA and its supervising body, the Ministry of Communication, Technology and Innovation (MoCTI), and houses critical national assets including the National Data Centre and the Network Operations and Regulatory Authority.

The President noted that while the project was initiated by a previous administration, his government completed it despite global economic challenges and supply chain disruptions. Highlighting digital sovereignty, he stressed the new data centre will play a vital role in protecting government information and preserving national dignity. He acknowledged the digital divide between rural and urban communities and the high cost of data.

Minister Salima Bah said co-locating NatCA and MoCTI will enhance coordination, and the national data centre will ensure secure storage and management of government information. She noted "14 out of 16 districts now connected by fiber." NatCA Director General Amara Brewah disclosed that construction of the 10-storey facility began in 2015; it includes a 200-seat auditorium and support infrastructure.
