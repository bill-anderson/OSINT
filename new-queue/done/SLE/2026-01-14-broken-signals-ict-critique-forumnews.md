NOTE: Partisan opinion/analysis column (APC-aligned "The Sierra Leone We Deserve" series, by Jarrah Kawusu-Konte). Admissible as analysis; cites primary reports. Treat framing as an attributed critical view, not neutral fact.

The piece critiques the 2021 "Smart Learning Sierra Leone" programme (MBSSE) that promised to connect secondary schools, universities and even school buses ("Wi-Fi-on-wheels"). Three years on, connectivity largely failed: teachers report modems that "lasted about two months"; Njala University and Fourah Bay College received only partial connectivity to limited administrative offices; campuses in Makeni, Kenema and Port Loko report unreliable, expensive internet compounded by electricity shortages. A NATCOM engineer confirmed the buses used commercial 4G routers on short-term prepaid SIMs with no renewal budget.

Cited data points:
- Mobile data prices: 1GB ≈ Le 5,000–7,000 (April 2018) vs Le 18,000–25,000 (2025–2026, roughly tripled).
- Sierra Leone ranks among the top 5 most expensive countries for mobile data in Sub-Saharan Africa (2024 Alliance for Affordable Internet / A4AI report), behind Ghana, Nigeria and Liberia.
- A 2023 UNESCO-UNICEF Education Connectivity Assessment found 71% of public secondary schools lack reliable internet, only 8% of students surveyed have used online learning platforms, and 63% of rural teachers received no digital skills training.

The author frames the failures as a governance culture of "over-promising and under-delivering" and calls for an audit of government ICT education programs since 2018 and mandatory NATCOM reporting on telecom revenues and universal access fund expenditures.
