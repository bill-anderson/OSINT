Key figures reported: Orange Sierra Leone inaugurated a €23 million ($26.7 million) backup datacenter in Bo on 29 November 2025, built as an identical replica of its main datacenter in Freetown. The site functions as a disaster-recovery hub. Orange also invested $50 million to modernise its nationwide network, now fully 4G- and 5G-compatible. Orange Sierra Leone controls 51% of the mobile market with 3.04 million subscribers (as of December 2024), competing with Africell and QCell.

The company said the Bo centre extends digital infrastructure to rural and regional communities in the southern and eastern corridors toward Makeni, Kono and Kenema, improving connectivity in underserved areas. The operator built the datacenter as Freetown remains exposed to landslides (such as the 2017 event) and recurrent flooding.

Communications Minister Salima Bah stressed the importance of uninterrupted service after a brief outage in August 2024. Beyond the datacenter, Orange Sierra Leone operates 616 telecom sites nationwide (including 40 recently deployed), powering 70% of those sites with green-energy sources.

(This report covers the same Orange Bo data-centre opening as the State House release of 2025-11-29; retained for its concrete investment and market-share figures. Both relate to the DCD item of 2025-12-08 already held.)
