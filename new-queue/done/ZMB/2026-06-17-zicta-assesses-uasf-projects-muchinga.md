The Zambia Information and Communications Technology Authority (ZICTA) reported on its ongoing Board assessment tour of Universal Access and Service Fund (UASF) projects across Muchinga, Northern, and Luapula Provinces. The tour is focused on evaluating how ICT infrastructure projects are helping to bridge the connectivity gap in unserved and underserved communities.

During the second day, the delegation visited Chinsali Special School, where ZICTA had previously made an ICT donation in 2024 including 15 desktop computers (five equipped with JAWS screen-reading software for visually impaired users), a braille printer, a projector, a tablet, headsets, braille paper, and internet connectivity. The assessment highlights the impact of the UASF programme in strengthening digital inclusion, and underscores ZICTA's continued efforts to expand ICT access in rural and underserved areas.
