# Gabon Breaks Ground on Landmark Data Center in Nkok Special Economic Zone

The data center is expected to be operational in five months and will serve as a foundational pillar for a more resilient and modern digital ecosystem.

The groundbreaking ceremony for a new data center in Gabon's Nkok Special Economic Zone signals a significant step forward for the country's digital future. This ambitious project, supported by the Ministry of the Digital Economy and led by IT provider ST DIGITAL, is more than just an infrastructure development. It's a strategic move to promote digital independence and diversify an economy traditionally reliant on resource extraction. The data center is expected to be operational in five months and will serve as a foundational pillar for a more resilient and modern digital ecosystem.

This investment will revolutionize Gabon's business landscape by providing essential digital infrastructure. The data center will offer sovereign cloud services, ensuring that all sensitive data remains within the country's borders. This is a critical security measure in an age of increasing cyber threats. Businesses in key sectors like finance, healthcare, and education will be able to operate more securely and efficiently. For example, the data center will enable the expansion of telemedicine, e-learning platforms, and digital banking, fostering greater financial inclusion and access to public services. This new digital environment is designed to boost innovation and competitiveness, making Gabon an attractive destination for foreign investment.

The project is also a testament to Gabon's commitment to sustainable and responsible development. By being strategically located within the Nkok Special Economic Zone, the data center is poised to amplify economic activity and job creation. Moreover, ST DIGITAL's focus on energy efficiency and minimal environmental impact aligns with Gabon's broader sustainability goals. By fostering a knowledge-based economy and developing local IT expertise, the data center is paving the way for long-term growth. Ultimately, this initiative is not only an investment in Gabon's technological future but also a powerful example for other African nations looking to harness technology for inclusive and sustainable development.
