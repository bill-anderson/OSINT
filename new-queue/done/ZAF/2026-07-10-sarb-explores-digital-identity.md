The South African Reserve Bank (SARB) is exploring a formal digital identity system that could make it easier and safer for people to access financial services.

The Prudential Authority said that a trusted digital ID could simplify customer verification, cut fraud, and strengthen security across the financial sector.

It could also make it easier for consumers to open accounts and use digital financial services.

The central bank said that digital identity forms part of its broader strategy to modernise South Africa's financial system and support the growth of digital payments.

However, it stressed that any future framework would need to balance innovation with strong privacy and consumer protection safeguards.

“Part of the work that the SARB is doing on payments modernisation is to look at how one could have a formal digital identity and how that identity can be verified and utilised for different things. I think there is usefulness in that but there is what we can do to help those who want to formalise and there is then the illicit side of things where people will opt out on purpose.” Said Fundi Tshazibana, Prudential Authority CEO
