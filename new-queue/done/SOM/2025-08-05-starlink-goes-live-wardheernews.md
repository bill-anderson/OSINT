# Starlink goes live in Somalia four months after securing approval

WardheerNews, August 5, 2025 (syndicated; source: Technext)

Elon Musk's SpaceX has officially launched Starlink in Somalia, with the high-speed, low-latency service now available across the country. This comes barely four months after the company was granted a 10-year licence in April by Somalia's National Communications Authority (NCA). Technology Minister Mohamed Adam Moalim Ali praised the initiative as a step toward affordable internet for all Somalis.

Somalia has struggled with internet access for years, with only about 30% of the population online; infrastructure damage from decades of conflict has limited traditional broadband, and rural and nomadic communities remain cut off. Starlink's constellation of over 7,600 LEO satellites (as of mid-2025, expanding to 12,000+) orbits at about 550km, reducing latency to 25–60ms and delivering speeds up to 220 Mbps. The rollout follows years of negotiations dating to May 2023. To access the service, residents order a dish for around $349 with a monthly plan of approximately $120. Starlink plans 2026 upgrades offering higher speeds.
