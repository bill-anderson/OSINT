# AstaanConnect becomes Somalia's first official Starlink reseller

Mogadishu (HOL), Wednesday September 24, 2025

Somali media and technology firm Astaan, through its connectivity arm AstaanConnect, has become the country's first official reseller of Starlink, in a move expected to expand high-speed connectivity to underserved regions. The announcement comes weeks after Starlink launched nationwide in August, following regulatory approval from the National Communications Authority.

AstaanConnect said it will make subscriptions more accessible by offering local sales, installation and 24/7 technical support to households, businesses, schools, humanitarian organizations and government offices. Customers can choose from three kits — Standard, Performance and Mini. Subscription plans begin at $36.9 per month for residential users, while global mobile packages start at $309 per month. Payments are accepted in U.S. dollars or Somali shillings via EVC Plus, eDahab, bank transfers or cash.

Somalia's internet market has grown rapidly through private mobile operators such as Hormuud Telecom and Somtel, but access remains uneven. With internet penetration under 10 percent, analysts say most households may find Starlink unaffordable, leaving NGOs, government offices and diaspora-supported families as the main users. Government officials say the launch supports national digital reforms including the ICT Policy and Strategy (2019–2024) and the national ID rollout. Somalia is Starlink's 25th African market.
