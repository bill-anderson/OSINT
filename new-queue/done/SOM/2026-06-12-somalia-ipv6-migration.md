# Somalia Upgrades Digital Infrastructure to Bypass Global IPv4 Address Depletion

By Isaac K. Kassouwi — We Are Tech Africa, June 12, 2026

Somalia is accelerating its national migration to Internet Protocol version 6 (IPv6) to expand digital infrastructure capacity and support economic growth. The National Communications Authority (NCA) and the National IPv6 Center hosted a specialized technical workshop on Wednesday, June 10, uniting internet service providers, academic institutions and telecommunications experts to synchronize deployment strategies, raise institutional awareness and upgrade existing network infrastructures.

"Today, we do not plan only for the current needs of the internet, but also for those of future generations," said NCA Director General Mustafa Yasiin Sheikh. Somalia adopted a dedicated national IPv6 strategy in February 2025, which enabled creation of the National IPv6 Center located at Jamhuriya University — a research and training hub preparing experts in IPv6 deployment and network management. IPv4, launched in 1981 with about 4.3 billion addresses, reached its allocation limits in 2011; IPv6 provides up to 340 undecillion addresses, a durable solution for rising connectivity and IoT demand.
