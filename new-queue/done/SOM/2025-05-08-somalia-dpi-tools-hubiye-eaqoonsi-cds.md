# Somalia unveils DPI tools for biometric verification, ID issuance and service access

By Chris Burt — Biometric Update, May 8, 2025

Three foundational digital public infrastructure systems have been introduced by Somalia's National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA) and federal government: the HUBIYE identity verification platform, the CDS (Certificate Delivery System), and the eAqoonsi app, which provides a mobile device-based digital ID for online and offline services. HUBIYE uses email and SMS OTPs to collect consent for ID verification; the name derives from the Somali word "hubin" (to check or verify). NIRA's records include fingerprint and face biometrics used to issue a National Identification Number (NIN). The digital ID provided through eAqoonsi is legally equivalent to the national ID card.

HUBIYE will eventually be used by financial institutions for biometric identity verification as part of KYC processes; government and private-sector providers were directed by Somalia's PM to begin integrating the national biometric ID card in April. "The systems — entirely developed by Somali engineers at NIRA — reflect strong national ownership and growing technical capacity," said NIRA Director General Abdiwali Ali Abdulle (Timacadde).

Development was supported by the World Bank's $75 million Scaled-UP project, whose deliverables include biometric deduplication, secure data storage, credential issuance, grievance redress and reliable authentication for financial service providers and social assistance programs. The tools were launched at an event in Mogadishu attended by government, central bank and Somali Bankers Association officials. Somalia also announced a phased expansion of registration across the country and to the diaspora.
