# Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI): Somalia Risks Repeating Old Mistakes in a Digital Form

By Hassan Mohamed Oshen — WardheerNews, April 28, 2026 (analysis/opinion)

Somalia is moving toward digital systems, but the risk is no longer that Somalia will fail to digitize — it is that Somalia will digitize the same fragmentation that already exists. At the recent National Identification Conference in Mogadishu, President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud said, "Let us embrace modern systems; our nation cannot remain where it was 35 years ago." The harder question is what kind of digital systems are being built and whether they can work together.

**Problem 1 — Fragmentation.** Somalia's digital landscape is active but not coordinated. Education platforms operate without connection to identity systems, health data is collected but not easily shared, and institutional records remain isolated. Data is duplicated, records become inconsistent, and national-level visibility remains limited. This is a failure of structure, not ambition.

**Problem 2 — Weak coordination.** Digital infrastructure is treated as a technical problem when it is a governance problem: systems require clarity in roles, standards for interoperability and accountability. Institutions, not technology, create alignment.

**Problem 3 — The trust deficit.** The issue is often not whether data exists but whether it can be believed and verified independently. The author cites the national electronic visa system, which shortly after deployment suffered a major breach exposing sensitive personal data of tens of thousands of applicants, including passport and identity information — a structural failure of verification, access control and data protection, not merely a cybersecurity lapse. The piece argues trust must be a foundational layer of DPI, not an afterthought.
