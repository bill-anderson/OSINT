# Somalia rolls out certified cybersecurity program for government ICT staff

By Isaac K. Kassouwi — We Are Tech Africa, April 1, 2026

Somalia's government announced the launch of a specialized cybersecurity training program for public-sector ICT professionals, offering internationally recognized certifications to enhance their ability to protect national data and respond to growing cyber threats. The training is supervised by the National Communications Authority (NCA) in partnership with the World Bank Group (under the SCALED-UP Project, implemented with AFRALTI). Modules range from basic to advanced topics including protection of computer systems, incident management and response, cloud data security, and auditing organizational security policies. Courses run in Mogadishu (online) and Nairobi between March 28 and May 8, 2026.

The initiative comes as Somalia steps up efforts to secure national cyberspace. On March 7 the country launched the Somalia Computer Incident Response Team (SOMCIRT), as mandated under the Cybersecurity Law approved by parliament in January 2026; in August 2025 Somalia passed a cybercrime bill, and the Data Protection Law of March 2023 created the Data Protection Authority. Somalia has also signed an MoU with Turkey (Feb 10, 2026) spanning cybersecurity and partnered with Malaysia, Nigeria and UNODC. It is a signatory to the Malabo Convention. The ITU's Global Cybersecurity Index 2024 ranked Somalia in Tier 4 of 5 (score 37.39/100).
