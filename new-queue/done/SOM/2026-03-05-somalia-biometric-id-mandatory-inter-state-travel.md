# Somalia makes biometric digital ID mandatory for inter-state travel

By Lu-Hai Liang — Biometric Update, March 5, 2026

Somalians flying domestically will have to show their biometric ID cards. The measure was introduced on Monday, with Director General of the Immigration and Citizenship Agency (ICA) Mustafa Duhulow inaugurating the new system at the domestic departure terminal of Aden Adde International Airport in Mogadishu. The move aims to digitize the internal migration system and monitor the movement of people; travellers between federal states must show the IDs for identity verification and processing of digital data. It is a joint effort with NIRA.

The requirement expands NIRA's critical use cases to drive adoption. Last July, NIRA introduced a mandatory measure to present a national digital ID to open or update a bank account, effective September 1 2025, supported by the Central Bank of Somalia and the Somali Bankers Association. NIRA and the Ministry of Transport and Aviation also introduced a national-ID requirement for driver's licenses and vehicle registration. Somalia is also using PISCES (Personal Identification Secure Comparison and Evaluation System), a U.S.-backed biometrics system in use across 23 countries, to bolster national border management.
