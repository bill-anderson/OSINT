# Prime Minister Hamza Orders Mandatory Use of National ID for Essential Services

Mogadishu (WDN), April 8, 2025

Somali Prime Minister Hamza Abdi Barre has issued a directive mandating all government and non-governmental agencies providing essential services to link service delivery to the national identity system. Chairing a high-level meeting in Mogadishu, he formally unveiled the policy aimed at integrating public services with citizen identification.

"This marks the end of over three decades of identity ambiguity for Somali citizens," the Prime Minister said, describing the initiative as a cornerstone for national security and sustainable development. "All federal and service-delivery institutions must now integrate national ID requirements into their operations," he instructed, adding that agencies must coordinate with the National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA). He said the decision would help stabilize Mogadishu and other urban centers where anonymity has posed security risks, enhance public trust, improve service delivery and combat fraud and terrorism, and emphasized that safeguards to protect citizens' data are already in place.
