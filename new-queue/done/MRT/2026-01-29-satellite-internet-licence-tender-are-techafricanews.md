# Mauritania — satellite internet licence tender (ARE); Starlink 2026 launch flagged

Mauritania is turning to satellite technology to expand internet access and reduce the digital divide, with nearly 63% of the population offline in 2023 (ITU). On **22 January 2026** the Mauritanian Regulatory Authority (**ARE**) announced an **international tender** to award licences for mobile electronic-communications services via satellite, or for the sale of satellite capacity. The tender has two batches: one for operators providing direct retail services to the public using geostationary (GEO) satellites, and one for the sale of satellite capacity to operators/private entities without direct public-service provision.

In Mauritania, 2G network coverage reached 97% of the population in 2023, while 3G and 4G coverage stood at 43.9% and 34.7% respectively in 2022, with internet penetration estimated at 37.4%. The market is dominated by Chinguitel, Mattel and Moov Mauritel, with the regulator enforcing service-quality and coverage obligations (including sanctions). LEO providers such as **Starlink** and Amazon are not yet authorised for direct public service but are expected to seek licences, with **Starlink planning a commercial launch in Mauritania in 2026**.
