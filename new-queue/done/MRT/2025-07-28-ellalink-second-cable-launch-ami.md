# EllaLink second submarine cable — project launch, Nouadhibou (ami.mr)

*Source: Agence Mauritanienne d'Information — the Mauritanian state news agency (government voice; capture is not endorsement).*

President Mohamed Ould Cheikh El Ghazouani supervised, on Monday, in Nouadhibou, the launch of the project to connect to the international submarine cable "EllaLink" — a strategic step to strengthen Mauritania's position on the global digital-connectivity map.

The cable extends 669 km at sea within Mauritanian territorial waters, directly linking the country to internet networks in Europe and America. It carries two optical-fibre pairs with a capacity of 200 Gb/s, extensible to 500 Gb/s, and a maximum capacity of 12 Tbit/s. The station is equipped with modern signal repeaters, an integrated power system and advanced workspaces, meeting quality, security and environmental standards.

The total project cost, financed by the European Investment Bank and supported by the European Union, is estimated at more than €43.5 million, of which €25.6 million is allocated to execution and €17.9 million to maintenance over a 25-year period.

Digital Transformation Minister Ahmed Salem Bede Atfagha said the project is a decisive turning point for the telecoms sector, serving several strategic objectives: reinforcing the security and flexibility of the national network by providing a second internet source, improving internet quality by increasing speed fivefold and reducing prices by 20%. He added it would enable projected economic growth of 2% of GDP, create digital-sector jobs, support scientific research and education by connecting universities to international research networks, accelerate government digital transformation, strengthen transparency and service quality, and attract investment.

*(Note: this is the July 2025 project-launch/foundation-stone event; the phase-1 coastal landing was completed 4 May 2026, commissioning expected Q1 2027. This is the second cable long referenced in the wiki as "contracted to Ellalink" — now under construction.)*
