# Mozambique opens bids for 5G spectrum

The Communications Regulatory Authority of Mozambique (INCM) is reviewing bids for 5G spectrum, with all three local operators showing interest.

By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa

The country's regulator said operators clearing the reviewing process may proceed with a phased commercial 5G rollout across the country.

The Communications Regulatory Authority of Mozambique (INCM) is reviewing bids from three operators – Tmcel, Vodacom Mozambique and Movitel – for 5G spectrum in the 700MHz, 2.6GHz and 3.5GHz bands.

The bids were opened publicly on April 17 and the INCM is reviewing the technical, economic and regulatory merits of the bids submitted by the three telcos.

The frequency bands were chosen to ensure an effective balance of coverage, capacity and service quality.

The regulator said that operators clearing this hurdle may proceed with a phased commercial 5G rollout across the country.

"The introduction of 5G represents a decisive step towards accelerating the digitalization of the economy, promoting the emergence of new digital services and applications, as well as strengthening the competitiveness of the communications sector," the regulator said in a statement.

The INCM said the proposals were submitted in line with the terms and conditions of administrative allocation.

It noted that such allocations are accompanied by regulatory obligations, including maximizing the social and economic value of the spectrum and progressively expanding nationwide coverage, with particular emphasis on rural and peri‑urban areas as well as regions of high economic and social importance.

It added that the obligations also require compliance with stringent service quality and network performance standards, while ensuring the sustainable deployment of mobile communications networks in Mozambique.

"The opening of the bids thus constitutes a crucial milestone in this process, marking the beginning of the technical, economic, and regulatory evaluation phase of the applications, with a view to the subsequent allocation of the spectrum and the phased implementation of 5G in Mozambique," the INCM concluded.

No firm commercial launch date has been announced, while pilot tests were carried out across all provincial capitals in 2025.

## Mozambique's mobile market

In May 2023, Vodacom Mozambique announced it was the first mobile operator to launch 5G in the Southern African nation, using 3.5GHz spectrum for its trials in select areas.

Vodacom Mozambique is the country's biggest mobile operator with 11.8 million customers at the end of 2025, according to statistics from Omdia, a sister company of Connecting Africa.

Movitel Mozambique is second-biggest with 4 million customers over the same period and Tmcel is the smallest operator with 1.6 million users at the end of the fourth quarter of 2025.
