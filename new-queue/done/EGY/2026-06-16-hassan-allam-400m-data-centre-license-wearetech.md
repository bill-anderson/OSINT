# Egypt Bets on Data Centers and AI to Drive Digital Economy Growth

Egypt's National Telecom Regulatory Authority (NTRA) granted a licence to Hassan Allam Digital Infrastructure and Data Center Solutions to build and operate a data centre, developed in partnership with technology/VC firm A15, with an initial $400 million first-phase investment. The facility will provide data hosting, cloud computing and AI-ready infrastructure serving government, financial institutions and local/international businesses.

It is the tenth data-centre licence NTRA has issued in two years. The government is preparing a national data-centre and cloud strategy leveraging Egypt's location at the Africa–Europe–Asia crossroads. Egypt's second national AI strategy targets a 7.7% contribution from digital tech/AI to GDP by 2030, and authorities estimate AI could generate up to $42.7 billion in economic value.
