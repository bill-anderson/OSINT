# Broad World Bank financing package; digital relevance = WURI digital-ID top-up (+$20M).
---

(Togo First, Friday 17 July 2026) — Togo and the World Bank Group signed five financing agreements worth a combined $429 million, or about 257.4 billion CFA francs, in Lomé on Thursday, July 16, 2026. Signed during a visit by World Bank Managing Director of Operations Anna Bjerde, the agreements support several strategic projects aligned with Togo Vision 2040 and the government's new 2026-2031 roadmap, structured around three priorities: "Protect, Unite and Transform."

Under the "Protect" pillar, two energy projects will receive a combined $140 million: the Promoting Gas Power Market Integration Project (PRIME-GAS, $40 million) and the West Africa Regional Electricity Market Program (WA-REMP, $100 million).

Under the "Unite" pillar, the West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion Project (WURI) will receive an additional $20 million to expand access to secure identification. Another $69 million will go to the Regional Social Cohesion Project (COSO), focused on northern Togo.

Most of the financing supports the "Transform" pillar: the Logistics and Transport Systems Improvement Program (PASLT) will receive $200 million.

The new financing adds to the World Bank's already substantial portfolio in Togo. Official data show active commitments now exceed $2 billion (the portfolio stands at $2.1 billion), up from $174 million in 2017, including six national projects and 10 regional projects.
