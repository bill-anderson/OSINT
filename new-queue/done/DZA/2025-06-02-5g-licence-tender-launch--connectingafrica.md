# Algeria begins tender process for 5G licenses

By Paula Gilbert, Editor — Connecting Africa, 2 June 2025

Algeria has kicked off a tender process for 5G licenses with an aim to have provisional licenses awarded by early July 2025.

Algeria has initiated a tender process for 5G licenses in the North African country, with an aim to launch the next generation service in the third quarter of 2025.

The Regulatory Authority of Post and Electronic Communications (ARPCE) has launched competitive bidding for three licenses to establish and operate 5G mobile networks and related services in the country.

Those interested had a limited time to collect the official bidding documentation package from the regulatory authority's headquarters in Algiers – between May 29, 2025, and June 1, 2025 – and had to pay a fee of 1 million Algerian Dinar (US$7,600) to receive the documentation.

Algeria's Ministry of Post and Telecommunications also put out a statement confirming the regulator's intentions and said provisional licenses are expected to be granted on July 3, 2025, coinciding with the nation's 63rd Independence Day celebrations.

The actual launch of the 5G services is expected during the third quarter of 2025.

The Ministry said 5G networks will be gradually deployed starting in high-density and industrial areas with a focus on smart cities, industry 4.0 technologies, digital health and smart mobility.

It said the launch of 5G reflected clear political will to make digital transformation a fundamental driver for economic growth and promote digital sovereignty.

"We are not just launching new technology. We are working to strengthen infrastructure, drive economic growth, create sustainable job opportunities, and launch a national vision aimed at accelerating innovation, empowering youth, and placing Algeria at the heart of the global digital map," said the Minister of Post and Telecommunications, Sid Ali Zerrouki, in a statement originally published in Arabic.

## Algeria's mobile ecosystem

Algeria has three major telecoms operators that are likely interested in accessing a 5G license.

The biggest is Algérie Télécom's Mobilis brand with almost 22.9 million mobile subscribers at the end of March 2025, according to Omdia, a sister company of Connecting Africa.

Djezzy had around 16.6 million mobile subscribers in the same period and Ooredoo Algeria had 15.1 million.

The majority of Algeria's mobile users have already switched to 4G services, with 83.3% of all subscribers using 4G at the end of the first quarter of 2025, Omdia stats show. Around 12.4% were using 3G while only 4.3% of mobile users were still on a 2G device.

## North African 5G rollout

North African neighbor Tunisia launched 5G services in February 2025, when local operators Orange Tunisie, Ooredoo Tunisie and Tunisie Télécom all began offering 5G mobile services.

In Egypt, Orange recently secured a syndicated loan of almost $85 million to support its planned 5G rollout. Telecom Egypt secured a 5G license in January 2024, while Vodafone Egypt, e& Egypt and Orange Egypt were awarded 5G licenses in October 2024.
