Dar es Salaam. A digital evolution is unfolding across Tanzania, reshaping how citizens send and receive monetary value. Spearheading this is the Tanzania Instant Payments System (TIPS) — an interoperable payments infrastructure designed to streamline high-volume, low-value real-time retail transactions. Inaugurated in 2020 by the Bank of Tanzania (BoT), with support from the Financial Sector Deepening Trust Tanzania (FSDT) and the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, TIPS has been recognised as a pivotal project addressing obstacles to financial inclusion.

Reflecting on the impact of TIPS as detailed in the case study "Building an Inclusive Payments Ecosystem in Tanzania," BoT Governor Emmanuel Tutuba stated the platform holds a central role in the nation's digital economy vision. "TIPS transcends the mere definition of a payments system; it serves as the very national digital backbone upon which inclusive finance is being constructed and strengthened. By facilitating immediate, low-cost transactional capabilities between diverse financial entities, including traditional banks, mobile money operators, and other payment service providers, TIPS actively empowers millions of Tanzanians to participate in the digital economy, irrespective of their geographical location or financial resources," Governor Tutuba explained. He underscored the BoT's commitment to cultivating a cash-lite economy.
