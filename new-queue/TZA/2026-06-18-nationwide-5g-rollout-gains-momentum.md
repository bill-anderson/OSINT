Tanzania. The deployment of fifth-generation mobile technology (5G) infrastructure in Tanzania increased by 139 per cent between June 2024 and March 2026, with all regions now having at least three radio base stations supporting the standard. The TCRA Communications Sector Statistics Report for January-March 2026, released by TCRA Director General Engineer Peter Mwasalyanda, shows the number of 5G facilities increased from 754 in June 2024 to 1,804 in March 2026, rising 44 per cent between December 2025 and March 2026.

The facilities are installed on 10,084 telecommunications towers nationwide. As of March 2026 there were 15,742 2G facilities, 14,521 3G facilities and 15,464 4G facilities. 5G services are now available in four regions that had no such facilities in June 2024: Katavi, Lindi, Mtwara and North Pemba. Regions recording the highest 5G growth included Zanzibar Urban West (from 37 to 95 facilities, up 157 per cent), Kagera, Tanga and Tabora.

The nationwide rollout contributed to a sharp rise in internet data consumption, which more than doubled from 426 petabytes in June 2024 to 932 PB in March 2026. The number of M2M SIM cards increased by 22 per cent, from 969,440 to 1,182,797.
