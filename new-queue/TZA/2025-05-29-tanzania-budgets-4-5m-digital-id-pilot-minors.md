Note: pilot targets minors in Zanzibar plus Iringa and Mbeya regions.

Tanzania's Home Affairs Minister, Innocent Bashungwa, has announced that the government has allocated 11.34 billion Tanzanian Shillings (approximately US$4.5 million) for a digital ID pilot targeting nearly 300,000 minors in three regions. The minister made the announcement in parliament while defending his ministry's 2025/2026 budget of 2.06 trillion TZS (about US$824 million). The pilot will be conducted ahead of an envisaged nationwide digital ID rollout during 2026/2027, targeting 235,826 minors in Zanzibar and the Iringa and Mbeya regions.

Bashungwa said the pilot is part of a plan for a unified digital ID system (Unique Number ID) issued to all citizens below 18 as well as foreign minors. NIDA has built an integrated portal connecting the immigration department, the Zanzibar civil status registration agency and RITA to ensure data exchange and ID issuance at airports, hospitals and border posts.

He also talked about a smart cities initiative deploying surveillance cameras across Dar es Salaam, Dodoma and Arusha, estimated at $145.2 million, $80 million of which are concessional loans provided by the Abu Dhabi Exports Office. Bashungwa also said cybercrime saw a 13.5 percent drop in the past year, thanks in part to enhanced policing and cooperation with the TCRA.
