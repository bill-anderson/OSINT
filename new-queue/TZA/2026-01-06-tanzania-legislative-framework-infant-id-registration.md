An Infant Registration Bill will be tabled for scrutiny during Tanzania's next parliamentary session, set to begin on January 27. The Director General of the National Identification Authority (NIDA), James Kaji, disclosed the information during a Staff Council meeting of the ID authority. Kaji explained that the essence of the legislation is to set a template for early citizen registration, outlining how identity registration will happen for newborns.

Under the proposed bill, children will be registered at birth and assigned a unique identification number which will be used to issue them a national digital ID card when they reach 18 years of age. Kaji said the policy is intended to streamline citizen identification by eliminating challenges the country faces in identifying adults whose births were never registered early.

The NIDA boss also raised concerns over the reticence in withdrawing ID cards which have already been produced, saying more than 300,000 printed cards await collection in district offices. Tanzania also recently upgraded the Registration, Insolvency and Trusteeship Agency (RITA) system, making it possible to issue birth certificates within 48 hours via the eRITA platform. Birth registration coverage reached 60 percent as of 2022, according to UNICEF.
