# The year ICT sector grappled with implementation woes

A year-in-review of Malawi's 2025 ICT sector: ambitious digital initiatives undermined by persistent implementation failures.

- **Passport saga:** government awarded a **$29.9 million contract to Madras Security Private Printers** in January 2025; the transition proved a technical nightmare exposing digital-infrastructure weaknesses (100,000+ backlog).
- **MACRA misinformation system:** MACRA introduced an integrated system to track misinformation and disinformation, sparking debate among digital-rights activists over mass surveillance and freedom of expression.
- **Elections:** during the **16 September 2025 Presidential, Parliamentary and Local Government Elections**, the Malawi Electoral Commission deployed **6,500 electoral management devices** for voter verification, which faced scrutiny over offline glitches in rural polling stations.
- **e-Government paralysis:** failure to operationalise the **Enterprise Service Bus** left government services siloed, denying online passport registration and driver's-licence payments/renewals.
- **Forex:** shortages hampered import of hardware for 5G expansion; 5G live in 10 cities/towns but device costs stayed out of reach.

Opportunities: the Public Sector Reforms Information Management System (Dec 2025); DMAP fibre to 500+ government buildings; the **National e-Payment Gateway** (FInES/World Bank) as single-entry point for government collections; and the **Malawi National Single Window** (May 2025) integrating MRA and the Ministry of Trade.
