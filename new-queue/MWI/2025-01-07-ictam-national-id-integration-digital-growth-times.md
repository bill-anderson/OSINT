# ID system said to be key to digital growth

The ICT Association of Malawi (ICTAM) urged government to **integrate the national ID system with key digital platforms** to accelerate digital transformation and boost financial inclusion — as Malawi targets 95% adult access to formal financial services by 2028 (NSFI III). About 60% of adults lacked access to formal financial services, with only 14% actively using them (RBM/UNCDF 2020 Survey).

ICTAM General Secretary Exile Njoka said ID integration would provide a secure foundation for identity verification, streamlining social welfare, healthcare and education delivery, and helping unbanked populations access banking and mobile money. He identified AI as a key trend for agriculture (weather forecasting, pest management), healthcare (diagnostics) and public services, calling for AI training programmes and innovation hubs. NRB PS Mphatso Sambo requested time before commenting. The NRB, with UNDP, is implementing the **National Registration and Identification System (NRIS)** project, which aims to provide an interface to other public/private systems for compliant data sharing.
