# Malawi eyes SDG 16.9 target with universal legal identity blueprint

Malawi has unveiled the **National Registration Bureau's 2025–2030 Strategic Plan** (launched September 2025) to accelerate progress toward the UN SDG **16.9** target — legal identity for all, including birth registration, by 2030. The country intends to ensure all births, marriages and deaths are registered by 2030, building on a high national-ID adoption rate (over 12 million citizens with legal identity).

The strategy rests on **six pillars**: universal civil registration within five years; a system factoring in marginalized groups; strengthening the national DPI ecosystem through interoperability of services; institutional capacity building; civic engagement and trust in the ID system; and aligning with Vision 2063. Minister of Homeland Security Ezekiel Ching'oma said the blueprint supports the NRB's mandate to build a comprehensive National Registration and Identification System. UNDP (which helped draft the strategy) Deputy Resident Representative Challa Getachew described identity as "a lifeline." The government plans to roll out its national digital ID system in 2026.
