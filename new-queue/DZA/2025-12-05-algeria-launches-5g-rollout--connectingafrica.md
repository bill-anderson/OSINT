# Algeria launches 5G rollout

Algeria's Ministry of Post and Telecommunications has announced the start of 5G deployment after granting licenses to Mobilis, Djezzy and Ooredoo.

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa

December 5, 2025

2 Min Read

(Source: starline on Freepik)

Algeria's Ministry of Post and Telecommunications has announced the start of 5G deployment after the country's Regulatory Authority of Post and Electronic Communications (ARPCE) granted licenses to Mobilis, Djezzy and Ooredoo.

The announcement comes five months after the country initiated a tender process for 5G licenses.

The licenses, costing the three operators a combined 63.9 billion Algerian Dinar (US$492 million), were officially confirmed and published in the government gazette following an executive decree last week.

At a ceremony held at the Abdelatif-Rahal International Conference Center (CIC) this week, Algerian Minister of Post and Telecommunications Sid Ali Zerrouki described 5G as a decisive step toward modernizing Algeria's telecom sector.

He said the launch's objectives were aligned with the national vision prioritizing AI, the Internet of Things (IoT), cloud computing and advanced technologies to stimulate innovation in the healthcare sector, Industry 4.0 technologies, digital health and smart mobility.

He indicated that the digital ecosystem is expected to generate US$9 billion in GDP contribution in 2025 and US$13 billion by 2030, through job creation, added value and attracting investors.

"Thanks to the strategic vision, constant support and clear guidance of the President of the Republic, Abdelmadjid Tebboune, we managed to ensure the efficiency and optimal quality of this project at all levels," Zerrouki said.

## Algeria's mobile ecosystem

All three operators are now cleared to begin rolling out 5G services, starting with eight provinces designated as pilot areas.

Under the government's national plan, Mobilis, Djezzy and Ooredoo must achieve full nationwide coverage within six years.

According to Omdia, a sister company of Connecting Africa, Algérie Télécom's Mobilis brand was the biggest telco with almost 23.5 million mobile subscribers at the end of the third quarter of 2025.

Djezzy had around 17.7 million mobile subscribers in the same period and Ooredoo Algeria had 14.8 million.

## North African 5G movements

Algeria's north African neighbor Tunisia launched 5G services in February 2025, when local operators Orange Tunisie, Ooredoo Tunisie and Tunisie Télécom all began offering them.

In Egypt, Orange recently secured a syndicated loan of almost $85 million to support its planned 5G rollout.
