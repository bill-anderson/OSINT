# Algeria Sets One-Month Deadline for Government Data Center Integration

By Samira Njoya — We Are Tech, 15 July 2026

- President Abdelmadjid Tebboune has given Algerian public administrations one month to connect their information systems to the National Data Center.
- The interconnection will support automated data sharing, expand digital public services and strengthen the government's "Dzair Digital Services" platform.
- The move follows the inauguration of Algeria's sovereign National Digital Services Center, which includes two Tier III-standard data centers with 99.98% availability.

Algeria has accelerated the implementation of its national digital transformation strategy. During a Cabinet meeting on July 12, President Abdelmadjid Tebboune ordered public administrations to complete intersectoral interconnection through the National Data Center within one month. The measure aims to expand the range of digital services available to citizens.

According to the Cabinet's statement, the decision forms part of "the acceleration of the implementation of the digitalization process and the achievement of integration between the various state institutions."

At the same meeting, the president also ordered authorities to distribute the country's tourist travel allowance temporarily through bank cards. The measure aims to reduce misuse of funds and improve payment traceability.

## Interconnection to Support Digital Public Services

The interconnection will allow public institutions to exchange data automatically. As a result, citizens will no longer need to submit the same documents repeatedly when completing administrative procedures.

The initiative also provides the technical foundation for the national Dzair Digital Services portal, which the government continues to deploy to simplify administrative procedures and expand online public services.

Moreover, the acceleration follows the inauguration of Algeria's National Digital Services Center in Algiers only a few days earlier. Authorities have described the complex as the country's first sovereign digital infrastructure.

The complex includes two data centers located in Algiers and Blida. The facilities comply with international standards and provide 99.98% availability. They will host government data, ensure the continuity of digital public services and enable interoperability across state institutions.

Earlier this year, the first National Data Center also received the Tier III Design certification from the Uptime Institute. The decision marks another milestone in Algeria's digital transformation strategy. In February, the government launched the National Data Governance Platform to harmonize the management of public-sector data.

Authorities now seek to make full administrative interoperability operational across government institutions. They also expect the initiative to support other strategic projects, including the national artificial intelligence strategy and the continued development of the Dzair Digital Services portal, both of which rely on secure and standardized public data sharing.
