# Algeria launches second satellite in 2026

Algeria has launched Alsat-3B, an Earth observation satellite developed in collaboration with China, its second satellite of 2026.

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa

February 3, 2026

2 Min Read

On January 30, 2026, Algeria launched the Alsat-3B Earth observation satellite from the Jiuquan Satellite Launch Centre in China. (Source: China Media Group)

Algeria has launched Alsat-3B, an Earth observation satellite developed in collaboration with China, its second satellite of 2026, after the launch of Alsat-3A which went into orbit in January.

Launched from the Jiuquan Satellite Launch Centre aboard a Long March-2C rocket at 12:01 p.m. Chinese Standard Time on January 30, 2026, the launch marks a step forward in Algeria-China space cooperation and the expansion of Algeria's space program.

The two launches conclude an Algeria-China contract signed in July 2023, which covers the development and launch of two optical remote-sensing satellites, as well as associated ground systems, training and support services.

Both satellites are designed to provide high-resolution Earth observation imagery, enhancing Algeria's geospatial intelligence capabilities.

In a joint statement, the Chinese President Xi Jinping said the Algerian remote sensing satellite project is another successful example of China-Algeria aerospace cooperation and an important demonstration of the two nations' comprehensive strategic partnership.

"The successful launch of the Algerian remote sensing satellite is another substantial achievement in aerospace cooperation between Algeria and China, and a new milestone in the development of bilateral relations, which will help both sides expand cooperation prospects," added Algerian President Abdelmadjid Tebboune.

## Africa's growing satellite ecosystem

The deployment of Algeria's two Earth observation satellites brings Africa's on-orbit satellite fleet to 68.

These satellites are largely focused on Earth observation and strengthening capabilities in land planning, disaster response and environmental monitoring.

According to Space in Africa, at least eight new African satellites are expected to launch this year, provided all planned missions proceed as scheduled.

Africa has seen many countries and companies launch satellite gateways or acquire licenses to build satellites on the continent.

Moreover, there has been more interest in satellite recently with companies like SpaceX's Starlink and Eutelsat OneWeb using low-Earth orbit (LEO) satellites to launch services in a number of African countries.

Back in April 2023, the Kenya Space Agency (KSA) launched its first operational Earth observation satellite Taifa-1, meaning Nation-1 in Swahili.

In May 2023, Côte d'Ivoire announced that it would launch its first observation satellite, the Yam-Sat CI 01 by August 2024.

In January 2023, the Djibouti government signed a US$1 billion agreement with the Hong Kong Aerospace Technology Group (HKATG) to develop an international commercial spaceport in the northern Obock Region of Djibouti.
