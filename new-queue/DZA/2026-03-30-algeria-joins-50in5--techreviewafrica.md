# Algeria joins 50in5 initiative, pledges to strengthen Africa's digital public infrastructure

Date: 2026-03-30

By: Nana Appiah Acquaye

Algeria has officially joined the 50in5 Initiative during the Global Africa Tech Summit in Algiers, signaling a major commitment to advancing secure, inclusive, and interoperable digital public infrastructure across the continent.

The announcement was welcomed by Kenya, whose government supports the initiative as a practical pathway for delivering digital services at scale and unlocking economic opportunities throughout Africa.

H.E. William Kabogo Gitau, E.G.H., highlighted that Algeria's participation demonstrates the continent's unified drive to expand access to digital platforms, improve connectivity, and enhance regional collaboration. Stakeholders expressed optimism about working with Algeria and other partners to accelerate implementation and deepen cooperation on continental digital development.

The 50in5 Initiative aims to establish 50 interoperable digital public infrastructure systems across Africa within five years, reinforcing governance, financial inclusion, and public service delivery.
