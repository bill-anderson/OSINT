NewZimbabwe.com invited Mr. Vengai Madzima, Senior Partner at Madzima Chidyausiku Museta Legal Practitioners (MCM Legal), to discuss the digitisation of the Deeds Registry.

**Why now?** The Deeds Registry is one of the most important offices in the country, housing records of security documents including property titles, charitable or family trusts, and financial security instruments such as mortgage bonds. Established through the Deeds Registry Act of 1960, consecutive governments made several attempts over the years to maintain security over the documents. Recent cases alleging fraud in deed registration processes - through collusion with staff members or outright forgery, the Matumbuka [Mutumbuka] case being a point in case - have necessitated further security measures in line with international best practices, already implemented in jurisdictions like South Africa, Namibia, Australia and the United Kingdom (jurisdictions where a significant number of Zimbabwe's diaspora reside). Hence the new regulations (Statutory Instrument 76 of 2025, issued by the Minister for Justice, Legal and Parliamentary Affairs).

**The new digital title deed:** The idea is to standardise title deeds issued going forward. This includes titles being on special security paper supplied by a single supplier so it is easily identifiable and verifiable. How the title deed is drawn up will also be prescribed for conveyancers - including font size, type of ink, and rules on alterations. The digital copy will be maintained at the deeds office, and a physical copy with these features issued to the deed owner, to reduce options for fraud.

**Old title deeds:** The new regulations acknowledge both new "securitised deeds" and "old title deeds." The idea is to integrate current title deeds into securitised deeds within 24 months. Holders of current title deeds are required to submit their original title deeds and documents to the deeds registry within 24 months for validation. Once validated, the Registrar issues the securitised deed on special paper to the holder, and a digital copy is maintained at the Registrar's office. A supporting implementation framework from the Registrar's office is anticipated, including attendant fees.

**Other security measures:** The regulations prescribe that lawyers follow strict identity procedures when drafting deeds and powers of attorney, with a requirement that documents be notarised. Digital log-ins on the system will be traceable for any person dealing with a particular property, allowing a digital footprint. In addition, criminal sanctions for fraud have been included, targeting Registry or government employees who collude with or assist fraudsters to tamper with deeds.

**Personal view:** Madzima believes the system will modernise the deeds registration system by improving security and transparency, reducing instances of fraud, and assisting property practitioners in verification of title - moving from a manual paper system to a digital and more efficient system.
