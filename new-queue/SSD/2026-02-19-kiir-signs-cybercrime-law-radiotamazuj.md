# Minister Ateny defends cybercrime law amid concerns

*Radio Tamazuj, 19 February 2026*

South Sudan's President Salva Kiir on Wednesday signed a new cybercrime law aimed at tackling online offenses and curbing harmful content on social media, including hate speech. The Cyber Crimes and Computer Abuse Act 2026 criminalizes activities such as unauthorized data transmission, hacking, espionage, cyberterrorism, economic sabotage, the creation of fake websites and social media accounts, publication of false information, online impersonation, and gender-based harassment.

Information Minister Ateny Wek told journalists in Juba on Thursday that the law is now in effect. He said it updates South Sudan's legal framework for the digital age, adding that previous laws — including the Criminal Procedure Act of 2008 and the National Security Act 2015 — addressed cyber-related crimes only partially.

The minister said the Ministry of Justice and Constitutional Affairs is responsible for interpreting and enforcing the law, while the Ministry of ICT will coordinate with the NCA and law enforcement to implement it, including through public awareness campaigns. He urged the media to educate the public, warning that ignorance of the law would not exempt offenders. However, the full text of the law was not immediately made available to journalists.
