# NCA adjusts telecom tariff exchange rate to avert network collapse

*Radio Tamazuj, 25 June 2026*

South Sudan's National Communications Authority (NCA) has approved a phased adjustment to the telecommunications tariff exchange rate effective Thursday midnight, saying the measure is intended to prevent financial strain on mobile network operators and avert service disruptions. NCA Director General Rizig Dominic Samuel said the decision aimed to ensure the sustainability of telecom services under pressure from a widening gap between regulated and market exchange rates.

According to NCA data, the sector generates between 18 billion and 25 billion South Sudanese pounds annually. Samuel said international telecom traffic is globally benchmarked at about $0.04 per minute (roughly 181 SSP at the official rate), but operators have been required to calculate tariffs using an outdated rate of 4,526 SSP per dollar while commercial market rates stand at about 27,000 SSP per dollar — resulting in significant foreign-exchange losses.

Under the new arrangement, an additional adjustment of about 70 SSP per minute would be applied on top of the existing baseline tariff, to be reviewed depending on macroeconomic conditions. Samuel warned some operators had considered scaling back infrastructure, particularly in remote areas, and said the NCA was engaging the central bank on improved forex access for the sector.
