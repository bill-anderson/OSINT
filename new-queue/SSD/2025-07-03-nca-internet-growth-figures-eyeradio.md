# NCA reports huge internet growth in South Sudan

*Eye Radio, 3 July 2025 (Koang Chang)*

Digital connectivity across South Sudan has significantly risen, with internet users increasing from 1.3 million in 2024 to 1.9 million in 2025, and mobile phone users growing from 3.97 million to 4.47 million over the same period, according to the National Communication Authority (NCA). This represents an internet penetration rate jumping from 12% to 15.7% in one year.

The NCA attributed the rise to better internet and mobile infrastructure development, including the launch of the National Broadband Roadmap, implementation of the Digital Government Strategy, and a partnership with Starlink to expand rural connectivity.

It noted that the Bank of South Sudan launched the National Instant Payment System in early 2025, and that the government approved a $9 million budget in June for the design of a National Fibre Network. The NCA framed the developments as significant progress in South Sudan's journey toward full digital inclusion.
