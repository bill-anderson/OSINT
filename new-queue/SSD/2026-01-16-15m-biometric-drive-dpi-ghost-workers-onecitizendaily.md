# $15M Biometric Drive: South Sudan Lays Foundations for Digital Public Infrastructure to Purge 'Ghost' Workers

*One Citizen Daily, 16 January 2026*

Armed with high-resolution cameras, thumbprint scanners, and a $15 million investment, the government is attempting to eliminate a payroll bloated by non-existent workers, duplicate names, and systemic inefficiencies. Using biometrics to sanitize the payroll marks one of the first major steps South Sudan is taking to build a national Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI).

For South Sudan, this biometric drive is a critical intervention in a landscape currently lacking a well-established DPI ecosystem. The country currently lacks a national digital ID system at scale, possesses a fragmented digital architecture, and most critically has no comprehensive data protection law to serve as a safeguard. In an economy heavily reliant on cash with limited ministerial interoperability, this $15M system is becoming, by default, the nation's first major DPI building block.

"The essence of this biometric registration is for us to have data that we can use for planning and decision making," Mangok explained. Although the current biometric information collected will be stored at the server of the Ministry of Finance and Planning, questions regarding digital governance remain critical: without a formal data protection authority or established laws, who ultimately controls this biometric data after the project ends? The system rolls out in three phases — first at national level, then the states, and finally the organized forces.

Maxwell Melingasuk Loboka, Project Director for the Public Financial Management and Institutional Strengthening Project (PFMIS), noted the current pilot is a litmus test. Targeting five key entities including the Office of the President and the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the pilot has already identified "digital divides" — internet connectivity and unstable electricity as primary threats. Experts warn the system must eventually achieve interoperability with future National ID and passport enrollment systems to create a unified digital identity for citizens.
