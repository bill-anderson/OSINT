Ghana's National Identification Authority (NIA) now requires first-time applicants to submit national ID card requests online only, to streamline the application process and premium services (card replacement, information update). Previously available at NIA premium centres, such requests must now be done via the NIA website. There are seven steps: fill the form online, pay, book a virtual/in-person interview, receive a QR code for biometric capture, book and attend the capture at a selected premium centre, then await card issuance. In February the NIA received 700,000 blank cards to clear a card backlog dating from 2023.

Separately, Ghana rolled out a new chip-embedded passport system. Foreign Minister Samuel Ablakwa said it carries the highest security inputs; it replaces the old biometric passport regime (to phase out by 2030), with chip technology, 24-hour operations, courier delivery and e-tracking, faster turnaround, reduced fees, and ICAO-standard security. Ghanaian firm Biometric Travel Solutions Ltd (BTS) is involved in production. The launch aligns with immigration-sector modernization (new biometric border management system and e-gates at major airports).
