Onafriq has launched a cross-border payment service in Ghana in partnership with the Pan-African Payment and Settlement System (PAPSS), to streamline regional transfers and strengthen intra-African trade. The service (now live) lets partner banks, fintechs, mobile money operators and traditional financial institutions in Ghana enable customers to send/receive funds directly to mobile wallets and bank accounts — addressing high transaction fees and non-transparent exchange rates while formalising informal financial activity.

The pilot emphasises SME and retail use, with real-time transactions on PAPSS infrastructure; Afreximbank acts as settlement agent. The Bank of Ghana approved a six-month pilot period to evaluate transaction volumes, FX outcomes and user engagement. Onafriq founder/CEO Dare Okoudjou and PAPSS CEO Mike Ogbalu III framed it as operationalising the partnership and advancing financial inclusion. Onafriq's infrastructure spans 43 African countries, 1 billion+ mobile-money wallets and 500 million bank accounts. PAPSS, established by Afreximbank and the African Union, supports AfCFTA.
