The NIA has announced that the backlog of unissued Ghana Cards accumulated between March 2023 and January 2025 has been fully cleared, enabling instant issuance nationwide. Head of Corporate Affairs Williams Ampomah E. Darlas (interview with the Daily Graphic, 11 September 2025) said the delays were largely due to a shortage of blank cards. "If you register today, you should be able to get your card today." There are 292 operational registration centres.

Updated statistics: 18,950,942 Ghanaians enrolled; 18,848,635 cards printed; 17,747,459 issued — leaving a little under a million to be distributed. Darlas said the Ghana Card is the backbone of identity verification, with 95 institutions (all major banks and government agencies) integrated with the NIA platform for real-time authentication; smaller institutions (e.g. microfinance) will be onboarded via a new mobile application under development.
