The Government of Ghana, through the Ministry of Gender, Children and Social Protection (MoGCSP), commenced the 99th cycle of Livelihood Empowerment Against Poverty (LEAP) cash-grant payments, with nationwide disbursement beginning Monday, 22 December 2025.

Minister Dr. Agnes Naa Momo Lartey said the Government released GH¢139,329,463.37 for the cycle, benefiting about 350,580 households (~1.5 million Ghanaians). LEAP is a flagship social-protection intervention and a pillar of the Government's Reset Agenda; the bi-monthly grants, structured by household size, range GH¢320-530. The Ministry of Finance allocated GH¢1.1 billion to LEAP — the highest since inception — and coverage will expand to 400,000 households in 2026. (LEAP disbursement increasingly runs on mobile-money-enabled G2P rails.)
