# Madagascar, World Bank Review Progress on $543 Million Digital Transformation Projects

Madagascar's government and the World Bank reviewed the implementation of two strategic digital projects: the **$400 million DECIM** (Digital and Energy Connectivity for Inclusion in Madagascar) project and the **$143 million PRODIGY** project.

**DECIM** aims to double access to electricity while providing internet connectivity to an additional **3.4 million people**. It has entered full operational phase, having distributed **664,000 digital devices**, while continuing fibre-optic rollout and connecting healthcare facilities and schools.

**PRODIGY** aims to host and secure the national digital identity system through **two data centres located in Tsimbazaza and Iavoloha**, powered by renewable energy for continuous operation during grid outages. Construction is underway; PRODIGY has entered its closing stage.

PRODIGY was launched in 2019 under the Public Administration Efficiency Reform Program (PREA); approximately **one in four Malagasy citizens lacks a legal identity**. DECIM received World Bank Board approval on April 7, 2023. Only **33.7% of the population had electricity access in 2020** (SSA average 48.4%); internet penetration rose to 22% in 2021 from 5.1% in 2016. By end-2025 Madagascar counted **6.71 million internet users (20.4% penetration)**.
