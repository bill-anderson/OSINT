Policy brief / analysis, Addis Standard, 5 February 2026. A critical argument against Ethiopia's mandatory national digital ID (Fayda). Thesis: making a National ID compulsory is a flawed and dangerous policy - the danger lies not in identification but in enforcement without consent, trust or accountability. Argues mandatory ID concentrates power (determining who exists officially, who accesses services, who is excluded); in contexts with weak/evolving data-protection safeguards and courts it can become a mechanism of surveillance, control and exclusion. References the April 2024 Ethio Telecom-National ID (Yodahe Zemichael) MoU enabling registration via Ethio Telecom service centres. A significant home-grown critical counter-narrative to the official Fayda framing. [Excerpt captured at sweep; full clip on promotion.]
