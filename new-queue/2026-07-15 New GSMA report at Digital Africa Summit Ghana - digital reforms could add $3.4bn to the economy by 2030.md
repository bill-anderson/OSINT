At the GSMA Digital Africa Summit in Ghana 2025 (Accra, Kempinski, 3 September 2025), the GSMA launched a report, 'Driving Digital Transformation of the Economy in Ghana'. (Note: the article headline/URL vary between figures; the report states strategic reforms could add ~$3.4 billion to Ghana's economy by 2030.)

Ghana's mobile industry already contributes 8% of GDP (GHS 94 billion), but despite 99% 4G coverage a 62% usage gap persists (people who could access mobile internet but don't). Sector potential: agriculture +GHS 10.5bn and 190,000 jobs; manufacturing +GHS 15bn (gold/cocoa processing via IoT/AI); e-government could raise GHS 5.8bn in tax revenue by cutting leakage (building on mobile-money-enabled systems like the LEAP programme). Barriers: device affordability (smartphones = 27% of monthly GDP per capita, 76% for the poorest 40%). The report praises the removal of the e-levy and calls for device-financing PPPs (MTN Design-to-Cost, Telecel microfinance), a clear 5G spectrum roadmap and streamlined rights-of-way. Angela Wamola (Head of Africa, GSMA) tied it to Ghana's RESET agenda; the report targets expanding mobile-internet adoption to 20.6 million unique users by 2029 (from 13.1 million).
