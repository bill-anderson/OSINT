AHORAEG coverage of the provisions of **Ley Núm. 7/2024 (23 December 2024)** regulating the use of social media, computer crimes and cybercrime — reported here on its rollout. Key provisions: minors under 14 may not access or belong to social networks without parental/guardian consent; personal data of such minors may not be requested or published without express authorisation. The law broadens privacy protection by prohibiting the dissemination of any person's data, photos, videos or files without express written consent (regardless of age), bans free downloading of copyrighted content, and criminalises identity usurpation via false profiles for illicit ends. The stated aim is to combat cybercrime and promote responsible use of ICTs.

[CLIP — full article text captured at search time.] Ley 7/2024 also creates the **Agencia Nacional de Ciberseguridad e Infraestructuras Críticas (ANCIC)** (under the Presidency) and is inspired by the Budapest Convention (2001) and the AU Malabo Convention (2014) — a candidate `instrument` entity.
