# Namibia forms cyber security alliance with India

Namibian President Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah and Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi signed an MoU exploring cooperation including cyber security, at a meeting at State House in Windhoek. The leaders discussed defence, maritime security, digital technology and the Unified Payments Interface (UPI), agriculture, health and pharmaceuticals, energy and critical minerals. The two countries also signed an MoU on an Entrepreneurship Development Centre.

India's Ministry of External Affairs said Modi offered India's support for quick-impact development projects in agriculture, IT, cyber security, healthcare, education, women empowerment and child welfare, and shared India's drone-for-agriculture experience. Nandi-Ndaitwah welcomed the agreements, including "the MoU on cooperation in cyber security as well [as] sharing digital solutions to foster digital governance, enable innovation in emerging technologies and to promote cyber-security initiatives."
