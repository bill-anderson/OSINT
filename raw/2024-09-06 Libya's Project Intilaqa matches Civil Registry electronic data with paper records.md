Tripoli-based PM Abdel Hamid Aldabaiba followed up on **Project Intilaqa**, which organises and matches electronic **[[civil-registry-authority-libya|Civil Registry]]** data with paper records. Attendees included Acting Interior Minister Imad Trabelsi, the Administrative Control Authority head, CRA head Major General Tariq Al-Thaman, and the Passports/Nationality/Foreigners Authority head.

The CRA reported **257 offices** completed matching and scanning, with data of **1.8 million Libyan families** printed — a **97% completion rate**; the project had not been completed since the mid-1970s. The CRA is pivotal: all Libyan nationals are issued a unique **National ID Number**, and only with a valid number can a citizen obtain a SIM card, passport, bank account, hard currency at the official rate, marry, or vote. The article recalls the controversy over **250,000-700,000 fake National ID Numbers** (a leaked intelligence estimate cited as a reason for postponing the 24 December 2021 elections) and a past CBL-CRA database dispute. Intilaqa aims to clean the database by comparing paper and electronic records.
