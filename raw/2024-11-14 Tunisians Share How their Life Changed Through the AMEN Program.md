The Amen Program is a social assistance initiative in Tunisia, launched in 2019 with support from the World Bank and implemented by Tunisia's Ministry of Social Affairs (MoSA), that targets the country's most vulnerable citizens with cash transfers, medical services, and economic empowerment opportunities.

As of December 2023, the program reached over 333,000 poor households (more than 10% of the population) with monthly cash transfers, and more than 620,000 low-income households with free and subsidized healthcare services. 54% of cash transfer beneficiaries are women-headed households, and more than 156,000 children under age 5 receive allowances.

The program provides Permanent Cash Transfers (PCT, or monthly cash transfers), medical services, and economic empowerment opportunities. An additional service for the poorest — family allowances for children aged 0 to 5 years, estimated at 30 dinars per month — currently benefits almost 156,000 children from 103,000 households enrolled in the AMEN social registry. Beneficiary stories (Itaf from Bizerte, Olfa from Tozeur, Abdallah from Manouba) illustrate the impact of monthly transfers, back-to-school transfers, healthcare cards, and prostheses.

Tunisia has faced significant economic challenges since 2011, worsened by COVID-19, the war in Ukraine, and severe droughts, with declining GDP growth, high food inflation, rising poverty, and an unemployment rate of 16.4%. During the COVID-19 pandemic, the program distributed emergency cash transfers to approximately 900,000 households, of which more than 630,000 were already enrolled in the AMEN social registry.

The project has prioritized digital transformation, with 44.4% of cash transfers now digitalized and over 76% of women beneficiaries receiving payments digitally. The World Bank Group's Country Partnership Framework (CPF) for 2023-2027 revolves around human capital strengthening, social resilience, and human development; 40% of the World Bank's portfolio in Tunisia is dedicated to social resilience and human development initiatives such as the AMEN Program.
