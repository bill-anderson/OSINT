In January 2024, the Central African Republic (CAR) enacted Law No. 24.001 on the Protection of Personal Data, establishing a comprehensive legal framework setting obligations for data controllers and processors while protecting individuals' rights.

Scope: the law applies broadly to any processing of personal data within CAR or having effects within its territory, including public security, defence, investigation and prosecution, and judicial proceedings, with exemptions for personal/household use and temporary technical storage.

Principles: lawfulness, fairness, transparency, accuracy, storage limitation, and purpose limitation. Lawful bases include consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public interest, official authority, and legitimate interests; parental consent is required for minors.

Sensitive data (racial origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, genetic and biometric data, health, sex life) is generally prohibited except under specific conditions. Direct marketing requires prior consent and an opt-out.

Cross-border transfers: a tiered system. Transfers are allowed to states offering similar protection; specific conditions apply for CEMAC and ECCAS member states; derogations apply for others.

Data subject rights: access, rectification, erasure, objection, not being subject to automated decision-making, and information. Controllers must appoint a resident Data Protection Officer (DPO) and maintain a processing register.

Supervision: the law provides for the establishment of an independent administrative authority (not yet established) empowered to investigate, handle complaints, and impose sanctions including warnings, cease-processing orders, or financial penalties of up to five percent of annual turnover, plus criminal sanctions.
