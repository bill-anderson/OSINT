(Togo First) - On May 5 [2025], the Togo government increased electricity prices by an average of 12.5%. The new rates were disclosed via an interministerial order dated March 24. The hike aims to strengthen CEET's financial stability amid ongoing funding challenges; the utility recently secured CFA40 billion from the West African Development Bank (BOAD) to handle energy emergencies.

The government kept a "social tranche" for households: CFA60 per kWh for the first 30 kWh in post-payment and CFA70 per kWh in prepayment. The new tariff structure is more progressive, varying by consumption and subscribed power. Domestic post-payment users with less than 2.2 kVA pay CFA93/kWh for 31-120 kWh and CFA130/kWh above that.

The reform separates domestic, professional, and industrial users. Medium-voltage large businesses face time-of-day pricing: CFA78/kWh off-peak, CFA87/kWh peak, up to CFA122/kWh at peak-demand hours; customers without time meters pay a flat CFA91/kWh. To foster efficiency, CEET removed reactive energy charges for industrial clients with a power factor of 0.90 or higher; those below face penalties.

Special tariffs for strategic zones: CFA82/kWh for the Free Zone and just CFA50/kWh for the Adetikope Industrial Platform (PIA), to boost regional competitiveness for energy-heavy investors. High Voltage: flat CFA80/kWh. Public lighting: flat CFA120/kWh. Officials may adjust tariffs over time based on costs, network performance, and energy access goals.
