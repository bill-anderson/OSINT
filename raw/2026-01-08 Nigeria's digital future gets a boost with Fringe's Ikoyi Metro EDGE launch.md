Nigeria's digital economy received an infrastructure upgrade with the launch of the Ikoyi Metro EDGE data center by Fringe Infrastructure Limited, in Ikoyi, a key commercial district of Lagos. It is the first regional node in Fringe's broader Metro EDGE platform, designed to bring high-performance compute and data services closer to users in dense urban centres — prioritising proximity for faster processing and improved reliability.

Sited in Lagos' financial and technology corridor, the facility places infrastructure close to banks, startups, multinationals and government institutions, supporting faster connectivity and helping organisations meet data-sovereignty and compliance requirements by keeping sensitive data hosted locally. Fringe positions the Ikoyi launch as the starting point for a wider rollout of Metro EDGE facilities across major African cities, signalling plans to support regional cloud providers, global tech firms and local enterprises with scalable, carrier-neutral infrastructure.
