Mali unveiled a set of new, locally developed digital platforms modernising access to public services across transport, health and public administration, part of the government's broader digital-transformation plan.

The Ministry of Transport introduced Sigui-Dolo, a platform for issuing secure transport documents (biometric driver's licences, vehicle registration cards), connected to TrésorPay (online payments) and SYDONIA (customs). Ten of 33 nationwide toll gates have been digitised. In health, the government introduced OpenClinic, an integrated health information system managing patient records across clinical, financial, pharmaceutical, laboratory and statistical areas; it is set to connect to the national identification system (NINA) and the compulsory health insurance scheme (AMO). The Ministry of Territorial Administration and Decentralisation launched the Integrated Prefectoral Information Management System (SIGIP) for workflow, data safeguarding, association monitoring and public-servant attendance.

Mali's wider agenda includes expanding legal-identity coverage (~90% of births registered). Earlier in 2025, authorities began developing a platform to support Malians abroad in accessing public services.
