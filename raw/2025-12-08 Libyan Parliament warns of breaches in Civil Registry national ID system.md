Libya's House of Representatives Defence and National Security Committee issued a warning over "manipulation and serious violations" within the **[[civil-registry-authority-libya|Civil Registry Authority]]**, saying breaches have allowed foreign nationals to unlawfully obtain Libyan national ID numbers and access benefits reserved for citizens.

The committee said the situation represents "a direct threat to national security and Libyan identity," stressing that tampering with official civil records undermines national sovereignty, and called on authorities to launch full investigations, refer those involved to the judiciary, and apply the toughest penalties. The warning comes amid heightened concern over the integrity of Libya's civil registry — central to elections, public services and citizenship rights — with lawmakers arguing that safeguarding it is essential to preventing identity fraud, illegal access to state benefits and compromised voter rolls.
