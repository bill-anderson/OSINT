## Description

UNICEF Kenya implements the Giga school connectivity programme in partnership with the Government of Kenya, ITU, and the EU. The EU provided a grant of EUR 9.8 million (~USD 10.5 million) to UNICEF Kenya to connect an additional 1,000 schools, reaching 369,400 children across all 47 counties. Building on earlier Giga work that connected 429 schools (230,000 learners) in 2019-2022, the EU-funded phase launched in October 2023. UNICEF Kenya leads procurement, leverages the National Optical Fibre Backbone Infrastructure (NOFBI), and has reduced school connectivity costs by 60 percent (from ~USD 120/month to ~USD 45/month per school). Part of the Team Europe Initiative (TEI) on Human-Centred Digitalisation in Kenya.
