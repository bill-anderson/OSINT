The Union of Comoros enacted its **Protection of Personal Data Law on 29 June 2021** — a comprehensive, GDPR-aligned framework with broad extraterritorial scope. It sets the standard principles and lawful bases; requires privacy notices, data-protection by design/default, processor agreements, security, **72-hour breach notification**, a mandatory **DPO** (public authorities + large-scale/sensitive processing) and records of processing; and grants full data-subject rights (access, erasure, portability, protection from solely-automated decisions). International transfers follow a risk-based model (adequacy / appropriate safeguards / derogations).

Critically, the law establishes a **National Authority for the Protection of Personal Data**, but as of **mid-2024 it had not been formally constituted or made operational** — the enforcement gap (the AfDB digitisation project separately funds standing up such an authority). Cite as analysis.
