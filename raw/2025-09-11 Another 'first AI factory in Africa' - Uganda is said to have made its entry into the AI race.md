The Aeonian Project, touted as potentially Africa's AI factory, is set for a swift rollout, with the first phase of deployment scheduled for the second half of 2026.

At its core, the project intends to retain critical computing power within the continent, reducing Africa's dependence on foreign entities storing its data, and to ensure homegrown researchers and developers build AI models in local languages rooted in African data.

The project's central component, the USIO, a 10MW sovereign supercomputer, was built in partnership with NVIDIA, AI infrastructure company MDCS.AI, and Automation NV of Belgium. It will use 100MW of surplus pre-transmission electricity from the government-owned 600MW Karuma Hydro Power Plant (K-HPP) on the River Nile.

"In the same way gold and oil once shaped economies, digital tokens will shape the next era of innovation. Africa must not just mine data, but also mint intelligence," said Niels Van Rees, Co-Founder of MDCS.AI. The machine targets applications in healthcare, life sciences, higher education and research.

In addition to using modular technologies to recycle heat and relying on natural river water for cooling, the plant will be connected to a new 2,500km fibre-optic backbone connecting Uganda to international cables via Kenya and Tanzania.

Per its official website, "Synectics Technologies is developing The Aeonian Project, which is a hybrid Tier-4 Plus (4+) off-grid green energy regional Hyperscale Data & High-Performance Computing Centre (DHPC) powered by 100MW of excess pre-transmission electricity from the government-owned 600MW Karuma Hydro Power Plant (K-HPP)." Data is sent via two core dark fibre-optic cables (leased) to the company's Kampala headquarters for remote surveillance and to the submarine cable in Kenya for global connection. The proposed service provider covers colocation, blockchain, data storage, AI and on-demand cloud services, and will provide Disaster Recovery Services (DRaaS) to existing regional data centers.

The article compares the project to "Project Mufungi" via Cassava Technologies (owned by Strive Masiyiwa), which partnered with NVIDIA to build an AI factory in Africa; Cassava said in April it intended to invest up to $720 million in the AI plant.
