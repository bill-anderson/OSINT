# Somalia to integrate more govt services with national ID as issuance reaches 1M

By Ayang Macdonald — Biometric Update, November 26, 2025

Somalia's NIRA has hailed growing adoption of the national ID card and says more government and private-sector services will be integrated with the ID system. Director General Abdiwali Ali Abdulle made the remarks as Somalia held its second national conference on the ID rollout in Mogadishu (November 24–25), under the theme "From Identity to Impact: Building Somalia's Digital Ecosystem."

Abdulle said at least one million citizens are already enrolled, and more than 24 public and private-sector services have integrated the system, with ID authentications for service access growing daily. Somalia targets 15 million issued national IDs by 2029 and hopes to introduce the digital version of the ID card in 2026. President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud, closing the event on November 25, urged every citizen — especially those in rural and hard-to-reach communities — to obtain the ID, calling it a backbone of Somalia's sustainable development. UNDP resident representative Lionel Laurens praised the government's commitment, noting Somalia presented a progress report at this year's ID4Africa AGM in May.
