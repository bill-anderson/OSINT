The Federal Government has made mandatory the use of the National Identification Number (NIN) to access all social services and government palliatives.

The Secretary to the Government of the Federation (SGF), George Akume, revealed this in Lagos while inaugurating an Inter-Ministerial retreat on the Nigeria Digital Identification for Development (ID4D) project. Akume (represented by Prof Babatunde Bolaji) emphasised that providing digital identity for all Nigerians remains a key priority of President Tinubu's administration. As chairman of the Project Ecosystem Steering Committee (PESC), he reiterated his commitment to backing NIMC reforms and called on all government agencies to support NIMC in building a robust, inclusive national identity system.

The Minister of State for Humanitarian Affairs and Poverty Reduction, Dr Tanko Sununu, reaffirmed his ministry's collaboration with NIMC to ensure NIN is used as a prerequisite for accessing social services and palliatives targeted at vulnerable groups. NIMC DG Dr Abisoye Coker-Odusote (represented by Ayobami Abiola) expressed gratitude for the support and pledged the commission's dedication to repositioning the national identity system so that every Nigerian and legal resident has a verifiable digital identity.
