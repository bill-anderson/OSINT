Morocco unveiled a digital-transformation framework known as **Digital X.0** — a proposed national law integrating **AI, data governance and digital identity** into the country's modernisation strategy. Presented by Amal El Fallah Seghrouchni, Minister Delegate for Digital Transition and Administrative Reform, the bill is under review by the General Secretariat of the Government and forms the legal foundation of **[[digital-morocco-2030|Maroc Digital 2030]]**.

**[[digital-x0-law|Digital X.0]]** focuses on three pillars: **data governance, digital identity, and interoperability**. It seeks to ensure secure and ethical data use under Morocco's **Law 09-08** on personal data protection, establish a national identity system giving citizens control over their information, and introduce a **traceable consent mechanism** regulating data exchanges between public institutions and private entities, with cybersecurity and transparency provisions. It marks Morocco's **first attempt to embed AI governance into national policy**, setting ethical and accountability principles for algorithms in public administration and private-sector applications — described as "balancing innovation with trust."
