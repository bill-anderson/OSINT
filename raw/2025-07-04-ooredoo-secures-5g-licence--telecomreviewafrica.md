# Ooredoo Algeria Secures 5G License Following Competitive Tender Process

Telecom Review Africa, 4 July 2025

Ooredoo Algeria has taken a major new step in its commitment to innovation and the modernization of the telecommunications sector in Algeria, having been selected as one of the successful bidders for the establishment and operation of a 5G network.

This announcement was officially made in Algiers by the Competitive Tender Commission of the Regulatory Authority for Post and Electronic Communications (ARPCE), during a public session held in accordance with Article 15 of Executive Decree No. 01-124 of May 9, 2001.

Following the evaluation of the bids submitted by the applicants, based on the technical and financial criteria defined in the tender specifications, the Commission ranked the offer of Wataniya Telecom Algeria (Ooredoo) in second place. As a result, Ooredoo Algeria has been awarded a license for the establishment and operation of a public fifth-generation (5G) mobile electronic communications network, including the provision of related services.

This designation marks a strategic milestone for Ooredoo as it prepares to introduce this fifth-generation technology into Algeria's digital landscape. The 5G network represents a profound transformation of the digital experience, offering ultra-fast speeds, low latency, and intelligent connectivity, all of which are features that will revolutionize the connectivity experience for citizens, businesses, and institutions alike.

Ooredoo's upcoming 5G network is expected to serve as a catalyst for Algeria's economic development and digital transformation. It will address the growing demand for connectivity while enabling new applications across key sectors such as healthcare, education, Industry 4.0, transportation, and smart cities.

Roni Tohme, CEO of Ooredoo Algeria, stated, "With great pride and honor, Ooredoo Algeria officially announces its selection by the Regulatory Authority for Post and Electronic Communications as one of the telecommunications companies qualified to obtain a license to launch and operate the 5G network in Algeria. This achievement marks a pivotal milestone in the journey of national digital transformation, reaffirming Ooredoo's vision and deep commitment to fostering innovation, supporting digital sovereignty, and actively contributing to the growth of the digital economy."

"In this new phase, we rely on the expertise of our teams, who have consistently demonstrated a high level of dedication and professionalism over the years. This recognition further strengthens our unwavering determination to deliver an exceptional digital experience that meets the aspirations of our customers, serves the needs of institutions and national bodies, and aligns with Algeria's evolving digital momentum."

With the launch of the fifth generation of mobile telecommunications, Ooredoo reaffirms its determination to offer its customers one of the most advanced technological experiences on the market, drawing on the international expertise of the Ooredoo Group, a global pioneer in commercial 5G deployment since 2018 in Qatar.
