Guinea-Bissau launched its **National Digital Transformation Strategy 2025-2030** in January 2025, built on six strategic axes including digital-infrastructure development. It wants to draw on Chad's experience in fibre-optic deployment; the matter was discussed on 27 August 2025 in N'Djamena between Chad's Telecommunications Minister Boukar Michel and his Bissau-Guinean counterpart Julio Mamadou Balde.

Guinea-Bissau connected to its first international submarine fibre-optic cable, Africa Coast to Europe (ACE), in March 2023, and in April 2025 approved commercial launch of US satellite ISP Starlink. However, international connectivity remains confined to the coast; the country still needs a **national backbone** to link its main cities. A 2022 World Bank report noted internet penetration reached only 32.5% in 2023 (ITU), with limited middle-mile infrastructure (mainly microwave links, an embryonic fibre metro network in Bissau, and a transport link to Sonatel's network in Senegal). No formal cooperation framework between the two countries has yet been signed.
