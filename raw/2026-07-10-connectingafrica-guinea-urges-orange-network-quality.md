# Guinea urges Orange to improve network quality

Guinea has called on telecoms operator Orange Guinea to strengthen network quality, expand coverage and lower Internet costs in support of the country's digital development goals. Guinea's Minister of Communication, Digital Economy and Innovation, Mourana Soumah met with Orange Guinea's CEO Ousmane Boly Traoré and appealed to him to improve the network service quality.

Guinea's push for improved service quality comes at a time when the country is prioritizing stronger digital infrastructure and expanded rural connectivity under its Simandou 2040 development program. Soumah said that the ministry is focused on expanding digital infrastructure through the rollout of a national backbone network, strengthening infrastructure resilience, improving rural connectivity and reducing mobile coverage gaps. The government said it plans to engage regularly with telcos to help advance the country's digital infrastructure objectives.

The engagement is part of a wider trend of African authorities tackling poor-quality telecom services, following similar moves by regulators and governments in Chad, Namibia, Nigeria, Zambia, Cameroon and Togo.
