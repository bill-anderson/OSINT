TechHive Advisory review of Mozambique's comprehensive draft Personal Data Protection Law, which aims to align with international standards, particularly the African Union's Malabo Convention (ratified by Mozambique in 2019). Scope: applies to processing of personal data within Mozambique by natural/legal persons, public or private, for economic and non-private purposes, in physical and digital formats, with exemptions for journalistic/artistic/literary purposes and carve-outs for national security/defence. Principles mirror international best practice (transparency, purpose limitation, legality/fairness, data minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, security, accountability).

Institutional framework (two-tier): a National Council for the Protection of Personal Data (CNPD), chaired by the Prime Minister, for strategic coordination; and a National Authority for the Protection of Personal Data (ANPD) for day-to-day regulation/enforcement — the law designates the existing Regulatory Authority for ICT to assume the ANPD role, as an independent public institution with administrative/financial autonomy. The government has 180 days from publication to issue implementing regulations; the law enters into force 90 days after publication. Obligations include lawful basis, restrictions on sensitive/credit/children's data, a mandatory Data Protection Officer, strong data-subject rights, and a risk-based approach to international data transfers (adequacy findings; ANPD authorisation otherwise).
