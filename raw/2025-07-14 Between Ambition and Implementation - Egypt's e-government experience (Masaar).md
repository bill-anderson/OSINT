# Between Ambition and Implementation — A Critical Reading of Egypt's E-Government Experience (Masaar)

A **rights-based critical reading** of Egypt's two decades of e-government by Masaar. On the UN **EGDI**, Egypt ranked **95th of 193 (2024)** — below its 2010 peak (86th) after fluctuations. The paper argues the EGDI's three pillars (online-service delivery, human capital, ICT infrastructure) capture the technical dimension but omit **legislative/administrative frameworks, equality of access among social groups, and protection of fundamental rights** (privacy, access to information, redress).

It examines Egypt's e-government evolution through a rights lens — regulatory and human-rights requirements, equitable access for marginalised groups — and offers recommendations for rights-aware e-government. Analytical/civil-society reference; cite for framing, not as primary evidence.
