Uganda's NIRA announced that more than 5.3 million people have already renewed their national IDs in the nationwide renewal and registration exercise that started on May 27. The authority said that while there was considerable progress, the pace didn't appear fast enough to meet set goals.

The campaign is officially set to run six months and is expected to touch 33 million persons: about 15.8 million ID card renewals and 17.2 million new registrations. A District Registrar in Kampala, Claire Ollama, praised the enthusiasm but feared it could stretch to nine months if average weekly renewals don't exceed the current 840,000. "Every week matters and every ID counts," she said, urging communities to rally, parishes to mobilize, and individuals to show up early.

This week, Ollama also announced that 100,000 new biometric ID cards have been printed and are ready for collection, but individuals must first receive an SMS notification before collecting. The campaign is part of government efforts to update the national population registry and modernise the ID management system. One innovation with the new ID card system is the addition of the iris biometrics modality.
