Malabo, 27 May 2025 — the Director General of Telecommunications and New Technologies chaired a high-level meeting to coordinate the **Plan Nacional de Cajeros Automáticos y Terminales de Pago Electrónico** (national ATM/POS plan), a strategic initiative driven by the Prime Minister. Held at the Ministry of Transport, Posts and AI Systems, with telecoms, banking, health, tourism and hospitality representatives, to define a roadmap for deploying ATMs and POS terminals at critical locations.

Key tension surfaced: **connectivity is the main obstacle**. BANGE representatives warned that low-quality connectivity/internet outages severely compromise electronic financial devices, and asked for faster, more stable networks. GETESA's representative urged closer bank–operator collaboration on installing/maintaining the supporting network (fibre or radio links). **GITGE** (responsible for national fibre) — via deputy director Marcos Ncogo — stressed that last-mile fibre expansion is essential ("everything this project needs is fibre") and urged decisive government investment. **Muni Dinero** presented its mobile-money app as a widely adopted CEMAC-region solution. Next step: a detailed technical study of connectivity, location and support at each planned ATM/POS site.
