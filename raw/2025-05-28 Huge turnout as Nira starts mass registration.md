*Ground-level launch-day account of the 27 May 2025 start. The Tororo RDC's claim that 'a survey shows more Kenyans than Ugandans currently hold Ugandan national IDs' is attributed to an unnamed survey — an assertion, not a fact, and not to be repeated as one.*

The National Identification and Registration Authority (Nira) launched a countrywide mass enrolment and renewal exercise on 27 May 2025, expected to run for six to 10 months. Ugandans flocked to centres across the country to renew their national identity cards.

In Adjumani District, local leaders raised concerns about registration of refugees. Adjumani District Chairperson Ben Anyama warned that close cultural and language ties between communities in Uganda and South Sudan (Madi, Kuku, Kakwa, Acholi) could make it difficult to distinguish citizens from refugees, risking misidentification or fraud. Adjumani hosts about 244,374 refugees (2024 UNHCR statistics).

The Nira District Registration Officer, Mr Fred Balaba, said stringent security measures were implemented: first-time applicants must present an LC1 letter with parents' names, tribe and clan, endorsed by the District Internal Security Officer (DISO), to screen out non-citizens. The Koboko District IT Officer noted the exercise was delayed by insufficient staff and that thorough screening was needed given the DRC and South Sudan borders. Per 2016 records, Koboko captured 88,878 people but only 66,000 received IDs; of Koboko's 271,781 population, only 217,525 have been registered.

In Arua City residents queued early. The Tororo Resident District Commissioner, Mr Sadiq Bategena Bakale, warned Nira officials not to solicit bribes and warned security officers and LC chairpersons not to recommend non-citizens; he claimed a survey shows more Kenyans than Ugandans currently hold Ugandan national IDs. Tororo District chairperson John Okeya urged officials to collect accurate data, citing older persons whose misrepresented biodata blocked access to the Social Action Grant for Elderly persons (SAGE).

The Nira Tororo Area Manager said the district received 75 biometric digital kits, with officers stationed at parish level moving deeper into villages as needed. In Kabale, registration began with UPDF personnel, police and essential workers. In Masaka City, the RCC warned against impatient citizens bribing staff to jump queues.
