Kenya allocated **KES 15.13 billion (US$117 million)** to digital ID and related projects for FY2024/25, disbursed by the State Department for Immigration.

Of the total, KES 6.88bn ($53.3m) goes to population management — issuing 7.5m digital/third-generation ID cards, 1.5m biometric passports, 6.3m birth certificates and 2.3m biometric visas, and rolling out the **Unique Personal Identifier (UPI)** from birth — and KES 7.03bn ($54.4m) to migration and citizen services (two high-capacity passport printers, fast-tracking the visa-free regime, facial recognition at nine one-stop border posts, and onboarding 33,800 more services to eCitizen, which launched in June 2023 with ~5,000). A further KES 1.22bn ($9.4m) covers general administration.
