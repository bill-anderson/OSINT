# EBRD — Yas Kenya/Senegal network expansion, 2026

The EBRD committed a facility of up to €170 million (part of a headline up to €270 million package) to pan-African operator Yas to expand 4G, 5G and fibre networks in Senegal and Kenya, in the Bank's first Senegal project and first sub-Saharan local-currency and A/B loan structure.

## Description

"The European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) is providing a senior loan of up to €270 million to pan-African telecoms operator Yas to fund network expansion in Senegal and Kenya." "The deal marks the EBRD's first investment in Senegal and its first local-currency financing and A/B loan structure in sub-Saharan Africa." "The committed facility totals up to €170 million, split between a €100 million EBRD A-loan, a B-loan of up to €50 million to be syndicated to institutional investors, and a local-currency facility of up to €20 million equivalent in Kenyan shillings. Amsterdam-based impact private credit fund ILX Fund will take a sizeable position in the B-loan. A further uncommitted facility of up to €100 million is available for future Yas acquisitions and capital expenditure." "Yas, owned by AXIAN Telecom, will use the funding to expand 4G, 5G and fibre infrastructure across both markets." In Kenya the capital modernises the Zuku fibre network (Yas acquired Zuku's parent Wananchi Group in 2025).
