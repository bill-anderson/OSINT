*Reports the Bank of Uganda 2023 annual report — the annual report is the primary and is not held. The mechanism by which the ID becomes a gate on financial life: 74 institutions gained NIRA database access under the same Registration of Persons Act 2015 provision the High Court declined to disturb in June 2025.*

Uganda's central bank, the Bank of Uganda (BoU), says up to 74 financial institutions now have access to the database of the National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA) for purposes of digital Know Your Customer (KYC) verification. The details are contained in the BoU 2023 annual report.

There were 74 financial institutions with access to the NIRA database listed by the Bank as of June, including commercial banks, microfinance institutions, a forex bureau, financial technology companies, insurance companies and one telecommunications company. Some are at full operation while others are still at the trial phase.

The Bank notes the move is in line with enforcement of a provision of the Registration of Persons Act 2015 which states that a national ID card or national identification number shall be required to access certain services such as financial services; implementation has been ongoing since 2020. Accessing the NIRA database enables companies to complete KYC checks by verifying and authenticating identity using data held by the national ID authority. The BoU posits that compliance not only enables secure and seamless access to financial services but also drives financial inclusion.

As part of measures to enhance transaction security and combat fraud, the BoU issued a directive in April requiring ID verification for all financial transactions of one million Ugandan shillings (US$260) and above. The move was met with criticism as some citizens said it would slow down business.

The article notes coordination between national ID systems and the financial sector is a growing trend, citing a similar partnership recently formed between banking and ID authorities in nearby Somalia.
