18 May 2026 - NW1941

Masango, Ms B to ask the Minister of Social Development

(1) With reference to the continued biometric verification of SA Social Security Agency (SASSA) grant beneficiaries, what total number of beneficiaries of each type of grant have been verified (a) to date and (b) in each month since the commencement of the verification process; (2) what (a) total number of grants for each type of grant have been suspended or terminated as a result of beneficiaries failing to undergo verification (i) to date and (ii) in each month since the commencement of the verification process and (b) were the reasons for non-verification; (3) whether her department has conducted any research on the most effective means of communicating to grant beneficiaries the requirement to undergo verification; if not, what is the position in this regard; if so, what were the findings; (4) what (a) total number of complaints and/or reports have been received from beneficiaries who attempted to undergo verification but were unable to do so and (b) is the nature of the complaints; (5) what total number of ineligible beneficiaries of each grant type were identified through the verification process (a) to date (b) in each month since the commencement of the verification process?

NB:

1. A grant is suspended 2 months after a beneficiary was notified to conduct a review and has not yet done such.
2. If after suspension, the beneficiary still does not come forward to conduct the review, the grant will be lapsed after one months' notice of such. The grant may also be lapsed if the beneficiary conducts a review and is found no-longer to qualify for the grant.
3. In collaboration with banking institutions, SASSA successfully initiated income testing for approximately 6 million Child Support Grant (CSG) recipients. This marks a significant milestone in enhancing the targeting and integrity of grant distribution. Hence during the Q3 targeted clients were CSG only.
4. No suspensions were effected during Q1–Q2, as the Agency introduced the fourth payment day for clients targeted for reviews. In addition, SMS notifications were only implemented in July 2025.

(b) The reasons for non-verification include, but are not limited to:

- General failure by beneficiaries to respond to notifications and follow-up requests issued by the Agency (whether for reviews, life certification, or biometric enrolment).
- Failure to complete life certification after being requested to do so.
- Unsuccessful facial verification attempts when using online systems. In these instances, the clients are notified to do fingerprint biometric in the local office.

3. The method for notifying a beneficiary of the need to conduct a review is provided for in the regulations to the Social Assistance Act. SASSA, while maintaining the formal legislated communication method, also conducts additional awareness campaigns and public announcements. SASSA is also exploring/ implementing the following direct channels (over and above the legislated requirements).

In support of beneficiary awareness - SASSA developed a Beneficiary Biometric Enrolment Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs) poster, which explains in plain language:

- What biometric enrolment and verification entail;
- Who is required to undergo biometric enrolment;
- Why biometric verification is necessary to protect beneficiaries and prevent fraud;
- Where and how beneficiaries can undergo biometric enrolment; and
- Assurance that biometric enrolment does not delay or stop grants, except in cases where identity cannot be confirmed or fraud is detected.

4. At present SASSA has not received any complaints regarding fingerprint biometric enrolment through any of its official channels (call centre, email or local office help desk); however, has received 7779 complaints about its Electronic Facial biometric system, that is used on the online application portals. These issues may be caused by clients attempting verification under poor conditions, such as inadequate lighting or unstable network connectivity at the time of verification, or not having a biometric image captured at the department of Home Affairs, which normally happens when a person applies for smart ID card. In such instances, clients are encouraged to complete their biometric enrolment using fingerprints at their nearest local office.

5. Refer to the response to question 2, total number of cases lapsed, this number represents the total number of grants lapsed due to the beneficiary not meeting the qualifying criteria
