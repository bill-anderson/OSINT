# Djibouti advances bill to create a National Cybersecurity Authority (ANC)

On **Monday 10 November 2025**, Minister of Digital Economy and Innovation **Mariam Hamadou Ali** presented a bill creating the **National Cybersecurity Authority (ANC)** to a National Assembly parliamentary committee. The **independent authority** would set national standards and protection procedures for critical sectors, run a national centre to monitor and respond to cyber incidents, promote international cybercrime cooperation, and expand training and awareness. The plan was first mentioned on **1 October 2025** at the Arab Council of Ministers Responsible for Cybersecurity (Riyadh). The ANC consolidates Djibouti's framework, complementing the **National Cybersecurity Strategy**, the **[[djibouti-digital-code]]**, and the annual National Cybersecurity Week.

**International cooperation:** in **late October 2025** in Hanoi, Djibouti signed the **UN Convention on Cybercrime** (one of 71 signatories, incl. 21 African countries); it had already ratified the AU **[[malabo-convention]]** in **November 2023**; and in November 2024 strengthened ties with Qatar. Djibouti sits in the **fourth tier of the ITU 2024 Global Cybersecurity Index** (11.84/20 on the legislative pillar), aiming to become a regional tech hub by 2035.
