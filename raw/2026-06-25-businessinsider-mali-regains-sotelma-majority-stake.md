Business Insider Africa (reporting confirmed by Maroc Telecom via Financial Afrik). Mali has regained majority ownership of SOTELMA (Societe des telecommunications du Mali; commercial brand Moov Africa Malitel), raising the state's stake from 49% to 56% and reducing Morocco's Maroc Telecom to a minority shareholder. Follows a 160 billion FCFA (~$277m; ~$264m per one report) agreement that renewed SOTELMA's operating licence and restructured ownership. In 2009 Mali had sold a 51% stake to Maroc Telecom for ~EUR275m/$294m. SOTELMA had ~8.5m subscribers (H1 2024, ~37% share); Orange Mali remains market leader (~54.9%). Framed by the transition government as a major win for economic sovereignty and state control over strategic assets (mirroring moves in mining/energy across the AES). Government pledged to maintain jobs and strengthen SOTELMA's investment capacity. (Corroborated: the stake increase was tied to extending SOTELMA's licence to 2039 per telecom-market analysts.)
