The Gambia has officially launched its first Inclusive Instant Payment System — a national interoperability platform enabling real-time digital payments for 3m+ Gambians across banks, mobile money operators, fintechs and public institutions. Branded **BANTABA 2.0** ("a common meeting place" in Mandinka) under the Gambia Interoperability Switch Program, it allows funds to move instantly between accounts regardless of provider.

Led by the **Central Bank of The Gambia** and the Ministry of Communications and Digital Economy, with support from the Institute for Inclusive Digital Africa (IIDiA) and built on **Mojaloop**, BANTABA 2.0 establishes a shared national backbone connecting banks, MMOs, MFIs, fintechs and PSPs through a single interoperable switch with real-time clearing and settlement. PS Hassan M. M. Jallow called a low-cost interoperable national switch "foundational digital public infrastructure for economic transformation"; Dr Paul Mendy, Second Deputy Governor of the CBG, stressed interoperability and real-time settlement. Following over a year of preparation (technical training from October 2024 with the Mojaloop Foundation) and a successful pilot, the platform is operated by **Gamswitch**, the national payment switch, ensuring domestic ownership.
