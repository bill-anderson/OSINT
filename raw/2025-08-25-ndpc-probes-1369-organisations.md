The Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) initiated its most extensive enforcement action to date, launching a sector-wide investigation into 1,369 organisations for suspected violations of the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023. Targeted companies span sensitive sectors: 795 financial institutions, 392 insurance brokers, 35 insurance companies, 10 pension firms, and 136 gaming operators. Head of Legal, Enforcement and Regulations Babatunde Bamigboye said the NDPC issued a 21-day compliance notice requiring proof of filing 2024 compliance audit returns, appointment of a Data Protection Officer, a summary of data protection measures, and evidence of registration as a Data Controller/Processor of Major Importance. Non-compliance could bring severe consequences.
