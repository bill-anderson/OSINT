# Towards a 5G-Enabled, Digitalized Namibia in 2026

Feature/backgrounder on Namibia's 5G and digital-transformation trajectory. Namibia's government, with the Office of the Prime Minister, completed a Digital Government Strategic Roadmap for 2024–2026 ("Digital First Services for All") with goals including transforming priority public services, reducing paperwork, and issuing digital IDs to at least 25% of the population by 2026, across six pillars (digital identity, interoperability, cybersecurity, etc.). This sits within the National Digital Strategy (NDS) launched in 2025 and NDP6, charting infrastructure and e-government transformation through 2025/26–2029/30, with targets such as raising internet use from 53% to 90% by 2030.

Geographic constraint: Namibia's population density averages 3.76 people per km² (one of the world's lowest), making fibre backhaul and tower rollout expensive and leaving rural communities on 2G/3G or unserved. The 5G rollout emphasises scalability, resilience and efficiency (SDN, virtualisation, edge computing) via a phased, city-first model anchored in upgrades to existing sites, with global technology partners accelerating deployment.
