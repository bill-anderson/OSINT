# Driving Digital Inclusion Within the AfCFTA Framework (SAIIA OP 338, Oct 2022)

A **think-tank analysis** (SAIIA Occasional Paper 338, by Michelle Chivunga and
Alastair Tempest) of the drivers of **digital inclusion** in Africa and the role of
the **[[afcfta|African Continental Free Trade Area]]** in harmonising digital policy.
Named-analyst analysis — admissible as framing, not evidence. Full paper held as the
companion artefact.

## Argument (as summarised by the authors)

- A **digital divide** splits populations into the data-rich and data-poor; **women
  and youth**, particularly in rural and poor areas, are most vulnerable to
  exclusion.
- Failing to close the divide leaves countries falling further behind peers, with
  consequences for trade, investment and social stability.
- **Education and training** (including upskilling, and digitalisation embedded in
  primary/secondary/tertiary curricula) should be at the core of government efforts.
- Governments can also spur inclusion via an investment-friendly policy/regulatory
  environment, hard and soft infrastructure, using digital tech to improve their own
  service delivery, and public–private partnerships.
- The **AfCFTA** is an important vehicle through which member states can formulate,
  harmonise and cooperate on digital policies and rules to expand intra-African
  trade.
