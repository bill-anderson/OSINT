The Government of Sierra Leone, through the Ministry of Communication, Technology, and Innovation (MoCTI) and the Ministry of Technical and Higher Education (MTHE), signed a strategic agreement with ELCOME SL Ltd. to provide high-speed internet connectivity to 12 public and private universities, powered by Starlink.

Currently, internet access in Sierra Leone's educational institutions remains limited. The government has been working on initiatives like "Giga," a global program with UNICEF and the Directorate of Science, Technology, and Innovation (DSTI), aiming to connect all schools.

Minister Salima Bah emphasized internet access as essential for students. She noted Starlink's entry has spurred competition among service providers: "Today, three local companies are providing Starlink services, each with its own unique approach." The first phase focuses on universities, with plans to extend connectivity to vocational schools next.

The initiative aligns with the National Broadband Strategy (2023-2028) and supports the National Digital Development Policy (NDDP), approved in 2021.
