# Senegal's Technological New Deal – A Digital Ambition Tested by Digital Rights

Digital-rights analysis (Paradigm Initiative, a named African civil-society organisation — attributed view,
not fact) arguing the **[[new-deal-technologique|New Deal Horizon 2034]]** **omits explicit provisions for
citizens' digital rights**. Across its four pillars (digital sovereignty, public-service digitisation,
digital economy, African leadership), the author flags gaps against precedents:

- **Online expression:** 2021 internet shutdowns/social-media restrictions during the Ousmane Sonko unrest —
  risk of recurrence absent a protective legal framework.
- **Data protection:** **[[senegal-data-protection-law|Law No. 2008-12]]** is outdated; revision reported
  underway. Without it, operators/platforms may exploit data without informed consent.
- **Equitable access:** persistent rural digital divide (evident during COVID-19 remote schooling).
- **Cybersecurity:** rising cyberattacks with citizens/businesses ill-prepared.

Recommendations: update the data-protection law (per the Londa Senegal 2023 report), guarantee online
expression, ensure universal affordable access, strengthen cybersecurity, and embed citizen participation.
