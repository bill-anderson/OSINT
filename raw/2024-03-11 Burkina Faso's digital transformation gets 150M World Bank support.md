The World Bank, through the IDA, approved **$150 million** (early 2024) for Burkina Faso's **"Digital Acceleration Project"** — the funding behind what the government brands **PACTDIGITAL** ([[pactdigital]]).

The project aims to expand digital connectivity to vulnerable populations — internally displaced persons and underserved, climate-vulnerable communities — develop digital public services, and train public-administration staff and young people in digital skills. Country director Clara Ana De Sousa framed it as improving broadband access and fostering competition and investment in the digital economy, education and health; Task Team Leader Tounwendé Alain Sawadogo cited broadband for displacement-affected areas and large-scale WURI unique-ID enrolment.
