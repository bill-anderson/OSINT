# Djibouti–Visa "Smart Wallet" national digital wallet MoU

Djibouti's government signed a **memorandum of understanding with [[visa]] on Tuesday 16 September 2025** to develop a national digital wallet, **"Smart Wallet"** — positioned as both a digital-payments channel and a gateway to government services and digital identity "within a secure and inclusive environment" (Ministry of Digital Economy and Innovation). State-owned **[[djibouti-telecom]]** amplified the announcement, signalling national buy-in.

- **Financial-inclusion rationale:** the World Bank reports only **~25% of Djibouti's 1.1 million people hold a bank account**. Authorities aim to **reduce cash usage by 60% within three years** of launch.
- **Biometric layer:** **[[tech5]]** will provide the biometric/digital-ID onboarding security (face, iris, fingerprint); Visa had formalised a seven-year collaboration with Tech5 earlier that month. A **Visa technical mission visited Djibouti on 15–16 September 2025** for infrastructure assessment and co-design workshops.
- **Inclusion of the unconnected:** citizens without smartphones or internet are to access the system through **post offices and "Maisons du Citoyen"** service centres; the wallet is intended to connect with local banks and existing payment systems.
- **Legal foundation:** the wallet rests on the June-2025 **[[djibouti-digital-code]]** (data protection, e-commerce, cybersecurity).

**This is an MoU** — a statement of intent, not a definitive agreement — with no detailed rollout calendar published. The Mobile ID (launched Feb 2026) can be stored in the Smart Wallet.
