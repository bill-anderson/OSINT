A comprehensive study commissioned by the Uganda Communications Commission (UCC) — "Impact of the Current Telecommunications Taxation Policy on the Communications Sector" — has proposed sweeping tax reforms to accelerate Uganda's digital economy by lowering internet-access costs and expanding infrastructure investment. It argues Uganda's telecom tax structure is among the most burdensome in East Africa, limiting broadband adoption.

The telecom value chain faces multiple overlapping taxes: a 12% excise duty on internet data, 18% VAT, mobile money withdrawal charges up to 0.5%, and import duties on ICT equipment and devices. Key proposals: cut excise duty on data and airtime from 12% to 5%; reduce VAT on mobile data from 18% to 14% short-term (with longer-term removal considered); eliminate/reduce import duties, excise and VAT on entry-level 4G/5G smartphones; reclassify internet access as an essential public service; and introduce capex incentives (VAT exemptions on towers, power systems, backhaul) targeted at rural/underserved areas. Reforms would be implemented with the Ministry of Finance and Uganda Revenue Authority.
