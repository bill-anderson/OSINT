Benin's **Directorate General of Taxes (DGI)** unveiled new online services (23 May 2024) to streamline tax payments for small businesses and property owners in **Cotonou**, with nationwide expansion planned — building on the **e-services.impots.bj** portal first launched in **2017**. DG Nicolas Yenoussi said the platform "significantly reduces the cost of tax compliance and the time required."

Framed within Benin's ambition — since President Patrice Talon took office in 2016 — to become West Africa's digital hub (accelerated after COVID-19 in 2021), the DGI reported that gradual digitisation contributed to a **13.1% rise in tax revenue between 2017 and 2021**, versus 5.7% over 2012–2016.
