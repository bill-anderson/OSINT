Libyan biometrics provider **Al Itissan Al-Jadeed** has been contracted to upgrade the passport and national-ID systems of the Republic of **Niger** — a rare signal of a Libyan identity-tech firm exporting abroad. PPP framework documents were signed 27 June in Niamey: two MoUs between Niger's Minister of State/Interior Major General Mohamed Toumba and the Libyan company's director Wissal Samtali — the first on financing, construction and maintenance of a **biometric passport system**; the second on upgrading Niger's national-ID architecture with a secure national digital ID.

The passport will use polycarbonate materials with biometric chips, and the contractor will build a digital archiving system. The move follows the Confederation of Sahel States (AES — Niger, Burkina Faso, Mali) launching a common passport after withdrawal from **[[ecowas]]**. See [[NER]].
