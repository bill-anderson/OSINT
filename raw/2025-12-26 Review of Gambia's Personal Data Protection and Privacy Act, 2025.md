TechHive Advisory review of The Gambia's enacted **Personal Data Protection and Privacy Act** — a landmark following validation of the National Data Policy earlier in the year and the conclusion of a legislative process that had stalled for several years. Core objectives: protect individual privacy, ensure lawful data processing, and establish an independent oversight **Commission**.

Scope: applies broadly to automated and non-automated processing, with **extraterritorial jurisdiction** (controllers in The Gambia and those outside whose processing relates to individuals within the country); exceptions for purely personal/household activities and anonymous data. It codifies fairness, transparency, lawfulness, purpose limitation, data minimisation, accuracy and security, and establishes **seven lawful bases** (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interest, archiving/research/statistics with safeguards). **Sensitive data** (including genetic, biometric and health data, and data revealing racial origin or political opinions) is generally prohibited absent a lawful basis and specific condition. Data-subject rights are robust: to be informed, access (free copy), rectification, erasure, restriction, objection (absolute for direct marketing), protection from solely-automated decision-making/profiling, Commission assistance, and remedy (complaints and compensation).
