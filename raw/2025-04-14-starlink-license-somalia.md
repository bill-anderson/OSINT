# Starlink launches in Somalia, expanding satellite internet to underserved regions

Mogadishu (HOL), Monday April 14, 2025

Elon Musk's satellite internet service, Starlink, is now operational in Somalia after the federal government issued a license on Sunday, capping off more than two years of negotiations with the Ministry of Telecommunications. "We have been in talks with this company (Starlink) for a period of two and a half years. We gave them the license today," said Mustafa Yasiin, Director of Communication at the ministry, in a video published by the state-run Somali National News Agency (SONNA).

The approval makes Somalia the latest African nation to authorize Starlink, following rollouts in Liberia and Niger earlier in 2025. The service uses low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites to deliver high-speed, low-latency internet even in regions with limited or no traditional infrastructure. While Somalia already connects to several undersea fibre-optic cables, vast stretches remain poorly connected, particularly in conflict-affected regions. As of 2022, only about 30% of Somalia's population had internet access, according to World Bank data. The license issuance was marked by a ceremony in Mogadishu attended by senior government figures and Starlink representatives. Musk confirmed the launch on X: "Starlink now in Somalia!"

(Note: this April piece records the licensing/authorization; nationwide commercial launch followed in August 2025.)
