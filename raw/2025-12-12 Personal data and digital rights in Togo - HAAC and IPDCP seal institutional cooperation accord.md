Togo's High Authority for Audiovisual and Communication (Haute Autorite de l'Audiovisuel et de la Communication, HAAC) and the Personal Data Protection Authority (Instance de Protection des Donnees a Caractere Personnel, IPDCP) sealed an institutional cooperation accord on 10 December 2025 on the future of media regulation. The two institutions said it is urgent to protect personal data in an audiovisual landscape where information circulates faster than vigilance. The memorandum aims to establish a framework of institutional cooperation to promote press freedom while guaranteeing the protection of personal data and digital rights.

Under the partnership, the HAAC pledges to integrate data protection into its procedures, its monitoring and its awareness-raising actions. The IPDCP will bring its expertise in a context where media practices evolve faster than norms. The two presidents stressed the urgency of working together, arguing that press freedom cannot hold if the protection of digital rights remains fragile.

With this accord, the HAAC now authorises the IPDCP to access its control and monitoring room (salle de controle et de monitoring) - a mechanism allowing more precise tracking of broadcast content, faster control of possible abuses, and above all a coordinated reaction in case of a breach of personal data. The memorandum promises a safer, more responsible audiovisual space better prepared for the demands of the digital age.
