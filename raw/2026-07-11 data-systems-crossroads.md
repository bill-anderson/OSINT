## Key facts

- Three-part crisis diagnosis: **legitimacy/trust/political will** (official statistics competing with
  misinformation and AI-generated content; political interference), **financing/sustainability** (ODA for
  statistics declined significantly in 2024–25; **~two-thirds of NSOs** surveyed by UNSD anticipated or
  faced budget cuts), and **capacity gaps** (technical/soft/leadership skills; low data use).
- Two cross-cutting stressors: **AI** (risks widening the gap between well-resourced and constrained
  offices) and **inclusive/participatory data** (citizen data, intersectional analysis).
- Proposes a framework with two paths — **Path One: optimisation** (operational excellence within
  existing frameworks) vs **Path Two: transformation** (new institutional/financing arrangements,
  privacy-preserving infrastructure, participatory approaches). Foundation for a forthcoming policy-brief
  series. Authors: Noe (Open Data Watch) & Fogarassy (PARIS21), guided by Badiee & Jütting; Hewlett-funded.
