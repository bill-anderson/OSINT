The Tanzanian government has invested 131 billion shillings to modernize the national ID card production system, aiming to expedite the issuance process and improve service delivery. Home Affairs Minister Patrobas Katambi announced the 131 billion shilling (approximately $56.3 million) allocation during an inspection visit to the National Identification Authority (NIDA) headquarters in Dar es Salaam.

The investment targets critical technological infrastructure designed to streamline the production of national identity cards, addressing long-standing bottlenecks in the issuance process. National ID cards are increasingly essential for accessing banking services, social welfare programs, and mobile communications in the East African nation of approximately 65 million people. The new technology is expected to significantly reduce processing times, which have historically stretched to several months in some regions.

During his inspection, Minister Katambi examined the complete production workflow, from data collection to card manufacturing and distribution. He also acknowledged NIDA's contribution of 38 billion shillings in dividends to the central government treasury. As NIDA implements the technological upgrades throughout 2026, officials expect to dramatically increase daily production capacity and establish more service centers in underserved regions.
