# Mixed reactions over Starlink license

*Public Eye, 22 April 2025. By Rethabile Mohono.*

The Lesotho Communications Authority (LCA) has granted the country's first satellite internet licence to Starlink, sparking excitement and suspicion. Many link the move to recent tensions between Lesotho and the US: weeks earlier the Trump administration imposed a 50% tariff on Lesotho imports (since paused for 90 days, with a 10% baseline remaining). Trade Minister Mokhethi Shelile dismissed the US claims as "baseless," but public discourse has cast the Starlink licence as "a strategic olive branch to Washington."

The LCA confirmed the 10-year operating licence for individuals and businesses. Starlink first applied in April 2024; the process included two rounds of public consultation (the most recent concluded March 2025). In response, Vodacom Lesotho (VCL) submitted an appeal calling for Starlink to establish local shareholding despite no legal mandate; MD Mohale Ralebitso (in comments submitted 20 March) argued Starlink should contribute through jobs, taxes and dividends, and obtain a unified licence with local obligations if operating independently.

VCL, operating since 1996, is 20% owned by Basotho through the Sekha-Metsi Consortium, the remainder by Vodacom Group (majority Vodafone). The article notes eSwatini's Starlink pricing (Gen 2 kit reduced to R6,800; R950 monthly) as a regional comparison.
