# Somalia's Digital ID — A Milestone on the Roadmap Towards Effective Governance and Service Delivery

> ## Excerpt
> Analytical essay tracing Somalia's ID architecture: DADSOM (Law 41, Dec 2020) →
> NIRA (Mar 2023); the NIN ("Tirsi Aqoonsi"); HUBIYE, e-Aqoonsi and the Certificate
> Delivery System; mass registration toward 15m by end-2029. <16% held ID (2022 SIHBS).

---

Somali governments' quest for identification has been on the agenda since the re-institution of the Somali state. One of the fundamental powers of the state is the ability to identify and locate individuals. In fragile states, personal data is either not captured systematically or scattered across uncoordinated databases created by humanitarian agencies, security actors or external partners; it is believed that half of Somalia's population data are captured by these external entities. Fewer than 16% of the population currently have some form of government-issued ID (2022 Somali Integrated Household Budget Survey). A report by the Heritage Institute for Policy Studies examined the fragmented landscape.

The move toward a unified system began with the creation of DADSOM under Law No. 41 in December 2020, consolidated in March 2023 with the establishment of the National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA) under the Identification and Registration Act. NIRA issues every citizen and legal resident a unique National Identification Number (NIN), known as Tirsi Aqoonsi in Somali, using biometrics including fingerprint and facial recognition.

Three and a half years after establishment, NIRA operates three registration centres. Applicants submit biographic information (forms in Somali, Arabic and English); identity proofing follows via documents or verified community witnesses; biometric data (fingerprints and photograph) is captured, with plans to add iris scans; all information undergoes validation and deduplication before a permanent 11-digit National ID number is issued. Credentials may be provided as a certificate, digital mobile ID or physical card.

New components: the HUBIYE Verification Platform (real-time authentication), the e-Aqoonsi Mobile App, and the Certificate Delivery System (CDS).

NIRA is preparing a national scale-up: a mass registration campaign to register all 3.5 million residents of Mogadishu, extend across Banadir region, and reach 15 million people nationwide by the end of 2029. NIRA is convening the second National ID Conference (SNIDC) this week.
