The Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe (RBZ) has reined in bank charges, setting a 2% ceiling on cash withdrawal fees and a 1.5% cap on point-of-sale (POS) charges, while scrapping account balance inquiry fees, in measures aimed at cutting banking costs. The decision followed years of depositor complaints about high charges.

Presenting the 2026 Monetary Policy Statement, RBZ governor John Mushayavanhu directed all banking institutions and deposit-taking microfinance institutions to implement by March 31, 2026: cash withdrawal charges capped at 2% (banking halls and ATMs, US$ and ZiG); POS charges capped at 1.5% for local and international cards (maximum US$20 or ZiG equivalent, no minimum fee); removal of account balance inquiry charges on all banking and mobile banking platforms; removal of cash deposit fees; and card issuance/replacement fees limited to cost-recovery.

The central bank also reduced real-time gross settlement (RTGS) charges to US$0.80 (from US$0.90) for Window 1 and 2 transactions, and US$1.10 (from US$1.20) for Window 3. Separately (per related reporting), the MPS raised ZIPIT and mobile money transaction limits from ZiG 8,000 to ZiG 13,000 per transaction with a monthly cap of ZiG 50,000, to promote wider ZiG usage, and introduced larger ZiG banknote denominations (10, 20, 50, 100, 200).
