Togo's Société d'Infrastructures Numériques (SIN) and ST Digital have signed a strategic partnership to launch private cloud services at the Lomé Data Centre (LDC).

Signed by SIN's Director General Attia Byll and ST Digital Togo Director General Justine Adebiyi, the partnership aims to strengthen Togo's digital sovereignty and support the development of local digital services.

Wholly owned by the Togolese government, SIN operates the LDC, a Tier III–certified facility.

By contrast, ST Digital provides sovereign cloud services, sovereign AI, and digital solutions across West Africa.

Under the agreement, Togo's local companies' data will be hosted and processed in the country as the initiative aims to reduce reliance on foreign infrastructure while improving the security and control of strategic data.

The partners said the collaboration will focus on offering cloud and AI services at competitive prices while strengthening local expertise through continuing education programs.

The two parties said they plan to position the LDC as a leading digital hub in West Africa, with the new services aimed at enhancing Togo's appeal to businesses and fostering the growth of a robust local digital ecosystem.

According to Byll the partnership will help expand access to quality digital services and the objective is to enable both large companies and smaller organizations to access services suited to their needs and budgets.

"Our partnership with ST Digital is about ensuring that an individual or a company that cannot afford to rent half a rack or a full rack for data hosting can still access a suitable service through an affordable solution," Byll explained.

Adebiyi said the agreement goes beyond a commercial partnership as the cooperation could help position Togo as a regional model for digital transformation and data management.

"Digital sovereignty is not decreed, it is built. By partnering with the SIN, ST Digital is committed to supporting Togo in the implementation of its digital transformation strategy, a reference model in West Africa," she explained.

## African data center expansions

The partnership between Togo's SIN and ST Digital comes amid a broader wave of data center expansion and development across Africa, especially in the first half of 2026.

Just last month, the Africa Congo Internet Exchange added [a second data center in the Democratic Republic of Congo](https://www.connectingafrica.com/data-centers/acix-adds-second-data-center-in-drc) making it the country's first Internet exchange distributor.

In April 2026, Trend Micro's enterprise cybersecurity business, TrendAI, announced a substantial enhancement and [expansion of its data center services portfolio in Africa](https://www.connectingafrica.com/data-centers/trendai-expands-data-center-capabilities-in-south-africa).

As part of efforts to strengthen the country's digital sovereignty, in February 2026, [Mali opened a new Tier III data center](https://www.connectingafrica.com/data-centers/mali-inaugurates-a-tier-iii-data-center).

Burkina Faso's government, in January 2026, inaugurated [two new mini data centers](https://www.connectingafrica.com/data-centers/burkina-faso-launches-two-mini-data-centers).

Those facilities are said to offer a combined storage capacity of around 3,000 terabytes, a tenfold increase from existing systems.
