MTN, the largest telecommunications company in Africa, has plans to enter the global race for artificial intelligence (AI) data centres.

The mobile network operator announced these plans alongside the release of its 2025 year-end financial [results](https://www.mtn.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/MTN-Group-FY-25-Complete-results_Booklet.pdf).

“MTN Digital Infrastructure continued to advance its data centre strategy, laying the groundwork for an expanded AI-enabled rollout plan to support accelerating AI, cloud and enterprise demand,” the group said.

South Africa and Nigeria have reportedly been identified as the priority markets for this new development following extensive market assessments by MTN.

The group’s new strategy focuses on three key areas to enable medium-term growth and create shared value: Connectivity, Fintech and Digital Infrastructure.

Part of the focus on Digital Infrastructure involves the expansion of MTN’s AI-enabled data centre network to meet growing digital needs across the African continent.

“Following a rigorous evaluation process, we have shortlisted key strategic partners with whom negotiations are progressing on co-investment structures and operating models,” the group explained.

“The broader industry shift toward large-scale AI compute infrastructure reinforces the substantial opportunity MTN Digital Infrastructure is positioning for in Africa.”

As part of this initiative, MTN launched the first phase of its Sifiso Dabengwa Data Centre in Lagos, Nigeria, in July 2025. It is the largest data centre in West Africa.

The centre currently offers 4.5MW of capacity, with a planned second phase potentially increasing this to anywhere between 14MW and 20MW.

MTN Group CEO Ralph Mupita expressed confidence in the potential growth and value that MTN will achieve through its new strategy.

“We are hugely excited about Africa’s potential and are well-positioned to leverage our scale, footprint and brand leadership to capture the significant structural growth opportunities identified,” Mupita said.

“We are committed to accelerate our impact and empower the people, businesses and nation states we serve.”

## The future of data storage in South Africa

Ralph Mupita, MTN Group CEO

In an interview on [702](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DmkUYNsNEK0), Durban University of Technology lecturer Siphumelele Zondi compared MTN’s data-centre expansion plan to those of companies around the world.

“They’re staying on the ground for now,” Zondi said. “MTN is just thinking of South Africa and Nigeria at the moment.”

This was in the context of Nvidia and SpaceX, which have both announced plans to launch solar-powered satellite-based data centres into Earth’s orbit.

Zondi also discussed the impact that these AI data centres could have on South Africa’s struggling electricity and water supply.

AI-powered data centres require not only large amounts of electricity to power them, but also large amounts of water for cooling.

“There have been various groupings that are raising worry about whether this is going to be a priority,” Zondi said.

“To prioritise a data centre and even build backup systems for it using power and other resources, including water, that a lot of citizens in South Africa have been struggling with.”

A proposed data centre [to be constructed in Amanzimtoti in KwaZulu-Natal](https://mybroadband.co.za/news/cloud-hosting/631231-korean-company-wants-to-build-a-billion-rand-data-centre-in-popular-south-african-holiday-town.html), for example, has raised concerns over unstable energy and water supply in the area.

DA Executive Committee member André Beetge asked for clarity from the eThekwini municipality on issues surrounding the centre’s construction and operation.

“We are already battling unstable electricity supply and significant water losses exceeding 50%,” Beetge said. “You have just reprioritised funding away from the Kingsburgh station upgrade.”

“How can we responsibly commit to a project of this magnitude without first stabilising core infrastructure?”

The eThekwini municipality responded to these concerns, saying no final plans had been approved for the energy requirement and supply of the centre.

They also said that should the development be approved, it would be subject to the proper legislative and regulatory requirements regarding environmental authorisation.
