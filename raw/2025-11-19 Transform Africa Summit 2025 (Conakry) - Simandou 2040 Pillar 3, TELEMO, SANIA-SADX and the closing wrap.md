Day-two and closing highlights of the Transform Africa Summit 2025 in Conakry (theme "AI for Africa"). On day two (17 November), Guinea launched Pillar 3 of the Simandou 2040 Program (digital as the backbone of industrialization — infrastructure, transport and technology to raise the digital sector to 20% of GDP), and rolled out TELEMO, a national e-procurement platform born of a Guinea-Rwanda South-South partnership, officially launched by Djiba Diakité on behalf of the Head of State. The Simandou Academy was announced to build local talent.

Smart Africa unveiled SANIA (a platform to connect Africa's innovation ecosystems) and signed several MoUs for the Single Digital Market: with YouthConnekt Africa (youth entrepreneurship); with Visa (digitalize government services, expand digital payments, financial inclusion, e-commerce); a pilot of the Smart Africa Data Exchange (SADX) connecting Benin, Ghana and Rwanda; the Smart Africa Digital Academy (SADA) with The Gambia; a partnership with AFTLD (digital sovereignty/cybersecurity for country domains); and with Women Political Leaders.

Closing figures (wrap, 19 November): the 7th edition, under the patronage of Presidents Doumbouya and Kagame, gathered 7,000+ participants from 79 nationalities (1,552 government representatives, 45 ministers, 584 CEOs, 590 startups); 14 MoUs signed. Minister Rose Pola Pricemou noted Guinea quadrupled its backbone (50→200 Gbps), began landing a second submarine cable, is interconnected with Mali, Côte d'Ivoire and Sierra Leone (soon Senegal, The Gambia, Guinea-Bissau), created a Tier III national data centre, is developing technopoles/smart cities and a network of digital hubs (6 operational, 20 by 2026), and is strengthening its regulatory framework with a new data-protection law and the agencies ANSSI and ANDE (Agence Nationale de Digitalisation de l'État). A first women's hackathon (ANSUTEN) trained 130+ young women in Conakry, Labé and Mamou.
