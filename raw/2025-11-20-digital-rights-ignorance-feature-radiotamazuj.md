# Feature: Digital rights ignorance puts South Sudanese at risk

*Radio Tamazuj, 20 November 2025*

Several South Sudanese citizens and legal experts have expressed growing concern over the limited legal protections regulating personal information online, noting that despite constitutional guarantees, the country lacks a comprehensive data protection law. South Sudan continues to rely on constitutional provisions and international human rights frameworks to safeguard online privacy; the Transitional Constitution (2011) affirms the right to privacy (Article 22), but absent dedicated legislation, enforcement remains uneven.

Emmanuel Bida, a digital expert and program manager at the Excellence Foundation, said they are advocating for data protection laws to protect the personal data of online users. "South Sudan does not have data protection laws in place at the moment," he says, adding that privacy is "somehow protected" via the constitution.

In October, digital security experts finalized drafting the country's first Data Protection Policy Bill. When passed, the bill will establish a Data Protection Commission mandated to safeguard the personal data of South Sudanese online users. The process is facilitated by Koneta Initiative and Paradigm Initiative, in collaboration with the National Communication Authority and civil society organizations. The Cybercrimes Bill under discussion in parliament aims to align with the Constitution, the Penal Code Act 2008, and the Malabo Convention.
