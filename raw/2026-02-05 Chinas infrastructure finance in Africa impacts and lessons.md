# China's infrastructure finance in Africa: impacts and lessons

> ## Excerpt
> An ODI working paper (+ country-perspectives appendix) on Chinese infrastructure finance in Africa —
> hydropower, rail, renewables and, most relevant here, **ICT/digital** (Ethiopia's telecom overhaul,
> Kenya's NOFBI fibre and Konza data centre), and the debt/sovereignty aftermath.

## Key facts (digital/ICT dimension)

- **Ethiopia:** 2006 ZTE "Millennium Plan" $1.5bn loan + a 2006 FOCAC ZTE–ETC sole-supplier framework
  (~$1.9bn); 2013 Huawei/ZTE 4G/3G "Telecom Transformation"; ZTE contract terminated 2014 (Ericsson).
  Internet users <1% (2001) → ~20% (2022).
- **Kenya:** NOFBI fibre backbone (Phase 1 $37m Eximbank 2007, ZTE/Huawei; Phase 2 concessional, still
  incomplete as of 2025); **[[konza-technopolis]]** data centre — 2019 $665m agreement, Eximbank $167m,
  Huawei EPC; Phase 1 completed 2020, **stalled since 2021**. Auditor-General flagged undisclosed ZTE
  contracts and idle NOFBI equipment (2022).
- Cross-cutting: Chinese lenders (**[[china-eximbank]]**, CDB, ICBC) + SOE contractors delivered fast
  build-out but projects foundered on weak feasibility, single-source procurement, corruption
  allegations and debt-servicing stress — feeding sovereign-debt restructuring under the G20 Common
  Framework (Ethiopia, Zambia). Sovereignty theme: single-supplier lock-in and ownership of critical
  digital/data infrastructure ("once raw minerals, now raw data").
