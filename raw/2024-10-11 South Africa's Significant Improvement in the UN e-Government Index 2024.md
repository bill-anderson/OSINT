*PRIMARY — the SA government's own statement on a third-party index; celebratory register, attribute. Complementary to the held Ecofin report of the same EGDI edition rather than duplicative: it uniquely carries the delivery numbers (255 government services identified for digitisation, 134 e-Services live on the SITA-hosted national portal), the MTSF 2019-2024 and 2030 whole-of-government targets, the LOSI city-portal detail, and the Malatsi quote. SA rose from rank 65 (2022) to 40 (2024) and entered the 'very high EGDI' group for the first time.*

The Department of Communications and Digital Technologies notes and welcomes the news of South Africa's significant improvement in the United Nations e-Government Index of 2024.

South Africa has improved its ranking from 65 in 2022 to 40 in 2024, out of 193 countries in the UN eGov Development Index. This places the country among well-established economies. South Africa is now leading in Africa, followed by Mauritius (ranked 76) and Tunisia (ranked 87). South Africa and Mauritius are the leaders in e-government development in Africa. For the first time, these countries are part of the very high EGDI group.

The United Nations E-Government Survey is a biennial project and publication created by the UN Department of Economic and Social Affairs (UN DESA) starting in 2001, evaluating the e-government development status of all 193 UN Member States. South Africa participates through inputs by national stakeholders, coordinated by the DCDT.

The EGDI is a composite calculated from a weighted average of three normalized indices: one-third from the Telecommunications Infrastructure Index (TII) based on ITU data, one-third from the Human Capital Index (HCI) based mainly on UNESCO data, and one-third from the Online Service Index (OSI) based on an independent online assessment conducted by UN DESA.

The improvement is attributed to the multi-stakeholder approach in implementing the National e-Government Strategy. The 2019–2024 Medium-Term Strategic Framework (MTSF) made provision for the DCDT to ensure implementation of the National e-Government Strategy and Roadmap to realize digitized government services for whole of government by 2030.

The DCDT, assisted by the State Information Technology Agency (SITA) as execution arm, jointly developed the National e-Government Portal. A total of 255 government services have been identified for digitization in consultation with the Department of Public Service and Administration (DPSA) and Government Communication and Information System (GCIS). The National e-Government Portal currently allows access to 134 e-Services ranging from agro-processing, exam queries, e-recruitment and various types of licensing. It is hosted by SITA and can be accessed at https://www.eservices.gov.za

Other contributors to the ranking include the drafting of the Data and Cloud Strategy and the National AI Plan. Since 2018, the Survey has also assessed selected city portals, leading to the Local Online Service Index (LOSI); South African cities such as Tshwane, City of Joburg and City of Cape Town have developed portals to offer e-services.

Minister Solly Malatsi highlighted that, "as the digital economy gains rapid momentum around the world, South Africa needs to accelerate her National e-Government Programme. The intention is to see less queues for ID's and passport documents and make the application process more seamless for our people... Licenses, permits and many more government services should be paid online."
