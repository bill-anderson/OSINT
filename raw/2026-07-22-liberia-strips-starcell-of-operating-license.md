# Liberia strips Starcell of operating license

The Liberia Telecommunications Authority (LTA) has revoked the Universal Telecommunications License and Spectrum Authorization of Starcell International Liberia with immediate effect.

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa — July 22, 2026

The Liberia Telecommunications Authority (LTA) has revoked the Universal Telecommunications License and Spectrum Authorization of Starcell International Liberia with immediate effect, after the telco failed to comply with key licensing and regulatory requirements.

LTA Chairperson Clarence Massaquoi said during a media briefing that the decision followed a comprehensive regulatory review conducted under the Telecommunications Act of 2007.

"Starcell failed to commence telecommunications operations in Liberia, did not meet its financial obligations, failed to make required contributions to the Universal Access Fund, violated LTA regulations, and did not respond to repeated regulatory directives," Massaquoi said.

Massaquoi said Starcell's inability to launch operations denied Liberians access to anticipated telecommunications services while tying up valuable national spectrum resources that could have been put to productive use.

He said the revocation comes after a 90-day suspension issued by the LTA from February 9 to May 10, 2026, during which Starcell was given an opportunity to address the identified violations.

Massaquoi said Starcell failed to respond or take corrective action before the suspension expired.

"With the revocation now in effect, Starcell is no longer authorized to operate as a licensed telecommunications service provider in Liberia," he said.

The LTA said all spectrum, frequencies, numbering resources and other regulatory privileges previously assigned to Starcell have reverted to the authority for reassignment.

The Liberian regulator reaffirmed its commitment to enforcing the Telecommunications Act of 2007 and ensuring that all licensed operators comply with regulatory requirements to promote a competitive, transparent and consumer-focused telecommunications sector in Liberia.

The LTA has worked tirelessly to ensure that the country's telcos are held accountable for wrongdoing.

Just last month, the authority fined Orange Liberia 4 million Liberian dollars (US$21,900 at the time) after finding the operator liable for the unauthorized issuance of a customer's SIM card to a third party, resulting in a privacy and communications breach.

## Liberia's mobile mix

Starcell's license revocation comes as Liberia is looking to expand competition in a mobile market still dominated by MTN and Orange.

Orange Liberia was the largest telco at the end of the second quarter of 2026 with an estimated 3.2 million mobile subscribers, according to market research company Omdia, a sister company of Connecting Africa.

Omdia reports Lonestar Cell (MTN Liberia) had 2.3 million mobile subscribers around the same time, while Libtelco lagged with just 14,000 subscribers.

Starcell International Liberia never initiated operations in the country, hence the revocation of its license.
