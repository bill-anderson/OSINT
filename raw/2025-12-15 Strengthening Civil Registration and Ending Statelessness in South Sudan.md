# Strengthening Civil Registration and Ending Statelessness in South Sudan

> ## Excerpt
> As of late 2025, only ~10% of South Sudan's ~12m population hold Nationality Certificates and the country is yet to issue National Identity cards and birth certificates at scale; a CRVS Costed Plan and Civil Registry Rules are adopted, with a birth-registration pilot at Juba Teaching Hospital.

---

Online Marketplace Project Snapshot. Lead Organization: Hold the Child. Country: South Sudan. Total Funding Need: USD 48,000 (total project cost USD 85,000). Project timeframe: less than 6 months. Supported Global Refugee Forum pledges: GRF-00981–00984. Contributes to SDG 10.7 and SDG 16.2.

**Main problem addressed.** Since independence in 2011, South Sudan's legacy of conflict, displacement, weak services and limited public awareness continues to strip individuals of their rights, exposing them to risks including statelessness. Anecdotal estimates suggest only about 10% of the ~12 million population possess Nationality Certificates; the country is yet to issue National Identity cards and Birth Certificates at scale. Vulnerable groups — returnees, border populations, and minorities — face significant barriers in securing legal identity (Nationality Certificates and Passports). Ongoing crises in Sudan have intensified these risks, with UNHCR reporting 590,956 refugees and 4,685 asylum seekers in South Sudan.

South Sudan has made key policy commitments: signatory to the 2017 ICGLR Declaration on the Eradication of Statelessness, accession to the 1954 and 1961 Statelessness Conventions, recently approved Civil Registry Rules and Procedures, and adoption of a Costed Plan for the Civil Registration and Vital Statistics (CRVS) System. Implementation is constrained by limited infrastructure and institutional weaknesses. Over the past five years, Hold the Child's work with the Directorate of Civil Registry, UNHCR and other stakeholders has piloted National ID processing and Birth Registration within existing constraints.

**Main change sought.** The project combines policy advocacy and capacity strengthening to pilot birth registration and national ID processing, creating a real-life user-case model to guide future scale-up. Key interventions: (A) Coalition building and advocacy — strengthen the ad-hoc technical working group into a structured multi-stakeholder platform to advance dialogue on financing, institutional strengthening, and integration within the broader National Identity for Development agenda; fundraising for an in-country donor conference on civil registration; advocating ratification and domestication of the Protocol to the African Charter relating to the Right to a Nationality and the Eradication of Statelessness in Africa (adopted 24 February 2024). (B) Policy and legal harmonization — review and update the 2011 Nationality Act and 2018 Civil Registry Act to align with international statelessness conventions. (C) Civil documentation services — provide light equipment and technical support to roll out the adopted birth-registration business process in Juba (Juba Teaching Hospital), with on-demand staff capacity building.

**Key partners.** Implemented by Hold the Child in collaboration with the Government of South Sudan Ministry of Interior, Department of Civil Registry, Nationality, Passport and Immigration (DCRNPI). Technical working group members include UNHCR, UNICEF, Norwegian Refugee Council (NRC), IOM, UNDP, IRC, UNECA, and Vital Strategies (resourcing the birth-registration pilot).
