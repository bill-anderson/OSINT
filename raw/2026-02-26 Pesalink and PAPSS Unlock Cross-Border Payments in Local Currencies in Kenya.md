Nairobi, 26 February 2026 — **[[pesalink]]**, Kenya's de facto instant payment network, has partnered with **[[papss]]** to ease cross-border payments and speed regional financial integration. The partnership enables instant 24/7 cross-border payments from PAPSS participants into banks and mobile-money operators within the Pesalink network in Kenya, **settled in local currencies**, reducing reliance on correspondent banking and foreign reserve currencies.

Pesalink becomes a **Technical Connectivity Provider**, linking 80+ Kenyan bank, fintech, SACCO and telco participants to 160+ commercial banks and fintechs on PAPSS. PAPSS CEO Mike Ogbalu III said Pesalink is the **first switch piloted for transaction termination in Kenya**; Pesalink CEO Gituku Kirika said Kenyan banks will offer faster, cheaper cross-border payments. PAPSS is an **[[afreximbank]]** initiative with the African Union and AfCFTA Secretariat; sending money across African borders costs on average 7-8% (2023 World Bank). Pesalink is operated by Integrated Payment Services Limited (IPSL), owned by the Kenya Bankers Association.
