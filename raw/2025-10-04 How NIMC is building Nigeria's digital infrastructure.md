[Opinion/analysis column.] The National Identity Management Commission (NIMC) is playing a crucial role in building Nigeria's digital public infrastructure (DPI), partnering with government agencies to enhance identity management, payment systems and data exchange. The DPI framework utilises data from NIMC and the Nigerian Interbank Settlement System (NIBSS) to facilitate secure access to public services.

President Bola Tinubu has reaffirmed his commitment to the initiative, tying it to his eight-point agenda (food security, poverty alleviation, economic growth, job creation, inclusivity, rule of law, anti-corruption). Speaking at NIMC's 6th National Day of Identity Conference in Abuja (represented by the SGF, Senator George Akume), he stressed data identity protection and harmonisation of the national database.

The Commission has implemented enhanced security measures for the National Identity Database. Under DG Abisoye Coker-Odusote (in office since August 2023), all Licensed Verification Agents, Front-End Partners (FEPs) and Diaspora FEPs undergo rigorous security vetting; violators face sanctions. Law enforcement has been authorised to crack down on fraudulent online platforms compelling Nigerians to submit personal information for NIN modification or enrolment.

NIMC and the National Population Commission (NPC) are collaborating to accelerate and digitalise birth registration, described as a fundamental right and key to accessing education, healthcare and social benefits, especially for children under 16.

NIMC has also cleared all outstanding payments owed to Front-End Partners (FEPs) for enrolments that hit the database and generated NINs. The FEPs were contracted in 2021 by the previous administration to scale up NIN enrolment but were only paid for one year; the 2021-2024 FEP enrolment was a stop-gap measure pending the World Bank/ID4D project. After a revalidation exercise, NIMC — with support from ID4D and the World Bank — paid the outstanding money (which was not part of the World Bank-funded project). Meanwhile the National Assembly has commenced a legislative exercise to repeal the 2007 NIMC Establishment Act and enact a new bill strengthening the Commission's regulatory role.
