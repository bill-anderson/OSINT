# Benin Expands Higher Education Access Through E-Learning Strategy

By : Samira Njoya
Date : lundi, 06 juillet 2026 07:02

- Benin has approved contracts with service providers to deploy a national e-learning system across public universities starting in the 2026–2027 academic year.
- The project includes a national digital platform, connected lecture halls, studios, audiovisual equipment and quality assurance systems for online education.
- The rollout will initially cover four public universities as part of a broader strategy to expand access and modernize teaching methods.

Benin has approved the contracting of multiple service providers to implement its national e-learning project, marking a major step toward the digital transformation of higher education. The decision, taken at a Council of Ministers meeting on July 1, authorizes the rollout of digital infrastructure required to launch distance learning in public universities starting in the 2026–2027 academic year. The project establishes a comprehensive digital learning ecosystem. It includes the development of the national platform etudiant.bj, which will serve as the central portal for course delivery and educational content distribution.

Authorities will also deploy connected lecture halls, build tele-teaching studios, install audiovisual and projection equipment, and implement a quality assurance system to ensure standards in online instruction.

The initial phase will cover Benin's four public universities. These include the University of Abomey-Calavi, the University of Parakou, the National University of Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (UNSTIM), and the National University of Agriculture (UNA). Officials said the program aims to modernize teaching practices, expand learning resources and improve access to higher education. They added that digital tools will allow students to follow courses regardless of their geographic location while encouraging resource sharing between institutions.

Benin's initiative aligns with a broader trend of digital transformation in African education systems. The United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization (UNESCO) has said that digital technologies can expand access to higher education, improve training quality and strengthen system resilience during crises.

However, UNESCO also stressed that success depends on infrastructure availability, connectivity, equipment and teacher training. With this e-learning rollout, Benin aims to progressively extend distance learning across its entire education system. Authorities said the long-term objective involves building a hybrid model that combines in-person and digital teaching to improve nationwide access to quality education.
