# South Sudan defends Crawford Capital deal as digital reforms boost revenue

*By Farhiya Hussein, The Eastleigh Voice, Thursday, 14 May 2026*

South Sudan authorities say their partnership with Crawford Capital has strengthened digital tax systems, boosting revenue collection, improving transparency, and modernising public administration under ongoing government reforms.

The South Sudan government has defended its partnership with Crawford Capital Ltd, saying the firm's role in ongoing digital reforms has significantly improved revenue collection and strengthened transparency in public administration.

In separate statements, the South Sudan Revenue Authority and the Ministry of Information, Communication Technology and Postal Services said the collaboration is part of a broader government strategy to modernise state systems through digital transformation.

The revenue authority said the introduction of digital revenue collection systems has addressed long-standing weaknesses associated with manual processes, including weak oversight, delayed reconciliations, and exposure to revenue leakages.

"The initiative was undertaken through established institutional procedures and approved government reform frameworks, including endorsement by the Council of Ministers," the authority said in a statement released by the Office of the Commissioner General in Juba.

According to the SSRA, the reforms have improved transaction monitoring, accountability, auditability, and reconciliation across the tax collection process, while also enabling real-time reporting and enforcement.

The authority disclosed that the digital systems are currently generating nearly 130 billion South Sudanese pounds monthly, with cumulative collections reaching close to one trillion pounds over the past eight months. It added that the highest monthly collections were recorded in April 2026.

"SSRA recognises Crawford Capital for its technical contribution and professional support in implementing systems that continue to strengthen revenue administration and institutional governance," the statement read.

The authority further maintained that the engagement was conducted "lawfully, transparently, and within the framework of government-approved reform initiatives aimed at modernising public revenue administration and strengthening state institutions".

Meanwhile, the Ministry of ICT and Postal Services reaffirmed its collaboration with the firm, describing digital transformation as a key governance reform aimed at improving service delivery and efficiency across government agencies.

The ministry said Crawford Capital is providing technical and research support to improve digital workflows, strengthen administrative coordination, and support the development of citizen-centred e-government services.

"This engagement forms part of the Government's broader digital transformation agenda aimed at improving efficiency, transparency, accountability, and accessibility in public service delivery," the ministry stated.

The ministry added that the partnership aligns with resolutions approved by the Council of Ministers and wider reform priorities under the Revitalised Transitional Government of National Unity.

ICT Minister Ateny Wek Aten said the government remains committed to reforms that support "a modern, efficient, and accessible government" through digital innovation.

The statements come as South Sudan intensifies efforts to modernise public institutions and improve financial governance amid growing calls for greater accountability and efficiency in public service delivery.
