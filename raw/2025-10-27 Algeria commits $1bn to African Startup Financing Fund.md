# Algeria commits $1bn to an African startup financing fund

Algeria committed **USD 1 billion** to finance African startups — announced **21 October 2025** by **Kamel Rezig**, Minister of Foreign Trade and Export Promotion, at the African Ministerial Meeting on the sidelines of UNCTAD16 (Geneva). The money flows through the **African Startup and Young Innovators Financing Fund** ([[african-startup-fund-algeria]]), launched by President **Tebboune** in September 2025, targeting technology, education, healthcare and humanitarian projects with strong social/economic impact.

Domestic backdrop: by mid-2025 Algeria reported **1,600 microenterprises, 130 startups, 1,175 innovative projects and 2,800 registered patents**, aiming for **20,000 startups by 2029** (via Algeria Venture and the National Startup Fund). It pairs this with continental infrastructure — the Trans-Saharan Highway, gas pipeline, and a high-speed fibre link between North and Sub-Saharan Africa. (African startups raised ~$3.63bn in 2022, ~$1.98bn in 2023.)
