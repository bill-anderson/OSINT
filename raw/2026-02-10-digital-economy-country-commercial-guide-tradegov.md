# Mauritania — Digital Economy (US Country Commercial Guide)

*Off-assigned-host capture (US ITA country commercial guide) — admissible dated reference/backgrounder (same class as the held Ghana Digital Economy guide). Cite for framing; do not absorb its figures as current state. Establishes two net-new reference objects: the data-protection authority (APD) and the National AI Strategy 2025–2029.*

Mauritania is executing a **state-led digital push** anchored in the National Digital Transformation Agenda (2022–2025): +2,500 miles of fibre backbone, stronger international links, broader 4G, expanded e-government; further backbone build-out signalled beyond 2025. The government inaugurated a **Tier III national data centre in Nouakchott in May 2025 (EIB-backed)**. Policy tracks include an **e-commerce strategy** (multi-stakeholder consultations 2024–2025 with UNCTAD/GIZ) and a **National AI Strategy (2025–2029)**.

Access baseline: early 2025 — **1.96 million internet users**, **6.14 million mobile connections** (1.17/capita, multi-SIM), mostly mobile broadband. Constraints: affordability, last-mile delivery, payments acceptance outside major cities, digital-literacy gaps, patchy power.

Legal/regulatory: **"The legislation that governs personal data protection exists, with the governmental data-protection authority (Autorité de Protection des Données, APD) pursuing a 2023–2026 strategic plan"** tied to bringing the law into effective operation. Cybersecurity and data strategies complement the APD plan; MTNIMA has published the AI strategy (2025–2029). **UNCTAD's eTrade Readiness Assessment (2024)** flags barriers across seven pillars: limited payments interoperability/acceptance, high logistics costs and weak addressing, patchy legal frameworks (consumer protection, data, e-signatures), and constrained SME finance for digitalisation.

Near-term priorities: cloud/data-centre services; API-driven payments and digital ID/KYC; cybersecurity aligned to national-strategy targets; AI/analytics for public services, energy, fisheries monitoring and transport. Milestones: eTrade Readiness launch (Apr 2024), e-commerce-strategy consultations (2024–2025), May 2025 data-centre inauguration.
