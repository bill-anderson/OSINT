On taking office as Minister of Urbanism, Housing and Territorial Planning, Mohamed Lamine Sy Savané (successor to Mory Condé) set out priorities including the full digitalization of the land cadastre to secure rights, fight fraud and guarantee transparency in the management of land titles.

He framed the ministry as central to the national transition — "the heart of land sovereignty, spatial justice, the fight against urban anarchy and the reduction of territorial inequalities" — and pledged to make urbanism a lever of development, social cohesion and national stability, in line with President Mamadi Doumbouya's vision. Alongside cadastre digitalization, his priorities include balanced territorial planning and modern, anticipatory urban planning. He called on staff to adopt a culture of performance and results, judging every reform by its real impact on citizens.
