The Nigerian government announced that the National Identification Number (NIN) will become mandatory to access social services and government assistance.

Secretary to the Government of the Federation (SGF) Senator George Akume made the announcement during the inauguration of an Inter-Ministerial retreat on the Nigeria Digital Identification for Development (ID4D) Project in Lagos. Dr Tanko Sununu, Minister of State for Humanitarian Affairs and Poverty Reduction, confirmed his ministry will work with NIMC to implement the NIN requirement for social services aimed at vulnerable populations.

NIMC Director-General Dr Abisoye Coker-Odusote stressed the need for inter-agency cooperation. As of October 2024, NIMC had issued NINs to 115 million Nigerians and legal residents, below the World Bank's expanded target of 180 million by 2026. The country is also preparing to launch a new biometric ID card combining passport, driver's licence and health insurance information. Citizens can retrieve their NIN by dialing *346#. The requirement's effective date was not yet announced. The move is part of broader digitalization including a biometric farmers' registry and plans for Nigeria's first biometric census in 2025.
