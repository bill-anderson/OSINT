Velmie, a banking technology provider, announced its collaboration with Metro Cable — Sierra Leone's provider of high-speed gigabit internet access — to launch Vult, a modern digital wallet. Vult offers users a way to make online transactions, deposits, withdrawals, pay bills and send and receive money in multiple currencies within a single platform.

Metro Cable is known for delivering the West African country's first fiber optic broadband internet service. The Vult app has both a wallet and a card function.

The Vult Wallet enables users to make online transactions securely, pay bills, and shop at any visa-approved location, with end-to-end encryption. Key features include multi-currency capability, multiple top-up options (link multiple bank accounts, add money through mobile money or via any visa-issued card), and enhanced security.

Alongside the wallet, Velmie and Metro Cable introduced the Vult Card, a globally accepted payment card for online and in-store purchases. Additionally, Vult Pay provides integration with existing business applications for payments and reporting, and the Vult Pay Point is a point-of-sale terminal service facilitating secure payment collections and cash-in/cash-out transactions.

"We are thrilled to partner with Metro Cable to launch Vult... By combining our expertise in Fintech software with Metro Cable's commitment to connectivity, we aim to bridge the gap in financial inclusion." — Slava Ivashkin, CEO, Velmie

"This is a really exciting initiative – the first of its kind in Sierra Leone. We believe that Vult will have massive ripple effects in the country – bolstering economic growth and financial inclusion." — Pierre Johnson, COO, Metro Cable

(Velmie's own case-study page later reported Vult onboarded 475,000+ users in its first year.)
