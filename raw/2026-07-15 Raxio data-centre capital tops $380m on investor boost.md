**[[raxio-group]]** (pan-African independent data-centre operator) said on **13 July 2026** that fresh equity
from **Roha** and **Meridiam** lifted its **committed capital from US$350m to US$380m** — following the
**US$100m** the World Bank's **[[ifc]]** put in last year, alongside debt from **Proparco** and the **Emerging
Africa and Asia Infrastructure Fund (EAAIF)**.

The trigger is demand: Raxio says it **contracted six times as much power in H1 2026 as in H1 2025**, a surge
it attributes to **cloud migration and AI workloads**. It operates Tier III, carrier-neutral facilities in
**Uganda, Ethiopia, Mozambique, DR Congo, Côte d'Ivoire and Angola**, with a **Tanzania** site under
development — one of the few operators building beyond the continent's established hubs into second-tier
markets. CEO **Robert Skjodt** cited accelerating digital adoption, cloud migration and AI. The open question,
per Meridiam's Mete Saracoglu and the report: whether **unreliable power** can keep pace with the capacity now
being contracted.
