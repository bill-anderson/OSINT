Kenya and Angola have signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to collaborate on technology advancement, satellite networks, ICT training, digital economy, rural connectivity, cybersecurity and digital literacy.

The MoU was signed on the sidelines of the Luanda Financing Summit held in Angola late last week.

According to Kenya's Principal Secretary for the State Department for ICT and Digital Economy, John Tanui, the agreement was made between Kenya's Cabinet Secretary for National Treasury and Planning, John Mbadi, Angola's Minister of Telecommunications, Information Technologies and Social Communication, Mário Augusto da Silva Oliveira, and Kenya's Ambassador to Angola, Joyce Khasimwa M'maitsi, to expedite Kenya-Angola ICT cooperation.

Tanui represented Kenya in engagements that included ministerial dialogues, strategic bilateral talks, cultural immersion and interactions with the Kenyan diaspora in Angola.

"The summit reaffirmed Africa's commitment to accelerate digital transformation, unlock infrastructure financing, and strengthen collaboration for shared prosperity," he said on his LinkedIn post.

Tanui added that he took part in the Investors-Ministerial Roundtable on Financing Africa's Digital Transformation, and shared Kenya's experience in expanding broadband, digital public services and innovative blended financing.

He said in meetings with ministers, the African Union Development Agency - New Partnership for Africa's Development (AUDA-NEPAD), African Development Bank (AfDB), World Bank, and private sector leaders, one message stood out, which was that Africa's digital transformation will be achieved through collaboration, investment and scalable infrastructure.

"Kenya is ready to work with partners in creating digital corridors, interoperable systems and youth-driven innovation to power the continent's digital future," he said.

"This summit strengthened Kenya-Angola ties under the leadership of Ambassador Joyce Khasimwa M'maitsi and reaffirmed Angola's role as a key partner in advancing Africa's digital and economic transformation," he concluded.

## African countries connecting underserved communities

The MoU between Angola and Kenya comes at a time when many other countries and companies are also partnering to advance digital transformation efforts, especially in the past couple of months.

Just last month, Kenya signed an agreement with Romania to establish a collaborative framework for information sharing, joint incident response, capacity building and the development of advanced cybersecurity solutions.

Also in October, Ethiopian state-owned Ethio Telecom partnered with Chinese Android smartphone manufacturer Tinno to boost smartphone adoption and accelerate access to devices across the country.

A week or two earlier, Namibia partnered with Angola to use the Angosat-2 satellite to improve connectivity in the country's remote areas where terrestrial infrastructure remains limited.

Telecoms operator Airtel Congo and Wing Wah, a private Congolese oil company and subsidiary of the Chinese group Southernpec, partnered to deploy a private telecom network at the Banga Kayo oil field in Kouilou in Congo-Brazzaville in August 2025.

Airtel Africa also signed a strategic infrastructure sharing agreement with Vodacom Group in Mozambique, Tanzania and the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC).
