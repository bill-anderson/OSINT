The Government of Mauritius has unveiled "A Blueprint for Mauritius - A Bridge to the Future" (2025-2029), a plan to modernise the national digital ecosystem and position Mauritius as a secure, globally competitive digital hub. On 21 August 2025, Dr Avinash Ramtohul, Minister of Information Technology, Communication and Innovation, announced (at the launch of Emtel's cloud services) that new digital regulations would follow the Blueprint.

Key measures highlighted: an e-commerce framework to register and license online sellers; amending the Electronic Transactions Act for blockchain-enabled Electronic Transferable Records; updating data protection law to align with the EU GDPR (data protection officers, e-privacy); revising the Cybersecurity and Cybercrime Act (IT security audits, a National Cyber Resiliency Agency, identity management services); changing the ICT Act to balance free expression with regulating new technologies such as NGSO satellite broadband; social media moderation guidelines; a National AI Strategy with an AI Unit and ethical AI policies; a unified government portal and mobile ID; and SME programmes and regulatory sandboxes.

(Source is the EDB, a government investment-promotion body, reporting the Minister's official policy announcement.)
