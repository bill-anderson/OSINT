# Senegal partners Alibaba to build sovereign cloud and train tech workforce ahead of Dakar 2026

Africa Business Insight - 29 April 2026

Senegal will train thousands of young people and develop a national cloud computing system in partnership with China's Alibaba Group as part of preparations for the 2026 Youth Olympic Games. According to Senegal's Digital Ministry, the programme will train around 100 engineers in advanced cloud technologies and expose several thousand more young people to digital skills. Alibaba will help design and deploy a "sovereign cloud" system intended to host sensitive national data within Senegalese territory.

Digital Minister Alioune Sall said the infrastructure is in the testing phase, with equipment installed and verified by Senegalese and Alibaba engineers, and tested for redundancy and reliability. The cloud will support the Dakar 2026 Youth Olympic Games (31 October - 13 November 2026), managing accreditation, security coordination, logistics and visitor services. The project forms part of the New Deal Technologique, whose central goal is to reduce dependence on foreign-managed infrastructure by building domestic capacity in cloud computing, cybersecurity and data management. The government plans to train up to 100,000 graduates in digital fields.
