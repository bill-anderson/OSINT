## Description

In September 2021, Africa Data Centres (ADC), a subsidiary of Cassava Technologies (formerly Liquid Intelligent Technologies), announced a $500 million investment programme to build 10 interconnected, cloud- and carrier-neutral data centres across 10 African countries over two years, doubling its existing footprint. The programme is financed through a combination of debt and equity, including a $300 million DFC direct loan (committed 2020, first disbursement Aug 2022 for South Africa), corporate equity from Cassava Technologies/ADC, and local debt facilities. Target countries include South Africa (Johannesburg JHB1, JHB2, Cape Town CPT1, CPT2), Kenya (Nairobi NBO1, NBO2), Nigeria (Lagos LOS1), Ghana (Accra ACC1), Togo (Lomé), and others. As of 2024, ADC has spent the $500M and operates facilities from South Africa to West Africa.
