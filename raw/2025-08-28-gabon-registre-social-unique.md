Gabon has finalized the technical work on the **Registre Social Unique (RSU / Unique Social Registry)**. A closing workshop, chaired by the Minister of Women, Family and Child Protection, brought together national and international experts on Thursday, 28 August 2025, to lay the foundations of a system designed to optimize the management of social programs and better target the most vulnerable.

The RSU is designed to reform social protection in depth, replacing fragmented mechanisms with an integrated, transparent system. By creating an exhaustive, reliable database, the RSU will rationalize public spending (avoiding duplication and waste) and improve coordination of social programs. It is seen as a key instrument to ensure vulnerable children, women and families — including those in remote areas — benefit from transparent, needs-adapted support.

Success rests on collaboration between the Ministry of Women, Family and Child Protection, the Ministry of Social Affairs and Inclusion, the **National Agency for Digital Infrastructure and Frequencies (ANINF)**, and technical/financial partners, notably the **World Bank**. Expected results: more precise beneficiary targeting and greater transparency in the management of social resources.
