# Airtel launches mobile money transfers in Chad

Bharti Airtel has launched its Airtel Money service in Chad, allowing its customers in the country to carry out financial transactions from their mobile phones.

The mobile commerce product was the outcome of a collaboration between Airtel Chad and African bank Ecobank Transnational Incorporated (ETI).

A valid form of identification is necessary to register for the service, and a personal password is required to complete each transaction. The service permits subscribers to transfer money from their phones safely and securely.

Airtel Chad chief executive officer Salia Gbane announced: "This service allows customers to send money to their relatives, pay essential bills, such as electricity and water, tuition fees, bookings, and even buy groceries without having to carry cash. The phone essentially becomes an electronic wallet."

"This partnership clearly demonstrates the revolutionary role that mobile communications can play in improving the living conditions of the communities that we serve," Gbane added.

According to Airtel Chad, the majority of the population in developing countries rely on expensive and unreliable channels of financial transactions, because formal financial services are only a viable option for a minority. Consequently, the popularity of mobile money services such as Airtel Money is expected to grow considerably across the continent.
