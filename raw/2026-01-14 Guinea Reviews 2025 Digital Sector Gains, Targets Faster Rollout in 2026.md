Guinea's Minister of Posts, Telecommunications and Digital Economy, Rose Pola Pricemou, presented a 2025 sector review. Headline gains: national backbone capacity quadrupled to 200 Gbps (from 50 Gbps); nearly 12,000 km of fibre optic deployed to connect all administrative regions; commissioned its first Tier 3-certified national data centre; operationalized an Internet Exchange Point; and secured sovereign management of the .gn country-code domain.

Public-administration digitalization: 39 public-service applications deployed; several procedures fully digitized including passport issuance via a unified portal; the TELEMO platform fully digitized public procurement; and the FUGAS unified civil-service registry streamlined workforce management, generating budget savings exceeding 233 billion Guinean francs (~$26.9 million).

Education/inclusion: 10,000+ people trained in digital skills; the Univ Connect project interconnected universities (80,000+ students/staff); the GIGA programme connected 585 schools (targeting 2,200 by 2026, ~900,000 beneficiaries); 20 additional regional digital hubs under construction; $4m+ invested in tech entrepreneurship. Telecoms now account for 4.4% of GDP and attracted ~$220 million in investment; mobile-internet costs fell ~25%. For 2026: extend connectivity to 600+ identified 'white zones', expand digital use in education/public services, and strengthen cybersecurity and local data hosting.
