The World Bank is supporting Mozambique to ease access to free national ID cards and bridge the identification gap. More than 140,000 individuals obtained national ID cards in the pilot phase that started in November 2023.

Through the Digital Governance and Economy Project (EDGE) and the ID4D initiative, the World Bank partnered with Mozambique's National Directorate of Civil Identification (DNIC) and other bodies to deliver credentials free of charge, removing cost and accessibility barriers. In a separate project started in 2022, the World Bank (via ID4D) helped issue ID cards and birth certificates to forcibly displaced persons; by early 2023 over 21,000 internally displaced persons had benefited. Mozambique's digital ID system was implemented by Mühlbauer, serving a majority of the country's ~33 million people. [Cluster with World Bank free-IDs feature and aimnews.]
