Guinea launched Trésor Pay, a digital platform to dematerialize the payment of administrative fees and charges owed to the state. It was officially rolled out on 22 December 2025 in Conakry, to improve public-revenue mobilization and reduce administrative friction. Per Economy and Finance Minister Mourana Soumah, Trésor Pay follows a presidential directive to improve citizens' access to public services and strengthen domestic revenue collection — reducing queues, limiting costly travel, curbing informal practices and ensuring payment traceability, while speeding fund availability and reducing fraud.

Operationally, users pay administrative fees and charges collected by the public treasury directly via mobile phones; the platform relies on electronic-money services and runs alongside the traditional circuit, with a dedicated call centre for support. Financial inclusion in Guinea remains limited (~30%), while mobile money expands rapidly. The reform is part of broader efforts to modernize public finances; over time Trésor Pay could improve non-tax revenue collection, shorten processing times, reduce fraud, and open the door to mass payments and other dematerialized public financial instruments.
