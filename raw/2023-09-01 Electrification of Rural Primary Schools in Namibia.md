Case study (fieldwork conducted January–September 2023) of the Grüne Bürgerenergie / Green People's Energy (GBE) off-grid solar electrification of 15 rural primary and combined schools in Namibia, implemented via the Ministry of Mines and Energy, Ministry of Education, and the Namibia Energy Institute. Nearly half of Namibia's population lacks electricity, and the country has over 180 unelectrified schools concentrated near the northern border.

By the time of writing, 13 of 15 containerised solar installations were complete, benefiting 2,740 pupils and 130 teachers. The relevance to digital access is direct: electrification enabled schools to introduce computers, printers, projectors, phone charging, internet and online learning where none was previously possible — a foundational rural digital-divide enabler.

(Borderline scope: primarily an energy/electrification case study, retained for its rural ICT-access dimension.)
