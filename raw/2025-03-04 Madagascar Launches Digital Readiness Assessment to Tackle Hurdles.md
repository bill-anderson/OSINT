Madagascar's Ministry of Digital Development, Posts and Telecommunications, with UNDP, launched a Digital Readiness Assessment (DRA) on 27 February 2025 in Antsirabe. The DRA aims to diagnose the digital sector and identify priority actions to close gaps.

Key obstacles cited: limited infrastructure access, high connectivity costs, and a digital-skills shortage. Per DataReportal, internet penetration was 20.6% in January 2024 (6.31 million users of 30.68 million people), uneven especially in rural areas. Mobile internet cost still represents 15.5% of monthly GNI per capita, far above the ITU's 2% recommendation. The 2023-2028 Digital Strategic Plan highlights a shortage of ~40,000 specialised technicians; the Ministry launched a programme to train 6,000 people in emerging digital professions over three years. The DRA is built on five pillars — connectivity, governance, regulation, businesses, and human capital — and serves as the basis for a strategic roadmap.
