# Liberia Launches Inclusive Instant Payment System to Enable Real-Time Transfers

By Jennifer Onyeagoro — TechAfrica News, December 16, 2025

The Central Bank of Liberia (CBL) officially launched the Inclusive Instant Payment System (IIPS) on December 16, 2025, marking a major milestone in the country's drive to modernize its national payments infrastructure. The new system primarily enables instant mobile money transfers between Liberia's two mobile network operators, Lonestar Cell MTN and Orange Liberia. The launch ceremony was held at the Ministerial Complex in Congo Town, with President Joseph Nyuma Boakai delivering the keynote address reaffirming the government's commitment to digital transformation under the ARREST Agenda.

The IIPS is a national digital payment platform regulated and supervised by the CBL. It enables full interoperability between banks, mobile money operators, and other financial service providers, allowing users to send and receive money in real time between bank accounts and mobile wallets, regardless of service provider. The system is designed to improve convenience, reduce transaction delays, and lower reliance on cash. It addresses long-standing challenges such as fragmented payment systems, slow processing times, and limited interoperability. For government, the platform supports efficient Government-to-Person (G2P) payments such as salaries and social benefits, improving transparency and reducing leakages. The CBL noted the rollout of IIPS services would continue beyond the official launch in close collaboration with participating banks and mobile money operators.
