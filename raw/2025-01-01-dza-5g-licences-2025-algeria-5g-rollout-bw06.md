# Mobilis, Djezzy, Ooredoo — Algeria 5G licences, 2025

Algeria awarded its first three 5G licences, worth DZD 63.9 billion (~US$492m), to operators Mobilis, Djezzy and Ooredoo, launching a national 5G rollout.

## Description

"Algeria has launched a landmark 5G rollout after awarding $492 million in licences to Mobilis, Djezzy and Ooredoo ... unveiling a national 5G rollout whose first three licences, awarded to Mobilis, Djezzy and Ooredoo, are worth $492 million (DZD 63.9 billion). The launch is one of North Africa's largest telecom investments this decade." The six-year national plan begins with eight pilot wilayas before full nationwide coverage by 2031.

## Notes

A 5G spectrum-licence award to three named operators (licence fees to the Algerian state; ownership is mixed — Mobilis and Djezzy state-controlled, Ooredoo Algeria Qatari-owned). Recorded as operator investment into DZA 5G; `finance_origin: non-state`. The three operators share the DZD 63.9bn aggregate figure — `mobilis` tagged as primary financier, Djezzy and Ooredoo carried in the body. The Ericsson estimate that full national deployment could cost US$3–8bn is a projection, not a commitment.
