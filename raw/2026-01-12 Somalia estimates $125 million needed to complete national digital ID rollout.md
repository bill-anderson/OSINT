# Somalia estimates $125 million needed to complete national digital ID rollout

> ## Excerpt
> Somali officials put the cost of completing the nationwide digital ID rollout at
> ~$125 million; funding sources unspecified. Single-sourced estimate (Jan 2026).

---

Somali officials have estimated that approximately $125 million is required to complete the nationwide rollout of the country's national digital ID program, citing the figure as the cost of moving from current progress to full operational coverage.

The estimate is presented as funding needed to fully deploy the system rather than to continue limited enrollment or platform development. Full rollout typically includes expanding enrollment operations (registration kits, staffing, training, transportation), strengthening core identity systems (lifecycle management, deduplication, security at scale), credential issuance (digital and physical), and integration with government and, in some cases, private-sector services. Ongoing support functions — help desks, exception handling, fraud management, governance — generally increase as enrollment volumes grow.

The $125 million estimate highlights the financial demands of scaling identity infrastructure in a fragile context. Funding sources have not been specified, raising questions about the balance between domestic financing, international donor support, and blended models.

Somalia's national digital ID program has been positioned as a foundational element for public administration and service delivery. The country recently launched three national digital identity platforms, including HUBIYE for identity verification and e-Aqoonsi for mobile digital IDs, following earlier biometric national ID system development backed by World Bank support.
