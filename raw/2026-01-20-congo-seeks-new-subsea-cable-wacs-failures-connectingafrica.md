# Congo wants to connect to new cable amidst WACS failures

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa. January 20, 2026.

The Republic of Congo is exploring a connection to a new submarine cable to mitigate ongoing Internet disruptions caused by repeated failures on the West Africa Cable System (WACS).

According to Benjamin Mouandza, electronic communications networks services director of the country's Regulatory Authority for Posts and Electronic Communications (ARPCE), the connection to a different cable could be operational within three weeks if all goes according to schedule.

Mouandza expressed hope that the future Dow Africa cable, the construction of which has been delayed, will also help strengthen connectivity once operational.

During a meeting with Léon Juste Ibombo, the Minister of Posts, Telecommunications, and Digital Economy for the Republic of Congo, telecom operators were asked to deploy emergency solutions to restore stable connectivity.

The minister also requested detailed technical information on long-term solutions to mitigate the recurring failures of the WACS cable.

"For several weeks, Congolese people have been experiencing repeated interruptions and exasperatingly slow Internet connections, direct consequences of failures in the WACS submarine cable, one of the vital arteries of fiber optics supplying Africa with connectivity," Ibombo said.

Congo's authorities argue that tapping into a new cable will equip operators with more stable, better-performing Internet services.

## Congo's dependence on WACS

Congo has depended on the WACS cable for international Internet access since 2012.

Congo's push to connect to a new subsea cable comes at a time when its neighbor, the Democratic Republic of Congo, is also experiencing widespread Internet disruptions.

Last week, the DRC's Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (ARPTC), said maintenance operations were underway to restore services as quickly as possible.

The ARPTC confirmed that it was working with telecom operators to implement appropriate measures to support subscribers, in accordance with regulations regarding the disruptions.

Despite both countries reporting WACS-related outages, the cable system itself has issued no communications on the matter.
