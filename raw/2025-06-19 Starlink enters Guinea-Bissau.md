Starlink – the satellite Internet service operated by Elon Musk's SpaceX – has launched in Guinea-Bissau, marking its seventh launch in West Africa.

The launch comes after Guinea-Bissau's National Regulatory Authority for Information and Communication Technologies (ARN-TIC) granted Starlink a provisional license in December 2024 and final approval to operate in April 2025.

Starlink's entry is expected to boost connectivity in one of West Africa's least connected nations, as statistics from DataReportal show there were 723,000 Internet users in Guinea-Bissau in January 2025, with an Internet penetration rate of just 32.5%. With a population of 2.23 million, Kepios analysis indicates the number of Internet users increased by 48,000, or 2.2%, between January 2024 and January 2025.

**Pricing:** Consumers can buy Starlink devices and access the service directly from the Starlink website. A standard Starlink kit costs 228,000 West African francs (US$400) with a monthly subscription of XOF36,000 ($63); a Starlink Mini Kit costs XOF117,000 ($206). A 250GB residential data plan carries a reduced monthly fee of XOF18,000 ($32). Starlink offers a 30-day trial with no contracts or data caps and estimates two-week shipping.

Guinea-Bissau is Starlink's seventh market in West Africa and its 20th across the African continent. Starlink has been making inroads in Africa since launching in Nigeria in January 2023; in 2025 it secured operational licenses in Somalia and Lesotho (service not yet available there) and has yet to launch in South Africa due to local ownership rules.
