Tanzania’s State Telecom Targets 1 Million Fiber Subscribers by 2028

Broadband expansion initially focuses on major Tanzanian cities and Zanzibar

TTCL seeks stronger broadband market position amid rising internet demand

Tanzania's state-owned telecommunications company TTCL is targeting one million fiber optic subscribers over the next three years as part of a broader broadband expansion strategy aimed at accelerating connectivity across the country.

TTCL Managing Director Moremi Marwa announced the target last week during a ceremony to hand over vehicles to field teams, local media reported. Marwa said the rollout began this year and will accelerate over the coming years.

For the current fiscal year, the company aims to deliver 280,000 new connections, of which about 200,000 have already been completed. TTCL plans to deploy 400,000 additional connections in 2026/27, followed by around 300,000 in 2027/28.

Marwa said the current phase is focused on Dar es Salaam, Dodoma, Mwanza, Iringa, Mbeya, Zanzibar and Arusha, where infrastructure and support systems are being installed to connect customers and improve service delivery. The next phase will gradually extend coverage to the rest of the country, with the aim of achieving nationwide coverage.

The strategy comes as Tanzania’s digital transformation accelerates, driven by rising demand for high-speed internet services. Fiber optic infrastructure is increasingly seen as essential for government institutions, businesses and households. For residential users, it supports applications such as smart home systems, streaming, gaming, e-learning and remote work.

President Samia Suluhu Hassan urged TTCL in March 2023 to refocus on broadband services, saying the company had struggled to establish itself as a mobile operator despite having access to public telecommunications infrastructure. At the time, TTCL set a target of reaching one million customers by 2027.

Marwa said the expansion program is also intended to strengthen TTCL’s position in the broadband market. According to regulator data, the company held 2.78% of the mobile internet market at the end of March, in a market dominated by Vodacom, Yas and Airtel.

In the fixed-line market, however, TTCL ranked third with 63,843 subscribers, behind Airtel with 213,010 and Vodacom with 114,109.
