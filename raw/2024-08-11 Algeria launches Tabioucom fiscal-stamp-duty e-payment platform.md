# Algeria launches Tabioucom fiscal-stamp-duty e-payment platform

Algeria's **General Directorate of Taxes (DGI)** launched **Tabioucom** (tabioucom.mf.gov.dz), a platform for **online payment of fiscal stamp duties**, at a ceremony chaired by Finance Minister **Laaziz Faid** (with Post & Telecoms Minister Karim Bibi Triki and High Commissioner for Digitisation **Meriem Benmouloud** — see [[high-commission-digitalisation-algeria]]). Users pay via **Edahabia or CIB cards** and download receipts, initially attachable to passport, national-ID and driving-licence applications; the service is to expand to visas, commercial registers, transport contracts, residence cards and consular documents (Arabic/English/French).

Built by working groups across Finance, Interior and Post with the **GIE Monétique** and the Digital Development Support Enterprise (EADN). A DGI–Interior agreement establishes e-payment-receipt verification procedures. Officials framed it as simplifying procedures, reducing stamp-duty fraud, protecting treasury rights and promoting electronic payment / financial inclusion.
