It may become more difficult for people in several West African countries to cross international borders following their exit from regional blocs that provide biometric travel documents and digital IDs. ECOWAS placed Burkina Faso, Mali and Niger under sanctions following military coups; the three nations are leaving the bloc, meaning their citizens will no longer be able to use the ECOWAS Passport or biometric ID card, and they will not be part of the WURI digital ID program. ECOWAS is lifting sanctions to salvage some regional integration (per Pulse). Commission President Dr. Omar Touray noted all three departing countries have benefitted from WURI.

The West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion (WURI) program is a World Bank-backed scheme supporting issuance of foundational IDs in ECOWAS countries: Guinea, Cote d'Ivoire, Togo, Benin, Burkina Faso and Niger.

WURI advances in Togo: A Togolese government agency says WURI test registration is underway after sites and supervisors were selected, per Togo First. Test registrations of the biometric digital ID are planned from March 5 to 15 [2024]. The Ministry of Planning, Development, and Cooperation (INSEED) and the Ministry of Digital Economy and Digital Transformation (ANID) are managing the project in Togo. Togo First notes the project is supported by $72 million in funding from the World Bank, from a total of $395 million for WURI. ANID had originally planned to launch the test in the fourth quarter of 2023.
