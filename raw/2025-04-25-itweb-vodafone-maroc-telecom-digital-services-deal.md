# Vodafone and Maroc Telecom sign digital services deal

By Lezeth Khoza, Junior journalist, Johannesburg, 25 Apr 2025

Vodafone Business and Maroc Telecom announce partnership to serve enterprise and public sector customers in Morocco.

Vodafone Business and Maroc Telecom have signed a Memorandum of Understanding to provide connectivity and digital services to Moroccan enterprise customers, with the initial phase focussing on smart city and energy management solutions.

According to Vodafone Business, the cooperation strengthens the company's existing footprint in eight African countries through Vodacom and contributes to Morocco's goal of becoming a significant digital hub by 2030.

The agreement, the parties said, aims to leverage Maroc Telecom's local experience and digital capabilities, as well as Vodafone's global, scalable, and secure platforms, to support innovative fintech, healthcare, and industrial applications powered by a young workforce and start-up culture.

Furthermore, the partnership wants to broaden its offerings over time to include a variety of enterprise products and services such as Software-Defined Wide Area Networks, Mobile Private Networks, cloud solutions, and cyber security.

The two companies went on to say they will also serve Maroc Telecom's multinational and regional customers in key industry sectors such as logistics, manufacturing, retail, and public services, offering a unified portfolio of managed services that can be quickly scaled across multiple markets.

"By partnering with Maroc Telecom, we can boost the adoption of new digital services in Morocco in support of the country's digital ambitions. Maroc Telecom's local expertise and digital skills combined with Vodafone's scalable, secure platforms will underpin new fintech, healthcare, and industrial applications driven by a young workforce and start-up culture," said Fanan Henriques, product and international business director of Vodafone Business.
