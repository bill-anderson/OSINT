Zambia's **Ministry of Technology and Science**, with the **COMESA** Secretariat and the **World Bank**, has
launched an **AI Sensitisation Workshop** in Lusaka under the **Inclusive Digitalization in Eastern and Southern
Africa (IDEA) Programme**. It aims to promote responsible AI adoption across the region and support the
development of **harmonised AI policies and regulatory frameworks**, and marks the first structured activity
under the Stakeholder Engagement Plan for the AI and Emerging Technologies Consultancy.

The event was opened by the Permanent Secretary, **Eng. Dr. Brilliant Habeenzu**, at the Radisson Blu Hotel,
Lusaka. COMESA Secretariat representative **Leonard Chitundu** said the engagements combine awareness creation,
capacity building and structured consultation so that future policy recommendations are evidence-based and
aligned with **regional harmonisation** objectives, addressing data governance, ethical AI, consumer
protection, cybersecurity, skills and regulatory preparedness.

Dr. Habeenzu warned that **without harmonised policy frameworks, Zambia and other COMESA member states risk
fragmented approaches, inconsistent safeguards and missed opportunities**. He cited AI's projected contribution
of **>US$15.7trn** to the global economy by 2030 (Africa ~**US$1.2trn** if adoption accelerates responsibly),
and reaffirmed Zambia's investments in broadband, e-government, cybersecurity, digital identity and digital
payments — noting **mobile broadband penetration reached 72% in 2025** (up from <40% a decade earlier) and
**300+ communication towers built since 2022**.

He highlighted Zambia's participation in the **US$2.48bn IDEA Programme** (with COMESA and the World Bank,
supporting 15+ countries). Recommendations will feed Zambia's national AI agenda and the **COMESA Regional AI
Strategy** and **Regional Digital Inclusion Strategy**.
