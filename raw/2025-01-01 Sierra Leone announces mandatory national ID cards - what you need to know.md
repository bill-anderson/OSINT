# Sierra Leone announces mandatory national ID cards - what you need to know

> ## Excerpt
> A joint NCRA/Sierra Leone Police/ONS ultimatum (dated 20 Dec 2024) made the NCRA national ID card mandatory for movement and services from 1 Jan 2025, ending acceptance of the voter's card as ID at banks, NGOs and institutions. Grounded in the 2016 NCR Act.

---

_Note: page carries no explicit publication date; content references the December 2024 ultimatum and its January 1, 2025 effect, so published early 2025 (year precision)._

Late last year (2024), State bodies in charge of national civil registration/identification and national security — the National Civil Registration Authority (NCRA), Sierra Leone Police (SLP), and the Office of National Security (ONS) — issued a joint 'ultimatum' in the form of a press release, mandating Sierra Leoneans and non-nationals residing within the country's borders to secure national civil identity cards to guarantee their free national movements, before January 1, 2025.

Several issues have arisen, ranging from registering for the identity card, the date for collection, the uses of the cards, the costs, security features, and the significance of the cards to nationals and non-nationals.

## National Civil Registration Act

The Sierra Leone Parliament passed into law an Act establishing the National Civil Registration Authority (NCRA) on June 30, 2016. The establishment of the Authority is essentially to record every Sierra Leonean by registering, collecting, and safekeeping necessary personal data of every Sierra Leonean residing in the country. The NCRA is the sole agency with responsibility to record, register, and safely keep the information to identify all Sierra Leoneans and non-Sierra Leoneans living in the nation.

## "Mandatory ultimatum"

The issuance of a joint 'ultimatum release' regarding the mandatory possession of the NCRA national identity card has sparked mixed feelings. Initially, most public service-producing institutions accepted the country's voter's card as a strong source of identification.

The ultimatum release dated December 20, 2024, clearly indicated the different services and places where the NCRA ID card will be used nationwide starting on January 1, 2025. The release states in parts: "…the general public is hereby informed that effective January 1, 2025, the security sector will commence checks for ID cards in communities and at various Security Check Points across the country."

Abu Bakarr Javombo, the Director of Information and Public Education for the NCRA, confirmed the ultimatum notice. He said the national identity card approved for every Sierra Leonean is a 'mandatory ID card' that all nationals must have to guarantee free movement in the entire state, and that in due time every other form of identity card will not be entertained by any institution in 2025.

## Significance of the NCRA ID card

Javombo said that in due time, every other form of identity card, including the 'great voter's card' — used over the years as a strong force of identification — will come to an end after January 1, 2025.

## Punishment for failing to produce the card

Javombo noted he could not predict the punishment or fines security personnel would institute, but confirmed 'drastic measures' would not be taken against citizens without the card. However, citizens without an NCRA national identity card by the end of 2024 (the grace period) should not be shocked if police stop them from moving freely.

## Will banks accept voter's cards after January 1, 2025?

No. Banks and other public institutions will not accept any other form of identity card than the national identification card from the NCRA. "From January 1, 2025, all banking halls, loan facilities, NGO & private organisations will not accept any voting card because the said card is only meant for electoral processes." The NCRA card will be the country's only recognised identity card for nationals and non-nationals.
