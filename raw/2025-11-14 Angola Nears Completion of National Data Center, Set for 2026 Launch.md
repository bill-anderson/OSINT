Angola's new national data center is expected to be operational in H1 2026, the government said after a site visit by Telecommunications Minister Mário Augusto da Silva Oliveira. Per André Pedro, Director General of the National Institute for the Promotion of the Information Society (INFOSI), construction is ~75% complete, shifting to equipment installation. The facility uses a modular design of 12 containers, each holding 28 racks, for 336 racks total.

The data center is developed alongside a unified government cloud, a combined ~$90 million investment; once inaugurated, all government services must migrate within 30 days. Angola ranks 156th of 193 on the UN's 2024 EGDI (0.4149). The government said in February 2024 that the Angola Digital Acceleration Program (PADA), backed by $300 million in World Bank funding, had faced delays partly due to low internet access. DataReportal figures show 17.2 million internet users at the start of 2023 (44.8% penetration).
