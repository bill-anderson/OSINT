# Exclusive Q&A: Africa's Blueprint for a Secure Digital Future

By Akim Benamara — TechAfrica News (#TechTalkThursday), 27 February 2025

A Q&A with Jules Hervé Yimeumi, President of Africa Data Protection (ADP), an organization dedicated to strengthening data privacy, cybersecurity, and digital governance across the continent.

Africa's data protection landscape is described as a patchwork of inconsistent laws, outdated policies, and weak enforcement mechanisms. Some nations, like Nigeria, have taken bold steps to regulate data use and hold global corporations accountable (evidenced by the record-breaking $220 million fine against Meta), while others still lack the legal frameworks necessary to protect citizens' digital rights. The disparity complicates efforts to establish a unified, secure, and sovereign digital ecosystem. Despite growing investments, the continent still relies heavily on foreign data centers to store and process sensitive information.

ADP's mission is to promote the confidentiality, security, and integrity of personal data across the continent, through awareness/education (online courses, digital literacy books), policy and regulatory advocacy (recommendations for harmonised frameworks), and research/publications on data protection and AI regulation.

On continental harmonisation, Yimeumi notes significant progress is still needed. The Malabo Convention on Cybersecurity and Personal Data Protection provides guidelines, but adoption has been slow — created in 2014, it was not adopted until nine years later in 2023. The continent faces challenges with cross-border data transfers and cybersecurity, and there is no single continental standard defining "adequate protection," leading to legal uncertainty. Recommendations include adopting continent-wide regulations aligned with global standards, promoting regional harmonisation through the AU, enforcing data sovereignty laws, developing more local data centers, supporting PPPs, and strengthening cross-border data flow frameworks including via the AfCFTA.
