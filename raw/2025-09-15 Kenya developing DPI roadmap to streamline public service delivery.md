The Kenyan government is developing a **DPI roadmap** to transform how citizens access public and private-sector services; the Ministry of ICT and Digital Economy held a stakeholder meeting with development partners including the EU and GIZ.

A representative of PS Mary Kerema said the roadmap seeks to lay a foundation for public-service delivery, digital innovation and cross-border digital trade, resting on five pillars: inclusivity, trust, innovation, interoperability and sustainability. Kenya already has a national ID (**Maisha Namba**) and digital payment systems (headlined by M-Pesa); the roadmap aims to make these more impactful. It follows the launch of a **National AI Strategy 2025-2030**, with a National Data Governance policy in preparation, all part of the **Kenya National Digital Masterplan 2022-2032**.
