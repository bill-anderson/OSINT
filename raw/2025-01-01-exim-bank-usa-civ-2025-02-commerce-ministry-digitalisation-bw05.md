# US EXIM Bank — Côte d'Ivoire Ministry of Commerce and Industry digitalisation, 2025

The Export-Import Bank of the United States approved a US$47 million (28.2 billion FCFA) guarantee, board-voted on 14 August 2025, to fund the digitalisation of Côte d'Ivoire's Ministry of Commerce and Industry, with Cybastion as lead exporter.

## Description

Fraternité Matin (2025-08-26): "après le vote par le Conseil d'administration le 14 août, l'octroi de 47 millions de dollars (28,2 milliards FCFA) pour la numérisation du ministère du Commerce et de l'Industrie." TechAfrica News (2025-09-22): "a $47 million guarantee for the digitalization of the Ministry of Finance and Industry. Cybastion, the lead exporter, will work with U.S. technology partners such as Cisco, Hewlett Packard Enterprise, and Schneider Electric."

We Are Tech Africa (on the EXIM board approval): "The Export-Import Bank of the United States (EXIM) board of directors approved... a $47 million financing package for the digitization of Côte d'Ivoire's Ministry of Commerce and Industry. The project will be implemented by the Washington-based Cybastion Institute of Technology in partnership with technology giants such as Cisco, Amazon Web Services (AWS), Motorola Solutions, and Microsoft" — to "modernize the ministry's information systems, strengthen cybersecurity, integrate cloud solutions, and improve administrative efficiency." Per EXIM acting chairman James Cruse the transaction supports around 100 U.S. jobs and counters the rise of PRC influence, framed as a China and Transformational Exports Program (CTEP) transaction.
