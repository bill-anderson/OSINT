# Mauritius Unveils National Fintech Strategy to Modernize Payments

*We Are Tech Africa, 29 June 2026.*

The government unveiled the **National Fintech Strategy 2026-2030** on **Thursday 25 June 2026**, with technical support from the **UN Economic Commission for Africa (ECA)**, to modernise payment systems, expand financial inclusion and enhance the competitiveness of the financial sector.

**Six priority pillars:** adapting the regulatory framework; supporting innovation; strengthening digital infrastructure and cybersecurity; developing digital skills; attracting investment; expanding financial inclusion. The strategy calls for a **National Fintech Governance Committee**, a national fintech skills framework, an AI learning platform, and stronger fintech-startup support.

Plans include encouraging new payment services, expanding cross-border transactions, promoting **open banking**, and integrating AI into financial services with stronger consumer protection and data governance. Implementation via legislative reforms including the planned **Business Facilitation Bill** and stronger coordination with the **Bank of Mauritius**. Context: Africa's fintech market could exceed **$65 billion by 2030** (ECA). Launched at Côte d'Or Technopole with 200+ stakeholders.
