By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa. May 26, 2026.

Orange Côte d'Ivoire and the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) have partnered to promote digital inclusion and youth empowerment in the country.

Orange's Ivorian subsidiary has partnered with the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) to promote digital inclusion and youth empowerment in the country.

The companies said the collaboration represents a significant milestone in advancing collaborative initiatives aimed at promoting sustainable development and reducing inequalities in Côte d'Ivoire.

In a LinkedIn post Orange Côte d'Ivoire CEO Mamadou Bamba said the partnership reflects the partners' common desire to use digital technology as a lever for inclusion, innovation and social transformation.

"Together, we want to create more opportunities for young people and women and actively contribute to the sustainable development of Côte d'Ivoire," he explained.

UNDP Resident Representative Blerta Cela said the organizations shared a common desire to contribute to building a more connected Côte d'Ivoire.

"This is the beginning of a common commitment based on a shared vision that of promoting innovation, accelerating the digital transformation and enabling Ivorian youth to actively participate in building the future of the country," she said.

The partnership is closely aligned with UNDP's strategy to scale up innovative and digital-based solutions on the continent.

Earlier this month, UNDP met with officials from Lesotho to discuss plans for establishing an AI academy in the country.

Orange Côte d'Ivoire and UNDP said the partnership will see them create digital opportunities for young people and women in the country.

## Orange's digital transformation agenda

The partnership arrives as the telecom provider intensifies its drive to advance the country's digital transformation.

In January 2026, Orange Côte d'Ivoire partnered with Eutelsat to launch a new satellite broadband Internet service called Orange Sat aimed at connecting the country's underserved areas.

That deal was built on an existing relationship between Orange and Eutelsat.

In June 2025, Orange signed a multi-year agreement with Eutelsat Group to leverage Eutelsat's OneWeb low-Earth orbit (LEO) constellation.

In February 2025, Eutelsat announced it had connected 1 million people in sub-Saharan Africa to satellite Internet via its Eutelsat Konnect hotspots.
