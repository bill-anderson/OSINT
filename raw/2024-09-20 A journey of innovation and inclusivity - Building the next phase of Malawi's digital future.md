Malawi established its first multi-modal biometric citizen database in 2018, registering 9.1 million people. As of 2024, over 12.5 million people aged 16+ have obtained a digital legal ID alongside 3 million birth registrations — over 75 percent of the estimated population and 99 percent of adults. The national registration and identification system won the World Summit on the Information Society Award 2021. The digital ID has been integrated across voting, payroll, SIM cards, passports, immigration and tax, with sectoral applications in health, banking and agriculture.

Malawi is transitioning from initial DPI deployment to operations and maintenance. NRB Principal Secretary Mphatso Augustine Sambo frames the foundational ID, linked to functional repositories, as "the only way to truly serve the public's interests." Next-phase risks span the technical (unreliable power/internet for biometric kits; a dedicated data centre near completion; need for interoperable APIs), the operational (timely ID delivery; retaining technical staff) and the normative (sustainable funding beyond donors; verification fees that must not exclude low-income users; commitment beyond electoral cycles). Malawi has launched the Inclusive Digital Transformation for Malawi (IDT4M) project, joined the 50-in-5 campaign, conducted a Digital Readiness Assessment, and passed a Data Protection Bill aligned with international standards.
