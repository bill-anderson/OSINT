Guinea is a West African country with a population of almost 14 million (2023). An alarmingly high percentage of the population has no birth, marriage or death certificates: official figures show only 45-60% of Guineans have birth certificates and less than 2% of marriages and deaths are registered. Enabel (the Belgian Development Agency) is helping Guinean authorities modernise the civil registry via an EU-funded pilot project in ten communes; Zoé Allado is project manager.

In 2022, 44,493 new vital events were recorded on time in the ten pilot municipalities, while 66,562 people obtained civil-registry certificates through a court procedure. Low registration makes it hard for citizens to access social services, education, healthcare, employment and land ownership.

The project has three phases: (1) a digital national civil registry in the ten pilot communes — paper deeds recorded at municipal level and entered into a national digital database (105,000+ records migrated to date); (2) three application modules developed with Belgian firm **DXC Technology** (enhancing records security, harmonising court decisions, and enabling automatic birth notification via mobile app within ten days); (3) adapting Guinean laws to support digitisation, with the Belgian Federal Public Service Justice involved. The project drew on best practice from Rwanda and Belgium (BOSA, the DABS/BAEC civil-status database). Belgian teams visit Guinea every two months. Key partners include the Ministry of Territorial Administration and Decentralisation and the ten pilot municipalities. The EU is convinced by results and an extension to consolidate and scale the registry is already envisaged.
