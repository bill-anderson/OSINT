RFI (21 Aug 2020) reports that **Al Madina** prints the CAR's secure documents — residence permits, driving
licences and, newly, the **national identity card**. Beyond cost, the report flags that **the company is
"registered in Oman" and "directed by Aziz Nassour"** — the source of political controversy in Bangui.

CAR deputies challenged the government repeatedly over the choice of firm — "we must entrust our data to a
reliable structure; this is a matter of the sovereignty of the Central African Republic" (the deputy heading
the National Assembly's Defence commission). Interior Minister **Henri Wanzet-Linguissara** said the firm was
chosen for its "expertise" in secure documents and that he was unaware of Nassour's past. Al Madina also
handles CAR vehicle registration, transport permits and foreigners' residence titles.
