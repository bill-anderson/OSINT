Angola is getting **$200 million in credit from India** to implement digital public infrastructure, announced after a New Delhi meeting between President João Lourenço and PM Narendra Modi (the first Angolan presidential visit in 38 years). The credit line spans DPI, digital governance, space, defence and healthcare, with digital-skills training.

Angola will adopt India's DPI model — enabling digital IDs, e-payments and social registries — and hopes to model its digital ID system on **Aadhaar**. India's Modular Open Source Identity Platform (**MOSIP**) has helped build DPI in Morocco, Ethiopia, Sierra Leone and Guinea, while Indian experts collaborated with Rwanda on UPI-inspired payments. Per Ecofin, Angola ranks 156th of 193 on the 2024 e-Government Development Index (0.4149), below the African (0.4247) and global (0.6382) averages.
