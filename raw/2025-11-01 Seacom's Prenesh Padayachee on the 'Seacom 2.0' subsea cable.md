Prenesh Padayachee, the group chief digital infrastructure officer at Seacom, sat down with Connecting Africa at Africa Tech Festival 2025 to share more about the planned 'Seacom 2.0' subsea cable system.

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ev3uYxbL5qE)

Prenesh Padayachee, group chief digital infrastructure officer at Seacom, sat down with Connecting Africa Editor Paula Gilbert at Africa Tech Festival 2025 in Cape Town, South Africa, to share more about the [planned "Seacom 2.0" subsea cable system](https://www.connectingafrica.com/connectivity/seacom-announces-ai-ready-2-0-subsea-cable-network).

Padayachee explained the rationale behind the decision to launch another subsea cable system to address the exponential growth in demand for artificial intelligence (AI), cloud and real-time data services.

Seacom launched the first privately owned subsea cable to connect countries around the Indian Ocean back in 2009.

Padayachee shared more about the technical capabilities of the new cable and the route it will take across the Indian Ocean Basin, Middle East, Mediterranean and Southern Europe – connecting more countries than the previous cable.

He provided some timelines for the different phases of the project, with phase one set to be complete by early 2030, and Seacom's long-term plans to retire its original subsea cable system in 2035.

Padayachee said the cable system is projected to cost between $1.5 billion and $2 billion to construct and that Seacom will raise capital from existing shareholders as well as new investors to complete the project.

He also touched on Seacom's view on the importance of data center neutrality and possible expansions into terrestrial fiber projects.
