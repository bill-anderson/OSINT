# Building Open Digital States: Country Case Studies on the Impact of DPGs for DPI

> ## Excerpt
> A DPGA-commissioned evidence review arguing digital public goods (open-source, meeting the DPG
> Standard) offer developing states a modular, lock-in-resistant path to sovereign DPI. Three case
> studies: Philippines, Kyrgyzstan and **Rwanda** (the sole African case).

> **Provenance note.** `published` is a proxy from PDF metadata (2026-01-20; no printed date). Health/
> payment outcomes are attributed as DPG *contribution*, not sole causation. Invokes Pablo Chavez's
> "hybrid sovereignty" framing.

## Key facts (Rwanda)

- **DHIS2** (adopted 2012; 700+ facilities): data completeness 88%→95%, timeliness 60%→90%+; COVID
  vaccination 77%+ and 5.8m+ tests tracked via DHIS2 + national-ID linkage. Maternal mortality fell
  >50% (476→203 per 100,000, 2010–2019).
- **Center for Digital Public Infrastructure (CDPI)** established within [[risa]] in **Feb 2025**;
  Mojaloop-based IPS (eKash); local firm **WiredIN** now exports Mojaloop expertise regionally
  (South–South cooperation, soft power). Value framework: economic, social, governance, political.
