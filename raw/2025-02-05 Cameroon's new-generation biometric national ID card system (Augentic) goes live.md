Cameroon launched a new-generation **biometric and digital national ID card (CNI)** system, run by the **General Delegation for National Security ([[dgsn-cameroon]]**, Delegate-General Martin Mbarga Nguele) under a **15-year build-operate-transfer contract signed May 2024 with German firm [[augentic]]**. Online pre-enrolment opened **17 February 2025**, biometric capture from 24 February, with delivery promised within **48 hours** at regional-capital identification posts.

The card carries an **electronic chip**, a Machine-Readable Zone and a unique QR code, and captures **ten fingerprints**; it meets **ICAO** and **ISO/IEC 19303** standards, and Cameroon became **the first African nation to adopt the ID-1 portrait format**. Actual issuance began in early **March 2025** (initially two Yaoundé centres), once a presidential decree defined the detailed modalities.
