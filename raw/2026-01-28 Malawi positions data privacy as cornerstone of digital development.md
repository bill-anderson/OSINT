The Malawi Government has reaffirmed its commitment to embedding data privacy at the core of Malawi’s digital transformation agenda, declaring “Privacy by Design” essential to national development, trust in digital systems, and economic growth.

Malawi Politics Analysis

Speaking at the commemoration of Data Privacy Week in Lilongwe on Monday, Minister of Information and Communications Technology, Shadrick Namalomba, said data protection is no longer optional but central to achieving the aspirations of Malawi 2063 and the Democratic Progressive Party’s governance agenda.

Namalomba described data as a strategic national asset powering innovation, service delivery, and private sector growth, while cautioning that digital progress must be secure, people-centred, and anchored in respect for citizens’ rights and dignity.

He pointed to the Data Protection Act of 2024 as evidence of government’s resolve to strengthen accountability, curb data misuse, and ensure ethical handling of personal information across both public and private institutions.

The Minister said adopting Privacy by Design, building privacy safeguards into systems from inception, would enhance public confidence in e-government services, digital identity platforms, and emerging technologies critical to efficient governance.

He further noted that a trusted digital ecosystem is key to attracting investment and enabling private sector-led growth, arguing that businesses and citizens alike will only fully embrace digital services when data protection is guaranteed.

Namalomba called on government agencies, the private sector, civil society, the media, and citizens to share responsibility for safeguarding data, urging stakeholders to translate policy commitments into practical action that supports inclusive, secure, and sustainable development.
