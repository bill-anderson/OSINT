Guinea marked a historic step in its digital transformation with the official launch of its national domain ".gn" and the inauguration of the country's first Tier III national data centre. The ceremony was chaired by Djiba Diakité (Minister, Chief of Cabinet and Chairman of the Simandou Strategic Committee) alongside General Amara Camara (Minister Secretary General of the Presidency), with republican institutions, the CNR, government, diplomatic corps and technical/financial partners.

A notable announcement: Conakry will host the 7th edition of the Transform Africa Summit (TAS25) from 12-14 November 2025, focused on Artificial Intelligence — making Conakry the first West African capital, and Guinea the first Francophone African country, to host the landmark continental forum. Authorities also confirmed a 25% reduction in internet tariffs by Orange Guinea, hailed as a breakthrough for digital inclusion.

Partners supporting the initiatives included the World Bank, Orange Guinea, Sky Connect, VDC Telecom, CBG, GUICOPRES, MTN, ARPT, ANSUTEN, SOGEB, GUILAB, Leader Net Service, Huawei, Poste SA, ANDE, ANSSI and Canal+.
