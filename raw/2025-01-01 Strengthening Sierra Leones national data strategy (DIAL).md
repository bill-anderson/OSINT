# Strengthening Sierra Leone's national data strategy (DIAL)

> ## Excerpt
> DIAL supported the Government of Sierra Leone in a focused 5-month 2025 engagement to update and refine its National Data Strategy, prioritising alignment with the AU Data Policy Framework. Sierra Leone's first National Data Strategy was developed off DIAL's 2022 work on the country's digital transformation strategy.

In recent years, Sierra Leone has made significant progress in laying the foundations for a thriving digital ecosystem, with internet penetration rates of 30.4% in 2024, up from just 12% in 2018. In 2022, DIAL worked with the Government of Sierra Leone together with policymakers, private sector leaders, and civil society to help shape the country's digital transformation strategy.

This collaborative effort led to the development of Sierra Leone's first ever National Data Strategy — a critical step shaping how data is collected, managed, and used across sectors. This strategy helped define governance frameworks, interoperability standards, and policies.

Regional and global frameworks such as the African Union Data Policy Framework are shaping the future of data governance on the continent, making it critical for Sierra Leone to align its strategy with these standards.

In 2025, DIAL supported the Government of Sierra Leone in updating and refining its National Data Strategy over a focused 5-month engagement, prioritising adherence to the African Union's Data Policy Framework. The work focused on three key pillars:

1. Actively engaging key stakeholders — government agencies, private sector, civil society, academia, and international experts — to foster whole-of-government engagement.
2. Conducting an extensive literature review and benchmarking against global and regional best practices.
3. Conducting key informant interviews with policy experts, data practitioners, and industry leaders.

These consultations directly informed the drafting and refinement of the National Data Strategy. DIAL cites its experience through initiatives such as the Africa Data Leadership Initiative (ADLI) and its work on Sierra Leone's first National Data Strategy. Contact: Talla Ndiaye.
