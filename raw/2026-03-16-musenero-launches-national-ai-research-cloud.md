A National AI Research Cloud has been launched to build local computing infrastructure for developing AI models and to let innovators store and process large datasets. Hosted at Makerere University and jointly run by the College of Engineering, Design, Art and Technology (CEDAT) and the College of Computing and Information Sciences (CoCIS), it was developed in partnership with the Research and Education Network for Uganda (RENU), allowing researchers to store and process data within the country — cheaper and more secure.

Dr Joyce Nakatumba Nabende (Makerere Computer Science) said innovators previously relied on international commercial cloud services (Amazon, Google) that were expensive and raised data-sovereignty concerns. "Data is the new gold that powers AI systems. The research cloud provides the space where innovators can upload their datasets and access computing resources to train their models." Speaking at the inaugural AI Innovation Demo Day, Minister for Science, Technology and Innovation Dr Monica Musenero Masanza warned Africa risks being left behind: "Many people thought AI would come gently like a breeze, but it has arrived like a tsunami... take our destiny into our own hands," calling for more funding through universities.
