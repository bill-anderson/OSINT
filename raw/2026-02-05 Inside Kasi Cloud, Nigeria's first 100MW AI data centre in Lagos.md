Spread across 42 hectares facing the Atlantic near the Lagos-Calabar coastal road, Kasi Cloud's site is described by its builders as Nigeria's first data centre campus purpose-built for artificial intelligence. Nigeria has about 17 operational data centres, none with capacity higher than 20MW; hyperscale AI campuses typically target 50-100MW+, driven by dense GPU racks (often 50-150 kW per rack).

"This was designed for AI from day one," said founder and CEO Johnson Agogbua during a 25 January 2026 site tour. Kasi Cloud broke ground on its $250 million hyperscale data centre in Lekki in April 2022 (construction began Q2 2023). It expects to complete 5.5 MW in April 2026, with commercial operations beginning in Q2 2026.

The first building is six floors, four dedicated to data halls, each floor designed for an 8MW hall (32MW per building). The company has a permit for four similar facilities; each initially 32MW, with the campus engineered to support up to 100MW of sellable power at full density (realistically about three facilities). Engineering highlights: four independent high-voltage feeds with separate A/B power paths and solid busbars; density of 10-100 kW per rack (versus 5-10 kW in typical Lagos facilities); liquid cooling direct to the chipset; magnetic-drive cooling for low noise; and triple-HEPA filtration to scrub ocean salt and dust.
