Pan-African telecoms operator Vodacom Group has announced a $2.1 billion deal that will see it increase its stake in Kenya's Safaricom to 55%.

Safaricom CEO Peter Ndegwa. (Source: Safaricom)

Pan-African telecommunications company Vodacom Group plans to increase its ownership in Kenya's Safaricom to over 50%.

Under the terms of the deal, Vodacom has entered into an agreement with the Government of Kenya to acquire 15% of Safaricom PLC and an additional 5% from Vodafone at 34 Kenyan shillings (US$0.26) per share, valuing the deal at $2.1 billion

Should the transaction receive requisite approvals from regulatory and governmental authorities in Kenya, Ethiopia and South Africa, this will see Vodacom's stake in Safaricom increase from 35% to 55%.

Safaricom is Kenya's biggest company by market capitalization and will remain listed on the Nairobi Securities Exchange.

Vodacom said that the deal reinforces its commitment in the East African markets of Kenya and Ethiopia where Safaricom operates telecom and mobile money services.

## Vodacom is expanding its reach and scale

Vodacom said the move is a key milestone in the company's [Vision 2030 strategy](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/vodacom-eyes-50m-more-subscribers-by-2030), which includes deepening its leadership in Africa's high-growth markets and scaling its diversified portfolio.

"This landmark transaction will mark a pivotal step in Vodacom's journey to accelerate growth and deepens our impact across Africa," said Shameel Joosub, Vodacom Group CEO.

Vodacom Group operates in South Africa, [Mozambique](https://www.connectingafrica.com/5g-networks/vodacom-launches-5g-services-in-mozambique), Lesotho, [Tanzania](https://www.connectingafrica.com/financial-inclusion/mwc-kigali-vodacom-tanzania-md-philip-besiimire-on-mfs-growth), the Democratic Republic of Congo and in [Egypt under the Vodafone brand](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/vodacom-completes-vodafone-egypt-acquisition). Safaricom operates in [Kenya](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/safaricom-posts-11-revenue-rise-grows-in-ethiopia) and [Ethiopia](https://www.connectingafrica.com/connectivity/safaricom-tops-10m-customers-in-ethiopia).

The group has over 223.2 million customers across Africa, including Safaricom, and 93.7 million financial services users.

"Acquiring a controlling stake in Safaricom strengthens our position as a market leader, while at the same time unlocks new opportunities to drive digital and financial inclusion at scale in Kenya and Ethiopia," Joosub added.

He said that Safaricom's outstanding financial track record and differentiated growth outlook complements Vodacom's vision going forward, empowering it to deliver sustainable value for stakeholders and to connect millions more people in Africa.

"I look forward to working even closer with the Safaricom team and taking some of the learnings from their success and leveraging it across the Group," Joosub continued.

Vodacom Group CEO Shameel Joosub. (Source: Vodacom)

In accordance with International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS), Safaricom's financial results will transition from being accounted for on an associate basis to being fully consolidated, increasing Vodacom Group's revenue towards R220 billion ($12.9 billion), Vodacom said.

"Vodacom has been a trusted partner in Safaricom's journey from the very beginning, and we welcome their continued commitment and long-term investment in our business," said Safaricom CEO Peter Ndegwa.

"We look forward to deepening our collaboration as we continue to scale innovation, expand regionally, and deliver transformative digital and financial services to our customers," Ndegwa added.

Safaricom has found [financial success](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/safaricom-posts-11-revenue-rise-grows-in-ethiopia) by offering telecom, technology and fintech services.

"Through the flagship platform M-Pesa in Kenya, it drives high-growth fintech revenue, while [expansion opportunities in Ethiopia](https://www.connectingafrica.com/connectivity/safaricom-tops-10m-customers-in-ethiopia) and a growing suite of cloud, IoT \[Internet of Things\] and enterprise services position Safaricom for continued growth," Vodacom said in a statement.

On behalf of the Kenyan government, Cabinet Secretary for the National Treasury and Economic Planning, John Mbadi said that the transaction is one of the first steps in President William Ruto's stated agenda of "innovatively unlocking capital, without increasing taxes or the countries debt burden, to allow additional investment in critical infrastructure to support future growth."

"Safaricom has been, and continues to be, a key strategic investment for us, as we are retaining a 20% stake as well as board representation," the Cabinet Secretary said.

The Kenyan government currently owns 35% of Safaricom and will sell 15% to Vodacom.
