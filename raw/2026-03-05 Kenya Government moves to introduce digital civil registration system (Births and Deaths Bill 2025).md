Stakeholders convened for a three-day validation workshop on the **Births and Deaths Registration Bill 2025**, a proposal to modernise Kenya's civil-registration system, led by the State Department for Immigration and Citizen Services through the Civil Registration Service (CRS).

Interior CS Kipchumba Murkomen backed the reform (part of improvements after the Jukwaa la Usalama forums); CRS Secretary Paul Mwangemi said the current Births and Deaths Registration Act (Cap 149) is outdated and paper-driven. The Bill introduces a modern legal framework supporting **digital registration** and technology in records creation/preservation, and addresses data protection, access to information, recognition of intersex persons, surrogacy, adoption-related re-registration and presumed deaths. It aligns civil registration with a **national population database**, the proposed **Unique Personal Identifier (UPI) at birth**, and legal identity/nationality at birth. Technical/financial support came from Vital Strategies and the Global Health Advocacy Incubator (GHAI).
