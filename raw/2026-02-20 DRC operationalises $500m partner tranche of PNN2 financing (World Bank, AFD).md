On **17 February 2026**, following talks led by Digital Economy Minister **Augustin Kibassa Maliba**
with **World Bank** and **AFD** (Agence Française de Développement) experts, the DRC government announced
the **operationalisation of a US$500 million financing tranche** for the National Digital Plan 2026-2030
([[drc-national-digital-plan-pnn2|PNN2]]), with technical assistance being finalised to launch
investments along two priority axes: **strengthening digital infrastructure** and **digitalising public
services / building skills**.

This source anchors the composition of the plan's **US$1.5bn financing envelope**:

- **US$1 billion** — government public investment planned over five years (**2026-2030**);
- **US$500 million** — **external-partner contributions already secured** (World Bank, AFD), the tranche
  operationalised here.

It also references earlier (**June 2025**) agreements — a World Bank package including **US$400 million**
for the digital sector, plus an EU contribution of **€100 million (~US$117.5m)**.

The report carries no direct interview quotes; it relays the ministry's own statement.
