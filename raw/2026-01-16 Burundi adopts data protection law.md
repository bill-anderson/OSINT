Burundi's National Assembly, meeting in plenary in Kigobe on **15 January 2026**, unanimously adopted a bill governing the collection, processing and exploitation of personal data across public and private sectors. The law was sponsored by the Ministry of Interior, Community Development and Public Security to close a legal gap created by rapid digitalisation.

It establishes core privacy principles, creates an **independent administrative data-protection authority** responsible for enforcement, and introduces specific criminal penalties for violations, complementing existing cybercrime provisions. Lawmakers tied the reform to the digitisation of the voter register, the expansion of computerised public administration, and preparations for a **biometric national identity card** — all increasing the volume and sensitivity of personal data handled.

The reform aligns Burundi with the AU's **Malabo Convention** on cybersecurity and personal-data protection; the article notes about **39 African countries** had adopted dedicated data-protection laws as of January 2026. Officials said the framework removes a key obstacle to deploying the biometric national ID, with registration software aligned to new administrative boundaries already installed and the system in pilot testing across about a dozen communes ahead of a phased nationwide rollout.
