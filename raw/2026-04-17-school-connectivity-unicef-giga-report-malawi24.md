# Malawi schools have no internet

At the dissemination of the UNICEF/Giga **School Connectivity Landscape Analysis** report in Lilongwe, findings showed **85 percent of Malawi's 9,000 schools have limited or no internet access**, and about **900 schools have no connectivity at all** — with lack of electricity and digital devices cited as major barriers (presented by Penelope Campbell). Education Minister Bright Msaka said government is committed to electrifying and connecting rural schools; ICT Minister Shadric Namalomba said the World Bank is already supporting a project to connect over 200 schools and called on the private sector to supply devices and renewable-energy solutions.

The piece notes a gap between pledges and budget: the 2026/27 National Budget (Finance Minister Joseph Mwanamvekha, Feb 2026) contains **no dedicated budget line for school internet connectivity**. Education received K1.28 trillion (11.7% of the budget), focused on free-education fees, classrooms, materials and universities. The closest digital allocation is K1.1 billion for assembling laptops/tablets at the Malawi University of Science and Technology (higher education only); the Transport & ICT sector's K664.4 billion is directed mainly at roads, rail and aviation.

Key figures: 85% of 9,000 schools with limited/no access; 900 with none; free primary/secondary fees K47.6bn; teaching materials K11bn; dedicated school-connectivity budget: none.
