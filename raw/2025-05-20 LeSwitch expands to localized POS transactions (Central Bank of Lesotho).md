The Central Bank of Lesotho (CBL), with leading banks, introduced localized Point of Sale (POS) transactions under the **[[leswitch|LeSwitch]]** platform, following the localization of ATM transactions in October 2024. Effective **11 May 2025**, LeSwitch rolled out POS functionality as a pilot, gradually onboarding merchants and customers toward nationwide implementation — so Basotho can transact on any POS machine using debit cards regardless of their bank.

Standard Lesotho Bank's Samuel Koetle recounted the journey: LeSwitch (formerly the National Payment Switch) was conceptualized in 2016; in 2021 it introduced instant payments between banks and mobile money (e.g. M-Pesa and Khetsi); in 2023 it domesticated ATM cards; and in October 2024 it piloted LeSwitch-enabled ATM transactions. By early 2025 **all local debit/credit card transactions were fully facilitated within the country** — reducing transaction fees and improving accessibility. The CBL and partners are preparing a next phase focused on e-commerce and retail payments; international payments continue through Visa and Mastercard.
