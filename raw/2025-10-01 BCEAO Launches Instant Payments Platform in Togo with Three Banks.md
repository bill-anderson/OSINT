(Togo First) - The West African States Central Bank (BCEAO) officially launched the interoperable Instant Payments Platform (PI-PSI) on September 30 [2025] in Dakar, enabling 24/7 secure fund transfers across UEMOA countries. Governor Jean-Claude Kassi-Brou presided; the event was broadcast to Lome via videoconference. It marks a major step in modernizing digital payments across the eight UEMOA countries, including Togo.

The platform allows instant, irrevocable transfers between banks, microfinance institutions, e-money issuers, and payment service providers, cutting transaction times and costs while promoting financial inclusion and encouraging electronic payments over cash.

Three banks operational in Togo: Coris Bank, Ecobank, and Orabank currently operate on the platform, per BCEAO. Additional institutions - BOA, BIAT, Cofina, and Sunu Bank Togo - plan to join. Guy Martial Awona, CEO of Orabank Togo and president of the UEMOA banking federation, called the launch a "decisive step toward an ecosystem where banks, microfinance institutions, and fintechs converge on a single infrastructure." Implementation received financial backing from the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation and the African Development Bank (AfDB).
