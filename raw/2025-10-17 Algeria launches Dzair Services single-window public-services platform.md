# Algeria launches Dzair Services single-window public-services platform

Algeria is finalising the rollout of **Dzair Services**, a national platform to centralise all public digital services — announced **16 October 2025** by **Meriem Benmouloud, High Commissioner for Digitalisation** (see [[high-commission-digitalisation-algeria]]), at the Ecsel Expo e-commerce fair in Algiers. Designed as a **single access point** ([[dzair-services]]), it aggregates existing public digital services to simplify procedures, ensure traceability and reduce institutional overlaps, becoming the central interface of Algeria's online administration.

It aligns with the **National Digital Transformation Strategy 2025–2030** ([[digital-algeria-2030]], unveiled May 2025; seven pillars incl. digital infrastructure, cybersecurity and technological sovereignty). Underlying build-out: **46 ministries and agencies connected to fibre**; a national data centre in **El Mohammedia** (a second under construction in **Blida**); and a **sovereign cloud** for public data in preparation. Dzair Services relies on an **interoperability system** for automatic verified-data exchange, laying groundwork for future AI integration.
