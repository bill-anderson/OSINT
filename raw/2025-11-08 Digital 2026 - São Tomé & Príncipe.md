# Digital 2026: São Tomé & Príncipe

> ## Excerpt
> DataReportal's late-2025 baseline (current edition): internet penetration flat at 61.5% (148k users), 154k mobile connections (63.9% of population, GSMA-rated 82.6% "broadband"), social-media reach up to 30.3%. The "broadband share" drop from 93.3% (Jan-2025) reads as a GSMA methodology shift, not a real regression.

Note on date: DataReportal published its Digital 2026 reports at the end of 2025; most data points are from October/late 2025. This is the current baseline edition (comparator: Digital 2025).

---

## State of digital in São Tomé and Príncipe in 2026 (data as of late 2025)

- **Mobile connections:** 154 thousand active cellular mobile connections in late 2025, equivalent to **63.9 percent** of the total population. (Some may include only voice/SMS.) Connections rose by 4,826 (+3.2 percent) between end-2024 and end-2025. GSMA Intelligence data suggests **82.6 percent** of connections are "broadband" (3G/4G/5G).
- **Internet users:** 148 thousand individuals using the internet in October 2025, with online penetration at **61.5 percent**.
- **Social media:** 73.2 thousand social media user identities in October 2025, equating to **30.3 percent** of the total population.

## Population context

- Population stood at **241 thousand** in October 2025 (UN data), up 4,725 (+2.0 percent) between end-2024 and end-2025.
- Urban 77.7 percent / rural 22.3 percent; female 50.3 percent / male 49.7 percent.
- Median age 19.5. Age breakdown (end-2025): 0–4 13.2%, 5–12 19.6%, 13–17 11.6%, 18–24 14.3%, 25–34 13.4%, 35–44 11.0%, 45–54 8.1%, 55–64 4.9%, 65+ 3.9%.

Report supported by Kepios; mobile data from GSMA Intelligence; internet-user analysis by Kepios. DataReportal notes readers should not calculate change-over-time by comparing Digital 2026 (published end-2025) directly against Digital 2025 figures because the publication cycle changed.
