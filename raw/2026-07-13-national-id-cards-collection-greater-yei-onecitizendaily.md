# Thousand begin collecting national identity cards in greater Yei

*One Citizen Daily, 13 July 2026*

More than 1,000 people turned up at the Directorate of Nationality, Passports, and Immigration in Yei River County on Friday as the distribution of newly processed South Sudanese national identity cards officially began. The exercise follows the arrival of more than 4,000 national identity cards for applicants from the four counties of the Greater Yei region, according to immigration officials.

Col. Lokiko said the distribution exercise had officially commenced and assured applicants that the Yei office was ready to issue the documents to eligible recipients. He encouraged all South Sudanese citizens to obtain national identity cards, describing them as essential legal documents. He further said that new applications submitted through the office are expected to be processed within approximately two weeks, provided applicants meet the required procedures and documentation.

The acting director of the Directorate in Yei, Philip Akuai, confirmed the office had received more than 4,000 national identity cards and had begun distributing them to applicants across the Greater Yei region. He said a South Sudanese passport costs US$100, while the processing fee for a national identity card is more than 50,000 South Sudanese Pounds (SSP), in accordance with applicable government charges.

Eluzai, an applicant, said she had waited many years to obtain a national identity card and described receiving the document as an important milestone that would enable her to access government and other public services. The distribution exercise is expected to continue in the coming days.
