# Mauritania — 5G provisional licence winners named (ARE)

Mauritania advanced its 5G rollout after the Regulatory Authority (**ARE**) designated provisional successful bidders for licences to build and operate fifth-generation (5G) mobile networks nationwide.

The licensing process began with a call for tenders on **2 December 2025**, closing **30 March 2026**. Eight companies bought the tender documents but only four submitted bids: **Mauritel, Mattel, Chinguitel, and Rimatel**. All four were declared admissible and technically qualified; financial bids were opened publicly on 7 April 2026.

Disclosed financial offers: Mauritel MRU 305,000,000 + 5% of annual 5G revenue; Rimatel MRU 265,000,000 + 5%; Chinguitel MRU 260,555,555 + 5%; Mattel MRU 252,600,000 + 5%. As all bids exceeded the reserve price, the ARE declared **all four provisional winners**. Operators were instructed to sign their specifications within 30 days from 9 April 2026 and complete payment of fixed obligations within 15 days after signing.

*(Note: introduces a fourth operator, **Rimatel**, alongside the three incumbents Mauritel/Mattel/Chinguitel.)*
