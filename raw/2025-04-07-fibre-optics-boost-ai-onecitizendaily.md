# Gov't targets fibre optics to boost AI development

*One Citizen Daily, 7 April 2025*

The government of South Sudan is poised to bolster its digital infrastructure, with plans to initiate the laying of its first-ever fibre optic network by September 2025. The project, revealed by Telecommunication Undersecretary Lado Wani Kenyi at the conclusion of the Global AI Summit on Africa in Kigali, Rwanda, is a step towards the nation's digital transformation and integration into the global AI economy.

Kenyi announced the Ministry has made substantial progress in procurement, having technically evaluated three companies for feasibility studies, commercial transaction modeling, and monitoring and evaluation. The government is awaiting a "no objection" from the World Bank, the potential funder. "Hopefully, by August or September this year, 2025, we will be able to put the tender out for the big company that is going to lay the fiber down," Kenyi stated.

Chol Joseph Mayen, Director for Research and Planning at the NCA, stressed the need for strong regulations and legal frameworks for responsible AI use, and the importance of education and skills development. The government is also focusing on building data centers to localize data. Abraham Kuot, founder and CEO of JubaPay, highlighted the potential of fintech, noting 70% of the population remains unbanked. The World Bank indicated readiness to support South Sudan through regional initiatives with a $3 million portfolio for the next five years.
