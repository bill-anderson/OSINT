A partnership between the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC) and Mastercard targets Nigeria's informal sector (65% of employment, 58% of GDP). A key goal is the NDPC's target to train and certify 250,000 Data Protection Officers (DPOs) annually. The NDPC said the data protection industry created 23,000 jobs in 2024, a 127% increase from 2023. Certified DPOs will expand compliance awareness among small and informal businesses and conduct data protection impact assessments. National Commissioner Dr Vincent Olatunji stressed urgent upskilling. The NDPC's stricter enforcement of the data protection act in 2024 included issuing N400 million (~$250,000) in fines. Pilot programmes will address unauthorised mobile money deductions and merchant data leaks.
