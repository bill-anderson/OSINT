# Guinea Digitizes Public Procurement with Launch of TELEMO Platform

Guinea has taken a major step toward modernizing its public sector operations with the launch of TELEMO, a national digital procurement platform aimed at transforming how government contracts are managed and awarded. Introduced in Conakry on May 22, the platform marks a shift away from paper-based procurement systems toward a fully digital framework. The initiative was developed in collaboration with Rwanda, widely recognized for its advances in digital governance.

TELEMO centralizes the full procurement cycle within a single online platform, allowing institutions to handle procurement planning, tender announcements, bid submissions, evaluations, contract awards, and document archiving electronically. Public procurement plays a crucial role in the national economy, representing between 11% and 15% of Guinea's GDP.

The government also sees the platform as a way to improve access to public contracts for small and medium-sized enterprises. Both local and international companies will be able to access procurement processes through the online system. Beyond digitizing procedures, Guinea aims to use TELEMO to strengthen governance standards and align its procurement practices with performance and transparency standards.

*(Note: TELEMO was first unveiled as a Guinea–Rwanda e-procurement platform at the Transform Africa Summit (Conakry, Nov 2025); this reports its national launch/rollout on 22 May 2026.)*
