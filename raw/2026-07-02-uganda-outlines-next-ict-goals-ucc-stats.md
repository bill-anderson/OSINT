Uganda's next ICT sector growth phase will require greater investment in digital skills, local content, innovation, affordability and cybersecurity, according to Fred Otunnu, director of corporate affairs at the Uganda Communications Commission (UCC), during a familiarisation visit attended by Ministry of ICT representatives.

Otunnu said national statistics show active SIM card subscriptions have reached approximately 47 million, while smartphone usage climbed from 18 million to 20 million devices. Internet subscriptions and data consumption maintain an upward trajectory as Ugandans pivot to online education, e-commerce, digital platforms and e-government; mobile money usage rose an estimated 9%, while internet-service costs continue to decline. ICT minister Justine Kasule Lumumba urged the UCC to tackle service quality, fair billing, internet routing and content regulation, and called for closer collaboration to accelerate digital inclusion and secure a resilient digital economy.
