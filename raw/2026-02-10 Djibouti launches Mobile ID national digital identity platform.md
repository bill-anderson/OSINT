# Djibouti launches Mobile ID national digital identity platform

President **Ismail Omar Guelleh** officially launched the **Mobile ID** platform on **Monday 9 February 2026**, at the closing session of the **National Digital Identity Forum** at the People's Palace under presidential patronage — organised by the Ministry of the Interior with the Ministry of Digital Economy and Innovation (Minister **Mariam Hamadou Ali**). Mobile ID unifies public digital services around a **single citizen digital identity**, enabling electronic authentication via smartphone or a unique digital identifier.

**Priority use cases:** civil-registry services, tax procedures, bank-account opening, and electronic signature of official documents. The government plans to use Mobile ID as a **data-interoperability tool** across government databases — over time linking driving licences and selected health records to the single identifier (the interior minister cited emergency doctors accessing patient data via biometric identification).

**Foundations.** Officials framed Mobile ID as building on the comprehensive **[[djibouti-digital-code]]** (legal framework for digital services, electronic signature and secure transactions), a **sovereign national cloud**, and enhanced cybersecurity (the planned **National Cybersecurity Authority** and national strategy). It is cast as a cornerstone of **[[vision-djibouti-2035]]** and the five-year **"Djibouti Digital Foundation"** programme, not a standalone project. The Mobile ID can be stored in the national digital wallet (**Smart Wallet** — see [[smart-wallet-djibouti]]), for which Djibouti signed a 2025 MoU with **[[visa]]** and **[[tech5]]**.

**Data sovereignty pledge.** The **Interior Ministry will store citizens' biometric data exclusively**; other institutions may access it only via a secure encrypted server-to-server request system. Guelleh warned of "threats of identity theft, digital cloning, hacking, espionage, or foreign interference."

Connectivity context (DataReportal, end-2025): **616,000** mobile connections (51.9%) and **772,000** internet users (65%).
