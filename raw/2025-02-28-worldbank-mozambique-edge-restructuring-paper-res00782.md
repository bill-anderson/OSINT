    I. PROJECT STATUS AND RATIONALE FOR RESTRUCTURING

       1. This restructuring paper seeks management approval to cancel unused funds following the closing of the
       contingent emergency response component (CERC) of the Digital Governance and Economy (EDGE) project. EDGE is
       a 5-year, US$150 million project with an original project development objective (PDO) “to increase access to legal
       identification, digital public services, and digital business opportunities”. The project included five components: (1)
       building blocks: analog reforms, connectivity, and cybersecurity (US$45 million); (2) enablers: identification systems
       and digital services (US$70 million); (3) levers: Govtech Business Ecosystem (US$30 million); (4) project management;
       and (5) CERC. Following CERC activation, Component 3 was dropped and the PDO was revised to “increase access to
       legal identification and public services leveraging digital technologies”. This is the third restructuring. The first
       restructuring added the Ministry of Justice (MoJ) as an implementing agency for activities related to civil and identity
       registration1. The second restructuring reallocated funds to the CERC, cancelled one component, revised the PDO and
       the results framework to align it with the revised activities.2
       2. The CERC activation reallocated US$50 million from the other components and added implementing agencies
       responsible for activities being supported. The CERC activities covered four sectors. Following the expiration of the
       CERC, the status of the allocated and disbursed amounts for the activities supported is as follows:
           • Agriculture - Purchase and distribution of seeds kits to affected farmers, implemented by the National
               Sustainable Development Fund (FNDS) (Allocated: US$ 19.0 million, Disbursed: US$17.8 million)
           • Energy - Repair and reposition of powerline transport network and equipment, implemented by Mozambique
               Electricity (EDM); and repair and reposition of powerline transport network and equipment, implemented by
               the National Energy Fund (FUNAE) (Allocated: US$ 17.8 million, Disbursed: US$ 4.0 million)
           • Health - Provision of temporary campaign hospitals, medicines and medical equipment, assessment of
               damages, and preparation of biddings documents for infrastructure rehabilitation, implemented by the
               Ministry of Health (MISAU) (Allocated: US$ 11.6 million, Disbursed: US$ 7.4 million)
           • Education - Emergency replacement of roofs of targeted education infrastructure, implemented by the
               Ministry of Education and Human Development (MINEDH) (Allocated: US$ 2.8 million, Disbursed US$2.8
               million).
       3. Organizational Changes. The new government is reconfiguring the structure of ministries and organizations
       tasked with digital reform. The Ministry of Science, Technology and Higher Education (MCTES), the original
       counterpart ministry of the project, has been disbanded and its digital regulation and delivery role has been allocated
       to the new Ministry of Digital and Communications. Additional planned organizational changes are likely to affect the
       National Institute for Electronic Governance (INAGE), currently an implementing agency of the project, as well as the
       National Institute for Information Technology and Communications (INTIC), another implementing entity of the
       project.
       4. Current Project Status. Project management has been a recurrent challenge though the previous restructurings
       have helped to address some issues. As of February 2025, progress toward achievement of PDO and Overall
       Implementation Progress are rated Moderately Unsatisfactory and will be reassessed in April 2025. Total
       disbursement is US$ 66 million, 47 percent of the financing.
       5. Rationale for the Current Restructuring. The cancellation responds to the Government of Mozambique’s (GoM)
       request to make unused CERC funds available for Additional Financing to the District and Community Health Services
       Revitalization Operation (P179913). It also expands the legal definitions of the MCTES, INAGE and INITIC to adequately
       cover the ongoing or potential institutional changes.

  II. DESCRIPTION OF PROPOSED CHANGES

                  6. The restructuring includes the following changes: (a) partial cancellation; (b) revisions to disbursement estimates;
                  and (b) update of the definition of MCTES, INITIC, and INAGE to account for the changes in the counterpart ministry
                  and the structure of INAGE and INITIC.
                    i.      Partial Cancellation: Unused CERC funds in the amount of USD 4.2 million equivalent are being cancelled from
                            Category 2.
                    ii.     Disbursement estimates are consequently revised.
                   iii.     The definitions listed in the Appendix to the Financing Agreement are being deleted and replaced with the
                            following:
                                 o “INAGE” means Instituto Nacional de Governo Electrônico, the Recipient’s National eGovernment
                                    Institute, or any successor thereto.”
                                 o “INTIC” means Instituto Nacional de Tecnologias de Informação e Comunicação, the Recipient´s
                                    National ICT regulator, or any successor thereto.”
                                 o “MCTES” means Ministério da Ciência, Tecnologia e Ensino Superior, the Recipient’s Ministry of
                                    Science, Technology, and Higher Education or any successor thereto.”
