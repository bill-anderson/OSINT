Congo began building a **national data centre** in the Bacongo district of **Brazzaville** — a three-storey facility to store and process national data, funded at **$72.8 million** (**$57m AfDB + $15.8m government** — i.e. ~€66.55m) and including a **600-km fibre backbone** linking Congo to **Cameroon and CAR** under the Central African Backbone (CAB) project.

Framed as the **first sovereign data centre in Central Africa**, it aims to host all public data domestically — "eliminating reliance on servers in Europe" — and offer storage/backup to telcos, banks and insurers. Originally expected operational by December 2024, it had reached **75% completion by March 2025** (AfDB regional DG Solomane Koné and Minister Léon Juste Ibombo site visit), with inauguration then anticipated August–November 2025.
