Nearly a year after being positioned as a linchpin of state modernization, Liberia's **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** lies dormant, its operations stalled by a contract dispute and governance impasse. The biometric ID program — mandated by **Executive Order No. 147** in 2025 and designed to underpin SIM registration, bank-account access, social-service enrollment and economic participation — stalled first with operational challenges (long queues, public frustration) and now with a full shutdown tied to a procurement controversy.

The editorial argues the root is not a lack of vision but a failure to uphold transparent, competitive procurement: the NIR sought to award a major biometric contract through **restricted bidding to a foreign firm**, rejected by the PPCC. Millions are cut off from a system they were told would unlock essential services. Civil society, lawmakers and local businesses have raised concerns — from calls for free issuance given entrenched poverty, to warnings that foreign-dominated contracting risks **technological dependency** at the expense of local firms. The paper calls for enforcing procurement law, rebooting the NIR's operational plan with clear timelines and public communication, and engaging stakeholders to prioritise inclusion, accountability and sustainable technology transfer.
