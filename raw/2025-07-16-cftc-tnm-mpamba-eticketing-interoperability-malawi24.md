# CFTC orders TNM Mpamba to open e-ticketing platform to other payment providers

The Competition and Fair Trading Commission (CFTC) has directed **TNM Mpamba Limited** to revise its exclusive e-ticketing arrangement for Super League football matches, finding it limits consumer choice and stifles competition in the mobile-payments market. CFTC CEO Lloyds Vincent Nkhoma (Blantyre) said requiring fans to use only the Mpamba platform and debit card would effectively block other providers such as **Airtel Mobile Commerce Limited**, banks and non-bank financial institutions.

The Commission found the arrangement inconsistent with Malawi's **Payment Systems (Interoperability of Retail Payment Systems) Directive of 2017** and the **Payment Systems (E-Money) Regulations of 2018**, both of which promote interoperability. It ordered TNM Mpamba to run e-ticketing and physical ticketing concurrently for a six-month transition and to **open the e-ticketing platform to other payment service providers within one year** to ensure interoperability, plus awareness campaigns and verifiable progress reports every 90 days for two years. A concrete interoperability-enforcement action against a mobile-money duopoly.
