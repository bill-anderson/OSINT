# Djibouti Telecom extends D-Money mobile payments to ports

State operator **[[djibouti-telecom]]** extended its **D-Money** mobile-payment service to **port workers** — officially launched by Prime Minister **Abdoulkader Kamil Mohamed on 11 December**. D-Money (originally launched in **2020** as a mobile-phone payment service: deposit/withdrawal, P2P transfer, airtime top-up, bill/merchant payment and salary payment) has been extended to serve small, medium and large e-commerce companies and public institutions, and to facilitate daily payments to port workers. Djibouti Telecom expected the move to raise transactions by ~150%.

Per **Djibouti Port Community Systems (DPCS)**, port-community payment transactions run at **2–3 billion Djiboutian francs per month (≈US$11.2m–16.9m)**, ~11% already paid online via DPCS integration with banks (CacBank, BCIMR, EximBank, SalaamBank). Ports are central to Djibouti's economy as the principal maritime gateway for Ethiopia.
