(Translated from French; key terms kept.)

The government has launched the process of modernising urban addressing (adressage urbain), starting with the Autonomous District of Greater Lome (District Autonome du Grand Lome, DAGL). The reform aims to equip the capital and its surroundings with a reliable, digital address system adapted to current urban realities.

The new arrangement rests on the creation of databases integrating coded and named roads (voies codees et nommees), plus precise address points according to the metric system, each associated with an entry number. On the ground, the modernisation will translate into the installation of signage plaques indicating the entry number and the code or name of the road, and the identification and certification of "doors" (portes) integrated into the database.

For DAGL officials, the objective is twofold: to better organise urban space and improve city management, while valuing local history and asserting the ambition of a modern Togo. "The address is a central element of domiciliation and an essential tool for access to public and private services," said DAGL Secretary-General Tagba Atafeyinam Tchalim.

The process was preceded by several technical works, notably the updating of the geographic address database of the 13 communes.
