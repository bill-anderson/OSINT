With the enactment of the Personal Data Protection Act, Chapter 44, Revised Edition 2023 (the PDP Act) and the Personal Data Protection (Collection and Processing) Regulations, Government Notice Number 449C of 2023, entities that collect and/or process personal data in Tanzania are subject to clearer, more stringent obligations in the event of a personal data breach. A key obligation is to assess and, where applicable, notify the Personal Data Protection Commission (the Commission) of a breach without undue delay.

**What constitutes a notifiable breach.** Section 27(5) of the PDP Act requires a data controller to notify the Commission, without undue delay, of any security breach affecting personal data processed by or on behalf of the controller. The Act does not define "security breach", but it is generally read to mean any incident resulting in unauthorised access to, disclosure of, loss of, or compromise of personal data, whether accidental or intentional. Unlike the EU GDPR — where notification is required only if the breach is likely to risk data subjects' rights and freedoms — section 27(5) does not condition notification on harm or risk: a breach becomes notifiable once a security incident affecting personal data has occurred. The Act requires notification "without undue delay" but does not define the term; comparable regimes in neighbouring jurisdictions interpret it as roughly 48 to 72 hours.

**Who must notify.** The data controller is primarily responsible. Data processors must promptly inform the controller. Where one entity is both controller and processor, it bears full responsibility for detecting, managing and reporting the breach directly to the Commission. The PDP Act imposes no obligation to notify data subjects, though as good practice affected subjects should be informed where harm is likely.

**Contents of a notification.** The Commission provides a prescribed format. A complete notification includes: details of the controller and/or processor; a summary of the breach; assessment of the breach; notifications and communications; root-cause analysis; technical and organisational measures; identified areas for improvement; supporting documentation; and a declaration of the controller or processor.

**Practical considerations.** Prompt assessment and reporting; a dedicated breach-response team; comprehensive record-keeping; communication with affected subjects where necessary; review and reinforcement of security measures; root-cause investigation and corrective actions; and applying lessons learned to update policies and incident-response plans.

**Penalties.** Non-compliance carries penalties under regulation 63 of the Collection and Processing Regulations: a fine from TZS 100,000 (approx. USD 41) up to TZS 5,000,000 (approx. USD 2,030), or imprisonment for up to five years, or both.
