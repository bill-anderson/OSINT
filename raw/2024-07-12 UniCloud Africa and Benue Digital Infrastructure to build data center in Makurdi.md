Cloud computing firm UniCloud Africa and state-run Benue Digital Infrastructure Company (BDIC) will build a data center in Makurdi, the Benue state capital, under a Memorandum of Understanding, with UniCloud providing the cloud infrastructure. Pan-African operator Africa Data Centres will build a complementary disaster recovery site in Abuja. The project is estimated at 10.8 billion naira ($6.6 million) and was to be completed in six months.

Sam Ode, deputy governor and BDIC chairman, said the project takes "a bold step towards transforming Benue into a digital hub." Other partners are Cedarview Communication (network infrastructure) and SIT Consulting (software). Founded in 2023, UniCloud Africa operated in Nigeria, South Africa and Ghana, with projects going live in Kenya, Uganda, DRC and Tanzania. Nigeria's data center markets are in Lagos and Abuja; earlier that month MTN Nigeria announced plans for a large Lagos data center.
