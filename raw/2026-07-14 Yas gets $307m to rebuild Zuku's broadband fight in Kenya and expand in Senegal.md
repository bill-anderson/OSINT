**[[yas]]** (pan-African telecoms arm of **[[axian-telecom]]**) secured up to **€270m (~US$307m)** in
long-term **[[ebrd]]** financing for digital-infrastructure expansion in **Kenya and Senegal** — the deal
already held as **[[ebrd-yas-network-loan]]**. This account adds detail:

- **Kenya / Zuku:** the capital modernises the **Zuku** fibre network — Yas acquired **Wananchi Group** (Zuku's
  parent) in **2025**, inheriting a once-leading Nairobi broadband brand that had lost ground to Safaricom and
  others. Fresh capex is a bid to make Zuku competitive again.
- **Senegal:** funds **4G, 5G, core and fibre** deployment.
- **EBRD firsts:** the bank's **first project in Senegal**, its **first local-currency financing in sub-Saharan
  Africa**, and its **first A/B loan** in the region; a committed facility plus an **uncommitted facility** for
  future acquisitions and eligible investment. Local-currency financing reduces the FX mismatch on
  hard-currency-priced equipment/loans against local-currency telecom revenue.
