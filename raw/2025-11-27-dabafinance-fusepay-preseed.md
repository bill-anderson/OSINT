# Fusepay Launches Payments Platform in Seychelles After $350K Pre-Seed | 27 November 2025
Daba Finance

- Fusepay, a Seychelles-founded fintech focused on frontier markets, has launched a digital payments platform to help businesses move away from cheque-based and manual processes.
- The rollout follows a $350,000 pre-seed round (August) backed by Hustle Fund, Everywhere Ventures, First Check Ventures, Startup Istanbul, and angel investor Ryan Nesbitt.
- The startup plans to expand to Mauritius and the Maldives within 18 months and is targeting more than 20 frontier markets.

With a Payment Service Provider licence and Central Bank of Seychelles approval, Fusepay offers virtual business accounts, instant transfers, post-dated digital payments to replace cheques, and automated reconciliation. Its API, Fusepay Bridge, enables billers such as utilities and telecoms to collect payments electronically and issue instant receipts. Founded in 2024 by Vidhyasahar Thiyagarajan and Francesco Rocchi; core product FuseCheq earns a 0.6% fee on each digital cheque replacement processed.
