## Synthesis

The snapshot frames South Africa as Africa’s largest economy and a leading digital hub — top of the
continent on several indices — while stressing that policy strength is undercut by structural
constraints. It walks the policy architecture (PC4IR/4IR agenda; National Digital and Future Skills
Strategy 2021–2025) and the legal backbone: **POPIA** as principal data-protection law, its **17 April
2025 amendment** alongside the Promotion of Access to Information Act (PAIA), the **Cybercrimes Act
2020**, and multi-regulator oversight (Information Regulator, ICASA, Competition Commission). It logs
recent moves — the **National AI Policy Framework** (public comment Oct 2024, no AI Act yet), the
Cabinet-approved **Digital Transformation of Government Roadmap** (Mar 2025, two phases to 2030),
**accession to the Digital Public Goods Alliance** (Sept 2025), and major infrastructure investment
(Visa, Microsoft). It situates SA regionally via the Malabo Convention (signatory) and GDPR alignment.

## Key facts (held on source; see provenance note)

- **POPIA + PAIA amended 17 Apr 2025**; Cybercrimes Act 2020; Malabo Convention status *signatory*.
- **National AI Policy Framework** — public comment Oct 2024; **Digital Transformation of Government
  Roadmap** Cabinet-approved Mar 2025 (Phase 1 to Feb 2027, Phase 2 to Feb 2030).
- **Joined the DPGA — Sept 2025.**
- **Visa** first Africa data centre, Johannesburg, Jul 2025 (~R1bn/$57m); **Microsoft** additional
  R5.4bn/$296m AI-infrastructure + skills (50,000 certifications).
- Internet penetration 74.7% (2024); mobile data ~$1.81/GB (up to $5.29/GB); UN EGDI **40th globally,
  2024** (highest in Africa); **CSEA Digital Preparedness Index 3rd of 43** (score 0.69, behind
  Mauritius and Seychelles).
- Rural Eastern Cape: 52.3% lacked internet, 38.5% cited affordability, 66.2% insufficient digital
  skills.
