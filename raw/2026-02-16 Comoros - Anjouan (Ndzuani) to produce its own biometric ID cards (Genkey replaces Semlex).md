Comoros' Interior Minister **Mohamed Ahamada Assoumani** announced (12 Feb 2026) imminent **local printing of biometric ID cards on Ndzuani (Anjouan)** — until now, residents had to travel to **Moroni** (the capital) to obtain a national identity card or passport, incurring cost and delay.

From **April 2026**, Dutch firm **[[genkey]] replaces Semlex** in producing Comoros' biometric documents, with additional equipment and refurbished premises. National-ID-card printing launches first on Ndzuani, aiming to cut travel to the capital and ensure uninterrupted service — a decentralisation of ID issuance paired with a vendor switch.
