The Malawi Government, through the Public Private Partnership Commission (PPPC) in partnership with the Department of e-Government (Ministry of Information and Digitalization) under the Digital Malawi Project (DIGMAP), is constructing a Government Data Centre in Lilongwe with World Bank funding, complementing the existing data centre in Blantyre. Lilongwe will serve as the Primary Data Centre and Blantyre as the Secondary/Backup.

PPPC CEO Patrick Kabambe framed the facility as consolidating siloed government systems "under one roof" and enabling interoperability between government and third-party/private systems in support of the Malawi 2063 digitalization agenda. DIGMAP technical project manager Paul Kanthambi described a Hyper-converged Infrastructure (HCI) design for high availability, security, scalability and disaster recovery. At the time of writing the build stood at 51 percent completion, with completion expected by May 2024.
