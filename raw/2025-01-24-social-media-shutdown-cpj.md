# South Sudan blocks social media access amid unrest

*Committee to Protect Journalists, 24 January 2025 (Nairobi)*

The Committee to Protect Journalists called on South Sudanese authorities to reverse a social media ban and ensure open, reliable internet access. On January 22, South Sudan's telecommunications regulator, the National Communication Authority, directed all internet service providers to "block access to all social media accounts" for a "minimum of 30 days" and a "maximum of 90 days," according to a copy of the authority's letter reviewed by CPJ.

On the evening of January 22, at least two telecom providers — Zain South Sudan and MTN South Sudan — published notices warning users that TikTok and Facebook would no longer be accessible. On January 23, journalists and human rights defenders told CPJ that Facebook and TikTok were inaccessible without a VPN. "We journalists are using VPNs to work," one said.

In a press briefing on January 23, the Authority's director general Napoleon Adok Gai said there was a possibility the ban would be lifted within 72 hours. (The block, justified as preventing the spread of graphic content related to violence in neighbouring Sudan, was lifted on 27 January 2025.)
