Since June 2024, citizens in Gabon have been able to obtain a new-generation national ID and a digital ID number — something that had not happened for at least 10 years. The physical card and digital ID management software/hardware are supplied by French identity services company **IN Groupe** (EVP Yann Haguet); the project was launched on 25 June.

Gabon is leveraging the digital ID component to build a digital public infrastructure (DPI) ecosystem, instrumental to the "Digital Gabon" transformation project for which the World Bank approved $68 million. Activities include building a new data center, digital applications, network infrastructure rehabilitation and expansion, strengthening data protection and cybersecurity, and enhancing inclusive access to vital services for vulnerable groups. It aligns with the 2025 Strategic Plan for an Emerging Gabon.

Gabon also signed an MoU with Huawei for a smart cities project and other digital services; Gabon Media Time reports Huawei pledged to spend at least $234 million. Jaume Dubois, CEO of digital-transformation consultancy ID30 and senior ID/DPI expert involved in the project, said Gabon's program rests on three components: creating a favourable environment for fast digital transformation, modernizing legal identity systems, and digitally transforming public administration.
