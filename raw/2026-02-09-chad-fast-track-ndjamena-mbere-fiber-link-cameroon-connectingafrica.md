# Chad tells operators to fast-track fiber link to Cameroon

*By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa — February 9, 2026*

The Chadian government has urged operators Sotel and Airtel to restart discussions to enable the N'Djamena-Mberé fiber optic route connecting Chad to Cameroon.

The Chadian government has called on telecom operators Sotel and Airtel to fast-track the agreement of the N'Djamena-Mberé fiber-optic route linking Chad to Cameroon.

The N'Djamena-Mberé fiber-optic route is strategic because it connects Chad's capital N'Djamena to the Cameroonian border, currently the only entry point for international capacity from submarine cables.

However, for the fiber route to be adopted, both parties must revise a memorandum of understanding (MoU) signed back in April 2025.

The MoU was suspended by Chad's Regulatory Authority for Electronic Communications and Post (ARCEP) due to certain clauses deemed contrary to the principles of national digital sovereignty.

Chad's Minister of Posts and Digital Economy, Dr. Boukar Michel, subsequently issued a series of observations and guidelines.

Michel invited Sotel to engage in discussions with Airtel to correct the problematic clauses, adopt amendments that align with Chad's strategic interests, and proceed with their signing to enable the effective start of work on the fiber route.

"This is part of a constant desire to strengthen the quality of the national telecommunications network, to improve the continuity and performance of services, while guaranteeing respect for the imperatives of digital sovereignty, security and public interest," the Ministry said in a Facebook post.

In August 2025, Michel also told telecom operators Airtel and Moov Africa to connect to the national fiber-optic network to improve quality of service (QoS), or face potential sanctions.

## Chad working towards connecting its citizens

The country has been working towards ensuring that it improves its QoS and connects underserved communities.

The regulator has even gone to lengths of fining telcos who have not delivered proper QoS.

In August 2023, Chad's regulator ARCEP slapped Airtel Chad with a fine of 5 billion Central Africa CFA francs (US$8.3 million at the time) for deteriorating network quality.

Connecting to the N'Djamena-Mberé fiber optic route is intended to boost connectivity in the Central African country which has an Internet penetration rate of just 13.2%, according to statistics from data platform DataReportal which found that Chad had only 2.79 million Internet users at the end of 2025.

Kepios analysis indicates that the number of Internet users in Chad increased by 83,000, or 3.1%, between October 2024 and October 2025.
