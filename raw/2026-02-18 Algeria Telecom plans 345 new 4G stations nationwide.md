# Algeria Telecom plans 345 new 4G stations nationwide

State-owned **[[algerie-telecom|Algeria Telecom]]** announced on **15 February 2026** a plan to add **345 4G base stations across 44 provinces** (195 in a first phase through March; 150+ in a second), to extend coverage to remote areas and improve speeds. The company's own name for the scheme is the **Coverage Network Enhancement Program**, and the stations are 4G-LTE (eNodeB) units, prioritised to areas of high connectivity demand. It follows a 12 Feb meeting where Post & Telecoms Minister **Sid Ali Zerrouki** pressed operators (Djezzy, Ooredoo, and Algeria Telecom's mobile arm **Mobilis**) on rural and road coverage, demanding at least basic/emergency service before June and full coverage by year-end.

A parallel programme is connecting villages of 500–2,000 people (first phase 1,400 localities; **4,500 more by 2027**), with calls to use the **Alcomsat-1** satellite for remote access. Per ITU, 3G/4G reached **99.1%** population coverage (2024); mobile penetration **92.7%**, internet penetration **~77.4%** (2024); World Bank smartphone ownership **84.34%** (15+).
