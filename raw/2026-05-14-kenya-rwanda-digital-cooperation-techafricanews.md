Kenya and Rwanda Strengthen Digital Economy Cooperation in AI and Infrastructure Push

May 14, 2026 1 min read Author: Kay-Lyne Wolfenden

The discussions focused on expanding collaboration in key areas including artificial intelligence (AI), data centres, digital infrastructure, innovation ecosystems, and talent development across both nations.

William Kabogo Gitau has held bilateral talks with Rwanda’s ICT and Innovation Minister, Paula Ingabire, aimed at strengthening cooperation between the two countries in the digital economy.

The discussions focused on expanding collaboration in key areas including artificial intelligence (AI), data centres, digital infrastructure, innovation ecosystems, and talent development across both nations.

Both sides agreed on the need to accelerate the implementation of existing agreements and frameworks while improving coordination through regional platforms such as Connected Africa and Smart Africa.

The engagement also highlighted the shared ambition of Kenya and Rwanda to strengthen regional integration and build a more competitive and resilient digital economy across the continent.

Kenya reaffirmed its commitment to working closely with Rwanda and other African partners to advance innovation-driven growth and deepen cooperation in emerging technologies that support long-term economic transformation.
