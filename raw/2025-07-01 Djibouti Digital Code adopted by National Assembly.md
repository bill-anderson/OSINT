# Djibouti Digital Code adopted by the National Assembly

On **30 June 2025**, the National Assembly of the Republic of Djibouti passed a law approving the country's **first-ever Digital Code**, developed over more than two years through a participatory process by the Ministry of Digital Economy and Innovation. The Code — described as comprising **eight volumes and nearly 800 articles** — consolidates all provisions applicable to the digital sector into a single ordered instrument to reinforce legal certainty and public trust in ICT services. It sits within Djibouti's digital-transformation road map under **[[vision-djibouti-2035]]**.

**Coverage:** data protection, cybersecurity and cybercrime, and e-commerce, with provisions on cryptology, digital consumption and innovative services. It regulates internet operators (online service providers, publishers, hosting) and providers of cryptology services; identifies punishable online offences (breaches of information systems and networks); and provides for a **cybersecurity body** responsible for defence of cyberspace and critical infrastructure, technical standards and cryptology resources (see [[national-cybersecurity-authority-djibouti]]). Its embedded data-protection regime establishes the **[[cndp-djibouti]]** (see [[2025-11-05 Review of Djibouti Data Protection Law]]).

The Code is expected to accelerate public digital projects (electronic identity, e-government) and structure relations between the State, tech companies and users, supporting Djibouti's goal of becoming a regional digital hub. The law comprises a legislative section (adopted 30 June 2025), still to be promulgated by the President and published in the *Journal Officiel*, and to be supplemented by regulatory decrees. Its success is framed as hinging on operationalisation — competent authorities and implementation decrees.

*The Code was drafted by **Jones Day**, which also drafted **Benin's** pioneering 2017 Digital Code; commentators place Djibouti among the more advanced African states in digital regulation.
