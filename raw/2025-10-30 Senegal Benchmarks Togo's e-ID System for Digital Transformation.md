(Rendered from French/English wire copy.)

Senegal's Ministry of Communication and Digital Economy sent a delegation to Togo for a benchmarking mission on the e-ID (digital identity) project. The mission aims to develop a robust digital identity infrastructure in Senegal, drawing inspiration from the Togolese approach. The visit aligns with the World Bank-supported WURI program ("Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion in West Africa").

The Senegalese Ministry disclosed on Monday, October 27, 2025, that the delegation visited Togo for the benchmarking mission. Yasmine Mama, Datacenter Network Specialist at Togo's National Identification Agency (ANID), stated: "We showed the technical architectures, infrastructures, and logic deployed within the context of the e-ID project. We discussed the technical solution, MOSIP [Modular Open Source Identity Platform], all its components, and how the country integrated it into Togo."

Through this cooperation, Dakar expects to accelerate implementation of its own e-ID system and strengthen digital trust. With backing from various technical and financial partners, Senegal intends to establish a secure, interoperable identification solution allowing every citizen easier access to online public and private services (education, healthcare, financial services, social programs).
