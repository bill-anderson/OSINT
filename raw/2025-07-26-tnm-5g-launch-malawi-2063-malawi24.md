Chiyamwaka hails 5G rollout as key driver of Malawi 2063 vision

The Principal Secretary in the Ministry of Information and Digitalisation, Baldwin Chiyamwaka, has described Telekom Networks Malawi’s (TNM) launch of its 5G network as a strategic leap forward in achieving the country’s long-term development goals under the Malawi 2063 vision.

During the launch ceremony at the Bingu International Convention Centre in Lilongwe, Chiyamwaka emphasised that expanding digital infrastructure is central to transforming Malawi into a self-reliant, industrialised, and technology-driven nation.

Chiyamwaka highlighted that TNM’s 5G deployment supports key national policy objectives, including the National Decentralisation Policy, and enables innovation across critical sectors. “This development has wide-ranging implications — from improving agricultural technology and expanding access to global medical resources, to enabling virtual education and fostering the development of smart cities,” he said.

The Principal Secretary noted that high-speed connectivity is not a luxury, but a necessity for Malawi’s socio-economic transformation.

He stressed the importance of extending 5G access to cities such as Blantyre, Mzuzu, Zomba, and Liwonde as part of the Malawi Implementation Plan I (MIP-1), the first 10-year phase of the country’s 2063 agenda. “Digitalisation is a critical enabler for our national vision. The benefits of 5G must reach every corner of the country to ensure inclusive development,” he added.

TNM Chief Commercial Officer Amon Jere said the launch marked a new era of local innovation. “What we’re unveiling today is a 5G network built and implemented by some of the brightest Malawian minds at TNM,” Jere said. He praised the dedication of local engineers and credited TNM’s strategic partner, Huawei, for providing technical support throughout the process.

Jere elaborated on the practical impact of the new network, noting that TNM’s 5G service will revolutionise connectivity for both individuals and businesses.

Users can expect faster speeds for video streaming, virtual meetings, online education, and mobile transactions. The upgraded TNM SmartApp and the introduction of 5G eSIM support are part of the operator’s push to provide user-friendly digital services.

Beyond improved communication, the 5G network is expected to fuel innovation in Malawi’s key economic sectors.

From real-time diagnostics in healthcare to smart farming, autonomous mining, and intelligent transport systems, the technology promises to enhance productivity and open new avenues for investment. “This is about more than internet speed — it’s about platforms for innovation and unlocking economic potential,” Jere said.

As TNM continues expanding its 5G footprint beyond Lilongwe, Chiyamwaka urged collaboration between the government, the private sector, and innovators to fully harness its benefits. “We must work together to ensure this digital infrastructure drives transformation where it matters most, in health, education, agriculture, and enterprise,” he concluded.
