# Seychelles Embraces Digital Revolution as Fusepay Ushers Out Paper Cheques | 21 November 2025
By Jennifer Onyeagoro — TechAfrica News

Fusepay, a new fintech platform focused on frontier markets, officially launched in Seychelles, processing its inaugural live business transaction on November 19, 2025. It deploys a digital payments infrastructure in Victoria designed to replace the nation's decades-long reliance on paper cheques — now being phased out by government mandate.

The centerpiece is a system of virtual business accounts enabling structured accounts-payable/receivable management, instant transfers within the Fusepay network, and post-dated digital payments as a modern alternative to cheques. Planned features include inventory and order management. Co-founders Vidhyasahar (CEO, formerly product design at a YC-backed MENA fintech) and Francesco (CTO, secure financial systems) lead a 13-person team drawn from firms including Goldman Sachs and Intel. The launch attracted pre-seed funding from global investors including Everywhere Ventures. Strategy: establish a foundational position in Seychelles before expanding to adjacent under-digitised markets.
