# Bank of Algeria joins the PAPSS network

The **Pan-African Payment and Settlement System ([[papss|PAPSS]])** welcomed the **Bank of Algeria** on **15 August 2025**, making Algeria PAPSS's **18th country of presence**. Deputy Governor **Mohamed Benbahane** framed membership as "an essential lever for strengthening Algeria's role within the African financial ecosystem." The accession coincided with Algeria hosting the **Intra-African Trade Fair 2025 (IATF2025)** in Algiers (4–10 Sep 2025).

PAPSS CEO **Mike Ogbalu III** noted the system has cut intra-African cross-border transaction costs, with **savings of up to 27%** for end users. Since its 2022 West-African debut, PAPSS has expanded across North Africa (Tunisia, Egypt, Morocco, now Algeria) and connects **18 countries, 150+ commercial banks and 14 switches**, with three solutions live: the Instant Payment System (IPS), the African Currency Marketplace (PACM) and the **[[papsscard|PAPSSCARD]]**.
