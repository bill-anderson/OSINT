Sierra Leone-based fintech Open Hub launched a new digital platform, Flot Business, along with an AI-powered chatbot, Flot, aimed at expanding financial inclusion and accelerating the transition toward a cashless economy. The launch event in Freetown brought together banks, mobile money operators (Afrimoney and Orange Money), and development partners.

The platform is designed as a secure, interoperable, cost-effective solution enabling users to send and receive money, pay bills, and interact directly with financial institutions. Company representative Nasif Jaward said the AI chatbot is accessible via WhatsApp and integrates multiple services (send money, purchase airtime, buy products) into a single interface, particularly for underserved communities.

Paul Kanu described Flot Business as allowing businesses to accept payments from multiple channels, including mobile money and international cards (Visa, Mastercard), with instant settlement, likening it to "the Harry Potter of our day" for its speed via QR code transactions. CEO David Kpakima said the platform helps shift the country from cash to a formal digital economy. MD of Sky Bank, Fasasi Charles Adeniye, commended the initiative.

The rollout is supported by the Bank of Sierra Leone's Sandbox initiative. The platform also features a no-code online store builder for entrepreneurs.
