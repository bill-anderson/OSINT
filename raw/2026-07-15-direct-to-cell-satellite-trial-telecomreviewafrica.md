# Madagascar Conducts First Direct-to-Cell Satellite Trial, Advancing Digital Inclusion

Madagascar has become the **third African country to test direct-to-cell satellite connectivity**, following a demonstration led by **Airtel Madagascar and SpaceX** in partnership with the Ministry of Digital Development, Posts and Telecommunications (MNDPT).

The trial, held on **July 11, 2026 in Sadabe**, was attended by Minister **Mahefa Andriamampiadana**, representatives from the regulator **ARTEC**, the National Disaster Risk Management Office (BNGRC), and other stakeholders. The achievement places Madagascar **alongside Kenya and Uganda** as the only countries on the continent to have tested the technology.

Direct-to-cell technology enables standard 4G smartphones to connect directly to satellites when beyond terrestrial mobile networks. Participants completed **Madagascar's first WhatsApp video call via satellite**, plus satellite-enabled messaging, voice and video calls, and access to Facebook and YouTube. The Ministry said the technology could improve connectivity in remote/underserved areas and offer resilient communications during emergencies and natural disasters. The government stressed compliance with legal, security and **digital sovereignty** requirements. Commercial deployment remains subject to ARTEC's regulatory assessment.
