# Source record — Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa (UNDP)

**Primary policy paper.** A UNDP Regional Bureau for Africa (RBA) + Digital, AI and
Innovation (DAI) Hub policy paper making the case for continental, national-scale DPI.
Attached PDF: `2026-07-10 undp-africa-dpi-full_report-digital-version-v2.3_compr2 (2).pdf`. Produced
under Ahunna Eziakonwa (UN ASG / RBA Director) and Robert Opp (UNDP CDO); research support
from Artha Global (funded by CoDevelop), with inputs from CDPI, C4EC, the African
Development Bank (AfDB), GIZ and the [[gates-foundation]]. This is the definitive
continental DPI reference in the wiki; its country evidence is folded into the place hubs
and DPI concept pages rather than re-listed here.

## Core argument

- **DPI as three connected layers.** Core (**digital identity, payments, data exchange**),
  sectoral applications built on the core (tax filing, health information systems, social
  registries), and enabling infrastructure (**connectivity, devices, data centres**). DPI
  is "not merely a technical stack" but a combination of technology, governance, standards,
  legal frameworks, financing and institutional capacity. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **Four interlinked barriers.** **Exclusion** (1 in 5 sub-Saharan adults lacks any legal
  ID), **inefficiency** (siloed paper-based delivery), **fiscal erosion** (unverifiable
  transactions), and **sovereignty** (proprietary tech + fragmented bilateral deals create
  lock-in that erodes strategic autonomy). See [[include.divides]], [[include.access]].
- **Regional integration.** DPI provides the interoperable rails — identity, cross-border
  payments, trusted data exchange — to operationalise the **AfCFTA** ([[afcfta]]) and its
  **US$450bn** opportunity; reinforced by the AfCFTA **Protocol on Digital Trade and
  Intellectual Property** endorsed at the **February 2025 AU Summit**. Anchored to AU
  **Agenda 2063**. See [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]].
- **Financing + "DPI done right."** Governance/coordination shifts remain under-financed
  and fragmented; the paper calls for proactive, principled, **African-led** investment,
  emphasising inclusion, accountability, safeguards and sovereignty. See [[finance.new]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]] (AI has made the cost of fragmented data infrastructure clear).

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: DPI as strategic autonomy — shared public rails vs. proprietary/bilateral
  lock-in; "promote and safeguard sovereignty in a shifting geopolitical landscape."
- `colonialism`: warns Africa's digital foundations could "entrench both new and existing
  patterns of dependency and exclusion" — the recurring externally-financed-sovereignty
  tension ([[zambia--dpi-id]], [[dmap]]).
