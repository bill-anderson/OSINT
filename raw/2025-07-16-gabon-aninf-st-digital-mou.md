# Gabon drives digital sovereignty through ANINF-ST Partnership

Gabon's National Agency for Digital Infrastructures and Frequencies (ANINF) has signed a memorandum of understanding with ST Digital. The agreement establishes a collaborative framework to enhance Gabon's technological autonomy while fostering innovation and cybersecurity.

ANINF Director General, Alberto Wenceslas Mounguengui Moudoki and ST Digital CEO Laïcka Mba formalized the partnership at ANINF headquarters, outlining joint initiatives to modernize Gabon's digital ecosystem. The collaboration will focus on developing secure digital infrastructure, promoting local tech talent, and implementing transparent governance frameworks for emerging technologies.

"This partnership represents our shared commitment to building a digitally sovereign Gabon," stated Moudoki during the signing ceremony. The agreement enables knowledge transfer between ANINF's regulatory expertise and ST Digital's technological capabilities, particularly in cloud computing, data analytics, and smart city solutions.

Key projects include the co-development of cybersecurity protocols for critical digital infrastructure and specialized training programs to build Gabonese technical capacity. The partners will also collaborate on digital identity systems and e-government platforms designed to improve service delivery nationwide.

The ANINF-ST Digital alliance aligns with Gabon's Emerging Digital Economy Strategy 2025, which prioritizes homegrown technological solutions over foreign dependencies. Industry analysts note the partnership's potential to position Gabon as a regional hub for secure digital innovation while creating high-value employment opportunities in the ICT sector.

With implementation beginning immediately, the collaboration marks a decisive step in Gabon's journey toward technological self-reliance and inclusive digital development.
