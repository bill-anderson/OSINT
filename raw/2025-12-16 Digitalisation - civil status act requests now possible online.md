*Official state-portal account of the civil-status rollout announced 15 Dec 2025; complementary to the Togo First report of 2025-12-17, which adds the Energy Ministry procedures and the January-2026 extension.*

(Translated from French; key terms kept.)

In Togo, citizens can now make requests for copies of civil status acts (actes d'etat civil - birth, marriage or death) directly online. The minister in charge of territorial administration and his colleague for digital transformation announced on Monday, 15 December [2025], the rollout of a digital tool accessible on the national public services counter (Guichet National du Service Public).

The system is currently deployed in five pilot communes: Agoe-Nyive 1, Golfe 1, Ave 2, Zio 2 and Lacs 1. It allows users not only to submit a request remotely but also to track the progress of their file in real time. An online chat tool is available on the national portal to assist users and guide them through each step.

The project, which will be progressively extended to other communes, is part of the national strategy for digitalising administrative procedures. It is recalled that the civil status services of the Golfe 3 commune were already available on the national public services platform.
