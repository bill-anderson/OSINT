Lesotho joined the **50-in-5 campaign** — advocacy to support at least 50 countries in building safe, inclusive and interoperable digital public infrastructure (DPI) within five years — becoming the **14th country** to join (the campaign launched November 2023).

Lesotho's Minister of Communications, Science, Technology and Innovation, Nthati Moorosi, said the commitment underscores collective efforts to drive economic growth through ICT, enabling government departments to draw up digital-transformation strategies; UNDP's Jacqueline Olewa noted an Innovation Hub being created with the Ministry of Education at Lerotholi Polytechnic (UNDP + UNICEF). The campaign will enact enabling legislation, coordinate a DPI implementation plan across government, and target women and youth. The article situates the move within UNDP/Vital Strategies advocacy for integrating DPI and **Civil Registration and Vital Statistics (CRVS)**, with **[[mosip]]** highlighted as a cornerstone open-source DPI platform (then in use in 17 countries).
