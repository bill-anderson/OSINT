NIRA says it has started issuing Uganda's new enhanced National ID cards across district offices. Addressing journalists at Police Headquarters in Naguru, Kampala, on October 27, 2025, registrar Claire Ollama said production and distribution are "in high gear" with thousands already printed and dispatched nationwide. "The new enhanced National ID cards are now being issued slowly but steadily across our district offices," she said, adding every district now has cards ready for collection.

She described the card as "a major upgrade... made from a stronger, high-endurance material. The photo is laser-engraved, not printed... Inside the card are hidden marks, a ghost image of your face and date of birth, and a digital signature that protects your data." The enhanced ID features a QR code and barcode enabling banks, telecoms, hospitals and government institutions to instantly verify identity and reduce forgery, plus a tilt-shifting image and raised texture.

NIRA works with district mass enrollment and registration coordination committees (DMERCCs). Renewal is free, but a shillings 50,000 fee applies for replacement of a lost card. Ollama warned against paying brokers or "facilitation fees," noting some areas face temporary form shortages being exploited by illegal sellers. Online pre-registration is available at www.nira.go.ug.
