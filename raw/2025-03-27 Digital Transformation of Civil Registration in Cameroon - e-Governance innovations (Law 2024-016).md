A peer-reviewed study of the e-governance innovations in Cameroon's **2024 civil-registration reform** (Law 2024/016, [[cameroon-civil-registration-law-2024]]): electronic declaration of civil-status acts, a **national database**, and the transition to **electronic certificates** — assessed for governance efficiency, transparency, service accessibility and vital-statistics accounting.

The authors find these can dramatically improve efficiency and access, but warn that successful implementation must overcome **insufficient technological equipment, limited internet access and low digital literacy**, and balance digitalisation with citizens' rights in electronic registration.
