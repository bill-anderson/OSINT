# Algérie Télécom, Sparkle plan subsea cable to link Italy to Africa

Algérie Télécom and Sparkle have signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to develop a new subsea cable linking Italy and Algeria.

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa

July 25, 2025

2 Min Read

Algérie Télécom CEO Adel Bentoumi and Sparkle Executive Director Enrico Bagnasco. (Source: Algérie Télécom)

Algerian operator Algérie Télécom and Italian international service provider Sparkle have signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to develop a new subsea cable linking Italy and Algeria.

According to the telco, the MoU provides for several projects that will benefit Algérie Télécom, including the construction of a new, latest-generation submarine cable linking Algeria to Italy.

"The opening of an Algérie Télécom point of presence in Europe, support and technical assistance to Algérie Télécom for the development of a datacenter meeting international standards, as well as the creation of a learning and training platform in the fields of cloud computing, networks, digital services and cyber security," the telco said in a statement.

Algérie Télécom CEO Adel Bentoumi said the partnership between the two companies confirmed the long-standing relationship between them and reflects the shared commitment to innovation.

"We are confident that this project will play a key role in diversifying our international routes and meeting the growing needs of our customers across the country," Bentoumi said.

Sparkle's executive director, Enrico Bagnasco, said the agreement marked an important step in strengthening digital ties between Europe and North Africa.

"We are proud to contribute to Algeria's digital future by providing modern infrastructure and innovative and secure solutions for fast and resilient international connectivity," he said.

## African subsea cable system growth

There are now a number of cables surrounding the continent and several systems linking Africa to other regions.

Earlier this month, Telecom Egypt, in collaboration with subsea supplier SubCom, successfully completed the dual landings of the SEA-ME-WE-6 subsea cable system on both of Egypt's coasts.

In June 2025, a consortium of four major subsea cable operators signed an agreement to collaborate on the construction of the Asia-Africa-Europe-2 (AAE-2) subsea cable system.

The four operators were PCCW Global, Sparkle, Telecom Egypt and Zain Omantel International (ZOI).

In recent years there has been growth from newer systems like Meta-backed 2Africa and Google-backed Equiano adding to more established systems like the West Africa Cable System (WACS), South Atlantic-3 (Sat-3) and Africa Coast to Europe (ACE).

In May 2024, Google announced it would build a subsea cable called Umoja that will connect Africa to Australia.

Egypt has become a key link point for cables connecting Africa to Europe, the Middle East and Asia.

In November 2024, Telecom Egypt and Alcatel Submarine Networks (ASN) landed the Africa-1 subsea cable system at the Ras Ghareb cable landing station in Egypt.

In the same month, Telecom Egypt and Mobily signed an agreement to land a submarine cable connecting Saudi Arabia and Egypt.

In January 2024, Telecom Egypt and ZOI also partnered to establish what they called a new digital corridor connecting the Mediterranean to the Arabian Sea and Arabian Gulf.
