Burundi's **CENI** extended the collection of voter-registration cards by one day, to **15 May 2025**, citing irregularities at several distribution centres and low turnout ahead of legislative and communal-councillor elections. Spokesperson François Bizimana noted arrangements for security personnel, diaspora (via embassies) and absentees.

The clip also documents **coercion** around registration: during the first phase (early 2025), citizens were reportedly compelled to register to obtain receipts, with access to markets, healthcare and even police services conditioned on proof of registration, and **Imbonerakure** (CNDD-FDD-affiliated youth) checking documents at strategic points. Burundi's Electoral Code (Article 60) prohibits such pressure, but no sanctions had been observed — a governance caveat on how the country's voter-registration data is being produced.
