Analysis, Addis Standard, 28 January 2026. Argues that as Ethiopia implements Digital Ethiopia 2030, the rapid rollout of digital platforms, e-services, fintech and national digital ID projects an image of decisive modernisation ("leapfrogging") - but digitisation can accelerate even as governance quality, development outcomes and public trust stagnate or erode, turning rapid expansion into systemic instability (the "Ethiopian paradox"). Frames digital transformation as socio-technical: a digital state is a set of load-bearing relationships (power/trust, networks/legitimacy, identity/consent, payments/redress, infrastructure/governance capacity). Warns against building "on quicksand" as systems become essential and tightly coupled. Pairs with the Addis Standard "Coercion Over Consent" critique.
