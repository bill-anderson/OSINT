# Senegal digitizes millions of civil records to improve citizen rights and public policy

Senegal had **digitised and indexed over 19 million civil-status records**, Minister Moussa Bala Fofana
announced on **25 June 2024** (5th steering-committee meeting). The work is part of the EU-funded
**[[nekkal-programme|Nékkal programme]]** (launched 2021; 42 months, CFA18bn / €27.4m) to interconnect
civil-status centres and systematically digitise registers, addressing birth traceability and data
reliability. Per UNICEF (2019), 1 in 5 children (20%) was unregistered (3 in 5 in rural areas); records often
carried multiple ID numbers or were lost to file deterioration. A second phase under consideration would let
citizens (home/diaspora) request documents without travelling.

*Historical baseline. The 2024 "19m records" figure differs from [[anec-senegal|ANEC]]'s "15.3m records
digitised" (2026-07) — likely different counting bases; flagged for reconcile.*
