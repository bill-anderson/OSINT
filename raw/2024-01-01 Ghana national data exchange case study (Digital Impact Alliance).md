# Ghana national data exchange case study (DIAL)

DIAL case study on Ghana's national data-exchange journey — part of its research series on integrated national data exchange systems. Analytical through-line: Ghana's **private-sector-led** digital backbone has produced a **decentralized, fragmented** approach to government data.
- Telecom liberalization (1990s) → near-universal mobile coverage, ~70% mobile-internet penetration; ICT ~3.6% of GDP ($1.7bn, 2017 WB diagnostic), projected >$5bn by 2030.
- The national data-exchange hub **eGIF** (launched 2009) **stalled** on funding and unclear mandates; MoCD re-issued bids for a National Data Exchange Hub in 2019 and 2022, status unclear.
- In its absence, **Ghana.gov** — the fintech-enabled government **payments portal** — became the de facto catalyst for streamlining services and data exchange (a 2017 Auditor-General report had cited >GHS 2bn / ~$165m annual revenue-collection losses across 254 MDAs and ~2,000 manual service points).
- Lesson: a galvanizing **use case** (payments) drove cross-government buy-in where a top-down interoperability framework could not.
