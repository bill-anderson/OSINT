# How does access to a local data centre affect business productivity? (BII / Liquid, Dec 2024)

An **independent evaluation study** (Steward Redqueen and Itad, commissioned by
British International Investment with Liquid) of how access to a **local data
centre** affected the productivity of Liquid business clients in **Kenya, Zambia,
Zimbabwe and South Africa**. Reference study — cited here, not absorbed as
country-current state. Full report held as the companion artefact.

## What it studied

BII invested in **[[liquid-intelligent-technologies|Liquid Intelligent
Technologies]]** — an initial 2018 equity investment, then a **2020 follow-on to
support a new Liquid data centre in South Africa** (BII cites two equity investments
totalling US$220m). This study, by independent evaluators, tests whether access to
that "local" data centre (operational **2019**) improved cloud-service quality and
firm productivity, via a desk review plus a **survey of 97 Liquid client companies**
across the four markets.

## Main findings (survey-based, as reported)

- The local data centre **improved latency and reliability** of cloud services as
  perceived by Liquid customers, in line with external evidence.
- Data reliability was **not the main driver** of cloud-service use, but improved
  reliability appears to have **supported increased cloud use**, especially for
  **digital-native firms** most reliant on cloud.
- Cloud-service use **supports firm productivity** — helping companies launch new
  products/services, expand into new markets, and offer existing services faster or
  cheaper.
- **Cost and quality** remain barriers to greater cloud use; **electricity
  affordability and reliability** is a further growth barrier that DFIs and private
  investment (including renewable energy) can help address.
- Conclusion: further investment in local data centres is "key to supporting
  business productivity," particularly in markets with lower reliability / higher
  latency.
