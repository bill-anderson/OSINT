The Kingdom of Eswatini has unveiled the findings of its first Digital Readiness Assessment, a collaborative effort between the Ministry of Information, Communications and Technology (ICT) and the UNDP. The assessment highlights key opportunities for digital transformation and underscores the need for global partnerships to bridge connectivity gaps.

The Digital Readiness Assessment, conducted with technical support from the Southern Africa Research and Policy Centre (SEPARC), evaluates Eswatini's readiness to leverage digital tools for economic growth, service delivery, and social inclusion. The report notes significant potential in digital infrastructure but challenges such as limited internet access, digital skills gaps, and infrastructure disparities.

Minister of ICT Hon. Savannah Maziya: "The findings in this report are key to the successful realization of the Eswatini Government Digital Transformation Strategy, as they provide fundamental insights into the digital public infrastructure foundational elements that need to be strengthened... It is encouraging that the findings of this DRA are aligned with the Government in Your Hand Strategy."

The findings will inform Eswatini's national digital transformation strategy, which includes initiatives to expand broadband coverage to rural areas, develop a national digital identity system, and strengthen cybersecurity and data governance frameworks.

Current state of digital access: Globally, 850 million people lack legal identity (World Bank estimates), including 470 million in sub-Saharan Africa. During 2021 to 2022, an average of 78 percent of eligible individuals over age 15 owned an ID; Eswatini sat above the average with 88 percent of adults recorded with government-issued IDs. Eswatini has called for global partners to contribute expertise, funding, and technology via the Eswatini Posts and Telecoms Corporation (EPTC), under group managing director Themba Khumalo.
