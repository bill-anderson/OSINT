Independent Electoral Commission (IEC) chairman Joseph Colley revealed the commission is preparing to introduce a **digital system for the next presidential election** to strengthen transparency and curb the circulation of fake election results, speaking at a stakeholder engagement forum.

The IEC plans a **Geographic Information System (GIS)** to map all polling stations across the country for the first time, digitalising them so the public can access voter-registration information by area. Colley said the commission is gradually transitioning to digital solutions including **results transmission**, has equipped offices with 65-inch screens to project results during collation, and will use tablets during the electoral process while maintaining manual systems as backups.
