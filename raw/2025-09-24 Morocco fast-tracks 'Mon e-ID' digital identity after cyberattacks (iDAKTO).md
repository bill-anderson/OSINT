Morocco's response to a series of cyberattacks (claimed by Algerian hacker group Jabaroot, which hit the CNSS, Ministry of Justice and land-registry agency ANCFCC) includes **fast-tracking adoption of the national digital identity** by government institutions and the public. The system includes the **"Mon e-ID"** mobile app and citizen portal, both built by **[[idakto|iDAKTO]]**.

The platform was built through a partnership with **Idemia Smart Identity by IN Group** (**[[idemia]]**/[[in-groupe]]), which also supplies contactless national ID cards, under contract to Morocco's Digital Development Agency (**[[add-morocco|ADD]]**) and the **[[dgsn-morocco|DGSN]]**. Ministries of Justice, Habitat and Youth, the CNSS, the DGI (tax authority) and TGR (treasury) have adopted the app; banks and telecoms are following (some integrated Mon e-ID in 48 hours). iDAKTO's Yann Bouan said the system uses a **hybrid model** — server-side 1:N matching for proofing and native device biometrics for 1:1 verification — and that iDAKTO never has access to citizens' biometric data. The DGSN's Mouhcine Yejjou said the system reached **2.5 million active users** and secured 7 million+ digital-identity transactions since April 2022.
