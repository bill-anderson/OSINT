For over a decade the Bioko Island Malaria Elimination Project (BIMEP) has worked with Equatorial Guinea's Ministry of Health and Social Welfare on malaria commodities. Already using DHIS2 for health data, BIMEP expanded it into an electronic logistics-management information system (eLMIS), replacing error-prone Excel tools that had contributed to central-warehouse stock-outs (notably during COVID-19).

Using the WHO malaria-logistics DHIS2 metadata package, the team tracks commodity distribution from warehouse to facility, with configured predictors estimating stock-on-hand and months of stock to forecast procurement, plus notifications for expiries and reorder thresholds. Triangulating stock and health-services data quickly surfaced discrepancies (e.g. RDTs issued exceeding tests performed), exposing reporting gaps and potential stock leakage and increasing accountability. The system is not a full transactional eLMIS (limited lot-level tracking). The biggest remaining challenge is instilling a data-use culture; the team is transitioning to the DHIS2 Android Capture app.
