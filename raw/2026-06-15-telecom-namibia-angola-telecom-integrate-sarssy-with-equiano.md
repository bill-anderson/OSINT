Telecom Namibia, Angola Telecom and Satélite e Cabo TV (Satcab) have a signed a master service agreement (MSA) to integrate the Southern African Regional Submarine System (Sarssy) with the Equiano Cable System, a milestone in strengthening digital connectivity and cooperation.

According to Telecom Namibia, the tripartite agreement unites regional and international operators, reinforcing a shared commitment to expanding resilient, high-capacity digital infrastructure across Southern Africa.

The telco added that the agreement built on the memorandum of understanding and commercial terms agreement signed in April 2026 in Swakopmund, Namibia, and signaled a transition from strategic intent to practical implementation of one of the region's most important digital infrastructure projects.

It said that the agreement falls within the framework of bilateral cooperation between Namibia and Angola, aimed at advancing regional integration, strengthening economic ties, and promoting digital sovereignty and connectivity across Southern Africa.

## Sarssy cable project to interconnect with Equiano

The Sarssy cable project will interconnect with the Equiano system via Telecom Namibia's Swakopmund landing station.

It will provide additional international capacity to Namibia, Angola, and other countries along Southern Africa's west coast.

Under the MSA, Telecom Namibia will serve as the landing party for Sarssy and host and support the infrastructure in its landing station in Swakopmund, including Angola Telecom's equipment.

"The landing party will provide an initial dedicated transmission capacity of 1.0 Tbps on the Equiano Cable System, ensuring high-capacity connectivity under the terms of the agreement," Telecom Namibia explained in a statement.

It said Angola Telecom will benefit from long-term, secure, and flexible access to capacity while Satcab will play a role in the delivery of the system.

## Telecom Namibia's infrastructure improvements

The partnership comes amid Telecom Namibia's efforts to strengthen its infrastructure for both local and international connectivity.

Earlier this month, Telecom Namibia teamed up with Demshi Investment Holdings to fast-track its fiber rollout, replacing copper infrastructure in high-risk areas prone to theft and vandalism.

In May 2026, the state-owned telco warned its subscribers of potential network disruptions as it continued a modernization program.

The warning came as the telco grappled with service quality issues in parts of the country, following a spate of network outages in recent months.

In April 2026, it also said it had made progress in resolving disruptions caused by international connectivity problems.
