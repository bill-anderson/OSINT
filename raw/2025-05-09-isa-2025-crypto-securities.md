On March 25, 2025, President Tinubu signed the Investments and Securities Act (2025), replacing the 2007 version. A landmark provision recognises digital assets as securities, giving the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) wide-ranging authority over issuance, trading and promotion of digital assets — legalising crypto under capital market rules. The SEC can monitor securities exchanges, conduct audits, impose penalties, suspend operations and remove executives. Context: in 2021 the CBN barred banks from processing crypto transactions; the SEC launched the Accelerated Regulatory Incubation Programme (ARIP) in June 2024. Under ISA 2025 a digital asset is "a digital token that represents assets such as a debt or equity claim on the issuer" including any asset issued on a blockchain — capturing cryptocurrencies, stablecoins, security tokens and tokenised real-world assets. The SEC also gains oversight of token issuers including meme-coin creators and ICOs.
