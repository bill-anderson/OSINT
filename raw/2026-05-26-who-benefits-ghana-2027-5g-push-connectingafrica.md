# Who will benefit from Ghana's 2027 5G push?

*Connecting Africa, by Eden Harris, Chief Special Correspondent, 26 May 2026.*

Ghana's renewed 5G push has drawn both optimism and skepticism after Samuel Nartey George, communications, digital technology and innovations minister, said that the government will target 70% nationwide 5G coverage by the country's 70th Independence Day next year.

George made the comments at the Ghana Chamber of Telecommunications' anniversary celebration in early May 2026.

The announcement came after Ghana's earlier rollout effort with Next Gen InfraCo (NGIC), a network-as-a-service (NaaS) operator, fell short of expectations.

Instead of providing 5G access to telecom operators and businesses as promised, the project faced repeated delays stretching from late 2024 into 2025, forfeiting its exclusive ten-year license with the nation.

Now, with a new March 6, 2027, target and a shift to an open model that allows multiple players rather than a single exclusive license, the promise of faster Internet has become deeply personal for freelance videographer and director, Settor Safo, whose livelihood depends on stable, high-speed uploads and livestreaming.

## A freelance reality

Safo said when the Internet connection drops, the pressure quickly builds.

"Clients will be expecting deliverables, and then the Internet will just mess everything up. Because usually when you try to explain to clients, it sounds like they're given so many excuses not to deliver, but this is the case, where the Internet provider isn't pulling through," he told Connecting Africa.

Safo added that delays can affect the quality of work in real-time, such as out-of-sync moments where quality and sound can fall out of sync.

Safo said he has to work around slow wireless Internet speeds and is often forced to use Ethernet cables to connect his camera or laptop or to transfer content to a streaming site. When it comes to unreliable Internet, he said, it is also financially draining because users pay for data expecting to complete uploads and downloads within a subscription period, but slow speeds can stretch a 1GB download to over six hours.

The GSMA, a global organization aiming to unlock connectivity, said in a 2025 report, focused on Ghana's digital transformation, that the average download speeds are moderate, below those reported in some other West African countries. According to the report, the average speed reported to Ookla by mobile Internet users in Ghana in May 2024 was 16 Mbit/s, which is below those reported in Nigeria (22 Mbit/s) or Côte d'Ivoire (23 Mbit/s) in the same month.

## Skepticism towards 5G

Asked whether he is optimistic or skeptical about the pledged new 5G rollout, Safo said, "I am skeptical."

"We've had 3G, we've had 4G, but we are still using 4G, and it doesn't seem like it sometimes," he said. "Because 4G is supposed to be quite fast, but most of the time it lags. It's slow, and you can't get much with that. You don't get your money's worth."

"If we have to move forward, I think in terms of infrastructure, then they have to upgrade to meet the speed. You can't have 5G and then have 4G equipment running it," he added.

Benedict Owusu, CEO of Bao Strategies, a Ghanaian firm focused on strengthening connectivity in the nation, said he agrees that 4G infrastructure to power 5G will slow down the efficiency of the newer technology. He warned that if MTN, an interested stakeholder, gets a 5G license, but still runs traffic through its old 4G core, then Ghana won't truly get 5G.

"If we are to give 5G licenses to MTN, we have to put it in the license [agreement] that MTN should deploy a 5G core network and not rely on the 4G core network to better serve the customers," he said. "The 5G wholesale entity, the NGIC, [which Ghana first] gave the license to, designed their own 5G core network, and it is cloud-based, which will have a huge impact on the speed of the network's license," he added.

MTN declined to comment as the regulator has yet to award the 5G license. However, MTN has sent signals to Ghana's government by inviting its minister to participate in its 30th anniversary celebration, which took place during the same week of the minister's announcement. George spoke at MTN's celebration, carefully avoiding committal remarks to the telecom and mobile money provider.

## Creator economy gains

David Adeleke, CEO and founder of Communiqué, a research and consulting firm which launched the first creator economy report in partnership with the Nigerian government's National Council for Arts and Culture, said having 5G in Ghana will unlock more than speed; it will unlock pathways.

"It gives Ghanaians access to better opportunities, and it puts them in better conversations with their counterparts in different parts of the world," he said.
