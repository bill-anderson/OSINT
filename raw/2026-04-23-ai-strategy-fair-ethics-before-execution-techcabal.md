# Mauritius' new AI strategy sets a new bar: ethics before execution

*TechCabal, 23 April 2026.*

Analysis of the **Mauritius National AI Strategy 2025-2029** and its **FAIR Guidelines** (introduced April 2026), which make governance and ethics the starting point rather than an afterthought. TechCabal contrasts Mauritius's **governance-led model** with Nigeria/Kenya (scale, ecosystem) and South Africa (institutional regulation).

Key features of the FAIR framework:
- **Vendor-neutral and border-agnostic** — any AI system operating in Mauritius, regardless of origin, must comply; imported tools face the same scrutiny as domestic ones.
- Pillars: **Fairness, Accountability, Inclusiveness, Integrity** (plus responsibility) across the full AI lifecycle (design → deployment → monitoring → decommissioning).
- **High-risk sectors (fintech, gaming): mandatory bias audits.**
- Foreign providers must **designate locally based representatives** accountable for system outcomes.
- Governance determined by **impact, not geography**.
- Currently **non-binding** (no immediate penalties) but on a legal/policy trajectory to shape procurement, sector regulation and future legislation — contrasted with South Africa's draft policy (fines ~$530,000 / up to 10 years).
