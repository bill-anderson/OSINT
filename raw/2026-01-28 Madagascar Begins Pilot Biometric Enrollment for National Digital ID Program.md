Madagascar has begun a pilot enrolment phase for a national biometric digital identity programme, starting in the Antsimondrano district, to assign a National Unique Identification Number (NUI) to citizens. Rollout is managed under the Ministry of Digital Development, Posts and Telecommunications, working with the National Office for Identification (ONI).

Targets: enrol 2 million people in urban areas between April and June 2026, expanding to roughly 8 million in rural areas later in 2026. Biometric modalities captured: fingerprints, facial image and iris. Iris capture hardware is supplied by IriTech (IriAegis-BK device). Operational planning references ~2,500 enrolment kits and 1,729 enrolment centres, teams working six days per week at ~40 registrations per agent per day. Sources: L'Express de Madagascar; Orange actu Madagascar.
