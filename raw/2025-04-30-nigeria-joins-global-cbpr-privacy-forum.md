Nigeria joined the Global Cross-Border Privacy Rules (CBPR) Forum as an associate member to explore better ways of cross-border data transfers. Dr Vincent Olatunji, National Commissioner of the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC), announced this ahead of the Network of African Data Protection Authorities (NADPA) conference (May 6-8, Abuja). Olatunji recalled the enactment of the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) in June 2023, which mandated NDPC to decide on adequacy of data protection. "Nigeria is exploring various cross-border data frameworks as provided under the NDPA for the purpose of safeguarding data subjects' rights and protecting Nigeria's data sovereignty." The government is setting up a strategic task team on cross-border data transfer. The NADPA event was expected to attract over 1,000 delegates from 30 African countries.
