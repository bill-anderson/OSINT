Tanzania has taken another step towards strengthening digital privacy and security after the **Open
University of Tanzania (OUT)**, in partnership with the **Personal Data Protection Commission (PDPC)**,
launched the **Certified Data Protection Officer (CDPO) Programme** — a national initiative designed to
increase expertise in handling and safeguarding personal information.

Participants will be equipped with the professional knowledge and practical skills required to ensure that
personal data is managed in accordance with the **Personal Data Protection Act**, which provides the legal
framework for protecting citizens' privacy rights.

Speaking at the launch at OUT Headquarters in Kinondoni, Dar es Salaam, OUT Vice Chancellor **Prof. Alex B.
Makulilo** said the programme demonstrates the institution's commitment to developing solutions that respond
to national challenges brought by technological advancement, calling personal data "a critical asset across
various sectors."

PDPC Director General **Dr. Emmanuel Mkilia** described the launch as a milestone in Tanzania's journey
towards a secure digital economy built on trust and responsible use of information, addressing the growing
demand for qualified Data Protection Officers who can help institutions meet their obligations under the Act.

Under the arrangement, **OUT delivers the training and assesses participants, while PDPC provides official
certification** to successful candidates. The initiative targets professionals in government, financial
services, telecommunications, healthcare, education and technology.
