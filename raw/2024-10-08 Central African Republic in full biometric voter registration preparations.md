The Central African Republic (CAR) is in the process of organizing local elections it has not held since 1988. The tentative date for the polls has been fixed for April 6, 2025, months before presidential and legislative elections scheduled for December of the same year.

As part of preparations, the National Election Authority (ANE) will launch a biometric voter registration drive to revise the country's electoral register. It was due to begin September 23 but was pushed back due to technical challenges; once launched it will run till December 30, 2024.

Several sources indicate the biometric enrollment software and hardware is provided by Dutch biometric firm GenKey. GenKey's system was also used for voter registration before general elections in 2020, with President Faustin-Archange Touadera the first person biometrically enrolled at the time.

On September 8, ANE announced the arrival of material: 1,680,000 registration forms, 48,000 amendment forms, and 36,000 transfer and deregistration forms. Spokesperson Théophile Momokoama said more than 70 percent of financial resources had been mobilized, over 2,000 enrollment personnel trained, and ANE had received at least 3,000 biometric enrollment tablets.

## Lack of ID may affect voter registration participation

A major problem is the lack of national ID documents. Under CAR law, the national ID card, birth certificate or passport can be used as proof of identity at registration, but obtaining any of these is difficult. Because of insecurity, national ID cards are issued only in the capital, making it costly for citizens in far-flung communities. Authorities will resort to verbal testimonies from local authorities such as traditional chiefs to confirm identity. An ID card enrollment center was opened in Bambari.

Al Madina, an Oman-headquartered security printing company in charge of producing the document, has been accused of increasing the fee from FCFA 4,500 (US$8) as fixed by the country's finance law, to FCFA 6,750 ($11).
