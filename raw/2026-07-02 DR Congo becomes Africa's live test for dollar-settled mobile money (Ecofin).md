**Ecofin Agency** (2 July 2026) corrects the "joint launch" framing that spread across trade press. It
states plainly that **there is no single, jointly-announced stablecoin pilot uniting Visa, M-Pesa and
Onafriq** — what exists is a **set of converging initiatives**: Visa's global stablecoin-settlement
programme, Visa Pay in the DRC (live since Q4 2025 via [[onafriq]]), and Onafriq's own stablecoin
treasury activity. Headlines announcing a three-party "launch" are a gloss on the on-record remarks of
Visa's Godfrey Sullivan, not a joint issuer announcement.

Ecofin frames the DRC as **Africa's live test for dollar-settled mobile money**, against a real
regulatory tension: the **DRC central bank is separately pursuing de-dollarisation**, which sits awkwardly
against any USD-denominated stablecoin rail. See [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]], [[gov.protect]].
