# K38tn transacted via mobile money

Per the Reserve Bank of Malawi's **2025 National Payments System (NPS) Report**, mobile money transaction value nearly doubled — up **94 percent to K37.9 trillion** from K19.5 trillion the previous year. Transaction volumes rose 31.6% to **2.4 billion** (from 1.8 billion in 2024), and mobile money subscriptions rose 23.3% to **19.9 million** (though this reflects registered wallets; only 44.8% — about 8.9 million — were active within a 30-day period).

Usage remains concentrated in basic services: airtime top-ups 42% of transactions, cash-in/cash-out 33.1%, while **merchant payments accounted for only 7.7%** — "continued dominance in cash-substitution use cases, which poses a challenge to the government's digitalisation agenda." The gender gap narrowed to 9.7 percentage points (54.9% male / 45.1% female) from 16.9 previously. The Ministry of Finance raised wallet holding limits from K1m to K5m and transaction limits from K750,000 to K1m to support higher-value digital transactions in 2026.
