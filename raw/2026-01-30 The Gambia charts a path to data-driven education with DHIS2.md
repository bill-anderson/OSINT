The Gambia's Ministry of Basic and Secondary Education (MoBSE) is shifting from traditional aggregate data systems toward **dynamic dashboards and individual-level learner tracking** using **[[dhis2]] for Education (DHIS2-Ed)**. The change enables real-time data on enrolment, attendance, performance and equity; principals and parents can view **School Report Cards** derived from the system.

The reforms grew through partnerships with the Global Partnership for Education (GPE), the University of Oslo's HISP network, and regional partners under the GPE Knowledge and Innovation Exchange (KIX). Key takeaways: begin with simple wins (turning annual data into real-time dashboards); use DHIS2's flexibility to move from aggregate counts to individual learner records; and engage communities by making data transparent. The article positions The Gambia as a model for African countries building real-time, learner-focused EMIS.
