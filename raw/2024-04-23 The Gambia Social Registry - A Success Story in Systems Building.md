The Gambia Social Registry (**GamSR**), a national social registry initiated in 2021, now covers **40 of 43 districts and 203,022 households** — about **70% of the population in just two years**.

GamSR data underpinned the 'Nafa Quick' COVID-19 response (78,422 households, 30 districts) and the subsequent Nafa cash-transfer programme (20 districts, 16,966 households); other users include the National Disaster Management Agency, the ROOTS project (Ministry of Agriculture) and the World Food Programme. Success factors: stakeholder buy-in; a standardized assessment method (digitized household questionnaire + proxy means test); institutional arrangements using existing bodies such as the Gambia Bureau of Statistics (GBoS); and technology enablers including computer-assisted personal interviewing (CAPI) and a **Social Registry Information System (SRIS)** with automated data-quality checks. Remaining challenges: reaching remote populations, data accuracy, infrastructure, and a data-secure environment.
