# Absa Seychelles Rolls Out Multi-Currency Virtual Card for Travel and Online Spend | 24 June 2026
The Seychelles Times

Absa Bank Seychelles unveiled a multi-currency prepaid card that lives entirely inside its mobile banking app, giving customers up to five separate currency wallets on a single digital product. The launch is timed for the country's 50th Independence anniversary. It was introduced by Murugan Pillay (personal and private banking) with digital product manager Patrick Hoareau demonstrating the UX.

Customers can generate a card in any of five currencies — Seychelles Rupee, US Dollar, Euro, British Pound and South African Rand — and build up to twenty-five cards in total, each funded from a linked Absa account (SCR 10 minimum top-up). The card builds on Absa Pay (rolled out December 2025, turning Android phones into tap-and-pay terminals); the new product is a pure digital wallet. Live exchange rates display before conversion; wallet-to-wallet transfers are immediate. Prepaid design limits breach exposure to the loaded balance.

The regulator prepared the ground via the National Payment System (Electronic Money) Regulations, 2022, and the Central Bank of Seychelles continues to lower merchant service charges and keep the local SEFT rails free for customers. Seychelles is the first island market in the Absa group to offer multi-currency cards (after Mauritius, Uganda, Botswana). Source: SN.
