Congo's President **Denis Sassou N'Guesso** activated the country's connection to the **2Africa** subsea cable (**28 February 2024**, Pointe-Noire) and commissioned a new data centre built by the telecom regulator **[[arpce-congo]]**. Per DG Louis-Marc Sakala, it is the state's **second data centre** (after one built in Brazzaville in 2021), for "the storage and security of so-called sovereign data" — **CFA3.8bn ($6.3m)**, ~2-year build, up to **54 racks / 156 m²**, with the 2Africa landing near the existing WACS station.

(2Africa, at 45,000 km the world's longest subsea cable — backed by Meta, Telecom Egypt, China Mobile, MTN GlobalConnect, Orange, STC, Vodafone and WIOCC — will connect 33 countries.) Distinct from the Brazzaville National Data Centre (CAB).
