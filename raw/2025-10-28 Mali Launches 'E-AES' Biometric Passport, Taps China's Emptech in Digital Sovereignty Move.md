Mali held an official launch ceremony on 27 October 2025 for its new national biometric passport, an ICAO-compliant travel document with an electronic chip holding the holder's fingerprints and photograph. The new supplier is the Chinese firm Emptech (Emperor Technology Co., Ltd), replacing France's Idemia.

Malian officials framed the supplier change as a political victory: Police Comptroller General Cheick Keita called it "a major step forward towards Mali's regained sovereignty," emphasising "technological independence and local data management." The passport is inscribed "E-AES," referencing the Confederation of Sahel States (Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger), which withdrew from ECOWAS. The document is presented as a "symbol of administrative and technological integration" among member states.

The decision to terminate Idemia was precipitated by a 2023 dispute in which Malian authorities accused Idemia of refusing to transfer technical and biometric data from the national system, raising digital-sovereignty concerns and driving the government to regain national control over its identity-document production chain.
