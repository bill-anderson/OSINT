Africell opened a data center in Luanda, the cornerstone of its Angola network and a platform for a digital ecosystem for mobile users, entrepreneurs and innovators. "The opening of the data center is a significant milestone," said Christopher Lundh, CEO of Africell Angola. "We want to lead a digital transformation... faster, more affordable and more reliable digital connectivity than [Angola] has had before."

The facility is fully cloud-based; international infrastructure brands include HP, Nokia, Dell and Oracle, with local partners CPC Africa, Jembas and Prosecurity. It is located at Africell Angola's HQ in the Kings Tower building, central Luanda, and supports Africell's mobile network plus low-cost hosting for Angolan developers.

Lundh thanked the General Tax Administration (AGT) for clearing equipment and the National Social Security Institute (INSS) for installation support. Africell was completing the first phase of a 5G-enabled network, with partners including Nokia, Oracle, Aviat, Angola Cables, MSTelcom, Angola Telecom, TPA and Infrasat. "The strong foundations we are laying today... will benefit our customers when we officially launch," said Ziad Dalloul, Chairman and CEO of Africell Group.
