# Mauritania — PAMIF financial-infrastructure modernisation; financial inclusion 21% → 55%

*Off-assigned-host capture (AfDB institutional primary): high-value financial-inclusion baseline serving the mobile-money watch-item. FR twin at afdb.org/fr/.../92327.*

Behind the everyday QR-code payments now common in Nouakchott is a **$4.78 million initiative, PAMIF ("Projet de modernisation des infrastructures financières")**, financed by the African Development Fund (concessional arm of the AfDB). Through PAMIF, Mauritania built financial infrastructure to international standards and **more than doubled its financial-inclusion rate, from 21% (2019–2020) to over 50% (55% in 2026)**.

Until November 2023, interbank transfers took days; clearing was manual (staff physically carrying envelopes between branches and the Central Bank of Mauritania); data-entry errors ran close to 40%; a cheque deposited in Néma or Zouerate (740 km from Nouakchott) could take two weeks to clear. PAMIF deployed several components simultaneously with the Central Bank of Mauritania:

- **Télécompensation** (remote cheque clearing): a cheque deposited 8am–noon now settles the same day by 3pm. BMCI processed 5,000 transfers in all of 2023 vs **94,000 in H2 2025 alone**.
- A **real-time system** for large-value corporate/interbank transfers ("funds available within five minutes").
- An **automated refinancing facility** giving the central bank tools for coherent monetary policy — inflation fell from double digits to below 2%, and a bond market now offers maturities up to ten years.

A **second PAMIF phase** is in the pipeline, targeting deeper financial inclusion and the emergence of a Mauritanian fintech ecosystem, with rural financial services and green/social finance in view.
