On **8 October 2025** in Kinshasa, the DRC's Minister of Digital Economy **Augustin Kibassa Maliba** launched the drafting of the **National Digital Plan 2026-2030 (PNN2)** and the country's **first National AI Strategy**, aiming to make the DRC a **regional digital hub by 2030** and to leverage its critical minerals as an investment catalyst.

The five-year plan has four pillars — **infrastructure & connectivity; digital public platforms & services; human capital & inclusion; cybersecurity & digital trust** — plus five cross-cutting priorities including **technological sovereignty and AI**. Reported here at **$1.5 billion** (government investment); other sources cite larger totals (see the $8.7bn account and REV-COD-001).
