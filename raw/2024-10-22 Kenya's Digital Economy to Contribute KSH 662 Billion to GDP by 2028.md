Nairobi, 22 October 2024 — the GSMA report *Driving Digital Transformation of the Economy in Kenya* projects Kenya's digital economy will contribute **KSH 662 billion to GDP by 2028**, driven by policy reforms in agriculture, manufacturing, transport and trade, with 300,000 new jobs and a KSH 150bn increase in tax revenues.

The GSMA also launched the **Digital Africa Index (DAI)**, with Kenya among the top performers (scoring above 50). In 2023 the mobile ecosystem contributed **KSH 1.2 trillion** to GDP and KSH 212bn in government revenues; Kenya has 99% 3G / 98% 4G coverage, but only **33.5%** use mobile internet (a usage gap the GSMA projects could shrink from 63% to 46% by 2028). A companion report, *Barriers to Smartphone Adoption: Kenya Case Study*, highlights affordability. Policy recommendations: reduce sector-specific taxes, streamline licence renewals, improve device affordability and support digital skills.
