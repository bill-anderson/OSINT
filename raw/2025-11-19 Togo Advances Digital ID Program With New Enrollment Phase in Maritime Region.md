*Reports a rollout phase but no cumulative enrolment count — see GAP-TGO-002.*

(Togo First) - Togo is expanding its national biometric enrollment drive to the Maritime region, allowing residents to obtain their new biometric identity cards starting Saturday, November 22 [2025]. The operation was announced by the National Identification Agency (ANID), which implements the e-ID biometric identification project. The expansion follows successful rollouts in the Gulf, Agoe-Nyive, and Savanes prefectures.

Enrollment will commence in Zone 1 of the Maritime region, which includes the prefectures of Vo, Yoto, Bas-Mono, and Lacs. The National Institute of Statistics, Economic and Demographic Studies (INSEED) is managing field personnel deployment.

As with previous phases, the operation aims to assign every resident a Unique Identification Number (NIU), intended to serve as a single access key to public, private, and social services, including education, social security, pensions, finance, health, vaccination, and monetary transfers. The system will also consolidate a secure and reliable central database.

All citizens are eligible for the e-ID Togo project, including children aged zero to five, who can be registered using a parent's NIU. The biometric enrollment is financed by the World Bank under the WURI-Togo project.
