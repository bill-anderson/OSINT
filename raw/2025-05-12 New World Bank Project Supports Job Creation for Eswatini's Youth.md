MBABANE, May 12, 2025 - The World Bank Board of Executive Directors has approved the Eswatini Youth Employment Opportunities Project (EYEOP), aimed at increasing income earning opportunities for targeted youth. EYEOP is the first project in Eswatini financed by the International Development Association (IDA) following the country's transition to an IDA/IBRD blended status.

Young people make up 30% of the population and nearly half the labor force. Only 1,000 new jobs are created annually compared to 25,000 new entrants. Young women face an unemployment rate of 52.4% compared to 45% for men. Over 53% of youth are not engaged in education, employment, or training (NEET). Through skills training in high-growth sectors (such as agricultural value chains and related services), and the establishment of a dynamic and digital social registry, EYEOP aims to promote youth economic empowerment. The project will empower approximately 30,000 young people, with at least 50% female beneficiaries, and include young people with disabilities.

Minister of Economic Planning and Development Honorable Dr. Thabo Gina and World Bank Division Director Satu Kahkonen welcomed the project.

EYEOP has two key components:
- Component 1: Youth Income-Earning Opportunities - expanding income-generating opportunities by promoting self-employment in on-farm agricultural production, agriculture-related value chains, and other services.
- Component 2: Strengthening Social Protection Targeting and Delivery Systems - supporting development of a dynamic and digital Social Registry to enhance identification and targeting of vulnerable youth, serving as a key tool for broader social protection programs.
