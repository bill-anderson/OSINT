Promotional/advertorial ministerial interview (Business Focus, "mauritaniareport" series) with Mohamed Abdallahi Louly, Mauritania's Minister of Digital Transformation, Innovation and Modernization of the Administration. It establishes the National Digital Transformation Agenda 2022-2025, a strategy built on four axes: digital infrastructure, digital government administration, support for the transformation of economic sectors, and e-business and innovation. The dedicated ministry was created in 2021.

Louly names digital infrastructure the first priority, citing a plan to invest at least $30 million over two years in fibre backbone projects for inclusive high-speed internet, including a south/southeast border project using renewable-energy technology. On e-government (the second axis), 12 of 26 ministerial departments had been digitalised for mail-processing and an integrated schools-enrolment system had eliminated 500,000 duplicate document requests, with tracking services focused on justice, education and health. On innovation, he outlines three pillars — a Startup ACT Mauritania bill (presented to the Council of Ministers in September), the IBTIKAR science-and-technology park, and an innovation fund — plus ambitions to attract Microsoft, Amazon or Google to host a first-class data centre and to launch masters programmes in AI and robotics.
