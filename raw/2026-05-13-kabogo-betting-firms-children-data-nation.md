# Register or face shutdown: Kabogo to betting firms with children's data

At least 185 gaming and betting firms in the country face closure for allegedly operating illegally, the government has announced.
...
The Ministry of ICT and Digital Economy said the firms have been operating without formal registration.
...
The development comes after ICT and Digital Economy Cabinet Secretary William Kabogo gave gaming firms 40 days to ensure full registration to collect or handle Kenyans’ data or risk licence cancellation.
...
Appearing before the Senate plenary on Wednesday, Mr Kabogo revealed that 185 gambling and betting firms handling children’s data in the country are not registered with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC).
...
In his submission, the former Kiambu governor said another five firms have expired certificates, while only 15 out of the 224 highlighted by the ODPC are fully registered as both data controllers and data processors.
...
In contrast, only 13 gaming and betting firms are partially registered as either data controllers or data processors, while six are still undergoing the registration process.
...
The CS said the 224 firms are among gaming and betting companies identified by the ODPC as engaging in gambling applications and broadcast gambling promotions targeting minors.
...
He said the relevant agencies had been given 40 days to ensure the 185 firms are properly registered, failing which their licences would be cancelled.
...
With the enforcement of the Data Protection Act, 2019, there has been a need for stronger and stricter data protection, with the ODPC tasked with overseeing compliance and accountability in the sector.
...
Consequently, all businesses in Kenya that collect, store or process personal data are required to comply with data protection regulations, including registering as data controllers or data processors with the ODPC.
...
Failure to comply can result in hefty fines and legal action, including penalties of up to Sh5 million or one percent of a company’s gross annual turnover from the previous year.
...
Some of the sensitive personal data collected include names, ID numbers, mobile phone numbers, gender, health status, marital status and biometrics, among others.

The CS admitted that the registration deficit among data handlers in the gaming and betting sector requires urgent enforcement action.
...
Mr Kabogo further noted that between February 2025 and April 2026, the Media Council of Kenya took 19 enforcement actions against media enterprises over gambling and lottery promotions broadcast or published in breach of disclosure, age-restriction, Gambling Regulatory Authority licence-display or watershed requirements.
...
He said the data points provide evidence of both a significant exposure risk for minors and an active regulatory response.
...
While admitting laxity on the part of the government, CS Kabogo said the ICT ministry has not commissioned a single dedicated and published national assessment covering all four risk vectors identified by senators.
...
He said the ministry is currently consolidating available evidence through the National Survey on Child Online Protection and Safety in Kenya.
