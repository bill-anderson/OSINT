Morocco's Ministry of Digital Transition and Administrative Reform signed cooperation agreements with French firms **[[idakto|iDakto]]** and **ShareID** (announced 12 February 2026) to support development of a **national digital identity wallet**, within the broader **[[idarati-x0|Idarati X.0]]** public-sector modernization program.

The wallet builds on a major 2025 digital-ID agreement that aligned the ministry and the **[[dgsn-morocco|DGSN]]** under the **[[digital-morocco-2030|Digital Morocco 2030]]** framework, and rests on biometric credential foundations from when **[[idemia|IDEMIA]]** won a biometric-ID contract in 2019 to modernize Morocco's national ID card (fingerprint and facial recognition). iDakto and ShareID will provide technical expertise for the wallet architecture (~six-month horizon for initial phases). The wallet becomes part of the same ecosystem the E-Police platform taps for identity verification, and may extend to private-sector authentication.
