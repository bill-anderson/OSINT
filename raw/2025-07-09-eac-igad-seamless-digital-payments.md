# EAC and IGAD Forge Path for Seamless Digital Payments Across Eastern Africa

TechAfrica News (Editorial Team), 9 July 2025

The East African Community (EAC) and the Intergovernmental Authority on Development (IGAD) have taken a step toward regional financial integration with a five-day workshop on payment systems interoperability. The IGAD-EAC-World Bank Joint Workshop, held 30 June–4 July 2025 in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia, brought together central banks, digital finance experts, and senior policymakers from nine countries to advance harmonised legal, regulatory, and supervisory frameworks for faster, safer, and more inclusive cross-border payments.

The workshop was organised under the **Eastern Africa Regional Digital Integration Project (EARDIP)**, a flagship initiative jointly implemented by IGAD and EAC with World Bank support. Its mission is to boost regional digital market integration by expanding broadband infrastructure and strengthening the environment for cross-border digital services, including digital payments.

A shared regional challenge is fragmented and non-interoperable payment systems. While Kenya, Tanzania, and Ethiopia have made strides in domestic interoperability, regional integration remains stifled by gaps in regulations, technical disparities, and cybersecurity concerns. IGAD's Dr. Mohyeldeen Eltohami called the workshop "a launchpad for transformation," urging cooperation not fragmentation. EAC's Eng. Daniel Murenzi stressed digital payments are the backbone of a functioning digital market and that interoperability is "no longer a luxury, but a necessity." Recommendations included investing in shared digital infrastructure, consumer-centric FPS design, regulatory sandboxes, and harmonised legal instruments, plus exploration of AI, blockchain and cross-border CBDCs.
