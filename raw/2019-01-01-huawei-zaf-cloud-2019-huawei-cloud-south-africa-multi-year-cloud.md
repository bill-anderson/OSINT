## Description

Huawei launched its first availability zone (AZ) in South Africa in November 2018/early 2019, becoming the first international cloud service provider with a hyperscale data centre in Africa. Between 2019 and 2022, Huawei Cloud invested more than ZAR 3.5 billion (approximately USD 219 million) in local cloud infrastructure in South Africa, including ultra-large cloud data centres, networks, and services, culminating in the launch of a third AZ in Johannesburg in January 2023. The investment supports IaaS, PaaS and SaaS services for public-sector, financial, telecom, and SME customers across Southern Africa. This is Huawei's own CapEx commitment, not a Chinese state-bank loan; no corresponding china.csv EXIM/CDB loan row covers this investment.
