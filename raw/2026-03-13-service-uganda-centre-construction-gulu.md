As part of efforts to extend government services closer to the people of northern Uganda, the National Information Technology Authority Uganda (NITA-U), in partnership with Posta Uganda, has launched construction of a regional Service Uganda Centre in Gulu city. The centre will host more than 40 workstations for government ministries, departments and agencies, including URSB, URA and NIRA.

The sh4b project, at the Posta Uganda premises in Gulu, is being undertaken by Prisma Construction Company with the UPDF Engineering Brigade, expected to be completed by June 2026. Diana Kyarikunda (Posta Uganda) said the one-stop centre will significantly improve service delivery across the Northern region. NITA-U executive director Dr Hatwib Mugasa said the facility will make it easier for residents of northern Uganda to access essential public services. (Part of the UDAP-GovNet Service Uganda Centre programme, with additional centres planned for Mbarara and Tororo.)
