Namibia's newly proposed Civil Registration and Identification Bill has been praised for containing essential data-protection elements, per a review by the Association for Progressive Communications (APC) in its series on digital rights and identity in southern Africa.

Novelties highlighted: linking the CRVS system to the national ID system with digital birth notification, and a design intended to "ultimately eliminate the need for overlapping biometric data collection and storage by multiple government agencies." The bill carries strict provisions on citizen-database access, confidentiality, data-sharing and access by security and intelligence agencies, and is expected to reach Parliament ahead of the still-draft Data Protection Bill.

APC stresses that widespread biometric collection — biometric passports, an Integrated Border Management System, voter registration, driver's licences, police certificates of conduct and SIM-card registration — makes a strong standalone data-protection law urgent. Notes UNDP's Accelerator Lab support for legal digital ID and that Namibia began issuing digital IDs to refugees in June 2024.
