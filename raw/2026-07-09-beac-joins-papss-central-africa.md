# BEAC Joins PAPSS to Strengthen Cross-Border Payments in Central Africa

By Akim Benamara — TechAfrica News, 9 July 2026

The Bank of Central African States (BEAC) has officially joined the Pan-African Payment and Settlement System (PAPSS), marking a significant step in strengthening Africa's cross-border payment infrastructure and financial integration in Central Africa. As one of only two regional central banks on the continent, BEAC serves the six member countries of the Central African Economic and Monetary Community (CEMAC) — Cameroon, Central African Republic, Republic of Congo, Gabon, Equatorial Guinea and Chad. Its participation provides PAPSS a strategic entry point into Francophone Africa.

With BEAC joining, PAPSS now connects **28 African countries**, more than **190 commercial banks and fintechs**, supported by **16 switches**; through extended network partners, participants can send money to more than **250 additional financial institutions**. BEAC strengthens PAPSS's connectivity with the CEMAC region (a market of more than 72 million people). Alongside a pilot with the Central Bank of West African States (BCEAO), scheduled to commence later this year, PAPSS aims to connect all regions of Africa through a single African network.

Governor Yvon Sana Bangui (Chair, AACB) said BEAC is "creating the conditions for faster, more affordable and more efficient cross-border payments between the CEMAC countries and Africa." PAPSS CEO Mike Ogbalu III called it "a significant milestone in advancing Africa's financial integration" that "opens new trade and payment corridors between Central Africa and the rest of the continent."

Developed by Afreximbank in partnership with the African Union and the AfCFTA Secretariat, PAPSS enables instant cross-border payments in local African currencies, allowing funds to move across markets within seconds without relying on third-party currencies — strengthening Africa's financial sovereignty by enabling payments to be processed and settled on the continent.
