The Department of Civil Registration (under MHAISS) received a budget allocation of N$178,122,000 (US$9.7 million) for 2025/2026, disclosed by Home Affairs and Immigration Minister Lucia Iipumbu in a budget speech.

Iipumbu cited 2024/2025 strides: a mass ID registration campaign between February and July 2024 that issued more than 110,000 identity documents, and nearly 15,000 stateless persons given legal identity. Three laws were passed by parliament in 2024 — the Regularization of Status Act, the Civil Registration and Identification Act, and the Marriage Act (replacing 1961 legislation).

The article frames Namibia's imminent national digital ID rollout as part of a broader DPI vision, citing lessons from India (India Stack, UPI) and Singapore and referencing a Carnegie Endowment paper, "Digital public infrastructure: A practical approach for Africa."
