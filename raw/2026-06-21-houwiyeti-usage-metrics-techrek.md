# Houwiyeti — latest usage indicators (ANRPTS, June 2026)

*Off-assigned-host capture (Tech Rek, Arabic tech outlet). Body in Arabic; English gloss follows. Fresh quantified update to the held Houwiyeti figures.*

The National Agency for Population Register and Secure Titles (**ANRPTS**) released the latest Houwiyeti usage indicators via a presentation by Maat Moulan Mohamed Abdi (head of development at the agency) at the Arab seminar on "Digitising government administration and the future of public service," Nouakchott, **16–17 June 2026**.

Official figures:
- **677,000 activated accounts** since launch.
- **554,027 services completed remotely** via the platform (estimated to have saved ~700,000 hours / ~86,500 working days).
- **1,511,139 actual beneficiaries** (account holders plus their minor children whose documents are extracted) — **~30% of Mauritania's ~5.1 million population**, in ~2.5 years since launch.
- Most-requested services: civil-status extracts **406,871**; passport requests **63,384**; national ID-card requests **59,363**; national electronic certificates issued **24,409**.
- ~4,750 document requests/month on average in 2026 (mainly passports and ID cards).
- Share of **passport requests made via the app rose from 24.8% (2024) → 32.9% (2025) → 35.7% (January 2026)**.

Houwiyeti was launched officially December 2023, opening to the public January 2024. In 2026 it won two African awards: Africa T-Awards (Dakar, digital government-solutions category) and African CIO Awards (Marrakech, government-projects category), on the margins of GITEX Africa.
