On 10 March 2025, Gabon's Ministry of National Education held a working session chaired by Minister of State Carmelita Ntoutoume Leclercq on school-data collection. The meeting gathered the education community and representatives of the Ministries of Commerce and Digital Economy, UNESCO, and the World Bank (by videoconference).

The **World Bank announced the provision of tablets**, and **UNESCO proposed more suitable software** to facilitate field data collection. Several establishments have already received equipment; training will be set up. The security of sensitive data remains a priority, with a rigorous protection framework to be established. Although databases already exist for exam management and school assets, their consolidation remains essential. Savina Ammassari, UN Resident Coordinator, stressed the importance of an efficient administrative system enabling regular, reliable data collection while respecting strict security standards.
