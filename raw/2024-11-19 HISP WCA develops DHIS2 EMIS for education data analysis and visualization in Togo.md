(Article dated 2024-11-19 on the DHIS2 site; describes an EMIS project rolled out from 2021. Rendered from the French version.)

HISP WCA, with support from GPE KIX and Norad, developed an Education Management Information System (EMIS / systeme d'information de gestion de l'education, SIGE) based on the DHIS2 platform that places schools and their local stakeholders at the heart of a decentralised information system.

Togo's Ministry of Primary, Secondary, Technical and Artisanal Education (Ministere de l'Enseignement primaire, secondaire, technique et artisanal, MEPSTA) uses statistical analysis of data from all schools for evidence-based decisions. Historically it relied on a hybrid paper-and-digital system: data from the country's 16,000 nursery, primary and secondary schools were first collected on paper forms, forwarded to education inspectorates for review and entry into StatEduc, a statistical application used in about 20 countries. StatEduc has limited analytical tools, leaving sub-national levels dependent on central analyses.

In 2020, MEPSTA developed an education sector plan centred on teaching/learning quality, equitable access to basic education, improved information systems, decentralisation, empowerment and accountability-based transparency. To support it, HISP West and Central Africa (WCA), with GPE KIX and Norad, built the DHIS2-based SIGE. In 2021, DHIS2 was implemented in interoperability with StatEduc (StatEduc as data-collection tool; DHIS2 processing and providing information products). The combination facilitates transmission and sharing of data at all hierarchical levels, giving schools and sub-national actors real-time access to dynamic dashboards.

Key indicators enabled include enrolments by level, teacher-learner ratio, national exam pass rate, and pass rate by gender (aggregated or disaggregated). Schools can compare themselves to peers via school report cards. The system also extracts historical data from StatEduc into DHIS2 for trend analysis, and supports individual-level data to track each learner's progress. The DHIS2 EMIS is currently under review by Togo's Ministry of Education. Contact: Edem Kossi, HISP WCA.
