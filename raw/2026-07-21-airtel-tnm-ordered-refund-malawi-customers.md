# Airtel, TNM ordered to refund Malawi customers

The Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) has ordered Airtel Malawi and Telekom Networks Malawi (TNM) to compensate subscribers for breaching mandatory tariff publication rules.

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa

July 21, 2026

2 Min Read

Airtel Malawi Plc and TNM are accused of not fully complying with the requirements of Section 76 of the Communications Act, 2016. (Source: Image by www.slon.pics on Magnific)

The Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) has ordered Airtel Malawi Plc and Telekom Networks Malawi (TNM) to compensate subscribers for introducing new tariffs without fully complying with legal requirements.

The regulator conducted a review and found that while the changes were approved following a comprehensive regulatory assessment, Airtel Malawi and TNM did not fully comply with the requirements of Section 76 of the Communications Act, 2016.

MACRA said that under the law, licensed operators are required to publish approved tariff changes in at least two daily newspapers seven days before the revised prices take effect.

"The purpose of this requirement is ensure transparency, provide consumers with adequate notice of tariff changes and allow them sufficient time to make informed decisions before revised prices are implemented," MACRA's statement explained.

It ordered the operators to compensate anyone affected by the price rises during the mandatory seven-day notice period.

"The compensation is intended to remedy the impact of the premature implementation of the revised tariffs and restore consumers to the position they would have been in had the statutory requirement been observed," it continued.

## African telcos ordered to compensate consumers

African regulators have increasingly cracked down on telecom operators, ordering them to compensate consumers for poor service quality and unauthorized tariff increases.

Just last week, the Guinean government called on Orange Guinea to strengthen network quality, expand coverage and lower Internet costs in support of the country's digital development goals.

Chad's government warned Moov Africa Chad and Airtel Chad that they need to improve network performance in June 2026, amid frustrations over the deteriorating quality of mobile services across the country.

In other countries, telcos took it upon themselves to improve the network.

Telecom Namibia in May 2026 warned its subscribers of potential network disruptions as it continued a network modernization program.

Efforts to improve the network also saw the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC), in March 2026, ordering all mobile network operators to compensate subscribers in the event of poor network coverage.

Other African countries resorted to fining telcos who were repeat offenders.

In July 2025, the Zambia Information and Communications Technology Authority (ZICTA), mandated Airtel to compensate customers after a network outage caused extensive disruption.

The regulator compelled Airtel Zambia to reimburse customers after a network outage, with a total compensation value of 4 million Zambian kwacha (approximately $142,500 at the time).
