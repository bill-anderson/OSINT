The Electoral Commission of Uganda (EC) is planning a new tender to select a procurement partner for biometric verification gear for the 2026 general elections. This follows President Yoweri Museveni's insistence that no voting should take place without such a system, which he believes will fight electoral fraud. Regulatory changes were initiated, the Minister of Justice and Constitutional Affairs Norbert Mao said two months earlier.

The EC is considering a redo of an initial plan to acquire 10,000 biometric verification devices and related software (bidding announced in March, bids due April 14). Spokesman Julius Mucunguzi confirmed a fresh procurement process. The initial process was deemed not comprehensive enough because the EC had expected access to biometric devices owned by the Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS) and NIRA. A request to use UBOS devices was rejected in April by the Legal and Parliamentary Affairs Committee; the UBOS devices, used in the 2024 census, suffered glitches and short battery life. Concerns persist over limited connectivity in remote areas.

These developments come as Uganda carries out its mass registration and renewal for a second-generation biometric ID. Digital rights lawyer Morgan Muhindo (writing in The Observer) argued that while DPI like national IDs can enhance access, poor legal frameworks and exclusionary system designs can enable serious human rights violations, urging a human-rights-based approach.
