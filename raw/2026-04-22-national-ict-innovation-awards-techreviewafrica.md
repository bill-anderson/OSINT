Malawi showcases digital transformation progress at National ICT Innovation Awards

Malawi has underscored its commitment to advancing digital transformation during the National ICT Innovation Awards held in Lilongwe, with government officials highlighting growing momentum in the country’s technology-driven development agenda.

The event, officially opened by Minister Dr. Shadric Namalomba, brought together policymakers, industry stakeholders, and innovators to celebrate achievements in the ICT sector and promote innovation as a driver of economic growth.

Secretary for Information and Communications Technology Harold Msusa, who participated in the event, said the awards reflect a broader shift in Malawi’s digital strategy from policy ambition to practical implementation.

He noted that beyond recognising excellence, the awards serve as a platform for nurturing young innovators, supporting local talent development, and positioning Malawi as a competitive player in the global digital economy.

The event also attracted senior government officials, including Cabinet Ministers Alfred Gangata, Joel Chigona, and Mary Navicha, signalling strong political commitment to leveraging ICT for inclusive growth and job creation.

Officials emphasized that continued investment in innovation ecosystems and digital infrastructure is key to sustaining progress, with ICT increasingly seen as a central pillar in Malawi’s socio-economic development strategy.

The awards highlighted the government’s focus on building digital platforms and systems incrementally, as part of a long-term effort to transform the economy through technology and innovation.
