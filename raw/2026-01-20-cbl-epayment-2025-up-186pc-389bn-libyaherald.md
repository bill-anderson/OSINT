The **Central Bank of Libya (CBL)** reported that transactions using e-payment methods increased **186% in 2025**, reaching **LD 389 billion** compared to LD 136 billion in 2024. The growth accompanies CBL Governor **Naji Issa's** monetary reforms: mandating e-payments for all public and private revenue collectors, reducing e-payment commissions, recalling old LD 5/20 notes by 30 September 2025 to fight cash hoarding, issuing Sharia-compliant Certificates of Deposit, and licensing official FX bureaux. The withdrawal of old denominations exacerbated the cash crisis but forced increased e-banking use.
