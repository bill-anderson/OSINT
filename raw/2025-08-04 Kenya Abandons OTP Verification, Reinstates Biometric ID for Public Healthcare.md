Kenya has discontinued **One-Time Password (OTP)** verification for patients in its public healthcare system, reinstating **biometric identification** to reduce fraud and improve service delivery — announced by the **[[social-health-authority-kenya|Social Health Authority (SHA)]]**, which manages the Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF).

The SHA had introduced OTP-based authentication in 2024 during the transition from the defunct NHIF, but it drew criticism: rural patients struggled with poor mobile coverage, over 60% of health facilities reported OTP verification delays, and it was susceptible to fraud via manipulated phone numbers. The Ministry of Health has begun distributing biometric authentication devices (fingerprint and facial, linked to a central database for real-time validation); Health CS Aden Duale said biometric checks are now mandatory at all Level 4-6 hospitals, with rollout to lower-tier facilities to follow.
