CSquared, a pan-African wholesale fibre company with roots in Google's "Project Link," is betting $125 million that open-access, carrier-neutral fibre networks can deliver affordable, reliable broadband across the continent. Launched in 2011 in Uganda and spun off in 2017 with backing from Google, Mitsui & Co, Convergence Partners and the IFC, CSquared builds shared "highways of connectivity" and leases capacity rather than each operator building duplicative infrastructure.

The company has laid over 7,500 kilometres of fibre across Uganda, Ghana, Liberia, Kenya, the DRC and Togo, powering more than 100 ISPs and mobile operators. Following a $25 million injection in late 2023 (part of a broader $65 million programme), CSquared is expanding beyond city grids toward longer-haul regional fibre "superhighways." (Uganda is CSquared's founding market and home base.)
