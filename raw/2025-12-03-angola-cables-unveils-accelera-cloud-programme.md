Angola Cables has announced the launch of the Accelera Cloud Program, an initiative designed to support small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and startups by providing free cloud credits and migration assistance.

The programme, unveiled on November 28, aims to accelerate digital transformation for businesses in Angola and across the African continent by removing financial and technical barriers to cloud adoption. According to the company, the initiative reflects its commitment to making advanced digital infrastructure more accessible to emerging and growing enterprises.

Through the Accelera Cloud Program, participating businesses will receive vouchers offering free credits to access a range of services on Clouds2Africa—Angola Cables' cloud platform, which is billed as the largest cloud infrastructure on the continent. These services include virtual machines, storage, backup solutions and other essential cloud resources.

In addition to the free credits, Angola Cables is offering complimentary support and full migration assistance to help SMEs and startups move from on-premise systems or other cloud environments to Clouds2Africa. The company notes that the entire process can be completed with payments in local currency, easing financial planning for businesses.

Angola Cables says the programme eliminates the financial risks typically associated with transitioning to the cloud, while reducing businesses' exposure to currency fluctuations that often impact the cost of cloud computing. By providing cost-free access and technical guidance, the company hopes to empower more African businesses to modernize their operations and take advantage of scalable, secure, and efficient cloud solutions.
