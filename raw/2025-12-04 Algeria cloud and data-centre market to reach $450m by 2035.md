# Algeria cloud and data-centre market to reach $450m by 2035

Per **DC Market Insights**, Algeria's data-centre/cloud market grows from **~$218m (2025) to ~$447m by 2035** (CAGR 7.39%), driven by government-led digitisation, 5G, enterprise modernisation and the country's first AI data centre. **No global hyperscaler** operates a data centre in Algeria, and **strict data-localisation** policies create a protected local market.

- **Digital sprint:** 500+ digital-transformation projects across 2025–26 (~75% digitising public services) under **"Digital Algeria 2030"** ([[digital-algeria-2030]]; target 100% of public services online by 2030), overseen by the **[[high-commission-digitalisation-algeria|High Commission for Digitalisation]]**. A 2024 report showed 67% of procedures dematerialised; foreign-trade clearance cut from 15 days to three.
- **Oran AI data centre:** ground broken March 2025 — state-owned, GPU/HPC, supporting the **7%-of-GDP-by-2027 AI target**. Algérie Télécom set up a ~$11m fund for AI/cybersecurity/robotics startups.
- **5G:** licences awarded to **Mobilis, Djezzy, Ooredoo** in December 2025 (8 pilot provinces; nationwide within six years); Mobilis tests reached 1.2 Gbps.
- **Connectivity:** **[[algerie-telecom|Algérie Télécom]]** passed **2.9m FTTH subscribers** by late 2025 (from 53,000 in 2020); copper to be phased out by 2027; a new **Sparkle (Italy)** subsea-cable partnership adds redundancy alongside **Medusa** (live 2026).
- **Digital banking:** Bank of Algeria **Instruction 02-2025** (Mar 2025) set licensing rules for fully digital banks; card penetration remains low (credit 2.8%, debit 22.9%, 2023).
