# Egypt launches DHIS2 in its national health information system

Egypt's **Ministry of Health and Population (MoHP)** officially launched **[[dhis2|DHIS2]]** as part of its national health information system (high-level meeting 18 Jan 2026; Deputy Minister **Dr Abla Alfi**, Assistant Minister for Digital Transformation **Dr Amr Ayd**), with **UNICEF Egypt**, the **HISP Centre (University of Oslo)**, HISP MENA and the Royal Norwegian Embassy. The open-source platform (used in 80+ countries) is framed to ensure **national ownership of health data, governance and privacy compliance**, supporting Egypt's **National Digital Health Strategic Priorities 2025–2029**.

First-year results: 180,000+ consultations for emergency evacuees from Gaza (189 sites); nutrition screening for ~20,000 Sudanese refugee children; school-health screening for 100,000+ children; TB follow-up for 7,500 cases (57 centres). A national DHIS2 training programme (HISP MENA, MoHP, UNICEF) began at launch. Tied to **[[egypt-vision-2030|Vision 2030]]**.
