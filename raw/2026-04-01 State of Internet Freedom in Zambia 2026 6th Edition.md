# State of Internet Freedom in Zambia — 2026 (6th Edition)

> ## Excerpt
> Bloggers of Zambia's annual digital-rights report, assessing 2025 ahead of the August 2026 elections —
> a contested new surveillance-enabling legal architecture, an operational data-protection regulator, and
> rising cyber-fraud.

## Key facts

- **Cyber Security Act No. 3 / Cyber Crimes Act No. 4 of 2025** (assented April 2025) mandate ISP
  real-time interception and a Central Monitoring Centre; a **CCTV (Public Protection) Bill** creates a
  National Public Security Command Centre. LAZ, MISA, GNI and the US Embassy warn of surveillance/chilling
  effects (arrests over posts; Munir Zulu's 18-month sedition sentence).
- The **Office of the Data Protection Commissioner** became operational (registration deadline 30 Apr
  2025) even as Cabinet plans to **repeal the 2021 Data Protection Act**. Digital ID (INRIS, ~1.3m
  enrolled) **deferred until after the 2026 elections**. Connectivity 33% penetration (govt claims 92%
  "connected"); DZAP ($100m World Bank). Rising fintech/deepfake fraud (~K111m losses); ZANACO ransomware
  claim. Rated "Partly Free" (62/100).
