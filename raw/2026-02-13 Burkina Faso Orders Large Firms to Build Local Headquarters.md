Burkina Faso adopted a decree on **12 February 2026** requiring large companies (annual revenue ≥ **CFA5 billion** over the past three fiscal years) to build their headquarters in-country — notably the three mobile operators. Construction standards scale by size across four categories: **Category A** (revenue ≥CFA100bn) must build at least a seven-storey (R+7) building with parking and energy-efficiency standards — **Moov Africa** and **Orange** qualify; **Telecel Faso** falls under Category C (CFA10–50bn), requiring at least R+4.

Per ARCEP 2024 revenue data: **Onatel CFA117.19bn**, **Orange CFA332.91bn**, **Telecel CFA40.44bn** — an economic-sovereignty/localisation measure, the built-environment counterpart to data localisation.
