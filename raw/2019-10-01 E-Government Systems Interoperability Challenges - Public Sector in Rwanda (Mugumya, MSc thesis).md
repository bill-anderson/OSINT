# E-Government Systems Interoperability Challenges: Public Sector in Rwanda (Mugumya, MSc thesis, 2019)

Companion source page for the binary artefact `2019-10-01 SYLVERE MUGUMYA Final Thesis book.pdf`
(cited as *Mugumya, Sylvere. 2019. "E-Government Systems Interoperability Challenges: Case of
Public Sector in Rwanda." MSc dissertation, University of Rwanda, College of Science and
Technology, School of ICT; supervisors Dr Shang Gao and Dr Said Rutabayiro Ngoga; submitted
October 2019*).

An original, first-hand **MSc dissertation** surveying e-Government systems interoperability across
eight Rwandan public agencies — [[nida-rwanda]] (whose **National Population Registry, NPR**, sat at
the centre of the graph with **seven integration points**), the Ministry of Local Government
(MINALOC), the Local Administrative Entities Development Agency (LODA), the City of Kigali, the
Rwanda Land Management and Use Authority (RLMUA), [[rura-rwanda]], the Rwanda Development Board (RDB)
and the Ministry of Public Service (MIFOTRA) — with [[rwanda-information-society-authority]] (RISA)
as the ICT authority. Based on 24 interviews (agency IT managers, non-IT users, RISA IT
specialists), coded against Scholl & Klischewski's (2007) nine interoperability barriers.

**Finding (as of 2019).** The top five interoperability challenges were **constitutional & legal**
(96% — no law obliging agencies to interoperate; integration remained each agency's own
initiative), **organizational** (88% — no common standards, guidelines, interoperability framework
or enterprise architecture), **collaborative** (58%), **technological** (58%) and **managerial**
(54% — no dedicated governance body between agencies). Jurisdictional, informational, cost and
performance barriers scored low. The central recommendation was a published **eGovernment
Interoperability Framework (eGIF)** and a standards/enterprise-architecture regime.

**Reference study (as of 2019). Cite, don't absorb** — a dated single-author academic baseline; its
percentages and integration-point counts are **not promoted** into synthesis pages and must never
corroborate a current claim. It predates the SDID rollout, [[ekash]] and the 2026
[[rwanda-dpi-strategy]], and establishes a **2019 interoperability/standards baseline** for
[[RWA]], [[dpi.exchange]] and [[dpi.govtech]]. Admitted on content per Bill's ISSUE-001 ruling
(single/student authorship is not a bar). See [[gov.standards]].
