A system to produce biometric driver's licences and vehicle registration cards is among several new digital service platforms introduced by the Malian government, presented to Prime Minister Abdoulaye Maiga. All were developed in Mali.

The Transport ministry's Sigui-Dolo issues secure transport documents and connects to a payments system (TrésorPay) and a customs platform (SYDONIA). A toll-gate digitisation system covers 10 of 33 gates; the ministry says revenue at one gate rose from XAF 30 million (~$53.7K) to XAF 60 million (~$107K). The Health Ministry's OpenClinic integrated health information system covers clinical, financial, pharmaceutical, laboratory and statistical aspects, with plans to link to the national identification system (NINA) and a compulsory health insurance scheme (AMO). The Ministry of Territorial Administration and Decentralisation presented SIGIP for data safety/confidentiality, association monitoring and public-servant attendance. Mali's birth registration is around 90 percent.
