The **[[lta-liberia|Liberia Telecommunications Authority (LTA)]]** and the **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** signed a Memorandum of Understanding to link SIM cards with government-issued IDs through biometric registration. The process is expected to complete by end of 2026, with a **10 July 2025 deadline** after which unregistered SIMs would be deactivated.

The project aims to register 3.5 million Liberians, beginning with a phase of free registration and verification. Under LTA Regulations, all SIM holders must register using the unique NIR-issued ID cards, with non-compliance within six months leading to SIM deactivation; the NIR has set up registration centres in every district and some townships. There are over 4 million SIM cards in use, and the NIR's centralised database can store over 7 million entries. LTA Acting Chairperson Abdullah L. Kamara said full digital transformation requires a functional database to protect subscribers from identity theft. A similar 2020 initiative reportedly failed due to low NIR enrolment and technical challenges; in May 2024 Liberia launched a Digital Transformation Project to train 10,000 young people in digital skills.
