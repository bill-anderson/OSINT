Reporting on The Global Findex Database 2025 (World Bank), showing how digital finance is reshaping Sub-Saharan African economies, with Uganda at the centre. In Uganda, along with Kenya and Ghana, "more than 20% of adults have borrowed money through mobile platforms" — phones are becoming sources of credit, not just payment tools. For many Ugandans, especially in rural and informal settings, mobile money is the backbone of financial life via agent networks and digital wallets.

Across developing economies, 61% of adults now use digital payments, a trend Uganda reflects through peer-to-peer transfers and small business transactions. Gaps remain: agricultural payments are still largely cash-based; smartphone and internet costs exclude many, especially in rural areas; and financial literacy and trust vary.
