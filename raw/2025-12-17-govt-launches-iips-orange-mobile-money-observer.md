# Gov't Launches IIPS with Orange Money, Mobile Money Integration

By Emery Watson — Liberian Observer, December 17, 2025

The government of Liberia has taken a decisive step toward a modern, cash-lite, and inclusive economy with the official launch of the Inclusive Instant Payment System (IIPS), a national mobile money interoperability platform designed to enable real-time, secure, and low-cost digital transactions. The system, formally launched on December 16, 2025, at the EJS Ministerial Complex in Congo Town, integrates Orange Money and MTN Mobile Money, creating a seamless nationwide digital payment ecosystem for individuals, businesses, and government institutions.

Speaking at the launch, President Boakai hailed the initiative as a national milestone: "The Inclusive Instant Payment System ensures that every Liberian — urban or rural, banked or unbanked — benefits from our nation's growth. Financial inclusion is not a privilege; it is a right. Through IIPS, farmers, entrepreneurs, students, healthcare workers, and civil servants will transact seamlessly, receive payments instantly, and participate fully in our economy."

CBL Executive Governor Henry F. Saamoi described the IIPS as a digital highway linking people, businesses, and government. "For decades, Liberia's financial system depended heavily on cash — a model that was costly, risky, and inefficient. The IIPS represents a major upgrade, enabling real-time payments, reducing cash reliance, and supporting innovation," he said. The system's national branding, "Pay Na Na – Inclusive. Instant. Liberia," reflects national ownership. The pilot phase currently supports Person-to-Person (P2P) and Government-to-People (G2P) payments, while integration of People-to-Government (P2G) transactions, including tax payments with the Liberia Revenue Authority, is underway.

A key highlight is the integration of Orange Money and MTN Mobile Money/Lonestar Cell. Ali Fakih, Deputy CEO of Lonestar Cell/MTN Liberia, called it "a major empowerment tool," and Jean-Marius Yao, CEO of Orange Money Liberia, said the interoperability "accelerates financial inclusion and innovation." Binta Massaquoi, Acting Country Manager for World Bank Liberia, described IIPS as "a public good." Saamoi emphasized robust cybersecurity safeguards: "Your money, your data, and your trust are sacred to us." The launch underscores collaboration among the CBL, commercial banks, mobile money operators, government agencies, and development partners including the World Bank, Gates Foundation, AfricaNenda, Mojaloop Foundation, and ThitsaWorks. The initiative incorporates the deployment of a full National Electronic Payment Switch (NEPS). Citing the SIIPS 2025 Report, 25 African countries now operate domestic instant payment systems.
