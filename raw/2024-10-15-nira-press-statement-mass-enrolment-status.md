## PRESS STATEMENT ON THE STATUS OF THE MASS ENROLMENT AND RENEWAL EXERCISE — 15TH OCTOBER 2024

### 1 STATUS UPDATE OF MASS ENROLMENT EXERCISE

The Registration of Persons Act 2015 established the National Identification and Registration Authority as a central registration body for the registration of all persons in Uganda; with the responsibility of establishing the National Identification Register of all persons. The Act harmonized and consolidated the law on registration of persons.

In August 2022, Cabinet approved the Mass Enrolment and Renewal Exercise with a Budget of UGX. 666.85 billion shillings. The exercise will enroll at least 17.2 million Ugandans and renew 15.8 million cards which expire 2024 – 2025 beginning August 2024. The breakdown of the budget was as follows;

| Item | Amount (UGX. billions) |
| --- | --- |
| System including software, hardware, operating systems and all supporting technologies | 293 |
| Blank Cards | 183 |
| Administration, fuel, transport, allowances for 13,000 registration assistants | 290.95 |
| TOTAL | 666.85 |

#### 1.1 RENEWAL OF EXPIRED CARDS

Beginning 24th August 2024 to 30th January 2025 up to 3.4 million are set to expire. On 12th August 2024, the Minister of Internal Affairs issued a statutory instrument number 67 – The Registration of Persons (Amendment Regulations 2024) extending the validity of National ID cards by one year from the date of expiry.

By virtue of this instrument, an expired identity card shall retain validity for an additional one year and may be used for purposes under Section 65 and 66 of the Registration of Persons Act e.g Government Services and Financial Transactions.

### 2 KEY INTERVENTIONS TO MITIGATE DELAYS IN START OF MASS ENROLMENT

#### 2.1 ELECTORAL COMMISSION ROAD MAP FOR 2026

NIRA embarked on a 53-day exercise that commenced on 27th September 2024 and will end on 23rd November 2024. This was in order to fulfill the Electoral Commission Road map requirements to submit the list of eligible voters from the National Identification Register by 30th November 2024.

The goal of the exercise is to ensure that all eligible voters, which includes persons 17 years and above, are registered and issued with National ID's before 30th November 2024. This exercise does NOT exclude Ugandan citizens below the age of 17 from registering but simply places an emphasis or focus on eligible voters. Therefore all Ugandans are welcome to register.

The Ministry of Internal Affairs is carrying out the exercise in conjunction with Electoral Commission, Ministry for Presidency (through RDC's and RCC's), Ministry of Education (through Schools), DISO's/GISO's, Ministry of Local Government and the entire local leadership structure including the LC 1 Chairpersons and all other stakeholders.

To facilitate the participation of eligible citizens, NIRA is conducting this registration exercise through mobile outreaches at Sub County headquarters and in the respective parishes, schools, places of worship and other public places.

NIRA District teams will publish and share schedules with the district and sub county leadership and mobile registration teams. Together with district teams the information will be disseminated to the public through the radios, the office of the RDC/RCC's and other local leaders to enable the communities know when the NIRA Teams will be in their area weekly, bi-weekly and monthly intervals.

During this exercise, all applicants are urged to observe the following:

1. The exercise is conducted every day within this period, including weekends, starting at 8:00 am and ending at 5:00 pm.
2. The registration exercise is free of charge for new registrations but persons seeking change of particulars and other services that attract fees will pay the prescribed fees.
3. This Mobile Registration Exercise is being carried out from schools, places of worship, Sports grounds and other public places in the entire country.
4. The NIRA District teams will share schedules for the mobile registration teams, with the public through the Radios, RDC's/RCC's, and the local leadership to enable communities know locations in time.
5. All Ugandan citizens who have never registered for National Identity Cards should appear for registration.
   a. Applicants of 18 years and above (adult), will be required to present an introduction letter signed by the LC 1 Chairperson, and stamped by the Internal Security Officers; the introduction letter must indicate the applicant's name, date of birth, tribe, clan, and nationality; the letter should further indicate parents' names, tribes, clans, nationality, and living status (alive or deceased or unknown)
   b. Applicants who are below the age of 18 years will be required to present a photocopy of at least one of the parent's National ID Card or a copy of the National ID Card of a blood relative, where both parents are deceased.
   - Parents and guardians are advised to provide details that will facilitate the registration of persons below 18 years. This includes date of birth, place of origin, and village of residence, among others.
6. Furthermore, during this countrywide outreach exercise, all citizens who were registered from their schools and communities, while still below the age of 16 years, and have since attained the age of 16 years, and above, will be able to present their National Identification Number (NIN), or Application ID or the copy of their registration form, or their names and date of birth, for data update to facilitate the processing of their National ID Cards. No other requirement is needed.
7. All Ugandan citizens who registered with NIRA but have never received their National ID Cards are urged to use this exercise to check the status of their applications at the NIRA Registration centres.

We appeal to all applicants, guardians, and duty bearers to ensure the accuracy of data provided and avoid falsification of information as this is an offence and the person liable can be punished by imprisonment.

For more information about the mobile outreach exercise, please visit the NIRA website: https://www.nira.go.ug or the nearest NIRA offices or your LC 1 Chairperson. You can also contact NIRA using the toll free number: 0800 211700.

#### 2.2 CHALLENGES

1. The exercise coincides with the end of the academic year where school administrations and students are saddled by promotional exams. This has complicated the process of registering students who form the bulk of persons to be registered.

   There are 27,4 million registered persons in the National Identification Register. The persons in age bracket 0-17 years are 6.3million representing approximately 22.1% of persons registered. The National Population stands at 45.9 million and the same age bracket accounts 59.7% of Uganda's population.

2. Every region of the country is experiencing heavy rainfall. This has negatively impacted the exercise as follows;
   a. Movement of kits and other registration resources within the districts is being impeded.
   b. Congregation of persons for registration and enrolment has been affected.
   c. Most districts are experiencing intermittent power and in some case after a heavy downpour prolonged hours of power outage.
   d. Internet connectivity is affected by power intermittency and outages and in some areas

3. Because of the delays in receiving the new equipment, the old equipment is being used during the intervention. Therefore, clients continue to experience slow service delivery and delays.

### 3 WAY FORWARD

The Ministry of Internal Affairs and NIRA will continue to strive to deliver the defined goals of the Mass Enrolment and Renewal Exercise to enroll 17.2 million unregistered Ugandans; and renew the National Identity Cards of 15.8 million Ugandans within the 2024/25 financial year.

We call upon all Ugandans to take this opportunity to register and be identified as a citizen of our beautiful country Uganda: The Pearl of Africa.
