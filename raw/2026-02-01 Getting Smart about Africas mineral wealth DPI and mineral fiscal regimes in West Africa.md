# Getting Smart about Africa's mineral wealth (ODI)

> ## Excerpt
> Bright Simons argues West African states can close the mining policy–practice gap ("Katanomics") with
> **DPI for mineral fiscal governance**: computable **"Rules as Code,"** a version-controlled **Digital
> Gazette**, and a sovereign **National Data Cloud** asserting ownership of "Geological IP" against
> foreign AI "mirror geology" extraction — reframed as **"Smart Sovereignty."**

> **Provenance note.** Single-author ODI working paper; fiscal figures are the author's own modelling. The
> curator's "Royale Protocol" term does **not** appear — it corresponds to the National Data Cloud +
> chain-of-use/chain-of-benefit B2B data-royalty model (detailed in a companion paper).

## Key facts

- Case study: **Ghana's Ewoyaa Lithium** (Atlantic Lithium) shift from a fixed 10% royalty to a
  price-pegged sliding scale (Minerals & Mining (Royalty) Regulations 2025); Simons dissects a "Cliff
  Effect" rewarding under-invoicing and proposes a smoothing algorithm. Upstream: a sovereign geological-
  data cloud licensed to AI firms via a **chain-of-use/chain-of-benefit** royalty. Proposes a low-stakes
  **feldspar pilot** as a WAEMU/ECOWAS/AfCFTA sandbox.
