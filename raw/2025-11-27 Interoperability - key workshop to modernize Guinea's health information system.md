A workshop on technical skills-transfer for the interoperability of health information systems opened in Conakry on Tuesday 25 November 2025 (running 25-28 November). Organized by the Terre des Hommes foundation with the Ministry of Health and Public Hygiene (MSHP) and financial support from Enabel via the Wehubit mechanism, it is part of the national drive to modernize the health sector and strengthen the Système National d'Information Sanitaire (SNIS).

Participants included Terre des Hommes Guinea, ICT4D/ICT teams from Lausanne and Dakar, MSHP technical staff from the Bureau des Stratégies et Développement (BSD) and the Service de Modernisation du Système d'Information (SMSI), and the ANSS. Dr Aminata Camara (Terre des Hommes) said the workshop falls under the Wehubit "improving data governance in Africa (DGA)" project, focused on data governance for the REC maternité tool — an electronic maternity consultation register piloted in 15 health centres to support midwives.

A consultant (Kaba Almamy Karamokoba) presented a mapping of Guinea's health-sector digital tools, stressing the need to avoid duplication; aggregated individual data would feed the SNIS for better decisions. Recommendations: strengthen data security and governance, support Guinean technical teams for project continuity, and use open-source technologies for sustainability. Dr Abdoul Karim Nabé (MSHP) framed it as centralizing all national health information in a single system.
