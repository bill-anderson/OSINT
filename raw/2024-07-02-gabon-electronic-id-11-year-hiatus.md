Gabon has launched its National Electronic Identity Card (CNIE) after an 11-year hiatus since its last ID card issuance. Citizens can now apply for the national digital ID card, issued initially only in Libreville with plans to expand to all police stations nationwide.

The development follows a $68.5M loan agreement between Gabon and the World Bank for the "Digital Gabon" project. The government plans to make the card usable for travel within CEMAC (six Central African countries); since 2008 a policy has allowed visa-free travel within the region for holders of the CEMAC biometric passport, though implementation has been ineffective. The digital ID is valid for banking and administrative procedures.

In May 2024, Gabon initiated a civil registry issuance campaign in the Libreville region, establishing registration centres for citizens to obtain their Numéro d'Identification Personnel (NIP), necessary for the CNIE. History: in 2012 Gabon launched a biometric national registry project awarded to Thales Digital Identity Security (France); the first ID cards were issued in 2013 but faced delays; Belgian firm Semlex was contracted to issue the cards but the contract was reportedly terminated. Reports indicate Thales is taking over the contract, setting up equipment and training personnel.
