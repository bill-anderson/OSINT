Nigeria's National Identity Management Commission (NIMC) launched a nationwide campaign on 16 February 2026 to conduct National Identification Number (NIN) enrollment at the ward level — the grassroots units for governance, electoral organization and resource distribution. The effort targets all wards in the 774 Local Government Areas across the 36 states and the FCT, to expand digital ID coverage and improve inclusion.

"The nationwide exercise is designed to ensure the comprehensive registration of every Nigerian citizen and legal resident, including children and adults," said NIMC Director for Communications and Public Relations Dr Kayode Adegoke, adding that enrollment is free of charge under President Tinubu's renewed hope agenda. He said the initiative decentralizes services and brings registration to communities, reducing travel burden. In prelude, NIMC engaged state governments, heads of local governments, traditional rulers, community leaders, market associations and faith-based organisations.

Nigeria's national digital ID program is funded by the World Bank, the European Investment Bank and the French Development Agency at around $430 million. Last year the program was extended by two years after Nigeria failed to meet key objectives; the revised target requires 180 million NINs by December 2026. By the close of 2025, NIMC said it had registered around 127 million people. In December 2025 NIMC received 350 biometric devices from Korea (via KOICA) to boost enrollment in difficult-to-reach communities.
