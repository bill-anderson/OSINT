# Bridging the digital divide: ICT adoption among Ghanaian headteachers (Wenchi)

Peer-reviewed empirical study (UTAUT framework; structured questionnaire, n=200 headteachers) of ICT adoption for **school management** in Wenchi Municipality, Ghana. Finds basic ICT is used for communication/record-keeping but advanced tools (financial and **data-management systems**) remain limited, with a pronounced **urban–rural divide**: rural headteachers face unreliable internet, limited electricity and insufficient technical support despite initiatives like One-Teacher-One-Laptop. Recommends infrastructure investment, targeted professional development and ongoing technical support. Small, single-municipality sample — a ground-level data point on the education-sector digital divide and the [[dpi.mis]] (EMIS/school-data) capacity gap, not a national statistic.
