A year after warning that unpaid government bills were choking its operations, state-owned TelOne says outstanding government debt has more than doubled to nearly US$42 million (from US$19.2 million a year earlier), constraining its ability to invest in network expansion and modernisation.

Per TelOne's 2025 annual report, the swelling receivables have limited capital projects and network maintenance. CEO Lawrence Nkala, speaking at the AGM, described the debt as a major operational constraint: "Currently, we do have close to US$42 million that we're owed, ZiG1.2 billion, and it continues to increase." He said TelOne needs equity funding, not only debt financing.

Despite the debt burden, demand grew: revenue increased 10% to ZiG2.6 billion, driven mainly by a 60% increase in data usage. TelOne also reported strong growth from its Starlink partnership, generating US$3.7 million in revenue during 2025, up from US$215,000 in 2024. Its data centre business also continued to grow, providing revenue diversification. ICT ministry chief director Prince Sibanda represented minister Tatenda Mavetera at the AGM.
