# Cameroon Fines Orange and MTN USD 4.6 Million for Poor Service and Regulatory Breaches

The Cameroon Telecommunications Regulatory Board (ART) has taken decisive action against the country's two largest mobile operators — Orange Cameroon and MTN Cameroon — by imposing significant financial penalties totaling CFA 2.6 billion (approximately USD 4.6 million).

Following a series of inspections conducted in April and May 2024, the ART found both companies to be in violation of several key regulatory standards, particularly in relation to network coverage and service quality. As a result, Orange Cameroon was fined CFA 1.5 billion (approx. USD 2.5 million), while MTN Cameroon was issued a penalty of CFA 1.1 billion (approx. USD 1.8 million).

In addition to the service quality violations, Orange Cameroon received an extra fine of CFA 217 million (approx. USD 357,600) for failing to comply with pricing regulations and inadequate safeguards for unsubscribing from value-added services (VAS). These VAS issues have long been a source of public complaint, as subscribers often find themselves unknowingly charged for services they did not actively choose or are unable to opt out of.

The ART stated that these penalties are part of a broader initiative to enforce compliance and hold operators accountable for the quality of services (QoS) they provide to Cameroon citizens. The regulator emphasized that subpar service delivery — such as dropped calls, slow internet, and inconsistent network availability — hinders national digital development and affects both consumer experience and business productivity.

This move sends a strong message to telecom operators across the country: non-compliance with national regulations and failure to meet service obligations will result in substantial consequences. It also reflects growing public demand for better accountability and transparency from service providers operating in the increasingly vital telecom sector.

Both Orange and MTN Cameroon are yet to issue public responses to the penalties.
