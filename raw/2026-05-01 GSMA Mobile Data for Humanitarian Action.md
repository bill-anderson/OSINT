# Mobile Data for Humanitarian Action

> ## Excerpt
> A GSMA M4H report on the governance conditions under which mobile network operator (CDR) data can be
> used responsibly for humanitarian decision-making — shifting the question from *whether* to *under what
> governance*. Global-LMIC in scope; Ghana is the one African headline case (Chile, Philippines the
> others).

> **Provenance note.** GSMA industry report; restated global figures should not be pushed into concept/
> place pages as fresh data (per the reference-study note). The genuinely wiki-actionable content is the
> **Ebola cross-border divergence** and the Ghana/DRC cases.

## Key facts (African-relevant)

- **Divergent national rules fragment cross-border analysis:** during Ebola (2014–16), MNOs in **Guinea
  and Sierra Leone** shared CDRs, but **Liberia's** more restrictive law blocked equivalent access — the
  canonical case for how African data-protection divergence shapes humanitarian data use.
- **Ghana** case: Telecel Ghana + Ghana Statistical Service + Flowminder (since 2018), pseudonymised CDRs
  used for 2023 floods and 2025 conflict displacement, feeding NADMO/Ghana Health Service; Ghana shared
  lessons with **Nigeria and Somalia** NSOs. **DRC**: Vodacom CDRs tracked eastern-conflict displacement
  (380 health zones) for cash transfers. **Togo**: CDR-based cash-transfer targeting cut exclusion errors.
- Five data-sharing models (favouring "algorithms-to-data" keeping raw data inside MNO systems);
  governance takes 12–18 months to establish. GDPR exerts a global "Brussels effect" on MNO practice.
