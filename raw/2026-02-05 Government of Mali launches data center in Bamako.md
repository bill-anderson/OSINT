The government of Mali inaugurated a new Tier III-standard data centre in Bamako on 31 January 2026. Minister of Communication, Digital Economy and Administrative Modernization Alhamdou Ag Ilyène said it is "a decisive step toward safeguarding and securing national data, helping ensure digital sovereignty and technological independence."

The facility offers 99.982 percent service availability with full redundancy of critical equipment; exact location, size and capacity were not shared. The government is undergoing a "gradual process" of repatriating data hosted outside the country. Previous reports suggested Huawei was working with Mali on a Tier III data centre as part of "Mali Numérique" (which also included installing 1,000 km of fibre-optic cabling), but Huawei was not mentioned in reports of this launch. The project is part of the Alliance of Sahel States digital-infrastructure push (Burkina Faso recently launched two mini data centres; Niger plans high-capacity data centres).
