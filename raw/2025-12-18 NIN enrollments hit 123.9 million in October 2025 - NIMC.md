Enrollments for the National Identification Number by Nigerians at home and abroad rose to 123.9 million in October 2025, per NIMC's latest data. At the end of 2024 the figure stood at 114 million, meaning 9.9 million new NINs were issued in the first 10 months of the year.

Lagos maintained the lead with over 13.1 million cumulative enrolments, followed by Kano (11.5 million) and Kaduna (7.3 million, displacing Ogun which had 5.1 million). By gender, 69.7 million (56.25%) of those captured are male and 54.2 million (43.75%) female.

The October figure remains far from target: in May 2025 NIMC DG Abisoye Coker-Odusote declared an ambitious plan to register 95% of Nigerians (about 190 million of an estimated 200 million) before December 2025 via a Ward Enrollment Exercise powered by improved infrastructure, expanded centres and public sensitization.

Context: Under the World Bank ID4D project, Nigeria missed the deadline to capture at least 148 million Nigerians by 30 June (last year), leading to restructuring and a two-year extension to 30 June 2026 to guarantee full disbursement of the $430 million co-financed by AFD and the EIB.
