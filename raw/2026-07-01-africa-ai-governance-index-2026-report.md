# Africa AI Governance Index (AAIGI) — Inaugural Report, 2026 Edition

**Published by** the Africa AI Policy Lab, an initiative of **Lawyers Hub**. Nairobi, Kenya, **July
2026**. Made possible by the **Patrick J. McGovern Foundation**. Reproducible in whole or part for
non-commercial purposes with acknowledgement.

**Suggested citation:** Africa AI Policy Lab, Lawyers Hub. (2026). *Africa AI Governance Index
(AAIGI): Inaugural Report, 2026 Edition.* Nairobi: Lawyers Hub.

**Disclaimer (report's own):** findings, interpretations and conclusions are those of the Africa AI
Policy Lab and Lawyers Hub, and do not necessarily reflect the views of the Patrick J. McGovern
Foundation or of contributors' affiliated institutions. **Country assessments reflect publicly
available evidence as at 30 June 2026.** Sub-regional groupings follow the geographic classification
of the **UN Economic Commission for Africa**.

## Scope and method

- **54 African states assessed**, **8 weighted pillars**, **80 indicators per state**, **4,320
  observations scored**. Scale is **0–4**.
- Pillars: **P1 Strategy & Vision · P2 Governance & Regulation · P3 Infrastructure & Data ·
  P4 Human Capital · P5 Innovation & Ecosystem · P6 Ethics & Inclusion · P7 Regional Integration ·
  P8 Implementation & Impact**.
- Methodology at Appendix A. Contents: Continental Overview (§2); Sub-regional results for North,
  West, Central, East and Southern Africa (§3); alignment with the AU Continental AI Strategy Phase 1
  (§4); Cross-Cutting Themes (§5); Recommendations (§6).

## The report's own headline findings

- **Implementation decoupling is universal.** Every sub-region scores **highest on Strategy & Vision
  and lowest on Implementation & Impact** — strategy diffusion consistently outpaces enforceable
  laws, ring-fenced budgets and measurable oversight.
- **The continent sits in the early-development band.** Sub-regional averages on the 0–4 scale:
  **North Africa 1.56**, **Southern Africa 1.49**, **East Africa 1.27**, **West Africa 1.25**,
  **Central Africa 1.07**. **No sub-region has crossed the midpoint** of the maturity scale.
- **Infrastructure concentration persists.** Africa holds **under 1% of global data-centre capacity**
  while hosting **18% of the world's population**, concentrated in South Africa, Egypt, Kenya,
  Nigeria and Morocco.
- **Talent scarcity is structural.** Africa accounts for an estimated **3% of the world's AI talent**;
  Human Capital is the weakest or near-weakest pillar in every sub-region.
- **Diaspora talent as strategic reserve.** An estimated **70,000 skilled professionals leave Africa
  each year**, with AI/ML specialists the likeliest to be recruited abroad.
- **Foreign dependence cuts both ways** — structural dependence on foreign hyperscalers for cloud,
  GPUs and subsea cables expands capacity while deepening data-sovereignty risk.
- **A regulatory patchwork governs AI.** **Over 70% of assessed countries have enacted data
  protection laws**, but dedicated enforceable AI legislation remains rare; most rely on indirect
  cybersecurity or digital codes lacking mandates such as algorithmic impact assessments.
- **Innovation is outrunning safety.** An estimated **US$1.25bn in AI venture capital since 2019**,
  but **over 80% concentrated in four economies**.
- **Continental aspirations outpace domestic law** — the AU Continental AI Strategy, the Kigali
  Africa Declaration on AI and the EAC's 2026 ministerial Declaration supply political momentum
  without domestic transposition.

## Regional pathways to sovereignty (the report's framing)

The report argues **sub-regional averages, not country ranks, carry the analytical weight**:
composite differences below ~0.1 are not meaningful, and the rubric **rewards adopted formal
instruments over ecosystem size or capability** — so a rank order "measures formalisation, not
capability". It states that **the sub-region is the minimum viable sovereignty unit**, since
sovereignty over compute, data and rules is not attainable at national scale for most of the
continent.

- **North Africa (1.56) — a production pathway.** Morocco's sovereign compute buildout **exceeding
  900MW combined** and Egypt's data-centre growth make it the continent's only potential **net
  compute exporter**; Mauritania is planned as a recipient of cross-border compute. Risk: no binding
  Arab Maghreb Union AI framework and no shared data-protection standard across the seven states.
- **West Africa (1.25) — a legal pathway.** Holds the continent's **only binding regional data
  instrument**, the **ECOWAS Supplementary Act on Personal Data Protection**, and the **highest
  Regional Integration average (1.51)**. Risk: an **Implementation & Impact average of 0.65, the
  lowest recorded anywhere in the Index**.
- **East Africa (1.27) — an institutional pathway, furthest advanced.** The **EAC ministers' 2026
  Declaration on AI**, a **Regional AI Technologies Fund**, the **EAC AI Alliance** and a regional
  **EAC AI Strategy (2026–2031)** amount to pooled sovereignty by treaty. Risk: bilateral MoUs with
  foreign partners operating outside regional oversight.
- **Central Africa (1.07) — pools by necessity.** No state has scale; shared assets include **ARCAI
  in Brazzaville** and the Republic of the Congo's sovereign data infrastructure.
- **Southern Africa (1.49) — harmonisation or fragmentation.** Highest continental averages on
  **Infrastructure & Data (1.72)** and **Ethics & Inclusion (1.48)**, but **no binding SADC AI
  framework**, and Angola's extraterritorial draft shows divergence underway. The report calls a
  **SADC model AI law** "the cheapest sovereignty intervention available on the continent".

**Its central structural claim:** every pathway breaks at the same link — **no REC framework has yet
been converted into national implementing legislation at scale**, and repairing that transmission
mechanism, "not drafting further strategies", is the binding task for the remainder of Phase 1.
