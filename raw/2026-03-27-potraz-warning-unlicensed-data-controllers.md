POTRAZ has issued a stern warning to organisations processing personal data without the requisite licence, noting widespread non-compliance despite the lapse of the March 2025 deadline. The warning follows the promulgation of the Cyber and Data Protection (Licensing of Data Controllers and Appointment of Data Protection Officers) Regulations, 2024 — Statutory Instrument 155 of 2024 — in September 2024, which established a mandatory requirement to obtain a Data Controller Licence by 12 March 2025.

As the designated Data Protection Authority under Section 5 of the Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07], POTRAZ observed that some organisations continue to process personal information without authorisation. "It is against this background that the Authority issues this Notice to all unlicensed organisations processing personal information to comply with the statutory licensing obligation," POTRAZ stated, demanding entities regularise their activities "with immediate effect" by applying for or renewing a licence.

The requirement applies to non-exempt natural and legal persons permanently established in Zimbabwe, including organisations using surveillance or biometric systems. POTRAZ framed licensing as "not just a statutory obligation" but "a demonstration of your commitment to safeguarding personal data." Unlicensed entities were urged to apply online through the dedicated licensing portal.
