# EIB Grants $100M to AXIAN Telecom for 4G/5G Rollout in Tanzania and Madagascar

The **European Investment Bank (EIB Global)** announced a financing package of **US$100 million to AXIAN Telecom** to support the expansion of its mobile broadband network infrastructure across Madagascar and Tanzania. The project will expand 4G mobile broadband network infrastructure across the two countries as well as continuing the introduction of 5G coverage.

This new Global Gateway investment, backed by a budgetary guarantee of the European Commission, will enhance access to high-speed communications, accelerate inclusive digitalisation, and drive sustainable development across the two countries.

AXIAN Telecom is a leading pan-African telecommunications company that currently serves over **44 million subscribers** and is present in nine Sub-Saharan African countries, with its key mobile and fixed operations being in Tanzania, Madagascar, Senegal, Togo and Comoros. **US$60 million of the financing will benefit Tanzania and US$40 million will go to Madagascar.** AXIAN Telecom operates under the **Yas** brand in both countries.

The financing is intended to expand mobile phone infrastructure, pave the way for socio-economic growth and digital inclusion, and help reduce geographic inequality of telecom access. The current project supports the objectives of the EU's Digital4Development (D4D) initiative and feeds into the Global Gateway strategy for Africa, which seeks to mobilise up to €150 billion by 2030 for physical and digital infrastructure across the continent.
