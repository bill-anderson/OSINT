South African real estate firm the Cavaleros Group is looking to move into the data center sector with plans for two campuses. The company has launched a new unit, Cosmas Data Cities, which plans to develop two sites in Johannesburg and Cape Town.

The Johannesburg Cosmas Data City in the Samrand area of the city could offer 200MW via a 450MW self-built switching station. The 100-acre Cape Town development could total 360MW. Further details on location, campus specifications, or development timelines weren't shared.

Cavaleros is working with Knight Frank and data center engineering services providers Future-tech.

"For over five decades, the Cavaleros Group has built a legacy of vision, quality, and long-term partnerships. These data center developments continue that legacy – combining our real estate expertise with Knight Frank's industry insight and Future-Tech's technical excellence to deliver infrastructure that powers Africa's digital transformation," said Penny Cavaleros, CEO, the Cavaleros Group.

The company also said it has plans in the works for sites in East and West Africa, though didn't share details.

"South Africa holds immense importance as a gateway for hyperscalers, neoclouds, and global enterprises. With strong cloud regions and fiber connectivity, it is the continent's primary destination for large-scale deployments. Cavaleros has positioned itself as a clear market leader with unmatched expertise," said Oscar Matthews, head of EMEA data center investment & development, Knight Frank.

"Cavaleros' investment in a self-built switching station significantly enhances Johannesburg's infrastructure readiness – making it one of the most deployment-ready locations for hyperscale and AI investment in the region," added Will Clarke, international head of site selection & Development director, Future-tech.

The Cavaleros Group began in 1926, initially as a shopfitting company. Today, it is a property investment and development firm with investments across retail, industrial, hotel, and commercial sectors in South Africa.
