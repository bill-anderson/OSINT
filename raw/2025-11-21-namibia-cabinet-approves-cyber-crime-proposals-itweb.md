# Namibia cabinet approves fresh proposals to combat cyber crime

Namibia's cabinet has approved several measures to tackle rampant cyber crime, disclosed by ICT Minister Emma Theofelus (from a cabinet meeting chaired by President Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah), speaking at the ministry's Ohangwena regional office.

Cabinet approved the establishment of a government-sector cyber security incident response team "as the central authority for cyber security incidents coordination across government," plus security operations centres across government. It also approved developing cyber security skills and specialisation among government IT personnel through targeted training in cyber hygiene, incident response and threat intelligence. Cabinet directed the Office of the Prime Minister to coordinate phased implementation and report progress at regular intervals.

The country faces significant cyber security challenges: cyber threats, an incomplete legal framework and low public digital literacy. In October [2025], the government reported over 1 million cyber security-related instances detected in the first half of 2025 alone.
