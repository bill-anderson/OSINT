A peer-reviewed study of Cameroon's **digital-health data-services landscape** (governance, interoperability, platform adoption; mixed methods, n=60 survey + 6 expert interviews) found major governance and policy gaps: **85% of respondents unaware of any guidelines**, **86.7% felt policy support inadequate** (90.9% at central level); 63.3% had used digital platforms, 63.3% received formal training, **80% were aware of DHIS2**, but **45% reported interoperability challenges** with DHIS2. Evidence of weak health-data (HMIS) governance and interoperability in Cameroon.
