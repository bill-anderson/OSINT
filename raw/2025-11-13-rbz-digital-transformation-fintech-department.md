At the second edition of the Tech Convergence Fora, RBZ Deputy Governor Dr Innocent Matshe announced the establishment of a new Department of Digital Transformation and Fintech Innovation, designed to accelerate Zimbabwe's transition toward a fully digital economy. The department will drive implementation of technology-led financial solutions, strengthen innovation-friendly regulation, and keep the financial ecosystem current with the global fintech landscape.

Matshe cited the Fintech Regulatory Sandbox as a key example — allowing innovators to test products under supervision before scaling — and said the department will oversee creation of an Innovation Hub for developers, startups and financial institutions to co-design next-generation technologies. It will also play a pivotal role in implementing the National Financial Inclusion Strategy II, expanding digital financial services to underserved communities and shifting focus "from mere access to practical usage."

He reaffirmed the RBZ's commitment to cybersecurity resilience, aligned with the Ministry's broader cybersecurity framework, and hinted at exploration of Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs), cryptocurrency regulation and other emerging technologies as part of the bank's modernization roadmap, which could redefine cross-border transactions and boost financial inclusion.
