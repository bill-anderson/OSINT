# No National ID, No Transaction

By David A. Yates — Liberian Observer, April 30, 2025

Subhead: Gov't mandates biometric for all Liberians, foreign nationals, as Boakai issues Executive Order #147.

President Joseph Nyuma Boakai has issued Executive Order No. 147, making it mandatory for all Liberian citizens and foreign residents to enroll in the National Biometric Identification System (NBIS) and obtain a national identification card. The Executive Order, announced through a statement by the National Identification Registry (NIR) on Tuesday, April 29, 2025, mandates that all citizens and residents, as well as foreign visitors residing in Liberia for 90 days or more, must obtain either a National ID card or a Foreigner ID card. These cards serve as essential credentials for accessing a wide range of government and private-sector services, including banking, healthcare, education, and telecommunications.

Boakai pointed to growing challenges of double-dipping, illegal migration, and fraud. The NIR will work in close collaboration with the Liberia Telecommunications Authority (LTA), which has already enacted regulations requiring the registration of SIM cards through government-issued IDs verified on the NIR platform. The NIR is tasked with enrolling all citizens and residents into the NBIS and ensuring timely distribution of ID cards, facilitating identification and verification across government services, mobile network registration, banking, and healthcare.

Boakai emphasized that the NIR database should be accessible to all relevant government and private institutions — ministries such as Education, Health, Internal Affairs, and Finance, as well as commercial banks, mobile network operators, and other service providers. In recognition of potential privacy concerns, the Executive Order stresses that all data collection, storage, and management will comply with Liberia's Data Protection and Privacy Laws. The Order references the 2011 repeal of PRC Decree #65, which led to the creation of the NIR, and mandates the Registry to issue biometric cards containing a unique National Identification Number (NIN) to serve as the primary government-approved identity number for registering births, deaths, passports, immigration documents, bank accounts, driver's licenses, social security, and education.
