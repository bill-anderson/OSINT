World Bank publication (Malabo, 4 March 2025) — the World Bank's first-ever Country Economic Memorandum on Equatorial Guinea, arguing the country urgently needs a new development model as declining oil revenues and weak diversification produce a prolonged recession.

Digital-relevant findings: public revenues are overly oil/gas-dependent (~80% of total revenues); the regulatory environment is not sufficiently investment-friendly (legal uncertainty, land-titling challenges, limited credit, and inadequate digitalization of public services). Recommended steps to improve the business environment include improving the effectiveness of the one-stop shop, enabling online registration, and digitizing the Commercial Registry database. Reforms are situated within the National Sustainable Development Strategy 2021-2035 (AGENDA 2035) and its Economic Diversification Initiative, benchmarked against CEMAC peers. A new Social Protection Law envisioning universal coverage has been drafted but not yet approved.
