Asmara, Eritrea (published 8 May 2026) — Under the **Joint Programme on Data for
Development (D4D)**, the **Eritrean National Statistics Office (NSO)** demonstrated
accelerated momentum in 2025 in strengthening national data and statistical systems.

Key facts:
- A key achievement was completion of the **Fourth Round Eritrea Population and
  Health Survey (EPHS 2025)** — the country's most comprehensive population/health
  data source in more than a decade. It covered all six regions, reached **9,794
  households across 405 Enumeration Areas**, achieving a **98.4% household completion
  rate** (women's interview response 92%, men's 72%).
- The survey used **advanced digital data-collection technology**, moving away from
  paper: **Computer-Assisted Personal Interviewing (CAPI)** enabled near-real-time
  gathering, validation and transmission of data, replacing "decades of manual
  paperwork." Over **85 field staff** from the NSO, Ministry of Health and regional
  administrations were trained and deployed.
- Questionnaires were translated into **seven local languages** (Tigrigna, Tigre,
  Saho, Afar, Bilen, Kunama, Nara). CAPI also supported household listing, random
  household selection, biomarker data collection and real-time data cleaning.
- Nearly **400 tables across 20 chapters** of the main report have been produced;
  report writing, printing and dissemination scheduled for **2026**.
- Technical support from **Mikro International Consultancy Service** (Oslo, Norway)
  and **UNECA**.
- Economic statistics: the NSO **compiled 2023 national accounts** (production /
  value-added approach) and has begun collecting secondary data for **2024 national
  accounts**.
- 2026 focus shifts to analysis, reporting, dissemination and statistical capacity
  building; next steps include finalising/disseminating the EPHS 2025 report,
  estimating 2024 GDP, and launching the **Eritrea Household Income and Expenditure
  Survey (EHIS)**.
