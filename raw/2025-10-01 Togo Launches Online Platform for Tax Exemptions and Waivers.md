(Togo First) - The Togolese Revenue Office (OTR) launched Gest-exo, a digital platform to manage requests for tax exemptions and waivers. Launched on September 30 [2025] in Lome, it enables businesses to submit tax exemption and waiver requests online, aiming to simplify, secure, and automate the process.

The system provides full traceability, real-time monitoring, and automatic integration with the customs software SYDONIA. Economic operators can file requests remotely and track applications from submission through validation in real time.

"This platform responds to an imperative of efficiency. It ensures smooth management of requests, from submission to automatic transmission to the SYDONIA customs system," said Atta-Kakra Essien, OTR's Commissioner for Customs and Indirect Taxes.

Use of Gest-exo becomes mandatory on January 1, 2026, after a three-month transition to complete requests under the old procedures. Gest-exo adds to other digital services already in use, including online management of laissez-passer permits and motor vehicle tax payments. OTR said these initiatives reflect its broader modernization strategy centred on digitization, transparency, and security.
