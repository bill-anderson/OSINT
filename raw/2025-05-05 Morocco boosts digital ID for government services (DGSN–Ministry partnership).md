The Moroccan government is firming up cooperation between state institutions to promote **digital ID use** in public services. The **[[dgsn-morocco|DGSN]]** (General Directorate of National Security) and the Ministry of Digital Transformation and Administration Reform signed a deal to facilitate service delivery in line with the **[[digital-morocco-2030|Digital Morocco 2030]]** strategy; the DGSN played a major role in launching Morocco's national digital ID.

The partnership aims to ensure all government entities can digitize services accessible via the **national biometric digital ID**, and puts in place a legal and organizational blueprint for identity verification using a **trusted third-party service**. Officials say it will fast-track public-sector digital evolution and address citizen concerns over service delays. Morocco believes Digital Morocco 2030, if executed, will create around **240,000 jobs** and add roughly **100 billion dirhams (~US$10.7 billion)** to GDP by 2030; GITEX Africa (third edition) was used to advance digital deals.
