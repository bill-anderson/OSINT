# Ooredoo Fintech Secures License to Launch Digital Wallet in Tunisia

March 7, 2026 — MENA Startup Digest (source: Fintech News Middle East)

Ooredoo Fintech, the digital financial services arm of the regional telecommunications group, has officially received approval from the Central Bank of Tunisia to launch its digital wallet, walletii by Ooredoo. This regulatory green light marks a significant step in the company's strategic expansion across the MENA region and its mission to enhance financial inclusion through regulated digital services.

To ensure a robust integration into the Tunisian financial ecosystem, Ooredoo Fintech is launching walletii in partnership with key local and regional players, including QNB Group and Monétique (Société Monétique Tunisie), Tunisia's national electronic payments operator. These partnerships are crucial for integrating the platform with the local financial system and ensuring full compliance with national payment regulations.

The walletii platform will enable users to pay bills, transfer money to peers, and withdraw cash. A core component of the service's strategy is its reliance on a widespread agent network throughout Tunisia, facilitating easy onboarding and cash-in/cash-out operations, specifically targeting the underbanked and unbanked segments of the population.

Mirko Giacco, International CEO of Ooredoo Fintech, stated: "This approval enables us to expand the walletii brand into a new market by bringing a proven, successful digital wallet to Tunisia... we aim to empower consumers and businesses with secure, everyday payment solutions that support financial inclusion and local economic growth."
