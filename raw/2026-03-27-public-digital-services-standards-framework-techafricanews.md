# Mauritania — national framework for public digital-services standards (GIZ / Digital-Y)

The Ministry of Digital Transformation and Administration Modernisation (**MTNIMA**) in Nouakchott launched a high-level three-day technical workshop to **unify standards for public digital services**, aimed at improving the quality and efficiency of government digital systems. It brought together public- and private-sector representatives and national/international experts to develop a cohesive framework for digital service delivery.

Opening the session, Minister Ahmed Salem Bede stressed that harmonising digital standards is "no longer optional but a strategic necessity," noting that fragmented systems have caused inefficiencies, resource wastage and reduced performance. A unified framework would ensure greater **interoperability**, consistency and improved service quality. The ministry presented a detailed technical study and implementation mechanism for stakeholder feedback before full adoption.

The initiative is supported through cooperation with Germany, via the German Agency for International Cooperation (**GIZ**) and the **Digital-Y** project (launched Jan 2025, €4m).
