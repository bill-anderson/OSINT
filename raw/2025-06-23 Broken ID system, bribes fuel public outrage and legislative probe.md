Public frustration is mounting in Montserrado County over long delays, limited oversight and irregularities at **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** centres. The Liberian Investigator documented allegations of bribery and favoritism — staff allegedly demanding L$200 or a US$20 "gentleman's agreement" fee to fast-track biometric ID processing — amid failed visits, long queues, and elderly applicants sleeping in chairs.

President Boakai's Executive Order 147 (mid-April) requiring NBIS enrollment created banking-sector fallout: a national ID is now required to open new accounts or reactivate old ones, and the NIR extended the mandatory-banking deadline to **31 August**. Ecobank and others posted public notices; banks reported reduced foot traffic as customers struggled to obtain IDs. The NIR denied any express services, reaffirmed the official **US$5 fee** (5 June statement) and warned that staff soliciting extra payments will be dismissed; a senior official said the Congo Town centre serves 500+ people daily against a 250 design capacity. ED Andrew Peters faced questioning as lawmakers launched a probe.
