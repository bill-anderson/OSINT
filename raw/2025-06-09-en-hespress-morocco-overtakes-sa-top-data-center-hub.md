# Morocco overtakes South Africa as Africa's top data center hub

Monday 9 June 2025 - 18:30

Morocco has officially surpassed South Africa as Africa's leading data center hub, hosting 23 facilities and positioning itself at the forefront of the continent's digital transformation, according to a recent report by Global Finance.

The report attributes Morocco's rapid rise to a combination of strategic investments, government incentives, and regulatory reforms. Since launching its digital roadmap in 2020, spearheaded by the Agency for Digital Development, the country has offered tax breaks and exemptions through its National Charter of Investment. A pivotal factor is a 2021 law mandating that sensitive data be stored locally, which has spurred data repatriation and accelerated the expansion of domestic data centers, reflecting Morocco's broader push for data sovereignty.

The sector is currently dominated by major telecom operators such as Maroc Telecom and Inwi, along with specialized firms like Medasys and N+One. Regional development incentives have fueled growth, particularly in Casablanca-Settat and Rabat-Salé-Kénitra. Notably, American firm Iozera has announced a $500 million data center project in Tetouan. "Location is key," said Doha Ammour of N+ONE Datacenters.

Morocco's digital expansion extends beyond data centers into fintech, AI, and e-government, consolidated under the "Digital Morocco 2030" strategy, spotlighted at the 2025 GITEX Africa summit in Marrakech (over 1,400 exhibitors, 45,000 participants from 130+ countries). A prior forecast by W. Media predicted the country's cloud and data storage sector will attract $50 million in investments by 2028, with an expected annual growth rate of 11.18% between 2023 and 2028.
