MTN Digital Infrastructure Secures 15-Year License to Expand Fibre Network in South Sudan

November 25, 2025 2 min read Author: Editorial Team

This license empowers MTN Infra Solutions to deploy and manage national and cross-border fibre networks.

MTN Digital Infrastructure, Africa’s leading digital infrastructure provider, has announced a major milestone in its mission to connect the continent. MTN Digital Infrastructure’s South Sudan subsidiary, Bayobab Infra Solutions Ltd, has secured a 15-year license from the National Communications Authority of South Sudan to construct, install, and operate electronic communications systems, effective 16 October 2025.

This license empowers MTN Infra Solutions to deploy and manage national and cross-border fibre networks. It establishes terrestrial and satellite systems, provides national and international leased circuits, delivers IP transit services, and extends high-capacity fibre connectivity across South Sudan.

The South Sudan license is a pivotal step in the expansion of Project East 2 West (E2W), which aims to create a resilient digital corridor connecting East and West Africa. By introducing robust fibre infrastructure, MTN Digital Infrastructure will unlock new opportunities for economic growth, digital inclusion, and innovation in South Sudan and beyond. High-speed, reliable connectivity will empower businesses, government institutions, and communities, bridging the digital divide and fostering socio-economic development.

“This achievement marks significant progress in our journey to build robust digital infrastructure across Africa. The South Sudan license strengthens the strategic E2W corridor, enhancing connectivity, resilience, and reach across East and Central Africa.”

– Mazen Mroué, CEO, MTN Digital Infrastructure

Project E2 W is supported by key partners, including Africa50, whose collaboration helps accelerate the delivery of critical infrastructure across the continent.MTN Digital Infrastructure’s entry into South Sudan supports the country’s ambitions to modernise its telecommunications sector and integrate more fully into the regional and global digital economy. It lays the groundwork for future advancements in e-government, education, healthcare, and financial services, all of which depend on strong digital foundations.
