# Current Nat'l ID Card "Could Undermine Nat'l Security"

By David A. Yates — Liberian Observer, June 24, 2025

Subhead: Top CSO executive criticizes quality of national ID card, as gov't halts issuance process.

Eddie D. Jarwolo, Executive Director of Naymote Partners for Democratic Development, has raised serious concerns about the current state of Liberia's National Identification (ID) Card system, calling for an immediate overhaul to enhance its security, quality, and credibility. In a strongly worded public statement, Jarwolo welcomed the idea of a national ID system but criticized the present card as substandard and lacking critical security features, warning that unaddressed deficiencies could pose a threat to national security and undermine the integrity of future elections.

"The National Identification card is a fantastic idea, but because it contains citizens' data, it must be secure and of high quality," Jarwolo said. "The current national identity card is low-quality and, if we're not careful, could undermine national security and electoral integrity in the future."

Jarwolo recommended that the government completely redesign the national ID card, integrating modern security elements: "I will suggest the government start over and redesign all ID cards with multiple security features. Fingerprints, facial recognition, a chip inside the card, and visual security features like machine-readable districts and counties and micro-holographic stamps should all be included." He pointed to neighboring Ghana and Nigeria, where ID cards contain embedded chips and advanced biometric features.

To ensure a successful transition, Jarwolo proposed that the government seek technical support from ECOWAS and coordinate with the National Identification Registry (NIR) and the National Elections Commission (NEC). Recognizing the economic challenges facing many Liberians, he also recommended that the government subsidize the cost of the new ID card rollout during the initial phase.
