# WURI ISR — Côte d'Ivoire and Guinea (P161329, Seq 11)

World Bank Implementation Status & Results Report (Seq No 11, archived 02-May-2025) for the **West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion (WURI)** Program — a regional MPA (ECOWAS Free Movement Protocol) whose Phase 1 covers **Côte d'Ivoire and Guinea**. Key content:

- **Guinea — WURI closed early.** The World Bank and Guinea finalized the **early closing of WURI-Guinea on 28 June 2024**, two years ahead of the planned June-2026 date; Phase 1 was restructured in June 2024. Institutional arrangements were "not fully" consolidated in Guinea. Through the pilot, only **8,000 persons received a Unique Identification Number (UNI)**.
- **Guinea legal shift (Oct 2023).** The National Transition Council passed **new personal-identification legislation** plus a **separate civil-registry law** to support the planned administrative census for civil registry (**RAVEC** — Recensement Administratif à Vocation d'État Civil). The ISR flags that the texts adopted "did not fully reflect the principles of identification for sustainable development" — a governance caution.
- **Côte d'Ivoire (contrast).** Steady progress: hosted by the social-protection ministry (MEPS), partnered with the universal-health fund (CNAM); CMU enrolment rose ~33% (early 2024) → ~60% (Apr 2025); disbursement 7% (early FY24) → 38% (Mar 2025), targeting 70% by Dec 2025.
- **ECOWAS component:** regional interoperability strategy, a Community Act on electronic identification, and an "fID to ENBIC" roadmap — disbursement lagging; Priority Plan adopted.

Reference/primary evidence — Guinea's early WURI exit reframes its later pivot to a domestic **MOSIP/TECH5** national ID + RAVEC (see [[oneci-guinea]], [[GIN]]).
