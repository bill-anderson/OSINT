A biometric voter registration exercise is ongoing in war-torn Central African Republic (CAR) as the country prepares to hold local elections next year, the first in nearly four decades.

President Faustin-Archange Touadera launched the process in a ceremony in Bangui and was the first to verify if his name features on the electoral list. The exercise consists of verifying the details of citizens who already registered in 2020, as well as registering those showing up for the very first time.

The Head of Communication and Public Relations of the country's National Elections Authority (ANE), Saint-Régis Zoumiri, said the exercise started in Bangui on November 24 and ended on Saturday December 7; it will end in the rest of the country on December 15. He reported high turnout, with particular interest from women and youth, but also difficulties in terms of logistics and security.

The biometric voter system in CAR has been supplied by GenKey. Zoumiri said a total of 3,000 biometric enrolment tablets have been deployed on the field, managed by 1,469 registration agents. CAR is supported by partners such as the United Nations Development Program (UNDP) as it prepares to hold local elections in April next year.
