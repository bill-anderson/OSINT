# Somalia — inauguration of the Data Protection Authority

> ## Excerpt
> Somalia inaugurated its Data Protection Authority (DPA) on 24 Feb 2024, following
> Data Protection Law No. 005 of 2023 (signed Mar 2023). Early-Feb 2024 DPA
> guidelines set compliance steps; violations carry fines of 2–4% of worldwide turnover.

---

On February 24, 2024, Somalia reached a significant step in its digital transformation with the inauguration of its Data Protection Authority (DPA). The creation of the DPA follows the approval of Somalia's Data Protection Law (Law No. 005 of 2023), ratified by both parliamentary chambers and signed by President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud in March 2023.

The DPA's inauguration ceremony was attended by key government officials, civil society organizations, and business-sector representatives, who emphasized the crucial role of data protection in Somalia's digital advancement and the importance of collaboration between stakeholders.

Somalia faces challenges in implementing effective data protection: strengthening technological infrastructure, coordinating regulatory authorities, and promoting a culture of compliance.

At the beginning of February 2024, the DPA published guidelines for implementing the Data Protection Law, recommending that organizations first make staff aware of data protection, create an inventory of collected data, carry out a risk assessment against the Law's requirements, and draw up a detailed action plan for compliance. Violation of compliance requirements can result in financial penalties reaching 2% to 4% of worldwide annual turnover.
