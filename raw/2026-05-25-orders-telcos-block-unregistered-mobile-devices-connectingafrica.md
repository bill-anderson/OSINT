# Cameroon orders telcos to block unregistered mobile devices

*The government of Cameroon has ordered MTN, Orange, and Camtel to block unregistered and uncleared digital devices starting May 25, 2026.*

*Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa — May 25, 2026*

The government of Cameroon has ordered telecom operators MTN, Orange, and Camtel to begin blocking digital devices that have not been registered and cleared through the country's new electronic customs system.

In a letter to telcos, Cameroon Customs Director General Fongod Edwin Nuvaga said operators needed to block mobile phones and other digital terminals which have not been properly registered at customs before connecting to a mobile network.

The order was issued on April 1, 2026, with enforcement beginning on May 25, 2026.

Aimed at curbing smuggling and tax evasion, the move enforces the provisions of a 2023 law that introduced a digital clearance system through which importers had to declare devices through the Cameroon Customs Information System.

The government said any operators allowing the connection of non-custom cleared terminals would be held liable and would be responsible for unpaid duties.

## Devices in Cameroon need verification

The Cameroonian system verifies devices via a centralized platform that links import records, device identifiers, and network activation data.

Each device must be authenticated by its international mobile equipment identity (IMEI) number before it can connect to local networks.

Only devices that have cleared customs duties electronically, operate in roaming mode, or qualify under a tax amnesty program are permitted network access.

This pressure on telecom operators reflects the government's intent to control the entire distribution chain of irregularly imported devices.

According to Cameroun24, the measure follows an earlier government communique informing citizens about the introduction of new identification and tracking mechanism for imported devices.

The publication reported that the order for telcos to block undeclared mobile devices came after the country's Finance Minister Louis-Paul Motazé disclosed that nearly 700,000 phones had connected to local networks between April 1 and April 25, 2026, without undergoing custom clearance.
