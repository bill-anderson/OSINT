# Model Framework for Building Internet Resilience in Africa (ATU-S Framework 001)

> ## Excerpt
> A six-step model framework (June 2025) for African regulators/ICT ministries to build and sustain
> Internet resilience across networks, critical infrastructure and market — the first deliverable of an
> ATU/AFRINIC/ISOC joint initiative. Standing instrument: [[internet-resilience-framework-africa]].

> **Provenance note.** June 2025 (pre-window; included in the drop). An **instrument** (standing
> framework), also filed here as a dated source. Modelled on the **US DHS** resilience approach — a
> US-security template applied to African Internet policy (worth flagging analytically).

**Artefact:** ![[2025-06-01 Model Framework for Building Internet Resilience in Africa.pdf]]

## Key facts

- Headline data point: **53 active IXPs across 47 cities in 36 African countries** (~2025).
- Six-step process: engage stakeholders → identify critical missions → criticality assessment → assess
  liabilities → identify gaps/solutions → integrate solutions (incl. financing). Three focus areas:
  Networks/ISPs, Critical Infrastructure (power, cable, IXPs, ccTLD/DNS), Market.
- Prescribes resilience metrics (MTBF, availability, MTIR; "% traffic exchanged via IXP", "% population
  within 10km of a fibre connection point") and financing via ODA loans and universal-service funds.
  Anchored in the African Declaration on Internet Rights and Freedoms. (PeeringDB is *not* cited.)
