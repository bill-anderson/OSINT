# How Zain Sudan restored mobile connectivity at a time of national crisis

Vendor-sourced case study (Totogi) hosted on Cloud Computing News — promotional in tone but documents
a concrete, dated Sudan connectivity development. **Verify vendor claims against primary reporting.**

- **The blackout.** Early 2024 civil-war escalation triggered a nationwide mobile-connectivity
  blackout; three MNOs including **Zain Sudan** (23m customers) could not restore services during a
  **~40-day** outage, cutting mobile-money and humanitarian channels. Zain was losing **~$12m/week**.
- **The fix.** Zain contracted **Totogi**'s AWS-hosted public-cloud Charging-as-a-Service; services
  restored **~18 days** after signing, migrating **>20m subscribers** to the cloud (incumbent vendor
  had projected 6–9 months).
- **Claimed results.** ~76% lower total cost of ownership, ~$100m revenue vs the slower path, reduced
  disaster-recovery costs. *(Vendor figures — unverified.)*
