Historical baseline (June 2018). On 4 June 2018, Equatorial Guinea inaugurated its link to the Ceiba-2 submarine cable, in a ceremony led by GITGE (the Manager of Telecommunications Infrastructure of Equatorial Guinea) with the President in Malabo.

Ceiba-2 (built Oct 2015 – Mar 2017) is a 400 km cable with 40 Gbps capacity (upgradable to 100 Gbps), connecting Malabo, Bata and Kribi (Cameroon) — the country's first direct international fibre-optic connection. It complements the domestic Ceiba-1 (Malabo–Bata) and the ACE cable, increasing total capacity and internet speed while reducing access cost.
