# Smart City Surveillance in Africa (IDS, March 2026)

The Institute of Development Studies report (Wairagala Wakabi and Tony Roberts) mapping Chinese
AI/"safe city" surveillance deployments across 11 African countries — the primary source behind the
March-2026 SCMP coverage already in the wiki.

## Key findings

- Surveyed 11 countries; **Chinese firms ([[zte]], [[hikvision]], [[huawei]]) supplied every nation
  surveyed**.
- **Nigeria** the largest market: China Exim Bank advanced **US$399m of the US$470m** Abuja spent on
  a ZTE/Hikvision security network; Lagos deployed ~**10,000 HD cameras**.
- African states spent **>US$2bn** on facial-recognition/ANPR systems, mostly Chinese-supplied.
- **Kenya** an early adopter: a ~US$100m Chinese grant funded **1,800 Huawei cameras** in Nairobi/
  Mombasa after the 2013 Westgate attack.
- Typical package ≈ **US$250m Eximbank loan** bundling cameras + a national data centre + vendor
  training — producing **institutional lock-in**.
- Documents surveillance repurposed against political opposition (Zimbabwe, Mozambique, Zambia,
  Senegal) despite no terror threat.
