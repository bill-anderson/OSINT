# Cabo Verde unveils TechPark CV "tech islands" vision, backed with $50m from AfDB

Cabo Verde officially unveiled **[[techpark-cv|TechPark CV]]**, seeking to become a hub for digital services. Backed by a **€45.59m ($50m) investment from the [[african-development-bank|African Development Bank]]**, the project aims to create up to **1,500 jobs** on the island of Santiago, with a smaller campus on São Vicente. It includes a **data centre in Santiago**, plus training, conference and incubation facilities.

**Pedro Lopes, Secretary of State for the Digital Economy**, called TechPark CV the "cornerstone" of the digital-transformation strategy, meant to reduce dependence on tourism and "position Cabo Verde as a competitive player in the digital economy." Cabo Verde (10 islands ~600km off West Africa, population ~500,000) leans on its **location and subsea cable landings** — an hour's flight from Senegal, three from Portugal — and its **diaspora** (roughly twice the resident population).

The park is a designated **Special Economic Zone for Technologies**: fiscal incentives include a **2.5% corporation-tax discount**, VAT/import-tax exemptions, and visa-free three-month stays for many nationalities. Executives from **Microsoft and Intel** attended the inauguration.
