A mass national ID registration and renewal exercise which the Ugandan government announced three years ago is now set to commence by the close of May 2025. Since the exercise was announced in August 2022 after Cabinet approval, the government set many deadlines and failed, with NIRA citing technical and financial constraints.

The Ministry of Internal Affairs and the ID authority say all is set for a nationwide rollout from May 27, at the end of a pilot scheduled to run from May 2 to 26. The government is targeting 33 million people: 15.8 million ID card renewals (many expiring by end of June) and 17.2 million first-time registrations.

The State Minister of Internal Affairs Gen David Muhoozi said the activity will begin in all 146 districts after the pilot. The contracted company, Tahaluf, already fully supplied all 5,665 biometric registration kits by January, distributed countrywide beginning February. Two high-speed card production machines were also procured, with combined printing capacity up to 100,000 laser-engraved cards per day, while a data centre and other critical communication equipment have been delivered and configured.

"A new national security information system has also been developed, built on the modular open-source identification platform (MOSIP)," Muhoozi said, adding the first phase includes five modules: new registration, renewal, change or correction of particulars, card replacement, and first-time card issuance for children aged 16. "The new system provides for online registration of biographical data. Furthermore, it will capture the iris as an additional biometric feature... Biometrics will only be collected at designated registration points by a NIRA registration officer." The pre-registration portal was to go live on May 27.
