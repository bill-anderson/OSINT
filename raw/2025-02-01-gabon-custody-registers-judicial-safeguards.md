(Roundtable held 18-19 February 2025 in Libreville; publication date not stated on page — event month used as proxy. Builds on the November 2024 NPM designation.)

Gabon's Ministry of Justice, in partnership with the **APT (Association for the Prevention of Torture)**, concluded a national roundtable in Libreville (18-19 February) on implementing the **Méndez Principles**, with emphasis on standardised custody registers. Participants included the Ministry of Justice, Police and Gendarmerie Directorates, the Internal Security Inspectorate, prosecutors, the Bar Association, and the NHRI.

Custody registers were recognized as a fundamental procedural safeguard: accurate, comprehensive recording of detention is essential to prevent torture and ensure accountability. APT presented a standardized custody-register model. Participants examined current record-keeping in Gabon's police stations and gendarmerie brigades, identifying gaps.

Discussions looked toward **modernization of custody records through digitalization**, exploring integration of new technologies (including AI tools) to enhance accuracy, accessibility and oversight — while recognizing paper-based registers as an essential first step and noting infrastructure limitations in remote areas and training needs. The roundtable builds on Gabon's ratification of UNCAT and OPCAT and the November 2024 designation of the NHRI as the National Preventive Mechanism (NPM).
