Madagascar launched a pilot to enrol citizens for its new national digital ID system, ahead of a full nationwide rollout expected from April 2026. The pilot began 27 January 2026 at a ceremony presided over by President Michael Randrianirina; the Ministry of Digital Development, Posts and Telecommunications announced it on its official Facebook page. The pilot first enrols the head of state and members of government to test the system infrastructure, mainly in the district of Antsimondrano.

Enrolment captures biographical data plus face, iris and fingerprint biometrics to create a Unique Identification Number for accessing public and private services. U.S.-based IriTech is the official hardware provider, supplying its IriAegis-BK binocular dual-eye iris scanner (with liveness detection/anti-spoofing).

The system is implemented under PRODIGY (World Bank-funded). At nationwide rollout the government expects to issue a national digital ID to more than 12 million citizens, targeting 2 million people (aged 18+) between April and June 2026. The multimodal choice is intended "to prevent fraud" and avoid duplicate cards. The project was approved by the Council of Ministers and its legal framework endorsed by the Senate in July 2025. The digital ID is to be integrated with ministries, agencies and public services via the national interoperability system, overseen by the Digital Governance Unit.
