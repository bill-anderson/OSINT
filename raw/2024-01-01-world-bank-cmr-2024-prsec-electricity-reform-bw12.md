# World Bank — Cameroon PRSEC electricity-sector reform (AMI smart metering / Douala data centre), 2024

The World Bank provided CFA180 billion in financing to Cameroon's Electricity Sector Reform / Recovery Programme (PRSEC-PforR, 2024–2026), whose digital components include Advanced Metering Infrastructure (20,000 smart meters, population-scale automated data capture) and a data centre under construction in Douala to centralise and secure meter data.

## Description

"The project is backed by the World Bank under the Electricity Sector Reform Programme (PRSEC-PforR) and relies on Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) technology capable of automatically transmitting electricity consumption data. … A data centre is also under construction in Douala to centralise and secure information collected by the smart meters. The initiative is part of the Electricity Sector Recovery Plan (PRSEC), a broader reform agenda running from 2024 to 2026 and valued at nearly 400 billion CFA francs ($710 million). The programme is supported by 180 billion CFA francs in World Bank financing and an additional 48 billion CFA francs from the African Development Bank."
