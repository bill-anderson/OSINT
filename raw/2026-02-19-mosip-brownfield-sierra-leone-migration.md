Biometric Update reports from MOSIP Connect 2026 in Rabat, Morocco, on "brownfield" implementations — migrating existing identity systems to MOSIP rather than building greenfield.

Sierra Leone context: "Sierra Leone has been working on migrating its enrolled identity records to the MOSIP platform since successfully completing a pilot in early-2024. National Civil Registration Authority (NCRA) Deputy Director Moses T.F. Vibbie told Biometric Update in an interview last week that countries like his replacing an existing identity system must meet the expectations that have been set by the previous system. A gradual rollout or phased approach will not satisfy stakeholders who are already using the system for service delivery."

Comparators: Uganda's NIRA completed the first brownfield MOSIP implementation, migrating more than 28 million records (finding ~200,000 duplicates over ~7 months) while adding 6.7 million new registrations; Nigeria is undertaking the largest brownfield MOSIP migration yet (~120 million records). The piece situates Sierra Leone within this dwindling cohort of countries moving from legacy systems to open-source MOSIP.
