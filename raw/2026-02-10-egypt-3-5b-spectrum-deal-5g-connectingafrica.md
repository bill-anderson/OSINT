Egypt seals $3.5B spectrum deal with telcos

Egypt has sealed a US$3.5 billion agreement with four telecoms operators to double the nation's available mobile spectrum and enable 5G expansion.

Matshepo Sehloho,Associate Editor,Connecting Africa

Egypt has signed a US$3.5 billion deal with Telecom Egypt, Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt and e& Egypt to double the country's mobile spectrum and support 5G expansion in the country.

Egypt's Minister of Communications and Information Technology, Amr Talaat, said the deal provides operators with an additional 410MHz of spectrum, effectively doubling their holdings in a single transaction.

Speaking at Siemens' 125th anniversary celebration in Egypt, Talaat added that the newly awarded frequencies match the total amount of spectrum allocated to operators in Egypt over the past 30 years.

Talaat also spoke of the success of a two-pronged growth strategy in the Egyptian mobile market.

Firstly, he said Egypt has doubled the number of mobile towers and basestations since 2019, and secondly, it has increased spectrum capacity.

"Over the past seven and a half years, fixed-line Internet speeds in Egypt have doubled, making the country first in Africa in average fixed-line speed. Plans are underway to expand mobile coverage to 40,000 towers by 2028," Talaat added.

The announcement aligns with the country's National Spectrum Strategy that covers the 2026-2030 period aimed at supporting the sustainability and development of telecom infrastructure, enhancing service quality, strengthening 5G services, and boosting market competitiveness and digital readiness.

Part of Egypt's National Spectrum Strategy for 2026-2030 aims to strengthen 5G services and boost market competitiveness. (Source: Image by Freepik)

The country's National Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (NTRA) announced the launch of 5G services in June 2025.

That launch came a year after the NTRA awarded 5G licenses to the three privately-owned mobile operators, Vodafone, Orange and e&.

The three telcos joined majority state-owned Telecom Egypt, which secured its 5G license in January 2024, for US$150 million.

Egypt's spectrum deal comes as other North African countries are also launching 5G services.

In July 2025, Morocco's National Telecommunications Regulatory Agency (ANRT) awarded 5G licenses to Maroc Telecom, Inwi and Orange following a competitive bidding process launched earlier that month.

In February 2025, Tunisian telcos Orange Tunisie, Ooredoo Tunisie and Tunisie Télécom all launched their 5G mobile services.

Five months after Algeria initiated a tender process for 5G licenses, the Regulatory Authority of Post and Electronic Communications (ARPCE) granted licenses to Mobilis, Djezzy and Ooredoo.
