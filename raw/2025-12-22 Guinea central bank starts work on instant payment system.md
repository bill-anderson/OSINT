Guinea's central bank (BCRG) launched work on an instant payment system (SPI) on 18 December 2025. At the opening, BCRG First Vice-Governor Mohamed Lamine Conté said the project stems from a consultative process involving banks, electronic-money institutions, microfinance institutions and fintechs. The SPI will allow real-time send/receive of funds at any time without geographic constraints, addressing delays in traditional channels and supporting both merchant payments and personal transfers.

Technically, the BCRG opted for a public, open, interoperable infrastructure based on Mojaloop — chosen to limit technological dependence, encourage local innovation and foster competition. Real-time transfers will be possible between bank accounts, mobile wallets and other instruments.

Inclusion context: BCRG data show 23% of adults hold an account with a financial institution, up from 7% ten years ago — still below the sub-Saharan average. Mobile-money transactions rose 8.6% to 46,795 billion Guinean francs in Q2 2024. The operational timeline is not yet specified; effective interoperability could lower transaction costs and strengthen transparency and trust in the formal financial system.
