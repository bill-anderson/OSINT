# Carte nationale d'identité biométrique AES : lancement de l'enrôlement de masse sous le signe de la souveraineté numérique

*(Le Sahel / ONEP is the Niger state daily. Capture is not endorsement.)*

The Secretary-General of the Ministry of Interior, Public Security and Territorial Administration, Commissaire Général de Police **Ayouba Abdourahamane**, launched the **mass enrolment for the AES biometric national ID card on Saturday 25 April 2026** at the Yantala police commissariat, Niamey. The launch follows the enrolment of **President Tiani on 27 March** and of **Prime Minister Ali Mahaman Lamine Zeine on 9 April 2026**.

Key details:
- Enrolment centres installed in the **20 commissariats of Niamey**, with progressive extension nationwide, to bring the service closer to citizens.
- The card is **valid 5 years, mandatory for citizens aged 15+**; enrolment fee **4,500 FCFA**, on presentation of birth certificate / supplementary judgment, nationality certificate, residence certificate, proof of profession and biometric-standard photographs.
- Biometric/behavioural capture includes **fingerprints, face, voice and iris**, producing a highly secure digital template (per Niamey regional governor Général de Division Assoumane Abdou Harouna).
- Framed as consolidating national sovereignty and modernising administration, a response to document fraud, identity theft and insecurity; positioned within AES integration (Niger, Burkina Faso, Mali) as a prelude to progressive interoperability of identification systems and a **future common biometric passport**.
- The card facilitates access to health, education, banking, mobility and civic participation.
