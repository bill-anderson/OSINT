*The electoral roll is a register distinct from the ANID e-ID/NIU system — the 4.2m voter figure must not be read as e-ID enrolment.*

(Translated from French; key terms kept.)

In Togo, the revision of electoral rolls (listes electorales) ahead of the 2025 municipal elections will take place from 7 to 23 April across the national territory. The announcement was made on Tuesday, 1 April [2025] by the authorities through a presidential decree fixing the modalities.

During the period, citizens not yet registered can enroll, notably young people who have recently turned 18. The operation also concerns voters who have changed domicile and those who lost their voter card, who can update their information at the dedicated centres. The existing electoral districting will serve as the framework for the revision; centres will open daily from 7am to 5pm.

Ahead of the operation, the Independent National Electoral Commission (Commission Electorale Nationale Independante, CENI) trained the actors involved in updating the electoral file: 324 trainers were trained in Lome before passing on their knowledge to nearly 6,000 data-entry operators for field implementation.

The revision comes a little over a year after the last electoral census, organised for the April 2024 legislative and regional elections, at the end of which the CENI had registered more than 4.2 million Togolese voters.
