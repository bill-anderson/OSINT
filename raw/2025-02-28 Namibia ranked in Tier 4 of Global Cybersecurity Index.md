Namibia was ranked in Tier 4 ("Evolving") of the ITU's Global Cybersecurity Index (GCI) for 2024, scoring 37.93 out of 100. Tier 4 countries score between 20 and 55, indicating basic cybersecurity measures with substantial areas needing development.

Pillar scores: legal measures 5.66; technical measures 3.54; organisational measures 16.35 (best-performing); capacity development 4.57; cooperation measures 6.81 — with the shortfalls concentrated in legal and technical measures. Tier 4 places Namibia alongside Angola, Gabon and Zimbabwe. ITU Telecommunication Development Bureau Director Cosmas Luckyson Zavazava noted progress in cybersecurity legislation, incident-response frameworks, and national and international cooperation.
