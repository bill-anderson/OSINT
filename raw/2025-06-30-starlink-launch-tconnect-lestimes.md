# T-Connect vows to end connectivity challenges in Lesotho

*Lesotho Times, 30 June 2025. By Leemisa Thuseho. Starlink satellite internet service officially launched; promises 10,000 direct jobs and 50,000 indirect jobs.*

GLOBAL tech mogul Elon Musk's Starlink satellite internet service is now officially available in Lesotho, following its launch by local digital transformation group, T-Connect Lesotho, which has vowed to tackle the country's persistent internet connectivity challenges through high-speed, low-latency satellite technology.

T-Connect, the authorised Starlink reseller in the country, unveiled the service this week during a ceremony at AVANI Maseru. The launch follows the granting of a 10-year operating licence by the Lesotho Communications Authority (LCA) on 14 April 2025, after a year-long regulatory review.

T-Connect Group Chairperson Nolo Letele emphasised that Starlink's presence is meant to complement rather than displace existing telecoms companies: "The project is not here to take away people's jobs." He said Starlink will reach far-flung areas current operators cannot, citing installations in schools in Thaba-Tseka (Ha Makunyapane).

T-Connect Group CEO Phelane Phomane said Lesotho "has the potential for 90% cellular coverage, but 80 percent of the rural population is offline." During the piloting phase, Marakabei High School in Thaba-Tseka was given free Starlink connection — computers there had sat unused since 2017 for lack of internet. T-Connect will launch community internet hotspots across the country, accessible via a unique code, costing M10 for uncapped daily data; Starlink business packages start from M1,600/month for speeds up to 150Mbps.

Phomane also revealed that, in collaboration with the Development Bank of Southern Africa (DBSA), T-Connect plans to develop AI Data Centres across Southern Africa, with one in Lesotho — claiming it "will be the highest data centre in the world" and would make Lesotho a regional hub exporting digital skills. He said the initiative will create 10,000 direct and over 50,000 indirect jobs.

Delivering remarks for the Prime Minister, Minister of Education and Training Ntoi Rapapa called Starlink's arrival "a monumental step in the country's digital transformation journey."
