# Securing Land Tenure in Egypt — Who Needs Registered Titles? (Sims)

Analytical essay by **David Sims** challenging the orthodoxy (de Soto-style) that formal registered titles are a precondition for secure property rights and investment in non-Western contexts. Drawing on Egypt, Sims argues informal tenure arrangements can function effectively and that mass titling programmes may be costly, slow and mismatched to how urban property actually works.

**Historical baseline / framing (2016)** — provides a critical counterpoint to Egypt's later push for formal digital property registration (the 2025 **[[egypt-property-id-law|Property ID Law]]**). Cite for analytical framing; it does **not** describe current registry state and must not out-rank fresher sources on Egypt's land-digitisation.
