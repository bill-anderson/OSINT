World Bank case study (June 2023) on the West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion (WURI) program, which links biometrically verified foundational identification (fID) credentials with digitally signed QR codes to help individuals access human-development services. WURI launched in 2018 (Cote d'Ivoire, Guinea and the ECOWAS Commission in Phase 1) and expanded in 2020 to Benin, Burkina Faso, Niger and Togo in Phase 2, with total financing of US$395.1 million and a runway through 2028.

The study examines how WURI leveraged data, technology and data governance to reach the unregistered, situating it against the ECOWAS-region identity gap. Reference case study; cite rather than absorb its figures. Co-authored by Jacob Bathanti, Conrad C. Daly and Tina George Karippacheril under the World Bank's Disruptive Technologies analytical program.
