# Source record — DZAP World Bank PAD

**Primary document.** World Bank Project Appraisal Document (Report No. PADHI00683),
Digital Development Global Practice, Eastern & Southern Africa Region. Attached PDF:
`2026-05-23 World Bank Zambia.pdf` (53pp). Task Team Leaders: Tim Kelly, Paul Ndungutse.

## What it is

Appraisal of a **US$100 million IDA grant** (SDR 73.9M) to the Republic of Zambia
for the **Digital Zambia Acceleration Project (DZAP, P505094)** — the fifth operation
under the **Inclusive Digitalization in Eastern & Southern Africa (IDEA)** Multiphase
Programmatic Approach ([[idea-mpa]]), a US$2.48bn Board-approved envelope approved
27 June 2024. Plus US$20M unguaranteed commercial financing (MFD-/private-capital-
enabling). Implementing agency: [[smart-zambia-institute]] (Office of the President).
Closing 31 Oct 2030; program closing 29 Oct 2032. Overall risk: Substantial.

- **PDO:** increase access to, and inclusive use of, the internet and digitally
  enabled services.
- **Components:** (1) Affordable broadband — US$50M; (2) Interoperable & secure
  platforms — US$38M; (3) Digital skills & capabilities — US$5M; (4) Program
  management & capacity building — US$7M.

## Key facts (as of PAD, 2025-09-11)

- **Country context:** ~20M people; sovereign default Nov 2020; 2024 drought cut
  hydro power to blackouts >20 hrs/day, since recovered to only 5–7 hrs on-grid/day;
  46% under 15.
- **Connectivity:** internet penetration ~21 per 100 (below ESA avg 28); fixed
  broadband (5GB/mo) costs >12% of GNI per capita; 4 MNOs (Airtel, MTN, Zamtel,
  Zedmobile); ~10,000 km fibre today, project adds ≥2,000 km; Starlink entered Oct
  2023; Govt 8NDP target 80% penetration by 2026.
- **Digital ID:** legacy paper NRC (pre-1964; 94% adult coverage 2021); INRIS 15%
  biometric; Govt planning a **homegrown, open-standards foundational ID** to "manage
  its infrastructure independently and reduce reliance on external vendors"; minimal
  data collected (MoHAIS); free enrolment; ID4D TA with UNDP/UNHCR.
- **DPI stack:** ZamPay (interoperable payments), ZamConnect (data exchange), ZamSign
  (e-signatures), ZamPortal/eServices (≈300 of ~1,500 govt services online).
- **Inclusion:** targets 3.7M women; explicit gender / PwD / older-person / rural
  divides (gender annex).
- **Alignment:** CPF FY25–29; AU Digital Transformation Strategy; SDG9; Agenda 2063;
  WB Digital Strategy (Jul 2025); IDA-21 Digital Policy Commitment; Single Digital
  Market for East Africa.

## Analytical hooks

- `sovereignty`: an explicit vendor-independence / homegrown-ID agenda — see
  [[zambia--dpi-id]].
- `colonialism`: the same sovereignty agenda is **externally financed** (IDA grant +
  ID4D/UNDP/UNHCR TA) — a case study in donor-funded digital self-reliance; the
  tension is the point, not a contradiction to resolve.
