# Namibian regulator rejects Starlink again

CRAN has rejected appeals for SpaceX's Starlink to obtain a telecommunications service licence, upholding its March 2026 denial. CRAN CEO Emilia Nghikembua, in a media briefing: "Following our decision of March 2026 to decline Starlink's license application, we received 624 requests for reconsideration from members of the public." 622 requests didn't meet the legal requirements to trigger a valid reconsideration and were declined; two satisfied the procedural threshold and were considered on their merits, but neither "presented new evidence nor demonstrated a material error in the original decision." CRAN therefore reaffirmed its March decision.

Nghikembua stressed the issue "has never been whether satellite technology is beneficial" — LEO can help reach underserved areas — but whether the application complied with the Communications Act. Of the six licence criteria, Starlink failed three: ownership and control, compliance history, and national security/public safety. She also noted CRAN received a further application from Starlink Internet Service Namibia on 8 June 2026.
