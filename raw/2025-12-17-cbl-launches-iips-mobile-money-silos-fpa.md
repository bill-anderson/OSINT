# Liberia: CBL Launches Inclusive Instant Payment System, Ends Era of Mobile Money Silos

Contributing Writer — FrontPage Africa, December 17, 2025

Monrovia — Liberia took a major leap toward a cash-lite, digitally inclusive economy on Tuesday with the official launch of the Inclusive Instant Payment System (IIPS), a nationwide mobile money interoperability platform spearheaded by the Central Bank of Liberia (CBL). Unveiling the system, President Joseph Boakai declared a new era for Liberia's economy, describing it as a bold national commitment to financial inclusion, transparency, and digital transformation.

Delivering the keynote address at the EJS Ministerial Complex in Congo Town, President Boakai said the IIPS signals Liberia's readiness to embrace the future of digital finance while ensuring progress reaches every citizen. "This is not just a technological achievement; it is a statement of intent," he said. "It is a promise to our people that progress will not leave them behind."

The President linked the platform to his government's ARREST Agenda (Agriculture, Roads, Rule of Law, Education, Sanitation, and Tourism), describing it as a "digital highway" connecting Liberians financially regardless of location, and emphasizing that digitized payments will enhance transparency and accountability by reducing leakages and delays in salaries, pensions, and social transfers. He stressed that financial inclusion must be treated as a fundamental right, particularly for rural communities and informal-sector workers. By reducing reliance on cash, he said, the system will lower transaction costs, increase speed, build trust, and attract investment.

The ceremony was attended by senior government officials, lawmakers, bankers, mobile money operators, business leaders and transport unions. CBL Executive Governor Henry F. Saamoi described the initiative as a "defining and transformative moment" in Liberia's financial services history. "With the launch of the Inclusive Instant Payment System, we take a bold and decisive step toward a modern digital financial economy — one where efficiency, transparency and inclusion are no longer aspirations, but lived realities for every Liberian," Saamoi said.

The IIPS positions Liberia among a growing number of African countries deploying real-time payment infrastructure. Citing the State of Inclusive Instant Payment Systems (SIIPS) 2025 Report, Saamoi noted that 25 African countries now operate domestic instant payment systems, with 36 platforms processing billions of transactions annually. The pilot phase currently supports Person-to-Person (P2P) and Government-to-People (G2P) transactions; work is ongoing with the Liberia Revenue Authority to roll out People-to-Government (P2G) payments. Future phases will incorporate commercial banks, fintech firms, merchant payments, bill payments and eventually cross-border transactions. Saamoi credited collaboration with the Gates Foundation, AfricaNenda, Mojaloop Foundation, ThitsaWorks, and the World Bank, which is supporting the deployment of a full National Electronic Payment Switch (NEPS). The system is branded "Pay Na-Na: Inclusive. Instant. Liberia."
