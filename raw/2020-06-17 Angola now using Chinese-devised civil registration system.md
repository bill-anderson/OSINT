The Angolan authorities began using a new **civil-registration system devised by China**, Jornal de Angola reported, quoting technical lead Fernando Fortes. The system, rolled out first in Luanda, will eventually give every Angolan a national identity card, supplying each citizen a copy of their birth certificate for use in applying for the ID.

The government aimed to register the births of **12 million Angolans by 2022**, more cheaply than before. In November 2019, state-owned **China National Electronics Import & Export Corp (CEIEC)** had won the contract to set up the system.
