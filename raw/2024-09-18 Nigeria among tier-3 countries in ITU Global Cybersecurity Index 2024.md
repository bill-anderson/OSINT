The International Telecommunications Union (ITU) has listed Nigeria among tier-3 countries in its Global Cybersecurity Index 2024 (GCI 2024), which groups countries by the cybersecurity measures they have in place.

The GCI 2024 assesses national efforts across five pillars: legal, technical, organizational, capacity development, and cooperation. Nigeria's highest score was in legal measures, where it scored 19.52 out of 20. The 194 countries measured are categorized into five tiers: tier-1 and tier-2 countries demonstrate strong commitment across all five pillars; tier-3 and tier-4 are expanding digital services and bringing people online but still have work to ensure cybersecurity is part of meaningful connectivity; tier-5 lag on all pillars. The report placed 46 countries in Tier 1 ("role modeling") and 105 in tiers 3 and 4.

ITU noted the African region has advanced the most in cybersecurity since 2021. ITU Secretary-General Doreen Bogdan-Martin said building trust in the digital world is paramount; Telecommunication Development Bureau Director Cosmas Luckyson Zavazava highlighted improvements in legal measures, capacity building and incident-response capabilities.
