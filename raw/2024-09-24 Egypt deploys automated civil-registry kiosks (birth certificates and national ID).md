# Egypt deploys automated civil-registry kiosks (birth certificates and national ID)

Egypt's **Ministry of Interior** deployed **automated civil-registry machines** in major malls across governorates — an initial phase of **8 offices** expanded to **16 locations** (Cairo, Giza, Gharbia, Alexandria, Hurghada, Cairo Airport, etc.). Via touchscreens the kiosks issue five documents: **birth, death, marriage and divorce certificates, and national-ID application forms** (standard/urgent/VIP). Users select the service, enter national-ID and phone numbers, confirm identity by **fingerprint or facial recognition**, pay (cash) and collect with a receipt.

Pricing (2024): a computerised birth certificate **63 EGP**; national-ID forms **175 EGP (urgent) – 305 EGP (VIP)**. Part of the government's digital-transformation drive to speed civil services.
