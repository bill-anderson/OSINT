# Parliamentary question NW5126 (19 September 2025) — SA Connect Phase 2 resumption and funding

Vilakazi, Mr S G to ask the Minister of Communications and Digital Technologies:

Given that there is no clear roadmap for the resumption of the SA Connect Phase 2 programme during or after the 2025-26 financial year, while his department is in negotiations with the National Treasury about the release of funds, what (a) is the revised implementation timeline for the resumption of the SA Connect programme in the 2026-27 financial year, (b) are the projected funding allocations and (c) lessons has his department learned from the delays in the 2025-26 financial year that will inform improved planning, contract structuring and risk mitigation in future? NW5734E

## Reply:

I have been advised by the Department as follows: (a) The resumption of the SA Connect Phase 2 programme is dependent on the approval of roll-over of funds by National Treasury. Subject to this approval, the Department, together with its implementing entities, will finalise an updated implementation plan for the 2026/27 financial year, including revised milestones, deliverables and provincial rollout schedules. (b) The SA Connect programme has been allocated R710-million in the MTEF, of which a large portion will be utilised to maintain connectivity under Phase 1. (c) The DCDT has undertaken an internal review of the challenges experienced in the 2024-25 financial year. Key lessons informing improved planning and execution include: 1 Funding Alignment — Ensuring that rollout milestones are tied to confirmed, multi-year funding allocations before committing to service providers. 2 Contract Management Capacity — Improve contract management capacity within the implementing entities to ensure a rigorous performance management regime. 3. Financial Support for SMMEs — Help create bespoke and sound financial assistance for SMMEs to mitigate against lengthy funding applications and cashflow risks. 4. Programme Management — Establish a well-resourced programme management office to improve full project lifecycle management, from planning to execution, and perform monitoring and evaluation Risk Mitigation 5. Strengthening governance, monitoring and escalation mechanisms to detect bottlenecks early and prevent service disruptions. 6. Stakeholder Coordination — Enhance collaboration with National Treasury, implementing entities, national and provincial departments, municipalities and the sector.
