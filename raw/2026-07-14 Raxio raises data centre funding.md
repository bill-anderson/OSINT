**Raxio Group** has secured additional equity investment from shareholders **Meridiam** and **Roha**, taking
its committed capital base **above US$380 million** as the African data-centre operator prepares for its next
phase of expansion.

The company said the investment builds on its existing capital base, including a **US$100 million** financing
package from the **International Finance Corporation** secured in 2025, plus debt from **Proparco** and the
**Emerging Africa and Asia Infrastructure Fund**. The capital will support expansion as demand grows across
Africa, driven by cloud adoption and AI workloads. It cited McKinsey projections that Africa's installed
data-centre capacity could rise from **0.4GW currently to 1.5–2.2GW by 2030**.

Raxio operates data centres in **Uganda, Ethiopia, Mozambique, the DRC, Côte d'Ivoire and Angola**, with
expansion into **Tanzania** under development — all **Tier III certified** and carrier-neutral colocation.

The company said it **signed contracts for six times more power capacity in H1-2026 than in H1-2025**, and is
seeing increased interest in projects requiring **10 megawatts or more**, significantly larger than previous
deployments; it is increasing rack densities to support higher-performance computing and AI workloads.

CEO **Robert Skjodt** said demand for high-quality data-centre infrastructure "continues to accelerate across
Africa, driven by rapid digital adoption, cloud migration and the emergence of significant AI workloads."
**Brooks Washington** (founder/CEO, Roha) and **Mete Saracoglu** (COO Africa, Meridiam) both cited confidence
in Raxio's platform and long-term growth potential.
