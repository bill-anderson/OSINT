Thirty years after placing Zimbabwe's first mobile network into service, state-owned NetOne says it is entering a new era of digital innovation and financial recovery, with a plan to become a US$1 billion business by 2029.

Under CEO Engineer Raphael Mushanawani (in post about five years), NetOne — technically insolvent when he took over, with audited losses including US$40 million in 2022 — has returned to growth. It reported a 62% increase in annual revenue and a 68% rise in EBITDA, with mobile data revenue more than doubling (110%). Revenue grew from a projected US$160 million in 2024 to US$210 million in 2025. "We want to be the number one network. We are seeing ourselves being a US$1 billion company within the next five years," Mushanawani said.

The company deployed 86 new base stations over the past year, including hundreds of 4G LTE installations and new 5G sites, and now accounts for more than 46% of Zimbabwe's rural mobile base stations. Beyond telecoms, its flagship OneHealth Telehealth Programme (with the Office of the First Lady) opened a virtual telemedicine hospital in Ntunungwe, Matabeleland South, in October 2025, with more than 20 telehealth centres established. NetOne operates under the Mutapa Investment Fund portfolio.
