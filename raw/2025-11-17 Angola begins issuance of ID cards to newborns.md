Angolan authorities announced a plan to strengthen birth registration and begin issuing national ID cards to newborns, starting in the Northern Province of Zaire. The move aligns with the **'Born with a birth certificate'** initiative supported by the Ministry of Health and UNICEF.

It commences in Mbanza Kongo municipality (which has the region's biggest hospital) before expanding to the province's other 10 municipalities, with registration equipment set up in maternity wards. Provincial delegate for Justice and Human Rights Nicolau Sozinho noted security measures to protect the equipment and to prevent the registration of foreigners, given the province borders the DR Congo. The campaign aims to enhance civil registration in rural and difficult-to-reach communities and expand legal identity. Angola is relying on India's experience and financial support to build its DPI and hopes to model its digital ID system after India's Aadhaar.
