Global Settlement Holdings (GSH), the investment firm behind US-based blockchain infrastructure company Global Settlement Network (GSX), will acquire a majority stake in AKIBA International, a Uganda-based investment and capital-markets advisory firm, deepening its bet on turning Uganda into a hub for tokenised finance in Africa. The deal (subject to regulatory approvals) gives GSH the licences and local infrastructure to run a broker-dealer off-ramp, a regulated exchange for tokenised assets, and a mining platform.

This comes six months after GSX's partnership with Uganda's Diacente Group to build Karamoja (a special economic zone) and launch a CBDC pilot, backed by $5.5 billion in planned commitments. The AKIBA deal extends that work into market infrastructure: infrastructure projects, mining output and export contracts can be tokenised and sold through a licenced exchange overseen by Ugandan regulators. Both companies plan to raise up to $1.5 billion, focusing on infrastructure, special economic zones, responsible mining and trade finance. The system will operate under the Bank of Uganda's regulatory sandbox with a licenced local bank as settlement partner; in 2025 Uganda's Capital Markets Authority (CMA) introduced a parallel fintech sandbox for capital markets. GSH CEO Ryan Kirkley praised Uganda's regulators for "actively building the framework for tokenised capital markets before the market forces them to."
