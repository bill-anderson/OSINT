# Takaful and Karama — Egypt's social-protection platform (World Bank interview)

At the programme's 10th anniversary, World Bank Division Director **Stephane Guimbert** discussed **[[takaful-and-karama|Takaful and Karama]]**, Egypt's flagship cash-transfer programme. Digital payments via **Meeza cards** improved efficiency and financial inclusion, cut leakages and enhanced transparency; the grievance system handled **6 million inquiries** (99.8% response rate).

It rests on a **unified social registry** (Ministry of Social Solidarity) covering **~12 million households**, and reaches **4.7 million active households (~17m individuals, ~20% of families)**. A **national ID-based system** and 3,000+ offices enable real-time monitoring; targeting combines proxy means testing with home verification. The World Bank has committed **$1.4bn+** ($400m 2015, $500m 2019, $500m 2022). Over 3 million families have exited on improved livelihoods.
