# Paratus to launch mobile network

Paratus Group will launch a mobile data network in Namibia challenging the dominant state-owned incumbent, MTC. Barney Harmse, group executive chairman at Windhoek-headquartered Paratus, revealed the plans on the TechCentral Show. The data-only network (using low-band and high-band spectrum for wide coverage plus high speeds) will launch commercially on 2 September [2025] under the Paratus brand. Paratus is already a major player in fibre-to-the-home in Namibia.

Namibia's mobile market: MTC (majority state-owned, some shares on the NSX) holds more than 90% market share; the only other player is state-owned Namibia Telecom (~8–10%) via its TN Mobile brand. "We will be the first private operator venturing into this space," Harmse said, noting Paratus has held the necessary CRAN licences for many years. On MTC's likely response: "They won't hold back their punches... But we are not going to get into a price fight. We will be competitive where it matters."
