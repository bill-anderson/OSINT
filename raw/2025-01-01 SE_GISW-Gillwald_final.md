## Key points

- Frames the **"digital inequality paradox"** — expanding access has coincided with deepening structural
  inequality — arguing supply-side connectivity metrics obscure demand-side exclusion (affordability,
  skills, meaningful use), drawing on RIA's [[research-ict-africa]] *After Access* survey evidence.
- Situates datafication/platformisation within "surveillance capitalism" (Zuboff) and the contested
  "data colonialism" (Couldry & Mejias); calls for a **global digital-public-goods framing** for
  governing digitalisation and datafication.
- Positions WSIS/GDC as flawed but "our only hope" of the global cooperation needed to redress widening
  digital inequality and data injustice.
