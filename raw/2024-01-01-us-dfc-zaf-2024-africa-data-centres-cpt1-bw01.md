# US DFC — Africa Data Centres Cape Town (CPT1) expansion, 2024

Africa Data Centres' CPT1 Cape Town 6 MW expansion was financed through a loan of up to US$300m from the US International Development Finance Corporation (DFC).

## Description

Africa Data Centres, a business of Cassava Technologies, expanded its CPT1 facility in Cape Town by 6 MW of IT load (from 5.5 MW to 11.5 MW), adding 1,000 racks across two colocation halls and one hyperscale hall. The expansion "was implemented through an up to $300 million loan from the US International Development Finance Corporation (DFC)."

## Notes

A separate Africa Data Centres financing in the same source — a ZAR 2bn (US$108m) facility arranged by Rand Merchant Bank for its wider SA expansion — is recorded as its own deal (`rand-merchant-bank-zaf-2024`).
