Paratus lights up new East Africa fiber route

Paratus Group has switched on a 2,000km fiber route connecting Mombasa in Kenya to Goma in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) via Nairobi as well as Kampala, Uganda and Kigali, Rwanda.

Pan-African telecommunications and network services provider Paratus Group has switched on an extensive new fiber route in East Africa.

The route directly connects Mombasa on the Kenyan coast to Goma in the eastern Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) through a protected terrestrial network.

The newly activated 2,000km G2M (Goma-to-Mombasa) fiber route runs via Kigali in Rwanda, Kampala in Uganda and Nairobi in Kenya, with direct interconnections into key data centers in each city.

Paratus said the route is now live and already carrying traffic for its first wholesale customers.

The company said the route delivers resilient, high-capacity connectivity designed specifically for carriers, Internet service providers (ISPs) and enterprise clients operating across borders.

Paratus said the launch significantly strengthens its East African footprint, creating a continuous, fully integrated regional network that links inland markets directly to global subsea cable capacity on the coast.

In partnership with Roke Telekom in Uganda and MoveOn Telecoms in Kenya, Paratus is fully licensed in all East African countries through its subsidiary operating companies, Paratus Rwanda, Paratus Kenya and Paratus Uganda.

The new G2M route also complements Paratus' low-Earth orbit (LEO) satellite footprint across DRC, Rwanda, Uganda and Kenya.

Martin Cox, chief commercial officer of Paratus Group, said G2M represents "a new digital highway for the region."

"By creating a protected route from the coast all the way into Goma, we're giving operators and enterprises direct, reliable access to global capacity. It dramatically improves resilience and performance, while opening new commercial opportunities across Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda and the DRC," Cox said.

"Our strategy has always been about connecting the dots across Africa with quality, contiguous infrastructure. The G2M route strengthens everything we've already built in East Africa," he added.

Paratus Group's new fiber route in East Africa. (Source: Paratus Group)

Paratus said that the route provides businesses and service providers in eastern DRC and neighboring countries with faster access to international networks, improved reliability and lower latency.

The new route complements the Paratus Group's broader East-West fiber backbone, which stretches from Maputo, Mozambique to Swakopmund in Namibia and interconnects with the Equiano subsea cable, ensuring high-capacity redundancy between Africa and Europe.

Paratus Group has operations in 16 African countries, and its footprint extends to international points of presence (PoPs) in Europe, the UK and the US.

East Africa is regarded as one of the continent's fastest-growing regional economies and is experiencing rapid expansion across sectors including fintech, manufacturing, mining, energy, agriculture and ICT.

Paratus said that demand for secure, enterprise-grade connectivity, cloud and managed services is accelerating accordingly.

"Digital infrastructure today is as critical as traditional trade routes were in the past. We're building the networks that make modern commerce possible - and this new route is a key part of that future," Cox added.
