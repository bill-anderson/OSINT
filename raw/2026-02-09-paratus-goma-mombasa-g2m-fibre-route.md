Pan-African telecommunications provider Paratus Group has switched on a 2,000km G2M (Goma-to-Mombasa) fiber route connecting Mombasa in Kenya to Goma in the eastern DRC through a protected terrestrial network, running via Kigali (Rwanda), Kampala (Uganda) and Nairobi (Kenya), with direct interconnections into key data centers in each city. The route is live and already carrying traffic for its first wholesale customers.

Paratus said the route delivers resilient, high-capacity connectivity for carriers, ISPs and enterprise clients operating across borders, significantly strengthening its East African footprint by linking inland markets directly to global subsea cable capacity on the coast. In partnership with Roke Telekom in Uganda and MoveOn Telecoms in Kenya, Paratus is fully licensed in all East African countries through its subsidiaries (Paratus Rwanda, Kenya and Uganda). The G2M route also complements Paratus' LEO satellite footprint across DRC, Rwanda, Uganda and Kenya. Chief commercial officer Martin Cox called G2M a new "digital highway" for the region.
