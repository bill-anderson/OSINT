By Samuel Nyangi, 2026-07-22

Starlink is now one of the fastest-growing satellite broadband providers in Africa, expanding to 26 markets and capturing an estimated 3% of its global subscriber base by June 2026. But the ~300,000 customers connected across the continent tell you less than it appears to.

The top three countries, Nigeria, Zimbabwe, and Zambia, account for approximately 64% of all African subscribers combined. At the same time, despite intense early enthusiasm, Starlink's market share remains marginal in countries like Kenya, commanding just 0.9% of the local ISP market.

These gaps persist because the African broadband landscape is not one uniform market. A consumer relying on Starlink due to the failure of local ISPs and a mobile network operator partnering for rural Direct-to-Cell coverage are operating under completely different conditions, facing different affordability constraints, and navigating a widely varying regulatory landscape. Treating them as part of the same continental growth story produces a misleading picture.

To address that, we built a comprehensive market intelligence report combining country-level subscriber statistics with regulatory developments, pricing, partnerships, service availability, and competitive dynamics. The result is a more granular view of where satellite broadband is expanding, where infrastructure ceilings are constraining it, and why.

What the data shows:

- ~300,000 customers connected across 26 active African markets as of June 2026.
- Nigeria, Zimbabwe, and Zambia account for roughly 64% of the continent's entire subscriber base.
- Zimbabwe emerged as the fastest-growing market, with an average quarterly growth rate of 126.1%, reaching 67,057 users as of December 2025.
- Licensing fees vary by a factor of 18.5 across the continent, ranging from USD 32,000 to USD 575,000.
- Starlink's landmark Direct-to-Cell partnership with Airtel Africa could extend satellite connectivity to approximately 174 million subscribers across 14 markets.
- Median download speeds for Starlink (54.7 Mbps in Q3 2025) are nearly 160% higher than those of local terrestrial ISPs, though local providers maintain a structural advantage in multi-server latency.
- Starlink Impact initiatives have connected over 1,600 schools and 2,000 healthcare centres across Africa, with significant NGO funding support.
