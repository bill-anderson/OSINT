Seychelles National Launch of the e-CO official statement by the Guest of Honour the Principal Secretary for Trade, Ms. Natalie Edmond

As we convene, the objectives of the Government of Seychelles remain clear and focused on the digital expansion of services within the public sector, the need to offer more speed and interoperability in service delivery through ecosystems that are easily accessible.

The shift from paper-based services to digital solutions is more than a call to action that needs to happen for Seychelles not to be excluded from the digital evolution.

SRC through its digital transformation plan continues to evolve its digital pathway, for maintaining Seychelles supply chain, trade facilitation and border security against illicit trade now more than ever is a necessity to safeguard the community, to maintain efficiency and enabling the sustainable growth of the country.

Today, we officially launch the implementation of the electronic certificate of origin with great pride, an essential platform for Seychelles to foster connectivity, modernize trade facilitation contributing towards the digital economic development agenda.

For that, I extend my sincere appreciation to the COMESA Secretariat for building and maintaining the ongoing engagement with the Seychelles Revenue Commission in achieving this project and for being honored in hosting the well planned event today.

As a small island developing state and a COMESA member state, we reaffirm our commitment to empower the trading community to use the platform effectively, to embrace the digital journey of the Customs Division that is present and ahead to come.

I urge the traders to make full use of the platform to benefit from the reduced duties available through the applicable trade agreements, and to also make Seychelles known through their products within the region and beyond, by seizing the opportunities being presented.

Last but not least for achieving this remarkable milestone, I express my gratitude to the project team of the Seychelles Revenue Commission, the staff members from the Information and technology team and the Classification and Valuation of Origin Unit for remaining resilient and for handling the challenges the project brought forth heads on.

It may have taken some time to implement the project, but slowly and steady you have remained positive, learnt from one another and continue to seek the support and guidance of the COMESA in ensuring you remain on track. You have made it, a round of applause for yourself and my congratulations.

To the traders who joined the testing phases of the certificate of origin, thank you for answering the Seychelles Revenue Commission call for participation and for reporting on site at each try out.

To all, thank you for your support, for being present here today and for making this event a success.
