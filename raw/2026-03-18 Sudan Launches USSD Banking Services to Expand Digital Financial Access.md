The Ministry of Digital Transformation and Communications of Sudan has announced the rollout of USSD-based banking services, in coordination with the Central Bank of Sudan and the Communications and Postal Regulatory Authority. Guided by Eng. Ahmed Dardery, Minister of Digital Transformation and Telecommunications, the service is designed to provide citizens with access to financial services via mobile phones even in areas without internet access, or where mobile banking apps are difficult to use during peak periods.

The first phase includes essential banking functions such as interpersonal bank transfers, credit inquiries, and transaction summaries. By leveraging the widespread use of mobile phones, the Ministry aims to expand digital financial services and financial inclusion. The Ministry emphasised close coordination with telecom and banking partners, who will announce operational details for their respective services.
