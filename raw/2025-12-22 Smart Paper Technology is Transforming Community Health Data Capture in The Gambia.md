For decades Gambian health facilities relied on paper registers for antenatal, delivery, postnatal, immunisation and child-health data — the backbone of the country's HMIS, with fragmented records and delayed reporting. At Bundung Maternal and Child Hospital (Serekunda), **Smart Paper Technology (SPT)** — a hybrid system bridging paper workflows with automated digitisation — has transformed health-information management.

Launched in 2017 as a collaboration between The Gambia's Ministry of Health, the Medical Research Council of The Gambia (MRCG) at LSHTM, the **Shifo Foundation**, the US CDC and Bundung Hospital (piloted at Bundung and Fajikunda), SPT assigns unique patient IDs, barcodes and QR codes to every mother and child; structured paper forms are scanned, digitised and synchronised with **[[dhis2]]**. Staff report reduced register use, easier retrieval, SMS appointment reminders, real-time vaccine stock levels and role-based access; reporting lags fell from months to days. Facility-based deliveries in Bundung's catchment rose from 82.5% (2022) to 83.9% (2023).
