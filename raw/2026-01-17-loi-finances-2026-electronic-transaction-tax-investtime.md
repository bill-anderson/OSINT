# Mauritania — 2026 finance law: 0.1% tax on electronic transactions

*Off-assigned-host capture (Invest-Time). Serves the flagged mobile-money watch-item — the fiscalisation of digital payments. Corroborated by trustmag.net (Bankily tariff adjustment, 4 Feb 2026, TTE + higher TOF 16%→20%).*

Mauritania is advancing the **fiscalisation of its digital economy**. The **2026 finance bill** introduces a specific **tax on electronic transactions**, targeting both digital payments and the commissions earned by digital-financial-service agents — part of a broader strategy to widen the tax base and secure public revenue as mobile money, payment apps and digital banking have become central to financial inclusion.

The measure's contours: a **0.1% rate on electronic transactions above 5,000 ouguiyas (MRU)**, covering payments and transfers via electronic wallets, payment apps and digital banking services; commissions earned by approved agents in electronic-payment networks are taxed at **10%**. Collection is at source (VAT-style), remitted monthly to the Treasury, with VAT-aligned penalties for non-compliance.

Sector actors warn of higher transaction costs passed to users, possible disincentive to mobile-money use (especially for low-income populations), and risk to the financial-inclusion dynamic. (Per subsequent reporting, the law was adopted end-2025 and applied from January 2026.)
