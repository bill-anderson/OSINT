# Mauritania–IsDB $50 million digital-transformation financing (principle agreement)

Mauritanian authorities are negotiating a **$50 million financing deal with the Islamic Development Bank (IsDB)** to support digital transformation; the two parties **signed a principle agreement on Tuesday, 14 April 2026**. The programme covers four areas: digital infrastructure and equipment; cybersecurity; interoperability and system modernisation; and digital skills, connectivity and innovation.

Context cited: the **Digital-Y** project (launched January 2025, €4m, with German development cooperation) integrating digital tools into public administration; and EU/World Bank support for the Nouakchott data centre and the new submarine cable (EIB €25m / $29.4m; World Bank $10m). Under its 2022–2025 plan, Mauritania targets digital-sector value added of $700m by 2025 (8% of GDP, up from $350m and 4.5% in 2020) and 15,000 direct/indirect jobs (from 5,000 in 2020).

Mauritania ranks **165th of 193 on the 2024 UN EGDI** (score 0.3491, below the African average 0.4247 and global 0.6382), and sits in the **fourth (second-lowest) tier of the ITU 2024 Global Cybersecurity Index**.
