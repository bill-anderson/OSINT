# Federal Republic of Somalia launches mass registration drive for its digital ID

> ## Excerpt
> On 16 Sep 2025 (International Identification Day) NIRA celebrated the launch of
> its foundational digital ID mass registration campaign; the Shangani/Boondheer
> pilot began 18 Aug 2025. Fewer than 16% of Somalis hold any government ID (2022 SIHBS).

---

This year's International Identification Day on September 16 was memorable: in Mogadishu, Somalia's National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA) celebrated the launch of the mass registration campaign for the long-anticipated foundational digital ID, backed by World Bank support.

On August 18, 2025, NIRA launched the pilot of its foundational ID mass registration campaign in the two districts of Shangani and Boondheer. The campaign aims to register all 3.5 million residents of Mogadishu, extend coverage across the entirety of Banadir region, and ultimately reach 15 million nationwide by the end of 2029. The effort includes training and deploying Federal Government representatives to every district, capturing biographic and biometric data. Once enrolled, citizens receive their foundational ID in physical, digital, and/or printed certificate versions.

One ID, three formats: the foundational ID is available as a physical card, a digital ID wallet (eAqoonsi), or a downloadable and printable verifiable credential (paper certificate with QR code).

The Federal Government, with support from the World Bank through the Somalia Capacity Advancement, Livelihoods and Entrepreneurship Through Digital Uplift Project (SCALED-UP) and the Somalia Productive, Resilient, and Inclusive Growth (SPRING) Project, is investing in the foundational ID. The launch of the digital ID and NIRA's ID verification platform, Hubiye, is seen as a milestone toward mitigating Money Laundering / Terrorist Financing (ML/FT) risks and facilitating financial inclusion.

Despite the importance of legal identity, fewer than 16% of the Somali population currently have some form of government-issued ID (2022 Somali Integrated Household Budget Survey). A 2025 World Bank-financed survey (N=556) and focus groups (N=71) across Banadir, South West State, and Galmudug found high demand: many Somalis see an ID as a tool for empowerment and, for women especially, a gateway to financial independence.

The digital ID also enables online identification, online authentication (streamlining KYC for banks and telecoms), increased inclusion, innovation through interoperability, and improved trust.
