South Africa's Department of Home Affairs has expanded its smart ID card program, granting eligibility to more than 1.4 million individuals previously excluded from the system. As of today, both naturalised citizens and permanent residents (approximately 800,000 and 700,000 people respectively) can now apply for the modern, biometric-enabled ID cards.

**New Era for Identity Inclusion**

Until now, naturalised citizens and permanent residents were required to use the outdated green bar-coded ID book, which has long been criticized for its vulnerability to fraud and forgery. This limitation was due in part to unresolved IT system constraints that barred these groups from accessing the modern smart ID system. The new policy not only expands access but also eliminates the previous invitation-only manual application process that allowed only a small number of applicants to obtain a smart ID under special circumstances.

"For years, these South Africans were forced to use only the green bar-coded ID despite the government's stated intent to do away with this document over fraud concerns," said Minister of Home Affairs Leon Schreiber. He described the reform as restoring dignity to individuals who had effectively been treated as "second-class citizens."

The shift also addresses a significant national security concern. According to recent assessments, the green ID book is up to 500% more vulnerable to identity fraud compared to the smart ID card, which incorporates advanced biometric technology and modern anti-forgery features.

**Phased Rollout and Digital Expansion**

This expansion is being implemented in phases, with initial applications prioritized for individuals from visa-exempt countries. It builds on the Department's broader modernization efforts, including the recent launch of a multi-agency digital ID platform and the agency's five-year plan to become fully digital-first by 2029.

Eligible individuals can now apply for smart ID cards at local Home Affairs branches or via the eHomeAffairs online portal. To manage the anticipated surge in demand, the department is extending weekend operating hours. It is also working to expand the geographic reach of smart ID services by increasing the number of participating bank branches nationwide.

Looking ahead, the Department of Home Affairs is considering the introduction of a home delivery option for ID cards, mirroring the convenience of bank card distribution models.
