**[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** Executive Director Andrew Peters launched a **Five-Year Strategic Plan (2026-2030)** (13 January 2026) aimed at ensuring every Liberian and legal resident is recognised through a secure, trusted and inclusive national identification system, saying that "without reliable identity, inclusion remains incomplete."

The strategy is aligned with the Government's ARREST Agenda, focused on digital transformation, financial inclusion and public-sector accountability. Peters acknowledged gains since the NIR's 2011 establishment (biometric enrollment, NIN issuance, institutional partnerships) but flagged persistent challenges: **low national coverage, weak infrastructure, institutional-capacity gaps, evolving data-protection demands, and fragile public trust**. The plan rests on six pillars — universal coverage; strengthened governance and institutional capacity; digital-ID modernization; infrastructure development; improved legal and data-protection frameworks; and financial sustainability through partnerships — with digital IDs, mobile credentials and interoperable platforms pursued responsibly, focused on rural and underserved communities.
