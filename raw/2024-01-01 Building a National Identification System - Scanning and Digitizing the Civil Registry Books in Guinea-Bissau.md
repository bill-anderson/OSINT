UNDP project page (year precision 2024/2025). The main objective is to enable the Government of Guinea-Bissau to ensure its citizens have access to identity documentation to fully exercise their citizenship rights. UNDP supports a robust identity-documentation system promoting inclusive citizenship — contributing to the right to identity, improving the registration system with progressive integration of various identification systems, and creating more transparent, reliable data systems.

Impact areas: setting up and equipping a **digitization centre**; organizing and recovering the **civil registry books**; and developing complete software to digitize the civil registry (births, deaths, marriages and annotations) as the main national platform. Funded by UNDP with co-financing from the Swiss Confederation; the main partner/beneficiary is the Ministry of Justice and Human Rights.
