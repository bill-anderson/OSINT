# Somalia digital ID major pillar of World Bank-funded SPRING initiative

> ## Excerpt

> 2025 >1 million Somalis were enrolled for the national ID with >24 public
> services integrated. President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud urged every citizen to enrol.

---

Since 2023, Somalia has been running an ambitious national identity project which the government views as a crucial part of its digital transformation agenda. The National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA) hopes to ensure at least 15 million Somalis have a national ID by 2029.

Last month, the country's Finance Minister launched the Somalia Productive, Resilience and Inclusive Growth (SPRING) initiative, citing NIRA among public institutions responsible for supporting its objectives. The SPRING project, estimated at more than $100 million, seeks to support efforts mostly driven by the private sector to create jobs and enhance the digital economy. Its main objective is to strengthen economic resilience by improving the business environment, expanding access to financial and digitally enabled services, and supporting micro, small, and medium enterprises (MSMEs).

Other institutions involved include the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, the Central Bank of Somalia, the Financial Reporting Center, and the agency responsible for MSMEs. Digital ID and DPI are framed as necessary for ensuring individuals — especially women, marginalized groups, and displaced persons — can securely access financial services, training, and credit, reducing exclusion risks and enabling transparent identification of beneficiaries for social protection.

At a recent National ID conference in Mogadishu, NIRA indicated plans to have more public services integrated with the national ID. Already, more than one million Somalis have been enrolled for the national ID, with more than 24 public services integrated. President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud underlined the importance of a national ID and urged every citizen to have one.
