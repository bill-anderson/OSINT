Note: same launch event as several other outlets (technomag, origins); this is the Techzim analytical account. Keep group-vs-opco distinction in mind (ISSUE-009): Econet AI is a unit under Econet Wireless Zimbabwe / Econet Group, drawing compute from Cassava Technologies (also Masiyiwa's Econet Group).

Econet has officially launched Econet AI, moving from selling connectivity to helping businesses use AI. It bundles tools (Yamurai AI), business solutions (OmniContact AI) and consulting/platform services, working with CEOs and leadership teams, not just IT departments.

The key element is AI compute: Econet AI says it now has access to compute through Cassava Technologies, which has been building an "AI factory," rolling out GPU infrastructure and building partnerships across Africa. However, Techzim notes it is not clear the compute is physically in Zimbabwe — businesses can use the power even if the machines are elsewhere — though Econet spoke of a commitment to bring more capability into Zimbabwe over time. For context, accessing high-performance computing locally (e.g. the University of Zimbabwe HPC) can cost tens of thousands of dollars, effectively pricing out students and small developers.

Econet also mentioned data centres and cloud services, a sovereign cloud for government and institutions, and work on local-language AI (making Yamurai sound more Zimbabwean, handling Shona) — a localisation angle that global AI tools often get wrong.
