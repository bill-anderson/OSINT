In 2024, 19 African countries are recognised as strong e-government performers (EGDI of at least 0.5). South Africa (0.8616), Mauritius (0.7506) and Tunisia (0.6935) top the continent in the latest UN E-Government Development Index, per UN DESA's "E-Government Survey 2024: Accelerating Digital Transformation for Sustainable Development."

The EGDI combines the Online Service Index (OSI), Telecommunications Infrastructure Index (TII) and Human Capital Index (HCI). Globally the average EGDI rose to 0.6382 in 2024 (from 0.6102 in 2022); Africa's average rose to 0.4247 (from 0.4054). South Africa and Mauritius are the only African states with very high scores (above 0.75), placing them among global leaders; also in the global top 100 are Morocco (0.6841), Seychelles (0.6773) and Egypt (0.6699). Africa's biggest driver was the TII, up 27.8% (0.3546 to 0.4534), reflecting increased digital-infrastructure investment.

Regional EGDI increases between the 2022 and 2024 editions: **Asia 7.7%**, **Africa 4.8%**, **the Americas and Oceania 4.1%**, **Europe 2.3%**.

African performance bands (2024): **19** countries at 0.5 or above, of which only **South Africa and Mauritius** exceed 0.75; **17** in the high band (0.50–0.75); **28** medium (0.25–0.50); **7** low. The lowest-ranked include Burundi, Niger, Chad, Eritrea, Somalia, South Sudan and the Central African Republic (named as a group, without individual scores).

**Tunisia has surpassed Seychelles** in this edition. The survey is framed against Africa's 2020–2030 Digital Transformation Strategy.
