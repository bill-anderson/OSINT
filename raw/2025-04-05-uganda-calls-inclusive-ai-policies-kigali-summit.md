Uganda has urged African countries to adopt inclusive artificial intelligence (AI) policies, in remarks by ICT state minister Joyce Nabbosa Ssebugwawo at the Global AI Summit on Africa in Kigali, Rwanda (a two-day event ending April 4, 2025, themed "AI and Africa's Demographic Dividend"). She urged inclusive and ethical approaches: "we must ensure these advances benefit all communities — urban and rural, rich and poor. Equity must guide innovation," and called for harmonising regulatory frameworks across African nations.

Ssebugwawo revealed that Uganda is in the final stages of developing a National AI Strategy aligned with its five-year Digital Transformation Roadmap. The strategy emphasises digital infrastructure, cybersecurity, data privacy, digital skilling, innovation and entrepreneurship. "We are not simply preparing for the future. We are actively shaping it... Our strategy is rooted in the belief that homegrown innovation, backed by sound policy and partnerships, can elevate lives and unlock inclusive growth."
