# Airtel Africa (Nxtra) — Kenya 44MW data centre, Tatu City, 2025

Airtel Africa, through its data-centre arm Nxtra, broke ground in September 2025 on a 44MW data centre in Tatu City near Nairobi, with ~KES 19 billion (~US$147 million) invested.

## Description

"India's Airtel has broken ground on a 44MW data center in Kenya, developed by Airtel Africa and its data-center arm Nxtra, located in Tatu City — a special economic zone outside Nairobi... The facility will be developed in two phases (22MW each), operational by Q1 2027 — reportedly the country's largest by capacity, with ~KES 19 billion (~$147 million) invested. Nxtra launched in Africa in January 2024 and is jointly owned by Airtel Africa and Carlyle (24% stake)."
