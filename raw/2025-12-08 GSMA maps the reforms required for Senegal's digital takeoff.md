# GSMA maps the reforms required for Senegal's digital takeoff

The **[[gsma]]** presented a study ("Stimuler la transformation numérique de l'économie au Sénégal") to the
government on **5 December 2025** at the first Digital Africa Summit Senegal (Dakar), mapping reforms to hit
**[[new-deal-technologique|New Deal]] 2034** targets. State of play (2025): 4G reaches 97% of population, ~39%
5G (urban); ~52% used mobile services but only ~42% mobile broadband (usage gap ~54%). Financial inclusion is
a standout — **76% of adults hold a mobile-money account** (BCEAO), 14.65m active accounts (2024), ~2.9bn
transactions worth CFA48,488bn; mobile money ~$6bn / ~8.6% of GDP (2023). Recommendations: cut sector taxes
to 3%, exempt sub-CFA17,500 smartphones from VAT/customs, **eliminate the 0.5% mobile-money transfer tax**,
harmonise operator taxation, and recognise telecom as critical national infrastructure integrated into
energy programmes (Energy Pact / Mission 300). Full implementation projected: +2.6m mobile-internet users
(13.1m total, 60%), CFA1,100bn added value, ~280,000 jobs by 2030. Minister Alioune Sall welcomed it.
