# Mauritania — second submarine cable + connectivity/affordability baseline (Agence Ecofin)

*Off-assigned-host capture (Agence Ecofin). Kept alongside the ami.mr landing source for its distinct dated connectivity BASELINE data (ITU affordability, internet-use rate) — the flagged "dated connectivity baseline is valuable."*

Mauritania is advancing its **second international fibre-optic submarine cable**. On **Saturday 4 May 2026** authorities installed the coastal section (28.4 km) at Nouadhibou; the main offshore cable-laying is scheduled for **August 2026**, with commissioning expected **January 2027**. Led by Digital Transformation Minister Ahmed Salem Ould Bede.

The new cable complements the **ACE cable, to which Mauritania has been connected since 2011 and on which it still depends heavily** — outages on ACE have repeatedly disrupted internet and mobile services nationwide, and even scheduled maintenance cuts bandwidth, given the lack of a fully operational alternative. The new cable offers **200 Gbps initial capacity, expandable to 12 Tbps**, linking Mauritania to Europe via Portugal and South America via Brazil, providing redundancy and resilience.

Baseline / affordability data (valuable, dated):
- A June 2025 FERDI report: doubling international capacity can cut prices up to **32% for fixed broadband and 50% for mobile broadband**.
- **Spending on 5 GB of mobile data = 2.94% of GNI per capita (2025, ITU)**; fixed internet = **17.6% of GNI per capita** — both far above the 2% affordability threshold (fixed especially).
- **Only 45.8% of Mauritanians used the internet in 2024 (ITU).**
