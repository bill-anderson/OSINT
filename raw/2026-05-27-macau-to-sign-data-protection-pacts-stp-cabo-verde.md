# Macau to sign personal data protection pacts with Cape Verde, Sao Tome and Principe

By LUSA, 27 May 2026

Macau's Personal Data Protection Bureau (DSPDP) will sign cooperation agreements with its counterparts in Cape Verde and Sao Tome and Principe, the government announced on Tuesday.

The authorisation granted to DSPDP Director Yang Chongwei was confirmed via two executive orders published in the Macau Official Gazette and signed by Chief Executive Sam Hou Fai. Under the directives, the DSPDP has received approval to sign memorandums of understanding with the National Data Protection Commission of Cape Verde (CNPD) and the National Personal Data Protection Agency of Sao Tome and Principe.

## Regional regulator deepens Lusophone network integration

The bilateral agreements follow the DSPDP's formal induction in early April into the Lusophone Personal Data Protection Network (RLPD). Prior to Macau's inclusion, the international body comprised the data protection authorities of Angola, Brazil, Cape Verde, Portugal, and Sao Tome and Principe. Established in 2024, the presidency of the RLPD is currently held by Brazil's National Data Protection Authority, while the Permanent Secretariat is managed by the Portuguese National Data Protection Commission.

At the time of joining, the DSPDP described its membership as an important step for Macau to leverage its unique advantages, serve national development goals, and support China's broader platform for opening up to the global community. The bureau pledged to utilize historical ties with the Portuguese language and local bilingual capabilities to establish a cooperative bridge between mainland China and Portuguese-speaking countries.

## Digital economy highlights cross-border data flows

The regulatory body emphasised the strong potential for mutual learning and reciprocal benefits among member watchdogs, particularly in critical areas such as personal data protection, data governance, and cross-border data flows. Data elements have become increasingly vital in economic cooperation between China and Lusophone countries, driven by the widespread adoption of technology, artificial intelligence, and the evolution of the digital economy.

The initiative aligns with broader global trends, as both China and the European Union place growing emphasis on data utilization and trade while accelerating the establishment of robust regulatory frameworks.

Regulatory ties between Macau and Cape Verde date back to 2017, when a delegation led by CNPD President Faustino Varela Monteiro visited Macau to meet with local officials, where both sides agreed to deepen institutional cooperation. The DSPDP serves as the primary authority responsible for supervising, coordinating, and enforcing personal data protection laws in Macau, including oversight of the city's public video surveillance system.
