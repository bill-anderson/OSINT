# African Guidelines on Data Access (draft, ACHPR Res.620)

> ## Excerpt
> A **draft** soft-law instrument circulated by the African Commission (via ALT Advisory) for
> consultation to **14 July 2026**, operationalising **ACHPR Resolution 620 (2024)** by reframing
> access to data as a component of the right to information (African Charter Art. 9) and the right to

## Synthesis

The Guidelines set out principles, legal/policy/institutional measures, exemptions, enforcement and
AI-specific provisions to compel **both public bodies and private actors** to make data accessible for
human-rights and development purposes. Core principles: maximum disclosure; proactive disclosure of
high-value public-interest datasets (budgets, procurement, health, environment); data-access-by-design;
data justice for marginalised groups; and **complementarity between access and data protection** so
privacy law cannot be weaponised to block legitimate public-interest access. The 30 numbered measures
direct states to domesticate the **Malabo Convention** and the **AU Data Policy Framework**, enact
open-data policies, create/strengthen independent oversight (an Information Commission or DPA),
prioritise national/regional data-centre storage "to promote sovereignty," and cooperate
pan-Africanly against transnational platforms’ data dominance. Appendices detail sector rules (health,
environmental, research, budget, children’s, platform data), institutional architecture (Information
Commission, National Statistical Office as data steward, National Data Advisory Council) and a
four-tier enforcement ladder culminating in judicial sanctions.

## Key facts

- Issued in fulfilment of **ACHPR/Res.620 (LXXXI) 2024**, adopted at the Commission’s 81st Ordinary
  Session; mandates the Special Rapporteur (FoE/ATI) to develop the standards. Foreword signed by
  **Commissioner Geereesha Topsy-Sonoo**.
- Self-describes as a **soft-law instrument** offering an authoritative interpretation of state-party
  obligations under African Charter Arts 9 & 22.
- Standard data-access requests answered within **30 days**; appeals resolved within max **90 days**;
  national frameworks reviewed every **3–5 years**; reporting via Art. 62 periodic reports.
- **Four-tier enforcement**: transparency → corrective action (≥90 days) → civil remedies → judicial
  sanctions.
- Pan-African / continental scope; **no specific countries named**. Draft — not yet adopted.
