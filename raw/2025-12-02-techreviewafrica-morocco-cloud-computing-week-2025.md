# Morocco launches Cloud Computing Week 2025

Morocco's Ministry of Digital Transition and Administration Reform has announced the launch of Cloud Computing Week 2025, an initiative aimed at strengthening the technical capacities of IT managers and RAED network administrators across public institutions. The program is being organized in collaboration with Oracle.

The workshop was chaired by Amal Al-Falah Al-Saghroushni, Minister Delegate to the Prime Minister responsible for digital transformation and administrative reform. It forms part of the wider Digital Morocco Strategy 2030 and the national Cloud Computing Roadmap for 2025–2030, both of which prioritize cloud adoption as a central driver of administrative modernization.

According to the Ministry, the initiative seeks to accelerate digital transformation within government departments, improve the quality and accessibility of services provided to citizens and businesses, and reinforce a secure environment grounded in cybersecurity and strong data governance.

Cloud Computing Week 2025 marks a key step in Morocco's efforts to embed advanced digital infrastructure across its public administration, supporting more efficient, resilient, and citizen-focused service delivery.
