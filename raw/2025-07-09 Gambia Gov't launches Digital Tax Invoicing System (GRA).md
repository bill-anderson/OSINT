The Government, through the Gambia Revenue Authority (GRA), launched a **Digital Invoicing System** to modernise tax administration and boost revenue collection. In April 2025 the government signed the e-invoicing agreement with **Avatar Technologies** (Greenbelt, Maryland, USA).

The platform simplifies invoicing, reduces errors and enables real-time monitoring of sales data to reduce under-declaration and evasion, accompanied by a tamper-proof digital signature. It follows a GRA due-diligence visit to Côte d'Ivoire and complements other digital reforms (Digital Weigh Bridge, Fuel Integrity Marking System, Digital Tax Stamp). Commissioner General Yankuba Darboe called it a critical step to widen the tax base and promote transparency; Finance Minister Seedy Keita said it marks a milestone toward a fully digital tax ecosystem.
