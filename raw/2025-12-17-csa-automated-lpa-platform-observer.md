# CSA Goes Live with Automated LPA Platform

By David A. Yates — Liberian Observer, December 17, 2025

The Civil Service Agency (CSA), in partnership with Mwetana Consulting and Technology Group, has officially gone live with the Legal Power of Attorney (LPA) Automated Platform, marking a step in modernizing public service delivery and strengthening accountability in government transactions. The rollout, led by CSA Director-General Dr. Josiah Joekai, took place on Tuesday, December 16, 2025, at M2 Stores in Monrovia.

The new automated system requires biometric enrollment of every government employee using fingerprint identification, ensuring real-time verification and significantly reducing opportunities for impersonation, forgery and unauthorized transactions. Vendors participating in the program are equipped with customized LPA Point-of-Sale (POS) verification machines connected directly to the CSA database. Civil servants can walk into any approved LPA store, verify their identity biometrically, select their goods and leave without making any down payment. The total cost is spread over six months with modest monthly salary deductions, covering civil servants across 24 Liberian and foreign businesses that have signed onto the program nationwide.

"We found a system that had lost its soul," Dr. Joekai said, describing the previous paper-based LPA process as outdated, cumbersome and prone to fraud. "Civil servants filled out endless forms. Files were misplaced. Approvals took longer. Honest workers were left waiting in despair." He said digitization was the only viable solution to eliminate manipulation and delays: "Every government employee will now be enrolled through fingerprint identification. Vendors will operate LPA-approved Point-of-Sale devices connected to the CSA database."

The Director-General disclosed that 5,000 civil servants have already been enrolled, including staff from 16 government entities. He commended President Boakai and thanked the Ministry of Finance and Development Planning, the Ministry of Justice, and financial institutions including ECOBANK, International Bank Liberia Limited (IBLL) and AfriLand Bank. Participating vendors, including M2 Stores owner Edith Greene Manini, welcomed the automated system as a major improvement over the old arrangement, saying the biometric system offers civil servants a safer and more efficient way to manage purchases.
