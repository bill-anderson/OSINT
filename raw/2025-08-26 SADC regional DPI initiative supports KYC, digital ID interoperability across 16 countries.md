A partnership unveiled (August 2025) as **"Africa's first" regional cross-border digital-ID framework** — the **SADC Committee of Central Bank Governors (CCBG)**, **Co-Develop** (a DPI fund and 50-in-5 campaign partner) and **FinMark Trust** — aims to build a **federated digital eKYC ecosystem across the 16 SADC member states** (Angola, Botswana, Comoros, DRC, Eswatini, Lesotho, Madagascar, Malawi, Mauritius, Mozambique, Namibia, Seychelles, South Africa, Tanzania, Zambia, Zimbabwe).

FinMark Trust provides technical support for the integrated components — **federated digital ID, interoperable data exchange and enhanced digital payments** — following a real-time cross-border ID-verification-for-payments pilot between **South Africa and Lesotho**. The problem it targets: siloed national ID systems block **more than 3.7 million** nationals living outside their home countries from banking and digital payments; a shared, reusable rail replaces the need for 16 bilateral agreements. Co-Develop's Robert Karanja called SADC's regional-DPI leadership a model for "transformation at unprecedented scale."
