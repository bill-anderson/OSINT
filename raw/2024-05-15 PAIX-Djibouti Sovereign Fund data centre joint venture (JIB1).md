# PAIX–Djibouti Sovereign Fund data-centre joint venture (JIB1)

**[[paix-data-centres|PAIX Data Centres]]** announced a strategic joint venture with the **[[fonds-souverain-djibouti|Fonds Souverain de Djibouti (FSD)]]** — Djibouti's Sovereign Fund — to build a **cloud- and carrier-neutral data centre** in Djibouti (**JIB1**). PAIX purchases the land, buildings and equipment; the facility will have about **50,000 sq ft of net usable space and up to 5 MW** of critical power, with the **first phase expected to open in 2026**. Per CNBC Africa, the collaboration brings a **$50 million investment**.

The centre is positioned as a strategic interconnection hub for ISPs, cloud providers, financial institutions and enterprises across Djibouti and the broader East-African region, tapping Djibouti's landing of **~10 undersea cables** (with more under construction). PAIX CEO **Wouter van Hulten** framed Djibouti as "a highly attractive gateway" "at the crossroads of connectivity between Africa, Europe, the Middle East, and Asia"; FSD's **Jean-Andre Gbarssin** called the country "the heart of Africa's digital economy." PAIX already operates in **Accra (Ghana)** and **Nairobi (Kenya)**.
