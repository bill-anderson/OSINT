# Sparc moves to end Malawi's reliance on offshore data

Sparc Systems has launched a new commercial data centre in Lilongwe (unveiled during its "Think Tech Thursday" event) aimed at strengthening local hosting capacity and reducing reliance on foreign-hosted data. Managing Director Wisely Phiri said the lack of affordable local infrastructure has forced many organisations to host their data outside Malawi: "most companies cannot afford to build and maintain their own data infrastructure, so they end up hosting data outside." He noted reliance on foreign systems increases operational costs due to foreign-currency payments while raising concerns around data ownership and sovereignty.

Phiri said the new facility provides a local alternative offering affordable hosting that reduces the need for heavy capital investment and supports both large enterprises and smaller firms. Paul Kanthambi, Data Centre Manager in the Department of e-Government (Ministry of Information), said the initiative marks a significant step toward strengthening Malawi's digital infrastructure and provides "a secure platform where data can be stored locally," aligned with Malawi 2063.

(Sits alongside the government's own National Data Centre and the AI-focused micro-data-centre plans by Converged Technology Networks — a growing local data-hosting market.)
