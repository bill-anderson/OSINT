# CNI biométrique : 16 milliards de BIF prévus dans le projet de loi budgétaire 2026-2027

The Burundian fisc has **tabled 16 billion BIF in the draft FY2026/27 budget bill** to
finance the **biometric national ID card (CNI biométrique)** — a proposed appropriation
for a digital-identity line, disclosed by the Minister of Finance, Budget and Digital
Economy, Alain Ndikumana, at a public broadcast in Gitega on 11 June 2026.

## Description

## Notes

- **`deal_id` uses a name slug**, not a vote/programme code: this is a reporting/statement
  source (spec case 4), so classification codes are left blank, not inferred.
- **`budget_stage: proposed`** — the figure is in the *projet de loi budgétaire* 2026-2027,
  tabled and not yet enacted; it is a record at `proposed`, to be reset/superseded by the
  appropriated figure when the FY2026/27 budget law is enacted.
- **Origin.** Recorded `domestic-state` at its stated origin (a line in the national budget
  bill); `funding_source` unstated. This is very likely the **fisc counterpart** to the
  World-Bank-financed ([[pafen-burundi|PAFEN]], P176396) identity workstream that has carried
  the biometric-ID project since 2022 — but the document states only the domestic budget line,
  not a counterpart split, so it is recorded whole at domestic-state per the origin gate ("only
  where the document states both parts separately" does a split apply).
- **Relation to the ~15 bn BIF whole-project cost** reported 2026-05-08 (Burundi Eco,
  [[2026-05-08-burundi-eco-carte-identite-biometrique-ambitions-defis]]): that figure is a
  *whole-project cost estimate*, not an appropriation, and failed the scope/stage test — no
  record built from it. This 16 bn BIF is the appropriated (proposed) counterpart and the figure
  the domestic-state series keys on.
- **`amount_usd` left blank deliberately.** Burundi runs a heavily managed / multiple exchange
  rate, and FY2026/27 is a future year with no published average; a USD figure cannot be derived
  safely and is not fabricated (spec SSOT rule). To be computed as an FY-average conversion once
  the year closes.
- `amount_capital` / `amount_recurrent` not stated — only a combined figure given; not split.
