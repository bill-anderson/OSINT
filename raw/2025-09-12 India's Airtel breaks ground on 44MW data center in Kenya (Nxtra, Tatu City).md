India's Airtel has broken ground on a **44MW data center** in Kenya, developed by **[[airtel-africa]]** and its data-center arm **Nxtra**, located in **Tatu City** — a special economic zone outside Nairobi offering low corporate taxes and import/stamp-duty exemptions, claiming ~95% renewable energy (a third solar).

The facility will be developed in two phases (22MW each), operational by **Q1 2027** — reportedly the country's largest by capacity, with ~KES 19 billion (~$147 million) invested. Nxtra launched in Africa in January 2024 and is jointly owned by Airtel Africa and Carlyle (24% stake). Most of Kenya's data centers cluster around Nairobi (Safaricom, iXAfrica, Digital Realty).
