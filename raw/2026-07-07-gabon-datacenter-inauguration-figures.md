# Gabon Launches $14 Million Tier III Data Center in Digital Sovereignty Push

Gabon has inaugurated its first national data center, a Tier III-certified facility built with an investment of 8 billion CFA francs ($14 million).

The government aims to reduce reliance on foreign hosting, as about 95% of data generated in Gabon is currently stored abroad.

The project supports Gabon's digital sovereignty strategy as Africa's data center market is projected to almost double by 2030.

President Brice Clotaire Oligui Nguema inaugurated the facility on Friday, July 3, in Nkok, within the Libreville Special Economic Zone. ST Digital built the facility and secured Tier III certification, an international standard that ensures high availability through redundant power supply and cooling systems. The project required an investment of CFA8 billion (about $14 million).

The infrastructure includes three dedicated areas: a cloud services room, a colocation room and a private hosting room. In addition, the facility includes infrastructure designed for artificial intelligence applications.

ST Digital also equipped the data center with environmentally sustainable technologies, including a partial solar power supply and a water-free cooling system. Moreover, the company staffed the facility exclusively with Gabonese engineers and technicians.

According to ST Digital, about 95% of the data generated in Gabon is currently hosted outside the country. The data center covers more than 3,000 square meters and provides capacity for 92 server racks, each capable of accommodating up to 42 physical servers. At full capacity, the facility could host about 3,000 physical servers or between 20,000 and 35,000 virtual servers.

The infrastructure currently has one megawatt of installed electrical capacity, and operators plan to increase capacity gradually through several expansion phases.

The project reflects a broader effort to address Africa's structural shortage of data center infrastructure. According to the State of African Energy 2026 report by the African Energy Chamber, Africa had only 223 data centers across 38 countries as of mid-2025, representing less than 0.02% of the global total.

South Africa accounted for 56 facilities, followed by Kenya with 19 and Nigeria with 17. Together, those three countries represented 41% of Africa's total data center capacity.

Central Africa continues to lag behind other regions in digital infrastructure. However, market research firm Research and Markets projects that Africa's data center market will grow from $3.49 billion in 2024 to $6.81 billion by 2030, representing a compound annual growth rate of 11.79%.

The firm expects public-sector digitalization, the expansion of fintech services and growing investment in artificial intelligence to drive that growth.
