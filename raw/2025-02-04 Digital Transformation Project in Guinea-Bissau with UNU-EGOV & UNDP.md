On 31 January 2025, UNU-EGOV and UNDP Bissau met Prime Minister Rui Duarte Barros to discuss next steps for the **National Digital Transformation Strategy for Guinea-Bissau (ENTD.GW)**, particularly its approval by the Council of Ministers. The meeting addressed the telecommunications market and its challenges, the Universal Access Fund as a mechanism to expand digital infrastructure, short-term priority interventions, and the decree needed for approval.

Since July 2024, UNU-EGOV has collaborated with UNDP Bissau to develop the ENTD.GW. The strategy was officially launched on 29 January 2025 (with the PM and the Minister of Transportation, Telecommunication and the Digital Economy). A full-day capacity-building event on 31 January gathered nearly 50 participants from ministries and public institutions, in partnership with ITMA (Technological Institute for Administrative Modernization). The ENTD.GW rests on **ten strategic pillars** (infrastructure, data, economy, people, literacy, governance, innovation, trust, sustainability and public services), with six strategic objectives and fifty-five interventions, and was stakeholder-driven (consultations led by Beatriz Lanza). A second phase of the UNU-EGOV/UNDP collaboration is underway.
