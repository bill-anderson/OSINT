On 18 February 2025, Mali unveiled a new digital platform to facilitate access to administrative, identity and travel documents for its diaspora, deployed across the country's diplomatic and consular missions worldwide, with advanced security and data-protection measures.

Foreign Affairs Minister Abdoulaye Diop said the platform launched and became operational within 15 days, reflecting a commitment to a more efficient administration and to safeguarding administrative data. Fully designed and developed by Malian experts, it strengthens digital sovereignty and will also digitise the visa application process; in future it will be compatible with the Visa Liptako, a confederal visa system introduced by the Alliance of Sahel States (AES) to facilitate regional mobility. Mali climbed 13 spots in the UN EGDI, now ranking 141st of 193.
