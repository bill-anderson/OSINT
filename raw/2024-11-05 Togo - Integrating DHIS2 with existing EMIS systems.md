(DHIS2 country implementation profile page.)

Togo's Ministry of Primary, Secondary, Technical Education has relied on collecting data on paper forms, forwarded to inspectorates for entry into StatEduc, an education statistical application used in about 20 countries. With support from Norad (Norwegian Agency for Development Cooperation), GPE KIX (Global Partnership for Education Knowledge and Innovation Exchange) and HISP West and Central Africa (WCA), the ministry is piloting DHIS2 alongside the existing StatEduc application. StatEduc continues as the data-collection tool, while DHIS2 processes and provides flexible information products.

The combination facilitates data-sharing at all levels of the education system, giving schools and sub-national actors access to dynamic dashboards. It addresses gaps in visualization and analysis of real-time data through indicators such as enrolment by level, teacher-pupil ratios, and national exam success rates (disaggregated by gender, disability status, etc.). Integration allowed legacy data to be used for trend visualization and comparative analysis.

Implementation profile:
- Start date & current scale: Since 2019, national scale.
- DHIS2 products in use: Aggregate integration with StatEduc.
- Local innovations & integrations: School observation tool, School report card, WhatsApp bot for exam results, MICS-EAGLE survey integration.
- Funders & partners: Norad, GPE KIX, Ministry of Education, HISP WCA.

Togo is one of six Ministries of Education in the global DHIS2 for Education research project (with Eswatini, Mozambique, The Gambia, Sri Lanka and Uganda). Contact: Edem Kossi, HISP WCA.
