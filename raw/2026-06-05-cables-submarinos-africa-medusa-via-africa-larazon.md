Regional-context reference piece (Spanish mainstream) confirming GNQ-specific submarine-cable developments. **Medusa** (backed by AFR-IX Telecom; EU contributed €14.3m to its African extension) will get a **new landing in Equatorial Guinea** following an agreement between the government and the cable's operators — the country's second submarine cable after ACE (operative since Dec 2012). For GNQ the landing is expected **2029–2030**, cost **€20–60m**, subject to further maritime studies.

Additionally, GNQ wants to connect to **Via Africa**, a cable backed by a consortium of seven operators that signed a development MoU on **12 May 2026**, planning a UK–South Africa Atlantic route with landings including the UK, France, Portugal, Canary Islands, Mauritania, Senegal, Guinea, Côte d'Ivoire and Nigeria. Article notes single-cable dependency vulnerability (the March 2024 multi-cable West/Central Africa outage; a Jan 2026 WACS fault cutting DRC) and, per TeleGeography's 2025 map, 77 cable systems now serve Africa.
