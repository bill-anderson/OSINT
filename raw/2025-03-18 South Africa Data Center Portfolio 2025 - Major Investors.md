[Market research database announcement / reference resource.]

The "South Africa Existing & Upcoming Data Center Portfolio" database has been added to ResearchAndMarkets.com's offering.

South Africa's data center market is set for major expansion, with $1.5 billion in investments by 2026 and 200MW of upcoming capacity. Johannesburg dominates the upcoming data center market in South Africa, with almost 75% of the total power capacity. Around $1.5 billion in new investments is expected to flow into upcoming data centers in South Africa by 2026.

Almost 170 MW of additional power capacity is expected to be added by the end of 2025. The existing data center capacity in South Africa is almost 300 MW, while the upcoming capacity is expected to be around 200 MW. The market includes 53 existing data centers and 10 new (upcoming) facilities across Cape Town, Durban, Pretoria, and more.

The database covers retail colocation pricing (quarter/half/full rack), wholesale colocation (per kW) pricing, and data points per facility including location, operator/owner name, core & shell area and power capacity, rack capacity, year of operations, design standards (Tier I–IV), and power/cooling redundancy. Upcoming facilities (10) are covered with investment snapshots, investor name, area, power capacity, investment ($ million) split across electrical/mechanical/general construction, announcement year, and project status.

Major operators/investors covered include: Africa Data Centres, Business Connexion, CipherWave, Digital Parks Africa, Equinix, Internet Solutions ZA, MTN, NTT & Dimension Data, Open Access Data Centres (OADC), Paratus, Teraco (Digital Realty), Vantage Data Centers, and Vodacom Business.
