The Guinean Backbone Management Society (SOGEB) and Sierra Leone's Leonecom signed an Interconnection Agreement to link their national fibre optic networks. The agreement, formalized in Conakry (signed 12/17 June 2025), builds on a broader telecommunications cooperation framework and the Guinea–Sierra Leone Digital Pact 2024.

The partnership is designed to enhance cross-border data exchange, cut internet and transit costs, and strengthen network resilience through shared infrastructure and redundancy. It aligns with ECOWAS and African Union initiatives promoting a unified digital market.

The interconnection point is at Pamelap, a key border town. Initial transmission capacity is 42.5 Gbps, with expansion plans to reach 100 Gbps by 2026, using fibre optic technology and secure IP/MPLS protocols.

Mohamed Kourouma, Director of SOGEB, called it "a major milestone for Guinea." Foday Rado Yokie, CEO of Leonecom, said the collaboration "will improve broadband access in Sierra Leone and stimulate economic interaction with Guinea." Nouhan Traoré, Secretary General of Guinea's Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and the Digital Economy, described it as "a key component of the Guinea–Sierra Leone Digital Pact 2024."

Operators Orange and MTN stand to gain from lower transit costs. Discussions are underway to extend the fibre network toward Liberia.
