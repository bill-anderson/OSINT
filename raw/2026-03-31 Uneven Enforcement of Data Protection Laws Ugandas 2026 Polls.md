# Uneven Enforcement of Data Protection Laws Puts Data Subjects’ Rights at Risk in Uganda’s 2026 Polls

> ## Excerpt
> A Data Protection Election Watch report by Unwanted Witness assessing how personal data was
> governed across Uganda’s **January 2026 general elections** — the country’s first fully digitised
> electoral cycle. Central finding: not a legislative vacuum but an **implementation deficit**.

> **Provenance note.** CSO election-monitoring report; funded by APC but findings independent. Several
> technical/data-flow claims are inference-based (authors could not access vendor contracts or backend
> architecture) and some allegations (MTN 2016 subscriber-data sharing) are self-referential — treat
> those as *alleged*. Hardware and registration figures are sourced to Electoral Commission statements
> and the public PDPO register.

## Synthesis

Uganda entered its January 2026 general elections with a modern data-protection framework on paper —
the **Data Protection and Privacy Act, 2019** (Act No. 9/2019), the **2021 Regulations** (SI 21/2021),
and the **Personal Data Protection Office (PDPO)** operational since August 2021 — yet the report finds
that framework operationally under-embedded and **selectively enforced**. Biometric voter verification
was made *mandatory* nationwide for the first time (via SI No. 98 of 2025), the **National
Identification Number (NIN)** was wired into the voter register (turning a civil identifier into a
gateway for political participation), and published DPIAs, privacy notices, retention policies and
inter-agency data-sharing frameworks were near-absent. The report documents the enforcement asymmetry:
civil-society actors were criminally pursued under DPPA s.35 while the **Electoral Commission itself
registered with the regulator only *after* polling** (21 Jan 2026) and no political party registered at
all. It benchmarks Uganda unfavourably against Kenya (IEBC DPIA; ODPC electoral guidance) and
Convention 108+/GDPR standards, framing control of the electoral data architecture as the exercise of
political power ("architecture as power").

## Key facts

- **109,142 Biometric Voter Verification Kits** deployed across **50,739 polling stations**; no ballot
  issuable without biometric verification (EC statements, Dec 2025).
- **SI No. 98 of 2025** elevated biometric voter verification from discretionary to **mandatory**.
- **Electoral Commission registered with the PDPO only on 21 Jan 2026** (post-election), ref
  PDPO-202601-9760; **no political party** registered during the cycle.
- Biometric voter verification first used 2016, re-deployed 2021, institutionalised 2026.
- DPPA 2019 = Act No. 9/2019 (Cap 97); DPP Regulations 2021 = SI 21/2021 (gazetted 12 Mar 2021); PDPO
  operationalised Aug 2021 (after the 2021 polls).
- **FANON** civic-tech voter-locator app enabled name-based searches of voter data; EC sought a police
  investigation and it was blocked days before polling (technical breach *alleged*, not established).
- Prosecution of civil-society actors under DPPA **s.35**; human-rights lawyer **Dr Sarah Bireete**
  named (detention cited to Amnesty International).
- Kenya comparator: IEBC custodian of **>22 million voter records**, conducted a DPIA/data inventory
  (Feb 2025); ODPC issued Guidance Notes for Electoral Purposes.
- Vendor: **Smartmatic International** (biometric technology). Mobile penetration 79.1% (DataReportal,
  Nov 2025).
