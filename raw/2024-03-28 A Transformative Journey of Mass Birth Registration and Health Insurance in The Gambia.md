The World Bank-financed Gambia Essential Health Services Strengthening Project ran a **simultaneous mass registration** of both birth certificates and national health insurance memberships. Between 1 August 2022 and 28 February 2023, more than half the population — nearly **1.17 million people** — was registered and issued birth certificates and national health insurance cards, each with a **unique national identification number and QR code**, using an **electronic Civil Registration and Vital Statistics (eCRVS)** system that replaced the paper process.

Prior to 2020, paper-based birth-registration completeness was only about **59%**. The multi-sectoral approach integrated the Ministry of Interior's national ID system with the Ministry of Health's civil registration, built an eCRVS tailored to the Gambian context with the private sector, and partnered with the National Health Insurance Authority for a combined campaign. Implementation adapted to challenges: satellite internet in remote areas, a public error-reporting portal, a daily electronic dashboard, and an online attendance register for 1,500+ officers. A March 2023 Gambia Bureau of Statistics survey found ~54% reported being registered. The eCRVS has become the **foundational system for interoperability** across ministries, using the unique national identification number as the common key.
