Côte d'Ivoire has presented its digital transformation roadmap to the leadership of the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), outlining the country's ambition to position itself as a leading digital hub in West Africa.

Minister of Digital Transition and Technological Innovation, Djibril Ouattara, held discussions with the Deputy Secretary-General of ITU, Tomas Lamanauskas, where he shared Côte d'Ivoire's strategic vision for advancing its digital economy.

During the meeting, the Minister presented the country's roadmap built around seven key pillars and 40 priority projects aimed at accelerating digital transformation, strengthening infrastructure, promoting innovation and improving digital inclusion.

The roadmap forms part of Côte d'Ivoire's broader strategy to become a sub-regional digital hub by leveraging emerging technologies, expanding connectivity and creating an enabling environment for digital businesses and services.

The ITU leadership commended Côte d'Ivoire's vision and efforts to advance digital development, recognising the importance of national strategies that contribute to Africa's wider digital transformation agenda.
