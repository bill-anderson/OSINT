The **[[african-union|African Union Commission]]** (AUC) and the **[[itu|International Telecommunication
Union]]** signed a **Memorandum of Understanding** on Africa's "inclusive, secure and sustainable digital
transformation," at the **AI for Good Global Summit** in Geneva — signed by AU Commissioner for
Infrastructure and Energy **Lerato D. Mataboge** and ITU Secretary-General **Doreen Bogdan-Martin**.

The framework covers **AI, digital public infrastructure, digital/AI skills, universal connectivity,
cybersecurity, space technologies, international standards and digital regulation**, aligning the AU's
**Digital Transformation Strategy for Africa** and **Continental AI Strategy** with the ITU's global
mandate across its 194 members. It positions the AUC as the continental coordinating partner for digital
transformation across the AU's 55 member states, with a structured mechanism for "interoperable sovereign
AI systems," universal connectivity and African participation in global standards-setting.
