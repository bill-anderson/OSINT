# AU Commission and UNECA discuss digital partnership

**By:**   Nana Appiah Acquaye

The African Union Commission (AUC) and the United Nations Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA) have reaffirmed their commitment to advancing Africa’s digital transformation through coordinated efforts in artificial intelligence, data governance, digital identity, cybersecurity and digital trade.

The discussions took place during a bilateral engagement between the two institutions in Geneva, Switzerland, with UNECA represented by Dr. Mactar Seck, Chief of the Technology and Innovation Section, and Robert Tama Lisinge, Chief of the Energy, Infrastructure and Services Section of the Private Sector Development and Finance Division.

The engagement highlighted the complementary roles of both institutions in shaping Africa’s digital future, with the African Union Commission providing political leadership and continental ownership, while UNECA supports countries through technical expertise, policy development, skills advancement and innovation ecosystem building.

UNECA has played a key role in supporting several major digital initiatives across the continent, including providing analytical and coordination support for the Digital Transformation Strategy for Africa. The organisation also collaborated with the AU Commission on the AU Strategy for an Enabling Policy and Regulatory Environment for Africa’s Digital Single Market, which was adopted by the AU Summit in February 2024.

The UN agency has further contributed to continental digital frameworks, including the AU Data Policy Framework, the AU Interoperability Framework for Digital ID, and the Continental Artificial Intelligence Strategy. UNECA also serves as an implementing partner of the AU Technical Assistance Programme on Data Governance, helping member states develop and strengthen national data policies.

The Geneva discussions focused on strengthening implementation efforts to ensure that continental digital frameworks translate into practical national action without fragmentation. Key areas of cooperation include artificial intelligence development, responsible data governance, digital identity systems, cybersecurity resilience and support for member states in implementing the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) digital trade protocols.
