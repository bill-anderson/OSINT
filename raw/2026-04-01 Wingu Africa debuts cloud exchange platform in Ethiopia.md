(Source: Wingu Africa)

Data center operator Wingu Africa has rolled out its new Wingu Cloud Exchange (WCX) in Ethiopia, offering a private cloud platform tailored to the needs of local enterprises.

Wingu Africa said WCX will enable Ethiopian organizations to keep their data securely in-country, supporting compliance with local regulations and safeguarding sensitive information.

The data center operator added that for businesses, WCX enables faster access to services, improved operational efficiency and reliable support through locally optimized infrastructure.

"We are delivering secure, compliant, and scalable cloud solutions built specifically for local needs. With this platform, we are setting a new standard for digital infrastructure in the region and accelerating Ethiopia's transformation into a digitally enabled economy," explained Wingu Africa's deputy CEO, COO and co-founder, Demos Kyriacou.

## WCX's offerings for Ethiopia

The company said that the platform brings essential cloud services such as computing, storage, container management, and security, making advanced technology accessible to companies of all sizes.

Wingu Africa added that enterprises can scale operations on demand, pay predictable prices in local currency, and avoid the uncertainties of foreign exchange or hidden fees.

"This gives businesses clarity in planning and the freedom to grow without constraints," the company said.

The WCX platform offers a range of services, including Wingu Compute, Wingu Kubernetes, Wingu Drive and Wingu Security, enabling Ethiopian businesses to deploy, manage and secure their applications.

It also integrates with existing on-premises systems and complements global providers such as Azure and Amazon Web Services (AWS), offering customers hybrid options tailored to their operational requirements.

Wingu's WCX was first launched in Tanzania in November 2025. This was after the company [raised US$60 million to fast-track its regional expansion](https://www.connectingafrica.com/data-centers/wingu-africa-secures-60m-to-fuel-east-african-expansion) in its East African markets including [Djibouti](https://www.connectingafrica.com/data-centers/omantel-enters-djibouti-with-wingu-group-deal), Ethiopia and Tanzania.
