Uganda has launched ABQ Cloud, described as the country's indigenous sovereign cloud ecosystem, unveiled at the Deep Tech Hub and Raxio Data Centre in Kampala (Namanve). The launch was officiated by Monica Musenero Masanza, Minister of Science, Technology and Innovation, and brought together leaders from the Science, Technology and Innovation Office of the President (STI-OP), ABQ Cloud, Raxio Data Centre, Roke Cloud, AfriQloud, EDiC and Uganda's innovation community.

The platform is positioned as a step toward securing Uganda's digital independence by providing affordable, secure and locally hosted cloud infrastructure for startups, enterprises, government institutions and innovators. As a sovereign cloud built "by Ugandans for Uganda and Africa," ABQ Cloud delivers local currency pricing, reduced latency, enhanced data sovereignty and predictable costs.

The event featured a guided tour of the Raxio Data Centre and a panel discussion, "Driving Innovation using AI Sovereign Cloud," with James Byaruhanga (Managing Director, Roke Cloud), Eric Mugerwa (CTO, ABQ Cloud), David Gonahasa (STI-OP) and Caroline Kamaitha (General Manager, Raxio Data Centre).

Eric Mugerwa described ABQ Cloud as "a foundation for Uganda's digital independence." Caroline Kamaitha said Raxio provides "secure, reliable, and globally certified infrastructure that enables platforms like ABQ Cloud to thrive." James Byaruhanga said the Roke Cloud partnership is "democratizing access to digital infrastructure." Minister Musenero called ABQ Cloud "a strategic pillar for Uganda's future."

A hackathon hosted by STI (Industry 4.0+ Bureau) recognized winners, with the top 5 awarded 2.5 million shillings starting capital, one year of free cloud credits, incubation and mentorship at the Deep Tech Centre of Excellence, and one year of free internet. ABQ Cloud is hosted within Uganda, priced in local currency and delivered through trusted local partners.
