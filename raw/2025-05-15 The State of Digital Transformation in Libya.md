An industry overview (Alwadi Communications, a private Libyan ICT firm) on Libya's digital transformation. Libya, "once seen as catching up," is making strides — with growing internet penetration, government-led initiatives and a growing digital economy.

Drivers cited: the **[[gia-libya|General Information Authority]]** developing a national digital-transformation strategy (e-governance, financial inclusion, cybersecurity); the EU-funded **E-nable Project**; and expanding internet access (14.6m mobile connections and 6.4m social-media users as of 2025). Challenges: infrastructure (consistent high-speed access, especially rural), bureaucratic regulatory processes, a talent/skills gap, and cybersecurity/data protection. Opportunities framed around cloud/AI adoption, e-commerce and digital payments, and strategic partnerships; future trends include AI/automation, 5G/IoT for smart cities, and cybersecurity investment. (Partly promotional — Alwadi positions itself as a digital-transformation partner.)
