# Cabo Verde and Central African Republic launch digital government platforms (Gov.CV)

Two African states launched dedicated digital-government platforms.

**Cabo Verde — the Gov.CV unified portal.** Prime Minister **Ulisses Correia e Silva** presided over the launch of **[[gov-cv|Gov.CV]]**, a unified government portal (web + mobile app) acting as the **single gateway** to public services across civil registration and identity, financial and tax operations, higher education, transportation and other social services. Built for efficiency, interoperability and transparency, with more services to be onboarded. **Minister of State Modernization and Public Administration Eurico Monteiro** framed it as a driver of citizen participation, speaking at a forum on Cabo Verde's open-governance strategy. (In 2020 Cabo Verde contracted Zetes for biometric gates at its main airport.)

**Central African Republic.** The CAR concurrently launched its own digital-government platform (see [[CAF]] and the CAR govtech thread).
