The Guinean government launched an initiative on Wednesday 5 November 2025 to connect 2,200 public primary schools to the internet by the end of 2026. The project is part of the global GIGA initiative (led by the ITU and UNICEF). About 600 schools have already received connectivity equipment; the government aims to connect 1,000 schools by end-2025, benefiting more than one million children.

Jean Paul Cédy, Minister of Pre-University Education and Literacy, said connecting schools is a major step toward digital equity, will help address the teacher shortage, and will keep Guinean children connected to global trends. The programme is part of a broader digital transformation of the education system — e.g. the Simandou Academy under Simandou 2040 — building on a 2021 digital roadmap to integrate ICT. Former Education Minister Mamadou Alpha Bano Barry noted digitalization would integrate textbooks, lessons, homework and grades nationally, enable cross-discipline discussion groups and virtual meeting spaces, and enhance transparency (parents accessing children's grades and assignments).
