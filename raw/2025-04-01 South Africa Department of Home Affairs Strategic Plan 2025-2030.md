# Department of Home Affairs Strategic Plan 2025 to 2030 (South Africa)

Companion source page for the artefact `2025-04-01 South Africa Department of Home Affairs Strategic Plan 2025-2030.pdf`, held in `raw/`.

Tabled April 2025 (printed on the cover as "DATE OF TABLING: APRIL 2025"; no day given). Executive
Authority: Minister Dr L A Schreiber; Accounting Officer: Livhuwani Makhode.

**TERMINOLOGY — the department's own wording differs from the reporting.** This plan does **not** use
"Intelligent Population Register". Its terms are the **National Population Register (NPR)** / "secure
population register", with an **"Integrated Identity System operational"** outcome indicator. Where the
trade press says IPR and the DHA says NPR, prefer the department's wording and record the variant.

Targets and admissions worth holding:

- **Universal enrolment in the Smart ID system planned for 2029**; the green barcoded ID book to be
  discontinued; Smart ID extended to naturalised citizens.
- **Live capture extended to 1,000 bank branches** by end of term 2029 (from roughly three dozen at
  baseline). Enabling-document target: **minimum 17,402,000 by 2030** — 13,750,000 Smart ID cards plus
  3,652,500 births (baseline to 30 Sep 2024: 10,715,520 Smart IDs, 3,446,484 births).
- **Digital ID with remote authentication**, developed **with the South African Reserve Bank (SARB)**,
  facial recognition primary and fingerprints secondary; "Digital ID created", "Digital wallet launched",
  15 additional credentials in the wallet by 2029; the text says **digital identity is "to become a
  reality in 2028/9"**. *This corroborates Deputy Minister Nzuza's SARB claim against the wiki's held
  SARS-hosting account — see reviews/contradictions/open/south-africa-digital-id-sarb-or-sars.*
- **A missed milestone stated in the plan itself:** ABIS Phase 2 (iris, footprint, palm-print back-end)
  completed **Feb 2024** after delay, against an original target of ABIS implemented by 2022/23;
  front-end live-capture integration expected 2025/26.
- Civil registration: online birth registration at high-volume health facilities — baseline **8 of 91**
  priority facilities equipped, with connectivity and IT capacity named as the blocker; target **>80% of
  births registered within 30 days**; **340 million paper civic records** to be digitised.
- A Security Operations Centre planned for the 2025 MTEF; a target of **top 50 in the World Bank GovTech
  Maturity Index** by 2029/30.

This is the department's own tabled plan — its targets are commitments, not achievements, and the 2028/9
digital-ID date is a fourth entry in the ladder of competing launch dates the wiki now carries.
