The **Burkina Faso Internet Exchange (BFIX)**, launched in **June 2015**, is the resilience backbone for landlocked Burkina Faso — which once routed all traffic to Europe and back (slower, costlier, riskier). With Internet Society support (2019 network-engineering training, plus a CDN cache-fill grant and equipment via the Sustainable Peering Infrastructure program), BFIX raised peak capacity **from 0.5 to 6 Gbit/s**, improving service quality and cutting operator costs — so local traffic keeps flowing even during subsea-cable cuts (as in 2024). BFIX executive director: Jean-Baptiste Millogo.
