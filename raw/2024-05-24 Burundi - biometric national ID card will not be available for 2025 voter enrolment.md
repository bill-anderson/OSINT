Burundi's electoral commission **CENI** announced (via president Prosper Ntahorwamiye, at a 17 May 2024 stakeholder forum in Bujumbura) that the **biometric national ID card would not be available** in time for the **2025** voter enrolment, so registration would rely on the **current ID card** — which stakeholders noted is easily falsifiable. OLUCOME's Gabriel Rufyiri and others raised fraud concerns, including foreign nationals allegedly obtaining Burundian ID cards, and mismatches created by new administrative boundaries.

CENI put the 2025 elections' cost at **114.4 billion FBu**. Absent a recent general census, it projected the 2008 population (~8 million) forward at 3%/year growth to about **13.2 million** for 2025. The voter census would run in 2024 and remain valid for the 2025 (legislative + communal councillors) and 2027 elections; the presidential vote is set for 2027.
