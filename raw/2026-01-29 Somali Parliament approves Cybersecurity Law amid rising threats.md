# Somali Parliament approves Cybersecurity Law amid rising threats

> ## Excerpt
> Somalia's House of the People approved the Cybersecurity Law on 26 Jan 2026,
> creating SOM-CIRT, a nine-member cybersecurity committee and an emergency
> intervention centre. Follows a Nov-2025 e-visa breach; Somalia ranked ITU GCI
> Tier 4 of 5 (2024, 37.38/100).

---

Somalia's parliament (the House of the People of the Federal Parliament) officially approved the Cybersecurity Law on Monday, January 26, 2026, strengthening the country's regulatory framework as authorities intensify efforts to secure national cyberspace.

The National Communications Authority (NCA) said the law establishes a national cybersecurity management framework, defining the responsibilities of the Ministry of Communications, assigning a technical role to the telecom regulator, setting obligations for operators of critical infrastructure, and detailing mechanisms for prevention, reporting and response. The law provides for the creation of the Somalia Computer Incident Response Team (SOM-CIRT), a nine-member cybersecurity committee, and an emergency intervention center.

The approval follows earlier steps: in August 2025 the government approved a draft cybercrime bill; in March 2023 the Data Protection Act entered into force, creating the Data Protection Authority (ADP). Somalia signed a cybersecurity MoU with Malaysia in August 2025 and, in November 2024, strengthened cooperation with the UN Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC).

Cyber threats are intensifying: the State of Cybersecurity in Somalia 2024 report cited ransomware attacks against public institutions and phishing targeting the financial sector. In November 2025, hackers breached the e-visa platform and compromised the personal data of several thousand people. Somalia ranked Tier 4 of 5 in the ITU's Global Cybersecurity Index 2024, scoring 37.38 out of 100.
