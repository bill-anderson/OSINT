The Director-General of the National Communications Authority (NatCA), Amara Brewah, called for predictable spectrum policies, balanced licensing frameworks, and stronger regional cooperation, speaking at two ministerial roundtables at the Mobile World Congress Barcelona 2026 Ministerial Programme (March 2-3).

Brewah said telecom operators require longer license durations (15-20 years) to justify large-scale 4G and 5G investments, warning that shorter cycles of 5-10 years weaken investor confidence. He cautioned that excessive upfront spectrum fees limit operators' ability to invest, recommending staggered payments and reasonable annual usage fees.

Drawing on Sierra Leone developments, he cited reforms including guidelines for non-geostationary satellite (NGSO) services, technology-neutral regulatory frameworks, and deployment of a modern Automated Spectrum Management System developed by LS Telcom. He noted Sierra Leone is reviewing its radio spectrum and licensing regulations through a stakeholder consultation.

At a second roundtable on inclusive spectrum policies, Brewah emphasized prioritizing low-band spectrum for rural coverage, and highlighted the Universal Access Development Fund's solar-powered community sites. He noted Starlink has been licensed and operating in Sierra Leone for two years, enabling remote clinics, schools and farmers to connect.
