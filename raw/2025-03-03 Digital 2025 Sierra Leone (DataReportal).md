# Digital 2025 - Sierra Leone

> ## Excerpt
> DataReportal's annual snapshot: 1.80m internet users in Sierra Leone at the start of 2025 (20.7% penetration), 8.66m mobile connections (99.2% of population), and 1.15m social-media identities (13.2%) — cite-don't-absorb reference figures, dated.

---

This page contains the data, insights, and trends for how people in Sierra Leone use digital devices, online platforms, and connected services in 2025.

## The "state of digital" in Sierra Leone in 2025

- A total of 8.66 million cellular mobile connections were active in Sierra Leone in early 2025, equivalent to 99.2 percent of the total population. (Some connections may only include voice/SMS and not internet access.)
- There were 1.80 million individuals using the internet in Sierra Leone at the start of 2025, when online penetration stood at 20.7 percent.
- Sierra Leone was home to 1.15 million social media user identities in January 2025, equating to 13.2 percent of the total population.

## Population of Sierra Leone in 2025

The population of Sierra Leone stood at 8.73 million in January 2025, up 178 thousand (+2.1 percent) from early 2024. In early 2025, 45.0 percent lived in urban centres and 55.0 percent in rural areas; 50.1 percent was female and 49.9 percent male. The median age was 19.7.

## Mobile connections in 2025

Data from GSMA Intelligence shows 8.66 million cellular mobile connections at the beginning of 2025, equivalent to 99.2 percent of the total population — an increase of 595 thousand (+7.4 percent) between the start of 2024 and the beginning of 2025. GSMA Intelligence data suggests that 79.0% of mobile connections can now be considered "broadband" (3G, 4G, or 5G), though this should not be taken as a proxy for mobile internet use.

## Internet use in 2025

The latest available data indicated 1.80 million internet users in January 2025, an internet penetration rate of 20.7 percent. The number of internet users increased by 42 thousand (+2.4 percent) between January 2024 and January 2025. These figures suggest that 6.93 million people (79.3 percent of the population) remained "offline" at the start of 2025. Published figures may under-represent current realities due to lags in data collection.
