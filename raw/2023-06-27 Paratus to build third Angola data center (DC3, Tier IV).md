Paratus Group is building its third data centre in Angola — the country's **first Tier IV facility** in Luanda: a 30,000 m² plot with >10 MW of IT capacity across 2,000 racks, its "biggest data center project yet." Paratus Angola (previously ITA) already runs a two-DC Luanda campus — DC1 (2017, ~1,500 servers) and DC2 (2019, ~7,000 servers) — plus a teleport and a OneWeb ground station.

In November 2023 Paratus secured a **$31 million** debt facility from the Emerging Africa Infrastructure Fund (EAIF, a Private Infrastructure Development Group company) and fund manager Ninety One, financing the 10 MW data centre and three new terrestrial fibre routes: Walvis Bay–Johannesburg–Maputo, Brazzaville–Johannesburg–Maputo, and Luanda–Lusaka–Dar es Salaam. CEO Schalk Erasmus: "Widening access to fiber and data centers in key African markets will progress development and inclusive growth." EAIF had earlier provided a $170m facility to Raxio.
