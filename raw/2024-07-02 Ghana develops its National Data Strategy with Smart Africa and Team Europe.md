Accra, 2 July 2024 — The Government of Ghana, the European Union and Smart Africa completed a National Validation Workshop for the development of a National Data Strategy, aligned with Ghana's Long-term National Development Plan (2018-2057) and the National Medium-term Development Policy Framework (2022-2025).

The two-day workshop united data-governance stakeholders to review and validate the draft strategy and provide inputs into its implementation roadmap, covering data governance structure, legal/ethical considerations (collection, use, storage, protection), data infrastructure and capacity building. Smart Africa CEO Lacina Koné framed data as a valuable cross-sector asset; Deputy Minister of Communications and Digitalisation Charles Acheampong stressed prioritising data governance for the digitisation effort.

Patricia Adusei-Poku, Executive Director of Ghana's Data Protection Commission (DPC), positioned the DPC as a regional trailblazer for authorised, people-centric data processing. Anna Lixi (EU in Ghana) and David Robert (GIZ Ghana Digital Transformation Center) underscored data's development value. The initiative was enabled by Smart Africa, the Government of Ghana, the African Union, the EU, BMZ, GIZ, the D4D Hub and Global Gateway. Benin is on a similar journey and conducted a peer-learning exchange, in line with the AU Continental Data Policy Framework.
