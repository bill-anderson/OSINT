Yonna Group, through its subsidiary Yonna Wallet, has signed a partnership with the Government of The Gambia (represented by the Accountant General's Department) to conduct payments for all government services — becoming the **first Gambian fintech** to get such authority. All government payments are now digitalised live on the Yonna Wallet app.

Under the agreement, all government fees — from rates and business registration to government services — can be paid securely using Yonna Wallet. GM Momodou Barrow said it accelerates digital transformation and reduces fraud, revenue leakages and middlemen while enabling easy reconciliation by the Accountant General's Department. Gambians can pay fees such as ID cards, passports and business registrations from their phones; the **Yonna Global Wallet** lets the diaspora pay for government services from abroad. The system automatically generates traceable receipts. Yonna has over 200 branches and outlets across the country.
