Terms of Reference for Consultancy Services to design and install a Government Service Bus (GSB) prototype (ref. QII-SG-217).

## Background

In Sierra Leone, there is very little information on how public spending at the central level and budget allocations to the local level translate into service delivery. The Ministry of Finance (MoF) has launched several critical public financial management (PFM) information systems over the last ten years. These core PFM systems include the Integrated Financial Management Information System (IFMIS) of the MoF, the Terminus 24 (T24) payment system of the Bank of Sierra Leone, the ASYCUDA World system of the Customs Department, and the Integrated Tax Administration System (ITAS) of the National Revenue Authority. Ongoing World Bank projects are supporting PFM Smart (local government FMIS), a Municipal Property Tax System (MPTS) for 22 Local Councils, an e-Procurement System, and an Audit Management Information System (AMIS).

Despite significant progress in automating core PFM systems, these platforms have been developed independently and operate as standalone systems. There is no government service bus (GSB) with universally agreed protocols within the PFM ecosystem to automate secure data exchange.

The MoF established a PFM interoperability working group (IWG) in 2024 to develop an interoperability framework. The Sierra Leone Second Financial Inclusion Project (SLSFIP, P177947) funds consultancy services for the development of an "Interoperability Roadmap for Public Financial Management Systems." The selection of consultants was completed in March 2025 and a contract was signed between the Bank of Sierra Leone and PCL International Ltd. in April 2025, expected to be completed by the end of September 2025.

The World Bank is also supporting the development of several PFM information systems (PFM Smart, AMIS, LoMEMIS, e-GP) through the Accountable Governance for Basic Service Delivery (AG4BSD, P172492) project; interfaces/APIs linking these to the GSB are expected to be fully operational until mid-2026.

The Government of Japan provided a Quality Infrastructure Investment (QII) grant on April 26, 2025, to support implementation of the PFM interoperability framework, including deployment of a GSB prototype based on an open-source platform (e.g., X-Road) to automate secure data exchange via APIs. The GSB will be a shared platform hosted in the MoF Data Center, interconnecting relevant government entities to securely share data (an information exchange highway with audit trails and monitoring tools).

## Objectives and Scope

The objective is to design and install a Government Service Bus prototype in line with the interoperability framework being developed by the IWG. Tasks include: reviewing the PFM interoperability framework; identifying interoperability requirements of existing government systems (IFMIS, ITAS, MPTS, Customs/ASYCUDA, PFM Smart, e-GP, AMIS, LoMEMIS, CRVS) with attention to cybersecurity risks; presenting the capabilities of X-Road and other open-source platforms; installing the prototype in the MoF data center; training a dedicated GSB team to take over ownership; supporting API development; and delivering source code and documentation to the MoF GSB team. The assignment was expected to start around June/July 2025 and run for 20 weeks.
