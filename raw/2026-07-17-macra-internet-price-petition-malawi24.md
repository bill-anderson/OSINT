Hundreds sign MACRA internet petition - Malawi24 Skip to content

Hundreds of Malawians have signed an online petition calling on the Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) to review internet prices and investigate mobile data billing practices. The petition, started by Stanley Mombera, has attracted 885 verified signatures within about a day of its launch at the time of publication. The organisers are targeting at least 1,000 signatures. The campaign gained momentum after it was shared on social media platform X with the message: “Petition to the Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) – Sign the Petition!” Addressed to MACRA’s Director General and Board, the petition urges the communications regulator to conduct an independent and transparent review of internet pricing across all licensed telecommunications operators. The petition also calls on MACRA to investigate internet data billing systems and bundle consumption to ensure subscribers are billed fairly and accurately. In addition, the organisers want the regulator to publish the findings of its investigations, explain how internet billing and data usage are measured, strengthen consumer protection regulations and promote policies that make internet services more affordable and accessible. According to the petition, affordable and reliable internet is essential for education, entrepreneurship, innovation, remote work, healthcare, financial inclusion and access to information. The petition argues that rising internet costs, concerns over how quickly data bundles are depleted, including on some unlimited packages, and what it describes as limited transparency in data consumption are making internet access increasingly difficult for many Malawians. It says the challenges are slowing the country’s digital transformation and creating barriers for students, software developers, researchers, businesses and other citizens who rely on affordable internet to participate in the digital economy. The organisers say MACRA, as the communications sector regulator, has a responsibility to protect consumers, promote transparency and ensure telecommunications services support Malawi’s digital and economic development. Efforts to obtain a comment from MACRA were unsuccessful by the time of publication. The organisers continue to collect signatures and say they intend to present the petition to the regulator as they push for action on internet pricing and data billing concerns.
