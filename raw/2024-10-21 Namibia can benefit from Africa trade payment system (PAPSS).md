The African Export-Import Bank (Afreximbank) was seeking to integrate Namibia into its Pan-African Payment and Settlement System (PAPSS), with a push planned at its Trade Finance Seminar and Factoring Workshop in Windhoek, 5–8 November 2024. PAPSS — an initiative of Afreximbank, the AU Commission and the AfCFTA Secretariat — is a centralised financial market infrastructure enabling cross-border payments settled in local currencies without converting through the US dollar, working with central banks to serve commercial banks and licensed payment service providers.

Afreximbank's MD for trade finance, Gwen Mwaba, said the PAPSS chief executive would ensure the Bank of Namibia and commercial banks sign up by the seminar's end. At the time (Oct 2024), around 13 African central banks had signed up and 115 commercial banks were on the platform. Namibia's integration was planned/being pursued, not yet live.
