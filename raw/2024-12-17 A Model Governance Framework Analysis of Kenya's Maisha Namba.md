Temple iLIT Access to Justice Clinic report (published 17 December 2024; report dated August 2024) — the first independent, public report applying the **UNDP Model Governance Framework for Digital Legal Identity Systems (MGF, September 2023)** to a national system.

It argues that Kenya's **Maisha Namba** identity-management and payments ecosystem, as envisioned since 2023, is poised to exacerbate existing inequalities and further marginalize people unable to obtain identity documents. Adopting an equality and non-discrimination lens across all metrics of digital-ID governance, it draws on prior work including Haki na Sheria (litigation on double registrations and statelessness), UC Berkeley privacy recommendations, prior Huduma Namba litigation (Nubian Rights Forum, KHRC) and Amnesty International. It focuses on marginalized groups — rural areas, nomadic/semi-nomadic communities, those without connectivity/power, in extreme poverty, or subject to heightened security vetting on account of tribe, religion or ethnicity. In July 2023, UNDP and the Kenyan government signed an MoU to partner on Kenya's digital ID system.
