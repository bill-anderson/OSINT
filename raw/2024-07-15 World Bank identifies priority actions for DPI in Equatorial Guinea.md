Biometric Update coverage (15 July 2024) of the World Bank Digital Economy Diagnostic. A World Bank team identified immediate actions Equatorial Guinea should take to ramp up digital transformation — including setting up a digital identity system and other DPI in line with the Digital Economy for Africa (DE4A) initiative.

The diagnostic assesses five foundational pillars (infrastructure, public platforms, DFS, digital business, digital skills) and positions digital as a diversification lever under the 2035 National Development Plan. Recommendations: address regulatory and governance barriers to digital infrastructure; streamline the digital civil registry and implement a digital identity ecosystem; develop digital payment infrastructure via a whole-of-government approach; strengthen digital trade; and adopt a national ICT education policy. The report notes a digital identity system "could help public entities cut dependence on manual documents… and avoid inefficiencies and fraud opportunities," and recommends a currently-absent national interoperability framework. The prior year, Equatorial Guinea launched a biometric enrollment drive for public servants to verify payroll eligibility.
