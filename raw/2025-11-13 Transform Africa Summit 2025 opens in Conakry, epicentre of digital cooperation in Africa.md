The 7th Transform Africa Summit (TAS) opened on Wednesday 12 November 2025 in Conakry, organized by the Smart Africa Alliance (42 member states) with the Government of Guinea, under the theme "AI for Africa: Innovate Locally, Impact Globally." For the first time the summit is hosted in Francophone West Africa, positioning Conakry as the digital capital of the continent.

Minister of Posts, Telecommunications and the Digital Economy Rose Pola Pricemou said Guinea is strengthening its digital sovereignty: national data is now hosted locally thanks to the Tier III Data Center, and the reinstatement of the .GN domain reaffirms Guinea's digital identity. Public-administration modernization is accelerating with FUGAS (civil-servant management) and the official launch on 12 November of TELEMO (national e-procurement, developed with Rwanda). Guinea has quadrupled fibre-optic capacity (12,000+ km deployed), is strengthening its position as a sub-regional digital hub (supported by a second submarine cable), and through the GIGA project has connected more than 500 schools; a national technopark is being designed. In October 2025, with UNDP support, a national workshop assessed the AI ecosystem, laying the foundation for a national AI strategy.

The opening was attended by President Mamadi Doumbouya (Guinea) and President Paul Kagame (Rwanda, Chairperson of the Smart Africa Board). Smart Africa DG Lacina Koné framed AI as an opportunity to rethink Africa's development. Founded in 2013, Smart Africa has grown from 37 to 42 member states and is unveiling a new three-year strategy, with flagship initiatives SANIA, SADX and Digital ID.
