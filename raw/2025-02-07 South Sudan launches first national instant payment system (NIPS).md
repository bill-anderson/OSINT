# South Sudan launches first national instant payment system (NIPS)

> ## Excerpt
> The Bank of South Sudan launched its first National Instant Payment System (NIPS) with AfricaNenda in February 2025 — real-time P2P/P2B/G2P/P2G rails integrating ACH/RTGS/IFT — three years after it was first proposed and following a 2023 proof-of-concept.

Three years after it was first proposed, the Bank of South Sudan (BoSS) has launched its first National Instant Payment System (NIPS) in collaboration with the AfricaNenda Foundation, an organisation funded by the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation to help achieve "universal African financial access by 2030." Central bank governor Johnny Ohisa Damian announced the launch at an event on 5 February 2025: "The start of the NIPS journey marks a monumental step forward for financial and socio-economic inclusion in South Sudan."

NIPS is designed to enable real-time, secure and cost-effective transactions, promoting interoperability between banks, mobile-money providers and other financial institutions. As "the backbone of South Sudan's digital payment ecosystem," it is intended to facilitate person-to-person (P2P), person-to-business (P2B), government-to-person (G2P) and person-to-government (P2G) payments. It integrates with existing systems such as the Automated Clearing House (ACH), Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) and Instant Fund Transfers (IFT). The system aims to improve financial inclusion for over six million adults with limited access to formal banking; currently MTN and Zain dominate the country's payment landscape due to the absence of a domestic or regional instant network. A proof-of-concept phase during 2023 demonstrated high demand and feasibility.

Focus areas of the BoSS–AfricaNenda partnership include developing a detailed NIPS implementation roadmap with clear milestones, building the capacity of BoSS and financial institutions to oversee and manage the system, and ensuring integration and interoperability with banks and telecoms. AfricaNenda Foundation chief executive Robert Ochola called it a "landmark initiative": "Instant payment systems are transformative tools that redefine access to financial services and promote inclusivity."

The initiative aligns with the East African Community's (EAC) push for instant retail payments under a 2024 master plan; of EAC members, only Burundi and the DRC lack an instant payment platform, while Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda and Rwanda have linked their RTGS systems via the East African Payments System. Instant payment systems were live in 26 African countries as of the SIIPS Report 2024, with 27 more preparing to launch.

**Context.** South Sudan was ranked 192nd of 193 in the UNDP Human Development Report 2023/2024. A World Bank Poverty and Equity Assessment (published December 2024) found poverty "endemic" and vulnerability "almost universal," with extreme poverty (under $2.15/day) affecting more than two-thirds of the population of 12.4 million.
