# Cross-border digital payments and identity in Nigeria under the AfCFTA

> ## Excerpt
> Nigeria's constraint on operationalising the AfCFTA Digital Trade Protocol is **not infrastructure but
> misalignment** across identity, payment and governance systems — cross-border B2B payments fail at the
> ID-verification (KYB) layer, not the payment rail.

> **Provenance note.** ODI research report (108-respondent purposive survey — indicative, not
> representative). Some GDP-projection figures are self-referential to the author team's prior ODI work.

**Artefact:** ![[2026-03-01 Cross-border digital payments and identity in Nigeria under the AfCFTA.pdf]]

## Key facts

- Nigeria is a payments powerhouse (~22bn electronic transactions, ₦1,558tn, H1 2024; leads Africa with
  **22 banks on PAPSS**) and individual ID has scaled (121m+ NINs, 66m BVNs). But **corporate** digital
  ID is a fragmented "noodle bowl" — only **561 LEIs (541 active) vs 3m+ firms** (GLEIF, Sep 2025), 0%
  AEI uptake among surveyed businesses.
- Survey pain points: 84% API-integration disruptions, 80% cross-border payment delays, 100% compliance
  delays, **71% unaware of the AfCFTA DTP**. CBN cut PAPSS documentary burden (28 Apr 2025) for low-value
  transfers. Recommends reusable corporate credentials, mutual recognition of ID, regulatory passporting.
