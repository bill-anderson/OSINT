Per a GSMA report (*Accelerating Smartphone Adoption in Africa*), Kenyan consumers perceive **locally-assembled phones as lower quality** despite lower prices — two years after Kenya's first smartphone assembly plant. GSMA recommends brand-building and consumer-trust strategies for local manufacturers.

In October 2023, Kenya's first smartphone assembly plant opened in Machakos (East Africa Device Assembly Kenya Ltd); President Ruto said it would produce up to 3m units/year and make Kenya's smartphones the cheapest in Africa. As of April 2024, 268,000 locally-assembled phones had been bought; M-Kopa reported producing 1.5m+ phones at its Nairobi factory (Nov 2024). GSMA findings: smartphone adoption stands at **62%**, 63% of the population on 4G, only 3% on 5G; nearly half of respondents are open to reputable pre-owned devices. At MWC Kigali (October), GSMA with Airtel, Axian, Ethio Telecom, MTN, Orange and Vodacom proposed baseline requirements for an affordable entry-level 4G smartphone (Vivek Badrinath, GSMA DG).
