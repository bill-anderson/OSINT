Dar es Salaam. Government-backed digital financial systems, applications and portals are impacting key economic sectors at a steady pace, with President Samia Suluhu Hassan having inaugurated a digital infrastructure expansion of 758 communication towers and new fibre optic connections across 85 districts. Tanzania's digital economy drive is bearing fruit across financial services, aviation, transportation, marine, trade and agriculture.

The government has allocated 200bn/- for funding startups, innovations and MSMEs. TCRA notes the mobile money ecosystem processed 43.4trn/- during the fourth quarter of 2025. The latest East Africa Banking Industry Outlook 2026 Report by Deloitte states Tanzania's financial institutions are leading the region in fast, credible digital financial solutions, with commercial banks increasingly leveraging transactional data from mobile money to refine credit evaluations. The ICT sector is projected to grow 13.5 percent in 2026.

In March 2026, an agreement to validate the ten-year Tanzania Electronic Data Governance Strategy (TZeDGS) was signed between the government and the UN Economic Commission for Africa (ECA). The Tanzania Startup Association's Startup Ecosystem Status Report 2024 reports active startups rose 24 percent to 1,041 ventures, creating over 138,453 jobs, with FDI doubling to $53 million (FinTech attracting $41.4 million). Government-backed systems cited include WEZESHA, TANCIS, TIPS, TMX and NeST, supporting the DIRA 2050 goal of a $1 trillion economy.
