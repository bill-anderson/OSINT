# Chad pushes telcos for network upgrades

By Matshepo Sehloho, Connecting Africa — June 26, 2026

Chad's government has warned Moov Africa Chad and Airtel Chad that they need to improve network performance, amidst frustrations over the deteriorating quality of mobile services across the country. Haliki Choua Mahamat, Chad's Minister of Telecommunications, Digital Economy and Digitalization of Public Administration, met with the telcos to discuss network coverage and service quality.

"If the operator Moov Africa claims to have made investments, nothing is felt, and the quality or lack of the network continues to provoke outrage," Mahamat said. He said the Ministry rejects fuel supply issues as a justification for poor network quality, and instructed Moov Africa to find solutions to their fuel supply or migrate to renewable energy — solar or wind — to power transmission sites.

Amid rising pressure, Airtel introduced a new investment plan to boost service quality, though Chadian officials remained skeptical, citing repeated promises yet to be implemented. Mahamat told Airtel officials that his government considers electronic communications networks as critical sovereign infrastructure, indispensable to the functioning of the state, national security, economic activity and development. Airtel Chad CEO Brahim Nallar said the operator would strengthen its technical infrastructure.

The minister criticized Airtel's reliance on subcontractors to manage equipment, technical staff and site maintenance, and encouraged the operator to connect to the government's national fiber backbone. This is not the first crackdown: in August 2025, Chad gave Airtel and Moov Africa one week to connect to the national fiber-optic network; in August 2023, ARCEP fined Airtel 5 billion CFA francs (US$8.3 million) for deteriorating network quality.
