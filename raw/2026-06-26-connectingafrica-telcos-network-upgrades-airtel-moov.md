Chad's government has told Moov Africa Chad and Airtel Chad to improve network performance.

Matshepo Sehloho,Associate Editor,Connecting Africa

2 Min ReadChad wants local telcos to improve the quality of the country's telecom networks. (Source: Allexxandar on Magnific)

Chad's government has warned Moov Africa Chad and Airtel Chad that they need to improve network performance, amidst frustrations over the deteriorating quality of mobile services across the country.

Haliki Choua Mahamat, Chad's Minister of Telecommunications, Digital Economy and Digitalization of Public Administration, met with the telcos to discuss network coverage and service quality.

Mahamat openly expressed his frustration with the poor quality of services affecting his citizens.

"If the operator Moov Africa claims to have made investments, nothing is felt, and the quality or lack of the network continues to provoke outrage," Mahamat said.

He said the Ministry rejects fuel supply issues as a justification for poor network quality.

Mahamat stood firm and instructed Moov Africa to find solutions to their fuel supply or migrate to renewable energy, solar or wind turbine as they see fit to power transmission sites.

Chadian authorities met with Moov Africa and Airtel to discuss poor network quality. (Source: Chad's Ministry of Telecommunications, Digital Economy, and Digitalization of Public Administration)

Amid rising pressure from Chadian authorities over network performance, Airtel has introduced a new investment plan to boost service quality.

Chadian officials have, however, remained skeptical, citing repeated promises that have yet to be fully implemented.

Mahamat told Airtel officials that his government considers electronic communications networks as critical sovereign infrastructure, indispensable to the functioning of the state, national security, economic activity and development.

Airtel Chad CEO Brahim Nallar said the operator would strengthen its technical infrastructure to improve network quality and introduce new services.

Mahamat urged the operator to revise its operating model, arguing that its current structure prevents rapid improvements in service quality.

The minister criticized Airtel's reliance on subcontractors to manage equipment, technical staff and site maintenance.

He encouraged Airtel to connect to the government's national fiber backbone.

This is not the first time that the country has cracked down on bad telecoms service.

In August 2025, the country gave Airtel and Moov Africa one week to connect to the country's national fiber-optic network.

In August 2023, Chad's Regulatory Authority for Electronic Communications and Post (ARCEP) also slapped Airtel with a fine of 5 billion Central Africa CFA francs (US$8.3 million at the time) for deteriorating network quality.
