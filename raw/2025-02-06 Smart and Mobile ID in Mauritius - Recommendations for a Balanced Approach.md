Analysis/opinion piece (see note below) recalling the July 2024 Intelwatch policy brief "Smart ID Cards in Mauritius: Balancing Rights and Freedoms with National Identification Needs," prompted by the new Minister's announcement that Government will proceed with the Mobile ID project. Part of a comparative research project on digital surveillance in Southern Africa.

Regulatory context: Government introduced three legal frameworks — the National Identity Card Regulations 2024, the National Identity Card (Card Usage) Regulations 2024, and the National Identity Card (Mobile ID) Regulations 2024. Two rollout phases: Phase 1 (launched Feb 2024), a new physical smart ID card with anti-fraud security features; Phase 2 (in development), the Mobile ID app. A January 2025 Government Information Service note reported the new Minister's reassurance that the mobile ID "will not involve selfie photographs and middleware but will be based on the physical identity card without any biometric data" — a shift from earlier fingerprint-integration descriptions.

Privacy concerns raised: Data Protection Act 2017 (GDPR-based) compliance; proportionality and data-subject rights; surveillance safeguards; independent security audits of the MNIC 3.0 system (recalling Mauritius's UN Human Rights Committee commitment); and sparing use of Section 44, which lets the Prime Minister exempt data from privacy rules on national-security grounds. The Data Protection Commissioner cited the Government Programme 2025-2029's emphasis on the constitutional right to privacy.
