The NCC and CBN approved a new End User Billing (EUB) model for USSD services, deducting USSD charges directly from airtime balances and ending years of financial disputes between telecom operators and banks. Previously, USSD banking charges were deducted from bank accounts (often without notice and with delayed telco payments, leading to billions of naira in unsettled debts), while telecom-service USSD was deducted from airtime. Under EUB, all USSD session costs (including banking) are deducted from airtime with real-time confirmation and opt-in/opt-out control. A USSD session is capped at ₦6.98 for 120 seconds — cheaper than the old ₦1.63 per 20 seconds (nearly ₦10 for the same duration).
