Marc-André Loko, Director-General of Benin's **Agency for Information Systems and Digital ([[asin-benin]]**, formed by merging the sector's four implementing agencies incl. the ADN), describes Benin's digital strategy as a deliberate **"diversified portfolio of best-in-class partners"** — a sovereignty play against single-vendor dependence. The 2021–2026 plan (successor to the 2016 Deloitte-built plan) shifts toward home-grown adoption of digital services, with digital payments/financial inclusion the fastest-moving strand.

Partnerships split into expertise (Estonia and Rwanda for e-government, via **Cybernetica** and **eGA**) and business/financing — where **Chinese** firms (**Huawei**, CITCC via China Development Bank and China EximBank) are privileged for financing and favourable debt deferrals — plus Microsoft/Oracle-certified firms, French feasibility houses (Sofrecom, Tactis, Horus) and Tunisian firms (Digitalis, MGI BFC). Benin's **digital code** (drafted with Jones Day) covers electronic communications, cybersecurity and personal-data protection. Notably, Benin **leads Smart Africa's digital-identity project** — a framework for cross-border ID interoperability letting a citizen of one country subscribe to mobile services in another (Senegal, Togo, soon Ghana). In 2021 Benin handed delegated management of its digital-infrastructure company to Senegal's **Sonatel** (Orange Group).
