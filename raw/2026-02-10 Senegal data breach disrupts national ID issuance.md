# Senegal data breach disrupts national ID issuance

National ID-card issuance in Senegal was **temporarily halted in early February 2026** after the government
reported a breach affecting the population database. The **[[daf-senegal|Directorate of File Automation
(DAF)]]** (Ministry of Interior) said it was working to restore operations and that citizens' personal data
"remain fully intact." The attacker, **"Green Blood Group,"** claimed (per The Gambia Journal) to have seized
~**139 terabytes** including biometrics, identity and immigration files. **SeneWeb** reported
**[[senegal-numerique|Senegal Numérique SA]]** was compromised around the same time (possibly coordinated).
**[[iris-corporation]]** (Malaysia, Senegal's ID-card producer ~a decade; 2016 contract ~CFA50bn/~$90m,
renewed 2021 at $19.5m/3m cards) dispatched investigators; the government is examining supplier liability. The
attack followed a strike on the tax-management system (Oct 2025). Cybersecurity is a **[[new-deal-technologique]]**
pillar (which received a $10m Gates grant in 2025).
