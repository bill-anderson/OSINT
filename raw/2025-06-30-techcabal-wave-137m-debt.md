# Wave raises $137 million in debt to expand mobile money services across Africa

TechCabal - 30 June 2025, by Muktar Oladunmade

Wave, one of Africa's most valuable startups, has raised a $137 million debt round to bolster working capital and drive expansion across existing and new markets, scaling its mobile money operations. Rand Merchant Bank (RMB) led the round alongside British International Investment (BII), Finnfund and Norfund.

Founded in 2018 and operating in eight West African markets, Wave serves over 20 million monthly active users through more than 150,000 agents and 3,000 employees. In June it received authorisation to operate in Cameroon. Its low-fee, mobile-first model - free deposits/withdrawals and a flat 1% transfer fee - disrupted a market previously dominated by telecom operators (Orange, Free, Expresso) charging 5-10% per transaction. Wave became Francophone Africa's first unicorn in September 2021 at a $1.7 billion valuation and has raised over $300 million in total.
