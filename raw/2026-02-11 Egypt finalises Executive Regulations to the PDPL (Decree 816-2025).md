# Egypt finalises Executive Regulations to the Personal Data Protection Law (Decree 816/2025)

Egypt published **Executive Regulations No. 816/2025** (Minister of Telecommunications Decree, issued 1 Nov 2025, circulated Dec 2025), operationalising the **Personal Data Protection Law (Law No. 151/2020)** more than five years after its passage — see [[egypt-pdpl]]. Enforcement is expected from **1 November 2026** (end of a one-year grace period).

Key points:
- The **[[pdpc-egypt|Personal Data Protection Center (PDPC)]]** is confirmed as the supervisory authority (implementation, monitoring, enforcement).
- Processing must stay aligned with declared purposes; a structured **PDPC approvals** model applies.
- **Cross-border transfers** require a separate **PDPC licence/permit** (destination, purpose, data categories, safeguards); extra approvals for electronic marketing and certain public-space surveillance.
- Foreign entities processing data on individuals in Egypt may need a **local representative**; some organisations must appoint a **PDPC-registered DPO**.
- **Children's data** safeguards (guardian consent for under-15s); **breach notification** to the PDPC within **72 hours** and to affected individuals within **3 business days**.
