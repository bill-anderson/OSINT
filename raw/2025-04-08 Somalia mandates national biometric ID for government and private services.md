# Somalia mandates use of the national biometric ID for government and private-sector services

> ## Excerpt
> On 7 Apr 2025 PM Hamza Abdi Barre directed all government and private-sector
> service providers to mandatorily integrate the national biometric ID for
> verification and authentication, ahead of a mass enrollment campaign.

---

Somalia's Prime Minister, Hamza Abdi Barre, has directed that all government and private sector service providers begin to mandatorily integrate the national biometric ID card for verification and authentication. The PM made the call while chairing a high-level meeting on April 7 aimed at reviewing implementation of the national identification program and finetuning strategies ahead of a mass citizen enrollment campaign.

According to NIRA, the PM's call is in line with the country's National Transformation Plan, which emphasizes digitizing and modernizing public service delivery. Barre reiterated the government's commitment to registering all citizens and protecting their personal data in conformity with existing laws. He said details of the national ID card adoption strategy would be unveiled soon.

Interior, Federal Affairs and Reconciliation Minister Ali Yusuf Ali, who attended, explained the necessity of integrating the national ID with services across the board. NIRA Director General Abdiweli Ali Abdulle outlined the features and uses of the national ID. The Somalia national ID project has support from Pakistan's NADRA, which recently held a working session with NIRA officials. Official printing of the ID cards was launched in January. NIRA also organized an event in Mogadishu to prepare staff for the upcoming nationwide ID registration exercise.

**Corroboration (WardheerNews, 2025-04-08).** WardheerNews reported the same directive with direct quotes from the PM: "This marks the end of over three decades of identity ambiguity for Somali citizens," describing a functional identity system as "the foundation for effective governance in security, economy, and politics." All federal and service-delivery institutions "must now integrate national ID requirements into their operations," coordinating with NIRA.
