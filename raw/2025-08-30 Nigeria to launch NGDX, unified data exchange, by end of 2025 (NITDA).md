The Federal Government announced plans to streamline identity management through the rollout of the Nigeria Data Exchange Platform (NGDX), a centralised system designed to eliminate repeated data submissions across agencies. For years Nigerians have re-presented the same personal details and biometrics for NIN registration, driver's licences, BVN, SIM registration and passports; NGDX aims to enable secure, seamless sharing of verified data among institutions.

Speaking at a stakeholders' workshop in Abuja, NITDA Director-General Kashifu Inuwa said the platform will serve as a unified backbone for identity and data verification: "Authorised institutions will be able to seamlessly verify and share records on the back end." He said it will cut costs and time, speed KYC checks for fintechs and service providers, and — via anonymised public data — open opportunities for startups in healthcare, agriculture, fintech and education technology. He described it as "essential digital infrastructure," on par with the nationwide fibre optic rollout.

NITDA is working with the Ministry of Communications, Innovation and Digital Economy. Minister Bosun Tijani said NGDX will enable secure data sharing across government and business under a framework of privacy, security and accountability, and is scheduled to go live by the end of 2025.
