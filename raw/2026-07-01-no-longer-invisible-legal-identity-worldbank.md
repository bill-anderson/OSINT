# No Longer Invisible: Unlocking Legal Identity for Millions in Madagascar

**Story highlights:**
- Around **6 million adults in Madagascar, nearly 40% of the population, lack a birth certificate**, limiting access to jobs, financial services and basic rights.
- A **new 2026 law** enables birth certificates to be issued at scale, building on nationwide biometric enrollment under **PRODIGY** (financed by the World Bank and the Global Financing Facility), which has **already registered over 3.25 million adults**.
- Reaching the most excluded remains the next frontier.

In theory, registering a birth in one of Madagascar's ~1,700 communes is straightforward if done within 30 days; after that, late registration requires appearing before a judge, presenting witnesses and obtaining a doctor's attestation — often out of reach for remote families.

On **May 28, 2026, the Government passed a new law** allowing births to be registered en masse "within the framework of the mass biometric enrollment operation," opening a pathway for adults long outside the civil registration system to obtain birth certificates without a costly judicial process.

Since April 2026, more than **2,000 teams**, led by Ministry of Interior staff and supported by PRODIGY, have been deployed with biometric kits, **operating 26 days a month**, collecting fingerprints, iris scans and personal information. As of **June 10, more than 3.25 million adults were enrolled**. More than **19,000 fokontany chiefs** can help confirm the identity of undocumented people via signed testimony combined with a de-duplicated biometric database. As of June 10, only about 50,000 of the 3.25 million enrolled had never held any identity document. World Bank Task Team Leaders: Zubair Bhatti and Lira Rajenarison. The enrollment campaign is branded **"Izaho Tokana."**
