Back in 2020, the Republic of Guinea was set to become the first African country outside Rwanda to host the Transform Africa Summit, but the summit was postponed due to COVID-19. Five years later, Guinea is the first West African and Francophone country to host it — Conakry, 12-14 November 2025.

**ICT sector.** Guinea treats ICT as one of the 5 pillars of "Simandou 2040." As of January 2025: 3.96 million internet users (26.5% penetration), 14.2 million mobile connections (~95% of population, ~81% 3G/4G/5G), 3.05 million social media identities; median fixed-line download 9.5 Mbps. The national e-GOV portal centralizes public digital services; the PM launched e-Conseil and e-Learning platforms in April 2024. Newly structured agencies — ANDE (state digitalization), ANSSI (cybersecurity) and ANSUTEN (universal service and inclusion) — institutionalize delivery, security and last-mile access. Ecosystem initiatives: Guinea Fintech Week, VivaTech 2025 presence, '200 Femmes dans les TIC'.

**Smart Africa.** Guinea joined the Smart Africa Alliance in 2016. Its country flagship project is "Intra-African Connectivity" (developed with TCTS/Tata) to link countries via terrestrial, submarine and satellite infrastructure. Through its regulator ARPT, Guinea chairs the Smart Africa Council of African Regulators (CAR); ARPT DG Mamady Doumbouya. Guinean policymakers have benefited from Smart Africa Digital Academy (SADA) capacity-building. TAS 2025 runs at the Radisson Blu Hotel, Conakry, 12-14 November 2025 (building on the 2023 Zimbabwe summit).
