# Technopolis launches Ol Kalou Jitume Digital Hub, graduates 106 youths

The Technopolis Development Authority (TDA) has launched the Ol Kalou Jitume Digital Hub in Nyandarua County and graduated 106 young people under the Jitume Digital Enablement Program, marking another step in the government's push to expand digital opportunities across the country.

The digital hub, located at Ol Kalou Technical and Vocational College (TVC), is the 112th facility launched by the Authority nationwide. Equipped with 50 computers and modern technology, the Centre is expected to serve as a hub for digital skills training, innovation, online work and digital entrepreneurship.

The launch comes as the government intensifies efforts to bridge the digital divide and equip young people with the skills needed to thrive in the digital economy. Nearly 350 Jitume Digital Hubs have so far been established across the country.

Speaking during the launch, Technopolis Development Authority Board Member Jackie Kiaraho, who was the chief guest, said the hub would open up new opportunities for young people in the area and urged them to take advantage of the facility.

"The launch of the Ol Kalou Jitume Digital Hub represents opportunity, inclusion, and empowerment. Through Jitume, we are ensuring that young people across the country have access to the, digital skills, tools and opportunities needed to thrive in the modern economy," she said.

The event also saw the graduation of 106 trainees who completed the Jitume Digital Enablement Program, adding to a growing pool of youths equipped with practical digital skills. A further 200 beneficiaries have received support through the program in the area.

Technopolis Development Authority Chief Executive Officer John Paul Okwiri said the program has made significant strides since its inception in 2023, training more than 140,000 youths and linking over 41,000 to economic opportunities.

"As we celebrate the graduation of 106 digital champions today, we reaffirm our commitment to building a digitally skilled workforce that will drive Kenya's innovation agenda and contribute meaningfully to national development," Okwiri said.

He added that the Authority remains focused on achieving its target of training one million Kenyans and creating sustainable pathways to employment through digital skills and innovation.

For many graduates, the programme has already begun changing lives.

One of the graduates, Loise Wanjiru Njoroge, said the training exposed her to new opportunities in the digital space and boosted her confidence.

"Before joining the Jitume program, I had limited exposure to digital work opportunities. Through the training, I gained practical digital skills, confidence, and a better understanding of how to earn income online. The program has opened new possibilities for my future," she said.

The Jitume Digital Enablement Programme is implemented by the Technopolis Development Authority under the Ministry of Information, Communications, and the Digital Economy in partnership with the ICT Authority, the Communications Authority of Kenya, and the National Government Constituencies Development Fund (NG-CDF).

The program seeks to enhance digital literacy, improve access to technology, and connect young people to employment and entrepreneurship opportunities as Kenya accelerates its digital transformation agenda.
