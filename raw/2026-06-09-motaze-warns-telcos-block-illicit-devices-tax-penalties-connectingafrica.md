# Cameroon warns telcos to block illicit devices or face tax penalties

*By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa — June 9, 2026*

Cameroonian Finance Minister Louis-Paul Motazé has instructed telecom operators MTN and Orange to block illicit devices or face tax penalties for non-compliance.

Cameroonian Finance Minister Louis-Paul Motazé has instructed MTN and Orange to block illicit devices or face tax penalties for non-compliance, and has requested measures to ensure that a government directive is effectively implemented.

In a meeting last month, Cameroon Customs Director General Fongod Edwin Nuvaga instructed telcos to begin blocking digital devices that have not been registered and cleared through the country's new electronic customs system.

The blocking was set to begin on May 25.

Following that meeting, Motazé wrote a letter expressing dissatisfaction with the telecom operators' inaction and demanding the immediate implementation of the blocking measure.

The minister said operators will be held accountable for any non-compliant devices accessing their networks, making them liable for tax payments.

## Cameroon collecting duties

The directive forms part of broader reforms to the system for collecting duties and taxes on phones, tablets and other digital devices, which came into effect in April this year.

The framework relies on identifying devices via their international mobile equipment identity (IMEI) numbers and cross-checking them against customs data to detect equipment brought into the country through unofficial channels.

In his letter, the minister clarified that the blocking should target IMEIs not listed in the reform monitoring platform, and not subscriber numbers.

The clarification came amid concerns from some users who feared their SIM cards would be blocked rather than the devices themselves.

According to Motazé nearly 700,000 phones had been connected to local networks between April 1 and April 25, 2026, without undergoing customs clearance.

He said this highlighted the ongoing role of parallel import channels, which significantly erode state customs revenues.

## MTN, Orange reservations

According to a report by Investir au Cameroun, MTN Cameroon and Orange Cameroon had sent out a joint letter expressing the telcos' support for the reform's objectives, which aim to boost customs revenue and clean up the mobile device market.

However, the telcos believed that several technical, operational and legal prerequisites have not yet been met to ensure the orderly implementation of the system.

They warned that they would not be able to automatically block devices by the May 25 deadline.

The operators warned that such a situation would create a distortion of competition as subscribers blocked on networks implementing the measure could continue to use their devices on the network of an operator that is not yet technically ready to implement the same blocking.
