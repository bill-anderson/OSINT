The Bank of Ghana (BoG) has issued a revised Supervisory Guidance Note on the use of the Ghana Card, reinforcing it as the primary — and in most cases sole — form of identification for financial transactions. The directive, effective immediately, replaces the June 2022 guidance and centres the NIA database in KYC, Customer Due Diligence (CDD) and AML compliance.

All BoG-licensed and regulated financial institutions must use the Ghana Card as the only ID document when onboarding customers — including Ghanaian citizens, permanent residents, ECOWAS nationals resident in Ghana, and foreign directors/shareholders who are account signatories. Institutions must verify customers biometrically using the card's embedded features and update records from the NIA database; primary data (name, DOB, nationality) must be corrected at the NIA.

Stricter rules apply to mobile/internet banking: institutions may no longer use a risk-based approach for identification at onboarding and must conduct full biometric verification including liveness checks for all digital onboarding; third-party and non-face-to-face transactions also require biometric verification. Customers without a Ghana Card (or approved alternative ID for non-citizens/refugees) are barred from transactions. Foreign non-residents in Ghana <90 days may only do limited one-off transactions (remittances, ATM/POS) with a passport plus KYC; diplomats are exempt. The note details 'no match' escalation procedures and offline MECO devices during downtime.
