# Medusa's ViaTunisia subsea cable now live

June 5, 2026 — Connecting Africa (Matshepo Sehloho)

Telecom operator Orange has announced that the "ViaTunisia" segment of the Medusa Submarine Cable System, linking France to Tunisia, is now operational, creating a new high-capacity digital corridor between Europe and North Africa.

The telco, which acts as the landing partner in both Marseille and Bizerte, said ViaTunisia connects directly to its global network in Marseille through a fully redundant urban fiber ring linking all the city's data centers. "This set up enables seamless interconnexion and distribution of international capacity across Europe," the telco said. Designed as an open, point-to-point system with a 25-year design life, ViaTunisia moved through each phase: marine surveys, factory acceptance tests, cable loading, laying, shore landings and final splicing.

Orange said ViaTunisia was co-financed by the European Union under the Connecting Europe Facility (CEF Digital) program; the grant agreement, signed in December 2022, provided funding covering 30% of the construction and management costs. The CEF Digital program also provided co-financing for the Medusa system, together with Orange and AFR-IX Telecom. The Medusa system stretches 8,760km and connects 17 landing points across the northern and southern Mediterranean.

The article situates ViaTunisia within a wider African subsea-cable boom in the first half of 2026, including a Europe–South Africa cable MoU (Canalink, GUILAB, Orange group entities, Sonatel, Silverlinks), Guinea joining Medusa Africa, and EllaLink's landing in Nouadhibou, Mauritania.
