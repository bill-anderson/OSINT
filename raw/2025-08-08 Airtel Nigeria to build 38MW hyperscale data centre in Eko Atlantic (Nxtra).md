Airtel Nigeria plans to build a carrier-neutral 38MW hyperscale data centre in Eko Atlantic City in Lagos to meet surging demand for cloud and AI services. Part of Airtel's broader Nxtra network, the facility will handle next-generation compute workloads including GPU-powered infrastructure for AI. It will host over 3,000 racks across six floors with high power density up to 25kW per rack, energy-efficient systems targeting a PUE under 1.4, and redundant power from multiple substations. Its Eko Atlantic location offers proximity to subsea cable landing stations and dense fibre routes.

Airtel Nigeria CEO Dinesh Balsingh told a media briefing in Lagos that the data centre will cost over US$120 million and was scheduled for completion in Q1 2026. Eko Atlantic (Nigeria International Commerce City), on reclaimed land next to Victoria Island, has drawn tenants including First Bank, Dangote Group and MTN Nigeria. The announcement came a month after MTN Nigeria launched the first phase of its US$235 million Lagos data centre; OADC separately plans to expand its Lagos facility from 1.5MW to 24MW by 2027.
