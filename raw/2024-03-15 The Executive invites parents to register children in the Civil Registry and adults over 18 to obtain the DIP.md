Official (PDGE, 15 March 2024). The Government will require the personal identity document (DIP) more strictly of those over 18 to access public services — the backdrop of a meeting between the Vice-President and the inter-ministerial commission charged with strategies for complying with the Child Protection Law.

Vice-President Nguema Obiang Mangue advised the Commission to collaborate with CNEDOGE (which already has a nationwide DIP tour plan) and to agree on a simple, forceful message: the whole population must understand the obligation to hold a DIP for any transaction in the country, and the importance of registering minors in the national civil registry. Officials complained of difficulty reconciling registered data on minors with apparent reality. The DIP is obligatory from age 18, and the government will require it more rigorously for any procedure or movement within national territory.
