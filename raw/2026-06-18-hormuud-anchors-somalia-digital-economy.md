# Hormuud anchors Somalia's digital economy

By Lezeth Khoza — ITWeb Africa, June 18, 2026

Somalia's largest telecom operator, Hormuud Telecom, is transitioning from a traditional connectivity provider into the central pillar of the country's digital economy, financial system and humanitarian response infrastructure, CEO Mohamed Aden Farah told ITWeb Africa. Hormuud launched its mobile money platform, EVC Plus, when Somalia lacked a formal banking system, making it free at the point of use. EVC Plus is now used by approximately 90% of the population for retail payments, salaries and remittances, and sits at the backbone of everyday economic activity.

Hormuud's ambitions extend into territory traditionally occupied by commercial banks and government agencies. Its Humanitarian Portal lets NGOs distribute aid digitally via EVC Plus, keeping funds circulating in the local economy. By combining mobile money data, customer behaviour and device access, Hormuud is also driving new lending, with device financing a key entry point for financial inclusion. Despite 4G covering more than 70% of the population, nearly 50% of Hormuud's device base still uses feature phones — "the barrier is no longer the mast in the distance but the handset in the pocket," Farah said, citing the recent Get-Phone smartphone financing initiative.

Looking ahead, Hormuud's strategy focuses on keeping more of Somalia's digital value within its borders: the operator is investing in local data centres to support domestic digital services, create jobs and reduce reliance on external systems, while deploying solar-powered infrastructure for network resilience.
