26sBolstered by the sweeping legislative powers of the newly enacted NIMC Act 2026, the National Identity Management Commission (NIMC) has launched an aggressive, nationwide digital identity enrollment campaign spanning all 8,809 political wards in Nigeria.

Director-General Dr. Abisoye Coker-Odusote detailed the massive structural initiative during a strategic security briefing with the Federal Ministry of Information on Wednesday. The legislative overhaul not only drastically broadens digital inclusion for isolated rural populations but fundamentally rewrites the nation's cybersecurity architecture by granting NIMC robust statutory authority to actively arrest and prosecute identity thieves.

## Legislative Overhaul and the NIMC Act 2026

The newly ratified legislation marks a profound paradigm shift for Nigeria's digital ecosystem. Previously, the repealed NIMC Act severely lacked enforceable data protection provisions, rendering the commission largely toothless against sophisticated cybercrime syndicates.

"The new NIMC Act empowers the commission, in collaboration with security agencies, to arrest and prosecute identity thieves," Dr. Coker-Odusote explained to the press. "In line with this new mandate, we have recently revamped our internal enforcement structure through the Inspectorate, Compliance and Enforcement (ICE) Unit to ensure effective implementation of the Act."

This aggressive enforcement posture is closely tethered to the 2023 Data Protection Act and the establishment of the Nigerian Data Protection Commission (NDPC), creating a highly fortified legal perimeter around the biometric data of over 136 million registered citizens.

## Grassroots Mobilization and Ward-Level Enrollment

To achieve absolute national coverage, NIMC has formally activated a ward-level enrollment protocol. Under direct marching orders from President Bola Tinubu, the commission bypassed traditional urban registration centers to deploy technological assets directly into the rural hinterlands.

- **Target Demographics:** The initiative specifically targets historically disenfranchised groups, including rural women, undocumented children, and persons with severe disabilities.
- **Refugee Inclusion:** Legal residents, displaced persons, and verified refugees are mandated for inclusion to ensure comprehensive national security tracking.
- **Inter-Agency Collaboration:** The National Orientation Agency (NOA) is utilizing its extensive grassroots network to conduct intensive public enlightenment campaigns ahead of NIMC enrollment teams.
- **Service Access:** A verified National Identification Number (NIN) remains the absolute prerequisite for accessing federal intervention programs, banking services, and telecommunications networks.

## The African Digital Identity Landscape

Nigeria's massive digital identity mobilization presents a striking parallel to the highly controversial Maisha Namba transition currently unfolding in Kenya. Both African economic powerhouses are racing to digitize their populations to streamline taxation, eliminate ghost workers in the civil service, and secure digital banking sectors.

However, unlike Kenya—where civil society groups have repeatedly dragged the government to the High Court over severe data privacy concerns and a lack of public participation—Nigeria's NIMC Act 2026 explicitly incorporates advanced cybersecurity and digital trust provisions. By preemptively building an Inspectorate Unit to prosecute internal data breaches and external hacks, Abuja is attempting to construct a legally resilient digital public infrastructure that commands absolute public confidence.

## Enforcement Mechanisms and Fraud Prevention

Information Minister Mohammed Idris, who hosted the NIMC delegation, praised the commission for maintaining a flawless data security record amidst escalating global cyber threats. "I am happy to hear that you have not had a single data breach," Idris noted. "Data privacy, transparency, and public confidence are essential to the success of digital identity management."

As the massive 8,809-ward rollout continues, NIMC officers will be accompanied by federal security personnel. This unprecedented integration of civilian identity management with hard state security apparatus ensures that the National Identity Database transitions from a mere demographic registry into the central pillar of Nigeria's future digital economy.
