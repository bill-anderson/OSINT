(Translated from French; key terms kept.)

The Togolese Revenue Office (Office Togolais des Recettes, OTR) is undertaking a vast operation to georeference land titles (titres fonciers) created before 2007. The initiative aims to modernise the cadastre (cadastre), secure property rights, and foster the country's economic development.

Before 2007, land titles were often established without precise georeferencing, leading to land disputes, overlapping titles, and difficulties in real-estate transactions. Georeferencing allows each parcel to be located precisely on a digital map, guaranteeing legal security of titles and facilitating transactions.

The benefits cited: securing property rights by delimiting parcels exactly and reducing disputes; promoting transparency through digitisation and accessibility of cadastral information, reducing corruption risks; facilitating real-estate investment and infrastructure development; enabling authorities to better plan land-use and urban development; and allowing more efficient collection of land taxes through updated cadastral data.

The operation is led by the Directorate of Cadastre and Land Conservation (Direction du Cadastre et de la Conservation Fonciere), in collaboration with local authorities and landowners. Teams of surveyors (geometres) go into the field to measure and locate parcels; the data are then entered into a digital database. The OTR calls on all owners of pre-2007 land titles to participate actively.
