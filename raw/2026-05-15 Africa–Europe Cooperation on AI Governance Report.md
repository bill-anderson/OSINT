# Africa–Europe Cooperation on AI Governance

> ## Excerpt
> A Lawyers Hub (AFD-supported) report arguing the Africa–Europe AI-governance relationship is
> structurally asymmetric ("regulatory imperialism"; the Brussels Effect; an "Adequacy Trap") but
> correctable within a time-limited "transition window" via deliberate co-governance design.

> **Provenance note.** Desk-review analysis (not fieldwork); its mechanisms are drawn from prior AU–EU
> designs. DP-law/DPA counts (~45/~39) are round estimates single-sourced to a secondary tracker. Two AI
> funds appear and must not be conflated: the *proposed* **$200m Africa AI Governance Capacity Fund**
> vs the **$60bn Africa AI Fund** (2025 Africa Declaration). The Africa Declaration signatory count is
> internally inconsistent (52 vs 49 states) — an in-source discrepancy, noted here.

## Key facts

- Maps four European power channels (GDPR adequacy, ISO/IEC standards, DFI conditionality, OECD/G7
  representation). **~45 African DP laws, ~39 operational DPAs (early 2026)** have not translated into
  influence; **France's CNIL alone (~€28.6m) exceeds all African DPA budgets combined**; **no African
  country holds a GDPR adequacy decision** (Mauritius most advanced).
- Proposes five immediate actions (AU–EU AI Regulatory Dialogue; a **$200m, African-managed AI
  Governance Capacity Fund**; an AU–EU Electoral AI Protocol before the 2027 election cycle; adequacy
  reform; a joint research consortium), expanded to a nine-action roadmap.
- Context: AU Continental AI Strategy (Jul 2024); **Africa Declaration on AI** (Kigali, 4 Apr 2025;
  $60bn Africa AI Fund); EAC AI Declaration (1 Apr 2026); EU AI Act phased enforcement. SASSA/POPIA
  facial-recognition enforcement (2022) cited as "the most significant AI enforcement by an African DPA."
  Notes **South Africa's Draft National AI Policy was withdrawn (April 2026)** after AI-fabricated
  references were found in the gazetted document.
