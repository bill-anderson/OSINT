Senegal has launched **stronger public–private cooperation to secure its critical digital infrastructure**,
centred on a **consultation meeting held 13 July 2026** by the Minister of Telecommunications and Digital
Affairs. The initiative prepares a **future legal framework governing critical-infrastructure protection** and
aims to strengthen prevention, detection and response to cyberattacks. It supports the **[[new-deal-technologique|New
Deal Technologique]]** strategy (sovereign cloud, data centres, advanced/high-performance computing, integrated
satellite + subsea + fibre connectivity).

The push follows a run of attacks on Senegalese state institutions: the **Public Treasury (May 2026)**, the
**Directorate General of Taxes and State Property (Oct 2025)**, and the **national ID-card agency (Feb 2026)**.
Reference points cited: **GSMA** estimates digital could add **CFA1.1tn (~$1.9bn)** to Senegal's economy by 2030;
Senegal scored **67.17/100** (3rd of 4 tiers) in the **ITU Global Cybersecurity Index 2024**.
