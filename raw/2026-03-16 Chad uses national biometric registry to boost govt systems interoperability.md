# Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability

Chad's Ministry of Finance, Budget, Economy, Planning and International Cooperation recently concluded a deal with the National Agency for Secure Documents (ANATS) to strengthen interoperability of its services as part of the country's digital government pursuits.

Signed a few days ago in the capital N'Djamena, the agreement aims to facilitate real-time data sharing, based on the national biometric database, to enhance transparency in administrative operations and financial management, ANATS announced.

Other key objectives are to streamline identity verification and authentication by preventing fraud and identity theft, and to strengthen digital trust in line with Chad's 2030 digital transformation strategy.

Apart from the Ministry of Finance, the Chadian government hopes to have many government ministries and agencies integrate their services with the national biometric registry, which the country intends to make the single source of truth for all identification purposes.

Africa 24 TV quotes government sources as saying that Chad has invested an estimated XAF 15 billion (approximately US$26.1 million) in the last few years to build and strengthen the biometric registry. Part of the investment includes the production of secure identity documents and the upgrade of basic digital infrastructure.

The Director General of ANATS, Youssouf Gorou Tchaby, emphasized: "This system, designed for interoperability, is an essential lever. All administrations, both public and private, will be able to connect to it to verify and authenticate identity." He noted private sector entities can also integrate the registry for identity verification and for access to credit.

Saleh Abakar Zene, deputy director general in charge of the Budget and Digitalization at the Ministry of Finance, said the collaboration shows the Chadian government's push towards a fully digital government is "built through concrete decisions, useful partnerships, courageous reforms, and coherent actions."

Chad is implementing an economic transformation strategy, Tchad Connexion 2030, which has digital technology at its core. The country intends to invest around $1.5 billion by 2030 on ICT infrastructure and digital services, with goals including at least 80 percent national internet coverage, about 80 percent mobile penetration, and lifting about 2.5 million Chadians out of poverty through digital-enabled services within five years.

Chad is already taking steps towards digital inclusion with the issuance of biometric ID cards to refugees using Idemia biometrics technology. ANATS also recently received biometric enrollment equipment from the UNHCR to facilitate its inclusive ID push.
