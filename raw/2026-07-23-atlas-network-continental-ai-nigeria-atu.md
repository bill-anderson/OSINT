# Bosun Tijani unveils Atlas Network to drive African AI development

*By Busola Aro, TheCable, 23 July 2026.*

Bosun Tijani, minister of communications, innovation and digital economy, has unveiled the Atlas Network, a continental artificial intelligence (AI) initiative designed to accelerate the development of African large language models, while urging African countries to collaborate on shaping the continent's digital future.

The initiative was launched on Thursday at the seventh ordinary session of the African Telecommunications Union (ATU) Conference of Plenipotentiaries in Abuja, where leaders also called for a united African position ahead of the 2026 International Telecommunication Union (ITU) Plenipotentiary Conference.

The minister said the Atlas Network builds on N-Atlas, which he described as Africa's first government-backed open-source large language model launched by Nigeria at the United Nations General Assembly last year.

"Today, I'm pleased to announce that together with partners from across Africa, we are taking the next step by expanding this initiative into a continental framework – the Atlas Network," Tijani said.

"The ambition is for the Atlas Network to become a platform that accelerates the development of locally relevant large language models in Africa, by Africa and for Africa.

"It will bring together governments, researchers, innovators, academia and ecosystem partners to ensure that Africa's languages, cultures and knowledge systems are reflected in the AI technologies that will shape our future."

He said fewer than two percent of Africa's languages are meaningfully supported by modern AI systems despite the continent being home to nearly one-third of the world's languages.

Tijani invited all ATU member states to join the initiative, saying the continent has an opportunity not only to adopt artificial intelligence but also to shape its future.

"This is our opportunity, not simply to adopt artificial intelligence, but to help shape its future for Africa and from Africa," he said.

The minister also called on African countries to strengthen cooperation ahead of the ITU Plenipotentiary Conference later this year, saying the continent must arrive "prepared, united and credible" to influence decisions on global telecommunications and digital governance.

He said Africa should continue to invest in technical preparation, deepen regional cooperation and present common positions on issues affecting the continent.

## 'ATU SEEKS $1.7M FUNDING'

On his part, John Omo, secretary-general of the ATU, urged member states to increase their financial contributions to sustain the organisation's programmes, saying additional resources would be required to implement activities planned for the 2027–2031 strategic cycle.

Omo disclosed that the conference preparatory committee had projected the Union's budget for the 2027–2031 cycle but identified an additional funding requirement of more than $1.7 million for mandatory activities.

"The conference preparatory committee allocated upwards of 1.7 million US dollars for certain mandatory activities for which further funding will be necessary," he said.

The secretary-general said that he had written to ministers across member states, urging them to increase their contributory units to sustain the Union's operations.

According to the ATU secretary-general, member state contributions remain the bedrock of the Union's activities despite growing support from development partners.

He said the conference would review the union's strategic direction, finances and leadership while strengthening Africa's preparations for the ITU Plenipotentiary Conference.

Also speaking, Doreen Bogdan-Martin, secretary-general of the ITU, said Africa is increasingly emerging as a global force in digital innovation and policymaking.

"Africa will not simply participate in the digital future. Africa will help shape it, not only as a consumer of technology, but as a creator, an innovator, a standard setter and a trusted global partner," she said.

The conference is expected to adopt Africa's common positions ahead of the ITU Plenipotentiary Conference and elect a new leadership for the African Telecommunications Union.
