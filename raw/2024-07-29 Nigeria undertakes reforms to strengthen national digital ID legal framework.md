[Earlier milestone / legislative precursor to the NIMC Act 2026.]

Legislation to usher in a new dispensation for legal and digital identity management in Nigeria is under study in the National Assembly. In a statement issued 26 July 2024, NIMC said the draft will "strengthen the regulatory function" of the ID authority and "enhance the efficiency and inclusivity of the identity management system."

The NIMC repeal and enact bill No. 23 — which embodies several proposed amendments to update the NIMC Act of 2007 — was first read in the Senate on 5 June 2024 and passed second reading early in July. The statement (signed by Head of Corporate Communications Kayode Adegoke) says the amendments expand the scope of registrable persons for a more comprehensive and inclusive system, streamline the sharing of personal data with robust data-protection measures, and enhance NIMC's administrative enforcement power.

Among the changes is the decriminalisation of the non-use of the NIN (the 2007 Act provided penalties for non-compliance). Bill sponsor Senator Barau Jibrin (Kano) said the new bill proposes "administrative enforcement measures to encourage compliance with NIN usage" instead of criminal penalties. The reform was welcomed by digital-rights advocates critical of NIMC's posture over recent data-breach reports.
