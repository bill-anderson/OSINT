# Preliminary Report of the Independent International Scientific Panel on AI (July 2026)

The first report of the UN's Independent International Scientific Panel on AI — the first global
scientific body on AI, established by General Assembly resolution 79/325 (2025) under a strictly
scientific, non-political mandate to document international scientific consensus and disagreement.
Co-chaired by **Yoshua Bengio** and **Maria Ressa**; an evidence-based assessment of AI's
capabilities, opportunities and risks to give Member States a shared evidence base.

## Africa relevance

- The Panel includes **eight Africa-affiliated scientists**: Girmaw Abebe Tadesse (Ethiopia),
  Tegawendé Bissyandé (Burkina Faso), Awa Bousso Dramé (Cabo Verde), Mennatallah El-Assady (Egypt),
  **Vukosi Marivate (South Africa)**, Joyce Nakatumba Nabende (Uganda), Adji Bousso Dieng (Senegal)
  and **Rita Orji (Nigeria)** — a rare African presence in a top global AI-science body.
- Presented at the **inaugural Global Dialogue on AI Governance** (Geneva, 2026-07-09), where the
  **[[african-union]]** Commission (Lerato Mataboge) pressed for a coordinated African position (see
  [[2026-07-09 African Union pushes for unified African voice at Global AI Governance Dialogue]]).
- The report is the first of its kind and will be updated progressively with thematic briefs.
