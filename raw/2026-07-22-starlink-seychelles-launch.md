Seychellois households and businesses can now access a new internet service following the soft launch of Starlink, the satellite-based broadband service that delivers high-speed internet through a network of low-Earth orbit satellites. The launch marks another step in Seychelles' efforts to expand digital connectivity and provide consumers with more internet service options.

The service was officially introduced during a press conference at State House, jointly hosted by Vice-President Sebastien Pillay and the Seychelles Communications Regulatory Authority (SCRA). The briefing outlined the regulatory framework governing Starlink's operations, the services it will offer, and how the satellite internet provider will complement the country's existing telecommunications landscape.

Speaking at the event, Vice-President Pillay described the arrival of Starlink as an important milestone in Seychelles' digital transformation journey. He said the government remains committed to ensuring that all Seychellois have access to reliable, affordable, and high-quality internet services while maintaining a competitive and fair telecommunications market.

He added that embracing emerging technologies is essential to driving economic growth, improving public service delivery, and creating new opportunities for businesses, students, and communities. At the same time, he emphasized the importance of maintaining a strong regulatory framework to protect consumers, safeguard national interests, and promote healthy competition within the sector.

Officials from the SCRA confirmed that Starlink has been authorized to operate in Seychelles under the country's telecommunications regulations. The Authority explained that it will continue to oversee the company's compliance with national laws, technical standards, and consumer protection requirements. The regulator also noted that satellite broadband can significantly improve internet access in areas where traditional telecommunications infrastructure is difficult or costly to deploy.

The briefing also highlighted the technical requirements for using the service. Unlike conventional broadband, Starlink users must install the receiver in an open location with a clear and unobstructed view of the sky to maintain a stable connection with the satellite network. Trees, buildings, and other obstacles may interfere with connectivity, and customers are advised to carefully assess installation sites to achieve optimal performance.

Subscriptions to the service are now available through Starlink's online platform, where customers can register, purchase the required hardware kit, and activate their subscriptions. Officials revealed that several users in Seychelles have already acquired Starlink equipment and are actively using the service.

Journalists attending the briefing also received updates on Starlink's rollout plans, licensing arrangements, subscription procedures, and the anticipated impact of the new entrant on Seychelles' telecommunications industry. Officials indicated that while the current event marked the service's soft launch, a formal launch ceremony involving representatives from Starlink will be held at a later date as the company continues to establish its local operations.

The introduction of Starlink represents another milestone in Seychelles' digital development strategy, expanding connectivity options while reinforcing the country's commitment to technological innovation under a transparent and effective regulatory framework.
