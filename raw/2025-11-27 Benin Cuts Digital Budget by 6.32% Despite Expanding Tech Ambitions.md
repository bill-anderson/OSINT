Benin set a **2026 digital budget of CFA27.2 billion (~US$48 million)** — **down 6.32%** from the CFA29.034 billion approved for 2025 — attributed mainly to a **12.22% drop in external financing**. Minister of Digital and Digitalisation Aurélie Adam Soulé Zoumarou presented the allocation to the National Assembly on 25 November 2025: CFA9.7bn operating and CFA17.4bn capital, across three programmes — Piloting and Support (CFA2.1bn), the Digital Programme (CFA12.3bn, nationwide digital transformation) and the Media Programme (CFA12.6bn, audiovisual/DTT).

Despite the cut, the government said it would still fund AI integration in public services, nationwide high-speed coverage, and a national digital-skills school. It cited the GSMA estimate that Benin's digital economy could add **CFA1,200 billion to GDP and create over 300,000 jobs by 2028**.
