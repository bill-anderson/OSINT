# In the Shadows of Mass Surveillance (editorial)

*Mauritius Times, 7 February 2025.*

Editorial on parliamentary revelations of extensive communications surveillance since 2017. PM **Navin Ramgoolam** confirmed illegal wiretapping and digital espionage allegedly begun when Pravind Jugnauth became PM. Preliminary audits by US experts describe a high-tech interception system, **PertSol**, monitoring phone calls, internet traffic and social media (Facebook, TikTok, WhatsApp, Signal, email), stored and analysed **without judicial oversight**.

The system's roots: in 2017 the then MSM-led government procured a surveillance system from the Israeli firm **VERINT** enabling large-scale interception; a further batch was acquired in 2022. Devices were deployed across major telecom operators, giving direct access to the **Counter Terrorism Unit (CTU)** and **National Security Services (NSS)**, under exclusive intelligence-agency control without transparent regulatory oversight.

A significant amount of intercepted data was reportedly **wiped from government servers immediately after the 2019 general elections**, raising questions of political (rather than national-security) intent and targeting of opposition figures, journalists and activists. The editorial draws parallels with global spyware scandals (Paragon/Graphite in Italy). Bears directly on Mauritius's data-protection credibility and the surveillance-law controversy.
