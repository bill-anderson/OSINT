# PPDA rolls out mandatory E-Procurement system

The Public Procurement and Disposal of Assets Authority (PPDA) has announced the rollout of Malawi's mandatory electronic procurement system — the Malawi National Electronic Procurement System (MANePS) — marking a shift from paper-based processes to a fully digital platform aimed at improving transparency and efficiency. The system officially takes effect from 1 April 2026, following a government directive requiring all public procurement activities to be conducted electronically.

PPDA Acting Director General Timothy Kalembo said the reform is a decisive step in modernizing public finance management: "all public procurement processes in Malawi are going to be utilizing this system. We are no longer going to use paper." He said the transition is backed by government policy issued in March 2026 mandating all public entities to adopt the platform without exception, aligned with Malawi 2063's priorities on transparency and value for money.

According to PPDA, the system is expected to reduce costs and logistical burdens for suppliers and institutions by enabling remote submissions and registrations: "Suppliers will no longer need to travel long distances just to submit documents or register."

(Advances the earlier eGP / e-Government Procurement work under the World Bank Fiscal Governance PforR into a mandatory nationwide system.)
