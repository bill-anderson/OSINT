# Algeria approves draft law on national digital ID and trust services

Algeria's Council of Ministers, chaired by President **Abdelmadjid Tebboune**, approved on **2 November 2025** a **draft law on trust services for electronic transactions and digital identification** — see [[algeria-digital-id-trust-law]]. It modernises the country's **2015 electronic-signature framework**, granting electronic documents, signatures, seals and timestamps the same legal validity as physical counterparts, and formally recognising digital signatures, electronic seals, secure time-stamping and web-authentication services.

A central component is a **national digital identification system linked to the existing biometric national ID card**, consolidating citizens' biometric data into a unified digital-identity platform to streamline e-government and e-commerce. Trust-service providers must be **certified** and meet conditions guaranteeing the integrity of the digital certificates they issue. The reform is framed as a pillar of the **"Digital Algeria 2030"** strategy ([[digital-algeria-2030]]) and of public-sector modernisation / anti-corruption efforts. The bill moves to the next legislative stage.

Context (early 2025): internet penetration ~77–80%, ~36.2m internet users, >55m mobile connections (~116% of population).
