Mozambique officially launched the process to draft a National Digital Transformation Strategy on 12 February 2026, during a special session of the National Conference on Digital Transformation (held 11-12 February in Maputo). The roadmap aims to leverage the country's geographic, energy and demographic potential and link these with digital public infrastructure for inclusive development and economic resilience.

The strategy design will include regional/national consultations, a vision, priorities and performance indicators, strategic objectives and an action plan, plus monitoring, evaluation and governance mechanisms, concluding with participatory validation. Lourino Chemane, chairman of the board of the National Institute of ICT (INTIC), said a nationally designed and owned strategy can mobilise investment and international support. About 80% of Mozambicans were offline in 2024 (ITU); mobile penetration 79.9%, mobile-internet penetration 20.5%. Mozambique ranked 177th of 193 on the 2024 UN EGDI (0.2848), and third-tier on the 2024 ITU Global Cybersecurity Index.
