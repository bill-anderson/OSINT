Burkina Faso inaugurated **two government mini/modular data centres** in Ouagadougou (commissioned by Prime Minister Rimtalba Jean Emmanuel Ouédraogo via the Ministry of Digital Transition, Posts and Electronic Communications) as a data-sovereignty move.

Combined capacity: about **3 PB (3,000 TB) of storage**, 105.6 TB of memory and **28,800 CPU cores**, supporting **over 7,000 virtual machines** — roughly a **tenfold** increase on prior national capacity, with each ministry allotted 100–300 VMs to host its digital platforms. The project cost about **16 billion FCFA (~$29 million)** and is projected to save ~30 billion FCFA (~$54m) over five years by reducing reliance on international hosting for government platforms.
