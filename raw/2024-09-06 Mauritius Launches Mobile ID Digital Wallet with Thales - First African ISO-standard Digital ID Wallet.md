Mauritius launched its Mobile ID system on 4 September 2024, supported by a Thales-led consortium and aimed at enhancing access to government services. Unveiled by Minister of Information Technology, Communication and Innovation Deepak (Darsanand) Balgobin, it forms part of the broader "Digital Mauritius 2030" effort. Balgobin said that with the Mobile ID implemented in the MWA digital wallet app, Mauritius becomes the first African country to introduce a digital ID wallet based on ISO standards. The app is available for Android and Apple.

To activate the wallet, users complete a native device biometric unlock (face or fingerprint), upload photos of both sides of their ID card and a selfie; a digital ID card is created along with a QR code for verification. Developed with Thales and local partner Harel Mallac Technologies, the Mobile ID lets citizens manage personal information (e.g. update address, declare a lost physical ID) and sign documents electronically, with dual authentication and identity-theft protection, covering banking, healthcare and public services.

E-government context: Mauritius is among Africa's top EGDI performers; four African countries — Mauritius, South Africa, Seychelles and Tunisia — rank above the global EGDI average.
