Airtel Nigeria has expanded its retail footprint to more than 200,000 customer touchpoints and over 4,000 exclusive shops nationwide, as the country's second-largest telecom operator races to strengthen customer acquisition and support amid intensifying competition and rising demand for digital services.

The expansion, announced by the company on Wednesday, is expected to improve access to services, including SIM registration, airtime and data purchases, device sales, customer support, and other digital offerings through a network of partner stores, neighbourhood agents, and channel partners.

The move comes as Nigeria's telecommunications sector continues to grow, with mobile subscriptions and data consumption increasing and operators investing heavily in network infrastructure and customer acquisition.

In 2025, the telecommunications and information services sector contributed ₦18.47 trillion ($13.4 billion) to Nigeria's real gross domestic product (GDP), accounting for 8.34% of the economy, according to the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS). It was the country's fourth-largest contributor to real GDP throughout the year, underscoring its increasing role in the economy.

Rising demand for mobile and broadband services is also driving the expansion. Nigeria's active mobile subscriptions climbed to 188 million in April 2026, while broadband subscriptions crossed 120 million for the first time, according to data from the Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC).

As of April 2026, Airtel remains Nigeria's second-largest mobile operator behind MTN, serving 64.7 million subscribers—about 34.4% of the market share.

"Telecommunications goes beyond the physical infrastructure; it is important that we are able to reach people where they live and work," Dinesh Balsingh, Airtel Nigeria's chief executive officer, said in a statement. "That is why we continue to invest in a strong retail and distribution ecosystem that enables us to bring Airtel closer to our customers while delivering a consistent, high-quality experience wherever they are."

Joypratip Sengupta, the company's director of sales and distribution, said the retail network combines logistics, partnerships, and community engagement to ensure customers can access Airtel's services efficiently across the country.

"Our retail network is designed to ensure that our services reach customers efficiently. It is a combination of efficient logistics systems, strong partnerships and local engagement that enables us to consistently meet the needs of our customers across the country," Sengupta said.

The retail expansion complements Airtel's broader investments in network infrastructure. In April, Airtel said it had expanded its network footprint in Nigeria from just over 13,000 sites to nearly 17,200 over the previous three years, including the rollout of more than 1,500 new base stations in the past year to boost coverage and network capacity.

Beyond improving customer access, Airtel said its retail ecosystem also supports thousands of distributors, retailers, and small business owners across Nigeria, creating economic opportunities through its sales and service value chain.

The company added that it will continue investing in service delivery and digital innovation as demand for mobile connectivity and digital services expands across the country.
