Agence Ecofin (11 Oct 2025) reports the DRC's **PNN2** launch (drafting launched Wed **8 October 2025**
by Digital Economy Minister **Augustin Kibassa Maliba**), and states the financing envelope's **split**
explicitly:

> "To support this roadmap, the government plans a **public investment of USD 1 billion over five years**,
> supplemented by **USD 500 million in external support already guaranteed by international partners**."

So the **US$1.5bn** envelope = **$1bn** public (2026–2030) + **$500m** secured partner support — the exact
composition that resolved REV-COD-001. Four pillars (infrastructure/connectivity; digital platforms &
public services; human capital & inclusion; cybersecurity & digital trust) plus five cross-cutting themes
(digital entrepreneurship, innovation, technological sovereignty, AI, strategic partnerships). A
**Congolese Academy of Artificial Intelligence** is to be created under the AI Strategy
([[drc-national-ai-strategy]]).

Context: PNN2 succeeds the **Plan National du Numérique Horizon 2025** (2019), which the ministry estimates
is **~60% complete** (national fibre + regional CAB5, an online tax portal, customs single-window, civil-
registration digitisation, and a digital-ID system underway). Per a **GSMA** report (Sept 2025), digital
could add **~$4.1bn** to the economy by 2029.
