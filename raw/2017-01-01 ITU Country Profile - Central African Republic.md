**Binary artefact:** [[2017-01-01 ITU Country Profile - Central African Republic.pdf]] (ITU ICT profiles of LDCs, 2017).

Reference profile of CAR's telecom/ICT sector.

**Key evidence:**
- **Four mobile operators:** MOOV (launched 2005, subsidiary of Maroc Telecom); TELECEL (oldest, 1996, subsidiary of South Africa's Econet Wireless); AZUR (2004, private Congolese group); and Orange (latest entrant, 2007, subsidiary of Orange France — see [[orange-centrafrique]]). Despite the number of operators, penetration is low, challenged by instability since 2013. Orange was first to launch 3G (2013), mainly urban. Inadequate electricity and backbone make network expansion hard.
- **Fixed:** incumbent **[[socatel]]** (partly privatised 1990, 40% sold to a France Telecom/Orange subsidiary); constrained by a fixed base of only a few thousand subscriptions. Most broadband is via mobile; some fixed-wireless WiMAX.
- **International connectivity:** as a **landlocked country with no direct undersea-cable access**, CAR relies on costly satellite for most international bandwidth, keeping prices high. The World Bank **Central African Backbone (CAB)** fibre project's cross-border/transit component was **cancelled** — reluctance to end SOCATEL's international-gateway monopoly plus an unstable institutional environment.
- **Policy:** the 2007 Law on Telecommunications Regulation was the main legislation; a draft Law on Electronic Communication was adopted October 2017, replacing the Agency for Regulation of Telecommunications with the Regulatory Authority for Electronic Communication (see [[arcep-central-african-republic]]).
