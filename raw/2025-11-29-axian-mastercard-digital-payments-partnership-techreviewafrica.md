# AXIAN Group and Mastercard partner to expand digital financial inclusion across Africa

Date: 2025-11-29
By: Nana Appiah Acquaye

The AXIAN Group has entered into a major strategic partnership with Mastercard to roll out a new suite of digital payment solutions in Madagascar, Tanzania, Togo, the Comoros and Senegal. The collaboration introduces both virtual and physical payment cards, along with merchant solutions offered through AXIAN's Mixx and MVola brands, enabling millions of consumers, small businesses and entrepreneurs to send and receive payments securely and easily from their mobile phones.

Mete Guney, Executive Vice President for Market Development in EEMEA at Mastercard, said the agreement reflects a shared commitment to expanding access to inclusive financial services across the continent. He noted that technology continues to unlock new opportunities for people and businesses, and the partnership aims to provide simple, secure ways to make payments and transfer money.

Through the Mixx and MVola mobile applications, users will be able to activate and top up virtual cards, check real-time exchange rates and track or cancel transactions. The features are designed to enhance the convenience, speed and security of international payments and provide users with greater control over their financial activity.

Erwan Gelebart, CEO of AXIAN's Digibank & Fintech division, said the company aims to play a central role in Africa's digital transformation by offering tools that simplify daily life, expand access to financial services and support customers in an increasingly interconnected world. He noted that the partnership with Mastercard gives individuals and businesses practical means to make purchases, send money across borders and manage operations safely through a mobile platform.

The collaboration broadens access to financial and payment services for underserved communities in multiple markets, supporting efforts to unlock the full potential of Africa's digital economy.
