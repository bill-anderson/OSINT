The municipality of Sanoyah, on the outskirts of Conakry, began issuing biometric birth certificates locally on 2 March 2026, extending Guinea's civil-registration modernization to a new commune. The programme is part of a national initiative to digitalize public identity services and build a coherent digital-identity ecosystem on the principle of "one citizen, one identity." The launch was attended by the Director General of Guinea's National Office for Civil Registration and Identification (ONECI) and officials from the Ministry of Territorial Administration and Decentralization.

Previously residents had to travel to neighbouring communes for identity documents; the new service allows both the biometric birth certificate and biometric national ID card enrolment in the home commune. For its first phase, 2,000 biometric birth certificates ("Extraits de naissance biométriques") were allocated. To obtain one, applicants present a previous birth certificate, a court-issued supplementary judgment, or a RAVEC document (Guinea's biometric voter-registration authority); documents from private data centres are not accepted. Certificates are free for residents aged 15-30; for children under 15 a 60,000 GNF receipt fee applies. The biometric national ID card is free for first-time applicants aged 16+.

The rollout builds on Guinea's broader national ID programme using MOSIP and TECH5 biometric infrastructure; the biometric birth certificate is the foundational document for the national biometric ID card, connecting civil registration from birth to the digital-identity pipeline.
