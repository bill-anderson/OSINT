# AI in government — Algeria's High Commission for Digitalisation and National AI Strategy

Algeria's digital transformation is steered from the Presidency: the **High Commission for Digitalisation (HCD/HCN)** was created by **Presidential Decree No. 23-314 (6 Sep 2023)**, led by High Commissioner **Meriem Benmouloud** (ministerial rank) — see [[high-commission-digitalisation-algeria]] — overseeing **"Digital Algeria 2030" (SNTN-2030)** ([[digital-algeria-2030]], unveiled May 2025). In November 2025 the government approved the **[[algeria-digital-id-trust-law|digital-ID and trust-services draft law]]** replacing the 2015 framework.

**National AI Strategy.** In **December 2024** the **National AI Council** — led by Professor **Merouane Debbah** (founding Director, 6G Research Center, Khalifa University) — adopted Algeria's **National AI Strategy** ([[algeria-national-ai-strategy]]) across six pillars (research, talent, hardware/infrastructure, investment/ecosystem, data protection & regulation, sectoral AI). Algeria targets **AI = 7% of GDP by 2027**; its AI market is projected to grow from **$498.9m (2025) to $1.69bn (2030)**.

**GovTech build-out:** the **Bawabatak** portal (bawabatic.dz) offers **342 digitised services** across 25 ministries; the **Dzair Services** platform (Oct 2025, see [[dzair-services]]) centralises services over an interoperability layer connecting 46 ministries/agencies; a national data centre runs in El Mohammedia (second under construction in Blida).
