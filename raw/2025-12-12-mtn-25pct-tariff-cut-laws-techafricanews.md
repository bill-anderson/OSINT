# South Sudan's ICT Ministry Commends MTN for 25% Tariff Reduction, Urges Further Cuts

*TechAfrica News, 12 December 2025 (Jennifer Onyeagoro)*

South Sudan's Ministry of Information, Communication Technology and Postal Services has commended MTN South Sudan for reducing its service tariffs by 25%, while calling on the operator to implement additional cuts to make services more affordable. Minister Ateny Wek Ateny gave the remarks during an MTN stakeholder appreciation event, saying further reductions would enhance digital inclusion and support economic participation.

The Minister also announced that the Cybersecurity and Computer Misuse Bill was awaiting final assent from the presidency; once signed, it is expected to strengthen the legal framework against hate speech, online harassment, and social media abuse.

In addition, he revealed that the Ministry, in collaboration with the Ministry of Justice, will soon begin work on the Data Protection Bill, aiming to safeguard citizen privacy and protect critical government data. The Ministry reiterated its dedication to expanding digital rights, improving regulatory policies, and making South Sudan's telecom landscape more accessible and affordable.
