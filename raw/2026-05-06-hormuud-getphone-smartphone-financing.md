# Hormuud, Get-Phone launch smartphone financing program in Somalia

By Paula Gilbert — Connecting Africa, May 6, 2026

Somali telecom operator Hormuud Telecom has entered a strategic partnership with Mogadishu-based device financing company Get-Phone to roll out a smartphone financing program in Central and South Somalia, aiming to finance 100,000 affordable smartphones by the end of 2026. The program targets low-income users (who on average earn only $150 a month) accessing smartphones for the first time, reducing upfront device cost and subsidizing data. The average customer currently spends around $0.50 on 1GB of data and 40 minutes of calls; through the financing model, users pay between $0.60 and $1.60 per day covering both a smartphone and a daily 1GB/40-minute bundle. Devices require a $19 upfront deposit, with repayments made daily, weekly or monthly through EVC Plus.

A pilot in Mogadishu (February–March 2026) saw default rates below 4%, with customers often acting as guarantors for relatives in rural and nomadic areas. The first phase will roll out 10,000 devices by June 2026, with a further 100,000 planned by end of year as the initiative scales through expansion in **Puntland and Somaliland**. Chairman Ahmed Yusuf said: "The barrier is not the mast in the distance; it is the handset in the pocket." Eligibility uses Hormuud's telecom-based credit scoring (SIM tenure and mobile money spending), requiring no formal credit history. More than 70% of Somalia's 19 million people are covered by 4G, but half of Hormuud's device base is still on 2G/feature phones.
