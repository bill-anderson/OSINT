Hormuud, Get-Phone launch smartphone financing program in Somalia

Somali telecom operator Hormuud Telecom and device financing company Get-Phone aim to finance 100,000 affordable smartphones by the end of 2026.

Somali telecom operator Hormuud Telecom has entered a strategic partnership with Mogadishu-based Get-Phone, to roll out a smartphone financing program in Central and South Somalia.

The program aims to give low-income users access to smartphone devices for the first time.

Hormuud said that in many low-income markets, both smartphones and mobile data remain expensive relative to incomes, limiting uptake even where developed networks exist. This program is designed to address the affordability gap directly.

The partnership aims to significantly reduce the upfront cost of devices and subsidize the cost of data.

Hormuud said the average customer currently spends around $0.50 on 1GB of data and 40 minutes of calls.

Through the financing model, users can now pay between $0.60 and $1.60 per day (depending on duration of financing term), covering both a smartphone and a daily bundle of 1GB data and 40 minutes of calls.

The aim is to make access affordable for lower-income users, who on average earn only $150 a month.

Devices are provided with an upfront deposit of $19, followed by repayments made daily, weekly or monthly through Hormuud's mobile money platform, EVC Plus.

Through the program, users can gain access to 7GB of data and 280 minutes per week, paying between $18 and $36 monthly for the service.

Pilot program results and growth ambitions

The financing model has already been tested through a pilot in Mogadishu that took place between February and March 2026.

The pilot focused on low-income users and tested both the financing model and technical integration.

Hormuud said default rates were below 4%, with strong uptake across target segments.

"In many cases, customers acted as guarantors for devices on behalf of relatives, including family members in rural and nomadic areas. This family support helped extend access while reducing risk," the telco explained.

The first phase of the program will see 10,000 devices rolled out by June 2026, with a further 100,000 planned by the end of the year as the initiative scales through expansion in Puntland and Somaliland.

Ahmed Yusuf, chairman of Hormuud Telecom, said the telco had spent 20 years building its network in Somalia but that affordability remained a key constraint for its users.

"The barrier is not the mast in the distance; it is the handset in the pocket. Today, we are removing that barrier, and in doing so, delivering the last mile of infrastructure this country has been waiting for," Yusuf said.

Data from the World Bank and GSMA shows that access to mobile broadband can increase household consumption by up to 8% and reduce extreme poverty by as much as 7%.

Meanwhile, World Bank data shows that a 10% increase in broadband penetration can increase GDP by up to 1.4% in developing economies.

Hormuud said that today more than 70% of Somalia's population of 19 million is covered by a 4G network.

However, half of Hormuud's existing device base is still on 2G or feature phones due to the high cost of 3G and 4G smartphones.

"Somalia is one of the most dynamic, entrepreneurial societies in the world. This programme is built on the belief that when you give people access to the right tools at the right price, they will do extraordinary things with them," Get-Phone CEO Omar Abdi said in a statement.

Eligibility for the program is determined using Hormuud's telecom-based credit scoring system, which assesses how long a customer has used their SIM card and their mobile money spending patterns.

Based on this, customers are placed into risk tiers and offered an appropriate device and payment plan. There is no requirement for a formal credit history.

Hormuud explained that a usage-based system ensures services remain active as long as payments are made.

If a payment is missed, access is temporarily restricted and the missed days are added to the end of the repayment period, rather than triggering penalties.

Three smart devices are offered under the financing program: the ZTE Blade A36, the ZTE Blade A56 and the ZTE A76.

Somalia's telecom infrastructure has expanded significantly over the past two decades.

The East African country has 11 mobile operators and Hormuud is by far the biggest.

Data from market research company Omdia, a sister company of Connecting Africa, shows that Hormuud had almost 4.9 million mobile users at the end of March 2026.

Telesom Mobile had almost 1.7 million subscribers, while the rest of the operators had between 920,000 and 8,460 users each.

Over the past few years, Hormuud has made some key investments including introducing fiber optic connectivity in Somalia, co-financing two subsea cables, and launching the country's first 5G service.
