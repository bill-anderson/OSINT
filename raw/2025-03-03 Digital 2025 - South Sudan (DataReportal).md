# Digital 2025: South Sudan

> ## Excerpt
> Early-2025 connectivity baseline for South Sudan: 4.47m mobile connections (37.1%), 1.90m internet users (15.7%), 868k social-media identities (7.2%) in a 12.1m population — figures to be carried with their as-of date, not promoted as current state.

---

DataReportal's essential headline figures for connectivity and digital adoption in South Sudan at the start of 2025:

- **Mobile connections:** 4.47 million active cellular mobile connections in early 2025, equivalent to **37.1%** of the total population. Mobile connections grew by 611 thousand (+15.8%) between the start of 2024 and the beginning of 2025. GSMA Intelligence data suggests 80.6% of connections are "broadband" (3G/4G/5G), though this is not a proxy for mobile internet use.
- **Internet users:** 1.90 million individuals used the internet at the start of 2025, an internet penetration rate of **15.7%**. Users grew by 169 thousand (+9.8%) year-on-year. This implies ~10.2 million people (84.3%) remained offline at the start of 2025.
- **Social media:** 868 thousand social media user identities in January 2025, equal to **7.2%** of the population.

**Population context.** The population stood at 12.1 million in January 2025 (up 247 thousand / +2.1% year-on-year). At that time 21.8% lived in urban centres and 78.2% in rural areas; 50.8% female, 49.2% male. The median age was 18.7.
