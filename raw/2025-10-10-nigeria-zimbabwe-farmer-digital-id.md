After the tobacco sector, cotton farmers in Zimbabwe will soon have a biometric ID as part of government efforts to combat illegal market practices, Biometric Update reports (drawing on The Herald). The Agricultural Marketing Authority (AMA) has been given the green light to proceed with the biometric enrollment process.

Deputy Minister of Lands, Agriculture, Fisheries, Water and Rural Development Vangelis Haritatos said the cotton farmer ID database will help combat corrupt practices that have characterized the country's cotton market for years. Speaking during a meeting of cotton stakeholders preparing for the 2025/2026 cotton season, he called on AMA to immediately commence biometric enrollment, saying the "digital registry will form the bedrock of a new era of transparency" and enforce the rule of "one farmer one merchant" to end cross-contracting and side marketing.

AMA acting CEO Jonathan Mukuruba said they had already engaged a partner for the biometric registration of cotton growers and hoped to dispatch field teams from October 15. Authorities say the biometric ID registry will streamline farmer identity verification, especially for crop validation.

The cotton sector is emulating the tobacco industry, where a farmer ID registry has been put in place; the Tobacco Industry and Marketing Board (TIMB) says this will help establish a sustainable, secure, transparent and efficient tobacco industry. The article notes parallel farmer-ID pushes in Nigeria (the All-Farmers Association of Nigeria digital ID, and the Federal Ministry of Agriculture / NIMC plan to register six million farmers in 2025) and India (a plan to issue 110 million farmer IDs).
