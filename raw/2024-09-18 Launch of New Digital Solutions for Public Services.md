*Primary: the issuing department's own announcement of the 17 Sept 2024 change.*

News from the Department of Immigration & Civil Status (ICS) in partnership with the Department of Information Communications Technology (DICT).

At a press conference held today at the Ministry of Internal Affairs, two new digital solutions designed to improve public services were introduced by the ICS team in collaboration with DICT.

1. SeyID: Virtual ID Cards for Permit Holders and Seychellois Diaspora

Starting 17th September 2024, the department will transition from issuing physical ID cards for permit holders. Instead, permit holders will be registered on the SeyID platform and issued a virtual ID card.

How it works:
- The process for obtaining an NIN remains the same, but the applicant will be issued with an ID letter which has their assigned NIN.
- Permit holders can register for their virtual ID card by visiting the SeyID website and downloading the app after receiving their National Identity Number (NIN).
- This service is also available to Seychellois citizens living abroad, who can register using their NIN card or ID letter along with their Passport.

This initiative helps reduce waste and better allocate resources, making ID issuance more sustainable while prioritizing Seychellois citizens.

2. CertExpress

CertExpress is a digital platform that allows Seychellois citizens to conveniently obtain birth, marriage and death certificates online. No more in-person visits — simply request and, in 2 working days, receive your certificates through the platform. A requested own birth or marriage certificate is saved in the user's E-Doc wallet on their SeyID app. The price of certificates remains the same at SCR 50.

Both SeyID and CertExpress are part of ongoing efforts to modernize public services, improve efficiency, and embrace environmentally-friendly solutions, aligning with the government's mission to digitize essential services and make them more accessible.
