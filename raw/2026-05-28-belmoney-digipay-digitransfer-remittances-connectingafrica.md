# Belmoney, DigiPay enable Congolese remittances from France and Belgium

The Staff, Contributors, Connecting Africa. May 28, 2026.

*Sweep note: this item covers **both** Congos — the DigiTransfer corridor serves the Republic of Congo (COG) and the DRC (COD). Tagged to both places; staged in COG because DigiPay Group was founded in Congo-Brazzaville. Not a COD/COG mis-file.*

Belmoney and DigiPay Group have partnered to enable money transfers from France and Belgium to the Republic of Congo and the Democratic Republic of Congo, with future European expansion in the pipeline.

Belmoney has partnered with the DigiPay Group to launch the DigiTransfer mobile application in France and Belgium aimed at the Congolese diaspora.

The new service enables instant and secure money transfers directly from a smartphone to mobile money wallets and bank accounts across the Republic of Congo and the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC).

By leveraging Belmoney's white-label application licensing and secure compliance framework, DigiPay Group can now deploy DigiTransfer under its own brand without having to undergo national licensing applications.

The application routes funds using international bank cards like Visa and Mastercard, operating through a regulated network that promises to authorize and finalize each transaction in under five minutes.

While the service is currently limited to outbound transfers from France and Belgium, the partnership establishes an operational precedence for a planned full-scale expansion across the Eurozone.

In April 2026, Belmoney and RwandaCash also partnered to enable more remittance corridors across the Eurozone for the Rwandan diaspora.

## Bringing down the cost of remittances

In a joint statement, Belmoney and DigiPay said the new remittance corridor is a response to a major financial challenge facing Africa.

According to World Bank data, personal remittance inflows to the DRC alone exceed $3.2 billion annually while figures from the Migration Data Portal show that sub-Saharan Africa faces the highest transaction costs globally, averaging at around 7.9%.

DigiTransfer aims to eliminate "exorbitant and outdated fees weighing on the diaspora" and promises to cut transfer times from several days to just a few minutes.

The companies said the new application aims to reduce cross-border barriers and maximize financial inclusion for families, professionals and small businesses.

"Our core focus is financial inclusion for African populations who are still largely unbanked but now mostly hold a mobile account," said Gachlem Ngassaki-Zoni, founder and CEO of DigiPay Group.

"When we created DigiPay in Congo during the pandemic, our priority was to keep families connected. Since then, we have expanded our offering," Ngassaki-Zoni added.

DigiPay Group is a pan-African fintech company that has processed over 4.2 million transactions since 2020. Founded in 2013, Belmoney was Europe's first specialized remittance-as-a-service (RaaS) provider and now offers fintech services in more than 150 countries.
