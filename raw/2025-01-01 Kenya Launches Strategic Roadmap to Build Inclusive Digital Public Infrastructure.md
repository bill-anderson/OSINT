Kenya has embarked on developing a **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) roadmap**, launched during the inaugural Inter-Ministerial Steering Committee meeting bringing together government agencies, civil society, academia and international partners.

"This roadmap will not be designed by the Ministry of ICT and Digital Economy alone," said PS Eng. John Tanui (in remarks read by Mary Kerema). It builds on Kenya's 20,000+ e-services on eCitizen and aims to consolidate fragmented systems. Grounded in the **GovStack** methodology and supported by GIZ, the ITU and the EU (following a letter of intent at the **Connected Africa Summit, May 2025**), it will serve as a blueprint to unify digital identity, payments, data exchange and trusted services, assessing five domains — digital data infrastructure, interoperability frameworks, digital identity and trust, access, and governance — drawing on Estonia, Singapore and India. KICTANet is credited as a key contributor advocating inclusive, rights-based digital development.
