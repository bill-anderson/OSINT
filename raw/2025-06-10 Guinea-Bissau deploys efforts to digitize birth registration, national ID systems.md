Considerable efforts are underway to digitize, integrate and modernize the civil-registration and national-ID systems of Guinea-Bissau. Civil registration is being digitized through a new **Integrated System of Citizen Registers and Statistics (SIREC)**, said Raul Gomes, Head of the Civil Registration Office at the Ministry of Justice, at the ID4Africa 2025 AGM.

The overall birth-registration rate is below 50% (about 46% of children under five). SIREC registration began roughly three months before the interview; following launch, UNDP trained civil-registry officers on enrollment kits and the platform. The government is exploring integrating birth registration with the health and education sectors, and training health technicians to carry out registration in facilities. Support comes from UNICEF, UNDP and the University of Aveiro (Portugal). Guinea-Bissau already has a functional biometric national ID card system, being modernized under a UNDP-supported "Building National ID" project (Swiss-financed), and is **one of six ECOWAS nations to have fully operationalized the regional ENBIC biometric ID card** for cross-border movement and trade. Challenges: inadequate funding and connectivity.
