# Somalia expands DPI for child immunization in Mogadishu

By Hassan Istiila — Hiiraan Online, Friday February 27, 2026

In Kahda, a district on the southern edge of Mogadishu, health workers now retrieve a child's immunization history in seconds via the Electronic Immunization Register (EIR). For years, an easily-lost paper card was the only proof a child had been vaccinated; when it disappeared, workers restarted schedules, repeated injections or marked children as "zero-dose."

The EIR was piloted in 14 health facilities across Banadir region in 2025. It assigns each vaccinated child a unique identification number, stores dose histories digitally and sends SMS reminders to caregivers; officials at district, state and federal levels see aggregated data in near real time. The Ministry of Health plans to expand it to more than 80 facilities nationwide by 2026. The system is built on DHIS2, the widely used open-source health information platform, with technical support from the World Health Organization.

Roughly 60 percent of children have gone without a single routine immunization dose in recent years amid conflict, drought and displacement; in the first eleven months of 2025 Somalia recorded more than 3,000 suspected diphtheria cases and over 130 deaths. The shift represents an experiment in Digital Public Infrastructure — the idea that interoperable, ID-linked registries can function as public goods. Constraints persist: "Sometimes we record on paper and upload later. It is not always real-time," one health worker noted, citing electricity cuts, weak connectivity and staffing gaps.
