In 2017, a quarter of Benin’s population was without a birth certificate, and less than half had a national identity card (NIC), with the figure for women even lower at 37%. Among those affected were Catherine, a 13-year-old middle school student in Cotonou, and Donatienne, a 44-year-old resident of Abomey-Calavi, both lacking any form of identity proof. This absence of official identification prevented them, along with a significant portion of the population, from enrolling in school, accessing financial services, or receiving the assistance for which they were eligible.

To address this, [the government of Benin embarked on a substantial effort between 2017 and 2018 to biometrically enroll about 90% of the population into the National Registry of Physical Persons.](https://twitter.com/) It also joined the World Bank-supported [**Regional West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion (WURI) project**](https://projects.worldbank.org/en/projects-operations/project-detail/P161329 "West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion (WURI) Program") to develop a foundational digital identification (fID) system, building on earlier efforts to facilitate access to services and enhance inclusion. fID systems are designed to uniquely identify individuals by linking minimal biographic data with biometric information. Their primary purpose is to confirm a person's unique identity - essentially asserting that "I am who I claim to be" - and nothing more. This enables various services, such as social protection, health, education, and finance, as well as SIM card providers, to rely on the fID system to identify and authenticate individuals.

Thanks to WURI's support, one-third of Benin's population, including Catherine and Donatienne, obtained a QR-code-enabled fID certificate, providing them with a lifelong and free proof of identity. This certificate eases access to services for those who previously lacked any government-recognized form of unique identification. The fID is available to all residents of Benin and to Beninese citizens living abroad and can be easily obtained online or in print. [The WURI program also supported the additional enrollment of 2.2 million individuals - 4,000 of whom live abroad - into the system, in an effort to leave no one behind, thus bringing the total biometric enrollment to nearly 99% of the population.](https://twitter.com/)

The fID certificate in Benin “Certificat NPI/fID” also known as “C’est moi” card.

[The WURI project gives priority to vulnerable populations such as women, teenage girls, children, and people with disabilities by collaborating with programs that target them](https://twitter.com/), like the Alafia microcredit program, the education system, or NGOs such as “Handicap à Visage Universel”.

These programs now benefit from a mobile application that allows for the easy authentication of fID certificates using the embedded QR code, reducing the risk of fraud.

Donatienne, 44 years, reseller in Abomey-Calavi, Atlantic department - Credit: Projet WURI-Benin

Donatienne's testimony underscores the project's positive impact: “Since the introduction of the fID certificate, obtaining microcredit has become much easier. With the help of the WURI project, I received my fID certificate, which enabled me to secure an Alafia microcredit to start my business.”

And Catherine emphasizes: “The “C’est moi” card made my task easier in compiling my administrative files for my school enrolment.”
