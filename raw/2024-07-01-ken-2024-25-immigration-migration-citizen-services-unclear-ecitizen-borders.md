# Kenya FY2024/25 budget — State Department for Immigration, migration and citizen services (KES 7.03bn)

Kenya's FY2024/25 budget allocated KES 7.03 billion (US$54.4m, source's conversion) to the State Department for Immigration's migration and citizen services — passport printers, facial recognition at nine border posts, onboarding 33,800 more services to eCitizen, the visa-free regime, and relocation of 48,000 refugees to designated camps.

## Description

"KES 7.03bn ($54.4m) [goes] to migration and citizen services (two high-capacity passport printers, fast-tracking the visa-free regime, facial recognition at nine one-stop border posts, and onboarding 33,800 more services to eCitizen, which launched in June 2023 with ~5,000)" (Biometric Update, 2024-06-17).

Corroborated by Techweez (2024-06-13): "KES 7.03bn goes to migration and citizen services (two high-capacity passport printers, moving 48,000 refugees to designated camps, facial recognition at nine key border crossings, and a visa-free regime). During FY24/25 the department will bring 78 agencies online through the **Integrated Population Registration System (IPRS)** and make 33,800 government services available on eCitizen."
