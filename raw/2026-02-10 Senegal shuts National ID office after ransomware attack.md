# Senegal shuts National ID office after ransomware attack

Senegal confirmed a cyberattack on the **[[daf-senegal|DAF]]** and temporarily closed the office, warning
the country's **19.5 million residents** that ID/passport/biometric services were suspended while integrity
was assessed; authorities said citizen data integrity was not affected. The **Green Blood Group** claimed to
have stolen **139 GB** (citizen records, biometrics, immigration documents), publishing a proof list. Hackers
leaked a **20 Jan email from an [[iris-corporation]] senior GM (Quik Saw Choo)** stating attackers breached
**two DAF servers on 19 Jan**, stealing card-personalisation data; IRIS cut network access, changed
passwords, blocked foreign-mission links, and planned a Dakar visit on 22 Jan. Per the Gambia Journal, the
attack came amid a **commercial dispute over unpaid invoices** between the state and Iris — which requested a
systems shutdown — raising unconfirmed questions of a link between the dispute and the attack.
