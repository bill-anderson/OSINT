US Country Commercial Guide (last published 31 July 2025). Morocco has pursued digital strategies since 2005; the **[[digital-morocco-2030|Digital Morocco 2030]]** strategy was launched on **25 September 2024** — accelerating e-government and a unified services portal, positioning Morocco as a leading African tech hub for FDI, and growing the startup ecosystem, with cloud, AI, connectivity and talent as enablers.

Supporting laws: the Public Services Charter (54.19); Simplification of Administrative Procedures (55.19); Electronic Data Exchange (53-05); inter-administration data interoperability; **Personal Data Protection (09-08)**; and Right of Access to Information (31-13). The tech sector contributes **6.8% of GDP**. E-government: Morocco rose from 140th (2008) to **90th (2024)** in the UN EGDI (11th in Africa), targeting the global top 50, with 300+ citizen and 200+ business digital services. Cloud & data centres: Casablanca hosts 25+ data centres; **[[oracle]]** announced two cloud regions (Casablanca and Settat, ~$140m over five years, May 2024); Microsoft, AWS and Google are exploring. Barriers: institutional fragmentation, data-localization requirements, skills gaps (goal: train 100,000 by 2030), and a digital divide (~1,800 rural villages with no internet).
