Progress report from ID4Africa 2025 (Day 1, Addis Ababa) featuring ID authorities from Benin, Malawi, Mauritania, Tanzania and Somalia. [Mauritania-focused extract.]

Mauritania has enrolled more than 94 percent of the population for national ID since a new ID system was put in place in 2020. The Director of the National Agency for the Population Register and Secure Titles (ANRPTS), Sidi Ali Nafee, highlighted the Integrated System of Population Management and Secured Titles (SIGPTS), which integrates all government structures involved in issuing ID documents. Efforts got a boost in 2023 with the launch of the digital ID and digital-government platform "Houwiyeti," meaning citizens no longer need to travel to government offices: 70 percent of passport requests are now made through Houwiyeti, and more than 622,000 document extracts were processed via the platform in the year.
