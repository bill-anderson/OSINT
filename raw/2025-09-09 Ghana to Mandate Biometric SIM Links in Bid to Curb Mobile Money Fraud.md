The Ghanaian government plans a new legal framework for SIM card registration to combat rising mobile money fraud, Minister of Communication, Digital Technology and Innovations Samuel Nartey George announced on Friday 5 September (Accra, meeting with telecom operators and religious leaders). The legislation, expected in parliament in October, would link every SIM card to the NIA biometric database, relying on biometric verification, a centralized equipment identity register (CEIR) and NIA data synchronization; it would also strengthen data protection among mobile money agents (the weakest link).

Context: Ghana has one of Africa's highest mobile-money adoption rates — a GSMA report shows 200% penetration at end-2023 with 66 million registered accounts for a population of ~33 million; more than 55% of the population uses it for payments/transfers. Per the Bank of Ghana's 2023 financial-stability report, 13,451 fraud cases were reported across the financial sector, with mobile money fraud ~20% (nearly 2,700 incidents involving wallets such as MTN MoMo, Vodafone/Telecel Cash, AirtelTigo Money).
