# Mauritania — parliamentary debate on civil-status enrolment and the ID-exclusion question

*Off-assigned-host capture (La Dépêche de Mauritanie et du Sahel); the single most substantive source on the flagged ID-exclusion / statelessness angle. Event corroborated in Arabic by nkt.mr, sahafatbladi.com and politicienmr.com; an op-ed at rapideinfo.mr frames it as "génocide biologique" (parked as a lead, not staged — anonymous opinion).*

The National Assembly devoted its Thursday public session (presided by Mohamed Bamba Meguett) to two oral questions with debate on civil status and citizens' difficulties obtaining administrative documents. The questions were raised by deputies **Kadiata Malick Diallo** and **Almamy Ilo Ba**.

Deputy Kadiata Malick Diallo argued that the **closure of registration operations in the national secure-documents register had left many citizens without identity papers**, and that the July 2025 decision to frame civil-status enrolment had not sufficiently accelerated or simplified procedures. She asked what measures Interior would take to guarantee all Mauritanians access to civil-status documents and the rights flowing from them. Deputy Almamy Ilo Ba highlighted persistent difficulties in registering citizens and correcting errors, and delays in setting up the commissions handling these files.

Interior Minister **Mohamed Ahmed Ould Mohamed Lemine** replied that enrolment in the National Population Register is **no longer tied to exceptional campaigns or time limits** — it is now a permanent, continuously available public service. He said the number registered rose **from 3.4 million in 2019 to more than 5.1 million today (over 1.7 million new enrolments)**, framing this as extending access to categories "long left at the margins of the system." He cited digitisation of services, mobile missions, and a **large survey that registered more than 613,000 citizens** facing enrolment difficulties; the **2025 legal reform** consecrating permanent open enrolment (**36,516 registered in under a year** under the new mechanism); and **238 local identification-and-statistics commissions** installed in all communes since end-September, having processed tens of thousands of files. He stressed the right to identity as a precondition for access to education, health, social protection and political life.

*(Analytical note — the flagged sovereignty/colonialism angle: named opposition deputies press the government on the persistent exclusion of citizens — disproportionately Haratine and Afro-Mauritanian groups per the wider debate — from the biometric register, i.e. de-facto statelessness for those unable to enrol. The wiki holds ANRPTS's "94% coverage" claim; this session surfaces the contested residual and the political charge around it.)*
