Nigeria's new tax laws reveal the mechanism to make crypto profits traceable: the Tax Identification Number (TIN) and National Identification Number (NIN). TechCabal's analysis of the Nigeria Tax Administration Act (NTAA) 2025 shows the government plans to tie crypto trades to real identities via TINs and NINs, allowing authorities to match trades against income declarations without touching the blockchain. This aligns Nigeria with the OECD's Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework (CARF), which took effect January 1, 2026. The TIN is jointly issued by the Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS).
