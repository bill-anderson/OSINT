The Mauritian government has established an inter-ministerial committee to coordinate implementation of its national digital strategy, unveiled in May 2025. The inaugural meeting took place on 26 August 2025 in Ebene, chaired by the Ministry of Information Technology, Communication and Innovation (MITCI). The committee serves as a single point of contact ensuring coherence across ministries.

Priority areas discussed: digitisation of schools, a national public alert system, a platform for managing social benefits, and reducing delays in public service delivery, alongside protecting vulnerable populations from online abuses.

Context on Mauritius's 2024 digital standing: ranked 76th globally for online administration development and at the forefront in East Africa (UN DESA); 69th globally and 2nd in Africa for AI readiness (Oxford Insights), ahead of South Africa and Morocco; and 3rd in Africa for digital infrastructure development (ITU).
