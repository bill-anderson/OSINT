Launched in 2021 by the National ID Program (NIDP), Fayda is the cornerstone of Ethiopia’s digital transformation strategy, with the ambition of issuing unique digital IDs to more than 90 million citizens and residents by 2028 and unlocking opportunities for people, government, and businesses alike. Ethiopia’s approach to building Fayda has focused on strengthening local capacity while tailoring globally recognized standards to the local context, always with an eye to the rights of Ethiopia’s citizens and residents. Sustaining this work requires coordinated focus, continuous evaluation, and iterative improvement — each central to achieving meaningful progress.

In early 2025, NIDP leveraged the [Digital ID Assessment](https://dial.global/work/digital-id-assessment/), developed by the Digital Impact Alliance (DIAL), as part of its commitment to refine the Fayda ID system. The self-assessment offered a structured way to evaluate Fayda’s design and processes — surfacing where [the system was strong and where improvements were needed](https://dial.global/research/digital-id-assessment-continuous-learning-ethiopia/) to support future growth and sharpen intended outcomes. Following the results, NIDP adopted the toolkit as one of its official monitoring mechanisms to gauge the progress and maturity of Fayda’s implementation.

Since then, Fayda has experienced rapid growth, with over 42 million total registrations reported and integrations with over 130 agencies. To take stock of how the Fayda ID system is evolving alongside adoption, NIDP employed the Digital ID Assessment in 2026 for a follow-up self-evaluation. This case study draws on both self-assessments to share what NIDP learned — and what changed between them.
