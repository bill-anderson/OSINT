Nairobi, 4 August 2025 — the Ministry of Health launched the **Biometric Health Identification (BHI)** system at Kenyatta University Teaching, Research and Referral Hospital (KUTRRH), part of the government's Digital Superhighway initiative under BETA, unveiled by Health CS Aden Duale. BHI is one of four components:

- **BHI:** patients access healthcare securely without physical documents; fingerprint verification reduces fraud and waiting times.
- **Practise360:** a geo-tagged, geo-fenced tool restricting pre-authorization codes to doctors within assigned facilities.
- **National Product Catalogue:** ensures only approved medicines are available.
- **Health Information Exchange (HIE):** real-time secure data exchange between hospitals, counties and national systems.

CS Duale noted 99% of KUTRRH walk-ins are registered under the **[[social-health-authority-kenya|Social Health Authority (SHA)]]**, with 29 biometric devices in use, and that biometric registration is operational in all Level 4-6 public facilities (Level 2-3 to follow); 24 counties are transitioning to Hospital Management Information Systems (HMIS), and a Track-and-Trace system for pharmaceuticals is in final testing.
