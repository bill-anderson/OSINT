On June 17, 2011, the National Assembly of Angola passed **Law 22/11 on Personal Data Protection** — omnibus privacy legislation applying to automated and non-automated processing by controllers based or operating in Angola. Highlights:

- Establishes a **Data Protection Agency** and sets principles: transparency, lawfulness, proportionality, purpose, accuracy and retention limits.
- The Agency must approve international transfers to countries without adequate protection; controllers notify the Agency of transfers to adequate countries.
- Processing requires the data subject's express consent and notification to the Agency; extra restrictions apply to sensitive data (including genetic), credit/solvency data, video surveillance and call recordings.
- Data subjects may restrict use for postal/electronic advertising; and have rights to access, object, rectify, update and delete.
- Willful or negligent non-compliance may incur penalties up to **$150,000**.
