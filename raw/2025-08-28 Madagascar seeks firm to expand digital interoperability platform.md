Madagascar's Digital Governance and Identification Management System Project (PRODIGY) is seeking a consulting firm to help scale up its national interoperability system. In a call for expression of interest published in August 2025 by the country's Digital Governance Unit (UGD), bidders had until 1 September to respond.

The contract aims to help the UGD operationalise the national interoperability platform, setting up a secure, trusted ecosystem accessible to public and private actors providing digital services to Malagasy nationals. The selected firm must have deployed at least two digital-government platforms using X-Road (or equivalent) in the past 15 years, plus experience in interoperability project management and distributed systems.

Funding is part of PRODIGY's World Bank IDA financing (quality-and-cost-based selection). The project was recently extended, with a new closing date of 30 June 2026. It has three objectives: a consolidated, interoperable identity management system; digital and mobile government services; and project management/implementation support.
