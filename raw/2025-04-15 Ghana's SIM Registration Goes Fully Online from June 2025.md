From June 2025, SIM card re-registration in Ghana goes fully online — citizens can complete the process online with no visits to physical centres. The announcement was made on 8 April by Minister for Communication Samuel Nartey George during a visit to Margins ID Group (Intelligent Card Production Systems, ICPS) in Accra. Citizens will register SIMs using their Ghana Card via a fully online platform, with automatic verification replacing in-person manual checks.

Ghana's SIM re-registration campaign began in 2021, requiring all SIM holders to re-register using the Ghana Card. It faced delays from low registration rates, long queues, technical issues and limited centre access. Per an 11 November 2022 Ministry update (then-Minister Ursula Owusu-Ekuful), 30,011,082 individuals had completed phase-one registration (linking Ghana Card to SIM), of which 20,892,970 completed full biometric verification (~70%). Millions remained unregistered. Moving fully online aims to close the gap, reduce administrative burden, improve data accuracy, and curb fraudulent SIM use.
