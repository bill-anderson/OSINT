# Sonatel Expands Gandoul Teleport with Eutelsat OneWeb Satellite Gateway

Tech Africa News - 10 July 2026, by Jennifer Onyeagoro

As part of its strategy to develop digital infrastructure and its commitment to the New Deal for Technology, Sonatel is strengthening its Gandoul teleport with the deployment of Eutelsat OneWeb's Satellite Portal Network (SNP). The infrastructure includes 16 new latest-generation satellite antennas on approximately 5 hectares, plus a Tier III technical room. It connects Eutelsat OneWeb's LEO satellite constellation to terrestrial networks, delivering high-speed Internet to Senegal, the sub-region and beyond.

CEO Brelotte Ba said the project strengthens the country's digital sovereignty, secures and diversifies international connectivity routes, improves network resilience against technical and geopolitical hazards, and accelerates digital inclusion. The Gandoul teleport - inaugurated in 1972 in the Thies region - is interconnected to Sonatel's submarine cables and terrestrial networks, offering a hybrid satellite-and-fibre infrastructure and positioning Senegal as a regional hub for satellite and digital connectivity in West Africa.
