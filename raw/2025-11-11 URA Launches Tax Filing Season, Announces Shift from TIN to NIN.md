The Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) has launched its annual tax return filing season with a major announcement marking a new chapter in Uganda's tax administration: the transition from the Tax Identification Number (TIN) to the National Identification Number (NIN).

In a statement shared on its official social media under the #LearnTaxUg campaign, URA revealed that "soon and very soon your NIN will be your TIN." The new system will allow Ugandans to use a single identification number to conduct all payments, registrations and license applications. "This change will enable you to conduct all payments, licenses and registrations using one channel," URA stated.

One digital identity for all services: The shift, under the Tax Procedures Amendment Bill, is aimed at digitally unifying government services, improving efficiency and reducing tax evasion. Citizens' NINs, issued by NIRA, will serve as their official tax identity. Businesses registered under the Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB) will similarly use their URSB registration numbers as TINs. Officials say the reform will streamline compliance, eliminate duplicate records and ease obligations without multiple registration processes.

Annual tax filing season opens: The announcement coincides with the opening of URA's annual tax return filing season, which began on Tuesday. The authority urged taxpayers to submit returns on time.

New incentives for startups: URA confirmed a three-year income tax exemption for qualifying startups registered after 1 July 2025 with capital not exceeding UGX 500 million, part of the latest tax amendments to promote innovation.

Towards a seamless digital economy: The integration of the NIN with tax systems is part of Uganda's broader digital transformation agenda to consolidate government services under a single, interoperable digital identity framework, aligning with Vision 2040's goal of a fully digital economy.
