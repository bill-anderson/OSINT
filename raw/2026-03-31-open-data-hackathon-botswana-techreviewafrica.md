# Open Data Hackathon kicks off in Botswana

Tech Review Africa — Date: 2026-03-31 — By: Nana Appiah Acquaye

The Open Data Hackathon has officially begun this week, bringing together innovators, developers, and data enthusiasts for three days of collaborative problem-solving. Organized by the Botswana Digital & Innovation Hub, the event aims to generate data-driven solutions addressing pressing challenges in health, agriculture, and tourism.

The first day saw participants forming teams, brainstorming ideas, and preparing to develop applications and tools that leverage open data for real-world impact. Organizers highlighted the hackathon as a platform for fostering creativity, promoting digital skills, and encouraging practical applications of data in critical sectors.

With the energy high and participants fully engaged, the event sets the stage for innovative solutions that could support sustainable development and economic growth across Botswana.
