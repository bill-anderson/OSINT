# Algeria Opens Dual Data Center Infrastructure to Support Digital Government

By Adoni Conrad Quenum — We Are Tech, 8 July 2026 (originally published in French, Agence Ecofin)

- Algeria has inaugurated a National Digital Services Center built around two active-active data centers to ensure uninterrupted government digital services.
- The infrastructure will host all public administration data within Algeria and support the digitalization of 40 public services through a national digital identity.
- The project forms part of Algeria's broader digital sovereignty strategy as Africa continues to account for only 0.6% of global data center capacity.

Algerian President Abdelmadjid Tebboune inaugurated the National Digital Services Center on Sunday, July 5, in Mohammadia, Algiers Province, as the government seeks to strengthen digital sovereignty, improve the resilience of public digital services and keep sensitive government data within the country.

The inauguration took place during celebrations marking the 64th anniversary of Algeria's Independence Day in the presence of Digitalization and Statistics Minister Meriem Benmouloud. The new center operates through two data centers located in Algiers and Blida using an active-active architecture, allowing both facilities to process requests simultaneously in real time. As a result, either site can immediately maintain operations if the other experiences a disruption.

The infrastructure delivers 99.98% service availability, limiting annual downtime to less than two hours. It also provides real-time data synchronization and proactively detects system anomalies before they affect service quality. The infrastructure now hosts all data generated by Algeria's public administrations on national territory, reducing the government's reliance on foreign digital infrastructure.

In addition, the platform enables interoperability among government ministries through a unified national portal. The government also plans to digitize 40 public services, which citizens will ultimately access through a national digital identity system. Chinese technology company Huawei built the center.

The project aligns with a broader push across Africa to strengthen digital sovereignty. According to the African Data Centre Association's (ADCA) Data Centres in Africa 2026 report, Africa accounts for only 0.6% of global data center capacity despite representing 19% of the world's population. Most of the continent's installed capacity remains concentrated in South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya and Egypt.

Algeria ranked among the 10 best-equipped African countries for data centers in 2025. The country inaugurated its first data center in Constantine in February 2023 and commissioned a second facility dedicated to artificial intelligence in Oran in March 2025.
