# Editorial: The high cost of mobile calls and data in South Sudan

*One Citizen Daily editorial, 27 November 2025*

The newly appointed Minister of Information, Communication Technology and Postal Services must make it an urgent priority to tackle the shockingly high cost of mobile calls and data in South Sudan. Recent figures reveal that South Sudan's mobile data is among the most expensive in Africa, with one gigabyte (1 GB) costing about US$23.70. By contrast, in Kenya, Nigeria, and Ghana, 1 GB often costs less than US$1.

The editorial argues this is not just a technical issue but a barrier to development, inclusion, and opportunity: exorbitant telecom charges effectively shut millions out of the digital world. Students cannot afford online learning, small business owners are locked out of digital markets, and families struggle to maintain affordable communication.

It calls for the new minister's mandate to be citizen-centred, treating telecommunications as basic utilities rather than luxuries: reviewing the taxation and fee structures weighing down the sector, fostering genuine competition among service providers, and engaging regulators and consumers to establish fair pricing models.
