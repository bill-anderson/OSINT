UNDP Namibia's final instalment in its digital legal identity (DLI) series, on how MHAISS is building Namibia's digital identity system with UNDP support.

Background: in 2021 MHAISS partnered with UNDP for a baseline assessment that found obsolete software, siloed databases and little interoperability. In 2024 Namibia became the first country to pilot UNDP's Model Governance Framework for Digital Legal Identity. The assessment flagged three themes: interoperability and capacity gaps between legacy and new platforms; public-trust concerns absent a comprehensive data-protection law (the promulgated Civil Registration and Identification Act, 2024 consolidates births/deaths/marriages/identity records and embeds privacy principles); and inclusive design for rural populations and people with disabilities.

Namibia built its own National Population Register with support from the Office of the Prime Minister and tested remote biometric enrolment via hand-held devices. The government is finalising preparations to launch the e-ID in 2026, and is deploying NamX, a data-exchange platform for secure inter-ministry sharing, and Nampay, a real-time payment system for instantaneous transfers.
