Uganda's Constitutional Court has struck down several provisions of the Computer Misuse Act, ruling that Parliament passed them without the required quorum. The annulled sections had criminalised unauthorised access to data, interception of communications, and recording individuals without consent. A panel of five justices led by Justice Irene Mulyagonja found several provisions overly broad, vague and ambiguous, and inconsistent with the Constitution.

Specifically nullified: Section 11 (unauthorised access, interception, or voice/video recording of another person); Section 23 (unauthorised sharing/transmission of any information about a child); Section 26 (sharing information likely to ridicule, degrade or demean, or promote hostility based on tribe, religion or gender); Section 27 (sending/sharing unsolicited information unless in the public interest); Section 28 ("malicious information" about another person); and Section 29 (misuse of social media through prohibited information under a disguised or false identity).

The court also declared Sections 162 and 163 of the Penal Code Act (criminal libel) unconstitutional as vague and ambiguous. Two petitions challenging the provisions were filed by activists in 2022 and consolidated. The provisions had been widely used to regulate online behaviour and prosecute offenders.
