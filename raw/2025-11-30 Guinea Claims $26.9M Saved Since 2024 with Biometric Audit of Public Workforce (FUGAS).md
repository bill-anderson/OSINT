Guinea has saved more than 233 billion Guinean francs ($26.9 million) through a unified administrative and payroll management system (FUGAS) implemented in July 2024, officials said. Of the 277,000 contract staff and civil servants registered, only about 130,000 were confirmed after biometric checks. Results were presented in Conakry at the system's official launch by Prime Minister Amadou Oury Bah.

Managed by the Ministry of Labour, FUGAS verifies employees' identities, secures salary payments, tracks career progression, and eliminates duplicates, irregular ID numbers and non-existent posts. It cost about 60 billion Guinean francs to implement. PM Bah said the system "must also integrate a unique personal identification number, whether you are a civil servant or not," enabling comprehensive national management. FUGAS is part of the Simandou 2040 strategy and a broader public-service reform, complementing the Telemo public-procurement platform, the national Data Center, and the Guinea Telecoms connectivity project. Public Service Minister Faya François Bourounou said nearly 20,000 public employees will change grade/category/hierarchy.
