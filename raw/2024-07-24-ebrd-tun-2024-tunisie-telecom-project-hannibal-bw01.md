# EBRD — Tunisie Telecom (Project Hannibal), 2024

The EBRD Board approved a development-linked loan of up to EUR 190m to Tunisie Telecom, with a first committed tranche of EUR 50m, to finance fibre, mobile-network upgrades and a Medusa submarine-cable connection.

## Description

EBRD Board report (approved 24 July 2024) for Project Hannibal — "a development-linked loan to the Company in the amount of up to EUR 190 million, in four tranches, including a first committed tranche of EUR 50 million." The EBRD Loan is supported by a first-loss EU EFSD+ Digital Transformation guarantee and complemented by an EUR 11 million EU grant. It finances Tunisie Telecom's capital expenditure — connecting households to fibre, upgrading the fibre backbone, transitioning 3G to 4G and 4G+/5G, and participation in the pan-European MEDUSA submarine cable. Tunisie Telecom is 65% state-owned, 35% owned by Emirates International Telecommunications; the operation is the Bank's first Tunisian public-sector project without a sovereign guarantee.
