Nigeria's CBN and NCC signed an agreement giving banks real-time access to telecom data via the Telecom Identity Risk Management System (TIRMS), allowing financial institutions to check whether a mobile number linked to a transaction has been recently swapped, recycled, flagged for suspicious activity, or gone inactive, before a payment clears. It addresses the identity layer in Nigeria's financial fraud problem — fraud fell 51% to ₦25.85 billion ($18.7 million) in 2025 (NIBSS), with many incidents involving SIM swaps and compromised numbers. NCC EVC Aminu Maida: "Mobile numbers increasingly underpin identity, authentication, and financial access."
