Key findings:
- About two-thirds (65%) of Angolans live in zones served by the national electric grid (up from 61% in 2019). But only **14% of rural residents** are within reach of the grid, vs **92% of urban** residents. Better-educated and better-off citizens are far more likely to be in grid-served zones.
- 59% live in households connected to the grid; of those, 78% say electricity works most or all of the time.
- Combining connection and reliability, fewer than half (**47%**) of Angolans enjoy a reliable supply — including just **6% of rural residents** and 28% of citizens in high lived poverty.
- Two-thirds (66%) say the government is doing a poor job on reliable electricity (83% among rural residents).

The government's Power Sector Action Plan targets a 60% electrification rate by 2025 (from 47% in 2020), requiring major rural advances where electrification is estimated at less than 10%.
