# Mozambique Approves IP Strategy to Study AI Patentability and Digital Rights • 360 Mozambique

**The Mozambican government has approved the Intellectual Property Policy and Strategy (PEPI) 2026-2035, which provides for a study on the patentability of artificial intelligence-related inventions and a revision of the country's legal framework to address the challenges of the digital environment. The document was approved through Council of Ministers Resolution No. 53/2026, dated 30 June.**

The new strategy succeeds the Intellectual Property Strategy 2008-2018 and introduces, for the first time, a dedicated national policy for the sector. Among its principal objectives is the modernisation of intellectual property (IP) rights administration through information and communication technologies (ICT), including AI, to align the national system with evolving technological demands.

In that context, the document sets as a target to 'modernise the administration of IP rights through the use of ICT, including AI', as well as to 'digitalise all IP rights administration procedures'.

Beyond the digitalisation of services, the strategy calls for the development of mechanisms to adapt the legal framework to the challenges posed by the digital environment and AI. To that end, it provides for the conduct of 'a study on the impact of the digital environment and the use of AI on the IP legal framework'.

According to the document, the study is to examine 'the possible forms of protection for inventions related' to AI, 'the patentability of AI inventions', and 'the level of inventive step and disclosure required', subsequently enabling the adoption of 'measures deemed appropriate to address the challenges posed' by the technology.

The strategy also envisages a revision of the legal framework to ensure 'better protection and economic exploitation of intellectual works in the digital environment', at a time of growing use of digital content and services. To achieve this objective, the legal and institutional modernisation component represents the largest planned investment under the strategy, at 719.6 million meticais (approximately 10.1 million euros), of which 632 million meticais (approximately 8.9 million euros) will be directed towards strengthening the IP institutional framework.

In addition to legislative revision, the strategy includes measures such as the mobilisation of resources for equipment and digital platforms — including cloud-based systems — incentives for the adoption of new ICT in rights administration, and the creation of support services for citizens and businesses for the registration and economic exploitation of IP rights.

On the institutional front, the strategy provides for the establishment of a National Intellectual Property Commission 'for the definition and harmonisation of legislation and policies on IP', as well as the creation of coordination mechanisms and the strengthening of collaboration among all sectoral and cross-sectoral institutions 'for the harmonious development of the IP system in Mozambique'.

Implementation of the PEPI will be coordinated by the National Intellectual Property Committee, chaired by Prime Minister Benvinda Levi, and comprising the ministers responsible for the Economy, Education and Culture, Science and Technology, Health, and Agriculture.

On the protection of rights, the document identifies as priorities the creation of 'coordination mechanisms among institutions responsible for overseeing IP rights', the development of 'mechanisms to counter social tolerance of IP rights infringement', and the development of 'effective mechanisms to combat IP rights violations in the digital environment'.

To support these objectives, the strategy provides for public awareness campaigns on the economic, health, and safety harms of counterfeiting and piracy, as well as enhanced cooperation among the bodies responsible for enforcement and oversight. The implementation plan will run from 2026 to 2035, with an initial review scheduled for 2028, a mid-term revision in 2030, and a second evaluation in 2033.
