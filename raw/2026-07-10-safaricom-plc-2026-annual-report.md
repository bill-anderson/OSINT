## INTRODUCTION

Safaricom Annual Report

This report marks 25 years of Safaricom's service and impact, reflecting on the journey to transform the lives of our customers, partners, employees and communities. Building on this legacy, we continued connecting people and businesses, enabling opportunity, and making everyday moments easier. We invite you to read this report to gain further insight into Safaricom's investments, innovations, outcomes, performance and prospects.

Our integrated reporting boundary considers risks, opportunities and outcomes for all stakeholders across our value chain

ABOUT THIS REPORT Welcome to our Annual Report, which documents our exciting and transformative journey as Safaricom PLC Group (the Group). We exit FY26 as a strong, resilient and ambitious group, deliberately accelerating our transition into becoming Africa's leading, purpose-led technology company by 2030, anchored by a robust Kenya core business and a stabilising Ethiopia growth engine.

Reporting purpose, scope and boundary

This Annual Report has been prepared to provide a concise, balanced and decision-useful view of Safaricom's ability to create value over the short, medium and long term. It provides an assessment of our performance in relation to the Safaricom PLC Group strategy, business model, and operating context, while presenting stakeholders with a clear sense of our prospects and plans. The preparation of the report followed a structured internal process that included management input, targeted interviews, content submissions from business units and functions, internal reviews, and governance oversight. This process was intended to support the integrity, relevance and connectivity of the information presented across the report.

### MESSAGE FROM THE CHAIRMAN

Stewarding Safaricom through a defining year

As Safaricom marked 25 years of Transforming Lives in 2025, we reflect on the progress achieved over the past quarter-century and reaffirm the role Safaricom continues to play in enabling inclusive growth, strengthening digital participation, and supporting socioeconomic development across Kenya and Ethiopia. The Board acknowledges the leadership of the Safaricom management team and employees in guiding the business through a strong year of performance. Their collective focus supported continued commercial momentum across the business, with service revenue surpassing KShs 414 billion and net income, excluding minority interest, reaching close to KShs 100 billion. We also continued to expand our scale and reach, growing our customer base to more than 72 million customers across Kenya and Ethiopia. Safaricom's journey to this point has been a truly remarkable story of growth and resilience, reflecting the strength of our strategy and the dedication of our people. Kenya delivered a third consecutive year of double-digit growth, while Ethiopia continued building momentum towards Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation and Amortisation (EBITDA) breakeven in FY27. We have sustained strong growth in service revenue driven primarily by M-PESA and mobile data, while maintaining profitability despite the continued start-up losses from our investment in Ethiopia, where we are beginning to see the benefits of scale with narrowing losses. This balance of growth and investment discipline is exactly what the Board expects at this stage of our journey. The achievements recorded during the year under review support Safaricom's continued ability to create value for shareholders. We recorded an interim dividend of KShs 0.85 per ordinary share (FY25: KShs 0.55), amounting to KShs 34.06 billion, which was declared and paid.

The proposed final dividend of KShs 1.15 per ordinary share (FY25: KShs 0.65), amounting to KShs 46.08 billion, will be submitted for approval at the Annual General Meeting (AGM) on 31 July 2026. This brings the total dividend for FY26 to KShs 2.00 per ordinary share (FY25: KShs 1.20), amounting to KShs 80.13 billion. These are not just metrics; they represent the trust that we have earned, the resilience demonstrated, and the lives that we have transformed. Read more about our financial performance in the CFIO report from page 85.

Safaricom's role in society

Safaricom plays an important role in Kenya's digital and financial ecosystem through connectivity, financial inclusion and digital public infrastructure. The outcome of the last True Value assessment also indicated that Safaricom contributed 5% of Kenya's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and supported over 1.3 million jobs across its broader ecosystem. Alongside this contribution, we are strengthening our core services. In addition, we are supporting the development of long-term digital growth platforms, including digital identity solutions, wallet-as-a service capabilities, digital public infrastructure and sector-specific digital solutions across health, education and agriculture. Safaricom Ethiopia is playing an increasingly transformative societal role by actively driving digital inclusion and economic empowerment. Since entering this market, we are deepening access to reliable 4G connectivity, effectively narrowing the digital divide between urban centres and underserved rural communities. Through M-PESA, we have been able to provide integrated financial solutions to millions of previously unbanked citizens, fostering local entrepreneurship. Beyond providing connectivity, Safaricom Ethiopia plays an important role as an engine for economic participation and job creation, supporting thousands of livelihoods across its growing ecosystem of local distributors, agents and technology partners.

From the elderly person in Kakamega County receiving social support through M-PESA to communities in Wajir County accessing 4G connectivity for the very first time to young Kenyans benefiting from our Citizens of the Future education programme, this is the true measure of Safaricom's impact, and this is something that the Board is immensely proud of.

Transforming Lives through the Foundations

Through the Safaricom and M-PESA Foundations, we continue to support impactful programmes in education, healthcare, environmental conservation, economic empowerment, livelihoods and humanitarian response, bringing our purpose to life beyond commercial operations. Over the past five years, the Foundations achieved the following impact: о Education: KShs 17.41 billion invested, reaching 2.61 million beneficiaries о Health: KShs 3.14 billion invested, reaching 11.85 million beneficiaries о Environment, conservation, water and livelihoods: KShs 1.46 billion invested, reaching 1.92 million beneficiaries о Humanitarian response and other programmes: KShs 820.19 million invested, reaching 284,700 beneficiaries Most importantly, more than 4.4 million lives were directly impacted through Foundation initiatives in FY26.

Shareholding structure update On 30 June 2026, Vodafone Kenya Limited completed the acquisition of an additional 15% shareholding in Safaricom PLC from the Government of Kenya, increasing its shareholding to 55%. Concurrently, the internal reorganisation of Vodafone Kenya was completed, resulting in Vodacom Group Limited becoming the majority shareholder of Vodafone Kenya Limited. Following completion of the transaction, the government of Kenya retains a 20% shareholding while public investors continue to hold 25% of the company's issued share capital. Safaricom remains listed on the Nairobi Securities Exchange and continues to operate as a Kenyan-incorporated and regulated company, subject to all applicable laws, regulations and licensing requirements. The completion of the Vodacom transaction marks an important milestone for Safaricom and further strengthens our strategic relationship within the Vodacom Group. The transaction enhances our ability to leverage broader scale, deeper expertise and regional capabilities, supporting the execution of our long-term growth strategy and strengthening our position as a leading technology company in the region.

Governance and Board stewardship Safaricom continues to benefit from a stable, diverse and highly committed Board, supported by well-functioning Committees that enable effective oversight and delivery against the Board Charter. While there were no changes to the composition of the Board during the year, the Board Committees were reconstituted with effect from 1 August 2025 to ensure an optimal balance of skills, experience and diversity across the governance structure. FY26 represented the first year of Safaricom's new five-year strategic cycle under our Vision 2030 Strategy. Governance oversight focused on disciplined execution of strategy, balanced growth in Kenya, sustainable scaling in Ethiopia, and embedding purpose across the business to support long-term value creation. Turning to Ethiopia, we continue to make steady and meaningful progress. Regulatory developments and especially the management of interconnection and infrastructure sharing by the Ethiopian Communications Authority are improving transparency within the market. The pricing rationalisation implemented in December 2025 was an important step towards aligning pricing with the cost of service delivery and supporting continued investment in network expansion. With network coverage now exceeding 50% alongside continued customer growth, Ethiopia is becoming an increasingly important part of the Group. During the year, our focus was on scaling the business responsibly while managing risk as we progress towards our target of achieving EBITDA break-even in FY27. We continue to focus on building a sustainable business and establishing a strong foundation for long-term value creation. The Board was also focused on ensuring that purpose is embedded across the business to ensure long-term value.

Advancing Safaricom's TechCo ambition

We continue to operate in a complex and evolving global environment characterised by geopolitical uncertainty, inflationary pressures, currency volatility and changing consumer dynamics, all of which continue to impact on the cost of doing business and customer affordability. In this context, the Board has been particularly encouraged by management's response. The focus was clear – protect customer value and affordability, improve precision and pricing segmentation, and continue innovating in products and digital services. At the same time, competition, particularly in fintech and digital services, continues to intensify. Our approach remains deliberate and disciplined: protect the core, innovate at the edge, and consistently deliver value to our customers. Progress continued in strengthening Safaricom's transition from a traditional telecommunications operator into a purpose-led technology company. The first year of the new five-year strategic cycle focused on expanding beyond core GSM and mobile financial services into platform-led technology solutions built on connectivity and financial services ecosystems. Future priorities include scaling fintech platforms, Internet of Things (IoT), AI-enabled services and digital ecosystems while maintaining leadership in connectivity and financial services.

Licensing and long-term resilience

FY26 was particularly significant for Safaricom as we secured renewal of our Operating and Spectrum licences from the Communications Authority of Kenya for the next 25 years. The renewal provides regulatory certainty and supports the execution of our long-term strategy. The Board acknowledges the constructive role of the regulator and the efforts of Safaricom's teams in achieving this important outcome.
