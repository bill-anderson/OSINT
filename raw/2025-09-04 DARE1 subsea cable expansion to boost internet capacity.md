# DARE1 subsea cable expansion to boost internet capacity from Kenya to South Africa

> ## Excerpt
> Djibouti Telecom plans to extend its DARE1 submarine cable (operational since
> 2021, 36 Tbps) ~3,200-3,500 km south to Mtunzini, South Africa. The existing
> system links Djibouti City with Bosaso and Mogadishu, then Mombasa; partners
> include Hormuud Telecom (Somalia), Somtel International and Telkom Kenya.

---

Djibouti Telecom is advancing regional connectivity with plans to extend its DARE1 submarine cable along Africa's east coast. The expansion is designed to increase capacity, provide alternative routes, and support enterprises, cloud services, and telecom operators across the continent.

The existing DARE1 system, operational since 2021, links Djibouti City with Bosaso and Mogadishu in Somalia, before reaching Mombasa, Kenya, offering 36 Tbps of bandwidth through three fiber pairs. Partners include Hormuud Telecom Somalia, Somtel International, and Telkom Kenya, reflecting a collaborative regional effort.

The upcoming extension will stretch approximately 3,200-3,500 km southward, reaching Mtunzini in South Africa and making strategic landings in Tanzania (Dar es Salaam and Mtwara), Mozambique (Nacala, Beira, and Maputo), and Madagascar (Mahajanga and Toliary). All new segments will connect at the Mombasa trunk station. Beyond adding capacity, the expansion aims to improve network resilience and reduce latency for carriers and cloud platforms across East and Southern Africa.
