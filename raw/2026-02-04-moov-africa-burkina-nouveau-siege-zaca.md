# Moov Africa Burkina Breaks Ground on State-of-the-Art Headquarters to Drive Digital Innovation

February 4, 2026

2 min read

Author: Joyce Onyeagoro

The new Moov Africa Burkina headquarters is expected to strengthen the company's position as a leading telecommunications operator in Burkina Faso while providing a modern and sustainable operational base.

Moov Africa Burkina has officially started construction of its new headquarters at the Commercial and Administrative Activities Zone (ZACA), with the foundation stone laid on February 3, 2026. The ceremony was presided over by Dr. Aminata Zerbo/Sabane, Minister of Digital Transition, Postal and Electronic Communications, representing the Prime Minister.

The building will occupy a 7,000 m² plot and rise to five floors (R+5), extendable to seven (R+7), with a total investment of 9 billion CFA francs. The project aligns with national directives encouraging large companies to establish headquarters that reflect their stability and commitment to the country.

The construction marks a major milestone in Moov Africa Burkina's development, establishing a modern hub for operations and innovation. The headquarters will support the company's transformation and its efforts to adapt to an increasingly digital environment.

The government emphasized its support for the project, noting that it demonstrates Burkina Faso's commitment to modernity, digital sovereignty, and sustainable development. Moov Africa, along with other electronic communication operators, has actively contributed to government initiatives, including the 12 major construction sites and expansion of connectivity in underserved areas.

All companies are being encouraged to comply with the law mandating the establishment of local headquarters through registration with ONATEL SA, supporting broader social and economic development.

The new Moov Africa Burkina headquarters is expected to strengthen the company's position as a leading telecommunications operator in Burkina Faso while providing a modern and sustainable operational base.
