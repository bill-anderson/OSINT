## Advancing responsible AI within Nigeria’s digital public infrastructure 

 ![](https://dial.global/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Blue-header-2025.png)

 ![](https://dial.global/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Blue-header-2025.png)

Nigeria is undergoing rapid digital transformation, driven by increasing internet penetration, widespread mobile connectivity, and the growth of fintech innovation. As of January 2025, the country had over 136 million internet users, ranking seventh globally, alongside more than 224 million mobile subscriptions. This growth reflects not only expanding access, but also rising smartphone adoption, multiple device ownership, and the proliferation of Internet of Things (IoT) technologies, positioning Nigeria as a key player in Africa’s digital evolution. 

As part of its efforts to build a thriving digital ecosystem, the Federal Government of Nigeria has been [advancing digital public infrastructure (DPI)](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1MWUm1SvRXJka3oDK8bQBJBWCzg_uughu/view) to strengthen identity systems, improve service delivery, and enable more data-driven policymaking. At the same time, the rapid emergence of AI technologies introduced both opportunity and complexity, raising important questions around responsible use, data protection, interoperability, and trust. 

Building on this momentum, we partnered with Co-Creation Hub (CcHUB) and Data Science Nigeria (DSN) to explore how artificial intelligence (AI) could be responsibly integrated into digital public infrastructure DPI. This project focused on how AI could be applied in ways that are practical, locally relevant, and aligned with strong governance principles. It created space to explore not just what is possible, but what is appropriate and sustainable within Nigeria’s context. 

## We brought together innovators, policymakers, and ecosystem actors to explore real-world applications of AI within DPI.

## We brought together innovators, policymakers, and ecosystem actors to explore real-world applications of AI within DPI.

At its core, the project focused on bridging the gap between emerging technologies and DPI by fostering collaboration across sectors. Working closely with partners, the project: 

-   Supported a cohort of innovators developing AI-enabled solutions across sectors such as health, agriculture, and financial inclusion 

-   Facilitated engagement between government institutions, private sector actors, and technology communities 

-   Enabled testing and refinement of solutions within real digital public infrastructure contexts 

-   Created spaces for dialogue on responsible AI, data governance, interoperability, and trust 

-   Strengthened connections across the ecosystem to support longer-term collaboration and scale 

This approach ensured that innovation was not only technically viable, but also aligned with public needs, policy considerations, and national priorities. 

## Our collaborative efforts demonstrated how locally-driven innovation can strengthen DPI.

## Our collaborative efforts demonstrated how locally-driven innovation can strengthen DPI.

The project delivered tangible progress across ecosystem building, innovation, and knowledge generation. Key outcomes included: 

-   Strengthening a growing ecosystem of AI and DPI practitioners in Nigeria 

-   Demonstrating practical, locally developed use cases for integrating AI into public systems 

-   Building cross-sector partnerships that continue beyond the life of the project 

-   Contributing insights to national and regional conversations on responsible AI adoption 

These highlighted the value of locally-grounded innovation, showing that context-aware solutions are critical to building effective, inclusive, and trusted digital systems. 

Through our efforts, we produced several resources designed to sharing insights, learnings and recommendations that surfaced during the project. These include: 

-   **White paper:** [_Privacy by Design in Early-Stage Innovation_](https://ndpc.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/NDPC_DIAL-White-Paper-Privacy-by-Design-in-Early-Stage-Innovation-.pdf), developed in collaboration with the Nigeria Data Protection Commission, exploring how to embed privacy, trust, and responsible data practices into AI-enabled systems 

-   **Impact videos:** Stories from three innovators showcasing how AI was applied within DPI contexts to address real-world challenges across sectors such as financial inclusion, agriculture, and public service delivery 

These resources provide both technical and human-centered insights, supporting policymakers, innovators, and ecosystem actors working at the intersection of AI and digital public infrastructure. 

## The learnings garnered will continue to shape future work on AI and DPI across Africa.

## The learnings garnered will continue to shape future work on AI and DPI across Africa.

While the project has concluded, its influence continues through the partnerships built, the insights generated, and the pathways it helped define. 

Key areas of continued impact include: 

-   Informing approaches to responsible AI integration within DPI systems across Africa 

-   Strengthening regional conversations on data governance, interoperability, and trust 

-   Providing a foundation for future collaborations between governments, innovators, and development partners 

This work reinforces the importance of combining innovation with governance, and experimentation with collaboration, as countries continue to navigate the opportunities and challenges at the intersection of AI and DPI.
