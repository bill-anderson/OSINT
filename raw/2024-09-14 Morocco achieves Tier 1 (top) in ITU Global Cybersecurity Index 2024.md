Morocco was classified in **Tier 1** (the highest, "role-modelling") of the ITU's 2024 **Global Cybersecurity Index (GCI)**, scoring **97.5 out of 100** across the five pillars: Legal Measures 20/20, Technical 18.12/20, Organizational 20/20, Capacity Development 19.38/20, and Cooperation 20/20.

The ITU noted 46 countries achieved Tier 1 in this edition (up from ~30 in 2021); other Tier 1 countries include Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Oman, Qatar, the UAE, Australia, Estonia, Singapore, the UK and the US. The global average country score rose to 65.7 (a 27% increase since 2020). Tier 1 nations typically have robust legal frameworks, national CIRTs, national cybersecurity strategies, capacity-development initiatives and active international cooperation. (Contrast Libya, which reached the third tier in the same edition.)
