Wingu Africa has launched the Wingu Cloud Exchange (WCX) in Tanzania, aiming to democratise access to cloud technology and provide local organisations with a secure, compliant, and cost-effective alternative to offshore services. In an interview with Daily News, Chief Strategy Officer and Co-founder Nicholas Lodge outlined how the platform is set to lower barriers to cloud adoption.

WCX is a locally hosted private cloud platform designed to give Tanzanian organisations a secure and compliant alternative to offshore services, including tools like Wingu Compute, Kubernetes, and Wingu Security. "By keeping data within regional borders, we provide better performance and lower latency, while our local-currency pricing model removes the unpredictability of fluctuating exchange rates," Lodge said.

On data sovereignty, Lodge said hosting infrastructure within Tanzania ensures organisations can keep data local and fully comply with laws like the Personal Data Protection Act, providing legal certainty as data remains under Tanzanian jurisdiction. He described the carrier-neutral model as breaking single-ISP monopolies, driving down bandwidth costs, and enabling local peering. WCX works alongside global providers such as AWS and Microsoft Azure via a hybrid cloud model, keeping sensitive data local while leveraging global platforms for other workloads.
