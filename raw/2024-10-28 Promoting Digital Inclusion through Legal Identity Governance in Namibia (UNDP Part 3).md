UNDP Namibia (Yrika Maritz, Head of Experimentation) Part 3 blog on the governance assessment of Namibia's digital legal identity using UNDP's Model Governance Framework, with MHAISS as custodian of the national population register.

The assessment methodology used focus-group interviews over four days across eight areas: accountability and rule of law; legal and regulatory framework; capable institutions; data protection and privacy; user value; procurement and anti-corruption; participation and access to information; and equality and non-discrimination.

Institutions consulted included MHAISS (lead), the Office of the Prime Minister (ICT systems architecture), MICT, the Ministry of Finance and Public Enterprises, the health and justice ministries and judiciary, Parliament, the Communications Regulatory Authority (CRAN), and civil society (IPPR and the Internet Society of Namibia). The piece notes Namibia is poised to advance in digital KYC, e-healthcare identifiers, e-learning credentials and online voting, but needs robust legal frameworks to safeguard personal data.
