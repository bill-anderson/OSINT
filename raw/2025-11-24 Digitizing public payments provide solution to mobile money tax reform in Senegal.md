# Digitizing public payments provide solution to mobile money tax reform in Senegal

Senegal proposed a **0.5% levy on mobile-money transfers** (plus steeper merchant-payment charges) to raise
~**CFA220bn over three years** under its 2025–2028 Economic and Social Recovery Plan. Critics (consumer
associations, fintechs, the National Union of Consumers UNCS, some ruling-coalition lawmakers) warn it could
trigger a cash comeback and erode financial inclusion: **>90% of adults use mobile wallets** (vs only 26%
with bank accounts) and platforms processed **CFA15.3 trillion in 2025**. Proposed alternatives: tax operator
revenue (~2.5%) instead of users, levy only withdrawals, digitise more fee-free public payments, or apply
progressive/high-value taxation. (#FeaturedPost commentary; source the underlying fiscal plan directly.)
