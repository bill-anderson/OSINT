Afrik Times (verify against official communique). On 1 June 2026 in Bamako, Burkina Faso's Security Minister Mahamadou Sana presided over the closing of a pilot phase of biometric enrolment for Burkinabe citizens residing in Mali, using mobile kits for near-instant production of the AES Biometric Identity Card (CIB-AES) and the AES Passport. Biometric data collected was transmitted in near-real-time to central production platforms in Burkina Faso. Framed as bringing administration closer to the diaspora via mobile kits in embassies/consulates, and a model for future international enrolment; the system will ultimately also produce unified AES consular cards. Directly extends the CIB-AES rollout (first issued in BFA from Dec 2025) into cross-border diaspora enrolment hosted on Malian soil, a concrete AES identity-integration step. NOTE on WURI parallel: unlike BFA and NER (WURI Phase 2 countries), Mali was never a WURI participant, so the AES ID is Mali's primary foundational-ID track, not a WURI replacement.
