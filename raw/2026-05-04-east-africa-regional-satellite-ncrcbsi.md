Kenya, South Sudan, Uganda and Rwanda have agreed to move forward with plans to deploy a regional satellite to enhance connectivity across East Africa's northern corridor. Kenya's Cabinet Secretary for Information, Communications and the Digital Economy, William Kabogo Gitau, said via LinkedIn that he met government representatives from the three other countries on the sidelines of the Connected Africa Summit 2026 in Nairobi, including Uganda's Minister of ICT and National Guidance Chris Baryomunsi, South Sudan's Minister of ICT and Postal Services Ateny Wek Ateny, and a Rwandan ICT ministry representative.

The meeting focused on advancing the Northern Corridor Regional Communication and Broadcasting Satellite Initiative (NCRCBSI), which aims to bring connectivity to East Africa and reduce reliance on external satellite systems and international connectivity routes by investing in infrastructure supporting regional priorities. As a first step, the countries will undertake a comprehensive feasibility study of the technical, financial and legal requirements, supported through shared funding by the four partner states.
