East African countries to launch regional satellite

Kenya, South Sudan, Uganda and Rwanda have agreed to deploy a regional satellite aimed at improving connectivity along East Africa's northern corridor.

Matshepo Sehloho,Associate Editor,Connecting Africa

2 Min ReadGovernment representatives from Kenya, South Sudan, Uganda and Rwanda met on the sidelines of the Connected Africa Summit 2026 in Nairobi, Kenya. (Source: Kenya's Ministry of Information, Communications and the Digital Economy)

Kenya, South Sudan, Uganda and Rwanda have agreed to move forward with plans to deploy a regional satellite to enhance connectivity across East Africa's northern corridor.

Kenya's Cabinet Secretary for the Ministry of Information, Communications and the Digital Economy, William Kabogo Gitau, said via a LinkedIn post that he met with government representatives from the other three countries on the sidelines of the Connected Africa Summit 2026, which took place in Nairobi, Kenya, late last week.

He met with Uganda's Minister of Information, Communications Technology and National Guidance, Chris Baryomunsi; South Sudan's Minister of ICT and Postal Services, Ateny Wek Ateny; and a representative from Rwanda's Ministry of ICT.

The meeting focused on advancing the project known as the Northern Corridor Regional Communication and Broadcasting Satellite Initiative (NCRCBSI).

The initiative aims to not only bring connectivity to East Africa, but also to reduce reliance on external satellite systems and international connectivity routes by investing in infrastructure that supports regional priorities.

"We further resolved to work towards the design and launch of a jointly owned regional communication and broadcasting satellite to support digital services and broadcasting across our countries," Gitau explained.

He said that as the first step, the countries will undertake a comprehensive feasibility study to assess the technical, financial and legal requirements of the project, which will be supported through shared funding by the four partner states.

"Through this collaboration, we are strengthening regional cooperation and advancing the development of digital infrastructure to enhance connectivity, service delivery, and economic growth across our countries," Gitau continued.

The Four East African countries have resolved to work towards the design of a jointly owned regional communication and broadcasting satellite. (Source: Kenya's Ministry of Information, Communications and the Digital Economy)

East African countries' cross-border agreements

The commitment to deploy a regional satellite by the four East African countries, comes at a time when other entities in other countries are also signing agreements to connect underserved communities.

In February 2026, East African telcos Ethio Telecom, Djibouti Telecom and the Sudatel Group turned their cross-border optical fiber memorandum of understanding into a binding agreement.

That agreement aimed at strengthening regional connectivity and network resilience.

In July 2025, Tanzania Telecommunications Corporation (TTLC) and Kenya's Information and Communication Technology Authority (ICTA) launched and implemented a terrestrial fiber link connecting Dar es Salaam in Tanzania to Mombasa in Kenya, to enhance digital connectivity across the East African region.

In May 2025, digital connectivity solutions company Bayobab completed a fiber optic network linking Kenya's east coast city of Mombasa to Kampala in Uganda.
