# Regulating Privacy and Digital Identities in the Age of AI — the View from Egypt (Cairo Review)

An essay on the tension between AI-driven technologies and privacy/surveillance/identity-management frameworks, with special attention to Egypt (**Law 151/2020**, see [[egypt-pdpl]]) and the broader MENA context. It argues AI's dependence on vast personal data (biometric identifiers, location histories, behavioural data) raises acute privacy concerns — invoking "surveillance capitalism" and Cambridge Analytica — and that regulatory/ethical frameworks lag the pace of data collection.

The piece assesses how societies can capture AI's benefits without compromising rights to privacy and control over digital identities, framed through Egypt's legislative efforts. Analytical reference; cite for framing, not as primary evidence.
