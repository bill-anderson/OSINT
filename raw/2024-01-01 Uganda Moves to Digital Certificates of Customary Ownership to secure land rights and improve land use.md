Over the years, projects spearheaded by the Government of Uganda have registered customary land rights under the 1998 Land Act and the 2013 Uganda National Land Policy (NLP), with development-partner support. The climax of registration has been issuance of Certificates of Customary Ownership (CCOs) to Ugandans residing on approximately 80% of the land in Uganda held under customary tenure. The CCO provides documentary evidence of ownership under customary land tenure; the application procedure is outlined in the Land Act 1998.

Since 2014, the Global Land Tool Network (GLTN), within the Land, Housing and Shelter Section of UN-Habitat, has partnered with Uganda's Ministry of Lands, Housing and Urban Development (MLHUD) to support NLP implementation — strengthening customary land rights, improving women's access to land, developing and implementing the national gender strategy for NLP, and building partner capacity on pro-poor, fit-for-purpose and gender-responsive land tools. Through these efforts, over 7,684 parcels were registered for CCOs.

Previously, certificates were created manually, with beneficiary and parcel information entered using a typewriter and issued as hard copies — time-consuming, expensive and error-prone. This prompted development of a digital CCO template using the Social Tenure Domain Model (STDM), a GLTN land-information tool conforming to international land administration standards that provides a flexible way of recording people-to-land relationships.

The digital CCO template has been approved by MLHUD and gazetted by the Uganda Printing and Publishing Corporation (UPPC) in the Uganda Gazette (notice of 18 September 2020). It replicates the manual CCO with improved features: a unique QR code strengthening document security; a map of the parcel; provision for signatures of the Area Land Committee members and stamp of the Area Land Committee chairperson; and a watermark of the Uganda Coat of Arms. The digital CCO reduces cost and processing time since data is entered on a computer and the certificate printed with ease.
