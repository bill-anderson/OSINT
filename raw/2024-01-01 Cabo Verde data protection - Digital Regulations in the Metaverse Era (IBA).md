# Cabo Verde data protection — Digital Regulations in the Metaverse Era (IBA)

Legal reference chapter on Cabo Verde's data-protection framework. Cabo Verde has no metaverse-specific rules, but its existing framework applies: the **[[data-protection-law-cabo-verde|Data Protection Law (Law 133/V/2001)]]** is the general regime and **closely mirrors the EU GDPR** — covering data-subject rights, controller/processor duties, special categories, international transfers, security measures and DPIAs; it applies within Cabo Verde and extraterritorially to processing of data of individuals located in Cabo Verde.

The law establishes the **[[cnpd-cabo-verde|National Data Protection Commission (CNPD)]]** as the independent supervisory authority. **Sanctions:** administrative fines of **CVE 50,000–3,000,000**, plus cessation/blocking/deletion measures; criminal penalties (unauthorised access, copying, transfer, interconnection, or false information) up to **two years' imprisonment** or a fine up to 240 days. The Constitution enshrines protection of private life; no case law yet exists on data infringements in a metaverse context.
