# Major AI, DPI initiatives to support Africa single digital market unveiled

By Ayang Macdonald — Biometric Update, 18 November 2025

Pan-African digital transformation organization Smart Africa announced major partnerships and initiatives at the 2025 Transform Africa Summit in Conakry, Guinea. The deals involved countries from across the continent, African entities like Youth Connekt Africa and Ascend Digital Solutions, and multinationals including Visa, Orange, Microsoft and Google — all geared toward digital integration as Africa works toward a single digital market, focused on AI strategy, DPI, tech innovation, and talent/skills development. Host Guinea announced alignment with Smart Africa's continental agenda, committing to expand broadband and build sovereign data centers under the Simandou 2040 strategy.

## Digital transformation partnerships

Smart Africa unveiled **SANIA**, a platform to bring together investors, startups, accelerators, and institutions to scale innovation across borders. Smart Africa and member states also signed MoUs to boost human and institutional capacity through the Smart Africa Digital Academy (SADA). The **Smart Africa Digital Xchange (SADX)**, a new framework designed to enable cross-border digital identity verification for digital trade and services across Benin, Ghana and Rwanda, was also unveiled — building on the Smart Africa Trust Alliance (SATA) declaration signed by six countries two years prior.

## Africa AI Council is born

The Smart Africa Board unveiled the **Africa AI Council**, expected to play a leading role in AI governance and innovation on the continent, providing "strategic, actionable recommendations." The Board is chaired by Rwanda's President Paul Kagame. The Council includes seven African ICT ministers — among them Togo's Cina Lawson, Nigeria's Bosun Tijani, Kenya's William Kabogo Gitau and Rwanda's Paula Ingabire — and eight independent members from organizations including the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, Orange, Microsoft and Google. "With the establishment of the Africa AI Council, we take a decisive step toward shaping an AI-driven future rooted in African values," said Smart Africa DG and CEO Lacina Koné.
