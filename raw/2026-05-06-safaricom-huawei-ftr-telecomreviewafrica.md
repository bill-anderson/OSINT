# Safaricom Launches Kenya's First Fiber-to-the-Room Service with Huawei

Safaricom, in collaboration with Huawei, has launched Kenya's first fiber-to-the-room (FTTR) solution, marking a significant milestone in the country's home connectivity landscape and setting a new standard for digital living.

As households increasingly rely on multiple connected devices and bandwidth-intensive applications, conventional broadband systems are struggling to meet modern connectivity demands. Safaricom's FTTR solution addresses this challenge by extending fiber connectivity into every room, ensuring seamless, ultra-fast, low-latency internet coverage throughout the home.

Unlike traditional FTTH setups that depend on a single router for Wi-Fi distribution, FTTR transforms the entire household into a high-performance fiber network. Each room is equipped with an optical access point, delivering stable speeds, minimal latency, and uninterrupted coverage without dead zones or signal degradation.

"Today's homes demand more than just connectivity; they require an always-on digital ecosystem. Through our partnership with Huawei, we are delivering a truly immersive connected home experience that meets the evolving needs of modern households," said Peter Ndegwa, CEO of Safaricom PLC.

With this launch, Safaricom becomes the first provider in Kenya to deliver end-to-end, in-home fiber connectivity. The initiative combines Safaricom's fiber infrastructure and local market expertise with Huawei's globally deployed FTTR technologies, including advanced optical terminals, Wi-Fi 6/6+ capabilities, and AI-driven network management.
