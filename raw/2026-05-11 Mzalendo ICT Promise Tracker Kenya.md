## Countrywide Universal Broadband Availability

The Kenya Kwanza government promised to increase internet broadband connectivity across the country through the construction of 100,000 kilometers of national fiber optic connectivity network.

-   _Kenya has made [significant strides](https://africacheck.org/sites/default/files/media/documents/2025-03/Kenya-President%20Ruto-Midterm%20Scorecard.pdf)  toward universal broadband but full countrywide availability remains a work in progress. [Rural areas](https://streamlinefeed.co.ke/news/digital-infrastructure) still face gaps in internet access, affordability, and infrastruvture._
-   The National Treasury reports that total fiber optic cable in Kenya reached 80,633 km by 2025. Of this, approximately 30,454 km is public sector fiber, a significant increase from the 22,486 km available when the administration took office in 2022.
-   To accelerate the rollout, the ICT Authority has partnered with Kenya Power to utilize existing electricity transmission lines for last-mile connectivity. This approach aims to connect 53,000 public institutions, including schools and hospitals.
-   Implementation has faced scrutiny regarding the effectiveness of the KSh 34 billion already spent, with [Members of Parliament](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JTHwvyLU8ck) raising questions about maintenance costs and the relevance of older infrastructure.

## Digitization and Automation of Government Services

The Kenya Kwanza government promised to enhance government service delivery through digitization and automation of all government critical processes, and make available 80 per cent of government services online.

-   _The number of digitised government services has increased from [350 in 2022 to over 23,000 in February 2026](https://www.delivery.go.ke/pillars.php?pillar=ict). This accounts for approximately 85% of identified essential services that were targeted for onboarding._
-    _A fully automated external debt payment platform was scheduled to go live on February 2, 2026, replacing manual paper-based processes within the [National Treasury](https://www.treasury.go.ke/sites/default/files/Latest%20updates/2026%20Budget%20Policy%20Statement.pdf)._
-   _The [State Department for Economic Planning](https://www.planning.go.ke/wp-content/uploads/2024/03/DIGITISATION-AND-AUTOMATION-FOR-SDEP.docx) is implementing its Digitisation and Automation Strategy (2023–2026) to ensure all internal critical processes are fully automated by the end of the 2026 financial year._

-   _Kenya’s goal to [digitize 80% of of government services](https://africacheck.org/sites/default/files/media/documents/2022-08/Kenya%20Kwanza%20UDA%20Manifesto%202022.pdf) through its [digital masterplan](https://share.google/44dIZJ93YuCjN8ozd) faces [challenges](https://share.google/Z66dk9i8eo6kUX6T3) such as lack of coordinated implementation across ministries, system integration issues, resistance from county governments, resource and capacity limitations, and governance and security risks._

## Establishment of the Africa Regional Hub

The Kenya Kwanza Government promised to establish an African Regional Hub and to promote the development of software for export.

-   _The government took steps towards its promise, most notably through advancements at the [Konza Technopolis](https://techafricanews.com/2025/09/11/kenya-advances-konza-technopolis-as-africas-leading-smart-city/#:~:text=%23News-,Kenya%20Advances%20Konza%20Technopolis%20as%20Africa's%20Leading%20Smart%20City,the%20Senate%20ICT%20Committee%20Hon.) and various [digital infrastructure projects](https://icta.go.ke/news?node=659&type=news#:~:text=The%20ICT%20Authority%20convened%20a,digital%20market%2C%22%20he%20added.). However, a specific completion timeline was not established._ 
-   _As of March 2026, the Kenya Kwanza administration has transitioned its manifesto promise of an “Africa Regional Hub” into an active operational phase branded as the “[Code Nation”](https://techafricanews.com/2025/11/06/kenya-unveils-ambitious-code-nation-plan-to-lead-africa-in-software-and-ai-innovation/#:~:text=The%20initiative%20builds%20on%20Kenya's,nurture%20a%20highly%20skilled%20workforce.) initiative. They reported that the strategy was aimed at shifting Kenya from a technology consumer to a global exporter of software and digital talent._
-   _Code Nation Plan was Launched in late 2025, this plan specifically targets the [export of software solutions](https://techafricanews.com/2025/11/06/kenya-unveils-ambitious-code-nation-plan-to-lead-africa-in-software-and-ai-innovation/) and AI-driven products. It is supported by the Skill-Up Kenya program._
-    _On the 11th of May 2026, President [Ruto signed into law](https://www.the-star.co.ke/news/2026-05-11-ruto-signs-tax-investment-and-tech-bills-into-law) the_ _Tehnopolis Bill, 2024, allowing for the creation of [Technopolis Development Authority (TDA)](https://www.kbc.co.ke/kenya-creates-technopolis-authority-to-develop-konza-like-cities/) to manage the creation, development, and management of Technopolises in Kenya._

## Free internet and calls

The Kenya Kwanza government made the commitment to provide its citizens with free internet and phone calls within the first 100 days.

-   _From its JiConnect programme, which is aimed at establishing 25000 public Wi-Fi hotspots in [urban and rural areas](https://www.businessdailyafrica.com/bd/corporate/technology/city-market-gets-free-internet-hotspot-in-e-commerce-push-4014484), the government reports that it has established 1,563 new public Wi-Fi hotspots countrywide. This includes areas such as [Kenyatta Market](https://nairobi.go.ke/free-wifi-hotspot-at-kenyatta-market), Githurai Market, Sikhendu Market, Kiminini Market and Diani Market._
-   _There is, however, [no public record](https://www.standardmedia.co.ke/national/article/2001513700/rutos-scorecard-vs-reality-is-kenya-kwanza-delivering-on-promises?utm_cmp_rs=amp-next-page) to indicate programmes and initiatives that have made phone calls free in Kenya. There is also concern that the promise of 25000 public Wi-Fi hotspots will not be attained at the established hotspots only represent 6.25% of the target. The current Wi-Fi hotspots are also reported to [not be working or unreliably slow](https://nation.africa/kenya/news/weak-slow-or-dead-why-kenya-kwanza-s-free-public-wi-fi-promise-is-a-hotspot-of-frustrations-4604722)._

## Strengthening Konza Technopolis

The Kenya Kwanza government promised to strengthen the Konza Technopolis to bring together industry, academic institutions, and other innovators to co-invest in emerging technologies to create high-quality jobs that leverage artificial intelligence, robotics, and other technologies, and thus enhance our regional and global competitiveness.

-    _The_ _[Technopolis Bill (National Assembly Bill No. 6 of 2024)](https://www.parliament.go.ke/sites/default/files/2025-03/The%20Technopolis%20Bill%2C%202024.pdf) was introduced to replace the previous 2023 version._
-   _Unlike the original bill, which was specific to Konza, this new law established a [Technopolis Development Authority](https://parliament.go.ke/node/22567) to oversee the creation and management of multiple tech hubs across all 47 counties, not just Konza._
-   _The National Assembly [passed the bill on November 19, 2024](https://www.kenyans.co.ke/news/106527-mps-pave-way-recreate-konza-technopolis-kibakis-brainchild-countrywide), and it moved to the Senate, where it reached the [Second Reading stage by July 2025](https://www.standardmedia.co.ke/business/business/article/2001525491/technopolis-bill-positions-kenya-as-tech-powerhouse-senators-say)._
-   _In May 2026, the President signed the Technopolis Bill into law allowing for the creation of [Technopolis Development Authority (TDA)](https://www.kbc.co.ke/kenya-creates-technopolis-authority-to-develop-konza-like-cities/) to manage the creation, development, and management of Technopolises in Kenya._

## Reduced cost of calls and data

The Kenya Kwanza Government promised to reduce the cost of calls and data to allow wananchi and especially the youth to use online platforms for entertainment, information and business.

-   _In November 2023, the Communications Authority of Kenya announced [a reduction in charges of mobile call rates beginning March 2024](https://www.the-star.co.ke/news/2023-11-18-communication-authority-of-kenya-reduces-phone-call-rates/), a move that was informed by the prevailing economic environment. However, the reduced rates are only applicable to  apply to only local voice traffic, which means calls originating and terminating within Kenya._
-   _The [Communications Authority of Kenya (CA)](https://www.kbc.co.ke/ca-publishes-new-mobile-termination-rates-lowering-call-charges/) has implemented a long-term “glide path” to systematically lower the costs telecommunications operators charge each other, which in turn reduces retail prices for consumers._
-    _While the government has pushed for lower regulatory rates, some gains have been offset by fiscal policies, such as the [2024 Finance Bill proposal](https://www.facebook.com/tuko.co.ke/posts/communications-authority-of-kenya-cuts-cross-network-call-rates-for-next-4-years/1269046802034232/) to increase excise duty on telephone and internet services to 20%, which remains a point of contention for affordability._
-   _The government has also [established](https://www.delivery.go.ke/pillars.php?pillar=ict) 563 new public Wi-Fi hotspots countrywide (incl. Kenyatta, Githurai, Wakulima markets; Starlink deployments for KWS and WELCOME Community Wi-Fi), according to the Global Delivery Unit reports._

## Establish a Presidential Advisory Council on Science and Technology Policy

The Kenya Kwanza Government promised to establish a Presidential Advisory Council on Science and Technology Policy that will ensure a whole-of-government approach to technological development and use and build necessary capacities across the government.

-   _In November 2023, the President, vide [Executive Order Number 2 of 2023](https://www.president.go.ke/wp-content/uploads/Executive-Order-No.-2-of-2023-.pdf), established the Presidential Advisory Council on Science and Technology as an institution under the office of the President. However, we cannot ascertain the status of the membership or operations of the Council from public records as at October 2025._
-   _In a landmark ruling on January 22, 2026, the High Court [declared](https://www.the-star.co.ke/news/2026-01-22-court-presidential-advisory-offices-unconstitutional) the creation of several “Advisory Offices” in the Office of the President unconstitutional. The court found these roles—which included 21 high-profile advisors—were created without the mandatory. recommendation of the Public Service Commission (PSC) and lacked parliamentary approval._
-   _While the Council exists as a formal entity, the court issued a permanent injunction against the recognition of, and salary payments to the individuals appointed to these advisory roles. The government has expressed its intent to appeal this decision._
