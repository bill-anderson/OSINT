# Botswana launches Tier III certified carrier-neutral data center

By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa — November 26, 2025

Botswana Fibre Networks (BoFiNet) has launched a new Tier III certified carrier-neutral data center, called Digital Delta Data Centre.

Botswana Fibre Networks (BoFiNet) launched a new Tier III certified carrier-neutral data center, called the Digital Delta Data Centre (DDDC), in the country's capital, Gaborone, this week.

In a ceremony attended by government officials, industry stakeholders, regional partners and private-sector delegates, the country's Minister of Communications and Innovation, David Tshere, said the DDDC is a project being run by BoFiNet, which is 100% owned by the government of Botswana and under the stewardship of his department.

"This project stands proudly as a symbol of our vision as a country, to transform Botswana into a digital powerhouse in the region; a vision that if supported by well-planned execution, is well within our reach," the minister said.

Botswana's Minister for State Presidency Moeti Mohwasa lauded the facility, saying it represented a significant milestone on the path toward realizing Botswana's digital transformation strategy.

"It is a symbol of national pride and a mark of our progress as a country. ICT infrastructure is a key enabler of connectivity to facilitate socio-economic progress," he told attendees at the launch.

Mohwasa believes that the addition of the Tier III certified data center will enable Botswana to realize the full potential of its fiber network and complete the digital infrastructure ecosystem that will fuel economic growth for the country.

## DDDC to be nerve center of Botswana's ICT services

BoFiNet's acting CEO, Keabetswe Segole, revealed that the facility has been a decade in the making.

"The journey started in 2016 when the management of BoFiNet identified a data center as the next trajectory in the evolution of the company," Segole said.

"BoFiNet conducted a feasibility study on the project, and given that the company was only three years old at the time, [having started operating in October 2013], the timing was not right as we did not have the funding. The conversation would be resuscitated in 2018, and the wheels were put in motion in 2020 as part of our five-year corporate strategy up to 2024/25," Segole explained.

Inspired by the Okavango Delta, which Segole believes is the heartbeat of the country's tourism industry, he said the DDDC was envisioned to be the nerve center of ICT services in Botswana.

"You will notice that the brand identity of the data center combines elements of the unique heritage of the Okavango Delta with elements of interconnectivity, modernity and progression," Segole highlighted.

In October 2025, the facility was awarded Tier III Certification of Constructed Facility (TCCF) by Uptime Institute.

"This means that this data center can undergo planned maintenance on its critical infrastructure components without interrupting core operations," Segole explained.

"I am proud to say that this is the first-Tier III carrier-neutral data center of this capacity in Botswana," he claimed.

## Local job creation through DDDC

Segole explained that in BoFiNet's procurement of services such as architectural designs, engineering, project management and supply of essential components, the company was holding true to its principles regarding citizen economic empowerment and skills transfer.

"Our approach ensured that the architectural design and project management were awarded to a consortium of citizen companies, Gabana Architects and Consultants," he explained.

"Similarly, we unbundled the construction works and supply of components to award work packages to 18 citizen companies. The work packages included diesel generators, UPS, data cabling and telemetry, fire detection and suppression systems, heating, ventilation and cooling systems, to name but a few," Segole continued.

He added that the employment opportunities created by the project were significant.

"The main contractor, Zhong Gan and our 18 nominated subcontractors employed 308 citizens during the construction of this data center," he claimed.
