President Joseph Nyuma Boakai signed **Executive Order No. 147** mandating enrollment in Liberia's **National Biometric Identification System (NBIS)** for all citizens and 90-day+ foreign residents. The biometric national ID card becomes the primary form of identification for public services and private-sector transactions; foreign nationals obtain a Foreigner ID Card.

The order took immediate effect, requiring all government ministries, agencies and private institutions to enforce compliance; oversight is shared among the **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]**, the Ministry of Finance and Development Planning, and the Liberia Revenue Authority. The NIR will provide an online identity-query portal and e-verification platform (fees set by its Board of Registrars), and the order reaffirms the unique **National Identification Number (NIN)** as required for education, healthcare, telecommunications, finance, employment and national security. The NBIS is a cornerstone of Liberia's Digital Transformation Initiative; the biometric effort began with voter-registration pilots in 2021, under the 2011 NIR Act (no religious, political or ethnic data collected).
