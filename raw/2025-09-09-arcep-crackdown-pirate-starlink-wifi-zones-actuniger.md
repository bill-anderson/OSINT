# Niger : l'ARCEP déclare la guerre aux « Wifi Zones » pirates utilisant Starlink

Niger's telecoms regulator **ARCEP** issued a formal notice against illegal resale of internet via Starlink satellite terminals — the "**Wifi Zones**" phenomenon, where operators capture the Starlink signal and resell connectivity to under-served communities without authorisation.

ARCEP invoked **articles 16 and 23 of Loi 2018-45** (prior authorisation required). Sanctions per **article 57**: **3 months to 1 year imprisonment** and/or a fine up to **60 million FCFA (~€91,500)**; import/use of non-homologated equipment carries fines of **6–12 million FCFA per terminal**. Rather than immediate raids, ARCEP set an **ultimatum to cease and regularise (via its Département des Ressources et Autorisations) by 3 October 2025**, after which it would proceed to controls and sanctions. Signed by interim DG **Sidi Chegou**. Telecoms framed as strategic for sovereignty and national security.
