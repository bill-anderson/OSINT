Congo relaunched production of the **Numéro d'Identification Unique (NIU) card** (from September 2025). Instituted by Décret 2004-469 as a mandatory **taxpayer** registration for the DGID, the NIU was modernised in 2020 (order 5327; "new NIU") with **biometric capture (iris + fingerprints)** — and has since become a **universal foundational ID**: issued to every foreign resident and Congolese citizen **from age 6**, valid **10 years**, free for individuals and non-commercial entities.

Order 25550 (Oct 2022) makes the NIU **mandatory for 8 economic transactions and 5 administrative/social formalities** — invoicing, contracts, bank accounts, transfers abroad, utility/telecom subscriptions; tax/customs declarations, Treasury collection, nationality/criminal-record certificates and social-security registration. Finance Minister **Christian Yoka** framed it as permanent unique digital identification to curb duplicates and fraud and to populate economic databases; a Sept 2025 statement reaffirmed the NIU remains free nationwide.
