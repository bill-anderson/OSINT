Historical baseline (Oxford Business Group, 2016). Morocco carved out ICT niches in **offshoring and electronic payments**, with ICT nearing **3% of GDP** (2015). The government was preparing **Plan Maroc Numeric 2020** (successor to Numeric 2013).

Governance: Law 24-96 (1996) liberalised the sector, ending Maroc Telecom's monopoly; successive plans followed (e-Morocco 2010; the Dh5.2bn Maroc Numeric 2013, with four priorities). Offshoring parks (Casanearshore, Technopolis Rabat, Fès/Tetouan/Oujda Shore) hosted blue-chips (Dell, IBM, Accenture); call centres turned over ~$480m. E-payment: 11.7m credit cards (Sept 2015), electronic-payment leaders like S2M, and $2.5bn spent online in 2014. Startup funding via the Dh100m Maroc Numeric Fund. Cloud was nascent (<10 data centres, 2014). On e-government Morocco ranked 85th (UN 2016), with initiatives like Portnet (18,000 importers/exporters). Long-run context for the later Digital Morocco 2030 strategy.
