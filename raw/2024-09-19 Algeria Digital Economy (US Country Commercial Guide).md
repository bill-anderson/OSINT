# Algeria — Digital Economy (U.S. Country Commercial Guide)

U.S. ITA reference chapter on Algeria's digital economy (published **19 September 2024**).

- **Market (early 2024):** 33.49m internet users (72.9%); 50.65m mobile connections; three operators — state-owned **Mobilis** (~22.1m, Q3 2024), **Djezzy** (~15.6m), **Ooredoo** (~12.9m). Low card usage (credit 2.8%, debit 22.9%, Jan 2024).
- **Data protection & localisation:** **Law No. 18-07** (enacted 10 Jun 2018; in force 10 Aug 2023) governs personal-data processing; the **National Authority for the Protection of Personal Data (ANPDCP)** enforces it (see [[anpdcp]]). **Data localisation is mandatory** — data must be stored in-country.
- **Cybersecurity:** the National Council for Information System Security (Presidency) leads; the Ministry of National Defence develops the national cybersecurity strategy.
- **Challenges:** a restrictive, slowly-adapting regulatory framework; limited rural/Saharan broadband; digital-skills gaps; procurement favouring lowest-cost bidders. European, Chinese and South Korean firms compete actively with U.S. companies.
