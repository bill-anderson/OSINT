# Making Non-bank Participation in Inclusive Instant Payment Systems a Reality in Africa

> ## Excerpt
> An AfricaNenda/AllianceDFA focus note: non-bank direct participation in instant payment systems is "not
> an optional reform" but a prerequisite for IPS to function as inclusive DPI. Distils a Sept 2025
> roundtable of fintech associations (Ghana, Nigeria, Rwanda, South Africa, Zimbabwe).

> **Provenance note.** Cover says April 2026; copyright says March 2026 (used April). Headline stats
> ("18 of 36 IPS cross-domain") trace to SIIPS 2025.

**Artefact:** ![[2026-04-01 Making Non-bank Participation in Inclusive Instant Payment Systems a Reality in Africa.pdf]]

## Key facts

- Most non-banks reach IPS only indirectly, via sponsoring banks — raising costs and excluding
  low-income users. As of June 2025, **13 IPS onboarded e-money issuers** (vs 7 for MFIs).
- **South Africa** is the positive precedent: qualified fintechs can now participate directly under the
  SARB's Payment Ecosystem Modernization Initiative. Recommends risk-proportionate, activity-based (not
  institution-based) licensing and cross-border "license passporting."
