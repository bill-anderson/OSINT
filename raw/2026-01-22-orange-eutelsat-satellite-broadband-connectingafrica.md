By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa. January 22, 2026.

Orange Côte d'Ivoire has partnered with Eutelsat to launch a new satellite broadband Internet service called Orange Sat aimed at connecting the country's underserved areas.

Orange's Ivorian subsidiary has partnered with Eutelsat to launch a new satellite broadband Internet service called Orange Sat, aimed at connecting the country's underserved areas.

Orange Sat will use Eutelsat's Konnect satellite platform, a service that extends high-speed Internet access beyond the reach of fiber and mobile networks to rural and hard-to-access areas.

In a LinkedIn post, Eutelsat said that by combining satellite connectivity with existing fiber, 4G and mobile broadband infrastructure, Orange is providing a huge expansion in Internet coverage over Côte d'Ivoire.

It added that the service supports access to essential digital services and is set to empower businesses and education, contributing to ever-improving digital inclusion in the country.

"There is no clearer example of how satellite can help to bridge the digital divide than the work we are doing alongside our partners across the vast continent of Africa," explained Eutelsat's VP for Connectivity in Africa, Philippe Baudrier.

Baudrier said that by working with Orange Côte d'Ivoire, Konnect is helping to improve national infrastructure and extend reliable Internet access to communities that have historically been difficult to serve, supporting inclusion, resilience and long-term digital development.

In a post on LinkedIn, Orange Côte d'Ivoire said Orange Sat completes its fiber, mobile broadband and now satellite connectivity portfolio to meet the geographical and economic realities of Côte d'Ivoire.

"By connecting underserved areas, Orange Côte d'Ivoire contributes to the development of economic activities, access to essential services and the emergence of new digital uses," the telco added.

## Orange, Eutelsat continue longstanding partnership

This new deal builds on an existing relationship between the two companies.

In June 2025, Orange signed a multi-year agreement with Eutelsat Group to leverage Eutelsat's OneWeb low-Earth orbit (LEO) constellation.

In February 2025, Eutelsat announced it had connected 1 million people in sub-Saharan Africa to satellite Internet via its Eutelsat Konnect hotspots.

In March 2024, Orange Africa and Middle East and Eutelsat signed a multi-year partnership at Mobile World Congress in Barcelona, Spain, whereby Orange would use the Eutelsat Konnect satellite to connect isolated areas to broadband.

In March 2023, Orange announced it would use OneWeb's LEO constellation to provide front and backhaul connectivity to its customers in Europe, Africa, the Middle East and Latin America.

Orange is also an exclusive reseller of Konnect VHTS (very high throughput satellite) capacity for consumer broadband in France under an agreement dating back to 2020.

The group has been expanding its reach across the globe via Eutelsat's geostationary (GEO) satellite fleet and OneWeb's LEO constellation.

Orange is headquartered in France and operates across 18 countries in the Middle East and Africa (MEA), serving 166.7 million mobile customers in the MEA region as of June 30, 2025.
