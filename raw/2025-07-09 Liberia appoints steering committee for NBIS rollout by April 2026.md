President Joseph Boakai appointed National Security Advisor Samuel Kofi Woods to lead a new steering committee overseeing Liberia's **National Biometric Identification System (NBIS)**, with a rollout deadline of **13 April 2026** (per a presidential memo).

The committee brings together the Ministries of Internal Affairs, Foreign Affairs, and Post and Telecommunications, along with the Liberia Telecommunications Authority, **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]**, Central Bank of Liberia, National Elections Commission, the President's Delivery Unit and the Senior Economic Advisor. It follows the April executive order making biometric ID mandatory for citizens and 90-day+ foreign residents, and builds on biometric efforts begun in 2024. The NBIS is designed to strengthen national security and provide verifiable identities; a key feature is its ability to operate **offline** for areas with limited internet, and it supports social welfare, banking and SIM registration.
