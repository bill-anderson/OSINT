- Ghanaian fintech KutanaPay uses an escrow platform powered by artificial intelligence and blockchain to secure cross-border business transactions.
- The startup complements its escrow service with cross-border payments, cargo insurance, dispute resolution and trade finance solutions.
- KutanaPay is targeting a growing market as Africa's trade finance gap stands at $74 billion to $92 billion annually, according to the African Development Bank.

KutanaPay has developed a digital platform that enables companies to manage international trade transactions through an escrow service enhanced with artificial intelligence and blockchain technologies.

According to the company, the platform reduces the risks of non-payment and non-delivery in cross-border trade. KutanaPay said it operates in several African countries and serves members of the African diaspora, while transaction volumes have increased since its commercial launch.

The Accra-based startup was founded in 2023 by Nicholas Murphy and Samuel Opoku. The platform allows businesses to create digital contracts that define the terms of each transaction. Once buyers deposit funds into an escrow account, the platform releases the money only after the parties validate agreed milestones, such as shipment or delivery of goods.

KutanaPay complements its escrow offering with cross-border payment services, cargo insurance, dispute resolution mechanisms and trade finance solutions designed to address working capital requirements associated with international trade.

In addition, the platform integrates artificial intelligence-powered analytics that provide businesses with insights into markets and trade flows.

KutanaPay launched its platform as intra-African trade continues to face operational challenges. In its African Trade Report 2025, Afreximbank said limited access to trade finance, the high cost of cross-border payments and inadequate financial infrastructure continue to constrain trade across the continent.

Meanwhile, the African Development Bank (AfDB) estimates that Africa's annual trade finance gap ranges between $74 billion and $92 billion. The institution projects that the shortfall could reach $86.6 billion by 2027.

By combining escrow services, payments, insurance and trade finance within a single digital infrastructure, KutanaPay has positioned itself in a growing market driven by the implementation of the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) and the increasing adoption of digital solutions for cross-border commerce.
