Dar es Salaam. Continued investment in telecommunications infrastructure is driving the expansion of mobile network coverage across Tanzania, with 5G mobile technology now reaching 32.83 percent of the population despite relatively low smartphone penetration. According to the Tanzania Communications Regulatory Authority (TCRA), 5G population coverage increased to 32.83 percent in the quarter ending March 2026, up from 30.13 percent in the previous quarter.

However, geographical coverage remains limited: TCRA data show 5G services cover only 11.04 percent of the country's land area, indicating deployment is still concentrated in urban centres. The regulator's statistics show 10,084 telecommunications towers installed nationwide, with Dar es Salaam leading at 1,242 towers. 2G, 3G and 4G networks cover more than 70 percent of the country geographically.

Internet subscriptions rose to 58.9 million during the review period, up from 58.1 million in December 2025. The regulator identified challenges including low smartphone penetration, which stood at 42.5 percent (28.5 million users, down from 29.8 million in the previous quarter), a 23.7 percent broadband geographical coverage gap, and continued telecom-related fraud in some regions. TCRA director general Peter Mwasalyanda said the performance reflects steady progress towards a more connected and digitally inclusive society.

A Dar es Salaam Institute of Technology lecturer, Mr Ally Jumanne, said low smartphone uptake is largely driven by high device costs and urged manufacturers to produce more affordable 5G-enabled smartphones. He noted most operators are still using non-standalone 5G, which relies on existing 4G core infrastructure.
