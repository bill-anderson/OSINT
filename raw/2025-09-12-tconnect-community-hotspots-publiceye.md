# Bridging the Digital Divide: First Community Hotspots Transform Lesotho's Rural Villages

*Public Eye, 12 September 2025. As T-Connect Lesotho hands over first hotspots in Matsieng, Thaba Bosiu and Mokhotlong powered by Starlink.*

T-Connect Lesotho officially handed over the first Community Hotspot in Matsieng, at the office of Principal Chief Seeiso Bereng Seeiso, marking a step towards bridging the digital divide between rural villages and the capital. The milestone follows rollouts in Thaba Bosiu (office of Principal Chief Khoabane Theko) and Mokhotlong (office of Principal Chief Lerotholi Seeiso).

At Matsieng, a parent recounted his nursing-graduate daughter's inability to find work; the hotspot gives her "a window to the world" to search for jobs and upskill. Thaba Bosiu — the birthplace of the Basotho nation — was the first community in Maseru District to receive a hotspot. In remote Mokhotlong, the chief called it "a bridge to dignity and equality."

T-Connect Group CEO Advocate Phelane Phomane said this was "the first step in a long journey to deliver on our brand promise — digital transformation of Africa." By prioritising rural areas over urban centres for the first installations, T-Connect signalled that "digital progress is for all, not just for cities." The hotspots enable online learning, remote work, digital-skills training and women's digital-literacy access.
