Explainer (year precision 2024) on Equatorial Guinea's National Centre for the Issuance of Official Documents (Centro Nacional de Emisión de Documentos Oficiales, CNEDOGE).

CNEDOGE is an autonomous public entity created by Decree No. 24/2010 of 11 March, organically attached to the Ministry of National Security, with its seat in Malabo. Its purpose is to guarantee the authenticity of official documents in circulation — national identity documents (DIP), official/ordinary/diplomatic/service passports, residence cards for foreigners, and civil-servant cards. Its governing bodies are a Board of Administration, a Directorate General and Area Chiefs (board members drawn from the Ministries of National Security, Foreign Affairs, Interior, Finance & Budgets, and the General Treasury).

CNEDOGE's statutory aims: issue all official documents carrying the biometric security system; install, control and manage the technical devices for issuing and verifying documents nationwide (including at diplomatic and consular missions abroad); and advise the government on reinforcing document authenticity. In the absence of a regulation on issuance deadlines, an unwritten norm is ~30 working days for a personal identity document (DIP) and 14-17 days for a passport; prolonged periods without documents cause serious problems for citizens and foreigners.
