Burkina Faso is making a significant stride in modernizing its public administration with the launch of e-Dimaakia, a digital platform designed to streamline the management of official missions and enhance transparency in public spending.

Officially unveiled on March 24, 2026, in Ouagadougou by Minister of Economy and Finance Aboubakar Nacanabo and Minister of Digital Transition, Postal and Electronic Communications Aminata Zerbo/Sabane, the platform marks a shift from traditional manual procedures to a fully automated public-sector system.

With e-Dimaakia, all state-funded official travel, including plane ticket bookings, must now be processed through the platform. This digital solution aims to simplify procurement processes and reduce inefficiencies associated with conventional methods.

According to Alfred Thiombiano, Director General of Public Procurement and Financial Commitment Control, the platform automatically identifies the most competitive offers, promoting fairness, transparency, and accountability in public transactions.

The system also strengthens financial oversight, ensuring better tracking and rational management of expenditures linked to official travel. This initiative aligns with the government's broader strategy to ensure public funds are efficiently allocated and fully traceable.

Officials stressed that the platform supports national objectives for fiscal discipline and optimized public spending. Public servants are encouraged to use e-Dimaakia daily to foster responsible governance and administrative efficiency.

Developed locally, e-Dimaakia is a cornerstone of Burkina Faso's digital transformation agenda, underscoring the government's commitment to leveraging technology as a driver for reform, transparency, and modern governance in the public sector.
