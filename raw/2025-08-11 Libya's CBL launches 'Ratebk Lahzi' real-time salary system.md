Libya's Central Bank (**[[central-bank-of-libya|CBL]]**) launched **"Ratebk Lahzi"** (Your Salary Instantly), a real-time salary-transfer platform depositing public-sector wages directly into employees' accounts via the **national instant payment network**. Developed with the Ministry of Finance, it aims to streamline payroll and reduce delays; data for ~2 million pensioners and public employees was collected, with records for a further 700,000 being verified for the August cycle.

PM Abdulhamid Dabaiba defended it as a "national necessity," arguing that digitizing payroll protects public funds and curbs payments to individuals not performing official duties (salaries account for **55%** of Libya's public spending). It met resistance from the eastern-based government (Osama Hammad), which **refused to share employee banking data** with the Tripoli Ministry of Finance, arguing the unity government's mandate had expired and warning of politicization of sensitive financial data — highlighting that the CBL holds the legal and technical mandate over payroll data, caught between reform and politicization fears.
