Congo's Interior Minister **Raymond Zéphirin Mboulou** inaugurated a **passport and national-ID (CNI) production centre in Pointe-Noire** (early 2025) — so residents no longer need to travel to Brazzaville. The 480 m² building has latest-generation machines and a **daily capacity of 300 CEMAC electronic passports and 20,000 CNI**, split into passport and ID-card zones, using high-security materials produced with a French security firm to harden documents against forgery. Framed as administrative equity, document security and citizen trust — a decentralisation of ID/passport issuance.
