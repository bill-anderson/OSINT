The Ministry of Transport, Telecommunications and Artificial Intelligence Systems announced an international tender to hire a specialised firm to lead a comprehensive reform of Ley N.º 7/2005 General de Telecomunicaciones and its regulations. The aim is to modernise the legal framework of the telecoms sector to promote investment, innovation, connectivity, digital inclusion and legal certainty.

The tender's scope covers: critical review of the current legal framework; diagnosis of the national telecoms ecosystem; and drafting of a new law and regulations. It explicitly includes artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, critical infrastructure, digital economy, satellite connectivity, cross-border digital services, next-generation networks and data protection, plus recommendations for sector governance and institutional strengthening. Bidders are valued on experience in similar legislative reforms in African/emerging countries and advanced knowledge in ICT regulation, AI and data governance; teams must be multidisciplinary (lawyers, engineers, economists). The consultancy lasts up to six months with monthly deliverables, evaluated 70% technical / 30% financial by an Inter-ministerial Commission. Framed within the government's stated vision to position Equatorial Guinea as a technology/digital hub for Central Africa.
