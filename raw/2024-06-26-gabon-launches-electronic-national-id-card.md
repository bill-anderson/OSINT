Gabonese citizens can now obtain the new Electronic National Identity Card, President of the Transition Brigadier General Brice Clotaire Oligui Nguema announced on Monday, 24 June 2024.

The government touts the ID card as "highly secure," featuring an electronic chip storing each citizen's digital identity and a unique 14-digit Personal Identification Number (NIP). The NIP certificate, issued upon capturing biometric data, is the key document for applying for the card. This comes after a near decade-long gap in card renewals following the 2015 termination of a contract with a Belgian service provider (Semlex).

Beyond secure identification, the CNIE will let citizens access digitized administrative services while safeguarding their personal data, with nationwide coverage.
