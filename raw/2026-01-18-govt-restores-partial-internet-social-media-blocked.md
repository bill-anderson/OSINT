The government defended shutting down internet access before, during and after the General Election as "necessary, proportionate and guided by national security considerations." UCC executive director Nyombi Thembo said the shutdown was implemented on the recommendation of the Interagency Security Committee to prevent misinformation, disinformation and malformation, curb electoral fraud and avert incitement to violence.

Addressing media on January 18, 2026, at UCC headquarters in Bugolobi, Thembo announced restoration of public internet access effective that day, following the conclusion of the 2026 elections and a security assessment, carried out "in line with international principles of proportionality and legitimate aim." As of midnight January 17, general internet services (websites, news, education, government portals, financial services, email) were fully restored.

However, social media platforms and over-the-top (OTT) messaging applications remained temporarily restricted "to guard against misuse that could threaten public order." Thembo warned users against bypassing restrictions with VPNs: "If you bypass these restrictions and use that bypass to break the law, do not be surprised if your device is blocked from the network."
