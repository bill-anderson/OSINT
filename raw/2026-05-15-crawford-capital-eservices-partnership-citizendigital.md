# South Sudan confirms digital services partnership with Crawford Capital Limited

*Citizen Digital, 15 May 2026*

The Ministry of Information, Communication Technology and Postal Services (MICT&PS) of South Sudan has confirmed an official collaboration with Crawford Capital Limited to support the development and research of electronic government services (e-services) as part of the country's digital transformation agenda. Officials said the partnership aims to improve efficiency, transparency, accountability and accessibility in public service delivery.

The ministry said the engagement follows established institutional procedures and aligns with government-approved reform priorities and Council of Ministers resolutions. Crawford Capital is providing technical and research support to help strengthen digital workflows and develop citizen-centred e-services. "The Ministry affirms its official engagement with Crawford Capital Ltd. in supporting the development and research of electronic government services," Information Minister Ateny Wek Ateny said.

Separately, the South Sudan Revenue Authority (SSRA) confirmed its collaboration with the firm in the digitalisation of revenue collection systems, saying reforms have improved revenue tracking and operational efficiency and supported rapid digitisation of cargo management and tax collection. The developments come amid heightened international scrutiny over governance and financial management in South Sudan; both bodies maintained the programmes are implemented within approved frameworks.
