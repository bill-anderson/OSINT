Telecom operator MTN has committed to invest R21.8 billion (US$1.3 billion) in South Africa over the next three years to expand ICT infrastructure and advance the digital economy.

MTN SA CEO Ferdi Moolman and South African President Cyril Ramaphosa at the 2026 South Africa Investment Conference.(Source: MTN SA)

Telecommunications operator MTN has pledged to invest 21.8 billion South African rand (US$1.3 billion) over the next three years to expand ICT infrastructure and advance the digital economy in South Africa.

The announcement was made at the annual South Africa Investment Conference in Sandton, Johannesburg, this week.

MTN said that broadband connectivity is fundamental to accelerating economic participation.

"This investment in network infrastructure and IT systems has significant multiplier effects, enabling entrepreneurship, innovation and growth across communities, industries and regions," MTN SA said in a statement about the investment.

Speaking at the conference, MTN SA CEO Ferdi Moolman said that in 2025 MTN SA had spent R6.8 billion ($404 million) on its network.

By the end of 2028, it expects to invest a total of almost R22 billion ($1.3 billion) in the country.

MTN SA is one of MTN Group's 16 mobile operations across Africa and the Middle East, with 15 of those being in Africa.

MTN Group has [over 300 million customers](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/mtn-group-earnings-bounce-back-as-subscribers-top-300m) across its footprint and more than 40 million subscribers in South Africa.

## MTN SA's investments over the past three years

The telco also highlighted its substantial contributions to the South African government's tax revenue as well as job creation and digital skills development in South Africa.

In 2024, MTN SA's total tax contribution was almost R5.4 billion ($321 million).

MTN said this included corporate tax, indirect taxes, operating license fees, payroll taxes, property rates, dividend taxes and withholding taxes.

"Alongside the continued investment of billions of rands in network infrastructure, job creation and digital skills development, MTN's contribution drives meaningful socio-economic impact across the nation and is key to our license to operate as well as our reputation as a responsible corporate citizen," said Moolman.

MTN SA spent over $400 million on network upgrades in 2025 and will invest another $1.3 billion by the end of 2028. (Source: aopsan on Freepik)

MTN employs more than 4,000 people in South Africa and has been working to develop graduate talent to support the country's future workforce.

MTN is a Level 1 Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment (B-BBEE) contributor, and the company said that in 2024 it spent over R9.8 billion ($583 million) with Black-owned suppliers and R18.9 billion ($1.1 billion) with Black women-owned suppliers.

Through the MTN SA Foundation, more than R1.1 billion has been invested in community initiatives since 2009.

These include more than 400 multimedia centers, which reach more than 220,000 learners, and programs that have equipped more than 16,000 educators with digital tools and training.

The MTN Online School now reaches more than 470,000 learners while MTN's entrepreneurship programs have exposed more than 36,000 learners to business principles.

In addition, more than 3,500 unemployed youth have been trained through digital skills programs, and more than 365 small, medium and micro-sized enterprises (SMMEs) have been supported through incubation and business development initiatives, MTN said.

"MTN's participation in the annual South African Investment Conference reinforces our strong partnership with government as well as our commitment to advancing South Africa's socioeconomic priorities," the telco said.

In March 2026, MTN said it had [joined a $45 million funding round](https://www.connectingafrica.com/ai/mtn-invests-in-startup-building-ai-native-radio-access-networks) for ORAN Development Company (ODC), a US-based startup building AI-native radio access networks (AI-RAN).

## SA Investment Conference rakes in pledges

The sixth South Africa Investment Conference saw 16 investments valued at R10 billion ($595 million) or more, with MTN's being the third biggest.

The largest pledge came from Sasol, which announced a R60 billion ($3.6 billion) investment across its chemicals and packaging operations.

In the tourism and property sector, Cornubia 957 committed R25 billion ($1.5 billion), with a strong focus on developments in the province of KwaZulu-Natal.

Members of the French Chamber of Commerce and Industry South Africa collectively committed R20.4 billion across multiple industries and provinces, while Diem Co and Salt Rock City pledged R20 billion ($1.2 billion) for tourism and property development.

In the green economy space, Seriti Green announced a R10 billion ($595 million) investment in Mpumalanga, supporting the country's transition toward decarbonization.

The SA government said a total of 65 investment projects were announced across six sectors, amounting to R113.5 billion ($6.75 billion).

SA Minister of Trade, Industry and Competition Parks Tau said the conference reaffirmed that investment is not an isolated transaction, but a partnership built on trust, policy certainty and mutual accountability.

"The responsibility we share is clear and that is to translate commitment into projects, to ensure timely execution and to deliver tangible outcomes that advance growth, create employment and improve livelihood," Tau added.

Historically, the annual investment conference has brought numerous commitments from the private sector, including major tech companies.

Ahead of last year's conference, [Microsoft said it would invest R5.4 billion](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/microsoft-pledges-major-ai-cloud-investment-in-south-africa) ($298 million at the time) by the end of 2027 to expand its cloud and AI infrastructure in South Africa.

At the 2023 conference, [Cassava Technologies pledged a total of R4.5 billion](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/cassava-technologies-to-invest-249m-in-south-africa) ($249 million at the time) through its business units – Liquid Intelligent Technologies, Africa Data Centres and Distributed Power Africa.
