Liberia's Ministry of Finance and Development Planning launched the **Governance Reform Accountability Transformation (GREAT)** project on 20 March 2025 in Monrovia. Funded by the World Bank's ID Credit Program with **$30 million** (plus an additional $13m for digital infrastructure), GREAT seeks to enroll at least **2 million** Liberians for the national digital ID through the **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]**.

Running to November 2030, the project also builds a digital-government ecosystem beginning with nine institutions (civil service, taxation/revenue, NIR, and the ministries of Education, Health, Finance and Trade). Finance Minister Augustine Kpehe Ngafuan cited four pillars: enhancing access to public services; improving tax revenue by curbing corruption; strengthening accountability; and capacity building. Acting World Bank Country Representative Oyewole Afuye highlighted strong government ownership; NIR ED Andrew Peters said enrollment teams would reach citizens at clan and chiefdom levels.
