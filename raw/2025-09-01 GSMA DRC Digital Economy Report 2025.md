# Driving Digital Transformation in the DRC (GSMA, Sept 2025)

A GSMA report (authored with Through Line Advisors) on the Democratic Republic of the Congo's
digital economy and the mobile sector's role in it.

## Key findings

- The telecoms sector is already a significant share of DRC GDP and government revenue, but
  **mobile coverage is low by regional standards** — reflecting the high cost of building and
  operating networks, and **smartphone penetration below other African countries**.
- Operators face a **complex tax framework** with numerous taxes levied at multiple levels of
  government, raising the overall burden and compliance cost and crowding out network investment.
- Policy recommendations: lower excise duties and operator taxes and simplify tax collection;
  expand the electricity grid to cut network-operating cost; improve policy/regulatory stability
  and transparency; grow digital public services and support device subsidies/financing.
- **Modelled impact if all recommendations are implemented:** +9.7 million internet users by 2029
  vs base case; the mobile-broadband usage gap cut by 8 percentage points; up to CDF 2,100bn
  additional value-add from agriculture and CDF 6,500bn from mining; net tax revenue up to
  CDF 3,000bn higher.
- Context: aligns with the DRC's **National Digital Plan Horizon 2025** and the **World Bank DRC
  Digital Transformation Project**.
