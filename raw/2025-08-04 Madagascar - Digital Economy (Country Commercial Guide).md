The U.S. Country Commercial Guide for Madagascar's digital economy (last published 4 August 2025). The digital sector accounts for 18 percent of Madagascar's economic growth and represents 1.7 percent of GDP. Thanks to private-sector investment in fibre optics, Madagascar has among the fastest internet speeds in Africa and is becoming a destination for technology-intensive business process outsourcing (BPO), serving clients including Amazon, Deliveroo and Air France.

The Government has established a Digital Transformation Plan 2023-2028 (Plan Stratégique Numérique, PSN), focused on telecommunications and digitising public services; it aims to raise the sector's GDP contribution by six percent by 2028. The Minister of Digital Development, Posts and Telecommunications (MNDPT) seeks partnerships with the US, France and India, with particular interest in the US. In February 2025, Madagascar launched "Choose Digital Madagascar" to attract investment and introduced the Excelia quality label for digital training/services.

Key institutions/law: regulator ARTEC; Ministry MNDPT; postal service PAOMA. Legal authorities: Law 2014-006 (cybercrime), Law 2005-023 (telecom reform), the 2014 electronic transactions law, and Law 2016-056 (digital money).
