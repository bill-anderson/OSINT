# IEC pushes for digital election reforms

Siamisang revealed that the IEC is rolling out a series of technology-driven reforms aimed at modernising voter registration PIC: MORERI SEJAKGOMO

The Independent Electoral Commission (IEC) is pressing ahead with plans to digitise Botswana's electoral processes ahead of the 2029 General Election, even as its Secretary, Jefferson Siamisang, maintains that Batswana are not keen on introducing Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs).

Appearing before the 62nd Public Accounts Committee (PAC), Siamisang revealed that the IEC is rolling out a series of technology-driven reforms aimed at modernising voter registration, managing electoral data more efficiently and eliminating administrative errors that have affected previous elections.

His remarks sparked interest after he stated that voters in Botswana do not want EVMs, a position he later clarified as his personal opinion based on previous attempts to introduce the technology and public reactions to the proposal.
