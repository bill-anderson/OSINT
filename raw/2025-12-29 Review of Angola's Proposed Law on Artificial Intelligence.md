Angola has introduced a **draft Artificial Intelligence law** — joining a growing wave of African AI legislative proposals (Egypt, Ghana, Morocco, Nigeria have specific proposals; Kenya, Mauritius, Namibia, Uganda, Zimbabwe have signalled intent). The review analyses the bill and its intersection with a **concurrent draft amendment to the Data Protection Act**.

The bill proposes a comprehensive framework governing the development, supply and use of AI, structurally influenced by **OECD principles** and the **EU AI Act's risk-based approach**. Its dual objective: actively build a domestic AI industry and technological sovereignty while establishing human-centric safeguards. Scope is broad, with strong **extraterritorial jurisdiction** (activities outside Angola that "affect the public interests, or the legitimate rights and interests of natural and legal persons domiciled in Angola"), excluding purely personal/family use and scientific R&D.
