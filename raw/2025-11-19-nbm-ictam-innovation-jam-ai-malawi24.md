K75.5 to help Malawian young innovators turn ideas into thriving solutions

There are expectations that Malawian young innovators will turn their ideas into thriving solutions, helping the country’s digital future courtesy of a K75.5 million boost for the 2025 Innovation Jam.

As part of its six-year partnership with the ICT Association of Malawi (ICTAM), National Bank of Malawi (NBM) plc has made the donation to support the Innovation Jam, which will run alongside the annual ICT conference from November 19 to 22, 2025 under the theme ‘AI for Sustainable Development’, highlighting the rising influence of artificial intelligence in national development.

Speaking during the cheque handover ceremony on Monday, NBM plc’s Head of Digital Financial Services, William Kaunda, said many promising ideas fail because young innovators lack structured guidance.

“Many talented innovators in Malawi’s ICT sector often lack the platform or support to grow their ideas. Our aim is to provide that structured support so these innovations can thrive,” said Kaunda.

He added that the Bank has signed a three-year memorandum of understanding (MoU) with ICTAM to ensure consistent mentorship, incubation, and technical support for innovations emerging from the event.

“With this MoU, we are committing to providing continuous support and guidance to the innovators over the next three years,” he said.

Kaunda also stressed that as digital services expand and cybercrime grows more sophisticated, artificial intelligence is becoming indispensable for fraud prevention and system security.

“Artificial intelligence is no longer optional for us. To combat modern fraud and protect our customers, we need advanced AI tools to detect and prevent threats efficiently,” he said.

ICTAM President, Clarence Gama, welcomed the continued support, saying the structured approach will strengthen Malawi’s innovation ecosystem.

“We are excited and grateful. The National Bank has held our hands for a very long time. This is a big testament to how the corporate sector is willing to drive technology and innovation in the country,” said Gama.

He added that the Innovation Jam will feature pitching sessions by emerging innovators, judged by a panel of experts to identify ideas with strong potential for incubation.
