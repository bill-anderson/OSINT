# Starlink faces opposition in Lesotho

*Connecting Africa, 11 March 2025. By Matshepo Sehloho.*

Starlink's telecom licence application in Lesotho faced opposition from a local rights group, the Advocates for the Supremacy of the Constitution (Section 2), over "a total absence of local ownership" — a departure from Lesotho's practice of ensuring Basotho participation in key sectors.

Section 2 cited ownership structures as precedent: **Econet Telecom Lesotho is 70% owned by Econet Wireless Global with the government of Lesotho retaining 30%; Vodacom Lesotho is 80% owned by Vodacom Group with 20% held by the Sekha-Metsi consortium** of local Basotho. By contrast, Starlink Lesotho is 100% held by Starlink Holdings Netherlands, with registered directors all US citizens — leaving "no room for Basotho ownership."

The objection responded to the LCA's public consultation on Starlink's Network Services Licence application (10-year duration if approved); the last day for comments was 20 March 2025. The article notes President Trump's dismissive reference to Lesotho ("a country nobody has ever heard of") in a US Congress address citing cut foreign spending.
