On November 10, 2025 the National Assembly held a public hearing on the National Digital Economy and E-Governance Bill, 2024 — landmark legislation to create a unified legal and institutional framework for the digital economy and e-government. Now in its final stage, the bill was expected to be transmitted to the President for assent before end of week (per Minister Bosun Tijani). The bill elevates NITDA to a "super regulator," consolidating its authority with broad supervisory, enforcement and coordination powers across government and private-sector digital operations. Sponsored by Senator Shuaib Afolabi Salisu (Ogun West) and co-sponsored by Adedeji Stanley Olajide in the House. It has raised concern among stakeholders about regulatory overlap and institutional balance.
