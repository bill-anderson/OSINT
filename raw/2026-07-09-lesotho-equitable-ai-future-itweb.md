# Lesotho pushes for equitable AI future

*ITWeb Africa, 9 July 2026. By Lezeth Khoza.*

Lesotho called for stronger global action to ensure developing countries are not left behind in the AI era, highlighting the need for affordable connectivity, reliable energy and strong digital foundations. MICSTI Principal Secretary Kanono Ramashamole made the remarks at the United Nations Global Dialogue on AI Governance, WSIS Forum and AI for Good events in Geneva.

Ramashamole said developing nations must participate in AI "not only as consumers, but also as co-creators of policy and technology." He said Lesotho has achieved **100% broadband coverage but only about 50% of the population regularly accesses the internet** — showing affordability remains a major barrier. He cited areas where people still travel long distances to charge phones, and noted challenges from fragmented data, weak governance frameworks and limited AI-ready data. Lesotho sees investment in DPI and open-source approaches (including participation in Open Source Week) as pathways to national AI readiness.
