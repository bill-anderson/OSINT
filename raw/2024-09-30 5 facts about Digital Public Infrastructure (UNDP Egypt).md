# 5 facts you need to know about Digital Public Infrastructure (UNDP Egypt)

UNDP Egypt explainer published as Egypt (led by **[[mcit-egypt|MCIT]]** with UNDP, ITU, OSET, the World Bank and Co-Develop) prepared to host the **Global DPI Summit** in the New Administrative Capital (1–3 October 2024). Sets out five DPI framings: DPI is more than technology; a game-changer for all countries; digital infrastructure ≠ automatic digital literacy; not one-size-fits-all; inclusion matters.

On Egypt: the **[[digital-egypt|Digital Egypt]]** strategy aligns with **[[egypt-vision-2030|Vision 2030]]** across human capital, digital services, IT infrastructure and innovation. The **Digital Egypt Portal** offers **170+ e-government services**; Egypt Post availed 27 digital services and digitised ~3,000 post offices; the **"Decent Life" (Haya Karima)** initiative reaches 4,500 villages / 58m citizens via fibre. Pipeline: 20+ Creativa Innovation Hubs, ~250,000 STEM graduates/year, 400,000 ICT trainees (2023/24), and the Egypt University of Informatics (Africa's first ICT-specialised university).
