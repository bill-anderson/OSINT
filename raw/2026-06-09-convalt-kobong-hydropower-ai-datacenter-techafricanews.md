# Lesotho Unveils Mega Hydropower and AI Data Center Project Under $6.2 Billion Agreement

*Tech Africa News, 9 June 2026. By Akim Benamara.*

The Kingdom of Lesotho signed a binding Memorandum of Agreement (MoA) between the Ministry of Energy and US-based Convalt Energy for a project valued at approximately **$6.2 billion (98 billion maloti)**, expected to become the **largest foreign direct investment in Lesotho's history**. The agreement was **signed on June 4, 2026**.

The announcement followed a meeting between **King Letsie III** and former US House Majority Leader and Convalt Energy board member **Richard Gephardt**. The initiative — "Project Kobong" — combines 1,200 MW of hydropower capacity with an integrated AI data centre. It is designed to strengthen energy security, reduce reliance on imported electricity (currently much from South Africa), and position Lesotho as a regional power exporter and a hub for digital infrastructure. American exports associated with the development could exceed $2 billion.

*(Note: this outlet describes the deal as a binding MoA signed 4 June 2026; Connecting Africa and other outlets characterise it as a non-binding MoU pending feasibility studies — a discrepancy to reconcile at ingest.)*
