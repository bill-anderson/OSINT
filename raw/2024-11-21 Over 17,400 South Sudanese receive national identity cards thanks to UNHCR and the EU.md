# Over 17,400 South Sudanese Receive National Identity Cards Thanks to UNHCR and the European Union

> ## Excerpt
> A joint EU/UNHCR project issued more than 17,400 National Identity Cards to South Sudanese returnees and people at risk of statelessness in 2024, weeks after South Sudan acceded to the two Conventions on Statelessness.

---

Published 21 November 2024. Source: UNHCR (originally via ReliefWeb).

Juba/Yei – The United Nations Assistant High Commissioner for Refugees, Mr Raouf Mazou, and the EU Ambassador, Mr Timo Olkkonen, took part in a ceremony in Yei during which hundreds of South Sudanese received National Identity Cards as part of a joint project in support of the Directorate of Civil Registry, Nationality, Passport, and Immigration (DCRNPI).

More than 17,400 South Sudanese have been issued with National Identity Cards through the joint EU/UNHCR partnership since the start of 2024. The exercise has targeted South Sudanese returning from being refugees in other countries, as well as persons at risk of statelessness, focusing on areas of high refugee return — in Central Equatoria, the counties of Morobo, Lainya, Yei, and Kajo-Keji. The issuance of ID cards provides returning citizens with a legal identity — an essential first step to access rights and services, opening avenues for employment, financial inclusion and access to education.

Ambassador Olkkonen welcomed the milestone, highlighting that a nationality document is a fundamental right enshrined in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, and called on the Government to continue efforts to guarantee citizenship rights to all who are entitled. He noted the EU-funded "Integrated Approach to Enhancing Protection, Resilience and Solutions in South Sudan" (2023–2025) project being implemented in Greater Yei (Lainya, Morobo, Yei and Kajo-Keji counties) and also in Torit and Wau, supporting civil documentation, GBV mitigation, livelihoods and education.

DCRNPI Director General Simon Majur highlighted the government's dedication to enhancing access to civil documentation: "Having a National Identity Card empowers returnees and displaced communities to access essential services such as education, healthcare, social protection, and formal employment."

Mr Mazou noted the ceremony came within weeks of South Sudan formally acceding to the two Conventions on Statelessness — a significant step for a country integrating large numbers of returning citizens. He added that while most refugees return from Uganda and Kenya voluntarily, the situation is particularly dire for the hundreds of thousands who have come back from Sudan in adverse circumstances since April 2023, many of whom had never lived in South Sudan and face a high risk of statelessness.
