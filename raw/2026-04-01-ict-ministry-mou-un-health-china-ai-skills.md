The Ministry of ICT and National Guidance signed an MoU with the United Nations Health Industry Foundation and the Prince Kimbugwe Foundation to strengthen collaboration in AI, digital innovation and youth capacity building. The partnership focuses on AI and emerging technologies, international exchange programs, and youth digital skills development, and will establish AI laboratories, initiate joint research projects and provide specialised training for Ugandan youth and ICT professionals.

Permanent Secretary Dr Amina Zawedde said the collaboration aligns with Uganda's vision of a $500b economy by 2040, with exchange programmes to China on smart agriculture. Chunxiao Huang, chairman of the UN Health Industry Foundation, emphasised China's commitment to integrating AI into Uganda's agricultural ecosystem and supporting smart cities, projecting over 50,000 jobs within five years. The initiative includes drones for rural farmers and promotion of Chinese agricultural investment.
