## Description

"The African Development Bank (AfDB) has approved a US$200 million loan towards Nigeria's Digital Value Chain Infrastructure for Boosting Employment (D-VIBE) project, also known as Project Bridge, a fiber-optic program aimed at expanding broadband access as well as strengthening the country's digital economy."

"The loan will help Africa's most populous country deploy 90,000km of open-access fiber nationwide, expand digital skills and drive job creation... extending Nigeria's national fiber backbone from around 30,000km to about 120,000km, connecting all 774 local government areas."

"The bank said the loan is part of an $800 million sovereign financing package for the project, alongside $500 million from the World Bank and $100 million from the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD). It added that the total project financing is estimated at $2 billion, and includes an EU grant of €22 million, a $2.6 million Multilateral Cooperation Center for Development Finance (MCDF) project preparation grant, and at least $1.2 billion investment from the private sector."
