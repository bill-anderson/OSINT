# MACRA cuts spectrum fees by 20% to boost digital access

Author: Eric Chiputula

The Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) has reduced spectrum licence fees by 20% to promote affordable communication services and drive digital transformation in line with the Malawi 2063 vision.

MACRA Director General Daudi Suleman said the fee cut supports both service providers and consumers by lowering barriers to digital connectivity.

He highlighted that high costs and limited providers have hindered progress toward a vibrant digital economy.

The reduced fees apply to the use of radio frequency spectrum—a critical resource for mobile networks, broadcasting, and emergency services.

While spectrum fees generate government revenue, they also increase telecom providers’ costs, potentially impacting consumer prices.

MACRA’s review of its pricing framework, including its 2016 Administrative Incentive Pricing model, aimed to better align costs with efficient spectrum use, accessibility, and affordability.
