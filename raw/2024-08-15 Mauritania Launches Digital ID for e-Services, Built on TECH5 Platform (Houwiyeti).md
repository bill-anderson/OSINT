Mauritania launched a digital identity app ("Houwiyeti") offering access to government e-services, building on a pilot using biometric technology from TECH5. TECH5 won the digital-ID pilot contract in June 2022, in partnership with local integrator SmartMS and UNDP, for the Ministry of Digital Transition, Innovation and Public Sector Modernisation (MTNIMA).

The digital ID revolves around selfie-based identity verification: TECH5's T5-OmniMatch Face BioSDK (1:1 matching), T5-AirSnap Face (smartphone capture), T5-LDS (liveness detection), and T5-IDencode/T5-Cryptograph (encrypted 2D machine-readable identity container). SmartMS provided the back-end and Identity Provider elements; TECH5 delivered iOS and Android apps. The pilot was demonstrated with MTNIMA employees in March 2023 (a citizen reads their e-passport/ID card, matches it via selfie, stores a digital ID on their phone, and enables biometric login to the e-services portal), concluding October 2023 and paving the way for the Houwiyeti app. TECH5 shared expertise with MOSIP (Modular Open Source Identity Platform).
