Per **[[ncc]]** (Nigerian Communications Commission) data, Nigeria's telecom operators recorded **155,397
fibre-cut incidents** in **April–May 2026** — rising from **74,276 (April)** to a record **79,121 (May)** —
**a 2,428% increase** on the 5,934 incidents of Q1 2026.

- **Vandalism** was the leading cause (**>54,000** incidents), despite telecom infrastructure being designated
  **Critical National Information Infrastructure** (penalties up to 10 years' imprisonment).
- Infrastructure degradation, cable/converter faults and **road construction** accounted for **>84,000** cuts —
  pointing to maintenance and inter-agency coordination gaps.
- Operators also logged **675 equipment thefts** and **4,319** denied-access cases at base stations, with
  insecurity behind ~three-quarters of disruptions.

Proposed fixes — a **Dig-Once policy**, **AI-powered fibre-sensing** for real-time cut detection, and an NCC
**cost-based shared-duct framework** — are not yet widely adopted. Nigeria has deployed ~**35,000 km** of fibre
under its National Broadband Plan, but protection has lagged network expansion.
