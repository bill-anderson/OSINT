A data center run entirely on geothermal energy will be built in Kenya as part of the **$1 billion Microsoft/G42 investment**, confirmed by a letter of intent during Ruto's US visit. The **Olkaria** campus is expected operational within two years with an initial **100MW** capacity, potentially rising to **1GW**; Kenya's state-owned **KenGen** operates four geothermal plants (700MW+).

Microsoft is also launching an East Africa cloud region and will boost connectivity (last-mile wireless for 20m in Kenya and 50m across East Africa by end-2025). G42 had announced a 100MW geothermal DC campus with local operator **EcoCloud** (March 2024); EcoCloud earlier broke ground on its 'Project Eagle' geothermal data center at the KenGen Green Energy Park in Olkaria (phase one: 24MW over eight 3MW halls).
