The Ministry of Home Affairs, Immigration, Safety and Security announced plans to launch Namibia's first electronic identity cards (e-IDs) by July 2026. Ministry executive director Etienne Maritz said the biometric-enabled e-ID would reduce identity fraud, eliminate duplicate records, and enhance data and national security, giving each citizen a unique, near-unforgeable verifiable identity.

The card is described as a smart, secure card embedded with a contactless microchip holding biometric and demographic data, enabling real-time identity verification across banking, healthcare, education, elections and government services, with mobile-enabled verification and outreach teams for rural communities. A public engagement in Windhoek on 26 June, themed "Un-Conference on Digital Legal Identity," was planned. The work is in collaboration with UNDP and aligned to the UNDP Model Governance Framework for Digital Legal Identity, framed as the foundation of Namibia's broader digital transformation and enabling cross-border mobility within SADC.
