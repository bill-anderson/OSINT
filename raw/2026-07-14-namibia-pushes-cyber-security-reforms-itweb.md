# Namibia pushes cyber security reforms

Namibia is finalising the Cybercrime Bill and the Data Protection Bill, ICT Minister Emma Theofelus said at the inaugural meeting of CRAN's newly appointed board in Windhoek. "These legislative measures are essential to strengthening Namibia's digital governance framework by providing robust mechanisms to combat cybercrime, address digital violence and protect the rights and personal information of citizens."

In the meantime, law enforcement and the judiciary rely on the Electronic Transactions Act, the Communications Act and the Penal Code. The Namibia Cyber Security Incident Response Team (Nam-CSIRT, managed by CRAN) recorded 103,085 cyber threat incidents and 367,670 cyber vulnerabilities during Q1 2026. Last month, CRAN issued an alert to 13 local organisations after security devices were compromised in the "FortiBleed" incident.

CRAN board chairperson Elvis Nashilongo said transforming the regulator into a smart, digitally enabled organisation would be a board priority: "We will prioritise innovation, enhance operational efficiency and support Namibia's digital transformation agenda."
