Ghana's National Identification System (the Ghana Card), a DPI meant to be the cornerstone of a national database, has yet to realize its full potential seven years on; structural challenges hinder its multifunctionality. Citizens still carry multiple ID cards for services the Ghana Card could perform (cf. Nigeria's single General Multipurpose Card).

As of October 2024, the NIA had registered about 18 million of Ghana's ~34 million population. Kwesi Atuahene (former Executive Director, Africa Centre for Digital Transformation, ACDT) said the Ghana Card is designed as a multipurpose single trusted identity (banking, voter registration, SIM registration, e-passport) but integration and technical issues remain; the card "is not progressive in design," and data captured at initial issuance in 2018/2019 has not been updated for most people.

Healthcare: the ID has largely replaced the health-insurance card, but full integration (e.g. with LHIMS) persists as a gap. Banking/payments: integration began in 2022; ACDT's Evelyn Kwarteng urged BoG–NIA collaboration to make the Ghana Card usable as an ATM card (withdrawals, transfers, payments) and to link all bank accounts to it via a secure national database, enabling Mastercard/Visa/PayPal partnerships. Business registration: an April 2021 NIA/GRA/Registrar-General statement began replacing the GRA TIN with the NIA PIN (Ghana Card PIN) as the unique identifier for transactions.
