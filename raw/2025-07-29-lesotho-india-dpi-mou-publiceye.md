# Lesotho, India forge digital partnership

*Public Eye, 29 July 2025. By 'Mathato Seboka. Ministers sign MoU to accelerate local digital transformation during high-level visit.*

Nthati Moorosi, Minister of Information, Communications, Science, Technology and Innovation, signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with India's Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology. The signing took place during an official visit to Maseru by India's Minister of State for External Affairs, Shri Pabitra Margherita.

Principal Secretary Kanono Ramashamole said the MoU will enable Lesotho to implement its national digital transformation strategies more effectively, and is "pivotal for deploying a robust Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI)." He identified three priorities for the year: (1) digital ID — "the foundation of everything" for secure, verifiable IDs and e-services, with pilots already started and continuing "with our Indian friends"; (2) a unified payment environment, modelled on India's QR-code payments, especially for vendors and informal businesses; and (3) enhanced information exchange across ministries, so citizens are not asked the same information repeatedly ("once-only").

Minister Moorosi said the MoU deepens collaboration using India Stack, noting India's model of "scalable, inclusive, and secure digital infrastructure." Lesotho and India mark over five decades of diplomatic relations (since 1971), including India's Lines of Credit and the India-Lesotho Centre for Advanced Education in Information Technology. She said the MoU was signed "in the wake of our national digital transformation strategy launch."
