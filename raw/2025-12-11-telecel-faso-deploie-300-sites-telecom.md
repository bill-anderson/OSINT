Telecel has begun deploying 300 telecom sites across Burkina Faso as part of a government project to provide coverage to 750 underserved areas.

Telecommunications operator Telecel Faso has begun deploying 300 telecom sites – some of them towers, some just antennas – across Burkina Faso as part of the government's project to provide coverage to 750 underserved areas.

The announcement was made by the telco's CEO Boris Compaoré during a meeting with the country's Minister of Digital Transition, Posts and Electronic Communications, Aminata Zerbo-Sabané, earlier this week.

"We are in the midst of deployment, and I came to report to the Minister, who has strongly supported and assisted us. We informed her that beyond the 150 sites initially planned, we are working to add another 150. This will accelerate the process and have a greater impact on the main towns of urban and rural municipalities," Compaoré explained.

Zerbo-Sabané praised Telecel Faso's commitment to reducing the digital divide in underserved areas, despite the challenges encountered on the ground.

By deploying new telecom sites, Telecel is expanding its network coverage, enabling the company to attract new customers in both competitive markets and previously underserved areas where it will be the first to establish a presence.

## Burkina Faso bridging digital divide

The deployment of the telecom sites comes at a time when the West African country is working towards connecting underserved communities.

In September 2025, the government of Burkina Faso signed a memorandum of understanding with Presight International Services, a subsidiary of the United Arab Emirates-based G42 Group, aimed at strengthening Burkina Faso's digital economy.

In August 2025, the Burkinabé government and Ghana joined forces to address cross-border signal interference to help mitigate challenges along their common borders.

In July 2024, pan-African operator Orange's Burkinabé unit chose Vox Solutions as the international application-to-person (A2P) short message service (SMS) and voice gateway for traffic into its network.

In November 2023, the country's military government began working on a law related to cybersecurity.

## Burkina Faso's mobile ecosystem

The initiative could help Telecel strengthen its position in a market dominated by companies like Orange and Moov Africa, while also contributing to narrowing the digital divide.

According to statistics from market research company Omdia, a sister company to Connecting Africa, Telecel Faso is the smallest telco in the country with 2.8 million mobile users at the end of the third quarter of 2025.

The largest telco is Orange, which recorded an estimated 14.2 million mobile users over the same period, while Moov had 12.7 million mobile users.
