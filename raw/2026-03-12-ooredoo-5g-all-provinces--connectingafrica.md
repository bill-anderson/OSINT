# Ooredoo expands 5G network to all Algerian provinces

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa
March 12, 2026 — 2 Min Read

Telecom operator Ooredoo Algeria has expanded its 5G network to all provinces in the North African country, ahead of the government's rollout schedule.

The telco said it is accelerating the nationwide rollout of its 5G network as demand continues to rise.

Ooredoo explained it now has 5G up and running in all wilayas (administrative subdivisions, equivalent to provinces or states) and will continue to increase the density of its basestations.

The telco said that to ensure reliable coverage and a user experience across all regions, it will reinforce the network.

"By extending 5G coverage nationwide, we reaffirm Ooredoo's commitment to investing in infrastructure and networks to deliver services that meet international standards, support the digital economy, and help young people and businesses seize opportunities created by digital transformation," explained Ooredoo Algeria CEO Roni Tohme.

Ooredoo Algeria's nationwide 5G expansion comes a week after fellow operator Djezzy announced its 5G deployment was also ahead of schedule, with its 5G network now live in 18 provinces.

Ooredoo Algeria and Djezzy's 5G expansion come after all the country's telcos were granted 5G licenses back in December 2025.

The licenses were granted after the country's Regulatory Authority of Post and Electronic Communications (ARPCE) initiated a tender process for 5G licenses.

## Algeria's mobile environment

Under the Algerian government's national plan, all the telcos must achieve full nationwide 5G coverage within six years.

According to Omdia, a sister company of Connecting Africa, Algérie Télécom's Mobilis brand was the biggest telco, with 23.6 million mobile subscribers at the end of the third quarter of 2025.

Djezzy had around 17.7 million mobile subscribers in the same period, and Ooredoo Algeria had 14.8 million.
