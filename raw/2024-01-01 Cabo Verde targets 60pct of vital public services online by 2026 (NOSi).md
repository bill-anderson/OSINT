# Cabo Verde targets 60% of vital public services online by 2026 (NOSi)

**Carlos Tavares Pina, CEO of [[nosi|NOSi]]** (Núcleo Operacional da Sociedade de Informação), Cabo Verde's digital-government agency, told GovInsider the government targets digitalising **at least 60% of vital public services by 2026**. He set out three pillars: **digital identity and authentication, interoperability between information systems, and disruptive innovations**.

Established ~25 years ago, NOSi spearheads digital transformation at home and abroad, and provides **data-centre hosting to other African countries** (Equatorial Guinea, Mozambique, Burkina Faso and more). Claims cited: company registration in a day; election results within two hours; Cabo Verde ranked **6th in Africa** in ICT development (ITU 2023).

Notable systems: the **Consular Portal and Digital Diaspora** initiative (launched 2020); the **Justice Information System (SIJ)**; and the open-source **Integrated Government Resource Planning (IGRP)** microservices framework underpinning the digital government. Skills programmes: **NOSiAkademia** (launched 2016; ~311 trained to 2020) and **WebLab** (44 secondary-school labs).
