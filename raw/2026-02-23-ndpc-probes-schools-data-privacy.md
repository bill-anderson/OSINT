Nigeria launched investigations into over 1,000 educational institutions (federal and state universities, polytechnics, colleges of education, technical colleges) to ensure compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection Act — one of the largest sector-wide investigations since the law's passage. Per NDPC's Babatunde Bamigboye, it is part of a targeted "sector-by-sector" compliance drive. Within 21 days, institutions must present 2024 data protection compliance audit returns, designate a Data Protection Officer, provide safeguards summaries, and prove registration as a Data Controller/Processor of Major Importance. Noncompliance may bring enforcement orders, administrative penalties or criminal prosecution.
