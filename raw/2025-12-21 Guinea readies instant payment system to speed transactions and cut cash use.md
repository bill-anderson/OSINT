Guinea is preparing a national instant and interoperable payment system (SPI) to modernize its financial infrastructure and expand financial inclusion. El Hadj Mohamed Lamine Conté, first vice-governor of the Central Bank of the Republic of Guinea (BCRG), opened a two-day workshop on the SPI project on 18 December 2025. The system will enable immediate transfers between bank accounts, mobile wallets and other instruments, facilitate merchant payments in the formal and informal sectors, reduce cash reliance, and support government payments (salaries, social assistance, supplier settlements).

Technology: after stakeholder consultations, Guinea selected a public digital infrastructure based on Mojaloop, enabling full interoperability among payment service providers while keeping the state in control of the infrastructure, limiting technological dependence, and favouring local fintech innovation. The system is designed to be scalable and compatible with regional initiatives — Guinea is preparing for future integration with PAPSS and ECOWAS-led projects.

Context: Guinea draws on WAEMU, where the BCEAO launched its interoperable instant-payment platform PI-SPI in late September 2025 across the eight member states. WAEMU electronic payments rose from 260 million (2014) to 11 billion+ (2024), with financial inclusion at 74%. Per AfricaNenda's 2024 SIIPS report, 31 instant-payment systems are operational in Africa (three cross-border). Ghana, Nigeria and Rwanda already run instant-payment systems.
