More than **247,000 heads of households** benefit from Angola's cash-transfer programme, **Kwenda**, receiving quarterly government payments of ~25,500 kz (~$40). Launched in May 2020 and implemented by the government's **Social Support Fund (FAS)**, Kwenda promotes financial inclusion by delivering payments digitally for most beneficiaries and aims to reach **1.6 million low-income families** nationwide, 60% female-headed.

Per Emma Mercedes Monsalve Montiel, World Bank Task Team Leader for the Digitizing G2P payments initiative (G2Px) via the Strengthening of the National Social Protection System Project, Kwenda is Angola's first cash-transfer programme delivering social assistance in cash and digital payments — unlocking financial inclusion and women's economic empowerment.
