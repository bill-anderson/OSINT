Togo, Luxembourg Sign Three Agreements on Development and Cybersecurity

Economic governance Thursday, 16 July 2026 05:24

(Togo First) - Togo and Luxembourg signed three cooperation agreements in Lomé on Wednesday, July 15, 2026, aimed at deepening their partnership in environmental protection, development and digital transformation.

The agreements cover financing for the INCLURE environmental program, the Togo-Luxembourg Indicative Cooperation Program (PIC, its French acronym) and the TogoCyber+ cybersecurity program.

The documents were signed by Togo’s Minister of Foreign Affairs, Regional Integration and Togolese Abroad, Robert Dussey, and Luxembourg’s Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs and Foreign Trade, Xavier Bettel. Togo’s Minister of Public Service Reform, Labor and Social Dialogue, Cina Lawson, who is also responsible for digital transformation, attended the ceremony.

INCLURE aims to stimulate local economic growth, harness local resources and improve the conservation of protected ecosystems. The program is expected to generate income and economic opportunities for local communities while embedding the protection of natural heritage in development policies.

The Indicative Cooperation Program will provide a framework for dialogue, financing and support for Luxembourg’s cooperation initiatives in Togo. It is designed to align those initiatives with the priorities set by Togolese authorities and establish the basis for a long-term partnership.

TogoCyber+ will support the country’s rapid digital transformation by strengthening cybersecurity across digital public services, financial transactions and critical infrastructure.

Dussey said the agreements demonstrated the “tangible and dynamic” nature of cooperation between the two countries and reflected “a partnership based on continuity, political trust and shared priorities.”

Lawson said the signing confirmed “the central role that digital technology now plays in Togo’s development strategy.”

The agreements come as ties between Lomé and Luxembourg deepen. Luxembourg already supports several development initiatives in Togo, particularly in environmental protection, education and digital transformation.
