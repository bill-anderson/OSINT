(Togo First, Thursday 16 July 2026) — Togo and Luxembourg signed three cooperation agreements in Lomé on Wednesday, July 15, 2026, aimed at deepening their partnership in environmental protection, development and digital transformation.

The agreements cover financing for the INCLURE environmental program, the Togo-Luxembourg Indicative Cooperation Program (PIC) and the TogoCyber+ cybersecurity program. The documents were signed by Togo's Minister of Foreign Affairs, Robert Dussey, and Luxembourg's Deputy Prime Minister and Minister of Foreign Affairs, Xavier Bettel. Togo's Minister of Public Service Reform, Cina Lawson, who is also responsible for digital transformation, attended the ceremony.

TogoCyber+ will support the country's rapid digital transformation by strengthening cybersecurity across digital public services, financial transactions and critical infrastructure. The Indicative Cooperation Program will provide a framework for dialogue, financing and support for Luxembourg's cooperation initiatives in Togo.

Dussey said the agreements demonstrated the "tangible and dynamic" nature of cooperation between the two countries. Lawson said the signing confirmed "the central role that digital technology now plays in Togo's development strategy." Luxembourg already supports several development initiatives in Togo, particularly in environmental protection, education and digital transformation.
