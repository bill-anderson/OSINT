Parliamentary scrutiny of the national document-issuing body CNEDOGE. On 1 April 2025 the Chamber of Deputies examined the functioning, tariffs and issuance processes of the Centro Nacional de Emisión de Documentos de Guinea Ecuatorial. Director General Teodomiro Monsuy Eyí set out the entity's operational difficulties and answered deputies' questions on the issuance of identity documents and passports.

Fees discussed: passport first issue 7,500 XAF vs renewal 10,000 XAF; DIP (Documento de Identidad Personal) first issue 1,000 XAF, renewal 2,000 XAF — deputies were puzzled that renewal costs more than first issue. Monsuy Eyí explained that documents are not "owned" by CNEDOGE but pass through several processes ending at the Dirección General de Seguridad Nacional (its supervising body). Deputies raised concern over stock shortages of booklets ("libretas") and plates; the director explained CNEDOGE does not own the consumables and they do not come from the Treasury ("since Thursday there were no booklets available, but we validate applications so they can be printed when they arrive"). Deputies proposed reducing delays and expanding issuance services to more diplomatic offices/embassies abroad; the CNEDOGE report was approved.
