# Review of Somalia's Data Protection Act (2023) and implementing regulations

> ## Excerpt
> Analysis of the Data Protection Act (effective 23 Mar 2023), Somalia's first
> comprehensive data-protection law: scope, lawful bases, data-subject rights,
> DPIA/DPO obligations and registration of "controllers of major importance."
> Folds CIPIT/Strathmore's critique of the undefined "public data" category and DPA independence.

---

On March 23, 2023, the Data Protection Act (DPA) was enacted and immediately became effective throughout Somalia — the country's first comprehensive law on data protection. Founded on the Constitution of the Federal Republic of Somalia 2012, it established the Somalia Data Protection Authority to oversee implementation. The Authority was officially launched in February 2024, although it commenced activities before then. Under Article 41, the DPA issued the Implementation Regulation, DPA Guidance, and the Alternative Dispute Resolution Procedure.

Key provisions:
- Scope (Arts 4-5): applies to controllers based or operating in Somalia processing data within Somalia; exemptions for household purposes, public health emergencies, crime prevention/investigation, national security, and defence of a legal claim.
- Data Protection Authority (Arts 6, 7, 13): powers to issue regulations, investigate, impose fines, register controllers/processors of major importance, promote awareness, accredit compliance organisations, and advise government.
- Lawful bases (Art 14): consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interest, public interest, legitimate interest, plus additional bases (legal claim, public health/humanitarian emergency, medical care, archiving/statistics, data made public by the subject).
- Third-party risk (Art 19): controllers must maintain an inventory of processors and sub-processors and have processing agreements.
- Data subject rights (Arts 20-23): access, rectification, deletion, withdrawal of consent, objection, and not to be subject to solely automated decisions.
- Security (Art 24); Data Protection Impact Assessment (Art 29) for high-risk processing by controllers of major importance, submitted to the Authority before processing.
- DPO (Art 34): controllers/processors of major importance must appoint a competent DPO within six months where the entity is a governmental body, processes over 1,000 individuals' data annually, or regularly handles sensitive personal data.
- Annual auditing (DPA Guidance): audit reports required from controllers processing over 1,000 data subjects in six months.
- Mandatory registration (Art 32): only controllers of major importance register with the Authority within six months; renewable annually.

**Folded analysis — CIPIT / Strathmore University (2024-05-30), "Ushering In a New Dawn for Data Protection."** The Act came into effect on 23 March 2023, two days after presidential assent, amid a continental wave of data-governance legislation (61% adoption across Africa as of April 2024). It flows from Article 19 of the 2012 Somali Constitution (inviolability of the home). Two critiques stand out: (1) the Act introduces a novel category, **"public data," but fails to define it**, casting uncertainty over the Authority's mandate; and (2) on **independence** — although Article 6 declares the Authority independent, its General Manager is proposed by the Minister, confirmed by Cabinet and appointed by the President (Art 13), and Article 6(3) requires the Authority to yield to executive direction (from the Minister of Communications and Technology, the President or Cabinet) in matters of national security, public order and defence — a broad scope for executive influence (lens: sovereignty). Exemptions (Arts 5(1)-5(3)) cover personal/household use, controllers not deemed "of major importance" by the second anniversary (23 March 2025), authority processing (police, public-health emergency, national security), and journalistic/educational/artistic expression (comparable to s.52 of Kenya's Data Protection Act No. 24 of 2019).
