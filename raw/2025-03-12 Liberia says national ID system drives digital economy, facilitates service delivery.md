The **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** says the national ID system is central to Liberia's digital economy and that partnerships have driven card adoption. NIR Director of Media Relations Daniel Sloh Sargbe said the registry is "creating a foundation for the future of Liberia's digital economy."

As of December 2024, the NIR had registered around **740,000** citizens (aged 31-65, 59% male). Sargbe attributed growth to partnerships including an MoU with the **Civil Service Agency** (May 2024) allowing NINs for **payroll verification** — helping identify ghost workers draining the government payroll (as Ethiopia does with its digital ID) — and an MoU with the **LTA** (Oct 2024) making the national ID a requirement for **SIM registration**. Other partnerships cover the Ministry of Gender, Children and Social Protection (social schemes) and UNICEF (registering under-fives into the NBIS). Work is ongoing on the World Bank-supported **[[great-project-liberia|GREAT]]** project; President Boakai has called for an aggressive enrollment campaign.
