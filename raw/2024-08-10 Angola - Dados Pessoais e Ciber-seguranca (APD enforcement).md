Angola's data-protection legal framework has been in force since 2011: the **Data Protection Law (Lei n.º 22/11, LPDP)** and the **Law of Electronic Communications and Information Society Services (Lei n.º 23/11, LCE)**. The LPDP elaborates personal-data protection as an essential component of the constitutional right to privacy (art. 32(2)).

The **Angolan Data Protection Agency (APD)** was formally constituted in **2016** and has in recent years intensified supervision and enforcement. In **June and July 2024**, the APD published on its website the application of **sanctions against several companies** operating in Angola for serious LPDP violations — a marker that the authority is now active rather than nominal.
