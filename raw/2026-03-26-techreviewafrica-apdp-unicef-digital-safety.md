# Benin Data Protection Authority and UNICEF explore expanded partnership on digital safety

Date: 2026-03-26
By: Nana Appiah Acquaye

Autorité de Protection des Données Personnelles has held discussions with UNICEF Benin to strengthen collaboration on promoting personal data protection and enhancing digital safety, particularly for children.

The meeting took place during an audience granted by APDP President Luciano Hounkponou to the UNICEF representative in Benin, where both parties reviewed ongoing cooperation and explored new areas for partnership.

The collaboration between the two institutions previously led to a joint awareness campaign conducted in November 2025, targeting students at CEG Bohicon and Lycée des Jeunes Filles d'Abomey. The initiative focused on raising awareness about online violence and promoting responsible digital behaviour among young users.

During the engagement, UNICEF conveyed its appreciation for the APDP's leadership in advancing data protection initiatives and expressed interest in expanding joint programmes to reach more communities.

The APDP reaffirmed its commitment to deepening the partnership, emphasizing the importance of sustained collaboration in building a secure digital environment and strengthening the protection of personal data for children and other digital users in Benin.
