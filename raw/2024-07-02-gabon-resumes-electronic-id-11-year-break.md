After an 11-year gap, Gabon has introduced the National Electronic Identity Card (CNIE). Citizens can now apply for and receive the new digital ID card, issued initially only in Libreville, with plans to extend to all police stations nationwide.

The advance follows a $68.5 million World Bank loan for the "Digital Gabon" initiative to improve access to legal identity verification and digital government services. The government intends to make the national ID card a valid travel document within CEMAC (six Central African nations); since 2008 CEMAC has permitted visa-free travel among members for holders of the CEMAC biometric passport, though implementation has faced challenges. The card is recognized for banking services and administrative processes.

Gabon commenced a civil registry issuance campaign in May 2024 in the Libreville region, setting up registration centers for citizens to acquire their Numéro d'Identification Personnel (NIP), essential for the CNIE. History: in 2012 the government initiated a biometric national registry project awarded to Thales (France); the first cards were issued in 2013 but faced setbacks; Belgian firm Semlex was contracted for issuance but the contract was terminated. Thales is reported to be taking over the contract, setting up equipment and training personnel.
