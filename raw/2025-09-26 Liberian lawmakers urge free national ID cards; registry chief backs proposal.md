Members of Liberia's House of Representatives are pushing to make the mandatory National Identification Cards **free of charge**, amid chaos and frustration over registration. The **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** charges US$5 per card to access services including banking and government services; lawmakers say the fee, though modest, is out of reach for many (a 2019 World Bank report found 3 of 10 in Monrovia and 8 of 10 in rural areas live in poverty).

Representative Moima Briggs-Mensah (Bong) triggered the hearing; Representative Ivar Jones (Margibi) referred the matter to committees. NIR ED Andrew Peters testified he had no objection to making the program free if the government shoulders the cost. Peters said only **16%** of Liberia's ~5.5 million people have enrolled; the NIR processes only 5,000 ID cards daily (nearly three years to serve all eligible), constrained by poor roads, limited postal services and logistics. Plans include 100 enrollment centres and six printing machines, funded through the World Bank's $30 million **[[great-project-liberia|GREAT]]** project; an earlier deadline was extended by 76 days.
