Zambia has launched GovLink Private 4G, a government-owned digital connectivity network designed to improve internet access across public institutions and strengthen digital government service delivery. Unveiled at Ndola Teaching Hospital, the initiative is being implemented by SMART Zambia Institute's e-Government Division, in partnership with IHM Southern Africa, Tuso Business Dynamics, Quadrupleplay, and the Ministry of Health.

GovLink aims to close Zambia's digital connectivity gap by linking hospitals, schools, and other public institutions through secure, shared national infrastructure. The health sector has been designated as the flagship for the rollout, with major digital health platforms, including Care 2.0 (piloted at Ndola Teaching Hospital, Arthur Davison Children's Hospital, and Kitwe Teaching Hospital) and SmartCare Pro (Zambia's electronic health records system already deployed in more than 2,000 facilities) migrating to the new network.

SMART Zambia Institute's National Coordinator, Percy Chinyama—speaking on behalf of Government Chief Technical Officer, Kasali Musenge—called GovLink a critical enabler of Zambia's digital transformation, connecting hospitals, schools, and farming communities through a government-owned platform. He highlighted the Copperbelt Province as a model for digital transformation, nearing full public-sector connectivity through government and private-sector collaboration.

The Ministry of Health's Permanent Secretary, Dr. Kennedy Lishimpi, called the launch a milestone in the country's digital health strategy, noting that reliable connectivity is essential to unlocking the full potential of systems like SmartCare, supporting electronic health records, telemedicine, and real-time clinical decision-making.

Health officials at the pilot sites reported early benefits: Dr. Justo Banda from Ndola Teaching Hospital said clinicians can now access patient records, radiology reports, and diagnostics electronically, improving efficiency and care quality, while Copperbelt Provincial's Health Director, Dr. Charles Mwinuna, said dependable connectivity will help reduce wait times and strengthen referral and supply chain systems.

Once fully deployed, GovLink is expected to provide unified digital infrastructure for government institutions, supporting secure, citizen-centered public services while expanding access to underserved communities across Zambia.
