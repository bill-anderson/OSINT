World Bank publication (25 June 2024). The Equatorial Guinea Digital Economy Country Diagnostic assesses the five foundational pillars of the Digital Economy for Africa (DE4A) initiative — digital infrastructure, digital public platforms, digital financial services, digital businesses, and digital skills — developed with the Government to support diversification away from oil.

Context: the National Development Plan 2035 (2021) introduced technology and innovation as a cross-cutting theme; the government is implementing the **Digital Agenda for Equatorial Guinea (ADIGE)**, with four objectives — universalize telecom access, digitize administrative procedures, support the ICT sector, and empower citizens.

Key findings: broadband affordability is improving but regulatory challenges, limited competition and cybersecurity weaknesses persist. A **digital civil-registry pilot launched in 2018** is a milestone for digital public platforms, but the regulatory framework needs strengthening and **digital identity is not yet implemented**. Digital financial services are nascent, with **no licensed e-money operator for mobile money**. Recommendations: cost-oriented obligations in wholesale fibre markets, a cybersecurity maturity assessment, a national strategy for digital public platforms, strengthening DFS agent networks, and a national ICT education policy. Cross-cutting priorities: data quality, institutional coordination, reliable electricity, the legal/regulatory framework, and bridging the island-continental and gender divides.
