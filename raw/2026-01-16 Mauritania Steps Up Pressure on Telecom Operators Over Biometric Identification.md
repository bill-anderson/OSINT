Mauritania's Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (ARE) announced on 15 January 2026 that it issued formal notices to telecom operators Mattel, Mauritel and Chinguitel over failures in subscriber-identification procedures found during inspections. Operators were given a two-month deadline before sanctions.

Under Mauritanian law, operators must carry out biometric identification of subscribers nationwide, conditioning the sale/activation of any SIM, USIM or eSIM on prior identification, and must protect subscribers' personal data. Non-compliance can lead to formal notice then sanctions — suspension, reduction or withdrawal of licences/authorisations or frequency rights — plus financial penalties up to 3% of annual turnover (5% for repeat offences; capped at 10 million ouguiyas / ~$251,320 if turnover is undeterminable, rising to 20 million for further violations). Authorities frame the policy as "national and citizen security" amid rising mobile fraud; in October 2023 ARE ordered deactivation of SIM cards lacking biometric identification. Mauritania counts about 6.37 million mobile subscriptions (DataReportal).
