With 41 days to Uganda's January 2026 general election, the government signalled possible social media restrictions, citing platforms' role in inciting chaos. The Electoral Commission (EC) confirmed it met representatives of major international social media platforms, lodging a formal complaint that the platforms are "unruly" and "ungovernable" and often used to incite chaos. African Union election observers attended the engagement, which aimed to harmonize guidelines for social media use during elections.

Acting EC Secretary Ronald Kamugisha said government asked the platforms to "whip their users," which they declined, "saying they do not want to annoy their social platform users." Government explained this is why it repeatedly switches off the internet during elections. The EC stressed its biometric voter-verification and ballot systems operate independently of the internet switch-off, though voter mistrust over ballot privacy persists.

Historical context: In January 2021, before the general elections, the government ordered a widespread internet shutdown blocking Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, WhatsApp and others, followed by a complete blackout. On restoration, all platforms except Facebook were reopened; as of 2024, Facebook remains officially restricted in Uganda (accessed by many via VPNs). UCC has warned that using VPNs to circumvent shutdowns is illegal.
