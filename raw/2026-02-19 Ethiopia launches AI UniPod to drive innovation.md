Ethiopia has launched an Artificial Intelligence University Innovation Pod (AI UniPod) in the country's capital, Addis Ababa.

Ethiopian dignitaries at the launch of the AI Unipod in Addis Ababa. (Source: Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia Government Communication Service)

Ethiopia has launched an Artificial Intelligence University Innovation Pod (AI UniPod) in Addis Ababa, a national innovation center designed to strengthen national capacities in AI and accelerate the development of local technological solutions.

Ethiopia's Deputy Prime Minister, Temesgen Tiruneh, announced the launch of the AI UniPod, a collaborative initiative by the Ethiopian Artificial Intelligence Institute, Addis Ababa University, and the UN Development Programme, in partnership with the Timbuktoo Initiative.

He said the facility is designed to serve as a center for innovation, research and talent development in AI.

Ethiopia's Deputy Prime Minister, Temesgen Tiruneh. (Source: Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia Government Communication Service)

At the launch Tiruneh said the country will not stand on the sidelines of the AI revolution but will help shape it.

"In this century, sovereignty means more than borders, it means owning our data, designing our algorithms and solving our challenges with our own solutions. Ethiopia is choosing to build its future on its own terms," he continued.

Tiruneh added that, in line with the Digital Ethiopia 2030 strategy and the country's economic reform agenda, the initiative showcases the creativity of Ethiopia's youth and the power of collaborative partnerships.

## Africa's AI initiatives

African countries have been working towards meeting AI targets and partnering with international companies to assist in meeting those goals.

In November 2025 at the G20 Leaders' Summit in Johannesburg, the United Arab Emirates (UAE) announced [an investment of US$1 billion](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/uae-to-invest-1b-to-expand-african-ai-infrastructure) to expand AI infrastructure and AI-enabled services across Africa.

Orange Group's cybersecurity arm, Cyberdefense, partnered with French cybersecurity startup Qevlar AI in October 2025 to [fight cybercrime across the African continent](https://www.connectingafrica.com/cybersecurity/orange-qevlar-ai-to-boost-cyber-security-in-africa).

The previous month, Google announced more investments in Africa, including four strategic subsea cable connectivity hubs, [free access to AI tools for students](https://www.connectingafrica.com/investment/google-invests-more-toward-ai-skills-subsea-cable-connectivity), and another $9 million for African universities and research institutions.

In July 2025, Google announced $37 million in funding and partnerships in Africa that aims [to strengthen AI research](http://vhttps//www.connectingafrica.com/ai/google-commits-37m-to-advancing-ai-in-africa), support African languages, improve food systems and expand digital skills.

In September 2024, Google and the Zambian government partnered to set up an [AI Center of Excellence](https://www.connectingafrica.com/ai/google-to-open-ai-hub-in-zambia-malawi-plans-smart-village-initiative) at the University of Zambia (UNZA).
