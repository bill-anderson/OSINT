# SoleCrypt plans 20MW solar powered data center in Bizerte, Tunisia

February 6, 2026 — Data Center Dynamics (Zachary Skidmore)

Anglo-Tunisian data center and solar developer SoleCrypt is planning to build a 20MW data center in Bizerte, in northern Tunisia, in partnership with Schneider Electric.

According to the companies, the data center will be located at the landing point for three submarine cables, including the 8,700km Medusa subsea cable, which spans 19 landing sites across Europe and North Africa. The companies said the project will be powered by 100 percent renewable energy, generated by a 60MW solar project in the Tozeur desert. The solar project, known as site T60, is being developed by SoleCrypt and is currently under development, with the company achieving operational status at the site's base camp in November.

The data center is part of a broader partnership between SoleCrypt and Schneider, with the two companies signing a non-binding Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) to design and develop next-generation data centers dedicated to AI, integrating high-energy-efficiency and low-water-consumption architectures across Tunisia.

"Our project positions Tunisia as a true digital bridge between Africa and Europe, serving industrial players, fintechs, and artificial intelligence laboratories. We want to work with Schneider Electric to transform Tunisia into a Mediterranean super hub for sustainable digital infrastructure," said Amir Ben Gacem, CEO of SoleCrypt.

Under the terms of the MoU, SoleCrypt intends to provide strategic sites across Tunisia with strong connectivity, enabling sub-millisecond latency to Europe. Schneider will act as the key technology advisor, supplying AI data center reference architectures co-developed with Nvidia, as well as electrification solutions, secure power systems, liquid cooling, and intelligent infrastructure management through EcoStruxure IT. Launched in 2022, SoleCrypt focuses on sustainable digital infrastructure development.
