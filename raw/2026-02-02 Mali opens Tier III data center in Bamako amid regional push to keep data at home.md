Malian authorities inaugurated a Tier III data centre in Bamako on Saturday 31 January 2026, during the third edition of Mali's Digital Week. Described as strategic infrastructure, it aims to strengthen digital sovereignty by encouraging local storage of public and sensitive data previously hosted abroad. Minister Alhamdou Ag Ilyène called it "a decisive step toward safeguarding and securing national data."

As a Tier III facility it guarantees 99.982% service availability through full redundancy and uninterrupted continuity even during maintenance; it will host e-government platforms and strategic state databases, and eventually private-sector digital services. A gradual repatriation of data hosted abroad is planned. The inauguration comes amid accelerating AES digital investments — Burkina Faso also inaugurated infrastructure a few days earlier, and Niger announced plans to acquire high-capacity data centres. The article notes digital sovereignty extends beyond infrastructure to applications, operating systems, equipment and service providers, which remain largely foreign-dominated.
