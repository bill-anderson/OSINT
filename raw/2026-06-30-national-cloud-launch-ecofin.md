# Mauritania — national cloud launched (digital sovereignty)

*Off-assigned-host capture (Ecofin Agency); event corroborated by Mauritanian outlets errakb.info and ghabess.net (29 June–1 July 2026). Major net-new development.*

Mauritania **officially launched its national cloud platform on 29 June 2026 in Nouakchott**, at the **National Data Center**, to host government data and digital public services domestically. The national cloud provides government agencies with shared computing resources (networking, servers, storage, applications), aiming to gradually centralise government digital services rather than maintaining separate IT infrastructure per institution. The platform is built on the Nouakchott National Data Center — a **Tier III-certified facility developed under the West Africa Regional Digital Integration Program (WARDIP)**.

The Ministry of Digital Transformation has not announced a migration timeline, nor disclosed storage capacity or total cost. Mauritanian-outlet coverage notes World Bank resident representative Ibou Diouf, the head of the personal-data protection authority (APD) and the WARDIP coordinator attended.

Context: the National Digital Transition Agenda 2022–2025 targets a digital-sector contribution of ~$700m (~8% of GDP); the ministry's 2026 budget is $24.2m (up 104.6%); IsDB talks are underway for $50m more. Mauritania ranked 165th on the 2024 UN EGDI; internet penetration reached 37.4% at the start of 2026 (DataReportal). The National Cybersecurity Strategy 2022–2025 (including a planned national CERT/CSIRT) is still being implemented.
