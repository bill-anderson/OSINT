# African countries are already building the foundational infrastructure required for the digital future

Companion write-up of a **DIAL** blog post (2 May 2025) reporting the second **Africa Data Leadership
Initiative (ADLI)** peer-learning session on DPI, with guest speakers Dr Talkmore Chidede (AfCFTA
Senior Digital Trade Expert) and Robert Karanja (Co-Develop). Pan-African discussion of DPI
exemplars and lessons; on-the-record reporting/analysis citing primary sources.

## DPI exemplars discussed (attributed to the ADLI session, as of May 2025)
- **Rwanda** — long-running open-source-first strategy (Smart Rwanda Master Plan 2015–2020); citizen
  portal [[irembo]]; Mojaloop-based inclusive payments ([[mojaloop]]).
- **Ghana** — enabling-environment focus; fintech/startup ecosystem; plans to open-source parts of
  its stack.
- **Tanzania** — homegrown **Jamii** stack (Jamii Namba ID, Jamii Malipo payments, Jamii X-Change),
  slated to be open-sourced.
- **Somalia** — open-source **GovNet** inter-agency exchange (15 ministries) and instant High School
  Certificate System despite low connectivity.
- **Ethiopia** — Digital Ethiopia 2025; **Fayda** national ID; state-backed Telebirr mobile money.
- **Morocco** — GENIE education-digitisation programme.

## Takeaways (ADLI, May 2025)
Local ownership of digital systems is paramount (open source over vendor lock-in); integrate rather
than sunset legacy systems (e.g. M-Pesa); cross-border payments need close regulator engagement; DPI
presupposes baseline energy/connectivity but people innovate around gaps. Reinforces the
[[sovereignty]] lens on African DPI. Related: [[adli]], [[dial]],
[[afcfta]].
