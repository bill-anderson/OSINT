# Why Africa Should Lead on Governing Global Tech Giants (CIGI PB226)

> ## Excerpt
> Andrew Heffernan argues Africa is uniquely positioned to *lead* on regulating US tech giants — which
> dominate its markets (Google ~90% search, Facebook ~85% social) while extracting data with little local
> reinvestment ("data colonialism") — via a pan-African, AU-led governance framework.

> **Provenance note.** Think-tank synthesis; its data points (market share, user counts, GDP,
> ratifications) trace to the primaries it cites. Malabo ratification count (15 as of 2023) is dated.

## Key facts

- African internet users 181m (2014) → 646m (2024), projected >1.1bn by 2029; 60%+ under 25; digital
  economy up to **$180bn** of GDP. Reviews national laws (NDPR 2019, Kenya DPA 2021, **[[popia]]**) and the
  **[[malabo-convention]]** — all GDPR-derived, weak enforcement, slow ratification. Recommends a
  pan-African AU-led framework, stronger DPAs, digital-inclusion investment, local-ecosystem development
  and an African Digital Trade and Technology Forum.
