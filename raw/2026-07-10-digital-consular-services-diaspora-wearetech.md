# Mauritania — digital consular-services platform for the diaspora

Mauritania **launched a suite of digital services for citizens living abroad on Thursday, 9 July 2026** in Nouakchott. Foreign Affairs Minister Mohamed Salem Ould Merzoug and Digital Transformation Minister Ahmed Salem Ould Beddah presented the initiative to modernise consular operations and expand remote access to administrative procedures.

It includes **three digital platforms**: a consular-services portal; a platform for Mauritanian communities abroad and the promotion of diaspora professional skills; and a website for the Forum of Mauritanian Communities. Users can complete procedures online, track applications and receive notifications.

The **first phase offers nine online services**, including consular registration, consular cards, exceptional travel documents, powers of attorney, document authentication, administrative certificates and online appointment booking. The project — designed after consultations with Mauritanian communities in 22 countries across four continents, and built by diaspora Mauritanian engineers with MTNIMA — also provides for a **unified digital consular registry** to improve diaspora mapping (skills, geographic distribution) and support public policymaking.
