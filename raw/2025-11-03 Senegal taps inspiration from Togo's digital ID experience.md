The Senegalese government is hoping to tap Togo's experience to build its digital ID system. A delegation from Dakar was in Lome recently to understudy Togo's model and understand how it has been implementing its Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) over the years.

Togo is understood to be making considerable progress implementing a MOSIP-based digital ID system since signing an MoU in 2021. The country was also acclaimed for building a platform dubbed Novissi that facilitated government-to-people payments as part of a social protection measure at the peak of the coronavirus pandemic.

The delegation from Senegal's Ministry of Communication and Digital Economy (NCTN) met officials of Togo's National Identification Authority (ANID) to discuss best practices for building a secure, trustworthy digital ID system (per TechAfrica News). Both countries are part of WURI (West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion), a World Bank-supported regional ID initiative to build robust foundational ID systems, drive financial inclusion, and streamline access to services.

ANID official Yasmine Mama is quoted by Ecofin Agency as saying they shared information about their technical architecture and infrastructure, and how they integrated the MOSIP-based digital ID system to facilitate access to services. The peer learning is vital for Senegal, which is implementing its New Deal Technologique, a digital transformation strategy launched by its president early in 2025, supported by the Gates Foundation with $10 million. Other bilateral DPI partnerships cited include Zambia-Ethiopia and Togo-Kazakhstan, plus the 50-in-5 campaign.
