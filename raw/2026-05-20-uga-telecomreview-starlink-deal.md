Uganda has officially authorized Starlink to begin operations in the country following the signing of a memorandum of understanding (MoU) and operational license agreement between the Uganda Communications Commission (UCC) and the satellite internet provider. The agreement marks an important step in Uganda's strategy to strengthen digital infrastructure and broaden internet access nationwide.

President Yoweri Kaguta Museveni, who witnessed the signing ceremony, described the agreement as a positive development for Uganda's telecommunications industry.

Our interest is security, revenue assurance, and proper accountability within the telecommunications sector so that we know who is operating and who the customers are.

The President also welcomed Starlink's commitment to comply with Uganda's legal and regulatory requirements ahead of its planned commercial launch.

The agreement was signed by UCC Executive Director, Nyombi Thembo, and representatives from SpaceX, led by Ryan Goodnight. Following the signing, UCC issued the certification required to support Starlink's rollout in the Ugandan market.

As part of the licensing conditions, Starlink will be required to establish a national gateway, maintain a physical point of presence, and open an operational office in Uganda with dedicated technical and legal staff. These measures intend to ensure regulatory compliance while supporting the company's integration into the country's telecom ecosystem.

The ceremony was also attended by senior government officials and diplomats, including Permanent Secretary at the Ministry of ICT and National Guidance, Dr. Aminah Zawedde; United States Ambassador to Uganda, William Popp; and Uganda's Ambassador to the United States, Robinah Kakonge.

Starlink's entry is expected to improve internet access across Uganda, particularly in remote and underserved communities where conventional broadband infrastructure remains limited. Through its low-Earth orbit satellite technology, the company aims to deliver faster and more reliable internet connectivity to areas with longstanding access challenges.

Speaking during the ceremony, Ryan Goodnight described the agreement as an important milestone for both Starlink and Uganda.

It is a great honor to be here in this beautiful country. We are incredibly excited to build this cooperation and bring this network to Uganda.

Goodnight noted that Starlink plans to help lower internet costs while enabling more people, businesses, and institutions to participate in the digital economy through dependable connectivity.

In addition to its commercial rollout, the company announced plans to support Uganda's education and healthcare sectors by donating internet connectivity devices to selected schools and health facilities across the country.

The Ministry of ICT and National Guidance welcomed the development, describing it as an opportunity to accelerate digital inclusion, support innovation, and unlock new socio-economic opportunities through expanded access to digital services.

As Uganda continues advancing its digital transformation agenda, Starlink's arrival is expected to strengthen national connectivity, enhance digital infrastructure, and connect more communities across the country.
