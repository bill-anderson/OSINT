## AWS in Africa

Thousands of active customers, from Absa, Investec, Old Mutual, Pick n Pay, University of Pretoria and Standard Bank use AWS to deliver flexibility, scalability and reliability.

## What's new

### [AWS re/Start](https://aws.amazon.com/training/restart/)

AWS re/Start is a skills development and job training program that prepares unemployed individuals for careers in the cloud. The program’s mission is to build diverse local talent by providing a pathway to become an AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner and connecting graduates with job opportunities. In October 2020, AWS re/Start launched a cohort in South Africa, Nigeria, and Ghana.

Learn more

### [B-BBEE Certificate](https://pages.awscloud.com/rs/112-TZM-766/images/ELC13936_Amazon%20Web%20Services_BEE%20Certificate_Final.pdf)

Amazon Web Services South Africa has achieved B-BBEE Level 1 Contributor Status for the seventh consecutive year. Achieving B-BBEE Level 1 status demonstrates our continued commitment, and long-term investment in South Africa.

View the Certificate

### [The new AWS Africa (Cape Town) Region is now open](https://aws.amazon.com/local/africa/cape-town/)

Starting today, developers, startups, and enterprises, as well as government, education, and non-profit organizations can run their applications and serve end-users with even lower latency, from data centers located in Africa, as well as leverage advanced AWS technologies to drive innovation. To see a complete list of AWS services available in the AWS Africa (Cape Town) Region please visit the AWS Region Table. Pricing for the AWS Africa (Cape Town) Region is available on the detail page for each respective AWS service.
