# DARE1 subsea cable to link Kenya to South Africa

*Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa — September 1, 2025*

Djibouti Telecom has announced plans to extend the Djibouti Africa Regional Express 1 (DARE1) subsea cable southward along Africa's east coast to broaden its reach and enhance route diversity.

Djibouti Telecom has announced plans to extend its regional subsea cable, the Djibouti Africa Regional Express 1 (DARE1), southward along Africa's east coast adding new landings in Tanzania, Mozambique, Madagascar and South Africa.

The subsea cable expansion, announced late last week, is intended to increase reach and improve route diversity, building on the success of DARE1, which connects Djibouti, Somalia and Kenya.

In a LinkedIn post, Djibouti Telecom said the DARE1 cable system has been operational since 2021 and currently runs along the Horn of Africa connecting Djibouti City with Bosaso and Mogadishu in Somalia and Mombasa, Kenya with 36 Tbit/s of capacity over three fiber pairs.

The telco added that the subsea cable will be expanded with a new 3,200km-3,500km link from Mombasa all the way down to Mtunzini in South Africa, with branches planned for Tanzania, Mozambique and Madagascar.

It said work on the extension is expected to begin in 2026, with a target ready-for-service date in 2028.

According to Djibouti Telecom, the system is built to provide high-capacity, low-latency and resilient connectivity, promoting the expansion of cloud, content and digital services while bolstering regional integration.

It added that DARE1's extension will increase capacity and diversify routes for cloud providers, carriers and businesses from East to Southern Africa.

## Africa's subsea cable boom

The DARE1 expansion comes at a time when many other subsea cable systems are connecting African communities.

In August 2025, Telecom Egypt and telecoms service provider NaiTel completed the laying of the express subsea cable Coral Bridge connecting Egypt and Jordan.

There are a number of cables surrounding the continent and several systems linking Africa to other regions.

July 2025 saw the signing of a memorandum of understandings between Algérie Télécom and Sparkle as well as Telecom Egypt and SubCom completing the dual landings of the SEA-ME-WE-6 subsea cable system on both of Egypt's coasts.

There has been growth from newer systems like Meta-backed 2Africa and Google-backed Equiano adding to more established systems like the West Africa Cable System (WACS), South Atlantic-3 (Sat-3) and Africa Coast to Europe (ACE).
