## Description

The vote was **restructured**: the Konza/BPO capital-transfer line disappears (its successor, ICT
Technical Support Services, carries 150m), E-Government splits into four function-named
sub-programmes, and a wholly new programme 0222000 puts cyber and data protection on their own
lines. Budget-day thematic: KSh 8.6bn "digital superhighway and creative economy" - the second
consecutive cut (16.3bn -> 12.7bn -> 8.6bn) - with KSh 4.3bn of it the World Bank-backed KDEAP
(externally financed - excluded from domestic-state totals at the origin gate; definite-match to
the held KDEAP deal record at full extract). **No Konza DC/KAIST allocation was disclosed at
budget day** (Business Daily, 2026-06-11) - not established whether it moved votes or ended.
