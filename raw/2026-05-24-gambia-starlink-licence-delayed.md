# Starlink Licence Gets Delayed By The Gambia

By Collins Eshiet — Orbital Today — 24 May 2026

SpaceX's wait for its Starlink licence to legalise its operations in The Gambia has been extended, despite making payments for an operating permit in February 2026. This delay comes together with concerns over the security and economic effects Starlink's operations will have within the African nation.

## Lack Of Oversight Infrastructure, Wrong Form

Over the past years, Starlink has been growing its presence in Africa, with more countries granting it operating permissions. However, things haven't been all rosy for the satellite connectivity provider, as African nations like The Gambia seem to be a hard nut to crack.

Speaking on Starlink's operation permit, which was paid for in February 2026, the Gambia's Minister of Digital Economy Hon Lamin Jabbie, said: "I refused to approve the permit." His reason for this decision was Starlink's lack of "competencies and equipment to operate a VSAT permit."

According to the minister, to operate in the Gambia, Starlink needs a general satellite licence and not a VSAT permit. But the issues don't just stop with Starlink not being granted a VSAT permit or needing a general satellite licence.

Some citizens of the Gambia argue that the nation doesn't have the structures to regulate Starlink's operations, while others raise concerns over the nation's economy. Kebba Bojang, Director General of Gamtel, asked, "How could we supervise a network present in the sky above our heads, but with no infrastructure in the country? To whom should we complain, report, prevent, and punish acts of cybercrime?"

Many fear that Starlink's operations might be able to knock local service providers out of business.

### What's Next For Starlink In The Gambia?

By operating in the Gambia, Starlink might be able to provide a solution to an existing issue that citizens have identified: the slow and expensive internet service. However, solving this issue won't be possible without proper licensing from the government of the Gambia.

For now, the delay to grant Starlink the licence to operate in the Gambia is temporary. While the next step isn't clear for both sides, more details on the progress of Starlink's efforts to enter the Gambia will become available in future.
