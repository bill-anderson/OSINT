On 27 June 2025 in Niamey, Niger's Minister of State for the Interior, General Mohamed Toumba, signed two memorandums of understanding with Wissal Samlali, director of the Libyan firm Al Itissal Al Jadeed, which specialises in biometric and digital identity solutions, setting up a public-private partnership to revamp Niger's identity documents.

The first MoU covers the design, financing, construction and maintenance of a system to issue electronic biometric passports, made of polycarbonate with secure chips. The second launches a new electronic national identity card (e-ID) meeting international security standards and enabling secure online identification. Niger aims to build a digital ecosystem where secure identification supports online authentication, e-government, banking, social protection and border control, while ensuring fair access, legal protections and citizen data security.
