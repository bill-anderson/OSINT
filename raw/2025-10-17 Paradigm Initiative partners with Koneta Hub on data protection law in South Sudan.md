# Press Release: Paradigm Initiative Partners with Koneta Hub to Drive Dialogue on Data Protection Law in South Sudan

> ## Excerpt
> A two-day Juba convening (Oct 2025) by Paradigm Initiative and Koneta Hub gathered lawmakers, CSOs and officials to improve the draft Data Protection Bill and secure parliamentary commitment to table it; the NCA's Chol Joseph Mayen keynoted.

---

Friday, 17 October 2025: Paradigm Initiative, together with its partner Koneta Hub, hosted a two-day convening bringing together lawmakers, tech and policy experts, civil society organisations (CSOs), the private sector, and government officials to discuss South Sudan's Data Protection Law.

The aim of the convening, held in Juba, was to foster dialogue around the need for a robust data protection framework in the country, increase awareness among Members of Parliament and other stakeholders on the relevance of data protection, document policy recommendations to improve the draft Data Protection Bill, and secure parliamentary commitment to table and debate the Bill in accordance with human rights standards.

The convening comes at a time when South Sudan is experiencing increased dependence on digital platforms for communication, financial transactions, humanitarian operations and governance, creating urgent data protection requirements. Without clear safeguards, citizens, businesses and institutions remain vulnerable to surveillance, data misuse and privacy violations.

In his keynote address, Mr Chol Joseph Mayen, Director of Research and Planning at the National Communication Authority, said the forum was timely, playing an important role in building trust in the digital ecosystem while ensuring the country's Data Protection Law is people-centred, forward-looking and responsive to the national context. "This dialogue could not have come at a better time for South Sudan," he stated.

Miriam Beatrice Wanjiru, Paradigm Initiative's (PIN's) Programmes Officer for East Africa, said the convening presented a strategic moment for intervention as parliamentarians gear up for the Bill. Denaya Dennis, Executive Director of Koneta, pointed out that the country lacks a comprehensive legal and institutional framework for the protection of personal data, while existing ICT regulations are limited and unable to adequately address privacy challenges.

The forum was held under PIN's Digital Policy Engagement Series (DiPES) initiative, which provides a platform for CSOs, lawyers, and parliamentarians to engage, evaluate and influence policies related to technology, digital rights and inclusion. The South Sudan edition brought together stakeholders to review the draft bill, contribute technical input and co-create a roadmap toward a rights-based law that can be implemented.
