World Bank ID4D diagnostic of Angola's identification ecosystem — civil registration (CR), the civil **National ID card (Bilhete de Identidade, BI)**, and their integration.

**Key evidence:**
- Angola's **civil-registration and identification services began in 2009 and 2002** respectively; much of the civil-registration system's design dates from **colonial times**. The country **remains far from universal identification coverage**.
- **Birth-registration gaps are severe:** a large share of children aged 1–4, and **78% of children under age 1, had no birth registration** (2014 baseline data) — with wide gaps by residence (rural worse), socioeconomic quintile and province.
- Births are registered through the **RCI** system, issuing a birth receipt (*Boletim de Nascimento*); civil-registration units are unevenly distributed (large average area/population per unit in some provinces).
- The diagnostic argues that **boosting ID coverage is critical so that digitalization does not exacerbate exclusion** — the through-line for Angola's later DPI push (newborn ID under 'Born with a birth certificate', the India DPI/Aadhaar model, and the Chinese-devised civil-registration rollout).
