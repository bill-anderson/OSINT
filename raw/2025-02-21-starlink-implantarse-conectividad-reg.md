Earliest 2025 signal of Starlink's interest in Equatorial Guinea. At a meeting in Washington DC between Equatoguinean diplomats (led by mission head Crisantos Obama Ondo) and Starlink representatives, Starlink's global licensing manager Brandi Oliver presented the company's low-latency satellite internet service and its potential to improve connectivity, especially in remote areas. Obama Ondo stressed GNQ's socio-political stability as a guarantee for investment. Technical work was subsequently initiated to facilitate Starlink's installation, per instructions of Vice-President Teodoro Nguema Obiang Mangue.
