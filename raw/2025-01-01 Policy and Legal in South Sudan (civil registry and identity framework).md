# Policy & Legal in South Sudan

> ## Excerpt

**Civil registration and legal identity.** A critical milestone on statelessness was reached with South Sudan's deposit of the instruments of accession to the 1954 Convention relating to the Status of Stateless Persons and the 1961 Convention on the Reduction of Statelessness in **November 2024**. The Government also adopted the **Civil Registry Regulations (2024) on 4 December 2024**, to support the operationalisation of the Civil Registry under the **Civil Registry Act of 2018**. In July 2024, South Sudan drafted the Anti-Trafficking in Persons Bill (2024).

**Foundational instruments.** The Transitional Constitution of the Republic of South Sudan, 2011 (as amended) enshrines rights and freedoms benefiting all persons within the territory. The Refugee Act, 2012 (No. 20) was adopted on 5 June 2012, establishing the Commission for Refugee Affairs (CRA), the Refugee Eligibility Committee (REC) and the Refugee Appeals Board (RAB); the 2017 Refugee Status and Eligibility Regulations complement it, alongside the 2019 National Comprehensive Migration Policy.

**Regional integration.** South Sudan acceded to the 1951 Refugee Convention and its 1967 Protocol in September 2018, and to the 1969 OAU Refugee Convention in June 2016. It ratified the 2009 African Union Convention on the Protection and Assistance of Internally Displaced Persons (Kampala Convention) in June 2019 and is progressing a draft "Protection and Assistance to Internally Displaced Persons, Bill 2024." South Sudan joined the **East African Community (EAC) in 2016** (including the Common Market Protocol on free movement of persons) and is a member of IGAD. In October 2024, with IGAD support, the Government adopted the National Durable Solutions Strategy for IDPs, Returnees, Refugees and Host Communities.
