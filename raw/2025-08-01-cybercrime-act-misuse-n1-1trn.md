Despite the Nigerian Cybercrimes (Prevention, Prohibition, etc.) and Amendment Act of 2024, Nigeria's cyberspace faces escalating attacks inflicting substantial losses on banks, telcos and government institutions — the financial sector lost N53.4bn to cybercriminals in 2024. Civil society activists and lawyers argue enforcement agencies are misapplying the Act to suppress dissent (harassing social critics) rather than bolstering cyber security; police deny this. Prominent SAN Kunle Edun said most such cases are never concluded because complainants (government officials, wealthy individuals) never testify, calling it abuse of power. The piece calls for reimagining Nigeria's cybercrime enforcement architecture.
