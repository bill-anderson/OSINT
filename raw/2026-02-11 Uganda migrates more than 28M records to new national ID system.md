The buried lede is the enrolment split: renewals 14.3m of 15.8m (90.5%) against first-time enrolments of only 6.4m of 17.2m (37.3%), with 10.1m cards printed but only 2.5m collected.*

The Registrar of Uganda's National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA), Claire Ollama, says the country has fully transitioned to its new national digital ID system with the migration of more than 28 million identity records from the legacy system. Ollama disclosed this on 9 February during a press conference reviewing the country's mass ID registration and renewal exercise, which started in May 2025 and targeted over 33 million people.

UAE-based IT company Tahaluf is the service provider for the new national ID system. Five modules are based on MOSIP, including ID registration, renewals, updating and correction, issuance and lost ID replacement services. Uganda's technology providers also include Tech5, Technoforte, Entrust and Hyf, NIRA ED Rosemary Kisembo said during MOSIP Connect 2026.

Speaking at the Police Headquarters in Kampala (reported by New Vision), Ollama said the ID records migration, plus new registrations and updates, takes the total number of ID enrollments in Uganda's population database to more than 35 million. "With the migrated data from the old system and the new people registered, the new national register has over 35 million Ugandans who can be uniquely identified via biometrics," she said.

For ID renewals, 14.3 million renewals were done of a target of 15.8 million (a 90.5 percent success rate), while only 6.4 million first-time enrollments were recorded from a target of 17.2 million (37.3 percent). Although the first phase officially ended at the parish level, the process will continue as a routine activity across NIRA offices. Of 10.1 million cards printed so far, only 2.5 million have been picked up (over 60 percent still uncollected).

State Minister for Internal Affairs Gen. David Muhoozi assured citizens that measures were being taken to resolve concerns including system integration and QR-code scanning failures on some new cards, and said the ID authentication module would be fully operational by the end of March. He also addressed bribery and extortion concerns, urging citizens to report corruption.
