The Gabonese government officially launched the **MADIGIPAIE** program on Tuesday, 9 December 2025, to modernize and secure public payments as part of the national digital-transformation strategy. Implemented with the **Bank of Central African States (BEAC)** and the **Central African Interbank Monetary Group (GIMAC)**, MADIGIPAIE lets citizens pay via mobile money by scanning a **GIMACPAY** QR code. More than 1,000 QR codes have already been deployed with merchants and service providers, with nationwide rollout continuing.

The launch follows the government's 8 December announcement of a **UNCDF** partnership to strengthen digital financial services, and a 21 November project to digitize payments for e-visa applications. In late October, the government began a pilot to digitize social and tax payments, with full deployment scheduled for January 2026. Earlier in the year, authorities engaged US-based **Visa** to modernize tax collection, automate disbursements, and introduce a secure digital identity system. In September, Gabon adopted a new legal framework to accelerate digital transformation across public administration.

The Ministry of Economy reported **4.5 million mobile money accounts at end-2024**, of which only **1.6 million were active**; operators processed **368.3 million transactions in 2024 worth CFA4,087 billion** (up from CFA3,487 billion in 2023); purchases of goods and services reached CFA291 billion (+15%).
