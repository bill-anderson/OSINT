NIR Executive Director Andrew Peters says the biggest challenge in expanding national ID enrollment is **lack of funding**, speaking at the ID4Africa 2025 AGM about the mandatory-registration executive order, development partnerships and Liberia's path to the SDG 16.9 target of legal identity for all by 2030.

The national ID registration rate is extremely low — **~14%** of Liberia's ~5 million citizens (per NIR figures). Peters said the main issue is payments (citizens are willing, but paying for services is an obstacle), noted robust political support from President Boakai and UNICEF assistance on birth registration, and said the **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** is seeking funding to make birth and ID registration **free**. A UNICEF child-enrollment pilot captured ~5,000 children in May (aligned with a 2023 NIR project to capture children's biometrics at birth); the NIR hopes to expand to 100,000-200,000. He warned that dependence on registration fees would prevent meeting the 2030 target. See [[great-project-liberia]].
