Morocco intends to give new momentum to its digital transformation with a draft law, **"Digital X.o,"** laying groundwork for **AI governance, data interoperability and digital identity**. Still being worked on before submission to parliament, the Minister Delegate for Digital Transition, Amal El Fellah Seghrouchni, said it brings clarity to data interoperability among government institutions (per data-protection Law 09-08), introduces **sectoral digital IDs** that keep data control in users' hands, and outlines how AI will streamline public services.

The draft emphasises cybersecurity and digital trust and supports **[[digital-morocco-2030|Morocco Digital 2030]]**'s objectives — strengthening digital sovereignty, encouraging innovation, harnessing local skills, and guiding responsible AI. The article notes Morocco is building its DPI ecosystem with a robust digital ID using **[[mosip]]** to support social protection and inclusion, and unveiled a **Digital for Sustainable Development (D4SD) Hub** in September with UNDP support.
