# 5G, AI, and Beyond, Ooredoo's Role in Shaping Algeria's Digital Economy : Dr. Medhat ElHusseiny

Ooredoo Algeria is positioning itself at the forefront of digital transformation, and as the country's telecom landscape rapidly advances, the company is leveraging 5G, expanding connectivity in underserved regions, and driving innovation and inclusion through AI powered solutions.

In an exclusive interview with Telecom Review Africa, Dr. Medhat ElHusseiny, Chief Technology and Information Officer (CTIO), highlighted the company's 5G strategy, efforts to expand connectivity in underserved regions, and the pivotal role of artificial intelligence (AI) in reshaping network operations and customer experiences.

**How is Ooredoo Algeria approaching the rollout and evolution of 5G, and what impact do you foresee on both consumers and enterprises?**

Our strategy for 5G at Ooredoo Algeria is built on a foundation of continuous network evolution and strategic investment. We are not just preparing for a new radio layer; we are executing a comprehensive transformation that includes modernizing our core network to a cloud-native, 5G standalone (5G SA) architecture, expanding our fiber backhaul capacity, and securing the necessary spectrum. This ensures that when 5G is launched, it will be a robust and transformative platform from day one.

For consumers, the impact moves beyond enhanced mobile broadband (eMBB) to redefine the mobile experience. We are talking about enabling immersive augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) applications, seamless cloud gaming on mobile devices, and revolutionizing fields like telemedicine and tele-education through reliable, high-definition mobile connectivity.

For enterprises, 5G is the catalyst for the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR). We are actively developing solutions around network slicing to provide guaranteed service-level agreements (SLAs) for mission-critical applications, massive IoT (MIoT) for large-scale sensor deployment across agriculture and logistics, and ultra-reliable low-latency communication (URLLC) for automation in the manufacturing and energy sectors. This will unlock new business models, drive operational efficiency, and significantly boost Algeria's digital economy. Our role is to be the foundational partner for this enterprise digital transformation.

**What strategies are being implemented to accelerate network coverage expansion, particularly in underserved rural and remote areas?**

Our network expansion strategy is a multi-faceted approach that leverages regulatory frameworks, technological innovation, and sustainable investment. The Universal Telecommunications Service (SUT) program is a critical partnership that provides a structured model for deploying infrastructure in economically challenging areas.

Technologically, we are deploying a range of cost-effective coverage solutions tailored to the unique challenges of Algeria's geography. This includes implementing innovative backhaul solutions to reach difficult locations and utilizing alternative power sources to ensure network resilience and operational sustainability in areas with limited infrastructure. We also utilize advanced network planning tools and carrier aggregation (CA) techniques to maximize the coverage and capacity of each site.

These efforts have yielded validated results, with Ookla recently awarding us the title of 'Best Mobile Network in Algeria for Speed.' The persistent challenge is the economic model of low-density areas. Our strategy addresses this through these efficient technologies and a commitment to viewing universal connectivity as a long-term investment in national digital inclusion.

**How does the Universal Electronic Communications Service contribute to bridging the digital divide, and what challenges remain in its implementation?**

The SUT is the primary instrument for democratizing digital access in Algeria. It effectively mitigates the commercial barriers to deployment in unprofitable areas, ensuring that network expansion is aligned with national social and economic goals. Its contribution is foundational: it provides the connectivity that enables access to critical e-services, e-learning, e-health, digital government services, and fintech for previously unconnected communities.

The implementation challenges are significant. Algeria's vast and diverse geography makes physical deployment complex and costly. Beyond the initial rollout, ensuring the long-term operational sustainability of this infrastructure is key. This involves maintaining sites with limited access, securing consistent power, and managing physical damage and site security issues. Furthermore, the mission of the SUT is only half-complete with infrastructure; the other half demands stimulation. This requires parallel efforts to improve device affordability and enhance digital literacy to ensure the population can fully leverage the new connectivity. We are committed to collaborating on these broader challenges.

**In what ways is artificial intelligence transforming network management, customer experience, and predictive maintenance within telecom operations?**

AI and machine learning (ML) are fundamentally reshaping our operations, moving us from a reactive to a predictive and autonomous model.

- Network Management: We employ AI for predictive traffic forecasting and dynamic resource allocation. Our systems use ML algorithms to analyze historical and real-time data, anticipating network congestion and automatically adjusting capacity to ensure consistent quality of experience (QoE).
- Customer Experience: AI powers our hyper-personalization engines, analyzing usage patterns to tailor plan recommendations and content offers. In customer support, AI-powered chatbots and virtual assistants handle routine queries, while sentiment analysis tools help us identify and proactively address customer frustration before it leads to a complaint.
- Predictive Maintenance: This is where AI delivers immense ROI. By implementing AI-driven analytics on network performance data, we can predict hardware failures—such as a power module degradation or a faulty transceiver—weeks in advance. This allows us to shift from scheduled to condition-based maintenance, performing repairs during low-traffic windows to prevent outages and dramatically improve network availability and reliability.

**Beyond operations, how can AI drive innovation in creating new telecom services and business models?**

Beyond operational efficiency, AI is the key to unlocking new revenue streams and evolving our role from a connectivity provider to a digital platform enabler.

We can leverage our unique position in the data value chain. Through AI-powered analytics on anonymized and aggregated network data, we can provide valuable insights into mobility patterns, economic activity, and population density for urban planners, retailers, and government agencies.

Furthermore, AI enables us to create and offer entirely new B2B services. We are exploring AI-driven cybersecurity-as-a-service for SMEs, leveraging our network visibility to detect and mitigate threats. We are also developing advanced IoT platforms where we don't just provide connectivity for sensors but offer AI analytics to interpret the data, providing farmers with predictive insights on irrigation or logistics companies with optimized fleet management routes.

In this way, AI allows Ooredoo Algeria to co-create value, moving up the stack and becoming an indispensable innovation partner in Algeria's broader digital ecosystem.
