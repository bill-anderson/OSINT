Burkina Faso and United Arab Emirates-based Presight International Services have signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) aimed at strengthening Burkina Faso's digital economy.

The government of Burkina Faso has signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Presight International Services, a subsidiary of the United Arab Emirates-based G42 Group, aimed at strengthening Burkina Faso's digital economy.

The MoU was signed by Aminata Zerbo-Sabane, Burkina Faso's minister of digital transition, posts and electronic communications, and Presight VP Muhammad Shameen Hosenbocus.

Through this agreement, the two parties intend to lay the foundations for solid collaboration to strengthen Burkina Faso's digital economy.

The MoU establishes a framework for cooperation on several key fronts, including developing skills in artificial intelligence (AI) and strengthening digital infrastructure to improve connectivity.

It also focuses on consolidating e-government services and implementing more robust cybersecurity measures in the west African country.

According to the partners, the goal is to provide Burkina Faso with modern and resilient digital solutions capable of supporting its public and private services and preparing the implementation of structuring projects in the country.

"This agreement paves the way for the implementation of structuring projects which should strengthen the implementation of major digital transformation projects that the government has initiated with the ambition of making digital technology a real lever for inclusive development in Burkina Faso," Burkina Faso's Ministry of Digital Transition, Posts and Electronic Communications said in a Facebook post.

The agreement aligns with the country's push to fast-track initiatives outlined in its 2025 roadmap.

Key priorities include completing data centers that are currently 70% finished and extending Internet coverage to at least 500 additional underserved areas known as "white zones."

The Burkinabé government also aims to accelerate the digitization of administrative services and launch a nationwide enrollment campaign for a new unique electronic identification system.

## Burkina Faso's digital transformation journey

The MoU comes at a time when the west African country has been working toward advancing its digital economy.

Just last month, Burkina Faso's communications regulator, ARCEP, and Ghana's National Communications Authority (NCA) held bilateral meetings to address concerns about cross-border signal interference.

In the same meeting, the two countries advanced talks on the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) free roaming initiative, designed to ease cross-border mobile roaming costs.

ECOWAS is a regional political and economic union of fifteen countries located in West Africa and its aim is to achieve "collective self-sufficiency" for its member states by creating a single large trade bloc through the creation of a full economic and trading union.

In July 2024, pan-African operator Orange's Burkinabé unit chose Vox Solutions as the international application-to-person (A2P) short message service (SMS) and voice gateway for traffic into its network.

In November 2023, the country's military government began working on a law related to cybersecurity.
