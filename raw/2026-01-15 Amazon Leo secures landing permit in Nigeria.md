The Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC) has granted Amazon Leo, formerly known as Project Kuiper, a seven-year landing permit.

Effective February 28, 2026, the NCC permits Amazon Leo to roll out Internet services from its low-Earth orbit (LEO) satellite constellation over Nigeria for a seven-year period.

The regulator said the approval aligns with global best practices and reflects the West African country's willingness to open its [satellite communications market](https://www.ncc.gov.ng/industry/space-services) to next-generation broadband providers.

Amazon Leo will be authorized to offer fixed satellite services (FSS), mobile satellite services (MSS), and mobile earth stations in motion (ESIM), covering household and business needs as well as mobility, logistics, air and maritime transport, and critical infrastructure requirements.

Amazon Leo plans to have a global constellation of up to 3,236 satellites.

## Nigeria's satellite market

Amazon Leo's entry into the Nigerian market ends satellite Internet service Starlink's dominance in the country.

Currently, SpaceX's Starlink is the most visible LEO satellite Internet provider in Nigera. However, capacity constraints in Lagos and Abuja have led to wait-lists.

The country's currency instability has also made pricing volatile.

In May 2025, Starlink increased its Nigerian prices, jumping from 38,000 Nigerian Naira (US$27) to N57,000 ($40) for a standard residential plan, even after [it halted a previous plan to raise prices](https://www.connectingafrica.com/connectivity/starlink-suspends-nigerian-tariff-hike) back in October 2024.

With over 66,000 subscribers in Nigeria at the end of 2025, Starlink has capitalized on its early lead.

However, Amazon Leo's arrival sparks direct competition between two global heavyweights, a clash that could redefine costs, performance and reach.

Amazon Leo is set to deliver 100MHz of bandwidth per channel in Nigeria.

Amazon Leo stated that its standard customer terminal is engineered to deliver speeds of up to 400 Mbit/s while maintaining terminal costs compatible with widespread adoption.
