# Review of Djibouti's Data Protection Law (within the Digital Code)

Analytical review of the **data-protection regime enacted in June 2025 as part of Djibouti's comprehensive [[djibouti-digital-code]]**. Comprising **156 articles**, it establishes a modern, detailed regime for the collection, processing, storage and transfer of personal data, and — unusually for primary legislation — embeds extensive operational guidance normally left to implementing directives.

- **Scope:** automated and non-automated processing (the latter within a structured filing system), with broad **extraterritorial reach** (controllers/processors outside Djibouti that offer goods/services to, or monitor, individuals in Djibouti). Excludes purely personal/household activity and transient technical copies.
- **Regulator:** creates the **[[cndp-djibouti|National Commission for the Protection of Personal Data (CNDP)]]** — independent, with administrative and financial autonomy and multi-stakeholder composition.
- **Key obligations:** accountability; processor contracts; possible local representative for foreign entities; **data protection by design and default**; confidentiality/security; **breach notification to the CNDP within 72 hours**; mandatory **DPO** for public authorities and large-scale systematic monitoring or sensitive/criminal-data processing; a public **national register** of processing; post-mortem privacy; limits on further processing.
- **Prior formalities:** a tiered system — declarations for routine processing, simplified declarations for low-risk, and **prior CNDP authorisation** for higher-risk cases (sensitive data for research, genetic/biometric data for identification, offence/conviction data, certain interconnections, and any processing involving the **national identification number**) — echoing South Africa's POPIA. A specific regime governs health-data processing.

*Reference/explainer content (not fresh event reporting); supplies datable, citable detail on the CNDP and the Code's privacy regime. The [[dpi.id|Mobile ID]] rollout's reliance on the national identification number places it squarely within the prior-authorisation tier described here.*
