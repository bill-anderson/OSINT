## Date Posted

Zambia is looking for an international partner to support the development of its national digital identity (ID) system, as part of efforts to modernise public services.

The project is being led by the Smart Zambia Institute and forms part of the broader Digital Zambia Acceleration Project.

Officials say the initiative is now in a critical implementation phase, with completion targeted for 2026. The programme is backed by a $100 million grant from the World Bank.

The digital ID system is expected to improve access to government services, strengthen identity verification, and support economic inclusion across the country.

Smart Zambia Institute National Co-ordinator Percy Chinyama says the partnership will be key to ensuring the successful rollout of the system.
