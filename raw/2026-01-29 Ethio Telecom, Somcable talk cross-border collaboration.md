Ethio Telecom and Somaliland Cable (Somcable) are in talks about forging a strategic cross-border digital and telecom partnership.

Somcable CEO Mohammed Ibrahim Ahmed and Ethio Telecom CEO Frehiwot Tamru. (Source: Ethio Telecom)

Ethiopian state-owned Ethio Telecom and telecom provider and Internet service provider Somaliland Cable (Somcable) are forging a strategic cross-border digital and telecom partnership.

Ethio Telecom CEO Frehiwot Tamru met with a delegation from Somcable, where discussions centered on accelerating growth in potential overseas markets through advanced digital and communications solutions.

During the meeting, Tamru highlighted the company's past achievements and future strategic priorities, showcasing its infrastructure and institutional capabilities.

Tamru reaffirmed Ethio Telecom's readiness to collaborate with Somcable to unlock new opportunities and drive mutual, sustainable growth.

"Collaboration is instrumental to unlocking growth. This partnership enables us to share our experience and infrastructure to create shared value and advance digital empowerment across Africa," Tamru said.

Somcable Chairman Mohamed Said Guedi said he was proud to be partnering with Ethio Telecom, underscoring a shared vision for the continent's digital future.

"This is more than a partnership between two companies; it is a collaborative commitment to harnessing technology to unlock human potential across Ethiopia and contribute to a more connected and prosperous Africa," added Somcable CEO Mohammed Ibrahim Ahmed.

## Ethio Telecom's push towards digital transformation

Ethio Telecom is actively driving digital transformation in the Horn of Africa, collaborating with international organizations to broaden the digital economy through new initiatives.

Late last year, the company partnered with Awash Bank [to launch Tila, a digital microfinance and savings service](https://www.connectingafrica.com/fintech/ethio-telecom-awash-bank-launch-digital-loans), along with a smart-device financing program through Telebir, Ethio Telecom's mobile money service.

In October 2025, the company partnered with Chinese Android smartphone maker Tinno [to boost smartphone adoption in the country](https://www.connectingafrica.com/connectivity/ethio-telecom-teams-with-tinno-to-boost-ethiopian-smartphone-adoption).

To accelerate the development of the country's growing electric vehicle (EV) ecosystem, the telco launched, in February and April 2025, charging stations in the Ethiopian capital [Addis Ababa](https://www.connectingafrica.com/emerging-technology/ethio-telecom-launches-ev-charging-station-in-addis-ababa) and [along the Bole-Megenagna main road](https://www.connectingafrica.com/emerging-technology/ethio-telecom-launches-another-ev-charging-station).

In January 2025, [the company began strategic talks with the African Development Bank (AfDB)](https://www.connectingafrica.com/partnerships/ethio-telecom-in-financing-talks-with-afdb) to further Ethiopia's digital transformation strategy.
