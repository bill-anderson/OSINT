# Hytera and CWS aim to drive digital transformation in Seychelles | 4 March 2025
By Vaughan O'Grady — Developing Telecoms

Hytera Communications has signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Cable & Wireless Seychelles (CWS), the country's pioneering quad-play operator, at MWC 2025 in Barcelona. The cooperation aims to drive digital transformation across Seychelles' enterprises and government sectors.

A major focus is the joint development of a resilient Seychelles emergency communications redundant network to ensure uninterrupted communications during natural disasters and other critical scenarios. The partnership will deliver tailored solutions for public safety, energy, transportation and tourism. Hytera will provide push-to-talk over cellular (PoC) devices, mission-critical services (MCX) core networks, and cloud-based platforms integrating with CWS's existing 4G/5G public networks, plus future private-network expansion.

Signed by Naadir Hassan (CEO, CWS), Oliver Fock-Tave (Associate Director, CWS) and Tony Wang (Business Development Director, Hytera). Hytera showcased mission-critical portfolio (4G/5G, MCX, PoC), body-worn cameras and digital evidence management.
