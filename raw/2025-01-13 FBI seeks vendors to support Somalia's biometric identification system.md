# FBI seeks vendors to support Somalia's biometric identification system

> ## Excerpt
> The FBI's CJIS Division issued an RFI (deadline 24 Jan 2025) for vendors to
> operate and maintain Somalia's Automated Biometric Identification System (ABIS) —
> a 2m ten-print / 50,000 latent police system at Mogadishu's Aden Adde airport —
> raising foreign-control and data-sharing concerns. Distinct from the civilian NIRA ID.

---

The U.S. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI) is seeking private vendors to help operate and maintain Somalia's Automated Biometric Identification System (ABIS). The FBI's Criminal Justice Information Services (CJIS) Division issued a Request for Information (RFI) to find qualified contractors to maintain and operate the ABIS, housed in a secure facility at Mogadishu's Aden Adde International Airport. ABIS allows the Somali Police Force (SPF) to identify individuals involved in criminal activities or terrorism.

With a capacity to hold two million ten-print records and 50,000 latent records, ABIS supports SPF investigations, providing biometric tools for tracking suspects, including remote enrollment and a backup server. The RFI calls for end-to-end support: on-site assistance, software and hardware maintenance, quarterly training for SPF personnel, fingerprint and document scanners, enrollment software, and integration of ABIS with other biometric databases.

For over three decades, Somalia lacked a centralized identification system. The Somali government has collaborated with international partners, including the World Bank and the UN. Through a partnership with the Somali Bankers Association (SBA), NIRA aims to reduce fraud and money laundering. Prime Minister Hamza Abdi Barre stressed the system's importance in counterterrorism against al-Shabab. The involvement of the FBI raises privacy concerns about unauthorized access to sensitive personal data and data sharing without explicit consent, amid limited regulatory oversight. Somalia has an ambitious plan to issue 15 million biometric IDs ahead of the 2026 national elections. The deadline for RFI submissions was January 24, 2025.
