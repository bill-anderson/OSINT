[Note: TechCabal Partner/Press Room item citing a named Verraki Partners report — verify against the Verraki primary at ingest.] A Verraki Partners (member of Andersen Consulting) report projects Nigeria's installed data centre capacity to surge from 65-86 MW today to over 400 MW within 3-5 years. Nigeria currently hosts ~17 active data centres with 9 more under construction/planned. History: Rack Centre's LGS1 (2012, first modern carrier-neutral facility); MainOne's MDXi Lekki (2015, 600 racks); Africa Data Centres' 10MW LOS1 (2021). Acceleration 2021-2025: Rack Centre scaling to 14.5MW, MTN's Dabengwa centre, Digital Realty, Equinix ($390m across Africa over five years), Open Access Data Centres ($500m), Airtel's Nxtra (live by Q1 2026). A 1MW Tier-III build generates ~US$17m in economic output with cumulative returns topping US$39m over 10 years.
