Botswana's **Data Protection Act No. 18 of 2024** — passed by the National Assembly and published on **29 October 2024** (presidential assent), effective **14 January 2025** — **repeals the Data Protection Act No. 32 of 2018**, which never actually came into force (its effective date was repeatedly extended by ministerial orders, and the 2018 Act did not bind the state or controllers outside Botswana).

The 2024 Act strengthens the **Information and Data Protection Commission**'s powers and imposes fuller obligations on controllers, processors and sub-processors — detailed provisions for children's-data processing, DPIAs, breach notification, records of processing (RoPA), a data-protection representative, and cross-border-transfer rules — addressing the gaps that stalled the 2018 statute.
