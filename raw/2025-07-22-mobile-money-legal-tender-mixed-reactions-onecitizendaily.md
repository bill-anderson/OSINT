# Central bank's push for mobile money as legal tender triggers mixed reactions

*One Citizen Daily, 22 July 2025*

The declaration by the Bank of South Sudan (BoSS) recognizing mobile money as legal tender has triggered debate among traders, buyers and economists over its feasibility and timing. The directive requires all sellers, service providers, and institutions to accept mobile money, with penalties for non-compliance.

Among buyers, the directive received a favorable reception, with some praising it as timely and convenient and noting it could reduce market-related theft. However, some economists criticized the move as premature. Economist Abraham Maliet described the policy as ill-timed and largely symbolic, arguing that over 90 percent of the country's economy remains outside the formal banking system and is heavily cash-based. He raised concerns over the lack of strong cybersecurity systems and data protection laws, warning mobile money could expose users to fraud and manipulation.

Maliet advised the central bank to suspend implementation and instead conduct a pilot program, and proposed introducing higher denomination banknotes such as SSP 10,000 and SSP 50,000. The article concludes that without adequate infrastructure, awareness, and security systems, the policy may struggle to achieve its intended impact.
