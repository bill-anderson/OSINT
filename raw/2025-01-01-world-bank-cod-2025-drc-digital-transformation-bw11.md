# World Bank — DRC Digital Transformation Project, 2025

The World Bank provided US$400 million (with €100 million co-financing from the French Development Agency) toward the DRC's Digital Transformation Project, building digital public infrastructure — digital identity, a data-sharing platform, e-signatures and a government payment gateway — and expanding broadband.

## Description

"The Democratic Republic of Congo is seeking a consultant as it launches a massive Digital Transformation Project... An announcement by the Congo-Kinshasa government states that $400 million in funding for the project will come from the World Bank, with another 100 million euros in cofinancing from the French Development Agency."

"DRC launched its draft digital transformation strategy last year, based on $1 billion in funding from the government and $500 million from external sources and partners."

"The project's deliverables would give the DRC functional Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI), including digital identity, a data-sharing platform, qualified electronic signatures and a government payment gateway. The country also plans to establish data protection regulations in line with the African Union's framework."
