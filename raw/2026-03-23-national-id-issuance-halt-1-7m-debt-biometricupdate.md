# Liberia's national ID issuance grinds to halt over $1.7M debt to service provider

By Ayang Macdonald — Biometric Update, March 23, 2026

For many months now, national ID cards have not been issued in Liberia, and reports suggest one of the reasons is a debt of around $1.7 million owed to Techno Brain, the Kenya-headquartered company managing the country's national identity infrastructure. Initially, ID delivery was suspended in June 2025 with a pledge by the government to fix anomalies including technical issues, issuance delays, and administrative irregularities. Liberians have also complained of high costs involved in obtaining a national ID card.

A steering committee was put in place in July by President Joseph Boakai to look into the problems, with the priority at the time being to speed up the selection process for a new ID card contractor. While the multi-sectoral committee is still expected to submit its report by next month, information about unpaid debts to Techno Brain has been received with reprobation by Liberians. As Front Page Africa reports, full access to the biometric registration database has not been possible for many months due to the $1.7 million debt; NIR Executive Director Andrew Peters declined a request for comment. Citizens have to sometimes use alternative identification documents such as passports and voter IDs to access important services. Concerns have also arisen about procurement irregularities involving new ID card infrastructure with Austrian firm OSD International.

Liberia's national ID program is a component of a World Bank-funded digital public infrastructure (DPI) initiative labeled the "Governance Reform and Accountability Transformation Project." Despite efforts over nearly 10 years, Liberia's national ID coverage stands at less than 15 percent — for a country counting on its national ID as the foundation of its digital economy ambitions.
