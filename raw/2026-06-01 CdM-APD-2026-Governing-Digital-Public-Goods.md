# Governing Digital Public Goods

A political-economy case study on the governance of global digital public goods (DPGs) for digital public infrastructure. It argues the multilateral trust deficit is structural and predates the current geopolitical rupture, and frames data's non-rivalrous nature as the foundational public good — with "openness" instrumental rather than primary. The paper critiques donor and multilateral DPI playbooks for producing "sovereignty-as-a-service", vendor lock-in and transparency gaps across the Global South. It proposes regional DPI — shared frameworks, infrastructure, human capital and single-market integration — as the most viable Global-South pathway.
