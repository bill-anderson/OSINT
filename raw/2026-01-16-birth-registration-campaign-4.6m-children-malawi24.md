# K17.8bn push to register 4.6 million children

The Malawi government requires K17.8 billion to register about 4.6 million children who remain without birth certificates and legal identity, Minister of Homeland Security Peter Mukhito announced. The National Birth Registration Campaign, implemented by the National Registration Bureau (NRB), targets children under 16 born before birth registration became mandatory or missed in earlier exercises. Mukhito said lack of registration exposes children to exploitation, early marriage, child labour, trafficking and exclusion from basic services, while leaving gaps in the national population register.

Birth registration became mandatory with the National Registration Act, which came into force in 2015, replacing a 1904 law. Since then the NRB has registered about 3.8 million children and issued over 12.8 million national IDs to citizens aged 16 and above. The new campaign will close the remaining gap by targeting an estimated 4.6 million unregistered children. Mukhito noted registering every child "will also end age manipulation that affects recruitment into security services."

The campaign will run in seven phases across 25 districts, using government primary schools as registration centres with trained teachers and NRB staff; birth certificates will be distributed at the same schools. K5.4 billion was allocated in the 2025/26 financial year for the first two phases, with the remaining five phases requiring K12.4 billion. Support acknowledged from UNICEF, UNDP and CDC.
