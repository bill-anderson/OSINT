# The Africa–India Blueprint for Growth

> ## Excerpt
> An edited CSEP–AUDA-NEPAD compendium framing Africa–India as strategic South–South partnership; its
> digital chapter (Research ICT Africa) argues Africa's ability to leverage digital innovation depends on
> robust data governance — and that DPI (adapting India's stack) is an alternative to frontier-AI
> dependence.

> **Provenance note.** Edited multi-author volume; chapter formats are non-uniform (some cite sources,
> some are expert opinion). "UPI overtook Visa" and CBDC benchmarks are single-sourced.

**Artefact:** ![[2026-01-01 The Africa-India Blueprint for Growth.pdf]]

## Key facts

- The **[[au-data-policy-framework]]** and **[[malabo-convention]]** "remain largely undomesticated"
  across member states — forcing African AI initiatives to depend on external data/imported governance.
  Positions **DPI** (Aadhaar=ID, UPI=payments, DigiLocker=data-sharing) as a modular template — argues
  digital sovereignty need not require data localisation. Concrete transfer: **Bank of Namibia–NPCI**
  (2024) UPI-like system. AfCFTA Digital Trade Protocol / Single Digital Market as the harmonisation frame.
