Mobile users in Mozambique will be required to register SIM cards with biometric data from the second half of 2024, the regulator (INCM) announced (via Lusa, 17 January 2024). The pilot phase began 15 January 2024 on an optional basis, becoming compulsory in the last six months of the year; from 2025 subscribers who fail to regularise risk having cards blocked.

The rules are contained in decree no. 12/2023 of 11 April, approving the Regulation on the Registration of Telecommunications Services. Registration requires biometric data (fingerprints and facial recognition) plus valid ID; cards cannot be registered with someone else's document. INCM administrator Constâncio Trigo said biometrics will ease identification of perpetrators of sector crime — an average of ~5,000 scams/frauds are reported monthly. Each subscriber receives a Unique Telecommunications Number (NUTEL) generated by INCM to uniquely identify them across operators. The regulation also requires registration of devices, distributors and resellers.
