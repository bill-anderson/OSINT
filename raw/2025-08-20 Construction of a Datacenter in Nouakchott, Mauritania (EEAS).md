EU External Action Service note (20 August 2025) on the Nouakchott data centre, a flagship EU Global Gateway initiative in Mauritania's digitalisation sector. Carried out through a €15 million EIB loan, the project includes construction, Tier III certification by the Uptime Institute, and construction supervision/monitoring.

Inaugurated in May 2025, the data centre strengthens Mauritania's digital sovereignty, enables local hosting of national digital data, and improves compliance with security standards for digital data exchanges. It is part of the West African Regional Communications Infrastructure Program (WARCIP, co-financed with the World Bank), covering interurban fibre-optic backbone links in the northeast, south and southeast with international connection points near the Senegal and Mali borders; this terrestrial network (inaugurated December 2021) interconnects with the ACE submarine cable (landed 2010). The centre will be operated by International Mauritania Telecom (IMT/GIE), bringing together the State (via Mauripost) and the country's three telecom operators. [Cluster with EIB press release.]
