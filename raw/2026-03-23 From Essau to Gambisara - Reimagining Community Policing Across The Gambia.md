Community policing in The Gambia is moving from a reform concept into practice across towns and villages — the Essau Model Police Station a national symbol, with change extending to communities such as Gambisara (Upper River Region). Since the 2017 post-transition, rebuilding confidence in the security sector has been a democratic priority.

Nationally the shift is structured and expanding: the 2022 State of the Nation Address reported **25 community-policing programmes and 175 active structures**. With UNDP support, officers and community actors have been trained in mediation and prevention, with patrol-visibility investments (including bicycles). Technology has entered through **mobile data-collection applications linked to case-management systems**, alongside **GIS mapping** of police facilities to guide deployment and improve coverage. The Essau model station's accessible design includes provisions for persons with disabilities and dedicated gender and child-welfare spaces.
