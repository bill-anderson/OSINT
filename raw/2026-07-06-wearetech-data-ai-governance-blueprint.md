Senegal Elevates Data and AI as Core Pillars of Public Governance Reform

Senegal is developing a national blueprint to use data and artificial intelligence (AI) to modernize public administration and improve policy effectiveness.

The government is focusing on priority sectors including health, agriculture, transport and taxation to deploy data-driven decision-making tools.

The New Deal Technologique strategy aims to strengthen data sovereignty, interoperability and digital infrastructure across public services.

Senegal has positioned data and artificial intelligence (AI) as strategic tools to modernize public administration, improve policy design and generate economic value through its national digital transformation agenda.

The Ministry of Telecommunications and Digital Economy convened public institutions, private sector actors, universities and technical partners on July 1–2, in collaboration with Smart Africa and Germany’s GIZ, to identify priority data and AI use cases.

The discussions will feed into a national Blueprint for Value Creation Through Data, which will support the implementation of Senegal’s New Deal Technologique, the country’s digital transformation strategy.

Authorities aim to define priority sectors where data can deliver the highest impact. The working groups identified health, agriculture, transport, education, taxation, territorial administration, security and public services as key areas for AI deployment.

Officials said AI systems could help anticipate population needs, optimize public resource allocation, improve infrastructure planning, automate administrative procedures and detect fraud or anomalies in public databases.

Senegal also plans to establish a governance framework enabling public administrations to share data securely. The system will ensure data quality, interoperability and protection while reducing fragmented databases that often create duplication, inefficiencies and incomplete decision-making.

Data sovereignty remains a central pillar of the New Deal Technologique, which also includes the development of national infrastructure for data hosting, digital identity and public service interoperability.

The World Bank has previously said that reliable and interoperable data systems help governments improve policy targeting, increase public spending efficiency and enhance service delivery to citizens. It also noted that high-quality data is essential for developing AI solutions adapted to local contexts.

Senegal’s initiative forms part of the New Deal Technologique, launched in 2025 under the broader Vision Sénégal 2050 development framework. The strategy aims to strengthen digital sovereignty, accelerate public sector digitization, develop a competitive digital economy and position Senegal among Africa’s leading technology hubs. The outcomes of the workshop will contribute to a national roadmap guiding future investments in data infrastructure and artificial intelligence applications.
