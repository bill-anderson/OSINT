MTN Nigeria has joined the cloud race by launching the first phase of its US$235 million data center in Lagos. Named after former MTN Group CEO Sifiso Dabengwa, the newly completed phase 1 features a 4.5MW IT load, spans three floors and houses 780 racks, having cost $100 million to build. Upon full development the facility will achieve 9MW capacity; Phase 2 (estimated $135 million) aims for Tier IV certification.

The project places MTN into a competitive landscape traditionally dominated by Equinix's MainOne, Open Access Data Centres, Rack Centre and Medallion's Digital Realty, and aims to support local cloud infrastructure with IaaS, AI-processing readiness and carrier-neutral hosting. MTN Nigeria CEO Karl Toriola said the company will expand capacity, including readiness for AI.

Context: In April 2025 Equinix announced expansion of its Lagos data center (LG2.3, adding to LG1 and LG2). In January 2023 AWS launched an AWS Local Zones location in Lagos. Other African expansions cited include Wingu Africa ($60m, East Africa), PAIX Dakar, Equinix JN1 Johannesburg (20MW), and Raxio facilities in Cote d'Ivoire, DRC and Mozambique.
