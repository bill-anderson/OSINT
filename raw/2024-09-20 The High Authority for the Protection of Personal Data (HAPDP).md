Profile of the High Authority for the Protection of Personal Data (HAPDP), Niger's independent data-protection authority. It was created in 2017 under Law No. 2017-28 (3 May 2017), amended by Law No. 2019-71 (24 December 2019); those texts were repealed by Law No. 2022-59 (16 December 2022), itself amended by Law No. 2023-31 (4 July 2023) and by Ordinances 2024-16 (26 April 2024) and 2024-29 (24 June 2024). The authority stems from Article 8 of the AU (Malabo) Convention, which required an independent enforcement body.

Processing of personal data is subject to a status-based regime: the State faces a request-for-opinion regime, private controllers require authorisation. The HAPDP's powers include adopting regulatory decisions, receiving declarations and complaints, referring offences to the courts, conducting inspections via sworn agents, opining on draft laws, and imposing administrative and financial sanctions (Articles 92-106). It has ten members (only the President permanent); its decisions are appealable before the Council of State (Conseil d'Etat). Installed in 2020, it has recorded very few complaints, attributed to low public awareness.
