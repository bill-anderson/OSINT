## What it covers

- **MOSIP as anti-lock-in DPI.** Modular open-source identity core + vendor ecosystem;
  lets governments select and change providers without proprietary lock-in or trapped
  biometric templates — the "vendor lock-in" problem named at the **ID4Africa 2018** AGM,
  "particularly for countries in the Global South." Conceptually influenced by India's
  **Aadhaar / UIDAI** ([[nandan-nilekani]]). Links identity to UN **SDG 16** (legal
  identity for all by 2030). See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]], [[tech.industry]].
- **Country case studies.** **Morocco** ([[MAR]]) — civil registration + social
  protection rather than a primary national ID; **Sierra Leone** ([[SLE]]), **Ethiopia**
  ([[ETH]]), **Uganda** ([[UGA]]), and **Sri Lanka** (non-African). Deployments span
  Africa, Southeast Asia and, increasingly, Latin America/Caribbean; hundreds of millions
  already enrolled.
- **Ecosystem, standards, marketplace.** Core-and-project funding model; MOSIP standards
  development; the **MOSIP Marketplace** and vendor accreditation (cf. the Fime lab
  accreditation already recorded on [[mosip]]); integrations now including **digital
  payments** ([[dpi.pay]]). MOSIP is increasingly shaping how countries link identity,
  payments and service delivery — a governance model, not just software.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: open-source + standardised interfaces give governments control over
  identity infrastructure and the ability to switch vendors.
- `colonialism`: the lock-in it counters falls hardest on Global-South states — the same
  externally-shaped-agency tension seen across [[gov.standards]] and [[zambia--dpi-id]].
