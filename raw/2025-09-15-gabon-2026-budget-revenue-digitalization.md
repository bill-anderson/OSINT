The Gabonese government plans to digitalize public revenue collection to support a CFA7,233.3 billion (~$13 billion) budget for 2026. The plan emerged as a top priority during a 5 September 2025 working session on the 2026 Finance Law chaired by Head of State Brice Clotaire Oligui Nguema; Digital Economy Minister Mark Alexandre Doumba detailed the mechanisms on public channel Gabon 24.

Doumba's three-point strategy: (1) **tax-base expansion** — the informal economy, 40-50% of GDP, must gradually integrate into the fiscal system via digital channels; (2) **promoting electronic payments** in commercial transactions, since much settlement is still cash, depriving the state of VAT; (3) **securing payments within public administration** to reduce leakages.

Gabon began this digitalization with the April 2024 launch of the **"Digitax Gabon"** platform for integrated management of fiscal processes (tax-base management, recovery, control, dispute processing, teleprocedure payment). Per the DGI's 2024 report, at least **80% of tax revenues are now collected via the system**; the DGI achieved CFA1,021.58 billion in treasury revenues against an CFA862.3 billion target (18% overshoot) and 107% of its CFA969.38 billion budgetary target. Per ITU data, 71.9% of the population had internet access in 2023.
