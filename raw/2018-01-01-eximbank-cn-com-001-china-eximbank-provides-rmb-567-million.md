## Description

On December 11, 2018, China Eximbank and La Société Comorienne de Télécommunication (Comores Télécom) — a state-owned telecommunication operator in Comoros — signed an RMB 567,000,000 ($85,826,411.40) government concessional loan (GCL) agreement for the Telecommunications Network Modernization Project. The GCL carries the following borrowing terms: a 30-year maturity, a 5-year grace period, and a 1% interest rate. The borrower is expected to use the proceeds of the GCL to finance an $81 million commercial contract that it signed with Huawei Technologies Co., Ltd. in 2017.   The purpose of the project is to renovate and modernize the infrastructure Comores Télécom and expand the ‘Huri network’ and cover the entire country. More specifically, it involves the extension of a fiber to the home (FTTH) network to 57 additional sites.  The project also seeks to build upon the achievements of the China Eximbank-financed National Fiber Optic Backbone Transmission Network Project (captured via Project ID#53292). Huawei Technologies Co., Ltd. is the contractor responsible for project implementation. On March 22, 2022, Huawei Technologies Co., Ltd. and Comores Télécom officially launched the Nafassi FTTH to provide household access to to high-speed Internet, television and landline telephone services (via submarine cables).
