The Uganda Communications Commission (UCC) convened a high-level stakeholder engagement (at UCC House, Kampala) to strengthen national efforts against digital piracy, describing it as a growing threat to Uganda's communications sector, creative economy and digital ecosystem. Participants included regulators, broadcasters, MTN Uganda, Airtel Uganda, Lyca Mobile, URA, URSB, the National Association of Broadcasters and content creators.

The engagement explored content-blocking technologies and laid groundwork for a national anti-piracy coordination framework. UCC Executive Director Nyombi Thembo said piracy discourages investment and reduces government revenue. UCC's Dr Abudu Sallam Waiswa revealed legitimate pay-TV subscriptions fell from about 1.65 million (2021) to approximately 800,000 by March 2026, contributing to job losses. MultiChoice Uganda MD Hassan Saleh warned piracy is becoming normalised. Canal+ presented Dynamic IP and DNS blocking technologies used internationally, with participants stressing the need to balance enforcement with transparency and protection of legitimate internet services.
