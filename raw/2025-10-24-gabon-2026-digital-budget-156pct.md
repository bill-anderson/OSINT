Gabon's Ministry of Digital Economy, Digitalization and Innovation announced a budget of **82 billion CFA francs for 2026, a 156.2% increase from 32 billion CFA in 2025**. Minister Mark-Alexandre Doumba presented the budget to the National Transition Assembly on 19 October 2025. The increase reflects a commitment to accelerating digital transformation through the digital entrepreneurial ecosystem, infrastructure deployment, and digital literacy.

Recent infrastructure initiatives include an agreement with an American technology company to construct a national data center. Private partners approached include Cisco, Tech 41, Visa, Huawei, Moov Africa Gabon Telecom, and Afrastructure SAS, with discussions on expanding the national fiber-optic network, universal service, telecom infrastructure sharing, 5G deployment, and connection to a new submarine cable. The government recently issued an ordinance mandating public administrations to digitize services, interconnect systems, and integrate the private sector to establish **digital sovereignty**.

Benchmarks: Gabon ranks **121st of 193 on the UN E-Government Development Index (0.5741)** — above the Central Africa (0.3354) and Africa (0.4247) averages but below the global average (0.6382); lowest sub-score online services (0.3188), highest telecom infrastructure (0.8263). It scored **76.1/100 on the ITU 2024 ICT Development Index (11th of 42 African nations)**. On the **ITU Global Cybersecurity Index 2024 it is Tier 4 (39.86/100)**, with full marks (20) on legal measures but weakness in technical, organizational and cooperation measures.
