The government has been ordered to table a copy of the directives that triggered the internet shutdown in the run-up to the January 2026 General Elections. Public Accounts Committee (Central) vice chairperson Gorreth Namugga issued the directive on March 10 during an engagement with Ministry of ICT and National Guidance officials led by permanent secretary Aminah Zawedde: "You will have to submit those directives on the shutdown and the re-opening."

Two days before polling (January 13), UCC ordered MNOs and ISPs to suspend public internet, new SIM sales/registration, and outbound One Network Area data roaming. UCC ED Nyombi Thembo argued it averted polling chaos from misinformation. MPs described the blackout — which paralysed social media, web browsing and mobile money — as high-handed and economically disruptive. Per Uganda's Banking Sector Report 2023, registered mobile money customers stood at 43.4 million (60% growth over five years) with 6.4 billion transactions worth sh227.5 trillion. Bugiri Municipality MP Asuman Basalirwa noted shutdowns have become routine at elections (2016, 2021, 2026).

Zawedde said the order originated from an inter-agency security committee. Board member Geoffrey Agoi said UCC's ED received a formal directive from the National Security Council. Agoi added that social media was the intended target, but mobile money "suffered collateral damage due to technical complexities," with Airtel specifically having implementation issues.
