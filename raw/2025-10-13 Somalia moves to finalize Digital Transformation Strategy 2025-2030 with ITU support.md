# Somalia moves to finalize Digital Transformation Strategy 2025-2030 with ITU support

> ## Excerpt
> MoCT and the ITU held a two-day national multi-stakeholder consultation (12-13
> Oct 2025, Mogadishu) to validate Somalia's Digital Transformation Strategy
> 2025-2030, aligned with Vision 2060 and AU/EAC integration; builds on a Jul-2025
> UNDP MoU. Somalia ranks 191/193 on the UN EGDI (2024).

---

The Ministry of Communications and Technology (MoCT), in partnership with the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), is hosting a two-day National Multi-Stakeholder Consultation Workshop to validate Somalia's Digital Transformation Strategy (2025-2030), a policy framework designed to steer the country's next phase of digital development.

The workshop, held on October 12-13 in Mogadishu, convenes representatives from key government institutions, licensed telecom operators, and development partners to review and refine the proposed digital roadmap, which aims to foster inclusive growth, innovation, and improved service delivery.

Mr. Mustafa Almahdi, Program Officer at the ITU Arab Regional Office, reaffirmed the ITU's commitment to supporting Somalia's digital vision. Mr. Mustafa Yasin Sheik, Director General of the National Communications Authority (NCA), described the strategy as a critical milestone in Somalia's digital evolution.

In his keynote address, H.E. Mohamed Adan Moallim Ali, Minister of Communications and Technology, stated that the strategy will establish a national framework for all ICT-related initiatives across sectors, aligning with Somalia's broader Vision 2060 agenda. He highlighted priorities in expanding broadband infrastructure, enhancing cybersecurity, and building digital skills.

Over the two days, stakeholders deliberated on Somalia's digital vision, strategic pillars, and implementation roadmap, addressing governance frameworks, financing models, and regional integration with the African Union and the East African Community (EAC).

The consultation builds on recent initiatives, including the launch of a national QR code standard for mobile payments and the introduction of wearable NFC-enabled devices for contactless transactions. In August, Somalia launched a Unified Digital Civil Registration Service nationwide. Earlier in July, the government signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) with the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) to accelerate digital transformation through cooperation in digital governance, innovation, public service delivery, and human capital development.

According to the UN's 2024 E-Government Development Index, the country ranks 191st out of 193 nations, with a score of 0.1468, far below the African average of 0.4247.
