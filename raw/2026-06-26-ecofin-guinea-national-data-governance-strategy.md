# Guinea Begins Work on National Data Governance Strategy

Guinea has begun work on a National Data Governance Strategy aimed at strengthening its digital economy. The government launched the drafting process on Friday, June 19. The strategy will establish rules governing the collection, sharing, security and use of data generated by public institutions, businesses and citizens.

Led by the National Directorate of Information Technology and the Digital Economy (DNTIEN), the strategy is intended to provide a framework for improving the quality of public data, strengthening interoperability and promoting its use in digital services. The authorities also aim to create an enabling environment for artificial intelligence while fostering innovation and the data economy, alongside protecting sensitive information and safeguarding national digital sovereignty.

The initiative comes as Guinea steps up investment in digital infrastructure and has made digital transformation a priority under its Simandou 2040 development program. Effective data governance could facilitate the secure sharing of information across government agencies, improve public decision-making and support the development of artificial intelligence applications in sectors including healthcare, education, agriculture and financial services.
