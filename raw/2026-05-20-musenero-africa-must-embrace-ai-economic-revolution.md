Uganda's Minister of Science, Technology and Innovation, Dr Monica Musenero Masanza, argues that African governments must stop seeing AI as merely a technology and view it as a platform for economic transformation. Speaking at the AI Everything Summit (part of Gitex Kenya, Nairobi), she said AI "has grown and swallowed all the others" of the fourth industrial revolution: "This is Africa's industrial revolution. It is going to shift everything."

She admitted Africa has so far only consumed AI rather than capturing its economic opportunity: "We have not yet actively positioned it as the new platform for economic and socioeconomic transformation," urging a mindset shift to see AI as "the new soil in which we are cultivating our wealth." She stressed following the money — Africa's AI market is projected to surge from US$4.5 billion (2025) to US$16.5 billion by 2030 (Mastercard data) — while claiming Africa currently retains only 1% of global AI wealth, a balance she says must be tipped toward the continent.
