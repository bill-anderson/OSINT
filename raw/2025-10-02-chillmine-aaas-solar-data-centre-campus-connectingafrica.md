# ChillMine, AAAS Energy to launch data center in Botswana

By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa — October 2, 2025

AAAS Energy and ChillMine have signed a memorandum of understanding to develop a new solar-powered data center campus in Botswana.

Netherlands-based energy company AAAS Energy and US-based data center operator, ChillMine have signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) to develop a new data center campus in Botswana.

The new campus will be designed to serve global hyperscalers as well as artificial intelligence (AI) computing and energy-intensive computing operations.

According to a joint statement, the new data center campus will be built to meet the growing demand for reliable, scalable and economically viable data infrastructure in the Southern African region.

"It will cater specifically to clients like large-scale cloud providers who require vast amounts of uninterrupted, low-cost power to support their operations," the statement added.

The statement said AAAS Energy will approach its joint development partner to include the data center campus in an Energy Hub and Industrial Park near Palapye, a town situated about halfway between Francistown in the east and the capital Gaborone, further toward the southeast.

It will consist of a 250-megawatt (MW) solar PV project with a foreseen battery energy storage system (BESS) of between 100MW and 400MW, which is currently under development.

According to the partners, the project has the potential to provide a consistent and powerful energy source, ensuring operational stability and efficiency for the data center facilities.

AAAS Energy MD Maarten Mennes said the deal is a significant step in the company's vision to connect sustainable energy development with the digital economy.

"By combining power from the Solar PV and BESS Project with natural gas projects in Botswana, currently being developed by third parties, we are creating a unique value proposition for global technology companies seeking to expand into Africa," Mennes explained.

ChillMine CEO and Co-Founder Brian Neirby highlighted that the partnership will help the company bring its data center expertise to Botswana.

"The combination of the energy infrastructure under development and our operational experience will enable us to deliver a high-performance data center campus that can meet the rigorous demands of the world's most sophisticated technology users," Neirby concluded.

## African data center boom

The MoU between AAAS Energy and ChillMine comes at a time when there is a hive of data center activity across the continent. Earlier this week, software development company Nugi Group announced that it would build a Tier IV data center in Calabar, Cross River State, in the south of Nigeria. That facility is designed to offer 99.99% uptime and it will leverage hydro, solar, and natural gas energy sources.

In September 2025, Airtel Africa's data center arm, Nxtra by Airtel, broke ground on the construction of a 44MW hyperscale data center in Tatu City, an economic zone on the outskirts of Nairobi, Kenya. Vendor-neutral data center provider Teraco completed the expansion of its JB4 Bredell Campus data center, located in Ekurhuleni, east of Johannesburg, South Africa in July 2025. Also in July 2025, MTN Nigeria launched phase one of a major data center project in Lagos, which features a 4.5MW IT load, spans three floors and houses 780 racks. Digital infrastructure firm Equinix announced the expansion of its data center in Lagos in April 2025, while PAIX Data Centres announced the construction of a new data center in Dakar, Senegal in January 2025.
