# Inside WAEMU's push for a unified fintech market

By Lina Kacyem — TechCabal (Francophone Weekly), 14 July 2026

On 3 July, the Pullman Abidjan hosted *Catapult: Inclusion Africa*, a conference by the Luxembourg House of Financial Technology (LHoFT), backed by the Luxembourg government (LuxDev), the BCEAO, GIM-UEMOA, the African Development Bank, ADA, and the Côte d'Ivoire Fintech Association. The event convened regulators, investors and founders around a single theme: how to build an inclusive, financeable fintech sector across the eight countries of the West African Economic and Monetary Union (UEMOA / WAEMU), a market of ~150 million people.

Two regulatory workstreams are converging simultaneously on the region:

- **CAF-STI/SSI cybersecurity certification** — the information-systems security audit framework is now mandatory for every payment-ecosystem operator, with a two-year grace period for new entrants to satisfy twelve requirements covering data security, banning default passwords, and periodic staff training. GIM-UEMOA's academy and its partnerships with cybersecurity firms (Luxembourg-based Stru Team, and the Africa Cyber Security Centre) exist to support compliance.
- **PI-SPI (Plateforme d'Interopérabilité des Systèmes de Paiement Instantané)** — BCEAO's instant-payment interoperability platform, launched 30 September 2025, imposes mandatory connection. A single connection covers all eight UEMOA countries; account-to-account (A2A) transfers settle in under five seconds on a free platform, replacing a classic transfer process that could take 48 hours and cost up to 12,000 CFA francs (~$20.87). PI-SPI does not replace the region's RTGS system (STAR); GIM-UEMOA remains focused on card/monétique interoperability, the two institutions presented as complementary under BCEAO supervision.

The BCEAO has already written open-banking obligations into its new banking law, though implementing texts remain pending while PI-SPI absorbs most regulatory energy. Founders were advised to treat compliance as a growth strategy — mapping every applicable regulation and approaching the regulator with a prepared file. The article frames UEMOA as potentially Africa's next fintech frontier.
