Smart Africa, through its **Council of African IT Agencies (CAITA)** — chaired by Morocco's Digital Development Agency (**[[add-morocco|ADD]]**) — brought together **11 Member States in Rabat (14 July 2025)** to jointly draft the continent's **first Cross-Border Data Exchange Guidelines**, a step toward trusted, interoperable digital services. The guidelines aim to define a harmonized framework for developing, interconnecting and operating data-exchange platforms within the **[[smart-africa]]** space, under the Smart Africa Trust Alliance (SATA).

Smart Africa CEO Lacina Koné called it "a game-changer for Africa's digital future"; ADD Director General Amine El Mezouaghi tied it to King Mohammed VI's vision of a united Africa. The visit ran in parallel with Morocco's first National AI Summit; participants explored the **GovStack Information Mediator (Data Exchange)** Building Block. Participating CAITA states: Benin, Burkina Faso, Côte d'Ivoire, Comoros, Gabon, Ghana, Mauritania, Nigeria, Tunisia, Zambia and Morocco. Partners: NORAD, GovStack, GIZ.
