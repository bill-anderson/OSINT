Developing the **[[rdc-pass|DRCPass]]** national digital ID requires **$97.1 million** in capital expenditure — covering only equipment, systems, infrastructure and facilities — per a Ministry of Planning document, with **financing still unclear**. The public-private partnership (signed June 2025 between the Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and New ICTs and **[[trident-digital-tech]]**) makes Trident the **exclusive e-KYC provider** on Web 3.0 technologies for a **20-year term**.
