## Description

UNICEF-ITU joint initiative launched in 2019 to connect every school to the internet and every young person to information, opportunity and choice. In SSA, Giga is active in 20+ countries including Kenya, Rwanda, Sierra Leone, Niger, Zimbabwe, Benin, Botswana, Namibia, South Africa and Guinea, providing school mapping, connectivity planning, procurement support, and financing facilitation. By November 2023, Giga had mobilized >USD 47.6 million from 14 partners (Ericsson, Dubai Cares, Musk Foundation, SoftBank, BCG, IHS Towers, GSMA, Dell, FCDO, and others). UNICEF is the primary lead of the Giga initiative with ITU as co-lead. Activities include open-source school mapping (Project Connect), real-time connectivity monitoring, government procurement support, and innovative financing models including outcome bonds and connectivity credits. As of 2023, Giga had connected 14,500+ schools globally, benefiting 7.79 million students, with SSA countries (Kenya, Rwanda, Sierra Leone, Botswana) accounting for a significant share.

## Notes

UNICEF is lead financier/programme owner of Giga. Disclosed partner contributions to the initiative total >USD 47.6M as of April 2023 (ITU TDAG30 document). Giga has additionally leveraged USD 1.7B+ in 8 countries for school connectivity from government USFs, loans, and auctions. Amount recorded is the directly raised partner contributions per disclosed figure. SSA countries include Kenya, Rwanda, Sierra Leone, Niger, Zimbabwe, Benin, Botswana, Namibia, South Africa, Guinea, Madagascar, Malawi (via Airtel/UNICEF Reimagine Education), Tanzania, Uganda. Giga Bond concept ($5B) was explored but not issued; pivoted to Giga Fund. Cross-ref: itu.csv for ITU's co-lead role and WTDC Resolution 87 implementation.
