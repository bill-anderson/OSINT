# Mozambique: Financial institutions have 60 minutes to report failures

Mozambican financial institutions will have 60 minutes to report payment service failures to the Bank of Mozambique, according to new rules applicable to the National Single Network for electronic payments, to which they are now legally required to be connected.

The obligation, set out in a central bank notice, applies to credit institutions, financial companies and the network operator. It mandates that they must "report to the Bank of Mozambique, within 60 minutes of the occurrence, any unavailability of payment services lasting more than 10 minutes".

According to Notice No. 2/GBM/2026 of 2 June, which "establishes the rules for connection to the Single Payments Network (National Switch) and revokes Notice No. 2/GBM/2015 of 22 April". The regulations will come into force 90 days after publication.

In addition to reporting failures, the new framework imposes continuous monitoring of system connections, establishing that entities must provide the central bank with "real-time information on the status of connection to the National Single Network".

The National Single Network operates as a common, shared infrastructure that centralises the processing of electronic transactions in the country, including card transactions, payment terminals and digital channels. It has been operated by SIMOrede since 2023 under the supervision and control of the Bank of Mozambique.

Connection to this single network is now a mandatory requirement for operations. The notice dictates that institutions must ensure this integration "as a condition for offering electronic payment products and services", including mobile financial services.

The measure covers the entire payments infrastructure, requiring electronic payment terminals to "be installed through the National Single Network", thereby centralising the processing of transactions carried out through ATMs, point-of-sale (POS) terminals and other electronic channels.

The framework also introduces obligations for incident management and operational risk, requiring the network operator to establish procedures for the "detection, classification, response and reporting of severe operational and security incidents or those with systemic impact". These mechanisms must be reviewed periodically, with the central bank determining that the incident management framework be updated "at least once a year" based on system risks and threats.

Furthermore, institutions must maintain business continuity and disaster recovery plans, featuring mandatory annual testing whose results must be reported to the central bank.

For entities already operating, a three-month deadline is set to integrate or migrate existing terminals to the network, standardising the national payments system. Non-compliance with the new rules will constitute an offence and a "punishable misdemeanour" under the applicable financial sector legislation.
