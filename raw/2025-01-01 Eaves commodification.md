# Parting Clouds: Creating a Competitive Marketplace for Compute

**Eaves, D. and Bester, K. (2025).** Published by the Canadian Anti-Monopoly Project
([[camp]]). Attachment: `2025-01-01 Eaves commodification.pdf`.

## Summary (as captured)

Cloud computing is core economic infrastructure controlled by a "remarkably small number
of firms": three hyperscalers — **Amazon (31%), Microsoft (24%), Alphabet (11%)** — hold
**66%** of global public cloud share; in Canada the same three control **85%** (federal
government spent >$156m on cloud in 2022–23, mostly to Microsoft and Amazon).

The report anatomises the costs of concentration — **vendor lock-in / competitor
lock-out, opaque pricing and asymmetric contracts, digital tollbooths (gatekeeping
adjacent markets), capture of innovation, systemic outage risk, and concentration as
geopolitical coercion** — and argues for market-shaping remedies (interoperability,
procurement leverage) over bespoke national "sovereign stack" industrial policy.

## Why it matters here

The fuller, evidenced version of the argument in Eaves' Tech Policy Press op-ed
