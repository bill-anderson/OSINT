[Reference/explainer; figures as of December 2023 - historical baseline.]

Around 850 million people globally lack any form of digital identification, a large share in Sub-Saharan Africa and South Asia. In Nigeria, financial inclusion increased to 74% in 2023 (up from 68% in 2020) but has failed to hit the CBN's 95% long-term target. The article analyses enrolment figures for BVN, NIN, voter registration, SIM registration, passport issuance and driver's licence.

BVN: In February 2014 the CBN deployed a centralised Bank Verification Number system for KYC and fraud control, with NIBSS providing the API for banks. The CBN's 2019 five-year target of 100 million BVNs fell 40% short: as of December 2023, 59,956,056 accounts were registered (NIBSS unique count). Per the CBN's December 2022 Financial Stability Report, of 159.42 million active accounts, 127.92 million were BVN-linked (the gap reflects multiple accounts per person). An NFIU study found that of 9,517 suspicious activity reports filed between 2015 and Q1 2018, 6,503 (68.3%) were BVN-related; as of December 2022, 7,399 BVN-linked accounts were watchlisted. From April 2024 the CBN said it would freeze accounts not linked to BVNs and NINs.

NIN: Only 42.6% of Nigerians have birth certificates (World Bank). Under the 2020 World Bank Digital Identification for Development agreement, NIMC aimed to capture 148 million Nigerians (65% of projected population) by June 2024. As of December 2023, 104.16 million NINs had been issued (59.12m/56.8% male; 45.04m/43.2% female), a 10.77% rise on the 94.03 million of 2022 but short of the 2024 target. Lagos, Kano and Kaduna (11.4m, 9.2m, 6.5m) account for 26% of registrations.

Voter registration: INEC began digitising the voter database in 2010 (Electronic Voters' Register), later adding BVAS and the IReV portal. Ahead of the 2023 elections, around 45% of "completed registrations" were deemed invalid; turnout was a historically low 27% despite a record 93.4 million registered voters.

SIM registration: The NCC maintains a central subscriber database. Registered SIMs crossed 300 million in 2022, but as of August 2023 there were 220 million active subscribers across four operators. An estimated 12.4 million lines were yet to be NIN-linked, prompting an NCC barring ultimatum of 28 February 2024.

Passport: The biometric passport was adopted in 2007; e-passport issuance was automated from 8 January 2024. Public data is scarce - the most recent NBS report (2018) recorded 1,011,158 passport applications (up 40.25% on 2017).
