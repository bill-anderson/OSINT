Acquisition item partly satisfied: "Jitume Digital Programme — programme documentation and hub register (Kenya)". This is KoTDA's own procurement document, not a programme page, annual report or hub register, and it carries **no budget, per-hub cost or utilisation figure**. What it does establish first-hand: the implementing agency (KoTDA, under the Ministry of Information, Communications and the Digital Economy); the programme's origin ("In December 2022 ... initiated the implementation"); the stated target of **1,450 Jitume Digital Hubs, one in every ward**, plus hubs in all TVET institutes; the three hub models (Basic, Standard, Centre of Excellence) plus renovation of existing facilities; and the **pilot scope of 47 Centres of Excellence and 50 Standard model hubs, at least one of each per county**. Note the queue item's held figure ("117th hub, as of 2026-07-20") should be read against this 1,450 target and against a separate 2025 report of 290 hubs — a discrepancy the main session may wish to route to contradictions.

INVITATION TO APPLY FOR PREQUALIFICATION

Konza Technopolis Development Authority (KoTDA) is the implementing agency under the Ministry of Information Communication and Digital Economy (MOIC&DE) for the Proposed Jitume Digital Hubs. MOIC&DE through Konza Development Technopolis Development Authority(KoTDA); and in line with Government's Bottom-up Economic Transformation Agenda (BETA),initiated the implementation of the Jitume Digital Enablement Programme. The programme seeks to establish high end computer hubs (Jitume Digital Hubs) in all the Technical and Vocational Education Training Institutes (TVETs) in Kenya. It further aims to set-up 1450 Jitume Digital Hubs in all the Wards (Villages). MOIC&DE through KoTDA invites interested and eligible bidders for the proposed pilot phase. This will involve the tender for the Construction of the Model Hubs in all the 47 Counties:
