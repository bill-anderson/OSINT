# Mozambique launches tender for consultancy on mobile digital identity and signature system

The Mozambican government plans to implement a national mobile digital identity and electronic signature infrastructure aimed at strengthening security and ensuring accessibility for all citizens.

According to a tender launched by the Ministry of Communications and Digital Transformation for consultancy services to create the national mobile digital identity and electronic signature infrastructure, the system will allow “the identification, authentication and digital signing of citizens, residents and companies”.

The initiative also aims to complement an ongoing plan for the secure exchange of data between government institutions and public and private service providers.

“The mobile digital identity solution to be implemented must be inclusive, scalable and accessible to all citizens, regardless of their geographical location, community or type of device,” the public tender notice states.

The government wants the infrastructure to support multiple implementation formats, including a mobile application, physical starter card and virtual card, in order to ensure “maximum coverage and digital inclusion”, especially in rural areas and communities with limited access to advanced and modern mobile devices.

The consultancy is also expected to develop and implement a self-service portal for managing digital identities and electronic signatures, allowing citizens to manage their devices, certificates and transactions securely.

Among the requirements listed is the full integration of the digital identity solution with the X-Road Interoperability Platform currently being implemented, ensuring full technical compatibility and compliance with global security and interoperability standards.

The infrastructure must also integrate with the National Population Registry through the National Directorate of Civil Identification, ensuring the legal validity of digital identities and electronic signatures.

The proposal must also “establish secure mechanisms for identity verification and user integration, in compliance with national legal and regulatory requirements and international best practices in security”, according to the tender notice, which also requires the training of professionals linked to the process to ensure independence in managing the technologies involved in the infrastructure.

The initiative forms part of the “Digital Economy and Electronic Government Project” financed by the World Bank, under which the government, through the Digital Transformation and Innovation Agency (ATDI), is implementing a digital transformation programme aimed at building a more efficient, transparent and citizen-centred public administration.
