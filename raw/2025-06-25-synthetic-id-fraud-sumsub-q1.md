Global verification company Sumsub's Q1 2025 report finds Nigeria's overall fraud rate rose to 4.44% (up 2.5% YoY) despite an 80% drop in document forgery. Nigeria saw a 192% increase in synthetic document fraud (fictitious or AI-generated identities), accounting for over 1.5% of all verification attempts — among the most affected African nations, trailing only Tanzania (over 2%) and South Africa (low base but 480% growth). Across Africa, traditional document forgery fell 82% YoY (0.51% to 0.09%). Sumsub's VP Sales Africa Hannes Bezuidenhout: "Enhanced verification tools have decimated traditional document forgery, but criminals are adapting with synthetic IDs and AI-powered scams." Financial services (banks, fintech) bear the brunt.
