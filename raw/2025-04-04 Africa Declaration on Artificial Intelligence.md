# Africa Declaration on Artificial Intelligence — primary document

Bilingual (English / French) declaration, **DONE at Kigali, Rwanda, 4 April 2025**, adopted at the
Global AI Summit on Africa (hosted by Rwanda's Centre for the Fourth Industrial Revolution and
Ministry of ICT & Innovation with the World Economic Forum). Artefact:
[[2025-04-04 Africa Declaration on Artificial Intelligence.pdf]].

The preamble aligns the Declaration with the **AU Continental AI Strategy**, the **Smart Africa AI
for Africa Blueprint**, the **AU Data Policy Framework**, the **AU Convention on Cybersecurity and
Personal Data Protection** (Malabo), and the **UN Global Digital Compact**.

## Signatories (verbatim from the document)

**Institutions:** African Union; Smart Africa.

**Countries — 49:** Algeria, Angola, Benin, Botswana, Burkina Faso, Burundi, Cabo Verde, Cameroon,
Central African Republic, Chad, Comoros, Côte d'Ivoire, Djibouti, Egypt, Equatorial Guinea, Eswatini,
Ethiopia, Gabon, Gambia, Ghana, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Kenya, Lesotho, Liberia, Madagascar, Malawi,
Mali, Mauritania, Mauritius, Morocco, Mozambique, Namibia, Niger, Nigeria, Republic of the Congo,
Rwanda, São Tomé and Príncipe, Senegal, Sierra Leone, Somalia, South Africa, South Sudan, Sudan,
Togo, Tunisia, Uganda, Zambia, Zimbabwe.

**Absent** (of the 54 UN-recognised African states): **DR Congo, Eritrea, Libya, Seychelles,
Tanzania** — plus **Western Sahara / SADR** (the 55th AU member).

## Key institutional commitments

- Endorses the **Africa AI Council**, under the Smart Africa Steering Committee, co-chaired by the
  **African Union Commission** and the **[[itu]]**, to align continental and global digital-transformation efforts.
- Guiding principles: **sovereignty, inclusivity and diversity**; safeguards for privacy, ethics,
  transparency and explainability; regional/global/public-private collaboration; and robust
  frameworks for **cross-border data flows**.
