## Feed post — Digital for Development (D4D) Hub

Sao Tome and Principe is one of Africa's smallest nations.

It is also one of the latest to take concrete steps toward translating a continental framework into a national action that safeguards people's data privacy and uses local data for prosperity.

On 9–10 July 2026 government officials and data practitioners explored how the African Union Data Policy Framework (AUDPF) can be adopted at a national level to enable trusted, secure, and inclusive data governance. The Data Governance Capacity Building Workshop brought together representatives from across institutions to build shared ownership of an issue now central to economic and social transformation everywhere.

"True success of this initiative will be measured by our collective ability to translate the knowledge into public policies, institutional reforms, improved administrative practices and concrete actions that contribute to accelerating the country's digital transformation." — Paula Medina, Representative of EU Delegation to São Tomé.

A sincere thank you to implementing partners GIZ African Union, AUDA-NEPAD and KICTANet for working with the Government of Sao Tome and Principe.

This activity is implemented as part of the Data Governance in Africa #D4DataGOV, an initiative supported by the African Union, European Commission, Germany, France, Belgium, Estonia and Finland.
