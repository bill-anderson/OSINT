**Binary artefact:** [[2023-06-27 Country Report - Rebuilding Identification and Stakeholder Engagement in the Central African Republic.pdf]] (28 pages).

Research report on the rebuilding of the Central African Republic's national ID system, argued through a stakeholder-engagement lens.

**Key evidence:**
- The **2013 crisis destroyed an estimated 60–80% of identity documents**, undermining citizens' right to identity and access to public services. From **2014** the government began re-issuing ID documents, initially *without* the previously adopted biometric (fingerprint) features.
- The report argues the CAR government is **not involving local stakeholders** in redeploying its ID system — with popular perceptions of **foreign control** of local populations' identity and a humanitarian identity crisis bound up with contested notions of being "100% Central African."
- **Finding: the ID contracting process is opaque.** The contract awarded to private company **[[al-madina]]** was not subjected to scrutiny by the relevant government bodies (notably the public-procurement department). A predecessor arrangement, **Centrafrique Carte System (CCS)**, held ID contracting in 2016–2017.
- Frames effective foundational ID as dependent on political stability for inclusive development, usage, retention and archival storage.

Supplies the deep background to the later town-by-town Al-Madina CNI rollout (Bossangoa 2023, Bouar/Berbérati, Birao 2025) and the price dispute — see [[caf--dpi-id]], [[al-madina]].
