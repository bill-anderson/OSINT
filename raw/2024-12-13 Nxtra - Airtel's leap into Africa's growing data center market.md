Airtel Africa, one of the largest telecom operators in Sub-Saharan Africa (present in 14 countries, serving over 160 million subscribers), has announced plans to enter the African data center market through its data center arm, Nxtra by Airtel, offering managed services for enterprises, corporates, banking & finance, hyperscalers, government, content providers and international carriers.

In India, Nxtra already runs 200+ megawatt (MW) capacity data centers. Nxtra Africa aims to replicate this, "democratizing cloud services" in Africa beyond South Africa. DCD spoke with Yashnath Issur, CEO of Nxtra Africa. He named Lagos and Nairobi as larger regional hubs (with access to large populations, enterprise businesses, submarine cable landing infrastructure, domestic fiber and skilled manpower).

Continent coverage: Issur lists Uganda, Tanzania, the DRC, Zambia and Rwanda as other high-potential countries where Nxtra will create data centers: "These countries will emerge as important Edge locations in the future," enabling global hyperscalers and enterprises to host content closer to African customers.

Nxtra plans to create total capacity of up to 180 MW across multiple African countries. The largest facilities are being built in Lagos and Nairobi, followed by Mombasa, Dar, Kampala, Kinshasa and Kigali, with more to follow. Airtel Africa already owns over 77,000 km of fiber across its markets plus submarine cable assets. Construction has started on a 40+ MW data center in Lagos and another in Nairobi.

Nxtra emphasizes advanced modular design handling AI workloads through high structural floor loading and next-gen cooling, multi-layer security (under-vehicle scanners, AI/ML surveillance, intrusion detection, man traps, centralized command center), and sustainability with new-build design PUE below 1.4.
