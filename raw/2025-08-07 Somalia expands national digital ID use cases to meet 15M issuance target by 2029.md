# Somalia expands national digital ID use cases to meet 15M issuance target by 2029

> ## Excerpt
> National ID made compulsory for passport applications from 1 Sep 2025 and for
> domestic travel from 1 Jan 2026; CBS mandated the ID (from 29 Jul 2025) for
> opening/updating bank accounts — use-case expansion to drive adoption toward 15m by 2029.

---

The Somali government has continued to increase use cases for its national digital ID to drive adoption and meet the target of issuing the ID to at least 15 million people by the end of 2029.

The government has directed that the ID will be compulsory for all passport applications from September 1, 2025, while the credential will also be required for domestic travel from the start of 2026. "Today, a crucial meeting was held at the Immigration and Citizenship Agency [ICA] headquarters. It was decided that starting September 1, 2025, the Somali passport will require a National ID Card for applications," NIRA announced on its X account.

The meeting was chaired by Internal Security Minister Abdullahi Sh. Ismail Fartaag; it was resolved that all domestic travellers must present their national ID card for verification from January 1, 2026. Attendees included the State Minister of Foreign Affairs, Ali Omar; the Director General of ICA, Mustafe Sh. Ali; and NIRA boss Abdiwali Abdulle. The move aims to enhance the security and reliability of the Somali passport, which has historically faced increased scrutiny and travel restrictions.

As part of expanding uses, the Central Bank of Somalia announced on July 29 that all citizens wishing to open new bank accounts or update existing ones will be required to present a national ID card. A preceding decision mandated the ID for transport ministry documents such as driver's licenses.

Somalia launched its new national ID system in 2023 with World Bank support, as a key aspect of its digital public infrastructure development.
