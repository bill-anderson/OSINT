# Kenya Artificial Intelligence and Other Emerging Technologies Policy, 2026 (draft)

Kenya's first national **AI and Other Emerging Technologies Policy**, published in
**draft** by the State Department for ICT and the Digital Economy (PS Eng. John K.
Tanui) and opened for **public participation on 21 July 2026** — written comments to
`aipolicy@moict.go.ke` (copy `legal@moict.go.ke`) by **4 August 2026** — ahead of a
final version and a proposed **AI Governance Act**. Full policy document held as the
companion artefact; key structure excerpted below.

## What the policy is

- Sets a single national framework for the **governance, development, deployment and
  use** of AI, and reaches beyond AI to other emerging technologies (blockchain,
  robotics, extended reality, quantum) that meet **two of four tests**: rapid
  evolution, convergence with other technologies, gaps in existing law, or
  unresolved ethics/safety/rights risks.
- **Risk-based, lifecycle approach.** Systems already running at commencement get a
  **two-year grace period** for a retrospective risk assessment (**three years** for
  high-risk legacy systems); new systems must comply once regulations are in force.
  Ordinary personal, non-commercial use and controlled research are out of scope.
- **Extraterritorial reach:** foreign providers, model developers and data-annotation
  firms whose AI is used in Kenya, or whose outputs affect Kenyans, are covered.
- Knits together the **Data Protection Act 2019**, the Kenya Information and
  Communications Act, the **Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act 2018**, and the
  **National AI Strategy 2025–2030** rather than replacing them; aligns with Vision
  2030, BETA, the **AU AI Strategy (2024)**, the **[[au-data-policy-framework|AU Data
  Policy Framework]] (2022)** and the UN Global Digital Compact (2024).

## Nine thematic pillars (Chapter 3 — Policy Statements)

1. Research, Innovation and Commercialization Ecosystem Development
2. Sectoral Applications of AI and other Emerging Technologies
3. Capacity and Human Capital Development
4. Environmental Sustainability and Green Intelligence
5. Governance, Legal and Regulatory Frameworks
6. Data Ecosystem Management
7. Ethics, Safety, Security and Inclusion
8. Infrastructure and Digital Readiness
9. Sovereignty and Strategic Autonomy

## Institutions proposed

A **National AI and Other Emerging Technologies Council** (strategic direction) plus a
coordination office; and a **Kenya AI Safety Institute** to assess high-risk systems
for bias, security and ethics before wide deployment. Implementation is a "hybrid
model" spread across ministries, counties, regulators, universities, private sector
and civil society. The policy commits to **sovereign digital infrastructure** —
investment in local data centres, cloud and high-performance compute to cut
dependence on foreign-owned systems — and requires Kenya-generated data to be
governed under Kenyan law.
