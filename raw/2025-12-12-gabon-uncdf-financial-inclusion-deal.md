The **United Nations Capital Development Fund (UNCDF)** has agreed to support Gabon in modernizing its national financial-services ecosystem and increasing financial inclusion. **About 54% of adults in Gabon are unbanked despite growing mobile money**, per the World Bank's Global Findex 2025.

The UNCDF will help the government develop policies to strengthen financial governance, aligned with the **National Growth and Development Plan (PNCD 2026-2030)** and **CEMAC's Regional Financial Inclusion Strategy (2023-2027)**. Digital Economy Minister Mark Alexandre Doumba said digital financial services are a major pillar of financial inclusion. Special attention will go to reforms promoting competition among providers, essential digital infrastructure, and consumer protection; **UNDP acts as facilitator**.

The deal comes months after Gabon concluded a partnership with **Visa** to accelerate financial inclusion (at the Africa CEO Forum in Côte d'Ivoire in May 2025). Gabon has also launched a digital visa (e-visa) platform to modernize consular services and discussed integrating digital payments with it, alongside advancing guidelines for a national digital identity. Gabon launched a new biometric national ID system in July 2024.
