# Closing the Adoption Gap: How AI Is Being Built in the Global South

**New America** report (REPORT / IN DEPTH), by **Akash Kapur** and **Gillian Rosenberg**, published **9 July
2026**. Empirical basis: **14 interviews, 6 structured surveys, and 53 case studies**. A previous draft was
presented at the inaugural Shangri-La Series (May 2026). PDF filed as companion artefact (linked in
frontmatter).

## Argument

The AI debate fixates on frontier capability; the more consequential problem is the **adoption gap** between
what models can do and what they actually do — acute in the **Global South** amid resource constraints, limited
state capacity and fragile institutions. Adoption is a problem of **alignment with real-world systems, not
model capability**; contextual fit beats benchmark performance. The report examines eight parameters — four
technical (compute, models, data, infrastructure) and four non-technical (social/institutional embeddedness,
capacity and skills, trust, funding).

## Cross-cutting findings (as reported)

- Adoption is a **whole-of-society** challenge, not resolvable at the technical layer alone.
- **Constraint is generative**: scarcity is producing frugal engineering, modular compute, edge deployment and
  participatory data collection — potentially relevant well beyond the Global South.
- Adoption gaps **compound for already-excluded populations, particularly women**.
- **The compute conversation has the wrong unit** — most Global South deployments fine-tune or run inference,
  not frontier training.
- **Open source needs a deployment ecosystem, not just a model** — grassroots orgs still lean on commercial US
  providers for onboarding/support.
- **AI-sovereignty laws can undermine the deployments they mean to protect** — data/cloud localization rules can
  block domestic start-ups from the compute they need. *(Bears on the localization-vs-access tension in
  [[gov.protect]] / [[infra.store]].)*
- **Embed AI in trusted platforms/workflows** — WhatsApp integration consistently drives adoption; voice/image
  interfaces reach low-literacy users but poor connectivity defeats them.
- **DPI stacks are an underused source of AI-ready data**, but only with consent/reuse governance in place —
  ties AI adoption to [[dpi.exchange]].
- **Community data collection** (capturing idioms and cultural meaning, with royalty/licensing models) is
  reshaping how AI is built in the region; **benchmarking is a hidden source of bias** (Global North
  assumptions).

Opening case: a Google Health / Thailand Ministry of Public Health diabetic-retinopathy deployment (2018) that
performed at >90% in the lab but faltered in 11 rural clinics — illustrating the lab-to-field adoption gap.
Cites Microsoft's *AI Diffusion Report 2025* (Global North genAI use 24.7% vs Global South 14.1%, and widening).
