# Rebuilding Sudan's digital infrastructure amidst conflict

VoxDevTalks interview with **Magdi Amin** (Managing Partner, African Renaissance Ventures) on
rebuilding Sudan's digital infrastructure to enable humanitarian aid and economic recovery.

- **Humanitarian scale.** Since the April 2023 SAF–RSF war, >half of Sudan's population needs
  humanitarian aid; **~15 million displaced** (International Rescue Committee). The war collapsed
  digital communications infrastructure, hitting banking, mobile payments and telecoms.
- **Single-provider concentration.** Only one provider — **Bank of Khartoum's Bankak** app — reliably
  kept moving money and remittances, creating systemic vulnerability (not all Sudanese have accounts or
  smartphones).
- **Pre-war fragility.** Years of underinvestment and sanctions; a national switch existed for
  intra-bank interoperability but broader investment was blocked. The **2021 coup** halted
  international support, including a major cash-transfer programme.
- **DPI as recovery tool.** Amin advocates **Mojaloop** (open-source, UPI-like interoperable payments)
  to reduce single-provider dependency, working with the central bank and development partners —
  acknowledging DPI has succeeded mainly where civil-service capacity is strong.
- **Policy takeaway.** Cash transfers are "here to stay" in humanitarian response; the challenge is
  delivering them fast in fragile environments via resilient, interoperable, inclusive digital rails.
