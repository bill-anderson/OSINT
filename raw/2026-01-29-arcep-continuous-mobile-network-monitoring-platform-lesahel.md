# Régulation des télécommunications : L'ARCEP inaugure une plateforme de contrôle continu des réseaux mobiles

*(Le Sahel / ONEP is the Niger state daily. Capture is not endorsement.)*

Niger's telecoms regulator **ARCEP (Autorité de Régulation des Communications Électroniques et de la Poste)** inaugurated a **continuous mobile-network monitoring platform** on **Wednesday 28 January 2026** at its Niamey headquarters. The platform was inaugurated by the Minister of the Civil Service, Labour and Employment, Aissatou Abdoulaye Tondi (representing the PM), alongside ARCEP DG **Colonel-major Idrissa Chaibou**.

The platform provides permanent, exhaustive supervision of mobile networks — technical, economic and regulatory — via three integrated tools: **TRAFSCAN, QUALCOP and NETFIX**. It enables automated, continuous, non-intrusive collection of technical/economic data with controlled integration into operators' information systems and network cores. Officials framed it as a "lever of digital sovereignty" and an instrument for piloting the national digital economy, better consumer protection and equitable operator contributions, consistent with President Tiani's "refondation nationale."

The platform was deployed by Tunisia-based provider **INFOCOM INTERNATIONAL**, which has deployed similar systems in Mali, Togo, Mauritania, Guinea-Bissau and the Central African Republic.
