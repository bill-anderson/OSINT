Nigeria is preparing to regulate AI, potentially among the first in Africa with comprehensive AI legislation. The National Digital Economy and E-Governance Bill was expected to be approved by end of March, giving regulators wide powers over data, algorithms and digital platforms. It introduces a risk-based framework subjecting high-risk AI systems (finance, public administration, surveillance, automated decision-making) to stricter oversight, mandating developers submit annual impact assessments. Regulators could impose fines of up to N10 million ($7,000) or 2% of an AI provider's annual Nigerian revenue. NITDA DG Kashifu Abdullahi (to Bloomberg) said the goal is to regulate AI early, closing gaps since the 2024 draft AI strategy.
