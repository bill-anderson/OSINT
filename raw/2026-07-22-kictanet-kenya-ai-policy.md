Kenya wants your input on its AI policy, deadline August 4
Kenya’s Ministry of Information, Communications and the Digital Economy has
opened its draft national AI policy for public comment *(Attached),* and
the window to weigh in closes on August 4, 2026.
The policy will shape how Kenya governs artificial intelligence across nine
key areas, from research and commercialisation to data protection, ethics
and national sovereignty. Estimates put AI’s potential economic value to
Africa at between 1.2 and 1.5 trillion dollars. How that value gets shared,
regulated and protected depends on decisions being made right now.
Whether you work in technology, health, agriculture, finance or civil
society, this policy will eventually touch how you build, work and operate.
Public participation is the one chance to influence that direction before
it’s finalised.
