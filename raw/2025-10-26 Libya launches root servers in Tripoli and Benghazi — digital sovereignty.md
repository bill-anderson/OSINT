Libya's Tripoli-based **[[gaci-libya|General Authority for Communications and Informatics (GACI)]]** announced the official entry into service of **Libya's root servers**, operating in the data centres of Al-Madar Al-Jadid Company in Tripoli and Benghazi — implementing the **National Telecommunications and Informatics Strategy 2023-2027** and enhancing digital sovereignty.

The servers were officially listed on the global map of root-server locations (root-servers.org), making Libya among the countries hosting this vital internet infrastructure. GACI described it as a strategic shift toward **national internet independence** — accelerating local internet response, reducing access time, enhancing cybersecurity, and ensuring the stability and independence of government and private-sector service systems — framed as national integration between public and private sectors toward "a secure and advanced digital Libya."
