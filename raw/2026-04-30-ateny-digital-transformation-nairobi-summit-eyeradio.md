# Ateny outlines S. Sudan's digital transformation plans at Nairobi summit

*Eye Radio, 30 April 2026 (Memoscar Lasuba)*

South Sudan's government spokesperson told the 15th Connected Africa Summit 2026 in Nairobi that the country is moving to strengthen its digital sector through key national projects aimed at expanding connectivity and improving data governance. Minister Ateny Wek Ateny said the government is in the process of establishing a national ICT Authority to guide the country's digital transformation.

He said construction of a national data center is ongoing and now halfway complete, a development expected to improve data management and digital services. He added that a Data Protection Act is in progress and will soon become law, complementing the Cybercrime and Computer Misuse Act (passed 2025, assented by the President in 2026).

Ateny also noted the government has begun laying fibre optic cables covering about 2,700 kilometres inland to improve connectivity. "Now we are about to establish the ICT Authority... we have a data centre being built. It is now halfway complete and the data protection act is on its way to becoming law," he said.
