The Nouakchott national data centre was inaugurated on Thursday 8 May 2025 by Mauritanian President Mohamed Ould El-Ghazaouani, Minister of Digital Transformation and Modernisation of Administration Ahmed Salem Bede Etvagha, the EU Ambassador and EIB officials. The project bolsters Mauritania's digital sovereignty and supports the West African Regional Communications Infrastructure Programme (WARCIP).

The EIB provided a €15 million loan for the Tier 3 data centre, including construction, construction-monitoring/auditing and Tier III certification by the Uptime Institute. The centre enables Mauritania to host national data locally and improve compliance with data-exchange security standards. It is part of WARCIP (co-financed with the World Bank), which builds fibre-optic backbone links in north-eastern, southern and south-eastern Mauritania with international connection points near the Senegal and Mali borders. Mauritania also connected to the Africa Coast to Europe (ACE) submarine cable in 2010 and awarded a contract to Ellalink for a second submarine cable (both EIB-financed). Once operational, the centre will be managed by International Mauritania Telecom (IMT), bringing together the government (via Mauripost) and the country's three telecom operators.
