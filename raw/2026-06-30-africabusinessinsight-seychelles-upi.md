# Seychelles to roll out India's UPI instant payment system by end-2026 | 30 June 2026
Africa Business Insight (secondary trade outlet — the underlying primary is the NPCI International–Central Bank of Seychelles MoU signed during PM Modi's 29 June 2026 visit; source the CBS / NPCI / India MEA primary at ingest)

Seychelles has announced plans to introduce India's Unified Payments Interface (UPI) by the end of 2026. The rollout will be implemented through collaboration between the Central Bank of Seychelles, local financial institutions and NPCI International (the global arm of the National Payments Corporation of India, which operates UPI). The system will enable residents and visitors to make instant mobile payments directly from bank accounts using smartphones and QR codes.

The tourism sector — with India an increasingly important source of visitors — is expected to be a major beneficiary. Financial inclusion is another stated objective. The CBS has indicated regulatory preparations are underway, including payment standards, cybersecurity requirements and interoperability between local banks and the UPI network, with training programmes for institutions and merchants expected before full launch. Seychelles would become one of the first small island economies in Africa to implement UPI nationally.
