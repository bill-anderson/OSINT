The State Department for Immigration was allocated **KES 15.13 billion** in the 2024/25 budget for digitizing government services. Population-management services receive **KES 6.88 billion**, funding 7.5 million (digital, third-generation) ID cards — meaning issuance of the **Unique Personal Identifier (UPI)** may finally begin — plus 1.5m e-passports, 6.3m birth certificates and 2.3m e-visas.

KES 7.03bn goes to migration and citizen services (two high-capacity passport printers, moving 48,000 refugees to designated camps, facial recognition at nine key border crossings, and a visa-free regime). During FY24/25 the department will bring 78 agencies online through the **Integrated Population Registration System (IPRS)** and make 33,800 government services available on eCitizen. The Treasury allocated KES 197.48bn to the Ministry of Interior in total.
