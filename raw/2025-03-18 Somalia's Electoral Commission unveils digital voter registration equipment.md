# Somalia's Electoral Commission unveils digital voter registration equipment ahead of local polls

> ## Excerpt
> The National Electoral Commission (chair Abdikarim Ahmed Hassan) unveiled digital
> voter-registration equipment in Mogadishu, to be tested at local government
> elections set for 30 June 2025 — the first direct "one person, one vote" local
> elections in five decades.

---

Somalia has officially unveiled new voter registration equipment in preparation for upcoming elections. The announcement was made by Abdikarim Ahmed Hassan, Chairman of the National Electoral Commission and the Borders Committee, during a ceremony in Mogadishu.

Mr. Hassan said the newly introduced equipment will support efforts to hold modernized, free, and fair elections. The equipment will be tested during the local government elections scheduled for June 30, 2025. "The first phase of this process will be public voter registration, and we are encouraging all citizens to participate," he said. This will mark the first time in five decades that Somali citizens directly elect their local leaders; previous elections used a delegate system.

Somalia's political system underwent a significant overhaul in 2024 following an agreement between the federal government and regional states, approved by Parliament, introducing a presidential system designed to implement "one person, one vote." This begins with local elections, followed by regional parliamentary and presidential elections in November. Mr. Hassan referenced a benchmarking tour to Ghana and participation in the General Assembly of the Union of African Electoral Commissions.
