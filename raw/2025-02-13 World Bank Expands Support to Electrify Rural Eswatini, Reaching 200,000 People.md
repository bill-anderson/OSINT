MBABANE, February 13, 2025 - A new World Bank financed project is helping set Eswatini on the path to universal energy access by 2030. The Accelerating Sustainable and Clean Energy Access Transformation (ASCENT) project for Eswatini will help the country reach the remaining 12% of the population with electricity access.

Deputy Prime Minister Honorable Thulisile Dladla: "This initiative is about the people of Eswatini, ensuring that every Liswati has access to electricity... as reaffirmed in our endorsement of the Dar es Salaam Energy Declaration at the recent Mission 300 Africa Energy Summit."

The support builds on the ongoing Network Reinforcement and Access Project Eswatini and follows the country's Rural Electrification Plan and the National Energy Policy 2018. ASCENT Eswatini aims to provide electricity access to 200,000 people, connecting 50,000 new households over five years - a 20% increase from the 249,014 households connected in 2023. The initiative uses both grid and off-grid solutions, particularly in underserved rural communities.

ASCENT Eswatini is the ninth phase of the ASCENT program for Eastern and Southern Africa. It received $39 million in concessional funding from IDA, a loan of $51 million from IBRD, and a performance-based grant of $10 million from the Livable Planet Fund (LPF1). World Bank Country Director Satu Kahkonen and Minister of Economic Planning and Development Honorable Thambo Gina welcomed the project. (Press Release No: 2025/048/AFE.)
