The Uganda Registration Services Bureau (URSB) has officially handed over the mandate of marriage registration to NIRA, executed under the Rationalisation of Agencies and Public Expenditure (RAPEX) framework. This consolidates civil registration so that births, marriages, and deaths are all recorded under a single national body. A handover ceremony took place at NIRA head offices in Kololo, Kampala, on April 24, 2025.

URSB Registrar General and CEO Mercy K. Kainobwisho said the handover follows amendment of the relevant laws (the URSB Act, 2024 and the Registration of Persons Act, 2024, enacted August 2024) placing marriage registration with NIRA. URSB extended logistical support (internet, office space, medical insurance) during the transition, and the Ministry of Public Service ran change-management training.

In FY 2022/2023, URSB collected sh1.94b in Non-Tax Revenue from marriage registrations; 2,717 civil marriages were registered in Kampala that period. NIRA Executive Director Rosemary Kisembo said NIRA has already registered 2,770 marriages and is seeing an increase: "the whole story — from birth, to marriage, to death — will now be stored in one place." With over 27.7 million Ugandans already captured in NIRA's national register, officials framed the RAPEX transfer as "the first seamless transition and handover" since the reform began.
