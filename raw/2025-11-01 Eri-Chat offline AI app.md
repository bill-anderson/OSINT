**Provenance note:** Shabait is the Eritrea Ministry of Information organ (state
voice). This is a Q&A/interview feature (by Sabrina Solomon) profiling a locally
developed app. Capture is not endorsement.

Guest: **Naod Goitom Isaac**, a 25-year-old computer science graduate of the College
of Science and Technology, now working at the Ministry of Information as a software
developer. He has developed **Eri-Chat, an offline artificial-intelligence mobile
application** intended to give Eritreans at home and in the diaspora access to
AI-based information without an internet connection.

Key points from the interview:
- The app runs an **open-source AI offline**; it launched "around six months ago"
  (developer states it became practical and was **launched in April 2025**).
- It is compared to offline knowledge software such as Kiwix / offline Wikipedia,
  but conversational — the depth of data a user can access depends on the phone's
  RAM.
- **Data stays local:** the developer stresses Eri-Chat stores conversation data
  only on the user's own phone (positioned as a privacy advantage over Google,
  ChatGPT or Gemini), saved until the user deletes it.
- Built with C++, Python, Java, XML and Kotlin; biggest challenges were resources
  and lack of senior mentors locally.
- The app has **restrictions on sensitive/obscene topics** (e.g. phone-hacking
  questions blocked).
- Currently English-only — the developer says local languages (Tigrigna, Tigre,
  etc.) are not yet supported because there is insufficient training data.
- Showcased at the **UN Day event at Hotel Asmara Palace on 24 October [2025]**,
  organised by the National Union of Eritrean Youth and Students, to ambassadors,
  UN officials and diplomats.
