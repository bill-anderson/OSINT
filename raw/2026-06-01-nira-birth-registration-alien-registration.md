NIRA has intensified its nationwide birth registration campaign and announced the rollout of a registration exercise for legally resident foreign nationals. At a June 1, 2026 press conference at Police Headquarters in Naguru, Kampala, registrar Claire Ollama said public interest grew after the registration of the child of energy and minerals state minister-designate Phiona Nyamutoro and her husband, musician Eddy Kenzo.

NIRA is encouraging groups of at least 10 parents with children below six months to organise community registration exercises. Children below nine months do not require biometric capture. Birth certificates for children below six months are currently free, and NINs remain free for all eligible applicants. Under the fee structure, birth certificates cost sh10,000 (6 months–7 years), sh20,000 (7–18 years) and sh50,000 (adults 18+). NIRA appealed to churches to integrate birth registration into pre-baptism preparations. "Every child has a name. They need a NIN. Every child deserves a legal identity from birth."

On alien registration: preparations are complete, with a pre-registration portal open to legally resident foreign nationals, who will be issued Alien Identification Numbers (AINs). Eligible applicants must hold valid immigration documents (work permits, dependent passes, permanent residence permits, or other long-term facilities exceeding 90 days) plus valid passports. The registration fee is US$100, paid via URA channels.
