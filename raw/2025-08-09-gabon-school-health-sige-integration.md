As part of modernizing its education system, Gabon's Ministry of National Education has begun integrating school health and well-being indicators into the **Education Management Information System (Système d'Information pour la Gestion de l'Éducation, SIGE)**. The reform aims to strengthen teaching quality and better understand students' learning conditions.

The Ministry continues its digital transformation by adapting its planning, management and monitoring-evaluation tools, optimizing the collection, analysis and use of education data to guide public policy. The SIGE becomes an essential lever for decision-making. Integrating health and well-being indicators marks a shift toward a more holistic approach, allowing authorities to cross pedagogical data with health indicators (hygiene, nutrition, water access, safety) to identify determinants of school performance and act upstream on learning inequalities.
