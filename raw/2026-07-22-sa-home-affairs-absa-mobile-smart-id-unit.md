# Home Affairs and Absa launch South Africa's first bank-based mobile smart ID renewal unit

*By Ali Bahati Juma, Current Affairs ZA, 22 July 2026.*

The Department of Home Affairs (DHA) and Absa have launched South Africa's first bank-based mobile unit for smart ID renewals in a bid to take the service closer to remote, peri-urban and previously underserved communities.

Home Affairs Minister Dr Leon Schreiber and Absa Group CEO Kenny Fihla officiated the launch on Wednesday (22 July) at the bank's Tembisa branch in Gauteng.

## Absa-Home Affairs mobile smart ID renewal

The mobile unit will operate initially in Gauteng. It comes a week after Absa became the fourth bank to join DHA's new digital partnership model after Capitec, FNB and Standard Bank.

The bank is also rolling out smart ID services at its branches, with 13 enabled so far. Customers can also make bookings and track appointments via the Absa banking app.

"For far too long, accessing Home Affairs meant taking a taxi or a bus, missing a day of work, standing in long queues and spending hours simply to apply for an identity document," said Schreiber.

"Our Home Affairs @ home reform programme is changing that forever by bringing Home Affairs to where people live, work and bank."

The digital partnership has expanded rapidly since it went live in March and now includes 327 participating bank branches. If one counts DHA's 214 modernised offices, South Africans could access smart ID services at a total of 541 locations at the time of publishing.

DHA aims to expand the service to 750 bank branches by the end of 2026, bringing access to over 1,000 locations.

"Identity is fundamental to economic participation. This partnership allows us to use Absa's infrastructure, digital capability and national footprint to help make an essential public service more accessible," said Fihla.

Schreiber concluded: "Through Home Affairs @ home, we are making it easier than ever before for South Africans to replace the fraud-prone green barcoded ID book with the secure smart ID card.

"Together, we are closing the chapter on the fraud-prone green barcoded ID book and opening a new chapter of secure, digitalised identity services that delivers dignity for all."
