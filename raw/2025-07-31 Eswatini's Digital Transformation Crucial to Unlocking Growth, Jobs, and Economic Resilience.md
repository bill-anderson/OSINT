MBABANE, July 31, 2025 - Eswatini needs to digitalize, strengthen public finances and address structural economic constraints to sustain growth, according to the latest Eswatini Economic Update (EEU) launched by the World Bank Group, titled: Harnessing the Potential of Digital Technologies for Eswatini's Growth and Job Creation.

Eswatini's economy is projected to grow by about 5% in 2025. The second edition of the EEU identifies digitalization as a key transformative strategy, particularly as the country addresses a 35.4% unemployment rate and structural inefficiencies in agriculture, trade, and services.

Honorable Thambo Gina, Minister for Economic Planning and Development: "This report aligns with the Kingdom of Eswatini's 2024-2028 digital strategy... how digital transformation can contribute to accelerating our ongoing efforts to boost inclusive economic growth and domestic revenues and in so doing reduce reliance on SACU transfers."

Eswatini is making progress in expanding digital access, with nearly 95% of the population now covered by 4G networks. However, only about 58% of people are using the internet. One main reason is the high cost of data, which takes up 3.47% of GNI per capita - above what is considered affordable in the region. The report recommends reforming the telecom market, including restructuring the telecom State-Owned Enterprise, adopting open access policies, and updating regulatory frameworks to promote competition and lower costs. Almost half of the country's SMEs face digital adoption barriers.

Satu Kahkonen, World Bank Division Director for Eswatini: "Realizing this will require bold reforms... including the restructuring of Eswatini Posts and Telecommunications Corporation (EPTC)."

The EEU recommends three core policy pillars: (i) enhance resilience through effective macroeconomic management; (ii) stimulate job creation through private sector development; (iii) provide better and more affordable services through efficient public spending. Policy options include strengthening digital governance through clearer institutional roles and a national change management program; accelerating EPTC reforms with open access; investing in DPI including a modern digital ID system; developing a National Digital Skills Action Plan; and fostering a competitive innovation ecosystem.
