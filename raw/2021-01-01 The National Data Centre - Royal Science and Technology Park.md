The National Data Centre (NDC) is an entity of the Royal Science and Technology Park (RSTP), which strives to provide secure, efficient and reliable ICT services to enterprises as well as aiding e-government development and promoting digital inclusion within the Kingdom of Eswatini.

Objectives: provide quality ICT services to public and private sectors using agile, secured modern technologies; be the e-government development and implementation platform; support research and high-performance computing (HPC); participate in regional and international ICT initiatives; and promote ICT to improve the economy.

Goals & vision: provide modern, agile ICT infrastructure; move Eswatini toward secure e-government and e-commerce infrastructure; deliver a scalable environment for web hosting, secure data repositories and national payment gateways.

Services: Platform as a Service; Infrastructure as a Service; Software as a Service; Remote hand services; Colocation service.

Monitoring: Data Centre Infrastructure Management (DCIM); Fire Detection System; Very Early Smoke Detection Apparatus (VESDA); Water Leakage Detection; surveillance cameras.

Connectivity: carrier-neutral; high-speed internet; two diverse feeds from service providers.

Power: redundant power feeds; 4 diesel generators (500 KVA each); automatic restart after power failure.

Access control and safety: restricted DC room access; 24/7 dedicated security personnel; video surveillance of all zones and racks; secure biometric authentication for access to server farms; off-site back-up for disaster recovery. Server cabinets consist of smart rows of 42U racks using biometric authentication.

Value proposition: the NDC provides IT managed services through advanced infrastructure, enabling companies to outsource ICT functions. Advantages cited: scalability, resiliency (24/7 availability, redundancy), energy metering via smart-row technologies, adoption of AI/ML/blockchain, an e-government platform, threat management, data analytics, business continuity/disaster recovery, and stimulation of e-commerce.
