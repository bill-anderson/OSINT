# Egyptian court judgment tightens data-protection enforcement (Orange Egypt, EGP 10m)

The **Economic Court of Alexandria** (February 2025) ordered **Orange Egypt** to pay **EGP 10 million** in compensation after it replaced a claimant's SIM card without consent (events from Nov 2022), enabling third parties to access her WhatsApp and blackmail her. The judgment holds that data-protection liability can rest on **tort law even absent the (then-pending) executive regulations** of the **[[egypt-pdpl|PDPL (Law 151/2020)]]**, alongside Cybercrime Law No. 175/2018.

The court applied **"custodian's liability"** — quasi-strict liability requiring no proof of fault, avoidable only by proving an external cause. Implications flagged for tech firms/startups: liability without fault; limited defences; uncapped civil indemnification into the millions; and reliance on civil/tort principles until (now) the Nov-2025 Executive Regulations. This enforcement judgment **predates** the Executive Regulations (see [[2026-02-11 Egypt finalises Executive Regulations to the PDPL (Decree 816-2025)]]).
