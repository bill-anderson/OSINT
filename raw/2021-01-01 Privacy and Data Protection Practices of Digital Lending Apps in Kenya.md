# Privacy and Data Protection Practices of Digital Lending Apps in Kenya

> ## Excerpt
> A CIPIT empirical study (7 apps; permission audits + traffic capture) finding Kenya's digital lending
> apps non-compliant with the Data Protection Act 2019 — collecting far more behavioural data than
> creditworthiness requires and silently connecting to third-party trackers.

> **Provenance note.** **2021** report (© 2021; latest citations Jan 2021) — old, but admissible as a
> dated reference. Traffic findings are inferred from endpoints (HTTPS payloads not observed), reflecting
> mid-2020 app versions. Included in the H1-2026 drop despite predating the window.

**Artefact:** ![[2021-01-01 Privacy and Data Protection Practices of Digital Lending Apps in Kenya.pdf]]

## Key facts

- Digital lenders build credit scores from smartphone behavioural data (contacts, location, SMS) —
  personal data as a "private digital ID." Seven apps studied (Tala, Branch, Okash/Opera, KCB, Equity,
  Timiza, Lioncash); several connect to Google location and Facebook Graph APIs at start-up; Branch
  requests microphone access.
- Context: financial access rose 26.7% (2006) → 89% (2019); ~14% of adults had taken a digital loan.
  **April 2020** CBK barred non-deposit-taking digital lenders from CRB access. Argues regulators must
  shift from the financial dimension (interest caps) to the **data** dimension of digital lending.
