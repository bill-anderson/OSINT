Opinion/analysis, 10 January 2026. An account of the ARDA TALK Series (Institute of Foreign Affairs / IFA) led by Safaricom Ethiopia CEO Wim Vanhelleputte, deconstructing the political economy of Ethiopia's digital transformation. Framed within Digital Ethiopia 2030, participants argued digital connectivity should be treated as basic national infrastructure (like roads, electricity, water); that without fair access, economic modernisation stays basic and leaves millions behind. Central theme: the historic liberalisation ending the state telecom monopoly is a governance shift (state as regulator/strategic partner, not exclusive provider), requiring predictable regulation, long-term partnerships and inclusive policy.
