# The Mobile Gender Gap Report 2025

Companion source page for the binary artefact `raw/2025-05-20-mobile-gender-gap-report-2025.pdf`.
Lead author Nadia Jeffrie; fieldwork by Ipsos; funded in part by UK Aid, Sida and the Gates
Foundation. Published May 2025 on 2024 data.

Flagship annual GSMA index of the gender gap in mobile across low- and middle-income countries
(LMICs). Captured as a **reference work — cite, don't absorb**; see [[gsma-mobile-gender-gap-report]].

## Headline figures (2024 data, LMICs — cite by edition, do not promote as current country state)
- Women **14% less likely** than men to use mobile internet across LMICs (unchanged); **1.5 billion**
  women in LMICs now use mobile internet, but adoption growth has slowed.
- Gender gap in **mobile ownership 15%**; **smartphone ownership 14%** (unchanged).
- **Sub-Saharan Africa** among the widest regional gaps in mobile internet adoption (~32% / ~29% in
  the widest-gap regions).

Country-level gender-gap detail (e.g. Nigeria) arrives via its own dated sources that cite this
series.
