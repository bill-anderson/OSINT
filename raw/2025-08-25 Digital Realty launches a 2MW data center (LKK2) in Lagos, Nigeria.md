Colocation giant Digital Realty has launched a new data center in the Lekki area of Lagos. LKK2 adds nearly 2MW of IT capacity across nearly 1,205 sqm (13,000 sq ft) of data hall space and will be interconnected with the existing LKK1 facility, which serves as the cable landing station for the 2Africa subsea cable. LKK2 will also interconnect with LOS1 and LOS2 on Victoria Island.

Managing director Ike Nnamani called LKK2 "a significant milestone in our journey to support digital transformation in Africa." Digital Realty entered Nigeria in 2021, acquiring Medallion Data Centers alongside Pembani Remgro Infrastructure Fund; Medallion has since rebranded to Digital Realty. LOS1 offers 2MW across 200 sqm; LOS2 (680 sqm, at 8 Saka Tinubu Street) opened in 2023. Competitors developing or operating Lagos data centers include WIOCC's OADC, Equinix's MainOne, Kasi Cloud, Airtel, MTN and Rack Centre.
