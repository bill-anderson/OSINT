# Somalia cleared to adopt and print the EAC e-Passport

Somalia has received official authorisation to adopt and print the East African Community (EAC) e-Passport, marking a practical step in its integration into the regional bloc, Somalia's ambassador to Tanzania and permanent representative to the EAC, Ilyaas Ali Hassan, said.

The envoy said in Dar es Salaam on Tuesday, February 17, 2026 that he formally handed over the decision to Somalia's Minister of Internal Security, Gen Abdullahi Sheikh Ismail "Fartaag," and the Director General of Immigration and Citizenship, Mustafa Sheikh Ahmed Dhuxulow.

The EAC e-Passport harmonises travel documents across member states and strengthens document security; it contains an embedded electronic chip with biometric identifiers. Somalia's move comes amid growing immigration cooperation with Tanzania: on February 16, the two countries signed a migration cooperation memorandum of understanding providing visa exemptions for diplomatic passport holders, faster processing for ordinary visas, and structured collaboration between immigration authorities.

Somalia is the EAC's newest partner state, admitted at the Summit of Heads of State on November 24, 2023, and becoming a full member on March 4, 2024 after depositing its instrument of ratification in Arusha. The regional e-Passport was launched on March 2, 2016; rollout among members has been phased (Kenya September 2017, Tanzania January 2018, Burundi May 2018, Uganda December 2018, Rwanda July 2019). Somali media reports in late 2025 cited immigration officials saying the country plans to introduce a new passport in 2026 as part of a wider modernisation drive.
