Burkina Faso's military leader Ibrahim Traoré set a new biometric national ID initiative in motion via a decree approved at a **6 November 2025** Council of Ministers meeting, replacing the legacy CNIB. Minister of Security Mahamadou Sana said the card aligns with the ID standards of the **Confederation of Sahel States (AES)** — with Mali and Niger — and with **ICAO**; the three AES states (after breaking from ECOWAS) launched a common biometric passport in early 2025.

Unveiled 1 December 2025 by the **Office National d'Identification (ONI)** (DG Arzouma Parfait Louré), the **Carte d'Identité Biométrique de l'AES (CIB-AES)** is the **5th generation** of Burkinabè ID (after 1960, 1976, 1984 and the current CNIB). It remains a **national** document — bearing the country name and flag — while carrying the AES logo and the bilingual "AES Identity Card" label: "harmonised at AES level while keeping national sovereignty." Per leFaso.net (6 Jan 2026), the CIB-AES will be deliverable from **age 5**, mandatory from **age 13**, cost **3,500 FCFA**, and use polycarbonate.
