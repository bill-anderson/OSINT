Vodacom launched a data centre in Maputo, Mozambique. Located in Tchumene, Matola, construction of the $25 million facility began in October 2023; it is said to be the country's largest. The carrier-neutral data centre offers direct access to the 2Africa subsea cable, hosted in Vodacom Mozambique's equipment room in Matola.

The launch was attended by Prime Minister Benvinda Levi, who said: "Our country now has 18 data centers, including this one from Vodacom Mozambique... a significant step forward in our country's digital transformation." Vodacom previously installed a modular data centre in Matola in 2013 and launched commercial 5G in Maputo in May 2023, reporting more than 10 million customers in Mozambique. iColo and Raxio also have a presence in Maputo.
