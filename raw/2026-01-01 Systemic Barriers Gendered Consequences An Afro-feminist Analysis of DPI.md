# Systemic Barriers, Gendered Consequences: an Afro-feminist analysis of DPI

> ## Excerpt
> A Pollicy/AIRA study of DPI (digital ID, payments, data exchange) in Uganda, Kenya and South Africa,
> arguing these systems are engineered for **state-centric** goals and reproduce gendered/minority
> exclusion — "digital marginalisation by design and by governance."

> **Provenance note.** Named-analyst analysis (Afro-feminist), not primary evidence; rests on desk review
> + 6 key-informant interviews (no end-users) — weight qualitative claims accordingly. Safaricom
> protest-tracking collusion is a **reported allegation**, not documented. Cover "January 2026" vs a
> "(2025)" citation line — cover date used.

## Key facts

- **Uganda:** Ndaga Muntu ID + UGHub + MoMo Pay; the Jan-2021 election shutdown (5 days, domestic traffic
  −95%, ~UGX 390bn cost) hit women-led informal traders hardest; NIRA ID rules (removing religious head
  coverings) humiliate Muslim women/nuns; 1GB data = 16.2% of average monthly income.
- **Kenya:** Maisha Namba (after failed Huduma Namba); ~43% (state body: 75%) paid bribes for an ID;
  ethnic-minority (Somali/Nubian) citizenship precarity; M-Pesa lifted ~1m out of poverty but 2024–25
  protest tracking alleged via **[[safaricom]]**.
- **South Africa:** the **"Thandi"** critique of **[[mymzansi]]**'s universal-user assumption; strong
  POPIA/Information Regulator guardrails yet persistent gendered exclusion (Home Affairs blocked >1m IDs,
  ruled unconstitutional). Calls for **gender-by-design** DPI governance.
