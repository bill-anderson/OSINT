# SITA and the Paradox of Centralized ICT Procurement (South Africa)

> ## Excerpt
> An AJIS case study (55 GITO interviews) finding that South Africa's statutory centralized ICT
> procurement via **SITA** produces bureaucratic rigidity rather than efficiency — proposing a
> Governance-Implementation Feedback Model.

> **Provenance note.** Peer-reviewed analysis (not primary evidence of the events). Reported figures
> (procurement "1.5–3 years"; pricing "up to 10× market") are interview testimony/single-interviewee
> claims — treat as reported perceptions.

**Artefact:** ![[2026-02-01 SITA and the Paradox of Centralized ICT Procurement in South Africas Public Sector.pdf]]

## Key facts

- **[[sita]]** (SITA Act 88/1998, amended 2002) mandates centralized public-sector ICT procurement; the
  study finds bureaucratic delay, misalignment with departmental needs, workarounds/exemptions, and
  eroding legitimacy — a self-reinforcing "path dependence." 2025 political pressure: a Public Service
  Commission probe ordered; government mulling changing SITA's mandate; SITA targeting Sept 2025 for an
  automated procurement system. Framed as the Global South's centralized digital-governance dilemma;
  recommends a hybrid/tiered model.
