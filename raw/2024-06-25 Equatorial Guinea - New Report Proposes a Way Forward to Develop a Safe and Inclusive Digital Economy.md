World Bank Group press release (25 June 2024, via eTrade for all). The Digital Economy Country Diagnostic for Equatorial Guinea — developed by the World Bank and the Government, supported by the Digital Development Partnership (DDP) — notes the country must step up efforts to capture the digital economy's dividends, focusing on five foundations: digital infrastructure, digital public platforms, digital financial services, digital businesses, and digital skills.

Resident Representative Aissatou Diallo highlighted digital marketplace platforms for firm expansion. Quick-win recommendations: cost-oriented obligations in international-capacity and national-network fibre wholesale markets; a cybersecurity maturity assessment; a national strategy for digital public platforms; strengthening DFS agent networks; an inclusive digital-entrepreneurship roadmap; and a national education ICT policy. Lead author Camila Mejia Giraldo stressed cross-cutting reforms on data quality, institutional coordination, reliable electricity, the legal framework, and bridging the island-continental and gender divides.
