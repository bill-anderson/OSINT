*Law-firm client briefing — attribute to Kemi Advocates. The instrument itself is dated 2025-05-23; this analysis is 2025-06-27.*

The Bank of Tanzania (BOT) has issued amendments to the Financial Consumer Protection Regulations, 2019 (GN No. 298 of 2025, dated 23 May 2025), aiming to strengthen consumer protection and enhance transparency in financial services. Main highlights:

1. **Expanded scope and definitions.** The regulations apply to all financial service providers licensed or supervised by the BOT, except where otherwise prescribed. "Dormant account" period extended from 5 to 12 months; new "inactive account" category (unused for more than 5 months); the High Court of Zanzibar included within "Court".

2. **Prohibition of certain charges.** Providers may not impose interest-rate hikes or non-disclosed late-payment penalties on existing balances, nor maintenance fees on dormant/inactive accounts. Clear guidelines introduced against abusive debt recovery (unlawful collections, false statements, harassment, coercion).

3. **Interest calculation.** Mandatory use of the reducing-balance method for calculating interest on loans.

4. **Consumer access and literacy.** Providers must run programmes promoting access for underserved populations; financial literacy must feature in strategic plans and be delivered by certified educators from accredited institutions.

5. **Clarity and language of agreements.** Technical terms restricted unless necessary and must be clearly explained; agreements must be in English or Kiswahili per the consumer's preference.

6. **Disclosure and approval of fees.** All fees, charges, interest, profits or returns must be fully disclosed before any transaction; new or increased fees require prior written BOT approval; the BOT may prohibit fees to protect consumers.

7. **Consumer asset protection.** Providers are liable for losses from fraud, scams or misuse of consumer assets and must promptly refund consumers unless the loss was due to consumer negligence or fraud; they must discipline responsible employees and notify the BOT.

8. **Data protection.** Providers must protect consumer financial and personal information in accordance with the Personal Data Protection Act.

9. **Complaint handling.** Resolution timeframes clarified with maximum periods (e.g. 12 hours for mobile money payments, 14 days for credit facilities). The BOT has enhanced enforcement powers, including fines of TSh 1 million per day for non-compliance. Consumers may escalate to the BOT for review.

10. **Publication and transparency.** Interest rates, fees, charges and related costs must be published quarterly in Kiswahili and English newspapers and on provider websites within five working days.
