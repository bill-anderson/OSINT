# Gov't approves $9 million for fiber optic design

*One Citizen Daily, 30 June 2025*

On Friday, the fibre optic implementation committee, chaired by Deputy Minister of ICT & Postal Services David Yau Yau, approved a budget of over $9 million US Dollars. This allocation marks the initiation of the design phase for the fiber optic project, signaling a commitment to bridging the persistent digital divide in the world's youngest nation.

The announcement was made by Telecommunication Undersecretary Thomas Gatkuoth Nyak, following deliberations on Phase Two of the project. He said approval of the design budget will pave the way for signing contracts with specialized companies and experts in July 2025 to design and lay down the country's fiber optic infrastructure.

In 2023, the World Bank pledged $67 million to develop broadband infrastructure, enhance public digital and financial management skills, and connect South Sudan to fibre optic networks through Kenya, Ethiopia, Sudan, and West Africa. Despite a 7.6 per cent increase in internet users between 2022 and 2023, the country's connectivity remains among the lowest in the region.
