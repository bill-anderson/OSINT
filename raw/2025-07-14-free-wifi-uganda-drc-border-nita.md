The Government, through NITA-U, has launched a free public Wi-Fi service in Bwera town, Mpondwe–Lhubiriha Town Council, Kasese district, along the porous Uganda–DRC border (a hotspot for ADF rebel activity, including the 2023 Mpondwe Secondary School attack). The initiative is part of a national drive to bridge the digital divide and provide vital communication services.

At the July 13, 2025 launch, State Minister for ICT Godfrey Kabbyanga urged locals to market products online and report security threats, noting the intervention falls under Uganda's Digital Acceleration Project (UDAP), which extends connectivity to all districts and helps decongest spectrum (many Ugandans still rely on bandwidth-heavy 2G). He also warned users about the Computer Misuse Act. NITA-U ED Dr Hatwib Mugasa said the free Wi-Fi stretches across Mpondwe Road, reaching schools, health centres and government offices, with more sites planned.
