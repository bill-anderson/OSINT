Note (ISSUE-009): concerns Econet Wireless Zimbabwe (the opco), distinct from the wider Econet Group.

Per POTRAZ's Q4 2025 Sector Performance Report, Econet Wireless Zimbabwe ended Q4 2025 with 340 5G base stations, 1,825 LTE, 1,997 3G and 2,981 2G stations — a total active network of 7,143 base stations, exceeding NetOne's 4,838 and Telecel's 1,123 combined. In Q4 alone Econet added 42 new 5G, 78 new LTE, 78 new 3G and 78 new 2G stations.

Econet's dominance extends across core metrics: 73.75% of active mobile subscribers (12,374,206 of a sector total 16,778,982), 88.32% of mobile voice traffic (up 1.63pp from Q3), and about 81.2% of mobile internet and data traffic (130.18 PB in Q4, up from 118.58 PB in Q3). The only metric where Econet conceded ground was mobile data traffic share, as NetOne's 18.50% traffic growth (89 new LTE stations in Q4) narrowed the gap marginally.

Sector-wide, mobile internet and data services accounted for 50.75% of total MNO revenue in Q4 2025 — the first time data clearly exceeded half of sector revenue. Total MNO revenue was ZWG 7.74 billion (up 6.33% from ZWG 7.27 billion in Q3). The sector's cost-to-income ratio worsened to 59.95% as operating costs grew faster than revenue, with Econet's aggressive 5G/LTE rollout contributing to near-term cost pressure.
