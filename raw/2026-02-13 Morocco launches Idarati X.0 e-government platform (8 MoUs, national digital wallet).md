Morocco signed **eight memorandums of understanding** (10 February 2026, Rabat) to launch the operational phase of the **[[idarati-x0|"Idarati X.0"]]** project — a meta-application designed as a unified entry point for digital public services, led by the Ministry of Digital Transition and Administrative Reform with the National Commission for the Control of Personal Data Protection (**CNDP**).

The initiative structures an ecosystem around a **national digital wallet**, letting citizens access multiple services through a single interface using the **electronic national ID** as the primary authentication mechanism. Signatories included the Ministry of Youth/Culture/Communication, the Ministry of Transport and Logistics, the National Social Security Fund (CNSS), the National Road Safety Agency, and the National Agency for Land Conservation, Cadastre and Cartography (ANCFCC). The rollout is planned in four phases over roughly six months, using **"privacy by design" and "security by design."** It extends the **[[digital-morocco-2030|Morocco Digital 2030]]** strategy; in 2024 Morocco placed 600 public services online, and ranks 90th globally / 4th in Africa in the 2024 UN EGDI (0.6841).
