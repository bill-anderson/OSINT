The Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN), with NIBSS, officially launched the Non-Resident Bank Verification Number (NRBVN) platform, allowing Nigerians in the diaspora to obtain a BVN remotely without physical presence. CBN Governor Olayemi Cardoso called it "a milestone in Nigeria's financial inclusion journey" using secure digital verification and KYC. Remittance flows through formal channels increased from $3.3 billion in 2023 to $4.73 billion in 2024 (aided by the willing buyer, willing seller FX regime); the CBN targets $1 billion monthly remittances. NIBSS MD Premier Oiwoh said many banks had already come onboard. The NRBVN is part of a broader framework including the Non-Resident Ordinary Account (NROA) and Non-Resident Nigerian Investment Account (NRNIA).
