A bill on the identification of natural persons in Madagascar received Senate approval on 4 July 2025 (days after the lower chamber), among 10 bills endorsed that day. Consultant Jean Aholou (who contributed to drafting, and is National Coordinator of the WURI Benin project) said the legislation modernises Madagascar's national ID system, aligning it with international standards and PRODIGY objectives, and recognises the unique identification certificate as a legal ID document. The bill also overhauls the civil registration mechanism to enhance inclusion and human-rights respect.

PRODIGY (launched ~2020) receives about $143 million from the World Bank to build DPI facilitating access to public services and growing the digital economy; a key objective is meeting SDG 16.9 (legal ID for all, including birth registration, by 2030); the project runs to 2026.

The development follows an imminent call to tender for large-scale printing of national biometric ID cards; the government held an online early-market-engagement event on 11 June 2025. The tender seeks a contractor to supply a secure national ID card production system, consumables, printing equipment, card personalisation, packaging/delivery, supervision and training. Likely candidates already hold PRODIGY contracts: Laxton (biometric registration kits) and IN Groupe (awarded a contract to modernise the civil registry and create a digital ID system; IN Groupe completed a takeover of Idemia Smart Identity).
