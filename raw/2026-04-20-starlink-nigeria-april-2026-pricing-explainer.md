By Eunice Olaleye

Is Starlink finally getting cheaper? After years of price increases, rollbacks, regulatory struggles, and seemingly static waitlists, it's completely reasonable to want a straight answer.

No, the residential subscription price hasn't dropped in April 2026. But the full idea is actually more interesting than that, and depending on where you live and what you need, the value proposition has quietly shifted in your favour.

Here's everything you need to know right now about this

## What Starlink Currently Costs in Nigeria — April 2026

The current official residential subscription rate, which is N57,000 monthly, and the hardware price reflect the current importation cost. That price has been steady since June 2025, when Starlink implemented a 50% increase from N38,000 to N57,000, in alignment with the Nigerian Communications Commission's January 2025 approval of a 50% tariff increase for telecom operators, the first such regulatory adjustment in over a decade.

Here's the full breakdown as of April 2026:

| Plan | Monthly Cost | Who It's For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Residential | N57,000 | Homes & small offices (fixed location) |
| Business / Priority | N159,000 | Multi-user offices, high-capacity needs |
| Roam | N38,000 | Flexible use across Nigeria |
| Hardware (Standard Kit) | N590,000 (one-time) | Dish, router, mount, cables |
| Hardware (Mini Kit) | N318,000 (one-time) | Portable, travel-friendly option |

The Standard Kit includes the dish, router, mount, and cables, and you can install it yourself without hiring a technician. That's genuinely one of the better things about Starlink. No engineer, no installation window, no waiting around.

## Why does the price keep changing, and what controls it?

If you've been following Starlink in Nigeria since 2023, you'll know the pricing history is like a thriller. Prices go up, the NCC pushes back, prices come down, then go back up again. So what's actually causing this?

The exchange rate is the single biggest driver of Starlink price changes in Nigeria. The standard hardware kit is priced at approximately $368 USD, which at N1,580 per dollar in March 2026 translates to approximately N581,440 before import costs are added.

Starlink prices in Nigeria are denominated in Naira, but they're tied to global USD costs. Every time the Naira weakens, your subscription cost in real terms goes up, even if the Naira figure on the website hasn't changed.

What happened with the NCC is worth understanding, too, because it directly affects whether prices rise again. Back in late 2024, Starlink announced a price increase to N75,000 per month.

The NCC's Director of Public Affairs stated the commission had not approved the increment, calling it a contravention of Sections 108 and 111 of the Nigerian Communications Act, 2003. The hike was suspended.

Then, with the NCC officially granting a sector-wide 50% tariff increase in January 2025, Starlink finally had its regulatory green light, and the N57,000 price came into effect. Future NCC decisions will directly determine whether Starlink can raise or must hold its subscription prices in Nigeria going forward.

So the honest answer to "will prices drop?" is not without either a significantly stronger Naira or a regulatory intervention. Neither looks like it's going to happen soon.

## The Availability Problem Nobody Talks About Enough

Getting a Starlink subscription isn't just about affording it; you also have to be able to get one.

In high-demand locations such as Victoria Island, Ikoyi, Lagos Island, Surulere, Lekki, and specific areas of Abuja, Starlink has temporarily paused new Residential Plan orders due to network congestion. New customers are asked to pay a refundable deposit and join a waitlist, with no confirmed timeline for when these areas will open again.

Since February 2026, Starlink has made its Business Priority service available in high-demand urban centres, including Lagos, Abuja, and Port Harcourt, as a workaround, but at N159,000 per month, it's a steep price for users who simply want home internet.

The good news is that two major southern cities — Port Harcourt and Benin City — have been freed up for new Residential Plan activations, thanks to a newly activated ground station at Okun Aja in Lagos State, which features 20 antennas, with room for 36 more.

If you're outside congested urban centres such as Ibadan, Enugu, and Kaduna, or in most semi-rural areas, you can still order the Residential Plan directly without joining a waitlist.

## Comparison Between Starlink vs. MTN 5G vs. Airtel 5G

Is Starlink actually worth the price tag compared to what MTN and Airtel are offering? Let's see the following:

| Feature | Starlink | MTN 5G | Airtel 5G |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Average Download Speed (Urban) | 50–100 Mbps | 200 Mbps – 1 Gbps | 200–700 Mbps |
| Average Download Speed (Rural) | 50–100 Mbps | Very limited/unavailable | Very limited/unavailable |
| Latency | 25–60 ms | ~15 ms | ~20 ms |
| Monthly Cost (Unlimited) | ₦57,000 | ~₦15,000–₦30,000 | ~₦20,000–₦30,000 |
| Hardware Cost | ₦590,000 (one-time) | ~₦80,000 | ~₦35,000–₦60,000 |
| Data Caps / Throttling | None — truly unlimited | FUP throttling applies | FUP throttling applies |
| Rural Availability | Nationwide (clear sky needed) | Major cities only | Major cities only |
| Performance During Power Outages | Unaffected (dish + UPS) | Cell towers affected | Cell towers affected |

From the above, MTN 5G delivers average speeds between 400 Mbps and 1 Gbps in major cities, with latency consistently around 15 ms, which on paper absolutely smokes Starlink's 50–100 Mbps average. But 5G speed in Nigeria varies considerably outside dense city centres, and the service is virtually nonexistent in most rural areas.

## What It's Actually Like to Use Starlink in Nigeria

Although Starlink performs well across Nigeria, heavy rainfall or dense tree cover may occasionally affect signal strength. Mounting the dish in an open area ensures optimal connectivity.

The key installation note that a lot of buyers overlook is that you must have a clear, unobstructed view of the sky. Trees, tall buildings, or any overhead obstruction between the dish and the horizon will degrade your connection, sometimes dramatically. The Starlink app has a built-in obstruction checker before you buy; use it.

## Should You Buy Starlink in April 2026?

Yes, if…

- You're in a rural or semi-urban area where 5G doesn't reach your street
- You work from home and can't afford the downtime that power grid instability causes for cell towers
- You need truly unlimited data without hidden FUP throttling
- You run a small business or tech operation where ₦57,000/month is justifiable as an infrastructure cost

No, if…

- You're in central Lagos or Abuja and can access MTN FibreX or Airtel Fibre, both of which are cheaper monthly and faster in raw speed
- You don't have a UPS or generator to keep the router powered during outages
- Your budget can't accommodate the N590,000 hardware cost upfront

For now, if you need reliable internet outside Nigeria's major urban corridors, Starlink remains the most dependable option on the market. If you're setting up a remote workspace, running a tech operation in Ibadan or Enugu, or simply tired of watching your connection collapse every time there's a rainstorm and NEPA takes the street, it's worth every Naira of that N57,000.
