Lusaka, 13th September, 2024 – The Ministry of Lands and Natural Resources (MLNR) has successfully concluded a four-day exhibition at Cosmopolitan Mall in Lusaka, which took place from Tuesday, 10th September, to Friday, 13th September 2024. During this period, the exhibition attended to over 250 clients, providing them with valuable information and services.

In an interview, ICT Officer Ms. Gwendoline Hamabele highlighted the government's recent adoption of the Government Service Bus (GSB), which hosts the Zambia Integrated Land Administration System (ZILAS) deployed by the SMART Zambia Institute. She emphasized that clients are now encouraged to open an account on Zamportal to access electronic government services, marking a significant shift towards digitalization.

Ms. Hamabele announced that the government has transitioned to digital platforms for all its services, making it a prerequisite for clients to open a Zamportal account to pay ground rent. Furthermore, she explained that the issuance of title deeds is now digitally processed, streamlining the land administration process.

This development demonstrates the government's commitment to leveraging technology to enhance service delivery and improve citizen engagement. By going digital, the government, through the Ministry of Lands and Natural Resources (MLNR), aims to increase efficiency, transparency, and accessibility in land administration services.

The SMART Zambia Institute is a Division under the Office of the President mandated to coordinate and implement electronic government (E-Government) for the citizens, businesses and within government for improved service delivery. The Institute was established through a Government Gazette notice No 836 of 2016.
