MTN Nigeria launched, on 1 July 2025, what it describes as West Africa's largest Tier III data centre. Named the Sifiso Dabengwa Data Centre, the facility has a capacity of nine megawatts and cloud infrastructure the telco claims rivals Amazon, Microsoft and Google. Phase 1 delivers 4.5 megawatts; phase 2 will follow based on demand. Per CTO Yahaya Ibrahim, both phases are valued at $235 million and the cloud infrastructure alone costs $20 million. GM for Infrastructure Roger Shutte said the facility is expected to scale to 14 or even 20 megawatts.

CEO Karl Toriola framed it as enabling growth and AI readiness. Chief Enterprise Business Officer Lynda Saint-Nwafor said the cloud service offers self-orchestration (a first in Nigeria), meaning developers can independently manage compute and storage like on AWS or Azure. She highlighted naira pricing (distinct from merely accepting naira) and lower latency from hosting data in Lagos, arguing Nigerian startups no longer need to rely on global providers. Toriola declined to disclose the expected ROI.
