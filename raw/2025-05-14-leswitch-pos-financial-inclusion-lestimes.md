# Banking sector in push for financial inclusion

*Lesotho Times, 14 May 2025. By Moroke Sekoboto. And innovative payment system.*

THE Central Bank of Lesotho (CBL), with the banking sector, has reaffirmed its commitment to driving innovation in the national payment system. Speaking at the launch of Point of Sale (POS) functionality on LeSwitch at the Lehakoe Recreational and Cultural Centre, Standard Lesotho Bank's Samuel Koatla said the initiative began in 2016, with conceptualisation concluding in 2021.

Koatla explained that the National Payment Switch, commonly referred to as LeSwitch, localises and modernises Lesotho's payment infrastructure. It comprises two streams: instant payments and card payments. The instant payments stream was implemented in 2022, enabling instant fund transfers across mobile networks. In 2023 the sector moved to the card stream (ATM then POS). The ATM phase was piloted October 2024 to February 2025, enabling cash withdrawal from any ATM at reduced cost.

"On Sunday, 11 May, the Central Bank of Lesotho, in collaboration with the four commercial banks, launched the third iteration … with the introduction of local transactions on POS machines," Koatla said — allowing cardholders to swipe at any merchant using any POS device, regardless of bank. With domestic ATM and POS transactions now processed locally through LeSwitch, transaction fees are expected to decrease over time; previously the international clearing system incurred high costs passed on to consumers.

The CBL and four commercial banks intend to expand LeSwitch to e-commerce/retail after ATM and POS. LeSwitch will not replace Visa or Mastercard for international transactions — it focuses on domestic payments — and will not eliminate cash. CBL Director of Payments and Settlements Mothetsi Sekoati said the mission was a secure, competitive, efficient and innovative payment ecosystem supporting financial inclusion.
