# Egypt to shift public services to the Digital Egypt platform

The Egyptian government plans to shift certain in-person services **exclusively to the [[digital-egypt|Digital Egypt]] platform**, per Cabinet spokesman **Mohamed Al-Homsani**, following a presentation by Communications Minister **Amr Talaat** to PM **Mostafa Madbouly**. Digital Egypt currently offers **175+ online services** — civil-status documents, traffic violations, real-estate registration, social insurance. Officials acknowledged many citizens still prefer obtaining essential documents (e.g. birth certificates) in person.

Launched in 2020, Digital Egypt is a cornerstone of the **Digital Transformation Strategy 2030**, intended to streamline access, cut bureaucracy and improve transparency.
