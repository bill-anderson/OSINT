# NCRA, Ministry of Agriculture partner on National Digital Farmer Registry integration

> ## Excerpt
> NCRA and the Ministry of Agriculture (MAFS/FSRP) agree to integrate the National Digital Farmer Registry with NCRA's biometric NIN system to de-duplicate and verify farmer identities, with the registry potentially hosted at NCRA's Tier-3 data centre. Anchored on the 2016 NCR Act and 2022 parliamentary ratification.

---

In a major step toward improving agricultural data systems in Sierra Leone in line with the Feed Salone flagship project of President Julius Maada Bio, the Director General of the National Civil Registration Authority (NCRA) welcomed officials from the Ministry of Agriculture and Food Security (MAFS), the Food Systems Resilience Project (FSRP), Ministry of Communications, Technology and Innovation (MoCTI) and project implementation vendor/firm (Tiwai Memory Masters) to discuss the development and integration of the National Digital Farmer Registry with the NCRA's Biometrics Digital ID and National Identification Number (NIN) system.

The meeting centered on leveraging the NCRA's NIN database to ensure that each farmer's record is unique, reliable, and verifiable. Through this integration, the Ministry of Agriculture's Digital Farmer Registry System will authenticate and verify farmers' identities using the NIN, eliminating duplications and ensuring accurate registration. This initiative aligns with the Government of Sierra Leone's digital transformation agenda, the National Civil Registration Act of 2016, and the 2022 Parliamentary Ratification, which mandate the use of the NIN as a unique identifier for accessing and delivering government services without discrimination.

The integration addresses longstanding challenges of fragmented and inconsistent farmer data faced by MAFS and its donor partners. "Over the years, multiple efforts to compile farmer records lacked consistency and uniqueness," noted Dr. Kepri, Project Coordinator for FSRP, and Mr. Dominic Bao, Head of ICT at MAFS. The upcoming Farmer Registry is set to serve as the foundational database for all digital agriculture systems, e-extension services, and other agtech platforms under MAFS.

Discussions also explored the possibility of hosting the Farmer Registry and other agriculture-related digital systems at the NCRA's tier 3 Data Centre to enhance security, local ownership of farmers' data and interoperability. Dr. Sankoh, Director of Technology at MoCTI, noted the need for infrastructure sharing among MDAs, a drive the NCRA has started with its ID and Data Center infrastructure.

The DG of NCRA, Mr. Mohamed Mubashir Massaquoi, expressed NCRA's commitment to making the national ID services and infrastructure accessible to individuals, MDAs, and the private sector: "The NCRA is fully committed to supporting MAFS on this project to ensuring its success, through our institution's efforts in providing all necessary tools to reducing duplication, wastage and to providing accountability across the sector." The DG immediately constituted a technical team from the NCRA ICT Directorate, MAFS, MoCTI, and Tiwai Memory Masters to advise on the NIN-Farmer Linkage project.

The NCRA has set up a robust Civil Registration and National ID infrastructure including the establishment of a Tier 3 data center that houses the country's civil registration and ID systems, with capacity to host or co-host other institutions' digital systems.
