Zimbabwe has officially launched its National Artificial Intelligence (AI) Strategy (2026-2030), positioning computational sovereignty as a cornerstone. The strategy marks a shift in how the nation manages data, aiming to dismantle a status quo in which most national and commercial data resides in foreign-owned clouds — a state described as "digital displacement" posing risks to national security and economic privacy.

The strategy establishes a Sovereign National Cloud to keep data within Zimbabwe's borders. The drive is rooted in protecting strategic assets: AI-driven predictive models for agriculture will analyse soil health and rainfall locally, preventing proprietary mapping data from being exploited externally, while high-resolution geological and mineral discovery maps will be housed on secure domestic servers. It identifies Heritage-Based Education 5.0 as the engine for producing AI specialists, data scientists and "ethical hackers," and formalises High-Performance Computing (HPC) centres as national assets.

Implementation is overseen by the AI Strategy Implementation Office (AISIO), supported by Treasury funding and the Universal Service Fund (USF) managed by POTRAZ. The roadmap includes immediate development of the National AI Act to provide legal scaffolding for data residency and algorithmic transparency. "Our data is our heritage," the strategy notes. "To control our data is to control our future."
