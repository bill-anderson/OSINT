# The State of Cash Dependency and Digital Financial Inclusion in Africa

> ## Excerpt
> Africa runs ~74% of global mobile-money transaction volume ($1.1tn across 1.1bn accounts, 2024), yet
> cash remains the default — over 90% of mobile-money value is cashed out immediately. Not a technology
> problem but an incentives problem.

> **Provenance note.** Named-author report (Michael Thompson, Yale); the market-tier framework
> (Catalytic/Tipping Point/Nascent) is the authors' own; $330bn SME-gap figure is IFC 2017 (dated).

**Artefact:** ![[2026-06-01 The State of Cash Dependency and Digital Financial Inclusion in Africa.pdf]]

## Key facts

- The "cash-in/cash-out trap": digital payments don't yet beat cash for the individual merchant, so value
  is withdrawn immediately. Costs: **$330bn SME financing gap**, an **$8bn/yr remittance "tax"**, 85%
  informal employment. Avg remittance fee to SSA 8.7% vs 3% SDG target.
- Prescription: make digital cheaper than cash for merchants; mandate real-time interoperability (PAPSS);
  create reasons for value to stay digital. Lessons drawn from India (the stack), Brazil (PIX mandate),
  Ethiopia (building everything at once). Framed by Mo Ibrahim as economic sovereignty.
