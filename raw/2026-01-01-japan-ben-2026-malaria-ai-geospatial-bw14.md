# Japan — Benin malaria AI/geospatial project (TICAD), 2026

Japan is financing a US$2.3 million project in Benin combining AI, drones and geospatial mapping to identify high-risk mosquito breeding areas and target larvicide spraying across six pilot municipalities.

## Description

"Benin launched a $2.3 million Japanese-funded project that will combine artificial intelligence, drones and geospatial mapping to strengthen malaria prevention in six pilot municipalities." "The government launched the Integrated Action for Malaria Prevention project in Cotonou on Tuesday, June 30. Japan is financing the initiative with $2.3 million." "Geospatial mapping tools and artificial intelligence will analyze environmental data to identify areas with the highest risk of mosquito breeding," with drones then used to spray WHO-approved larvicides.
