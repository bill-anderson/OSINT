# Rising Repression Meets Global Resistance: Internet Shutdowns in 2025

> ## Excerpt
> Access Now / #KeepItOn's annual report: **313 shutdowns in 52 countries in 2025** (a record). Africa is
> a heavy locus — ~31 shutdowns across ~15 sub-Saharan states (36 / 18 incl. North Africa).

> **Provenance note.** Counts are "latest as of publication" and subject to revision. **Correction to a
> common claim:** the report documents **no "order to Starlink" in Uganda** — Uganda's Jan-2026 blackout
> is described only as cutting internet/social media. LEO-satellite (Starlink) orders were in Chad, Eq.
> Guinea and Tanzania.

## Key facts (Africa)

- **Tanzania** the standout offender (**8** shutdowns; five-day nationwide blackout during the disputed
  29 Oct 2025 election). Cameroon 4 (a cross-border cut hit CAR via the WACS cable); Kenya 3 (3rd-year
  exam-time Telegram block, now litigated); DRC (Goma, M23); Togo (protest throttling); Eq. Guinea
  (Annobón offline since Jul 2024). LEO-satellite shutdowns tripled globally to 14/7 countries.
- **Resistance wins:** the **ECOWAS Court** ruled Senegal's shutdown unlawful; the **ACHPR** condemned
  Tanzania and Uganda; the AU Election Observation Mission cited shutdown impact for the first time; and
  the **ICC** (Dec 2025) recognised the link between shutdowns and crimes against humanity.
