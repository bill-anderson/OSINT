The Postal Regulator Services, in collaboration with Seychelles Postal Services, the Ministry of Lands and Housing (MLH), the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS), the Department of Information, Communication and Technology (DICT), the Attorney General's Office, Land Transport Division and the Local Government Department, has received Cabinet approval to implement a National Addressing and Postcode System. This initiative aims to enhance the structure, accessibility, and management of address data across the country, in line with international standards.

The Cabinet endorsed the adoption of the S42 address standard, a globally recognized format designed to improve the uniformity and precision of postal addressing systems. The introduction of this format will contribute significantly to more efficient service delivery, accurate location mapping, urban planning, emergency response, and improved national statistics.

A key component of the system's foundation is the establishment of a robust legal framework to guide and legitimize the assignment and management of addresses. The legal framework is currently being developed by the technical committee in close consultation with the Attorney General's Office. Once finalized, the draft legislation will be submitted to the Cabinet for approval. This legal backing is essential to ensure consistency and authority in the implementation process.
