**CSquared Group** (pan-African wholesale infrastructure) has added capacity on the **[[2africa-cable|2Africa
West]]** submarine system to its own West Africa subsea network, to **expand route diversity and improve
resilience** of international/regional connectivity across West and Central Africa. 2Africa — the world's
longest open-access subsea system, first to link East and West Africa continuously — had its core completed
**Nov 2025** with an initial reach of **33 countries**.

The move responds to West Africa's **landing-station concentration** (Accra, Lagos, Abidjan), where a single
disruption hits many networks at once — **three times in the last 28 months**: the Mar 2024 Côte d'Ivoire
seabed-landslide cable damage, and two June 2026 Abidjan-area subsea cuts that disrupted Ghana and neighbours.
CSquared's new 2Africa West capacity adds an **independent subsea route** to its existing architecture (which
includes the **[[equiano-cable|Equiano]]** system landing in Nigeria/Togo, reaching Ghana, Benin, Burkina Faso
via terrestrial backbone), enabling **automated rerouting** across physically diverse paths. Its fibre backbone
connects landing points inland across Burkina Faso, Mali, Guinea, Sierra Leone and Liberia. CTO **Samuel Owusu
Yeboah** framed it around fault-tolerant, uninterrupted service.
