Morocco implemented a **Cloud First Policy** as a cornerstone of its **[[digital-morocco-2030|Digital Morocco 2030]]** strategy, establishing cloud solutions as the mandatory default choice for all public-administration technology investments. Salma Tazi, Director of Cloud Infrastructure and Offshoring at the Ministry of Digital Transition, said "no investment will be planned or executed without first considering cloud solutions as a priority."

Implementation rests on three institutional pillars: a **cloud excellence centre** (with the Digital Development Agency, **[[add-morocco|ADD]]**) to create technical standards; a **national cloud observatory** launching in 2027; and training programs. All ministries must rethink data classification and management. For the private sector (just 40% of SMEs used cloud in 2024), the government plans "Move to Cloud" and "Post Move to Cloud" incentive programs linked to job creation. Morocco is also embracing an emerging **AI First** strategy, positioning itself as a digital hub connecting Africa and Europe.
