# AU Protocol on Digital Trade expected to boost DPI in Africa: AfCFTA SG

By Ayang Macdonald — Biometric Update, 21 April 2026

The Secretary General of the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA), Wamkele Mene, is hopeful that the adoption of the African Union (AU) Protocol on Digital Trade will inspire African governments to increase their investments in digital public infrastructure (DPI) in the push for an inclusive digital future and a single African digital market by 2030. In a keynote at the GITEX Africa event in Morocco this month, Mene made a strong case for digital infrastructure as a lever of Africa's single digital market ambition.

The Protocol on Digital Trade was adopted in 2024, but its eight annexes were adopted by African Heads of State in February last year, paving the way for a comprehensive framework for digital trade on the continent. Issues addressed in the annexes include cross-border digital payments, cross-border data transfers, digital identity, fintech, advanced technologies like AI and blockchain, and digital safety and security. The blueprint will go into full force once ratified by at least 22 State parties.

Mene said with this rules-based framework in place, the challenge is now on African governments to build the necessary digital infrastructure. He lauded progress by countries like Morocco. "This protocol will underpin all of the progress that we have made. It will establish a harmonized regulatory framework, enable the private sector to invest in digital public infrastructure, help our banks to conduct and enable payments across borders in a manner that is interoperable, and most importantly, facilitate and nurture emerging technologies," Mene told a hall in Marrakech.

Mene cited IFC projections that Africa's digital economy will reach $721 billion by 2050. He also estimated that by 2035, Africa will require over 700 data centres to power its digital economy: "This gap remains a significant opportunity and a challenge... an opportunity for investment, and an opportunity for us to expand our capacity as a continent."
