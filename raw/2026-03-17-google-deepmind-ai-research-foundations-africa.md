# Broadening advanced AI education across Africa (AI Research Foundations)

*Google's own announcement (the primary behind the deleted AI-mill rewrite), captured excerpt.*

Google DeepMind is launching additional courses of its AI Research Foundations curriculum —
advanced AI education for technical learners across Africa, developed with pedagogy experts and
academics at University College London, available at no cost on Google Skills; learners build and
fine-tune a language model from the ground up. Google.org is supporting the rollout in African
classrooms with **US$4 million in funding to the FATE Foundation**, in partnership with the
**African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS)** — lecturer toolkits and train-the-trainer
programmes. The curriculum is localized to African contexts with AIMS; "one in five people on the
planet live in Africa [but] 1.8% of [AI] research come[s] from the continent." Four courses live
since Oct 2025; two more 17 Mar 2026; final two late April and June 2026 (per University World
News's promoted account, same date). Delivery partners include Data Science Africa (15 partner
universities across 11 countries, per DSA's own note, 2026-03-05).
