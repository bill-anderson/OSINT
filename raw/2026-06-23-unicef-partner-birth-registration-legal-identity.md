The United Nations Children's Fund (UNICEF) has launched an expression of interest call to select a national NGO in Tanzania that will support the country in expanding birth registration and legal identity services, specifically for children and youth aged 17 and below. Interested candidates had up to July 3 to bid for the project, part of the EU-funded BRIDGE-Tanzania initiative, slated to run from August 1, 2026 to August 31, 2027.

Tanzania is advancing its digital public infrastructure (DPI) implementation, with birth registration a focus area as the foundation of legal identity. Tanzania used to be among the Sub-Saharan African nations with low birth registration, but recent government-led reforms including a Simplified Birth Registration System have increased under-five registration to between 61-76 percent in the last five years. UNICEF has played a critical role since establishing a partnership with the government in 2012.

The country earlier this year announced plans to introduce a bill on infant biometric registration in parliament, after conducting a pilot in 2025 with UNICEF support. The project targets ten districts in the regions of Tanga, Mwanza and Dar es Salaam. UNICEF is looking for an NGO with experience in community work related to birth registration and identity.
