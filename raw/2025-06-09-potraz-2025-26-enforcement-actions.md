POTRAZ has laid out its enforcement roadmap for data protection compliance under the Cyber and Data Protection Act (CDPA), signalling a structured but firm approach. The timeline begins with voluntary compliance measures in 2025 and culminates in stricter penalties, including fines and criminal sanctions, by 2026.

According to POTRAZ Director General Dr Gift Kalisto Machengete, the strategy is designed to encourage voluntary compliance before punitive measures. The first phase focuses on education, support and warnings. POTRAZ will issue public notices outlining regulatory priorities, and high-risk sectors like banking, healthcare and telecoms will receive targeted guidelines. Businesses are encouraged to self-assess, with corrective warnings and clear deadlines issued before penalties apply. A Data Controller Licence handover ceremony was scheduled for October 10, 2025.

POTRAZ will conduct routine inspections; investigations will be triggered by consumer complaints, whistle-blowers, cross-border data transfer violations and suspected unlicensed data processing. While 2025 emphasises collaboration, 2026 marks a shift toward stricter enforcement: non-compliant organisations may incur hefty fines scaled by severity, repeat offenders risk losing their licence, and in extreme cases (large-scale breaches, deliberate negligence) POTRAZ may pursue criminal charges. Any organisation processing personal data must register, and businesses transferring data outside Zimbabwe must notify POTRAZ. Machengete likened compliance to "wearing a seatbelt" — essential even if inconvenient.
