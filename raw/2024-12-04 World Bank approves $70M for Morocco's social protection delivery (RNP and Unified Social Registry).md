The World Bank's Board approved the **US$70 million Morocco Second Identity and Targeting for Social Protection Project** (4 December 2024), building on the first project that designed and implemented the **National Population Registry (RNP)** and **Unified Social Registry (RSU)** and enrolled about **half the Kingdom's population**.

In 2018 King Mohammed VI called for a harmonized social-protection system, leading to a unified social registry; **Law 72-18** established the National Population Registry, the Social Registry, and the **National Register Agency** (supported by three decrees). Key achievements: replacing RAMED health insurance with **AMO-TADAMON** and, in December 2023, introducing the **Direct Social Benefit** program covering over half the population (annual budget ~1.6% of GDP); the RSU serves as a single-entry point for new social programs. The new project has three components — reinforcing/extending the RNP and operationalizing the National Register Agency; expanding the RSU; and project management — extending coverage to rural/remote areas (digital/remote applications), aiming to impact at least five programs by end-2024 and eight by 2028.
