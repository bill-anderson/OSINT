The National Space Program Management Office (GGPEN) has held an institutional meeting with UNITEL to assess opportunities for a strategic partnership aimed at strengthening internet connectivity and advancing digital transformation initiatives in Angola.

The discussions, held on Thursday, brought together GGPEN Director General, Zolana Joao, and UNITEL Chief Executive Officer, Amilca Safeca, focusing on potential collaboration within the framework of the Conecta Angola Comercial project.

According to officials, the meeting sought to identify areas of synergy that would support startups, promote technological innovation, and accelerate digital transformation across the country. Central to the dialogue was the expansion of connectivity services, particularly in remote and underserved regions.

Both institutions examined avenues for joint initiatives linked to the space economy, highlighting the growing intersection between satellite-enabled solutions and terrestrial telecommunications infrastructure. The engagement also emphasized the importance of closer cooperation between Angola's space and telecom sectors as part of broader national development priorities.

The talks concluded with a shared commitment to further explore collaborative projects designed to enhance digital access, improve network reach, and contribute to Angola's evolving digital ecosystem.
