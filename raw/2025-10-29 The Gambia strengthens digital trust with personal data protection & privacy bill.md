Parliament in The Gambia approved a draft Personal Data Protection and Privacy legislation. The Ministry of Information, Media and Broadcasting Services described it as "a major step forward in strengthening the protection of personal data, safeguarding the privacy rights of citizens, and building trust in The Gambia's digital transformation journey" — for the first time establishing a comprehensive legal framework for the personal data and privacy rights of all Gambians. The bill aligns with the government's **Open Data Strategy 2023-2026**.

World Bank DPI expert Tariq Malik praised the move, calling it a major step toward digital trust that "closes an important gap"; he suggested next steps of implementation, awareness raising, capacity-building for oversight, and enforcement. The article notes that in 2024 The Gambia signed an MoU with **Presight** to support its digital-transformation ambitions, with a first phase covering digital ID and digital government systems.
