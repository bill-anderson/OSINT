# Digital IDs poised to go live in September

The National Registration Bureau (NRB) says it is at an advanced stage of rolling out **digital national ID cards**, with launch scheduled for **September** (2026). The system will let people with smartphones store their national IDs on their devices and present them when accessing services; the digital IDs contain the same information as the current plastic IDs. NRB Principal Secretary **Patrick Machika** said the move would reduce plastic-ID printing costs and offer convenience — "you can forget your wallet and your ID, but most of the time you do not forget your phone."

Machika clarified the digital IDs will **complement, not replace**, plastic IDs, and that a person must already be registered with a national ID before self-registering for the digital ID on their phone. Centre for Human Rights and Rehabilitation Executive Director **Michael Kaiyatsa** welcomed the initiative but warned technological innovation "should not come at the expense of equal access, particularly for people living in rural areas and those who are digitally excluded," urging stronger legal frameworks, cybersecurity, digital literacy and safeguards against data breaches and identity theft.
