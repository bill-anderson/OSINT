Following an executive order signed by President Joseph Nyuma Boakai on **28 April 2025**, it is now obligatory for Liberian citizens and foreign residents to enroll in the **National Biometric Identification System (NBIS)** and obtain an ID card. Boakai said the move tackles identity fraud, illegal migration and impersonation; foreign residents staying more than 90 days must obtain a foreigner ID card (enabling SIM registration and residency verification).

The initiative is implemented by the **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** with government and private-sector partners, enabling access to banking, healthcare and education. The NIR oversees enrollment, ID distribution and verification via its query and e-verification platforms (fees set by the Board of Registrars); all ministries, agencies and private institutions must use the NIR database for verification. The order stresses that data collection and management must align with Liberia's data-protection legislation.
