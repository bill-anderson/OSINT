# SIIPS 2025 — State of Inclusive Instant Payment Systems in Africa

> ## Excerpt
> AfricaNenda's fourth annual census of instant payment systems in Africa — the payments layer of DPI.

> **Provenance note.** AfricaNenda flagship (with World Bank/UNECA; research by Genesis Analytics/Lime
> Group; Gates-funded). No printed date; `published` is a **proxy** (~Nov 2025 — data cutoff June 2025,
> references the Sept 2025 WAEMU launch as post-cutoff; SIIPS launches annually in November). The
> orientation lead dated it 2026-03; internal evidence favours Nov 2025. Historical values were
> **restated via the World Bank Atlas method**, so 2020–23 figures differ from prior editions
> (methodological, not a contradiction). PAPSS declined data; its country list is stated as unverified.

## Key facts (as of June 2025)

- **36 live IPS** (up from 31): 33 domestic across **25 countries** + 3 regional (GIMACPAY/CEMAC,
  PAPSS/continental, TCIB/SADC) reaching 22 countries; combined coverage 31 countries.
- **5 new domestic systems since Jul 2024**: Algeria (Switch Mobile), Eswatini (EPS Fast Payment,
  Dec 2024), Libya (LYPay), Sierra Leone (SAPS), Somalia (SIPS) — 4 cross-domain.
- **Volumes 19.7bn (2020) → 64.6bn (2024), ~35% avg annual (volume)**; **values $775.7bn → $1,980.6bn,
  ~26% avg annual (value)**. Avg transaction: mobile money $11, cross-domain $95, bank $154.
- **Nigeria's NIBSS Instant Payment (NIP) = first African IPS rated "mature"** on the 2025 Inclusivity
  Spectrum (10 progressed, 15 basic, 10 unranked). EthSwitch and eKash moved to "progressed."
- **Ownership pivot: 17 of 36 central-bank-owned** (up from 11); SARB took 50% of BankservAfrica.
  19 domestic + 4 regional (COMESA/EAC/ECOWAS/WAEMU) IPS in development.
- Standards: 14 IPS on ISO 20022, 12 on ISO 8583. Exclusion gap: **42% of adults lack an account; 49%
  have never made a digital payment (2024).** Persistent gender gap (NIP: women $66 vs men $121).
