INTERPOL's **I-CEMAC** project (2019-2024; EUR 4.9m; funded by CEMAC) strengthens border security across the six **CEMAC** states — Cameroon, Chad, Congo, Gabon, Equatorial Guinea and the Central African Republic — by extending INTERPOL's secure **I-24/7** global communications network and databases to selected border points (targeting 40) and modernizing the six INTERPOL National Central Bureaus (NCBs).

The project enables screening of passengers and travel documents at transport hubs, improves connectivity and **interoperability among national border systems**, and strengthens NCB infrastructure. Updates: Gabon completed four border posts (Eboro, Myo-Kye, Léconi, Port-Gentil) with automated database searches (Mar 2024); in **Equatorial Guinea**, an assessment at NCB Malabo (Feb 2023) preceded I-24/7 extension to 10 sites including the Malabo, Bata and Mongomeyen airports and border crossings (Kiosi, Mongomo, Dimbala); in Cameroon, connected sites (Douala/Yaoundé) logged 6,000+ searches in 72 hours.
