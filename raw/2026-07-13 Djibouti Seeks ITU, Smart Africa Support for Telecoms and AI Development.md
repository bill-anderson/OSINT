# Djibouti Seeks ITU, Smart Africa Support for Telecoms and AI Development - We are Tech

> ## Excerpt
> Djibouti strengthened partnerships with the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), Smart Africa and other international stakeholders to accelerate its digital transformation and artificial intelligence agenda.

---
-   **Djibouti strengthened partnerships with the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), Smart Africa and other international stakeholders to accelerate its digital transformation and artificial intelligence agenda.**
-   **The government aims to build on its position as a regional connectivity hub by expanding digital services, AI capabilities and digital inclusion.**
-   **Authorities are preparing the country's first national AI strategy, which forms part of the Vision Djibouti 2035 plan to transform the country into a "Smart Nation."**

Djibouti continued implementing its digital economy strategy during the World Summit on the Information Society (WSIS 2026), held in Geneva from July 6 to July 10.

On the sidelines of the summit, Minister of Communication, Posts and Telecommunications Mohamed Abdoulkader Moussa Helem held a series of meetings with international partners to accelerate projects in telecommunications, digital transformation and artificial intelligence.

The minister met with Doreen Bogdan-Martin, Secretary-General of the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), and Cosmas Zavazava, Director of the ITU Telecommunication Development Bureau (BDT).

The discussions focused on international support for several priority initiatives, including the digitalization of public services, skills development in digital technologies and artificial intelligence, and efforts to reduce the digital divide between urban and rural areas.

Separately, Mohamed Abdoulkader Moussa Helem met with Lacina Koné, Chief Executive Officer of Smart Africa, to discuss aligning Djibouti's national digital projects with broader continental initiatives on data sovereignty and artificial intelligence.

The discussions covered the Djibouti Digital Foundation (DFN) project and the Eastern Africa Regional Digital Integration Project (EARDIP).

The new partnerships aim to reinforce the digital strategy of a country that has already established itself as a regional connectivity hub. Djibouti's strategic location on the Red Sea has made it a landing point for several international submarine cables linking Africa, the Middle East, Europe and Asia, positioning the country as one of the Horn of Africa's main internet transit hubs.

The country also operates three data centers and an Internet Exchange Point (IXP), infrastructure that is expected to play an expanding role in hosting digital services locally.

Djibouti's digital infrastructure continues to support growing internet usage. According to the latest DataReportal figures, the country had approximately 772,000 internet users at the end of 2025, representing an internet penetration rate of 65%. Active mobile connections reached 616,000, with more than 92% operating on mobile broadband networks.

Djibouti is also working to establish a national artificial intelligence ecosystem. In January 2026, the Ministry Delegate for the Digital Economy and Innovation launched work on the country's first national AI strategy with support from the United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Western Asia (ESCWA) and UNESCO.

The roadmap forms part of Vision Djibouti 2035, the government's long-term plan to transform the country into a "Smart Nation." Beyond infrastructure development, Djibouti now aims to strengthen AI skills and expand practical applications of the technology. The country's participation in the AI for Good Summit, held alongside WSIS 2026, reflects that objective.

The government expects its discussions with the ITU, Smart Africa and Chinese authorities to provide technical support for deploying AI applications in public services, strengthening domestic capabilities and deepening Djibouti's participation in regional digital governance initiatives.
