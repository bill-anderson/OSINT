Digital Realty inaugurated its new ACR2 data centre in Accra at the end of October 2025. The carrier-neutral facility has 1.7 MW installed IT capacity and space for 500 server racks over 1,100 sqm, sits within a dense network of fibre routes and subsea cables (including 2Africa), and connects to Digital Realty's global platform of 300+ data centres. Managing Director Joseph Koranteng (Digital Realty Ghana) said it offers banks, fintechs, telecoms and cloud providers reliability, compliance and connectivity — "the same infrastructure quality as in London, Amsterdam, or Johannesburg, without leaving Accra."

The launch follows Digital Realty's August opening of a fourth Lagos data centre. Digital Realty entered Nigeria in 2021 via the $29m Medallion acquisition (part of a $500m ten-year Africa plan), and in 2022 completed the $3.5bn Teraco acquisition (South Africa), now operating ~15 African facilities (also Kenya, Mozambique, Nigeria). Context: Africa held <2% of global colocation capacity as of mid-2023; the market is projected to rise from $1.94bn (2025) to $3.85bn by 2030 (Mordor Intelligence, 14.7% CAGR). Digital Realty reported $4.49bn revenue and $1.22bn net profit for the first nine months of 2025.
