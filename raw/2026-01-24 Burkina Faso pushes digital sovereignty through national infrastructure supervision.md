Burkina Faso launched work on a **Digital Infrastructure Supervision Centre** to strengthen national oversight of digital public infrastructure and reduce exposure to external digital risks — a core pillar of its digital-sovereignty strategy. Led by the Ministry of Digital Transition, the ~**$5.4 million** facility (completion targeted for October 2026) will centralise oversight of the national backbone, secure cyberspace operations and supervise domestic data centres "instead of relying on external monitoring." (Corroborating account of the supervision-centre launch already recorded on the [[BFA]] hub.)
