Revenue Services Lesotho (RSL) and Standard Lesotho Bank (SLB) launched an integrated electronic payment system (Maseru, 11 February 2026) to enhance convenience, efficiency and security in tax transactions. RSL Acting Commissioner General Obed 'Nete said the system simplifies tax payments and makes them secure and reliable, building on a partnership begun in 2022.

The rollout includes three digital platforms — **USSD, internet banking and the Standard Lesotho Bank mobile application** — enabling individuals and businesses to pay Corporate Income Tax, PAYE, VAT and customs duties electronically. 'Nete said the integration reduces manual processes and improves accuracy, noting 103 customs-duties and 148 inland-tax transactions already recorded; SLB CEO Samuel Seboka said full integration allows seamless payments via the bank's digital platforms. The initiative forms part of broader efforts to modernise revenue collection and support Lesotho's digital transformation.
