Speech by Mr Phil Mnisi, Governor of the Central Bank of Eswatini and Chairman of the COMESA Committee of Governors of Central Banks, at the Eswatini Payment Switch Fast Payments product launch, Ezulwini, 11 December 2024. (BIS Review published 14 January 2025.)

Introduction: The launch marks completion of the first phase of the three phases to be delivered by the Payment Switch Project. The Fast Payments phase will be followed by Open Banking, and the Point of Sale and ATM Switching features. Fast Payments ushers in real-time payments across all payment service providers, both banks and non-banks.

Background: The Central Bank of Eswatini, with the Eswatini payments industry, embarked on a project to implement a domestic payment switch. This aligns with the National Payment Systems Strategy 2022-2025. Objectives: facilitate interconnectedness and interoperability among banks and non-banks; enable processing of payments within seconds (creating a cash-lite economy); and provide 24/7 availability.

Fast Payments: Eswatini currently has eight participants working toward integration. Three participants offered Fast Payment services as of launch day: Eswatini Bank, Swaziland Building Society and E-mali Limited. Participation is mandated for all payment service providers offering local payments; remaining participants were expected to offer Fast Payment Services by the end of January 2025.

Fast Payment transactions are limited to a maximum of E50,000 for a single transaction for Bank Accounts, and E10,000 per transaction for Digital Wallets. The daily limit for senders is a maximum of E50,000. Emaswati can transact using existing digital channels (cellphone banking, app, online banking).

Consumer education: an extended campaign was scheduled to commence in the first week of February 2025, reaching consumers in urban, peri-urban and rural communities.

Conclusion: The Governor applauded industry stakeholders and acknowledged implementation Service Provider PayLogic and technical assistance from the World Bank, and introduced the new EPS (Eswatini Payment Switch) logo.
