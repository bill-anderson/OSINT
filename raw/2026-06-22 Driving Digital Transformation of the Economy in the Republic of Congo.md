# Driving Digital Transformation of the Economy in the Republic of Congo

> ## Excerpt
> A GSMA country study: Republic of Congo's digital-transformation ambitions (National Development Plan
> 2022–2026, **Congo Digital Strategy 2030**, World Bank PATN) will stall unless the government reforms
> the mobile sector — heavy taxes, high infrastructure/energy costs, an outdated 2009 legal framework.

> **Provenance note.** GSMA is an operator industry body — reform/tax recommendations are advocacy;
> the FCFA 870bn / 8%-GDP upside is a **GSMA-modelled 2030 projection**, not observed. `published` is a
> **proxy** (no printed date; treats the 2026 Finance Law as enacted → written ~Q1 2026). Country =
> **COG** (Congo-Brazzaville), not COD.

## Key facts

- Congo scores below African and CEMAC averages on GSMA's indices (DNSI 26, DPRI 33/100, MCI 41; ITU
  EGDI 162nd/193). Two operators (Airtel, MTN) at ~100% SIM penetration but only **18.8% unique
  mobile-internet use** (70% usage gap, mostly rural — rural 4G just 21%). 5G launched Nov 2024.
- Mobile-money account access rose 2%→37% (2014–2021); transaction value FCFA 222bn (2024). The **2026
  Finance Law** raised the RGL sector levy to 5% and added a FCFA 10,000 SIM-device tax (but cut fibre
  fees up to 80%). Recommendations: cut taxes; advance a **Cybersecurity & National Data Governance
  Strategy** (continue Malabo participation); ARPCE–BEAC/COBAC mobile-money co-regulation + MFS
  interoperability; and **develop a national AI strategy**. Modelled upside by 2030: +FCFA 870bn GDP.
