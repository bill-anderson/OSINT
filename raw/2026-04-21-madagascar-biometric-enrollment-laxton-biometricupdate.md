# Madagascar navigates biometric enrollment challenges with Laxton's help

Madagascar has navigated an exceptional set of circumstances during the rollout of its national digital ID project, in part with contributions from citizen registration firm **Laxton**. In a case study, the company noted mid-project challenges including **regime change that led to civil unrest and a cyclone disaster**.

Through **PRODIGY**, Laxton was commissioned to design, supply, deploy and support the mass biometric enrolment solution underpinning Madagascar's national ID rollout — covering enrolment kits, software deployment, database integration, field logistics, commissioning, quality assurance, training and in-country operational support. Its kits capture iris, face and fingerprint modalities and function with or without internet connectivity. Laxton (which recently became a MOSIP full-service partner) supported deployment of **2,500 biometric enrollment kits**.

The **January 2026 pilot**, presided over by President Michael Randrianirina, lasted over four weeks: **nearly 130,000 persons were enrolled through 116 registration centres in 26 communes**, with average enrollment time under 10 minutes. Other pilot results: **100% data completeness, 99% kit availability**, and successful database integration. French company **IN Groupe** is also involved (database management system). PRODIGY's closing date is June 30, 2026.
