US tech giant Starlink has accepted Uganda's request to restrict its devices in the country. Ben Macwilliam, director of Starlink Market Access at Space Exploration Technologies Corp (SpaceX), in a January 2 letter to Uganda Communications Commission (UCC) executive director Nyombi Thembo, admitted that people have been illegally using its internet services in Uganda.

Per the pull-quote from the letter: "Any Starlink usage in Uganda was without our approval or consent and in violation of our terms of service. Starlink Uganda has never imported or distributed terminals in Uganda. We reiterate that there are no Starlink terminals operating in Uganda as of January 1, 2026." (Context: this preceded the January 2026 election-period internet shutdown and Uganda's tightened military oversight on Starlink equipment imports; Starlink was subsequently licensed in May 2026.)
