Senegal: Sonatel Connects Max it App to Dakar Bus Rapid Transit Network

Dakar Mobilité and Sonatel signed a partnership agreement on July 14 to integrate Sonatel’s Max it application into Dakar’s Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) network.

Passengers will progressively gain access to ticket purchases, subscription top-ups and additional digital mobility services through a single platform.

Digital payments on the BRT network have risen to 39% from 10% at launch, while user satisfaction has reached 99%, according to Dakar Mobilité.

Dakar Mobilité, the BRT concessionaire, and Sonatel signed a partnership agreement on Monday, July 14, to accelerate the digitalization of services across the network. The agreement calls for the gradual integration of Max it, which will allow passengers to purchase tickets, reload subscriptions and eventually access additional digital services linked to their journeys.

“This partnership fully aligns with our vision of sustainable and innovative transportation,” Dakar Mobilité Chief Executive Officer Cheikh Yatt Diouf said. He said the collaboration would improve the passenger experience while accelerating the adoption of digital services across the network.

Beyond ticketing, the partners plan to add transport card top-ups, financing solutions through Orange Bank, Wi‑Fi deployment in stations and data analytics tools to optimize passenger flow management. Sonatel will also receive advertising space across the network, including branding opportunities on buses and stations.

The agreement extends the digitalization strategy that Dakar Mobilité launched after the BRT began commercial operations in May 2024. During the first months of service, Dakar Mobilité partnered with Orange Finances Mobiles to enable ticket purchases through Orange Money before broadening the range of digital payment options.

The integration of Max it represents the next phase of that strategy because it consolidates multiple services within a single application.

Dakar Mobilité said digital payments have increased to 39% of transactions from 10% when the network launched. The company added that user satisfaction has reached 99%, indicating stronger adoption of cashless services.

Dakar’s BRT is the first fully electric Bus Rapid Transit network in sub‑Saharan Africa. The system was designed to carry as many as 300,000 passengers per day and remains one of Senegal’s flagship urban transport modernization projects.

For Sonatel, the partnership fits a broader strategy to expand the digital ecosystem around Max it. The application already supports payments, money transfers, service purchases and other everyday transactions. The company now aims to extend that model into the mobility sector as Senegal prepares to host the Dakar 2026 Summer Youth Olympics. The event is expected to increase demand for digital transport and payment services.
