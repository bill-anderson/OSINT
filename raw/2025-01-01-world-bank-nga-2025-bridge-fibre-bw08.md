# World Bank (IDA) — Nigeria BRIDGE nationwide broadband fibre project, 2025

The World Bank Board approved US$500 million in IDA concessional credit (8 October 2025) for Nigeria's US$2 billion BRIDGE project to deploy 90,000 km of fibre-optic cable, with the Federal Government holding a 49% minority stake in a privately-controlled project company.

## Description

"Nigeria has begun the financial advisory process for its ambitious plan to deploy 90,000 kilometres of fibre-optic cable under the $2 billion Building Resilient Digital Infrastructure for Growth (BRIDGE) Project, supported by the World Bank." "As part of the agreement with the Bank, which has already approved $500 million in financing, the government is required to appoint a financial advisory firm." "The BRIDGE Project, approved by the World Bank Group Board of Directors on October 8, 2025... Funding will be disbursed in five tranches, with the first $150 million going toward the company's initial equity capitalisation." The financing agreement with IDA was signed 30 December 2025; the Federal Government holds a 49% stake channelled through the Ministry of Finance Incorporated (MOFI). The programme deploys about 90,000 km of fibre "built around seven national rings, 37 metropolitan loops, 77 regional networks, and multiple edge data centers" (We Are Tech Africa), narrowing the broadband gap in underserved regions.
