Tanzania is launching an innovation hub to support the country's digital transformation efforts, as well as those of the entire region and continent. The facility has been developed in collaboration with the International Telecommunications Union (ITU), and received final approval at the institution's Digital Innovation Board meeting in Geneva. The initiative is part of the ITU's Innovation and Entrepreneurship Alliance for Digital Development Foundation, established in 2025.

An agreement for Tanzania to host the facility was signed in February 2024, months after the country was selected to host one of 17 such ITU Acceleration Centers across the world. The Tanzania Information and Communication Technologies Commission (ICTC) will manage the initiative, with the Tanzania Communications Regulatory Authority another frontline body involved; its DG Dr Jabiri Bakari attended the Geneva meeting.

At the foundation of Tanzania's efforts is its national digital identity system, Jamii Namba, part of its DPI. Daily News reports NIDA has improved the speed of application processing and issuance for NINs and ID cards, with NIDA MD James Kaji noting distribution of thousands of long-delayed ID cards in regions like Mwanza. Permanent Secretary Ally Senga Gugu of the Ministry of Home Affairs explicitly linked the national ID to digital economic growth and national security.
