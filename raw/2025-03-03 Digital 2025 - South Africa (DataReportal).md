*Cite, don't absorb. Worth a framing note rather than a contradiction: the held hub says 4G reaches 98% of the population, while this says 21.1% of people remained OFFLINE at the start of 2025. Coverage is not use — the two are compatible, but the 98% figure must not be allowed to imply near-universal access when a fifth of the population is offline.*

[Reference resource: DataReportal Digital 2025 country report for South Africa. Figures are "at the start of 2025" unless noted.]

**Headline numbers (early 2025)**

- A total of 124 million cellular mobile connections were active in South Africa in early 2025, equivalent to 193 percent of the total population. (Some connections may only include voice and SMS.)
- There were 50.8 million individuals using the internet in South Africa at the start of 2025, when online penetration stood at 78.9 percent.
- South Africa was home to 26.7 million social media user identities in January 2025, equating to 41.5 percent of the total population.

**Population**

The population of South Africa stood at 64.4 million in January 2025, up 759 thousand (+1.2 percent) between early 2024 and the start of 2025. In early 2025, 69.5 percent lived in urban centres and 30.5 percent in rural areas; 51.3 percent was female and 48.7 percent male. The median age was 28.7.

**Mobile connections**

Data from GSMA Intelligence shows 124 million cellular mobile connections at the beginning of 2025 — equivalent to 193 percent of the total population — up 5.2 million (+4.4 percent) between the start of 2024 and the beginning of 2025. GSMA Intelligence's data suggests that 97.5% of mobile connections in South Africa can now be considered "broadband" (3G, 4G, or 5G).

**Internet use**

The latest available data indicated 50.8 million internet users in South Africa in January 2025, an internet penetration rate of 78.9 percent. The number of internet users increased by 2.6 million (+5.4 percent) between January 2024 and January 2025. The figures suggest 13.6 million people (21.1 percent of the population) remained offline at the start of the year.

**Connection speeds (Ookla, early 2025)**

- Median mobile internet download speed (cellular): 51.43 Mbps (up 1.72 Mbps / +3.5 percent year-on-year).
- Median fixed internet download speed: 48.34 Mbps (up 2.72 Mbps / +6.0 percent year-on-year).
