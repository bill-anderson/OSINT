# CAMTEL highlights digital sovereignty and data protection at Government Action Fair in Yaoundé

*Date: 2026-06-12 — By: Robert Kwaku Annor*

The Government Action Fair (SAGO) is currently underway at the Yaoundé City Hall, bringing together public institutions, policymakers, and key stakeholders to showcase national development initiatives and public sector innovation.

As official sponsor of the event, CAMTEL is participating in this year's edition, which runs from June 6 to June 13, with a series of engagements focused on digital transformation and public infrastructure development.

A major highlight of CAMTEL's participation took place on June 9, when the company hosted a high-level conference led by its General Manager, Judith Yah Sunday Achidi. The session was attended by government officials, public administration representatives, parliamentarians, and development partners.

The discussion centered on the theme "Protection of Public Data and Digital Sovereignty: The Zamengoe Data Centre at the Service of Government Action and Cameroon's Emergence," underscoring the strategic role of data infrastructure in national development.

CAMTEL emphasized that the Zamengoe Data Centre, described as the largest in Central Africa and Tier III certified, serves as a critical national asset for ensuring the security, continuity, and local control of government data.

The company noted that strengthening data protection and digital sovereignty is essential to building public trust and supporting the modernization of state services.

SAGO 2026 continues through June 13, with CAMTEL inviting stakeholders and visitors to engage with its experts at its exhibition stand to learn more about ongoing efforts to develop and secure Cameroon's digital ecosystem.
