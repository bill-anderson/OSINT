# Ethiopia Signs Up to New Global Artificial Intelligence Alliance

Ethiopia has joined a new international artificial intelligence cooperation body aimed at strengthening global collaboration and governance on AI, after signing the establishment agreement of the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO) at the 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference in Shanghai, China.

Minister of Innovation and Technology Dr Belete Mola (PhD) signed the agreement on behalf of Ethiopia, making the country one of 29 nations participating in the establishment of the organisation.

The agreement defines WAICO as an independent intergovernmental international organisation established in line with the purposes of the United Nations Charter. The organisation is designed to promote international cooperation and global governance on artificial intelligence while supporting the development of AI in a direction that is beneficial, safe and fair for humanity.

Headquartered in Shanghai, China, WAICO will operate under the principles of extensive consultation, joint contribution and shared benefits, with a focus on ensuring the responsible and orderly development of artificial intelligence.

The move comes as Ethiopia expands efforts to develop domestic artificial intelligence capabilities through institutional, policy and infrastructure investments.

The country established the Ethiopian Artificial Intelligence Institute in 2020 to coordinate AI research, innovation and applications, with projects targeting areas including agriculture, healthcare and public services. The country also adopted its National Artificial Intelligence Policy in 2024, which outlines priorities including AI research, human capital development, digital infrastructure, innovation and responsible adoption of the technology.

The AI institute has expanded work in areas such as cloud computing, big data, data mining, robotics and immersive technologies. It has also supported innovation through an AI start-up centre, which has provided support to 200 innovators developing AI-related prototypes and seeking investment opportunities. In partnership with the United Nations Development Programme and Addis Ababa University, Ethiopia has also established an AI University Innovation Pod equipped with laboratories, robotics facilities and high-performance computing systems to support research and innovation.

Speaking at the organisation’s inauguration, Belete (PhD) said Ethiopia had begun recognising the role of artificial intelligence in national development and security through initiatives including the establishment of the Ethiopian Artificial Intelligence Institute and the implementation of a national AI policy.

"Ethiopia is ready to learn from others and share its experiences and work in cooperation," Dr Belete said during the launch event, adding that the new organisation could contribute to responsible and equitable global AI development.

The Shanghai conference brought together government leaders, technology experts, researchers and representatives of international organisations to discuss artificial intelligence development, governance and cooperation.

Chinese President Xi Jinping, opening the conference, said artificial intelligence development should contribute to global prosperity while ensuring the security of countries and the wider international community.

United Nations Secretary-General António Guterres said the use of artificial intelligence should prioritise common prosperity and security.

President Xi said WAICO would become a platform for international AI governance and cooperation. He also announced that China would provide 5,000 AI training opportunities over the next five years, support the joint development of open-source AI models, participate in AI infrastructure investments and establish an AI cooperation centre to facilitate knowledge sharing.

The establishment of WAICO comes as countries seek greater coordination on artificial intelligence standards, governance frameworks and technology access amid rapid global expansion of AI applications.
