Reporting on a Global Partnership for Sustainable Development Data / Centre of Excellence compendium of good practices for advancing SDG target 16.9 (legal identity for all), featuring Namibia. Namibia is presented as operating a fully integrated National Population Registration System (NPRS) that registers citizens, permanent residents and refugees, built by combining databases linked by a unique identification number (UIN), with Vision 2030 and the e-Government strategy providing the policy alignment.

Births and deaths are registered via e-birth and e-death notification platforms hosted at health facilities, police mortuaries and civil registration offices, linked to the NPRS through the National Data Exchange Service Bus interoperability layer, allowing the population register to link to functional registers run by government and the private sector. The case study notes that making birth/death registration a prerequisite for social grants drove demand, and cautions that legal frameworks and technology must be kept in sync.
