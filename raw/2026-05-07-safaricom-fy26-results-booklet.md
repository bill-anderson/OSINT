Peter Ndegwa, Safaricom PLC CEO commented

Overview Financial Year 26 was a landmark year for Safaricom. Beyond delivering strong commercial performance, the year carried deep symbolic and strategic significance, marking 25 years of how our network, platforms, products and services have transformed lives and continue to improve everyday life for millions of Kenyans. Through nationwide celebrations, grassroots engagements and community initiatives, we connected directly with Kenyans from all walks of life. These engagements directly shaped improvements in network experience, customer care, platform innovation and affordability, ensuring that the value we create is felt widely across our ecosystem. We remain deeply grateful to our customers, partners and communities who co created impact with us, not just by using our services, but by helping us shape solutions that reflect real needs. Their voices reaffirmed our purpose of Transforming Lives and strengthened our responsibility to serve Kenya as its most trusted technology and financial services partner.

Purpose Driven Foundation Initiatives & ESG

Through the Safaricom and M-PESA Foundations, we advanced digital inclusion, financial empowerment and youth opportunity, underpinned by a strong sustainability agenda. The launch of Citizens of the Future, our flagship education programme, expanded access to quality learning, digital tools and safe learning environments, creating long term opportunity for learners, teachers and communities nationwide. In health, maternal and childcare improved through Uzazi Salama, while free medical camps and telemedicine expanded access to quality care. Economic empowerment advanced through Wezesha Agri and Wezesha Mama, supporting youth and women with skills training, grants and digital tools. These initiatives reflect our inclusive approach to innovation, anchored in partnerships, purpose led brand storytelling and measurable national impact, while reinforcing Safaricom's role as a catalyst for sustainable social and economic progress.

Operating Environment

Safaricom continues to operate in a highly regulated environment, with evolving oversight across telecommunications, financial services, data protection, and digital platforms. FY26 benefitted from a stable and more predictable macroeconomic environment that enabled consistent execution and forward planning. In Kenya, inflation remained below 5% and GDP growth averaged around 5%. The Kenyan shilling held stable for most of the year, supporting business confidence and consumer spending.

For customers, this translated into continued access to affordable connectivity and financial services during a period of high living costs.

Safaricom Ethiopia Our purpose in Ethiopia remains to transform lives for a digital future and our vision is to establish an innovative brand loved by our customers. We are building a world-class network focused on earning customer loyalty and are experimenting/learning from the lessons and the challenges as the business evolves, to create a digital future for our customers guided by strong values in customer obsession, trust and respect, speed and innovation and compliance & integrity. Our brand has been established as a data network and our customers appreciate the superior customer experience we offer, reliable connectivity & speeds, as well as secure access to the fastest data network in Ethiopia.

In Ethiopia, the operating environment remained stable, with GDP expanding 9.2% (2024/25) and inflation easing into single digits, driven by declining food inflation. This marked the lowest inflation levels since 2018 and reinforced the National Bank of Ethiopia's achievement of its single digit inflation objective. ETB continued to depreciate against both the dollar and the Euro, with the second half of the year remaining stable compared to the first half. Industry-led regulations to build a sustainable industry on Reference Infrastructure Sharing Offer (RISO) and guidelines on cost of service for voice and mobile data, provided improved pricing clarity and predictability in operating conditions. The combination of macro stability, ongoing regulatory driven policy changes, and disciplined internal execution enabled the business to maintain momentum across our key segments.

Customer growth remained strong, underpinned by rising usage and increasing customer density, while network investment focused on optimising capacity and sweating existing assets in priority regions rather than aggressive footprint expansion. The business implemented price adjustments in December 2025 across key products, with further gradual adjustments expected as part of the phased implementation of the regulator mandated cost of service for voice and data. These pricing actions are supporting revenue repair while maintaining affordability in a market that continues to have among the lowest telecom prices in Africa, despite sustained inflationary pressures.

Key Business Updates We successfully returned to the domestic bond market for the first time in over a decade, securing capital at competitive rates. The strong investor appetite reflected confidence in Safaricom's fundamentals, balance sheet strength, and long-term strategy. Together with our Sustainability Linked Loan, this funding further aligns our capital structure with our sustainability commitments, linking financing terms to measurable progress in carbon reduction, digital inclusion, and social impact.

Across FY26, we maintained sharp focus on delivering scalable technology platforms that meet the evolving needs of our customers. In Kenya, continued expansion of 4G+ adoption supported broadband usage and digital content growth. In Ethiopia, we sustained rollout in priority towns and strengthened distribution to enhance availability and customer experience. The successful rollout of Fintech 2.0 enhanced stability, capacity and fraud controls, materially improving resilience while unlocking new customer and merchant experiences. This upgrade lays the foundation for platform monetisation and advanced capabilities, including AI driven reconciliation and ecosystem services. The launch of Ziidi Trader marked an important milestone, broadening access to capital markets and demonstrating early traction as a new driver of financial deepening and inclusion. We implemented data minimization to person-to-person transactions, with recipients only seeing two names and a masked number. This reflects our ongoing commitment to embedding privacy across our financial ecosystem.

Vision 2030

FY26 marked meaningful progress in advancing our Vision 2030- To become Africa's leading purpose-led technology company, reaffirming our commitment to transforming lives and becoming a technology company that delivers inclusive digital and financial solutions across our markets. This progress was underpinned by the continued strength of our Kenya business and the steady scale up of Safaricom Ethiopia, despite a more complex operating environment.

Our ambition remains centred on delivering personalised, value adding services to our customers, while partnering with the public sector and stakeholders to accelerate digitisation. Connectivity and mobile financial services continue to form the backbone of this transformation. Through disciplined execution and sustained investment, we are strengthening the foundations for long term growth, expanding digital inclusion, and unlocking new opportunities for individuals, businesses, and communities across the Group.

Dividends

During the year, an interim dividend of KShs 0.85 per ordinary share (2025: KShs 0.55) amounting to KShs 34.06Bn (2025: KShs 22.04Bn) was declared and paid. The directors have proposed a final dividend in respect of the year ended 31 March 2026 of KShs 1.15 per ordinary share (2025: KShs 0.65) amounting to a total of KShs 46.08Bn (2025: KShs 26.04Bn) to be approved at the Annual general meeting (AGM) to be held on 31 July 2026. This brings the total dividend for the year to KShs 80.13Bn (2025: KShs 48.08Bn) which represents KShs 2.00 per share in respect of the year ended 31 March 2026 (2025: KShs 1.20 per share). The final dividend will be payable on or about 4 September 2026 to the shareholders on the Register of Members as at the close of business on 4 August 2026.

FY27 Guidance

Safaricom delivered a resilient performance in FY26 against a backdrop of continued macroeconomic pressure and heightened geopolitical disruption affecting supply chains and energy costs. Despite these headwinds, the Group sustained strong commercial momentum across both Kenya and Ethiopia, reflecting disciplined execution and reinforcing confidence in our strategic direction. FY26 marked the first full year of execution under Vision 2030, our ambition to become Africa's leading purpose‑led technology company. This strategy is anchored in customer‑centric innovation, leveraging technology to deliver relevant, impactful solutions that deepen engagement, drive inclusion and create long‑term value.

In Kenya, we maintained robust performance across our core businesses, supported by strong customer activity, continued innovation and resilient execution. We focused on protecting affordability while competing on value and experience, enabling us to defend market leadership and deliver high‑quality earnings. Our Enterprise and Digital businesses continued to scale, reflecting rising demand for connectivity, cloud, fintech and integrated digital solutions. In Ethiopia, we progressed from market entry to early scale. The successful launch of M‑PESA, expanding network coverage and improving regulatory clarity represent important milestones as the market normalises. We remain focused on disciplined investment and commercial scaling as Ethiopia transitions toward becoming a sustainable second growth engine for the Group. Collectively, these outcomes underscore our commitment to protecting shareholder value, strengthening business resilience and positioning the Group for sustainable long‑term growth.

In FY27 we expect Group EBIT of KShs 180–187 billion and Group Capex of KShs 64–70 billion. Kenya EBIT is expected to be KShs 195–199 billion, with Ethiopia delivering an EBIT loss of KShs 12–15 billion as we continue to reduce startup losses. Kenya Capex is expected to be KShs 58–61 billion, with KShs 6–9 billion in Ethiopia. FY27 guidance numbers exclude IAS 29 impact. We remain confident in our ability to deliver on our strategic priorities, navigate near‑term volatility and continue building a resilient, future‑ready organisation well positioned to capture long‑term opportunities.

Looking Ahead

In FY27, we will accelerate growth by deepening customer value through more personalised, affordable, and integrated propositions, while scaling M PESA's next frontier across merchant solutions, credit, savings, insurance, wealth, and cross border payments. We will also advance AI driven customer experience, network optimisation, and productivity across the business, strengthening our digital capabilities. At the same time, we will grow Safaricom Ethiopia sustainably as mobile financial services unlock new revenue pools, while reinforcing our sustainability, climate, and digital inclusion commitments.

Business Review – Group We are pleased to report a strong performance for FY26, reflecting the Group's resilience and strategic focus amidst a dynamic operating environment. Group Service Revenue grew by 11.5% YoY to KShs 414.1Bn, driven by double digit growth in Kenya and accelerated growth in Ethiopia. Profitability improved in line with revenue growth, supported by disciplined cost management and operational efficiencies, enabling strong cash generation to support ongoing investment and shareholder returns. Our customer base also expanded significantly, with total Group three-month active customers rising by 25.4% to 71.56Mn, and one month active customers increasing by 14.2% to 50.66Mn.

In Kenya, the macroeconomic environment remained stable and supportive, with inflation contained below 5%, while GDP grew by 4.6% (2025), and the Kenyan shilling broadly stable at approximately 129/USD. This stability continued to support customer usage, affordability and overall investor confidence. Against this backdrop, Safaricom Kenya sustained its market leadership, maintaining a 66.8% GSM customer market share as at December 2025, according to the Communications Authority of Kenya.

In Ethiopia, inflation eased to single digit, 9.4% as at March 2026. However, the Birr depreciated by 23.7% against the US Dollar in FY26 to 154.96 as at 31 March 2026 while it weakened by 39.6% to 180.96 against the Euro in the same period. This FX impact has been reflected in our consolidated financial statements. Ethiopia is moving into a more stable, scaling business phase, clearer regulation (cost of service pricing, Reference Interconnection Offer (RIO)/ Reference Infrastructure Sharing Offer (RISO) approvals), improved security, rising customers, and early signs that revenue per user is getting healthier relative to costs.

Business Review – Kenya

1. Financial Services/M-PESA M-PESA delivered another strong performance, with revenue growing by 13.4% YoY to KShs 182.7Bn, driven by growth in both customer numbers and usage. The number of one-month active customers grew by 14.5% YoY to 40.99Mn. Transactional momentum remained robust, reflected by value growth of 8.9% to KShs 41.68Trn and volume growth of 25.1% to 46.41Bn, while chargeable transactions recorded even stronger expansion, with values up 17.0% and volumes increasing by 27.6%, reinforcing the resilience and depth of the M-PESA ecosystem. Chargeable transactions per active one-month customer rose by 11.5% YoY to 42.27.

The merchant base expanded by 71.0% to 3.1Mn, driven mainly by Pochi, which grew by 81.5% to 2.1Mn, while Lipa Na M-PESA merchants increased by 54.2% to 1.0Mn. Wealth management also gained traction, with AUM more than doubling to KShs 21.0Bn, driven by Ziidi's strong uptake with AUM growing >100% to KShs 18.7Bn. During the year, we launched Ziidi Trader, enabling customers to seamlessly trade listed securities directly from their mobile phones. The platform expands M‑PESA's Wealth Management proposition beyond savings into active investing, supporting broader retail participation in capital markets. M-PESA now contributes 45.6% of total service revenue in Kenya from 44.2% in FY25.

2. Connectivity Business The Connectivity Business delivered steady growth, with revenue increasing 6.9% YoY to KShs 197.9Bn, accounting for 49.4% of service revenue in Kenya, compared to 50.8% in the prior year. Mobile Data became the largest contributor to connectivity revenue, representing 42.1%, overtaking Voice at 41.3%, underscoring the continued shift in customer usage patterns. Messaging revenue declined by 11.8% YoY, driven by structural changes in customer behaviour as usage continues to migrate toward IP based and over the top messaging platforms, in line with global industry trends. Mobile incoming revenue grew by 15.5% YoY to KShs 9.1Bn, while content revenue increased marginally by 0.5% YoY to KShs 2.4Bn.

Mobile Data Mobile Data recorded strong performance, with revenue increasing 14.4% YoY to KShs 83.35Bn, supported by continued customer growth, increased usage and device penetration. One month active mobile data customers grew 11.2% YoY to 34.13Mn, while customers consuming more than 1GB per month increased 22.4% to 14.52Mn, signalling stronger engagement. Average data consumption per chargeable subscriber rose 16.6% YoY to 4.92GBs, while ARPU increased 2.6% YoY to KShs 274.00. We continue to enhance affordability through our targeted CVM initiatives. Rate per MB declining 12.1% YoY to 5.44 cents.

Growth was further underpinned by enhanced device penetration. The number of smartphones in our network grew 21.2% to 33.16Mn, expanding the addressable market for digital services. The migration toward higher capacity devices continued, with active 4G+ devices growing 31.8% to 30.78Mn, driven by 4G growth of 30.6% to 29.15Mn and 5G growth of 55.5% to 1.64Mn, supporting sustained growth in data consumption. Mobile Data now contributes 20.8% of total service revenue in Kenya, reinforcing its role as a core revenue growth pillar within the Connectivity portfolio.

Voice and Messaging Voice revenue grew 1.3% YoY to KShs 81.82Bn, supported by higher usage and a growth in customer base. Minutes of use (MOU) per subscriber increased 5.4% YoY to 211.61 minutes, while one month active voice customers grew 4.7% YoY to 31.55Mn, reflecting sustained engagement. This growth was partly offset by yield pressure, with the outgoing rate per minute declining 8.5% YoY to KShs 1.02, driven by targeted commercial offers focused on enhancing affordability and stimulating usage.

Messaging revenue declined 11.8% YoY to KShs 11.0Bn, as messages per subscriber fell 24.0% YoY to 121.45. The decline was partially mitigated by growth in the customer base, with one month active messaging customers increasing 24.7% YoY to 26.24Mn.

3. Fixed Service & IoT

Fixed Services and IoT revenue grew 12.2% to KShs 20.20Bn, driven by increased connections and a strengthened value proposition. Growth was supported by a diversified portfolio across FTTH (Fiber to the Home), FTTB (Fiber to the Business), wholesale, and IoT solutions. Fixed service and wholesale transit revenue rose by 11.7% YoY to KShs 19.07Bn, supported by strong growth in Consumer revenue, which increased by 19.5% YoY to KShs 9.03Bn, and a 5.5% YoY uplift in Enterprise fixed revenue to KShs 10.04Bn. IoT revenue rose by 20.8% to KShs 1.13Bn.

Fixed connectivity continued to scale strongly, with FTTH customers increasing 35.0% YoY to 407.08k, driven by expanded fibre coverage and growing demand for high speed internet. Network expansion remained robust, with homes connected growing 45.9% YoY to 739.38k (532.97k six months active) and homes passed increasing 16.2% YoY to 807.02k. Enterprise fixed customers grew 19.7% YoY to 83.61k, reflecting sustained uptake among SMEs and corporates seeking reliable connectivity solutions. Overall connection growth was significantly supported by 5G Fixed Wireless Access (FWA), which contributed 52.3% of total connection growth, with FWA customers increasing 53.0% YoY to 132.06k, while fibre customers grew 25.8% YoY to 358.64k.

Profitability Earnings Before Interest Tax, Depreciation and Amortisation (EBITDA) rose by 13.7% YoY to KShs 233.89Bn while Earnings Before Interest Tax (EBIT) grew by 15.3% YoY to KShs 182.30Bn during the period. Net income increased by 24.7% YoY to KShs 119.10Bn, underpinned by strong contributions from M‑PESA and Mobile Data. Performance was supported by continued expansion in the customer base, higher usage levels and effective monetisation.

Business Review – Ethiopia

Service Revenue Service revenue recorded a strong growth of 58.3% YoY to KShs 14.08Bn, driven by customer growth across all revenue lines, as well as increased adoption and deeper market penetration. The revenue mix continued to evolve, with voice contribution rising to 21.4% of total service revenue, up from 15.1% in FY25, reflecting accelerating traction in voice services. Mobile Data remained the dominant revenue stream, accounting for 67.9% of total service revenue, compared to 75.4% in FY25, underscoring sustained demand while indicating a progressively more balanced revenue structure as the business scales.

Mobile incoming revenues contributed 5.1%, with the remaining 5.6% derived from Messaging, M-PESA and Fixed Services, supporting further diversification of revenue sources. Overall, Ethiopia contributed 12.5% to total Group service revenue growth, highlighting its growing strategic importance within the Group's medium term growth portfolio. Customer momentum remained strong, with 90 day active customers surging 54.2% YoY to 13.63Mn, while one month active customers increased 48.3% YoY to 10.75Mn, reflecting accelerating adoption and deepening engagement across the market.

Voice and Messaging Voice revenue surged by 123.9% YoY to KShs 3.01Bn, driven by sustained growth in the customer base and rising usage levels. One month active voice customers increased by 39.6% YoY to 8.50Mn, while 90 day active voice customers grew by 42.3% YoY to 11.13Mn, reflecting broad based adoption and deepening engagement. Usage trends remained positive, with outgoing minutes of use per subscriber increasing 13.6% YoY to 144.64, supported by improved monetisation, as ARPU increased 21.2% YoY to KShs 34.46. Messaging revenue rose strongly by 82.3% YoY to KShs 169.38Mn, driven by growth in customers and higher usage intensity. One month active SMS customers grew 22.1% YoY to 3.64Mn, while 90 day active SMS customers increased 27.7% YoY to 6.17Mn, signalling expanding reach across the customer base. Usage also strengthened, with SMS usage per subscriber rising 38.4% YoY to 24.73.

Mobile Data Mobile Data revenue grew by 42.5% YoY to KShs 9.56Bn, driven by rapid customer acquisition and sustained increase in usage. One month active mobile data customer base expanded by 48.5% YoY to 7.84Mn, while 90 day active data customers increased by 46.3% YoY to 10.40Mn, reflecting accelerating adoption and deepening customer engagement. Usage remained resilient, with average data consumption per subscriber rising 6.8% YoY to 6.90GB, supported by improved affordability and expanding network availability. Overall, Mobile Data accounted for 67.9% of total service revenue in Ethiopia, reaffirming its position as the primary revenue driver while supporting the broader scaling of the business. Performance was supported by targeted Customer Value Management (CVM) initiatives, which enabled more personalised bundles and sustained customer engagement. These initiatives, alongside expanding network coverage and improved service quality, supported broader access, higher usage intensity and a more sustainable, diversified revenue base as the business continues to mature.

Fixed service and Wholesale transit Fixed service and wholesale transit revenue rose by 185.6% YoY to KShs 200.90Mn, supported by increased connections. Year-on-Year growth rates for Safaricom Ethiopia are calculated excluding IAS 29 Impact. Detailed financials for the Group including separate disclosures of our Kenya and Ethiopia business operations are included in this booklet for your review.
