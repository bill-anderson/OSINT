# Can Mauritians Still Trust the Privacy of Their Communications? (Q&A with LEX)

*Mauritius Times, 13 July 2026.*

Legal Q&A (columnist "LEX") prompted by the revocation of **Nishta Jooty-Needroo as CEO of the Sugar Investment Trust (SIT)** after a controversial audio recording circulated on social media (alleged political discussion of an electoral database in Constituency No. 5). The piece links to the wider **"Moustass Leaks"** saga and examines whether Mauritius's legal/regulatory framework is equipped for the digital era.

Key legal points:
- Admissibility of **electronic evidence** (audio recordings) under Mauritian law (a hybrid of civil law and English common law) requires **relevance, authenticity, integrity** (no tampering) and **legality** (not obtained in breach of privacy/fundamental rights).
- Under the **Data Protection Act 2017**, unauthorised recording and public dissemination of private conversations — including via social media — may constitute **unlawful processing of personal data** and a breach of privacy, with penalties.
- Raises the emergence of **AI-generated deepfakes** as a challenge to evidentiary standards and privacy.

Reflects ongoing public-trust and data-protection concerns tied to leaked communications in Mauritius.
