# Senegal Maps 2026 Digital Drive With World Bank Backing

We Are Tech Africa - 17 February 2026, by Samira Njoya

Senegal aligned the Digital Economy Acceleration Project (PAENS) with its national digital transformation agenda for 2026. During the World Bank's portfolio review, the Minister of Communication, Telecommunications and Digital Affairs met on 12 February in Dakar with Ousmane Diagana, World Bank Vice President for Western and Central Africa. The updated PAENS framework integrates the deployment of an integrated public financial management system (SIGIF), strengthening of cybersecurity capabilities, establishment of a sovereign government cloud, and development of AI use cases.

Operational milestones for H1 2026 include a tender to connect underserved "white zones" (groundnut basin, Lower Casamance), construction of the SIGIF accounting module, and rollout of an electronic patient record system. The Ministry of Digital Affairs secured CFA81.06 billion (about $146 million) for 2026, up 38.7% on 2025, with nearly 60% allocated to investment. The World Bank committed CFA95.05 billion to strengthen the legal/regulatory framework, expand broadband connectivity, promote digital inclusion and support digital adoption in public services, particularly digital health.
