Budget Vote Speech delivered by Hon Njabulo B Nzuza (MP), Deputy Minister of Home Affairs, on the occasion of the Home Affairs Budget Vote Debate, NCOP Plenary.

The Budget tabled is premised on priorities of the 7th Administration. It enables delivery of the vision of a digital first Home Affairs, symbolised as "Home Affairs @ home" — a digitally transformed government department driving inclusive economic growth and job creation, enhancing national security and delivering efficient and dignified services, leveraging the opportunities that Artificial Intelligence offers.

**Digital identity system.** To this end, the Department is working with the South African Reserve Bank to develop a robust digital identity system. This system will use facial recognition as the primary biometric modality thereby enhancing national security and reducing fraud risks.

**Smart ID Cards.** In the 2024/25 financial year, the Department issued a record 3,597,856 Smart ID Cards against a target of 2.5 million. The Live Capture system has enabled citizens to apply for Smart ID Cards and machine-readable Passports in 212 modernised offices. At the end of 2024/25, the total number of persons issued with Smart ID Cards was over 25 million (25,102,201) since the inception of the modernisation programme. In 2025/26, Live Capture will be rolled out to an additional 10 front offices.

**Birth registration.** Early Registration of Birth ensures a clean and reliable National Population Register (NPR). In addition to birth registration at 323 front offices, this service is also rendered at 391 health facilities. To promote early registration, in 2025/26 the department is rolling out Online Birth Registration at 25 health facilities with maternity wards, and automating the birth registration process at 47 health facilities. Last year, the department registered 868,476 births and 143,301 Late Registrations of Birth (LRBs). The budget will allow rollout of the Online Birth Registration System in all 1,445 public health facilities with maternity wards. For 2025/26, the target for early registration is 667,000 births registered within 30 days.

**Bank partnership.** In 2024/25, a total of 95,191 Smart ID Card requests were processed through 30 bank branches; up from 73,364 in 2023/24. The Branch Appointment Booking System (BABS) allows people to book a visit to an office to apply or collect passports and Smart ID Cards.

**Mobile offices and malls.** During 2024/25, mobile offices were deployed to 10,149 service areas; of these, 7,870 visits were to schools. Mobile offices collected 247,231 Smart ID Card applications, compared to 229,416 in 2023/24. Home Affairs offices now operate at Menlyn Shopping Centre (Pretoria, opened 2023), Cresta Shopping Mall (Randburg), The Pavilion (Durban) and Tyger Valley (Cape Town).

**Self-service kiosks.** The Department has procured 75 AI-powered virtual interactive self-service machines to be rolled out to non-modernised offices, allowing citizens to apply for and collect Smart ID Cards and Passports, and obtain reprints of birth/death/marriage certificates.

**Digitisation of Civic Records.** This Presidential Project converts manual, paper-based files into digital records. Over 49 million civic records have been digitised since 2023; the target for 2025/26 is to digitise 25 million civic records. Once digitised, amendments can be effected almost instantly, compared to the eight weeks it currently takes.

The Department continues disaster-relief document replacement (free of charge) and supported the Electoral Reform Consultation Panel during the period under review.
