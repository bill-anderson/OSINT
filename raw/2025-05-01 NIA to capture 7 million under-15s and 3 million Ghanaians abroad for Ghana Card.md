The NIA is planning a continuous registration exercise — a final attempt to capture the remaining ~2.5 million Ghanaians aged 15+ still without a Ghana Card (from a first cohort of ~20 million over-14s), enabling access to banking, tax, land, health insurance, driver's licence, SIM and passport services. Registration data: 17.56 million Ghanaians aged 15+ (of ~30.8m population) registered as of 31 October 2023; 17.51m+ cards printed, 16.62m issued, 895,000 printed and awaiting collection; ~49,000 not yet printed; ~101,000 registrations blocked for duplicate attempts; 192,277 foreigners issued non-citizen ID cards.

Plans include registering ~3 million diaspora Ghanaians (fee $20 West Africa / $30 rest of Africa / $50 outside Africa), 7 million under-15s, and 10,000 refugees/asylum-seekers. Executive Secretary Prof. Kenneth Agyemang Attafuah said cards are free for Ghanaians ($120 for foreigners), and that a pilot with the Births & Deaths Registry and Ghana Health Service will include babies aged 0-6. The Ghana Card uses three biometrics (fingerprints, facial templates, iris) with physical, logical and technical security layers. (Supports SDG 16.9 legal-identity-for-all by 2030.)
