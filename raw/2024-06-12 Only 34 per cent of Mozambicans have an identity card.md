Only 34 percent of adult Mozambican citizens have an identity card, though 55 percent have a birth certificate, per the International Organization for Migration (IOM), data revealed 11 June 2024 at the opening of the Second Regional Conference on Legal Identity in Maputo. IOM head Laura Tomm-Bonde said extreme violence by islamist terrorists in Cabo Delgado over six years has worsened the situation for vulnerable populations.

France funded (€100,000) an IOM project issuing civil-registration documents to displaced people in Cabo Delgado. The article notes a policy critique: every five years Mozambique re-registers its entire electorate and issues voter cards (about 17 million issued in 2023-2024), which lose validity after each election; the author argues issuing every citizen an identity card at age 18 (usable at polling stations) would save time and money and solve the legal-identity problem.
