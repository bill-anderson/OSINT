# Guinea Launches Nine Digital Projects to Modernize Public Administration

Guinea is accelerating the modernization of its public administration. The Ministry of Public Administration Modernization and Civil Service presented nine strategic projects on Friday, May 15, in Conakry. The initiatives aim to strengthen the digitization of public services, improve administrative management and modernize state governance tools.

The projects notably focus on digitizing public services through the One-stop shop for public services (GUSP), modernizing human resources management systems and deploying digital equipment across government institutions. The program includes a detailed mapping of the public administration system, an inventory of IT systems currently used for personnel management and expanded digital-skills training for civil servants. Authorities also plan to rehabilitate and modernize the Gouv-Lab, restructure public administrative institutions, and strengthen digital tools including the FUGAS platform dedicated to public-sector HR management.

The initiative forms part of the Simandou 2040 program. Guinea already operates a national public services portal that provides online access to procedures including passport applications, visa requests, civil registry documents and biometric permits, plus platforms such as eTax for online tax filing. The National Agency for State Digitalization (ANDE), established in 2022, is overseeing several projects related to interconnection between government administrations and deployment of the Conakry metropolitan fiber-optic network.
