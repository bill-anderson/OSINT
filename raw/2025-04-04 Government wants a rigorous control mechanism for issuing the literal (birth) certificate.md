Official — Vice-Presidency Press Office (4 April 2025). Equatorial Guinea wants to establish a control mechanism in its civil registries, with special attention to the granting of the literal birth certificate, presented by the Vice-President at a multisectoral meeting with the Prime Minister in Malabo on 4 April 2025.

The concern arose after reports that some expatriates fraudulently obtain the Personal Identity Document (DIP) and passport merely by irregularly holding the literal certificate. The 2011 Nationality Law sets the conditions for Equatoguinean nationality, but many foreign residents allegedly falsify the literal certificate to obtain these documents. The Vice-President instructed the Ministry of Justice to formulate concrete proposals to improve the civil-registry process. Separately, CNEDOGE Director General Teodomiro Monsuy Eyi reported results of the national tour to produce the DIP for all adult Equatoguineans: over almost a month, 7,670 dossiers were obtained, 7,275 of which have already been processed.
