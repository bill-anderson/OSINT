The implementation timeline of Madagascar's PRODIGY project has been extended to a new closing date of 30 June 2026, to make the project more adaptive after earlier activity timelines were missed. PRODIGY is supported by about $143 million from the World Bank.

The updated project document focuses on putting in place core identity infrastructure — a unified biometric national ID system — and emphasises speeding up the tender for the biometric ID system, building a data centre, and modernising civil-registry infrastructure. Planned procurements include civil-registration integration systems, data-security and privacy software/hardware, training/capacity building, and outreach/communications.

Between June 2025 and June 2026, procurements planned include the biometric ID system and the purchase of biometric enrolment kits and scanners. Some kits were already supplied by Laxton, and IN Groupe was selected to supply a database management system. The adjustment favours national and direct procurement methods for more local sourcing and faster execution. PRODIGY's three objectives: a consolidated and interoperable identity management system; digital and mobile government services; and project management/implementation support.
