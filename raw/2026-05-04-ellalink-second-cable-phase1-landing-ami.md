# EllaLink second cable — phase-1 coastal landing completed, Nouadhibou (ami.mr)

*Source: Agence Mauritanienne d'Information — Mauritanian state news agency (government voice; capture is not endorsement).*

Digital Transformation Minister Ahmed Salem Bede Etvagha supervised, on Monday in Nouadhibou, the completion of the first phase of Mauritania's second international submarine-cable connection project, with the Dakhlet Nouadhibou wali and the president of the Nouadhibou Free Zone Authority present. The advance follows the connection of the deep-ocean cable to the landing station via an advanced technical segment — one of the project's most sensitive technical phases.

The project aims to strengthen national digital sovereignty, double internet speed, improve digital-service quality, and extend coverage to interior regions (digitally de-isolating remote areas). It is financed through a partnership between the Mauritanian State, the European Union and the European Investment Bank, and implemented by "Ellalink" and "Alcatel Submarine Networks", with commissioning expected in the first quarter of 2027.

The advanced coastal landing section (PLSE) extends more than 28 km, forming the vital link between the main submarine cable (at a depth of up to 670 km from the coast) and the submarine landing station (CLS) under construction on land at Nouadhibou, reaching a depth of 15 metres before connecting to the junction point (Bu7) that links the Mauritanian branch to the main EllaLink trunk. Initial capacity is 200 Gbit/s. The minister framed the connection as moving Mauritania "from recipient to actor" in the global telecommunications system, and cited the directives of the President and the government action programme under PM Moctar Ould Djay. A head of cooperation at the EU delegation spoke on behalf of the EU.
