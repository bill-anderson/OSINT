Liberia, The Gambia, and Sierra Leone signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) aimed at reducing telecommunications costs and enhancing roaming services across the three countries. Implemented in phases, the agreement took effect on May 2, 2025, enabling Liberian travelers in Sierra Leone to receive calls free of charge while making calls, sending SMSs, and using mobile data at local rates without needing a new SIM card. By July 1, 2025, similar benefits extended to travelers between Liberia and The Gambia.

The initiative, spearheaded by the Liberia Telecommunication Authority (LTA) in partnership with Sierra Leone's National Communications Authority (NatCA) and The Gambia's Public Utilities Regulatory Authority, aims to foster economic growth and seamless communication for the region's 16 million residents.

LTA Chairman Abdullah Kamara emphasized these agreements mark a significant step toward implementing ECOWAS regulations on roaming. The move follows similar agreements including Ghana's free roaming deal with Benin and Togo (2023) and a Senegal-Mauritania agreement.
