# Djibouti National Cybersecurity Strategy 2024–2030

Djibouti released its **National Cybersecurity Strategy 2024–2030** on **28 November 2024**, developed — per this source — by a body styled the **"National Cybersecurity Authority (ANCS)."** The framework has **five pillars**: strengthening institutions and governance; protecting critical infrastructure; intensifying efforts against cybercrime; cybersecurity training for citizens and experts; and national/international cooperation. The launch coincided with the inaugural **Hackathon Cybersecurity – Djibouti 2024** and aligns with **[[vision-djibouti-2035]]**.

Benchmark: per the **ITU Global Cybersecurity Index 2024** (published Sep 2024), Djibouti ranked **Tier 4 with 31.47/100**, underscoring the need for stronger capabilities.

> **Naming note (flagged):** this Nov-2024 strategy attributes authorship to an existing **"ANCS."** The Nov-2025 bill (see [[2025-11-12 Djibouti advances bill to create National Cybersecurity Authority]]) creates a **"National Cybersecurity Authority (ANC)"** *by law*. Whether ANCS was an administrative precursor later given statutory form as the ANC is unresolved — see [[national-cybersecurity-authority-djibouti]] and `_review.md` (REV-DJI-001).
