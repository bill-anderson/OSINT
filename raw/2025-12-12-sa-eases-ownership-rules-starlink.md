JOHANNESBURG, Dec 12 (Reuters) - South Africa will amend rules on local Black ownership requirements after a directive from the telecommunications minister, which could pave the way for satellite-internet companies, including Elon Musk's Starlink, to operate in the country.

Starlink's parent company SpaceX wrote to the telecoms regulator ICASA last year saying that local shareholding laws were a significant barrier and that it should rethink the 30% ownership requirement for licensees.
