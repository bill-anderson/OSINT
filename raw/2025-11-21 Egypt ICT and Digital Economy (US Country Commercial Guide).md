# Egypt — ICT and Digital Economy (U.S. Country Commercial Guide)

U.S. ITA reference chapter (published **21 November 2025**). The ICT sector grew **15.2% in FY2022/23**, contributing **5.1% of GDP** (up from 4.4% in FY2019/20) with $4.2bn investment. Under **ICT 2030**, the government pursues investment, training, digital-government reform and infrastructure.

- **Connectivity:** average internet speed **75.3 Mbps** (Apr 2024, up from 6.5 Mbps in Jan 2019). **Nationwide 5G launched June 2025**, led by **[[ntra-egypt|NTRA]]** with all four operators — **Vodafone Egypt, Orange Egypt, e& Egypt, [[telecom-egypt|Telecom Egypt]]** — after a combined $2.7bn investment; nationwide by 2026. Internet penetration **81.9%** (96.3m users, early 2025). **"Decent Life"** connects 1.5m rural homes with fibre and upgrades 1,200 post offices.
- **Institutions:** **[[mcit-egypt|MCIT]]** oversees NTRA, ITIDA, Egypt Post, ITI, NTI; six technology parks under development (Minya, Menoufiya, Mansoura, Sohag, Qena, Aswan).
