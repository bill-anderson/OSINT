World Bank feature on Mozambique's free-ID campaign. More than one in every two people in Mozambique lack official proof of identity (nearly 13 million+), blocking access to education, professional qualifications, land, bank accounts, formal employment and business registration.

The World Bank's Digital Governance and Economy Project (EDGE) and its Identification for Development (ID4D) Initiative supported the National Directorate of Civil Identification (DNIC) — with the Ministry of Justice's National Directorate of Registries and Notary (DNRN) — to deploy a free-ID pilot in three provinces (starting Homoíne district, September 2023). Overwhelming demand led DNIC to spearhead, on 13 November 2023, a nationwide free-ID campaign for all new ID registration — the first in the country's history. At least 140,000 IDs were delivered free within a few months, and DNIC is scaling up nationwide. [Cluster with Biometric Update 202407 and aimnews.]
