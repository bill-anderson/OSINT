# ICT ministry launches strategic plan, policy to expand digital access and strengthen cybersecurity

The Ministry of ICT launched its strategic plan for 2025 to 2030 and the national ICT policy at Opuwo in the Kunene region. ICT Minister Emma Theofelus said the documents would expand digital infrastructure, improve broadband access, strengthen cybersecurity and reduce the digital divide, with focus on underserved regions such as Kunene, guiding the ICT sector over the next five years.

Per the policy, government will expand affordable broadband and mobile coverage prioritising rural/underserved areas; it identifies high data costs, limited infrastructure and unequal access as key challenges. It will also strengthen cybersecurity, data protection and consumer safeguards while expanding e-government services. The policy aligns with Vision 2030 and NDP6 and sets institutional roles for implementation and monitoring. The strategic plan prioritises digital-infrastructure expansion, innovation and digital-skills development, and improved public-service delivery — key targets include wider broadband coverage, increased digital literacy and stronger cybersecurity frameworks.
