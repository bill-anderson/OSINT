Libya advanced to the **third tier** of institutional maturity in the ITU's 5th-edition **Global Cybersecurity Index (GCI 2024)**, reflecting growing efforts to bolster its cybersecurity infrastructure. The report highlights progress in legal, technical and regulatory measures, capacity building and international cooperation, pushing Libya's cybersecurity maturity to **68.09%** — a notable increase from the 2020 assessment.

The advancement is primarily credited to the Cybersecurity Project team at Libya's **Telecommunications Holding Company**, which has collaborated with local and international partners to protect national digital infrastructure and citizens' data, as Libya positions itself toward becoming "a digitally secure and smart nation."
