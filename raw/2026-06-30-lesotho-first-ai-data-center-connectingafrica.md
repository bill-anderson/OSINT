# Lesotho's first AI data center set to shape the country's digital economy

*Connecting Africa, 30 June 2026. By Daniel Itai.*

Lesotho plans to build its first AI data centre in the Mokhotlong district, powered by green energy. Earlier in June the government signed a **US$6.2 billion MoU with US-based Convalt Energy** to construct the AI data centre along with 1.2 GW of hydropower, 4.6 GW of solar generation capacity and up to 4 GWh of battery energy storage (BESS). Lesotho currently has only two data centres, focused on telecom and government services. Construction is expected to begin in 2029; once complete, the centre would be among the largest in the region, linking regional fibre infrastructure.

The MoU is an expression of intent rather than a construction commitment — feasibility studies and regulatory approvals must be completed first. Lesotho's online penetration stood at just **48% at end-2025**, with youth unemployment around 30% (the government declared a national state of disaster in July). Convalt CEO Hari Achuthan said the company is evolving toward integrated infrastructure for AI-driven, energy-intensive industries. The US Embassy said the project gives Lesotho "a central role in powering the AI future" with American exports expected to exceed $2 billion. AI specialist Thabang Pinyane said local data centres reduce latency by hosting data in-country, complementing Starlink and future fibre routes.
