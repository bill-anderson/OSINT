ID registrations in Kenya's Kakamega County jumped after the scrapping of application fees for first-timers, with positive effects in other counties via mobile registration campaigns. Interior CS Kipchumba Murkomen said many more people are enrolling for the **Maisha Namba** digital ID after authorities terminated the KES 300 (US$2.30) application fee; also scrapped was the KES 500 vetting/verification fee for birth certificates when applying for an ID or passport. President Ruto signed a gazette notice scrapping fees for first-time ID applicants in **March 2025**. At Kakamega, an ongoing live-capture technology pilot is enhancing efficiency; the county has 40 ID registration centres across 14 sub-counties. Since Maisha Namba's approval and launch in 2023, Kenya has driven adoption and implemented civil-registration reforms.
