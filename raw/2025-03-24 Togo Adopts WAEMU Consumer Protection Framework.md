*Headline says 'Adopts', but the body describes a 20-21 March 2025 stakeholder workshop on the June-2023 WAEMU directive, not an adoption instrument. Write as alignment/consultation, not as an enacted legal fact. The directive is general consumer protection, not data or digital consumer rights — not tagged gov.protect.*

(Togo First) - Togo is aligning with WAEMU's regional directive on consumer protection, adopted in June 2023. On March 20 and 21, 2025, stakeholders in Lome attended a meeting organized by the Ministry of Commerce and the WAEMU Commission to discuss the directive. The goal is to create a harmonized legal framework that strengthens consumer rights across the eight WAEMU member states.

Discussions focused on key issues such as consumer information, product quality, and dispute resolution, aiming to boost consumer confidence in Togo's participation in the common market.

Comlan Nomadoli Yakpey, Secretary General of the Ministry of Commerce, emphasized that the directive must be implemented practically to drive economic governance. Tankari Ibrahim, representing the WAEMU Commission, announced plans for capacity-building programs and a regional consultative committee on consumer rights. The directive is expected to balance relations between consumers and businesses amid growing intra-regional trade, particularly relevant for Togo.
