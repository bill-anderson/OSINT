On **7 January 2026**, a birth certificate collected at **Limbe I Council** became the **first civil certificate issued through a fully digitalised civil-registration system** in Cameroon's North-West and South-West regions — reportedly with no delays and free of charge.

Limbe I is one of **ten municipalities** under UNDP Cameroon's Denmark-funded "Contributing to Social Cohesion Efforts in the Far-North, North-West and South-West Regions" project (Maroua III, Koza, Mindif, Méri, Mora, Limbe I, Buea, Tiko, Bamenda III, Bamenda City Council). The concrete first-issuance milestone under the **[[bunec]]**-led digital-CRVS reform ([[cameroon-civil-registration-law-2024]]).
