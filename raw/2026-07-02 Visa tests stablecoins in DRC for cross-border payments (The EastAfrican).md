Reporting from **The EastAfrican** (2 July 2026) originates the "Visa–M-Pesa–Onafriq DRC stablecoin"
story. On record, **Godfrey Sullivan**, Visa's **Senior Vice-President and Head of Products &
Solutions, CEMEA**, described an existing DR Congo use case:

> "There is a use case that we have in DR Congo where we are settling M-Pesa mobile money top-ups with
> stablecoins through a partnership with Onafriq. We have launched a proposition called VisaPay and, as
> you top up your M-Pesa wallet, the transaction is settled in stablecoins in the background."

This is an executive **describing a live settlement use case**, not a formally, jointly-announced
three-party "pilot." The report carries an **editor's note** that "one of the planned partnerships with
Visa is still awaiting regulatory approval." Visa did not disclose which stablecoin or blockchain is
used, the peg (the "USD" framing elsewhere is inferred from Visa's broader USDC work), transaction
volumes, or customer numbers.

Confirmed context: **Visa Pay launched in the DRC in September 2025** via an [[onafriq]] API. See
[[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]].
