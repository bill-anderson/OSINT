Bayobab connects Uganda, Kenya with new fiber route

Bayobab, a digital connectivity solutions company, has completed a fiber optic network linking Mombasa in Kenya to Kampala in Uganda.

Matshepo Sehloho,Associate Editor,Connecting Africa

3 Min ReadBayobab representatives at the fiber route launch event. (Source: Bayobab)

Bayobab, a digital connectivity solutions company has completed a fiber optic network linking Mombasa, Kenya, to Kampala, Uganda, marking a significant advancement in East Africa's digital connectivity.

Bayobab said the 260 km fiber route runs along the Uganda Railway corridor from Kampala to Tororo, connecting directly to Kenya's National Long Distance (NLD) fiber network that extends from Mombasa to Malaba via the Kenya Railways meter gauge route.

"Built between December 2024 and February 2025, this fiber cable delivers reliable, high-capacity connectivity from Uganda's capital, connecting urban and rural communities along the route," Bayobab said.

The company said the route provides Uganda with a low-latency, high-capacity connection exceeding 1 Tbit/s into Kenya's broader digital infrastructure, including subsea cable landing stations in Mombasa.

"It offers the shortest and most resilient path for Uganda to access international Internet gateways, enhancing regional digital resilience and creating alternative traffic routes," the company continued.

Bayobab Kenya MD Sylvia Anampiu said that by interconnecting with Uganda via this high-capacity route, Bayobab is enhancing regional digital resilience, creating alternative routes for traffic, and opening new opportunities for businesses and communities along the corridor.

"This is not just fiber in the ground, it's a new pathway for digital transformation across East Africa," she reflected.

Bayobab Uganda MD Juliet Nsubuga said the route connects major data centers across Uganda and Kenya and supports hyperscalers and enterprises looking to expand within the East African region.

"It caters to the needs of international and national technology players, telecoms, and Internet service providers (ISPs) serving communities, demonstrating our commitment to connecting Africa," she explained.

The digital connectivity solutions company has been working towards connecting remote areas in recent years.

In August 2024, Eutelsat Group and Bayobab announced a multi-year agreement for low-Earth orbit (LEO) capacity on Eutelsat's OneWeb constellation with the aim to deliver fixed connectivity services across Africa.

According to Eutelsat, that collaboration sought to address the connectivity needs for enterprises and cellular backhaul, providing reliable solutions to enhance network performance and customer experience across the continent.

In June 2024, Bayobab announced plans to build a fiber optic cable network across South Sudan.

The fiber optic cable network was expected to help strengthen South Sudan's digital infrastructure.

In April 2024, Bayobab announced that it had completed the structural separation and transfer of fiber assets from its sister company, MTN Zambia.

It said the move was in line with its Ambition 2030 strategy and paved the way for the company's expansion plans.

This was further highlighted when it landed the 45,000 km 2Africa cable in Ghana and Nigeria in February 2024.

In November 2023, it signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Smart Africa Alliance and infrastructure investment platform Africa50 to support the fiber optic cable initiative Project East2West.

Bayobab has been steadily working towards connecting underserved communities in the most remote areas in Africa.

In May 2023, it partnered with Africa50 to develop a terrestrial fiber optic cable network connecting the eastern shores of Africa to those on the west coast.

Bayobab secured a contract to manage, operate and maintain the national fiber optic infrastructure of the Central African Republic in July 2023.
