Libreville, 24 November 2025 — Ludovic Megne Ndong, Minister of Housing, Habitat, Urbanism and Cadastre, received a delegation from IGN FI (Institut Géographique National France Internationale), marking a step in the digitization of Gabon's land sector, aimed at eliminating land disputes and optimizing territorial planning.

IGN FI submitted a strategic proposal: an Integrated Urban Land Management System (SIG) based on digital centralization of data to guarantee transparency. Expected benefits: securing transactions (reducing double-sale and fraud risk), fluid parcel registration (modernizing land-title issuance), and urbanism planning (a single platform to plan infrastructure and housing).

The Minister cited IGN FI's pan-African experience, referencing land reforms in Uganda and Côte d'Ivoire, adapted to local land-law specifics.
