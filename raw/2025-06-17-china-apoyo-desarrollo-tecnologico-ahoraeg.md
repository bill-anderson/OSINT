China–Equatorial Guinea technology cooperation. Following Vice-President Teodoro Nguema Obiang Mangue's visit to China, both sides reviewed implementation of signed agreements and ongoing negotiations with Chinese firms interested in strategic sectors. Minister of Transport Honorato Evita Oma said the country has set a national roadmap to become a regional technology hub, stressing cooperation with China in cybersecurity, artificial intelligence and critical-infrastructure management, and that qualified human-resource training is fundamental.

Training will run through the Instituto Nacional de Tecnologías de la Información y la Comunicación (INSTTIC) in coordination with Chinese universities/tech centres; the minister proposed training 200+ Equatoguinean students in cybersecurity, AI, telecoms and complex-systems maintenance. Also discussed: Equatorial Guinea's forthcoming co-chairing of the China–Africa Cooperation Forum (FOCAC) in 2027, and a Cybersecurity and AI Forum to be held in Djibloho in September (150+ tech companies/institutions). The piece frames GNQ as "the gateway to technology investment in Central Africa" via CEEAC (230m+ market), and cites the Malabo adoption of the AU Convention on Cybersecurity and Personal Data Protection. Ambassador Wang Wengang offered to share China's experience in digital economy, AI and communications technology.
