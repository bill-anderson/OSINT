About 35 million people have been registered by NIRA following completion of the first major milestone of the mass enrolment and renewal exercise 2025/2026. Addressing journalists on February 9, 2026, at Police Headquarters in Naguru, Kampala, registrar Claire Ollama said the parish-level rollout, which started on May 27, 2025, officially closed its first milestone on February 8, 2026, following a government-approved extension (originally planned to end November 8, 2025).

Data presented: 100% of legacy data migration covering 28,571,893 records completed. Renewals, changes of particulars and first ID issuance for those previously registered as infants had a target of 15.8 million, with 14,311,877 completed (90.5%). New registrations lagged: only 6,416,958 achieved against a target of 17.2 million (37.3%) — "an area for concerted effort." NIRA's printery produced 10,152,559 cards; 7,261,435 shipped to districts; but only 2,581,457 collected (35.5% of issued stock).

Despite the closure of parish-level operations, all NIRA services (registration, renewal, change of particulars) continue at district offices. Kampala and Wakiso have division-based collection arrangements. Ollama also announced mass National ID issuance for the diaspora, starting with the UAE (three-day exercise Feb 9-11, 2026, at Hyatt Regency Dubai Creek Heights).
