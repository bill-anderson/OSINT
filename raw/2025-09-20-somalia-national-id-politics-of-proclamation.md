# Somalia's National ID and the Politics of Proclamation

By Isha Qarsoon — WardheerNews, September 20, 2025 (analysis/opinion)

When President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud declared in September 2025 that it was a crime for citizens to receive services without a national identity card, he crossed the line between policy and law. His words reverberated across banks, telecoms and ministries — yet the law itself does not say what he claims.

**The law as written.** The National Identification and Registration Authority Act, Law No. 009 of 2023, created NIRA, empowered it to collect personal data, build a civil register and issue IDs, and criminalized fraud, forgery, impersonation and obstruction. It does not impose a general criminal duty on citizens to obtain an ID, nor make providing services without verifying an ID a crime. Citizens have a right to an ID, but no obligation to seek one; NIRA has a duty to issue, not a power to compel.

**The president's pronouncement.** Speaking on 18 September 2025, the president said: "It is a crime for someone without a national ID to receive services from a telecom company, a bank, or a ministry." With that statement a right became an obligation and a policy goal became a criminal offence — without legislative debate or statutory language.

**The regulatory landscape.** Sector-specific regulations already move in this direction: the Central Bank requires national IDs for banking transactions, the Ministry of Transport for driver's licenses and vehicle registration, and Immigration for passports. These impose administrative conditions (denial of service), not crimes. The author warns of the substitution of executive proclamation for statutory rule and the erosion of the rule of law.
