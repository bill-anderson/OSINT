Stakeholders warned that Nigeria loses an estimated $850 million annually by failing to fully leverage its indigenous digital identity infrastructure and country code domain (.ng), due to growing dependence on foreign digital infrastructure. The warning came at the Nigeria Internet Registration Association's (NiRA) third Tech Convergence conference in Abuja ("Strengthening Nigeria's Digital Independence"). NiRA President Adesola Akinsanya said digital independence should not be mistaken for isolation from the global internet; there was consensus that Nigeria must take ownership of its digital identity, infrastructure and online presence. NiRA launched a .ng Ambassador Programme.
