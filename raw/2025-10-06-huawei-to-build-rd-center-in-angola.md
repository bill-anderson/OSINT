Chinese multinational Huawei has announced plans to establish a research and development (R&D) center in Angola, aimed at advancing digital inclusion across the country.

The exact location of the center is still being evaluated, but Huawei said its establishment forms part of the company's broader strategy to deepen its footprint across Africa.

During a meeting with Angolan President João Lourenço, Huawei's director general for Portuguese-speaking countries, António Hou, said the research and development center will be built in Angola by 2027.

"The objective is to strengthen digital inclusion and accelerate the development of strategic sectors such as education, health and connectivity, by promoting equitable access to new technologies," Hou explained.

Hou also announced plans to collaborate with Angolan universities to train 7,000 specialists in cutting-edge fields such as artificial intelligence (AI), connectivity, and emerging technologies by 2027.

"We have already inaugurated our Huawei regional headquarters for all of Africa this year, and this research and development center will be the second major initiative that we will implement between now and next year," he added.

## African countries strengthen digital inclusion efforts

The announcement by Huawei comes at a time when African countries are working toward digital transformation targets and partnering with international companies to assist in those goals.

Just last week, the National Cybersecurity Authority of Rwanda (NCSA), in collaboration with Cisco and the Deutsche Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ) unveiled the CyberHub, a center dedicated to cybersecurity talent development and innovation in Rwanda.

In September 2025, the government of Burkina Faso signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) with Presight International Services, a subsidiary of the United Arab Emirates-based G42 Group, aimed at strengthening Burkina Faso's digital economy.

In June 2025, Global satellite operator Intelsat partnered with Academy Award-winning actor and humanitarian Forest Whitaker's nonprofit, the Whitaker Peace & Development Initiative (WPDI), to enhance access to education in conflict-affected regions across Africa.

In September 2024, tech giant Google and the Zambian government partnered to set up an AI Center of Excellence at the University of Zambia (UNZA).
