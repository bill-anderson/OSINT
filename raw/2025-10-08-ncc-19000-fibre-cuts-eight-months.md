Nigeria's telecom operators suffered over 19,000 fibre cuts between January and August 2025 (19,384 fibre cuts, 3,241 cases of equipment theft, over 19,000 denials of access to telecom sites), per the NCC. EVC Aminu Maida, at a broadband-investment roundtable in Abuja, called it a major threat to Nigeria's digital economy ambitions. Broadband penetration stood at 48.81% with over 140 million Nigerians online. Under the National Broadband Plan (2020-2025), Nigeria targets 70% broadband penetration and 90,000km of fibre backbone by end-2025. A World Bank study shows every 10% increase in broadband penetration can boost GDP by up to 1.38%.
