# Mauritius Bets on Digital Transformation to Improve Welfare System Efficiency

*We Are Tech Africa, 2 June 2026.*

Mauritius formally launched, on **27 May 2026**, the **"Bulk Scanning of Beneficiary Files"** project to digitize **more than 10.5 million social-benefit documents** and integrate them into the future **E-Social Security platform** — after thirteen years of waiting.

Context and figures:
- Social-protection spending reached **Rs 91.4 billion ($1.9 billion)** in FY2024-25 — **37.4% of total government expenditure** and **12.8% of GDP**.
- As of **June 2025, 279,559 people** received the Basic Retirement Pension; agencies process nearly **800,000 payments/month** for at least **350,000 beneficiaries**.
- Records span pensions, social assistance, disability benefits, National Pension Scheme (NPS) and National Solidarity Fund (NSF); full integration estimated at ~**18 months**. Minister of Social Integration, Social Security and National Solidarity **Ashok Subron** cited paper archives "from floor to ceiling."
- Ties to pension-system restructuring: the 2025-26 budget phases the Basic Retirement Pension eligibility age up from **60 to 65**.
