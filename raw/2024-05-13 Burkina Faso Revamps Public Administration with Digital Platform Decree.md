Burkina Faso's Council of Ministers approved a decree (May 2024) to standardise and streamline the deployment of government information systems and digital platforms — cutting processing times, easing citizen access, and fixing past failures (inadequate pre-launch testing, platform incompatibility, poor inter-department communication). Championed by Digital Transition Minister Aminata Zerbo/Sabané, it aligns with the **National Strategy for the Modernization of Public Administration (SNMAP) 2021–2025**, which targets a "model public administration by 2025" — the governance frame behind Burkina's proliferation of e-gov platforms.
