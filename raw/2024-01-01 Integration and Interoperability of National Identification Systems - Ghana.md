# Integration and Interoperability of National ID Systems — Ghana (HSRC Ghana, 2024)

Research study (survey of data subjects + interviews with data custodians) on Ghana's national data-governance situation and its impact on cross-border data flow. Note the sample is small and highly skewed (104 online responses, 93% tertiary-educated) — treat findings as indicative, not representative.

Key findings:
- **98%** of respondents hold the [[ghana-card]] (Ghanaians free; non-Ghanaians paid ~US$62, raised to **US$75** for rest-of-Africa applicants in 2024); most acquired cards in 2019.
- Despite the Ghana Card being the **base document** for other IDs (63% of respondents), Ghanaians still carry **multiple credentials**: 80% Voter ID, 66% National Health Insurance card, 63% passport, 53% driver's licence, 45% SSNIT/social-security — evidence of the persistent **interoperability gap** the study argues against.
- Frames fragmented functional/foundational ID systems as a barrier to integrated data governance and cross-border data flow (AU Data Policy Framework alignment).
