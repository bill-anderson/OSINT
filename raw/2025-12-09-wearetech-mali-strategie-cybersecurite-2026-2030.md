We Are Tech Africa (Samira Njoya). Mali's Council of Ministers on Wednesday 5 December 2025 formally took note of the National Cybersecurity Strategy 2026-2030, the country's first coordinated national cyber framework (previously each actor acted in isolation despite scattered legislative texts). It aligns with "Mali Kura nyetaasira ka ben san 2063 ma" and the 2024-2033 National Strategy for Emergence and Sustainable Development. Mali ranks Tier 4/5 on the ITU Global Cybersecurity Index 2024 ("basic" capabilities). Cited breaches: August 2022, Russian cybercriminals allegedly compromised data of 312,000 taxpayers at the Directorate General of Taxes; February 2023, Bank of Africa Mali hit by one of the largest cyberattacks on a Malian financial institution; plus rising identity-theft and online fraud. The strategy targets critical-infrastructure protection, common security standards, incident response and international cooperation. NOTE (Russia angle, ISSUE-014): the 2022 attack is attributed to "Russian cybercriminals"; geopol taxonomy has no Russia slug, flagged not force-tagged. (The ANSSI agency to implement this was created 10 June 2026.)
