# South Sudan Tax: 'Digital First' Strategy to Combat Revenue Leakages

*One Citizen Daily, 10 March 2026 (Kei Emmanuel Duku)*

South Sudan is overhauling its domestic resource mobilization through an aggressive digital transformation, aiming to plug revenue leakages and modernize a tax system long hampered by manual inefficiencies. The pivot is being spearheaded by the South Sudan Revenue Authority (SSRA). Chol Paul Kur, Commissioner of Domestic Taxes at the SSRA, said the transition is critical for meeting national revenue targets, allowing the authority to generate revenue from streams including work permits and e-visas.

The commissioner noted the path to a fully digital landscape is fraught with structural hurdles — a deficit in ICT infrastructure and a lack of specialized personnel. To address internet accessibility and connectivity, the SSRA is involving the National Communication Authority (NCA) to support the rollout of e-government services. The authority has already seen early success with e-tax and electronic work permit services.

Currently, the SSRA maintains head offices in every state; seven of these offices have already been successfully digitized. The shift comes at a critical juncture for an economy that has historically relied on oil for roughly 90% of its national budget. Central to the modernization is the e-tax system, designed to automate tax filings and payments, reducing physical points of contact that previously facilitated leakages. The effort is part of a broader Public Financial Management (PFM) reform agenda supported by international partners.
