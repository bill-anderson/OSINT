to fill the vacuum after Eritrea's independent press was shut down). It is admissible
as reporting/analysis — NOT an AI synthesis — but its stance is adversarial to the

(quoted below) is the primary artefact of value here; the surrounding commentary is
opposition analysis by Saleh "Gadi" Johar.

By Saleh "Gadi" Johar, 2 July 2026.

Reported primary event: two days prior (~30 June 2026) the Eritrean government issued
a legal notice ordering individuals and businesses to **deposit their cash in banks
by 31 July 2026, or face legal action.**

The notice, as broadcast on state media and reproduced by Awate:

> **Legal Notice** — It is obvious that every citizen and institution has only the
> right to use the national currency. But it is not only a right; it is also a duty.
> It is illegal for individuals and institutions to hold the national currency
> outside banks in excessive amounts or for an unlimited period. Accordingly, those
> who have bank accounts must deposit such funds by July 31, 2026. Those who do not
> have bank accounts must open one and deposit the money. Failure to do so will
> result in legal action. — **Bank of Eritrea**

Awate's analysis (opposition framing): the announcement offered no definition of an
"excessive amount" or enforcement mechanism; it revived memories of past currency /
demonetisation measures (Eritrea last changed its Nakfa notes in 2015) that the
outlet argues damaged an already fragile, largely **cash-based and under-banked**
economy; it notes that a herdsman in a remote village may have no bank access at all,
banks being confined to towns and cities.
