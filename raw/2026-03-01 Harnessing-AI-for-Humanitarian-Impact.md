# Harnessing AI for Humanitarian Impact

A synthesis of 11 case studies of AI in humanitarian operations (2024–2025). Key findings: AI delivers measurable operational impact but needs realistic expectations; data readiness trumps model sophistication; sustainability needs multi-year funding; localization needs both technical and community governance; and responsible-AI governance is fragmented — the "missing middle". Metrics cited include 80% faster building-ID for disaster mapping (fAIr/HOT), 83% flood-prediction accuracy triggering anticipatory cash (GiveDirectly), 1.9M users served (Signpost AI), and 40+ language localizations.
