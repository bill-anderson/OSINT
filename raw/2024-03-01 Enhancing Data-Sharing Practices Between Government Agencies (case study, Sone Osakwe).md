Case study (March 2024) on enhancing data-sharing and interoperability between government ministries, departments and agencies (MDAs) in African countries. It sets out the value of data interoperability for governance and the common barriers (weak data-sharing/governance structures, divergent legal frameworks and formats, data silos, infrastructure cost, bureaucratic culture), then centres on Mauritius as an African success story that used a supportive data-governance framework and centralised, secure data infrastructure to enable effective inter-agency information exchange.

Reference case study; cite rather than absorb its figures. Continental/global in framing with Mauritius as the worked example.
