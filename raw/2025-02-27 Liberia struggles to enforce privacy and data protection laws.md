Analysis of Liberia's weak data-protection regime. Liberia has **no dedicated data-protection law and no National Data Authority**, despite being a signatory to the **ECOWAS Data Protection Act** (which requires member states to establish one). Constitution Article 16 protects privacy, but Assistant Postal Affairs Minister Melvin Yealue confirmed there is no dedicated privacy/data law, no national DPA, and no designated data-protection officers.

The government is drafting a policy to implement the ECOWAS Act (Minister Worlea Saywah Dunah, working with the LTA). The piece situates this against the National ICT Policy 2019-2024 (Structure-Empower-Transform themes) and a rising internet-user base (1.80m / 33.6% penetration, Digital 2023), with rural access gaps (e.g. Tubmanburg, where a college accesses the internet once a week). Finance Minister Samuel Tweah blames limited funding for the enforcement gap; journalists endorsed an EU-supported self-regulation manual on data privacy (2022). A live data-sovereignty risk as Liberia mandates biometric ID without a data-protection authority.
