Benin's electronic-communications and postal regulator **[[arcep-benin]]** decided (reported February 2025) that **SIM cards unregistered after 30 March 2025 would be deactivated from 30 April 2025**, with operators sending daily SMS notifications of the update/deactivation calendar. Authorities framed mandatory SIM registration as a way to protect subscribers against fraud, scams and cybercrime by precisely identifying each user, to better regulate the sector, and to secure transactions amid growing mobile financial services.

The report stresses that SIM registration **requires a strict personal-data-protection framework** to avoid leaks or misuse — citing Mauritius, which cancelled its registration operations over such concerns — and warns that bad actors can register SIMs under false identities, blunting the anti-crime rationale.
