# The Gambia signs blockchain pact to boost digital economy

By Mthulisi Sibanda, Contributor. Johannesburg, 08 Jan 2025

The Kalp Foundation says its partnership with The Gambia marks the beginning of a series of initiatives to empower nations with sustainable and inclusive digital public infrastructure.

The Gambia has partnered with the Kalp Foundation to develop Gambia One, a blockchain-powered digital public infrastructure (DPI) platform, while also promoting capacity-building efforts for government leaders and technology skilling programs for youth.

The Kalp Foundation is a non-profit organisation dedicated to creating digital public infrastructure that promotes equitable growth through blockchain technology.

This collaboration with The Gambia, according to a Kalp Foundation statement, displays a common commitment to promote sustainable development via innovation, compliance, and inclusion.

The Foundation said: "Together, the government of The Gambia aim to empower underserved communities, bridge the digital divide, and create scalable solutions aligned with global regulatory and ethical standards."

Tapan Sangal, founder and director, Kalp Foundation, commented: "We are deeply honoured to collaborate with The Gambia in its journey towards becoming a future-ready nation powered by a robust blockchain-driven digital ecosystem.

"Our partnership with The Gambia marks the beginning of a series of transformative engagements designed to empower nations with sustainable and inclusive digital public infrastructure."

Lamin Jabbi, minister of communications and digital economy, said: "The Gambia welcomes Kalp Foundation's vision and expertise in fostering an inclusive digital ecosystem.

"Together, we will harness the power of blockchain-enabled DPI to deliver innovative, citizen-centric solutions that align with global standards of trust, transparency, and accountability. This initiative is a testament to our commitment to building a brighter, more inclusive future for our nation."
