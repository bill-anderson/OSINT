Vodacom Group CEO Shameel Joosub has met with Mozambique President Daniel Chapo in Maputo to discuss expanding the country's digital infrastructure and accelerating investments that support inclusive economic growth.

The meeting focused on strengthening collaboration between Vodacom and the Mozambican government to improve connectivity, expand fibre infrastructure, advance artificial intelligence (AI) adoption, and drive digital transformation across the country. The discussions also highlighted the importance of building the foundations needed to foster innovation, increase competitiveness, and promote digital inclusion.

As a long-standing investor in Mozambique, Vodacom reaffirmed its commitment to partnering with government, businesses, and local communities to create new opportunities, empower citizens through digital access, and contribute to the country's sustainable economic development.

The engagement underscores Vodacom's continued investment in Mozambique's digital future, with both parties sharing a vision of leveraging technology and infrastructure to connect more people and unlock the country's long-term economic potential.
