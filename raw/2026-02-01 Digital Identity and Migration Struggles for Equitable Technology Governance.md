# Digital Identity and Migration: Struggles for Equitable Technology Governance

> ## Excerpt
> A Caribou report (funded by Robert Bosch Stiftung) synthesising the *Identity in the Age of Migration*
> project — fieldwork in **Kenya and Germany** — arguing digital-identity systems are political
> infrastructures that include and exclude migrants, refugees and asylum seekers.

> **Provenance note.** Synthesis of Caribou’s own project outputs (Weitzberg et al.; Kirsch et al.;
> Dullaert). Admissible as dated, attributable published analysis — but trace load-bearing figures to
> the cited primaries and avoid circular self-corroboration among Caribou pieces. Non-African cases
> (EU/UK/Germany) are covered in the report but only African places are tagged.

## Synthesis

The report advances through three lenses. **Digital-wallet case studies** — the EU Digital Identity
(EUDI) Wallet, the UK BritCard proposal, and the Red Cross **DIGID** initiative in Kenya — show one
paradigm serving divergent political projects (citizen empowerment, migration control, closed-loop
humanitarian aid), each enacting exclusions. **Humanitarian power dynamics** — donor-driven
"pilotitis," privatisation amid post-2025 US aid cuts, centralisation and localisation struggles.
**Migrant experience** in Kenya reveals concrete exclusions: the Social Health Authority not integrating
refugee IDs; SIM-registration cutting refugees off from **M-PESA**; and double-registration denying tens
of thousands of Kenyan citizens national IDs. It closes with nine recommendations (service integration,
risk-proportionate assurance, privacy-by-design / "No Phone Home", participatory governance) and a call
for systemic political reform over technological solutionism.

## Key facts (African-relevant; figures sourced to the report)

- Kenya’s **Social Health Authority (SHA)** requires online registration but does not integrate refugee
  IDs, causing exclusions; SIM-registration enforcement cuts many refugees off from M-PESA.
- **Tens of thousands of Kenyan citizens denied national IDs** due to double registration in refugee
  databases (Weitzberg 2025).
- Kenya **Refugees Act 2021** moves toward socioeconomic inclusion, undermined by implementation gaps.
- **Ethiopia’s [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]**: UNHCR and Ethiopia’s Refugees and Returnees Service set
  up a joint committee to decriminalise double registration — cited as a rare participatory model.
- Non-African context: EUDI Wallet (available to every EU citizen/resident by end-2026, 80% target by
  2030); UK **BritCard** proposal (£400m est. dev cost, not yet law); Germany BAMF phone-scraping of
  asylum seekers.
