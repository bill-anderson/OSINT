# Morocco champions inclusive digital future at GITEX Africa 2025 kick-off

Monday 14 April 2025 - 16:00

At the opening of GITEX Africa 2025 in Marrakech, key Moroccan and international officials underscored the importance of digital transformation and artificial intelligence (AI) in shaping the continent's future, while warning of widening digital divides and urgent cybersecurity threats.

Amal El Fallah Seghrouchni, Minister Delegate for Digital Transition and Administrative Reform, stressed that the digital economy now contributes 15% of global GDP—equivalent to $6.5 trillion—and Morocco is fully committed to shaping a future where digitalization and AI serve all segments of society. She highlighted that nearly 40% of startups participating this year are integrating AI, with the global market for Agentic AI projected to exceed $320 billion by 2030.

The Minister flagged serious gaps: Africa holds less than 1% of global data center capacity, produces just 0.5% of AI research output, and faces a shortfall of 10 million digital professionals. In addition, 95% of AI training datasets overlook African languages and contexts. She called for policies positioning Africa as a producer of technology, with initiatives such as "Morocco Digital for Sustainable Development" and the "Jazari Institutes."

Mohammed Drissi Melyani, Director General of Morocco's Digital Development Agency (ADD), called GITEX Africa a key platform for promoting digital integration across Africa, highlighting local innovation in AI, cybersecurity, fintech, and e-governance, and the role of digital tools in preparing Morocco to co-host the 2030 FIFA World Cup ("SportTech"). Mohamed Al Kuwaiti, Head of Cybersecurity for the UAE government, underlined the urgency of investing in AI to meet cyber challenges. This year's edition features over 1,500 exhibitors, 800 startups, 130 international delegations, and 350 investors (Marrakech, April 14–16).
