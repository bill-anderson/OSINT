# Scaling Instant Payments in Africa: Policy Choices for Central Banks

> ## Excerpt
> A four-institution white paper: building the rail is no longer the constraint — usage, trust,
> sustainability and fair competition are. Central banks play four roles: regulator, overseer, catalyst,
> operator.

> **Provenance note.** Consortium (AfricaNenda/BTCA/World Bank) are simultaneously authors, funders'
> partners and source of the core SIIPS dataset — treat figures as consortium-asserted. Data-protection
> coverage figures rest on the 2022 World Bank GovTech survey (dated).

## Key facts

- **25 African countries had a live domestic IPS in 2025** (~half of AU members; up from 20 in 2022);
  19 more in development. **17 of 33 domestic IPS are public-body-owned** — evidencing the "operator"
  trend, driven partly by a stated fear of "loss of digital sovereignty by relying on foreign operators."
- Drawn from PSP focus groups/interviews in **20 African countries**. Top barriers: (#1) weak PSP
  business case, (#2) policy/legal/regulatory (under central-bank control).
- Discrete facts: **Senegal introduced a 0.5% mobile-money transfer levy in 2025**; **SARB took a 50%
  stake in IPS operator PayInc/BankservAfrica in 2025** (PEM programme). Regional IPS live in WAEMU,
  GIMAC, North Africa/Middle East and SADC; the EAC is following.
