# Madagascar completes 2.6M digital ID enrollments in two months

Madagascar has **enrolled 2.6 million people into its digital identity system in just two months**, surpassing the government's target of two million (April–June). Government figures reported by Copmad show the effort exceeded expectations.

The digital ID system is a key component of **PRODIGY** ($143 million World Bank funding). **Laxton's biometric registration kits and fingerprint scanners** supported mass enrollment, while **IN Groupe** provided the database infrastructure.

DPI expert **Tariq Malik** (former NADRA chairman) noted that using nearly **2,500 multimodal biometric registration kits**, teams achieved around **10 minutes average enrollment time per person**, an "extraordinary operational accomplishment" given difficult terrain and connectivity gaps. He highlighted the country's **"retroactive" civil registration framework** enabling previously undocumented people to access foundational documents: "identity linked with civil registration, legal inclusion before digital transactions." Malik noted the final stages of a rollout — reaching remote, marginalized and undocumented populations — are the most challenging.
