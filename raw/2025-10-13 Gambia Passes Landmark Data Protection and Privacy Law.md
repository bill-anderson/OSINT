The Gambia's National Assembly has passed the **Personal Data Protection and Privacy Bill 2025**, marking a major step toward protecting citizens' personal information and reinforcing privacy rights. Approved during the Assembly's Third Ordinary Session, the new law establishes The Gambia's **first comprehensive legal framework** for personal data protection, setting standards for how data can be collected, processed and shared and aligning the nation with international best practices.

Information Minister Dr Ismaila Ceesay hailed the passage as a "major milestone" in The Gambia's digital transformation. The Ministry of Information said the legislation was the product of collaboration among government officials, the National Assembly's ICT and Education Committee, civil society and development partners. Next steps focus on implementation through capacity-building, public awareness, and establishing institutional mechanisms for compliance and enforcement.
