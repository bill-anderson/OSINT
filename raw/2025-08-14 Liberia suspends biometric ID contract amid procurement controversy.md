A biometric-identification contract in Liberia was suspended after the **Public Procurement and Concessions Commission (PPCC)** rejected the process used to award it. The **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** had sought to award the contract to Austrian firm **OSD International** through **restricted bidding** (reported values $9.8m-$44m); on **7 July 2025** the PPCC denied the NIR's request, citing Sections 96 and 97 of the PPCC Act of 2010 and requiring competitive bidding.

The decision came two days after a 5 July presidential memo established a 10-member Steering Committee (chaired by National Security Advisor Samuel Koffi Woods) to finalize contractor selection and proceed with OSD. Reports (GNN Liberia, The Liberian Investigator) allege senior aides failed to inform President Boakai of the rejection, and cite an unsubstantiated claim of a ~$120,000 illicit facilitation payment. The Central Bank of Liberia and NIR then **suspended issuance of the new biometric ID cards** citing "administrative concerns," effectively halting enforcement of Executive Order No. 147. Civil society and lawmakers are urging a formal investigation.
