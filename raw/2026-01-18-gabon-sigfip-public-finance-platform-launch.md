Gabon officially launched its **Integrated Public Financial Management System (SIGFIP)** on 15 January 2026. The unified platform is connected to the public treasury and consolidates all of the state's tax, customs, and non-tax payments and revenues.

Four main functions: centralising tax and customs revenue; executing public spending (operating expenditure, investment, debt); managing public procurement; and monitoring non-tax revenue (royalties, administrative fees). It strengthens the audit trail, improves budgetary and accounting management, and moves Gabon from fragmented systems to an integrated platform. Every collection and payment is recorded in a single database, enabling full traceability, real-time tracking of commitments and payments, and rapid production of budgetary/accounting reports.

For taxpayers and businesses, SIGFIP introduces a single payment interface, a Treasury-issued receipt, and immediate visibility of transactions. It will be rolled out nationally and in Gabon's representations abroad, connecting the state to banks and electronic payment operators, and integrates electronic signatures and digitised public procurement. The project follows a directive issued 3 October 2025 by President Brice Oligui calling for digitisation of financial agencies.
