The **[[leswitch|Lesotho National Payment Switch (LeSwitch)]]**, led by the Central Bank of Lesotho with the banking sector (including Standard Lesotho Bank) and payment service providers, aims to modernize the country's electronic-payments infrastructure by connecting POS terminals, ATMs and mobile money into a **single central hub** for authorization and processing.

Key goals: near real-time payments; 24/7 availability; **digital interoperability** (seamless processing between banks and non-bank entities); **physical interoperability** (access to ATMs/agents across providers); and support for e-commerce gateways and e-government payment integrations. Implementation is staged: the initial fast-payments phase was piloted in October 2022 and officially launched on **20 March 2024**; the next phase — card-stream switching for POS and ATMs — was in testing, with an ATM pilot set for 5 October 2024. The switch is exclusively for domestic payments; cross-border transactions continue through existing systems.
