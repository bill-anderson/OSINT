# Nigeria's CBN Data Localisation Directive (circular PSS/DIR/PUB/CIR/001/004)

*Vendor-blog account, held for its detailed rendering of the instrument the wiki has chased since
the Cencori flag — the circular itself is not held (absence dated on `nigeria--gov-protect`).*

On **June 15, 2026**, the Central Bank of Nigeria released **circular PSS/DIR/PUB/CIR/001/004**
(signed by **Rakiya Yusuf, Director of Payments System Supervision**), requiring all entities
facilitating payment transactions in Nigeria — DMBs, microfinance banks, MMOs, switches, PTSPs,
super agents and all licensed payment operators — to **store and manage payment transaction data
generated within Nigeria in Nigeria by January 1, 2027**, in line with Nigerian data-protection
law. Quoted text: "All Financial Institutions and participants facilitating payments within
Nigeria shall ensure that payment transaction data generated within Nigeria are stored and managed
in Nigeria in accordance with data protection laws and regulations applicable in Nigeria."

Also in the circular: **UBO disclosure** obligations (records available to CBN on request, aligned
with AML/CFT); **market concentration caps** — no institution may hold >25% of card issuing while
holding >15% of merchant acquiring (and vice versa), with monthly market-share returns, compliance
by **December 31, 2026**; and enhanced systemic oversight for critical PSPs. Open question the
account flags: whether international cloud providers' local availability zones qualify as
compliant infrastructure — unanswered by the circular.
