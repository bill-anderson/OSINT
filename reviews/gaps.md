## What belongs here now

A **gap** is a genuine research lead for Bill: *"consider sourcing this."* It is
not a page CC should mint, not a document sitting at a known URL, not a claim
awaiting reconcile, and not a fact that has not happened yet — those are the four
files above. What is left is the narrow case the register was designed for: an
absence where **the decision of whether to pursue it at all is Bill's**, because
pursuing it would extend the wiki's scope, commit it to a new standing thread, or
cost more than the answer is worth.

On that test, most of the 102 were never gaps. They were work.

**Two rules keep it that way.**

1. **Every item must name what would close it.** A lead with no closing condition
   is a watch item, and belongs in `watch.md`.
2. **Nothing sits open indefinitely.** An item unresolved after two reconcile
   attempts, or one Bill has passed over twice, closes to `watch.md` with a
   re-check trigger. It stops counting against the live queue. A question that
   has been unanswerable twice is a horizon, not a backlog.

CC appends here only when a lead genuinely turns on Bill's judgment. If a written
rule determines the action, that is a tier-1/2 action CC just does.
