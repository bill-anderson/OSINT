# Research: Gabon CNIE / NIP enrolment coverage

## Verdict

**Resolved.** A dated, ministerially-attributed figure now exists, given on the record to parliament.

## The figure (as of 2026-06-02)

Interior Minister **Adrien Nguema Mba**, answering oral questions in the National Assembly on
**2 June 2026** (first questions-orales session of the 14th legislature, Palais Léon-Mba), gave:

| Measure | Value |
|---|---|
| **Persons enrolled** since the 2024 launch | **335,384** |
| **Cards produced** | **164,944** |
| **Cards collected** by holders | **147,224** |
| **Cards produced but uncollected** | **17,720** |
| **Files in processing** | **92,614** |
| **Files classed as litigious** (needing further verification) | **78,500** |

These are **CNIE enrolments and credentials**, not NIP holders — see the counting caveat below.

## Prior data points (trajectory)

- **Baseline: 0**, December 2024 — World Bank results-framework indicator "% of population 16+ with a
  NIP" (already held by the wiki).
- **~2025-02-25** — then-Interior Minister **Hermann Immongault**, on Gabon 24's *Le Gouvernement
  s'exprime*: **>120,000 enrolled**, ~**50,000 cards produced**, ~**22,000 uncollected**.
- **2026-06-02** — 335,384 enrolled (above).

So enrolment roughly **tripled** between Feb 2025 and Jun 2026. Against a population of ~2.57m
(DataReportal, early 2025), 335,384 enrolments is on the order of **13% of total population** — and the
NIP indicator is defined on the **16+** population, so the coverage percentage against the indicator's
own denominator is higher but not directly derivable from these numbers.

## Counting caveat (the brief explicitly asked for this)

The three figures measure **different things** and must not be conflated:

- **335,384 = enrolments** (biometric capture events / applications lodged).
- **164,944 = credentials produced** (cards physically personalised).
- **147,224 = credentials delivered** (cards actually in citizens' hands).

None of these is stated as **unique NIPs issued**. Note that the NIP campaign (opened **3 April 2024**)
and the CNIE campaign are **sequential but distinct**: the NIP attestation is the prerequisite document
for a CNIE application, and citizens already biometrically enrolled on the electoral roll since 2013
receive a NIP attestation **without** re-enrolment. So the NIP-holder population is **larger** than the
CNIE enrolment count, and the 335,384 figure is a **floor**, not a measure of NIP coverage.

**A separate NIP-coverage figure remains unpublished.** The World Bank indicator's denominator (16+ with
a NIP) still has no dated value above baseline.

## Context worth carrying

- IBOGA voter registration had produced **~690,000 unique records = 52% of the over-18 adult
  population** (World Bank PAD, P175987). That legacy registry is the seed data for the RBPP.
- The 78,500 "litigious" files are ~23% of all enrolments — a material data-quality problem in the
  build, and a governance story in its own right.
