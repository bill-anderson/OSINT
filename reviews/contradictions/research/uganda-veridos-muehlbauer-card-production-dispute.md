# Research: the Veridos / Mühlbauer dispute over Uganda's ID card production

## Headline: the wiki had the roles INVERTED

The wiki (`veridos.md`, `uganda--dpi-id.md`) describes Veridos as NIRA's *intended* partner
competing against Mühlbauer as "the prior supplier to the national security information system,"
with the outcome unknown. The record is the other way round on the key point: **Mühlbauer was the
incumbent card producer (2010–2018); Veridos has held the card-production arrangement since 2018.**
The 2023 parliamentary fight was an attempt to *displace* Veridos and return to Mühlbauer — not a
contest to appoint a first vendor.

## The "overcharging scandal" allegation — NOT substantiated as stated

The wiki's phrasing — Mühlbauer "embroiled in a scandal concerning overcharging" — does not
match what the record supports. What the record shows is:
- the **2010 Mühlbauer award itself** was contested as procurement-rule-breaching and
  presidentially directed (Monitor 2017-07-07);
- NIRA's complaint against Mühlbauer was **vendor lock-in and obsolescence**, not overcharging,
  and Mühlbauer **publicly rebutted it** (Biometric Update 2022-11-24);
- the **cost** allegations in 2022–23 ran the *other* way — MPs argued the Veridos **smart ID was
  too expensive** versus upgrading Mühlbauer's kit.

Treat the wiki's "overcharging" wording as **unsupported** and correct it.

## Resolution of the dispute

Institutionally resolved **in Veridos's favour, via USPC** — Parliament's attempt to redirect the
work to Mühlbauer had no procurement force (as Katuntu and Lubega both argued), and by **June 2024
NIRA was paying USPC for blank cards**. There is **no record of any contract being awarded to
Mühlbauer**. Note the two layers must not be conflated:
- **card production / security printing** → USPC (GoU 51% / Veridos 49%);
- **enrolment & registration system** → Tahaluf, Tech5, Technoforte, Entrust, Hyf, on MOSIP
  (2026-02).
Veridos's absence from the 2026 provider list is therefore **not evidence** it lost — it is a
different layer of the stack. The wiki's caution "do not infer the outcome" was right; the
inference is now unnecessary because the answer is on the record.
