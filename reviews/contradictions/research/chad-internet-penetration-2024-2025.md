## Verdict: RESOLVED — DataReportal rebased its Chad internet-user series downward between the 2024 and 2025 editions; 13.2% is correct and 22.5% (Jan 2024) is a withdrawn figure. No fall in access occurred.

The dispute dissolves on reading *Digital 2025: Chad* itself, which the wiki did not hold. The
publisher's own year-on-year statement in that edition is the decisive evidence: it reports
**2.74 million internet users at January 2025 (13.2%)** and states that users grew by
**113 thousand (+4.3%) between January 2024 and January 2025**, with the adoption rate
"**remained unchanged**" over that period. Those three statements are only mutually consistent
against a **restated January-2024 baseline of roughly 2.63 million users at 13.2%** — not the
**4.18 million / 22.5%** the *Digital 2024* edition published a year earlier. The publisher
therefore silently revised its own Chad January-2024 figure **down by ~1.55 million users and
~9.3 percentage points**, and then reported growth off the revised base. The apparent
"9.3-point collapse" is entirely an artefact of comparing across the rebase — exactly the
comparison DataReportal warns readers not to make.

The rebase is confirmed as durable, not a one-off slip: *Digital 2026: Chad* (published
2025-11-08, data as of October 2025) again reports **13.2%** (2.79 million users, +83k / +3.1%
year on year, adoption again "unchanged"). Two consecutive editions hold the series flat at
13.2%, so 13.2% is the publisher's settled level, not a transient error.

**ITU corroborates the lower figure decisively.** The ITU/World Bank series for Chad
("Individuals using the Internet, % of population", indicator IT.NET.USER.ZS) runs
**8.34% (2020) → 9.65% (2021) → 11.17% (2022) → 12.04% (2023) → 12.63% (2024)** — a smooth
low-teens series with no discontinuity. DataReportal's 13.2% sits directly on this trajectory,
roughly half a point above the ITU 2024 value. The old 22.5% figure sits **~10 points above**
ITU — nearly double — and is irreconcilable with it. *Digital 2026* moreover states explicitly
that ITU is among the sources informing its internet-user figures, which explains why the
rebased series tracks ITU so closely: the rebase appears to be a re-alignment onto ITU-anchored
data, replacing whatever over-estimate produced the 2024-edition figure.

Two secondary points for the record. First, the **We Are Tech Africa relay was accurate** — its
13.2% faithfully reproduces the publisher's own figure; the wiki's problem was a provenance gap
(no primary on file), not a bad relay. Second, the **population denominator was also rebased**:
the 2024 edition put Chad's January-2024 population at 18.56m, while the 2025 edition reports
20.7m at January 2025 after +857k (+4.3%) growth, implying a restated January-2024 population of
~19.8m. Both numerator and denominator moved, but the numerator moved far more, so the rebase is
overwhelmingly a **user-count** revision.

**Implication for the wiki beyond Chad.** This is a publisher-wide rebase between the Digital
2024 and Digital 2025 editions, not a Chad-specific correction. Any DataReportal *Digital 2024*
penetration figure held elsewhere in the corpus may carry the same defect and should be
re-checked against the 2025/2026 editions. Note also that DataReportal **changed its publication
cycle** with the 2026 series (published end-2025, data as of October 2025), so "Digital YYYY"
no longer implies a January-YYYY as-of date — the 2026 edition warns readers explicitly against
computing change over time across its own editions.
