# Research: (a) status of Senegal's data-protection law; (b) Iris dispute ↔ breach link

## (a) Law No. 2008-12 — RESOLVED: still in force, unamended. The reform has been stuck for seven years.

**As of 2026-03-27, Law No. 2008-12 of 25 January 2008 remains Senegal's data-protection statute,
unamended.** APS quotes cybersecurity and AI-governance consultant **Saïkou Fall**:
*"la loi sénégalaise en matière de protection des données, la loi 2008-12 du 25 janvier 2008, est
devenue caduque"*, and *"Le Sénégal tarde encore en tout cas à mettre à jour"* sa loi. He adds that a
bill *"serait déjà dans le circuit"* awaiting a National Assembly vote.
URL: https://aps.sn/donnees-personnelles-la-legislation-senegalaise-doit-etre-modernisee-et-adaptee-aux-enjeux-de-lia-expert/

**Chronology of the stalled reform**, assembled from dated secondary accounts:

| Date | Step |
|---|---|
| 2017 | CDP initiates revision of the 2008 law (per Le Tech Observateur) |
| 2018–2019 | Stakeholder workshops; first draft validated |
| 2019 | Draft bill published — would replace the CDP with an **APDP** of 12 members, widen "sensitive data" to include filiation and genetic data, address biometrics, big data, AI, geolocation, cloud (CIPESA, 2020-01-14) |
| 2022 | **Favourable opinion from the Cour Suprême**; adoption by the Conseil des ministres still pending (Le Tech Observateur, 2024-10-03) |
| 2024-05-13 | Nine Senegalese experts publish seven reform recommendations, first of which is to *accelerate* adoption (Digital Business Africa) |
| 2024-10-10/11 | CDP + Ministry hold a Dakar workshop to finalise the updated bill and its implementing decree |
| undated (post-SND/SNIA) | Further CDP/Ministry "mise à niveau" workshop at Saly, per Le Quotidien — CDP president **Ousmane Thiongane** says the bill must be levelled up for the New Deal technologique, data governance and AI |
| 2026-03-27 | Still not adopted (APS, above) |

**The wiki's earlier "revision underway (2025)" phrasing was not wrong, but it was under-dated and
under-stated.** The accurate finding is stronger: a revision has been "underway" continuously since
2017, has cleared the Cour Suprême (2022), and has still not reached a vote as of 2026-03-27 —
through a national biometric-ID breach in February 2026. That is a finding about state capacity, not
a data gap.

## (b) Iris Corp unpaid-invoices dispute ↔ February-2026 breach — NOT ESTABLISHED, and correctly so.

**No authority, court filing or party to the contract has stated any causal connection on the
record.** What exists:

- The **dispute itself is on the record** in reporting: Libération (relayed by GMS, SeneNews,
  Seneweb, 2026-02-06) reports a *"différend"* between the state and Iris Corporation over
  **factures impayées** under the 2016 contract (50bn FCFA, 10m cards).
- The **DSC investigation is examining Iris's access**, not the dispute as a motive: reporting says
  investigators are looking at "les connexions ou les accès maintenus dans le cadre de cette
  collaboration." That is a supply-chain access question, not an allegation about non-payment.
- **Netcomsn (2026-02-06)** is the only outlet to advance the dispute as a possible *cause*, and
  frames it explicitly as speculation: the unpaid invoices *"pourrait avoir créé des failles
  (maintenance négligée ou acte de malveillance)."* Conditional, unattributed, unsupported.
- **ZATAZ (2026-02-11)** states the correct position plainly: *"Sans conclure au lien de causalité, la
  concomitance entre tensions contractuelles, arrêt prolongé et incident cyber renforce un risque
  bien connu : celui d'une dépendance critique à un prestataire."*

**Conclusion:** the link is press juxtaposition. The defensible statement is the structural one —
critical dependence on a single foreign contractor, in commercial dispute, at the moment the state
needed it to respond — not a causal claim. Neither IRIS nor the DAF answered requests for comment
(ZATAZ). No litigation record was found.
