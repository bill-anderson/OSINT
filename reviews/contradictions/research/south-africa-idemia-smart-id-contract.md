# Research: IDEMIA's alleged ten-year, 80-million-card South African ID contract

## Headline

**Resolved — negative.** The contract does not appear to exist. The claim traces to **one unsourced
sentence** in a trade-aggregator post, and the documented procurement record contradicts it on the
central point: **South Africa's Smart ID cards are produced in-house by the Government Printing Works,
not by IDEMIA.**

## Where the claim comes from

The wiki's only account is `raw/2025-02-04 South Africa Plans Major Expansion of Smart ID Program for
2025.md` — **ID Tech Wire**, 2025-02-04, "ID Tech Editorial Team", footer-credited to **BizNews**.
https://idtechwire.com/south-africa-plans-major-expansion-of-smart-id-program-for-2025/

The entire basis of the claim is one sentence: *"The program builds upon IDEMIA's recent 10-year
contract to upgrade South Africa's ID system, which includes the provision of 80 million smart ID cards
and implementation of comprehensive biometric enrollment capabilities."*

No award date, no value, no awarding body, no citation. It is a **second-hand aggregator summarising a
second-hand aggregator** — the shape of item the wiki's admissibility rules already treat with
suspicion.

## What the record actually shows

**No trace of such a contract anywhere.** No DHA announcement, no gov.za media statement, no National
Treasury or eTenders award record, no IDEMIA press release, no coverage by ITWeb, MyBroadband, News24,
Moneyweb or Biometric Update — all of which cover IDEMIA's South African contracts closely and
adversarially. A ten-year, 80-million-card national award would be the largest identity procurement in
the country's history and could not have passed unreported by this press corps.

**Smart ID card production is a GPW in-house function.**
- GPW (a Government Component reporting to the DHA) states its identity-documents unit *"produce[s] the
  country's green book identity document and ID smartcards"* with capacity of *"a minimum 3 million
  cards up to 9 million cards [per] annum"* (GPW, Manufacturing and Engineering).
- Minister Pandor, 2013, on launch: *"our own Government Printing Works taking on the exciting
  assignment of producing this on government's behalf."*
- The DHA credited its record **4,002,964 Smart IDs issued in calendar 2025** to closer coordination
  with **GPW** (TimesLIVE, 2026-01-29) — no vendor named.

**The card-supply contracts that do exist are Gemalto's and Altech's, from 2013, not IDEMIA's.**
Announced by Minister Pandor 2013-05-23: **Gemalto Southern Africa** to supply GPW with blank
pre-printed polycarbonate cards with contactless chip (**€16m / ~R210m**; Gemalto's own release,
2013-06-05, "Sealys eID"; ~**3m cards/year**); **Altech Card Solutions** to supply GPW with card
personalisation machines and automated mailing (**R40m**).

**IDEMIA's actual DHA relationship is ABIS — a biometric matching system, not cards.**
- ABIS announced Dec 2017; awarded to **EOH** at **R400m**, IDEMIA a subcontractor.
- DHA **ceded** the contract to IDEMIA effective **2021-03-31**, after EOH exited government work.
  **SITA objected** that the cession bypassed tender and breached the Constitution and the PPPFA;
  Treasury approved it. R224m had already been paid to EOH.
- DHA then concluded a **new R191m agreement** with IDEMIA to complete the failed project (deadline
  2021-10-31, missed).
- DHA levied fines of **R65m (FY2021/22)** and **R69m (FY2022/23)** on EOH and IDEMIA for missed
  milestones. **NEC Africa** litigated to set the award aside (2022-02).
- Per the DHA Strategic Plan 2025–2030, **ABIS Phase 2 completed Feb 2024** against a 2022/23 target.

**IDEMIA's only South African card-production contract was for driving licences — and it was struck
down.** DoT/DLCA named IDEMIA preferred bidder **2024-08-08** (budget R486.385m; value reported at
**R898m**). The **Auditor-General** found the tender irregular (2025-03). The **Gauteng North High
Court** declared it *"irregular, invalid, unlawful and unenforceable"* on **2026-01-06**, set it aside,
and ordered re-advertisement within 30 days — with printing meanwhile outsourced to the DHA. That is a
**Department of Transport** matter, **not** the national ID card.

**Also terminated:** ACSA's airport biometrics contract with IDEMIA (R380m, awarded Aug 2023;
terminated Aug 2024 amid the InfoVerge BBBEE dispute).

## Why "80 million" is implausible on its face

South Africa's population is ~63 million; ~**16 million** green ID book holders remained as of 2026-05
(Schreiber). At GPW's stated ceiling of 9m cards/year, 80m would consume essentially the whole decade
of maximum national capacity. The figure has the texture of aggregator confabulation rather than a
contract term.

## GPW's real 2025 role — which the brief flagged and which checks out differently

GPW is the **state security printer** reporting to the DHA (established 2009, Government Gazette
32616), producing smart ID cards, passports, certificates and — per the DoT's own 2026-01 statement —
now the **prototype driving licence card**, approved by the **State Security Agency**, with an RTMC↔GPW
network link successfully tested. GPW's role is expanding into *more* in-house production, not less.
This is the opposite direction of travel from an outsourced ten-year vendor card contract.

## Conclusion

The claim should be **struck as unverified** and recorded as an aggregator error. IDEMIA is a real and
material actor in South African identity infrastructure — but as the **ABIS biometric-matching
contractor** (ceded 2021, litigated, fined, Phase 2 completed 2024), not as the supplier of the Smart
ID card.
