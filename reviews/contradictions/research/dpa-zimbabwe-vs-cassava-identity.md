# "Distributed Power Africa" — RESOLVED: one brand, three distinct registered entities

The brief asked whether the ZAF solar company and the ZWE InfraCo shell are the same registered
entity. **They are not** — and the South African PPA counterparty is a *third* company the wiki had
not identified at all.

## The decisive document

**Cassava Technologies / Africa Data Centres release, 2023-03-14** (the announcer of record for the
March-2023 PPA the wiki attributes to "DPA"):

> "Africa Data Centres… has signed a 20-year Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with **DPA Southern
> Africa Pty Ltd (DPA SA)**, **a joint company of Distributed Power Africa and the French utility
> company, EDF**."

> "Through the creation of a **50:50 Joint Venture between DPA and EDF** in South Africa **via DPA
> Southern Africa (Pty) Ltd**…"

So Account A's counterparty is **DPA SA (Pty) Ltd** — South African-registered, 50% EDF-owned,
its own CEO (**Nawfal El Fadil**, per Cassava 2024-04-08). Not DPA itself, and certainly not the
Zimbabwe entity.

## Common origin, confirmed

- **2021-11-10** (Econet Group / Liquid): Cassava Technologies launched, folding in "Liquid
  Intelligent Technologies, Africa Data Centres, Liquid Cloud, Sasai Fintech, Vaya Technologies,
  and **Distributed Power Technologies**." Econet's *mobile* businesses (incl. Econet Wireless
  Zimbabwe) explicitly **unaffected** — i.e. the Cassava/EWZL split is the group boundary the two
  DPAs sit either side of.
- **2026-06-04** (Energy & Power Insider): DPA "was originally built on the **Econet Group's**
  extensive experience operating power systems and battery backups for its mobile network base
  stations" — confirming a single origin, and DPA "now manages energy infrastructure across
  several African nations, including South Africa, Kenya, Zambia and the DRC."

## Post-restructuring, in Zimbabwe

Econet InfraCo's own about page (2025-12-13) lists three constituent businesses: **Econet Towers,
Distributed Power Zimbabwe (DPZ), and Econet Properties**. The Zimbabwe distributed-power operating
business is now branded **DPZ** — a rename that decouples it from the DPA brand still used by
the Cassava-side pan-African business.

**Caution — one unreconciled looseness.** Energy & Power Insider (2026-06-04) calls "Econet InfraCo
**the parent company of** DPA" and refers to "Distributed Power Africa **(Private)** Limited"
applying for a 50MW Harare solar licence, *after* that entity converted to a public company. Either
the trade press is imprecise, or a separate still-private DPA operating subsidiary sits under
InfraCo. Do not build on this sentence.
