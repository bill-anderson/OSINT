## Verdict: PARTIALLY CONCLUSIVE — the three figures are *not* a growth trajectory, and the brief's "2.000 km = truncation of 22.000" hypothesis is disproved; but no reconciled current km figure can be established, because Angolan official statements are themselves mutually inconsistent inside an eight-month window.

### What the research establishes

**1. 22,000 km is a stale boilerplate figure, not a 2026 measurement.** It has been in
circulation since at least a **2019 World Bank report** (cited by Freedom House's *Freedom on
the Net 2023*: "Angola Telecom also controls 12,000 kilometers of the 22,000 kilometers of
fiber-optic cables in the country"), and was stated verbatim by MINTTICS' National Director of
Telecommunications, Matias Borges, in a **May 2023** Jornal de Angola interview published on the
ministry's own site: *"Ao nível da rede de fibra óptica terrestre, possui mais de 22.000 km que
conecta todas as capitais provinciais e comunais."* The minister's April-2026 restatement
(TechAfrica News, Ecofin) is therefore a **repetition of a ≥3-year-old figure**, not a
contemporaneous count. Its true as-of date is c. 2019–2023.

**2. The decisive datum: >30,000 km, stated in November 2025 — *before* both other figures.**
Secretary of State for Telecommunications **Ângelo Buta João**, speaking at the ITU World
Telecommunication Development Conference in Baku, said Angola has *"more than 30,000 kilometers
of terrestrial fiber, with another 4,860 under repair and 1,400 new ones under construction, as
part of the national broadband network project"* (Angop, 2025-11-28; reprinted OPaís
2025-12-02). This figure is **higher than the 28,000 km asserted seven months later** and
**higher than the 22,000 km asserted five months later**. Monotonic growth is therefore
arithmetically impossible, and the "14,000 → 22,000 → 28,000" trajectory hypothesis in the brief
must be rejected outright.

**3. 14,000 km is UNITEL's own footprint, not the national total.** The Angop 2025-12-13 sentence
— *"A fibra óptica nacional, hoje com 14 mil quilómetros de comprimento, permite a presença em
todas as capitais provinciais, possuindo também redes metropolitanas dentro das grandes
cidades"* — closes a report of **UNITEL's** 2Africa landing-station inauguration at Cacuaco,
quoting UNITEL executives. The wording ("a fibra óptica nacional") is genuinely ambiguous, but
the figure **cannot** be the country total: the official total was ≥22,000 km from 2023 and
>30,000 km one month earlier. Read as UNITEL's national-scope network — presence in all
provincial capitals plus metro rings — it is coherent. This reading is **strongly inferred, not
directly stated**; the ambiguity is Angop's.

**4. "Mais de 2.000 km" is an annual new-build increment, not a truncation of 22,000.** The brief
speculated the March-2026 Angop rendering was a typo. It is not. The same ~2,000 km appears as a
*planned annual addition* across three separate years: President **João Lourenço** at ANGOTIC,
June 2024 (*"estando em preparação a instalação de cerca de mais 2.000 Km"*); Minister **Mário
Oliveira** at the III Conselho Consultivo, May 2025 (*"Angola vai contar, ainda este ano, com
mais de dois mil kilómetros de fibra óptica"*); and again in May 2026 (allAfrica: *"the
deployment of over 2,000 kilometres of fibre optics"*). It is a recurring RNBL build target.

**5. A fourth and fifth official figure exist, deepening the incoherence.** MINTTICS' *Estado da
Nação 2025* (Oct 2025) put the network at *"mais de 20 mil km"*. The 28,000 km figure, meanwhile,
is not UNESCO's own measurement: it was given by **Vicente Lopes**, UNESCO's *national consultant*
for the RAM, presenting **preliminary data** on 2026-06-30 (pti.ao) — a consultant's input
parameter, not an independently verified count.

### Why it cannot be closed

No Angolan official source distinguishes **national backbone** from **total deployed fibre**
from **all-operator aggregate**; every statement says only "rede de fibra óptica terrestre" or
"fibra óptica nacional". INACOM's *Relatório Anual Estatístico* (the authoritative series, 2024
edition launched at ANGOTIC 2025) is the instrument that would settle it, but the PDF was not
retrievable from `observatoriotic.gov.ao` or `inacom.gov.ao` in this pass. **This is a data-quality
defect in the Angolan official record, not a wiki error.**

| ~2,000 km | Pres. Lourenço 2024-06; Min. Oliveira 2025-05 & 2026-05 | recurring | **Annual new-build increment** under the RNBL | **Not** a truncation of 22,000. A flow, not a stock |
| 28,000 km | Vicente Lopes, UNESCO RAM national consultant (preliminary) | 2026-06-30 | Total national fibre, as an input to the AI-readiness assessment | Third-party consultant's figure; unverified, and *lower* than the Nov-2025 official figure |

**Bottom line:** these are a **stock/flow confusion (2,000 km), an operator-vs-national confusion
(14,000 km), and a genuinely incoherent set of official national totals (20,000 / 22,000 / 28,000
/ 30,000 km) that do not form a time series.** The wiki must present them as dated, attributed,
mutually inconsistent official claims — never as a trajectory, and never with a single bare
"current" value.
