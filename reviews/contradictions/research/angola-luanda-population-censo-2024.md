## Verdict: RESOLVED — neither Novo Jornal figure is Luanda's definitive provincial total; the definitive figure is 8,816,297 (INE, national definitive results, reference date 19 Sep 2024, published 2025-11-20). Claim A (7,961,235) is the BI indicator's aged-5-and-over denominator, not a population total; Claim B ("mais de 8,6 milhões") is INE's *own* Luanda provincial-report total of 8,665,510 (Feb 2026) — which is itself 150,787 lower than the national report, an unresolved INE-internal discrepancy now filed separately.

The dispute as framed dissolves once the two Novo Jornal numbers are read against what INE actually
published. It is not a preliminary-vs-definitive revision — Angola's Censo 2024 preliminary results
were never the source of either figure, and both Novo Jornal pieces post-date the definitive release.

**The definitive national figure.** INE presented the *Resultados Definitivos* of the RGPH 2024 on
**20 November 2025**, with a census reference date (*momento censitário*) of **19 September 2024**.
Quadro 7 of INE's own *Relatório Síntese* gives Luanda province **8,816,297** (4,338,624 men /
4,477,673 women) — 24.1% of the national 36,604,681. The table's sixteen municipal rows sum to
exactly 8,816,297, so the figure is internally consistent, and Angop's same-day report of the launch
quotes it verbatim. INE director-general Joel Futi stated the totals were produced by dual-system
estimation, combining field enumeration with the post-enumeration Inquérito de Cobertura to adjust
for omissions and duplications — i.e. the national figure is an *adjusted* count, not a raw tally.

**Claim A (7,961,235) is not a population total.** Novo Jornal's own sentence reads: "Dos 7.961.235
de cidadãos contados pelo Censo 2024, apenas 5.463.017 têm BI." The INE indicator being reported is
explicitly *"Proporção da população **com 5 ou mais anos** com Bilhete de Identidade"* (national
value 52.4%). 7,961,235 is 90.3% of 8,816,297, implying 855,062 (9.7%) aged 0–4 — plausible for
low-fertility urban Luanda. The number appears in **no INE population table** as a provincial total.
Novo Jornal appears to have taken the BI table's age-restricted denominator and described it as
citizens "counted by the census". *Caveat: the underlying INE table could not be fetched directly
(the full definitive-results PDF download link did not resolve), so the aged-5+ reading is a strong
inference from the indicator definition and arithmetic, not a table-level confirmation.*

**Claim B ("mais de 8,6 milhões") is a real INE figure — from a different INE report.** On
**24 February 2026** INE held the Luanda provincial launch, closing the national exercise, and gave
Luanda **8,665,510** (4,263,902 men / 4,401,607 women). Novo Jornal's 17 March 2026 piece post-dates
this and matches it. But Novo Jornal pairs "mais de 8,6 milhões" with **24.1%** — a share that
belongs to 8,816,297; 8,665,510 is 23.7% of the national total. So Claim B is a genuine INE number
hybridised with the national report's percentage.

**A residual INE-internal discrepancy.** The national definitive report (8,816,297) and INE's own
Luanda provincial report (8,665,510) differ by **150,787** — and the sex split differs consistently
(74,722 fewer men, 76,066 fewer women). The provincial figure is also structurally lower at municipal
level: the provincial launch put the top five municipalities at 4,566,754 (~53%), whereas the same
five in the national table sum to 4,717,542 — a difference of 150,788, i.e. the entire gap. The most
plausible reading is that the provincial report carries the **raw enumerated count** while the
national report carries the **dual-system-adjusted** count, but INE has published no reconciliation
note and neither report was obtainable in full. This is **not** resolved here and is filed as a new
contradiction.

**Bonus reconciliation — a fourth circulating figure.** Ponto de Situação (2025-11-21) reported
Luanda at **10,188,967**. That is exactly 8,816,297 + 1,372,670 (Icolo e Bengo), i.e. Luanda on its
**pre-2025 boundaries**, before the Divisão Política Administrativa carved out Icolo e Bengo and
took Angola from 18 to 21 provinces in 2025. Any Luanda figure near 10.2m is the old boundary; near
8.8m is the current one.
