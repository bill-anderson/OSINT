# REV-DJI-001 research — Djibouti ANCS (2024) vs ANC (2025)

## Verdict: the statutory body is the ANC; "ANCS" is a pre-statutory/informal label, not a separate ongoing institution — and not a simple rename.

The real institution is the **Autorité Nationale de Cybersécurité (ANC)**, created by **Loi n° 195/AN/25/9ème L**
(adopted by the National Assembly **12 Nov 2025**, promulgated, published in the **Journal Officiel n°23 of
15 Dec 2025**). It is an **établissement public à caractère administratif** with legal personality and
financial/administrative autonomy, HQ Djibouti, placed **under the Presidency / Direction Générale de la
Sécurité Nationale** (Art. 2), led by a Director-General appointed by decree; a **Conseil National de
Cybersécurité** (chaired by the DG de la Sécurité Nationale) sets strategy and acts as its board.

**Institutional lineage (law Art. 24 — decisive):** the ANC's *initial nucleus* is the **DSSI (Direction de la
Sécurité des Systèmes d'Information) of ANSIE** (Agence Nationale des Systèmes d'Information de l'État, created
by Loi n°100/AN/15). DSSI's missions, means and personnel transfer to the ANC, and ANSIE's law is amended so
state-IT-security functions move to the ANC. So the genuine predecessor is **ANSIE's DSSI**, NOT a body called
"ANCS."

**On "ANCS":** no primary source names a statutory "ANCS." The only "ANCS" mention is We Are Tech (2 Dec 2024)
attributing the 2024–2030 Strategy to a "National Cybersecurity Authority (ANCS)." Best read as an
**informal / pre-legal designation** for Djibouti's cybersecurity-authority function (operating within ANSIE's
remit) that produced the Strategy — before the statutory ANC was constituted. Ecofin (Nov 2025) treats the
Strategy as a pre-existing *initiative* and the ANC as the *new* consolidating institution, consistent with this.

**Operational status:** the ANC is operational from **Dec 2025** — Director-General **Abdallah Ibrahim Yonis**
appointed; equipment handover and official launch (techafricanews, 9 Dec 2025).
