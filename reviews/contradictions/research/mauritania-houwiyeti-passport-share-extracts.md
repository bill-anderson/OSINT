## Verdict: LARGELY CONCLUSIVE — a metric/basis difference, not a genuine reversal; the ANRPTS 24.8%→32.9%→35.7% series is the like-for-like measure of app-initiated passport requests, and the ID4Africa "70%" cannot be a share of national passport requests. The two extract counts are demonstrably on different bases, but what the 622,000 figure counts cannot be established from sources on file.

### The passport share — settled by arithmetic

Both claims trace to the same institution (ANRPTS), so this was never an inter-agency conflict; it is a
question of what each figure's denominator is. An independent denominator anchor settles it.

ANRPTS DG Sidi Ali Ould Nafee told a Nouakchott press conference (18 Aug 2023) that national passport
demand rose from **20,000 in 2020 to 94,000 in the year to mid-2023** — i.e. Mauritania runs on the order
of **90,000–100,000 passport requests a year across all channels**.

Against that denominator, Claim B's own numbers check out and Claim A's does not:

- Claim B reports **63,384 cumulative passport requests via Houwiyeti** over roughly 2.5 years of public
  operation (app opened to the public Jan 2024; figures presented June 2026) — about **25,000/year**.
- 25,000 against ~90,000–100,000/year is **~25–30%**, which sits squarely inside the reported series
  (24.8% in 2024 → 32.9% in 2025 → 35.7% in Jan 2026).
- For 70% of national passport requests to run through the app, Houwiyeti would have had to carry roughly
  **63,000 passport requests per year**, i.e. essentially its entire *cumulative* total every single year.
  That is not compatible with the same presentation's own cumulative count.

So the two figures cannot both describe "share of passport requests submitted via the app." Claim B's
series is the internally consistent, month-dated, like-for-like measure.

What the "70%" *is* cannot be pinned down from sources on file. It was an **oral remark at a conference
panel**, relayed by a journalist, with no denominator stated and no slide deck published — the ID4Africa
2025 Knowledge Hub page lists the Mauritania country progress report but serves no downloadable deck.
Plausible readings (none verifiable here): renewals only; a single office or the Nouakchott catchment; the
diaspora/consular channel; or requests that *touch* the digital pipeline at any stage rather than those
initiated in the app. It should be treated as an **unverified, undenominated claim**, not as a competing
value for the same metric.

### The extract counts — different bases, proven by an impossibility

Claim A: "more than **622,000 document extracts** were processed **this year**" (as of May 2025).
Claim B: **406,871 cumulative civil-status extracts**, inside **554,027 total remote services** of all
types (as of June 2026).

A cumulative total cannot shrink. If Claim A's 622,000 were app transactions on Claim B's basis, the June
2026 all-service cumulative total (554,027) would have to exceed a figure already reached thirteen months
earlier — it does not. The two are therefore **certainly not comparable**. Claim A's figure most likely
counts something wider than app-initiated service requests (extracts issued agency-wide across all
channels via SIGPTS, or digital-extract generations/downloads/verifications), but **no source on file
establishes which**, and that gap should be recorded rather than guessed.

### Corroboration of Claim B's transcription

A second Mauritanian outlet, *Mourassiloun*, independently reported the same June 2026 ANRPTS presentation
with **identical figures** (677,000 activated accounts; 554,027 remote services; 1,511,139 beneficiaries;
406,871 extracts; 63,384 passport requests; 59,363 ID-card requests; 24,409 e-certificates). Claim B's
numbers are therefore reliably transcribed from the presentation, not a Tech Rek artefact. *(Not clipped:
payload identical to the Tech Rek source already held, and the page carries no publication date.)*

### Residual uncertainty

- No ANRPTS primary statistical publication (annual report, open dashboard, statistics page) was found.
  `anrpts.gov.mr` publishes service descriptions and verification tooling only; the ARMP procurement portal
  shows ANRPTS commissioned a "Dashboard and BI Houwiyeti" system, but no output from it is public. All
  usage figures reach the public domain only as conference remarks reported by third parties.
- The ID4Africa 2025 Mauritania slide deck is not downloadable, so the "70%" cannot be checked against its
  own stated denominator.
- The 2023 passport-volume anchor is three years older than Claim B's series; if national demand rose
  sharply after 2023 the implied share would fall further, which weakens the "70%" reading further, not
  less.
