# Care 2.0 vs SmartCare Pro — resolved

**Answer: "Care 2.0" is a truncation of *SmartCare 2.0*, and SmartCare 2.0 is a
tier-specific edition ("Hospital Edition") of the same national SmartCare EHR
lineage — not a distinct build, not a rename of SmartCare Pro, and not a
replacement for it. The two run concurrently at different tiers of the health
system.**

## The version lineage (implementer/MoH portal, smartcarezambia.io)

Stated on the SmartCare programme portal: "SmartCare Pro is the latest iteration of
Zambia's national Electronic Health Record (EHR) system, following its predecessors,
**SmartCare Legacy** and **SmartCare Plus**."

Programme-managers deck (smartcarezambia.io, 2025-03) gives the evolution timeline:
- **2003** — CCPTS piloted ANC module.
- **2005** — SmartCare approved as the MoH EHR standard (US Government partnership;
  MoH Permanent Secretary circular of 5 April 2006 makes SmartCare "the approved
  patient level health care information system").
- **2017** — release of **SmartCare Plus**.
- **2019** — SC+ v1.6 scaled to 350 facilities.
- **2022/2023** — launch of **SmartCare Pro**.
- Legacy + Plus were collectively in ~1,800 facilities across 10 provinces at the
  time of the Pro transition.

SmartCare Pro national launch: **2023-11-21**, Chongwe District, by Health Minister
Sylvia T. Masebo (Efficacy News, 2023-11-21).

## SmartCare 2.0 = the hospital tier, piloted 2026-04-27

- **Event date 2026-04-27** (published 2026-04-28, TechAfrica News): SMART Zambia
  Institute, with the **Ministry of Health** and **IHM Southern Africa**, launched the
  **"SmartCare 2.0 — Hospital Edition" pilot** at **Ndola Teaching Hospital**.
- IHM's own account (LinkedIn, 2026-04-27) confirms the name and the framing: the
  pilot "will run alongside **Arthur Davison Children's Hospital**", represents "a
  coordinated move toward hospital-level digital transformation" across "Zambia's
  **tertiary and specialized hospitals**", and is designed so "a single patient record
  [can] move seamlessly across departments". IHM describes itself as continuing "to
  support the **evolution of Zambia's national EHR**" — i.e. same lineage.
- SZI National Coordinator **Percy Chinyama** described SmartCare 2.0 as "the result of
  years of development"; Government CTO **Kasali Musenge** said the platform is
  "**locally developed and owned**".

## The naming drift is settled

- ITWeb Africa (2026-07-16) states it plainly: "**SmartCare Pro and SmartCare 2.0 are
  the latest versions of Zambia's electronic health record systems**, developed by the
  Ministry of Health and the Smart Zambia Institute."
- The GovLink launch coverage (Efficacy News / Telecom Review Africa / SZI LinkedIn,
  2026-07-16) renders it "**Care 2.0**" — a truncation, and the source of the wiki's
  apparent second platform. Same three pilot sites in both accounts (Ndola Teaching,
  Arthur Davison Children's, Kitwe Teaching), which is the confirming fingerprint.
- So the ITWeb "SmartCare 2.0" and the GovLink "Care 2.0" are the **same object**.

## Scale figures (for the record, dated)

- 400 facilities (SZI, 2024-08-15), target 600 by end-2024, "1,250 sites within the
  next few months".

  trained** (92% knowledge retention), replacing **1,600+ facilities on incompatible
  legacy databases** — Chinyama at the World Internet Conference, Hong Kong,
  **2026-04-14** (TechAfrica News, 2026-04-15).
