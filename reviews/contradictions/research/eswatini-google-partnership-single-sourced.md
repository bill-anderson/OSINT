## New this pass

### 1. Event date — the wiki has a publication-date-as-event-date error

The page states **"Announced: 2025-01-22"**. That is **Techpoint Africa's publication date**.
The domestic account — **Inside Biz (Eswatini), 2025-01-20**,
`https://www.insidebiz.co.sz/eswatini-partners-with-google-to-drive-digital-transformation/` —
reports the launch as already held, two days earlier. MICT's own LinkedIn (2025-01-21) posts the
thanks-to-organisers note. The launch event was held at the **ERS Auditorium** on or shortly
before **2025-01-20**; day precision on the event itself is not firmly established, so record
**"launched on or before 2025-01-20"**.

### 2. Google was on the record at the launch

Not a Google corporate press release, but **Basil Ayass, Regional Sales Leader for Africa, Google
Cloud**, spoke and is extensively quoted (Inside Biz 2025-01-20; MICT LinkedIn 2025-01-21;
Celumusa Thusi 2025-01-21). Named workstreams, in his words:
1. **digital modernisation of e-government services**;
2. **AI** "to help become more efficient";
3. **Google Workspace for public servants** — "productivity and collaboration";
plus **digital skills academies**, certification in ML/AI/data analytics, and **digital addressing**
for areas without street addresses. Maziya's claim: Eswatini is the **first African country to
integrate Google Workspace into government operations**.

### 3. A parliamentary accountability trail — the strongest corroboration found

**Senate Portfolio Committee, FY2025/26 budget debate, reported 2025-04-02** (Africa-Press,
syndicating Eswatini press): **Senator Fezeka Dlamini** asked whether national data would be
compromised, noting the global trend to keep data within borders. **Maziya answered on the record**:
the ministry **signed a non-disclosure agreement**, "which is a standard global practice… access
granted on a **need-to-know basis**, and is normally **deactivated post implementation**." She also
said the **Access to Information Bill** was being finalised.

This is the minister answering to Parliament about the deal — a materially better provenance class
than a trade re-report, and it settles the "did either party put anything on the record" question.

### 4. Instrument type and value

**Still undisclosed.** Referred to throughout as a **partnership / MoU**, never as a contract with
a stated value or term. The Apr-2026 investigation (already in the wiki) established the MoU was
with **Google Cloud EMEA** and was a **cloud-services / e-government framework, not a monetisation
deal**. **No value or duration has been published by either party** — record that as an established
absence, not an open question.

### 5. Continuation

**2025-10-01** (Inside Biz): Eswatini "exploring Google Cloud services, including **Digital
Addressing, Public Key Infrastructure, and AI-powered tools**" — i.e. the Jan-2025 MoU is live and
extending toward PKI, the layer the GIYH privacy criticism identified as missing.
