## Verdict: RESOLVED — 2027 and 2030 are different milestones of the same chantier: 2027 is the political headline horizon covering two 750-locality phases (~1,500 sites); 2030 is the operational horizon for clearing all ~2,000 white zones to 100% national coverage.

The « Zéro zone blanche » chantier is the **first of the 12 chantiers** of the Burkinabè ministry's
**"Transition Digitale 2030"** roadmap — the roadmap's own horizon year is 2030, which is the strongest
structural clue that 2030 is not a slip or a rival figure but the programme's designed end-state.

The arithmetic reconciles cleanly. Burkina Faso has **~2,000 uncovered administrative villages**
(consistently stated by the ministry from 2025-09-30 onward). Against that:

- **223 localities** covered 2020–2024 via the FASU (some accounts give ~330 since 2019).
- **Phase 1 — 750 localities**, launched 2025-09-30 at Ouahigouya. 75bn FCFA total (37.5bn state via
  FASU + PACTDIGITAL, 37.5bn from ONATEL / Orange Burkina Faso / Telecel). Operators have an 8-month
  execution window; completion targeted **end-2026**, with the project formally running "de 2026 à
  2027" (DG Nongobzanga Zongo, Gaoua, 2026-02-20).
- **Phase 2 — a further 750 localities**, announced for **2027** (ministry working session, 2026-04-04).

750 + 750 + ~223–330 already done ≈ **1,720–1,830 of ~2,000**. The two phases therefore get the country
most of the way to zero by end-2027 but demonstrably **not to zero** — which is exactly the residual the
2030 date covers.

So the two figures are not in conflict about the same thing; they attach to different scopes. What *is*
real, and worth recording as a caveat rather than a contradiction, is that **the ministry's own political
messaging conflates them**: the minister at launch called end-2027 the "objectif ultime … zéro zone
blanche", and the Prime Minister repeated "d'ici 2027" in the state-of-the-nation address (2026-01-30),
while the Director-General of Electronic Communications — the operational official — twice gave the
full-territory horizon as **2030**. Where the two framings diverge, the DG's 2030 figure is the
operationally authoritative one and the 2027 figure is a political target attached to the funded phases.

A third date appears in the coverage and should not be mistaken for a fourth position: **end-2026** is
the delivery deadline for Phase 1's 750 sites, not a programme-completion date.

~2,000-locality denominator and the "Transition Digitale 2030" roadmap framing, all of which are
consistently reported. The inference is strong but the ministry has not itself published the distinction.
