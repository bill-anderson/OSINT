# Research: Eswatini GIYH app — launch date and the privacy criticism

## Launch — FIXED at 2025-05-21, Ezulwini

**The wiki already held this and had not applied it.** `raw/2025-05-21-eswatini-launches-new-apps-giyh-taxpal.md`
(Independent News Eswatini, `date_source: source`, ingested in the SWZ-2026-07-17 sweep) reports
the launch as same-day ("were unveiled today"), at **Ezulwini**, led by ICT Minister **Savannah
Maziya** with the **Eswatini Revenue Service**; the **ERS TaxPal** app launched alongside. Finance
Minister **Neal Rijkenberg** and ERS Commissioner General **Brightwell Nkambule** spoke.

Corroborating: TechAfrica News 2025-05-22; Maziya's own LinkedIn 2025-12-12 ("In May this year, we
officially launched"). So the `government-in-your-hand.md` page's vague "May 2025" attributed to a
2025-08-05 World Bank piece was simply **stale**, not unknowable.

Launch-day service set: check status of IDs, passports and travel documents; register a business
name; pay vehicle licences via Mobile Money; access educational-financing information. Login is
**national ID + OTP by SMS**. Maziya: new services "every six months."

Uptake: **over 2,000 downloads** by 2025-06-11 (Eswatini Positive News, quoting MICT engineer
Sakhile Dlamini) — against a stated ambition of ~200 services (ToR / Digital Eswatini).

## The privacy criticism — SUBSTANCE NOW ESTABLISHED

Primary account: **Biometric Update, 2025-07-04**,
`https://www.biometricupdate.com/202507/digital-government-app-in-eswatini-sparks-public-criticism-over-data-privacy`

Two dated elements:

1. **The Sunday Observer finding (reported 2025-07-04).** The local paper called the app
   "**a glaring security risk**," reporting that **its own reporter was able to locate public data
   available on the system**. The outlet framed this as fuelling questions about the security of
   personal data entered into the app.
2. **Government acknowledgement (reported 2025-07-04).** **Bongani Ndlangamandla**, Director of
   eGovernment Services: *"we are aware of some gaps, hence the need for a proper digital ID and
   digital signatures."* This is an admission, not a denial — and it points at the missing
   foundational layer (digital ID + PKI), which is exactly what the Digital Eswatini ToR scopes.

**Note a sourcing defect in the wiki:** the criticism was attributed to
`2025-08-05 Robust DPI inevitable for Eswatini's economic, jobs boom - World Bank`, which mentions
it only in passing. The substantive and **earlier** account is 2025-07-04. The 2025-08-05 date was
functioning as the criticism's date; it is a re-mention, not the origin.

## Later escalation — the criticism went to the top of government

- **PM Russell Dlamini, reported Eswatini Observer 2026-03-18** (`PM Denies Firing ICT Minister
  Amid Cabinet Row`): denies opposing GIYH, but — *"The prime minister's office is, however,
  concerned about the protection of people's data should our systems not be robust enough, hence
  the emphasis that Government In Your Hand must be **watertight**."* He adds the PM's office
  **does not support outsourcing data to global IT companies**, and that any MoU signed without
  proper data-protection consideration is **"meaningless."**

This connects the two Eswatini briefs: the GIYH privacy critique and the Google-MoU controversy
are the same argument at different moments.
