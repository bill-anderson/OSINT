## Verdict: RESOLVED — a **binding Memorandum of Agreement** (signed 4 June 2026) that is nonetheless only a **development framework**, not a construction or investment commitment. The two outlets were describing the *same* instrument at different levels: its legal form (binding MoA) versus its practical effect (expression of intent, pending feasibility).

The dispute dissolves on reading the primary. Convalt Energy's own press release of **8 June 2026**
states plainly that the company "has entered into a **binding Memorandum of Agreement** with the
Government of Lesotho **for the opportunity to develop**" the projects — and, in the same release,
that "**progression of this project remains subject to the ordinary course of development
milestones, including feasibility studies, permitting, financing, regulatory approvals, and
definitive project agreements**."

So the instrument is *binding in form* — it is an executed MoA, not a non-binding MoU, and the
parties are bound to a framework of cooperation — while *committing no party to build anything*.
There is no final investment decision, no EPC contract, no financing, no offtake. The $6.2bn
(M98bn) figure is the **announced value of the proposed project**, not contracted or committed
capital.

The Lesotho government's own framing corroborates this dual character rather than contradicting it.
Government spokesman **Boitelo Rabele**, quoted across the 5–6 June coverage, said the "**proposed**
project will **assess** the development of no less than 1,200MW…", cautioned that feasibility
studies and other processes remain outstanding, and described the signing as "**a strong statement
of intent and a clear indication of Lesotho's strategic direction**" — the government itself using
intent language about an agreement it had just signed.

**Where each outlet went wrong (mildly).** Tech Africa News is correct on the legal form ("binding
MoA", 4 June 2026) but omits the conditionality, leaving an impression of a committed project.
Connecting Africa is correct on the practical effect ("expression of intent rather than a
construction commitment") but **mislabels the instrument as an MoU**, which the primary contradicts.
Neither is wholly wrong; neither is complete. Note also that a **non-binding MoU did precede** the
MoA (per Lesotho Tribune, 14 June 2026), which is the most likely origin of the "MoU" label
persisting in later coverage.

**Secondary date discrepancy noted.** Signature date is **4 June 2026** in the great majority of
accounts (Convalt/Ministry of Energy). bne IntelliNews says "signed on June 6" — an outlier against
Bloomberg (5 June), Business Insider Africa (4 June, explicit), Tech Africa News (4 June) and
Lesotho Tribune (4 June). Treat **4 June 2026** as the signature date; Convalt's public
announcement was **8 June 2026**.

**Residual uncertainty:** the text of the MoA itself is not public, so "binding" rests on the

the government's words reach the record only through outlets quoting Rabele's statement. That is a
