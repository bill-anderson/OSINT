# Three threads: two confirmed in full, one genuinely unconfirmed

## 1. ZRHCP — REAL, fully documented (World Bank P503941)

The project document exists and is public.

**Project Appraisal Document, Report No. PADHI00410**, IDA, **5 September 2024**,
"Zambia Refugee and Host Communities Project", Social Sustainability and Inclusion,
Eastern and Southern Africa. Operation ID **P503941**.
<https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099090924120535793/txt/BOSIB12c22668f0f51a0d117949b7b8fe34.txt>

**Dates — this is the correction that matters.** The wiki dates ZRHCP from a
**2025-10-03** Biometric Update piece, which is a secondary re-report. The real chronology:
- PAD dated **2024-09-05**; Board approval **2024-09-26**; World Bank press release
  **2024-09-27**.
- Grant **effective 23 December 2024** (MoHAIS statement via Lusaka Times, 2025-09-30).
- **National launch event 30 September 2025** (MoHAIS, Lusaka) — this is what the
  October 2025 coverage was reporting.
- Closing **31 October 2028** — the wiki's date, confirmed.

**Financing (PAD datasheet).** SDR **22.6 million** = US$**30 million** equivalent, an
**IDA grant**, of which **US$27m from the WHR** window and **US$3m from national
Performance-Based Allocations**. The wiki's "$30m under IDA20 WHR" is imprecise — only
$27m is WHR money. Expected disbursements: FY25 $2m, FY26 $4m, FY27 $15m, FY28 $7m,
FY29 $2m. Environmental & social risk: **Substantial**.

**Components.** Datasheet: Strengthening the Enabling Environment **$7m**; Climate
Resilient Agriculture **$4m**. Earlier concept/ESMF documents give Component 1 at $8m and
Component 2 at $12–14m; the PAD datasheet is the later authority. Component 4 (Project
Management) $4m.

**Subcomponent 1.2 — "Issuing of ID cards to refugees and former refugees nationally",
US$6 million** (ESMF gives $6.5m). This is the identity limb, and its detail is
analytically substantial:
- Biometric NRCs led by the **Department of National Registration, Passports and
  Citizenship under MoHAIS, through the INRIS project** — i.e. refugees are being folded
  into the *same* register as citizens, "enabling them for the first time to be part of
  the national civil registry".
- **Three refugee-hosting districts: Kalumbila (North-Western), Nchelenge (Luapula),
  Kaoma (Western)**, plus possible "hotspots" such as Lusaka.
- **Procurement of ~150,000 cards** (PAD) / ~100,000 (ESMF) "out of the **20 million**
  that the Government will buy for citizens" — this fixes the national card procurement
  scale, which the wiki did not hold.
- **"Transfer of the UNHCR ProGres database to the MoHAIS (separate servers and
  peripheral equipment)"** — ProGres is UNHCR's registration and case-management system.
  **This is the significant finding**: refugee registration data moves from UN custody to
  the Zambian state. The wiki previously recorded "no account of consent, data-sharing
  terms" — this is the data-sharing term, and it is a wholesale custody transfer.
- IT upgrading at MoHAIS offices in the three settlements so documentation requests are
  handled locally rather than requiring travel to Lusaka.
- **Biometric registration kits**; mobile registration camps targeting women and girls.
- Safeguards language: "The project will uphold approved operating procedures applied by
  the Department of National Registration within the MoHAIS in safeguarding personal
  data, **aligned with the GRZ's Data Protection Act of 2021**."

**Population figures — a supersession chain, not a conflict.**
- **101,837** refugees and former refugees (PAD, 2024-09) — the precise administrative count.
- "over 100,000" (World Bank press release, 2024-09-27).
- **112,000** refugees and forcibly displaced persons (Biometric Update, 2025-10) — later,
  and on a broader definition ("forcibly displaced" ⊋ "refugees and former refugees").
- Meheba settlement alone hosts **40,000+**.
The wiki's held **105,000** (UNDP, 2024-09) sits between the PAD count and the 2025 figure;
all three are on different definitions and none should be adopted as *the* number.

**Rationale stated in the PAD** — worth recording because it is the state's own case:
existing refugee documents (asylum-seeker certificates, registration certificates, refugee
ID cards, alien cards) are "not aligned with the existing national documentation system
and are, therefore, not widely known and accepted by law enforcement", leaving holders
"susceptible to extortion, arrest, and detention" and unable to open bank accounts or
take loans.

**Implementation status.** An ISR (Implementation Status Report) Sequence No. 2 is
disclosed — <https://documents.worldbank.org/en/publication/documents-reports/documentdetail/099062425070539436>
— not fetched; would give current disbursement and progress. Route to acquisitions.

## 2. SMART Zambia MVNO PPP tender — NOT CONFIRMED

Searched: SZI procurement notices, DZAP procurement, tender aggregators, Zambian trade press.

**Nothing beyond the single held mention.** The Biometric Update line (2025-10-03) is
carefully hedged and does not describe a live tender: Zambia's E-Government Division "**is
taking proposals as it prepares to tender** a contract for a Mobile Virtual Network
Operator for the SMART Zambia Institute (SZI) under a public-private partnership (PPP)".
That is a pre-tender stage, which explains the absence of a reference number.

**No tender reference, no value, no bidders, no award, no outcome is findable.** State
this plainly rather than carrying it as a live procurement.

What *was* found in the same period is a different set of SZI arrangements — **not** the
MVNO, and they should not be conflated with it:
- **SZI × MTN Zambia / MTN Group** partnership (2025-08-01) — connectivity, digital
  identity, and expansion of "Project Diplomatique" using MTN service centres, banks and
  post offices as government-service points.
- **SZI × inq Zambia × Mezzanine** on the Helium platform, under a **Kwacha-denominated
  SLA** for local hosting, and **SZI × VISA** MoU on digital payments — both announced at
  the **Digital Government Africa Summit, Lusaka, 2025-10-09**.
- **DZAP procurement that is real and referenced**: "Pre-Purchase of Bulk Dedicated
  International Bandwidth Capacity for Government", RFB ref **ZM-SZ-547791-NC-RFB**,
  issued 2026-05-20, bids due 2026-07-02, under DZAP (P505094). Two lots: primary and
  redundant international IP transit. Also an RFP for "Design, Reengineering, Development
  & Deployment of E-Services", deadline 2026-01-22.

The plausible reading — **not asserted, since nothing states it** — is that the
government-owned **GovLink Private 4G** network launched 2026-07-15 is where the
"MVNO under a PPP" ambition actually landed. Recorded as an open question, not a finding.

## 3. TBI–UNDP MoU — REAL, signed, fully documented

**UNDP Zambia press release, "UNDP and Tony Blair Institute Join Forces to Boost Zambia's
Digital and Development Transformation", dated 20 November 2025** (body text datelines
"Lusaka, Zambia, 21st November 2025").
<https://www.undp.org/zambia/press-releases/undp-and-tony-blair-institute-join-forces-boost-zambias-digital-and-development-transformation>

- **Signed** — not merely proposed. Signatories: **Dr James Wakiaga**, UNDP Resident
  Representative, and **Maria Mkandawire**, TBI Country Director. Signed **in Lusaka**.
- **Note the date discrepancy in the primary itself**: the release is stamped 20 November
  2025 but its own dateline reads 21 November 2025. Carry as **2025-11-20** with the
  discrepancy noted; do not silently pick one.
- **It is a TBI–UNDP bilateral, not a government agreement.** The Government of Zambia is
  not a party — the areas were "identified jointly with the Government of Zambia", but
  the MoU binds the two organisations to coordinate their *support to* government. The
  wiki should not record this as a state commitment.
- Five cooperation areas: digital transformation and digital inclusion (public-sector
  performance, connectivity, rural digital tools and skills); innovation and public-sector
  capability development; SDG-aligned investment promotion; resilient health systems
  (technology-enabled service delivery, **rural health facility electrification**,
  improved data systems); climate-resilient agriculture and rural development.
- **No value, no duration, no termination terms disclosed**; MoU text not published.
- TBI's named contact is **Felix Ochoro, Senior Manager, Digital Transformation and
  Technology** — indicating where TBI's weight sits in this.
- Pattern context, not a Zambia fact: UNDP signed a materially similar MoU with TBI in
  **Angola on 2025-06-23** (Denise Antonio / Jackie Chimhanzi, TBI Regional Director for
  Southern Africa), "building on a growing collaboration initiated in 2024". This is a
  **regional template**, which is the analytically interesting part — TBI is placing
  country directors alongside UNDP resident representatives across Southern Africa.
