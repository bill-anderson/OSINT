# Research: does an EBRD €190m facility finance the Tunisia–Medusa connection?

## Verdict

**Yes — and the "trade-press conflation" hypothesis is wrong.** The announcer of record says it plainly.
Both claims stand; they are **different money for different objects**, not competing accounts of one thing.

## What the lender's own record says

EBRD press release, **16 January 2026**, "EBRD and Tunisie Telecom announce strategic partnership"
(https://www.ebrd.com/home/news-and-events/news/2025/eur-190-million-to-tunisie-telecom.html):

- **Borrower:** Tunisie Telecom (majority state-owned national operator). *Not* Orange Tunisia.
- **Amount:** up to **€190 million**, development-linked loan, four tranches, first committed tranche €50m.
- **Purpose (multi-component):** 4G→5G mobile access upgrade; fibre expansion; **FTTH to up to 200,000
  households**; backbone and core network modernisation; energy efficiency and energy production —
  **and**, explicitly, "*Tunisie Telecom's connection to the MEDUSA submarine cable*".
- **Structure:** interest tied to sustainability and SOE-reform milestones; **first EBRD
  non-sovereign-guaranteed public-sector financing in Tunisia since 2012**.
- **Complementary EU support:** **€11m grant** under the Neighbourhood Investment Platform (investment
  grant for core networks + cybersecurity; technical-assistance grant for the transformation programme);
  first-loss guarantee from **EFSD+** via its Digital Transformation Platform; aligned to **Global Gateway**.
- MEDUSA characterised as EU-supported, led by Spanish company **AFR-IX**, >8,000 km, ~13 countries.

The EBRD project page (project 54140, Tunisie Telecom, https://www.ebrd.com/home/work-with-us/projects/psd/54140.html)
resolves to a navigation shell on fetch — the structured PSD fields (signing date, board date, ESG
category) were **not** retrievable. The press release is the substantive record obtained.

## How this sits against the landing-station financing

The two are **unconnected instruments** that happen to touch the same cable:

| | Landing station | EBRD facility |
|---|---|---|
| Money | EU **CEF-Digital grant, €10m** (30% of costs) | EBRD loan, **up to €190m** |
| Recipient | **Orange Tunisia** | **Tunisie Telecom** |
| Date | signed **Dec 2022** | announced **16 Jan 2026** |
| Object | construction of the **Bizerte landing station** | TT's corporate network programme, of which **connecting into** MEDUSA is one component |

Orange owns the Bizerte landing station; Tunisie Telecom is a **capacity customer** connecting to the
cable. So "financing the landing" and "financing the connection" are genuinely different objects, and the
EBRD loan is the latter. The trade press did not invent the EBRD–MEDUSA link; the imprecision to guard
against is treating the €190m as **landing-station** money or as a **MEDUSA-only** facility — it is
neither.
