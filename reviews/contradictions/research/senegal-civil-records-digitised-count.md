# Senegal digitised civil-status records: 19 million vs 15.3 million

## Verdict: RESOLVED — not a regression. Two different counting bases: ~19–20m = legacy paper acts **scanned and indexed** from archives under NEKKAL; 15.3m = records **loaded into the live national database (RNEC)**, a subset of that scanned stock.

The reconciliation is confirmed by the asserting minister's own words. At the 5th NEKKAL national
steering committee on 25 June 2024, Moussa Bala Fofana said: *"Plus de 19 millions d'actes d'état
civil sont numérisés et indexés. **Une bonne partie** accessible à partir du Registre national de
l'état civil"* (Le Quotidien, 26 June 2024). The minister therefore explicitly distinguished the
archive-digitisation stock (19m+) from the smaller portion actually reachable through the national
register — precisely the gap the 2026 figure occupies. ANEC's July 2026 statistic is described by
APA as "more than 15.3 million records **integrated into the national database**", reported alongside
network/centre-connectivity metrics (629 centres, 67% on the LGEC management software, 27% on the
Sama État Civil platform) — i.e. an operational-registry count, not an archive-scanning count.

The archive stock has moved only upward on its own base: 19m+ (June 2024) → "près de 20 millions"
(Aug 2024, Ziguinchor) → 20m scanned and indexed against a 30m target (March 2025, interministerial
meeting; repeated in ANEC DG Aliou Ousmane Sall's August 2025 slide deck, which lists the remaining
10m as the outstanding work). Nothing in the record suggests records were removed or the count
revised down.

Supporting structural evidence: NEKKAL was **designed** with scanning and indexing as separate,
differently-sized workstreams. In July 2022 registrar-general Aliou Ousmane Sall stated the programme
would *"digitize 15 million civil status records, create an index of 30 million records"* — two
distinct targets. "Numérisé" and "indexé" are not interchangeable in this programme's own accounting,
which is why headline totals citing them jointly must be read carefully.

### Residual discrepancy (new, secondary)

At NEKKAL's **final** steering committee (29 June 2026) the ministry's representative credited the
programme with having *"numériser 10 millions d'actes et d'en indexer 20 millions"* — 10m scanned,
20m indexed. This does not sit cleanly with the 2024–2025 official line of "20 millions d'actes
numérisés **et** indexés" (Fofana June 2024; Sall March 2025 and August 2025 deck). Either the
earlier joint phrasing over-stated the scanning count by conflating it with the indexing count, or
the 2026 close-out re-baselined it. The 10m/20m split is the more granular and more recent statement
and is closer to NEKKAL's original 15m-scan/30m-index design. This should be treated as an open
question about the **scanning** count specifically; it does not affect the main resolution above.

Also note **headline drift**: APA and downstream outlets headline the 15.3m as "birth records", but
the body text and ANEC's own framing say *civil registration records* / *actes d'état civil* — all
civil-status event types (births, deaths, marriages, divorces), not births alone. The narrower
"birth records" wording appears to be a headline compression, not ANEC's claim.
