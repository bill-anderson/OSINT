# Research: the Sénégal Numérique SA (SENUM SA) compromise

## Verdict: RESOLVED — the company issued its own communiqué, and it says less than the press did.

### What the company itself said (the primary that was missing)

**SENUM SA communiqué, 2026-02-09**, reported by **EnQuête+**: the company says it was the target of
**"tentatives de cyberattaques"** — *attempted* cyberattacks — against its information systems "au
cours des derniers jours." Measures: **temporary restriction of access to certain services**,
reinforced threat detection, Cyberdéfense/Systèmes/Réseaux teams mobilised "en étroite coordination
avec les autorités nationales en charge de la cybersécurité," and a **Helpdesk** for affected
customers. The communiqué does **not** confirm a breach, an exfiltration, an attacker, or any
affected dataset.
URL: https://www.enqueteplus.com/content/cybersécurité-sénégal-numérique-signale-des-tentatives-de-cyberattaques-et-restreint
(Republished by Lactuacho, 2026-02-13, citing EnQuête.)

### What the press said, three days earlier

**L'Observateur (Groupe Futurs Médias), 2026-02-06 edition**, relayed by Seneweb, Senego, Atlanticactu,
SeneNews and others: SENUM SA "a également été compromise," alongside the DAF, and "la simultanéité
de ces attaques laisse craindre une offensive coordonnée." Same reporting adds that the **Division
spéciale de cybersécurité (DSC)**, under **commissaire Pape Djidiack Faye**, is investigating, with a
line of enquiry running through **Iris Corporation**'s maintained access under its 2016 CNI contract
(50bn FCFA, 10m cards), and that investigators describe "une exploitation systématique des failles de
sécurité au plus haut niveau de l'État."
URL: https://www.seneweb.com/fr/news/Technologie/apres-la-daf-senum-sa-gardienne-des-donnees-de-letat-piratee_n_482614.html

### The gap between the two

The press said **compromised**; the company said **attempted**. Nobody has reconciled them, and no
authority has published a technical accounting. The **"coordinated campaign" framing is press
inference** — attributed in every account to L'Observateur's own reading of simultaneity, never to
the DSC, SENUM or the ministry. Green Blood Group claimed the DAF; **no actor has claimed SENUM**.

## Separately: was there a national CERT?

**No.** **Clément Domingo**, quoted by INCYBER NEWS (2026-02-06), states that Senegal has neither a
national cybersecurity agency nor a national CERT, and should establish both urgently:
*"le pays ne dispose toujours ni d'une agence nationale de cybersécurité ni d'un CERT national."*
URL: https://incyber.org/article/fuite-aurait-compromis-donnees-sensibles-toute-population-senegal/
This is an expert assertion on the record, not an official confirmation; the wiki already holds the
same expert calling for a national cybersecurity agency in
`2026-03-09-connectingafrica-free-internet-cyber-risks`. No evidence found of a CERT being
established since.
