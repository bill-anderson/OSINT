# South Sudan: NIPS (Feb 2025) vs "IBPSS" (Oct 2025)

## Verdict

CONCLUSIVE — **(b)-leaning, not (a) or (c):** they are two distinct programmes, not a rebrand. **NIPS** (Feb 2025) is a *retail instant-payment rail still in development* — the February announcement was the **start of a BoSS–AfricaNenda project**, not a live system; **SSIPS/SSIPSS** (the brief's "IBPSS") is the **wholesale interbank settlement layer** (RTGS + ACH on an ATS+ platform) that **went live 2025-10-10** and is the live national payment infrastructure as of early 2026, operated by the Bank of South Sudan. NIPS is intended to ride on top of that layer; it was **not live** as of AfricaNenda's own June-2025 cut-off.

## What the record shows

**The February 2025 "launch" was a project launch, not a system going live.** AfricaNenda's own announcement (2025-02-05, dateline Juba 3 February) describes an "initiative to launch the country's first National Instant Payment System" whose "key areas of focus" are *"developing a detailed roadmap for NIPS implementation, with clear milestones"* and *"building the capacity of BOSS."* Governor Johnny Ohisa Damian called it *"the start of the NIPS journey."* Global Government Fintech headlined it accurately — "South Sudan **looks to** launch national instant payments system" — while TechCabal, Fintechnews and others compressed it to "launches." Semafor (2025-02-19) reported AfricaNenda CEO Robert Ochola saying full rollout was targeted **by December 2025** after a pilot, which is only coherent if nothing was live in February. A 2023 proof-of-concept preceded it.

**AfricaNenda's SIIPS 2025 report corroborates the negative.** SIIPS 2025 counts **33 live domestic IPS across 25 African countries as of June 2025**, including only five new since July 2024 — Algeria, Eswatini, Libya, Sierra Leone and Somalia. **South Sudan is not among them.** AfricaNenda would be the first to list South Sudan had NIPS gone live; it did not.

**The October 2025 system is a different animal, and the acronym in the brief is mangled.** BoSS's own statement (2025-10-10) announces the *"first electronic Interbank Payment and Settlement System,"* rendered variously in coverage as **SSIPS**, **SSIPSS** and **IPSS** — never "IBPSS." Its architecture is explicitly wholesale: BoSS National Payment System Director David Akum Saba-Elkher described the platform as the **Automated Payment System Plus (ATS+)** combining **RTGS** (high-value, urgent, individually settled) and **ACH** (bulk low-value batch — salaries, utilities), built on **ISO 20022**. Thirteen commercial banks connected at launch; Circular No. 07 made connection **mandatory for all licensed commercial banks by 2026-01-31** with a **$100,000** late-connection penalty.

**Why the two get conflated.** Governor Addis Ababa Othow marketed SSIPS in retail language at the launch — *"it will enable real-time payment, instant funds between individuals and businesses 24 hours a day and 7 days a week"* — and the SSIPS press framing promises mobile-money integration and interoperability. That is aspiration for the layer above, not delivered retail instant payments: RTGS+ACH is bank-to-bank settlement, and mobile-money/fintech onboarding was still being solicited at launch ("to our partners, telecom operators, fintech companies... your role is very important"). The February NIPS announcement itself had flagged the dependency in the other direction — NIPS "aligns with broader efforts to integrate other critical infrastructures, such as ACH and RTGS." **The Oct 2025 system is the prerequisite plumbing the Feb 2025 system needs.**

**Governor changed between the two events** — Johnny Ohisa Damian (Feb 2025) → Dr Addis Ababa Othow (by Oct 2025) — a further reason the two announcements read as unconnected.

**No evidence found that NIPS went live.** No BoSS, AfricaNenda or vendor announcement of a NIPS go-live surfaced for the Dec-2025 target or since. An Africaspoint piece (2026-04-16) claiming South Sudan "has activated its first-ever instant payments system" is **derivative and unreliable** — it recycles the February 2025 TechCabal framing ("three years after the proposal was tabled") over a 2026 dateline and cites no go-live event; it should not be treated as evidence of a NIPS launch.
