## Verdict: RESOLVED — the two figures are not competing totals; they describe different scopes of the same AfDB operation, and the proposed reconciliation in the brief is confirmed by the AfDB's own record.

The African Development Bank's press release of **2024-05-17** — the Bank's own account of the project it
finances — states the CAB Congo component's **total funding is €66.55 million, of which €52.47 million is
AfDB and €14.50 million is the government of Congo**, and that this envelope covers **both** the 600 km of
fibre on the Cameroon (341 km) and Central African Republic (281 km) interconnection routes **and** the
construction of the national data centre. It then states plainly: *"Some €13.8 million of the total has been
allocated to build and run the data centre."*

So the **$57m / $72.8m** pair (Claim A) is the **whole CAB Congo component** — fibre backbone plus data
centre — with the dollar figures being press conversions of €52.47m and €66.55m at roughly May-2024 rates.
The recurring **"13 million"** (Claim B) is the **data-centre line item alone** (€13.8m), which the Congolese
press has restated in dollars. Claim B is therefore a *scope-narrowed restatement of the same AfDB
financing*, not a rival figure for the AfDB's contribution.

Two honest caveats, both minor and neither disturbing the verdict:

1. **Currency slip in Claim B.** ADIAC and Tribune Eco render the data-centre allocation as **"13 millions de
   dollars."** The AfDB's figure is **€13.8 million**, which at May-2024 rates is roughly **US$15 million** —
   the conversion Developing Telecoms and ITWeb Africa both used. The Congolese outlets appear to have
   carried the numeral across from euros into dollars. The **€13.8m is the load-bearing figure**; any dollar
   rendering of the data-centre line should be given as ~US$15m, not $13m, or better, kept in euros.
2. **Whose €13.8m?** The AfDB says €13.8m "of the total" — i.e. of the combined €66.55m envelope — is
   allocated to the data centre. It does **not** state that the whole €13.8m is AfDB money as opposed to
   blended AfDB/government funds. The Bank funds 78.8% of the overall envelope; Congo's telecoms minister
   Léon Juste Ibombo said on **2025-02-28** that the BAD had financed **90% of the works** with the State's
   counterpart share still outstanding, and government counterpart delay is the documented cause of the
   2025 slowdown the ADIAC piece reports. Treat the data-centre line as **~€13.8m, overwhelmingly but not
   verifiably-exclusively AfDB**.
