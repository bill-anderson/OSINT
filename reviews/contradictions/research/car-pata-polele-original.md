# Pata Polélé — RESOLVED

Domestic original located: **Ndjoni Sango, 2026-04-18** (Bangui). Not a mirror-only claim.

- **What it is:** a **digital payment system for public revenue collection** ("mini-recettes" — administrative fees and duties) across ~20 revenue-generating ministries. Sango-language name (pata = money; polélé = clear/open).
- **Who runs it:** Ministry of Finance and Budget (Albert Ndinabé, revenue-mobilisation mission chief), built with a **Tunisian expert** in collaboration with the **Office National de l'Informatique (ONI)**.
- **Funder:** **EU**, delivered by **Expertise France** — €17.5m programme total, €8.5m to the "Justice pour l'Ouest 1" component, 4-year execution.
- **Dates:** harmonisation/restitution workshop **14–16 April 2026**, Hôtel Kitika, Bangui. A separate outlet (La Voix de l'Afrique) dates the launch event to **14 April 2026**. Pilot ministries: Justice, Finances, Intérieur.
- **Function:** replaces cash payment and manual quittances with digital receipts; traffic-fine capture and online payment via mobile money; nationality-certificate issuance.
- **Prior existence:** a 2024 Panafricaine.info feature already lists "Pata polele" alongside "Patapaye" (civil-servant salary digitisation) among CAR public-finance applications — so the system predates the 2026 workshop as a project.
