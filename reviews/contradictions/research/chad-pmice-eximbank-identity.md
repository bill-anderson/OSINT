# PMICE lender identified: China Eximbank

## Finding

The unnamed "Eximbank" is the **Export-Import Bank of China (Banque export-import de Chine)**.

- **Concessional loan ~111.4 billion FCFA**, **20-year** repayment, **2% interest**. Ratifying bill
  adopted by Chad's National Assembly, reported **8 May 2020**.
- Follows the **Chad–China framework and economic cooperation agreement signed 26 June 2019**,
  ratified **20 August 2019**.
- Loan scope as stated in 2020: national data centre; **200 sites** for Sotel Tchad; **1,200 km**
  fibre transmission network. Matches PMICE Phase I scope exactly.
- **Project launched 7 July 2020** (foundation stone, N'Djamena) at ~**114 billion FCFA (~€174m)**,
  twelve regions.
- **Contractor: Huawei Technologies**; technical supervision **GOLF/Gulf Consulting** (2025-01-10,
  Ecofin). Chinese ambassador Wang Xining present at the January 2025 site visit.
- Repayment had **already begun** by 19 May 2026 (minister Haliki, PMICE follow-up meeting).

Not a contradiction: loan ≠ total cost, and the euro-denominated total (€174m → €175.1m) is stable
across six years. The USD figures diverge only through exchange-rate movement.

## Naming discrepancy (minor)

PMICE is rendered two ways in the press: *Projet de Modernisation des Infrastructures de
**Connectivité de l'État*** (Ecofin, Dec 2025) and *Projet de Modernisation des Infrastructures des
**Communications Électroniques*** (Digital Business Africa, the 2020 launch coverage, and the
ministry's own 2026 meetings). The **2020 launch and all official-meeting coverage use
"Communications Électroniques"** — that is very likely the correct expansion; "Connectivité de
l'État" appears to be a later trade-press variant.
