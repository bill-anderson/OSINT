# Togo e-ID cumulative enrolment — research findings

## The figure, its basis and its as-of date

**More than 6 million people hold a NIU (Numéro d'identification unique) as of end-December 2025.**

- **Issuing basis:** the **Secrétariat permanent pour le suivi des politiques de réformes et des
  programmes financiers**, in its **reform-implementation report for the second half of 2025**.
  This is a Togolese government body reporting on the government's own programme — the agency-side
  statistic the brief asked for, with its own stated as-of date (**fin décembre 2025**).
- **Reported by:** Togo First, 2026-04-23 (FR) / 2026-04-24 (EN). Biometric Update's 2026-04-27 piece
  is a re-report of Togo First and explicitly says so.

## What it counts — the enrolled / issued / delivered distinction

This is the distinction the brief flagged, and the sources are unusually clean on it.

Togo First: *"les populations ont reçu **à la fois** une carte biométrique **et** un numéro unique"* —
in Togo's rollout, **enrolment, NIU issuance and card delivery are a single counter**, because the
biometric card (QR-coded) is handed over at the enrolment campaign. Biometric Update reads it the same
way: *"at least six million people have already been issued a Unique Identification Number (NIU) **and
a biometric ID card**"*.

So the safest formulation is: **6 million+ people issued a NIU and a biometric card, end-Dec 2025** —
i.e. this is a *credential-in-hand* count, not a bare enrolment-transaction count. There is no
independent published figure separating the three, so the wiki should not assert a gap between them.

Caveat worth carrying: the phrasing is *"plus de 6 millions"* — a rounded floor, not a precise count.

## Corroborating sub-national datapoint (different counter, do not sum)

Economiknews, 2025-08-14: **1,410,135 people enrolled in Golfe prefecture** in the first phase — **108%
of an initial target** set at 90% of the 1.305m residents counted at RGPH-5. ANID then moved to
Agoè-Nyivé targeting 120%. This is a **per-prefecture campaign figure**, not a national cumulative;
it is useful as evidence the campaigns over-perform their targets, and it is the only granular
enrolment number found.

## Programme framing (for context, already largely held)

- ANID's own communiqué (anid.gouv.tg, 2025-03-21): WURI-Togo project development objective runs
  **15 September 2020 → 30 July 2026**. Proof-of-concept 4 Oct–4 Nov 2023 (~2,000 people); large-scale
  pilot in Cinkassé 27 Nov–20 Dec (10,000 target).
- ANID FAQ: the programme target is **credentials for more than 8 million people by 2026** — which
  reconciles with the World Bank WURI status report's "8 million by June 2024" only as a **slipped
  target**, not a conflicting one. 6m at end-2025 against an 8m goal = **~75% of target**.
- Population context: Togo ~9.62m (Jan 2025, held on the hub). 6m/9.62m ≈ **62% of the population**.

## Conclusion

The provenance gap is closed. The wiki's floating "6 million unique numbers issued (per a May-2026
Biometric Update explainer)" is **substantially correct but was mis-attributed and undated**; it should
now rest on Togo First 2026-04-23 reporting the Secrétariat permanent's S2-2025 report, dated
**end-December 2025**, and described as NIU + biometric card issued.
