## Verdict: RESOLVED — Claim B is correct and Claim A is factually wrong. Malawi's Data Protection Act, 2024 (No. 3 of 2024) was assented 2024-01-31, gazetted 2024-02-02, and commenced 2024-06-03; the May-2025 assertion that Malawi "has not yet passed a comprehensive data protection law" is an error, not a compressed operational point.

The brief's "likely reconciliation to test" — that the Act was enacted but not operational, and a journalist compressed that into "no law" — is **not** what happened. It is close to the truth about the *operational* layer but wrong about the *legal* layer, and the two must not be merged: by May 2025 the Act had been in force for eleven months, MACRA had been designated the Data Protection Authority by statute, a compliance handbook and guidelines had been published, and the first implementing regulations were made in April 2025. ID Tech Wire additionally mis-describes the instrument, calling it the "Data Protection and Privacy Bill, first introduced in 2021, [which] remains stalled" — that Bill was passed by Parliament on 2023-12-07 and became the Data Protection Act, 2024. This is a straightforward factual error in a secondary trade outlet, and the wiki should record it as such.

There *is* a real and separately-datable operational gap, which is what makes the error superficially plausible. The Act's own transitional provision (s.53) gave data controllers and processors of significant importance six months from commencement to comply (to ~2024-12-03) and exempted all other controllers and processors for twenty-four months, i.e. until 2026-06-02 — so for most of the period the ID Tech Wire piece describes, the substantive obligations did not yet bite on the general population of controllers. More significantly, the Data Protection Authority unit inside MACRA was still being institutionally stood up well after commencement: in **October 2025** the Government of Malawi, through the World-Bank-financed Digital Malawi Acceleration Project, tendered for consultancy support to design the DPA unit's organisational structure, governance charter, job descriptions, operations manual, monitoring/enforcement framework and *further* secondary legal instruments. As of **2026-07-14** MACRA was still consulting stakeholders on draft registration-fee regulations. So: enacted and in force since mid-2024, but enforcement machinery genuinely incomplete into 2026.
