# CAR état-civil / digital-identity strategic report (April 2025) — RESOLVED

**Domestic original: Oubangui Médias, 2025-04-18.** The `rca.news-pravda.com` item is a verbatim mirror of it. A second, independent domestic account exists from the state agency **ACAP** (also carried on the mirror), giving two domestic origins.

- **What it is:** the **final report of the strategic diagnostic of the digital-identity and civil-registration (état civil) ecosystem** — 97 pages, titled *« Diagnostic de la République centrafricaine »*, the product of **nearly two years of study**.
- **Who validated it:** the government, at a validation workshop **17 April 2025** in Bangui (Salle des fêtes Lory), opened by **Prime Minister Félix Moloua**, with ministers of Health, Digital Economy, Public Service and Interior present.
- **Funder / frame:** **World Bank**, under the **Projet de Gouvernance Numérique du Secteur Public (PGNSP)** — i.e. World Bank **P174620**. WB represented by Innocent Nukuri and resident rep Guido Rurangwa; PGNSP coordinator Enock Gotias.
- **Coverage:** the identification ecosystem in two axes — civil registration (vital-events recording, birth declarations) and identity documents (CNI for citizens, resident card for foreigners, refugee card). Serves as a high-level roadmap to sequence a move to an inclusive, interoperable identification system, aligned to the ID4D Principles and the recently validated Politique nationale de l'état civil.
- **Recommendations** span inclusive registration access, procedural harmonisation, coverage mapping, mass regularisation campaigns, legal-framework harmonisation, digitisation of état civil, archiving, statistics; and for CNI, more enrolment centres, decentralised adjudication, service standards.
- **Corroboration:** Corbeau News (2025-04-29) independently reports the **Council of Ministers of 17 April 2025** noting the validation of this World-Bank-backed diagnostic.

**Downstream, not in the wiki:** the **Système national d'identité numérique (SNID)** was launched **28 November 2025** — $13m, a unique identification number (NUI), a new **Office national d'identification (ONI)**, implementation **2027–2032**, building on a **2021 AfDB-commissioned, EU-co-financed feasibility study** (source: Ambition Journal). This is the diagnostic's successor step. Flagged for acquisitions.
