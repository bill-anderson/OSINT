# Zambia digital ID target: end-2026 vs end-2027

## Verdict: RESOLVED — not a contradiction but two different deliverables owned by two different bodies; and *neither* date is the funded programme's completion target, which the World Bank sets at **4 million people with digitally verifiable ID by October 2030**.

## Reading

The two statements are not competing estimates of one milestone. They describe different
things, at different scopes, from the two agencies that respectively **issue** the credential and
**build** the platform.

**Ownership is documented and unambiguous.** The World Bank's own project documents state that
"The Ministry of Home Affairs and Internal Security (MoHAIS), through its Department of National
Registration, Passport, and Citizenship (DNRPC), is legally mandated to issue National Registration
Cards (NRCs) and collect biometric data" (PID, 2025-01-20). SMART Zambia Institute (SZI) is named
as the **implementing agency** for the Digital Zambia Acceleration Project (DZAP) — it procures and
builds the system, it does not issue the credential. So **DNRPC owns the issuance milestone; SZI owns
the system milestone.** Chikalanga is speaking about his own department's deliverable; Chinyama is
speaking about the platform his agency is funding and procuring. That the operating registrar is the
more pessimistic is exactly what one would expect, and is a signal rather than a discrepancy.

**Neither date is the programme's end target.** The DZAP Project Appraisal Document (approved
2025-09-11) gives an **Expected Closing Date of 31-Oct-2030**, and both PAD and PID carry the same
results framework: "People with digitally verifiable identification (number) — Baseline (Dec 2022)
1,500,000 → End Target (Oct 2030) 4,000,000." DZAP's own project site states "The initial goal is to
provide 2 million individuals with digital IDs." Against a ~20 million population and ~10 million
eligible adults, **2 million by end-2026 and 4 million by 2030 are the funded numbers** — so
Chinyama's "Zambia will have digital identification cards by the end of 2026" is a **first-issuance /
initial-rollout milestone, not population coverage**, and Chikalanga's end-2027 "for the population"
is a coverage aspiration that the financing instrument does not underwrite either.

**The elections compress the 2026 window to roughly four months.** Zambia's general election is
**13 August 2026** (ECZ, as of 2026-07-18). ZNBC (2025-12-28) reported that procurement of smart-card
printers and blank cards would enable issuance of secure biometric NRCs **after the 2026 General
Elections** — i.e. any 2026 issuance falls in an August–December window. This is the ordinary
Zambian pattern of holding ID issuance away from an election period (the same sensitivity that drew
rigging accusations in 2024), and the DPI-exemplar material records "election sensitivities" as a
standing constraint on INRIS since 2008.

**Procurement timing makes end-2026 nationwide issuance implausible on its face.** The
system-integrator RFP (ZM-SZ-505117-GO-RFP) was issued 2025-12-02 with a 2026-01-22 deadline, then
extended by corrigenda to 2026-02-05 and 2026-02-27. As of **2026-03-22** ZNBC reported SZI had only
"begun the process of engaging an international partner." No contract award has been reported as of
2026-07-20. A MOSIP integrator not yet under contract by Q2-2026, with an election recess in Q3,
cannot deliver nationwide card issuance by December 2026.

**Neither date has formally moved, but the language has softened.** As late as **2026-06-08**
Biometric Update reported Zambia "hopes to **begin** full issuance of its national digital ID by the
end of this year" — a retreat from December's "Zambia **will have** digital identification cards."
No official deferral has been announced; no official reaffirmation of end-2027 has appeared since
June 2025 either.

**One genuine open question remains: one system or two?** DZAP funds a "homegrown foundational ID
system" on MOSIP, integrated with the civil-registration architecture, while INRIS (the legacy
Dermalog-built system, Japan/UNDP-funded, 1.5m biometric enrolments) continues. UNDP describes a
transition "from a legacy National [system] where both systems need to run simultaneously." Whether
the biometric NRC issued by DNRPC and the MOSIP digital ID delivered by SZI are one credential or
two is **not settled by the material found** — see Flags below.
