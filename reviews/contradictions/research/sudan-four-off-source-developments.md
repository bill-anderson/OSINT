# Sudan: the four off-source developments — all four established

## 1. Instant-payment tender and the national payment switch / EBS

**The tender.** Central Bank of Sudan issued a **public tender for the National Instant Payment System
(NIPS)**, announcement period **20 April – 4 May 2026**, bids to **14 May 2026**. Scope is a **software
solution only** — CBoS supplies the hardware to the winner's specification. Bids to the General
Administration of Payment Systems, CBoS Omdurman branch. CBoS is not obliged to accept the lowest bid.
- English: https://cbos.gov.sd/en/content/tender-announcement-national-instant-payment-system-project-nips
- Arabic: https://cbos.gov.sd/ar/node/24595
- Ministry-adjacent Arabic write-up: https://alnawrs.com/209567 (2026-04-30)
- Clipped to `new/2026-04-20-cbos-nips-national-instant-payment-system-tender.md`.

**The switch and EBS.** Interview with **Saif El-Din Hassan Ahmed Bari, CEO of Electronic Banking
Services (EBS)**, Sudanhorizon, **2026-07-08**:
https://sudanhorizon.com/ebs-ceo-to-sudanhorizon-new-national-payment-switch-launching-soon-market-remains-open-to-private-sector/
- A **new national payment switch has been contracted** and is "expected to be launched soon" — as of
  2026-07-08 it is **not yet live**.
- CBoS has entrusted EBS with the **technical operation** of the National Payment Switch: EBS is the
  "switch of switches" connecting bank- and fintech-operated switches under central-bank supervision.
- Bari's framing: "EBS does not monopolize the market—it organizes it"; linking private switches to EBS
  keeps **data sovereignty within Sudan's borders**.
- EBS was **accredited as an authorised SWIFT service bureau** in June 2026, and says it has processed
  **more than 234 million account-to-account transfers** between commercial banks since resuming
  operations in 2025.
- Ownership (from ISTinaD/Atar, 2026-03-02): EBS is CBoS 49%, Sudatel 30%, commercial banks 21%; created
  1999. It lost access to critical systems after April 2023 and clearing-services oversight remained
  unresolved; CBoS has since assumed direct monitoring/auditing of national switch transactions while EBS
  runs operations. https://atarnetwork.com/?p=22328

**Analytical note.** This directly bears on the wiki's concentration-of-payments thesis, and complicates
it: the design is *deliberately* a sovereign hub plus competing private spokes, not a single-provider
monopoly. The Al-Asjad episode (below) is the same story's other half.

## 2. Revocation of Al-Asjad's licence

**Established, with the central bank's own statement quoted.** Sequence:
- **~2026-07-01 (Wednesday)** — **شركة العسجد للحلول الذكية والرقمية** (Al-Asjad Smart and Digital
  Solutions Ltd) launched its digital platform in **Port Sudan** at a high-profile ceremony attended by
  Lt-Gen **Mahjoub Bushra** representing Sovereignty Council member Lt-Gen **Ibrahim Jaber**, plus CBoS
  representatives, the head of the Banks' Union and bank managers. The platform was pitched as unifying
  Sudanese banks on one central platform: instant transfers, retail payments, bill and government
  payments, card management.
- **2026-07-03 (Friday)** — CBoS ordered all banks and licensed fintechs **not to enter any contractual,
  operational or technical relationship, or perform any linkage or data exchange, without its prior
  written approval**, financial switch operators included.
- **2026-07-04 (Saturday)** — CBoS **revoked** Al-Asjad's authorisation, on the recommendation of a
  **technical and legal committee** formed by the CBoS Governor (**Dr Amna Mirghani**), after "a
  comprehensive review of the company's position against the announced technical, financial and
  supervisory standards." CBoS did **not** disclose the specific breaches. It said arrangements were made
  to protect customers' rights.
- **Grounds stated vs. controversy reported.** The stated grounds are non-compliance with published
  standards. Separately, and **not confirmed by CBoS**, Sudanese outlets reported the controversy that
  preceded the decision: the company was **founded only in late 2025** and began operating in early 2026,
  yet obtained a licence within months; and journalist **Azmi Abdelrazig** reported it holds a **Dubai
  branch** intended as a MENA regional hub for digital-identity and cloud services. Treat the UAE tie as
  **reported allegation, not established fact**.
- **2026-07-13** — the Ministry of Finance terminated a separate contract with **شركة ساعد لوجستك
  للتجارة**, linked to businessman **Asjad Yahya**, over food-basket procurement under the "إيد على إيد"
  initiative. https://www.sudanakhbar.com/1811922

## 3. Baladna platform — 28 digital services

**Established.** Minister of Digital Transformation and Communications **Dr Ahmed Dirdiri (Ghandour)**
announced the launch of **28 digital services** under the **Baladna (بلدنا)** national platform, at a
press conference in **Khartoum** convened by Cabinet Affairs Minister **Dr Limiaa Abdelghaffar**.
- **Event date 2026-01-31**; reported 2026-01-31 to 2026-02-03. The official spokesperson platform
  published 2026-02-01 and says the conference was held "أمس" (yesterday).
- Also announced at the same conference: a **National Data Centre in Port Sudan** as an alternate site;
  **60 4G towers** built with **200 more** planned during 2026; and that a **cybersecurity and network
  security law had been passed** (text not located).
- Baladna's own site (https://baldna.gov.sd/) describes government-to-government data-exchange services,
  private-sector services and citizen services. By **2026-04-06** the minister said Baladna links
  **55 government institutions**, with a Khartoum State rollout starting in Karari locality and Gadarif
  next. https://sudafax.com/547338/

## 4. Khartoum data centre reopening

**Established — and it is a *state* data centre, not a commercial one.** Minister **Ahmed Dirdiri**
announced, in a press statement to **SUNA** reported **2025-10-10**, the **restoration and operation of
the State Data Centre in Khartoum State**, **1,300 m²**, after completion of technical rehabilitation.
Equipped with cloud computing, data security and AI-application support, qualifying it to host government
systems and services centrally. https://maasader.com/إستعادة-وتشغيل-مركز-بيانات-الدولة-في-الخرطوم
Clipped to `new/2025-10-10-khartoum-state-data-centre-restored-and-operational.md`.

**Related context, distinct facts — do not conflate:**
- **Sudatel's data centre**, southwest Khartoum, was **seized by the RSF** in December 2023. An IT expert
  told Radio Dabanga that ~75% of public- and private-sector institutions subscribed to it, including the
  Interior Ministry, the civil registry and most banks. https://www.sudanakhbar.com/1460270 (2023-12-07)
- The **alternate National Data Centre in Port Sudan** is a *different, later, still-planned* project —
  described by the minister on **2026-03-15** to the Red Sea State governor as among the largest technical
  infrastructure investments under way, framed as restoring sovereign national infrastructure.
  https://nabdsudan.net/231651

So the chronology is: Sudatel's Khartoum facility falls to the RSF (Dec 2023) → the *state* data centre in
Khartoum is rehabilitated and brought back into operation (Oct 2025) → a Port Sudan alternate national
data centre is planned (announced Jan–Mar 2026, not yet built).
