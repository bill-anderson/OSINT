# African tech equity funding CY2025 — +8% or −21%?

## Verdict: RESOLVED. Not a dispute, and **not a rebase**. The sign flips because the two publishers
## sit on **different 2024 bases**, and neither has revised anything.

The brief's hypothesis — that a silent prior-year revision would explain the flip — is **disconfirmed**.
Both 2024 figures are as originally published and unchanged. The flip is arithmetic on two different
universes.

## The two series, side by side

| | 2024 equity | 2025 equity | Change | 2025 equity deals |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Partech** | **US$2.2bn** (published 2025-01-23, unchanged) | **US$2.41bn** | **+8%** | 462 |
| **AVCA** | **≈US$2.7bn** (derived — see below) | **US$2.1bn** | **−21%** | 432 |

**Partech's 2024 base is directly sourced.** The *2024 Partech Africa Tech VC Report* (published
2025-01-23) states equity funding of **US$2.2B, −2% YoY** across **457 equity deals (−3%)**, within a
US$3.2B equity-plus-debt total (−7%) across 534 deals. Partech's own release and multiple contemporaneous
re-reports (Businessday NG, Africa Global Funds, Connecting Africa, BitKE) agree. Nothing has been revised.

**AVCA's 2024 base is not stated in the 2025 report and must be derived** — a finding in itself. From the
report's own numbers: 2024 total venture value **US$3.6bn** (Figure 4); 2025 debt **US$1.8bn** at
**+91% YoY** ⇒ 2024 debt ≈ **US$0.94bn**; therefore 2024 equity ≈ **US$2.66bn**. Cross-check: 2025 equity
US$2.1bn at −21% ⇒ base **US$2.66bn**. The two derivations agree, so the derived base is sound. AVCA
reports the −21% **without printing the base it is taken from** — which is precisely why the figure should
never travel alone.

**The gap that flips the sign is in 2024, not 2025.** The two 2025 figures differ by ~US$0.3bn (2.41 vs
2.1 — ordinary tracker variance). The two 2024 figures differ by ~**US$0.46bn** (2.2 vs ≈2.66). That
larger, earlier gap is what makes one series rise while the other falls.

## Definitions — what each counts

**Partech** (methodology stable since its first report, restated in the 2020, 2021 and 2023 editions):
- **Scope:** "tech & digital VC equity and debt deals **above $200K**, in African start-ups". Late Seed
  (Seed+) through Growth. Angel and sub-US$200K seed rounds **deliberately excluded**.
- **Instrument:** equity numbers are equity only — excludes grants, awards, prizes, conventional debt,
  venture debt, loans, ICOs, non-equity/technical assistance, post-IPO and M&A. Debt is reported as a
  **separate subtotal**, not blended into the equity line.
- **Sector test:** tech & digital only — "companies where the value is built around digital technology".
  Partech's own worked example excludes NextProtein (insect-based feed) despite an US$11.2m Series A.
- **Geography test:** **primary market by operations and/or revenues in Africa — explicitly *not* HQ or
  incorporation.** A company that goes global stays African (its example: Gro Intelligence). Conversely a
  Seychelles-HQ'd company whose business is not African is excluded (its example: Bitget).
- **Disclosure:** the report covers "**fully disclosed, partially disclosed and confidential deals**" — so
  Partech carries value for rounds whose amounts were never announced.

**AVCA:** **the methodology page could not be read.** It sits at **p. 41** of the report PDF and the
retrieval truncated before it. What the retrieved body does establish:
- Equity and debt are **reported separately and explicitly**: "these figures reflect venture capital
  (equity) activity only and exclude venture debt". The US$2.1bn is unambiguously equity.
- Stage range runs **Seed → Early → Late**, plus venture debt tracked separately since 2022.
- **No deal-size floor is stated** anywhere in the retrieved text — and AVCA's Seed median of US$2.2m
  against Partech's US$200K floor suggests any floor is low or absent. An absent floor would pull AVCA's
  count *up*, not down, so it does not explain AVCA's lower 2025 figure.
- The report has a distinct section **"5.2 Technology Penetration in African Venture Capital"** — which
  implies **tech is a subset of AVCA's universe, not the whole of it.** AVCA counts African *venture
  capital*; Partech counts African *tech & digital* venture capital. AVCA's universe should therefore be
  the **wider** one.

## The residual anomaly, stated honestly

If AVCA's universe is wider (all venture, no evident floor) and Partech's narrower (tech-only, ≥US$200K),
AVCA should exceed Partech **in both years**. It does in 2024 (2.66 vs 2.2) but **not in 2025** (2.1 vs
2.41). Something moved between the two years that scope alone does not explain.

**The best-evidenced candidate is the treatment of undisclosed rounds.** Partech explicitly imputes value
for confidential deals; nothing in AVCA's retrieved text says it does. And AVCA itself flagged the
phenomenon mid-year — its *Q3 2025 Venture Capital Activity in Africa* note reads: "Deal value fell to
US$0.2bn in Q3, but **a surge of undisclosed deals suggests capital is still flowing albeit less
transparently**." A year with unusually many undisclosed rounds would depress a disclosed-only series
relative to an imputing one, in exactly the direction observed.

**This is a hypothesis consistent with the evidence, not an established fact.** Confirming it requires
AVCA's methodology page (p. 41). It is flagged as such on the wiki pages and routed to acquisitions.

## Secondary series named in the brief

- **AVCA US$3.9bn / 506 deals** — this is AVCA's **equity + debt** total (2.1 + 1.8), directly stated. It
  is the AVCA analogue of Partech's US$4.1bn/570, not a rival equity series. Ecofin confirms the 2024
  comparator total as US$3.6bn.
- **TechCabal Insights US$3.42bn / 502 deals** — **basis not established in this pass.** No TechCabal
  methodology statement was surfaced. It remains an unbased number and should not be cited.
- **Disrupt Africa US$1.64bn / 178** — already resolved on
  `wiki/entities/disrupt-africa.md`: a self-declared minimum floor, by funded venture.

## Prior-year revision check — negative

- **Partech:** 2024 equity US$2.2bn as published 2025-01-23; the 2025 report's +8% to US$2.41bn is
  consistent with that base. No revision.
- **AVCA:** its *2024* report headlined "a 22% drop in deal value and a 28% decline in deal volume" for
  2024. A 2024 equity of ≈US$2.66bn at −22% implies a 2023 equity of ≈US$3.4bn, which is internally
  coherent. No revision detected.

Contrast with the DataReportal case found elsewhere in this corpus this month, which **was** a genuine
silent rebase. This is not one.

## Which headline is more defensible

**Neither, alone.** Both are correct within their own definitions and both are misleading stripped of
their base. Concretely:

- **Partech** is the better series for **African tech & digital venture specifically**, because it states
  its floor, its sector test and its geography test; separates equity from debt; and covers confidential
  rounds. Its weakness is that imputed confidential value is unauditable.
- **AVCA** is the better series for **African venture as an asset class** — it carries exits, fundraising,
  LP composition and investor location, which Partech does not. Its weakness here is acute: **it publishes
  a −21% without publishing the base**, and its methodology page was not readable.

The defensible statement is directional-with-attribution, never a bare figure: *Partech records African
tech equity up 8% to US$2.41bn in CY2025 against its own US$2.2bn 2024 base; AVCA records African venture
equity down 21% to US$2.1bn against a base it does not print (≈US$2.7bn derived). The series are not
comparable and the divergence is in the 2024 denominators.*
