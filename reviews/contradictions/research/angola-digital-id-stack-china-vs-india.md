# Angola's identity stack: China vs India — RESOLVED, not a contradiction

**Verdict: a division of labour across time, not competing accounts.** One is an executed,
operational vendor contract; the other is an exploratory diplomatic pledge with no contract behind it.

## Claim A — the Chinese stack. Confirmed, and re-dated.

- **2017-01-03** — *Despacho Presidencial nº 8/17*, signed by **José Eduardo dos Santos**, selects
  **CEIEC** (China National Electronics Import & Export Corporation) for a **$243m** contract for
  "fornecimento de bens, instalações e suporte técnico" to implement the **Plataforma de Gestão
  Integrada da Identificação Civil e Criminal**, contracted by **MINJUSDH**. Financed on a Chinese
  commercial credit line. (Lusa via Novo Jornal / Rede Angola / VOA Português, 2017-01-16/17.)
- **Late 2017 → Jan 2018** — new BI begins issuance, rolled out gradually across posts
  (Valor Económico, citing Despacho 8/17).
- **2018-06** — construction of the production centre begins (ANGOP/einpresswire).
- **2019-11-08** — **João Lourenço inaugurates** the *Centro Nacional de Produção e Controlo do
  Bilhete de Identidade*, Kilamba, Luanda: 12,000 m², 16 production lines, ~20,000 IDs/day,
  real-time links to all 18 provinces. Built by CEIEC. Minister **Francisco Queiroz**.
- Implementation partner **UNIPRIME** (Chinese) documents the same programme: a unified national
  demographic information centre and BI/civil-registry sharing platform for MINJUSDH, supplying
  data to MININT, AGT, Ministério da Terra, GUE and INSS; claims an **80% cut** in ID-system
  operating cost and inter-ministerial voter-record interoperability.

**→ The wiki's "CEIEC won the contract Nov 2019" is wrong.** Nov 2019 is the *centre's
inauguration*. The contract is **January 2017**. Classic event-date/publication-date conflation.

## Claim B — the Indian stack. Does not exist as a contract.

Primary: **Modi's own remarks**, joint press statement with Lourenço, New Delhi, **2025-05-03**
(pmindia.gov.in). Two *separate* sentences:

> "I am pleased to announce the approval of a **$200 million defense credit line** for the
> modernization of Angola's armed forces."

> "Taking our development partnership forward, we will **share our capabilities** with Angola in
> **Digital Public Infrastructure**, Space Technology, and Capacity Building."

MEA Secretary (ER) **Dammu Ravi**, same-day briefing: DPI "is another area that was discussed and
again there seems to be great promise there and **for Angola admittedly it is a new area**. But
Honourable Prime Minister explained the benefits of it… and **we will see how our exports can take
this forward**." Also: "many of the African countries are still **new to this**… there is this need
for **awareness building**… **there's a lot of work ahead**."

**→ Three wiki errors, all traceable to one Biometric Update headline (2025-05-07):**
1. The **$200m credit line is for defence**, not DPI. Biometric Update's "India helps Angola with
   DPI, digital ID as part of $200M credit line" welds them; neither Modi nor the MEA does.
2. **No Aadhaar/MOSIP deployment for Angola exists.** Biometric Update mentions MOSIP only as
   *context* about Morocco, Ethiopia, Sierra Leone and Guinea — not Angola.
3. **Nothing is contracted.** No vendor, no figure, no signed instrument, no timetable.

Corroborating: Angop (**2025-11-09**) lists what was actually signed in May 2025 — MoUs on
traditional medicine, agriculture and culture, plus the ISA framework accession and the $200m
**defence** credit line. No DPI instrument.

## The synthesis

Angola's **operational** civil-registration and ID-issuance stack is **Chinese-built (CEIEC),
contracted 2017 and running since 2019**. India's 2025 involvement is a **government-to-government
expression of interest** at the awareness-building stage. There is **no hand-off and no conflict** —
the two accounts sit eight years and one order of commitment apart. The wiki's "three-way
sovereignty contest" framing over-reads a diplomatic pledge into a procurement.
