# Chad's digital law: not enacted. The October 2025 ordinance: identified, and narrower than the wiki says.

## Q1 — Has the "digital law" (Code du numérique) been enacted? No.

As of the latest reporting found (2026-04-28), it remains **at drafting stage**:

- **2025-12-09** — Boukar Michel announces a **Code du numérique** in preparation.
- **2026-04-27** — successor Haliki Choua Mahamat installs a **34-member committee** in N'Djamena
  with a **45-day deadline** to propose modernised texts covering cybersecurity, personal-data
  protection, internet governance and AI. Deadline therefore ~**11 June 2026**.
- **No adopted text, number, promulgation date or gazette reference exists** — because the instrument
  does not yet exist. This is a **known vacuum**, not a provenance gap.

Chad still has **no standalone data-protection law**; the function is being absorbed into ANSICE
(see below), which the wiki already records.

## Q2 — The October 2025 ordinance: fully identified

**Ordonnance n°011/PR/2025 du 5 août 2025**, amending **Article 18** of the **Loi n°013/PR/2014 du
21 mars 2014** on the regulation of electronic communications and postal activities.

Ratification passage:
- **National Assembly, 22 October 2025** — adopted **105 for, 31 against, 2 abstentions**. Presided
  by Speaker Ali Kolotou Tchaïmi; defended by Boukar Michel.
- **Senate, 23 December 2025** — adopted **65 of 67** senators.

**Scope correction — this matters.** The wiki (TCD hub and `chad--gov-protect`) presents this as a
broad modernisation of the telecom code. The ordinance amends **a single article (18)** and its
actual subject is the **conditions for awarding licences to mobile operators**. The
"modernise/competition/sovereignty" language is the *bill's stated rationale* as relayed by trade
press, not the amendment's content. Deputies' concerns in debate were about monopoly, licence
sales, corruption and non-respect of the cahier des charges.

**Bonus finding from the same debate:** Boukar Michel stated on the record (22 Oct 2025) that
**Starlink's Chadian concession is a multiservice ISP (FAI) licence, not a mobile-operator licence**,
issued on application after favourable ARCEP technical opinion and **without public tender**. He also
said market conditions did not permit a new ISP entrant and that past calls for candidates had failed.
This is directly relevant to `chad--infra-connect`.

## Law-number discrepancy (settled)

Trade press splits between **Loi n°014/PR/2014** (Tchadinfos, Toumaï Web Médias) and **Loi
n°013/PR/2014** (Afrique IT News, Walaw, and the wiki's held source). The **text of the law itself**,
published at africa-laws.org, reads **"Loi N° 013/2014"** — so **013/PR/2014 is correct** and the
wiki already has it right. Not opened as a new contradiction: the primary settles it.

## Adjacent instruments surfaced

- **Ordonnance n°001/PR/2026 du 22 janvier 2026** — ANSICE reform. Senate ratified **13 March 2026**,
  **58 for, 3 against, 0 abstentions**. Presented by Minister of Public Security **Ali Ahmat
  Akhabache** (not the telecoms minister). Already held by the wiki in substance.
- **Loi on biometric data in judicial procedures** — adopted by the National Assembly **10 April
  2026**, **138 for, 1 against**. Covers collection, processing, retention, use and destruction of
  biometric data in judicial and judicial-police procedures. **New to the wiki** — directly bears on
  `chad--dpi-id` and `chad--gov-protect`. Routed to acquisitions for a proper clip.
