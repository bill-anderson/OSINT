## Verdict: RESOLVED — no genuine contradiction. Both accounts hold: "all 116 local authorities connected to GWAN" is a *national, council-premises* count (phase 1, confirmed 100% complete by SMART Zambia's own Chief Technical Officer as of 2026-03-25); the July 2026 North-Western item is a *provincial, later-phase* extension to a wider institution set (post offices, schools, health facilities) over fibre — not a first connection of councils.

### What the record shows

The 116 figure originates with SMART Zambia Institute (SZI) itself, not with a third-party summary. SZI's
e-Government Division announced "full deployment of the Government Wide Area Network (GWAN) across all 116
local authorities" as the first of six milestones on **2025-09-08**. The wearetech.africa piece of
2026-07-17 is a late, accurate paraphrase of that SZI announcement — it introduces no independent claim.

The figure was then **restated by SZI at a media briefing on 2026-03-24** (reported 2026-03-25), and this
is the decisive evidence. Government Chief Technical Officer **Kasali Musenge** said: *"All 116 councils
have been connected to the government network, which means **phase 1 is completed 100 percent**. On top of
that, we have provided solar power to all the councils."* National Coordinator **Percy Chinyama** at the
same briefing listed "connecting all 116 local authorities to the government network" among four years'
(2021–2025) progress. The explicit **"phase 1"** framing is what dissolves the apparent conflict: SZI
treats connecting the 116 council premises as a completed *first* phase, with subsequent phases widening
both the institution set and the bearer technology.

The **2026-07-07** statement (Chinyama, live on Mwinilunga Radio; reported 2026-07-08) is a phase-2-type
milestone and is **not scoped to local authorities alone**. Read precisely, it says all local authorities
**and post offices** in North-Western Province are now connected via **fibre-optic** infrastructure, with
the network linking "government offices, schools and health facilities". Three things differ from the 116
claim: it is one province, not the nation; it covers institutions well beyond councils; and it specifies
fibre. Presenting it as a milestone is consistent — the new content is post offices/schools/health
facilities on fibre, not councils.

### Caveat on what "connected" has meant

There is a real, separate softness in the word — though it is not the disputed conflict. ITWeb Africa
(2025-02-12) reported SZI's Western Province principal ICT officer Enoch Moonde describing GWAN works as
"completed" at 12 named sites **while last-mile connections via ZAMTEL were still outstanding**: *"We are
working closely with ZAMTEL and other stakeholders to complete the last-mile connections, which will
enable us to fully utilize the GWAN network."* So SZI's "completed"/"connected" has at least sometimes
meant **site works delivered**, not **service live end-to-end**. The 116 count should therefore be read as
*network reach established to council premises*, not as an assertion that every council has a
fully-utilised live service. Rollout-stage figures elsewhere are consistent with a build of this shape
(GWAN extended to 88 locations across all ten provinces; a 2024 programme targeting 182 sites).

GWAN sits within the World Bank-funded **Digital Zambia Acceleration Project (DZAP)**.

### Residual uncertainty

No official statement gives a post-March-2026 national figure, and none disaggregates the 116 by
"link established" vs "service live". SZI has not published a council-by-council status list. The 116
figure should be cited as SZI's own claim with its phase-1 meaning, not as independently verified
end-to-end service.
