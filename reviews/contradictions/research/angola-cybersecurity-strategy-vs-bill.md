## Verdict: RESOLVED — not rival structures. The executive track was enacted first and stood up the bodies administratively (Dec 2025); the legislative track is a separate, still-unpassed statute meant to give the same architecture a binding legal regime, suspended in committee since 2026-07-07.

Both claims in the brief are true, and they are sequential rather than competing. TechAfrica News (2025-12-09) reported the executive track at a moment when it had *already been enacted*, not while still in draft — its "draft Presidential Decree" framing is wrong. The decree was in fact published in the **Diário da República, Iª Série n.º 227, of 3 December 2025** and entered into force on publication.

The executive track is not one decree but three, all approved in the same Council of Ministers session of **27 October 2025**:

- **Decreto Presidencial n.º 256/25, 3 Dec 2025** — approves the **Estratégia Nacional de Cibersegurança** (ENCS). Signed 26 Nov 2025, in force from publication.
- **Decreto Presidencial n.º 258/25, 3 Dec 2025** — **creates the Conselho Nacional de Cibersegurança** and approves its Regimento. A 21-member *consultative* body chaired by the President.
- **Decreto Presidencial n.º 263/25, 10 Dec 2025** (Diário da República Iª Série n.º 232) — **creates the Centro Nacional de Cibersegurança** and approves its Estatuto Orgânico. A public institute with legal personality and administrative, financial and patrimonial autonomy, under MINTTICS superintendence.

The relationship is stated inside the primaries themselves and is the key to the reconciliation. The ENCS text (Objectivo Estratégico I, §7.1.1 b/c/d) lists *creating* the Conselho, the Centro and the CERT.ao/CSIRTs as **strategic actions** — so the decree track is self-consciously the instrument that stands the bodies up. Separately, ENCS **Objectivo Estratégico IV** ("consolidar a legislação nacional de cibersegurança") commits to **amending Lei n.º 7/17 (Protecção das Redes e Sistemas Informáticos)** via a two-phase legislative reform in which phase 1 is passage of laws **by the legislature**. The cybersecurity bill *is* that phase-1 action. The ENCS also refers forward to a "**Lei da Cibersegurança**" as a document that does not yet exist (Section VI: "Sem prejuízo do disposto na Lei da Cibersegurança…"), and Decreto 258/25 Art. 3(f) and Art. 20 both presuppose a Centro Nacional with regulatory and budgetary functions that the decree alone does not confer.

So: the decrees created the **policy architecture** (strategy + consultative council + operational institute); the bill would supply the **binding legal regime** — obligations on operators of critical infrastructure and essential services, incident-notification duties, supervision, and **sanctions** — which is precisely what OPaís (2026-01-26) identifies as the gap in the existing Lei 7/17. MPLA deputy Nazário Vilhena's line at the general-approval debate is the clearest statement of intent: with this law Angola "deixa de agir aos pedaços e passa a ter um sistema nacional de cibersegurança, um conselho nacional de cibersegurança e um centro nacional de cibersegurança, **com poder de coordenação, supervisão e quando necessário fiscalização e sanções**." The bill therefore *re-founds on statute* bodies that already exist by decree, adding enforcement powers — it does not create rivals to them.

Bill status, as of **2026-07-07**: approved *na generalidade* on **2026-01-23** (105 for, 1 against, 75 UNITA/PRS abstentions); committee-stage (*especialidade*) discussion opened on 2026-07-07 and was **suspended the same day** by unanimous agreement of the Executive and the specialised committees, over the preamble and Chapters I–II. UNITA warned that the redrafting risks producing an entirely new bill, which would require restarting the legislative process. No resumption date was set, and searches to 2026-07-20 surface no later development.

One dating caution: the bill was **presented to public consultation by MINTTICS in February 2025**, appraised by the Council of Ministers on **2025-10-27** (same session as the three decrees), and voted in the Assembleia Nacional in January 2026. The decrees and the bill are thus a **single 2025 policy package** that split into two tracks at the Council of Ministers, not two independent initiatives.
