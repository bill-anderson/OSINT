## Verdict: RESOLVED — not a contradiction. £50m is the fund's *target* total commitments; £10m is anchor-committed capital; £5m is the Phase One / first-close deployment. Domicile is Guernsey; Botswana is the operational base.

The two "competing values" are three different bases for the same fund, reported at the same
moment, and every account is internally consistent once the basis is attached. The defect is in the
promotional coverage, not in the underlying facts: the press-release-derived headlines convert a
**target** into an apparently-raised amount ("Debuts $67.5 Million Vehicle", "Botswana raises $64
million"), which is what made the bylined TechCabal figures look contradictory.

The fund's own launch release (botswanatech.com, dateline Gaborone, 21 April 2026) is explicit and
settles the target: "its launch as a **targeted** £50 million multi-stage venture capital fund **with a
first close in Q2/2026**." It never claims £50m is raised. Trade reporting on the same day (Africa
Private Equity News, 21 Apr) reads it the same way — "The fund **targets** £50 million in total
commitments. Phase one will deploy up to £5 million: £1 million across a pre-seed cohort, and two to
four growth-stage investments." Launch Base Africa (21 Apr) states the residual plainly: "The real
question is whether Phase One generates the performance data — and the LP relationships — needed to
raise the remaining **£45 million** into subsequent phases."

The committed and first-close figures come from Managing Partner Martin Davis on the record to
TechCabal (27 Apr), in his own words: "We have an anchor investor that has committed £10 million
($13.5 million) to the fund. The first £5 million ($6.7 million) is now, and that's what we're
deploying over the next six months… The first close is £5 million ($6.78 million), just to get us
going. The commitment is £10 million ($13.5 million)." So as of late April 2026 the fund had **£10m
committed against a £50m target**, i.e. roughly one-fifth of target, with £5m at first close. The
$67.5m / $64m figures are simply £50m converted at different rates — not a separate claim.

**Domicile.** Also not a conflict, but a conflation of two facts. Davis to TechCabal: "The fund is
**domiciled in Guernsey**, but we have chosen **Botswana as our operational base**." The fund's own
release corroborates the Botswana half without naming a domicile — "Botswana serves as BTF's
operational and strategic hub" — and independent reporting consistently describes anchor investor
Pula Investments as "the **Guernsey-based** family office of Stephen Lansdown CBE." The
"Gaborone-based" descriptor in the promotional coverage is therefore accurate as to *operations* and
silent (not wrong) as to *domicile*.

**Two honest residuals.** (1) No entry for the fund was located on the Guernsey Financial Services
Commission register; the Guernsey domicile rests on Davis's on-record statement plus Pula
Investments' Guernsey base, not on a verified regulatory filing. (2) The fund's own site footer states
"**Botswana Tech Ltd (FRN 1057855) is an Appointed Representative of Thema Capital Limited (FRN
978496), a firm authorised and regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA)**" — a *UK*
regulatory wrapper. UK-FCA-regulated marketing/advisory over a Guernsey-domiciled vehicle with a
Botswana operating base is an ordinary structure, but the wiki should not state the domicile as
register-verified.

**Admissibility note.** wearetech.africa, techafricanews, Business Insider Africa, Disrupt Africa,
Empower Africa, Innovation Village, TTYBrand, Nigeriabrief, Villpress, African-Startups and Techarena
are all recognisably rewrites of the same launch release, several reproducing its phrasing verbatim.
Under the wiki's paid-placement / self-marketing rule they are **not admitted as separate sources**;
the fund's own release is the admissible first-hand company statement behind them, and TechCabal's
bylined interview is the admissible independent reporting.
