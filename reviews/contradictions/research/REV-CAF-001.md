# REV-CAF-001 research — Al Madina (CAR ID printer): Lebanese or Omani?

## Verdict: both, at different levels — Omani corporate domicile, Lebanese ownership. Not two firms.

"Al Madina" / "Almadina" is **registered and headquartered in the Sultanate of Oman** (Muscat/Ruwi;
"Al Madina Development & Supply LLC" and its "Security Printing Press Oman LLC" division, founded 1984),
but is **Lebanese-owned and -run** — directed by Lebanese businessman **Aziz Nassour**, with associate
**Moustapha (Mustapha) Nassour**. The CAR/French press's "société **libanaise** Almadina" refers to the
**ownership/management nationality (Lebanese)**; Biometric Update's "**Oman**-headquartered" refers to the
**corporate registration/domicile**. Both are accurate; there is no conflation of two separate firms.

- As of the CNI contract's contested award, the company presented internationally as an **Oman-based**
  security-document firm with representations in Oman, Sudan, Somalia, Niger, Congo-Brazzaville, Dubai,
  Afghanistan and Mauritania (per associate Moustapha Nassour, CNC/abangui interview), while CAR political
  and civil-society actors consistently described it as **Lebanese** because of the **Nassour** family's
  control.
- **Aziz Nassour** is a controversial figure (RFI notes questions over his past) — relevant to the
  sovereignty/`colonialism` reads already on the page, but not needed to settle the home-country question.
