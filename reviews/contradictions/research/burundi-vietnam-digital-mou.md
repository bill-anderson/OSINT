# Burundi–Vietnam digital-cooperation MoU — research findings

## Verdict: the MoU exists and is fully datable

- **Signed:** **19 November 2024**
- **Where:** on the sidelines of **Vietnam International Digital Week 2024** (Hanoi, running to 22 Nov)
- **Parties:** **Vietnam's Ministry of Information and Communications** (Minister **Nguyen Manh Hung**)
  ↔ **Burundi's Ministry of Communication, Information Technologies and Media** (Minister
  **Léocadie Ndacayisaba**, leading the visiting delegation)
- **Subject:** cooperation on and support for **telecommunications technology development** — digital
  transformation, digital economy, workforce training

The IFC's undated passing reference ("around late 2024") was accurate.

## What it actually commits

Very little, and this should be stated plainly. The MoU is a cooperation-intent instrument. The
concrete items announced alongside it:

- **Scholarships** — Vietnamese MIC programme, up to **$10,000 each**, undergraduate/postgraduate, in
  telecoms, digital economy and digital transformation; plus short online courses tailored to Burundi.
- **Legislative/regulatory expertise sharing** in telecoms — which Burundi's delegation thanked
  Vietnam for as *already ongoing*, i.e. predating the MoU.

No financing, no infrastructure commitment, no project, no timetable.

## The real substance in the room is Lumitel, not the MoU

Both ministers spent most of the meeting on **Lumitel**, the Viettel-owned Burundian operator — already
the dominant-market-share incumbent, and stated to have paid **>$500 million in taxes** to Burundi
since establishment. Hung's asks were commercial and specific, and they were **not** in the MoU:
rural mobile-network expansion, **5G spectrum allocation**, extension of investment incentives, and a
telecom tax-reduction roadmap. Ndacayisaba's reciprocal ask was that Lumitel expand coverage and
improve service quality. Read honestly, the MoU is diplomatic packaging around an existing
Vietnamese commercial position in Burundi's telecom market.

An independent read (Extensia, 2024-11-20) reaches the same conclusion: *"For the time being, the
potential collaboration is only at the stage of exchanges and exploratory discussions."*

## Has anything followed?

**No digital-specific follow-through found.** The subsequent bilateral event is President
**Évariste Ndayishimiye's state visit to Hanoi, 4 April 2025** — the first ever at that level in 50
years of relations — at which **two instruments** were signed: a **General Cooperation Agreement** and
an **MoU on Political and Diplomatic Consultations**. ICT was named as one of five priority sectors
(alongside mining, trade, agriculture, tourism), with technical teams tasked to draft sector
agreements *later*. As of **2026-07-20 no such digital sector agreement has been reported**.

So: **signed 2024-11-19; ICT reaffirmed as a priority sector 2025-04-04; nothing concrete since.**
