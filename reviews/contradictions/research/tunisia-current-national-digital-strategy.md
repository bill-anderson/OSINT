# Research: which national digital strategy currently governs in Tunisia?

## Verdict

**There is no standalone successor document to "Digital Tunisia 2020-2025."** The current national digital
framing is the **unified national digital-transformation vision embedded in the *plan de développement
2026-2030*** — the whole-of-government five-year development plan, not a sectoral ICT strategy with its own
title. That is what the EU ambassador's phrase "the National Digital Strategy" (2026-01) points to.

## The chain of evidence

- **2025-06-20** — Communication Technologies Minister **Sofiene Hemissi** launches preparation of the
  **2026-2030 development plan** for the ICT sector, with working teams across ministries and the
  **Ministry of Economy and Planning as national coordinator**. Explicitly framed as evaluating current
  digital-economy projects to set directions for the plan.
  (Web Manager Center, https://www.webmanagercenter.com/2025/06/23/548064/strategie-digitale-2026-2030-vers-une-transformation-technologique-inclusive-en-tunisie/;
  Tunisie Tribune, 2025-06-23.)
- **2026-01-06** — Head of Government **Sarra Zaafrani Zenzri** chairs a ministerial council on the draft
  **plan de développement 2026-2030**, prepared for the first time by a **bottom-up** approach (local →
  regional → district → national). Digital transformation and AI named among its transformations. To go to
  the Council of Ministers. (Portail de la Présidence du Gouvernement, https://pm.gov.tn/en/node/2964.)
- **2026-02-13** — **The decisive statement.** At a ministerial council on administration digitalisation,
  Zenzri states that the State has equipped itself **"dans le cadre du plan de développement 2026-2030,
  d'une vision nationale unifiée pour la transformation digitale"** — a unified national vision defining
  objectives, priorities and a delivery timetable. 2026 declared the year of "effective start of complete
  digitalisation" of the administration; **192 projects under national tracking**.
  (pm.gov.tn — clipped as a primary.)
- **2026-05-25** — A **national AI strategy** strand: priority sectors (digital health, agriculture,
  energy, transport/smart mobility, digital administration); four axes (human capital, research and
  innovation, governance/regulatory frameworks, infrastructure); framed on **digital sovereignty** and
  data protection. (Web Manager Center.)
- **2026-07-11** — Hemissi presents this at the **WSIS Forum ministerial, Geneva**, describing "une vision
  nationale de la transition numérique **inscrite dans son plan de développement 2026-2030**", resting on
  digital infrastructure, generalised public-service digitalisation, and business-climate improvement; AI
  principles of human primacy, **national and data sovereignty**, and ethical/cultural values.
  (Le Temps, https://letemps.news/2026/07/11/la-tunisie-place-lintelligence-artificielle-et-la-souverainete-des-donnees-au-coeur-de-sa-strategie-numerique/.)

## What happened to Digital Tunisia 2020-2025

**It lapsed.** No source found reports a formal extension, a formal repeal, or a named replacement, and no
final-outcomes report was published. The ministry's own site (mtc.gov.tn) still carries a "Tunisie Digitale
2020" navigation item as of 2026-07 — a legacy artefact, not evidence of currency. The successor framing
arrives not as "Digital Tunisia 2026-2030" but as the **digital chapter of a general development plan**,
which is a real change in instrument type: from a sectoral ICT strategy owned by the communication-
technologies ministry to a whole-of-government plan coordinated by the Ministry of Economy and Planning.

## Caveat — what remains unheld

**The text of the 2026-2030 development plan itself was not located**, nor a standalone published national
digital-strategy or AI-strategy document. Everything above is official *statements about* the plan
(government portal, ministerial communiqués) rather than the plan document. Whether the plan had been
formally adopted by the Council of Ministers by 2026-07 is **not established** — as of 2026-01-06 it was
still a *projet* going to the Council. That is a live acquisition target, not a contradiction.
