## Verdict: RESOLVED — definitional, as suspected, but the decisive fact is that the "18 million" is NIRA's own figure, not an advocacy estimate: it is `total population − total register`, counted across **all ages**, and NIRA's Executive Director stated on the record that **most of those 18 million are children under 17**. Measured against adults, the exclusion rate is roughly 9%, not 40%.

### Short prose

The contradiction dissolves once the **denominator** is named. Both claims are arithmetically
correct and neither is about the same population.

**The 18 million figure originates inside NIRA, not with the advocacy author.** NIRA Executive
Director Rosemary Kisembo told Parliament's Committee on Commissions, Statutory Authorities and
State Enterprises (COSASE) on **2024-07-01**: *"Based on the current census figures of 45.9 million
people, we haven't registered about 18.1 million individuals. **Most of those unregistered are
children under 17 years old.** Since NIRA's establishment in 2015, it has registered 27.4 million
people with NINs and issued 17.3 million cards, although it has printed 20 million cards."* The
brief's framing of Claim A as "the author's own figure, uncited" is therefore **wrong on
provenance** — Michael Aboneka was repeating a government number. It is wrong on *framing*
instead: the figure is presented in advocacy contexts as an access barrier (implying excluded
adults) when NIRA's own gloss says it is mostly children below card-eligibility age.

**The arithmetic.** 45.9m (census, May 2024) − 27.4m (register, mid-2024) ≈ 18.5m, rounded to
18.1m on NIRA's own working. 18.1 ÷ 45.9 = **39.4%** — hence "about 40%". The denominator is the
**total population including infants**, not the ID-eligible population.

**The adult picture is entirely different.** NIRA's own press statement of **2024-10-15** breaks
the register down by age: *"There are 27,4 million registered persons in the National
Identification Register. The persons in age bracket 0-17 years are 6.3million representing
approximately 22.1% of persons registered."* That leaves roughly **21.1m adults (18+) on the
register**. The 2024 census counts **23,154,716 adults aged 18+**. The adult gap is therefore
about **2.0 million, ~9% of adults (as of 2024-10-15)** — a very long way from 40%, and broadly
consistent with the 23–33% adult-exclusion range Unwanted Witness computed for 2019–2020 having
narrowed substantially since.

**The 35 million is a cumulative-ever-enrolled count.** 28,571,893 legacy records migrated +
6,416,958 new registrations = **34,988,851** — the "over 35 million" is that sum, exactly. It
counts everyone ever assigned a NIN since 2015, **all ages**, and it exceeds the entire adult
population (23.2m) precisely because it includes registered children and carries no attrition.

**Card possession is the count that has collapsed, and it is the analytically interesting one.**
Cumulatively, 20m cards printed and 17.3m issued by 2024-07-01. But 15.8m of those cards expired
between August 2024 and December 2025, forcing a renewal cycle; the government extended expired
cards' validity by one year (SI 67 of 2024, gazetted 2024-08-12) as a stopgap. Under the 2025/26
mass exercise, 14.3m renewals were processed but only **10,152,559 cards printed**, **7,261,435
dispatched to districts**, and **2,581,457 actually collected (35.5%)** as of **2026-02-08** —
NIRA's registrar putting "over five million cards" sitting uncollected in district offices. So a
large population holds a NIN and a renewal receipt but no valid card in hand.

**Does the 35m include stale records? Yes, and NIRA has confirmed the mechanism.** The migration
was explicitly total — *"Every record that we did have in the old system is now present in the new
system"* — i.e. unfiltered, not a cleaned subset. The Auditor General's FY2024/25 report (reported
2026-03) found that the National Security Information System **cannot register births and deaths**
and is **not integrated with third-party systems**, so there is no death-deregistration pathway:
the register can only grow. NIRA separately disclosed (2025-06) that **450,000+ records share
identical three names and identical date of birth**, though it maintains biometric deduplication
guarantees uniqueness. Emigration is likewise not deducted. The 35m is therefore best read as
**"NINs ever issued and never removed"**, not "living Ugandans currently identifiable".

### Flag: an arithmetic error in NIRA's own press statement

NIRA's 2024-10-15 statement asserts that the 0–17 age bracket "accounts 59.7% of Uganda's
population". The 2024 census puts under-18s at 22,750,701 of 45,905,417 = **49.6%**. NIRA's 59.7%
appears to be wrong (or to use an undisclosed different band). The 22.1%-of-register figure in the
same sentence is internally consistent with 6.3m of 27.4m and is the one relied on above. Worth a
`needs-review` note wherever NIRA's statement is cited.
