## Verdict: RESOLVED — not a contradiction. Two non-commensurable series: Partech counts equity + debt across a wide tech universe (US$4.1bn, 570 deals); Disrupt Africa publishes a self-declared *minimum* floor for equity-and-debt raised by *startups* (US$1.637bn, 178 ventures). Both are correct on their own terms; neither may be restated as "African tech raised $X in 2025".

Both trackers' own primary publications were obtained and read. The gap is fully accounted for by
four stacked differences in what is counted, and the divergence is **structural and persistent** —
in 2024 the same two series stood at US$3.25bn (Partech) against US$1.1198bn (Disrupt Africa), a
2.9× gap that *narrowed* to 2.5× in 2025. A one-year measurement error would not reproduce itself
across years.

**The brief's key supporting observation does not survive contact with the primaries.** The brief
treated Partech's big-four share of **capital** (72%) and Disrupt Africa's 72.5% as a striking
convergence. They are not the same statistic. Disrupt Africa's 72.5% is the big four's share of
**funded ventures by count** (129 of 178); its share of **funding value** is **88%**
(US$1,452,850,000 of US$1,636,700,000). Set like against like, the two trackers **diverge by 16
percentage points** on big-four concentration (72% vs 88%) rather than converging. The "trackers
that disagree on level but agree on structure" reading should be dropped from the wiki.

**A second trap to record explicitly.** Partech's **debt** subtotal for 2025 is **US$1.64bn** —
numerically identical, to three significant figures, to Disrupt Africa's **total** of US$1.6367bn.
This is coincidence, not derivation: Disrupt Africa's total is overwhelmingly equity (only 21 of its
178 funded ventures, 11.8%, raised any debt element at all). Any future note that appears to
reconcile the two by equating these figures is wrong.

### Where the ~US$2.4bn gap sits — the country evidence

The two trackers do not merely differ on level; they **rank the big four in opposite orders**, and
the divergence is concentrated exactly where debt megadeals sit:

| Country | Partech (equity + debt) | Disrupt Africa | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Kenya | US$1.04bn (rank 1) | US$273,200,000 (rank 4) | **3.8×** |
| South Africa | US$715m (rank 2) | US$335,895,000 (rank 3) | 2.1× |
| Egypt | US$604m (rank 3) | US$378,950,000 (rank 2) | 1.6× |
| Nigeria | US$572m (rank 4) | US$464,805,000 (**rank 1**) | 1.2× |

Kenya is the tell. Partech attributes 48% of Kenyan capital to debt and four of the year's nine
megadeals to Kenya; AVCA independently records six debt megadeals worth US$1.1bn headlined by
Kenya's Sun King and Senegal's Wave. Sun King (founded 2007) sits outside Disrupt Africa's
"usually no older than 10 years / still scaling" startup test. Nigeria, whose funding is
equity-heavy and startup-shaped, is where the two series nearly meet. **The gap is debt megadeals
to mature companies, plus Disrupt Africa's deliberate floor, not disagreement about facts.**

### Recommendation for the wiki

1. **Adopt Partech as the citable headline series** for total African tech funding — it is the most
   comprehensive, splits equity from debt, and is the series most widely relayed. Always cite it as
   *"US$4.1bn in combined equity and debt (Partech, CY2025)"* — never bare.
2. **Retain Disrupt Africa as a named floor series**, useful precisely because it is conservative
   and startup-scoped, and because it publishes a full named startup list. Cite as *"at least
   US$1.64bn to 178 startups (Disrupt Africa's stated minimum, CY2025)"*.
3. **Never sum, average, or difference the two**, and never use one to check the other.
4. The brief's practical instruction stands and is reinforced: **no bare "Africa raised $X in
   2025"** — tracker named, basis (equity vs equity+debt) stated, year given, every time.

### Flagged onward (report only — not actioned)

- **New contradiction, equity direction CY2025.** Partech reports equity **up 8%** to US$2.4bn;
  AVCA's *Venture Capital in Africa Report* reports equity **down 21%** to US$2.1bn for the same
  year. Opposite signs on the same quantity — a sharper conflict than the one investigated here,
  since both claim to measure equity. Not investigated in this pass.
- **Third and fourth series exist**: AVCA US$3.9bn total (506 deals; US$2.1bn equity + US$1.8bn
  debt) and TechCabal Insights US$3.42bn (502 disclosed-amount deals). Any wiki page carrying a
  2025 funding total should be aware at least four incompatible series are in circulation.
- **Provenance gap.** The wiki holds Claim B only via a relay (Invest Africa), not Partech's own
  publication. The primary is now extracted to `new/`.
