## Verdict: CONCLUSIVE — neither claim is right as written. Econet InfraCo is a *pre-existing* Econet subsidiary (Distributed Power Africa) renamed and repurposed, into which *pre-existing* passive-infrastructure assets were legally transferred from other group SPVs. Nothing was founded from scratch.

Both trade reports flattened a two-part restructuring into a single wrong word.

**The corporate vehicle was not newly incorporated.** Econet's own notice to stakeholders of
**24 December 2025** names the entity precisely: "Distributed Power Africa (Private) Limited,
trading as Econet InfraCo." DPA is Econet's long-standing renewable-energy subsidiary, operating
commercially since at least 2018. The Supplementary Pre-Listing Statement of **26 March 2026**
confirms the vehicle's identity, recording that "the Company is currently undertaking a name change
from Distributed Power Africa Limited to Econet InfraCo Limited," and that it "completed its
conversion from a private limited company to a public company" with a new Certificate of
Incorporation issued **24 March 2026** (registration number 74120A02122025). A conversion and
renaming of an existing registered company is not an incorporation.

**The assets and operations were not new either — but they were not a single named "unit."** The
24 December notice states the transferred assets "comprise real estate assets and passive
telecommunications infrastructure **previously held by EWPL and other wholly owned SPVs** within
the Group" — i.e. dispersed across Econet Wireless (Private) Limited (the MNO-licence SPV) and
several other vehicles, not sitting in one tidy division. The move was "executed as a going concern
transfer between registered operators … formally established and confirmed under a **Scheme of
Reconstruction Agreement**," and "ownership and control of the infrastructure assets did not change."
So Developing Telecoms' "long operated as a dedicated unit within Econet Wireless Zimbabwe" is
directionally right about the *business* (mature, revenue-generating, decades old) but imprecise
about the *structure*: a real legal consolidation happened, it was not merely a public naming.

**Where ITWeb's "newly founded" came from.** Econet's own further cautionary announcement of
**15 December 2025** says "the Company has established Econet Infrastructure Company Limited
('Econet InfraCo')" — language that reads as incorporation and is the likely origin of the ITWeb
formulation. Nine days later the company named DPA as the actual vehicle. Chairman Godfrey Gomwe
put the substance plainly at the listing: "Econet InfraCo is operating at scale — we are not
building from scratch."

### Corporate structure (as of 2026-03-31)

- **Econet Global / Econet Group** — Strive Masiyiwa's founder vehicle. Sits *above and outside* the
  Zimbabwean listed structure; not a party to this restructuring in the documents reviewed. ITWeb's
  "Econet CEO" and Developing Telecoms' "Econet Group chief executive" both refer to **Dr Douglas
  Mboweni**, who is listed in EWZL's own board line-up as **Chief Executive Officer of Econet
  Wireless Zimbabwe Limited**. Developing Telecoms' "Group" is EWZL-as-group, not Econet Global.
- **Econet Wireless Zimbabwe Limited (EWZL)** — the Zimbabwean holding company, which calls *itself*
  "the Group" in its own filings. ZSE-listed 1998 until voluntary delisting effective 31 March 2026;
  shares now trade OTC on a VFEX-operated platform.
- **Econet Wireless (Private) Limited (EWPL)** — wholly owned SPV holding the **mobile network
  operator licence**. Retains active network infrastructure and customer-facing platforms; leases
  passive infrastructure back from InfraCo under long-term tenancy.
- **Econet InfraCo** (Distributed Power Africa Ltd t/a, renaming to Econet InfraCo Limited) — holds
  real estate, passive telecoms infrastructure (towers, power systems) and renewable-energy assets.
  Wholly owned by EWZL until March 2026; listed on VFEX **31 March 2026** (ticker INFR.VX) by way of
  introduction at ~US$1bn, Zimbabwe's largest initial listing. Post-transaction ownership:
  **70.000% EWZL, 25.215% Econet shareholders** (dividend in specie, register date 27 March 2026),
  **4.785% exiting shareholders** (Exit Offer). CEO **Fayaz King** from 1 March 2026; chairman
  **Godfrey Gomwe**.

### Residual uncertainty (minor, does not disturb the verdict)

The 15 December 2025 cautionary names "Econet Infrastructure Company Limited" while every document
from 24 December 2025 onward names Distributed Power Africa. The most economical reading is that
the December cautionary described the intended vehicle by its intended name before the group settled
on repurposing DPA. No document reviewed confirms a separately incorporated "Econet Infrastructure
Company Limited" ever existed, and none is referenced in the circular, PLS or listing documents.
