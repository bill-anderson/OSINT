# NDPC / MultiChoice ₦766.2m fine — research findings

## Verdict

**The penalty is real and abundantly documented.** The vendor compliance guide was right about the
figure; it was simply the wrong source to rest it on. The regulator's own statement is available.

## Established facts

- **Amount:** ₦766,242,500 (₦766.2m; ~US$501,340 at the time — Reuters).
- **Decision announced:** **Sunday 6 July 2025**, Abuja. Statement signed by **Babatunde Bamigboye**,
  Head, Legal, Enforcement & Regulations, NDPC. The National Commissioner **Dr Vincent Olatunji** also
  announced it on LinkedIn the same day (per DataGuidance).
- **Investigation opened:** **Q2 2024**.
- **Conduct penalised:**
  1. Violating the data-privacy rights of subscribers **and of non-subscribers** ("their friends who
     are not necessarily subscribers");
  2. **Illegal cross-border transfer** of personal data of Nigerian data subjects;
  3. Data processing found "patently intrusive, unfair, unnecessary and disproportionate", framed as
     an affront to **s.37 of the 1999 Constitution**;
  4. **Failure to cooperate** — remedial measures directed under the NDPC's standard remediation
     procedure were judged unsatisfactory. The fine is expressly "for want of cooperation".
- **Further direction:** Olatunji ordered that **all outlets** through which MultiChoice collects
  Nigerians' personal data be investigated for non-compliance.
- **Significance:** reported as the **largest single NDPC fine** since the NDP Act came into force in
  2023 (Nairametrics).

## Date discrepancy noted and resolved

A law-firm note (Kayode Sofola & Associates, https://kslegal.org/ndpcs-fine-against-multichoice-nigeria/)
dates the penalty **"6th June 2025"**. This is an isolated error against seven contemporaneous
accounts and the regulator's own statement, all of which put it at **6 July 2025**. Do not carry the
June date.

## Not established — remains open

- **Whether the fine was paid, contested or varied.** No record found either way as of 2026-07-20.
- **The underlying findings in detail.** The **Foundation for Investigative Journalism (FIJ)** filed
  an FOI request on 2025-07-09 seeking the extent of the violations, the remedial instructions issued,
  and how MultiChoice obtained non-subscriber data. The NDPC acknowledged receipt but had **not
  responded** more than two weeks later — beyond the seven-working-day limit in the FOI Act 2011,
  which deems non-response a denial. Reported via Digivation Network, 2026-07-26 (sic — page dated
  2025-07-26): https://digivationnetwork.com.ng/ndpc-withholds-details-of-multichoice-privacy-breach-despite-foi-request-fij/
  FIJ noted no publicly documented instance of the NDPC fully answering an FOI request with detailed
  investigation findings. **This is an enforcement-transparency finding worth carrying on the page.**
