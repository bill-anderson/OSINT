# Zambia INRIS enrolment — why the figure appears to fall

## Verdict: RESOLVED — the fall is not real. INRIS biometric enrolment stood at ~1.3m (May 2024), ~1.5m (Sep 2024), and still ~1.5m (Sep 2025, World Bank's own explicit wording); the Dec 2025 "about 1.3 million" is a stale/rounded figure recycled in a ministry PR briefing, not a de-duplication, a purge, or a change of base.

## Prose

**No source anywhere reports a de-duplication, purge or record clean-up of INRIS between 2024 and
2025.** The benign explanation the brief anticipated does not appear in any primary, partner or
trade-press account. Nor is there a change of base: every count in the sequence is a count of
*persons whose biometrics have been captured onto INRIS*, and the World Bank PAD settles the
"same act?" question directly — INRIS enrolment *is* biometric capture ("Name, Date of Birth,
Address, Gender and biometric attributes — 10 fingerprints and a photograph"). "Enrolled with
biometrics" (UNDP) and "enrolled onto INRIS" (ZNBC) denote the same act.

What actually explains the sequence is that **1.3 million is an older number that came back**. The
figure "1.3 million biometric records" was in circulation from **May 2024** (CSIS, relayed by
Biometric Update, 2024-08-28). UNDP, citing DNRPC, put it at **1.5 million in September 2024**. The
World Bank's DZAP PAD of **2025-09-11** states in its own words: *"Biometric data has been collected
for around 1.5 million people so far and the project targets increasing this to at least 4 million."*
The MoHAIS Principal PRO's **December 2025** "about 1.3 million" therefore matches the **May 2024**
figure exactly, and sits below the World Bank's figure of three months earlier. In a briefing whose
subject was travel documents, border security and card procurement — not registration statistics —
the most economical reading is a recycled, rounded programme figure, not a register that shrank.

**A correction to the brief's own arithmetic.** The brief inferred ~1.6m from the PAD's "15% of
adults". That inference should be retired: the same PAD states the absolute number explicitly as
**~1.5 million**, and 1.5m against a ~10m adult base *is* the 15%. There is no 1.6m data point; the
sequence never rose above ~1.5m.

**The real story the numbers tell is a plateau, not a decline.** Enrolment ran ~1.0m (Sep 2023) →
~1.3m (May 2024) → ~1.5m (Sep 2024) → ~1.5m (Sep 2025): essentially flat for a year. UNDP (Sep 2024)
notes "mass registration efforts paused"; the PAD frames the remaining coverage gap as "about 85
percent". Note also that the April 2025 mobile campaign targeting 3.5m citizens was for **paper
NRCs and birth certificates**, not INRIS biometric enrolment — a distinction that will otherwise
corrupt any coverage figure built from it.

**De-duplication:** no evidence, in any source consulted. De-duplication is discussed only as a
*motivation* for INRIS (the legacy paper system's duplicate NRC numbers), never as an executed
operation reducing the INRIS count.

**Bases:** consistent across all counts (persons biometrically enrolled). Denominators vary — UNDP
uses 9m eligible adults, the PAD uses ~10m adults, population is quoted as 20m (UNDP/PAD) and 21m
(Registrar General, Jun 2025) — so *percentages* between sources are not directly comparable, but
the absolute counts are.
