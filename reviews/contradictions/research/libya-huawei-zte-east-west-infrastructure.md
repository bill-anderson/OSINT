# Research: the Huawei / ZTE thread in Libya's east–west infrastructure contest

All three claims **confirmed**, with two material corrections to the brief.

## 1. Huawei suspension — confirmed, but it was lifted after three days

- **2025-08-17** — the GNU's **General Authority for Telecommunications and Informatics (GTI)** announced
  the suspension of **all Huawei activities in Libya**, citing entry into "illegal contracts with
  illegitimate entities", breach of **Law No. 22 of 2010** (which reserves licensing to the GTI), and
  violation of ITU principles on respect for state sovereignty. All contracts concluded outside GTI-approved
  frameworks declared null and void.
  Reported: Libya Observer (https://libyaobserver.ly/tech/libya-suspends-huaweis-operations-over-national-security-violations),
  Libya Security Monitor (2025-08-17), Arab Weekly (2025-08-18), Libya Review (2025-08-20).
- **2025-08-20 — SUSPENSION LIFTED.** The GTI announced agreement with **Huawei Technizon Technical
  Services – Libya Branch** to continue operations, Huawei having committed to full compliance with Law
  22/2010. **The brief did not carry this.** Primary: Libyan News Agency (LANA), state agency
  (https://lana.gov.ly/post.php?id=339579&lang=en). Corroborated by Libya Herald (2025-08-21) and Libya
  Observer.
- **The contract behind it.** Per Libya Security Monitor: eastern authorities requested a **USD 198m
  Letter of Credit** from the CBL as down payment on a **USD 700m Huawei contract** to build an eastern
  mobile network. Single-outlet for the figures — **treat as reported, not established**.
- **Naming correction.** "Ozon" is **not** the network. **OZON Aljaded For Telecom and Technology Group**
  is the licensee: granted a unified telecoms licence by the **HoR-appointed government's Ministry of
  Communications**, tender announced 5 Dec 2024, 25 firms interested, only Ozon and Giga Communications
  bought documents; licence **effective 4 March 2025** (Libya Review, 2025-05-13). The network to launch
  from Benghazi is reported as **"Barqa 217"** (Arab Weekly, 2025-08-18), tied to the **Libya Development
  and Reconstruction Fund**.

**The east–west logic:** Tripoli's alarm is that a Huawei-built eastern network would let the east run its
own telecoms space without routing through Tripoli-administered operators under GTI control. The
suspension was the western regulator asserting the licensing monopoly; the rapid reversal suggests it was
a jurisdictional signal rather than an enforcement campaign.

## 2. Trans-Sahara / Tatweer Huawei data centre — confirmed, long gestation

- **2026-06-24 (Data Center Dynamics)** — a **Huawei FusionModule1000A** containerised prefabricated
  facility to be deployed in **Tripoli "early next year"** (i.e. early 2027). Operated by **Trans-Sahara**
  (private, member of HB Group) in JV with **Tatweer Research** (public body owned by the **Libyan Local
  Investment & Development Fund, LLIDF**). Purpose: **private cloud to government agencies and local
  companies**; parties intend **PCI DSS** certification so the financial sector can use it. Ehab
  Elghariani, Trans-Sahara data-centre unit manager, cited power and infrastructure as the main obstacles.
  No capacity figure (MW/racks) published.
- **Prior history (not in the brief):** the Trans-Saharan–Tatweer partnership dates to an agreement signed
  in **Benghazi, early October 2019**, for a **TIER 3** data centre (Libya Herald, 2019-10-09; signed by
  Tatweer chairman Khalid Al-Mufti and Trans-Saharan board member Naaman ElBouri). Trans-Sahara separately
  signed with Morocco's **Data Protect** in Nov 2019 for Libya's first Security Operations Centre. So the
  2026 announcement is the **realisation of a seven-year-old plan, scaled down** from a Tier-3 build to a
  containerised module.

## 3. LTT–ZTE MoU — confirmed, date corrected

- **Signed 9 March 2026** at **MWC Barcelona 2026** — **not 11 March**, which is The Register's
  partner-content republication date. Primary: ZTE's own press release, release time 2026-03-09.
- **Signatories:** ZTE Corporation (Zhang Wanchun, SVP) and **LTT / Libya Telecom & Technology**
  (**Ammar Albouzaidi, Chairman**).
- **Scope:** 4G/5G planning and deployment; digital-service ecosystem (**e-wallet, e-commerce, CDN**);
  terminals (**FWA, CPE, MiFi**); new business models. Energy-efficient / green network technologies
  flagged. **No timetables, no value, no binding commitments** — Developing Telecoms notes explicitly
  there are "no timetables or further details on these plans as yet".

## The vendor split that answers the brief's question

- **ZTE → west.** LTT is the GNU-side state operator; the MoU is a **west-side, Tripoli-administered**
  modernisation partnership.
- **Huawei → east (contested).** The USD 700m eastern network is contracted through the **HoR-side**
  ministry and its licensee Ozon — the arrangement the GNU regulator declared null.
- **Huawei → west too, legitimately.** Huawei has operated in Libya 20+ years, contributed to 3G/4G
  rollout with local operators, and supplies the FusionModule for the Tripoli data centre. So this is
  **not** a clean vendor-per-region split: Huawei works both sides, and the conflict is about **which
  authority may license it**, not about excluding the vendor.
