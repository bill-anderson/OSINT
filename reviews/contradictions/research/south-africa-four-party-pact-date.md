## Verdict: CONCLUSIVE — the four-party SARS–DHA–BMA–GPW agreement was signed on **3 April 2025** (Thursday); the held "April 2024" is simply wrong, and the "2010" element is a separate bilateral SARS–DHA Direct Master Agreement that the same ceremony revived. The Digital Transformation of Government Roadmap was **approved by Cabinet on 26 March 2025** and **publicly launched 12 May 2025**.

The contradiction resolves exactly as the brief's assessment predicted, and slightly better than that:
the confusion is not one conflation but two, and both are settled by primaries.

**The signing date.** SARS's own media release, headed "3 April 2025", says the four bodies "took a
giant step **today**" by signing. The Department of Home Affairs' statement on gov.za, issued
04 April 2025, says the agreement "was jointly signed today" — a next-day issue of a ministerial
statement about the previous day's ceremony, which is why the two government dates differ by one.
Business Day (04 April) and SAnews (04 April) both independently fix the ceremony to "Thursday",
and 3 April 2025 was a Thursday. The signing date is therefore **2025-04-03**, and the gov.za
statement's own 4 April date should not be promoted to the event date (the Currency-discipline
event-date-≠-publication-date rule applies).

**No April 2024 pact exists.** A targeted search for any 2024 SARS/DHA/BMA/GPW agreement returns
nothing — every result for that party combination is the April 2025 event. The held claim in
`wiki/intersections/south-africa--dpi-id.md` that the SARS-hosted architecture "extends an
April-2024 pact" appears to be a year error in a retrospective secondary mention, not a reference
to a real prior instrument.

**Two agreements, not one — and the 2010 date belongs to one of them.** SARS's release is explicit
that 3 April 2025 produced *two* instruments: (1) a **bilateral direct agreement** between the SARS
Commissioner and the DHA Director-General, which "revives the previous **Direct Master Agreement**,
which has been the bedrock of the long-standing relationship between the parties, **which began in
2010**" — i.e. the 2010 instrument is a SARS–DHA IT-collaboration master agreement under which SARS
helped DHA build its own IT systems, and it is bilateral, never four-party; and (2) the **four-party
memorandum of understanding** between DHA, SARS, BMA and GPW, which creates the governance framework
over the wider partnership. Reporting that describes "the four-party pact dating from 2010" or "a
renewal since 2010" is collapsing (1) into (2). ITWeb (07 April) reads it correctly.

**The Roadmap dates.** SARS's release states the agreements are in line with "the Digital
Transformation of Government Roadmap, which the Cabinet approved on **26 March 2025**" — and the
GCIS Cabinet statement of that meeting (issued 27 March 2025) confirms it at section C.4: "Cabinet
approved the Digital Transformation of Government Roadmap". The **May 2025** date held on the ZAF
hub is not wrong so much as a different event: the Presidency **launched** the Roadmap publicly on
**12 May 2025** as a pillar of Operation Vulindlela Phase II (itself launched 7 May 2025), and the
Cabinet meeting of 14 May 2025 "welcomed the launch". Engineering News (12 May) states it plainly:
"Approved by Cabinet in March and implemented by the … Digital Service Unit". So approval and launch
are six weeks apart and both are real — the hub should carry approval 2025-03-26 and launch
2025-05-12 rather than choosing between them. Biometric Update's "approved by cabinet late last
month" (writing on 8 April) is consistent with 26 March.

Secondary detail worth carrying: the four-party MoU's stated first-year deliverables are an
Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) system, integration of Home Affairs services with bank
branches and banking apps, courier delivery of documents, an upgraded Movement Control System at
ports of entry, and Smart IDs for naturalised citizens and permanent residents. GPW's role is
framed as Certification Authority and Digital Trust Centre — verification and certification of
digital IDs. One inconsistency in the SARS release itself, immaterial: the DHA Director-General is
named "Livhuwani Makhode" in the body and "Livhuwani Tommy Makhode" in the photo caption (other
outlets use "Tommy Makhode") — same person.
