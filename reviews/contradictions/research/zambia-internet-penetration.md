# Zambia internet penetration — 21% vs 33% vs 56.8%

## Verdict: CONCLUSIVE — not three definitions but **two**, plus a vintage artefact. 56.8% is ZICTA's **active internet subscriptions per 100 inhabitants** (end-2022), not people; 21% and 33% are the *same* "individuals using the internet" measure at two different Kepios methodology vintages, the 21% superseded by a restatement Kepios made between its Digital 2023 and Digital 2024 reports.

## What actually happened

**The 56.8% is a subscriptions count.** ZICTA's 2022 Annual Market Performance Report states it in terms that leave no room for doubt: active internet subscriptions rose from 10.4m (end-2021) to 11.1m (end-2022), and *"the increase in the total number of active internet subscriptions reflected a marginal increase in the internet penetration rate from **56.7 per 100 inhabitants** reported in 2021 to **56.8 per 100 inhabitants** attained in 2022."* The denominator is population; the numerator is **SIM/data subscriptions**, which multi-SIMming inflates well past the number of humans. The UNDP blog (2024-09-17) lifted this figure, restated it as *"Zambia only has a 56.8 percent internet penetration,"* and dropped both the unit ("per 100 inhabitants") and the vintage (2022) — so the wiki inherited a two-year-stale subscription statistic dressed as a current people-based rate.

That series has kept climbing on the same basis: ZICTA reported **12.6m subscriptions = 64.1%** at December 2023 (cited by Freedom House), and Zambia's Ministry of Technology and Science claimed **72.1%** in June 2026 off 14.68m subscriptions. A "penetration" measure heading for three-quarters of a population of whom ~37% are under 13 is a subscriptions series, not a usage series.

**The 21% and the 33% are the same measure at different vintages.** The World Bank DZAP PAD's own text reads: *"Internet penetration, at around 21 users for every 100 inhabitants, is below the Eastern and Southern Africa regional average of 28 percent"* and, separately, *"in 2021 the figure stood at only 21.2 percent."* That 21.2% is **exactly** DataReportal/Kepios' Digital 2023 headline (4.30m users, January 2023, 21.2%) — the same number the Zambian National Assembly committee report also cites. The PAD is carrying a Kepios-family figure, not a rival definition.

Kepios then **rebased**. Digital 2024 reports 6.51m users / 31.2% *and simultaneously* states growth of *"175 thousand (+2.8 percent) between January 2023 and January 2024"* — arithmetic that implies a restated January-2023 base of ~6.33m, not the 4.30m Kepios had published a year earlier. The 21.2% → 31.2% jump is therefore a **methodology correction, not growth**. DataReportal itself warns readers against computing change across report vintages. The wiki's 2.7× "spread" is, in the 21-vs-33 half, an artefact of comparing a retired vintage with a current one.

**Where the ITU sits.** ITU DataHub's "individuals using the Internet" for Zambia — defined as *"the proportion of individuals who used the Internet from any location in the last three months"* — is **31.2% for 2022**, flagged as an **ITU estimate** (modelled, not an official Zambian return). That independently corroborates the ~31–33% magnitude and rules out both 21% (stale) and 56.8% (wrong unit). Note ITU's own methodology paper confirms most member-state values are imputed or modelled rather than official.

**Zambia's own survey evidence is thinner than any of them.** The ZamStats/ZICTA National Survey on Access and Usage of ICTs series — the actual household-survey basis — recorded individuals who had used the internet at **4.8% (2013), 8.8% (2015), 14.3% (2018)**. A 2022 round was conducted (6,466 households, 320 EAs, individual questionnaire to household members aged 10+), and the 2022 Census separately measured internet use in the three months prior for the population aged 5+. **I could not retrieve the headline individual-use percentage from either 2022 instrument** — the ZamStats PDFs did not yield the figure through search or fetch. This is the one genuine hole, and it matters: it is the only *nationally-representative survey* measure Zambia has, and every figure in dispute is either administrative (ZICTA) or modelled (Kepios/ITU).

## Recommendation for the wiki

Carry **33.0% (Kepios/DataReportal, October 2025)** as the citable headline for individuals using the internet, corroborated by the ITU's 31.2% estimate (2022). Retire the ~21% as current state — it is a superseded Kepios vintage, not a competing claim, and the DZAP PAD should be cited for the *project* and its policy framing, not for a live penetration rate. Keep ZICTA's subscription series on the page but **explicitly labelled as subscriptions per 100 inhabitants**, because the gap between 33% of people and 72% of "penetration" is itself an analytically interesting fact about how Zambian digital-inclusion targets are measured and reported — including the 8NDP's "80 percent by 2026" goal, whose own basis is ambiguous between the two families.
