# SOS Télécoms licence — RESOLVED

**The domestic original behind the Pravda mirror is Oubangui Médias, 2025-07-21** (byline Déus Gracias Tchémanguéré). Text is identical; Oubangui timestamps 17:23, the mirror 17:56 — the mirror is the copy.

- **Licence type:** licence d'exploitation des **infrastructures passives** de télécommunications — a **towerco** licence (pylons, antenna sites, technical sites, non-active equipment), to build and **lease** passive infrastructure to mobile operators. Explicitly **the first of its kind in CAR's history** (Minister Gourna Zacko).
- **Granting authority:** **ARCEP** (Autorité de Régulation des Communications Électroniques et des Postes). Cahier des charges signed by ARCEP DG **Bonaventure Benjamin Panzet Sébas** and SOS Télécoms PDG **Djargué Kertoumar**.
- **Date:** **18 July 2025** (signature of the cahier des charges). Company first applied **13 February 2024**.
- **Company:** SOS Télécoms — founded 2010, HQ **N'Djamena, Chad**; branches in Niger, Burkina, Togo, South Sudan and CAR (own site). Electrical installation, pylon construction, internet services, fibre deployment.
- **Positioning:** not a competitor to Orange/Telecel/Moov — a wholesale infrastructure partner, aimed at rural and low-margin coverage.
- **Related, separate thread:** Corbeau News (2025-04-29) reports the **Council of Ministers of 17 April 2025** approved an agreement with SOS Telecom to deploy **fibre in Lobaye, Mambéré-Kadéï, Ouham-Pendé and on the Bangui–Bambari axis**. That is a distinct fibre deal predating the July licence.
