## Verdict: RESOLVED — the handover took place on 13 November 2024; neither disputed date was ever an event date

The contradiction dissolves on inspection. It rests on treating two **publication dates** as
**event dates**. UNDP Eswatini's own press release, dated **13 November 2024**, states that MICT and
UNDP "**today** officially launched the results of the Digital Readiness Assessment," and carries the
photo caption "UNDP Resident Representative, Henrik Franklin, **after handing over the DRA results
report** to [Minister] of ICT, Hon. Savanna Maziya" — same photographer credit (UNDP/Mantoe Phakathi)
as the 10 December blog reuses. The handover is therefore a **single act on 13 November 2024**, at the
Hilton Garden Hotel, Mbabane — the same day and venue ESEPARC gives for the Validation Workshop.

There was **no second handover**. Claim A (ESEPARC, 5 Dec) and Claim B (UNDP blog, 10 Dec) are both
**retrospective write-ups** of the 13 November event, published weeks later; the UNDP blog asserts no
date for the handover at all — "December 10, 2024" is the post's own publication stamp, and its text
refers to the minister speaking "at the report's launch" in the past tense. The brief's premise that
"UNDP states Franklin handed the report to Maziya on that date" is a misreading of a CMS date field.

The Biometric Update piece (22 Nov) is likewise a **secondary re-report** of the 13 November launch,
not a separate unveiling three weeks earlier — it quotes Maziya's launch remarks verbatim as they
appear in the 13 November UNDP release.

This is a textbook instance of the CLAUDE.md rule **"Event date ≠ publication date (secondary
re-reports)"** — three outlets' `published` dates were read as three events.

**One residual uncertainty, minor:** the MICT Ministry's own LinkedIn post says the handover happened
"today at the Hilton Hotel" but is timestamped **14 November 2024**. UNDP's press release — the
convening organisation, publishing on the day — is the better primary, and 13 November stands. The
±1 day is a plausible next-day social post or timezone artefact, not a competing account.
