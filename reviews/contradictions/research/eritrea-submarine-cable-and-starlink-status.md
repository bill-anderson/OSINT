# Eritrea: submarine-cable landings and Starlink status — research notes

## Claim A — Eritrea is the only coastal African state with zero submarine-cable landings

**Established, with a caveat on precision.**

- **Submarine Networks (submarinenetworks.com), Africa landing-stations page** — industry registry:
  "Among the 54 African countries recognized by United Nations, there are 38 countries that have seashore
  and 16 that are land locked. Out of these 38 countries that have seashore, 37 countries have at least one
  submarine cable landing. **The lone exception is Eritrea**, considering Western Sahara is considered
  disputed territory." Page is **undated** (site © 2011–2024); its adjacent statistics are stamped
  "by the end of 2019". Usable as a registry statement, **not** as a dated 2026 fact.
- **Middle East Eye, 2024-06-10** (on-the-record trade reporting from the Submarine Networks conference,
  London) — corroborates the *behaviour* behind the absence, dated: cable firms sought to route Red Sea
  cables through Eritrean waters to bypass Yemen; Eritrea "is not even acknowledging their approaches";
  Hasnain Ali (Pioneer Consulting Middle East) on the record: *"Typically if you ask governments for
  permission they say yes or no, but the Eritreans simply don't reply. It's a black hole."* Also: Eritrea
  is one of the few states **not party to UNCLOS**, and has "rebuffed the approaches of the cable industry
  to use its waters, even at a price." Eritrean waters are at an optimal ~170m depth for cable-laying.
- **Corroborating context (not cited on pages):** dig.watch country page — "without a public submarine
  cable landing station, EriTel's internet connectivity relies on geostationary satellite links"; The Africa
  Report 2025-04-24 on Djibouti's ten landings — "Eritrea's communications environment is highly regulated
  and relies on a satellite for digital communications."

**Precision note.** The *strong* form of the claim ("the **only** coastal African state") rests on the
undated Submarine Networks registry line. The **dated, citable** form is narrower and safer: no submarine
cable lands in Eritrea, and as of **June 2024** the state was actively non-responsive to the cable
industry's approaches. Write the narrow form; attribute the "lone exception" framing to the registry.

## Claim B — Starlink was unlicensed in Eritrea as of 2024

**Established, and now refreshable to 2026-07-17 — better than the original claim.**

- **MyBroadband, 2026-07-17** — tabulates Starlink's own coverage-availability map by African country.
  **Eritrea: "No planned launch date."** Same status recorded in the same publication's 2025-09-30 table
  and its 2023-01-18 table ("unconfirmed launch window"), so the position is **stable across 2023 → 2026**,
  not a 2024 snapshot.
- Article-internal inconsistency worth noting: the prose says the seven no-date countries besides South
  Africa are "all in Northern Africa", but its own table lists Djibouti, Eritrea and Sudan among them. The
  **table** (drawn from Starlink's map) is the reliable element; the prose sentence is loose. Do not cite
  the prose claim.
- Nothing found asserting Eritrea ever **refused** a licence, or that SpaceX ever **applied**. The honest
  statement is absence of any announced launch date and no evidence of a licensing process — not a
  documented refusal.

## Searched and not found

- No Eritrean regulatory notice on satellite licensing. The Ministry of Information and Communication
  Technology performs regulatory functions in the absence of an independent sector regulator; no public

- No Shabait / Ministry of Information statement on submarine cables or Starlink surfaced.
- **This is a legitimate dated null result**, consistent with a state with one operator, no independent
  press, and minimal official publishing — not a failed search.
