# CAR national data centre — the three threads worked

## Thread 1 — AfDB / Central African Backbone procurement: RESOLVED NEGATIVE

The AfDB's own inauguration release (**6 February 2023**) for the CAR national fibre backbone and the Congo interconnection enumerates what the project delivered: **935 km** of terrestrial and sub-fluvial fibre (Bangui–Douala corridor via Bossembélé, Yaloké, Baoro, then Berbérati, splitting to Cameroon via Gamboula and Congo via Bomassa); a **digital training centre and incubator at the University of Bangui**; a **cybersecurity and electronic-certification platform**; and technical assistance to create the **Agence centrafricaine de développement du digital**. EU + AfDB co-financed.

**No data centre appears in the delivered scope.** The "Datacenter principal et de secours" line therefore did **not** result in a commissioned facility under CAB. As of **2026-07-20** no award, completion or commissioning record exists. This is now a dated negative, not an open unknown.

(Corbeau News, 2025-04-29, gives the backbone as **900 km** and **€33m** EU+AfDB — a minor divergence against AfDB's own 935 km. AfDB is the announcer of record and should be preferred.)

## Thread 2 — AIAL Kigali MoU: CONFIRMED AS AN MoU, never advanced

The Oubangui Médias original (2025-01-22) confirms the wiki's existing record and is **not** aggregator-only. MoU signed **21 January 2025** by Minister **Justin Gourna-Zacko** and AIAL Group Limited executive vice-president **Dr Nurudin Mithani**, for construction and management of a data centre plus satellite internet provision. The article is explicit that **this is not the project contract** — the ministry must first examine AIAL's dossier and take it to the Council of Ministers before any contract signature.

**No subsequent contract, Council-of-Ministers approval or construction record was found as of 2026-07-20.** The thread appears dormant.

## Thread 3 — World Bank P174620: RESOLVED, and the brief's framing was wrong

**P174620 is not CARDIP.** P174620 is the **CAR Public Sector Digital Governance Project (PSDGP / PGNSP)** — a bilateral CAR operation. CARDIP (Central African Digital Integration Program) is a separate regional six-country program. The paywalled Africa Business Insight lede the wiki holds is about CARDIP and does **not** bear on P174620.

P174620, from World Bank project documentation:

- IDA grant **US$35m** (SDR 25.1m). PAD Report No. PAD4661, 7 March 2022.
- Board approval **5 May 2022**; signed 19 May 2022; effective **29 December 2022**; closing **31 December 2026**.
- Implementing agency: **Ministry of Finance and Budget**. Lead practice area: Digital Development.
- Components: 1. Public resources management ($17m); 2. Digitally transform public administration and service delivery ($15m); 3. Implementation support, citizen engagement, M&E ($3m); 4. CERC ($0).
- **Additional financing, May 2025:** +SDR 4.8m (**US$6.4m**) adding **Component 5, Mining Sector Governance** ($5.9m) — a geological and mineral information system (GMIS), digitisation of paper geological records, KP/EITI roll-out. Total project cost **US$41.4m**. Closing date unchanged.
- **Results as of April 2025:** disbursement **US$11.35m (33%)**; Midterm Review September 2024 found **5 of 6 PDO indicators on track**; overall rated **Moderately Satisfactory**. Achievements: modernised customs systems; payroll clean-up removing **more than 2,000 ghost workers**; mobile-money salary payment for **1,702 civil servants** in unbanked regions; IFMIS implementation; **enactment of two laws (cybersecurity and data protection)**; strengthened ARCEP capacity; a cross-agency identification and civil-registration working group.

**Critically: P174620 contains no data-centre component.** The World Bank is not a funder of a CAR national data centre. This removes the third thread from the data-centre question entirely — it belongs instead to the govtech / data-protection record, where it is load-bearing: it is the project that produced Law 24.001 and funded the April 2025 état-civil diagnostic.

See `car-socatel-national-digital-hub.md`: the **Greenline Technologies / SOCATEL** Tier 3 data centre (MoU 2025-09-18; operational phase 2026-07-16; $150m). This is the same object as the "SOCATEL National Digital Hub" dataset line.

## Net position as of 2026-07-20

CAR has **no built national data centre**. Four proposals have been floated in eighteen months — **Huawei** (Tier III, 2026-05), **Greenline/SOCATEL** (Tier 3, 2025-09, now operational-phase), **AIAL** (MoU 2025-01, dormant), and the **AfDB/CAB** line that was never delivered. Three different geopolitical sponsors — China, the United States, Rwanda — circling the same unbuilt facility.
