# Enugu smart green schools — research findings

## Verdict

**The programme exists, is large, and is documented far better than the wiki's single sponsored
placement suggested — but the wiki has the wrong headline number and the wrong completion status.**

The canonical programme figure is **260** — one school per each of Enugu State's 260 political wards —
not 267. And as of **May 2026** the programme was **incomplete**, with schools opened before
construction finished, multiple structural failures, at least one demolition, a terminated contract
and an active EFCC recovery.

## The documentary trail the brief assumed was absent

### Budget lines (Enugu State appropriations, via TheCable investigation, 2026-05-17)

- **2025:** **₦266.7bn** allocated to the smart-school initiative — ₦100bn construction of 200
  integrated smart schools; **₦146bn equipping 260 ward smart schools** with digital tools; ₦10.7bn
  upgrading three senior secondary structures; ₦10bn establishing Smart Senior Secondary Schools.
- **2026:** **₦198bn** — ₦158bn for 16 smart senior secondary schools; ₦10bn equipping/furnishing
  existing smart schools; ₦30bn school feeding.
- **Unit cost:** ~**₦1.5bn per school** (commissioner for education); other information puts it at
  **₦1.3bn** construction + equipping.
- **Education share of state budget:** 33% in 2024 and 2025 (Mbah); **32.27% of the 2026 budget**
  (cited by Yemi Osinbajo, 2026-05).

### Procurement / contract award

- **2024-07-02:** a construction company awarded a contract worth **over ₦11.4bn** for **22 smart
  green schools**. ~**₦5.7bn (50%)** paid upfront. Contractor accused of substandard work; contract
  terminated at expiry of the completion time in **February 2025**. **EFCC investigation** has
  recovered **₦1.23bn** so far (as of 2026-05-17). The contractor is publicly identified in the
  earlier THISDAY coverage as **Sujimoto Luxury Construction Limited** (CEO Olasijibomi "Suji"
  Ogundele), who disputes the figures, saying he received ₦5.2bn and had spent ₦4bn.
- Other named contractors, each with multiple sites: **Testimony Enterprises** (10 schools),
  **Intasog Limited** (12 sites), **Alubond Nigeria Limited** (10 schools), **Ever Progressive
  Integrated Resources Venture** (multiple). A contractor states Enugu indigenes got at least 90% of
  the school projects. Contract sums were **revised upward** by the state for inflation.

### Funder

**Self-funded from the state budget.** No donor or DFI facility was found. UNESCO's Nigeria head
(Jean-Paul Ngome Abiaga) offered praise on a courtesy visit (April 2026) but **no UNESCO funding is
indicated**. Osinbajo's May-2026 commendation is likewise endorsement, not finance.

## The number: 260, not 267

- **260** is the design figure, repeated consistently by Governor **Peter Mbah** and in all
  substantive coverage: "one in every political ward across our great state" (2025-05-28); September
  2025 state broadcast, "each of the 260 schools"; THISDAY 2025-09-22 and 2025-09-24; BusinessDay
  2025-09-27; Osinbajo, 2026-05-20 ("across its 260 wards"); TheCable, 2026-05-17 ("all 260 wards").
- **267** appears only in the **claimed-delivery** register — a state-government claim that 267 have
  been *built*, with construction continuing (e.g. NaijaChoice, 2026-07-15, "We Have Built 267 Smart
  Green Schools"), and in the Commissioner's remark at NESS 2026 that the wiki currently holds.
- A plausible reconciliation — 260 ward schools plus a handful of senior-secondary/pilot builds — is
  **not established by any source found**. Do not assert it. Treat 267 as a **state delivery claim**,
  not the programme's size.

## Completion status (the wiki held none of this)

- **Pilot:** Owo Smart Green School (governor's hometown) opened **2024-02-07**.
- **Transition formally began 2025-09-22** — the state's own launch date for migrating pupils.
- **Full opening of all 260 promised for January 2026**, with free daily meals for **over 300,000
  children**.
- **As of May 2026 (TheCable), many schools were still not physically complete or fully equipped.**
  The governor: "We know that every smart green school is not yet complete. There have been setbacks,
  delays, and challenges to overcome."
- Design spec: ~**25 digitally connected classrooms** each, ICT centres, robotics/AI labs,
  e-libraries, digital whiteboards, science labs, solar power, smart farms, clinic, water, community
  hall, on-site teacher housing; free uniforms, books, meals and **tablets**; capacity **840–1,000
  pupils** per campus, early childhood through full basic education.
- **~11,000 new teachers** reported in recruitment/training at the Centre for Experiential Learning
  and Innovation; complaints that successful applicants were not contacted.

## Structural failures — a material counterweight

- **New Haven** (Enugu): roof sheets blown off, rain dripping into occupied classrooms (April 2026);
  wall cracks, incomplete electrical/plumbing, bamboo scaffolding still up.
- **Ishielu Amagunze, Nkanu East LGA** — windstorm tore off roof, **February 2026**.
- **Amofia Nara, Nkanu East** — community complaints about materials, **2026-03-11**.
- **Iheaka, Igbo-Eze South LGA** — roof collapse, **2026-04-08**; another at **Orba**.
- **Mbu, Isi-Uzo LGA** — school **demolished July 2025** after failing structural integrity tests;
  contract terminated. LGA chairman Obiora Obeagu: "The contractors misrepresented their compliance."
- **Amankpunato, Achi Uno, Oji River LGA** — staircase demolished after failing integrity tests
  (July 2025).
- Commissioner for works **Ben Osy Okoh refused three interview requests** (April 9, 14, 16, 2026).

## Connectivity supplier

**Not established.** No vendor is named in any source found for the satellite or terrestrial
connectivity, nor for the solar installations. The Commissioner's NESS 2026 description ("satellite
plus local internet infrastructure, supply routers and access points") remains the only account, and
it names no supplier. **This part of the original gap stands.**
