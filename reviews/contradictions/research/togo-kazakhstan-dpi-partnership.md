# Togo–Kazakhstan digital partnership — research findings

## Verdict: it exists, it is dated, and it is *not* a DPI-identity partnership

The Biometric Update reference (2025-11-03) was real but mislabelled by association. The arrangement
is an **e-government software licensing and expertise transfer**, not digital-ID / DPI cooperation.

## Chronology (three distinct instruments — do not collapse them)

| Date | Instrument | Parties |
|---|---|---|
| **Nov 2023** | Presidential visit — cooperation first discussed | Faure Gnassingbé → Kazakhstan (Tokayev) |
| **Feb 2024** | **Memorandum of cooperation**, signed at **Digital Almaty 2024** | Cina Lawson (Togo, Min. Digital Economy) ↔ Bagdat Mussin (Kazakhstan, Min. Digital Development) |
| **~25–27 June 2024** | **Three licensing agreements** — the operative instrument | **Agence Togo Digital (ATD)**, MD Kafui Ekouhoho ↔ **National Information Technologies (NITEC/NIT)**, acting head Dmitry Mun |

## What the June 2024 agreements actually transfer

Per NITEC's own account (Kursiv, 2024-06-20), three eGov program units, **as a grant of source code**
(per Astana Times, 2024-09-04):

1. **Smart Bridge** — services showcase / interoperability layer
2. **Single platform of internet resources for public agencies** (the "E-Peer" website builder)
3. **Smart Data Ukimet** — information-analytical system for big-data-based public governance

Stated coverage per Nouvel Angle (2024-06-26): digital infrastructure development, training
programmes, and pilot projects.

## Has anything followed?

**Nothing found after September 2024.** Searches surfaced no Togolese or Kazakh announcement of a
deployment, go-live, pilot result or renewal. Astana Times (2024-09-04) is the last substantive
mention, and it is a Kazakh-side export-progress story listing Togo alongside Tajikistan and Sierra
Leone. Kazakhstan reported >5 websites built in Tajikistan on the platform; **no equivalent Togo
delivery figure was published**. The honest finding is: **signed June 2024, no publicly reported
implementation as of 2026-07-20.**

## Why Biometric Update called it DPI

Biometric Update is an identity-industry outlet; it appears to have folded a govtech licensing deal
into a list of "bilateral DPI partnerships". Kazakhstan is **not** a partner in Togo's e-ID — that is
**MOSIP**, with Atos and Idemia, under WURI. The wiki must not let the Kazakhstan link contaminate the
e-ID account.

## Note for the corpus

Two entities are named that the wiki does not appear to hub: **Agence Togo Digital (ATD)** — created by
décret n°2021-102/PR of 29 Sept 2021, JO 19 Nov 2021, mandated to digitise 75% of administrative
procedures — and **NITEC** (Kazakhstan, created 2000). ATD in particular is a significant Togolese
government body that the hub does not currently list.
