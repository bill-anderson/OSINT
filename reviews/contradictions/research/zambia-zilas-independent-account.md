# ZILAS — independent account found

Two accounts that are **not** SMART Zambia's own portal, both substantially more
informative than the material held:

## 1. Parliamentary record — the ministerial statement (primary)

**Ministerial Statement on the Deployment of the Zambia Integrated Land Administration
System (ZILAS)**, by the Minister of Lands and Natural Resources, **Hon. Elijah
Muchima, MP**, presented to the National Assembly **Friday 16 June 2023**.
<https://www.parliament.gov.zm/node/11091> ·
PDF: <https://www.parliament.gov.zm/sites/default/files/images/publication_docs/MINISTERIAL%20STATEMENT%20-%20Ministry%20of%20Lands.pdf>

This corrects and substantially predates everything the wiki held:

- **Cabinet approved ZILAS in 2020.**
- **Data migrated from ZILMIS to ZILAS on Saturday 13 May 2023.**
- **ZILAS went live on 17 May 2023** — the wiki previously had no go-live date and
  implicitly dated the system to the SZI portal pages of **2024-09-13**, over a year late.
- **Sixty e-services** activated on the Government Service Bus.
- **Zampass account on Zamportal required** to access the services (predates, and is
  broader than, the ground-rent-specific claim the wiki held).
- Predecessor **ZILMIS** was used **nine years, 2014–2023**; its **maintenance contract
  with the developer ended 31 March 2022** and the ministry ran it **without a contract**
  thereafter — "we were at risk in the event of system challenges". Muchima states ZILMIS
  ran nine years without enhancements or online payment, "thereby creating a **conduit for
  corruption**".
- ZILAS features listed by the minister: mandatory fields (gender, phone, DOB, NRC,
  email); payment rails plugged in (**mobile money, internet banking, Visa, ZANACO Bill
  Muster**); a **staff performance-tracking mechanism** that turns red past the service
  charter; **role-based security**; an **audit trail**; **GSB integration to MoHAIS for NRC
  verification and to PACRA for company profiles**; 90-day expiry on Invitations to Treat;
  e-filing from anywhere; documents scanned but **physical files still kept**; an
  **electronic cashbook disaggregated by province**.
- **The independent-signal passage** — the minister answering public complaint:
  "*there is an assertion by the members of the general public that the ZILAS has crushed
  [crashed]. To the contrary, the system is currently operational*". He concedes
  "**teething challenges**" and describes remedies: staff re-orientation, "**deployment of a
  team offsite to work on the backlog**", and "**the presence of dot.gov (the developer of
  the system) in the country**".
- **Public apprehension about mandatory data cleaning** is recorded: the ministry "has
  noted with concern that members of public are apprehensive with the ministry's
  requirement to clean their data before they can access some of the services". Cleaning
  is stated to take ~10 minutes; ZILMIS data was incomplete (missing gender, DOB, NRC,
  phone, email).
- **Cut-off date of 17 June 2023** for clients who had submitted documents before
  migration.

**Vendor identity is NOT established.** The record says "dot.gov (the developer of the
system)". Whether that is the wiki's `dot-com-zambia` (Dot Com Zambia PLC) or a
distinct firm is **not stated and must not be inferred** — a Hansard transcription of a
spoken name. Filed as a gap, not asserted.

## 2. Independent practitioner account — Corpus Legal Practitioners

**"Unlocking Efficiency — The Digital Clearance Process"**, Namakuzu Shandavu and
Rabecca Banda, Corpus Legal Practitioners, **19 September 2023**.
<https://corpus.co.zm/unlocking-efficiency-the-digital-clearance-process/>

A Zambian law firm's client note — conveyancing practitioners describing the system as
they actually experience it. This is the operator-independent account the brief asked for:

- ZILAS introduced a mandatory preliminary step called **"Digital Clearance"** — digital
  confirmation and verification by the ministry of the details on the certificate of
  title, including the registered title holder's name.
- **"No land transaction can take place until this process is completed."**
- Submission takes **30 minutes to 1 day "depending on the functionality of the system"**.
- But: **"the timeframe of approval of the Digital Clearance is currently uncertain. In
  practice, it takes approximately three (3) to four (4) weeks for the Ministry to
  approve"** — against SZI's "eliminating queues" self-description.

Historical baselines only, on the **predecessor** ZILMIS — do not present as ZILAS state:
- ZILMIS held ~**600,000 documents**, of which at most **200,000–250,000** estimated
  complete titles (Kaunda 2017, cited in World Bank, *Making Secure Land Tenure Count*).
- **141,625** total titled records in 2013, +14,700 new that year (USAID/World Bank Land
  and Poverty Conference paper, 2017).
- ZILMIS served ~**200 users** country-wide, mostly provincial HQs; storage servers "at
  their limit", data duplicated, system "sluggish" (Tembo, 2018).
- **Under 10% of Zambian households have title**; 44% perceive a risk of losing their land
  (2018 Labour Force Survey, World Bank analysis).
