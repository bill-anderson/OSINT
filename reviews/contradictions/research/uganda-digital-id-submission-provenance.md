## Verdict: RESOLVED — origin established (CHRGJ/NYU + ISER + HEAPI, 25 July 2024) and the ~1m unmatchable-fingerprints claim traced to a dated first-hand report of NIRA ED Rosemary Kisembo's parliamentary testimony (UBC, 2 July 2024).

### 1. The submission's origin

The document is **not** anonymous — the parked PDF simply lost its cover attribution. An
exact-title search located it hosted at `iser-uganda.org/wp-content/uploads/**2024/07**/` under
the identical filename as the parked lead, and both publishers carry a dated landing page:

- **CHRGJ (NYU School of Law)** posted it as *"Seizing the opportunity to improve Uganda's
  national digital ID system"*, dated **25 July 2024** on the page itself ("July 25, 2024."),
  byline Youssef Farhat.
- **ISER Uganda**'s publication page names the coalition explicitly: *"Together with Health
  Equity and Policy Initiative (HEAPI) and the Center for Human Rights & Global Justice – NYU,
  we raise 5 urgent recommendations…"*
- CHRGJ's **Everyone Counts Initiative** project page independently corroborates the same
  three-party authorship and the same "5 urgent recommendations" framing.

So the publishing coalition is **CHRGJ (NYU) + ISER + HEAPI**, published **2024-07-25**. This
sits neatly inside the June–July 2024 bracket the brief inferred from content. The same three
organisations (with Unwanted Witness in place of/alongside HEAPI on earlier work) are the
long-running litigants in *ISER, UW & HEAPI v. Attorney General and NIRA* and co-authors of
*Chased Away and Left to Die* (2021) — a coherent, well-documented advocacy lineage, not an
orphan document.

Note: a **separate and distinct** Unwanted Witness position paper, *"Championing an Inclusive,
Trustworthy and Accountable Approach to Uganda's ID Infrastructure"* (dated 07.06.2024), exists
and is easily confused with this one. They are different documents by different (overlapping)
coalitions.

### 2. The ~1 million unmatchable-fingerprints claim

Traced to its origin report. **UBC (Uganda Broadcasting Corporation, the state broadcaster),
2 July 2024** reports NIRA Executive Director **Rose Mary Kisembo** stating "there are currently
1 million people in the register whose fingerprints did not satisfy the requirements", made
**while appearing before Parliament's Committee on Statutory Authorities and State Enterprises**
to answer queries in the **Auditor General's report for FY2022/23**.

Biometric Update (4 July 2024) carries the same figure but is explicitly a **secondary
re-report** — it attributes the account to UBC. It adds one detail: Kisembo saying NIRA was
"three months behind projections for mass enrollment". Under the duplicate ladder, UBC is the
primary and the keeper; Biometric Update was not clipped.

The claim as the submission renders it ("cannot be matched") is a slightly stronger paraphrase of
what Kisembo is reported saying ("did not satisfy the requirements" / "no longer recognizable").
The wiki should carry the **reported wording**, attributed, not the advocacy paraphrase.

**Caution on the pre-held corroboration.** The brief's existing partial corroboration — IriTech
via Biometric Update, "more than 1 million Ugandans could not register fingerprint biometrics at
all" — is a **different proposition** from Kisembo's. Kisembo's 1m are people **already in the
register** whose stored prints fail matching; IriTech's framing is people who **could not enrol**
fingerprints in the first place. Same order of magnitude, materially different populations. They
should not be treated as two witnesses to one fact.

### 3. Event date vs publication date

Neither UBC nor Biometric Update dates the committee appearance itself; UBC says only that
Kisembo made the remarks "while appearing before" the committee. The **event date is unknown**
(late June / early July 2024). 2024-07-02 is the publication date and must not become the
dated "as of" for the testimony.
