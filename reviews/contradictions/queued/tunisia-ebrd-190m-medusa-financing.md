# Provenance hunt: does an EBRD €190m facility finance the Tunisia–Medusa connection?

**Queued:** 2026-07-20 (migrated from gaps.md)

## The claim in doubt

- **Claim A — the EBRD's €190 million finances the Tunisia–Medusa submarine-cable connection.**
  - Asserted by: trade press. **No primary is held for this claim.**
- **Claim B — the construction financing for the Tunisian landing is demonstrably an EU Connecting Europe
  Facility grant to Orange**, not an EBRD facility.
  - Established in reconcile research, which is quarantined and not citable.

The likely reading is that the EBRD facility is a **separate corporate or network loan** that trade press
conflated with landing-station financing. The wiki should not repeat the claim before this is settled.

## Sources held

The trade-press account asserting the EBRD–Medusa link is held. **No EBRD project record and no primary for
the Connecting Europe Facility grant is on file** — that is the provenance gap on both sides.

## Instruction

Establish what the €190 million facility actually finances and who the borrower is, from the lender's own
project database, and separately establish what financed the construction of the Tunisian landing of the
cable. Determine whether the two are the same money, related, or unconnected. Record the amount, borrower,
purpose, signature date and an as-of date for each.
