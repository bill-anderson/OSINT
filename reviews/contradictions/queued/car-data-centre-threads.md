# Provenance hunt: three unresolved threads behind a Central African Republic national data centre

**Queued:** 2026-07-20 (migrated from gaps.md)

## The three under-sourced threads

The wiki's national-data-centre page for the Central African Republic carries three claims it cannot stand
behind:

1. **An older African Development Bank / Central African Backbone procurement for a national data centre.**
   Its **outcome and commissioning status are unknown** — no held source says whether anything was built.
2. **An unconfirmed data-centre memorandum of understanding with Kigali (Rwanda), involving AIAL.**
   Carried by **a single aggregator** and confirmed by nobody.
3. **The World Bank project P174620 / CARDIP institutional and financing arrangements.** The wiki's CARDIP
   material **rests on one paywalled Africa Business Insight lede** — a partial free portion, not the full
   account.

## Sources held

The single aggregator item on the Kigali memorandum, and the paywalled Africa Business Insight lede on
CARDIP, are held. **No African Development Bank project page or backbone-project closure report is on file;
no primary for the Kigali memorandum is on file; the World Bank project document is not on file.** Those
absences are the provenance gap.

## Instruction

Establish, for each thread: whether the earlier development-bank-funded data-centre procurement completed
and what was commissioned, with dates; whether the Kigali memorandum was in fact signed, by whom, when and
covering what; and the actual financing, institutional arrangements and current status of the World Bank
project, from the project documentation itself. Record an as-of date for each finding.
