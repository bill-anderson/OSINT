# Contradiction: is South Africa's digital ID being built with the Reserve Bank or the Revenue Service?

**Opened:** 2026-07-17 (ZAF·ZMB·ZWE batch)

## The dispute

**SARB and SARS are different institutions** — the central bank and the revenue service — and the wiki
now holds primary government sources naming each as the digital-ID partner.

- **Claim A — the South African Reserve Bank (SARB).**
  - "the Department is working with the **South African Reserve Bank** to develop a robust digital
    identity system. This system will use facial recognition as the primary biometric modality."
  - Asserted by: **Njabulo Nzuza, Deputy Minister of Home Affairs**, Budget Vote speech to the NCOP,
    2025-07-03 — an on-the-record ministerial statement.
  - Source URL held:
    https://www.gov.za/news/speeches/deputy-minister-njabulo-nzuza-home-affairs-dept-budget-vote-202526-ncop-03-jul-2025
  - **Independently corroborated** by the DHA's own tabled **Strategic Plan 2025–2030** (April 2025),
    which states the digital ID with remote authentication is being developed **with the SARB**, facial
    recognition primary, fingerprints secondary. Held at
    `raw/2025-04-01 South Africa Department of Home Affairs Strategic Plan 2025-2030.md`.
- **Claim B — the South African Revenue Service (SARS).**
  - The core digital-ID stack "is being built **inside the SARS hosting environment**".
  - Asserted by: held material on `wiki/intersections/south-africa--dpi-id.md`, sourced to
    `raw/2026-04-21 South Africa's digital ID gets a targeted launch date.md`.
  - Corroborated by the four-party agreement in which **SARS** signs with DHA, the Border Management
    Authority and Government Printing Works:
    https://www.biometricupdate.com/202504/south-african-agencies-sign-multiparty-digital-id-deal

## Likely reconciliation to test

These are **not necessarily inconsistent** — SARS could host the infrastructure while SARB is a design
or standards partner, particularly given the central bank's interest in identity for payments and eKYC.
The identical "facial recognition primary" detail across both accounts suggests **one system, not two**.
But two DHA sources (a ministerial speech and the department's own tabled plan) say SARB, and neither
mentions SARS in that role — so this cannot be dismissed as an acronym slip in a single speech.

Establish, with an as-of date: which institution hosts the platform; which is a design/governance
partner; whether SARB's involvement is specific to payments/eKYC; and whether the wiki's "built inside
the SARS hosting environment" framing is accurate or itself a compression.

## Instruction

Primary South African government sources name both the Reserve Bank and the Revenue Service as the
partner for the national digital ID. Investigate and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date and
distinguishing hosting from design partnership.
