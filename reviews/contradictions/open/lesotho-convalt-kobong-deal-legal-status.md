# Contradiction — Lesotho / Convalt "Project Kobong": binding agreement or non-binding intent?

**The claim in dispute:** the legal status of the ~$6.2 billion Lesotho–Convalt Energy "Project Kobong"
hydropower + AI-data-centre deal (signed early June 2026).

**Competing values:**

1. **A binding Memorandum of Agreement (MoA), signed 4 June 2026.**
   - Asserted by: Tech Africa News (Akim Benamara), 9 June 2026.
   - URL: https://techafricanews.com/2026/06/09/lesotho-unveils-mega-hydropower-and-ai-data-center-project-under-6-2-billion-agreement/

2. **A non-binding Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) / expression of intent**, pending feasibility
   studies and regulatory approvals before any construction commitment.
   - Asserted by: Connecting Africa (Daniel Itai), 30 June 2026.
   - URL: https://www.connectingafrica.com/data-centers/lesotho-s-first-ai-data-center-set-to-shape-the-country-s-digital-economy

**Not in dispute:** the value (~$6.2bn / 98bn maloti), the parties (Lesotho Ministry of Energy + US-based
Convalt Energy), the components (1.2 GW hydropower, an AI data centre in Mokhotlong, plus solar/BESS in
the fuller account), the King Letsie III–Richard Gephardt meeting, and the ~2029 construction horizon.

**What is not held:** neither the signed instrument itself nor an official Lesotho-government or Convalt
statement specifying its legal character is on file — only the two secondary reports above. This is itself
a provenance gap on the primary.

**Instruction:** these two outlets characterise the same June-2026 deal differently — one as a *binding*
agreement already signed, the other as a *non-binding* letter of intent pending studies. Investigate the
discrepancy and determine the instrument's actual legal status, recording an as-of date; the practical
question is whether this is a committed project or an early-stage intent.
