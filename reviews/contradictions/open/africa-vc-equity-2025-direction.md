# Contradiction: did African tech equity funding rise or fall in 2025 — +8% or −21%?

**Opened:** 2026-07-20 (surfaced by the ingest of the Partech and Disrupt Africa primaries, itself a product of
the 2026-07-20 reconcile pass).

## Why this is sharper than the contradiction it replaced

The just-closed `africa-tech-funding-2025` item resolved cleanly: Partech and Disrupt Africa measure
different universes in different units (equity+debt by deal vs a minimum floor by funded venture), so their
2.5× gap was never a dispute. **This one is not like that.** Two trackers report the *same* quantity —
total **equity** venture funding into African technology companies for CY2025 — and disagree on
**direction**, not merely level.

## The dispute

- **Claim A — equity UP 8% year-on-year, to US$2.4bn** across 462 deals.
  - Asserted by: **Partech Partners**, *2025 Partech Africa Tech VC Report*, published **2026-01-22**.
  - Source URL held: https://partechpartners.com/news/2025-partech-africa-tech-vc-report-african-tech-funding-rebounds-to-us41b-driven-by-record-debt-activity-and-disciplined-equity-growth
  - Held at: `raw/2026-01-22-partech-africa-tech-vc-report-2025.md` (the publisher's own release).

- **Claim B — equity DOWN 21% year-on-year, to US$2.1bn.**
  - Asserted by: **AVCA** (African Private Equity and Venture Capital Association), *Venture Capital in
    Africa Report*, **February 2026**.
  - Source URL held: https://www.avca.africa/media/umlh132j/avca25-15-vc-annual-report_4.pdf
  - **The PDF itself is not held** — that is a provenance gap. The figure reaches the wiki via the reconcile
    pass's reading of it.

Both present the figure as total African VC **equity** for CY2025.

## Why scope alone cannot explain it

A difference in *level* between trackers is ordinary — coverage, deal-size floors and geography tests
differ. A difference in **sign** is not, because each is comparing 2025 against its **own** 2024 baseline.
For both to be right, the two 2024 baselines would have to differ enough to flip the derivative — which is
itself a finding worth establishing, and would mean neither year-on-year figure should be quoted without
its base.

## Context that constrains any reconciliation

At least four incompatible CY2025 series circulate on adjacent definitions:

| Tracker | Total | Deals | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Partech | US$4.1bn | 570 | equity + debt, by deal |
| AVCA | US$3.9bn | 506 | (basis to establish) |
| TechCabal Insights | US$3.42bn | 502 | (basis to establish) |
| Disrupt Africa | US$1.64bn | 178 | self-declared minimum floor, by funded venture |

## What to establish

1. **What each publisher counts as an equity venture deal** — stage range, deal-size floor, company age,
   geography test (Africa-**headquartered** vs Africa-**operating**), and whether corporate venture and
   growth equity are in scope.
2. **What 2024 base figure each subtracts from**, stated explicitly. This is the crux: the sign depends on
   the base, not only the 2025 number.
3. **Whether either publisher has revised a prior-year figure** — a silent rebase would explain the flip
   entirely, and this corpus has already found one such rebase this month (DataReportal's penetration
   series).

## Instruction

Two investment trackers report opposite directions of travel for the same quantity — total equity venture
funding into African technology companies in calendar year 2025 — one at **+8% to US$2.4bn**, the other at
**−21% to US$2.1bn**. Investigate the discrepancy and suggest a resolution, recording for each figure its
definition of an equity venture deal, its stated 2024 base, an as-of date, and whether any prior-year
figure has been revised. State which, if either, is the more defensible headline for African tech equity
funding in CY2025 — or whether the two series are simply not comparable, in which case say what each is
good for.
