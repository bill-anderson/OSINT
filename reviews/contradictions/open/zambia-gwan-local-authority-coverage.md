# Contradiction — how many of Zambia's local authorities are connected to GWAN, and when

## Internal note (not part of the brief)

Two held sources describe the coverage of Zambia's Government Wide Area Network (GWAN) across local
authorities in ways that are hard to reconcile. Affected pages set to `needs-review`:
`wiki/intersections/zambia--dpi-govtech.md`.

*Distinct from* the ZamPortal onboarding count (Lusaka City Council "first of 116 councils", May 2026)
— network connection and service-portal onboarding are different measures and are not themselves in
conflict.

---

## Research brief (paste-ready)

**Claim in dispute:** how many of Zambia's 116 local authorities are connected to the Government Wide
Area Network (GWAN), and as of when.

**Competing accounts:**

1. **All 116 local authorities were connected during 2025.** Asserted by wearetech.africa (17 July
   2026), reporting that Zambia's Smart Zambia Institute announced in 2025 that it had connected all
   116 of the country's local authorities to the Government Wide Area Network.
   Source held: https://www.wearetech.africa/fr/fils/actualites/telecom/connectivite-gouvernementale-la-zambie-lance-un-reseau-4g-prive

2. **Local authorities in North-Western Province were connected as of July 2026 — presented as a new
   milestone.** Asserted by the Smart Zambia Institute itself, via reporting of 8 July 2026 stating
   that all local authorities and post offices in Zambia's North-Western Province "have now been
   connected" through the Government Wide Area Network using fibre-optic infrastructure.
   Source held: https://techafricanews.com/2026/07/08/smart-zambia-expands-digital-infrastructure-to-reach-every-corner-of-the-country/

**Why this matters:** the two accounts imply very different states of national coverage, and the
second is the agency's own later statement, which would normally out-rank a third-party summary of an
earlier claim.

**Instruction:** these sources report different states of Government Wide Area Network coverage across
Zambia's local authorities. Investigate the discrepancy — check whether the 2025 announcement covered
all 116 authorities or only a subset, whether "connected" meant something different in each case
(e.g. a link established versus fibre delivered versus a service live), and whether any subsequent
official statement gives a current figure. Suggest a resolution and record an as-of date for each
value you can substantiate.
