# Contradiction: Nigeria's 2008 market loss in dollars — $55.03bn or $5.03bn? (plus two smaller conversion mismatches)

**Opened:** 2026-07-19 (daily-2026-07-19 sweep batch, payments/fintech chunk)

## The dispute

Unusually, all competing values come from **the same article**, which states each figure twice with
different numbers. The naira figures are consistent throughout; the **dollar conversions are not**, and
in one case the discrepancy is a factor of ten. The wiki has therefore compiled the **naira figures
only** and left every disputed dollar value out of its synthesis pages, recording the inconsistency
here rather than silently picking one.

### Claim 1 — the 2008 crash loss (the material one)

The naira value is stated identically in both places: **₦6.96 trillion** lost between March and
December 2008.

- **Claim A — $55.03 billion**, explicitly at "the then exchange rate of ₦126.48/$".
- **Claim B — $5.03 billion**, stated later in the same piece with no rate given.

Arithmetic favours A: ₦6.96tn ÷ 126.48 ≈ **$55.0bn**. B appears to be a dropped digit, but that is an
inference, not a verified correction, and the wiki does not correct a source by arithmetic alone.

- Asserted by: **TechCabal** (Temitayo Jaiyeola), published 2026-07-18.
- Source URL held:
  https://techcabal.com/2026/07/18/their-parents-lost-fortunes-theyre-buying-nigerian-stocks-anyway/
- **No second source is held for either value.** That is itself a provenance gap: a widely-cited
  historical loss figure resting on one trade-press account with an internal contradiction in it.

### Claim 2 — NGX market capitalisation (minor)

- **$113.79 billion**, stated in the article's prose.
- **$113.02 billion**, stated in an embedded interactive data widget in the same article.
- Same source and URL as above. Roughly a 0.7% divergence — likely different capture dates, but
  neither is dated.

### Claim 3 — institutional trading volume conversion (minor)

- Retail volume is given as **₦2.86 trillion ($2.07 billion)** — implying roughly **₦1,382/$**.
- Institutional volume is given as **₦4.06 trillion ($4.06 billion)** — implying **₦1,000/$**, which is
  inconsistent with the retail conversion and with the ~₦1,500/$ the article's own widget cites
  elsewhere. At the retail rate, ₦4.06tn would be ~$2.94bn.
- Same source and URL as above. Both underlying naira figures are attributed to the **Nigerian
  Exchange (NGX) Domestic & Foreign Portfolio Report (May 2026)**, which is **not held**.

## Likely reconciliation to test

Most probably three editing errors rather than three factual disputes — a dropped digit in Claim 1, a
stale widget value in Claim 2, and a naira figure mistakenly carried into the dollar column in Claim 3.
But each needs an independent value before the wiki carries any dollar figure, and Claim 1 in
particular is the kind of round number that propagates uncorrected for years once repeated.

Acquiring the **NGX Domestic & Foreign Portfolio Report (May 2026)** would settle Claims 2 and 3
directly and is worth doing on its own merits — it is the recurring primary behind the retail-investing
series. Claim 1 needs a contemporaneous 2008–09 source (regulator, exchange or financial press) rather
than a 2026 retrospective.

## Instruction

A single article gives two different dollar values for the same ₦6.96 trillion loss in Nigeria's 2008
market crash — $55.03 billion and $5.03 billion — and two further internally inconsistent dollar
conversions for NGX market capitalisation and institutional trading volume. Investigate the
discrepancies against independent sources, establish which values are correct, and record an as-of date
and an exchange rate for each dollar figure.
