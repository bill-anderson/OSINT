# Contradiction: how many Senegalese civil-status records have been digitised — 19 million or 15.3 million?

**Opened:** 2026-07-16 (NGA·RWA·SDN·SEN batch)

## The dispute

Two dated official figures for the count of digitised civil-status/birth records point in an odd
direction — the **newer** figure is **lower** than the older one.

- **Claim A — 19 million (as of June 2024).**
  - Asserted by: Minister Moussa Bala Fofana (25 June 2024), via We Are Tech Africa.
  - Source URL held: https://www.wearetech.africa/en/fils-uk/news/tech/senegal-digitizes-millions-of-civil-records-to-improve-citizen-rights-and-public-policy
- **Claim B — 15.3 million (as of July 2026).**
  - Asserted by: ANEC (15 July 2026), via Biometric Update.
  - Source URL held: (held on file as the raw source "2026-07-15 Senegal digitizes more than 15 million birth records in civil registration overhaul".)

## Likely reconciliation to test

Probably different counting bases rather than a real regression: "records digitised/indexed/scanned"
(19m) vs "validated civil-status entries / de-duplicated birth records" (15.3m). Confirm the
definition behind each figure and its as-of date before treating either as the current count.

## Instruction

These sources report different totals for Senegal's digitised civil records. Investigate the
discrepancy (likely a counting-base difference) and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date and
definition for each figure.
