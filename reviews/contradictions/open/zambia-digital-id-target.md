# Contradiction: when will Zambia issue digital IDs — end-2026 or end-2027?

**Opened:** 2026-07-17 (ZAF·ZMB·ZWE batch)

## The dispute

Two Zambian officials give different target dates, and — the analytically interesting part — **the
disagreement runs along institutional lines**: the agency that actually runs registration says later
than the agency that runs digital transformation.

- **Claim A — end of 2027.**
  - Asserted by: **Davies Chikalanga**, Registrar General, Department of National Registration, Passport
    and Citizenship (DNRPC) — *the body that operates civil registration and ID issuance* — speaking at
    ID4Africa 2025: "our aim is to have digital IDs in place for the population by the end of 2027."
  - Source URL held:
    https://www.biometricupdate.com/202506/zambia-says-using-multiple-strategies-to-meet-50-percent-birth-registration-goal-by-2026
    (2025-06-12)
- **Claim B — end of 2026.**
  - Asserted by: **Percy Chinyama**, National Coordinator, SMART Zambia Institute — *the
    digital-transformation agency* — in a press release during a working visit to the United States: "By
    the end of 2026, Zambia will have digital identification cards."
  - Source URL held:
    https://www.biometricupdate.com/202601/zambia-programs-national-digital-id-rollout-for-year-end
    (2026-01-05)
  - Corroborated by held material: `raw/2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project.md`
    records SMART Zambia targeting full rollout and 80% of citizens by end-2026.

## Why recency should not simply settle it

A newest-wins reading takes end-2026. But two held items point the other way:
`raw/2025-12-28 Over 1.3 Million Citizens Enrolled on INRIS.md` (ZNBC) reports biometric NRC issuance
coming only **after the 2026 General Elections**, and the held April-2026 Internet Freedom material
already flags a **post-election INRIS deferral**. Chikalanga's 2027 may simply be the operator's more
realistic view of his own programme.

## What to establish, with an as-of date

1. Whether "digital IDs in place" (Chikalanga) and "digital identification cards" (Chinyama) mean the
   same deliverable — a credential, a card, or a live system.
2. Whether either date has since moved, and what the 2026 general elections did to the schedule.
3. Which body actually owns the issuance milestone — DNRPC or SMART Zambia.

## Instruction

Two Zambian agencies give different target dates for the same digital-ID rollout, and the operating
registrar is the more pessimistic. Investigate the discrepancy and suggest a resolution, recording an
as-of date for each claim and noting which body owns the milestone.
