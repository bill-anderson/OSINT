# Who announced Ghana's mandatory biometric-verification amendment, and who heads the NIA?

Surfaced 2026-07-20 during dedup of a third account of the same announcement. Two sources
already on file, published the **same day** on the **same event**, name **different people** as
the National Identification Authority's Executive Secretary. Low stakes individually, but the
name is load-bearing for any future attribution of NIA statements, and one of the two is wrong.

## Paste-ready research brief

Two published reports of the same 15 July 2026 announcement in Ghana — the coming into force of
an amendment to the National Identity Register Regulations, 2012 (L.I. 2111), making biometric
verification the only lawful means of verifying a Ghana Card holder during a transaction —
attribute the announcement to two different officials:

- **"Yayra Koku", Executive Secretary of the National Identification Authority**, quoted from a
  Facebook post of Wednesday 15 July 2026, stating "biometric verification is now MANDATORY" and
  that the L.I. 2111 amendment was "gazetted today, Wednesday, July 15, 2026".
  Asserted by: MyJoyOnline —
  https://www.myjoyonline.com/biometric-verification-now-mandatory-photocopying-or-visual-inspection-of-ghana-card-for-transactions-illegal/

- **"Wisdom Kwaku Deku", Executive Secretary and Chief Executive Officer of the National
  Identification Authority**, announcing on Wednesday, 15 July 2026 that the amendment had been
  gazetted.
  Asserted by: Graphic Online —
  https://www.graphic.com.gh/news/general-news/photocopying-ghana-card-now-attracts-fines-of-up-to-ghc24-000.html

A **third** account of the same measure gives a different date for the gazetting and a different
date for the announcement, without naming the official:

- The amended identity law "was gazetted **last month**" (i.e. June 2026), with the NIA making its
  announcement on **16 July 2026**. Asserted by: Biometric Update —
  https://www.biometricupdate.com/202607/mandatory-biometric-verification-for-ghana-card-holders-takes-effect

No source on file states the NIA Executive Secretary's name from an official NIA or Government of
Ghana channel; both names come from news reports. That absence is itself part of the finding.

**These sources report different names for the head of the same authority, and different dates for
the gazetting of the same instrument — investigate the discrepancy and suggest a resolution,
recording an as-of date for each value.** The two questions to settle are (1) who held the post of
NIA Executive Secretary on 15 July 2026, and (2) the actual gazette date of the L.I. 2111
amendment.
