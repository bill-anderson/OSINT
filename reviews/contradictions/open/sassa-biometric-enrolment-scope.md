# Contradiction: does SASSA's mandatory biometric enrolment cover all grant beneficiaries, or only some?

**Opened:** 2026-07-17 (ZAF·ZMB·ZWE batch)

## The dispute

The scope question is not administrative trivia — it is precisely what is being litigated at the Supreme
Court of Appeal. Whether biometrics gate **the undocumented** or **everyone** decides who is excluded.

- **Claim A — narrow, from 5 May 2025.**
  - Biometrics apply to new clients who use "**alternative forms of identification** other than the
    standard 13-digit South African ID number", to existing clients undergoing reviews or changes of
    personal information, and to proxies. Explicitly: "**Existing grant recipients using their standard
    13-digit South African ID number will not be affected.**"
  - Asserted by: **SASSA spokesperson Paseka Letsatsi**, via The Citizen (Jarryd Westerdale), 2025-04-24.
  - Source URL held:
    https://www.citizen.co.za/news/south-africa/new-id-verification-process-sassa-grants-which-recipients
- **Claim B — universal, from 1 September 2025.**
  - Headline: "SASSA begins **mandatory biometric enrollment for all grant beneficiaries**." Body: "From
    September 1, **all new and updated grant applications** will require biometric data collection."
  - Asserted by: ID Tech (Ji-seo Kim), 2025-08-25, quoting SASSA CEO **Themba Matlou**, who concedes the
    plan was to start at the beginning of FY2025/26 "however, we hit a snag".
  - Source URL held:
    https://idtechwire.com/sassa-to-launch-mandatory-biometric-enrollment-for-grant-beneficiaries-on-september-1

## Assessment

Probably **one slipped-and-widened programme rather than two**, and note that **B's headline overreaches
B's own body**: "all grant beneficiaries" versus "all new and updated applications", the latter being
close to A's scope. But the **start dates differ (5 May vs 1 September)**, Matlou's "we hit a snag"
implies the May date slipped, and the two scopes are not identical — A protects existing 13-digit-ID
recipients explicitly, B does not repeat that carve-out.

The wiki must not let B's headline stand as "all beneficiaries" without establishing it.

## What to establish, with an as-of date

1. The actual commencement date, and whether the May start happened at all.
2. Whether existing recipients using a standard 13-digit ID are now required to enrol biometrically.
3. What "updated grant applications" covers — any change of detail, or a review cycle.
4. How many beneficiaries fall in the "alternative identification" category, since they are the cohort
   the exclusion litigation concerns.

## Instruction

These sources give different scopes and different start dates for the same biometric-enrolment
requirement. Investigate and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date for each and establishing
precisely which beneficiaries are covered.
