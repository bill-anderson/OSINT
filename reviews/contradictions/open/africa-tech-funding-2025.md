# Contradiction: how much did African tech raise in 2025 — $1.64bn or $4.1bn?

**Opened:** 2026-07-17 (ZAF·ZMB·ZWE batch)

## The dispute

Two funding trackers, reported **one day apart**, give totals for the same calendar year that differ by
**2.5×**. The wiki now holds both.

- **Claim A — $1.64 billion, up 46.2%, across 178 funded startups (CY2025).**
  - Asserted by: **Disrupt Africa**, via ITWeb (Sibahle Malinga), 2026-02-27.
  - Source URL held: https://www.itweb.co.za/article/sa-powers-africas-164bn-tech-funding-rebound/4r1lyMR9YJR7pmda
  - Disrupt Africa's own methodology note calls its figure "**a minimum funding benchmark**", built from
    publicly disclosed rounds plus polling, with conservative estimates applied to undisclosed rounds.
- **Claim B — $4.1 billion, up 25% (CY2025).**
  - Asserted by: **Partech**, via Invest Africa, 2026-02-26.
  - Held at: `raw/2026-02-26 The AfDB's push for African financial sovereignty, and a tech investment rebound.md`
  - Source URL held: https://www.investafrica.com/insights-/anewfinancialarchitectureforafrica

## Assessment

Almost certainly **methodological rather than an error by either**: Partech typically counts equity plus
debt across a wider deal universe, while Disrupt Africa explicitly floors its own number. A striking
point in favour of that reading — **the two agree closely on structure even as they diverge on level**:
Partech puts the "big four" (South Africa, Nigeria, Egypt, Kenya) at **72%** of the total; Disrupt Africa
at **72.5%**. Trackers measuring different universes would not normally converge that tightly on share.
They also agree on direction: a rebound.

The practical consequence matters more than the resolution: **the wiki must never state a bare "Africa
raised $X in 2025".** Every such figure must name the tracker and the date.

## What to establish

1. What each tracker counts — equity only vs equity plus debt; disclosed vs estimated; minimum deal size;
   whether exits, grants and ICOs are in scope.
2. Whether a like-for-like comparison is possible at all, or whether the two series are simply not
   commensurable.
3. Which, if either, the wiki should adopt as its citable series — and on what stated basis.

## Instruction

Two trackers report African tech funding for the same year with a 2.5× gap, while agreeing closely on
the big-four share. Investigate and suggest a resolution, recording what each figure counts, and advise
whether the two series can be compared at all.
