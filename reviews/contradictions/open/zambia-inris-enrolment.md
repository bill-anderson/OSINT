# Contradiction: how many Zambians are enrolled on INRIS — and why does the figure fall over time?

**Opened:** 2026-07-17 (ZAF·ZMB·ZWE batch)

## The dispute

A **later** source reports a **lower** enrolment figure than an earlier one, on the same register. That
is not supersession — on a register that only accumulates, enrolment cannot go backwards without an
explanation, and none is offered.

- **Claim A — 1.5 million people enrolled with biometrics on INRIS** (as of September 2024).
  - Asserted by: **UNDP** (Rachel Chang and Tariq Malik), citing Zambia's DNRPC. UNDP is a direct
    participant — it designed the Legal Digital ID Model Governance Assessment — so this is a first-hand
    implementer account.
  - Source URL held:
    https://www.undp.org/digital/blog/dpi-safeguards-come-online-zambias-next-phase-digital-integration
  - The same piece frames the gap as "only 1.5 out of 9 million people".
- **Claim B — approximately 1.3 million citizens enrolled onto INRIS** (as of December 2025).
  - Asserted by: **Mwala Kalaluka**, Principal Public Relations Officer, Ministry of Home Affairs and
    Internal Security, via ZNBC (Joshua Jere).
  - Source URL held: https://znbc.co.zm/?p=10211

Held material sides with the **older, higher** figure: the World Bank DZAP PAD (2025-09-11, held at
`raw/2025-09-11 DZAP World Bank PAD.md`) records INRIS as having "registered ~15% of adults
biometrically" — which on that document's own base (~20m population, 46% under 15, so ~10.8m adults)
implies roughly **1.6 million**.

The sequence therefore reads **1.5m (Sep 2024) → ~1.6m implied (Sep 2025) → 1.3m (Dec 2025)**. A
newest-wins rule would install the **lowest** number as current state.

## What to establish, with an as-of date for each

1. The actual INRIS enrolment count at each date, from DNRPC or MoHAIS directly.
2. Whether the register was **de-duplicated** between 2024 and 2025 — the most likely benign
   explanation, and one that would make the fall real rather than erroneous.
3. Whether the figures use **different bases** (all persons vs adults vs citizens vs 16+).
4. Whether "enrolled with biometrics" (UNDP) and "enrolled onto INRIS" (ZNBC) denote the same act.

## Instruction

These sources report different enrolment totals for the same national register, with the later source
reporting a lower figure than the earlier one and than the World Bank's implied figure in between.
Investigate the discrepancy and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date for each value and
establishing whether any de-duplication or change of base explains the fall.
