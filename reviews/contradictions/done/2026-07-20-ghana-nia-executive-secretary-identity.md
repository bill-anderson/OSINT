# Who announced Ghana's mandatory biometric-verification amendment, and who heads the NIA?

## RESOLUTION (2026-07-20, attempt 1) — both questions settled from primaries

### Q1 — the officeholder: NOT a contradiction. One man, two renderings of his name.

**"Yayra Koku" and "Wisdom Kwaku Deku" are the same person.** His full name is rendered
**Wisdom Yayra Koku Deku**; Ghanaian outlets commonly use the short form *Yayra Koku*, while the NIA's
own site uses *Wisdom Kwaku Deku*. Neither report was wrong about the person — MyJoyOnline used the
familiar name and Graphic Online the official one, and dedup read two names as two officeholders.

Evidence:
- **Primary — NIA's own site:** `nia.gov.gh/our-governing-board-managment-members/wisdom-kwaku-deku/`
  names **Mr. Wisdom Kwaku Deku** as Executive Secretary (observed 2026-07-20). Ingested as
  `raw/2025-01-23-nia-executive-secretary-wisdom-kwaku-deku.md`.
- **Bridging evidence — BBC News, 16 July 2026:** *"said **Wisdom Yayra Koku Deku**, the NIA chief"* —
  the full name, joining both renderings. Ingested as
  `raw/2026-07-16-ghana-card-digital-checks-compulsory-bbc.md`.

**Residual, stated on the page as a dated vacuum:** whether the appointment is **acting or substantive**
is **not established**. The NIA's own site is internally inconsistent as observed 2026-07-20 — the
holder's profile page says "Executive Secretary", the homepage and the Governing Board & Management
listing both say "**Acting** Executive Secretary". The wiki holds no appointment instrument or Public
Services Commission record. Recorded on `wiki/entities/national-identification-authority-ghana.md`.

### Q2 — the gazette date: **neither** 15 July 2026 **nor** June 2026. It was **27 March 2026**.

The instrument is not loosely "an amendment to L.I. 2111" but the **National Identity Register
(Amendment) Regulations, 2026 — L.I. 2523**. Per the **NIA's own press release of 16 July 2026**
(issued in Accra, signed by Williams Ampomah Emmanuel Darlas, Head, Corporate Affairs Directorate):

- **Signed** by the sector Minister, **Muntaka Mohammed Mubarak**, on **27 March 2026**, under
  **s.73(1), National Identity Register Act, 2008 (Act 750)**, as amended.
- **Gazetted the same day — 27 March 2026.**
- **Came into force 9 June 2026.**
- **Publicised 16 July 2026** (press release), the day after the Executive Secretary's Facebook post.

Both held datings are explained and corrected:
- **"Gazetted today, Wednesday, July 15, 2026"** (MyJoyOnline, quoting Deku's Facebook post; Graphic
  Online same day) — **wrong, and the error originated with the announcing official**, not the press.
  15 July is the date of the post, nothing else.
- **"Gazetted last month"** i.e. June 2026 (Biometric Update) — **conflates gazetting with commencement**.
  June is the **9 June** coming-into-force date.

**This is not a third live value.** RECONCILE's third-value rule guards against picking between
competing *secondary* accounts; here the institution's own signed press release — carrying the
instrument number, the signing minister, the enabling section and all three dates — supersedes news
reports that a primary can settle. Both news datings are explicable errors, not rival evidence.

## Original brief (as opened 2026-07-20)

Surfaced 2026-07-20 during dedup of a third account of the same announcement. Two sources
already on file, published the **same day** on the **same event**, name **different people** as
the National Identification Authority's Executive Secretary. Low stakes individually, but the
name is load-bearing for any future attribution of NIA statements, and one of the two is wrong.

### Paste-ready research brief

Two published reports of the same 15 July 2026 announcement in Ghana — the coming into force of
an amendment to the National Identity Register Regulations, 2012 (L.I. 2111), making biometric
verification the only lawful means of verifying a Ghana Card holder during a transaction —
attribute the announcement to two different officials:

- **"Yayra Koku", Executive Secretary of the National Identification Authority**, quoted from a
  Facebook post of Wednesday 15 July 2026, stating "biometric verification is now MANDATORY" and
  that the L.I. 2111 amendment was "gazetted today, Wednesday, July 15, 2026".
  Asserted by: MyJoyOnline —
  https://www.myjoyonline.com/biometric-verification-now-mandatory-photocopying-or-visual-inspection-of-ghana-card-for-transactions-illegal/

- **"Wisdom Kwaku Deku", Executive Secretary and Chief Executive Officer of the National
  Identification Authority**, announcing on Wednesday, 15 July 2026 that the amendment had been
  gazetted.
  Asserted by: Graphic Online —
  https://www.graphic.com.gh/news/general-news/photocopying-ghana-card-now-attracts-fines-of-up-to-ghc24-000.html

A **third** account of the same measure gives a different date for the gazetting and a different
date for the announcement, without naming the official:

- The amended identity law "was gazetted **last month**" (i.e. June 2026), with the NIA making its
  announcement on **16 July 2026**. Asserted by: Biometric Update —
  https://www.biometricupdate.com/202607/mandatory-biometric-verification-for-ghana-card-holders-takes-effect

No source on file states the NIA Executive Secretary's name from an official NIA or Government of
Ghana channel; both names come from news reports. That absence is itself part of the finding.

**These sources report different names for the head of the same authority, and different dates for
the gazetting of the same instrument — investigate the discrepancy and suggest a resolution,
recording an as-of date for each value.** The two questions to settle are (1) who held the post of
NIA Executive Secretary on 15 July 2026, and (2) the actual gazette date of the L.I. 2111
amendment.
