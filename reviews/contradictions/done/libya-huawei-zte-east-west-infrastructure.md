## Resolution (2026-07-20)

**All three confirmed, with two corrections to the brief and one disambiguation.**

1. **Huawei suspension — and its reversal.** The GNU's **General Authority for Telecommunications and
   Informatics (GTI)** suspended all Huawei activities on **2025-08-17**, citing "illegal contracts with
   illegitimate entities", breach of **Law No. 22 of 2010** (sole GTI licensing power) and violation of
   **ITU** sovereignty principles. **CORRECTION: the suspension was lifted on 2025-08-20** — three days
   later — when the GTI announced agreement with **Huawei Technizon – Libya Branch** on a compliance
   undertaking. The brief carried only the suspension. Primary now held (LANA, the state news agency).
   **DISAMBIGUATION: "Ozon" is not the network.** **OZON Aljaded For Telecom and Technology Group** is the
   licensee, granted a unified telecoms licence by the **HoR-appointed** Ministry of Communications,
   effective **2025-03-04**. The eastern network is reported as **"Barqa 217"**, Benghazi-launched, tied
   to the Libya Development and Reconstruction Fund. The **USD 700m** contract and **USD 198m** CBL letter
   of credit rest on **single-outlet** reporting (Libya Security Monitor) — recorded on the page as
   reported, not established.
2. **Trans-Sahara / Tatweer data centre — confirmed.** Huawei **FusionModule1000A** containerised
   facility for **Tripoli, early 2027**; **Trans-Sahara** (private, HB Group) in JV with **Tatweer
   Research** (public, owned by the Libyan Local Investment & Development Fund); private cloud for
   government agencies and local companies; **PCI DSS** certification sought. No capacity figure published.
   **New finding:** this realises a partnership **first signed in Benghazi in October 2019** for a
   **Tier-3** data centre — scaled down over seven years to one prefabricated module.
3. **LTT–ZTE MoU — confirmed, date corrected.** Signed **2026-03-09** at MWC Barcelona (**not 2026-03-11**,
   which is The Register's partner-content republication date) by LTT chairman **Ammar Albouzaidi** and ZTE
   SVP **Zhang Wanchun**. Scope: 4G/5G, e-wallet/e-commerce/CDN, FWA/CPE/MiFi terminals. **No value, no
   timetable, non-binding.** Primary now held (ZTE's own release).

**The analytical answer to "which vendor built which segment":** the question is mis-framed. It is **not**
a vendor-per-region split — **Huawei works both sides** (20+ years in Libya, 3G/4G rollout, and the
Tripoli data-centre module), and ZTE's MoU is west-side. The contest is over **which authority may license
a vendor**: the rupture came only where Huawei contracted with the eastern administration, and closed as
soon as it acknowledged GTI licensing primacy. Written up as "The east–west vendor contest" in
`wiki/intersections/libya--infra-connect.md`, whose lead previously said the connectivity picture was
"All GNU/Tripoli-side" — that gap is now closed.
