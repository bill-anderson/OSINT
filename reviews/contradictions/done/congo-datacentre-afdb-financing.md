# Contradiction: what is the AfDB's co-financing of the Congo-Brazzaville National Data Centre — $13 million or $57 million?

**Opened:** 2026-07-18 (COG batch)

## The dispute

Two sources give different figures for the **African Development Bank's contribution** to Congo-Brazzaville's
**National Data Centre** (Brazzaville, Bacongo — the CAB "first sovereign data centre in Central Africa").

- **Claim A — AfDB co-financing of ~$57 million, within a $72.8m total ($57m AfDB + $15.8m government),
  a figure that *includes* a 600-km fibre backbone to Cameroon and CAR.**
  - Asserted by: Techpoint Africa, 2024-05-20.
  - Source URL held: https://techpoint.africa/news/congo-central-africas-first-fibre-data-centre
- **Claim B — the data-centre works are "cofinancé par la BAD à 13 millions de dollars."**
  - Asserted by: Les Dépêches de Brazzaville (ADIAC), 2025-05-29 (recurs in the outlet's 2024 coverage).
  - Source URL held: https://www.adiac-congo.com/content/construction-du-datacenter-la-bad-sindigne-du-ralentissement-des-travaux-165004

## The question

These sources report different values for the AfDB's contribution to the same project. Investigate the
discrepancy and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date for each figure. A plausible reconciliation
(not confirmed): the **$57m/$72.8m** may be the *total project* envelope — the data-centre building **plus**
the 600-km fibre backbone — while the recurring **$13m** may be the AfDB's share of the **building alone**.
The primary is the AfDB project record / loan agreement for the Central African Backbone (CAB) Congo
component. Both figures are held dated and attributed pending that primary.
