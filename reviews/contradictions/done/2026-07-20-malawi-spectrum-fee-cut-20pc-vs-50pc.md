# Malawi spectrum licence fee reduction: 20% or 50%?

## Resolution (2026-07-20, attempt 1)

**One reduction, two magnitudes. The wiki carries 20%.**

**The 20% side — five independent accounts of one announcement (2025-06-23):**

| Outlet | Published | Value | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Malawi24 | 2025-06-23 | 20% | earliest; AIP model dated 2016 |
| Ecofin Agency | 2025-06-25 | 20% | *"announced on June 23"* — fixes the event date |
| Telecompaper | 2025-06-26 | 20% | wire pickup |
| TechAfrica News *(held)* | 2025-06-26 | 20% | two direct DG quotes |
| Nyasa Times | 2025-07-01 | 20% | AIP model dated 2016 |

**The 50% side — four reports of one speech (2026-07-17):** Nyasa Times, Malawi Voice and
Maravi Post (all 2026-07-18, all placing it "on Friday") and ITWeb Africa (2026-07-20, held,
undated session). Four reports, **one assertion** — not four corroborations.

**Decisive reasoning.** The announcing party's own contemporaneous figure, quoted directly and
carried by five outlets, outranks a minister's recollection of it thirteen months later. Critically,
**no source of any kind records a second or further reduction** between June 2025 and July 2026 —
so the cumulative-vs-single-cut hypothesis has no evidence behind it. CLAUDE.md's "prefer the newest
value" governs *supersession*: a newer **state**. The minister's 50% is not newer state, it is a
later retelling of the same 2025 event, so supersession does not apply.

**Corroborating negative:** MACRA DG Suleman, in a statement a month after the cut
(mwnation, 2025-07-17, *"Data cost down—Macra"*, not ingested — different story), refers to having
"revised the pricing formula for spectrum fees" with **no second cut mentioned**.

## Original research brief (as opened)

**The claim in dispute:** the size of the reduction in radio-frequency spectrum licence fees announced
by Malawi's telecommunications regulator.

**Competing values:**

1. **20%.** A reduction of **20% across all frequency bands**, announced by the **Malawi Communications
   Regulatory Authority (MACRA)**, attributed to Director General **Daud Suleman**, and described as
   following a review of the Administrative Incentive Pricing (AIP) model introduced in 2018, with an
   updated pricing schedule rolled out in 2024.
   - Asserted by: MACRA (via TechAfrica News), 26 June 2025.
   - URL on file: https://techafricanews.com/2025/06/26/malawi-slashes-spectrum-fees-by-20-to-boost-digital-inclusion/

2. **50%.** A **"50% reduction in spectrum licence fees"**, cited by Malawi's **Minister of Information
   and Communication Technology, Shadric Namalomba**, in a parliamentary response, as one of the steps
   government has taken to reduce the cost of digital services.
   - Asserted by: Shadric Namalomba (via ITWeb Africa), 20 July 2026.
   - URL on file: https://itweb.africa/article/malawi-seeks-new-telecom-competition/rW1xLv5n1Xb7Rk6m

**What is not established from the material on file:** whether these describe **one** reduction reported
at two different magnitudes, or **two successive** reductions (a 20% cut in 2025 followed by a further
cut, or a cumulative figure). No primary MACRA fee schedule, gazette notice or annual report is held that
would settle it, and neither account states an effective date for the fee schedule itself. **Nothing
further is on file** — no additional source is held for either value.

**Instruction:** these sources report different values for the same policy measure — investigate the
discrepancy and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date for each value, and establishing whether
one supersedes the other or they are distinct measures. The decisive document would be MACRA's published
radio-frequency spectrum fee schedule (or the amending instrument) and its effective date.
