# Contradiction: Zambia's internet penetration — 21%, 33% or 57%?

**Opened:** 2026-07-17 (ZAF·ZMB·ZWE batch)

## The dispute

Three figures, spanning a **2.7× range**, for what is nominally the same metric. The wiki currently
carries the lowest of them, bare and unqualified, on `wiki/places/ZMB.md`.

- **Claim A — about 21 per 100 people.**
  - Asserted by: the **World Bank**, Digital Zambia Acceleration Project PAD.
  - Held at: `raw/2025-09-11 DZAP World Bank PAD.md` (2025-09-11).
- **Claim B — 56.8%.**
  - Asserted by: **UNDP** (2024-09-17).
  - Source URL held:
    https://www.undp.org/digital/blog/dpi-safeguards-come-online-zambias-next-phase-digital-integration
- **Claim C — 33.0% (7.13 million users, January 2025).**
  - Asserted by: **DataReportal / Kepios** (published 2025-03-03).
  - Source URL held: https://datareportal.com/reports/digital-2025-zambia

## Likely reconciliation to test

Almost certainly **three definitions rather than a factual dispute**: the ITU-family
"individuals using the internet" measure, a ZICTA subscription-based count (which inflates by counting
subscriptions rather than people), and Kepios' modelled estimate. But nothing on file says so, and the
wiki should not carry one of them as though it settled the question.

Establish, with an as-of date and a stated basis for each: what each figure measures, over what
population, from what survey or administrative source. Then decide which the wiki should carry as its
citable value — and write the others as dated alternatives rather than dropping them.

## Instruction

These sources give values ranging from 21% to 56.8% for Zambian internet penetration over an
overlapping period. Investigate the discrepancy and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date and
a definitional basis for each value.
