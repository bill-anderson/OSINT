# Contradiction: Houwiyeti (Mauritania) passport-request share and document-extract counts

**Opened:** 2026-07-18 (MRT batch)

## The dispute

Two dated sources describe usage of Mauritania's national digital-ID / e-government app with figures that do
not reconcile cleanly — most starkly the app's share of passport requests (70% vs ~36%) and the count of
document extracts. It is likely a difference of metric, basis or reporting period rather than a straight
reversal, but the wiki holds both and cannot silently pick one.

- **Claim A — the app carries "70% of passport requests" and processed "622,000+ document extracts in a
  year."**
  - Asserted by: **Biometric Update** (Ayang Macdonald), reporting from **ID4Africa 2025**, dated
    **2025-05-20**.
  - Source URL held:
    https://www.biometricupdate.com/202505/african-nations-making-digital-id-gains-in-the-face-of-common-challenges

- **Claim B — the app's share of passport requests was 24.8% (2024) → 32.9% (2025) → 35.7% (Jan 2026); with
  554,027 services completed remotely, 406,871 cumulative civil-status extracts, 63,384 passport requests,
  59,363 ID-card requests and 24,409 e-certificates, and 1,511,139 total beneficiaries.**
  - Asserted by: **Tech Rek** (Arabic tech outlet), reporting an ANRPTS presentation at a Nouakchott seminar
    (16–17 June 2026), dated **2026-06-21**.
  - Source URL held:
    https://techrek.info/1-5-%d9%85%d9%84%d9%8a%d9%88%d9%86-%d9%85%d8%b3%d8%aa%d9%81%d9%8a%d8%af-%d9%85%d9%86-%d9%87%d9%88%d9%8a%d8%aa%d9%8a-%d9%85%d8%a4%d8%b4%d8%b1%d8%a7%d8%aa-%d8%aa%d9%83%d8%b4%d9%81/

## Assessment

Two readings are plausible and not mutually exclusive:
1. **Different metric.** Claim A's "70% of passport requests" may count passport requests that *touch* the
   platform end-to-end (or a 2025 snapshot at a specific office), while Claim B counts the share *initiated
   via the app*. The two are measuring different things and both could be true at their respective dates.
2. **Re-dating / trajectory.** Claim B's explicit 2024→2026 series (24.8%→32.9%→35.7%) is internally
   consistent and dated to the month; if it is the like-for-like measure, then the "70%" is either an older,
   differently-scoped figure or a mis-statement. The extract counts likely differ on basis too — "622,000+ in
   a year" (a 12-month flow) vs "406,871 cumulative civil-status extracts" (a running total for one document
   type only, alongside 554,027 total remote services).

Both source URLs are held. No third, more authoritative source (e.g. an ANRPTS annual report) is on file.

## What to establish

1. What "70% of passport requests" in the ID4Africa 2025 account actually measures, and as of what date.
2. Whether Claim B's "share of passport requests via the app" is the same metric measured later (a genuine
   change over time) or a different one.
3. The basis of the two extract counts — flow vs cumulative, all documents vs civil-status only — and whether
   they are even comparable.

## Instruction

Two dated sources report different figures for the same Mauritanian digital-ID app — the share of passport
requests it carries (70% vs ~36%) and the number of document extracts. Investigate the discrepancy, determine
whether it is a difference of metric/basis/period or a genuine conflict, and suggest a resolution, recording an
as-of date for each figure.
