# SA Connect Phase 2 — the ">R3 billion" figure cannot be an FY2025/26 budget line

Filed 2026-07-22 by the budget extraction pass (ZAF FY2024/25 corpus). Attempts: 1. **RESOLVED 2026-07-22.**

## Resolution (2026-07-22, reconcile pass)

The ">R3 billion" is the **cumulative two-year SA Connect Phase 2 allocation announced at Budget 2023**
— 2023/24 R1.1bn (cut 15% to R935m) + 2024/25 R1.9bn — per parliamentary answer **NW3503** (2023-12-12,
with the entity split: BBI R1.36bn, Sentech R566m, SITA R576m, balance to Phase-1 maintenance), cited
retrospectively in Budget-2026 reporting ("supported by a R3 billion allocation over the MTEF that was
announced in 2023", ITWeb 2026-02-25 quoting the Budget Review). It was never an FY2025/26 line: NW5126
(2025-09-19) confirms Phase 2 was suspended in FY2025/26 pending a Treasury rollover with only R710m in
the MTEF, and the ENE 2024 MTEF put the Broadband subprogramme's 2025/26 estimate at R267.4m.

**Applied:** the held FY2025/26 record (`zaf-2025-26-dcdt-sa-connect-phase-2-unclear`) retired — its
figure was a mis-attributed cumulative total; the FY2024/25 slice is held at document grain
(`zaf-2024-25-vote-30-sa-connect-appropriated`, R1,894.6m, with the R3bn context on the record).
Three primaries staged for ingest: NW3503, NW5126, ITWeb Budget-2026. Residual tension held dated, not
resolved: the Budget Review 2026 declares Phase 2 "complete" (6,343 facilities, 32,055 hotspots) in the
same year officials describe it as stalled awaiting rollover — both positions now on file.

## The claim in dispute

The scale and fiscal-year attribution of South Africa's **SA Connect Phase 2**
funding: reporting around the 2026 Budget speech attributes "more than R3 billion"
to SA Connect Phase 2 in FY2025/26, but the government's own budget documents make
a single-year line of that size impossible.

**multi-year Phase 2 programme total** (or includes off-vote/entity financing),
announced at Budget 2026 — but no held source states which.
