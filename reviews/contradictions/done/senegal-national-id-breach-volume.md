# Contradiction: how much data was taken in Senegal's Feb-2026 national-ID (DAF) breach — and was anything leaked at all?

**Opened:** 2026-07-16 (NGA·RWA·SDN·SEN batch)

## The dispute

Sources disagree on the **volume of data exfiltrated** in the ransomware attack on Senegal's national-ID production directorate (DAF), and even on **whether a genuine leak occurred**.

- **Claim A — ~139 terabytes.** A ransomware/leak-site claim put the stolen volume at **139 TB**.
  - Asserted by: the "Green Blood Group" leak-site claim, reported via The Gambia Journal and Biometric Update.
  - Source URLs held: https://www.biometricupdate.com/202602/senegal-data-breach-disrupts-national-id-issuance
- **Claim B — ~139 gigabytes** (three orders of magnitude smaller).
  - Asserted by: Security Affairs (Pierluigi Paganini) and Dark Reading.
  - Source URLs held: https://securityaffairs.com/187811/data-breach/senegal-shuts-national-id-office-after-ransomware-attack.html ; https://www.brightdefense.com/news/senegal-id-system-breach/
- **Claim C — no confirmed leak; systems intact.** An official communiqué stated no citizen data was confirmed leaked.
  - Asserted by: the DAF (Commissioner Ibrahima Dieng), via APS/RTS.
  - Source URL held: (official DAF/APS communiqué — no clean standalone URL on file; provenance gap.)

## Likely reconciliation to test

"139 TB" vs "139 GB" looks like a unit error propagated from the leak-site claim; the official line may be minimising, or the leak-site claim inflating. Establish: (1) the credible exfiltration volume and its unit; (2) whether the leak-site sample was verified; (3) the authorities' confirmed position and date.

## Instruction

These sources report different values (and existence) for the data taken in the Senegal DAF breach. Investigate the discrepancy and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date for each claim.
