## Resolution (2026-07-20)

**Resolved: there is no standalone successor document. Digital Tunisia 2020-2025 lapsed, and the current
framing is a chapter of a general development plan.**

Searched in French and Arabic. No formal extension, repeal or outcomes report for the 2020-2025 plan was
found. The current national digital framing is the **"vision nationale unifiée pour la transformation
digitale" carried inside the *plan de développement 2026-2030***, stated in those terms by Head of
Government **Sarra Zaafrani Zenzri** on **2026-02-13**. Preparation of the ICT component was launched by
minister **Sofiene Hemissi** in **June 2025**; the draft plan went to a ministerial council on
**2026-01-06** under a first-time bottom-up method; a **national AI strategy** strand (digital health,
agriculture, energy, transport, digital administration; framed on digital sovereignty) sits within it and
was presented at the **WSIS ministerial in Geneva on 2026-07-10**.

**This answers Claim B:** the EU ambassador's "National Digital Strategy" (2026-01) denotes this
development-plan vision — **not** the 2020-2025 plan renamed or extended. The substantive finding is a
**change of instrument type**: digital policy moved from a sectoral strategy owned by the
communication-technologies ministry to a chapter of a whole-of-government plan coordinated by the
Ministry of Economy and Planning.

**What remains unheld** (routed to `reviews/acquisitions.md`, not a contradiction): the text of the
2026-2030 plan itself, whether the Council of Ministers formally adopted it, and any standalone national
AI-strategy document.
