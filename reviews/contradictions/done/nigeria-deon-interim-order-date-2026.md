# Date of the interim court order halting Nigeria's DEON digital-lending regulations — April vs May 2026

## The claim in dispute

When the Federal High Court's interim order restraining the FCCPC from enforcing the Digital,
Electronic, Online or Non-Traditional Consumer Lending Regulations, 2025 (DEON Regulations) was
issued, in Suit No. FHC/L/CS/760/2026 (WASPAN v FCCPC). The order was discharged by the final
judgment of Justice Lewis-Allagoa delivered 20 July 2026.

## Competing values

1. **April 2026** — "the interim ex parte order issued in April, which had temporarily halted their
   implementation" — asserted by Tech Africa News (reporting the FCCPC's announcement), 22 July 2026.
   Held source URL:
   https://techafricanews.com/2026/07/22/nigeria-strengthens-consumer-protection-as-fccpc-resumes-digital-lending-enforcement/
2. **~May 2026** — operators suspended airtime/data credit in April 2026 after the FCCPC classified
   airtime advances as digital loans; "a month later, WASPAN secured an interim injunction
   restraining the FCCPC" — asserted by TechCabal (Frank Eleanya), 21 July 2026. Held source URL:
   https://techcabal.com/2026/07/21/nigerias-airtime-lending-battle-ends-with-a-split-decision/

## Notes

The two accounts may describe two distinct orders (an ex parte interim order, then an interlocutory
injunction on notice), in which case both dates could stand; neither source distinguishes them. A
related framing divergence in the same pair: the FCCPC-side account says the court "dismissed the
case in its entirety, rejecting all reliefs sought", while TechCabal reports the court agreed with
WASPAN's argument that the FCCPC cannot licence telecommunications operators (possibly a holding in
the reasoning rather than a relief granted). The judgment text itself is not on file — that absence
is part of the finding.

## Instruction

These sources report different dates for the interim order that halted the DEON Regulations —
investigate whether one order (April or May 2026) or two distinct orders were made in Suit No.
FHC/L/CS/760/2026, ideally from the judgment or court records, and suggest a resolution recording
an as-of date for each order and clarifying whether the final judgment granted WASPAN any relief on
the telecommunications-licensing point.

## Resolution (2026-07-23, attempt 1)

**One order date: 15 April 2026.** The FCCPC's own notice (fccpc.gov.ng, dated 2026-05-22 — the
primary, now staged for ingest) states the ex parte order was "made on the 15th day of April, 2026"
(FHC Lagos, coram Hon. Justice A.L. Allagoa). THISDAY's court report of the judgment confirms the
court discharged "the four interim ex parte injunctions granted on April 15, 2026" — four
injunctions, one date, not two distinct orders. TechCabal's "a month later" (~May) timeline was
loose, not a second order. The FCCPC announced its suspension of the regulations 2026-05-22 — the
gap between order and announced suspension likely explains the May impression.

**Reliefs framing also resolved.** The Nation's judgment account (2026-07-20, staged for ingest):
all WASPAN reliefs refused and the suit dismissed in its entirety, while the court *held* the FCCPC
"lacks the powers to issue licences and that nothing in the DEON Regulations creates a
telecommunication licensing" — the licensing point is a holding in the reasoning, not a relief
granted. Both held accounts correct at different levels. Judgment text itself still not held.
