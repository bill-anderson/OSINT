- **Claim A — the EBRD's €190 million finances the Tunisia–Medusa submarine-cable connection.**

## Resolution (2026-07-20)

**Resolved — and the premise of the brief was wrong.** The EBRD is the announcer of record and says
plainly that the loan finances the MEDUSA connection; this is not trade-press conflation.

Per the EBRD's own release of **16 January 2026**, the facility is a **development-linked loan of up to
€190m to Tunisie Telecom** (four tranches, first committed tranche €50m), financing 4G→5G, fibre
expansion, **FTTH to 200,000 households**, backbone/core modernisation, energy efficiency — **and Tunisie
Telecom's connection to the MEDUSA submarine cable**. Complemented by an **€11m EU grant** (Neighbourhood
Investment Platform) and an **EFSD+** first-loss guarantee; the EBRD's first non-sovereign-guaranteed
public-sector financing in Tunisia since 2012.

**The two financings are unconnected instruments touching the same cable:** the **€10m EU CEF-Digital
grant to Orange Tunisia** (Dec 2022, ~30% of costs) built the **Bizerte landing station**, which Orange
owns; the **EBRD €190m** funds Tunisie Telecom's **connection into** the cable as one component of a
corporate network programme. TT is a capacity customer of Orange's station. Different recipients,
different dates, different objects — neither displaces the other.
