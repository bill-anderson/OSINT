# Morocco's Nexus AI Factory: 500 MW or ~36 MW?

Surfaced 2026-07-20 by lint #3 (currency/freshness), while restoring the dirham figure
behind the USD-only "$1.2bn" carried on the Morocco place hub. The two held sources agree
on the money and disagree on the capacity by roughly **14×**.

The money reconciles: **MAD 12 billion** and **US$1.2 billion** are the same commitment at
a plausible rate. The capacity does not.

## Paste-ready research brief

A sovereign AI-compute build in Morocco, unveiled at a trade show in April 2026 and led by
a company called Nexus Core Systems with the digital-transition ministry (MTNRA), MICEPP
and AMDIE, is reported at two irreconcilable capacities:

- **Up to 500 MW**, at a single site on the outskirts of Casablanca, powered entirely by
  renewable energy from TAQA Morocco, using Nvidia technology, valued at **US$1.2 billion**
  — described as "the first sovereign AI infrastructure platform of its kind in Africa."
  Reported 2026-04-10:
  https://www.connectingafrica.com/partnerships/gitex-africa-2026-companies-ink-deals-to-transform-morocco
- **Two phases totalling roughly 36 MW**, valued at **MAD 12 billion**, deployed from
  **2027**: phase one at **Nouaceur**, MAD 5 billion, **16 MW**, 50 skilled jobs; phase two
  in **northern Morocco**, MAD 7 billion, **more than 20 MW**, taking total employment to
  125 jobs. Reported 2026-04-13:
  https://techafricanews.com/2026/04/13/morocco-launches-nexus-ai-factory-to-position-itself-as-africas-leading-ai-hub/

Both are on file. No primary — no company statement, ministry release or investment-agreement
text — is held for either figure; both are trade-press write-ups of the same launch event
three days apart. That absence is part of the finding.

**Questions to settle:**
1. What capacity does the operator or the Moroccan government itself state? Prefer a company
   or ministry release over either write-up.
2. Are the two describing the **same** build? Nouaceur sits in the Casablanca region, so
   phase one and the "Casablanca outskirts" site are plausibly one and the same — but the
   500 MW figure may be a **long-run design ceiling** or a full-site headline, against the
   36 MW of **contracted phases one and two**. If so this is a scope difference, not an
   error, and both figures stand once labelled.
3. Is **MAD 12bn / US$1.2bn** the whole programme or only the two named phases? If the
   phases sum to the full envelope, a 500 MW build at that price would be far below
   prevailing per-MW data-centre build costs — which is itself evidence for reading 500 MW
   as a ceiling rather than a contracted capacity.
4. Confirm the dirham figure is the announcing party's own, and establish the conversion
   date behind US$1.2bn — neither source dates its conversion.

## RESOLVED 2026-07-21 — not a conflict; two quantities a year apart

**500 MW is contracted renewable *power supply*; 36 MW is contracted *IT load*.** Both stand once
labelled.

- **MTNRA ministry release, 2026-04-08** (the primary every April-2026 write-up derives from): **MAD
  12bn "investissement initial" over two phases**, **125 direct jobs by end-2027** — phase 1 Nouaceur
  **MAD 5bn / 16 MW / 50 jobs**; phase 2 north **MAD 7bn / +20 MW**, **36 MW cumulative end-2027**.
  Contains **no 500 MW and no USD figure**.
- **Nexus Core Systems consortium release, 2025-06-18**: programme "powered by **up to 500 MW of
  renewable energy under a strategic agreement with TAQA Morocco**", first phase then **over 40 MW**.

The apparent contradiction is a **press artefact** — the April-2026 trade write-up welded the June-2025
consortium framing (500 MW, Casablanca outskirts, Nvidia, TAQA) onto the April-2026 MoU's money.
"Single site" is also wrong: Nouaceur is in the Casablanca region but **phase 2 is a separate northern
site**.

**Supersession recorded:** phase 1 slipped and shrank — "over 40 MW" launching Q1 2026 (Jun 2025)
became **16 MW from 2027** (Apr 2026).

**Money corrected:** MAD is the announcing party's own currency and **no primary carries any USD**.
MAD 12bn covers **phases 1-2 only**, not the full programme. At the 2026-04-08 signing-day rate that is
**~US$1.29bn**, not the undated **US$1.2bn** the wiki carried.

**Unestablished:** whether the TAQA 500 MW supply agreement is still live as of the April-2026 MoU; any
capital cost for the 500 MW build-out; whether phase 1's 16 MW IT load sits in a larger gross facility.
Applied to `wiki/entities/nexus-ai-factory.md` and `wiki/places/MAR.md`. Both underlying releases
**queued for acquisition** — the wiki still holds only the two trade-press write-ups.
