# South Sudan's 2026 visa-fee instrument: does it exist at all?

**Re-routed from `reviews/acquisitions.md` on 2026-07-21.** It entered the queue as a missing document —
"the immigration/visa fee instrument introduced w/c 2026-07-13". The acquisition attempt could not
retrieve it, but established something that changes its class entirely: **the issuing authority denies
the instrument exists.** That is not a fetch failure. It is a live dispute, and it belongs here.

## The dispute

**Trade and regional press reported**, from around 2026-07-13, that South Sudan had introduced a new
visa fee schedule for African nationals. The wiki holds that reporting.

**South Sudan's Ministry of Interior publicly denied it** (reported by Sudans Post, 2026-07-20). Per
that account the Ministry stated that **neither Minister Gen. Aleu Ayieny Aleu nor the Directorate of
Civil Registry, Nationality, Passports and Immigration issued "any directive, policy decision, or
statutory instrument"**, that **no revised fee schedule was approved**, and that the document in
circulation is a **leaked internal directive dated 24 July 2019**, signed by the then-Director for
Immigration, **Maj. Gen. Nyang Thiciot Dak**.

**The reported fees are also mutually inconsistent**, which is itself evidence that no single
authoritative schedule underlies the coverage:

- **TechCabal**: **US$50** for Kenya / Uganda / Rwanda / DRC nationals; **US$100** for Somalia /
  Burundi / Nigeria.
- **Legit.ng**: **US$100 / US$200 / US$350** for single-entry / three-month / six-month visas.

These are not the same schema — one is banded by nationality, the other by visa duration. They cannot
both be descriptions of one instrument.

## Why it matters

A fee schedule discriminating between African nationalities bears directly on **free movement, AfCFTA
implementation and digital border systems**, and it is the kind of claim that propagates fast. If the
underlying document is a **2019 internal directive** being reported as a 2026 policy, then any wiki
statement dated to July 2026 is wrong on both **date** and **status**.

## Paste-ready research brief

An East African state was reported in mid-July 2026 to have introduced a new visa fee schedule applying
differential rates to nationals of other African countries. Within a week, that state's interior
ministry publicly denied issuing any such directive, policy decision or statutory instrument, said no
revised fee schedule had been approved, and asserted that the document circulating was a leaked
internal directive from **July 2019**. Separately, the fee figures reported by different outlets are
structurally incompatible — one set banded by nationality, another by visa duration.

Establish:

1. **Does a 2026 instrument exist?** Look for a gazette notice, ministerial order, statutory instrument
   or official fee schedule from the South Sudan Ministry of Interior or the Directorate of Civil
   Registry, Nationality, Passports and Immigration.
2. **Is the circulating document the 2019 directive the Ministry describes?** If it can be obtained,
   check its date and signatory against the Ministry's account.
3. **What fees, if any, are actually in force**, on what basis, and from what date? Record each with its
   own as-of date and its own source.
4. **Was the denial itself subsequently retracted, confirmed or overtaken?** A denial can be followed by
   quiet implementation; check for later official statements.

Record an as-of date for each finding. If the conclusion is that **no 2026 instrument is established**,
say so plainly — that is a complete and useful answer.

## What the wiki holds

Trade-press reporting of the claimed policy, and the Sudans Post account of the Ministry's denial.
**No primary instrument of any date is held** — not the claimed 2026 schedule, and not the 2019
directive. The `evisa.gov.ss` fee table renders client-side and returned no content on the acquisition
attempt (2026-07-21).

## Affected pages

Any South Sudan page carrying the July-2026 fee claim — check `wiki/places/SSD.md` and its

## RESOLVED 2026-07-21 — no 2026 instrument exists; and this brief was wrong on two points

**Verdict: there is no 2026 instrument.** The fee schedule reported as new in July 2026 is the
**December-2024** schedule, set by **circular No. RSS/SSRA/CG/SPECIAL/VOL.1/104 of 2024-12-03** under
the **Financial Act 2024-25**, **effective 2024-12-16**, signed by Director-General **Maj. Gen. Simon
Majur Pabek**. It has been visible on `evisa.gov.ss` since at least 2023. Fees: single entry **USD
100** (USD 160 US citizens), three-month multiple **USD 200** (**USD 125 regional**), six-month
multiple **USD 350**. The circular denominates in USD and directs charging at the **Central Bank's
prevailing rate**, so USD is the announcing party's own currency.

**This brief got two things wrong, and both are worth recording.**

1. **The two fee schemas are not incompatible — they are one table read two ways.** The portal table
   has columns `Country | Visa? | Single Entry | 3 Months Multiple | 6 Months Multiple`, banded by
   nationality. TechCabal reported the **single-entry column** ($50 regional / $100 standard);
   Legit.ng reported the **standard row across all three columns** ($100/$200/$350). Same table. The
   premise that "outlets disagree" was mistaken.
2. **The 2019 directive is not a fee schedule.** It grants **free visas to Tanzanian citizens** under
   Article 7 of the EAC Common Market Protocol. This brief conflated it with the fee figures, which
   come from the portal and have a 2024 legal basis. The Ministry denied a *visa-free-entry* policy for
   Egypt and Tanzania; that is a different object from the fee table.

**Propagation:** the story traces to a **TRT Afrika social-media post** (c. 2026-07-16), not to any
instrument — TRT → YEN/Pulse/Legit (07-17) → Tuko/Black Star (07-20) → TechCabal (07-20). **No outlet
in the chain cited a gazette, order or circular.** Also corrected: South Sudan has **charged EAC
nationals visa fees since at least 2020** (EABC complaint, Nov 2020), so the "departure from EAC
free-movement" framing was six years late; and **Egypt's visa-free status is long-standing**, not a
2026 grant.

**Open horizon, not a contradiction:** whether the **24 July 2019** Tanzania directive remains
operational. The Ministry's statement **neither confirmed nor revoked it**. Stated dated on `SSD.md`;
the directive itself is not held.

**Not obtainable:** `evisa.gov.ss/eligibility` renders client-side and returned zero rows; Wayback had
no capture. Primaries ingested: the 2024-12-16 fee circular reporting and the 2026-07-20 Ministry
denial. Applied to `wiki/places/SSD.md`, which previously carried the false 2026 framing as a dated
development.
