# Provenance hunt: what is Care 2.0, and how does it relate to SmartCare Pro?

**Queued:** 2026-07-20 (migrated from gaps.md)

## The unexplained second platform

- **Care 2.0 is a Zambian digital-health platform piloted at Ndola Teaching Hospital, Arthur Davison
  Children's Hospital and Kitwe Teaching Hospital, and migrating onto GovLink alongside SmartCare Pro.**
  - Asserted by: **four held sources**, all of which name it and none of which explains it.

**Nothing on file says** what Care 2.0 is, who built it, or how it relates to SmartCare Pro — successor,
parallel system, or the "SmartCare 2.0" of the ITWeb account — or whether it touches the same biometric
patient identity. The wiki carries a detailed page on one national electronic health record and a blank
beside it where the second sits. The naming drift alone is worth settling.

## Sources held

The four sources naming Care 2.0, and the ITWeb account rendering it as "SmartCare 2.0", are held. **No
source dedicated to the platform is on file** — no health-ministry statement, no implementer's description,
no procurement or donor record. That is the provenance gap.

## Instruction

Establish whether Care 2.0 is a distinct system from SmartCare Pro or a version of it: what it is, who
developed and funds it, when the pilots began, which facilities use each system, whether they share a
patient identifier or biometric enrolment, and which is intended to be the national record. Settle the
naming. Record an as-of date for each finding.
