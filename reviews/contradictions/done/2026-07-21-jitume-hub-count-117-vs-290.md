# Kenya's Jitume hubs: 117 in July 2026, or 290 in February 2025?

Surfaced 2026-07-21 by the acquisition pass, while fetching Jitume programme documentation. The wiki
records Kenya commissioning its **117th Jitume Digital Hub** on **2026-07-20**, and treats that as the
first programme-scale figure it holds. The acquisition search surfaced a **February-2025** report of
**290 hubs already established** — a **larger** count at an **earlier** date, seventeen months before.

**A count cannot run backwards.** This is not supersession and not a stale figure; one of the two is
measuring something other than what it appears to measure, or one is wrong.

## Why it matters

The 117 is currently doing real work on `wiki/places/KEN.md`: it was written up as the **wiki's first
figure for Jitume's scale**, explicitly framed as "a baseline, not a trajectory". If 290 hubs existed in
early 2025, that framing is wrong and the baseline is misleading — the programme would be **shrinking,
recounting, or reporting two different things under one name**.

The newly-acquired KoTDA procurement document
(`raw/2024-03-07-kotda-jitume-hubs-prequalification-tender.md`) supplies a plausible mechanism and
should be the starting point: it describes **three hub models — Basic, Standard and Centre of
Excellence** — a pilot of **47 CoE + 50 Standard**, and a target of hubs in **all TVET institutions
*and* 1,450 wards**. Those are at least **two different denominators** and **three different unit
types**, which is exactly the shape of a counting discrepancy.

## Paste-ready research brief

A national digital-hub programme in Kenya, implemented by a state technology-park authority under the
ICT ministry, has been reported at two incompatible scales:

- **117 hubs**, on the commissioning of what was described as the 117th hub, in **July 2026**;
- **290 hubs already established**, in a **February 2025** report.

The programme is known to distinguish at least three hub types (a basic model, a standard model and
centres of excellence), to be deployed both in TVET institutions and at ward level, and to have run a
pilot phase before general rollout.

Establish:

1. **What each figure counts.** Are the 117 and the 290 counting the same unit — all hub types, or only
   one model? Ward hubs only, or TVET hubs too? Commissioned, constructed, connected, or operational?
2. **Which is current**, and what the count is at the most recent date for which a figure exists.
3. **Whether the programme has a published register or results framework** giving hub counts by county
   and by model over time — that would settle it directly.
4. If the two are genuinely incompatible on the same basis, **which is wrong**, and whether the error
   is in the reporting or in the programme's own communications.

Prefer the implementing authority's own programme documentation, annual report or results framework
over any press account. Record an as-of date for every figure.

## RESOLVED 2026-07-21 — two parallel series, and the mechanism is not the one this brief guessed

**Verdict: the 117 is a launch-ceremony ordinal, not a national count.** The 290 series is the
installed base and is the honest scale figure.

- **Launch ordinal:** 40th (2023-08-07) · 100th (2023) · 112th (2026-06-13) · 117th (2026-07-20)
- **Installed base:** 114 (2023-08-07) · **290 (2025-02-15)** · 284 (2025-04-25) · **~350 (2026-06)**

**The decisive evidence is a single event.** On **2023-08-07** at Kenya Coast National Polytechnic, two
officials of the same ministry gave both figures in the same room: **CS Eliud Owalo** — "Today's launch
is the **40th** Jitume Digital Hub" — and **PS John Tanui**, at that same event — "We have so far
**deployed 114 centres**. We expect to **launch** the remaining 74…". *Deployed* and *launched* are
different verbs on different subsets. That dates the divergence to the programme's first year, and it
is the 117-vs-290 gap in miniature.

**The brief's hypothesis was wrong.** It guessed the mechanism was the three hub models or the
TVET-vs-ward denominators. It is neither. Brand conflation is also ruled out — Tanui said "under
Jitume" explicitly. The divergence is plausibly real rather than clerical: World Bank supervision
reports record **KDEAP procurement as near-stalled through 2024–25** (one completed contract worth
US$72,000, two years after effectiveness), so ceremonial launches slowed while equipment deployment
continued.

**The larger finding, now on the page: Jitume's published metrics are internally inconsistent on nearly
every headline, and the inconsistency is in the programme's own communications, not the reporting.**
Youth trained, all from named officials: 400,000 (Feb 2025) → 140,000 (Jun 2026) → 240,000 (May 2026).
Job linkages: 41,000 / 42,000 / 92,000 within weeks in mid-2026. Start date: December 2022 (KoTDA
tender, first-hand) vs December 2023. Hub models: three (tender) vs four (Tanui). Standard pilot: 50
(tender) vs 53 (Tanui). **No Jitume figure should be cited without its date and its speaker.**

**Not established:** no published hub register or results framework exists. KoTDA's `jitume.konza.go.ke`
and `jitume-std.konza.go.ke` are JavaScript applications returning nothing to automated retrieval; the
KDEAP results framework tracks broadband users, not hub counts, and its June-2025 ISR records "no
progress recorded on results indicators". Programme reach in users rather than buildings stays
unestablished.

Applied to `wiki/places/KEN.md`, whose "first figure for Jitume's scale" framing — written by CC on
2026-07-20 — is withdrawn.
