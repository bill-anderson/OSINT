# Nigeria's new identity statute: the "NIMC Act, 2025" or the "NIMC Act 2026"?

Surfaced 2026-07-21 during ingest of a Vanguard report on integrating digital identity into
Nigeria's health sector. That source names the enabling statute the **"National Identity
Management Commission (NIMC) Act, 2025"** and calls it "newly enacted". The wiki already holds the
same statute, from separate sources, as the **"NIMC Act 2026"**, recorded as **enacted in June 2026
by President Tinubu** and as replacing the NIMC Act 2007.

Both cannot be the Act's correct short-title year. This is not a substantive disagreement about
what the law does — the wiki's description of its content (NIN as foundational identifier, replacing
the 2007 Act) is not in dispute — it is a **citation-year conflict on a statute the wiki cites
repeatedly**, including on `wiki/entities/nimc.md`, `wiki/concepts/gov.legislate.md`,
`wiki/concepts/dpi.id.md` and `wiki/concepts/infra.cybersec.md`.

It matters because a wrong statutory year propagates into every downstream citation and is the kind
of error that is very hard to detect later — and because the wiki is now attaching a **health-sector
digital-identity programme** to this Act as its legal basis.

The most likely benign explanation is an ordinary legislative-drafting gap: a bill passed by the
National Assembly in **2025** and assented in **June 2026** can be referred to by either year, and
Nigerian practice generally dates an Act to assent. That would make **2026** correct and the Vanguard
reference loose. **But that is a hypothesis, not a finding** — nothing on file establishes the
assent date from a primary, and the wiki should not settle its own citation by inference.

## What the wiki actually holds

- **For "NIMC Act 2026"**: secondary reporting only, compiled onto `wiki/entities/nimc.md` and three
  concept pages. **No gazette, no assent record and no Act text is held.**
- **For "NIMC Act, 2025"**: a single mention inside
  `raw/2026-07-19-fg-digital-id-healthcare-uhc-2030.md` (Vanguard, 2026-07-19), reporting the Health
  Minister and NIMC Director-General.

So the wiki has **no primary for either designation**, which is the more important finding: a statute
cited across four pages rests entirely on press description.

## Paste-ready research brief

A Nigerian federal statute reconstituting the National Identity Management Commission — replacing the
NIMC Act of 2007 and establishing the National Identification Number as the country's foundational
identifier — is referred to in circulation under two different years:

- the **"NIMC Act, 2025"**, used in July 2026 reporting of a Federal Ministry of Health and Social
  Welfare engagement with the Commission, which describes it as newly enacted;
- the **"NIMC Act 2026"**, used in other July 2026 reporting, which states it was enacted in June 2026
  by the President and replaces the 19-year-old 2007 Act.

Source URLs on file for each assertion:

- "NIMC Act, 2025" — https://www.vanguardngr.com/2026/07/fg-moves-to-integrate-digital-id-into-healthcare-targets-uhc-by-2030/
- "NIMC Act 2026" — no source URL is recorded on file for this designation; it reaches the wiki
  through compiled secondary reporting whose originating URLs are not captured against the claim.
  **That absence is itself part of the finding.**

Establish, from primary sources, the Act's **correct short title and year**, its **date of passage by
the National Assembly** and its **date of presidential assent**, and whether it has been **gazetted**
— ideally from the *Federal Republic of Nigeria Official Gazette*, the National Assembly's bill
record, or NIMC's own publication of the Act. Record an as-of date for each. If both years are in
legitimate concurrent use (e.g. a 2025 passage with 2026 assent), say so explicitly and state which
form should be used as the wiki's canonical citation.

## RESOLVED 2026-07-21 — the wiki's "NIMC Act 2026" is correct

**Government primary found and ingested:** the Presidency's own record —
`raw/2026-06-26-state-house-tinubu-signs-nimc-act-2026.md` — establishes assent on **Friday 26 June
2026**, at the State House, Abuja.

**The "2025" is the bill-year, not a competing short title.** Legislative history: bill began at the
National Assembly **2024-03-19**; Senate passed the **NIMC (Repeal and Enactment) Bill 2025** on
**2025-10-15** (on rescission and re-committal); **assent declined 2026-05-05** under **s.58(4) of the
1999 Constitution** over drafting defects — including a Long Title reciting repeal of the 2007 Act
without indicating re-enactment; corrected bill re-passed and **assented 2026-06-26**. The 2025 bill
**never became law**, and under the Acts Authentication Act an Act belongs to its year of assent.
**Canonical citation: National Identity Management Commission Act, 2026.** No wiki page needed
changing on the year — all four were already correct.

**The brief's own prediction held, and is the more important finding: the Act's text is not public.**
No gazette reference, no Act number, no commencement notice; NIMC has not published it; a Nigerian law
firm was still writing a fortnight after assent that the full text was unavailable, and the one outlet
with section-level detail describes its copy as exclusively obtained. **Stated as a dated vacuum on
`wiki/entities/nimc.md`** — for a statute cited across four pages and now the legal basis for wiring
digital identity into healthcare, an unpublished text is itself a governance finding. Act text and
gazette reference queued for acquisition. **Residual gap:** the date of the corrective re-passage is
not established.
