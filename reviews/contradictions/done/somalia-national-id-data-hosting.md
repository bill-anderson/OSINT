# Contradiction: where is Somalia's national-ID (SNID) data actually hosted — inside Somalia or in Pakistan?

**Opened:** 2026-07-16 (SLE·SOM·SSD·STP batch)

## The dispute

Somalia's foundational national digital ID (SNID) is being built with technology from Pakistan's NADRA. Sources disagree on **where the biometric/identity data is physically hosted and who controls it** — a core data-sovereignty question.

- **Claim A — data is stored securely within Somalia, under Somali control.**
  - Asserted by: NIRA Director-General Abdiwali Ali Abdulle (a.k.a. Abdiwali Timacadde), via Hiiraan Online (2025-11-17).
  - Source URL held: https://www.hiiraan.com/news4/2025/Nov/203644/a_smart_national_id_powers_somalia_s_digital_public_infrastructure_dpi_vision.aspx
- **Claim B — the system is based in Pakistan and managed by foreign workers.**
  - Asserted by: Garowe Online reporting on personal-data-security concerns in the ID rollout (2025, page undated — date is a proxy).
  - Source URL held: https://garoweonline.com/en/news/somalia/somalia-growing-concerns-over-personal-data-security-in-national-id-rollout

## Likely reconciliation to test

Establish, with an as-of date: (1) where SNID enrolment/biometric data is physically stored (on-shore Somali data centre vs NADRA/Pakistan-hosted); (2) who administers the production environment (Somali NIRA staff vs NADRA personnel); (3) whether a data-localisation or hosting clause exists in the NADRA arrangement. The two claims may both be partly true at different stages (e.g. NADRA-built and initially NADRA-operated, migrating on-shore) — pin the current state and date it.

## Instruction

These sources give different accounts of where Somalia's national-ID data is hosted and who controls it. Investigate the discrepancy and suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date for each claim.

## Reconcile pass, 2026-07-20 — PARTIALLY RESOLVED, still open

**Reconcilable as a dated trajectory; the current endpoint is not established.**

The two claims describe opposite ends of a migration, not the same moment. The decisive find is a **Somali
government primary neither side of the brief cited**: MoCT stated twice on the record — **2024-04-28** and
again **2025-05-06** — that the National Data Center would "eliminate dependencies of national data on
international data storage solutions, **that is stored outside the country**." That is the FGS conceding
offshore hosting in the present tense, corroborating Garowe's *direction* from a far better source than its
anonymous analyst. **Caveat:** MoCT speaks of **government data generally**, not SNID specifically.

NADRA's own release (**2025-03-13**) records delivery of "advanced data center infrastructure" to Somalia —
the mechanism for an on-shore migration. So NIRA DG Abdiwali Ali Abdulle's **2025-11-17** on-shore assurance
is not obviously false, but it is an **unverified assertion naming no facility**.

**Three threads keep it open:**
1. The **National Data Center had not launched** — the World Bank SCALED-UP restructuring (2025-10) extends
   the project to **2026-06-30** precisely to finish it.
2. NIRA instead **procured commercial colocation** (award ref 4500377, USD 179,220, published 2026-02-25)
   whose **location is paywalled** — and Somali providers such as Dalkom run Mogadishu, Nairobi *and* UAE
   facilities.
3. **No hosting or data-localisation clause is public.** The 2018 Pakistan–Somalia grant agreement and the
   SNIDS Addenda-1/-2 texts are unpublished.

**Dating fix available:** the Garowe piece cited as Claim B is undated (proxy). Its sister article,
"Somalia's New National ID System Raises Data Privacy Fears," carries a firm **2025-01-25** and makes the
same allegation, so that strand can be anchored to a real date.

**Wiki state meanwhile:** pages carry the trajectory dated — offshore hosting acknowledged by MoCT
(2024-04-28, restated 2025-05-06), NADRA on-shore infrastructure delivered (2025-03-13), NIRA's on-shore
assertion (2025-11-17) recorded as an assertion, not established fact.

**Instruction (revised):** establish where SNID enrolment and biometric data is **physically hosted as of
now**. Two concrete routes: (a) a subscriber pull of Somali tender ref **4500377** would likely name the
colocation facility; (b) confirm whether the **National Data Center** has launched since the World Bank's
2026-06-30 revised closing date. Record an as-of date.
