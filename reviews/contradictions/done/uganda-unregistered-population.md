# Contradiction: how many Ugandans lack a national ID — 18 million, or is the register at 35 million?

**Opened:** 2026-07-17 (TUN·TZA·UGA batch)

## The dispute

Two accounts, five months apart, describe the reach of Uganda's national ID (Ndaga Muntu) in terms
that cannot both anchor a page. This is **not** simple supersession: the figures do not reconcile
by arithmetic, and the gap between them is the analytically interesting part.

- **Claim A — 18 million people, about 40% of the population, lack a National ID.**
  - Asserted by: **Michael Aboneka**, writing for the **Digital Rights Alliance Africa**, in advocacy
    analysis of a High Court ruling (published **2025-09-13**).
  - **The estimate carries no cited source in the piece — it is the author's own figure.** That is
    itself part of the finding, and the brief should say so.
  - Source URL held:
    http://digitalrightsalliance.africa/ugandas-digital-id-barrier-worsens-as-court-delivers-blow-to-access
- **Claim B — the new national register holds over 35 million uniquely biometrically identifiable
  Ugandans**, following migration of 28m+ records; renewals reached **14.3m of a 15.8m target
  (90.5%)** while first-time enrolments reached only **6.4m of a 17.2m target (37.3%)**; and of
  **10.1m cards printed, only 2.5m were collected**.
  - Asserted by: **Claire Ollama, Registrar of NIRA**, at a press conference of **9 February 2026**,
    reported by Biometric Update (published **2026-02-11**).
  - Source URL held:
    https://www.biometricupdate.com/202602/uganda-migrates-more-than-28m-records-to-new-national-id-system

Context that constrains any reconciliation:

- **NIRA's own 2024 baseline** put the unregistered population at **17.2 million**, with 15.8m
  renewals needed.
  - Asserted by: NIRA, via ID Tech (published **2024-08-19**).
  - Source URL held: https://idtechwire.com/uganda-prepares-large-scale-biometric-registration-for-national-id

## Why this does not resolve as supersession

NIRA's own numbers, taken at face value, imply roughly **10.8 million people still unregistered as
of February 2026** (17.2m target minus 6.4m enrolled). That figure sits *between* the advocacy
estimate of 18m and the triumphal framing of a 35m register, and cleanly contradicts neither. A
register of 35m against a national population in the mid-40-millions is also not obviously
compatible with either reading.

The most likely explanation — and the thing to test — is that **"registered", "holds a NIN" and
"holds a usable card" are three different populations**. The same source reports **10.1m cards
printed but only 2.5m collected**, and a separate vendor account reports **more than 1 million
Ugandans could not register fingerprint biometrics at all**
(https://www.biometricupdate.com/202603/iritech-provides-iris-biometrics-for-ugandas-upgraded-registration-kits).
If so, the divergence is definitional rather than factual — but no source on file says this, and it
must not be assumed.

## What to establish, with an as-of date for each

1. Uganda's current population and the current 16+/eligible population, on a stated basis.
2. How many people are **enrolled** in the National Identification Register; how many hold a
   **NIN**; how many hold a **valid physical card in hand**. These are three counts, not one.
3. What the **18 million / 40%** figure rests on, if anything — or whether it is an unsourced
   estimate.
4. Whether the **35 million** register figure includes records that are migrated-but-unverified,
   duplicated, or belong to deceased or emigrated people.

## Instruction

These sources give sharply different accounts of how many Ugandans the national ID system has and
has not reached, and the figures do not reconcile by arithmetic. Investigate the discrepancy and
suggest a resolution, recording an as-of date for each value and distinguishing enrolment from NIN
issuance from card possession.
