# Provenance hunt: what was actually announced at the AllMyT Summit 2026?

**Queued:** 2026-07-20 (migrated from gaps.md)

## The announcements with no primary source

The only item the wiki holds on the summit (**16–17 July 2026**) is a **thin Tech Review Africa PR-wire
notice**. Richer contemporaneous coverage, from outlets outside the sweep source set, reports that the
national telecommunications operator announced:

- **A memorandum of understanding with Amazon Web Services.**
- That its **data centre now hosts NVIDIA H200 GPUs**, with **B300 units due by end-2026**.
- The launch of a **Mauritius AI Registry** (airegistry.mu), on an open-sourced stack.
- **Models-as-a-Service**, in a TM Forum context.
- A platform partnership with **SBM Bank**.
- **Mauritius–Seychelles cross-border payments** (Eboo).

**None of these is primary-sourced.** A related item — the mytGPT Education pilot of **2026-01-26** — is
likewise PR-wire only.

## Sources held

The Tech Review Africa PR-wire notice on the summit is held, as is the PR-wire item on the education pilot.
**No operator, vendor, bank or registry announcement is on file for any of the six claims** — that is the
provenance gap.

## Instruction

Establish which of these announcements were actually made and what each consists of, from the announcing
parties themselves: the memorandum's parties, date and scope; the hardware deployment and its timing; what
the artificial-intelligence registry is, who runs it and when it launched; the service offering; the banking
partnership; and the cross-border payments arrangement. Record an as-of date for each, and flag any that
turn out to be promotional claims without substance.
