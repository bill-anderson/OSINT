# Provenance hunt: whose technology underpins Angola's foundational digital ID — the Chinese civil-registration stack, or an Aadhaar-modelled Indian one?

**Queued:** 2026-07-20 (migrated from gaps.md)

## The competing accounts

Two different foreign technology stacks are described for Angola's identity infrastructure, and no held
source reconciles them.

- **Claim A — a Chinese-devised civil-registration system.** State-owned **CEIEC** won the contract in
  **November 2019**; the system went live in Luanda in **2020**.
- **Claim B — a 2025 pivot to India**, modelling Angola's digital ID on **Aadhaar**, under the **$200m DPI
  credit line** (with MOSIP as context).

These may be complementary — China supplying civil registration/CRVS, India supplying a future foundational
digital ID — or they may describe a hand-off from one to the other. No single source states which stack
underpins the **Bilhete de Identidade (BI)** or the planned digital ID.

## Sources held

The two accounts reach the wiki through held material on the CEIEC contract and on the Angola–India DPI
credit line. **No primary from INFOSI, the responsible ministry, or either vendor is on file** stating what
the current or planned identity platform actually is — that is the provenance gap.

## Instruction

Establish which technology platform currently runs Angola's civil registration and identity card issuance,
and which is planned for the foundational digital ID, distinguishing the two clearly. Identify the
contracting parties and the status of each arrangement, and record an as-of date for each. Watch for a
straightforward conflict between the Chinese and Indian accounts as opposed to a division of labour.
