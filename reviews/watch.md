# reviews/watch.md — standing watches (dormant, not a queue)

Items where the wiki has established that **the answer is not currently
obtainable** — because the event has not happened yet, the document does not
exist, the figure has never been published, or the publisher does not publish.

These are **not** open work. They were previously carried in `gaps.md`, where
they accumulated indefinitely because nothing could ever close them: research
cannot resolve a fact that no one has yet recorded. Carrying them as live queue
entries made the register look like a backlog when it was really a horizon.

**Rules.** Nothing here is actioned. Each item carries a **re-check trigger** —
the event or publication that would make it answerable. When that trigger fires
(usually via the daily sweep or an ingest batch touching the country), the item
moves to `contradictions/open/` or `acquisitions.md` and becomes live work. An
item may sit here for years; that is the point.

**Discipline that still applies.** Several of these are load-bearing *absences* —
"no data-protection law exists," "no national figure exists" — and those are
findings the wiki should **state on the page**, dated, not merely wait on. A
watch item is not permission to leave a page silent about a known vacuum.

> `log.md`). Original wording and provenance are in git history of `gaps.md`.

## Awaiting an event that has not happened

- **Liberia has no data-protection law and no National Data Authority**, despite mandating
  biometric ID (EO 147) and SIM-ID linkage and being an ECOWAS Data Protection Act signatory.
  A live data-sovereignty risk that should be **stated** on the Liberia pages, not just watched.
  *Trigger: the drafted implementing policy lands.* *(ex-GAP-LBR-001)*
- **Libya has no data-protection law or DPA**; the National AI Strategy 2025–2030 itself flags
  the vacuum, and DLA Piper plus NISSA (public-sector only) corroborate it. Underpins the Libyan
  digital-ID fraud crisis and payments data exposure. *Trigger: the draft promised for 2026.*
  *(ex-GAP-LBY-002)*
- **Madagascar's CMIL was legislated but never established** — the Commission Malagasy de
  l'Informatique et des Libertés, provided for by law n°2014-038 (2015), still does not exist,
  while PRODIGY biometric enrolment (5.6M+) and Rahaka interoperability accelerate; AFAPDP flagged
  it urgent in Feb 2025. *Trigger: any CMIL establishment decree.* *(ex-GAP-MDG-001)*
- **Zimbabwe SI 76 of 2025 commencement date** — the securitised-deeds regime runs a 24-month
  validation window from the Minister's commencement announcement, not from gazetting; as of
  August 2025 commencement was "expected shortly" and untriggered. Until it fires the wiki cannot
  say when the window opens or closes. *Trigger: the ministerial announcement.* *(ex-GAP-ZWE-003)*
- **Chad–Azerbaijan digital MoU** — agreed (reported 2025-12-02) covering a national data centre,
  infrastructure modernisation and e-government, with no timeline announced and no signed
  instrument held. *Trigger: signature or publication.* *(ex-GAP-TCD-004)*

## The figure has never been published

- **SASSA's "alternative identification" cohort** (ZAF) — the population the whole exclusion
  argument turns on, and the only figure found is Gauteng-only and five years stale (5,050 quad-7
  recipients, 2021-10). No national figure exists. *Trigger: a SASSA parliamentary reply or annual
  report.* *(ex-GAP-ZAF-00X)*
- **ANRPTS publishes no usage statistics** (MRT) — every Houwiyeti figure in circulation reaches
  the public domain as a conference remark relayed by third parties, which is precisely how the
  unreconcilable "70%" entered the record. ARMP procurement records show ANRPTS commissioned a
  *"Dashboard and BI Houwiyeti"* system, so an authoritative internal series exists but is
  unpublished. *Trigger: standing watch on anrpts.gov.mr.* *(ex-GAP-MRT-00X)*
- **Zambia has no retrievable survey-based internet-use figure since 2018** — the ZamStats/ZICTA
  National ICT Survey series (4.8% 2013, 8.8% 2015, **14.3% 2018**) is the only nationally
  representative household measure; a 2022 round was conducted and the 2022 Census measured
  3-month internet use, but neither headline could be retrieved. Every current Zambian figure is
  therefore administrative or modelled — **which is itself worth stating on the page.** *Trigger:
  publication of either 2022 headline.* *(ex-GAP-ZMB-00X)*
- **Burkina Faso white-zone programme** — every source in the corpus is press reporting; no ARCEP
  or MTDPCE primary exists to hold, because the ministry publishes via Facebook, which is not a
  sweep target. *Trigger: a ministry or regulator publication route that is fetchable.*
  *(ex-GAP-BFA-00X)*
- **Botswana Tech Fund's Guernsey domicile is not register-verified** — no Guernsey Financial
  Services Commission entry was found; the domicile rests on the managing partner's on-record
  statement plus Pula Investments' Guernsey base. The entity page already says so explicitly rather
  than asserting it as filed fact, which is the correct handling. *Trigger: a GFSC register entry.*
  *(ex-GAP-BWA-00X)*
- **Somalia SNID data-localisation clause is unknowable from open sources** — the **2018
  Pakistan–Somalia grant agreement** and **SNIDS Addenda-1/-2** are unheld and unpublished, so
  whether such a clause exists cannot be established. Bears directly on the open SNID hosting
  contradiction. *Trigger: publication or leak of the agreements.* *(ex-GAP-SOM-00X)*
- **Zambia — three figures rest on single government briefings with no stated basis.** SZI's
  "approximately 80 percent" connectivity claim has **no stated unit** and is irreconcilable with
  both the 33.0% user figure and ZICTA's 72.1-per-100 subscriptions; the **K7 billion**
  digital-transactions figure (2021–2025) has no breakdown or baseline; and no official statement
  disaggregates the 116 GWAN councils by *link established* vs *service live*. *Trigger: any
  official statement showing a basis.* *(ex-GAP-ZMB-00X)*
