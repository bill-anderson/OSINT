# Why more Lesotho migrant workers are choosing fintechs to send money home

*TechCabal, 12 June 2026. By Phathisani Moyo.*

[CLIP — article is paywalled ("Become a TC Insider to finish this story"); only the free lede was captured. Full text to be clipped at promotion.]

Every month, Mampe Seema, a Johannesburg-based domestic worker, remits part of her salary to her family in Lesotho, covering school fees, groceries and household expenses. For years cross-border transfers were straightforward, then the process began taking longer and required additional steps.

"When the banking process became more difficult, I worried that my family would not receive the money when they needed it most," Seema told TechCabal. "I decided to try Mukuru after hearing about it from a friend. The registration was straightforward, and I could send money without the uncertainty I had started experiencing elsewhere."

*(In-scope significance: illustrates Lesotho's cross-border remittance dependence on South Africa and a shift from banks to fintechs such as Mukuru as SA remittance/banking rules tighten — a genuine cross-border/sovereignty angle for a country enclaved within ZAF.)*
