Yas, a subsidiary of AXIAN Telecom Holding & Management PLC, has completed the acquisition of 99.63% of Wananchi Group (Holdings) Ltd — the parent company of Zuku and Simbanet — in a transaction valued at approximately US$63 million. Executives announced this at a Kampala news conference on 10 November, confirming all country and regional regulatory approvals (including COMESA) were completed before clearance in Uganda and other markets.

Officials emphasized no job losses will result; the strategy is to facilitate business growth, network expansion and employment. "This acquisition marks a strategic step in our mission to make high-quality digital connectivity accessible and affordable to all. East Africa remains a priority growth market for AXIAN Telecom, and Uganda in particular offers tremendous opportunities," said Hassan Jaber, CEO of AXIAN Telecom. Both Zuku and Simbanet will continue operating under their established brand identities.

Genue Mwaura, Group CEO of Wananchi, said the company now needs "to move to the attacking side." Bertrand Lacroix, CEO of AXIAN Telecom Fibre, called it a major step toward building a leading pan-African broadband provider.

Over the next 12 to 24 months, Yas plans to upgrade Wananchi's fibre network in Uganda, focusing on speed, reliability and coverage, and expanding fibre-to-the-home (FTTH), plus next-generation solutions such as enterprise cloud services and IoT connectivity.

Wananchi is a leading East African provider of pay TV, broadband and internet services, best known for Zuku (retail broadband) and Simbanet (enterprise connectivity), operating in Kenya and Uganda. Yas/AXIAN Telecom is a pan-African telecom and digital infrastructure group with operations in 11 countries, spanning mobile, tower, fibre, data centre and fintech services, serving over 36 million customers.
