# Central Bank Of Tunisia Reports 81% Surge In Mobile Payments As Tunisia Accelerates Its Digital Transformation In 2025

> ## Excerpt
> (3 Minutes Read)

---
**(3 Minutes Read)**

**_In 2025, Tunisia’s payments landscape experienced a profound and structural transformation, marked by an 81% year-on-year surge in mobile payments and transfers. According to the latest bulletin published by the Central Bank of Tunisia, this remarkable growth underscores the country’s accelerating shift toward a digitally driven economy and reduced dependence on cash-based transactions._**

AfricaInvestment Analysis

Mobile payment activity climbed to 8.4 million transactions over the year, generating a total value of TND 1,769 million. This sharp rise reflects not only growing consumer confidence in digital financial services but also the rapid expansion of Tunisia’s supporting digital infrastructure. The number of active digital wallets reached 815,000 in 2025, up significantly from 595,000 in 2024. At the same time, the ecosystem of licensed payment service providers expanded to 435 entities, compared with 368 the previous year—highlighting increased competition, innovation, and service accessibility across the sector.

Transaction patterns reveal that person-to-person (P2P) transfers were the dominant driver of activity, accounting for 50.7% of total mobile operations. Merchant payments followed, representing 17.8% of transactions, signaling a steady normalization of mobile payments in everyday retail and commercial exchanges. These figures indicate that mobile platforms are no longer supplementary tools but are rapidly becoming central to daily financial interactions for individuals and businesses alike.

The Central Bank described this expansion as “explosive growth,” attributing it to deliberate policy initiatives designed to promote financial inclusion and reduce reliance on physical cash. Among the most significant enablers was the integration of the national E-Houwiya digital identity system with the Paysmart platform, enabling secure, seamless authentication for users. In parallel, modernization efforts strengthened the financial backbone of the country, including the adoption of the SWIFT MX (ISO 20022) messaging standard and the comprehensive overhaul of the National Clearing System—measures that improved interoperability, efficiency, and transaction security.

The 81% increase in mobile payments occurred alongside a 25% rise in overall electronic transactions nationwide, while traditional instruments such as checks continued their downward trajectory. Collectively, these developments point to a lasting behavioral shift among Tunisian consumers and businesses. Mobile solutions are increasingly the preferred channel for peer-to-peer transfers, bill payments, and retail purchases, signaling Tunisia’s decisive progress toward a more inclusive, resilient, and digitally integrated financial ecosystem.
