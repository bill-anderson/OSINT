Only 23% of rural communities in Nigeria have internet access, compared with 57% in urban areas, the NCC said, warning the disparity widens the digital divide. EVC Dr Aminu Maida disclosed this at the Rural Connectivity Summit in Lagos ("Leaving Nobody Behind"). "A community without digital connectivity is functionally invisible... This 'digital invisibility' is an unacceptable situation." Broadband penetration stands at 48.81%; a 10% increase can boost GDP by up to 1.38%. Through the Universal Service Provision Fund (USPF), the Commission runs targeted interventions including the Rural Broadband Initiative (RUBI) and Accelerated Mobile Phone Expansion (AMPE).
