Equinix (which acquired Nigeria's MainOne) announced plans to build a $22 million data centre in Lagos — christened LG3 — its first newly constructed facility in West Africa, targeted at local and international companies, scheduled operational by Q1 2026. It is the first step in Equinix's $100 million investment strategy in Africa over two years. Lagos hosts over 18 million active internet subscribers (Q1 2024), the highest of any Nigerian state, and multiple subsea cable landings. The Nigerian data centre market is projected to grow to over $684 million by 2030, with Lagos holding over 30% market share. LG3 will feature Equinix Fabric connecting to a global ecosystem of cloud providers across 270+ Equinix data centres.
