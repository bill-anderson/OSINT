The second phase of the Legal Basis Project (Legal Basis 2.0), an initiative aimed at strengthening compliance with Nigeria’s data protection laws, has been unveiled in Abuja, with regulators and industry stakeholders reaffirming their commitment to protecting the privacy rights of data subjects.
The project seeks to bridge the gap between policy and practical compliance by helping organisations implement the provisions of the Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDP Act) 2023 while promoting secure and rights-based data processing practices.
The unveiling took place during a Regulatory Engagement Session attended by key stakeholders in Nigeria’s digital economy, including representatives of the Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC), National Human Rights Commission (NHRC), Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN), Medical Laboratory Science Council of Nigeria, Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS), National Identity Management Commission (NIMC), Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission (FCCPC) and the Federal Ministry of Justice.
Participants stressed the need for a robust regulatory framework that ensures data processors and regulators respect the privacy and rights of individuals while maintaining accountability beyond reliance on consent alone.
The Legal Basis Project, developed by Tech Hive Advisory with support from Meta, was launched to simplify and promote the six lawful bases for data processing under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023. The six legal bases are Consent, Contract Necessity, Legal Obligation, Legitimate Interest, Vital Interest and Public Interest.
Speaking at the event, Meta’s Director of Privacy and Data Policy for Africa, the Middle East and Türkiye, Dr. Ololade Shyllon, described collaboration with regulators as critical to the success of the initiative.
She noted that the first phase of the project recorded significant engagement within six months of its launch, attracting 4,360 unique visitors from 72 countries, generating more than 1.58 million video views and reaching an estimated 1.6 million people across digital platforms.
According to her, while the first phase demonstrated growing awareness of data protection compliance, it also exposed major implementation gaps.
“The survey revealed that knowing is not the same thing as doing. Many organisations could identify the appropriate legal basis for processing data but failed to document it or build the necessary compliance structures around it,” she said.
Emerging Technologies and Policy Lead and Co-founder of Tech Hive Advisory Africa, Ridwan Oloyede, said the project is now moving from creating awareness to enabling implementation through practical compliance tools.
He explained that Legal Basis 2.0 is designed to help organisations close knowledge gaps, align with international best practices and better understand the application of Nigeria’s data protection laws.
According to him, the upgraded platform introduces an automated suite of compliance tools, including a Legal Basis Audit Tool for identifying compliance gaps, a guided Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) Builder, a Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) Builder, a Basis Switching Checker and a Privacy Notice Generator.
The platform also features an interactive Legal Basis Finder and an AI-powered chatbot, “Paddy,” to provide organisations with instant compliance guidance.
Oloyede said the new phase will also include a structured regulatory engagement programme with key data protection agencies to support organisations in meeting their compliance obligations under the Nigeria Data Protection Act.
Stakeholders at the event expressed confidence that the project would strengthen Nigeria’s data protection ecosystem by improving regulatory compliance, enhancing accountability and safeguarding the rights of data subjects in the country’s rapidly growing digital economy.
