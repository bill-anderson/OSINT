By Dr. Nizar Ben Neji, ICT Expert and Strategist and Former Minister of Communication Technologies of Tunisia (2021-2024).

Tunisia introduced E-Houwiya on August 2, 2022, to deploy a state-recognized nationwide digital identity system, legally equivalent to face-to-face identification and compliant with international standards. The author led the design and implementation of E-Houwiya during his tenure as Minister of Communication Technologies, establishing it as a cornerstone of Tunisia's 2025 digital strategy.

E-Houwiya is now open to the banking sector, enabling unbanked populations to access bank accounts, mobile payments, and financial services. It strengthens compliance with KYC (Know Your Customer) regulations published on February 28, 2025 by the Central Bank of Tunisia, and streamlines remote onboarding. As of March 2025, the total number of mobile-ID subscribers reached 200,000 citizens, including over 15,000 Tunisians living abroad.

E-Houwiya is integrated in digital procedures to: onboard citizens (create/update online accounts), log in with at least two authentication factors, digitally sign transactions and documents, prove consent/approve actions in self-service administrative procedures, prove eligibility online (e.g. age verification), autofill official-source data into forms, and approve wire transfers.

Issuing process and cost: Operation is the responsibility of the ICT department and affiliated entities — the National Digital Certification Agency, the National Computer Center, and local telecom operators. The ID is issued after identity confirmation, either physically at the operator's office or online through a visual interview. It is linked to the national ID number and issued only to holders of a physical ID (over age 18). It can be requested and renewed online, valid for three years. A personal X.509 certificate and digital signing crypto keys are generated on first issuance. The digital signature is a fully qualified mobile signature, legally equivalent to a handwritten signature. The E-Houwiya ID is free of charge to encourage adoption; identification via the digital ID is free and other functions (e.g. digital signature) are affordable or free.

E-Houwiya as SSO for e-government: It was developed as an inclusive, accessible, centralized authentication system enabling login to all government portals — E-Bawaba, E-CNAM, E-People, E-CNSS, Madrassati, and many others. There is no legal obligation to possess a digital identity, but more public and private entities are using E-Houwiya, and in the near future citizens without it will not be able to fully benefit from available online services. To ensure success and sustainability, the system must quickly reach critical mass and a large user base.
