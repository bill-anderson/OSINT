# Madagascar Set to Publish New Cybersecurity Law Amid Rising Digital Threats

Madagascar plans to publish a **new cybersecurity regulatory framework** soon, after launching a legal overhaul in December 2025. The Ministry of Digital Development, Posts and Telecommunications used the **first Cybersecurity Symposium, held in Antananarivo on June 22-23, 2026**, to provide an update.

Madagascar still relies on **legislation adopted in 2014**, considered inadequate for emerging technologies and threats. Authorities launched a comprehensive review in December 2025, initially targeting completion within three months. "The process is nearing completion and the new regulatory framework will be published shortly," said Minister **Mahefa Andriamampiadana**.

The new law will provide a precise definition of a **security incident** and establish criteria for identifying **Critical Infrastructure Organizations (Organisations d'Importance Vitale, OIVs)**, strengthening enforcement across public and private sectors. "This legal framework will not be merely theoretical. It will incorporate a system of strict injunctions… authorities will apply severe sanctions," said **Éric Rakotomaniraka**, DG of the Computer Incident Response Team (CIRT).

Per INTERPOL's 2025 Africa Cyberthreat Assessment Report, cyber-related crimes now account for **30% of all reported offenses in West and East Africa**, with ransomware rising sharply in 2024 (South Africa and Egypt highest, followed by Nigeria and Kenya).
