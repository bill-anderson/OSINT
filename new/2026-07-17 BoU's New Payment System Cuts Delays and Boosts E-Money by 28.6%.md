According to the Bank of Uganda's (BoU) latest Integrated Annual Report, electronic money transactions grew by 28.6% to UGX 326 trillion in financial year 2024/25. The main driver is the new third Automated Clearing House (ACH) session that started in February 2025, helping banks process payments faster. "We have reduced the time it takes to complete electronic fund transfers (EFTs), especially for government payments," said BoU Governor Michael Atingi-Ego. The new session closes at 3:30 p.m., enabling same-day receipt.

The central bank is preparing for the Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS) Replacement and ISO 20022 Migration, set to go live in November 2025, to make payment systems faster, more secure and regionally compatible. The National Payment Switch, also in progress, will connect banks, mobile money operators and fintech companies.

The value of transactions handled by Payment System Operators (PSOs) rose 49.3% to UGX 57.5 trillion. Agent banking jumped 76.1% to UGX 29.4 trillion, with agents rising 49.1% to 22,793. The BoU partnered with the Uganda Bankers Association through its Anti-Fraud Consortium; a new AI-powered Payments Control System (PCS) detects suspicious transactions instantly, reducing cyber fraud by 15%.

The report showed Uganda's e-payments index at 37% and financial inclusion at 70%. BoU trained 133 new financial literacy trainers (60% women), bringing certified trainers to 2,489. Through the Agricultural Credit Facility (ACF) and Small Business Recovery Fund (SBRF), BoU provided UGX 10.5 billion to 2,500 farmers and small businesses.

Challenges persist: debit card use dropped 5.1% to 3.6 million, and internet banking users fell 6.6% to 0.9 million, mainly due to cyber-security fears. Regionally, the East African Payment System (EAPS) saw inflows of UGX 1.3 trillion, a 17% increase, with Kenyan and Tanzanian currency transactions making up 99% of activity. MTN Uganda, handling 40% of the country's e-money transactions, reported a 28.6% rise in value to UGX 319.6 trillion. In the coming year, BoU plans to study a Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) and update its fintech sandbox program, including cashless school wallets. UNISS payment volumes were up 21.8% to 2.5 million.
