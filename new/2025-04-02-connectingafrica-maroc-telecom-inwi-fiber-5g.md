# Maroc Telecom and Inwi partner to deploy fibre and 5G

Moroccan operators Inwi and Maroc Telecom have joined forces to deploy fiber and 5G infrastructure in the North African nation.

Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa. April 2, 2025

Moroccan operators Inwi and Maroc Telecom have partnered to boost connectivity in the North African country through the deployment of fiber optic and 5G infrastructure.

The two are fast-tracking Morocco's fiber and 5G rollout by establishing two joint ventures, FiberCo and TowerCo, aimed at strengthening fiber expansion and supporting the development of 5G networks in the Kingdom.

FiberCo aims to expand fiber optic networks to reach 1 million connections within two years and 3 million within five years.

Meanwhile TowerCo is tasked to oversee the construction of 2,000 new towers in three years and 6,000 in ten years, to support the rollout of 5G.

The first phase of the project is valued at 4.4 billion Moroccan dirhams (US$458 million) over three years, pending approval by Morocco's National Telecommunications Regulatory Agency (ANRT).

According to Jassem Alzaabi, the chairman of e&, the owner of Maroc Telecom, the strategic collaboration between Maroc Telecom and Inwi, and the joint investments in fiber and 5G infrastructure, strengthens Morocco's position as an attractive destination for investment.

"Our focus will be on expanding digital infrastructure, promoting digital inclusion, and enabling various economic sectors to benefit from technological advancements. We will continue working closely with the government and private sector to build a robust digital ecosystem that enhances Morocco's competitiveness and supports sustainable development," Alzaabi explained.

## Old rivals share bread

This joint venture suggests that the Moroccan operators have settled a previous long-running legal dispute.

In 2024, Maroc Telecom was ordered by a court to pay MAD6.38 billion ($635 million at the time) to its competitor Inwi in reparation for violating fair competition rules.

According to Reuters, under the new fiber and 5G deal, Maroc Telecom has agreed to pay Inwi MAD4.38 billion ($456 million) in return for both operators abandoning legal appeals.

This all stems from 2021, when Inwi accused Maroc Telecom of abusing its dominant position in the market, leading to profit losses for Inwi.

The Commercial Court of Rabat originally ordered Maroc Telecom to compensate Inwi in January 2024, Maroc Telecom appealed, but the appeals court upheld the previous ruling in July 2024.

## Moroccan mobile competition

Wana Corporate (known commercially as Inwi) is the largest mobile operator in Morocco with 35.3% market share at the end of December 2024, according to the market intelligence from market research company Omdia, a sister company of Connecting Africa.

Maroc Telecom was second biggest with 32.5% market share, while Omdia's statistics place Orange Morocco close behind with 32.3% market share over the same period.

In 2020, regulator ANRT also ordered Maroc Telecom to pay MAD3.3 billion ($333 million at the time) for contravening the country's antitrust regulations on the implementation of local loop unbundling (LLU).

Maroc Telecom is 53% owned by the UAE's Etisalat (e&), while the Moroccan government owns 22% of the company. It is also listed on Casablanca stock exchange and on Euronext Paris.

North African countries have either been awarding 5G licenses or deploying the technology recently. Earlier this week, Swedish ICT company Ericsson and telecommunications operator Tunisie Télécom rolled out 5G mobile technology in Tunisia. The announcement came after Orange Tunisie, Ooredoo Tunisie and Tunisie Télécom all launched their 5G mobile services in February 2025, less than three months after receiving licenses. In July 2024, Telecom Egypt and Nokia partnered to bring 5G technology to Egypt.
