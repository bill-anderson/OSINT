# Data Protection Day 2026: Building Trust in a Digital Seychelles | 28 January 2026
Information Commission of Seychelles (author: Diana Benoit)

28 January is recognised globally as Data Protection Day, marking the anniversary of Convention 108, the first legally binding international treaty on privacy and data protection. Seychelles has been a signatory to Convention 108 since 1981. Local progress is matched by the Data Protection Act 2023, which gives people in Seychelles clearer rights over their personal data.

The statement sets out data-subject rights (to know what is collected, how it is used and shared; to correct or delete; to control their information) and organisations' responsibilities (collect only what is needed, protect against unauthorised access, be transparent), and stresses "privacy by design." This year's Data Protection Day theme is "Empowering Individuals to Take Control of Their Data"; Data Privacy Week 2026 carries "Prioritise Privacy by Design." The Information Commission is the national body responsible for compliance, guidance and enforcement, and can intervene where rights are not respected.
