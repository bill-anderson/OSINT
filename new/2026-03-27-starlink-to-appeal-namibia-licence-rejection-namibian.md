# Starlink to appeal Namibia's licence rejection

Starlink says it will pursue an appeal following CRAN's rejection of its application to operate in Namibia, describing the decision as a "disappointing outcome... Especially for the thousands of you who placed deposits, participated in the public consultation, and made your voices heard." The company said "hope is not lost," noting CRAN's process allows continued public input over the next 90 days and invites requests for reconsideration.

CRAN declined both the Class Comprehensive Telecommunications Service Licence and the spectrum licence, citing failure to meet local-ownership, regulatory-compliance and legal requirements — chiefly the requirement that telecom providers be at least 51% locally owned. Starlink is entirely foreign-owned and did not request an exemption. Starlink claims 98.6% of Namibian respondents in a public consultation supported its entry.
