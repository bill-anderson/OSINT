# Madagascar Set to Launch Starlink Direct-to-Cell Services

In a notice to the **US Federal Communications Commission (FCC) dated 13 May 2026**, SpaceX stated it is securing authorisations to launch **Direct-To-Cell (DTC) services in Madagascar**. The initiative, known as **Supplemental Coverage from Space (SCS)**, will be implemented in partnership with local operator **Airtel** and could connect millions of mobile subscribers to the Starlink network.

SpaceX's SCS operations in Madagascar will be conducted within spectrum bands and geographic areas where Airtel holds exclusive licensing rights, and SpaceX confirmed spectrum access agreements with Airtel granting use of designated portions of Airtel's licensed spectrum for SCS.

The notice follows Airtel Africa's confirmation of successful DTC testing integrating data and messaging via Starlink Mobile, where customers accessed low-data apps such as WhatsApp calling and Facebook Messenger and conducted financial transactions through the Airtel app.

**Serving over 5 million customers in Madagascar as of 2025**, Airtel will enable satellite connectivity directly on mobile devices, potentially reducing the need to build additional terrestrial infrastructure. Starlink operates a constellation of **650 low Earth orbit (LEO) satellites** designed for direct-to-device communication. With more than an estimated 80% of the population still unconnected, DTC could help bridge coverage gaps; Starlink's user-terminal broadband has been operational in Madagascar since 2023.
