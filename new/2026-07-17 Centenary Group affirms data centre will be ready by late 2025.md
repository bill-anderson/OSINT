Last month, the Centenary Bank Uganda Board of Directors visited the Centenary Group Data Centre construction site in Masaka City, confirming the project remains on schedule for completion in late 2025.

The facility will serve businesses, banks and organizations pursuing digital transformation across the region. Built with technology designed specifically for African conditions, it aims to provide reliable digital infrastructure for Uganda's growing economy. The Centenary Group Data Centre will consume 40% less power than conventional data centres through innovative cooling and power management systems.

"This data centre embodies our vision of meaningful inclusion in Uganda's digital economy," said Bwoch Gustavio, Centenary Bank Board Chairperson. "We're creating opportunities for every Ugandan business, from small enterprises to large corporations, to participate fully in the digital future."

The data centre features cloud-ready services and open-access connectivity, allowing all internet service providers to offer services through the facility, promoting competition. "Data centres provide the foundation for genuine digitalization," said Bishop Callist Rubaramira, Centenary Bank Board Director.

Centenary Technology Services confirmed the delivery schedule. "We're positioned to complete this facility by the end of 2025, targeting Tier 3 certification standards," stated Dr. Grace Ssekakubo, Centenary Technology Services CEO. "The green technology integrated into this data centre establishes new regional benchmarks. Our 40% power reduction represents responsible technology deployment."

Construction generated substantial benefits for Masaka City, with most supplies and materials sourced from local businesses, strengthening supply chains, creating employment and building technical capacity. The facility's open-access design ensures all ISPs can deliver services through the platform.
