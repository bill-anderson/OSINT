# SpaceX's Starlink goes live in Senegal

Connecting Africa - 6 February 2026

SpaceX's satellite Internet service Starlink has launched in Senegal, which now shows as "available" on Starlink's connectivity map. The launch supports Senegal's ambition to expand connectivity to remote and underserved communities, with the goal of connecting one million citizens by the end of 2026, closing last-mile gaps where terrestrial infrastructure is limited.

Senegalese users pay either XOF30,000 ($54) per month for a residential package or XOF22,000 ($39.50) for a residential lite package, plus XOF146,000 ($262) for a standard kit or XOF117,000 ($210) for a Mini kit. Starlink promises download speeds up to 305 Mbit/s. Senegal is the 26th African market for Starlink, which has been expanding across the continent since launching in Nigeria in January 2023.
