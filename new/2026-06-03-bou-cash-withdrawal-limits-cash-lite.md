The Bank of Uganda (BOU) has introduced limits on over-the-counter cash withdrawals from supervised financial institutions, starting January 1, 2027. Daily withdrawals are capped at sh50m for individual accounts and sh500m for corporate/business accounts; weekly caps are sh250m (individuals) and sh2.5b (businesses). The measures are in a circular (dated May 29, 2026) to CEOs of commercial banks, credit institutions and microfinance deposit-taking institutions.

"In line with the Bank of Uganda e-payments strategy, which aims to promote a cash-lite economy as part of the broader national digitisation agenda, the Bank of Uganda has reduced the interbank cheque value limits and introduced over-the-counter cash withdrawal caps," the circular says. BOU also cut interbank cheque limits by 50% (Uganda shilling from sh10m to sh5m; US dollar from $2,750 to $1,375; euro from €2,250 to €1,125; pound from £2,200 to £1,100; Kenya shilling from KSh300,000 to KSh150,000), aiming to shift transactions to mobile banking, internet banking and RTGS. The new limits take effect January 1, 2027.
