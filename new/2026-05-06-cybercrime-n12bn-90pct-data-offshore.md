NDPC figures presented at the IoT West Africa Conference 2026 indicate Nigeria records over 4,000 cyberattacks weekly with estimated financial losses of about N12 billion in 2024. Nearly 90% of Nigeria's data is hosted outside Africa, leading to an estimated $850 million annual spend on foreign cloud infrastructure, raising data sovereignty and economic-leakage concerns. National Commissioner Dr Vincent Olatunji said Nigeria's digital economy (estimated at $18.3 billion, contributing nearly 20% to GDP) is increasingly driven by data from mobile connectivity, AI and IoT: "Data is no longer just an asset; it has become critical infrastructure." Nigeria is among Africa's most targeted cyber environments.
