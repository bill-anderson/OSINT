# Digital dawn in the highlands

*Public Eye, 17 January 2026. By Keiso Mohloboli. T-Connect Impact launches at Soofia School, bringing Starlink-powered future to Butha-Buthe.*

*(Note: the article body dates the launch "Wednesday, 15 January 2025"; this appears to be a typo for 15 January 2026 given the 2026-01-17 publication. Flagged for ingest verification.)*

In the highlands of Butha-Buthe, T-Connect Lesotho officially launched its social impact initiative, T-Connect Impact, at Soofia International School. The launch introduced the T-Connect Impact Account, powered by Starlink Priority Connectivity, designed for schools, clinics and community centres — bringing stable satellite internet for digital education, telehealth, e-administration and innovation.

T-Connect MD Advocate Phelane Phomane said: "When we connect a school, we are connecting minds, dreams, and futures." Soofia International School (Botha-Bothe) has ~1,400 students, follows the Cambridge curriculum, employs 60 teachers, operates three computer labs with 120 computers and a robotics hub. Academic Advisor Jayant Vijayakumar said reliable connectivity enables uninterrupted access to digital learning platforms, virtual labs and online assessments.

The article describes this as one of the first structured efforts to deploy Starlink for public service institutions in Lesotho, with clinics gaining telemedicine, digital health records and remote specialist consultations.
