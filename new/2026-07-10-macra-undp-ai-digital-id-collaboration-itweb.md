# Malawi explores AI, digital IDs collaboration

The Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) and the UNDP are exploring new areas of collaboration to accelerate digital transformation, focusing on **artificial intelligence, digital identity and data protection**. Priority areas identified include **integrating the National Identity system for SIM card verification**, promoting responsible use of AI, strengthening data protection frameworks, and expanding digital skills among young people (building on MACRA's Universal Service Fund and programmes to equip secondary-school learners with digital skills).

MACRA Director General Mayamiko Nkoloma said partnerships are critical to ensuring Malawi's digital transformation is "secure, inclusive and responsive to emerging technologies." UNDP digital advisor Azhar Saeed Malik said successful adoption depends on protecting personal data and ensuring no one is left behind. Integrating the National Identity system with SIM verification is expected to improve telecom-service integrity, strengthen consumer protection and combat identity-related fraud.
