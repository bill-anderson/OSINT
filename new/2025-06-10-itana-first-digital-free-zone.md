Itana, Nigeria's and Africa's first fully digital free zone (Alaro City, within Lagos's Lekki Free Zone corridor), shifts special-economic-zone focus from manufacturing to services, technology and innovation. It creates a digital-first jurisdiction where companies can remotely incorporate, scale and serve global markets, inspired by Delaware and Dubai Internet City, leveraging Nigeria's 30-year-old free-zone regulations. Startups register with a $2,000 incorporation fee and $1,150 yearly renewal. Its first district (72,000 sq m in Alaro City) is under construction, backed by the Africa Finance Corporation (AFC), which committed $100 million to phase one — offering 24/7 gas-fired power, dual fibre internet and city management. Itana partners with Future Africa (founded by Flutterwave co-founder Iyinoluwa Aboyeji) to fund early-stage startups. Director of Business and Compliance Adetayo Oduwole: "Africa must tap into trade in services or risk falling further behind."
