# Malawi's ICT exports soar to K270 billion amid forex solutions

Speaking at the ICTAM Expo 2026 in Lilongwe, Sparc Systems Limited Group Managing Director Wisely Phiri said Malawi's ICT services exports have grown from about US$70 million in 2020 to US$154 million in 2023, demonstrating a functioning and competitive digital export sector capable of contributing meaningfully to foreign-currency inflows. He argued Malawi cannot continue to lament foreign-exchange shortages while underinvesting in industries already proving their capacity to generate external earnings.

Phiri presented Sparc Systems' blueprint for scaling IT services exports across Africa, saying the opportunity lies in expanding the capacity of local firms backed by investment in infrastructure, skills and supportive policy. Sparc showcased locally developed platforms across finance, agriculture, education, HR, construction and CRM — including Comply Quest (anti-money-laundering/compliance), Sparc Force, Sparc HR, Sparc CRM, Saris (student academic records), Farmers Payment Systems and Procomos (project/contractor monitoring).

Sparc Systems operates across Malawi, Zambia, Tanzania, Mozambique and Rwanda, specialising in enterprise infrastructure, cloud solutions, cybersecurity, software development, digital transformation, data-centre services and ICT training.
