Hundreds of Starlink kits installed at rural health centres under the government's digitisation programme are gathering dust as the parent ministry dithers on subscription payments. This emerged in the National Assembly when Health and Child Care minister Douglas Mombeshora was grilled by MPs on why the kits have not been activated so facilities can access network services during emergencies.

Mombeshora acknowledged that more than 400 health facilities have already received Starlink kits, with 300 more to be added, and said the ministry was negotiating a review of the subscription fee. "We understand they have been too high for us as a ministry to be able to pay the subscriptions. So, negotiations are taking place," he said. He added he would engage ICT minister Tatenda Mavetera to tap the Universal Service Fund (USF) to expedite payments.

Mavetera said the USF currently only supports subscription costs for digital centres and schools for one year, but would consider extending it to rural health centres. "Currently, the scope is mainly for our digital centres and schools. I am sure it is important that we also extend that to the hospitals as you have suggested," she said.
