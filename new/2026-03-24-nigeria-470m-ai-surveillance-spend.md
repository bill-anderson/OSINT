Nigeria has built one of Africa's largest AI-driven surveillance networks, spending over $470 million on facial recognition and vehicle (automatic number plate) tracking — leading the continent, per a March 2026 Institute of Development Studies report, "Smart City Surveillance in Africa: Mapping Chinese AI Surveillance across 11 Countries." Of at least $2.1 billion spent across 11 African nations, Nigeria accounts for roughly 10,000 smart cameras in major urban centres, surpassing Mauritius and Kenya. Researchers Wairagala Wakabi and Dr Tony Roberts found little evidence that expanded digital surveillance reduces crime; court records show minimal use of footage in prosecutions. Peter Obi has criticised the spending gap.
