Dar es Salaam. The Ministry of Information, Communication and Information Technology is launching a major initiative to ensure every Tanzanian is registered in the National Physical Addressing (NaPA) system.

NaPA's assistant coordinator, Arnold Mkude, announced the plans during an interview at the 48th Dar es Salaam International Trade Fair (DITF), themed "Tanzania: Your Best Destination for Business and Investment." Mr Mkude said the current fiscal budget is dedicated to a special operation to achieve complete registration of all Tanzanians in the NaPA database. "We're dedicating resources to achieving complete NaPA registration by the next fiscal year," he said. "However, this will be an ongoing effort as new houses and plots are continuously developed."

He said that since the release of the 2022 Population and Housing Census report, NaPA has reached 85 percent of Tanzanians nationwide. Despite this coverage, a number of people have yet to collect their placards and are unaware of their house numbers and street names.

Mr Mkude highlighted the importance of a physical address in business, investment and everyday activities, and urged people to visit the NaPA pavilion at the DITF to verify their data. For those not yet registered, the ministry set up facilities at the DITF to add them promptly to the database.

The ministry created a digital system called NaPA, which has been implemented in many places across the country, including residences, hospitals, shops, schools, banks and other government and private institutions. The aim is to enhance the accuracy and reliability of the national addressing system, contributing to the country's growth and development.
