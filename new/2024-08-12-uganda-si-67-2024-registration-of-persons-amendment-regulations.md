> Full verbatim text of the instrument (it is two operative pages).
>
> **The instrument is not what the acquisition queue described.** It was queued as "the instrument holding the KYC gate open." It is not. SI 67 of 2024 does one thing: it amends regulation 19 of the Registration of Persons Regulations, 2015 to extend national identification card validity **from ten to eleven years**, and it is **deemed to have come into force on 20 November 2015** — a retroactive commencement of almost nine years, which has the effect of validating cards that had expired on their printed ten-year term. Any wiki claim resting on SI 67/2024 as a KYC/SIM-registration instrument needs re-sourcing.

STATUTORY INSTRUMENTS

2024 No. 67.

The Registration Of Persons (Amendment) Regulations, 2024

(Under section 85 of the Registration of Persons Act, Cap. 332)

In Exercise of the powers conferred upon the Minister responsible for internal affairs by section 85 of the Registration of Persons Act, Cap. 332, these Regulations are made this 12th day of August, 2024.

1. Citation

These Regulations may be cited as the Registration of Persons (Amendment) Regulations, 2024.

2. Commencement

These Regulations shall be deemed to have come into force on the 20th day of November, 2015.

3. Amendment of S.I. No. 67 of 2015

The Registration of Persons Regulations, 2015 are amended in regulation 19—

a) by substituting for subregulation (1) the following—

"(1) A national identification card shall be valid for eleven years from the date of issue and shall be renewable in accordance with these Regulations."; and

b) by inserting immediately after subregulation (1), the following—

"(1a) A national identification card issued by the Authority with a validity period of ten years shall be valid for eleven years from the date of issue."

MAJ GEN KAHINDA OTAFIIRE,
Minister of Internal Affairs
