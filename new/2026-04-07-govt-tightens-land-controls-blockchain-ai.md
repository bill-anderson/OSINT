Faced with a sharp rise in land-related disputes, the Ugandan government is moving to tighten oversight, enforce existing laws, and introduce new technologies to restore order to one of the country's most contested sectors. Under Administrative Circular No. 1 of 2025, Resident District Commissioners and city authorities must take a more active role in overseeing land processes; no eviction should take place without district security committees and Ministry of Lands consultation. Stricter checks apply to larger parcels (above 0.5 acres in central Uganda, one acre elsewhere), which must be inspected to verify ownership before subdivision or transfer.

Beyond enforcement, the government is turning to technology: plans are underway to introduce blockchain and artificial intelligence systems to reduce fraud in land transactions, acknowledging that paper-based systems are vulnerable to manipulation. Mass land titling programmes aim to clarify ownership and resolve boundary disputes. Minister Judith Nabakooba reaffirmed the shift from reactive dispute resolution to preventive governance.
