The Zambian government is banking on a new partnership with Chinese tech giant Huawei to power digital government services across the country's 25 government ministries using AI infrastructure. A Memorandum of Understanding was signed between the Smart Zambia Institute and Huawei during the 2026 Zambia Mobile Congress in Lusaka. The partnership includes AI infrastructure, sovereign cloud hosting, digital skills training and support for DPI.

Vice President Dr. W.K. Mutale Nalumango said Huawei will support the country in building AI and national cloud infrastructure. As part of the deal, the two parties will co-invest to build a national data centre which will make it possible for critical data to be stored within the country. The MoU also includes digital skills development, with about 5,000 young Zambians expected to be trained in the next three years through the Huawei Global Academy. The MoU reinforces Huawei's partnership with the Zambian government, which has lasted over two decades (Huawei established a Zambia office in 2004 and built the Zambia National Data Centre). The partnership comes as Zambia prepares for full rollout of its national digital ID by year-end under the Digital Zambia Acceleration Project, funded through a $100 million World Bank loan.
