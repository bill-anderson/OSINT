Dar es Salaam. Tanzania is positioning itself to be a competitive player in the global digital economy through the strategic and ethical deployment of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies. This resolve was reinforced at the country's first National AI Forum held in Dar es Salaam from July 28-29, 2025, which convened tech experts from around the world to examine the transformative role of AI and discuss Tanzania's readiness to integrate it responsibly.

Themed "Reimagining Tanzania's Future through Inclusive and Ethical Artificial Intelligence," the forum underscored the urgency of ensuring that AI serves as a force for inclusive socioeconomic development rather than a source of division or harm. AI is already revolutionising sectors including healthcare, education, finance, agriculture, and communication, and Tanzanian stakeholders are eager to adapt tools such as Google AI Studio, ChatGPT, Gemini and Microsoft Copilot to local realities.

"The world has changed and AI is here to stay," said Director General of the Tanzania ICT Commission (ICTC), Dr Nkundwe Mwasaga, while opening the two-day forum. "This platform provides an opportunity to reflect on our digital journey, where we have come from, where we are, and where we are heading as a nation." Dr Mwasaga noted that while technological advances bring immense opportunities, they also raise questions of ethics, data privacy, and the responsible use of AI systems. "We must embed our Tanzanian values into these systems. Without ethical frameworks, our platforms will not be trusted nor effective," he emphasised.

Tanzania has demonstrated increasing commitment to becoming a digitally enabled economy, launching the National ICT Policy review process in 2021 and, in 2024, inaugurating major digital initiatives under President Samia Suluhu Hassan.
