Dar es Salaam. Tanzania's communications sector has grown rapidly between March 2021 and December 2025, with more enabling infrastructure, service penetration, competition and heightened consumer experience. Data released by the TCRA Director General, Dr Jabiri Bakari, shows sound policies and effective spectrum allocation have led to up to 400 percent growth in service areas.

5G, which entered Tanzania in 2022, has penetrated 30.13 percent of the population and covers 10.9 percent of the country; by December 2025 all regions had at least one 5G installation. Between March 2021 and December 2025, 2G penetration rose to 98.64 percent (from 96.1), 3G to 93.92 percent (from 70.3), and 4G to 94.2 percent (from 50.4).

The size and capacity of the National ICT Broadband Backbone (NICTBB) quadrupled, increasing tenfold from 200 Gbps to 2,000 Gbps, and the NICTBB has been extended to 85 districts. The number of international submarine fibre optic cables landing in Tanzania increased from two to three, and three more public data centres were built and commissioned, bringing the total to four. Tanzania also pioneered equipment type approval in East, Central and Southern Africa, with an equipment-type approval laboratory in Dar es Salaam completed on 27 March 2024.
