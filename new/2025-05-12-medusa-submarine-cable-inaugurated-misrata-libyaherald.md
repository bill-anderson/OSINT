The **Medusa** submarine cable project — an 8,700 km system connecting Libya to Europe and the Mediterranean basin (Italy, Spain, Portugal, Morocco, Tunisia, Greece, Egypt, Cyprus) — was inaugurated on 10 May in **Misrata**. State telecoms holding company **LPTIC** called it "a quantum leap," connecting Libya to major global ISPs and supporting 5G, data centres and cloud computing in Tripoli, Misrata and Benghazi.

LPTIC framed the cable as making Libya a "digital gateway to Africa" (exporting connectivity to Chad, Niger, Mali and Central Africa) and supporting "digital sovereignty." The 2023 contract was signed by the private-sector Libyan United International Company (LUIC); the cable is to land in Tripoli and Benghazi.
