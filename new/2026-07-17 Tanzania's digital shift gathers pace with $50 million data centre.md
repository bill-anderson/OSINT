Dar es Salaam. Wingu Africa has launched the second phase of its data centre in Tanzania, a $50 million investment expected to bolster the country's digital infrastructure and position it as a competitive player in the global digital economy.

Speaking at the launch in Dar es Salaam, Wingu Africa CEO Anton Volianskyi highlighted the new Tier 3 data centre, part of the company's expansion across Africa. The facility is designed to accommodate growing demand for data processing and cloud services, especially in an era driven by artificial intelligence and high-speed digital transactions. "As the world moves towards more data-driven technologies, Africa must be ready to support the power and connectivity requirements of these systems," he said.

The expanded facility, located in the Mbezi Industrial Area, is now the most connected data centre in Tanzania, with interconnections to 13 operators. It is designed to keep local internet traffic within the country, reducing latency and costs. With a 99.983 percent uptime guarantee, it is built for uninterrupted service. The investment adds 80 high-density racks, bringing the total to 110, tailored to the financial and technology sectors.

Tanzania Internet Service Providers Association (TSPAR) secretary-general Noah Maina said extending the internet exchange to Wingu Africa's data centre ensures data traffic remains local, improving efficiency, reducing costs and optimising network performance for all operators. He added that the partnership is expected to strengthen Tanzania's digital infrastructure and broader digital transformation efforts.
