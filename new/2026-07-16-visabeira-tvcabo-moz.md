The company increased its stake in TV Cabo to 80% through the purchase of a 30% position held by the state-owned mobile operator TMCEL.

The Portuguese group Visabeira intends to strengthen its investment in telecommunications in Mozambique, increasing its stake in TV Cabo from 50% to 80% through the purchase of a 30% position held by the state-owned mobile operator TMCEL.

The transaction is part of a merger notification received by the Competition Regulatory Authority (ARC), which opened a 15-day public consultation period to collect observations on the transaction, according to a notice to which Lusa had access this Thursday. According to the regulator, the acquisition will be carried out through Visabeira Moçambique, the local subsidiary of the Portuguese group, and involves a stake corresponding to 30% of the share capital of TV Cabo Comunicações Multimédia, currently held by TMCEL. With the completion of the operation, Visabeira will hold 80% of the company, acquiring exclusive control.

TV Cabo is one of the country's main electronic communications operators, providing integrated telecommunications services to the residential and business segments, including subscription television, fixed internet access, and voice services.

In a second notice today, the ARC refers that Visabeira also notified the regulator about the acquisition of an additional 30% stake in Televisa – Sociedade Técnica de Obras e Projetos, also held by TMCEL.

According to the regulator, the Portuguese group currently holds 50% of Televisa through Visabeira Global SGPS and, after the transaction is completed, will likewise control 80% of that company, which specializes in telecommunications network engineering, infrastructure construction, end-customer installation, and the operation and maintenance of electronic communications systems.

The two transactions simultaneously reinforce Visabeira's presence in complementary areas of the telecommunications sector in Mozambique, combining the provision of services to the final consumer through TV Cabo with engineering, construction, and infrastructure maintenance activities through Televisa.

According to the reports and accounts of Visabeira Moçambique for the fiscal years 2023 and 2024, published by the Mozambique Stock Exchange (BVM), the Portuguese group acquired an additional 30% stake in TV Cabo Moçambique and another 30% in Televisa in 2023, both previously held by TMCEL. According to the same documents, those operations then raised Visabeira's stakes in the two companies to 50% and included a repurchase option in favor of TMCEL until the end of 2025. With the transactions now notified to the ARC, the Portuguese group intends to move from a joint position to an 80% stake in both companies, consolidating control of both assets.

The investment comes at a time when Visabeira continues to deepen its presence in Mozambique, one of the Portuguese company's historical African markets. In December, the group signed an agreement with the Mozambican state-owned enterprises Eletricidade de Moçambique (EDM) and Hidroelétrica de Cahora Bassa (HCB) to create Soluções Elétricas Globais (SEG), a company destined to provide engineering services in the area of energy in Mozambique and other markets in Southern Africa.

On that occasion, the vice-president of Visabeira, Fernando Daniel Nunes, told Lusa that Mozambique represented less than 5% of the group's consolidated turnover, but remained a market of reference and one of the Portuguese company's main African geographies. "We continue to be a reference within the economic landscape in Mozambique," said the administrator at the time, also highlighting the historical and emotional connection of the group's founder to the country.

The two transactions are now under evaluation by the ARC, which has granted a 15-day period for the presentation of observations by interested third parties.
