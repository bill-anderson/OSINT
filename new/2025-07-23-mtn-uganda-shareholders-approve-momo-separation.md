MTN Uganda has received overwhelming shareholder approval to hive off its mobile money unit, MTN MoMo (U) Limited, from its core telecom business and list it separately on Uganda's stock exchange. The vote (at an Extraordinary General Meeting) followed weeks of investor pushback, postponed meetings and legal threats.

MoMo will merge into a new entity called MTN New FinCo, majority-owned by MTN Group Fintech Holdings and a trust representing minority shareholders, eyeing a future listing on the Uganda Securities Exchange. CEO Sylvia Mulinge called it a pivotal step aligning with MTN Group's Ambition 2025. Chairman Charles Mbire suggested a dual listing within three to five years. MTN Uganda has over 21 million subscribers; MoMo handles over $14 billion (UGX 55 trillion) in annual transactions and over 10 million active users. MTN Uganda joins MTN Ghana in advancing MoMo spin-offs (MTN Nigeria already operates MoMo PSB separately).
