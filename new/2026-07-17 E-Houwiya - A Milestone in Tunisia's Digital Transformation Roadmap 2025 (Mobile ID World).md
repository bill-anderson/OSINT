By Dr. Nizar Ben Neji, ICT Expert and Strategist and Former Minister of Communication Technologies of Tunisia (2021-2024). (This is a repost of the same authored article carried by IDTechWire.)

Tunisia introduced E-Houwiya on August 2, 2022, to deploy a state-recognized nationwide digital identity system, legally equivalent to face-to-face identification and compliant with international standards. The author led the design and implementation of E-Houwiya during his tenure as Minister of Communication Technologies, establishing it as a cornerstone of Tunisia's 2025 digital strategy.

E-Houwiya is now open to the banking sector, a significant milestone toward financial inclusion. By integrating with digital banking and fintech services, it enables unbanked populations to securely access bank accounts, mobile payments, and financial services. As of March 2025, the total number of mobile-ID subscribers reached 200,000 citizens, including over 15,000 Tunisians living abroad. The ID is designed to prove online identity and is integrated in digital procedures for onboarding, authentication, digital signatures, consent approvals, and financial transactions.

Issuing process and cost: The issuing process and operation are managed by the ICT department and affiliated entities. The digital ID is issued after identity verification and is valid for three years. Digital signatures made with the E-Houwiya ID are legally valid and equivalent to handwritten signatures. The E-Houwiya ID is free of charge to encourage adoption, with affordable or free digital signature services.

E-Houwiya as SSO for e-government: It serves as a centralized authentication system enabling users to log in to digital portals such as E-Bawaba, E-CNAM, E-People, and more. While not legally required, its growing adoption is making it essential for accessing online services. E-Houwiya is a key enabler for digital services in Tunisia; achieving a high adoption rate is crucial to ensuring sustainability and scalability.
