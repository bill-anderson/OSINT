Tanzania has a domestic national identification card distinct from the passport or emergency travel document, issued by the National Identification Authority (NIDA). It serves as the official National ID card for Tanzanian citizens and eligible residents.

**National ID Card (NIDA Card).** A biometric identity card used for a wide range of domestic purposes, including access to government services, banking, voting and employment.

Key details:
- Eligibility: all Tanzanian citizens aged 18 and above, and legal residents with valid permits.
- Application: complete the online form via the NIDA Online Portal; print and submit with required documents at the nearest NIDA district office; undergo biometric registration (fingerprints, photo and signature).
- Required documents: birth certificate or sworn affidavit; parent's NIDA ID or birth certificate (for citizenship by descent); for residents, passport plus residence/work permit.
- Use cases: access to healthcare, education and financial services; voter registration; employment verification; SIM card registration.

**Process for a National ID card:**
1. Start online through the NIDA Online Registration Portal (open to Tanzanian citizens or non-citizens with a valid Resident or Work Permit). Complete the online form and upload scanned copies of required documents (PDF one page per document, JPG or PNG). For citizens: birth certificate; a copy of a parent's birth certificate or National ID; if previously registered, the citizenship registration (Dossier) number. For non-citizens: passport number and a valid Tanzanian Resident or Work Permit.
2. Visit the local government office to obtain the official stamp and signature of the Local Chairman.
3. Submit the stamped, signed form and printed documents to the NIDA District Office.
4. Attend a biometric appointment (fingerprinting and photo) scheduled by NIDA.

Additional requirements include a valid email address (used to receive the registration link), a mobile phone number, names of parents, house number/street/ward, any previous names, and, if available, school certificates, TIN and voter's card.

Official information is available via the NIDA main website, the NIDA Online Services Portal (apply for a NIN, generate payment control numbers for ID replacement, track application status) and the NIDA e-Registration Portal (https://eonline.nida.go.tz/). The Tanzania Investment Centre also publishes guidance (https://procedures.tic.go.tz/procedure/412?l=en).

Non-citizens may apply provided they hold a valid Tanzanian Resident or Work Permit; the absence of a local birth certificate is not a barrier where such permits are in place. It is not possible to obtain a National ID without a valid residence permit — foreign nationals must first obtain a Residence Permit through the Tanzania Immigration Department.
