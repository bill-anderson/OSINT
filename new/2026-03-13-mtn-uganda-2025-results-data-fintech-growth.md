Strong demand for data and fintech services drove MTN Uganda to double-digit growth in 2025. The Kampala-listed operator reported revenue of $970 million (Ush3.6 trillion) for the year ended December 2025, a 13.6% increase, amid low inflation, a stable currency and rising foreign investment. Data revenue jumped 28.8% to $270 million (Ush1.0 trillion), supported by 1.8 million new active data users (total 12 million); smartphone penetration rose to 42.8% via device financing.

Fintech (largely mobile money) revenue climbed 17.3% to $285 million (Ush1.1 trillion). CEO Sylvia Mulinge said MTN will continue investing in network expansion, fibre infrastructure and fintech innovation. Uganda's central bank projects economic growth of 6.5–7.0% in 2026. (Uganda was the third MTN market to publish 2025 earnings, after MTN Nigeria and MTN Ghana.)
