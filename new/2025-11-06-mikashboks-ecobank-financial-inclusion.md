MikashBoks, a local fintech, entered a strategic partnership with Ecobank Sierra Leone, unveiled on Wednesday, November 5, 2025, at Ecobank's Head Office on Charlotte Street in Freetown. The collaboration, themed "Empowering Digital Payments for a Brighter Future," aims to expand savings and financial access for underserved populations, particularly in the informal sector. Stakeholders included the Bank of Sierra Leone, the British High Commission, and the UNDP.

Simon Levell, CEO of MikashBoks, described the platform as a homegrown fintech conceived by founder Sultan Ma Sallie to promote savings and financial access. Through the collaboration, customers can use the MikashBoks app or a USSD code to create a savings plan ("Interest Plus") offering 6% annual interest. Deposits made via mobile money are held in a master account at Ecobank. "With this partnership, every mobile phone becomes a bank branch," Levell said, noting alignment with the Bank of Sierra Leone's National Financial Inclusion Strategy.

British High Commissioner Josephine Gauld commended the initiative, noting "80% of the funding comes from UK-based investors." UNDP's Richard Musinguzi highlighted work on formalizing informal businesses, especially female-led enterprises.

Representing the Governor of the Bank of Sierra Leone, Morial Bangura recalled the Central Bank's efforts since 2008 — the National Financial Inclusion Strategy, the National Switch, and the Regulatory Sandbox. "With innovation and collaboration, we can move financial inclusion from 39% to much higher levels," he said. Sebastian Ashong-Katai, MD of Ecobank Sierra Leone, said the partnership symbolizes the future of digital banking in Africa.
