Analysis (Equity Axis, Blessing Kanyemba) of a new phase in Zimbabwe's mobile money competition, where the winner will be the platform that can put physical cash in a customer's hand without forcing them to an agent's shop.

The shift began with O-Mari, which capitalised on Old Mutual's integration of CABS (its banking subsidiary) to let O-Mari wallet holders withdraw cash directly at CABS ATMs — without deploying a single new machine, by routing withdrawals through existing interbank settlement connectivity. EcoCash, which has defined the market since the early 2010s on its agent network, responded by integrating withdrawals into TN CyberTech Bank's ATM network. Innbucks went further, building and deploying its own cash recycler ATMs (ten installed, 78 in the pipeline) — machines that accept deposits and immediately re-dispense the notes, co-located at Simbisa Brands food outlets trading until 10pm for 24-hour-style cash access.

The piece argues NetOne holds the clearest rural inclusion opportunity through its unactivated One Wallet and a potential integration with AFC Holdings (Agricultural Finance Corporation) — both ultimately owned by the government via the Mutapa Investment Fund — which could extend cash access into farming communities, crop buying points and underserved districts, but has not moved. It notes ZIPIT allows some cross-platform transfers already but has not achieved seamlessness, so network effects still favour EcoCash's scale.
