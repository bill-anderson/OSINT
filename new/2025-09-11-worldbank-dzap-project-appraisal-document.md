9. A key element supported by the project is Digital ID. Zambia is transitioning from its legacy paper-based National Registration Card (NRC) system, introduced before independence in 1964, to a more modern and integrated digital identity ecosystem. The NRC system covered some 94 percent of the adult population by 2021, and subsequent advances through the Integrated National Registration Information System (INRIS) have registered 15 percent of adults using biometrics. However, the Government is planning to implement a homegrown foundational ID system, based on open standards, and building on the existing investments in order to close the coverage gap of about 85 percent. This new system will prioritize privacy, security, and interoperability, enabling the Government to manage its infrastructure independently and reduce reliance on external vendors. The Ministry of Home Affairs and Internal Security (MoHAIS) is currently collecting minimal demographic data (such as Name, Date of Birth, Address, Gender and biometric attributes -- 10 fingerprints and a photograph); only what is needed to support a unique ID. No sensitive or ethnic data is captured. ID enrollment is free of charge with the first issuance of the ID credential expected to be free. All citizens, marginalized communities, Refugees and Legal residents are eligible for enrollment. Through the ongoing ID4D technical assistance, the government is being supported to develop an operational plan, which includes expanding registration sites via post offices and implementing a public engagement strategy to accelerate registration campaigns.

10. Digital ID is part of Zambia's broader vision to establish a robust DPI, with digital ID serving as a foundational building block for inclusive service delivery. Complementary platforms -- such as ZamPay, the Government's eServices portal, ZamSign for e-Signatures, ZamConnect for data integration, ZamPay for interoperable digital payments and ZamConnect for data exchange -- are critical enablers of this vision, facilitating seamless access to public services, enhancing financial inclusion, and driving private sector innovation. ZamPortal, currently offers access to just 300 out of an estimated 1,500 government services. The government plans to provide various authentication options, including via biometrics, smart cards, QR codes, One-Time Passcodes (OTPs) and Paper-Based Credentials with digital features ensuring inclusivity and accessibility for all users. Through integration with various public and private services, this will enable seamless access to healthcare, financial services, and social welfare programs fostering trust and empowering individuals to manage their identity data securely.
