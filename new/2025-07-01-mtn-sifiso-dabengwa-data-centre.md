MTN launched a $240 million data centre in Ikeja, Lagos, named after late MTN Group CEO Sifiso Dabengwa — Nigeria's largest pre-fabricated modular data centre, with 96 containerised modules and 1,500 racks across three floors. The 9MW facility is built in two parts; the first phase (a 4.5MW Tier 3 facility worth $120 million) opened July 1, 2025. It localises data hosting, reducing risks such as internet disruptions from undersea cable failures (as in early 2024). CEO Karl Toriola stressed AI, cloud and enterprise solutions; the pay-as-you-use model priced in naira helps firms avoid FX challenges. Phase 1 required $100 million in infrastructure plus $20 million for cloud integration, positioned as a cost-effective alternative to hyperscalers AWS and Microsoft Azure.
