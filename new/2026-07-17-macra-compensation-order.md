By Mphatso Khutcha Richard

Airtel Malawi and TNM customers will receive compensation after the Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority (MACRA) ruled that the two mobile network operators unlawfully increased tariffs without following required legal procedures.

MACRA has directed the companies to compensate affected customers for the period the unlawful tariffs were in effect, saying telecommunications operators must comply with the law before implementing price increases.

According to MACRA Director General Mayamiko Nkoloma, both operators violated Section 76 of the Communications Act 2016, which requires new tariffs to be published in at least two daily newspapers 7 days before they effect the adjustments.

MACRA says following this, customers affected between June 26 and July 2, 2026 will get bundle credits equal to the price difference.

"Following MACRA's intervention Airtel and TNM will compensate customers who were affected during the mandatory seven day notice period" reads the letter.

The Authority says the compensation intends to remedy the impact of the premature implementation of revised tariffs and restore consumers to position.

MACRA has given the companies until July 31, 2026 to finish paying back customers, and has ordered them to fix their compliance systems and submit proof.

Many Malawians woke up to new tariffs weeks ago, a development which some social media commentators said the two mobile network providers collisioned.
