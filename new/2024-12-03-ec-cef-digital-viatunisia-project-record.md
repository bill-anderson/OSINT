**Acquisition note (2026-07-20):** this is the European Commission's own CEF Digital project record
for ViaTunisia — the Bizerte–Marseille segment of Medusa. It puts the funding on a documentary
footing and **corrects the figure the wiki carried from trade press**: the EC gives the CEF Digital
contribution as **€9.6 million** against a **€32 million** total project budget, not the "€10m /
~30%" reported by Developing Telecoms, Computer Weekly and Submarine Networks. €9.6m of €32m is
exactly 30%, so the trade-press "€10 million" is a rounding of this figure, and the EC record is the
precise one. Note also that the EC record gives the **project start as 1 March 2023** and states no
grant-agreement signature date; the "signed December 2022" date in the queue line comes from Orange
via the trade press, not from this record. Page carries "Publication 03 December 2024" and
"Last update 3 December 2024".

# ViaTunisia

- PROJECTS STORY
- Publication 03 December 2024

This project aims to strengthen the quality, capacity and competitiveness of connectivity between the EU and Africa by deploying a new, direct submarine route between France and Tunisia.

Locations

France – Marseille

Tunisia – Bizerte

Cable length

1,050 km

Project start - end date (duration)

1 March 2023 – 31 August 2025 (32 months)

## What will it provide?

The project will offer a direct, high-capacity and secure route addressing the expected exponential traffic increase between Tunisia and France, at competitive market prices.

## How will it work?

Following the 'open access cable' model, the system owner, a neutral and independent cable operator, will provide wholesale access under fair, cost-effective and attractive conditions, in a non-discriminatory manner, allowing operators to benefit, through wholesale offers, from the latest transmission technologies at competitive prices.

To enhance technological and commercial competition, the cable is designed as an open system: the line terminal equipment allowing traffic transmission between France and Tunisia is not purchased with the submarine vendor (turn-key offer). Instead, fibre pairs can be lit at different times with different vendors.

## How will it be built?

The project will manage the works associated to the design and the construction of the submarine system, including the obtention of permits, route cable study and route surveys, the manufacture of the subsea equipment such as optical cable and active equipment powered from the landing stations, up to the final marine operations for the deployment of the cable

The ViaTunisia cable will carry 20 Tb per fibre pair and will rely on state-of-the-art technologies for submerged equipment and end-to-end (E2E) hardware and software solutions that are already field-proven.

## How is it financed?

The ViaTunisia project has a total budget of €32 million of which €9.6 million funded under the Connecting Europe Facility (CEF) Digital programme.

## Who is involved?

The ViaTunisia project is being undertaken by Orange Wholesale, as coordinator.
