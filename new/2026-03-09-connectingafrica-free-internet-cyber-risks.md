# Senegal's free Internet plan could pose cybersecurity risks

Connecting Africa - 9 March 2026

Senegal's plan to roll out free Internet to one million people is commendable, but without a rigorous cybersecurity system beneficiaries could face regular cyberattacks. In February, President Faye officially launched the Universal Connectivity initiative; two days before the launch, the Directorate of File Automation (DAF) - which manages the biometric population register and issues national ID cards, passports and residency documents - was hacked, reportedly by the "Green Blood Group," which took a large cache including biometrics, identity records, birth records and immigration files.

In October 2025, the national tax authority (Direction Generale des Impots et des Domaines, DGID) was reportedly hacked by "Black Shrantact," which took about 1TB of data including tax declarations, identity documents and scanned credentials. As of end-2025, online penetration stood at 60.6%, but only 3% of rural households have Internet access. Cybersecurity expert Clement Domingo called for a national cybersecurity agency, an observatory and a task team, warning that Senegal's geopolitical position makes persistent attacks likely.
