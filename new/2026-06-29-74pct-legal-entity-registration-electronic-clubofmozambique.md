# Mozambique: 74% of legal entity registration applications now submitted electronically, minister says

Mozambique’s Minister of Justice, Constitutional and Religious Affairs, Mateus Saize, said on Wednesday in St Petersburg, Russia, that 74% of legal entity registration applications in the country are now submitted electronically.

Speaking at the 14th St Petersburg International Legal Forum, held from 24 to 26 June, Saize said all registration and notarial services, as well as the prison system and criminal records registry, now operate with digital databases as part of the government’s efforts to reduce bureaucracy and enhance public transparency.

The minister identified Law No. 8/2024 of 7 June, which governs the electronic processing of legal proceedings, as the cornerstone of Mozambique’s judicial modernisation programme.

Saize also highlighted the approval of the country’s cybersecurity and cybercrime laws, which are awaiting publication, and said the adoption of artificial intelligence within Mozambique’s justice system would be guided by strict ethical principles.

He concluded by expressing Mozambique’s interest in strengthening international technical cooperation to accelerate these structural reforms.
