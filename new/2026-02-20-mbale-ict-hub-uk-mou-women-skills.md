Uganda and the United Kingdom have signed a memorandum of understanding to establish a shillings three billion ICT Hub in Mbale district, a project led by UK-based civil society organisation Spotlight on Africa. The initiative aims to accelerate digital transformation, expand access to technology, and create opportunities for young women to pursue careers in ICT and entrepreneurship, positioned as a catalyst for inclusive growth as the world adapts to AI.

Hefin Rees, chairperson of Spotlight on Africa, said (February 19, 2026) the group already operates two ICT centres in Mbale and has graduated 800 young women, 60% of whom are now employed: "This is not charity for the middle class... We are here for the poorest communities." British High Commissioner Lisa Chesney said the initiative "demonstrates the power of partnerships in driving innovation and economic growth" and empowers young women and underserved communities.
