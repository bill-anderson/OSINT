# Renforcement de capacités en IA appliquée à la sécurité humaine : le Bureau de la coordination des Nations unies au Niger perfectionne 140 cadres

*(Le Sahel / ONEP is the Niger state daily. Capture is not endorsement.)*

The UN Resident Coordinator's Office in Niger, funded by the **UN Trust Fund for Human Security** via its **Illimi Niger** programme, ran a capacity-building workshop **1–12 June 2026 in Niamey** for **140 actors** on **AI applied to human security**, within the UN 2.0 approach (strategic use of data, digital, innovation and AI toward the SDGs). Opening on Monday 9 June 2026, interim UN Resident Coordinator **Djanabou Mahondé** stressed that the capacity to collect, analyse and use data intelligently "is no longer a luxury [but] a strategic necessity."

Participants (four cohorts of 25) come from implementing partners: the Ministry of Interior, the Haute Autorité à la Consolidation de la Paix, the Direction Générale de la Planification et de la Programmation du Développement, the Cellule d'Analyse Stratégique, the **Institut National de la Statistique (INS)** and the **ANSI**. Illimi Niger is building a **multisectoral data platform** — a shared government/UN reference space to anticipate crises, guide public policy and protect vulnerable populations.
