Zimbabwean billionaire Strive Masiyiwa and Google Senior VP James Manyika helped anchor Google's $1 billion Africa cloud and AI investment drive at the inaugural Africa Cloud Summit in Johannesburg, alongside President Cyril Ramaphosa — Google's biggest infrastructure bet on the continent to date.

Through Liquid C2, part of Masiyiwa's Econet Group, Africa's first "Google Cloud Partner Experience Centre" was launched in Johannesburg, giving developers and enterprises direct, low-latency access to Google's cloud and AI tools on-continent — critical for banks, telcos and governments with tightening data-sovereignty laws. The piece frames this as Liquid C2 exporting Zimbabwean-owned digital infrastructure into Africa's largest economy, flipping the script "from Zimbabwe as a tech consumer to a regional cloud enabler."

Three moves landed at the summit: (1) an infrastructure deal putting African compute inside Africa, cutting reliance on Europe/US servers; (2) an AI jobs pipeline — Masiyiwa announced compute grants for researchers and a target to move 1 million young Africans into AI-related jobs, pairing Google Cloud credits with Liquid's fibre; and (3) policy influence, with Manyika (a Zimbabwean-born AI ethicist driving Google's global tech policy) shaping both capital and rules. Masiyiwa said the goal is "African problems solved with African compute"; Manyika said Africa's AI future rests on "infrastructure + skills + governance."
