# Somalia opens national ID center in Boondheere to expand access to services

Mogadishu (HOL), Sunday April 20, 2025

Somalia's National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA) has opened a new service center in Mogadishu's Boondheere District, advancing a national campaign to expand civil registration. The center, launched by NIRA Deputy Director-General Abdirahman Said and District Commissioner Abdullahi Hasan, will issue national ID cards, birth certificates and related documents. It supports NIRA's mission under the 2023 Identification and Registration Act, which mandates issuance of unique National Identification Numbers (NINs).

The launch also supports preparation for Somalia's first one-person, one-vote elections since 1967; NIRA is working with the National Independent Electoral Commission to integrate the ID system into voter registration, with elections scheduled for 2026. Authorities view improved identity verification as a tool to counter al-Shabab infiltration and organized crime; the Somali Police Force, with FBI support, uses a biometric identification system to track criminal suspects. NIRA has also partnered with the Somali Banks Association to allow customers to use national IDs for banking.

The rollout faces challenges: some regional states, including Puntland, have raised concerns over federal overreach and data sovereignty, launching their own civilian registration systems, while others have voiced fears over data privacy and where personal information is stored.
