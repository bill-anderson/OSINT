# Malawi eyes wider digital access in $180m Huawei ICT deal

Malawi is pursuing a proposed US$180 million investment with Huawei to expand internet access, strengthen ICT infrastructure and bring more digital services to communities over the next four years. Minister of Information and Communications Technology Shadric Namalomba held high-level discussions with Huawei officials in China, presenting (during a Monday meeting) a four-year roadmap focused on improving access to government services and strengthening digital infrastructure.

The plan is built around two key projects. The first is a one-stop digital government platform allowing citizens to access various public services through a single online portal — passport applications, business registration and tax payments. The second focuses on national connectivity: a high-speed network linking all 28 districts of Malawi and incorporating ring-protection technology designed to reduce service disruptions caused by cable failures.

Namalomba said the initiative goes beyond infrastructure development, aiming to provide citizens with more efficient, transparent and reliable access to public services. Details on the funding structure, implementation timeline and any agreements reached with Huawei have not yet been made public. (Companion coverage frames the plan as anchored on the Boma Lathu platform and the National Data Centre; itweb.africa and techreviewafrica reported the parallel Government Data Sharing Regulation.)
