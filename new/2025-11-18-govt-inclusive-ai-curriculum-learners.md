The government will soon introduce an inclusive Artificial Intelligence (AI) curriculum aimed at improving personalised learning for students with diverse needs, according to Dr Fredrick Edward Kitoogo, principal of the Uganda Institute of ICT (UICT). The curriculum will enable learners to use AI to create localised, culturally relevant content reflecting Ugandan realities, develop skills for local problems (agriculture, healthcare), support AI-powered teaching, and reduce the digital divide, ensuring equitable access including for learners with disabilities.

Kitoogo said the government is working towards establishing functional innovation hubs in all regions by 2027, under Pillar 5 (Innovations and Entrepreneurship) of Uganda's Digital Transformation Roadmap (2023/24–2027/28). He delivered a keynote on National Direction on AI Digital Skills and Inclusion at the Annual Disability Lecture 2025 at Makerere University Business School (MUBS) on November 14, 2025, themed "AI for Tomorrow." Gorretti Byomire (MUBS Disability Resource and Learning Centre) said within five years anyone who has not embraced AI may struggle to find employment.
