Powertel Communications, a ZESA subsidiary, has partnered with Paratus Zimbabwe to launch the first phase of a high-capacity fibre optic corridor linking Zimbabwe with Botswana, enhancing regional digital connectivity. The first operational section, between Plumtree and Bulawayo, is now live and carrying traffic under a public-private partnership (PPP) signed in June 2025. Once completed, the corridor will connect Zimbabwe to Botswana, Zambia and South Africa.

Powertel MD Willard Nyagwande called it "a defining moment," noting the project is "planned, built, owned and operated by Powertel as the licensed national carrier under POTRAZ and the telecommunications arm of ZESA." An Indefeasible Right of Use (IRU) agreement with Paratus enabled the project to attract investment while allowing Powertel to retain operational control. Paratus Group CCO Martin Cox said it was "about building the digital foundations that enable economic growth, regional integration and improved access to world-class connectivity."

The route operates with an initial traffic capacity of 800 Gbps using Dense Wavelength Division Multiplexing (DWDM), capable of supporting more than 10 Tbps as demand increases. The second phase, extending the corridor from Bulawayo to Livingstone in Zambia, is under construction and expected to be commissioned in September 2026, completing the Botswana-Zimbabwe-Zambia digital corridor. Shareholder Mutapa Investment Fund was represented at the launch.
