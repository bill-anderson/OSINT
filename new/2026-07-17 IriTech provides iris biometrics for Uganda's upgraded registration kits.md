The next-generation biometric registration kits rolled out across Uganda — as the country attempts to reach 33 million people registered for the national ID system — include upgraded iris biometrics scanners from IriTech.

Uganda's National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA) rolled out more than 5,665 biometric registration kits to enroll residents to Ndaga Muntu under its 2024 to 2026 campaign. The biometric registry is used to issue ID cards and to support public service delivery and financial inclusion. The new kits collect fingerprint biometrics but also iris biometrics using the IriShield BK binocular scanner from IriTech.

Iris biometrics were introduced to better serve elderly people, manual laborers and others: more than 1 million Ugandans had difficulty registering their fingerprint biometrics, according to NIRA figures cited by IriTech. The inclusion of iris biometrics also makes the system highly resilient against identity fraud, the company says.

The company attributes NIRA's decision in large part to the accuracy of IriTech's iris image processing and matching algorithm in NIST testing and global certifications including India's STQC and BIS, and MOSIP L0 compliance. The IriShield-USB BK212U iris scanner was one of the first scanners certified to MOSIP's MACP Framework, through an assessment by BixeLab. The IriShield also provides on-board processing and liveness detection, with a "compact yet rugged" design for reliable operation in remote rural areas.

NIRA has migrated 28 million biometric records to the MOSIP digital ID platform, in the open-source platform's first completed brownfield implementation.
