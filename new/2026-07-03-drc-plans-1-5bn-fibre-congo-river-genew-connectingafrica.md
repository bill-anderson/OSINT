# DRC plans $1.5B fiber-optic project along Congo River

*By Matshepo Sehloho, Associate Editor, Connecting Africa. July 3, 2026.*

The Democratic Republic of the Congo has partnered with China's Genew Technologies to build a fiber-optic route along the Congo River worth US$1.5 billion.

The Democratic Republic of the Congo's (DRC's) Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and Digital Affairs, has partnered with China's Genew Technologies to build a fiber-optic project along the Congo River worth US$1.5 billion.

The two entities signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) for the fiber deployment project.

The project covers about 2,100km of infrastructure, including 1,700km of fiber-optic cable along the Congo River from Muanda to Kisangani and nearly 400km of terrestrial connections.

According to the DRC's ministry, the project aims to establish key connectivity corridors that will help strengthen and expand the country's national broadband network.

It added that the Congo River and its tributaries would serve as deployment corridors for the infrastructure.

The ministry said that this approach would allow the project to bypass some of the land-based challenges that often slow infrastructure development in the DRC, including the country's vast size, poor road conditions, logistical constraints and security risks along the route.

Genew Technologies Chief Executive Wu Minhua said the project's total cost is estimated at $1.5 billion and more than $400 million would be required for the first phase.

## African fiber deals

The MoU between Genew Technologies and the DRC comes as many other entities are expanding fiber networks in their territories.

Just last month, Seacom launched a high-capacity terrestrial fiber network route between Kenya's capital, Nairobi, and Uganda's capital, Kampala, reinforcing one of East Africa's most critical digital corridors.

Also in June, Telecom Namibia teamed up with Demshi Investment Holdings to fast-track fiber rollout.

The African Development Bank (AfDB), in April 2026, approved a $200 million loan towards Nigeria's Project Bridge, a fiber-optic program aimed at expanding broadband access in the country.

Vodacom Mozambique and Bayobab Zambia, in February 2026, announced the launch of a fiber interconnection at the Zambia-Mozambique border.

February also saw Ethio Telecom, Djibouti Telecom and the Sudatel Group turn a previous cross-border optical fiber MoU into a binding agreement.

That month also saw the Chadian government urging operators Sotel and Airtel to restart discussions to enable the N'Djamena-Mberé fiber optic route connecting Chad to Cameroon.
