# 2Africa Is Here: How Africa Can Capture the Full Potential of the Subsea Cable

By Akim Benamara — TechAfrica News (#TechTalkThursday), 12 February 2026

In late 2025, the **2Africa** subsea cable system — the largest open-access undersea cable in the world — was declared complete and activated, a pivotal milestone for global and African digital infrastructure. The cable spans approximately 45,000 km, linking over 46 landing points in 33 countries across Africa, Europe, and Asia, with a design capacity of up to **180 terabits per second (Tbps)** — surpassing all existing submarine cables serving Africa combined. It can transform connectivity for more than 3 billion people worldwide, including Africa's 1.4 billion residents.

Developed over six years by a consortium of global and African telecoms companies, 2Africa includes Meta Platforms as lead sponsor, China Mobile International, Orange S.A., Telecom Egypt, STC Group (center3), and WIOCC. African telcos are central: MTN GlobalConnect (Bayobab) led landings in Ghana and Nigeria, while Vodacom oversaw multiple South African landings. The cable was manufactured and laid by Alcatel Submarine Networks (ASN).

Independent research by RTI International estimates 2Africa could add between US$26.2 billion and US$36.9 billion to Africa's GDP within two to three years (~0.42–0.58 percent of continental GDP at PPP). The article stresses that the cable's capacity is only as useful as the networks that deliver it to homes, businesses, and institutions — closing the gap between potential capacity and actual access is essential so urban and rural populations benefit equally.
