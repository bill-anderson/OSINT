# Lesotho, UNICEF partner to develop MOSIP-based integrated national ID

*Biometric Update, 3 March 2026. By Ayang Macdonald.*

UNICEF will support the government of Lesotho in developing an integrated national digital ID ecosystem, as part of the 2026 Action Plan under the Lesotho–UNICEF Programme of Cooperation 2025–2028. The Ministry of Information, Communications, Science, Technology and Innovation (MICSTI) and the UNICEF country office signed the agreement.

The UN agency will assist in putting in place a national ID ecosystem to facilitate renewal of passports and national ID cards, with enhancements in biometric registration, proof-of-life verification and updating of expired identity documents. The move will strengthen identity verification for financial services and contribute to improved monitoring of financial flows and prevention of fraud and money laundering. It aligns with the National Strategic Development Plan II and the National Social Protection Strategy II.

Lesotho is a member of the 50-in-5 campaign (secure, inclusive, interoperable DPI by 2028) and is building its DPI based on MOSIP, the open-source platform for building DPI ecosystems. UNICEF co-chairs the UN Legal Identity Agenda Task Force, supporting SDG 16.9 (legal identity for all, including civil registration, by 2030).
