Electronic payments in Nigeria reached N295.5 trillion ($193 billion) in Q1 2025, a 24.4% jump year-on-year, per NIBSS data. Nigerians carried out 2.2 billion digital transactions in Q1; instant payments totalled N285 trillion ($186 billion) and PoS transactions N10.52 trillion ($6.8 billion). The NIBSS Instant Payment (NIP) platform (launched 2011) remains the backbone. January 2025 saw the highest NIP value ($65 billion), dipping to $57 billion in February then $62 billion in March. For comparison, Kenya ranks highest on the continent for cashless transactions (75.8% of population, per Statista).
