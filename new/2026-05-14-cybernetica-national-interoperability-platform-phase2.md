# Cybernetica Expands Tunisia's National Interoperability Platform to Business Services

May 14, 2026 — Cybernetica (company statement)

Cybernetica and the Tunisian Ministries of Communication Technologies and Economy and Planification have signed a contract to continue advancing digital transformation in the country. Building upon the foundation established during Phase 1, which introduced Cybernetica's Unified eXchange Platform (UXP) to five key ministries and institutions to support public service digitalisation in Tunisia, Phase 2 expands the focus to cover digital services for the business sector.

The second phase targets key public institutions that play a critical role in the business environment, including the National Register of Enterprises (RNE), Tunisia Investment Authority (TIA), Agency for the Promotion of Industry and Innovation (APII), Center for Promotion of Exports (CEPEX), Ministry of Finance, and the Central Bank of Tunisia.

The project is funded and supported by the European Bank for Reconstruction and Development (EBRD) and the Swiss State Secretariat for Economic Affairs (SECO) under their Sector Reform and Capacity Building program. It is scheduled to complete in 2027.

Oliver Väärtnõu, CEO of Cybernetica, said: "Having established a strong foundation during Phase 1, we are now focused on enhancing the resiliency of the platform through deploying high availability modules and extending the platform's reach to the business sector — a step we believe will further strengthen Tunisia's economic competitiveness and investment climate." Moez Kamoun, Partner at PwC (the local implementation partner), noted that Phase 1 had delivered several lessons and that Phase 2 begins "with much more serenity and commitment from all the stakeholders." Key objectives include enhancing e-services for businesses, integrating government databases, improving information-management governance, and improving Tunisia's ranking in the UN's E-Government indices.
