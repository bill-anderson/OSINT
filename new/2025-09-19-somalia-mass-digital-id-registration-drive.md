# Somalia kicks off mass digital ID registration drive

WardheerNews, September 19, 2025 (syndicated; source: Biometric Update)

The Somali government has since the previous month been conducting a mass registration pilot for its national digital ID in the districts of Shangani and Boondheer. NIRA plans to expand the drive to Mogadishu, where it hopes to register all 3.5 million inhabitants, and then to the wider Benadir region, pursuing its objective of registering 15 million citizens for the digital ID by 2029. NIRA used this year's ID Day on September 16 to offer identity services including registration, and the federal government is training staff to deploy to districts.

The program is funded by the World Bank and aims to provide every citizen with a government-issued identity to facilitate access to public services and drive financial inclusion. A World Bank survey found many citizens see the ID as "a tool for empowerment," and one prospective registrant said the ID would let her open her own bank account rather than rely on a family member's. The ID has been made a requirement to access services from opening a bank account to driver's licenses, passport applications and domestic travel.
