# Morocco positions GITEX Africa as gateway for African digital investment

Tuesday 31 March 2026 - 22:50

Morocco is positioning the 2026 edition of GITEX Africa as a platform to strengthen its role as a regional technology and investment hub. The fourth edition takes place in Marrakech from April 7 to 9, bringing together more than 1,450 exhibiting companies and startups alongside participants from over 130 countries. Held under the patronage of King Mohammed VI and overseen by the Ministry of Digital Transition and Administration Reform, the event is hosted by the Digital Development Agency and organized by KAOUN International.

Amine El Mezouaghi, director general of the Digital Development Agency, said the event reflected Morocco's ambition to become "a strategic technology hub serving the African continent." Minister Delegate Amal El Fallah Seghrouchni said AI represented "a structuring opportunity for Africa," with the Kingdom's ambition being "to make AI a pillar of digital sovereignty, economic competitiveness, and territorial inclusion." Organizers framed the event as a platform where "global innovation meets African sovereignty," estimating AI could contribute $1.2 trillion to Africa's economy by 2030.

GITEX Africa will host "North Star Africa," the continent's largest startup showcase, with more than 800 startups and more than 400 investors from over 40 countries managing a combined $350 billion in assets. Morocco is expanding its "Morocco 300" initiative (up 50% from "Morocco 200"). New sector showcases include AI infrastructure and data centers, fintech, future mobility, sports technology and cybersecurity—including a dedicated cybersecurity program organized with Morocco's General Directorate for Information Systems Security (DGSSI) focused on AI-driven threats to critical infrastructure.
