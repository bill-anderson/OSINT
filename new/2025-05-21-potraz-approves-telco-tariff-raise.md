Zimbabwe's telecommunications companies, battling rising operational costs, are set to implement long-anticipated tariff increases for voice, data and SMS bundles after POTRAZ approved them on Monday. The new tariffs affect promotional bundle prices only, with headline tariffs unchanged. Although POTRAZ first approved the increases late last year, operators delayed implementation to shield consumers during the festive season.

With the Zimbabwe Gold (ZiG) currency having shed 43% of its value since its launch in April 2024, and the government recently repealing Statutory Instrument 81A (which enforced the official exchange rate), telecom firms say they had no option but to adjust. Econet, the largest MNO, announced bundle price changes effective 21 May 2025. While US dollar bundle prices moved little, ZiG bundle tariffs were reviewed upward by an average of 35%.

POTRAZ warned in its latest Annual Sector Performance Report that escalating costs were threatening the sector's profitability. MNOs experienced a 42.83% surge in operating expenses in 2024, outstripping 14.45% revenue growth. Total sector revenue grew from US$626.2 million (2023) to US$716.6 million (2024), while operating costs ballooned from US$256 million to US$365.6 million. Despite the pressure, Econet, NetOne and Telecel collectively invested US$83.3 million in infrastructure in 2024, up from US$26.9 million the prior year.
