Tunisia is moving to adopt biometric ID cards and passports in line with International Civil Aviation Organisation (ICAO) recommendations, with the new system to be implemented in the first half of 2025.

A ministerial working session set a target of starting the rollout in the first half of 2025. The decision was made by a steering committee comprising the Ministry of Interior, the Ministry of Communication Technologies, and the Ministry of Economy and Planning.

The move aligns with ICAO recommendations, which encourage all countries to adopt this technology by 2026. The goal is to enhance security and streamline identification for Tunisian citizens both within the country and when travelling internationally.

The adoption of biometric ID cards and passports was made possible through approval of Organic Law No. 22 of 2024 and Organic Law No. 23 of 2024. Key ministers involved include the Minister of Interior, Minister of Finance, Minister of Economy and Planning, and Minister of Communication Technologies. Organic Laws in Tunisia rank higher than regular statutes, typically dealing with fundamental aspects of government structure or basic rights and freedoms of citizens.

Tunisia's decision to implement biometric identification highlights a commitment to modernizing security systems and aligning with international standards, joining a growing list of countries with developing or expanding national ID programs, including Nigeria.
