Zambia's digital payment and service delivery platforms have collected more than K8.8 billion (US$488 million) while expanding access to public services, according to the Office of the Accountant General. Speaking at the 2026 Zambia International Trade Fair on behalf of the accountant general, Daniel Mwewa, principal accountant at the Ministry of Finance and National Planning, said the Government Service Bus, Government Electronic Payment Gateway, ZamPortal and ZamPay have become central to modernising public service delivery.

The platforms enable citizens and businesses to access and pay for government services electronically through multiple channels, including bank transfers, debit and credit cards, mobile money platforms and cash deposits. More than 400 government services have been onboarded onto the digital ecosystem, including passport applications, rent payments and road tax transactions. The government has extended the digital payment ecosystem to local authorities, with eight councils already onboarded and plans to roll out to all 116 local authorities.
