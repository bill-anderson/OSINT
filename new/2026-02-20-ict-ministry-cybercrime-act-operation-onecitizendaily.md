# ICT Ministry vows to ensure effective operation of Cyber crime Act

*One Citizen Daily, 20 February 2026*

The Ministry of Information, Communication Technology and Postal Services has vowed to ensure effective operationalization of the Cybercrime and Computer Misuse Act 2026 in order to safeguard individuals from cyber threats. Addressing the media on Thursday, Ateny Wek Ateny — information minister and government spokesperson — said the historic legislation marks a decisive step in modernizing South Sudan's legal framework for the digital age.

"The ministry will, in collaboration with the National Communication Authority, law enforcement agencies and the Ministry of Justice and Constitutional Affairs, ensure the effective operationalization of this Act," Ateny said. He stressed the Act provides a comprehensive legal basis for the prevention, detection, investigation and prosecution of offences related to computer systems and cyber activities, and facilitates international cooperation in combating cybercrime.

"As the ministry responsible for ICT and postal services, we view this law as a fundamental pillar in building a secure, resilient and trusted digital ecosystem," he said, adding that implementation would include capacity building, institutional coordination and nationwide public awareness initiatives. He emphasized the law was not intended to stifle innovation or restrict legitimate digital expression, but to create a balanced framework that protects freedoms while safeguarding against misuse of technology.
