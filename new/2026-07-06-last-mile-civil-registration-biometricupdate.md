# Madagascar's last-mile civil registration strategy closing digital ID inclusion gap

Madagascar is achieving a foundational inclusion milestone, with **more than 3.25 million people enrolled** for the ID as it advances **PRODIGY** ($143 million World Bank funding). The strategy prioritizes **last-mile civil registration** in all parts of the country.

Upon its launch in April 2026, authorities opted to move from a passive, facility-based registration (which often misses rural and poor populations) to a **proactive, community-driven enrollment approach using mobile technology**. Madagascar's example demonstrates that **offline-first and online-later** identity registration models are critical for reaching the most marginalized.

A World Bank blog post highlights that the project enrolled more than two million people within two months using **Laxton** gear. **With civil registration reforms anchored in a legal framework that went operational in May 2026**, millions of citizens can now obtain an ID card and access services.

Last month, teams from the Malagasy government and the World Bank met to review progress on the **PRODIGY** and **DECIM** (Digital and Energy Connectivity for Inclusion in Madagascar) projects; both projects have **total funding of $543 million**.
