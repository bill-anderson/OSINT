# Zimbabwe's rejected US health-funding package: $350m or $367m?

**Verdict: $367 million is the correct headline figure.** The $350m version was an early-reporting approximation that pre-dates the official US announcement.

**As-of date:** 16 July 2026 (verified via Exa search + fetch). Figures reflect a five-year MoU value.

---

## Answer in one line

The rejected US–Zimbabwe bilateral health MoU was valued by the **US Embassy in Harare at US$367 million over five years**. Of the two sources on file, **Ecofin Agency ($367m) is right**; **Business Insider Africa ($350m) is an early under-count** taken from the leaked Zimbabwean memo before the embassy released its precise figure.

## The primary source

The authoritative number comes from the **official statement of the US Ambassador to Zimbabwe, Pamela Tremont** (US Embassy Harare, issued Tuesday 24 Feb 2026), quoted directly across wire and government-side coverage:

> "We will now turn to the difficult and regrettable task of winding down our health assistance in Zimbabwe. This MoU would have provided **$367 million over five years** to support Zimbabwe's priority health programs, including HIV/AIDS treatment and prevention, tuberculosis, malaria, maternal and child health, and disease outbreak preparedness."

This is the closest thing to a primary US announcement (the funder stating the value of its own offer). It is reproduced verbatim by **AP** (25 Feb), **BBC News** ("$367m (£272m)", 25 Feb), **Africanews** ("$367M", 25 Feb), **Health Policy Watch** ("MOU worth $367-million over five years", 26 Feb), and Zimbabwe's own **HealthTimes** and **iHarare** (25 Feb). The convergence of the US funder plus independent international wires on $367m is decisive.

## Where the three numbers come from

There are actually **three** figures in circulation. Understanding why resolves the $350m/$367m conflict:

- **$367 million** — the **US Embassy's own stated value** of the MoU (Ambassador Tremont). Authoritative; use this. Appears once the embassy issued its statement on 24 Feb and dominates all coverage from 25 Feb onward.

- **$350 million** — an **early approximation** used in the first wave of reporting (ZimLive, 23 Feb; Business Insider Africa, 24 Feb) that broke the story from the **leaked 23 December 2025 memo** by Foreign Affairs Secretary Albert Chimbindi. These pieces ran *before* the US Embassy released the precise figure, so they carried a round-number estimate. Notably, the figure resurfaced later in a different context: the Zimbabwe National Network of People Living with HIV called for a roadmap to fill "the **$350 million gap**" (Health Policy Watch) — i.e. a programme-gap estimate, not the MoU's face value.

- **US$328 million** — Zimbabwe's **own hedged characterisation**, from government spokesman Nick Mangwana's clarification via the state-owned *Herald* (25 Feb): the deal "offered substantial health funding—**reported to be in the region of US$328 million**." The wording ("reported to be in the region of") shows Harare itself was not asserting a precise figure and was not disputing the US number so much as under-emphasising it.

## Why $367m wins over $350m

The $367m figure is stated by the party that made the offer (the US government), is precise rather than rounded, and is corroborated by AP, BBC and Africanews independently. The $350m figure has no primary anchor — it is a pre-announcement estimate from the leaked Zimbabwean memo, later reused loosely to describe a funding "gap." On the specific question "what was the headline value of the deal," **$367 million** is the defensible answer.

## Provenance note / caveats

- The two figures are not describing different deals — they are the **same rejected MoU** under the US "America First Global Health Strategy." The gap is a reporting-timeline artefact (round early estimate vs. official figure), not a factual dispute between parties.
- The full text of the US Embassy statement was accessed here only as quoted within secondary coverage (AP, BBC, HealthTimes, iHarare, Health Policy Watch), not from a State Department / embassy web page directly. The quotation is consistent verbatim across all of them, which is strong corroboration, but the embassy's own posted release was not independently retrieved.
- The **Channel Africa / Reuters (5 Mar)** item flagged in the task remains a provenance gap: only the publisher homepage is on file, no clean article URL, so it was not used here.
- Parallel Zambia deal (rejected same week) is a separate agreement and does not bear on Zimbabwe's figure.
