# Verification: Visa / M-Pesa (Safaricom) / Onafriq "USD-stablecoin pilot" in DR Congo

**As-of date:** 16 July 2026
**Question:** Have Visa, M-Pesa (Safaricom) and Onafriq actually launched a USD-stablecoin pilot in the Democratic Republic of Congo?
**Method:** Exa search + fetch; targeted checks of Visa, Safaricom/M-Pesa and Onafriq primary channels and reputable trade press.

---

## Verdict

**Partly confirmed, but materially overstated as framed — downgrade the "joint launch of a USD-stablecoin pilot" wording.**

There *is* a Visa primary behind the core fact: an on-record senior Visa executive publicly confirms a live DR Congo use case in which mobile-money (M-Pesa) wallet top-ups are settled in stablecoins in the background, via the Visa Pay platform, in partnership with Onafriq. That lifts the claim well above the single weak aggregator (Streamline Feed) it was originally flagged on.

However, the maximalist framing — a formally, *jointly announced* three-party "launch" of a "USD-stablecoin pilot" — is **not** confirmed by any joint issuer primary. There is no Visa, Safaricom/M-Pesa or Onafriq press release announcing such a pilot. One reputable outlet (Ecofin Agency) states plainly that no single jointly announced Visa–M-Pesa–Onafriq stablecoin pilot exists, and that what is really happening is a set of converging initiatives. The specific stablecoin, the blockchain, transaction volumes and customer numbers are **undisclosed by Visa**, the "USD" peg for the DRC leg is inferred (not primary-stated), and one planned Visa partnership was flagged as still awaiting regulatory approval.

**Net:** treat "Visa, M-Pesa and Onafriq launched a USD-stablecoin pilot in DRC" as **substantially true in substance but loosely reported in form.** The reliable, primary-anchored statement is narrower — see below.

---

## What a primary source actually supports

The origin of the entire news wave is remarks by **Godfrey Sullivan, Visa's Senior Vice-President and Head of Products & Solutions, CEMEA**, first reported by *The EastAfrican* (and syndicated via Zawya) on **2 July 2026**, and repeated verbatim across dozens of trade outlets. On record, Sullivan said:

> "There is a use case that we have in DR Congo where we are settling M-Pesa mobile money top-ups with stablecoins through a partnership with Onafriq. We have launched a proposition called VisaPay and, as you top up your M-Pesa wallet, the transaction is settled in stablecoins in the background."

This on-record attribution to a named senior Visa executive, carried consistently by multiple reputable outlets (The EastAfrican, Zawya, FintechFutures/Informa, International Finance), is the closest thing to a primary and it **corroborates the core mechanism**: a live DRC settlement use case, M-Pesa top-ups, stablecoin settlement in the background, Visa Pay, Onafriq as the connecting infrastructure.

Confirmed context that is on solid primary/near-primary footing:
- **Visa Pay launched in the DRC in September 2025** via an API integration with Onafriq (Onafriq's own comments to Connecting Africa, Jan 2026, confirm it "supported the launch of Visa Pay in the DRC in Q4" 2025).
- **Onafriq's stablecoin treasury move is real but separate** — a February 2026 partnership with **Conduit** to on-ramp payments in **USDC** and manage treasury in stablecoins (BusinessWire primary, 10 Feb 2026).
- **Visa's global stablecoin settlement pilot is real** — Visa newsroom releases confirm a multi-blockchain USDC settlement programme (~$7bn annualised run rate as of Apr 2026). This is Visa's *global* figure, **not** DRC volume.

---

## What is NOT confirmed by any primary

1. **A jointly announced three-party pilot.** No Visa, Safaricom/M-Pesa Africa or Onafriq press release announces a "Visa + M-Pesa + Onafriq USD-stablecoin pilot" in the DRC. Ecofin Agency (2 Jul 2026) states directly: *"there is no single, jointly announced stablecoin pilot uniting Visa, M-Pesa and Onafriq… what exists is a set of converging initiatives."* Headlines like "Visa, M-PESA Africa and Onafriq Launch Stablecoin Pilot" are trade-press glosses on Sullivan's remarks, not a joint announcement.
2. **Any Safaricom / M-Pesa primary** on the stablecoin-settlement leg. M-Pesa's own publicly announced stablecoin move is a *separate* January 2026 alliance with the Abu Dhabi–based **ADI Foundation** built around a **UAE dirham-backed** stablecoin — not a USD stablecoin, and not this DRC settlement pilot.
3. **The "USD" specificity for the DRC leg.** Many outlets assert "U.S. dollar-pegged," but that is inference from Visa's broader USDC work; Visa has not disclosed which stablecoin, or which blockchain, is used in the DRC use case.
4. **Scale and status.** Volume, customer count, and whether this is a contained technical test vs. a live commercial rollout are undisclosed. *The EastAfrican* appended an editor's note that **"one of the planned partnerships with Visa is still awaiting regulatory approval."** The DRC central bank is separately pursuing de-dollarisation, a live regulatory tension over any USD-stablecoin rail.

---

## Provenance assessment (revised)

The original flag — *"single low-authority source (Streamline Feed); no Visa/Safaricom/Onafriq primary corroborates it"* — is **out of date**. Streamline Feed (10 Jul 2026) is a late, low-authority rewrite of a story that had already circulated for a week and traces to an on-record Visa executive quote via The EastAfrican/Zawya (2 Jul 2026). The provenance gap on the *core mechanism* is largely closed by that Visa executive attribution.

The residual provenance gap is on the **framing and the specifics**: there is still **no issuer press release**, **no Safaricom/M-Pesa or Onafriq primary** on the stablecoin-settlement leg, and **no disclosure** of the stablecoin, chain, USD-peg (for DRC), volumes or regulatory clearance. Reporting has amplified a single executive soundbite into a "joint launch," which the record does not support.

**Recommended handling:** cite the narrow, Visa-attributed claim ("Visa says it is settling M-Pesa top-ups in stablecoins in the DRC in the background, with Onafriq, via Visa Pay"), and explicitly downgrade / caveat the broader "Visa + M-Pesa + Onafriq jointly launched a USD-stablecoin pilot" wording as trade-press framing not backed by a joint issuer primary. Flag USD-peg and regulatory clearance as unconfirmed.
