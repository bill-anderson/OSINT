# DR Congo National Digital Plan 2026–2030 (PNN2): what does it actually cost?

**Reconciliation note — COD batch**
**As of:** 16 July 2026 (Europe/London). Sources retrieved via Exa on this date.
**Prepared for:** Bill Anderson

---

## Bottom line

There is one **officially stated** figure and one **secondary-media** figure, and they are not the same kind of number.

- **US$1.5 billion is the government's stated financing envelope** for PNN2 (plus the parallel National AI Strategy). It breaks down as **US$1 billion of public treasury money over 2026–2030** plus **US$500 million of external partner support already secured** (World Bank, AFD and others, framed as 2025–2029). This figure is attributed directly to the Ministry of the Digital Economy and to President Tshisekedi at the 8 October 2025 launch, and it has been restated unchanged through at least July 2026. Treat this as the **confirmed committed cost/budget**.

- **US$8.7 billion is a total-programme figure that does not trace to any primary/official source.** It appears only in trade-tech aggregator outlets (Extensia, iAfrica, Tech In Africa, The African Mirror, africaainews) from January–March 2026. It is internally inconsistent on currency — the same publisher (Tech In Africa) headlines both "$8.7 billion" and "€8 billion ($8.8 billion)" — and every one of those articles, in its own body text, still cites only the government's $1bn + $500m commitment. An independent commentator (africaainews, 30 Jan 2026) explicitly flags "some creative accounting" and says it is "not clear whether this $8.7-B includes the $1.5-B."

**So:** the $1.5bn is real and official; the $8.7bn is an unverified, currency-ambiguous "total ambition" number that likely folds in private-sector and not-yet-mobilised capital (e.g. the $1bn General Technologies MOU, the $150m UIL data-centre/fibre deal, the Vodacom/Orange solar-tower rollout) and may double-count or sit alongside — not inside — the $1.5bn.

## Why the split, not the total, is the defensible number

The **$1.5bn = $1bn public + $500m partners** split is the one claim that is:

1. **Attributed to the source of record** — the Ministry of the Digital Economy's launch communiqué (8 Oct 2025), echoed in the President's announcement, and
2. **Stable over time** — repeated verbatim by Ecofin, Bankable, Digital Business Africa, CIO Mag, Biometric Update, numerico.cd and finance-cd from October 2025, and again by We Are Tech / Ecofin in February and by ACP in July 2026.

Notably, at the 1 July 2026 launch of the inter-donor group (Groupe inter-bailleurs digital), the minister presented digital-**infrastructure** financing needs of **"at least US$1 billion"** — i.e. still in the $1bn register, with no $8.7bn headline surfacing from government. That is a further signal that the multi-billion "total" figures are media constructs rather than the plan's official budget.

## Important caveat on the primary document

As of this date I could not locate a **published, validated PNN2 document** carrying an official headline total. The plan's *drafting* was launched on 8 October 2025 (an inter-institutional commission was still writing it), so the "cost of PNN2" in circulation is the **announced financing envelope**, not a line-item total extracted from a finalised, costed plan. If you need the definitive total (currency + amount) and pillar-by-pillar costing, that has to come from the finalised PNN2 / Stratégie nationale de l'IA document from the Ministère de l'Économie numérique — which does not appear to be publicly posted yet. Worth a direct request to the ministry or ADN (Agence pour le Développement du Numérique).

## Recommended wording for the record

> **PNN2 (2026–2030) — cost.** Officially, the DRC government has committed a **US$1.5 billion financing envelope**: **US$1bn** from the public treasury (2026–2030) plus **US$500m** in external partner support already secured (World Bank, AFD; framed 2025–2029). Source of record: Ministry of the Digital Economy, launch of 8 Oct 2025; restated through July 2026. A widely-circulated **"$8.7 billion" total is unverified** — it appears only in secondary tech-trade media, is currency-ambiguous ($8.7bn vs €8bn/$8.8bn in the same outlet), and is not traceable to any primary source; it most plausibly represents a fuller multi-source investment ambition (incl. private capital) rather than committed budget. A **€8.7bn / $10.4bn total could not be corroborated** from any public source. *As-of 16 Jul 2026.*
