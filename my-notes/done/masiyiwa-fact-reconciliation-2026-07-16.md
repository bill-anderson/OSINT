# Two Masiyiwa fact reconciliations — AMSP facility figure & Econet founding dates

**Prepared:** 16 July 2026 · **Method:** Exa search + fetch of primary and authoritative sources · **Verification as-of date:** 16 July 2026

---

## (a) AMSP / PATIMFA facility figure — $3bn vs $3.8bn

**Verdict: $3 billion is correct. Keep the wiki at ~$3bn with $200m reserved. The $3.8bn is an unpinned verbal slip and should not be used.**

### The primary Afreximbank record
The facility backing the Africa Medical Supplies Platform (AMSP) is the **Pandemic Trade Impact Mitigation Facility (PATIMFA)**, and Afreximbank's own constitutive press release fixes its size:

> "The African Export-Import Bank (Afreximbank) has announced a **$3-billion facility, named Pandemic Trade Impact Mitigation Facility (PATIMFA)** … approved by the Bank's Board of Directors during its sitting on 20 March."
> — Afreximbank press release, *Afreximbank Announces $3-Billion Facility to Cushion Impact of COVID-19*, **24 March 2020** (facility as-of date: Board approval 20 March 2020).

The AMSP-specific carve-out (the $200m the wiki already carries) is confirmed by two independent records, both **as-of 18 June–1 July 2020**:

- South African Presidency, AMSP launch release (18 June 2020): "Afreximbank brought to bear its **$3-billion Pandemic Trade Impact Mitigation Facility (PATIMFA), of which $200 million has been reserved** to support food production as well as the manufacture of, and trade in, medical equipment and supplies."
- Strive Masiyiwa (written), *Best practices from Africa*, CWSRG, **1 July 2020**: "Afreximbank is doing this by bringing to bear its **$3-billion Pandemic Trade Impact Mitigation Facility (PATIMFA), of which $200 million has been reserved** …"

Note these are distinct from the later **US$2 billion** vaccine advance-purchase guarantee (Jan 2021) and the **$100 million** procurement overdraft (Sept 2020) — neither of which produces a $3.8bn figure.

### Where the $3.8bn actually comes from
Traced across the three on-file Masiyiwa sources, the $3.8bn resolves to a **single spoken remark** in one interview:

- **Milken Institute interview, 18 June 2020 (source of the $3.8bn):** "… they open a credit line with the Afrexim Bank, and there's a **facility of 3.8 billion**, which is supporting this program." — an off-the-cuff figure in conversation.
- **CWSRG, 1 July 2020 (same author, in writing, two weeks later):** "$3-billion PATIMFA, of which $200 million has been reserved" — i.e. Masiyiwa's own considered, written number is **$3bn**.
- **Tools and Weapons with Brad Smith, 2023 (transcript):** does **not** carry any AMSP facility figure — it is a general interview on Econet's telecoms history and connecting Africa's youth, not COVID procurement. It neither supports nor mentions $3.8bn.

**Conclusion.** The $3.8bn has no primary Afreximbank basis. It rests entirely on one verbal figure Masiyiwa gave in the Milken interview, contradicted by his own written account of the same period and by the Afreximbank record. Most likely a spoken rounding/slip (possibly loosely aggregating other tranches). The wiki's ~$3bn / $200m-reserved is the defensible figure; the "one source" giving $3.8bn is now pinned — it is the Milken interview — and can be footnoted as an uncorroborated misstatement rather than a rival estimate.

## (b) Econet founding — licensed 1997 vs incorporated 1998

**Verdict: the wiki's "licence 1997 vs incorporation 1998" framing is slightly off. Incorporation was 1993, not 1998; the "1998" everywhere is the operational launch. There are three distinct dates, not two.**

### The three dates, pinned
1. **Incorporated: 1993.** Wikipedia states plainly: "Econet was **incorporated in 1993**, and only started to operate in 1998." Masiyiwa's own account and Stanford GSB corroborate the business beginning in 1993 ("When he started Econet in 1993"). **No primary source on file supports "incorporated 1998."**
2. **Licence secured: late 1997 / turn of 1998** — commonly rounded to **"1997."** Biographical and secondary sources give 1997: NYU School of Law (4 Apr 2017) — "It wasn't until 1997 that the Supreme Court granted Masiyiwa's company, Econet Wireless, a license"; CSMonitor (2000) — "finally awarded a license to operate in 1997"; African Leadership Magazine (20 Jan 2014) — "By 1997, the court ruled in his favour." Masiyiwa's own narration places the decisive Supreme Court ruling (Justice Sandura declaring Econet "duly licensed") right at the turn of 1997–1998, with the network launching the following July.
3. **Network launched: 10 July 1998** (→ ZSE listing three months later, September 1998). Econet's own site is the primary/company record: "we **launched Zimbabwe's first private network on 10 July 1998** following a five-year protracted legal battle fighting for a license." Wikipedia agrees ("began operation in July 1998"). The SSRN case study frames the licence as effectively secured into the launch ("given the license in July 1998"; five-year process 1993–1998), which is why some sources attach 1998 to the licence too.

### Reconciliation
There is **no true conflict**, but the wiki mislabels the axis. The split is not "licence (1997) vs incorporation (1998)." It is:

- **1993** — incorporation / start of the venture and the legal battle
- **~1997 (late 1997/early 1998)** — the court secures the licence; "1997" is the common shorthand
- **10 July 1998** — commercial launch; September 1998 — ZSE listing

The stray "1998 = incorporation/established" reading (which the wiki attributes to "The Guardian, 2014," with no matching Guardian source on file) is best read as a **launch date mislabelled as a founding/incorporation date**. Recommended wiki wording: *incorporated 1993; licence secured through the courts around 1997; commercial launch 10 July 1998.* Drop any implication that 1998 is the incorporation year.
