# Mozambique Cyber Security Law (2026): what does implementation actually cost?

**Reconciliation note — MOZ batch**
**As of:** 16 July 2026 (Europe/London). Sources retrieved via Exa on this date.
**Prepared for:** Bill Anderson

---

## Bottom line

The two figures are **not two different costs**. They are the **same number**, one of them printed with the wrong unit word. Corrected, the stated implementation cost is:

> **≈ 167.26 million meticais (≈ US$2.5–2.6 million)**, financed from the State Budget and donations.

The "**167,257.94 million meticais**" reading is a **units error**: the base figure **167,257.94** is expressed in **thousands of meticais** (the standard unit in Mozambican budget/impact tables, *10³ MT*), which the newspaper mislabelled as "millions." Read correctly, 167,257.94 × 1,000 MT = **167,257,940 MT ≈ 167.26 million MT** — i.e. the "just over 160 million meticais" of the same article, loosely rounded down. There is no ~1,000× real-world discrepancy; there is one figure and a typo in the multiplier.

---

## Where the two figures come from

Both figures originate in **a single primary report** — O País (Paulo Viriato), 16 April 2026 — and Club of Mozambique's 17 April English version is a faithful translation of it. The contradiction is therefore **internal to the one source**, then propagated verbatim to the aggregators (Club of Mozambique, Revista Tempo, mznews, jornalmz).

| As printed | Where in the article | In plain terms |
|---|---|---|
| "impacto financeiro de **pouco mais de 160 milhões de meticais**" | Lede / headline bullet | ~160m MZN ≈ US$2.5m — a rounded restatement |
| "impacto financeiro de **167.257,94 milhões de meticais**" (PT format) → "**167,257.94 million meticais**" (EN) | Body paragraph | The precise figure, but with "milhões/million" written where the table unit is thousands |

Note the Portuguese decimal formatting: **167.257,94** = 167,257.94 in English (dot = thousands separator, comma = decimal). O País itself wrote "**milhões**," so the mislabel is in the original Portuguese, not introduced by translation.

---

## Why the corrected reading is the right one

Three independent checks all point the same way:

1. **Internal arithmetic.** The paper's own conversion — "pouco mais de 160 milhões de meticais … around US$2.5 million" — implies **~64 MZN/USD**, which matches the actual metical rate in April 2026 (~63–64/USD). So "just over 160 million MZN ≈ US$2.5m" is internally coherent, and 167.26m MZN lands at ~US$2.6m on the same rate.

2. **The two figures reconcile to one number.** 167,257.94 read as **thousands of MT** = 167.26 million MT = "pouco mais de 160 milhões." They are the same amount to the metical.

3. **Plausibility of the literal reading.** Taken literally, "167,257.94 million MZN" = **~167.26 billion MZN ≈ US$2.6 billion**. Mozambique's entire annual State Budget is on the order of ~500–700 billion MZN, so a single law's implementation costing US$2.6bn — a double-digit share of all national spending — is not credible on its face. The billion-scale reading fails the smell test; the million-scale reading passes.

**Verdict:** the correct figure is **~167.26 million MZN (≈ US$2.5m)**. The "160 million" and the "167,257.94 million" are the same cost; only the second's unit word is wrong.

## Provenance gap (what is NOT yet confirmed against a primary budget line)

This reconciliation is **arithmetic + convention**, not a direct read of the government's own costing table. Specifically:

- **The gazetted law does not carry the figure.** The enacted texts — **Lei n.º 13/2026 (Segurança Cibernética)** and **Lei n.º 14/2026 (Crimes Cibernéticos)** — were published in the *Boletim da República* of **1 July 2026**, promulgated by President Daniel Chapo (12 June 2026), and enter into force **90 days later, on 29 September 2026** (implementing regulation due within 180 days). Enacted laws state no implementation cost, so the *Boletim* cannot confirm the number.
- **The figure lives in the government's financial-impact statement** attached to the *proposta de lei* presented to parliament on 16 April 2026 — i.e. the *nota justificativa / mapa de impacto orçamental*. **That document is not in the held sources and does not appear to be publicly posted.** It is the one artefact that would show the raw figure with its unit label (expected: "167.257,94 (10³ MT)" or "milhares de MT"), converting this from a high-confidence inference to a confirmed fact.

**To close the gap:** obtain the *proposta de lei* impact annex (or the Comissão do Plano e Orçamento *parecer*) from the Assembleia da República / INTIC / Ministério das Comunicações e Transformação Digital, and read the unit heading on the 167,257.94 line.

> **Mozambique Cyber Security Law — implementation cost.** The stated cost is **≈ 167.26 million meticais (≈ US$2.5m)**, from the State Budget plus donations, per the government's proposta de lei presented to parliament on 16 April 2026 (reported by O País, 16 Apr 2026; Club of Mozambique, 17 Apr 2026). The single held source prints two figures — "just over 160 million MZN" and "167,257.94 million MZN" — but these are **one number, not two**: 167,257.94 is a thousands-of-meticais figure (≈167.26m MZN) that the reporting mislabelled as "millions." The literal "167,257.94 million" (≈167bn MZN / ~US$2.6bn) is a **units error**, implausible against Mozambique's national budget. The enacted Lei n.º 13/2026 (*Boletim da República*, 1 Jul 2026) does not state the cost; the figure originates in the bill's financial-impact annex, which has not been obtained. *Confidence: high on the reconciliation; unit not yet confirmed against the primary budget line.
