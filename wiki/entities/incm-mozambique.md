# National Communications Institute (INCM)

Mozambique's **telecommunications/ICT regulator** (Instituto Nacional das Comunicações de Moçambique). Under
**decree 12/2023**, the INCM mandated **biometric SIM registration** (fingerprints + facial recognition):
a pilot began 15 January 2024, became compulsory in the second half of 2024, with unregistered cards facing
blocking from 2025. Each subscriber is assigned a **Unique Telecommunications Number (NUTEL)** to identify
them across operators — framed as easing detection of the ~5,000 monthly scams/frauds.

The INCM also drives connectivity programmes flagged in Mozambique's draft Digital Transformation Strategy —
**"Internet for All"** (launched December 2024, targeting 100% coverage by 2030) and **"Internet nas
Escolas"** (13,000 schools/institutions by 2030) — and plans tenders for rural connectivity. It is distinct
from **[[intic-mozambique|INTIC]]** (ICT institute), INAGE (e-government) and **[[atdi-mozambique|ATDI]]**
(the digital-transformation agency).
