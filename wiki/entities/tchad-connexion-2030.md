# Tchad Connexion 2030

**Chad's US$1.5 billion national digital umbrella strategy**, also rendered in English as *"Chad
Connection 2030"* and referred to by Russell Southwood simply as *"the Tchad Connexion
initiative."* It puts digital technology at the core of [[TCD]]'s national development plan and
carries the government's headline connectivity, e-government and poverty-reduction targets.

**It is not the same thing as [[pmice]]** (the €175.1m State Connectivity Infrastructure
Modernization Project) or the [[chad-digital-transformation-project]] (the World Bank's US$120m
operation). No source held states how PMICE relates to this strategy.

## Targets

The fullest statement (Biometric Update, 2026-03-16): around **$1.5 billion by 2030** on ICT
infrastructure and digital services; **at least 80% national internet coverage**; about **80%
mobile penetration**; and lifting about **2.5 million Chadians out of poverty** through
digital-enabled services **within five years**. Source:
[[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].

Ecofin (2025-12-02, 2025-12-10) states the same $1.5bn as a plan to raise Chad into Africa's
leading economies **within six years** — a differently-phrased horizon for the same headline sum.
Both accounts also call for expanded network coverage and the **digitisation and interconnection
of all public and para-public services** to deliver full e-government access. Southwood
(2026-02-16) summarises the strategy as **telecoms sector reform, infrastructure upgrades and
e-government**, with US$1.5bn to be spent **by 2030**. Sources:
[[2025-12-02 Chad Accelerates Digital Cooperation to Support Its Transformation Agenda]],
[[2025-12-10 Chad Finalizes Digital Law to Align National Framework With Global Standards]],
[[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]].

## The baseline it starts from

- **E-government.** Chad ranked **189th of 193** on the UN E-Government Development Index, score
  **0.1785** of 1 — below the African average of **0.4247** and the global average of **0.6382**
  (**2024** EGDI). Sources:
  [[2025-12-10 Chad Finalizes Digital Law to Align National Framework With Global Standards]],
  [[2026-02-06 Chad Moves to Secure National Data Center Ahead of Launch]].
- **Cybersecurity.** Chad sat in **Tier 4**, the second-lowest category, of the [[itu]]'s **2024**
  Global Cybersecurity Index, scoring **48.67/100** — performing relatively well on legislation
  and cooperation, with gaps in technical capability, organisational preparedness and national
  capacity-building. Source:
  [[2025-12-10 Chad Finalizes Digital Law to Align National Framework With Global Standards]].
- **Internet penetration — contradicted across sources.** [[datareportal]]'s *Digital 2024: Chad*
  reports **4.18 million internet users and 22.5% penetration at the start of 2024**; We Are Tech
  Africa, relaying DataReportal, reports **only 13.2% online at the start of 2025**. Both trace to
  the same publisher. **Resolved (2026-07-20): a publisher rebase, not a fall in access.** DataReportal's *Digital 2025:
Chad* reports 2.74m users / **13.2% at January 2025** with +4.3% growth and the adoption rate "unchanged" -
only self-consistent against a **restated January-2024 base of ~13.2%**, not the 22.5% its own 2024 edition
published. The 13.2% holds at October 2025 (2.79m users) and matches the ITU/World Bank series
(**12.63%, 2024**). The **22.5% is superseded**, not evidence of a collapse. Separately, the government's
  own roadmap is framed as raising penetration **30% → 80%** (as stated 2026-07-01). See
  [[include.divides]]. Sources: [[2024-02-23 Digital 2024 - Chad]],
  [[2025-12-29 Chad Steps Up Rollout of Tchad Connexion 2030 in Talks With Huawei]],
  [[2026-07-01 Chad and US Explore Partnerships on Satellite Connectivity and Cybersecurity]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Huawei talks (2025-12-24).** Minister [[boukar-michel]] met a [[huawei]] North Africa
  delegation led by Vice President for North Africa **Jason Ye** to discuss expanding telecom
  infrastructure, widening access to connected services, and accelerating the digitalisation of
  public administration. Both sides explored turning the talks into concrete projects aligned with
  Tchad Connexion 2030; narrowing the **rural** digital divide was named as central. See
  [[geopol.china]], [[digital.rural]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-29 Chad Steps Up Rollout of Tchad Connexion 2030 in Talks With Huawei]].
- **Legal alignment (2025-12-09).** Michel announced at the third Chadian School of Internet
  Governance (TCSIG) that Chad is finalising a **new digital law** incorporating international
  standards on cybersecurity, data protection and internet governance: *"In the digital age, a
  country that does not control its data does not control its destiny."* On **22 October 2025**
  the National Assembly approved an ordinance amending the **2014** electronic-communications and
  postal-services law, aimed at modernising the sector, promoting competition, improving coverage
  and reinforcing digital sovereignty. See [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]].
  Source: [[2025-12-10 Chad Finalizes Digital Law to Align National Framework With Global Standards]].
- **Identity layer.** The [[chad-biometric-registry]] interoperability push is explicitly framed
  as strengthening digital trust *"in line with Chad's 2030 digital transformation strategy."* See
  [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].
- **Data centre.** We Are Tech Africa links the [[chad-national-data-centre]] audit to the
  strategy. Source: [[2026-02-06 Chad Moves to Secure National Data Center Ahead of Launch]].

## Reading it

Southwood's verdict is worth recording as a named analytical view rather than fact: he argues the
Tchad Connexion funding *"clearly illustrates that [the government] has the kind of funding that
could solve"* Chad's unresolved international fibre problem, and concludes *"maybe the Government
finds performative rhetoric easier than actually getting stuff done"* (2026-02-16). See
[[sotel-tchad]]. Source:
[[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]].
