# ARCEP Burkina Faso

Burkina Faso's **electronic-communications and postal regulator**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **DNSSEC for ".bf" (Dec 2025).** ARCEP launched DNSSEC for the national ".bf" domain (5 Dec 2025), with ICANN support — a DNS-integrity/trust measure. Source: [[2025-12-09 Burkina Faso Targets Online Trust with New Internet Security Measures (DNSSEC)]].
- **Operator revenue data / local-HQ decree (Feb 2026).** ARCEP's 2024 operator-revenue figures (Onatel CFA117.19bn, Orange CFA332.91bn, Telecel CFA40.44bn) underpin a decree requiring large firms — the three mobile operators — to build local headquarters. Source: [[2026-02-13 Burkina Faso Orders Large Firms to Build Local Headquarters]].
- **The "zéro zone blanche" white-zone drive (2025–26).** [[digital.rural]] The flagship coverage
  programme (FASU + [[pactdigital|PACTDIGITAL]] financed) aims to eliminate uncovered "white zones": **138
  covered in 2024**, a **750-locality** tranche launched (Nando 104, Nakambe 61 towns; 37.5bn FCFA), then a
  **1,000-white-zone 2026** push — against **~2,000 localities (10–12% of territory) still with no coverage
  at all**, targeting 1.5–2m beneficiaries. **The completion horizon is contradicted** — **end-2027**
  (ministry) vs **2030** (a DG) — see `reviews/contradictions/open/`. Sources:
  [[2025-09-30-services-communications-electroniques-750-zones-bientot-couvertes]],
  [[2026-07-13-pactdigital-1000-white-zones-2026]], [[2025-12-18-zero-zones-blanches-2027-61-localites-nakambe]].
- **Operators met 2025 site commitments (2026-06).** [[infra.connect]] Orange 200, Moov 200, Telecel 150
  sites built (**80–90%** of engagements); **2,600 MHz** freed; **Telecel** doubled 150→300 sites (Omdia
  Q3-2025: Orange 14.2m, Moov 12.7m, Telecel 2.8m); **Moov** built a 9bn-CFA ZACA HQ and fibre Wi-Fi (Tougan).
  But tower rollout is **blocked by local opposition/misinformation** in places, prompting "digital
  ambassadors." Sources: [[2026-06-21-telecommunications-taux-execution-engagements-80-90]],
  [[2025-12-11-telecel-faso-deploie-300-sites-telecom]], [[2026-07-01-arcep-national-drive-expand-digital-infrastructure]].
- **Affordability, IPv6, fibre oversight (2025–26).** [[gov.standards]] ARCEP's 2023 consumer rules became
  enforceable after a court ruling — though a basic plan still costs **~8.8% of GNI** (vs the 2% ITU
  benchmark); a **Fibre Monitoring Committee** and a **WebSIG "Observatoire de l'aménagement numérique"**
  were stood up; and ARCEP is driving **IPv6** migration (3rd Forum, sovereignty-framed). Sources:
  [[2025-12-06-burkina-faso-tightens-telecom-regulations-affordability]],
  [[2026-06-19-oversight-national-fibre-optic-infrastructure]], [[2025-10-26-observatoire-amenagement-numerique-lance]],
  [[2026-07-03-abdi-adoption-ipv6-internet-nouvelle-generation]].
- **Cross-border frequency coordination + AES roaming (2025).** [[gov.regional]] ARCEP finalised **signal/
  frequency coordination with Ghana's NCA**, and Burkina/Ghana/Mali/Niger **harmonised frequencies and
  free roaming** — regional integration cutting across the ECOWAS split. Sources:
  [[2025-08-21-ghana-burkina-interference-frequences-transfrontalieres]],
  [[2025-11-23-burkina-ghana-mali-niger-harmonisent-frequences]].

- **Connectivity in a security crisis.** [[digital.rural]] Insecurity both cuts and is targeted by
  connectivity: **Gassan** was reconnected after a **4-year** insurgency-driven outage (2026-03), while
  the **Emergency Telecommunications Cluster** (Luxembourg/Ericsson/Sweden, 2021–2026) funds humanitarian
  connectivity in Burkina and Niger. Sources: [[2026-03-24-gassan-reconnecte-reseau-apres-4-ans]],
  [[2026-06-15-ericsson-luxembourg-sweden-sahel-digital-connectivity]],
  [[2025-01-10-luxembourg-pledges-reduce-sahel-digital-gap]].
