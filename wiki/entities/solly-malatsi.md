# Solly Malatsi

South Africa's **Minister of Communications and Digital Technologies** (head of the
**[[dcdt-south-africa]]**), the political principal for the country's digital-government
agenda — including **[[mymzansi]]**, DPI, spectrum/connectivity policy and social-media
regulation. A recurring figure across the ZAF digital-transformation record (showcased the
MyMzansi prototype and the digital driving licence).

## Activity in this wiki

- **"Digital-first must not become digital-only" (2026-07, Social Media Summit).** Malatsi
  warned that digitising government before fixing **affordability, connectivity and digital
  literacy** risks building "a premium service layer for the already included," not a digital
  state — "a promise made only to the connected is not a promise to the nation." He tied it to
  democratic risk ("the digital divide becomes a democratic divide"; mistaking online
  visibility for representativity), positioned **[[mymzansi]]** as the "one trusted point of
  entry" that must reach citizens regardless of location or income, and pressed for low-data,
  multilingual and **offline** channels. On platforms, he signalled a preference for
  responsible **self-regulation** over legislation, but reserved the right to "regulate where
  necessary." See [[include.divides]], [[include.access]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]],
  [[ZAF]]. Source: [[2026-07-13 Digital-first must not become digital-only, says Malatsi]].

- **Smartphone luxury-tax removed → device affordability (2026-07-10, Sowetan op-ed).** Malatsi
  framed a smartphone as "access to opportunity, not a luxury" and detailed the DCDT/National
  Treasury removal of the **9% ad valorem luxury excise** on **sub-R2,500 entry-level smartphones**.
  Cites the **[[gsma-sa-smartphone-tax-reform-2026]]** report: **+1.1m** entry-level units in 11
  months, monthly sales **250k→460k**, feature-phone sales falling, sales value **R2.7bn→R3.1bn/mo**
  — "good public policy does not have to choose between economic growth and social progress." The
  supply-side/affordability complement to his "digital-first ≠ digital-only" inclusion argument.
  See [[include.access]], [[include.divides]], [[tech.industry]], [[gov.policy]], [[ZAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 SOLLY MALATSI  Removing smartphone tax means access to opportunity]].
