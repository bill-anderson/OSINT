# Wingu Africa

East-African data-centre and cloud operator building carrier-neutral infrastructure across
its regional markets. Deputy CEO, COO and co-founder: **Demos Kyriacou**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Wingu Cloud Exchange (WCX) launched in Ethiopia (April 2026).** A private-cloud
  platform explicitly designed for **in-country data residency** and compliance with local
  regulations — keeping Ethiopian organisations' data securely onshore. Positioned as setting
  "a new standard for digital infrastructure in the region." See [[ETH]], [[infra.store]],
  [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-01 Wingu Africa debuts cloud exchange platform in Ethiopia]].
- **Services and pricing.** WCX bundles **Wingu Compute, Wingu Kubernetes, Wingu Drive and
  Wingu Security**, priced in **local currency** (avoiding forex uncertainty and hidden fees),
  and integrates with on-premises systems and global providers **Azure** ([[microsoft]]) and
  **AWS** ([[amazon-web-services]]) for hybrid deployments. Source:
  [[2026-04-01 Wingu Africa debuts cloud exchange platform in Ethiopia]].
- **Regional rollout.** WCX **first launched in Tanzania in November 2025**; the Ethiopia
  debut follows a **$60m raise** to fast-track expansion across its East African markets —
  **Djibouti, Ethiopia and Tanzania**. See [[TZA]], [[DJI]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-01 Wingu Africa debuts cloud exchange platform in Ethiopia]].
- **$60m raise — the primary (2025-06).** The **$60m** came from **Rand Merchant Bank (RMB)** to fund
  hyperscale/cloud/enterprise expansion; in Ethiopia Wingu's **Tier III campus hosts the ADDIX exchange** (a
  cloud/content-acceleration foundation), and it opened a **second Djibouti site** (integrated cable landing +
  data centre; 12 operational subsea cables; AMS-IX Djibouti IX). CEO Anthony Voscarides framed it as "an
  investment in Africa's digital independence." Source:
  [[2025-06-06-wingu-africa-secures-60m-east-africa-connectingafrica]].
- **Horn-of-Africa hub footprint (2026-01).** Wingu's Djibouti technology park is cited among
  the infrastructure anchoring the Horn's rise as a set of "digital powerhouses" — the
  city-state's carrier-neutral Tier-3 ecosystem (12 subsea landings, 65% penetration, the
  region's highest) positions it as East Africa's "digital switchboard." See [[DJI]],
  [[infra.store]], [[XEA]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-07 Why Ethiopia, Somalia and Djibouti are starting to matter in digital Africa]].

- **TO7 Technology Park inaugurated (2024-11-25).** President Ismail Omar Guelleh inaugurated
  Wingu's **TO7 Technology Park** in Djibouti — a **Tier-3 carrier-neutral data centre**
  (99.982% uptime), a **cable landing station**, and an innovation centre; the first integrated
  facility there combining a data centre and landing station, complementing Wingu's decade-old
  existing Djibouti data centre. See [[DJI]], [[infra.store]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-25 Djibouti inaugurates Wingu TO7 Tier-3 data centre and cable landing station]].
