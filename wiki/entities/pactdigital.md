# PACTDIGITAL (Burkina Faso Digital Acceleration Project)

Burkina Faso's flagship **World Bank-financed digital-transformation programme** — the **Digital Acceleration Project** (P177022), development objective approved **23 January 2024**. Reported at **$150 million** at approval (2024); the ministry later cited **~$170 million / 96.7 billion CFA** (World Bank IDA loan plus national counterpart) in its March 2026 PACTDIGITAL-BF framing — the fuller figure incl. counterpart funding.

## Profile

- **Aims:** expand broadband to underserved and displacement-affected/climate-vulnerable areas; develop digital public services; train public-administration staff and youth in digital skills; and support large-scale **WURI** unique-ID enrolment. Country director Clara Ana De Sousa; TTL Tounwendé Alain Sawadogo. Sources: [[2024-03-11 Burkina Faso's digital transformation gets 150M World Bank support]], [[2025-12-28 World Bank - Burkina Faso Digital Acceleration Project (PACTDIGITAL) Implementation Status Report]].
- **Delivery strands:** the 2025 agenda tied it to completing the national data centre, 500 underserved localities and WURI enrolment ([[2025-02-19 Burkina Faso Outlines 2025 Digital Agenda]]); a digital-education strand supports curricula, an online learning platform and 95 educational apps ([[2025-09-15 Burkina Faso Advances Digital Education Despite Connectivity, Training Challenges]]).

- **Scale, safeguards + budget (2025–26).** [[finance.new]] PACTDIGITAL-BF is **~$170m** (>96bn CFA IDA
  loan + state counterpart, five components), in force since Sept 2024; the PM cited **$30m** to the
  Legislative Assembly (2025) and the **2026 digital budget doubled to 61bn CFA (~$109.4m)** across a
  156-activity work plan. World Bank **safeguards/E&S workshops** ran in the regions (e.g. Tannounyan,
  Jul 2025), and a **mid-term review** (Feb 2026) confirmed the Bank still runs **PACTDIGITAL + [[wuri-programme|WURI]]
  jointly** in Burkina despite the ECOWAS exit — 750 white zones covered, four 2026 priorities. Sources:
  [[2026-03-23-pactdigital-bf-unveiled-digital-inclusion]], [[2025-12-30-burkina-faso-earmarks-109-4mln-digital-projects-2026]],
