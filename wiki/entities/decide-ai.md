# Decide (Decide AI)

Nigerian AI startup whose product is a **spreadsheet agent** — users analyse spreadsheets and
business data with natural-language prompts instead of formulas. Launched **2025** by
**[[abiodun-adetona]]**, a former [[flutterwave]] software engineer.

## What matters now

- **Decide for Work (launched 2026-07-17).** An **enterprise deployment arm** distributing the
  agent through organisations rather than individual sign-ups — universities, co-working spaces
  and professional communities. Decide works directly with the organisation to provision access,
  onboard users and integrate into existing workflows; **pricing varies by deployment size, user
  count, support requirements and custom integrations** (no published rate card). Same underlying
  agent, repackaged for organisational distribution.
- **First deployment partner: [[cafeone]] (2026-07-17).** CafeOne members on an active
  subscription receive **premium credits** unlocking Decide, with access lasting while the
  credits do. Members activate via instructions from CafeOne or by asking staff at any location.
  Adetona says talks are underway with further co-working spaces, universities, professional
  communities and companies.
- **Usage and benchmark figures, company-reported (as of 2026-07-17).** **>41,000 analysis runs**;
  **>21,000 spreadsheets** created and analysed since launch; users in **>10 countries**; and
  **82.5% verified accuracy on SpreadsheetBench**, a benchmark for AI spreadsheet agents, which
  the company says places it alongside models from OpenAI and Anthropic. *All self-reported. The
  benchmark score is the only externally-defined measure, and the wiki holds no independent
  verification of it or of the leaderboard position claimed.*

## Why it matters here

The distribution model is the point of interest, not the product. Decide is routing an AI tool
into workplaces through **the physical and institutional intermediaries where knowledge work
already happens** — co-working networks, universities, professional communities — rather than
through individual subscription or a hyperscaler channel. That makes adoption a function of who
controls the membership relationship, and puts a third party (the co-working operator) between
the AI vendor and the end user handling business data. See [[tech.ai]], [[tech.industry]],
[[nigeria--tech-ai]].
