# Uganda Communications Commission (UCC)

Uganda's telecommunications regulator, headed by Executive Director Nyombi
Thembo. It licenses and can direct operators, and holds the statutory power (UCC
Act s.87) to suspend communication services — but only on a declared state of
emergency under Article 110 of the Constitution.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Ordered the pre-election internet shutdown (2026-01-13).** UCC ordered
  operators to freeze internet access on the evening of 13 January 2026, two days
  before the presidential election, citing misinformation and fraud risks. The
  full blackout lasted four days (restored a day after President Museveni was
  declared winner with 71%); social media stayed blocked longer. Biometric Voter
  Verification Kits (>$70m procurement) failed on election morning, and the
  outage cost operators [[mtn-group]] and [[airtel-africa]] an estimated ~$15m,
  with regional trade spillover on the Northern Corridor ([[UGA]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]]). Source: [[2026-01-26 The cost of Uganda's internet shutdown]].
- **Named in an illegality lawsuit (2026-01).** Lawyers filed suit against UCC,
  [[mtn-group]], [[airtel-africa]], Lyca Mobile and the Attorney General. The
  Uganda Law Society calls the order illegal, null and unconstitutional as no
  state of emergency was declared, so s.87 could not apply ([[gov.legislate]]).
  Source: [[2026-01-26 The cost of Uganda's internet shutdown]].
