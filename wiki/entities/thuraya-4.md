**Thuraya-4** is the mobile-satellite platform of UAE operator [[space42]], delivering mobile satellite communications (voice and data / direct-to-device connectivity) across Africa. Its rollout is presented as a milestone in extending resilient, accessible satellite connectivity to underserved regions of the continent.

## South Africa launch

Space42 marked the launch of **Thuraya-4 connectivity services in South Africa** on **9 February 2026** at an event in **Johannesburg** held **under the patronage of President Cyril Ramaphosa**. Space42 claims the launch brings **mobile satellite communications coverage to the entire African continent for the first time** — a key selling point for connectivity in areas beyond terrestrial network reach.

The launch was paired with demonstrations of Space42's **geospatial intelligence and Earth-observation** capabilities and the announcement of a forthcoming **"Map Africa"** geospatial initiative to support digital transformation across the continent through data-driven technologies. Government participation — a keynote by communications minister **Solly Malatsi**, Deputy Minister **Mondli Gungubele** on a national-development satellite panel, and remarks from the UAE Embassy — frames Thuraya-4 as part of UAE–South Africa bilateral technology cooperation and of the wider Gulf satellite presence in African connectivity. Reliance on a foreign-operated satellite platform for national and continental communications underlies the sovereignty lens.
