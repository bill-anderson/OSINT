# Simandou 2040

Guinea's flagship national development strategy to 2040 — the umbrella framing that anchors the country's digital-transformation agenda under President Mamadi Doumbouya's transition government. Named for the giant Simandou iron-ore project whose revenues are meant to fund it, the strategy is structured around **five pillars**, with **ICT/digital as one pillar** ([[gov.policy]]). Its **Pillar 3** positions digital as the **backbone of industrialization** — infrastructure, transport and technology — targeting a rise of the **digital sector to 20% of GDP**. It is coordinated via the Simandou Strategic Committee (chaired by Djiba Diakité). See [[GIN]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Pillar 3 (digital) launched at TAS 2025 (2025-11).** [[tech.industry]] Guinea launched the digital-technology pillar during the Transform Africa Summit ([[transform-africa-summit-2025]]), with the **Simandou Academy** to build local talent. Sources: [[2025-11-19 Transform Africa Summit 2025 (Conakry) - Simandou 2040 Pillar 3, TELEMO, SANIA-SADX and the closing wrap]], [[2025-11-13 Transform Africa Summit 2025 opens in Conakry, epicentre of digital cooperation in Africa]].
- **Umbrella for the digitization drive.** [[dpi.govtech]] FUGAS ([[fugas]]), the national data centre, TELEMO ([[telemo]]) e-procurement, Guinea Telecoms connectivity and the GIGA school-connectivity/Simandou Academy programme are all framed as Simandou 2040 deliverables.
