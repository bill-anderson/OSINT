# Edson Guyai

**Director of ID Management at [[nida-tanzania]]** (Tanzania's National Identification
Authority) — as of **2025-06**. His **[[id4africa]] 2025 AGM** presentation is the fullest
public account held here of **[[jamii-namba]]**'s coverage, economics and design.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Coverage account (2025-06-17, ID4Africa 2025 AGM).** *"We have successfully registered over
  25 million individuals... the eligible age for registration is 18 years and above. Based on the
  2022 national census, the total population aged 18 and above stands at over 31.5 million. This
  means we've covered **about 81 percent** of the eligible adult population."* NIDA had
  **personalised over 21.2 million IDs** and **issued more than 20.1 million cards**;
  registration extends to citizens, legal residents, refugees and stateless persons. **These
  figures corroborate the Ministry of Home Affairs budget speech of 2025-05-27** (25.8m
  registered = 81.9% of 31,582,976 aged 18+) — a conference panel and a parliamentary budget,
  independent routes, agreeing closely. See [[jamii-namba]],
  [[ministry-of-home-affairs-tanzania]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].
- **The ID as a revenue stream (2025-06-17).** *"So far, we have integrated with **over 124
  institutions**, both public and private. Through ID verification services, we've generated
  **approximately $45.4 million**. We charge **$0.18 per transaction** and additional fees for
  card replacement and legal resident registration."* He credited **SIM card registration** with
  boosting ID registration by **about 25 percent**. Named challenges: legal constraints,
  financial shortfalls, geographic accessibility, and lack of foundational documents. See
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].
- **Infant registration and the mother-linked workaround (2025-06-17).** Guyai disclosed plans
  for infant biometric registration of **children below age five**, with a pilot **expected in
  July 2025** in selected regions with **[[unicef]]** support, integral to the Universal ID /
  Jamii Number project: *"Upon birth, a child will receive both a birth certificate and a
  National ID number and document."* **Because of challenges finding a biometric solution that
  works for under-fives, NIDA opted for the interim measure of linking the child's identity to
  the mother's National ID.** He added that NIDA was *"upgrading ICT infrastructure, reforming
  our registration processes, and **reviewing our legal frameworks to align with these new
  goals**"* — an admission that the statutory basis (**[[tanzania-infant-registration-bill]]**,
  still pending as of 2026-01) trailed the pilot. See [[rita-tanzania]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].
