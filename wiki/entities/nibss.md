# Nigeria Inter-Bank Settlement System (NIBSS)

NIBSS is Nigeria's core interbank payments and settlement operator, jointly owned by the
[[cbn]] and licensed deposit banks. It runs the shared rails beneath Nigeria's retail
digital-payments market and is a foundational piece of the country's [[dpi.pay]] stack.
Managing Director/CEO: **Premier Oiwoh**.

## What matters now

- **National Payment Stack (NPS).** In 2025 NIBSS unveiled the [[national-payment-stack]],
  an ISO 20022-compliant infrastructure integrating payments, identity and data in one
  platform (real-time single/bulk/merchant settlement, automated reconciliation,
  request-to-pay, direct debit, and KYC via BVN/TIN/RC Number), with multi-currency and
  cross-border ambitions. First live transaction: **7 Nov 2025**, PalmPay → Wema Bank,
  instant settlement. See [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.exchange]].
- **NIBSS Instant Payments (NIP).** NIP was Africa's first real-time, account-based digital
  payment system (launched over a decade ago) and is rated **"Mature"** on the AfricaNenda
  ([[africanenda]]) Inclusivity Spectrum — the first African instant-payment system to reach
  the top level. Per-transaction fees fell N5 → N3.75 (Jul 2023) → **N2 (Jan 2026)**, with a
  stated goal of a zero-rated framework (free at point of use). See [[include.access]].
- **Continental positioning.** The NPS unveiling in Lagos drew deputy governors and
  executives from **20+ African central banks**, part of Nigeria's pitch as a continental
  digital-finance hub. Thematically linked to — but distinct from — the continental
  [[papss]] rail. See [[gov.regional]], [[XAF]].
- **The "payments end-game" read (analysis, 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki cites NIP as a lead case
  in his margin-collapse thesis: NIBSS processed **~$702.6bn in 2024** (volumes +120% in two
  years; operating since 2011), so as central-bank instant rails become near-free utilities,
  durable fintech value migrates off processing into credit, data and compliance layers.
  Analyst framing, attributed to Kariuki. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]].
