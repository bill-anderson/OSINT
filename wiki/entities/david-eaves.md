# David Eaves

Digital-government and DPI scholar (UCL Institute for Innovation and Public Purpose),
a leading voice for **"sovereignty through interoperability / commodification"** —
taming hyperscalers via standards and procurement rather than proprietary sovereign
stacks.

## Positions / arguments

- **Sovereign tech stack via a *commodified* stack** (Tech Policy Press, 2025-12-15):
  standardise storage around S3, commodify government platform services, use
  procurement power to force interoperability. See [[gov.standards]], [[infra.store]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[geopol.usa]].

## Body of work in this wiki

- [[2025-12-15 The Path to a Sovereign Tech Stack is Via a Commodified Tech Stack  TechPolicy.Press]]
  — op-ed (Tech Policy Press, 2025-12-15).
- [[2026-06-02 Eaves commodification]] — **"Parting Clouds: Creating a Competitive Marketplace for

- [[2025-01-01 dpi_conceptualisation_and_measurement]] — **DPI: a framework for conceptualisation and

- **Profile.** Co-Deputy Director of [[ucl-iipp]] (ex-Harvard Kennedy School); created the
  **DPI Map** (dpimap.org) and leads the annual **State of Digital Public Infrastructure**
  report. Co-authored the 2026 "what is 'public' about DPI" paper with
  [[mariana-mazzucato]] and Beatriz Vasconcellos. Sources:
  [[2026-01-01 David Eaves Personal Site]],
  [[2026-04-03 Digital public infrastructure and the economics of public value what is 'public' about DPI]].

- **Framework operationalised in the DPI Map methodology (2026-07).** The [[dpi-map]]'s
  published "Measuring DPI" methodology explicitly rests on Eaves & Rao's WP 2025-01 (6
  attributes; ID/payment/data-exchange outcome indicators) as its counting authority. See
  [[dpi-map]], [[ucl-iipp]]. Source: [[2026-07-12 Methodology - DPI Map]].

## Relevance

His commodification thesis is the analytical counterpoint to ownership-based digital
sovereignty — directly relevant to how African states (e.g. [[ZMB]]'s open-standards,
vendor-independent ID) should read their DPI choices.
