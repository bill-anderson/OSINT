# EU AI Act

The European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act — the world's first comprehensive, horizontal
AI law and, in this wiki, the reference instrument against which African AI governance is
repeatedly measured (as model, as transplant, and as extraterritorial constraint). Enacted by
the [[european-union]].

## Key facts (as of 2026-07-12)

- **In force 2024-08-01**; applies on a staggered timeline.
- **Risk-based, four-tier structure**: unacceptable (banned), high, limited (transparency), minimal.
- **Prohibited-practice bans from 2025-02-02** — e.g. workplace emotion recognition, untargeted
  facial-image scraping, government social scoring, and real-time public facial recognition for
  law enforcement.
- **High-risk full enforcement 2026-08-02** — obligations (risk management, human oversight,
  technical documentation, transparency, accuracy) for high-risk categories including credit
  scoring, hiring, healthcare and education.
- **Extraterritorial reach** via a "market placement / where the output lands" test — binds
  non-EU providers and deployers whose AI output affects the EU market, and shapes tools used in
  Africa indirectly (per [[zuria-nagadya]]).
- **Penalties** scale to global turnover (up to ~3% for high-risk non-compliance, ~7% for
  prohibited practices) — contrasted in the wiki with Kenya's KES 5m flat cap.
