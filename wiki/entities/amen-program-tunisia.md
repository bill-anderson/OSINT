# AMEN Program (Tunisia)

**Tunisia's flagship social-assistance initiative — launched in 2019 with [[world-bank]] support and
implemented by the [[ministry-of-social-affairs-tunisia]] — targeting the most vulnerable citizens
with cash transfers, medical services and economic empowerment.** Its data significance is the
**AMEN social registry**, the beneficiary database that made targeted and, increasingly, digital
payments possible.

**Not the same thing as the [[subsidies-compensation-platform-tunisia]].** The two are adjacent —
both concern targeted transfers to Tunisian households — but **no source held establishes them as
the same platform**, and the Access Now allegations about data-protection impact assessments attach
to the Subsidies Compensation Platform, not to AMEN. Keep them separate; the relationship is
**unestablished**.

*The [[world-bank]] source is a first-party feature on a programme the Bank funds — promotional
register. Capture is not endorsement.*

## Activity in this wiki

- **Design.** Launched **2019**. Provides **Permanent Cash Transfers** (monthly), medical services
  and economic empowerment opportunities, plus family allowances for children aged 0–5. See
  [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-14 Tunisians Share How their Life Changed Through the AMEN Program]].
- **Reach (as of December 2023).** Over **333,000 poor households** — **more than 10% of the
  population** — on monthly cash transfers; more than **620,000 low-income households** with free
  and subsidised healthcare; **54%** of cash-transfer beneficiaries are women-headed households;
  almost **156,000 children under 5** receiving allowances of about **30 dinars per month**, from
  **103,000 households** enrolled in the **AMEN social registry**. See [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-14 Tunisians Share How their Life Changed Through the AMEN Program]].
- **Payment digitalisation (as of December 2023).** **44.4%** of cash transfers digitalised; **over
  76%** of women beneficiaries paid digitally. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-14 Tunisians Share How their Life Changed Through the AMEN Program]].
- **The registry proved its value under COVID.** During the pandemic the programme distributed
  emergency cash transfers to approximately **900,000 households**, of which **more than 630,000
  were already enrolled in the AMEN social registry** — the clearest evidence held of a
  pre-existing registry enabling shock response at speed. See [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-14 Tunisians Share How their Life Changed Through the AMEN Program]].
- **Economic context (as of 2024-11).** Tunisia has faced significant economic challenges since
  2011, worsened by COVID-19, the war in Ukraine and severe droughts — declining GDP growth, high
  food inflation, rising poverty, and **unemployment at 16.4%**. **40%** of the World Bank's
  portfolio in Tunisia is dedicated to social resilience and human development initiatives such as
  AMEN, under the Country Partnership Framework 2023–2027. See [[finance.new]],
  [[data.statistics]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-14 Tunisians Share How their Life Changed Through the AMEN Program]].
- **Reference study — cite, don't absorb.** The IDRC Centre of Excellence for CRVS Systems'
  five-country volume *The Nexus between Civil Registration and Social Protection Systems* (2020)
  includes a Tunisia chapter treating AMEN in the civil-registration/social-protection linkage. It
  is a **reference study**: consult it, but its dated figures are not promoted here as current
  state. See [[idrc-crvs-centre-of-excellence]]. Source:
  [[2020-01-01 Nexus between Civil Registration and Social Protection - Tunisia chapter (IDRC)]].
