# Kasi Cloud

Nigerian interconnection and data-centre platform, backed by the [[nsia]] (Nigeria
Sovereign Investment Authority), building domestic hyperscale capacity as a bid to
keep African data and compute onshore.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Broke ground on a $250m Lekki hyperscale campus (2026-02-26).** Kasi Cloud began
  construction of a $250 million hyperscale data-centre campus in Lekki, Lagos, backed
  by sovereign capital from [[nsia]] — a marker of state-directed investment into
  [[NGA]] domestic [[infra.store]] rather than reliance on foreign hyperscalers.
  Source: [[2026-02-26 Lagos Is Getting a $250 Million Hyperscale Data Center. Here's Why Nigeria Still Can't Power the Cloud]].
- **Power, not capital, is the binding constraint ([[infra.energy]], 2025–2026).** The
  project lands in a market where Nigeria's operational data-centre capacity was ~86MW
  in 2025 with >320MW planned, yet the national grid has never exceeded ~6GW for 230m
  people. Diesel backup runs $0.40–0.50/kWh and construction costs ~$12/watt — the
  economics that keep global hyperscalers cautious about Nigeria.
  Source: [[2026-02-26 Lagos Is Getting a $250 Million Hyperscale Data Center. Here's Why Nigeria Still Can't Power the Cloud]].
