# Zimbabwe Cyber and Data Protection Act (2021)

**Zimbabwe's principal data protection law, applying to all public and private
organisations regardless of size**, and enforced not by a dedicated authority but by
the telecom regulator, [[potraz]]. Held material in this wiki already leans on the
Act — the [[ZWE]] hub cites **s.164C** in the MISA critique of
[[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]] — so this page exists to describe the instrument
that other pages were already relying on.

**The gazetted text is now held (2026-07-20).** The wiki previously described this Act only
through a law-firm briefing; it now holds the **verbatim gazetted text of the Chapter 12:07
consolidation** and the **verbatim text of SI 155 of 2024**, both from the Government Gazette
via Veritas Zimbabwe. Statutory claims below are cited to the instrument, not to the secondary.

## The document, and its dates

- **Act No. 5 of 2021**, cited as the **Cyber and Data Protection Act [Chapter 12:07]**. The
  text held is the **Chapter 12:07 consolidation as gazetted 11 March 2022**.
- **Commencement is not established from the text.** The consolidation carries **no
  commencement clause** — s.1 gives the short title only. The wiki therefore dates the Act by
  its enactment year (2021) and its gazetted consolidation (2022-03-11), and does **not** assert
  a commencement date (as of 2026-07-20). Assent, gazette and commencement are three different
  dates and only the second is on file.
- **Long title (verbatim).** To provide for data protection with regard to the Declaration of
  Rights; **to establish a Cyber Security Centre and a Data Protection Authority**; to create a
  technology-driven business environment; and **to amend ss.162–166 of the Criminal Law
  (Codification and Reform) Act [Chapter 9:23]** for the investigation of cybercrime and the
  admissibility of electronic evidence. *The dual character the MISA critique turns on is in the
  long title itself: a data-protection statute and a cybercrime-enforcement statute in one
  instrument.*
- **s.5 — the designation, from the statute.** "Designation of Postal and Telecommunications
  Regulatory Authority as Data Protection Authority." **POTRAZ's dual role is not an
  administrative arrangement but an express provision of the Act** — previously asserted here
  on a law-firm briefing, now cited to s.5. See [[potraz]].
- **s.164C, verbatim.** Inserted into the Criminal Code by this Act: *"Any person who unlawfully
  and intentionally by means of a computer or information system makes available, broadcasts or
  distributes data to any other person concerning an identified or identifiable person knowing
  it to be false with intend to cause psychological or economic harm shall be guilty of an
  offence and liable to a fine not exceeding level 10 or to imprisonment for a period not
  exceeding five years or to both."* (*sic* — "with intend" is as gazetted.) **The provision the
  [[ZWE]] hub and [[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]] both lean on is now quoted from the gazette
  rather than relayed.** Source:
  [[2022-03-11-zimbabwe-cyber-and-data-protection-act-chapter-12-07]].

## SI 155 of 2024 — the licensing regulations, from the gazette

Made by the **Minister of Information Communications Technology, Postal and Courier Services**
in consultation with the Authority under **s.32 of the Act**, and published in the **Government
Gazette dated 13 September 2024**.

- **Licensing deadline for existing controllers: 13 March 2025** — s.4(5) gives six months from
  promulgation. *This is the deadline whose lapse POTRAZ was enforcing against in its March 2026
  warning; the wiki previously recorded that the deadline had "lapsed" without holding the date.*
- **DPO appointment: 90 days** from promulgation (s.12(5)) — **12 December 2024**.
- **Licences run 12 months** (s.5(1)), renewable; four categories (s.6); fees in the Second
  Schedule scale with data-subject numbers.
- **Breach notification: 24 hours to the Authority** (s.17(1)), data subjects within 72 hours
  where risk is high.
- **Forms**: DP1 (licence application/renewal), DP2 (DPO designation), DP3 (breach
  notification) — the instruments behind the "570 licences / Form DP1" reporting on
  [[zimbabwe--gov-protect]].
- **Correction to a held figure (2026-07-20).** The Michalsons briefing dated the regulations
  **into force 12 September 2024**; the gazette is dated **13 September 2024**. A one-day
  discrepancy, corrected to the primary. The **12 December 2024** DPO deadline is unaffected —
  it is 90 days from the 13th.

Source: [[2024-09-13-zimbabwe-si-155-2024-licensing-data-controllers-dpo-regulations]].

## Provisions (per Michalsons, 2025-06-30 — secondary, retained where the text is not yet mined)

- **Scope.** All processing of personal data, automated or partly automated, by
  individuals, private bodies and public bodies alike.
- **Extraterritorial reach.** Covers those outside Zimbabwe using equipment, systems
  or services located in Zimbabwe; **foreign controllers must appoint a local
  representative**.
- **Licensing Regulations.** *(Superseded by the gazetted SI above — Michalsons' "12
  September 2024" is corrected to **13 September 2024**.)* Registered-but-not-licensed
  controllers need no DPO.
- **Registration fees scale with the number of data subjects** processed.
- **Breach notification.** POTRAZ within **24 hours**; affected individuals within
  **72 hours** where the breach poses a high risk.
- **Cross-border transfers.** Notify POTRAZ **before** transferring personal data out
  of Zimbabwe; permitted only where the destination offers adequate protection or a
  specific condition (consent, legal necessity) is met.
- **The Act does not regulate the sale of personal data.**
- **No internal ROPAs required** — instead **POTRAZ maintains a public register of
  automated data processing**, an inversion of the GDPR-style controller-held record.
- **Special categories.** Sensitive, genetic, **biometric** and health data require
  **written consent**, subject to exceptions including **national security**,
  employment law, vital interests, public health and research. Health data may only
  be processed under a health professional's supervision unless the subject consents
  or harm is imminent, and **the controller must issue a unique health ID**.
- **Children.** A child is anyone **under 18**; processing requires parental or
  guardian consent, **DPIAs are mandatory** for children's data, and data protection
  by design and default applies.
- **Principles.** Lawfulness/fairness/transparency, purpose limitation, minimisation,
  accuracy, storage limitation, integrity and confidentiality, accountability.

## Two reads, not a contradiction

Michalsons' **compliance-framed** reading (a workable framework; here are the
deadlines) and MISA Zimbabwe's **rights-framed** reading (s.164C as scaffolding for
centralised data and verification authority without judicial oversight — see
[[ZWE]], [[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]]) **do not conflict on any fact**. They
weight the same statute differently. That is two reads, not a contradiction, and no
reconcile item is opened.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the cross-border-transfer notification duty and the
  local-representative rule for foreign controllers are jurisdictional assertions —
  Zimbabwe claiming reach over data processing conducted on or about it from abroad.
