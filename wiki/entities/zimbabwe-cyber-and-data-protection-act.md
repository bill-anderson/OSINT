# Zimbabwe Cyber and Data Protection Act (2021)

**Zimbabwe's principal data protection law, applying to all public and private
organisations regardless of size**, and enforced not by a dedicated authority but by
the telecom regulator, [[potraz]]. Held material in this wiki already leans on the
Act — the [[ZWE]] hub cites **s.164C** in the MISA critique of
[[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]] — so this page exists to describe the instrument
that other pages were already relying on.

**Provenance caveat.** The description below rests on a single **law-firm briefing**
(Michalsons, which markets data-protection compliance services) — admissible as a
dated explainer, but attribute, and note its compliance-urgency framing is
commercial. **The Act text itself is not held (GAP-ZWE-001).**

## Provisions (per Michalsons, 2025-06-30)

- **Scope.** All processing of personal data, automated or partly automated, by
  individuals, private bodies and public bodies alike.
- **Extraterritorial reach.** Covers those outside Zimbabwe using equipment, systems
  or services located in Zimbabwe; **foreign controllers must appoint a local
  representative**.
- **Licensing Regulations.** The Cyber and Data Protection (Licensing of Data
  Controllers and Appointment of Data Protection Officers) Regulations were
  introduced **September 2024** and came **into force 12 September 2024**. Licensed
  controllers had until **12 December 2024** to appoint a DPO; registered-but-not-
  licensed controllers need none.
- **Registration fees scale with the number of data subjects** processed.
- **Breach notification.** POTRAZ within **24 hours**; affected individuals within
  **72 hours** where the breach poses a high risk.
- **Cross-border transfers.** Notify POTRAZ **before** transferring personal data out
  of Zimbabwe; permitted only where the destination offers adequate protection or a
  specific condition (consent, legal necessity) is met.
- **The Act does not regulate the sale of personal data.**
- **No internal ROPAs required** — instead **POTRAZ maintains a public register of
  automated data processing**, an inversion of the GDPR-style controller-held record.
- **Special categories.** Sensitive, genetic, **biometric** and health data require
  **written consent**, subject to exceptions including **national security**,
  employment law, vital interests, public health and research. Health data may only
  be processed under a health professional's supervision unless the subject consents
  or harm is imminent, and **the controller must issue a unique health ID**.
- **Children.** A child is anyone **under 18**; processing requires parental or
  guardian consent, **DPIAs are mandatory** for children's data, and data protection
  by design and default applies.
- **Principles.** Lawfulness/fairness/transparency, purpose limitation, minimisation,
  accuracy, storage limitation, integrity and confidentiality, accountability.

## Two reads, not a contradiction

Michalsons' **compliance-framed** reading (a workable framework; here are the
deadlines) and MISA Zimbabwe's **rights-framed** reading (s.164C as scaffolding for
centralised data and verification authority without judicial oversight — see
[[ZWE]], [[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]]) **do not conflict on any fact**. They
weight the same statute differently. That is two reads, not a contradiction, and no
reconcile item is opened.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the cross-border-transfer notification duty and the
  local-representative rule for foreign controllers are jurisdictional assertions —
  Zimbabwe claiming reach over data processing conducted on or about it from abroad.
