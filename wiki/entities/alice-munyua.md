# Alice Munyua

A digital-policy analyst and one of the wiki's recurring **named sovereignty critics**, author
of the **"The Gatekeepers"** analysis series. Her work reads flagship African digital-governance
partnerships against the question of who actually holds control, and supplies the
sovereignty/colonialism-lens counterpoint to official framings.

## Analytical position (in this wiki)

- **AU–Google MoU ([[au-google-mou]], "The Gatekeepers Part XII," 2026-03-05).** Argues the deal
  is "the formalisation of African exposure": because the infrastructure is US-owned/managed, the
  **[[cloud-act]]** can compel Google to produce data regardless of residency clauses; the press
  release used "sovereign" four times but in no binding clause (no data-localisation requirement,
  audit right or exit clause). Likens it to the 2012 Huawei-fitted AU HQ that reportedly
  exfiltrated data to Shanghai for five years.
- **AU Continental AI Strategy ([[au-continental-ai-strategy]]).** Critiques it as **not
  substantively African-authored** — a sovereignty gap between rhetoric and drafting.
- **US–Kenya health framework as "the new scramble" ("The Gatekeepers Part V: The New Maps of
  Africa," 2025-12-15).** Reads the early-Dec-2025 [[us-kenya-health-framework]] alongside the
  Trump National Security Strategy (signed the same day) as data-colonialism: **25 years of
  access** to Kenyan health data, pathogen specimens and genetic sequences for **5 years of
  funding**, with benefit-sharing "to be determined" (Art 3(f)) and a clause disclaiming any
  international-law rights or obligations (Art 5(g)). Notes Kenya's National Intelligence Research
  University launched a **facial-recognition surveillance hackathon** days after the signing; cites
  Estonia as the counter-model. See [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-15 THE GATEKEEPERS PART V The New Maps of Africa]].
- **Internet-governance double standard ("The Double Standard," 2025-11-04).** Charges [[icann]]
  with co-developing and funding [[smart-africa]]'s **Continental Africa Internet Governance
  Architecture ([[caiga]])**, which would let the Smart Africa Heads-of-State Summit politically
  endorse **[[afrinic]]** governance reforms, install a Smart Africa-provided Permanent Secretary,
  and substitute political endorsement for member ratification — i.e. governmental capture of a
  community-governed registry. Points to a 2024 ICANN/Smart Africa MoU and the CAIGA blueprint
  drafted by a third party based in France, and asks whether ICANN would treat RIPE NCC/ARIN/APNIC
  the same way. See [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-04 The Double Standard Why Are Internet Governance Rules Different for Africa]].
- **Kenya AI Bill 2026 as EU-AI-Act transplant ("Gatekeepers XIV," 2026-05-19).** Argues the
  **[[kenya-ai-bill-2026]]** is a wholesale transplant of the **[[eu-ai-act]]** ("EU AI Act with a
  Kenyan patronage chassis," self-declared on p.73 of its Memorandum of Objects) that regulates a
  stack Kenya did not build: a presidentially-appointed AI Commissioner (patronage), a **KES 5m
  (~$40k) max fine** where the EU Act uses 3%/7% of global turnover, no extraterritorial reach, no
  pre-deployment impact assessment and no community standing. Situates it in the "philanthropic
  industrial complex" (Gates Foundation; the Kigali **[[africa-ai-declaration]]** / $60bn Africa AI
  Fund) with the Social Health Authority algorithm as the live "proof of concept." Munyua counts
  the Kigali declaration as signed by **54 African governments** — high against the **primary
  Declaration text, which lists 49** ([[africa-ai-declaration]]; the authoritative count). See
  [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-05-19 GATEKEEPERS XIV]].
