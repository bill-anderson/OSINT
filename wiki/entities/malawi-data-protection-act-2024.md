# Malawi Data Protection Act (2024)

Malawi's national data-protection statute, enacted in 2024, establishing the legal framework for
lawful handling of personal information across public and private institutions. The government
positions it as the anchor for a trusted digital ecosystem and the aspirations of Malawi 2063.

## Legislative history (from the Act's own text)

The statute's own front matter and Part I settle the enactment chain:

- **Passed by Parliament — 2023-12-07.**
- **Assented — 2024-01-31** by President Dr Lazarus McCarthy Chakwera; **gazetted 2024-02-02** as
  **Act No. 3 of 2024**.
- **Commenced — 2024-06-03.** s.1 provides that the Act "shall come into operation on such date as the
  Minister may appoint by notice published in the Gazette"; that power was exercised by **Government
  Notice 40 of 2024**, appointing 3 June 2024.
- **Supervisory authority — s.4** designates the **[[macra-malawi|Malawi Communications Regulatory
  Authority]]** (established under s.4 of the Communications Act) as the **Data Protection Authority**,
  to act "independent of the direction, influence or interference of any person or entity". No new body
  was created — the Zambian model rather than a standalone DPA.
- **Structure.** 53 sections in ten Parts: principles (Part III), data-subject rights (Part IV),
  controller/processor duties (Part V), data security and breach notification (Part VI), **cross-border
  transfers and adequacy** (Part VII, ss.38–40), registration of controllers/processors *of significant
  importance* (Part VIII), complaints and compliance orders (Part IX). Extraterritorial reach: s.3(1)(b)
  applies the Act to processing outside Malawi that targets goods/services at, or monitors the behaviour
  of, data subjects within Malawi.
- **Transitional grace — s.53** (reported, *not* in the captured range of the source text): six months for
  controllers/processors of significant importance (to ~2024-12-03) and twenty-four months for all others
  (to 2026-06-02). **Needs verification against the full statute on manual re-clip.**

Source: [[2024-02-02-malawi-data-protection-act-2024]] (partial capture — front matter, Parts I–II
verbatim, breaking off in s.6).

> The Act being **in force since 2024-06-03** is the settled position. Note that
> [[2025-05-21 Malawi to Launch Mandatory Biometric SIM Registration in July 2025]] (ID Tech Wire) asserts

## Activity in this wiki

- **Operationalisation tendered under DMAP (Oct 2025).** The Government, through the Public Private
  Partnership Commission under the World-Bank-financed **[[dmap]]** (P505095, IDA-E338-MW), issued
  **Terms of Reference** for consultancy to stand up the DPA unit inside **[[macra-malawi|MACRA]]** —
  organogram and governance charter, job descriptions, operations manual, an M&E and **enforcement**
  framework, drafting of secondary instruments, a business plan targeting **cost recovery within five
  years**, and a **scale of fines**. 100 staff-days over 16 weeks. Sixteen months after commencement, the
  authority's operating machinery was still being designed. Source:
  [[2025-10-01-dmap-tor-operationalization-data-protection-authority]].
- **Cited as evidence of government resolve (2026-01-28).** At Data Privacy Week in Lilongwe,
  the [[malawi-ministry-of-ict]] (Minister Shadrick Namalomba) pointed to the Act as proof of
  the government's commitment to strengthen accountability, curb data misuse and ensure ethical
  handling of personal data, declaring data protection "no longer optional" and central to
  Malawi 2063. Framed alongside a "Privacy by Design" approach to e-government, digital ID and
  emerging-technology systems. Tags [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[MWI]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Malawi positions data privacy as cornerstone of digital development]].
- **Operationalisation — draft registration regulations (2026-07).** **[[macra-malawi|MACRA]]** (the designated
  DPA) consulted on **Draft Data Protection Regulations, Guidelines and Registration Fees** requiring
  organisations that process personal data to **register**, with **turnover-based fees** (K50,000/~$29 →
  K7m/>$4,000). The move puts implementation machinery behind the 2024 statute as Malawi expands digital ID,
  e-government, online finance and AI. Sources:
  [[2026-07-16-macra-data-protection-registration-compliance-malawi24]],
  [[2026-07-14-macra-data-protection-registration-fees-consultation-itweb]].
