# Kenya Cyber Resilience (KCR) Project

An EU-funded programme to harden Kenya's critical information infrastructure and build
national cyber skills, launched in Nairobi in the week of **2026-01-27**. Budget **$3.56m
(€3m)** over **36 months**; funded by the **[[european-union]]** and implemented by
**[[expertise-france]]** alongside **[[estdev]]**.

## Profile

- Responds to rising cyber threats as **>20,000 government services** moved online over the
  past year; financial services, telecoms, government platforms and healthcare are the most
  targeted sectors.
- Strengthens cybersecurity governance, institutional coordination and public awareness;
  includes nationwide cyber-hygiene campaigns targeting women, youth and users of digital
  public services, with an interactive training/certification platform.
- Aligned to Kenya's **National Cybersecurity Strategy** and **Digital Master Plan**, and
  lays groundwork for a future **National Cybersecurity Agency** to coordinate major-incident
  response.
- Officials at launch: PS **Steve Isaboke** (Broadcasting & Telecommunications), PS **John
  Tanui** (ICT & Digital Economy).
