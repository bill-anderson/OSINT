**Forensic Data Analysts (FDA)** is a South African software contractor that built and operated biometric and evidence-management systems for the [[saps]]. In court proceedings the name "FDA" was used by Judge Sulet Potterill as shorthand for a group of related suppliers — **Forensic Data Analysts, Investigative Software Solutions and Solve Forensics**. FDA is the contractor at the centre of the R1bn-plus SAPS firearms-system dispute.

## Systems built for SAPS

- **Firearm Permit System (FPS)** — biometric processing of all firearm permit applications in South Africa, tracking weapons issued to officers and booked into police custody.
- **Property Control and Exhibit Management System (PCEM)** — management of forensic exhibits and evidence across the police service.
- **VA-AMIS** — an intelligence tool supporting DNA testing and investigative analysis for the Hawks and Crime Intelligence.

## The dispute

SAPS, under commissioner Khehla Sitole, stopped paying FDA's licensing fees in late **2017** while continuing to use the systems in defiance of court interdicts. In **2019** FDA **switched the systems off** over the accumulated non-payment; per Judge Potterill's ruling, FDA had to **retrench 70 employees** as a result. On **31 January 2020** FDA sent a sheriff to physically remove its systems from [[sita]]'s premises. The **Pretoria High Court** found in FDA's favour in **December 2025**, ordering SAPS to pay **R644 million** for the intellectual property and **R120 million** for maintenance/support, with interest accruing from the date of breach — roughly R1 million a week while SAPS appeals to the Supreme Court of Appeal. The court additionally found that [[sita]] had **illegally extracted FDA's source code** in an attempt to build replacement systems (all as of the 2026-03-08 report). FDA owns the copyright in the three systems, a point the court found Sitole had acknowledged despite conflicting sworn testimony.
