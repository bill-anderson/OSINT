# Axian Telecom

A pan-African telecommunications group, part of the Madagascar-headquartered ([[MDG]])
Axian conglomerate, operating mobile, fibre and digital-financial-services businesses
across several African markets under the **[[yas]]** operating brand. CEO **Hassan Jaber**.
Appears in this wiki as a telco voice on connectivity and AI-driven digital inclusion
([[infra.connect]], [[tech.ai]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Collaboration framing for AI inclusion (2026).** CEO Hassan Jaber argued operators
  "cannot do it alone" — governments, public institutions, operators and digital players
  must work together to ensure Africa is included in the AI-driven era, in the context of
  using AI as an access layer to reach feature-phone users. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].

- **"Second division" player, US$600m raise (Russell Southwood, 2025-12-04).**
  Southwood's MNO state-of-market places Axian in the "second division" (with
  Africell, Econet, Globacom, Telecel) rather than the four power players, but
  notes it raised **US$600m** and **bought Wananchi** (the East African
  broadband/ISP group) — consolidation as smaller operators scale up. See
  [[tech.industry]], [[finance.new]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].

- **EBRD €270m loan to Yas for Kenya/Senegal (2026-07).** Axian's [[yas]] brand is the
  borrower in the **[[ebrd-yas-network-loan]]** ([[ebrd]]) — 4G/5G/fibre in [[SEN]] and
  fibre in [[KEN]] (on the Wananchi base) — the group's largest-ever financing. See
  [[finance.new]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 EBRD Extends €270m Financing to Yas for Kenya, Senegal Network Expansion]].
