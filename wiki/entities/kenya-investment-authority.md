# Kenya Investment Authority (Invest Kenya)

Kenya's national investment-promotion agency, led by chief executive **[[john-mwendwa]]**.

## Residency by investment (2026-07)

Invest Kenya is **developing proposals for a residency-by-investment programme** — long-term or
permanent residency for foreign investors who commit substantial capital and create jobs — reviving a
plan **first floated in 2019 and never implemented**. Mwendwa, 2026-07-17: *"We are exploring
residency by investment. Directionally, that's the way investors would like it."*

Three constraints stated on the record and worth holding as the test of seriousness:

- **Thresholds and qualifying sectors are undetermined** as of 2026-07-17.
- **It requires legislation** — immigration policy sits **outside Invest Kenya's mandate**, so the
  agency can propose but not enact.
- **"We have to have parameters that make commercial sense"** — the design question, unanswered.

The agency's framing is competitive rather than domestic: strengthening Nairobi against **Cape Town,
Kigali and Mauritius** as an investment base, and shifting the incentive instrument from tax holidays
to residency rights — chasing capital whose tax contribution stays for decades rather than the length
of a holiday.

**Existing route, unchanged:** foreign investors currently need a **Class G Investor Permit**,
available to those investing at least **$100,000** in an active Kenyan enterprise, before becoming
eligible to apply for citizenship after several years of residence.
