# Société gabonaise de garantie (SGG)

Gabonese **public credit-guarantee institution** — the vehicle through which the state absorbs part of
the lending risk banks carry when financing local enterprises. Director-general (2026): **Alban Etho**.

Its entry into the digital file matters because it addresses the constraint Gabon's own ministry names
first: **access to finance**, not connectivity, as the binding limit on start-up formation. A guarantee
mechanism is a cheaper instrument than a state fund — the state underwrites risk rather than deploying
capital — which also means the volume of credit it unlocks depends entirely on bank appetite.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Start-up guarantee accord with the digital-economy ministry (2026-07-14).** Signed
  **[[gabon-sgg-startup-guarantee-accord]]** to establish guarantee mechanisms reducing the risk borne
  by financial institutions lending to innovative young firms. Etho said **risk-sharing is a key
  condition** for stimulating innovation and improving start-ups' access to bank financing. **No
  guarantee ceiling, coverage ratio, term or budget was published.** Source:
  [[2026-07-17-gabon-garantie-publique-huawei-start-up]].
