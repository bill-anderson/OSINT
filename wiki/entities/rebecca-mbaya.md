# Rebecca Mbaya

Rebecca Mbaya writes at *reamby.substack.com* ("Beyond the Algorithm") and is the founder of
**TAIS** — a weekly interview series documenting African AI and technology practitioners,
running since March 2025 — and its associated **Bridge Builders Circle**, a community
connecting African innovators with non-African thinkers who want to engage with the work on
its own terms. Her throughline is *context as infrastructure*: the idea that context is not
a variable to be adjusted for after the fact but the ground on which everything else is
built, and that stripping it out of sovereignty debates is itself a political choice about
whose reality counts.

## What she argues in this wiki

- **Controlling the keys vs owning the building.** She critiques the Nadella/Sunak (Davos
  2026) framing of sovereignty as portfolio risk-management as context-stripping for Africa,
  distinguishes data sovereignty from digital sovereignty, and argues that 54 nations without
  a shared definition negotiate individually and from weakness — "we cannot sum 54 individual
  positions and arrive at a continental one." She draws structurally on Siyabonga Mabuza's
  "Rewriting the Algorithm" for the colonial-inheritance account of AI bias and the
  latecomer-advantage and PESTEL framings
  ([[2026-06-24 Does AI Sovereignty Mean Controlling the Keys, or Owning the Building]]).
- **Documentation as an instrument of sovereignty.** In building the **TAIS Knowledge Map**
  — an interactive network of ~53 interviewed African AI innovators across six thematic
  clusters — she reflects on the contradiction of documenting African AI sovereignty using
  non-African tools (NotebookLM, Claude, Gemini, Lovable) and on the offline-first /
  connectivity gap that keeps the very people it documents from accessing it
  ([[2026-03-25 On Documenting African AI Knowledge What Becomes Possible]]).
