# New Deal Technologique (Senegal)

Senegal's national digital roadmap ("Horizon 2034"), built on four strategic pillars — digital sovereignty,
digitisation of public services, development of the digital economy, and positioning Senegal as a
leading African digital hub. **Launched 24 February 2025** by President Bassirou Diomaye Faye within the
"Senegal 2050" agenda; **estimated budget ~$1.7bn (cumulative CFA1,105bn by 2026)**, targeting by 2034
95% internet coverage, all sensitive data hosted onshore, 500+ startups and 150,000+ digital-economy jobs.

## Activity in this wiki

**Full strategy detail** (four pillars, ATN financing breakdown, 2034 KPIs, launch, one-year stocktake, GouvNum, e-Senegal): [[senegal--gov-policy]].

- **Founding launch (2025-02-24).** Faye unveiled the strategy: universal affordable internet; a **unique
  biometric-based digital ID** able to spawn functional/sectoral identities across life events; a single
  citizen portal; and an integrated services platform (business creation, tax, land registry, payments).
  Enablers: updated Startup Act, DPI, legal/regulatory frameworks, informal-economy digitisation, AI and
  cybersecurity. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.policy]], [[SEN]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-27 Senegal unveils New Deal Technologique digital transformation strategy]].
- **Gates Foundation $10m (2025-09).** The **[[gates-foundation]]** committed **$10m** (announced when Faye
  met Bill Gates at UNGA80) toward a **universal digital identity**, an **AI hub** (health/agriculture) and a
  **Delivery Unit** for transparency; Senegal needs ~$1.7bn for the 2025–2034 strategy. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[finance.new]], [[SEN]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-29 Gates Foundation fuels Senegal's digital transformation with $10M]].
- **GSMA reform map (2025-12-05).** A GSMA study presented at the first Digital Africa Summit Senegal
  recommended cutting sector taxes to 3%, exempting sub-CFA17,500 smartphones from VAT/customs, scrapping the
  **0.5% mobile-money transfer tax**, and treating telecom networks as critical national infrastructure —
  estimating full implementation could add 2.6m mobile-internet users and ~280,000 jobs by 2030. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.policy]], [[include.divides]], [[SEN]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-08 GSMA maps the reforms required for Senegal's digital takeoff]].
- **2026 budget: 60% to investment (2025-12).** The 2026 Digital Ministry budget was set at **CFA81.06bn
  (~$143.9m)**, ~60% to investment, funding universal coverage, free connectivity for **1m people**, a
  **sovereign cloud**, **biometric digital ID**, interoperability and "zero-paper" services. Senegal ranked
  **135th/193 on the UN EGDI (score 0.5162, 2024)**. See [[finance.new]], [[gov.policy]], [[infra.store]],
  [[SEN]]. Source: [[2025-12-09 Senegal allocates 60% of 2026 digital ministry budget to investment]].
- **Onshore-cloud thread.** The sovereignty pillar is served by the **[[google-senegal-sovereign-cloud|Google
  sovereign-cloud MoU (Sep 2024)]]** and the **National Interoperability Platform** (JICA-supported, launched
  2025-04-22) for cross-ministry data exchange. See [[infra.store]], [[dpi.exchange]].
- **Digital-rights critique.** Paradigm Initiative argued (2025-03) the strategy **omits explicit digital-
  rights provisions** — flagging risks around online expression (2021 shutdowns precedent), an outdated 2008
  data-protection law, the rural digital divide, and cybersecurity. Attributed analysis, not fact. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[colonialism]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-27 Senegal's Technological New Deal – A Digital Ambition Tested by Digital Rights]].

- **Critical-infrastructure cybersecurity cooperation (2026-07-13).** At a consultation led by the
  Minister of Telecommunications and Digital Affairs, Senegal launched stronger **public–private
  cooperation** to secure critical digital infrastructure and to **prepare a future CI-protection legal
  framework** — strengthening prevention, detection and response. Prompted by attacks on the **Public
  Treasury (May 2026)**, the **tax authority (Oct 2025)** and the **national ID-card agency (Feb 2026)**.
  (ITU GCI 2024: **67.17/100**, tier 3 of 4.) See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]], [[SEN]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-15 Senegal pushes stronger cybersecurity cooperation to protect critical infrastructure]].
- **Flagship projects launched at CICAD (2026-03-24).** One year into implementation, Senegal
  launched key projects under the New Deal Technologique at a high-level event at CICAD. Headline
  components: a Universal Connectivity Project combining fibre and satellite to reach remote
  areas, schools and health centres, targeting free connectivity for more than 1 million people
  in 2026; a National Interoperability Platform for secure real-time data exchange between
  ministries; and national data centres and cloud infrastructure to keep sensitive, strategic
  data onshore. The programme also advances digital public services, digital payments, digital
  identity, cybersecurity, e-signature and Startup Act support. Tags [[gov.policy]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[infra.store]], [[infra.cybersec]] in [[SEN]];
  lens [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-25 Senegal Accelerates Digital Sovereignty with Rollout of Flagship Technology Initiatives]].
