# United Arab Emirates (UAE)

Gulf state; one of Africa's largest investors (>$118bn 2020–2024) and — via
sovereign vehicles and **[[g42]]** — a rising force in African AI infrastructure,
increasingly positioned as an intermediary between US AI strategy and African
markets.

> **Taxonomy note:** UAE/Gulf geopolitics is tracked under the **`geopol.gulf`** slug
> (adopted 2026-07-10; concept page [[geopol.gulf]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **$1bn AI-for-development initiative (2025-11-22, G20 Johannesburg).** UAE
  Minister of State Saeed bin Mubarak Al Hajeri announced a **$1bn** initiative
  ([[uae-ai-for-development-initiative]]) for AI infrastructure/services across
  Africa (education, health, climate). See [[XAF]], [[tech.ai]], [[finance.new]].
  Source: [[2025-11-22 UAE announces $1 billion initiative to expand AI in Africa]].
- **Kenya deepening (2026-06, GITEX Kenya).** Framed as "managed interdependence"
  — UAE–Kenya AI ties, the **[[servernah-cloud]]** sovereign-cloud launch, and a
  proposed UAE–Kenya–East Africa digital corridor. UAE partnerships span >50
  African countries. See [[KEN]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-18 UAE-Kenya Tech Partnership Gains Momentum as AI Investments Grow]].
- **US–Gulf–Africa triangle.** [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]] casts the UAE (a
  **[[pax-silica]]** signatory) as the bridge extending US AI architecture into
  Africa; **[[g42]]**/Microsoft in Kenya is the working example. See [[geopol.usa]].
- **G42 "Intelligence Grid" / India deal (2026-05).** **[[g42]]** (backed by Abu Dhabi's
  Mubadala) signed a **15 May 2026** deal to deploy an AI supercomputer of **64 Cerebras
  systems** in India — the first customer of its build-own-operate **"Intelligence Grid"** of
  AI facilities for governments, pitched as a non-US alternative to Amazon/Microsoft/Google
  cloud. A reference case for the Gulf sovereign-compute model the UAE is extending into
  Africa. See [[tech.ai]], [[infra.store]], [[geopol.gulf]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-01 India's AI deal with the UAE challenges U.S. cloud dominance]].
- **Zimbabwe 1.5m coding programme (2026-04).** The UAE is delivering a flagship
  **1.5 million coding programme** with Zimbabwe, equipping young Zimbabweans with
  AI, cybersecurity and software-development skills as part of Harare's
  AI-readiness push (alongside a free public Wi-Fi rollout). See [[ZWE]],
  [[capacity.training]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-07 Zimbabwe switches on free wifi to power AI future]].

- **BRICS membership / BRICS Pay (undated explainer).** Named among BRICS members (with South
  Africa, Egypt, Ethiopia) in an explainer on **[[brics-pay]]**, the proposed cross-border
  settlement system aimed at reducing dollar dependence. Weak-provenance advocacy source —
  noted lightly, not hardened. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-07-12 BRICS portal]].

- **UAE–Microsoft as a sovereignty reference point (Rebecca Mbaya, 2026-06-24).**
  Mbaya's critique of the Nadella/Sunak (Davos 2026) "sovereignty as portfolio
  risk-management" framing cites the **UAE–Microsoft** model as the kind of
  bilateral deal that, imported into Africa's fragmented 54-state landscape
  without a shared sovereignty definition, produces weakness rather than leverage.
  See [[geopol.gulf]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-24 Does AI Sovereignty Mean Controlling the Keys, or Owning the Building]].

See [[g42]], [[tech.ai]], [[XAF]], [[KEN]].
