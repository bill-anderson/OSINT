# Cyber Security Act No. 3 / Cyber Crimes Act No. 4 of 2025 (Zambia)

Zambia's 2025 cyber legislation. The **Cyber Crimes Act No. 4 of 2025** was **assented 8 April 2025** by
President Hichilema, **published in the Government Gazette on 15 April 2025**, and **commenced 12 May 2025**
(Commencement Order). The sister **Cyber Security Act No. 3** establishes the **[[zambia-cyber-security-agency|ZCSA]]**
and a critical-information-infrastructure regime (and, per the US Embassy alert, **requires ICT providers to
proactively intercept all electronic communications** to detect "critical information" — a Central Monitoring
Centre; *the text of Act No. 3 is not held — the interception mandate is not a provision of Act No. 4*).

## What the Act No. 4 text actually says (gazetted text now held, 2025-04-15)

The **gazetted text of Act No. 4** ([[2025-04-15-zambia-cyber-crimes-act-no-4-of-2025]]) makes the provisions
readable rather than press-summarised. It borrows core definitions ("interception", "private communication",
"critical information", "computer system", "law enforcement officer") wholesale from the **Cyber Security Act,
2025**, so the two Acts operate as one regime.

- **The private-communications-recording offence (s.10)** — the clause behind the 2026-07-18 enforcement
  declaration. It is an offence to **record a private conversation using a device without notifying the
  parties**, *whether or not the recorder is a party to it* (fine ≤200,000 penalty units or ≤2 years).
  Exceptions: unintentional recording; a law-enforcement officer reasonably suspecting a threat to life,
  serious violence, substantial property damage or an offence; or where reasonably necessary to protect a
  party's lawful interests. **Publishing/distributing** such recordings is reached via the harassment and
  cyber-extortion offences (ss.13, 22).
- **Investigatory / surveillance-adjacent powers (Part III).** **Search and seizure with a warrant** and
  extension of the search to any linked device (s.27); a **7-day expedited data-preservation notice**
  extendable only by judicial order (s.30); **compelled disclosure of traffic data** by a communications
  provider on a warrant (s.31); and **real-time collection/recording of traffic data** on an *ex-parte*
  judicial order (s.32). These are Act No. 4's actual investigatory reach — narrower and more court-gated
  than the blanket-interception reading attached to Act No. 3.
- **Content and speech offences.** Cyber extortion incl. threatening to publish a private sexual image
  (s.13); identity offences (s.14); a broad **harassment/false-information offence** — publishing data
  "false… that causes damage to the reputation of another" or subjects them to "ridicule, contempt, hatred
  or embarrassment" (s.22, ≤2 years); child online protection (ss.15–17); online trafficking (s.18);
  **cyber-terrorism** incl. using a computer to "incite ethnic divisions", carrying **life imprisonment**
  (s.24). Penalties run to 25 years / life for the gravest offences.

The Act is the axis of a live democratic-vs-security debate around the **13 August 2026 election** — see
[[zambia--infra-cybersec]] (US alert, LAZ challenge, opposition repeal pledge, ZCSA's protective framing).
*This gazetted text is the **2025-04-15 instrument baseline**; it underlies, and does not supersede, the
**2026-07-18** enforcement-commencement event.*
