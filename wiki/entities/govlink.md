# GovLink Private 4G

Zambia's **government-owned private 4G network**, launched **15 July 2026** at **Ndola Teaching
Hospital** and intended to connect the country's public institutions — health facilities, schools,
farming communities and other administrations — over a single secure, shared national rail. It is
implemented by the **e-Government Division of [[smart-zambia-institute|SMART Zambia Institute]]**
with **[[ihm-southern-africa]]**, its technology division **TuSo Business Dynamics**,
**Quadrupleplay**, and the **[[ministry-of-health-zambia|Ministry of Health]]**.

## What it is for

- **Health is the flagship sector.** **Care 2.0** (piloted at Ndola Teaching Hospital, Arthur
  Davison Children's Hospital and Kitwe Teaching Hospital) and **[[smartcare-pro|SmartCare Pro]]**
  — the national EHR, deployed in **2,000+ facilities (2026-07)** — migrate onto GovLink. MoH
  Permanent Secretary **Dr Kennedy Lishimpi** framed it as the infrastructure that finally lets
  Zambia's digital-health investments deliver: electronic health records, telemedicine and
  real-time clinical decision support at national scale.
- **Reported early effects at the pilot sites (2026-07).** **Dr Justo Banda** (Ndola Teaching
  Hospital) said clinicians can now pull patient records, radiology reports and diagnostics
  electronically; **Dr Charles Mwinuna**, Copperbelt Provincial Health Director, said dependable
  connectivity should cut waiting times and strengthen referral and supply-chain systems. *These
  are operator statements at launch, not an evaluation — no baseline or measured outcome is held.*
- **Copperbelt as the model.** SZI National Coordinator **Percy Chinyama** — speaking for
  Government Chief Technical Officer **Kasali Musenge** — cited the province as approaching
  **near-full public-sector connectivity (2026-07)**.

## Where it sits in the wider build

GovLink is the newest layer of a multi-year connectivity push by SZI: **Starlink kits in
underserved public facilities since 2024**; **all 116 local authorities connected to the
Government Wide Area Network (GWAN) in 2025**, plus a **[[zesco]]** partnership to extend digital
access to schools, health facilities and other administrations. Zambia ranked **130th of 193 on
the UN E-Government Development Index (2024)**, score **0.5424/1**, with an online-services
sub-index of **0.4858/1** — above the African average, below the global one.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: a **state-owned** 4G rail for public services is the clearest expression yet of
  Zambia's connectivity-sovereignty posture, of a piece with the Cabinet open-source directive and
  the home-grown [[mosip]]-based ID. The counterpoint sits in the same programme: for the genuine
  rural last mile the same agency installs **[[starlink]]**, a foreign LEO service. Ownership of
  the core, dependency at the edge.
