The **Abia State Government** is the sub-national administration of Abia State in
south-eastern Nigeria, led as of 2026 by Governor **Dr Alex Chioma Otti**. It
matters to this wiki as an example of a Nigerian *state-level* (sub-national)
push into digital public infrastructure and data-driven governance — a layer
often overlooked in a DPI story dominated by federal agencies such as [[nimc]],
[[nitda]] and the [[cbn]].

## Unified data architecture push (Feb 2026)

On 19 February 2026 (a Thursday, reported by The Whistler) Governor Otti —
represented by Deputy Governor Engr. Ikechukwu Emetu — opened a one-day workshop
on **Strategic Digital Public Infrastructure and Delivery** for public-service
stakeholders at the Michael Okpara Auditorium in Umuahia. The workshop theme was
**"One Data, One Identity, One Government"**, with a sub-theme framed by
Commissioner for Budget and Planning Kingsley Anosike as *"One Citizen, One
Identity: Unlocking Data-Driven Governance."*

Otti called for a **unified data architecture** to improve performance tracking,
resource allocation and public-service delivery across the state, arguing that
"governance without accurate and reliable data is mere guesswork" and that a
unified system strengthens social protection, improves internally generated
revenue (IGR), and allocates resources more efficiently than agencies "working
in isolation." He described the effort as anchored on a **reliable identity
system** — echoing at state level the NIN-anchored integration debate playing
out nationally (see [[national-digital-farmers-registry-nigeria]] for the
federal analogue of the same silo problem).

Secretary to the State Government Dr Emmanuel Meribeole reinforced the case,
noting that "assumptions and fragmented records are unreliable for effective
planning" and calling for "an integrated data architecture anchored on a
reliable identity system."

## Institutional actors

Otti credited the state **Ministry of Budget and Planning**, the **Office of the
Chief Information Officer**, the **Office of the Chief Economic Adviser**,
development partners, and relevant federal agencies for supporting the
initiative. As of the February 2026 workshop this remained a stated policy
direction and stakeholder-consultation exercise rather than a launched system or
funded procurement.

## Significance

Abia's framing — one data layer, one identity, one government — mirrors the
national ambition to harmonise siloed registries. It is a data point on how
Nigerian states are independently articulating DPI strategy, and a candidate for
an intersection page (`nigeria--digital-localgov` or `abia--dpi-registry`) if
the state's programme develops substantively.
