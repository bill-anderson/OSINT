# ANID (Agence Nationale d'Identification, Togo)

**Togo's national identification agency, and the implementing body for the
[[e-id-togo]] biometric identification project.** Created by the **foundational unique
identification law of September 2020** and sitting under the **Ministry of Digital
Economy and Digital Transformation** ([[cina-lawson]]), ANID runs the MOSIP-based
([[mosip]]) enrolment that issues every resident a **Numéro d'Identification Unique
(NIU)**. Its enrolment work is financed by the [[world-bank]] under the WURI-Togo
project ([[wuri-programme]]). See [[TGO]], [[dpi.id]].

**Name renderings — read with care.** The French *Agence Nationale d'Identification*
resolves to "National Identification **Agency**", the form used by Togo First
(2025-11-19) and Ecofin Agency (2025-10-30). Biometric Update renders it "National
Identification **Authority**" (2025-11-03), and elsewhere in this wiki a WURI item
renders Togo's body as the "National Agency for the Identification of Persons (NAIP)".
**Agency** is used here as canonical; the others are variant renderings of the same body.

**A wire-copy error not propagated.** The Biometric Update piece of **2024-03-06**
describes "the Ministry of Digital Economy and Digital Transformation (ANID)" and
"the Ministry of Planning, Development, and Cooperation (INSEED)" — rendering both
agencies as *ministries*. ANID and [[inseed-togo]] are agencies **under** those
ministries respectively, not the ministries themselves. Institutional structure here
follows the Togo First account of 2025-11-19.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Maritime region enrolment (from 2025-11-22).** ANID announced the expansion of the
  national biometric enrolment drive into the **Maritime region**, opening in **Zone 1**
  (prefectures of **Vo, Yoto, Bas-Mono and Lacs**) from **Saturday 22 November 2025**,
  following earlier rollouts in **Golfe, Agoè-Nyivé and Savanes**. [[inseed-togo]] manages
  field-personnel deployment. **No cumulative enrolment count is reported** in this or any
  other 2025 source held here — see [[e-id-togo]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2025-11-19 Togo Advances Digital ID Program With New Enrollment Phase in Maritime Region]].
- **Senegal benchmarking mission (reported 2025-10-27/30).** A delegation from Senegal's
  Ministry of Communication and Digital Economy visited Togo to study the e-ID model.
  **Yasmine Mama**, Datacenter Network Specialist at ANID: "We showed the technical
  architectures, infrastructures, and logic deployed within the context of the e-ID
  project. We discussed the technical solution, MOSIP, all its components, and how the
  country integrated it into Togo." Dakar frames the peer learning as accelerating its own
  e-ID under the **New Deal Technologique**. Both states are WURI participants. See
  [[SEN]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[sovereignty]]. Sources:
  [[2025-10-30 Senegal Benchmarks Togo's e-ID System for Digital Transformation]],
  [[2025-11-03 Senegal taps inspiration from Togo's digital ID experience]].
- **Statutory origin (WURI ISR, 2023-12-21).** The World Bank's implementation report
  records ANID's creation under the **September 2020** foundational unique identification
  law, alongside the wider e-ID legal scaffolding: a **data protection law (September
  2019)**, **cybersecurity/cybercrime amendments (June 2022)** creating **ANCy**, the
  creation of the **[[ipdcp-togo]]** data protection authority, and a **February 2022**
  decree establishing project governance bodies. See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2023-12-21 ISR - WURI Phase 2 Seq 7 (P169594)]].
- **Test registration (March 2024) — historical baseline.** ANID and [[inseed-togo]] were
  reported managing WURI test registrations of the biometric digital ID, planned **5–15
  March 2024** after sites and supervisors were selected; ANID had originally planned the
  test for **Q4 2023**. This is a 2024 baseline and does not out-rank the November-2025
  rollout state above. Source:
  [[2024-03-06 1 step forward, 3 steps back for West Africa's regional digital ID projects]].
