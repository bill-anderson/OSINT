**Global Gateway** is the [[european-union]]'s flagship international
investment strategy — the vehicle through which the EU and its member states
(under the **Team Europe** approach, alongside development banks such as the
[[ebrd]] and the European Investment Bank) mobilise finance for infrastructure in
partner countries, including digital connectivity, data infrastructure, health
and agricultural value chains. In this wiki it is the branding under which EU
digital-infrastructure money flows into Africa, and it is explicitly framed as
Europe's geopolitical answer to infrastructure competition on the continent
(`geopol.eu`).

## In Nigeria

Global Gateway is the named framework for the **[[eu-nigeria-global-gateway-290m]]**
package: **€290m** across seven operations announced at the 8th EU–Nigeria
Ministerial Dialogue in Abuja (24 March 2026). Its digital component (€131m)
co-finances — with the [[ebrd]] — a **90,000 km fibre roll-out**, **secure and
interoperable digital public infrastructure and data-exchange systems**, and
workforce training under Nigeria's 3 Million Technical Talent (3MTT) programme.
Commissioner **Jozef Síkela** characterised Global Gateway investments as creating
"new quality infrastructures, sustainable jobs and long-term economic
opportunities" for Nigerians while also opening "new opportunities for Europe" —
capturing the strategy's dual development-and-strategic-interest framing.

## How it operates

- **Team Europe delivery.** Global Gateway is implemented through pooled EU +
  member-state + development-bank finance. Member-state programmes such as the
  Finland-led Team Europe Initiative on Digital Public Services (behind the
  [[nigeria-finland-digital-mou]]) sit under this umbrella.
- **Blended finance.** Grants are combined with development-bank lending — e.g.
  the EIB credit line via the Bank of Industry in the Nigeria agriculture tranche.

## Significance

Global Gateway is the recurring instrument to watch when the EU appears in
African digital/data infrastructure stories. It converges EU money on the same
priorities other funders back (the 90,000 km national fibre backbone also
supported by the World Bank's Project BRIDGE — see
[[national-digital-farmers-registry-nigeria]]), making it a key node in the
external-financing picture for African DPI.
