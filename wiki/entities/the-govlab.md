# The GovLab

Research centre (NYU) on data, technology and governance; home of **[[stefaan-verhulst]]**
and the "digital self-determination" agenda.

## Activity in this wiki

- **"Triad of Tech Sovereignty" (2026-06).** Verhulst & Begoña Gonzalez Otero assess the
  EU Technological Sovereignty Package across **dependency / openness / agency**, arguing
  that sovereignty-as-ownership misses the point — what matters is *agency*: who can
  shape, govern and challenge systems. Proposes a "stewardship architecture." See
  [[gov.standards]], [[geopol.eu]], [[gov.legislate]].

- **Data for Policy network (2023 profile).** GovLab's [[stefaan-verhulst]] sits on the board of
  **[[data-for-policy]]** (the UK CIC and its Cambridge-published *Data & Policy* journal). See
  [[gov.policy]], [[data.open]]. Source: [[2023-09-04 Data for Policy - About Us]].
- **Governance landscape review (Data & Policy, 2025).** The *Data & Policy* journal published a
  named-authorship **[[digital-data-driven-transformations-landscape-review-2025|landscape
  review]]** conceptualising digital & data-driven transformations in governance (participation/
  collective intelligence; relationships & organisations; open data) — reference-shelf
  scholarship, not Africa-specific. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 Digital & data-driven transformations in governance a landscape review  Data & Policy]].

## Relevance

The agency-over-ownership thesis converges with **[[david-eaves]]**' commodification
argument — two independent 2025–26 sources reframing digital sovereignty away from
ownership, directly applicable to how African states read their own sovereign-stack
choices.
