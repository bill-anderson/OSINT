# Civil Registry Department (Zimbabwe)

**The department that registers births and issues national identity documents in
Zimbabwe**, under the [[ministry-of-home-affairs-zimbabwe]]. Its mobile registration
exercises are the one part of Zimbabwe's ID programme the wiki holds as *delivered*
rather than announced.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Binga mobile registration, 7–11 July 2025.** The Department ran a mobile birth-
  registration and national-ID exercise in **Binga district**, in partnership with
  **[[unicef]]** and the **Embassy of Sweden**, reaching villages such as Siamusanga
  approximately **160 km from Binga Centre**, near the Zambian border. An accomplished
  exercise, not an announcement. See [[digital.rural]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-10 Zimbabwe Launches Mobile ID Registration Program to Serve Remote Binga Communities]].
- **The barriers it addresses.** Long travel distances with **journey times up to seven
  hours**, poor road infrastructure, **risk from wildlife**, and limited connectivity to
  government services — a concrete account of what rural registration exclusion costs in
  Zimbabwe. See [[include.divides]]. Source: as above.
- **Birth registration coverage: 57% (ZDHS 2023–24).** The figure comes from the
  **Zimbabwe Demographic and Health Survey 2023–24** — **date it to the survey, not to the
  2025 article that repeats it**. See [[data.statistics]]. Source: as above.
- **Part of a continuing programme (2025-07-15).** Biometric Update records that mobile
  registration is being extended to several remote areas with UNICEF and Swedish Embassy
  support, framed as the government's route to driving ID adoption ahead of digital ID.
  Source:
  [[2025-07-15 Zimbabwe looks forward to digital ID rollout after population registry reform]].

## Provenance note

The Mobile ID World piece quotes **"Bongani Ndlovu, reporting from Binga"** as though a
subject; **he is a state-media journalist, not an official** — that quote is not a
government or UNICEF statement and should not be presented as one. The exercise is also
framed by the source within "the Second Republic's policy reforms" — political framing,
carried by the source, not a finding.
