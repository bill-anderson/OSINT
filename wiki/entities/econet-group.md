# Econet Group

The diversified, African-owned telecoms and technology group founded by **[[strive-masiyiwa]]** —
the founder vehicle (Econet Global / Econet Group) from which the **[[cassava-technologies]]**
digital-infrastructure group later emerged. Distinct from the Zimbabwean operating company
**[[econet-wireless-zimbabwe]]** (the bare name "Econet Wireless" is disambiguated on
[[econet-wireless]]; ISSUE-009). In this wiki the Group matters as (a) the origin of Africa's
mobile-money story via **[[ecocash]]**, and (b) the corporate lineage behind Cassava / Liquid.

## Profile

- **Origins as a group.** Grew out of the Zimbabwean operator Masiyiwa launched in 1998 (see
  [[econet-wireless-zimbabwe]]); **HQ moved to South Africa in 2000**. Described across sources
  (2014) as operating in **15–18 markets** including Burundi, Lesotho, Kenya, Nigeria, Botswana,
  Rwanda (plus UK/New Zealand) — *figures as of 2014; treat as dated.*
- **Businesses.** Mobile telephony; mobile money / fintech ([[ecocash]]); insurance; renewable
  energy (incl. solar phone-charging kiosks); e-commerce; education.
- **Group structure.** Econet Global / Econet Group is the founder vehicle; the infrastructure arm
  is **[[cassava-technologies]]** → **[[liquid-c2]]** (connectivity / cloud / cyber) and
  **[[africa-data-centres]]**.
- **Parentage of the Zimbabwean opco — established 2026-07-20.** **Econet Global Limited holds
  1,247,179,784 shares in [[econet-wireless-zimbabwe]], or 41.68%** of a 2,992,163,203-share
  register — the largest single holding by a wide margin (next: Stanbic Nominees 11.61%). EWZL's
  own circular states that Econet Global Limited **"will not participate in the Exit Offer and
  will remain the controlling shareholder of the Company for the foreseeable future"** (as of
  2026-02-04). Econet Global Limited, **Econet Life (Private) Limited** and **Econet Insurance
  (Private) Limited** are the related parties excluded from voting on the delisting resolution
  under **paragraph 11.5 of the ZSE Listings Requirements** — a related-party exclusion that is
  itself evidence of the control relationship. Source:
  [[2026-02-04-econet-wireless-zimbabwe-circular-and-econet-infraco-pre-listing-statement]].

  **Note the shape of it: control on a minority economic stake.** 41.68% is not a majority, and
  the company characterises it as control rather than the shareholding compelling that reading.
  The wiki's earlier formulation — "shares the same founder lineage, no shareholding document on
  file" — is **retired**; it rested on three EWZL filings in which the parent genuinely did not
  appear. The circular, the document where a parent *would* appear, names it. The circular also
  uses the phrase **"other Econet Group's SPVs"** of its own accord (PLS Part D, para. 4.1.1),
  so "Econet Group" is the company's own usage, not only the wiki's.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Liquid acquired an Equiano fibre pair (~2022).** The Group's infrastructure arm [[liquid-c2]]
  (Liquid Intelligent Technologies) acquired a **fibre pair on [[google]]'s Equiano** subsea cable
  (up to **12 Tbps**), adding to its **>100,000km** African fibre and plans to interconnect
  Equiano's landings east–west — announced days after Equiano's first African landing (Togo). See
  [[infra.connect]], [[equiano-cable]]. Source:
  [[2022-03-01 Liquid Intelligent acquires Google's fiber pair to improve offers]].
- **Google Cloud Partner Experience Centre (2026).** Liquid C2 launched Africa's first Google Cloud
  Partner Experience Centre (Johannesburg), exporting Zimbabwean-owned digital infrastructure across
  Africa. See [[zimbabwe--tech-ai]]. Source: [[2026-06-30-masiyiwa-manyika-google-1b-africa-cloud]].
- **"Second division" operator (Russell Southwood, 2025-12-04).** Southwood's MNO state-of-market
  places Econet in the "second division" of operators (with Africell, Axian, Globacom, Telecel) —
  below the four pan-African "power players" — even as its sister infrastructure arm
  [[cassava-technologies]] operates at the continental frontier. See [[tech.industry]],
  [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].
