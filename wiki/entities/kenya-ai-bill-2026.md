# Kenya Artificial Intelligence Bill 2026

A proposed Kenyan statute (before the Senate as of mid-2026) to establish a national AI
governance regime. Introduced by a nominated senator on the ruling-party list. In this wiki it is
a recurring reference instrument, analysed principally through [[alice-munyua]]'s critique.

## Key facts (as of 2026-07-12)

- **Self-declared EU AI Act transplant**: the Memorandum of Objects (p.73) states the bill was
  designed to align with global standards including the [[eu-ai-act]]. Imports the risk
  classification framework, conformity audits, regulatory sandboxes and public high-risk register.
- Centres a **presidentially-appointed AI Commissioner** (five-year renewable term, sole authority
  to classify systems as unacceptable-risk) — critiqued as a patronage position with no
  independent accountability.
- **Maximum fine KES 5m (~$40,000)**, non-scaling — contrasted with the EU AI Act's 3%/7%
  of-global-turnover penalties; **no extraterritorial reach**, no pre-deployment impact
  assessment, no community/class standing, no linguistic provisions.
- One genuine merit noted: a **right to explanation and human review** of automated decisions
  (undercut, per Munyua, by capture of the enforcement mechanism).

## Critique in this wiki

Munyua ([[2026-05-19 GATEKEEPERS XIV]]) reads the bill as "the EU AI Act with a Kenyan patronage
chassis" — regulating a technology stack Kenya did not build, while the
Social Health Authority (SHA) algorithm operates as a live "proof of concept" already extracting
from the poorest Kenyans. Situated inside the Gates/Kigali "philanthropic industrial complex"
(see [[africa-ai-declaration]], [[gates-foundation]]).
