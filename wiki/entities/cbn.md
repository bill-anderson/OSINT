# Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN)

Nigeria's central bank and prudential regulator of its banks, fintechs and payment
companies — here as the author of a data-localization directive reshaping the country's
cloud and data-centre market.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Data-localization directive (circular 2026-06-15; deadline 2027-01-01).** All
  financial institutions must move transactional data off overseas hyperscaler clouds
  (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud, IBM) to **data centres inside Nigeria** by 1 Jan 2027 — one
  of Nigeria's largest-ever cloud migrations, set against digital-payment volumes of
  **₦1.07 quadrillion (~$702bn) in 2024** (NIBSS, +80% YoY). Operators read it as a
  demand signal that could pull hyperscalers to build **local cloud regions** rather than
  force banks off familiar platforms. See [[gov.protect]], [[infra.store]],
  [[nigeria--gov-protect]]. A second analysis (TechBooky, 2026-07-10) corroborates the
  directive (issued June 2026; ~6-month window to 1 Jan 2027) and warns domestication ≠
  protection — flagging tension with the **[[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]]**'s
  cross-border-transfer rules and urging encryption, key-control and hybrid hosting over
  forced relocation. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 CBN's Data Localisation Directive Puts Nigerian Fintechs In A Cloud Dilemma]].
- The primary circular (signed Dir. Rakiya Yusuf) is reproduced in a parked lead
  (`_leads/`) and is still to be obtained directly.
- **Four-pillar deep-read of the June-2026 circular (analysis, 2026-07).** Anjola Adebowale's
  two-part *Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure* reads the circular
  (**[[cbn-payments-circular-2026]]**, PSS/DIR/PUB/CIR/001/004) as four pillars — market-structure
  caps (card-issuing >25% share caps merchant-acquiring at ≤15%), **data localisation** (full
  compliance by **1 Jan 2027**), UBO disclosure, and monthly market-share returns — judging the
  strategy sound but the deadline ahead of Nigeria's data-centre and grid capacity (~75MW live
  across ~26 facilities; ~6GW grid for 230m people vs South Africa's ~48GW; 1.5MW facility ≈
  90,000L diesel). Part II argues the rule should have been *sequenced* against infrastructure
  milestones, contrasting India's 2018–19 RBI localisation rollback with Rwanda's phased,
  sandbox-tested DPI. Analyst framing, attributed to Adebowale. See [[dpi.pay]], [[infra.store]].
  Sources: [[2026-07-01 Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part I Inside the CBN's June 2026 Payments Circular]],
  [[2026-06-19 Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part II The Opportunity and the Case for Sequencing]].
- **Data silos (2026-06).** The CBN's **BVN** is named among eight siloed, non-interoperable Nigerian government databases that experts say hamper the country's AI ambitions. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[NGA]]. Source: [[2026-06-17 Government data silos threaten Nigeria's AI ambitions]].
- **Stablecoins vs monetary sovereignty (2026-04).** As dollar stablecoins (USDT) surged — ~$92bn/yr in Nigerian digital-asset flows and 95% of crypto-active users preferring stablecoins over the naira, against a >60% naira devaluation since 2023 — the CBN convened a **15-member stablecoin task force** (Oct 2025, "to understand, not stop" them) and, with the SEC, launched **cNGN**, a regulated naira-backed stablecoin, to defend monetary sovereignty against the erosion SARB Gov. Kganyago warned of amid **$205bn** of SSA digital-rail flows (Jul 2024–Jun 2025, +52%). See [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-04-23 Africa's $205 Billion Digital Shift Is Redefining Money, and Central Banks Are Losing Control]].
- **PAPSS–Onafriq Naira payment pilot approved (2026).** The CBN approved an
  [[onafriq]]–[[papss]] pilot for wallet-based outbound payments from Nigeria to Ghana,
  settled **fully in Naira** with no hard-currency conversion — a six-month pilot from
  1 December aimed at SMEs and cross-border traders, supporting [[afcfta]]
  operationalisation. See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[XWA]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-04 PAPSS, Onafriq launch new trade rail for West Africa]].
- **Fintech Policy Insight Series 2025 (regional fintech roadmap).** The CBN's
  [[cbn-policy-insight-series-2025]] ("Shaping the Future of Fintech in Nigeria") sets an
  18-month, three-pillar roadmap — a Single Regulatory Window, a Responsible AI in Finance
  Hub, and **regulatory passporting** pilots with Ghana, Kenya, Senegal and South Africa for
  mutual recognition of fintech licences — read across the region as a test of
  [[ecowas]]/[[afcfta]] financial-services integration (87.5% of NG fintechs say compliance
  costs constrain innovation). See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.pay]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-06 Nigeria just raised the bar for West African Fintech]],
  [[2026-02-05 Nigeria's fintech blueprint raises stakes for Kenya, Africa's digital economy]]
  (Kenya-angle reporting: bilateral licence-recognition pilots with Kenya/Ghana/Senegal/SA).
- **Onshoring mandate paired with the FCCPC probe (2026-07).** Reporting frames the CBN local-hosting mandate as an active "test" of Nigeria's data sovereignty, twinned with the FG's directive to the [[fccpc]] to investigate Meta/Alphabet/X and GenAI platforms over anti-competitive practices — a joint assertion of data and economic sovereignty. Nigeria's ~26 data-centre facilities (18 commercial, 50–56 MW live of 124 MW installed) are cast as Africa's second-largest market, ready to absorb the bank migration. Reconfirms **₦1.07 quadrillion (~$702bn), 11.2bn e-transactions in 2024**. See [[infra.store]], [[nigeria--gov-protect]]. Source: [[2026-07-10 Nigeria tests data sovereignty as foreign tech firms facing probe]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: data repatriation, law-enforcement access, keeping cloud spend onshore
  ("national resilience and sovereignty").
- `colonialism`: the dependence being unwound is on foreign hyperscalers and foreign
  jurisdictions.
