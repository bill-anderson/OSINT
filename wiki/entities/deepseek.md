# DeepSeek

Chinese AI company whose open-weight models have become the leading edge of Chinese
open-source AI adoption in the Global South, and a low-cost counter-pole to US
hyperscalers in the African AI market. Its models are open-weight — code and trained
parameters publicly downloadable — and priced well below the dominant closed US models
(ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini), which drives their appeal in cost-sensitive markets.

## Activity in this wiki

- **R1 release triggers a Chinese open-weight wave (Jan 2025).** The release of DeepSeek
  R1 in January 2025 was followed by a surge of Chinese open-weight models (Qwen, Kimi,
  Minimax) entering the market. By one OpenRouter measure, Chinese open-weight models grew
  from **1.2% of global AI usage in late 2024 to nearly 30% by late 2025** — described by
  US analysts as a competitiveness threat "particularly in the Global South" on price and
  architectural efficiency. Framed against the US AI-export push ([[tech-corps]],
  [[american-ai-exports-program]]). See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-02 The U.S. Launches a Peace Corps for AI to Compete With China]].
- **African chatbot share (as of Oct 2025 reporting).** Bloomberg-cited data put DeepSeek
  at **11–14% of chatbot usage across Africa**, rising to **~20% in Ethiopia ([[ETH]]) and
  Zimbabwe ([[ZWE]])**, with growing influence in Nigeria. Its traction rests on
  affordability, low-cost handsets, and China's Belt-and-Road infrastructure footprint on
  the continent. This positions DeepSeek as a Chinese alternative to the US firms
  ([[microsoft]], [[openai]], [[anthropic]], [[google]]) expanding across African markets.
  See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12 China's DeepSeek gives US tech giants a run for their money as Microsoft and others expand AI push in Africa]].

- **US export-program framing (Politico, 2026-07).** DeepSeek (with Alibaba) is the Chinese
  open-weight benchmark against which Washington's [[american-ai-exports-program]] is framed;
  the program's underwhelming 78-application launch is read partly against these models' gains
  in developing countries. See [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-11 'Not where they hoped it'd be' Launch of Trump AI promotion program underwhelms]].
