# Africa Data Centres

Africa Data Centres (ADC) is a [[cassava-technologies]] subsidiary and a leading continental colocation operator. It matters as the physical estate hosting Cassava's sovereign-cloud and AI-factory compute.

## Profile
- Leading continental colocation operator across twelve renewable-powered halls.
- Cape Town: a 6 MW expansion at CPT1 went live in July 2024, adding 1,000 racks; CPT2 (20 MW IT load) is under build.
- Backed by a $300m facility from the [[us-dfc]].
- Hosts the first [[cassava-ai-factories]] GPU cluster in Johannesburg — 3,000 Nvidia H100 GPUs, from mid-2025.
- **Nigeria (Lagos):** operates a **20.65 MW Tier III facility in Eko Atlantic City**, with 9,800 m² of white space across 10 data halls — ADC's West-African anchor in a Lagos market dominated by diesel-dependent economics (as of 2026-02). Source: [[2026-02-26 Lagos Is Getting a $250 Million Hyperscale Data Center. Here's Why Nigeria Still Can't Power the Cloud]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **The 20-year wheeled-solar PPA — counterparty corrected (announced 2023-03-14).** ADC signed a
  **20-year PPA for 12 MW** of wheeled solar with **DPA Southern Africa (Pty) Ltd**, a **50:50 joint
  venture between [[distributed-power-africa]] and the French utility [[edf]]** — *not* with DPA
  itself, as later trade-press accounts have it. Power comes partly from a solar farm near
  **Bloemfontein**; ADC said it would supply "over 30% of our South African data centres" with
  renewables. Prefer the announcer's own account. See [[infra.energy]], [[ZAF]]. Source:
  [[2023-03-14-cassava-adc-dpa-sa-edf-20-year-ppa]].
- **Hybrid power as grids falter (2026-04, GITEX Africa).** ADC is named among operators
  (with Liquid Telecom and Teraco) building **hybrid energy** stacks — grid plus solar,
  battery storage and diesel — as executives argue "a grid-first approach cannot be
  Africa's strategy." Reflects ADC's renewable-powered estate model amid unreliable
  grids. See [[infra.energy]], [[liquid-c2]], [[NGA]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-14 Africa's data centres rethink power as grids falter]].
- **10× capacity call (2026-04, 58th UNECA Conference of Ministers).** ADC CEO **Adil el
  Youssefi** urged Africa to **10× its data-centre capacity** and build local "AI
  factories" — supported by reliable, affordable energy — to host and process African data
  on the continent, which generates **<1% of global data** despite ~20% of world
  population. See [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]], [[uneca]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].
