# Ministry of ICT, Postal and Courier Services (Zimbabwe)

Zimbabwe's ICT ministry (Minister **Tatenda Annastacia Mavetera**) — parent of **[[potraz]]**, and driver
of the data-protection licensing regime, the National Cybersecurity Strategy, free public Wi-Fi and the
**[[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]]**. See [[zimbabwe--gov-protect]], [[ZWE]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **MSME digital roadmap set out (2026-07-17).** Mavetera used the **inaugural National SMEs and
  Cooperatives Meeting** — themed *"Towards Vision 2030: Building an Enabling MSMEs and Cooperatives
  Ecosystem"*, hosted by the Minister of Women Affairs, Community, SME Development (Sen. Monica
  Mutsvangwa) with Local Government and Industry & Commerce — to present the ministry's digital agenda as
  an MSME growth instrument. She put the MSME sector at **more than 60% of GDP and ~70% of the national
  workforce (2026-07)** and described digital transformation as *"a powerful equaliser"* letting small
  rural firms reach markets, finance and knowledge on the same footing as large ones. Flagship items
  cited: **17,000+ km fibre backbone**, continued **5G rollout**, **[[starlink]]** licensing for
  underserved areas, the **[[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]]** (launched **March 2026**) and its **AI
  Grand Challenge**, a digital-skills programme **targeting 1.5 million Zimbabweans** in coding, AI and
  data skills, and **digital centres** offering free internet, ICT equipment and literacy training.
  She also claimed **internet penetration "has now reached 87%"** — see the flag below. Full connectivity
  detail: **[[zimbabwe--infra-connect]]**. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-zimbabwe-digital-roadmap-msme-growth]].

- **At the AI Strategy launch (2026-03-13).** Mavetera introduced President Mnangagwa at the launch of the
  **[[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]]** at the New Parliament Building, Mount Hampden, framing him as "the
  architect of **Vision 2030**, which aims to transform Zimbabwe into a full digital economy by the year
  2030". She credited his guidance with taking internet penetration to **84.55% in Q4 2025**, "on course to
  reach 100% before the year 2030", cited a fibre backbone past **14,000 km (2026-03)**, and said "we are
  committed to ensuring that our citizens have access to affordable and reliable internet connectivity".
  Ministry programmes named: the **Digital Skills Ambassadors** programme, a **cyber-security training
  programme**, and a planned **National AI Innovation Fund** for AI start-ups, pursued with telcos. See
  [[capacity.training]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-13-mavetera-84-55-internet-penetration-ai-strategy]].

**Resolved (2026-07-20).** The ministerial **87% internet-penetration** claim (2026-07-17) and the
**38.4% / 61.6% offline** the wiki holds for end-2025 measure different things. The 87% is **POTRAZ's own
series — active internet/data subscriptions ÷ population** (84.55% at Q4 2025, which the minister quoted
to two decimals on 2026-03-13); DataReportal's 38.4% counts **actual users** (6.54m, Oct 2025). The
minister's round numbers consistently lag POTRAZ — her "more than 17,000 km" of fibre (2026-07-17) against
the regulator's 19,000+ km for Q1 2026 is the same pattern, true but stale rather than wrong (she had
cited **"surpassed the 14,000 km mark"** four months earlier, on 2026-03-13). The **2026-03-13 source is
the bridge**: quoting **84.55% for Q4 2025 to two decimals** is a POTRAZ quarterly figure, not a rounded
usage estimate, which is what identifies the series behind July's "87%". Sources:
[[2026-03-13-mavetera-84-55-internet-penetration-ai-strategy]],
[[2026-07-17-zimbabwe-digital-roadmap-msme-growth]].
