# Ministry of Communication Technologies (Tunisia)

**The lead ministry for Tunisia's digital transformation — owner of the
[[digital-tunisia-2020-2025]] national strategic plan, driver of the national interoperability
platform [[uxp-tunisia]], and the department under which the national digital identity
[[e-houwiya]] is operated.** Also referred to in sources as the Ministry of ICT.

**Officeholders on the record here:** **[[nizar-ben-neji]]** (2021–2024); **Sofiene Hemissi**
(as of 2025-03).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Leads the national interoperability platform (as of 2024-02).** The implementation effort for
  [[uxp-tunisia]] — [[cybernetica]]'s UXP deployed as a proof-of-concept for a cohort of **five
  ministries** — is led by the Ministry of ICT, framed as a significant step toward
  [[digital-tunisia-2020-2025]]. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2024-02-14 National interoperability platform in Tunisia (Cybernetica UXP)]].
- **e-Governance Academy MoU (2022).** The ministry signed a memorandum of understanding with the
  Estonian **[[e-governance-academy]]** to advance digital transformation and develop the eID
  landscape — the collaboration credited with making [[e-houwiya]] possible. Marit Lani serves as
  Tunisia's smart government program director. See [[finance.mou]], [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
- **On the biometric-ID steering committee (2024-04-22).** Sat on the steering committee — with the
  [[ministry-of-interior-tunisia]] and the Ministry of Economy and Planning — that decided practical
  measures to begin actual use of the biometric ID card and passport in **H1 2025** (a target; see
  [[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]]). Then-minister [[nizar-ben-neji]] attended. Source:
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia - Biometric ID card and passport usage to begin in H1 2025]].
- **Digital ID and AI agenda (as stated 2025-03).** Per Minister **Sofiene Hemissi**, Tunisia is
  evolving its digital ID and AI plans, focused on digitising government services, improving digital
  infrastructure and boosting AI in sectors such as health and education; an **AI strategy was
  scheduled for 2025**, to support open data and technology industries. Stated intention as of
  2025-03 — no source held confirms publication. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.policy]], [[data.open]].
  Source: [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
- **EBRD engagement (as of 2026-01).** The EBRD's up-to-**€190m** loan to [[tunisie-telecom]] is
  described as adding to the Bank's existing engagement with this ministry and the telecom regulator
  to promote e-services for businesses and citizens, sector competitiveness and innovation. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 EBRD and Tunisie Telecom Partner on €190 Million Digital Infrastructure Upgrade]].
- **Partner to the GIZ Digital Transformation Centre (as of 2025-03).** The GIZ-founded **Digital
  Transformation Centre Tunisia** works with this ministry and the Ministry of Industry. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].

- **192 projects under cabinet monitoring (Feb 2026).** At a ministerial council (13 Feb 2026, chaired by
  PM **Sarah Zafrani Al-Zanzari**), minister **Sofiene Hemissi** reviewed the digital-transformation
  portfolio: **192 initiatives under active follow-up** within the **2026–2030 Development Plan**'s unified
  national vision, with **2026 declared the launch of full-scale administration digitisation**. 2025
  completions cited: the **Unified Gateway for Administrative Services**, e-stamping, Digital Hospital
  phase 1, the national project-monitoring platform, remote ID-card services for students, the building-
  permits platform, and export/company-registration systems. PM directives: expand e-payments, interlink
  citizen services, embed cybersecurity, AI and open data. See [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-13-tunisie-conseil-ministeriel-digitalisation-complete]].
- **Tunisia Digital Summit, 10th edition (Apr 2026).** Opening the summit (22–23 Apr 2026, theme
  public-private-institution partnership for rapid innovation; ~2,000 experts, 80 exhibitors), Hemissi
  called digital transformation **"no longer a choice but a historical necessity,"** set the strategic goal
  of making Tunisia a **regional digital hub** radiating across the Maghreb, Africa and the Mediterranean,
  and pledged continued development of **legal/regulatory frameworks, especially on AI and data
  protection**. Tunisia was to enter the **15th Startup World Cup** (San Francisco, 4–6 Nov 2026) for the
  first time (3 startups selected). See [[tech.innovate]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-22-assabah-tunisia-digital-summit-10]].
