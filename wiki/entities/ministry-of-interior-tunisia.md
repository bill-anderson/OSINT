# Ministry of Interior (Tunisia)

**The originating and controlling department of Tunisia's biometric national ID project — the
ministry that first proposed mandatory chip-enabled biometric ID cards in August 2016, revived the
initiative in late 2023, and would hold the citizens' biometric data.** It is the counterparty in
the eight-year civil-society campaign led by [[access-now]] and the focal point of the objection
that a centralised biometric database would sit with the police ministry under an outdated data
protection law.

**Minister on the record here:** **Kamel Feki** (as of 2024-04).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Origin of the biometric ID proposal (August 2016).** The Biometric ID Bill first emerged when
  the MoI proposed replacing traditional identity cards with **mandatory chip-enabled biometric
  ones for all citizens aged 15 and above**. After setbacks, the ministry revived the initiative in
  **late 2023**: a fourth draft law secured approval at a ministerial council session on
  **30 November 2023** and advanced to Parliament's Committee on Rights and Freedoms on
  **25 December 2023**. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-07 Eight years in the making - Tunisia's Biometric ID and Passport Bills risk rights]].
- **The centralised-database amendment, dismissed (2024-03-05).** **One day before the vote**, MPs
  introduced an amendment to prohibit the creation of a centralised biometric database by the MoI —
  **it was dismissed**. Committee deliberations had **lacked public consultations**. The bills were
  adopted on **6 March 2024** by an overwhelming majority, becoming
  [[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]] and [[organic-law-2024-23-tunisia]]. Reported by [[access-now]],
  a first-party campaigning organisation — its account of the process, not a neutral record.
  See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-07 Eight years in the making - Tunisia's Biometric ID and Passport Bills risk rights]].
- **On the implementation steering committee (2024-04-22).** With the
  [[ministry-of-communication-technologies-tunisia]] and the Ministry of Economy and Planning, the
  MoI sat on the steering committee whose ministerial working session at the Government Palace in
  the Kasbah — chaired by PM Ahmed Hachani, with Interior Minister **Kamel Feki** attending —
  targeted the start of actual biometric ID and passport use in **H1 2025**. A target as of
  2024-04; no source held confirms it was met. Source:
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia - Biometric ID card and passport usage to begin in H1 2025]],
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia to Get Biometric IDs in H1 of 2025]].
- **Civil-society objection restated (2025-03, attributed).** Biometric Update relays [[access-now]]
  warning that the **proposed** biometric ID would compel citizens to give biometric data to the
  MoI, and that Tunisia's weak data protection regulations and history of cyberattacks make the
  prospective biometric database a security concern. Note the tense: as of **March 2025** the card
  is still described as proposed. See [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
