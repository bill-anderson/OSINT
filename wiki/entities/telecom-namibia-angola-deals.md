# Telecom Namibia–Angola connectivity agreements (2026)

Two agreements signed **2026-06-15** by **[[telecom-namibia]]** with Angolan
partners, moving an April 2025/26 MoU/CTA into operational implementation.

- **Satellite (PCA):** pilot commercial agreement with **GGPEN** (Angola's space
  programme office) to trial **Angosat-2** across Namibia and Southern Africa,
  transitioning from proof-of-concept to structured trial (target customers:
  lodges/tourism, mining, schools, agriculture, rural communities).
- **Subsea (MSA):** master service agreement with **Angola Telecom + SATCAB** to
  integrate the **SARSSy** system with Google's **Equiano** cable via Telecom
  Namibia's **Swakopmund** landing station — providing Angola Telecom **1 Tbps**
  of dedicated Equiano capacity.

## The ministerial ICT layer

The operator-level deals sit under a **government-to-government** frame. At
**ANGOTIC 2026** (Luanda, 11–13 June), Namibia's ICT minister
**[[emma-theofelus]]** and Angola's Minister of Telecommunications
**[[mario-oliveira]]** ([[ministerio-das-telecomunicacoes-tecnologias-de-informacao-e-comunicacao-social-angola]])
concurrently signed a **ministerial ICT & media-cooperation MoU** (2026-06-11/12):
a framework for joint ICT and media projects, exchange of technical expertise among
state employees, and **joint cybersecurity operations** — cybersecurity featuring
prominently. It formalises the political mandate the operators execute.

The chain runs back through the **April 2026 Swakopmund MoU/CTA** (2026-04-24)
between **[[angola-telecom]]** and Telecom Namibia — Angola Telecom accessing
Equiano capacity landing at Swakopmund and laying groundwork for SARSSy
collaboration, positioning Erongo as a regional digital gateway — to the
**October 2025 proof-of-concept** on [[angosat-2]], where Namibia's MICT first
partnered with GGPEN to test satellite backhaul for its remote areas.
