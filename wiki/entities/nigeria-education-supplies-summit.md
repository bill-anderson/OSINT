# Nigeria Education Supplies Summit (NESS)

Annual Nigerian education-sector convening organised by **[[skld-integrated-services]]**, bringing
together school leaders, regulators, policymakers, financiers, technology providers and education
suppliers. **NESS 2026** was held at the **Civic Centre, Victoria Island, Lagos, on 2026-07-16**,
themed *"Resilience in Motion, Schools Leading Change"*.

> *Sponsored TechCabal Partner / Press Room placement — an organiser's account of its own event,
> not independent reporting. Everything below is a position stated by a named speaker at a
> vendor-convened summit; none of it is a finding, and no figure below is independently verified.*

## Positions on record (2026-07-16)

- **AI as amplification, not reinvention.** Oluwakoyejo Oluwatosin (Chronicles Software
  Development Company) urged school leaders not to rebuild operations around AI but to use it to
  sharpen existing practice: *"Don't try to do anything new with AI. Learn the skills and enable
  your current landscape, because AI will confuse the directionless, but AI will enable those with
  clarity of vision."* He added the sharper claim that *"If you are a teacher and you are not
  engaging AI, then you are going to be extinct"* — a **vendor's assertion about teacher
  obsolescence**, recorded as such. See [[tech.ai]].
- **Data literacy as the distinct skill.** Oluwatosin separated data literacy from the AI
  conversation: *"Your ability to make sense of data gives you an edge… We are now in an
  intelligence age. So the data you gather must provide intelligence, and must give you the
  capacity to predict what is going to happen tomorrow."* The clearest statement in this batch of
  **data literacy as school-leadership competence** rather than a pupil curriculum item. See
  [[capacity.literacy]].
- **The named system failures.** Professor **[[ndubueze-mbah]]**, Enugu State Commissioner for
  Education, listed the sector's core problems as **foundational learning, quality of learning
  access, "the gap in digital interest", and the skills gap**, linked to graduate labour-market
  distortion. See [[include.divides]].
- **Enugu's answer.** Mbah set out **[[enugu-smart-green-schools]]** — 267 schools, solar-powered,
  connected by combined satellite and local internet infrastructure — as the reform in motion.
- **Financing the cash-flow problem.** Olubiyi Ogundepo (Head of Treasury, Lotus Bank) named
  **late or unpaid school fees** as a central sector constraint even at well-established
  institutions, and described the bank's *Edupartner* product extending financing **to parents as
  well as schools** — credit substituting for fee collection. Called Nigeria *"a very murky
  business environment"*.
- **Leadership framing.** Goodness Morakinyo (Valencia Schools / Greenwood British School Ibadan)
  offered a VUCA framework — volatility answered with vision, uncertainty with understanding,
  complexity with clarity, ambiguity with agility — with "getting clarity or understanding from
  data" as the operative instruction. Tinuke Bosun-Okusaga (People Plus Consulting) addressed
  talent attraction and retention.

## Why it matters here

NESS is the wiki's window onto **how Nigerian school operators themselves talk about digital
transformation** — and the striking thing is where the emphasis falls. The constraint named from
the floor is not curriculum or connectivity but **cash flow and foundational learning**, with AI
positioned as an efficiency layer over whatever already works. Against the state-level programmes
tracked on [[nigeria--tech-ai]] and [[dnemis-nigeria]], this is the demand side saying its binding
problem is solvency, not technology.
