# Organic Law No. 23 of 2024 (Tunisia Biometric Passport)

**The Tunisian organic law on the biometric passport, adopted by Parliament on 6 March 2024 in the
same overwhelming-majority vote as its companion [[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]] (biometric identity
card).** The two are consistently handled as a pair in the sources held, but the passport is the
travel-document half — the piece with the direct international-standards driver.

**Organic laws rank higher than regular statutes in Tunisia**, typically dealing with fundamental
aspects of government structure or basic rights and freedoms of citizens.

**Currency — a dated target, not an accomplished rollout.** Adoption is established; deployment is
not. The **22 April 2024** ministerial session targeted usage beginning in **H1 2025**, and **no
source held confirms the target was met**. See **GAP-TUN-002**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Adoption (6 March 2024).** Parliament adopted the Biometric ID and Biometric Passport bills by
  an overwhelming majority — the end of an eight-year civil-society campaign. **[[access-now]]**'s
  Marwa Fatafta, MENA Policy and Advocacy Director, called the vote **"a sad end to eight years of
  civil society campaigning for privacy and data protection"**, warning that "pursuing digital
  transformation without prioritizing people's fundamental rights is both risky and
  counterproductive"; Kassem Mnejja, MENA Campaigner, said the group's commitment to advocating for
  the rights and privacy of every Tunisian citizen "remains unwavering." Access Now is a first-party
  campaigner — its position, attributed, not a neutral record. Access Now said it would continue to
  monitor implementation and rollout. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-07 Eight years in the making - Tunisia's Biometric ID and Passport Bills risk rights]].
- **Implementation target (22 April 2024).** A ministerial working session at the Government Palace
  in the Kasbah, chaired by PM Ahmed Hachani, decided practical measures to begin actual use of the
  biometric passport (and ID card) **during the first six months of 2025**, following approval of
  Organic Laws 22 and 23. The steering committee — [[ministry-of-interior-tunisia]],
  [[ministry-of-communication-technologies-tunisia]], Ministry of Economy and Planning — deliberated
  the future technical and logistical path for issuance, stressing that citizens should benefit
  **inside and outside the country's borders**. Source:
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia - Biometric ID card and passport usage to begin in H1 2025]].
- **The ICAO driver (2024-04).** The move aligns with **[[icao]]** recommendations that **all
  countries adopt biometric documents by 2026** — the standards horizon against which the passport
  law is framed, aimed at enhancing security and streamlining identification for Tunisian citizens
  at home and travelling internationally. See [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia to Get Biometric IDs in H1 of 2025]].
