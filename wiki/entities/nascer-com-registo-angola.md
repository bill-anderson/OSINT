# Nascer com Registo — Angola

Angola's **birth-registration-in-the-maternity** initiative ("Born with a Record") —
placing civil-registry posts in hospital maternity wards so newborns leave already
registered and issued their first **[[bilhete-de-identidade-angola|BI]]**, free of charge.
It is the operational front of the third phase of BI universalisation (BI at birth).

## Current state

- **EU-funded, UNICEF-supported**, delivered jointly by the ministries of Justice
  (**[[ministerio-da-justica-e-dos-direitos-humanos-angola|MINJUSDH]]**), Health, and
  Social Action, Family and Women's Promotion. Aims to close the child civil-registration
  deficit (birth registration was 63% in 2024) by registering births in real time. Source:
  [[2026-03-04-zaire-inicia-registo-de-recem-nascidos-na-maternidade]].
- **Zaire launch (2026-03-04).** A post opened at Mbanza Kongo's Hospital Provincial Maria
  Eugénia Neto — civil registration had in fact run there since **October 2025** before the
  official launch — also registering all under-1s born elsewhere and training traditional
  birth attendants. Source: [[2026-03-04-zaire-inicia-registo-de-recem-nascidos-na-maternidade]].
- **Nationwide.** Civil-registry and identification posts now sit in the main hospitals,
  though the minister flags a **cultural barrier** — mothers still leaving without
  registering the baby (delays over naming, or parents' own lack of documents). Source:
  [[2026-06-12-justica-reforca-emissao-do-bi-registo-eleitoral]].
