# Wamkele Mene

Secretary General of the [[afcfta]] Secretariat; the principal continental voice for
operationalizing the AfCFTA digital single market.

## Activity in this wiki

- **AfCFTA Digital Trade Forum (2026-07, Lagos).** Used the forum to argue digital trade
  can bring more people into the formal economy, create jobs and boost regional growth,
  and (at GITEX Africa, April 2026) that operationalizing the Protocol on Digital Trade
  will push states to increase DPI investment. See [[afcfta]], [[gov.regional]].
- **700-data-centre call (2026-04-08, GITEX Africa, Marrakech).** Told GITEX Africa that
  Africa needs **>700 data centres** (from ~220 today, ~1% of global commercial capacity) to
  host AfCFTA digital-trade workloads, pegging the single market at **$3.6tn** and the digital
  economy at **$721bn by 2050**. See [[afcfta]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-08 Africa's digital trade push requires 700 data centres, says AfCFTA chief]].
- **18th Council of Ministers (2026-07-09, Abuja).** Opening the Council as SG, framed AfCFTA
  as entering an "implementation" phase — >12,000 Certificates of Origin issued by March 2026,
  full liberalisation targeted by 2030 — as **Nigeria** took the Council Bureau Chair from
  Egypt. See [[afcfta]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Nigeria Takes AfCFTA Council Chair as Digital Forum Charts Roadmap for Implementation]].
- **PAPSS launch backing (2022-08).** As AfCFTA SG, Mene championed the Afreximbank-built
  [[papss]] at rollout, saying it gave Africa "greater capacity to conduct cross-border
  transactions and expand... intra-African trade"; Afreximbank approved **$500m** for the
  West African pilot and projected a further **$3bn** for continental rollout — the payments
  rail underpinning the AfCFTA single market. See [[papss]], [[afreximbank]], [[dpi.pay]].
  Source: [[2022-08-30 Pan-African Payment and Settlement System drives Africa's banking integration]].

- **Seychelles Digital Trade Corridor MoU (2026-03-05).** During a Mene visit, the AfCFTA
  Secretariat signed the precursor MoU with Quest Ghana Ltd and the Govt of Seychelles that led
  to Seychelles' Cabinet approving (8 Jul 2026) the **AfCFTA Digital Trade Corridor** HQ in
  Victoria. See [[SYC]], [[afcfta-digital-trade-corridor]], [[seychelles-afcfta-mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Seychelles to Host AfCFTA Digital Trade Corridor Headquarters]].
