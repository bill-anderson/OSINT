# Cable wars: Mapping the political economy of submarine cables (Abels & Babic, 2026)

**Authors:** Joscha Abels & Milan Babic.
**Venue:** *Globalizations* (Taylor & Francis), peer-reviewed; part of a Special
Issue on geoeconomic competition. **Published:** 20 January 2026.
**Companion data:** [[submarine-cable-political-economy-dataset]] (open-access,
OSF).

## Argument

The paper challenges the popular "cable wars" narrative that frames the submarine
network as a US–China state contest. Its central claim: the network is
**overwhelmingly owned and controlled by private corporations**, whose commercial
logic acts as a **structural barrier to states' instrumentalization** of the
infrastructure. Geoeconomic competition *influences* the network but its effects
are **partial and fragmented**. Corporations are theorised not as mere
"instruments" of states but as **at least partially autonomous network builders**;
state action succeeds only where it **aligns** with the strategic orientation of
central corporate actors (clearest in the supply sector).

## Key empirical findings

- Submarine cables carry **>97% of internet traffic** and enable ~$10 trillion in
  daily financial transactions; the market is projected to grow from **~$13bn
  (2022) to >$30bn (2030)** (~12% CAGR).
- **Three build-out phases**: privatised Western telecoms (1990s); Global South /
  BRICS players post-dot-com-crash; and hyperscalers (Google, Meta) from ~mid-2010s.
- **[[google]] is the single most prominent owner** (16% of core ≥5,000 km cables,
  sole owner of most 200+ Tbps systems); [[meta]] second among hyperscalers (sole
  owner of Anjana, the highest-capacity transatlantic cable). Telecom carriers and
  a rising SOE share persist.
- **Supply is oligopolistic**: the "big four" (SubCom, ASN, NEC, HMN Tech) supply
  **~85%**. US statecraft (Team Telecom, entity-list sanctions Dec 2021) pushed
  **HMN Tech from >25% to <10%** market share, confining it to Africa/Asia (e.g.
  the **PEACE cable** — France→Singapore via Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Kenya, Pakistan).
  France nationalised **ASN** (€350m, finalised Nov 2024) via APE.
- **[[2africa-cable]]** (~45,000 km) is the network's extreme length outlier;
  **Medusa** ([[medusa-submarine-cable]], AFR-IX Telecom, 480 Tbps) is cited as a
  regionally-owned Global South project.

## Why we cite it

The definitive scholarly source on **who owns and supplies** the global cable
network and on the limits of the geopolitical "cable wars" framing. Read through
**sovereignty/colonialism** lenses. A specific dated figure drawn from it (a
market share, a capacity, a sanction date) becomes a normal source citing this
publication.
