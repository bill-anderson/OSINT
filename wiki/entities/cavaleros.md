# Cavaleros Group

**A South African property investment and development firm — founded 1926 as a shopfitting company —
that in November 2025 launched a data-centre unit, [[cosmas-data-cities]], and immediately announced
two of the largest campuses ever proposed in the country.** CEO **Penny Cavaleros**. Cavaleros
matters to the wiki less for its megawatts than for its **posture**: it is the operator that
declines to say where its power comes from.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Entry into data centres (2025-11-11).** The real-estate group launched **Cosmas Data Cities** to
  develop two sites: a **Johannesburg** campus in the **Samrand/Kosmosdal** area offering **200 MW
  via a 450 MW self-built switching station**, and a **Cape Town** development on **100 acres**
  totalling up to **360 MW**. **Further details on location, campus specifications and development
  timelines were not shared.** Advisers: **[[knight-frank]]** and data-centre engineering firm
  **Future-tech**. Plans for sites in East and West Africa were also mentioned without detail.
  Source:
  [[2025-11-11 South African real estate firm Cavaleros Group launches data center unit, plans two campuses]].
- **Penny Cavaleros on the legacy pitch (2025-11-11).** "For over five decades, the Cavaleros Group
  has built a legacy of vision, quality, and long-term partnerships. These data center developments
  continue that legacy — combining our real estate expertise with Knight Frank's industry insight
  and Future-Tech's technical excellence to deliver infrastructure that powers Africa's digital
  transformation." *Vendor framing — attribute it.* Source: same.
- **The non-disclosure, and why it is the story (2026-02-09).** Cavaleros marketing manager
  **Mikaela Potgieter** said electricity supply and detailed **power procurement structures were
  "commercially sensitive and therefore cannot be publicly disclosed,"** with further details
  "subject to strict non-disclosure agreements"; environmental and water considerations would be
  handled "through standard regulatory approval processes." **Daily Maverick explicitly contrasts
  this with [[teraco]]**, which "appears to have adopted a much more transparent approach." On a
  grid facing constrained supply by 2029 (see **[[eskom]]**), refusing to disclose procurement is a
  governance fact, not a commercial preference. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Major SA data centre expansion plans emerge, set to more than double current power demand]].
- **The Microsoft question, unresolved (2026-02-09).** The Cosmas Data City project is "**believed to
  be linked mainly to [[microsoft]]**," which had announced a further **R5.4-billion** investment
  (on top of **R20.4-billion over the previous three years**) for SA's first "enterprise-grade" data
  centres in Johannesburg and Cape Town, jointly announced by President **[[cyril-ramaphosa]]** and
  Microsoft President Brad Smith. **Neither Cavaleros nor Microsoft would confirm or deny** a
  business relationship. *Note: this source treats the Microsoft announcement as already made as of
  2026-02-09, against held sources dating it 2026-04 — see
  `reviews/contradictions/open/microsoft-sa-investment-date`.* Source: same.
- **Announcement dating.** Daily Maverick recalls Cavaleros announcing "shortly before Christmas"
  (i.e. December 2025); the **earlier, more precise DCD report of 2025-11-11** is preferred and the
  later recollection treated as loose.
