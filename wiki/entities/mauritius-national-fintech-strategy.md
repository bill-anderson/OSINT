# Mauritius National Fintech Strategy 2026-2030

Mauritius's fintech-sector strategy, unveiled **25 June 2026** (Côte d'Or Technopole, 200+ stakeholders) with
technical support from the **UN Economic Commission for Africa (ECA)**, to modernise payments, deepen financial
inclusion and sharpen the financial sector's competitiveness. It is another instrument in
**[[ministry-ict-mauritius|MITCI]]**'s stacked 2025-2029 policy programme (see [[gov.policy]]).

**Six priority pillars:** adapting the regulatory framework; supporting innovation; strengthening digital
infrastructure and cybersecurity; developing digital skills; attracting investment; expanding financial
inclusion. It calls for a **National Fintech Governance Committee**, a national fintech skills framework, an AI
learning platform, and stronger startup support — plus **open banking**, expanded cross-border transactions, and
AI in financial services with stronger consumer protection and data governance. Delivery runs through legislative
reform (a planned **Business Facilitation Bill**) and coordination with the **[[bank-of-mauritius|Bank of
Mauritius]]**. Context: the ECA sees Africa's fintech market exceeding **$65bn by 2030**.
