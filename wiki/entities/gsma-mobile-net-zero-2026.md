# GSMA Mobile Net Zero — State of the Industry on Climate Action

An **annual [[gsma]] flagship** tracking the mobile industry's energy consumption, emissions and
decarbonisation against its science-based pathway (45% reduction by 2030; net zero by 2050). The
**2026 edition** draws on energy and emissions data from **more than 110 operators representing 85%
of global mobile connections (as of the 2026 edition, on 2024 data)** — the standing reference for
network-energy and towerco-diesel figures.

**Cite, don't absorb.** These are global aggregates on a global operator panel, not African
country-level state. Pull a dated figure as a citation of the relevant edition; leave the rest in the
report. Africa-specific energy evidence arrives via country clippings — see [[infra.energy]].

## What the 2026 edition establishes (published 2026-07)

- **Emissions trajectory.** Industry operational emissions **115 Mt CO2e in 2024** (~0.2% of global
  GHG), **down 5% in 2024** and **down 13% between 2019 and 2024** — achieved while mobile
  connections grew **10%** and data traffic more than quadrupled over the same period.
- **Renewables as the main lever.** Operators purchased or generated **~70 TWh** of renewable
  electricity **in 2024**; the renewable share of operator electricity rose **10% (2019) → 24%
  (2024)**. Regional shares **in 2024**: Europe **~70%**, North America **~50%**, Latin America
  **~45%** — the report's central argument being that **access to renewables in emerging markets** is
  now the rate-limiting factor.
- **Target adoption.** **81 operators** held near-term science-based targets **as of June 2026**
  (~half of global connections, >two-thirds of industry revenue); **50** have committed to net zero,
  **46 validated** by the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi).
- **Towercos are the weak link — and the African-relevant one.** The world's **100 largest tower
  companies** ran **~4 million sites** and burned **>2 billion litres of diesel in 2024**; **fewer
  than a quarter** of the largest towercos held validated targets **(as of the 2026 edition)**. This
  is the segment that carries African off-grid and bad-grid coverage.
- **Scope 3 dominates.** ~**three-quarters** of the sector's footprint sits in the value chain
  (handset makers, network vendors, cloud/IT providers).
- **Policy asks.** Liberalise electricity markets and improve corporate access to renewables,
  streamline clean-energy permitting, and recognise **communications networks as critical
  infrastructure within national resilience planning**.
