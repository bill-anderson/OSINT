# MINTTICS (Ministry of Telecommunications, Information Technologies and Social Communication)

Angola's central digital-transformation ministry — the state actor that convenes the country's sovereignty agenda across connectivity, the sovereign cloud, the space programme and ICT diplomacy. Minister: **[[mario-oliveira]]** (Mário Augusto da Silva Oliveira). It is the counterparty on essentially every foreign-partner ICT MoU in this wiki and the political home of the [[livro-branco-tic-angola-2023-2027]] (ICT White Paper) and the Governo Digital 2027 agenda.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Structuring projects (2026-03).** MINTTICS set out its "projectos estruturantes" — a domestic submarine cable linking the Lobito Corridor to Equiano, the national broadband build-out and the sovereign-cloud migration; ~28m mobile subscribers, ~17m broadband users (2026-03). See [[angola--infra-connect]], [[angola--infra-store]]. Source: [[2026-03-26-minttics-projectos-estruturantes-base-tecnologica]].
- **National data centre & Nuvem Nacional (2026-04).** The ministry framed the ~$89m Camama facility (~17.5m broadband subscribers, ~75% penetration, 2026-04) as **digital sovereignty**, with progressive and mandatory migration of public services to the national cloud. See [[angola--infra-store]], [[infosi]]. Source: [[2026-04-28-pr-inaugura-novo-centro-de-dados-do-governo]].
- **ICT diplomacy (2025-11 → 2026-06).** Minister Oliveira signed ministerial ICT MoUs with **Kenya** (Luanda Financing Summit, Nov 2025) and **Namibia** (ANGOTIC 2026), and space/digital MoUs with **Mozambique** (Maputo, Jun 2026); MINTTICS is the convenor of Angola's turn as a regional space/DPI **exporter** via ANGOSAT-2. See [[AGO]], [[gov.regional]], [[kenya-angola-ict-mou]], [[telecom-namibia-angola-deals]]. Sources: [[2025-11-04-kenya-and-angola-strengthen-digital-ties-luanda-summit]], [[2026-06-11-namibia-and-angola-join-forces-angotic-mou]].
