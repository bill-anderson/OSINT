# Zain Sudan

Mobile network operator in Sudan, a subsidiary of the Kuwait-based [[zain-group]] MENA
operator. Reported serving **~23 million customers** (2025); mobile wallets are a primary
financial tool for much of its base, making it systemically important to payments as well as
connectivity in a conflict-disrupted state.

## Key facts

- **Civil-war connectivity blackout (early 2024).** After the April 2023 SAF–RSF war escalated,
  Sudan suffered a nationwide mobile blackout; three MNOs including Zain Sudan could not restore
  service, leaving communities isolated during a **~40-day** outage that severed mobile-money and
  emergency-aid channels. Zain reported losing **~$12m/week** during the blackout.
- **Cloud migration to restore service (2024).** Rather than its incumbent vendor's 6–9-month
  hardware timeline, Zain Sudan contracted [[totogi]] for a public-cloud, AWS-hosted
  charging-as-a-service platform and reported restoring service **~18 days** after signing,
  migrating **>20 million subscribers** to the cloud. Vendor-reported gains: ~76% lower total cost
  of ownership; ~$100m revenue vs the slower path. *(Vendor case study — figures unverified against
  primary reporting; capture is not endorsement.)*

Illustrates the resilience argument for cloud-native telecom infrastructure in fragile states, and
the concentration risk when a handful of operators carry a nation's payments rails. See
[[infra.connect]], [[dpi.pay]].
