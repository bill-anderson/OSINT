# ANGEO-1 (Earth-observation satellite)

**Angola's first national Earth-observation satellite**, built by **[[airbus]]** Defence and Space
(Toulouse) on its next-generation **S250 optical platform** and designed to deliver **>1,000
high-resolution images per day**. Client-side lead is **[[ggpen]]**; framed by the government as
**sovereign access to very-high-resolution imagery** for territory, natural-resource, agriculture,
urban, environmental, mining, oil-and-gas and border applications.

## Timeline

- **2025-01-23** — Financing signed at the Angola–France Economic Forum: **€225,257,201** via
  **Société Générale** ([[angola-france-satellite-financing]]), covering design, manufacture,
  launch and in-orbit installation.
- **2025-10** — President [[joao-lourenco]] announces the **Angolan Space Agency**.
- **2026-03-16** — **Official kick-off** at Airbus Toulouse (~**€225m / US$259m**), signed by
  GGPEN DG **Zolana João** and Airbus (Christophe Lemercier / EVP Alain Fauré), with Minister
  **[[mario-oliveira]]** present. Positioned as a **European pole** in Angola's foreign-stack contest.
- **2026-05-14** — **System Key Point (SKP) milestone validated**; first Earth-observation **ground
  infrastructure** delivery expected from **June 2026**.

Complements [[angosat-2]] (communications) with an EO capability; part of the National Space
Programme's aim to move Angola from **user to producer/operator** of space technology.
