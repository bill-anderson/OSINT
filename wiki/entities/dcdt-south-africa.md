# Department of Communications and Digital Technologies (DCDT, South Africa)

South Africa's national ministry for communications, connectivity and digital
government (Minister **[[solly-malatsi]]**) — the department behind digital-public-
infrastructure policy, broadband/spectrum, the SABC/ICASA policy environment and
social-media/online-safety regulation. It sits alongside the Presidency's Digital
Services Unit and Operation Vulindlela on the delivery of **[[mymzansi]]**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Inclusion-first framing of digital government (2026-07).** Via Minister
  **[[solly-malatsi]]**, the DCDT publicly cautioned that "digital-first" must not become
  "digital-only" — success measured by whether the excluded, offline and priced-out are
  included, not by the count of online services — and signalled a self-regulation-preferred,
  intervene-if-necessary stance toward global social-media platforms. See [[include.divides]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 Digital-first must not become digital-only, says Malatsi]].

- **Smartphone luxury-tax removal (2026-07).** With the **National Treasury**, the DCDT removed
  the **9% ad valorem luxury excise** on **sub-R2,500 entry-level smartphones** to widen device
  access — GSMA-measured impact (**+1.1m** units/11 months; monthly sales 250k→460k; value
  R2.7bn→R3.1bn/mo; [[gsma-sa-smartphone-tax-reform-2026]]). A concrete affordability lever pairing
  with the department's "digital-first ≠ digital-only" inclusion stance. See [[include.access]],
  [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 SOLLY MALATSI  Removing smartphone tax means access to opportunity]].
