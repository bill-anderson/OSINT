# Electronic Deeds Registration System (eDRS, South Africa)

**South Africa's electronic land-registration system, launched 1 April 2025** by the
**[[department-of-land-reform-south-africa]]** through the office of the Chief Registrar of Deeds.
It is the country's move from paper deeds registration to a digital register — and, notably, a
system that neighbouring jurisdictions cite as a model.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launch and legal basis (2025-04-01).** The **Electronic Deeds Registration Systems Act 19 of
  2019** was brought into **full operation by Proclamation 250 of 2025**, effective the launch date.
  It empowers the Chief Registrar of Deeds to develop, establish and maintain the electronic system
  for the preparation, lodgement, registration, execution and storing of deeds. Source:
  [[2025-04-01 Land Reform and Rural Development implements Electronic Deeds Registration System]].
- **Registration is in PILOT — read the phases separately (as of 2025-04-01).** Two components
  launched at different maturities: **information provisioning** (the online portal, publicly
  accessible for information on registered deeds and documents) and **electronic access** (registers
  and documents available without visiting a Deeds Registry) are live; the **electronic registration
  process and its system module entered a pilot**, to be rolled out to the full user base
  afterwards. The system is accessible "**in a limited capacity**" at
  `https://eservices.gov.za/edrs` — the capacity limit is stated by the department itself. Source:
  same.
- **Five years of parallel running, at the conveyancer's discretion.** A **dual registration
  process** runs for **five years** from launch, allowing **both manual and electronic lodgement and
  registration at the discretion of the conveyancer**. Once a registration is completed
  electronically, manual registration is no longer permitted for it. The manual procedure is to be
  phased out gradually. Source: same.
- **The 2030 endgame: repeal both Acts.** eDRS is to be enhanced to cover outstanding registration
  procedures with the aim of **repealing both the Deeds Registries Act 47 of 1937 and the Electronic
  Deeds Registration Systems Act 19 of 2019**, replacing them with new legislation **expected to be
  concluded by 2030**, at which point all deeds registration is envisaged to be **solely
  electronic**. Source: same.
- **Stated aims.** To replace manual processes with digital ones that **improve security, reduce
  turnaround times and curb corruption**, letting practitioners lodge deeds from any location in the
  country to the designated registration office. *Departmental framing — attribute it.* Source: same.
