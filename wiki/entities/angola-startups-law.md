# Angola Startups Law (Lei das Startups)

Angola's **startup framework law**, positioned as a core pillar of the national digital-transformation strategy and a signal of intent to pivot from oil dependence toward technology-enabled growth. It fills a legal vacuum the **[[ifc|IFC]]** had flagged and explicitly recommended closing (2023).

## Provisions and passage

- **Legislative sequence (2026-03).** Approved unanimously **in general** on **19 March 2026** (**181 votes**), presented by Industry & Commerce minister **Rui Miguêns de Oliveira**; **unanimous passage** reported **23 March 2026**.
- **Definition.** Distinguishes startups from micro/small/medium enterprises by scalability, technology adoption and global-market potential.
- **Threshold.** Annual-revenue ceiling ~**US$3.5m** (~3.2bn Kz) with **no minimum**, so pre-revenue ventures qualify; a **provisional seal** valid one year for entities still being formally constituted.
- **Incentive.** A **75% tax reduction** on amounts invested by startup investors, to stimulate private financing.

## Context

- **A thin, underfunded ecosystem.** The **[[ifc|IFC]]**'s 2023 assessment counted ~**125 startups** with **no exits yet**; **<2% of identified Angolan startups have secured venture funding** (Launch Base Africa), deal activity below $250k is scarce and angel/seed structures largely absent, and a 2024 UNDP report puts Angola behind Botswana, Namibia and Zambia on Southern-African startup funding. The law's impact will hinge on **capital formation**, not just legal clarity.
- **Surrounding scaffolding.** Sits with the **[[luanda-technology-park|Luanda Science & Technology Park]]**, the **$125m AfDB Crescer** youth-employment project, the INAPEM–INFOSI **DIGITAL.AO** incubation programme, and IFC partnerships. Nascent private capital is emerging via the **[[kimbo-fund]]**.
