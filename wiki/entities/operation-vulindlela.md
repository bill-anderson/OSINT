**Operation Vulindlela** is South Africa's structural-reform programme, a **joint initiative of the Presidency and the National Treasury** aimed at driving the implementation of structural reforms to support more rapid and inclusive economic growth. Now in its **phase two**, it organises reform across **seven priority focus areas** — including energy, water, logistics, visas, spatial inequality, state efficiency and **digital infrastructure** — and publishes quarterly progress reports through the Treasury.

## Digital public infrastructure pillar

One of the seven priority reforms is a **digital public infrastructure (DPI)** pillar whose objective is to harness digital tools to improve government efficiency, enable inclusion and support economic transformation. It is the programmatic home of South Africa's single-digital-entry-point effort, [[mymzansi]], and related building blocks:

- The **Q3 2025/26 progress report** (October–December 2025, released by National Treasury on 30 January 2026) rated the DPI pillar "on track" and set enabling **public access to MyMzansi for targeted test groups in March 2026** as the next step.
- The **MzansiXchange** data-exchange pilot was introduced in **October 2025** to enable seamless data sharing between departments.
- Work has commenced on a **national digital-government standards framework** to govern how government systems are designed, built and connected.
- A **payments ecosystem modernisation programme** is progressing, with design validation underway for a government-to-person (G2P) payments orchestration layer and a beneficiary-preference mapping tool; engagements with the South African Social Security Agency aim to link government payments to verified accounts and store-of-value wallets.
- The [[mymzansi]] prototype was built in ~10 weeks by the Presidency's **[[digital-services-unit-presidency-sa|Digital Services Unit]]**; by January 2026, however, the digital-ID reform it anchors was reported **behind schedule**, likely to take several more years despite an end-2026 target (keep both "as of" datings). Sources: [[2025-11-09 Behind the speedy MyMzansi screens and the Presidency's race to build a digital government]], [[2026-01-28 Bad news about South Africa's newest ID book plan]].

Deputy finance minister **Dr David Masondo** has stressed that "the economy cannot afford pauses or reversals in the reform programme." The DPI work sits within government's broader **national digital transformation roadmap** unveiled in May 2025, and echoes President **Cyril Ramaphosa's** State of the Nation commitment to put a digital identity system at the heart of service delivery. Treasury positions South Africa as one of the Global South leaders in building practical, open and inclusive DPI.
