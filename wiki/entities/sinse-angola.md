# SINSE (Serviço de Inteligência e Segurança de Estado)

Angola's **state intelligence and security service**, directed by **General Fernando Miala** since 2018. It appears in this wiki as the **named end-user in the first forensically-confirmed deployment of Intellexa's Predator spyware in Angola** — the surveillance counter-story to the state's digital-sovereignty rhetoric.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Predator spyware procurement and use (confirmed 2026-02).** Portugal's *Expresso*, working from a Predator information sheet **created April 2021**, names SINSE as the software's **end-user**; the reseller was Carlos Gandini (representing Intellexa via the consultancy Adsum), and a first meeting between the Angolan government and Nexa — the French firm in the Intellexa consortium — dates to **June 2018**. Amnesty International's Security Lab confirmed **with high confidence** that Predator was **active on journalist Teixeira Cândido's phone on 4 May 2024**. Cândido (former secretary-general of the Angolan journalists' union) filed a **criminal complaint** with the Attorney-General. Amnesty had already flagged Angola as a Predator buyer in 2023; a **pirated clone of the *Novo Jornal* site** (plus Jornal de Angola, Angop, Camunda News lookalikes) was used as an infection lure. This is the **first forensic confirmation** of Predator use in Angola. See [[predator-spyware-angola]], [[angola--infra-cybersec]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]].
