# Nizar Ben Neji

**Dr Nizar Ben Neji is Tunisia's former Minister of Communication Technologies (2021–2024), an ICT
expert and strategist, and the minister who led the design and implementation of the national
digital identity system [[e-houwiya]].** He is the principal official associated with Tunisia's
identity and interoperability layer in the period this wiki holds evidence for.

**Alias — transliteration variance.** He appears in
[[2024-02-14 National interoperability platform in Tunisia (Cybernetica UXP)]] as **"Nizar bin
Naji"**, quoted there as sitting Minister of ICT. Same person; the variant is recorded here so both
renderings are greppable.

**Attribution rule attached to this name.** Where Ben Neji writes about E-Houwiya he is **not a
neutral observer**: he is its architect writing about his own programme. His account
([[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]])
is **first-hand principal testimony** — valuable precisely because it is authoritative on design
intent and operating detail, but to be attributed as a named participant view, never as independent
verification of the programme's success.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Author of the E-Houwiya anchor account (2025-03-19).** Writing as "ICT Expert and Strategist and
  Former Minister of Communication Technologies of Tunisia (2021-2024)", Ben Neji set out
  E-Houwiya's design, issuance terms, cost, SSO scope and adoption (**200,000** mobile-ID
  subscribers as of **March 2025**, including over 15,000 abroad), and stated plainly that he "led
  the design and implementation of E-Houwiya" during his tenure. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **Sitting minister on the interoperability platform (2024-02, as "Nizar bin Naji").** Quoted on
  the [[uxp-tunisia]] deployment: "We aim to facilitate transactions between the citizens, the
  government and the structures between them… reducing deadlines, saving time and reducing errors…
  We are looking forward to all ministries, institutions and state structures engaging in this
  project." See [[dpi.exchange]], [[cybernetica]]. Source:
  [[2024-02-14 National interoperability platform in Tunisia (Cybernetica UXP)]].
- **At the biometric-ID ministerial session (2024-04-22).** Attended the working session at the
  Government Palace in the Kasbah, chaired by PM Ahmed Hachani, that targeted the start of biometric
  ID card and passport use in **H1 2025**. See [[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]],
  [[organic-law-2024-23-tunisia]]. Source:
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia - Biometric ID card and passport usage to begin in H1 2025]].
- **Credited as E-Houwiya's supervisor by later reporting (2025-03).** Biometric Update describes
  E-Houwiya as "developed under Dr. Nizar Ben Neji's supervision" — an independent restatement of
  his own claim, though the piece is heavily derivative of it and is not corroboration. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
- **Succession, not conflict.** By **March 2025** the Minister of Communication Technologies is
  **Sofiene Hemissi**. The two names attach to different dates and reflect a change of officeholder,
  not a contradiction in the record. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
