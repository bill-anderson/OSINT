# FATF (Financial Action Task Force)

**The international standard-setter on anti-money-laundering and counter-terrorist-financing,
whose Recommendations are transposed into national law.** In this wiki the FATF matters as a
**source of identity and registry obligations**: its recommendations pull states toward
beneficial-ownership registers, customer due diligence and — increasingly — coverage of virtual
asset service providers. See [[gov.standards]], [[gov.legislate]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Transposed into Togo's AML law (2025-12).** Togo's National Assembly adopted a new
  anti-money-laundering law ([[togo-aml-law-2025]]) that **replaces a 2018 statute** by
  incorporating **updated FATF recommendations** alongside a **2023 [[waemu]]/UEMOA directive**.
  The resulting framework introduces a **risk-based approach**, extends due-diligence obligations
  to **virtual asset service providers**, and **strengthens beneficial-ownership identification
  requirements** — the data-governance payload of an ostensibly financial-integrity reform. See
  [[TGO]], [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-30 Togo Adopts New Law to Strengthen Anti-Money Laundering Rules]].

**Scope note.** The evidence held here establishes the FATF only through its transposition into
Togolese law; no membership, mandate, listing status or evaluation history is asserted from it.
