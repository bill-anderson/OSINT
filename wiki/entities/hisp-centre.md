# HISP Centre (University of Oslo)

The **Health Information Systems Programme** centre at the **University of Oslo** (HISP UiO)
that develops and stewards the open-source [[dhis2]] platform. DHIS2 is used by ministries of
health in **75+ countries** at national scale, serving as the routine-data backbone for
programmes touching **>40% of the world's population** (~3.2bn people). HISP UiO coordinates a
global network of **~23 local HISP groups** across Africa, Asia, the Middle East and Latin
America.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Case for local ownership (2025-04).** In a first-party post, HISP UiO argued that after the
  **2025 US/USAID funding cuts** — which shut US-run parallel systems (e.g. PEPFAR's DATIM) and
  briefly cut Kenya's MoH off from its national EMR — an informal HISP-network survey found
  that **locally-owned national DHIS2 systems stayed online** while donor-funded parallel
  systems collapsed. [[africa-cdc]] estimated countries depend on aid for up to 80% of malaria/HIV
  programme costs. The lesson: fund **routine, government-owned, open-source** health-data
  systems, not project cycles. See [[dpi.mis]], [[capacity.research]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-03 Now is the time to invest in locally-owned health data systems]].
- **Amplifying the ODW report (2025-11).** HISP reported [[open-data-watch]]'s *Rebuilding
  Global Health Data*, reinforcing the call for permanent, budgeted national HMIS capacity and
  multi-year training partnerships between ministries and universities. See [[data.statistics]].
  Source: [[2025-11-05 New report highlights urgent need to rebuild and sustain health data systems]].
- **DNEMIS, Nigeria (2026-07).** HISP Centre supported the Federal Ministry of Education's
  launch of **[[dnemis-nigeria|DNEMIS]]**, a [[dhis2]]-based national education MIS and now
  the largest DHIS2 deployment worldwide — HISP's stewardship extending from health into
  education. See [[NGA]], [[dpi.mis]]. Source: [[2026-07-05 Post by DHIS2 on LinkedIn]].
