# Vodacom–ITU digital-inclusion protocol (2026)

A protocol signed by **[[vodacom]]** with the **[[itu|International Telecommunication Union]]**
to deepen collaboration on **digital inclusion and telecoms development across Africa**, reported
**2026-07-17**. No value, term or start date is given in the held source.

## Scope as announced

Vodacom is to **share operational expertise and best practices** from its African markets to
support broader industry development. The stated focus areas:

- **Regulatory frameworks for satellite communications and spectrum management** — the most
  concrete item, and the one with the clearest policy edge given the operator's own exposure to
  LEO competition (see [[data.satellite]]).
- **Digital-inclusion strategies.**
- **Expanding connectivity for educational purposes.**

Vodacom Group Chief External Affairs Officer **Ayman Essam** framed it as positioning ITU and
Vodacom as "genuine collaborators, not just in policy discussions, but in real implementation,"
arguing that "public-private partnership isn't just aspirational language; it's the only viable
pathway to scale."

## Reading it

An operator writing into the standards and spectrum process that regulates it is not a neutral
act. Vodacom has separately been named among the telcos losing premium customers to
**[[starlink]]** and other LEO operators under lighter licensing regimes, and has publicly backed
treating satellites as wholesale rather than retail competitors — so "regulatory frameworks for
satellite communications" is a live commercial interest, not only a development agenda. Capture is
not endorsement; the protocol is recorded here as announced, with no assessment of outcomes, which
the source does not supply.
