# After Access (RIA survey programme)

**After Access** is **[[research-ict-africa]]**'s recurring, nationally representative
**demand-side survey of ICT access and use** across the Global South, and the standing
data asset the wiki returns to whenever a claim rests on how people actually acquire and
use connectivity — as opposed to supply-side operator/subscriber counts. Successive waves
(2012, 2017/2018, 2022) underpin a long series of RIA country and thematic reports.

## Why it's a standing OSINT asset

After Access is the reference demand-side counterweight to supply-side statistics (ITU,
operator subscriber counts), which RIA argues systematically misrepresent the digital
divide. Its microdata drive RIA's work on affordability, gender gaps, device ownership,
rural/urban disparity and the income/education determinants of meaningful access. For this
wiki it is the primary evidentiary anchor for **[[include.divides]]** / **[[include.access]]**
claims across Sub-Saharan Africa.

## How to use / cite

Consult it for specific, dated figures, then file **those** as normal sources that cite
this resource and its host **[[research-ict-africa]]**. Each After Access report in the
corpus (platform-work, the qualitative gender studies, the *Digital Africa post the
pandemic* country reports, and analyses drawing on the microdata) is a `type: source`
tagging the relevant place and this resource.

## Key reference figures (captured — do NOT promote into concept/place pages)

Per the wiki's reference-study discipline, these are held here for reference only; fresher
country data arrives via dated clippings and belongs on the place hubs.

- The 2017 After Access survey ran across **16 Global South countries (7 African)**; in the
  7 African countries surveyed individuals held **~2 SIM cards on average**, inflating
  supply-side subscriber counts (as of the 2018 Gillwald commentary).
- Uganda: internet access rose **8% (2018) → 22% (2022)**; **68%** own a mobile, **16%**
  smartphones, **2%** computers (After Access 2022 Uganda report).
- South Africa: **~half** the population lacked basic internet access and device price was
  the top barrier for non-users (After Access 2018 wave, per Gillwald analyses).
