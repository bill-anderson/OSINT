# Emma Theofelus

Namibia's Minister of Information and Communication Technology; advocate for a
sovereign national data centre and regional (Angola) cooperation.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Angola datacentre inspiration (2026-06).** Visiting Angola's newly-inaugurated
  National Data Center (Luanda) during ANGOTIC, drew inspiration for Namibia's own
  planned **sovereign** datacentre (a pillar of its 2025–2029 digital strategy);
  also raised e-waste cooperation with the ITU. See [[NAM]], [[AGO]],
  [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-15 Namibia encouraged by Angola on datacentre establishment]].
- **Free internet for schools and clinics (2026-01).** Announced that schools and clinics
  under towers built in the last two years — nine radio-access-network towers funded with
  **N$80m (~$4.98m)** — will receive **free internet for seven years**, part of an ongoing
  push into Namibia's most remote areas. See [[NAM]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[include.access]], [[digital.rural]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27 Namibian schools, clinics to get free internet]].
- **4G-device adoption push (2026-02).** At an MTC tower launch in Kunene, distributed 100
  Universal Service Fund-funded phones to top-performing learners, stressing adoption over
  infrastructure ("It means nothing when the tower is here but the community is unable to
  use it"), alongside [[cran]]'s proposed 4G-device tax breaks. See [[include.access]],
  [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-02 Namibian regulator proposes tax breaks on 4G devices]].

See [[NAM]], [[telecom-namibia]], [[infra.store]].
