# WURI (West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion)

A regional foundational-identity programme run by the [[world-bank]] with the
[[ecowas]] Commission as co-secretariat, building **interoperable, mutually-recognised
foundational digital ID** across West Africa so credentials work across borders
despite differing legal, technical and institutional environments. It targets the
region's **~300 million citizens** and is framed as a flagship contribution to
**ECOWAS Vision 2050** ("ECOWAS of the People"). See [[XWA]], [[dpi.id]],
[[gov.regional]].

## Structure and envelope (WB primary, 2023-12-21)

The World Bank's Phase 2 implementation status report is the primary account of WURI's
architecture as a **Multiphase Programmatic Approach (MPA)**:

- **MPA approved 5 June 2018; overall envelope US$317.1m.**
- **Phase 1 (P161329) — US$122.1m:** [[CIV]] Côte d'Ivoire, [[GIN]] Guinea, and the
  [[ecowas]] Commission.
- **Phase 2 (P169594) — US$273m:** [[BEN]] Benin, [[BFA]] Burkina Faso, [[NER]] Niger and
  [[TGO]] Togo. **Approved 28 April 2020; effective 15 September 2020; closing 30 July
  2026.**

Source: [[2023-12-21 ISR - WURI Phase 2 Seq 7 (P169594)]].

**A financing discrepancy that probably reconciles arithmetically.** Biometric Update, citing
Togo First (**2024-03-06**), reports **$72 million for Togo out of a total of $395 million for
WURI** — which does not match the ISR's **$317.1m** MPA envelope (2023-12-21). Neither source
explains the gap, but the two phases as separately stated in the ISR **sum to $395.1m**
($122.1m + $273m), so the "$395m" figure is most likely **the two phases added together**,
against **$317.1m** as the *overall envelope approved in 2018* — a programme whose phases were
later financed above their original ceiling, not two competing totals. That reading is
inference from the ISR's own arithmetic, not a statement either source makes, so both figures
stay recorded, dated and attributed. Note also that Togo's **$72m** here is the
**foundational-ID** money and is **not** the World Bank's separate **$100m** broadband/skills
operation of December 2024 ([[togo-digital-acceleration-financing]]) — the two must never be
summed. See [[finance.new]]. Sources:
[[2024-03-06 1 step forward, 3 steps back for West Africa's regional digital ID projects]],
[[2023-12-21 ISR - WURI Phase 2 Seq 7 (P169594)]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Regional exits shrink the programme (2024-03).** [[ecowas]] placed **Burkina Faso, Mali
  and Niger** under sanctions following military coups; the three are **leaving the bloc**,
  meaning their citizens **will no longer be able to use the ECOWAS Passport or biometric ID
  card, and will not be part of WURI**. Commission President **Dr. Omar Touray** noted all
  three departing countries had benefitted from WURI; ECOWAS was reported lifting sanctions to
  salvage some regional integration. A foundational-ID programme premised on regional
  integration losing members to the collapse of that integration — with **[[BFA]]** and
  **[[NER]]** both Phase 2 countries. See [[XWA]], [[gov.regional]], [[include.access]],
  [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-06 1 step forward, 3 steps back for West Africa's regional digital ID projects]].
- **Togo — legal scaffolding and enrolment.** WURI-Togo finances the biometric enrolment of
  **[[e-id-togo]]**, implemented by **[[anid-togo]]** with **[[inseed-togo]]** deploying field
  personnel. The Phase 2 ISR carries the primary account of Togo's e-ID legal scaffolding
  (data protection law **Sept 2019**; foundational unique identification law **Sept 2020**
  creating ANID; cybersecurity/cybercrime amendments **June 2022** creating ANCy; creation of
  the **[[ipdcp-togo]]**; a **Feb 2022** governance decree) and a goal of registering **8
  million individuals by June 2024**, with a proof of concept in four regions **Oct–Nov 2023**.
  Enrolment reached the **Maritime region Zone 1** from **22 November 2025**. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]]. Sources:
  [[2023-12-21 ISR - WURI Phase 2 Seq 7 (P169594)]],
  [[2025-11-19 Togo Advances Digital ID Program With New Enrollment Phase in Maritime Region]].
- **2nd Supervisory Committee (reported 2026-03-30).** The committee met **virtually**,
  chaired by **Silété Devo**, DG of Togo's National Agency for the Identification of
  Persons (NAIP) and Togo's permanent representative to the WURI governance committee
  ([[TGO]]). It reviewed progress since the inaugural 2023 meeting and **validated key
  strategic documents** — the Stakeholders' Assessment/Engagement/Management Strategy,
  the **Regional and Cross-border Interoperability Strategy**, the Communication
  Strategy, and the **Monitoring & Evaluation Framework** — plus the **2026 Regional
  Work Plan** aimed at full interoperability and **mutual recognition of ID
  credentials**. WURI project coordinator Albert Siaw-Boateng (ECOWAS) and Paul Nguyen
  (World Bank) tied the effort to free movement of persons, cross-border trade and
  social protection. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 ECOWAS, World Bank Advance West Africa Digital Identity]].
- **Benin outcomes — near-99% coverage.** In Benin ([[BEN]]), WURI (with [[anip-benin]]) built
  the foundational-ID (fID) "Certificat NPI/fID" / "C'est moi" certificate — a QR-code, lifelong,
  free proof of identity carried in a mobile app. Building on the 2017–18 biometric enrolment of
  ~90%, WURI enrolled **2.2m more** people (4,000 abroad), lifting biometric coverage to **near
  99%** and easing access to finance (Alafia microcredit), education and social protection. The
  Bank also donated **2,050** mobile registration terminals (2024) to feed civil-registration
  events into the RNPP/RNEC; by 2025 **≥75%** of Benin's identification services were accessed
  digitally, and ANIP plans a citizen mobile wallet by end-2026. WURI targets legal ID for
  ≥100m people across six West African nations by 2028. See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]],
  [[dpi.registry]]. Sources:
  [[2024-06-27 WURI digital ID project improving access to public services in Benin]],
  [[2025-11-01 Identification System in Benin Increasing Access to Services]],
  [[2024-10-28 Benin receives 2,050 biometric terminals from World Bank to boost civil registration]],
  [[2026-02-02 Benin reports 75% of identification services accessed through digital platforms]].

- **Phase 1 uneven — Guinea exits early, Côte d'Ivoire advances (ISR, 2025-05-02).** [[dpi.id]]
  The WB Implementation Status Report (P161329, Phase 1 = **[[CIV]] Côte d'Ivoire and [[GIN]]
  Guinea**) records a **sharp divergence**: **WURI-Guinea closed early on 28 June 2024** (two years
  ahead of schedule, restructured June 2024), institutional arrangements "not fully" consolidated and
  only **~8,000 unique IDs** issued through the pilot — with the ISR cautioning that Guinea's Oct-2023
  personal-identification and civil-registry laws (underpinning the **RAVEC** census) "did not fully
  reflect the principles of identification for sustainable development." Côte d'Ivoire, by contrast,
  advanced steadily (hosted by MEPS, linked to the CNAM universal-health fund; CMU enrolment ~33% →
  ~60% between early-2024 and Apr-2025). The **ECOWAS** regional component (interoperability strategy,
  a Community Act on electronic identification, an "fID to ENBIC" roadmap) lagged on disbursement. A
  concrete illustration that the WURI MPA lets countries join and exit at different levels — and that
  Guinea has since pivoted to a **domestic MOSIP/TECH5** ID path ([[oneci-guinea]]). See [[gov.regional]],
  [[gov.standards]]. Source: [[2025-05-02 WURI Implementation Status Report - Cote d'Ivoire and Guinea (P161329 Seq 11)]].

- **Regional interoperability strategy validated (Accra, 2025-08).** ECOWAS/WURI stakeholders validated a
  **cross-border digital-ID interoperability strategy** for mutual recognition of foundational IDs across
  member states (Niger [[NER]] among them) — the regional-integration layer above national fID systems.
  A June-2023 World Bank programme brief frames WURI's unique identifiers as the enabler of access to human-
  development services for ≥100m people. Sources:
  [[2025-08-11 Stakeholders validate strategy on digital ID interoperability in West Africa (WURI)]],
  [[2023-06-01 WURI Program - Unique Identifiers to Enable Access to Human Development Services (World Bank)]].
