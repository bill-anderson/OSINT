# WURI (West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion)

A regional foundational-identity programme run by the [[world-bank]] with the
[[ecowas]] Commission as co-secretariat, building **interoperable, mutually-recognised
foundational digital ID** across West Africa so credentials work across borders
despite differing legal, technical and institutional environments. It targets the
region's **~300 million citizens** and is framed as a flagship contribution to
**ECOWAS Vision 2050** ("ECOWAS of the People"). See [[XWA]], [[dpi.id]],
[[gov.regional]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **2nd Supervisory Committee (reported 2026-03-30).** The committee met **virtually**,
  chaired by **Silété Devo**, DG of Togo's National Agency for the Identification of
  Persons (NAIP) and Togo's permanent representative to the WURI governance committee
  ([[TGO]]). It reviewed progress since the inaugural 2023 meeting and **validated key
  strategic documents** — the Stakeholders' Assessment/Engagement/Management Strategy,
  the **Regional and Cross-border Interoperability Strategy**, the Communication
  Strategy, and the **Monitoring & Evaluation Framework** — plus the **2026 Regional
  Work Plan** aimed at full interoperability and **mutual recognition of ID
  credentials**. WURI project coordinator Albert Siaw-Boateng (ECOWAS) and Paul Nguyen
  (World Bank) tied the effort to free movement of persons, cross-border trade and
  social protection. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 ECOWAS, World Bank Advance West Africa Digital Identity]].
- **Benin outcomes — near-99% coverage.** In Benin ([[BEN]]), WURI (with [[anip-benin]]) built
  the foundational-ID (fID) "Certificat NPI/fID" / "C'est moi" certificate — a QR-code, lifelong,
  free proof of identity carried in a mobile app. Building on the 2017–18 biometric enrolment of
  ~90%, WURI enrolled **2.2m more** people (4,000 abroad), lifting biometric coverage to **near
  99%** and easing access to finance (Alafia microcredit), education and social protection. The
  Bank also donated **2,050** mobile registration terminals (2024) to feed civil-registration
  events into the RNPP/RNEC; by 2025 **≥75%** of Benin's identification services were accessed
  digitally, and ANIP plans a citizen mobile wallet by end-2026. WURI targets legal ID for
  ≥100m people across six West African nations by 2028. See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]],
  [[dpi.registry]]. Sources:
  [[2024-06-27 WURI digital ID project improving access to public services in Benin]],
  [[2025-11-01 Identification System in Benin Increasing Access to Services]],
  [[2024-10-28 Benin receives 2,050 biometric terminals from World Bank to boost civil registration]],
  [[2026-02-02 Benin reports 75% of identification services accessed through digital platforms]].
