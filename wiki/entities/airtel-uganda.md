# Airtel Uganda

Uganda's **second-largest mobile operator** after [[mtn-uganda]], part of [[airtel-africa]]. MD and CEO
(2026): **Soumendra Sahu**. Mobile financial services run through its **Airtel Money** platform; the
operator also reports partnerships with government agencies covering **rural connectivity and digital
education programmes** (2026-07).

- **2025 results — data overtakes voice.** Profit after tax **+41% to $124.3m (UGX 446.8bn)**; revenue
  **$620m (+13.3%)**; **data revenue >$306m** now exceeding voice ($292m). **19.2m subscribers** (+14%),
  8.7m data customers; rolled out 258 4G + 164 5G sites (5G at 364 sites, 100% sites 4G). Planning fibre,
  deeper 5G and **satellite partnerships for rural coverage** (see [[uganda--infra-connect]]). Source:
  [[2026-02-24-airtel-uganda-2025-results-data-overtakes-voice]].

- **Courtesy visit to the new ICT minister (2026-07-17).** Sahu led a delegation to
  **[[ministry-of-ict-and-national-guidance-uganda]]** and its newly appointed minister, congratulating
  her on the appointment and reaffirming Airtel Uganda's commitment to the national digital-transformation
  agenda. The stated focus was **expanding smartphone access** — framed by both sides as the binding
  constraint on online learning, digital financial services and entrepreneurship. Airtel Uganda said it
  was *"grateful to the minister for recognising Airtel Uganda's continued contribution to connecting
  communities through investments in network expansion, digital innovation and programmes that empower
  schools, young people and underserved communities."* **No target, timeline, device-financing scheme or
  investment figure accompanied the commitment** — the engagement is, on the record so far, a statement of
  intent rather than a programme. Sources:
  [[2026-07-17-airtel-uganda-advances-digital-inclusion-talks]],
  [[2026-07-17-airtel-uganda-ict-minister-meeting]].
