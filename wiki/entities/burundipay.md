# BurundiPay (national instant payment system)

Burundi's **national instant-payment platform**, launched by the [[bank-of-the-republic-of-burundi|BRB]]
on **23 April 2026** in Bujumbura. BurundiPay is an interoperability **bridge** — connecting banks,
microfinance institutions and mobile-money networks so users transfer across them via bank accounts,
e-wallets or phone numbers — rather than a replacement for existing providers. It made Burundi the
**22nd African country** with a live instant-payment system. See [[BDI]], [[dpi.pay]].

## What we know (as of 2026-04)

- **Design.** Real-time, **24/7**, ~**1,000 transactions/second**; supports merchant payments at POS
  regardless of provider; runs on smartphones and basic phones via **USSD** (internet penetration ~11%);
  built on **ISO 20022** and integrated with the RTGS and Automated Clearing House. Digital-payments
  director **Libère Nduwimana**: "use money in one's bank account directly via a phone."
- **Backers.** Funded by the World Bank **[[pafen-burundi|PAFEN]]** project; operated under the BRB's
  national switch ("Banki Nkuru"), coordinated with [[setic-burundi|SETIC]].
- **Context + targets.** Cash still dominates (deposits/withdrawals ~45% of value, Q2 2025); the BRB
  targets **~78% digital-payment usage by 2040**, near-universal by 2060. Success hinges on institutional
  uptake, pricing and network reliability.
