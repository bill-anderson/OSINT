# Pay Na-Na (Liberia Inclusive Instant Payment System)

Liberia's **Inclusive Instant Payment System**, branded **Pay Na-Na: Inclusive. Instant. Liberia** —
an interoperable national rail launched by President Joseph Nyuma Boakai that lets users send and receive
money **across mobile-money networks**, removing the earlier need to hold accounts on separate networks.
Regulated and supervised by the [[central-bank-of-liberia|CBL]] on open-source [[mojaloop]], it was
deployed in **73 business days** and cut some payment-processing times from days to seconds.

## What we know (as of 2026-07)

- **Launch (2025-12-16).** The CBL officially launched the IIPS on **16 December 2025** at the EJS
  Ministerial Complex in Congo Town, President Boakai keynoting and tying it to the **ARREST Agenda** as
  a "digital highway." CBL Executive Governor **Henry F. Saamoi** called it a "defining and transformative
  moment." The launch was preceded by **Participation and Service Level Agreements** signed (12 Dec 2025)
  binding the CBL, **Lonestar Cell/MTN Mobile Money**, **Orange Money** and switch operator
  **ThitsaWorks** — a three-year renewable framework with settlement in USD and LRD, Mojaloop-based
  reconciliation, and SLA penalties for downtime/late settlement. Sources:
  [[2025-12-16-liberia-launches-iips-techafricanews]],
  [[2025-12-17-cbl-launches-iips-mobile-money-silos-fpa]],
  [[2025-12-17-govt-launches-iips-orange-mobile-money-observer]],
  [[2025-12-15-cbl-formalizes-iips-agreements-observer]].
- **Scope + roadmap.** The pilot supports **P2P and G2P** (salaries, pensions, social transfers);
  **P2G** (tax payments with the Liberia Revenue Authority) is under way; future phases add commercial
  banks, fintechs, merchant and bill payments, and eventually cross-border. It incorporates a full
  **National Electronic Payment Switch (NEPS)** supported by the [[world-bank]]. Partners: Gates
  Foundation, [[africanenda]], [[mojaloop]] Foundation, ThitsaWorks. Citing SIIPS 2025, Saamoi noted
  **25 African countries** now run domestic instant-payment systems (36 platforms). See
  [[2025-12-17-cbl-launches-iips-mobile-money-silos-fpa]].
- **Context.** Finance Minister Augustine Kpehe Ngafuan frames digitised payments as improving
  revenue collection, compliance and financial inclusion as Liberia moves off a cash-heavy economy;
  transaction costs are **capped below 1%**.
- **Adoption.** World Bank Global Findex shows Liberia reached **52% account ownership by 2024**
  (past its 50% target), driven largely by **>11m mobile wallets**. The Liberia Telecommunications
  Authority granted **Value-Added Services** authorisation to Orange Money for regulated mobile
  money, digital payments and electronic transfers.
- **Enabling connectivity.** Orange Liberia and [[zte]] installed **128 solar-powered LTE sites**
  in rural areas in 2024 (coverage to >580,000 residents); internet use reached **32.4%** of the
  population in early 2025 (from 19% in 2019). See [[infra.connect]].
