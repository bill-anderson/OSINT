# Pay Na-Na (Liberia Inclusive Instant Payment System)

Liberia's **Inclusive Instant Payment System**, branded **Pay Na-Na** — an interoperable
national rail launched by President Joseph Nyuma Boakai that lets users send and receive money
**across mobile-money networks**, removing the earlier need to hold accounts on separate networks.
Officials say it was deployed in **73 business days** and cut some payment-processing times from
several days to seconds.

## What we know (as of 2026-07)

- **Context.** Finance Minister Augustine Kpehe Ngafuan frames digitised payments as improving
  revenue collection, compliance and financial inclusion as Liberia moves off a cash-heavy economy;
  transaction costs are **capped below 1%**.
- **Adoption.** World Bank Global Findex shows Liberia reached **52% account ownership by 2024**
  (past its 50% target), driven largely by **>11m mobile wallets**. The Liberia Telecommunications
  Authority granted **Value-Added Services** authorisation to Orange Money for regulated mobile
  money, digital payments and electronic transfers.
- **Enabling connectivity.** Orange Liberia and [[zte]] installed **128 solar-powered LTE sites**
  in rural areas in 2024 (coverage to >580,000 residents); internet use reached **32.4%** of the
  population in early 2025 (from 19% in 2019). See [[infra.connect]].
