# AfricaNenda Foundation

African foundation advancing instant, inclusive payment systems and DPI; works with
**30+ central banks** across the continent. CEO **Dr Robert Ochola**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **2025 execution year — SIIPS 2025, Scaling, DPI Roadmap (2026-03).** The Annual Report and the SIIPS
  2025 census record **36 live IPS** ($1.98tn in 2024) with Nigeria's NIP the first "mature"; **Liberia**
  launched a [[mojaloop]] IPS in **73 days** ([[central-bank-of-liberia]]) and Rwanda's [[ekash]]
  relaunched. With the AU Commission (AACB **Decision 21**) it is building a cross-border Regulatory
  Harmonization Framework, and co-authored the **[[dpi-roadmap-playbook]]** and *Scaling Instant Payments*.
  Sources: [[2026-03-01 AfricaNenda Annual Report 2025]],
  [[2025-11-01 SIIPS 2025 State of Inclusive Instant Payment Systems in Africa]],
  [[2026-03-01 Scaling Instant Payments in Africa Policy Choices for Central Banks]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]].

- **South Sudan NIPS partnership (2025-02-05).** BoSS announced a partnership with AfricaNenda to
  build South Sudan's first **[[nips-south-sudan|National Instant Payment System]]** — roadmap,
  capacity-building at the **[[bank-of-south-sudan]]**, and bank/telco interoperability, following a
  **2023 proof-of-concept**. CEO **Robert Ochola** framed it as "laying the foundation". This was a
  **project launch, not a go-live**; AfricaNenda's own *SIIPS 2025* census (data to June 2025) does not
  list South Sudan among live IPS. See [[SSD]], [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-05-africanenda-boss-nips-announcement]].
- **DPI partnership (2026-07-07).** MoU with **[[equity-group-holdings]]** and the
  **[[gates-foundation]]** to move DPI "from policy to implementation" across 20–30
  markets — combining AfricaNenda's technical/policy/ecosystem role with Equity's reach.
  Deal: [[equity-africanenda-gates-dpi-partnership]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].
- **Instant-payments maturity benchmark (2026-02).** Nigeria's NIBSS Instant Payments (NIP)
  is cited as the **first African instant-payment system rated "Mature"** on AfricaNenda's
  inclusive-instant-payments maturity spectrum, as [[nibss]] unveiled its ISO-20022 National
  Payment Stack (20+ African central banks attending the Lagos launch). See [[NGA]],
  [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-02 How Strategic  Shift of NIBSS Is  Rewiring Nigeria's Payments System]].

- **eKash lead partner (2025-12).** AfricaNenda is named a **lead partner** in Rwanda's
  instant/interoperable payment system **[[ekash]]** (launched 5 Dec 2025, powered by
  [[rswitch-rwanda]]) — its inclusive-instant-payments mandate in national practice. Provenance
  note: the launch account is by an RSwitch insider, so volume figures are project-stated. See
  [[RWA]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-05 From Access to Empowerment Introducing Rwanda's Instant Payment System — eKash]].
- **SIIPS data in the "payments end-game" read (analysis, 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki draws on
  AfricaNenda's **SIIPS 2025** report — Africa's cross-domain instant-payment systems moved
  **~$1.98tn** in 2024, with transaction volumes growing at a **35% CAGR** (19.7bn→64.6bn,
  2020–2024) — to argue instant rails are becoming utilities. Figures are AfricaNenda's own SIIPS
  data, cited by Kariuki. See [[dpi.pay]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]].
