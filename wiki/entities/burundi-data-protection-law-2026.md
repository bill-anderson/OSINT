# Burundi Personal Data Protection Law (2026)

Burundi's first dedicated **personal-data-protection law**, adopted unanimously by the National Assembly on **15 January 2026** (Kigobe) and sponsored by the Ministry of Interior, Community Development and Public Security.

## Key provisions & context

- Governs collection, processing and exploitation of personal data across public and private sectors; sets core privacy principles; creates an **independent administrative data-protection authority** for enforcement; introduces **criminal penalties**, complementing existing cybercrime provisions.
- Explicitly motivated by the digitisation of the **voter register**, computerised public administration, and preparations for a **biometric national ID card** — the law is framed as removing a key obstacle to that ID rollout (pilot testing in ~a dozen communes as of Jan 2026). See [[burundi--dpi-id]].
- Aligns Burundi with the AU **[[malabo-convention]]**; the source noted ~**39 African countries** had adopted dedicated DP laws as of January 2026. Source: [[2026-01-16 Burundi adopts data protection law]].

Ranks Burundi among the recent wave of African states legislating data protection — but the independent authority is newly created and untested.
