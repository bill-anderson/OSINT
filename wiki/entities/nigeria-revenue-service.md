# Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS)

Nigeria's federal tax administration, **formerly the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS)**. The
wiki holds it as a **digital-identity and digital-payments actor** rather than a fiscal one: it is
the body wiring the tax base to the country's identity numbers and to the virtual-asset regime.

- **Identity as the tax key (2026-01-12).** The **Tax Identification Number (TIN)** is issued jointly
  by the NRS and the **Joint Revenue Board**, and the reform ties it to the **NIN** — Nigeria's
  nearest equivalent to a social-security number — to give the revenue authority visibility
  comparable regimes already have. VASPs must additionally flag large or suspicious transactions to
  both the tax authorities and the **[[nfiu|NFIU]]**. Source:
  [[2026-01-12-tin-nin-crypto-tax-carf]].
- **VAT on electronic banking (from 2026-01-19).** Banks and fintechs were directed to collect
  **7.5% VAT** on electronic-banking fees and remit it to the NRS — the change that made the
  FIRS→NRS renaming visible to ordinary account holders. Source:
  [[2026-01-15-fg-7-5-vat-electronic-banking]].
- **Virtual Asset Council vice-chair (2026-07-20).** Under the
  [[virtual-assets-executive-order-2026|Executive Order on Virtual Assets Coordination]] the NRS is
  **vice-chair** alongside the **[[sec-nigeria|SEC]]**, under a **[[cbn]]** chair, and is to release
  a **tax policy for the virtual-assets sector** operationalising existing tax law for the asset
  class. Source: [[2026-07-20-nigeria-coordinated-regulatory-framework-virtual-assets]].
