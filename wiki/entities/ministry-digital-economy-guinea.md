# Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and Digital Economy (Guinea)

Guinea's lead ministry for the digital agenda — driving connectivity, e-government, data sovereignty and the sector's contribution to GDP under [[simandou-2040]]. Minister (2025): **[[rose-pola-pricemou]]**. It oversees the national backbone, the Tier III data centre ([[guinea-national-data-centre]]), sovereign .gn management, and the newly structured delivery agencies **ANDE** (state digitalization), **ANSSI** (cybersecurity) and **ANSUTEN** (universal service/inclusion). See [[GIN]], [[gov.policy]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **2025 sector review (2026-01-14).** [[infra.connect]] Reported: national backbone **quadrupled to 200 Gbps** (from 50); ~**12,000 km** fibre to all administrative regions; first Tier 3 data centre; an IXP operationalised; sovereign .gn; **39 public-service apps** (incl. passport via unified portal); TELEMO ([[telemo]]) and FUGAS ([[fugas]]); 10,000+ trained; GIGA connected 585 schools; telecoms **4.4% of GDP** and ~$220m investment; mobile-internet costs −25%. 2026 targets: 600+ 'white zones', e-gov/education, cybersecurity and local hosting. Source: [[2026-01-14 Guinea Reviews 2025 Digital Sector Gains, Targets Faster Rollout in 2026]].
- **Digital-sovereignty framing at TAS 2025 (2025-11).** [[infra.store]] Minister Pricemou tied local data hosting (Tier III DC) and .GN reinstatement to Guinea's digital sovereignty, and flagged an October-2025 UNDP-supported national workshop laying the foundation for a **national AI strategy**. Source: [[2025-11-13 Transform Africa Summit 2025 opens in Conakry, epicentre of digital cooperation in Africa]].
