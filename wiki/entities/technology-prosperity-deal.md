# Technology Prosperity Deals (TPDs)

The US's bilateral technology-cooperation instrument — nonbinding MoUs (signed by
the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy with partner countries)
to align tech standards, accelerate joint research, and **expand US full-stack AI
exports**. The unit of analysis in Carnegie's argument for structured US–Africa
AI engagement.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Four signed to date** — UK (Sep 2025), Japan & South Korea (Oct 2025), Sweden
  (May 2026); common architecture (AI innovation, regulatory alignment, full-stack
  exports, standards) tailored per partner. **No African country has signed one.**
- **Kenya as model case (Carnegie, 2026-06).** [[jane-munga]] argues a **US–Kenya
  TPD** could be a replicable baseline for Africa, organised around seven focus
  areas (AI infrastructure, governance alignment, skills/research, commercial
  diplomacy, financing, regional strategy incl. **[[pax-silica]]**, soft
  diplomacy). Tensions flagged: digital **sovereignty** / lock-in; disruption by
  unrelated bilateral disputes (the paused US–UK TPD; stalled US–Zambia health
  deal); the risk of a China-competition framing that "won't land in Nairobi,
  Lagos or Lusaka." Delivered via the **[[american-ai-exports-program]]**
  interagency machinery (USTDA, EXIM, DFC). See [[KEN]], [[tech.ai]],
  [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]].

- **Deal-bidding phase still pending (2026-07).** Reporting on the sibling
  [[american-ai-exports-program]] notes its **Phase 2** (deal bidding) — the stage where
  TPD-style packages would be brokered — had **not yet launched** as of Jul 2026, amid an
  underwhelming 78-application first round. Source:
  [[2026-07-11 'Not where they hoped it'd be' Launch of Trump AI promotion program underwhelms]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty` / `colonialism`: a TPD is framed as "an entry point into the
  architecture of American AI leadership" — the open question (Data Landscapers'
  recurring one) is whether it builds African capability or deepens dependence on
  the US stack. Compare the EU's own dependency debate: [[2026-06-29 How AI Keeps Europe Hooked on US Cloud]].
