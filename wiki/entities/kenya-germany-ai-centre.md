# Centre of African Excellence for Applied AI (Kenya–Germany)

A proposed bilateral initiative to establish a **Centre of African Excellence for
Applied Artificial Intelligence** in Kenya, developed jointly by the Kenyan
government and Germany. As of 2026-07-11 the centre is at the
**preliminary-consultation stage** — not yet formalised.

## What it is

The centre is intended to strengthen AI research, teaching, innovation and
capacity building in Kenya, positioning the country as a **continental hub for
ethical AI innovation** and locally relevant solutions. It is explicitly aligned
to **Kenya's National AI Strategy 2025–2030**.

The [[kenya-aist|Kenya Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (Kenya-AIST)]]
is designated as the **central institutional vehicle** for the centre.

## Parties / origin

On **2026-02-03** in Nairobi, Principal Secretary [[john-tanui]] (Ministry of
Information, Communications and the Digital Economy) welcomed a delegation from
the **German Federal Ministry of Research, Technology and Space**, led by Erik
Hansalek (head of department for cooperation with Africa and the Middle East),
accompanied by ministry officials and German Embassy Nairobi officials. Kenya-AIST
council leadership — Prof. Emmanuel Mutisya (chairman) and Prof. J.W. Khamasi
(principal/council secretary) — attended.

Discussions covered collaboration frameworks, the central role of Kenya-AIST,
responsible AI governance, talent development, data ecosystems, and next steps
toward formalising the partnership.

## Significance

The initiative is one strand of a wider pattern of external partnerships shaping
Kenya's AI trajectory in early 2026 — alongside the
[[kenya-india-italy-sovereign-ai-partnership]] (India/Italy trilateral) — and
represents a **European (German) vector** into Kenya's AI capacity-building
agenda. It should be read against the sovereignty debates surrounding foreign
partnerships in African AI governance (see the
[[au-google-mou]] critique).
