The **Tony Blair Institute for Global Change (TBI)** is a policy and advisory organisation founded by former UK Prime Minister Tony Blair that works with governments — heavily across Africa — on digital government, digital ID and technology-enabled public-sector reform. In the OSINT domain it recurs as an external advisor and delivery partner on national digital-transformation and digital-identity programmes.

## Relevance here: Kenya digital credential wallet

TBI partnered with Estonian IT firm **[[cybernetica]]** on a **verifiable-credentials (VC) proof-of-concept in Kenya**, completed and announced around **9 March 2026**. Key facts:

- **Purpose:** combat academic and recruitment fraud in the civil service. A recent audit found **more than 2,000 fake certificates** in a public-service recruitment drive.
- **How:** a **wallet-based digital credential system** for education degrees, following the **W3C Digital Wallet and Verifiable Credential model**; designed to be **decentralised** (no single credential database to hack) and interoperable.
- **Partner agency:** Kenya's **Public Service Commission** ([[kenya-public-service-commission]]).
- **Alignment:** built to align with Kenya's **Trust Framework and Trust Registry**; Estonia has separately funded Kenyan digital-government work.
- Cybernetica's Sven Heiberg called all aspects of the pilot successful, with user feedback "overwhelmingly positive" and appetite to extend it to professional certificates, digital IDs and driver's licences.

TBI's role in this pilot is as the institutional/advisory partner working with the delivery vendor Cybernetica and the Kenyan government.
