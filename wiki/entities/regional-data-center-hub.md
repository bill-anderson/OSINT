# Regional Data Center Hub (RDH)

**[[telecom-egypt]]**'s data-centre business, the vehicle for Egypt's bid to convert its
submarine-cable concentration into a regional colocation and cloud market. It operates the
**RDH 1** (2.5 MW) and Tier-III-design-certified **RDH 2** (4.6 MW) facilities.

## Status

- **Carve-out as a wholly-owned subsidiary (announced 2026-07-17).** After cancelling the
  sale of a majority stake to **[[helios-investment-partners]]**, Telecom Egypt said it would
  instead carve RDH out as a **wholly-owned subsidiary** with "a specialized team possessing
  the necessary technical and commercial expertise," to accelerate growth in the data-centre
  market "both locally and globally." The parent cited the sector's growth and rising Egyptian
  demand as requiring "a specialized operating model." See
  [[telecom-egypt-helios-rdh-stake-sale]], [[egypt--infra-store]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-telecom-egypt-cancels-helios-data-center-stake-sale]].

Retaining full ownership keeps a strategically-sited data-centre estate under a
state-controlled operator rather than a financial sponsor — a [[sovereignty]] outcome,
whether or not it was the motive; the stated reason is unmet transaction conditions.
