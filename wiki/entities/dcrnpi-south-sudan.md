# Directorate of Civil Registry, Nationality, Passport and Immigration (DCRNPI/DNPI)

South Sudan's civil-registration and identity authority, within the Ministry of
Interior, responsible for the civil registry, nationality certificates, national
identity cards, passports and immigration. Styled variously **DCRNPI** and
**DNPI** across sources. It is the institutional anchor for what national legal
identity exists in a country that, as of late 2025, still had **no unified
national digital ID** and issued civil documents at low coverage.

## Activity in this wiki

- **National ID issuance via EU/UNHCR (2024).** More than **17,400 National
  Identity Cards** were issued to returnees and people at risk of statelessness
  through a joint EU/UNHCR project since the start of 2024, focused on Central
  Equatoria return areas (Morobo, Lainya, Yei, Kajo-Keji). DG at the time:
  **Simon Majur**. Source:
  [[2024-11-21 Over 17,400 South Sudanese receive national identity cards thanks to UNHCR and the EU]].
- **Passport/ID resumption (2026-01-16).** After months of booklet shortages,
  DNPI resumed issuing passports, nationality certificates and national ID cards.
  The **personal ID card (Butaka Shakhsia)** — not the nationality certificate
  (Jinsia) — is now required for **banking, land transactions and court
  processes** (adults 18+). Stock held: 380,000 personal ID cards, ~35,000
  nationality certificates, plus passport booklets. A SIM-linked foreigner
  registration was also announced. DG: **Maj. Gen. Elia Kosta Faustino**. Source:
  [[2026-01-16 Juba - Passport, ID card issuance resumes]].
- **Civil registration / statelessness (late 2025).** As of Dec 2025 only ~10%
  of the ~12m population held Nationality Certificates and national ID/birth
  certificates were not yet issued at scale; a **CRVS Costed Plan** and Civil
  Registry Rules are adopted, with a birth-registration pilot at Juba Teaching
  Hospital (partner: Hold the Child, UNHCR, UNICEF, NRC, IOM, UNECA, Vital
  Strategies). Source:
  [[2025-12-15 Strengthening Civil Registration and Ending Statelessness in South Sudan]].
- **Legal framework.** Operates under the Civil Registry Act 2018, Civil Registry
  Regulations 2024 (adopted 4 Dec 2024) and Nationality Act 2011. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 Policy and Legal in South Sudan (civil registry and identity framework)]].

_Note: the directorate is styled both **DCRNPI** (Ministry of Interior usage) and
**DNPI**; leadership changed from Simon Majur (2024) to Maj. Gen. Elia Kosta
Faustino (2026) — a personnel change over time, not a conflict._
