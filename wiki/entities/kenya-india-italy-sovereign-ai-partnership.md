# Kenya–India–Italy Trilateral Sovereign AI Partnership for Africa

- **Parties:** Kenya (Directorate of Digital Economy, Ministry of ICT), India
  (via the **EkStep Foundation**), and Italy (Ministry of Enterprises and Made in
  Italy), in partnership with the [[undp|United Nations Development Programme]].
- **What:** A trilateral strategic partnership to develop and deploy
  **sovereign AI solutions** across Africa, formalised through a **letter of
  intent**.
- **When / where:** Signed **2026-02-19** ("Thursday") in New Delhi at the
  **AI Impact Summit 2026** (India AI Impact Summit).
- **Target:** **100 AI deployment programs by 2030** — shifting AI adoption from
  isolated pilots to structured "AI diffusion pathways."
- **Status:** Letter of intent signed; early stage as of 2026-07-11.

## Focus

The partnership prioritises **multilingual voice AI systems** designed for
**low-bandwidth environments**, with explicit emphasis on **data sovereignty and
local control**. Target sectors: agriculture, health, education, public services
and livelihoods. It will provide shared infrastructure — including **open voice
models and affordable computing resources** — to lower barriers for African
innovators.

The framework combines the three partners' complementary strengths: India's
experience in **digital public goods** (EkStep is associated with the
India-stack lineage), Kenya's position as a regional technology hub, and Italy's
industrial AI expertise.

## Lineage and context

The collaboration **builds on the G7-backed AI Hub for Sustainable Development**
and **aligns with Italy's Mattei Plan for Africa**. It follows the **2026 Nairobi
AI Forum**, which helped African innovators access computing resources and
funding.

The signatories frame the deal as laying groundwork for a sovereign, inclusive
and sustainable AI infrastructure **led by African stakeholders** and tailored to
the continent's economic and linguistic needs. The
[[african-development-bank]] estimate that inclusive AI deployment could add as
much as **$1 trillion to Africa's GDP by 2035** (driven partly by productivity
gains in agriculture, health, education and public services) is cited as the
prize.

The "sovereign AI" framing here contrasts pointedly with the sovereignty critique
levelled at the [[au-google-mou]] — this arrangement foregrounds open models and
African-led control rather than proprietary hyperscaler infrastructure.
