# Ethio Telecom

Ethiopia's state-owned telecom incumbent (CEO **Frehiwot Tamru**), long a monopoly and
still the structural centre of [[ETH]]'s connectivity and mobile-money market despite
liberalisation. It positions itself as a national digital-infrastructure partner rather
than a pure connectivity operator, anchoring the [[telebirr]] platform and aligning to
[[digital-ethiopia-2030]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **H1 FY2025/26 results (as of 2026-01-30).** Added ~**4m** new customers to a base of
  **>87m**; revenue climbed to **$545m** (ETB 85.02bn) on rising data usage; paid **$228m**
  (ETB 35.6bn) in taxes — one of Ethiopia's largest fiscal contributors — and secured
  ~$88m in foreign currency. Rolled out its **Next Horizon: Digital & Beyond 2028**
  strategy, extending 4G LTE to dozens of new towns and investing in cloud, AI-assisted
  operations, cybersecurity and data-centre capacity, all framed as aligned to
  [[digital-ethiopia-2030]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-30 Inside Ethio Telecom's bold TechCo transformation]].
- **Telebirr scale (mid-2025).** Its [[telebirr]] mobile-money service reached **72m
  customers**, anchoring payments, savings and micro-loans at national scale. As the
  state incumbent it cross-subsidises data through voice revenue, keeping tariffs low and
  challenging the unit economics of competitors like [[safaricom-ethiopia]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-01-07 Why Ethiopia, Somalia and Djibouti are starting to matter in digital Africa]].
- **Somcable talks (2026-01-29).** Tamru met a [[somcable]] delegation (Somaliland) to
  discuss a strategic cross-border digital/telecom partnership — talks only, no signed
  deal. Earlier moves: began strategic financing talks with the **African Development
  Bank** ([[african-development-bank]]) in **January 2025**; partnered with Awash Bank on
  the Tila microfinance/savings service and with Tinno (Oct 2025) on smartphone adoption.
  See [[gov.regional]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-29 Ethio Telecom, Somcable talk cross-border collaboration]].

- **Government-incumbent category (Russell Southwood, 2025-12-04).** Southwood's
  MNO state-of-market classes Ethio Telecom among the surviving **government-owned
  incumbents** — a distinct segment from the pan-African "power players" and the
  stranded former fixed-line incumbents — its scale sustained by monopoly legacy
  and voice-subsidised data as private entrant [[safaricom-ethiopia]] rolls out
  cross-network M-PESA. See [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].
