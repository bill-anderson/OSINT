# MTN Group

Pan-African telecom operator; increasingly a data-centre and cloud builder.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Dabengwa Sifiso Data Centre, Lagos (2025-07).** MTN Nigeria launched Phase I:
  **4.5 MW / 780 racks**, Tier III, **$120m**, with cloud infrastructure built with Dell
  Technologies. Phase II targets **14–20 MW**; two-phase commitment **>$235m**.
- **Local cloud, priced in naira.** Launched a **local cloud service** with
  self-orchestration and **naira billing**, positioned as an accessible alternative to
  hyperscaler pricing for Nigerian businesses and government — a sovereignty-flavoured
  competitive move. See [[infra.store]], [[NGA]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **Ambition 2030 (2026-06).** "One MTN, three platforms" (connectivity, fintech,
  digital infrastructure), AI throughout: **313m subscribers / 19 markets**; targets to
  **triple fibre**, grow fintech **13×**, double data usage; homes connected 2.8m → 20m+
  by 2030; **MoMo** ~$500bn across 23.3bn transactions/yr; **Alipay+** super-app; a stake
  in an **AI-RAN consortium with [[nvidia]], Nokia, Cisco**. CEO **[[ralph-mupita]]**.
  See [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.pay]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-10 MTN Group goes all-in on platforms and AI]].
- **"New Frontier" (MWC 2026).** Mupita: coverage largely solved; the frontier is the
  **usage gap** ($40 handsets), **African-language LLMs** (2,000+ languages), and
  reintegrating IHS towers under a "geotechnology" ownership lens. See [[include.divides]].
  Source: [[2026-04-09 Ralph Mupita on MTN's New Frontier Connectivity, Content, and African AI]].
- **Mobile-money AI-governance gap (ICTworks, 2026-04-16).** MTN's **MoMo bot** and its
  GSMA Open Gateway fraud APIs are named among African mobile-money AI systems (fraud
  detection, credit scoring, chatbots) woven into a **$1.68tn / 108bn-transaction (2024)**
  infrastructure without independent, disaggregated bias/accuracy audits. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 3 Reasons Mobile Money Programs Have a $1.7 Trillion Problem]].
- **Satellite revenue-leakage (2026-05-26).** MTN (which paid **$273.6m** for its Nigeria
  5G spectrum) is cited among telcos whose high-value customers LEO operators like
  [[starlink]] capture under far lighter licensing; the Africa CEO Forum/Askya report urges
  a hybrid model treating satellites as wholesale, not retail, competitors. See
  [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-26 Africa risks losing billions to satellite internet operators, report says]].
- **AI data-centre bet (2026-03, FY2025 results).** MTN announced entry into the AI
  data-centre race, naming **South Africa and Nigeria** as priority markets and
  shortlisting co-investment partners; builds on the **Sifiso Dabengwa Data Centre** in
  Lagos (largest in West Africa, 4.5 MW → 14–20 MW). Analysts flagged SA water/electricity
  strain. CEO [[ralph-mupita]]. See [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]], [[ZAF]], [[NGA]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-28 MTN makes big bet on AI data centres in South Africa]].
- **SA investment — R21.8bn / $1.3bn to 2028 (2026-04).** At the 6th South Africa
  Investment Conference MTN pledged **R21.8bn (~$1.3bn)** over three years to end-2028 to
  expand ICT infrastructure — the conference's third-biggest pledge. MTN SA spent **R6.8bn**
  on its network in 2025 and has **>40m** subscribers. See [[mtn-sa-2026-investment]],
  [[finance.new]], [[ZAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-01 MTN to invest $1.3B in South Africa by 2028]].
- **MTN Ghana — $1.1bn / 3 years (2026-02-23).** During a Mupita visit, **[[mtn-ghana]]** committed
  **US$1.1bn** over three years to 4G/5G, hyperscale data centres and AI/cloud, positioning Ghana as
  a West African digital hub. MTN Ghana holds ~**58%** market share, **>25m** subscribers and MoMo
  **>11m** active users; echoes a 2022 >$1bn/5yr pledge. CEO **[[ralph-mupita]]**. See [[GHA]],
  [[finance.new]], [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 MTN Ghana secures $1.1bn boost as AI push deepens]].
- **South Sudan green network (2026-02-20).** **[[finnfund]]** committed a **$5m** follow-on (total
  $10m; backed by the EU's EFSD+/Africa Connected) into CREI to expand solar-hybrid energy at **MTN
  South Sudan's 499 telecom sites** (490 renovated, 4m+ served) under a 10-year Energy-as-a-Service
  contract; national coverage rose **69%→80%**. See [[SSD]], [[infra.energy]], [[infra.connect]].
  Source: [[2026-02-20 South Sudan green network gets $5m boost]].
- **Uganda election internet shutdown (2026-01).** MTN Uganda (with **[[airtel-africa]]**)
  implemented the **[[uganda-communications-commission]]**-ordered four-day internet blackout around
  the Jan 2026 presidential election — disrupting MoMo withdrawals, costing the two operators an
  estimated **~$15m**, and drawing a lawsuit naming MTN, Airtel and UCC (the Uganda Law Society
  called the order illegal absent a state of emergency). See [[UGA]], [[gov.legislate]],
  [[infra.connect]]. Source: [[2026-01-26 The cost of Uganda's internet shutdown]].
- **"Miss Baza" voice-AI assistant (MWC Kigali 2025).** MTN launched **Miss Baza**, a
  voice-first assistant supporting 4 languages on **feature phones** — a bid to bring
  non-smartphone users into digital services in what CEO [[ralph-mupita]] frames as the
  "voice era" of African inclusion. See [[tech.ai]], [[include.access]], [[capacity.literacy]].
  Source: [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].
- **MTN Nigeria 2025 Wrapped — vandalism vs recovery (2026-01).** [[mtn-nigeria]] reported
  **9,218 fibre-cut incidents** and vandalism at **211** sites in 2025, alongside a return to
  profit (**PAT N750.19bn / ~$525m** for 9M to Sept 2025, from a year-earlier loss). Physical
  sabotage, not cyberattack, is the fibre threat. See [[NGA]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[tech.industry]]. Source: [[2026-01-23 MTN Nigeria fibre network takes a hit]].
- **State-of-market — "holding company" thesis (Russell Southwood, 2025-12-04).**
  Southwood classes MTN among the four "power players" (with Airtel, Orange,
  Vodacom) now running as **data/holding companies** as data revenue overtakes
  voice: mobile-money is **28.5%** of MTN revenue, and the group spins non-core
  units out as arms-length businesses — towers, data centres, and **Bayobab**,
  MTN's group wholesale/carrier sub-brand (distinct from the Zambia-specific
  [[bayobab-zambia]]). Notes MTN–Airtel **infrastructure-sharing** deals in
  **[[UGA]]** and **[[NGA]]**, with >½ of MTN group CAPEX in Nigeria and top-3
  operators each guiding ~$1bn CAPEX. See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.connect]].
  Source: [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].
- **AI unit — EVA and "Genova" (Russell Southwood, 2026-05-19).** MTN's data
  platform **EVA** runs on Azure Databricks (**>20bn records, 800 workflows,
  1,700 data streams**); a new unit **"Genova"** is being set up to lease out AI
  compute, and **Bayobab** hired the former IXAfrica data-centre head — MTN
  positioning as a specialist AI-training/inference provider, not just consumer
  connectivity. See [[tech.ai]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-19 Trying to make sense of who will use Artificial Intelligence in Africa and how – Going beyond the hype]].
  (Southwood's [[2026-06-25 Regulating for Africa's future digital economy – A draft agenda to speed things up]]
  cites MTN's "Open Time" zero-rating as an access measure and MTN as an example
  of the self-reinforcing wholesale dominance regulators now struggle to check.)
- **Data-centre power crunch (2026).** In analysis of Africa's data-centre electricity
  squeeze — DC demand growing ~**20–25%/yr** while ~**600m** Africans still lack power —
  CEO **[[ralph-mupita]]** said Africa needs "hundreds of billions" in energy
  infrastructure to sustain the AI/data-centre buildout (World Bank/AfDB **Mission 300**
  targets 300m new connections). See [[infra.energy]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-05 Can Africa's Power Grid Withstand the Next Wave of Emerging Technologies]].
