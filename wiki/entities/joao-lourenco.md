# João Lourenço

President of Angola (MPLA) since 2017 and chairperson of the **African Union in 2025** — the head of state under whom Angola has pushed its **digital-sovereignty** agenda from policy into build-out, and personally the signatory or convenor of most of its foreign-partner deals. His presidency is where the multi-pole courtship over Angola's digital stack is transacted.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Inaugurated the sovereign national data centre (2026-04-28).** Opened the ~$89m Camama (Luanda) government data centre and **Nuvem Nacional**, framing it as digital sovereignty — the physical anchor of the state-cloud strategy. See [[angola--infra-store]], [[infosi]]. Source: [[2026-04-28-pr-inaugura-novo-centro-de-dados-do-governo]].
- **Cybercrime as a justice priority (2026-03).** Named cybercrime a major challenge for the justice system; Angola has **signed the UN Convention against Cybercrime**, and ~$20m in cybercrime-related assets were seized in 2025. Sits against the parliamentary cybersecurity bill, which stalled in July 2026 ([[angola--infra-cybersec]]). Source: [[2026-03-03-joao-lourenco-cybercrime-desafio-da-justica]].
- **India $200m DPI credit line (2025-05).** The Lourenço–Modi credit line under which Angola models its digital ID on Aadhaar — the Indian pole of the ID-stack contest. See [[angola--dpi-id]], [[angola-india-dpi-credit-line]]. Source: [[2025-05-07 India helps Angola with DPI, digital ID as part of 200M credit line]].
- **ANGEO-1 financing on the France state visit (2025-01).** Angola's first Earth-observation satellite (Airbus) was financed by a **€225m Société Générale** facility signed at the Angola–France Economic Forum during Lourenço's state visit — the European pole. See [[angola--data-satellite]], [[angeo-1]]. Source: [[2025-01-23-angola-and-france-secure-225-million-for-satellite-projects]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Lourenço personalises a **multi-pole** partner strategy — China (Huawei R&D, agreed direct with him), India (DPI credit), the UAE (G42/Presight), the US (Cybastion), Europe (Airbus/Société Générale) and Brazil (Serpro) — hedging across suppliers rather than committing the state's core to any one.
