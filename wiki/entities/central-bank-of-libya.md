# Central Bank of Libya (CBL)

Libya's central bank and the institution with the legal and technical mandate over the country's digital
payments and payroll data ([[dpi.pay]]). It operates the national instant-payment network (LYPAY ONEPAY)
and drives a rapid shift to electronic payments — but sits at the centre of the country's east-west
political split, which complicates data-sharing for its payroll-digitization reforms. See [[LBY]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digital-payments boom (2025).** [[data.statistics]] CBL statistics (to Feb 2026): mobile banking
  **4.29m subscribers / 200.7m transactions / 313.6bn LYD**; POS 165,313 terminals; **LYPAY ONEPAY**
  instant transfers 18.3m / 108.7bn LYD; 5.5m activated cards. Source:
  [[2026-02-24 Libya's digital payments exceed 313 billion dinars in record year (CBL statistics)]].
- **Ratebk Lahzi real-time salary system (2025-08).** [[gov.protect]] A real-time public-sector payroll
  platform on the instant-payment network (with the Ministry of Finance), covering ~2m employees/pensioners
  — pitched as anti-fraud (salaries = 55% of public spending) but resisted by the eastern government,
  which refused to share employee banking data. Source:
  [[2025-08-11 Libya's CBL launches 'Ratebk Lahzi' real-time salary system]].

- **Keystone 2025 annual figure — LD 389bn, +186% (2026-01).** [[data.statistics]] E-payment
  transactions rose **186% in 2025 to LD 389bn** (from LD 136bn), under Governor **Naji Issa**'s reforms
  (e-payment mandate, commission cuts, old-note recall, licensed FX bureaux). By EPAIX 2026 (June), H1
  ran ~25m transactions / **LD 162bn**, projected **~LD 700bn** by year-end. Full thread:
  [[libya--dpi-pay]]. Sources: [[2026-01-20-cbl-epayment-2025-up-186pc-389bn-libyaherald]],
  [[2026-06-17-cbl-epaix-2026-softpos-visa-standin-libyaherald]].
- **Rails & products (2026).** [[finance.new]] QR-code POS on LYPay (Mar); PM Aldabaiba decree mandating
  e-payments for **state revenue collection** + FX-commission caps (Mar); **Circular 9/2026** opening
  e-wallets to resident foreigners (Mar); and at EPAIX 2026 (June) **SoftPOS**, **international Visa
  acceptance**, and a **National Stand-In Processing Service**. Sources:
  [[2026-03-06-cbl-mandates-epayments-state-revenue-fx-cut-libyaherald]],
  [[2026-03-11-cbl-allows-foreigners-ewallets-circular-alwasat]],
  [[2026-06-16-cbl-epaix-softpos-visa-alwasat]].
- **Resilience gap (2026-05).** [[infra.cybersec]] A record **2m POS transactions across 67,000 points
  in one day crashed the system**; the CBL apologised — adoption outpacing backbone investment. Source:
  [[2026-05-27-cbl-epayment-system-crash-2m-pos-libyaherald]].
