# Africa Data Leadership Initiative (ADLI)

ADLI is a peer-learning network for senior government data-governance practitioners, run by the [[dial]] (Digital Impact Alliance). It brings ministries, revenue authorities and digital-ID agencies together to align data governance across African borders.

## Activity in this wiki

- **2026 cohort works on the AfCFTA Digital Identity Annex (as of 2026-05-27).** The cohort partners with [[afcfta]] on a test-and-learn approach to the Digital Identity Annex, adopted by the [[african-union]] in February 2025. It spans six countries across three trade corridors, drawing in ministries, revenue authorities and digital-ID agencies. Source: [[2026-05-27 Africa's economic future hinges on breaking down silos. This ADLI cohort advances integration]].
- **Anchored in regional frameworks.** The initiative references existing [[eac]], ECOWAS and SADC data-governance frameworks as reference points for cross-border harmonisation. Source: [[2026-05-27 Africa's economic future hinges on breaking down silos. This ADLI cohort advances integration]].
- **Framed as a vehicle for data-exchange sustainability (as of 2026-06-16).** ADLI is highlighted as a structure for sharing business-model and governance innovations to keep national data-exchange systems running past donor funding. See [[dpi.exchange]]. Source: [[2026-06-16 The financing gap for data exchange is well documented so why does it persist]].
