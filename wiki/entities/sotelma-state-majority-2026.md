# Mali regains SOTELMA majority stake (2026)

Reported **25 June 2026** (confirmed by Maroc Telecom via Financial Afrik). Mali's state
**raised its stake in [[sotelma]] from 49% to 56%**, reducing **[[maroc-telecom]]** to a
minority shareholder, via a **160 billion FCFA (~$277m; ~$264m per one report)** agreement that
also **renewed SOTELMA's operating licence** (reportedly extended to 2039).

- **History:** in 2009 Mali had sold a **51%** stake to Maroc Telecom for ~€275m / ~$294m; the
  reversal comes **17 years after** that privatisation.
- **Market:** SOTELMA (Moov Africa Malitel) had ~8.5m subscribers (H1 2024, ~37% share);
  **[[orange-mali]]** remains market leader (~54.9%).
- **Framing:** cast by the transition government as a major win for **economic sovereignty** and
  state control over strategic assets, mirroring AES ([[alliance-of-sahel-states]]) asset-reclaim
  moves in mining/energy; government pledged to maintain jobs and strengthen investment capacity.
- **Provenance:** staged via Business Insider Africa because the confirming primary (Financial
  Afrik, 2026-06-24) is paywalled; same event also reported by Businessday NG (2026-06-26).
