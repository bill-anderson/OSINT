# State Information Technology Agency (SITA)

South Africa's central government ICT procurement and service-delivery agency — the
"digital backbone" of the state.

## Activity in this wiki

- **PSC investigation (findings released 2026-07-06/07).** A Public Service Commission
  probe (commissioned Dec 2024 by Minister **Solly Malatsi**) found **systemic execution
  failure**: of 1,443 procurements reviewed, **1 in 4 never resulted in an award**
  (25.2% attrition); 529 matters still in the pipeline; 203 processes took **>1 year**;
  some contracts stuck **>400 days**. **R2bn+ ($123m)** irregular expenditure across four
  years, but the report's headline is that SITA "cannot consistently procure, manage and
  deliver technology for government." Departments (SAPS, Home Affairs, Justice) are
  seeking **exemptions** to bypass it. Root cause identified as leadership instability
  (2020–2025). Board given **30 business days** to submit a stabilisation plan.
- **Read as:** the institutional bottleneck behind South Africa's stalling
  [[dpi.govtech]] — a governance-capacity failure, not a strategy gap. See [[ZAF]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[tech.industry]].
- **AG confirms governance rot (2026-04).** The Auditor-General's 2024/25 consolidated
  report (cited alongside the stalled Sentech–BBI merger) finds SITA has run **without a
  permanent CIO for >3 years**, with no permanent board or MD and a **54% executive
  vacancy rate** during the audit period — the leadership-instability pattern behind a
  restructuring "promised for years without meaningful progress." Broadband Infraco's loss
  of its SITA contract underscores the agency's diminished delivery role. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-28 State broadband merger limps into a second decade]].
- **Firearm Permit System — court finds SITA extracted FDA source code (2025-12).** In the
  SAPS / Forensic Data Analysts dispute, the Pretoria High Court (Dec 2025) held SAPS liable
  **>R1bn** (R644m for IP + R120m maintenance) after FDA switched off its biometric
  **Firearm Permit System** in 2019 over non-payment — and found **SITA illegally extracted
  FDA's source code**. Stations reverted to pen-and-paper; >3,000 police firearms have gone
  missing since — a govtech-procurement failure implicating SITA. See [[ZAF]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-08 South African police forced to track firearms with pen and paper after R1-billion system blunder]].
