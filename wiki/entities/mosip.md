# MOSIP (Modular Open Source Identity Platform)

Open-source foundational-identity platform incubated at **IIIT-Bangalore** (led by
**S. Rajagopalan**), conceived so countries could rebuild Aadhaar's *ideas* as a global
public good rather than buy a proprietary vendor system. Funded by Tata Trusts,
[[gates-foundation]], Omidyar Network and Norad.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Scale (2026 reporting).** ~**2 million lines of code** (20% built pro bono);
  **30 active country engagements, 14 national rollouts**.
- **Morocco, first adopter.** [[MAR]] signed an MoU in 2018 and now registers **22m of
  39m** people; used to reach 5m households with social programmes. Contrast with the
  ~20 countries that took the conventional tender-and-vendor route and "ended in lots of
  trouble" with lock-in and cost. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]], [[geopol.india]].
- **Cited as Global-South momentum (2026-07).** In the Sierra Leone digital-ID coverage,
  Sign CEO Claire Ma points to the rapid spread of **MOSIP-based deployments** as evidence
  that African / Global-South governments are moving faster on foundational ID than many
  advanced economies. (Sierra Leone's own build uses the [[bhutan-ndi]] open-source
  framework, not MOSIP — see [[sierra-leone-digital-id-mou]].) Source:
  [[2026-07-08 Sierra Leone partners with Bhutan, Sign on digital identity platform]].
- **Fime accreditation (2026-06).** MOSIP accredited French digital-ID firm **Fime** to run accredited **biometric device testing** under the MOSIP Advanced Compliance Program (MACP) — independent validation for MOSIP-based national ID programmes. See [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.id]]. Source: [[2026-06-09 MOSIP accredits Fime for biometric device testing]].
- **Reference explainer (Biometric Update, May 2026).** A dated backgrounder anchoring
  MOSIP's model — modular open-source core + vendor ecosystem defeating **vendor lock-in**
  (the problem named at **ID4Africa 2018**), Aadhaar-influenced ([[nandan-nilekani]]),
  linked to UN **SDG 16**, and now integrating **digital payments** ([[dpi.pay]]). Country
  case studies: **Morocco** ([[MAR]], civil registration + social protection rather than a
  primary ID), **Sierra Leone** ([[SLE]]), **Ethiopia** ([[ETH]]), **Uganda** ([[UGA]]) and
  Sri Lanka. MOSIP is increasingly a governance model, not just software. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[tech.industry]]. Source: [[2026-05-01 Understanding-MOSIP-Biometric-Update-report]].
- **Ecosystem scale (Biometric Update, mid-2026).** Now engaged with **30+ countries**;
  **195m+ residents** issued MOSIP-based ID across **14+ national deployments**; 120+
  commercial partners. Flagship is **Ethiopia (Fayda)** — see
  [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]], [[ETH]] for the deployment detail. Source:
  [[2026-07-07 Beyond the Platform Understanding the Growing MOSIP Ecosystem]].
- **Decentralized-ID explainer (Biometric Update, 2026-05).** MOSIP now holds **MoUs with
  29 countries** for rollouts/pilots (incl. Philippines, Sri Lanka and **Togo**, where 6m
  unique numbers have been issued) and underpins national ID in Ethiopia, Uganda and Sierra
  Leone (Morocco the first implementer). Framed at **[[id4africa]] 2026** (Abidjan) as
  evidence African authorities are leading global thinking on foundational ID, with
  decentralised/federated architectures and voluntary enrolment the emerging direction.
  See [[TGO]], [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-28 Why decentralized digital identity looks different in every country]].
- **Uganda deployment (2026-05).** Uganda's new MOSIP-based national ID system has had
  **>28m records migrated**, anchoring a government "prototype showcase" and
  technology-sovereignty push toward locally-built DPI. See [[UGA]], [[dpi.govtech]].
  Source: [[2026-05-22 Uganda launches prototype program for digital ID and public infrastructure]].
- **Trust formula for DPI (ID4Africa 2026, 2026-05).** At the World Bank-led ID4Africa
  session, specialists noted that Kenya, India and Uganda built successful digital-ID
  systems yet suffered legal/operational breakdowns from weak data-lifecycle safeguards —
  underscoring that platforms like MOSIP need strong governance, not just software. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].
- **Zambia SI procurement (2026-03).** Zambia's **[[smart-zambia-institute]]** is
  seeking an international **system integrator** to deploy its MOSIP-based digital
  ID integrated with civil registration — insisting on **open standards** to avoid
  lock-in (Laxton recently certified as a MOSIP SI), targeting 80% of citizens by
  end-2026. Funded via the World Bank-backed **DZAP** (>$100m), with an MoU to
  learn from Ethiopia's **[[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]**. See [[ZMB]],
  [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]].
- **Lesotho DPI (2026-02).** Lesotho's **[[micsti-lesotho]]** ministry and the National University
  of Lesotho reviewed a DPI stack in which **MOSIP** provides the secure biometric digital ID,
  alongside **X-Road** for government interoperability and a Decision Support System for
  planning/budgeting. See [[LSO]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-20 MICSTI AND NUL ADVANCED LESOTHO DIGITAL PUBLIC INFRASTRUCTURE]].
- **OpenCRVS interoperability (2026-07-02).** At the **[[opencrvs]] v2.0** launch, the World Bank
  reaffirmed its collaboration with MOSIP to keep the two open digital public goods interoperable —
  OpenCRVS authenticates identities against MOSIP (via e-Signet), pairing civil registration with
  foundational ID as "one ecosystem." See [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-02 OpenCRVS v2.0 Product Launch Webinar - The Next Generation of Civil Registration]].
- **Digital-public-good context (DPGA, 2026).** MOSIP sits among the digital public goods
  promoted by the **[[dpga]]**, whose "Building Open Digital States" report profiles
  open-DPI state-building (Rwanda, Kyrgyzstan, Philippines) — Rwanda's DHIS2 lifting health
  data completeness 88%→95% and exporting DHIS2/Mojaloop regionally. See [[gov.standards]].
  Source: [[2026-01-01 Digital Public Goods Alliance]].

- **Nigeria NIN migrating to MOSIP (Anjola Adebowale, 2026-06-12).** Adebowale
  reports Nigeria's **>130m-enrolled NIN** system ([[nimc]], ~$430m World Bank/EU
  backing) is **migrating to MOSIP** — adding Africa's largest ID base to the
  MOSIP footprint, in a piece arguing governments need an open, sequenced
  "sovereign data" layer. See [[NGA]], [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-12 Beyond the AI Stack Why Governments Need Dynamic Digital Infrastructure]].
