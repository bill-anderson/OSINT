# National Directorate of Civil Identification (DNIC)

Mozambique's national identity-card authority (Ministry of Justice), issuer of the national ID card and
operator of the country's biometric ID system (implemented by **Mühlbauer**, serving a majority of ~33m
people). Legal-identity coverage remains low: only **~34% of adults held an ID card (2024)** though ~55% had
a birth certificate (IOM).

Backed by the World Bank's **[[edge-mozambique|EDGE]]** project and **ID4D**, and working with the Ministry
of Justice's National Directorate of Registries and Notary (DNRN), the DNIC ran a free-ID pilot in three
provinces from September 2023 and then spearheaded, on **13 November 2023, the country's first nationwide
free-ID campaign** — delivering 140,000+ free IDs within months and scaling up thereafter.
