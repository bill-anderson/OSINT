# The Aeonian Project

**A planned hyperscale data and high-performance computing centre at the
[[karuma-hydropower-plant]] on the Nile**, developed by [[synectics-technologies]] (CEO
**Oladele Oyekunle**) with **[[schneider-electric]]**. Its centrepiece is **USIO, a 10MW
sovereign supercomputer** built with **[[nvidia]]** (Blackwell GPU platform),
**MDCS.AI** and Belgium's **Automation NV**. Described by Synectics as a hybrid
Tier-4 Plus off-grid green-energy regional DHPC centre.

**One facility, three accounts** — Business Insider Africa (2025-09-11), Ecofin Agency
(2025-09-15) and Watchdog Uganda (2026-01-28) all report the same project. They are
complementary, not duplicates, and they do not agree on what makes it a "first."

## Activity in this wiki

- **Design and siting.** Uses **100MW of surplus pre-transmission electricity** from the
  government-owned **600MW** Karuma plant; **cooled by Nile water**, with modular
  heat-recycling; connected to a new **2,500km fibre-optic backbone** reaching submarine
  cables via **Kenya and Tanzania**. Services span colocation, blockchain, data storage,
  AI, on-demand cloud and Disaster Recovery (DRaaS) for regional data centres. Targets
  healthcare, life sciences, higher education and research, and the preservation of African
  languages and datasets. See [[infra.energy]], [[infra.connect]], [[infra.store]].
  Sources:
  [[2025-09-11 Another 'first AI factory in Africa' - Uganda is said to have made its entry into the AI race]],
  [[2025-09-15 Synectics, Schneider to build AI center in Uganda starting 2026]].
- **Schedule (as of 2025-09-15).** **Rollout begins 2026 with the first AI module, full
  capacity by 2028.** Business Insider (2025-09-11) puts first-phase deployment in the
  **second half of 2026**. Source: as above.
- **Cost — single-sourced.** **$1.2 billion**, reported only by Watchdog Uganda
  (2026-01-28), the thinnest-attributed of the three accounts. **Flag as single-sourced
  wherever used.** Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Uganda to Launch Africa's First Home-Grown AI Supercomputer Center at Karuma Hydropower Plant]].
- **Status trap — USIO does not exist yet.** Business Insider says USIO *"was built"* in
  partnership with NVIDIA (past tense); Ecofin, four days later, says it *"is being
  developed."* Almost certainly loose sub-editing. **State USIO as planned / under
  development as of 2025-09-15 — never as existing.**
- **The "first" label is contested — the wiki adopts none of them.** Three clips offer
  three different superlatives — "potentially Africa's AI factory" (BI), a "large-scale
  sovereign AI center" (Ecofin), "Africa's first AI data center fully owned and run by
  Africans" (Watchdog) — against [[cassava-technologies]]' competing NVIDIA-partnered
  **"first AI factory in Africa"** claim (Project Mufungi; Cassava said in April it
  intended to invest up to **$720 million**). **The Business Insider headline — *Another
  'first AI factory in Africa'?* — is itself the evidence that the label is contested
  marketing.** See [[tech.ai]], [[sovereignty]].
- **The sovereignty framing.** The project is pitched at retaining compute on the
  continent so researchers build models in local languages on African data. *"About 98% of
  African data is processed outside the continent"* (Ecofin). Niels Van Rees, co-founder
  of MDCS.AI: *"Africa must not just mine data, but also mint intelligence."* Oyekunle:
  *"By digitalizing clean energy through best-in-class infrastructure, we are empowering
  Africa to control its data backbone responsibly, sustainably, and sovereignly."*
