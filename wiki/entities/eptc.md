# Eswatini Posts and Telecommunications Corporation (EPTC)

**Eswatini's state-owned posts and telecommunications operator** ([[SWZ]]) — and the
single enterprise the World Bank has made the pivot of its connectivity reform
argument. Group Managing Director: **Themba Khumalo (as of 2024-11-22)**. Regulated by
[[esccom]]. See [[infra.connect]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Restructuring named as a precondition for growth (2025-07-31).** The
  [[eswatini-economic-update-2025]] makes EPTC reform one of its central digital
  recommendations: restructure the telecom SOE, adopt **open-access** policies, update
  regulatory frameworks to promote competition and lower costs, and expand rural
  broadband via public-private partnerships. [[satu-kahkonen]]: "Realizing this will
  require bold reforms... including the restructuring of Eswatini Posts and
  Telecommunications Corporation (EPTC)." See [[gov.policy]], [[include.divides]].
  Source:
  [[2025-07-31 Eswatini's Digital Transformation Crucial to Unlocking Growth, Jobs, and Economic Resilience]].
- **The cost problem it sits on.** Mobile 4G coverage reached **nearly 95% of the
  population (2025-07-31)** while only **about 58% (2025-07-31)** were using the
  internet, with data costing **3.47% of GNI per capita (2025-07-31)** — above the
  regional affordability threshold. The World Bank also urges **open access to backbone
  connectivity infrastructure (2025-08-05)**. Sources:
  [[2025-07-31 Eswatini's Digital Transformation Crucial to Unlocking Growth, Jobs, and Economic Resilience]],
  [[2025-08-05 Robust DPI inevitable for Eswatini's economic, jobs boom - World Bank]].
- **Channel for partnership appeals (2024-11-22).** Eswatini called for global partners
  to contribute expertise, funding and technology via EPTC when launching the
  [[eswatini-digital-readiness-assessment]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-22 Eswatini unveils readiness report for digital ID, transformation push]].
- **2021 shutdown compliance.** During the June–July 2021 unrest, government directed
  EPTC — alongside [[mtn-eswatini]] and Eswatini Mobile — to suspend internet services,
  a state-ownership pressure point [[apc]] treats as structural rather than incidental.
  See [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-11 Eswatini strives for digital sovereignty amid technological advancements]].
