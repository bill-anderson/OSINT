# National Treasury (South Africa)

**South Africa's fiscal authority — and, in the SRD grant litigation, a named respondent whose
budget allocation is itself alleged to be the exclusion mechanism.** With the Department of Social
Development, Treasury is one of the two entities competent to make social-assistance policy;
**[[sassa]]** administers, but does not set, the rules.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Named in the SRD challenge (as reported 2026-01-28).** The
  **[[institute-for-economic-justice]]** and **[[paythegrants]]** accuse the National Treasury and
  the Department of Social Development of **deliberately excluding millions of eligible people**
  from the SRD grant via administrative barriers designed to keep spending down. From the papers:
  "Treasury **persistently allocates funding that is sufficient to fund payment to fewer than half**
  of the 18.3-million people who the Department of Social Development has estimated would qualify
  for it" — against the department's own June-2022 figures of **13.4m** working-age people with no
  income and **18.3m** below the food poverty line. The groups **reject Treasury's claim that the
  grant is unaffordable** and ask the court to dismiss the state's SCA appeal with costs. *These are
  the litigants' contentions, not findings.* Source:
  [[2026-01-28 State is blocking access to SASSA grants, argue activists]].
- **Policy competence, per SASSA (2026-01-19).** SASSA's own statement confirms that **National
  Treasury and the Department of Social Development are "the two competent government entities
  responsible for any policy shift in social assistance"** — and that no COVID-19 SRD regulations or
  eligibility criteria had been amended, whether at the Minister of Finance's Medium-Term Budget
  Policy Statement or the Minister of Social Development's Budget Vote. This is the line that puts
  Treasury, not the delivery agency, at the point of decision. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 SASSA on qualification criteria for COVID-19 SRD]].
