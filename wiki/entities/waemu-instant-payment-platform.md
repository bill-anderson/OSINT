# WAEMU Instant Payment Platform (PI-SPI)

**The interoperable instant-payment platform operated by the [[bceao]] across the eight
[[waemu]]/UEMOA member states**, launched **30 September 2025** in Dakar by Governor
**Jean-Claude Kassi-Brou** (broadcast to Lomé by videoconference). It enables **24/7 instant,
irrevocable transfers** between banks, microfinance institutions, e-money issuers and payment
service providers, targeting **transfers in under ten seconds**, and is presented as cutting
transaction times and costs, promoting financial inclusion and shifting usage from cash to
electronic payments. Implementation received financial backing from the **[[gates-foundation]]**
and the **[[african-development-bank]]**. See [[TGO]], [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]].

**Acronym — canonical form and variants.** This wiki uses **PI-SPI** as canonical ("SPI" =
*Système de Paiement Instantané*). The **same publisher (Togo First)** renders the platform
three ways across three months: **PI-PSI** (2025-10-01), **PI** (2025-10-31) and **PI-SPI**
(2025-11-18). These are treated as renderings of one platform, not distinct systems.

## Participation in Togo — two dated states, not one trend

The two figures below describe **different things** and the later does **not** simply
supersede the earlier. They are recorded separately and dated.

- **As of 2025-10-01 — three banks OPERATIONAL.** Per the BCEAO, **Coris Bank, [[ecobank]]
  and [[orabank]]** were operating on the platform in Togo. Institutions then **planned to
  join**: BOA, BIAT, Cofina and **Sunu Bank Togo**. **Guy Martial Awona**, CEO of Orabank Togo
  and president of the UEMOA banking federation, called the launch a "decisive step toward an
  ecosystem where banks, microfinance institutions, and fintechs converge on a single
  infrastructure." Source:
  [[2025-10-01 BCEAO Launches Instant Payments Platform in Togo with Three Banks]].
- **As of 2025-11-18 — seven institutions COMMITTED / preparing interconnection.**
  **[[ecobank]], [[orabank]], BOA, Coris Bank, COFINA, Banque Atlantique and BIAT** were
  reported as having committed and as "preparing the necessary interconnection" to support
  24/7 instant payments. Source:
  [[2025-11-18 BCEAO Launches Developer Sandbox for Instant Payments]].

**Two real discrepancies between the lists.** (1) The November list **drops Sunu Bank Togo**
and **adds Banque Atlantique** — neither change is explained in the sources. (2) The states
differ in kind: **"operational" is not "committed/preparing interconnection."** Seven
committed institutions in November therefore does **not** straightforwardly supersede three
operational banks in October, and this wiki does not write "seven banks are on the platform."

## Activity in this wiki

- **Developer sandbox (2025-11-18).** The BCEAO launched a **public developer sandbox** for
  its **API-Business**, letting Togolese companies and IT solution providers build instant-payment
  solutions against the platform. The environment lets developers simulate operations, verify
  technical compliance and prepare integration, and supplies technical documentation,
  integration guides and assistance to fintech startups. Testable products include merchant
  payment solutions, cross-border transfer services and aggregation products linking mobile
  money, banks and microfinance. The stated aim is to cut deployment times and support adoption
  of new digital uses within the Union. See [[tech.innovate]], [[gov.standards]],
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-18 BCEAO Launches Developer Sandbox for Instant Payments]].
- **Distinct from Ecobank's own cross-border service.** Ecobank's [[thunes]]-powered instant
  **international** transfer service ([[ecobank-thunes-partnership]], launched in Togo, reported
  2025-10-31) is a **separate, bank-led product**, not the BCEAO platform. That report notes
  Ecobank is "among the three banks in Togo testing the BCEAO's instant payment system, known as
  PI" — a fourth state-description ("testing") alongside "operational" and "committed". Source:
  [[2025-10-31 Ecobank launches instant international payment service in Togo]].
