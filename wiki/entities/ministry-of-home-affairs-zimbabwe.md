# Ministry of Home Affairs and Cultural Heritage (Zimbabwe)

**The ministry responsible for Zimbabwe's national identity architecture** — the
[[registrar-general-zimbabwe]], the [[civil-registry-department-zimbabwe]] and the
[[department-of-immigration-zimbabwe]] sit under its remit — and the announcing body for
a digital ID rollout that had **not** started as of the last date held.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digital ID: announced, not launched (2025).** The Ministry was **quoted by local
  media in May 2025** as announcing that digital ID issuance **would begin in July
  2025**. The Biometric Update piece of **15 July 2025** records that there were **"no
  official indications yet when exactly the process will take off"**. **This is a missed
  start, not a launch** — write it as programme status. Issuance was **programmed** to
  begin in **Harare and Bulawayo** before extending nationally. **No coverage figure
  exists** in held material. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-15 Zimbabwe looks forward to digital ID rollout after population registry reform]].
- **Minister Kazembe Kazembe** (Home Affairs and Cultural Heritage) said at the time of
  the May 2025 announcement that the digital ID "will help curb identity theft, increase
  access to government services, and align Zimbabwe with global standards" — a statement
  of intent, relayed second-hand. Source: as above.
- **Registry modernisation from ~2023.** The digital ID plan follows a process begun
  around **2023** to modernise the legal ID management architecture: an upgrade of the
  **national population registry**, linkage to other services, and an announced
  integrated digital system built on the upgraded register. See [[dpi.registry]].
  Source: as above.

## Provenance warning

The single held source is **almost entirely aspiration and wholly second-hand**: every
claim in it is relayed ("local media quoted the Ministry", "was cited as saying",
"according to Bulawayo 24"). Nothing here evidences a delivered system.
