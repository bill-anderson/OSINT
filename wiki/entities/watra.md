# West Africa Telecommunications Regulators Assembly (WATRA)

Regional assembly of **16 West African telecommunications regulators**, coordinating
harmonised regulatory frameworks and peer learning across its member states. Executive
Secretary: **Aliyu Yusuf Aboki**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **4th Working Groups Meeting concluded (Ouagadougou, 2026-04-12).** Hosted by Burkina
  Faso's telecoms regulator **ARCEP**, under the theme "Building a Secure, Inclusive, and
  Resilient Digital Ecosystem for West Africa." Aboki framed it as a shift "from dialogue to
  the delivery of practical regulatory tools," nearly two years after the working groups were
  established. See [[XWA]], [[BFA]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 WATRA rallies Africa for region's $216b digital economy]].
- **Technical reports finalised.** The working groups completed a set of technical reports
  covering **5G deployment, submarine-cable resilience, cybersecurity frameworks, consumer
  protection, and non-geostationary satellite (NGSO) regulation** — intended as practical
  instruments to guide policy across the 16 member states. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 WATRA rallies Africa for region's $216b digital economy]].
- **$216bn digital economy.** WATRA cited West Africa's digital economy at **$216 billion**
  as of April 2026, expanding rapidly on the back of digital financial services, AI and IoT.
  Source: [[2026-04-13 WATRA rallies Africa for region's $216b digital economy]].

- **Subsea-resilience call to the region (2026-07-15).** Following publication of the
  **[[international-advisory-body-on-submarine-cable-resilience]]**'s reports — on which Aboki
  **served as a member** — he called for stronger regional cooperation on submarine-cable
  resilience, describing it as essential to safeguarding West Africa's digital economy and framing
  cable protection as an **economic**, not merely telecommunications, priority. He cited the **March
  2024** multi-cable disruptions that hit digital payments, communications and commerce across
  several West African states, and endorsed the report's central ask: **regional cooperation and
  regulatory harmonisation** through regional/intergovernmental frameworks, to cut fragmented
  requirements and build collective preparedness. He credited co-chairs **[[bosun-tijani]]**
  (Nigeria) and Sandra Maximiano (ANACOM, Portugal), reading Nigeria's role as a marker of subsea
  resilience's rising strategic weight for Africa. This gives WATRA's April-2026 subsea-cable
  technical report an explicit implementation hook. See [[XWA]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]], [[NGA]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-15-watra-regional-subsea-resilience-plan]].
