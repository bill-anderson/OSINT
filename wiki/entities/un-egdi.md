# UN E-Government Development Index (EGDI)

**The UN's composite index of national e-government capability**, published by **UN DESA** in its
biennial *E-Government Survey*. It is a **reference instrument**: a standing measuring rod the wiki
cites repeatedly across countries, not a report of any development.

The EGDI is a composite of three sub-indices — the **Online Service Index (OSI)**, the
**Telecommunications Infrastructure Index (TII)** and the **Human Capital Index (HCI)** — scored
0 to 1 and published as both a score and a rank of 193 member states.

> **Cite, don't absorb.** Index readings live here and on the source pages, dated and attributed
> to the edition that produced them. They are **not** promoted onto place or concept pages as
> current state — an EGDI reading measures a moment and a methodology, and fresher country-level
> evidence reaches the wiki via clippings. Where a place hub needs the number, it carries it
> dated ("189th of 193, 0.1785 — 2024 index"), never bare.

## Why it matters analytically

The EGDI is the single most-quoted external yardstick in this corpus: it is how governments
themselves frame progress, and how the trade press frames league tables. That makes it worth
holding as an object rather than a fact — its **methodology and rebasing** decide who "rises" and
"falls" as much as any policy does, and a country's movement between editions is frequently
reported as achievement without that caveat. Treat rank changes with particular care: a rank can
move because others moved.

## 2024 edition — readings held in this wiki

From *E-Government Survey 2024: Accelerating Digital Transformation for Sustainable Development*
(UN DESA), as reported 2024-09-23:

| Place | Score (2024) | Note |
|---|---|---|
| [[ZAF]] South Africa | **0.8616** | continental leader; one of only two African states above 0.75 |
| [[MUS]] Mauritius | **0.7506** | the other above 0.75 |
| [[TUN]] Tunisia | **0.6935** | leads North Africa; surpassed Seychelles this edition |
| [[MAR]] Morocco | **0.6841** | global top 100 |
| [[SYC]] Seychelles | **0.6773** | global top 100; ranked 92nd of 193 |
| [[EGY]] Egypt | **0.6699** | global top 100 |
| [[TCD]] Chad | **0.1785** | 189th of 193 |

Aggregates (2024): **global average 0.6382** (from 0.6102 in 2022); **African average 0.4247**
(from 0.4054). Africa's largest driver was the **TII, up 27.8%** (0.3546 → 0.4534). Regional
increases: Asia 7.7%, Africa 4.8%, the Americas and Oceania 4.1%, Europe 2.3%. African bands:
**19** countries at ≥0.5, of which only ZAF and MUS exceed 0.75; **17** high (0.50–0.75), **28**
medium (0.25–0.50), **7** low.
