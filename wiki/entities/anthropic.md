# Anthropic

US frontier-AI company; developer of the **Claude** family of models. Appears in this
wiki as the AI partner behind a South African **"sovereign AI"** deployment, not as a
general-market actor.

## Activity in this wiki

- **TrendAI partnership (announced April 2026).** Claude is embedded across [[trendai]]'s
  **Vision One** platform to power agentic workflows, automation and AI-native security
  operations (incl. TrendAI's Zero Day Initiative / Pwn2Own vulnerability research).
  Anthropic's Ash Alhashim (head of cyber-security GTM) framed it as "tilting the scales
  towards defenders." See [[tech.ai]].
- **Sovereign-AI angle (2026-07).** The deployment runs **Claude Opus 4.7** inside a
  **locally-governed South African data centre with its own data lake**, governed under
  SA law — positioned as the first credible implementation of "sovereign AI" a local CISO
  can point to, and as compliant-by-architecture with [[popia]] §72/§19 cross-border
  limits. Described as "phase one" of an African rollout (Johannesburg → Lagos, Nairobi,
  Cairo). See [[ZAF]], [[infra.store]], [[south-africa--gov-protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Anthropic's Claude to bolster data sovereignty in SA]].
- **Earlier Africa footprint (2024-03-06).** [[liquid-c2]] (a [[cassava-technologies]]
  business) partnered with Google Cloud and Anthropic to offer Anthropic's models via
  **Vertex AI** across Africa; Anthropic's models also feature in Cassava AI's
  **[[caimex]]** LLM exchange — and Claude is now one of the two models (with Google Gemini)
  offered through Cassava's **[[national-sovereign-cloud]]** Cloud Partner Programme for African
  governments (2026-03). Sources:
  [[2024-03-06 Liquid C2 partners with Google Cloud and Anthropic to bring advanced cloud, cyber security and generative AI capabilities to Africa]],
  [[2026-02-28 CAIMEx - Cassava AI]], [[2026-03-06 Cassava launches sovereign cloud for Africa's public sector]].
- **Gates Foundation partnership (2026-05-14).** A four-year, **$200m** partnership
  with the **[[gates-foundation]]** — grant funding, API credits and technical
  support — to build AI public goods across health, education and agriculture in
  low- and middle-income countries; deal [[gates-anthropic-partnership]], with
  illustrative use-cases including a farmer in Kenya and a health worker in
  Nigeria. See [[tech.ai]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-14 Making AI work for more people]].
- **Rwanda multi-sector government MoU (2026-02, Kigali).** Anthropic signed a **three-year MoU**
  with the **Rwanda** ([[RWA]]) government ([[anthropic-rwanda-mou]]) — its **first multi-sector
  government deal in Africa** — to embed Claude across the **health ministry** (cervical-cancer
  elimination, malaria/maternal mortality), give government developers **Claude Code + API access
  and training**, and extend an education partnership (building on a fall-2025 ALX deal with 2,000
  Claude Pro licences) deploying the **Chidi** Claude-powered learning companion across **eight
  African countries**. Analysts read it two ways: a real capacity lift, but also an **accountability
  vacuum** — the MoU is non-binding while the dependency is not, with no parliamentary, multilateral
  or civil-society review, and Anthropic becoming "load-bearing infrastructure" across sensitive
  public domains (the [[african-union]] Continental AI Strategy has not yet been applied to such
  commercial partnerships). Signed even as the US "Department of War" labelled Anthropic a
  "supply-chain risk" for refusing to drop military guardrails. See [[colonialism]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[capacity.training]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-04 U.S.-based AI firm Anthropic bags first multi-sector government deal in Africa despite policy pressures at home]],
  [[2026-03-17 Anthropic is Becoming the Backbone of Rwanda's Government. But Who is Accountable]],
  [[2026-03-04 Rwanda–Anthropic AI deal targets health, education gains]],
  [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]].

- **US foreign-access order imposed then reversed (Politico, 2026-07).** The Trump
  administration abruptly ordered restrictions on Anthropic's **foreign** model access, then
  **reversed** the order — a move that unnerved AI firms weighing Commerce's
  [[american-ai-exports-program]] and underscored the policy risk sitting over US AI exports to
  developing markets (a live concern given Anthropic's African government footprint). See
  [[geopol.usa]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-11 'Not where they hoped it'd be' Launch of Trump AI promotion program underwhelms]].
