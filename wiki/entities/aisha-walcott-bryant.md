**Aisha Walcott-Bryant** is the **head of Google Research Africa** (as of 2026-02) and the public lead behind [[google]]'s African speech-technology work, notably the **[[waxal]]** open-source voice dataset for African languages announced on 2 February 2026.

## Why she matters here

Walcott-Bryant is the authoring voice of Google's framing that African-language AI should be built by and for African institutions on their own terms. On the WAXAL launch she wrote:

> "This dataset provides the critical foundation for students, researchers, and entrepreneurs to build technology on their own terms, in their own languages, finally reaching over 100 million people."

That "on their own terms" framing — paired with WAXAL's model of African partners **retaining ownership** of the corpora they collect — is the substantive position she represents: Google as convenor/funder, African institutions ([[waxal]] partners Makerere University, University of Ghana, Digital Umuganda, AIMS) as owners of the underlying data. She heads the Google Research Africa group that coordinated the three-year, 21-language, 11,000+-hour effort.
