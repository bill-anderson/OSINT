# Anjola Adebowale

Anjola Adebowale is a data, AI and digital-transformation analyst who writes from an
**implementation** vantage — explicitly closer to data engineering and delivery than to
policy theory — publishing long-form commentary at *anjolaadebowale.substack.com*. Her
recurring concern is what technological change looks like when African institutions have
to absorb it in practice, and how governments can build durable foundations rather than
technology-specific stacks.

## What she argues in this wiki

- **"Dynamic tech stack" over "AI stack."** Governments should invest in the permanent
  layers — trust/identity, sovereign data, an orchestration/API layer, adaptive
  governance, and human absorption — that can absorb any future technology, rather than
  procuring for AI specifically. She benchmarks Africa's anchor economies (Nigeria, Egypt,
  South Africa, Kenya) plus Rwanda against that standard, contrasting Kenya's accidental
  M-Pesa-era foundations with Rwanda's deliberate, open, sequenced DPI
  ([[2026-06-12 Beyond the AI Stack Why Governments Need Dynamic Digital Infrastructure]]).
- **Sovereignty as leverage, not self-sufficiency.** Reading the Oxford Frontier AI Forum
  (May 2026), she maps where Africa already holds durable leverage — data diversity,
  data-protection regimes, open-source models — against the energy constraint underneath
  compute ambition, and frames the "middle power" position as where the continent could
  realistically aim ([[2026-06-01 Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate]]).
- **Reading vendor announcements for African terms of engagement.** Her walkthrough of
  Microsoft Build 2026 stresses that the real question is who controls the data, workflows
  and learning loops that run on top of hyperscaler platforms
  ([[2026-06-05 Microsoft Build 2026 What Caught My Attention and What It Means for Africa]]).

She also authored the two-part analysis of the CBN's June 2026 payments circular in
Nigeria's fintech regulatory reform (Writer A's cluster), applying the same
foundations-before-features lens to payment infrastructure.
