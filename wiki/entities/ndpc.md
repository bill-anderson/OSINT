# Nigeria Data Protection Commission (NDPC)

Nigeria's data-protection regulator, enforcing the **Nigeria Data Protection Act
2023**; National Commissioner/CEO **Dr Vincent Olatunji**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Meta-funded M-SIDP (2026-06).** After a **2025 court-approved settlement**
  with **[[meta]]**, the NDPC launched the two-year **Meta-Supported Initiatives
  for Data Protection (M-SIDP)** — Meta funding governance/research, DPO
  capacity-building and public-awareness work. Framed as a landmark case of a
  global platform funding compliance at an African regulator's demand. Deal:
  [[ndpc-meta-settlement]]. See [[NGA]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-09 Meta backs NDPC in landmark privacy initiative]].
- Named among the regulators stressing lawful, trust-centred processing in the
  Nigeria data-silos debate. Source:
  [[2026-06-17 Government data silos threaten Nigeria's AI ambitions]].

- **Regulatory harmonisation committee (2026-07).** Named — with [[ncc]] and [[nitda]] —
  to Minister [[bosun-tijani]]'s joint technical coordination committee developing a unified
  national digital-economy policy/governance framework (a response to converging telecoms /
  platform / AI / data-governance mandates). See [[gov.policy]], [[nigeria--gov-protect]].
  Source: [[2026-07-08 FG Halts Enforcement of New Regulations on Internet Platforms]].
- **Deepfake / AI enforcement coalition (2026-03).** The NDPC joined the Global Privacy Assembly's
  **International Enforcement Cooperation Working Group** (60+ regulators) against AI-driven deepfakes
  and synthetic media, pressing privacy-by-design, data minimisation, watermarking and traceability;
  Commissioner Olatunji said major data controllers must "demonstrate how their AI models collect,
  process and protect personal information — effectively treating algorithmic accountability like
  financial reporting," complementing [[bosun-tijani]]'s National AI strategy. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-03-04 Nigeria takes new measures to regulate AI]].
- **Independence critique.** The NDPC enforces the **[[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]]** but critics
  note the Act placed it **under ministerial control** — "a regulator that needs ministerial clearance
  before acting" is hardly independent — while enforcement of breaches (e.g. the alleged ~43m NIN-record
  exposure) rarely produces visible sanctions. On political processing, a lawyer flags party membership
  as **sensitive data**: the NDPC can issue advisories (its 2025 GAID applies across sectors) requiring
  lawful basis, genuine consent and DPIAs for high-risk party e-registration. Samiat Akande frames data
  protection as economic infrastructure — a "trust deficit" issue where legislation alone is
  insufficient (GDPR as the model). See [[gov.legislate]], [[nimc]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-27 Who Protects Nigeria's Digital Citizens Unmasking Accountability Gaps in Digital Rights Governance]],
  [[2026-02-02 NIN, Party e-registration and the NDPA 2023 When politics meets privacy (and why Nigerians should care)]],
  [[2026-03-04 Data Protection is the Next Frontier for Nigeria's Digital Economy, Says Samiat]].

- **INEC privacy-policy suit (2026-07).** [[digital-rights-lawyers-initiative]] and lawyer
  Solomon Etisang sued [[inec]] (Federal High Court, Yenagoa) for failing to publish a
  website privacy policy — an alleged breach of the [[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]]
  (ss.24/27) the NDPC enforces, following FIJ's Oct-2025 report and a May-2025 voter-record
  leak. A civil-society test of NDPA enforcement pursued through the courts rather than the
  NDPC's own channels; distinct from [[ndpc-meta-settlement]]. See [[gov.legislate]],
  [[nigeria--gov-protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 FIJ Showed That INEC Broke Data Law. Digital Rights Lawyers Sue the Commission 10 Months Later]].
- **Cited as enforcement precedent (2026-07).** Reporting on the Tinubu-ordered [[fccpc]]
  probe of [[meta]]/[[google]]/X and GenAI firms cites the NDPC's prior **₦7.2bn** privacy
  penalties as precedent — the data-protection flank to a competition-law action. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Why Nigeria Is Investigating Meta, Google, X and AI Firms]].

- **MultiChoice fine — the NDPC's largest (announced 2025-07-06).** ₦**766,242,500** (~US$501,340)
  on **[[multichoice-nigeria]]** for breaching the [[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]], following an
  investigation opened **Q2 2024**: privacy violations against **subscribers and non-subscribers**
  alike, **illegal cross-border transfer** of Nigerian personal data, and processing found "patently
  intrusive, unfair, unnecessary and disproportionate" contrary to **s.37 of the 1999 Constitution**.
  Imposed expressly **"for want of cooperation"** after directed remediation was judged
  unsatisfactory — the sanction endpoint of the NDPC's remediation-first model. Statement signed by
  **Babatunde Bamigboye**, Head, Legal, Enforcement & Regulations; National Commissioner **Vincent
  Olatunji** ordered all MultiChoice data-collection outlets investigated. **Payment / challenge
  status not established as of 2026-07-20**, and the NDPC did **not** answer FIJ's 2025-07-09 FOI
  request for the detailed findings within the FOI Act's statutory window — an enforcement-
  transparency gap on the Commission's own flagship penalty. Distinct from the
  [[ndpc-meta-settlement]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[nigeria--gov-protect]].
  Sources: [[2025-07-06-ndpc-multichoice-fine-statement]],
  [[2026-06-01 Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate]] (analyst note citing it as a marker of
  enforcement teeth in the AI-sovereignty debate).
