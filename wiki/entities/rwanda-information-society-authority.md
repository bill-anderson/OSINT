The **Rwanda Information Society Authority (RISA)** is the Rwandan government agency responsible for implementing the country's digital government and ICT infrastructure agenda. It is the lead agency behind Rwanda's national DPI push and the convenor of the country's DPI events and coordination.

## Role and leadership

- **CEO: Antoine Sebera** (as of 2026-03). Sebera frames DPI as "roads and electricity for the digital economy" and stresses it must be built collaboratively across government, private sector, civil society and development partners — no single institution can deliver it.
- RISA organised Rwanda's **first DPI Day** on 9 March 2026 with Access to Finance Rwanda and the Ministry of ICT and Innovation, where the national [[rwanda-dpi-strategy]] was unveiled.
- RISA's mandate covers interoperability of government platforms, digital-service delivery and the data-exchange layer that underpins Rwanda's AI-hub ambitions.

RISA works alongside the **Rwanda Centre for Digital Public Infrastructure** (CDPI, established within RISA in **February 2025**, director Sharon Umunyana), which handles foundational-system development and capacity-building, and alongside [[nida-rwanda]] (the National Identification Agency) on the digital-identity component — a digital ID rollout was planned for June 2026. On the data-governance layer, RISA operates the platform side of the 2025 **National Data Sharing Policy** and **seconds staff into the [[nisr]] Data Governance Unit** (a NISR-governance / RISA-platform split). Source: [[2025-06-09 Rwanda charts a new course for government data sharing]].

*(Leadership, dated: the DPGA case study (Jan 2026) names then-CEO **Innocent Bagamba Muhizi**; **Antoine Sebera** is named CEO as of March 2026 — a leadership change over the period.)*

On the payments layer, RISA (with MINICT) is named among the government partners behind **[[ekash]]**, Rwanda's national instant/interoperable payment system launched **5 December 2025** (powered by [[rswitch-rwanda]]) — the operational payments building block of the [[rwanda-dpi-strategy]]. Provenance: the launch account is by an RSwitch insider. Source: [[2025-12-05 From Access to Empowerment Introducing Rwanda's Instant Payment System — eKash]].

RISA operates within the ICT policy sphere associated with Rwanda's digital-transformation leadership, including minister [[paula-ingabire]] (Ministry of ICT and Innovation).

The agency reads through a **sovereignty** lens: Rwanda's DPI model emphasises open-source digital public goods and sovereign control of core national systems (see [[dpga]]).
