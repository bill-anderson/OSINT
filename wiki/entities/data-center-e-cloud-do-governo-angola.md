# Data Center e Cloud do Governo (Camama national data centre)

Angola's **sovereign national data centre** — the physical anchor of its digital-sovereignty
strategy. Owned by **[[infosi]]** under **[[minttics]]**, sited between **Camama and Kilamba**,
Luanda, and inaugurated by President **[[joao-lourenco]]** on **28 April 2026**. It is the base
platform for the **[[nuvem-nacional-de-angola]]** (Government Cloud).

## Specifications (as inaugurated, 2026-04)

- **6,225 m²**; built in **~10 months**; **Tier III** (Uptime Institute).
- **208 racks / 1,040 kW** live; **modular** design of **12 containers/modules** of ~28 racks each,
  giving a **~336-rack** design capacity; two-storey pre-fabricated main building; 24/7 operation.
- **Cost: US$89m** (Angop, three accounts) / **~US$90m** (Novo Jornal) — kept dated, not reconciled.
- A **secondary government data centre** replicates all data held in the primary.
- Interconnects the five most tech-intensive ministries — **Finance, Telecommunications, Interior,
  Education, Health** — by fibre.

The build follows the **[[raxio-group]]** carrier-neutral Tier III facility (Cacuaco) that opened
**six months earlier** — the state build layered over the commercial estate.

## Framing — repatriation and sovereignty

Officials framed the centre explicitly as **"autonomy in the storage of documents"**: Angolans'
**passports and education certificates**, previously held by foreign entities or platforms, now
sit under **national jurisdiction** (INFOSI DG André Pedro). Minister **[[mario-oliveira]]** called
it "a clear affirmation of Angola's digital sovereignty." A **progressive and mandatory migration**
of public services onto the Nuvem Nacional follows commissioning. TechAfrica News framed the build
as UAE-origin financed (tagged `geopol.gulf`), though the account's body does not itself substantiate
the UAE link — a provenance point, not a confirmed fact.
