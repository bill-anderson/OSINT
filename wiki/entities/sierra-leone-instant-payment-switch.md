# Sierra Leone National Instant Payment Switch

Sierra Leone's real-time, interoperable retail-payment system, operated under the
**Bank of Sierra Leone (BSL)** and delivered with **World Bank / FASTT** technical
assistance. It enables instant transfers between banks and mobile-money operators.

## Timeline

- **April 2023** — national payment switch launched; ATM and point-of-sale
  interoperability, then bank-card interoperability rolled out (origin phase).
- **13 February 2025** — **instant-payment phase launched** in Freetown (VP Mohamed Juldeh
  Jalloh officiating). Real-time interoperable transfers between banks and mobile money;
  **seven banks and two MNOs** connected at launch, with full integration mandated by
  **1 April 2025**. Providers connected include **Afrimoney (Africell)** and **Orange Money
  (Orange)**. Source: [[2025-02-14 Sierra Leone Launches National Instant Payment System]].
- **Planned phase 3** — integration with international payment gateways so Sierra
  Leonean-issued cards work abroad.

## Impact so far

- The switch is cited by the World Bank as accelerating financial inclusion; the flagship
  social-assistance programme is shifting to digital payments, and a recent **emergency cash
  transfer paid 35,000 households by mobile phone — a national first (2025)**. Source:
  [[2025-12-09 Unlocking opportunity - How digital ID can transform lives in Sierra Leone]].

## Context figures

- As of 31 Dec 2023: **8.2m mobile-money accounts (1.7m active)**, 22.3m bank accounts;
  BSL estimated **29% of adults** had a financial/mobile-money account (2022).
