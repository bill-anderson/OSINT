- **Parties:** the [[european-union]] (Team Europe) and the Federal Government of
  Nigeria, with implementing finance from the [[ebrd]] and the European
  Investment Bank (EIB via the Bank of Industry).
- **What:** a **€290 million** investment package across **seven new operations**
  in digitalisation, health, agricultural value chains and migration management,
  under the EU **[[global-gateway]]** strategy.
- **When / where:** announced Monday 24 March 2026, in the margins of the **8th
  EU–Nigeria Ministerial Dialogue** in **Abuja**, co-chaired by Nigeria's Foreign
  Minister **Yusuf Maitama Tuggar** and EU High Representative/Commission
  Vice-President **Kaja Kallas**.
- **Value:** €290m, "to be immediately mobilised."
- **Status:** announced; framed as underpinning "renewed political ambition"
  under Nigeria's Renewed Hope agenda.

## Breakdown

- **Digitalisation — €131m.** Upgrading digital connectivity and enhancing
  services and skills. Together with the [[ebrd]], the EU will contribute to the
  roll-out of **90,000 km of fibre optic cable** to reach unserved Nigerians
  (the same national backbone as Project BRIDGE — see
  [[national-digital-farmers-registry-nigeria]]). Also funds **secure,
  interoperable digital public infrastructure and data-exchange systems**, and —
  within Nigeria's **3 Million Technical Talent (3MTT)** programme — large-scale
  training/employment and a technical and vocational education and training
  (TVET) programme (`dpi.exchange`, `capacity.training`).
- **Agriculture — €86m.** Easier access to finance for local companies in the
  **cocoa and dairy** value chains, plus support for climate-smart agriculture
  policy; delivered partly via a new **EIB credit line** through the Bank of
  Industry for companies and smallholder farmers.
- **Migration — €16m.** Reintegration of returning migrants and strengthening the
  fight against human smugglers and traffickers.

(The three named tranches total €233m; the €290m headline covers all seven
operations, including health, which the statement flags but does not itemise.)

## Stated positions

- **Kaja Kallas:** "In the current geopolitical context, the European Union is
  keen to enhance its partnership with Nigeria," aligning with the Renewed Hope
  agenda.
- **Jozef Síkela** (Commissioner for International Partnerships): the Global
  Gateway investments create "new quality infrastructures, sustainable jobs and
  long-term economic opportunities" for Nigerians "but also create new
  opportunities for Europe."
- EU delegation spokesperson **Modestus Chukwulaka** detailed the digital and
  agriculture components.

## Significance

The package is the flagship instance in this batch of the EU's [[global-gateway]]
model in Nigeria — explicitly framed in geopolitical terms ("in the current
geopolitical context") as Europe competing for influence via infrastructure
finance. It converges with the [[nigeria-finland-digital-mou]] (Team Europe
digital-public-services track) on the same fibre backbone and data-exchange
priorities.
