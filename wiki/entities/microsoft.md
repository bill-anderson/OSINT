# Microsoft

US hyperscaler; a central actor in the African cloud/AI build-out and — via the
CLOUD Act and its **[[g42]]** partnership — a recurring case study in the limits
of African data sovereignty.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Microsoft–G42 Kenya data centre stalled (2026-07-09).** The **$1bn**
  Olkaria project ([[microsoft-g42-kenya-datacenter]]) missed its May 2026 launch;
  its **1 GW** draw is a third of Kenya's grid and Treasury clearance failed. See
  [[KEN]], [[infra.capacity]], [[infra.energy]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-09 Microsoft and G42 data center in Kenya stalled due to lack of power capacity]],
  [[2026-05-21 Africa Data Center Investment 2026 The $4.5B Race and the Power Problem]].
- **SA public-sector skills (2026-06-19).** Microsoft SA placed **64
  Power-Platform-certified graduates** across **11 government departments** under
  its B-BBEE **Equity Equivalent Investment Programme (EEIP)**, funding salaries
  through June 2028. See [[ZAF]], [[capacity.training]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-19 Microsoft SA secures digital talent for govt]].
- **SA expansion — $329m / R5.4bn (2026-04).** Microsoft confirmed a **$329m
  (R5.4bn)** SA cloud/AI expansion by end-2027 — land for future DC growth,
  power/water readiness, added Azure capacity — on top of a prior **R20.4bn /
  $1.2bn**; paired with an AI-skills push (skill 1m South Africans, fund 50,000
  certification exams; Jan 2026 SABC+ deal) and local LLM partner **Lelapa AI**.
  Nvidia GPUs in these SA DCs were cited as part of the buildout behind SA's
  data-centre-as-critical-infrastructure shift. See [[microsoft-sa-2026-expansion]],
  [[ZAF]], [[infra.store]], [[capacity.training]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-17 Microsoft picks South Africa for $329 million expansion in cloud infrastructure and AI training]],
  [[2026-04-15 Microsoft secures land for future South Africa data centre growth]],
  [[2026-02-25 South Africa puts data centres on par with energy, ports in big policy shift]].
- **North West mobile digital lab (2026-05-27).** With [[liquid-c2]] (a
  [[cassava-technologies]] business) and SA's DCDT, launched a solar-powered
  mobile digital lab at the Royal Bafokeng Institute, Phokeng, running a 3-month
  Microsoft Power Platform cohort (certifications, Copilot / AI fluency) —
  building on the Mthatha centre (515 trained, 96% pass, 149 placed). See
  [[ZAF]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-27 Liquid, Microsoft unveil mobile digital lab for North West youth]].
- **Nigeria onshoring pressure (2026-07-10).** Azure named among the foreign
  hyperscalers Nigerian banks/fintechs must migrate off under the [[cbn]]
  local-hosting mandate (transactional data onshore by **2027-01-01**); Nigeria
  is Africa's 2nd DC market. See [[NGA]], [[infra.store]], [[gov.protect]].
  Source: [[2026-07-10 Nigeria tests data sovereignty as foreign tech firms facing probe]].
  A parallel analysis of the CBN directive (which cites prior **Microsoft–FG** cloud
  collaboration and Nigeria's National Cloud Computing Policy) argues the mandate conflates
  data localisation with data protection and urges encryption/hybrid-hosting over pure
  geography. Source: [[2026-07-10 CBN's Data Localisation Directive Puts Nigerian Fintechs In A Cloud Dilemma]].
- **OpenAI partnership restructured (2026-04-28, global context).** OpenAI ended
  its Microsoft exclusivity — Azure stays primary through 2032, but OpenAI can now
  sell models on AWS/Google Cloud and Microsoft drops its revenue-share to OpenAI.
  See [[openai]], [[amazon-web-services]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-28 OpenAI Breaks Free Of Microsoft, Expands AWS Partnership]].
- **Frontier Company (2026-07-03, global context).** A new **$2.5bn** AI-services
  business with 6,000 embedded specialists; model-agnostic (OpenAI, Anthropic,
  Microsoft AI, open source). No Africa-specific content — filed as global context
  on Microsoft's enterprise-AI push. Source:
  [[2026-07-03 Microsoft Launches Frontier Company with $2.5 Billion AI Transformation Investment]].

- **Microsoft Elevate — SA AI-skills push (2026-03).** Interview profiling
  **[[tiara-pathon]]**, SA National AI Skills Director at **[[microsoft-elevate]]**,
  positioned as the "connective tissue" linking government, industry and
  communities on AI literacy — named partners SABC+, Civic AI and Ikamva Digital
  (with GIZ). See [[ZAF]], [[capacity.training]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-27 Tiara Pathon on Turning AI Literacy From a Privilege Into a Public Good]].
- **Overland fibre pivot (2026-03).** Microsoft — alongside [[google]], [[meta]]
  and [[amazon-web-services]] — is moving beyond subsea to build ultra-high-speed
  **overland fibre** across Africa's interior with local players. In Ethiopia,
  [[wingu-africa]]'s new in-country **Wingu Cloud Exchange** offers a
  data-residency alternative that runs hybrid with **Azure**/AWS. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[infra.store]], [[ETH]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-16 Why Google, Amazon and Meta are going overland in Africa]],
  [[2026-04-01 Wingu Africa debuts cloud exchange platform in Ethiopia]].

- **Partner2Connect pledge — Kenya rural hubs (2026-07).** Pledged to the [[itu]]
  [[partner2connect]] coalition to connect **450+ rural/underserved community hubs in
  Kenya** via satellite-enabled infrastructure integrated with **Azure Space** (alongside
  ~$18bn AI/cloud investment in Australia). See [[KEN]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 ITU's Partner2Connect crosses $100 billion in global connectivity pledges]].

- **Continental AI-skilling target + MTN Copilot push (2026-03).** As part of the US–China AI
  contest on the continent, Microsoft (MEA president **Naim Yazbeck**) reaffirmed a plan to
  **train 3 million Africans in AI by 2026** and a partnership with **[[mtn-group]]** to roll out
  Microsoft 365 and **Copilot** to MTN's ~**300 million** subscribers — even as Chinese
  **[[deepseek]]** captures 11–14% of African chatbot usage. See [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.training]],
  [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12 China's DeepSeek gives US tech giants a run for their money as Microsoft and others expand AI push in Africa]].
- **Nigeria AI skilling — TeKnowledge Phase 2 (2026-03-09).** **[[teknowledge]]** named delivery
  partner for **Phase 2** of Microsoft's **AI National Skilling Initiative** in **[[NGA]] Nigeria**,
  committing to train **10,000** participants (youth, women, developers) via universities and the
  NYSC. Phase 1 (2025) reached 50,000+ with 3,000+ earning Microsoft AI certifications, coordinated
  with the 3MTT initiative. See [[capacity.training]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-09 Over 10,000 Nigerians to Receive Advanced AI Training Through TeKnowledge-Microsoft Partnership]].

- **Build 2026 — vertically-owned AI platform (Anjola Adebowale, 2026-06-05).**
  Adebowale reads Microsoft Build 2026 (Microsoft Discovery, the in-house **MAI**
  model family incl. **MAI-Thinking-1** at 35B active params with no third-party
  distillation, **Frontier Tuning**, the **Scout** agent, **Project Solara**
  hardware, and the **Majorana 2** quantum chip pulling a commercial-quantum
  target to 2029) as Microsoft assembling a **full-stack, vertically-owned AI
  platform** — and warns Africa's risk is engaging on terms (data, workflows,
  learning loops) set by others. See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.store]],
  [[geopol.usa]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-05 Microsoft Build 2026 What Caught My Attention and What It Means for Africa]].
- **Named in the AI-sovereignty debate (2026-06).** Adebowale uses the stalled
  G42 Kenya facility as a "sovereignty signal"
  ([[2026-06-01 Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate]]); Rebecca Mbaya
  critiques the **Nadella/Sunak (Davos 2026)** "sovereignty as portfolio
  risk-management" framing and the UAE–Microsoft model as context-stripping for
  Africa ([[2026-06-24 Does AI Sovereignty Mean Controlling the Keys, or Owning the Building]]);
  Microsoft's prior FG-cloud collaboration recurs in the sovereign-stack argument
  ([[2026-06-12 Beyond the AI Stack Why Governments Need Dynamic Digital Infrastructure]]).

## Reads

- `sovereignty` / `colonialism`: [[2026-06-08 The Promise and Limits of African Data Sovereignty]] uses Microsoft France's June 2025 sworn admission — it "cannot
  guarantee" data sovereignty against a US court order — as the anchor case; the
  Kenya "trusted data zone" runs on Azure with Microsoft holding a minority G42
  stake and board seat. Enforcement stops at the subsidiary–parent boundary.
- `sovereignty` / `colonialism`: [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]]
  lists Microsoft (with Google, Nvidia, Meta) among the US firms four African
  economies' draft AI strategies name as a dependence risk; the stalled G42 Kenya
  DC is the case-in-point. The same concentration underpins the "frugal AI"
  counter-movement — Microsoft Research's own data shows AI adoption grew ~2x
  faster in wealthy than in low/middle-income countries, and US/Chinese firms run
  >90% of AI data centres ([[2026-04-02 Nations priced out of Big AI are building with frugal models]],
  [[2026-03-12 Western AI models 'fail spectacularly' in farms and forests abroad]]).
