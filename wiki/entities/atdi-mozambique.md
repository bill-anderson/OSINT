# ATDI — Digital Transformation and Innovation Agency of Mozambique

ATDI is [[MOZ]]'s digital-transformation agency, created in 2026 and chaired since March 2026 by Adilson dos Santos Cousin Gomes. It leads Mozambique's national DPI and [[dpi.id]] agenda, framed around "technological sovereignty" and reduced vendor dependence.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Three-year MoU with Ethiopia (2026-04-28).** ATDI signed a three-year MoU with Ethiopia's National ID Program to build digital ID and DPI capacity (see [[mozambique-ethiopia-dpi-mou]], [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]), explicitly pursuing technological [[sovereignty]] and lower dependence on external vendors. Source: [[2026-04-28 Mozambique taps Ethiopia's digital ID expertise in DPI partnership]].
- **National digital-government overhaul (2026-02-21).** President Daniel Chapo, opening Mozambique's
  first national digital-transformation conference (Maputo), announced a plan to **digitise all
  government services** — modelled on **Kenya and Rwanda** — via a **Multi-Sector Technical
  Commission** drafting an interoperability roadmap by **mid-2026** and a new **Ministry of
  Communications and Digital Transformation**. Framed as governance overhaul over infrastructure
  ("no technological islands within the state"); Mozambique ranks **177/193** on the 2024 UN
  E-Government Index. This is the national programme within which [[atdi-mozambique]] runs the DPI/
  [[dpi.id]] agenda. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-21 Mozambique Launches Digital Government Overhaul, Looking to Kenya and Rwanda as Models]].
