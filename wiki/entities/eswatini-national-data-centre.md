# Eswatini National Data Centre (NDC)

**Eswatini's national data centre**, an entity of the [[rstp]], offering colocation and
cloud-style services to public and private tenants and positioned as the country's
e-government hosting platform. See [[SWZ]], [[infra.store]].

> **Provenance caveat — capture is not endorsement.** The wiki's only source is the
> operator's own facility page: marketing-inflected, undated apart from a 2021 CMS
> signal (held at year precision, `date_source: proxy`), and disclosing **no capacity,
> tenant, uptime-tier or utilisation figures**. There is no independent verification of
> any claim below, and nothing here establishes that the facility carries meaningful
> national workload.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Stated mission.** Provide ICT services to public and private sectors on modern,
  agile, secured technology; serve as the e-government development and implementation
  platform; support research and high-performance computing (HPC); participate in
  regional and international ICT initiatives. Stated vision includes a scalable
  environment for web hosting, secure data repositories and **national payment
  gateways** — an ambition adjacent to [[eswatini-payment-switch]], with no source
  linking the two. See [[dpi.govtech]].
- **Claimed services.** Platform / Infrastructure / Software as a Service; remote-hands;
  colocation.
- **Claimed facility characteristics.** Carrier-neutral connectivity with two diverse
  service-provider feeds; redundant power feeds and **four 500 KVA diesel generators**
  with automatic restart; DCIM, VESDA smoke detection, water-leak detection and
  surveillance; 24/7 security, biometric authentication for server-farm access, 42U
  smart-row racks, off-site backup for disaster recovery. See [[infra.energy]],
  [[infra.cybersec]].
- **Context.** The [[eswatini-digital-readiness-assessment]] (2024) separately called
  for investment in **scalable, energy-efficient data centres** — an assessment that
  does not name the NDC, and which sits oddly against a facility already claiming that
  role. Source: [[2024-12-10 Unveiling Eswatini's Digital Path]].
