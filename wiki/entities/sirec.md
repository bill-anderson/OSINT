# SIREC (Integrated System of Citizen Registers and Statistics, Guinea-Bissau)

Guinea-Bissau's new civil-registration digitization platform (Sistema Integrado de Registos e Estatísticas do Cidadão), run by the Civil Registration Office of the Ministry of Justice ([[dpi.registry]]). It is the foundation for an integrated digital-identity system in a country where birth registration is **below 50%** (~46% of under-fives). Launched in 2025 with UNDP support (officer training on enrollment kits), it is being developed alongside a UNDP "Building National ID" project (Swiss-financed) that scans and digitizes the paper civil-registry books. The government is exploring integration with the health and education sectors. See [[GNB]], [[guinea-bissau--dpi-id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **SIREC rollout + civil-registry digitization (2025).** [[dpi.id]] Civil Registration head Raul Gomes (MoJ) described SIREC as the digitization backbone; UNDP trained officers, and health technicians are being trained for facility birth registration. In parallel the UNDP "Building National ID" project sets up a digitization centre and software to digitize births/deaths/marriages.
