# Central Bank of Kenya (CBK)

Kenya's central bank and the regulator of the National Payments System — overseeing the
KEPSS (RTGS) large-value rail, cheque clearing, and mobile/instant-payment infrastructure
including M-PESA ([[safaricom]]) and [[pesalink]]. A recurring monetary and payments-policy
actor in Kenya's digital-finance story.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Cheque reform over abolition (as of 2026-01).** In its commentary on the National
  Payments System, CBK has **resisted an outright cheque phase-out**, arguing that reform
  (faster clearing, safeguards) rather than abolition protects financial inclusion —
  bucking a continental trend toward ending cheques (Burkina Faso ended public-entity cheque
  use Oct 2025; Zambia stops accepting them June 2026; Seychelles and Ghana tightening
  rules). Cheques still move ~**$17bn/year** in Kenya, clearing within a day (down from five
  in 2011) and capped at $7,750. See [[dpi.pay]], [[KEN]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-13 The rise of cheques as Kenya's preferred payment method]].

- **Kenya Faster Payments in development (analysis, 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki notes CBK's
  **Faster Payments System is in development** — placing Kenya among the African markets building
  central-bank instant rails (alongside TIPS, GhIPSS, PayShap) whose spread, he argues, turns
  payments into a low-margin utility and shifts durable value to credit, data and compliance. His
  broader thesis reads M-PESA's Kenyan dominance as "Ecosystem Debt." Analyst framing, attributed
  to Kariuki. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]].
