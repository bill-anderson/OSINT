# IriTech

**An iris-biometrics company** — supplier of the **IriShield BK binocular scanner** used in
[[nira-uganda]]'s upgraded registration kits for [[ndaga-muntu]]. Its account of Uganda is
**vendor-sourced — attribute** — but it carries two facts held nowhere else in this wiki.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Iris in Uganda's kits (2026-03-02).** NIRA rolled out **more than 5,665** biometric
  registration kits to enroll residents to Ndaga Muntu under its **2024–2026 campaign**, as
  Uganda attempts to reach **33 million people** registered. The kits collect fingerprint
  biometrics **and iris** via the **IriShield BK binocular scanner**. (The "more than
  5,665" figure supersedes the *5,680 planned* reported in 2024, with rounding — not a
  conflict.) See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-02 IriTech provides iris biometrics for Uganda's upgraded registration kits]].
- **Why iris was added: over 1 million fingerprint failures.** **More than 1 million
  Ugandans had difficulty registering their fingerprint biometrics**, according to **NIRA
  figures cited by IriTech** — the stated reason for introducing iris, to better serve
  **elderly people, manual labourers and others**. IriTech adds that iris makes the system
  highly resilient against identity fraud. **This figure is held nowhere else.** See
  [[include.divides]], [[asamo-hellen-grace]]. Source: as above.
- **MOSIP's first completed brownfield implementation.** IriTech states that **NIRA has
  migrated 28 million biometric records to the [[mosip]] digital ID platform, in the
  open-source platform's first completed brownfield implementation** — a quotable
  characterisation held nowhere else, and an independent corroboration of the 28m figure
  reported by NIRA on 2026-02-11. Source: as above.
- **Credentials claimed (company account — attribute).** NIST testing; India's **STQC** and
  **BIS** certifications; **MOSIP L0 compliance**. The **IriShield-USB BK212U** was **one
  of the first scanners certified to MOSIP's MACP Framework**, assessed by **BixeLab**. The
  scanner provides on-board processing and liveness detection in a *"compact yet rugged"*
  design for remote rural areas. See [[gov.standards]], [[geopol.india]]. Source: as
  above.
