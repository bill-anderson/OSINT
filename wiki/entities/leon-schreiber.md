# Leon Schreiber

**Leon Schreiber** is South Africa's **Minister of Home Affairs** in the Government of National Unity and the political driver of the [[home-affairs-south-africa]] digital-transformation programme branded **"Home Affairs @ home."** He has become the most visible ministerial voice in the country's digital-identity and population-register modernisation push, framing the shift from the fraud-prone green ID book to the Smart ID and a future digital ID as a matter of both service delivery and national security.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Smart ID record (as of 2026-01-29).** Announced that DHA issued a record **4,002,964 smart IDs** in 2025 (+17% on 2024), crediting the repair of the **Online Verification Service (OVS)** and the population register, and previewing a bank-branch rollout. Source: [[2026-01-28 Home Affairs sets new record by delivering over four million Smart IDs in 2025]].
- **OVS fee-hike fight (as of 2026-01-27).** Defended the Home Affairs regulation raising the OVS ID-verification fee from 15c to **ZAR 10** per real-time check (+~6,500%), against a High Court review filed by the telco/bank body **[[act-south-africa]]**. He argued the decade-old 15c fee was unsustainable, caused >50% system-failure rates, and that the new revenue funds an **"Intelligent Population Register"** underpinning the digital ID. Source: [[2026-01-27 SA's Telcos, Banks At War With Govt Over 6,500% ID Verification Fee Hike]].
- **Digital ID timeline slippage.** First said (April 2025) the digital ID policy would "shortly" go to Cabinet; as of Jan 2026 the policy remained **under development** and unapproved, putting the [[mymzansi]] roadmap's Q4-FY2025 functional-ID target out of reach. Source: [[2026-01-28 Bad news about South Africa's newest ID book plan]].

He also launched the first live bank-branch Smart ID partnership (Capitec, Orange Farm, 2026-03-09) documented on [[home-affairs-south-africa]].
