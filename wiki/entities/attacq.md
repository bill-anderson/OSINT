# Attacq

**A South African real-estate developer — the developer of Waterfall City in Johannesburg — and
[[vantage]]'s 50:50 joint-venture partner on Phase II of its JNB1 data-centre campus.** Attacq is an
instance of a pattern worth tracking: **property capital entering the data-centre build as landlord
rather than operator**, taking the shell and leaving the technical risk to the operator.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The Vantage joint venture (2025-03-11).** Attacq and Vantage partnered in a **50:50 joint
  venture** to build a data centre forming the **first half of Phase II** of Vantage's South African
  campus at Waterfall City. The facility spans **10,363 m²** over **two storeys** with **four data
  halls**; construction under way, **completion expected Q1 2026**. Source:
  [[2025-03-11 Vantage partners with Attacq for Phase II of 80MW campus in Jo'burg, South Africa]].
- **Shell only, on a 20-year lease.** The investment for the **data-centre shell** is estimated at
  **R628 million (~$34m)**, **backed by a 20-year lease**, and **Attacq is expected to partner on
  the shell only** — the structural point: Attacq supplies the building and the balance-sheet
  duration; Vantage supplies the data centre. Source: same.
- **Waterfall City as the host estate.** Attacq is the developer of Waterfall City, whose other
  occupants include **Pragma, Nokia and Siemens Energy** — the JNB1 campus sits inside an existing
  managed commercial estate rather than on a standalone industrial site. Source: same.
- **The campus it feeds.** Phase II extends Vantage's **JNB1**: **30 acres**, **three facilities**,
  **60,000 m²**, **80 MW at full buildout** (2025-03), following the **16 MW JNB11** completed in
  **2022**. Source: same.
