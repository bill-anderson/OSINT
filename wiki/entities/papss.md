# Pan-African Payment and Settlement System (PAPSS)

Continental cross-border payment-and-settlement infrastructure, operated by
**[[afreximbank]]** and positioned under the [[afcfta]] agenda as the rails that let money
"move as freely as goods and services" across Africa.

## Activity in this wiki

- **AfCFTA Digital Trade Forum (2026-07, Lagos).** Cited as already reducing the cost and
  friction of intra-African payments, and as a core building block (with harmonized
  e-signatures and mutual recognition of digital identity) of the **digital single
  market**. See [[afcfta]], [[gov.regional]], [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Nigeria Takes AfCFTA Council Chair as Digital Forum Charts Roadmap for Implementation]].
- **Afreximbank payments suite (2026-05-30, Egypt).** [[afreximbank]] showcased PAPSS as the
  flagship of a wider suite — with **PAPSSCARD** (a continent-wide payment card) and the
  **African Currency Marketplace** — enabling local-currency settlement to cut US-dollar
  dependence. In Kenya, PAPSS has integrated with the **Pesalink** network, extending
  real-time cross-border reach. PAPSS CEO Mike Ogbalu III; Afreximbank president Benedict
  Oramah. See [[dpi.pay]], [[KEN]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-30 Afreximbank Banks on Digital Payments to Unlock Africa's Trillion-Dollar Trade Potential]].
- **Onafriq West Africa trade rail (2026-02).** PAPSS and [[onafriq]] launched a wallet-based
  **Nigeria→Ghana** outbound-payment pilot fully in Naira (CBN-approved, six months from 1
  Dec), bridging bank and mobile-money silos to support [[afcfta]]; PAPSS network cited at
  **160+ commercial banks / 400m+ accounts across 19 countries**. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[XWA]], [[NGA]], [[GHA]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-04 PAPSS, Onafriq launch new trade rail for West Africa]].
- **Ghana interoperability & PAPSS reach (2026-02).** [[bank-of-ghana]] cited PAPSS as
  extending Ghana's domestic mobile-money interoperability (value +87% to GHc5.8bn, ~29m
  transactions in 2025) to cross-border local-currency settlement; Ghana also runs fintech
  passporting with Rwanda. See [[GHA]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-05 Ghana Mobile Money Interoperability Nearly Doubles in 2025]].
- **Pesalink joins as Technical Connectivity Provider (2026, Kenya).** [[pesalink]]
  linked into PAPSS as a **Technical Connectivity Provider**, wiring **80+** Kenyan
  banks, fintechs and SACCOs into PAPSS's 160+ member banks for instant
  local-currency settlement — the **first time PAPSS piloted a national switch** for
  transaction termination. See [[KEN]], [[dpi.pay]], [[afcfta]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-02 Pesalink and PAPSS link networks for African payments]].
- **Bank of Ghana payments-overhaul call (2026, African Prosperity Dialogue).**
  [[bank-of-ghana]] (2nd Deputy Gov. Matilda Asante-Asiedu) pressed PAPSS as the fix
  for costly rails: intra-African payment costs run **7–10%** vs ~3% globally, and
  **>80%** of inter-African payments still route through correspondent banks abroad,
  costing ~**$5.3bn/yr** across a **1.5bn-person / ~$2.8tn** [[afcfta]] market. See
  [[GHA]], [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-05 Bank of Ghana Calls for Payment System Overhaul to Unlock AfCFTA Trade]].
- **BEAC / Central Africa accession (2026-07-11).** The Bank of Central African States
  (**[[beac]]**, the CEMAC regional central bank) officially joined PAPSS, extending the
  continental rail into **Central Africa** ([[XCA]]) beyond its West/East African cores.
  (Lede-only source; accession terms unconfirmed.) See [[XCA]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-11 BEAC joins PAPSS and accelerates cross-border payments integration in Central Africa]].
- **Nigeria's H1 2025 dip — a narrow metric, not a counter-signal (REV-002 resolved 2026-07-16).**
  CBN's Financial Stability Report (30 Jun 2025) shows **Nigeria's** reported PAPSS flows **fell 53%
  to ₦5.6bn** in H1'25 (from ₦11.97bn) with transactions **−29% to 3,246**. The drop is **real, not
  an FX artefact** — the transaction *count* fell independently and the naira was flat-to-stronger
  over the period, so the USD decline (~52–55%) tracks the naira one. But ₦5.6bn is only **~US$3.7m
  for a half-year** — far too small to be Nigeria's *total* PAPSS throughput given Nigeria is the top
  participant (22+ banks) on a rail now spanning ~28 countries (post-**[[beac]]**/CEMAC accession). It
  is almost certainly a **narrow CBN line item**, not a proxy for whether Nigeria is scaling on PAPSS:
  local contraction on this metric and continental scaling **coexist rather than contradict**. Two
  open caveats — the press disputes the **base period** (H1'24 vs H2'24), and **no H2 2025 figure
  exists yet** (watch the H2 2025 CBN FSR). In the same window **[[papsscard]]** launched (Afreximbank
  + Mercury Payment Services). See [[NGA]], [[nigeria--dpi-pay]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27 Nigeria's PAPSS cross-border payments falls 53% to N5.6bn]].
- **Davos 2026 advocacy (2026-01-23).** At Africa Collective Davos, [[afreximbank]] again
  positioned PAPSS as the cornerstone rail without which AfCFTA's 1.4bn-market integration
  stalls — advocacy, no new transaction. Source:
  [[2026-01-23 Afreximbank flags Africa's digital trade gap at Davos]].
- **Origins and design (2022 backfill; mark "as of 2022").** Launched by [[afreximbank]] with
  the [[afcfta]] Secretariat — formally **January 2022**, HQ **Cairo** — on a cloud platform
  built by **StoneX**; settles intra-African payments across ~42 currencies by netting
  central-bank RTGS balances, aiming to save businesses ~**$5bn/yr** in transaction costs and
  cut dollar dependence. Afreximbank approved **$500m** for the West African Monetary Zone
  pilot and signalled a further **$3bn** for continental rollout; an MoU with **BUNA** (Arab
  Monetary Fund) targets interoperability. 28 banks had joined within nine months, against an
  ambition of all 500–600 African banks. See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]], [[finance.new]].
  Sources: [[2022-05-11 Pan-African Payment and Settlement System]],
  [[2022-08-30 Pan-African Payment and Settlement System drives Africa's banking integration]].
