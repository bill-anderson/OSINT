# African Digital Compact (ADC)

A continental digital-governance compact developed by the **[[african-union]]** Commission
(**July 2024**) to articulate Africa's unified position and priorities for the digital era —
explicitly to **prepare African countries' negotiation of the UN Global Digital Compact (GDC)** ahead
of the UN Summit of the Future, and to align continental digital transformation with international
best practice.

## What it is (as of 2024-07)

- **Purpose.** Charts Africa's digital future through a **human-centred digital economy** that
  "leaves no one behind" — harnessing digitalisation for prosperity, governance, inclusion and
  quality of life, in tandem with **Agenda 2063**.
- **Basis.** Builds on prior AU summit decisions and strategic frameworks — the
  **[[au-digital-transformation-strategy]]** (2020-2030), the **[[au-data-policy-framework]]**
  (2022) and the **[[au-continental-ai-strategy]]** (2024) — and on multi-stakeholder engagement
  (governments, private sector, civil society, RECs and international partners).
- **Frame.** Africa's collective input into the **UN Global Digital Compact** process — a
  sovereignty play to shape global digital governance as co-author rather than rule-taker. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[geopol.usa]].
