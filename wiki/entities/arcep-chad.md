# ARCEP Chad (Communications and Postal Regulatory Authority)

**Chad's telecommunications and postal regulator** — the body that audits network service quality
and holds operators to account for it. In the sources held, ARCEP's visible function is the
**service-quality audit**: a periodic, published technical assessment of operator networks, which
in its most recent reported cycle came down hard on the state of Chadian infrastructure.

## Activity in this wiki

- **15th service-quality audit (conducted September–October 2025).** ARCEP found **improved signal
  stability in several urban centres**, reflecting recent operator investment — but said
  **significant shortcomings remain**: faulty equipment, inadequate maintenance, unstable power
  supply, and, in some areas, **sites entirely offline**. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[infra.energy]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Chad Plans 50 Telecom Sites, 50 km of Fiber in N'Djamena]].
- **The enforcement context.** The audit lands amid sustained public criticism of service quality
  in [[TCD]] — consumers report dropped calls, slow data speeds, frequent outages and patchy
  coverage — and in **late December 2025** authorities again reprimanded operators over recurring
  service disruptions. The government's response, announced 2026-02-11 by [[boukar-michel]], was
  50 new telecom sites and 50 km of FTTH in N'Djamena via [[sotel-tchad]]. See
  [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Chad Plans 50 Telecom Sites, 50 km of Fiber in N'Djamena]].
- **One statutory power now established: ARCEP gates ISP licensing (2025-10-22).** Defending the
  bill ratifying **Ordonnance n°011/PR/2025** on the National Assembly floor, Minister
  [[boukar-michel]] stated that a **multiservice Fournisseur d'Accès Internet (FAI) licence** — the
  category under which **[[starlink]]** operates in Chad — is granted **on the operator's
  application after a favourable ARCEP technical opinion, without public tender**, whereas
  mobile-operator licences follow a different route. This is the **first concrete ARCEP power any
  source held describes**, and it is a consultative gate rather than an award power: ARCEP opines,
  the ministry licenses. See [[gov.legislate]], [[chad--gov-protect]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-22-toumaiwebmedias-ratification-ordonnance-011-pr-2025-communications-electroniques]].
- **The regulator-to-ministry revolving door (2026-04).** **Haliki Choua Mahamat**, appointed
  Minister of Telecommunications, the Digital Economy and Digitalisation on **2026-04-01** and
  installed **2026-04-08**, is a **former Director-General of ARCEP** — the supervised regulator's
  ex-head now running the supervising ministry. Recorded as a structural fact; **no source held
  comments on it**. See [[gov.policy]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-01-tchadinfos-remaniement-gouvernement-haliki-choua-mahamat]],
  [[2026-04-08-portail-td-passation-charges-ministere-telecommunications]].
- **Promotional voice on PMICE (2020-07-08).** At the PMICE launch ARCEP offered the boosterish
  line that the project *"will without doubt put Chad on the road to digital development"* —
  a regulator advocating for a ministry programme rather than assessing it. Source:
  [[2020-07-08-magazinedelafrique-tchad-lancement-pmice-financement-chine]].

## Reading it

The audit is a useful, dated, technical instrument — and the finding that some sites are *entirely
offline* is a harder measure of Chadian network reality than any penetration statistic. Note the
regulatory gap the sources leave open: it is the **ministry**, not ARCEP, that announces network
remedies, and the reprimands of December 2025 are attributed only to "authorities."

On powers, the picture sharpened in 2026-07: ARCEP's role in **ISP licensing is consultative** —
it issues a technical opinion, the ministry awards — and its former Director-General now heads
that ministry. **Still not established by any source held (as of 2026-07-20): ARCEP's founding
instrument, its independence guarantees, its budget, and any sanction it has actually imposed.**
Fifteen audit cycles have produced findings on this page but no recorded penalty.
