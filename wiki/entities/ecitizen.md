# eCitizen (Kenya government digital-services platform)

Kenya's **single-window government-services and payments platform** — "the digital face of government"
— launched 2013 as an IFC-supported pilot and now hosting **~22,000 services** (target 25,000) via a
single sign-on portal and the central **paybill 222222**. It is a core pillar of Kenya's DPI and its
revenue architecture, but its governance, ownership and controls are deeply contested. Operated by a
private consortium (**ECS / Electronic Services Solutions LLP** — Webmasters Kenya, **Pesaflow**, Olive
Tree), with the state asserting full ownership under a January 2023 handover agreement. See [[KEN]],
[[dpi.govtech]], [[kenya-data-marketplace]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Scale & revenue (2026-03).** [[dpi.pay]] Daily collections rose from **<KES 60m (2022, manual era)**
  to **>KES 600m ($4.6m/day)** — attributed to digitisation cutting leakages. PSs (Tanui, Kiptoo,
  Kipsang) briefed the Public Accounts Committee following the Auditor-General's Special Audit. Sources:
  [[2026-03-26-ecitizen-22000-services-audit-techafricanews]], [[2026-03-26-ecitizen-revenue-46m-daily-itweb]].
- **Auditor-General crisis (2025-26).** [[gov.protect]] [[sovereignty]] The AG flagged eCitizen as a
  **"single point of failure"** through vendor dependency, alleged **KES 9.4bn irregularly funnelled** to
  private entities, and recommended the platform be **handed to the State**; the 2023/24 audit found
  variances between revenue statements, the e-portal and the ledger (KES 44.8bn collections). A summonsed
  probe found **eCitizen payments not reaching Treasury accounts** (PS Kiptoo summoned), and The Standard
  detailed a **"Pesaflow heist" of KES 6.3bn** diverted to private accounts. Seven private entities/banks
  were summoned over the suspected diversion. Sources:
  [[2026-03-26-ecitizen-revenue-46m-daily-itweb]] *(carries the "single point of failure" framing, the
  KES 9.4bn figure, the KES 44.8bn collections variance and the seven summonsed entities)*,
  [[2025-07-23-ecitizen-payments-not-reach-treasury-standard]],
  [[2026-03-10-hand-over-ecitizen-auditor-general-standard]] and
  [[2026-03-27-ecitizen-heist-pesaflow-63b-standard]] *(both **headline-only** — The Standard serves a
  hard login wall over the body. Checked 2026-07-20: the articles are real and bylined (Mate Tongola;
  Josphat Thiong'o), but the wiki holds only their headlines. They establish that the recommendation to
  hand the platform to the State was reported on 10 Mar 2026, and that a KES 6.3bn Pesaflow diversion
  was alleged on 27 Mar 2026 — and nothing further. The mechanism of the alleged diversion is **not
  established**; a subscriber clip is queued as an acquisition.)*
  - *Status of the allegations, as of 2026-07-20:* these are audit findings and press allegations.
    Nothing on file records a charge, a court determination, or a Pesaflow or eCitizen response. That
    absence is itself the current state, not a gap to be papered over.
- **Contract scrutiny (2025-04).** [[gov.legislate]] MPs sought to **cancel the eCitizen consultancy
  agreement**, calling it lopsided and signed by a junior officer without Treasury/Interior CS signatures
  — exposing Kenyans to loss of money and data. Source:
  [[2025-04-20-mps-multi-billion-ecitizen-deal-cancelled-nation]].
- **Coercion & the KES 50 fee.** [[include.access]] The High Court **quashed mandatory school-fee
  payment via eCitizen** (Apr 2025, no legal basis, "potential indirect discrimination") and later
  **declared the KES 50 convenience fee illegal** (discriminatory, a double levy, no public
  participation). The government is re-legislating via the **PFM (eCitizen System Management)
  Regulations 2026**, proposing a value-pegged fee. Source:
  [[2025-04-01-court-halts-school-fees-ecitizen-nation]].
- **Migration non-compliance (2026-06).** [[dpi.govtech]] Against a 2023 presidential order to migrate
  **11,000 services/year**, only **1,695 were onboarded** in FY2025/26 (Controller of Budget: "dismal"),
  with ~20,000 services now live — non-compliance tied to officials protecting bribe/leakage channels.
  Source: [[2026-06-23-agencies-defy-ecitizen-order-1695-services-nation]].
