# National Cyber Security Agency (NCSA), Kenya

Kenya's **central cybersecurity authority**, established mid-2026 by the **National Cyber Security Agency
Order, 2026** (President Ruto, under the State Corporations Act) — a shift from siloed, reactive cyber
defence to a centralised command structure. Mandate: protect critical digital infrastructure, coordinate
incident response, own national cyber strategy, and audit the resilience of key government and
private-sector systems. See [[KEN]], [[infra.cybersec]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Creation & parliamentary approval (2026-06).** [[gov.legislate]] Parliament greenlit the NCSA Order;
  the Interior Ministry framed it as institutionalising **digital trust** as the economy (mobile money,
  e-government, online commerce) grows more exposed to cybercrime, ransomware and breaches. Sources:
  [[2026-06-23-kenya-approves-national-cyber-security-agency-itweb]],
  [[2026-06-24-institutionalize-trust-digital-government-biometricupdate]].
- **Oversight concerns (2026-06).** [[sovereignty]] "Puzzle over Ruto cybersecurity unit": confusion
  over whether the anchoring regulations secured parliamentary approval, and disquiet over the agency's
  **wide-ranging mandate** bringing together senior security chiefs — an oversight/concentration worry.
  Source: [[2026-06-22-puzzle-ruto-cybersecurity-unit-nation]].
- **Cyber + AI-governance bundle (2026-07).** [[tech.ai]] The government paired the cyber-agency build
  with tightened **AI governance** (Omollo). Source:
  [[2026-07-01-government-cybersecurity-ai-governance-standard]] *(summary-only clip)*.
- **Threat backdrop (2025-11).** [[infra.cybersec]] Hackers defaced and took down multiple government
  sites — including **president.go.ke** and the Interior, Energy, Labour, Health and Education ministries
  — with extremist messaging; eCitizen, NPS, Treasury and Defence were unaffected. The kind of
  state-cyber-resilience gap the NCSA is meant to close. Source:
  [[2025-11-17-hackers-government-websites-presidency-nation]].
