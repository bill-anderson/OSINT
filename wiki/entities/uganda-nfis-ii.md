# Uganda National Financial Inclusion Strategy 2023-2028 (NFIS II)

**Uganda's second National Financial Inclusion Strategy**, covering **2023–2028**,
published **November 2023** (month precision) by the **Ministry of Finance, Planning and
Economic Development with [[bank-of-uganda]]**, and developed with FSD Uganda support. The
foreword is signed by the BoU Deputy Governor. It succeeds NFIS I.

## What it changes

**Priority groups were widened over NFIS I to add refugees and persons with disabilities**,
alongside women, youth, rural dwellers and MSMEs. Governance runs through the
**Inter-Institutional Committee on Financial Inclusion**; the strategy is aligned to
**Vision 2040**, **NDP III** and the **Parish Development Model**. Source:
[[2023-11-01 Uganda National Financial Inclusion Strategy 2023-2028 (NFIS II)]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **The PWD priority, tested.** The strategy's addition of persons with disabilities as a
  priority group can be read against [[asamo-hellen-grace]]'s call two years later
  (2025-12-02) for financial institutions to strengthen PWD inclusion, citing continuing
  barriers — financial exclusion, limited employment, restricted microcredit — and
  persistent gaps in research and funding. See [[include.access]], [[include.divides]].
- **The refugee priority, tested.** Compare the district-level concerns raised at NIRA's
  2025 mass registration about distinguishing citizens from refugees in Adjumani (see
  [[nira-uganda]]) — registration being the upstream condition of the financial access
  this strategy targets.
