# Horizon Fiber

A tri-operator **cross-border terrestrial optical-fibre corridor** linking the
international subsea-cable landing stations in **[[DJI|Djibouti]]** through
**[[ETH|Ethiopia]]** to **[[SDN|Sudan]]** — a high-capacity (multi-terabit)
overland route positioned to connect East Africa directly to global networks and
give Sudan a role as a regional digital hub. Partners: **[[sudatel]]**,
**[[djibouti-telecom]]** and **[[ethio-telecom]]**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **From MoU to binding deal (Dec 2024 → Feb 2026).** The three operators signed
  the founding MoU in **December 2024**; on **4 Feb 2026** they signed a
  strategic agreement in Djibouti to launch the initiative, and by **10 Feb 2026**
  had converted the MoU into a **binding agreement**. Framed by Djibouti
  Telecom's CEO as strengthening the region's "digital sovereignty" and by
  Sudatel's CEO as "a living model of innovative partnership among African
  operators." Combines terrestrial and subsea routes; expected to improve
  redundancy through route diversification and cut latency for hyperscalers.
  See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]], [[finance.mou]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-04 Sudatel-Djibouti Telecom-Ethio telecom launch Horizon Fiber cross-border initiative (SUNA)]],
  [[2026-02-10-horizon-fiber-binding-agreement-telecomreviewafrica]].
- **Wartime build.** Sudatel says it laid **~2,700 km of new fibre** during the
  war, framing Horizon as an extension of long-running regional interconnection
  (including routes toward Chad and Ethiopia).
