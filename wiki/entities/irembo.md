# Irembo

Rwanda's **single-window e-government platform** and the private company that builds
and runs it. Irembo (irembo.gov.rw) digitises public services onto one portal, and is
frequently cited as a nationally-designed GovTech / DPI exemplar (e.g. the DIAL
cross-border-identity analysis on the [[RWA]] hub). Led by CEO **Faith Keza**. See
[[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]].

## What it is (model)

- **Public-private single window.** A private organisation, but **part-owned by the
  Government of Rwanda**, operating under a **25-year agreement** to work with agencies
  to digitise and maintain public services on one platform. Aligned to Rwanda's
  ambition to become a digital society ([[rwanda-dpi-strategy]]). (Interview with CEO
  Faith Keza; date approximate.)
- **Commission-funded, usage-aligned.** Many government services are paid; Irembo
  provides the payment engine and takes a **commission on each successful paid
  application** — so revenue only accrues when citizens actually complete
  transactions, aligning incentives to usability. See [[dpi.pay]].
- **Insourced local delivery.** After ~40 services built via outsourced teams proved
  unsustainable, Irembo **insourced and built local engineering teams** (~70 staff);
  it also runs a network of **~4,000 agents ("digital ambassadors")** who help citizens
  without devices or skills transact — a digital-literacy channel as well as a service
  channel. See [[capacity.training]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Scale (as of the ~2021 interview).** Irembo offered **over 100 services** online —
  by its own account still only **~20% of all Rwandan public services** — from driver's
  licences and residence certificates to traffic fines and civil-status documents.
  Cited efficiency gain: a **birth certificate downloadable within hours** where the
  paper process once needed 4 journeys, 3 offices and ≥4 days. Ambition stated to
  expand the model to other African nations. Source:
  [[2021-06-01 The Irembo model in Rwanda]].
- **Foundation in the digital-transformation stack (2024 analysis).** Profiled as the
  citizen-facing services layer built atop Rwanda's KT-Corp-era 4G backbone; the
  platform "offers over a hundred services" (driver's licence, identity certificates,
  marriage). Source:
  [[2024-09-30 Rwanda's digital transformation an effort at intergovernmental collaboration]].
- **CRVS role.** In the DHIS2 CRVS–EIR integration, the **NIN issued at birth is used
  in the Irembo portal to request a digital birth certificate** — Irembo is the
  citizen retrieval point for civil-registration credentials. Source:
  [[2022-04-28 Interoperability of CRVS and EIR systems for improved EPI management in Rwanda]].
