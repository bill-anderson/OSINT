- **Parties:** Nigeria (Federal Ministry of Communications, Innovation and
  Digital Economy — Minister Dr [[bosun-tijani]]) and Finland (Ministry for
  Foreign Affairs — Under Secretary of State for International Trade, **Jarno
  Syrjälä**).
- **What:** a Memorandum of Understanding on digitalisation and innovation,
  strengthening the framework for cooperation in **digital government, emerging
  technologies, digital public infrastructure, cybersecurity, innovation
  ecosystems, and capacity building**.
- **When / where:** signed in **Abuja**, March 2026 (reported by AriseNews,
  ingested 2026-07-11).
- **Value:** none stated — a framework/cooperation MoU, not a financial commitment.
- **Status:** signed. Described as an "important step"; concrete cooperation to
  follow.

## Context and lineage

The MoU follows Nigeria's **February 2026 engagements in Helsinki**, where the
minister met stakeholders including **Finnvera** and [[finnfund]] and discussed
digital infrastructure, the **Data Exchange Platform**, and opportunities for
Finnish participation in **Project BRIDGE** (the $500m World Bank–backed fibre
programme — see [[national-digital-farmers-registry-nigeria]] and
[[eu-nigeria-global-gateway-290m]] for the same 90,000 km backbone).

Crucially, it complements Finland's **lead role in the EU's Team Europe
Initiative on Support to Digital Public Services** and inclusive digital skills
in Nigeria — a programme implemented by Finland's development agency **HAUS** in
consortium with Estonia's development agency [[estdev]]. This places the bilateral
MoU inside the wider [[european-union]] / [[global-gateway]] digital-cooperation
architecture in Nigeria rather than standing alone (hence `geopol.eu` and the
`XGL` tag for the multilateral dimension).

## Stated positions

- **Tijani:** the agreement is a "significant next step" to "leverage digital
  transformation as a catalyst for sustainable growth and shared prosperity."
- **Syrjälä:** Finland is "pleased to deepen its partnership with Nigeria in
  building resilient, secure, and human centric digital societies"; Nigeria is
  "a key partner for Finland in Africa," and the MoU provides a basis for
  cooperation across governments, institutions and private sectors toward
  "interoperable, future fit" digital solutions.
