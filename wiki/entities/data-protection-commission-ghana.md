# Data Protection Commission (DPC, Ghana)

Ghana's independent data-protection regulator, established under the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843), responsible for registering data controllers and enforcing personal-data protection ([[gov.protect]]). Executive Director (as of 2024) **Patricia Adusei-Poku**, who has positioned the DPC as a regional trailblazer for authorised, people-centric data processing. The DPC is one of the oversight bodies (with NITA, the NIA and the Ministry of Communications and Digitalisation) in Ghana's data-governance architecture, and a lead stakeholder in the emerging [[ghana-national-data-strategy]]. A new **Governing Board** (chair **Teki
Akuetteh**) was sworn in **Aug 2025**; the DPC declared **2026 a "year of enforcement"** (fines/imprisonment)
after reaching ~25m people in 2025. See [[ghana--gov-protect]], [[GHA]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Lead stakeholder in the National Data Strategy (2024-07-02).** [[gov.policy]] At the National Validation Workshop for Ghana's [[ghana-national-data-strategy]] (with [[smart-africa]] and Team Europe), ED Patricia Adusei-Poku framed the DPC as a regional trailblazer for authorised, people-centric data processing; the strategy aligns to the AU Continental Data Policy Framework. Source: [[2024-07-02 Ghana develops its National Data Strategy with Smart Africa and Team Europe]].
- **Named in Ghana's institutional map (2024-09-20).** The US Country Commercial Guide lists the DPC among the key agencies under the Ministry of Communications and Digitalization, alongside NITA, NCA, GIFEC and the Cyber Security Authority. Source: [[2024-09-20 Ghana Digital Economy (US Country Commercial Guide)]].
