# MultiChoice Nigeria

**The pay-TV operator on which the [[ndpc]] imposed its largest data-protection penalty to date.** In this wiki MultiChoice matters less as a media business than as the test case for what Nigeria's data-protection enforcement actually does when a large processor declines to cooperate.

*Single-sourced, from the regulator's own statement. The wiki holds nothing on MultiChoice's Nigerian operations outside this enforcement action, as of **2026-07-20**.*

## The NDPC fine (announced 2025-07-06)

- **₦766,242,500** (**~US$501,340**, at the rate implied in the regulator's statement — a **dated conversion**, not a standing equivalence) for breaching the [[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]].
- Following an investigation **opened Q2 2024**.
- Findings: privacy violations against **subscribers and non-subscribers alike**; **illegal cross-border transfer** of Nigerian personal data; and processing found **"patently intrusive, unfair, unnecessary and disproportionate"**, contrary to **s.37 of the 1999 Constitution**.
- Imposed expressly **"for want of cooperation"** after directed remediation was judged unsatisfactory — *the sanction endpoint of the NDPC's remediation-first model, not its opening move.*
- Statement signed by **Babatunde Bamigboye**, Head of Legal, Enforcement & Regulations. National Commissioner **Vincent Olatunji** ordered **all MultiChoice data-collection outlets investigated**.

Source: [[2025-07-06-ndpc-multichoice-fine-statement]].

## Reads

- The non-subscriber finding is the sharp edge: a broadcaster processing data on people who never contracted with it makes the cross-border transfer question a *population* question, not a customer one. The penalty's stated trigger — non-cooperation rather than the breach itself — also tells you what the NDPC is actually pricing.
