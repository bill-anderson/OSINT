The **Nigeria Digital Postcode System** is a federally approved **geographic
intelligent addressing** initiative — a GIS-enabled, alphanumeric digital
addressing layer intended to give every location in Nigeria an accurate,
machine-readable address. Approval was announced by Minister of Communications,
Innovation and Digital Economy **Dr [[bosun-tijani]]** and reported by ITWeb
Africa on 2026-03-05.

## What it is meant to do

The government positions the system as **foundational digital public
infrastructure** rather than merely a postal upgrade. Stated benefits:

- faster, more reliable mail and parcel processing / strengthened postal
  operations;
- better national **planning**;
- improved **emergency response**;
- more efficient **logistics and e-commerce**;
- improved government **service delivery**.

Tijani framed it (on X) as part of building "the infrastructure required to
connect people, businesses, and services more efficiently across the country"
and as a step toward "a modern, inclusive, and globally competitive digital
economy."

## Status

As of March 2026 this is an **approved** initiative announced by the minister;
the clipping gives no operator, budget, timeline or roll-out detail. It sits
within Tijani's broader digital-economy agenda alongside other DPI efforts such
as the NIN system ([[nimc]]), Project BRIDGE fibre roll-out, and the data-exchange
platform referenced in the [[nigeria-finland-digital-mou]] and
[[eu-nigeria-global-gateway-290m]] packages.
