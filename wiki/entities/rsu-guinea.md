# Registre Social Unifié (RSU, Guinea)

Guinea's **unified social registry** — the single entry point for the country's social-protection programmes, held by the Ministère de la Promotion Féminine, de l'Enfance et des Personnes Vulnérables ([[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]]). Created by **Decree D/2019/271/PRG/SGG of 1 October 2019** (following a national targeting-tools harmonisation workshop in Kindia, July 2017), it maintains a household database for targeting benefits — a natural downstream consumer of a future unique identifier ([[oneci-guinea]]) and a social-protection counterpart to the civil-registry/ID stack. See [[GIN]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Establishing profile (presentation, 2025).** Ministry presentation sets out the RSU's origins, legal basis and rollout stages (regional/prefectural sensitisation → targeting/data collection → analysis pipeline feeding the database).
