# Novissi

**Togo's fully-digital government-to-person (G2P) cash-transfer programme, enacted in April
2020 as a pandemic response.** Novissi delivered **twice-monthly unconditional transfers**
to the **urban informal sector**, initially in **Greater Lomé** and later extended to
**Tchaoudjo Prefecture**. It is the case around which the [[world-bank]]'s [[g2px]]
end-to-end digital G2P case study is built, and the programme for which Togo is most often
cited internationally. See [[TGO]], [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Reach (programme's own published figures).** Novissi distributed benefits to **over
  500,000 people**, **65% of them women** — figures published by Novissi and reported via
  the World Bank G2PX case study, not independently verified here. See [[dpi.pay]].
  Source: [[2023-01-01 Case Study - G2PX in Togo, End-to-End Digital G2P (World Bank)]].
- **The registry Togo did not have.** The analytically load-bearing fact in the case study
  is the starting position: **before COVID-19, Togo had no social registry and low national
  ID coverage**, but **did have a voter registry covering 93% of the population**. Novissi
  was built as an end-to-end digital G2P scheme on that footing — a targeting problem solved
  through the register that existed rather than the one a social-protection system would
  normally use. See [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2023-01-01 Case Study - G2PX in Togo, End-to-End Digital G2P (World Bank)]].
- **Cited as Togo's DPI credential (2025-11).** In coverage of Senegal's benchmarking visit
  to Lomé, Togo "was also acclaimed for building a platform dubbed Novissi that facilitated
  government-to-people payments as part of a social protection measure at the peak of the
  coronavirus pandemic" — Novissi and [[e-id-togo]] together forming the reputation that
  brought the Dakar delegation. See [[SEN]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-03 Senegal taps inspiration from Togo's digital ID experience]].

**Reference-study note.** The G2PX case study is a World Bank reference work (prepared by
Brian Webster, drawing on a background report by Boko and Akogbeto, 2022). Its figures are
cited here as the study's, and are not promoted onto place or concept pages as current
state.
