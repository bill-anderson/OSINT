# Cross-border Data Flows in Africa: An Analysis of the Alignment with AfCFTA (RIA, 2025)

A **[[research-ict-africa]]** research report (published 2025-09-22) by **Sandra
Makumbirofa, Jackline Akello and Nawal Omar** mapping African data-sharing regulatory and
governance frameworks and assessing their alignment with the **[[afcfta]]**. Captured as a
standing reference entity (`instrument`) — named-authorship lit-review material likely to be
cited structurally on cross-border data governance.

## Argument and findings

- Most **domestic frameworks only loosely align** with AfCFTA; **fragmentation** plus weak
  "adequacy" recognition block intra-African data sharing.
- Personal and non-personal data rules are **unharmonised** across the continent.
- Recommends: **operationalise data-protection authorities**, adopt technical
  interoperability standards, review restrictive national laws, build **regional mutual
  recognition**, and implement AfCFTA commitments.
- Anchored to the **[[au-data-policy-framework]]** and the Malabo Convention as the
  continental reference instruments.
