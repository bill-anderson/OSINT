# Intelligent Population Register (IPR)

**A DRAFT PROPOSAL, NOT LAW.** A proposed modernisation of South Africa's National Population
Register, described by **[[home-affairs-south-africa]]** as "a modern, digitally enabled Intelligent
Population Register (IPR)" to serve as the basis for a new digital-identity dispensation. It appears
in the **draft revised White Paper on Citizenship, Immigration and Refugee Protection**, gazetted by
Minister **[[leon-schreiber]]** around **12 December 2025**, with **public input open 12 December
2025 to 31 January 2026** and a nine-province consultation campaign planned for **15–30 January
2026**. Nothing here is enacted.

**TERMINOLOGY DIVERGENCE — record it, and prefer the department's own wording.** The DHA's **tabled
Strategic Plan 2025-2030 does not use "Intelligent Population Register"**. Its terms are the
**National Population Register (NPR)** / "secure population register", with an **"Integrated
Identity System operational"** outcome indicator. "IPR" is the trade-press and White Paper framing;
"NPR" is what the department commits to in its own plan.

## Activity in this wiki

- **What the IPR is proposed to be (2025-12-15).** DHA claims the IPR would use "**artificial
  intelligence, machine learning, biometrics, interoperability and real-time data integration**, to
  improve governance, integrated service delivery, and national planning" — advantages asserted over
  the ordinary register currently in place. Source:
  [[2025-12-15 South Africa plans Intelligent Population Register in proposed civil registration reform]].
- **Mandatory digital civil registration.** Under the envisaged IPR, government intends to make
  **birth and death registration mandatory through digital channels**, for citizens and legal
  residents alike — a compulsion worth watching against the digital-divide exclusion arguments the
  wiki holds elsewhere (see [[include.divides]], [[digital.rural]]). Source: same.
- **Detail deferred.** DHA says the **major elements and functionality of the IPR will be set out in
  a separate digital ID policy document** it is preparing — i.e. the substance is not in the White
  Paper. Source: same.
- **Competing target dates.** Schreiber said early in 2025 that SA hopes for a fully functional
  digital ID **before the 2029 national elections**; the DHA's own Strategic Plan says digital
  identity is "**to become a reality in 2028/9**". These sit alongside the wiki's held 2027/28
  platform target — a ladder of competing dates, none of them retired. Sources: same;
  [[2025-04-01 South Africa Department of Home Affairs Strategic Plan 2025-2030]].
- **The wider White Paper (2025-12-15).** Also proposes **merit-based naturalisation** (citizenship
  no longer granted "solely on the number of years a foreigner has resided in the country"), a
  **merit-based points system** for certain visas and permanent residency, tightened asylum rules,
  and an **Electronic Travel Authorisation recording the biometrics of all foreigners entering
  South Africa**. DHA frames the package as clamping down on fraud and abuse and enhancing national
  security; it requested **$618 million** for FY2025/26. Source: same.
