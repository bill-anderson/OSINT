# Niyel

Pan-African **advocacy organisation** that convened the first shared **AI-governance framework
for Francophone West Africa**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Six-country AI-governance guidelines (2026-07-06).** Niyel led **Benin, Burkina Faso,
  Côte d'Ivoire, Guinea, Mali and Senegal** in adopting shared guidelines for ethical,
  inclusive and responsible AI — announced on the sidelines of the UN's inaugural **Global AI
  Dialogue on Governance** and submitted as a written contribution. Developed by researchers,
  technical experts and senior digital officials, it recommends transparent/safe/inclusive AI,
  stronger data protection, skills, algorithmic-bias mitigation, local innovation and **digital
  sovereignty**; it complements (not replaces) national AI strategies and aligns with UNESCO's
  2021 AI-ethics recommendation and the AU's 2024 Continental AI Strategy, warning that
  uncontrolled adoption of externally-developed AI could deepen technological dependence. It is
  **[[MLI|Mali]]'s first substantive continental AI-governance commitment**. See [[tech.ai]],
