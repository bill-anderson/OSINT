# Liquid C2

Liquid C2 is the cloud, cyber-security and AI business of Liquid Intelligent Technologies (formerly Liquid Telecom), part of the [[cassava-technologies]] group. It matters as the connectivity-and-cloud backbone underpinning Cassava's sovereign-AI ambitions in Africa.

## Profile
- Operates roughly 100,000 km of fibre across around 14 African countries.
- Provides managed cloud and security in 22 countries; more than 80% of the largest organisations in 31+ African countries use Liquid technologies.
- Became the first Google Cloud Interconnect provider in Africa (2024-06).
- The Google Cloud relationship runs on the **[[liquid-c2-google-cloud-mou]]** (signed 10 Nov 2023): a 2024-03-06 partnership adds [[anthropic]] models via Vertex AI and MSSP status, extended in Dec 2025 by **Liquid G**, an AI-driven Google Cloud reseller-distribution programme (via Cloudmania, local-currency billing). Sources: [[2023-11-14 Liquid C2 - signs strategic partnership with Google Cloud]], [[2025-12-01 Liquid C2 expands Google Cloud access in Africa with AI-driven distribution programme, Liquid G]].
- Built the **terrestrial leg of Google's [[umoja-cable]]** (Kenya→SA) and holds a fibre pair on Google's **[[equiano-cable]]** (up to 12 Tbps, ~2022). Sources: [[2024-05-28 Google invests in terrestrial-subsea cable linking Kenya to Australia]], [[2022-03-01 Liquid Intelligent acquires Google's fiber pair to improve offers]].
- Launched Africa's first Google Cloud-powered Experience Centre in Johannesburg (April 2026).
- **Digital-skills push (2026-05).** With [[microsoft]] and SA's Department of Communications and Digital Technologies, launched a **solar-powered mobile digital lab** at the Royal Bafokeng Institute (Phokeng, North West) — Microsoft 365/Copilot/AI-fluency training and a Power Platform certification cohort. Builds on Liquid's Mthatha digital-skills centre (515 trained, 96% pass rate, 149 placed). See [[capacity.training]], [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-05-27 Liquid, Microsoft unveil mobile digital lab for North West youth]].
- CEO of Liquid C2: Oswald Jumira.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Google Cloud Partner Experience Centre, Johannesburg (2026-04).** Opened Africa's
  first **Google Cloud-powered Partner Experience Centre** — a hub giving partners/resellers
  a path to official Google Cloud accreditation and certification, hands-on access to
  **Gemini Enterprise** and the "Gemini Playspace," "Solutions Pods" for enterprise bids,
  sector platforms (financial services, healthcare, retail), and **local-currency billing
  and credit** for resellers. Cassava CEO Hardy Pemhiwa framed it as the first of its kind
  on the continent. See [[google]], [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.training]], [[ZAF]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-08 Liquid C2, Google open first-for-Africa Partner Experience Centre]],
  [[2026-04-08 Liquid C2's new centre puts Google Cloud within Africa's reach]].
- **DRC country CEO appointed (2026-07).** Liquid named **Bernard Beya** permanent CEO of its
  **[[COD]] DR Congo** operations (effective 1 Apr 2026, after acting from Nov 2025; joined Liquid
  Sept 2022 as CFO). Liquid frames the DRC as one of its fastest-growing, most strategic African
  markets; Beya's mandate — expand connectivity, cloud, cyber security, colocation and **AI compute**
  "as a business of [[cassava-technologies]]." See [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-14 Liquid appoints Bernard Beya as DRC CEO]].
- **Hybrid power for data centres (2026-04, GITEX Africa).** Liquid is cited among
  operators (with Teraco and [[africa-data-centres]]) adopting **hybrid energy** — grid
  plus solar, battery and diesel — as executives argue Africa's fragile grids cannot be
  the primary power source for AI-era compute. See [[infra.energy]], [[infra.store]].
  Source: [[2026-04-14 Africa's data centres rethink power as grids falter]].
