# Nigerian Financial Intelligence Unit (NFIU)

Nigeria's financial-intelligence body — the recipient of suspicious-transaction reporting, and
therefore the point at which the country's **identity, payments and anti-money-laundering layers
meet**.

- **The reporting endpoint for automated AML (2026-03-12).** The **[[cbn]]**'s baseline standards
  require financial institutions to run automated AML systems producing **Suspicious Transaction
  Reports (STRs) and Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) in the formats the NFIU requires**,
  integrated with core banking over secure APIs. The mandate is proportional — larger banks with
  higher transaction volumes carry heavier expectations. Source:
  [[2026-03-12-cbn-automated-aml-baseline-standards]].
- **Crypto folded into the AML perimeter (2026-01-12).** Under the tax and OECD-CARF alignment
  reforms, **VASPs must flag large or suspicious transactions to the NFIU as well as to the tax
  authorities**, extending virtual-asset oversight into the wider anti-money-laundering framework.
  Source: [[2026-01-12-tin-nin-crypto-tax-carf]].
- **Virtual Asset Council member (2026-07-20).** The
  [[virtual-assets-executive-order-2026|Executive Order on Virtual Assets Coordination]] seats the
  NFIU on the Council alongside **[[onsa-nigeria|ONSA]]**, under a **[[cbn]]** chair with the
  **[[sec-nigeria|SEC]]** and **[[nigeria-revenue-service|NRS]]** as vice-chairs — money laundering
  and terrorism financing being among the named risks the Order exists to close off. Source:
  [[2026-07-20-nigeria-coordinated-regulatory-framework-virtual-assets]].

The NFIU also sits in the wiki's baseline account of Nigeria's identity estate, where the financial
identifiers (BVN, NIN) are the substrate its reporting runs on. Source:
[[2024-04-10 Understanding Nigeria's digital identity landscape (BVN, NIN, voter, SIM, passport)]].
