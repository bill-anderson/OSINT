# Disrupt Africa

**A publisher and funding tracker whose annual *African Tech Start-ups Funding Report* is one of the
standing datasets the wiki consults on African startup investment.** Its method: tracking all
**publicly disclosed** investment rounds, supplemented by polling startups, investors, hubs and other
ecosystem stakeholders, with **conservative estimates applied to undisclosed rounds**.

**Cite, don't absorb.** Disrupt Africa's figures are a tracker's figures, not the market. It says so
itself: the report "reflects a **minimum funding benchmark**." Never write a bare "Africa raised $X
in 2025" — **always name the tracker and date the figure**.

## The tracker contradiction

**[[disrupt-africa]] reports Africa's 2025 total at $1.64bn; the wiki holds Partech's $4.1bn for the
same year — a 2.5x gap.** **Resolved (2026-07-20): the two series are not commensurable and must never
be summed, averaged or used to check one another.** Partech counts **equity plus debt, by deal**, across
a wide tech universe (US$4.1bn, 570 deals — equity US$2.41bn/462 deals, debt US$1.64bn/107; report
published 2026-01-22). Disrupt Africa publishes a **self-declared minimum floor**, **by funded venture**,
restricted to companies passing a narrow startup test (US$1,636,700,000 across 178 startups, 11th
edition, 2026-02-03); its methodology states the true total "is likely to be higher than our total" and
excludes corporate spin-offs and companies over roughly ten years old.

*Two cautions.* First, the apparent agreement on the big-four share is **a false parallel**: Disrupt
Africa's 72.5% is the big four's share **by count** (129/178), while its share **by value** is **88%** —
so against Partech's 72% of capital the two **diverge by 16 points**. Second, Partech's *debt* subtotal
(US$1.64bn) is numerically identical to Disrupt Africa's *total* — coincidence, not reconciliation, and a
live trap for a future reader. The gap localises to debt megadeals to mature companies: the trackers rank
the big four in **opposite orders**, Kenya being Partech's #1 (US$1.04bn, 48% debt) but Disrupt Africa's
#4 (US$273.2m).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Africa CY2025 (reported 2026-02-27).** Total capital raised across the continent **$1.64 billion,
  up 46.2% year-on-year**, across **178 funded startups** (a decline in count — capital concentrating
  in larger rounds and established ecosystems). The **"big four"** (SA, Nigeria, Egypt, Kenya)
  accounted for **129 funded startups (72.5%)** and **$1.45bn (88% of capital)**. Source:
  [[2026-02-27 SA powers Africa's $1.64bn tech funding rebound]].
- **South Africa CY2025.** **42 startups funded, up 68% from 25 in 2024**; **$335.9m raised = 20.5%
  of Africa's total**; a **234% surge from $100.4m in 2024**, but **still below the $512.3m 2023
  peak**. **Average deal size $7.99m**, nearly double the $4.02m of 2024. Only **63.2% of disclosed
  rounds were pre-Series A or earlier** — lower than peer markets, suggesting investors backing
  scale-stage businesses locally. Source: same.
- **South African gender diversity, declining (CY2025).** Only **16.7% of funded SA startups had at
  least one female co-founder**, and **just one company (2.4%) was led by a female CEO**. One-third
  participated in incubator or accelerator programmes. Source: same.
- **SA sector split (CY2025).** Fintech **31% of funded ventures, $124.97m (37.2%)**; energy
  **$94.6m (28.2%)**; AI and IoT **$22.83m (6.8%)**; agritech **$19.6m**. Source: same.
- **Continental placings (CY2025).** Nigeria first at **$464.8m (28.4%)** across only 25 startups
  (highest average deal size in Africa, **$18.6m**); Egypt second, **$378.95m** across 43; Kenya
  fourth, **$273.2m** across 19 (energy-dominated); Morocco **$29.7m** (nine); Ghana **$41.2m**;
  Tunisia **$12.25m** (nine). Source: same.
