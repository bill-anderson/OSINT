# AFRINIC

The **African Network Information Centre** — Africa's **Regional Internet Registry (RIR)**, one
of five global RIRs (with RIPE NCC, ARIN, APNIC, LACNIC) managing IP-address and AS-number
allocation for the continent. A community-governed, bottom-up technical institution whose
legitimacy rests on member self-governance and independence from political direction.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Governance capture concern (2025–2026).** [[alice-munyua]]
  ([[2025-11-04 The Double Standard Why Are Internet Governance Rules Different for Africa]])
  argues that [[smart-africa]]'s [[caiga]] blueprint — co-developed with [[icann]] — would let
  the Smart Africa Heads-of-State Summit politically endorse AFRINIC governance reforms, install a
  Smart Africa-provided Permanent Secretary, and substitute political endorsement for member
  ratification, contrary to ICP-2. Smart Africa minutes described the **September 2025 AFRINIC
  board elections** as "7 of 8 coalition-backed candidates elected" — framed by Munyua as
  top-down political coordination rather than community governance.
