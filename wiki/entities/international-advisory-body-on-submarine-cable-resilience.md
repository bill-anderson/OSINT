# International Advisory Body on Submarine Cable Resilience

A joint **[[itu]] – [[international-cable-protection-committee|ICPC]]** multistakeholder body
established in **2024** to build international consensus on protecting and restoring submarine
telecommunications cables — the infrastructure carrying **more than 99% of global data traffic** (ITU
figure, 2026). It ran a **two-year work programme**, completed on **10 July 2026**.

Its African significance is direct and unusual: it was **co-chaired by Nigeria's Minister of
Communications, Innovation and Digital Economy [[bosun-tijani]]**, alongside **Sandra Maximiano**,
Chairwoman of Portugal's regulator ANACOM. Executive secretaries were ITU Deputy Secretary-General
Tomas Lamanauskas and ICPC Legal Advisor Kent Bressie. Its summits ran in **Abuja, Nigeria (2025)**
and **Porto, Portugal (2026)**, and the final meeting was held during **ITU's WSIS Forum 2026**
(Geneva Digital Week).

## The final report (approved 2026-07-10)

The report develops recommendations agreed earlier in 2026 across **three pillars**: **timely
deployment and repair**; **risk identification, monitoring and mitigation**; and **fostering
connectivity and geographical diversity**. It names the sector's key challenges as high exposure to
physical risk, lengthening repair times, geographical concentration of infrastructure, and heavy
dependence of many states on a small number of cable systems — **particularly small island developing
states (SIDS), Least Developed Countries (LDCs) and other underserved regions**, the category into
which much of coastal Africa falls.

Priorities for action:

- strengthening government–industry coordination;
- streamlining regulatory and permitting processes;
- improving risk identification and monitoring;
- increasing route diversity and infrastructure redundancy;
- enhancing preparedness and response capabilities;
- addressing the needs of vulnerable regions;
- integrating climate and environmental considerations.

ITU Secretary-General **[[doreen-bogdan-martin]]** called it "a practical roadmap to keep undersea
networks reliable." Tijani framed the recommendations as "an important milestone… through greater
international cooperation, practical policy guidance, and shared responsibility." Source:
[[2026-07-10-itu-icpc-advisory-body-cable-resilience-report]].

## Regional uptake — West Africa

**[[watra]]** Executive Secretary **Aliyu Aboki**, who sat on the Advisory Body, called on the
**16 West African regulators** to act on the recommendations collectively (2026-07-15), reading them
against the **March 2024** multi-cable break that disrupted payments, communications and commerce
across the region. He singled out the report's call for **regional cooperation and regulatory
harmonisation** — using regional and intergovernmental frameworks to reduce fragmented requirements
and strengthen collective preparedness — and argued cable protection is now an **economic**, not
merely a telecommunications, question. See [[XWA]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
[[2026-07-15-watra-regional-subsea-resilience-plan]].

## Reads

The body is the clearest instance to date of an African state holding a **co-chair seat in global
digital-infrastructure standard-setting** rather than receiving its outputs — while the underlying
vulnerability (three repair ships serving the continent; single-cable states) remains unaddressed by
recommendations that are, by design, non-binding.
