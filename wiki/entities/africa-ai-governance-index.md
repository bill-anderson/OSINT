# Africa AI Governance Index (AAIGI)

A continental **AI-governance benchmarking instrument** published by the **Africa AI Policy Lab**, an
initiative of **Lawyers Hub** (Nairobi), funded by the **Patrick J. McGovern Foundation**. The
**inaugural edition is dated July 2026**, with country assessments reflecting publicly available
evidence **as at 2026-06-30**.

**Structure:** **54 African states**, **8 weighted pillars**, **80 indicators per state**, **4,320
scored observations**, on a **0–4 scale**. Pillars: Strategy & Vision · Governance & Regulation ·
Infrastructure & Data · Human Capital · Innovation & Ecosystem · Ethics & Inclusion · Regional
Integration · Implementation & Impact. Sub-regional groupings follow **UNECA**'s geographic
classification.

## ⚠ Not to be confused with the African AI Governance Index (AAGI)

*Flagged by lint 2026-07-21.* **A second, unrelated index exists with a near-identical name and an
identical published structure**, and the two will be conflated unless the difference is stated here:

| | **AAIGI** (this page) | **AAGI** |
|---|---|---|
| Publisher | **Lawyers Hub / Africa AI Policy Lab** | **AAGI Foundation** |
| Base | **Nairobi, Kenya** | **Accra, Ghana** (subsidiaries Kenya, Delaware) |
| Published | **July 2026**, inaugural full index | **March 2026**, *methodology report* — full index slated **Q4 2026** |
| Coverage | **54** African states, **4,320** scored observations | **55** AU member states, **10-country pilot** |
| Structure | **8 pillars, 80 indicators** | **8 pillars, 80 indicators** |
| Identifier | no DOI recorded; document URL unrecovered | **DOI 10.5281/zenodo.18895975** |

**They are different instruments from different organisations.** Do not merge them, cross-cite them, or
read a score from one as a score from the other. AAGI's own material reports inter-rater reliability
(**Cohen's κ = 0.74**) and concurrent validity with the Oxford Insights index (**r = 0.87**) — figures
that belong to AAGI, **not** to the instrument on this page.

**A third series compounds the risk:** the Lawyers Hub platform (`aipolicy.africa`) separately publishes
an **"AI Readiness Index"** with a **0–100** scale (Egypt 81, South Africa 76, Kenya 72 — as displayed
2026-07-21), which is **not** the AAIGI composite (a **0–4** scale). Same publisher, different series,
different scale. **Always name the instrument and the scale when citing any of these.**

## Handling — cited, not absorbed

**This is a reference study.** Its per-country composites and pillar scores are **not** promoted onto
place or concept pages as those countries' current state, per the wiki's standing rule on reference
studies. Cite the Index for **the Index's own findings and framing**; cite underlying primaries for
facts about any country.

Two cautions come from the report itself and are worth carrying whenever it is cited:

- **The rubric rewards adopted formal instruments, not capability.** The report states plainly that a
  rank order "measures formalisation, not capability", and that **composite differences below ~0.1 are
  not meaningful distinctions**. A state can score well by publishing instruments it does not
  implement — which is, self-referentially, the report's own headline finding.
- **The report argues its country ranks are the wrong unit of analysis.** It directs weight to
  **sub-regional averages**, on the argument that **the sub-region is the minimum viable sovereignty
  unit** for compute, data and rules. Citing a single country's rank from it therefore uses the
  instrument against its own stated method.

## What it establishes

Its central empirical claim is a **uniform inversion**: every sub-region scores **highest on Strategy
& Vision and lowest on Implementation & Impact**. The report frames this as a **shared structural
condition rather than a national failing** — strategy diffusion outpacing enforceable law,
ring-fenced budget and measurable oversight.

Sub-regional composites (0–4): **North Africa 1.56 · Southern Africa 1.49 · East Africa 1.27 · West
Africa 1.25 · Central Africa 1.07**. **No sub-region has crossed the midpoint** of the maturity
scale.

Its diagnosis of the binding constraint is specific and testable: **no Regional Economic Community
framework has yet been converted into national implementing legislation at scale**, and repairing
that transmission mechanism — "not drafting further strategies" — is the task it sets for the
remainder of AU Continental AI Strategy Phase 1. It identifies **West Africa's ECOWAS Supplementary
Act on Personal Data Protection** as the continent's **only binding regional data instrument**, and
proposes a **SADC model AI law** as "the cheapest sovereignty intervention available on the
continent".

## Figures it carries (attributed to the Index, not adopted)

Africa holds **under 1% of global data-centre capacity** against **18% of world population**;
approximately **3% of the world's AI talent**; an estimated **70,000 skilled professionals emigrate
annually**; **over 70% of assessed countries have enacted data-protection laws** while dedicated
enforceable AI legislation remains rare; and an estimated **US$1.25bn of AI venture capital since
2019**, **over 80% of it in four economies**. Each of these is the Index's own figure, sourced to its
reference list; **none of the underlying publications is held**.
