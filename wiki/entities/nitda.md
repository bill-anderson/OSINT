# National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA)

Nigeria's IT-development regulator, responsible for the National Cloud Policy and
data-exchange frameworks — the body tasked with breaking down the country's
government data silos.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Data silos vs AI ambitions (2026-06).** At the AI Summit Nigeria (Abuja),
  experts warned that **eight** major agencies (NIMC/NIN, CBN/BVN, NCC, NIS, FIRS,
  FRSC, CAC, INEC) hold **siloed, non-interoperable** citizen datasets — a
  structural block on Nigeria's AI plans. NITDA is developing data-exchange
  frameworks via the **National Cloud Policy**; 2025 saw the **National AI
  Strategy** and **N-Atlas** (Africa's first govt-backed multilingual LLM). DG
  Kashifu Inuwa Abdullahi. See [[NGA]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]],
  [[tech.ai]]. Source: [[2026-06-17 Government data silos threaten Nigeria's AI ambitions]].

- **Regulatory harmonisation committee (2026-07).** Named — with [[ncc]] and [[ndpc]] — to
  the joint technical coordination committee established by Minister [[bosun-tijani]] to
  develop a unified national digital-economy policy framework while existing regulations
  are held in place. See [[gov.policy]], [[nigeria--gov-protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-08 FG Halts Enforcement of New Regulations on Internet Platforms]].

- **NGEA takeover (2026-04).** NITDA formally took control of the **Nigeria Government
  Enterprise Architecture ([[ngea]])** infrastructure at an Abuja handover — built over
  ~2.5 years with Korea's KOICA under the e-Government Masterplan 2.0, hosted by
  **[[galaxy-backbone]]**, with pilots at [[nimc]], Customs and Immigration — to drive
  MDA interoperability and whole-of-government data exchange. Policy oversight stays with
  the Ministry of Communications, Innovation & Digital Economy. See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-07 NITDA Assumes Control Of NGEA Infrastructure]].

- **Digital-sovereignty / national software infrastructure (GITEX, 2026-04).** DG
  [[kashifu-inuwa]] told Ericsson at GITEX Africa (Morocco) that Nigeria is building
  "national software infrastructure" and high-standard data-sovereignty policy — not
  banning China but requiring hyperscalers to partner with local firms so value is
  "captured here." Cites the EU DMA/DSA/Data Governance Act as models; an executive order
  already designates all digital infrastructure as critical national infrastructure.
  Ericsson flagged an Oct-2024 government MoU (joint innovation hub, national hackathon,
  digital-skills programmes). See [[gov.policy]], [[geopol.china]], [[sovereignty]],
  [[mtn-group]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-14 Nigeria Seeks Stronger Digital Sovereignty, National Software Infrastructure]].

- **Trust Stamp digital-trust talks (2026-02).** NITDA held "strategic discussions" in Abuja with
  **[[trust-stamp]]** on its AI biometric-tokenization technology as the basis for a national
  digital-trust framework; DG [[kashifu-inuwa]] tied it to Nigeria's trillion-dollar digital-economy
  goal by 2030. Trust Stamp is already used by a Nigerian MNO for SIM-swap fraud prevention and by
  Ghana (Sept 2025); the parties agreed to pursue technical consultations (no signed deal yet). See
  [[dpi.id]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-24 Nigeria explores avenues for digital trust collaboration with Trust Stamp]].

- **Digital Literacy for All (DL4ALL) — disability inclusion (2026-01).** NITDA (DG
  [[kashifu-inuwa]]) trained 50 persons with disabilities in a two-day DL4ALL programme in
  Abuja; the informal-sector track has trained **>480,000 Nigerians across 30 states + FCT
  since Sept 2024**, toward national digital-literacy targets of **70% by 2027 and 95% by
  2030**. See [[dl4all-nitda]], [[include.access]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-23 NITDA Commits to Digital Inclusion for Persons with Disabilities]].
- **.ng domain sovereignty (2026-01).** NITDA enforces the **National Policy on Government
  Second-Level Domain** (FEC-approved 2022-02-16) mandating MDA migration to **.gov.ng**;
  commentary frames the under-adopted **.ng** ccTLD (~**237,266** active registrations as of
  Dec 2025, vs .za >1m, .ke >100k), managed by [[nira-nigeria]], as an unrealised
  digital-identity/sovereignty pillar — Anambra has migrated ~95% of its MDAs. See
  [[nira-nigeria]], [[dpi.id]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-21 How foreign domains compromise digital identity]].
- **WSIS+20 implementation push (2026-07).** At WSIS Forum 2026, NITDA co-hosted (with
  [[aficta]]) a session arguing Africa has the strategies but must now deliver **measurable
  implementation**, referencing UNECA's Africa 2035 roadmap and Nigeria's 3MTT skills drive.
  See [[gov.policy]], [[capacity.training]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Africa urged to turn WSIS+20 commitments into measurable digital progress]].
