# Bank of South Sudan

South Sudan's central bank, responsible for the country's monetary system and
its nascent interbank payments infrastructure.

## Activity in this wiki

- **NIPS project launch (2025-02-05).** BoSS announced a **project** to build a
  National Instant Payment System with the **[[africanenda]]** Foundation
  (governor **Johnny Ohisa Damian**) — a roadmap-and-capacity partnership, not a
  system go-live: the release commits to "developing a detailed roadmap for NIPS
  implementation" and quotes Damian on "the start of the NIPS journey", following a
  **2023 proof-of-concept**. Designed for real-time interoperable P2P/P2B/G2P/P2G
  rails integrating ACH, RTGS and IFT. **No NIPS go-live is documented** as of
  2026-07. See **[[nips-south-sudan]]**, [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-05-africanenda-boss-nips-announcement]]; trade-press account:
  [[2025-02-07 South Sudan launches first national instant payment system (NIPS)]].
- **SSIPS launch (2025-10-10).** BoSS launched the **South Sudan Interbank Payment
  and Settlement System (SSIPS)** — the **ATS+** platform combining **RTGS** (high-value,
  real-time) and **ACH** (bulk, batched) on **ISO 20022**, replacing paper-based manual
  interbank process. Rollout is **phased**, starting with a select number of banks;
  security rests on encrypted channels, MFA and an isolated network. Governor
  **Dr Addis Ababa Othow** presided; EAC and AfDB representatives framed it as aligning
  South Sudan with regional reform. This is **wholesale settlement plumbing, not a retail
  instant rail** — and it is distinct from NIPS, not a rebrand ("IBPSS" was a mangled
  acronym; resolved 2026-07-20, see **[[nips-south-sudan]]**). See [[SSD]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Sources:
  [[2025-10-10-boss-launches-first-electronic-interbank-payment-system]],
  [[2025-10-01-eyeradio-boss-ssips-ats-plus-explainer]].
- **Uptake caveat (2026-01).** Uptake remains limited: **not all banks are fully
  integrated**, outages and manual workarounds persist, and confidence is undercut by
  governance concerns that key tax/revenue systems are controlled by individuals
  rather than institutionally owned. Source:
  [[2026-01-30 Can a digital economy exist in South Sudan without reliable DPI]].
- **Context — no national digital ID.** The settlement system sits atop a wider
  DPI gap: South Sudan lacks a national digital identity layer, leaving mobile
  money, biometric payroll and humanitarian registries siloed and its de-facto
  DPI donor-driven and proprietary. See [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-30 Can a digital economy exist in South Sudan without reliable DPI]].
