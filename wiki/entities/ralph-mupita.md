# Ralph Mupita

Group President & CEO of **[[mtn-group]]**; a leading voice for African-owned
digital infrastructure and African-language AI.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Ambition 2030 (2026-06).** Unveiled MTN's "one MTN, three platforms"
  (connectivity, fintech, digital infrastructure) strategy with AI throughout —
  tripling fibre, 13× fintech, an AI-RAN consortium with **[[nvidia]]**. Source:
  [[2026-06-10 MTN Group goes all-in on platforms and AI]].
- **"New Frontier" interview (MWC 2026).** Argued coverage is largely solved and
  the next frontier is closing the **usage gap** ($40 handsets), embedding
  Africa's **2,000+ languages** into LLMs, and reintegrating tower infrastructure
  (IHS) under a "geotechnology" ownership lens. See [[include.divides]],
  [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-09 Ralph Mupita on MTN's New Frontier Connectivity, Content, and African AI]].
- **AI data-centre bet (2026-03).** On MTN's FY2025 results, Mupita backed the group's
  entry into the AI data-centre race — **South Africa and Nigeria** as priority markets —
  saying MTN is "well-positioned to leverage our scale, footprint and brand leadership to
  capture the significant structural growth opportunities." See [[mtn-group]],
  [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-28 MTN makes big bet on AI data centres in South Africa]].
- **MTN Ghana $1.1bn (2026-02).** On a three-day working visit to Ghana, Mupita announced
  MTN Ghana will reinvest **~$1.1bn over three years** in network resilience, 4G/5G, hyperscale
  data centres and Ghana's AI/cloud ambitions, plus youth digital-skills programmes — MTN Ghana
  holds ~58% market share and >25m subscribers (MoMo >11m active). See [[mtn-ghana]], [[GHA]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 MTN Ghana secures $1.1bn boost as AI push deepens]].

- **Feature-phone "voice era" (2026-01).** Mupita is quoted framing voice/NLP AI (e.g. MTN's
  **Miss Baza** assistant) as the route to bring hundreds of millions of feature-phone users
  into digital services — the "voice era" of African inclusion. See [[include.access]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.literacy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].

See [[mtn-group]], [[tech.ai]], [[infra.connect]].
