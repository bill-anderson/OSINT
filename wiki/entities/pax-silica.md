# Pax Silica

A US-led coalition (launched Dec 2025) committed to a "secure, resilient,
innovation-driven" AI supply chain, with a **$250m fund** for AI-supply-chain
security. As of May 2026, **14 signatories** across Europe, Asia, the Gulf and
Oceania — no African member.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Africa entry point (Carnegie).** [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]] proposes Kenyan
  participation via a TPD ([[technology-prosperity-deal]]); Gulf signatories like
  the **[[uae]]** are cast as a foundation for trilateral US–Gulf–Africa
  engagement. See [[KEN]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **EU accession (2026-06).** [[2026-06-11 The Case Against the EU's Tech Sovereignty Package]] notes the **EU joining** the US-led chip alliance — evidence, the
  author argues, that collaboration beats the EU's protectionist sovereignty push.
  See [[geopol.eu]].

- **India joins (2026-02).** India joined Pax Silica around the India AI Impact Summit
  (New Delhi), cited alongside the launch of the US [[tech-corps]] "Peace Corps for AI" and
  the [[american-ai-exports-program]] as part of Washington's push to diffuse the American
  AI stack into partner nations against Chinese alternatives. See [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-02 The U.S. Launches a Peace Corps for AI to Compete With China]].

- **Critique — alignment over industrial heft (CEPA, 2026).** A CEPA commentary argues
  Pax Silica's membership (founding signatories Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Israel, UAE,
  UK, Australia) reflects political alignment more than semiconductor industrial weight, and
  notes the coalition's coherence is undercut by Washington allowing **NVIDIA H200** sales
  to China for a **25% export fee**. See [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-14 Pax Silica — Can it Stop China]].

- **Full minerals-strategy context (IISS, Jan 2026).** An IISS analysis frames Pax Silica
  as the third track of Trump's state-capitalist critical-minerals strategy — after **EO
  14241** (Mar 2025, fast-tracking mineral permitting under the 1950 Defense Production Act)
  and the **OBBBA** (Jul 2025) appropriating **$7.5bn** for critical minerals and letting the
  Pentagon take equity (e.g. **$400m DoD equity** in MP Materials; the ~$1.8bn Orion
  Consortium with an Abu Dhabi SWF). It dates the coalition's launch to **Dec 2025** with a
  founding roster of Australia, Israel, Japan, Netherlands, Singapore, South Korea and the UK,
  Qatar and the **[[uae]]** joining, led by Under Secretary of State **Jacob Helberg** — a
  line-up that predates and differs from the "14 signatories (as of May 2026)" count above, as
  membership expanded over the intervening months (dating both, not overwriting). Africa's
  relevance is a **DRC** bilateral minerals track tied to US security guarantees. See
  [[COD]], [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 US critical-minerals diplomacy from America-First deals to Pax Silica]].
