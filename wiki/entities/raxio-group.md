# Raxio Group

A pan-African **data-centre operator** building carrier-neutral, **Tier III** colocation facilities in
second-tier and frontier African markets. Backed by infrastructure investors **Meridiam** and **Roha**, with
development-finance debt from the **International Finance Corporation (IFC)**, **Proparco** and the **Emerging
Africa and Asia Infrastructure Fund**. CEO: **Robert Skjodt**.

## Profile

- Operating data centres in **Uganda, Ethiopia, Mozambique, DR Congo, Côte d'Ivoire and Angola**; expansion
  into **Tanzania** under development. All facilities Tier III certified and carrier-neutral colocation.
- Positioned to serve African markets under-served by the hyperscale-heavy South Africa / Nigeria / Kenya
  cluster — a distributed footprint across smaller national markets.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Equity top-up above US$380m committed capital (2026-07).** Existing shareholders **Meridiam** and
  **Roha** injected additional equity, lifting Raxio's committed capital base **above US$380m** — building on
  a **2025 US$100m IFC** package plus Proparco / EAAIF debt. The company reported signing **6× more power
  capacity in H1-2026 than H1-2025** and rising demand for **10MW+** high-density deployments and AI workloads,
  citing McKinsey's projection that African data-centre capacity rises from **0.4GW to 1.5–2.2GW by 2030**.
  Confirmed 13 Jul 2026: the **Roha + Meridiam** top-up lifted committed capital **$350m → $380m**; the
  open question is whether **unreliable power** can keep pace with contracted capacity. See [[infra.store]],
  [[finance.new]]. Sources: [[2026-07-14 Raxio raises data centre funding]],
  [[2026-07-15 Raxio data-centre capital tops $380m on investor boost]].
- **Raxio Angola (AG1), Cacuaco — country's largest DC (2025-10).** Opened Angola's biggest and only
  **Tier III** commercial data centre: up to **3 MW** / 800+ racks across 19,000 m² (Raxio's biggest
  campus), a **$30m** development sited near Luanda's undersea-cable landings to host local traffic;
  plan to exceed 7 MW within five years. Telecom/IT Minister Oliveira officiated. See [[AGO]],
  [[angola--infra-store]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-02 Raxio Opens Angola's Largest Data Centre in Landmark 30 Million Expansion]].
