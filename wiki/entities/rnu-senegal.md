# RNU — Registre National Unique (Senegal social registry)

Senegal's **National Single Registry** — the principal mechanism for **targeting poor and vulnerable
households** for social-protection programmes. Line/coordination agency: **[[dgpsn-senegal|DGPSN]]**; database
historically hosted by **[[senegal-numerique|ADIE/SENUM]]**; legal framework and data use regulated by the
**[[cdp-senegal|CDP]]**; geographic targeting/scoring supported by **ANSD**, with **[[world-bank]]** and
UNICEF as technical/financial partners.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Origins (2015→).** Government opted to build the RNU in **2015**, extending tools from the National
  Family Security Grant programme (PNBSF, launched 2013) under the Emerging Senegal Plan (PSE). Source:
  [[2019-05-01 Senegal National Single Registry RNU (IPC-IG research brief 63)]].
- **Coverage expansion (2024).** The RNU **grew from 542,000 to one million households in 2024**, now
  integrating both poor households and those vulnerable to climate/economic shocks (per DGPSN, Dec 2025). It
  underpins the **Family Security Grant** (Bourse de sécurité familiale) — e.g. 64,000 people with
  disabilities as recipients (2025). See [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]], [[SEN]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-09 Senegal's RNU social registry rises from 542,000 to one million households]].

The RNU is the targeting database the **National Interoperability Platform**'s first phase is designed to
serve for faster social-assistance delivery.
