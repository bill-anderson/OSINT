# Ministry of Information, Communication and Information Technology (MICIT), Tanzania

Tanzania's **ICT policy ministry** — owner of the **[[tanzania-digital-economy-framework]]**
(the 2024–2034 national digital economy strategy) and the ministry behind the **National
Physical Addressing** programme (**[[napa-tanzania]]**). The **[[TZA]]** hub names **Angela
Kairuki** as ICT Minister (2026-04).

**Naming variants — recorded, not normalised.** Sources render this ministry two ways, and the
difference is left visible rather than silently harmonised:

- **"Ministry of Information, Communication and Information Technology"** — the 2024-07-10 press
  account and the MICIT imprint on the 2024 Digital Economy Strategic Framework.
- **"Ministry of Communication and Information Technology"** — the 2026-02-09 account.

Almost certainly the **same ministry** (a rendering difference or a rename between 2024 and
2026); held as one page pending confirmation of whether a formal rename occurred.

## Activity in this wiki

- **National Physical Addressing (NaPA) drive (2024-07-10).** The ministry launched a push to
  register every Tanzanian in the NaPA system; assistant coordinator **Arnold Mkude** said at
  the 48th Dar es Salaam International Trade Fair that NaPA **"has reached 85 percent of
  Tanzanians nationwide"** since the 2022 Population and Housing Census report, targeting
  complete registration "by the next fiscal year" while conceding it must be continuous as new
  houses and plots are built. The ministry describes NaPA as a digital system deployed across
  residences, hospitals, shops, schools, banks and government institutions. See
  [[napa-tanzania]], [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.localgov]]. Source:
  [[2024-07-10 Tanzania ramps up national physical address registration]].
- **Address programme at scale (2026-02-09).** Deputy Permanent Secretary **Nicholas Mkapa**
  told the Annual Residential Address Conference that **"Tanzania began its nationwide
  residential address registration in 2010. To date, over 12.9 million addresses have been
  successfully recorded and integrated into our digital systems"**, with **29 systems
  interconnected**, including the **[[tanzania-posts-corporation]]**. See [[napa-tanzania]],
  [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Tanzania registers 12 million housing addresses to boost digital governance]].
- **Digital Economy Strategic Framework 2024–2034 (MICIT, 2024).** The ministry published
  Tanzania's ten-year digital economy strategy, with a foreword by **President Samia Suluhu
  Hassan** as Chairperson of the Tanzania National Business Council, anchored to the National
  ICT Policy, the National Five-Year Development Plan 2021/22–2025/26, SDG Agenda 2030 and AU
  Agenda 2063. See [[tanzania-digital-economy-framework]], [[gov.policy]],
  [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2024-01-01 Tanzania Digital Economy Strategic Framework 2024-2034 (MICIT)]].
- **A statutory role in the data regime.** Under the **[[tanzania-pdpa]]**, the **Minister for
  Communication**, in consultation with the **[[pdpc-tanzania]]**, may issue regulations
  specifying countries to which cross-border transfers are **not permitted** — an executive
  lever over data flows held by this ministry, not by the regulator alone. See [[gov.protect]],
  [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-07 The Tanzanian Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) and its applicability in Arbitration Proceedings]].
