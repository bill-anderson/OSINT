# Sierra Leone Data Protection and Right to Access Information Bill, 2025

Sierra Leone's first comprehensive data-protection instrument, combining **data protection**
and **right-to-access-information** in a single statute. It would create, for the first time,
a **single independent authority** responsible for both regimes.

## Status

- **Final national validation concluded (2025-11-09).** Announced by Chernor Bah, Minister
  of Information and Civil Education. Next steps: Cabinet approval, then Parliament for
  enactment. **Not yet law as of Nov 2025.** Source:
  [[2025-11-09 Government Finalizes Sierra Leones Data Protection and Right to Access Information Bill]].

## Provisions (as validated)

- Stricter data-protection obligations on **banks, mobile network operators and government
  agencies** for citizens' financial, digital, tax and health records.
- A **single independent authority** for data protection and access to information.
- Boundaries on journalists/citizens sharing personal information; alignment with
  international data standards for trade; regulation of intelligence-agency use of data,
  balancing national security and privacy.
- Drafted inclusively (Parliament, local councils, security agencies, ECSL, civil society,
  media, academia, private sector); development-partner support from **Irish Aid** and
  **UNDP**.

## Why it matters here

The absence of an enacted data-protection framework is repeatedly flagged (World Bank) as
the binding constraint on safe reuse of the [[ncra]] NIN across services — this bill is the
gap-filler. Tracks the wider African [[gov.protect]] wave. See [[SLE]], [[gov.legislate]].

**Cabinet approval (2026-04-21).** Cabinet approved a **National Data Protection Policy** and authorised enactment of a new **Data Protection and Right to Access Information Act** — repealing the RAIC Act and merging data-protection and access-to-information into a single strengthened authority (the existing **RAIC** transformed with enhanced investigative/compliance/enforcement powers over public and private data handling). MoICE (Min. Chernor Bah) and MoCTI piloted it; the AG's office to table the Bill in Parliament. Source: [[2026-04-22-cabinet-data-protection-policy]].
