# Eswatini-Google Digital Transformation Partnership

**Parties:** the Government of Eswatini ([[SWZ]]), via [[ministry-of-ict-eswatini]], and
[[google]] (counterparty **Google Cloud EMEA**). **What:** an **MoU** — a cloud-services and
e-government framework, not a monetisation deal — to integrate Google's tools into the
[[government-in-your-hand]] initiative. **Launched on or before 2025-01-20** by Minister of
ICT [[savannah-maziya]], at an event at the **ERS Auditorium** themed *"Innovating Together:
Strategic Partnerships for Eswatini's Digital Transformation and Economic Prosperity."* See
[[finance.mou]], [[geopol.usa]].

> **Date correction (2026-07-20).** This page previously gave the announcement as
> **2025-01-22** — that was **Techpoint Africa's publication date**, promoted to the event
> date. The domestic account (Inside Biz, **2025-01-20**) reports the launch as already held,
> and the Ministry of ICT posted its organiser thanks on 2025-01-21. Day precision on the
> event itself is not firmly fixed; read it as *on or before 2025-01-20*.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Stated focus (2025-01-20).** Three strands: equipping young people with digital
  competencies ([[capacity.training]]); leveraging Google technologies to improve the
  efficiency and accessibility of public services ([[dpi.govtech]]); and fostering
  innovation and entrepreneurship ([[tech.innovate]]). Sources:
  [[2025-01-20-insidebiz-eswatini-partners-with-google]],
  [[2025-01-22 Eswatini partners with Google to drive digital transformation and economic growth]].
- **The workstreams, in Google's own words (2025-01-20).** **Basil Ayass**, Google Cloud's
  regional lead for Africa, set out three: **digital modernisation of e-government services**;
  **AI** "to help become more efficient"; and **Google Workspace for public servants**. Plus
  **digital skills academies** with certification in ML, AI and data analytics, and
  **digital addressing** for areas without street addresses. Maziya claimed Eswatini as the
  **first African country to integrate Google Workspace into government operations**. See
  [[tech.ai]]. Source: [[2025-01-20-insidebiz-eswatini-partners-with-google]].
- **Answered to Parliament on data sovereignty (2025-04).** In the Senate Portfolio Committee's
  FY2025/26 budget debate, **Senator Fezeka Dlamini** asked whether national data would be
  compromised, noting the global move to keep data within borders. Maziya replied that the
  ministry had **signed a non-disclosure agreement** — "standard global practice" — with access
  **"granted on a need-to-know basis, and… normally deactivated post implementation,"** and
  said the **Access to Information Bill** was being finalised. See [[sovereignty]],
  [[gov.protect]].
- **Extension toward PKI (2025-10).** Eswatini was reported exploring further Google Cloud
  services — **digital addressing, Public Key Infrastructure and AI tools**. PKI is the layer
  the GIYH privacy criticism identified as missing; see [[government-in-your-hand]].
- **Maziya's framing.** "This partnership is a step towards positioning Eswatini as a
  leader in the digital economy, unlocking opportunities for our people, especially the
  youth"; the collaboration "symbolises a shared vision of leveraging innovation to
  transform Eswatini into a digital hub that competes globally." Her accompanying **"70%
  of our population being youth"** is ministerial rhetoric — the [[world-bank]] puts
  young people at **30% of the population (2025-05-12)** on a different definition; see
  [[savannah-maziya]].
- **Context supplied by the outlet, not the parties.** Techpoint situates the deal in
  Google's prior African activity — a $1bn Africa investment fund announced in 2021, the
  [[google-for-startups-accelerator-africa]], and the Equiano subsea cable. None of these
  is stated to form part of this partnership.

**Provenance — resolved (2026-07-20), formerly GAP-SWZ-001.** No longer single-sourced. The deal
now rests on a domestic Eswatini account
([[2025-01-20-insidebiz-eswatini-partners-with-google]]), an in-window trade primary
([[2025-01-21-google-works-with-eswatini-to-accelerate-digital-economy]]), **Google Cloud's own
regional lead quoted at length at the launch**, and **the minister answering for it in the
Senate (2025-04)**. In Apr 2026 it became a cabinet controversy: PM Russell Dlamini first
dismissed it, then acknowledged **Cabinet had approved** it (the AG confirmed the minister
consulted Cabinet and the AG). An investigation established the MoU — with **Google Cloud
EMEA** — was a cloud-services/e-government framework, not a monetisation deal. Eswatini later
featured at the Google Cloud Summit Africa 2026. Full case: [[eswatini--dpi-govtech]].

**What remains undisclosed — an established absence, not an open question.** Neither party has
published a **value, term or signature date**, and no agreement document is public. The
instrument is consistently described as a **partnership/MoU**, never a contract with
consideration. Two-plus years of parliamentary questioning has not produced these figures; treat
their absence as a finding about the deal's opacity, not a gap in the wiki's collection.
