# Eswatini-Google Digital Transformation Partnership

**Parties:** the Government of Eswatini ([[SWZ]]), via [[ministry-of-ict-eswatini]], and
[[google]]. **What:** a partnership to integrate Google's tools into the
[[government-in-your-hand]] initiative and drive digital transformation and economic
growth. **Announced:** **2025-01-22** by Minister of ICT [[savannah-maziya]], at a launch
event themed *"Innovating Together: Strategic Partnerships for Eswatini's Digital
Transformation and Economic Prosperity."* See [[finance.mou]], [[geopol.usa]].

> **Provenance gap — read this page with care.** The wiki's **only** source is a single
> secondary outlet, **Techpoint Africa**. No [[google]] statement, no Government of
> Eswatini announcement, and no primary agreement document is held. The instrument type
> (MoU? contract? letter of intent?), its value, duration, deliverables and signature
> date are all **unknown** — the announcement is the whole of the record. Nothing here
> is corroborated.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Stated focus (2025-01-22).** Three strands: equipping young people with digital
  competencies ([[capacity.training]]); leveraging Google technologies to improve the
  efficiency and accessibility of public services ([[dpi.govtech]]); and fostering
  innovation and entrepreneurship ([[tech.innovate]]). Source:
  [[2025-01-22 Eswatini partners with Google to drive digital transformation and economic growth]].
- **Maziya's framing.** "This partnership is a step towards positioning Eswatini as a
  leader in the digital economy, unlocking opportunities for our people, especially the
  youth"; the collaboration "symbolises a shared vision of leveraging innovation to
  transform Eswatini into a digital hub that competes globally." Her accompanying **"70%
  of our population being youth"** is ministerial rhetoric — the [[world-bank]] puts
  young people at **30% of the population (2025-05-12)** on a different definition; see
  [[savannah-maziya]].
- **Context supplied by the outlet, not the parties.** Techpoint situates the deal in
  Google's prior African activity — a $1bn Africa investment fund announced in 2021, the
  [[google-for-startups-accelerator-africa]], and the Equiano subsea cable. None of these
  is stated to form part of this partnership.

**Provenance & the 2026 controversy (resolves GAP-SWZ-001).** The Jan-2025 MoU is now backed by in-window journal primaries ([[2025-01-21-google-works-with-eswatini-to-accelerate-digital-economy]]). In Apr 2026 it became a cabinet controversy: PM Russell Dlamini first dismissed it, then acknowledged **Cabinet had approved** it (AG confirmed the minister consulted Cabinet/AG). An investigation established the MoU — with **Google Cloud EMEA** — was a **cloud-services/e-government framework, not a monetisation deal**. Eswatini later featured at the Google Cloud Summit Africa 2026 (Ramaphosa acknowledgement). Full case: [[eswatini--dpi-govtech]].
