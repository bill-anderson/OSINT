# Claire Ollama

**Registrar of [[nira-uganda]]** (as of 2026-02) — the official who announced Uganda's
full transition to its new national digital ID system, and, in the same briefing, the
figures that show how far short of its target the enrolment fell.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The Feb-2026 review (press conference, 9 February 2026; reported 2026-02-11).** Ollama
  said Uganda had **fully transitioned** to the new national digital ID system, migrating
  **more than 28 million identity records** from the legacy system. The exercise **started
  May 2025** and **targeted over 33 million people**. With migrated records plus new
  registrations and updates, total enrolments reached **more than 35 million**: *"With the
  migrated data from the old system and the new people registered, the new national
  register has over 35 million Ugandans who can be uniquely identified via biometrics,"*
  she said. She also gave the split — **renewals 14.3m of a 15.8m target (90.5%)**,
  **first-time enrolments only 6.4m of a 17.2m target (37.3%)** — and that of **10.1m
  cards printed, only 2.5m had been picked up**. Phase one ended at parish level; the
  process continues as routine activity at NIRA offices. Speaking at Police Headquarters,
  Kampala (reported by New Vision). See [[ndaga-muntu]], [[dpi.id]], [[mosip]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-11 Uganda migrates more than 28M records to new national ID system]].
