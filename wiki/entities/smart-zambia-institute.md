# Smart Zambia Institute (SZI)

Zambia's central digital-government body, sitting under the **Office of the President**.
Designated **implementing agency** for [[dzap]] — hosts the Project Implementation Unit
and answers to the Project Steering Committee.

## Role (as of 2026-07)

- Lead institution for Zambia's e-government and DPI agenda, including the ZamPortal /
  eServices stack ([[dpi.govtech]]) and the National e-Government Plan 2023–2026.
- Executes the World Bank–financed DZAP programme on connectivity, platforms and
  skills. See [[ZMB]].
- **Open-source directive + NSDI (2026-06).** Zambia's Cabinet directed ministries to adopt **open-source technology**, alongside the launch of the National Spatial Data Infrastructure (NSDI) Policy and Portal — reinforcing the vendor-independence / open-standards posture. See [[data.open]], [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2026-06-17 Zambia's govt leans towards open-source]].
- **MOSIP system-integrator procurement (2026-03).** National Coordinator Percy Chinyama confirmed SZI is seeking an international system integrator to deploy Zambia's [[mosip]]-based national digital ID integrated with the civil-registration architecture — insisting on a home-grown build on open standards to avoid lock-in, with full rollout and 80% citizen digital-economy inclusion targeted for end-2026, financed via [[dzap]]. Zambia holds an MoU with Ethiopia to draw on the [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]] model. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[ZMB]]. Sources: [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]], [[2026-03-24 Zambia seeks partner for national digital ID system]].
- **Online passport system (2026-01).** SZI's e-Government Division launched Zambia's **Online Passport Application & Payment System** (~8 Jan 2026); 1,000+ passports processed online in the first weeks, part of a paperless push projected to save >ZMW400m (~$15m) in stationery costs. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-01-08 Zambia unveils online passport application system]].
- **E-Government Act amendment (2026-01).** SZI tabled an **Electronic Government (Amendment) Bill** revising Act No. 41 of 2021 — establishing legal standing for electronic submissions and digital authentication via certified channels and targeting laws still requiring physical documents/signatures — to shift Zambia from fragmented, vendor-locked systems to an integrated, interoperable digital-service ecosystem. See [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]]. Source: [[2026-01-23 Zambia begins amending Electronic Government Act]].
