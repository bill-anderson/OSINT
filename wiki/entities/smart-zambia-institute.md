# Smart Zambia Institute (SZI)

Zambia's central digital-government body, sitting under the **Office of the President**.
Designated **implementing agency** for [[dzap]] — hosts the Project Implementation Unit
and answers to the Project Steering Committee.

## Role (as of 2026-07)

- Lead institution for Zambia's e-government and DPI agenda, including the ZamPortal /
  eServices stack ([[dpi.govtech]]) and the National e-Government Plan 2023–2026.
- Executes the World Bank–financed DZAP programme on connectivity, platforms and
  skills. See [[ZMB]].
- **Open-source directive + NSDI (2026-06).** Zambia's Cabinet directed ministries to adopt **open-source technology**, alongside the launch of the National Spatial Data Infrastructure (NSDI) Policy and Portal — reinforcing the vendor-independence / open-standards posture. See [[data.open]], [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2026-06-17 Zambia's govt leans towards open-source]].
- **MOSIP work began earlier than the SI procurement (2025-01).** SZI e-government developers had **completed MOSIP training** — delivered by **[[ihm-southern-africa]]**'s software team — aimed at integrating [[mosip]] with the **[[smartcare-pro]]** EHR for e-KYC and a "central source of truth", financed via [[dzap]]. The architecture **allows integration with existing biometric databases without full system replacement**, and the training was explicitly aimed at letting **Zambian developers independently manage and maintain the platform**. **The March-2026 SI hunt was not the start of Zambia's MOSIP thread** — see [[inris]] and [[zambia--dpi-id]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.mis]], [[capacity.training]]. Source: [[2025-01-17 Zambia Advances Digital ID System with MOSIP Training for Healthcare Integration]].
- **End-2026 rollout asserted, and disputed (2026-01-05).** Percy Chinyama, on a working visit to the **United States**, said citizens will receive national digital ID cards **by the close of 2026** — *"By the end of 2026, Zambia will have digital identification cards"* — a [[dzap]] milestone. But Registrar General **[[davies-chikalanga]]** of [[dnrpc-zambia]], who runs registration, puts it at **end-2027**, and [[inris]] reporting has biometric NRC issuance starting only **after the 2026 General Elections**. **Resolved (2026-07-20): different deliverables, not rival dates** — SZI's end-2026 is **first issuance** against an initial 2m goal, DNRPC's end-2027 is **population-scale coverage** by the body legally mandated to issue; the funded DZAP target is **4m people with digitally verifiable ID by October 2030**, project closing 2030-10-31. With issuance deferred past the 2026-08-13 election and no MOSIP integrator contracted as of 2026-07-20, end-2026 reads as aspiration. Chinyama added that "**the World Bank has expressed satisfaction with the progress made, which may result in additional support**" — the dependency dynamic stated plainly. DZAP sits in phase 2 of the **[[idea-mpa]]** programme (~**$2.48bn**, **15 countries**, **eight years**, [[comesa]]-coordinated). A **[[tony-blair-institute]]**/[[undp]] MoU was also signed to support Zambia's digital transformation. Source: [[2026-01-05 Zambia programs national digital ID rollout for year-end]].
- **Ethiopia MoU dated and named (2025-10).** The MoU recorded elsewhere in the wiki undated and unsigned was signed by **Percy Chinyama** (SZI National Coordinator) and **Yodahe Zemichael** (ED of Ethiopia's NIDP), to collaborate on digital-identity implementation. Chinyama said at the **Africa Innovation Conference** (April 2025, Addis Ababa) that Zambia wants to follow Ethiopia's model **because it is centred on a domestically developed system** — the sovereignty rationale stated plainly. SZI's E-Government Division was also tendering an **MVNO contract under a PPP**; on the current stack, "**the current platform is hindering government efforts to identify duplicate social program beneficiaries**." See [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]], [[ETH]], [[finance.mou]], [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2025-10-03 Zambia plans domestic digital ID development with help from international partners]].
- **MOSIP system-integrator procurement (2026-03).** National Coordinator Percy Chinyama confirmed SZI is seeking an international system integrator to deploy Zambia's [[mosip]]-based national digital ID integrated with the civil-registration architecture — insisting on a home-grown build on open standards to avoid lock-in, with full rollout and 80% citizen digital-economy inclusion targeted for end-2026, financed via [[dzap]]. Zambia holds an MoU with Ethiopia to draw on the [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]] model. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[ZMB]]. Sources: [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]], [[2026-03-24 Zambia seeks partner for national digital ID system]].
- **Online passport system (2026-01).** SZI's e-Government Division launched Zambia's **Online Passport Application & Payment System** (~8 Jan 2026); 1,000+ passports processed online in the first weeks, part of a paperless push projected to save >ZMW400m (~$15m) in stationery costs. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-01-08 Zambia unveils online passport application system]].
- **E-Government Act amendment (2026-01).** SZI tabled an **Electronic Government (Amendment) Bill** revising Act No. 41 of 2021 — establishing legal standing for electronic submissions and digital authentication via certified channels and targeting laws still requiring physical documents/signatures — to shift Zambia from fragmented, vendor-locked systems to an integrated, interoperable digital-service ecosystem. See [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]]. Source: [[2026-01-23 Zambia begins amending Electronic Government Act]].

- **GovLink Private 4G, and the connectivity ladder behind it (2026-07).** SZI's **e-Government
  Division** launched **[[govlink|GovLink Private 4G]]** (15 Jul 2026, Ndola Teaching Hospital) — a
  government-owned network for public institutions, health first, built with
  **[[ihm-southern-africa]]**/TuSo/Quadrupleplay and the **[[ministry-of-health-zambia|Ministry of
  Health]]**. It caps a multi-year push: **[[starlink]] kits in underserved public facilities since
  2024**; **all 116 local authorities connected to the Government Wide Area Network (GWAN) in
  2025** *(see contradiction note in [[zambia--dpi-govtech]])*; a **[[zesco]]** partnership to
  extend access to schools, health facilities and other administrations; and a **decentralised
  digital-skills training programme for government staff launched December 2025**. Zambia ranked
  **130th of 193 on the UN E-Government Development Index (2024)**, **0.5424/1**. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[capacity.training]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-17-zambia-govlink-private-4g]], [[2026-07-17-zambie-lance-reseau-4g-prive-govlink]].
