# Department of Land Reform and Rural Development (South Africa)

**The South African department responsible for land administration and the deeds registry** — and,
through the office of the **Chief Registrar of Deeds**, the owner of the country's transition from
paper to electronic land registration. (The gov.za statement issues under the Department of
Agriculture, Land Reform and Rural Development.)

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launched the eDRS (2025-04-01).** The department announced the launch of the **Electronic Deeds
  Registration System** (**[[edrs-south-africa]]**) on Tuesday **1 April 2025**, framed as "a
  significant advancement of South Africa's land registration system as well as the provisioning of
  deeds registration information to clients and the public." Source:
  [[2025-04-01 Land Reform and Rural Development implements Electronic Deeds Registration System]].
- **Statutory instruments it operates under.** The **Electronic Deeds Registration Systems Act 19 of
  2019**, in full operation from the launch date per **Proclamation 250 of 2025**, which empowers
  the department's **Chief Registrar of Deeds** to develop, establish and maintain the system; and
  the **Deeds Registries Act 47 of 1937**, still in force. The department intends to see **both
  repealed** and replaced with new legislation **by 2030**. Source: same.
- **Its own stated constraints (2025-04-01).** The department is explicit that the **registration
  module is in a pilot phase**, that the portal is accessible only "**in a limited capacity**", and
  that **manual and electronic registration will run in parallel for five years at the conveyancer's
  discretion** — an unusually candid set of caveats to publish alongside a launch. Source: same.
- **Regional signal.** This clip arrived mis-routed with the wiki's Zimbabwe batch — because
  **Zimbabwean deeds-registry material cites South Africa as the model being copied**. The eDRS is
  therefore a regional reference point, not only a domestic one. Source: same.
