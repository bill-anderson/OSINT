# Digital Services Unit (Presidency of South Africa)

The **Digital Services Unit (DSU)** is a small delivery team inside the **South African Presidency** that builds the government's digital public infrastructure. Operating "as a startup inside the Presidency," it is the builder of **[[mymzansi]]**, the single digital entry point to government services, and the operational engine of the government's **[[sa-digital-transformation-roadmap]]**. It is led on service design and delivery by **Richard Gevers**, founder of the civic-tech outfit Open Cities Lab before he joined government.

## What it does

The DSU's method is deliberately fast and reuse-first: it assembles government services from modular **digital public goods (DPGs)** rather than commissioning bespoke systems. For MyMzansi it built a working prototype in **10 weeks**, using an open-source stack — Silverstripe for content/forms, the UK's **GOV.UK Pay** and **GOV.UK Notify**, and verifiable credentials via NG by Credisure — with technical partners including **[[amazon-web-services]]** (Pete Herlihy, AWS global DPI lead) and Silverstripe. The resulting prototype demonstrated a functional digital ID, driver's licence, payments gateway and a verifiable digital wallet with **Bluetooth offline** credential validation, showcased live at the **Global DPI Summit** in Cape Town (November 2025).

More than **200 public servants** are involved across the wider programme, guided to 2030 by the Presidency's **Inter-Ministerial Committee on Digital Transformation (IMC)**. Gevers frames the ambition as an **African-led** approach to DPI that other countries can "borrow, remix and localise," and designs services for "people in South Africa" — potentially including residents and migrant workers — rather than citizens alone. The unit's positioning stresses interoperability and open standards to avoid vendor lock-in.
