# Afreximbank (African Export-Import Bank)

Pan-African multilateral trade-finance institution, headquartered in Cairo, that positions
digital payments infrastructure as the settlement backbone for intra-African trade under
[[afcfta]]. Its sovereignty pitch is explicit: local-currency settlement that reduces the
continent's dependence on the US dollar and correspondent banking.

## Activity in this wiki

- **African Trade Report 2025 — the named constraints on intra-African trade (as cited 2026-07-17).**
  Afreximbank's annual flagship identifies **limited access to trade finance, the high cost of
  cross-border payments, and inadequate financial infrastructure** as the persistent brakes on trade
  across the continent — the same three the bank's own [[papss]] work targets, and the demand-side
  argument private trade-payments entrants such as [[kutanapay]] cite. The report itself is **not
  held**; it reaches the wiki through a trade-press account. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-kutanapay-ai-blockchain-cross-border-trade]].
- **Digital-payments suite unveiled in Egypt (2026-05-30).** Afreximbank launched a suite of
  products centred on [[papss]] (the Pan-African Payment and Settlement System), enabling
  local-currency settlement and cutting USD dependence. New products include the African
  Currency Marketplace and PAPSSCARD, a continent-wide card scheme. PAPSS is integrated with
  Kenya's Pesalink ([[KEN]]), extending reach into East African retail rails. The bank frames
  the suite as unlocking Africa's "trillion-dollar" trade potential under [[afcfta]]. Tags
  [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]], [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-05-30 Afreximbank Banks on Digital Payments to Unlock Africa's Trillion-Dollar Trade Potential]].

- **PAPSS founding role (backfill, 2022).** Afreximbank developed and launched [[papss]] with the
  [[afcfta]] Secretariat (Sept 2021; formal launch Jan 2022, HQ Cairo, platform by StoneX), acting
  as the **main settlement agent and settlement guarantor**. It approved **$500m** to back the West
  African Monetary Zone pilot and pledged a further **$3bn** for continental rollout; PAPSS spans
  ~40+ African currencies and targets **~$5bn/yr** in saved transaction costs by cutting reliance on
  non-African correspondent banking. President **Benedict Oramah** chairs the PAPSS Management Board.
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]]. Sources:
  [[2022-08-30 Pan-African Payment and Settlement System drives Africa's banking integration]],
  [[2022-05-11 Pan-African Payment and Settlement System]].

- **Davos digital-rails advocacy (2026-01).** At an Africa Collective panel at Davos/WEF 2026,
  Afreximbank (Ekene Uzor) argued **AfCFTA will stall without shared digital "rails"** —
  payments, data exchange, digital ID, connectivity — with [[papss]] positioned as the
  cornerstone for a 1.4bn-person market. Advocacy, not a new transaction. See [[gov.regional]],
  [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-01-23 Afreximbank flags Africa's digital trade gap at Davos]].
- **PAPSSCARD + Nigeria's H1'25 dip (2026-01).** PAPSSCARD (the continent-wide card scheme with
  Mercury Payment Services; PAPSS CEO Mike Ogbalu III) is the retail extension of the settlement
  rail. On the Nigeria dip: CBN's Financial Stability Report (30 Jun 2025) shows **a reported
  Nigerian PAPSS line fell 53% to ₦5.6bn in H1'25** (3,246 txns, −29%) — a **real** drop (the count
  fell too, so not an FX effect), but at **~US$3.7m/half-year** a **narrow line item**, not Nigeria's
  total throughput and not a genuine counter-signal to the rail's growth (REV-002 resolved
  2026-07-16). See [[NGA]], [[nigeria--dpi-pay]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27 Nigeria's PAPSS cross-border payments falls 53% to N5.6bn]].
- **Settlement agent for the Onafriq–PAPSS Ghana pilot (2025-06-23).** [[dpi.pay]] Afreximbank acts
  as **settlement agent** for the [[onafriq]]–[[papss]] cross-border service launched in Ghana under a
  BoG six-month pilot ([[onafriq-papss-ghana-launch]]) — the same settlement-guarantor role it plays
  across PAPSS. See [[GHA]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-23 Onafriq and PAPSS launch cross-border payments in Ghana]].
