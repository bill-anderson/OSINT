# Gambia Personal Data Protection and Privacy Act, 2025

The Gambia's **first comprehensive personal-data-protection law**, passed by the National Assembly in
October 2025 after a multi-year stalled process, establishing an independent oversight **Commission**
([[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]]). It follows the validation of the National Data Policy and aligns
with the Open Data Strategy 2023-2026. See [[GMB]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Passed (2025-10).** Sets standards for collecting, processing and sharing personal data; Information
  Minister Dr Ismaila Ceesay called it a "major milestone." Sources:
  [[2025-10-13 Gambia Passes Landmark Data Protection and Privacy Law]],
  [[2025-10-29 The Gambia strengthens digital trust with personal data protection & privacy bill]].
- **Provisions (review, 2025-12).** [[gov.legislate]] Extraterritorial jurisdiction; seven lawful bases
  (consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public task, legitimate interest,
  archiving/research/statistics); heightened protection for sensitive data (genetic, biometric, health,
  race, politics); robust data-subject rights (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, objection
  incl. direct marketing, protection from solely-automated profiling, complaints + compensation). Source:
  [[2025-12-26 Review of Gambia's Personal Data Protection and Privacy Act, 2025]].
