# Orange Mali

Mali's **leading mobile network operator** (OOKLA-awarded "Western Africa's fastest mobile
network" / "Mali's fastest ISP"), a subsidiary of the **[[orange]]** group majority-held via
Senegal's **Sonatel**; serves **>12 million subscribers (2025)** and holds the market lead
(~54.9% share). Market rival to **[[sotelma]]** (Moov Africa Malitel).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Intelsat 4G-over-satellite (2025-05).** Selected **[[intelsat]]** to bring 3G/4G to
  hard-to-reach areas by satellite — "a first in Francophone West Africa" — reaching
  ~360,000 remote residents (per a Nov 2025 account), directly addressing Mali's rural
  connectivity gap (internet penetration ~35%). See [[data.satellite]], [[include.divides]],
  [[MLI]]. Source: [[2025-05-07-techafricanews-orange-mali-intelsat-4g-satellite]].
- **IFC €80m social loan (2025-11).** Anchor of the **[[ifc-orange-mali-loan]]** package —
  €80m (~$92.6m) from **[[ifc]]** and co-investors ([[world-bank|BOAD/IDA PSW]]) for 300 new
  4G towers (half rural), fibre to ~300,000 households, a women's digital-training target of
  70% by 2032, and diesel-to-solar conversion. First deal under the IFC–Orange MEA (OMEA)
  partnership. See [[infra.connect]], [[finance.new]], [[infra.energy]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-17-ifc-orange-mali-80m-loan-4g-fibre]].
- **Orange Money–Orabank Mastercard (2025-06).** With Orabank Mali, launched a co-branded
  prepaid Mastercard (virtual/physical, linked to the Orange Money wallet via the Max it app)
  giving the unbanked national and international card rails; caps 2m FCFA/transaction, 10m
  FCFA/month. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-11-maliweb-orange-money-orabank-mastercard-prepaid]].
