# Ministry of Health (Tunisia)

**The ministry driving Tunisia's digital health reform — and, on the evidence held here, the
clearest institutional statement of digital sovereignty in the Tunisian material: a stated plan to
adopt fully Tunisian cloud solutions to safeguard national health data.** Its digital work runs
through a partnership with the national operator [[tunisie-telecom]] and the
[[digital-hospital-project-tunisia]].

**Minister on the record here:** **Dr Mustafa Al-Furjani** (as of 2026-02).

**Read this page as intention, not achievement.** Everything below comes from a single report of a
single meeting. It carries **no amounts, no dates, no named agreement and no "as of" figures** —
these are stated plans and priorities, and are written as such.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digitalisation meeting with Tunisie Telecom (reported 2026-02-13).** Health Minister **Dr
  Mustafa Al-Furjani** met **[[tunisie-telecom]]**'s president and general manager to accelerate the
  digitalisation of healthcare services, focused on using technology to improve quality of care and
  bring health services closer to citizens. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Tunisia Advances Digital Health Reform With AI and National Cloud Strategy]].
- **Stated priorities for the next phase.** Expansion of **high-speed internet connectivity across
  health institutions, particularly primary care facilities**, to strengthen frontline delivery,
  **enhance data exchange** and expand **telemedicine**; an **advanced digital call centre** to
  improve communication between citizens and health authorities; and **AI** to improve data
  analysis, support decision-making and optimise service delivery. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[tech.ai]], [[digital.rural]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Tunisia Advances Digital Health Reform With AI and National Cloud Strategy]].
- **Digital sovereignty, explicitly (stated 2026-02).** The Ministry **confirmed plans to adopt
  fully Tunisian cloud solutions to safeguard national health data**, saying the move aims to
  reinforce security, protect patient privacy and ensure **local control over sensitive medical
  information** as part of a broader strategy to build resilient digital infrastructure. A plan, not
  a procurement: no provider, cost or timeline is stated. See [[infra.store]], [[gov.protect]],
  [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Tunisia Advances Digital Health Reform With AI and National Cloud Strategy]].
- **Digital Hospital Project.** Al-Furjani praised Tunisie Telecom's technical and logistical
  support, describing the partnership as key to advancing the government's
  **[[digital-hospital-project-tunisia]]** and saying the collaboration will help translate digital
  transformation into faster, more efficient and citizen-focused healthcare services. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Tunisia Advances Digital Health Reform With AI and National Cloud Strategy]].

- **National Health Identifier (NHI) pilot (Jul 2026).** A **unique lifetime code** per citizen threading
  a patient's full medical history across providers; piloted from early July at **Habib Thameur University
  Hospital (Tunis)**, using the **national ID card** at registration; **9,000+ patients** enrolled in the
  first weeks (Regional Directorate of Health, Tunis), with nationwide extension targeted by end-2026. The
  concrete first instance of the digital-health agenda above. See [[dpi.id]], [[tunisia--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-15-tmo-national-health-identifier-pilot]].
- **Digital birth-declaration pilot (Jun 2026).** First trial of an **electronic birth-declaration**
  system — a CRVS digitisation step tying into the health-identifier programme. *(Body not captured —
  headline/date only.)* See [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-11-alchourouk-digital-birth-declaration-pilot]].
