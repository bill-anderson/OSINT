# Ghana National Data Strategy

A cross-government data-governance strategy under development by the Government of Ghana with [[smart-africa]] and Team Europe (EU, BMZ, GIZ, the D4D Hub, Global Gateway), aligned to Ghana's Long-term National Development Plan (2018-2057), the National Medium-term Development Policy Framework (2022-2025) and the **AU Continental Data Policy Framework**. It covers data-governance structure, legal/ethical considerations, data infrastructure and capacity building, with the [[data-protection-commission-ghana]] as a lead stakeholder ([[gov.policy]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.exchange]]). As of the July 2024 National Validation Workshop the draft strategy was validated pending an implementation roadmap. See [[GHA]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **National Validation Workshop (2024-07-02).** A two-day Accra workshop validated the draft strategy and gathered inputs to its implementation roadmap. Smart Africa CEO Lacina Koné framed data as a cross-sector asset; DPC ED Patricia Adusei-Poku positioned Ghana's regime as a regional trailblazer; Benin ran a parallel peer-learning exchange. See [[gov.regional]], [[smart-africa]]. Source: [[2024-07-02 Ghana develops its National Data Strategy with Smart Africa and Team Europe]].
