# Japan (Government of Japan)

Bilateral donor in African education and ICT capacity building, working here **through UNESCO
rather than directly** — the funder behind projects implemented by
**[[unesco-iicba]]** and national ministries.

> **Taxonomy note:** the subject taxonomy carries no `geopol.*` slug for Japan (it has `usa`,
> `china`, `eu`, `india`, `gulf`). Japanese activity is therefore tagged by the topics it funds,
> not by a geopolitics slug. Flagged in `reviews/issues.md` rather than decided here.

## Activity in this wiki

- **ICT equipment to 15 Nigerian Colleges of Education (handed over 2026-06-02).** Japan funded
  the donation of **laptops, tablets, desktop computers, smart boards, projectors, printers and
  backup hard drives** to **15 Colleges of Education** across Nigeria, handed over at the
  **[[federal-ministry-education-nigeria|Federal Ministry of Education]]** in Abuja by the
  Chargé d'Affaires of the Embassy of Japan in Nigeria, **Hitoshi Kozaki**. It falls under the
  Japan-funded project *Capacity Building of Teachers to Promote Continuous and Inclusive Access
  to Safe and Quality Education for Girls in West Africa*, implemented by the ministry with
  **[[unesco-iicba]]**. Stated aims: strengthen teacher education, improve digital literacy and
  support gender-responsive teaching. **No monetary value, per-institution allocation, list of
  recipient colleges or project timeframe is disclosed** in the held source. See [[NGA]],
  [[capacity.training]], [[capacity.literacy]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-japan-ict-equipment-nigerian-colleges]].

## Why it matters here

Japan enters this wiki as a **teacher-education and equipment donor routed through a UN
implementing agency** — a materially different channel from the vendor-led (Huawei), hyperscaler
(Google/Microsoft) and state-to-state (China, UAE, US) skilling routes tracked on
[[capacity.training]]. The device-donation model also sits at the weakest evidentiary end of that
page: hardware handed over is countable, but the wiki holds nothing on maintenance, connectivity,
power or use, so a donation is recorded as an input and never as an outcome.
