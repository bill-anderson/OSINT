# Catherine Nakalembe

Ugandan geographer, Africa program director at [[nasa-harvest]] and assistant
professor at the University of Maryland. She leads locally-grounded satellite
crop-mapping using machine learning, computer vision and deep learning across
Kenya, Uganda and Senegal.

## Activity in this wiki

- **"Fail spectacularly" argument (2026-03-12).** Nakalembe told *Rest of World*
  that most AI models are trained on European and US data and are "largely
  useless" on African farms unless adapted to local contexts — warning that
  unadapted systems deepen inequality and can prioritise corporate profit over
  farmers. When Western-trained models could not recognise local crops in western
  [[KEN]], her team collected **5m+ ground images in two weeks** (*Nature
  Scientific Data*, 2025) to map cropland in Kenya, Uganda ([[UGA]]) and Senegal.
  See [[tech.ai]], [[data.satellite]], [[capacity.research]], [[nasa-harvest]].
  Source: [[2026-03-12 Western AI models 'fail spectacularly' in farms and forests abroad]].
