# India–Angola digital-cooperation pledge and $200m defence credit line (2025-05-03)

> ⚠ **This page's slug is a misnomer** — `angola-india-dpi-credit-line` encodes an error corrected on
> 2026-07-20 (below). It is retained because the slug is cited from an immutable `raw/` source page;
> read the title, not the filename.

Two **separate** announcements made at the **Lourenço–Modi** state visit to New Delhi on
**2025-05-03** — the first Angolan presidential visit to India in 38 years. The wiki previously
merged them into a single "$200m DPI credit line". They are not one instrument.

## What was actually announced (resolved 2026-07-20)

From Modi's own remarks at the joint press statement — the announcer of record:

- **A $200 million credit line, for defence.** "I am pleased to announce the approval of a **$200
  million defense credit line for the modernization of Angola's armed forces**." Repair and overhaul
  of defence platforms and training of the Angolan armed forces were discussed alongside it.
- **A capability-sharing pledge on DPI, with no money attached.** In a distinct sentence: "Taking our
  development partnership forward, we will **share our capabilities** with Angola in **Digital Public
  Infrastructure, Space Technology, and Capacity Building**." No figure, no vendor, no timetable.

Source: [[2025-05-03-pmindia-joint-press-statement-angola]].

**Nothing is contracted.** India's MEA Secretary (ER) **Dammu Ravi**, briefing the same day,
described DPI as an area where "**for Angola admittedly it is a new area**… we will see how our
exports can take this forward", with African states generally "still **new to this**" and requiring
"**awareness building**". Angop's account (**2025-11-09**) of the instruments actually signed in May
2025 lists MoUs on traditional medicine, agriculture and culture, plus accession to the
International Solar Alliance and the **defence** credit line — **no DPI instrument**.

## The corrections this page carries

1. **The $200m is defence money, not DPI money.** The conflation traces to a Biometric Update
   headline of **2025-05-07** ("India helps Angola with DPI, digital ID as part of $200M credit
   line"); neither Modi nor the MEA links them.
2. **There is no Aadhaar-modelled Angolan digital ID, and no MOSIP engagement in Angola.** The
   MOSIP deployments that article cites are **Morocco, Ethiopia, Sierra Leone and Guinea** — offered
   as context, not as Angolan facts.
3. **This is not "the India-facing leg of a three-way contest".** Angola's identity stack is
   Chinese-built and operational — CEIEC, contracted **2017-01-03**, production centre inaugurated
   **2019-11-08**. This pledge sits eight years later and at a far lower order of commitment. See
   [[angola--dpi-id]].

*As of 2026-07-20, no instrument advancing the DPI strand past May 2025 has been found.*
