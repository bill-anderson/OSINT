# Samuel Nartey George

Ghana's Minister for Communication, Digital Technology and Innovations; leads the
government's digital-transformation agenda.

## Activity in this wiki

- **UNDP partnership (2026-07).** Reaffirmed commitment to an inclusive digital ecosystem;
  said Ghana is using the **UNESCO Readiness Assessment Methodology (RAM)** to sequence
  initiatives, and previewed a **national fintech fund** aligned with a forthcoming
  **Innovation and Startup** legislative framework. See [[GHA]], [[undp]], [[gov.policy]],
  [[gov.legislate]].

- **$250m National AI Hub + AI Strategy approved (2026-04).** Announced Cabinet approval of a
  **$250m national AI computing centre** and the **[[ghana-national-ai-strategy]]** (2023–2033,
  eight pillars, Responsible AI Office), with public launch set for 24 Apr 2026 — positioning
  Ghana as West Africa's AI hub. Strategy developed with [[smart-africa]], GIZ FAIR Forward and
  The Future Society; national readiness assessed via UNESCO's RAM. See [[GHA]], [[tech.ai]],
  [[finance.new]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-03 Ghana approves $250M AI Hub & National AI Strategy]].

- **Huawei AI-training deal for 3,000 girls (2026-03).** Secured a commitment from [[huawei]]
  (SVP Steven Yi, at MWC 2026 Barcelona) for free AI training for 3,000 girls in Ghana's Girls
  in ICT Programme, feeding into the One Million Coders Programme; the talks also covered the
  $250m AI Compute Centre, 5G, rural telephony and a possible smartphone-assembly plant. See
  [[capacity.training]], [[geopol.china]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-05 Ghana strikes AI training deal with Chinese multinational amid $250m tech investment push]].

- **.GH domain state control (2026-01).** Announced plans to bring Ghana's **.GH ccTLD fully under
  state ownership** — managed privately since 1996 by internet pioneer Prof. Nii Narku Quaynor via
  the **[[ghana-domain-name-registry]]** (GDNR) — and to push businesses onto **.com.gh** (auto-assigned
  to newly registered companies) as a "national digital identity," framing the root-server transfer as
  digital sovereignty. See [[GHA]], [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.id]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-22 Ghana to take state control of .GH domain to strengthen digital identity]].

- **Voice-AI digital inclusion (2026-01).** Quoted in a pan-African analysis on using voice/NLP
  AI to bring feature-phone users into digital services across health, agriculture, education
  and finance. See [[include.access]], [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.literacy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].
