# DPI and Development: A World Bank Group Approach

The World Bank Group's **Digital Transformation White Paper, Volume 1** (March 2025), authored by
Julia Clark, Georgina Marin, Oya Pinar Ardic Alper and Guillermo Alfonso Galicia Rabadan. It sets
out a "one-WBG" framework for understanding and implementing **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI)**
and is a standing normative reference in this wiki — a dated fact drawn from it becomes a source
citing this instrument. Reference document: `2026-07-11 P505739-84c5073b-9d40-4b83-a211-98b2263e87dd.pdf`.

## Summary

- Defines DPI as **"foundational, digital building blocks designed for the public benefit"** —
  essential digital functions provided at society scale and reused across sectors so public and
  private providers can build on them.
- Centres **three core DPIs**: **digital identity + e-signatures**, **digital payments**, and
  **data sharing/exchange** ([[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]]).
- Distinguishes centralized, **federated** and **decentralized** identity models; open standards in
  fast payment systems; and data-sharing models — with cautions on safety and inclusion.
- **Five key lessons:** safety and inclusion first; focus on outcomes not technology; users at the
  centre; invest in people; "it takes a village" to build good DPI.
- Illustrative cases include **Zambia** (DPI for social protection), **Kenya** (Big Data Platform
  for agriculture), Brazil (PIX), Indonesia (SatuSehat) and India (AgriStack); delivered via the
  WBG's ID4D, G2Px and FASTT initiatives.
