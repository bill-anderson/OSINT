# São Tomé and Príncipe national digital ID (MOSIP-based)

STP's initiative to build an **inclusive national digital ID** on the open-source
**[[mosip]]** platform, anchored on the country's strong civil-registration base. Led
out of the **Justice Ministry** (spokesperson Elissandro Kelman Torres, Systems
Analyst), the programme deliberately **started with civil registration** before
identity issuance.

## Status (as of 2025-06)

- **Pilot phase**, confirmed publicly at **ID4Africa 2025** ([[id4africa]], June 2025,
  Ethiopia). Pilot **began in 2024**.
- **Nationwide rollout** hoped **within one to two years** of mid-2025; **no fixed
  timeline** stated.
- **Registration foundation:** ~**98% of the population held a birth certificate as of
  2023** (World Bank), attributed to digitisation, legal reform and service
  integration.
- **MOSIP's small-states focus:** MOSIP President S. Rajagopalan cited STP (alongside
  Comoros, Belize) as a target for **cost-reduced ID solutions for sub-350,000-population
  nations**.
- **Digital-government aim:** officials also want to connect institutions into a
  national digital-government system — a system "that can capture all citizens' data"
  exists, but cross-institution interoperability is the stated gap (see
  [[sao-tome-interoperability-framework-2024]]).
- **Financing context:** in 2023 STP was among ten African countries earmarked for an
  [[african-development-bank]]-funded digital-trade and digital-payments project.

This pilot predates and underpins the World Bank-funded **Citizen Portal** (beta,
Mar-2026) recorded on the [[STP]] hub — the same MOSIP/CRVS foundation feeding a
verifiable-credentials pathway.
