# Nékkal programme (Senegal civil-registration modernisation)

Senegal's **civil-registration (CRVS) modernisation programme, launched 2021**, aiming to interconnect
civil-status centres and systematically digitise registers to give every Senegalese an identity document and
improve data reliability. A **42-month programme funded by the [[european-union|EU]] at CFA18bn (€27.4m)**.
The operational successor thread is run by **[[anec-senegal|ANEC]]**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **19m+ records digitised (2024).** By **25 June 2024**, Senegal had digitised and indexed **over 19 million
  civil-status records** (announced by Minister Moussa Bala Fofana). A second phase under consideration would
  let citizens (home or diaspora) request civil-status documents without travelling. Context: UNICEF (2019)
  found ~1 in 5 children unregistered at civil status (3 in 5 in rural areas). See [[dpi.registry]],
  [[SEN]]. Source: [[2024-06-27 Senegal digitizes millions of civil records under the Nekkal program]].
- Foundational CRVS gaps documented for the UN CRC (2023): 22.3% of under-fives unregistered (2019). Source:
  [[2023-05-01 Children's Right to Identity in Senegal (CHIP CRC submission)]].
