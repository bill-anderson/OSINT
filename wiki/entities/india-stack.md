# India Stack

India's layered **digital public infrastructure** reference architecture — foundational biometric ID
(**Aadhaar**, >1.3bn enrolments, the world's largest digital ID), instant payments (**UPI**, live in
~8 countries incl. UAE, Singapore, Mauritius, France), consent-based data exchange, and online public
services. Promoted by New Delhi as an **exportable, open-source "global public good"** and a route to
**digital sovereignty** — letting smaller economies skip the trial-and-error of building DPI from scratch
and reduce reliance on proprietary foreign vendors. Aligned with the World Bank **ID4D**/**G2Px**
approaches. As of 2026, India had DPI cooperation agreements with **≥23 countries**. See [[dpi.id]],
[[dpi.pay]], [[geopol.india]].

## African uptake

- **Partners** include Kenya, Tanzania, Ethiopia, Sierra Leone, The Gambia and Lesotho; **[[BDI]]
  Burundi** opened exploratory talks in June 2026 to adapt Aadhaar-style ID and UPI-style payment rails
  (→ [[burundi--dpi-id]]). Others weighing the model include Morocco, the Philippines and Sri Lanka. The
  export drive traces to India's **G20 presidency (2023)** South-South DPI advocacy. Sources:
  [[2026-06-30-burundi-india-stack-digital-id-payments-biometricupdate]],
  [[2026-06-23-burundi-studies-india-stack-digital-id-payments]].
