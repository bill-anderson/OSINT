# Rose Mutiso

Kenyan researcher and science-policy voice on energy, technology and AI in Africa. She is the **founder of the African Tech Futures Lab**, an initiative to cultivate grounded, African-led thinking and a cohort of African writers on the economics of AI and technology, and was previously **Research Director at the Energy Growth Hub**. She writes the Substack *Kibao* and has published on technology leapfrogging and AI in *Science* and via Energy for Growth.

## What she argues in this wiki

In a VoxDevTalk interview she sets out the physical preconditions for AI in Africa, boiling them down to three fundamentals — **power, connectivity and data** — with data centres as "the physical backbone of the AI economy." Her central claim is that **AI is not a classic leapfrog technology**: unlike mobile, it depends on heavy energy, compute and data infrastructure, so relying on offshore infrastructure (European data centres, undersea cables, global cloud) is not a sustainable long-term foundation. She notes Africa has roughly 166 times Switzerland's population but only about twice its data centres, and among the world's highest electricity prices.

Her three-step agenda: build affordable, reliable power at scale (with data centres as anchor utility customers); position governments as **enablers not planners** (clear regulation, less friction, regional data-standard harmonisation, local-hosting demand); and lower entry barriers for local researchers (cheaper cloud/compute, subsidised access, small grants). She argues for **sequencing** adoption where feasible now rather than waiting for universal electrification.
