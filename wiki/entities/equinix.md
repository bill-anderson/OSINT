# Equinix

US-headquartered global data-centre operator; expanded into Africa partly via its
**$320m acquisition of MainOne** (2022), a case study in African-founded
infrastructure passing into US ownership.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Cape Town environmental challenge (2026-06-04, Reuters).** A formal objection
  (Housing Assembly + UK non-profit **Foxglove**, backed by the Legal Resources
  Centre) argues Equinix's two planned Cape Town data centres — combined draw up
  to **160 MW** — should not be approved without full water/power/environmental
  disclosure, invoking Cape Town's "Day Zero" drought. Equinix says it bought the
  land (King Air Industria) but has not yet filed planning applications. See
  [[ZAF]], [[infra.energy]], [[infra.capacity]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-04 Equinix faces challenge to Cape Town data centres over environmental concerns]].
- **Sovereignty framing.** [[2026-06-08 The Promise and Limits of African Data Sovereignty]]
  lists Equinix (via MainOne) and Digital Realty (via Teraco) as African-founded
  operators now US-owned — putting African data under CLOUD Act reach.
- **SA build-out context.** Cited among the hyperscale-adjacent operators driving
  SA's data-centre policy shift. Source:
  [[2026-02-25 South Africa puts data centres on par with energy, ports in big policy shift]].
- **SA channel — [[axiz]] (2026-04-28).** Axiz, Equinix's South Africa distribution
  partner, welcomed a fresh Equinix SA investment as reinforcing the country's data-centre
  hub role and channel access to global interconnection — demand driven by AI workloads,
  hybrid cloud and data-sovereignty rules (no figure disclosed for the outlay). See
  [[ZAF]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-28 Axiz Welcomes Equinix Investment, Citing Boost to Africa's Digital Infrastructure]].
- **Nigeria LG3 (opening Q1 2026).** Equinix (in Nigeria since its 2022 MainOne
  acquisition; runs LG1/LG2) announced a new **$22m LG3** data centre in Lagos — the first
  phase of a **$100m** pan-African expansion plan — adding Equinix Fabric interconnection
  to the metro. See [[NGA]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-26 Lagos Is Getting a $250 Million Hyperscale Data Center. Here's Why Nigeria Still Can't Power the Cloud]].
- **SA expansion — $438m / R7.5bn (2026-04).** Equinix announced a **$438m (R7.5bn)**
  self-funded South Africa expansion (Johannesburg + Cape Town), having bought **R890m** of
  land (~327,000 m² secured) to add **~160 MW** of capacity — on top of its first SA data
  centre (Johannesburg, 2024). SA hosts ~three-quarters of Africa's ~409 MW operational
  capacity; the SA market is projected to grow from ~$2.6bn (2025) to >$5bn by 2031. See
  [[equinix-sa-438m-expansion]], [[ZAF]], [[finance.new]], [[infra.energy]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-01 US data centre firm Equinix is investing $438 million in South Africa]],
  [[2026-04-01 US tech giant from Silicon Valley buys R890 million worth of land in South Africa]].

- **MainOne acquisition in the literature (Nwankwo 2025).** A peer-reviewed study of
  submarine-cable ownership frames Equinix's **$320m** acquisition of **MainOne** (Funke
  Opeke's 7,000km Portugal–Nigeria cable), the largest Nigerian tech deal, as part of how
  content providers and colocation players become "middlemen" in African connectivity —
  against [[google]]'s partner-landed **Equiano** (144 Tbps). See [[NGA]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[colonialism]]; publication [[equiano-mainone-nwankwo-2025]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-20 Redefining competition and submarine cable ownership Equiano, MainOne, and the making of middlemen]].

- **TeKnowledge partnership (2026-07).** Equinix partnered technology-services firm
  [[teknowledge]] ([[teknowledge-equinix-partnership]]) to combine its interconnected data-centre
  platform (>280 DCs) with TeKnowledge's AI/cybersecurity services for **AI-ready, locally-hosted
  infrastructure** across [[NGA]] and West Africa (MD West Africa Wole Abu). See [[infra.store]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[XWA]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 TeKnowledge, Equinix partner to accelerate digital transformation]].
