# Meta Platforms

US social-media company (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp); in Nigeria, both the
target of record data-protection enforcement and — post-settlement — a funder of
the regulator's programme.

## Activity in this wiki

- **M-SIDP with NDPC (2026-06).** Following a **2025 court-approved settlement**
  over its handling of Nigerian users' data, Meta launched the two-year
  **Meta-Supported Initiatives for Data Protection (M-SIDP)** with the
  **[[ndpc]]** — funding governance, DPO capacity-building and public-awareness
  work under the Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023. Deal:
  [[ndpc-meta-settlement]]. See [[NGA]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-09 Meta backs NDPC in landmark privacy initiative]].
- **FCCPC penalty + new probe (2026-07-10).** Nigeria's **[[fccpc]]** fined
  Meta/WhatsApp **$222m** (July 2024, upheld April 2025, now under appeal; earlier
  reported ~$220m, unpaid); Nigeria's FG has now directed the FCCPC to open a
  fresh **anti-competitive-practices probe** of Meta (with Alphabet and X) after a
  Nigerian Press Organisation petition. Cited as evidence African regulators can
  assert jurisdiction but struggle to enforce against US-parent firms. See
  [[NGA]], [[gov.protect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-06-08 The Promise and Limits of African Data Sovereignty]],
  [[2026-07-10 Nigeria tests data sovereignty as foreign tech firms facing probe]]. A second
  outlet adds the copyright dimension: the probe targets **uncompensated AI-training on
  Nigerian news content** (abuse of dominance + copyright, FCCP Act 2018), directed via
  Information Minister Mohammed Idris after a Nigerian Press Organisation petition. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Why Nigeria Is Investigating Meta, Google, X and AI Firms]].
- **GovGuide Nigeria (2026-05-22).** Meta built **[[govguide-nigeria]]** — an
  AI chatbot for public-service access on its open-source **Llama** models
  (English/Hausa/Igbo/Yoruba; NLLB-200 now 50+ African languages) — with FMCIDE,
  NCAIR and Publica AI, launched by minister **[[bosun-tijani]]**. Context: a prior
  **$32.8m** NDPC fine (reportedly waived), distinct from the $222m FCCPC penalty.
  See [[NGA]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-22 Nigeria launches GovGuide, an AI chatbot for public service access]].
- **Sama contract ended → 1,108 Kenya layoffs (2026-04-17).** Meta terminated its
  content-moderation / AI-data-annotation contract with **[[sama]]**, triggering
  1,108 redundancies in Kenya — underscoring the AI-labour supply chain's
  dependence on a few US clients; ties to the Daniel Motaung case, where Kenyan
  courts held Meta can be sued locally. See [[KEN]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-17 Meta contract end sparks 1 100 Sama job cuts]].
- Cited (via its Head of Legal Africa) in the Nigeria data-silos debate. Source:
  [[2026-06-17 Government data silos threaten Nigeria's AI ambitions]].
- **WhatsApp as tax infrastructure — Kenya (2026-04-02).** Kenya's
  **[[kenya-revenue-authority]]** launched a **WhatsApp**-based tax-filing chatbot
  (cutting the process from 8 steps to 3, auto-pulling PAYE data) to reach the
  ~15m of 22m registered taxpayers who don't file — turning Meta's messaging
  platform into public-service/tax rails. See [[KEN]], [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-02 WhatsApp tax filing set to pull millions into Kenya's tax net]].
- **Subsea + overland cables (2026-03/07).** Meta's **2Africa** (world's longest at
  ~45,000km) and **Project Waterworth** subsea cables anchor the thesis that
  hyperscalers now own most new high-capacity subsea into Africa; Meta is also
  moving overland, joining [[google]], [[amazon-web-services]] and [[microsoft]] to
  build ultra-high-speed **inland fibre** across the continent's interior. See
  [[infra.connect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-06-03 Submarine cables serving Africa]],
  [[2026-05-23 Orange-backed subsea cable to connect Nigeria, 19 other countries]],
  [[2026-03-16 Why Google, Amazon and Meta are going overland in Africa]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: an African regulator compelling a global platform to fund
  compliance — jurisdiction asserted over the data of nationals, though the
  enforcement/parent-liability gap remains (see [[microsoft]], CLOUD Act).
  [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]]
  names Meta among the four US firms African draft AI strategies flag as a
  dependence risk, and notes several open-source African AI initiatives run on
  Meta grants and Google Cloud — inclusion that deepens dependency.

- **Analyst framing (2026 batch, light).** Chinasa Okolo (2026-02-17) names Meta
  among the Big Tech firms that **supported drafting Nigeria's National AI
  Strategy** — cited as a corporate-capture risk (author's argument). Russell
  Southwood (2025-12-04) frames Meta (and Google) as the hyperscalers African MNOs
  struggle to compete with in "digital life" verticals, part of why operators
  retreat to neutral infrastructure/platform roles. See [[tech.industry]],
  [[tech.ai]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-17 Corporate Interests Must Not Co-Opt Africa's AI Moment]],
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].
