# Google (Alphabet)

US hyperscaler; the most active of the three big cloud providers in Africa's
2026 build-out, positioning itself as the infrastructure and AI backbone of the
continent's digital economy.

## Activity in this wiki

- **First Africa Cloud Summit + $1bn exceeded (2026-07-01, Sandton).** Google
  said it has **exceeded its five-year $1bn Africa investment target**, opening
  the summit (2,500+ attendees) with SA President **[[cyril-ramaphosa]]** and
  Google SVP James Manyika. Five new initiatives: a **Digital Exchange Port
  connectivity hub in the Eastern Cape** (first of **4 planned continental
  hubs**; links to Australia via the **Umoja** cable and a new India route);
  **Africa's first applied AI lab in Ghana** ([[GHA]], Accra, applications close
  31 Aug 2026); a **>$1M** creative-AI program with Idris Elba's Akuna Group; an
  **R3M** innovation centre in Soweto (with WeThinkCode); and a **Google for
  Startups Accelerator** cohort (15 SA firms; part of a pledge to back 50 African
  ventures 2024–2028). Sources:
  [[2026-07-01 Google Cloud Summit in Africa Highlights the Continent's Digital Transformation and Unveils New Agentic AI and Infrastructure Investments]],
  [[2026-07-01 Google has exceeded $1 billion Africa investment target]].
- **Johannesburg Cloud Region economics (2026-07-03).** Google projects its 2025
  Johannesburg region could add **R1.7tn / $90.6bn** in output and **~315,000
  jobs by 2030** as enterprises move from AI pilots to production; unveiled a
  SA **Digital Exchange Port**. Adopters cited: [[vodacom]], Discovery, Pepkor,
  Naspers — all stressing **data residency**. Source:
  [[2026-07-03 Google Cloud Region forecasts R1.7tn boost for SA]].
- **Accelerator Africa Class 10 (2026-04-21).** Named the **15 startups** (from
  ~2,600 applications, 8 countries; AI-focused fintech/agritech/healthtech) for
  the **13 Apr–19 Jun 2026** cohort; the [[google-for-startups-accelerator-africa]]
  program has backed 106 startups across 17 countries since 2018, alumni raising
  **>$263m** and creating **>2,800 jobs**. See [[tech.innovate]], [[finance.new]].
  Source: [[2026-04-21 Meet the 15 Startups Joining the Google for Startups Accelerator Africa Class 10]].
- **Nigeria FCCPC probe (2026-07-10).** Alphabet (Google) named — with [[meta]]
  and X — in the Federal Government's directive to the [[fccpc]] to probe
  anti-competitive practices (after a Nigerian Press Organisation petition);
  Google Cloud is also among the foreign hyperscalers under the [[cbn]]
  onshoring mandate. See [[NGA]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Nigeria tests data sovereignty as foreign tech firms facing probe]]. A fuller
  account frames the probe around **uncompensated AI-training on Nigerian news content**
  (copyright + abuse of dominance); a separate July-2026 analysis names **Google Cloud**
  among the hyperscalers the [[cbn]] localisation directive would force to onshore payment
  data by 1 Jan 2027. Sources:
  [[2026-07-10 Why Nigeria Is Investigating Meta, Google, X and AI Firms]],
  [[2026-07-10 CBN's Data Localisation Directive Puts Nigerian Fintechs In A Cloud Dilemma]].
- **Startup cohort / the capacity gap (2026-06-19).** MD Africa Alex Okosi:
  15 startups from 8 countries graduated (60% profitable); AfDB projects AI could
  add **up to $1.5tn to Africa by 2035**, but Africa holds only **~1% of global
  data-centre capacity**, forcing data offshore. Program: 190+ startups, 17
  markets, >$300m raised. Source:
  [[2026-06-19 What Google's latest startup cohort says about AI in Africa]].
- **AI-skilling push — DeepMind, Hustle Academy, DHET (2026-03/04).** Google
  DeepMind extended its free **AI Research Foundations** curriculum
  ([[google-ai-research-foundations]], [[google-deepmind]]) into African
  classrooms (co-developed with UCL, localised with AIMS; **Google.org gave $4m**
  to Nigeria's FATE Foundation for lecturer training); relaunched
  **[[hustle-academy]]** with UpSkill Universe, widening it from entrepreneurs to
  employees/students/jobseekers across SSA (**>18,000 SMEs** trained since 2022);
  and signed a **5,000 Google-certification scholarship** deal with SA's
  **[[dhet-south-africa]]** (AI/digital skills across universities/TVET/CET,
  train-the-trainer). See [[capacity.training]], [[tech.ai]], [[NGA]], [[ZAF]].
  Sources: [[2026-03-17 Broadening advanced AI education across Africa]],
  [[2026-04-10 Google Expands Hustle Academy to Train Africans in Practical AI Skills -]],
  [[2026-03-30 South Africa Teams Up With Google to Train 5,000 in Digital Skills]].
- **Localisation profile — Taslim Okunola (2026-03).** Profile of Google Global
  Strategy & Operations Manager **[[taslim-okunola]]** (from Ibadan), who helped
  bring Nigerian English plus Yoruba/Hausa/Igbo into Google Assistant and Maps;
  argues African products should be "built from scratch," not scaled down. See
  [[NGA]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-28 Meet Taslim Okunola, the Nigerian behind Google's push for localised technology in Africa]].
- **Liquid C2 Partner Experience Centre (2026-04-08).** [[liquid-c2]] (a
  [[cassava-technologies]] business) opened Africa's first **Google
  Cloud-powered Partner Experience Centre** in Johannesburg — Gemini
  Enterprise/Playspace, Google Cloud accreditation, local-currency reseller
  billing. See [[ZAF]], [[tech.ai]], [[infra.capacity]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-08 Liquid C2's new centre puts Google Cloud within Africa's reach]],
  [[2026-04-08 Liquid C2, Google open first-for-Africa Partner Experience Centre]].
- **Overland fibre pivot (2026-03).** Google — with [[meta]],
  [[amazon-web-services]] and [[microsoft]] — is moving beyond subsea
  (Equiano/2Africa) to build ultra-high-speed **overland fibre** across Africa's
  interior with local players (Yangtze Optics fibre factory, Durban). See
  [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 Why Google, Amazon and Meta are going overland in Africa]].

- **Cassava / Gemini distribution (2025-11-12).** Partnered with [[cassava-technologies]]
  to push **Gemini** across Africa — a six-month Google AI Plus trial plus **data-free
  (zero-rated)** access to the Gemini app over Cassava's network. Liquid C2
  ([[liquid-c2]]) is Google Cloud's first African **Interconnect** provider and a Google
  Cloud + [[anthropic]] MSSP (since 2024); it opened Africa's first Google Cloud
  Experience Centre in Johannesburg (2026-04). Source:
  [[2025-11-12 Cassava Tech - Partners with Google to bring Google Gemini to millions in africa]].
- **Google Distributed Cloud (GDC).** Google's air-gapped/on-prem "sovereign" cloud+Gemini
  product (DoD IL6; 25 countries) is the technology plausibly under hyperscaler
  data-residency narratives. Source: [[2026-01-01 Google Distributed Cloud  Google Cloud]].
- **Subsea backbone — Equiano + Umoja + four hubs (2022–2027).** Google's two wholly-owned
  African cables anchor its "Africa Connect" program: **[[equiano-cable]]** (Portugal→South
  Africa, 144 Tbps, first landing Togo Mar 2022) and the new **[[umoja-cable]]** — the first
  direct **Africa–Australia** route, whose terrestrial leg (Kenya→Uganda→Rwanda→DRC→Zambia→
  Zimbabwe→South Africa) was built with [[liquid-c2]]/Liquid Intelligent, RFS 2027. In Sept
  2025 Google announced **four subsea-cable connectivity hubs** (N/S/E/W Africa, each with
  landing stations + data centres, ~3-yr build). See [[infra.connect]]. Sources:
  [[2024-05-28 Google invests in terrestrial-subsea cable linking Kenya to Australia]],
  [[2025-09-22 Google plans to build four subsea cable connectivity hubs for Africa]].
- **Direct stake in Cassava — $90M round (2024-12).** Google took a **direct equity stake**
  in [[cassava-technologies]] alongside [[us-dfc]] and Finnfund (the $90m equity tranche of a
  $310m raise) — deal [[google-cassava-90m-round]] — cementing the connectivity/AI partnership
  (Umoja, Liquid C2) under its $1bn Africa pledge. See [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2024-12-17 Google joins $90M investment into Cassava to bolster Africa's digital infrastructure]].
- **WAXAL open speech dataset (2026-02).** Google Research Africa (Aisha Walcott-Bryant)
  released **[[waxal]]** on Hugging Face — 21 African languages, 11,000+ hrs / ~2m recordings
  (~1,250 hrs ASR + 20+ hrs TTS); African partners (Makerere, Univ. Ghana, Digital Umuganda,
  AIMS) retain corpus ownership. See [[data.open]], [[include.access]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-02 Introducing WAXAL A New Open Dataset for African Speech Technology]],
  [[2026-02-04 Google launches WAXAL, an open-source voice dataset for African languages]].
- **AUC MoU (2026-02-17, Addis Ababa).** Signed **[[au-google-mou]]** with the
  [[african-union]] Commission — AI/cloud infrastructure, skills (3m students/teachers by 2030),
  free Gemini Pro + Amharic support, and AI-readiness training for officials via Apolitical —
  framed on the AU Continental AI Strategy. See [[gov.regional]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-20 AUC Signs Partnership With Google]]. (Critically read below.)

  also holds an Equiano fibre pair (up to 12 Tbps, ~2022). Sources:

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Google frames local **data residency** and continental
  connectivity as enabling African digital ascendancy — but the backbone,
  models and clouds remain US-owned. The same dependency tension read critically
  in [[2026-06-08 The Promise and Limits of African Data Sovereignty]] and the EU parallel
  in [[2026-06-29 How AI Keeps Europe Hooked on US Cloud]]; [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]]
  names Google among the four US firms African draft AI strategies flag as a
  dependence risk (Google's Kabelo Makwane separately flagging appetite for an
  Africa-based cloud region, per [[2026-04-17 ITWeb TV Making AI inclusive for Africa]]).
  The AUC–Google MoU ([[au-google-mou]]) is read as the sharpest case of this: Alice
  Munyua's [[2026-03-05 THE GATEKEEPERS PART XII]] argues "sovereign" appears four times in
  the press release but in no binding clause, and that placing AU institutional data on
  US-owned infrastructure exposes it to the **US CLOUD Act** ([[cloud-act]]) regardless of
  data-residency wording — jurisdiction following ownership, not location.
- `colonialism`: [[2026-04-22 2026-04 - AI is not built for African users, exposing a governance chasm]]
  (Wits African Cyber Law Conference) cites Google's Sipho Mtombeni on African
  language-dataset efforts, while framing data-level inclusion as insufficient
  against "digital extractivism." Google's dominance in big-model compute and
  agri-AI recurs in the "frugal AI" and "digital colonialism" critiques —
  [[2026-04-02 Nations priced out of Big AI are building with frugal models]]
  (US/Chinese firms run >90% of AI data centres; Kenya/Nigeria in talks with
  Cambridge's Frugal AI Hub) and
  [[2026-03-12 Western AI models 'fail spectacularly' in farms and forests abroad]]
  (Western-trained models fail on local crops absent locally-collected data).
  The **subsea-cable** critique sharpens this at the physical layer: Google is the single
  most prominent owner of the global cable network (~16% of core long-haul systems, per the
  peer-reviewed [[cable-wars-abels-babic-2026]] / [[2026-01-20 Cable wars Mapping the political economy of submarine cables in an era of geoeconomic competition]]);
  Nwankwo's Equiano/MainOne study ([[equiano-mainone-nwankwo-2025]] /
  [[2025-10-20 Redefining competition and submarine cable ownership Equiano, MainOne, and the making of middlemen]])
  shows Equiano's 144 Tbps exceeding all other African cables combined and Google using
  landing partners (WIOCC/OADC) to avoid country-specific regulation while chasing the "Next
  Billion Users" and their data; and [[2026-03-02 I traced who owns the undersea cables that carry 95% of global internet traffic — the map is a colonial one]]
  maps cable landings onto colonial-era ports (Google/Meta/Microsoft/Amazon owning/leasing
  ~70% of undersea bandwidth).
