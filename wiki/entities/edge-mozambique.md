# EDGE — Digital Governance & Economy Project

The **World Bank** operation (P172350) financing Mozambique's foundational [[dpi.id|digital-ID]] and
[[dpi.govtech|digital-government]] agenda. Appraised June 2021 and approved for a **US$150m** IPF, its
development objective is **to increase access to legal identification, digital public services and digital
business opportunities**. Implementing agencies are the **National Institute of Electronic Government
(INAGE)** and the **[[intic-mozambique|National Institute of ICT (INTIC)]]**; components span analogue
reforms and government connectivity, identification systems and digital services, and the digital-business
ecosystem.

Working with the World Bank's **ID4D** initiative, EDGE supported the **[[dnic-mozambique|National
Directorate of Civil Identification (DNIC)]]** to launch Mozambique's **first-ever nationwide free national-ID
campaign** (from 13 November 2023), which had delivered **140,000+ free IDs** within months against a
backdrop where more than half of adults lack proof of identity. A related ID4D project issued IDs and birth
certificates to forcibly displaced persons in Cabo Delgado.

  (23 Mar → 31 Dec 2026) targets **~7m people** at **25,000/day** via up to **500 mobile brigades**
  (President Chapo urging enrolment); EDGE procurement sought services to strengthen civil registration and
