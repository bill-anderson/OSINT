# EDGE — Digital Governance & Economy Project

The **World Bank** operation (P172350) financing Mozambique's foundational [[dpi.id|digital-ID]] and
[[dpi.govtech|digital-government]] agenda. Appraised June 2021 and approved as an IDA grant of SDR 105m,
**US$150m equivalent (2021)**; the current net IDA commitment is **US$137.85m (February 2025)**, after a
**US$4.2m** cancellation of unused contingent-emergency (CERC) funds at the government's request and the
drift of the SDR-denominated grant against the dollar. Its development objective is **to increase access to
legal identification, digital public services and digital business opportunities**.

The implementing agency is the **Ministry of Science, Technology and Higher Education (MCTES)**, working
through the project implementation unit already running MozSkills — not INAGE or INTIC, which the appraisal
document funds and advises rather than putting in charge. **As of February 2025 MCTES has itself been
disbanded**, its role passing to a new **Ministry of Digital and Communications**, with INAGE and INTIC also
facing restructuring — so the counterpart institution is in flux and the arrangement above is dated, not
settled. Of the original components, **Component 3 (Govtech Business Ecosystem, US$30m) was dropped at an
earlier restructuring** and the development objective revised; identification systems and digital services
remain the largest at **US$70m**.

Working with the World Bank's **ID4D** initiative, EDGE supported the **[[dnic-mozambique|National
Directorate of Civil Identification (DNIC)]]** to launch Mozambique's **first-ever nationwide free national-ID
campaign** (from 13 November 2023), which had delivered **140,000+ free IDs** within months against a
backdrop where more than half of adults lack proof of identity. A related ID4D project issued IDs and birth
certificates to forcibly displaced persons in Cabo Delgado.
