# Thales

French technology group (its Thales Digital Identity & Security arm, ex-Gemalto) supplying biometric
and identity systems internationally.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Gabon biometric registry (IBOGA) — held via Gemalto, not won directly.** The registry was awarded to
  **Gemalto** by international tender, **announced 20 September 2012**: Coesys biometric enrolment,
  fingerprint deduplication, Coesys Issuance and a PKI, plus training and maintenance
  ([[2012-09-20-gemalto-gabon-biometric-national-registry]]). **Thales holds this thread because it
  acquired Gemalto in 2019** — earlier wiki text saying "Gabon awarded Thales in 2012" was wrong
  (corrected 2026-07-20). Belgian firm **[[semlex]]** was separately contracted for issuance and
  **terminated (~2015)**, producing the ~11-year card-renewal gap. Thales presented IBOGA **jointly with
  Gabon at ID4Africa in June 2023**, covering the RBPP, CNEC and NIP format — and named **"risk of
  vendor lock-in"** as an external challenge in its own deck. Sources:
  [[2012-09-20-gemalto-gabon-biometric-national-registry]],
  [[2024-07-02-gabon-electronic-id-11-year-hiatus]].

**Disambiguation (resolved 2026-07-20).** Thales and **[[in-groupe]]** hold **different layers** of
Gabon's ID stack — Thales the **population registry**, IN Groupe the **credential/secure-document**
platform — and the two are routinely confused because the companies **traded identity assets in both
directions**: IN Groupe bought **Thales's** Identity & Biometrics arm (effective **9 May 2017**), and
Thales bought **Gemalto** (2019). Full treatment: [[gabon--dpi-id]].

- **Mauritius Mobile ID (2024-09).** Thales built the **[[mauritius-mobile-id|Mauritius Mobile ID]]** wallet,
  launched 4 Sep 2024 — billed as the first African ISO-standard mobile digital-ID credential. See [[MUS]],
  [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2024-09-06 Mauritius Launches Mobile ID Digital Wallet with Thales - First African ISO-standard Digital ID Wallet]].
