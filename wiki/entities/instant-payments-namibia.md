# Instant Payments Namibia (IPN)

The entity building Namibia's **instant payment system (IPS)** — a real-time retail-payments rail launched as
part of the DPI drive in 2024, led by COO **Marsorry Ickua** (seconded from the **[[bank-of-namibia|Bank of
Namibia]]**). Objectives echo M-Pesa (move money into the remotest corners, cut transaction costs, deepen
inclusion), with the **India Stack** (digital ID + high connectivity underpinning UPI) as the model.

- **Biometric authentication (2025).** IPN is likely to use **biometrics** for user authentication — good
  authentication being "one of the underlying pillars" of a successful IPS — leaning on the new
  **[[namibia-digital-id|digital ID]]** rather than easily-forged cards; KYC hardware cost is the constraint. See
  [[dpi.id]]. Source: [[2025-07-01-namibia-weighs-biometrics-instant-payment-biometricupdate]].
- **BoN–IPN–NamClear pact (2026-07).** The BoN (Gov. Ebson Uanguta), IPN (Ickua) and **NamClear** (MD Fabian
  Tait) signed a **Declaration of Intent** to integrate the IPS with NamClear's clearing infrastructure and the
  **Namibia Interbank Settlement System (NISS)** — improving efficiency, resilience and interoperability under
  the **[[namibia-payment-system-management-act-2023|Payment System Management Act 2023]]**. See [[finance.mou]].
  Source: [[2026-07-02-bon-ipn-namclear-instant-payments-pact-techafricanews]].
