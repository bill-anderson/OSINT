# ONATEL Burundi (Office National des Télécommunications)

Burundi's **state-owned** telecommunications operator (mobile arm: Onamob), Director-General **Sixte
Niyuhire**. ONATEL is by far the smallest of the country's mobile operators and has been in **financial
crisis for years**, but the government routes rural-connectivity investment through it as the vehicle of
"digital sovereignty" where private returns are thin. See [[BDI]], [[infra.connect]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **$5.9m PAFEN rural-coverage contract (2026-04-29).** [[digital.rural]] ONATEL signed a **$5,907,000**,
  18-month contract with [[setic-burundi|SETIC]]/[[pafen-burundi|PAFEN]] to bring 2G/3G/4G to **92 hills**
  with no coverage — **372,035 people**, plus schools and health facilities — framed by Minister
  Ndikumana as "digital sovereignty and territorial equity." Sources:
  [[2026-05-05-onatel-5-9m-deal-digital-access-rural-burundi]],
  [[2026-05-04-burundi-5-9m-contract-rural-network-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-05-05-burundi-lands-funding-4g-rural-expansion-itweb]].
- **The landline-revival problem (2026-05).** [[include.access]] When President Ndayishimiye proposed
  banning civil servants' mobile phones in favour of office landlines, experts noted ONATEL — the
  primary fixed-line operator — is financially unable to rebuild copper exchanges (worsened by the
  18→5 province reform); VoIP was floated as the only feasible path. Source:
  [[2026-05-29-burundian-president-mobile-phone-ban-civil-servants]].
