# Safe City Project (Zimbabwe)

**The framework under which Zimbabwe deployed an AI-based smart traffic system in
Harare**, developed by the state-owned [[telone]]. One source is held, it is
second-hand, and it is thin — this page exists to hold the thread, not to characterise
the programme.

## Activity in this wiki

- **AI smart traffic system, Harare (as of 2025-07-15).** Launched "recently" as of the
  July 2025 source, with a **plan to extend to other major cities**. Under the project,
  **sensors and cameras** are deployed along **intersections and major roads**, addressing
  **traffic congestion, road accidents and traffic-law enforcement**. Described — per
  Bulawayo 24, relayed by Biometric Update — as **"home-grown, developed by" TelOne**. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[telone]], [[digital.localgov]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-15 Zimbabwe looks forward to digital ID rollout after population registry reform]].
- **A separate smart-city initiative, criticised (as of 2025-07-15).** The same source
  notes Zimbabwe **also** has a smart city initiative covering **a new capital being built
  on the outskirts of Harare**, a project that **"has faced sharp criticism from digital
  rights advocates"**. The source names neither the critics nor their objections, and does
  not say whether this is connected to the Safe City Project. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  as above.

## Tension flagged, not resolved

A camera-and-sensor network built for **traffic-law enforcement** invites comparison with
the held IDS survey (2026-04-04) listing Zimbabwe among states running **Chinese-supplied
([[hikvision]] / [[zte]] / [[huawei]]) camera and command-centre systems without adequate
legal oversight** — where critics fear facial-recognition targeting of opposition and
activists (see [[ZWE]]). **These may be different systems entirely**, and neither source
addresses the other. **Insufficient evidence to call.** Flagged for the reconcile pass;
not a contradiction.

## Provenance

Single, doubly second-hand source (Biometric Update citing Bulawayo 24). No procurement,
budget, vendor stack, legal basis or oversight arrangement is held for this project.
