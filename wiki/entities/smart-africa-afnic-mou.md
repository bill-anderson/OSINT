# Smart Africa–AFNIC MoU (internet infrastructure)

**Deal / MoU.** Memorandum of Understanding between **[[smart-africa]]** and **[[afnic]]**,
signed in **Geneva** (2026-07) by Lacina Koné (Smart Africa CEO) and Pierre Bonis (AFNIC
DG). No financial value disclosed — a technical-cooperation agreement.

## Scope

- **Capacity of African ccTLD operators** — strengthening the management and sustainability
  of national internet domain registries.
- **DNS security and resilience** — hardening a critical layer of internet infrastructure.
- **Registry modernisation** — advanced technologies and best practices for performance,
  security and efficiency; improved internet governance.

Framed by Smart Africa as a step toward a "more secure, resilient and modern internet
ecosystem for Africa," supporting the continent's digital-transformation agenda. See
[[XAF]], [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.standards]], [[gov.regional]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: African control over the continent's own domain-name infrastructure and
  internet governance — though the technical capacity is transferred from a European
  registry (AFNIC), the familiar externally-scaffolded-agency pattern.
