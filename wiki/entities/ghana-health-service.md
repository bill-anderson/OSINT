# Ghana Health Service (GHS)

Ghana's public health-service delivery agency and the custodian of the country's digital-health direction. GHS owns the [[ghana-digital-health-policy-2023-2027]] and drives the modernization of its health-information systems ([[dpi.mis]]) — the interoperability and connectivity of clinical and administrative ICT systems across national, regional and district levels. GHS is also a pilot partner with the [[national-identification-authority-ghana|NIA]] and Births & Deaths Registry on capturing children aged 0-6. See [[GHA]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digital Health Policy and Strategy 2023-2027 (2023).** [[dpi.mis]] GHS issued its five-year digital-health policy — ten strategic objectives including strengthening digital-health governance and improving interoperability/connectivity of all ICT systems; developed with WHO/UNICEF/USAID evidence and DANIDA + Global Fund support. See [[ghana-digital-health-policy-2023-2027]], [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2023-01-01 Ghana Policy and Strategy on Digital Health 2023-2027 (GHS)]].
