# National Authority for the Protection of Personal Data (ANPDCP, Algeria)

Algeria's **data-protection supervisory authority** (also rendered **ANPDP**), established under **Law No.
18-07** on the protection of personal data (enacted **10 June 2018**; in force from **10 August 2023**),
**amended and completed by Law No. 25-11**. Law 18-07 governs the collection and processing of personal data
and is paired with a **mandatory data-localisation** rule — personal data must be stored within Algeria.
President: **Samir Bourehil** (2026).

- **Enforcement push (2025-12).** ANPDP pressed public and private bodies to comply with Law 25-11 —
  **DPO designation, a processing register, and an automated audit log**.
- **Security framing (2026-07).** At a DGSN study day (police chief Ali Badaoui: *"national security is now
  cybersecurity first"*), with the Justice and Interior ministers, the ANPDP president and an ASSI
  representative — a unified guidance manual on data-protection procedures was announced.

Algeria's data-protection regime is being extended by the November-2025 **[[algeria-digital-id-trust-law|digital-ID and trust-services draft law]]** and the 2025 **[[algeria-data-governance-framework|public-data governance framework]]**, both of which lean on this base and its localisation stance.
