# Chad National Biometric Registry

**Chad's national biometric database**, operated by [[anats]] and intended by the government to
become **the single source of truth for all identification purposes** — the authentication layer
that other ministries, agencies and private entities connect to rather than maintaining their own
identity silos. The registry is the identity foundation of [[TCD]]'s digital-government agenda and
is framed by the authorities as an instrument of [[tchad-connexion-2030]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Interoperability as the operating model (March 2026).** The Ministry of Finance, Budget,
  Economy, Planning and International Cooperation concluded a deal with ANATS to share data in
  **real time** off the registry, aimed at transparency in administrative and financial
  management, streamlined identity verification and authentication, fraud and identity-theft
  prevention, and strengthening digital trust in line with Chad's 2030 strategy. The government
  hopes many further ministries and agencies will integrate. ANATS DG **Youssouf Gorou Tchaby**
  notes private-sector entities can also connect — for identity verification and for **access to
  credit**. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].
- **Build cost — second-hand attribution.** *Africa 24 TV, quoting government sources*, puts
  Chad's investment in building and strengthening the registry at an estimated **XAF 15 billion
  (~US$26.1 million)** over recent years, including production of secure identity documents and
  upgrades to basic digital infrastructure (as reported 2026-03-16). Relayed through Biometric
  Update; no primary provenance is held for it. See [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].
- **Inclusive enrolment.** Chad issues **biometric ID cards to refugees** using [[idemia]]
  technology, and ANATS received **biometric enrolment equipment from [[unhcr]]** (as reported
  2026-03-16). Separately, the civil-registration arm feeds the identity base from below: since
  health-facility integration in **2023**, **120,000+ children** registered via hospital-based
  registry offices (~70% of rural population covered) and **180,000+ pupils** via school
  campaigns, as of 2025-06-03 — with biometric registration missions run for older individuals and
  nomadic populations. See [[include.access]], [[digital.rural]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]],
  [[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]].

## Reading it

The "single source of truth" ambition and the open-to-private-sector connection model are stated
by ANATS, not independently verified in the sources held. No source in this batch reports the
registry's enrolment total, its technology stack, or a data-protection authority overseeing it —
notable given the registry is being wired into finance and credit decisions. See
[[gov.protect]].
