# Africa CDC (Africa Centres for Disease Control and Prevention)

The [[african-union]]'s continental public-health agency, coordinating disease
surveillance, health-data systems and pandemic response across member states. Its
digital work bears directly on health-management information systems
([[dpi.mis]]) and cross-facility data exchange ([[dpi.exchange]]), making it a
key voice on the infrastructure constraints behind African health data.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Power and connectivity as a binding constraint (2026-04-23).** Africa CDC
  Chief Digital Advisor Jean-Philbert Nsengimana is cited that more than 50% of
  African health facilities are not yet connected to power or the internet — a
  foundational gap limiting electronic-health-record and interoperability
  progress across the continent, independent of software maturity. Source:
  [[2026-04-23 Health Data Without Continuity Why Electronic Health Records Still Have Not Scaled Across Africa]].
- **Aid-dependence and funding-shock deaths (2025-04-03).** DG John Kaseya warned
  that African countries depend on external aid for up to **80%** of the cost of
  critical programmes like malaria and HIV, and that the 2025 US funding cuts
  could cause ~**4 million** extra deaths a year and raise pandemic risk — cited by
  [[dhis2]]/HISP in its case for locally-owned systems. See [[usaid]],
  [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-03 Now is the time to invest in locally-owned health data systems]].
- **Health Security & Sovereignty Agenda (2025-11).** Kaseya launched the Africa
  Health Security and Sovereignty Agenda, calling data ownership "non-negotiable"
  ("We want to own our data in Africa"). Africa CDC declined a US observer role in
  the [[america-first-global-health-strategy]] bilateral health MOUs but pledged
  technical support to all members regardless — advisory authority without power to
  direct sovereign decisions as 18 states signed data- and specimen-sharing deals.
  See [[usaid]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-19 African Digital Health Data Is a Condition of Global Health Funding]].
- **Backing sovereign pushback on US health MoUs (2026-02).** As [[ZMB]] halted its ~$1bn US health
  deal over mining-linked and data-sharing clauses ([[zambia-us-health-mou]]) — joining [[ZWE]] and
  [[KEN]] against the [[america-first-global-health-strategy]] terms — DG Kaseya publicly backed
  countries renegotiating: "We want to own our data in Africa... We cannot accept not owning our
  data." See [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-26 Zambia rejects U.S. health aid over mining partnership ties]].
- **"Huge concerns" over data and pathogens (2026-03-05).** At a virtual press conference DG Kaseya
  said the [[america-first-global-health-strategy]] deals carry "huge concerns regarding data,
  regarding pathogen sharing," confirming [[ZWE]] had pulled out of a $367m/5-yr deal a day earlier
  and [[ZMB]] had pushed back. He said he had first welcomed the strategy (more direct funding,
  required co-investment) but declined an observer role to respect member-state sovereignty, offering
  Africa CDC support to any country renegotiating. See [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-05 Africa CDC head cites major concerns over data, pathogen sharing in US health deals]],
  [[2026-01-20 Africa Pushes Back On US Health Deals Over Data, Power]].
- **Aggregated-sovereignty role (2026-02).** Analysts position Africa CDC — with the **African
  Pooled Procurement Mechanism** operationalised at the **39th AU Assembly (Feb 2026)** and the
  African Medicines Agency — as the vehicle for converting fragmented national demand into a
  collective "Health Bloc," a structural-decoupling counter to bilateral divide-and-rule. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[african-union]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-24 Global Health Nigeria and the Pathology of the Hostage State in an Era of Fragmentation]].
