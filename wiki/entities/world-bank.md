# World Bank

The World Bank Group — here as financier and appraiser of African digital
transformation through IDA (International Development Association) grants and
credits, plus IFC/MIGA under a "One World Bank" approach.

## Relevant activity (as of 2026-07)

- **[[idea-mpa]]** — the US$2.48bn Inclusive Digitalization in Eastern & Southern
  Africa MPA (approved 27 Jun 2024), through which the Bank finances national digital
  programmes across the region.
- **[[dzap]]** — US$100M IDA grant to Zambia (PAD 2025-09-11), the 5th IDEA operation.
  See [[ZMB]] and source [[2025-09-11 DZAP World Bank PAD]].
- Runs the **ID4D** (Identification for Development) Trust Fund and Principles guiding
  digital-ID work; the WB **Digital Strategy** was approved Jul 2025; **IDA-21**
  carries a Digital Policy Commitment.
- **[[dmap]]** — the Digital Malawi Acceleration Project, financing Malawi's Bomalathu
  single digital-services platform and cross-MDA interoperability (2026-07 immigration
  integration). See [[MWI]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **[[nigeria-id4d]]** — co-financier (with AFD and the EIB) of Nigeria's NIN expansion;
  the WB-funded project requires the government to issue **≥180m digital IDs by end-2026**
  (a background source dated 2025-02). See [[NGA]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-13 Digital ID necessity increases in Nigeria despite lingering challenges]].
- **2026-07 batch.** Financing/engagement across: **Eswatini**'s Digital Transformation Project ([[eswatini-digital-transformation-project]]); the **[[ifc]]** (WBG) **$150m** [[airtel-africa]] loan; and Nigeria's **$430m** ID4D ([[nigeria-id4d]]). See [[SWZ]], [[XSS]], [[NGA]], [[finance.new]]. Sources: [[2026-05-18 Eswatini promotes digital transformation project through World Bank engagements]], [[2026-07-01 IFC and Airtel Africa Expand Digital Connectivity to Strengthen Jobs and Inclusion]], [[2026-06-05 Nigeria looks beyond enrollment as digital ID passes 130 million]].
- **Tanzania Jamii Namba (2026-04).** The Bank's **Digital Tanzania Project** (funded since
  2021) underpins Tanzania's national digital-ID drive; NIDA ([[nida-tanzania]]) is procuring
  contractors for **Phase II** of Jamii Namba (adding iris/facial, a NIDA HQ in Dodoma,
  expansion to 31 districts), alongside a separate ~$70m Korea Eximbank loan. See [[TZA]],
  [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-22 Tanzania seeks biometrics contractors for Phase II of national digital ID project]].
- **Trusted-DPI "formula" at ID4Africa 2026 (2026-05-16).** At the [[id4africa]] AGM the Bank
  led a session on **data-lifecycle trust**, cataloguing six identity-data storage risks
  (vendor immaturity, single points of failure, function creep, cryptographic gaps, weak
  insider controls, absent redress) and convening African DPAs (Mauritius, Benin, Liberia)
  and the [[eac]] on regional data-protection cooperation. See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.id]],
  [[mosip]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].
- **WURI / West Africa digital ID (2026-03-30).** The Bank co-secretariats (with [[ecowas]])
  the [[wuri-programme]] (West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and
  Inclusion); its 2nd Supervisory Committee validated a cross-border interoperability
  strategy and the 2026 work plan toward mutual recognition of ID credentials across
  ~300m ECOWAS citizens (aligned to ECOWAS Vision 2050). See [[XWA]], [[TGO]], [[dpi.id]].
  Source: [[2026-03-30 ECOWAS, World Bank Advance West Africa Digital Identity]].
- **Zambia digital ID via DZAP (2026-03).** Under the $100m [[dzap]] IDA grant,
  [[smart-zambia-institute]] is procuring a MOSIP system integrator for a home-grown,
  open-standards national digital ID (full rollout targeted end-2026). See [[ZMB]],
  [[dpi.id]], [[mosip]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]],
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia seeks partner for national digital ID system]].
- **WARDIP2 — $137m, three ECOWAS states (2026-03-11).** The Bank committed **$137m** to the
  second phase of the [[wardip]] (Western Africa Regional Digital Integration Program) for
  **Benin, Liberia and Sierra Leone** — broadband to >5m people and digital public services to
  >5m more (~10.6m total) across three pillars (infrastructure/data-centre capacity, enabling
  environment, cross-border services). See [[BEN]], [[LBR]], [[SLE]], [[XWA]], [[finance.new]].
  Source: [[2026-03-16 New $137M initiative to enable digital service access for millions in three ECOWAS nations]].
- **GovTech Maturity Index 2025 (2026-01).** The Bank's **2025 [[gtmi]]** placed Tanzania in
  Group A ("Extensive"), alongside Kenya, Rwanda, Uganda and Egypt; the 48-indicator, four-pillar
  index now stresses interoperability, adoption and outcomes over portals launched. See [[TZA]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-26 Tanzania's GovTech success raises bar for East Africa's digital states]].
- **Burkina Faso PACTDIGITAL-BF (2026-03).** A ~**$170m** IDA credit (plus national counterpart)
  funds Burkina's Fast-Tracking Digital Transformation programme (12 flagship projects by 2030;
  in force Sept 2024) — connectivity, public-service digitisation, a national data centre and a
  "zero data abroad" localization drive (two Ouagadougou mini data centres commissioned Jan 2026).
  See [[BFA]], [[infra.store]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-23 Burkina Faso steps into the future with digital investment]].
- **Cameroon PATNUC cybersecurity (2026-01).** Under the Bank-funded [[patnuc]] (Project for the
  Acceleration of Digital Transformation of Cameroon), [[antic-cameroon]] took delivery of ~**$1.3m**
  of intrusion-detection/response hardware and software for its national CIRT. See [[CMR]],
  [[infra.cybersec]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-27 Cameroon deploys cybersecurity system to protect DPI, boost digital trust]],
  [[2026-01-22 Cameroon boosts cyber defense systems]].
- **Benin foundational ID & civil registration (WURI).** Bank-supported [[wuri-programme]] work
  drove Benin's fID ("C'est moi") certificate to **near-99%** biometric enrolment (via
  [[anip-benin]]); the Bank also donated **2,050** mobile biometric terminals (2024) feeding
  births/marriages/deaths into the RNPP/RNEC, and by 2025 ≥75% of Benin's identification services
  were accessed digitally. See [[BEN]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]]. Sources:
  [[2025-11-01 Identification System in Benin Increasing Access to Services]],
  [[2024-10-28 Benin receives 2,050 biometric terminals from World Bank to boost civil registration]],
  [[2024-06-27 WURI digital ID project improving access to public services in Benin]].
- **Nigeria BRIDGE — $500m fibre (approved 2025-10).** The Bank's **$500m BRIDGE** project
  funds **90,000km** of fibre to widen connectivity, cited against Nigeria's disharmonised
  digital-ID/finance stack (siloed NIMC-NIN, CBN-BVN and SIM registries) that locks
  low-income farmers out of NIRSAL/GEEP credit — only ~**38%** of Nigerians were online in
  2024. See [[NGA]], [[infra.connect]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]]; cf. the EU/GIZ
  [[digital-investment-facility]] as a parallel DPI-finance vehicle. Source:
  [[2026-03-03 Nigeria's Disharmonised Digital System Leaving Low-Income Farmers Behind]].

- **EAC cross-border payment PoC (2026-07-10).** The Bank was among the partners (with [[giz]],
  the RWA/TZA central banks and the EAC Secretariat) at the Zanzibar technical meeting on a
  proof-of-concept interlinking Tanzania's **[[tips-tanzania]]** and Rwanda's
  **[[rswitch-rwanda]]** under the [[eac-cross-border-payment-masterplan]]. See [[RWA]],
  [[TZA]], [[XEA]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 New Rwanda-Tanzania Payment Pilot could transform trade across East Africa]].
- **Digital Gabon Project — $68.5m, chronic under-execution (2024).** The Bank's
  **[[digital-gabon-project]]** (P175987; IBRD-92830) finances Gabon's legal-ID, interoperability,
  data-protection/cybersecurity and data-centre build. Signed 2 Jan 2024 (approved Jul 2021) but the
  Bank's own **Implementation Status & Results Reports** document a stark delivery gap: not yet
  effective by mid-2024, effective only 30 Jul 2024 "after a significant delay," and **1.92%
  disbursed** with all results indicators at baseline by Dec 2024 — the primary evidence behind
  Gabon's 2026 push to raise disbursement. The Bank also partners on the [[registre-social-unique-gabon]]
  social registry and the [[sige-gabon]] education MIS (tablets). See [[GAB]], [[dpi.id]],
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Sources: [[2024-01-02-world-bank-digital-gabon-68m-loan]],
  [[2024-06-29-world-bank-digital-gabon-isr-seq6]], [[2024-12-19-world-bank-digital-gabon-isr-seq7]].
- **MyMzansi / Google–World Bank public-services partnership (2025-11, [[ZAF]]).** SA's
  Presidency-built **[[mymzansi]]** DPI prototype references a Google–World Bank partnership to
  reach 100m people with AI-driven public services — the Bank cited as a backer of AI-enabled
  digital-government delivery. See [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-09 Behind the speedy MyMzansi screens and the Presidency's race to build a digital government]].

## Why it matters here

The Bank is a structuring actor in African DPI: it sets the financing terms, the
results frameworks (corporate results indicators), and the standards (ID4D
Principles). That makes it central to both the `sovereignty` reading (financing
homegrown, vendor-independent systems) and the `colonialism` reading (external
finance and technical assistance shaping national digital architecture).

## Reference instruments

- **[[world-bank-theme-taxonomy]]** — the Bank's official Theme Taxonomy (June 2025), the
  controlled vocabulary it uses to classify the themes of the operations recorded here
  (e.g. [[dzap]], [[dmap]], [[nigeria-id4d]]). Captured as the wiki's first `instrument`
  entity. See [[gov.standards]].
- **[[world-development-report-2025]]** — the Bank's flagship WDR 2025, "Standards for
  Development" (incl. Spotlight 5 on Standards & DPI), now captured as an `instrument`
  entity alongside the theme taxonomy. See [[gov.standards]].
- **[[infrastructure-foundations-straub-2026]]** — the Bank's flagship *Infrastructure
  Foundations* (Straub et al., 2026, Sustainable Infrastructure Series), a global
  geolocated measurement of energy/transport/**digital** capital stocks and returns.
  Captured as a `publication` (analytical, not normative). Source of unit-cost and
  digital-divide reference data for [[infra.connect]], [[infra.store]].
