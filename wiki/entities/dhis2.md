# DHIS2

Open-source health-management information platform and one of the world's largest
digital public goods, developed and run by the HISP Centre at the University of
Oslo. Deployed in 75+ countries, it is license-free and locally owned, and is a
standing OSINT resource for African health-management information systems
([[dpi.mis]]) — disease surveillance, health-system monitoring and routine
statistics ([[data.statistics]]). It is a core reference point when assessing
electronic-health-record and interoperability progress on the continent.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Large African deployments (as of 2026-04-23).** Ethiopia runs one of the
  largest DHIS2 deployments on the continent, strengthening disease surveillance
  and health-system monitoring, though patient-level records remain incomplete;
  DHIS2 sits alongside OpenMRS and proprietary national systems as a
  frequently-siloed layer in African EHR interoperability. Source:
  [[2026-04-23 Health Data Without Continuity Why Electronic Health Records Still Have Not Scaled Across Africa]].
- **Shared Services Fee financing model (2026-05-13).** The HISP Centre
  introduced a collective, income-tiered annual financing model to sustain the
  platform as donor funding fragments. Payers are recognised as "Contributing
  Partners" with input into the DHIS2 roadmap and governance; fees scale by
  sector and World Bank income group and are charged per production instance,
  while keeping the software free, open and free of vendor lock-in. Source:
  [[2026-05-13 Shared Services Fee]].
- **Post-USAID-cut resilience (2025-04-03).** A HISP-network survey found that
  locally-owned national DHIS2 systems stayed online through the 2025 US/USAID
  funding shock, even as US-funded parallel systems (e.g. PEPFAR's DATIM) were
  switched off — the [[hisp-centre]]'s core argument for routine, locally-owned
  open-source health data. DHIS2 is used in 90+ countries (75 at national scale),
  backing routine data that touches ~3.2bn people. See [[usaid]], [[africa-cdc]].
  Source: [[2025-04-03 Now is the time to invest in locally-owned health data systems]].
- **Open Data Watch report (2025-11-05).** ODW's *Rebuilding Global Health Data*
  confirms >75 LMICs rely on DHIS2 as their national HMIS (>40% of world
  population) and flags three risks — donor over-reliance, weak domestic
  financing/governance, poor country coordination — recommending permanent,
  government-funded HMIS roles in recurrent budgets. See [[open-data-watch]],
  [[hisp-centre]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-05 New report highlights urgent need to rebuild and sustain health data systems]].
- **African HIS literature review (2021-08-04).** A review of health information
  systems across 12 African countries found all had adopted a DHIS2-based
  data-warehouse approach, but 11 of 12 aligned to donor strategies rather than a
  national HIS strategy (Malawi the exception) — governance, not the tool, the
  binding weakness. See [[his-developing-countries-review]]. Source:
  [[2021-08-04 Health information systems in developing countries case of African countries]].
- **Tanzania HMIS political economy (2023-10-30).** DHIS2 was rolled out to all
  Tanzanian district councils in 2013 and now carries >90% of facility-to-national
  data flows, atop an ecosystem of 18 fragmented systems; a PEA finds
  integration/interoperability stalled by capacity, financing and closed-source
  lock-in. See [[hmis-tanzania-pea]], [[TZA]]. Source:
  [[2023-10-30 A political economy analysis of strengthening health information system in Tanzania]].
- **Ethiopia maturity + data-use (2018 national rollout).** DHIS2 has run
  nationally in Ethiopia since 2018 with >95% facility uptake; a SOCI maturity
  assessment rates the system at the 'defined' stage — weakest on ICT
  infrastructure and interoperability, partly because Ethiopia lacks a national
  digital ID/client registry — while a facility data-use index rose modestly
  2019→2020. See [[dhis2-maturity-ethiopia]], [[hmis-data-use-index-ethiopia]],
  [[ETH]]. Sources:
  [[2024-07-26 Maturity Assessment of District Health Information System Version 2 Implementation in Ethiopia Current Status and Improvement Pathways]],
  [[2023-05-11 Data for local decision-making, not a mere reporting requirement development of an index to measure facility-level use of HMIS data  Published in Journal of Global Health Reports]].
- **Malawi facility data-use (2025-10-21).** In Malawi's DHIS2-based HMIS, a study
  of six Synod of Livingstonia facilities found data widely collected but
  under-used for decisions (70% facility vs 33% district review), constrained by
  missing SOPs, training gaps and weak supervision. See [[hmis-malawi-livingstonia]],
  [[MWI]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-21 Utilisation of data and factors affecting the performance of the Health Management Information System (HMIS). The case of six Synods of Livingstonia health facilities in Malawi]].

- **Rwanda impact (DPGA "Building Open Digital States," 2026).** The **[[dpga]]** report
  credits Rwanda's DHIS2 with lifting health-data completeness from **88% to 95%** and
  contributing to a **>50%** fall in maternal mortality (2010–19); Rwanda now exports DHIS2
  (and Mojaloop) regionally. See [[RWA]], [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Digital Public Goods Alliance]].
- **Nigeria DNEMIS — now the world's largest DHIS2 (2026-07-05).** Nigeria's Federal Ministry
  of Education launched **DNEMIS**, a DHIS2-based national **education** MIS, in Abuja —
  described as the **largest DHIS2 implementation in the world** and the platform's expansion
  beyond health into the education sector. Built with the [[hisp-centre]], HISP Nigeria,
  UNICEF and the [[european-union]]; Minister Maruf Tunji Alausa framed it as data sovereignty
  (data domiciled in Nigeria). See [[dnemis-nigeria]], [[federal-ministry-education-nigeria]],
  [[NGA]], [[dpi.mis]]. Source: [[2026-07-05 Post by DHIS2 on LinkedIn]].
- **[[GNQ]] Equatorial Guinea — DHIS2 eLMIS for malaria logistics (2022).** The Bioko Island Malaria
  Elimination Project (BIMEP) extended its DHIS2 health instance into an **electronic logistics MIS**
  for malaria commodities — forecasting stock-on-hand, triangulating stock vs services data (exposing
  reporting gaps/leakage) and alerting on expiries — replacing error-prone Excel tools. See [[dpi.mis]].
  Source: [[2022-12-08 Deploying DHIS2 for eLMIS to improve Malaria stock management in Equatorial Guinea]].
- **[[GMB]] The Gambia — DHIS2 for health and education (2017-2026).** The Gambia runs DHIS2 as its
  HMIS backbone: **Smart Paper Technology** (Shifo Foundation, MRCG-LSHTM, US CDC) scans structured
  paper forms into DHIS2 for maternal/child health (unique IDs, QR codes; reporting lags months→days),
  and **MoBSE** is building a learner-level education MIS on **DHIS2 for Education** (GPE/HISP). See
  [[dpi.mis]], [[GMB]]. Sources:
  [[2025-12-22 Smart Paper Technology is Transforming Community Health Data Capture in The Gambia]],
  [[2026-01-30 The Gambia charts a path to data-driven education with DHIS2]].
