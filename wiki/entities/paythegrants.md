# #PayTheGrants

**A South African grassroots campaign for social-grant access, co-litigant with the
[[institute-for-economic-justice]] in the Supreme Court of Appeal challenge over the Social Relief
of Distress (SRD) grant.** Its significance to the wiki is evidentiary: until this case, the wiki
held only rhetorical warnings that digital-first becomes digital-only. Here that argument is
litigated, with numbers, against a live national system.

*The arguments below are the campaign's own, reported from filed court papers — pleadings, not
findings.*

## Activity in this wiki

- **Opposing the state's SCA appeal (as reported 2026-01-28).** #PayTheGrants and the IEJ are
  responding to the government's application to the Supreme Court of Appeal against a **January
  2025** high court ruling that found several SRD regulations unlawful — including the
  **online-only application system**, the **R624 monthly income threshold** and the **grant's
  value**. They accuse **[[national-treasury-south-africa]]** and the Department of Social
  Development of deliberately excluding millions of eligible people. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 State is blocking access to SASSA grants, argue activists]].
- **The core charge.** From the papers: the state made the grant "inaccessible to millions of people
  who need it, refused it to eligible beneficiaries based on inaccurate information, reduced its
  value and the means threshold for eligibility, and **persistently failed to pay hundreds of
  thousands of approved beneficiaries**." **[[sassa]]**'s own 2026-01-19 statement supplies dated
  government corroboration of the unpaid-approvals limb (**2,690 paid of 8,368 approved, 32%**, as
  of 1 Jan 2026, for PR/refugee/permit-holder applicants). Source: same.
- **The online-only fight, in its own words.** The campaign argues the online-only system is
  irrational and unconstitutional, adopted to "serve the improper purpose of reducing uptake."
  Against the department's argument that **complaints posted on X prove there is no barrier to
  online access**, the groups counter that a significant number of applicants **have no internet
  access at all**, and that the system poses "**an insurmountable hurdle**" for those without
  digital literacy or with disabilities. See [[include.divides]], [[capacity.literacy]],
  [[digital.rural]]. Source: same.
- **Bank verification as the exclusion engine.** Treating **any money entering a bank account** as
  income, and testing it on a **single month's snapshot**, wrongly excludes eligible applicants —
  on what the groups characterise as the government's own unchallenged evidence. Source: same.
- **Scale claimed (department's June-2022 figures).** **13.4m** working-age people with no income;
  **18.3m** below the food poverty line; Treasury funds **fewer than half** of those the department
  estimates would qualify. Source: same.
