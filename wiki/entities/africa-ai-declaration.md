# Africa Declaration on Artificial Intelligence (Kigali)

A continental AI declaration adopted at the **April 2025 Global AI Summit in Kigali, Rwanda**,
sitting alongside and building on the [[au-continental-ai-strategy]]. In this wiki it recurs as
the marquee continental-governance commitment — invoked both as a genuine building block and, by
critics, as "performed sovereignty".

## Key facts (as of 2026-07-13)

- **Signed 4 April 2025, Kigali.** The **primary Declaration document** settles a prior source
  discrepancy: its signatory block lists **49 country signatories** plus the **African Union** and
  **Smart Africa** ([[2025-04-04 Africa Declaration on Artificial Intelligence]]). Absent of the 54
  UN-recognised African states: **DR Congo, Eritrea, Libya, Seychelles, Tanzania** (and Western
  Sahara). Secondary accounts had reported **54** ([[2026-05-19 GATEKEEPERS XIV]];
  [[2026-06-01 Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate]]) and **52** — "only Tanzania and Western
  Sahara absent" ([[2025-05-13 Optimism but uncertainty over Africa's AI Declaration]]); both are high
  against the primary. **[[zuria-nagadya]]**'s **49** matches. *(Resolves REV-001(a).)*
- Established the **Africa AI Council**, co-chaired by the **African Union Commission** and the
  **[[itu]]**.
- Committed a **$60 billion Africa AI Fund**; [[gates-foundation]] named as the largest supporter
  (per Munyua).
- Named the headline gap it responds to: **African datasets ≈ 1% of global AI data despite Africa
  being 17% of world population**.

## Two readings in this wiki

- **[[zuria-nagadya]]** (Africa in the Room) treats it as a real building block that nonetheless
  shares the informal-economy blind spot of all current African AI frameworks.
- **[[alice-munyua]]** ([[2026-05-19 GATEKEEPERS XIV]]) reads the Kigali summit — co-convened with
  the World Economic Forum, a Gates-endorsed declaration with a Google-co-organised sandbox
  workshop — as "performed sovereignty" facilitated by the philanthropic industrial complex.
