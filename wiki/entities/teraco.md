# Teraco

**South Africa's largest data-centre operator and the anchor of the continent's colocation
market** — a vendor-neutral colocation and interconnection business founded **2008**, in which
**[[digital-realty]] took a majority stake in January 2022 at a valuation of ~$3.5 billion**.
CEO **[[jan-hnizdo]]**; CFO Samuel Erwin. Teraco is the single largest component of the South
African data-centre power thesis on [[south-africa--infra-store]], and the operator against whose
disclosure practice [[cavaleros]]' non-disclosure is explicitly contrasted.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Scale (as of 2026-02).** SA's largest operator at **nearly 190 MW IT load**, serving
  ~**670 colocation customers**. An internal Teraco document listing capacities and expansion plans
  targets **500 MW total** — including **290 MW of new Johannesburg capacity** and a further
  **60 MW in Cape Town**. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Major SA data centre expansion plans emerge, set to more than double current power demand]].
- **Portfolio (as of 2025-06).** **Isando** (Johannesburg, three facilities, 70 MW after JB5);
  **Bredell** campus, Johannesburg (JB2/JB4, **63 MW**); **Cape Town** campus at Brackenfell
  (CT1/CT2, **53 MW**); **Durban, 2 MW**. Source:
  [[2025-06-08 Teraco completes JB5 data center in Johannesburg, South Africa (DCD)]].
- **JB5 completed (2025-06).** Teraco's **eighth** SA data centre, at Isando, taking the campus to
  **70 MW** of critical power. **12 data halls × 1,000 m²**, **55,000 m²** total building footprint,
  **120 MVA** utility supply, built in a **single phase**; broke ground **2022**. Closed-loop chilled
  water with 100% free-air cooling — zero water for ongoing cooling. Sources:
  [[2025-06-03 Teraco Unveils 8th Data Centre, JB5, Strengthening Africa's Digital Infrastructure]],
  [[2025-06-08 Teraco completes JB5 data center in Johannesburg, South Africa (DCD)]].
- **JB7 under construction (announced 2024-11, completion 2026).** A **40 MW hyperscale** build at
  Isando and Teraco's **ninth** data-centre development: **71,000 m²**, **68 MVA** utility supply,
  **8 data halls × 1,500 m²**, single-phase. It lifts **Isando to 110 MW** and **Teraco's total
  critical power to 228 MW**. Funded by an **R8-billion syndicated loan secured through Absa** and
  other financial institutions — a loan that **also part-funds a 120 MW utility-scale solar PV
  plant** feeding the **[[eskom]]** network and wheeling power across multiple municipal grids.
  Designed for liquid-to-air and liquid-to-liquid cooling for high-density cloud and AI. Source:
  [[2024-11-13 Teraco to build 40 MW JB7 data centre at Isando Campus]].
- **Interconnection.** The Isando campus carries **>16,500 interconnects** (2024-11) — the density
  that makes it the sub-Saharan colocation hub rather than merely the largest floorspace. Hnizdo
  frames SA as the springboard for cloud into Africa, strengthened by subsea investment including
  **[[equiano-cable]]** and 2Africa. Source:
  [[2024-11-13 Teraco to build 40 MW JB7 data centre at Isando Campus]].
- **Transparency, noted approvingly (2026-02).** Daily Maverick contrasts Teraco's disclosure of
  current capacities, expansion plans and energy approach with **[[cavaleros]]**' position that its
  power procurement is "commercially sensitive" and under strict NDAs. On a governance question —
  who is buying how much of a constrained grid — Teraco is the operator that answers. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Major SA data centre expansion plans emerge, set to more than double current power demand]].
- **Market listing (vendor-asserted, 2025-03).** Teraco (Digital Realty) is named among the major
  SA operators in the **[[research-and-markets]]** commercial database promotion — a dated prior,
  not current state. Source: [[2025-03-18 South Africa Data Center Portfolio 2025 - Major Investors]].
