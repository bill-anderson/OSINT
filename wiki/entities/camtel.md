# CAMTEL (Cameroon Telecommunications)

Cameroon's **state-owned telecom incumbent** (Managing Director **Judith Yah Sunday epse Achidi**) —
owner of the national fibre backbone and the submarine-cable landing stations, and the institutional
face of the country's **digital-sovereignty** push. In Russell Southwood's incumbents study it is the
*only profitable* former fixed-line incumbent on the continent — but the profit rests on **protected
wholesale and landing-station monopolies**, not retail mobile, where it is negligible.

## Profile and market position

- **Retail vs wholesale split (2026-03).** Mobile market share: **Camtel 2.67%, Orange 50.08%, MTN
  47.2%** — Camtel's retail presence is marginal; its value is wholesale bandwidth and gateway control.
  It nonetheless tendered for **2.3 million SIMs** (Mar 2026) to push a retail play. Source:
  [[2026-03-30-camtel-tender-2-3-million-sims-itweb]].
- **Fibre backbone.** ~**12,000 km** national backbone; **180,000 fibre ports deployed, target 230,000
  by end-2025**; 4G/LTE in 160+ municipalities (Sep 2025). Source:
  [[2025-09-02-camtel-expands-fiber-network-fixed-telephony-techafricanews]].
- **Regional gatekeeper.** As the landing-station monopolist, Camtel gatekeeps international bandwidth
  for landlocked neighbours — **Chad's incumbent Sotel Tchad renegotiated its debt to Camtel** (May
  2025), direct evidence of the dependency from the Chadian side; Cameroon is Chad's only submarine-cable
  entry point. Source: [[2025-05-16-lejour-sotel-tchad-renegotiates-camtel-debt]].

## Financing — MNE (and the CBC figure reconciled)

- **CBC / Mobile Network Expansion (21 Jan 2026).** Camtel and Commercial Bank–Cameroun (CBC) closed a
  **FCFA 44.884bn syndicated financing** for **phase 1** of the MNE project, with **CBC as Lead
  Underwriter / Lead Arranger / Collateral Agent** — i.e. CBC *arranged* the package, not solely funded
  it. **Reconciliation:** the wiki also holds a "Jan 2026 **US$81.2m** CBC package" (Southwood); given
  the syndicated structure, the FCFA 44.884bn phase-1 tranche and the ~US$81.2m facility are plausibly
  **different quantities (tranche vs total), not a conflict** — both kept, dated and distinctly labelled,
  pending a CAMTEL/CBC primary. (Also ~US$65.65m from BEAC in 2025.) Sources (both PR-wire, same day):
  [[2026-01-22-camtel-cbc-44-88bn-syndicated-financing-mne-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-01-22-camtel-cbc-44-884bn-fcfa-mobile-network-expansion-techafricanews]].

## The sovereignty play — and its tension

- **South–South substitution: Ethio Telecom MSA (4 Dec 2025).** Camtel signed a Master Service Agreement
  with **[[ethio-telecom]]** — four workstreams: Blue Money fintech, a **Sovereign Government Cloud**
  architecture, 4G/5G, and digital culture — explicitly framed "African solutions to African problems"
  (Yah Sunday: "Africa does not need to import all its models"). A Dec-2025 execution visit followed.
  Sources: [[2025-12-05-ethio-telecom-camtel-master-service-agreement-telecomreviewafrica]],
  [[2025-12-16-ethio-telecom-team-concludes-strategic-visit-camtel-telecomreviewafrica]].
- **Zamengoe Data Centre.** Camtel positions the **[[zamengoe-data-centre]]** (Tier III, claimed
  "largest in Central Africa" — unverified) as the state-data sovereignty asset, pitched to institutional
  buyers including [[beac|BEAC]].
- **…against foreign hyperscaler talks.** Simultaneously Camtel is in talks with **AWS** (2026-07-08, US
  Embassy present) and **[[avanti-communications]]** (satellite, 2026-04-09, UK High Commissioner present)
  on cloud/satellite/AI — sitting in direct tension with the sovereign-cloud line and the
  [[artac|ARTAC]] data-residency position. Sources:
  [[2026-07-08-camtel-aws-digital-transformation-talks-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-04-09-camtel-avanti-satellite-partnership-techafricanews]].
