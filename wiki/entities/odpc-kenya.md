# Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC), Kenya

Kenya's independent **data-protection regulator**, established under the **Data Protection
Act 2019** with an active adjudication process. It oversees cross-border transfers of
sensitive personal data — including the health-data localization rules central to the
[[us-kenya-health-framework]] dispute.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Health-data dispute (2025–26).** The ODPC's statutory framework — sensitive-data
  classification and cross-border-transfer safeguards under the Data Protection Act 2019,
  reinforced by the Digital Health Act 2023 — underpins the legal challenge that froze the
  [[us-kenya-health-framework]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[america-first-global-health-strategy]].
  Source: [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]].
- **Health-deal litigation moved (2026-06).** The [[consumer-federation-of-kenya]] (COFEK) petition
  invoking the Data Protection Act and Digital Health Act won a High Court suspension of the
  framework's data-transfer component; the **Court of Appeal reversed** it (case continuing). See
  [[us-kenya-health-framework]], [[gov.protect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-06-08 Kenyan Citizens Took A $2.5 Billion Deal With America To Court To Fight For Their Health Data]],
  [[2025-12-15 THE GATEKEEPERS PART V The New Maps of Africa]].
- **Draft National Data Governance Policy (May 2026).** Rose Mutiso reads Kenya's draft policy —
  behind the "Kenya wants to sell your data" headline — as a mostly plumbing/interoperability reform
  in which a data marketplace (see [[kenya-data-marketplace]]) is a small part; she flags **weak
  anonymisation**, non-consent given **mandatory eCitizen** use, vague benefit-sharing, and EU/German
  technical assistance whose own frameworks (GDPR, Data Governance Act) would bar the same
  revenue-maximising reuse at home. The policy would add a Data Governance Office alongside the ODPC.
  See [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-08 Kenya Wants to Sell Your Data. But It's Complicated]].
- **AI Bill / SHA proof-of-concept.** Munyua's "Gatekeepers XIV" (2026-05-19) reads the
  [[kenya-ai-bill-2026]] as a data-thin EU-AI-Act transplant and the Social Health Authority
  algorithm as already extracting from the poorest Kenyans — a live data-protection gap ahead of any
  AI Commissioner. See [[gov.legislate]]. Source: [[2026-05-19 GATEKEEPERS XIV]].
- **Worldcoin precedent.** The ODPC **suspended Worldcoin** operations in 2023 after it
  collected biometric data from ~**300,000** Kenyans via its Orb devices; in 2025 a Kenyan
  court found it breached the Data Protection Act and ordered **deletion** of all Kenyan data
  collected — a marker of enforcement teeth cited in the AFGHS analysis. See [[dpi.id]].
  Source: [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]].
- **Strategic plan (per 2025 policy review).** A pan-African year-in-review notes the ODPC's
  **2025–2029 Strategic Plan** prioritised cross-border-transfer guidance — a lead to verify
  against the ODPC's own publication before hardening. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-12 (3) Africa's Digital Policy Review 2025 in Perspective and Outlook for 2026]].
- **Biometrics & age-assurance guidance (2025-05).** [[gov.standards]] The ODPC issued **draft Guidance
  Notes** under the Data Protection Act — one on **processing children's data** (proportionate,
  privacy-preserving, risk-based **age verification**) and a substantive note on **biometric data**
  (mandatory registration of all biometric processors; data minimisation; adherence to **ISO/IEC
  39794-1/-4/-5** to avoid interoperability lock-in, with a compliance checklist). See [[dpi.id]].
  Source: [[2025-05-19-draft-guidance-biometrics-age-assurance-biometricupdate]].
- **Phone-number-as-identifier ruling (2026-03).** [[dpi.id]] A Milimani High Court judgement treated a
  **registered phone number as a protected personal digital identifier**, barred non-consensual
  number recycling, and directed the Attorney General to build a protective framework within six months —
  extending data-protection duties onto the telco/M-Pesa layer. See [[kenya--dpi-id]], [[safaricom]].
  Source: [[2026-03-23-high-court-phone-numbers-digital-id-biometricupdate]].
- **Personnel.** Former deputy data commissioner **Rose Mosero** (who helped build Kenya's
  data-protection regime and the Digital Health Act) co-authored the Carnegie analysis with
  [[jane-munga]].
