# Paula Ingabire

Rwanda's Minister of ICT and Innovation; drives Rwanda's positioning as a
continental AI/innovation hub.

## Activity in this wiki

- **National AI Agency (2026-06).** Associated with the Cabinet's creation of the
  **[[rwanda-national-ai-agency]]**, building on Rwanda's 2023 AI policy and AI
  Scaling Hub. See [[RWA]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-10 Rwanda Launches Dedicated AI Agency to Drive Investment and Innovation]].

- **EAC AI-sovereignty keynote (2026-03).** Keynote speaker at the 4th [[eac]] Regional STI
  Conference (Kigali), where the eight-member bloc explored a regional **AI Policy Roadmap** to
  act as a unified "AI sovereignty" bloc; argued "our data stays in our hands... the economic
  value generated by AI in East Africa accrues to East Africans." See [[XEA]], [[gov.regional]],
  [[tech.ai]]. Source: [[2026-03-31 EAC moves to create unified AI Sovereignty bloc]].

- **2G/3G phase-out (2026-03).** As MINICT minister, led talks with device importers on a
  gradual phase-out of 2G/3G devices to widen access to modern smartphones and advanced digital
  services (no firm timeline set). See [[RWA]], [[infra.connect]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 Rwanda Moves to Phase Out 2G and 3G Devices in Digital Transition Push]].

- **Anthropic–Rwanda MoU (2026-02).** As ICT & Innovation Minister, championed Rwanda's
  three-year (non-binding) MoU with [[anthropic]] ([[anthropic-rwanda-mou]]) embedding Claude
  across the health ministry, public-sector agencies and education — calling it "an important
  milestone in Rwanda's AI journey." Named-expert analysis flags an accountability vacuum: no
  parliamentary, multilateral or civil-society review of a deal making a private firm
  load-bearing infrastructure. See [[RWA]], [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-04 U.S.-based AI firm Anthropic bags first multi-sector government deal in Africa despite policy pressures at home]],
  [[2026-03-17 Anthropic is Becoming the Backbone of Rwanda's Government. But Who is Accountable]],
  [[2026-03-04 Rwanda–Anthropic AI deal targets health, education gains]].

- **National DPI Strategy launched (2026-03-09).** At Rwanda's first **DPI Day** (organised
  by the **[[rwanda-information-society-authority]]** with Access to Finance Rwanda), the
  government unveiled a national **[[rwanda-dpi-strategy]]** — building on the Rwanda Centre
  for Digital Public Infrastructure (est. 2025), with a digital-ID rollout planned by **June
  2026** and the interoperable **e-Kash** payment platform, positioning Rwanda as a regional
  AI hub via stronger data exchange. See [[RWA]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-20 Rwanda Launches Digital Public Infrastructure Strategy to Lay Groundwork for AI and Inclusive Growth]].

- **Horizon1000 endorsement (2026-01).** Endorsed the **$50m** Gates Foundation–OpenAI
  **[[horizon1000]]** health-AI pilot, which begins in **Rwanda** and scales to 1,000
  primary-care clinics by 2028 — stressing "responsible deployment" with people at the centre.
  See [[RWA]], [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.mis]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-22 Gates Foundation and OpenAI Commit $50m to AI-Powered Health Systems in Africa]].

See [[RWA]], [[paul-kagame]], [[tech.ai]].
