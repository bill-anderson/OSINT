# Nick Opiyo

**A Ugandan human rights lawyer**, detained in **December 2020**. He **believes there was
an ulterior motive** to that imprisonment, having "become ensnared in a surveillance
dragnet" — enduring arbitrary detention and harassment for exposing state-backed human
rights transgressions.

## Precision — what the held source does and does not show

The clip this wiki holds is **Biometric Update reporting on a Bloomberg / Lighthouse
Reports investigation**; **that investigation is the primary and is not held**
(GAP-UGA-002).

**It does not document which ID records were pulled on Opiyo.** What it establishes is the
**mechanism**: the investigation *"unveils how the Museveni regime has built the system to
consolidate power, as underlying software and datasets are easily accessed by individuals
at all levels of law enforcement."*

**The causal link from his ID record to his arrest is asserted by Opiyo and implied by the
investigation — it is not shown. Do not assert it.**

**[[rosemary-kisembo]], NIRA Executive Director, refuted on the record** (in comments to
Bloomberg) that law enforcement could readily access the database.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The case as reported (2024-06-05).** Opiyo's arrest is used to frame Uganda's
  national ID card — introduced by [[nira-uganda]] nearly a decade earlier and touted as
  streamlining administration and citizen services — as an instrument of surveillance and
  government control. The piece pairs it with a **2023 [[acme-uganda]] study** finding
  that biometric and digital identity programmes gave room for surveillance and intrusion
  on journalism, with journalists targeted. See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.id]],
  [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2024-06-05 Ugandan human rights lawyer's arrest exposes use of national ID for surveillance]].
