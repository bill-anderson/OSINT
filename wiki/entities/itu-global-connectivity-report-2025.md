# ITU Global Connectivity Report 2025

The ITU Telecommunication Development Bureau's flagship "Global Connectivity Report 2025" — its authoritative assessment of global progress toward Universal and Meaningful Connectivity (UMC). It defines the UMC statistical framework reused by national studies (e.g. [[sadis-2025]]), and is a standing reference in this wiki: a dated fact drawn from it becomes a normal source citing this instrument.

## Summary
- Assesses global progress toward Universal and Meaningful Connectivity (UMC).
- Covers usage and the "many divides" — gender, urban/rural, age, income.
- Covers infrastructure (submarine cables, satellites), affordability, digital skills, and national data ecosystems for measuring UMC.
- Africa is consistently the least-connected region in its data.
- Defines the UMC statistical framework reused by national studies.
- Reference document: `2026-07-10 Global Connectivity Report 2025.pdf` (in `raw/`).
