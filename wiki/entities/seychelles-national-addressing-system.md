# Seychelles National Addressing and Postcode System

**Seychelles' national addressing and postcode programme** — a Cabinet-approved initiative led by
the [[seychelles-postal-regulator]] to structure, standardise and manage address data across the
country in line with international standards. It is a **foundational registry** play as much as a
postal one: the stated benefits run to service delivery, location mapping, urban planning,
emergency response and **national statistics**. See [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.standards]], [[SYC]].

> **Dating caveat — read every status statement below as undated.** The sole source is an
> **undated government portal page**, filed `published: 2025-01-01` with `date_precision: year`
> and `date_source: proxy`. The 2025 date is a bare-year, in-window estimate, not a publication
> date. The programme's status **may have moved since** and no as-of date can be relied on.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Cabinet approval to implement.** Postal Regulator Services received Cabinet approval to
  implement the system, in collaboration with Seychelles Postal Services, the **Ministry of Lands
  and Housing**, the **National Bureau of Statistics**, **[[dict-seychelles]]**, the **Attorney
  General's Office**, the **Land Transport Division** and the **Local Government Department**.
  *(Approval reported on an undated page proxy-dated 2025; no decision date is held.)* Source:
  [[2025-01-01 National Addressing System - Seychelles Postal Regulator]].
- **S42 address standard endorsed.** The Cabinet endorsed adoption of the **S42** address
  standard — a globally recognised format for uniformity and precision in postal addressing.
  Adopting an international address standard rather than a bespoke national scheme is a
  [[gov.standards]] choice with downstream interoperability consequences. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 National Addressing System - Seychelles Postal Regulator]].
- **Legal framework still being drafted — status undated and likely stale.** As described on the
  undated portal page (proxy-dated 2025), the **legal framework was still being developed** by
  the technical committee in consultation with the Attorney General's Office, and the **draft
  legislation had not yet been submitted to Cabinet**. The page calls the legal backing
  "essential to ensure consistency and authority in the implementation process" — i.e. by its own
  account the system was, at that point, **approved but not yet legally underpinned**. Given the
  year-precision proxy date, treat this as a **historical snapshot of unknown vintage**, not
  current state. See [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 National Addressing System - Seychelles Postal Regulator]].
- **Intended uses.** More efficient service delivery, accurate location mapping, urban planning,
  emergency response, and improved national statistics — linking the address registry to
  [[data.statistics]] and [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 National Addressing System - Seychelles Postal Regulator]].
