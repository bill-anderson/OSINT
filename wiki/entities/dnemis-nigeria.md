# DNEMIS (Nigeria national education MIS)

DNEMIS is Nigeria's national **education management information system**, built on the
[[dhis2]] platform and owned by the [[federal-ministry-education-nigeria]]. At launch it was
described as the **world's largest DHIS2 implementation** — and a notable pivot of DHIS2 from
its health-sector origins into education ([[dpi.mis]]).

## Key facts

- **Launched ~2026-07-05** at an event in Abuja. Partners: [[hisp-centre]] (University of
  Oslo) and its DHIS2 core team, HISP Nigeria (local partner), UNICEF Nigeria and the
  [[european-union]]. HISP Centre Deputy Director Ola Hodne Titlestad framed the launch as
  "just the beginning" of a long-term collaboration.
- Purpose: data-driven resource allocation and decision-making across all levels of Nigeria's
  education system, with capacity and ownership built at every level.
- **Data-sovereignty framing.** Minister Maruf Tunji Alausa tied DNEMIS explicitly to data
  being **domiciled in Nigeria** — "data sovereignty is what it's called" — and positioned
  Nigeria as a model for other countries' education systems. See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[sovereignty]].
