# Amazon Web Services

AWS is Amazon's cloud arm and the global cloud market leader (~31% global share per competition analyses). It matters here both as an infrastructure provider landing on African soil and as the reference point for debates over who controls where public data sits.

## Activity in this wiki
- Launched AWS Outposts in Rwanda (2026-04), the first in the region and third on the African continent, delivered with [[sand-technologies]] — see the deal [[aws-outposts-rwanda]].
- Outposts is a hybrid-cloud offering placing local compute and storage tied back to AWS regions, aimed at latency- and data-residency-sensitive workloads.
- Appears as the counter-example in European "digital sovereignty" moves: Finland shelved a plan to move election data to AWS, and DNS Belgium left AWS (per the WIRED piece).
- **Africa footprint (profile).** Runs the **Cape Town Region** (ZAF), holds B-BBEE Level 1 status, and launched **AWS re/Start** cloud-skilling cohorts in South Africa, Nigeria and Ghana (Oct 2020); customers include Absa, Investec, Old Mutual, Standard Bank, Pick n Pay. Source: [[2026-07-11 AWS in Africa]].
- **OpenAI on Bedrock (2026-04-28, global context).** OpenAI's Microsoft exclusivity ended, letting AWS offer OpenAI models/Codex on **Amazon Bedrock** — "the beginning of a deeper collaboration," per AWS. See [[openai]], [[microsoft]]. Source: [[2026-04-28 OpenAI Breaks Free Of Microsoft, Expands AWS Partnership]].
- **Nigeria onshoring pressure (2026-07-10).** AWS named among the foreign hyperscalers Nigerian banks/fintechs must migrate off under the [[cbn]] local-hosting mandate (transactional data onshore by 2027-01-01); New America's critique notes hyperscaler-owned facilities mean "sovereignty" data still sits on rented infrastructure. See [[NGA]], [[gov.protect]]. Sources: [[2026-07-10 Nigeria tests data sovereignty as foreign tech firms facing probe]], [[2026-05-21 Africa Data Center Investment 2026 The $4.5B Race and the Power Problem]]. Anjola Adebowale's close reading of the CBN circular (2026-07-01) makes the dependency concrete: Nigerian financial institutions today host on **AWS Cape Town** (and Azure Johannesburg), so the 1 Jan 2027 localisation deadline outruns Nigeria's own ~75 MW / 26-facility data-centre capacity. Source: [[2026-07-01 Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part I Inside the CBN's June 2026 Payments Circular]].
- **Amazon–Globalstar / Project Kuiper (2026-04-14).** Parent **Amazon** is in advanced talks to buy satellite operator **[[globalstar]]** for **~$9bn** to accelerate its **Project Kuiper** (rebranded "Leo") LEO constellation against [[starlink]] — deal [[amazon-globalstar-acquisition]]; Globalstar's existing spectrum/partnerships in ZAF, RWA, MOZ and GAB would be a regulatory shortcut into African markets. Kuiper had ~180 satellites in orbit vs Starlink's 7,000+ (not yet finalised as of 2026-04-14; Apple holds 20% of Globalstar). See [[infra.connect]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source: [[2026-04-14 Amazon's $9bn satellite deal set to benefit Africa]].
- **Overland fibre + in-country cloud (2026-03/04).** Amazon — with [[google]], [[meta]] and [[microsoft]] — is building ultra-high-speed **overland fibre** across Africa's interior (beyond subsea); in Ethiopia, [[wingu-africa]]'s new **Wingu Cloud Exchange** offers local data residency running hybrid with **AWS**/Azure. Amazon is also among the tech firms whose big-model dominance drives the "frugal AI" counter-movement (>90% of AI data centres are US/Chinese-run). See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.store]], [[ETH]]. Sources: [[2026-03-16 Why Google, Amazon and Meta are going overland in Africa]], [[2026-04-01 Wingu Africa debuts cloud exchange platform in Ethiopia]], [[2026-04-02 Nations priced out of Big AI are building with frugal models]].
- **AWS Wavelength via Orange, Morocco & Senegal (backfill, 2024-05).** AWS and [[orange]] Middle East & Africa brought **AWS Wavelength Zones** to Morocco and Senegal — the **first in Africa** and the first in countries **without an AWS Region** — placing AWS compute/storage inside Orange data centres for in-country **data residency** and low latency across regulated sectors (telecom, finance, public sector), with Orange as anchor customer. See [[MAR]], [[SEN]], [[infra.store]], [[infra.connect]]. Source: [[2024-05-30 Orange and AWS partner to deliver cloud services in Morocco and Senegal]].

- **MyMzansi DPI build partner (2025-11, [[ZAF]]).** AWS was a key technical partner (DPI global
  lead Pete Herlihy) as South Africa's Presidency **[[digital-services-unit-presidency-sa]]**
  built the **[[mymzansi]]** digital-government prototype in 10 weeks on an open-source
  digital-public-goods stack — a hyperscaler embedded in a state's "African-led" DPI build. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-09 Behind the speedy MyMzansi screens and the Presidency's race to build a digital government]].
