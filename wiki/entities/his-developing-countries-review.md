# Health information systems in developing countries: case of African countries

Peer-reviewed **literature review** by **Koumamba, Bisvigou, Ngoungou and Diallo** (Gabon /
[[GAB]]-based authorship), *BMC Medical Informatics and Decision Making* **21:232 (2021)**.
Screened **2,492 articles**, retaining **14**, covering health information systems (HIS) in
**12 African countries**. The **anchor reference** for the wiki's HMIS/[[dhis2]] evidence
cluster ([[dpi.mis]]); cited here as literature, with later dated facts drawn from it
becoming sources citing this page.

## Argument

- **All 12** countries reviewed adopted a **data-warehouse approach supported by
  [[dhis2]]** — evidence of DHIS2's near-universal role as the routine-HIS backbone across
  sub-Saharan Africa ([[XSS]]).
- **11 of the 12 lacked a national HIS strategy**, instead aligning their systems to
  **donor strategies** — a structural dependence with clear [[sovereignty]] implications
  (cf. [[hmis-tanzania-pea]] on donor influence). **Malawi** was the single exception with
  its own national strategy.
- Diagnoses weak standardisation ([[gov.standards]]), fragmented data flows, and thin local
  research/analytic capacity ([[capacity.research]]) as recurring constraints.
