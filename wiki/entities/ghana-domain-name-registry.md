# Ghana Domain Name Registry (GDNR)

The body that manages Ghana's `.gh` country-code top-level domain (ccTLD). The `.gh` root
server has been managed privately since 1996 by internet pioneer Professor Nii Narku
Quaynor; the registry is framed by government as controlling "our national identity on the
internet."

## Activity in this wiki

- **Government moves to nationalise `.GH` (2026-01-22).** Communications, Digital Technology
  and Innovation Minister [[samuel-nartey-george]] announced plans to bring the `.gh`
  domain fully under state ownership, transitioning the root server out of private custody,
  and appealed to Vice President Jane Naana Opoku-Agyemang to champion the transition. He
  also pushed for Ghanaian firms to register `.com.gh` domains — engaging the Registrar of
  Companies to auto-assign them to newly registered businesses — casting `.com.gh` as a
  "national digital identity" versus foreign-looking `.com`. Framed explicitly as digital
  sovereignty over online infrastructure and national data. Tags [[gov.policy]],
  [[dpi.id]], [[infra.connect]]. Source: [[2026-01-22 Ghana to take state control of .GH domain to strengthen digital identity]].
