# CBN Policy Insight Series 2025 — Shaping the Future of Fintech in Nigeria

A [[cbn]] fintech policy framework, subtitled *"Innovation, Inclusion and Integrity,"*
drawing on a nationwide survey, industry workshop and high-level roundtable. It sets out an
18-month roadmap organised around three pillars — innovation-friendly regulation, digital
infrastructure for inclusion, and system integrity — and is read regionally as a blueprint
for ECOWAS/AfCFTA financial-services integration, not merely a domestic reform agenda.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Framework released; regional-integration test (2026-02).** Proposes a **Single
  Regulatory Window**, a standing Fintech Engagement Forum, and Compliance-as-a-Service
  utilities; explicit **regulatory passporting** via bilateral pilots with [[GHA]], [[KEN]],
  [[SEN]] and [[ZAF]] for mutual recognition of fintech licences; a **Responsible AI in
  Finance Hub**; and a Fintech Credit Guarantee Window plus secondary markets for fintech
  debt. Cites survey data: 87.5% of Nigerian fintechs say compliance costs materially
  constrain innovation; 87.5% already use AI for fraud detection; 62.5% operate in or plan
  to expand to other African markets. Framed as Nigeria proposing the infrastructure for
  cross-border ECOWAS fintech ([[ecowas]], [[afcfta]]). Tags [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.policy]],
  [[gov.standards]], [[tech.ai]]. Sources: [[2026-02-06 Nigeria just raised the bar for West African Fintech]],
  [[2026-02-05 Nigeria's fintech blueprint raises stakes for Kenya, Africa's digital economy]]
  (Kenya-angle read of the same framework; puts the compliance-as-innovation-constraint figure at 88%).
