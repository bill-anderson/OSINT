# Mathesis Analytics

Nigerian credit-analytics startup selling AI-based creditworthiness assessment to banks, aimed at
borrowers who lack conventional bank records.

## The method — alternative data as the credit file

Mathesis's system scores **people without bank accounts** by analysing their **payment patterns across
mobile apps** rather than a formal credit history. This is the alternative-data credit-scoring pattern
seen across African lending markets: the thin-file problem is answered by treating transactional
behaviour as the file. It makes credit reachable for the unbanked and simultaneously converts everyday
payment traces into an underwriting input — a data-governance question (consent, purpose limitation,
contestability of an automated decision) the held source does not address.

- **Funding raised from [[first-ally-capital]]** to expand AI capability, announced **2026-07-17**

- The company says its system has **supported eight million loans in Nigeria** (as of 2026-07-17).
  This is a company figure reported without a start date, so it is a cumulative total of unknown
  period; loan *value*, default performance and the number of distinct borrowers are unknown.
