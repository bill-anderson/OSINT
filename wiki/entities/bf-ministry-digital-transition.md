# Burkina Faso Ministry of Digital Transition, Posts and Electronic Communications

Burkina Faso's lead ministry for digital transformation, headed by Minister
Aminata Zerbo Sabané (Dr Aminata Zerbo-Sabané). Under a military government
re-asserting national sovereignty across domains, it frames digital policy around
data localization and infrastructure independence. Burkina Faso is part of the
[[alliance-of-sahel-states]] (AES) trio that broke away from [[ecowas]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **PACTDIGITAL-BF flagship programme (in force 2024-09).** The ministry runs
  Fast-Tracking Digital Transformation (PACTDIGITAL-BF), a five-year strategy for
  12 major digital projects by 2030, funded ~$170m (96.7bn CFA) via a
  [[world-bank]] IDA loan plus national counterpart. Five components:
  connectivity, public-service transformation, skills, innovation and emergency
  response ([[gov.policy]], [[dpi.govtech]]). Source: [[2026-03-23 Burkina Faso steps into the future with digital investment]].
- **Two Ouagadougou mini data centres commissioned (2026-01).** An intermediate
  step toward a planned national data centre, supporting the "zero data abroad"
  localization policy under which sensitive national data is hosted locally
  ([[infra.store]]). Source: [[2026-03-23 Burkina Faso steps into the future with digital investment]].
- **Digital Infrastructure Supervision Center launched (2026-01-22).** A ~$5.4m
  facility (nine-month build, delivery expected ~October 2026) to centralise
  supervision of the national backbone, secure cyberspace and oversee data-centre
  operations — pitched explicitly as a tool for "sovereign management" of national
  digital systems ([[infra.store]], [[infra.cybersec]]). Source: [[2026-01-22 Burkina Faso aims for digital sovereignty with infrastructure supervision center]]. Fuller build-out on **[[bf-national-datacenters]]**.
- **Traoré's sovereignty framing (Semaine du numérique 2025, Nov).** [[gov.policy]] At the 20th Digital
  Week (Ghana guest of honour; Mali/Niger special guests), President Ibrahim Traoré framed digital as
  inseparable from sovereignty — objecting to paying software licences "to those who are fighting us" — with
  datacentres "en cours d'acquisition" and a new polytechnic academy. The **twelve national chantiers**
  underpin the 2030 vision. Sources: [[2025-11-18-traore-souverainete-integre-numerique]],
  [[2025-11-11-transformation-digitale-burkina-douze-chantiers-avenir-inclusif]].
- **A governance decree + national-hosting doctrine (2024–26).** [[gov.legislate]] DG **Teg-Wendé Idriss
  Tinto** set out the decree fixing operation of state digital platforms (signed May 2024, widely reported
  2026) to end siloed, duplicated ministry platforms and mandate interoperability; national hosting runs
  on a **PM circular**, with health data needing a derogation to leave and a **CIL declaration** a
  precondition. A **Manual of Procedures for IS Management** (validated Sept 2025) closed a 20-year
  regulatory gap. Source: [[2026-03-03-tinto-hebergement-national-souverainete]].
- **Delivery scorecards (2025).** [[dpi.govtech]] The ministry reported **43.61%** performance at
  mid-2025, then **~85–91%** for the full year — but the **unique electronic ID stalled on a failed
  tender**, with vendor procurement relaunched, and 2026 targets include **7m eID enrolments**. Source:
  [[2026-03-24-transformation-digitale-bilan-2025-85-pourcent]].
