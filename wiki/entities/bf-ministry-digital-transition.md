# Burkina Faso Ministry of Digital Transition, Posts and Electronic Communications

Burkina Faso's lead ministry for digital transformation, headed by Minister
Aminata Zerbo Sabané (Dr Aminata Zerbo-Sabané). Under a military government
re-asserting national sovereignty across domains, it frames digital policy around
data localization and infrastructure independence. Burkina Faso is part of the
[[alliance-of-sahel-states]] (AES) trio that broke away from [[ecowas]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **PACTDIGITAL-BF flagship programme (in force 2024-09).** The ministry runs
  Fast-Tracking Digital Transformation (PACTDIGITAL-BF), a five-year strategy for
  12 major digital projects by 2030, funded ~$170m (96.7bn CFA) via a
  [[world-bank]] IDA loan plus national counterpart. Five components:
  connectivity, public-service transformation, skills, innovation and emergency
  response ([[gov.policy]], [[dpi.govtech]]). Source: [[2026-03-23 Burkina Faso steps into the future with digital investment]].
- **Two Ouagadougou mini data centres commissioned (2026-01).** An intermediate
  step toward a planned national data centre, supporting the "zero data abroad"
  localization policy under which sensitive national data is hosted locally
  ([[infra.store]]). Source: [[2026-03-23 Burkina Faso steps into the future with digital investment]].
- **Digital Infrastructure Supervision Center launched (2026-01-22).** A ~$5.4m
  facility (nine-month build, delivery expected ~October 2026) to centralise
  supervision of the national backbone, secure cyberspace and oversee data-centre
  operations — pitched explicitly as a tool for "sovereign management" of national
  digital systems ([[infra.store]], [[infra.cybersec]]). Source: [[2026-01-22 Burkina Faso aims for digital sovereignty with infrastructure supervision center]].
