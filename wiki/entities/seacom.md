# Seacom

African subsea-cable operator (original Seacom cable launched 2009 with Tata
Communications, Egypt–Red Sea–South Africa/India). Owns east-coast landing stations and
West-African terrestrial fibre.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Seacom 2.0 (announced 2025-09-24, Submarine Networks World, Singapore).** A **25,000
  km, 48-fibre-pair** system with **20 landing points across 15 countries** — Marseille
  through the Med and Red Sea, branching west to Singapore (via India/Pakistan) and south
  to South Africa then up to West Africa. Pitched for **low-latency AI workloads** across
  the Indian Ocean Basin (2.9bn people, 33 nations), landing stations reframed as "AI
  communication nodes… linking African nations' sovereign AI infrastructure to global
  data hubs," with diversified, carrier-neutral routes for resilience after recent cable
  cuts. Group CEO **Alpheus Mangale**: "ensuring Africa and its neighbours control their
  digital destinies." See [[infra.connect]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-24 Unveiling Seacom 2.0 - ​ ​A new digital renaissance and subsea network to power the Indian Ocean Basin's digital and AI future]].
- **Seacom 2.0 phasing & economics (ATF 2025 interview, Padayachee).** Group chief
  digital infrastructure officer **Prenesh Padayachee** set the rationale — exponential
  AI, cloud and real-time-data demand — and the numbers: route across the Indian Ocean
  Basin, Middle East, Mediterranean and Southern Europe; **phase one complete by early
  2030**; the **original 2009 cable retired in 2035**; construction cost **$1.5bn–$2bn**,
  funded by existing shareholders plus new investors. Stressed **data-centre neutrality**
  and possible expansion into terrestrial fibre. Source:
  [[2025-11-01 Seacom's Prenesh Padayachee on the 'Seacom 2.0' subsea cable]].
- **Nairobi–Kampala terrestrial route (launched 2026-06-17).** A new high-capacity
  inland backbone (Nairobi–Kisumu–Kampala) carrying traffic from the Mombasa subsea
  landings — **1 Tbps, scalable to 30 Tbps**, dual-path with **sub-50ms ASON
  auto-reroute**, two border crossings (Malaba, Busia), extending toward Rwanda, Burundi
  and South Sudan. A **distinct** development from Seacom 2.0 (subsea). CTO David Kariuki.
  Seacom present in eight countries. See [[XEA]], [[infra.connect]], [[infra.capacity]].
  Sources: [[2026-06-17 Seacom deploys a new high-capacity Nairobi–Kampala route to strengthen East Africa's digital backbone]],
  [[2026-06-18 Seacom boosts East Africa connectivity]].
- **Submarine-cable factsheet (2026-07).** Featured in the Data Landscapers factsheet
  (incl. **SEACOM 2.0**) documenting the shift from telco-consortium to hyperscaler-owned
  subsea cables. See [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-03 Submarine cables serving Africa]].
- **Investors:** Industrial Promotion Services (Aga Khan Fund for Economic Development),
  Remgro, Solcon Capital, Sanlam.

- **TENET low-cost university bandwidth (historical; Russell Southwood, 2026-04-16).**
  In Southwood's ICT4D retrospective, the original 2009 Seacom cable's deal with
  **TENET** (South Africa's national research/education network) is cited as a
  foundational success — it delivered dramatically cheaper international bandwidth
  to universities and NRENs, part of the 2000s international-fibre financing wave
  that reshaped African access terms. See [[infra.connect]], [[capacity.research]].
  Source: [[2026-04-16 The Long View - What ICT4D did for Africa and why that deep past matters for today]].
