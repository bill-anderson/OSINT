# IN Groupe

French identity-services company (formerly Imprimerie Nationale), a supplier of national ID documents
and digital-identity systems across Francophone Africa.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Gabon secure-titles platform (2013, extended 2015).** Imprimerie Nationale delivered its **ID
  Platform** secure-title issuance and management system to the Gabonese state in **2013** (first
  contacts **2010**), and in **November 2015** signed a **five-year partnership with the DGDI** covering
  **maintenance of that platform plus extension of the main secure-titles supply contract** — signed by
  Interior Minister Pacôme Moubelet Boubeya and CEO Didier Trutt. Published scope: **e-passports, visas,
  residence permits and refugee cards**; **the national identity card was not named**. The stated aim was
  **technology transfer**, so that Gabon would itself become a secure-title production platform. Source:
  [[2015-11-25-in-groupe-gabon-dgdi-secure-titles-partnership]].
- **Gabon CNIE supply (from June 2024) — secondary-sourced only.** IN Groupe is credited with supplying
  the **physical CNIE card and the digital-ID management software/hardware** (EVP **Yann Haguet**), the
  flagship legal-identity component of the [[digital-gabon-project]]. **No primary award has been found**
  (checked 2026-07-20), and Digital Business Africa reported (2024-06-27) that the **DGDI itself produces
  the CNIE** — consistent with the 2015 technology-transfer aim, but *platform supplier* and *producer of
  record* are different claims the sources do not settle. Source:
  [[2024-09-25-gabon-digital-id-forefront-transformation]].

**Disambiguation (resolved 2026-07-20).** IN Groupe holds the **credential** layer; **[[thales]]** holds
the **population registry** (IBOGA), inherited from Gemalto. The two are routinely confused because the
companies **traded identity assets in both directions** — IN Groupe acquired **Thales's** Identity &
Biometrics arm (effective **9 May 2017**), and Thales acquired **Gemalto** (2019). Full treatment:
[[gabon--dpi-id]].

A French vendor at the core of a sovereign national-ID build invites the [[colonialism]] /
[[sovereignty]] reading also flagged on Gabon's [[gabon--dpi-id]] story — the ID stack is
domestically framed but foreign-supplied on both layers.
