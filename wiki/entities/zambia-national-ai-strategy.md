# Zambia National AI Strategy

Zambia's forthcoming national artificial-intelligence strategy, led by the
**[[ministry-of-technology-and-science-zambia|Ministry of Technology and Science]]**.

**Status: nearing completion, not published (as of 2026-07-17).** Kusobile Kamwambi, head of the
**[[presidential-delivery-unit-zambia|Presidential Delivery Unit]]**, said the strategy is
approaching completion and invited stakeholders to contribute to it. A ministry representative
separately reaffirmed the government's commitment to advancing AI "through the forthcoming
National AI Strategy and **Startup Bill**". **No publication date, draft text, budget,
institutional home or governance architecture is on file** — the wiki holds the announcement of
its imminence and nothing of its content.

## What sits around it

- **A twin legislative track.** The strategy is named alongside a **Startup Bill** — the first
  mention of that bill in the wiki. No text, sponsor or timetable held. See [[gov.legislate]],
  [[tech.innovate]].
- **A proposed ITU AI for Good Lab.** The ministry disclosed an opportunity to establish
  **Zambia's first AI for Good Lab in partnership with the [[itu]]**, under the United Nations
  framework, expected to support AI policy development, innovation, skills and digital
  infrastructure. **An opportunity, not an agreement** — no signature, funding or siting is
  reported. See [[zambia--tech-ai]].
- **Regional context.** Zambia hosted the **COMESA regional-AI workshop (2026-07-14,
  Lusaka)** — [[comesa]] and the [[world-bank]] under [[idea-mpa]] — a first step toward a COMESA
  Regional AI Strategy, so the national instrument is being drafted against a parallel regional
  one. See [[gov.regional]].
- **The infrastructure side, already contracted.** SMART Zambia's **June-2026 [[huawei]] MoU**
  ([[national-ai-data-centre-zambia]]) commits AI infrastructure, sovereign cloud and a
  co-invested national data centre. The strategy is therefore arriving **after** the compute
  arrangements it would ordinarily govern — the sequencing point the wiki records for Zambia
  generally.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: a national AI strategy drafted while the national AI data centre is already the
  subject of a vendor MoU, and while the flagship AI institution on offer comes through a UN
  agency, is a policy instrument being written around commitments rather than ahead of them.
  Recorded as sequencing, **not** as a stated intent of any party.
