# ANATS (Agence Nationale des Titres Sécurisés)

**Chad's secure-documents agency** — the operator of the [[chad-biometric-registry]] and of the
country's civil registration reform. **Note the alias:** Biometric Update renders ANATS as the
*National Agency for Secure Documents* (2026-03-16) and as the *National Agency for Secure Titles*
(2025-06-03). Same body; translation variance on *titres sécurisés*. Director General:
**Youssouf Gorou Tchaby**. Coordinator of Cooperation and Civil Registration: **Brahim Ali Seid**.

ANATS sits at the junction of two agendas that the sources treat separately: making the biometric
registry the **single source of truth** for identification across government, and closing Chad's
birth-registration gap through health facilities, schools and mobile units.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Ministry of Finance interoperability deal (March 2026).** ANATS concluded an agreement in
  N'Djamena with Chad's Ministry of Finance, Budget, Economy, Planning and International
  Cooperation to enable **real-time data sharing** off the national biometric database —
  targeting transparency in administrative and financial management, streamlined identity
  verification, and fraud/identity-theft prevention. Tchaby: *"This system, designed for
  interoperability, is an essential lever. All administrations, both public and private, will be
  able to connect to it to verify and authenticate identity."* Saleh Abakar Zene, deputy DG for
  Budget and Digitalization at Finance, called the push to fully digital government one *"built
  through concrete decisions, useful partnerships, courageous reforms, and coherent actions."*
  See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].
- **Registry investment — attributed, not asserted.** *Africa 24 TV, quoting government sources*,
  reports Chad has invested an estimated **XAF 15 billion (~US$26.1 million)** over recent years
  building and strengthening the biometric registry, including production of secure identity
  documents and upgrades to basic digital infrastructure (as reported 2026-03-16). This figure is
  relayed second-hand through Biometric Update and carries no primary provenance in the sources
  held. See [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].
- **Civil registration: the colonial inheritance, named.** At the ID4Africa 2025 AGM
  ([[id4africa]]), Brahim Ali Seid gave an explicitly decolonial account of the reform:
  *"We inherited a colonial civil registration system governed by colonial laws. But progress has
  been made. Starting from the 1990s, we adopted new legal texts that allowed our civil registry
  system to evolve. Between 1990 and 2010, and later in 2020, new legislation was introduced,
  including a key ordinance that modernized the system and laid the groundwork for
  digitalization."* Chad's framework now supports universal, free and mandatory birth
  registration. See [[colonialism]], [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]].
- **Health-facility integration (as of 2025-06-03).** Since integrating civil registration with
  health facilities in **2023**, more than **120,000 children** have been registered through
  hospital-based registry offices, with community health workers acting as intermediaries to
  record births in rural areas — covering about **70% of Chad's rural population**. See
  [[digital.rural]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]].
- **School campaigns (as of 2025-06-03).** More than **180,000 pupils and students** without birth
  certificates have been registered through school campaigns since **2023**. Seid: *"Over 80
  percent of children in primary and secondary schools lack birth certificates. As part of a
  national education acceleration plan, these students are allowed to enroll without documents,
  but we've run regularization campaigns since 2023 to register them."* Services have been
  extended to nomadic populations via mobile phones running the ANATS application, plus biometric
  registration missions for older individuals. See [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]].
- **The SMS-backed architecture.** Seid: *"Our system connects civil registry offices, hospitals,
  vaccination centers, municipalities, mobile units, refugee camps, and schools. All data is
  transferred via SMS to our national agency, which validates the information and sends it back
  for issuance."* See [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]].
- **Targets (as stated 2025-06-03).** Digitised hospital-based civil registration points to rise
  from **97 to 600** in the next few years; school registration to continue; **zero unregistered
  births targeted by 2030**. A [[world-bank]]-sponsored project sought to identify the factors
  hampering civil registration in Chad. Source:
  [[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]].
- **Refugee and inclusive ID.** Chad issues **biometric ID cards to refugees** using [[idemia]]
  biometrics technology, and ANATS recently received **biometric enrolment equipment from
  [[unhcr]]** to support its inclusive-ID push (as reported 2026-03-16). See [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].
