**SpaceX** is the US space and satellite-communications company founded by South-African-born billionaire **Elon Musk**, and the parent of the low-Earth-orbit (LEO) broadband service [[starlink]]. In the OSINT record here SpaceX/Starlink is notable for a sustained lobbying and advocacy push to obtain an operating **licence in South Africa**, which has become a flashpoint over Black economic empowerment and digital sovereignty.

## The South Africa licensing standoff

Starlink cannot currently apply for South African electronic-communications licences because the country's rules — under the **Broad-Based Black Economic Empowerment (B-BBEE)** framework and section 9(2)(b) of the **Electronic Communications Act** — require licensed operators to have at least **30% ownership by historically disadvantaged South Africans**. Key developments (as of the 2026-02-18 report):

- **May 2025** — Department of Communications and Digital Technologies minister **Solly Malatsi** issued a draft policy directive proposing that firms like Starlink meet local requirements via **Equity Equivalent Investment Programmes (EEIPs)** rather than local equity ownership.
- **June 2025** — Starlink committed **R500 million (US$31 million)** to provide free high-speed internet to 5,000 rural schools, claimed to reach 2.4 million learners annually.
- **October 2025** — SpaceX confirmed it planned to **comply with B-BBEE** and pledged to invest a total of **R2.5 billion** (about $145.6 million at the time) in South Africa.
- **12 December 2025** — Malatsi issued the formal **policy direction to [[icasa]]** via Government Gazette, urging alignment of regulations with the ICT Sector Code; ICASA said it would consider it through established processes.

## Advocacy campaign and pushback

In **January 2026** SpaceX/Starlink emailed its South African customer database urging recipients to send pre-written messages pressuring [[icasa]]. It followed this with an online **"Starlink's Efforts in South Africa"** advocacy page addressing critics' concerns about Black economic empowerment, national security, monopoly risk and local commitment. The campaign drew sharp political pushback: **Khusela Sangoni Diko**, chair of the parliamentary Portfolio Committee on Communications and Digital Technologies, called for the immediate withdrawal of Malatsi's policy direction, branding it "an affront to the centuries-old fight for equity and redress by the Black majority" and an attempt to circumvent the mandatory 30% ownership rule — the core of the sovereignty framing here. SpaceX/Starlink has meanwhile expanded rapidly across the continent (live in more than 20 African markets, from Nigeria since January 2023 to Senegal in early 2026).

## Telco partnerships

Beyond the SA standoff, SpaceX has pivoted from competitor to wholesale supplier via two
continent-scale [[starlink]] tie-ups (Jan 2026): the **[[starlink-airtel-d2c-partnership]]**
(Direct-to-Cell + backhaul across [[airtel-africa]]'s 14 markets; SpaceX already licensed in
9 of 14 by May 2025) and the **[[starlink-vodacom-leo-partnership]]** ([[vodacom]] LEO
backhaul + resale). See [[infra.connect]], [[data.satellite]]. Source:
[[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]].
