# Telecom Egypt

Egypt's **state-owned integrated telecom operator** (CEO **Mohamed Nasr**), central to the country's role as a **submarine-cable and data-centre hub**. Operates the **Regional Data Hub** data centres — **RDH 1** (2.5 MW) and the Tier-III-certified **RDH 2** (4.6 MW) — leveraging the subsea cable systems landing on Egypt's eastern coast (part of ~15 active international systems, Africa's largest concentration). One of the four operators in Egypt's June-2025 5G rollout; secured its **5G licence Jan 2024 ($150m)** and shared the **$3.5bn / +410MHz spectrum deal** (2026-02).

- **Cables:** activated **2Africa Mediterranean** capacity with **Cisco** (2025-01).
- **Fibre:** launched Egypt's first **[[huawei]] FTTR** (fibre-to-the-room, 2026-05); laid **~5m fibre lines across 1,250+ villages** under the "Decent Life" (Hayat Karima) rural initiative. See [[egypt--infra-connect]].
- **Data-centre ownership (2026-07-17):** the board **cancelled** the sale of a **75–80%** stake in the unit owning the **[[regional-data-center-hub]]** to **[[helios-investment-partners]]** — an agreement announced **2025-09-07** — on the grounds that the transaction's conditions had not been met, and will instead **carve RDH out as a wholly-owned subsidiary** with its own technical and commercial team. The operator cited data centres as among the fastest-growing global segments and rising Egyptian demand as requiring a specialised operating model.
