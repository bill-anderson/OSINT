# InTouch (payment aggregator)

Pan-African **payment aggregator** granted, by the Cameroonian **[[ministry-of-territorial-administration-cameroon|Ministry of Territorial Administration]]**, **exclusive rights over online-gaming
payment aggregation** in Cameroon — a state-created payments monopoly.

## Activity in this wiki

- **State-granted monopoly and its collateral harm (2025).** Because the directive used the legal term
  "online games," it swept **video-game studios** in with gambling/lottery operators. **[[kiroo-games]]**
  (Central Africa's first pro game studio) was given **three weeks** to switch to InTouch and reported
  **10+ days with no customer payments at all**. The concentration risk is structural: ~**80% of
  Francophone-African online payments run on Mobile Money**, and a single aggregator removes the
  redundancy studios rely on to keep transactions flowing. The rare CMR item showing what **state
  control of payment rails costs the local digital economy** — from a founder who *supports* the
  underlying regulate-gambling objective but attacks the instrument. Source:
  [[2025-02-27-kiroo-games-payment-aggregation-monopoly-intouch-wearetech]].
