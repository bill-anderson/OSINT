# Project Kobong ($6.2bn Convalt hydropower + AI data centre)

Lesotho's **largest-ever proposed foreign direct investment** — a **~$6.2bn (98bn maloti)** deal between
the Ministry of Energy and US firm **[[convalt-energy|Convalt Energy]]** combining **1.2 GW hydropower**
(with 4.6 GW solar and up to 4 GWh battery storage) and an **AI data centre** in **Mokhotlong**,
green-powered. Framed as energy security (cutting South-African electricity imports), regional power
export, and a digital-infrastructure hub for AI. Construction expected from **2029**; associated US
exports could exceed **$2bn**. See [[lesotho--tech-ai]].

## Status disputed (as of 2026-06)

The deal's **legal status differs across outlets** — Tech Africa News reports a **binding Memorandum of
Agreement signed 4 June 2026**, while Connecting Africa calls it a **non-binding MoU / expression of
intent** pending feasibility studies and regulatory approval. Recorded as an open contradiction; the
underlying primary (the signed instrument) is not held. See `reviews/contradictions/open/`.
