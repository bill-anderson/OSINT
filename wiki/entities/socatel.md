# SOCATEL (Société Centrafricaine de Télécommunications)

The Central African Republic's **incumbent fixed-line operator**, partly privatised in 1990
when 40% was sold to a France Telecom (later Orange) subsidiary.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Incumbent fixed operator with an international-gateway monopoly.** SOCATEL's few-thousand
  fixed subscriptions are dwarfed by mobile. Per the ITU profile, reluctance to end SOCATEL's
  **monopoly over international gateways** — combined with institutional instability — caused
  the cross-border/transit component of the World Bank **Central African Backbone (CAB)**
  fibre project to be cancelled, leaving landlocked CAR dependent on costly satellite for
  international bandwidth. See [[caf--infra-connect]],
  [[2017-01-01 ITU Country Profile - Central African Republic]].
- **The "National Digital Hub" is now sourced and dated (resolved 2026-07-20).** What the CAF dataset
  carried as an uncited "SOCATEL National Digital Hub, planned Tier III data centre, Bangui" is the
  **Greenline Technologies MoU**: on **18 September 2025**, at the Ambition28 / PND 2024-2028 financing
  roundtable in **Casablanca** and before President **[[faustin-archange-touadera]]**, the government
  awarded US firm **Greenline Technologies Inc.** the mandate to **privatise and transform SOCATEL**, with
  a **$150m** initial investment to build "a national digital hub at the heart of Africa, complete with a
  new **Tier 3 data center**" and a national connectivity ecosystem
  ([[2025-09-18-greenline-socatel-privatisation-mou-greenline]]). Implementation entered an
  **operational phase on 16 July 2026**, though the takeover agreements, joint steering committee and
  deployment schedule remain unfinalised and the financing modalities undisclosed
  ([[2026-07-17-socatel-greenline-relance-150m-agenceecofin]]). **Nothing is built**; see
  [[car-national-datacentre]] for the four competing data-centre threads.
- **A long line of unfunded rescues.** Before Greenline, the government signed with French firm
  **Global Technologies** in **November 2020** to restore the decades-old hertzian network as a fibre
  backup reaching 40% of the population — a €20m project for which, as of **2026-07 (Agence Ecofin)**,
  no public record of progress or disbursement exists. A Russian cooperation on SOCATEL restructuring was
  also floated. SOCATEL's decline dates to the political-security crises of the late 1990s–2000s and the
  **2005 withdrawal of France Câbles Radio** (which became [[orange-centrafrique]]); a 2021 state-enterprise
  performance report described a vicious circle of ageing infrastructure and no capital to escape it.
