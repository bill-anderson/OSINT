# SOCATEL (Société Centrafricaine de Télécommunications)

The Central African Republic's **incumbent fixed-line operator**, partly privatised in 1990
when 40% was sold to a France Telecom (later Orange) subsidiary.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Incumbent fixed operator with an international-gateway monopoly.** SOCATEL's few-thousand
  fixed subscriptions are dwarfed by mobile. Per the ITU profile, reluctance to end SOCATEL's
  **monopoly over international gateways** — combined with institutional instability — caused
  the cross-border/transit component of the World Bank **Central African Backbone (CAB)**
  fibre project to be cancelled, leaving landlocked CAR dependent on costly satellite for
  international bandwidth. See [[caf--infra-connect]],
  [[2017-01-01 ITU Country Profile - Central African Republic]].
- The CAF dataset also lists a planned **SOCATEL National Digital Hub** (Tier III data
  centre, Bangui) — status *planned*; unconfirmed by a dated source, tracked for follow-up.
