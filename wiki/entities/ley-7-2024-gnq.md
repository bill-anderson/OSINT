# Ley 7/2024 (Equatorial Guinea cybercrime / social-media / cybersecurity law)

Equatorial Guinea's **cybercrime, social-media and cybersecurity law** (**Ley Núm. 7/2024, 23 Dec 2024**;
provisions rolled out 2026) — inspired by the **Budapest Convention (2001)** and the AU **[[malabo-convention|Malabo
Convention]] (2014)**. Key provisions: **under-14s** may not join social networks without parental consent and
their data may not be published; **no dissemination** of anyone's data/photos/videos without express written
consent; bans free downloading of copyrighted content; **criminalises identity usurpation** via false profiles.
It **creates the Agencia Nacional de Ciberseguridad e Infraestructuras Críticas (ANCIC)** (under the Presidency).
Sits alongside GNQ's pre-existing **Ley 1/2016 de Protección de Datos Personales** and a 2025 tender to rewrite
the 2005 telecoms law. See [[gnq--dpi-govtech]].
