# Somalia SPRING Project (World Bank)

The **Somalia Productive, Resilient, and Inclusive Growth (SPRING) Project** — a World
Bank-funded initiative estimated at **more than $100 million**, launched (per its Nov 2025
framing) by Somalia's Finance Minister. It aims to strengthen economic resilience by
improving the business environment, expanding access to financial and digitally-enabled
services, and supporting MSMEs, in a mostly private-sector-driven model.

- **Digital ID and DPI are a major pillar** — SPRING names [[nira-somalia]] among the
  responsible institutions (alongside the Ministry of Commerce and Industry, the
  [[central-bank-of-somalia]], the Financial Reporting Center and the MSME agency),
  framing legal ID as necessary for women, marginalised groups and displaced persons to
  access financial services, training and credit and for transparent social-protection
  targeting.
- Related World Bank vehicle: **SCALED-UP** (Somalia Capacity Advancement, Livelihoods and
  Entrepreneurship Through Digital Uplift Project), which — with SPRING — funds the
  foundational digital ID mass-registration drive.
