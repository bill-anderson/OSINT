# Social Health Authority (SHA, Kenya)

Kenya's public health-insurance body, managing the **Social Health Insurance Fund (SHIF)** — the
successor to the defunct NHIF — and the identity/authentication layer for accessing public healthcare
([[dpi.mis]], [[dpi.id]]). Its 2024-25 shift from **OTP to biometric** patient verification is a notable
DPI-design reversal, tied to the Ministry of Health's Biometric Health Identification (BHI) and Health
Information Exchange under the Digital Superhighway (BETA). See [[KEN]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **OTP abandoned for biometric ID (2025-08-04).** [[include.divides]] SHA dropped OTP patient
  verification (2024) after rural-coverage failures (60%+ of facilities reported delays) and phone-number
  fraud, reinstating fingerprint/facial biometrics; mandatory at Level 4-6 hospitals. Source:
  [[2025-08-04 Kenya Abandons OTP Verification, Reinstates Biometric ID for Public Healthcare]].
- **Biometric Health Identification launched (2025-08-05).** [[dpi.mis]] MoH launched BHI at KUTRRH
  (99% of walk-ins SHA-registered, 29 devices) alongside Practise360, a National Product Catalogue and a
  Health Information Exchange; 24 counties moving to HMIS. Source:
  [[2025-08-05 Kenya Ministry of Health Launches Biometric Health Identification]].
