# GenKey

Dutch biometric-identity firm supplying **biometric identity and voter-registration** systems in Africa.
In the Central African Republic, GenKey is the vendor behind the **[[ane-central-african-republic]]** (ANE)
electoral-register system; in the **Comoros**, it replaced Semlex on the national biometric passport/ID
stack, **repatriating data storage** from Belgium to domestic servers.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Comoros — replaced Semlex, repatriated data (2026).** GenKey **officially succeeded [[semlex|Semlex]]**
  (31 Mar/1 Apr 2026) on biometric **passport and national-ID production**, with printing decentralised to
  Anjouan. The sovereignty fact the earlier coverage lacked: **Semlex stored all Comorian biometric data in
  Belgium; GenKey stores it domestically**. See [[com--dpi-id]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-01-genkey-to-replace-semlex-from-april-data-domiciled-in-comoros-alwatwan]],
  [[2026-03-09-genkey-takes-over-comoros-biometric-passport-id-production-biometricupdate]],
  [[2026-04-01-genkey-officially-succeeds-semlex-biometric-production-alwatwan]].

- **CAR biometric voter registration.** GenKey supplied the biometric enrolment system used
  for CAR's **2020** general-election register and again for the **2024–25** revision ahead
  of the country's first local elections in ~36 years — ~3,000 enrolment tablets, ~1,469
  registration agents. President Touadera was the first person biometrically enrolled in
  2020. See [[caf--dpi-id]]. Sources:
  [[2024-10-08 Central African Republic in full biometric voter registration preparations]],
  [[2024-12-10 Central African Republic enrols citizens for first local elections in 36 years]].
