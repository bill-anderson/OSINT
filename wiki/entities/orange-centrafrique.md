# Orange Centrafrique

Central African Republic mobile subsidiary of **[[orange]]** (Orange France), operating in
CAR since **2007** with an estimated just-under-**2 million** subscribers — the market's
latest major entrant but its connectivity front-runner.

## Activity in this wiki

- **First 4G licence in CAR (Nov 2024).** Orange Centrafrique became the **first operator to
  win a 4G licence** in the country, signed with the Ministry of Digital Economy, Posts and
  Telecommunications; GM Max Francisco framed it as a major step for connectivity. No rollout
  date given. Source: [[2024-11-11 Orange secures 4G license in Central African Republic]].
- **First 3G (2013), mobile-only internet.** Orange was first to launch 3G in CAR (2013,
  mainly urban). Internet access in CAR is essentially entirely via mobile operators
  (Orange, Télétel/Telecel, Moov). See [[caf--infra-connect]],
  [[2017-01-01 ITU Country Profile - Central African Republic]].
- Competes with **Telecel** (Econet Wireless group) and **Moov Africa** (Maroc Telecom).
  The incumbent fixed operator is **[[socatel]]**.

A European incumbent carrying CAR's connectivity — the [[geopol.eu]]/colonial-legacy
dimension noted on the parent [[orange]] page applies here too.
