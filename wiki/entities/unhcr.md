# UNHCR — UN Refugee Agency

The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees. In this wiki UNHCR recurs as a partner in
**civil registration, legal identity and national-ID issuance** for displaced, returnee and
stateless populations across Africa — often jointly with the **[[european-union]]** and national
civil-registration authorities. Its programmes bear on the `dpi.id` and `include.access` questions
of who gets counted and documented.

## Activity in this wiki

- **South Sudan (2024-11).** UNHCR and the EU supported issuance of **17,400+ national identity
  cards** to returnees/at-risk populations, working with the civil-registry authority
  [[dcrnpi-south-sudan]]. See [[SSD]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-21 Over 17,400 South Sudanese receive national identity cards thanks to UNHCR and the EU]].

- **Zambia — [[zrhcp]] (2024-09-27).** UNHCR is the **technical-support partner** on the
  [[world-bank]]-financed **Zambia Refugee and Host Communities Project**, a **US$30m IDA
  grant** approved **2024-09-26** and announced 2024-09-27, whose identity limb enrols
  refugees and former refugees into **[[inris]]**, Zambia's national register. UNHCR
  Representative **Preeta Law** framed it as advancing "refugee inclusion, human development,
  and self-reliance" under the **Global Compact on Refugees**. **The consequential term is not
  in the press release:** the [[zrhcp]] page records the **transfer of UNHCR's ProGres
  registration database to Zambia's Ministry of Home Affairs and Internal Security** — an
  agency whose mandate is protection handing registration data to a national-security ministry.
  **That term rests on the project appraisal document, which the wiki does not hold** (as of
  2026-07-20; on `reviews/acquisitions.md`). See [[ZMB]], [[gov.protect]], [[include.divides]].
  Source: [[2024-09-27-world-bank-zambia-refugee-host-communities-grant]].
