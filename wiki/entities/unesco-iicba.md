# UNESCO International Institute for Capacity Building in Africa (UNESCO-IICBA)

UNESCO's Africa-focused institute for education capacity building, working with national
education ministries on teacher training and teacher-education systems. Implementing partner for
donor-funded teacher-capacity projects on the continent.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Teacher-capacity project in Nigeria, Japan-funded (handover 2026-06-02).** UNESCO-IICBA
  implements *Capacity Building of Teachers to Promote Continuous and Inclusive Access to Safe and
  Quality Education for Girls in West Africa* in partnership with the
  **[[federal-ministry-education-nigeria|Federal Ministry of Education]]**, funded by
  **[[japan]]**. Under it, **15 Nigerian Colleges of Education** received ICT equipment —
  laptops, tablets, desktops, smart boards, projectors, printers and backup hard drives — to
  strengthen teacher education, improve digital literacy and support gender-responsive teaching,
  with a stated focus on **girls and other vulnerable learners**. See [[NGA]], [[XWA]],
  [[capacity.training]]. Source: [[2026-07-17-japan-ict-equipment-nigerian-colleges]].

## Why it matters here

IICBA is the continental body sitting at the **teacher-education** layer of the digital-skills
stack the wiki tracks — upstream of the learner-facing programmes on [[capacity.training]] and
[[capacity.literacy]]. Equipping the institutions that train teachers is a compounding
intervention in principle; in practice the wiki holds only the input (a handover), with no
enrolment, deployment or outcome data attached.
