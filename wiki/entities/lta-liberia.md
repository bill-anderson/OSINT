# Liberia Telecommunications Authority (LTA)

Liberia's telecommunications regulator, responsible for regulating the sector, attracting
investment and supporting the country's digital agenda. Board Chairman: **Clarence Massaquoi**
(earlier acting chair: Commissioner **Patrick R. Honnah**, 2026-02).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Lead on the Digital Liberia strategy (~2026-02-03).** The LTA is a lead body, alongside the
  Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications, on the [[digital-liberia-strategy]] 2025–2029. At
  the launch, acting chair Honnah framed the plan as timely direction as technology reshapes
  economies and reaffirmed the LTA's commitment to collaboration, arguing a more modern ministry
  would enhance regulation, attract investment and deliver benefits to citizens. Tags
  [[gov.legislate]], [[infra.connect]], [[gov.policy]], [[LBR]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-03 Liberia Unveils 5-Year 'Digital Liberia' Strategy to Boost Sovereignty and Innovation]].
- **5G + fibre modernization plans (2026-01).** [[infra.connect]] Chairman Massaquoi announced plans to
  introduce **5G** nationwide and reduce single-cable dependence: a first fibre phase landed, a second is
  sought at **Buchanan**, and private firms are extending fibre from Buchanan to Rivercess, Sinoe,
  Maryland, Grand Gedeh and Nimba — pitched as universal access under "rebuilding Liberia." Source:
  [[2026-01-29-liberia-verge-5g-revolution-observer]].
- **Sweeping telecom reforms + Star Cell revocation (2026-07).** [[gov.legislate]] The LTA's biggest
  overhaul since the 2007 Telecommunications Act: splitting the universal licence into **infrastructure
  vs. service** licences (with rural incentives), infrastructure/tower-sharing rules, Liberia's first
  **satellite regulations** (as [[starlink]] and others enter), an **IMEI** anti-theft regime, and
  repatriating the **.lr ccTLD** to local control. It **revoked Star Cell's licence** (issued Mar 2020,
  never operational) and fined **Orange Liberia** L$4m for a SIM-swap privacy breach ([[gov.protect]]);
  the LTA plans to spend ~US$3m on spectrum monitoring. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-lta-telecom-reforms-star-cell-revoked-fpa]].
