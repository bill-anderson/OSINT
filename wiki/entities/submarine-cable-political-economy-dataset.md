# Submarine cable political-economy dataset (Abels & Babic)

A **novel open-access dataset** on the political economy of the global submarine
fibre-optic cable network, compiled to accompany the paper
[[cable-wars-abels-babic-2026]] (Joscha Abels & Milan Babic, *Globalizations*,
2026). Published open-access on **OSF**, it is presented by the authors as the
**first empirical mapping** of cable ownership and supply, and as a foundation
for follow-up research. We consult it as a standing reference asset for cable
ownership, capacity and supplier structure.

## What it contains

- **622 cable systems** (all active and planned), scraped from
  [[telegeography]]'s submarinecablemap.com **as of October 2024**.
- Reaches back to **1989** (the ready-for-service year of the oldest cable in the
  set).
- Variables per cable: **name, RFS year, length, owners, suppliers, and landing
  points (city and country)**.
- **Manually researched capacity data** added to the TeleGeography base (a
  variable TeleGeography did not supply); capacity obtained for **61%** of cables.
  Detailed ownership *shares* are not included (rarely public).

## Headline findings the dataset supports

- **[[google]] is the single most prominent owner** — most frequent as general
  and sole owner, largest share of total cable length, present in >5% of all
  systems and **16% of "core" (≥5,000 km) cables**.
- Telecom carriers (Orange, Telefónica, Vodafone, Tata) remain central; SOEs
  (China Unicom, China Telecom) and [[meta]] feature prominently in the core.
- **Supply is highly oligopolistic**: the "big four" (SubCom, ASN, NEC, HMN Tech)
  account for **~85%** of all cable kilometres. HMN Tech's share fell after the US
  added it to the Commerce Department entity list in **December 2021**.
- Capacity is even more unequally distributed than length; growth accelerated
  sharply post-2015 with hyperscaler entry.

Use the dataset (with the paper) for cable-network ownership and supplier
structure at global scale; pair it with [[telegeography]] for current specifics.
