# Njabulo Nzuza

**Deputy Minister of Home Affairs, South Africa (MP)** — deputy to
**[[leon-schreiber]]** at **[[home-affairs-south-africa]]**. His **3 July 2025** Budget Vote speech
to the NCOP is the richest hard-data item the wiki holds on South African Smart ID and civil-records
delivery, and it also introduces a live architectural conflict.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The SARB claim — a recorded contradiction (2025-07-03).** Nzuza states that DHA "is working with
  the **South African Reserve Bank** to develop a robust digital identity system," using **facial
  recognition as the primary biometric modality**. The wiki's held material has the digital-ID stack
  built **inside the SARS hosting environment** — and **[[sarb]] is not [[sars]]**. The DHA's **own
  tabled Strategic Plan 2025-2030 corroborates SARB**, naming it explicitly. **Resolved
  (2026-07-20): both are true, in different roles.** The DHA's Annual Performance Plan 2026/27
  (tabled 2026-03-30) names both in one sentence and settles the split - the core digital-identity
  and PKI infrastructure is **hosted within the SARS environment**, while **SARB is an integration
  partner on the payments/eKYC side** (its own Digital Payments Roadmap states it is "not the leading
  authority in relation to identification in SA"). Nzuza's 2025 statement was **incomplete, not
  wrong**. Sources:
  [[2026-03-30-dha-annual-performance-plan-2026-27-digital-identity]] (**the primary that settles
  it** — "hosted within the SARS environment" in the narrative, SARB named only under *Assumptions*:
  "SARB, banks and telecom partners collaborate on integration"),
  [[2025-07-03 Deputy Minister Njabulo Nzuza - Home Affairs Dept Budget Vote 2025-26, NCOP]],
  [[2025-04-01 South Africa Department of Home Affairs Strategic Plan 2025-2030]].
- **Smart ID delivery (FY2024/25).** A record **3,597,856 Smart ID cards issued against a 2.5m
  target**; **cumulative 25,102,201** issued since the modernisation programme's inception.
  **Caution: this is a fiscal-year figure and must not be conflated with the calendar-year counts
  the wiki already carries (4,002,964 in CY2025; 3,427,468 in CY2024).** Source:
  [[2025-07-03 Deputy Minister Njabulo Nzuza - Home Affairs Dept Budget Vote 2025-26, NCOP]].
- **Live Capture footprint (FY2024/25).** Live Capture operating in **212 modernised offices**, with
  **10 additional front offices** targeted in 2025/26. Source: same.
- **Civic-records digitisation (2025-07-03).** **Over 49 million civic records digitised since
  2023**, with a **25 million** target for 2025/26. Once digitised, amendments can be effected
  **almost instantly, against the eight weeks** it took at the time of speaking — the clearest
  single statement of the service-delivery payoff. A Presidential Project. Source: same.
- **Banks as ID front-ends (FY2024/25).** **95,191 Smart ID card requests processed through 30 bank
  branches**, up from **73,364 in 2023/24** — the baseline the later Capitec bank-branch launch and
  the Strategic Plan's 1,000-branch 2029 target build on. Source: same.
- **Mobile offices (FY2024/25).** Deployed to **10,149 service areas**, of which **7,870 were school
  visits**; collected **247,231 Smart ID applications** (vs 229,416 in 2023/24). See
  [[digital.rural]], [[include.access]]. Source: same.
- **Birth registration and the register.** **868,476 births** and **143,301 late registrations**
  registered in the year; birth registration at **323 front offices and 391 health facilities**;
  online birth registration rolling out to 25 maternity facilities in 2025/26, target **667,000
  births within 30 days**. Framed as keeping the **National Population Register** clean. Also: **75
  AI-powered self-service kiosks** procured for non-modernised offices. Source: same.
