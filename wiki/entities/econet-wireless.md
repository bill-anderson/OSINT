# Econet Wireless

Diversified African telecoms and technology group founded by **[[strive-masiyiwa]]**,
the platform from which the [[cassava-technologies]] digital-infrastructure group later
emerged. In this wiki Econet matters as (a) the origin of Africa's mobile-money story via
**[[ecocash]]**, and (b) the corporate lineage behind Cassava/Liquid.

## Profile

- **Origins.** Licensed in Zimbabwe in **1997** after a five-year constitutional-court
  battle against the state PTC monopoly (incorporated ~1998); HQ moved to **South Africa
  in 2000**. Described across sources (2014) as operating in **15–18 markets** including
  Burundi, Lesotho, Kenya, Nigeria, Botswana, Rwanda (plus UK/New Zealand) — *figures as
  of 2014; treat as dated.*
- **Businesses.** Mobile telephony; mobile money / fintech ([[ecocash]]); insurance;
  renewable energy (incl. solar phone-charging kiosks); e-commerce; education.
- **Group structure.** Econet Global / Econet Group is the founder vehicle; the
  infrastructure arm is **[[cassava-technologies]]** → **[[liquid-c2]]** (connectivity /
  cloud / cyber) and **[[africa-data-centres]]**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Liquid acquired an Equiano fibre pair (~2022).** Econet's infrastructure arm
  [[liquid-c2]] (Liquid Intelligent Technologies) acquired a **fibre pair on [[google]]'s
  Equiano** subsea cable (up to **12 Tbps**), adding to its **>100,000km** African fibre and
  plans to interconnect Equiano's landings east–west — announced days after Equiano's first
  African landing (Togo). See [[infra.connect]], [[equiano-cable]]. Source:
  [[2022-03-01 Liquid Intelligent acquires Google's fiber pair to improve offers]].

- **"Second division" operator (Russell Southwood, 2025-12-04).** Southwood's MNO
  state-of-market places Econet in the "second division" of operators (with
  Africell, Axian, Globacom, Telecel) — below the four pan-African "power players"
  — even as its sister infrastructure arm [[cassava-technologies]] operates at the
  continental frontier. See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].
