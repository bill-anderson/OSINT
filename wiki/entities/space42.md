**Space42** is a **United Arab Emirates** satellite and geospatial-technology company operating the **Thuraya** mobile-satellite service. It combines satellite communications, geospatial intelligence and Earth-observation capabilities, and is expanding aggressively into Africa as part of the broader Gulf technology push onto the continent (see [[uae]]).

## Thuraya-4 launch in South Africa

On **9 February 2026**, Space42 marked the launch of **[[thuraya-4]]** connectivity services in South Africa at an event in **Johannesburg** held **under the patronage of President Cyril Ramaphosa**. The company claims Thuraya-4 brings **mobile satellite communications coverage to the entire African continent for the first time**. The event featured live product demonstrations and presentations of Space42's geospatial and Earth-observation capabilities, and unveiled a planned **"Map Africa"** geospatial initiative aimed at supporting continental digital transformation through data-driven technologies.

Speakers underscored the bilateral, UAE–South Africa dimension:

- **Sulaiman Al Ali**, Chief Commercial Officer, Space42 — opened the event and described meeting President Ramaphosa to present Space42's portfolio and the introduction of Thuraya services in South Africa;
- **Solly Malatsi**, South Africa's Minister of Communications and Digital Technologies — keynote on the country's digital transformation strategy;
- **Fatima Yousef AlSuwaidi**, Deputy Head of Mission, UAE Embassy — remarks on international cooperation;
- a "Satellite Solutions for National Development" panel including **Deputy Minister Mondli Gungubele**, UAE Embassy economic researcher **Jacob Ssali**, Morevest's **Zunaid Bulbulia**, and Space42's **Muiz Saad**.

The launch reinforces South Africa's positioning as a regional technology hub while deepening a foreign (Gulf-state) satellite operator's role in continental connectivity infrastructure — the basis for the sovereignty lens on foreign control of communications and geospatial data.
