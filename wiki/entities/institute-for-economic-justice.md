# Institute for Economic Justice (IEJ)

**A South African economic-policy organisation that, with [[paythegrants]], is litigating the
country's clearest test of digital-by-default exclusion.** The IEJ opposes the state's Supreme
Court of Appeal appeal against a **January 2025** Pretoria High Court ruling that struck key
Social Relief of Distress (SRD) grant regulations as unlawful and unconstitutional.

*Capture is not endorsement; the arguments below are the parties' own, reported from filed court
papers — they are pleadings, not findings.*

## Activity in this wiki

- **The SCA appeal (as reported 2026-01-28).** The IEJ and #PayTheGrants accuse
  **[[national-treasury-south-africa]]** and the Department of Social Development of deliberately
  using administrative barriers to keep SRD spending low. The high court ruling under appeal struck
  the **online-only application system**, the **R624 monthly income threshold** and **the grant's
  value**. A hearing date was **yet to be determined** as of 2026-01-28. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 State is blocking access to SASSA grants, argue activists]].
- **The charge, from the papers.** The state made the grant "inaccessible to millions of people who
  need it, refused it to eligible beneficiaries based on inaccurate information, reduced its value
  and the means threshold for eligibility, and **persistently failed to pay hundreds of thousands
  of approved beneficiaries**." *Note the corroboration: **[[sassa]]**'s own statement nine days
  earlier reports that as of 1 Jan 2026, only **2,690 of 8,368 approved** SRD applications from
  PRs/refugees/permit-holders had been **paid (32%)** — the government independently evidencing the
  activists' unpaid-approvals claim.* Source: same.
- **Scale, on the department's own June-2022 figures.** **13.4 million** working-age people with
  **no income**; **18.3 million** working-age people below the **food poverty line**. Treasury
  "persistently allocates funding that is sufficient to fund payment to **fewer than half** of the
  18.3-million people who the Department of Social Development has estimated would qualify."
  Source: same.
- **The mechanism of exclusion.** Any money entering an applicant's bank account is treated as
  **income**; the groups say the government's "unchallenged evidence" shows bank verification
  **wrongly excludes eligible applicants** by misclassifying funds and relying on a **single
  month's snapshot** — catching people who receive occasional help from friends or relatives.
  Source: same.
- **The digital-access argument, and the state's reply.** The groups argue the online-only system is
  irrational and unconstitutional, adopted to "serve the improper purpose of reducing uptake." **The
  department argued that complaints posted by applicants on platforms like X prove there is no
  barrier to online access.** The groups countered that while some applicants have social media,
  a significant number **have no internet access**, and that the system is "**an insurmountable
  hurdle**" for people without digital literacy or with disabilities. The groups reject Treasury's
  unaffordability claim and ask the court to dismiss the appeal with costs. Source: same.

## The grant, dated

Introduced **2020** as a temporary covid emergency measure; extended annually, current extension
running to **31 March 2027**. Value raised **R350 → R370 in April 2024**. Qualifying threshold:
earnings **under R625/month**, with monthly bank checks. It reaches **significantly fewer people
than at its March-2022 peak**. Source: same.
