# Ministry of Home Affairs, Eswatini

**Custodian of Eswatini's national identity and civil registration** ([[SWZ]]) — the
ministry that holds the National Identification Card, the Personal Identity Number
(PIN) and the central civil registry on which any future digital ID would rest. See
[[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **The National ID card, and its limits.** The Kingdom initiated the National
  Identification Card in **2003**; it carries facial identity, full names, date of
  birth, chief code and a **Personal Identity Number (PIN)**, centrally stored by the
  Ministry. Service providers including commercial banks have gained access to the
  civil registry for identity verification — but [[apc]] assessed **(as of 2024-07-22)**
  that the card "does not yet meet the criteria for digital identification": facial
  identity alone does not grant direct access to the PIN or other details. Source:
  [[2024-07-22 Risk of human rights infringement as Eswatini rushes to regulate the ICT sector]].
- **CRVS Act.** The Ministry's FY2024/25 annual report to Parliament notes the launch
  of the CRVS Act. Source:
  [[2024-01-01 Eswatini Ministry of Home Affairs Annual Report 2024-25]].
- **The register as DPI foundation (2025-08-05).** The World Bank identifies the
  national population register — the Ministry's asset — as the foundation on which
  robust DPI should be built, advising it be equipped with digital capability, a
  resilient digital security architecture and a biometric authentication system. See
  [[eswatini-economic-update-2025]]. Source:
  [[2025-08-05 Robust DPI inevitable for Eswatini's economic, jobs boom - World Bank]].
