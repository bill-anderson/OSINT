# BoFiNet (Botswana Fibre Networks)

Botswana's **state-owned wholesale connectivity operator**, developer and operator of the national data-centre infrastructure.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digital Delta Data Centre (DDDC), 2025.** BoFiNet launched Botswana's **first national data centre** in Gaborone — the country's only **Tier III** certified, carrier-neutral facility, fully government-owned (built by Chinese firm Zhong Gan) — positioned as a national digital hub and data-sovereignty asset. Full case: **[[digital-delta-data-centre]]**. See [[infra.store]] and the [[BWA]] hub. Source: [[2025-11-26 Botswana launches Tier III Digital Delta Data Centre (national data centre)]].
- **International gateway upgrade — P100m, citizen-owned (2025).** [[infra.connect]] BoFiNet invested
  **>P100m** upgrading Botswana's internet gateway (peak traffic **100 Gbps** Feb 2025 vs <8 Gbps in 2015;
  London/Johannesburg IXP upgrades, Gaborone IXP relocation), **awarded to 100%-citizen-owned** firms
  Hexaract and Comsoft — a localisation/sovereignty angle. Sources:
  [[2025-04-16-bofinet-invests-p100m-internet-gateway]], [[2025-04-17-bofinet-p100m-internet-gateway-upgrade-techafricanews]].
- **Regional infrastructure play (2025–26).** [[gov.regional]] BoFiNet was **shortlisted to host the SADC
  regional internet exchange** (against one Zimbabwean bidder), and signed a **cross-border fibre MoU with
  MTC Namibia** (Buitepos–Ngoma corridor, capacity exchange, IP transit; Feb 2026). New CEO **Dr Gaone
  Seleka** replaced Keabetswe Segole (Mar 2026). Sources: [[2025-11-27-botswana-bids-to-host-sadc-internet-exchange-itweb]],
  [[2026-02-18-mtc-namibia-bofinet-cross-border-fibre-mou-techafricanews]], [[2026-03-06-bofinet-appoints-ceo-gaone-seleka-connectingafrica]].
