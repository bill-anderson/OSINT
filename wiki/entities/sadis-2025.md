# South Africa Digital Infrastructure Investment Study (Sadis) 2025

Sadis 2025 is a national digital-infrastructure investment study and granular broadband-map GIS, dividing South Africa into ~1.5m hexagonal cells of ~0.76 km² each. It matters here as a data asset that maps the connectivity gap with unusual precision — though the underlying data is not yet public.

## Profile
- Commissioned by the [[dbsa]] and the National Planning Commission; produced by Africa Analysis (with Ellipsis Regulatory Solutions and Networks Anonymous).
- Uses the ITU Universal & Meaningful Connectivity framework; models the investment needed to reach universal and meaningful access.
- Gives 213 municipalities a composite broadband-access score.
- Findings: 4G reaches 98% of the population; ~400,000 households lack 4G+; 74% of households are within 10 km of a fibre node but 12.2% are beyond 20 km; 6,700+ of ~15,000 public schools and 1,000+ clinics sit 10–25 km from fibre.
- Data is DBSA-owned and not yet public.
