# COSASE (Committee on Commissions, Statutory Authorities and State Enterprises), Uganda

**The Ugandan parliamentary oversight committee that scrutinises state enterprises and statutory bodies — and, in this wiki, the venue where almost every load-bearing admission about Uganda's national digital-ID programme was made on the record.** COSASE hearings are the reason the wiki can date NIRA's own failure figures rather than rely on advocacy estimates.

*Paged for that function: it is not a passing institutional mention but the evidentiary source of the [[uganda--dpi-id]] case.*

## Why it matters here

Uganda's ID programme generates two competing accounts — the government's and civil society's. COSASE testimony is a **third category**: the implementing agency's own numbers, given under parliamentary questioning, on a dated record. Where this wiki states a NIRA figure with confidence, it usually traces to COSASE.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The 18.1 million, from NIRA itself (2024-07-01).** NIRA Executive Director **[[rosemary-kisembo]]** told COSASE: *"Based on the current census figures of 45.9 million people, we haven't registered about 18.1 million…"* — **NIRA's own figure, not an advocacy estimate**, and the single most load-bearing correction in the Uganda ID file. Source: [[2024-07-16-nira-cosase-18-million-unregistered-staffing]].
- **A second, different million (2024-07-02).** Testifying on the [[office-of-the-auditor-general-uganda|Auditor General]]'s 2022/23 report, Kisembo said **"there are currently 1 million people in the register whose fingerprints did not match"** — prints already on file that no longer resolve. Source: same.
- **The structural staffing admission (2024-07-01).** *"NIRA's staffing levels have never exceeded 50%, meaning that the ratio of unregistered Ugandans to NIRA staff is **1:80,000**."* NIRA was present in **112 districts**, leaving **24 districts and 10 cities** uncovered. Asked by COSASE chair **Medard Sseggona** whether staff could rotate between districts, Kisembo said rotation was **not feasible** against existing demand. Source: same.
- **The enrolment slip, dated by NIRA (2024-07-01).** *"We are three months behind projections… We were supposed to begin the enrolment process on June 1, 2024."* Source: same.
- **The Veridos procurement contested (hearings 2022-11, event 2023-03-30).** COSASE, then under **Joel Ssenyonyi**, questioned the **Privatisation Unit of the Ministry of Finance** on whether [[veridos]] was properly procured for national-ID card printing; Veridos had told the committee its factory would not be producing until **2027**. Sources: [[2023-03-29-parliament-uganda-cosase-uppc-veridos-contract]], [[2023-04-30-monitor-fight-over-national-id-deal-splits-parliament]], [[2022-11-24-biometricupdate-uganda-parliament-probe-veridos-card-printing]].

## Reads

- Parliamentary oversight here works as a *disclosure* mechanism more than a corrective one: COSASE extracted the numbers but the record does not show the programme changing course as a result. The value to this wiki is documentary.
