# Centro Nacional de Cibersegurança (Angola)

Angola's **National Cybersecurity Centre**, **created by Presidential Decree 263/25 of 10 December 2025**, which also approved its *Estatuto Orgânico*. It is an **instituto público with legal personality and administrative, financial and patrimonial autonomy**, seated in Luanda, operating nationwide and empowered to open local services. It runs **under the superintendence of the minister responsible for telecommunications and IT** ([[ministerio-das-telecomunicacoes-tecnologias-de-informacao-e-comunicacao-social-angola|MINTTICS]]), who appoints and dismisses its Director-General, approves its plans and budget and may suspend or revoke its acts.

**It is a regulator, not only a response centre.** Art. 6 gives it regulation, rule-making, supervision, inspection and **sanctioning** powers over cybersecurity; Art. 7 lets it issue binding circulars, deliberations and *instrutivos* on supervised entities, publishable in the *Diário da República*. It also drafts the National Cybersecurity Strategy, sets the national cyber-alert level, and supports the Conselho Nacional de Cibersegurança technically.

**Structure.** Management: a Conselho Directivo chaired by the Director-General with two Deputy Directors-General (Cyber Management; Incidents and Emergencies), plus a three-member Conselho Fiscal. Executive services: **CERT.ao — a department inside the Centro, not a separate agency** — plus a Critical Infrastructure and Essential Services Protection department and a **24/7 Cybersecurity Operations Department**.

**Funding.** Receipts (state budget allocations, subsidies, and the proceeds of the fines it levies) are split **40% Treasury / 30% the Centro / 30% a *Fundo de Cibersegurança*** (Art. 25) — a sanctioning body retaining a share of its own penalties. The Cybersecurity Fund is not established in any instrument the wiki holds *(as of 2026-07-20)*.

Source for all of the above: [[2025-12-10-angola-decreto-presidencial-263-25-centro-nacional-ciberseguranca]].

## Status in this wiki

- **Decree track — in force.** DP 263/25 was approved in Council of Ministers **2025-10-27**, signed **2025-12-02** and published **2025-12-10**, entering into force on publication. It sits alongside DP 256/25 (National Cybersecurity Strategy) and DP 258/25 (Conselho Nacional de Cibersegurança), both of 2025-12-03. Its **Anexos I–III** (staff tables and organigram) are tabular/graphic and **not held as of 2026-07-20**.
- **Legislative track — stalled.** The [[angola-cybersecurity-law]] would also have created the Council and Centre; it was **approved in general terms 23 Jan 2026 (105–1–75)** and its committee-stage discussion **suspended 2026-07-07** over drafting non-conformities. The Centre does not depend on it — but the bill would supply the statutory obligations and offence framework a decree cannot confer. See [[angola-cybersecurity-law]].
- **Operational status is not established.** Creation is documented; **no source the wiki holds records a Director-General appointed, a budget line, staff in post or the Centro exercising any power** *(as of 2026-07-20)*.
- **Presidential framing.** President [[joao-lourenco]] listed the *Centro Nacional de Cibersegurança* among the digital-sovereignty investments (with the National Broadband, National Data Centre and National Cloud) at the 2026 Judicial Year opening (2026-03-03) — after its creation. See [[angola--infra-cybersec]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Sources: [[2026-01-23-proposta-lei-ciberseguranca-aprovada-generalidade]], [[2026-03-03-joao-lourenco-cybercrime-desafio-da-justica]].
- **Interface with data protection.** Art. 6(c) requires the Centro to act in articulation with the [[apd-angola|APD]] where an incident has caused a personal-data breach.
