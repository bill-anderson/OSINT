# i.Lab — Innovation Laboratory for Public Finance

An innovation lab inside Angola's **Ministry of Finance (MINFIN)**, run by the Public Finance ICT Service **SETIC-FP**, to experiment with, validate and scale digital solutions in public financial management — "test, measure impact, and scale what improves services" (unit coordinator Maer Neto).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launch (announced 2026-03-27, reported 2026-03-30).** i.Lab was presented with a **proof of concept** introducing **qualified digital signatures** into SETIC-FP's internally built **Integrated Document Management System (SIGD)** — strengthening authenticity/integrity of electronic documents and cutting manual validation. SETIC-FP's prior in-house tools include the Public Debt Management Unit Portal, the Integrated Public Investment Programme system (**SIGPIP**) and the Fiscal Council Management System (CGCF). See [[angola--dpi-govtech]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2026-03-30-angola-pushes-digital-reform-with-new-public-finance-innovation-hub]].
- **Brazil at the table — the sovereignty framing.** At the launch, **[[serpro]]** president **Wilton Mota** tied public-sector innovation to state sovereignty: *"Innovation in the public sector is not just about technology, it is about sovereignty… we need environments like this i-Lab to experiment, make mistakes fast on a small scale and get it right… The State needs to own its own digital destiny,"* mastering data orchestration and eliminating external dependencies that pose security risks. i.Lab also sits alongside a **March 2026 Angola–Cape Verde** digital-governance agreement and strengthened ties with **Brazil** — two partners largely outside the wiki's China/India/UAE reading of Angola's DPI contest. See [[serpro]], [[CPV]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source: [[2026-03-30-angola-launches-i-lab-public-finance-techafricanews]].
