# Zimbabwe–US health MoU (rejected)

**Parties:** United States and Zimbabwe. **What:** a proposed five-year US health-funding
MoU (HIV/AIDS, TB, malaria, maternal/child health, epidemic preparedness) under the
[[america-first-global-health-strategy]] that **Zimbabwe rejected**. **Status:** talks
collapsed; the US Embassy in Harare confirmed the wind-down of US health assistance on
**2026-02-25**.

**Amount — resolved (REV-004, 2026-07-16): $367 million over five years.** This is the **US Embassy
Harare's own stated MoU value** (Ambassador Pamela Tremont, 24 Feb 2026), reported by Ecofin Agency
([[2026-02-25 Zimbabwe Turns Down U.S. Health Funding, Citing Sovereignty Concerns]]) and independently
corroborated by Africanews (26 Feb) and Reuters/Africa CDC (5 Mar). The earlier **$350m** (Business
Insider Africa, 24 Feb — [[2026-02-24 ⁠Zimbabwe rejects $350 million US health funding deal warns It threatens national sovereignty]])
was a **pre-announcement round estimate** carried by the first wave of reporting off the leaked 23 Dec
2025 Zimbabwean memo, before the embassy released its precise figure — the **same deal**, not a rival
value. (A third figure, ~**$328m**, circulated as Harare's own hedged characterisation — "in the region
of" — via the state *Herald*; not held on file.)

## Activity in this wiki

- **Rejection and rationale.** In a December 2025 memo/letter (a Dec 23 directive via the
  Secretary for Foreign Affairs), President **Emmerson Mnangagwa** called the draft
  "clearly unbalanced" / "lop-sided" and said it compromised national sovereignty. Objections:
  **long-term US access to national health data**, an attempt to **fold in Zimbabwe's critical
  minerals**, and refusal to endorse a bilateral health architecture outside the WHO. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.mis]], [[sovereignty]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-25 Zimbabwe Turns Down U.S. Health Funding, Citing Sovereignty Concerns]],
  [[2026-02-24 ⁠Zimbabwe rejects $350 million US health funding deal warns It threatens national sovereignty]].
- **Cost of rejection.** **1.2 million** Zimbabweans receive HIV treatment via US-supported
  programmes and face a funding vacuum as [[usaid]] winds down. US Ambassador Pamela Tremont
  said Washington would "align with Harare's decision." Source:
  [[2026-02-25 Zimbabwe Turns Down U.S. Health Funding, Citing Sovereignty Concerns]].
- **Regional signal.** Zimbabwe tied its refusal to the WHO PABS negotiations, arguing a
  bilateral deal would undermine African solidarity in multilateral forums — contrast with
  [[us-kenya-health-framework]] (signed, then frozen). Zambia similarly pushed back.
- **Regional pattern corroborated (2026-02).** Africanews and the Guardian place Zimbabwe's
  rejection within a wider pushback: **Zambia** halted a ~$1bn MoU over mining-linked, 10-year
  data-access terms (health minister fired after denying the mining link); **Kenya**'s High Court
  froze its deal; and [[africa-cdc]]'s Jean Kaseya backed renegotiation ("We want to own our data
  in Africa"). Africanews independently corroborates the **$367m** Zimbabwe figure. See
  [[zambia-us-health-mou]], [[america-first-global-health-strategy]], [[geopol.usa]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-26 Zambia rejects U.S. health aid over mining partnership ties]],
  [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]].
- **Reuters/Africa CDC confirmation (2026-03-05).** Reporting on Kaseya's press conference, Reuters again
  states Zimbabwe pulled out of the **$367m/5-yr** deal (a third corroboration of the figure), calling it
  "unequal" and citing sensitive-data concerns; [[africa-cdc]] flagged "huge concerns regarding data,
  regarding pathogen sharing." Source:
  [[2026-02-27 Africa CDC head cites major concerns over data, pathogen sharing in US health deals]].
