# Independent Communications Authority of South Africa (ICASA)

South Africa's converged regulator for the telecommunications, broadcasting and postal
sectors — it licenses operators, manages spectrum and sets sector regulations.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Draft rapid-deployment regulations (gazetted ~2026-04-10).** ICASA published draft
  regulations on the rapid deployment of electronic communications networks, opening a
  30-working-day comment window (closing late May 2026). They give effect to the national
  rapid-deployment policy issued by the communications minister in **March 2023**, and
  prescribe land-access, wayleave, consultation and compensation procedures — though,
  notably, they impose no binding municipal turnaround times or deemed-approval mechanism.
  See [[ZAF]], [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-12 Icasa moves to mandate national infrastructure database]].
- **National GIS infrastructure database (Regulation 7).** The most consequential
  provision creates a national, geo-referenced GIS database: every licensed operator must
  submit **bi-annual** data on the location and type of all passive infrastructure — fibre
  routes, copper, ducts, poles, manholes and base stations — plus service availability at
  address-level granularity and forward-looking roll-out plans. Submissions must conform to
  the **WGS84** coordinate system and **ESRI Shapefile / GeoJSON / GeoPackage** formats.
  Non-compliance carries fines up to **R1-million**. The GIS obligations take effect **six
  months after** publication. This is the first comprehensive regulator-held map of
  fixed and mobile infrastructure in South Africa; operators have historically resisted such
  disclosure on competitive-sensitivity grounds, and forward-looking roll-out disclosure is
  expected to be the most contested element. See [[gov.standards]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[data.open]]. Source: [[2026-04-12 Icasa moves to mandate national infrastructure database]].
- **Target of Starlink licence lobbying (2026-01).** [[starlink]] is running an online
  advocacy campaign urging SA customers to pressure ICASA for market entry (a Jan 2026
  email prompted the push). Entry is blocked by SA's 30% historically-disadvantaged
  ownership rule; Minister Malatsi's Dec 2025 policy direction to ICASA proposed **Equity
  Equivalent Investment Programmes (EEIPs)** as an alternative, with SpaceX pledging
  **R2.5bn** toward B-BBEE compliance. See [[gov.policy]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-18 Starlink pushes for SA license with advocacy campaign]].
