# Bank of Ghana (BoG)

Ghana's central bank and the regulator behind the country's interoperable digital-payments
stack. Governor (as of early 2026) Johnson Pandit Asiama. BoG launched Ghana's mobile-money
interoperability system in 2018, letting customers move value across mobile networks and
between wallets and bank accounts, and positions this domestic infrastructure as a platform
for regional integration.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Interoperable mobile-money volumes near-double in 2025 (reported 2026-02-04).** At the
  African Prosperity Dialogue, Second Deputy Governor Matilda Asante-Asiedu reported that
  interoperable mobile-money transaction value rose from GH¢3.1bn (December 2024) to
  GH¢5.8bn (December 2025), up 87%; transaction count rose from ~20m to ~29m (up 45%). BoG
  cited complementary work: participation in [[papss]] for cross-border local-currency
  settlement, a **fintech passporting arrangement with Rwanda**, next-generation DPI pilots,
  and a recently passed **Virtual Asset Service Providers Act**. Framed under AfCFTA
  financial integration. Tags [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.legislate]], [[data.statistics]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-05 Ghana Mobile Money Interoperability Nearly Doubles in 2025]].
- **Payment-system overhaul call (African Prosperity Dialogue, 2026).** Second Deputy
  Governor **Matilda Asante-Asiedu** urged an overhaul of Africa's cross-border
  payment rails: intra-African payment costs run **7–10%** (vs ~3% globally), with
  **>80%** of inter-African payments routed through correspondent banks abroad at
  ~**$5.3bn/yr** — pressing [[papss]], fintech passporting with Rwanda and the Virtual
  Asset Service Providers law as the route to unlocking the **1.5bn-person / ~$2.8tn**
  [[afcfta]] market. See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-05 Bank of Ghana Calls for Payment System Overhaul to Unlock AfCFTA Trade]].

- **GhIPSS in the "payments end-game" thesis (analysis, 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki lists Ghana's
  **GhIPSS** among the African instant-payment switches (with Tanzania's TIPS, South Africa's
  PayShap and Kenya's in-development Faster Payments) whose spread, he argues, compresses processing
  margins and pushes durable fintech value into credit, data and compliance. Analyst framing,
  attributed to Kariuki. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]].
- **Bank of Ghana–Rwanda fintech licence-passporting MoU (analysis, 2026-06).** Russell Southwood's
  regulatory-reform agenda records that BoG and the **National Bank of Rwanda signed an MoU for a
  fintech licence-passporting framework** — cited as a model for the kind of cross-border regulatory
  reciprocity African regulators should pursue; corroborates the passporting arrangement noted above.
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-25 Regulating for Africa's future digital economy – A draft agenda to speed things up]].
