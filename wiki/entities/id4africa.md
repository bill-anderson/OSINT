# ID4Africa

ID4Africa is a pan-African identity movement, chaired by Joseph Atick, whose annual General Meeting (AGM) convenes governments, data-protection authorities and vendors around digital identity and digital public infrastructure. Its AGM functions as a key venue for setting continental norms on trusted identity.

## Activity in this wiki

- **2026 AGM held in Abidjan.** The meeting served as the forum where [[world-bank]] and African data-protection authorities set out data-lifecycle trust principles for digital identity and DPI. Source: [[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].
- **Tracks vendor and country coverage.** ID4Africa is the venue where [[mosip]]'s country coverage — some 29 country MoUs — is tracked, and where debates over decentralised versus centralised identity models play out. Source: [[2026-05-28 Why decentralized digital identity looks different in every country]].
- **2025 AGM — Benin's ANIP showcase.** At the ID4Africa 2025 AGM, **[[anip-benin]]** Director-General Aristide Guy Adjinacou presented Benin's national ID progress (register covering ~98% of the population, ~200,000 biometric authentications/day) and its planned citizen mobile wallet — the AGM serving as the platform where high-coverage national programmes are profiled. See [[BEN]], [[dpi.id]]. Source: [[2025-06-18 Benin envisages mobile wallet launch in inclusive national digital ID push]].
