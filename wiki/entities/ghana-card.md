# Ghana Card

Ghana's national identity card, issued by the **[[national-identification-authority-ghana|National Identification Authority (NIA)]]**
and built and operated by Ghana's **[[margins-id-group]]** under a DBFOT arrangement.
It is the country's foundational ID credential. **Coverage trajectory (dated):** 17.56m adults
(15+) registered (Oct 2023) → ~18m (Oct 2024) → 18.95m enrolled / 17.75m issued (Sep 2025) →
**19.2m registered**, integrated into **262 institutions**, as of early 2026 (per Margins). Unlike
a growing number of African schemes, the Ghana Card is **not MOSIP-aligned** ([[mosip]]). By a
Jan-2026 BoG directive it is the primary — and mostly sole — ID for financial transactions. See
[[GHA]], [[dpi.id]], [[ghana--dpi-id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Mandatory for banking / digital transactions (BoG, 2026-01-11).** [[dpi.pay]] [[gov.legislate]]
  A revised Bank of Ghana Supervisory Guidance Note ([[bank-of-ghana]]) makes the Ghana Card the
  primary — and in most cases **sole** — ID for financial onboarding, with **full biometric +
  liveness verification** for all digital onboarding and no risk-based exemption; customers without
  a card are barred. The NIA database is centred in KYC/CDD/AML. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-11 Ghana Card mandatory for banking and digital transactions - BoG]].
- **NIA operations: backlog cleared, online-only, continuous registration (2025).** [[dpi.id]]
  [[dpi.registry]] Under the **[[national-identification-authority-ghana|NIA]]**, the Mar-2023–Jan-2025
  unissued-card backlog was cleared for same-day issuance (Sep 2025, 292 centres); first-time
  applications moved online-only (May 2025); and a continuous-registration plan targets the remaining
  adults plus ~3m diaspora, 7m under-15s and 10,000 refugees, with a Births & Deaths Registry / GHS
  pilot for ages 0-6. Three biometrics (fingerprint, face, iris). Sources:
  [[2025-09-13 NIA clears Ghana Card backlog, issues instant card nationwide]],
  [[2025-05-06 Ghana takes first-time national ID card requests online (plus new chip-embedded passport)]],
  [[2025-05-01 NIA to capture 7 million under-15s and 3 million Ghanaians abroad for Ghana Card]].
- **SIM linkage and the multi-card gap (2025).** [[dpi.id]] The card anchors a planned biometric
  SIM-registration framework (SIMs linked to the NIA database via a CEIR) and the NIA PIN has
  replaced the GRA TIN since April 2021 — yet critics note the card has "yet to realize its full
  potential," with citizens still holding multiple credentials and unrefreshed 2018/2019 enrolment
  data. See [[ghana--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-20 National ID system yet to realize full potential (Ghana Card)]].
- **Payment-functionality claim RETRACTED (2026-04-01).** [[dpi.pay]] TechCabal
  published, then **retracted and removed**, a story titled *"Ghana's National ID
  cards can now make payments."* On further verification the outlet found the claim
  **incorrect** — Ghana Cards do **not** support payment functionality — and issued a
  full retraction taking responsibility for the error. Recorded here so the wiki does
  **not** carry the reversed claim; any page asserting Ghana-Card payment capability
  should be treated as unsupported. Source:
  [[2026-04-01 RETRACTION Ghana's National ID cards can now make payments]].
- **Producer profile — Margins ID Group / ICPS (2026-01-28).** [[dpi.id]] The card is produced
  by **[[margins-id-group]]**'s manufacturing arm **Intelligent Card Production Systems (ICPS)** —
  the only West African facility holding **ISO 14298** government-level security-printing
  certification and Mastercard/Visa/Europay-certified for high-security payment cards — making
  the Ghana Card Africa's first domestically-produced foundational ID credential. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Margins ID Group emerges as Africa's leading provider of certified digital identity solutions]].
- **Ghana Card model exported to The Gambia (2026-01-20).** Margins — architect and operator of
  Ghana's National Identification System (19.2m registered aged 15+, 262 institutions) — won a
  DBFOT contract to build **The Gambia**'s national + integrated digital ID system, the first
  African-owned firm to export a full national ID system. The Gambia's interior minister called
  "the Ghana Card system an example for Africa." See [[GMB]], [[gambia-national-id-contract]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-01-20 Margins takes Ghana's ID success to The Gambia]].

- **Ghana Card as the "sole financial identifier" (analysis, 2025-08).** In Samora Kariuki's
  six-country DPI-maturity read (Ghana joint-top, ~40/60), the Ghana Card is scored as Ghana's
  **sole financial identifier** underpinning fully interoperable mobile-money and bank onboarding —
  i.e. the **identity/KYC anchor** of the payments stack, not a payment instrument itself (cf. the
  2026-04 retraction above). Kariuki flags Ghana's weaker data-exchange layer and the e-levy cost
  barrier. One analyst's rubric, attributed to Kariuki. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-08-04 93 - Ecosystem Debt or Generative Platforms A Case for Digital Public Infrastructure]].
