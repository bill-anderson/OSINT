# Ghana Card

Ghana's national identity card, issued by the National Identification Authority
(NIA) and built and operated by Ghana's **[[margins-id-group]]** under a DBFOT arrangement.
It is the country's foundational ID credential; as of early 2026 Margins reports **19.2 million
Ghanaians aged 15+** registered and the system integrated into **262 institutions**. Unlike a
growing number of African schemes, the Ghana Card is **not MOSIP-aligned** ([[mosip]]). See
[[GHA]], [[dpi.id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Payment-functionality claim RETRACTED (2026-04-01).** [[dpi.pay]] TechCabal
  published, then **retracted and removed**, a story titled *"Ghana's National ID
  cards can now make payments."* On further verification the outlet found the claim
  **incorrect** — Ghana Cards do **not** support payment functionality — and issued a
  full retraction taking responsibility for the error. Recorded here so the wiki does
  **not** carry the reversed claim; any page asserting Ghana-Card payment capability
  should be treated as unsupported. Source:
  [[2026-04-01 RETRACTION Ghana's National ID cards can now make payments]].
- **Producer profile — Margins ID Group / ICPS (2026-01-28).** [[dpi.id]] The card is produced
  by **[[margins-id-group]]**'s manufacturing arm **Intelligent Card Production Systems (ICPS)** —
  the only West African facility holding **ISO 14298** government-level security-printing
  certification and Mastercard/Visa/Europay-certified for high-security payment cards — making
  the Ghana Card Africa's first domestically-produced foundational ID credential. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Margins ID Group emerges as Africa's leading provider of certified digital identity solutions]].
- **Ghana Card model exported to The Gambia (2026-01-20).** Margins — architect and operator of
  Ghana's National Identification System (19.2m registered aged 15+, 262 institutions) — won a
  DBFOT contract to build **The Gambia**'s national + integrated digital ID system, the first
  African-owned firm to export a full national ID system. The Gambia's interior minister called
  "the Ghana Card system an example for Africa." See [[GMB]], [[gambia-national-id-contract]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-01-20 Margins takes Ghana's ID success to The Gambia]].

- **Ghana Card as the "sole financial identifier" (analysis, 2025-08).** In Samora Kariuki's
  six-country DPI-maturity read (Ghana joint-top, ~40/60), the Ghana Card is scored as Ghana's
  **sole financial identifier** underpinning fully interoperable mobile-money and bank onboarding —
  i.e. the **identity/KYC anchor** of the payments stack, not a payment instrument itself (cf. the
  2026-04 retraction above). Kariuki flags Ghana's weaker data-exchange layer and the e-levy cost
  barrier. One analyst's rubric, attributed to Kariuki. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-08-04 93 - Ecosystem Debt or Generative Platforms A Case for Digital Public Infrastructure]].
