# Nigeria Government Enterprise Architecture (NGEA)

Nigeria's whole-of-government **enterprise-architecture framework and shared
infrastructure** for interoperability and data exchange across ministries,
departments and agencies (MDAs). It is a core plank of Nigeria's **e-Government
Masterplan 2.0 (Ne-GMP 2.0)**, designed to align digital initiatives with national
priorities, cut duplication, and turn policy into executable digital outcomes. See
[[NGA]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.standards]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **NITDA assumes control (Abuja handover, 2026-04-07).** [[nitda]] formally took over
  the NGEA infrastructure at a handover ceremony in Abuja, giving it a structured
  mandate over cross-government IT investment and information-resource management. The
  platform was **built over ~2.5 years** by Nigerian technical teams with **Korea's
  KOICA** (Korea International Cooperation Agency); with it now operational, government
  platforms are designed for **interoperability and seamless data exchange** across
  MDAs. See [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-07 NITDA Assumes Control Of NGEA Infrastructure]].
- **Pilots and hosting.** Pilot programmes have run at the **[[nimc]]**, the Nigeria
  Customs Service, the Nigeria Immigration Service and NITDA. The infrastructure is
  **hosted by [[galaxy-backbone]]**. The Federal Ministry of Communications, Innovation
  and Digital Economy provides policy oversight while NITDA scales adoption.
  (Korea-funded — no geopol tag applies.)
