# Liberia Data Protection Bill (Personal Information Act)

Liberia's first comprehensive personal-data-protection law, in progress: **"An Act for the Collection,
Processing, Transmission, Storage, Protection, and Use of Personal Information in Liberia."** Its
enactment is the missing piece named repeatedly across Liberia's digital push — biometric ID mandates,
the [[osd-international|OSD]] concession, and EO 163 all advance while the country still has **no
dedicated data-protection law or authority** ([[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]]). See [[LBR]],
[[liberia--dpi-id]].

## Status (as of 2026-07)

- **Origin + drafting.** Submitted to the Legislature by President Boakai; developed by the **Ministry
  of Posts and Telecommunications (MoPT)** with **Internews** under the EU-funded **Liberia Media
  Empowerment Project (LMEP)**. Validated by dozens of stakeholders in Monrovia in **December 2024**.
- **Scope.** A standalone comprehensive instrument governing processing of personal data by individuals
  and entities handling the data of any resident in Liberia, the ECOWAS region "or beyond," binding
  **data controllers and processors**. On enactment it would **take precedence over all existing laws**
  on personal data and impose penalties for unauthorised access/processing and for **concealing
  breaches**. It aligns with the **ECOWAS Supplementary Act on Personal Data Protection** (Feb 2010).
- **Legislative progress.** On **10 June 2025** the House of Representatives referred the bill to its
  **Committee on Post and Telecommunication** for review within two weeks. MoPT Minister Sekou M. Kromah
  and EU Ambassador Nona Deprez backed it. Source:
  [[2025-06-11-house-reviews-privacy-data-protection-bill-observer]].
