# UCL Institute for Innovation and Public Purpose (IIPP)

University College London department reframing the state as a market-shaper; academic
home of **[[david-eaves]]** and the **DPI Map** research project.

## Activity in this wiki

- **DPI conceptualisation & measurement (WP 2025-01).** Eaves & **Krisstina Rao** develop
  a grounded-theory framework to *define* DPI (technology, public-interest values,
  adoption context) and *measure* it in real implementations — feeding the global "DPI
  Map." Provides the conceptual scaffolding behind the sources documenting African DPI
  builds ([[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]]). See
  [[2025-01-01 dpi_conceptualisation_and_measurement]].
- **Public-value framing of DPI (2026).** Directed by **[[mariana-mazzucato]]**, IIPP is
  the home of [[david-eaves]]'s DPI Map and the 2026 DPI public-value paper, bringing its
  market-shaping / public-value / common-good frameworks to DPI. Sources:
  [[2026-04-03 Digital public infrastructure and the economics of public value what is 'public' about DPI]],
  [[2026-01-01 David Eaves Personal Site]].
- **State of DPI Report / DPI Map baseline (2026-01-16).** IIPP's DPI Map published the
  first **State of DPI Report**, establishing a global baseline — **64 digital IDs, 97
  digital payment systems, 103 data-exchange systems** worldwide; of 288 registered
  DPI-community participants only **6 from Africa**, with the top community concern being
  **DPI excluding vulnerable populations** ([[dpi-map]]). See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-02 Offering a Window into the DPI Community Challenges]].

- **DPI Map methodology & codebook captured (2026-07).** The [[dpi-map]]'s "Measuring DPI"
  framework (6 attributes; 38 ID / 20 payments / 20 data-exchange outcome indicators) and its
  classifying codebook (23 ID / 22 payments / 18 data-exchange variables) rest on the IIPP's
  **Eaves & Rao (2025) WP 2025-01**. See [[dpi-map]], [[david-eaves]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-12 Methodology - DPI Map]], [[2026-07-12 Codebook - DPI Map]].
