# Angola–Huawei digital-health MoU

A digital-health memorandum of understanding between Angola's **Ministry of Health (MINSA)** and **[[huawei]]**, signed in **Shenzhen on 26 January 2026** during health minister **Sílvia Lutucuta**'s week-long China visit — the China pole of Angola's digital-health build. Signed by MINSA planning/statistics (GEPE) acting director Pedro Duarte Gabriel and Huawei Angola vice-CEO **Qian Lei**.

## Terms

- Support for **training ~38,000 health workers**; **500,000+ remote medical consultations over four years**.
- **Progressive digitisation** of hospitals and health units nationwide.
- **Telemedicine, tele-nursing, digital imaging and AI applied to health**; national ICT-in-health talent development.
- Framed within construction of a new tertiary reference hospital and the Health Human-Resources Training Programme (PFRHS), with distance nursing-specialisation training across 12 provinces.

Captured as a **deal/MoU**; capture is not endorsement. The China-vendor role in the national health-data stack carries a data-[[sovereignty]] question given the MoU spans hospital data centres, connectivity and AI.
