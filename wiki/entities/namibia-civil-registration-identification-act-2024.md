# Namibia Civil Registration and Identification Act, 2024

Namibia's consolidated **civil-registration and identity statute** (Act 13 of 2024, assented 30 December
2024). It establishes a single **Civil Register** — Birth, Marriage, Death and Identity registers — with a
system of **e-notices** for civil events, a **unique identifier** assigned to every registered person, and
**biometric identity documents**, all administered by the Registrar-General under the
**[[ministry-home-affairs-namibia|Ministry of Home Affairs]]**. It modernises the 1996 Identification Act and
makes the Civil Register the sole national identity register for verification.

The Act is the legal foundation for Namibia's **[[namibia-digital-id|national digital-ID rollout]]**.
Reviewers noted it "reflects key data-protection aspects," but digital-rights analysts (APC / Hubbard) warned
that its **biometric data collection** runs ahead of a still-weak national data-protection regime — a
privacy-governance gap to watch.
