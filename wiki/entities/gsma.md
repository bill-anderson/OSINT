# GSMA

Global mobile-operators' industry body; its annual **Mobile Economy Africa**
report and affordability/AI initiatives are a primary evidence base on the
continent's connectivity and usage gaps.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Mobile Economy Africa 2026.** Mobile contributed **$240bn (7.8% of GDP)** and
  ~13m jobs in 2025; the binding problem has shifted from **coverage (9% gap)** to
  **usage (63% gap)**. Operator revenue $60.5bn → $90.3bn by 2030; **$76bn**
  network investment 2025–2030. Handset Affordability Coalition pushing to remove
  taxes on sub-$100 phones (a $40-handset pilot in six countries); with the **G6
  operators**, a continent-wide **African-language AI model** collaboration (first
  open Swahili reasoning model). See [[include.divides]], [[include.access]],
  [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-18 Africa's $240 Billion Mobile Economy A Checkpoint on Africa's Digital Transformation Journey]].
- **Satellite cross-subsidy warning.** Cited in [[2026-06-16 Who Owns Africa's Sky]] on the
  **$77bn** operators are due to invest 2024–2030 and the risk LEO entrants pose
  to the economics that fund rural expansion. See [[infra.connect]], [[starlink]].
- **Partner2Connect pledge (2026-07).** GSMA pledged to the [[itu]] [[partner2connect]]
  coalition an **African AI Language Models** project — AI systems reflecting African
  languages, cultures and knowledge (extending the G6/African-language-model thread above).
  See [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 ITU's Partner2Connect crosses $100 billion in global connectivity pledges]].
- **Mobile-money data as AI-risk evidence base (2026-04).** GSMA figures anchor an ICT4D
  evidence audit: **2.1bn registered accounts** processed **108bn transactions worth
  $1.68tn** in 2024 (Sub-Saharan Africa holds >1bn accounts), **44%** of providers now offer
  credit, and the mobile-money gender gap widened (women **36%** less likely to hold an
  account). The audit calls on the **GSMA Mobile Money programme** to condition operator
  membership on anonymised data-sharing for research and false-positive audits of deployed AI
  fraud systems. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.divides]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 3 Reasons Mobile Money Programs Have a $1.7 Trillion Problem]].
- **State of the Industry: Mobile Money 2026 (2026-03-24).** GSMA's flagship report
  put 2025 global mobile-money value at **$2.091tn** across **2.3bn** registered
  accounts; **Africa held ~66%** of value (~**$1.432tn**, +27% y/y), ~74% of
  transactions and **52% of accounts (~1.2bn, 347m monthly-active)**. It stresses the
  adoption ceiling: SSA smartphone ownership just **33%**, low digital-financial
  literacy, and women **14%** less likely to use mobile internet (885m women still
  unconnected). See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.divides]], [[include.access]],
  [[2026-03-01 GSMA State of the Industry Report on Mobile Money 2026]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-24 Mobile Money in Africa Strong Growth, but Adoption Still Falls Short]].
- **UNECA DPI roundtable (2026-04-13).** At the 58th [[uneca]] ECA Conference of
  Ministers, GSMA's Angela Wamola pressed for the removal of duties on entry-level
  smartphones to close the usage gap — ~1bn Africans remain offline despite living
  within reach of a mobile signal. See [[include.access]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].
- **Ethiopia digital-economy projection (2026-01).** GSMA figures anchor the Horn-of-Africa
  digital story: Ethiopia's telecom reforms are projected to lift the digital economy's
  contribution to **~$10bn (ETB 1.3tn) of GDP by 2028**, unlocking jobs and mobile-money/financial
  inclusion. See [[ETH]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-07 Why Ethiopia, Somalia and Djibouti are starting to matter in digital Africa]].
- **Power as the next constraint (2026).** The network and data-centre buildout GSMA tracks
  runs into an energy squeeze: African DC electricity demand is growing ~**20–25%/yr** while
  ~**600m** Africans lack power, with the World Bank/AfDB **Mission 300** targeting 300m new
  connections. See [[infra.energy]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-05 Can Africa's Power Grid Withstand the Next Wave of Emerging Technologies]].

- **The "voice era" of inclusion (2026-01).** GSMA framing of a feature-phone "voice era"
  anchors the case for voice/NLP AI reaching non-smartphone users: an early GSMA study of
  Zambia's **Ask Viamo Anything** found **59% of users were women**, with higher engagement
  than legacy IVR. See [[include.access]], [[capacity.literacy]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].

- **ICT4D investment/jobs baseline (Russell Southwood, 2026-04-16).** Southwood's
  ICT4D retrospective uses GSMA's estimate that **MNOs invested >US$35bn in
  2000–2007** and that mobile employed **>100,000** (Heeks, by 2008/9), against
  GSMA's present-day **1.5m direct + 2.2m indirect** jobs — the long-run evidence
  base for mobile's structural role, even as network effects re-created monopolies
  (M-Pesa, MoMo). See [[tech.industry]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 The Long View - What ICT4D did for Africa and why that deep past matters for today]].

- **Digital Africa Summit Ghana report (2025-09-03).** [[gov.policy]] At its Digital Africa Summit
  (Accra, 3 Sep 2025) GSMA launched *Driving Digital Transformation of the Economy in Ghana*:
  strategic reforms could add ~**$3.4bn** by 2030; mobile is already **8% of GDP (GHS 94bn)** but a
  **62% usage gap** persists despite 99% 4G coverage. Barriers centre on device affordability
  (smartphone = 27% of monthly GDP/capita; 76% for the poorest 40%); it praises the e-levy removal and
  calls for device-financing PPPs, a 5G roadmap and rights-of-way reform, targeting 20.6m unique
  mobile-internet users by 2029 (from 13.1m). Tied to Ghana's RESET agenda. See [[GHA]],
  [[include.divides]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-03 New GSMA report at Digital Africa Summit Ghana - digital reforms could add $3.4bn to the economy by 2030]].

- **Mobile Net Zero 2026 — renewables access as the emerging-market brake (2026-07-17).**
  [[infra.energy]] GSMA's climate flagship (**[[gsma-mobile-net-zero-2026]]**) argues the mobile
  sector's decarbonisation now turns on **access to renewable electricity in emerging markets**, and
  presses governments to liberalise electricity markets, streamline clean-energy permitting and
  **recognise communications networks as critical infrastructure in national resilience planning** —
  the same asks African operators make of their own grids. Two findings bear directly on Africa: the
  **100 largest towercos ran ~4m sites and burned >2bn litres of diesel in 2024**, with **fewer than
  a quarter** holding validated targets **(as of the 2026 edition)**; and renewable shares are
  concentrated in rich markets (**Europe ~70%, North America ~50%, Latin America ~45% in 2024**),
  with the global operator average at **24% (2024, up from 10% in 2019)**. Regional figures for
  Africa are not broken out in the material held. *Cite, don't absorb* — global panel figures stay on
  the report page. See [[infra.energy]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-gsma-mobile-net-zero-renewable-energy]].
