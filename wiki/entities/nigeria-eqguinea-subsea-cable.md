# Nigeria–Equatorial Guinea subsea fibre agreement

**Deal (signed 2026-02).** A landmark subsea fibre-optic agreement between Nigeria ([[NGA]])
and Equatorial Guinea ([[GNQ]]) to create a direct high-capacity link across West and
Central Africa, aimed at broadband capacity, cybersecurity and reduced reliance on external
connections.

## Terms

- **Parties.** [[backbone-connectivity-network-nigeria]], represented by CEO Ibrahim Dikko,
  and Equatorial Guinea's Minister of Transport, Telecommunications and AI Systems,
  Honorato Evita Oma. Signed in Malabo, Equatorial Guinea, in February 2026.
- **Scope.** A direct high-capacity subsea link plus construction of a **commercial data
  centre**; intended to cut the detour of data through European servers and localise storage.
- **Status.** Technical specifications, investment figures and project timelines not
  disclosed as of signing.
- **Framing.** Regional redundancy and **data sovereignty** — Equatorial Guinea has
  historically relied heavily on the Africa Coast to Europe (ACE) cable, while Nigeria
  already has seven major subsea cables (e.g. Equiano, 2Africa). Backed rhetorically by
  Nigeria's Foreign Minister Yusuf Tuggar as African-led digital infrastructure. Tags
  [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]].
