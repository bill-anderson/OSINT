# The burden of recording and reporting health data in primary health care facilities in five low- and lower-middle income countries

Peer-reviewed article by **Siyam, Ir, York, Antwi, … Boerma and O'Neill**, *BMC Health
Services Research* **21(Suppl 1):691 (2021)**. Measures the data-recording and -reporting
workload across **80 primary health care (PHC) facilities** in **Cambodia, Ghana ([[GHA]]),
Mozambique ([[MOZ]]), Nigeria ([[NGA]]) and Tanzania ([[TZA]])**, fieldwork **2016–17**.
Cited here as literature; a later dated fact drawn from it becomes a source citing this page.
Part of the HMIS/[[dhis2]] evidence cluster ([[dpi.mis]]).

## Argument

- The **recording burden is severe**: an average of **34 registers per country**, with
  recording consuming roughly **one-third of consultation time** — data work crowding out
  care ([[capacity.training]], [[data.statistics]]).
- **Reporting burden:** an average of **35 monthly reporting forms**, taking from **4 hours
  (Mozambique, Nigeria)** to **13–15 hours (Tanzania, Ghana)** per health worker per month.
- Calls for **international standards for facility registers and reporting forms**
  ([[gov.standards]]) to cut duplication — a systems-design critique complementing the
  cluster's data-*use* findings.
