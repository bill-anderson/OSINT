# Somalia Unified Digital Civil Registration Service (CRVS)

Somalia's national digital civil registration and vital statistics system, owned by
[[somalia-moifar]] (Ministry of Interior, Federal Affairs and Reconciliation) and built on
the [[opencrvs]] digital public good, with UNICEF and Germany support. It digitally
registers births, marriages, divorces and deaths, storing records in a centralised national
database and integrating (planned/underway) with the national ID ([[snid]]), healthcare,
immigration and education.

## Trajectory

- **10 Aug 2025** — pilot launched across **19 districts** (Jubbaland, Southwest,
  Hirshabelle, Galmudug, Northeastern State) on Africa CRVS Day; districts equipped with
  tablets. Baseline: only **3% of under-fives** have a registered birth and **0.3%** hold a
  birth certificate (2020 DHS) — among the world's lowest.
- **16 Jan 2026** — OpenCRVS-based system at production level across **30 districts / 160
  hospitals**, with **28,000+ births** registered at point of occurrence.

Predecessor/related: the CRMVS system built and handed over by Pakistan's NADRA (Nov 2024).
