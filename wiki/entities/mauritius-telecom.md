# Mauritius Telecom (MT)

Mauritius's incumbent telecom operator (CEO **Veemal Gungadin**), positioning the island as a **digital gateway
between Africa and Asia**. Its **2026-2029 strategy, "Bridging Africa & Asia"** (e-booklet published Jan 2026;
launched at the **MT Connect** event, 5 Dec 2025), is a reported **three-year Rs 20 billion (~US$434 million)**
programme structured around **four corridors**:

- **Connectivity Corridor** — resilient low-latency Africa–Asia links; expanding fibre, **5G-Advanced**, and new
  submarine cables **T4** and **SEACOM 2.0** (complementing SAFE/LION/T3);
- **Financial Corridor** — secure cross-border transaction infrastructure, scaling **my.t money** (130,000+
  active users) into a cross-border payment solution ([[dpi.pay]]);
- **AI & Compute Corridor** — sovereign data/cloud/AI services, GPU clusters, Tier IV data-centre expansion
  ([[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]]);
- **Innovation & Scale-Up Corridor** — "Build in Mauritius, Scale across continents."

Two national initiatives sit alongside it: **AI Enablement for All** and a **Digital Talent Pipeline**.

**Rose-Belle Tier IV data centre.** MT's **Rose-Belle** facility (Tier IV-certified) is now framed as national
critical infrastructure and the sovereign-hosting anchor of the Blueprint's ~80 projects — hosting **e-Health**,
the **new government portal**, **[[mauritius-mobile-id|Mobile ID]]**, the penalty-points licence system and
government **AI** workloads, with redundancy to the ageing **Government Online Centre (GOC)** in Ébène (created
2005; ~80 racks, under pressure). See the **[[mauritius--infra-store]]** intersection.

**Partnerships.** MT signed an **MoU with SIL** (working session, Ébène, ~30 May 2026) to accelerate digital
transformation across citizens, government, parastatals and private enterprise, and to explore scaling solutions
across African markets (PR-wire; SIL's identity/scope and the MoU's terms warrant primary confirmation). Source:
[[2026-05-30-sil-mauritius-telecom-mou-techreviewafrica]].
