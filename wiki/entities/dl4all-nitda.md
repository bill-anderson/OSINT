# Digital Literacy for All (DL4ALL)

DL4ALL is [[nitda]]'s flagship national digital-literacy programme, a core component of the
agency's Strategic Roadmap and Action Plan. It targets **70% digital literacy by 2027 and 95%
by 2030** and is positioned within President [[bola-tinubu]]'s Renewed Hope Agenda. DG:
[[kashifu-inuwa]].

## Key facts

- **Three delivery tracks:** an informal-sector programme, an education-sector programme
  (embedding digital skills in learning institutions), and a workforce-readiness programme.
- The **informal-sector track has trained >480,000 Nigerians across 30 states and the FCT
  since Sept 2024**. See [[capacity.training]], [[capacity.literacy]].
- **Disability inclusion (2026-01-23).** NITDA completed a two-day DL4ALL training for **50
  persons with disabilities (PWDs)** in Abuja, delivered with the Inclusive Friends
  Association (IFA) and SIMBED — framed as proof that intentional, accessible training works.
  See [[include.access]], [[include.divides]].
