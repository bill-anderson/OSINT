# Cameroon Cybercrime & Cybersecurity Law (Law 2010/012)

**Law n°2010/012 of 21 Dec 2010** on cybercrime and cybersecurity — the framework governing online
interactions and data in Cameroon, and the object of a **civil-society rights critique** that it is used
against speech.

## Profile

- **Rights critique (DIPES workshop, Jun 2026).** A Digital Policy Engagement Series workshop (Yaoundé,
  8–9 Jun 2026), convened by **[[paradigm-initiative]]** with Civic Watch Cameroon, analysed the law's
  limits: provisions meant to protect the public also **restrict freedom of expression**, and it is
  invoked to justify measures such as the **23 Oct 2025 election-period internet restrictions** —
  read by lawyers and civil society as violating the ICCPR and Articles 9 & 19 of the African Charter.
  The counterweight to the wiki's otherwise capability-building framing of CMR cybersecurity: the same
  statutory apparatus that signs the `.cm` zone also underpins shutdowns. Source:
  [[2026-06-16-lejour-cybercrime-laws-threaten-freedoms]].
