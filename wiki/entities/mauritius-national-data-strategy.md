# Mauritius National Data Strategy 2025-2029

Mauritius's national data-governance strategy, launched on **28 January 2026** (World Data Protection Day) at
Ébène under the theme **"A New Era of Trust,"** led by the **[[ministry-ict-mauritius|Ministry of IT,
Communication and Innovation (MITCI)]]** with the **[[data-protection-office-mauritius|Data Protection Office]]**.
It sets the long-term vision for **responsible data governance, protection of personal data, and the strategic
use of data as a development lever**, with a stated aim of building public trust while supporting innovation.
It sits alongside the **[[mauritius-digital-blueprint-2025-2029|Digital Transformation Blueprint 2025-2029]]** as
the data-governance pillar of Mauritius's digital programme.

Announced at launch:
- A **Freedom of Information Bill** in preparation (Commissioner Madhub framing access to information as central
  to modern democracy and the DPO as a facilitator of transparency) — see [[data.open]].
- A signed **MoU between the Mauritius Police Force, the DPO and CERT-MU** on data protection, cybercrime and
  incident response.
- Explicit attention to **online risks to minors** (Minister Ramtohul citing Australia's under-16 and France's
  under-15 social-media restrictions).
