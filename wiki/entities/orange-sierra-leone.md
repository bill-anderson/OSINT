# Orange Sierra Leone

Sierra Leone mobile operator, the local subsidiary of [[orange]] (via the Sonatel Group, in
which Orange holds ~42%). Formerly **Airtel Sierra Leone**, acquired in 2016 and rebranded.
CEO (2025): **Sekou Amadou Bah**. Operates **Orange Money Sierra Leone** (CEO David
Mansaray), a mobile-money provider connected to the national instant-payment switch.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Bo backup data centre (€23m / $26.7m, commissioned Dec 2025).** A full disaster-recovery
  replica of Orange's Freetown data centre, in Bo City; President Julius Maada Bio attended
  the launch, calling it a milestone in the country's digital transformation. See
  [[infra.store]], [[SLE]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-08 Orange launches backup data center in Sierra Leone (Bo)]].
- **Freetown data centre (2018).** Orange's primary facility — 940 sqm, capacity for 154
  racks across 350 sqm; site broken ground by then-Airtel in 2016. Source:
  [[2025-12-08 Orange launches backup data center in Sierra Leone (Bo)]].
- **Instant-payment switch participant (2025-02).** Orange Money is one of two MNOs
  connected at the launch of the [[sierra-leone-instant-payment-switch]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-14 Sierra Leone Launches National Instant Payment System]].
