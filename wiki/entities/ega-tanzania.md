# e-Government Authority (eGA), Tanzania

Tanzania's **central e-government body** — the institution behind the national e-Government
strategies and the government's shared digital infrastructure (GovNet, government data centres,
interoperability). Originally the **e-Government Agency**, it became a **fully-fledged
e-Government Authority in 2019**, alongside the **e-Government Act 2019** and its **2020
Regulations** — a statutory upgrade from agency to authority.

## Activity in this wiki

- **e-Government Strategy 2022 (covering 2022–2027).** Tanzania's **second** national
  e-Government Strategy, published under the **President's Office – Public Service Management
  and Good Governance (PO-PSMGG)**, replacing the **e-Government Strategy 2013** (which ran
  2013–2018 and expired). Built on a stakeholder survey of **379 respondents across 38
  institutions** and organised around **seven strategic pillars**. See
  [[tanzania-e-government-strategy-2022]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2022-01-01 Tanzania e-Government Strategy 2022 (2022-2027)]].
- **The problems it names (2022).** The strategy is candid about the constraints on its own
  starting point: **non-interoperable service delivery systems**, unharmonised business
  processes, **limited GovNet bandwidth**, **inadequate data centre resources**, **rising
  cyber-attacks**, and **ICT skills gaps**. A government's own diagnosis of why its services
  don't join up is unusually useful evidence — and it is the interoperability gap, not the
  connectivity gap, that leads the list. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[infra.store]]. Source: [[2022-01-01 Tanzania e-Government Strategy 2022 (2022-2027)]].
