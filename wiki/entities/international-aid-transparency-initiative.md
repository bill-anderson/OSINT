# International Aid Transparency Initiative (IATI)

A **global open-data standard and datastore** for development and humanitarian aid
resources and results. Publishers report activity- and transaction-level data to a common
schema, making IATI the primary open dataset for tracing who funds what, where. A standing
OSINT asset for grounding aid- and donor-flow claims (including into African recipient
countries) against reported primary data rather than press releases.

## Why it's a standing OSINT asset

The **[[data.open]]** / **[[data.statistics]]** reference for aid flows: it lets the wiki
attribute funding to specific donors and recipients with dated, activity-level granularity,
subject to the standard's well-documented reporting idiosyncrasies (double-counting,
inconsistent sector coding). Data quality across publishers is assessed by
**[[publish-what-you-fund]]**.

## Key reference figures (captured — do NOT promote into concept/place pages)

- **2,000+ publishing agencies** (early 2026); the full datastore is **~13.7 GB (~2.4bn
  words)**; first publisher was **UK DFID in 2011**.
- A 2026 PWYF worked example (Ethiopia 2025 food aid): ~**$456M** under sector codes
  72040/52010, incl. Germany BMZ $71.3M and USAID $43.5M — held here as illustration only.

## How to use / cite

Query the IATI Datastore for a specific, dated fact, then file **that** as a normal source
citing this resource — e.g. donor X committed $Y to country Z per IATI as of a given date.
Mind the interpretation caveats above; verify anomalies against publisher documentation.
