# MEDUSA submarine cable

MEDUSA is a Mediterranean subsea cable network spanning more than 8,000 km and connecting close to
13 countries across Europe, North Africa and the Middle East. It is supported by the European
Union and led by the Spanish company AFR-IX, and is described as a Global Gateway flagship for
Mediterranean connectivity.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Tunisia connection financed via the [[ebrd]] loan (2026-01).** [[tunisie-telecom]]'s
  up-to-€190m EBRD development-linked loan includes financing to connect Tunisia to MEDUSA,
  enhancing the country's international connectivity and positioning it as a Mediterranean digital
  hub. Tags [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 EBRD and Tunisie Telecom Partner on €190 Million Digital Infrastructure Upgrade]].

- **Bizerte landing (Nov 2025).** **[[tunisie-telecom]]** held an official ceremony (1 Nov 2025) marking
  the **landing of Medusa in Bizerte** — a **1,000+ km, 22 Tbps Bizerte–Marseille fibre pair** it said
  increases its international capacity **eightfold**. CEO Lassâad Ben Dhiab framed it as positioning Tunisia
  as a "central player in Mediterranean digital sovereignty." Medusa: 8,700 km, EU-supported, led by AFR-IX
  Telecom. Source: [[2025-11-03-zawya-tunisie-telecom-medusa-landing-bizerte]].
- **ViaTunisia segment live (Jun 2026).** **[[orange-tunisia|Orange]]** announced the **ViaTunisia**
  France–Tunisia segment **operational** — landing partner in both Marseille and Bizerte; open point-to-
  point, 25-year design life; **co-financed by the EU (CEF Digital, 30% of costs)**. Orange states it
  **owns the Tunisian section and the Bizerte landing station** (17 landing points across the Med). Sources:
  [[2026-06-05-connectingafrica-viatunisia-subsea-live]], [[2026-06-26-techafricanews-orange-medusa-cable-operational]].
- **⚠ Ownership discrepancy (CONTRA-TUN-001).** Tunisie Telecom (Nov 2025) and Orange Tunisia (Jun 2026)
  **both present the Bizerte Medusa landing as their own** — TT as landing a 22 Tbps pair (with EBRD
  financing for "the Tunisia connection"), Orange as owner of the Tunisian section and the Bizerte landing
  station. Held unresolved; see [[tunisia--infra-connect]].
