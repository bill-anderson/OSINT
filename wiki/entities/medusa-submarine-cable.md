# MEDUSA submarine cable

MEDUSA is a Mediterranean subsea cable network spanning more than 8,000 km and connecting close to
13 countries across Europe, North Africa and the Middle East. It is supported by the European
Union and led by the Spanish company AFR-IX, and is described as a Global Gateway flagship for
Mediterranean connectivity.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Tunisia connection financed via the [[ebrd]] loan (Board approval 2024-07-24; announced 2026-01-16).**
  [[tunisie-telecom]]'s up-to-**€190m** EBRD development-linked loan (four tranches; first committed
  tranche **€50m**) lists **"connection to the EU-supported MEDUSA submarine cable"** as the first of four
  financed capital-expenditure components. The EBRD's own board report sets the target as raising Tunisia's
  international bandwidth from **1.95 Tbit/s (2022) to 10 Tbit/s within four years** (against a SEMED
  average of **6.98 Tbit/s**). This buys **capacity on the cable**, not the landing station. Tags
  [[infra.connect]]. Source: [[2024-07-24-ebrd-board-report-project-hannibal-tunisie-telecom]] (the board
  report, the authority).

- **Bizerte landing (2025-10-31).** Medusa's **first African landing**, marked by an official ceremony
  attended by both Tunisian operators' chief executives — which is why each subsequently presented the event
  as its own. Source: [[2025-11-03-zawya-tunisie-telecom-medusa-landing-bizerte]].
- **Ownership settled (2026-07-20).** **[[orange-tunisia|Orange Tunisia]] is the landing party and owns and
  operates the Bizerte landing station** and the ~1,050 km Tunisian segment, branded **ViaTunisia** — per the
  hosting agreement signed **2023-05-11** and an EU **CEF Digital** grant of **€9.6m against a €32m total
  project budget** (exactly 30%), the project running **1 March 2023 – 31 August 2025** per the European
  Commission's own CEF project record. Maximum capacity to Orange Tunisia is **24 Tbps (2025-11)**; the
  segment reached ready-for-service **2026-06-03**. **[[tunisie-telecom]] is a capacity customer** on the same
  segment, having agreed on **2025-02-07** a dedicated Bizerte–Marseille fibre pair of **20 Tbps (2025-02)**,
  which it says raises its own international capacity eightfold (2025-11). The two accounts describe different
  roles, not rival ownership claims. The system itself is owned by **AFR-IX Telecom** and operated
  carrier-neutrally. Sources: [[2024-12-03-ec-cef-digital-viatunisia-project-record]] (the EC's own project
  record, the authority for the grant), [[2026-06-05-connectingafrica-viatunisia-subsea-live]],
  [[2026-06-26-techafricanews-orange-medusa-cable-operational]].
  - *Capacity note:* Medusa's own record gives Tunisie Telecom's pair as **20 Tbps (2025-02-10)**; trade press
    renders it 22 Tbps. The announcer of record is preferred.
  - *Grant-figure correction (2026-07-20):* the page previously carried "**€10m signed December 2022**" from
    trade press (Developing Telecoms, Computer Weekly, Submarine Networks). The EC record gives **€9.6m of
    €32m** — the trade figure is a rounding — and states a **project start of 1 March 2023 with no
    grant-agreement signature date**. The "December 2022" signature is Orange-via-press and **is not in the
    EC record**; it is no longer carried here.
