# MEDUSA submarine cable

MEDUSA is a Mediterranean subsea cable network spanning more than 8,000 km and connecting close to
13 countries across Europe, North Africa and the Middle East. It is supported by the European
Union and led by the Spanish company AFR-IX, and is described as a Global Gateway flagship for
Mediterranean connectivity.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Tunisia connection financed via the [[ebrd]] loan (2026-01).** [[tunisie-telecom]]'s
  up-to-€190m EBRD development-linked loan includes financing to connect Tunisia to MEDUSA,
  enhancing the country's international connectivity and positioning it as a Mediterranean digital
  hub. Tags [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 EBRD and Tunisie Telecom Partner on €190 Million Digital Infrastructure Upgrade]].

- **Bizerte landing (2025-10-31).** Medusa's **first African landing**, marked by an official ceremony
  attended by both Tunisian operators' chief executives — which is why each subsequently presented the event
  as its own. Source: [[2025-11-03-zawya-tunisie-telecom-medusa-landing-bizerte]].
- **Ownership settled (2026-07-20).** **[[orange-tunisia|Orange Tunisia]] is the landing party and owns and
  operates the Bizerte landing station** and the ~1,050 km Tunisian segment, branded **ViaTunisia** — per the
  hosting agreement signed **2023-05-11** and an EU **CEF Digital** grant of €10m signed December 2022 covering
  30% of construction and management costs. Maximum capacity to Orange Tunisia is **24 Tbps (2025-11)**; the
  segment reached ready-for-service **2026-06-03**. **[[tunisie-telecom]] is a capacity customer** on the same
  segment, having agreed on **2025-02-07** a dedicated Bizerte–Marseille fibre pair of **20 Tbps (2025-02)**,
  which it says raises its own international capacity eightfold (2025-11). The two accounts describe different
  roles, not rival ownership claims. The system itself is owned by **AFR-IX Telecom** and operated
  carrier-neutrally. Sources: [[2026-06-05-connectingafrica-viatunisia-subsea-live]],
  [[2026-06-26-techafricanews-orange-medusa-cable-operational]].
  - *Capacity note:* Medusa's own record gives Tunisie Telecom's pair as **20 Tbps (2025-02-10)**; trade press
    renders it 22 Tbps. The announcer of record is preferred.
