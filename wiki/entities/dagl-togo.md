# DAGL (District Autonome du Grand Lomé)

**The Autonomous District of Greater Lomé — the metropolitan authority for Togo's capital and
its surroundings, comprising 13 communes.** In this wiki it is the body driving the
modernisation of **urban addressing** (*adressage urbain*), the address layer beneath service
delivery. Secretary-General (as of 2025-12): **Tagba Atafeyinam Tchalim**. See [[TGO]],
[[digital.localgov]], [[dpi.registry]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Urban addressing modernisation (launched, reported 2025-12-18).** The government began
  modernising urban addressing **starting with the DAGL**, aiming to equip the capital and its
  surroundings with a **reliable, digital address system**. The design rests on **databases
  integrating coded and named roads** (*voies codées et nommées*) plus **precise address points
  on the metric system, each associated with an entry number**. On the ground this means
  **installing signage plaques** showing the entry number and the road code or name, and the
  **identification and certification of "doors"** (*portes*) integrated into the database. The
  process was preceded by technical works, notably **updating the geographic address database of
  the 13 communes**. See [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-18 Urban addressing - modernisation effort]].
- **Why addressing is registry infrastructure.** DAGL officials framed the objective as
  twofold: better organisation of urban space and improved city management, while "valuing local
  history and asserting the ambition of a modern Togo." Tchalim: **"The address is a central
  element of domiciliation and an essential tool for access to public and private services."** An
  address register is a precondition for the service access that [[e-id-togo]] and
  [[services-publics-togo]] promise. See [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-18 Urban addressing - modernisation effort]].

**Coverage note.** The single account held here reports **no budget, timeline, completion
target or count of addressed parcels**, and no extension plan beyond Greater Lomé.
