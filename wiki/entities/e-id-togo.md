# e-ID Togo

**Togo's foundational biometric identification programme, implemented by [[anid-togo]] on
the open-source [[mosip]] platform and financed by the [[world-bank]] through WURI-Togo
([[wuri-programme]]).** It assigns every resident a **Numéro d'Identification Unique
(NIU)** — intended as a single access key to public, private and social services spanning
education, social security, pensions, finance, health, vaccination and monetary transfers —
and consolidates a central population database. Togo **signed the MOSIP MoU in 2021**. See
[[TGO]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]].

## Legal scaffolding

The World Bank's WURI implementation report (2023-12-21) is the primary account:

- **Data protection law — September 2019.**
- **Foundational unique identification law — September 2020**, creating **[[anid-togo]]**.
- **Cybersecurity/cybercrime amendments — June 2022**, creating **ANCy**.
- **Creation of the [[ipdcp-togo]]** (Instance de Protection des Données à Caractère
  Personnel), the data protection authority.
- **February 2022 decree** establishing project governance bodies.

See [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.protect]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Enrolment reach — a dated sequence, not a coverage figure.** A Biometric Update
  explainer records **6 million unique numbers issued (as of May 2026)**, placing Togo among
  MOSIP's active African rollouts. Rollout order: proof of concept in **four regions
  (October–November 2023)**; test registrations planned **5–15 March 2024**; then **Golfe,
  Agoè-Nyivé and Savanes**; then the **Maritime region Zone 1** (Vo, Yoto, Bas-Mono, Lacs)
  from **22 November 2025**. All citizens are eligible, **including children aged 0–5**, who
  can be registered using a parent's NIU. Sources:
  [[2026-05-28 Why decentralized digital identity looks different in every country]],
  [[2025-11-19 Togo Advances Digital ID Program With New Enrollment Phase in Maritime Region]],
  [[2023-12-21 ISR - WURI Phase 2 Seq 06 (P169594)]].
- **The enrolment-count gap.** The programme's stated goal was **8 million individuals
  registered by June 2024** (as of the December-2023 ISR). **No 2025 source held here
  reports a cumulative enrolment count** — the November-2025 rollout report gives phases but
  no total. Coverage should not be inferred from the phase sequence; the 6m figure above is
  a May-2026 MOSIP-explainer datapoint, not a Togolese-government total. See
  [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-19 Togo Advances Digital ID Program With New Enrollment Phase in Maritime Region]].
- **Not the electoral roll.** The **[[ceni-togo]]** electoral register (**4.2m+ voters** at
  the April-2024 electoral census) is a **separate register** with its own revision cycle.
  It must not be read as, or added to, e-ID enrolment.
- **Exported as a model (2025-10/11).** Senegal's Ministry of Communication and Digital
  Economy sent a benchmarking delegation to Lomé to study the e-ID technical architecture and
  the country's MOSIP integration — Togo functioning as a regional reference implementation
  rather than an importer. See [[SEN]], [[gov.regional]], [[sovereignty]]. Sources:
  [[2025-10-30 Senegal Benchmarks Togo's e-ID System for Digital Transformation]],
  [[2025-11-03 Senegal taps inspiration from Togo's digital ID experience]].
- **Financing.** Biometric enrolment is financed by the World Bank under **WURI-Togo**.
  Biometric Update, citing Togo First (**2024-03-06**), reported **$72 million for Togo**.
  This is **distinct from** the World Bank's separate **$100m** broadband/skills operation of
  December 2024 ([[togo-digital-acceleration-financing]]) — the two must not be summed. See
  [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-06 1 step forward, 3 steps back for West Africa's regional digital ID projects]].
