# GGPEN — Gabinete de Gestão do Programa Espacial Nacional

Angola's **National Space Programme Management Office**, under **[[ministerio-das-telecomunicacoes-tecnologias-de-informacao-e-comunicacao-social-angola|MINTTICS]]**, run by
Director-General **Zolana João**. GGPEN is the institutional owner of Angola's space stack: it
operates **[[angosat-2]]** (from the Satellite Control and Mission Centre, MCC, in **Funda**), runs
the **[[conecta-angola]]** connectivity programme, is client-side lead on the **[[angeo-1]]**
Earth-observation satellite, and builds EO/geospatial products. (Sources also render the abbreviation
**GGPN**.)

## What it does

- **Satellite operations & connectivity** — manages ANGOSAT-2 capacity, opened to commercial ISPs
  from December 2025 via the Funda hub, and drives Conecta Angola (satellite broadband to remote
  areas). The National Space Programme is claimed among Africa's **top five** (Space in Africa, 2026).
- **Earth observation & geospatial products** — high-resolution imagery combined with **AI and
  geospatial data** for infrastructure monitoring, **asset management and land-use mapping**, sold
  into public finance, banking, oil/gas (spill detection), mining and agriculture. Its **GEDAE**
  geodata centre is the productisation vehicle. This is the registry/satellite crossover — GGPEN
  selling EO into **asset registers** and public-finance digitalisation.
- **Skills & ecosystem** — space-technology training and certification, a NewSpace startup pipeline,
  and university partnerships.

## Key partnerships

- **[[angeo-1]]** with **[[airbus]]** Defence and Space (kick-off March 2026).
- **MoU with [[unitel]]** (ANGOTIC, June 2026) on AI-driven geospatial solutions, building on a
  Feb 2026 precursor meeting.
- **MoU with [[africell]]** (June 2026) to expand ANGOSAT-2 services and the GEDAE geodata centre.
- Licensing of local satellite-ISP startups (e.g. NomaTech, Moxico) via INACOM.
