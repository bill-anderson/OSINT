# RICA (South Africa)

The **Regulation of Interception of Communications and Provision of Communication-Related
Information Act** — South Africa's lawful-interception statute and, through its mandatory SIM
registration regime, the instrument that ties every mobile connection in the country to an identity
record.

- **What it requires.** RICA sets the legal conditions for intercepting communications and
  **mandates that communications companies operate interception-capable networks**, with
  restrictions on what intercepting parties may do with the data they obtain. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 South African Digital Sovereignty at the Crossroad of Securitization and Development (Chapter 4) - Digital Sovereignty in the BRICS Countries]].
- **The compliance gap sits at the point of sale (meeting 2024-11-18, reported 2026-07-06).** A
  ministerial meeting with a SIM-packaging and logistics firm was convened over **RICA
  non-compliance in the mass distribution of improperly registered SIM cards**, described as
  undermining law enforcement and financial security. Source:
  [[2026-07-06-malatsi-starlink-lobbying-allegations]].
- **The first overhaul in nearly two decades (2026-07-20).** Mobile operators and the
  **Department of Justice and Constitutional Development (DoJ&CD)**
  developed proposed reforms replacing document presentation with **real-time identity verification
  against the Department of Home Affairs** — the point at which SIM registration stops being a paper
  process and becomes a consumer of the national digital-ID stack. Full case:
  [[south-africa--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-20-south-africa-sim-registration-rica-digital-id]].
