# East African Community (EAC)

The East African Community is a regional economic bloc pursuing harmonised cross-border data governance across its member states. It maintains an EAC Data Governance Framework and is building shared institutions among its national data-protection authorities.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Body for national DPAs to align cross-border data governance (as of 2026-05-16).** The EAC created a body for its eight national data-protection authorities to share knowledge, align rules and collaborate on cross-border data governance, advised by Mateo Garcia Silva and Rose Mosero. Source: [[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].
- **AI-sovereignty bloc push (2026-03-31).** At the 4th EAC Regional Science, Technology and Innovation Conference in Kigali, the bloc explored a regional AI Policy Roadmap to harmonise rules and values across its eight members (Burundi, DRC, Kenya, Rwanda, Somalia, South Sudan, Tanzania, Uganda), framed as building a unified platform for African "AI sovereignty." Rwanda's [[paula-ingabire]] keynoted on data sovereignty — data staying in the region, citizens' rights protected by local laws, and AI's economic value accruing to East Africans. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]], [[RWA]]. Source: [[2026-03-31 EAC moves to create unified AI Sovereignty bloc]].
