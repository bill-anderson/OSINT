# ELECAM (Elections Cameroon)

Cameroon's **electoral management body** (Elections Cameroun), which runs the country's
**biometric voter register** — a second, election-side biometric identity system distinct
from the DGSN/CNI stack ([[dgsn-cameroon]]) and the BUNEC/CRVS reform ([[bunec]]).
Director-General: Eric Essousse.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Third-generation biometric enrolment kits (May 2025).** ELECAM took delivery of **1,000
  new 3rd-generation biometric enrolment kits** (presented 7 May 2025) — laptop, fingerprint
  scanner, camera, Bluetooth printer, GPS, solar-powered autonomy in a ruggedised case, built
  "to international standards" and costing "several billion FCFA" (exact figure undisclosed).
  They replace the ageing **1st/2nd-generation kits acquired in 2012 and 2017**, still in use
  "despite the end of their life-cycle." Trainer training ran 7–11 May 2025 ahead of communal
  deployment across the ten regions. Source:
  [[2025-05-08-lejour-elecam-acquires-1000-biometric-enrolment-kits]].
- **Register-integrity and exclusion harm (2024–25).** In the 2024 registration campaign,
  **~12,000 Cameroonians holding registration receipts were left off the national file over
  fingerprint-capture failures** — later reinstated after a public outcry. Opposition leader
  Maurice Kamto (MRC) alleged the exclusions were deliberate; the poor capture is blamed partly
  on the **2012-vintage kits**. This is the antecedent to the May-2025 kit purchase, and the
  hardest inclusion-harm evidence in the CMR corpus. Source:
  [[2025-01-31-cameroon-gabon-uganda-biometric-voter-registers-integrity-biometricupdate]].
