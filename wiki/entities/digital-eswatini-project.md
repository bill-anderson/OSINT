# Digital Eswatini Project (P508948)

**The [[world-bank]] project financing Eswatini's digital-government build — approved June 2026.**
**US$65m**; implementing agency [[ministry-of-ict-eswatini]]. Project ID **P508948** (PID
**PIDDC01229**), Concept Stage Project Information Document published **2025-06-28**, which gave an
*Estimated* Appraisal Date of **2025-08-01** and an *Estimated* Approval Date of **2025-09-01**.
**Those planned dates slipped by roughly nine months:** grant negotiations concluded **2026-05-18**
and **loan approval followed in June 2026** (month precision), per the Ministry of ICT's First
Quarter Performance Report for FY 2026/27. See [[SWZ]],
[[finance.new]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Reported in Eswatini press as the **"Eswatini Digital
Transformation Project"** — retained as an alias; the former separate page was merged here per
**ISSUE-005** (2026-07-20).

## Activity in this wiki

- **★ Approval confirmed, and the money broken down (reported 2026-07-19).** The **Ministry of ICT's
  First Quarter Performance Report for FY 2026/27**, presented by ICT Minister **Savannah Maziya**,
  records that **World Bank grant negotiations concluded 2026-05-18** and **loan approval followed in
  June 2026** — clearing the way to finalise the financing agreements. Financing is a **[[world-bank]]
  IBRD loan plus an IDA credit** (**the split between the two instruments is not stated**), and the
  project is aligned with the **COMESA Inclusive Digital Economic Advancement (IDEA) Programme**.

  **Money is carried in the World Bank's own currency (USD), with the Eswatini outlet's dated
  conversion at E16.37 = US$1 (2026-07-19) in brackets** — not the other way round:

  | Component | Amount | Dated SZL conversion |
  |---|---|---|
  | Affordable broadband infrastructure | **US$36m** | ~E589m |
  | **Government in Your Hand** ([[government-in-your-hand]]) | **US$25m** | ~E409m |
  | Programme management, M&E, safeguards, fiduciary, PIU | **US$4m** | ~E65.5m |
  | **Project total** | **US$65m** | ~E1.06bn |
  | Project Preparation Grant (separate, closes **Dec 2026**) | **US$2m** | ~E32.7m |
  | **Total investment** | **US$67m** | ~E1.1bn |

  *(The components sum exactly to US$65m and every conversion checks out at the stated rate.)*

  **Broadband component (US$36m):** financial stabilisation and **reform of [[eptc]]**, extension of
  the national backbone to **schools, health centres and Tinkhundla centres**, and strengthening the
  **ICT legal and regulatory environment**. **GIYH component (US$25m):** a **national digital identity
  system**, cybersecurity, **data governance**, a **whole-of-government electronic payment gateway**,
  digitalisation of **50+ priority services** (health, education, home affairs, commerce), and
  **digital skills for 200,000 citizens**, targeted at women and young people.

  **Delivery state as reported:** commercial, legal and financial **due diligence on EPTC** has begun
  alongside an infrastructure assessment; procurement is **nearing completion** for a **Digital
  Transformation Roadmap** to classify the 50+ services; assessments of the **Royal Science and
  Technology Park data centre** and the IT service-delivery model are progressing; a **six-member
  Project Implementation Unit** is operational. Parliament's Portfolio Committee has been briefed, with
  legislators pressing for **stronger cybersecurity and protection of children and elderly citizens**.

  **The ministry names its own risks**, which is the most useful part: **incompatibility between
  government's Integrated Financial Management System and World Bank reporting requirements**, **PIU
  capacity constraints**, and the **tight timeline before the preparation grant expires end-December
  2026**. *So the binding constraint on a US$65m digital-government programme is, on the implementer's
  own account, public-financial-management plumbing and staffing — not technology.*

  **Still not held:** the appraisal-stage **PAD**, the **IBRD/IDA split**, the results framework,
  effectiveness and disbursement status, and the **ministry's report itself** (queued for acquisition)
  — this reaches the wiki through a single Eswatini outlet reporting a government document. Source:
  [[2026-07-19-eswatini-1-06-billion-world-bank-digital-funding]].
- **Reported as the "Eswatini Digital Transformation Project" (2026-05).** A 2026-05
  secondary report described Eswatini "moving closer to securing funding" through World Bank
  negotiations (inter-ministerial: ICT, Finance, Economic Planning), framing the programme as
  modernising government systems and delivering services — passports, business registration,
  vehicle licensing, health, education, social services — via the [[government-in-your-hand]]
  platform, plus **cybersecurity frameworks, digital skills and rural broadband**; **no funding
  figure** was stated. P508948 is the World Bank record it corresponds to: same country, same
  [[world-bank]] financier, same implementing ministry, same GIYH delivery platform. The apparent
  chronology objection dissolves on inspection — the PID's appraisal and approval dates are printed
  as *Estimated*, so a 2026-05 report of live negotiations is consistent with slipped planned dates
  rather than a second project. **Superseded 2026-07-19: approval is now confirmed** — negotiations concluded 2026-05-18, loan approved June 2026 (see the entry above). The 2026-05 report of live negotiations reads correctly in hindsight as the immediate run-up to it. *(The former
  `eswatini-digital-transformation-project` page was merged here per ISSUE-005, 2026-07-20;
  "Eswatini Digital Transformation Project" retained as an alias.)* Sources:
  [[2025-06-28 PID Concept Stage - Digital Eswatini (P508948)]],
  [[2026-05-18 Eswatini promotes digital transformation project through World Bank engagements]].
- **Scope via the GIYH roadmap ToR (2025, year precision).** The Digital Government
  Implementation Roadmap procured under Digital Eswatini covers **digital ID, data
  exchange, social registries, PKI, payments and ~200 services** — see
  [[government-in-your-hand]]. This is the fullest statement of project scope the wiki
  holds. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 ToR - Government in Your Hand Digital Government Implementation Roadmap (Digital Eswatini)]].
- **What is not held (updated 2026-07-21).** No appraisal-stage **PAD**, no **IBRD/IDA split**, no
  results framework, and no effectiveness or disbursement status. The **component breakdown is now
  held** (US$36m / US$25m / US$4m) and **approval is confirmed for June 2026** — what remains missing
  is the Bank-side documentation and the Ministry of ICT's own quarterly report, both queued for
  acquisition. Note the earlier PID figure was **US$65.16m** against **US$65m** as reported at
  approval; the difference is immaterial and probably rounding, but the exact approved principal is
  **unestablished** pending the PAD.
