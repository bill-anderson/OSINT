# SIGFIP (Integrated Public Finance Management System, Gabon)

Gabon's unified public-financial-management platform, **officially launched 15 January 2026** and
connected to the public treasury. It consolidates all state tax, customs and non-tax revenues and
payments into a single database, replacing fragmented legacy systems.

## Key facts

- **Four functions:** centralising tax and customs revenue; executing public spending (operating,
  investment, debt); managing public procurement; and monitoring non-tax revenue (royalties,
  administrative fees).
- **For taxpayers/businesses:** a single payment interface, a Treasury-issued receipt and real-time
  transaction visibility; integrates **electronic signatures** and digitised procurement.
- **Rollout:** national plus Gabon's diplomatic representations abroad, connecting the state to banks
  and electronic-payment operators.
- **Trigger:** follows a **3 October 2025 directive** by President Brice Oligui Nguema to digitise
  financial agencies; first announced at a 13 January 2026 finance/digital-economy strategic meeting
  ([[2026-01-14-gabon-public-finance-egov-initiatives]]) alongside [[madigipaie-gabon]] and the Unique
  Participation File (FUP).

Complements the revenue-side [[digitax-gabon]] / e-tax platform run by the [[dgi-gabon]], and the
payments-side [[madigipaie-gabon]].
