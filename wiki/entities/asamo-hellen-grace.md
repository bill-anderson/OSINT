# Asamo Hellen Grace

**Uganda's Minister of State for Disability Affairs** — the only voice this wiki holds on
disability inclusion in Uganda's digital and financial systems.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Call for PWD financial inclusion (2025-12-02).** Speaking at the **5th National
  Disability Symposium** (ahead of the International Day of Persons with Disabilities,
  3 December, Mubende District), Asamo called on financial institutions to strengthen and
  prioritise strategies including Persons with Disabilities, noting equal access to
  financial services is a fundamental right. Her ministry, she said, continues to receive
  numerous complaints from PWDs facing difficulty accessing loans, banking facilities and
  essential financial services: *"We must create systems that provide fairness and dignity.
  Persons with disabilities deserve the same financial opportunities as every Ugandan."*
  **Uganda is estimated to have more than 1.3 million persons living with disabilities (as
  of 2025-12-02).** Barriers named: **financial exclusion, limited employment
  opportunities, restricted access to microcredit**; and stakeholders noted persistent
  gaps in **research and funding** for disability-inclusive financial planning. See
  [[include.access]], [[include.divides]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-02 Minister Asamo Calls for Stronger Financial Inclusion for Persons with Disabilities]].

## Why this connects

The [[uganda-nfis-ii]] strategy (November 2023) widened its priority groups to add
**persons with disabilities** — two years before this call reported the barriers as
persisting. And the exclusion has a biometric edge: **more than 1 million Ugandans had
difficulty registering fingerprint biometrics** for [[ndaga-muntu]], **disproportionately
elderly people and manual labourers** (NIRA figures cited by [[iritech]], 2026-03-02) —
the same population, meeting the same gate, at enrolment rather than at the bank counter.
