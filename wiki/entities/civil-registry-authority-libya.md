# Civil Registry Authority (CRA, Libya)

Libya's civil-registration and national-identity authority — issuer of the unique **National ID Number**
that gates access to SIM cards, passports, bank accounts, official-rate currency, marriage and voting
([[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]]). The registry is central to elections and citizenship, and its integrity
is a recurring national-security concern (fake-number scandals, foreign-national infiltration). Head
(2024): Major General Tariq Al-Thaman. See [[LBY]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Project Intilaqa — cleaning the database (2024-09).** [[dpi.registry]] Matching electronic Civil
  Registry data with paper records reached **97% completion** (257 offices, 1.8m families printed) —
  the first such completion since the mid-1970s — against a history of 250,000-700,000 alleged fake
  National ID Numbers (cited for postponing the Dec-2021 elections). Source:
  [[2024-09-06 Libya's Project Intilaqa matches Civil Registry electronic data with paper records]].
- **Parliament warns of breaches (2025-12).** [[gov.protect]] The House Defence & National Security
  Committee warned of "manipulation and serious violations" letting foreign nationals obtain Libyan
  national ID numbers — "a direct threat to national security and Libyan identity." Source:
  [[2025-12-08 Libyan Parliament warns of breaches in Civil Registry national ID system]].
