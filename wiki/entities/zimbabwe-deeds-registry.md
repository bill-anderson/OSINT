# Zimbabwe Deeds Registry

**Zimbabwe's register of property titles and security instruments, established by the
Deeds Registry Act of 1960**, and the object of the digitisation programme created by
[[zimbabwe-si-76-2025]]. It holds **property titles, charitable and family trusts, and
mortgage bonds** — the paper record on which Zimbabwean property rights rest.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The insider-fraud problem (2025).** The registry's own staff are the named risk.
  Recent cases allege fraud in deed registration **through collusion with staff members
  or outright forgery** — the [[zimbabwe-si-76-2025]] regulations respond by prescribing
  security paper from a single supplier, traceable digital logins, and criminal sanctions
  **explicitly targeting Registry or government employees** who assist fraudsters. The
  trigger case (**Dr Dzingai Mutumbuka**, whose Chisipite deed was stolen from the
  Registry and fraudulently altered with an internal official's help) is detailed on the
  SI page. Source:
  [[2025-07-28 Legal Discussions with Vengai Madzima - Digitization of the Deeds Registry in Zimbabwe]].
- **Digitisation role (2025).** Under the new regulations the Registrar **validates**
  submitted paper deeds, **issues securitised deeds** on special paper, and **maintains
  the digital copy** at the deeds office. A supporting implementation framework from the
  Registrar's office — including fees — was **anticipated but not yet published** as of
  July 2025. The Registrar also holds a role in any extension of the transitional window
  (the Minister may extend by Gazette notice with the Registrar's approval). See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Sources:
  [[2025-07-28 Legal Discussions with Vengai Madzima - Digitization of the Deeds Registry in Zimbabwe]],
  [[2025-08-01 How to secure your property in Zimbabwe with the new digital deed system]].
- **Prior attempts (per Madzima, 2025-07-28).** "Consecutive governments made several
  attempts over the years" to secure the documents — i.e. SI 76 is the latest of a
  series, not a first move. No detail on the earlier attempts is held.

## Provenance

**Both held sources are law-firm-authored — attribute.** The Nehanda Radio piece has a
**commercial interest**: a conveyancing practice writing about a validation process it is
paid to perform, whose "milestone… enhance local and international confidence" framing is
advocacy rather than evidence. The Madzima interview is explicitly labelled a personal
view.
