# Vantage Data Centers

**A global hyperscale data-centre developer, and one of the three operators driving South Africa's
projected capacity build-out** alongside **[[teraco]]** and **[[cavaleros]]**. Its South African
platform is the **JNB1 campus at Waterfall City**, Johannesburg, developed in partnership with
JSE-listed property firm **[[attacq]]**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The Attacq joint venture (2025-03-11).** Vantage and Attacq formed a **50:50 joint venture** to
  build a data centre forming the **first half of Phase II** of Vantage's Waterfall City campus.
  **10,363 m²**, **two storeys**, **four data halls**; construction under way with **completion
  expected Q1 2026**. Shell investment estimated at **R628 million (~$34m)**, **backed by a 20-year
  lease**; **Attacq partners on the shell only**. Source:
  [[2025-03-11 Vantage partners with Attacq for Phase II of 80MW campus in Jo'burg, South Africa]].
- **JNB1 campus (as of 2025-03).** **30 acres**, **three facilities** across **60,000 m²**, offering
  **80 MW of IT capacity at full buildout**. The first facility, **JNB11 — 16 MW — was completed in
  2022**, delivered using prefabricated electrical containers and equipment. Campus plans were first
  announced in **October 2021** with a **planned investment of more than R15 billion**. Source: same.
- **Antoine Boniface on siting (Oct 2021, reported 2025-03-11).** The president of Vantage EMEA:
  "Johannesburg is the data center hub for sub-Saharan Africa due to its strategic location, IT
  ecosystem, fiber connectivity to the rest of Africa, and the availability of renewable energy."
  *Vendor framing — attribute it.* Source: same.
- **Two campuses, ~100 MW (2026-02-09).** Daily Maverick reports Vantage among operators hoping to
  expand, aiming to provide a **100 MW load at two data campuses in Johannesburg** — part of the
  build-out pushing SA from ~400 MW to at least 1,200 MW (see **[[eskom]]**). **This is a different
  scope from the 2025 JNB1 figure — 100 MW across two campuses vs 80 MW at full buildout on one — and
  is complementary, not a conflict.** Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Major SA data centre expansion plans emerge, set to more than double current power demand]].
- **Market context (2025-03).** Other Johannesburg operators named alongside Vantage: Digital Parks
  Africa, OADC, **[[digital-realty]]**, **[[teraco]]**, NTT and **[[africa-data-centres]]**.
  **[[equinix]] entered the South African market in October 2024.** Source:
  [[2025-03-11 Vantage partners with Attacq for Phase II of 80MW campus in Jo'burg, South Africa]].
- **Vendor listing (2025-03-18).** Vantage is named among major SA operators/investors in the
  **[[research-and-markets]]** commercial-database promotion — a dated prior, vendor-asserted, not
  current state. Source: [[2025-03-18 South Africa Data Center Portfolio 2025 - Major Investors]].
