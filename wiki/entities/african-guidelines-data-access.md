# African Guidelines on Data Access (ACHPR Res.620)

**Draft** continental soft-law instrument issued by the [[african-commission]] under Resolution 620
(2024), reframing access to data as a component of the right to information (African Charter Art. 9) and
the right to development (Art. 22). **Not yet adopted** — consultation ran to 14 July 2026.

## What it does

- Compels **public bodies and private actors** to make data accessible for human-rights and development
  purposes; principles of maximum disclosure, proactive disclosure of high-value datasets,
  data-access-by-design, data justice, and complementarity between access and data protection.
- 30 measures direct states to domesticate the **[[malabo-convention]]** and **[[au-data-policy-framework]]**,
  enact open-data policies, strengthen independent oversight, prioritise national/regional data-centre
  storage "to promote sovereignty," and cooperate against transnational-platform data dominance.
- Four-tier enforcement (transparency → corrective action → civil remedies → judicial sanctions);
  30-day access response; 3–5-year framework review.
