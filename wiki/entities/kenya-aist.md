# Kenya Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (Kenya-AIST)

The Kenya Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (Kenya-AIST) is a Kenyan
state science-and-technology institute positioned as the country's designated
academic anchor for advanced research, teaching and innovation. It is governed by
a council; as of early 2026 **Prof. Emmanuel Mutisya** is council chairman and
**Prof. J.W. Khamasi** is principal and council secretary.

## Why it matters here

Kenya-AIST is being cast as the **central institutional vehicle** for the
proposed [[kenya-germany-ai-centre]] — a "Centre of African Excellence for
Applied Artificial Intelligence" that Kenya and Germany are jointly exploring. At
a Nairobi meeting on 2026-02-03, Principal Secretary [[john-tanui]] (Ministry of
Information, Communications and the Digital Economy) hosted a delegation from the
German Federal Ministry of Research, Technology and Space, with Kenya-AIST's
council leadership present. Tanui identified "the central role of Kenya-AIST" as a
core element of the collaboration frameworks under discussion, alongside
responsible AI governance, talent development and data ecosystems.

The proposed centre — and Kenya-AIST's role within it — is aligned to **Kenya's
National AI Strategy 2025–2030**, and is intended to strengthen AI research,
teaching, innovation and capacity building, positioning Kenya as a continental hub
for ethical AI innovation and locally relevant solutions.

As of 2026-07-11 the partnership remained at the preliminary-consultation stage
(no formalised agreement), with Kenya-AIST expected to host or operate the centre
once terms are settled.
