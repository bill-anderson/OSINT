# Strive Masiyiwa

Zimbabwean-born telecoms entrepreneur and philanthropist; founder and executive
chairman of **[[econet-group]]** and of **[[cassava-technologies]]** (parent of
[[liquid-c2]] and [[africa-data-centres]]). The single most important private actor in
this wiki's **African digital-sovereignty** thread — from mobile telephony and
[[ecocash]] mobile money to today's **[[cassava-ai-factories]]** sovereign-compute push.

## Biographical spine (dated; sources disagree — see reconciliation)

Born **1961** in Rhodesia (now Zimbabwe); family went into exile in Zambia after Ian
Smith's 1965 UDI; schooled in Scotland from age 12; **electrical/electronic engineering
at the University of Wales (Cardiff)**; returned to Zimbabwe, worked at the state telco
before starting his own business (**1988**, per MoneyWeek). **Incorporated Econet in 1993**
and fought a **five-year legal battle** for a licence, won in Zimbabwe's Supreme Court on
free-expression grounds (**licence secured ~1997**); Econet **commercially launched in 1998**.
Named the **first Black billionaire on the Sunday Times Rich List (2021)**. Now London-based.

## Roles

- **[[econet-group]]** — founder, executive chairman; the group spans
  telecoms, mobile money ([[ecocash]]), fintech, insurance, renewable energy, education.
- **[[cassava-technologies]]** — co-founder / executive chairman; pan-African digital-
  infrastructure group ([[liquid-c2]], [[africa-data-centres]], [[cassava-ai]]).
- **AU Special Envoy** (2020–21, under AU chair [[cyril-ramaphosa]]): COVID-19
  procurement lead — built the **[[africa-medical-supplies-platform]]** — and vaccine-
  acquisition envoy (African Vaccine Acquisition Task Team). Also led 2014–15 Ebola
  corporate mobilisation. See [[african-union]].
- Board roles cited across sources: **Unilever, Netflix, the Gates Foundation**
  ([[gates-foundation]]), Rockefeller Foundation, Asia Society, Africa Progress Panel.
- Philanthropy: with wife **Tsitsi Masiyiwa** — the **Higher Life Foundation /
  Capernaum Trust** (orphan scholarships) and the **[[aisca-foundation]]** (AI skills).

## Activity in this wiki

- **"The time for talking about AI is over" (2026-04).** At the launch of
  [[cassava-technologies]] / [[liquid-c2]]'s first Google Cloud-powered Partner Experience
  Centre in Johannesburg, Masiyiwa argued AI "is not going to happen because we talk it
  into being ... it will happen because we ... act and invest in ... the infrastructure,"
  pointing to the upcoming Cassava **AI factory in Cape Town** (billed the largest on the
  continent) and further [[cassava-ai-factories]] planned in Kenya, then Nigeria, Morocco
  and Egypt. See [[tech.ai]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-08 Liquid C2, Google open first-for-Africa Partner Experience Centre]].

## ⚠ Figure reconciliation (dated "as of")

Sources conflict; treat each as of its date, do not average:
- **Business start:** 1986 (NYU Stern) vs **1988** (MoneyWeek) — prefer 1988.
- **Econet dates (REV-003 resolved 2026-07-16):** three distinct dates, not two —
  **incorporated 1993** (Stanford GSB, "started Econet in 1993"; also the start of the five-year
  legal battle), **licence secured ~1997** (NYU Law, Afr. Leadership — late-1997/early-1998 Supreme
  Court ruling), and **commercial launch 1998** — **10 July 1998** per Econet's own record
  ([[2026-07-16 Econet Wireless Zimbabwe About Us (network launch 10 July 1998)]]), with the ZSE
  listing within three months. The earlier "*established/incorporated 1998*" reading — attributed to a
  "Guardian 2014" piece **not on file** as a source for incorporation — was a launch date mislabelled
  as incorporation; dropped.
- **Wealth:** ~$600M "fortune"/market cap (2014) → FT stake ~$345m → "first Black billionaire"
  (2021). Rising/different measures.
- **Orphan support:** 28,000 (2014) / ~30,000 point-in-time (2014) / 40,000 students (~2015) /
  **250,000 cumulative over 25 years** (Tools and Weapons, ~2023) — point-in-time vs cumulative.
- **Ebola health workers:** 850 (NYU Law) vs 1,000 (Tools and Weapons).
- **Foundation naming:** "Capernaum Trust" vs "Higher Life Foundation" — related/successor.
