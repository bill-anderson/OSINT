# EriTel

Eritrea's **sole, state-owned telecommunications operator** — the country runs a
telecom monopoly with no private mobile or ISP competition, one structural driver of
its very low connectivity (roughly **23.7%** mobile penetration in late 2025, among
Africa's lowest; see [[ERI]], [[infra.connect]]).

**International transit is satellite-only.** No submarine communications cable lands in Eritrea, so
EriTel — which also holds the international gateway monopoly — carries international voice and data over
**geostationary satellite**. This is a policy position, not an accident of geography: as of **June 2024**
cable operators were actively seeking a Red Sea route along the Eritrean shore (waters at an optimal
~170m depth) to bypass Yemen, and the state **did not respond to their approaches, even at a price**;
Eritrea is also one of the few states not party to UNCLOS. Satellite-only transit is the binding
constraint behind the country's bandwidth ceiling and among the highest access costs on the continent.
Source: [[2024-06-10-cable-firms-eye-eritrea-red-sea-houthi-attacks]].

Network expansion is directed by the **Ministry of Transportation and Communications**
(Minister **Tesfaselasie Berhane**), which reported (May 2026, state-media claims,
unverifiable) cumulative telecom investment of **over 35 billion Nakfa** since
independence, **184 towers** installed, **~90% geographic** (not population) mobile
coverage, service "reaching 4G level," and total bandwidth of **~1,536 Mbps** built up
over a decade from 0.5 Mbps in 2001.
