# EriTel

Eritrea's **sole, state-owned telecommunications operator** — the country runs a
telecom monopoly with no private mobile or ISP competition, one structural driver of
its very low connectivity (roughly **23.7%** mobile penetration in late 2025, among
Africa's lowest; see [[ERI]], [[infra.connect]]).

Network expansion is directed by the **Ministry of Transportation and Communications**
(Minister **Tesfaselasie Berhane**), which reported (May 2026, state-media claims,
unverifiable) cumulative telecom investment of **over 35 billion Nakfa** since
independence, **184 towers** installed, **~90% geographic** (not population) mobile
coverage, service "reaching 4G level," and total bandwidth of **~1,536 Mbps** built up
over a decade from 0.5 Mbps in 2001.
