# Somalia Cybersecurity Law (2026)

Somalia's Cybersecurity Law, **approved by the House of the People on 26 January 2026**. It
establishes a national cybersecurity management framework — defining the Ministry of
Communications' responsibilities, assigning a technical role to the telecom regulator
(NCA), setting obligations for critical-infrastructure operators, and detailing prevention,
reporting and response mechanisms.

Creates: the **Somalia Computer Incident Response Team (SOM-CIRT)**, a **nine-member
cybersecurity committee**, and an **emergency intervention centre**.

Context: follows an Aug-2025 draft cybercrime bill, a Malaysia cybersecurity MoU (Aug 2025)
and UNODC cooperation (Nov 2024). Threat backdrop includes a **November 2025 e-visa
platform breach** (personal data of several thousand people) and ransomware/phishing cited
in the State of Cybersecurity in Somalia 2024 report. Somalia ranked **Tier 4 of 5** on the
ITU Global Cybersecurity Index 2024 (37.38/100).
