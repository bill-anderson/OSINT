# Somalia Cybersecurity Law (2026)

Somalia's Cybersecurity Law, **approved by the House of the People on 26 January 2026**. It
establishes a national cybersecurity management framework — defining the Ministry of
Communications' responsibilities, assigning a technical role to the telecom regulator
(NCA), setting obligations for critical-infrastructure operators, and detailing prevention,
reporting and response mechanisms.

Creates: the **Somalia Computer Incident Response Team (SOM-CIRT)**, a **nine-member
cybersecurity committee**, and an **emergency intervention centre**.

## Implementing instrument — National Cybersecurity Risk Management Framework (June 2026)

Released **June 2026** *(month precision; reported 2026-07-21, and the exact release date is
**unestablished** — the framework document itself is **not held** and is queued for acquisition)*, and
issued by the MoCT with the **[[nca-somalia|NCA]]** as **lead cybersecurity regulator**. It converts the
Law's critical-infrastructure obligations into a concrete duty: organisations — **public and private** —
that own or operate **Critical Information Infrastructure** must run prescribed risk assessments against
**confidentiality, integrity and availability**, repeat them **regularly**, and **file annual
risk-assessment reports with the NCA** under the **Somalia Cybersecurity Act**.

Built on **ISO/IEC 27000** and **ISO/IEC 27005**, with a defined process (asset identification → threat
analysis → risk evaluation → risk treatment) and assets grouped into **security domains**. Adds
**sector-specific business-impact models** for **defence, public safety, critical infrastructure and
financial services**, and appendices covering a **national cybersecurity maturity model**,
**emerging-technology risk-management guidelines** and a **change-management strategy**.

**Not established as of 2026-07-21:** which entities are designated CII, any compliance deadline or
first reporting date, penalties for non-filing, and whether the NCA has the supervisory capacity to
process annual returns. Source:
[[2026-07-21-somalia-national-cybersecurity-risk-management-framework]].

Context: follows an Aug-2025 draft cybercrime bill, a Malaysia cybersecurity MoU (Aug 2025)
and UNODC cooperation (Nov 2024). Threat backdrop includes a **November 2025 e-visa
platform breach** (personal data of several thousand people) and ransomware/phishing cited
in the State of Cybersecurity in Somalia 2024 report. Somalia ranked **Tier 4 of 5** on the
ITU Global Cybersecurity Index 2024 (37.38/100).
