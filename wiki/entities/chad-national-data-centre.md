# Chad National Data Center

**Chad's sovereign state data facility**, built under Phase I of [[pmice]] and, as of early 2026,
awaiting commissioning. [[adetic]]'s Director General calls it *"a strategic base for sovereign
housing of state data and continuity of public digital services"* and attributes the project to
President **Mahamat Idriss Déby Itno**. It is designed to sit at the centre of a hub-and-spoke
government architecture: **100 micro-data centres** across public institutions, interconnected
with it. Its **size and location have not been disclosed** (as of 2026-01-06).

Not to be confused with **Chad's first data centre** — a separate, much smaller **2016** facility;
see [[flexenclosure]].

## Status

- **Building and equipment: complete.** Phase one — construction of the building and installation
  of operational equipment — was already finished **as of 2026-02-06**. Source:
  [[2026-02-06 ADETIC, ANSICE, and TECHSO-GROUP Join Forces to Protect Chad's Digital Infrastructure]].
- **Audit and certification: in progress.** On **2026-02-05**, [[adetic]], [[ansice]] and
  [[techso-group]] signed the [[adetic-ansice-techso-audit-agreement]] for a security audit and
  certification against national and international critical-infrastructure standards — described
  by ADETIC as *the final milestone before inauguration*. See [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[gov.standards]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-06 ADETIC, ANSICE, and TECHSO-GROUP Join Forces to Protect Chad's Digital Infrastructure]],
  [[2026-02-06 Chad Moves to Secure National Data Center Ahead of Launch]].
- **Still described as "under construction" a week later.** Ecofin's 2026-02-13 telecom-
  infrastructure round-up states *"a national data center is also under construction."* Sources
  differ in framing rather than fact — one reports the build complete and certification pending,
  the other reports the project as ongoing; neither reports an inauguration date. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Chad Plans 50 Telecom Sites, 50 km of Fiber in N'Djamena]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Financing and scope.** The facility is part of **Phase I of [[pmice]]**, a **€175.1 million
  (US$204.7m)** package also covering 1,200 km of fibre and GSM/3G/4G expansion; commencement
  announced **20 December 2025**. A **2023** [[world-bank]] document put PMICE's cost at **$180
  million**, financed by **Eximbank** (*origin country unstated in the source*) — a 2023 estimate
  superseded by the 2025/26 figures, not a conflicting account. See [[infra.store]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-01-06 Chad invests 175m in national data center and networks]].
- **The micro-data centre layer.** The 2026-02-05 agreement covers operating **100 micro-data
  centres** across government institutions. Interconnected with the National Data Center, ADETIC
  says these will form *"a unified, secure and high-performance digital architecture"* supporting
  the dematerialisation of administrative procedures. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]].
  Source: [[2026-02-06 Chad Moves to Secure National Data Center Ahead of Launch]].
- **A second, unresolved audit offer (2026-07-01).** Following Chad's Washington mission (26–29
  May 2026), US firm [[cybastion]] expressed willingness to support **auditing, certification and
  protection** of the National Datacenter. No source in this batch relates this offer to the
  ADETIC/ANSICE/TECHSO-GROUP mandate signed five months earlier, or reports whether it was taken
  up. See [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-01 Chad and US Explore Partnerships on Satellite Connectivity and Cybersecurity]].
- **A recurring bilateral ask.** A *"modern national data center"* was one of three priorities in
  Chad's November 2025 partnership talks with **Azerbaijan** at the World Telecommunication
  Development Conference — alongside digital-infrastructure upgrades and e-government services.
  The parties agreed to formalise by MoU; no timeline announced as of 2025-12-02. Source:
  [[2025-12-02 Chad Accelerates Digital Cooperation to Support Its Transformation Agenda]].

## Reading it

The facility is doing heavy rhetorical work — "sovereign housing of state data" — while the
sources hold no disclosed capacity, footprint, location, tier rating or operator. Meanwhile Chad
has courted at least three external parties on the same asset (Azerbaijan on building it,
TECHSO-GROUP on certifying it, Cybastion on auditing it), which sits awkwardly with the
sovereignty framing. See [[sovereignty]], [[infra.store]].
