# SeyID

**Seychelles' national digital-identity platform** — the web and mobile channel through which
Seychellois citizens, the diaspora and foreign permit holders hold and present a national ID.
Operated in the context of the [[department-of-immigration-and-civil-status-seychelles]] (ICS) in
partnership with [[dict-seychelles]]; the underlying technology traces to the Swiss firm
[[wisekey]]. It is the wallet layer for [[certexpress]] certificates and the authentication
front door for Seychelles' e-government stack. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[SYC]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Origins (2014).** The Seychelles government selected [[wisekey]], a Swiss cybersecurity
  company, as its partner for public-key digital-signature technology for electronic
  transactions. WISeKey International Holding subsequently developed the SeyID project on its
  **WISeID** digital-identity and online-security platform. See [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2024-09-18 Seychelles Makes Digital ID Mandatory for Work Permit Holders]].
- **Official launch (2022-11-01).** The platform was launched at a ceremony attended by
  Vice-President **Ahmed Afif** and President **[[wavel-ramkalawan]]**. At launch it was
  described as in the final phases of integration with eGovernment services and financial
  institutions. Source:
  [[2024-09-18 Seychelles Makes Digital ID Mandatory for Work Permit Holders]].
- **Virtual ID mandatory for new permit holders (from 2024-09-17).** The ICS stopped issuing
  **physical** ID cards to new work-permit holders; instead permit holders are registered on
  SeyID and issued a **virtual ID card**, valid for the duration of the permit. The route to a
  National Identity Number (NIN) is unchanged — the applicant receives an ID letter carrying the
  assigned NIN, then registers on the SeyID website and downloads the app. Seychellois citizens
  living abroad may register using their NIN card or ID letter plus passport. **Seychellois
  citizens retain the option of a physical ID**, which expires every ten years. PS for
  Immigration **Alain Volcere** framed the change as reducing administrative burden, speeding
  identity verification and cutting in-person visits; the ICS framed it as reducing waste and
  "prioritizing Seychellois citizens" in card issuance. See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]].
  Sources: [[2024-09-18 Launch of New Digital Solutions for Public Services]],
  [[2024-09-18 Seychelles Makes Digital ID Mandatory for Work Permit Holders]].
- **Capabilities (as described at 2024-09).** Web- and mobile-based, with online **KYC
  onboarding**, **OTP authentication**, **QR-code secure login**, a **cloud signature** solution
  for legally valid digital document signing, and an **E-Doc wallet** into which CertExpress
  certificates are saved. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Sources:
  [[2024-09-18 Seychelles Makes Digital ID Mandatory for Work Permit Holders]],
  [[2024-09-18 Launch of New Digital Solutions for Public Services]].
