# National Civil Registration Authority (NCRA)

Sierra Leone's sole civil-registration and national-identity agency, established by the
**National Civil Registration Act, 30 June 2016**. NCRA registers vital events and issues
the country's foundational identifier — the **National Identification Number (NIN)** — and
the securitised, ECOWAS-compliant biometric ID card. Director General (2025):
**Mohamed Mubashir Massaquoi**. It operates a **Tier-3 data centre** housing the civil-
registration and ID systems, with capacity to host other MDAs' systems. NIN usage is
underpinned by a **March-2022 Cabinet Directive (parliament-ratified)** mandating the NIN
as the unique identifier for accessing government services.

## Activity in this wiki

- **93% ID coverage / 80% services digitized (as of 2025-06, ID4Africa AGM).** NCRA
  (Deputy Director ICT Moses T.F. Vibbie) reports 93% civil-registration/ID coverage and
  >80% of services digitized; ~30% of paper records linked to digital records so far. NIN
  now required to open a bank account, enrol in public education, or take up public/private
  employment. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-05 Sierra Leone reaches 93 percent national ID coverage 80 percent services digitized]].
- **ID card made mandatory (ultimatum 2024-12-20, effect 2025-01-01).** A joint
  NCRA/Sierra Leone Police/Office of National Security notice made the NCRA card mandatory
  for movement and services, ending acceptance of the voter's card as ID at banks and
  institutions. See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 Sierra Leone announces mandatory national ID cards - what you need to know]].
- **ID-card deadline extended (2024-03-28).** Deadline to obtain National/ECOWAS/Non-
  National cards extended 31 Mar → 28 Jun 2024; security-sector enforcement from 1 Jul 2024
  (historical baseline, later superseded by the Dec-2024 mandatory ultimatum). Source:
  [[2024-03-28 Government Extends Deadline for Obtaining National Identity Cards]].
- **NIN–Farmer Registry integration (2025-05).** NCRA and the Ministry of Agriculture
  agreed to integrate the [[sierra-leone-national-digital-farmer-registry]] with the NIN
  system to de-duplicate farmer identities, with hosting at NCRA's Tier-3 data centre under
  discussion. See [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-19 NCRA, Ministry of Agriculture partner on National Digital Farmer Registry integration]].

## Open tensions

- **Registration ≠ cards.** NCRA reports 93% *registration* coverage, but the World Bank
  counts **6.4m NIN holders (~8 in 10)** and **fewer than 0.5m ID cards issued as of 2024**,
  with a **$6–7 fee** pricing most out. Registration coverage and card issuance are being
  conflated in public messaging; see reconcile note under [[SLE]] and
  [[2025-12-09 Unlocking opportunity - How digital ID can transform lives in Sierra Leone]].
- The **[[sierra-leone-data-protection-rti-bill-2025]]** was still pre-enactment as of Nov
  2025, so NIN data-sharing runs ahead of a statutory data-protection framework.
