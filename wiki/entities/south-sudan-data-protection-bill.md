# South Sudan Data Protection Bill (draft)

South Sudan's first-ever data-protection law, in **draft** as of late 2025 and
slated by government for introduction in **2026**. It would be the country's
first comprehensive personal-data protection framework; South Sudan currently
lacks any such legal or institutional regime.

## Status and activity

- **Civil-society convening (2025-10).** Paradigm Initiative and
  **[[koneta-hub]]** hosted a two-day Juba forum to improve the draft bill and
  secure a parliamentary commitment to table it. Source:
  [[2025-10-17 Paradigm Initiative partners with Koneta Hub on data protection law in South Sudan]].
- **Ministerial commitment (2025-12).** ICT minister Ateny Wek Ateny said the
  bill would be introduced in 2026 to complement the
  **[[south-sudan-cybercrime-bill-2025]]** ("Cybercrime and data protection are
  two laws that must work together"), jointly drafted by the ministries of
  information and justice. If enacted it would align South Sudan with Kenya's
  Data Protection Act and the AU Malabo Convention. Source:
  [[2025-12-11 South Sudan to get data protection bill in 2026, says minister]].
