# African Union Data Policy Framework (AUDPF)

- **Issuing body:** [[african-union|African Union]] (endorsed at continental level).
- **Primary document now held (February 2022).** The full AU Data Policy Framework is in `raw/`; prior citations here were secondary. Source: [[2022-02-01 AU Data Policy Framework]].
- **Scope:** Pan-African; the AU's continental reference framework for data governance —
  setting shared principles for data protection, cross-border data flows, data value
  creation and interoperability to underpin an integrated African digital economy and a
  digital single market.
- **Status:** Active; the continental anchor that national data-governance efforts are
  expected to operationalise, cited alongside the [[au-continental-ai-strategy]] and the
  Malabo Convention.

## What it is

The AUDPF is the AU's flagship data-governance instrument, positioned as the framework
against which member states align national data-protection laws, data-classification
standards and cross-border data-transfer regimes. It is invoked as the normative reference
for building "secure, interoperable digital ecosystems" and for realising the data
dimension of **[[afcfta]]** and its digital-trade agenda.

  [[us-kenya-health-framework]] ($2.5bn deal), against the "17% of people, 1% of AI training data"
  gap. See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-08 Kenyan Citizens Took A $2.5 Billion Deal With America To Court To Fight For Their Health Data]].

  Europe) with **[[giz]]**, **[[auda-nepad]]** and **[[kictanet]]** — the framework moving from
  continental reference to national institutional reform in one of Africa's smallest states.
  See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.policy]], [[XWA]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 D4D Hub on Sao Tome and Principe]].
