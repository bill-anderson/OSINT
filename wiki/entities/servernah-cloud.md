# Servernah Cloud

Kenyan-hosted cloud platform marketed as **"Kenya's first sovereign AI cloud"**
(launched March 2026), backed by local firm **EverseTech** — a working example of
the "sovereignty as control, not isolation" model.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launch at iXAfrica (2026-03).** Formally launched as **Kenya's first sovereign-hosted cloud +
  AI platform** at **[[ixafrica-data-centres]]**' carrier-neutral Nairobi campus — a partnership of
  **Atlancis Technologies**, EverseTech and iXAfrica running AI workloads near where African data is
  generated. Atlancis co-founder Daniel Njuguna framed sovereignty as retaining "talent, revenue and
  skills" on the continent, not merely local data storage. See [[KEN]], [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]].
  Source: [[2026-03-12 iXAfrica hosts Kenya's first sovereign-hosted AI cloud]].
- **Sovereign-hosted AI (2026-06).** Presented at **AI Everything Kenya x GITEX
  Kenya 2026** as letting local organisations run AI workloads **without sending
  data offshore**. EverseTech CEO **Michael Michie** argued sovereignty should
  mean control of sensitive data and critical workloads, not isolation, calling
  for a **UAE–Kenya–East Africa digital corridor** of concrete pilots ("managed
  interdependence"). See [[KEN]], [[tech.ai]], [[uae]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-18 UAE-Kenya Tech Partnership Gains Momentum as AI Investments Grow]].
- Previously cited (as hosted on IXAfrica) in the Nairobi data-centre market —
  see [[KEN]], [[2026-06-23 Africa's Data Centre Boom Where The New Facilities Are Being Built]].
