# Mauritius National AI Strategy (AI for Mauritius / AIM) + FAIR Guidelines

Mauritius's national AI strategy — branded **AI for Mauritius (AIM)** — launched **9 April 2026** by the
**AI Unit** (Minister Ramtohul; President Gokhool; PM Ramgoolam), aligned to the
**[[mauritius-digital-blueprint-2025-2029|Digital Transformation Blueprint 2025-2029]]**. It sets a vision of
**regional leadership in responsible, human-centred AI** ("strengthen human judgement, not replace it") across
public services, climate resilience, education and growth.

**Six strategic pillars:** infrastructure, skills, innovation, governance, sectoral adoption, international
cooperation. Unveiled initiatives: the **AIM portal** (aim.govmu.org), a **Regional AI Marketplace**, the
**KOREK Super App** (single window for public services), the **DIVA** virtual assistant, and **AI for All**.

## FAIR Guidelines — governance-led, ethics before execution

The accompanying **FAIR Guidelines** (**Fairness, Accountability, Inclusiveness, Integrity**, plus
responsibility) make governance the *starting point*. Distinctively:

- **Vendor-neutral and border-agnostic** — any AI system operating in Mauritius, whatever its origin, must
  comply; imported tools face the same scrutiny as domestic ones (governance by **impact, not geography**).
- Covers the full lifecycle (design → deployment → monitoring → decommissioning).
- **Mandatory bias audits** in high-risk sectors (fintech, gaming).
- Foreign providers must **designate locally based representatives** accountable for outcomes.
- Currently **non-binding** (no immediate penalties) but on a legal/policy trajectory to shape procurement,
  sector regulation and future legislation — a **governance-led model** distinct from Nigeria/Kenya (scale) and
  South Africa (statutory penalties). See [[gov.standards]], [[mauritius--tech-ai]].
