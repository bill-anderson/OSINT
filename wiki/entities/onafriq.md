# Onafriq (formerly MFS Africa)

Pan-African payments network (rebranded from MFS Africa) that interconnects mobile-money and
bank rails across the continent, acting as the interoperability layer for cross-border digital
payments. Founder/CEO **Dare Okoudjou**; connects ~**1bn mobile wallets and 500m bank
accounts across 43 African countries**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **DRC stablecoin pilot (early July 2026).** Onafriq is a partner in the USD-stablecoin
  cross-border pilot in the DRC ([[COD]]) alongside [[visa]] and [[safaricom]]'s M-Pesa —
  see [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]]. Tags [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]].
  Source: [[2026-07-10 Visa and M-Pesa Launch Blockchain Stablecoin Pilot in DR Congo]].

- **Visa Pay API in DRC (2025-09).** Onafriq provided the API through which [[visa]] launched
  Visa Pay in the DRC in September 2025. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Visa and M-Pesa Launch Blockchain Stablecoin Pilot in DR Congo]].

- **DRC digital-finance expansion (2026-01).** Despite eastern-DRC conflict, Onafriq is
  deepening [[COD]] payments infrastructure — it enabled [[visa]]'s Visa Pay DRC launch (Q4
  2025, card–mobile-money interoperability at scale). DRC had **29m mobile-money accounts /
  34.6% penetration** (Q4 2024), with the central bank (BCC) supportive; Okoudjou frames DRC
  as an "underserved giant" and an early-cycle interoperability market. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-26 How Onafriq is growing digital finance amid uncertainty in DRC]].

- **PAPSS Nigeria→Ghana trade rail (2026-02).** With [[papss]], Onafriq is piloting
  wallet-based outbound payments from Nigeria to Ghana **fully in Naira** (no hard-currency
  conversion), CBN-approved, a six-month pilot from 1 Dec — connecting bank and mobile-money
  silos and supporting [[afcfta]] operationalisation. PAPSS network cited at 160+ banks /
  400m+ accounts across 19 countries. See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[XWA]], [[NGA]],
  [[GHA]]. Source: [[2026-02-04 PAPSS, Onafriq launch new trade rail for West Africa]].

- **Cited among the cross-border aggregators (analysis, 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki's "payments
  end-game" essay lists Onafriq (**40 countries**) alongside Flutterwave, Moniepoint and OPay as
  the African payment aggregators whose processing margins, he argues, will compress as instant
  rails commoditise — durable value moving to the credit, data and compliance layers. Analyst
  framing, attributed to Kariuki. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]].
