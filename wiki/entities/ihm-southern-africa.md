# IHM Southern Africa

**The technology firm behind Zambia's national EHR** — through its technology division
**TuSo Business Dynamics**, IHM Southern Africa built **[[smartcare-pro]]** in partnership
with the Government of Zambia. Its software development team also **delivered the
[[mosip]] training** to [[smart-zambia-institute]]'s e-government developers. It is the
private implementer sitting inside two public layers at once — the health MIS
([[dpi.mis]]) and the identity integration ([[dpi.id]]) that connects to it.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Delivered MOSIP training to SMART Zambia (2025-01-17).** **IHM's software development
  team** conducted the specialised MOSIP training for developers from
  [[smart-zambia-institute]]'s e-government division, concentrated on **integrating MOSIP
  with the SmartCarePro EHR** for e-KYC and a "central source of truth." Notably, the
  training was **explicitly aimed at enabling Zambian developers to independently manage and
  maintain the platform** — a vendor whose stated brief includes reducing dependence on
  vendors. Funded via the **$100m [[world-bank]] grant** through **[[dzap]]**. Source:
  [[2025-01-17 Zambia Advances Digital ID System with MOSIP Training for Healthcare Integration]].
- **Built SmartCare Pro (reported 2026-01-15).** The Government of Zambia launched
  [[smartcare-pro]] "in partnership with IHM Southern Africa's technology division TuSo
  Business Dynamics" — a centralised EHR using the National Digital ID service, described as
  live in rural Kafulafuta. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 How Digital Public Infrastructure Is Transforming Health and Identity in Rural Zambia]].

## Provenance

**Both sources are the implementer's own account, and the 2026-01 one is authored by IHM
alongside [[co-develop]]** and published on Co-Develop's dpi.africa — promotional, with a
single patient testimony and **no figures, baseline or evaluation**. Capture is not
endorsement: the wiki holds **no independent evidence** of IHM's delivery record, contract
value, procurement route or performance, and no account of how it came to hold both the EHR
build and the identity-integration training. Those are structural absences, not findings
against the firm.
