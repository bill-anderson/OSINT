# E-MARK Innovation Hub

Zambian innovation hub running youth AI and robotics talent programmes. CEO: **Emmanuel Mwanza
(2026-07)**.

## What matters now

- **Prepared Team Zambia for the 2026 Robotics for Good Youth Challenge (2026-07-17).** Mwanza
  described a **nine-month** cycle of identifying, mentoring and preparing Zambia's delegation for
  the international competition, which reached the global finals in Geneva among **nearly 50
  participating countries (2026)**. The hub co-organised the national recognition ceremony with
  the **[[presidential-delivery-unit-zambia|PDU]]** and the
  **[[ministry-of-technology-and-science-zambia|Ministry of Technology and Science]]**.
- **Positions taken.** Mwanza argued the qualification demonstrates Zambia's talent pool but that
  **greater investment in research, technology and innovation is needed**, and welcomed the
  proposed **[[itu]]-backed AI for Good Lab**, calling for stronger national investment in AI
  infrastructure, digital skills and innovation ecosystems.
- **A named channel into the school system.** The Ministry of Education's Permanent Secretary
  (Administration), Noriana Muneku, encouraged closer collaboration with E-MARK to identify and
  nurture technology talent across the country, **including in rural communities** — see
  [[digital.rural]], [[capacity.training]].

## Why it matters here

E-MARK is the **non-state delivery layer** in Zambia's AI-skills story: the state supplies
framing, ceremony and the forthcoming [[zambia-national-ai-strategy]], while the identification
and mentoring pipeline that produced the result sits in a private hub, run — on Mwanza's own
account — under resource constraints. The wiki holds **no figures** on E-MARK's size, funding,
participant numbers or geographic reach.
