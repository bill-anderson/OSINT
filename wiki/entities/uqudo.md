# Uqudo

**A digital identity verification provider partnered with Tunisia for verification of passports and
national IDs.** In the material held here Uqudo sits on the KYC/AML side of the identity stack —
document authentication rather than credential issuance — and enters the wiki through a single
roundup mention. Its ownership, headquarters, founding and wider African footprint are **not
established** by any source held; this page will remain thin until better evidence arrives.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Tunisia identity-verification partnership (as of 2025-03).** Uqudo **has partnered with Tunisia
  to provide secure digital identity verification for passports and national IDs**, using **AI,
  biometrics and cognitive document analysis** to verify identity data against the **MRZ
  (machine-readable zone) field** for **KYC/AML** purposes. The counterparty, contract, date and
  scale are not stated. See [[dpi.id]], [[tech.ai]], [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].

*Context worth keeping straight: MRZ-based document verification reads the machine-readable zone of
an existing travel or identity document. It is therefore adjacent to — but not the same as — the
biometric card programme under [[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]] or the mobile-ID credential
[[e-houwiya]]. No source held links Uqudo to either.*
