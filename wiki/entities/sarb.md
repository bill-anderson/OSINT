# South African Reserve Bank (SARB)

South Africa's central bank and the regulator of the National Payment System (NPS Act, 1998).
Under its Vision 2025 it is opening the NPS — long reserved for banks — to non-bank payment
service providers, and it operates the country's instant and cross-border retail rails.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Opening the NPS to non-bank PSPs, from 2026 (as of 2026-03).** SARB is moving from a
  bank-only NPS to activity-based licensing under Vision 2025, so fintechs can clear and settle
  without a sponsoring bank. As a bridge to the pending legislative amendment, SARB issued a 2025
  Exemption Notice and Draft Directive identifying seven payment-activity categories (e-money
  issuance, instrument issuance, acquiring, third-party services, remittances, clearing,
  settlement); Tier 1 e-money issuers (>R5m/month) face minimum-capital, fund-segregation and
  AML requirements, supervised by SARB and its Prudential Authority. Tags [[dpi.pay]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.policy]] in [[ZAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-17 South Africa's National Payment System PSP licences and the 2026 payments reform]].

- **Instant and cross-border rails (as of 2026-03).** SARB oversees PayShap (live 2023), which
  processed 100m+ transactions in its first year and raised its per-transaction limit to R50,000,
  and participates in the SADC TCIB scheme for real-time multi-currency cross-border transfers on
  ISO 20022. Source: [[2026-03-17 South Africa's National Payment System PSP licences and the 2026 payments reform]].

- **SARS instant-payment partnership (2026-04).** SARB is partnering with [[sars]] to build a
  cost-effective instant payment system aimed at financial inclusion and reducing cash in
  circulation. Source: [[2026-04-03 Say goodbye to SARS tax numbers in South Africa]].

- **Payments Ecosystem Modernisation — 2026 outlook (Webber Wentzel).** SARB's **Payments
  Ecosystem Modernisation (PEM)** programme is cited as the vehicle opening payments to
  non-banks, alongside a broader SA fintech-reform year — an FSCA/Prudential Authority
  AI-in-finance survey (end-2025), an expected 2026 AI regulatory framework, 300
  crypto-asset-service-provider licences by end-2025 and "2026 the year of stablecoins,"
  with the COFI Bill still pending. See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-30 2026 fintech year ahead]].
- **PayShap in the "payments end-game" thesis (analysis, 2025-08 / 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki
  cites **PayShap** (live 2023) — with SARB having taken a stake via **PayInc** — as a South
  African instance of central banks turning instant payments into low-cost utilities, and in his
  six-country DPI-maturity read notes SARB favouring a **UPI-like** interoperable framework over
  today's high-cost card rails. Analyst framing, attributed to Kariuki. See [[dpi.pay]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]],
  [[2025-08-04 93 - Ecosystem Debt or Generative Platforms A Case for Digital Public Infrastructure]].
