# Digital Readiness Assessment - Eswatini (UNDP)

**Eswatini's first Digital Readiness Assessment (DRA)** — an application of the [[undp]]
DRA instrument, conducted by [[ministry-of-ict-eswatini]] with UNDP and [[eseparc]].
Eswatini was among the first countries in the African region to implement the DRA. It
scores digital maturity across **six pillars: connectivity, people, digital public
infrastructure, government, regulation, economy**. See [[SWZ]], [[gov.policy]].

> **Reference study — cite, don't absorb.** This is a country-level reference work. Its
> figures belong to it and are dated to it; do not promote them onto concept or place
> pages as current state.

## What it found

- **Overall: "systematic" stage, score 2.6 out of 5 (2024)** per [[eseparc]]. Strengths
  in the **people, economy and government** pillars; **digital public infrastructure,
  connectivity and regulation** lag.
- **Government.** A national digital strategy is in place but with only moderate
  political support and limited institutional coordination; progress in digitalising
  tax and utility services.
- **DPI.** Duplicated processes and **absence of an interoperability framework** are
  major limitations; comprehensive digital ID, data exchange and digital payments all
  named as areas to develop.
- **Connectivity.** High costs and limited infrastructure; **poor internet connectivity
  especially in schools** remains critical despite public Wi-Fi. Calls for investment in
  scalable, energy-efficient data centres and network expansion for equitable urban and
  rural access.
- **People.** Strong basic literacy but significant gaps in digital competencies,
  particularly among older and rural populations; digital exclusion among the elderly,
  people with disabilities and rural populations.
- **Economy.** Digital economy at an early stage — limited payment systems, tech
  providers and financing options.
- **Regulation and cybersecurity.** Notable progress on data protection and
  cybersecurity frameworks, but **enforcement remains inconsistent**.

## Publication sequence

There was **one release event, on 2024-11-13**. The survey was launched as a stakeholder
exercise on **2024-08-21** (UN House, Mbabane), with fieldwork to **2024-09-13**. The
**results launch, validation workshop and formal handover were a single occasion on
2024-11-13** at the Hilton Garden Hotel, Mbabane (panel: UNDP, MICT, [[mtn-eswatini]],
[[world-bank]], InstaCash, [[itu]]), where UNDP Resident Representative **Henrik Franklin**
handed the report to Minister [[savannah-maziya]] — per UNDP Eswatini's own press release
published that day.

The later accounts are retrospective write-ups, not separate events: Biometric Update
(**2024-11-22**), [[eseparc]] (**2024-12-05**) and UNDP's own blog (**2024-12-10**). The
blog **asserts no handover date at all** — "10 December 2024" is its CMS publication stamp,
and its text refers to the launch in the past tense. The apparent five-day discrepancy was
an artefact of reading publication dates as event dates. The PDF held in `raw/` is dated
**2024-11** at month precision.
