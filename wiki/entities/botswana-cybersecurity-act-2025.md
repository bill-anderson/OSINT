# Botswana Cybersecurity Act 2025 / National Cybersecurity Authority

Botswana's **cybersecurity law**, passed **November 2025**, establishing a **National Cybersecurity
Authority** to protect **critical information infrastructure** and build trust in digital services — the
governance layer completing Botswana's data-protection + e-government stack. See [[BWA]], [[infra.cybersec]].

## What we know

- **The Act + Authority (2025-11).** [[gov.legislate]] Passed Nov 2025, it creates a **National
  Cybersecurity Authority** over critical information infrastructure — pitched alongside the
  [[digital-services-act-botswana|Digital Services Act]] as the trust foundation for digital services.
  Source: [[2026-05-25-botswana-cybersecurity-act-national-authority-wtisd-itweb]].
- **A live threat picture.** [[infra.cybersec]] A first cyber-incident signal appeared in BOCRA's 2024
  annual report — a spike tied to **Russian-intelligence-linked APT28 / Moobot** botnet activity; the
  **2026 National Cyber Drill** ([[bocra]] CEO Martin Mokgware) flagged **AI, IoT and quantum** threats;
  and police signalled tougher **Cybercrime Act** enforcement on social media. Earlier, a **UK Africa
  Cyber Programme** resilience exercise (2025) predated the Act. On the upside, **TransUnion** put
  Botswana at **0.9% suspected digital fraud in H1 2026 — the lowest in Africa**. Sources:
  [[2025-02-16-russian-intelligence-linked-hackers-attack-botswana]],
  [[2026-03-09-cyber-drill-sounds-alarm-ai-iot-quantum-threats]],
  [[2026-06-15-botswana-records-lowest-suspected-digital-fraud]].

- **Awareness + literacy.** [[capacity.literacy]] Public-facing work runs alongside: **BOCRA** ICT/CIRT
  training in schools, and consumer campaigns against elderly-targeted scams (e.g. a Mascom workshop in
  Pitsane). Sources: [[2026-01-22-bocra-ict-training-schools-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-06-22-grandparents-fight-back-against-cyber-scammers]].
