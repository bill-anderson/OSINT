# Samora Kariuki

African digital-financial-services analyst and author of **Frontier Fintech**
(frontierfintech.substack.com), a widely-read Substack on African payments,
fintech infrastructure and digital public infrastructure. Kenya-based; writes
structurally about payments-market economics across Nigeria, Kenya, South Africa
and the wider continent. His work is cited in this wiki as external expert
**analysis**, not primary evidence.

## What he argues in this wiki

- **Payments are becoming a public utility (as of 2026-02).** In *#111 — Winning
  But Losing*, Kariuki argues African payment aggregators face the same
  margin-collapse that hit Brazil's StoneCo/PagSeguro (post-Pix) and India's
  PhonePe/Paytm (post-UPI): once low-cost, central-bank-mandated instant-payment
  systems become utilities, transaction volume grows but processing take-rates
  collapse, so durable value migrates to credit, data-intelligence and compliance
  layers rather than the "pipe." See [[dpi.pay]], [[tech.industry]].
- **Payments follow trade — build the Africa-Asia corridor.** In *#117* (a
  partner piece with [[waka]]), he argues Africa's cross-border rails should be
  rebuilt around the Africa-China trade corridor, using stablecoins as invisible
  backend settlement plus two-sided compliance capability. See [[geopol.china]].
- **DPI as a public good.** In *#93*, he contrasts "Ecosystem Debt" (closed
  platforms like M-Pesa) with "Generative Public Platforms" (India Stack, Pix),
  scoring six African DPI markets on his own 1-5 maturity rubric — a framework,
  not an authoritative index.
- **Infrastructure first (2021 backgrounder).** *#31* argues foundational
  identity/address/shared-rail infrastructure must precede consumer fintech;
  historical framing, dated figures.
