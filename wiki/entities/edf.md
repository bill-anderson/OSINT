# EDF (Électricité de France)

**The French utility that owns half of [[dpa-southern-africa]] — the South African joint venture that is the actual counterparty to the [[africa-data-centres]] solar-wheeling PPAs.** EDF is the foreign principal behind a build the wiki had, until 2026-07-20, attributed wholly to [[distributed-power-africa]].

*Minted on 2026-07-20 from dead-link triage: EDF carried ten body references across six pages while having no page. It rests on **one held source** — the 2023-03-14 Cassava announcement — so treat everything below as single-sourced.*

## Activity in this wiki

- **The 50:50 JV (announced 2022-05-24, recorded via the 2023 announcement).** EDF acquired a **50% shareholding in DPA's South African operations**, forming **DPA Southern Africa (Pty) Ltd**, a separately registered South African company. The stated intent, per the announcer: through the JV **"EDF intends to develop hybrid energy solutions for clients across Africa"** — i.e. South Africa as the entry point to a continental commercial-and-industrial energy business, not a one-project vehicle.
- **The 20-year, 12 MW wheeled-solar PPA (2023-03-14).** DPA SA — not DPA — signed the 20-year PPA supplying **12 MW** of solar to [[africa-data-centres]]' South African facilities, wheeled across the **[[eskom]]** grid partly from a solar farm near **Bloemfontein**. **Valérie Levkov**, EDF's Senior VP Africa and Middle East: *"This agreement with Africa Data Centres re-affirms EDF's commitment to provide low carbon solutions for commercial and industrial client in Africa."*
- **The identity correction it forces.** Because EDF holds half of DPA SA, the South African solar history belongs to a jointly foreign-owned company and **must not be carried onto [[econet-infraco]]**, the Zimbabwe-registered company sharing the DPA brand. See [[distributed-power-africa]] for the three-company split.

Source for all of the above: [[2023-03-14-cassava-adc-dpa-sa-edf-20-year-ppa]].
