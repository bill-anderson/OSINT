# Uganda e-Government Interoperability Framework (e-GIF)

**Uganda's published interoperability framework for government systems**, dated
**6 May 2021** and issued by the Government of Uganda (MoICT&NG / [[nita-uganda]]). It is
the reference instrument governing how Ugandan government systems exchange data, and the
companion to the [[uganda-digital-government-strategy]].

## Structure

- **Twelve underlying principles** — including subsidiarity/proportionality, openness,
  transparency, reusability, technological neutrality and data portability,
  user-centricity, inclusion and accessibility, security, and privacy.
- **Six interoperability layers** — governance, integrated public service governance,
  legal, organisational, semantic, and technical.
- **Conceptual model** — includes base registries, open data and catalogues.
- Project duration printed as **10 Nov 2020 – 9 Sep 2021**. No version number appears on
  the cover, unlike its companion Digital Government Strategy v1.1.

Source: [[2021-05-06 Uganda e-Government Interoperability Framework (e-GIF)]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Renewed (2026-05-27).** [[nita-uganda]], with the **[[e-governance-academy]]** and
  [[world-bank]] support, **renewed the e-GIF** alongside the Government Enterprise
  Architecture — the renewal launched at a Kampala workshop concluded **2026-02-18** and
  delivered by **2026-05-27**. The renewed e-GIF is built for **secure, standardised,
  seamless data exchange across government systems**, with the GEA realigned to Uganda
  Vision 2040; the stated purpose is to reduce duplication and prevent fragmentation across
  MDAs and local governments. NITA-U's **Brenda Maniraguha**: *"The blueprint ensures
  government systems are designed to collaborate together and not grow in isolation."*
  **Rollout is not yet reported** — delivered, not implemented. See [[gov.standards]],
  [[geopol.eu]]. Sources: [[2026-02-18-ega-uganda-launches-renewal-gea-egif]],
  [[2026-05-27-ega-uganda-renewed-digital-government-frameworks]].
