# Angola Digital Government Bill (Proposta de Lei do Governo Digital)

Angola's **draft Digital Government Law** — a framework bill (Lei-Quadro do Governo Digital) intended to end the **"dispersão legislativa"** (legislative fragmentation) that has governed the ICT sector since Angola's digitisation began in **1997**.

## Profile

- **Public consultation opened 6 May 2026.** Led by the **[[ima-angola]]** (Instituto de Modernização Administrativa; DG Meick Afonso), with contributions collected through end-May 2026. The diagnosis: sector-by-sector legislating prevents "a single view of the State over the citizen," producing bureaucratic, contradictory procedures.
- **Explicit alignment with data protection and cybersecurity.** The stated aim is that "the digital law and the data-protection law move in step, without creating blockages" — flagging conflict-of-norms risk with the [[angola-data-protection-law-22-11]] revision and the cybersecurity track, and proposing joint work with legal specialists. Prof. Carlos Feijó argued for institutionalising a dignity-of-public-rights regime to overcome fragmentation across courts, ministries and public-rights bodies.
- **Context.** Part of the wider **Governo Digital 2027** agenda. See [[angola--gov-protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2026-05-06-consulta-publica-proposta-de-lei-do-governo-digital]].
