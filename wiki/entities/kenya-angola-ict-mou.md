# Kenya–Angola ICT Cooperation MoU (2025)

A bilateral ICT-cooperation Memorandum of Understanding agreed on the sidelines of the **Luanda Summit on Financing Africa's Infrastructure Development** (the "Transformative Week for Financing Africa's Infrastructure Development"), late October 2025. Signed between Kenya's Cabinet Secretary for the National Treasury and Planning, **John Mbadi**, Angola's Minister of Telecommunications **[[mario-oliveira]]** ([[ministerio-das-telecomunicacoes-tecnologias-de-informacao-e-comunicacao-social-angola]]), and Kenya's Ambassador to Angola, Joyce Khasimwa M'maitsi; technical teams were to begin work immediately.

## Terms

- Covers technological development, **satellite networks**, ICT training, digital-economy growth, **rural connectivity**, **cybersecurity** and **digital literacy**.
- Sits inside a summit convened around infrastructure finance — **[[african-development-bank]]**, **[[world-bank]]** and **AUDA-NEPAD** present — with an Investors–Ministerial Roundtable on financing data centres, fibre and IXPs.
- Diaspora/trade dimension: new **direct Nairobi–Luanda flights** framed as an enabler of ICT, fintech and logistics ties.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: an **East–Southern Africa** bilateral struck at an Africa-financing summit — Angola positioning Luanda as a digital-diplomacy venue, and both states pursuing intra-African "digital corridors" over dependence on external hubs.

## Provenance note

The 2025-10-31 ITWeb Africa report's **opening line mis-states the parties as "Kenya and Uganda"** — an error in the original; its headline, body and the two corroborating accounts (Connecting Africa, TechAfrica News) confirm the MoU is **Kenya–Angola**.
