# Digital Realty

US-headquartered global data-centre and colocation operator (300+ facilities worldwide; $4.49bn revenue / $1.22bn net profit for the first nine months of 2025). It built its African footprint through acquisition — the $29m Medallion deal (Nigeria, 2021, within a $500m ten-year Africa plan) and the **$3.5bn Teraco acquisition** (South Africa, 2022) — and operates ~15 African facilities (South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya, Mozambique and now Ghana). Its African expansion is a leading edge of the US-hyperscaler-adjacent [[geopol.usa]] build-out of the continent's colocation capacity ([[infra.store]]). See [[GHA]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **ACR2 Accra data centre inaugurated (2025-11-04).** [[infra.store]] Digital Realty opened **ACR2** in Accra (end-Oct 2025): **1.7 MW** installed IT capacity, **500 racks over 1,100 sqm**, connected to subsea/fibre routes including **2Africa** ([[2africa-cable]]) and to Digital Realty's global platform — pitched to banks, fintechs, telecoms and cloud providers as London/Amsterdam/Johannesburg-grade infrastructure "without leaving Accra." Follows an August 2025 fourth Lagos facility. Context: Africa held <2% of global colocation capacity (mid-2023); market projected $1.94bn (2025) → $3.85bn (2030), 14.7% CAGR (Mordor). See [[GHA]], [[geopol.usa]], [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2025-11-04 Digital Realty Expands African Footprint With New Data Center in Ghana (ACR2)]].
