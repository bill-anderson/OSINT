# Burkina Faso national data centres / "zero data abroad"

The concrete infrastructure behind Burkina Faso's **digital-sovereignty doctrine** — repatriating state
(and eventually private) data onto national soil under a **"zéro donnée de l'administration à
l'extérieur"** ("zero data abroad") policy. It is the most tangible expression of the Traoré government's
framing of digital as an instrument of national and Sahel sovereignty. See [[BFA]], [[infra.store]],
[[burkina-faso--dpi-id]].

## What we know

- **Two mini data centres commissioned (2026-01-23).** [[infra.store]] PM Rimtalba Jean Emmanuel
  Ouédraogo commissioned two mini data centres for public administration: **~3,000 TB** (a tenfold
  increase), **7,000+ virtual machines** (100–300 per ministry), **16bn FCFA** cost, projected **~30bn
  FCFA savings over five years** (an editorial cites **85–95bn over ten years**) from ending foreign
  hosting — operated by **national technical teams**, putting Burkina among the sub-region's top three for
  storage capacity. Sources: [[2026-01-23-burkina-faso-asserts-digital-sovereignty-mini-data-centres]],
  [[2026-01-27-nos-donnees-rentrent-a-la-maison]].
- **Toward a national Big Data Centre (due ~2028).** The mini centres are an **intermediate milestone**
  toward a national Big Data Centre meant to fully repatriate public and private data currently hosted
  abroad. Source: [[2026-01-25-gouvernement-inaugure-deux-datacenters]].
- **The doctrine is a circular, not (yet) hard law.** [[gov.legislate]] Per digital-transformation DG
  **Teg-Wendé Idriss Tinto**, national hosting rests on a **PM circular**, not the platforms decree; health
  data needs a **derogation** to leave the country; a **CIL declaration** is a precondition to operating a
  state platform; and the interoperability referential still **dates to 2018, unrevised**. The governing
  decree on state digital platforms (signed **2024-05-08**, widely reported only in 2026) targets
  interoperability and an end to siloed, duplicated ministry platforms. Source:
  [[2026-03-03-tinto-hebergement-national-souverainete]].
- **Supervised centrally.** A **Digital Infrastructure Supervision Center** (R+5, ~3bn FCFA, delivery
  ~Oct 2026) will centralise oversight of the backbone, cyberspace and data-centre operations. Source:
  [[2026-01-20-digital-infrastructure-monitoring-center-construction]].
