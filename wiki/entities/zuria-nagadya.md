# Zuria Nagadya

Author of **"Africa in the Room"** (africaintheroom.substack.com), a popular-register
newsletter on AI governance for an African business/policy audience. Writes advocacy-styled
explainers (each piece closes with a promoted "AI Readiness Audit" product) that translate
technical governance material for non-specialist readers. Her work is treated here as external
expert **analysis**, not primary evidence.

## What she argues in this wiki

A single, recurring thesis across four 2026 pieces: Africa should not *copy* imported AI
governance but **extend** it from the realities of the informal economy — mobile money, chamas,
~2000 languages and communal (not only individual) redress.

- **The EU AI Act binds Africa already** ([[2026-05-18 Europe Wrote The World's Most Advanced AI Law. The EU AI Act]]) — walks the four risk tiers and the "where the output lands" extraterritorial test; argues Africa should keep the risk-based model but add informal-economy, language-accuracy and communal-redress provisions. See [[eu-ai-act]].
- **A binding African framework is missing** ([[2026-05-04 The World's Most Needed AI Governance Framework Doesn't Exist Yet. Africa Should Build It]]) — the AU Continental AI Strategy, [[africa-ai-declaration|Kigali Declaration]], Rwanda's Responsible AI Office, Nigeria's [[cbn]] fintech gate and Kenya's data-sovereignty principle all share one blind spot: the informal economy.
- **Facial-recognition governance** ([[2026-06-15 If The Technology Can't See Us Accurately, We Build The Rules That Make It]]) — catalogues large African biometric-surveillance spends and the absence of legal safeguards; proposes pre-deployment accuracy testing on African faces.
- **Data sovereignty in the courts** ([[2026-06-08 Kenyan Citizens Took A $2.5 Billion Deal With America To Court To Fight For Their Health Data]]) — narrates [[consumer-federation-of-kenya|COFEK]]'s litigation against the Kenya–US health deal as data sovereignty in action.
