# Somalia Data Protection Act (Law No. 005 of 2023)

Somalia's first comprehensive data-protection law — **Law No. 005 of 2023**, effective
**23 March 2023** (two days after presidential assent), ratified by both parliamentary
chambers and signed by President Hassan Sheikh Mohamud. Grounded in Article 19 of the 2012
Constitution, it establishes the [[somalia-data-protection-authority]] to oversee the
processing and protection of personal — and "public" — data within Somali territory.

## Key provisions

- **Scope (Arts 4-5)** — controllers based/operating in Somalia processing data in
  Somalia; exemptions for household use, public-health emergencies, crime
  prevention/investigation, national security, and defence of a legal claim.
- **Lawful bases (Art 14)** — consent, contract, legal obligation, vital/public/legitimate
  interest, plus additional bases (legal claim, health/humanitarian emergency, medical
  care, archiving/statistics, data made public by the subject).
- **Data-subject rights (Arts 20-23)** — access, rectification, deletion, withdrawal of
  consent, objection, and protection from solely automated decisions.
- **Accountability** — processor inventories and agreements (Art 19); security (Art 24);
  **DPIA** for high-risk processing (Art 29); **DPO** within six months for controllers of
  major importance (Art 34); **mandatory registration** of controllers of major importance
  (Art 32). Annual audits for controllers processing >1,000 data subjects in six months.
- **Penalties** — fines up to **2-4% of worldwide annual turnover**.

## Analytical flags (lens: sovereignty)

- Introduces a novel category, **"public data," but does not define it** — casting
  uncertainty over the Authority's mandate (CIPIT/Strathmore).
- **Independence concerns** — although Art 6 declares the Authority independent, its head
  is proposed by the Minister, confirmed by Cabinet and appointed by the President (Art 13),
  and Art 6(3) requires deference to executive direction in national-security/public-order
  matters — a broad scope for executive influence.

Not to be confused with the separate **Regulatory Framework for the Public Data Protection
Law** adopted by the Council of Ministers in Feb 2026 (see [[SOM]] hub) or the
[[somalia-cybersecurity-law]] (2026).
