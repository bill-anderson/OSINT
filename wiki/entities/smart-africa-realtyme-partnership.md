# Smart Africa–RealTyme partnership

Alliance between the [[smart-africa]] Digital Academy and Swiss zero-trust firm
[[realtyme]], announced 2026-04-16, to establish a continental "national
protection framework."

- **Parties:** [[smart-africa]] (Digital Academy) and [[realtyme]].
- **What:** A 2026 rollout on three pillars framed as "digital decolonisation" —
  (1) absolute data sovereignty via state-controlled communication enclaves that
  keep sensitive intelligence within national borders; (2) tactical AI governance
  ([[tech.ai]]) against deepfake-driven disinformation; (3) post-quantum
  resilience to safeguard state secrets.
- **Positioning:** Explicitly a defensive counter-measure to foreign laws such as
  the US Cloud Act, which lets external jurisdictions subpoena data on
  international servers; positioned against both Silicon Valley and Chinese tech
  giants. Koné: Smart Africa is "no longer just digitising but actively
  defending."
- **Stated rationale:** African nations face a claimed ~60% higher cyber-attack
  rate than the global average (partnership figure, not independently verified
  here).
- **Status:** Launched; rollout through 2026.
