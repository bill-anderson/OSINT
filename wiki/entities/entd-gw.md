# ENTD.GW (National Digital Transformation Strategy for Guinea-Bissau 2025-2030)

Guinea-Bissau's national digital-transformation strategy (Estratégia Nacional de Transformação Digital), developed since July 2024 by **[[undp]]** Bissau with **UNU-EGOV** (project manager Beatriz Lanza) and officially launched **29 January 2025** ([[gov.policy]]). It is built on **ten strategic pillars** (infrastructure, data, economy, people, literacy, governance, innovation, trust, sustainability, public services), with six objectives and 55 interventions, plus a Roadmap to 2030. A citizen-centred blueprint for public-sector digitisation in one of West Africa's least-connected states; as of early 2025 it awaited Council of Ministers approval. See [[GNB]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launch + approval push (2025-01/02).** [[dpi.govtech]] Launched **29 Jan 2025**: PM **Rui Duarte
  Barros** called it "a historic milestone" toward "a more digital, inclusive and prosperous"
  Guinea-Bissau, Minister **Marciano Silva Barbeiro** framed it as an ambitious vision, and UNDP
  Resident Representative **Alessandra Casazza** pledged implementation support — the domestic ANG
  account naming the 2025–2030 period. UNU-EGOV/UNDP then met the PM (31 Jan 2025) on the approval
  decree, the telecom market and the Universal Access Fund; a capacity-building event trained ~50
  officials (with ITMA). Sources: [[2025-01-29 Guinea-Bissau launches ENTD.GW national digital transformation strategy (ANG)]], [[2025-02-04 Digital Transformation Project in Guinea-Bissau with UNU-EGOV & UNDP]], [[2024-01-01 National Strategy for Digital Transformation in Guinea-Bissau (project)]].
- **Governance/data pillars materialise — three decrees (2025-07-10).** [[gov.policy]] The Council of
  Ministers approved draft decrees on **interoperability of public/private digital systems**, a
  **National Data Governance and Protection Policy**, and a **National Cybersecurity Strategy** —
  turning the strategy's data, governance and trust pillars into concrete instruments. Full case:
  [[guinea-bissau--gov-policy]]. Source: [[2025-07-10 Guinea-Bissau Council of Ministers approves three digital decrees (interoperability, data governance, cybersecurity)]].
- **Infrastructure axis — learning from Chad (2025-08).** [[infra.connect]] The strategy's six-axis framing includes digital infrastructure; GNB sought Chad's fibre-deployment experience for a national backbone (international connectivity is coast-confined via ACE). Source: [[2025-08-29 Fibre-optic deployment - Guinea-Bissau wants to learn from Chad]].
