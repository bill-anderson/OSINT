# Trans-Saharan Fiber Optic Backbone (Dorsale Transsaharienne, DTS)

**A trans-Saharan fibre backbone and a key component of [[TCD]]'s regional interconnection
strategy** — the effort to give landlocked Chad international connectivity routes beyond its
historic, costly dependence on Cameroon. Reported **60% complete as of 2026-02-13**.

## Status

- **60% complete (as of 2026-02-13).** The DTS is described as a key component of a broader
  regional interconnection strategy that includes **planned links to Niger, Nigeria, Algeria,
  Libya and Egypt**, and runs alongside the separate rehabilitation of the **N'Djamena–Mberé**
  fibre link, which diversifies international routes via Cameroon. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Chad Plans 50 Telecom Sites, 50 km of Fiber in N'Djamena]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **[[NER]] Niger component — 1,031 km received + a Tier III data centre (2025-11).** Niger took **provisional
  acceptance** (14 Nov 2025, at Niger Telecoms) of **1,031 km of fibre on five cross-border axes** (to Algeria,
  Chad, Nigeria, Benin and Burkina Faso) plus an **88-km urban loop to the future national data centre**, under
  the **AfDB-financed** DTS/FO (~**30bn FCFA / €43m**), alongside a **Tier III data centre in Niamey** billed as
  guaranteeing "Niger's digital sovereignty." Fibre laid by the LORYNE grouping; framed within Tiani's
  "Programme pour la Refondation." See [[niger--infra-cybersec]], [[infra.store]], [[african-development-bank]].
  Source: [[2025-11-17-trans-sahara-fibre-backbone-1031km-tier3-datacentre-lesahel]].
- **A possible EU-funded relative — not equated here.** A **2023** [[world-bank]] document
  detailing Chad's wider digital-transformation plans referenced a **"Trans-Saharan Corridor
  Project, funded by the EU"**, alongside an Internet Exchange Point and [[pmice]]. **No source
  held states that the EU-funded Trans-Saharan Corridor Project and the DTS are the same
  programme**, and they are not asserted to be. The similarity of name is recorded as a lead, not
  a finding. See [[geopol.eu]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-06 Chad invests 175m in national data center and networks]].

## Reading it

The DTS is the direct answer to the structural problem [[russell-southwood]] identifies in the
Chadian market: as a landlocked state facing civil unrest in five of its six neighbours, Chad's
historic fibre route through Cameroon is unreliable and expensive because of the Camtel wholesale
monopoly — with the wholesale price forming *a large percentage of the final retail internet
price* in Chad. A completed trans-Saharan route would break that. See [[sotel-tchad]] for the
parallel SudaChad concession and N'Djamena–Mberé corridor, and for how little the alternatives
have delivered so far.

The sources hold **no cost, financier, completion date, operator or route detail** for the DTS —
only the 60% figure. That is a thin basis for a project of this strategic weight.
