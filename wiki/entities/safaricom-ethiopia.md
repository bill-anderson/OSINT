# Safaricom Ethiopia

Safaricom's Ethiopia operator — a [[safaricom]]-led joint venture (CEO **Wim
Vanhelleputte**), the first private competitor to break [[ethio-telecom]]'s monopoly.
Entered [[ETH]] in 2021 on an **$850m licence** and runs M-PESA Ethiopia in competition
with [[telebirr]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Q3 FY26 update (period to 2025-12-31).** **12.2m** three-month active customers
  (+71.7% YoY); 4G covers **57.1%** of the population across 3,483 sites; service revenue
  **KES 9.68bn** (+54.2% YoY), with mobile data 67% of revenue. **M-PESA Ethiopia**
  reached **5.2m** three-month active customers (+258.5% YoY), transaction values more
  than doubling to ETB 20.37bn, after **[[ethswitch]] integration in October 2025**
  enabled full interoperability and EthQR merchant payments. Won first place at the
  Ethiopian Quality Award. See [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-02 Safaricom Ethiopia Surpasses 12 Million Users as M-PESA Adoption Grows]].
- **Structural handicaps (2025 World Bank).** Has deployed **$2.27bn** in capital since
  2021 but, lacking an open-access infrastructure regime, has **self-built ~60% of its
  sites**; competes against an incumbent that cross-subsidises data via voice revenue,
  keeping tariffs low. Illustrates a liberalisation still tilted toward the state operator.
  See [[sovereignty]] context in [[digital-ethiopia-2030]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-07 Why Ethiopia, Somalia and Djibouti are starting to matter in digital Africa]].

- **"M-PESA Lehulum" cross-network wallet (Russell Southwood, 2025-12-04).**
  Southwood notes Safaricom Ethiopia rolled out **M-PESA Lehulum**, a cross-network
  wallet — a competitive move against [[ethio-telecom]]'s [[telebirr]] incumbency in
  a market still tilted toward the state operator. See [[dpi.pay]], [[tech.industry]].
  Source: [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].
