# International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO)

**The UN specialised agency that sets the global standards for machine-readable and biometric travel
documents.** Held here not for aviation but because **ICAO's document standards are one of the
strongest external forces pushing African states toward biometric identity systems** — a deadline
set in Montreal shapes procurement in Tunis.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The 2026 recommendation as a driver (Tunisia, 2024-04).** Tunisian reporting on the ministerial
  session that targeted biometric ID and passport usage for H1 2025 cites, as rationale, that
  **ICAO has recommended all countries adopt biometric documents by 2026**. That recommendation is
  the external clock against which [[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]] and
  [[organic-law-2024-23-tunisia]] were being implemented. See [[TUN]], [[dpi.id]],
  [[gov.standards]]. Sources:
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia - Biometric ID card and passport usage to begin in H1 2025]],
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia to Get Biometric IDs in H1 of 2025]].

## Why it matters analytically

ICAO is a recurring, under-examined actor in this corpus: it supplies a **standards deadline that
governments cite as a reason for biometric adoption**, and that rationale travels well beyond the
passport into national ID design. When a state explains a domestic biometric programme by pointing
at an ICAO date, that is worth reading as what it is — an international standard doing domestic
political work, not a neutral technical fact.
