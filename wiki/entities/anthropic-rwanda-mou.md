# Anthropic–Rwanda Government AI Partnership (MoU)

**Parties:** [[anthropic]] and the Government of Rwanda ([[RWA]]).
**What:** a three-year, non-binding Memorandum of Understanding to deploy Anthropic's AI
across Rwanda's health ministry, public-sector agencies and education system.
**When/where:** signed in Kigali, **17 February 2026**; targets training up to **2,000
teachers and civil servants** ([[2026-03-04 Rwanda–Anthropic AI deal targets health, education gains]]).
**Status:** active; non-binding. Anthropic's **first multi-sector government deal in Africa**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Three coverage areas.** (1) Health — supporting Rwanda's goals of eliminating cervical
  cancer and reducing malaria and maternal mortality; (2) Public sector — **Claude, Claude
  Code, training and API access/credits** for government developers to integrate AI across
  government; (3) Education — deepening a **November 2025 ALX partnership**, building on a
  fall-2025 agreement (2,000 Claude Pro licences) and expanding **Chidi**, a Claude-powered
  learning companion, to students across **eight African countries**. Rwanda's ICT & Innovation
  minister **[[paula-ingabire]]** called it "an important milestone in Rwanda's AI journey";
  President [[paul-kagame]]'s government frames it as a calculated first-mover bet. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[capacity.training]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-04 U.S.-based AI firm Anthropic bags first multi-sector government deal in Africa despite policy pressures at home]],
  [[2026-03-17 Anthropic is Becoming the Backbone of Rwanda's Government. But Who is Accountable]].
- **Accountability critique.** Analysts (Tech Policy Press) flag an **accountability vacuum**:
  no external review was triggered — no parliamentary body, multilateral oversight or
  civil-society disclosure — because none exists for commercial AI partnerships of this kind.
  The concern is that a private company becomes "load-bearing infrastructure" across three of
  the most sensitive domains of public life before any governance framework defines its
  obligations (model deprecation, pricing, data-handling), echoing the Huawei 4G-dependency
  pattern. The [[african-union]]'s Continental AI Strategy has not yet applied its governance
  ambitions to such commercial partnerships. See [[gov.protect]], [[colonialism]],
  [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-17 Anthropic is Becoming the Backbone of Rwanda's Government. But Who is Accountable]].
- **Domestic contrast.** The deal was struck even as Anthropic faced US federal pressure
  (Department of War / "supply-chain risk" designation) to drop safety guardrails, which CEO
  Dario Amodei refused. Source:
  [[2026-03-04 U.S.-based AI firm Anthropic bags first multi-sector government deal in Africa despite policy pressures at home]].
