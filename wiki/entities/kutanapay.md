# KutanaPay

Accra-based trade-payments startup, **founded 2023** by **Nicholas Murphy** and **Samuel Opoku**,
building an escrow platform for cross-border B2B trade with AI analytics and blockchain components.

## What it does

- **Escrow as the core primitive.** Businesses create digital contracts defining transaction terms;
  the buyer deposits funds into escrow and the platform releases them only once both parties validate
  agreed milestones — shipment or delivery of goods. The pitch is reduction of **non-payment and
  non-delivery risk**, the two failure modes that make unfamiliar-counterparty African trade expensive.
- **Bundled around escrow:** cross-border payments, cargo insurance, dispute resolution, and trade
  finance aimed at the working-capital gap in international trade.
- **AI analytics layer** giving businesses insight into markets and trade flows.

## Positioning and what is *not* established

The company says it operates in **several African countries** and serves African diaspora members, and
that **transaction volumes have risen since commercial launch** (company statement, as reported
2026-07-17). **No country list, transaction volume, customer count, funding or licensing status is on
file** — every operational claim traces to the company via a single trade-press account, and none is
independently corroborated. The blockchain component is asserted rather than specified: the held source
does not say which chain, what is recorded on it, or whether settlement touches it at all.

The market framing is better evidenced than the company: **[[african-development-bank|AfDB]]** puts
Africa's annual trade-finance gap at **$74–92bn**, projected to reach **$86.6bn by 2027**, and
**[[afreximbank]]**'s *African Trade Report 2025* names limited trade-finance access, high cross-border
payment costs and weak financial infrastructure as persistent constraints — the demand side
**[[afcfta]]** implementation is expected to enlarge.
