# American AI Exports Program

US federal program (run by the **[[us-department-of-commerce]]** / ITA) to promote
export-ready, **full-stack** US AI systems abroad — "hardware, data, models,
cybersecurity, and sector-specific applications."

## Activity in this wiki

- **Application window closed (2026-07-07).** **78 applications** submitted by consortia.
  Designated packages get "coordinated federal support… priority government advocacy,
  federal financing access, and expedited export licensing review," aligned with US
  national-security and foreign-policy objectives (aiexports.gov).
- **Underwhelming launch (Politico, 2026-07-11).** The 78 first-round applications fell far
  below the **"hundreds"** officials had expected (per three former officials; ITA disputes
  a shortfall). AWS confirmed it applied and NVIDIA signalled interest, but **[[openai]]** and
  AMD were non-committal — several majors unnerved after the administration abruptly ordered
  restrictions on **[[anthropic]]**'s foreign model access, then **reversed** the order.
  **Phase 2** (deal bidding) had not yet launched. The wobble is read against Chinese
  open-weight models ([[deepseek]], Alibaba) gaining ground in developing countries. See
  [[geopol.china]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-11 'Not where they hoped it'd be' Launch of Trump AI promotion program underwhelms]].
- **Relevance to Africa.** A supply-side push of US AI stacks into foreign markets —
  the geopolitical counterpart to Africa's demand-side affordability/agency problem
  ([[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]]) and to India's open-source DPI export ([[geopol.india]]).
- **Africa delivery vehicle (Carnegie, 2026-06).** Carnegie frames the program's interagency machinery (USTDA, EXIM, DFC) as the coordinated delivery model for a possible **US–Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal** ([[technology-prosperity-deal]]). See [[KEN]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source: [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]].
- **Data Landscapers critique (2026-07).** "You can have any AI stack you like, so long as it's American" — the analysis argues the program (with **EO 14179/14320** and **NIST** standards) pressures poor African states into all-American **full AI stacks**, redefining sovereignty as the right to use what others own. Source: [[2026-02-27 You can have any AI stack you like, so long as it's American]].
- **Tech Corps announced (2026-02, New Delhi).** At the India AI Impact Summit, White House S&T
  adviser Michael Kratsios launched **[[tech-corps]]** — thousands of US technical volunteers to give
  "last-mile support" deploying American AI in partner nations' health, education and agriculture.
  Framed as the human-capital arm of the same full-stack export push (AI Action Plan → export EO →
  this program), explicitly steering countries toward the US stack and away from cheaper Chinese
  open-weight models (which grew to ~30% of global AI usage by late 2025). See [[geopol.china]],
  [[tech.ai]]. Source: [[2026-03-02 The U.S. Launches a Peace Corps for AI to Compete With China]].
