# EBRD–Yas €270m network-expansion loan

**Deal / financing operation (announced 2026-07-13).** A senior loan of **up to €270m**
from **[[ebrd]]** to pan-African operator **[[yas]]** ([[axian-telecom]]) to fund **4G, 5G
and fibre** expansion in **[[SEN]] Senegal** and **[[KEN]] Kenya**.

## Structure — €170m committed + €100m uncommitted

- **Committed facility: up to €170m**, comprising:
  - a **€100m EBRD A-loan**;
  - a **B-loan of up to €50m** syndicated to institutional investors — Amsterdam-based
    impact private-credit fund **[[ilx-fund]]** takes a sizeable position;
  - a **local-currency facility of up to €20m** equivalent in **Kenyan shillings**.
- **Uncommitted facility: up to €100m** available for **future Yas acquisitions and capex**
  across other EBRD countries of operation in the region.
- Headline "€270m" = the committed €170m plus the uncommitted €100m ceiling — not a single
  drawn amount.

## Firsts and framing

- The EBRD's **first investment in Senegal**, and its **first local-currency financing and
  A/B loan structure in sub-Saharan Africa** — blending long-term debt, local-currency support
  and institutional capital mobilisation in one transaction. Senegal and Kenya both became EBRD
  shareholders / countries of operation in **2025**.
- **Use of proceeds:** in Senegal, network modernisation + accelerated fibre; in Kenya, fibre
  expansion building on Yas' **2025 Wananchi acquisition** — specifically modernising the **Zuku**
  broadband brand, which had lost ground to Safaricom — in a highly competitive market.
- Framed by EBRD President **Odile Renaud-Basso** as strengthening digital-infrastructure
  investment and mobilising institutional capital; by Yas CEO **Hassan Jaber** as the group's
  largest-ever financing, aimed at Africa's mobile-coverage gap (~1 in 10 excluded).
