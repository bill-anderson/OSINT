# ADETIC–ANSICE–TECHSO-GROUP National Data Center Audit Agreement

**Parties:** [[adetic]] (Director General **Adoum Djimet Saboun**), [[ansice]] (Deputy General
Manager **Nadjma Saleh Kebzabo**) and [[techso-group]] (co-founder **Hamza Waraki**).
**What:** a tripartite agreement to conduct a **security audit and certification** of the
[[chad-national-data-centre]], plus the operational deployment of **100 micro-data centres**
across government institutions, interconnected with it. **When/where:** signed **Thursday
5 February 2026**, [[TCD]]. **Status:** active.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The last step before inauguration.** ADETIC's Saboun called the agreement *"the final
  milestone before the inauguration of the Data Center."* Phase one — construction of the
  building and installation of operational equipment — was **already complete as of 2026-02-06**;
  the current phase is audit and certification against national and international standards for
  critical-infrastructure protection. See [[infra.store]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-06 ADETIC, ANSICE, and TECHSO-GROUP Join Forces to Protect Chad's Digital Infrastructure]].
- **Rationale: cybercrime and sovereign data housing.** Kebzabo framed the audit as the state's
  answer to *"growing challenges related to cybercrime and the protection of sensitive data";*
  Saboun described the facility as *"a strategic base for sovereign housing of state data and
  continuity of public digital services,"* attributing the project to President **Mahamat Idriss
  Déby Itno**. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[sovereignty]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-06 ADETIC, ANSICE, and TECHSO-GROUP Join Forces to Protect Chad's Digital Infrastructure]],
  [[2026-02-06 Chad Moves to Secure National Data Center Ahead of Launch]].
- **The 100 micro-data centres.** Beyond the National Data Center, the agreement covers operating
  100 micro-data centres across government institutions. Interconnected with the central facility,
  ADETIC says these will form *"a unified, secure and high-performance digital architecture"*
  supporting the dematerialisation of administrative procedures. This is the deal's e-government
  payload, not merely a cybersecurity engagement. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-06 Chad Moves to Secure National Data Center Ahead of Launch]].
- **Strategy framing.** We Are Tech Africa links the audit to the **[[tchad-connexion-2030]]**
  national plan and notes Chad's poor standing on international benchmarks — Tier 4 (the
  second-lowest category) in the ITU's **2024** Global Cybersecurity Index, and 189th of 193 on
  the UN's **2024** E-Government Development Index. See [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-06 Chad Moves to Secure National Data Center Ahead of Launch]].
