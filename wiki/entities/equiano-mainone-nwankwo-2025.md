# Redefining competition and submarine cable ownership: Equiano, MainOne, and the making of middlemen (Nwankwo, 2025)

**Author:** J. Emeka Nwankwo.
**Venue:** *The Information Society* (Taylor & Francis), peer-reviewed.
**Published:** 20 October 2025.

## Argument

Through two contrasting West-African cables — **[[equiano-cable]]** (independently
built and wholly owned by [[google]]) and **MainOne** (a Nigerian-built cable
later acquired by US firm [[equinix]]) — the paper examines how **Big Tech's
growing ownership of submarine cables is redefining competition and the digital
economy**. It tracks Google's evolution from a content provider into a
telecommunications/media/tech company (sector convergence under one owner), and
theorises a **"remaking of middlemen"**: co-location technology lets owners
terminate cables directly inside data centres, obsoleting traditional landing
stations while consolidating incumbents. Read through **coloniality** and
**sovereignty** lenses (disintermediation as echo of the historical elimination
of local middlemen).

## Key facts we draw from it

- **Equiano: 144 Tbps**, exceeding the **combined capacity of all other cables
  serving Africa**; Equiano + 2Africa together dwarf every other African cable by
  ~20×. Older cables rendered "functionally obsolete."
- Google lands Equiano via **partners** (WIOCC/OADC) rather than owning landing
  assets — deliberate **disintermediation** to avoid country-specific regulation;
  the goal is user/data acquisition ("Next Billion Users"), not direct cable profit.
- A **Google-sponsored** study (Africa Practice/Genesis Analytics, 2022) claims
  Equiano cut Nigerian retail internet price ~21%, ~6× faster downloads, ~1.6m
  indirect jobs and **+$10.1bn to Nigerian GDP by 2025** — cited by Nwankwo with
  scepticism about its sponsorship.
- **MainOne**: founded by **Funke Opeke (2008)**; a **7,000 km** Portugal–Nigeria
  cable (RFS 2010), West Africa's first privately owned open-access subsea cable,
  built with $240m debt/equity (AFC, AfDB) and contracted to Tyco/TE SubCom.
  8 Tbps; branches to Ghana, Côte d'Ivoire, later Cameroon/Senegal; 1,200 km
  terrestrial fibre; four data centres.
- **[[equinix]] acquired MainOne for $320 million** (announced 2021, closed 2022)
  — reportedly Nigeria's largest tech deal and Equinix's first African entry.
- Africa is the **world's fastest-growing international-bandwidth market at 44%
  CAGR (2019–2023)** (per TeleGeography, cited in-paper). Funke Opeke argues
  Google/Meta entry expands rather than competes with MainOne's addressable market.

## Why we cite it

The best scholarly treatment of **Equiano and Nigerian/West-African cable
ownership**, the Equinix–MainOne deal, and the political economy of hyperscaler
disintermediation. A specific figure drawn from it becomes a source citing this
publication.
