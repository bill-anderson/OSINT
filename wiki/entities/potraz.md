# Postal and Telecommunications Regulatory Authority of Zimbabwe (POTRAZ)

**Zimbabwe's telecommunications regulator, which also serves as the country's data
protection authority** — enforcing the [[zimbabwe-cyber-and-data-protection-act]]
alongside its licensing of postal and telecoms operators. This dual mandate is the
structurally significant fact about POTRAZ: the body that licenses carriers is the
same body that registers data controllers, receives breach reports and authorises
cross-border personal-data transfers. Zimbabwe therefore has no separate, dedicated
DPA of the kind found in several peer jurisdictions.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Data protection functions (per Michalsons, 2025-06-30).** As enforcer of the
  Cyber and Data Protection Act, POTRAZ: operates the **registration and licensing**
  regime for data controllers (fees scaling with the number of data subjects);
  receives **breach notifications within 24 hours**; must be **notified before**
  any cross-border transfer of personal data out of Zimbabwe; and maintains a
  **public register of automated data processing** in place of requiring
  controllers to keep internal ROPAs. Under the Licensing Regulations in force
  **12 September 2024**, licensed controllers had until **12 December 2024** to
  appoint a DPO. Attribute: this is a law-firm briefing by a compliance-services
  vendor, not the Act text (which the wiki does not hold — GAP-ZWE-001). See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-30 Zimbabwe's Cyber and Data Protection Act - Overview]].
- **Telecoms licensing role (2026-07-06).** Powertel MD Willard Nyagwande describes
  Powertel as "the licensed national carrier **under POTRAZ**" in announcing phase 1
  of the [[powertel-paratus-fibre-corridor]] — POTRAZ appearing here in its
  conventional regulator role, the "regulator-facing relationship" the carrier
  retains. See [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-06 Powertel, Paratus Zimbabwe switch on new digital highway]].

## Reads

- The regulator/DPA fusion is worth watching against the rights critique already held
  on [[ZWE]]: MISA Zimbabwe's reading of [[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]] warns of
  data and verification authority centralised without safeguards, layered on Cyber
  and Data Protection Act s.164C and an executive-run monitoring centre with no
  judicial oversight. A DPA that is also the telecom licensor is a thin firewall.
