# Clément Domingo

**The security researcher whose examination of the leaked samples established that Senegal's January 2026 national-ID breach involved genuine exfiltrated records** — the finding on which this wiki's whole reading of the [[daf-senegal|DAF]] incident rests.

*Paged as a person because the person is the story: the evidentiary status of the breach turns on an identified researcher's independent verification, not on an institution's statement.*

## Activity in this wiki

- **Exfiltration confirmed (Feb 2026).** Domingo examined the samples published by the **[[green-blood-group]]** crew and confirmed **genuine birth records and national ID cards** among them. This is why the wiki records the breach as an **unrebutted exfiltration** rather than an allegation: the DAF's communiqués (**5 Feb 2026**, **7 Apr 2026**) assert only that data **integrity** remains intact and are **silent on confidentiality**, never denying a leak. Source: [[2026-02-12-darkreading-senegal-breach-security-maturity]].
- **No CERT, no agency (stated 2026-02-06).** Domingo stated that Senegal at the time of the breaches had **neither a national cybersecurity agency nor a national CERT** — a dated statement of institutional absence that anchors the [[senegal--infra-cybersec]] page. Source: same.
- **Corroborating coverage.** **ZATAZ** independently reported the crew's claimed volume as **139 Go** (11 Feb 2026), reaching the same reading as the researcher-adjacent account and making the competing "139 TB" figure look like the outlier. *No volume has been independently verified by anyone, including Domingo — both outlets report the attacker's own number.* Source: [[2026-02-11-zataz-senegal-frappe-fichier-identite]].

## Reads

- The case shows independent researchers doing the verification work a national CERT would otherwise do — and the wiki relying on that, because in **2026-02** Senegal had no CERT to rely on instead. That dependency is itself the finding.
