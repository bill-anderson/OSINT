# Eskom

**South Africa's state-owned electricity utility — and the reason the country's data-centre boom is
a governance story rather than a business story.** Every megawatt of hyperscale capacity announced
by **[[teraco]]**, **[[cavaleros]]**, **[[vantage]]** and others is a claim on a grid with a
documented history of load shedding. Eskom is where the data-centre thesis on
[[south-africa--infra-store]] meets its constraint.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The aggregate thesis (2026-02-09).** South African data-centre capacity is set to rise from
  **about 400 MW to at least 1,200 MW** — an increment of roughly 1,000 MW, which is **just over one
  stage of Eskom load shedding**. That single comparison is the reason the build-out is a public
  question: the sector is adding a load-shedding stage's worth of demand to a constrained grid.
  Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Major SA data centre expansion plans emerge, set to more than double current power demand]].
- **Constrained supply by 2029 — Chris Yelland (2026-02-09).** **Chris Yelland**, managing director
  of **EE Business Intelligence**, told Daily Maverick that **on Eskom's own latest risk analysis
  report**, rising demand from new large data centres — combined with a possible rollout of **EV
  charging stations** and **government subsidies for mothballed metal smelters** — would probably
  lead to **constrained electricity supplies by 2029** unless substantial generation capacity is
  added rapidly. Source: same.
- **The "looming base supply cliff" (2026-02-09).** Yelland reports Eskom has also cautioned about a
  "**looming base supply cliff**" arising from the **retirement of certain coal-fired power
  stations** and the **expiry of power imports from the Cahora Bassa Scheme**. Two structural
  subtractions from supply, arriving as demand is scheduled to triple. Source: same.
- **Wheeling as the operators' answer (2024-11-13).** Part of Teraco's **R8-billion syndicated
  loan** (via Absa) augments its renewable-generation programme, including a **120 MW utility-scale
  solar PV plant that will feed into the Eskom network and wheel power across multiple municipal
  grids** — self-generation routed through the utility rather than around it. Teraco CFO Samuel
  Erwin frames it as "a unique opportunity in South Africa to integrate renewable energy generation
  into the provision of data centre infrastructure." Compare **[[distributed-power-africa]]**'s
  wheeling PPA with [[africa-data-centres]]. Source:
  [[2024-11-13 Teraco to build 40 MW JB7 data centre at Isando Campus]].
- **Disclosure asymmetry (2026-02-09).** Teraco discloses capacities and energy plans; Cavaleros
  says its electricity supply and **power procurement structures are "commercially sensitive and
  therefore cannot be publicly disclosed"**, under strict NDAs. On a constrained public grid, who
  is contracting for how much is not a private matter — which is what makes the contrast
  analytically live. Source: same.
