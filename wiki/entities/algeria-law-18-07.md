# Algeria — Law No. 18-07 on the protection of personal data

Algeria's governing personal-data statute: **enacted 10 June 2018** and **in force only from 10
August 2023** — a five-year gap between enactment and commencement — **amended and completed by Law
No. 25-11**. It is enforced by the **[[anpdcp|ANPDP/ANPDCP]]**.

## Provisions held

- **Mandatory data localisation.** Personal data must be stored **within Algeria**. Source:
  [[2024-09-19 Algeria Digital Economy (US Country Commercial Guide)]].
- **Purpose-limited retention.** Data may be kept only as long as the purpose of collection
  requires — the authority's worked example being a **maximum of three years for non-customer
  prospect data** — with longer retention allowed for legal archiving, documentation, or defence of
  interests in litigation. Source:
  [[2025-12-01-anpdp-data-conservation-law-compliance-elmoudjahid]].
- **Article 25 authorisations.** The ANPDP issues the authorisations the law requires. Same source.
- **Compliance machinery (under the 25-11 amendment).** DPO designation, a processing register, and
  an **automated audit log** tracing every processing operation — collection, retention,
  modification, extraction, consultation, communication, encryption, erasure — with reason, date,
  time and the identity of whoever accessed the data. The authority describes the log as an "audit
  document" for personal data. Same source.

## How it is being extended

- **Images are personal data (délibération adopted Wednesday 15 July 2026).** The ANPDP ruled that
  **any photograph or video permitting direct or indirect identification** falls under Law 18-07, so
  its collection, storage, retention and publication require a lawful basis — and posting to a
  foreign-hosted platform may constitute an international transfer. Source:
  [[2026-07-20-algerie-anpdp-regles-publication-images-plateformes]].
- **Read through a security frame (2026-07).** A DGSN study day on the law, attended by the Justice
  and Interior ministers and ANPDP president **Samir Bourehil**, was held under police chief Ali
  Badaoui's framing that *"national security is now cybersecurity first"*; a unified guidance manual
  on data-protection procedures was announced. Source:
  [[2026-07-01-dgsn-digital-space-data-protection-elmoudjahid]].

The wider enforcement architecture around the law — the National Council for Information Systems
Security under the Presidency, the High Commission for Digitalization, and the ICT-crimes authority
— is set out in
[[2026-01-26-cybersecurity-framework-national-infrastructure--techafricanews]].
