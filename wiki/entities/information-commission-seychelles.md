# Information Commission (Seychelles)

**Seychelles' data-protection authority** — the competent authority designated under **section 5**
of the [[data-protection-act-2023-seychelles]] to enforce and implement the Act. It is not a
purpose-built privacy regulator: the Commission was **created under the Access to Information
Act** and then designated as the DPA, a *designate-an-existing-authority* model TechHive Advisory
compares to **South Africa's**. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[SYC]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Designated as the data-protection authority (s.5).** The Act designates the Commission as
  the regulatory body responsible for enforcement — the single largest gap the 2023 Act closes
  against the repealed 2003 Act, which created no authority at all. Analyst comment (TechHive
  Advisory, 2024-06) notes the Commission pre-existed under the Access to Information Act and
  was designated rather than newly constituted, as in South Africa. See [[gov.protect]].
  Sources: [[2024-06-04 Review of Seychelles DPA]],
  [[2024-03-25 Data Protection Law Now In Force In Seychelles]].
- **Powers.** To establish additional rules and procedures; conduct **investigations and
  audits**; raise public awareness; **impose fines**; **cooperate with foreign supervisory
  authorities**; request a preliminary injunction from the court; and issue enforcement notices.
  Sources: [[2024-06-04 Review of Seychelles DPA]],
  [[2024-03-25 Data Protection Law Now In Force In Seychelles]].
- **Breach notification recipient (ss.43-44).** Controllers must notify the Commission within
  **72 hours** of discovering a breach, with a possible extension on explanation of the delay;
  the Act does not specify the extension time frame. See [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2024-06-04 Review of Seychelles DPA]].
- **Enforcement record: none held.** No source in this wiki records an investigation, audit,
  fine, enforcement notice or published guidance by the Commission — nor its resourcing or
  leadership. Given that the Act's 18-month transitional period ran from 22 December 2023 and
  therefore **elapsed around 22 June 2025**, the absence of any post-transition enforcement
  signal is a coverage gap, not evidence of inactivity.
