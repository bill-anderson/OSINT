# Eswatini Economic Update 2025 - Harnessing the Potential of Digital Technologies

**The [[world-bank]]'s second Eswatini Economic Update (EEU)**, launched in Mbabane on
**2025-07-31**, subtitled *Harnessing the Potential of Digital Technologies for
Eswatini's Growth and Job Creation* — a 58-page report making digitalisation the
organising strategy for the country's growth, fiscal resilience and job creation. See
[[SWZ]], [[gov.policy]].

> **Reference study — cite, don't absorb.** Its dated figures belong to the report. Do
> not promote them onto concept or place pages as current state; cite the EEU and leave
> its numbers in it.

## What it argues

**Three core macro policy pillars:** (i) enhance resilience through effective
macroeconomic management; (ii) stimulate job creation through private-sector development
by improving the enabling environment; (iii) provide better and more affordable services
through efficient public spending.

**Five digital priorities:**

1. **Digital governance** — clarify mandates for [[ministry-of-ict-eswatini]] and
   regulators ([[esccom]]) to eliminate overlaps; fully operationalise the
   **Cybersecurity Agency** and **Data Protection Office** through a national
   change-management programme. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]].
2. **Telecom reform** — restructure [[eptc]], adopt **open-access** policies to reduce
   data costs, expand rural broadband via PPPs, enable open access to backbone
   infrastructure. See [[infra.connect]].
3. **Digital public infrastructure** — launch a **biometric digital ID** for e-services
   (eKYC, G2P payments), built on the national population register
   ([[ministry-of-home-affairs-eswatini]]) with a resilient security architecture and
   biometric authentication; establish **interoperability standards** integrating
   health, education and social protection. Also: fully digitise tax administration,
   set up an online business registry, roll out digital procurement, and fully utilise
   the [[eswatini-payment-switch]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]].
4. **Skills** — a **National Digital Skills Action Plan** aligned to labour-market needs,
   plus expanded STEM. See [[capacity.training]].
5. **Digital entrepreneurship** — stronger IP protections, startup access to government
   contracts (e-GP), and a **blended finance fund** for high-growth tech firms. See
   [[tech.innovate]].
