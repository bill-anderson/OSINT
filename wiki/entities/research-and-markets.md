# Research and Markets

**A commercial market-research database vendor, promoted via press release, whose "South Africa
Existing & Upcoming Data Center Portfolio" database is a paywalled asset the wiki can point to but
cannot read.** Held as a resource so that its existence and coverage are known — **not** as a source
of current state.

**Cite, don't absorb — with an extra caution.** These are **vendor-asserted figures in a marketing
announcement for a paywalled product**. They must not be promoted into concept or place pages as
current state. The wiki has fresher, better-provenanced country-level data arriving via clippings.

## Activity in this wiki

- **What the database covers (announced 2025-03-18).** Retail colocation pricing
  (quarter/half/full rack), wholesale colocation (per kW) pricing, and per-facility data points:
  location, operator/owner name, core & shell area and power capacity, rack capacity, year of
  operations, design standards (Tier I–IV), and power/cooling redundancy. **10 upcoming facilities**
  are covered with investment snapshots — investor name, area, power capacity, investment ($m) split
  across electrical/mechanical/general construction, announcement year and project status. Source:
  [[2025-03-18 South Africa Data Center Portfolio 2025 - Major Investors]].
- **Vendor-asserted SA figures (early 2025) — a dated prior only.** **~53 existing data centres** and
  **~300 MW** existing capacity; **~200 MW upcoming**; **~$1.5bn** in new investment expected by
  2026; **almost 170 MW** to be added by end-2025; **Johannesburg ~75%** of upcoming power capacity.
  **This is not a contradiction of held material** — the wiki holds **55 facilities (Feb 2026)** and
  **56 (May 2026)**, which with ~53 (early 2025) forms a **consistent growth series**. Source: same.
- **Operators it names.** [[africa-data-centres]], Business Connexion, CipherWave, Digital Parks
  Africa, [[equinix]], Internet Solutions ZA, [[mtn-group]], NTT & Dimension Data, Open Access Data
  Centres (OADC), Paratus, **[[teraco]]** ([[digital-realty]]), **[[vantage]]**, and Vodacom
  Business — useful chiefly as a **coverage list** of who was considered a material SA operator in
  early 2025. Source: same.

## Why it is held as a resource

The clip reports no development: it announces a database. Its standing value is knowing **what
exists to be consulted** and on what terms (paywalled, vendor-compiled) — the classic
`resource` case. Any dated figure later drawn *from* it becomes an ordinary source citing this page.
