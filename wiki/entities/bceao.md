# BCEAO (Banque Centrale des États de l'Afrique de l'Ouest)

**The Central Bank of West African States — the common central bank of the eight
[[waemu]]/UEMOA member states, headquartered in Dakar.** Governor (as of 2025): **Jean-Claude
Kassi-Brou**. In this wiki the BCEAO appears chiefly as the operator of the regional
**instant-payment platform** ([[waemu-instant-payment-platform]]) and, through it, as a
builder of shared regional payments infrastructure rather than a national regulator. See
[[XWA]], [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Instant Payment Platform launch (2025-09-30).** The BCEAO launched its interoperable
  instant-payment platform in **Dakar**, presided over by Governor Kassi-Brou and broadcast to
  Lomé by videoconference. It enables **24/7 secure, irrevocable transfers** across UEMOA
  between banks, microfinance institutions, e-money issuers and payment service providers,
  targeting settlement in **under ten seconds**. Implementation was financially backed by the
  **[[gates-foundation]]** and the **[[african-development-bank]]**. In Togo, **three banks were
  operational as of 2025-10-01** (Coris Bank, [[ecobank]], [[orabank]]); by **2025-11-18**,
  **seven institutions had committed** or were preparing interconnection — a different state,
  not a straightforward increase (see the platform page). See [[TGO]], [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2025-10-01 BCEAO Launches Instant Payments Platform in Togo with Three Banks]].
- **Developer sandbox for API-Business (2025-11-18).** The BCEAO opened a **public developer
  sandbox**, letting Togolese firms and solution providers simulate operations, verify technical
  compliance and prepare integration ahead of going live — with documentation, integration guides
  and startup assistance. A central bank acting as a platform steward courting fintech
  developers. See [[tech.innovate]], [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-18 BCEAO Launches Developer Sandbox for Instant Payments]].
- **Reference point for bank-led products.** Ecobank's separate Thunes-powered cross-border
  service is positioned against the BCEAO system, the bank being "among the three banks in Togo
  testing the BCEAO's instant payment system, known as PI." See
  [[ecobank-thunes-partnership]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-31 Ecobank launches instant international payment service in Togo]].
