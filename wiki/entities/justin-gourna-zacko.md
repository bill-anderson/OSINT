# Justin Gourna-Zacko

**Minister of the Digital Economy, Posts and Telecommunications of the Central African Republic — the source of the connectivity figures that circulate as CAR's internet-penetration rate, and the signatory voice on its first towerco licence.** [[CAF]] carries him as over the entity page bar on source count; he is paged here because his own statements are the origin of a figure the wiki had to correct.

**Succession caveat (as of 2026-07-20):** *Agence Ecofin names **Roger Andjalandji** in the post as of **2026-07-16**. All Gourna-Zacko material held here dates to **2026-02 and earlier**. The wiki holds no primary confirming or dating a handover — treat his tenure as unconfirmed after early 2026, which is why this page is `needs-review`.*

## Activity in this wiki

- **The ~30% figure, and what it actually measures (2025-07-30).** Government statements of **~30% (Jul 2025)** are a **network-coverage** figure, **not usage**. In the primary at the root of that number, Gourna-Zacko describes the rate rising to **50%** with fibre interconnections taken to **Congo (Ouesso)** and **Cameroon (Kenzo/Kentzou)**, and to **"70% 80%"** once the build reaches the **Centre-Est** — naming it a *"taux de connectivité"* against a target of **70% couverture nationale**. **No survey, no sample, no population denominator.** *A usage rate cannot be moved by laying transit fibre.* Source: [[2025-07-30-car-taux-penetration-internet-30-pourcent]].
- **The first passive-infrastructure licence (2025-07-18).** ARCEP signed the *cahier des charges* granting **SOS Télécoms** a licence for the operation of **passive telecom infrastructure** — pylons, antenna sites, technical sites and other non-active equipment, to be **built and leased** to the mobile operators. Gourna-Zacko called it **"a first in our history"**. ARCEP DG **Bonaventure Benjamin Panzet Sébas** and SOS Télécoms PDG **Djargué Kertoumar** signed; the company had applied on **13 February 2024**. Explicitly **not** a competitor to [[orange-centrafrique]], Telecel or Moov but a wholesale partner, aimed at rural and low-margin coverage — a **towerco** model where the binding constraint is capex, not demand. Source: [[2025-07-21-sos-telecoms-licence-infrastructures-passives-oubanguimedias]].

## Reads

- The minister's own framing is the cleanest available example of the **coverage-for-usage substitution**: a government reports what it has built, the number is re-reported as what citizens do, and the gap between the two is where the digital-divide story actually lives.
