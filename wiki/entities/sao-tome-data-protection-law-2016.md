# São Tomé and Príncipe Personal Data Guarantee and Protection Law (Law 03/2016)

STP's primary personal-data protection statute — the **Personal Data Guarantee and
Protection Law (Law 03/2016)** — modelled closely on the **EU Data Protection Directive
(Directive 95/46/EC)**. It safeguards privacy and individual liberties in personal-data
processing and is enforced by the **[[national-data-protection-agency-sao-tome]]**
(ANPDP).

## What it mandates

- **Wide scope:** protects all types of personal data of individuals; applies to
  paper-based or digital processing where a controller is headquartered or established
  in STP, where STP law applies under international public law, or where equipment in
  STP is used by a foreign controller (transit-only equipment excepted).
- **Core principles:** lawfulness, transparency, proportionality, minimisation and
  veracity.
- **Enforcement / sanctions:** fines and criminal sanctions for breaches, including
  unauthorised data communication or transfer. The ANPDP can impose fines and ancillary
  measures (cessation, blocking, deletion, destruction). Notification failures or
  fraudulent information carry fines of **STN 50m–120m** (individuals) and **STN
  250m–500m** (legal entities); most intentional violations carry fines upwards of 120
  days' salary and/or one year imprisonment.
- **Constitutional backing:** the STP constitution enshrines protection of private life
  and personal identity.

Complemented by the [[sao-tome-interoperability-framework-2024]] (public-sector data
sharing) and STP's signatory status to the [[malabo-convention]].
