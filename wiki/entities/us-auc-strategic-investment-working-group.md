# US–AUC Strategic Investment Working Group (SIWG)

A US–African Union Commission working group launched **28 January 2026** (Addis
Ababa) to channel US private investment into AU-backed infrastructure and drive
continent-wide digital transformation — the continental complement to bilateral
tracks like a possible Kenya TPD.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launch (2026-01).** Announced after Deputy Secretary Landau met AUC Chair
  Youssouf; anchored to **Agenda 2063**, **PIDA** corridors and **[[afcfta]]**.
  Scope spans critical-minerals supply chains, transport/energy corridors,
  regulatory harmonisation, and **secure digital infrastructure** — with an
  explicit "durable, profitable investments in place of foreign assistance"
  framing. See [[XAF]], [[african-union]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Joint Statement of the United States and African Union on the Launch of a Strategic Investment Working Group]].
- Cited by Carnegie as a continental-level signal of shifting US posture toward
  Africa: [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]].

## Reads

- `colonialism` / `sovereignty`: "investment not aid" paired with critical-minerals
  access invites both the empowerment and the extraction reading — the same tension
  as the [[technology-prosperity-deal]] framework.
