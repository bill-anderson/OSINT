# Eswatini Communications Commission (ESCCOM)

**Eswatini's converged ICT-sector regulator and, unusually, also its data protection
authority.** Established in 2013 under the **Swaziland Communications Commission Act
No. 10 of 2013**, ESCCOM oversees the communications sector and administers the
country's 2022 digital statute bundle — the Electronic Communications Act 2022, the
Electronic Communications Transactions Act 2022, the
[[computer-crime-and-cyber-crime-act-2022-eswatini]] and the
[[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]]. See [[SWZ]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Designated National Data Protection Agency (s.5).** The
  [[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]] names ESCCOM as the Data Protection Agency,
  mandating it to regulate the handling of personal information, investigate breaches
  and resolve complaints, issuing any sanction available under the Act including
  administrative fines. Controllers must notify ESCCOM (and the data subject) of
  security breaches under s.17(1). See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 Role of ESCCOM as National Data Protection Agency]].
- **Sanction ceiling.** Fines up to **E5,000,000 or 2% of annual turnover**;
  [[apc]] reports s.6(3)(b) as the administrative-fine power, putting five million
  Emalangeni at **~USD 268,000 (2024-03-11)** — a conversion that must never be
  repeated bare. See [[gov.legislate]]. Sources:
  [[2024-01-01 Data Protection Act 5 of 2022 - Eswatini]],
  [[2024-03-11 Eswatini strives for digital sovereignty amid technological advancements]].
- **Regulator-as-DPA is contested.** APC's Ndimphiwe Shabangu notes the same body
  regulates the sector and polices data protection, and that all four 2022 Acts were
  tabled under certificates of urgency, compressing stakeholder input. During the
  June–July 2021 unrest, government directed [[eptc]], [[mtn-eswatini]] and Eswatini
  Mobile to suspend internet services. See [[sovereignty]], [[infra.cybersec]].
  Source:
  [[2024-07-22 Risk of human rights infringement as Eswatini rushes to regulate the ICT sector]].
- **Mandate overlap flagged (2025-07-31).** The [[eswatini-economic-update-2025]]
  recommends clarifying mandates between [[ministry-of-ict-eswatini]] and regulators
  to eliminate overlaps, and fully operationalising the Cybersecurity Agency and Data
  Protection Office. See [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-31 Eswatini Economic Update - Digital Transformation Key to Unlocking Growth]].
