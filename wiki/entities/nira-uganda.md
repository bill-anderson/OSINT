# National Identification and Registration Authority (NIRA)

**Uganda's foundational-identity and civil-registration authority** — operator of the
national identification register and issuer of the National ID ([[ndaga-muntu]]) and the
National Identification Number (NIN). It first introduced the national ID card roughly a
decade before 2024 (Source: [[2024-06-05 Ugandan human rights lawyer's arrest exposes use of national ID for surveillance]]),
and took over the [[mvrs-uganda]] mobile vital-records system from [[ursb]] in a 2015
institutional change. Registrar (2026-02): **Claire Ollama** ([[claire-ollama]]);
Executive Director: **Rosemary Kisembo** ([[rosemary-kisembo]]).

## The spine

Read across the material this wiki holds, NIRA's register has become **load-bearing for
finance, tax, education and social protection** — while **NIRA's own figures show it
reached barely over a third of the people it set out to reach.** As of 2026-02-11, 90.5%
of renewals were done but only **37.3% of first-time enrolments** (6.4m of a 17.2m
target). The gate closed faster than the register filled. See [[ndaga-muntu]] for the
full chain.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Full transition to the new system (2026-02-11).** Registrar Claire Ollama told a
  press conference on **9 February 2026** that Uganda had **fully transitioned** to its
  new national digital ID system, migrating **more than 28 million identity records**
  from the legacy system. Total enrolments reached **more than 35 million**: *"the new
  national register has over 35 million Ugandans who can be uniquely identified via
  biometrics."* Service provider is UAE-based [[tahaluf]]; **five modules are
  [[mosip]]-based** (registration, renewals, update/correction, issuance, lost-ID
  replacement), with [[tech5]], Technoforte, Entrust and Hyf also named by ED Kisembo at
  MOSIP Connect 2026. State Minister for Internal Affairs **Gen. David Muhoozi** said
  measures were being taken on **system-integration and QR-code scanning failures** on
  some new cards, and that the **ID authentication module** would be fully operational
  **by end of March**. See [[dpi.id]], [[geopol.gulf]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-11 Uganda migrates more than 28M records to new national ID system]].
- **The enrolment split (as of 2026-02-11).** Renewals **14.3m of a 15.8m target
  (90.5%)**; first-time enrolments **6.4m of a 17.2m target (37.3%)**. Of **10.1m cards
  printed, only 2.5m picked up** — over 60% uncollected. Phase one ended at parish level;
  the process continues as routine activity at NIRA offices. Source: as above.
- **MOSIP's first completed brownfield implementation (2026-03-02).** [[iritech]] states
  NIRA migrated **28 million biometric records** to MOSIP in the open-source platform's
  **first completed brownfield implementation** — a characterisation held nowhere else,
  and an independent corroboration of the 28m figure. **More than 1 million Ugandans had
  difficulty registering fingerprint biometrics** (NIRA figures cited by IriTech), the
  stated reason for adding iris capture. Vendor-sourced — attribute. Source:
  [[2026-03-02 IriTech provides iris biometrics for Uganda's upgraded registration kits]].
- **Registration chronology — a slipping schedule.** Originally slated **1 June 2024**,
  rescheduled to **July 2024** (Source:
  [[2024-06-05 Ugandan human rights lawyer's arrest exposes use of national ID for surveillance]]),
  then to **October 2024** with 5,680 kits, 13,000 personnel across 10,717 parishes, and
  alignment to the [[electoral-commission-uganda]]'s **30 November** voters'-register
  deadline ahead of the **2026 general elections** (Source:
  [[2024-08-19 Uganda Prepares Large-scale Biometric Registration for National ID]]) — and
  **actually launched 27 May 2025** (Source:
  [[2025-05-28 Huge turnout as Nira starts mass registration]]). Each account is accurate
  as of its date; the ~12-month slip against a hard electoral deadline is the signal.
- **Launch-day ground truth (2025-05-28).** Six-to-ten-month exercise; district-level
  concerns over distinguishing citizens from refugees in Adjumani (~244,374 refugees per
  2024 [[unhcr]] statistics); first-time applicants required an LC1 letter endorsed by
  the District Internal Security Officer. Tororo's RDC claimed *a survey shows more
  Kenyans than Ugandans hold Ugandan national IDs* — attributed to an unnamed survey, an
  assertion rather than a fact. Older persons' misrepresented biodata blocked
  [[sage-uganda]] access. See [[digital.rural]], [[include.divides]]. Source: as above.
- **The financial gate (2024-10-14).** Per the **Bank of Uganda 2023 annual report**, up
  to **74 financial institutions** had access to the NIRA database for digital KYC
  (**74 listed as of June 2024**). See [[bank-of-uganda]],
  [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2024-10-14 Uganda financial institutions sign on for digital KYC through national ID]].
- **NIN becomes tax identity (2025-11-11).** Under the **Tax Procedures Amendment Bill**,
  NIRA-issued NINs become Ugandans' official tax identity, replacing the TIN. See
  [[ura-uganda]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-11 URA Launches Tax Filing Season, Announces Shift from TIN to NIN]].
- **Surveillance allegations and NIRA's rebuttal (2024-06-05).** Reporting on a
  **Bloomberg / Lighthouse Reports investigation** (the primary, not held — GAP-UGA-002)
  which unveils *"how the Museveni regime has built the system to consolidate power, as
  underlying software and datasets are easily accessed by individuals at all levels of law
  enforcement."* **ED Rosemary Kisembo refuted on the record** that law enforcement could
  readily access the database. See [[nick-opiyo]], [[acme-uganda]], [[gov.protect]],
  [[infra.cybersec]]. Source: as above.
- **The ISER litigation (dismissed 2025-06-10).** [[iser-uganda]]'s challenge to
  mandatory NIN/National ID for services was dismissed by the High Court. See
  [[iser-uganda]], [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-13 Uganda's Digital ID Barrier Worsens as Court Delivers Blow to Access]].
- **Interoperability posture (undated deck, `date_source: proxy`).** NIRA is among
  [[ughub]]'s largest data providers — its services used by **90 of 135 entities** on the
  platform. Governance runs through a Cabinet Technical Sub-Committee Working Group headed
  by the Ministry of ICT, with the [[nita-uganda]] Executive Director on the NIRA Board.
  *The deck is undated — do not treat these figures as current-dated.* Source:
  [[2026-07-17 A Collaborative Approach to Digital Identity and Digital Transformation (NIRA and NITA-U)]].
- **CRVS baseline (reference studies — cite, don't absorb).** An IDRC snapshot built from
  a questionnaire NIRA completed in **January 2019**
  ([[2019-01-01 Snapshot of CRVS Systems of Uganda (IDRC Centre of Excellence)]]); and the
  COVID-19 brief recording that **Jan–Aug 2020 NIRA recorded ~83,200 birth events
  electronically, 21% of the same period in 2019** ([[mvrs-uganda]]). Source:
  [[2020-01-01 Maintaining civil registration amid COVID-19 - Uganda's mobile notification system (IDRC)]].

## Contradiction — open, not resolved

DRAA (2025-09-13) estimates **18 million (about 40%) of the population** lack a National
ID; NIRA (2026-02-11) frames a **35 million** register. The DRAA figure **carries no
cited source**. **Resolved (2026-07-20) — the divergence is a denominator, not a dispute.**
The 18.1m is **NIRA's own figure**, not the advocacy author's: ED Rosemary Kisembo told
COSASE on **2024-07-01** that against a census population of 45.9m, "we haven't registered
about 18.1 million individuals… **most of those unregistered are children under 17**." That
is 39.4% of the **total** population including infants. NIRA's press statement of
**2024-10-15** gives the split: of 27.4m on the register, 6.3m are aged 0–17, leaving ~21.1m
adults against a census adult population of **23,154,716 (18+, 2024-05-09)** — an adult gap of
**~2.0m, ~9% of adults**, not 40%. The 35m register figure (2026-02-08) is cumulative
enrolments ever issued, with no deregistration for death or emigration (the Auditor General
found the NSIS cannot register deaths, FY2024/25). The binding constraint is **card
possession**: of 10,152,559 printed in the 2025/26 exercise, only **2,581,457 collected
(35.5%, 2026-02-08)**.
