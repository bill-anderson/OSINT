# Seychelles Electronic Funds Transfer (SEFT)

**Seychelles' electronic funds transfer system** — the digital payment rail cited by the
[[central-bank-of-seychelles]] as the volume evidence for withdrawing cheques from the financial
system. In this wiki SEFT appears only through that argument: it is the destination rail of the
2025-26 cheque phase-out, not yet a separately documented system. See [[dpi.pay]], [[SYC]].

> **Provenance caveat.** The only figure held for SEFT comes from a **single source** whose
> canonical Seychelles News Agency URL returned HTTP 403; the body was reconstructed from a
> same-day syndicated republication and the source page is marked `date_source: proxy`.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Volume (2023).** **Liz Julienne**, CBS Director of Financial Inclusion, said card
  transactions **and** electronic funds transfers via SEFT together exceeded **10 million in
  2023**, against around **600,000 cheques** in the same period. **The figure is a combined
  card-plus-SEFT total — no SEFT-only volume is held here.** See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2024-09-22 Seychelles to phase out cheques for financial transactions from 2025]].
- **The rail cheques are being pushed onto.** CBS's phased cheque withdrawal (banks stop issuing
  to individuals from **2025-01-01**; stop accepting from individuals from **2025-05-01**; stop
  issuing to non-residents from **2026-01-01**; stop accepting from non-residents after
  **2026-05-01**) makes SEFT and cards the residual retail options. Consultations flagged
  **financial exclusion** of cheque-reliant groups and the **cost of adopting digital
  solutions** — the inclusion counterweight to SEFT's headline volumes. See
  [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2024-09-22 Seychelles to phase out cheques for financial transactions from 2025]].
- **What is not held.** No source here records SEFT's operator, launch date, architecture,
  participating institutions, settlement model, fees or whether it interoperates beyond
  Seychelles.
