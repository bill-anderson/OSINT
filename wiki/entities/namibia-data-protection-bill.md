# Namibia Data Protection Bill

Namibia's long-gestating **personal-data-protection statute** — **~12 years** in the making — which ICT Minister
**[[emma-theofelus]]** said would be tabled in **September 2025** and called "a cornerstone of Namibia's digital
governance strategy" (personal-data protection, privacy, secure digital transactions, alignment with
international best practice). As of **July 2026** it was still being **finalised** alongside the **Cybercrime
Bill**; in the interim, law enforcement relies on the Electronic Transactions Act, the Communications Act and the
Penal Code.

**The data-subject-rights concern.** The **IPPR**'s Frederico Links warned the bill may fall short: a section on
**data-subject rights was removed in 2020** and, as far as he knew, not substantially reintroduced, and the
public had not seen the latest draft — "our concerns remain the same as in 2023 – that it does not adequately
address the rights of data subjects." The draft was split from the financial-intelligence bill in 2019. This is
the **weak-data-protection-regime gap** flagged against the biometric **[[namibia-digital-id|e-ID]]** rollout
(see [[namibia--dpi-id]]).
