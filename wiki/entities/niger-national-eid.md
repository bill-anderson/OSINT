# Niger national e-ID / AES biometric ID & e-Passport

Niger's programme to modernise foundational identity with a **biometric national ID card and electronic
passport**, aligned to the **[[alliance-of-sahel-states|Confederation of Sahel States (AES)]]** regional
credential. The Council of Ministers approved the enabling **decrees on 26 December 2025** (following the AES
biometric-ID framework), with President Tiani the first enrollee at the March-2026 launch. Production is being
digitised under **two MoUs signed 27 June 2025 with the Libyan firm [[al-itissal-al-jadeed|Al Itissal Al
Jadeed]]**, framed as a digital-sovereignty and technological-independence move.

*(Note: a public "National Electronic Identity" portal snapshot exists but is of uncertain provenance — its
figures are not relied upon here.)*

**Mass enrolment (from 25 Apr 2026).** The card is **mandatory for citizens 15+, valid 5 years, 4,500 FCFA**,
capturing **fingerprint, face, voice and iris**; enrolment runs from the **20 commissariats of Niamey** then the
interior (PM Zeine enrolled 9 Apr). **[[nita-niger|NITA]]** kiosks let citizens pay the fee on-site.

**AES e-passport phase (from 16 Jul 2026).** Tiani became the **first enrollee for the AES biometric passport** —
a second phase (launched Aug 2025) delivering **five categories** (ordinary, service, diplomatic, refugee and an
innovative **Hadj** passport), produced by the **Police DST** with **[[al-itissal-al-jadeed|Al Itissal Al
Jadeed]]** and local partner **HASDI**. Full two-track picture: **[[niger--dpi-id]]**.
