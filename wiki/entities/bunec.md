# BUNEC (National Civil Status Registration Office)

Cameroon's **National Civil Status Registration Office** — the authority driving the digital transformation of civil registration and vital statistics (CRVS).

## Activity in this wiki

- **CRVS digitisation to 2029.** Under the new civil-registration law (**[[cameroon-civil-registration-law-2024]]**, Law 2024/016 — digital birth/marriage/death certificates + a **Unique Personal Identification Number** for every citizen), BUNEC partnered Luxembourg's **[[liber-technologies]]** (Oct 2025) toward a **fully digital CRVS system by 2029**, integrated with the biometric national ID and voter kits. See [[cameroon--dpi-id]]. Source: [[2025-10-27 Cameroon's BUNEC partners with Liber Technologies for CRVS digitization]].
- **Law enacted 23 Dec 2024, 130 sections.** The civil-registration law ([[cameroon-civil-registration-law-2024]]) was enacted by President Biya on **23 Dec 2024** — 130 sections that refocus and broaden BUNEC's mandate and, notably, let **internally displaced persons re-establish lost birth certificates in their place of residence** (via the local State Counsel) rather than travelling back to the issuing court — a material change for the ~million-plus displaced by the Anglophone conflict. Frames the push toward SDG target 16.9 (legal identity for all by 2030). Source: [[2025-01-27-new-civil-registration-law-sdg-16-9-biometricupdate]].
- **First digital birth certificate (Jan 2026); councils begin issuance.** The first civil certificate via a fully digitalised system in the North-West/South-West was issued at Limbe I (7 Jan 2026), under a UNDP/Denmark project across 10 municipalities; councils then began routine issuance of **digital birth certificates carrying the same legal worth as physical ones** under the 2024 law. Sources: [[2026-01-10 Cameroon - First digital birth certificate in the North-West and South-West regions (UNDP)]], [[2026-01-12-councils-begin-issuance-digital-birth-certificates-biometricupdate]].
