# SudaPass

Sudan's planned **unified national digital identity platform**, announced by the
Ministry of Telecommunications & Digital Transformation as part of the wider
**Baladna** e-government programme, and positioned as a foundation for
post-conflict recovery and state coherence.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Preparing for launch (as of 2026-01).** SudaPass is being readied to unify
  fragmented registries — ministries currently use different identifiers and
  humanitarian agencies run parallel databases — into a single identity layer to
  support service delivery, social protection, financial inclusion and fiscal
  recovery. See [[SDN]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]],
  [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-29 How to build trust alongside Sudan's digital identity system]].
- **Crisis context.** Sudan has **>30m people needing humanitarian aid** and
  **>14m displaced** out of a population of ~51m, with **no updated population
  data since the 2008 census** — conditions that both raise the value of a
  reliable ID layer and complicate inclusive enrolment. Source:
  [[2026-01-29 How to build trust alongside Sudan's digital identity system]].
- **Recommended safeguards.** The World Economic Forum commentary framing
  SudaPass urges anchoring it in the **[[african-union]] Malabo Convention** (AU
  Convention on Cyber Security and Personal Data Protection) and the **[[world-bank]]
  Principles on Identification for Sustainable Development**, with inclusive
  enrolment for displaced and nomadic populations and interoperability without
  unrestricted data pooling. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-29 How to build trust alongside Sudan's digital identity system]].
