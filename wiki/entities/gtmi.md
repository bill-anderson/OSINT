# GovTech Maturity Index (GTMI)

A [[world-bank]] reference index scoring countries' GovTech (public-sector digital
transformation) maturity. It is a standing benchmark for cross-country GovTech comparison —
explicitly "not a ranking" but a maturity grouping, with the Bank stressing a shift in focus
from announcements and pilot portals toward **interoperability, adoption and measurable
impact**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Methodology.** GTMI evaluates **48 indicators across four pillars**: core government
  systems, online public services, digital citizen engagement, and GovTech enablers (legal
  frameworks, institutional capacity, digital skills). See [[gov.standards]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[data.statistics]].
- **2025 edition — East African "Group A."** The **2025 GTMI** placed **Tanzania, Kenya,
  Rwanda, Uganda and Egypt** in **Group A ("Extensive GovTech Maturity")**, its highest
  category. Tanzania's placement rested on back-end architecture (the GovESB data-exchange
  bus, HCIMS, Ajira Portal, NeST procurement, GePG payments); the Bank noted that even
  high-maturity countries struggle with usage data, outcome measurement, and the
  politically-sensitive **digital citizen engagement** pillar, which lags region-wide. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-26 Tanzania's GovTech success raises bar for East Africa's digital states]].
