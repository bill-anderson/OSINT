# Guinea National Data Centre (Tier III, Conakry)

Guinea's **first Tier III national data centre**, inaugurated in Conakry on **4 September 2025**, certified **Tier 3 by the Uptime Institute** (redundant power/cooling, annual downtime capped ~1.6h). Owned by the state via the Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and Digital Economy, it hosts national data locally — the physical anchor of Guinea's **digital-sovereignty** narrative ([[infra.store]], [[sovereignty]]), launched alongside the reinstatement of sovereign management of the **.gn** ccTLD. See [[GIN]], [[simandou-2040]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Inaugurated + .gn launch (2025-09-04/05).** Presented as securing national data hosting and control over national information management; a UN E-Government Survey 2024 backdrop put Guinea's EGDI at 0.4006 (29th in Africa, up from 0.2955 in 2022). Sources: [[2025-09-04 Guinea opens Tier 3 national data centre to strengthen digital transition]], [[2025-09-05 Guinea Launches National Domain .gn and Unveils First Tier III Data Center]].
- **Sector-review headline (2026-01).** Cited as a 2025 gain enabling local data hosting; 2026 priorities include strengthening cybersecurity and local hosting. Source: [[2026-01-14 Guinea Reviews 2025 Digital Sector Gains, Targets Faster Rollout in 2026]].
