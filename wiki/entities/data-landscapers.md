# Data Landscapers

Independent research and analysis practice on **data governance and digital
transformation in Africa**, publisher of the long-form analytical work at
[data-landscapers.com](https://data-landscapers.com/). The site describes itself as
"a collection of writings … curated by Bill Anderson"; the practice operates as
**Data Landscapers Ltd**, a UK-registered company. Principal and author: [[bill]].
*(Founding year not stated on the site — not inferred.)*

## Role in this wiki

Data Landscapers is the **publisher** of the author's own work. Under the schema's
admissibility rules its **published** pieces are ingestible as **external expert
analysis** (author [[bill]], publisher Data Landscapers, dated by publication), while
internal notes and unpublished drafts are not. The three circularity guards in
CLAUDE.md apply:

1. a piece that merely renders existing wiki pages is **not** re-ingested to harden them;
2. factual claims still trace to their underlying primary sources — the analysis is
   ingested, not the facts as a shortcut around sourcing;
3. self-citation is always attributed as such ("as Data Landscapers has argued"), never
   presented as independent third-party corroboration.

## Editorial angles

Published work runs along the site's standing themes — **sovereignty, colonialism,
governance, transformation, and OSINT method** — mapped in this wiki to the
`sovereignty` and `colonialism` lenses over the subject taxonomy. Recurring analytical
spine: *CLOUD-Act jurisdiction defeats simple data residency → single-country
localisation is self-defeating → regional (REC-level) localisation by African-owned
operators is the escape route.*
