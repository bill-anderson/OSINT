# Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC)

INEC is Nigeria's constitutional electoral-management body. It holds **one of Nigeria's
largest databases of personal data** (the voter register), which makes it a significant
[[dpi.registry]] and data-protection actor.

## What matters now

- **Data-protection litigation (2026).** The [[digital-rights-lawyers-initiative]] (DRLI),
  alongside lawyer Solomon Etisang, **sued INEC at the Federal High Court, Yenagoa** for
  failing to publish a website privacy policy — alleging breach of the
  [[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]] (ss.24(1)(a), 24(3), 27(1)) and the constitutional
  right to privacy (s.37). Counsel: Olumide Babalola. The suit followed an Oct 2025 FIJ report
  that INEC's site carried no privacy policy. A live enforcement/accountability data point for
  [[gov.protect]] — distinct from the [[ndpc-meta-settlement]] (different actor and cause).
- **Voter-data exposure (2025).** In May 2025 Lere Olayinka (an aide to the FCT Minister)
  shared a voter's registration record on social media; INEC denied a breach, saying an
  authorised user misused valid admin credentials. See [[dpi.registry]],
  [[nigeria--gov-protect]].
