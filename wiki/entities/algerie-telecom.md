# Algérie Télécom

Algeria's **state-owned incumbent telecom operator**; its mobile subsidiary **[[mobilis]]** competes with
[[djezzy]] and [[ooredoo-algeria]]. Central to the national connectivity build-out (see
[[algeria--infra-connect]]):

- **Fixed/fibre ("All Fiber"):** **3m FTTH households** and Africa's top FTTH ranking claimed (2026-02); by
  2026-05 **>7m fixed-internet subscribers** with **>3.4m on fibre**; legacy copper to be fully replaced by
  **end-2026 / early-2027**. Backbone upgraded to nationwide **400G WDM** ([[huawei]], 2025-02); **WiFi 7**
  launched (2025-05); **IPv6** generalisation (2026-07).
- **4G expansion:** 345 new base stations across 44 provinces (Feb 2026), plus a rural programme (4,500
  localities by 2027); universal-service phase 1 reached 1,400 low-density areas.
- **5G:** Mobilis among the three operators awarded 5G licences (Dec 2025, $492m); tests reached 1.2 Gbps.
- **International:** new **[[sparkle]]** (Italy) subsea-cable MoU ([[algerie-telecom-sparkle-cable]]) adding
  redundancy alongside **Medusa**, plus a European PoP and training platform.
- **Innovation:** established a ~1.5bn DZD (~$11m) fund for AI, cybersecurity and robotics startups.
