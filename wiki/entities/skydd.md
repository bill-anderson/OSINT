# Skydd

Nigerian **insure-fintech** ("protection-tech") company building embedded insurance alongside savings,
wallets and payment cards, with a stated ambition to supply protection infrastructure across Africa.

> **Provenance caveat — company statement, not independent reporting.** Everything below comes from a
> **TechCabal Partner / Press Room** item (sponsored placement, byline "Partner", 2026-07-17). It is
> admissible as a **first-hand company announcement**, and is treated as one: the claims are Skydd's
> own about itself, unverified by the outlet, and the promotional framing ("Nigeria's leading
> insure-fintech") is the company's characterisation, not an established market position.

## Model

Skydd's argument is that insurance in Nigeria has been sold as a discrete, forced purchase —
"disconnected from the rest of their financial lives" — and that protection should instead sit
alongside savings, payments and wallets in one platform. Two surfaces follow:

- **Consumer platform** — protection combined with savings, digital wallets and payment cards.
- **Embedded protection infrastructure** — opening its rails to **banks, fintechs, employers,
  merchants and developers** so protection can be embedded into their own products, "as seamlessly as
  they offer payments or savings". This B2B2C turn is the announcement's substantive news: an insurer
  positioning itself as infrastructure rather than a retail brand.

## Reported scale (company figures)

- **More than 20,000 active customers** (as of 2026-07-17).
- Claims **positive EBITDA and positive cash flows** (as of 2026-07-17) — notable in a sector where
  growth is usually funded, but self-reported with no filings on file.
