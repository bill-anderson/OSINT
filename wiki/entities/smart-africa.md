# Smart Africa (Smart Africa Alliance)

Pan-African alliance of member states, international organisations and private-sector
partners advancing the continent's digital single market and infrastructure (CEO **Lacina
Koné**; secretariat in Kigali, [[RWA]]). A **bloc/programme entity**, not a place. Long
listed as a "mentioned" body — hubbed here as it becomes the subject of a source.

## Activity in this wiki

- **AFNIC MoU (2026-07, Geneva).** Smart Africa signed a Memorandum of Understanding with
  **[[afnic]]** (France's domain registry) to strengthen Africa's internet infrastructure:
  building the capacity of African **ccTLD operators**, hardening **DNS** security and
  resilience, modernising national domain registries, and improving internet governance.
  Signed by Lacina Koné and AFNIC DG Pierre Bonis. See [[smart-africa-afnic-mou]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.standards]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Smart Africa and AFNIC Partner to Strengthen Africa's Internet Infrastructure]].
- **Smart Africa Data Exchange (SADX) (2026-07).** Launched the **SADX** cross-border
  data-exchange (pilot **Benin, Ghana, Rwanda**) — which the Data Landscapers "building the
  same road twice" analysis pairs against the AfCFTA Secretariat's competing **ADAPT**
  ([[afcfta]]) as wasteful duplication. A prior **SADX Technical Assessment Mission** (Kigali,
  Apr 2026) with the same three states worked the build-out — PKI trust chains, API contracts,
  cross-border verification-request processing — toward a "functional, federated and
  decentralised" architecture ([[sadx]]). See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]]. Sources:
  [[2026-05-28 Building the same road twice]],
  [[2026-04-27 Smart Africa drives cross-border data interoperability through SADX Technical Mission]].
- **ESTDEV MoU (2026-05-28, Brussels).** Signed an MoU with Estonia's development agency
  **[[estdev]]** ([[estdev-smart-africa-mou]]) to build interoperable, reusable DPI across its
  **42 member states**, reduce vendor lock-in and strengthen cybersecurity and institutional
  capacity. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[geopol.eu]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-28 Estonia backs Africa digital infrastructure]].
- **RealTyme partnership (2026-04-16).** The Smart Africa Digital Academy partnered with Swiss
  zero-trust firm **[[realtyme]]** ([[smart-africa-realtyme-partnership]]) on a "national
  protection framework" — sovereign communication enclaves explicitly positioned against the
  **US Cloud Act**, framed as "digital decolonisation" (claiming Africa faces a ~60% higher
  cyber-attack rate than the global average). See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 Smart Africa, RealTyme join forces for data sovereignty]].
- **Africa AI Council (2026-05).** Cited as having established the **Africa AI Council**
  (Nov 2025) to pool AI resources, part of the continental AI-sovereignty push ($60bn Africa
  AI Fund, Apr 2025 Kigali). See [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]].
- **CAIGA — internet-governance capture critique (Munyua, 2025-11-04).** Munyua ("The Double
  Standard") charges that Smart Africa's **Continental Africa Internet Governance Architecture
  ([[caiga]])** — co-developed and funded by **[[icann]]** under a 2024 ICANN/Smart Africa MoU, with a
  blueprint drafted by a third party based in France — would let the Smart Africa Heads-of-State
  Summit **politically endorse [[afrinic]] governance reforms**, install a Smart Africa-provided
  Permanent Secretary, and substitute political endorsement for member ratification: i.e. governmental
  capture of a community-governed regional registry. Presented at ICANN84 Dublin as "two years of
  joint work with ICANN," and at the Africa Internet Summit Accra with 24 hours' notice. Smart Africa
  given here as **40 member states** (cf. 42 elsewhere on this page). See [[gov.regional]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-04 The Double Standard Why Are Internet Governance Rules Different for Africa]].
- **Ghana National AI Strategy partner (2026-04).** Ghana's Cabinet approved its
  **National AI Strategy (2023–2033)** and a **$250m** national AI computing centre; the
  strategy was developed with **Smart Africa**, GIZ FAIR Forward and The Future Society,
  with public launch set for 24 Apr 2026. See [[ghana-national-ai-strategy]], [[GHA]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.training]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-03 Ghana approves $250M AI Hub & National AI Strategy]].
- **WARDIP2 collaborator (2026-03).** Named a partner — with **[[ecowas]]**, the **[[african-union]]**
  and the **[[world-bank]]** — on **[[wardip]]**, the **$137m** Western Africa Regional Digital
  Integration Program phase 2 (Benin, Liberia, Sierra Leone), targeting broadband and digital
  public services for ~10.6m people and a common regional digital market. See [[gov.regional]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[XWA]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 New $137M initiative to enable digital service access for millions in three ECOWAS nations]].
  (Smart Africa's **Digital ID Blueprint** and cross-border trust-framework work also underpin Benin's
  national-ID and e-gov programmes.)

- **Djibouti alignment sought (2026-07).** At WSIS 2026, CEO Lacina Koné met Djibouti's
  communications minister to discuss aligning **[[DJI]] Djibouti**'s national digital projects
  (the Djibouti Digital Foundation) and the **[[eardip]]** regional integration project with
  Smart Africa's continental data-sovereignty and AI initiatives. See [[gov.regional]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[XEA]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 Djibouti Seeks ITU, Smart Africa Support for Telecoms and AI Development]].
