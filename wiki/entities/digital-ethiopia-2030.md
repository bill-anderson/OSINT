# Digital Ethiopia 2030

Ethiopia's national digital strategy (DE2030), successor to the Digital Ethiopia 2025
plan. It frames data governance explicitly as **digital sovereignty** and a defence
against "new forms of digital colonization," and aligns to the **AU Digital Transformation
Strategy (2020–2030)** ([[african-union]]) and the **AfCFTA Digital Trade Protocol**
([[afcfta]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Targets (as of 2026-01-27).** 100% 5G coverage in 100 cities; **128m** mobile
  subscribers; **every adult a [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]] digital ID by 2030** (key to
  all public/private services via the MESOB one-stop system); digital payments up
  **7x** via [[telebirr]] and the National Payment Gateway. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27 Digital Ethiopia 2030 Charting a Sovereign Path to Prosperity]].
- **Continental positioning.** Three strategic pillars: **green-energy leadership** —
  GERD hydropower at **$0.03–0.05/kWh**, positioning Ethiopia as the envisioned "**Cloud
  of Africa**" for regional data hosting; **regional integration** — the **East African
  Digital Corridor** with Kenya and digitised Moyale border trade; and **sovereignty
  benchmarking** — a sovereign cloud plus the Amharic-language "**Mela**" AI model. Skills
  via the **5 Million Coders** program (70% of the population is under 30), targeting
  5,000+ startups. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]], [[infra.energy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27 Digital Ethiopia 2030 Charting a Sovereign Path to Prosperity]].
- **Anchoring initiatives (2026).** [[ai-unipod-ethiopia]] (Feb 2026) launched explicitly
  under DE2030, and [[ethio-telecom]]'s "Next Horizon: Digital & Beyond 2028" strategy is
  aligned to it. See [[tech.ai]], [[tech.industry]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-19 Ethiopia launches AI UniPod to drive innovation]],
  [[2026-01-30 Inside Ethio Telecom's bold TechCo transformation]].
