# Nexus AI Factory (Morocco sovereign AI data centre)

A landmark **sovereign AI-compute build** unveiled at **GITEX Africa 2026**, led by **[[nexus-core-systems|Nexus
Core Systems]]** with the digital-transition ministry (MTNRA), MICEPP and AMDIE — integrating a
**high-performance (HPC) data centre**, a Center of Excellence (skills) and an Innovation Hub, positioned
to bridge Africa, Europe and global markets under **[[digital-morocco-2030|Digital Morocco 2030]]**. See
[[morocco--infra-store]].

## Capacity — resolved 2026-07-21: 500 MW and 36 MW measure different things

The wiki carried **500 MW** and **~36 MW** as an unresolved ~14× conflict. **They are not in
conflict, and neither is wrong** — they are two different quantities from two announcements a year
apart, welded together by trade press:

- **36 MW is contracted IT load across phases 1–2.** The **MTNRA ministry release (2026-04-08)** —
  the primary every April-2026 write-up derives from — states an **initial investment of MAD 12bn over
  two phases**, targeting **125 direct jobs by end-2027**: **phase 1, Nouaceur hub, MAD 5bn, 16 MW, 50
  direct jobs**; **phase 2, northern expansion, MAD 7bn, a further 20 MW**, bringing **cumulative
  capacity to 36 MW by end-2027**. The release contains **no "500 MW" and no USD figure at all**.
- **500 MW is contracted renewable *power supply* for the full programme**, not IT load. It comes from
  the **June-2025 consortium announcement**: the programme "will be powered by **up to 500 MW of
  renewable energy under a strategic agreement with TAQA Morocco**", with a first phase then billed at
  **over 40 MW** of AI accelerators.

So: **power-supply ceiling vs contracted IT load, a year apart.** Both stand once labelled, and the
"single site on the outskirts of Casablanca" framing is wrong on its own terms — **Nouaceur is in the
Casablanca region, but phase 2 is a separate northern site**.

**A supersession worth recording:** phase 1 **slipped and shrank**. June 2025 said "over 40 MW"
launching **Q1 2026**; April 2026 says **16 MW from 2027**. Same phase, materially downgraded.

**Money — carry MAD, and the USD the wiki held is wrong.** MAD is the announcing party's own currency
and **no primary carries any USD figure**; every dollar number is press conversion. **MAD 12bn is
explicitly an "investissement *initial*" covering only phases 1 and 2** (5 + 7), not the full-programme
envelope — and **no primary states a cost for the 500 MW build-out**. At the signing-day rate
(2026-04-08) MAD 12bn is about **US$1.29bn**, not the **US$1.2bn** the wiki carried undated.

**Not established (2026-07-21):** whether the 500 MW TAQA supply agreement is still live as of the
April-2026 MoU — the ministry release does not mention TAQA, and only trade press carries it forward;
any capital cost for the 500 MW programme; and whether phase 1's 16 MW IT load sits inside a larger
gross facility. **Nexus Core Systems' own site publishes no Morocco capacity figures at all.**

*Sources for this resolution are the reconcile pass's research; the underlying MTNRA and consortium
releases are **queued for acquisition** — the wiki still holds only the two trade-press write-ups.*
