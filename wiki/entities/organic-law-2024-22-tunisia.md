# Organic Law No. 22 of 2024 (Tunisia Biometric Identity Card)

**The Tunisian organic law on the biometric national identity card, adopted by Parliament on
6 March 2024 "by an overwhelming majority" after an eight-year contest.** Its companion is
[[organic-law-2024-23-tunisia]] (biometric passport).

**Organic laws rank higher than regular statutes in Tunisia**, typically dealing with fundamental
aspects of government structure or basic rights and freedoms of citizens — which makes Laws 22 and
23 of 2024 constitutionally weighty instruments rather than ordinary enabling legislation.

**Currency — write this as a dated target, not an accomplished rollout.** Adoption of the law is
established. **Deployment is not.** A ministerial session on **22 April 2024** targeted usage
beginning in **H1 2025**; **no source held confirms that target was met**, and the freshest evidence
in the corpus (**2025-03**) still describes the biometric ID as **proposed**. See **GAP-TUN-002**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Adoption (6 March 2024).** Parliament adopted the Biometric ID and Biometric Passport bills by
  an overwhelming majority, culminating a campaign led by [[access-now]] and Tunisian civil society
  **since 2016** against a mandatory biometric ID system without robust data protection and privacy
  safeguards. Committee deliberations **lacked public consultations**; the Committee on Rights and
  Freedoms green-lit the bills on **28 February 2024**. **One day before the vote, MPs introduced an
  amendment to prohibit the creation of a centralised biometric database by the
  [[ministry-of-interior-tunisia]] — it was dismissed.** See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-07 Eight years in the making - Tunisia's Biometric ID and Passport Bills risk rights]].
- **Origins (August 2016).** The bill first emerged when the MoI proposed replacing traditional
  identity cards with **mandatory chip-enabled biometric ones for all citizens aged 15 and above**;
  after setbacks it was revived in late 2023 (fourth draft approved at ministerial council
  **30 November 2023**; to committee **25 December 2023**). Source:
  [[2024-03-07 Eight years in the making - Tunisia's Biometric ID and Passport Bills risk rights]].
- **Implementation target set (22 April 2024).** A ministerial working session at the **Government
  Palace in the Kasbah**, chaired by **Prime Minister Ahmed Hachani**, decided practical measures to
  start actual use of the biometric national ID card and biometric passport **during the first six
  months of 2025**, following approval of Organic Laws 22 and 23. The steering committee comprises
  the [[ministry-of-interior-tunisia]], the
  [[ministry-of-communication-technologies-tunisia]] and the Ministry of Economy and Planning;
  participants stressed speeding up selection of the best issuance route. Attending: Interior
  Minister Kamel Feki, Finance Minister Sihem Nemsia Boughdiri, Economy and Planning Minister Feryel
  Ouerghi Sebai, and Communication Technologies Minister [[nizar-ben-neji]]. Source:
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia - Biometric ID card and passport usage to begin in H1 2025]].
- **Rank of organic laws, and the ICAO horizon (2024-04).** Organic laws in Tunisia rank higher than
  regular statutes, typically dealing with fundamental aspects of government structure or basic
  rights and freedoms. The move aligns with **[[icao]]** recommendations, which encourage **all
  countries to adopt biometric documents by 2026**. See [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2024-04-23 Tunisia to Get Biometric IDs in H1 of 2025]].
- **Still "proposed" as of March 2025 — the target is unconfirmed.** Biometric Update describes the
  **proposed** biometric ID as one that "would force citizens to provide biometric data to the
  Ministry of Interior", and relays [[access-now]]'s allegation that the biometric ID project was
  among three programmes developed with minimal transparency and public consultation. The present
  tense here is the point: eleven months after the H1-2025 target was set, and inside the target
  window, the freshest source held does not describe a live card. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
