# MTN Nigeria — Sifiso Dabengwa Data Centre (Lagos)

**[[mtn-nigeria]]**'s **US$235m** carrier-neutral Lagos data centre (named after former MTN Group
CEO Sifiso Dabengwa), paired with a cloud service pitched to rival AWS, Microsoft and Google —
notably **naira-denominated pricing** (not merely naira acceptance), local low-latency hosting
and **self-orchestration** (a first in Nigeria).

- **2025-07-01** — Phase 1 launched: Tier III, **780 racks**, **4.5 MW** IT load, ~$100m build
  plus **$20m** cloud infrastructure. MTN claims West Africa's largest Tier III facility to date.
- **Phase 2** — planned to Tier IV, lifting capacity to **9–14 MW** (some execs cite up to 20 MW),
  est. ~$135m, demand-dependent (no firm timeframe).

Figures vary across outlets (9 MW full-build vs 14/20 MW scale-up). A load-bearing anchor in the
Lagos data-centre cluster and cited as the local capacity to absorb the **[[cbn]]** data-
localisation mandate. See [[infra.store]], [[nigeria--infra-store]].
