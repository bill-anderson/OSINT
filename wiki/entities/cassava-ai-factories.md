# Cassava AI Factories

A roughly $720 million [[cassava-technologies]] program to build "AI factories" — sovereign GPU compute — on African soil. It matters as the flagship bid to keep African model training and data onshore.

## Profile
- Plan to build five AI factories in South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya, Egypt and Morocco.
- First facility at the [[africa-data-centres]] Johannesburg campus: 3,000 Nvidia H100 GPUs, more than 90% pre-reserved.
- Full cluster reported at 12,000–15,000 GPUs, able to train LLMs without offshoring data.
- Target: lift African researchers' access to pre-training GPUs from about 5% (2024) to more than 50% by end-2027.
- First Johannesburg pods take workloads Q3 2026; full network Q4 2026; carbon-negative via renewable PPAs.
- **AI Factory launched/deploying in SA (2026-03)**, scaling to NGA/KEN/EGY/MAR; Cassava bills itself the **continent's first NVIDIA Cloud Partner**, offering GPUaaS and AIaaS/APIs, with model tuning starting in **Swahili**, then Zulu and Afrikaans. Runs alongside **[[caimex]]** (launched 2025, NVIDIA Blueprints/NIM) and the **Cassava Autonomous Network** blueprint for MNOs. Sources: [[2026-03-18 Cassava Technologies Launches AI Factory in South Africa, Plans Expansion Across Africa]], [[2026-03-03 Cassava unveils NVIDIA-backed autonomous network solution]].
- Compute is packaged for governments through the **[[national-sovereign-cloud]]** Cloud Partner Programme (NVIDIA Cloud Partner solutions + CAIMEx with Anthropic Claude and Google Gemini). Source: [[2026-03-06 Cassava launches sovereign cloud for Africa's public sector]].
- NVIDIA reported as a Cassava shareholder since November 2025.
- **Phase-1 delivery + first workloads (Russell Southwood, 2026-05-19).** Southwood
  reports **3,000 GPUs delivered to Cape Town in June 2025** (phase 1), with early
  demand coming from **Rockefeller Foundation grantees** (Digital Green, Jacaranda
  Health, Rising Academies); a further **~12,000 GPUs** are planned across EGY/NGA/
  KEN/MAR over 3–4 years, and Cassava is described as the **only Nvidia cloud
  partner** on the continent — a concrete demand signal in an otherwise thin
  specialist-AI market. Source:
  [[2026-05-19 Trying to make sense of who will use Artificial Intelligence in Africa and how – Going beyond the hype]].
- Anjola Adebowale (2026-06-12) independently cites the **~$720m Cassava–Nvidia
  partnership** as a marquee sovereign-compute datapoint. Source:
  [[2026-06-12 Beyond the AI Stack Why Governments Need Dynamic Digital Infrastructure]].
- ⚠ Caveat: the $720m figure and GPU counts are lightly sourced (Ecofin plus a niche blog citing TIME) — though the $720m now corroborates across Adebowale's independent piece; verify precise counts against Cassava/Nvidia primary sources.
