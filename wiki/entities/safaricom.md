# Safaricom

East African telecommunications group (Kenyan-headquartered; operator of M-PESA). Its
Ethiopian subsidiary, **Safaricom Ethiopia**, is a delivery partner in Ethiopia's
national digital-ID rollout.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Ethiopia — Fayda registration (2026-07).** Safaricom Ethiopia signed a **Joint
  Venture Agreement** with Ethiopia's **National ID Program** and **Africom Technologies
  PLC** to deliver **Lots 3 & 4** of the [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]] Digital ID
  Registration Project — large-scale citizen registration across Afar, Amhara, Tigray,
  Sidama and the South West / Central / South Ethiopia regions. CEO **Wim Vanhelleputte**
  framed it around digital inclusion, service access and short-term registration jobs.
  See [[safaricom-ethiopia-fayda-jv]], [[ETH]], [[dpi.id]].
- **Vodacom's East Africa anchor (2026-07).** [[vodacom]] is raising its Safaricom
  stake **35%→55%** via a **$2.1bn** deal ([[vodacom-safaricom-stake-deal]]) — buying
  15% from the Kenyan government and 5% from Vodafone at **KES 34/share**, subject to
  Kenya/Ethiopia/SA approvals with Kenya retaining 20% and board seats — making
  Safaricom (and M-PESA) the centrepiece of its East-Africa financial-services
  strategy (103m → 130m FS customers by 2030). See [[XEA]], [[dpi.pay]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-03 Vodacom expands East Africa strategy]],
  [[2025-12-04 Vodacom Group to take controlling stake in Safaricom]].
- **M-PESA as "Ecosystem Debt" (analysis, 2025-08 / 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki frames M-PESA as
  the archetypal **closed platform** — a "regressive tax" and "Ecosystem Debt" contrasted with
  generative public rails (India Stack, Brazil Pix) in his six-country DPI-maturity read, and, in
  the "payments end-game" essay, cites M-PESA handling **KShs 40.24tn** on a **>90%** Safaricom
  network share to argue such dominance faces margin compression once low-cost central-bank instant
  rails arrive. One analyst's rubric/thesis, attributed to Kariuki — not an authoritative index.
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.id]]. Sources:
  [[2025-08-04 93 - Ecosystem Debt or Generative Platforms A Case for Digital Public Infrastructure]],
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]].
- **Mobile money as tax infrastructure (2026-05).** M-PESA is the centrepiece example as
  African governments ([[KEN]], Nigeria, Senegal) fold mobile-money rails into tax
  collection to widen domestic revenue — trading traceability for the risk that higher
  transaction costs push fee-sensitive, low-value users back to cash. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-29 Africa's Mobile Money Networks Are Becoming Tax Infrastructure]].
- **Huawei AI "Idea-to-Cash" (2026-04).** An ICT4D evidence audit flags Safaricom's
  Aug-2025 **Huawei CBS "Idea-to-Cash"** platform — AI that generates and configures
  financial products from user-behaviour data with a disclosed **revenue-optimisation**
  objective (an earlier pilot lifted ARPU 24%) — alongside its Zuri chatbot, biometric/
  SIM-swap fraud monitoring and ML credit scoring, raising governance questions about
  unaudited AI in M-PESA. See [[tech.ai]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 3 Reasons Mobile Money Programs Have a $1.7 Trillion Problem]].
- **M-PESA top-ups settled in stablecoins in the DRC (2026-07).** In a live DRC use case confirmed on
  record by a **[[visa]]** exec (Sullivan), **M-PESA** wallet top-ups are settled in **stablecoins in the
  background** via Visa Pay, with **[[onafriq]]** — bypassing correspondent banking (~8% SSA remittance
  fee), building on Visa Pay's Sept-2025 DRC launch. M-PESA is the wallet topped up, not a confirmed
  stablecoin-leg partner; its *own* announced stablecoin move is a **separate** Jan 2026 ADI Foundation
  (UAE-dirham) alliance. The "joint USD-stablecoin *pilot* launch" framing is downgraded (no joint issuer
  announcement; USD peg inferred) — REV-006 resolved 2026-07-16. See
  [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]], [[finance.new]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-02 Visa tests stablecoins in DRC for cross-border payments (The EastAfrican)]],
  [[2026-07-02 DR Congo becomes Africa's live test for dollar-settled mobile money (Ecofin)]].
- **M-PESA Ethiopia +258% via EthSwitch (2026-03).** M-PESA Ethiopia active users surged
  **258.5% YoY to 5.2m** (quarter to Dec 2025) after the **Oct 2025 [[ethswitch]]
  integration** linking M-PESA to 30+ banks/wallets with real-time wallet-to-bank
  transfers and interoperable **EthQR** merchant payments (50,000+ merchants). Safaricom
  Ethiopia service revenue rose **54.2% to $75m**. See [[ETH]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 M-PESA Ethiopia surges 258% as EthSwitch fuels growth]].
- **My OneApp (2026-04).** Safaricom unveiled **My OneApp**, merging the M-PESA App and
  MySafaricom App into a single super-app spanning payments, account management and telecom
  services. See [[KEN]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-02 Safaricom Introduces My OneApp to Simplify Payments, Accounts, and Service Access]].
- **Ethiopia — 12.2m customers (2026-02).** [[safaricom-ethiopia]] closed the quarter to Dec 2025
  with **12.2m three-month active customers (+71.7% YoY)**; 4G now covers **57.1%** of the
  population (3,483 sites) and Q3 FY26 service revenue rose **54.2%**. Since its 2021 entry it has
  deployed **$2.27bn** in capital, self-building **~60%** of sites for lack of an open-access
  regime while [[ethio-telecom]] cross-subsidises data via voice — corroborating the M-PESA
  Ethiopia surge (5.2m active, +258.5% via [[ethswitch]], against [[telebirr]]'s 72m). See [[ETH]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.pay]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-02 Safaricom Ethiopia Surpasses 12 Million Users as M-PESA Adoption Grows]],
  [[2026-01-07 Why Ethiopia, Somalia and Djibouti are starting to matter in digital Africa]].
