# Vodacom Group

South Africa-headquartered pan-African operator (Vodafone-controlled); with
**[[safaricom]]** (55% owned) the largest mobile-money franchise on the
continent, pivoting from connectivity to financial-services and digital
platforms.

## Activity in this wiki

- **East Africa strategy (2026-07-03).** Anchored by its raised **55% Safaricom
  stake**, prioritising **M-PESA / VodaPay / Vodafone Cash** over pure
  connectivity. FY2026 financial-services revenue **R16.8bn (~$1.03bn, +20%)**;
  beyond-core now **46.4%** of M-PESA revenue; **103m** financial-services
  customers targeting **130m by 2030**; unbanked opportunity flagged in Egypt,
  SA, Tanzania, DRC, Lesotho, Mozambique. See [[XEA]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-07-03 Vodacom expands East Africa strategy]].
- **Safaricom control deal (2026-07).** Moving to lift its Safaricom holding
  **35%→55%** in a **$2.1bn** transaction ([[vodacom-safaricom-stake-deal]]) — buying
  15% from the Kenyan government and 5% from Vodafone at **KES 34/share**, pending
  Kenya/Ethiopia/SA approvals (Kenya keeps 20% and board seats); full consolidation
  would lift Vodacom Group revenue toward **R220bn ($12.9bn)**. See [[KEN]],
  [[safaricom]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-04 Vodacom Group to take controlling stake in Safaricom]].
- **Scale on Google Cloud (2026-07).** Cited as a marquee Google Cloud adopter:
  **237m customers**, 103m financial-services customers, **~$526bn** transactions
  processed annually, consolidating data on BigQuery with stress on **data
  residency**. Source:
  [[2026-07-03 Google Cloud Region forecasts R1.7tn boost for SA]].
- **Mobile-economy context.** One of the G6 operators behind the GSMA
  continent-wide African-language AI-model collaboration; see
  [[2026-06-18 Africa's $240 Billion Mobile Economy A Checkpoint on Africa's Digital Transformation Journey]].
- **Satellite revenue-leakage (2026-05-26).** Named among the telcos (with MTN, Airtel)
  whose premium customers offshore LEO operators like [[starlink]] capture under far
  lighter licensing; the Africa CEO Forum/Askya report urges a hybrid model treating
  satellites as wholesale, not retail, competitors. See [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-26 Africa risks losing billions to satellite internet operators, report says]].
- **Starlink LEO backhaul + resale (2026-01).** Vodacom moved from rival to ally, signing
  the **[[starlink-vodacom-leo-partnership]]** for LEO backhaul and equipment resale across its
  footprint (>223m customers; Vision 2030 target 260m customers / 120m financial-services
  users) — the wholesale model the leakage report urged, now adopted. See [[data.satellite]],
  [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]].
- **Zambia–Mozambique fibre interconnector (2026-02-23).** Vodacom partnered with
  [[bayobab-zambia]] (MTN) to commission the **Chanida–Cassacatiza** cross-border fibre link,
  joining Zambia's backbone to Mozambique's submarine-cable network — part of the drive that
  makes Zambia, per Minister Mutati, "the most digitally connected country in Southern Africa"
  (now fibre-linked to eight neighbours). See [[ZMB]], [[MOZ]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 Zambia and Mozambique launch fibre-optic interconnector at Chanida–Cassacatiza border]].

- **Maziv 30% + "managed decline" of voice (Russell Southwood, 2025-12-04).**
  Southwood's MNO state-of-market lists Vodacom among the four "power players"
  diversifying out of connectivity: acquiring **30% of Maziv** (owner of Vumatel
  and Dark Fibre Africa) to anchor SA fibre, running SA fixed broadband at
  **198,000** customers, and — per Vodacom's own IR — treating **prepaid voice as
  a "managed decline"** business. Mobile-money is **11.6%** of group revenue but
  **42%** of Safaricom's. See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.connect]], [[ZAF]].
  Source: [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].

- **Vodacom Business Data Centre, Matola (2025-03).** Vodacom Mozambique opened a **$25m**
  carrier-neutral **Tier 3** (Uptime-certified) data centre in Tchumene, Matola — said to be the
  country's largest — with direct access to the **2Africa** subsea cable; 2,600 m² starting at 128
  racks (expandable past 500). At the launch PM Benvinda Levi noted Mozambique now had **18 data
  centres**. See [[MOZ]], [[infra.store]]. Sources:
  [[2025-03-31 Vodacom opens data center in Maputo, Mozambique]],
  [[2025-04-23 Vodacom Business Data Centre - A Strategic Step Towards Business Digitalisation in Mozambique]].

- **Lesotho — market leader at 30 (2025-26).** [[infra.connect]] Vodacom Lesotho (80% Group / 20%
  Sekha-Metsi) marked 30 years with **1.21m customers / 85% share**, 422 base stations, and **14,000
  M-Pesa agents**; it invested **$40m** in a network/AI upgrade (Joosub visit; AWS digital-skills pledge),
  engaged the government on digitisation ("as in Kenya and Egypt"), and spun M-Pesa off as **VCL
  Financial Services** — amid LCA consumer-protection/UAF scrutiny. See [[LSO]], [[lesotho--infra-connect]].
  Sources: [[2026-06-17-vodacom-lesotho-30-years-publiceye]],
  [[2026-03-13-vodacom-40m-investment-vcl-financial-itweb]],
  [[2025-08-25-vodacom-network-upgrade-joosub-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-02-17-lesotho-vodacom-digitisation-partnership-itweb]].
