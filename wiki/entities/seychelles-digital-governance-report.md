# Digital Governance Analysis - Seychelles, 2024 Edition

**A country-level digest of Seychelles' standing on international digital indices**, published by
the **Economic Forum for Sustainable Development** (eforumsd.com) and stated on its cover as
*"Report Created For: The Secretary General of The Commonwealth of Nations"*. Held here as a PDF
artefact with a companion source page: a **reference object to consult and cite**, not a report of
a development. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]], [[SYC]].

> **Reference study — cite, don't absorb.** Under this wiki's currency discipline, this study's
> dated figures are **not** to be promoted onto concept or place pages as current state. Cite the
> study; leave its numbers in it. Fresher country-level data on Seychelles arrives via clippings —
> see [[SYC]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **What it is.** A "2024 Edition" analysis, filed `published: 2024-01-01` with
  **`date_precision: year`** — the January date is padding for sortability, not a publication
  date. Its chapters are index readings rather than original research: active mobile-broadband
  subscriptions, the **E-Government Development Index**, innovation capacity, the **Online Service
  Index**, the **Telecommunication Infrastructure Index**, the **Human Capital Index** and the
  **Global Cybersecurity Index**. Source:
  [[2024-01-01 Digital Governance Analysis - Seychelles 2024 Edition]].
- **Why the cite-don't-absorb guard binds doubly here.** The figures are themselves **second-hand
  restatements of ITU and UN index readings**. Promoting them onto a concept or place page would
  launder an index number through an intermediary and detach it from the body that computed it —
  precisely the failure the rule exists to prevent. Where the wiki needs the EGDI reading for
  Seychelles, it takes it dated and attributed to the index itself (92nd of 193, score 0.6773,
  2024 index — recorded on [[SYC]]).
- **Provenance, now established.** The publisher was not recoverable from the artefact's filename
  or metadata and was initially filed unknown; it was read off the cover page on 2026-07-17. The
  commissioning relationship ("created for" the Commonwealth Secretary-General) is the report's own
  claim about itself — no Commonwealth publication record is held to corroborate it, and no author
  or methodology note is stated in the document.
- **The document itself is real (established 2026-07-20).** An earlier check flagged it as possibly
  non-existent, on the grounds that its stored title and date matched nothing on the open web. That
  is wrong: the canonical PDF is served by the publisher at
  `eforumsd.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Seychelles-Digital-Governance-Report.pdf`, and its cover
  matches the stored title and the "Report Created For" line verbatim. The bare-domain `url:` on the
  source page has been replaced with that document-specific URL. **What remains genuinely
  unestablished, as of 2026-07-20, is the publisher's standing**: the Economic Forum for Sustainable
  Development self-describes as a "consortium" advancing sustainable economic growth, names no
  authors, states no methodology, and the wiki holds nothing independent on it. Capture is not
  endorsement — the cite-don't-absorb guard above is what governs use of its numbers, and it binds
  regardless.
- **How to use it.** Consult it as background on Seychelles' index standing around 2024, and as a
  pointer to the primary indices. A dated fact drawn *from* it becomes a normal source citing this
  entity — it does not make the study evidence of current state.
