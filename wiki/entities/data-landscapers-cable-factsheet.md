## What it is

An **internal Data Landscapers reference fact sheet**, *"Submarine cables serving
Africa,"* dated **June 2026**, described as the **human-readable companion to the
downloadable `cable-factsheet.csv`**. It lists submarine cables — operating or
planned — connecting Africa to the rest of the world, with per-cable lifecycle,
ownership, route/physical, capacity and commercial/access fields (definitions in
the CSV metadata table).

**No raw `.md` clipping exists.** Compiled from the PDF held in `raw/`:
`2026-06-03 cable-factsheet.pdf` (companion file: `cable-factsheet.csv`).

## Coverage (unverified — for orientation only)

The quick-reference table spans cables including **SAT-3/WASC** (RFS 2001,
36-operator consortium, 0.12 Tbps design, 2 pairs, est. end-of-life 2026),
**EASSy** (2010, WIOCC largest shareholder), **SEACOM** (2009, Remgro/IPS–Aga
Khan), **TEAMS** (2009), **MainOne** (2010, [[equinix]]), **WACS** (2012,
14-member consortium), **ACE** (2012, [[orange]]-led), **[[equiano-cable]]**
(2018, [[google]], 144 Tbps, 12 pairs), **[[2africa-cable]]** (2020/2023, [[meta]]
consortium lead, 180 Tbps, 16 pairs), **[[umoja-cable]]** (Google), **Waterworth**
(Meta, 24 pairs) and **SEACOM 2.0**. Detailed per-cable pages follow for each.
