# OTDI / NDMI (Liberia digital-government reform)

Liberia's **whole-of-government digital-governance reform**, established by **Executive Order No. 163**
(signed 22 April 2026). EO 163 creates the **National Digitalization and Modernization Initiative
(NDMI)** and, within the Office of the President, the **Office of Technology, Digitalization and
Innovation (OTDI)** — the Executive Branch's coordinating authority for standards-based, interoperable
digital government ([[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]]). See [[LBR]].

## What we know (as of 2026-04)

- **OTDI's mandate.** Develop enterprise-wide technical standards, ensure interoperability, and provide
  **technical clearance** for major ICT investments affecting cross-government operations, sensitive
  personal data, and core registries. It mandates consolidation/reuse of shared digital capabilities, a
  full inventory of government digital systems, a **Government Enterprise Architecture Framework** and a
  **Government Interoperability Framework**.
- **Cybersecurity + data protection.** OTDI must set **baseline cybersecurity requirements** for all
  government systems (identity/access management, logging, backups, vulnerability management, incident
  response), coordinated with the Ministries of Posts & Telecommunications and Justice. Data-sharing must
  comply with constitutional privacy protections (**Article 16**) under formal agreements — even as
  Liberia still lacks a comprehensive [[liberia-data-protection-bill|data-protection law]]
  ([[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]]).
- **Governance + timeline.** A **Digital Government Steering Committee**, chaired by the Minister of
  State for Presidential Affairs, oversees implementation with OTDI as technical secretariat (quarterly
  reports). MACs submit a systems inventory within **60 days**; OTDI presents a prioritised roadmap
  within **180 days**; draft institutionalising legislation within **12 months**. The order preserves
  the statutory independence of the [[central-bank-of-liberia|CBL]] and Liberia Revenue Authority, and
  keeps ICT procurement under the Public Procurement and Concessions Act.
- **Reception.** Tech-sector figures (Kpetermeni Siakor/Kwagei Group, Dwalu Khasu, Sekou Keita/Liberia
  Technology Summit, Vivien Jones/Mwetana Consulting) welcomed EO 163 but warned success depends on
  execution and **meaningful inclusion of Liberian expertise** — the sovereignty tension also running
  through the national-ID concession. Source:
  [[2026-04-29-executive-order-163-digital-government-ndmi-observer]].
