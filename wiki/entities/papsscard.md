# PAPSSCARD

PAPSSCARD is an Africa-wide retail card scheme introduced by [[papss]] in collaboration with
the [[afreximbank]] and **Mercury Payment Services (MPS)**. It aims to route card payments
**entirely within the continent** — rather than through global card networks — to cut fees
and keep transaction value and data in Africa. A [[sovereignty]]-framed complement to the
PAPSS interbank rail.

## Key facts

- Launched to enable fast, low-cost cross-border retail payments across African borders,
  addressing the fact that most African card payments are currently routed through global
  systems (raising fees and ceding data control).
- **PAPSS CEO Mike Ogbalu III** described it as "a powerful symbol of progress and a bold
  step towards financial independence"; **John Bosco Sebabi** is acting CEO of PAPSSCARD.
- Positioned to benefit corporates, banks, merchants and individuals, and to reduce costs for
  public institutions.

Distinct from the interbank PAPSS settlement system itself; PAPSSCARD is the consumer/retail
card layer. See [[dpi.pay]], [[papss]], [[XAF]].
