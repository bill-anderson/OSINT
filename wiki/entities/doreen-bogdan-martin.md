# Doreen Bogdan-Martin

**Secretary-General of the [[itu]]** — the principal voice of the UN's digital-technologies agency,
and the counterpart African ministers and regulators meet when they seek ITU technical or political
backing. Created here on the same "principals" bar as ministers and heads of agency already hubbed in
this wiki; she recurs across the connectivity, AI-governance and cable-resilience threads rather than
appearing once.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Submarine-cable resilience roadmap (2026-07-10).** Welcomed the final report of the
  **[[international-advisory-body-on-submarine-cable-resilience]]** — the ITU–ICPC body co-chaired by
  Nigeria's **[[bosun-tijani]]** — as "a practical roadmap to keep undersea networks reliable,"
  closing its two-year programme at WSIS Forum 2026. See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.standards]].
  Source: [[2026-07-10-itu-icpc-advisory-body-cable-resilience-report]].
- **AUC–ITU cooperation MoU (2026-07).** Signed a cooperation MoU with the **[[african-union|AU
  Commission]]** (Commissioner Lerato Mataboge) at the AI for Good Global Summit, covering AI, DPI,
  digital/AI skills, universal connectivity, cybersecurity, space tech, standards and digital
  regulation. See [[gov.regional]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-16 African Union Commission, ITU sign cooperation agreement]].
- **Bilateral demand from small states (2026-07).** Met **[[DJI]] Djibouti**'s communications minister
  on the WSIS-2026 sidelines on public-service digitalisation, AI/digital skills and urban–rural
  divide reduction. See [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 Djibouti Seeks ITU, Smart Africa Support for Telecoms and AI Development]].
- **Partner2Connect (2026-07).** Presided over the **[[partner2connect]]** coalition crossing **USD
  100bn** in connectivity pledges, against ITU's **USD 2.6–2.8tn** estimate for universal meaningful
  connectivity by 2030. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 ITU's Partner2Connect crosses $100 billion in global connectivity pledges]].
