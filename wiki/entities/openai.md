# OpenAI

US AI lab; entering Africa via skills/academy proposals and — through **OpenAI
for Countries** / Stargate — the sovereign-AI-infrastructure model the US is
exporting.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Kenya OpenAI Academy (proposed, 2026-06).** President **[[william-ruto]]**
  disclosed G7-sidelines talks with CEO **Sam Altman** on siting the **first
  OpenAI Academy in Eastern Africa** in Nairobi. A proposal, not a signed deal.
  See [[KEN]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-18 Kenya Eyes First OpenAI Academy in East Africa Following Ruto–Sam Altman Talks]].
- **OpenAI for Countries context.** [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]] cites Stargate
  UAE / OpenAI for Countries as the private-sector template for exporting US AI
  infrastructure abroad. See [[geopol.usa]], [[technology-prosperity-deal]].

- **"Horizon1000" health-AI pilot (announced 2026-01-22).** A **$50m** pilot with the
  **[[gates-foundation]]** ([[horizon1000]]) combining funding, technology and technical support
  to embed AI in African national health systems — beginning in **Rwanda** ([[RWA]]), scaling to
  **1,000 primary-care clinics by 2028**, targeting SSA's 5.6m health-worker gap; endorsed by
  Rwanda's [[paula-ingabire]]. Critiqued by Data Landscapers. See [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.mis]],
  [[finance.new]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-22 Gates Foundation and OpenAI Commit $50m to AI-Powered Health Systems in Africa]],
  [[2026-01-28 A bit of quality assurance advice for Bill Gates]].

- **Microsoft partnership restructured (2026-04-28, global context).** OpenAI
  ended its Microsoft exclusivity — Azure stays primary through 2032, but OpenAI
  can now sell models on **AWS** ([[amazon-web-services]], via Bedrock) and Google
  Cloud; Microsoft drops its revenue-share to OpenAI, and OpenAI's payments to
  Microsoft are now capped. No African place — filed as global cloud/AI context.
  See [[microsoft]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-28 OpenAI Breaks Free Of Microsoft, Expands AWS Partnership]].

- **DeepSeek competition (2026-03 context).** Named among the US AI firms whose African
  push is being challenged by Chinese [[deepseek]]'s low-cost open models (11–14% of African
  chatbot usage); the **$50m** [[gates-foundation]]–OpenAI health-AI partnership (Jan 2026)
  is cited as part of the US response. See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12 China's DeepSeek gives US tech giants a run for their money as Microsoft and others expand AI push in Africa]].
- **US AI-stack export / Tech Corps (2026-03 context).** OpenAI cited as the dominant US
  stack (~90% of Fortune 500 reportedly using its technology) that Washington's [[tech-corps]]
  "Peace Corps for AI" and the [[american-ai-exports-program]] aim to diffuse into partner
  nations against cheaper Chinese alternatives. See [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-02 The U.S. Launches a Peace Corps for AI to Compete With China]].
- **Non-committal on AI Exports Program (Politico, 2026-07).** OpenAI (with AMD) stayed
  **non-committal** on Commerce's [[american-ai-exports-program]], whose first round drew only 78
  applications — several majors unnerved by the administration's imposed-then-reversed order on
  [[anthropic]]'s foreign model access. See [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-11 'Not where they hoped it'd be' Launch of Trump AI promotion program underwhelms]].

- **OpenAI Academy at University of Lagos (Chinasa Okolo, 2026-02-17).** Okolo's
  op-ed dates the **first African-university OpenAI Academy partnership** to
  **Oct 2025** at the University of Lagos, and cites it (with the Gates–OpenAI
  Horizons1000 Rwanda pilot) as evidence that foreign corporate/philanthropic
  actors are already shaping African AI agendas — attributed as the author's
  concern, not a wiki claim. See [[NGA]], [[capacity.training]], [[geopol.usa]].
  Source: [[2026-02-17 Corporate Interests Must Not Co-Opt Africa's AI Moment]].
