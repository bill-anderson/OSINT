# TelOne

**Zimbabwe's state-owned telecommunications provider**, and — on the single source held —
the developer of the AI-based smart traffic system deployed under the
[[safe-city-project-zimbabwe]]. TelOne is distinct from **Powertel** (ZESA's telecom arm,
a Mutapa Investment Fund company, which runs the
[[powertel-paratus-fibre-corridor]]); the two are separate state-linked operators and
should not be conflated.

## Activity in this wiki

- **AI smart traffic system, Harare (as of 2025-07-15).** Zimbabwean authorities launched
  an **AI-based smart traffic system** in Harare, with a plan to extend it to other major
  cities. The system is described — **per Bulawayo 24, relayed by Biometric Update** — as
  **"home-grown, developed by" TelOne**, under a **Safe City Project**: sensors and cameras
  along intersections and major roads addressing congestion, road accidents and
  **traffic-law enforcement**. See [[tech.ai]], [[safe-city-project-zimbabwe]],
  [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-15 Zimbabwe looks forward to digital ID rollout after population registry reform]].

## Tension flagged, not resolved

The **"home-grown, developed by TelOne"** claim sits awkwardly beside held material on
[[ZWE]]: an IDS survey (2026-04-04) lists Zimbabwe among **11 states running
Chinese-supplied ([[hikvision]] / [[zte]] / [[huawei]]) camera and command-centre systems
without adequate legal oversight**. **These may be entirely different systems** — traffic
management is not the same programme as smart-city surveillance, and neither source
addresses the other. **The evidence held is insufficient to call it.** Flagged for the
reconcile pass; **not** recorded as a contradiction.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: thin but arguable. The only basis is the second-hand **"home-grown"**
  descriptor — a claim about domestic technological capability, relayed from state-adjacent
  media and unverified, and in tension with the Chinese-supply picture above. Cited as a
  claim, not as evidence of indigenous capability.
