# National Bank of Rwanda (BNR)

Rwanda's central bank (Banque Nationale du Rwanda / BNR) — the monetary
authority and payments-system regulator overseeing the country's banks,
mobile-money operators and national payment infrastructure, including the
**[[ekash]]** instant payment system operated via **[[rswitch-rwanda]]**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Payments oversight & eKash.** BNR is a named partner behind eKash, Rwanda's
  interoperable instant-payment system (official launch 5 Dec 2025), alongside
  MINICT, [[rwanda-information-society-authority]], [[africanenda]] and
  [[gates-foundation]]. See [[dpi.pay]].
- **eKash interoperability directive (2026-07).** BNR's **Directive No. 45/2026** brought
  eKash to **full interoperability effective 14 Jul 2026** — any-bank/any-wallet transfers and
  merchant payments on one rail, capped at a **RWF 20** fee and **RWF 10m** per transaction —
  the regulator setting the pricing and reach of the national instant-payment utility. See
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 Rwanda's eKash System Achieves Full Interoperability for Digital Payments]].
- **Regulatory sandbox (est. 2022), cited as a sequencing model.** In Anjola
  Adebowale's *Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part II*, BNR's phased,
  sandbox-tested approach — regulation tied to a multi-year national strategy,
  infrastructure built before the rule — is held up as the contrast to Nigeria's
  deadline-first CBN payments circular (see [[cbn-payments-circular-2026]],
  [[rwanda-dpi-strategy]]).
