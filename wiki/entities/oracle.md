# Oracle

US enterprise-software and cloud multinational, pitching **sovereign cloud** offerings to
African governments — a [[geopol.usa]] actor in the public-sector cloud market.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Kenya (2026-07).** Oracle Kenya MD and Public Sector Leader for Africa **David Bunei**
  held exploratory discussions with **Eng. John Tanui**, PS of Kenya's State Department of
  ICT and Digital Economy, on cloud computing, AI and **sovereign cloud infrastructure**
  for secure, citizen-centred government services. Discussions only — no MoU signed. See
  [[KEN]], [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]].
- **iXAfrica to host OCI Nairobi region (2026-01-28).** [[ixafrica-data-centres]] named
  host/colocation partner for the **Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI) region in Nairobi** —
  **Kenya's first public cloud region** ([[ixafrica-oracle-oci-nairobi]]); President Ruto
  first announced the region in 2024, and the facility's power/connectivity are in advanced
  stages (David Bunei, Oracle Kenya country leader). The concrete deployment behind the
  "sovereign cloud" pitch above. See [[KEN]], [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 iXAfrica, Oracle to launch Kenya's first public cloud region]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: "sovereign cloud" as the marketing frame for keeping government workloads
  under national control.
- `colonialism`: that "sovereign" cloud is still a US-vendor platform — the tension
