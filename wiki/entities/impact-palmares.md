# Impact Palmarès Group

**Côte d'Ivoire-based IT firm** (CEO Justin Giresse Tella) modernising Cameroon's **consular
services and secure-document infrastructure** — a *third* foreign identity/document vendor in
the CMR stack alongside Germany's [[augentic]] (national ID) and Luxembourg's
[[liber-technologies]] (CRVS), and the first from another African state. The pattern is a
recurring theme of the CMR identity build: advanced but foreign-run.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Consular / biometric-visa contract (from Apr 2022).** A **10-year renewable contract** with
  the [[ministry-of-external-relations-cameroon]] to modernise consular services; Cameroon
  launched its biometric visa system in 2023 under it. As of 30 Sep 2025 the government had
  processed **411,439 paid visas for FCFA 46.8bn (~US$82.9m)** (PM Dion Ngute, National Assembly,
  26 Nov 2025). An amended agreement (signed late 2025) commits the firm to fully operational
  2nd/3rd-generation digital consular services by 2026 and to deliver the Ministry's Digital
  Transformation Centre (CTDM) by June 2026. Users have reported platform downtime. Source:
  [[2025-12-10-impact-palmares-digital-visa-deal-printing-contract-biometricupdate]].
- **National Printing Press upgrade (Dec 2025).** A separate deal (overseen by Communication
  Minister René Emmanuel Sadi) hands Impact Palmarès the modernisation of Cameroon's **National
  Printing Press** — a facility dating to 1906, restructured by presidential decree in 2023 — to
  strengthen secure-document printing and combat document fraud. This extends the wiki's
  "foreign-vendor-built identity" thesis to a new document class and to the physical printing of
  state documents. Sources:
  [[2025-12-10-impact-palmares-digital-visa-deal-printing-contract-biometricupdate]],
  [[2025-12-08-leconomie-diplomatic-documents-impact-palmares]].
