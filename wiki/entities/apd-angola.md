# APD (Agência de Proteção de Dados de Angola)

Angola's **data-protection authority** — notable as one of the relatively **active** African DPAs: constituted in **2016** and now supervising and **sanctioning** controllers, in contrast to the many African states where the authority exists only on paper.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Enforcement wave (2024).** In **June–July 2024** the APD published sanctions against several companies operating in Angola for serious violations of the Data Protection Law (**[[angola-data-protection-law-22-11]]**, Law 22/11). Cited as evidence the authority "has teeth." See [[angola--gov-protect]]. Source: [[2024-08-10 Angola - Dados Pessoais e Ciber-seguranca (APD enforcement)]].
- Supervises international data transfers (approval for non-adequate destinations) under Law 22/11.
- **Steering the Law 22/11 revision (2026-06).** Executive administrator **Amaro Figueiredo** announced (17 Jun 2026, closing a UNESCO/Smart Africa PALOP Lusophone data-governance workshop) that the revised data-protection law has **cleared public consultation** and heads to the competent state organs — expected at the **Council of Ministers then the National Assembly in the next legislative year**. The revision responds to AI, the digital economy and data governance: it introduces a **data-protection officer (DPO)** role for high-volume processors, strengthens oversight and sanction mechanisms, and **re-denominates fines in Kwanza**. See [[angola--gov-protect]], [[angola-data-protection-law-22-11]]. Source: [[2026-06-17-nova-lei-de-proteccao-de-dados-proximo-ano-legislativo]].
