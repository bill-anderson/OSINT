# Digital & data-driven transformations in governance: a landscape review (Data & Policy, 2025)

A named-authorship scholarly **landscape review** by **Sarah Giest, Keegan McBride,
Anastasija Nikiforova and Sujit Kumar Sikder**, published in *Data & Policy* (Cambridge
University Press, Vol. 7 ≈ 2025) — the flagship "Focus Area 1" review for the
**[[data-for-policy]]** community. It conceptualises the field of digital and data-driven
transformations in governance and proposes a research agenda around three transformations:
public participation / collective intelligence; relationships and organisations; and open
data and government.

## Nature and scope

- A **scoping review** — no new data analysed; received no specific grant funding.
- Defines the field and maps trends, connecting to AI, smart cities, the digital divide,
  data governance and cross-border data exchange.
- **Global/theoretical**, with no Africa-specific content.

## How to use / cite

Capture as **reference-shelf literature**: cite for its conceptual map of the field. Per
the reference-study discipline, do **not** push its abstractions into place or concept
pages. Author persons are deferred and promoted only if separately cited.
