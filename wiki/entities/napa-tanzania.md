# National Physical Addressing (NaPA), Tanzania

Tanzania's **national residential and physical addressing system** — a digital registry assigning
house numbers and street names, run by the ICT ministry (**[[micit-tanzania]]**). Registration
**began nationwide in 2010** (as of 2026-02), and the system is deployed across residences,
hospitals, shops, schools, banks and other government and private institutions. It is the
quieter third registry alongside civil registration (**[[rita-tanzania]]**) and foundational ID
(**[[nida-tanzania]]**) — the one that locates people rather than naming them.

## Two different metrics — not one trajectory

The two accounts held here report **different quantities**, and **neither is derivable from the
other**:

- **85% is a population-coverage rate** — the share of Tanzanians reached (2024-07).
- **12.9 million is an absolute address count** — houses and plots recorded (2026-02).

They measure different objects (people vs addresses) and **cannot be charted together** as a
trend. Both are recorded below, dated; no trajectory is inferred between them.

## Activity in this wiki

- **85% population coverage claimed (2024-07-10).** NaPA assistant coordinator **Arnold Mkude**,
  interviewed at the **48th Dar es Salaam International Trade Fair**, said that since the 2022
  Population and Housing Census report, NaPA **"has reached 85 percent of Tanzanians
  nationwide"**, with the fiscal budget dedicated to a special operation for **complete
  registration by the next fiscal year** — though he conceded it "will be an ongoing effort as
  new houses and plots are continuously developed." See [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.localgov]].
  Source: [[2024-07-10 Tanzania ramps up national physical address registration]].
- **Registration is not adoption (2024-07-10).** Mkude flagged the friction plainly: despite that
  coverage, **many people have not collected their placards and are unaware of their house
  numbers and street names**. A registry can be populated without its subjects knowing their
  entry — the same collect-your-document gap that dogs the national ID (cf.
  **[[james-kaji]]**'s appeal to **300,000+** citizens to collect processed ID cards, 2026-01).
  See [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2024-07-10 Tanzania ramps up national physical address registration]].
- **12.9 million addresses recorded; 29 systems interconnected (2026-02-09).** Deputy Permanent
  Secretary **Nicholas Mkapa** told the Annual Residential Address Conference and Postal Services
  Technology Exhibition: **"Tanzania began its nationwide residential address registration in
  2010. To date, over 12.9 million addresses have been successfully recorded and integrated into
  our digital systems."** Since the programme began, **29 systems have been interconnected**,
  including the **[[tanzania-posts-corporation]]**. Mkapa framed addresses as development tools
  linking communities to national systems, supporting healthcare delivery, school enrolment,
  business licensing and taxation. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]].
  Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Tanzania registers 12 million housing addresses to boost digital governance]].
  *(That article's **headline says "12 million"; its body and the quoted official say 12.9
  million** — the body figure is used here.)*
- **Certified against UPU S42; named a continental benchmark (2026-02-09).** **[[papu]]**
  Secretary General **Dr Sifundo Chief Moyo** said **"Tanzania's achievements in addressing,
  including its Universal Postal Union (UPU) S42 certification, set a benchmark for the
  continent,"** arguing that without harmonised addressing **e-commerce cannot scale** and
  **AfCFTA cannot reach its full potential**. **[[tcra]]** DG **Dr Jabiri Bakari** added that the
  system has strengthened postal services into a channel for financial, social and government
  services, **particularly benefiting rural communities**. See [[upu-s42]], [[upu]],
  [[gov.standards]], [[digital.rural]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Tanzania registers 12 million housing addresses to boost digital governance]].
