# Benin digital strategy stack (Vision 2060 / Stratégie 2031)

Benin's layered digital-policy framework: the long-horizon **Vision Bénin 2060 (ALAFIA)** with a digital
pillar launched 3 Feb 2026, and a medium-term **Stratégie numérique 2031** (Deloitte-facilitated,
successor to the 2021–2026 plan) — carried forward, with an AI-first re-scoping, by the new
**Wadagni government** (2026). The framing is consistently **digital sovereignty**:
producing and mastering technology, not just consuming it. See [[BEN]], [[gov.policy]].

## What we know (as of 2026-07)

- **The base it builds on.** Reforms since 2016 delivered **>3,000–3,500 km of fibre** (targeting all 77
  communes), ~90% territory 4G, the **RNPP** national register, a modern **Code du numérique**, and
  **>250 digital public services** — putting Benin among Africa's **top performers in the 2025 GovTech
  Maturity Index** (with Algeria, South Africa, Nigeria). Sources:
  [[2026-02-09-wearetech-benin-2031-digital-strategy-roadmap]],
  [[2026-03-24-wadagni-talata-hub-technologique]].
- **Stratégie 2031 priorities.** Universal internet coverage, e-service expansion, **AI in high-potential
  sectors**, aligning local skills to labour-market needs, and financing mechanisms — a drafting process
  opened at a Cotonou workshop (5–6 Feb 2026). Source:
  [[2026-02-09-wearetech-benin-2031-digital-strategy-roadmap]].
- **The new government's manifesto (Wadagni–Talata, 2026).** [[tech.ai]] Four levers (infrastructure,
  training, innovation, inclusion) with concrete asks: **secure data centres and national
  supercomputers**, an **AI-based government "Super App,"** a **national data-localisation law** (data
  sovereignty), an industrial-innovation framework law with SEZ-linked IP/tax regimes, Sèmè City hubs,
  and community "digital relays." Source: [[2026-03-24-wadagni-talata-hub-technologique]].
