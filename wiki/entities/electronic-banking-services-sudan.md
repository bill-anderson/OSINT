# Electronic Banking Services (EBS), Sudan

**The [[central-bank-of-sudan|CBoS]]'s operational arm for electronic payments — entrusted with the technical operation of Sudan's national payment switch as a "switch of switches" above multiple licensed bank- and fintech-operated switches.** EBS is the hub in what the wiki reads as a deliberately **multi-provider architecture under sovereign control**, not a single-provider monopoly.

## Position in the architecture

- **Ownership** (carried from [[SDN]]): **CBoS 49%**, **[[sudatel]] 30%**, commercial banks **21%**. *Provenance gap, flagged 2026-07-20: this split and the "switch of switches" operating role are held on the SDN place hub without a source citation attached, and no held source on file states them. Treat as uncited until a primary is acquired — do not build on the percentages.*
- **The design.** A **sovereign hub with deliberately competing private spokes**: EBS operating above multiple licensed switches, with CBoS having licensed at least four operators (**Bright Technologies, Al-Asjad, Nahda for Technology, SudaPost**) by mid-2026.
- **Why this matters.** It **qualifies the "single-provider concentration" reading** of Sudanese payments. That reading holds as a description of *wartime practice* — Bankak carried the traffic because little else worked (2025) — but **not** as a description of *intended architecture*. Anything elsewhere in the wiki resting on the concentration thesis should be re-read against this.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The NIPS tender (2026-04-20).** CBoS opened a public tender for a national instant-payment system: announcement period **20 Apr – 4 May 2026**, bids to **14 May 2026**; scope reported as a **software solution only**, with CBoS supplying hardware to the winner's specification — *though that detail sits in a separate specifications document linked from the tender notice and **not held**; the stored notice does not carry it (checked 2026-07-20)*. Source: [[2026-04-20-cbos-nips-national-instant-payment-system-tender]].
- **The switch contracted but not launched (as of 2026-07-08).** A new national payment switch had been **contracted but not launched**, with EBS entrusted by CBoS with its technical operation; its CEO framed the design as sovereign hub plus competing private spokes, **keeping data sovereignty inside Sudan**.
- **Entry policed, not closed (2026-07-04).** CBoS **revoked Al-Asjad Smart Digital Solutions' payment-switch authorisation** — evidence of the regulator policing entry to the switch market rather than shutting it. Source: [[2026-07-04-cbos-revokes-al-asjad-payment-switch-licence]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the stated design is unusually explicit about *why* the hub is domestic — data sovereignty, named as such by the operator's own CEO. It is a rare case of an African payments architecture where multi-provider competition and sovereign control are presented as complements rather than trade-offs. Whether the build matches the design is not yet establishable.
