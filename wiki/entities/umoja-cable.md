# Umoja subsea cable

**Umoja** (Swahili for "unity") is a [[google]] terrestrial-plus-subsea cable
system — the **first route to directly connect Africa to Australia**. Announced in
May 2024 and slated **ready-for-service in 2027**, it begins as an overland fibre
route starting in **Kenya**, crossing **Uganda → Rwanda → DR Congo → Zambia →
Zimbabwe → South Africa**, before traversing the Indian Ocean floor to land in
**Australia**.

## The terrestrial innovation

Umoja's distinguishing feature is that its overland segment turns major inland
African cities into cable stations rather than leaving them as "hard-to-reach
endpoints remote from the coastal landing sites." The route was built in
partnership with **[[liquid-c2]]** (Liquid Intelligent Technologies, a
[[cassava-technologies]] subsidiary with 110,000+ km of African fibre across
~21 countries). [[strive-masiyiwa]], LIT chairman/founder, framed Nairobi,
Kampala, Kigali, Lubumbashi, Lusaka and Harare as now being "stations on a data
superhighway" (quoted in a Google Cloud blog by VP Brian Quigley, May 2024).

## Google Africa investment context

Umoja is part of Google's **Africa Connect** infrastructure programme (alongside
[[equiano-cable]] and the Johannesburg Cloud region). It sits within Google's
**2021 pledge to invest $1 billion in Africa over five years**; by May 2024 the
company reported >$900 million spent, and by September 2025 said it had exceeded
the $1bn commitment. In September 2025 Google announced **four new subsea-cable
connectivity hubs** (north/south/east/west Africa) with landing stations and data
centres to be completed within ~three years — with Kenya and South Africa likely
hosting two of the four, anchored by Equiano and Umoja. Alongside the May 2024
cable announcement, Google signed a **Statement of Collaboration with Kenya's
Ministry of Information, Communications and the Digital Economy** on
cybersecurity, data-driven innovation, upskilling and AI, including Kenya's
Immigration department evaluating Google Cloud's CyberShield/Mandiant tools for
its eCitizen platform.
