# Subsidies Compensation Platform (Tunisia)

**A Tunisian government platform designed for direct cash transfers in place of subsidies — and one
of three digital programmes that [[access-now]] alleges were developed with minimal transparency and
public consultation.** It enters this wiki almost entirely through that critique; no source held
describes its operator, launch date, scale or architecture.

**Distinct from the [[amen-program-tunisia]].** Both concern targeted transfers to Tunisian
households, and they are plainly adjacent — but **no source held establishes them as the same
platform**. The allegations below attach to the Subsidies Compensation Platform as named. The
relationship between the two is **unestablished**; they are kept as separate entities deliberately.

**Everything here is attributed allegation, not established fact.** The claims are Access Now's
position, reaching this wiki via a secondary outlet's summary of an Access Now report rather than
from the report itself.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Named in the Access Now critique (reported 2025-03).** Biometric Update reports that, per an
  [[access-now]] report, **three major programmes — the biometric ID project ([[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]]),
  Mobile ID [[e-houwiya]], and the Subsidies Compensation Platform** — were developed with minimal
  transparency and public consultation, and carry risks of **mass surveillance, identity theft and
  data exploitation**. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
- **The specific allegation (reported 2025-03).** The subsidy compensation platform, **designed for
  direct cash transfers**, **allegedly violated data privacy laws**, as authorities **failed to
  consult Tunisia's data protection agency (INPDP) for impact assessments**. Read against
  [[organic-law-2004-63-tunisia]] — the 2004 statute whose adequacy is the underlying objection.
  Allegation as relayed; no government response and no primary document is held. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].

## Related (mentioned, not hubbed)

INPDP (Instance Nationale de Protection des Données Personnelles — Tunisia's data protection
agency, allegedly not consulted); the Access Now report itself (not held).
