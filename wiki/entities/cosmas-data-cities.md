# Cosmas Data Cities

**The data-centre unit launched by South African real-estate group [[cavaleros]] on 11 November
2025**, formed to develop two hyperscale campuses in Johannesburg and Cape Town. It is the newcomer
in the South African market whose announced megawatts are large enough to reshape national demand —
and whose specifications remain almost entirely undisclosed.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The two campuses, as announced (2025-11-11).** **Johannesburg**: a Cosmas Data City in the
  **Samrand** area (Kosmosdal), potentially **200 MW**, served by a **450 MW self-built switching
  station** — the switching station being roughly double the campus's own stated load. **Cape
  Town**: a **100-acre** development potentially totalling **360 MW**. **No location, campus
  specification or development timeline detail was shared.** Source:
  [[2025-11-11 South African real estate firm Cavaleros Group launches data center unit, plans two campuses]].
- **Sized against the national grid (2026-02-09).** Daily Maverick puts the Cavaleros plans at a
  **360 MW Cape Town campus on a 100-hectare site** and a **200 MW Johannesburg campus on a 40-hectare
  site** — the two projects, with Teraco's, driving SA capacity from **~400 MW to at least 1,200 MW**,
  an increment equal to **just over one stage of Eskom load shedding**. See **[[eskom]]**. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Major SA data centre expansion plans emerge, set to more than double current power demand]].
- **Believed linked to Microsoft — unconfirmed (2026-02-09).** The Cosmas Data City project is
  "believed to be linked mainly to **[[microsoft]]**," which announced a further **R5.4-billion**
  investment (atop **R20.4-billion over three years**) for SA's first "enterprise-grade" data centres
  in Johannesburg and Cape Town. **Neither Cavaleros nor Microsoft would confirm or deny** the
  relationship. Held as reported belief, not fact. Source: same.
- **Colocation vs captive — the structural distinction.** Where **[[teraco]]** is a colocation
  operator renting space to ~670 customers (2026-02), Cosmas Data City is understood to be built
  around a **single anchor hyperscaler** — a different model with different sovereignty implications
  for who ultimately controls the capacity. Source: same.
- **The disclosure position.** Power procurement for the campuses is, per Cavaleros, "commercially
  sensitive and therefore cannot be publicly disclosed," under strict NDAs — the position Daily
  Maverick contrasts unfavourably with Teraco's transparency. See [[cavaleros]]. Source: same.
- **Advisers.** **[[knight-frank]]** (industry insight) and **Future-tech** (engineering). Future-tech's
  Will Clarke: Cavaleros' self-built switching station "significantly enhances Johannesburg's
  infrastructure readiness." *Adviser framing — attribute it.* Source:
  [[2025-11-11 South African real estate firm Cavaleros Group launches data center unit, plans two campuses]].
