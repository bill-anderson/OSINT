# ENBIC (ECOWAS National Biometric Identity Card)

The **ECOWAS National Biometric Identity Card** — a regional standard for a biometric national ID that doubles as a **cross-border travel and trade credential** within the ECOWAS free-movement zone ([[dpi.id]], [[gov.regional]]). It is the mutual-recognition target that the World Bank **[[wuri-programme|WURI]]** programme's "fID to ENBIC" roadmap works toward. See [[XWA]], [[ecowas]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Guinea-Bissau among six operationalized (2025).** [[GNB]] Guinea-Bissau is cited as **one of six ECOWAS nations to have fully operationalized the ENBIC** biometric ID card for cross-border movement and trade. See [[gov.standards]]. Source: [[2025-06-10 Guinea-Bissau deploys efforts to digitize birth registration, national ID systems]].
- **São Domingos field deployment — women traders (2026-03-16).** [[GNB]] **262 women cross-border
  traders** received ENBIC cards in São Domingos (Cacheu region) under a GNB–Senegal women-traders
  mobility project run by the National Borders Commission, backed by [[ecowas]] and GIZ-financed — a
  pilot-phase field deployment of the operationalised card, attended by the governors of Cacheu and
  Ziguinchor (Senegal). See [[guinea-bissau--dpi-id]], [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-03-16 Women traders receive ECOWAS biometric ID in Sao Domingos, Guinea-Bissau]].
- **Nigeria rollout (2025-11-28).** [[NGA]] Nigeria's Federal Government rolled out ENBIC at the Transcorp Hilton, Abuja ("Enhancing Regional Integration and Security"); Interior Minister **Olubunmi Tunji-Ojo** framed it as advancing President **[[bola-tinubu]]**'s border-security agenda and the transition from the paper ECOWAS certificate to a biometric card — linking Nigeria's foundational ID ([[nimc]]/NIN) to regional free-movement policy. See [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source: [[2025-11-28 Nigeria Unveils ECOWAS ID Card To Boost Regional Travel, Security]].
- **Senegal (2025).** [[SEN]] Senegal separately targets ECOWAS biometric ID holders for voter registration ([[senegal--dpi-id]]).
