# Ministry of Transport (Chad)

**Chad's transport ministry** — holder of the country's vehicle, driver and transport-licensing
registries, and the sponsor of a planned **integrated digital platform** to consolidate them.
Minister: **Fatima Goukouni Weddeye**. The ministry is a sectoral test case for [[TCD]]'s wider
e-government agenda: a single department's paper-based licensing estate being pulled onto one
platform.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Integrated transport-services platform (announced 2025-09-09).** A meeting chaired by Minister
  Weddeye, focused on **digitizing financial services** within her department, discussed
  centralising all ministry services onto a **single platform**. In scope: **vehicle registration
  cards, driver's licences, technical inspection certificates, transport licences and permits, and
  the mandatory consignment note** (*lettre de voiture obligatoire*, LVO). Stated goals — make
  services more accessible, ensure **traceability**, and cut processing times and costs for users
  and businesses. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-12 Chad to deploy integrated digital platform for transport services]].
- **Situated in the national push.** We Are Tech Africa frames the plan as aligned with a broader
  national effort to modernise public administration and digitise government services; Chad has
  already **partially digitised administrative procedures** and created **online platforms to
  track official documents**. Anticipated effects — simpler processes, lower costs, reduced fraud
  and document loss, better transparency and competitiveness — are the outlet's and the
  government's expectations, not reported outcomes. See [[gov.policy]], [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2025-09-12 Chad to deploy integrated digital platform for transport services]].
- **The benchmark cited.** The same source repeats Chad's standing at **189th of 193** on the UN
  E-Government Development Index, score **0.1785** of 1 (**2024** EGDI). Source:
  [[2025-09-12 Chad to deploy integrated digital platform for transport services]].

## Reading it

As of the single source held (2025-09-12) this is a **plan discussed at a meeting**, not a
procurement, a contract or a deployment: no vendor, budget, timeline or delivery milestone is
reported. Nor does any source held connect the platform to the [[chad-biometric-registry]] — the
obvious identity layer for licensing, and one ANATS is explicitly opening to other ministries.
