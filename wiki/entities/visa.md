# Visa

Global US payments network extending into African cross-border rails through partnerships with
mobile-money operators. The **Visa Pay DRC launch is independently corroborated** (Connecting
Africa, 2026-01-26). The July 2026 DRC **stablecoin-settlement** story is now anchored to an on-record
Visa executive (The EastAfrican, 2 Jul 2026) — its **core mechanism is corroborated**, but the "joint
USD-stablecoin *pilot* launch" framing is downgraded (no joint issuer announcement; USD peg inferred);
see the deal page [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]] (REV-006 resolved 2026-07-16).

## Activity in this wiki

- **DRC stablecoin settlement (early July 2026).** On record, Visa SVP CEMEA **Godfrey Sullivan**
  described a **live DRC use case** settling [[safaricom]] M-Pesa top-ups in **stablecoins in the
  background** via **Visa Pay**, with [[onafriq]]. Core mechanism corroborated by the exec quote; the
  "joint USD-stablecoin *pilot* launch" framing is trade-press gloss (Ecofin: no jointly-announced
  pilot; USD peg inferred; one partnership awaiting regulatory approval). See the deal page
  [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]]. Tags [[dpi.pay]], [[geopol.usa]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-02 Visa tests stablecoins in DRC for cross-border payments (The EastAfrican)]],
  [[2026-07-02 DR Congo becomes Africa's live test for dollar-settled mobile money (Ecofin)]].

- **Visa Pay in DRC (2025-09).** The settlement use case builds on Visa Pay, launched in DRC in
  September 2025 via an [[onafriq]] API — independently corroborated by
  [[2026-01-26 How Onafriq is growing digital finance amid uncertainty in DRC]].

- **Visa Pay DRC — corroborated (2026-01-26).** A higher-authority account (Connecting Africa)
  independently confirms Visa Pay's DRC launch in **Q4 2025**, which [[onafriq]] supported to bring
  "interoperability between card and mobile-money networks at scale"; Onafriq connects 1bn wallets
  across 43 countries, and DRC had ~29m mobile-money accounts (34.6% penetration, Q4 2024). Corroborates
  the Visa Pay leg; the DRC stablecoin-settlement mechanism is now separately corroborated by an on-record
  Visa exec (only the "joint pilot" framing is downgraded — REV-006 resolved 2026-07-16).
  See [[COD]], [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-26 How Onafriq is growing digital finance amid uncertainty in DRC]].

- **Card economics under pressure (analysis, 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki's "payments end-game"
  thesis uses card interchange as the cautionary baseline: in Brazil, Pix drove effective payment
  costs from a **2.34% card take-rate to ~0.33%**, the pattern he expects African central-bank
  instant rails to repeat — squeezing card networks toward the credit/data/compliance layers.
  Analyst framing, attributed to Kariuki. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]].

- **Djibouti "Smart Wallet" MoU (2025-09-16).** Visa signed an MoU with the government of
  Djibouti to co-develop a national digital wallet ("Smart Wallet") spanning payments, government
  services and digital identity, with **[[tech5]]** providing the biometric layer under a
  seven-year Visa–Tech5 collaboration. See the initiative page [[smart-wallet-djibouti]]. Tags
  [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.id]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-19 Djibouti-Visa Smart Wallet national digital wallet MoU]].

- **Gabon financial-inclusion partnership (2025-05, reported).** Gabon concluded a partnership with
  Visa at the **Africa CEO Forum (Côte d'Ivoire, May 2025)** to accelerate financial inclusion —
  reported to cover modernising tax collection, automating disbursements and a secure digital-identity
  system. Reported second-hand in Dec-2025 coverage, **not via a primary Visa release**; the digital-ID
  element in particular is uncorroborated. Visa also sits among private partners approached for Gabon's
  2026 infrastructure build. See the deal page [[gabon-visa-partnership-deal]], [[GAB]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[geopol.usa]]. Sources: [[2025-12-12-gabon-uncdf-financial-inclusion-deal]],
  [[2025-12-11-gabon-madigipaie-digital-payments-launch]].
