# Visa

Global US payments network extending into African cross-border rails through partnerships with
mobile-money operators. The **Visa Pay DRC launch is independently corroborated** (Connecting
Africa, 2026-01-26), so this entity now sits at `active`. One claim remains uncorroborated — the
July 2026 **USD stablecoin pilot**, sourced only to a low-authority aggregator; that caveat is held
on the deal page [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]] pending a primary Visa/Safaricom release.

## Activity in this wiki

- **DRC stablecoin pilot (early July 2026).** With [[safaricom]]'s M-Pesa and [[onafriq]],
  Visa launched a USD-pegged stablecoin cross-border pilot in the DRC ([[COD]]), settling
  mobile transactions instantly on blockchain and bypassing correspondent banking. See the
  deal page [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]]. Tags [[dpi.pay]], [[geopol.usa]].
  Source: [[2026-07-10 Visa and M-Pesa Launch Blockchain Stablecoin Pilot in DR Congo]].

- **Visa Pay in DRC (2025-09).** The pilot builds on Visa Pay, launched in DRC in September
  2025 via an [[onafriq]] API. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Visa and M-Pesa Launch Blockchain Stablecoin Pilot in DR Congo]].

- **Visa Pay DRC — corroborated (2026-01-26).** A higher-authority account (Connecting Africa)
  independently confirms Visa Pay's DRC launch in **Q4 2025**, which [[onafriq]] supported to bring
  "interoperability between card and mobile-money networks at scale"; Onafriq connects 1bn wallets
  across 43 countries, and DRC had ~29m mobile-money accounts (34.6% penetration, Q4 2024). Partly
  eases the `needs-review` flag on the Visa Pay leg (the stablecoin pilot remains uncorroborated).
  See [[COD]], [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-26 How Onafriq is growing digital finance amid uncertainty in DRC]].

- **Card economics under pressure (analysis, 2026-02).** Samora Kariuki's "payments end-game"
  thesis uses card interchange as the cautionary baseline: in Brazil, Pix drove effective payment
  costs from a **2.34% card take-rate to ~0.33%**, the pattern he expects African central-bank
  instant rails to repeat — squeezing card networks toward the credit/data/compliance layers.
  Analyst framing, attributed to Kariuki. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]].
