# SNIA (Cameroon National AI Strategy)

Cameroon's **National Artificial Intelligence Strategy**, a **2040 roadmap** framed around
**digital sovereignty, inclusiveness and sustainability** — unveiled at the second edition of the
**National Consultations on AI ([[conia]])** on 7–8 Jul 2025 by Posts & Telecoms Minister
Minette Libom Li Likeng ([[ministry-of-posts-and-telecommunications-cameroon]]).

## Origin and authorship

- **Origin: CONIA of 25–26 June 2024**, not 2025 — the national consultations where Cameroon first
  resolved to build an AI strategy. *(This re-dates the wiki's earlier single SNIA line.)*
- **Author: the Cabinet Danielle Moukouri ([[cabinet-danielle-moukouri]])**, a digital-law firm that
  **won the mandate on public tender** (reported Mar 2025) — to formulate the vision, a financing and
  implementation plan, and the government AI roadmap. **[[st-digital]]** is a sovereign-cloud/
  infrastructure partner, *not* the strategy's author — a role the wiki previously conflated. Sources:
  [[2025-03-11-leconomie-cabinet-danielle-moukouri-national-ai-strategy]],
  [[2025-01-22-unveils-plans-national-ai-strategy-afd-index-wearetech]].

## The seven pillars (2040 roadmap)

1. **Governance & digital sovereignty** — a dedicated **AI Authority**, a **Presidential Council on AI**, and a national AI legal framework.
2. **Data & infrastructure** — a government **Data Lake**, mass digitisation of public services, national interoperability standards, an **Open Data policy**.
3. **Multilingual & inclusive AI** — a sovereign LLM, **"GPT Cameroon,"** for local/national languages, with voice-data collection and linguistic research.
4. **Sovereign technological infrastructure** — **15 edge-computing nodes powered by solar microgrids** across all regions.
5. **Human capital & research** — **4,000 trained per year**, **five national AI centres of excellence**, a diaspora-talent programme, an **AI focal point per ministry**.
6. **Innovation & sectoral use cases** — AI in health, agriculture, education, justice; startup support; a "Made in Cameroon" export label.
7. **Regional & international cooperation** — a **Central Africa AI network**.

Government estimates: **12,000 direct jobs** and up to **+1.2% of GDP by 2040**; targets **60,000 AI
specialists by 2040 (40% women)**. Sources:
[[2025-07-08-snia-2040-ai-roadmap-seven-pillars-techafricanews]],
[[2025-07-10-snia-60000-ai-experts-by-2040-wearetech]],
[[2025-07-08-cameroon-discusses-national-ai-strategy-consultations-itweb]].

## Benchmarks and trajectory

- **AFD AI Investment Potential Index: 30/100 (Jan 2025)** — level with Angola, Guinea, Ethiopia, Burkina Faso. Source: [[2025-01-22-unveils-plans-national-ai-strategy-afd-index-wearetech]].
- **ITU AI readiness: 46.3/100, 30th of 42 African states (2025)** — below the 56 continental average. Source: [[2025-07-10-snia-60000-ai-experts-by-2040-wearetech]].
- **Newest state (Feb 2026): a public-administration AI-governance workshop with the ITU** (Cosmas Zavazava present), theme "responsible AI / building digital trust," feeding recommendations into SNIA implementation. Source: [[2026-02-18-lejour-ai-governance-workshop-public-administrations]].
