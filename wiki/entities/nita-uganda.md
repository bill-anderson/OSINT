# National Information Technology Authority — Uganda (NITA-U)

**Uganda's central IT authority** — overseeing ICT policy implementation, digital
infrastructure development and the standardisation of government technology frameworks.
It owns the country's interoperability stack: the [[ughub]] data-exchange platform, the
[[ugpass]] authentication and e-signature platform, the Government Enterprise
Architecture (GEA) and the [[uganda-e-gif]] interoperability framework.

## Activity in this wiki

- **GEA / e-GIF renewal workshop (undated clip, `date_source: proxy`).** NITA-U concluded
  a multi-stakeholder workshop on renewing Uganda's **Government Enterprise Architecture**
  and **[[uganda-e-gif]]**, held in collaboration with the **[[e-governance-academy]]**,
  aligning technology systems, data-management practices and institutional processes
  across ministries, departments, agencies and local governments. **Brenda Maniraguha**,
  NITA-U Project Manager for GEA and the interoperability framework: *"The blueprint
  ensures government systems are designed to collaborate together and not grow in
  isolation."* *The clip's date could not be established and defaults to the ingest date —
  it is not a publication date (GAP-UGA-004).* See [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.exchange]],
  [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17 NITA-Uganda concludes workshop on renewal of government enterprise architecture and interoperability framework]].
- **UGHub, researched by DIAL (2024-06-28).** [[dial]]'s case study makes Uganda's
  **[[ughub]]** the worked example of an integrated national data exchange: **utilized by
  over 100 government MDAs** plus banks, insurers and fintechs (as of 2024-06), cutting
  wait times **from days to hours**, particularly for **passport applications, work permits
  and vehicle registrations** — against continuing challenges of **financial
  sustainability, slow uptake and connectivity gaps**. See [[dpi.exchange]],
  [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2024-06-28 Integrated National Data Exchange Systems - Uganda Case Study]].
- **Joint governance with NIRA (undated deck, `date_source: proxy`).** UGHub is positioned
  as a digital public good with [[nira-uganda]] among its largest data providers — NIRA's
  services used by **90 of 135 entities** on the platform. Governance runs through a
  Cabinet Technical Sub-Committee Working Group headed by the Ministry of ICT, with the
  **NITA-U Executive Director sitting on the NIRA Board**. *Undated — do not treat these
  figures as current-dated.* Source:
  [[2026-07-17 A Collaborative Approach to Digital Identity and Digital Transformation (NIRA and NITA-U)]].
- **Instrument custodian.** NITA-U (with MoICT&NG) issued the **[[uganda-e-gif]]**
  (**6 May 2021**) and the **[[uganda-digital-government-strategy]] v1.1**
  (**26 February 2022**), the latter a formal addendum to the GEA/e-GIF engagement.
  Sources: [[2021-05-06 Uganda e-Government Interoperability Framework (e-GIF)]],
  [[2022-02-26 Uganda Digital Government Strategy v1.1]].
