# U.S. Department of State

Lead US foreign-affairs agency; driving the "trade-and-investment, not aid"
posture toward Africa and the diplomatic scaffolding (working groups, Pax Silica,
commercial-diplomacy strategy) around US AI/infrastructure exports.

## Activity in this wiki

- **US–AUC Strategic Investment Working Group (2026-01-28).** State and the AU
  Commission launched the **[[us-auc-strategic-investment-working-group]]** after
  Deputy Secretary Landau met AUC Chair Youssouf in Addis Ababa. See [[XAF]],
  [[african-union]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Joint Statement of the United States and African Union on the Launch of a Strategic Investment Working Group]].
- **Chad bilateral track (2026).** State (with Commerce) hosted a Chadian mission
  and reaffirmed support for Chad's digital transformation (infrastructure,
  satellite connectivity, cybersecurity, digital health). See [[TCD]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-01 Chad and US Explore Partnerships on Satellite Connectivity and Cybersecurity]].
- **Pax Silica / commercial diplomacy.** [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]] situates
  State's **[[pax-silica]]** initiative and TPD instrument at the centre of US
  AI diplomacy. See [[technology-prosperity-deal]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **Critical-minerals diplomacy (IISS, 2026-01).** An IISS analysis places State at the head of
  the **[[pax-silica]]** coalition (launched Dec 2025, led by Under Secretary **Jacob Helberg**),
  the third track of a state-capitalist minerals strategy after EO 14241 (Mar 2025) and the
  OBBBA's $7.5bn appropriation (Jul 2025). Africa relevance is a **DRC** ([[COD]]) bilateral
  minerals track tied to security guarantees. See [[geopol.china]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 US critical-minerals diplomacy from America-First deals to Pax Silica]].

- **Kenya data-governance track (2026-07-09, Nairobi).** US Embassy officials met Kenya's
  PS for ICT & Digital Economy **John Tanui**, ICT Security Directorate Secretary Emmanuel
  Kata and Data Protection Commissioner Immaculate Kassait on Kenya's **Draft Data
  Governance Policy (2026)** — strengthening data governance, **secure cross-border data
  flows**, and bilateral digital cooperation. Discussions only (no MoU). See [[KEN]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Kenya and U.S. Discuss Draft Data Governance Policy to Strengthen Digital Economy]].

- **Rubio "data sovereignty" cable (2026-02-18).** An internal State Department cable
  signed by Secretary **Marco Rubio**, classified as an "action request," directs US
  diplomats worldwide to monitor and counter data-sovereignty and data-localization
  laws — naming **GDPR** as an offending example and promoting the US-led Global
  Cross-Border Privacy Rules Forum as the "trusted" alternative. It sits within a wider
  offensive (Section 301 vs Brazil, anti-EU-DSA lobbying, a WTO e-transmission duty
  moratorium opposed by South Africa, India and Indonesia). Key backdrop to Africa's
  data-sovereignty debate. See [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12 How Trump's Data Regulation Crackdown Undermines Digital Sovereignty]].
- **Bilateral health-MoU data terms (2026-02).** State's Bureau of Global Health Security
  and Diplomacy negotiated the [[america-first-global-health-strategy]] bilateral health
  deals; a State spokesperson defended them as advancing "American national interests"
  even as **Zambia** ([[ZMB]]) balked at a ~$1bn MoU tying aid to 10-year health-data
  access and reported mining concessions, and **Zimbabwe** rejected its own. See
  [[zambia-us-health-mou]], [[zimbabwe-us-health-mou]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]].

See [[XAF]], [[us-department-of-commerce]], [[geopol.usa]].
