# South Sudan National Revenue Authority (NRA)

South Sudan's revenue-collection agency, driving the digitisation of government
fee collection to curb leakage and informal practices.

**Name variance — record, don't resolve.** The agency appears in sources as both
**South Sudan Revenue Authority (SSRA)** and **National Revenue Authority (NRA)**,
both abbreviations in current use as of 2026-07-20. This wiki files it under one
slug; no source on file establishes which form is the statutory name.

**Leadership (dated).** Commissioner-General **Simon Akuei Deng** (Oct 2025,
signing the Ensoft/N-Soft agreement); **William Anyuon Kuol** (Jan 2026). Read as
succession rather than conflict — no source on file states a handover date.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Cashless immigration payments (~2026-01-19).** The NRA **abolished cash at
  immigration offices**, replacing it with an **EU-supported, bank-linked digital
  payment system** for passports and national ID cards. An invoice is generated
  automatically and the bank receipt linked to the immigration database, so
  officers process applications without handling cash — intended to curb revenue
  leakage and black-market passport issuance. See [[SSD]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 South Sudan introduces digital payment system for passports after supply crisis]].
- **Context — passport-booklet crisis.** The reform followed a months-long
  passport shortage (blamed on manual cash collection and unpaid supplier
  arrears); South Sudan resumed issuance after receiving 10,000 booklets on
  clearing debts. See [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 South Sudan introduces digital payment system for passports after supply crisis]].
- **Biometric registration of public servants (2025-11).** A parallel government
  drive registered public servants biometrically to purge **ghost workers** from
  the payroll — one of several fragmented biometric databases operating in the
  absence of a unified, interoperable national digital ID. See [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 South Sudan introduces digital payment system for passports after supply crisis]].
