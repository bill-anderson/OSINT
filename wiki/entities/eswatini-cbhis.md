# Eswatini Community-Based Health Information System (CBHIS)

**The electronic system digitising Eswatini's community-health reporting** — introduced
by [[ministry-of-health-eswatini]] with [[world-bank]] support under the
[[eswatini-health-system-strengthening-project]], to take **Rural Health Motivators
(RHMs)** off paper registers. It runs **offline on handheld devices**, syncing when
connectivity allows, and integrates with the national electronic health record,
[[eswatini-cmis]]. See [[SWZ]], [[dpi.mis]], [[digital.rural]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **The problem it addresses.** RHMs previously spent hours each month on health
  registers and summary reports, often travelling at their own expense to the chiefdom,
  Inkhundla, nearest health facility or regional health offices — sometimes completing
  **multiple parallel forms for different funding partners**. Poor data quality and no
  real-time access limited supervision, analysis and timely intervention. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 From Paper to Pixels - Digital Transformation of Community Health Services in Eswatini]].
- **Modules.** Non-communicable diseases (diabetes and hypertension), antenatal care,
  nutritional screening for children under five, health education, malaria and HIV.
- **Design.** Offline-first on a handheld device, synchronising once network
  connectivity is available — an explicit accommodation of the rural connectivity gap
  the [[eswatini-digital-readiness-assessment]] flagged. Integrated with
  [[eswatini-cmis]] for consistency and accuracy. See [[dpi.exchange]].
- **Status (as of 2026-02-23).** Pilot **completed** in the **Mpolonjeni Inkhundla,
  Lubombo region**; RHM training under way as national rollout begins. No coverage,
  device-count or completeness figures are reported. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 From Paper to Pixels - Digital Transformation of Community Health Services in Eswatini]].
- **Why the tier matters.** RHMs accounted for **nearly 22% of the national health
  workforce (2026-02-23)**, serving a population estimated **77% rural (2026-02-23)**.
  See [[include.access]].
