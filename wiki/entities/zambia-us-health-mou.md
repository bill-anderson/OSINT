# Zambia–US health-financing MoU (proposed, halted)

**Parties:** United States (State Department, Bureau of Global Health Security and Diplomacy)
and Zambia. **What:** a proposed five-year bilateral health-financing MoU under the
[[america-first-global-health-strategy]], covering HIV/AIDS, malaria, TB and disease
surveillance. **When:** leaked draft reported 2026-02-25; Zambia halted it and requested
"revisions" (2026-02-26). **Status:** stalled; draft included a termination clause if no
agreement was reached by 2026-04-01.

**Amount / terms:** the US offered **$1.012bn** over five years (down from a $1.5bn figure the
health ministry announced in November 2025; 2026 tranche ~$320m, below the $367m committed to
HIV alone in the prior year). Zambia was to hire **40,000 new health workers** and contribute
**$400m** in additional health services, and meet performance targets or risk termination.
Data terms were the harshest on record: **10-year US access to national health data** (longer
than any other country) plus a **25-year pledge to share data on new or emerging pathogens** —
no other publicly-known MoU includes a 25-year pathogen clause (Kenya capped health-data
sharing at seven years). Financing was reportedly tied to a "bilateral compact" opening
Zambia's **copper and cobalt** to US interests, with monthly embassy briefings on trade and
investment.

## Activity in this wiki

- **"Shameless exploitation" and the mining link (2026-02-25).** Health Gap's Asia Russell and
  Zambian civil-society groups (Chapter One Foundation, Civil Society Self-coordinating
  Mechanism) condemned the terms as conditioning life-saving health services on the country's
  mineral wealth and signing away citizens' health-data rights. Advocates' foremost demand was
  stripping the data-sharing provisions; some weighed a constitutional court challenge. The US
  embassy in Zambia had confirmed in early December 2025 that financing was contingent on
  "collaboration in the mining sector." Tags [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.mis]], [[sovereignty]],
  [[colonialism]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]].
- **Minister denies, then fired (Feb 2026).** Health minister **Elijah Muchima** publicly
  denied on 2026-02-15 that the financing was linked to mining concessions; President Hichilema
  fired him three days later without explanation. Source:
  [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]].
- **Halt and regional pushback (2026-02-26).** Zambia confirmed requesting revisions, with
  Hichilema framing aid cuts as a chance to "take care of our own affairs." The move mirrored
  [[zimbabwe-us-health-mou]] (rejected over pathogen-data terms) and the [[us-kenya-health-framework]]
  (frozen by Kenya's High Court); [[africa-cdc]]'s Jean Kaseya backed renegotiation ("We want
  to own our data in Africa"). Part of the [[america-first-global-health-strategy]] pattern
  across 16+ African states. Source:
  [[2026-02-26 Zambia rejects U.S. health aid over mining partnership ties]].
- **Africa CDC confirmation (2026-03-05).** Reuters, reporting DG Kaseya's press conference, confirmed
  Zambia "had pushed back on a section of its deal" — the same day Kaseya voiced "huge concerns
  regarding data, regarding pathogen sharing." See [[africa-cdc]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-05 Africa CDC head cites major concerns over data, pathogen sharing in US health deals]].
