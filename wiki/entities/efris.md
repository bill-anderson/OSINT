# Electronic Fiscal Receipting and Invoicing System (EFRIS), Uganda

**Uganda's electronic fiscal receipting and invoicing system**, operated by
[[ura-uganda]] — one of the tax DPIs through which transactions are recorded in real time
and VAT compliance enforced. It sits alongside ITAS (with TINs), Payment Identification
Numbers (PINs) and Digital Tax Stamps (DTS) in URA's stack.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Revenue effect (IMF analysis, 2025-04-21 — attribute; analysis, not primary).** EFRIS
  **enabled real-time transaction tracking, leading to a 12 percent rise in VAT
  collections** and curbing tax fraud, in an analysis arguing DPI is how Uganda closes the
  gap between a tax-to-GDP ratio **stagnant near 13 percent for a decade** and its **18
  percent by 2030** target. See [[ura-uganda]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-21 Leveraging Digital Public Infrastructure to Enhance Uganda's Tax Revenue Potential]].
- **Named in the DPI-authentication critique (2025-09-13, advocacy analysis —
  attribute).** Michael Aboneka's rebuttal to the High Court's finding that Uganda's ID
  system is *"offline and non-digital"* rests on the government's own **5-year Digital
  Transformation Roadmap (launched 17 August 2023)**, which shifts service provision to
  DPI — the National ID system, mobile money, **EFRIS**, the Express Penalty Scheme,
  Digital Tax Stamps and Payment Registration Numbers — **all requiring authentication**.
  See [[iser-uganda]], [[ndaga-muntu]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-13 Uganda's Digital ID Barrier Worsens as Court Delivers Blow to Access]].
