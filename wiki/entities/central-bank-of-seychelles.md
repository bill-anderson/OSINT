# Central Bank of Seychelles (CBS)

**Seychelles' monetary authority and payments regulator**. In this wiki its footprint is the
**phased abolition of cheques** — a four-step, 16-month timetable running from 2025-01-01 to
2026-05-01 that makes CBS the driver of Seychelles' digital-first payments agenda and the
custodian of its financial-inclusion risk. Operator context for the
**[[seft-seychelles]]** electronic funds transfer system. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]],
[[SYC]].

> **Provenance caveat.** The figures and dates below rest on a **single source** whose canonical
> Seychelles News Agency URL returned HTTP 403; the body was reconstructed from a same-day
> syndicated republication and the source page is marked `date_source: proxy`. The payload is

## Activity in this wiki

- **Cheque phase-out announced (2024-09).** CBS set a four-stage timetable, decided after a
  notable decline in cheque-based transactions alongside a significant rise in digital payments:
  - **From 2025-01-01** — banks stop **issuing** cheques to individuals.
  - **From 2025-05-01** — banks stop **accepting** cheques from individuals.
  - **From 2026-01-01** — banks and the **Seychelles Credit Union (SCU)** stop **issuing**
    cheques to non-residents.
  - **After 2026-05-01** — they stop **accepting** cheques from non-residents.
  *(Announced timetable as at 2024-09; no source held here confirms each stage took effect on
  schedule.)* See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2024-09-22 Seychelles to phase out cheques for financial transactions from 2025]].
- **The volume case (2023 figures).** **Liz Julienne**, CBS Director of Financial Inclusion:
  around **600,000 cheques** were used for transactions **in 2023**; over the same period, card
  transactions and electronic funds transfers via **[[seft-seychelles]]** exceeded **10
  million** — a roughly 17:1 ratio that is the stated evidentiary basis for withdrawal. Source:
  [[2024-09-22 Seychelles to phase out cheques for financial transactions from 2025]].
- **Inclusion concerns acknowledged.** CBS reported extensive consultation with the private and
  public sectors and businesses before deciding. Concerns centred on **potential financial
  exclusion** of groups that rely on cheques, and the **cost of adopting digital financial
  solutions**; CBS said it continues to work with partners to address them. This is the
  inclusion tension a mandated payment-rail withdrawal creates — see [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2024-09-22 Seychelles to phase out cheques for financial transactions from 2025]].
