# Unitel

Angola's **largest mobile operator** and long-dominant incumbent — majority state-controlled since 2012. Runs enterprise data-centre capacity in Luanda (Unitel Luanda Sul, Unitel FILDA). Its market dominance is the backdrop to Africell's 2022 entry.

## Activity in this wiki

- The **"single state-owned player"** that dominated Angolan mobile before **[[africell]]**'s 2022 entry (Africell took ~25% share within eight months) — the competitive context for mobile-money rollout. Source: [[2023-06-26 Mobile Money in Angola - How Afrimoney is paving the way]].
- Part of Angola's data-centre estate (Unitel Luanda Sul, FILDA enterprise facilities) alongside [[angola-cables]]/AngoNAP, [[paratus]], [[raxio-group]] and MSTelcom. See [[angola--infra-store]].
- Market leader by subscribers: **20.8m mobile users at end-2024** (Omdia), against [[africell]]'s
  **~5m** and Movicel's **941,000** — a state-controlled operator heading toward re-privatisation
  (a 15% IPO opened July 2026).

## Activity in this wiki

- **2Africa branch — $43m, Cacuaco landing (2025-12).** Unitel, the **sole Angolan member** of the
  2Africa consortium, invested **US$43m** to build Angola's branch of **[[2africa-cable]]** and the
  **Cacuaco landing station** (activated 13 Dec 2025), with **23.6 Tbps** allocated to Angola —
  positioned to replace the end-of-life SAT-3 and at-capacity WACS. See [[angola--infra-connect]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2025-12-13-unitel-investe-43-milhoes-usd-ramal-2africa]].
- **Ericsson dual-mode 5G core (2025-03).** Ericsson modernised Unitel's core network — a **dual-mode
  5G Core**, IMS and cloud-native infrastructure supporting 5G Standalone, network slicing and 2G/3G
  migration — after a 20-year partnership. Source:
  [[2025-03-11-ericsson-modernizes-unitels-network-in-angola]].
- **ANGOTIC 2026 agreements (2026-06).** Signed an **MoU with [[ggpen]]** (AI-driven geospatial, space
  cooperation) and an **infrastructure-sharing deal with [[angola-telecom]]** to extend broadband to
  underserved border regions (building on a Feb 2026 GGPEN precursor meeting). See
  [[angola--infra-connect]]. Sources: [[2026-06-12-unitel-agreements-expand-broadband-angotic]],
  [[2026-02-20-ggpen-and-unitel-partner-to-expand-internet-connectivity]].
