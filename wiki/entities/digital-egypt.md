# Digital Egypt

Egypt's national **digital-transformation strategy and e-government platform** (MCIT), launched **2020** and a cornerstone of the **Digital Transformation Strategy 2030** under **[[egypt-vision-2030|Vision 2030]]**. Built around human capital, digital services, IT infrastructure and innovation.

- **Digital Egypt Portal (as of Jan 2026):** **210 online services** (up from 170 in 2024), **~10.7m users** (+32.1%), **73 exclusive** services; **transactions 7.8m (2024) → 25m+ (2025)**. Nine **commercial-registry** services made exclusive to the platform from **1 Jan 2026** (~5,000 requests/day). MCIT targets **270 services in 2026** (+50).
- **2026 target:** full automation of government services + a **digital identity card** for remote verification; recently eliminated the vehicle-licence "clearance certificate."
- **Supporting rails:** the CBE's **[[haweya]]** (financial ID) and Interior's **[[moieg-pass]]** (government-services ID) provide the identity layer.

Delivered via **[[mcit-egypt|MCIT]]** with UNDP/ITU/World Bank support.
