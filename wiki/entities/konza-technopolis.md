# Konza Technopolis (Kenya)

Kenya's flagship "smart city" ~60km from Nairobi. Its national data centre + smart-ICT + public-safety
project was financed under a **2019 $665m agreement** (China [[china-eximbank]] $167m loan; Huawei EPC);
Phase 1 data centre completed 2020, **stalled since 2021** (later Korea Eximbank involvement). Cited by
ODI as a case of Chinese-financed digital infrastructure with feasibility/transparency challenges. See
[[infra.store]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
[[2026-02-05 Chinas infrastructure finance in Africa impacts and lessons]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Technopolis Act — statutory framework (2026-05).** [[gov.legislate]] [[tech.innovate]] President Ruto
  signed the **Technopolis Act** (with the Income Tax and SEZ (Amendment) Bills), Kenya's first
  standalone legal framework for technology zones — one-stop licensing/permitting, a **Technopolis
  Dispute Resolution Tribunal**, and a **Technopolis Development Authority** succeeding the Konza
  Technopolis Development Authority, with a mandate to replicate technopolises nationally rather than
  back a single project. Source: [[2026-05-13-kenya-law-tech-zones-itweb]].
- **Innovation decentralisation (2026-01).** [[tech.innovate]] Konza's National Data Centre and the
  planned **[[kenya-aist|KAIST]]** (opening academic programmes 2026) anchor Konza in a wider
  redistribution of Kenya's tech economy beyond Nairobi — Kisumu (LakeHub/Zone 01), Mombasa (SwahiliPot),
  Eldoret (EldoHub), Nyeri (DeKUT semiconductor facility). Kenya's digital economy is projected to
  contribute **KES 662bn ($5.1bn)** and 300,000+ jobs by 2028. Source:
  [[2026-01-07-kenya-tech-future-no-longer-nairobi-techcabal]].
