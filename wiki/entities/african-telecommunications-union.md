# African Telecommunications Union (ATU)

A specialised African Union agency for the telecommunications/ICT sector, coordinating
continental positions on spectrum, connectivity policy and digital inclusion. Secretary
General **John Omo**. In this wiki it appears as a continental voice on
connectivity-and-inclusion policy ([[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Content-and-backhaul framing of digital inclusion (2026).** SG John Omo argued that
  putting a terminal in someone's hands is not enough — inclusion requires **relevant local
  content and adequate backhaul** to deliver it (health, education, agriculture), in the
  context of AI-as-access-layer approaches (voice/NLP over feature phones) being used to
  bypass the smartphone barrier. See [[infra.connect]], [[include.access]],
  [[capacity.literacy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].

- **Convener of the Algiers Declaration (2025-12, pub 2026-07).** Under the ATU's aegis, African
  telecom/ICT/digital-economy ministers adopted the **[[algiers-declaration]]** (Algiers, 6–8 Dec
  2025) — a continental framework to regulate global **OTT platforms** (local-reinvestment "fair
  share," data sovereignty, culturally-adapted content moderation, responsible AI), DSA-inspired and
  heading to the AU for adoption in early 2026. See [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]],
  [[tech.industry]], [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-12 Algiers Declaration Africa's OTT Platform Rules]].
