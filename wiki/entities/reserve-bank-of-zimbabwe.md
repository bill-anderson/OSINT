# Reserve Bank of Zimbabwe (RBZ)

**Zimbabwe's central bank.** One source is held, and it captures the RBZ in a single
narrow but telling role: removing a price barrier to small-value electronic payments in a
country whose mobile-money story ([[ecocash]]) is one of the wiki's foundational
Zimbabwe threads.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Sub-US$10 transactions exempted from bank charges, effective 1 September 2024.** The
  RBZ exempted electronic transactions **under US$10 or the ZiG equivalent** from bank
  charges. The measure followed **public outcry over excessive transaction charges**,
  which the source says had **derailed public confidence** in electronic payment, with
  experts urging government to reduce charges and lobby for terms that promote electronic
  transactions. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2024-10-23 Electronic Transactions A Consumer Right - Consumer Protection Commission]].

The exemption is the price-side complement to the
[[consumer-protection-commission-zimbabwe]]'s enforcement-side push (compelling merchants
to *accept* electronic payment under the Consumer Protection Act and the Finance Act
2018): one obliges the merchant to offer the rail, the other makes small transactions on
it free at the point of use.

## Provenance

Held only via a secondary consumer-affairs report; **no RBZ statement, circular or
directive is held**, and nothing on the exemption's duration, uptake or effect is
recorded. Nothing else about the RBZ — monetary policy, ZiG, licensing, its role in mobile
money — is held in this wiki.
