# Mário Oliveira

**Angola's Minister of Telecommunications, Information Technologies and Social Communication (Mário Augusto da Silva Oliveira) — the state's standing voice on digital sovereignty and the named counterparty on essentially every foreign-partner ICT agreement the wiki holds for Angola.** He heads [[ministerio-das-telecomunicacoes-tecnologias-de-informacao-e-comunicacao-social-angola|MINTTICS]], under which [[infosi]] and [[ggpen]] sit.

*Paged because he is an actor, not a mention: the sovereignty framing that recurs across Angola's connectivity, cloud and space files is his, stated in his own words in the primaries, and he is the signing party on the 2026 outbound MoU batch. Below him, officeholders are tagged to their institutions, per the wiki's rule.*

## Activity in this wiki

- **2Africa / Cacuaco landing (2025-12-13).** Framed the landing station — activated 13 Dec 2025 with **23.6 Tbps** of the cable's ~180 Tbps allocated to Angola — as decisive for digital sovereignty, precisely because **SAT-3 has reached end-of-life after 25 years** and WACS is ageing. See [[2africa-cable]], [[angola--infra-connect]]. Source: [[2025-12-13-cabo-submarino-2africa-unitel-soberania-digital]].
- **The sovereign data centre and Nuvem Nacional (2026-04-28).** Called the Camama government data centre "a clear affirmation of Angola's digital sovereignty", with passports and education certificates previously held by foreign platforms now under **national jurisdiction**, on a progressive and mandatory migration. See [[data-center-e-cloud-do-governo-angola]], [[nuvem-nacional-de-angola]]. Source: [[2026-04-28-centro-de-dados-do-governo-soberania-digital]].
- **Cybersecurity bill suspended (2026-07-08).** Called the committee-stage suspension of the [[angola-cybersecurity-law]] strategic, after discussion was halted by unanimous agreement of the Executive and deputies over drafting inconsistencies in the preamble and chapters 1–2. See [[angola--infra-cybersec]]. Source: [[2026-07-08-deputados-suspendem-discussao-lei-da-ciberseguranca]].
- **The fibre figure that recurs (2025-05-09).** Told the III MINTTICS Consultative Council that Angola would gain "more than two thousand kilometres of optical fibre **this year**" — an annual-build figure, repeatedly misread elsewhere as a stock. See [[angola--infra-connect]]. Source: [[2025-05-09-angola-minttics-2000km-fibra-optica-este-ano]].
- **Submarine and satellite expansion (2025-11-07, 2026-03-16, 2026-06-11).** MINTTICS plans for submarine fibre expansion; present at the ANGEO-1 project launch with [[ggpen]] and Airbus; and the positioning of Angola as an African reference in space technologies. See [[angeo-1]], [[equiano-cable]]. Sources: [[2025-11-07-minttics-expandir-rede-de-fibra-optica-submarina]], [[2026-03-16-arranque-do-projecto-angeo-1-airbus]], [[2026-06-11-angola-referencia-africana-em-tecnologias-espaciais]].
- **ICT diplomacy — the outbound turn (2025–2026).** The signing minister on the Kenya ICT MoU ([[kenya-angola-ict-mou]]), the Namibia ministerial ICT and media MoU with [[emma-theofelus]] ([[telecom-namibia-angola-deals]]), the Mozambique space and digital memoranda with Américo Muchanga ([[angola-mozambique-space-digital-mou]]), and the Nigeria space-cooperation agreement ([[nigeria-angola-space-cooperation]]).
- **The digital-transition agenda abroad (2025-03-19).** Presented Angola's digital-transition agenda in the United States. Source: [[2025-03-19-agenda-de-transicao-digital-apresentada-nos-eua]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Oliveira supplies the *rhetoric* of Angola's multi-pole strategy while [[joao-lourenco]] transacts it. The recurring claim — a stable international route and state data "under national jurisdiction" — is consistent across cables, cloud and space, and is the clearest single articulation of digital sovereignty by a serving African minister in this wiki.
