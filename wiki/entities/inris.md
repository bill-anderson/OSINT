# Integrated National Registration Information System (INRIS)

**Zambia's foundational population-registration platform**, built and run by
**[[dnrpc-zambia]]** under the Ministry of Home Affairs and Internal Security. It holds
the digitised civil register and the biometric enrolment base beneath the legacy paper
**National Registration Card (NRC)** — the substrate the country's [[mosip]]-based
national digital ID is being built onto. The wiki settles on the full name
**Integrated National Registration Information System**; held pages and most sources use
the bare acronym, and ZNBC (2025-12-28) expands it differently, as "National Registration
Information System".

## The MOSIP thread starts earlier than the wiki records it

The held **[[zambia--dpi-id]]** intersection dates Zambia's MOSIP work from the
**March-2026 system-integrator procurement**. That is the page missing its own
prehistory: **MOSIP work was already live in January 2025**.

- **2025-01-17** — Developers from **[[smart-zambia-institute]]**'s e-government division
  had **completed MOSIP training**, delivered by **[[ihm-southern-africa]]**'s software
  development team, aimed at integrating [[mosip]] with the **[[smartcare-pro]]** Electronic
  Health Record system — for **e-KYC** and a **"central source of truth"** for digital
  identification, with identity verification against NRC data and de-duplication of records.
  Two details matter beyond the date: the **technical architecture allows integration with
  existing biometric databases, eliminating the need for complete system replacement**; and
  the training was explicitly aimed at enabling **Zambian developers to independently manage
  and maintain the platform** — the vendor-independence posture in practice, 14 months
  before the SI hunt. Financed by the **$100m [[world-bank]] grant via [[dzap]]**. Source:
  [[2025-01-17 Zambia Advances Digital ID System with MOSIP Training for Healthcare Integration]].

**The SI procurement was not the start.** See [[zambia--dpi-id]] and
[[smart-zambia-institute]].

## Enrolment — a contradiction, recorded not resolved

Three figures, and the later one is *lower*:

- **1.5 million** enrolled biometrically, **~81%** of legacy civil-registration records
  digitised (UNDP, **2024-09-17**) — against 9 million adults.
- **~1.6 million (~15% of adults)** implied by the held [[dzap]] PAD (**2025-09-11**).
- **~1.3 million**, plus **500,000+ births and deaths** registered (ZNBC quoting MoHAIS
  PRO **Mwala Kalaluka**, **2025-12-28**).

A later source reporting a lower figure is not supersession. Recorded, not resolved:
`reviews/contradictions/open/zambia-inris-enrolment`.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Biometric NRCs wait for the polls (2025-12-28).** Government is procuring **smart card
  printers and blank cards**, but secure **biometric NRC issuance comes only *after* the
  2026 General Elections** — the cards to be fully interoperable within the INRIS ecosystem.
  This bears directly on the competing end-2026 / end-2027 rollout targets
  (`reviews/contradictions/open/zambia-digital-id-target`). End-to-end automation of birth
  and death notifications is being implemented through the **INRIS API Manager**. Source:
  [[2025-12-28 Over 1.3 Million Citizens Enrolled on INRIS]].
- **Next-phase plan (2024-09).** Public-awareness campaigns, registration points near
  populated communities, a **unique legal ID number**, integration via an API manager, and
  improved child registration; the old system to be phased out. Source:
  [[2024-09-17 DPI Safeguards come online in Zambias next phase of digital integration]].
- **A candid admission (2025-10-03).** On the e-government platform: **"The current platform
  is hindering government efforts to identify duplicate social program beneficiaries."**
  Source: [[2025-10-03 Zambia plans domestic digital ID development with help from international partners]].
- **Deferral flagged (2026-04).** The held digital-rights reporting records a post-election
  **INRIS deferral** — see [[ZMB]].
