# Ministry of Digital Transformation, Innovation & Modernization of Administration (MTNIMA)

Mauritania's lead digital ministry, **created in 2021**. In 2022 it launched the **National Digital
Transformation Agenda 2022–2025**, structured on four axes: digital infrastructure, digital government
administration, transformation of economic sectors, and e-business/innovation (including a Startup Act and
the IBTIKAR science-and-technology park). Ministers include Mohamed Abdallahi Louly (2023) and Ahmed Salem
Ould Bede (2026).

The ministry drove Mauritania's digital-ID programme (**[[houwiyeti]]**, with **[[tech5]]**/SmartMS/UNDP) and
e-government portals — **Ijraati** (consolidating 800+ administrative procedures, launched February 2026) and
**Khdamati** for remote transactions — plus the €4m German-backed **[[digital-y|Digital-Y]]** project
(January 2025). Mauritania ranked **165th on the 2024 UN EGDI (0.3491)**, its weakest score in online services.

Through 2025–26 the ministry (led by Ahmed Salem Ould Bede) pushed a wider stack: a **national framework for
public-digital-services standards** (interoperability workshop, March 2026, with **[[giz|GIZ]]**/Digital-Y); a
**national cloud** launched 29 June 2026 on the WARDIP-built Nouakchott data centre ([[infra.store]]); a
**digital consular-services platform** for the diaspora (9 online services, July 2026); and a new e-service
extending **[[houwiyeti|Houwiyeti]]** into education certificates. Its 2026 budget rose 104.6% to $24.2m, and
it is negotiating a **$50m [[islamic-development-bank|IsDB]]** digital-transformation programme.

Its WARDIP unit issued the **national PKI / e-certification tender AP-01_DP-15_WARDIP-2026** on
**2026-04-28** (IDA-7445-MR) — **unawarded as of 2026-07-20**, deadline extended twice to
**21 Jul 2026**. Despite the notice title, the operative clauses cover **e-certification only**, not a
new digital-ID system. Source: [[2026-04-28-wardip-mauritanie-ap01-identite-numerique-pki]].
