# HAAC–IPDCP Institutional Cooperation Accord (Togo)

**Parties:** Togo's **[[haac-togo]]** (Haute Autorité de l'Audiovisuel et de la
Communication), the broadcast regulator, and the **[[ipdcp-togo]]** (Instance de Protection
des Données à Caractère Personnel), the data protection authority. **What:** a memorandum
establishing a framework of institutional cooperation to promote press freedom while
guaranteeing the protection of personal data and digital rights. **When:** sealed **10
December 2025**. **Status:** active. See [[TGO]], [[finance.mou]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **The operative mechanism: monitoring-room access.** Under the accord the **HAAC
  authorises the IPDCP to access its control and monitoring room** (*salle de contrôle et de
  monitoring*) — presented as allowing more precise tracking of broadcast content, faster
  control of possible abuses, and above all a **coordinated reaction in case of a breach of
  personal data**. Beyond this, the HAAC pledges to integrate data protection into its
  procedures, its monitoring and its awareness-raising actions, while the IPDCP contributes
  expertise "in a context where media practices evolve faster than norms." Both institutions
  framed the accord as urgent, arguing that press freedom cannot hold if the protection of
  digital rights remains fragile. See [[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-12 Personal data and digital rights in Togo - HAAC and IPDCP seal institutional cooperation accord]].

**Provenance and framing note.** The sole account held here is published by Africa
Cybersecurity Mag **sourced to the IPDCP itself**, and is promotional in register — it
records a data protection authority gaining access to a broadcast-monitoring room **without
any critical framing** of what that access entails, and reports no limits, safeguards,
duration or oversight conditions attached to it. The absence is noted; no inference beyond
the evidence is drawn here. Capture is not endorsement.
