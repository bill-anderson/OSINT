# Nvidia

US GPU maker; the indispensable hardware layer beneath the African (and global)
AI build-out — its chips are the scarce input that data-centre and telco AI
strategies are built around.

## Activity in this wiki

- **MTN AI-RAN consortium.** [[mtn-group]]'s Ambition 2030 includes a stake in an
  **AI-RAN consortium with Nvidia, Nokia and Cisco**. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[infra.connect]]. Source: [[2026-06-10 MTN Group goes all-in on platforms and AI]].
- **SA data centres.** Microsoft's SA facilities cited as deploying **Nvidia GPUs
  for AI**, part of the buildout behind SA's data-centre-as-critical-infrastructure
  shift. Source: [[2026-02-25 South Africa puts data centres on par with energy, ports in big policy shift]].
- **The bundling dynamic (EU parallel).** [[2026-06-29 How AI Keeps Europe Hooked on US Cloud]] describes Nvidia backing "neoclouds" (Nebius, NScale) — the same
  GPU-scarcity leverage that shapes who can build sovereign compute anywhere,
  including Africa (see [[infra.capacity]]).
- **Cassava AI Factories.** NVIDIA H100 GPUs anchor Cassava's [[cassava-ai-factories]] —
  **3,000** in the Johannesburg [[africa-data-centres]] campus, with the full cluster
  reported at **12,000–15,000** — and NVIDIA is reported as a **Cassava shareholder**
  (Nov 2025). Cassava planned 3,000 GPUs across SA by mid-2025 with expansion into
  Nigeria, Kenya, Egypt and Morocco. Sources:
  [[2026-01-01 Africa's Sovereign AI Play Cassava Technologies and Zimbabwean Strive Masiyiwa $ 720 million Bets - Ecofin Agency]],
  [[2026-06-16 The digital sovereignty dividing line]],
  [[2026-05-21 Africa Data Center Investment 2026 The $4.5B Race and the Power Problem]].
- **Africa AI Fund GPUs (2025).** The **$60bn Africa AI Fund** (announced April
  2025, Kigali Summit) earmarks **12,000 Nvidia GPUs** for centres in the "Big
  Four" nations and Morocco — Nvidia named among the four US firms (with Google,
  Microsoft, Meta) that African draft AI strategies flag as a dependence risk.
  See [[tech.ai]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]].
- **Cassava as "continent's first NVIDIA Cloud Partner" (2026-03).** [[cassava-technologies]]
  brands its SA **AI Factory** as the continent's first NVIDIA Cloud Partner (GPUaaS/AIaaS),
  and ships the **Cassava Autonomous Network** — an agentic RAN-optimisation blueprint powered
  by **NVIDIA AI infrastructure, NIM microservices and the NVIDIA Network Configuration
  Blueprint**, running on [[caimex]]. Its **[[national-sovereign-cloud]]** Cloud Partner
  Programme also resells NVIDIA Cloud Partner solutions. See [[cassava-ai-factories]],
  [[tech.ai]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-18 Cassava Technologies Launches AI Factory in South Africa, Plans Expansion Across Africa]],
  [[2026-03-03 Cassava unveils NVIDIA-backed autonomous network solution]],
  [[2026-03-06 Cassava launches sovereign cloud for Africa's public sector]].
- **Pax Silica / export-control politics (2026-01, global context).** In the US-led "Pax
  Silica" semiconductor coalition, Washington let Nvidia sell **H200 chips to China for a 25%
  export fee** — read by CEPA as "discipline for the coalition, carve-outs for Washington,"
  underscoring how Nvidia's hardware sits at the centre of US–China chip statecraft. See
  [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]]. Source: [[2026-01-14 Pax Silica — Can it Stop China]].
- **Cerebras as an emerging challenge (2026-05, global context).** [[g42]]'s
  Intelligence Grid deploys **64 Cerebras systems** to India — a non-Nvidia
  inference alternative — even as India's $1.25bn national AI program still runs on
  ~34,000 Nvidia GPUs. A marker of where GPU-scarcity leverage may erode. See
  [[infra.capacity]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-01 India's AI deal with the UAE challenges U.S. cloud dominance]].

- **Nvidia–Cassava AI data centres, ~$700m (2026-03 context).** Nvidia and
  [[cassava-technologies]] reported developing AI-ready data centres across Egypt, Nigeria,
  Kenya and Morocco with total investment estimated ~**$700m** — the buildout unfolding as
  Chinese [[deepseek]] gains share (11–14% of African chatbot usage; ~20% in Ethiopia and
  Zimbabwe), pressuring the US AI vendors that Nvidia hardware underpins. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[infra.store]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12 China's DeepSeek gives US tech giants a run for their money as Microsoft and others expand AI push in Africa]].

- **American AI Exports Program (Politico, 2026-07).** Nvidia signalled it "looks forward to
  participating" in Commerce's [[american-ai-exports-program]] (AWS also applied), even as the
  first round drew only 78 applications — a supply-side vehicle for pushing the US AI stack
  (Nvidia hardware included) into developing markets against Chinese alternatives. See
  [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-11 'Not where they hoped it'd be' Launch of Trump AI promotion program underwhelms]].

- **GPU-partner field widening in Africa (Russell Southwood, 2026-05-19).** In
  Southwood's demand-side survey, Nvidia's African footprint spreads across
  GPU-as-a-Service partners: [[cassava-technologies]] (the only Nvidia *cloud*
  partner), **[[ixafrica-data-centres]]** — which installed **Africa's first GPU
  infrastructure in Nov 2025** — and an **Altron–Nvidia** deployment hosted in
  Teraco (SA). See [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-19 Trying to make sense of who will use Artificial Intelligence in Africa and how – Going beyond the hype]].
