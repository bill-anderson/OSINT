# Cameroon Civil Registration Law (Law 2024/016)

Cameroon's **2024 civil-registration reform law** — the legal basis for the digital CRVS overhaul. Introduces electronic declaration of civil-status acts, a **national database**, **digital birth/marriage/death certificates**, and a **Unique Personal Identification Number (UPIN)** for every citizen. **Enacted by President Biya on 23 Dec 2024; 130 sections.**

## Profile

- **Enactment dated precisely (23 Dec 2024, 130 sections).** The wiki previously held only the Nov-2024 *draft* stage; the law was enacted 23 Dec 2024. Among its novelties: an internally-displaced person can re-establish a lost birth certificate through the State Counsel of their **place of residence**, rather than travelling back to the court of the issuing locality (as the 1981 ordinance, amended by Law 2011/011, required) — significant for the displaced of the Anglophone conflict. Source: [[2025-01-27-new-civil-registration-law-sdg-16-9-biometricupdate]].
- Passed alongside the **personal-data-protection law** — its paired statute, enacted the same day as **[[cameroon-data-protection-law-2024]]** (Loi n°2024/017, 23 Dec 2024). *(This resolves the wiki's earlier draft-vs-adopted ambiguity: both were enacted 23 Dec 2024; the wiki had held only the Nov-2024 draft stage.)* Implemented by **[[bunec]]** (with Luxembourg's [[liber-technologies]]) toward a **fully digital CRVS by 2029**, integrated with the biometric national ID. Analysis flags barriers — weak equipment, limited internet, low digital literacy, and rights safeguards. See [[cameroon--dpi-id]]. Sources: [[2024-11-19 Cameroon introduces draft laws on civil registration reform, data protection]], [[2025-03-27 Digital Transformation of Civil Registration in Cameroon - e-Governance innovations (Law 2024-016)]].
