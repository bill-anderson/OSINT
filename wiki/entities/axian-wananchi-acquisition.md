# Yas / AXIAN Telecom — Wananchi Group acquisition

**Parties:** **[[yas]]**, a subsidiary of AXIAN Telecom Holding & Management PLC
([[axian-telecom]]), and **[[wananchi-group]] (Holdings) Ltd** — parent of **Zuku**
(retail broadband) and **Simbanet** (enterprise connectivity), operating in **Kenya and
Uganda**.
**What:** acquisition of **99.63% of Wananchi Group (Holdings) Ltd**.
**Price:** **approximately US$63 million**.
**When/where:** **completed**; announced at a **Kampala news conference on 10 November
2025**, reported **2025-11-11**.
**Approvals:** all country and regional regulatory approvals — **including
[[comesa]]** — completed before clearance in Uganda and other markets.
**Status:** closed.

## Why this page exists

**It closes a held provenance gap.** [[yas]], [[axian-telecom]] and a 2026-07 EBRD source
all assert this acquisition **with no date, price or stake**. This source supplies all
three.

**TRAP — do not conflate:** the **$63m purchase price** is a **different quantity** from
AXIAN's **$600m capital raise** held on [[axian-telecom]].

## Terms and undertakings (as reported 2025-11-11)

- **No job losses** will result; the stated strategy is business growth, network expansion
  and employment.
- **Both Zuku and Simbanet continue operating under their established brand identities.**
- Over the **next 12 to 24 months**, Yas plans to **upgrade Wananchi's fibre network in
  Uganda** (speed, reliability, coverage), expanding **FTTH** plus enterprise cloud and IoT
  — projections, not achievements.

## On the record

- **Hassan Jaber**, CEO, AXIAN Telecom: *"This acquisition marks a strategic step in our
  mission to make high-quality digital connectivity accessible and affordable to all. East
  Africa remains a priority growth market for AXIAN Telecom, and Uganda in particular
  offers tremendous opportunities."*
- **Genue Mwaura**, Group CEO, Wananchi: the company now needs *"to move to the attacking
  side."*
- **Bertrand Lacroix**, CEO, AXIAN Telecom Fibre: a major step toward building a leading
  pan-African broadband provider.

Yas / AXIAN Telecom is described as a pan-African telecom and digital infrastructure group
with operations in **11 countries**, serving **over 36 million customers** (as of
2025-11-11).
