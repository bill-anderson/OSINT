# ARCT (Agence de Régulation et de Contrôle des Télécommunications, Burundi)

Burundi's telecommunications regulator (also rendered ACRT). Director-General: **Samuel Bahizi**. The
ARCT administers spectrum, licensing and service-quality control in a three-operator market
(Econet, [[lumitel|Lumitel]], [[onatel-burundi|Onatel]]) plus new entrant
[[lyca-mobile]], and implements EAC roaming rules. Critics say it under-enforces on quality — operators
advertising 4G that users cannot reach. See [[BDI]], [[infra.connect]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **EAC One Network Area + RDI (2024–25).** [[gov.regional]] The ARCT implemented Decree 100/202
  (Oct 2023) cutting regional roaming tariffs as Burundi joined the EAC **One Network Area** (1 Aug 2024)
  alongside Kenya, Rwanda, Uganda, Tanzania, South Sudan; the Senate ratified the EAC Regional Digital
  Integration protocol (Jan 2025). Source:
  [[2025-01-21-burundi-embraces-eac-digital-integration-ict-growth]].
- **A 5G roadmap on a weak base.** [[infra.connect]] The ARCT's 2023 report framed 5G as an economic
  driver (IoT, low latency), with spectrum to be re-farmed for it; meanwhile 2G covered ~97% but mobile
  internet penetration was ~22% (2023), and observers press the ARCT to police real service quality.
  Source: [[2025-09-10-unreliable-connectivity-impacts-burundi-citizens]].
