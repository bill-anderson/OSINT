# African Union (AU)

The continental bloc (55 member states; Commission HQ Addis Ababa). As a
political bloc it is an **entity, not a place**. Author of the **Continental AI
Strategy** (adopted July 2024) and Agenda 2063, and the continental counterparty
in US–Africa engagement.

## Activity in this wiki

- **US–AUC Strategic Investment Working Group (2026-01-28).** The AU Commission
  and US agreed to establish the **[[us-auc-strategic-investment-working-group]]**
  to channel US private investment into AU-backed infrastructure — including
  continent-wide digital transformation — anchored to Agenda 2063, PIDA and
  **[[afcfta]]**, and an explicit "investment in place of aid" posture. See
  [[XAF]], [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Joint Statement of the United States and African Union on the Launch of a Strategic Investment Working Group]].
- **Continental AI Strategy** cited as the frame for African AI ambitions in
  [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]]
  and as a sovereignty reference point in [[2026-06-08 The Promise and Limits of African Data Sovereignty]]
  (which proposes AfCFTA digital-trade protocols as continental leverage). See
  [[tech.ai]], [[gov.protect]]. The Strategy (Jul 2024) is again the reference point in the
  2026 AI-sovereignty debate, alongside Smart Africa's **Africa AI Council** (Nov 2025) and
  the **$60bn Africa AI Fund** (Apr 2025 Kigali). See [[smart-africa]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]].
  The April-2025 Kigali **[[africa-ai-declaration]]** (Africa AI Council co-chaired by the AUC and
  [[itu]]; $60bn Fund) is read by analysts in this batch as one pillar — with the Continental AI
  Strategy — of the emerging African AI-governance architecture; its signatory count is disputed
  (54 per Munyua/Adebowale vs 49 per Nagadya — see [[au-continental-ai-strategy]]). Corporate-capture
  critics urge regional coordination through the AU as the guard against competitive deregulation.
  Sources:
  [[2026-05-04 The World's Most Needed AI Governance Framework Doesn't Exist Yet. Africa Should Build It]],
  [[2026-02-17 Corporate Interests Must Not Co-Opt Africa's AI Moment]].
- **AU finance & NAFA (2026-02 to 05).** At the AU's Feb 2026 Addis summit, AfDB
  ([[african-development-bank]]) president [[sidi-ould-tah]] launched the **New African
  Financial Architecture** ([[nafa]]) to mobilise domestic capital; the AfDB's African
  Economic Outlook 2026 urges the AU to expedite the **African Financing Stability
  Mechanism** and **African Credit Rating Agency**, noting the AU still self-funds only
  **~22–25%** of its budget (a decade after the 2016 Kigali Decision). See [[finance.new]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-26 The AfDB's push for African financial sovereignty, and a tech investment rebound]],
  [[2026-05-26 AfDB says Africa must fund its own growth as aid model frays]].
- **AfCFTA Digital Identity Annex (2026-05).** The AU-adopted (Feb 2025) Digital Identity
  Annex to the [[afcfta-digital-trade-protocol]] anchors the AU's "African Single Digital
  Market" vision; DIAL's ADLI cohort is working to operationalise it. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[afcfta]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-27 Africa's economic future hinges on breaking down silos. This ADLI cohort advances integration]].
- **UNECA calls for AI-Strategy localisation (2026-04-13).** At the 58th [[uneca]] ECA
  Conference of Ministers DPI roundtable, ministers and industry ([[gsma]], Africa Data
  Centres) urged translating the AU Continental AI Strategy into actionable national
  frameworks that mandate dedicated data-governance funding — alongside sovereign
  clouds, "data embassies" and a 10x expansion of African data-centre capacity — so
  that the economic value of African data is retained on the continent. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[gov.regional]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].
- **Unified African voice at the Global Dialogue on AI Governance (2026-07-09, Geneva).** AUC
  Commissioner **Lerato Mataboge** led the Commission's engagement at the inaugural Global Dialogue
  on AI Governance (held alongside AI for Good + WSIS Forum 2026), pressing an Africa-centred,
  Agenda 2063-aligned position anchored in the Continental AI Strategy, AU Data Policy Framework and
  Malabo Convention. The **UN Independent International Scientific Panel on AI**
  ([[un-ai-scientific-panel]]) presented its Preliminary Report; Mataboge convened the four African
  panel scientists (Marivate, Tadesse, Orji, El-Assady) to align continental scientific expertise
  with AU political leadership in global AI-governance forums. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]].
  Source: [[2026-07-09 African Union pushes for unified African voice at Global AI Governance Dialogue]].
- **AU–Google AI MoU (2026-02-17).** The AUC (Commissioner Lerato Mataboge) signed an MoU with
  **[[google]]** at Addis Ababa ([[au-google-mou]]) on AI/cloud infrastructure, skills, R&D and
  AI-policy frameworks, anchored in the [[au-continental-ai-strategy]] and the Digital Transformation
  Strategy (2020–2030); Google pledged to train 3m students/teachers by 2030, free Gemini Pro/NotebookLM
  with Amharic support, and AI-readiness training for officials via Apolitical. Alice Munyua's
  *Gatekeepers XII* reads it as formalising African data exposure to the US **CLOUD Act** (jurisdiction
  follows ownership, not server location), noting the AU's 2025 programme budget was **77.5%
  external-donor funded** and arguing the Continental AI Strategy was itself externally drafted
  (UNESCO "entry point," Global Center on AI Governance drafting) — sovereignty "in the press release,
  not the binding clauses," echoing the 2012 Chinese-built AU HQ data-exfiltration reports. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[sovereignty]], [[colonialism]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-20 AUC Signs Partnership With Google]], [[2026-03-05 THE GATEKEEPERS PART XII]].
- **Undersea-cable resilience (Carnegie, 2025-04).** Jane Munga's reference paper casts the AU as the
  body that should champion a harmonized continental legal/regulatory framework for the **77 cable
  systems** landing in Africa (convening a specialist working group), noting the ITU/ICPC International
  Advisory Body for Submarine Cable Resilience (Nov 2024) is co-chaired by Nigeria's [[bosun-tijani]] as
  a cross-pollination point. See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-03 Beneath the Waves Addressing Vulnerabilities in Africa's Undersea Digital Infrastructure]].
- **Health-data sovereignty and the APPM (2026-02).** Amid the [[america-first-global-health-strategy]]
  pushback, analysts (Ahmed Olayinka Sule; Nelson Evaborhene) urge the AU to convene an extraordinary
  summit and renegotiate the US bilateral health deals as a bloc under Agenda 2063; the **39th AU
  Assembly (Feb 2026)** operationalised the **African Pooled Procurement Mechanism (APPM)** — converting
  fragmented national demand into collective market power and "sovereign data clouds" as a
  structural-decoupling counter (working with [[africa-cdc]] and the African Medicines Agency). See
  [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-01 America First, Africa Last  Data Extraction from Africa's Bodies and the Erosion of Health Sovereignty]],
  [[2026-02-24 Global Health Nigeria and the Pathology of the Hostage State in an Era of Fragmentation]].
- **DPI norms vs practice.** Hlengiwe Dube frames the AU's Malabo Convention, AU Data Policy Framework
  and ACHPR Declaration as an existing normative floor for DPI that practice outpaces (under-resourced
  DPAs, rare human-rights impact assessments, vendor-driven procurement); Rwanda's national DPI strategy
  ([[rwanda-dpi-strategy]], launched 2026-03-09) is continental momentum toward interoperable data
  exchange. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-01 (2) Digital Public Infrastructure and the Politics of Data in Africa]],
  [[2026-03-20 Rwanda Launches Digital Public Infrastructure Strategy to Lay Groundwork for AI and Inclusive Growth]].

- **Africa CDC Central Data Repository (2026-01).** The AU agency [[africa-cdc]] launched a
  secure, **federated** Central Data Repository ([[africa-cdc-cdr]]) integrating national
  surveillance, lab and programme data — **member states retain data ownership**, a clean
  continental data-sovereignty model responding to a 40% rise in public-health emergencies
  (2022–2024, 213 incidents). Financed by the [[global-fund]]; prototyped by the
  [[public-health-informatics-institute]]. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.mis]], [[gov.regional]].
  Source: [[2026-01-28 Africa CDC Establishes Central Data Repository to Strengthen Public Health Surveillance]].
- **AU–EU AI-governance co-creation (ECDPM, 2026-01).** [[ecdpm]] analysts (Musoni, Mabika) urge
  the AU and **[[european-union]]** to co-create a human-centric AI-governance framework blending
  the EU AI Act with the [[au-continental-ai-strategy]] and **AU Data Policy Framework** — moving
  past a "standards-setter vs standards-taker" (digital-colonialism) dynamic. They note the 7th
  AU–EU summit (Nov 2025) declaration was weak and **omitted** the AU AI/data frameworks, while a
  May 2025 ministerial communiqué was more ambitious. See [[geopol.eu]], [[tech.ai]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-01-21 How Africa and Europe could create global AI governance rules]].
  *(A lead-rich pan-African 2025 policy year-in-review separately notes the AU Digital
  Transformation Strategy reached its 2020–2030 midpoint in 2025 — cited lightly:
  [[2026-01-12 (3) Africa's Digital Policy Review 2025 in Perspective and Outlook for 2026]].)*

## Reads

- `sovereignty` / `colonialism`: the AU frames AI and DPI as tools of continental
  self-determination, yet its flagship investment vehicles are structured around
  US/external capital and critical-minerals access — the recurring
  externally-financed-sovereignty tension.
