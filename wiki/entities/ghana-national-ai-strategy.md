# Ghana National AI Strategy (2023–2033)

Ghana's ([[GHA]]) national artificial-intelligence strategy, a ten-year roadmap
(2023–2033) organised around **8 pillars** and **7 priority sectors**. Developed
with [[smart-africa]], German development agency **GIZ FAIR Forward** and **The
Future Society**, with an AI Readiness Assessment run with **UNESCO** (EU-funded).
Implementation is to be overseen by a dedicated Responsible AI Office.

**Definitive launch (2026-04).** President **John Dramani Mahama** unveiled the strategy as a **$270m**
investment — **$250m for a computing/AI centre + a $20m implementation fund** — to make Ghana West Africa's
AI hub (ecosystem horizon to ~2035, Responsible AI Office). Cabinet approved it Feb 2026; **KNUST's RAIL**
led the technical work (2022–25). Traces to a 2025 "Africa's AI hub in 4 years" vision (Minister Sam George).
See [[ghana--tech-ai]]. Sources: [[2026-04-24-mahama-unveils-10-year-ai-strategy-myjoy]],

## Activity in this wiki

- **Cabinet approval and launch date (2026-04-03).** Ghana's Cabinet approved the
  strategy and set a public launch for **24 April 2026**. The eight pillars span
  AI education, youth employment, digital infrastructure, data governance,
  ecosystem development, sectoral adoption, applied research and public-sector
  deployment; the seven priority sectors include healthcare, agriculture,
  financial services and energy. Minister for Communication, Digital Technology
  and Innovations [[samuel-nartey-george]] made the announcement. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[gov.standards]], [[tech.ai]].
  Source: [[2026-04-03 Ghana approves $250M AI Hub & National AI Strategy]].
- **$250m AI computing centre (2026-04).** Cabinet paired the strategy with an
  approved **$250 million national AI computing centre** to support AI research,
  development and deployment across agriculture, healthcare, education and
  financial services — a large capital commitment for an African government. See
  [[finance.new]], [[infra.store]].
  Source: [[2026-04-03 Ghana approves $250M AI Hub & National AI Strategy]].
- **Global AI Index ranking.** Ghana ranked **72nd** on the Global AI Index 2025
  (6th in Africa, behind Egypt, Mauritius, South Africa and Tunisia).
  Source: [[2026-04-03 Ghana approves $250M AI Hub & National AI Strategy]].
- **Huawei training deal (2026-03-05).** Alongside the $250m push, Ghana secured
  a commitment from [[huawei]] (agreed at MWC 2026, Barcelona) to give free AI
  training to **3,000 girls** in the government's Girls in ICT Programme, with
  graduates feeding the One Million Coders Programme. Talks also covered a 5G
  rollout, rural telephony and a smartphone-assembly plant. See
  [[capacity.training]], [[geopol.china]].
  Source: [[2026-03-05 Ghana strikes AI training deal with Chinese multinational amid $250m tech investment push]].

- **Cited among Africa's AI-governance building blocks (2026-05-04).** Nagadya situates Ghana's
  strategy among the national AI frameworks that, with the AU Continental AI Strategy and the Kigali
  [[africa-ai-declaration]], form Africa's emerging AI-governance architecture — sharing, she argues,
  one blind spot: the informal economy. Light analytical attach. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]].
  Source: [[2026-05-04 The World's Most Needed AI Governance Framework Doesn't Exist Yet. Africa Should Build It]].
