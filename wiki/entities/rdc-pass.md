# RDC-PASS (DR Congo national digital ID)

DR Congo's national digital-identity platform, slated to launch **13 June 2026**
in Kinshasa under President **[[felix-tshisekedi]]**, within the "DRC 2030 Digital
Nation" strategy.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Structure.** Built on Web 3.0 tech; four use cases — biometric SIM
  authentication, unified e-gov access, automated **e-KYC** for financial
  services, and secure digital IDs. Estimated cost **$97.1m**; part of a broader
  **$1bn** DRC digital plan 2026–2030. See [[COD]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-11 DR Congo to Launch National Digital Identity System RDC-PASS on June 13]].
- **The 20-year concession.** Operated under a **20-year PPP** (signed June 2025)
  with **[[trident-digital-tech]]** (Singapore), granting it **exclusive e-KYC
  rights for 20 years**.
- **Underpinning the digital-government stack (2026-01).** RDC-PASS is the identity
  layer beneath DRC's wider e-gov ambition: authorities aim for a functional
  **digital-government platform by March 2026**, plus a national interbank payment
  platform, online tax portal and customs system, within the 2026–2030 blueprint
  (needs ~**$10.4bn**; government committing ~$1.5bn; **[[world-bank]]** IDEA program
  ([[idea-mpa]]) + AFD among funders). See [[COD]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-30 DRC envisages a functional digital government platform by March]].

## Reads

- `colonialism` / `sovereignty`: a foundational national ID whose operation — and
  exclusive KYC rights — sit with a foreign private firm for two decades. The most
  externalised of the batch's national-ID cases; contrast Zambia's
  vendor-independence model ([[zambia--dpi-id]]) and Ethiopia's homegrown stack.
