# RDC-PASS (DR Congo national digital ID)

DR Congo's national digital-identity platform, **launched 13 June 2026** in a state ceremony in Kinshasa
under President **[[felix-tshisekedi]]** (confirmed independently by two outlets), within the "DRC 2030
Digital Nation" strategy.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Structure.** Built on Web 3.0 tech; four use cases — biometric SIM
  authentication, unified e-gov access, automated **e-KYC** for financial
  services, and secure digital IDs. Estimated cost **$97.1m**; part of a broader
  **$1bn** DRC digital plan 2026–2030. See [[COD]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-11 DR Congo to Launch National Digital Identity System RDC-PASS on June 13]].
- **The 20-year concession.** Operated under a **20-year PPP** (signed June 2025)
  with **[[trident-digital-tech]]** (Singapore), granting it **exclusive e-KYC
  rights for 20 years**.
- **Underpinning the digital-government stack (2026-01).** RDC-PASS is the identity
  layer beneath DRC's wider e-gov ambition: authorities aim for a functional
  **digital-government platform by March 2026**, plus a national interbank payment
  platform, online tax portal and customs system, within the 2026–2030 blueprint.
  The blueprint's official financing is the **[[drc-national-digital-plan-pnn2|PNN2]]
  $1.5bn government envelope** ($1bn treasury + $500m secured partners; **[[world-bank]]**
  IDEA ([[idea-mpa]]) + AFD among funders); the larger "~$10.4bn / €8.7bn total" once
  carried here **could not be corroborated** (REV-COD-001, resolved 2026-07-16). See
  [[COD]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-30 DRC envisages a functional digital government platform by March]].

## How it got here (2024–26)

- **Aug 2024 — the reset.** The DRC cancelled a **$1.2bn** national biometric-ID contract with **[[idemia]]** (via intermediary Afritech) after a Bloomberg/Lighthouse overbilling investigation ([[onip-drc]]). See [[2024-09-04 DR Congo cancels $1.2B IDEMIA-Afritech biometric ID contract]].
- **Dec 2024 → Jun 2025 — the pivot to Trident.** A collaboration framework with the Office of the President (Dec 2024) led to an implementation agreement (12 Feb 2025), a **validation phase** launched in Kinshasa (15 Mar 2025, on Trident's Tridentity blockchain platform), and the **definitive 20-year exclusive-e-KYC PPP** (25 Jun 2025). Phased deployment + public-education campaign underway by Aug 2025. Sources: [[2025-02-13 DR Congo signs Trident implementation agreement for national digital ID (DRCPass validation)]], [[2025-06-25 Trident and DR Congo sign final DRCPass PPP; nationwide deployment begins]].
- **A parallel card track.** Separately, the DRC is contracting **[[margins-id-group]]** (Ghana) to produce physical ID cards via [[onip-drc]] — so a Web3 digital-ID/e-KYC concession (Trident) and a physical-card programme (Margins) run at once.
- **Cost & scrutiny.** Capex put at **$97.1m** (Ministry of Planning; financing unclear, Jan 2026); an NTU-SBF analysis warned the 20-year foreign-exclusive concession risks controversy without transparency safeguards — sharpened by the DRC's **still-unoperational data-protection authority** ([[drc-digital-code]] Art. 262). See [[drc--dpi-id]]. Sources: [[2026-01-26 DRCPass digital ID priced at $97.1M, financing unclear]], [[2025-07-30 DR Congo gives Singapore firm exclusive digital-ID rights (NTU-SBF risk analysis)]].

## Reads

- `colonialism` / `sovereignty`: a foundational national ID whose operation — and
  exclusive KYC rights — sit with a foreign private firm for two decades. The most
  externalised of the batch's national-ID cases; contrast Zambia's
  vendor-independence model ([[zambia--dpi-id]]) and Ethiopia's homegrown stack.

- **Launch confirmed (13 Jun 2026).** Two independent outlets confirmed the state ceremony; the Ministry of
  Planning valued RDC-PASS at **$97.1m**, Trident raised only **$2.6m** for rollout, and phased plans open
  with **technical audits and pilot testing** (Trident also holds an **$800m Ghana JV**). A June-2025
  ministry *denial* of a "DRCPass launch" refers to the 2025 PPP signature, a different moment — not the
  2026 activation. Sources: [[2026-06-15-drc-launches-rdc-pass-20-year-ppp-trident-biometricupdate]],
  [[2026-06-19-trident-launches-rdc-pass-sovereign-digital-identity-techafricanews]].
