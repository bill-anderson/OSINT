# WAEMU / UEMOA (West African Economic and Monetary Union)

**The eight-member West African Economic and Monetary Union — rendered WAEMU in English and
UEMOA in French — whose common central bank is the [[bceao]].** As a bloc it is an **entity,
not a place** in this wiki. Its significance here is as a **directive-issuing body** whose
regional instruments drive national legislation and infrastructure in member states such as
Togo. See [[XWA]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Consumer protection directive (June 2023) — Togo aligning, not adopting.** WAEMU adopted
  a **regional directive on consumer protection in June 2023**. On **20–21 March 2025**
  stakeholders met in **Lomé** at a session organised by Togo's **Ministry of Commerce** and the
  **WAEMU Commission** to discuss it, aiming at a harmonised legal framework strengthening
  consumer rights across the eight member states. Discussion covered consumer information,
  product quality and dispute resolution. **Tankari Ibrahim** (WAEMU Commission) announced
  planned capacity-building programmes and a **regional consultative committee on consumer
  rights**; **Comlan Nomadoli Yakpey** (SG, Ministry of Commerce) stressed practical
  implementation. **This was a stakeholder workshop, not an adoption** — despite the source
  headline "Togo Adopts WAEMU Consumer Protection Framework", the body describes consultation
  and alignment, and no enacted Togolese instrument is evidenced. The directive concerns general
  consumer protection, not data or digital consumer rights. See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-24 Togo Adopts WAEMU Consumer Protection Framework]].
- **2023 UEMOA AML directive incorporated by Togo (2025-12).** Togo's new anti-money-laundering
  law ([[togo-aml-law-2025]]) brings national legislation into line with a **2023 UEMOA
  directive** alongside updated **[[fatf]]** recommendations — a worked example of the bloc's
  directives landing as national statute. See [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-30 Togo Adopts New Law to Strengthen Anti-Money Laundering Rules]].
- **Union-wide instant payments.** The BCEAO's [[waemu-instant-payment-platform]] (launched
  2025-09-30) covers the eight UEMOA states, with a **UEMOA banking federation** presided over
  (as of 2025-10) by Orabank Togo CEO Guy Martial Awona. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-01 BCEAO Launches Instant Payments Platform in Togo with Three Banks]].
