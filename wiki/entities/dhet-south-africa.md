# Department of Higher Education and Training (DHET), South Africa

South African government department responsible for universities, TVET and CET colleges, using
industry partnerships to close AI and digital-skills gaps against high youth unemployment.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Google digital-skills agreement (signed 2026-03-30).** DHET agreed with [[google]] to fund
  5,000 Google certification scholarships in AI and digital skills for top candidates across
  public universities, TVET and CET colleges, with special attention to rural and peri-urban
  communities. The partnership uses a train-the-trainer model for educators and covers
  professional certifications in high-demand fields — cybersecurity, data analytics and IT
  support — plus Google-supplied digital tools and IT-infrastructure support. The agreement,
  overseen by Deputy Minister Mimmy Gondwe, was signed at Google's Johannesburg (Bryanston)
  offices. Context: SA youth (15-34) unemployment ~46.1% in Q1 2025 ([[world-bank]] frames the
  skills-access divide). Tags [[capacity.training]], [[tech.ai]], [[include.divides]] in [[ZAF]].
  Source: [[2026-03-30 South Africa Teams Up With Google to Train 5,000 in Digital Skills]].
