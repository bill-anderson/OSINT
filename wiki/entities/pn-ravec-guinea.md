# PN-RAVEC / RNPP & biometric electoral file (Guinea)

Guinea's **administrative census with civil-registry purpose** — the **Programme National de Recensement
Administratif à Vocation d'État Civil (PN-RAVEC)** — launched **15 April 2025** (MATD; toll-free line **105**,
24/7). It feeds the **Registre National des Personnes Physiques (RNPP)**, which the state has made the **single
official source** for a **permanent biometric electoral file** — pitched as ending Guinea's history of electoral
disputes. President Doumbouya separately **decreed the biometric national ID card free** for all first
applications (Feb 2025).

## Scale (dated)

- **Provisional (Jul 2025):** **9,121,500 recensed → 9,010,191 on the RNPP → 6,748,923 biometric electors**.
- **Definitive (Aug 2025):** **6,768,458 electors** ahead of the **21 Sept 2025 referendum**.
- **Updated (Apr 2026):** **6,948,894 electors / 23,681 polling stations** for the **31 May 2026** coupled
  legislative/communal elections.
- **RGPH-4 (via PN-RAVEC, Mar 2026):** resident population **17,521,167** (Conakry 3,407,327).

## Institutionalisation

- **Online verification.** The **DGE** launched **verification.dge.gov.gn** (32-char code / QR) for voters to
  check their RNPP data (Aug 2025).
- **DGE–ONECI convention (2026-01).** Institutionalises **RNPP → electoral-file** data-sharing, with the RNPP
  as the "single, official, reliable" identification source (DGE DG Djenabou Touré). See [[oneci-guinea]].
- **Secured civil-registry intrants (2026-03).** PN-RAVEC equipped Conakry's 13 communes with secured
  birth/marriage/non-remarriage/death certificates — modernising a very weak civil-registry base.

See [[guinea--dpi-id]].
