# GITEX Africa (Morocco)

**Africa's largest technology and startup event**, held annually in **Marrakech** under royal patronage —
organised by the Ministry of Digital Transition with the **[[add-morocco|Digital Development Agency]]** and
KAOUN International. Morocco uses it as a **digital-investment gateway** and a stage for "African
sovereignty meets global innovation." See [[tech.industry]], [[MAR]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **2025 (3rd edition, Apr).** [[gov.policy]] Marrakech; Minister Seghrouchni framed the digital economy at
  **15% of global GDP ($6.5tn)** with ~40% of participating startups integrating AI; a focus on African
  DPI and emerging AI ecosystems (IFC an Economic Development Partner). Sources:
  [[2025-03-18-techafricanews-gitex-africa-morocco-2025]], [[2025-04-14-en-hespress-morocco-gitex-africa-2025-kickoff]].
- **2026 (4th edition, 7–9 Apr).** [[finance.new]] [[sovereignty]] **1,450+ exhibitors, 130+ countries**;
  positioned as an African digital-investment gateway (AI could add **$1.2tn** to Africa by 2030); ADD DG
  Amine El Mezouaghi. Source: [[2026-03-31-en-hespress-morocco-gitex-gateway-digital-investment]].
- **2026 deal round-up.** [[finance.mou]] Signings included **Inwi–China Mobile** (Morocco's first
  industrial private 5G, Nador 52-ha site), **Orange Maroc–Ericsson** (private-5G enterprise lab), the
  **[[nexus-ai-factory|Nexus AI Factory]]** ($1.2bn), Comba–Sicotel indoor coverage, and a **Huawei**
  gaming-industry MoU. Source: [[2026-04-10-connectingafrica-gitex-africa-2026-morocco-deals]].
- **D4SD pavilion (2026).** [[gov.policy]] PM **Akhannouch**, Minister Seghrouchni and UNDP inaugurated the
  **Digital for Sustainable Development (D4SD)** pavilion — digital/AI for inclusive development across
  Morocco, Africa and the Arab region. Source: [[2026-04-10-techafricanews-morocco-d4sd-pavilion-gitex]].
