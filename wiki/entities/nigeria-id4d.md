# Nigeria ID4D (Identity for Development)

**Deal / financing operation.** Nigeria's Identity for Development initiative — the
external financing behind the [[nimc]] NIN expansion — funded by the **[[world-bank]]**,
the **French Development Agency (AFD)** and the **European Investment Bank (EIB)**, and
restructured (with a two-year extension noted in late 2024) to drive toward full
population coverage.

## Activity in this wiki

- **NIN scale-up (2026-07).** Underpins the drive from **136M** NIN registrations toward
  the **180M target by 31 Dec 2026**, and the shift from NIN as a mere identity token to
  an enabler of public- and private-sector service access, backed by the new
  [[nimc]] Act 2026. See [[NGA]], [[dpi.id]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]], [[finance.new]].
- **Project scale (2026-06).** The Nigeria Digital Identification for Development project (World Bank + AFD + EIB) totals **$430m**; initiated 2020, restructured 2024 (two-year extension), tied to SDG 16.9 — scaffolding the drive to 180M NIN by end-2026. Source: [[2026-06-05 Nigeria looks beyond enrollment as digital ID passes 130 million]].
- **Project mandate (2025-02, background).** The World Bank-financed operation set the
  requirement to issue **≥180m digital IDs by end-2026**; SGF George Akume, also ID4D
  ecosystem steering-committee chair, pressed government agencies to collaborate with
  [[nimc]] to lift NIN adoption. See [[dpi.id]], [[NGA]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-13 Digital ID necessity increases in Nigeria despite lingering challenges]].
- **Gender-equity gap in the financed rollout (2026-01).** The externally-financed NIN drive
  shows a stark gender skew — only **38.8% of enrolled NIN holders are women** (NIMC, June
  2025) — with rural Niger State served by just 21 centres and cost/mobility barriers; a
  [[nimc]]–[[world-bank]] study flags awareness, mobility and biometric-capture gaps,
  underscoring that headline coverage targets (180M by end-2026) can mask exclusion of rural
  women from DPI-gated services. See [[dpi.id]], [[include.divides]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]].
  Source: [[2026-01-28 The Gender Gap in Rural Niger State's Digital Identity]].
- **Enrolment numbers vs functional identity (2026-03).** The $430m facility (initially targeting
  **148m** enrolments, extended to Dec 2026) is criticised for rewarding raw enrolment over usable
  identity: with ~94m still unenrolled and device-locked correction failures leaving even the
  "enrolled" functionally excluded, advocates argue the World Bank should condition support on
  **human-rights impact assessments, digital-exclusion audits, grievance mechanisms and mandatory
  offline alternatives** rather than headline coverage figures. See [[include.access]], [[gov.protect]],
  [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-07 Nigerians Groan Under Digital Identity Regime That Turns Basic Rights into Privileges]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: a foundational national identity system under the "one person, one
  identity" policy.
- `colonialism`: that system is externally financed (World Bank / AFD / EIB) — the
  recurring externally-scaffolded-sovereignty tension (cf. [[zambia--dpi-id]]).
