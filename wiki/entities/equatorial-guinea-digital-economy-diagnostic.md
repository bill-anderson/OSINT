# Equatorial Guinea Digital Economy Diagnostic (World Bank)

The World Bank's **Digital Economy Country Diagnostic** for Equatorial Guinea (World Bank 2023; released June 2024, with Digital Development Partnership support) — the reference assessment of the country's digital economy across the five **DE4A** pillars (infrastructure, public platforms, DFS, businesses, skills), framed as a lever for diversification away from oil. Reference figures — cite, don't absorb. See [[GNQ]], [[adige]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Key findings.** [[dpi.id]] Broadband affordability improving but limited competition and cyber weaknesses persist; a **digital civil-registry pilot launched 2018** exists but **digital identity is not yet implemented**; DFS are nascent with **no licensed e-money/mobile-money operator**; the digital business ecosystem is emerging. A prior-year biometric enrollment drive verified public-servant payroll eligibility.
- **Priority recommendations.** [[gov.policy]] [[dpi.pay]] Cost-oriented wholesale-fibre obligations; a cybersecurity maturity assessment; a national digital-public-platforms strategy; **streamline the digital civil registry + implement a digital identity ecosystem**; develop digital-payment infrastructure (whole-of-government); adopt a national ICT education policy; and establish a **currently-absent national interoperability framework**. Cross-cutting: data quality, coordination, electricity, legal framework, and the island-continental + gender divides.
