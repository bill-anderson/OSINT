**Parties:** [[google]] (direct equity investor), the U.S. International Development Finance Corporation ([[us-dfc]]), and Finnish development-finance company Finnfund, investing into [[cassava-technologies]].

**What:** A **$90 million equity tranche** into Cassava Technologies, in which Google took a **direct equity stake** in the pan-African infrastructure group. The equity round was part of a broader **$310 million** financing package — the remaining ~$220m was a credit facility to refinance a South African rand term loan for a Cassava subsidiary — announced alongside a wider corporate reorganisation.

**When / where:** Announced **17 December 2024** (backfilled item). Cassava operates across Africa (data centres, fibre, renewable energy, cloud, cybersecurity) plus growth markets in the MiddEast, India and Latin America.

**Value:** $90m equity (Google + DFC + Finnfund); $310m total package.

**Status:** Completed / announced Dec 2024.

## Why it matters

Google framed the stake around bolstering African connectivity — Cassava is a partner on Google's **Umoja** cable, and its [[liquid-c2]] / Liquid Intelligent Technologies unit built Umoja's **terrestrial segment**, running from Kenya through the DRC, Rwanda, Uganda, Zambia and Zimbabwe to South Africa. Africa's 2024 wave of subsea-cable outages sharpened Google's incentive to fund resilient infrastructure serving its own cloud demand. The investment also sits within Google's earlier **$1 billion commitment** to Africa's digital economy, and connects to the separate [[liquid-c2-google-cloud-mou]] cloud/AI/cybersecurity partnership.

Cassava CEO Hardy Pemhiwa called it "a pivotal milestone" expected to "unlock immense value and catalyze the further expansion of our digital infrastructure." Read through a **sovereignty** lens, a US hyperscaler taking a direct stake in a flagship African infrastructure group is a notable ownership signal.
