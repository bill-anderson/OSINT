# Eswatini Payment Switch (EPS)

**Eswatini's domestic interoperable payment switch**, sponsored by the
[[central-bank-of-eswatini]] under the National Payment Systems Strategy 2022–2025,
built by [[paylogic]] with [[world-bank]] technical assistance. Delivered in **three
phases: Fast Payments (first), then Open Banking, then Point of Sale and ATM
Switching**. See [[SWZ]], [[dpi.pay]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Fast Payments launched 2024-12-11 at Ezulwini.** Governor [[phil-mnisi]] launched
  the Fast Payments product — real-time payments across all payment service providers,
  banks and non-banks — marking completion of phase one of three. *(The launch date is
  2024-12-11; the BIS Review carrying the speech published 2025-01-14. Date the
  development, not the publication.)* See [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-14 Phil Mnisi - Eswatini Payment Switch Fast Payments product launch]].
- **Participants at launch.** **Three of eight** integrating participants offered Fast
  Payment services on launch day **(2024-12-11)** — **Eswatini Bank, Swaziland Building
  Society and E-mali Limited**. Participation is mandated for all PSPs offering local
  payments; the remainder were expected to go live **by end-January 2025**. The wiki
  holds no source confirming whether they did — a currency gap. See [[dpi.exchange]].
  Source:
  [[2025-01-14 Phil Mnisi - Eswatini Payment Switch Fast Payments product launch]].
- **Transaction limits (2024-12-11).** Maximum **E50,000** per single transaction from
  bank accounts, **E10,000** per transaction for digital wallets, and a **E50,000**
  daily sender limit. Transactions run over existing digital channels — cellphone
  banking, app, online banking. A consumer-education campaign was scheduled to begin
  the first week of **February 2025**, reaching urban, peri-urban and rural
  communities. See [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-14 Phil Mnisi - Eswatini Payment Switch Fast Payments product launch]].
- **Under-used, on the World Bank's reading (2025-08-05).** Financial inclusion "will
  gain more traction if the digital payment system (National Payment Switch) is fully
  utilized"; **more than 90% of People-to-Business payments remained cash-based (as of
  2025-08-05)**. Building the rails has not yet shifted the behaviour. Source:
  [[2025-08-05 Robust DPI inevitable for Eswatini's economic, jobs boom - World Bank]].
