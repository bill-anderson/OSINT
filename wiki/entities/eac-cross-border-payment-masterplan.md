# EAC Cross-Border Payment System Masterplan

The East African Community's regional programme to build **interoperable payment
infrastructure** capable of secure, real-time, lower-cost transactions across the bloc's
member states — interlinking national instant-payment systems rather than imposing a single
centralised rail. Distinct from Afreximbank's continental [[papss]]; this is the **[[eac]]**
regional switch initiative.

## Profile

- Advances under the EAC's **"variable geometry"** principle — groups of members can move at
  different speeds. As of mid-2026 only **Rwanda and Tanzania** are in the first proof of
  concept.
- **First corridor (as of 2026-07-10):** a week-long technical meeting in Zanzibar advanced a
  proof of concept interlinking Tanzania's **[[tips-tanzania]]** (TIPS) and Rwanda's
  **[[rswitch-rwanda]]** (RSwitch) — the intended first operational cross-border
  instant-payment corridor under the masterplan, initially for person-to-person transfers; no
  launch date announced.
- Work covers governance (joint steering committees, SLAs), transaction pricing, FX
  conversion, revenue-sharing, and a common risk/compliance framework (settlement risk,
  fraud, cybersecurity, business continuity).
- Partners present: central banks of Rwanda/Tanzania, the EAC Secretariat, the
  **[[world-bank]]** and **[[giz]]**. Chair: Nestory Nicholas Maro (Bank of Tanzania).
