# Ministry of Finance, Budget and Digital Economy (Burundi)

Burundi's lead ministry for the **digital economy** (the "Économie numérique" portfolio sits with
Finance), Minister **Alain Ndikumana**. It anchors most of Burundi's flagship digital initiatives — the
national **AI strategy**, the **e-KORI** tax platform, the [[pafen-burundi|PAFEN]] rural-connectivity
contracts, and exploratory **[[india-stack|India Stack]]** cooperation — all framed around Burundi's
**Vision 2040 (emerging) / 2060 (developed)** and macroeconomic-stabilisation reforms. The
[[setic-burundi|SETIC]] executive secretariat sits under this cluster. See [[BDI]], [[gov.policy]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **National AI Strategy 2025/26–2030 (validated 2026-04-22).** [[tech.ai]] With [[undp]] support, the
  ministry validated a six-pillar AI strategy (governance/ethics, infrastructure & sovereign cloud,
  education/research, innovation & startups, human capital — train >1,000 officials, ~15 health/
  agriculture pilots). Preceded by an **AILA/EPIA diagnostic workshop** (25–27 Nov 2025). Sources:
  [[2026-04-22-lia-au-coeur-de-la-strategie-numerique-burundi]],
  [[2026-04-23-burundi-six-pillar-ai-strategy-wearetech]],
  [[2025-11-25-atelier-national-paysage-intelligence-artificielle-burundi]].
- **e-KORI tax platform (2026-01).** [[dpi.govtech]] Minister Ndikumana presided over the launch of
  **e-KORI** ([[obr-burundi|OBR]]) to mobilise domestic revenue "without relying exclusively on external
  aid." Source: [[2026-01-20-burundi-revenue-authority-e-kori-digital-tax-platform]].
- **India Stack cooperation (2026-06).** [[dpi.id]] [[geopol.india]] Ministry adviser **Isidora
  Ntakiyiruta** met India's ambassador to explore adapting **[[india-stack]]** (Aadhaar-style ID + UPI
  payments) to Burundi. Sources: [[2026-06-30-burundi-india-stack-digital-id-payments-biometricupdate]],
  [[2026-06-23-burundi-studies-india-stack-digital-id-payments]].
- **IFC engagement (2025-02).** [[finance.new]] SETIC (under the ministry) met an [[ifc]] delegation on
  PPP financing for digital projects, amid Burundi ranking **46th of 47** African states on the ITU 2024
  ICT Development Index. Source: [[2025-02-12-burundi-seeks-ifc-support-for-digital-projects-wearetech]].
