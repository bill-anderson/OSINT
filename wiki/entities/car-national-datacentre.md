# CAR national data centre (Huawei / Greenline / AIAL / AfDB threads)

The wiki's standing line — CAR has **"no national data centre"** — remains true as of **2026-07-20**:
**nothing is built**. But it sits atop **four competing threads** with **three different geopolitical
sponsors — China, the United States and Rwanda — circling the same unbuilt facility.** A national data
centre is pitched in each case as the platform for whole-of-government data consolidation and "data
sovereignty." See [[CAF]], [[infra.store]], [[caf--dpi-govtech]].

## The four threads

- **Huawei Tier III — the live thread (2026-05).** [[geopol.china]] China-backed, discussed with a Huawei
  delegation (26 May 2026; directeur de cabinet **Adolphe Nicaise Samafou**), on **four axes**: (1) a
  **national Tier III data centre** for availability/security/**data sovereignty**; (2) a **dedicated
  network of five microwave-relay sites** interconnecting ministries; (3) e-government modernisation; and
  (4) — the axis the Ecofin copy omits — a **secure eLTE radio network for the police**. Huawei building
  the data centre *and* the security radio is the sovereignty story. Sources:
  [[2026-05-27-huawei-tier-iii-data-centre-egov-modernisation-agenceecofin]],
  [[2026-06-03-huawei-egovernance-partnership-four-axes-mbetimedia]].
- **Greenline / SOCATEL Tier 3 — the US thread (2025-09-18).** [[geopol.usa]] [[finance.mou]] An MoU for
  the **privatisation and transformation of [[socatel]]**, awarded to **Greenline Technologies Inc.**
  (US, Cleveland OH), signed **18 Sep 2025 in Casablanca** before **[[faustin-archange-touadera]]** at the
  **Ambition28 / PND 2024-2028 financing roundtable**. A **$150m** initial investment to build "a national
  digital hub at the heart of Africa, complete with a new **Tier 3 data center**" plus a national
  connectivity ecosystem and cybersecurity frameworks. Moved to an **operational phase on 16 Jul 2026**
  (Digital Economy Minister **Roger Andjalandji** met Greenline VP of operations Max Sicari) — but the
  definitive takeover agreements, joint steering committee and deployment schedule are **still unfinalised**,
  and Ecofin notes the deal structure and financing modalities are **not yet determinable**. This is the
  origin of the previously-uncited "SOCATEL National Digital Hub" dataset line. Sources:
  [[2025-09-18-greenline-socatel-privatisation-mou-greenline]],
  [[2026-07-17-socatel-greenline-relance-150m-agenceecofin]].
- **AIAL Group MoU (2025-01-21) — dormant.** [[finance.mou]] An MoU (explicitly **not yet a contract**) with
  **AIAL Group Limited** (Kigali, Rwanda; VP Dr Nurudin Mithani) for a data centre + satellite internet,
  described by Minister **Gourna-Zacko** as a whole-of-government platform tracing casier judiciaire,
  certificat de nationalité, diplomas, birth acts and revenue *quittances*. The source is explicit that the
  ministry must first examine AIAL's dossier and take it to the Council of Ministers before any contract.
  **No contract, Council-of-Ministers approval or construction record exists as of 2026-07-20** — the thread
  appears dormant. (Reconcile pass 2026-07-20 confirmed the Oubangui Médias account is a domestic original,
  not aggregator-only.) Source: [[2025-01-22-aial-group-data-centre-mou-oubanguimedias]].
- **AfDB/CAB "Datacenter principal et de secours" — never delivered.** The AfDB's own inauguration release
  for the CAR national fibre backbone (**event 6 Feb 2023**) enumerates the delivered scope: **935 km** of
  terrestrial and sub-fluvial fibre, a **digital training centre and incubator at the University of Bangui**,
  a **cybersecurity and e-certification platform**, and technical assistance to create the Agence
  centrafricaine de développement du digital. **No data centre appears in that scope**, and no award or
  commissioning record exists as of **2026-07-20**. Treated as a **dated negative**, not an open unknown.
  (Corbeau News, 2025-04-29, gives 900 km / €33m; AfDB is the announcer of record and is preferred.)

## What this is *not*

**World Bank project P174620 is not a data-centre project, and is not CARDIP.** P174620 is the bilateral
**CAR Public Sector Digital Governance Project** (US$35m IDA grant, approved 5 May 2022, closing 31 Dec 2026;
+US$6.4m additional financing in May 2025 for a mining-governance component, total US$41.4m). Its four
original components cover public-resources management, digital transformation of public administration,
implementation support and a contingent emergency window — **none of them a data centre**. It belongs to the
govtech and data-protection record (it is the project behind [[car-data-protection-law-24-001]] and the
April 2025 état-civil diagnostic), not here. **[[cardip]]** is a separate regional six-country program.
(Established by the reconcile pass, 2026-07-20.)
