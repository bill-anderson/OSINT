# CAR national data centre (Huawei / Greenline / AIAL / AfDB threads)

The wiki's standing line — CAR has **"no national data centre"** — remains true as of **2026-07-20**:
**nothing is built**. But it sits atop **five threads** with **three different geopolitical sponsors —
China, the United States and Rwanda — circling the same unbuilt facility**, plus a **donor pair from 2022
that was already "under construction" and never surfaced again**. A national data centre is pitched in each
case as the platform for whole-of-government data consolidation and "data sovereignty." See [[CAF]],
[[infra.store]], [[caf--dpi-govtech]].

The recurring pattern is not absence of intent but **absence of completion**: from 2022 onward every thread
reaches announcement, MoU or appraisal and stops short of a commissioning record.

## The threads

- **Huawei Tier III — the live thread (2026-05).** [[geopol.china]] China-backed, discussed with a Huawei
  delegation (26 May 2026; directeur de cabinet **Adolphe Nicaise Samafou**), on **four axes**: (1) a
  **national Tier III data centre** for availability/security/**data sovereignty**; (2) a **dedicated
  network of five microwave-relay sites** interconnecting ministries; (3) e-government modernisation; and
  (4) — the axis the Ecofin copy omits — a **secure eLTE radio network for the police**. Huawei building
  the data centre *and* the security radio is the sovereignty story. Sources:
  [[2026-05-27-huawei-tier-iii-data-centre-egov-modernisation-agenceecofin]],
  [[2026-06-03-huawei-egovernance-partnership-four-axes-mbetimedia]].
- **Greenline / SOCATEL Tier 3 — the US thread (2025-09-18).** [[geopol.usa]] [[finance.mou]] An MoU for
  the **privatisation and transformation of [[socatel]]**, awarded to **Greenline Technologies Inc.**
  (US, Cleveland OH), signed **18 Sep 2025 in Casablanca** before **[[faustin-archange-touadera]]** at the
  **Ambition28 / PND 2024-2028 financing roundtable**. A **$150m** initial investment to build "a national
  digital hub at the heart of Africa, complete with a new **Tier 3 data center**" plus a national
  connectivity ecosystem and cybersecurity frameworks. Moved to an **operational phase on 16 Jul 2026**
  (Digital Economy Minister **Roger Andjalandji** met Greenline VP of operations Max Sicari) — but the
  definitive takeover agreements, joint steering committee and deployment schedule are **still unfinalised**,
  and Ecofin notes the deal structure and financing modalities are **not yet determinable**. This is the
  origin of the previously-uncited "SOCATEL National Digital Hub" dataset line. Sources:
  [[2025-09-18-greenline-socatel-privatisation-mou-greenline]],
  [[2026-07-17-socatel-greenline-relance-150m-agenceecofin]].
- **AIAL Group MoU (2025-01-21) — dormant.** [[finance.mou]] An MoU (explicitly **not yet a contract**) with
  **AIAL Group Limited** (Kigali, Rwanda; VP Dr Nurudin Mithani) for a data centre + satellite internet,
  described by Minister **Gourna-Zacko** as a whole-of-government platform tracing casier judiciaire,
  certificat de nationalité, diplomas, birth acts and revenue *quittances*. The source is explicit that the
  ministry must first examine AIAL's dossier and take it to the Council of Ministers before any contract.
  **No contract, Council-of-Ministers approval or construction record exists as of 2026-07-20** — the thread
  appears dormant. (Reconcile pass 2026-07-20 confirmed the Oubangui Médias account is a domestic original,
  not aggregator-only.) Source: [[2025-01-22-aial-group-data-centre-mou-oubanguimedias]].
- **AfDB/CAB "Datacenter principal et de secours" — never delivered.** The AfDB's own inauguration release
  for the CAR national fibre backbone (**event 6 Feb 2023**; release carried 8 Feb 2023) enumerates the
  delivered scope: **935 km** of terrestrial and sub-fluvial fibre, a **digital training centre and incubator
  at the University of Bangui**, a **cybersecurity and e-certification platform**, and technical assistance
  to the MDEPT to create the **Agence centrafricaine de développement du digital**. **No data centre appears
  in that scope**, and no award or commissioning record exists as of **2026-07-20**. Treated as a **dated
  negative**, not an open unknown. Source (now held):
  [[2023-02-08-afdb-inauguration-dorsale-optique-nationale-rca]].
  *(A divergent backbone figure of 900 km / €33m is attributed to Corbeau News, 2025-04-29, but **that source
  is not on file** — no Corbeau News item of that date is held in `raw/`, so the divergence rests on an
  unheld citation. The AfDB is in any case the announcer of record and its 935 km is preferred.)*

- **The 2022 pair that predates all four — two government data centres "under construction".**
  [[infra.store]] The World Bank's own **PAD4661** (7 Mar 2022) records that, at appraisal, **two government
  data centres were under construction**: one by the **Ministry of Finance and Budget**, to host all internal
  PFM systems; one by the **Ministry of Digital Economy, Posts and Telecommunications (MDEPT)**, intended to
  host systems and data from *any* public or private institution — the national-facility role. The MDEPT
  facility was specified as a **Tier II build in Phase 1** with dual connection to the Bangui fibre loop, and
  a **Phase 2 extension to Tier III** contingent on further funding; the PAD states it **"still needs
  co-financing to be successfully delivered and operationalized"**. The Bank's diagnosis was that government
  "lacks a strategic approach" to cost sharing, energy efficiency, cybersecurity and backup, and lacks any
  shared architecture or interoperability layer. **No completion or commissioning record for either facility
  is held as of 2026-07-20** — and the 2025 additional-financing paper's account of what the project achieved
  makes no mention of either. This is the earliest documented CAR national-data-centre ambition the wiki
  holds, and it makes the 2026 Huawei "Tier III" pitch a **repeat of a 2022 MDEPT Tier III target**, not a
  new idea. Sources: [[2022-03-07-worldbank-pad4661-car-public-sector-digital-governance]],
  [[2025-05-21-worldbank-project-paper-af-car-public-sector-digital-governance]].

## What this is *not*

**World Bank project P174620 is not a data-centre project, and is not CARDIP.** This is now established from
the **project documents themselves**, not inferred. P174620 is the bilateral **CAR Public Sector Digital
Governance Project (PSDGP)** — an **SDR 25.1m / US$35m IDA grant**, appraised **7 Mar 2022** and approved
**5 May 2022**, closing **31 Dec 2026**, implementing agency the **Ministry of Finance and Budget**; a further
**SDR 4.8m / US$6.4m** additional grant was appraised **21 May 2025** for a mining-governance component,
taking the total to **US$41.4m**.

Its component structure is fully captured in the 2025 project paper's cost table, so the negative is
**documented, not merely unobserved**: Component 1 public-resources management (US$17.0m), Component 2
digital transformation of public administration and service delivery (US$15.0m — sub-components 2.1
connectivity foundations US$6.0m, 2.2 digital-government foundations US$5.5m, 2.3 access to select public
services US$3.5m), Component 3 implementation support (US$3.5m after AF), Component 4 contingent emergency
response (US$0.0m), Component 5 mining-sector governance (US$5.9m). **No component or sub-component is a data
centre.** It belongs to the govtech and identity record, not here. **[[cardip]]** is a separate regional
six-country program. Sources:
[[2022-03-07-worldbank-pad4661-car-public-sector-digital-governance]],
[[2025-05-21-worldbank-project-paper-af-car-public-sector-digital-governance]].

**Caution on the reading, though.** "P174620 funds no data centre" is *not* the same as "the World Bank had
no data-centre involvement": the same PAD documents the two ministry facilities above as under construction
in 2022 and names co-financing as the MDEPT facility's blocker. The project declined to build one while
recording that two were being built around it.
