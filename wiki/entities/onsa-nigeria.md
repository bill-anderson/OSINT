# Office of the National Security Adviser (ONSA, Nigeria)

ONSA is not a digital regulator, but it recurs at every seam of Nigeria's digital-governance
architecture — infrastructure protection, identity enforcement, cyber-law and now virtual assets.
The wiki holds it as **the security node that keeps being written into civilian digital mandates**,
which is what makes it load-bearing rather than merely named.

- **Telecom infrastructure as security asset (2025-10-08).** ONSA works with the **NCC** and the
  Federal Ministry of Communications, Innovation and Digital Economy on measures to protect telecom
  assets, against a backdrop of **~19,000 fibre cuts in eight months**. Source:
  [[2025-10-08-ncc-19000-fibre-cuts-eight-months]].
- **Written into the digital-economy bill (2025-11-10).** The NITDA-backed Digital Economy and
  E-Governance Bill proposes a **National Digital Cooperation and Interoperability Framework**
  formalising coordination among NITDA, the NCC, the **[[ndpc]]** and ONSA. Source:
  [[2025-11-11-digital-economy-egovernance-bill-nitda]].
- **The accountability question (2026-02-27).** Analysis of Nigeria's digital-rights governance
  places ONSA inside the cybersecurity and intelligence architecture operating alongside
  **Cybercrimes Act** enforcement — the point at which regulatory authority and security power
  overlap without, on that account, visible deterrence on the rights side. *Attributed analysis, not
  settled fact.* Source:
  [[2026-02-27 Who Protects Nigeria's Digital Citizens Unmasking Accountability Gaps in Digital Rights Governance]].
- **Identity enforcement (2026-07-09).** NIMC's enrolment readout carries an enforcement payload —
  ICE Unit reactivation and prosecution powers exercised with security agencies and the Federal
  Ministry of Justice, in **cooperation with ONSA**. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 NIMC Enrols 136 Million Nigerians as Coker-Odusote Pushes Stronger Inter-Ministerial Partnership]].
- **Virtual Asset Council member (2026-07-20).** Seated on the Council created by the
  [[virtual-assets-executive-order-2026|Executive Order on Virtual Assets Coordination]] alongside
  the **[[nfiu|NFIU]]**. Source:
  [[2026-07-20-nigeria-coordinated-regulatory-framework-virtual-assets]].
