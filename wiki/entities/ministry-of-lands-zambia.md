# Ministry of Lands and Natural Resources (MLNR), Zambia

**Zambia's land ministry** — the policy and service owner for land administration, title
deeds and ground rent, and the client ministry for **[[zilas]]**, the land-administration
system deployed for it by **[[smart-zambia-institute]]**'s E-Government Division. It is the
sectoral counterpart in land to what [[dnrpc-zambia]] is in population registration: SZI
builds and hosts the platform, MLNR runs the service.

**Provenance, corrected 2026-07-20.** The wiki previously held MLNR's digitisation only
through **SMART Zambia's own portal pages** — the implementing agency's account of its client
ministry. Two operator-independent accounts have since been added: the **parliamentary
record** (2023-06-16) and a **Zambian law firm's practitioner note** (2023-09-19). Still **no
independent evaluation or audit** of MLNR's digitisation is held (as of 2026-07-20).

## Activity in this wiki

- **The ministerial statement to the National Assembly (2023-06-16).** Minister of Lands and
  Natural Resources **Elijah Muchima** dated [[zilas]] from the floor: **Cabinet approved it
  in 2020**, data migrated from **ZILMIS on 13 May 2023**, and **ZILAS went live 17 May
  2023** — **sixteen months before** the SZI portal pages this page previously rested on.
  **Sixty e-services** were placed on the Government Service Bus (the minister tabling a list
  distinguishing those "working" from those "**work in progress**"), a **Zampass account on
  [[zamportal]] is required to reach them**, and clients who filed before migration had a
  **cut-off of 17 June 2023**. The ministry's own admission about the predecessor is the
  finding: ZILMIS ran **nine years (2014–2023)** and its **maintenance contract with the
  developer expired 31 March 2022**, after which MLNR kept running the national land register
  **with no contract** — "we were at risk in the event of system challenges" — nine years
  "without enhancements and inclusion of online payment platforms thereby creating a
  **conduit for corruption**." Muchima also answered public complaint that "the ZILAS has
  crushed [crashed]", conceding **teething challenges**, a **backlog team deployed offsite**,
  and public apprehension at mandatory data-cleaning. See [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2023-06-16-zambia-zilas-ministerial-statement-parliament]].
- **The practitioner account (2023-09-19).** **Corpus Legal Practitioners** set out
  **"Digital Clearance"**, the mandatory preliminary step MLNR introduced with ZILAS —
  ministry verification of the details on a certificate of title, and **"no land transaction
  can take place until this process is completed."** Submission takes **30 minutes to 1 day**,
  but **"the timeframe of approval… is currently uncertain. In practice, it takes
  approximately three (3) to four (4) weeks"** (2023-09). Against SZI's "eliminating queues"
  framing, the queue was **relocated**, not removed — and an opaque three-to-four-week
  approval became a precondition of **every land transaction in the country**. See
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2023-09-19-zambia-zilas-digital-clearance-corpus-legal]].

- **"Goes digital" (SZI, 2024-09-13).** MLNR concluded a **four-day exhibition** at
  **Cosmopolitan Mall, Lusaka (10–13 September 2024)**, **attending to over 250 clients**.
  ICT Officer **Ms. Gwendoline Hamabele** described the ministry as having **transitioned to
  digital platforms for all its services**: a **Zamportal account is now a prerequisite to
  pay ground rent**, **title deeds are now digitally issued**, and services run over the
  **Government Service Bus (gsb.gov.zm)**, which hosts [[zilas]]. Framed by the ministry and
  SZI as efficiency, transparency and accessibility gains — a claim, not a measurement.
  Source: [[2024-09-13 Ministry of Lands and Natural Resources Goes Digital (SMART Zambia)]].
- **Platform (2024-09).** [[zilas]] — deployed by SZI's Electronic Government Division,
  100% digital, web and mobile. Source:
  [[2024-09-13 ZILAS - Your Gateway to Modern Land Administration (SMART Zambia)]].
