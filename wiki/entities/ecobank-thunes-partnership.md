# Ecobank–Thunes Instant Cross-Border Payments Partnership

**Parties:** **[[ecobank]]** Group, the Lomé-headquartered pan-African bank (CEO **Jeremy
Awori**), and **[[thunes]]**, the Singaporean fintech (CEO **Peter De Caluwe**). **What:** an
instant cross-border transfer service letting Ecobank customers — individuals and businesses —
send and receive money in real time from or to **more than 130 countries**, powered by Thunes'
global **Direct network** reaching **over seven billion mobile wallets and bank accounts**.
**When/where:** launched in **Togo**, reported **2025-10-31**; **Togo is the first market**, the
start of a continental rollout eventually covering Ecobank's **35 markets**. **Status:** active.
See [[TGO]], [[finance.mou]], [[dpi.pay]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Use cases and framing.** The service is aimed at household and business transactions,
  including **family remittances and cross-border trade payments**. Awori: "This partnership
  aligns with Ecobank's mission to provide borderless banking services and support financial
  inclusion in Africa." De Caluwe: "Together, we provide quicker and more reliable access to
  liquidity and open new growth opportunities." See [[include.access]], [[tech.industry]].
  Source: [[2025-10-31 Ecobank launches instant international payment service in Togo]].
- **Distinct from the BCEAO platform.** This is a **bank-led product on private rails**, not
  the [[bceao]]'s regional [[waemu-instant-payment-platform]]. The same report notes Ecobank is
  separately "among the three banks in Togo testing the BCEAO's instant payment system, known as
  PI." Togo thus hosts two parallel instant-payment tracks — one regional and public, one
  corporate and global — a distinction worth preserving rather than collapsing. Source:
  [[2025-10-31 Ecobank launches instant international payment service in Togo]].

**Coverage note.** No transaction value, fee structure, contract term or rollout schedule
beyond "Togo first" is reported in the single account held here.
