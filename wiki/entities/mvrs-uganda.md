# Mobile Vital Records System (MVRS), Uganda

**Uganda's mobile birth- and death-notification system for civil registration** — built by
the former Uganda Registration Services Bureau ([[ursb]]) **from 2011**, and moved to
[[nira-uganda]] by an institutional change in **2015**. It is the channel by which health
facilities and districts notify vital events electronically.

## Activity in this wiki

- **COVID-19 stress test (IDRC brief; undated, content runs to Aug 2020 — year-precision
  proxy date).** Uganda's **14-day national lockdown from 31 March 2020** (extended to
  end-June) did **not** list civil registration as an essential service, so **all district
  registration offices closed**. **Jan–Aug 2020 NIRA recorded ~83,200 birth events
  electronically — 21% of the same period in 2019.** Coverage of the system itself: MVRS
  in use in **112 of 135 districts** and **222 of 5,230** birth-handling health facilities.
  Birth-certificate holding: **21% (2006), 31% (2011), 32% (2016)**. *The brief carries no
  printed date; the 2020-01-01 prefix is padding for sorting at year precision, not a
  publication date.* See [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]]. Source:
  [[2020-01-01 Maintaining civil registration amid COVID-19 - Uganda's mobile notification system (IDRC)]].
