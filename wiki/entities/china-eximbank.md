# Export-Import Bank of China (China Eximbank)

China's principal policy lender for overseas infrastructure (with CDB and ICBC); the dominant financier
in ODI's 2026 review of Chinese infrastructure in Africa — including digital projects (Ethiopia's
ZTE/Huawei telecom overhaul, Kenya's NOFBI fibre backbone and the **[[konza-technopolis]]** data centre).
See [[geopol.china]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
[[2026-02-05 Chinas infrastructure finance in Africa impacts and lessons]].

## Cameroon — fibre backbone Phase IV (2026)

By decree **n°2026/084 of 6 Mar 2026**, Cameroon took a **China Exim Bank loan of ~¥1.35bn (~108.4bn
FCFA / ~US$195m)** for **Phase IV of the national fibre backbone** — **+4,000 km** on Camtel's existing
~12,000 km network, Huawei-linked, the **largest single China exposure in CMR holdings**. It reframes the
wiki's connectivity story: the backbone the World Bank's PATNUC extends is itself **Chinese-financed**,
amid IMF high-risk-of-debt-distress warnings. *(One outlet mis-headlined the sum "$108 Billion CFA,"
conflating $195m with CFA 108.4bn; ITWeb's figures are correct — see [[cameroon--infra-connect]].)*
