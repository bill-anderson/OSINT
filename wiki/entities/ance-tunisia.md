# National Digital Certification Agency (ANCE, Tunisia)

**Tunisia's national digital certification authority — the state body underwriting the
public-key infrastructure behind the country's digital identity and qualified electronic
signatures.** Named as one of the operators of [[e-houwiya]], alongside the National Computer Center
([[cni-tunisia]]) and local telecom operators, under the ICT department.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Operator of the national mobile digital ID (as of 2025-03).** ANCE is named as one of the
  affiliated entities responsible for operating [[e-houwiya]]: the system issues each holder a
  **personal X.509 certificate** and digital signing keys on first issuance, and the resulting
  signature is a **fully qualified mobile signature** legally equivalent to a handwritten signature —
  the certification function that sits at ANCE's core. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]],
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].

  *Note on provenance: this account is written by [[nizar-ben-neji]], the former minister who led
  E-Houwiya's implementation — principal testimony, not independent reporting.*
