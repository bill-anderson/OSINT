The **Digital Investment Facility (DIF)** is a donor-funded programme that de-risks and structures green, secure digital-infrastructure projects — data centres and internet exchange points (IXPs) — across Sub-Saharan Africa, taking them from early design to contract closing to improve their bankability and access to finance.

## Who runs and funds it

- **Co-funded by** the European Commission, Germany and Finland.
- **Implemented by** [[giz]] and HAUS.
- Sits within the EU's **Global Gateway / D4D Hub** digital-partnership architecture. (NB: the source page is mis-titled "Data Governance in Africa"; its content is the DIF profile.)

## What it does

DIF provides technical and financial advisory support — feasibility and pre-feasibility studies, master plans, capex/opex reviews, project-promotion strategy, and contract-closing/financing negotiation support — and connects promoters to multilateral financiers. Support is free technical assistance, not capital itself; DIF's role is to make projects investable.

**Eligibility criteria** for its Expression of Interest support:
1. Minimum investment size of **EUR 5 million**
2. A digital-infrastructure project (data centre or IXP)
3. Revenue-generating
4. Located in Sub-Saharan Africa
5. Contributes to climate action with high ESG standards
6. Private-sector ownership with socio-economic additionality

## Market briefs

DIF commissioned and publishes a set of **data-centre market briefs** (the [[xalam-dc-market-briefs-2026]]), produced by Xalam Analytics, covering Southern, East, and West & Central Africa plus an overall Africa Digital Infrastructure Report. A recurring theme is the integration of **green energy sourcing** into data-centre expansion. These briefs are a standing reference for the wiki on African data-centre markets, energy supply, skills gaps and investment trends.
