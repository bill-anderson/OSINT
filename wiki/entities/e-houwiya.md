# E-Houwiya (Tunisia national mobile digital identity)

**Tunisia's state-recognised nationwide digital identity system, introduced on 2 August 2022 and
legally equivalent to face-to-face identification.** A mobile-ID/mobile-signature scheme rather than
a card, E-Houwiya is the authentication layer of Tunisia's 2025 digital strategy: single sign-on for
government portals, remote KYC for the banking sector, and a qualified digital signature. It is
operationally distinct from the (still-pending) biometric national ID card created by
[[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]] — see that page for the card.

**Provenance caution — the anchor source is principal testimony, not neutral trade reporting.** The
fullest account of E-Houwiya held here is authored by **[[nizar-ben-neji]]**, Tunisia's former
Minister of Communication Technologies (2021–2024), **who led E-Houwiya's design and
implementation**. It is a first-hand participant account of his own programme and should be
attributed as such, not read as third-party verification. The Biometric Update roundup
([[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]]) largely restates
it; cite the origin, not the retelling.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Adoption (as of March 2025).** **200,000** mobile-ID subscribers, including **over 15,000**
  Tunisians living abroad. *(The Biometric Update retelling drops the "over" and renders it a flat
  15,000; the origin says "over 15,000" — cite the origin.)* Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **Issuance and terms (as of 2025-03).** Issued only to holders of a physical ID, **over age 18**,
  and linked to the **national ID number**. Identity is confirmed either physically at a telecom
  operator's office or **online via a visual interview**. It can be requested and renewed online, is
  **valid three years**, and is **free of charge** to encourage adoption; identification is free and
  other functions are affordable or free. On first issuance the system generates a **personal X.509
  certificate** and digital signing keys; the resulting signature is a **fully qualified mobile
  signature, legally equivalent to a handwritten signature**. See [[gov.standards]],
  [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **Operators.** Run by the ICT department and affiliated entities — the National Digital
  Certification Agency ([[ance-tunisia]]), the National Computer Center ([[cni-tunisia]]) and local
  telecom operators. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **Function set.** Onboarding (create/update online accounts), login with at least two
  authentication factors, digital signature of transactions and documents, proof of consent in
  self-service administrative procedures, online eligibility proof (e.g. age verification), autofill
  of official-source data into forms, and approval of wire transfers. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **SSO for e-government.** A centralised authentication system for government portals — **E-Bawaba,
  E-CNAM, E-People, E-CNSS, Madrassati** and others. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **Voluntary in law, conditional in practice (as stated 2025-03).** There is **no legal obligation**
  to hold a digital identity. But the same source — its architect — warns that as more public and
  private entities adopt it, "in the near future citizens without it will not be able to fully
  benefit from available online services." A de-facto gate without a de-jure mandate. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **Banking and KYC (as of 2025-03).** Opened to the banking sector to let unbanked populations
  reach bank accounts, mobile payments and financial services, supporting compliance with the KYC
  regulations the **[[central-bank-of-tunisia]]** published on **28 February 2025** and streamlining
  remote onboarding. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **Paysmart integration credited for payments growth (reported 2026-02-23).** The
  [[central-bank-of-tunisia]] named the integration of E-Houwiya with the **[[paysmart]]** platform —
  giving secure, seamless authentication — among the principal enablers of what it called the
  "explosive growth" in mobile payments during **2025** (**+81%** year-on-year). Source:
  [[2026-02-23 Central Bank Of Tunisia Reports 81% Surge In Mobile Payments As Tunisia Accelerates Its Digital Transformation In 2025]].
- **Estonian design lineage (reported 2025-03).** Development is credited to collaboration with the
  Estonian **[[e-governance-academy]]**, which signed an MoU with the
  [[ministry-of-communication-technologies-tunisia]] in **2022**; Marit Lani, Tunisia's smart
  government program director, described using the Estonian example to organise existing building
  blocks into a digital identity ecosystem. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
- **Named in the civil-society critique (attributed, not asserted).** Biometric Update reports
  **[[access-now]]** alleging that three programmes — the biometric ID project, E-Houwiya, and the
  **[[subsidies-compensation-platform-tunisia]]** — were developed with minimal transparency and
  public consultation, carrying risks of mass surveillance, identity theft and data exploitation.
  This is Access Now's characterisation as relayed by a secondary outlet, not an established finding.
  See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
