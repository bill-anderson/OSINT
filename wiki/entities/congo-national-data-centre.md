# Congo National Data Centre (Brazzaville)

A **national data centre** under construction in the Bacongo district of Brazzaville, billed as the **first sovereign data centre in Central Africa** — the storage anchor of the Central African Backbone (CAB) project.

## Profile

- **Financing:** **$72.8m** (~€66.55m) — **$57m AfDB + $15.8m government**; includes a **600-km fibre backbone** connecting Congo to **Cameroon and CAR**. Three-storey facility with server rooms, monitoring/supervision spaces and energy/cooling.
- **Aim:** host all public data domestically ("eliminating reliance on servers in Europe") and offer storage/backup to telcos, banks and insurers — a data-sovereignty flagship.
- **Timeline (updated — stalled, then inaugurated).** Originally expected operational Dec 2024; **75%
  (Mar 2025)** → **80% and stalled dead** in May 2025 when the government **failed to pay its counterpart
  share**, prompting the AfDB to threaten to walk and leave a "white elephant" → unblocked to **>90%** by
  Aug 2025 (Chinese technicians; AfDB inspection by Léandre Bassolé), inauguration pinned to **28 Nov 2025**
  (Sassou N'Guesso State of the Nation) → **reported inaugurated** by the minister (in passing, Jan 2026).
  Sources: [[2025-03-04-congo-national-data-centre-80pc-complete-wearetech]],

- **⚠ Financing conflict (open contradiction).** The held **$57m AfDB / $72.8m total** (Techpoint, 2024)
  conflicts with Les Dépêches' recurring **"cofinancé par la BAD à 13 millions de dollars"** (2025-05-29).
  A plausible (unconfirmed) reconciliation: $57m/$72.8m = whole project incl. the 600-km fibre; $13m =
  AfDB's building-only share. Both held dated; see
  `reviews/contradictions/open/congo-datacentre-afdb-financing.md`.
- **A third DC thread (2026-06).** Beyond this DC and the ARPCE Pointe-Noire (2Africa) one, a CEO–ONG Pratic
  MoU (signed 29 May 2026) launched a feasibility study for a **regional data centre on the Côte-Matève gas
  plant, Pointe-Noire** — an energy–digital-nexus play. Source:
