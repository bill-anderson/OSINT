# Nigeria Internet Registration Association (NiRA)

NiRA is the registry that manages Nigeria's **.ng** country-code top-level domain (ccTLD),
established in 2005. Analogous to the [[ghana-domain-name-registry]], it is framed as a
neglected pillar of Nigeria's DPI and digital sovereignty — the domain layer that anchors
institutional identity, legal jurisdiction and trust online. President: **Adesola Akinsanya**;
COO: **Seyi Onasanya**.

## What matters now

- **Chronic under-adoption of .ng (as of Dec 2025).** NiRA data show only **~237,266 active
  .ng registrations** — very low for Africa's most populous economy. Comparators: Brazil .br
  >5M, India .in >4M, South Africa .za >1M, Kenya .ke >100,000. Framed as a governance/
  enforcement gap, not a market failure. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[infra.cybersec]].
- **Policy vs enforcement.** The National Policy on the Government Second-Level Domain (FEC-
  approved **16 Feb 2022**, enforced by [[nitda]]) mandates MDAs migrate to .gov.ng, and .ng
  is one of NITDA's accreditation conditions for IT service providers. Adoption lags,
  especially at state/local level. **Anambra State** migrated ~95% of its MDAs to .gov.ng as a
  sub-national model. Akinsanya frames .ng as a "Nigeria First" strategic national asset. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[sovereignty]].
