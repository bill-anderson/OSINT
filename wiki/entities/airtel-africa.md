# Airtel Africa

Pan-African telecom operator; building hyperscale data centres under the **Nxtra by
Airtel** brand.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Lagos hyperscale (broke ground 2024-03).** A **38 MW** hyperscale facility on
  Victoria Island, completion targeted early 2026 — positioned as the **first of five**
  hyperscale data centres Airtel plans across the continent. See [[infra.store]], [[NGA]].
- **IFC $150m connectivity loan (2026-07-01).** The **[[ifc]]** lent **$150m** to two
  Airtel Africa subsidiaries to expand/upgrade networks across its **14-country SSA**
  footprint (longer-tenor, local-currency). Deal: [[ifc-airtel-africa-loan]]. See [[XSS]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-01 IFC and Airtel Africa Expand Digital Connectivity to Strengthen Jobs and Inclusion]].
- **Airtel–SpaceX Direct-to-Cell (Dec 2025).** An agreement to bring
  **[[starlink]]** direct-to-cell across its **14 SSA markets** / 174m customers — recurring
  as the marquee telco–satellite tie-up in the sovereignty debate, cast either as
  cooperation or as offshoring of revenue and control. In **[[KEN]]**, the
  [[communications-authority-of-kenya]] opened a technical review (Mar 2026) of
  Airtel's Direct-to-Cell application, probing LEO-to-handset interference with
  licensed 3G/4G/5G spectrum (audit due to conclude mid-2026). See
  [[infra.connect]], [[geopol.usa]]. Sources: [[2026-06-16 Who Owns Africa's Sky]],
  [[2026-05-22 View How SpaceX is making a quiet bet on Africa]],
  [[2026-05-26 Africa risks losing billions to satellite internet operators, report says]],
  [[2026-03-10 Kenya begins audit of Airtel's Starlink Direct-to-Cell tech]].
  The tie-up is now formalised as **[[starlink-airtel-d2c-partnership]]** (Direct-to-Cell +
  cellular backhaul launching 2026 across all **14 markets**; SpaceX licensed in 9 of 14 by
  May 2025) — Airtel reported **173.8m subscribers** (Sep 2025), 78.1m data users and
  **$1.16bn** H1 FY2026 data revenue. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]].
- **Mobile-money AI-governance gap (ICTworks, 2026-04-16).** **Airtel Assist** and Airtel's
  GSMA Open Gateway integration are named among African mobile-money AI (fraud/credit/
  chatbots) deployed across a $1.68tn infrastructure without independent, disaggregated
  audits. See [[dpi.pay]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 3 Reasons Mobile Money Programs Have a $1.7 Trillion Problem]].
- **Uganda election internet shutdown (2026-01-13).** The [[uganda-communications-commission]]
  ordered Airtel Uganda and [[mtn-group]] to cut internet for four days around the Jan 2026
  presidential election; the blackout cost the two operators an estimated **~$15m**, crippled mobile
  money and (per the order) briefly severed even Uganda Police connectivity. Airtel and MTN are named
  co-defendants in a suit challenging the shutdown as unconstitutional. See [[UGA]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-01-26 The cost of Uganda's internet shutdown]].

- **Data-centre CAPEX & data>voice (Russell Southwood, 2025-12-04).** Southwood's
  state-of-market lists Airtel among the four "power players": **data revenue
  overtook voice by end-FY2025**, mobile-money is **16.8%** of revenue, and
  roughly **a quarter of Airtel's CAPEX** now goes to data centres (the Nxtra
  build-out). See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]].
- **Chad fixed-line corridor (Russell Southwood, 2026-02-16).** In the
  former-incumbents piece, **Sotel Tchad** signed an **April 2025** deal with
  Airtel to run the N'Djamena–Mberé fibre corridor — Airtel using a distressed
  state incumbent's assets as wholesale capacity. See [[TCD]], [[infra.connect]].
  Source: [[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]].
