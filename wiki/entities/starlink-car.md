# Starlink in the Central African Republic

Starlink's arrival in CAR is a compressed **acquire-then-control** story — a **US-brokered** satellite deal
in a state the wiki otherwise frames around Russian influence, launched to bridge a near-total connectivity
gap, then **partly suspended within six weeks** on national-security and traceability grounds. It is a
sharp case of a fragile state moving from *acquiring* connectivity to *controlling* it. See [[CAF]],
[[caf--infra-connect]], [[geopol.usa]].

## What we know

- **Signing — US-brokered (2025-12-18).** [[geopol.usa]] CAR and **SpaceX** signed a Starlink convention
  (ceremony hosted by President Touadéra), **brokered with US-Embassy facilitation** — the chargée
  d'affaires used it to trail a bilateral commercial dialogue. SpaceX committed **several hundred terminals**
  for isolated schools, clinics and administrations. Sources:
  [[2025-12-19-starlink-convention-signing-primature-cf]], [[2025-12-21-starlink-arrival-us-embassy-brokered-corbeaunews]].
- **Launch — 27th African market (2026-03-16).** [[infra.connect]] Local rep **DEVEA Centrafrique** (also
  rendered *DEVEAG*); **450 government kits** with 12-month subscriptions. But affordability is brutal:
  **~33,000 FCFA/month ≈ 136% of monthly GNI per capita** (vs the ITU 2% threshold) — against GSMA-2024
  mobile-coverage **30/100** and 4G/5G "**inexistantes**." Sources:
  [[2026-03-17-starlink-commercial-launch-affordability-agenceecofin]],
  [[2026-03-18-starlink-officially-launches-car-techafricanews]], [[2026-03-19-car-taps-starlink-itweb]].
- **Suspension — the turn (2026-04-28).** [[gov.legislate]] Six weeks in, the Digital Economy ministry
  **deactivated roaming** on non-homologated kits, citing **national security and user traceability**, with
  **[[arcep-central-african-republic|ARCEP]]** handling regularisation. Grey-market imports were the
  affordable route online (cf. the South Africa precedent) — "this is about visibility." Sources:
  [[2026-04-28-car-suspends-starlink-kits-techafricanews]], [[2026-04-28-car-blocks-starlink-roaming-techcabal]].
