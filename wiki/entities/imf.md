# International Monetary Fund (IMF)

**Multilateral financial institution**, held here as a publisher of analysis on digital public
infrastructure as a **fiscal instrument** — DPI read through revenue mobilisation rather than
service delivery.

## Activity in this wiki

- **DPI as tax machinery (Uganda, 2025-04-21).** An IMF **PFM Blog** piece by **Amos Sanday** sets
  out the revenue case for Uganda's DPI: the country targets **tax-to-GDP of 13% rising to 18% by
  2030**, against a decade stagnant near **13%** — below a Sub-Saharan average of **16%** and an
  OECD average of **34%** (2024 OECD revenue statistics). It attributes concrete gains to specific
  systems: **TINs** integrated with national ID registered **over 350,000 informal taxpayers by
  2022**; **[[efris]]** real-time transaction tracking produced a **12% rise in VAT collections**;
  **PINs** moved **25% of tax payments** to digital channels; **Digital Tax Stamps** reduced illicit
  trade. Named constraints: limited technical capacity in revenue authorities, a **digital gap in
  which revenue administrations advance faster than other agencies** (hampering data sharing), and
  smaller taxpayers lacking digital access. See [[UGA]], [[ura-uganda]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-21 Leveraging Digital Public Infrastructure to Enhance Uganda's Tax Revenue Potential]].

> **Analysis, not primary evidence.** Named IMF-published analysis, cited as such. Its OECD and
> Sub-Saharan comparators are reference figures — cited here, not promoted onto place pages.

## Why it matters analytically

The IMF framing supplies the **motive** behind developments the news coverage reports without
explaining — most directly Uganda's TIN-to-NIN merger. It also states the underlying logic plainly:
identity infrastructure widens the tax base by making informal economic activity legible. That is
the same mechanism the rights literature describes as surveillance, told approvingly. Holding both
readings against each other is the point of keeping this page.
