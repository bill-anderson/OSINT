# Enugu Smart Green Schools

Enugu State's flagship education-infrastructure programme, and the signature initiative of Governor
**Peter Mbah**: **260 solar-powered, digitally-equipped "smart green schools" — one in each of the
state's 260 political wards**. It is the largest sub-national digital-education deployment on the
wiki's file. Construction began in the **2023–2024** period; the pilot at **Owo** (the governor's
hometown) opened **2024-02-07**; formal transition of pupils from existing public schools began
**2025-09-22**; full opening of all 260 was promised for **January 2026**. As of **May 2026**, by the
governor's own admission, **"every smart green school is not yet complete."**

## The number: 260, not 267

The programme is designed and consistently described by the state as **260 schools** — one per ward.
The figure **267** appears only in the **claimed-delivery** register: a mid-2026 state claim to have
*built* 267 with construction continuing, and Commissioner for Education **[[ndubueze-mbah]]**'s
remark at [[nigeria-education-supplies-summit|NESS 2026]] (2026-07-16). Treat **260 as the programme's
size** and **267 as an undated state delivery claim**, not a verified count of schools in service. A
plausible reconciliation — 260 ward schools plus a handful of senior-secondary or pilot builds — is
**not established by any source held**. *(Corrected 2026-07-20; this page previously carried 267 as
the programme's size on the strength of the NESS remark alone.)*

## Design, as specified

- **Power.** Solar / renewable energy on-site — the stated precondition, not an add-on, given grid
  unreliability. See [[infra.energy]].
- **Connectivity.** Satellite internet combined with local internet infrastructure (supply routers,
  access points). **No connectivity or solar vendor is named in any source held** — this remains the
  principal open gap.
- **Facilities per school.** ~**25 digitally connected classrooms**, ICT centres, robotics and AI
  labs, e-libraries, digital whiteboards, science labs, smart farms, a medical clinic, water systems,
  community hall and on-site teacher housing. Free uniforms, books, meals and **tablets** for pupils.
- **Capacity.** **840–1,000 pupils** per campus, early childhood through full basic education on one
  site; **300,000+** children targeted for the associated feeding programme.
- **Teachers.** ~**11,000** new smart-school teachers reported in recruitment and training at the
  Centre for Experiential Learning and Innovation, amid complaints from successful applicants who say
  they were never contacted.

## Finance — the documentary trail (as of 2026-05-17)

Self-funded from the **state budget**; **no donor or DFI facility** is involved in any source held.
UNESCO's Nigeria representative (April 2026) and former Vice-President Yemi Osinbajo (May 2026) have
each praised the programme, but neither is a funder.

- **2025 allocation: ₦266.7bn** — ₦100bn constructing 200 integrated smart schools; **₦146bn
  equipping 260 ward smart schools**; ₦10.7bn upgrading three senior secondary structures; ₦10bn
  establishing Smart Senior Secondary Schools.
- **2026 allocation: ₦198bn** — ₦158bn for 16 smart senior secondary schools; ₦10bn equipping and
  furnishing existing schools; ₦30bn school feeding.
- **Unit cost:** ~**₦1.5bn per school** (commissioner for education); other information puts it at
  **₦1.3bn** for construction plus equipping.
- **Education share of the state budget:** 33% (2024 and 2025, per Mbah); **32.27% of the 2026
  budget**.

Source: [[2026-05-17-thecable-enugu-smart-schools-investigation-part-ii]]. *The Enugu State
appropriation documents themselves are not held; these figures come via TheCable's reading of them.*

## Delivery, quality and procurement — the counterweight

A two-part TheCable investigation (**2026-05-17**) is the only independent scrutiny on file, and it
substantially qualifies the programme's public presentation:

- **Incomplete at scale.** As of **May 2026**, many schools were **not physically complete nor fully
  equipped**, with pupils already resuming in buildings still under construction (New Haven:
  scaffolding up, electrical and plumbing ongoing, laptops undelivered, wall cracks).
- **Structural failures.** Roof torn off at **Ishielu Amagunze** (Feb 2026); materials complaints at
  **Amofia Nara** (2026-03-11); roof collapse at **Iheaka** (2026-04-08) and another at **Orba**; rain
  entering occupied classrooms at **New Haven** (April 2026).
- **A demolition and a terminated contract.** A completed smart green school at **Mbu, Isi-Uzo LGA**
  was **demolished in July 2025** after failing structural integrity tests, the contract terminated —
  the LGA chairman saying "the contractors misrepresented their compliance." A staircase at
  **Amankpunato, Oji River LGA** was demolished the same month.
- **Procurement under investigation.** A contract worth **over ₦11.4bn** for **22 smart green
  schools** was awarded **2024-07-02**; ~**₦5.7bn (50%)** was paid upfront; the contract was
  terminated at the completion deadline in **February 2025** amid accusations of substandard work.
  An **EFCC** investigation has recovered **₦1.23bn** so far (as of 2026-05-17). Other named
  contractors hold 10–12 sites each; contract sums were revised upward for inflation.
- **No response from the state.** The commissioner for works declined interview requests on three
  occasions (9, 14 and 16 April 2026).

Development experts quoted question both the procurement oversight and the policy choice of building
new campuses rather than retrofitting existing schools, and the feasibility of maintaining digital
equipment in remote areas.

## What is still not held

**No connectivity or solar supplier is named; no completion count is verifiable; no learning-outcome
data or independent evaluation exists.** The 2026-07 "267 built" claim has no procurement or
inspection record behind it. TheCable's **Part I** was not retrieved.

## Why it matters here

This is a **sub-national** digital-infrastructure programme — a state government building power and
connectivity into schools directly rather than waiting on federal or telco rollout. It is the clearest
current instance in the wiki of the [[digital.localgov]] pattern in education, and it inverts the usual
sequencing: energy first, then connectivity, then instruction. It sits alongside the federal
education-data layer ([[dnemis-nigeria]]) as the physical counterpart to that system's information
layer. It is also the wiki's clearest case of the **gap between a digital-development programme's
announced scale and its verifiable delivery** — worth holding as a caution against reading state
announcements of DPI-adjacent infrastructure as completed fact.
