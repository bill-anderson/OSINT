# Enugu Smart Green Schools

Enugu State's flagship education-infrastructure programme: **267 "smart green schools" across
Enugu State (as of 2026-07-16)**, described by Commissioner for Education Professor
**[[ndubueze-mbah]]** at [[nigeria-education-supplies-summit|NESS 2026]].

## Design, as described

- **Power.** Schools run on **clean, renewable and reliable solar energy** — the stated
  precondition, not an add-on, given grid unreliability. See [[infra.energy]].
- **Connectivity.** A combination of **satellite internet and local internet infrastructure**,
  including supply routers and access points.
- **Purpose.** Mbah's stated goal is that "no matter where every child is, they have access to the
  standardised instruction and tuition" — **urban and rural alike**, with the reform agenda
  described as "gender inclusive [and] class inclusive". See [[include.divides]],
  [[digital.rural]].

## What is not held

**No cost, funding source, completion status, timeline, pupil numbers, connectivity uptime,
vendor or independent evaluation is on file.** The count of **267 schools (2026-07-16)** is the
Commissioner's own figure, stated at a supplier-organised summit and carried by a sponsored
placement; the wiki holds no procurement record, budget line or third-party verification. Treat as
a described programme, not a verified deployment.

## Why it matters here

This is a **sub-national** digital-infrastructure programme — a state government building power
and connectivity into schools directly rather than waiting on federal or telco rollout. It is the
clearest current instance in the wiki of the [[digital.localgov]] pattern in education, and it
inverts the usual sequencing: energy first, then connectivity, then instruction. It sits
alongside the federal education-data layer ([[dnemis-nigeria]]) as the physical counterpart to
that system's information layer.
