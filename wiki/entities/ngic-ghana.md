# Next-Gen InfraCo (NGIC)

Ghana's designated **wholesale 4G/5G neutral-host infrastructure provider**. It received **NCA clearance** and
**activated Ghana's first shared 5G wholesale network** (Nokia partner, Mar 2026). But in **July 2026 the
government ended NGIC's exclusive rights**, opening the wholesale-5G market to competition (MTN, Telecel to
bid) — a reversal of the single-wholesaler model that analysts had questioned (4G-core vs 5G-core doubts). See
[[ghana--infra-connect]].

## The exclusivity revocation, formally (effective 2026-07-15)

The **[[national-communications-authority-ghana|NCA]]** amended NGIC's wholesale 5G licence to strike the
exclusivity provision, **effective 15 July 2026**, on a public-interest finding after a market review. Its
statutory basis: **Article 6.1.2 of NGIC's licence** and **Section 14 of the Electronic Communications Act,
2008 (Act 775)**, which empowers licence amendment in the public interest.

NGIC contested it through the full statutory process — NCA **Notice of Proposed Amendment, 2 March 2026**;
NGIC **statement of objection, 18 March** under **s.14(4)**; a meeting on **1 April**; and **oral
representations to the NCA Governing Board on 28 May**. The Authority proceeded after considering both the
written and oral submissions.

Crucially, the amendment is **narrow**: it removes only the exclusivity condition. The rest of NGIC's
licence stands, **including its spectrum assignment** — so NGIC is not de-licensed but demoted from sole
wholesaler to one participant in a contested market. Source:
[[2026-07-17-ghana-ends-ngic-exclusive-5g-licence]].
