# Digital Rights Lawyers Initiative (DRLI)

The Digital Rights Lawyers Initiative (registered as the Incorporated Trustees of the DRLI) is
a Nigerian civil-society legal organisation that litigates data-protection and digital-rights
cases. It is emerging as a recurring private-enforcement actor testing the
[[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]] in court, complementing the regulator [[ndpc]].

## What matters now

- **Suit against INEC (2026).** DRLI, with lawyer Solomon Etisang, sued the
  [[inec]] at the Federal High Court, Yenagoa (Bayelsa), for failing to publish a website
  privacy policy — alleging breaches of NDPA 2023 ss.24(1)(a), 24(3) and 27(1) and the
  constitutional right to privacy (s.37). Counsel: Olumide Babalola. The action seeks a
  declaration of breach plus an order compelling INEC to publish a compliant policy. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[nigeria--gov-protect]].
