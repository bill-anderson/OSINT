# EllaLink (Mauritania branch)

Mauritania's **second international submarine cable** — a EllaLink-operated branch giving the country a
redundant link to Europe (via Portugal) and South America (via Brazil), ending sole dependence on the
ageing **[[ace-cable|ACE]]** cable (connected 2011). President Ghazouani launched the project at Nouadhibou
in **July 2025**; it is financed by the Mauritanian State, the EU and the **[[eib|European Investment Bank]]**
(>€43.5m: €25.6m execution + €17.9m for 25-year maintenance) and implemented by EllaLink with
**[[alcatel-submarine-networks|Alcatel Submarine Networks]]**.

Specifications: 669 km at sea in Mauritanian waters; two fibre pairs at **200 Gb/s initial, extensible to
500 Gb/s, max 12 Tbit/s**. The **phase-1 coastal landing (PLSE, 28 km, Bu7 junction) completed 4 May 2026**;
offshore laying scheduled August 2026; **commissioning expected Q1 2027**. Officials frame it as moving
Mauritania "from recipient to actor" in global telecoms and expect a fivefold speed increase and ~20% price
fall. Long referenced in the wiki as the "second cable contracted to Ellalink" — now under construction.
