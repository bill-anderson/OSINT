# Microsoft–G42 Kenya data centre ($1bn)

A **$1bn** geothermal-powered data centre at **Olkaria, Kenya**, first announced
May 2024 by **[[microsoft]]**, UAE's **[[g42]]** and the Kenyan government, and
marketed as a "trusted data zone" running on Microsoft Azure.

## Status & activity

- **Stalled (2026-07, DCD).** Missed its targeted May 2026 launch. Full proposed
  capacity **1 GW** = ~a third of Kenya's **~3 GW** grid; President
  **[[william-ruto]]** said serving it would mean cutting power to half the
  country. A concept note failed National Treasury clearance. A Bloomberg update
  (May 2026) reframed the breakdown as failed negotiations over a government
  **capacity-lease** commitment; talks continue, project may be scaled back.
  Initial phase was **100 MW** geothermal. See [[KEN]], [[infra.capacity]],
  [[infra.energy]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Microsoft and G42 data center in Kenya stalled due to lack of power capacity]].
  Corroborated (2026-05, TechMoonshot DC-race analysis): the project **never broke ground**,
  and by **August 2025** Kenyan officials confirmed it would miss the May 2026 launch; the
  Treasury concept-note failure and the unmet capacity-lease commitment trace back to power.
  Source:
  [[2026-05-21 Africa Data Center Investment 2026 The $4.5B Race and the Power Problem]].
- **Sovereignty case.** Cited in [[2026-06-08 The Promise and Limits of African Data Sovereignty]] as a "sovereign" zone that still runs on a US hyperscaler's stack
  under the CLOUD Act (Microsoft holds a minority G42 stake + board seat), and in
  [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]]
  as the model US–UAE–Africa co-investment.
- **Demand-side reading (Russell Southwood, 2026-05-19).** Southwood argues the
  facility exposed **weak anchor-tenant demand** — Africa's specialist AI
  training/inference market (large MNOs, banks, governments, universities) is
  still too thin to fill hyperscale GPU capacity, so the stall is as much a
  demand as a power problem. Source:
  [[2026-05-19 Trying to make sense of who will use Artificial Intelligence in Africa and how – Going beyond the hype]].
- **Recurs as the cautionary case (2026).** Anjola Adebowale cites the suspension
  (100 MW phase 1, Ruto's "half the country" line) as the live warning that
  Nigeria's Jan-2027 data-localisation deadline outruns grid and data-centre
  capacity ([[2026-07-01 Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part I Inside the CBN's June 2026 Payments Circular]]),
  and as the contrasting "stalled sovereignty signal" against MTN's Lagos build
  ([[2026-06-01 Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate]]). Zuria Nagadya
  lists the "$1bn geothermal data centre" among Kenya's data-localisation stack
  ([[2026-06-08 Kenyan Citizens Took A $2.5 Billion Deal With America To Court To Fight For Their Health Data]]).

## Reads
