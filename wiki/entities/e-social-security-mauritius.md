# E-Social Security (Mauritius)

Mauritius's programme to digitise and integrate the national welfare system, run by the **Ministry of Social
Integration, Social Security and National Solidarity** (Minister **Ashok Subron**). On **27 May 2026** the
government launched the **"Bulk Scanning of Beneficiary Files"** project to digitise **more than 10.5 million
social-benefit documents** into a future **E-Social Security platform** — the first digitisation of the welfare
system in **13 years**, replacing floor-to-ceiling paper archives.

Scale (as of the 2026 launch): social-protection spending reached **Rs 91.4 bn (~$1.9 bn) in FY2024-25** —
**37.4% of total government expenditure** and **12.8% of GDP**. As of **June 2025, 279,559 people** drew the
Basic Retirement Pension; agencies process nearly **800,000 payments a month** for at least **350,000
beneficiaries**. Records span pensions, social assistance, disability benefits, the National Pension Scheme (NPS)
and the National Solidarity Fund (NSF); full integration is estimated at **~18 months**. The programme ties to a
pension restructuring that phases the Basic Retirement Pension eligibility age from **60 to 65**.
