# Rwanda National Artificial Intelligence Agency

Rwanda's first institution fully dedicated to AI, approved by Cabinet on **8 June
2026** to coordinate public/private AI initiatives and governance.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Creation (2026-06-08).** Mandated to drive AI investment and coordinate use
  across public administration, healthcare, education and agriculture. Follows
  Rwanda's **2023 national AI policy** (~70% skills-focused) and complements the
  **Rwanda AI Scaling Hub** (~RWF 25bn / $17m funding). ICT & Innovation Minister
  **[[paula-ingabire]]**. See [[RWA]], [[tech.ai]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-10 Rwanda Launches Dedicated AI Agency to Drive Investment and Innovation]].

- **Responsible AI Office (analyst readings, 2026).** Nagadya describes Rwanda's **Responsible AI
  Office** inside the Ministry of ICT (with [[rwanda-information-society-authority|RISA]] as
  implementing body) running a **3-year ethical-review** cycle and an **annual AI Readiness Index** —
  cited as one of the African AI-governance building blocks that nonetheless share a blind spot for
  the informal economy. A separate facial-recognition survey notes Rwanda's AI policy carries a
  **human-oversight requirement**, one of the few African safeguards of its kind. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]]. Sources:
  [[2026-05-04 The World's Most Needed AI Governance Framework Doesn't Exist Yet. Africa Should Build It]],
  [[2026-06-15 If The Technology Can't See Us Accurately, We Build The Rules That Make It]].
