# G42

Abu Dhabi–based AI and cloud company, UAE sovereign-wealth-linked, in which
**[[microsoft]]** holds a minority stake and a board seat — the vehicle through
which UAE ([[uae]]) capital and US technology co-invest in African AI
infrastructure.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Microsoft–G42 Kenya data centre ($1bn).** The Olkaria project
  ([[microsoft-g42-kenya-datacenter]]), first announced May 2024 as a "trusted
  data zone" running on Azure, **stalled in 2026** over power capacity (it never
  broke ground; a Treasury concept note failed clearance). See [[KEN]],
  [[infra.capacity]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-09 Microsoft and G42 data center in Kenya stalled due to lack of power capacity]],
  [[2026-05-21 Africa Data Center Investment 2026 The $4.5B Race and the Power Problem]],
  [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]].
- **Intelligence Grid — India deal (2026-05-15).** G42 (Mubadala-backed) signed to
  deploy a **64-system Cerebras** AI supercomputer in India — the first customer of
  its **Intelligence Grid**, a global network of AI facilities G42 "builds, owns
  and operates" for governments, run by a non-US partner with data under national
  rules. The template for G42's compute-diplomacy model (other governments in
  talks); note G42 works with the same US hyperscalers at home (via Khazna) that it
  offers an alternative to abroad. See [[uae]], [[nvidia]], [[geopol.india]].
  Source: [[2026-06-01 India's AI deal with the UAE challenges U.S. cloud dominance]].
- **Sovereignty critique.** [[2026-06-08 The Promise and Limits of African Data Sovereignty]] cites the Kenya deal as a case where a "sovereign" data zone
  still runs on a US hyperscaler's stack under the CLOUD Act, with Microsoft
  holding equity and a board seat in G42. See [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **US–Gulf–Africa triangle.** [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]] frames the UAE (and G42)
  as intermediaries linking US AI strategy to African markets; the UAE is a
  **Pax Silica** signatory ([[pax-silica]]).
- **SCPT / DRC (Russell Southwood, 2026-02-16).** In the former-incumbents piece,
  **Benya** (described as **G42-backed**) took a **2021 shareholder deal** in the
  DRC state operator **SCPT** but exited after **six months** — a marker of G42/
  Gulf capital probing distressed African state telecoms before the later Kenya
  data-centre bet. See [[COD]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]].
- **Recurs in the Kenya-stall analyses (2026).** The Olkaria suspension is cited
  as the cautionary AI-infrastructure case by Russell Southwood (weak anchor-tenant
  demand, [[2026-05-19 Trying to make sense of who will use Artificial Intelligence in Africa and how – Going beyond the hype]])
  and Anjola Adebowale (power-constraint warning,
  [[2026-07-01 Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part I Inside the CBN's June 2026 Payments Circular]];
  "stalled sovereignty signal," [[2026-06-01 Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate]]).

See [[KEN]], [[microsoft]], [[uae]], [[tech.ai]].
