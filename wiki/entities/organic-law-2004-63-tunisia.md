# Organic Law 2004-63 (Tunisia Personal Data Protection Law)

**Tunisia's personal data protection law, dating from 2004 — the instrument at the centre of civil
society's objection to the country's biometric ID programme, on the grounds that a 2004 statute
cannot govern 2020s biometric processing.** It is the legal baseline against which
[[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]], [[e-houwiya]] and the
[[subsidies-compensation-platform-tunisia]] were all criticised.

**Currency — do not read this page as "the 2004 law is Tunisia's current data protection regime,
full stop."** The critiques below are dated **2024** and **2025-03**, and describe the law as it
stood then. **Fresher state is held:** by **January–February 2026**, Tunisia was **introducing a
123-article data protection bill to update the existing law**. That is **supersession in progress,
not contradiction** — the "outdated 2004 law" framing is a **historical baseline as of its sources'
dates**, and the 2024/2025 critiques are not evidence about the position in 2026.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Reform under way (Jan–Feb 2026, reported 2026-06-23).** Tunisia is **introducing a 123-article
  data protection bill to update the country's existing law** — reported alongside Mauritius's
  parallel effort to amend its Data Protection Act 2017, in a round-up noting African DPAs moving
  hard on enforcement in 2026. Framed in the source as an effort to curb AI and algorithm risks.
  This is the freshest state held on the Tunisian data protection framework. See [[gov.legislate]],
  [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-23 Bimonthly Update on Privacy in Africa (January & February 2026)]].
- **The core objection to the biometric ID (2024-03, Access Now).** [[access-now]] pointed to "the
  outdated 2004 Data Protection Law, coupled with a lack of clarity on how authorities will use,
  store, and protect citizens' sensitive information against abuse or unlawful access" — the legal
  ground of an eight-year campaign against a mandatory biometric ID without robust safeguards.
  First-party advocacy framing; attributed, not asserted. See [[ministry-of-interior-tunisia]],
  [[organic-law-2024-22-tunisia]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-07 Eight years in the making - Tunisia's Biometric ID and Passport Bills risk rights]].
- **Restated a year later (2025-03).** Biometric Update reports civil-society warnings that a lack
  of transparency and **outdated data protection legislation** might expose millions to identity
  theft and mass surveillance, and that "Tunisia's weak data protection regulations and history of
  cyberattacks" make the prospective biometric database a security concern. Derivative of the Access
  Now critique rather than independent corroboration of it. See [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-01 Tunisia drives digitization agenda forward while navigating risks]].
