# Central Bank of Tunisia (BCT)

**Tunisia's central bank and the regulator of its payments system — issuer of the KYC rules that
let the national digital identity into banking, licensor of payment service providers, and the
source of the country's headline digital-payments statistics.** In the material held here the BCT
appears in two roles: rule-maker (KYC, clearing, messaging standards) and statistical authority
(the payments bulletin).

**Provenance caution.** The BCT's own bulletin is **not held** by this wiki. Its 2025 figures reach
these pages through an aggregator's secondary report — see **GAP-TUN-001**. Cite them as reported,
not as read from the primary.

## Activity in this wiki

- **KYC regulations (28 February 2025).** The BCT published Know Your Customer regulations;
  [[e-houwiya]] is credited with strengthening compliance with them and streamlining remote
  onboarding, opening bank accounts, mobile payments and financial services to unbanked
  populations. See [[gov.legislate]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
- **2025 payments bulletin — headline figures (all as of the bulletin reported 2026-02-23).**
  - Mobile payments and transfers **+81%** year-on-year, which the BCT called **"explosive growth"**.
  - **8.4 million** mobile payment transactions over the year, worth **TND 1,769 million**.
  - Active digital wallets **815,000 (2025)**, up from **595,000 (2024)**.
  - Licensed payment service providers **435 (2025)**, up from **368 (2024)**.
  - **P2P transfers = 50.7%** of total mobile operations; **merchant payments = 17.8%**.
  - **+25%** rise in overall electronic transactions nationwide; cheque use continued to decline.
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[data.statistics]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 Central Bank Of Tunisia Reports 81% Surge In Mobile Payments As Tunisia Accelerates Its Digital Transformation In 2025]].
- **Enablers the BCT credits (reported 2026-02-23).** The bank attributed the expansion to
  deliberate financial-inclusion and de-cashing policy, singling out the **integration of
  [[e-houwiya]] with the [[paysmart]] platform** for secure authentication. Alongside it: adoption
  of the **SWIFT MX (ISO 20022)** messaging standard and a **comprehensive overhaul of the National
  Clearing System**, aimed at interoperability, efficiency and transaction security. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 Central Bank Of Tunisia Reports 81% Surge In Mobile Payments As Tunisia Accelerates Its Digital Transformation In 2025]].

## Related (mentioned, not hubbed)

SWIFT MX / ISO 20022 (messaging standard adopted); National Clearing System (overhauled).
