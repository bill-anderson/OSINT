# FUGAS (Unified Administrative and Payroll Management Platform, Guinea)

Guinea's biometric civil-service registry and payroll-management platform (Fichier Unique de Gestion Administrative et Salariale), implemented from **July 2024** and managed by the Ministry of Labour. It verifies employee identities, secures salary payments, tracks careers and eliminates duplicates, irregular IDs and ghost posts — a flagship anti-graft and public-service-digitization deliverable under [[simandou-2040]] ([[dpi.govtech]]). Officially launched in a Conakry ceremony chaired by PM Amadou Oury Bah (late 2025). See [[GIN]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Biometric audit exposes ghost workers, saves ~$26.9m (2025).** Of ~277,000 registered contract staff and civil servants, **only ~130,000 were confirmed** after biometric checks, yielding savings exceeding **233bn GNF (~$26.9m)** since 2024; implementation cost ~60bn GNF. ~20,000 employees are set to change grade/category. PM Bah pressed that FUGAS "must integrate a **unique personal identification number**." Sources: [[2025-11-30 Guinea Claims $26.9M Saved Since 2024 with Biometric Audit of Public Workforce (FUGAS)]], [[2025-12-03 Guinea launches biometric system to weed out 'ghost' public workers (FUGAS)]].
- **Cited in the 2025 sector review** as a headline public-administration gain (233bn GNF saved). Source: [[2026-01-14 Guinea Reviews 2025 Digital Sector Gains, Targets Faster Rollout in 2026]].
