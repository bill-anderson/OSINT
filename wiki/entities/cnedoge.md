# CNEDOGE (National Centre for the Issuance of Official Documents, Equatorial Guinea)

Equatorial Guinea's document-issuance authority (Centro Nacional de Emisión de Documentos Oficiales) — an autonomous public entity created by **Decree 24/2010 (11 March)**, attached to the Ministry of National Security, seat in Malabo. It guarantees the authenticity of the country's official documents: the **DIP** (Documento de Identidad Personal / national ID), passports, foreigners' residence cards and civil-servant cards, all carrying a biometric security system, and it installs and manages issuance/verification devices nationwide (including consulates) ([[dpi.id]]). In the absence of statutory deadlines, an unwritten norm is ~30 working days for a DIP and 14-17 days for a passport.

- **Parliamentary scrutiny (2025-04).** The Chamber of Deputies examined CNEDOGE over document fees, processing
  and consumable shortages affecting ID/passport issuance. Source:
  [[2025-04-02-cnedoge-camara-diputados-tarifas-documentos-ahoraeg]].
- **Expansion & online booking (2026-06).** CNEDOGE opened **new provincial offices** (Mongomo, Ciudad de la
  Paz) and enabled an **online appointment (cita previa) portal** at **cnedoge.com** — its first citizen-facing
  e-government service. Source: [[2026-06-12-cnedoge-nuevas-oficinas-citas-online-ahoraeg]].
- **Civil registry / CRVS.** GNQ **hosted the continental African CRVS experts' meeting** (Sipopo, Jun 2025;
  AU/UNECA, APAI-CRVS Strategic Plan 2023–2027) — signalling a civil-registry/vital-statistics modernisation
  push (INEGE-led). Source: [[2025-06-17-reunion-africana-crvs-sipopo-reg]].

See [[GNQ]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **DIP national tour (2025-04).** [[dpi.registry]] DG Teodomiro Monsuy Eyi reported a roughly month-long national tour producing the DIP: **7,670 dossiers** obtained, 7,275 processed. The government is pushing to require the DIP more strictly of over-18s for any transaction, and to register minors in the civil registry (working with CNEDOGE's tour plan). Sources: [[2025-04-04 Government wants a rigorous control mechanism for issuing the literal (birth) certificate]], [[2024-03-15 The Executive invites parents to register children in the Civil Registry and adults over 18 to obtain the DIP]].
- **Document-integrity concerns (2025-04).** A control mechanism was sought for the civil registry's "literal" (birth) certificate after reports that expatriates fraudulently obtain the DIP and passport via irregular literal certificates (against the 2011 Nationality Law). Source: [[2025-04-04 Government wants a rigorous control mechanism for issuing the literal (birth) certificate]].
- **Establishing profile.** Source: [[2024-01-01 On the functioning of Equatorial Guinea's National Centre for the Issuance of Official Documents (CNEDOGE)]].
