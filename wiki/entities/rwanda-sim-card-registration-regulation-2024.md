# Regulation Governing SIM Card Registration in Rwanda (RURA No. 18/2024)

**Regulation No. 18/R/SM-ICT/RURA/2024 of 09/08/2024**, issued by the
[[rura-rwanda]], governing SIM-card registration, deactivation, transfer and KYC in
Rwanda. The legal basis for Rwanda's move to **biometric SIM registration verified
against the [[nida-rwanda]] national-ID database**. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]],
[[infra.cybersec]].

## Key provisions (structural)

- **Registration by category** — Rwandan citizens, foreign residents, foreigners
  without residence permits, corporate persons, diplomatic missions, statutory bodies,
  and minors each have defined requirements (Arts. 7–13).
- **Identity verification** — SIM registration integrity is tied to identity
  validation entities; operators must synchronise their databases with identity
  validation (Arts. 6, 22, 26), and a SIM is registered under the holder's ID.
- **Anti-fraud controls** — prohibition of proxy registration (Art. 14), OTP-validated
  activation (Art. 42), SIM-swap safeguards (Art. 20), limits on the number of SIM
  cards per subscriber (Art. 24).
- **Data protection** — the operator's SIM-registration database is subject to
  security, confidentiality and third-party-access controls (Arts. 27–32); regulatory
  access is defined (Art. 30). See [[gov.protect]].
- **Enforcement** — administrative fines for a schedule of breaches (Chapter VI,
  Arts. 35–44).
