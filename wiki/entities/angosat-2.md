# ANGOSAT-2

Angola's **national communications satellite**, operated by **[[ggpen]]** from the Funda Mission
Control Centre. Launched **2022** to replace the failed ANGOSAT-1, it carries broadband, TV
broadcasting and secure communications across Angola and parts of Southern Africa. Framed by the
government as **strategic infrastructure that generates state revenue** and reduces dependence on
foreign providers.

## From government-only to commercial (2025–2026)

- **Commercial access opened December 2025** — previously government-reserved, ANGOSAT-2 capacity is
  now requestable by ISPs, startups and telecoms through the Funda access hub, mostly for **VSAT**
  business models; the start of a phased domestic satellite-services market.
- Anchors **[[conecta-angola]]**: **32 free public access points** and **six connected universities**
  (Agostinho Neto, Katyavala Bwila, Kimpa Vita, Mandume Ya Ndemufayo, Namibe) as of June 2026;
  **37 firms** entered the commercial programme (April 2026).
- **Border management** — a VSAT terminal at the **Luvo Border Post** wires ANGOSAT-2 into border
  and tax administration ([[agt-angola]]/AGT-led), May 2026 — state-capacity use, not just access.
- **Education last mile** — combined with **TV White Space (TVWS)** (up to 10 km range) to connect
  **3,972 students** in Ramiros, Luanda (July 2026); **5,600+ University of Namibe students** given
  free access plus hands-on VSAT training (June 2026, with [[unitel]], [[africell]], ST Engineering,
  [[airbus]]).
- **Ilumina Angola** uses ANGOSAT-2 for backhaul on solar-powered shared towers in remote areas
  (see [[ilumina-angola]]... e.g. Lago Dilolo, Moxico).
