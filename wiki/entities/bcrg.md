# Central Bank of the Republic of Guinea (BCRG)

Guinea's central bank and the architect of the country's emerging digital-payments stack ([[dpi.pay]]). Facing a structural **cash-preference problem** — nearly 94% of issued banknotes never return to the banking system, and only ~23-26% of adults hold an account — the BCRG is pushing a coordinated shift to electronic money: a national payment switch, a merchant/mobile payment system, and an instant, interoperable payment system built on open infrastructure. First Vice-Governor (2025): Mohamed Lamine Conté. See [[GIN]], [[include.access]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Instant Payment System (SPI) work begins (2025-12-18).** [[dpi.pay]] [[sovereignty]] The BCRG opened a workshop launching an **instant, interoperable payment system (SPI)** on **[[mojaloop]]** — a public, open, state-controlled infrastructure chosen to **limit technological dependence** and favour local fintech innovation, enabling real-time transfers across bank accounts, mobile wallets and other instruments, and supporting government payments (salaries, social assistance, suppliers). Designed to integrate with **[[papss]]** / ECOWAS in future. Context: 23% of adults hold an account (up from 7% a decade ago); mobile-money transactions +8.6% to 46,795bn GNF in Q2 2024. Guinea draws on WAEMU's BCEAO **PI-SPI** (launched late Sept 2025, 8 states). Sources: [[2025-12-22 Guinea central bank starts work on instant payment system]], [[2025-12-21 Guinea readies instant payment system to speed transactions and cut cash use]].
- **Anti-cash strategy + M-Pesa-style merchant system (2025-11-04).** [[gov.legislate]] Presented to the CNT budget commission: a **merchant payment system inspired by M-Pesa** (built with World Bank support) plus operationalising the **national payment switch** for interoperability among banks, EMIs, MFIs and fintechs. Guinea now licenses **11 EMIs** (up from 6 in 2024). The government moved to ban cash in public administration (Aug 2025) after a liquidity crisis eased only via air-freighted banknotes (>1.4tn GNF / $161m, Aug 2025). Source: [[2025-11-07 Guinea Plans Mobile Payment System to Ease Cash Shortage]].
