# Airtel Chad

**The Chadian operating company of [[airtel-africa]]** (Bharti Airtel) — the mobile operator that
brought **Airtel Money** to [[TCD]], and, at group level, the counterparty in Chad's most
consequential recent fibre arrangement. Where [[sotel-tchad]] is the state incumbent that has not
delivered, Airtel is the commercial operator the state keeps having to deal with — and, in the
N'Djamena–Mberé corridor, then tried to renegotiate with.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Airtel Money launch (date uncertain — see caveat).** Bharti Airtel launched Airtel Money in
  Chad, letting customers transact from their phones. The product came out of a collaboration
  between Airtel Chad and **Ecobank Transnational Incorporated (ETI)**. Registration requires a
  valid form of identification; each transaction requires a personal password. Airtel Chad CEO
  **Salia Gbane**: *"This service allows customers to send money to their relatives, pay essential
  bills, such as electricity and water, tuition fees, bookings, and even buy groceries without
  having to carry cash. The phone essentially becomes an electronic wallet."* Airtel Chad's stated
  rationale: most people in developing countries rely on expensive, unreliable transaction
  channels because formal financial services suit only a minority. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[include.access]], [[geopol.india]]. Source:
  [[2024-05-06 Airtel launches mobile money transfers in Chad]].
  > **Date caveat.** The source page is filed `date_source: proxy`: the clip carries no internal
  > date and the event reads as considerably older than the clipper's **2024-05-06** stamp. The
  > launch year should not be treated as established.
- **Group investment in the Chadian market (announced by 2026-02-13).** **[[airtel-africa]] plans
  to invest 50 billion CFA francs (US$90.45 million)** in a programme running **through June
  2026** — reinforcing microwave links, deploying new towers, extending the fibre network and
  upgrading the core network. *Reported at Airtel Africa group level in the Chadian
  infrastructure context; the source does not attribute the spend to the Airtel Chad entity.* See
  [[finance.new]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Chad Plans 50 Telecom Sites, 50 km of Fiber in N'Djamena]].
- **The N'Djamena–Mberé corridor (April 2025).** The government signed an agreement with
  [[sotel-tchad]] and Airtel to open a **second N'Djamena–Mberé fibre corridor**; Camtel committed
  the following month to the connecting section on its side of the border. The government then
  sought to revise the contract terms on grounds of **"national sovereignty"** and strategic
  interest — which [[russell-southwood]] reads as likely concerning the state's capital
  contribution being too small to guarantee control, and **Airtel's desire to manage the link**.
  See [[sovereignty]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]].

## Reading it

Two things the sources leave open and that this page does not fill in: Airtel Chad's **subscriber
base, market share and Airtel Money uptake** are not reported anywhere in the material held, and
the [[arcep-chad]] service-quality findings of Sept–Oct 2025 (faulty equipment, unstable power,
sites entirely offline) are levelled at *operators* collectively, not at Airtel by name.
