# NASA Harvest (Africa program)

NASA's satellite-agriculture program, which uses satellite imagery to improve
agricultural production. Its Africa program is directed by [[catherine-nakalembe]]
and works across Kenya, Uganda and Senegal, and other African nations, on crop
mapping, crop-type classification and yield estimation.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Local-data crop mapping (2026-03-12).** Africa director [[catherine-nakalembe]]
  showed that most AI models — trained on European and US data — could not
  recognise local crops when she set out to map crop types in western [[KEN]].
  Her team fitted GoPro cameras to volunteers' helmets and collected **5m+ ground
  images in two weeks** (published in *Nature Scientific Data*, 2025) to train
  models mapping cropland across Kenya, Uganda ([[UGA]]) and Senegal ([[SEN]]).
  See [[tech.ai]], [[data.satellite]], [[capacity.research]].
  Source: [[2026-03-12 Western AI models 'fail spectacularly' in farms and forests abroad]].
- **Digital-colonialism framing.** Nakalembe argues Western AI systems ignore
  Global-South realities (high internet costs, limited bandwidth, scarce labelled
  data) and risk "digital colonialism" — big tech extracting data from poor
  communities to train proprietary models, then selling services back. A case
  study in local-data sovereignty vs extraction. See [[digital.rural]], [[XSS]].
  Source: [[2026-03-12 Western AI models 'fail spectacularly' in farms and forests abroad]].
