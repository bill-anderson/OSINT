# National Communications Authority (NCA, Ghana)

Ghana's telecommunications regulator (licensing, spectrum, and the communications sector under the Ministry of Communications and Digitalization). It publishes the **Quarterly Statistical Bulletin on Communications in Ghana**, the authoritative source for subscription and penetration data ([[infra.connect]], [[data.statistics]]), and is a lead participant in the planned biometric SIM-registration framework linking SIMs to the [[national-identification-authority-ghana|NIA]] database. Director-General (2025): Rev. Edmund Yirenkyi Fianko. See [[GHA]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Telecom statistics, Q2 2024.** [[data.statistics]] Mobile voice subscriptions **37.55m** (penetration **114.63%**) at end-Q2 2024; three MNOs — MTN **75.41%** (28.32m), Telecel **16.97%** (6.37m), AT **7.62%** (2.86m). Reference figures — cite, do not treat as current. Source: [[2024-09-01 Quarterly Statistical Bulletin on Communications in Ghana Q2 2024 (NCA)]].
- **Co-leading the biometric SIM framework (2025-09).** [[gov.legislate]] The NCA joined operators and the NIA in shaping the planned Legislative Instrument to link SIMs to the Ghana Card via a Centralized Equipment Identity Register (CEIR); DG Fianko said the measures would make the forthcoming SIM-registration drive seamless. Source: [[2025-09-08 Ghana plans new framework for SIM card-digital ID linkage]].
- **Named in Ghana's institutional map (2024-09-20).** Listed as the communications regulator/licensing authority under MoCD. Source: [[2024-09-20 Ghana Digital Economy (US Country Commercial Guide)]].
