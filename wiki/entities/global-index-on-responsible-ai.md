# Global Index on Responsible AI (GIRAI)

A comparative index measuring whether governments' responsible-AI commitments actually reach the
people most exposed to AI harm — produced by the South Africa-based [[global-center-on-ai-governance]]
(lead author Rachel Adams). A standing reference in this wiki: a dated fact drawn from it becomes a
normal source citing this instrument. Reference document: `2026-07-11 Global-Index-on-Responsible-AI-2026.pdf`.

## Summary (2nd Edition, 2026)

- Assesses **135 countries** on how far commitments reach in practice (scored /100 across rights,
  redress and oversight), built from **>68,000 data points** gathered by in-country researchers.
  Data period **1 Nov 2023 – 30 Sep 2025**.
- **Global average score: 35/100.** 126 of 135 governments show a *commitment*, but implementation
  lags: where frameworks are active, only **55%** show evidence of implementation — **45%** in the
  Global South.
- A **two-tier world**: 22% of Global-South cases were addressed by *binding* instruments vs **58%**
  in the North; the **EU AI Act alone accounts for ~two-thirds** of enforceable protections gained
  since the 1st Edition. GIRAI reframes low Southern scores as *development gaps*, not ambition gaps.
- Where protections cluster: **AI literacy** strongest (active in 106 countries); at the weak end,
  **fewer than 1 in 5** governments disclose their own use of AI, only **39** protect workers, and
  just **28** have independent oversight bodies.
- Funded by IDRC (Canada) and the FCDO; CC BY 4.0. Directly relevant to Africa's AI-governance and
  data-sovereignty debate (see [[tech.ai]], [[gov.policy]]).
