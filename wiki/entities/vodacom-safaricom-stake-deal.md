**Parties** — [[vodacom]] (Vodacom Group, buyer), the **Government of Kenya** (seller of 15%) and **Vodafone** (seller of 5%); target is [[safaricom]].

**What** — Vodacom Group acquires a **controlling stake in Safaricom**, raising its holding from **35% to 55%**. It buys **15% from the Government of Kenya** and a further **5% from Vodafone**, both at **34 Kenyan shillings (US$0.26) per share**.

**When / where** — announced **4 December 2025**; Safaricom operates in **Kenya** and **Ethiopia**, and Vodacom is headquartered in **South Africa** — hence approvals are required from regulatory and governmental authorities in **Kenya, Ethiopia and South Africa**.

**Value** — **US$2.1 billion**.

**Status** — announced and subject to regulatory/governmental approvals in the three jurisdictions; pending as of the 4 December 2025 report.

## Significance

The deal is a milestone in Vodacom's **Vision 2030** strategy of deepening leadership in Africa's high-growth markets. On completion, Safaricom moves under **IFRS full consolidation** (from associate-basis accounting), lifting Vodacom Group revenue **towards R220 billion (US$12.9 billion)**. The group already reports over **223.2 million customers** and **93.7 million financial-services users** across Africa, including Safaricom. Control of Safaricom strengthens Vodacom's position in **M-Pesa** (Kenya's dominant mobile-money platform) and in Safaricom's Ethiopian expansion, scaling digital and financial inclusion — hence the `dpi.pay` and `infra.connect` tags.

**Kenya retains 20%** of Safaricom plus **board representation**. Cabinet Secretary for the National Treasury **John Mbadi** framed the sale as one of the first steps in President **William Ruto's** agenda of "innovatively unlocking capital, without increasing taxes or the country's debt burden." Safaricom remains listed on the **Nairobi Securities Exchange** and is Kenya's biggest company by market capitalisation. Quotes from Vodacom Group CEO **Shameel Joosub** and Safaricom CEO **Peter Ndegwa** both frame it as deepening a long-standing partnership.
