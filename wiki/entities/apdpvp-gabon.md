# APDPVP (Autorité pour la Protection des Données personnelles et de la Vie Privée, Gabon)

Gabon's **personal-data-protection authority** (the renamed successor to the CNPDCP) — president **Joël
Dominique Ledaga**. It anchors Gabon's data-localisation drive: it reviewed the **[[gabon-national-datacenter|national
data centre]]** for compliance (2026-06), tying digital sovereignty to citizen data-security.

## Statutory basis (established 2026-07-20)

Gabon has had an operative data-protection statute since **2011**, twice amended:

| Instrument | Date | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Loi n°001/2011** | promulgated **25 Sept 2011** (Décret n°01098/PR), *JO* n°74, 24–31 Oct 2011 | founding statute; **art. 15 created the CNPDCP** |
| **Ordonnance n°00002/PR/2020** | dated **31 Jan 2020**, *JO* n°60 *Bis* of **27 Mar 2020** | amends arts. 13, 14, 20, 37, 101, 102, 104; adds the right to erasure and portability (arts. 14 *bis*, 14 *ter*) and a bar on solely-automated adverse decisions; creates the **RPPVP** funding levy (art. 40 *bis*, **1,000 FCFA per person per year**, split **40% state / 60% Commission**) and the **DPD** data-protection-officer role (art. 51); raises fines to **1–100m FCFA** |
| **Loi n°025/2023** | adopted **9 July 2023**, promulgated **12 July 2023** (Décret n°166/PR), *JO* n°218 *Bis* of **15 July 2023** | **current governing text**; **art. 7 creates the APDPVP** in place of the CNPDCP; **art. 219 sets no grace period** |

The APDPVP therefore rests on **art. 7 of loi n°025/2023**, not on a standalone founding decree. The
Council of Ministers approved the reform on **1 February 2023**, five months before promulgation.

**The two amending instruments are now held in full** (established 2026-07-20): the **2020 ordonnance**
and the **2023 law**, both from the gazette. Claims about the current Gabonese data-protection regime can
therefore be cited to the statutes themselves rather than to secondary readings. The **1,000 FCFA RPPVP
levy** and the **art. 219 no-grace-period rule** — both previously absent from the wiki — come from those
texts. The **gazetted text of loi n°001/2011 itself is still not held (as of 2026-07-20)**; this matters
little for current state, since loi n°025/2023 is a 221-article restatement that repeals contrary prior
provisions (art. 221), but the 2011–2020 baseline is read through the amending texts, not the original.

**Citation caution.** Two dates circulate for the same instrument: the *Journal Officiel*'s own record
header and DLA Piper give **"du 09/07/2023"**, while the APDPVP's own hosted PDF and AFAPDP give
**"du 12 juillet 2023"** — matching the promulgation decree. These are one instrument (JO n°218 *Bis*),
not two; cite it as **loi n°025/2023, promulgated 12 July 2023**, noting adoption on 9 July. Separately,
africadataprotection.org miscategorises it as a "**Règlement**" — it is a **loi**.

**Powers relevant to this wiki.** Art. 78 requires automated processing to be **declared** to the APDPVP
(exceptions at arts. 80–82, 111); **art. 75 prohibits processing of biometric and genetic data** absent a
listed derogation — directly engaging the CNIE/NIP build at [[gabon--dpi-id]]; art. 66 gives a right to
object to **solely-automated decisions including profiling**. The precedent for authority review of the
biometric ID programme is old: the CNPDCP issued **Délibération n°004/2013 (12 Feb 2013)** on IBOGA,
pushing back on centralised storage of all ten fingerprints as potentially excessive for an
electoral-list purpose.

**Entry into force: no grace period.** The full gazetted text is now held
([[2023-07-15-gabon-loi-025-2023-protection-donnees-personnelles]]), and its final provisions settle a
point the wiki previously left open. **Art. 219**: *"Les responsables de traitement sont tenus de se
conformer dès publication de la présente loi."* Controllers' obligations therefore bite **from
publication — 15 July 2023** — with **no transitional compliance window**. This is the opposite of the
usual assumption for a data-protection statute of this scope, and it matters: every controller in Gabon
has been formally in-scope since that date, so any subsequent non-compliance is not excused by a phase-in.
Art. 220 leaves implementing texts to regulation and art. 221 repeals contrary prior provisions,
including parts of loi n°001/2011.

**Three dates, not one.** For this instrument the wiki distinguishes: **adopted 9 July 2023** ·
**promulgated 12 July 2023** (Décret n°166/PR, and the date borne by the law itself) · **published
15 July 2023** (*JO* n°218 *Bis*), which is also the **commencement** date for controller obligations by
art. 219. The same distinction applies to the 2020 amendment: **ordonnance dated 31 January 2020**,
**published 27 March 2020** (*JO* n°60 *Bis*) — [[2020-03-27-gabon-ordonnance-00002-pr-2020-protection-donnees]].

- **Enforcement (2026-01).** APDPVP **supervised the destruction of 2022 public-procurement bidders' personal
  data** held by the Direction Générale des Marchés Publics (DGMP) — unsuccessful bidders' files, past their
  legal retention period (arrêté n°032-22, art. 8) — a concrete data-governance action. Source:
  [[2026-01-20-gabon-apdpvp-procurement-data-destruction]].
