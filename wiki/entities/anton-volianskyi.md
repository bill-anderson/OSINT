# Anton Volianskyi

**CEO of [[wingu-africa]]** — the East African data-centre and cloud operator — as of
**2025-03**. (Wingu's Deputy CEO, COO and co-founder is Demos Kyriacou; see the
**[[wingu-africa]]** page.)

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launching Wingu's $50m Tanzania phase 2 (2025-03-19, Dar es Salaam).** Volianskyi launched
  the **second phase** of Wingu's Tanzania data centre — a **$50 million investment** — framing
  it as part of the company's African expansion and as readying the continent for AI-era
  workloads: *"As the world moves towards more data-driven technologies, Africa must be ready to
  support the power and connectivity requirements of these systems."* The **Tier 3** facility in
  the **Mbezi Industrial Area** is described as **the most connected data centre in Tanzania,
  with interconnections to 13 operators**, designed to **keep local internet traffic within the
  country**, reducing latency and costs, with a **99.983% uptime guarantee**. The investment adds
  **80 high-density racks, bringing the total to 110**, tailored to the financial and technology
  sectors. **[[tspar]]** secretary-general **Noah Maina** said extending the internet exchange to
  the facility keeps **data traffic local**. See [[infra.store]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[finance.new]], [[TZA]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 Tanzania's digital shift gathers pace with $50 million data centre]].

**Keep the objects distinct.** This **$50m data-centre phase 2** (March 2025) is a *different
object* from Wingu's **WCX cloud-exchange platform**, which launched in Tanzania in **November
2025**; and the **$50m investment** is a *different quantity* from Wingu's **$60m raise**.
Likewise the **99.983%** uptime figure belongs to the **Mbezi** facility — the **99.982%** on the
**[[wingu-africa]]** page is the **Djibouti TO7** site. Each figure stays attached to its own
facility.
