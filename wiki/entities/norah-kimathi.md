# Norah Kimathi

Kenyan engineer, founder of **[[zerobionics]]** (co-founded 2021), which builds AI-powered
humanoid robots that translate speech into sign language for deaf students. A graduate of
informatics and computer science from **Strathmore University**, and **22 years old and about to
graduate as of 2026-07-17**. Registered a first company, **Clyteds**, at 15 (now dormant).

## Positions on record (2026-07-17)

- **Origin of the product.** While mentoring young people in STEM she met deaf students struggling
  through STEM classes because qualified sign-language interpreters were scarce, and framed it as
  an engineering problem: *"If technology could automate factories, navigate roads, and diagnose
  disease, why couldn't it bridge one of education's oldest accessibility gaps?"*
- **On accessibility as a right.** Argues assistive tech in Africa has historically been treated
  as a compliance metric — "one person representing this, or chairs that looked a certain way to
  pass tests and be called inclusive" — rather than as a claim: education access "shouldn't be a
  privilege but a right."
- **On the funding climate.** Reports a **rise in interest and funding for assistive technology
  over the past two to three years (as of 2026-07)**, contrasted with a period when assistive-tech
  funding was near-unobtainable, and cites a large assistive-technology conference held **in Kenya
  in mid-2026** as evidence of the shift. *An impression from a practitioner, not a measured
  figure — the wiki holds no funding data on African assistive tech.*
- **On the cost constraint.** Names cost as the problem she most underestimated, aggravated by
  there being no community of comparable builders from whom to source or share components — hence
  recycled-plastic casings, cheaper components and material-frugal designs.

## Why she is held here

Kimathi is the sole named source for everything the wiki holds on ZeroBionics, and her account is
also the wiki's clearest first-hand statement of a **hardware-in-Africa** position: no
predecessors to copy, no local component ecosystem, and cost engineering as the precondition for
any deployment at all. See [[zerobionics]], [[tech.innovate]], [[include.access]].
