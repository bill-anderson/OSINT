# Huawei

Chinese technology multinational; a principal vehicle of China's digital-infrastructure
engagement in Africa (smart cities, national data centres, cloud, AI, skills). A
recurring [[geopol.china]] actor.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Namibia (2026-07).** Hosted President **Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah** at its Shenzhen HQ
  during her state visit to China. Collaborating with Namibia's Ministry of Home Affairs,
  Immigration, Safety and Security and the MICT on **smart-city solutions and an AI-ready
  national data centre**, plus digital-skills / workforce development. Senior VP Leo Chen
  framed Namibia's goals as aligned with Huawei's AI, cloud and digital-infrastructure
  expertise, pledging to help "safeguard data sovereignty." See [[NAM]],
  [[china-namibia-smart-city]], [[tech.ai]], [[infra.store]].
- **Ethiopia — enterprise/SME digital push with Ethio Telecom (2026-07-15).** Expanding its
  **[[ethio-telecom]]** partnership beyond telecoms infrastructure into **cloud, AI, cybersecurity, IoT,
  data-centre services and digital platforms** for enterprises/SMEs (Huawei N. Africa president Li Shen met
  CEO Frehiwot Tamru), under Ethio Telecom's Next Horizon: Digital & Beyond 2028 — plus local capability-
  building and affordable-device financing. Deepens the Chinese-vendor role in Ethiopia's enterprise-digital
  layer. See [[ETH]], [[tech.ai]], [[infra.store]]. Source: [[2026-07-15 Ethio Telecom, Huawei expand digital push]].
- **Zambia (2026-06).** Named (with ZTE) as an existing partner in Zambia's telecom networks and ICT-skills work as Lusaka and Beijing discussed deeper digital cooperation. See [[ZMB]], [[geopol.china]]. Source: [[2026-06-08 Zambia, China deepen digital ties]].
- **"African smart cities" analysis (2026-07).** Named across the Data Landscapers OSINT analysis as a **principal supplier** of the surveillance-capable infrastructure bundled into African "smart city" projects — deployed ahead of legal frameworks. See [[XAF]], [[infra.store]]. Source: [[2026-01-08 African smart cities A critical analysis]].
- **Chinese surveillance supplier — IDS report (2026-03/04).** Named, with **[[zte]]** and **[[hikvision]]**, across the UK IDS survey [[2026-03-01 Smart City Surveillance in Africa (IDS 2026)]] (11 countries, Chinese firms in every one) as a supplier of "safe city" surveillance; in the typical bundled ~$250m Eximbank package, ZTE/Huawei technicians build the AI command-and-control centre analysing camera streams (institutional lock-in). Kenya was an early adopter — **1,800 Huawei cameras** across Nairobi/Mombasa via a $100m Chinese grant after the 2013 Westgate attack. See [[KEN]], [[NGA]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-04-04 Chinese surveillance rolled out in Africa ZTE, Hikvision, Huawei at the helm]].
- **Ghana Girls-in-ICT AI training (2026-03).** At MWC 2026 (Barcelona), Huawei agreed to give **free AI training to 3,000 girls** in Ghana's Girls in ICT programme (graduates feeding the One Million Coders programme), amid Ghana's **$250m** AI-compute / 5G / smartphone-assembly push. See [[GHA]], [[capacity.training]], [[ghana-national-ai-strategy]], [[samuel-nartey-george]]. Source: [[2026-03-05 Ghana strikes AI training deal with Chinese multinational amid $250m tech investment push]].
- **Gabon MoU (2025-12-23).** Signed an MoU with the Gabonese government (Digital Economy
  Minister Mark-Alexandre Doumba; witnessed by Huawei CEMAC CEO Wang Lei), building on talks
  with President Oligui Nguema. Three pillars: a joint project committee, integration of
  Gabonese startups, and skills transfer "to ensure digital sovereignty"; expands the Connected
  Schools Program. See [[GAB]], [[gabon-huawei-mou]], [[finance.mou]], [[capacity.training]].
  Source: [[2026-01-05 Gabon and Huawei Forge Partnership to Boost National Digital Transformation]].
  An earlier engagement is corroborated in DPI reporting: Gabon signed an MoU with Huawei for a
  **smart-cities project**, with Huawei reported (Gabon Media Time) to have **pledged at least
  $234m** — the 2025-12 MoU builds on this. Source:
  [[2024-09-25-gabon-digital-id-forefront-transformation]].
- **South Sudan re-engagement (2026-02).** New global CEO **Wang Shibo** (in role since Dec 2025)
  met ICT minister Ateny Wek Ateny in Juba, pledging to expand partnerships and reaffirming
  Huawei's continued presence in South Sudan's ICT sector — a reconciliation more than a decade
  after a 2014 rift (Juba accused Huawei of hacking government emails and forging documents to
  damage a rival **[[zte]]** contract). See [[SSD]], [[infra.connect]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Huawei reconnects with South Sudan]].
- **Kenya public-sector skills MoU under discussion (2026-01-12).** Huawei Kenya and the
  **[[kenya-school-of-government]]** opened talks on a prospective MoU to train public servants
  in AI, cybersecurity, cloud and digital governance (nothing signed yet). See [[KEN]],
  [[capacity.training]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-12 Kenya, Huawei to boost public sector digital skills]].
- **5G lock-in analogy (2026-03).** In the US "Tech Corps" AI-diffusion analysis, Huawei's
  dominance in 5G equipment is invoked by US analysts as the cautionary precedent US policymakers
  fear repeating with the Chinese AI stack. See [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-02 The U.S. Launches a Peace Corps for AI to Compete With China]].
- **Safaricom "Idea-to-Cash" (Aug 2025; cited 2026-04).** Huawei's CBS Billing team and
  [[safaricom]] launched **"Idea-to-Cash,"** an AI platform that mines user behaviour to
  generate and configure financial products; an earlier pilot lifted ARPU **24%** by targeting
  free-data users. An ICT4D audit flags that its disclosed objective function is revenue
  optimisation rather than user welfare. See [[KEN]], [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 3 Reasons Mobile Money Programs Have a $1.7 Trillion Problem]].

- **Smartphone-brand dominance (Kariuki & Long, 2026-04-20).** In the Frontier
  Fintech "Payments Follow Trade" analysis of the Africa–China corridor, Huawei is
  named among the **four of Africa's top-five smartphone brands that are Chinese**
  (with Xiaomi) — the consumer-hardware layer of a China-Africa trade relationship
  that hit a record **$348bn in 2025**. See [[geopol.china]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-20 117 - Payments Follow Trade]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: pitched explicitly as *enabling* Namibian data sovereignty and smarter
  governance.
- `colonialism`: sovereignty delivered on a foreign vendor's stack and foreign finance —
  the recurring dependency question. See [[geopol.china]].
