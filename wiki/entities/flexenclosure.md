# Flexenclosure

**The Swedish company that built Chad's first data centre** — a prefabricated facility
manufactured in Sweden and shipped to N'Djamena. Flexenclosure enters this wiki through a single
historical fact in the reporting on Chad's current data-centre build, and is held as the
established counterparty to that 2016 predecessor facility.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Chad's first data centre (2016).** Chad saw its first data centre developed in **2016**. At a
  cost of **then $6 million**, the **374 sqm (4,025 sq ft)** facility was **built in Sweden by
  Flexenclosure and shipped to Chad's capital, N'Djamena**. It is designed to support an **IT
  power load of 400kW**. See [[infra.store]], [[infra.energy]], [[TCD]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-06 Chad invests 175m in national data center and networks]].

## Distinguish from

The 2016 Flexenclosure facility is **not** the [[chad-national-data-centre]] — the sovereign state
data facility built a decade later under Phase I of [[pmice]], whose size and location remain
undisclosed (as of 2026-01-06). Data Center Dynamics reports the 2016 build as historical context
for the new project, not as the same asset. The sources hold nothing on the 2016 facility's
current operator, status or utilisation.

## Reading it

Worth noting for the sovereignty framing around the new build: Chad's first data centre was a
**Swedish-manufactured box shipped in**. Nothing in the sources establishes Flexenclosure's
involvement — or non-involvement — in the current National Data Center.
