# Cassava Technologies

Pan-African digital-infrastructure group founded/chaired by **[[strive-masiyiwa]]**
(out of the [[econet-group]] lineage) — the central private actor in this wiki's
**African digital-sovereignty** thread. This page is an **index** into the group; the
detail lives on the subsidiary/initiative pages.

## Group structure (hub → detail)

- **[[africa-data-centres]]** — continental colocation (12 renewable-powered halls; Cape
  Town CPT1/CPT2; $300m [[us-dfc]] facility).
- **[[liquid-c2]]** — connectivity, cloud, cyber-security, AI (Liquid Intelligent
  Technologies' ~100,000 km fibre; Google Cloud + [[anthropic]] partner).
- **[[cassava-ai]]** — the AI arm, owning **[[caimex]]** (multi-model LLM exchange) and
  **[[national-sovereign-cloud]]** (government cloud).
- **[[cassava-ai-factories]]** — the ~$720m, 5-country GPU "AI factory" program.
- Philanthropy/skills: founding technology partner of the **[[aisca-foundation]]**.

## The sovereign-compute thesis

Cassava's pitch is that African data can be trained and governed on African soil. The
[[data-landscapers]] essay [[2026-06-16 The digital sovereignty dividing line]] tests
this and finds the stack African only at the app layer — chips ([[nvidia]]), cloud and
models remain US and CLOUD-Act-reachable. The **[[2025-11-12 Cassava Tech - Partners with Google to bring Google Gemini to millions in africa]]**
Gemini deal sharpens the tension: distributing a US hyperscaler model under a
sovereignty banner. The Rest of World survey of Africa's AI-sovereignty push frames
Cassava's Nvidia AI factory (SA, launched Mar 2025) as exactly this Western-tech-backed
"sovereign" build — African at the app layer, US at chips/cloud/models
([[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]]). The
continent-wide $4.5bn data-centre race lists Cassava among the hyperscalers placing bets
(3,000 Nvidia GPUs across SA by mid-2025, expanding to NGA/KEN/EGY/MAR)
([[2026-05-21 Africa Data Center Investment 2026 The $4.5B Race and the Power Problem]]).
Skills arm: [[liquid-c2]] + Microsoft launched a solar-powered mobile digital lab in
North West, SA (May 2026). On the demand side, Russell Southwood (2026-05-19) reports
Cassava took **3,000 GPUs in Cape Town (June 2025, phase 1)** with early uptake from
Rockefeller Foundation grantees and **~12,000** more planned across EGY/NGA/KEN/MAR —
concrete traction in an otherwise thin specialist-AI market; Anjola Adebowale (2026-06-12)
cites the **~$720m** partnership as a sovereign-compute benchmark. See [[geopol.usa]],
[[tech.ai]], [[infra.store]]. Sources:
[[2026-05-19 Trying to make sense of who will use Artificial Intelligence in Africa and how – Going beyond the hype]],
[[2026-06-12 Beyond the AI Stack Why Governments Need Dynamic Digital Infrastructure]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Google $90M equity round + Umoja cable (2024).** [[google]] took a **direct equity stake**
  in Cassava (the $90m equity tranche of a $310m raise, with [[us-dfc]] and Finnfund) — deal
  [[google-cassava-90m-round]] — alongside the connectivity partnership: Cassava's [[liquid-c2]]
  /Liquid Intelligent built the **terrestrial leg of Google's Umoja cable** ([[umoja-cable]],
  Kenya→[[COD]]→[[RWA]]→[[UGA]]→[[ZMB]]→[[ZWE]]→South Africa). See [[finance.new]],
  [[infra.connect]]. Sources:
  [[2024-12-17 Google joins $90M investment into Cassava to bolster Africa's digital infrastructure]],
  [[2024-05-28 Google invests in terrestrial-subsea cable linking Kenya to Australia]].
- **Cassava Autonomous Network (2026-03).** Debuted an **NVIDIA-backed agentic** solution that
  self-optimises mobile RAN (minor-fault repair cut from 4 days to ~35 min, +75% efficiency;
  all vendors, 2G–5G), powered by NVIDIA NIM microservices and running on **[[caimex]]**. See
  [[cassava-ai-factories]], [[nvidia]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-03 Cassava unveils NVIDIA-backed autonomous network solution]].
- **National Sovereign Cloud + AI Factory (2026-03, MWC/launch).** Launched the
  **[[national-sovereign-cloud]]** for governments (CLOUD-Act-exposure pitch) via a Cloud
  Partner Programme, and began deploying its **[[cassava-ai-factories]]** AI Factory in SA
  (billed "continent's first NVIDIA Cloud Partner"; GPUaaS/AIaaS; languages Swahili→Zulu/
  Afrikaans), scaling to NGA/KEN/EGY/MAR. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[tech.ai]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-06 Cassava launches sovereign cloud for Africa's public sector]],
  [[2026-03-18 Cassava Technologies Launches AI Factory in South Africa, Plans Expansion Across Africa]].
- **Partner Experience Centre, Johannesburg (2026-04).** [[liquid-c2]] opened Africa's
  first **Google Cloud-powered Partner Experience Centre** in Johannesburg — partner
  accreditation, **Gemini Enterprise** / "Gemini Playspace," and reseller local-currency
  billing and credit. CEO Hardy Pemhiwa called it "the first cloud and AI experience
  centre of its kind ... on the African continent"; chairman [[strive-masiyiwa]] declared
  "the time for talking about AI is over." Pairs with the upcoming **Cassava AI factory in
  Cape Town** (billed the largest on the continent), with further [[cassava-ai-factories]]
  planned in [[KEN]], then [[NGA]], [[MAR]] and [[EGY]]. See [[tech.ai]], [[google]],
  [[ZAF]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-08 Liquid C2, Google open first-for-Africa Partner Experience Centre]],
  [[2026-04-08 Liquid C2's new centre puts Google Cloud within Africa's reach]].
- **DPI sovereignty roundtable (2026-04, 58th UNECA Conference of Ministers).**
  [[africa-data-centres]] CEO Adil el Youssefi argued Africa must **10× its data-centre
  capacity** and build local "AI factories," noting the continent generates **<1% of
  global data** despite nearly 20% of world population. See [[uneca]], [[infra.store]],
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].
- **US–China AI battleground (2026-03).** A Business Insider Africa survey of the
  continental AI race lists Cassava's Nvidia-backed data-centre build (~$700m across
  [[EGY]], [[NGA]], [[KEN]], [[MAR]]) among the US-aligned bets, set against Chinese
  models — [[deepseek]] at 11–14% of African chatbot usage (~20% in [[ETH]]/[[ZWE]]) —
  gaining on affordability and BRI-financed infrastructure. Situates Cassava's "sovereign"
  compute pitch inside a US-vs-China contest for African AI infrastructure. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.china]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12 China's DeepSeek gives US tech giants a run for their money as Microsoft and others expand AI push in Africa]].
