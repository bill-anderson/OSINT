# South Africa digital-identity regulations (DHA Identification Regulations amendments & NIR Bill)

The emerging legal and regulatory basis for South Africa's national digital identity system, run by the Department of Home Affairs (DHA) under minister Leon Schreiber. It comprises draft amendments to the Identification Regulations, 1998 (published for comment 2026-05-04) and a separate, forthcoming National Identification and Registration (NIR) Bill intended to underpin the system. Compliance backdrop: [[popia]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Draft Identification Regulations published (2026-05-04; comment to 2026-06-06).** DHA gazetted draft amendments introducing voluntary digital-ID credentials issued by the Director-General with legal parity to physical ID; "trusted entities" (banks, telcos, agencies) granted API access to the population register under DG-approved data-sharing agreements; three identity-assurance levels (register check / +biometric / in-person with liveness); and audit logs kept at least seven years. Source: [[2026-05-29 Opinion South Africa's Digital Identity Regulations A Governance Crossroads]].
- **Implementation timeline (APP 2026/27, tabled 30 Mar 2026).** Hosting infrastructure to be complete by 31 Mar 2027; platform live and first verifiable credentials issued via mobile wallet in 2027/28. The PKI / certificate-authority / platform stack is to be built inside the SARS hosting environment, extending an April-2025 Home Affairs–SARS digital pact. Facial recognition is the primary biometric, fingerprints secondary. DHA's own plan concedes cyber maturity "remains low." Source: [[2026-04-21 South Africa's digital ID gets a targeted launch date]].
- **Governance critique — Mark Burke (2026-05-29).** Named-analyst opinion argues the regulations fall short on rights: no right to erasure of biometric data, weak/implied consent, no citizen access logs or notification, no selective disclosure, and power concentrated in the Director-General (rule-setting, accreditation, standards, enforcement). Burke also warns the regulations may be ultra vires — making substantive policy under the pre-smartphone Identification Act, 1997 ahead of the NIR Bill that should provide the proper statutory foundation. Frames a [[sovereignty]] / citizen-control tension. Source: [[2026-05-29 Opinion South Africa's Digital Identity Regulations A Governance Crossroads]].
- **Traditional-leader opposition (2026-03).** Leaders affiliated with Amadumisa (under the United Kingdoms of South Africa, UCoSA) publicly opposed the proposed digital ID, demanding prior consultation, public education and possibly a **referendum** before rollout; they flagged fraud risk, rural exclusion (no smartphones/reliable internet) and privacy/"microchip" fears — a grassroots-legitimacy challenge to the DHA rollout. See [[include.divides]], [[digital.rural]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-03-31 Concerns over South Africa's proposed digital ID system from traditional leaders]].
- **SARS single-ID push (2026-04).** [[sars]] Commissioner Edward Kieswetter framed a whole-of-government drive (Modernisation 3.0) for one biometric-secured digital identity per person — the population register as the single identity — to replace the ~7–8 identifiers each of ~62m South Africans holds and end separate Home Affairs ID and SARS tax numbers. Reinforces the plan to build the digital-ID/PKI platform inside the SARS hosting environment; SARS is separately partnering [[sarb]] on an instant-payment system. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-04-03 Say goodbye to SARS tax numbers in South Africa]].
- **Bank-branch Smart ID rollout (2026-03-09).** [[home-affairs-south-africa]] (minister Leon Schreiber) launched its first **live bank-branch** Smart ID partnership (with Capitec, at Orange Farm), ending a decade in which bank access was limited to just 30 branches for 63m people. A secure **digital gateway** lets banks integrate with Home Affairs systems while DHA remains custodian of the National Population Register; the drive accelerates migration off the **green ID book** (~16m still hold it) — a FATF grey-list and identity-fraud concern — under the "Home Affairs @ home" programme. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-03-09 Home affairs launches Smart ID partnership with banks]].
- **ETA as biometric visa gateway (2026-02).** DHA is making the **[[south-africa-eta]]** the sole channel for tourist (then all) visas, retiring diplomatic-mission processing after an SIU report found ~20 years of visa maladministration. The ETA runs **machine-learning document checks, facial recognition and rules-based automated decisions with no human review** (initially for China/India/Indonesia/Mexico nationals via three airports) and is to be wired to the smart population register and future biometric digital ID, with facial recognition at airports/land borders alongside the BMA and [[sars]]. It had already rejected 30,000+ applications. See [[dpi.id]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-02-24 South Africa makes the ETA the cornerstone of its visa reform]].
- **MyMzansi single entry point (2026-03).** Per the Operation Vulindlela phase-two report, the unified digital-government platform **[[mymzansi]]** goes live to test groups in March 2026 — a zero-rated app connecting digital identity, payments and services (licences, health, social, home-affairs), backed by the **MzansiXchange** data-exchange pilot (Oct 2025) and a national digital-government standards framework now underway. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[operation-vulindlela]]. Source: [[2026-02-02 Momentum builds on SA's single digital entry point MyMzansi]].
- **Rollout behind schedule (as of 2026-01-28).** Home Affairs confirmed the digital-ID **policy is still under development** (Cabinet approval then public comment pending), so the system will take several more years despite the Planning Ministry punting an end-2026 rollout — Schreiber's April-2025 "shortly to Cabinet" not met, and MyMzansi's functional-ID target set to slip. See [[mymzansi]], [[include.divides]]. Source: [[2026-01-28 Bad news about South Africa's newest ID book plan]].
