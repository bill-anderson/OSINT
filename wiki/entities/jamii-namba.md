# Jamii Namba (Universal ID), Tanzania

Tanzania's **foundational national digital ID programme**, run by **[[nida-tanzania]]** — the
"Universal ID" project whose stated design principle is registration **from birth**: *"Upon
birth, a child will receive both a birth certificate and a National ID number and document"*
(**[[edson-guyai]]**, NIDA Director of ID Management, 2025-06). **Naming variant:** *Jamii
Namba* and *Jamii Number* are the same programme — Swahili and English renderings of one name.

## Coverage — corroborated by two independent routes

Two accounts of NIDA's coverage arrive by **completely different routes** — an international
conference panel and a parliamentary budget speech — and **agree closely**. Independent
corroboration of a state's own ID coverage claims is uncommon enough to be worth stating.

- **ID4Africa 2025 AGM (2025-06-17), Edson Guyai, NIDA Director of ID Management:** **over 25
  million** registered; eligible age **18+**; the **2022 census** puts the 18+ population at
  **over 31.5 million** — **"about 81 percent"** of the eligible adult population covered;
  **over 21.2 million IDs personalised**; **more than 20.1 million cards issued**. Source:
  [[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].
- **Ministry of Home Affairs budget speech (2025-05-27), Minister Innocent Bashungwa, DODOMA:**
  **844,994** registered July 2024–April 2025, total **over 25.8 million = 81.9%** of the
  **31,582,976** aged 18+ (2022 Population and Housing Census); **757,189 NINs** generated in the
  period, total **21,514,741 = 83.2%** of those registered. Source:
  [[2025-05-27 Home Affairs unveils 2.06tri- budget]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **The ID ecosystem as a revenue line (2025-06-17).** Guyai: NIDA has **integrated with over 124
  institutions**, public and private, and ID verification services have generated
  **approximately $45.4 million**, charged at **$0.18 per transaction** plus fees for card
  replacement and legal-resident registration. Registration extends to citizens, legal
  residents, refugees and stateless persons. He credited progress to political commitment,
  sustainable financing, stakeholder engagement, and use cases — notably **SIM card
  registration, which boosted ID registration by about 25 percent**. Named challenges: legal
  constraints, financial shortfalls, geographic accessibility, and a lack of foundational
  documents. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].
- **Infant registration: the biometric workaround (2025-06-17).** NIDA planned infant biometric
  registration for **children below age five**, with a pilot **expected in July 2025** in
  selected regions with **[[unicef]]** support, framed as **integral to Jamii Namba**. The most
  striking detail: **because NIDA could not find a biometric solution that works for under-fives,
  it opted for the interim measure of linking the child's identity to the mother's National ID.**
  Guyai also said NIDA was **"upgrading ICT infrastructure, reforming our registration processes,
  and reviewing our legal frameworks to align with these new goals."** Separately, **$4.5
  million** was budgeted for a minors-enrolment pilot — which **the article itself flags as
  *seemingly separate*** from the UNICEF pilot; that hedge is preserved here. See [[unicef]],
  [[id4africa]], [[rita-tanzania]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].
- **The pilot ran ahead of its own legislation — a real finding, not a contradiction.** An
  operational UNICEF-backed infant-registration pilot was slated for **July 2025**, while the
  enabling **[[tanzania-infant-registration-bill]]** was still **unpassed as of January 2026** —
  **[[james-kaji]]**, NIDA Director General, said then that it was only *"preparing to be tabled
  in the next Parliament."* The pilot therefore ran roughly **six months ahead of the law meant
  to authorise the activity**. The two sources are internally consistent about this (Guyai
  expressly said NIDA was "reviewing our legal frameworks to align with these new goals"), so
  this is **not** a contradiction between them — it is a finding about sequencing: capability
  first, statutory basis after. See [[gov.legislate]], [[sovereignty]]. Sources:
  [[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]];
  [[2026-01-04 Tanzania rolls out infant registration for stronger citizen ID]].
- **Under-18 registration budgeted (2025-05-27).** The FY2025/26 Home Affairs budget named as a
  critical new initiative the **commencement of registration and identification of individuals
  under 18**, to be issued national identification numbers. See
  [[ministry-of-home-affairs-tanzania]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-27 Home Affairs unveils 2.06tri- budget]].
- **Enrolment is hybrid, not digital end-to-end (undated Baker McKenzie guidance).** Eligibility
  is **citizens aged 18+ and legal residents with valid permits**; **it is not possible to obtain
  a National ID without a valid residence permit**. The process runs: online form via the NIDA
  portal → **a local government office for the Local Chairman's stamp and signature** → the NIDA
  District Office → a biometric appointment. That middle step is a concrete
  **[[include.access]]** friction point in an otherwise "online" service. **Attribution and
  currency:** this **[[baker-mckenzie]]** page is **undated** (`date_source: proxy`, year
  precision); its biometric modality set — **fingerprints, photo and signature** — is the
  **pre-Phase-II baseline** and **must not** be read as current-state modalities against the
  2026-04-22 Phase II source, which adds **iris and facial** recognition. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 Tanzania - National Identification Card guidance (Baker McKenzie)]].
