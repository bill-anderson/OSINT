# Department of Immigration & Civil Status (ICS), Seychelles

**Seychelles' identity and civil-registration authority**, sitting under the Ministry of Internal
Affairs. It issues National Identity Numbers (NINs) and ID cards, administers work permits, and
holds the civil register constituted under the [[seychelles-civil-status-act]]. It is the issuing
body for both of Seychelles' citizen-facing identity services in this wiki — [[seyid]] and
[[certexpress]] — delivered in partnership with [[dict-seychelles]]. See [[dpi.id]],
[[dpi.registry]], [[SYC]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launched two digital services (2024-09-18).** At a press conference at the Ministry of
  Internal Affairs, the ICS team with DICT introduced [[seyid]] virtual ID cards and the
  [[certexpress]] online certificate platform, framed as part of ongoing efforts to modernise
  public services, improve efficiency and "embrace environmentally-friendly solutions." Source:
  [[2024-09-18 Launch of New Digital Solutions for Public Services]].
- **Stopped issuing physical IDs to new permit holders (from 2024-09-17).** New work-permit
  holders are registered on SeyID and issued a **virtual ID card** valid for the permit's
  duration; the NIN process is unchanged (an ID letter carries the assigned NIN). Seychellois
  citizens **retain** the physical-ID option (ten-year expiry), and citizens abroad can register
  with a NIN card or ID letter plus passport. PS for Immigration **Alain Volcere** cited reduced
  administrative burden, faster identity verification and fewer in-person visits; the department
  framed the change as reducing waste and prioritising Seychellois citizens in card issuance.
  See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]]. Sources:
  [[2024-09-18 Launch of New Digital Solutions for Public Services]],
  [[2024-09-18 Seychelles Makes Digital ID Mandatory for Work Permit Holders]].
- **Civil registration.** CertExpress delivers **birth, marriage and death certificates** online
  in **2 working days** at an unchanged **SCR 50** (as announced 2024-09), saved to the E-Doc
  wallet in the SeyID app — the digital channel onto the register whose legal basis is the
  [[seychelles-civil-status-act]] (originally 1893; consolidated as at 2022-11-01). See
  [[dpi.registry]]. Sources: [[2024-09-18 Launch of New Digital Solutions for Public Services]],
  [[2022-11-01 Seychelles Civil Status Act Cap 34 (as at 1 Nov 2022)]].
