# ASCENT Eswatini (Accelerating Sustainable and Clean Energy Access Transformation)

**A [[world-bank]]-financed energy-access project for [[SWZ]], announced 2025-02-13** —
the **ninth phase** of the ASCENT programme for Eastern and Southern Africa, aimed at
reaching the **remaining 12% of the population (2025-02-13)** without electricity and
putting the country on a path to universal access by **2030**. See [[infra.energy]],
[[finance.new]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Financing (2025-02-13).** **US$39m** concessional funding from **IDA**, a **US$51m**
  loan from **IBRD**, and a **US$10m** performance-based grant from the **Livable Planet
  Fund (LPF1)** — US$100m in total. Source:
  [[2025-02-13 World Bank Expands Support to Electrify Rural Eswatini, Reaching 200,000 People]].
- **Targets (2025-02-13).** Electricity access for **200,000 people**, connecting
  **50,000 new households over five years** — a **20% increase** on the **249,014
  households connected in 2023**. Grid and off-grid solutions, particularly in
  underserved rural communities. See [[digital.rural]], [[include.access]].
- **Policy context.** Builds on the ongoing Network Reinforcement and Access Project
  Eswatini; follows the country's **Rural Electrification Plan** and the **National
  Energy Policy 2018**. Deputy Prime Minister Thulisile Dladla tied it to Eswatini's
  endorsement of the **Dar es Salaam Energy Declaration** at the Mission 300 Africa
  Energy Summit. Welcomed by [[satu-kahkonen]] and Minister Thambo Gina. Press Release
  No: 2025/048/AFE.
- **Why it sits in this wiki.** Rural electrification is the precondition beneath
  Eswatini's rural digital agenda — the offline-first design of [[eswatini-cbhis]] and
  the rural-broadband ambitions of [[eswatini-economic-update-2025]] both presuppose
  power. No source in the wiki links ASCENT to any digital programme directly.
