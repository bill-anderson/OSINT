# Ministry of Lands, Housing and Urban Development (MLHUD), Uganda

**Uganda's land ministry** — custodian of the **1998 Land Act** and the **2013 Uganda
National Land Policy**, and the body that approved and gazetted the digital **Certificate
of Customary Ownership (CCO)** template. Approximately **80% of Uganda's land is held
under customary tenure**, which makes the CCO the primary documentary instrument of land
rights for most Ugandans.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digital CCOs with GLTN / UN-Habitat (year-precision, proxy date — implementer's own
  account, attribute).** Since **2014**, [[gltn]] (within the Land, Housing and Shelter
  Section of [[un-habitat]]) has partnered with MLHUD to support National Land Policy
  implementation — strengthening customary land rights, improving women's access to land,
  developing the national gender strategy for the NLP, and building partner capacity on
  pro-poor, fit-for-purpose land tools. **Over 7,684 parcels were registered for CCOs**
  through these efforts. Certificates were previously typewritten and issued as hard
  copies — slow, expensive and error-prone. The digital CCO template, built on the
  **[[stdm]]** Social Tenure Domain Model, was **approved by MLHUD and gazetted** by the
  Uganda Printing and Publishing Corporation in the Uganda Gazette (**notice of
  18 September 2020**); it adds a **unique QR code**, a parcel map, provision for Area Land
  Committee signatures and stamp, and a Uganda Coat of Arms watermark. *The GLTN blog is
  undated; the assigned 2024 year rests on an intake assertion and the only hard in-body
  date is the 2020 Gazette notice — do not reason about it as a 2024 datapoint.* See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.rural]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2024-01-01 Uganda Moves to Digital Certificates of Customary Ownership to secure land rights and improve land use]].
