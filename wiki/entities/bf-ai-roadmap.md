# Burkina Faso National AI Roadmap (2026–2030) / sovereign-AI-by-language

Burkina Faso's national AI policy, which crossed from **"no strategy" to a validated roadmap** over
2025–26 — and whose distinctive slant is **sovereign AI by language**: building AI that works in the
national languages rather than importing French/English-only tools. It sits inside the same digital-
sovereignty doctrine as the [[bf-national-datacenters|"zero data abroad"]] build-out. See [[BFA]],
[[tech.ai]].

## What we know

- **From no strategy to a roadmap (2025→26).** In **April 2025** a national research grant (**CITADEL**,
  **$1m IDRC**, Mooré–French machine translation) was reported against the flat statement that Burkina had
  **no national AI strategy**; a **Maison de l'IA** launched (Jul 2025) and a national AI conference opened
  the debate (Oct 2025); by **June 2026** the **National AI Roadmap 2026–2030** was **validated**, tied to
  the RELANCE Plan under an "AI for All" banner. By mid-2026 Burkina was counted among the **four UEMOA
  states** already holding a national AI strategy. Sources:
  [[2025-04-16-citadel-financement-recherche-ia]],
  [[2026-06-17-national-ai-roadmap-2026-2030-validated-techafricanews]].
- **Sovereign AI by language (pillar 11).** [[include.divides]] A flagship strand builds an **LLM corpus
  in Mooré, Dioula, Fulfuldé and Gulmancema** — "culturally-inspired AI" framed as digital sovereignty and
  inclusion. Panels at the SIPEN-UEMOA / Grand Salon de la Data cast **local-language voice AI + open data
  + sovereign datacentres** as the stack. Source: [[2026-03-25-burkina-faso-culturally-inspired-ai-local-languages]].
- **Capacity + governance.** [[capacity.training]] **SPIVTEN** trained ~30 **ALP lawmakers** (Jul 2026)
  ahead of an AI regulatory framework; the **US** launched AI training for youth across Ouagadougou,
  Bobo-Dioulasso and Koudougou (2026). Sources: [[2026-07-15-ia-deputes-formes]],
  [[2026-01-28-us-burkina-faso-alliance-ai-training-youths]].
