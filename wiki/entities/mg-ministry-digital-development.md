# Madagascar Ministry of Digital Development, Posts & Telecommunications

The Malagasy government ministry (Ministère du Développement Numérique, des Postes et des
Télécommunications, MNDPT) leading Madagascar's national digital-identity programme, public-administration
digitisation and digital-economy strategy. It is the implementing ministry for **[[prodigy-madagascar|
PRODIGY]]**, working through the **[[oni-madagascar|National Office for Identification]]** and the
**[[ugd-madagascar|Digital Governance Unit]]**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **National biometric-ID pilot (27 Jan 2026).** The ministry launched the pilot phase of the national
  biometric-registration programme, enrolling the head of state and government first to stress-test the
  infrastructure (fingerprints, face and iris → a Unique Identification Number). Nationwide rollout targets
  **2m adults (Apr–Jun 2026)**, then ~8m rural residents; full ID for 12m+ citizens. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[dpi.govtech]]. Sources: [[2026-01-27 Madagascar Tests Biometric ID System, Aims to Register 2 Million People by Mid-2026]],
  [[2026-01-28 Madagascar digital ID pilot kicks off, testing infrastructure ahead of nationwide rollout]].
- **Digital-economy strategy.** Runs the **Digital Transformation Plan 2023–2028 (Plan Stratégique
  Numérique, PSN)**, aiming to lift the sector's GDP share; launched "Choose Digital Madagascar" (Feb 2025)
  and a UNDP-backed **Digital Readiness Assessment** (Feb 2025). Regulator ARTEC; postal service PAOMA.
  Sources: [[2025-08-04 Madagascar - Digital Economy (Country Commercial Guide)]],
  [[2025-03-04 Madagascar Launches Digital Readiness Assessment to Tackle Hurdles]].
- **Civil-registry reform.** The 4 July 2025 identification bill modernises the national ID system and
  overhauls civil registration (PRODIGY alignment). Source:
  [[2025-07-07 Madagascar codifies civil registry reform, readies biometric ID card tender]].
