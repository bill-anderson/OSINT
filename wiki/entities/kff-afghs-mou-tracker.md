The **KFF Tracker of America First MOU Bilateral Global Health Agreements** is a standing, continuously-updated dataset maintained by **KFF** (Kaiser Family Foundation) documenting the bilateral health-cooperation MOUs signed under the U.S. **[[america-first-global-health-strategy]]** (AFGHS).

## What it tracks

Following the AFGHS release on **18 September 2025**, the U.S. began signing bilateral **Memorandums of Understanding** with countries that receive U.S. global-health assistance. Each MOU is a **five-year plan (2026–2030)** intended to transition programs from U.S. assistance toward "country ownership," with partner countries pledging to increase domestic health co-investment as U.S. assistance declines. Signing began in late 2025 and is ongoing; implementation slated for later in 2026.

The tracker captures, by country:
- Which MOUs have been **signed to date**
- **Funding** and **co-financing** amounts
- **Program areas** (including global health security)
- Historical vs proposed five-year funding comparisons

## Sources and method (how we use it)

Data are drawn from publicly available **U.S. State Department, U.S. embassy, and partner-country Ministry of Health** press releases plus **MOU texts** where public. KFF flags an important limitation: full MOU text — the most detailed source — is public for **only a limited number of countries**, so program-area coverage (captured via keyword search) may be incomplete. Country funding totals, where MOU text exists, are summed from annual amounts in the text.

- **First published:** 13 January 2026; **most recently updated:** 1 July 2026 (updated as agreements are signed).

This is the go-to running reference for *which* countries have signed AFGHS MOUs and on *what financial terms*.
