# Bank of Tanzania (BOT)

Tanzania's **central bank** — and, unusually, the **builder and operator of the national retail
payments switch** as well as its regulator. BOT licenses and supervises financial service
providers, sets financial-consumer-protection rules by regulation, and runs
**[[tips-tanzania]]** (the Tanzania Instant Payment System) in-house through its National
Payments Directorate.

## Activity in this wiki

- **TIPS built and operated in-house (BOT/FSDT case study, dated 2025-05-16 — see date caveat
  below).** TIPS is a national **real-time retail payment switch built and operated in-house by
  the Bank of Tanzania**, interoperating banks and non-bank digital financial service providers
  (DFSPs). It was built **from scratch from 2018** with support from **[[fsdt]]** and the
  **[[gates-foundation]]**, on **Level One Project** design principles. Reported volumes: **over
  453.7m interoperable retail transactions worth over TZS 29.9 trillion in 2024**, against
  **63.2m active mobile money wallets at end-2024** (TCRA Q4 2024 — see **[[tcra]]**). An
  in-house central-bank build is a markedly different sovereignty posture from licensing a
  vendor switch. See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-16 Building an Inclusive Payments Ecosystem in Tanzania through TIPS (BOT and FSDT)]].
  **Date caveat:** that case study is `date_source: proxy` — no date is printed in the document
  and 2025-05-16 is a CMS filename stamp, so the day precision is unverified.
- **Regulatory lineage of mobile money.** A **2006 amendment to the Bank of Tanzania Act** gave
  BOT its mandate over non-banks, and a **2007 "letter of no objection"** enabled mobile money —
  the permissive step that precedes the interoperable rail. Source:
  [[2025-05-16 Building an Inclusive Payments Ecosystem in Tanzania through TIPS (BOT and FSDT)]].
- **Financial Consumer Protection amendments (GN No. 298 of 2025, dated 2025-05-23).** BOT
  amended the Financial Consumer Protection Regulations, 2019 — extending the "dormant account"
  period from **5 to 12 months**, creating an "inactive account" category (unused **more than 5
  months**), mandating **reducing-balance** interest calculation, prohibiting maintenance fees on
  dormant/inactive accounts, and requiring **prior written BOT approval** for new or increased
  fees, with fines of **TSh 1 million per day** for non-compliance. See
  [[tanzania-financial-consumer-protection-regulations]] for the instrument in full. Analysis
  attributed to **Kemi Advocates** (2025-06-27). Source:
  [[2025-06-27 Key Highlights of the Bank of Tanzania Financial Consumer Protection Amendment Regulations GN 298 of 2025]].
- **A BOT-side cross-reference into the data-protection regime.** The amended regulations
  require providers to protect consumer financial and personal information **in accordance with
  the Personal Data Protection Act** ([[tanzania-pdpa]]) — a financial regulator importing the
  data-protection statute into prudential-style supervision, rather than the
  **[[pdpc-tanzania]]** reaching in from outside. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]].
  Source:
  [[2025-06-27 Key Highlights of the Bank of Tanzania Financial Consumer Protection Amendment Regulations GN 298 of 2025]].
