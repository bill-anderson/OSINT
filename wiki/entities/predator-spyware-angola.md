# Angola–Intellexa Predator spyware procurement

The Angolan state's acquisition of **Predator**, the highly invasive mobile spyware built and sold by the mercenary-surveillance consortium **Intellexa** — the first such procurement **forensically confirmed** to have been used inside Angola.

## Profile

- **The transaction.** Per Portugal's *Expresso* (2026-02-26), a Predator information sheet **created April 2021** records the contract as closed, with **[[sinse-angola]]** (the state intelligence service, under Gen. Fernando Miala) as end-user and **Carlos Gandini** — carrying a German mobile number and an Adsum-consultancy email — as the reseller representing Intellexa. A first government–vendor meeting with **Nexa** (the French firm in the Intellexa consortium) dates to **June 2018**.
- **Confirmed use.** Amnesty International's Security Lab confirmed **with high confidence** that Predator was installed and **active on journalist [[teixeira-candido]]'s phone on 4 May 2024**. Predator can access calls, messages, contacts and files and remotely activate microphone and camera. The case emerged from a wider 2025 surveillance investigation by Friends of Angola and Front Line Defenders; Amnesty had named Angola as a buyer as early as **2023**, and a **pirated *Novo Jornal* site** was used as an infection lure.
