# World Bank Theme Taxonomy

**Reference instrument (classification framework).** The World Bank's official
**Theme Taxonomy** (June 2025 edition, issued by **Operations Policy & Country
Services**) — the controlled vocabulary the Bank uses to classify the *themes* of the
operations and advisory work it supports. This is the **first `instrument` entity** in
the wiki, captured under the amended CLAUDE.md rule that published standards, taxonomies
and frameworks are reference objects, not discards. (An earlier capture of this same
document was discarded on 2026-07-10 before the `instrument` type existed; that decision
is now superseded.) Full archived text: [[2025-06-01 World Bank Theme Taxonomy]].

## What it is

- **Themes = goals / objectives / policy areas** of Bank-supported activities, used to
  capture the Bank's support to the UN **Sustainable Development Goals**. Themes are
  explicitly **not** methods or instruments of delivery, and are **not mutually
  exclusive** (an operation can carry several).
- A hierarchical vocabulary of themes and sub-themes, each with scope notes and
  "examples of excluded activities," plus an extensive abbreviations/acronyms glossary
  (e.g. `DPA` Data Protection Authority, `AI` Artificial Intelligence, `AML/CFT`).
- The classification backbone against which Bank Project Appraisal Documents are coded —
  including the ones already in this wiki: see the [[dzap]] PAD ([[world-bank]]) and
  [[dmap]].

## Relevance to this wiki

- **Methodological anchor.** It is the authority for how the Bank frames "digital
  development," "data," "governance" and adjacent themes in its own operations — useful
  when reconciling Bank project language against this wiki's own subject
  [taxonomy](../taxonomy.md). See [[gov.standards]], [[gov.policy]].
- **Not a place-tagged source.** Global (`XGL`), reports no development. Any dated fact
  later pulled *from* it (e.g. how a specific operation is themed) is filed as a normal
  source citing this instrument.
