# Starlink Gambia licence saga

The Gambia's **stalled Starlink licence** — a case study in **digital-sovereignty vs foreign pressure**.
**[[starlink|Starlink]]** says it **agreed terms (Nov 2024) and paid the fee (2 Dec 2024)**, but by 2026 the
government had **not issued the licence** (>1 year). Minister **Jabbi** said he **refused a VSAT licence Starlink
does not qualify for** despite **US State Department / Embassy** pressure to fast-track. The debate turned on:

- **Classification** — a VSAT-classification dispute over the right licence category.
- **Data sovereignty** — an expert urged **licence conditions first**: in-country data **localisation**, **lawful
  interception**, local **registration**, and **opt-in consent for AI-training** data use.
- **Economics** — fears of GSM-operator job losses vs an economist's pro-entry case; a proposed **balanced
  strategy** (fibre backbone + satellite last-mile) to close rural gaps while protecting sovereignty.

A `geopol.usa` thread and a live test of small-state regulatory autonomy. See [[gambia--infra-connect]].
