# AfCFTA Digital Trade Protocol (and Digital Identity Annex)

The [[afcfta]] Protocol on Digital Trade (DTP), adopted by African Union Heads of State, is the legal instrument meant to turn the continent's "African Single Digital Market" ambition into a working framework for cross-border digital trade, data flows, digital payments and e-commerce — "preserving national sovereignty" while enabling recognised credentials across borders. Its Digital Identity Annex was adopted by the AU in February 2025.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digital Identity Annex (AU-adopted Feb 2025).** Calls state parties to adopt digital-identity regimes, harmonise technical regulations and standards, conduct joint assessments of one another's digital-ID systems, and promote interoperability through common technical specifications — the [[dpi.id]] / [[gov.standards]] backbone for trusted cross-border verification of people, businesses and goods. Source: [[2026-05-27 Africa's economic future hinges on breaking down silos. This ADLI cohort advances integration]].
- **Operationalised via the ADLI cohort (2026).** The 2026 [[adli]] (Africa Data Leadership Initiative) cohort, in partnership with the AfCFTA, takes a test-and-learn approach to helping countries comply with the Digital Identity Annex, convening six countries across three trade corridors. Situated alongside the [[african-union]] Data Policy Framework and EAC/ECOWAS/SADC regional data frameworks. Source: [[2026-05-27 Africa's economic future hinges on breaking down silos. This ADLI cohort advances integration]].
- **2nd Digital Trade Forum "Eight Calls to Action" (Lagos, 2026).** The AfCFTA Secretariat and Government of Nigeria convened the second AfCFTA Digital Trade Forum, adopting Eight Calls to Action to operationalise the DTP — fast-tracking implementation, digital infrastructure, paperless trade, cybersecurity, digital inclusion, investment, PPPs and interoperable cross-border payments. Held as Nigeria took the AfCFTA Council of Ministers Bureau chair; Africa's digital economy cited at ~US$180bn now, ~US$712bn by 2050. Source: [[2026-07-09 Nigeria Takes AfCFTA Council Chair as Digital Forum Charts Roadmap for Implementation]].

- **Seychelles Digital Trade Corridor HQ (Cabinet approval 2026-07-08).** Seychelles approved
  hosting the **AfCFTA Digital Trade Corridor** headquarters in Victoria — an operational seat
  for continental digital commerce sitting alongside (not replacing) the Accra Secretariat. It
  builds on the DTP (ministers approved Feb 2024; 8 annexes still open) and follows a precursor
  MoU signed **5 Mar 2026** (AfCFTA Secretariat + Quest Ghana Ltd + Govt of Seychelles, during a
  [[wamkele-mene]] visit) and the AfCFTA Digital Trade Forum 2026 (Lagos, concluded 2 Jul 2026).
  See [[SYC]], [[afcfta-digital-trade-corridor]], [[seychelles-afcfta-mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Seychelles to Host AfCFTA Digital Trade Corridor Headquarters]].
- **Cross-border-data alignment gap (RIA, 2025-09).** A [[research-ict-africa]] analysis finds
  most African domestic data frameworks only **loosely align** with AfCFTA — fragmentation and
  weak "adequacy" recognition block intra-African data sharing — and calls for operational DPAs,
  interoperability standards and regional mutual recognition to give the DTP's data-flow
  provisions teeth. See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-22 Cross-border data flows in Africa An analysis of the alignment with AfCFTA]]
  (via [[cross-border-data-flows-afcfta-ria-2025]]). *(Afreximbank made the parallel "shared
  digital rails or AfCFTA stalls" case at Davos 2026 — see [[afreximbank]].)*
