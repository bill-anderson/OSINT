# Registration of Persons Act 2015 (Uganda)

**The statute underpinning Uganda's national identity system** — establishing
[[nira-uganda]] and the National Identification Register, and containing the provision
that makes the national ID a condition of access to services. It is the legal hinge on
which both the financial-inclusion case and the digital-exclusion critique turn.

## The operative provision

The Act provides that **a national ID card or National Identification Number shall be
required to access certain services such as financial services**; [[bank-of-uganda]]
records **implementation ongoing since 2020**. Source:
[[2024-10-14 Uganda financial institutions sign on for digital KYC through national ID]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **The provision in force: 74 institutions (as of June 2024).** BoU's 2023 annual report
  records up to **74 financial institutions** with access to the NIRA database for digital
  KYC, in line with enforcement of this provision. **The pairing is the story: 74
  institutions gained database access under the very provision the High Court declined to
  disturb.** See [[bank-of-uganda]], [[ndaga-muntu]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source: as above.
- **Challenged and upheld (dismissed 2025-06-10).** *Initiative for Social and Economic
  Rights (ISER) & 2 Others v Attorney General & NIRA*, **Miscellaneous Cause No. 0086 of
  2022**, High Court of Uganda at Kampala (Civil Division), Wamala J. *(Corrected
  2026-07-20 from HCT-00-CV-MC-0066-2022, which came from secondary coverage; the ruling
  is headed 0086.)* The applicants challenged the **mandatory requirement of a NIN or
  National ID ([[ndaga-muntu]]) under this Act** to access **[[sage-uganda]] benefits,
  public health services and proof of nationality**. **Dismissed on all three issues.** See
  [[iser-uganda]] for the reasoning. Source:
  [[2025-06-10-iser-v-attorney-general-nira-uganda-digital-id-ruling]].
- **The Court construed ss.65–66 authoritatively, and both readings bind this page.**
  **s.66 is mandatory** — the State argued "shall" was directory and lost the point:
  *"In view of the head note and the express provision of section 66 of the Act, it is
  undebatable as to whether the provision is mandatory or not."* But **health services are
  not in the s.66(2) list**, so *"in law … there is no requirement for use of the national
  ID as a primary data source and mandatory means of identification … for purpose of access
  to public health services."* **Mandatory ID for healthcare is a practice, not a statutory
  requirement** — a distinction this wiki previously blurred. The s.66(2) list as recited by
  the Court: employment; identification of voters; passports; opening bank accounts;
  insurance; land purchase/transfer/registration; **pension and social security
  transactions**; consumer credit; payment of taxes; financial services; registration
  services; statistical services; and anything further prescribed by the minister. Source:
  as above.
- **The s.83 complaints committee exists.** Established by the **Registration of Persons
  (Identification and Registration Committee) (Practice and Procedure) Notice, 2015 (Legal
  Notice No. 13 of 2015)** — the Court so found, refusing a structural interdict. Source:
  as above.
- **Amended by SI 67 of 2024 (12 August 2024).** The Registration of Persons (Amendment)
  Regulations 2024, made under **s.85**, amend **reg. 19 of the Registration of Persons
  Regulations 2015** to extend national ID card validity **from ten to eleven years**, and
  are **deemed in force from 20 November 2015** — a retroactive commencement of eight years
  and nine months that validates cards already expired on their printed term. Source:
  [[2024-08-12-uganda-si-67-2024-registration-of-persons-amendment-regulations]].
