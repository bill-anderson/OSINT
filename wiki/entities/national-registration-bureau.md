# National Registration Bureau (NRB), Kenya

Kenya's **national identity registration authority**, under the Ministry of Interior and National
Administration, responsible for the **national ID card** and the underlying civil/identity registers that
feed the **[[maisha-namba|Maisha Namba]]** UPI programme. It sits at the centre of Kenya's identity
modernisation — issuance-speed reforms, digitisation of records, and the contested proposals to open its
database to law enforcement. See [[KEN]], [[kenya--dpi-id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Issuance-speed procurement (2026-07).** [[dpi.govtech]] The government is procuring a new
  ID/passport issuance system meant to cut the national-ID wait to **10 days and passports to a week**.
  Source: [[2026-07-01-ids-10-days-new-system-standard]] *(summary-only clip)*.
- **Online civil documents (2026-06).** [[dpi.registry]] Kenyans can now **download birth certificates
  online** via eCitizen — part of CRVS digitisation. Source:
  [[2026-06-22-download-birth-certificates-online-standard]] *(summary-only clip)*.
- **CCTV / facial-recognition linkage proposal (2026-06).** [[gov.protect]] Interior CS Kipchumba
  Murkomen said the government plans to give the **National Police Service** access to the **NRB
  database** through a Ministry-of-Interior integration, enabling **facial recognition** across a
  six-city CCTV network (Nairobi, Mombasa, Kisumu, Nakuru, Eldoret, Nyeri), with a tender of up to
  **KES 25bn (~$190m)** — treating the civil register as a law-enforcement identification tool. See
  [[kenya--dpi-id]], [[kenya--gov-protect]]. Source: [[2026-06-26-national-id-cctv-facial-recognition-biometricupdate]].
- **Registration-integrity failures (2026-06).** [[infra.cybersec]] A KTN investigation exposed an
  **illegal trade in Kenyan IDs, passports and birth certificates**, underlining document-fraud networks
  around the registration system. Source: [[2026-06-15-ktn-illegal-trade-ids-passports-standard]]
  *(summary-only clip)*.
