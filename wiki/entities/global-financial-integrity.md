# Global Financial Integrity (GFI)

A Washington-based research and advocacy organisation (first IFF report 2008) that estimates
**illicit financial flows** using **trade-data-discrepancy ("mirror trade") methodology**. Its
work is a standing reference for the wiki on the **quality, completeness and transparency of
official African trade statistics** — the analysis is built entirely on the **UN Comtrade**
open dataset, comparing each country's reported exports/imports against its partners' mirror
figures at HS6 product level and flagging persistent "value gaps" as a proxy for trade
misinvoicing. Relevant here as a data-governance/data-quality actor, not only an
illicit-finance one — the value gaps it measures are, at root, a **data-integrity** signal.

## Relevant output

- ***Trade-Related Illicit Financial Flows in Africa, 2013–2022*** (January 2026) — value-gap
  estimates for all Sub-Saharan African nations. Editor Philip Nyakundi. See the source record
  for figures. Methodology and headline findings are captured on that page.
