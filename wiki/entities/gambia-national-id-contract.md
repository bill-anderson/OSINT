# The Gambia National ID + Integrated Digital ID (DBFOT)

**Deal (signed 2026-01).** A design-build-finance-co-operate-transfer (DBFOT) contract
under which [[margins-id-group]] will deliver both a National ID System and an Integrated
Digital ID System for the Government of The Gambia ([[GMB]]).

## Terms

- **Parties.** Margins ID Group (Ghanaian-owned) and the Government of The Gambia, signed
  with Minister of Interior **Abdoulie Sanyang**; the Ministry of Interior's Permanent
  Secretary Matar Ceesay and officials from the Ministry of Digital Economy, Justice,
  Immigration and the Directorate of Public-Private Partnerships attended — a
  whole-of-government process.
- **Selection.** Tender opened **22 July 2024**, drawing 11 international bidders; three
  were shortlisted after technical and financial evaluation, with Margins selected. Gambian
  delegations inspected the ICPS card-production facility and MIDSA in Ghana as due diligence.
- **Scope / model.** DBFOT — Margins designs, builds, finances, co-operates and transfers
  the system; described as an evolution of Ghana's architecture (enhanced security controls),
  not a replication, with diaspora access and knowledge transfer emphasised.
- **Significance.** The **first African-owned company to export a full national ID system**
  beyond its home market; framed as Africa-to-Africa collaboration under AfCFTA. This is
  **The Gambia's third attempt** at a national ID system after two prior failures.
- Amount/timeline not disclosed. Tags [[dpi.id]], [[finance.new]].
