# Digital Delta Data Centre (DDDC, Botswana)

Botswana's first **Tier III–certified, carrier-neutral national data centre**, in Gaborone — built and run
by **[[bofinet|BoFiNet]]** (100% government-owned) and framed as a **digital-sovereignty asset** and the
hosting foundation for the government's 1Gov migration. Minister Tshere cast it as making Botswana "a
digital powerhouse in the region." See [[BWA]], [[infra.store]].

## What we know

- **Launch + build (2025-11).** Launched Gaborone ~25 Nov 2025, **Uptime Tier III certified** (TCCF, Oct
  2025) after a decade-long build; main contractor **Zhong Gan**, with **18 citizen subcontractors** and
  **308 jobs**. Government systems are migrating to it under the
  **[[digital-services-act-botswana|1Gov/Digital Services Act]]** plan, with a **99.9% availability**
  pitch and a **second data centre planned outside Gaborone**. Sources:
  [[2025-11-26-digital-delta-data-centre-launch-connectingafrica]],
  [[2026-05-06-digital-delta-data-centre-improve-public-service-delivery]].
- **One of several data-centre threads.** Alongside the DDDC, Botswana has a **Gulf-linked Kala Data
  Centre** (Tlou Energy gas-to-power at Lesedi + UAE's Kala Data FZCO) and a proposed **solar-powered
  hyperscaler/AI campus at Palapye** (US **ChillMine** × Dutch **AAAS Energy**; 250 MW solar + BESS) —
  a public national DC plus two private, energy-anchored builds. Sources:
  [[2025-09-26-kala-data-centre-tlou-energy-itweb]], [[2025-10-06-dutch-us-firms-solar-powered-data-centre-palapye]].
