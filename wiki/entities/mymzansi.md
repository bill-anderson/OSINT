**MyMzansi** is South Africa's **single digital entry point** for government services — a citizen-facing "super-app" designed to be the unified front door to public services. It is the flagship deliverable of the digital public infrastructure pillar of [[operation-vulindlela]] and of government's national digital transformation roadmap (unveiled May 2025), and it aims to **connect digital identity, payments and services** in one place.

## What it is

Once fully operational, MyMzansi will be a **zero-rated mobile or web app** enabling remote access to government services including **driver's-licence renewal, health, social and Home Affairs services**, among others. It is explicitly designed to bring together three DPI building blocks:

- **Digital identity** — linked to the emerging biometric digital ID (see [[sa-digital-id-regulations]] and [[home-affairs-south-africa]]);
- **Payments** — the payments-ecosystem modernisation and G2P orchestration layer;
- **Services** — integrated departmental service delivery.

## Status and milestones

- **November 2025** — communications and digital technologies minister **Solly Malatsi** showcased a working prototype demonstrating a full **digital driver's-licence renewal integrated with NaTIS**.
- **October 2025** — the **MzansiXchange** data-exchange pilot (a supporting pillar) was introduced to enable seamless inter-departmental data sharing.
- **March 2026** — per the Operation Vulindlela phase-two Q3 2025/26 progress report (released 30 January 2026), public access is set to begin for **targeted test groups**, focusing on high-impact service use cases; the DPI reform pillar was rated "on track."

- **Built in 10 weeks (as of 2025-11-09).** The prototype was built in ~10 weeks by the Presidency's **[[digital-services-unit-presidency-sa|Digital Services Unit (DSU)]]** (led by Richard Gevers), with **[[amazon-web-services]]** and Silverstripe as key technical partners, on a **modular open-DPG stack** (Silverstripe, GOV.UK Pay/Notify, verifiable credentials) spanning identity, driver's licence, payments and an offline-capable verifiable wallet. First public rollout was then planned for **H1 2026**. Source: [[2025-11-09 Behind the speedy MyMzansi screens and the Presidency's race to build a digital government]].
- **Schedule slip (as of 2026-01-28).** By January 2026 the digital-ID reforms MyMzansi anchors were **behind schedule** — Home Affairs confirmed the policy was still under development (Cabinet then public comment pending), and the earlier functional-ID target was set to be missed even as the Planning Ministry punted an end-2026 rollout. Source: [[2026-01-28 Bad news about South Africa's newest ID book plan]].
- **Digital driving licence (as of 2026-01-07).** SA is introducing a **digital driving permit** (alongside extending licence-card validity 5→8 years), running on MyMzansi and showcased by Minister Solly Malatsi at the Nov-2025 Global DPI Summit. Source: [[2026-01-07 Digital driving licence coming to South Africa]].
- **Inclusion caveat from the minister (2026-07).** Minister **[[solly-malatsi]]** publicly framed MyMzansi as the "one trusted point of entry" to government but warned "digital-first" must not become "digital-only" — it must reach the priced-out and offline via low-data, multilingual and offline channels, or the digital state becomes "a premium service layer for the already included." See [[include.divides]]. Source: [[2026-07-13 Digital-first must not become digital-only, says Malatsi]].

MyMzansi is underpinned by a **national digital-government standards framework** (in development) meant to govern how government systems are designed, built and connected, and by the payments modernisation programme linking government payments to verified accounts and store-of-value wallets. Treasury cites it as evidence of South Africa positioning itself among Global South leaders in open, inclusive DPI.
