# DARE1 — Djibouti Africa Regional Express 1

DARE1 is a submarine communications cable running down the East African coast,
operational since **2021**. It carries roughly **36 Tbps** over three fibre pairs
and forms a primary international-connectivity spine for the Horn of Africa,
directly linking Somalia into the global internet backbone.

- **Route (original).** Djibouti City → Bosaso and Mogadishu (Somalia) →
  Mombasa (Kenya). The Somali landings at Bosaso and Mogadishu give the country
  dedicated high-capacity international bandwidth.
- **2025 southward expansion.** An extension of roughly **3,200–3,500 km**
  continues south to **Mtunzini, South Africa**, with additional landings in
  **Tanzania, Mozambique and Madagascar**, broadening DARE1 into an
  East-Africa-to-Southern-Africa corridor.

## Ownership and partners

- **Owner / operator:** [[djibouti-telecom]].
- **Partners / landing parties:** [[hormuud-telecom]] and [[somtel]] (Somalia),
  Telkom Kenya (Kenya).

## Significance in this wiki

DARE1 underpins the connectivity story for Somalia and the wider East African
region — a concentration of international capacity on a small number of cables
and landing points that carries both a resilience and a data-sovereignty
dimension. See [[infra.connect]], [[SOM]], [[DJI]], [[XEA]].
