# US–Kenya Health Cooperation Framework

**Parties:** United States (Sec. State Marco Rubio) and Kenya (Prime Cabinet Secretary
Musalia Mudavadi). **What:** a five-year, **$2.5bn** health cooperation framework —
Kenya the **first** signatory under the [[america-first-global-health-strategy]].
**When:** signed at the US State Department, **2025-12-04**. **Status:** suspended by the
Kenyan High Court and under legal challenge.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The data-sharing core.** The framework's annexed **Data-Sharing Agreement** (section
  "Provision of Data to U.S. Government") institutionalises US access to Kenyan health
  information systems — surveillance systems, electronic medical records, warehouse-management
  platforms — plus genomic and pathogen data. Kenya's government said only aggregated/anonymised
  data would be shared, but analysts warn such data is re-identifiable. See [[dpi.mis]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]].
- **Legal challenge.** Petition filed **2025-12-10** (*Okiya Omtatah Okoiti v. Ministry of
  Foreign Affairs*); the High Court granted an **injunction 2025-12-19**, first suspending the
  data-sharing provisions and later extending the suspension to the **entire agreement**.
  A legal opinion found it violates the **Kenya Data Protection Act 2019**, the **Digital
  Health Act 2023** and the **Health Act** (health-data localization requirements), and that
  it bypassed parliamentary approval for an ~$850m spending commitment. See [[gov.legislate]],
  [[odpc-kenya]]. Source: [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]].
- **Analysis.** Carnegie's [[jane-munga]] and Rose Mosero frame Kenya's response as an "early
  warning" — a demonstration effect for other AFGHS signatories with data-protection laws,
  and evidence that domestic law can constrain external financing that treats data as a lever.
- **Litigation moved — Court of Appeal reversed the suspension (as of 2026-06).** Nagadya narrates
  the case as data sovereignty in action: the **[[consumer-federation-of-kenya]]** (COFEK) petition,
  filed ~8 days after signing citing the Data Protection Act and Digital Health Act, won a **High
  Court suspension of the data-transfer component**, which the **Court of Appeal later reversed**
  (case continuing). She frames the deal within an emerging African data-localisation stack ([[au-data-policy-framework]]
  2022, Kenya Cloud Policy 2025, Ghana's Draft Data Protection Bill 2025) and the "17% of people, 1%
  of AI training data" gap. Consistent with this page's figures: **$2.5bn, signed 2025-12-04**. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-08 Kenyan Citizens Took A $2.5 Billion Deal With America To Court To Fight For Their Health Data]].
- **Structural read — "the new scramble" (2025-12-15).** Munyua reads the framework beside the Trump
  National Security Strategy (same day) as data-colonialism: **25 years of data/specimen/genetic
  access for 5 years of funding**, benefit-sharing "to be determined" (Art 3(f)), and Art 5(g)
  disclaiming any international-law obligations. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-15 THE GATEKEEPERS PART V The New Maps of Africa]].
- **Corroboration and detail (2026-01).** The framework supports accelerating Kenya's national
  **electronic medical record** rollout; the High Court order halting the data-transfer provisions
  set a hearing for **2026-02-16**, with a parallel petition by the **Consumer Federation of Kenya**
  on data-privacy grounds. Kenya's exposure sits within the wider asymmetry (5% of facilities open to
  US audit; 7-day outbreak detection / 1-day US notification; no reciprocity). Justice for Health's
  Allan Maleche: "Health data is a strategic public asset… how it is governed now will shape Africa's
  control over public-health policy for decades." See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.mis]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-20 Africa Pushes Back On US Health Deals Over Data, Power]],
  [[2026-01-01 America First, Africa Last  Data Extraction from Africa's Bodies and the Erosion of Health Sovereignty]].
