# APDP — Mali's data-protection authority

Mali's **data-protection authority** (Autorité de Protection des Données à caractère
Personnel), an **independent administrative authority created by Law No. 2013-015 of 21 May
2013**, charged with protecting the personal data of all persons on national territory and
regulating the sector. Under the Act, any **state data processing requires a reasoned APDP
opinion**. The wiki previously held no APDP entity; H2-2025 material establishes it as an
**actively enforcing, territorially expanding DPA** (relevant to GAP-003).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Extraordinary review of ~2,000 files + lease-declaration decree (2025-10-06).** The APDP
  opened a five-day session to review ~2,000 declaratory-formality files (legal conformity,
  information-system security, retention periods, data-subject rights), including a draft
  interministerial decree instituting **mandatory declaration of property leases** (residential,
  commercial, professional, mixed-use) to improve traceability of real-estate transactions —
  a new **lease-registry data workstream**. See [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-07-apanews-apdp-examine-2000-dossiers-baux]].
- **Enforcement escalation (2025).** Fined a Bamako clinic **5 million FCFA** for obstructing
  a verification mission tied to an undeclared video-surveillance system, and issued formal
  notices to **100+ companies** for non-compliance. See [[gov.protect]].
  Source: [[2025-10-07-apanews-apdp-examine-2000-dossiers-baux]].
- **Regional antenna in Ségou (2025-12-04).** Opened its first regional antenna (national
  delegation led by 2nd rapporteur Selly Ouane) to bring the institution closer to citizens
  and controllers, provide compliance guidance, and intensify awareness/oversight/training —
  DPA territorial expansion. Source: [[2025-12-05-lessor-apdp-segou-antenne]].
