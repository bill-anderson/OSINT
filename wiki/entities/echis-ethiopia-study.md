# Barriers, facilitators and motivators of eCHIS use among health workers in Ethiopia

Peer-reviewed article by **Bogale et al.**, *Frontiers in Digital Health* **5:1162239
(2023)**. A **phenomenological qualitative study** based on **54 interviews (May 2022)** on
adoption of the **electronic Community Health Information System ([[echis-ethiopia]])** — the
digital tool for Ethiopia's **~40,000 Health Extension Workers (HEWs)**. Cited here as
literature; a later dated fact drawn from it becomes a source citing this page. Part of the
HMIS/[[dhis2]] evidence cluster ([[dpi.mis]]).

## Argument

- **Infrastructure is the binding constraint** in rural settings ([[digital.rural]]): Ethio
  Telecom coverage of **85% 2G / 66% 3G / only 4% 4G** ([[infra.connect]]) and rural
  electricity access of just **9%** ([[infra.energy]]).
- **Barriers to use:** burdensome **parallel paper-and-electronic recording**, connectivity
  and power failures, poor training ([[capacity.training]]), and HEWs' **fear of
  theft-liability** for the devices issued to them.
- **eCHIS is distinct** from **[[dhis2]]** (the national aggregate reporting platform):
  eCHIS is the community/household-level electronic record for frontline HEWs — see
  [[echis-ethiopia]].
