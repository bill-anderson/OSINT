# Internet Society — Africa Internet History: Highlights

A reference document, **"Africa Internet History: Highlights,"** published by the
**Internet Society (ISOC)** (Geneva / Reston, VA). It is a standing
**historical-reference asset** we consult for the institutional and pre-2001
history of internet development in Africa.

**No raw `.md` clipping exists for this entity.** It is compiled from the PDF held
in `raw/`: `2026-07-11 history_internet_africa.pdf`.

## What the asset is

A curated collection of information (self-described as "a set of global
information on the Internet mainly from 1990 to 2001 in Africa," not a
country-by-country history), organised in five sections: **Organizations/
Initiatives, Technologies, Impact, African institutions and Internet governance,
and Some pioneers.** Its value is establishing *who did what* in the early build-
out of African connectivity.

## Key content we draw from it

- **NEPAD e-Africa Programme** (an [[african-union]] programme adopted Lusaka
  2001): its **ICT Broadband Infrastructure Network** aimed to connect all African
  countries via the planned **Uhurunet** submarine cable (Baharicom Development
  Company; MoU with **ACE / Africa Coast to Europe**) and **Umojanet** terrestrial
  network — an early continental-cable vision predating today's Equiano/2Africa era.
- **Donor and research initiatives**: the **USAID Leland Initiative** (1996, ~$15m
  to connect ~20 African countries); Canada's **IDRC Acacia** project (1997, C$60m);
  **UNECA PADIS/AISI**; **UNDP SDNP**; **World Bank infoDev** (including support for
  the EASSy cable consensus process alongside APC); **UNESCO RINAF**.
- **ORSTOM's RIOnet** — a French research network linking ~25 UNIX hosts across ~10
  West/Central African countries to Montpellier by dial-up, explicitly described as
  emerging "from France's imperialistic ties to Africa" (a colonial-continuity data
  point).
- **[[nsrc]]** profile — its roots in late-1980s southern-African networking and
  formalisation (1992, NSF support), complementing NSRC's own "Stepping Stones"
  asset.

Use this document for early-institutional and governance history of African
connectivity; pair it with [[nsrc]] for first-connection chronology and
[[jagun-sat3-open-access-2008]] for the SAT-3 era.

## Contemporary standards participation

RIA's [[mapping-african-digital-infrastructures]] study (Part 3, Phokeer, 2022) quantifies how
thin African participation in **IETF Internet-standards** work remains — as of Jan 2022, ~15
RFCs and 9 drafts from African authors (~0.26% of 9,170 RFCs) — and credits ISOC's
**IETF-Africa** initiative for sponsoring participants and hackathons. A present-day companion
to this asset's early-governance history. Source:
[[2022-06-12 Mapping African Digital Infrastructures (Part 3) Understanding the motivations and challenges of African contributions to the Internet standards development]].
