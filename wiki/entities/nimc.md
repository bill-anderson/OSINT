# National Identity Management Commission (NIMC)

Nigeria's foundational-identity authority, custodian of the National Identification
Number (NIN) and the "one person, one identity" policy. Led by Director General
**Abisoye Coker-Odusote**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **NIMC Act 2026 (enacted June 2026 by President Tinubu).** Replaces the 19-year-old
  NIMC Act 2007 (described as obsolete); recognises the NIN as the foundational
  credential and underpins "trusted DPI." NIMC opened nationwide multi-stakeholder
  consultations (including the Ministry of Budget & Economic Planning) in July 2026 to
  build the enabling environment for full operationalization.
- **Enrolment.** NIN registrations passed **136M** by July 2026 (up from 123.9M in
  Oct 2025 — 12M+ added), against a **180M target by 31 Dec 2026** (~44M still to issue).
  Enrolment is scaffolded by [[nigeria-id4d]] ([[world-bank]] / AFD / EIB financing). See
  [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]].
- **Beyond enrolment (2026-06).** NIN passed **130M** (timeline 123.9M Oct 2025 → 130M Jun 2026 → 136M Jul 2026), toward **180M by 31 Dec 2026**; the ID4D project ([[nigeria-id4d]]) totals **$430m** (World Bank/AFD/EIB). Focus shifting from enrolment to **data integrity and service access**. DG Abisoye Coker-Odusote. See [[dpi.id]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source: [[2026-06-05 Nigeria looks beyond enrollment as digital ID passes 130 million]].
- **8,809-ward enrolment drive + enforcement powers (2026-07).** Under the NIMC Act 2026,
  NIMC launched a nationwide ward-level enrolment campaign across all **8,809 political
  wards**, bypassing urban centres to reach rural women, undocumented children, persons
  with disabilities, and verified refugees — the inclusion push tied to service access
  (NIN as prerequisite for banking, telecoms, federal programmes). The Act newly empowers
  NIMC (with security agencies) to **arrest and prosecute identity thieves**, enforced via
  a revamped **Inspectorate, Compliance and Enforcement (ICE) Unit** — tethered to the 2023
  Data Protection Act / [[ndpc]]. Coverage draws a **Maisha Namba** contrast with [[KEN]]
  (where civil-society litigation over privacy/participation has dogged the rollout),
  arguing Nigeria is building cybersecurity/trust provisions in up front. See
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[include.access]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 NIMC Act 2026 Director-General Coker-Odusote Launches 8,809-Ward Digital ID Drive]].
- **NIN mandated for social services (2025-02, background).** Earlier in the drive, SGF
  **George Akume** tied access to **all** social services to the NIN, under the World
  Bank-financed [[nigeria-id4d]] target of ≥180m IDs by end-2026; a discovery of ~6,000
  Niger nationals holding NINs prompted a Tinubu-ordered database audit and clean-up. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-13 Digital ID necessity increases in Nigeria despite lingering challenges]].
- **NIN suspensions — who bears the cost? (op-ed, 2026-03).** A legal commentary argues that
  when validly issued NINs are suspended over enrolment-centre "bypass," NIMC — as the body
  that accredits and supervises those centres — not the citizen should bear remediation,
  warning that monetised "unsuspension" fees convert regulatory failure into revenue and
  erode trust in a now-compulsory identifier gating banking, telecoms and tax. External
  analysis. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[include.access]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]].
  Source: [[2026-03-29 When digital identity fails the citizen Who bears responsibility for NIN suspensions]].
- **Gender gap in NIN enrolment (2026-01, Niger State).** NIMC's own June-2025 data shows only
  **38.8% of enrolled NIN holders are women** vs 61.1% men (22.3-point gap). In Niger State
  just **21 NIN centres** serve ~7.5m people; ~N3,000 round-trip transport and patriarchal
  mobility restrictions are barriers, and NIN is required for the maternal-care Basic Health
  Care Provision Fund — locking rural women out of DPI-gated services. A NIMC–[[world-bank]]
  gender study cited low awareness, mobility, time and biometric-capture barriers; enrolment
  is concentrated in urban centres. See [[include.divides]], [[digital.rural]],
  [[include.access]], [[nigeria-id4d]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 The Gender Gap in Rural Niger State's Digital Identity]].
- **Functional exclusion via device-locked corrections (2026-03).** Investigative reporting found NIN
  enrolment (**126m+** claimed, ~**94m** unenrolled) increasingly gates healthcare, education, banking,
  telecoms and welfare — yet NIMC's **2024 policy** halted in-person corrections, confining data fixes
  to a smartphone self-service portal **uniquely tied to the original enrolment device/browser**,
  excluding the **72% of adults without smartphones** (Research ICT Africa) and stranding even the
  tech-literate behind portal glitches. Correction demand runs to "hundreds of thousands"; accredited
  and banned agents extort **N12,000–62,000** vs the official N2,000–28,574 fees; monthly enrolment
  slowed to **~648,888** (Jan–Sep 2024, down from 1.05m in 2023). Advocates argue this breaches the NDPA
  2023 accuracy duty ([[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]]) and the African Charter, citing Kenya's
  partly-unconstitutional Huduma Namba as the cautionary precedent. See [[gov.protect]],
  [[include.access]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-07 Nigerians Groan Under Digital Identity Regime That Turns Basic Rights into Privileges]].
- **DPI silos block farmers (2026-03).** Because NIMC's NIN, the CBN's BVN and MNO SIM registers
  operate in silos, farmers failing NIN/BVN verification are locked out of NIRSAL and GEEP agri-loans;
  the 2025 **National Digital Farmers Registry** ([[national-digital-farmers-registry-nigeria]],
  NIN-anchored) already shows duplications, and the World Bank's **$500m BRIDGE** project (Oct 2025;
  90,000km of new fibre) is meant to close the rural connectivity gap (only ~38% of Nigerians online in
  2024). See [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.rural]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-03 Nigeria's Disharmonised Digital System Leaving Low-Income Farmers Behind]].
- **NIN as coerced political data (2026-01/02).** The APC's nationwide membership **e-registration**
  requires a valid NIN, with allegations that some Enugu civil servants were pressured to submit NINs
  under threats tied to salary; a data-protection lawyer frames party membership as **sensitive data
  (political affiliation)** under the [[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]], where coerced consent is no
  consent — and warns NIMC's own advice against disclosing NINs sits awkwardly with mass NIN collection
  by political actors. See [[gov.protect]], [[ndpc]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-02 NIN, Party e-registration and the NDPA 2023 When politics meets privacy (and why Nigerians should care)]].
- **Breach exposure and accountability vacuum (2024–26).** Paradigm Initiative alleged a **~43m
  NIN-record exposure** in 2024 (names, addresses, phone numbers, passport photos) that led to
  litigation but no public audit or breach-notification process, illustrating the weak accountability
  around NIMC's biometric database — one node in a fragmented digital-rights governance map (NIMC, NCC,
  [[ndpc]], ONSA) where mandates exist but coordinated redress does not. See [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-27 Who Protects Nigeria's Digital Citizens Unmasking Accountability Gaps in Digital Rights Governance]].
- **NIN as identity anchor beyond enrolment (2026-01/07).** Nigeria's new **National Payment
  Stack** ([[nibss]]) uses the NIN alongside BVN/TIN/RC for KYC, wiring the identifier into
  payments infrastructure; separately the [[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]] suit against
  [[inec]] over an unpublished privacy policy underscores the data-protection duties on
  NIN-linked registers. Light cross-links; see [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.protect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-02 How Strategic  Shift of NIBSS Is  Rewiring Nigeria's Payments System]],
  [[2026-07-10 FIJ Showed That INEC Broke Data Law. Digital Rights Lawyers Sue the Commission 10 Months Later]].

- **Agriculture Ministry adopts NIN for farmer verification (2026-07).** The Federal Ministry
  of Agriculture and Food Security will deploy NIMC's NIN / identity-verification platform to
  authenticate beneficiaries of government agri programmes — building a credible centralised
  **farmers database** to target subsidies (a phased three-year taper) at "genuine farmers," cut
  leakages and eliminate ghost beneficiaries; DG Coker-Odusote framed the **NIMC Act 2026**-
  strengthened mandate as enabling secure verification services to MDAs. A concrete sectoral use of
  foundational ID for targeting/MIS. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.mis]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-12 Agric Ministry adopts NIMC identity system to verify genuine farmers]].

- **Migration to MOSIP (Anjola Adebowale, 2026-06-12).** In benchmarking Africa's
  "big four" on a sovereign-data standard, Adebowale reports the **>130m-enrolled
  NIN** base is **migrating to [[mosip]]** (open-source), and that Nigeria loses
  ~**$850m/yr** to foreign data hosting — framing homegrown, open ID/data
  infrastructure as the sovereignty play. See [[dpi.id]], [[infra.store]],
  [[nigeria--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-12 Beyond the AI Stack Why Governments Need Dynamic Digital Infrastructure]].
