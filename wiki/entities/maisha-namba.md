# Maisha Namba (Kenya digital ID / Unique Personal Identifier)

Kenya's third-generation digital-identity programme, launched in 2023, built around a lifelong
**Unique Personal Identifier (UPI)** issued from birth as a single key across government services — the
successor to the litigated **Huduma Namba** ([[dpi.id]]). It runs on the **Integrated Population
Registration System (IPRS)** and eCitizen. Its governance and inclusion record is contested: independent
analyses (Temple iLIT, Amnesty Kenya, HRRC) warn it risks **entrenching exclusion** of the ~5m
Kenyans without IDs — rural, nomadic, poor and security-vetted communities — and flag privacy risks from
privately-contracted IT firms. See [[KEN]], [[kenya--dpi-id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Governance critique (2024-12).** [[gov.protect]] The first independent application of the UNDP Model
  Governance Framework to a national system found Maisha Namba "poised to exacerbate inequalities."
  Source: [[2024-12-17 A Model Governance Framework Analysis of Kenya's Maisha Namba]].
- **Exclusion baseline (2025-09).** [[include.divides]] ~5m without national ID; only 34% of registered
  children have a birth certificate (KDHS 2022); worst in arid/semi-arid lands. Source:
  [[2025-09-18 Why Do IDs and Birth Certificates Matter - A Case Study of Kenya]].
- **Adoption push (2024-26).** [[finance.new]] KES 15.13bn ($117m) FY2024/25 budget (7.5m digital ID
  cards, UPI rollout); first-time ID fees scrapped (Mar 2025, boosting Kakamega registrations); a Births
  and Deaths Registration Bill 2025 would allocate the **UPI at birth**. Sources:
  [[2024-06-17 Kenya dedicates $117M to digital ID, related projects in 2024-2025 budget]],
  [[2025-09-08 Scrapping fees for first-timers boosts Kenya digital ID registration]],
  [[2026-03-05 Kenya Government moves to introduce digital civil registration system (Births and Deaths Bill 2025)]].
