# ZTE

Chinese telecom and surveillance-equipment vendor, a leading supplier of "safe
city"/"smart city" surveillance systems across Africa under China's Digital Silk
Road programme. ZTE technicians and trainers often build the local AI and command-
and-control capacity that accompanies these deployments.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Abuja security network (per Mar 2026 IDS report).** In Nigeria ([[NGA]]) —
  the continent's largest market for Chinese surveillance kit — **China Exim
  Bank advanced $399m of the $470m** Abuja spent on a public-security network
  built by ZTE and [[hikvision]]. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[geopol.china]].
  Source: [[2026-04-04 Chinese surveillance rolled out in Africa ZTE, Hikvision, Huawei at the helm]].
- **Bundled "safe city" model.** The UK-based Institute of Development Studies
  ([[2026-03-01 Smart City Surveillance in Africa (IDS 2026)]], March 2026) mapped Chinese-supplied
  surveillance across 11 countries. A typical ~**$250m Eximbank package** bundles
  facial-recognition cameras + ANPR + a build-and-transfer **national data
  centre** + vendor training (ZTE/[[huawei]]) — producing institutional and
  technical **lock-in**. See [[infra.store]], [[XAF]], [[hikvision]].
  Source: [[2026-04-04 Chinese surveillance rolled out in Africa ZTE, Hikvision, Huawei at the helm]].
- **South Sudan contract history (2014, recalled 2026-02).** Reporting on [[huawei]]'s 2026
  re-engagement with South Sudan ([[SSD]]) recalls a 2014 rift in which South Sudanese officials
  accused Huawei of forging documents to damage a rival contract awarded to **ZTE** — an early
  marker of ZTE's footprint in the market. See [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Huawei reconnects with South Sudan]].
