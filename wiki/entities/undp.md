# United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)

UN development agency; a recurring technical-assistance and co-financing partner in
African digital-transformation programmes (also coordinated into World Bank ID4D work —
see [[zambia--dpi-id]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Ghana (2026-07).** Deepening partnership with Ghana's Ministry of Communication,
  Digital Technology and Innovations. Progress cited on three flagships — an
  Italian-supported digital project, an **MSME digital platform**, and a **Japanese-backed
  AI programme** for public services — plus an **AI & digital-governance capacity-building
  programme for the Civil Service** (multiple cohorts, gender-inclusion emphasis). UNDP
  Ghana Representative **Niloy Banerjee** pressed for government co-financing to unlock
  more development-partner funding. See [[GHA]], [[capacity.training]], [[gov.policy]].
- **Flagship continental DPI paper (2026).** UNDP's Regional Bureau for Africa + Digital,
  AI and Innovation (DAI) Hub published **"Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa: A
  leapfrog catalyst for inclusive growth and prosperity"** — the wiki's definitive
  continental DPI reference. Frames DPI as three layers (core: [[dpi.id]]/[[dpi.pay]]/
  [[dpi.exchange]]; sectoral apps: [[dpi.govtech]]; enabling infra: [[infra.connect]]) and
  four barriers (exclusion, inefficiency, fiscal erosion, sovereignty), tying national DPI
  to **AfCFTA** ([[afcfta]], US$450bn) and AU Agenda 2063, and calling for **African-led**
  DPI investment. Produced under Ahunna Eziakonwa and Robert Opp; inputs from the
  [[gates-foundation]], AfDB, CDPI, CoDevelop. See [[XAF]], [[gov.regional]],
  [[finance.new]], [[include.divides]]. Source: [[2026-01-01 Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa - UNDP]].
- **Ethiopia — AI UniPod (2026-02).** Co-launched Ethiopia's **[[ai-unipod-ethiopia]]** (AI University
  Innovation Pod) in Addis Ababa with the **[[ethiopian-ai-institute]]**, Addis Ababa University and the
  Timbuktoo Initiative — a national AI research, innovation and talent hub under [[digital-ethiopia-2030]],
  which the Deputy PM framed as sovereignty meaning "owning our data, designing our algorithms." See
  [[ETH]], [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.research]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-19 Ethiopia launches AI UniPod to drive innovation]].
- **Cameroon — digital civil registration (2026-01).** Implemented (with Denmark funding via UNDP
  Funding Windows) a **digital civil-status system** for birth/marriage/death certificates at council
  level across 10 municipalities in Cameroon's Far North, North-West and South-West regions — aimed at
  legal-identity access for youth, women and vulnerable/displaced groups and service continuity in
  crisis areas. See [[CMR]], [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-13 Cameroon intros digital system for civil documents]].
- **Malawi — AI & DT strategy validation (2026-02).** Partnered Malawi's Ministry of ICT / Department of
  E-Government ([[malawi-ministry-of-ict]]) on validation of the **Draft National AI Strategy** and
  **Draft National Digital Transformation Strategy** (Lilongwe workshop, ~18 Feb 2026), plus a public
  "Shape Malawi's Digital and AI Future" feedback portal. See [[MWI]], [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]].
  Source: [[2026-02-20 Malawi validates AI strategy, digital transformation policy - CAJ News Africa]].
- **Kenya–India–Italy trilateral sovereign AI (2026-02-19).** UNDP is a partner in a
  Kenya–India–Italy letter of intent signed at the **India AI Impact Summit** (New Delhi) —
  targeting **100 AI deployment programs by 2030** and multilingual voice AI for
  low-bandwidth settings; led by EkStep Foundation, Kenya's Directorate of Digital Economy
  and Italy's enterprise ministry, building on the G7 AI Hub and Italy's Mattei Plan
  ([[kenya-india-italy-sovereign-ai-partnership]]). See [[KEN]], [[tech.ai]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-20 Kenya, India and Italy Launch Trilateral Sovereign AI Partnership for Africa]].

- **Eritrea — data systems / EPHS 2025 (2026-05).** Under the **Joint Programme on Data
  for Development (D4D)** (with UNECA), supported the **[[eritrea-national-statistics-office]]**
  in completing the **Fourth Round Eritrea Population and Health Survey (EPHS 2025)** — a
  CAPI-based digital survey of 9,794 households (98.4% completion) and 2023/24 national-
  accounts work, Eritrea's most concrete data-systems development on record. See [[ERI]],
  [[data.statistics]], [[capacity.research]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-08 Eritrea strengthens data systems with EPHS 2025 (UNDP)]].
