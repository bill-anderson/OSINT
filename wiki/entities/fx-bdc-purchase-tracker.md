# FX BDC Purchase Tracker (Nigeria)

A **[[cbn|Central Bank of Nigeria]]** digital reporting platform that records **every foreign
exchange purchase made by licensed Bureau de Change (BDC) operators**, announced in a **circular of
15 July 2026** signed by **Aderinola Shonekan**, director of the CBN's Trade and Exchange
Department.

## How it works

- **Real-time or same-day reporting** of dollar purchases by all licensed BDCs, giving the CBN
  **transaction-level** compliance monitoring of the retail forex market.
- **Banks as first-line enforcement** — required to run KYC and due diligence, verify ownership
  information and reject non-compliant BDCs; **prohibited** from imposing exclusive relationships
  or referral fees that restrict competition.
- **No warehousing of official-market FX** — any unsold balance must return to the Nigerian Foreign
  Exchange Market within **24 hours** of the permitted utilisation period; all transactions must run
  through licensed financial institutions. Violations attract sanctions under existing banking and
  FX law.

## Why it matters

It converts a **policy allowance into a monitored data flow**: the framework builds on the CBN's
**February 2026** policy letting licensed BDCs buy up to **$150,000 a week** from authorised dealer
banks, and follows the licensing of **82 BDCs in December 2025**. The CBN's stated aims are
compliance, liquidity, faster detection of suspicious transactions and confidence in the FX market
— in wiki terms, **formalising the retail forex market by instrumenting it**, a supervisory-data
play rather than a payments rail. Source:
[[2026-07-17-nigeria-central-bank-digitises-forex-oversight]].
