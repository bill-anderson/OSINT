# Tanzania Internet Service Providers Association (TSPAR)

The **industry association of Tanzanian internet service providers** — and the body associated
with the country's **internet exchange**, the peering fabric that determines whether domestic
traffic stays domestic or transits abroad. Secretary-general: **Noah Maina** (as of 2025-03).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Extending the internet exchange into Wingu's data centre (2025-03-19).** At the launch of
  **[[wingu-africa]]**'s **$50 million** phase-2 data centre in Dar es Salaam,
  secretary-general **Noah Maina** said extending the **internet exchange** to Wingu's facility
  **ensures data traffic remains local** — improving efficiency, reducing costs and optimising
  network performance for all operators. He framed the partnership as strengthening Tanzania's
  digital infrastructure and its broader digital transformation. Keeping traffic local is the
  practical, unglamorous end of data sovereignty: an exchange point inside a carrier-neutral
  facility, not a policy declaration. See [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.exchange]],
  [[infra.store]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 Tanzania's digital shift gathers pace with $50 million data centre]].
  *(The same facility is described as **the most connected data centre in Tanzania, with
  interconnections to 13 operators** — the Mbezi Industrial Area site; distinct from Wingu's
  **WCX cloud-exchange platform**, launched in Tanzania in November 2025.)*
