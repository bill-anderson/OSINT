# Baker McKenzie

**International law firm**, held here as the publisher of practitioner reference guidance the wiki
cites for procedural detail on national identity systems.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Tanzania national ID guidance (undated; year-precision proxy).** Its "I Am Here" Tanzania page
  sets out how the **[[nida-tanzania]]** national ID card is actually obtained: eligibility for
  **all citizens aged 18 and above** plus **legal residents with valid permits**; biometric capture
  of **fingerprints, photo and signature**; and a **hybrid, not digital-end-to-end** process — an
  online form, then a **local government office for the Local Chairman's stamp and signature**,
  then a NIDA District Office, then a biometric appointment. It also records that **it is not
  possible to obtain a National ID without a valid residence permit**, so foreign nationals must
  clear the immigration department first. See [[TZA]], [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 Tanzania - National Identification Card guidance (Baker McKenzie)]].

> **Two cautions on this source.** It is **undated** — the year on file is an inference from content
> (it describes the current NIDA online portal), not a publisher statement, so it is held
> `date_source: proxy` and must never be presented as a dated snapshot. And its modality set
> (fingerprints, photo, signature) is the **pre-Phase-II baseline**: it must not be read as
> contradicting the 2026 addition of iris and facial capture. Its value is **structural process**,
> which does not age the way coverage figures do.

## Why it matters analytically

Law-firm client guidance is an underrated OSINT asset for identity systems: it documents the
**actual procedural friction a person faces** — the stamps, the offices, the sequencing — which both
government communications and coverage statistics omit. The Local Chairman's stamp and the
residence-permit precondition are concrete [[include.access]] barriers that appear in no official
account held here.
