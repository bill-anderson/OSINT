# Botswana virtual-asset / crypto regime (Virtual Asset Act 2022)

Botswana licenses crypto under the **Virtual Asset Act 2022** — an early African mover on a VASP framework —
but the regime is **thin and under AML scrutiny**: only two licensed providers, no legal-tender status, and
regional flags over oversight gaps. See [[BWA]], [[dpi.pay]].

## What we know

- **Licensed but not legal tender (2025).** [[gov.legislate]] Two VASPs are licensed — **Yellow Card** and
  **Express Minds** — under the Virtual Asset Act 2022; the **Bank of Botswana** declines to recognise
  crypto as legal tender, even as boosterish coverage casts Botswana as an "emerging crypto/tokenisation"
  hub. Sources: [[2025-07-16-crypto-finds-footing-but-far-from-legal-tender]],
  [[2025-07-13-why-botswana-is-africas-emerging-crypto-giant]].
- **AML oversight gaps (2026-02).** [[gov.regional]] The **ESAAMLG** 12th Enhanced Follow-Up Report flagged
  **VASP-supervision shortcomings** — a "digital-asset blind spot" raising money-laundering concerns.
  Sources: [[2026-02-23-crypto-oversight-gaps-persist-in-botswana]],
  [[2026-02-23-botswana-digital-asset-blind-spot-laundering-alarm]].
