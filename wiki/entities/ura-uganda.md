# Uganda Revenue Authority (URA)

**Uganda's tax administration** — and one of the most digitally advanced arms of the
Ugandan state, running a stack of tax DPIs (ITAS, [[efris]], PINs, Digital Tax Stamps)
and now folding the national ID into tax identity itself. Commissioner General:
**John R. Musinguzi** ([[john-musinguzi]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **TIN → NIN (2025-11-11).** URA announced the transition from the Tax Identification
  Number to the **National Identification Number** under the **Tax Procedures Amendment
  Bill**: *"Soon and very soon, your NIN will be your TIN"* (#LearnTaxUg). NIRA-issued
  NINs become citizens' official tax identity; businesses registered with **[[ursb]]** use
  their URSB registration numbers as TINs. Framed as consolidating government services
  under a single interoperable digital identity, aligned to **Vision 2040**. Also
  announced: a **three-year income tax exemption** for qualifying startups registered
  after **1 July 2025** with capital not exceeding **UGX 500 million**. See
  [[ndaga-muntu]], [[nira-uganda]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-11 URA Launches Tax Filing Season, Announces Shift from TIN to NIN]].
- **Tax payment on MTN MoMo (2025-12-23).** URA, with [[mtn-uganda]] and MTN MoMo,
  launched enhanced tax payment services: the new USSD code ***165*18#**, free of charge,
  working on any phone, with the existing ***285#** retained for other tax services. URA
  reported collecting **UGX 15.25 trillion net in H1 FY2025/26 against a UGX 14.93tn
  target — 102% performance, a UGX 322bn surplus**. Commissioner General Musinguzi's
  rationale, plainly stated: *"By shifting transactions from cash to digital platforms,
  economic activity becomes visible and traceable. Informal traders can be identified and
  gradually integrated into the tax net."* See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-23 URA Launches Enhanced Tax Payment Services on MTN MoMo to Boost Digital Compliance]].
- **The revenue case for DPI (IMF analysis, 2025-04-21 — attribute; analysis, not
  primary).** [[imf]]-published analysis records Uganda targeting **tax-to-GDP from 13 to
  18 percent by 2030** against a ratio that has **stagnated near 13 percent for a decade**
  (SSA average **16 percent**, OECD **34 percent**, per a 2024 OECD report — reference
  figures, cited not absorbed). Results attributed to URA's DPIs: **TINs** expanded the
  base by integrating national ID, registering **over 350,000 informal taxpayers by
  2022**; **[[efris]]** produced a **12% rise in VAT collections**; **PINs** brought
  **25% of tax payments** onto digital channels; **DTS** curbed illicit trade. Challenges
  noted: limited technical capacity, a digital gap where revenue administration outpaces
  other agencies (hampering data sharing), and smaller taxpayers without digital access.
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-21 Leveraging Digital Public Infrastructure to Enhance Uganda's Tax Revenue Potential]].
