# Visa–Onafriq DRC stablecoin settlement (via Visa Pay)

**Parties:** [[visa]], [[onafriq]] (via **Visa Pay**); [[safaricom]] M-Pesa is the wallet being
topped up, not a confirmed stablecoin-leg partner.

## What the record actually supports (REV-006 resolved 2026-07-16)

The reliable, primary-anchored statement is **narrow**: Visa says it is **settling M-Pesa wallet
top-ups in stablecoins in the background, in the DRC, with Onafriq, via the Visa Pay platform**. This
rests on an **on-record quote from a named senior Visa executive** — Godfrey Sullivan (SVP & Head of
Products & Solutions, CEMEA), reported by The EastAfrican (2 Jul 2026) and syndicated widely:

> "There is a use case that we have in DR Congo where we are settling M-Pesa mobile money top-ups with
> stablecoins through a partnership with Onafriq … as you top up your M-Pesa wallet, the transaction is
> settled in stablecoins in the background."

Confirmed context, on solid footing:
- **Visa Pay launched in the DRC in Sept/Q4 2025** via an [[onafriq]] API — independently corroborated
  ([[2026-01-26 How Onafriq is growing digital finance amid uncertainty in DRC]]).
- The mechanism targets the ~**8%** average SSA remittance fee by settling on blockchain rather than
  through correspondent banking.

## What is NOT supported — downgraded framing

The maximalist "**Visa + M-Pesa + Onafriq jointly launched a USD-stablecoin pilot**" wording is
**trade-press framing, not a joint issuer announcement**. Ecofin Agency (2 Jul 2026) states directly
that **no single, jointly-announced pilot exists** — only "a set of converging initiatives."
Specifically unconfirmed as of 2026-07-16:
- **No issuer press release** (Visa, Safaricom/M-Pesa Africa or Onafriq) announces a three-party pilot.
- **The "USD" peg** for the DRC leg is **inferred** from Visa's broader USDC work, not primary-stated;
  Visa has disclosed neither the stablecoin nor the blockchain.
- **M-Pesa's own** announced stablecoin move is **separate** — a Jan 2026 ADI Foundation alliance around
  a **UAE-dirham** stablecoin, not this USD DRC settlement.
- **Scale/status** (volumes, customers, test-vs-rollout) undisclosed; one planned Visa partnership is
  **still awaiting regulatory approval** (EastAfrican editor's note), and the **DRC central bank is
  pursuing de-dollarisation** — a live regulatory tension over any USD-stablecoin rail.
