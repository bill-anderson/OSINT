# Data Systems at a Crossroads: Official Statistics for a New Era

**PARIS21 Discussion Paper 22** (2026), authored by **Lorenz Noe** (Open Data Watch) and
**Mercedes Fogarassy** ([[paris21]]) under the guidance of **Shaida Badiee** (Open Data Watch) and
**Johannes Jütting** (PARIS21); funded by the Hewlett Foundation. A named-authored scholarly
discussion paper — cited here as literature; a dated fact drawn from it becomes a source citing it.
Reference document: `2026-07-11 data-systems-crossroads.pdf`.

## Argument

- Official statistics face **converging pressures**: 2024–25 **donor funding cuts** threaten the
  operational viability of national statistical offices (NSOs) in low-income countries; **AI** is
  simultaneously disrupting and enhancing data production; mandates keep expanding (disaggregation,
  citizen engagement) even as resources shrink; and **trust** is under strain from political
  interference and post-COVID credibility gaps.
- Diagnoses **three crisis categories** — legitimacy/trust/political will; financing and
  sustainability; and critical capacity gaps.
- Frames a **"fork in the road"**: **Path One** — optimisation and smarter use of existing solutions;
  **Path Two** — transformation of data systems. Includes a box on India's NSO connecting official
  statistics to AI tools.
- Directly relevant to the wiki's [[data.statistics]] thread and the post-aid-shock funding crisis
  for African statistical systems (cf. [[data.statistics]], and the Development Initiatives
  bottom-up-data thesis).
