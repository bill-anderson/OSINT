# Pesalink

Pesalink is a **Kenya-based instant payment network** that interconnects the
country's banks and financial institutions for real-time, account-to-account
transfers. Its network spans **more than 80 Kenyan banks, fintechs, SACCOs and
telcos**, giving it broad reach across Kenya's financial system.

## Why it matters here

As reported **2026-03-02** (and confirmed in an **[[afreximbank]]** primary release, 2026-02-26),
Pesalink signed a **partnership agreement with the
[[papss|Pan-African Payment and Settlement System (PAPSS)]]**, joining the PAPSS
platform as a **Technical Connectivity Provider**. The integration connects
Pesalink's 80+ Kenyan institutions to **over 160 commercial banks and fintechs**
participating in PAPSS, enabling instant, 24/7 bank-to-bank and mobile-money
transfers **settled in local currencies** — reducing dependence on foreign
reserve currencies and correspondent-banking chains.

Notably, the agreement marks the **first time PAPSS has piloted a national switch
for transaction termination** (in Kenya). The model connects national and private
switches into PAPSS infrastructure, and lets Kenyan financial institutions offer
cross-border payment services over **existing Pesalink rails** without building
separate correspondent relationships.

## Significance

- **Cost and speed:** Cross-border payments within Africa remain among the
  world's most expensive and slowest — the World Bank's 2023 Remittance Prices
  report put the average cost at **7–8%** of transaction value (above the ~6–7%
  global average), with settlement of three to seven business days. The
  Pesalink–PAPSS integration targets both.
- **Continental integration:** The partnership is positioned to support intra-
  African trade under the **[[afcfta|AfCFTA]]** framework, and to expand
  cross-border payment access by connecting more national switches into PAPSS.
- **Payments mix (2026-01):** even with instant rails dominant, paper **cheques still move
  ~$17bn/yr in Kenya** (avg ~$1.57bn/month, 11 months to Nov 2025); the
  [[central-bank-of-kenya]] favours reform over abolition — clearing cut to 1 day (from 5 in
  2011), cheques capped at $7,750 — while Burkina Faso, Zambia, Seychelles and Ghana move to
  phase them out. Source: [[2026-01-13 The rise of cheques as Kenya's preferred payment method]].

Pesalink now functions as Kenya's on-ramp into the pan-African instant-payments
architecture, a switch-to-switch model that PAPSS intends to replicate elsewhere.

Pesalink is operated by Integrated Payment Services Limited (IPSL), owned by the Kenya Bankers
Association, and is profiled in AfricaNenda's **SIIPS 2022** case study as Kenya's bank-led instant
payment system. Sources: [[2026-02-26 Pesalink and PAPSS Unlock Cross-Border Payments in Local Currencies in Kenya]],
[[2022-01-01 SIIPS 2022 Case Study - Pesalink (AfricaNenda)]].
