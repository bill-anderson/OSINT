# Abeba Birhane

Ethiopian-born cognitive scientist and AI-ethics researcher (University College Dublin / Lero;
formerly Mozilla Foundation), one of the most-cited scholars in the wiki's **algorithmic-
colonialism / data-justice** literature. Her work supplies much of the theoretical backbone for
the `colonialism` lens as applied to African data and AI.

## Key work in this wiki

- **"The Algorithmic Colonization of Africa" (2020).** The foundational essay framing Western
  tech monopolies' control of African digital infrastructure and data extraction as a
  continuation of colonial domination — "we are merely data-producing 'human natural resources'."
  See [[colonialism]] framing on [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2020-08-21 Algorithmic Colonisation of Africa]].
- **"Narratives and Counternarratives on Data Sharing in Africa" (FAccT '21, with Rediet Abebe,
  Kehinde Aruleba et al).** Empirical/ethical analysis of data-sharing practices and their power
  asymmetries in Africa. Source: [[2021-03-01 Narratives and Counternarratives on Data Sharing in Africa (Abebe, Birhane et al, FAccT 2021)]].
- **"Power to the People? Participatory AI" (EAAMO '22).** On the opportunities and limits of
  participatory approaches to AI (global scholarship). Source:
  [[2022-10-01 Power to the People - Participatory AI (Birhane et al, EAAMO 2022)]].
