# Bank of Tanzania (Financial Consumer Protection) (Amendment) Regulations, GN No. 298 of 2025

An amendment instrument issued by the **[[bank-of-tanzania]]**, **dated 23 May 2025**, amending
the **Financial Consumer Protection Regulations, 2019** to strengthen consumer protection and
transparency in financial services. It applies to **all financial service providers licensed or
supervised by the BOT**, except where otherwise prescribed. The analysis held here is a
**Kemi Advocates** client briefing (**2025-06-27**) — attribute the reading to them; the
instrument itself is the primary object.

## What it changes

- **Account status.** "Dormant account" extended from **5 to 12 months**; a new **"inactive
  account"** category for accounts unused **more than 5 months**; the **High Court of Zanzibar**
  brought within the definition of "Court".
- **Charges prohibited.** No interest-rate hikes or non-disclosed late-payment penalties on
  existing balances; **no maintenance fees on dormant or inactive accounts**. Guidelines against
  abusive debt recovery — unlawful collections, false statements, harassment, coercion.
- **Interest calculation.** Mandatory **reducing-balance method** on loans.
- **Fees.** Full disclosure of all fees, charges, interest, profits or returns **before any
  transaction**; **new or increased fees require prior written BOT approval**; the BOT may
  prohibit fees outright to protect consumers.
- **Publication.** Interest rates, fees, charges and related costs published **quarterly in
  Kiswahili and English** newspapers, and on provider websites **within five working days**.
- **Language.** Agreements must be in **English or Kiswahili per the consumer's preference**;
  technical terms restricted unless necessary and clearly explained.
- **Access and literacy.** Providers must run programmes promoting access for **underserved
  populations**; financial literacy must feature in strategic plans and be delivered by
  **certified educators from accredited institutions**. See [[include.access]],
  [[capacity.literacy]].
- **Consumer asset protection.** Providers are liable for losses from fraud, scams or misuse of
  consumer assets and must promptly refund consumers **unless the loss was due to consumer
  negligence or fraud**; they must discipline responsible employees and notify the BOT.
- **Complaints.** Maximum resolution periods — **12 hours for mobile money payments**, **14 days
  for credit facilities**; consumers may escalate to the BOT for review. Enhanced BOT enforcement
  powers, including fines of **TSh 1 million per day** for non-compliance.

## The data-governance hook

Providers must **protect consumer financial and personal information in accordance with the
Personal Data Protection Act** (**[[tanzania-pdpa]]**). This is the analytically interesting
part: a **financial regulator writing the data-protection statute into its own supervisory
regime**, giving the BOT — not only the **[[pdpc-tanzania]]** — a lever over how licensed
providers handle personal data. Two enforcement routes converge on the same conduct, one of them
carrying a **TSh 1m/day** fine and licence-based leverage. See [[gov.protect]],
[[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
[[2025-06-27 Key Highlights of the Bank of Tanzania Financial Consumer Protection Amendment Regulations GN 298 of 2025]].
