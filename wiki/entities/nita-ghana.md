# NITA (National Information Technology Agency, Ghana)

Ghana's **IT/e-government agency** — operator of Ghana.gov, the national data centre and government network,
and an ICT-security/standards regulator. Under reform in 2026:

- **New NITA Act — split of functions (2026-07).** Proposed legislation would **separate NITA's regulatory and
  commercial roles**, with infrastructure payments routed to NITA; a **new Director-General, Nana Yaw
  Amoah-Yeboah**, succeeded Mark-Oliver Kevor (2026-07). Sources:
  [[2026-07-16-nita-split-regulatory-commercial-myjoy]], [[2026-07-16-nita-new-director-general-techafricanews]].
- **NITA Bill controversy.** Critics (part of the "15 digital bills" debate) flagged **broad licensing powers
  and a 1% levy**; Minister **Sam George** rebutted, saying funding detail will sit in an L.I. and the rules
  won't be rewritten. Sources: [[2026-05-26-tech-consultant-questions-15-digital-bills-myjoy]],
  [[2026-05-30-nita-bill-sam-george-responds-critics-myjoy]].
- Authored the **[[ghana-data-harmonisation-bill|Data Harmonisation Bill 2025]]** and re-engineers
  **[[ghana-gov-platform|Ghana.gov]]**. See [[ghana--gov-protect]], [[ghana--dpi-govtech]].
