# AU Digital Transformation Strategy for Africa (2020–2030)

- **Issuing body:** [[african-union|African Union]].
- **Primary document now held (2020–2030 strategy).** The full AU Digital Transformation Strategy for Africa is in `raw/`. Source: [[2020-02-01 Digital Transformation Strategy for Africa 2020-2030]].
- **Period:** 2020–2030 (ten-year strategy).
- **Scope:** Pan-African; the AU's overarching blueprint for building an integrated,
  inclusive African digital single market — spanning enabling infrastructure, digital ID
  and payments, skills, data governance and a supportive policy/regulatory environment.
- **Status:** Active; reached its **midpoint in 2025**, shifting the emphasis from
  strategy-setting to execution.

## What it is

The DTS is the top-level continental digital-policy instrument under which the AU's more
specialised frameworks — the [[au-continental-ai-strategy]], the
[[au-data-policy-framework]] and the [[afcfta]] digital-trade agenda — are nested. It is the
recurring reference point invoked to anchor AU-level digital partnerships (e.g. the AUC–Google
MoU cites both the DTS and the Continental AI Strategy).

## In this wiki

- The 2025 pan-African policy review marks the DTS reaching its midpoint, framing 2026 as a
  test of the continent's ability to operationalise continental frameworks in digital
  identity, payments, cross-border data flows and digital trade — a **light-attach
  reference**, figures not hardened onto pages. Source:
  [[2026-01-12 (3) Africa's Digital Policy Review 2025 in Perspective and Outlook for 2026]].
