# MTN Eswatini

**The country's incumbent mobile operator and, for nearly two decades, its only one.**
The [[mtn-group]] operation in [[SWZ]] held a **mobile monopoly from 1998 until 2017**,
when competition finally entered the market — the structural fact behind Eswatini's
persistent data-cost problem. Regulated by [[esccom]]. See [[infra.connect]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Monopoly until 2017.** Until 2017 citizens had only one mobile operator, MTN
  Eswatini, which had held a monopoly since 1998; regulatory oversight shifted to
  [[esccom]] on its establishment in 2013. See [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2024-07-22 Risk of human rights infringement as Eswatini rushes to regulate the ICT sector]].
- **2021 internet shutdown.** During the June–July 2021 unrest, government directed
  service providers — [[eptc]], MTN Eswatini and Eswatini Mobile — to suspend internet
  services. The International Commission of Jurists wrote to the CEO of MTN South
  Africa urging resumption. In October 2021, during protests, government restricted
  access to social media platforms. See [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-11 Eswatini strives for digital sovereignty amid technological advancements]].
- **DRA validation panel (2024-11-13).** MTN Eswatini sat on the
  [[eswatini-digital-readiness-assessment]] validation workshop panel at the Hilton
  Garden Hotel, Mbabane, alongside [[undp]], [[ministry-of-ict-eswatini]],
  [[world-bank]], InstaCash and the [[itu]]. Source:
  [[2024-12-05 Eswatini Advances Toward Digital Transformation - Key Findings from the Digital Readiness Assessment]].
- **SIM registration.** Operators collect names, addresses, identity numbers and
  facial images under Legal Notice No. 26 of 2016, retaining them for up to five years
  after contract termination. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2024-07-22 Risk of human rights infringement as Eswatini rushes to regulate the ICT sector]].
