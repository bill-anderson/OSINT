# Michael Aboneka

**Digital rights writer publishing with the Digital Rights Alliance Africa (DRAA).** The wiki holds
**one** piece by him (2025-09-13) — this page records what that piece argues and nothing more.

He is the wiki's **named attribution point for the critique of the *ISER* judgment** on Uganda's
mandatory national ID. That matters because **the judgment itself is not held (GAP-UGA-001)**: where a
page states what the High Court decided and why it is contested, the "why it is contested" traces to
this author, as **advocacy analysis** — not to the primary.

## What he argues (2025-09-13)

On the dismissal of ***Initiative for Social and Economic Rights ([[iser-uganda]]) & 2 Others v
Attorney General & NIRA*, HCT-00-CV-MC-0066-2022 (High Court of Uganda, 10 June 2025)**:

- **The ruling legitimises an exclusionary system** — one that denies access to services for those
  without a National ID ([[ndaga-muntu]]) under the [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]].
- **The Court's "offline" finding is contradicted by the government's own roadmap.** His central
  rebuttal: the **5-year Digital Transformation Roadmap, launched 17 August 2023**, shifts service
  provision onto DPI — the National ID system, mobile money, **[[efris]]**, the Express Penalty
  Scheme, Digital Tax Stamps and Payment Registration Numbers — **all of which require
  authentication**. A system whose services demand authentication is not, on his account, a system that
  "operates primarily offline."
- **The dependencies are already built.** The **Ministry of Education** requires a NIN for learner
  registration on the **EMIS** portal, excluding those without from enumeration, planning and
  budgeting; **[[sage-uganda]]** benefits only those on the register.
- **The Court offered no alternatives** (baptism cards, voters' cards, residence identity cards) and
  relied heavily on respondents' submissions — deepening exclusion of rural communities, older
  persons, women and the digitally illiterate.
- **Uganda's DPI lacks safeguards** — no strong legal safeguards, comprehensive data protection or
  independent oversight — risking surveillance, discrimination and misuse; and falls short of the
  [[world-bank]]'s **ID4D principle of universal coverage from birth to death, free from
  discrimination**.

## The unsourced figure

The same piece estimates that **18 million Ugandans (about 40% of the population)** lack a National ID
(**as of 2025-09-13**). **That estimate carries no cited source.** It is his figure, and **the absence
of a source is itself the finding** — it sits unreconciled against [[nira-uganda]]'s **35 million**
register (2026-02-11). The wiki adopts neither value. See
`reviews/contradictions/open/uganda-unregistered-population`.
