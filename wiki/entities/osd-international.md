# OSD International

**Austrian identity-technology firm** (referred to as OSD or OSD International) hired to rescue and
operate Liberia's stalled national identity system ([[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]]). OSD is the firm at
the centre of Liberia's shift from a procurement contract to a **public-private-partnership concession**
— the arrangement that both restarts ID issuance and concentrates the sovereignty concerns running
through the whole crisis. See [[LBR]], [[national-identification-registry-liberia]], [[liberia--dpi-id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **From rejected bid to concession (2025→2026).** OSD was originally lined up under a restricted-bid
  award tied to the April 2025 EO 147 rollout; the PPCC rejected that award (Jul 2025), contributing to
  the issuance suspension. President Boakai then directed that instead of a standard contract the
  government enter a **concession agreement via PPP**. Source:
  [[2026-05-28-osd-concession-issuance-restart-biometricupdate]].
- **Concession terms (2026-05).** OSD would **finance** enrollment and issuance infrastructure with a
  goal of enrolling Liberia's **entire ~5.3m population** plus foreign residents within **18 months**,
  operate the system for a defined period, recover its investment, then **transfer it to the state**
  (a build-operate-transfer structure). An estimated **3.5m vulnerable Liberians** would receive the
  card free; OSD delivers a **polycarbonate card** with advanced security features. The model is drawn
  from **Ghana's** national-ID PPP (Identity Management Systems Ltd., a Margins Group subsidiary).
  Sources: [[2026-05-28-osd-concession-issuance-restart-biometricupdate]],
  [[2026-05-13-osd-austrian-firm-rescue-national-id-security-concerns-fpa]].
- **Secrecy + data-protection risk (2026-05).** Civil society warns the concession puts the biometric
  data of **5.3m Liberians** at risk with **no data-protection law** in place; how OSD was selected,
  the deal's terms, and the presidential committee report all remain undisclosed. The concession must be
  ratified by the Legislature. Source:
  [[2026-05-13-osd-austrian-firm-rescue-national-id-security-concerns-fpa]].
