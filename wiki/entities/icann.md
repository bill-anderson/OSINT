# ICANN

The **Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers** — the global body coordinating the
internet's unique-identifier systems (domain names, IP-address allocation to the Regional
Internet Registries, protocol parameters). Operates on a multistakeholder, bottom-up policy
model. Appears in this wiki as a contested actor in African internet governance.

## Activity in this wiki

- **CAIGA controversy (2024–2026).** Per [[alice-munyua]] in
  [[2025-11-04 The Double Standard Why Are Internet Governance Rules Different for Africa]], ICANN
  co-developed, funded and lent institutional legitimacy to [[smart-africa]]'s [[caiga|Continental
  Africa Internet Governance Architecture]] via a **2024 ICANN/Smart Africa MoU** for joint
  research on government involvement in internet-infrastructure governance. Munyua argues this
  facilitates governmental capture of [[afrinic]] (a community-governed RIR) and asks whether
  ICANN would do the same to RIPE NCC, ARIN, APNIC or LACNIC — i.e. a double standard for Africa.
  CAIGA was presented at **ICANN84 Dublin** as "two years of joint work with ICANN".
