# Digital Gabon Project (P175987)

The **World Bank**–financed umbrella operation ("Gabon Digital" / "eGabon") anchoring Gabon's
digital-transformation programme. World Bank IPF, FY2022, code **P175987**; implementing agency
**CN-TIPPEE** (National Commission for Public Infrastructure Works and Promotion of Small-Scale
Enterprises).

**Development objective:** enhance adoption of select digitalized public services and increase the
number of persons with a legal-identity credential facilitating access to services.

## Key facts

- **Financing:** $68.5m IBRD loan (**IBRD-92830**). Signed 2 January 2024 by Mays Mouissi (Economy)
  and Cheick Kante (World Bank); board approval had come **30 July 2021**. See [[world-bank]].
- **Components:** enabling environment for data protection, cybersecurity and secure data exchange
  ([[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[dpi.exchange]]); modernised legal identity — digitalised
  civil registration and a unique ID on a one-stop platform ([[dpi.id]]); a new **data center**,
  digital applications and network rehabilitation ([[infra.store]]); inclusive access for vulnerable
  groups ([[include.access]]).
- **Delivery vehicles:** the digital-ID rollout (CNIE/NIP, supplied by [[in-groupe]]) is the flagship
  DPI component; the [[registre-social-unique-gabon]] social registry draws on the same World Bank
  partnership.

## Execution trajectory (the story the primary docs tell)

- **Long pre-effectiveness delay.** Approved July 2021 but **not yet effective by mid-2024** (ISR
  Seq 6, [[2024-06-29-world-bank-digital-gabon-isr-seq6]]); a Level-2 restructuring was approved
  Dec 2023.
- **Effective July 2024, then near-idle.** ISR Seq 7 ([[2024-12-19-world-bank-digital-gabon-isr-seq7]])
  records effectiveness 30 July 2024 "after a significant delay," with **disbursement at just 1.92%**
  by December 2024 and all results indicators still at baseline (targets: 100,000 successful citizen
  digital transactions and 5 entities on the interoperability platform by the June 2026 closing).
- **2026 acceleration push.** The **5th eGabon / Gabon Digital steering committee** (23 Feb 2026,
  [[2026-02-24-gabon-digital-reform-execution-speedup]]) explicitly flagged the **too-low disbursement
  rate** and weak interministerial coordination — the government moving from structuring to execution.
