# National Identification Agency (NIDA), Rwanda

Rwanda's foundational-identity authority, responsible for the national population
register and civil identity. It runs **e-Indangamuntu**, the country's biometric
digital national ID, and is led by Director General **Josephine Mukesha**. Distinct
from the similarly-named [[nida-tanzania]]. See [[RWA]], [[dpi.id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **SDIS project — origins and financing.** The scheme sits within the **Single Digital
  Identification System (SDIS)**, started in earnest in **2023** with a legislative
  amendment; bids for the digital-ID core and support modules were received in **May
  2024**. Aim: a secure biometric digital identity for **every citizen, refugee and
  foreign resident**. Cost is reported inconsistently — **~$38m "upon completion"**
  (Aug 2025), with **~$8.5m** allocated for enrolment across FY2025–26, versus **~$57m**
  when framed as the digital-ID component of the **Rwanda Digital Acceleration Project
  (RDAP)** (Feb 2026); the figures may cover different scopes. The unique ID number is
  **assigned for life** (an ABIS deduplicates the biometric database), explicitly fixing
  the legacy problem of a person accumulating multiple numbers. Sources:
  [[2025-08-08 Rwanda National Identification Agency begins digital ID enrollment]],
  [[2026-02-10 Rwanda digital ID initiative advances with 1.5 million enrollments]].
- **Enrolment chronology.** Nationwide **registration launched 7 Aug 2025**; biometric
  capture began ~mid-Sept 2025, first in southern regions. By **Nov 2025 >300,000** had
  completed; the **Kigali drive opened 7 Feb 2026**; by **10 Feb 2026 ~1.5m had
  completed all stages**, with **~1.69m more** (many children under 16, needing guardian
  consent) awaiting biometric capture — rising to **2,373,854 by 16 Mar 2026** (~40,000/
  day). Biometrics captured: **face, iris and fingerprints**. Sources:
  [[2025-08-08 Rwanda National Identification Agency begins digital ID enrollment]],
  [[2026-02-10 Rwanda digital ID initiative advances with 1.5 million enrollments]],
  [[2026-03-16 Digital IDs How registration is progressing so far]].
- **Inclusion of non-citizens.** Legal residents, refugees, asylum seekers, migrants
  and stateless persons are enrolled, with document rules per category (residence
  permit + passport for foreigners; refugee ID or proof of registration; a three-witness
  administrative letter for stateless persons). The **Directorate General of Immigration
  and Emigration** ran the Kigali foreigner/refugee registration (7–15 Feb 2026) across
  eight sector offices. See [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-10 Where can refugees, foreigners register for digital ID]].
- **Legacy data-quality flaw (Auditor-General, 2025-06).** The **2025 Auditor-General's
  report** (AG Alexis Kamuhire) found that under the old system NIDA **issued a wholly
  new 16-digit NIN on every correction or card replacement** — so one person could hold
  two or more NINs, breaking downstream systems (banking, land, SIM, voting, pension)
  that assume one number per person. Recommendation: **separate the identity number from
  the physical card** and re-issue the same NIN on replacement. This is precisely the
  defect the SDIS "one number for life" design is meant to close. See [[dpi.registry]],
  [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-18 Why Are Rwandans Getting Multiple National ID Numbers - Auditor General Points to a System Flaw]].
- **SIM linkage.** Under [[rwanda-sim-card-registration-regulation-2024]] ([[rura-rwanda]]),
  SIM registration is verified against NIDA's biometric database and each SIM is bound to
  the holder's unique ID number. See [[infra.cybersec]].
- **Biometric digital ID rollout (as of 2026-03-16).** NIDA had registered
  **2,373,854 citizens of an estimated 14 million** target, enrolling **~40,000 per
  day** through a nationwide seven-week rotating exercise (Kigali deadline set at
  2026-04-02). e-Indangamuntu **adds an iris scan** to the existing fingerprint and
  facial biometrics; DG Mukesha framed the iris capture as the only genuinely new
  feature over the ID system Rwanda has run since 1962. See [[dpi.registry]].
  Source: [[2026-03-16 Digital IDs How registration is progressing so far]].
- **Single Digital Identification Law.** Governs the scheme: **no citizen may hold
  two government-issued identities**, and **all current ID cards become invalid,
  to be replaced by June 2027** under a sunset provision. See [[gov.legislate]].
- **Three ID forms.** A holder may take just the **ID number**, generate a **digital
  token**, or obtain a **physical QR-coded card** carrying minimal information —
  the holder chooses. The system is integrated with Rwanda's **Civil Registration
  and Vital Statistics (CRVS)** and links parent-child family trees for service
  delivery.
- **Consent and access controls.** Data use requires consent (self-consent at **age
  16+**; guardians for minors and persons with mental disabilities). Via a **digital
  wallet and the eRainbow platform**, citizens can **see which institutions accessed
  their data and when, and revoke consent** — a data-subject-rights design point.
  See [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]].
