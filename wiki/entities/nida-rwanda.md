# National Identification Agency (NIDA), Rwanda

Rwanda's foundational-identity authority, responsible for the national population
register and civil identity. It runs **e-Indangamuntu**, the country's biometric
digital national ID, and is led by Director General **Josephine Mukesha**. Distinct
from the similarly-named [[nida-tanzania]]. See [[RWA]], [[dpi.id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Biometric digital ID rollout (as of 2026-03-16).** NIDA had registered
  **2,373,854 citizens of an estimated 14 million** target, enrolling **~40,000 per
  day** through a nationwide seven-week rotating exercise (Kigali deadline set at
  2026-04-02). e-Indangamuntu **adds an iris scan** to the existing fingerprint and
  facial biometrics; DG Mukesha framed the iris capture as the only genuinely new
  feature over the ID system Rwanda has run since 1962. See [[dpi.registry]].
  Source: [[2026-03-16 Digital IDs How registration is progressing so far]].
- **Single Digital Identification Law.** Governs the scheme: **no citizen may hold
  two government-issued identities**, and **all current ID cards become invalid,
  to be replaced by June 2027** under a sunset provision. See [[gov.legislate]].
- **Three ID forms.** A holder may take just the **ID number**, generate a **digital
  token**, or obtain a **physical QR-coded card** carrying minimal information —
  the holder chooses. The system is integrated with Rwanda's **Civil Registration
  and Vital Statistics (CRVS)** and links parent-child family trees for service
  delivery.
- **Consent and access controls.** Data use requires consent (self-consent at **age
  16+**; guardians for minors and persons with mental disabilities). Via a **digital
  wallet and the eRainbow platform**, citizens can **see which institutions accessed
  their data and when, and revoke consent** — a data-subject-rights design point.
  See [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]].
