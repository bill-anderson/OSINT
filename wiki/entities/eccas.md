# ECCAS (Economic Community of Central African States)

The **Regional Economic Community for Central Africa** (CEEAC in French), founded **18 October
1983** by members of the Central African Customs and Economic Union (UDEAC — Cameroon, CAR,
Chad, Congo, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon) plus the Great Lakes states (Burundi, Rwanda, DRC),
Angola and São Tomé and Príncipe. It began functioning in 1985 and hibernated 1992–1998 amid
regional conflict. Broader than the CEMAC monetary union served by **[[beac]]**.

## Relevance to this wiki

- **Regional frame for cross-border data.** CAR's data-protection law (**[[car-data-protection-law-24-001]]**)
  sets *specific conditions* for personal-data transfers to **CEMAC and ECCAS** member states
  — so ECCAS is a named tier in the country's cross-border-transfer regime. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[gov.protect]].
- **Integration deficit (2024 analysis).** A Pan-African University study finds ECCAS's
  Annex-VII protocol on **free movement of persons and goods** remains poorly implemented —
  weak infrastructure/communication links, political instability, nationalism, inter-state
  rivalry and pervasive roadblocks. Background on the bloc's weak integration capacity (the
  free-movement content is tangential to data governance). Source:
  [[2024-02-19 Challenges to the Implementation of Regional Integration in ECCAS]].
