# Niger Data Protection Law (Loi 2022-59)

Niger's **personal-data-protection statute**. Niger first legislated with **Loi n° 2017-28** (3 May 2017,
amended by **Loi n° 2019-71** of 24 Dec 2019), which created the supervisory
**[[hapdp-niger|High Authority (HAPDP)]]** — but per the HAPDP president (2025) that arsenal was **repealed and
replaced by Loi n° 2022-59 of 16 December 2022** (and subsequent modifying texts, incl. **Loi n° 2023-31**), to
keep pace with technology and align with international standards. *(Current-law correction, 2026-07-19: the wiki
previously carried Loi 2017-28/2019-71 as the operative regime; Loi 2022-59 supersedes them — the older laws
remain the dated antecedents.)*

The framework rests on Niger's **ratification of the AU Malabo Convention on 23 May 2022** (Art. 8 requires an
independent DP authority). Under the law, HAPDP ensures personal-data processing does not harm public liberties
or private life — issuing decisions (*délibérations*), receiving declarations/authorisation requests and
complaints, conducting controls via sworn agents, referring offences to justice, and opining on draft laws. It is
the legal backdrop for Niger's fast-moving **[[niger-national-eid|biometric e-ID and e-passport]]** programme.
Source: [[2025-07-28-hapdp-president-interview-loi-2022-59-lesahel]].
