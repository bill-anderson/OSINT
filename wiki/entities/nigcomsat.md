# Nigerian Communications Satellite Ltd (NigComSat)

Nigeria's state-owned satellite operator, providing broadband, broadcasting and enterprise
connectivity across African markets. Managing Director and CEO: **Jane Nkechi
Egerton-Idehen**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Revenue tripled to >$2bn (disclosed 2026, Abuja).** At the 2026 Nigerian Satellite
  Week — marking the company's **20th anniversary** — NigComSat reported revenue rising from
  **$650m in 2023 to over $2bn by end-2025**, having moved beyond its legacy single-satellite
  role into a broader portfolio (broadband, broadcasting infrastructure, enterprise). Figures
  are single-source (company disclosure). See [[NGA]], [[tech.industry]],
  [[infra.capacity]]. Source: [[2026-04-01 Nigeria satellite revenue soars to $2 billion]].
- **Two new satellites procured.** NigComSat has concluded procurement for **NIGCOMSAT-2A**
  and **NIGCOMSAT-2B**, high-capacity Ka/Ku-band satellites slated for **2028 and 2029**, to
  support 5G backhaul and national-security operations (real-time data gathering and
  intelligence). The project is entering its financing and implementation phase. See
  [[infra.connect]]. Source: [[2026-04-01 Nigeria satellite revenue soars to $2 billion]].
- **Ageing fleet.** The existing **NIGCOMSAT-1R** (launched 2011) is nearing its 15-year
  design life in 2026; the Federal government has approved replacement acquisition. Regionally
  Nigeria trails continental leaders **Egypt (Nilesat)** and **South Africa (Sentech)** in
  total satellite count. Source: [[2026-04-01 Nigeria satellite revenue soars to $2 billion]].
