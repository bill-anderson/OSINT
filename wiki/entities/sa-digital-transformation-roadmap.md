# South Africa Digital Transformation Roadmap

The **Digital Transformation Roadmap** is South Africa's whole-of-government digital public infrastructure programme — **approved by Cabinet on 2025-03-26** and **publicly launched 2025-05-12** (*both dates are real and six weeks apart; neither corrects the other*) — that provides the strategic frame for **[[mymzansi]]** and the delivery work of the **[[digital-services-unit-presidency-sa]]**. Its stated aims are to create a **single digital identity** for citizens, enable **data sharing across government**, support **digital payments**, and offer a **zero-rated platform** on which people can access public services even without mobile data.

## Scope and status

The roadmap is the umbrella under which specific citizen-facing services are being built and sequenced:

- **Digital driving licence** — a digital driving permit alongside the physical card, plus an extension of card validity from **5 to 8 years**, both reported "in motion" as of January 2026 and demonstrated on the MyMzansi platform. Source: [[2026-01-07 Digital driving licence coming to South Africa]].
- **Digital ID, payments and credentials** — delivered through MyMzansi's modular open-source stack, with a first public rollout targeted for H1 2026 (a target subsequently under schedule pressure; see [[mymzansi]] and the digital-ID timeline).

- **The problem Cabinet said it was solving (2025-03-26).** The approving Cabinet statement names the
  target issues as **interdepartmental coordination, outdated systems, overlapping mandates and
  limited skills or capacity**, and the goal as "a **people-centered digital government** that better
  understands the needs of the people, **particularly vulnerable groups**". **The stated diagnosis is
  institutional fragmentation, not technology.** The same meeting approved **Operation Vulindlela
  Phase 2** with **digital transformation** as a new focus area — the Roadmap and the reform engine
  were authorised together. Source: [[2025-03-27-cabinet-statement-26-march-2025]].
- **First instrument issued under it (2025-04-03).** SARS, Home Affairs, the BMA and GPW signed
  digital-ID collaboration agreements **expressly "in line with the Digital Transformation of
  Government Roadmap, which the Cabinet approved on 26 March 2025"** — evidence the Roadmap operates
  as a live authority departments cite, eight days after approval and six weeks before its public
  launch. See [[south-africa--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-03-sars-collaboration-agreements-dha-bma-gpw]].

The roadmap lists **interoperability and scalability** as core service principles and stretches to a **2030 horizon**, guided by the Presidency's Inter-Ministerial Committee on Digital Transformation. It is closely aligned with, and overlaps, the DPI pillar of **[[operation-vulindlela]]**.
