# MTN Nigeria

MTN Nigeria is the Nigerian operating subsidiary of the [[mtn-group]] and the country's
largest mobile network operator — a distinct entity from sister operations such as
[[mtn-ghana]]. CEO: **Karl Toriola**.

## What matters now

- **Fibre vandalism at scale (2025).** Per MTN Nigeria's "2025 Wrapped" (Toriola social-media
  disclosure): **9,218 fibre-cut incidents** (1 Jan–31 Dec 2025) and vandalism at **211
  telecom sites** (1 Jan–30 Nov 2025) caused severe network disruption — a physical-security,
  not cyber, problem ([[infra.connect]]). Service disruptions drove **1,624,263 consumer
  complaints** in 2025 (all reported resolved).
- **Return to profit.** Profit after tax of **₦750.19bn (~$525m)** for the nine months to Sept
  2025, versus a ₦514.9bn loss in the same period of 2024; an interim dividend of ₦5.00/share
  was approved. See [[tech.industry]].

- **Sifiso Dabengwa data centre in the AI-sovereignty read (analysis, 2026-06/07).** Two analyst
  pieces treat MTN Nigeria's Lagos **Sifiso Dabengwa Data Centre** — **$235m, launched July 2025**,
  a **4.5MW Tier III** facility (largest in West Africa, via Bayobab) paired with a **$20m domestic
  cloud priced in naira for AI/GPU workloads** — as the kind of local capacity Nigeria needs to
  absorb the [[cbn]] data-localisation mandate and assert AI/compute sovereignty. Analyst framing,
  attributed to Anjola Adebowale and to *Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate*. See
  [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.pay]]. Sources:
  [[2026-06-19 Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part II The Opportunity and the Case for Sequencing]],
  [[2026-06-01 Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate]].

MTN Nigeria is also a priority market in the group's Lagos data-centre / AI push (the Sifiso
Dabengwa centre); the group-level thread also sits on [[mtn-group]] and the [[NGA]] hub.
