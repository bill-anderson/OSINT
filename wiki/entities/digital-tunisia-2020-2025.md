# Digital Tunisia 2020-2025 (National Strategic Plan)

**Tunisia's National Strategic Plan for digital transformation over 2020–2025 — the policy frame
under which the national interoperability platform and the national digital identity are both
positioned as foundational building blocks.** Owned by the
[[ministry-of-communication-technologies-tunisia]].

**Stated priorities:** ICT governance, digital infrastructure, cybersecurity, and data-driven
government — with **the roll-out of e-identity and interoperability described as the foundational
building blocks**.

*The plan document itself is not held; it is characterised here from sources that cite it. Its
2020–2025 window has now closed (as of 2026-07); no source held describes a successor plan or
reports on its final outcomes.*

## Activity in this wiki

- **The frame for the national interoperability platform (as of 2024-02).** [[cybernetica]]
  described the [[uxp-tunisia]] project — the national interoperability platform for G2G, G2B and
  G2C service delivery — as "a significant step toward the National Strategic Plan 'Digital Tunisia
  2020-2025'", listing the plan's priorities as ICT governance, digital infrastructure,
  cybersecurity and data-driven government, with e-identity and interoperability as the foundational
  building blocks. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2024-02-14 National interoperability platform in Tunisia (Cybernetica UXP)]].
- **E-Houwiya as a cornerstone (as of 2025-03).** [[nizar-ben-neji]] presents [[e-houwiya]] —
  introduced **2 August 2022** — as "a cornerstone of Tunisia's 2025 digital strategy" and a
  milestone in the country's digital transformation roadmap. Principal testimony from the minister
  who led the programme, not third-party assessment. See [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
