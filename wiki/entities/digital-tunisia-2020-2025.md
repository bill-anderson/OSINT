# Digital Tunisia 2020-2025 (National Strategic Plan)

**Tunisia's National Strategic Plan for digital transformation over 2020–2025 — the policy frame
under which the national interoperability platform and the national digital identity are both
positioned as foundational building blocks.** Owned by the
[[ministry-of-communication-technologies-tunisia]].

**Stated priorities:** ICT governance, digital infrastructure, cybersecurity, and data-driven
government — with **the roll-out of e-identity and interoperability described as the foundational
building blocks**.

*The plan document itself is not held; it is characterised here from sources that cite it. Its
2020–2025 window has now closed, and **no source held reports its final outcomes** — no completion or
evaluation report was located (as of 2026-07-20).*

## Succession: what replaced it (as of 2026-07-20)

**The plan lapsed rather than being extended or repealed, and its successor is not another sectoral ICT
strategy.** The current national digital framing is the **unified national digital-transformation vision
carried inside the *plan de développement 2026-2030*** — the whole-of-government five-year development
plan. Head of Government **Sarra Zaafrani Zenzri** stated on **13 February 2026** that the State had
equipped itself, *"dans le cadre du plan de développement 2026-2030, d'une vision nationale unifiée pour
la transformation digitale"*, defining objectives, priorities and a delivery timetable
([source](https://pm.gov.tn/fr/decision-gouvernementale/zenzri-lors-dun-conseil-ministeriel-2026-annee-du-demarrage-effectif-de-la)).

This is a **change of instrument type**, and the analytically interesting part: digital policy moved from
a sectoral strategy owned by the [[ministry-of-communication-technologies-tunisia]] to a chapter of a
general development plan coordinated by the **Ministry of Economy and Planning**. Preparation of the ICT
component was launched by minister **Sofiene Hemissi** in **June 2025**; the draft plan went to a
ministerial council on **6 January 2026** under a first-time bottom-up (local → regional → district →
national) method. A **national AI strategy** strand sits within it (priority sectors: digital health,
agriculture, energy, transport, digital administration; framed on **digital sovereignty** and data
protection, as of May 2026), presented at the **WSIS Forum ministerial in Geneva, 10 July 2026**.

**Resolved:** the "National Digital Strategy" referenced by the EU ambassador in the January 2026
EBRD/Tunisie Telecom announcement denotes this development-plan vision — **not** this 2020–2025 plan under
another name. *(Reconcile pass, 2026-07-20.)*

**Still unheld:** the text of the 2026-2030 development plan itself, and whether it had been formally
adopted by the Council of Ministers by mid-2026 — as of 6 January 2026 it was still a *projet* going
before the Council.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The frame for the national interoperability platform (as of 2024-02).** [[cybernetica]]
  described the [[uxp-tunisia]] project — the national interoperability platform for G2G, G2B and
  G2C service delivery — as "a significant step toward the National Strategic Plan 'Digital Tunisia
  2020-2025'", listing the plan's priorities as ICT governance, digital infrastructure,
  cybersecurity and data-driven government, with e-identity and interoperability as the foundational
  building blocks. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2024-02-14 National interoperability platform in Tunisia (Cybernetica UXP)]].
- **E-Houwiya as a cornerstone (as of 2025-03).** [[nizar-ben-neji]] presents [[e-houwiya]] —
  introduced **2 August 2022** — as "a cornerstone of Tunisia's 2025 digital strategy" and a
  milestone in the country's digital transformation roadmap. Principal testimony from the minister
  who led the programme, not third-party assessment. See [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-19 E-Houwiya - A Milestone in Tunisia's Digital Transformation Roadmap 2025 (ID Tech)]].
