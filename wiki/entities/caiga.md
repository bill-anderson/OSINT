# CAIGA (Continental Africa Internet Governance Architecture)

A proposed **[[smart-africa]]** framework for continental internet governance, developed in
partnership with [[icann]] under a 2024 MoU. Presented at ICANN84 Dublin as "two years of joint
work with ICANN"; the blueprint was reportedly drafted by a third party based in France.

## What CAIGA would do (per critics)

As described by [[alice-munyua]] in
[[2025-11-04 The Double Standard Why Are Internet Governance Rules Different for Africa]], CAIGA
would:

- Create a **paid membership structure** for internet-governance participation (replacing open
  community process).
- Let the **Smart Africa Heads-of-State Summit politically endorse [[afrinic]] governance
  reforms** if the AFRINIC membership does not adopt them.
- Install a **Permanent Secretary "provided by the Smart Africa Secretariat"**, creating dual
  reporting outside member oversight.
- Enable governments to make recommendations directly to AFRINIC's board, bypassing community
  policy development.

Munyua frames this as substituting political endorsement for member ratification — governmental
capture of a community-governed RIR, and a "double standard" applied to Africa that ICANN would
not apply to RIPE NCC, ARIN, APNIC or LACNIC. Presented at the Africa Internet Summit (Accra)
with reportedly 24 hours' notice.
