# eKash (Rwanda instant payment system)

Rwanda's national instant, interoperable retail payment system, powered by
**[[rswitch-rwanda]]** (RSwitch, the national payment switch) and **officially
launched on 5 December 2025**. eKash connects banks and mobile-money wallets
into a single interoperable rail, succeeding the bank-to-bank RIPPS as the
consumer-facing instant-payments layer of Rwanda's DPI (see
[[rwanda-dpi-strategy]]).

eKash is the **consumer brand of RNDPS 2.0** — the second generation of Rwanda's
National Digital Payment System. RNDPS 2.0 was **launched onstage on 27 Feb 2025** at
the Inclusive Fintech Forum (Minister [[paula-ingabire]], BNR Governor John Rwangombwa,
RSwitch, RISA), then given its official consumer launch in Dec 2025. Unlike RNDPS 1.0
(a different switch), **RNDPS 2.0 is built on [[mojaloop]]** — the open-source DPG
provides the core switching infrastructure — adding **P2M and P2B** payments and
prioritising **integration of SACCOs and MFIs**. Implementation was led by
**[[rswitch-rwanda]]** with Rwandan systems integrator **WiredIn** (a Mojaloop
Accelerator graduate) and support from the Mojaloop Foundation. Source:
[[2025-03-10 Powering Rwanda's Payments Future How Mojaloop Enables RNDPS 2.0 and eKash]].

## Full interoperability (effective 2026-07-14)

Rwanda declared eKash's interoperability programme **complete**, effective **14 July
2026** under **National Bank of Rwanda Directive No. 45/2026** ([[national-bank-of-rwanda]]).
From that date customers can move money **across any bank or e-money provider** on a single
rail — and pay merchants from any bank account or mobile wallet regardless of issuer —
subject to a **maximum fee of RWF 20** and a **RWF 10m per-transaction limit**. Access is via
existing USSD, mobile-banking apps and internet banking, with **no new app** required. This
turns eKash from launched (Dec 2025) to a fully interoperable national utility — the
account-to-account exchange layer of the [[rwanda-dpi-strategy]]. See [[dpi.exchange]].
Source: [[2026-07-13 Rwanda's eKash System Achieves Full Interoperability for Digital Payments]]
(low-authority aggregator; verify fee/limit against the BNR directive).

## Project-stated facts (as of 2025-12)

- **P2P** technically launched May 2022 (Airtel + MTN first): >30m transactions,
  >160bn RWF cumulatively.
- **P2M** technically launched Feb 2025: ~35,000 merchant payments, >2bn RWF
  (pre-official-launch figures).
- >20 financial institutions connected; Rwanda hosts >75 fintechs; 96% of
  Rwandan adults use at least one formal/informal financial product.
- **Partners:** Government of Rwanda (MINICT, [[rwanda-information-society-authority]]),
  **[[national-bank-of-rwanda]]** (BNR), **[[africanenda]]** (lead),
  **[[gates-foundation]]**, Access to Finance Rwanda, Mojaloop Foundation,
  WiredIn.
