# Angola Law 22/11 on the Protection of Personal Data (LPDP)

Angola's **omnibus data-protection law**, passed **17 June 2011** — one of the earlier such laws in Sub-Saharan Africa. Unlike many regional peers, it is now **actively enforced** by the **[[apd-angola]]** (APD).

## Key provisions & status

- Principles: transparency, lawfulness, proportionality, purpose, accuracy, retention limits. Processing requires **express consent + notification** to the authority; extra restrictions on sensitive (incl. genetic), credit/solvency, video-surveillance and call-recording data. Rights: access, object, rectify, update, delete. Penalties up to **$150,000**.
- Sits within a wider stack (per the CMS guide): Electronic Communications Law **23/11**, Network/IT Systems Protection **7/17**, video-surveillance **2/20**, mobile-surveillance **11/20**, the Criminal Code **38/20**, and **Resolution 33/19** ratifying the **AU Malabo Convention** on cybersecurity and data protection.
- **Enforced:** the APD (constituted 2016) issued **sanctions in June–July 2024**. A **draft amendment** to the DP Act is under way alongside the [[angola-ai-bill]]. See [[angola--gov-protect]].
