# National Payment Stack (NPS)

The National Payment Stack is Nigeria's unified next-generation payments infrastructure,
built and operated by [[nibss]]. Unveiled in 2025, it integrates **payments, identity and
data** into a single platform intended to modernise the country's [[dpi.pay]] rails and
position Nigeria as a regional digital-finance hub.

## Key facts

- **ISO 20022-compliant** messaging, adopted for data quality, regulatory transparency and
  global interoperability ([[gov.standards]]).
- Supports real-time single, bulk and merchant settlement; automated reconciliation and
  dispute management; request-to-pay and direct debit; and KYC using national identifiers —
  **BVN, TIN and RC Number**.
- **Multi-currency capability and cross-border potential**, framed as scaffolding for
  Nigeria's Digital Public Infrastructure and continental payments leadership
  ([[dpi.exchange]]).
- **First live transaction: 7 Nov 2025, 11:56 a.m.** — PalmPay → Wema Bank, processed in
  milliseconds with instant settlement. The Lagos unveiling drew officials from 20+ African
  central banks.

Sits under NIBSS/[[cbn]] governance; complements (rather than duplicates) the retail
[[papss]] rails at the continental level.
