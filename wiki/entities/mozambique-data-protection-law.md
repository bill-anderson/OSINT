# Mozambique Personal Data Protection Law (proposed)

Mozambique's comprehensive **draft Personal Data Protection Law**, reviewed by TechHive Advisory (September
2025). It aims to align the country with international standards and the **African Union Malabo Convention**
(ratified by Mozambique in 2019). Scope covers processing by public or private persons, physical and digital,
for economic and non-private purposes, with journalistic/artistic exemptions and national-security carve-outs;
principles mirror best practice (transparency, purpose limitation, minimisation, security, accountability).

The draft sets up a **two-tier** framework: a **National Council for the Protection of Personal Data (CNPD)**,
chaired by the Prime Minister, for strategic coordination; and a **National Authority (ANPD)** for day-to-day
enforcement — a role the law assigns to the **existing ICT Regulatory Authority** as an independent body.
Obligations include a lawful basis, restrictions on sensitive/children's/credit data, a mandatory Data
Protection Officer, strong data-subject rights, and a risk-based regime for international transfers (adequacy
or ANPD authorisation). It enters into force 90 days after publication, with implementing regulations due
within 180 days.
