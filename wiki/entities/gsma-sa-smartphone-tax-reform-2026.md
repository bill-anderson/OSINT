# GSMA — Accelerating Smartphone Adoption through Tax Reform: Evidence from South Africa (2026)

A **[[gsma]]** evidence report (© 2026 GSMA) measuring the impact of South Africa's removal
of the **9% ad valorem luxury excise duty** on entry-level smartphones. **Primary now held**
in `raw/`: `2026-07-01 GSMA Accelerating Smartphone Adoption Through Tax Reform - Evidence from
South Africa.pdf` (publication date not stated; `date_precision: month`, `date_source: proxy` —
analyses data through **Feb 2026** and was "recently published" as of the 2026-07-10 op-ed).

## The reform

- Effective **1 April 2025**, South Africa removed the **9% ad valorem (luxury) excise duty** on
  entry-level smartphones priced **below R2,500 (~USD 150)** — per the National Treasury's 2025
  Budget Review. Entry-level devices are framed as **essential, not luxury** goods, with the tax
  falling proportionally hardest on low-income buyers.

## Key findings (primary figures)

- **Trend break:** average monthly growth in entry-level smartphone units shifted from **−7.9%
  before** the reform to **+6.2% after** — reversing a sustained pre-reform decline (the R2,499
  segment had been the only smartphone band still falling).
- **Substitution to smartphones:** between **March 2025** (month before removal) and **February
  2026**, entry-level smartphone sales **rose 80%** while feature-phone sales **fell 87%**.
- **Value grew, not just volume:** total smartphone market value rose **+15%**, from **R2.7bn/mo**
  (Jul 2024–Mar 2025) to **R3.1bn/mo** (Apr 2025–Feb 2026) — affordability gain without lost
  market value.
- **Framing:** affordability is a multi-stakeholder problem (manufacturers, chipset suppliers,
  MNOs, distributors, financing, government), but governments are central via handset taxation;
  rising global memory/chipset prices are a headwind to device affordability and AI/5G readiness.

> **Note on the minister's numbers.** Minister **[[solly-malatsi]]**'s Sowetan op-ed
> ([[2026-07-10 SOLLY MALATSI  Removing smartphone tax means access to opportunity]]) cited
> "**+1.1m** entry-level units in 11 months" and monthly sales "**250k→460k** by February" — a
> rounder presentation directionally consistent with the report's growth-rate and 80%/87% figures
> above. Where they differ in precision, prefer the report's figures here.
