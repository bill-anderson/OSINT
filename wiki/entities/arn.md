# ARN-TIC (National Regulatory Authority for ICT, Guinea-Bissau)

Guinea-Bissau's telecommunications/ICT regulator — the *Autoridade Reguladora Nacional das
Tecnologias de Informação e Comunicação* — responsible for licensing operators and managing the
national telecom market. It is the licensing authority behind the country's satellite-broadband
entry and operates under a telecom legal regime that is being modernised away from the outdated
2010 law. See [[GNB]], [[infra.connect]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Starlink provisional licence (2025-04-10).** [[infra.connect]] ARN-TIC granted **[[starlink]]
  Guiné-Bissau** a general provisional authorization to operate satellite internet nationally,
  following an initial provisional licence in December 2024; framed around digital inclusion and
  connectivity in one of West Africa's least-connected states. Commercial launch followed in June
  2025. Sources: [[2025-04-10 ARN-TIC grants Starlink Guinea-Bissau provisional authorization]],
  [[2025-06-19 Starlink enters Guinea-Bissau]].
- **New Electronic Communications Law (transition-era, 2025/26).** [[gov.legislate]] The Council of
  Ministers (transitional presidency of Horta Inta-á) approved a new Electronic Communications Law
  establishing the legal regime for networks and services, revising the outdated 2010 telecom law —
  the statutory frame within which ARN-TIC regulates. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Guinea-Bissau Council of Ministers approves new Electronic Communications Law]].
