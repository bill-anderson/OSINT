# Cybercrime and Abuse of Computer Bill 2025 (South Sudan)

South Sudan's cybercrime law, **passed by parliament and awaiting presidential
assent** as of December 2025. Intended to work in tandem with the forthcoming
**[[south-sudan-data-protection-bill]]**.

## Status and activity

- **Passed, awaiting assent (2025-12).** ICT minister Ateny Wek Ateny presented
  the bill as one half of the cyberspace legal framework, to be completed by a
  data-protection act. Civil-society activist **Edmund Yakani** welcomed the
  legislation but warned it could be **ineffective or abused** without
  accompanying reforms, training and public awareness — flagging rising
  digital-facilitated gender-based violence, AI-manipulated images defeating
  courts, and coercive digital tactics by some security personnel. Source:
  [[2025-12-11 South Sudan to get data protection bill in 2026, says minister]].

## Sources

- [[2025-12-11 South Sudan to get data protection bill in 2026, says minister]]

**Enacted (2026).** Passed by the RNLA on **25 Nov 2025** and **signed by President Kiir on 18 Feb 2026** as the **Cyber Crimes and Computer Abuse Act 2026** (now in effect) — criminalising hacking, espionage, cyberterrorism, fake accounts, false information, impersonation, hate speech and GBV harassment; the full text was withheld from journalists at signing.
