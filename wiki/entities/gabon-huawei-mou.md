# Gabon–Huawei Digital Transformation MoU

**Parties:** the Government of the Republic of Gabon ([[GAB]]) and [[huawei]] Technologies
(Gabon). **What:** a Memorandum of Understanding to advance Gabon's national digital
transformation. **When/where:** signed **2025-12-23**; overseen by **Mark-Alexandre Doumba**,
Minister of Digital Economy, Digitalization and Innovation, and witnessed by Wang Lei,
Huawei CEO for Central Africa (CEMAC), following talks with President Brice Clotaire Oligui
Nguema. **Status:** active.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Three pillars.** (1) A joint project committee to identify and prioritise national
  digital projects; (2) integration of Gabonese startups and enterprises to strengthen local
  innovation; (3) skills-transfer programs to build national expertise and ensure **digital
  sovereignty**. Existing initiatives such as the **Connected Schools Program** are to be
  expanded from 2026, and new high-tech projects launched. Gabon frames the partnership as
  positioning itself as a Central African innovation leader. See [[finance.mou]],
  [[capacity.training]], [[geopol.china]], [[sovereignty]], [[colonialism]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-05 Gabon and Huawei Forge Partnership to Boost National Digital Transformation]].

- **Successor accord, narrower scope (2026-07-14).** A **separate, later** agreement —
  **[[gabon-huawei-kimba-connect-accord]]** — gives Huawei Gabon a technical-accompaniment role in the
  **[[kimba-connect]]** open-innovation programme (smart rooms; training in networks, cybersecurity, AI
  and software development; certifying kits). It operationalises this MoU's pillars (2) and (3) for the
  start-up ecosystem specifically, and does not supersede it. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-gabon-garantie-publique-huawei-start-up]].
