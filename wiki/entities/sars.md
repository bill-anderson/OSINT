# South African Revenue Service (SARS)

South Africa's national tax authority, led by Commissioner **Edward Kieswetter**. In
2026 it **crossed R2 trillion in annual revenue** for the first time, after roughly
seven years rebuilding institutional capacity lost during state capture. Beyond
revenue collection, SARS has become the lead driver of a whole-of-government digital-ID
push. See [[ZAF]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[sovereignty]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Single digital identity push (2026-04).** Through its **Modernisation 3.0**
  programme, SARS is spearheading a **whole-of-government single biometric-secured
  digital identity** to replace the **~7–8 separate identifiers each of ~62 million
  people** currently carries (ID number, tax number, VAT number, hospital number).
  Kieswetter frames the fragmentation as an opening for arbitrage, crime and fraud;
  the fix is to make the **population register the single identity**, ending the split
  between a Home Affairs ID number and a SARS tax number. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-04-03 Say goodbye to SARS tax numbers in South Africa]].
- **PKI stack inside SARS.** The digital-ID PKI infrastructure is being built within
  the **SARS hosting environment**, tying the national digital-ID build to the tax
  authority's systems — see [[sa-digital-id-regulations]] for the regulatory track.
- **Authentication modernisation.** SARS has deployed **biometric plus two-factor
  authentication** over the past ~18 months (extra layers for high-risk actions like
  refunds and bank-detail changes), moving off password/OTP login, with WhatsApp and
  feature-phone self-help for credential recovery.
- **Instant-payment system with SARB.** SARS is partnering with [[sarb]] to build a
  cost-effective **instant payment system** intended to widen financial inclusion,
  reduce cash in circulation and strengthen the national payments system. See
  [[dpi.pay]].
- **Border biometrics for the ETA (2026-02).** SARS is partnering the Border Management Authority
  (BMA) on expanded **facial recognition at international airports and major land-border posts** as
  part of the **[[south-africa-eta]]** rollout — the automated, biometrically-secured visa gateway
  whose PKI/platform stack sits inside the SARS hosting environment. See [[south-africa-eta]],
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-24 South Africa makes the ETA the cornerstone of its visa reform]].
