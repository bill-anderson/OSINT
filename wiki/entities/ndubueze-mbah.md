# Ndubueze Mbah

Professor; **Commissioner for Education, Enugu State, Nigeria (2026-07)**. The political owner of
**[[enugu-smart-green-schools]]**.

## Positions on record (2026-07-16, [[nigeria-education-supplies-summit|NESS 2026]])

- **Names four core problems** in Nigerian education: *"foundational learning, quality of learning
  access for children in schools, the gap in digital interest and the skills gap"* — which he
  links to the labour-market distortion facing graduates. Notably, he frames the digital problem
  as one of **interest**, not only access or infrastructure.
- **Describes the Enugu reform agenda as "gender inclusive [and] class inclusive"**, and its
  infrastructure programme as the practical expression of an "experiential learning system of
  innovation".
- **Sets the delivery standard as uniformity of access**: "that no matter where every child is,
  they have access to the standardised instruction and tuition" — urban or rural, powered by
  solar and connected by satellite plus local internet infrastructure.
- **Frames scale as the point**: *"Education sector transformation at scale and of this ambition
  becomes an avenue for catalysing inclusive prosperity that is transglobal."*

*Source caveat: these are remarks at a supplier-organised summit, carried by a sponsored TechCabal
Partner / Press Room placement. Recorded as stated positions of a serving commissioner, not as
verified programme facts.*
