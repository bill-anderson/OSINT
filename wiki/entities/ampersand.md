# Ampersand

**Electric-motorcycle and battery-swap operator in East Africa** — one of the two largest players in
African electric mobility, alongside **[[spiro]]**.

## Key facts (as reported 2026-07)

- **Over 4,000 electric motorcycles in Rwanda**; **more than 1,300 in Kenya**.
- **25 battery-swap stations** supporting the fleet.
- **Announced in August 2025** a plan to reach **13,000 motorcycles across East Africa by early 2026**
  — against the fleet figures above, that target appears **not to have been met**; the wiki holds no
  company statement reconciling the two.

## Why it sits in this wiki

Battery-swap networks are **energy infrastructure built by private operators to substitute for grid
and fuel-retail capacity** — a parallel to how mobile money substituted for branch banking. The
two-wheeler dominance of African EV deployment (see [[infra.energy]]) is the backdrop against which
[[gocab]]'s four-wheeler bet in Abidjan is the outlier.
