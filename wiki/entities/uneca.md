# UN Economic Commission for Africa (UNECA / ECA)

One of five UN regional commissions, mandated to promote the economic and social
development of Africa and its regional integration. Executive Secretary: **Claver Gatete**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **58th Conference of Ministers DPI roundtable (2026).** ECA's flagship ministerial
  session hosted a roundtable — "Empowering Africa's Future: Advancing Digital Public
  Infrastructure, Data Centres and Sovereignty" — arguing Africa must move from being a
  passive **consumer** of technology to a **primary architect** of DPI and sovereign data
  solutions, and beyond the "rail" of infrastructure toward the "train" of intelligence.
  See [[XAF]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].
- **Data-centre and sovereignty framing.** [[africa-data-centres]] CEO **Adil el Youssefi**
  ([[cassava-technologies]]) urged Africa to increase data-centre capacity **tenfold** and
  build local "AI factories," noting the continent generates **<1% of global data** despite
  ~20% of the world's population. Ministers pushed sovereign clouds, "data embassies" and
  governments acting as anchor clients. See [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].
- **Statistics and measurement.** Gatete stressed that technology's impact on GDP cannot be
  monetised if it cannot be measured, calling for well-funded national statistical systems;
  the session closed with a call to translate the **[[african-union]] AI Strategy** into
  funded local data-governance frameworks. ([[gsma]]'s Angela Wamola separately urged removal
  of duties on entry-level smartphones, noting ~1bn Africans remain offline despite mobile
  coverage.) See [[data.statistics]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].

- **Africa 2035 Digital Implementation Roadmap (WSIS Forum 2026).** At a WSIS+20 session
  (organised by [[aficta]] + [[nitda]]), UNECA (Afework Temtime) presented an "Africa 2035
  Digital Implementation Roadmap" — **9 thematic pillars, 15 priority indicators** and a unified
  reporting template — framed as the shift from digital declarations to measurable
  implementation. See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Africa urged to turn WSIS+20 commitments into measurable digital progress]].
