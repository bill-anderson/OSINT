# Telecommunications Regulatory Authority (ARE)

Mauritania's telecoms regulator (Autorité de Régulation). ARE enforces **mandatory biometric identification
of mobile subscribers**: under Mauritanian law, operators must biometrically identify subscribers nationwide,
conditioning the sale/activation of any SIM, USIM or eSIM on prior identification, and must protect
subscribers' personal data. In **October 2023** ARE ordered deactivation of SIM cards lacking biometric
identification; on **15 January 2026** it issued **formal notices to Mattel, Mauritel and Chinguitel** over
identification failures found in inspections, giving a two-month deadline before sanctions.

Sanctions escalate from formal notice to suspension/withdrawal of licences or frequency rights, plus financial
penalties up to **3% of annual turnover** (5% for repeat offences). The policy is framed as "national and
citizen security" amid rising mobile fraud.

ARE also runs market-structure and spectrum policy. In **January 2026** it launched an international
**satellite-internet licence tender** (two batches — GEO retail service and satellite-capacity sale) to widen
access where ~63% remained offline (2023); LEO providers such as **[[starlink|Starlink]]** are not yet
authorised for direct service but Starlink signalled a **2026 commercial launch**. In **April 2026** ARE named
**provisional 5G licence winners** — incumbents **[[mauritel]]**, **[[mattel]]**, **[[chinguitel]]** plus new
entrant **[[rimatel]]** (all four cleared the reserve price; process opened December 2025).
