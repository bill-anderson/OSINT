# Partner2Connect (P2C) Digital Coalition

[[itu]]-run multistakeholder coalition (launched 2021) mobilising commitments toward
**universal meaningful connectivity** and closing the digital divide. Pledgers self-deliver
via projects; ITU tracks progress. Champions: Amazon, e&, Google, IDB, Microsoft, ZTE.

## Activity in this wiki

- **$100bn milestone (2026-07, Geneva Digital Week / WSIS Forum 2026).** Passed **USD 100
  billion** in cumulative pledges (up from $82bn). Africa-relevant new pledges:
  - **[[microsoft]]** — connect **450+ rural/underserved community hubs in Kenya**
    ([[KEN]]) via satellite-enabled infrastructure integrated with **Azure Space**.
  - **[[gsma]]** — **African AI Language Models** project (AI reflecting African languages,
    cultures and knowledge). See [[tech.ai]].
  - **Telkom** — **USD 6.1m Telkom AI Institute** to build South African ([[ZAF]]) AI/digital
    skills. See [[capacity.training]].
  - Broader pledges: ADB Asia-Pacific Digital Highway ($20bn), BCG ($500m AI-for-impact),
    ZTE ($450m), SoftBank (AI-RAN).

  See [[infra.connect]], [[include.divides]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 ITU's Partner2Connect crosses $100 billion in global connectivity pledges]].
- **Corroboration (ITWeb, same event).** ITWeb reports the same >$100bn WSIS Forum 2026
  milestone, adding ITU's framing that **~25% of the world remains offline** and that
  universal connectivity would cost **~USD 2.6–2.8tn by 2030**. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Africa benefits from connectivity milestone]].
