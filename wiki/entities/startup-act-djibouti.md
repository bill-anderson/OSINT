# Djibouti Startup Act

Djibouti's dedicated **startup law** — a state-conferred "Startup" **label**, a **fund-of-funds**, a
**regulatory sandbox** and a crowdfunding regime — positioning Djibouti alongside the wave of African
startup acts. **The gazetted primary text is now held**, so the provisions below are read from the law
itself rather than from trade-press reporting. Source:
[[2026-01-08-djibouti-loi-179-an-25-startup-act]].

## Enactment chain

Five distinct dates, kept apart:

- **Conseil des Ministres — 15 April 2025.**
- **Adopted by the Assemblée Nationale — 12 November 2025**, in its second public sitting. (The bill had
  been presented to the Assembly on **9 November 2025** by the Minister Delegate for the Digital Economy
  and Innovation — [[2025-11-14-startup-act-djibouti-wearetech]].)
- **Signed at Djibouti — 1 December 2025** by the President of the Republic.
- **Journal Officiel édition n°23 — dated 15 December 2025.**
- **eJO publication — 8 January 2026** (the `published` date of the held source).

**Art. 35: the law takes effect from its promulgation** — so commencement attaches to the December 2025
signature, not to the January 2026 electronic publication.

## What the law does

- **Definition of a startup (art. 2)** — seven cumulative criteria: a Djiboutian-law company **under 7
  years old**; significant innovation in product, service, business model or operating method; that
  innovation resting substantially on **new or advanced technologies**; **more than half the share
  capital** held by natural persons (Djiboutian or foreign) and/or seed-investment entities; **no
  dividend distribution** for the whole labelling period; a demonstrable rapid-scaling business model;
  and possession of the label itself.
- **The label and its governance (arts. 3–6)** — a **Collège des Startups** sits with the ministry
  responsible for the digital economy and administers award, suspension and withdrawal, assisted by a
  technical committee. The label is awarded **by arrêté on the Minister's proposal after the Collège's
  binding opinion** (*avis conforme*); the technical-committee role may be delegated by convention, but
  **award itself may not be delegated**. Withdrawal (art. 6) follows a recorded finding and a hearing of
  the legal representative — whose absence does not stop the procedure.
- **"Startup Djibouti" national portal (art. 5)** — a single digital portal centralising administrative
  formalities, labelling applications, support-scheme candidacies and ecosystem resources. See
  [[dpi.govtech]].
- **Fiscal and customs incentives (Titre III)** and **market-access guarantees (Titre IV)**.
- **Fund-of-funds (arts. 25–28)** — finances startups across the cycle from ideation to expansion
  (incubation, acceleration, pre-seed, seed and later rounds) and **invests in seed and venture funds
  that establish themselves in Djibouti**. Board of public and private representatives appointed by
  decree for renewable three-year terms; resourced by the State, technical and financial partners and
  donors; **operational management tendered to a management company under the public-procurement code**,
  bound to professional investment, transparency and audit standards.
- **BSA-Air instrument (art. 23)** — a convertible subscription-warrant vehicle (*Bon de Souscription
  d'Actions avec Accord d'investissement Rapide*) converting on a contractually defined later event.
- **Crowdfunding (arts. 29–30)** — participatory finance by donation, loan or equity.
- **Regulatory sandbox (arts. 31–32)** — labelled startups may test innovative products, services or
  business models under a time-limited, supervised regime with **relaxed conditions set by the
  commission**, which sits under the **Haut Conseil Supérieur de l'Économie Numérique et de
  l'Innovation**. Applications run **exclusively through the Startup Djibouti platform**. The commission
  files a final report whose recommendations may feed future legislative or regulatory change.
- **Monitoring and evaluation (art. 33)** — a standing evaluation process for the Act itself, under the
  ministry responsible for the digital economy.
- **Repeal (art. 34)** — removes the reference to "Startup" in art. 16 of Finance Law n°69/AN/19/8ème L
  of 31 December 2019 and its **Class-6 patente liability of 288,000 Djiboutian francs**.

**Implementation rests on decrees not yet held.** The Act defers to *décret pris en Conseil des Ministres*
on nearly every operative detail — the Collège's composition and labelling procedure, the portal's
operating rules, the scaling criteria, the fund's instruments and reporting obligations, and the
sandbox's terms. **As of 2026-07-20 none of those implementing decrees is held by this wiki**, so the
scheme's actual operation — how many labels, on what tests — cannot be stated.
