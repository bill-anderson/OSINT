# Universal DPI Safeguards Framework

A global principles framework (UN / development-partner led) for building **safeguards into
the design and deployment of digital public infrastructure** — guiding governments to make
DPI fair, inclusive and safe from the outset, preventing exclusion (e.g. of people lacking
documentation, with disabilities, or in hard-to-reach areas) and strengthening protections
over how personal data is collected, stored and used.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Applied in Lesotho's NISSA social-registry redesign (2026-01).** Lesotho ([[LSO]]) is
  applying the framework as it redesigns the **[[nissa-lesotho]]** National Information System
  for Social Assistance — a registry holding data on >90% of households — around a
  **children's-rights-centred** approach, with support from **UNICEF** and the **Co-Develop
  Fund**. The safeguards aim to keep the system efficient without being exclusionary, and
  secure without being inaccessible, and to protect children's data; Lesotho is using it to
  develop its own local safeguards framework. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.standards]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-16 Lesotho's Digital Leap How Child-Centred Systems are Reshaping Social Protection]].
