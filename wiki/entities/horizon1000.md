# Horizon1000

A **$50m** pilot programme announced **2026-01-22** by the **[[gates-foundation]]** and
**[[openai]]** to help African governments integrate AI into national health systems,
combining funding, technology and technical support to strengthen primary-healthcare
delivery. Implementation **begins in Rwanda**, with plans to scale to **1,000 primary
healthcare clinics** and surrounding communities across multiple countries **by 2028**.

## Profile

- Targets Sub-Saharan Africa's estimated **5.6 million** health-workforce shortage, using AI
  to augment clinical capacity, streamline workflows and extend patient reach.
- Endorsed by Rwanda's ICT & Innovation Minister **[[paula-ingabire]]**, who stressed
  purpose-driven, values-led, people-centred deployment.
- **Distinct** from the [[gates-anthropic-partnership]] — a sibling in the Gates health-AI
  thread but with a different technology partner (OpenAI, not Anthropic). Data Landscapers
  has separately published a critique of the pilot (see [[RWA]]).
- **Cited as a "philanthropic capture" case (Chinasa Okolo, 2026-02-17).** In her
  op-ed warning that corporate and philanthropic interests could co-opt Africa's
  AI governance, Okolo names the OpenAI–Gates "Horizons1000" Rwanda initiative
  (alongside the OpenAI Academy at Lagos) as an example of foreign actors shaping
  national AI agendas. Attributed as the author's argument, not a wiki judgement.
  See [[geopol.usa]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-17 Corporate Interests Must Not Co-Opt Africa's AI Moment]].
