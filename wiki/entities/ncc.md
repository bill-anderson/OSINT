# Nigerian Communications Commission (NCC)

Nigeria's telecommunications regulator (Governing Board Chairman **Idris Ibikunle
Olorunnimbe**). Recurs across the Nigeria digital-economy thread; hubbed here as it becomes
a named party to a governance action.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Record fibre-cut surge (2026-07, NCC data).** NCC data show **155,397 fibre-cut incidents** in
  **Apr–May 2026** (74,276 → a record **79,121**), up **2,428%** on Q1 — **vandalism** the leading cause
  (>54,000) despite the **Critical National Information Infrastructure** designation (penalties to 10 yrs);
  road works/faults/degradation added >84,000. NCC is developing a **cost-based shared-duct framework**, and
  operators are trialling **AI fibre-sensing**; the **Dig-Once** policy is not yet widely adopted. ~35,000 km
  of fibre deployed under the National Broadband Plan. See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-15 Nigeria records surge in fibre cuts (NCC)]].
- **Regulatory harmonisation committee (2026-07).** Named — with [[nitda]] and [[ndpc]] —
  to the joint technical coordination committee established by Minister [[bosun-tijani]] to
  develop a unified national digital-economy policy and governance framework, while
  maintaining the existing regulatory framework in the interim. See [[gov.policy]],
  [[nigeria--gov-protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-08 FG Halts Enforcement of New Regulations on Internet Platforms]].
- **National Telecommunications Policy rewrite (2026-05).** The NCC convened a
  policy-review workshop in Lagos to rewrite Nigeria's **National Telecommunications
  Policy for the first time since 2000**, as stakeholders warned that no hyperscaler runs a
  full cloud region in Nigeria — investment is flowing to [[KEN]] and [[ZAF]] instead —
  amid high infrastructure/import-duty costs, unreliable power (a ~20 MW bitcoin project
  decamped to [[ETH]]) and multiple taxation. EVC **Aminu Maida** cast telecoms as
  "productivity infrastructure for the entire economy." See [[gov.policy]], [[infra.connect]].
  Source: [[2026-05-22 Why global hyperscalers are avoiding Nigeria despite Africa's biggest market]].
  Russell Southwood (2026-06-25) corroborates: the NCC (founded 1992) announced a **May 2026**
  review of its **26-year-old legal/regulatory framework** — his lead example of first-generation
  liberalisation-era regulators needing to repurpose around digital infrastructure. Source:
  [[2026-06-25 Regulating for Africa's future digital economy – A draft agenda to speed things up]].
- **Tower rollout / NUCAP (2026-06).** The NCC said operators have pledged to upgrade
  **12,000 network sites in 2026** (migrating legacy 2G/3G to 4G/5G), alongside the
  [[nucap]] tower programme backed by China Industrial Bank. See [[infra.connect]].
  Source: [[2026-06-04 Chinese bank backs Nigeria towers project]].
- **Amazon Leo landing permit (2026-01-15).** The NCC granted **[[amazon-leo]]** (formerly
  Project Kuiper) a **7-year landing permit** (effective 28 Feb 2026; authorising FSS, MSS and
  ESIM services), ending [[starlink]]'s sole-LEO position in Nigeria and putting two US
  hyperscaler LEO players in the market. See [[infra.connect]], [[geopol.usa]],
  [[tech.industry]]. Source: [[2026-01-15 Amazon Leo secures landing permit in Nigeria]].
- **Spacecoin (2026-01).** [[spacecoin]] reported continued satellite work under its existing
  NCC licence, alongside a new Kenyan transmission licence. See [[data.satellite]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-13 Spacecoin strikes major deals to launch satellite internet]].
- **SIM–NIN linkage background (2025-02).** In its 2020–2024 SIM-registration campaign the
  NCC claimed (Oct 2024) that all active SIM cards were linked to the NIN; the drive later
  drew a Tinubu-ordered probe into unauthorised NIN–SIM linkages. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-13 Digital ID necessity increases in Nigeria despite lingering challenges]].
