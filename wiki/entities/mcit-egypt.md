# Ministry of Communications and Information Technology (MCIT, Egypt)

Egypt's lead ministry for the digital economy and e-government, owner of the **[[digital-egypt|Digital Egypt]]** strategy and the **ICT 2030** agenda. **Minister: Amr Talaat** (named across sources through early 2026); the **June-2026 Senate AI session was led by Communications Minister Raafat Hindi**, indicating a portfolio handover in H1 2026. Oversees a cluster of bodies: **[[ntra-egypt|NTRA]]**, **[[itida-egypt|ITIDA]]**, Egypt Post, ITI and NTI. Reported the ICT sector at **5.1% of GDP** (FY2022/23, +15.2% growth) and digital exports of **$7.4bn**. Drives 5G rollout (June 2025), the "Decent Life" rural-fibre programme, six new technology parks, the **[[egypt-ai-strategy|national AI strategy]]**, and the DPI/GovTech build-out (hosted the 2024 Global DPI Summit).

## Activity in this wiki

- **World Bank cooperation deepened (2026-07-17, Cairo).** ICT minister **Raafat Hendy** met
  **Paschal Donohoe**, [[world-bank|World Bank Group]] managing director and chief knowledge
  officer, agreeing **digital skills, AI and innovation** as priority areas alongside digital
  infrastructure. MCIT presented its flagship projects — strengthening DPI, expanding high-speed
  internet and upgrading telecoms in **[[decent-life-initiative|Decent Life]]** villages — and stated
  it has taken **fibre to more than 1,250 rural villages (as of 2026-07)**. Stated forward plans:
  more public-private partnerships, **more data-centre investment**, stronger **data governance**
  and improved **children's online safety**. Donohoe's framing — *"knowledge is now as important as
  financing"* — pitched the Bank's role as **expertise in data governance, regulation and
  institutional development** rather than lending. Attending: **Ahmed Elzaher** (CEO,
  [[itida-egypt|ITIDA]]), **Mahmoud Badawi**, **Hoda Baraka**, **Samah Aziz**, **Mahmoud Sofrata**.
  See [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.training]], [[egypt--infra-connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-egypt-deepens-world-bank-digital-partnership]].

  *No agreement, instrument or financing figure is reported — this is a cooperation meeting, and
  should not be read as a commitment. Note also the minister's name is rendered **Raafat Hendy**
  here and **Raafat Hindi** in the June-2026 Senate reporting — transliteration drift, same person.*
