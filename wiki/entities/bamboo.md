# Bamboo

Nigerian digital investment platform giving retail users phone-based access to US and Nigerian
equities. Co-founded and led by **[[richmond-bassey]]** (co-founder and CEO).

## Position

- **Added Nigerian equities to the platform in May 2024**, having started with US stocks.
- **Reported overtaking CardinalStone to become the [[nigerian-exchange|NGX]]'s largest broker by
  weighted market share in April 2026**, executing **542,582 trades** that month for an **11.13%
  market share** — company figures, as reported 2026-07-18.
- Says **much of its customer base is aged 18–34** (as of 2026-07-18).

The broker-league claim is the substantive fact: an app-first entrant displacing an incumbent
brokerage at the top of a national exchange's league table within two years of listing local equities.
It is the company's own figure and is not independently confirmed in the held source.

## Bassey's reading of the market

Bassey dates the retail surge tightly — *"the retail growth of the market has happened in the last 18
months"* (i.e. from roughly early 2025) — and argues comparisons with institutional volume mislead,
since "institutional capital has been building in the market for decades". He identifies **registrar
practice, not brokerage or exchange technology, as the binding retail frustration**: unpaid or late
dividends, often caused by inconsistencies in how shareholders' names are recorded, with complaints
landing on brokers who cannot fix them.
