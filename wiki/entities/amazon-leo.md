# Amazon Leo

Amazon Leo is Amazon's low-Earth-orbit (LEO) satellite broadband constellation, **formerly
known as Project Kuiper**. It is a distinct object from [[amazon-web-services]] and the
[[amazon-globalstar-acquisition]]. Amazon Leo plans a global constellation of up to **3,236
satellites** and is emerging as a direct rival to SpaceX's [[starlink]] in African markets.

## What matters now

- **Nigeria landing permit (2026).** The [[ncc]] granted Amazon Leo a **seven-year landing
  permit, effective 28 Feb 2026**, authorising fixed satellite services (FSS), mobile
  satellite services (MSS) and mobile earth stations in motion (ESIM) over Nigeria. Amazon Leo
  will deliver **100 MHz per channel** with standard terminals engineered for **up to 400
  Mbit/s**. The permit ends Starlink's sole-LEO position in Nigeria (Starlink had >66,000 NG
  subscribers at end-2025). See [[NGA]], [[geopol.usa]], [[infra.connect]].

Two US hyperscaler-linked LEO players are now licensed in Nigeria, a notable
[[geopol.usa]] datapoint. The permit is a regulatory authorisation, not a commercial deal
entity.
