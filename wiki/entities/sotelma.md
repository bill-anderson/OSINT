# SOTELMA (Moov Africa Malitel)

Mali's **second telecom operator** (Société des télécommunications du Mali), trading as
**Moov Africa Malitel**; had ~8.5m subscribers (H1 2024, ~37% share) against market leader
**[[orange-mali]]** (~54.9%). Part of the **[[maroc-telecom]]** / Moov Africa footprint until
the 2026 ownership reversal that returned it to majority state control.

## Activity in this wiki

- **State regains majority control (2026-06).** See **[[sotelma-state-majority-2026]]** — a
  **160 billion FCFA (~$277m)** deal raised the state's stake from **49% to 56%**, reducing
  **[[maroc-telecom]]** to a minority shareholder and renewing SOTELMA's licence (reportedly to
  2039), **17 years after** the 2009 privatisation (Maroc Telecom had bought 51% for ~€275m).
  The transition government framed it as a win for **economic sovereignty** over strategic
  assets, mirroring AES asset-reclaim moves in mining/energy. See [[tech.industry]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[sovereignty]], [[MLI]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-25-businessinsider-mali-regains-sotelma-majority-stake]].
- **Fibre doubling + AI assistant MIA (2026-06-12).** Doubled fibre-plan speeds at no extra
  cost (up to 100 Mbps in Bamako, Kayes, Sikasso, Ségou, Mopti, Koulikoro) and launched **MIA**
  (Moov Intelligence Artificielle), an AI digital assistant — intensifying fixed-broadband
  competition with Orange Mali (Mali had 8.91m internet users, 35.1% penetration, early 2026).
  See [[infra.connect]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-15-ecofin-moov-africa-doubles-fibre-ai-assistant-mia]].
- **Moov Money–Mastercard prepaid card (2026-04, GITEX Africa).** Moov Money Mali launched a
  co-branded prepaid Mastercard linked to the mobile wallet, targeting the unbanked (online
  payments, ATM withdrawals, merchant settlement, no bank account) — the second telco-led card
  after Orange Money's. See [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-09-le360-moov-money-mali-carte-prepayee-mastercard-gitex]].
