# Seychelles Civil Status Act (Cap 34)

**The statute underpinning civil registration in Seychelles** — the legal basis for the register
of births, marriages and deaths, and therefore the legal foundation beneath the [[certexpress]]
online certificate service operated by the
[[department-of-immigration-and-civil-status-seychelles]]. **Originally 1893**; the text held
here is the **consolidated version as at 2022-11-01**. See [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.legislate]],
[[SYC]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Held as a consolidated text (as at 2022-11-01).** This wiki holds the consolidated statute as
  a PDF artefact with a companion source page. **Its provisions are not summarised here** — no
  source in this wiki analyses the Act's sections, and none should be inferred. Source:
  [[2022-11-01 Seychelles Civil Status Act Cap 34 (as at 1 Nov 2022)]].
- **Why it matters: the digital service rests on a 19th-century statute.** CertExpress delivers
  birth, marriage and death certificates online (2 working days; SCR 50 as announced 2024-09) into
  the E-Doc wallet of the [[seyid]] app — a fully digital channel onto a register constituted by
  an **1893** Act, consolidated but not, on the evidence held here, rewritten for digital
  issuance. Whether the Act's provisions on certified copies, signatures and registrar functions
  accommodate app-delivered certificates is **not addressed by any source held here** — an open
  question, not a finding. See [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]].
- **Continued legal reform is signalled but unlinked.** President [[wavel-ramkalawan]] announced
  "upcoming legislative reforms" to support modernised operations at the
  [[registrar-general-office-seychelles]] on **2025-02-07** — but that concerns the business/IP
  registry, and **no source held here connects those reforms to the Civil Status Act**. Source:
  [[2025-02-10 Seychelles Pledges to Modernize Public Services Through Digital Transformation]].
