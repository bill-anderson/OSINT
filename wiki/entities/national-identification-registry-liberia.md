# National Identification Registry (NIR, Liberia)

Liberia's identity authority, created by a **2011 Act of the Legislature**, operating the **National
Biometric Identification System (NBIS)** and issuing the unique **National Identification Number (NIN)**
and biometric ID card ([[dpi.id]]). Executive Director (2025-26): **Andrew Peters**. The NBIS is the
cornerstone of Liberia's Digital Transformation Initiative and the [[great-project-liberia|GREAT]]
project, but coverage is very low (~14-16% of ~5.5m enrolled) and the system entered **crisis** in 2025
— long queues and bribery allegations, then a full suspension over a **procurement scandal**. See
[[LBR]], [[liberia--dpi-id]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Five-Year Strategic Plan 2026-2030 (2026-01).** [[gov.policy]] "No ID, No Inclusion" — six pillars
  (universal coverage, governance/capacity, digital-ID modernization, infrastructure, legal/
  data-protection, financial sustainability), aligned to the ARREST Agenda. Source:
  [[2026-01-14 'No ID, No Inclusion' — NIR launches Five-Year Strategic Plan 2026-2030]].
- **Mandatory enrollment — Executive Order 147 (2025-04).** [[gov.legislate]] President Boakai signed
  EO 147 mandating NBIS enrollment for all citizens and 90-day+ residents; the NIN becomes the primary ID
  for public and private transactions (SIM, banking, health, education). Sources:
  [[2025-04-30 Liberia makes biometric ID mandatory — Executive Order 147]],
  [[2025-03-12 Liberia says national ID system drives digital economy, facilitates service delivery]].
- **Procurement crisis + suspension (2025-06→08).** [[gov.protect]] Bribery/"express service"
  allegations at overwhelmed centres; the NIR sought to award a major biometric contract to Austria's
  **OSD International** by restricted bidding ($9.8m-$44m), which the **PPCC** rejected (7 Jul 2025,
  requiring competitive bidding). The CBL and NIR then **suspended new biometric ID issuance** —
  effectively halting EO 147. Sources:
  [[2025-08-14 Liberia suspends biometric ID contract amid procurement controversy]],
  [[2025-06-23 Broken ID system, bribes fuel public outrage and legislative probe]].
- **Coverage + funding gap; free-ID debate (2025-06→09).** [[include.access]] Only ~14-16% enrolled
  (740,000 as of Dec 2024); the $5 fee is a barrier; lawmakers urge free issuance, and ED Peters agrees
  "if the government shoulders the cost." A UNICEF child-enrollment pilot captured ~5,000 children.
  Sources: [[2025-06-09 Liberia needs more funding to widen national ID coverage (NIR's Peters)]],
  [[2025-09-26 Liberian lawmakers urge free national ID cards; registry chief backs proposal]].
