# UGHub

**Uganda's government data-exchange and integration platform**, operated by
[[nita-uganda]] and positioned as a digital public good — the layer through which
ministries, departments and agencies, and licensed private institutions, consume each
other's services. It is the spine of Uganda's interoperability story and the reason
[[dpi.exchange]] is an active topic on the [[UGA]] hub.

## Activity in this wiki

- **DIAL case study (2024-06-28).** [[dial]]'s own primary research makes UGHub the
  worked example of an integrated national data exchange. As of **2024-06**, UGHub was
  **utilized by over 100 government ministries, departments and agencies** as well as
  private entities including **banks, insurance providers and fintech firms**. It has
  **reduced wait times from days to hours** in many instances, particularly for
  **passport applications, work permit applications and vehicle registrations**.
  Challenges recorded: **financial sustainability, slow uptake and connectivity gaps**.
  DIAL's learnings: trust among government stakeholders is decisive for adoption;
  financial sustainability remains a concern for UGHub as for national data-exchange
  platforms generally, and governments should explore business models built on
  open-source software; and investment in policy and institutional change could
  significantly raise UGHub's value. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2024-06-28 Integrated National Data Exchange Systems - Uganda Case Study]].
- **NIRA as anchor data provider (undated deck, `date_source: proxy`).** UGHub is
  described as Uganda's government data and integration platform, with [[nira-uganda]]
  among its largest data providers — NIRA's services used by **90 of 135 entities** on the
  platform. Companion to [[ugpass]] for authentication. *The deck is undated and dated to
  the ingest date as a proxy — do not treat these figures as current-dated.* Source:
  [[2024-05-21 A Collaborative Approach to Digital Identity and Digital Transformation (NIRA and NITA-U)]].
