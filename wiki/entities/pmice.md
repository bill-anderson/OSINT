# PMICE (Projet de Modernisation des Infrastructures de Connectivité de l'État)

**Chad's State Connectivity Infrastructure Modernization Project** — the build programme behind
the [[chad-national-data-centre]] and a national fibre and mobile-network expansion. **Phase I is
a €175.1 million (US$204.7m) investment** by the government of [[TCD]], announced as commencing
**20 December 2025** in a Ministry post to Facebook, with an inauguration ceremony held in
**Sarh, Moyen-Chari**, in December 2025.

**PMICE is a distinct financing from both [[tchad-connexion-2030]] (the US$1.5bn national
umbrella strategy) and the [[chad-digital-transformation-project]] (the World Bank's US$120m
operation). No source held states the relationship between PMICE and Tchad Connexion 2030** —
they are not asserted here to nest.

## Phase I scope

- A **National Data Center** (size and location undisclosed as of 2026-01-06).
- **1,200 km (745 miles)** of fibre optic cabling.
- Expansion of wireless networks, including **GSM, 3G and 4G** sites across the country.

Source: [[2026-01-06 Chad invests 175m in national data center and networks]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **The lender is China (resolved 2026-07-20).** PMICE is financed by the **Export-Import Bank of
  China** under a concessional loan of **~111.4 billion FCFA, repayable over 20 years at 2%
  interest**, whose ratifying bill passed Chad's National Assembly as reported **2020-05-08**. It
  follows the **Chad–China framework and economic-cooperation agreement signed 26 June 2019 and
  ratified 20 August 2019**. The loan's stated scope — national data centre, **200 sites** for
  [[sotel-tchad]], **1,200 km** of fibre transmission — is PMICE Phase I. The project was formally
  launched with a foundation-stone ceremony in N'Djamena on **7 July 2020**. Sources:
  [[2020-05-08-ciomag-tchad-pret-eximbank-chine-tic]],
  [[2020-07-08-magazinedelafrique-tchad-lancement-pmice-financement-chine]]. See [[geopol.china]],
  [[finance.new]].
- **Built by Huawei.** **[[huawei]] Technologies** is the contractor and **GOLF/Gulf Consulting** the
  supervising engineer **(2025-01-10)**. Chinese ambassador **Wang Xining** attended the January 2025
  site visit and asked pointedly *"how to take ownership of this building and its machines, even in
  the absence of Chinese technicians"* — the dependency question, raised by the lender itself.
  Source: [[2025-01-10-agenceecofin-tchad-centre-donnees-national-finalisation]].
- **Repayment under way.** Minister **Haliki Choua Mahamat** reminded project stakeholders on
  **19 May 2026** that Chad **had already begun repaying** the PMICE financing, while the works
  remained incomplete and behind schedule.
- **Costing, dated — supersession, not contradiction.** A **2023** [[world-bank]] document put PMICE
  at **$180 million** financed by "Eximbank" (unnamed); the announced Phase I package **(2025-12)** is
  **€175.1m (US$204.7m)**. The **euro-denominated total is stable across six years** — ~114bn FCFA
  (**~€174m**) at launch **(2020-07)**, **€175.1m** at Phase I **(2025-12)** — and the divergent USD
  figures reflect exchange-rate movement, not a cost change. Note also that the **loan (111.4bn FCFA)
  is smaller than the project total (114bn FCFA)**; they are not the same number. The 2023 document
  also referenced an **Internet Exchange Point** and the **EU-funded Trans-Saharan Corridor Project**
  (see [[trans-saharan-fibre-backbone]]). See [[finance.new]], [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-06 Chad invests 175m in national data center and networks]].
- **Name, unsettled.** Rendered both as *Projet de Modernisation des Infrastructures de
  **Connectivité de l'État*** and *…des **Communications Électroniques***. The **2020 launch coverage
  and the ministry's own 2026 meeting readouts use "Communications Électroniques"**; "Connectivité de
  l'État" appears to be a later trade-press variant. Recorded, not resolved.
- **Framed as territorial justice and sovereignty.** At the Sarh ceremony, Minister
  [[boukar-michel]] said: *"Digital must not stop at capitals, it must irrigate territories,
  connect regions, and unite the nation,"* and *"The sovereignty of a modern state is also
  measured by its ability to master its digital infrastructures."* He called fibre optic networks
  a national security issue as well as *"a tool of modern governance and a lever for territorial
  justice."* The choice of Sarh — not N'Djamena — for the ceremony reads with that framing. See
  [[digital.rural]], [[sovereignty]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-06 Chad invests 175m in national data center and networks]].
