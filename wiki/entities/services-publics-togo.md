# Services Publics (Guichet National du Service Public, Togo)

**Togo's national public-services portal — the single counter through which administrative
procedures are moved online.** As of December 2025 it carries civil-registry requests in five
pilot communes and a growing catalogue of ministerial procedures. Ministers associated with it:
**Hodabalo Awate** (Territorial Administration) and **[[cina-lawson]]** (Digital
Transformation). See [[TGO]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Civil-status acts online (announced 2025-12-15).** Citizens can request copies of **civil
  status acts** — **birth, marriage and death certificates** — directly online and **track
  processing in real time**. The pilot covers **five communes: Agoè-Nyivé 1, Golfe 1, Ave 2, Zio
  2 and Lacs 1**. An **online chat tool** on the national portal assists users through each step,
  and the platform includes **a secure interface designed to protect the confidentiality of
  personal data**. The civil-status services of **Golfe 3** were already available on the portal
  before this rollout. See [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.localgov]], [[gov.protect]]. Sources:
  [[2025-12-16 Digitalisation - civil status act requests now possible online]],
  [[2025-12-17 Togo Expands Public Service Digitisation With Online Civil Registry Platform]].
- **Ministerial procedures onboarding (2025-12).** The **Ministry of Energy** recently added
  **29 administrative procedures** to the national portal, covering services provided by
  **[[ceet]], AT2ER and ARSE**. **Other ministerial departments were expected to join from
  January 2026**, and the two ministers reaffirmed a commitment to extend civil-status service
  **gradually to all municipalities nationwide**, aiming to improve administrative efficiency
  while reducing costs for users. See [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-17 Togo Expands Public Service Digitisation With Online Civil Registry Platform]].

**Coverage note.** Both accounts describe a **pilot** — five communes out of Togo's
municipalities — and neither reports usage volumes, transaction counts or a completion date for
the nationwide extension. Reach should not be inferred from the commitment to extend.

**Provenance caveat — a thin evidentiary base.** Togo First supplies **9 of the 19 Togo clips**
in this batch, and is the **sole source for most of Togo's current-state picture** held in this
wiki. The civil-status rollout is the exception that proves the point: it is one of the few
Togolese developments carried by **two independent outlets** (République Togolaise / Togo Officiel
on 2025-12-16 and Togo First on 2025-12-17), and even then both are Lomé-based and one is the
state portal. Single-outlet dependence is a live limitation on confidence in Togolese
current-state claims across these pages, not a defect of this rollout in particular.
