# Amazon–Globalstar acquisition (proposed)

- **Parties:** [[amazon-web-services]]/Amazon (acquirer) and [[globalstar]] (target).
- **What:** Proposed acquisition of the US satellite-communications firm, structured around
  its spectrum rights and existing satellite infrastructure.
- **Amount:** ~**$9 billion**.
- **When:** Reported in **advanced negotiations as of 2026-04-14** (per the Financial Times),
  described as "complex and not yet finalised."
- **Rationale:** Fast-track Amazon's low-Earth-orbit **Project Kuiper** (now branded "Leo")
  to challenge [[starlink]]'s dominance in fast-growing African satellite-internet markets.
  Kuiper had just over **180 satellites** in orbit versus Starlink's **7,000+** — Globalstar's
  spectrum and African partnerships ([[ZAF]], [[RWA]], [[MOZ]], [[GAB]]) would offer a
  regulatory shortcut.
- **Terms / sticking point:** **Apple's ~20% stake** in Globalstar is the reported major
  complication.
- **Status:** **Not finalised** (as of 2026-04-14). The framing that the deal will "benefit
  Africa" is speculative reporting, not a confirmed commitment.
