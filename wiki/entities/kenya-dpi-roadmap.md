# Kenya DPI Roadmap

Kenya's **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) roadmap** — a cross-government blueprint to consolidate
fragmented systems into coherent national infrastructure unifying digital identity, payments, data
exchange and trusted services ([[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]]). Grounded in the **GovStack**
methodology, developed via an Inter-Ministerial Steering Committee (with GIZ, ITU, EU), building on
Kenya's 20,000+ eCitizen e-services and existing assets (**[[maisha-namba]]**, M-Pesa). It sits within
the **[[kenya-national-digital-masterplan|National Digital Masterplan 2022-2032]]** and follows the
National AI Strategy 2025-2030. See [[KEN]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launch + five pillars (2025).** Inclusivity, trust, innovation, interoperability, sustainability;
  assesses five domains (data infrastructure, interoperability, digital identity/trust, access,
  governance), drawing on Estonia/Singapore/India; LoI at the Connected Africa Summit (May 2025);
  KICTANet a key contributor. Sources:
  [[2025-01-01 Kenya Launches Strategic Roadmap to Build Inclusive Digital Public Infrastructure]],
  [[2025-09-15 Kenya developing DPI roadmap to streamline public service delivery]].
