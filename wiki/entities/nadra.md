# NADRA (National Database & Registration Authority)

NADRA is Pakistan's national identity and civil-registration authority, operator
of one of the world's largest biometric population databases. Beyond Pakistan, it
**exports foundational-ID technology, systems and advisory services** to other
states building national identity infrastructure — positioning a state agency of
one country as a cross-border DPI vendor.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Technology partner for Somalia's foundational ID.** NADRA is the technology
  partner behind Somalia's **SNID / national-ID build**, working with
  [[nira-somalia]] on the National Identity Management System. NIMS rolled out in
  2024; the CRMVS civil-registration system was handed over in November 2024, and
  Somali officials visited Islamabad in January 2026 to deepen cooperation. See
  [[snid]], [[nira-somalia]], [[SOM]].
- **Sovereignty and data-residency question.** A foreign-built foundational-ID
  stack raises live **data-sovereignty and data-security** concerns — where
  Somali identity data is hosted and who controls it is disputed (an open
  contradiction in this wiki). The dependence of a rebuilding state on an external
  state agency for its most sensitive population register is read here through
  both sovereignty and colonialism lenses. See [[somalia--dpi-id]].

## Note

The precise hosting arrangement for Somali ID data (in-country vs. NADRA-operated
infrastructure) is subject to an open contradiction; this page does not assert a
resolution.
