# Angola National Cybersecurity Strategy (Presidential Decree 256/25)

Angola's **National Cybersecurity Strategy**, **approved by Presidential Decree 256/25** — adopted in the Council of Ministers session of **2025-10-27** and published in *Diário da República* Iª Série n.º 227 of **2025-12-03**, in force from publication. It is the executive/decree-track counterpart to the (separately stalled) legislative [[angola-cybersecurity-law]]. *(Corrected 2026-07-20: trade-press coverage describing it as a "draft" decree was wrong about its status, and its enactment date is **2025-12-03** from the Diário da República, never the re-report's own publication date. Note also that some outlets render the decree number as "2256/25" — a syndicated typo.)*

## Profile

- **Instrument form.** A four-article Presidential Decree (approval; repeal of prior conflicting legislation; resolution of doubts by the President; entry into force on publication), grounded in the Constitution and aligned with the **Law on the Protection of Networks and Information Systems** (Law 7/17). It sets a long-term, structured framework in place of the short/medium-term cyber policies used before.
- **Institutions created.** Actions include a **National Cybersecurity Council** (with an Executive Secretariat and annual evaluation, five-yearly comprehensive review) and **incident-response teams (CERTs)** — echoing the bodies the [[angola-cybersecurity-law]] would also create ([[centro-nacional-de-ciberseguranca-angola]]).
- **Six strategic objectives:** strengthening cybersecurity structures; awareness and a cybersecurity culture; national cyber capabilities; consolidating legislation; adopting best practices; and international cooperation. Principles: subsidiarity, complementarity, proportionality, inclusion, digital literacy — with citizens and private entities bearing primary responsibility and the State intervening for sovereignty and institutional functioning.
- **Two tracks, sequential not rival.** **Resolved (2026-07-20): the two tracks are sequential, not rival.** The executive track was **enacted, not draft** — three decrees approved in the Council of Ministers session of **2025-10-27**: **DP 256/25** (approving the Estratégia Nacional de Cibersegurança) and **DP 258/25** (creating the **Conselho Nacional de Cibersegurança**, a 21-member consultative body chaired by the President), both published in *Diário da República* Iª Série n.º 227 of **2025-12-03**; and **DP 263/25** (creating the **Centro Nacional de Cibersegurança** as a public institute with legal personality and financial autonomy, under MINTTICS), published **2025-12-10**. The relationship is stated inside the strategy itself: ENCS §7.1.1 lists creating the Conselho, the Centro and CERT.ao/CSIRTs as **strategic actions of the strategy**, while §7.4 separately commits to a legislative reform whose phase 1 is passage of laws *by the legislature* — that is the bill. So **the decrees built the policy architecture; the bill would supply the binding regime** — operator obligations, incident notification, supervision and **sanctions**, which decrees alone cannot confer. The ENCS even refers forward to a "Lei da Cibersegurança" that does not yet exist. Companion decrees: **DP 258/25** (Conselho Nacional de Cibersegurança, 2025-12-03) and **DP 263/25** ([[centro-nacional-de-ciberseguranca-angola|Centro Nacional de Cibersegurança]], 2025-12-10).
