# Internet Society (ISOC)

Global non-profit for an open, resilient Internet; runs the Pulse measurement platform
([[internet-society-pulse]]), the Sustainable Peering Infrastructure Grant Program, and the **50/50
Vision** (keep half of traffic local). Active in Africa on IXPs, peering and internet resilience (with
[[afrinic]] and the [[african-telecommunications-union]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Peering → affordability (2026-05).** In **40 of 50** grant countries the income share for basic
  mobile broadband fell >10%; Burkina Faso's BFIX cut it from 13% (2020) to **0.2%** (2025), Uganda/Kenya
  >70%. See [[infra.connect]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-25 Local Infrastructure Lower Costs How Peering Is Moving the Needle on Internet Affordability]].
- **Content-locality measurement (2026-06).** New Pulse methodology (bytes, not domains): US 83% local,
  South Africa 66% (a regional hub), **Malawi 3% local / 62% regional**, Algeria 13%, Egypt 15% — US,
  Germany and France still host the bulk of content. See [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-09 Beyond the Homepage Measuring What It Really Takes to Keep Web Traffic Local]].
