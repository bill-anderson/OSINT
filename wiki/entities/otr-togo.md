# OTR (Office Togolais des Recettes / Togolese Revenue Office)

**Togo's revenue authority, covering tax and customs — and, through its Directorate of
Cadastre and Land Conservation, the country's land register.** The OTR is the most active
digitising body in Togo's public administration as recorded here: it runs the **[[gest-exo]]**
exemptions platform, feeds the **[[sydonia]]** customs system, and is georeferencing the
cadastre. Its Commissioner for Customs and Indirect Taxes (as of 2025-10) is **Atta-Kakra
Essien**. See [[TGO]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.registry]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Gest-exo launched (2025-09-30), mandatory from 2026-01-01.** The OTR launched
  **[[gest-exo]]** in Lomé, a digital platform for **tax exemption and waiver requests**,
  offering full traceability, real-time monitoring and **automatic integration with the customs
  software [[sydonia]]**. Essien: "This platform responds to an imperative of efficiency. It
  ensures smooth management of requests, from submission to automatic transmission to the SYDONIA
  customs system." Use became **mandatory on 1 January 2026** after a three-month transition.
  See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-01 Togo Launches Online Platform for Tax Exemptions and Waivers]].
- **Other digital services.** The OTR describes Gest-exo as adding to services already in use,
  including **online management of laissez-passer permits** and **motor vehicle tax payments**,
  under a modernisation strategy it frames as centred on digitisation, transparency and security.
  Source: [[2025-10-01 Togo Launches Online Platform for Tax Exemptions and Waivers]].
- **Georeferencing pre-2007 land titles (2025-11-19).** The OTR is running an operation to
  **georeference land titles (*titres fonciers*) created before 2007**, led by its **Directorate
  of Cadastre and Land Conservation** with local authorities and landowners. Before 2007, titles
  were often established **without precise georeferencing**, producing land disputes, overlapping
  titles and difficult real-estate transactions. Teams of surveyors (*géomètres*) measure and
  locate parcels in the field; the data are entered into a digital database, placing each parcel
  precisely on a digital map. Claimed benefits: securing property rights, transparency and reduced
  corruption risk, easier real-estate investment, better land-use planning — and **more efficient
  collection of land taxes**, the revenue authority's own interest in the cadastre. See
  [[data.satellite]], [[digital.localgov]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-19 Georeferencing of land titles - a revolution for security and transparency in Togo]].

**Provenance note.** The georeferencing account is the **OTR's own publication** and
self-promotional in register; it reports **no parcel counts, budget or completion date**.
Capture is not endorsement.
