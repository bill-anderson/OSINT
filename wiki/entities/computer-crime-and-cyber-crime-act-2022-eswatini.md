# Eswatini Computer Crime and Cyber Crime Act, 2022

**Eswatini's cybercrime statute**, enacted 2022 as one of four digital Acts passed that
year, and administered by [[esccom]]. It complements the
[[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]] by criminalising conduct intended to manipulate,
modify or destroy data. See [[SWZ]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.legislate]].

*The wiki holds no copy of the Act itself — only [[apc]]'s advocacy analysis of it. The
provisions below are as APC reports them, and are not verified against the gazetted
text (unlike the Data Protection Act, whose gazette the wiki holds).*

## Provisions and context

- **Section 6** addresses **illegal data interference**, carrying a fine on conviction.
- **Enacted after a consultative phase** in which civil-society organisations made
  submissions — but, like the other three 2022 Acts, **tabled before Parliament with a
  certificate of urgency**, shortening the window for stakeholder and public input.
- **Rights concerns.** APC frames the Act, with the Data Protection Act, as carrying a
  perceived **threat to press freedom** and the possible curtailment of social-media
  criticism of government — read against the June–July 2021 unrest and the internet
  suspensions and social-media restrictions of that year. See [[sovereignty]].
- **The 2022 bundle.** Sits alongside the Electronic Communications Act 2022, the
  Electronic Communications Transactions Act 2022 and the
  [[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]], all overseen by [[esccom]].
