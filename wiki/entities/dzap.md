# Digital Zambia Acceleration Project (DZAP)

**Deal / financing operation.** World Bank operation **P505094** — a **US$100M IDA
grant** (SDR 73.9M, national performance-based allocation) to the Republic of Zambia,
plus **US$20M unguaranteed commercial financing**. The 5th operation under
[[idea-mpa]]. MFD-enabling and private-capital-enabling.

## Terms (as of PAD, 2025-09-11)

- **Financier:** [[world-bank]] (IDA). **Borrower:** Republic of Zambia.
  **Implementing agency:** [[smart-zambia-institute]] (Office of the President).
- **Board approval:** 27 Jun 2024 (as part of IDEA). **PAD:** 11 Sep 2025.
  **Closing:** 31 Oct 2030. **Overall risk:** Substantial.
- **Components:** (1) Affordable broadband US$50M → [[infra.connect]]; (2)
  Interoperable & secure platforms US$38M → [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[infra.cybersec]]; (3) Digital skills US$5M → [[capacity.training]];
  (4) Program management US$7M.
- Governance: Project Steering Committee + PIU within Smart Zambia Institute; MoUs
  with implementing partners required within 30 days of effectiveness.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digital-ID procurement launched (2026-03).** Under DZAP's Component 2,
  [[smart-zambia-institute]] began seeking a [[mosip]] system integrator for Zambia's
  home-grown, open-standards national digital ID linked to civil registration, with
  full rollout and 80% citizen digital-economy inclusion targeted for end-2026 and an
  MoU with Ethiopia to draw on the [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]] model. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[dpi.registry]], [[ZMB]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]],
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia seeks partner for national digital ID system]].

## Why it matters here

DZAP is the concrete instrument through which Zambia's digital-sovereignty ambitions
(homegrown ID, vendor independence — [[zambia--dpi-id]]) are **externally financed**.
See [[ZMB]] and [[finance.new]].
