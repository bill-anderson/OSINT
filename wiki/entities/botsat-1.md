# BOTSAT-1 (Botswana's first satellite) + Artemis Accords

Botswana's **entry into space and earth observation**: a first satellite (**BOTSAT-1**) built by a national
university, followed 15 months later by accession to the US-led **Artemis Accords** — a data-and-
diplomacy thread the government is actively commercialising. See [[BWA]], [[data.satellite]].

## What we know

- **BOTSAT-1 launch (2025-03).** [[data.satellite]] Built by engineers at the **Botswana International
  University of Science and Technology (BIUST)**, carrying a **hyperspectral camera**, launched on a
  **SpaceX** rocket (California; Botswana has no domestic launch site), orbiting at **~600 km** (six passes/
  day) with an **in-country ground station**. Uses: environmental monitoring, agriculture, disaster
  response, urban planning; a **second satellite** was hinted. President Boko attended. Source:
  [[2025-03-13-botsat-1-launch-telecomreviewafrica]].
- **Artemis Accords (2026-06-25).** [[gov.regional]] Botswana became the **68th signatory** to the
  Artemis Accords, signed by Minister Tshere at NASA HQ (Washington) — a formal alignment with the US-led
  framework for peaceful/transparent space exploration. Source:
  [[2026-06-30-botswana-signs-artemis-accords-techreviewafrica]].
- **Commercialisation + SKA.** BOTSAT-1 **data commercialisation** and Square Kilometre Array (SKA)
  participation feature in the FY2026/27 govtech plan (geospatial analytics, precision agriculture, big
  data). See [[digital-services-act-botswana]].
