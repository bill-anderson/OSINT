# Association of Communications and Technology (ACT)

The **Association of Communications and Technology (ACT)** is South Africa's industry body for the major electronic-communications operators, whose members include **[[vodacom]]** and **[[mtn-group]]**. It represents the sector in regulatory and policy disputes with government.

## Activity in this wiki

- **OVS ID-verification fee challenge (as of 2026-01-27).** ACT filed a **High Court review** to overturn a Department of Home Affairs ([[home-affairs-south-africa]]) regulation raising the fee for a private-sector check against the **National Population Register** via the **Online Verification Service (OVS)** from **15 cents to ZAR 10** per real-time check — a jump of roughly **6,500%** — effective 1 July 2025. ACT argues the increase was imposed without meaningful consultation, is disproportionate, and will cause "irreparable harm" by raising the cost of the identity checks banks, telcos and insurers use for anti-money-laundering compliance and fraud prevention, ultimately passed to consumers or making low-income customers unprofitable to serve. Minister **[[leon-schreiber]]** defends the hike as funding an upgraded "Intelligent Population Register." Source: [[2026-01-27 SA's Telcos, Banks At War With Govt Over 6,500% ID Verification Fee Hike]].

This dispute sits at the intersection of South Africa's digital-ID / population-register modernisation and financial-inclusion policy; the contested OVS is the same system whose repair DHA credits for the 2025 record smart-ID issuance.
