# Veridos

**A German identity-solutions company** (a Giesecke+Devrient venture) — since **4 October 2018**
one half of the **Veridos GmbH / Giesecke+Devrient GmbH consortium** holding **49%** of Uganda's
security-printing vehicle **USPC**, and so the producer behind the [[ndaga-muntu]] card. Not, as
this page previously recorded, a mere candidate vendor — nor, as it also recorded, the sole 49%
holder.

## Activity in this wiki

**Correction (2026-07-20).** This page previously described Veridos as NIRA's *intended*
partner competing against an incumbent Mühlbauer "embroiled in a scandal concerning
overcharging." **The roles were inverted and the allegation is unsupported.** Mühlbauer was
the incumbent (2010–2018); Veridos succeeded it; the 2022–23 parliamentary fight was an
attempt to *displace* Veridos and return the work to Mühlbauer, and it failed.

- **The vendor succession — what the held sources establish.** **Mühlbauer** *(rendered
  "Muhlbauer"/"Muehlbauer"/"Maulbauer" across the outlets; the company is **Mühlbauer High Tech
  International**)* **"produced national ID cards for Ugandans between 2010 and 2018"** and
  **"set up the current system that Nira uses"**; Veridos holds the card-printing contract
  from 2018 and by 2022 **"runs"** the national digital ID card project. That succession — and
  its direction — is directly sourced. Sources:
  [[2022-11-24-biometricupdate-uganda-parliament-probe-veridos-card-printing]],
  [[2023-04-30-monitor-fight-over-national-id-deal-splits-parliament]].
- **The USPC joint venture — now sourced to primaries (2026-07-20).** Veridos announced on
  **4 October 2018** that it had *"entered into 15-year-partnership with the government of
  Uganda to provide the country's citizens with all relevant security documents"*, and that a
  new joint venture, **Uganda Security Printing Company (USPC)**, had been created with the
  state-owned **Uganda Printing and Publishing Corporation (UPPC)**. The scope: a security
  printing factory producing **ePassports and polycarbonate ID cards**, plus Veridos'
  **IMAGO** identity-management platform for enrolment, data management and personalisation.
  State Minister for Investment **Evelyn Anite** framed it as *"a more cost-efficient way to
  supply national ID"* creating *"more than 100 jobs"*; Veridos CEO **Hans Wolfgang Kunz** tied
  it to Uganda meeting **East African Community ePassport targets**. USPC's own constitutive
  record gives the shareholding: **Government 51% through UPPC**; **49% held by the consortium
  of Veridos GmbH *and* Giesecke+Devrient GmbH** — making USPC a **State Owned Enterprise**
  supervised by the **Office of the President** and **MoFPED**, formulated in **November 2018**
  as a special purpose vehicle to revamp UPPC under **s.2(iv) of the PERD Act**. Sources:
  [[2018-10-04-veridos-uppc-uganda-security-printing-factory]],
  [[2023-12-05-uspc-who-we-are-constitutive-record]].
- **Three prior claims are corrected or confirmed by those primaries.** The JV date is
  **4 October 2018**, *not* the **2016-06-11** this page previously carried — a **28-month
  error**, now withdrawn. The **15-year term is confirmed** by the announcer of record. And the
  **49% is a consortium stake, not Veridos alone** — correct anywhere the wiki says otherwise.
- **What remains unverified (as of 2026-07-20).** Two claims from reconcile-pass research are
  **still in no held source** and are **not** retired by the above: the **€64.2m** March-2010
  Mühlbauer award on presidential direction, and the **UGX 59.952bn** Kololo plant cost. The
  **2017-07-03** award by Finance Minister Matia Kasaija over De La Rue also remains unheld —
  see the acquisition note below. Do not cite any of the three onward. The one USPC operational
  fact the wiki holds is its **delivery of card stock to NIRA** (1.5m of an expected 8.1m by
  April 2025, with UV / UV-Bi-fluor anti-forgery features, per USPC project manager Hajji Abdul
  Nsubuga). Source: [[2025-04-02-nira-acquires-high-capacity-id-machines]].
- **The procurement was contested in Parliament (event 30 March 2023).** Parliament's
  **Committee on Commissions, Statutory Authorities and State Enterprises (COSASE)**, chaired by
  **Joel Ssenyonyi**, put officials of the **Privatisation Unit of the Ministry of Finance** on
  the spot over *"circumstances surrounding selection of Veridos GmbH International"*, probing
  whether due diligence was done and whether the award was single-sourced. Hon. **Nathan Itungo**
  put it directly: *"What are the odds that a German company would know there are security gaps
  in printing processes of a national entity in Uganda?"* Privatisation Unit officer **Christopher
  Mugisha** said an evaluation committee was constituted under Attorney General guidance and that
  Veridos passed legal and financial due diligence, while confirming **correspondence from the
  Ministry of the Presidency to the Ministry of Finance** about Veridos' interest. He denied
  knowledge of an allegation, put by Hon. **Elijah Okupa**, that a Veridos director was a relative
  of a highly placed Ugandan. The committee demanded all procurement documentation within a day.
  **No outcome of that demand is held.** *(Parliament posted the item 29 March 2023 while its own
  body dates the meeting to Thursday 30 March 2023; the event date is the 30th.)* Source:
  [[2023-03-29-parliament-uganda-cosase-uppc-veridos-contract]]. See [[gov.policy]].
- **The parliamentary fight (2022-11-24 → 2023-04-30).** Parliament's **Defence and Internal
  Affairs Committee** (chair Rosemary Nyakikongoro) **announced by 2022-11-24** that it would
  probe *Veridos*, after meeting officials of Mühlbauer; the company's vice-president
  **Karl Kohler** was quoted in Mühlbauer's press release. *(The wiki previously dated the
  committee resolution to 2022-11-22 and gave the VP as "Matthias Karl Kohler" — neither is in
  the held source; corrected 2026-07-20 to what it actually says.)* NIRA's
  complaint against the legacy kit was **vendor lock-in and obsolescence** — "slow, inflexible
  and vendor-locked" — which **Kohler publicly rebutted**, saying the handover was open-source
  and modifiable and that Mühlbauer had provided free maintenance under full warranty. By
  **2023-04-30** the committee had split: the **majority report** recommended NIRA re-engage
  **Mühlbauer** to upgrade the existing system; a **minority report** (Bashir Sempa Lubega)
  backed Veridos and alleged the committee leadership had held **unsanctioned private meetings
  with Mühlbauer in Uganda and in Germany**, "in total contravention of the Public Procurement
  and Disposal of Public Assets Act." Veteran MP **Abdu Katuntu**: Parliament "is not mandated
  to do procurement." Veridos had told the committee its factory would not be producing until
  **2027**. See [[gov.policy]], [[parliament-of-uganda]]. Sources:
  [[2023-04-30-monitor-fight-over-national-id-deal-splits-parliament]],
  [[2022-11-24-biometricupdate-uganda-parliament-probe-veridos-card-printing]].
- **On the cost allegation.** The wiki's earlier "overcharging scandal" framing is **not
  supported**. What the record carries is (i) contested procurement on the **2010 Mühlbauer
  award**, (ii) a **vendor-lock-in** dispute that Mühlbauer denied, and (iii) cost objections
  running the *other* way — MPs arguing the **Veridos smart ID was too expensive** against
  upgrading the legacy system. Treat the old wording as withdrawn.

**Resolved (2026-07-20) — Veridos prevailed, through USPC.** Parliament's recommendation had
no procurement force, and **no contract was ever awarded to Mühlbauer**. **Two layers must not
be conflated:** *card production and security printing* runs through **USPC**; the *enrolment
and registration system* is [[tahaluf]] with [[tech5]], Technoforte, Entrust and Hyf on
[[mosip]] (2026-02-11). **Veridos' absence from that 2026 provider list is therefore not
evidence of anything** — it is a different layer of the stack. USPC is the supplier of the
blank card stock those printing figures draw on (**10,152,559 cards printed as of
2026-02-08**), though **no source held here attributes that print run to USPC by name**, and
**no unit or programme cost for USPC card production is on file (as of 2026-07-20)**.

30 March 2023 for the full procurement file has **no published outcome on the parliamentary record**.
