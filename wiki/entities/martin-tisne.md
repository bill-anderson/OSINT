# Martin Tisné

AI-governance thinker and open-AI advocate. He is founder/CEO of **Current AI**, a
philanthropic platform for open, public-interest AI launched at the Paris AI Action Summit
(2025). His work argues that AI sovereignty for middle powers is best pursued through open,
shared, resilient AI stacks rather than fully national ones.

## Activity in this wiki

- **"Le non-alignement à l'ère de l'IA" (Le Grand Continent, 2026-02-16).** A signed essay,
  published as the India AI Impact Summit opened in New Delhi, calling for an AI
  "non-aligned movement" of **middle powers** — naming France, Germany, **Nigeria**, India
  and **Morocco** — organised around open, resilient, collaborative AI stacks. Core claims:
  only the US and China can hold fully sovereign AI stacks, and pursuing a wholly national
  stack is neither feasible nor desirable; sovereignty should be reconceived as *resilience*
  (the more open and diversified a stack, the less fragile), sharing what can be shared and
  owning what must be owned. AI models are becoming commodities, so value sits in the data
  and application layers; he calls for major investment in **data innovation** (privacy-safe
  data sharing, data trusts) and **open source**, citing **ROOST** as an exemplar. Responds
  to Mark Carney's Davos call for an "alliance of middle powers." See [[tech.ai]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[data.open]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-16 Martin Tisné  le non-alignement à l'ère de l'IA]].
