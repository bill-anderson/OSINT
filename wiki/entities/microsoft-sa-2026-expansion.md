# Microsoft South Africa R5.4bn cloud & AI expansion

[[microsoft]]'s South Africa [[ZAF]] investment programme for data-centre
expansion, power and water readiness, and AI skills.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Parties.** [[microsoft]] and South Africa [[ZAF]].
- **Announced 2025-03-06**, at Microsoft's "Building an AI Economy" event in Bryanston,
  Johannesburg — vice chair and president **Brad Smith** alongside **President Cyril
  Ramaphosa**. *(Corrected 2026-07-20: the held April-2026 dates were the re-reports'
  own publication dates, not the announcement — a 13-month error.)*
- **Amount.** **R5.4bn** additional, to be spent by end-2027, on top of **R20.4bn** over
  the preceding three years, which established South Africa's first enterprise-grade
  data centres in Johannesburg and Cape Town.
- **Allocation detailed April 2026** — land acquisition for future data-centre growth,
  power and water readiness, and added capacity in existing regions. Reported in dollar
  terms as ~$329m; **this is the same R5.4bn, not a further investment.**
- *Currency note:* R5.4bn is the fixed primary figure. Dollar equivalents float with the
  rate at each report ($296.8m / $298.6m in March 2025, $329m in April 2026) and are the
  **same commitment** — prefer rand, and treat any dollar value as a dated conversion.
- **What.** Data-centre expansion with power and water readiness, plus land
  acquisition for future data-centre growth ([[infra.store]], [[infra.energy]],
  [[tech.ai]]).
- **Skills.** Pay for 50,000 young people's certification exams over 12 months,
  toward a goal of skilling 1 million South Africans; a January 2026 digital-
  education collaboration with SABC+; partner Lelapa AI ([[capacity.training]]).
- **Status.** Active.
