# Chinasa T. Okolo

AI-governance researcher focused on Africa, the Global South and the political economy of AI. Her work spans the **Oxford Martin AI Governance Initiative (AIGI)** and the **Brookings Institution**, and she writes the *Digital Cognoscente* Substack. She is a prominent commentator on national AI strategies, data governance and how foreign corporate, philanthropic and state actors shape AI policy across African states.

## What she argues in this wiki

In a Pan African Review op-ed she warns that **corporate and philanthropic capture threatens African AI governance**. Big Tech, foreign governments, aid agencies and foundations already help draft national AI strategies and could steer regulation toward data extraction, low-paid labour and cultural homogenisation. She cites the rapid pace of announcements — the OpenAI Academy at the University of Lagos (Oct 2025), the US$50m OpenAI–Gates Foundation Horizons1000 health initiative in Rwanda, and the many countries entering vendor partnerships (Morocco–Mistral, Ghana–G42, Trinidad & Tobago–OpenAI, Ecuador–Palantir) — as evidence of a multidimensional capture risk, drawing the Brazil 2025 AI-Act amendment as a cautionary precedent of lobbying success.

Her five safeguards: **independent, publicly funded AI regulators**; mandated transparency on lobbying and partnership terms; prioritising local solutions and institutions; **regional regulatory coordination** (via the AU Continental AI Strategy) to prevent competitive deregulation; and investment in regulatory capacity — toward African **AI self-determination**.
