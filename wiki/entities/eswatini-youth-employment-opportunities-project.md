# Eswatini Youth Employment Opportunities Project (EYEOP)

**A [[world-bank]] project approved 2025-05-12** to raise income-earning opportunities
for targeted youth in [[SWZ]] — and, for this wiki's purposes, the vehicle building
Eswatini's **dynamic and digital Social Registry**. EYEOP is the **first project in
Eswatini financed by IDA** following the country's transition to **IDA/IBRD blended
status**. See [[dpi.registry]], [[finance.new]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Two components.** (1) *Youth income-earning opportunities* — promoting
  self-employment in on-farm agricultural production, agriculture-related value chains
  and other services. (2) *Strengthening social protection targeting and delivery
  systems* — developing a **dynamic and digital Social Registry** to improve
  identification and targeting of vulnerable youth, intended as a key tool for broader
  social protection programmes. The registry is the DPI payload. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-12 New World Bank Project Supports Job Creation for Eswatini's Youth]].
- **Target (2025-05-12).** Approximately **30,000 young people**, at least **50%
  female**, including young people with disabilities.
- **The labour-market case (2025-05-12).** Young people make up **30% of the
  population** and nearly **half the labour force**; only **1,000 new jobs** are created
  annually against **25,000 new entrants**; young women face **52.4% unemployment**
  against **45% for men**; over **53% of youth are NEET** (not in education, employment
  or training). *(On youth share: the World Bank's 30% is the figure to prefer;
  Minister [[savannah-maziya]]'s "70% of our population being youth" (2025-01-22) rests
  on a different definition and is rhetoric — see [[savannah-maziya]].)*
- **Welcomed by** [[satu-kahkonen]] and Minister of Economic Planning and Development
  Dr Thabo Gina. Press Release No: AFE/066/2025.
