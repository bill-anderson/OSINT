# Ministry of Home Affairs, Tanzania

The **parent ministry of [[nida-tanzania]]** — and therefore the body that carries the national
ID programme to Parliament each year for money. Its budget speech is one of the few routes by
which NIDA's coverage figures enter the public record **under parliamentary scrutiny** rather
than through the authority's own communications. Minister: **Innocent Bashungwa** (2025-05).
Permanent Secretary: **Ally Gugu** (2026-01). Not to be confused with the same-named ministries
in other jurisdictions.

## Activity in this wiki

- **FY2025/26 budget, 2.06tri/- (2025-05-27, DODOMA).** Minister **Innocent Bashungwa** presented
  a **2.06tri/-** budget to the National Assembly, up from **1.71tri/-** in 2024/25, framed
  around national security, digital services and identification. The speech carried NIDA's
  numbers: **844,994** people registered July 2024–April 2025, taking the total to **over 25.8
  million — 81.9% of the 31,582,976 aged 18 and above** per the 2022 Population and Housing
  Census; **757,189 NINs** generated in the period for a total of **21,514,741 = 83.2%** of those
  registered. Allocations: **461.4m/-** to resolve **4,342,926** registered-but-no-NIN
  applications, and **5.81bn/-** to register **2,389,290** people in FY2025/26, against a target
  of **six million more over three years**. A new 2025/26 initiative: registering **under-18s**
  and issuing them NINs. See [[nida-tanzania]], [[jamii-namba]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-27 Home Affairs unveils 2.06tri- budget]].
- **Corroboration by an independent route.** These parliamentary figures agree closely with what
  **[[edson-guyai]]** told the **[[id4africa]] 2025 AGM** three weeks later (**over 25 million**
  registered, **about 81 percent** of the 18+ population, **over 21.2 million** IDs personalised)
  — a conference panel and a budget speech, arriving by **completely different routes**, landing
  on the same picture. Independent corroboration of a state's own ID coverage claims is rare
  enough to be worth stating. Sources: [[2025-05-27 Home Affairs unveils 2.06tri- budget]];
  [[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].
- **Beyond ID (2025-05-27).** The same speech reported online court services extended to **66
  prisons** (full coverage targeted by 2028/29); a **13.5% fall in cybercrime incidents** (**748**
  offences reported July 2024–April 2025, down from **865**); diaspora registration via embassies
  in Comoros, the US, Belgium, the UK, the Netherlands and the UAE (**1,300** registered, **707**
  issued NINs and IDs); and **239,660** asylum seekers and refugees hosted as of April 2025,
  mainly from Burundi (**152,848**) and DR Congo (**86,144**). See [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2025-05-27 Home Affairs unveils 2.06tri- budget]].
- **Audited alongside NIDA (2021-03).** The **[[nao-tanzania]]** value-for-money audit of national
  ID registration and card issuance named the **Ministry of Home Affairs and NIDA** as the
  audited entities — the ministry is accountable for the programme, not merely its sponsor. See
  [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2021-03-01 Performance Audit - Registration and Issuance of National ID Cards by NIDA (NAO Tanzania)]].
- **Infant registration (2026-01-04).** Permanent Secretary **Ally Gugu** addressed the 28th NIDA
  Staff Council in Kibaha alongside DG **[[james-kaji]]**, where the pending
  **[[tanzania-infant-registration-bill]]** was set out. Source:
  [[2026-01-04 Tanzania rolls out infant registration for stronger citizen ID]].
