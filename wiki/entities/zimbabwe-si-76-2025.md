# Zimbabwe Statutory Instrument 76 of 2025 (securitised deeds)

**Regulations gazetted 18 July 2025, issued by the Minister for Justice, Legal and
Parliamentary Affairs, creating a new class of "securitised deeds"** and beginning
the digitisation of the [[zimbabwe-deeds-registry]]. Both held accounts are
**law-firm-authored — attribute** (see Provenance below).

## The critical mechanic: the window has no start date

The **24-month validation window runs from the date the Minister announces
commencement — not from gazetting.** As of **1 August 2025** commencement had **not**
been triggered; the source says only that the SI is "expected to come into effect
shortly". **Without that announcement date the wiki cannot state when the window
opens or closes** (GAP-ZWE-003). Treat every "two years" reference here as running
from an unknown t₀.

## Provisions (per Madzima 2025-07-28 and Timba 2025-08-01)

- **Validation.** Holders of old paper title deeds must submit originals and
  supporting documents to the Registrar for validation within the 24-month window.
- **Securitised deeds.** After verification the Registrar issues a **securitised
  deed** — digitally recorded and electronically authenticated — plus a **printed
  copy on securitised paper from a single supplier**, so it is identifiable and
  verifiable. A **digital copy is maintained at the deeds office**.
- **Prescriptions for conveyancers.** How a deed is drawn is prescribed: **font size,
  ink type, and rules on alterations**. Lawyers must follow strict identity procedures
  when drafting deeds and powers of attorney; documents must be notarised.
- **Traceability.** **Digital logins on the system are traceable**, creating a digital
  footprint for any person dealing with a given property.
- **Insider sanctions.** Criminal sanctions for fraud **explicitly target Registry or
  government employees who collude with or assist fraudsters** to tamper with deeds —
  an unusually direct naming of the insider threat.
- **After the window.** Only securitised deeds are legally valid for legal and
  administrative purposes — **unless the Minister, with the Registrar's approval,
  extends the transitional period by notice in the Gazette**.
- **Farmland.** Extends to **lease agreements, sub-leases and occupation arrangements**
  over registered land, not only the deeds themselves.
- **Comparators.** Explicitly modelled on **South Africa, Namibia, Australia and the
  United Kingdom** — chosen, Madzima says, because **that is where Zimbabwe's diaspora
  lives**, making remote verification the design goal rather than an afterthought.

## Trigger case

**Dr Dzingai Mutumbuka**, Zimbabwe's first black Minister of Education, nearly lost his
**Chisipite** home while residing abroad: the original deed was **stolen from the Deeds
Registry**, fraudulently altered **with the help of an internal official**, and the
property sold to a third party. The fraudster was later convicted. *(The Madzima piece
renders the name "Matumbuka" — a typo; Mutumbuka is correct.)*

## Provenance

- **Madzima (NewZimbabwe.com, 2025-07-28)** — a senior partner's interview, explicitly
  labelled a "personal view". Carries the ministry, the Deeds Registry Act 1960 origin,
  the paper/font/ink prescriptions, traceable logins, insider sanctions and comparators.
- **Timba (Nehanda Radio, 2025-08-01)** — **a conveyancing practice writing "avoid the
  rush" about a process it is paid to perform**. Its framing of the SI as a "milestone…
  [that] will do much to enhance local and international confidence" is **advocacy, not
  finding**. Carries the gazette date, post-window invalidity, the Gazette extension
  power, farmland leases and the full Mutumbuka detail.

## Note for a future reader

Zimbabwe's deeds-digitisation programme is the natural home for a colonial-dispossession
read, and **this batch contains no source that makes it**. The only adjacent gesture is a
single euphemistic clause in the Timba piece — "perceptions about the country's
commitment to the sanctity of private property rights that arose in the wake of **the
land reform process**" — framed entirely as an investor-confidence problem, by an author
with a commercial stake. That is topic adjacency, not evidence, and no `colonialism` lens
is tagged on this material. Worth sourcing properly.
