# eCHIS (electronic Community Health Information System), Ethiopia

Ethiopia's **offline-first mobile application** for community-level health data, built for the
country's **~40,000 Health Extension Workers (HEWs)**. Part of the Ministry of Health's
([[ethiopia-moh]]) **Information Revolution / "going digital"** agenda. eCHIS is **distinct
from [[dhis2]]**: it is the household/community-level electronic record used by frontline
workers, whereas DHIS2 is the national **aggregate** reporting platform ([[dpi.mis]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **Design and rollout (as of 2023 reporting).** The app comprises **4 modules across 5
  releases**; **releases 1–2** had reached **more than 7,000 health posts**. It targets rural,
  community-based care delivered by HEWs ([[digital.rural]], [[capacity.training]]).
- **Adoption constraints (2022 study).** Qualitative research on eCHIS use ([[ETH]]) found
  adoption limited by rural connectivity and power gaps, **parallel paper-and-electronic
  recording**, weak training, and HEWs' theft-liability fears for issued devices. See
  [[echis-ethiopia-study]] for the full evidence. Source:
  [[2023-06-07 Barriers, facilitators and motivators of electronic community health information system use among health workers in Ethiopia]].
