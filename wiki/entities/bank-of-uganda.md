# Bank of Uganda (BoU)

**Uganda's central bank** — payments-system operator and regulator, co-author (with the
Ministry of Finance, Planning and Economic Development) of the
**[[uganda-nfis-ii]]** financial-inclusion strategy, and the body that turned the national
ID into a condition of financial access. Governor: **Michael Atingi-Ego**
([[michael-atingi-ego]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **The ID as a financial gate (2024-10-14).** Per the **BoU 2023 annual report**, up to
  **74 financial institutions** had access to the [[nira-uganda]] database for digital KYC
  — commercial banks, microfinance institutions, a forex bureau, fintechs, insurers and
  one telco; some at full operation, others in trial (**74 listed as of June 2024**). BoU
  frames this as enforcing the [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]] provision that a
  national ID/NIN **shall be required to access certain services such as financial
  services**, implementation ongoing since **2020**, and posits that compliance drives
  financial inclusion. Separately, BoU **issued a directive in April 2024 requiring ID
  verification for all financial transactions of one million Ugandan shillings (US$260)
  and above** — *"met with criticism as some citizens said it would slow down business."*
  The pairing is the story: **74 institutions gained database access under the very
  provision the High Court declined to disturb** in June 2025 (see [[iser-uganda]]).
  Source: [[2024-10-14 Uganda financial institutions sign on for digital KYC through national ID]].
- **Payments, FY2024/25 (reported 2025-10-06).** Per BoU's Integrated Annual Report:
  electronic money transactions **+28.6% to UGX 326 trillion**; a **third ACH session
  started February 2025**, closing **3:30 p.m.** for same-day receipt; the **RTGS
  Replacement and ISO 20022 Migration** set to go live **November 2025**; a **National
  Payment Switch** in progress to connect banks, mobile money operators and fintechs. PSO
  transaction value **+49.3% to UGX 57.5tn**; agent banking **+76.1% to UGX 29.4tn** with
  agents **+49.1% to 22,793**. An **AI-powered Payments Control System** with the Uganda
  Bankers Association's Anti-Fraud Consortium **cut cyber fraud by 15%**. E-payments index
  **37%**, financial inclusion **70%**. Against the trend: **debit card use fell 5.1% to
  3.6m** and **internet banking users fell 6.6% to 0.9m**, mainly on cyber-security fears.
  Regionally, **[[eaps]]** inflows **UGX 1.3tn, +17%**, with Kenyan and Tanzanian currency
  transactions **99%** of activity. BoU **plans to study a CBDC** and update its fintech
  sandbox. See [[dpi.pay]], [[tech.ai]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-06 BoU's New Payment System Cuts Delays and Boosts E-Money by 28.6%]].
- **Internal inconsistency in that report — carried as reported.** The source gives
  national e-money as **UGX 326tn (+28.6%)** and [[mtn-uganda]]'s as **UGX 319.6tn
  (+28.6%)** while stating MTN handles **40%** of transactions — two near-identical values
  incompatible with a 40% share. **Do not compute derived shares from these figures.**
- **NFIS II (November 2023).** BoU co-issued Uganda's second National Financial Inclusion
  Strategy (2023–2028) with MoFPED; the foreword is signed by the BoU Deputy Governor. See
  [[uganda-nfis-ii]]. Source:
  [[2023-11-01 Uganda National Financial Inclusion Strategy 2023-2028 (NFIS II)]].
