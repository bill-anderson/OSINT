# Digital Impact Alliance (DIAL)

The Digital Impact Alliance is a Global South-focused organisation working on digital public infrastructure, data governance and digital identity. It runs peer-learning networks and assessment toolkits that help governments design and evaluate national digital systems.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Runs the ADLI peer-learning network (2026).** DIAL convenes the [[adli]] (Africa Data Leadership Initiative), a network for senior government data-governance practitioners; the 2026 cohort partners with [[afcfta]] on a test-and-learn approach to the AfCFTA Digital Identity Annex across six countries. Source: [[2026-05-27 Africa's economic future hinges on breaking down silos. This ADLI cohort advances integration]].
- **Maintains the Digital ID Assessment toolkit (as of 2024-01-16).** DIAL authors the [[digital-id-assessment]], a self-assessment framework for national digital-ID systems used across the Global South. Source: [[2024-01-16 Digital ID]].
- **Supported Ethiopia's Fayda program (2025–2026).** DIAL's assessment toolkit was adopted by Ethiopia's National ID Program ([[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]) as an official monitoring mechanism, re-run in 2026. Source: [[2026-05-12 Strengthening Fayda Ethiopia's improvement journey through the Digital ID Assessment]].
- **Co-drafted Ethiopia's national data-governance strategy (2025).** Under Ethiopia's Office of the PM, DIAL was invited to expand the technical committee's ([[insa-ethiopia]], MiNT, [[ethiopian-ai-institute]], Addis Ababa University) draft framework into a full data strategy — bringing in civil-society, academic and regional voices and aligning to the AU Data Policy Framework — a locally-led model DIAL frames as digital sovereignty done "from the inside out." See [[ETH]], [[gov.policy]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2025-09-18 Ethiopia's approach to their National Data Strategy offers lessons for Africa's digital future]].
- **Grounded the Ethiopia strategy in use cases (2026).** DIAL surfaced 20+ sector priorities and helped narrow them to pilotable use cases — a multi-tenant EMR interoperability pilot for health (70+ siloed platforms, 200+ pilots), AI advisory and farmer credit for agriculture, with [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]] as a cross-cutting enabler — arguing use cases must precede governance design. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[data.statistics]]. Source: [[2026-03-04 Ethiopia's approach to use cases shows how to ground data governance in real-world priorities]].
- **Cross-border identity research fellowship (2026-07).** A DIAL fellowship examines responsible cross-border identity-data governance, naming three problems — data sufficiency, data adequacy and sovereignty — behind [[afcfta]] identity portability (e.g. Kenya/Rwanda/Uganda ID cards mutually recognised for travel but inadequate for cross-border finance), and proposes standardised identity schemas with purpose-specific, locally-compliant credentials. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-07-10 Every country defines identity differently. Can digital public infrastructure bridge those differences]].
