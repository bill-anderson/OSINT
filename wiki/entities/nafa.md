# New African Financial Architecture (NAFA / NAFAD)

An [[african-development-bank]] framework, launched February 2026, to rebuild African
financial sovereignty by mobilising domestic capital and reducing dependence on external debt
and donor finance. Presented by AfDB president [[sidi-ould-tah]] at the AU summit around four
"Cardinal Points": mobilising African capital, rebuilding financial sovereignty, the
demographic dividend, and resilient infrastructure.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Launched at the AU summit (2026-02, Addis Ababa).** NAFA responds to a sharp debt
  deterioration: African external debt rose from $220bn (2009) to $685bn at end-2023 — about
  24.5% of GDP — with more than $90bn of repayments due in 2026 (S&P). It also targets the
  AU's own funding weakness (the Union self-funds only 22–25% of its ~$650m budget). Proposed
  instruments include an African Financing Stability Mechanism and an African Credit Rating
  Agency. Tags [[finance.new]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-26 The AfDB's push for African financial sovereignty, and a tech investment rebound]],
  [[2026-05-26 AfDB says Africa must fund its own growth as aid model frays]].
