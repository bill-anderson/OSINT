# Gates Foundation

Philanthropic funder of DPI, financial inclusion and digital identity across the Global
South.

## Activity in this wiki

- **AfricaNenda–Equity DPI partnership (2026-07-07).** Backing the MoU; adviser
  **Nanjira Sambuli**: "building DPI is only a first step. The real measure of success is
  whether people use it." Deal: [[equity-africanenda-gates-dpi-partnership]].
- **MOSIP funder.** Named (with Tata Trusts, Omidyar Network, Norad) among funders of the
  open-source **[[mosip]]** identity platform — a **core funder** of the ecosystem. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[geopol.india]]. Source: [[2026-07-07 Beyond the Platform Understanding the Growing MOSIP Ecosystem]].
- **Anthropic AI partnership (2026-05-14).** A four-year, **$200m** partnership with
  **[[anthropic]]** — grant funding, API credits and technical support — to build AI
  public goods (datasets, benchmarks, infrastructure) across health, education and
  agriculture in LMICs; deal [[gates-anthropic-partnership]], with illustrative
  African use-cases (a Kenyan farmer, a Nigerian health worker). See [[tech.ai]],
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-05-14 Making AI work for more people]].
- **Horizon1000 health-AI pilot with OpenAI (announced 2026-01-22).** A **$50m**
  pilot ([[horizon1000]]) with **[[openai]]** — funding, technology and technical support to
  embed AI in African national health systems — beginning in **Rwanda** ([[RWA]], endorsed by
  [[paula-ingabire]]), scaling to **1,000 primary-care clinics by 2028**, targeting SSA's 5.6m
  health-worker gap. Data Landscapers critiques Gates' use of health-workforce evidence. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.mis]], [[finance.new]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-22 Gates Foundation and OpenAI Commit $50m to AI-Powered Health Systems in Africa]],
  [[2026-01-28 A bit of quality assurance advice for Bill Gates]],
  [[2026-03-12 China's DeepSeek gives US tech giants a run for their money as Microsoft and others expand AI push in Africa]].
- **Data for Policy "Equity Initiative" funder (from 2022).** Backs [[data-for-policy]]'s
  proactive-inclusion programme, initially focused on **Sub-Saharan Africa** participation and
  inclusion scholarships. See [[XSS]], [[capacity.research]]. Source:
  [[2023-09-04 Data for Policy - About Us]].
- **African-language AI (2026-06).** Cited (with Google) among backers of African-language AI datasets in the GSMA Mobile Economy Africa 2026 checkpoint. See [[tech.ai]]. Source: [[2026-06-18 Africa's $240 Billion Mobile Economy A Checkpoint on Africa's Digital Transformation Journey]].
- **GSMA mobile-money report backer (2026-03).** Supports GSMA's flagship **State of the Industry Report on Mobile Money 2026** (>$2.1tn moved through mobile money in 2025; Africa ~66% of value) — extending Gates' long-standing backing of mobile-money and financial-inclusion evidence. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-03-01 GSMA State of the Industry Report on Mobile Money 2026]].
- **PMA Ethiopia HMIS data-use index (Gates-funded; 2023, ingested 2026-07).** Funder (grant INV 009466) of the Johns Hopkins / Addis Ababa University **Performance Monitoring for Action (PMA) Ethiopia** work that built a 10-item facility-level **HMIS data-use index** (305 facilities; **[[dhis2]]** used by >95%). See [[ETH]], [[data.statistics]]. Source: [[2023-05-11 Data for local decision-making, not a mere reporting requirement development of an index to measure facility-level use of HMIS data  Published in Journal of Global Health Reports]].
- **Zambia DPI collaboration (2026-03).** A Gates Foundation delegation met the **[[smart-zambia-institute]]** team to strengthen support for Zambia's **[[mosip]]**-based digital-ID / DPI rollout (World Bank-financed DZAP). See [[ZMB]], [[dpi.id]]. Source: [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]].

- **UNDP continental DPI paper (2026).** Named contributor (via Nanjira Sambuli) to UNDP's
  "Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa," alongside AfDB, CDPI and CoDevelop. See
  [[undp]], [[XAF]]. Source: [[2026-01-01 Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa - UNDP]].
- **OpenCRVS donor (2026-07-02).** Named — with **NORAD** and **CoDevelop** — among the donors
  behind the launch of **[[opencrvs]] v2.0**, the open-source civil-registration digital public
  good re-architected to interoperate with **[[mosip]]**. Extends Gates' DPG funding (MOSIP,
  Mojaloop) into the civil-registration layer. See [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-02 OpenCRVS v2.0 Product Launch Webinar - The Next Generation of Civil Registration]].
- **CDPI peer-learning / Sanjay Jain on DPI (2025-06).** In the Centre for Digital Public
  Infrastructure ([[cdpi]]) peer-learning session on **[[BEN]] Benin**'s DPI, Gates' DPI director
  **Sanjay Jain** argued well-built DPI on open platforms like **[[mosip]]** and Mojaloop can
  "serve as the rails for an entire economic system," citing reusability, interoperability,
  country ownership and accountability as guiding principles. See [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-23 Benin shares its X-Road-inspired DPI interoperability experience in peer learning webinar]].

- **"Philanthropic capture" critiques (2026 batch, attributed).** Two analysts in
  this batch frame Gates as a shaper of African AI governance: Alice Munyua's
  *Gatekeepers XIV* (2026-05-19) names Gates as the **largest supporter of the
  $60bn Africa AI Fund** and a central node of a "philanthropic industrial
  complex" behind Kenya's AI agenda; Chinasa Okolo (2026-02-17) lists Gates among
  the foundations that could steer AI regulation toward extraction. Both are the
  authors' arguments, cited as commentary, not wiki findings. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[XAF]], [[KEN]]. Sources:
  [[2026-05-19 GATEKEEPERS XIV]],
  [[2026-02-17 Corporate Interests Must Not Co-Opt Africa's AI Moment]].

See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]], [[XAF]].
