# Kashifu Inuwa Abdullahi

Director-General of Nigeria's [[nitda]] (the National Information Technology Development Agency),
and a leading voice for Nigerian data sovereignty and locally-controlled digital infrastructure.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Digital-sovereignty framing at GITEX Africa (~2026-04).** At GITEX Africa in Morocco, Inuwa
  set out Nigeria's push to build national software infrastructure and high-standard regulatory
  policy, casting it as securing long-term technological independence rather than picking sides in
  geopolitical rivalry: "It is not about banning China... it is about how we, as a country, have
  control." He wants hyperscalers to enter but partner with local firms so value is "captured
  here," cited the EU's Digital Markets Act, Digital Services Act and Data Governance Act as
  models, and noted an existing executive order designating all digital infrastructure as critical
  national infrastructure. Tags [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]], [[gov.standards]] in [[NGA]]; lens
  [[sovereignty]] (with a colonial-critique framing on value extraction). Source:
  [[2026-04-14 Nigeria Seeks Stronger Digital Sovereignty, National Software Infrastructure]].

- **Digital-literacy drive / DL4ALL (2026-01-23).** Inuwa framed [[nitda]]'s **[[dl4all-nitda]]**
  ("Digital Literacy for All") programme — which has trained **>480,000** Nigerians across 30
  states since Sept 2024, most recently 50 persons with disabilities in Abuja — as central to
  national targets of **70% digital literacy by 2027 and 95% by 2030**. See [[capacity.literacy]],
  [[capacity.training]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-23 NITDA Commits to Digital Inclusion for Persons with Disabilities]].

- **NGEA infrastructure takeover.** Inuwa oversaw [[nitda]]'s takeover of the [[ngea]]
  infrastructure (see [[nitda]] / [[ngea]]).

- **Digital-trust framework talks (2026-02).** At an Abuja meeting Inuwa opened "strategic
  discussions" with **[[trust-stamp]]** on AI biometric-tokenization for a national digital-trust
  framework, arguing trust is "a critical foundation for the growth of the digital economy" en route
  to the trillion-dollar-by-2030 target. See [[dpi.id]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-24 Nigeria explores avenues for digital trust collaboration with Trust Stamp]].
