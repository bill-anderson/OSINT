# Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS)

The West African regional bloc. As a political-economic bloc it is treated as an
**entity, not a place** — its work tags to the ECOWAS entity plus the affected
countries. Its long-range agenda is **ECOWAS Vision 2050**, reframing the community
"from an ECOWAS of States to an ECOWAS of the People: Peace and Prosperity to All"
for the region's ~300 million citizens. See [[XWA]], [[gov.regional]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Co-runs the WURI digital-ID programme (reported 2026-03-30).** The ECOWAS
  Commission acts as **co-secretariat with the [[world-bank]]** on the
  [[wuri-programme]] — the regional foundational-ID effort for interoperable,
  mutually-recognised credentials. At the 2nd WURI Supervisory Committee, ECOWAS's
  WURI coordinator Albert Siaw-Boateng cast interoperable ID as a critical enabler of
  free movement, cross-border trade and social protection. In Benin, WURI enrolled
  2.2m more people and helped lift legal-plus-digital ID coverage to ~99%. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[gov.policy]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-30 ECOWAS, World Bank Advance West Africa Digital Identity]],
  [[2024-06-27 WURI digital ID project improving access to public services in Benin]].
- **Adopted a regional digital roadmap (2026-01, Lagos Thematic Dialogue).** ECOWAS
  committed to a **Regional Digital Single Market** — harmonising policy to "remove digital
  borders" for its 400m+ citizens via interoperable fintech, a unified digital ID and rural
  broadband — and resolved to develop a **Regional AI Governance Framework** (flagging
  algorithmic bias, labour displacement and information disorder). Nigeria's [[cbn]]
  fintech-passporting proposal is read as an early test of whether that financial-services
  integration can move from communiqué to infrastructure. See [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]],
  [[dpi.pay]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-24 ECOWAS Adopts Digital Roadmap; Targets Unified Single Market and AI Governance for West Africa]],
  [[2026-02-06 Nigeria just raised the bar for West African Fintech]].
- **SIGMAT customs-transit interoperability (presented 2026-01, WCO conference, Abu
  Dhabi).** ECOWAS showcased [[sigmat]] (Interconnected System for the Management of Goods
  in Transit) — launched 2019, now operational along major corridors via a secure SIGMAT
  e-Hub — as a scalable blueprint for regional digital interoperability, with non-ECOWAS
  countries expressing interest; the roadmap adds risk management, mobile field capabilities
  and a regional guarantee-management system. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]].
  Source:
  [[2026-01-30 ECOWAS PRESENTS 'SIGMAT' AT THE WORLD CUSTOMS ORGANIZATION 2026 TECHNOLOGY CONFERENCE IN ABU DHABI   Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS)]].
- **WARDIP2 regional digital integration (2026-03).** ECOWAS collaborates with the
  [[world-bank]], [[african-union]] and [[smart-africa]] on the [[wardip]] programme, whose
  **$137m second phase** targets broadband for 5m+ and digital public services for 5m+ more
  (~10.6m total) across Benin, Liberia and Sierra Leone, harmonising regulation and
  expanding cross-border digital markets. See [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 New $137M initiative to enable digital service access for millions in three ECOWAS nations]].
- **Countervailing bloc.** The [[alliance-of-sahel-states]] (Burkina Faso, Mali,
  Niger) **severed all ECOWAS ties in 2024** and is building its own AES biometric ID
  and passport regime — a fragmentation pressure on ECOWAS's integration and
  mutual-recognition goals; Burkina Faso is pushing a "zero data abroad" sovereignty drive
  around its own digital-infrastructure supervision centre. Source:
  [[2026-01-22 Burkina Faso aims for digital sovereignty with infrastructure supervision center]].
