# Kimbo Fund (BFA Asset Management)

Angola's **first private-equity vehicle actually deploying** into small and mid-market companies — run by **BFA Asset Management**, a Luanda spin-off of Banco de Fomento Angola (the country's second-largest private bank by assets) that manages **~US$600m** across public and private markets. CEO/co-CIO **Rui Oliveira**. A rare inside view of Angolan capital formation, where **<2% of startups have secured venture funding**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Anchoring and first deployments (2024–2026).** Angola's sovereign wealth fund **FSDEA anchored Kimbo with US$5m in 2024**, making it Angola's first private-credit vehicle focused on small/mid-market firms. First deal: **FoodCare**, an agri-food processor exporting to Europe and North America. Second (announced March 2026): a **US$1.2m** investment in **Anda**, a mobility startup formalising Angola's motorcycle-taxi market via drive-to-own financing (Anda has raised $3.4m from Breega, Speedinvest and 4DX Ventures). Kimbo's diligence on Anda ran **three years** — bank statements line by line, supplier/employee interviews — with the firm treating Angola's information asymmetry as an edge and casting itself as an **ecosystem builder**, not a passive cheque-writer; it targets high (20%+) returns despite an "impact" label. BFA's parent completed a **$239m IPO** in 2025 — **Angola's largest ever, 5× oversubscribed**. See [[angola--dpi-govtech]], [[tech.innovate]], [[angola-startups-law]].
