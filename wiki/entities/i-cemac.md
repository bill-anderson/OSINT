# I-CEMAC (INTERPOL border-management project, Central Africa)

An INTERPOL border-security project (2019-2024; EUR 4.9m; funded by **CEMAC**) extending INTERPOL's
**I-24/7** secure communications network and databases to border points across the six CEMAC states —
Cameroon, Chad, Congo, Gabon, Equatorial Guinea and CAR ([[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]]). It
targets 40 border points and modernizes six National Central Bureaus, improving **interoperability
among national border-management systems** and travel-document screening. See [[XCA]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Rollout (2023-24).** [[infra.cybersec]] Gabon completed four border posts (Mar 2024); Equatorial
  Guinea's NCB Malabo was assessed (Feb 2023) with I-24/7 extended to airports (Malabo, Bata,
  Mongomeyen) and crossings; Cameroon's connected sites logged 6,000+ database searches in 72 hours.
  Source: [[2024-01-01 I-CEMAC (INTERPOL border-management project, Central Africa)]].
