# Public Service Commission of Kenya (PSC)

The Public Service Commission (PSC) is the Kenyan constitutional body responsible
for recruitment, appointments and standards across the national public service.
Its recruitment processes are largely merit-based, which makes the integrity of
applicants' academic and professional credentials central to its mandate.

## Why it matters here

The PSC is a participating stakeholder in a **verifiable-credentials (VC) digital
wallet** proof-of-concept in Kenya, completed as reported on **2026-03-09**. The
pilot was facilitated by Estonian IT firm [[cybernetica]] in collaboration with
the [[tony-blair-institute|Tony Blair Institute for Global Change]].

The driver was **credential fraud**: a recent audit revealed that **more than
2,000 academic and professional certificates** submitted for a public service
recruitment drive were fake. For merit-based recruitment, such fraud both erodes
trust and hands undeserving candidates an unfair advantage — a problem that lands
squarely on the PSC.

The proof-of-concept is a **wallet-based system for managing education degree
credentials**, following the **W3C Digital Wallet and Verifiable Credential
Model**, designed to be interoperable and **decentralized** (no single database
of credentials vulnerable to theft or hacking). It lets institutions issue
certificates as "cryptographically backed verifiable credentials" that government
agencies such as the PSC can verify instantly.

Cybernetica reported all aspects of the pilot as successful, with user feedback
"overwhelmingly positive" and requests to extend it to other professional
certificates, digital IDs and driver's licences. The wallet is positioned as part
of Kenya's digital transformation and aligns with the country's **Trust Framework
and Trust Registry**. Estonia has provided funding support for Kenya's digital
government efforts.
