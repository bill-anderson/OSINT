# GoCab

**Mobility fintech** operating **[[yango]]'s largest fleet in Côte d'Ivoire**. Country manager and
managing director of GoCab Côte d'Ivoire (2026): **Moulaye Tabouré**.

Its business is **drive-to-own asset finance**, not ride-hailing: drivers make regular payments out of
ride-hailing income over **three years**, after which the vehicle transfers to them — the model that
carried Nigeria's [[moove]] to a $2bn valuation (2026-07 reporting).

## Key facts

- **Funding:** raised **$45m in February 2026**.
- **Abidjan EV handover (2026-07-18):** handed **100 fully electric cars** to driver-partners — the
  **first half of a 200-vehicle programme**, and described at the time as one of Africa's largest
  operational fleets of **electric ride-hailing cars**. Vehicles operate mainly on **GOYA**, Yango's
  premium service in the country.
- **Driver base:** **over 300 existing GoCab drivers** had completed **more than two years** in the
  programme as of 2026-07, and are expected to **begin taking ownership from 2027**.

## Unit economics (company's own operating benchmarks, 2026-07)

- Full charge **~8,000 FCFA ($14)** for up to **470 km**; equivalent petrol/diesel distance
  **20,000–40,000 FCFA ($35–$70)** — a claimed **60–80% cut in energy costs**.
- Over 10,000 km, a driver retains **255,000–681,000 FCFA (~$444–$1,186)**.
- Tabouré: *"For a professional driver, fuel is not a minor expense. It is one of the highest daily
  costs of doing business… Reducing that cost by 60% to 80% can fundamentally transform a driver's
  economics."*

**These are company figures, not independently verified**, and the savings claim is the load-bearing
assumption of the whole drive-to-own repayment structure.
