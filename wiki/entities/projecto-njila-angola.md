# Projecto Njila — Angola

A **World Bank-financed ($250m) local-governance project**, launched 2023 and coordinated
by the **[[ministerio-da-administracao-do-territorio-angola|MAT]]** (with Finance, Justice,
Public Works and Public Administration). Implemented in **eight provinces** — Benguela,
Bié, Cuanza-Sul, Cunene, Huambo, Huíla, Luanda and Uíge — across **53 municipalities**.
Its **Component III** (run by MINJUSDH) expands **birth registration and BI issuance**,
tying the identity build into municipal capacity.

## Current state (as of 2026-04)

- **Registry-post rehabilitation:** 11 birth-registration posts rehabilitated in Bié in
  2025 (5 operational since August 2025, mostly in mother-and-child centres); 13 in Huambo
  near completion; further posts identified in Ecunha (Huambo) and Chitembo (Bié).
- **Mass campaigns:** birth-registration/BI drives reached 2,500+ people in Huambo
  municipality and 2,800+ in Cuito (Bié), the latter mostly aged 13+.
- **Capacity-building:** 18 training actions with the national administration school
  (ENAPP) in 2025; 1,600+ residents trained in Huambo and Bié.
