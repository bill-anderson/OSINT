# Starlink (SpaceX)

[[spacex]]'s low-earth-orbit (LEO) satellite-broadband service — the fastest-growing
connectivity entrant in sub-Saharan Africa and the sharpest current test of who
controls African connectivity infrastructure.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Performance (Ookla, SSA Q1 2026).** Starlink beat local ISPs on **download
  in every market except Madagascar**; median ~75 Mbps in Botswana, Rwanda,
  Mozambique; beat 50 Mbps in **16 of 23** countries; **Johannesburg + Nairobi
  PoPs cut latency >80%** (Kenya lowest at 39ms). Local ISPs usually win on price
  and latency; **>500,000** SSA subscribers end-2025. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[digital.rural]]. Source: [[2026-06-15 Starlink drives African broadband speeds higher]].
- **The extraction critique (Who Owns Africa's Sky, 2026-06).** LEO operators
  "cherry-pick" high-value urban/enterprise customers while paying a fraction of
  local operators' costs — **Senegal: Sonatel paid >$50m for 5G vs Starlink's
  $150,000 licence** — threatening the cross-subsidy that funds rural expansion.
  Divergent national responses: **South Africa blocked entry** (BEE ownership
  rules); **Namibia rejected** its licence (Mar 2026); Zimbabwe fastest-growing
  (67,057 subs Q4 2025). **Airtel Africa–SpaceX** Direct-to-Cell deal (Dec 2025,
  14 markets). Amazon **Kuiper** entering (Nigeria permit, Kenya filing). See
  [[gov.policy]], [[gov.legislate]], [[include.divides]], [[airtel-africa]].
  Source: [[2026-06-16 Who Owns Africa's Sky]].
- **SpaceX S-1 / $1.75tn valuation (2026-05-22, Semafor).** SpaceX's 277-page IPO
  prospectus values the company at **$1.75tn** and frames Starlink around connecting
  **>3bn people** / "bridging the digital divide." Semafor's read: Africa's divide is
  **affordability, not coverage**, so the risk is satellite expansion hollowing out local
  telecoms and shifting revenue/data/control offshore — SA regulatory disputes still
  delay entry, though Pretoria is mulling telecom-law changes. See [[geopol.usa]],
  [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2026-05-22 View How SpaceX is making a quiet bet on Africa]].
- **Revenue-leakage report (Africa CEO Forum + Askya, 2026-05-26).** Drawing on 30+
  execs/regulators, warns LEO operators capture high-value urban/enterprise customers
  under **far lighter licensing** than telcos ([[mtn-group]], [[airtel-africa]],
  [[vodacom]]) — Starlink authorised in **≥25 African markets** (~66k NGA users, >67k ZWE,
  its fastest-growing). The sector supports ~8m jobs and >$30bn in annual taxes; the
  report recommends a **hybrid model** positioning satellites as **wholesale** providers,
  not retail competitors. See [[gov.policy]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-26 Africa risks losing billions to satellite internet operators, report says]].
- **Namibia licence denied — CRAN (2026-03-26).** Namibia's **[[cran]]** denied
  Starlink both its service (ECS/ECNS) and spectrum licences, finding it met only
  **3 of 6** criteria — failing **ownership** (100% foreign, breaching the 51%
  Namibian-ownership rule, with no ministerial exemption), **national security /
  data sovereignty**, and **compliance history** (after a Nov 2024
  cease-and-desist for operating unlicensed). Starlink claims 98.6% consultation
  support; CRAN says it stays open to compliant LEO providers. See [[NAM]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[sovereignty]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-26 Starlink blocked from operating in Namibia]],
  [[2026-03-24 Namibia puts foot down against Musk's Starlink]] (a Govt Gazette notice ~23 Mar 2026
  confirmed the refusal, citing the fully foreign-owned local subsidiary; Musk branded South Africa's
  parallel ownership rules "openly racist"; Starlink is now licensed in **27** African markets).
- **Namibia reconsideration rejected; re-applied (2026-06-24).** After the March denial, CRAN received **624
  reconsideration requests** — **622** failed the legal threshold; **2** were heard on merits but showed "no new
  evidence nor material error." CRAN **upheld** the denial (still failing ownership, compliance and national
  security of the 6 criteria). Starlink (via Ben MacWilliams) had called the decision "misleading," citing
  deposits, a 3-year local-company effort and compliance "in 164 markets," and filed a **fresh application on 8
  June 2026**. Full timeline: **[[namibia--infra-connect]]**. Source:
  [[2026-06-24-namibian-regulator-rejects-starlink-again-connectingafrica]].
- **Amazon Kuiper/Globalstar challenge (2026-04-14).** [[amazon-web-services]]'
  parent Amazon is in **~$9bn** talks to buy **[[globalstar]]**
  ([[amazon-globalstar-acquisition]]) to accelerate its **Project Kuiper** ("Leo")
  LEO constellation against Starlink; Globalstar's ZAF/RWA/MOZ/GAB spectrum could
  be a regulatory shortcut. Kuiper had ~180 satellites vs Starlink's **7,000+**.
  See [[infra.connect]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-14 Amazon's $9bn satellite deal set to benefit Africa]].
- **Zimbabwe licensed (2026-04).** Zimbabwe has recently **licensed Starlink** as
  part of its connectivity/AI push (free public Wi-Fi rollout, UAE-backed coding
  programme). See [[ZWE]], [[uae]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-07 Zimbabwe switches on free wifi to power AI future]].
- **Senegal live — 26th African market (2026-02).** Musk announced **Starlink is now live in
  Senegal**, its **26th African deployment**; digital-economy minister Mamadou Talla called it "a
  historic milestone" for digital inclusion. The rollout list now covers 25 other markets — Benin,
  Botswana, Burundi, Cape Verde, Chad, Congo, Eswatini, Ghana, Guinea-Bissau, Kenya, Lesotho,
  Liberia, Madagascar, Malawi, Mauritius, Mozambique, Niger, Nigeria, Rwanda, São Tomé, Sierra
  Leone, Somalia, South Sudan, Zambia and Zimbabwe. See [[SEN]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-02-05 Senegal embraces Starlink to connect the future]].

- **[[NER]] Niger — junta deal, local distributor, telco integration (2025-2026).** Starlink launched
  commercially (**13 Mar 2025**) under an **Oct-2024 SpaceX–junta agreement** — Niger the 18th African market;
  distributed by "100% Nigerien" **[[ninetec]]** under KYC rules, with **[[arcep-niger|ARCEP]]** cracking down on
  pirate "Wifi Zones" (Loi 2018-45) and **[[airtel-niger|Airtel]]** later folding it into "Starlink by Airtel."
  A US-satellite/rural-access vs state-sovereignty story: **[[niger--infra-connect]]**. Source:
  [[2025-03-13-starlink-officially-available-niger-actuniger]].
- **Kenya CA audit of Airtel Direct-to-Cell (2026-03-10).** The
  [[communications-authority-of-kenya]] opened a technical review of
  [[airtel-africa]]'s application to use Starlink **Direct-to-Cell**, probing
  whether LEO-to-handset signals interfere with licensed 3G/4G/5G spectrum — a
  potential global precedent for satellite-mobile coexistence; audit due to
  conclude by mid-2026. See [[KEN]], [[gov.standards]], [[infra.capacity]].
  Source: [[2026-03-10 Kenya begins audit of Airtel's Starlink Direct-to-Cell tech]].
- **SA licence advocacy campaign (2026-01).** Starlink is running an online
  campaign urging South African customers to lobby for market entry, after a Jan
  2026 email pressed users to pressure [[icasa]]; SA's 30%
  historically-disadvantaged-ownership rule remains the block, with Minister
  Malatsi's Dec 2025 policy direction proposing **Equity Equivalent Investment
  Programmes (EEIPs)** as an alternative and [[spacex]] pledging **R2.5bn** toward
  B-BBEE compliance. A parliamentary committee chair called the campaign "an
  affront." See [[ZAF]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-18 Starlink pushes for SA license with advocacy campaign]].

- **From rival to ally — Airtel + Vodacom partnerships (2026-01).** Two continent-scale
  telco tie-ups reframe Starlink from competitor to wholesale supplier:
  **[[starlink-airtel-d2c-partnership]]** (Direct-to-Cell + cellular backhaul, launching 2026
  across all **14 [[airtel-africa]] markets**; SpaceX licensed in 9 of 14 by May 2025) and
  **[[starlink-vodacom-leo-partnership]]** ([[vodacom]] LEO backhaul + equipment resale across
  its footprint, >223m customers). Starlink was live in **25 African countries** as of Nov 2025;
  its SA commercial rollout stays delayed on B-BBEE compliance despite the **ZAR 2.5bn (~$145.6m)**
  30%-HDI-ownership pledge. See [[data.satellite]], [[include.access]], [[ZAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]].

- **Guinea-Bissau — licensed then live (2025-04 / 2025-06).** [[GNB]] Regulator [[arn]] (ARN-TIC)
  granted a general provisional authorization on **2025-04-10** (after a Dec 2024 provisional
  licence); commercial launch followed on **2025-06-19**, GNB being Starlink's **7th West African**
  and **20th continental** market. A standard kit cost **XOF 228,000 (~$400)** with a **$63/mo**
  subscription; GNB internet penetration was just **32.5%** (723k users, Jan 2025). See
  [[infra.connect]], [[include.access]]. Sources: [[2025-04-10 ARN-TIC grants Starlink Guinea-Bissau provisional authorization]], [[2025-06-19 Starlink enters Guinea-Bissau]].

- **Liberia — school connectivity + first satellite rules (2026-07).** [[LBR]] The [[lta-liberia|LTA]]
  is overseeing installation of Starlink in **156 public schools** (~90 reached); Starlink supplied
  equipment worth ~**US$156,000** while the LTA finances distribution/installation (~US$135,000). The
  LTA is drafting Liberia's **first comprehensive satellite-communications regulations** as more LEO
  operators are expected. See [[include.access]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-lta-telecom-reforms-star-cell-revoked-fpa]].

- **Burundi — licensed but priced out (2024–26).** [[BDI]] Starlink was licensed **May 2024** and
  launched **Sept 2024**, greeted as a fix for one of the region's worst connectivity gaps (internet
  penetration ~11–12.5%) — but at **>$500** for a kit ("my whole year's salary," one civil servant) it is
  out of reach for most; it is being weighed for [[elmis-medexis|health-facility]] connectivity in
  underserved areas. See [[infra.connect]], [[include.divides]]. Sources:
  [[2025-09-10-unreliable-connectivity-impacts-burundi-citizens]],
  [[2026-06-26-burundi-elmis-medexis-health-rollout-wearetech]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty` / `colonialism`: a foreign-owned constellation supplying scarce
  downlink capacity on its own commercial terms — regulators debate pushing
  satellites from direct-to-consumer *competitors* into wholesale *partners*. The
  connectivity analogue of the compute-capacity dependency in [[infra.capacity]].
