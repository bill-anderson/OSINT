# Starlink–Vodacom LEO Backhaul Partnership

**Parties:** [[starlink]] / [[spacex]] and [[vodacom]]. **What:** integration of **Low
Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite backhaul** into Vodacom's network as a hybrid system, plus
authorisation for Vodacom to **resell Starlink equipment and broadband** (pay-as-you-use
backup internet, device-as-a-service, an "unbreakable" service for critical operations).
Unlike the Airtel D2C deal, this is an **enterprise/SMB and reseller** play. **Where:**
across the **25 African countries** where Starlink operates (as of Nov 2025), including
South Africa, Kenya, DR Congo, Lesotho, Tanzania and Mozambique.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Scale and South Africa compliance.** Vodacom serves **>223m customers** across Africa;
  the deal supports its **Vision 2030** target of 260m customers / 120m financial-services
  users. In **South Africa** ([[ZAF]]), Starlink committed **ZAR 2.5bn (~$145.6m)** toward
  the local **30% historically-disadvantaged-ownership** requirement, though its SA
  commercial rollout has been **delayed on compliance**. Targets rural schools, clinics,
  mining, oil & gas, agriculture and tourism. Vodacom CEO **Shameel Joosub** and SpaceX's
  **Chad Gibbs** quoted. See [[infra.connect]], [[data.satellite]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]].
