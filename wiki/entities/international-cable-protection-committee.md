# International Cable Protection Committee (ICPC)

The **International Cable Protection Committee (ICPC)** is the world's leading
organisation promoting submarine-cable protection and resilience. Founded in the
**UK on 22 May 1958**, it is a non-profit whose membership comprises governmental
administrations and commercial companies that own or operate submarine
telecommunications or power cables, plus cable-ship operators and other industry
interests. As of **September 2025** it had **more than 235 members from over 70
countries/territories** — including most of the world's major cable-system owners
and cable-ship operators.

## Mandate and function

The ICPC provides a forum for exchanging technical, legal and environmental
information, and it is the recognised **specialist standards body** for the
sector. Its prime activities are: promoting awareness of cables as critical
infrastructure; establishing internationally agreed **recommendations and best
practices** for cable installation, protection, maintenance and repair across the
project life cycle; monitoring international treaties and national legislation to
protect cable interests; and liaising with UN bodies. Governance runs through an
elected Executive Committee; the Chair (as of 2025) is Dean Veverka (Southern
Cross Cables). Full membership costs £3,500/year.

## Relevance to Africa and cable resilience

The ICPC is central to the governance response to Africa's cable
vulnerabilities analysed by [[jane-munga]] / [[carnegie-endowment]]. Its
**"Government Best Practices for Protecting and Promoting Resilience of Submarine
Telecommunications Cables"** (25 November 2024) is a key reference the Carnegie
paper draws its governance recommendations from — e.g. designating a single point
of contact for incident reporting, marine spatial planning / cable corridors, and
recognising subsea cables as critical infrastructure.

In **November 2024** the ICPC co-launched, with the **[[itu]]** (ITU), the
**International Advisory Body for Submarine Cable Resilience** — co-chaired by
Nigerian Communications Minister [[bosun-tijani]] — bringing governments,
regulators and industry together on cable resilience. The ICPC also notes that,
as of 2022, only ~60 cable ships existed worldwide; the Carnegie paper adds that
fewer than ~100 repair ships exist globally and **only three serve Africa**.

## Update (2026-07) — Advisory Body final report

- **Final report approved (2026-07).** The ITU–ICPC **International Advisory Body for Submarine
  Cable Resilience** completed its **two-year programme** (est. 2024) and approved its **final
  report** — a practical roadmap across three pillars: **faster deployment/repairs, better risk
  monitoring, and route/geographic diversity** — plus streamlined permitting, stronger public-
  private coordination and climate integration. Co-chair [[bosun-tijani]] and ITU SG Doreen
  Bogdan-Martin hailed the milestone; programme summits ran in **Abuja (2025)** and **Porto (2026)**.
  Vulnerabilities are most acute for LDCs/SIDS and **Sub-Saharan Africa**, where a single cut can
  cause "digital darkness." See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.standards]], [[gov.regional]], [[XSS]].
