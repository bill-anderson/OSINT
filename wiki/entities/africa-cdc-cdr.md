# Africa CDC Central Data Repository (CDR)

A secure, interoperable **federated data platform** operated by **[[africa-cdc]]** (the
African Union's public-health agency) that integrates surveillance, laboratory and
programme data from national and regional systems into a single continental foundation for
public-health intelligence — outbreak detection, forecasting and cross-border response
(including One Health).

## Why it's a standing OSINT asset

The CDR is the emerging continental **[[dpi.mis]]** / **[[dpi.exchange]]** backbone for
health data — the layer against which African disease-surveillance interoperability,
data-sharing terms and public-health data sovereignty can be tracked. Its **federated
model — member states retain data ownership** while Africa CDC generates continental
insights — is a clean data-sovereignty datapoint, and a notable contrast to the US
bilateral health-data-sharing MOUs (see [[america-first-global-health-strategy]]).

## Key facts (as of launch)

- **Launched January 2026** (announced 27 Jan 2026, Addis Ababa); preceded by a feasibility
  study, prototype and a validation workshop on **17–18 November 2025** at Africa CDC HQ.
- **Financed directly by the [[global-fund]]**; needs assessment, cost analysis, operational
  planning and prototype built by the **[[public-health-informatics-institute]]** (PHII) of
  the Task Force for Global Health.
- Motivating context: public-health emergencies rose **40% between 2022 and 2024** (213
  incidents continent-wide).
