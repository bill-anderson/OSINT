# Econet InfraCo

**The infrastructure arm of [[econet-wireless-zimbabwe]]** — towers, fibre, power systems and
data facilities — being separated out as a dedicated infrastructure company, and the
vehicle for a **300-hectare industrial and IT park near Harare's airport** including a new
data centre.

**Corporate-entity note (resolved, ISSUE-009).** Three related names appear in the sources:
**[[econet-wireless-zimbabwe]]** (the listed Zimbabwean operator), **[[econet-group]]** / Econet
Global (Strive Masiyiwa's founder vehicle), and **Econet InfraCo**. **Both held sources agree
InfraCo sits under Econet Wireless *Zimbabwe***, so InfraCo is parented to
[[econet-wireless-zimbabwe]], not the Group. The formerly conflated `econet-wireless` page has been
split into those two entities and is now a disambiguation pointer ([[econet-wireless]]).

## Was InfraCo new? — resolved

- **ITWeb Africa (2025-12-19):** InfraCo is Econet's **"newly founded** tower and
  infrastructure firm".
- **Developing Telecoms (2025-12-22):** the name was "publicly unveiled recently as part
  of a broader corporate restructuring", but the company says it is **"not a new business
  but has long operated as a dedicated unit within Econet Wireless Zimbabwe"**, providing
  towers, fibre, power systems and data facilities — attributed to the company itself.

**Resolved (2026-07-20): neither "newly founded" nor a simple rename.** Econet InfraCo **is Distributed
Power Africa (Private) Limited**, **incorporated in Zimbabwe on 21 March 2016 under company registration
number 2030/2016**, renamed and converted private→public (new Certificate of Incorporation 2026-03-24).
The assets were transferred as a going concern from **EWPL and other Econet Group SPVs** under a Scheme
of Reconstruction Agreement — made **in terms of s.15(1)(b) of the Capital Gains Tax Act [Chapter
23:01]** — completed **2025-12-24**: a real legal consolidation across several SPVs, not a rebrand in
place and not a founding. ITWeb's "newly founded" is wrong; Developing Telecoms is directionally right
on maturity but imprecise on structure. It listed on the **VFEX on 2026-03-31** (INFR.VX) with EWZL
retaining 70%.

**Correction (2026-07-20): DPA was not a renewables subsidiary.** The wiki previously described the
vehicle as "Econet's pre-existing **renewable-energy** subsidiary". The Pre-Listing Statement says
otherwise: **"Econet InfraCo has provided backbone tower support services to Econet since inception"**
(para. 5.2.2.4). The 2016-incorporated company was a **tower and infrastructure support entity from the
start**; renewable energy is one of three operating divisions, not its original business. The
"renewables" reading was an inference from the *name* Distributed Power Africa, and the name was
misleading. See [[distributed-power-africa]].

**The decisive document is EWZL's own stakeholder notice of 2025-12-24**, which states that the Group
"completed a reorganisation that resulted in the consolidation of its real estate (immovable properties)
and passive telecommunications infrastructure assets into a dedicated real estate entity, **Distributed
Power Africa (Private) Limited, trading as Econet InfraCo**", and that those assets were "**previously held
by EWPL and other wholly owned SPVs**", transferred **as a going concern** under a **Scheme of
Reconstruction Agreement**. Ownership and control did not change; EWZL remains beneficial owner, and where
title still stands in another SPV's name it is held **as nominee or in trust** for InfraCo. Source:
[[2025-12-24-econet-notice-completion-infrastructure-restructuring]].

*Minor residual, not resolved:* the **2025-12-15** cautionary announcement names the vehicle "**Econet
Infrastructure Company Limited**", a name that appears nowhere in the record after 2025-12-24. The most
economical reading is an intended name dropped once DPA was chosen as the vehicle, but the wiki does not
hold a document saying so. Source:
[[2025-12-15-econet-wireless-zimbabwe-further-cautionary-announcement]].

*Brand caution (resolved 2026-07-20):* the DPA name attaches to **three distinct registered companies**
of common Econet origin. InfraCo is the **Zimbabwe-registered** one. It does **not** inherit the South
African solar-wheeling history of **DPA Southern Africa (Pty) Ltd**, the DPA–[[edf]] joint venture that
signed the [[africa-data-centres]] PPAs on the [[cassava-technologies]] side of the group. See
[[distributed-power-africa]].

## Operating structure — three divisions, not three companies

The Pre-Listing Statement sets out InfraCo as **one company with three internal operating divisions**
(para. 4.4), which matters because the wiki briefly recorded a separate power *company*:

- **TowerCo (Tower Operations)** — ownership, deployment, operation, maintenance and security of
  passive tower infrastructure: ground-based towers, rooftop sites, in-building solutions. Operates on
  a **neutral-host** model, aiming at co-location by multiple licensed operators; stated as **the
  primary contributor to operating leverage** over the medium to long term.
- **PropCo (Real Estate)** — the land, buildings and sites; USD-denominated rental and access income
  under long-term contracts.
- **PowerCo (Power Operations)** — grid interfaces, diesel generation, fuel management, battery
  storage and **renewable solutions including solar and hybrid configurations**, serving the
  infrastructure portfolio "and other commercial applications". Mandate extends to **"provision of
  energy and power related services to a broad range of customers including Econet and the broader
  Zimbabwean market"** (para. 4.3.2).

***Correction (2026-07-20): there is no "Distributed Power Zimbabwe (DPZ)".*** The wiki recorded that
InfraCo's power business had been "rebranded Distributed Power Zimbabwe (DPZ)". **Neither the circular
nor the Supplementary PLS contains that name** — the string appears nowhere in either document. It
entered the wiki through an ingest-time editorial note, not a source. The residual power business is
the **PowerCo division inside Econet InfraCo**, unnamed as a separate legal entity in any held
document. The underlying point the DPZ claim was making still holds — the Zimbabwean power business is
**not** the South African DPA–EDF joint venture — but it is made by the division structure, not by a
rename. Source:
[[2026-02-04-econet-wireless-zimbabwe-circular-and-econet-infraco-pre-listing-statement]].

**Anchor-tenant concentration is the risk to carry.** Econet "will remain the principal customer in
the initial phase" and the anchor tenant under long-term contracts (paras. 1.4, 3.4, 5.2.2.2).
InfraCo's diversification is stated as an *intention*, "subject to commercial viability" — so a
listed, USD-denominated infrastructure vehicle currently derives its predictable revenue from its own
71%-parent. That is the standard tower carve-out shape, and worth stating plainly rather than reading
the neutral-host language as achieved third-party demand (as of 2026-02-04).

## Conversion, listing and shareholding (2026)

- **Conversion private → public.** The Company completed its conversion from a private to a public
  company; a **new Certificate of Incorporation was issued 2026-03-24** under registration number
  **74120A02122025**. A **name change from Distributed Power Africa Limited to Econet InfraCo Limited**
  was still in train as at 2026-03-26, expected to complete before trading began. *Two registration
  numbers are correct and are not a conflict: **2030/2016** is the original 2016 incorporation as a
  private company, **74120A02122025** the 2026 certificate on conversion to public. Cite whichever the
  context calls for, but do not present them as competing records of one event.*
- **Listing.** Unconditional approval for the **VFEX** listing (by way of introduction) was confirmed, with
  trading expected to commence **2026-03-31**. The Original Pre-Listing Statement is dated **2026-02-04**,
  as is the circular to EWZL shareholders.
- **Shareholding.** After the **Exit Offer**, EWZL held **2,848,982,817 shares (95.215%)** and exiting
  shareholders **143,180,386 (4.785%)**. A **dividend in specie of 754,468,575 InfraCo shares** to EWZL
  shareholders on the register at **2026-03-27** takes the final structure to **70.000% EWZL / 25.215%
  Econet shareholders / 4.785% exiting shareholders**.
- **Share capital (2026-03-26).** Authorised raised to **5,000,000,000** ordinary shares (from
  3,000,000,000); issued **2,992,163,203**, unchanged.
- **CEO.** **Fayaz King** appointed Chief Executive Officer effective **2026-03-01** — 30+ years in
  telecoms/IT, formerly Assistant Secretary-General and Deputy Executive Director, Field Results and
  Innovation at **[[unicef]]**, and **COO of Econet Wireless Zimbabwe (2015–2019)**. *(He succeeds Douglas
  Mboweni, who announced the Harare park as CEO in Dec 2025.)*
- **Stated business focus (2026-03-26).** Real estate and infrastructure asset ownership; passive
  telecommunications infrastructure; power and energy solutions including renewable energy.

## The delisting that drove it (2025-12-15)

EWZL's board resolved to pursue a **voluntary delisting from the [[zimbabwe-stock-exchange]]** under
Section 11.5 of the ZSE Listings Requirements, with a **voluntary Exit Offer** settled **partly in cash and
partly in InfraCo shares** before delisting took effect. The stated reason is a valuation gap: EWZL "has
traded at a significant discount to its peers across Africa which trade at **6–8x EV/EBITDA**", peers
having already separated and realised value from their towers. Post-delisting, EWZL's Memorandum and
Articles were to be **amended to reinstate shareholder rights of pre-emption** removed on listing. Source:
[[2025-12-15-econet-wireless-zimbabwe-further-cautionary-announcement]].

## The Harare industrial and IT park (as of December 2025)

- **300 hectares** near **Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport**, Harare.
- Powered by a **100 MW solar plant**.
- Includes a **data centre whose capacity is not disclosed** — Developing Telecoms notes
  precise capacity details "do not yet appear to be available". See [[infra.store]].
- **Phased rollout in line with demand**; work on **phase 1 has apparently already begun**
  (2025-12-22, hedged).
- Framed as **possibly "one of the largest private-sector developments since Zimbabwe's
  independence"** — the source's framing, hedged.
- Intended as a modern industrial and logistics hub leveraging airport proximity for
  manufacturing, processing and export-oriented business.
- **Approved by founder [[strive-masiyiwa]]**; announced by CEO **Douglas Mboweni**, who
  said: "Our vision is to create infrastructure, such as power and water facilities, needed
  for a modern industrial park. This will make it easier for investors to come in, take
  advantage of the location, and create jobs." *(Mboweni refreshes to 2025 as CEO; the former
  `econet-wireless` page dated him to 2014 — now on [[econet-wireless-zimbabwe]].)*

## Why separate the towers

Econet said the creation of InfraCo "aligns with international best practice, whereby
mobile network operators separate passive infrastructure into a dedicated infrastructure
company", enabling **clearer visibility of asset values, focused capital allocation and a
distinct operational strategy**. The stated commercial rationale is peer pressure: African
MNO peers had **"already separated and realised value from their tower infrastructure"**,
while Econet **still owns its towers and other passive equipment** (as of December 2025).

## Listing

InfraCo was **listed on the [[victoria-falls-stock-exchange]], which accepts only US dollars**, in order
to attract international investors — trading expected to commence **2026-03-31** (2026-03-26). EWZL's own
rationale (2025-12-15) is that infrastructure assets "represent a different class of investment, one that
is better understood and valued within USD-based property and infrastructure markets", citing the higher
P/E multiples at which listed real estate and infrastructure companies trade on VFEX. See [[finance.new]].

## Parentage: EWZL, and above it Econet Global

InfraCo's immediate parent is **[[econet-wireless-zimbabwe]]** (70.000% post-listing). EWZL's own
controlling shareholder is **Econet Global Limited (41.68%)**, which the circular names as the party
that "will remain the controlling shareholder of the Company" — so InfraCo sits under EWZL, and EWZL
under [[econet-group|Econet Global]]. **Both limbs of the old parentage question are now closed on
company primaries**; the earlier caution that InfraCo might report to the Group rather than the opco
was correct to prefer the opco, and the Group sits a level above rather than in its place.
