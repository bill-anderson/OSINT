# Ndaga Muntu (Uganda National ID)

**Uganda's national identity credential** — the National ID card and its underlying
National Identification Number (NIN), issued by [[nira-uganda]] and, since the 2024–2026
mass registration campaign, enrolled on a [[mosip]]-based platform. *Ndaga Muntu* is the
name under which residents are enrolled to the national biometric registry, which is used
to issue cards and to support public service delivery and financial inclusion (Source:
[[2026-03-02 IriTech provides iris biometrics for Uganda's upgraded registration kits]]).

## The spine

Read together, the material this wiki holds shows one chain: **Ndaga Muntu is now
load-bearing for finance, tax, education and social protection — while NIRA's own numbers
show the register reached barely over a third of the people it set out to reach.**

- **Finance.** Up to **74 financial institutions** had NIRA database access for digital
  KYC (**74 listed as of June 2024**), under the [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]]
  provision that a national ID/NIN **shall be required to access certain services such as
  financial services**; implementation ongoing since **2020**. [[bank-of-uganda]] issued a
  directive in **April 2024** requiring ID verification for all financial transactions of
  **one million Ugandan shillings (US$260) and above** — *"met with criticism as some
  citizens said it would slow down business."* Source:
  [[2024-10-14 Uganda financial institutions sign on for digital KYC through national ID]].
- **Tax.** The NIN becomes the official tax identity, replacing the TIN under the **Tax
  Procedures Amendment Bill** — *"Soon and very soon, your NIN will be your TIN"*
  ([[ura-uganda]], #LearnTaxUg). Source:
  [[2025-11-11 URA Launches Tax Filing Season, Announces Shift from TIN to NIN]].
- **Education.** The Ministry of Education mandates learner registration on an **EMIS**
  portal requiring a NIN; those without are excluded from enumeration, planning and
  budgeting. Source:
  [[2025-09-13 Uganda's Digital ID Barrier Worsens as Court Delivers Blow to Access]].
- **Social protection.** [[sage-uganda]] benefits only those on the National
  Identification Register with a national ID. Source: as above.
- **Against that: coverage.** As of **2026-02-11**, renewals reached **14.3m of a 15.8m
  target (90.5%)** but first-time enrolments only **6.4m of a 17.2m target (37.3%)**; of
  **10.1m cards printed, only 2.5m were picked up** (over 60% uncollected). Source:
  [[2026-02-11 Uganda migrates more than 28M records to new national ID system]].
- **And: capture failures.** **More than 1 million Ugandans had difficulty registering
  fingerprint biometrics** (NIRA figures cited by [[iritech]], 2026-03-02) —
  disproportionately elderly people and manual labourers, the stated reason iris capture
  was added. Compare [[asamo-hellen-grace]] on disability and financial exclusion.

The gate closed faster than the register filled.

## Provenance note

The wiki previously held the **28m-migration** fact **only second-hand**, as a passing
clause in [[2026-05-22 Uganda launches prototype program for digital ID and public infrastructure]],
**which hyperlinks to this very article**.
[[2026-02-11 Uganda migrates more than 28M records to new national ID system]] is the
**origin account**. It is corroborated independently by [[iritech]] (2026-03-02): NIRA has
migrated **28 million biometric records to MOSIP, in the open-source platform's first
completed brownfield implementation** — a line held nowhere else.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The system as of 2026-02-11.** Fully transitioned to the new digital ID platform;
  **>28m records migrated**, **>35m total enrolments**. Five [[mosip]]-based modules;
  provider [[tahaluf]]. QR-code scanning failures on some new cards were acknowledged by
  State Minister Gen. David Muhoozi, with the authentication module due fully operational
  **by end of March**. Source: as above.
- **Enrolment kits (2026-03-02).** **More than 5,665** kits rolled out under the
  **2024–2026 campaign**, collecting fingerprint **and iris** via [[iritech]]'s
  **IriShield BK binocular scanner**. (Supersedes the *5,680 planned* of 2024 with
  rounding — not a conflict.) Source: as above.
- **Registration launched 27 May 2025**, after slipping from 1 June 2024 → July 2024 →
  October 2024. See [[nira-uganda]] for the chronology. Sources:
  [[2024-08-19 Uganda Prepares Large-scale Biometric Registration for National ID]],
  [[2025-05-28 Huge turnout as Nira starts mass registration]].
- **Court challenge dismissed (2025-06-10).** The mandatory NIN/National ID requirement
  under the [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]] survived challenge in the High Court. See
  [[iser-uganda]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-13 Uganda's Digital ID Barrier Worsens as Court Delivers Blow to Access]].

## Contradiction — open, not resolved

DRAA (2025-09-13) estimates **18 million (about 40%) of the population** lack a National
ID; NIRA (2026-02-11) frames a register of **more than 35 million**. **The DRAA figure
carries no cited source — it is the author's estimate, and that is itself the finding.**
**Resolved (2026-07-20):** the figures measure different populations. The 18.1m unregistered
is **NIRA's own count against the total census population of 45.9m** (ED Kisembo to COSASE,
2024-07-01), and NIRA stated most of those unregistered are **children under 17**. Measured
against the **23,154,716 adults aged 18+ (2024-05-09)**, the adult registration gap was roughly
**2.0m, ~9% (2024-10-15)**. The **35m** register figure (2026-02-08) is cumulative enrolments
ever issued, carrying no deregistration for death or emigration. The real constraint is **card
possession**: 10,152,559 printed, **2,581,457 collected (35.5%, 2026-02-08)**.
