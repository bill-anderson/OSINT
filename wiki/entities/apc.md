# Association for Progressive Communications (APC)

**A global network of civil-society organisations working on internet rights**, and —
in this wiki — the principal critical voice on Eswatini's digital statutes through its
**Southern Africa Digital Rights** publication. See [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]].

**Standing:** APC is named-analyst **advocacy analysis**, not neutral record. Attribute
its claims to APC and its authors; where a section-level legal claim matters, verify
against the gazetted [[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]] text the wiki holds.

## Activity in this wiki

- **"Eswatini strives for digital sovereignty" (Ndimphiwe Shabangu, 2024-03-11;
  updated 2024-11-18).** Reads the [[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]] and
  [[computer-crime-and-cyber-crime-act-2022-eswatini]] against the June–July 2021
  unrest, arguing both carry a perceived threat to press freedom and to social-media
  criticism of government. Supplies the section-level detail the wiki holds on the DPA
  — s.6(3)(b) administrative fines up to five million Emalangeni (**~USD 268,000 as of
  2024-03-11**), s.9(2) explicit consent, s.17(1) breach notification, s.44 direct
  marketing, and s.36 as a human-rights concern (grounds for non-action on complaints).
  See [[esccom]], [[eptc]], [[mtn-eswatini]]. Source:
  [[2024-03-11 Eswatini strives for digital sovereignty amid technological advancements]].
- **"Risk of human rights infringement as Eswatini rushes to regulate the ICT sector"
  (Melusi Matsenjwa, 2024-07-22; updated 2024-11-04).** Argues rollout of biometric and
  digital systems — SIM registration, biometric voter registration, civil registration,
  financial services — preceded comprehensive legal frameworks, and that all four 2022
  Acts were tabled under **certificates of urgency**, compressing stakeholder input.
  Also the wiki's source for the national ID card's history and limits
  ([[ministry-of-home-affairs-eswatini]]) and for digital-ID work being conceptualised
  through the [[rstp]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2024-07-22 Risk of human rights infringement as Eswatini rushes to regulate the ICT sector]].
