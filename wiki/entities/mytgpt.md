# mytGPT (Mauritius Telecom sovereign AI platform)

**[[mauritius-telecom|Mauritius Telecom]]**'s enterprise-grade **sovereign AI platform**, presented as the
country's first, keeping data under Mauritian jurisdiction and running on MT's Tier IV data centre / GPU compute
(the "AI & Compute Corridor" of MT's 2026-2029 strategy). CEO **Veemal Gungadin** frames it around
**democratising AI access** ("an AI reserved for specialists is not an AI that truly serves a country").

**mytGPT Education** — an AI personal-tutor pilot launched **26 January 2026** (demonstrated at James Burty David
Secondary School; with the Ministry of Education) — is evolving toward **my.t Learn** and is folded into the
2026-2027 Budget's **50,000-trainee** AI-skilling plan (the MyT-GPT Education pilot scaling from 10 schools into
secondary). See [[mauritius--tech-ai]], [[capacity.training]].

**Capability as of 2026-07-16** (CEO keynote, AllMyT Summit): mytGPT now integrates **Microsoft 365, Gmail
and Shopify** and operates agentically — calling other software directly, and demonstrated generating a
complete website from a text brief. Alongside it MT demoed a **Mauritian Creole-speaking AI call-centre
agent** ("Kevin") handling fault diagnosis, ticket creation and human handoff. Source:
[[2026-07-16-mauritius-telecom-allmyt-keynote-sovereign-ai-platformafrica]].

**my.t Learn — a documented pivot away from the student-facing tutor.** The education pilot completed across
**ten schools**, but teacher and parent resistance ("Are we teaching kids that they should outsource their
thinking rather than doing the thinking by themselves?", Gungadin, 2026-07-16) prompted a deliberate
refocus **from the student tutor to a teacher AI coach** — auto-correcting homework from a phone photograph
and generating custom exam papers. MT's stated goal is a personal AI assistant for **every secondary school
teacher by 2027**. A rare case of an African operator publicly narrowing an AI education deployment on
pedagogical grounds. See [[capacity.training]].

*Provenance note (resolved 2026-07-20, closing GAP-MUS-001).* The pilot launch remains PR-wire sourced, but
the **AllMyT Summit announcements are now primary-sourced** and the earlier "unconfirmed" caveat is retired.
What carries forward is a distinction, not a doubt: MT's **NVIDIA H200** hosting claim is an **operator
assertion with no vendor or independent corroboration** (B300 is a plan for end-2026), and
"**Models-as-a-Service**" is a **TM Forum standards work-stream** (MODaaS, initiative GB1087,
announced 2026-07-20, MT as founding contributor and co-chair) — **not a product in market**. Sources:
[[2026-07-16-mauritius-telecom-allmyt-keynote-sovereign-ai-platformafrica]],
[[2026-07-20-mauritius-telecom-tm-forum-modaas-telecomtv]].
