# Starlink–Airtel Direct-to-Cell Partnership

**Parties:** [[starlink]] / [[spacex]] and [[airtel-africa]]. **What:** a partnership to
deploy **Direct-to-Cell (D2C)** satellite connectivity and cellular backhaul, letting
standard LTE smartphones connect directly to satellites (no special hardware, no cell
tower) and linking isolated ground stations to Airtel's core network via satellite.
**Where/when:** across **all 14 Airtel Africa markets**, with the satellite-to-mobile
service set to **launch in 2026** (initially select text/data). Marks a shift from Starlink
as rival to Starlink as ally of incumbent telcos.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Licensing and scale (as of the Jan 2026 report).** By **May 2025, SpaceX had secured
  licences in 9 of Airtel's 14 markets**, with the remainder in process. Airtel Africa
  served **173.8m subscribers** (Sep 2025), 78.1m of them data users; H1 FY2026 (Apr–Sep
  2025) data revenue was **$1.16bn**. The partnership targets remote schools, clinics, rural
  businesses and network resilience during disasters. Airtel CEO **Sunil Taldar** framed it
  as reliable connectivity for "the most remote and currently underserved parts of Africa."
