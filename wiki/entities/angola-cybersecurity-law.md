# Angola Cybersecurity Bill (Proposta de Lei de Cibersegurança)

Angola's **draft cybersecurity law** — an Executive-initiated bill to set the legal regime for national cyberspace, critical infrastructure and network/information-system security, and to create a **National Cybersecurity Council** and **[[centro-nacional-de-ciberseguranca-angola]]**. As of the window's close (Jul 2026) it is **approved in general terms but stalled in committee**.

## Status & provisions

- **General approval — 23 Jan 2026, 105–1–75.** The National Assembly passed the bill in general terms; **UNITA's 75 abstentions** were explicitly grounded in fear of **election-day/protest-day internet shutdowns**, citing 2024 precedents in **Tanzania, Kenya and Uganda** and arguing the existing Law 7/17 could instead be amended. The bill's own text **concedes Angola sits near the bottom of the ITU Global Cybersecurity Index** ([[itu]]; Tier 4, 2024). Framed around "digital sovereignty," combating disinformation and institutional resilience; applies to digital-service providers established in Angola or with a local representative.
- **Suspended — 7–8 Jul 2026.** Committee-stage ("especialidade") discussion was **suspended, by unanimous agreement of the Executive and deputies**, over "inconformidades na redacção, na forma e no conteúdo" — specifically the preamble and chapters 1–2. UNITA warned a wholesale rewrite could force a fresh legislative track. Minister of Telecommunications **[[mario-oliveira]]** called it strategically important; the opposition warned that without strong judicial guarantees and independent oversight it risks becoming "an instrument of abusive surveillance and digital repression."
- **Track tension.** The bodies it would create overlap with those in the decree-track [[national-cybersecurity-strategy-angola]] (Dec 2025) — the relationship between the two is unresolved in sources held.
