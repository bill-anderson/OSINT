# Congo Digital Strategy 2030

The Republic of Congo's national digital strategy, built on four pillars — Infrastructure & Connectivity;
Digital Public Platforms (interoperable IDs & payments); Human Capital & Inclusion (250k youth trained);
Cybersecurity & Digital Trust. Backed by the World Bank-funded Digital Acceleration Project (PATN). See
[[COG]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
[[2026-01-01 Driving Digital Transformation of the Economy in the Republic of Congo]].

## Status & leadership (2025–26)

- **Awaiting decree (Oct 2025).** The strategy was at **general-secretariat review, awaiting decree**;
  benchmarks: **EGDI 166th (down from 161st in 2022), 0.3391; IDI 49.6 vs African average 56**. Source:
  [[2025-10-16-congo-nears-adoption-digital-strategy-2030-wearetech]].
- **New minister, nine-point roadmap (Jul 2026).** A **new ICT minister, Frédéric Malik Nguema Nzé**
  (superseding **Léon Juste Ibombo**, whom the wiki still carried), made ARPCE his "fer de lance," auditing
  it for five hours and setting a nine-point roadmap: cross-ministry digital-revenue mobilisation, a
  **citizen portal + DPI (with ADEN)**, Sopéco turnaround, postal addressing, and joint work on the 2027
  finance bill. Source: [[2026-07-14-nguema-nze-arpce-fer-de-lance-ldb]].
