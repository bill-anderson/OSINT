# DRC National Artificial Intelligence Strategy

The DRC's **first National AI Strategy**, launched for drafting (8 Oct 2025) alongside the **[[drc-national-digital-plan-pnn2|PNN2]]** — framed around technological sovereignty and leveraging the country's critical minerals as both an investment catalyst and an AI-solutions input.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Congolese Academy of AI planned (2025-10).** The Strategy provides for creating a **Congolese Academy
  of Artificial Intelligence** to train young talent, encourage applied research and stimulate local
  innovation — the human-capital arm of the sovereignty framing. Financing sits within the PNN2 **$1.5bn**
  envelope ($1bn public + $500m secured partners). See [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-11 La RDC prevoit 1,5 milliard $ de budget pour son Plan national du numerique et de l'IA 2026-2030 (Agence Ecofin)]].

- **AI already in the classroom (2025).** Ahead of the strategy, DRC **used AI to mark the 2024-25 Examen
  d'État**, put **e-Diplôme** live and consecrated distance learning (EAD) by arrêté — Raïssa Malu ([[adis-drc|ADIS]] 2025) arguing for **endogenous platforms over foreign ones**. And **[[bokeli-fogec|"Bokeli"]]**
  (FOGEC, live 3 Feb 2026, built by Congolese firm Quantum Vertex) is a **live AI-assisted state platform**
  generating bankable business plans. Sources:
  [[2025-11-27-adis-2025-raissa-malu-education-numerique-actualite]],
  [[2026-02-04-bokeli-plateforme-fogec-actualite]].
- **Skills ground-truth vs the goal.** Against the **250,000-youth** AI/cyber training goal, Femintech had
  trained ~1,000 girls total — this cohort was **41 brevets** — and practitioners called for
  **African-adapted AI regulation** ahead of the strategy. Sources:
  [[2025-11-05-femintech-41-brevets-ia-informatique-actualite]],
  [[2025-07-18-cadre-juridique-jeunes-derives-numeriques-actualite]].
- **$500m WB/AFD transformation + "AI Sphere" (2026-02).** A **$500m** World Bank/AFD digital-transformation
  programme adds **state-data migration, a government cybersecurity centre, and an "AI Sphere" for
  students** — the financing and skills scaffolding under the strategy. Source:
  [[2026-02-20-drc-500m-digital-transformation-world-bank-afd-techafricanews]].
- **Regional ambition + a Chinese cyber tie.** DRC claimed **regional digital-governance leadership**
  (Africa Tech Invest 2025) and showcased at OSIANE; separately, a **first DRC–China police-cooperation
  accord** (Beijing, 26 May 2026) names **cybercrime** as an explicit PNC capacity-building target, with a
  standing joint working group. Sources: [[2025-06-26-africa-tech-invest-2025-actualite]],
  [[2025-05-20-rdc-salon-osiane-9e-edition-actualite]], [[2025-05-21-forum-jeunes-gouvernance-internet-actualite]],
  [[2026-05-27-accord-cooperation-policiere-chine-cybercriminalite-actualite]].
