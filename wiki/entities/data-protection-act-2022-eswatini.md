# Eswatini Data Protection Act, 2022 (Act No. 5 of 2022)

**Eswatini's comprehensive data protection statute** — gazetted and effective
**4 March 2022** (Government Gazette Vol. LX No. 41), assented by Mswati III. It
governs the collection, processing, storage and disclosure of personal information, and
designates the sector regulator [[esccom]] as the **National Data Protection Agency
(s.5)**. Aligned by its drafters to regional standards, notably [[sadc]]. See [[SWZ]],
[[gov.protect]].

## Provisions

- **Scope.** Applies to data controllers and data processors operating in Eswatini or
  using its infrastructure; covers automated and non-automated processing. Reaches
  cross-border processors moving data in or out, and businesses outside Eswatini
  targeting Eswatini residents.
- **Data subject rights.** Access and the right to challenge accuracy; correction or
  deletion of outdated, irrelevant or unlawfully processed data; protection against
  decisions based solely on automated processing. **s.9(2)** stresses explicit consent.
- **Controller obligations.** Transparency; security measures; **s.17(1)** breach
  notification to [[esccom]] and the data subject where there are reasonable grounds to
  believe personal information has been accessed or acquired by an unauthorised person.
  Organisations must designate data protection officers.
- **Sensitive data.** Processing of sensitive personal data — health, race, religion,
  political beliefs, biometric and genetic data, children's data, criminal records — is
  prohibited unless explicitly authorised.
- **Trans-border flows.** Transfers within [[sadc]] Member States permitted under
  conditions; transfers to non-SADC countries require adequate protection. See
  [[gov.regional]].
- **Penalties.** Fines up to **E5,000,000 or 2% of annual turnover**; severe
  non-compliance may carry imprisonment, and violations can lead to civil remedies.
  [[apc]] identifies **s.6(3)(b)** as the administrative-fine power, putting five
  million Emalangeni at **~USD 268,000 (2024-03-11)** — a dated conversion, never to be
  repeated bare.
- **Exemptions.** Personal/household activity without public disclosure; fully
  de-identified information; state processing for national security, defence or public
  safety; purely journalistic, artistic or literary activity.
- **General.** Regulations for unsolicited electronic communications (**s.44**, right to
  halt direct marketing) and whistleblowing; support for class actions; transitional
  provisions.

## Contested points

- **Regulator as DPA.** The Act makes the communications regulator the privacy
  authority — a concentration [[apc]] treats as structural, and which the
  [[eswatini-economic-update-2025]] approaches from the other side, recommending
  mandates be clarified and a **Data Protection Office** fully operationalised
  (2025-07-31).
- **s.36.** [[apc]] flags the grounds it provides for non-action on filed complaints as
  a potential encroachment on rights (2024-03-11).
- **Process.** Tabled with a certificate of urgency alongside the other three 2022 Acts,
  shortening stakeholder and public input (2024-07-22).
- **A terminology mismatch to watch.** The gazetted text speaks of establishing a **Data
  Protection Commission**; [[esccom]]'s own mandate page and the ITLawCo explainer
  describe ESCCOM as the designated **Data Protection Agency (s.5)**. The gazette is the
  primary against which the secondary accounts should be verified.
