# ART / TRB (Telecommunications Regulatory Board)

Cameroon's **telecom regulator** (Director-General **Prof. Philémon Zoo Zame**). **TRB = ART**: the
English "Telecommunications Regulatory Board" and the French *Agence de Régulation des
Télécommunications* are the **same body** — do not treat as two entities. The wiki previously listed
ART as "mentioned, not hubbed"; it is now an active enforcer.

## Activity in this wiki

- **First real enforcement — Orange & MTN fined (Jul 2025).** Off Apr–May 2024 inspections, ART fined
  **CFA 2.6bn total** (Orange CFA 1.5bn + CFA 217m VAS; MTN CFA 1.1bn) for poor service and regulatory
  breaches. Source: [[2025-07-11-art-fines-orange-mtn-4-6-million-telecomreviewafrica]].
- **QoS instrumentation via PATNUC (Jan 2026).** The World Bank / [[patnuc]] handed the regulator
  **QoS-monitoring platforms** — the measurement base behind enforcement. Followed by **Douala field
  inspections (13–14 Apr 2026)** of Orange, MTN (IMS/VoLTE, 5G prep) and **IHS Cameroon (CFA 9.3bn
  invested in 2025** on passive infrastructure), with energy named as the resilience constraint. Sources:
  [[2026-01-07-trb-receives-qos-monitoring-platforms-patnuc-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-04-16-art-field-inspections-qos-coverage-douala-techafricanews]].
- **2024 Annual Observatory (standing dataset).** ART's annual telecom-market observatory reports the
  sector **grew 18% in 2024**: revenue **CFA 1,022bn (+18%)**, investment CFA 194.5bn (+35%), **3.16% of
  GDP**, 31.5m mobile subscriptions, mobile-financial-services revenue **+146%**; corroborates Orange >
  MTN share and Camtel's ~12,000 km fibre. Cited as a recurring reference dataset, not a one-off. Source:
  [[2025-12-09-art-2024-annual-observatory-telecom-market-techafricanews]].
- **Device certification & IMEI groundwork (Nov 2025).** Issued device-certification guidelines — the
  pre-enforcement baseline for the later IMEI device-registry regime (see [[camcis]] / the e-gov thread).
  Source: [[2025-11-10-trb-device-certification-guidelines-digital-safety-techafricanews]].
- **Regional — ARTAC (Feb 2026).** Zoo Zame led Cameroon's delegation to the **[[artac]]** 10th Ordinary
  Session (Kinshasa), which adopted a Central African **data-residency / NGSO** position. Source:
  [[2026-02-24-trb-artac-10th-ordinary-session-kinshasa-ngso-techreviewafrica]].
- **Funding its own research base (Feb 2026).** Established an **Academic Chair in Digital Governance** at
  **[[university-yaounde-ii]]** — steering committee explicitly preserving academic freedom. Source:
  [[2026-02-11-university-yaounde-ii-art-digital-governance-chair-techafricanews]].
