# USAID

The US Agency for International Development. It matters here as a funder whose withdrawal exposed how dependent African national data systems had become on external support.

## Activity in this wiki
- The January-2025 US aid freeze left more than two-thirds of national statistical offices reporting funding cuts.
- The USAID-funded DHS (Demographic and Health Surveys) Program had 90% of its contracts cancelled in 2025.
- Health-data systems in Ethiopia, Kenya and Zambia were disrupted.
- The shock triggered a pivot toward data sovereignty and DPI — South Africa joining the Digital Public Goods Alliance / 50-in-5, and Senegal's New Deal Technologique.
- **2025 cuts shut PEPFAR's DATIM (2025-04-03).** The Jan-2025 aid freeze and USAID wind-down turned off PEPFAR's global HIV database DATIM overnight and cost Kenya's Ministry of Health access to its national EMR (March 2025); a HISP survey found locally-owned [[dhis2]] systems far more resilient than these US-funded parallel systems. See [[hisp-centre]], [[KEN]]. Source: [[2025-04-03 Now is the time to invest in locally-owned health data systems]].
- **America First Global Health Strategy replaces USAID (2026-03).** The Trump administration folded USAID and restructured PEPFAR into the [[america-first-global-health-strategy]], under which 26 bilateral health MOUs — bundling data-sharing (10-yr post-termination US access to national surveillance/lab databases) and specimen-sharing terms — were signed with African governments. See [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]], [[africa-cdc]]. Source: [[2026-03-19 African Digital Health Data Is a Condition of Global Health Funding]].
- **Zimbabwe rejects successor funding (2026-02-25).** Zimbabwe walked away from a $367m five-year US health MOU (the USAID-successor framework), calling it "clearly unbalanced" and citing sovereignty over data and pathogen sharing. See [[zimbabwe-us-health-mou]], [[ZWE]]. Source: [[2026-02-25 Zimbabwe Turns Down U.S. Health Funding, Citing Sovereignty Concerns]].
- **Zambia MoU controversy (2026-02-25).** Zambia was offered a $1.012bn five-year MoU under the USAID-successor [[america-first-global-health-strategy]] — with 10-year health-data access and reported mining conditions — that would cut total 2026 health funding to $320m (from $367m for HIV alone in 2025); civil-society groups called it "shameless exploitation" and the health minister was fired after denying the mining link. See [[zambia-us-health-mou]], [[ZMB]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source: [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]].
- **Legacy HIS footprint (background).** USAID's PRISM (Performance of Routine Information System Management) framework is a standard tool for evaluating African health information systems, and USAID was a long-standing development partner in Tanzania's HMIS ecosystem. See [[hmis-tanzania-pea]], [[his-developing-countries-review]].

- **IATI reporting idiosyncrasy (PWYF, 2026-07).** In [[publish-what-you-fund]]'s test of an
  LLM agent against [[international-aid-transparency-initiative]] data, USAID's
  **transaction-level sector coding** was the kind of publisher-specific quirk the model missed —
  a concrete illustration that USAID's aid data carries idiosyncrasies requiring expert
  interpretation. See [[data.open]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-02 Can AI solve open aid data's access problem]].
