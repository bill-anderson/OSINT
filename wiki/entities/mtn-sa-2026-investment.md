# MTN South Africa R21.8bn ($1.3bn) investment (to 2028)

[[mtn-group]]'s pledge to invest R21.8 billion (~$1.3 billion) in South African ICT
infrastructure and IT systems over three years to end-2028, announced at the 6th South Africa
Investment Conference.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Pledge announced (~2026-04).** At the 6th South Africa Investment Conference in Sandton,
  [[mtn-group]] committed R21.8bn ($1.3bn) over three years to expand network infrastructure in
  [[ZAF]] — the third-largest pledge of the conference, which drew 65 projects totalling R113.5bn
  ($6.75bn). MTN SA (led by CEO Ferdi Moolman) had spent R6.8bn (~$404m) on its network in 2025
  and serves more than 40 million South African subscribers. Group CEO [[ralph-mupita]] leads the
  wider MTN Group (300m+ customers). Tags [[infra.connect]], [[finance.new]], [[infra.capacity]].
  Source: [[2026-04-01 MTN to invest $1.3B in South Africa by 2028]].

- **AI data-centre ambition (context).** MTN is also entering the AI data-centre race, with
  [[ZAF]] and Nigeria as priority markets — including the 4.5MW Sifiso Dabengwa data centre in
  Lagos. (See [[ralph-mupita]] and [[mtn-group]] for the broader platform/AI strategy.)
