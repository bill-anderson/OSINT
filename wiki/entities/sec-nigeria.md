# Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC, Nigeria)

Nigeria's capital-market regulator and, since the **Investments and Securities Act 2025**, the
country's **primary licensing authority for digital assets**. The wiki holds it chiefly as the
securities-side pole of Nigeria's virtual-asset regime, opposite the **[[cbn]]** on the payments
side.

## Digital assets became securities (2025)

The **ISA 2025** recognises digital assets as securities and gives the SEC authority over their
**issuance, trading and promotion** — the clearest regulatory framework Nigeria has given the
sector. The Commission can monitor securities exchanges, conduct audits and impose penalties.
Source: [[2025-05-09-isa-2025-crypto-securities]].

The Act brings **VASPs, Digital Asset Operators (DAOPs) and Digital Asset Exchanges (DAXs)** under
SEC oversight. Non-compliant VASPs face **₦10 million** in the first month of default and **₦1
million** for each subsequent month, alongside licence suspension or revocation. Source:
[[2026-01-12-tin-nin-crypto-tax-carf]].

## Enforcement is inter-agency, not solo

- **Wallet freezing (2025-11-03).** DG **Emomotimi Agama** disclosed that the SEC is working with
  the **[[cbn]]** and the **EFCC** to track and freeze illicit digital wallets tied to money
  laundering and crypto scams, against **over ₦174bn** lost to fraudulent schemes. Source:
  [[2025-11-03-sec-cbn-efcc-freeze-illicit-wallets]].
- **Virtual Asset Council (2026-07-20).** Under the
  [[virtual-assets-executive-order-2026|Executive Order on Virtual Assets Coordination]] the SEC
  sits as **vice-chair** alongside the **[[nigeria-revenue-service|NRS]]**, under a **[[cbn]]**
  chair. Registration follows the activity: **securities-like activity registers with the SEC**,
  while payment, settlement and custody of non-security virtual assets register with the CBN.
  Source: [[2026-07-20-nigeria-coordinated-regulatory-framework-virtual-assets]].

## Licensing in practice

The SEC's **Regulatory Incubation Programme** was the route by which **Busha** listed the
private-sector naira stablecoin **cNGN** (Wrapped CBDC Ltd) in **February 2025**; Busha's later
cross-border stablecoin work is described by the parties as built on its **SEC-licensed digital
asset foundation**. Sources: [[2025-02-10-busha-lists-cngn-stablecoin]],
[[2026-07-20-busha-business-tether-stablecoin-infrastructure-africa]].
