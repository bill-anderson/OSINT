# PayServices–DRC banking-modernisation dispute

**Parties.** [[payservices]] (Idaho-based US fintech) v. the Democratic Republic
of the Congo ([[COD]], President Félix Tshisekedi's administration) and several
senior officials.

**What.** A contract from late 2023 for PayServices to digitise state-linked
financial operations — modernising the Caisse Générale d'Épargne du Congo,
rolling out electronic government payments, and building a national digital
payments platform for public salaries, social transfers and merchant
transactions, meant to "lay the foundation for a cash-light Congolese economy."

**When.** Contracted late 2023; suit filed in US federal court ~January 2026
(report published 2026-01-19).

**Amount / claims.** PayServices seeks damages that could run to hundreds of
millions, with some filings citing up to $4bn (headline figure ~$400m). It says
it invested more than $72m while awaiting an agreed $20m state contribution that
was never paid, and that the project collapsed after it refused bribe demands
from senior officials.

**DRC position.** The Ministry of Public Enterprises denies the claims as
"devoid of any legal, budgetary, or accounting basis," insisting the documents
signed were a non-binding memorandum of understanding.

**Status (as of 2026-01).** Live in US federal court. The dispute risks
straining US–DRC relations as Kinshasa courts Washington on security and
strategic-mineral cooperation ([[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[finance.new]],
[[geopol.usa]]). Source: [[2026-01-19 US fintech sues DR Congo for $400m over failed banking deal]].
