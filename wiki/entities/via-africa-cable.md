# Via Africa (subsea cable)

[[orange]]-led consortium building a >20,000km Atlantic submarine corridor connecting
West Africa to Europe — new [[infra.connect]] capacity that also deepens the
continent's [[geopol.eu]] connectivity dependence.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Announced at the Africa Forward Summit, Nairobi (2026-05-12).** First unveiled at
  the summit — with Emmanuel Macron present — and reported more widely on 2026-05-23.
  Expected landings in [[NGA]], [[SEN]], Guinea ([[GIN]]), [[CIV]] and Mauritania
  ([[MRT]]), with more to be added, each cable terminating at major data centres.
  Source: [[2026-05-23 Orange-backed subsea cable to connect Nigeria, 19 other countries]].
- **Build timeline and open questions ([[finance.new]]).** A 3–4 year build is expected
  once the consortium is finalised; total capacity is undisclosed. Context: Africa had
  ~77 active or planned cable systems in 2025, and [[meta]]'s 2Africa runs to 45,000km,
  against which Via Africa's >20,000km sits.
  Source: [[2026-05-23 Orange-backed subsea cable to connect Nigeria, 19 other countries]].
