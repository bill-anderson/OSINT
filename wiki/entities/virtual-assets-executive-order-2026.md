# Presidential Executive Order on Virtual Assets Coordination, 2026 (Nigeria)

Signed by **President Bola Ahmed Tinubu** under **section 5 of the Constitution of the Federal
Republic of Nigeria, 1999 (as altered)**, and **effective immediately** on **20 July 2026**. Its
stated object is *coordination, not regulation*: it explicitly **does not create a new regulator and
does not transfer powers between agencies** — each institution retains its full statutory mandate
and independence.

## What it establishes

- **A Virtual Asset Council** — chaired by the **[[cbn]]**, with the
  **[[nigeria-revenue-service|NRS]]** and the **[[sec-nigeria|SEC]]** as vice-chairs and the
  **[[nfiu|NFIU]]** and **[[onsa-nigeria|ONSA]]** as members. It sets policy direction and works with
  the Attorney-General of the Federation toward a harmonised legal and institutional framework.
- **A Virtual Asset Office** — the Council's operational body, **secretariat domiciled at the CBN**,
  running day-to-day information sharing, applications and reporting across an **integrated
  supervisory technology platform** that gives shared visibility while **each agency keeps ownership
  and control of its own data**.
- **Registration by activity, not by institution.** Securities-like activity registers with the
  **SEC**; payment, settlement, custody and related services involving **non-security** virtual
  assets register with the **CBN**; the Council resolves any case where responsibility cannot be
  readily determined.
- **A CBN regulatory sandbox** for virtual assets — a controlled environment in which eligible
  operators test products, services and blockchain solutions under supervision, so the agencies can
  assess implications for monetary sovereignty, financial stability, market integrity, consumer
  protection, financial inclusion and revenue administration before wider release. Detail deferred
  to the CBN.
- **An NRS tax policy for the sector**, operationalising existing tax law as it applies to virtual
  assets.

## The diagnosis it rests on

The Order's own framing is of a **fragmented** environment in which virtual assets blur the lines
between currencies, money, commodities and securities; agencies operate in silos, overlapping in
some areas and leaving gaps in others; and unregistered or fraudulent operators exploit the seams —
exposing Nigeria to money laundering, terrorism financing, cybersecurity and data-privacy threats,
fraud and revenue loss.
