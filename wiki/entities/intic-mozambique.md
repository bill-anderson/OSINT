# INTIC (National Institute of Information and Communication Technologies, Mozambique)

Mozambique's national ICT institute (Instituto Nacional das Tecnologias de
Informação e Comunicação), chaired by **Lourino Chemane**, acting as the
country's personal-data-protection regulator. Distinct from
[[atdi-mozambique]], the separate digital-transformation agency.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Data-breach reporting platform (~2026-02).** INTIC established a **pilot
  platform for reporting personal data breaches**, unveiled on the sidelines of
  a webinar on "Digital Literacy in Mozambique." The regulator framed it as part
  of consolidating the national **personal-data-protection regime**, giving data
  subjects a simple, safe and accessible way to submit complaints and holding
  data-processing agents accountable. See [[MOZ]], [[gov.protect]],
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-06 Mozambique intros data breach platform]].

- **First digital-operator licences — Electronic Transactions Law (2026-06).** [[gov.legislate]]
  [[tech.industry]] The government issued its **first licences to intermediate electronic-service
  providers and digital-platform operators** (Maputo, 8 Jun) under the **Electronic Transactions Law** —
  a registration/supervision/licensing regime (fee = one minimum wage + 1% of annual revenue thereafter),
  formalising the digital economy. Source: [[2026-06-08-issues-first-licenses-digital-operators-techafricanews]].
- **National Data Governance Policy validation (2025-12).** [[gov.policy]] [[data.open]] [[sovereignty]]
  INTIC ran the workshop validating Mozambique's **National Data Governance Policy, Strategy & Action Plan**
  — treating **data as a strategic national asset**, aligned to the **AU Data Policy Framework (2022)** and
  embedded in the Five-Year Government Programme (2025–2029) (INTIC chair Lourino Chemane). Source:
  [[2025-12-05-prioritizes-data-governance-digital-sovereignty-techafricanews]].
- **Draft National AI Strategy.** [[tech.ai]] Chemane presented Mozambique's draft AI strategy at the ITU
  "AI for Good" workshop (GITEX Kenya, May 2026). See [[mozambique--tech-ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-25-presents-draft-national-ai-strategy-itu-techafricanews]].
