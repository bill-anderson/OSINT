# DPA Southern Africa (Pty) Ltd

**The South African-registered 50:50 joint venture between [[distributed-power-africa]] and the French utility [[edf]] — and the actual counterparty to both [[africa-data-centres]] solar-wheeling power purchase agreements.** It is *not* DPA itself, and the distinction is load-bearing: it is what separates the South African solar history from the Zimbabwe-registered company trading as [[econet-infraco]].

## Identity

- **South African**-registered, formed on EDF's acquisition of a **50% shareholding in DPA's South African operations** (announced **2022-05-24**).
- CEO **Nawfal El Fadil** (as of 2024-04).
- Trades as **"DPA SA"**. Trade-press accounts routinely collapse it into "Distributed Power Africa"; prefer the announcer's own account. See [[distributed-power-africa]] for the full three-company split under the DPA brand.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The 20-year, 12 MW PPA (announced 2023-03-14).** DPA SA contracted to supply **12 MW** of renewable solar to Africa Data Centres' South African facilities on a **20-year** term, wheeled across the **[[eskom]]** grid rather than built behind the meter, partly from a solar farm **near Bloemfontein**. ADC said it would supply **"over 30% of our South African data centres"** with renewables. Source: [[2023-03-14-cassava-adc-dpa-sa-edf-20-year-ppa]].
- **The Free State solar farm (2024-11-20).** A second **20-year PPA** with ADC to establish a solar farm in the **Free State**, powering ADC's **Cape Town** data centres and to extend eventually to Johannesburg — the uplift that took three South African sites to **50 MW**. Source: [[2024-11-20 Africa Data Centres expands by 20 megawatts to meet cloud, AI demand]].
- **What the wheeling powers (as of 2024-07).** **CPT1**, the wheeled-solar-supplied Cape Town facility, went from **5.5 MW to 11.5 MW** of IT load on a **6 MW** uplift. ADC's claim to the **first successful solar wheeling in Africa** attaches to this lineage — *a company claim, not an independently verified first*. Source: [[2024-07-25 Africa Data Centres adding 6MW capacity in Cape Town]].
