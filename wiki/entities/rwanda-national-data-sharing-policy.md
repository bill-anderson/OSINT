# Rwanda National Data Sharing Policy

Rwanda’s public-sector data-sharing policy — the **National Data Sharing Policy between
Government Agencies**, proposed by **MINICT** and drafted through the Rwanda Economy
Digitalisation programme, and **approved by Cabinet in 2025**. It positions government
data as **open by default** (subject to classification and access controls) and
institutions as **stewards, not owners**. It is anchored to the **Second National
Strategy for Transformation (NST2)** and **Vision 2050**, and requires compliance with
Rwanda's **Data Protection and Privacy Law (DPPL)**. Operationalised through the Data
Governance Unit at the [[nisr]].

## Structure and split mandate

- **Governance vs technology split.** Implementation is coordinated by MINICT, with
  execution split: **[[nisr]]** leads data-governance (via a **Data Governance Unit** and
  a **Technical Subcommittee**) — data standards, unified national identifiers, ISO
  code conformance, classification guidelines, metadata management, retention/disposal
  — while **[[rwanda-information-society-authority]]** (RISA) builds and maintains the
  **Data Sharing Platform** for secure cross-institutional exchange. Source:
  [[2025-10-27 Introduction to the Rwanda National Data Sharing Policy]].
- **Seven annexes** operationalise it: draft ToRs for the Data Governance Unit and the
  Technical Subcommittee, a Data Sharing Application Form, a Data Sharing Agreement, a
  high-level process overview, the **Five Safes** data-sharing framework, entity
  responsibilities, and a data-sharing technology platform. Obligations fall on both
  data **Requestors** and **Providers** (nominated approvers, due diligence, signed
  agreements; strict DPPL compliance for personal data). Sources:
  [[2025-10-27 Introduction to the Rwanda National Data Sharing Policy]],
  [[2025-06-09 Rwanda charts a new course for government data sharing]].
- **Regional context.** Placed alongside Kenya's Data Sharing Code and the national
  data strategies/policies of Nigeria, Senegal and South Africa as part of a wider
  African move to formalise inter-governmental data sharing. Source:
  [[2025-06-09 Rwanda charts a new course for government data sharing]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Implementation phase (2026-05).** Requires institutions to catalogue and classify datasets, set
  access rights, and staff internal data teams (chief data officers, stewards, custodians) before
  sharing via a central platform. The hard shift is behavioural — ownership to stewardship. See
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-07 What will determine the success of Rwanda’s data governance push]].
