# Tanzania e-Government Strategy 2022 (2022-2027)

Tanzania's **second national e-Government Strategy**, published by the **President's Office –
Public Service Management and Good Governance (PO-PSMGG)** for implementation **2022–2027**. It
**replaces the e-Government Strategy 2013**, which ran 2013–2018 and expired — leaving a gap of
several years between the lapse of the first strategy and the arrival of the second. Organised
around **seven strategic pillars** and built on a stakeholder survey of **379 respondents across
38 institutions**. Not to be confused with Uganda's e-GIF and Digital Government Strategy.

**Institutional lineage cited as predecessor achievements:** the **e-Government Act 2019** and
its **2020 Regulations**, and the upgrade of the **e-Government Agency into a fully-fledged
[[ega-tanzania]] (e-Government Authority) in 2019**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **The state's own diagnosis of why services don't join up (2022).** The Strategy names its
  constraints plainly: **non-interoperable service delivery systems**, **unharmonised business
  processes**, **limited GovNet bandwidth**, **inadequate data centre resources**, **rising
  cyber-attacks**, and **ICT skills gaps**. Interoperability leads the list — not connectivity —
  which is a useful corrective to the assumption that the binding constraint on African
  e-government is bandwidth. That diagnosis rhymes with the fragmentation documented elsewhere in
  **[[TZA]]**'s health information systems (18 systems; see the HMIS political-economy analysis
  on the place hub). See [[dpi.exchange]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[infra.store]],
