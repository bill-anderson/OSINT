# US International Development Finance Corporation (DFC)

US government development-finance institution; a vehicle for American digital-
infrastructure financing in Africa positioned against Chinese capital.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Africa Data Centres (2026 reporting).** Provided a **$300m** financing arrangement to
  ADC ([[cassava-technologies]]), which the DFC frames "explicitly as a counterweight to
  Chinese infrastructure financing on the continent." See [[geopol.usa]],
  [[geopol.china]], [[infra.store]].
- **Cassava $90m equity round (2024-12).** DFC joined **[[finnfund]]** and **[[google]]**
  (a direct stake) in a **$90m** equity round into **[[cassava-technologies]]** — part of a
  **$310m** raise for data centres, fibre and AI infrastructure across Africa. See
  [[finance.new]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2024-12-17 Google joins $90M investment into Cassava to bolster Africa's digital infrastructure]].

- **Retooled as an economic-security instrument (IISS, 2026-01).** An IISS analysis notes the
  DFC (with EXIM) is being repurposed to back Trump's bilateral **critical-minerals** pacts —
  the third track alongside domestic America-First deals and the Dec-2025 [[pax-silica]]
  coalition — with **DRC** ([[COD]]) among the bilateral partners (minerals linked to security
  guarantees). See [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 US critical-minerals diplomacy from America-First deals to Pax Silica]].
