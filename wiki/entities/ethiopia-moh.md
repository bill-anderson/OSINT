# Ethiopia Ministry of Health

The federal ministry responsible for Ethiopia's health system and its health information
infrastructure ([[dpi.mis]]). It operates the national **[[dhis2]]** deployment (introduced
**2018**) and the community-level **[[echis-ethiopia]]** system, framed by the ministry's
**Digital Health Strategy** and the **HSTP-II Information Revolution** pillar
([[gov.policy]]). **USAID's Digital Health Activity** is a named partner ([[usaid]]).

## Activity in this wiki

- **DHIS2 maturity (2024 assessment).** The ministry's national DHIS2 implementation scores
  at the **"defined" stage (2.81)** against a **"managed" (4.09) 2025 target**, with ICT
  infrastructure the weakest dimension and interoperability/data-use only emerging. The
  assessment flags the absence of a national digital ID / client registry ([[dpi.id]]) as an
  interoperability gap — the layer later addressed by [[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]. See
  [[dhis2-maturity-ethiopia]]. Source:
  [[2024-07-26 Maturity Assessment of District Health Information System Version 2 Implementation in Ethiopia Current Status and Improvement Pathways]].
- **eCHIS for HEWs (2022–23).** The ministry runs [[echis-ethiopia]], the offline-first
  mobile system for ~40,000 Health Extension Workers, as part of its "going digital" plan;
  adoption is constrained by rural connectivity, power and training gaps. See
  [[echis-ethiopia-study]]. Source:
  [[2023-06-07 Barriers, facilitators and motivators of electronic community health information system use among health workers in Ethiopia]].
