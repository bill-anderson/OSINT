# Africa AI Council

A continental **AI governance and innovation body** created in two dated stages under the **[[smart-africa]]**
umbrella, to give "strategic, actionable recommendations" for an "AI-driven future rooted in African values"
(Smart Africa CEO Lacina Koné).

- **Endorsed 4 April 2025** by the Kigali **[[africa-ai-declaration|Africa AI Declaration]]**: an Africa AI
  Council **under the Smart Africa Steering Committee, co-chaired by the [[african-union|African Union
  Commission]] and the [[itu|ITU]]**, to align continental and global digital-transformation efforts. Source:
  [[2025-04-04 Africa Declaration on Artificial Intelligence]].
- **Formally established 17 November 2025** by the Smart Africa Board at Transform Africa Summit, Conakry
  ([[GIN]]): **15 members named — seven African ICT ministers** (Algeria's Sid Ali Zerrouki, Chad's Boukar
  Michel, Kenya's William Kabogo, Nigeria's Bosun Tijani, Rwanda's Paula Ingabire, Togo's Cina Lawson,
  Zimbabwe's Tatenda Mavetera) and **eight independent members** (Gates Foundation, InstaDeep, Orange MEA,
  Masakhane, Microsoft, Univ. of Pretoria/Lelapa AI, Tunisie Haut Débit, Google). Balanced across **six
  thematic areas — Infrastructure, Data, Market, Talent, Investment, Governance** — with selection **led by
  Smart Africa, the AUC and the ITU** from **400+ applications across 57 nationalities**. The **Smart Africa
  Board** that unveiled it is **chaired by Rwanda's President Kagame** and comprises 42 heads of state, the AU
  Commissioner for Energy & Infrastructure and the ITU Secretary-General — i.e. Kagame chairs the *Board* over
  the Council, which the founding Declaration puts under **AUC/ITU co-chairs**. Source:
  [[2025-11-17-smart-africa-inaugural-africa-ai-council]] (Smart Africa's own release; the Nov-2025
  establishment also reported by [[2025-11-18-smart-africa-ai-dpi-initiatives-single-market]]).

*The July-2026 "Africa launches its first AI council" framing (an Infos-IT rewrite parked in `_leads/`, no
event date of its own) is a **re-report of this same November-2025 Conakry establishment** — identical detail
(42 heads of state, AUC + ITU, 400 applications / 57 nationalities), its 2026-07-21 a publication date, not a
new body, formalisation or later meeting. No contradiction (resolved 2026-07-22).*
