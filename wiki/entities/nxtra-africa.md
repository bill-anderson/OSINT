# Nxtra Africa (Nxtra by Airtel)

**[[airtel-africa]]'s data-centre arm** — the vehicle for Airtel's announced entry into the
African data-centre market, offering managed services for enterprises, corporates, banking
and finance, hyperscalers, government, content providers and international carriers. CEO:
**Yashnath Issur**.

## Read this page with its provenance

The only source held is **marked `(sponsored)`** — marketing-adjacent content, not
independent reporting. It is admissible (a named CEO on the record) but it is **the
company's account of its own plans: every figure below is a corporate projection, not an
achievement. Do not state Nxtra "will" build anything as fact.**

## Activity in this wiki

- **Announced plans (as of 2024-12-13).** [[airtel-africa]] — present in **14 countries**
  with **over 160 million subscribers** — announced entry into African data centres via
  Nxtra. In India, Nxtra **already runs 200+ MW** of data-centre capacity; Nxtra Africa
  aims to replicate this, *"democratizing cloud services"* in Africa beyond South Africa.
  Nxtra **plans total capacity of up to 180 MW** across multiple African countries.
  **Construction has started on a 40+ MW data center in Lagos and another in Nairobi** —
  named as the larger regional hubs — **followed by Mombasa, Dar, Kampala, Kinshasa and
  Kigali**. Airtel Africa already owns **over 77,000 km of fibre** plus submarine cable
  assets. New-build design targets **PUE below 1.4**, with modular design for AI workloads
  and multi-layer physical security. See [[infra.store]], [[geopol.india]]. Source:
  [[2024-12-13 Nxtra - Airtel's leap into Africa's growing data center market]].

## Uganda — the honest ceiling

**Uganda appears only in passing lists.** Issur names Uganda among "high-potential
countries" that *"will emerge as important Edge locations in the future"*, and Kampala
appears in the build order after Lagos, Nairobi, Mombasa and Dar. **No site, capacity
figure or date is given.** The most this supports for [[UGA]] is: **Kampala named as a
future Nxtra Edge site (as of 2024-12-13).**
