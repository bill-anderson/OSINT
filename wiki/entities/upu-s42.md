# UPU S42 (international addressing standard)

The **[[upu]]**'s **international addressing standard** — the technical specification against
which national postal addressing systems are structured and **certified**. S42 is the sort of
instrument that rarely surfaces in digital-governance debate yet quietly determines whether a
country's address data can interoperate with anything beyond its own borders.

**Why it belongs in this wiki.** An address registry is DPI in all but name: it is the layer
that binds a person to a place, and therefore the precondition for delivery, service targeting,
licensing and taxation. S42 is where that layer meets a **global standards regime** — and, per
**[[papu]]**, where a national registry becomes a continental benchmark.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Tanzania certified; named a continental benchmark (2026-02-09).** **[[papu]]** Secretary
  General **Dr Sifundo Chief Moyo** told Tanzania's Annual Residential Address Conference:
  **"Tanzania's achievements in addressing, including its Universal Postal Union (UPU) S42
  certification, set a benchmark for the continent,"** encouraging other African nations to adopt
  **harmonised addressing standards**. He argued that without reliable and harmonised addressing,
  **e-commerce cannot scale**, delivery networks remain unreliable, and **AfCFTA cannot reach its
  full potential** — a continental trade agenda resting on an addressing standard. See
  [[napa-tanzania]], [[gov.standards]], [[gov.regional]], [[TZA]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Tanzania registers 12 million housing addresses to boost digital governance]].
