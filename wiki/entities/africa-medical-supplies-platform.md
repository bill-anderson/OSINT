# Africa Medical Supplies Platform

The Africa Medical Supplies Platform (AMSP, amsp.africa) is an African-designed procurement marketplace launched around June 2020 by AU chair [[cyril-ramaphosa]]. It matters as a continental DPI case: pooling demand to counter pandemic-era supply inequities.

## Profile
- Not-for-profit centralized e-commerce/procurement marketplace, modelled on Jumia/Amazon/Alibaba, pooling continental demand for COVID-19 test kits, PPE and clinical devices across Africa's 54–55 states.
- [[african-union]] / Africa CDC allocated supply by a population and disease-burden formula.
- Afreximbank guaranteed payment via its **$3bn PATIMFA** (Pandemic Trade Impact Mitigation Facility, Board-approved 20 Mar 2020), of which **$200m was reserved** for AMSP-related food and medical-supply production and trade. *(REV-003 resolved 2026-07-16: $3bn is the primary Afreximbank figure, and matches [[strive-masiyiwa|Masiyiwa]]'s own written account ([[2020-07-01 Strive Masiyiwa. Best practices from Africa Collaboration in the time of COVID-19]]). The stray **$3.8bn** traces to a single verbal remark in his Milken interview ([[2020-06-01 Interview with AU Special Envoy Strive Masiyiwa on the Launch of the Africa Medical Supplies Platform  Milken Institute]]) — an uncorroborated slip, not a rival estimate.)*
- Built by African tech teams: Fatoumata Ba (Janngo); Oswald Jumira (Vaya Africa); Precious Lunga (Baobab Circle).
- Secured 200M+ facemasks per month and offered a "Made-in-Africa" supplier option.
- [[strive-masiyiwa]] led it as AU Special Envoy.
