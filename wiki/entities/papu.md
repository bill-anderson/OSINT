# Pan-African Postal Union (PAPU)

The **African Union's specialised agency for postal services** — a continental body coordinating
postal policy and standards across African states, headquartered in Arusha, Tanzania. Secretary
General: **Dr Sifundo Chief Moyo** (as of 2026-02). As a bloc-level body, PAPU is held here as
an **entity**, not a place.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Addressing as the precondition for e-commerce and AfCFTA (2026-02-09).** At Tanzania's Annual
  Residential Address Conference and Postal Services Technology Exhibition, Secretary General
  **Dr Sifundo Chief Moyo** argued that **without reliable and harmonised addressing systems,
  e-commerce cannot scale, delivery networks remain unreliable, and the African Continental Free
  Trade Area (AfCFTA) cannot reach its full potential**. This is a notable framing: the
  continental trade agenda is made to depend on a mundane national registry layer — addresses —
  rather than on tariffs or corridors. See [[gov.regional]], [[dpi.registry]],
  [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Tanzania registers 12 million housing addresses to boost digital governance]].
- **Tanzania named a continental benchmark (2026-02-09).** **"Tanzania's achievements in
  addressing, including its Universal Postal Union (UPU) S42 certification, set a benchmark for
  the continent,"** Moyo said, encouraging other African nations to adopt **harmonised addressing
  standards**. See [[upu-s42]], [[upu]], [[napa-tanzania]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-09 Tanzania registers 12 million housing addresses to boost digital governance]].
