# Ministry of ICT and National Guidance (Uganda)

Uganda's ICT ministry — the policy principal above **[[nita-uganda]]** (implementation, architecture,
interoperability) and **[[uganda-communications-commission]]** (sector regulation). Its remit pairs
information and communications technology with **national guidance**, the state's public-communication
function; the same portfolio therefore holds broadband and digital-inclusion policy alongside
government messaging.

## Leadership

- **Minister: Rt Hon. Justine Kasule Lumumba**, **newly appointed as of July 2026** — described as
  such when [[airtel-uganda]]'s MD paid a courtesy visit on **2026-07-17**. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-airtel-uganda-ict-minister-meeting]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Operator engagement on digital inclusion (2026-07-17).** Received an [[airtel-uganda]] delegation
  led by MD/CEO Soumendra Sahu. The ministry framed the meeting as part of ongoing government–industry
  engagement, with discussion focused on **digital inclusion, innovation and expanding access to
  technology-enabled services** — and, specifically, on **increasing smartphone access** as a
  precondition for online learning, digital financial services and entrepreneurship. No commitment,
  target or funding figure was announced by either side. Sources:
  [[2026-07-17-airtel-uganda-advances-digital-inclusion-talks]],
  [[2026-07-17-airtel-uganda-ict-minister-meeting]].
