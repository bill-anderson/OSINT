# ONECI (National Office for Civil Registration and Identification, Guinea)

Guinea's civil-registration and identification authority — the body operating the modernised civil registry and the biometric national-ID pipeline built on **[[mosip]]** and **[[tech5]]** infrastructure, on a "one citizen, one identity" principle ([[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]]). (Distinct from Côte d'Ivoire's [[oneci|ONECI]].) It works with the Ministry of Territorial Administration and Decentralization, and inherits a very weak baseline — only 45-60% of Guineans held birth certificates and <2% of marriages/deaths were registered (2023). See [[GIN]].

## Activity in this wiki

- **Biometric birth certificates in Sanoyah (2026-03-02).** [[dpi.registry]] Sanoyah commune began issuing **biometric birth certificates** and enrolling the **biometric national ID card** locally (phase 1: 2,000 certificates), extending civil registration into the home commune. Base documents: prior birth certificate, court supplementary judgment, or a **RAVEC** document; documents from private data centres are rejected. The biometric birth certificate is the **foundational document** for the national biometric ID card. Source: [[2026-03-04 Guinea Launches Biometric Birth Certificates in Sanoyah in Civil Registry Modernization Push]].
- **WURI exit → domestic pivot (context, 2024-25).** [[dpi.id]] The World Bank **WURI-Guinea** foundational-ID project **closed early (28 June 2024)** with only ~8,000 unique IDs issued; Guinea instead legislated a new personal-identification law + a civil-registry law (Oct 2023) underpinning the **RAVEC** administrative census. Source: [[2025-05-02 WURI Implementation Status Report - Cote d'Ivoire and Guinea (P161329 Seq 11)]].
- **Enabel/Belgium civil-registry pilot (baseline, 2023).** [[dpi.registry]] An EU-funded, Enabel-supported pilot across 10 communes digitised the civil registry (105,000+ records migrated), with DXC Technology building modules for records security, court-decision harmonisation and automatic birth notification — drawing on Rwanda and Belgium. Source: [[2023-08-03 The modernisation of Guinea's civil registry inspired by Belgium's experience (Citizenship Rights in Africa)]].
