# Angola Law against False Information on the Internet ("fake news" law)

Angola's **anti-"fake news" statute** (Lei contra Informações Falsas na Internet) — **passed definitively on 21 May 2026** with **105 votes** from the MPLA, PRS and PHA, **UNITA voting against**. An Executive initiative submitted alongside the [[angola-cybersecurity-law]].

## Provisions & contest

- **Offence and penalties.** Defines the crime of disseminating false information, with tiered punishment: a **base penalty of 1–5 years** where public order, fundamental rights, individual integrity or national security are affected, rising to **up to 10 years** for incitement to hatred, violence or discrimination, or for **compromising electoral processes**.
- **The debate.** UNITA (dep. Mihaela Webba) called it a tool to **repress free expression and criminalise journalists and users**, faulting the absence of transparency over *who* decides what is "false." The MPLA (dep. José Ribero Uefo) countered that the target is not content control but "structured disinformation" and actors able to amplify falsehoods — "the internet cannot be a territory without rules." The adversarial reading is *Novo Jornal*'s; the government's framing is the MPLA's. See [[angola--infra-cybersec]], [[gov.legislate]], [[infra.cybersec]].
