# DRC National Digital Plan 2026-2030 (PNN2)

The DRC's **second National Digital Plan** (Plan National du Numérique 2026-2030), launched for drafting **8 October 2025** by Digital Economy Minister Augustin Kibassa Maliba, alongside the country's **first National AI Strategy** ([[drc-national-ai-strategy]]) — aiming to make the DRC a **regional digital hub by 2030** and to leverage its critical minerals.

## Profile

- **Four pillars:** infrastructure & connectivity; digital public platforms & services; human capital & inclusion; cybersecurity & digital trust — plus five cross-cutting priorities including **technological sovereignty and AI**.
- **Targets:** 650 additional communities with broadband (~30m people + 1,000 institutions), +$4.1bn GDP by 2029, 700,000 jobs, 9.7m new mobile-internet users; a national interbank payment platform. Funders cited: World Bank **[[idea-mpa|IDEA]]**, AFD.
- Umbrella over **[[rdc-pass]]** (digital ID), data-centre FDI ([[2026-02-09 DRC steps up talks with foreign firms on data centres and digital infrastructure]]) and the connectivity backbone.

## Cost (resolved 2026-07-16)

The defensible figure is a **US$1.5bn government financing envelope** — **US$1bn** from the public treasury (2026–2030) plus **US$500m** in external partner support already secured (World Bank, AFD). Attributed to the Ministry of the Digital Economy and President Tshisekedi at the **8 Oct 2025 launch** ([[2025-10-10 DR Congo launches National Digital Plan 2026-2030 (PNN2) and first AI strategy]]) and restated through **July 2026**. The **$1bn + $500m split** was stated explicitly **at launch** — Agence Ecofin (11 Oct 2025): "a public investment of **$1 billion over five years**, supplemented by **$500 million in external support already guaranteed by international partners**" ([[2025-10-11 La RDC prevoit 1,5 milliard $ de budget pour son Plan national du numerique et de l'IA 2026-2030 (Agence Ecofin)]]) — and confirmed again by the ministry's **17 Feb 2026** operationalisation of the $500m partner tranche after talks with World Bank and AFD experts ([[2026-02-20 DRC operationalises $500m partner tranche of PNN2 financing (World Bank, AFD)]]), which also references earlier June 2025 agreements (World Bank $400m for the digital sector; EU €100m/~$117.5m).

The widely-circulated **"$8.7bn total" is unverified** ([[2026-02-01 DRC unveils $8.7 billion digital plan to expand connectivity and AI skills by 2030]]): it appears only in secondary tech-trade media, is currency-ambiguous ($8.7bn vs €8bn/$8.8bn in the same outlet), and traces to no primary source — most plausibly a fuller multi-source *ambition* envelope folding in not-yet-mobilised private capital, not committed budget. A **€8.7bn / $10.4bn total** (previously carried on [[rdc-pass]]) **could not be corroborated** from any public source. *As of 16 Jul 2026, no finalised, published PNN2 document with an official headline total was locatable — the plan was still being drafted, so the circulating "cost" is the announced financing envelope, not a costed line-item total.* (REV-COD-001 resolved — see `log.md`.)
