# America First Global Health Strategy (AFGHS)

The US bilateral global-health-funding framework, launched under the Trump administration
(strategy paper Sept 2025), that **replaced USAID and restructured PEPFAR** — swapping
multilateral, NGO-mediated aid for government-to-government five-year MoUs. Its defining
feature: access to a country's health data and pathogen specimens is a **condition of
funding**, making AFGHS as much a digital-governance instrument as a health one.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Scope (early 2026).** Reporting ranges from ~22 to 26 African countries signing bilateral
  agreements over four months (late 2025–early 2026); only ~5 of the texts were published.
  One tally put 16 signatories at >$18.3bn in new financing ($11.2bn US + $7.1bn co-investment).
  The **State Department's own December list** named **14 African signatories** with headline
  values: Uganda $2.3bn, Kenya $2.5bn, Rwanda $228m, Nigeria $5.1bn, Eswatini $242m, Ethiopia
  $1.466bn, Mozambique $1.8bn, Côte d'Ivoire $937m, Cameroon $850m, Lesotho $364m, Madagascar
  $175m, Sierra Leone $173m, Liberia $176m, Botswana $486m (~$16bn total; MOUs are five-year,
  2026–2030). The [[kff-afghs-mou-tracker]] is the standing dataset tracking signings/funding
  (strategy released 2025-09-18). See [[geopol.usa]], [[data.statistics]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-19 African Digital Health Data Is a Condition of Global Health Funding]],
  [[2026-01-20 Africa Pushes Back On US Health Deals Over Data, Power]],
  [[2026-07-01 KFF Tracker America First MOU Bilateral Global Health Agreements]].
- **The two sub-agreements.** Each MoU bundles a **[[specimen-sharing-agreement]]** (physical
  pathogen specimens + genetic-sequence data within 5 days of detection, reportedly up to
  **25-yr** access) and a **Data-Sharing Agreement** (US government/contractor access to
  national surveillance databases and lab-management platforms, **10-yr** post-termination).
  Negotiators reportedly sought **25 years of data sharing in exchange for 5 years of
  assistance**, with no reciprocal benefit-sharing obligation. See [[dpi.exchange]],
  [[dpi.mis]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2026-03-19 African Digital Health Data Is a Condition of Global Health Funding]].
- **Specimen-Sharing Agreement — full text confirms the asymmetry (primary doc).** Emily Bass
  reproduced the model **[[specimen-sharing-agreement]]** in full: the country shares specimens
  within **5 days** of a US request; the US may onward-share to **up to 10 non-US-government US
  entities**; the agreement runs **25 years**; and it carries **no reciprocity** — the US "shall
  prioritize" a signatory's request for any resulting countermeasure only *behind* US domestic
  need and only via "best efforts" at cost. Unlike the data-sharing terms (which survive 10 years
  post-cancellation), the specimen agreement can be cancelled on one year's notice, but the US
  retains use of any specimens already shared. Ahmed Olayinka Sule's analysis adds the asymmetric
  audit/notification duties (Kenya must open 5% of facilities to US audit, detect outbreaks in 7
  days and notify the US in 1 day, with no reciprocal US obligation) and the "not an international
  agreement… no rights or obligations under law" clause stripping legal recourse. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]]. Sources:
  [[2025-11-21 First look Full Text of America First Global Health Strategy Specimen Sharing Agreement]],
  [[2026-01-01 America First, Africa Last  Data Extraction from Africa's Bodies and the Erosion of Health Sovereignty]].
- **Africa CDC's data/pathogen concerns (2026-03-05).** DG Jean Kaseya voiced "huge concerns
  regarding data, regarding pathogen sharing," confirming [[ZWE]] pulled out of a $367m/5-yr deal
  and [[ZMB]] pushed back; he declined an [[africa-cdc]] observer role citing member-state
  sovereignty but pledged support to any country choosing to renegotiate. Critics (SciDev.Net's
  interviewees; Makerere's Peter Waiswa) stress the deals route outbreak reporting to a foreign
  government within days, sidelining continental systems and forfeiting collective bargaining. See
  [[africa-cdc]], [[gov.regional]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-05 Africa CDC head cites major concerns over data, pathogen sharing in US health deals]],
  [[2026-01-20 Africa Pushes Back On US Health Deals Over Data, Power]].
- **Nigeria compact — the $5.1bn breakdown (2026-02).** Nigeria's is the largest MOU: **~$2.1bn in
  US grants paired with a $3.0bn Nigerian domestic co-investment** ("health independence by 2030"),
  i.e. the headline $5.1bn is the combined figure. The Christian-healthcare framing drew opposition
  (ADC citing Constitution s.42(1); MURIC calling it "pro-Christian… divisive"), and analyst Nelson
  Evaborhene reads it as a "hostage state" bind, sharpened by the Dec-2025 US airstrikes in NW
  Nigeria and a ~200-troop US special-ops deployment (Feb 2026). See [[NGA]], [[geopol.usa]],
  [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-24 Global Health Nigeria and the Pathology of the Hostage State in an Era of Fragmentation]].
- **Divergent African responses.** [[KEN]] was the first signatory ([[us-kenya-health-framework]],
  5-yr $2.5bn, signed 2025-12-04) — the High Court suspended the data-transfer component after the
  [[consumer-federation-of-kenya]] (COFEK) petition, but the **Court of Appeal reversed** that
  suspension (as of 2026-06, case continuing); Munyua reads the framework as data-colonialism's "new
  scramble" (25-yr data access for 5-yr funding). See
  [[2026-06-08 Kenyan Citizens Took A $2.5 Billion Deal With America To Court To Fight For Their Health Data]],
  [[2025-12-15 THE GATEKEEPERS PART V The New Maps of Africa]]. [[ZWE]] **rejected** the deal outright
  ([[zimbabwe-us-health-mou]]). [[RWA]] negotiated a bespoke $228m MoU (2025-12-05) naming
  **Zipline** and **Ginkgo Bioworks**. [[NGA]] signed the largest ($5.1bn) with a
  Christian-healthcare conditionality. [[ZMB]] pushed back on the data-sharing clause;
  [[SEN]]'s later MoU added text disclaiming US access to private patient data. Sources:
  [[2026-03-19 African Digital Health Data Is a Condition of Global Health Funding]],
  [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]].
- **Bypasses the WHO.** The bilateral architecture sidesteps the WHO's Pathogen Access and
  Benefit-Sharing (PABS) framework, which the US rejected before withdrawing from the WHO
  (Jan 2025). [[africa-cdc]]'s **"Africa Health Security and Sovereignty Agenda"** (Nov 2025,
  DG Jean Kaseya) is the continental counter — Africa CDC declined an observer role in the
  negotiations. See [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-03-19 African Digital Health Data Is a Condition of Global Health Funding]].
- **Zambia halts its deal over mining + data terms (2026-02).** [[ZMB]]'s draft **$1.012bn/5-yr** MoU
  ([[zambia-us-health-mou]]) carried the harshest terms yet — 10-yr health-data access plus a
  **25-yr pathogen-data pledge** (longer than any published MoU) — and reportedly conditioned funding
  on **copper/cobalt mining access**. President Hichilema requested "revisions" and fired health
  minister Muchima three days after he denied the mining link; the 2026 funding ($320m) is below the
  $367m Zambia received for HIV alone in 2025. Civil society (Health GAP: "shameless exploitation")
  is pressing to strip the data-sharing clause. See [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]], [[colonialism]].
  Sources: [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]],
  [[2026-02-26 Zambia rejects U.S. health aid over mining partnership ties]].
