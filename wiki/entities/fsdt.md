# Financial Sector Deepening Tanzania (FSDT)

A **financial-sector development organisation** working on financial inclusion in Tanzania —
and, in the payments story, a **long-run technical and financial partner to the central bank**
rather than an external advocate. FSDT co-published the case study on **[[tips-tanzania]]** with
the **[[bank-of-tanzania]]**.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Co-builder of TIPS from 2018 (BOT/FSDT case study, dated 2025-05-16 — proxy, see caveat).**
  TIPS — the national real-time retail payment switch **built and operated in-house by the Bank
  of Tanzania** — was **built from scratch from 2018 with FSDT and [[gates-foundation]]
  support**, on **Level One Project** design principles. The case study frames the work as
  building an *inclusive* payments ecosystem: interoperability between banks and non-bank DFSPs
  as an inclusion instrument, not just a plumbing upgrade. Reported volumes: **over 453.7m
  interoperable retail transactions worth over TZS 29.9 trillion in 2024**, with **63.2m active
  mobile money wallets at end-2024** (TCRA Q4 2024 — see [[tcra]]). See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[include.access]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-16 Building an Inclusive Payments Ecosystem in Tanzania through TIPS (BOT and FSDT)]].
  **Date caveat:** the case study is `date_source: proxy` — no date is printed in the document;
  2025-05-16 is a CMS filename stamp, so the day precision is unverified. Internal evidence
  supports 2025 at the earliest.
- **Donor-adjacent, state-operated.** The pairing is worth noting: philanthropic and
  development-finance support (FSDT, Gates) behind a rail the **central bank owns and runs
  itself** — a different pattern from the vendor-built national switch. See [[sovereignty]].
  Source:
  [[2025-05-16 Building an Inclusive Payments Ecosystem in Tanzania through TIPS (BOT and FSDT)]].
