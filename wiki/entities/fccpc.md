# Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission (FCCPC), Nigeria

Nigeria's federal competition and consumer-protection regulator, empowered to
police anti-competitive conduct and consumer harms across the economy, including
the digital-platform sector.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Directed to probe foreign tech platforms (2026-07-10).** Acting on a Federal
  Government directive following a petition by the Nigerian Press Organisation,
  the FCCPC is investigating alleged anti-competitive practices by [[meta]],
  Alphabet/[[google]], X and generative-AI platforms — part of a widening test
  of Nigerian [[NGA]] regulatory reach over foreign tech firms ([[tech.industry]],
  [[gov.legislate]]). Source: [[2026-07-10 Nigeria tests data sovereignty as foreign tech firms facing probe]].
  A fuller account specifies the trigger — a **Nigerian Press Organisation** petition over
  **uncompensated scraping and AI-training on Nigerian news content** (abuse of dominance +
  copyright, FCCP Act 2018) — issued through Information Minister Mohammed Idris, with EVC
  Tunji Bello framing it as fact-finding; targets include [[meta]], Alphabet/[[google]],
  [[x-corp]] and GenAI firms. Cited precedents: the 2025 $220m Meta penalty (under appeal),
  NDPC's ₦7.2bn privacy penalties, and SA's Competition Commission remedy on Google
  (~R688m/yr). Source: [[2026-07-10 Why Nigeria Is Investigating Meta, Google, X and AI Firms]].
- **$222m penalty on Meta (2025, under appeal).** The FCCPC imposed a $222m fine
  on [[meta]] for competition and consumer-protection breaches; Meta is
  appealing. This is distinct from the separate $32.8m data-protection fine
  levied by the [[ndpc]] ([[gov.protect]]). Source: [[2026-07-10 Nigeria tests data sovereignty as foreign tech firms facing probe]].
