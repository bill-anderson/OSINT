# Equinix South Africa $438m expansion

Self-funded ~$438 million (R7.5 billion) expansion of [[equinix]]'s South African data-centre
footprint, adding new facilities in Johannesburg and Cape Town to capture rising AI and cloud
demand across the continent.

## Activity in this wiki

- **Expansion announced (~2026-04).** [[equinix]] (Nasdaq-listed, Redwood City) disclosed a
  R7.5bn ($438m) plan to build additional facilities in [[ZAF]], building on its first South
  African data centre (Johannesburg, opened 2024). SA MD Sandile Dube said the firm has secured
  ~327,000 sqm of land across Johannesburg and Cape Town — including an R890m land purchase — and
  plans to add ~160MW of capacity. Explicitly balance-sheet funded: "All investments are funded
  by our own balance sheet... with all of our future investments in South Africa." Tags
  [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]], [[finance.new]], [[infra.energy]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-01 US data centre firm Equinix is investing $438 million in South Africa]],
  [[2026-04-01 US tech giant from Silicon Valley buys R890 million worth of land in South Africa]].

- **Market context (as of 2026-04).** Africa hosts ~409MW of operational data-centre capacity —
  under 1% of the global total — with a further ~172MW under construction; [[ZAF]] accounts for
  roughly three-quarters of the continental total. South Africa's data-centre market is projected
  to grow from ~$2.6bn (2025) to more than $5bn by 2031 (Arizton). Equinix operates in West
  Africa but has not yet entered East Africa; further continental expansion is contingent on how
  current investments perform.
