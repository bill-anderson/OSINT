# Kenya × Data centres & sovereign cloud

Nairobi is East Africa's densest data-centre market, and 2025–26 turned it into a **sovereign-cloud
proving ground**: a wave of "AI-ready" and "sovereign" cloud launches, most anchored on
**[[ixafrica-data-centres|iXAfrica]]**'s NBOX1 campus, marketed on **data residency, local billing and
GPU compute** — even as the flagship hyperscale build (Microsoft/G42) stalls on power (see
[[kenya--tech-ai]]).

## What we know

- **iXAfrica as the anchor (2025-05).** [[tech.ai]] Safaricom and **[[ixafrica-data-centres]]** launched
  **East Africa's first AI-ready data-centre services** — local hosting of AI-intensive workloads for
  enterprise and government. Source: [[2025-05-14-safaricom-ixafrica-ai-data-centre-itweb]].
- **Sovereign-cloud wave (2025-07 → 2026-06).** [[sovereignty]] [[gov.protect]] A run of locally-hosted
  "sovereign" clouds at iXAfrica NBOX1: **[[touchnet]]**'s Zadara AI Sovereign Cloud (GPUaaS, 2025-07);
  **[[servernah-cloud]]** ("Kenya's first sovereign AI cloud", Mar 2026); **[[baobab-cloud|Baobab]]**
  (Mitsumi) sovereign **public** cloud — KES billing, zero egress, 99.9% SLA, data residency (May 2026);
  and a GITEX-Kenya "country-first sovereign cloud" launch (Jun 2026). The common pitch: escape
  foreign-currency billing and offshore hosting. Sources:
  [[2025-07-17-touchnet-sovereign-ai-cloud-ixafrica-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-05-22-ixafrica-baobab-sovereign-public-cloud-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-06-16-tech-firms-country-first-sovereign-cloud-standard]].
- **Local AI-compute ecosystem — Qubit Hub (2026-05).** [[tech.innovate]] **[[qubit-hub]]** (Qhala/QTrust
  incubated) united **Qhala, Amini AI, [[angani]] and iXAfrica** to host an African AI research/compute
  lab at NBOX1 — "solving for compute, talent and community" for builders region-wide. Source:
  [[2026-05-25-qhala-amini-angani-ixafrica-ai-hub-techafricanews]].
- **Competition — Airtel enters (2026-05).** [[finance.new]] **[[airtel-africa|Airtel]]** is building a
  **KES 5.6bn data centre** (Nxtra, Tatu City) to take on Safaricom in the DC market. Source:
  [[2026-05-10-airtel-56b-data-centre-standard]] *(summary-only clip)*.
- **Policy push (2026-06).** [[gov.policy]] PS **[[john-tanui]]** convened a stakeholder forum (ICTA,
  KRA, ODPC, TESPOK, SEZ Authority) to refine DC/cloud regulation, pitching Kenya as East Africa's
  preferred digital-investment destination on connectivity, renewable energy and regulation. Existing
  ecosystem: Safaricom, **[[oracle]]**, Liquid, iColo and the government-owned **[[konza-technopolis|Konza]]**
  National Data Centre. Source: [[2026-06-05-kenya-targets-ai-data-centres-expansion-itweb]].
