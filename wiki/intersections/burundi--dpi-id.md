# Burundi × Digital Identity

Burundi is assembling foundational identity **from a very low base and under a dominant-party political system** — a biometric national ID that has slipped repeatedly, a voter register produced amid documented coercion, and a brand-new data-protection law passed largely *to enable* the ID rollout. Sequencing and trust, not technology, are the story.

## What we know

**A biometric ID that keeps slipping.** The government said in **2023** it would roll out a national **biometric ID before 2025**; in **May 2024** the **[[ceni-burundi]]** confirmed the biometric card **would not be ready** for the 2025 voter enrolment, which fell back on the current, easily-falsifiable ID card. By **January 2026**, officials reported the biometric ID in **pilot testing across about a dozen communes**, with registration software aligned to Burundi's new administrative boundaries, ahead of a phased national rollout.

**A DP law built for the ID.** The **[[burundi-data-protection-law-2026]]** (adopted 15 Jan 2026) was explicitly motivated by the digitisation of the voter register, computerised administration and the biometric-ID preparations — creating an independent authority and criminal penalties, and framed as removing "a key obstacle" to deploying the card. Whether a newly-created authority can actually check a state ID drive is untested.

**Registration under coercion.** The voter register — Burundi's largest identity-data exercise — was produced in early 2025 amid reports (SOS Médias) of citizens **compelled to register** (services conditioned on proof of registration; Imbonerakure checking documents), and stakeholders flagged fraud in the current card (foreign nationals allegedly obtaining it; boundary mismatches). Absent a recent census, population was projected from **2008 (~8m) to ~13.2m** for 2025.

The DPI scaffolding — a national ID enabling service access — is a stated **[[pafen-burundi]]** goal, but it is being built in one of the world's poorest countries and a contested political space. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.protect]], [[BDI]].
