# Gabon × Data centres, cloud & digital sovereignty

Gabon's data-sovereignty push is anchored on ending its reliance on **foreign hosting (~95% of data abroad)**
through a **state + [[st-digital-gabon|ST Digital]]** model.

- **[[gabon-national-datacenter|National (Nkok) data centre]]** — Tier III, ST-Digital-built, **$14m / 8bn CFA**,
  92 racks / 3,000 m² / 1 MW, inaugurated **3 Jul 2026** by President Oligui Nguema; peered to ACE + SAT-3/WACS.
- **ANINF–ST Digital MoU (2025-07).** **[[aninf-gabon|ANINF]]** and ST Digital signed a framework for **secure
  digital infrastructure, cloud, cybersecurity, digital identity and e-government**, plus local capacity-building
  — pairing ANINF's regulatory role with ST Digital's build capacity.
- **Data-protection anchor.** Localisation is tied to the **[[apdpvp-gabon|APDPVP]]** (data-protection
  authority) compliance review — sovereignty framed explicitly as citizen data-security.
- **Vendor track.** A **[[gabon-huawei-mou|Huawei]]** technology-partnership exploration (Jul 2025) preceded the
  later Huawei MoU — a `geopol.china` thread alongside the domestically-built data centre.
