# Côte d'Ivoire × GovTech / e-Government

Côte d'Ivoire is wiring its e-government toward a **"zero paper 2030"** goal — an X-Road-family interoperability layer under the identity backbone, e-services stacking on top (justice, nationality, social registry), and a **rising digital budget** funding it. It is one of francophone West Africa's more deliberate govtech builds.

## What we know

**The interoperability layer.** The **[[sndi-cote-divoire]]** (national IT company), commissioned by the Ministry of Digital Transition (MTND), signed **[[cybernetica]]** to deploy its **UXP** (Unified eXchange Platform, X-Road family) for secure real-time inter-ministry data exchange — a 20-month build (with Digital Afrique Telecom), launched Sept 2025, under the National Digital Development Strategy and "zero paper 2030." The same UXP model Benin runs.

**E-services on the ID backbone.** **[[oneci]]**'s certified identity (RNPP/NNI) underpins new e-services: the **e-Justice** platform (Jan 2025, online nationality-certificate applications) and a June 2025 decree **digitising nationality certificates**. The **[[rsu-cote-divoire]]** social registry (200,000+ households registering, 2025) adds the social-protection data layer.

**Strategy and money.** The economy ministry adopted a **2026–2030 Information System Master Plan (SDSI)** (Dec 2025); the digital-transition budget rose steadily — CFA52.6bn (2024) → CFA60.78bn (2025) → **CFA83.2bn (~$146m, 2026, +37%)** — funding connectivity, cybersecurity and AI.

Alongside a booming Abidjan/Grand-Bassam **data-centre cluster** (Raxio CIV1, ST Digital, PAIX, MDXi — see the [[CIV]] hub), the pieces of a francophone West African digital hub are assembling. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[CIV]].

## The e-Justice arc, dated end-to-end

The national-press record turns the hub's single "e-Justice launched 30 Jan 2025" line into a clean
**17-month trajectory** (and corrects a compression: the **30 Jan 2025** launch was a **two-court pilot**
— Dabou and Yopougon — issuing nationality certificates only; the **18 Jun 2025 Council-of-Ministers
decree** *formalised* the digital nationality certificate rather than launching e-Justice):
**30 Jan 2025** two-court pilot → **27 Jun 2025** Abidjan first-instance tribunal → **18 Jun 2025** decree
→ **20 Apr 2026** 16 courts added (to 28) → **30 Jun 2026** all **39 first-instance tribunals connected**,
with an adoption/change-management workshop along the way. Sources:

## Interoperability corroborated, and its Estonian lineage

National-press coverage (Kalil Konaté, 25 Sep 2025) corroborates the UXP interoperability launch the hub
held via trade press; the Estonian pedigree is explicit — **SNDI contracted Cybernetica's UXP** (X-Road
family, 20-month build) for **12 pilot institutions** (incl. ONECI, DGI, CNPS), and CIV later studied the
**Estonia DPI model** at a Europe–East Africa DPI session (May 2026). Sources:

## Interop in use — trade window, an AI front door, service mapping

- **Trade single window ([[guce-ci|GUCE-CI]]).** The trade single window began **exchanging data with the
  tax directorate (DGI)** (Dec 2025) and hosted the first **GUCE CONNECT** ecosystem event (May 2026); a
  parallel **Tradepost** e-commerce platform launched at an ECOWAS workshop. Sources:
  [[2025-12-23-guce-ci-dgi-echange-donnees-fratmat]],
  [[2026-05-12-guce-connect-digitalisation-commerce-exterieur-linfodrome]],
  [[2025-12-03-tradepost-ecommerce-platform-itweb]].
- **AI front door ([[emy-101|EMY 101]]).** A government AI assistant on WhatsApp/Messenger plus a toll-free
  **101** line for civil-status and tax guidance and citizen reporting (Mar 2026). Sources:
  [[2026-03-19-emy-101-ai-chatbot-wearetech]], [[2026-03-20-emy-101-ai-chatbot-public-services-techafricanews]].
- **Service mapping (232 services).** Under the World-Bank-cofinanced **PADCI**, an OKTO/IT6 exercise
  **mapped 232 public services** across four categories and five priority sectors — the demand-side inventory
  behind "zero paper." Plus sectoral platforms: an **e-mining cadastre** (Landfolio, EITI-aligned) and a
  **transport-fluidity** platform. Sources:
  [[2025-10-02-digital-roadmap-232-public-services-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2025-09-23-e-mining-cadastre-portal-wearetech]],
  [[2026-04-15-oft-stad-plateforme-transports-fratmat]].
