# Côte d'Ivoire × GovTech / e-Government

Côte d'Ivoire is wiring its e-government toward a **"zero paper 2030"** goal — an X-Road-family interoperability layer under the identity backbone, e-services stacking on top (justice, nationality, social registry), and a **rising digital budget** funding it. It is one of francophone West Africa's more deliberate govtech builds.

## What we know

**The interoperability layer.** The **[[sndi-cote-divoire]]** (national IT company), commissioned by the Ministry of Digital Transition (MTND), signed **[[cybernetica]]** to deploy its **UXP** (Unified eXchange Platform, X-Road family) for secure real-time inter-ministry data exchange — a 20-month build (with Digital Afrique Telecom), launched Sept 2025, under the National Digital Development Strategy and "zero paper 2030." The same UXP model Benin runs.

**E-services on the ID backbone.** **[[oneci]]**'s certified identity (RNPP/NNI) underpins new e-services: the **e-Justice** platform (Jan 2025, online nationality-certificate applications) and a June 2025 decree **digitising nationality certificates**. The **[[rsu-cote-divoire]]** social registry (200,000+ households registering, 2025) adds the social-protection data layer.

**Strategy and money.** The economy ministry adopted a **2026–2030 Information System Master Plan (SDSI)** (Dec 2025); the digital-transition budget rose steadily — CFA52.6bn (2024) → CFA60.78bn (2025) → **CFA83.2bn (~$146m, 2026, +37%)** — funding connectivity, cybersecurity and AI.

Alongside a booming Abidjan/Grand-Bassam **data-centre cluster** (Raxio CIV1, ST Digital, PAIX, MDXi — see the [[CIV]] hub), the pieces of a francophone West African digital hub are assembling. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[CIV]].
