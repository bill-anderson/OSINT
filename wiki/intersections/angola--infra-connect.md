# Angola × Connectivity (subsea, fibre, satellite, market)

Angola's connectivity build in 2025–26 is a **sovereignty-framed replacement of a legacy Atlantic
stack**: the incumbent subsea cables are ageing out just as the government pursues an independent
international route, a mobile-led market of ~28m subscribers, and a multi-orbit satellite layer for
the rural last mile. The unifying claim, repeated by [[minttics]], is **digital sovereignty** — a
stable international route "under national jurisdiction."

## Subsea — out with SAT-3, in with 2Africa and Equiano

- **2Africa lands at Cacuaco (2025-12-13).** **[[unitel]]** — the **sole Angolan operator** in the
  consortium (Meta, Vodafone, WIOCC, China Mobile International, MTN, Orange, Telecom Egypt, STC) —
  invested **US$43m** to build Angola's branch of **[[2africa-cable]]** and the Cacuaco landing
  station, with **23.6 Tbps** of the cable's ~180 Tbps allocated to Angola. The government's own
  rationale is generational: **SAT-3 is at end-of-life after 25 years and WACS is at capacity**, so
  2Africa is the modern, redundant replacement.
- **The Equiano route and a domestic cable (2025–26).** Angola reaches Google's **[[equiano-cable]]**
  indirectly. The **[[sarssy]]** system interconnects with Equiano via **[[telecom-namibia]]**'s
  **Swakopmund** landing station: an MSA signed **12 June 2026** by Telecom Namibia,
  **[[angola-telecom]]** and SATCAB gives Angola Telecom an **initial 1.0 Tbps** of dedicated Equiano
  capacity, with an option to assume ownership over time (see [[telecom-namibia-angola-deals]]).
  Separately, MINTTICS is planning a **domestic submarine cable** to interlink the coastal cities and
  connect the **Lobito Corridor** to Equiano (2026-03).
- **Atlantic wholesale corridors.** **[[angola-cables]]** — claiming **>70% of Africa's internet/data
  traffic flows** — extends the SACS/MONET/WACS backbone through deals with **[[uniti-fiber|Uniti
  Wholesale]]** (386,000 km US fibre, 2026-05), Megaport and MEO Portugal, marketing "distributed,
  sovereign-compliant infrastructure." → [[angola--infra-store]].

## Fibre and the mobile market

National fibre length is written dated because the sources diverge: **~14,000 km (2025-12,
Unitel)** rising to **">22,000 km" (2026-04, minister at the data-centre launch)**, with a further
**28,000 km** figure circulating by mid-2026 — treat as a growth series to watch, not a settled
number (see Flags). Fibre now reaches all provincial capitals with metro rings in major cities.

The market is **mobile-led and concentrated**: at **end-2024**, **[[unitel]] 20.8m**, **[[africell]]
~5m** and **Movicel 941,000** mobile users (Omdia). By **2026-03** MINTTICS cited **>28m mobile
subscribers and >17m broadband users**; at the April 2026 data-centre launch, **~17.5–17.7m
fixed+mobile broadband subscribers**, **~75% penetration** and **>85% population broadband coverage**
— **5.5pp above** the SADC broadband target under NDP 2023-2027. **[[unitel]]** runs an Ericsson
**dual-mode 5G Core** (2025-03) and is heading toward re-privatisation (15% IPO, July 2026).

## Satellite and the rural last mile

- **LEO — Eutelsat's monopoly licence.** **[[eutelsat]]** holds a **15-year OneWeb licence** (2025-06)
  as the **first and only licensed LEO operator in Angola**, with a Luanda ground station; at ANGOTIC
  2026 it signed a multi-year LEO capacity deal with local provider **[[mercury-telecom-angola|Mercury]]**.
  A striking contrast with SA/Namibia, which blocked [[starlink]] on ownership grounds.
- **GEO/national — ANGOSAT-2.** The **[[conecta-angola]]** programme uses **[[angosat-2]]** for remote
  connectivity (32 free access points, six universities, border posts, TVWS-to-schools) — treated in
  full at [[angola--data-satellite]].
- **Ilumina Angola.** A national-roaming and infrastructure-sharing programme (shared towers/antennas,
  **solar power**, ANGOSAT-2 backhaul) extending voice/data to remote areas — e.g. Lago Dilolo,
  Moxico (pop. 40,882), June 2026. Paired with a **[[unitel]]–[[angola-telecom]]** infrastructure-
  sharing deal for underserved **border regions** (ANGOTIC, 2026-06).

## Broadcast

**Japan/JICA** committed **~US$10m** (2025-08) to Angola's analogue-to-**DTT** transition, modernising
transmitters at Viana, Luanda and Bengo — motivated partly by disaster-alert broadcasting and the fact
that **~70% of Angolans speak national languages other than Portuguese**, driving multilingual needs.
