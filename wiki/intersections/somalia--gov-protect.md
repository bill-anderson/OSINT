# Somalia × Data Protection

Somalia has assembled a data-governance legal stack unusually fast for a fragile state,
even as enforcement capacity and the security of citizen data lag the statute book.

## The framework

- **[[somali-data-protection-act]] (Law 005/2023)** — first comprehensive data-protection
  law, effective **23 Mar 2023**; grounded in Art 19 of the 2012 Constitution. Establishes
  lawful bases, data-subject rights, DPIA/DPO duties, registration of "controllers of major
  importance," and fines of **2-4% of worldwide turnover**.
- **[[somalia-data-protection-authority]]** — inaugurated **24 Feb 2024**; issued
  implementation guidelines and regulations. Independence is qualified (executive
  appointment and override powers).
- **Regulatory Framework for the Public Data Protection Law** — adopted by the Council of
  Ministers **Feb 2026** to operationalise the law (see [[SOM]] hub).
- **[[somalia-cybersecurity-law]]** — approved **26 Jan 2026**; creates SOM-CIRT, a
  nine-member committee and an emergency centre. Somalia ranked ITU GCI **Tier 4/5** (2024).

## Tensions (lens: sovereignty)

- The Act introduces but **fails to define "public data,"** clouding the regulator's
  mandate; the Authority's **independence** is constrained by executive appointment and
  national-security override (CIPIT/Strathmore).
- **Security of the national-ID data itself** is contested — a Pakistan-hosted / foreign-
  operated system (per Garowe Online) sits uneasily beside a young DPA, and a **Nov 2025
  e-visa breach** exposed several thousand people's data. The gap between legal framework
  and operational data security is the live risk. See [[somalia--dpi-id]].
