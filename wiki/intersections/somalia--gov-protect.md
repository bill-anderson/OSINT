# Somalia × Data Protection

Somalia has assembled a data-governance legal stack unusually fast for a fragile state,
even as enforcement capacity and the security of citizen data lag the statute book.

## The framework

- **[[somali-data-protection-act]] (Law 005/2023)** — first comprehensive data-protection
  law, effective **23 Mar 2023**; grounded in Art 19 of the 2012 Constitution. Establishes
  lawful bases, data-subject rights, DPIA/DPO duties, registration of "controllers of major
  importance," and fines of **2-4% of worldwide turnover**.
- **[[somalia-data-protection-authority]]** — inaugurated **24 Feb 2024**; issued
  implementation guidelines and regulations. Independence is qualified (executive
  appointment and override powers).
- **Regulatory Framework for the Public Data Protection Law** — approved by the **Council of
  Ministers on 8 Jan 2026** (extraordinary session, President Hassan Sheikh chairing; drafted by the
  Ministry of Communications & Technology) and **gazetted/adopted Feb 2026** — operationalising the law
  and aligning it to the digital-transformation agenda. Source:
  [[2026-01-08-somalia-approves-public-data-protection-regulation]].
- **DPA enforcement begins (Jun 2026).** The Data Protection Authority issued a notice under **Article
  32** requiring **all data controllers and processors** to complete **mandatory registration**,
  accepting applications and issuing compliance certificates — the regulator's first nationwide
  enforcement step. Source: [[2026-06-02-somalia-dpa-enforces-data-compliance-registration]].
- **[[somalia-cybersecurity-law]]** — approved **26 Jan 2026**; assigns policy to MoCT, technical
  oversight to the NCA, and obligations to critical-infrastructure operators; creates SOM-CIRT. Somalia
  ranked ITU GCI **Tier 4/5 (37.39/100, 2024)**; a signatory to the Malabo Convention.

## Cybersecurity build-out (2026)

- **SOMCIRT operational (7 Mar 2026).** PM Hamza opened the **Somalia Computer Incident Response Team**
  (mandated by the Cybersecurity Law) to coordinate prevention/detection/response and issue alerts — 45
  officials in first-phase training. Follows an Aug-2025 cybercrime bill and MoUs with **Turkey** (10 Feb
  2026), Malaysia and UNODC. Source: [[2026-03-09-somalia-launches-somcirt]].
- **Compliance framework consultation (Apr 2026).** The **[[nca-somalia|NCA]]** convened a national
  consultation to develop a **Cybersecurity Risk Management & Compliance Framework** (risk identification,
  regulatory compliance, institutional responsibilities). Source:
  [[2026-04-25-somalia-cybersecurity-framework-consultation]].
- **Certified training for govt ICT staff (2026).** The NCA + **World Bank SCALED-UP** (with **AFRALTI**)
  ran internationally-certified cybersecurity training (incident response, cloud security, auditing;
  Mogadishu/Nairobi, Mar–May 2026). See [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-01-somalia-cybersecurity-certification-program]].

## Tensions (lens: sovereignty)

- The Act introduces but **fails to define "public data,"** clouding the regulator's
  mandate; the Authority's **independence** is constrained by executive appointment and
  national-security override (CIPIT/Strathmore).
- **Security of the national-ID data itself** is contested — a Pakistan-hosted / foreign-
  operated system (per Garowe Online) sits uneasily beside a young DPA, and a **Nov 2025
  e-visa breach** exposed several thousand people's data. The gap between legal framework
  and operational data security is the live risk. See [[somalia--dpi-id]].
