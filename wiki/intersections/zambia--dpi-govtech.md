# Zambia × E-Government & Public-Service Digitisation

The **[[smart-zambia-institute|SMART Zambia Institute]]** is running a broad, **in-house-and-local-vendor**
digitisation of the state — e-registries, a property system, a contract-tracking system, councils and land
all onboarding to a single **[[zamportal|ZamPortal]]/[[zampay|ZamPay]]** front end over a government fibre
network — framed explicitly as **anti-corruption and vendor-independent**. It is the delivery arm of the same
open-standards sovereignty agenda that drives the [[zambia--dpi-id|digital ID]].

## The systems, on one portal

- **SMART Office e-registry (2025).** An **in-house** SMART Zambia records/e-registry system piloted at the
  **[[ministry-of-health-zambia|Ministry of Health]]** HQ (Luapula pilot, on GWAN), rolling to PSMD, Finance,
  Education and the National Archives — estimated to save **~K400m** in stationery/printing. Source:
  [[2025-06-23-smart-office-system-rollout]].
- **PMIS — property (May 2026).** A **Property Management Information System** (Ministry of Infrastructure/
  Housing + SZI, built by **local firm CoreLink Consulting**) integrated with **ZamPortal + ZamPay** for
  rentals, valuation and housing services online (8NDP). Source: [[2026-05-13-zambia-launches-pmis-property-management]].
- **CTMIS — contracts (Jul 2026).** A **Contract Tracking & MIS** (SZI + Ministry of Justice / AG's Chambers)
  for digital submission, review and real-time tracking of government contract approvals — AG **Mulilo
  Kabesha** pitching it against approval delays. See [[dpi.mis]]. Source: [[2026-07-12-govt-launches-ctmis-contract-system]].
- **Councils on ZamPortal (May 2026).** **[[lusaka-city-council|Lusaka City Council]]** became the **first of
  116 councils** live on ZamPortal, with pilot councils (Ndola, Kitwe, Solwezi, Chingola, Kalulushi,
  Kalumbila, Chilanga) to follow by **30 Jun 2026** — the e-Council rollout. See [[digital.localgov]].
  Source: [[2026-05-29-councils-digital-lcc-first-zamportal]].
- **CDF tracking + e-Council, dated from SZI (Mar 2026).** A **Constituency Development Fund management
  information system** was in data entry across councils as of **2026-03**, with **full operationalisation
  expected June 2026**: "we will be able to **track transaction by transaction, payment by payment**
  happening in the CDF space… government will see where the money is going" (Percy Chinyama) — pitched at
  cutting duplication and spotting funds repeatedly awarded to the same beneficiaries. The **e-Council**
  platform digitising council revenue management and financial reporting entered a **proof-of-concept
  phase one with eight councils — Ndola, Kalulushi, Kalumbila, Kitwe, Solwezi, Chingola, Chilanga** —
  targeting **system demonstration by 19 June 2026**, covering property taxes, business levy, market levy,
  health permits, fire certificates and market occupancy before extension to the rest. *(The article names
  seven of the stated eight.)* This dates the e-Council pilot **two months earlier than the May-2026
  ZamPortal onboarding item** and gives it a distinct scope: revenue systems, not portal front-end. See
  [[digital.localgov]], [[dpi.mis]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-25-kalemba-zambia-cdf-ecouncil-116-councils-gwan]].
- **The four-year scorecard (Mar 2026).** At the same briefing SZI put **K7 billion** of revenue realised
  in **digital transactions across 2021–2025** (Kasali Musenge), claimed internet connectivity had reached
  "**approximately 80 percent**" (Percy Chinyama — *no unit or basis stated; not a penetration rate, and
  not reconcilable with the 33.0% Oct-2025 user figure*), and called for **PPP** investment toward a
  "digitally inclusive middle-income country by **2030**", citing a **[[zesco]]** partnership for
  infrastructure expansion and digital record-keeping at the Ministries of Agriculture, Lands, and
  Community Development and Social Services. Source:
  [[2026-03-25-zanis-smart-zambia-digital-infrastructure-investment]].

## The reach: fibre, health, land

- **To every corner (Jul 2026).** SZI connected **all local authorities and post offices in North-Western
  Province** via the **Government Wide Area Network (GWAN)** fibre; **[[smartcare-pro|SmartCare Pro]]** reached
  **~80% of health facilities**; and the **ZNSDI GeoPortal** underpins transparent land mapping and artisanal
  mining licences. A parallel plan targets full digitalisation with all **health facilities and schools**
  connected (Muchinga engagement, May 2026). See [[infra.connect]], [[digital.rural]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-08-smart-zambia-expands-digital-infrastructure]], [[2026-05-07-zambia-targets-full-digitalisation-smart-zambia]].

- **GWAN coverage — a contested claim (2025 vs 2026).** wearetech.africa reports SZI announced in **2025**
  that it had connected **all 116 local authorities** to the **Government Wide Area Network (GWAN)** — but
  SZI's own **July-2026** statement presents the connection of **North-Western Province's** local
  authorities and post offices as new. **Resolved (2026-07-20): different measures, both hold.** The
  116 is SZI's own claim, restated and sharpened by its Government Chief Technical Officer Kasali
  Musenge on **2026-03-25** — "**All 116 councils have been connected to the government network, which
  means phase 1 is completed 100 percent. On top of that, we have provided solar power to all the
  councils, meaning that they are able to operate 24 hours in a day with regard to service provision.**"
  The North-Western Province item (2026-07-07) covers local authorities **and post offices** over
  **fibre**, extending to schools and health facilities — a later-phase deepening, not a first connection
  of councils. **Caveat on "connected":** SZI has at times meant completed site works rather than live
  end-to-end service — in **Western Province, 12 sites** (Luampa, Mitete, Limulunga, Sioma, Mwandi,
  Mulobezi, Nkeyema, Nalolo DC offices and town councils) were reported complete on **2025-02-12** while
  SZI's own provincial ICT officer **Enoch Moonde** said completion "is dependent on the finalisation of
  **last-mile connections by other parties, particularly ZAMTEL**", which "will enable us to **fully
  utilize** the GWAN network". Cite the 116 as network reach to council premises, not verified live
  service. *(Note this is distinct from **ZamPortal onboarding**, where LCC was the **first of 116
  councils** in May 2026: a council can sit on the network without being live on the service portal — the
  two counts measure different things.)* Sources:
  [[2026-03-25-kalemba-zambia-cdf-ecouncil-116-councils-gwan]],
  [[2025-02-12-itweb-zambia-deploys-govt-wan-service-delivery]],
  [[2026-07-17-zambie-lance-reseau-4g-prive-govlink]],
  [[2026-07-08-smart-zambia-expands-digital-infrastructure]].
- **A government-owned network under it all (Jul 2026).** **[[govlink|GovLink Private 4G]]** — launched 15
  Jul 2026 — is the connectivity layer the whole portal stack depends on, with health as flagship. Full
  treatment: **[[zambia--infra-connect]]**. Zambia ranked **130th of 193 on the UN EGDI (2024)**, score
  **0.5424/1**, online-services sub-index **0.4858/1**. Sources:
  [[2026-07-17-zambia-govlink-private-4g]], [[2026-07-17-zambie-lance-reseau-4g-prive-govlink]].
- **Skilling the civil service (Dec 2025).** SZI launched a **decentralised digital-skills training
  programme** for staff inside government institutions — the human side of the same build. See
  [[capacity.training]]. Source: [[2026-07-17-zambie-lance-reseau-4g-prive-govlink]].

## Beyond SZI: the revenue authority digitises too

- **ZRA–ICTD research partnership (2026-07-17).** The **[[zambia-revenue-authority|Zambia Revenue
  Authority]]** signed an **MoU and an NDA** ([[zra-ictd-mou]]) with the
  **[[international-centre-for-tax-and-development|ICTD]]** on research, capacity building and innovation
  for **evidence-based, data-driven tax administration** and domestic resource mobilisation. Commissioner
  General **Dingani Banda** cast it as answering "the growing complexities of the digital economy"; ICTD ED
  **Giulia Mascagni** framed it around DRM. It is the **fiscal-capacity** limb of Zambian state
  digitisation, running outside the SZI/ZamPortal spine — and the **NDA is the interesting half**: what
  taxpayer microdata moves, and under what safeguards, is not disclosed. See [[capacity.research]],
  [[finance.mou]]. Source: [[2026-07-17-zambia-revenue-authority-ictd-deal]].

## The legal frame — and the anti-corruption claim

- **E-Government Act amendment (2026).** SZI's Percy Chinyama argued **digitisation has reduced corruption by
  "removing the human factor,"** and that the **Electronic Government Act No. 41 of 2021 Amendment Bill**
  fixes the legal environment that "currently hinders digital transformation" (the 2021 Act created SZI and
  gave e-service delivery statutory standing). *(News Diggers lede-only.)* See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-24-digitisation-reduced-corruption-smart-zambia]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Zambia's govtech is distinctive for **who builds it** — SMART Zambia's own e-Government
  Division and **local ICT firms (CoreLink)**, not foreign primes — matching the open-standards, avoid-
  lock-in posture of the [[zambia--dpi-id|ID]] and the Cabinet open-source directive (ZMB hub). Consolidating
  everything behind one ZamPortal/ZamPay rail is a genuine single-front-door design, and the anti-corruption
  framing ("remove the human factor") is the government's honest political case for it. The unstated risk is
  the same one the whole Zambia file carries: **a state digitising service delivery on top of thin
  connectivity and thin civil registration** — the portal is only as inclusive as the fibre and the IDs
  beneath it reach (see [[zambia--dpi-id]], and connectivity on the [[ZMB]] hub).
