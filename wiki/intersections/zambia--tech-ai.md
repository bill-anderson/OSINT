# Zambia × Artificial Intelligence

Zambia's AI agenda is assembling **out of order**. The compute arrangements were contracted before
the policy existed; the regional strategy is being drafted alongside the national one; and the
flagship national AI institution on offer is a **UN-agency lab**, not a domestic body. The talent
pipeline that has produced Zambia's only concrete international result to date sits in a private
innovation hub operating, on its own account, under resource constraints.

## Policy — announced, not published

- **[[zambia-national-ai-strategy|National AI Strategy]] "nearing completion" (2026-07-17).**
  Announced by **Kusobile Kamwambi**, head of the
  **[[presidential-delivery-unit-zambia|Presidential Delivery Unit]]**, who invited stakeholder
  contributions; a **[[ministry-of-technology-and-science-zambia|Ministry of Technology and
  Science]]** representative reaffirmed commitment to advancing AI through the strategy **and a
  Startup Bill** — the first mention of that bill on file. **No draft text, publication date,
  budget or institutional architecture is held for either.** See [[gov.policy]],
  [[gov.legislate]]. Source: [[2026-07-17-zambia-ai-leadership-robotics-challenge]].
- **The regional track runs in parallel (2026-07-14).** Lusaka hosted the [[comesa]] /
  [[world-bank]] regional-AI workshop under [[idea-mpa]], a first step toward a **COMESA Regional
  AI Strategy** framed explicitly against policy fragmentation. Zambia is therefore drafting a
  national instrument while helping shape the regional one that would frame it. See
  [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-14 Zambia and COMESA Launch AI Initiative to Advance Regional Digital Transformation]].

## Institutions — a proposed ITU lab

- **An [[itu]] "AI for Good Lab", offered not agreed (2026-07-17).** The ministry disclosed an
  opportunity to establish **Zambia's first AI for Good Lab** with the ITU under the UN framework,
  expected to support AI policy development, innovation, skills development and digital
  infrastructure. **[[e-mark-innovation-hub]]** CEO Emmanuel Mwanza welcomed it and called for
  stronger national investment in AI infrastructure, digital skills and innovation ecosystems.
  **An opportunity — no signature, funding, siting or timetable is reported.** Source:
  [[2026-07-17-zambia-ai-leadership-robotics-challenge]].

## Compute — contracted ahead of the policy

- **The [[huawei]] "sovereign AI" MoU (2026-06-08).** SMART Zambia and Huawei signed
  **[[national-ai-data-centre-zambia]]** at the 2026 Zambia Mobile Congress: AI infrastructure,
  sovereign cloud, DPI for 25 ministries, a co-invested national data centre keeping critical data
  in-country, and **~5,000 youth** trained via the Huawei Global Academy. The sovereignty paradox
  the wiki records for Zambia: a "sovereign" AI and cloud build delivered by the Chinese vendor
  that built the earlier Zambia National Data Centre. See [[geopol.china]], [[infra.store]],
  [[ZMB]]. Source: [[2026-06-08-zambia-huawei-ai-sovereignty-partnership]].

## Talent — the one concrete result

- **Team Zambia reaches the global finals (2026-07-17).** Zambia advanced to the global finals of
  the **2026 AI for Good Global Summit and Robotics for Good Youth Challenge** in Geneva, among
  **nearly 50 participating countries (2026)**, and has been **invited to the 2027 summit**. The
  delegation came out of a **nine-month** identification and mentoring cycle run by
  **[[e-mark-innovation-hub]]** — a private hub, whose CEO stressed the talent pool is real but
  that investment in research, technology and innovation is not. The recognition ceremony was
  co-organised by the Ministry of Technology and Science, the PDU, E-MARK, the Ministry of
  Education and the Ministry of Youth, Sport and Arts. Deputy Secretary to the Cabinet **Dr Oliver
  Kalabo** called it a national milestone; officials tied it to President Hakainde Hichilema's
  "knowledge-based digital economy" vision. See [[capacity.training]], [[tech.innovate]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-zambia-ai-leadership-robotics-challenge]].
- **Into the school system.** Ministry of Education Permanent Secretary (Administration) **Noriana
  Muneku** said the challenge **aligns with Zambia's Competency-Based Curriculum** — strengthening
  STEM, creativity and problem-solving — and urged closer collaboration with E-MARK to identify
  talent nationally, **including in rural communities**. Ministry of Youth, Sport and Arts PS
  **Kangwa Chileshe** cited youth resource centres equipped with **[[starlink]]** connectivity,
  innovation hubs, digital-entrepreneurship programmes and the Youth Portal. See [[digital.rural]],
  [[include.divides]]. Source: [[2026-07-17-zambia-ai-leadership-robotics-challenge]].

## Applied AI — the inclusion case

- **Voice AI on feature phones ([[viamo]], 2026-01).** *Ask Viamo Anything* — dial-in AI voice
  responses on any handset — is cited as a leading feature-phone inclusion example, **59% women
  users** in early GSMA studies. Against Zambia's device and literacy constraints (see [[ZMB]]),
  this remains the only deployed AI application on file reaching Zambians directly. See
  [[capacity.literacy]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Zambia's stated AI ambition — "a regional leader in emerging technologies" — is
  being pursued through three externally-supplied channels at once: Chinese vendor compute, a UN
  agency lab, and a World-Bank-scaffolded regional strategy. The domestic contributions on file
  are the **talent** (E-MARK's pipeline, the Geneva result) and the **curriculum hook**
  (Competency-Based Curriculum alignment) — real, but the smallest-financed parts of the stack.
  The sequencing is the finding: a strategy drafted *after* the data-centre MoU governs less than
  a strategy drafted before it.
- **What is not held.** No AI budget line, no compute inventory, no strategy text, no lab
  agreement, no participant numbers for E-MARK's programme, and no figure behind the PDU's claim
  that ICT is among Zambia's fastest-growing sectors.
