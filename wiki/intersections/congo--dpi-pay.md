# Congo-Brazzaville × Digital payments & financial inclusion

A **mobile-money duopoly** over a thin banked base, an **interoperability layer forming via a licensed
aggregator**, private-sector entrants, and a **tax debate** that inverts the usual framing.

## The market

Densest COG payments dataset (a 2025 tribune): **MTN 74.5% / Airtel 25.5%** of 3.431m mobile-money subs
(2023), via **MMC** (MTN×Ecobank) and **MCC** (Airtel×BGFI); fees crept **2.5%→3.5%** since 2019. *Two
figures to reconcile as metric-definition clashes, not contradictions:* **banking penetration 17%→7%
(2023)**, and **mobile internet 61% (2023)** vs GSMA's 19%. Source:
[[2025-02-24-inclusion-financiere-mobile-money-tribune-okamba-ldb]].

## Interoperability, entrants & remittances

- **A licensed aggregator.** **[[kerry-payments-congo|Kerry Payments]]** (a **PawaPay** subsidiary,
  Brazzaville SARLU) secured a **VAS payment-aggregator licence from ARPCE** — a **unified API across MNOs,
  banks and MFIs**, concrete evidence on the ARPCE/BEAC mobile-money co-regulation question GSMA flagged.
  Source: [[2026-06-16-pawapay-kerry-payments-vas-aggregator-licence-techafricanews]].
- **Private entrant.** **[[gozem-congo|Gozem]]** launched in Congo-Brazzaville after a **$30m Series B**
  (MSC's SAS Shipping, Al Mada Ventures) — and, notably, **Yango has exited Brazzaville**. Source:
  [[2025-11-07-gozem-launches-congo-brazzaville-wearetech]].
- **Remittances.** **[[digipay-congo|DigiPay]]** (founded in Congo-B) × Belmoney (licensed by the National
  Bank of Belgium) opened a **DigiTransfer** FR/BE → COG (+COD) corridor on a RaaS model, targeting the SDG
  3% cost cap. Sources: [[2026-05-28-belmoney-digipay-digitransfer-remittances-connectingafrica]],
  [[2026-05-28-belmoney-digipay-instant-transfer-app-wearetech]].
- **Sectoral rails.** Airtel Money into **education** (E-SCHOOL fees, YABISOSOFT) and MTN Congo into an
  **obstetric-referral HMIS** (Moukondo → 50 maternities; ~6 maternal deaths weekly). Sources:
  [[2026-04-23-airtel-money-education-digitalisation-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-05-16-mtn-congo-digital-obstetric-reference-project-techreviewafrica]].

## The tax that funds connectivity (inverting GSMA)

A levy in force from **1 Jun 2026** on newly **imported** SIM-equipped terminals — the government frames it
as **funding connectivity**, the inverse of GSMA's "taxes are the brake" diagnosis (reconcile against the
wiki's "FCFA 10,000 SIM tax"). Source: [[2026-07-17-taxe-nouveaux-terminaux-numeriques-cartes-sim-ldb]].
