# Niger × Digital Identity & CRVS

Niger runs **two parallel foundational-ID tracks** at once — a **sovereignty-branded AES card** built with a
Libyan vendor, and a **World-Bank WURI/MOSIP open-source** programme — over a very thin connectivity base
(**23.2%** internet penetration, end-2025).

## Track 1 — the AES card & e-passport (sovereignty)

- **CNIB-AES mass enrolment (from 25 Apr 2026).** The junta launched **mass enrolment** for the AES biometric
  national ID at Niamey's Yantala commissariat — President **Tiani** the first enrollee (27 Mar), PM **Zeine** on
  9 Apr — rolled out across the **20 commissariats of Niamey** then the interior. The card is **mandatory for
  15+, valid 5 years, 4,500 FCFA**, capturing **fingerprint, face, voice and iris**. It is the harmonised
  **[[cib-aes|CIB-AES]]** credential of the **[[alliance-of-sahel-states|AES]]** (Niger the 2nd adopter after
  Burkina), enabling passport-free AES travel. See [[gov.regional]], [[niger-national-eid]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-07-cnib-aes-mass-enrolment-launch-lesahel]].
- **AES e-passport phase (from 16 Jul 2026).** Tiani became the **first enrollee for the AES biometric
  passport** — a second phase (launched Aug 2025) delivering **five categories** (ordinary, service, diplomatic,
  refugee, and an **innovative Hadj** passport), produced by the **Police DST** with **[[al-itissal-al-jadeed|Al
  Itissal Al Jadeed]]** and local partner **HASDI**. See [[niger-national-eid]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-16-aes-biometric-passport-tiani-first-enrollee-actuniger]].
- **Paying the fee — NITA kiosks (2026-04).** Money-transfer leader **[[nita-niger|NITA Transfert]]** deployed
  kiosks at enrolment sites so citizens could pay the 4,500-FCFA fee on the spot (receipts for the enrolment
  file) — putting a fintech at the **ID × payments** intersection (NITA is also a bank-to-wallet interoperability
  actor). See [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-04-27-cnib-aes-enrolment-nita-payment-kiosks-actuniger]].

## Track 2 — WURI / MOSIP (World Bank, open-source)

- **WURI operational despite the ECOWAS exit.** Niger's **[[wuri-programme|WURI]]** project (launched 2022; goal
  **22m** enrolled by 2026) remains active: a **May-2025 birth-certificate pilot in Filingué** targeted
  **21,202 under-fives** and digitised existing local birth records — CRVS reform tied to foundational ID. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]]. Source: [[2025-05-01-wuri-niger-birth-certificate-pilot-filingue-biometricupdate]].
- **A MOSIP open-source pilot (2025-02).** Under WURI-Niger and the Interior Ministry, **[[bioenable]]** (India)
  ran a **[[mosip|MOSIP]]**-based national-digital-ID pilot across **four communes** (Tanout, Mayahi, Doutchi,
  Tagazar), **3,000+ enrolled** with dual-iris scanners — an **open-source/interoperability** ID track distinct
  from the Al-Itissal/AES path. See [[tech.industry]]. Source: [[2025-02-21-bioenable-mosip-national-digital-id-pilot-biometricupdate]].

## The critique

- **Surveillance, exclusion, vendor dependence (2026-04, Connecting Africa).** Analyst **Sani Suleiman** warns
  the rollout risks "privacy breaches, state surveillance and systemic exclusion" amid **weak legal frameworks,
  limited oversight and reliance on foreign vendors** — biometric systems integrating electoral + civil data
  give governments "vast surveillance capabilities while disenfranchising rural communities, migrants and those
  without foundational IDs." Cost (~**US$6**) and **23.2%** penetration mean years to universal coverage; the
  privacy backstop is the young **[[hapdp-niger|HAPDP]]** regime. See [[gov.protect]], [[include.divides]].
  Source: [[2026-04-21-niger-rollout-biometric-ids-aes-connectingafrica]].

The through-line: **identity as digital sovereignty** for a junta-led AES state — even as a parallel
World-Bank/open-source track keeps running underneath. See [[NER]], [[dpi.id]], [[sovereignty]].
