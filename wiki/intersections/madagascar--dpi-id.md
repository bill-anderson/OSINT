# Madagascar × Digital Identity & Civil Registry

Madagascar's flagship digital-transformation story is the **World Bank-funded [[prodigy-madagascar|PRODIGY]]**
biometric national-ID + civil-registry reform (**RECI**) — a mass enrolment assigning every citizen a
**Numéro d'Identification Unique (NUI)** under the "**Izaho Tokana**" ("one person, one identity")
principle — delivered across regime change, civil unrest and a cyclone.

## What we know

- **Vendor awards (2025-05).** [[dpi.registry]] Under PRODIGY (~$143m World Bank), **[[in-groupe|IN Groupe]]**
  won the **€8.5m** civil-registry/database-management contract and **[[laxton]]** won the **biometric
  registration kits** (over Augentic/Thales/Idemia). Source:
  [[2025-05-16-madagascar-selects-in-groupe-8-5m-digital-id-biometricupdate]].
- **Procurement & data centres (2025-12).** [[infra.store]] The **[[ugd-madagascar|UGD]]** opened **five
  tenders** — biometric-enrolment supplies (bid deadline 15 Jan 2026) and **rehabilitation of the main
  data centre + national ID-card personalisation centre at Tsimbazaza** plus the **secondary DC at
  Iavoloha** — after an Aug-2025 rejig moved the closing date to **30 Jun 2026** (national/direct
  procurement for local sourcing). Source: [[2025-12-22-prodigy-tenders-data-center-biometricupdate]].
- **RECI framing & training (2026-01).** [[gov.legislate]] The **Ministry of Interior & Decentralisation
  (MID)** ran a RECI strategy workshop (Anosy, 21–22 Jan) — a legal, secure identity for every citizen by
  **2030** — deploying **2,500 enrolment kits** (mobile/ultra-mobile for enclaved zones), with **70
  districts trained**. Source: [[2026-01-22-modernisation-etat-civil-reci-biometrique-midi]].
- **Enrolment launch (2026-01).** [[dpi.id]] Biometric enrolment launched **27 Jan 2026** (national
  coordinator Fidèle Rafalimanana), starting with top officials then **Atsimondrano** district — **2,500
  kits across 1,729 centres**, 6 days/week, targeting **2m adults (Apr–Jun)** then **12–13m** citizens
  (40 enrolments/day/kit). Source: [[2026-01-24-reforme-enrolement-biometrique-lexpress]].
- **POC validated, national rollout (2026-03).** [[dpi.id]] The steering committee (COPIL) **unanimously
  validated the pilot/POC results**, greenlighting nationwide deployment of the 2,500 kits. Source:
  [[2026-03-16-etat-civil-enrolement-biometrique-national-lexpress]].
- **Pilot delivery amid crisis (2026-04).** [[dpi.id]] [[sovereignty]] The Jan-2026 pilot (under President
  Michael Randrianirina) enrolled **~130,000 across 116 centres in 26 communes** — **100% data
  completeness, 99% kit availability**, <10-min average — despite **regime change/civil unrest and a
  cyclone** ([[laxton]] delivery). Source: [[2026-04-21-madagascar-biometric-enrollment-laxton-biometricupdate]].

- **Enrolment at scale — a dated progression (2026).** [[include.access]] Enrolment climbed through the
  window (**different scopes/dates, not a contradiction**): **2.6M in two months** (surpassing the 2m
  target, ~10-min average, DPI expert Tariq Malik on the "retroactive" civil-registration framework, 5
  Jun); **3.25M adults** (World Bank, 10 Jun; only ~50,000 had never held any ID document); **>5.6M total**
  (L'Express, 24 Jun) — with the "Izaho Tokana" volet continuing to year-end after PRODIGY's 30-Jun close.
  Sources: [[2026-06-05-madagascar-2-6m-digital-id-enrollments-biometricupdate]],
  [[2026-07-01-no-longer-invisible-legal-identity-worldbank]],
  [[2026-06-24-population-enregistrement-biometrique-5m-lexpress]].
- **Mass-birth-registration law (2026-05-28).** [[gov.legislate]] With ~**6m adults (≈40%) lacking a
  birth certificate**, a **28 May 2026 law** allows births to be registered **en masse** within the
  biometric-enrolment operation — bypassing the costly judicial late-registration process; **2,000+ teams**
  (26 days/month) and **19,000+ fokontany chiefs** confirm undocumented identities against a de-duplicated
  biometric database. The legal framework went operational **May 2026**; an **offline-first, online-later**
  last-mile model targets the most excluded. Sources:
  [[2026-07-01-no-longer-invisible-legal-identity-worldbank]],
  [[2026-07-06-last-mile-civil-registration-biometricupdate]].
- **AI-digitised civil archives + renewable data centres (2026).** [[tech.ai]] [[infra.store]]
  **[[studia-inc]]** digitised **~10m civil records** (1,695 communes, OCR/AI indexing, mobile satellite/
  solar units) under EC-MADA; and the **$543m PRODIGY+DECIM** World Bank review (24 Jun) confirmed two
  **renewable-powered data centres (Tsimbazaza, Iavoloha)** securing the ID system, plus **[[decim-madagascar|DECIM]]**'s
  664,000 devices distributed. Sources: [[2026-05-08-studia-ai-digitisation-civil-records-intelligentcio]],
  [[2026-06-24-madagascar-worldbank-review-543m-prodigy-decim-ecofin]].
