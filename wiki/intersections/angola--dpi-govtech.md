# Angola × Other GovTech and e-Gov

Angola is assembling a **coordinated e-government stack from a low base** (UN EGDI **156/193**, though up **18 places in a decade**): a single umbrella agenda, a concrete DPI contract, a PKI trust layer, and sectoral and local-government rollouts. The state framing is **sovereignty and interoperability** ("no technological islands"); the thin but valuable user-side evidence says the citizen experience lags the architecture.

## What we know

**The umbrella agenda — Governo Digital 2027.** The **Agenda de Transição Digital da Administração Pública** now counts **218 structuring projects** (2026-03-05) across six objectives and seven axes, up from **214 projects** presented at the World Bank Digital Summit (Washington, March 2025). It is coordinated by the **[[ima-angola|IMA]]** under the Casa Civil, with the World Bank **PADA** underwriting the **Identidade Digital Única** and **Infra-estrutura Nacional de Dados**. The government wants a **Lei-Quadro do Governo Digital** (framework law) to standardise technology and enforce institutional coordination, and signed three MoUs — broadband, digital inclusion, and the **national digital-identity system**. Minister Dionísio da Fonseca: a real digital state is judged by cut bureaucracy, not platform count.

**The concrete DPI build — the $150m IMA–TIS contract.** On 2026-06-17 IMA signed a **US$150m** contract (Despacho Presidencial 38/26, 12-month execution, in the 2026 PIP) with **[[tis-angola|TIS Tech Angola]]** for the **Projecto de Infra-estruturas Digitais Públicas**: a secure-zone **critical-computing component** hosting the **interoperability platform**, **PKI-based e-signatures**, **digital identity**, and structured **cybersecurity** — the technological backbone for cross-ministry data exchange. A sole-referenced state ICT award under a presidential order (procurement route unstated), echoing the sole-source pattern on the MINDCOM $80m network and the FATCA licence.

**The PKI / e-signature trust layer.** Announced in mid-2025 ([[2025-06-30-infra-estrutura-de-chaves-publicas-e-carimbo-do-tempo]]) and elaborated at the 7th IMA Forum (2026-05-23), Angola is building **public-key infrastructure and time-stamping** as the trust pillars of a paperless state — validating acts and contracts remotely, curbing date-tampering fraud. **[[agt-angola|AGT]]** is the vanguard: **e-invoicing mandatory from 1 Jan 2026** (500+ software registered, 21 certified), all e-invoices **auto-reported to AGT** with a verification code; and a **cross-border data flow to the US IRS** under the **FATCA** agreement (€552k licence, 2026-06-08) — a sovereignty edge case, with Angolan financial-account data routed to a foreign authority.

**Sectoral and public-finance digitisation.** The Ministry of Finance's **[[i-lab-angola|i.Lab]]** (SETIC-FP, launched March 2026) tests solutions like qualified digital signatures in its SIGD document system — where **[[serpro|Serpro]]** (Brazil) president Wilton Mota framed public-sector innovation as sovereignty ("the State needs to own its own digital destiny"), and Angola signed an Angola–Cape Verde digital-governance pact. **Social security**: a Regime Jurídico da Comunicação e Tramitação Electrónica bill (2025-11-26) digitises **INSS** procedures (contribution statements, e-payslips, pension requests), keeping physical channels where technology is lacking.

**What it costs, and who pays for it (2025–26).** The e-government stack is funded from **three separate places**, none of them the MINTTICS vote — which is why a sector-vote reading of the budget misses most of it. (1) The **proposta OGE 2026** puts **Kz 136,7 mil milhões** in *Capacitação e Modernização da Administração Pública* and **Kz 89,6 mil milhões** in *Reforma e Modernização da Administração da Justiça*, both described as carrying digital-transformation and digital-access projects — but each also funds non-digital reform, so no digital share is separable ([[2025-11-07-ago-oge-2026-modernizacao-digital-programas]]). (2) **Presidential despachos** authorise spend directly: **DP 12/26** (7-Jan-2026) **Kz 9,2 mil milhões** for telecoms circuits and infrastructure hosting across MINFIN and its supervised bodies, opened as a framework-agreement tender ([[2026-01-07-ago-dp-12-26-minfin-telecoms-9-2bn]]); **DP 11/26** (13-Jan-2026) **Kz 8,6 mil milhões** for **Microsoft licences for the whole public administration**, procured centrally by IMA — 80,000 licences in 2026, IMA claiming an ~80% saving on per-institution buying, under Microsoft Tech for Social Impact's DDP/DPG programmes ([[2026-01-16-ago-dp-11-26-microsoft-licencas-8-6bn]]). A centralised state commitment to one foreign hyperscaler's licensing stack sits awkwardly beside the sovereign-cloud framing. (3) The **$150m IMA–TIS contract** above, under DP 38/26 in the 2026 PIP.

**The execution gap.** Voting the money is not spending it. The first published per-organ execution table (**Q1 2026**) puts **MINTTICS at 3% execution**, with 20 of 50 programmes below 5%, against a whole-of-government Q1 spend of Kz 9,12 biliões ([[2026-04-29-ago-oge-2026-i-trimestre-execucao-lei-identificacao]]). The report itself is not held — queued in `reviews/acquisitions.md` as `ago-execucao-i-2026` — so the 3% figure rests on trade-press reading of a MINFIN table, dated and attributed as such.

**Sub-national rollout.** Cubango province approved **SIGDA** (integrated document/archive management) from January 2026 ([[digital.localgov]]) — a provincial move to electronic administration with a citizen app for proposals and public-tender tracking, explicitly budgeting around **weak internet and power** in municipalities like Mavengue.

**Rare user-side evidence.** At the SME (migration) and DTSER (traffic) services, digitisation of passports, driving licences and vehicle documents (mid-2025) **ended the queues and the informal "mixeiros" fixer economy** ([[2025-11-06-servicos-digitais-no-sme-e-dtser-acabam-com-enchentes]]) — users only attend in person, by appointment, for fingerprints/signature. But citizens report the **DTSER portal is hard to use** (first-time licence steps unclear) and **document delivery remains slow** — a concrete reminder that killing the middleman is not the same as a usable service.
