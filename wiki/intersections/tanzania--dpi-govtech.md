# Tanzania × E-Government & Digital Economy

Tanzania's e-government is its **strongest external scorecard**: a World Bank **GTMI Group A** (top tier)
classification built on a state-built interoperability backbone (**GovESB**) and core systems, wrapped in a
ten-year **Digital Economy Strategic Framework (2024–2034)** with concrete targets. The build is genuinely
state-led — but the growth story leans on foreign partners (Huawei, ITU, India) and a startup ecosystem
still small in absolute terms.

## Top-tier maturity — GTMI Group A

- **World Bank GTMI 2025 (Group A).** Tanzania was classified **Group A – Extensive GovTech Maturity** (top
  tier) in the GTMI 2025 (released Dec 2025) — having climbed **90th (2021) → 26th (2022)**, from Group B to
  A, **2nd in Africa after Mauritius** and leading East Africa, now grouped with Kenya, Egypt, Uganda and
  Rwanda. e-GA DG **Benedict Ndomba** stressed the year-long, evidence-based assessment. Core systems
  behind it: **HCIMS** (payroll), the **Ajira Portal** (recruitment), and **GovESB** as the interoperability
  backbone. Source: [[2026-01-13-tanzania-joins-global-leaders-digital-government]].
- **GovESB, made mandatory (Jun 2025).** PM **Kassim Majaliwa** ordered **all public institutions onto
  GovESB by 30 July 2025** (built free by the **[[ega-tanzania|e-GA]]** for secure system-to-system data
  exchange), launching it alongside **e-Wekeza** (a public-servant investment fund). See [[dpi.exchange]].
  Source: [[2025-06-23-majaliwa-taasisi-umma-kujiunga-govesb]].

## The Digital Economy Strategic Framework (2024–2034)

- **Targets (TAIC 2026).** At the 9th Tanzania Annual ICT Conference the framework's goals were set out:
  digital economy **>$1.1bn to GDP by 2034**; **ICT to 3% of GDP by 2029** (from ~1.5% in 2022/23); **80%
  broadband penetration** and **mobile money >70%** by 2029; **innovation hubs in every region** and
  **1,000 startups in five years**. By Dec 2025: **758 towers** on, 304 upgraded 2G→3G/4G, **660 public
  institutions connected** (target 900). ICTC's Mwasaga: **~95% of digital infrastructure is in place** —
  the shift now is "from building networks to driving innovation." Source:
  [[2026-01-22-digital-economy-1bn-gdp-2034]].
- **Adoption across sectors (2026-04).** Government-backed systems (**WEZESHA, TANCIS, TIPS, TMX, NeST**)
  are spreading across finance, aviation, transport, marine, trade and agriculture; **Sh200bn** allocated
  for startups/MSMEs; **mobile money Sh43.4tn in Q4-2025** (TCRA); ICT projected **+13.5% in 2026**; the
  Startup Ecosystem Report 2024 counts **1,041 active startups (+24%), 138,453 jobs, FDI doubled to $53m**
  (fintech $41.4m). A ten-year **Tanzania Electronic Data Governance Strategy (TZeDGS)** was validated with
  **UNECA** (Mar 2026). See [[tech.industry]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-21-digital-economy-systems-adoption-key-sectors]].

## Foreign partners in the growth layer

- **ITU Acceleration Center (Apr 2026).** Tanzania is launching one of **17 ITU Acceleration Centers**
  worldwide (hosting agreement Feb 2024; final Geneva approval), **managed by the [[ict-commission-tanzania|ICTC]]**
  with **[[tcra]]** involved — anchored, tellingly, on the **[[jamii-namba|Jamii Namba]]** ID as the DPI
  foundation (PS Ally Senga Gugu linking national ID to economic growth *and national security*). See
  [[gov.regional]], [[tech.innovate]]. Source: [[2026-04-02-tanzania-itu-innovation-center-launch]].
- **Huawei in government and banking (Jun 2025).** **[[huawei]]** (with distributor Redington) pledged
  support for public-institution and **banking data hosting**, launching **Wi-Fi 7 for government offices**
  and an SME "eKit." A [[geopol.china]] presence in the e-gov/data-hosting layer. Source:
  [[2025-06-20-huawei-yajitosa-kuboresha-huduma-kidijitali]].
- **India in Zanzibar (Jul 2026).** **[[iit-madras-zanzibar|IIT Madras Zanzibar]]** — IIT Madras's first
  international campus — graduated Data-Science/AI cohorts as Zanzibar pitches itself as an African
  innovation hub (Minister Mudrik Soraga), targeting **100 startups** from the islands. See
  [[capacity.training]]. Source: [[2026-07-05-zanzibar-positions-africas-technology-innovation-hub]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: e-government is where Tanzania's "build it yourself" model has the best external
  validation — a GTMI Group A rooted in **state-owned core systems and a state-built service bus**, made
  mandatory by prime-ministerial fiat. That is real GovTech sovereignty. The complication is the growth
  layer stacked on top: the ID that anchors the ITU centre is explicitly framed as a **national-security**
  asset (echoing the IGP surveillance critique on [[tanzania--gov-protect]]), the data-hosting push invites
  **Huawei**, and the innovation ambition leans on **ITU and Indian** institutions — sovereign plumbing,
  partnered growth. The startup numbers (1,041 firms, $53m FDI) show the ambition is still early against the
  $1tn-by-2050 rhetoric.
