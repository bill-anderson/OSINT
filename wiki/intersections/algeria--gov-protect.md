# Algeria × Data Protection & Sovereignty

Algeria pairs a personal-data regime with an unusually hard **data-localisation** stance and a state-centric data-sovereignty model.

- **Personal-data law.** **Law No. 18-07** (enacted 2018; in force Aug 2023) governs data processing, enforced by the **[[anpdcp|National Authority for the Protection of Personal Data (ANPDCP)]]**. **Data must be stored in-country** — mandatory localisation, which also shapes the cloud market (no hyperscaler present).
- **Public-data governance.** The **[[algeria-data-governance-framework|national data-governance framework]]** (Presidential Decree 25-350, Dec 2025; launched Feb 2026) treats data as a sovereign strategic asset — interoperability, a unified national data-source database, and information-system security across public administrations.
- **Digital identity & trust.** The 2025 **[[algeria-digital-id-trust-law|digital-ID & trust-services law]]** modernises the 2015 e-signature framework and consolidates biometric data into a unified identity platform, with certified trust-service providers.

The through-line is **technological sovereignty**: localisation + sovereign cloud + a state-owned data-centre and AI-compute base. See [[DZA]], [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]].
