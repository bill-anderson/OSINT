# Algeria × Data Protection & Sovereignty

Algeria pairs a personal-data regime with an unusually hard **data-localisation** stance and a state-centric data-sovereignty model.

- **Images are personal data, and posting them abroad may be a transfer (délibération adopted
  2026-07-15).** The ANPDP adopted a délibération on **Wednesday 15 July 2026** applying
  **[[algeria-law-18-07|Law 18-07]]** to visual content: **any photo or video permitting direct or
  indirect identification of a natural person is personal data**, so collection, storage, retention
  and publication require a legal basis and must satisfy lawfulness, transparency, proportionality
  and purpose limitation. Publishing images publicly on social or digital platforms needs **free,
  explicit, prior and verifiable consent** unless a statutory exception applies, preceded by notice
  of controller identity, purpose, distribution method, retention period and the rights of access,
  rectification, objection and consent withdrawal. **The localisation doctrine is extended to
  posting**: the Authority holds that diffusing images on social networks **whose infrastructure is
  hosted outside Algeria may constitute an international transfer of personal data**, so controllers
  must satisfy **arts. 44–45** adequacy requirements — which, read literally, reaches routine use of
  foreign platforms by Algerian controllers. Controllers must implement technical and organisational
  measures against unauthorised access, loss, alteration or disclosure; data subjects may complain to
  the ANPDP in addition to the courts, and non-compliance carries the statutory sanctions. It sits in
  a run of ANPDP application texts — workplace **video surveillance** and **DPO designation**
  preceded it. *The délibération's own number, text and commencement are not held as of 2026-07-20;
  the capture is a press account of it.* See [[gov.legislate]], [[DZA]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-20-algerie-anpdp-regles-publication-images-plateformes]].

- **Personal-data law.** **Law No. 18-07** (enacted 2018; in force Aug 2023), **amended by Law 25-11**,
  governs data processing, enforced by the **[[anpdcp|National Authority for the Protection of Personal Data
  (ANPDCP/ANPDP)]]** (president Samir Bourehil). **Data must be stored in-country** — mandatory localisation,
  which also shapes the cloud market (no hyperscaler present). ANPDP began an active compliance push in 2025-12
  (DPO, processing register, audit log), and a 2026-07 DGSN study day tied data protection to national security.
- **Public-data governance.** The **[[algeria-data-governance-framework|national data-governance framework]]**
  (**Presidential Decree n° 25-320 of 30 Dec 2025**, *Journal Officiel* n° 87 — launched Feb 2026) treats data
  as a sovereign strategic asset — interoperability, a unified national data-source database, and
  information-system security across public administrations. (Some coverage, including the held English source,
  numbers it **25-350**; no such act appears in that gazette issue — verified against the JORADP text,
  2026-07-20, now held: [[2025-12-30-joradp-decret-25-320-gouvernance-donnees]].) On personal data the decree
  is deferential rather than novel: Art. 4 subjects personal data within the dispositif to the existing
  personal-data-protection regime, and **Art. 15 gives [[anpdcp|ANPDCP]] the job of policing whether the
  classification and cataloguing of personal data conform** to that regime — a supervisory hook, not a new
  substantive standard. **Art. 18 excludes defence, national-security and judicial data** from the framework
  altogether. A named expert critiques the decree for
  omitting **open-data / valorisation** — flows stay locked inside public institutions.
  **Not yet in force on its core duties.** Art. 8 leaves the two *référentiels* (classification;
  data-source cataloguing) to be published **by decision of the High Commissioner for Digitalisation**, and
  Art. 19 starts the classification and cataloguing obligations only **from the date those référentiels are
  published**. **As of 2026-07-20 no such publication has been traced** in the *Journal Officiel* or in any
  HCN announcement — so the decree's operative duties are **not shown to have commenced**, six months after
  gazetting and five after the 9 Feb 2026 launch. The launch installed a national follow-up commission; it
  did not trigger Art. 19. (Negative finding from a French-language search, not a proven absence — a
  *décision* could be gazetted without press notice. See
  [[algeria-data-governance-framework]].)
- **Digital identity & trust.** The 2025 **[[algeria-digital-id-trust-law|digital-ID & trust-services law]]** modernises the 2015 e-signature framework and consolidates biometric data into a unified identity platform, with certified trust-service providers.

The through-line is **technological sovereignty**: localisation + sovereign cloud + a state-owned data-centre and AI-compute base. See [[DZA]], [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]].
