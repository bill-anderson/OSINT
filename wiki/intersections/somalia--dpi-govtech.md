# Somalia × GovTech & the DPI Question

Somalia is standing up **service-delivery DPI** — a national job portal, an electronic immunization
register — on the ID/payments spine. But the country's own analysts warn the central risk is **not
failing to digitise, but digitising the existing fragmentation**.

## Service platforms

- **National Job Portal (2026).** The **Somali National Job Portal for Employment Opportunities**
  replaces a clan/diaspora/WhatsApp "patchwork hiring culture" with a **centralised, domestically-hosted**
  platform — where each ministry previously recruited in silos (vacancies on office gates and radio). A
  test case for whether unified digital infrastructure can deliver transparency and trust. See
  [[capacity.training]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source: [[2026-02-25-somalia-national-job-portal-dpi]].
- **Electronic Immunization Register (EIR, 2026).** Built on the open-source **DHIS2** (WHO support),
  the EIR assigns each vaccinated child a **unique ID**, stores dose histories and sends SMS reminders,
  giving district/state/federal officials near-real-time data — piloted in **14 Banadir facilities**,
  scaling to **80+ nationwide**. Context: **~60% of children** are "zero-dose"; 3,000+ suspected
  diphtheria cases (2025). A concrete **ID-linked registry as a public good** — though power cuts and
  connectivity force "record on paper, upload later." See [[dpi.mis]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-27-somalia-electronic-immunization-register]].

## The fragmentation warning

- **"Digitising the same fragmentation" (2026).** An analysis (echoing the DPI Summit 2026 communique)
  argues Somalia's digital landscape is **active but uncoordinated**: education platforms unconnected to
  identity, health data collected but not shared, records isolated and duplicated. The core problems are
  **(1) fragmentation** and **(2) weak coordination** — "digital infrastructure is treated as a technical
  problem when it is a governance problem" requiring role clarity, interoperability standards and
  accountability. "Institutions, not technology, create alignment." See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]].
  Source: [[2026-04-28-dpi-somalia-risks-repeating-old-mistakes]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the promising markers are **national ownership** (Somali-built NIRA tools, domestically-
  hosted job portal, open-source DHIS2/OpenCRVS) — but sovereignty of infrastructure is undercut if the
  systems don't interoperate. Somalia's DPI test is **governance and coordination**, not procurement:
  whether a fragile, federated state can impose the standards that make ID-linked registries a genuine
  public good rather than a set of new silos. Cf. the federal-state ID fault line in [[somalia--dpi-id]].
