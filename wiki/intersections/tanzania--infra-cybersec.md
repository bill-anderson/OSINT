# Tanzania × Cybersecurity

Tanzania scores well on the **international index** and runs an annual forum and skills partnerships — but
the ground-level picture is **weak institutional hygiene** (shared passwords, unpatched systems; see
[[tanzania--tech-ai|hospitals]]) and a **fraud economy that runs through the SIM-registration layer**.
Capable at the policy tier, exposed at the edge — the recurring Tanzania shape.

## The index and the forum

- **ITU GCI tier-1 (top 47).** Tanzania is placed in the **first tier (top 47 globally)** of the ITU
  **Global Cybersecurity Index** — cited as an investor draw (dated placement, cite-not-absorb). Source:
  [[2025-04-11-kuelekea-uchumi-kidijitali-usalama-mtandaoni]].
- **4th Cybersecurity Forum (Apr 2025).** The **[[ict-commission-tanzania|ICTC]]** ran the forum in Arusha;
  DG **Dr Nkundwe Mwasaga**: cyber safety is **"no longer optional"** for the 2030 digital-economy goal.
  Serensic Africa CEO **Esther Mengi** flagged phishing/ransomware against a backdrop of ~85% mobile
  penetration. See [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2025-04-10-experts-back-tanzania-cyber-security-push]].

## Building the skills

- **South Korea (KISA) training (Aug 2025).** ICTC partnered **South Korea's KISA** to train Tanzanian
  specialists in **digital forensics and misinformation detection** — Mwasaga noting Korea targets 10,000
  cyber experts and Tanzania needs "more," given its user base. See [[infra.capacity]]. Source:
  [[2025-08-17-tanzania-kuongeza-wataalamu-wadukuzi-mtandaoni]].
- **Public-sector hardening (Sep 2025).** The President's Office (PSMGG) PS **Juma Mkomi** urged public
  institutions to **strengthen cyber systems, hire ICT professionals and work with the [[ega-tanzania|e-GA]]**,
  and warned internal auditors to handle AI cautiously. Source:
  [[2025-09-23-government-urges-strengthen-cyber-security]].
- **Zanzibar MoU (Jun 2026).** Zanzibar's Ministry of Communication, ICT & Innovation signed a
  capacity-building MoU with **Serensic Africa** — targeting user awareness (phishing, weak passwords) and
  specialist skills (Deputy Minister Mohamed Sijamini for Minister Mudrick Ramadhan Soraga). See
  [[finance.mou]]. Source: [[2026-06-11-zanzibar-wizara-yawekeza-usalama-mtandao]].

## The fraud economy — through the SIM layer

- **Complicit registration agents (Jul 2026).** A Mwananchi investigation found **SIM-registration agents
  feeding online-fraud rings** ("halohalo"): Morogoro police (5 Mar 2026) seized **198 SIMs used in fraud,
  88 from a single agent** registered under different names; a Kilombero operation netted 10 suspects
  accused of stealing **Sh12m+**. Scams pose as job ads, rent demands, student emergencies and faith
  healing. The fraud rides the **SIM-registration/ID layer** — tying cyber-fraud directly to identity
  integrity (see [[tanzania--dpi-id]], where biometric SIM verification blocks ID-borrowing but agents
  remain a workaround). See [[dpi.id]]. Source: [[2026-07-13-nyuma-ya-pazia-utapeli-mtandaoni]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Tanzania's cybersecurity posture is genuinely institutional — a tier-1 GCI score, an annual
  forum, an e-GA coordinating role, and skills partnerships (notably with **South Korea**, a non-Western
  capacity partner). But the same evidence base shows the gap the index hides: **the weakest points are
  operational** — public hospitals and offices with poor hygiene, and a fraud economy that exploits the
  very SIM-registration and ID rails the state built for control. Cyber-*governance* is ahead of cyber-
  *hygiene*, and the identity infrastructure is both the defence and, via dishonest agents, the attack
  surface.
