# Zimbabwe — registries (dpi.registry)

Zimbabwe's registry reform runs on three tracks that do not yet meet: a **deeds
programme** with a fully drafted legal architecture but no commencement date; a **tax
registry** already operating a self-service portal; and a **population registry** whose
modernisation is the stated precondition for a digital ID that has not launched. The
common feature is that the most consequential dates are the ones the wiki cannot pin.

## The deeds programme: securitised title

**[[zimbabwe-si-76-2025]]** was **gazetted on 18 July 2025** by the **Minister for
Justice, Legal and Parliamentary Affairs** ([[ministry-of-justice-zimbabwe]]), creating a
class of **"securitised deeds"** to replace traditional paper title.

**The critical mechanic is a date the wiki does not hold.** The **24-month validation
window** runs **not from gazetting but from the date the Minister announces
commencement** — and **as of 1 August 2025 commencement had not been triggered**, the
regulations being only "expected to come into effect shortly"
([[2025-08-01 How to secure your property in Zimbabwe with the new digital deed system]]).
Since the window's start governs when every old deed in the country expires, the entire
scheme keys off an announcement that no held source records. **No source held establishes
whether commencement has since been triggered (GAP-ZWE-003).** Once the window closes,
**only securitised deeds are valid for legal and administrative purposes**, unless the
Minister — with the Registrar's approval — extends the transition **by notice published in
the Gazette**.

Mechanics of the new deed:

- Digitally recorded and **electronically authenticated**, with a **printed copy on
  security paper from a single supplier**, so the physical artefact is itself verifiable.
- **Prescribed drafting rules for conveyancers** — font size, type of ink, and rules on
  alterations.
- A **digital copy retained at the deeds office**; the holder receives the printed one.
- **Digital logins on the system are traceable**, creating a digital footprint for anyone
  dealing with a given property.
- **Criminal sanctions explicitly targeting Registry or government employees who collude
  with or assist fraudsters** — the insider threat is named in the instrument itself.
- Strict identity procedures and **notarisation** for deeds and powers of attorney.
- Extends to **farmland**: lease agreements, sub-leases and occupation arrangements, with
  supporting documents evidencing lawful occupation also submitted and digitised.

Existing holders must **submit original deeds to the Registrar for validation** within the
window; once validated, the Registrar issues the securitised deed. An implementation
framework from the Registrar's office, **including fees, was anticipated** as of July 2025
and is not held.

The **[[zimbabwe-deeds-registry]]** was established by the **Deeds Registry Act of 1960**
and holds property titles, charitable and family trusts, and **mortgage bonds** — which
makes it collateral infrastructure, not merely a record office. The programme is
explicitly modelled on **South Africa, Namibia, Australia and the United Kingdom** —
chosen, per Madzima, because those are the jurisdictions where much of Zimbabwe's diaspora
lives, making remote verification the design goal rather than a side benefit.

### The trigger case

**Dr Dzingai Mutumbuka**, Zimbabwe's **first black Minister of Education**, nearly lost his
**Chisipite** home while resident abroad: his **original deed was stolen from the Deeds
Registry itself**, fraudulently altered **with the help of an internal official**, and used
to sell the property to a third party. The fraudster was later convicted. The case is what
both accounts credit for the reform — and it locates the vulnerability *inside* the
registry, which is why the insider sanctions and login traceability are the substantive
provisions rather than the digitisation as such. *(The Madzima interview renders the name
"Matumbuka"; "Mutumbuka" is correct.)*

### Provenance — read both accounts as interested

**Both deeds sources are law-firm-authored and must be attributed.** The Madzima piece
([[2025-07-28 Legal Discussions with Vengai Madzima - Digitization of the Deeds Registry in Zimbabwe]])
is explicitly labelled a personal view. The Nehanda piece carries a **commercial interest**:
a conveyancing practice writing "avoid the rush" about a validation process it is paid to
perform, and its claim that the regulations "enhance local and international confidence in
the rule of law" is **advocacy, not finding**. Neither is the instrument text; **SI 76 of
2025 itself is not held**.

### The reading no source makes

A deeds-securitisation programme in Zimbabwe is the natural home for a
**colonial-dispossession** analysis — title, land and who holds record of it is the
country's central historical question. **No source in this batch makes that read.** The
single clip that approaches it does so in one euphemistic clause: "perceptions about the
country's commitment to the sanctity of private property rights that arose in the wake of
**the land reform process**" — framed wholly as an *investor-confidence* problem, by a
lawyer with a stake in the transaction volume that confidence produces. That is topic
adjacency, not evidence, and this page is **not** tagged `colonialism` on the strength of
it. The absence is itself worth recording: the held material treats Zimbabwean land title
purely as a fraud-and-confidence engineering problem, and the wiki should not mistake that
framing for the whole of the question.

### The pilot commences, and the deadline gets a date (2026-04) — GAP-ZWE-003 updated

The commencement the wiki could not previously establish is now evidenced. Per a notice of **4 April 2026**,
**16 law firms** (including Madzima's MCM Legal) were selected for a **Title Deed Validation & Reissuance
Pilot** to test/refine the **[[digital-land-administration-platform|Digital Land Administration Platform
(DLAP)]]** — the operational system for securitised deeds. Madzima now states the **24-month validation window
runs from the SI's publication date, giving a digitisation deadline of ~18 July 2027** (paper-deed holders
validate through conveyancers, with **fingerprints + notarized ID**); a **parallel company re-registration
deadline of 20 April 2026** applies (lapsed re-registration lets third parties reuse an abandoned company
name, imperilling its title deeds). *Nuance to carry: the earlier held sources framed the clock as running
from a ministerial **announcement of commencement**, whereas Madzima here dates it from **SI publication** —
the pilot's existence resolves the "has it commenced?" gap, but the trigger-date framing is now stated two
ways; both recorded.* Source: [[2026-04-07-title-deed-validation-reissuance-pilot-madzima]].

## Tax: ZIMRA and TaRMS

**[[zimra]]**'s **[[tarms-zimbabwe]]** Self-Service Portal issues a **Taxpayer
Identification Number (TIN)** on successful registration, which is **mandatory for all
persons carrying out any trade or business in Zimbabwe** (including Authorised Economic
Operators). Registration offers four categories — individual, organisation, representative
of a deceased taxpayer, and **"Register with existing BP Number"** — that last one
confirming a **migration from a legacy Business Partner numbering system** rather than a
greenfield registry. The TIN gates e-filing, e-payments and automated tax clearance.

**Date and provenance caveats.** The source page is **undated**; the year is the clipper's
inference from a news-ID sequence, so it is filed **year-precision proxy** and the
2025-01-01 prefix is padding. **Only "post-February 2024" is genuinely established
(GAP-ZWE-005).** It is **official first-party and effectively promotional** — "enhances
transparency, improves service delivery" are the agency's claims about itself — and it
carries **no figures at all**: no registration counts, no migration progress, no adoption
rate. It establishes the mechanism, not its uptake.
Source: [[2025-01-01 ZIMRA Taxpayer Registration]].

## Population registry and civil registration

The **population registry upgrade** — begun **around 2023**, per
[[2025-07-15 Zimbabwe looks forward to digital ID rollout after population registry reform]]
— is presented by government as the **foundation** of the planned digital ID: the registry
modernised and linked to other services, with an integrated digital system to be based on
the upgraded register. Registrar-General **Henry Machiri** ([[registrar-general-zimbabwe]])
says personnel are trained and materials in place. **All of this is relayed second-hand and
none of it is a completion.** See [[ZWE]] for the digital ID programme status.

Against that, the **[[civil-registry-department-zimbabwe]]** delivered the one *accomplished*
registry fact in the batch: a **mobile registration drive in Binga district, 7–11 July
2025**, with **[[unicef]]** and the **Embassy of Sweden**, reaching villages some **160 km
from Binga Centre** — journeys of **up to seven hours** over poor roads, with wildlife
risk. **Birth registration coverage stood at 57% (ZDHS 2023–24)** — dated to the survey,
not to the article reporting it. Source:
[[2025-07-10 Zimbabwe Launches Mobile ID Registration Program to Serve Remote Binga Communities]].
The gap between a registry being modernised in Harare and a birth being registered 160 km
from Binga is the substance of the coverage problem; see [[digital.rural]],
[[include.divides]].
