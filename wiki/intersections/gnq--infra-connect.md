# Equatorial Guinea × Connectivity, cables & telecom

Equatorial Guinea is trying to fix chronically poor connectivity, managed by the state via **[[gitge-gnq|GITGE]]**
(infrastructure) and **[[getesa-gnq|GETESA]]** (operator) — with a heavy **Chinese-vendor** hand and a thickening
regional-integration layer.

## International capacity

- **Cable diversification.** A single problem-prone base (the **ASD cable** degrades quality) is driving
  **[[gnq-medusa-cable|Medusa]]** (landing at Bome/Bata, €20–60m, 2029–30) and **Via Africa** plans; GITGE remains
  the ACE landing party (hosting the 36th ACE O&M committee, May 2026).
- **Regional integration.** **Gabon–GNQ** signed **fibre-interconnection, free roaming and frequency-coordination**
  accords (Mongomo, Nov 2025) — Central-African digital sovereignty and a step toward "Roaming Cero."
- **Satellite.** **[[starlink]]** signalled entry (2025-02), one of several licence questions.

## Operators & modernisation

- **[[getesa-gnq|GETESA]]** modernisation (5G, eSIM, public Wi-Fi, fibre reactivation, Getesa Money) with a
  **[[huawei|Huawei]]** network contract (9 Jul 2026); a **new second state operator [[telgesa-gnq|Telgesa]]**
  (~$50m, 260 towers, 90% coverage) alongside MUNI/ORTEL — the independent press flags opaque procurement and
  overlapping state operators.
- **Legal.** A tender to **rewrite the 2005 telecoms law** (Ley 7/2005) to cover AI, cybersecurity, data
  protection and satellite (2025-06).
