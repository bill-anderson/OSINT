# Libya × Connectivity

Libya's connectivity build is **state-led through [[lptic|LPTIC]]** and framed as a "digital gateway to
Africa": new European subsea capacity, a first 5G launch, and IPv6/protocol modernisation — set against
the fragility that a single flood-severed fibre exposes. The build below is **GNU/Tripoli-side**; the
contested east–west vendor thread is treated in its own section at the foot of this page.

## What we know

- **Medusa subsea cable — gateway to Europe (2025-05).** [[geopol.eu]] Libya inaugurated the **Medusa**
  system (**8,700 km**, Italy/Spain/Portugal/Morocco/Tunisia/Greece/Egypt/Cyprus) at **Misrata** — LPTIC
  calling it "a quantum leap" supporting 5G, data centres and cloud in Tripoli/Misrata/Benghazi, and a
  "digital gateway to Africa" (Chad, Niger, Mali). 2023 contract via LUIC; landings planned for Tripoli
  and Benghazi. Source: [[2025-05-12-medusa-submarine-cable-inaugurated-misrata-libyaherald]].
- **First 5G — Almadar (2026-01).** [[infra.connect]] **[[almadar-aljadid|Almadar Aljadid]]** (2nd state
  operator, LPTIC subsidiary) launched Libya's **first 5G** service in central Tripoli (countrywide to
  follow; initially thin coverage). Source: [[2026-01-29-almadar-launches-first-5g-tripoli-libyaherald]].
- **Vendor partnerships — KBR (US) & Nokia (Finland).** [[finance.mou]] LPTIC signed an MoU with US
  engineering firm **KBR** (infrastructure, data centres, cybersecurity, **5G**, Jan 2026) and met
  **Nokia** to accelerate 5G modernisation (Jul 2026) — playing US and European vendors as strategic
  partners. Context: 4G launched 2017; by end-2024 ~**10% of the population lacked coverage** and **18%
  had never used the internet** (ITU). Sources:
  [[2026-01-25-lptic-kbr-mou-5g-infrastructure-libyaherald]], [[2026-07-16-libya-nokia-5g-cooperation-alwasat]].
- **IPv6 transition (2026-04).** [[infra.cybersec]] [[sovereignty]] **[[gaci-libya|GACI]]** chairman
  Al-Baour convened operators to activate the national **IPv4→IPv6** move — a strategic project for
  scalability, security and a sustainable digital-economy backbone. Source:
  [[2026-04-22-gaci-ipv6-transition-libyaherald]].
- **Fragility — Ghat–Tahala fibre cut (2026-05).** [[include.divides]] Floods severed the **Hatif Libya**
  fibre between **Ghat and Tahala (south-west)** on 27 May, cutting connectivity; **11 of 12 sites (91.7%)**
  were restored, most via **VSAT** backup — exposing the rural/south digital divide and single-route
  fragility. Source: [[2026-05-30-ghat-tahala-fibre-cut-restored-alwasat]].

## The east–west vendor contest (as of 2026-07-20)

The build above is GNU/Tripoli-side; the **contested** thread runs through which authority may license a
foreign vendor. The dividing line is **not** vendor-per-region — Huawei works both sides — but
**licensing jurisdiction**.

- **Huawei suspended, then reinstated in three days (Aug 2025).** [[sovereignty]] [[geopol.china]] On
  **17 August 2025** the GNU's **General Authority for Telecommunications and Informatics (GTI)**
  suspended **all Huawei activities in Libya**, citing entry into "illegal contracts with illegitimate
  entities", breach of **Law No. 22 of 2010** (which reserves licensing to the GTI alone), and violation
  of **ITU** principles on respect for state sovereignty; contracts concluded outside GTI-approved
  frameworks were declared null and void. **On 20 August 2025 the GTI announced agreement with Huawei
  Technizon Technical Services – Libya Branch** to continue operating, Huawei having committed to full
  compliance with Law 22/2010 — so the suspension **lasted three days** and reads as a jurisdictional
  signal rather than an enforcement campaign. Huawei has operated in Libya **20+ years**, contributing to
  3G/4G rollout with local operators. Source: [[2025-08-20-lana-gti-huawei-libya-agreement]]
  (Libyan News Agency, the state agency — carries both the agreement and the suspension's stated grounds).
- **What triggered it: an eastern network licensed by the rival administration.** The **HoR-appointed
  government's Ministry of Communications** granted a **unified telecoms licence to OZON Aljaded For
  Telecom and Technology Group**, effective **4 March 2025** (tender announced 5 Dec 2024; of 25
  interested firms only Ozon and Giga Communications bought documents). Eastern authorities were
  **reported** to have sought a **USD 198m letter of credit** from the CBL as down payment on a **USD
  700m Huawei contract** to build an eastern mobile network, with a Benghazi-launched network reported as
  **"Barqa 217"**, tied to the Libya Development and Reconstruction Fund. *These contract figures and the
  network name rest on **single-outlet** reporting (Libya Security Monitor; Arab Weekly) and **no source
  is held** — treat as reported, not established.* Tripoli's stated concern is that such a network would
  let the east run its own telecoms space outside GTI oversight.
- **ZTE ↔ the west: LTT MoU at MWC Barcelona (Mar 2026).** [[finance.mou]] **ZTE Corporation** and
  **LTT (Libya Telecom & Technology)**, the GNU-side state operator, signed a **memorandum of
  understanding on 9 March 2026** at **MWC Barcelona 2026** — signed for LTT by chairman **Ammar
  Albouzaidi** and for ZTE by SVP **Zhang Wanchun**. Scope: 4G/5G planning and deployment; a digital-
  service ecosystem (**e-wallet, e-commerce, CDN**); terminals (**FWA, CPE, MiFi**); and new business
  models, with energy-efficient network technologies flagged. **No value, no timetable and no binding
  commitment** were published — this is a framework, not a contract. It extends the west-side pattern of
  playing multiple foreign vendors (KBR/US, Nokia/Finland, now ZTE/China) as strategic partners. *The
  signing date is **9 March 2026** on ZTE's own record; a 2026-03-11 date in circulation is The
  Register's republication date, not the event's.* Source: [[2026-03-09-ltt-zte-mou-mwc-barcelona]].
- **Huawei kit on the west side too — the Tripoli data centre.** A **Huawei FusionModule1000A**
  containerised facility is to be deployed in **Tripoli in early 2027**, operated by **Trans-Sahara**
  (private, HB Group) in joint venture with **Tatweer Research** (public body owned by the **Libyan Local
  Investment & Development Fund**), providing **private cloud to government agencies and local
  companies**, with **PCI DSS** certification sought so the financial sector can use it (reported
  2026-06-24; **no capacity figure published**, and power/infrastructure named as the main obstacles).
  Notably this realises a **partnership first signed in Benghazi in October 2019** for a **Tier-3** data
  centre — scaled down over seven years to a single prefabricated module. *No source held; see
  acquisitions.* See [[infra.store]], [[libya--infra-cybersec]].

**The reading:** Libya's connectivity contest is less a Huawei-vs-ZTE split than a **regulatory-sovereignty
dispute wearing a vendor costume**. Both Chinese vendors serve the GNU side under licence; the rupture came
only where a vendor contracted with the eastern administration, and it closed as soon as the vendor
acknowledged GTI licensing primacy.

## Acquisition note (2026-07-20)

The **USD 700m Huawei eastern-network contract and the USD 198m CBL letter of credit remain
single-outlet as of 2026-07-20.** One acquisition attempt found no corroborating or primary account:
the only source carrying both figures is still Libya Security Monitor (2025-08-17), with the network
name "Barqa 217" from The Arab Weekly (2025-08-18). Libya Herald, The Libya Observer and LANA all
report the suspension without either figure, and Libya Monitor's Huawei items concern unrelated, far
smaller contracts (a EUR 4.1m / LD 6.6m eastern cable award; a Libyana network-development contract).
Neither the Central Bank of Libya nor the eastern administration has published anything on the LC.
**Both figures must continue to be carried as *reported, not established*.**

The **GTI suspension statement of 2025-08-17 was acquired** on 2026-07-20 (LANA, Arabic, staged in
`new/`), so the suspension itself no longer rests on outlet re-reports.
