# Libya × Connectivity

Libya's connectivity build is **state-led through [[lptic|LPTIC]]** and framed as a "digital gateway to
Africa": new European subsea capacity, a first 5G launch, and IPv6/protocol modernisation — set against
the fragility that a single flood-severed fibre exposes. All are **GNU/Tripoli-side**.

## What we know

- **Medusa subsea cable — gateway to Europe (2025-05).** [[geopol.eu]] Libya inaugurated the **Medusa**
  system (**8,700 km**, Italy/Spain/Portugal/Morocco/Tunisia/Greece/Egypt/Cyprus) at **Misrata** — LPTIC
  calling it "a quantum leap" supporting 5G, data centres and cloud in Tripoli/Misrata/Benghazi, and a
  "digital gateway to Africa" (Chad, Niger, Mali). 2023 contract via LUIC; landings planned for Tripoli
  and Benghazi. Source: [[2025-05-12-medusa-submarine-cable-inaugurated-misrata-libyaherald]].
- **First 5G — Almadar (2026-01).** [[infra.connect]] **[[almadar-aljadid|Almadar Aljadid]]** (2nd state
  operator, LPTIC subsidiary) launched Libya's **first 5G** service in central Tripoli (countrywide to
  follow; initially thin coverage). Source: [[2026-01-29-almadar-launches-first-5g-tripoli-libyaherald]].
- **Vendor partnerships — KBR (US) & Nokia (Finland).** [[finance.mou]] LPTIC signed an MoU with US
  engineering firm **KBR** (infrastructure, data centres, cybersecurity, **5G**, Jan 2026) and met
  **Nokia** to accelerate 5G modernisation (Jul 2026) — playing US and European vendors as strategic
  partners. Context: 4G launched 2017; by end-2024 ~**10% of the population lacked coverage** and **18%
  had never used the internet** (ITU). Sources:
  [[2026-01-25-lptic-kbr-mou-5g-infrastructure-libyaherald]], [[2026-07-16-libya-nokia-5g-cooperation-alwasat]].
- **IPv6 transition (2026-04).** [[infra.cybersec]] [[sovereignty]] **[[gaci-libya|GACI]]** chairman
  Al-Baour convened operators to activate the national **IPv4→IPv6** move — a strategic project for
  scalability, security and a sustainable digital-economy backbone. Source:
  [[2026-04-22-gaci-ipv6-transition-libyaherald]].
- **Fragility — Ghat–Tahala fibre cut (2026-05).** [[include.divides]] Floods severed the **Hatif Libya**
  fibre between **Ghat and Tahala (south-west)** on 27 May, cutting connectivity; **11 of 12 sites (91.7%)**
  were restored, most via **VSAT** backup — exposing the rural/south digital divide and single-route
  fragility. Source: [[2026-05-30-ghat-tahala-fibre-cut-restored-alwasat]].
