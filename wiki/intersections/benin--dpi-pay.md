# Benin × Digital Payments & Fintech

Benin runs on the **BCEAO's regional instant-payment rail** with an unusually deep mobile-money base — and
a fiscal policy that is actively pushing merchants off cash and onto it.

## The regional rail

- **[[pi-spi]] — UEMOA-wide.** Benin is on the BCEAO's regional **[[pi-spi]]** instant-payment rail
  (launched **30 Sep 2025**, **all 8 states**, **free domestically**); banks and mobile money were
  **100% interconnected by May 2026**. See [[gov.regional]], [[XWA]], [[bceao]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-07-lancement-pi-spi-uemoa]].

## Scale

- **Mobile-money penetration 88.09%** — **11.65m active accounts (end-2025)**, MTN ~**51%** — atop
  **248.7m** UEMOA e-money accounts (**2024**). Source:
  [[2026-05-19-arcep-benin-essor-usages-numeriques]].
- **UEMOA financial inclusion 47% → 72.3%** on digital reforms. Source:
  [[2025-07-11-inclusion-financiere-uemoa-reformes-digital]].

## Tax as the push onto digital rails

- **A 1% levy on large cash payments** and **PLFR-2026** digital taxes push merchants onto mobile money.
  See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.legislate]]. Sources:
  [[2025-01-06-mobile-money-contourner-taxe-especes]], [[2026-06-11-plfr-2026-fiscalite-numerique]].
