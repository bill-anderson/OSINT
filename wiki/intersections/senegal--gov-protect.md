# Senegal × Data Protection

Senegal's data-protection regime is **eighteen years old and unamended**, and it did not move even
through a breach of the national biometric-ID directorate. That is a state-capacity finding, not a
coverage gap.

## The framework

- **[[senegal-data-protection-law|Law No. 2008-12 of 25 January 2008]]** (in force 2014), enforced
  by the **[[cdp-senegal|Commission de protection des données personnelles (CDP)]]**. A revision
  was reported underway (2025).
- The law remains **operative and unamended as of 2026-03-27**, with a revision bill *"dans le
  circuit"* awaiting a vote.

## The law did not move through the breach

Law No. 2008-12 **did not advance through the February-2026 breach** of the national biometric-ID
directorate. A state-capacity finding, not a data gap. See [[dpi.id]], [[senegal--dpi-id]].

## The New Deal's rights gap

[[paradigm-initiative]] argues the **[[new-deal-technologique|New Deal Technologique]]** omits
explicit digital-rights provisions — an ambitious digital-transformation programme built over a
static protection statute. See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
[[2025-03-27 Senegal's Technological New Deal – A Digital Ambition Tested by Digital Rights]].

## Regional note

Senegal's CDP is a convention partner of **[[BEN]] Benin's APDP** — part of the francophone DPA
cooperation network also joined by Mali's [[apdp-mali|APDP]]. See [[benin--gov-protect]],
[[gov.regional]].
