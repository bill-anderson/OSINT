# Kenya × Connectivity

Kenya's connectivity story runs on three fronts: **Safaricom's dominance** re-cemented by regulatory
certainty and 5G/fibre build-out, **new international capacity** (subsea cables, terrestrial cross-border
routes, local peering), and a **Starlink-led LEO disruption** eating into the fixed-broadband market.
The state's Digital Superhighway threads rural and county connectivity beneath all three.

## What we know

- **Market structure — Safaricom leads, Starlink rises (2026 Q3).** [[include.access]] CA Sector
  Statistics (Jan–Mar 2026): **84.1m SIMs**; Safaricom **68.9%** of mobile (57.9m) and **35.4%** of
  fixed broadband (941,501 subs); **Starlink** entered the **top-10 fixed ISPs** for the first time
  (24,999 subs, 0.9% — satellite the fastest-growing fixed tech, +11.4%). International capacity **+16.4%
  to 28,130 Gbps**; fibre dominates fixed (1.47m subs); 5G users consume 53.5 GB/month. Earlier (Jan
  2025), local ISPs were already "feeling the heat" as Starlink doubled share. Sources:
  [[2026-06-24-safaricom-broadband-leadership-starlink-telecomreviewafrica]],
  [[2025-01-25-isps-starlink-doubles-market-share-standard]].
- **Device shift — 50m smartphones (2026-03).** [[include.divides]] Smartphone connections crossed
  **50m** for the first time (up from Dec 2025), while feature phones fell to **28.5m** (−3.7%) — an
  internet-enabled-device inflection. Source: [[2026-06-20-smartphones-hit-50m-basic-ditched-nation]].
- **Regulatory certainty — 25-year licence (2026-05).** [[gov.legislate]] Safaricom secured a **25-year
  unified licence** from the CA (replacing a temporary 2-year permit; historically 10-year), consolidating
  spectrum and authorisations — even as licence-related costs rose to **~$126.7m** (FY-ended Mar 2026).
  Source: [[2026-05-18-safaricom-25-year-licence-itweb]].
- **International capacity — Daraja subsea cable (2025-10).** [[geopol.usa]] **[[meta-daraja-subsea-cable|Daraja]]**
  — Meta's second Kenya cable (via Edge Network Services), **4,108km, 24 fibre pairs, Oman (Salalah)–
  Mombasa**, $23m, ASN-built, RFS 2026 — with **[[safaricom]]** as landing/operating partner. Follows
  2Africa. Source: [[2025-10-30-meta-safaricom-daraja-subsea-cable-telecomreviewafrica]].
- **Terrestrial cross-border — Bayobab (2025-05).** [[gov.regional]] MTN's **[[bayobab]]** completed a
  **260km Kampala–Tororo route** along the Uganda Railway corridor, interconnecting to Kenya's NLD fibre
  (Mombasa–Malaba) — giving Uganda **>1 Tbit/s low-latency** access to Mombasa subsea landings and
  regional data centres. Source: [[2025-05-08-bayobab-uganda-kenya-fiber-connectingafrica]].
- **Local peering — LINX Nairobi (2026-07).** [[dpi.exchange]] **[[linx-nairobi|LINX]]** added regional
  cloud provider **[[angani]]** to its Nairobi hub (**55+ networks**, incl. TikTok and Starlink); combined
  Nairobi+Mombasa exchange traffic grew **20× in a year to 330Gbps** — keeping local traffic local
  (Angani: "80% of our traffic is local"). Source: [[2026-07-10-linx-kenya-peering-network-itweb]].
- **5G transport & home fibre.** [[geopol.china]] **[[ericsson]]** extended a multi-year MINI-LINK
  microwave deal to fortify Safaricom's 5G, enabling **1 Gbps in remote North Kenya** beyond 100km; and
  Safaricom launched Kenya's **first fiber-to-the-room (FTTR)** service with **[[huawei]]** (per-room
  optical access points, Wi-Fi 6/6+, AI network management). Sources:
  [[2026-03-09-ericsson-safaricom-5g-transport-telecomreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-05-06-safaricom-huawei-ftr-telecomreviewafrica]].
- **Digital Superhighway — rural & county (2025-26).** [[digital.rural]] Under BETA, Kenya is rolling out
  **100,000km of fibre + 25,000 public Wi-Fi hotspots + digital hubs** (Kabogo–Ngcaba/SEACOM PPP talks).
  Kirinyaga County installed **102.4km fibre** connecting all five sub-counties to the **One Government
  Network (OGN)** with nine public hotspots (Phase II underway). Sources:
  [[2025-11-05-kenya-fiber-wifi-ppp-ngcaba-telecomreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-01-20-kirinyaga-county-fiber-ogn-telecomreviewafrica]].
