# Côte d'Ivoire × Data centres, cloud & digital sovereignty

Côte d'Ivoire's data-centre story is the batch's biggest find: **the United States has become the anchor
financier of Ivorian "digital sovereignty,"** through a first-of-its-kind EXIM data-centre package — and
the hub, which tagged CIV **`geopol.eu` only**, carried no `geopol.usa` for the country at all.

## The US-EXIM package (the headline)

- **A first for sub-Saharan Africa.** US **[[exim-bank-usa|EXIM Bank]]** approved **$66m** for Côte
  d'Ivoire's **[[national-data-centre-civ|National Data Centre]]** (~**39.6bn FCFA**) — **the first data
  centre EXIM has ever backed in sub-Saharan Africa**, implemented by Washington-DC firm **[[cybastion]]**,
  and reported as **displacing a Chinese competitor**. A parallel **$47m** (approved 14 Aug 2025) digitises
  the **Ministry of Commerce & Industry**; the two were consolidated into a **$100m+ CTEP guarantee**
  (Cisco, HPE, Schneider) under the *China and Transformational Exports Program* — explicitly framed as
  countering the PRC. The US ambassador cast it as backing "the digital sovereignty of Côte d'Ivoire."
  By **Jul 2026** the Cybastion/EXIM project had grown to **$170m** and been scoped to add **smart-border
  surveillance**. Sources:

- **The wider US package.** Around it sits a **$550m US trade package** for digital sovereignty and tech
  talent (a George Mason/Cybastion/ministry MoU on AI and cyber skills), and Cybastion's broader CIV
  footprint: an **ERP + GUCII** business-registration build at Commerce, the **DigiEmpower** skills
  programme (200k trained / 5 yrs against a projected 5m digital jobs by 2030), a **Digital City**
  sovereign-data-centre core (construction began May 2025), and **eTourism** ("Sublime Côte d'Ivoire")
  under its **Digital Fast Track** sovereignty programme. Sources:

## The competing sovereign-cloud cluster (VITIB / Grand-Bassam)

Under the US package, a **francophone sovereign-cloud cluster** builds at **[[vitib|VITIB]]** (Grand-Bassam):
**[[st-digital]]** opened a **Tier III** data centre (with **OVHcloud**; inaugurated Oct 2025) and had
earlier launched **NVIDIA-GPU AI hosting** for Abidjan/Douala; **[[raxio-group|Raxio]]** (CIV1) signed a
**data-sovereignty MoU** to host government workloads; and VITIB itself sought **$311m** (Indian
investors / Exim Bank of India) for a 2028 plan — a *third* external financier. A local vendor, **Door
(Cloudoor / [[cloudoor]])**, markets an "African sovereign cloud." Sources:
