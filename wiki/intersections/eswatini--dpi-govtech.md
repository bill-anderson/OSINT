# Eswatini × GovTech & Digital Government

The delivery spine is **[[government-in-your-hand]]** (GIYH), funded by the **[[digital-eswatini-project]]**
(WB, E1.2bn/$65m) — but Eswatini's govtech year was dominated by a **Google-MoU cabinet controversy** that
was, on the evidence, a **communication failure** more than a scandal.

## GIYH — the citizen super-app

- **Launch (May 2025).** ICT Minister Savannah Maziya and the Eswatini Revenue Service launched the
  **GIYH app** (track ID/passport/travel-doc status, register businesses, **pay vehicle licences via
  Mobile Money**, education-loan info — new services every 6 months) and **ERS TaxPal** (tax accounts,
  clearance certificates). See [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2025-05-21-eswatini-launches-new-apps-giyh-taxpal]].
- **The delivery vehicle.** The **E1.2bn ($65m) Digital Eswatini Project** (WB loan, P508948) is training
  cross-ministry **Change Agents** for a **2027 rollout** — **E648m broadband + E450m GIYH** — planning
  **Electronic IDs and USSD-based services** so citizens **without smartphones** can transact, engaging
  rural communities via the **Tinkhundla** system. See [[dpi.id]], [[digital.rural]], [[finance.new]].
  Source: [[2026-06-24-ict-trains-change-agents-e1-2bn-digitization]].

## The Google MoU controversy

- **What it was.** The **[[eswatini-google-partnership|Google MoU]]** (Jan 2025, integrating Google tools
  into GIYH — youth skills, entrepreneurship) became a **cabinet controversy** in Apr 2026: **PM Russell
  Dlamini first dismissed** it ("Google unaware... a national-security threat"), then acknowledged in
  writing that **Cabinet had approved it**, after the **Attorney-General confirmed** Maziya consulted
  Cabinet and the AG before signing. An investigation established the MoU with **Google Cloud EMEA** was a
  **cloud-services and e-government framework — not a monetisation deal** — the public row "a failure of
  communication." See [[geopol.usa]], [[finance.mou]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-10-former-minister-defends-google-mou]], [[2026-04-13-the-real-google-problem-in-eswatini-is-not-the-mou]].
- **The real gap.** The opinion analysis argues creators can't earn from Google because of a **business/
  monetisation-eligibility** problem, not the MoU — a useful reframing. Source:
  [[2026-04-13-the-real-google-problem-in-eswatini-is-not-the-mou]].
- **Continental spotlight (Jul 2026).** Maziya represented Eswatini at the **Google Cloud Summit Africa
  2026** (Johannesburg), where **President Ramaphosa acknowledged** the kingdom's growing digital
  prominence — GIYH showcased as the flagship. Source: [[2026-07-05-eswatini-digital-progress-earns-continental-spotlight]].

## Sector platforms (MTN partnership)

- **Umelusi / TruMD / Content Creator Hub (Jun 2026).** **[[mtn-eswatini]]** and the ICT Ministry launched
  three platforms — **Umelusi** (agriculture: farm info, livestock tools), **TruMD** (health: cheaper
  remote access), and a **Content Creator Hub** (creative-economy skills/opportunities). See [[dpi.mis]],
  [[tech.innovate]], [[digital.rural]]. Source: [[2026-06-20-eswatini-rolls-out-digital-platforms-health-agriculture-creative]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the Google episode is instructive — a routine cloud/e-gov MoU triggered a
  **national-security panic and a PM–AG public contradiction**, revealing how thin the coordination and
  communication around foreign-tech partnerships is. The substance (GIYH on a WB loan, USSD for
  feature-phone users, MTN sector apps) is sound and inclusion-minded; the governance *around* it — who
  signs what with whom, and who tells the public — is the weak point.
