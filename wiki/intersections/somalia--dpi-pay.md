# Somalia × Digital Payments

Somalia's cash-light, privately-built mobile-money economy is now getting its first
central-bank regulatory foothold via the Somalia Instant Payment System (SIPS) and the
national QR standard SOMQR.

## What we know

Somalia runs one of Africa's most cash-light economies — mobile money worth ~**$8bn (36%
of GDP)**, ~650m transactions/yr, built by Hormuud/Telesom in the state's absence. The
[[central-bank-of-somalia]] launched the **[[somalia-instant-payment-system]] (SIPS)** and a
national QR standard **SOMQR** in early 2025 to bridge isolated wallets and the emerging
banking sector — its first real regulatory foothold. SIPS is operated by Somalia Payment
Switch (SPS) on **BPC's SmartVista** platform (launched Jan 2025 via cloud, moving to a
local data centre), with **7 institutions** live at launch and CBS as clearing/settlement
agent. From 29 Jul 2025 the CBS also mandated the national ID ([[snid]]) for opening or
updating bank accounts, linking payments KYC to foundational identity. A first National DPI Summit
(Mogadishu, 14–15 Feb 2026) with CBS, the NCA, [[nira-somalia]] and the Data Protection
Authority targets interoperability. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]],
[[include.access]].

## The private rail — EVC Plus

- **GSMA re-certification (2025).** **[[hormuud-telecom]]**'s **EVC Plus** was GSMA Mobile Money
  re-certified — one of only **~10 platforms worldwide** to meet the standard (alongside M-PESA and MTN
  MoMo), on ~300 criteria (fraud, AML, consumer protection). Somalis run **~155m mobile-money
  transactions/month worth $2.7bn** (World Bank); ~¾ of adults use mobile money; **EVC Plus serves 4.5m
  daily users** and **145+ NGOs** distribute humanitarian cash through it. See [[finance.new]],
  [[tech.industry]]. Source: [[2025-08-20-hormuud-evc-plus-gsma-recertification]].

## The device barrier — smartphone financing

- **Hormuud × Get-Phone (2026).** "The barrier is not the mast in the distance; it is the handset in the
  pocket" — **~50%** of Hormuud's base still uses 2G/feature phones despite 70%+ 4G coverage. A device-
  financing programme targets **100,000 affordable smartphones by end-2026** (Central/South Somalia,
  expanding to **Puntland and Somaliland**): **$19 deposit** then **$0.60–1.60/day** (phone + a 1GB/40-min
  bundle) repaid via EVC Plus, using **telecom-based credit scoring** (SIM tenure + mobile-money spend, no
  formal credit history) — a Feb–Mar pilot saw **<4% default**. See [[include.divides]], [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2026-05-06-hormuud-getphone-smartphone-financing]].
