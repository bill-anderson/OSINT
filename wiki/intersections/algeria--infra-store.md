# Algeria × Data centres, cloud & digital sovereignty

Data localisation and a **state-owned data-centre / national-cloud estate** are the defining feature of
Algeria's model — the country hosts government data on national territory and keeps hyperscalers out of a
protected cloud market. Against a continent holding only **0.6% of global data-centre capacity** (ADCA 2026),
Algeria ranked **among the 10 best-equipped African countries for data centres in 2025**.

## The sovereign data-centre estate

- **National Digital Services Center** ([[national-digital-services-center-algeria]]) — inaugurated by
  President Tebboune **2026-07-05**; **two active-active DCs in Algiers (Mohammadia) & Blida**, **Huawei**-built,
  **Uptime Tier III Design**, **99.98%** availability; hosts the national cloud + Dzair Digital Services;
  administrations ordered to interconnect **within one month** (2026-07-12).
- **Earlier estate:** first data centre at **Constantine (Feb 2023)**; an **AI-dedicated data centre at Oran**
  (Mar 2025); **>200,000 km** of fibre underpinning the whole build (state-daily "grand bond numérique").
- **Parliament sovereign cloud** agreement (2026-06) — digitalising the legislature on national infrastructure.

## Research & innovation compute

- **[[cerist]]** launched a **cloud-computing platform**, drone system and incubator (Mar 2025) on
  OpenStack/Kubernetes — "autonomous and high-performance" sovereign infrastructure.
- **SATICOM** — minister inspection (2025-09) highlighting domestic telecom/satellite-equipment innovation.

All of it is supervised through the **[[high-commission-digitalisation-algeria|HCN]]** and framed explicitly
as **digital sovereignty**, with **[[huawei]]** (China) the recurring build partner — a `geopol.china` thread.
