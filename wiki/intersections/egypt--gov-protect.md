# Egypt × Data Protection

Egypt's personal-data regime went from **five years of dormancy to imminent enforcement** — even as courts began imposing liability without waiting for it.

- **The law and its regulations.** The **[[egypt-pdpl|PDPL (Law 151/2020)]]** was operationalised only by **Executive Regulations No. 816/2025** (Nov 2025), with **enforcement expected 1 Nov 2026**. It confirms the **[[pdpc-egypt|Personal Data Protection Center (PDPC)]]** as supervisor, requires **PDPC licences for cross-border transfers**, mandates DPOs and 72-hour breach notification, and adds children's-data safeguards.
- **Enforcement ahead of the regulations.** The **Alexandria Economic Court** (Feb 2025) awarded **EGP 10m** against Orange Egypt for an unconsented SIM swap, grounding liability in **tort / "custodian's liability"** even before the Regulations existed — a warning that data-protection exposure was already live.
- **AI-era framing.** Analysts (Cairo Review) situate Law 151 against AI-driven surveillance and biometric-data risks.

The rollout of biometric IDs ([[haweya]], [[moieg-pass]]) and a hyperscaler-linked cloud footprint make the PDPC's cross-border-transfer and biometric rules consequential. See [[EGY]], [[gov.protect]], [[egypt--dpi-id]].
