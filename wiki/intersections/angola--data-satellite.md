# Angola × Satellite data (space programme)

Angola is building a **full space stack as a sovereignty asset** — communications (ANGOSAT), Earth
observation (ANGEO), and the institutions to run them — under **[[ggpen]]**, the National Space
Programme Management Office. The stated arc is to move Angola **from a user of space services to a
producer and operator**, claiming a **top-five African** programme (Space in Africa, 2026). President
Lourenço created an **Angolan Space Agency in October 2025**.

## Earth observation — ANGEO-1 (the European pole)

**[[angeo-1]]** is Angola's **first national Earth-observation satellite**, built by **[[airbus]]**
Defence and Space (Toulouse) on the **S250** optical platform, designed for **>1,000 high-resolution
images/day** (soil/agriculture, urban mapping, environment, mining, oil-and-gas, border control).

- **Financing (2025-01-23):** **€225,257,201** via **Société Générale**
  ([[angola-france-satellite-financing]]), signed at the Angola–France Economic Forum.
- **Kick-off (2026-03-16):** ~**€225m / US$259m** contract launched at Toulouse (GGPEN DG Zolana
  João + Airbus), framed as **sovereign access to critical data**.
- **Progress:** **System Key Point milestone validated (2026-05-14)**; first EO **ground
  infrastructure** delivery from June 2026.

GGPEN is already selling the downstream: EO imagery + **AI and geospatial data** for infrastructure
monitoring, **asset registers and land-use mapping**, pitched into public finance and banking (via
its **GEDAE** geodata centre) — the registry/satellite crossover.

## Communications — ANGOSAT-2 opens to the market

**[[angosat-2]]** (launched 2022, operated from the Funda Mission Control Centre) was **opened to
commercial ISPs in December 2025** — previously government-only — starting a phased domestic
satellite-services market aimed at **reducing dependence on foreign providers**. It anchors
**[[conecta-angola]]**:

- **32 free public access points** and **six connected universities** (Agostinho Neto, Katyavala
  Bwila, Kimpa Vita, Mandume Ya Ndemufayo, Namibe) by June 2026; **37 firms** entered the commercial
  arm by April 2026.
- **Border management** — a VSAT terminal at the **Luvo Border Post** wired into tax/border
  administration (AGT-led), May 2026.
- **Education last mile** — ANGOSAT-2 + **TVWS** connected **3,972 students** in Ramiros (July 2026);
  **5,600+ University of Namibe students** given free access plus VSAT training (June 2026, with
  [[unitel]], [[africell]], ST Engineering, [[airbus]]).
- **Edge market-opening** — INACOM licensed a **local Moxico startup (NomaTech)** as a satellite ISP
  (July 2026); **[[africell]]** signed a June 2026 MoU to expand services (7–8 new sites, 7+ provinces
  by mid-2027). Ilumina Angola uses ANGOSAT-2 for rural backhaul (→ [[angola--infra-connect]]).

## Next — ANGOSAT-3

The **PAC2025** procurement plan (2025-02) earmarked **~278bn kwanza** — nearly half the MINTTICS
capital plan — for **ANGOSAT-3**, a domestic **Earth-observation** satellite pitched to support
agriculture and drought response. (The same plan carries the **US$248m China Eximbank / Sinohydro**
national broadband loan — the Chinese pole alongside ANGEO-1's European one.)
