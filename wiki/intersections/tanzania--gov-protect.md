# Tanzania × Data Protection

The **[[tanzania-pdpa]]** has been in force **since 1 May 2023**, with a supervisory commission
(**[[pdpc-tanzania]]**), regulations, an expired registration deadline and a mandatory-DPO duty. The
analytical line worth making is that this is **a capable regime with real definitional softness at its
core**: the statute is strict on paper — breach notification unconditioned on harm, extraterritorial
reach — yet leaves **"security breach"** and **"without undue delay"** undefined, provides **no
adequacy mechanism** for cross-border transfers, imposes **no duty to notify data subjects**, and
narrows Zanzibar to Union Matters. It arrived fast, and is being given meaning by practitioners rather
than by its text.

## The statute's shape (per Malekela and Jurisic, 2025-05-07)

*Attribution: the scope and adequacy readings below are **Malekela and Jurisic** writing in the Kluwer
Arbitration Blog, not the wiki's own construction of the Act.*

- **Passed 27 November 2022**; **in force 1 May 2023** (**Chapter 44, R.E. 2023**), supplemented by
  Regulations **GN No. 449C of 2023**, establishing **[[pdpc-tanzania]]** as supervisory authority.
- **s.15** requires controllers and processors to register — the **final deadline expired 30 April
  2025**.
- **s.3** gives the Act **extraterritorial** reach.
- **s.2 / Art. 4(3) of the Constitution** — it applies to the Mainland **and to Zanzibar, but in
  Zanzibar only to 'Union Matters'**. [[sovereignty]]
- **s.31 et seq.** require safeguards or express consent for cross-border transfers but — **unlike the
  GDPR — provide no adequacy-decision mechanism**, so **each controller self-assesses the destination**;
  the **Minister for Communication** ([[micit-tanzania]]), with the PDPC, may specify destinations where
  transfers are **not permitted**.
- **s.27(3)** mandates a **Data Protection Officer** — this is **the statutory basis for the CDPO
  programme** held on [[pdpc-tanzania]] (below).

## Breach notification: strict on paper, undefined in the text (per Clyde & Co, 2026-02-09)

*Attribution: the GDPR-divergence reading of the breach duty is **[[clyde-and-co]]**'s.*

- **s.27(5)** requires a controller to notify [[pdpc-tanzania]] of any security breach **without undue
  delay** and — **unlike the GDPR** — the duty is **not conditioned on risk or harm**: a breach is
  notifiable once it has occurred.
- But **"security breach" is undefined**, and so is **"without undue delay"** (Clyde & Co note
  comparable regimes read it as ~**48–72 hours**).
- Processors must inform the controller; there is **no duty to notify data subjects**.
- Penalties (**reg. 63, GN 449C of 2023**): **TZS 100,000 (~USD 41) to TZS 5,000,000 (~USD 2,030)**, or
  up to **five years**, or both *(conversions as of 2026-02)*.

## The BOT cross-reference: payments wired into the data regime (2025-06-27)

Per **Kemi Advocates**, the **[[tanzania-financial-consumer-protection-regulations]]** (**GN No. 298 of
2025**, dated **23 May 2025**) extend the **dormant account** period **5 → 12 months** and add an
**"inactive account"** category (unused **>5 months**); mandate the **reducing-balance** method for loan
interest; set complaint-resolution maxima (**12 hours** mobile money, **14 days** credit) with fines of
**TSh 1 million per day**; require **prior written [[bank-of-tanzania]] approval** for new or increased
fees; and require rates published **quarterly** in Kiswahili and English.

**The data hook: providers must protect consumer financial and personal information *in accordance with
the PDPA*** — a BOT-side cross-reference wiring the payments regime into the data-protection regime.

## Compliance capacity: the National CDPO Programme (2026-07-14)

[[pdpc-tanzania]] and the **Open University of Tanzania** launched a national **Certified Data
Protection Officer** programme — **OUT trains and assesses, PDPC certifies** — targeting government,
finance, telecoms, healthcare, education and tech. It is the capacity answer to the **s.27(3)**
mandatory-DPO duty above (PDPC DG Dr. Emmanuel Mkilia; OUT VC Prof. Alex B. Makulilo). Source:
[[2026-07-14 OUT, PDPC Launch National Data Protection Programme]]. [[capacity.training]]
