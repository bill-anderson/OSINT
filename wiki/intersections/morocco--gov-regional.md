# Morocco × Regional digital cooperation & open government

Morocco actively positions itself as a **continental convener** on digital governance — hosting open-government
fora, striking South-South bilaterals, and engaging global digital-cooperation bodies — an
influence-projection strand of its digital-sovereignty agenda (Minister **Seghrouchni**).

## What we know

- **African Open Government Conference, Rabat (2026-05).** [[data.open]] [[dpi.govtech]] Morocco hosted the
  conference (5–7 May, "Towards an Open Africa") — transparency, accountability and citizen-centred
  administration — as a continental platform on open governance. Source:
  [[2026-05-07-techreviewafrica-morocco-african-open-government-conference]].
- **Morocco–Ghana Statement of Intent (2026-05).** [[finance.mou]] Signed at the forum's close (with
  Ghana's Akanvariva Lydia Lamisi), deepening cooperation on **digital transformation and administrative
  modernisation** — Africa-led collaboration on digital governance. See [[GHA]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-11-techafricanews-morocco-ghana-statement-of-intent]].
- **Morocco–Gabon digital-skills agreement (2026-04).** [[capacity.training]] A bilateral (GITEX Africa
  2026) between the two digital ministries on **digital skills, capacity-building and AI** cooperation.
  See [[GAB]]. Source: [[2026-04-13-techafricanews-morocco-gabon-digital-skills]].
- **Morocco–DCO engagement (2026-07).** [[gov.regional]] Minister Seghrouchni and **Digital Cooperation
  Organization** SG Deemah AlYahya met (UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance, Geneva) on deepening
  cooperation and the upcoming DCO General Assembly. Source:
  [[2026-07-09-techreviewafrica-morocco-dco-collaboration]].
