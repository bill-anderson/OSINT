# Senegal × Connectivity

Senegal is building a **Dakar-as-hub** subsea/terrestrial backbone through **[[sonatel]]** while
attacking a stubborn rural divide — **~7m people offline, ~40% penetration (2026)** — with a
state-negotiated **Starlink** free-connectivity programme under the New Deal ([[senegal--gov-policy]]).

## Backbone — Dakar as a West African hub

- **2Africa commissioned (Dec 2025).** [[sonatel]] owns and operates the **Senegal landing station** of
  the **45,000km / 33-country / 180 Tbps** 2Africa cable (16 fibre pairs, ~10× prior capacity; first to
  reach all African coasts) — carrier-neutral, open-access. Source:
  [[2026-02-23-techafricanews-sonatel-2africa-cable]].
- **Phase3 × Sonatel — 3,500km Lagos→Dakar terrestrial fibre (May 2025).** A land route (latency as low
  as **32ms**) extending Phase3's Lagos-Accra corridor, giving redundancy after the 2024 subsea
  disruptions and feeding Sonatel's **Djoliba** network and next-gen **Ikasira** platform, plus local
  access to AWS Wavelength in Dakar. See [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-09-techafricanews-phase3-sonatel-fibre]].
- **Gandoul teleport + Eutelsat OneWeb (Jul 2026).** Sonatel upgraded its 1972-era Gandoul teleport
  (Thiès) with **16 new antennas** and a Tier III room, linking Eutelsat OneWeb's **LEO** constellation
  to terrestrial networks — a hybrid satellite+fibre gateway CEO Brelotte Ba framed as strengthening
  **digital sovereignty** and route diversity against "geopolitical hazards." See [[data.satellite]],
  [[geopol.eu]]. Source: [[2026-07-10-techafricanews-sonatel-gandoul-oneweb]]. (Alongside the Orange-led
  **[[via-africa-cable]]** Atlantic corridor — see [[SEN]] hub.)

## Sonatel — scale (2025)

- Group revenue **1,923.1bn CFA (+8.3%)**, net profit 413.6bn; **mobile data 22.5m**, **fixed broadband
  1.16m (+26.1%)**, **FTTH >640,000**, **Orange Money 13m**. 1,000+ solar sites in Senegal, 5G in prep,
  Dakar-2026 Youth Olympics partner. Source: [[2026-04-22-techafricanews-sonatel-ftth-annual-report]].
- **First satellite offer + 99% coverage (Jan 2026).** Sonatel launched Senegal's first satellite
  internet (Eutelsat/Konnect), reaching **99% coverage** with fibre/4G/5G; committed **1m new fibre
  sockets 2026–2028** (→~2m by end-2028), first deployed in isolated localities (Moudéry, Saré Bidji…).
  Source: [[2026-01-05-techafricanews-sonatel-99-satellite]].

## Starlink — the state's free-connectivity bet

- **The deal (Feb 2026).** The state negotiated **5,000 Starlink kits** at a preferential price to
  connect **1m Senegalese free** in H1 2026 — a **5-year renewable licence**, with an expected **64bn
  FCFA saving** over five years vs classic rural deployment. Sall (interview) stressed the authorisation
  bakes in **data sovereignty, cybersecurity and protection of local operators**. Targets: white-zone
  community wifi, schools/universities, health centres, border zones and the **Plan Diomaye** for
  Casamance. See [[geopol.usa]], [[digital.rural]], [[include.divides]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-04-lesoleil-starlink-5000-kits]], [[2026-02-14-seneweb-alioune-sall-clauses-starlink]],
  [[2026-02-26-lesoleil-internet-satellite-decisions-gouvernement]].
- **Deployment begins (Apr 2026).** A national kit-deployment tour opened **9–13 Apr** (Ziguinchor,
  Kolda, Kaolack); the first kits went to **Kolda/Casamance** (10 Apr) — of 1,000 kits gifted by
  Starlink (with 2 years' free subscription), a first lot of 200 ready — framed as "internet access is
  no longer a luxury, it is a citizen's right" (equity of territory). Sources:
  [[2026-04-09-lesoleil-connectivite-universelle-tournee]], [[2026-04-11-lesoleil-alioune-sall-kolda-starlink]].

## SIM registration

- **ARTP enforcement (Jan 2025).** The regulator **[[artp-senegal]]** (DG Dahirou Thiam) warned
  operators and distributors over "flagrant" violations of point-of-sale SIM-registration/identification
  rules. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2025-01-22-artp-sim-registration-warning]].

## Passive infrastructure: a $150m tower intention (2026-07-20)

- **[[helios-towers]]** CEO **Tom Greenwood** told President **Bassirou Diomaye Diakhar Faye** the company
  **plans to invest USD 150 million** in Senegal to expand telecoms infrastructure and improve coverage,
  per the **Senegalese Presidency**. *(Meeting date not stated; 2026-07-20 is the publication date.)*
- **Hold it at its stated strength.** The source uses *planning* and *proposed*: **no signed agreement,
  no schedule, no site count, no disbursement window, no named operator counterparty**, and **only the
  Presidency's account** — no Helios statement or filing is on file as of 2026-07-20. Treat as an
  announced intention until a primary lands.
- **Why it matters anyway.** Senegal's connectivity story on this page is dominated by **[[sonatel]]**
  fibre/FTTH, subsea cables and the **[[starlink]]** satellite bet — i.e. active networks and backbone.
  An **independent tower company** entering at scale would shift the **passive** layer, which is where
  rural coverage economics are actually set, and would be the first Helios position in Senegal the wiki
  holds (its tracked footprint is [[TZA]] and [[COD]]). See [[finance.new]], [[include.access]],
  [[digital.rural]]. Source: [[2026-07-20-helios-towers-150-million-senegal-digital-infrastructure]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the connectivity build is explicitly sovereignty-framed — Sonatel's cable/teleport
  diversity for "geopolitical" resilience, and a Starlink deal deliberately hedged with data-sovereignty
  and local-operator clauses. The tension is real: closing the rural divide fast means leaning on a
  **foreign LEO operator** at the access layer even as the backbone is nationalised.
