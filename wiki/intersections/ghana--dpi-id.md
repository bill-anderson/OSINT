# Ghana × Digital Identity

Ghana runs a mature, high-coverage **non-MOSIP** national ID — the [[ghana-card]], operated by the
**[[national-identification-authority-ghana|NIA]]** and produced domestically by **[[margins-id-group]]**.
Through 2025 the NIA cleared its card backlog for instant issuance, moved applications online and
planned to close the remaining coverage gap (under-15s, diaspora); a Jan-2026 BoG directive then made
the card the **sole ID for banking**. Two counter-currents run against the "success story": the card
remains **under-integrated** across sectors (citizens still carry multiple credentials), and a widely
repeated claim that Ghana Cards can make **payments** was **retracted** and is incorrect (the card is
the KYC *identifier*, not a payment instrument). Ghana has also become the **first country to export an
African-owned national ID system**, via Margins to The Gambia.

## What we know

- **[[GHA]] Coverage trajectory (dated).** Registered adults (15+): **17.56m** (31 Oct 2023, of ~30.8m
  pop) → ~**18m** (Oct 2024) → **18.95m enrolled / 17.75m issued**, 292 centres, 95 institutions on
  real-time authentication (Sep 2025) → **19.2m registered / 262 institutions** (early 2026). Sources:
  [[2025-05-01 NIA to capture 7 million under-15s and 3 million Ghanaians abroad for Ghana Card]],
  [[2025-03-20 National ID system yet to realize full potential (Ghana Card)]],
  [[2025-09-13 NIA clears Ghana Card backlog, issues instant card nationwide]].
- **[[GHA]] Backlog cleared, online-only, continuous registration (2025).** The
  **[[national-identification-authority-ghana|NIA]]** cleared the Mar-2023–Jan-2025 backlog for
  same-day issuance; moved first-time applications online-only; commissioned a new governing board
  (May 2025); and planned continuous registration of the remaining adults plus **~3m diaspora, 7m
  under-15s** and 10,000 refugees, with a Births & Deaths Registry / GHS pilot for ages 0-6. A new
  **chip-embedded passport** (ICAO-standard) rolled out alongside. Sources:
  [[2025-09-13 NIA clears Ghana Card backlog, issues instant card nationwide]],
  [[2025-05-06 Ghana takes first-time national ID card requests online (plus new chip-embedded passport)]],
  [[2025-05-12 Ghana's new National Identification Authority board to focus on public trust, inclusive service delivery]],
  [[2025-05-01 NIA to capture 7 million under-15s and 3 million Ghanaians abroad for Ghana Card]].
- **[[GHA]] Card as mandatory KYC anchor; SIM linkage (2025–2026).** [[gov.legislate]] A revised
  **[[bank-of-ghana]]** guidance note (Jan 2026) makes the Ghana Card the primary/sole ID for all
  regulated financial institutions with full biometric + liveness onboarding. A planned Legislative
  Instrument (announced Sep 2025 by [[samuel-nartey-george]]) would link every **SIM** to the NIA
  database via a **CEIR** to curb mobile-money fraud, and SIM re-registration moved fully online via
  the card. The NIA PIN replaced the GRA TIN from April 2021. Sources:
  [[2026-01-11 Ghana Card mandatory for banking and digital transactions - BoG]],
  [[2025-09-08 Ghana plans new framework for SIM card-digital ID linkage]].
- **[[GHA]] "Yet to realize full potential" (analysis, 2025-03).** Seven years on, integration and
  technical gaps persist: citizens still carry multiple cards (cf. Nigeria's single multipurpose card),
  the 2018/2019 enrolment data is unrefreshed for most, and full health-system (LHIMS) integration
  lags — the DPI's multifunction promise is only partly realized. Source:
  [[2025-03-20 National ID system yet to realize full potential (Ghana Card)]].

- **[[GHA]] Ghana Card payments claim retracted (2026-04).** TechCabal **retracted** its 1 Apr 2026
  story that Ghana Cards can now make payments; the claim is **incorrect** and the article was
  removed — recorded so the wiki carries no Ghana-Card payment functionality. Entity [[ghana-card]].
  Source: [[2026-04-01 RETRACTION Ghana's National ID cards can now make payments]].
- **[[GHA]]→[[GMB]] Margins exports a national ID system (2026-01).** Ghana's **[[margins-id-group]]**
  (founded 1990) won a **design-build-finance-operate-transfer** contract for **The Gambia's National
  ID + Integrated Digital ID** ([[gambia-national-id-contract]]) — the **first African-owned firm to
  export a full national ID system** (tender opened 22 Jul 2024; 11 bidders → Margins). Its ICPS arm
  is the **only West African ISO 14298 security-printing facility** (Mastercard/Visa/Europay-certified)
  and produces the **[[ghana-card]]** (**19.2m** registered 15+, integrated into **262 institutions**);
  it is The Gambia's third ID attempt, framed Africa-to-Africa and AfCFTA-interoperable. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]], [[colonialism]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-28 Margins ID Group emerges as Africa's leading provider of certified digital identity solutions]],
  [[2026-01-20 Margins takes Ghana's ID success to The Gambia]].
