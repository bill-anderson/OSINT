# Lesotho × Digital Payments

Lesotho's payments picture combines a **sovereignty-minded domestic rail** (LeSwitch, localising card and
instant payments to cut international-clearing fees) with competing **mobile-money platforms** (Vodacom
M-Pesa, Econet EcoCash) and a **cross-border remittance dependence** on South Africa that is pushing
migrants toward fintechs.

## What we know

- **LeSwitch — localising domestic payments (2025-05).** [[include.access]] [[sovereignty]] The
  **[[central-bank-of-lesotho|CBL]]** and four commercial banks launched **local POS transactions on
  [[leswitch|LeSwitch]]** (11 May 2025), so any-bank cards work on any POS **processed in-country** — the
  card stream after instant payments (2022) and ATM localisation (Oct 2024), with e-commerce/retail next.
  Cuts fees vs international clearing (Visa/Mastercard retained for cross-border). Source:
  [[2025-05-14-leswitch-pos-financial-inclusion-lestimes]].
- **EcoCash platform upgrade (2025-04).** [[tech.industry]] **Sasai Econet Financial Services (SEFS)**, a
  subsidiary of **[[econet-telecom-lesotho|ETL]]**, migrated **[[ecocash|EcoCash]]** to a new system (Oct
  2024) — integrating Mokhatlo (stokvels/savings/burial clubs), enhanced payroll, stronger fraud
  detection/authentication and forthcoming wallet **interest payouts**. Source:
  [[2025-04-30-econet-ecocash-upgrade-lestimes]].
- **Mobile money at scale.** Vodacom's **M-Pesa** (spun off as **VCL Financial Services**, 14,000 agents)
  and Econet's **EcoCash** are the two mobile-money rails — see [[lesotho--infra-connect]] for the
  operator context.
- **Cross-border remittances — ZAF dependence (2026-06).** [[gov.regional]] [[finance.new]] As South
  African remittance/banking rules tightened, Lesotho migrant workers shifted from banks to fintechs like
  **[[mukuru]]** — underscoring the enclaved country's cross-border payment dependence. Source:
  [[2026-06-12-lesotho-migrants-fintech-remittances-techcabal]] *(paywalled clip)*.
