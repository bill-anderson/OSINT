# Libya × Digital Payments

Electronic payments are Libya's **strongest and fastest-moving digital theme** — a **[[central-bank-of-libya|CBL]]**-driven
shift, accelerated by a cash crisis, that is expanding faster than the backbone that carries it. The
figures are all **GNU/Tripoli-side** (national claims are effectively west-administration claims), and the
build pairs rapid adoption with a live resilience question.

## What we know

- **Keystone annual figure — LD 389bn, +186% (2025).** [[data.statistics]] The CBL reported e-payment
  transactions rose **186% in 2025 to LD 389bn** (from LD 136bn in 2024), alongside Governor **Naji
  Issa**'s monetary reforms: mandating e-payments for revenue collectors, cutting e-payment commissions,
  recalling old LD 5/20 notes (by 30 Sep 2025) to fight cash-hoarding, Sharia-compliant CDs, and licensed
  FX bureaux. The note withdrawal deepened the cash crisis but forced e-banking uptake. Source:
  [[2026-01-20-cbl-epayment-2025-up-186pc-389bn-libyaherald]].
- **Instrument detail (2025 → 15 Feb 2026).** [[data.statistics]] Banking apps passed **4.29m
  subscribers** (200.7m transactions / **LD 313.6bn**); **POS 165,313 terminals** (from 150,205; **LD
  37.8bn over 288.6m operations**); **5.5m activated cards**; ATM **23.9m withdrawals / LD 12.6bn**;
  **186,817 e-wallets**; **LYPAY–ONEPAY** instant transfers 18.3m / LD 108.7bn. By Jan–Feb 2026, POS
  reached 170,149 and app subscribers 4.30m. Sources:
  [[2026-02-21-cbl-epayment-2025-stats-313bn-alwasat]], [[2026-03-03-cbl-record-epayment-qr-launch-alwasat]].
- **Mandate + rails push (2026-03).** [[gov.legislate]] PM **Aldabaiba** decreed e-payment an approved
  method for **collecting state revenues and public dues** (Finance treasuries and agencies must accept
  it); the CBL directed banks to activate **LYPay QR-code POS** (by 8 Mar) and capped FX-bureau
  commissions (1% cash / 0.5% cheque-transfer-e-payment). Sources:
  [[2026-03-06-cbl-mandates-epayments-state-revenue-fx-cut-libyaherald]],
  [[2026-03-03-cbl-record-epayment-qr-launch-alwasat]].
- **Inclusion — foreigners' e-wallets (2026-03).** [[include.access]] CBL **Circular 9/2026** let
  legally-resident foreigners open e-wallets via licensed payment firms (passport/residency + registered
  phone), with daily transfer caps (citizens up to LD 100k P2P / LD 2m B2B; residents LD 50k / LD 100k).
  Source: [[2026-03-11-cbl-allows-foreigners-ewallets-circular-alwasat]].
- **EPAIX 2026 — SoftPOS, Visa, Stand-In (2026-06).** [[finance.new]] At the 2nd Libya International
  Electronic Payment Exhibition (EPAIX 2026, 40+ entities), the CBL launched **SoftPOS** (NFC phones as
  POS), **international Visa acceptance** in FX via local POS, and a **National Stand-In Processing
  Service** (continuity during comms/banking disruption). H1-2026 ran **~25m transactions / LD 162bn**,
  projected **~LD 700bn** by year-end. Sources:
  [[2026-06-17-cbl-epaix-2026-softpos-visa-standin-libyaherald]], [[2026-06-16-cbl-epaix-softpos-visa-alwasat]].
- **Resilience gap — the 2m-POS crash (2026-05).** [[infra.cybersec]] A record **2m POS transactions
  across 67,000 points in a single day collapsed the e-payment system**; the CBL apologised. Commentators
  warned adoption is racing "at breakneck speed" without parallel **backbone investment/stress-testing**
  — the central risk under the growth story. Source: [[2026-05-27-cbl-epayment-system-crash-2m-pos-libyaherald]].
- **Bank-level build-out.** [[finance.new]] **[[jumhouria-bank|Jumhouria Bank]]** (largest state bank)
  launched Libya's **first petrol-station e-payment** (OnePay Mini Pay via Masrafi Plus, no commission,
  Jan 2026). A Finnovate report noted **Tadamon Bank + JMR Infotech (India)** building an AI chatbot/IVR,
  and **Network International (UAE) + Seraj Islamic Bank** (Benghazi/east, est. 2024) an integrated
  digital-payments/Visa/prepaid-card stack — India and Gulf vendors entering on both sides of the split.
  Sources: [[2026-01-15-jumhuria-first-petrol-station-epayment-libyaherald]],
  [[2026-04-25-finnovate-libyan-banks-digital-expansion-alwasat]].
