# Mauritius × Data-centre & Connectivity Hub

Mauritius is building an **Indian Ocean data-and-connectivity hub** under the **Digital Mauritius 2030** strategy,
combining hyperscale data centres, dense cable landings and an explicit **data-sovereignty** pitch.

## Data-centre estate (as of mid-2025)

- **10 operating data centres across five locations** (Port Louis, Réduit, Arsenal, Quatre Bornes, Rose-Belle),
  including Aphelion DC3, several **Rogers Capital** sites (Port Louis; La Tour Koenig DR; Les Cascades HA),
  **Harel Mallac Technologies** (Tier III), the carrier-neutral **Bhumishq** (Tier IV-designed, Réduit), the
  **Rogers Capital Ébène** centre hosting the extended **Mauritius Internet Exchange Point (MIXP)**, **Emtel**
  (Arsenal), and **BIRGER** Candos recovery. Source:
  [[2025-07-16-hyperscale-data-hub-data-centres-telecomreviewafrica]].
- **Rose-Belle Tier IV** — the sovereign-hosting anchor. Visited by Minister Ramtohul on 29 Dec 2025 and
  certified **Tier IV**, it hosts **e-Health**, the **new government portal**, **[[mauritius-mobile-id|Mobile
  ID]]**, the penalty-points licence system and government **AI** workloads — providing the disaster-recovery
  redundancy the ageing **Government Online Centre** (Ébène, ~80 racks) lacks; designed for a 5–7-year horizon
  with on-demand compute/storage. See [[mauritius-telecom]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-31-rose-belle-tier-iv-data-centre-national-strategy-lexpress]].

## Connectivity

Linked to **SAFE, LION/LION2 and METISS** cables, with the new **T4** cable set to replace SAFE (retiring
~2027) and **SEACOM 2.0** among [[mauritius-telecom|MT]]'s planned landings; **5G-Advanced** rollout under way.
~**1.01 million** internet subscriptions (Jan 2025); Mauritius ranks **3rd in Africa** on the ITU ICT
development measure, holds a Tier-1 cybersecurity rating, and participates in the Smart Africa Alliance. Sources:
[[2025-07-16-hyperscale-data-hub-data-centres-telecomreviewafrica]],
[[2026-01-21-mauritius-telecom-strategy-2026-2029-bridging-africa-asia-lexpress]].

## The sovereignty pitch (2026)

A *Tech Trends* analysis frames **data sovereignty** as strategic for Mauritius amid rising cyber-threats and
big-tech dependence — citing the **dark-web leak of citizen data** (the Missie Moustass / Baie-Jacotet affair,
see [[mauritius--gov-protect]]). Local experts argue for keeping critical state/enterprise data close to the
territory: **Jacques David Commarmond** (GWS Technologies) on proximity as control; **Avinash Meetoo**
(Knowledge7) on siting data centres outside conflict zones and near home jurisdictions; **Julien Tessier** (Hodi)
on Mauritius as a **regional cloud-and-cybersecurity hub** where local hosting improves legal clarity (which law
applies, who intervenes, contractualised liability). Data is cast as a strategic asset, not an operating cost.
See [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]]. Source: [[2026-04-03-tech-trends-data-sovereignty-lexpress]].
