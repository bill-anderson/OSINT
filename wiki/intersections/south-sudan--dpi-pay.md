# South Sudan × Digital Payments

In a **~90% cash** economy, telecom **mobile money** is the de-facto payments layer. The
**[[bank-of-south-sudan]]** is now asserting a formal regime — declaring mobile money **legal tender**,
building interbank rails (NIPS/IBPSS/RTGS) and payment-security capacity — but ahead of the cybersecurity
and data-protection frameworks meant to underpin it.

## Mobile money as legal tender

- **The declaration (Jul 2025).** BoSS affirmed **mobile money as legal tender** (under the 2017
  Electronic Money Regulation), targeting **30% adult usage by 2027** (Strategic Plan 2023–27), with
  **refusal to accept it a punishable offence**. Source:
  [[2025-07-19-central-bank-mobile-money-legal-tender-onecitizendaily]].
- **The critique.** Economists called it **premature and "largely symbolic"** — over **90% of the
  economy is cash-based and outside formal banking**, and the **absence of strong cybersecurity and
  data-protection laws** exposes users to fraud/manipulation; pilot-first was urged. A useful contra-view.
  See [[include.access]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source: [[2025-07-22-mobile-money-legal-tender-mixed-reactions-onecitizendaily]].

## Operators and adoption

- **Nationwide agent expansion.** **[[mtn-south-sudan]]** (MTN MoMo) and **Digitel** pledged nationwide
  agent expansion with BoSS toward a cash-light economy. Source:
  [[2025-08-15-mobile-money-providers-nationwide-expansion-onecitizendaily]].
- **MTN MoMo "Mata fekir" campaign (Aug 2025).** BoSS commended the nationwide MoMo campaign and vowed to
  **safeguard a secure, inclusive, trust-based national payment system**. Source:
  [[2025-08-29-boss-safeguard-payment-system-mtn-momo-onecitizendaily]].

## The state rails and payment security

- **RTGS uptake (Oct 2025→).** BoSS and the Finance Ministry reported **"exceptional uptake"** of a
  **Real-Time Gross Settlement (RTGS)** system (launched Oct 2025) for real-time bank/government
  settlement — the interbank layer (cf. the **IBPSS**/NIPS reconcile flagged on the [[SSD]] hub). See
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Source: [[2026-05-20-rtgs-system-uptake-cajnews]].
- **DFS Security Lab (2026).** The **[[national-communication-authority-south-sudan|NCA]]** and BoSS,
  with the **ITU** (DFS Knowledge Transfer, Phase 3), stood up a **Digital Financial Services Security
  Lab** to security-test mobile-payment apps and align to international best practice. See
  [[infra.cybersec]]. Source: [[2026-03-17-itu-dfs-security-lab-eyeradio]].

## Reads

- The sequencing is inverted: **mandate first, safeguards later**. Declaring mobile money legal tender
  and standing up interbank rails are real steps, but in a cash-dominant economy without enacted data-
  protection law they front-run the trust infrastructure — the same "digitising ahead of governance"
  pattern that runs through South Sudan's DPI ([[SSD]] hub).
