# DR Congo × Govtech & state-function digitalisation

Beneath the RDC-PASS/PNN2 headline, the DRC is digitising state functions across sectors — education,
social protection, revenue, oversight, labour — much of it donor-driven, some of it into conflict zones.

## The DPI/e-gov spine

The DRC is **seeking a consultant for its DPI / digital-transformation project** (May 2026) and names a
**National e-Government Strategy 2025–2030** (not previously held) alongside the **$400m WB + €100m AFD**
split; the same account **re-dates RDC-PASS** ("targeted a March rollout, but no launch or delay
announced" — the March target slipped). A thin PR-wire piece also asserts new fintech/cloud/platform
**regulatory frameworks** without naming an instrument (treated as a lead). Sources:
[[2026-05-06-drc-seeks-consultant-digital-transformation-dpi-biometricupdate]],
[[2026-04-07-drc-improves-digital-economy-framework-regulatory-measures-techreviewafrica]].

## Sectoral registers & platforms

- **Social protection — [[rsu-drc|RSU]].** A **Registre Social Unique** decree (25 Jul 2025) creates a
  **second population register** alongside ONIP, targeting poor/vulnerable households. Source:
  [[2025-07-27-adoption-decret-registre-social-unique-actualite]].
- **Education.** The **[[esursi-portal|ESURSI]]** higher-education portal + first national digital library
  went live (30 Mar 2026, WB/AFD; student ID, transcripts, equivalences; DRC joined the UNESCO/UNICEF
  "Gateways"); a **TICEF** national ICT-for-education policy (12 Jun 2026, 19 principles) explicitly aims to
  make scattered initiatives **interoperable**; and a **MINEDU-NC/FDSU MoU** connects schools into an
  interoperable education data ecosystem. Sources:
  [[2026-04-02-rdc-portail-numerique-universitaire-esursi-wearetech]],
  [[2026-06-15-politique-nationale-ticef-education-actualite]],
  [[2026-04-03-memorandum-digitalisation-systeme-educatif-fdsu-actualite]].
- **Revenue & oversight.** An **E-Contrôle** platform (economy ministry, Jun 2026) authenticates documents
  against forgery for economic inspections; the **IGF** made digitalisation the backbone of its 2026–2028
  plan to track state-portfolio fraud (the IGF found the IDEMIA overbilling); and **Go Pass** at N'djili
  airport goes electronic via private partner **TRAFIGO**. Sources:
  [[2026-06-26-economie-nationale-plateforme-e-controle-actualite]],
  [[2026-07-16-igf-digitalisation-entreprises-publiques-rdcmonde]],
  [[2026-06-11-ndjili-go-pass-paiement-electronique-rdcmonde]].
- **Labour — [[onem-mikuba|MIKUBA]].** A **Belgian-funded (Enabel)** national skills/labour-market database
  — a new state registry and an EU-cooperation footprint. Source:
  [[2026-07-01-onem-mikuba-application-emploi-rdcmonde]].

## Surveillance & conflict-zone digitalisation

- **Kinshasa drones + KIN ALERTE.** Drone surveillance plus a citizen-reporting app via **Call for Trading
  DRC Sarl** — the outlet itself raises privacy/operator-integrity concerns. Source:
  [[2026-01-01-kinshasa-drones-kin-alerte-rdcmonde]].
- **WFP biometric cards in AFC/M23 zones.** "Chakula sokoni" biometric cards (~30,000 people, Masisi) built
  to **route aid around Kinshasa's closure of banks in conflict zones** (WFP delivered <$39m of $75m planned
  2025 cash transfers) — the ID-and-payments stack meeting the war directly. Source:
  [[2026-04-01-pam-carte-biometrique-chakula-sokoni-rdcmonde]].
- **Capacity.** A UNESCO workshop on digital governance and AI in public services (Jul 2025). Source:
  [[2025-07-13-unesco-atelier-tic-services-publics-actualite]].
- **State operator near-insolvency.** The incumbent **SCPT** conceded, in its own turnaround communiqué,
  that finances would only support salaries **every four months** ("improved" to 2–2.5) — the state operator
  admitting near-insolvency as reform is promised. Source:
  [[2026-06-01-scpt-redressement-communique-rdcmonde]].
