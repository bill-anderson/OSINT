# Benin × Digital Identity

Benin has built one of Africa's most complete foundational-ID stacks: near-universal biometric enrolment, a national register wired into public and private services, and a diaspora passport-renewal app riding on an X-Road-inspired data exchange — a near-universal foundational ID pushing toward legal identity for every citizen by 2028.

## What we know

One of Africa's most complete ID stacks. The **2017 Identification Law** created **[[anip-benin]]** (ANIP) and a unique personal identifier (~**10m** in six months); the World Bank-supported **[[wuri-programme]]** added **2.2m** more (incl. 4,000 abroad), lifting biometric enrolment to **~98–99%** — the free, lifelong QR-code **"Certificat NPI/fID" / "C'est moi"** card, linked to services from Alafia microcredit to telecoms. The **National Register of Natural Persons (RNPP)** connects **80+ institutions** at ~**200,000 biometric authentications/day**; in 2025 **≥75%** of ID services went digital (10.5m services, 25m eKYC verifications). The diaspora **[[epass-benin]]** passport-renewal app (Oct 2025, 80+ countries, ~15-min renewals), built by Nigeria/UK vendor **[[seamfix]]** on GovSmart and tied to national ID and immigration, rides on the X-Road-inspired **[[uxp-benin]]** exchange. ANIP plans a **citizen mobile wallet by end-2026** and targets **legal identity for every citizen by 2028** (ahead of SDG 16.9).

The stack is now the **electoral backbone**: ANIP's mid-2025 platforms make the RNPP the **single source of electoral data** (law 2017-08), letting citizens find their NPI, change polling station and obtain voting documents — via app or a **USSD *151#** channel for the unconnected — with diaspora-voter interoperability, ahead of the **2026** elections. The identity net also tightened at the SIM layer: **[[arcep-benin]]** ordered unregistered SIMs **deactivated from 30 April 2025**, an ID-linked anti-fraud measure that reporting noted itself demands a strong data-protection frame. The foundational **fID** instrument rests on **décret 2020-099** (2020).

**Governance is the design choice Benin foregrounds.** At ID4Africa 2026 (May) DG **Aristide Adjinacou** framed Benin's model as "a structural decision, not a technical one": **one institution — ANIP — owns all identity matters**, correcting an old system where multiple bodies issued different credentials. ANIP is **directly attached to the presidency**, holds the **population register, civil registry and electoral register as one integrated system**, and its DG doubles as **National Civil Registrar**; ASIN itself was formed by consolidating **four ICT agencies in 2022**. On that basis Benin joined the UNDP/World Bank **50-in-5** campaign (2026-06) as the **16th African member** (represented by [[asin-benin]]). It has issued the **ECOWAS-standard biometric card since 2020**, and inaugurated an **AFIS** fingerprint system (30 Sep 2025) wired into the ECOWAS **SIPAO** police data-exchange (EU-funded, Interpol-implemented). Sources: [[2026-05-12-biometricupdate-id4africa-2026-benin-anip-governance]], [[2026-06-02-biometricupdate-benin-joins-50-in-5]], [[2026-06-03-carte-identite-biometrique-cedeao-medias]], [[2025-10-02-afis-empreintes-digitales-sipao-cedeao]].

**The register is the electoral backbone — and it drew regional scrutiny.** Because the RNPP *is* the electoral roll, ANIP compiled and managed the list for the **12 April 2026 presidential poll**; **ECOWAS** (experts from Senegal, Nigeria, Guinea, 6 Apr) and an **African Union** mission (led by former Burundian president Sylvestre Ntibantunganya, 10 Apr) assessed its preparedness and pronounced themselves satisfied. Around the vote ANIP **waived CIP "C'est Moi" fees** and kept web/mobile/USSD/photo-update channels open to polling morning; the NGO **"Je suis citoyen béninois"** issued **130,000+ free CIP cards** across nine communes in eight months with an explicit electoral-participation rationale — which also exposed the **RAVIP-to-card gap** (failed fingerprints, dead RAVIP numbers). The poll returned a **new government under President Romuald Wadagni**, so agenda attributions to former president Talon are now dated. Sources: [[2026-04-11-techreviewafrica-ecowas-au-benin-electoral-roll]], [[2026-04-08-anip-voter-identification-scrutin]], [[2026-05-18-ong-je-suis-citoyen-beninois-id-cards]].

**The honest gap is civil registration, not enrolment.** Behind near-universal *biometric* coverage sits a weak *birth-registration* leg: **2021–2025 ~2.5m births were declared but under 1m actually registered**, because parents had to make a second trip to an ANIP unit to convert a declaration into a record. The remedy is a **guichet unique** at maternities (WURI/UNICEF/World Bank): a six-month pilot in **10 maternities** lifted the registration rate to **76.56%** (17,000+ declared, 13,000+ registered) versus **~22%** nationally in 2025, though generalisation slipped **3–4 months** into a consolidation phase. Law **2025-12** (Jun 2025) extended the biometric register to **foreigners and Afro-descendants** (a résidents file, distinct foreigner and Afro-descendant PINs) for migration control and civic inclusion. Sources: [[2026-07-16-adjinacou-maternite-identite-legale]], [[2026-02-26-guichet-unique-naissances-bilan-six-mois]], [[2025-06-23-article-6-loi-identification-retouche]].
