# Liberia × Digital Identity

Liberia's national ID is a **cautionary case**: mandated by executive order as the linchpin of a digital
state, then stalled by its own governance failures. The **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]**'s
NBIS is meant to underpin SIM, banking, social protection and payroll — but coverage is only **<15%** of
~5.3m, a **$5 fee** excludes the poor, and a **procurement scandal** (a rejected restricted-bid award to a
foreign firm) forced a **suspension** of issuance. It then emerged the system was **locked** by its
Kenyan builder [[techno-brain]] over a **$1.7m debt** — resolved only by conceding the whole system to
Austria's [[osd-international|OSD]] under a **PPP** to enroll all 5.3m. The World Bank
**[[great-project-liberia|GREAT]]** project funds the enrollment push, while Liberia mandates biometric
ID and hands citizens' data to a foreign operator with **no data-protection law** in place — the
[[liberia-data-protection-bill|DP bill]] still only before the House.

## What we know

- **[[LBR]] Mandatory NBIS enrollment — Executive Order 147 (2025-04).** [[gov.legislate]] President
  Boakai mandated NBIS enrollment for all citizens + 90-day residents; the NIN becomes the primary ID for
  public/private transactions; foreigners get a Foreigner ID card (enabling SIM). "No National ID, No
  Transaction": the NIN becomes mandatory for banking, health, education and telecom; the order cites the
  2011 repeal of PRC Decree #65 that created the NIR. Sources:
  [[2025-04-30 Liberia makes biometric ID mandatory — Executive Order 147]],
  [[2025-04-30-no-national-id-no-transaction-eo147-observer]],
  [[2025-03-24 Liberia launches DPI project (GREAT) with $30M World Bank funding]].
- **[[LBR]] Procurement crisis + suspension (2025-06→08).** [[gov.protect]] Bribery allegations at
  overwhelmed centres; the NIR's restricted-bid award to Austria's **[[osd-international|OSD]]** was
  rejected by the PPCC (7 Jul 2025); the CBL and NIR then suspended new biometric-ID issuance, halting
  EO 147. [[naymote]]'s Eddie Jarwolo called the existing card substandard — a "national security and
  electoral integrity" risk needing a full redesign (chip, biometrics). President Boakai appointed NSA
  **Samuel Kofi Woods** to chair a steering committee (deadline 13 Apr 2026), after extending the
  registration deadline (Aug 1 → Aug 31). Sources:
  [[2025-08-14 Liberia suspends biometric ID contract amid procurement controversy]],
  [[2025-06-23 Broken ID system, bribes fuel public outrage and legislative probe]],
  [[2025-06-24-current-id-card-undermine-national-security-observer]],
  [[2025-07-09-kofi-woods-leads-national-id-initiative-observer]].
- **[[LBR]] The $1.7m debt lock (2026-03).** [[dpi.registry]] Seven months after the halt, the NIR still
  could not run the system: [[techno-brain]] (Nairobi), which built it for **$5.9m**, withheld full
  database access pending a **$1.7m** unpaid balance absent from the approved 2026 budget. ~**640,000**
  prior registrants were locked out; a **$2m** World Bank tranche went on hold; the steering committee's
  report to Boakai stayed secret. Sources:
  [[2026-03-20-national-id-rollout-held-up-1-7m-debt-fpa]],
  [[2026-03-23-national-id-issuance-halt-1-7m-debt-biometricupdate]].
- **[[LBR]] OSD concession + restart, and the sovereignty critique (2026-05).** [[finance.mou]] The deal
  was restructured into a **PPP concession** with [[osd-international|OSD]]: finance enrollment of all
  ~5.3m (plus foreigners) in **18 months**, operate, then **transfer** to the state; ~3.5m vulnerable
  Liberians free; polycarbonate cards; modelled on Ghana's PPP. Awaiting legislative ratification amid
  secrecy over selection and terms. A Liberian Observer editorial warns Liberia "cannot build digital
  sovereignty by outsourcing its digital spine" — "partnership must not become dependency." Sources:
  [[2026-05-13-osd-austrian-firm-rescue-national-id-security-concerns-fpa]],
  [[2026-05-28-osd-concession-issuance-restart-biometricupdate]],
  [[2026-05-15-editorial-digital-sovereignty-outsourcing-spine-observer]].
- **[[LBR]] Coverage, cost + free-ID debate (2025).** [[include.access]] ~14-16% enrolled (740,000 as of
  Dec 2024); the $5 fee and logistics (5,000 cards/day) are barriers; lawmakers urge free issuance and ED
  Peters agrees "if the government pays." A UNICEF child-enrollment pilot captured ~5,000. Sources:
  [[2025-06-09 Liberia needs more funding to widen national ID coverage (NIR's Peters)]],
  [[2025-09-26 Liberian lawmakers urge free national ID cards; registry chief backs proposal]].
- **[[LBR]] SIM linkage + a data-protection void (2024-25).** [[gov.protect]] An **[[lta-liberia|LTA]]**-NIR
  MoU (Oct 2024) makes the national ID mandatory for SIM (10 Jul 2025 deadline) — yet Liberia has **no
  data-protection law and no National Data Authority** despite being an ECOWAS DP Act signatory. A
  comprehensive [[liberia-data-protection-bill|Personal Information Act]] was referred to a House
  committee in June 2025 but is not yet enacted. Sources:
  [[2024-10-10 Liberia sets July 2025 deadline for SIM registration linked to national ID (LTA-NIR MoU)]],
  [[2025-02-27 Liberia struggles to enforce privacy and data protection laws]].
