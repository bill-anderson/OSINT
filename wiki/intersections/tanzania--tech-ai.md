# Tanzania × AI & Digital Health

Tanzania's AI stance is **ethics-and-sovereignty-forward and capability-cautious**: it convened an early
national forum, elevated AI to a Vision 2050 pillar, and frames the central question as **whether East
Africa contributes to AI or merely consumes it** — while the domestic reality is thin capacity, real risks
(deepfakes, hospital cyber-exposure), and health-sector adoption running ahead of safeguards.

## Governance and the "responsible AI" frame

- **First National AI Forum (Jul 2025).** The **[[ict-commission-tanzania|ICTC]]** convened it (theme
  *Inclusive and Ethical AI*); DG **Dr Nkundwe Mwasaga**: **"we must embed our Tanzanian values… without
  ethical frameworks our platforms will not be trusted."** Source: [[2025-07-29-tanzania-charts-path-responsible-ai]].
- **AI as a Vision 2050 pillar (Geneva, Jul 2026).** Minister **Angellah Kairuki** told the **Global
  Dialogue on AI Governance** (190+ delegates) that AI is a strategic pillar of Vision 2050, and that
  Tanzania is building **regulatory frameworks, modernised data governance, privacy/cyber protections and
  national guidelines for AI in education and health** — calling for inclusive, equitable *global*
  frameworks so developing countries aren't left behind. See [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-07-tanzania-calls-global-cooperation-ai-governance]].
- **State lawyers and AI (Jul 2026).** AG **Hamza Johari** told the 3rd Government Attorneys training
  (Arusha) that state lawyers must build capacity in **strategic contracts and AI-era risks**
  (misinformation, cybercrime, data security) to protect national and investor interests under Vision 2050
  (launched 1 Jul 2026). See [[gov.legislate]]. Source: [[2026-07-15-mawakili-serikali-kujiandaa-ai-mikataba]].

## Consumers or contributors — the sovereignty debate

- **"Spectators or participants?" (UDSM, Jul 2026).** A workshop on **Regional Integration, AI and
  Pan-African Architecture** (Tanzania-German Centre for Eastern African Legal Studies) warned that
  **governance alone won't deliver success** unless Tanzania **builds local innovation capacity, protects
  its data, and empowers startups** to make home-grown solutions — Dr Petro Protus asking whether East
  Africans are "merely spectators of these technologies, or participants in developing them." The sharpest
  statement of the [[colonialism]]/[[sovereignty]] read in the AI material. See [[tech.innovate]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09-experts-warn-ai-governance-alone]].

## Building the skills

- **Students abroad + local build (Mar 2026).** Education Minister **Prof Adolf Mkenda**: **16 graduates
  sent to South Africa and 34 to Ireland** for AI/data-science study, alongside strengthening domestic
  teaching/research (UDSM **CoICT** building under the HEET project). Source:
  [[2026-03-16-serikali-kupeleka-wanafunzi-nje-kusomea-ai]].
- **Universities as the risk-managers (Mar 2026).** A parliamentary committee visit to UDSM CoICT framed
  universities as the place to train for AI's **governance, ethics and security** dimensions — Mkenda
  flagging **job displacement** as the deeper concern beyond fake content. See [[capacity.research]].
  Source: [[2026-03-24-varsity-led-plan-respond-ai-threats]].

## The risks

- **Deepfakes and information integrity (Feb 2026).** AI-generated images/videos are eroding trust in
  online content; Berkeley's **Prof Hany Farid** warned the human eye can no longer detect the fakes,
  urging **content-authentication tools**; the Media Council of Tanzania's Ernest Sungura cast AI as
  double-edged. See [[infra.cybersec]]. Source: [[2026-02-19-wimbi-picha-video-ai-imani-watumiaji]].

## Digital health — adoption ahead of safeguards

- **Hospitals exposed (Mar 2026).** As the **Digital Health Strategy 2025–2030** pushes **AI diagnostics
  into public hospitals**, auditors warn most facilities lack basic protections — **shared passwords, no
  encryption, no backups** — so a single ransomware hit (à la Kenya/SA/Nigeria) could paralyse Muhimbili or
  Mbeya Referral and expose patient records. Under the **[[tanzania-pdpa|PDPA]]** hospitals must protect
  data, but **enforcement is weak** and cyber is treated as optional. The digital-health analogue of the
  "capability ahead of safeguards" pattern. See [[dpi.mis]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[tanzania--gov-protect]].
  Source: [[2026-03-13-hospitals-at-risk-ai-without-cyber-safeguards]].
- **Workforce readiness (Jul 2026).** The 4th Human Resources for Health Conference urged a shift to
  **prevention** and to equip health workers with AI skills — MOI's Dr Mpoki Ulisubisya calling AI a top
  opportunity for diagnosis and decision-making but insisting it **"cannot replace the human mind… it has
  no compassion."** Source: [[2026-07-15-tanzania-urged-prepare-health-workforce-ai]].
- **JKCI, a working example (Jul 2026).** The **[[jakaya-kikwete-cardiac-institute|JKCI]]** runs a **digital
  patient-tracking system** for medical-camp follow-up (records accessible on return), plus **AI-enabled
  echocardiography**, the contactless **"Dozee"** vital-signs monitor, robotic surgery and a "Know Your
  Numbers" NCD campaign — the concrete, working end of the digital-health agenda. Source:
  [[2026-07-16-jkci-digital-patient-tracking-system]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty` / `colonialism`: Tanzania talks about AI in exactly the right register — ethics, Tanzanian
  values, "contributors not consumers," data protection — and has the institutional habit (a forum, a
  Vision 2050 pillar, university training) to mean it. But the material also shows the gap between the frame
  and the base: capacity is being *imported* (students to Ireland/South Africa) while local capacity is
  still under construction, the risks are concrete (deepfakes, ransomware-exposed hospitals) and the
  safeguards lag, and the one unambiguously working AI deployment is a single cardiac institute's tooling.
  The "spectator vs participant" question the UDSM workshop poses is the honest one — Tanzania is trying to
  answer "participant," but on today's evidence is closer to an ethically-articulate consumer.
