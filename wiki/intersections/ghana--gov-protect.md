# Ghana × Data protection & tech-law reform

Ghana is rebuilding its data-governance legal base — a strengthened regulator plus a wave of ~15 digital bills.

- **Regulator.** A new **[[data-protection-commission-ghana|DPC]] Governing Board** (chair **Teki Akuetteh**)
  was sworn in (2025-08), anchoring a national data-governance framework; the DPC ran a **16-region privacy
  awareness campaign** (GAPP inaugurated) and reached **~25m people in 2025**, declaring **2026 a "year of
  enforcement"** (fines/imprisonment for non-compliance) with a **Data Protection Week**.
- **Tech-law reform — "15 digital bills."** At the **TechLaw Conference 2026** (AITLAP Africa launch), the
  ministry framed reforms for **AI, fintech and cybersecurity**; data protection cast as the **foundation of
  digital transformation**. The **[[ghana-data-harmonisation-bill|Data Harmonisation Bill 2025]]** (NITA,
  consultation Nov 2025) supplies the interoperability legal base; the **[[nita-ghana|NITA Bill]]** drew
  licensing/levy criticism and a ministerial rebuttal.
