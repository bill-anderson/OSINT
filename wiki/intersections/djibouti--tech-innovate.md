# Djibouti × Strategy, AI, skills & digital diplomacy

On top of its cable-hub base, Djibouti is layering a **strategy stack** (PND + AI strategy under Vision
2035), an **aggressive skills build** for a country with youth unemployment near 76%, and unusually busy
**small-state digital diplomacy**.

## The strategy stack

- **PND-ADEEG 2025–2030** — the **3rd National Development Plan** — was **formally adopted by decree** (30 Jun
  2026), the Vision-2035 successor frame cited across all digital coverage. Source:
  [[2026-06-30-conseil-ministres-pnd-adeeg-2025-2030-adoption-adi]].
- **First national AI Strategy.** Launched Jan 2026 with **UNESCO RAM + UN ESCWA** support (27 Jan
  workshop), under Vision 2035's "Smart Nation" goal; a DERE think-tank keynote positioned digital
  transformation as a core development pillar. Sources: [[2026-01-29-national-ai-strategy-wearetech]],
  [[2025-12-20-digital-transformation-core-pillar-techreviewafrica]].
- **China as an AI patron.** Minister Dawaleh pitched **Djibouti as an "emerging platform for AI / digital
  innovation" to Chinese capital (China Exim Bank, Beijing, Jun 2026)** — a `geopol.china` vector. Source:
  [[2026-06-22-djibouti-china-industrial-ai-roadmap-beijing-adi]].

## Skills for a high-unemployment economy

Against **youth unemployment ~76.32% (2024)**, an **E-SKILLS "Digital Houses"** initiative (5 inland
regions, **3,000 trained by 2029, €7M**), an **École 42** coding school (EU/Expertise France / Association
42), a civil-servant **digital-skills programme** (DCO/Oracle University/INAP), and a new **national
digital-enterprise federation** (a founding member of the African Federation, Marrakech Declaration).
Sources: [[2026-05-16-digital-houses-e-skills-wearetech]], [[2026-04-06-ecole-42-djibouti-techreviewafrica]],
[[2025-02-24-digital-skills-program-govt-officials-wearetech]],
[[2025-12-15-federation-nationale-entreprises-numerique-wearetech]].

## A Startup Act, and busy diplomacy

- **Startup Act.** A bill (label, fund-of-funds, Startup Visa) was presented **9 Nov 2025** — later
  promulgated (Loi n°179/AN/25, JO 08/01/2026; the primary text on eJO is a standing acquisition gap).
  Sources: [[2025-11-12-startup-act-advances-techafricanews]], [[2025-11-14-startup-act-djibouti-wearetech]].
- **Small-state digital diplomacy.** New bilateral tracks with **Romania** (2025) and **Japan** (2026, under
  new Minister Delegate **Safia Ali Mohamed Gadileh**, who replaced Mariam Hamadou Ali), and a busy **WSIS
  2026** (Geneva) presence — Helem seeking ITU / Smart Africa / China support for the "inclusive hub" vision
  and the DFN/EARDIP alignment. Sources: [[2025-09-23-djibouti-romania-digital-cooperation-wearetech]],
