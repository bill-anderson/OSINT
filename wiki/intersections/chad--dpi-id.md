# Chad × Digital Identity & CRVS

Chad's identity story runs from the bottom up and the top down at once: **[[anats]]**
is simultaneously wiring a **national biometric registry** into government as the
single source of truth, and closing a birth-registration gap through hospitals,
schools and mobile units. It is also one of the few cases where the **colonial
inheritance is named by the officials doing the reform**, not imputed by an analyst.

## The registry as single source of truth

The **[[chad-biometric-registry]]**, operated by **[[anats]]** (Agence Nationale des
Titres Sécurisés), is intended by the government to become **the single source of
truth for all identification purposes** — an authentication layer other bodies connect
to rather than maintaining identity silos. In **March 2026** ANATS concluded an
agreement in N'Djamena with the **Ministry of Finance, Budget, Economy, Planning and
International Cooperation** for **real-time data sharing** off the biometric database,
aimed at transparency in administrative and financial management, streamlined identity
verification and authentication, fraud and identity-theft prevention, and digital trust
in line with the country's 2030 strategy. Further ministries and agencies are hoped to
integrate.

ANATS Director General **Youssouf Gorou Tchaby**: *"This system, designed for
interoperability, is an essential lever. All administrations, both public and private,
will be able to connect to it to verify and authenticate identity."* He noted **private
sector entities can also integrate** — for identity verification and for **access to
credit**. Saleh Abakar Zene, deputy DG for Budget and Digitalization at Finance,
described the push to fully digital government as *"built through concrete decisions,
useful partnerships, courageous reforms, and coherent actions."* The registry is framed
by the authorities as an instrument of **[[tchad-connexion-2030]]**. See
[[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
[[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].

**Investment — attributed, not asserted.** *Africa 24 TV, quoting government sources*,
reports Chad has invested an estimated **XAF 15 billion (~US$26.1 million)** over
recent years building and strengthening the registry, including production of secure
identity documents and upgrades to basic digital infrastructure (as reported
**2026-03-16**). The figure is relayed second-hand through Biometric Update and carries
no primary provenance in the sources held. See [[finance.new]].

## Civil registration — the colonial inheritance, named

At the **[[id4africa]] 2025 AGM**, ANATS Coordinator of Cooperation and Civil
Registration **Brahim Ali Seid** gave an explicitly decolonial account of the reform:
*"We inherited a colonial civil registration system governed by colonial laws. But
progress has been made. Starting from the 1990s, we adopted new legal texts that
allowed our civil registry system to evolve. Between 1990 and 2010, and later in 2020,
new legislation was introduced, including a key ordinance that modernized the system
and laid the groundwork for digitalization."* Chad's framework now supports
**universal, free and mandatory** birth registration, with civil registration
integrated across health facilities, schools and refugee camps. This is the
**[[colonialism]]** lens carried by direct evidence rather than inference. See
[[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.registry]]. Source:
[[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]].

## Filling the registry from below (all figures as of 2025-06-03)

- **Health-facility integration.** Since integrating civil registration with health
  facilities in **2023**, more than **120,000 children** have been registered through
  hospital-based registry offices, with community health workers acting as
  intermediaries in rural areas — covering about **70% of Chad's rural population**.
- **School campaigns.** More than **180,000 pupils and students** without birth
  certificates registered since **2023**. Seid: *"Over 80 percent of children in
  primary and secondary schools lack birth certificates. As part of a national
  education acceleration plan, these students are allowed to enroll without documents,
  but we've run regularization campaigns since 2023 to register them."* Services were
  extended to **nomadic populations** via mobile phones running the ANATS application,
  plus biometric registration missions for older individuals.
- **The architecture is SMS.** Seid: *"Our system connects civil registry offices,
  hospitals, vaccination centers, municipalities, mobile units, refugee camps, and
  schools. All data is transferred via SMS to our national agency, which validates the
  information and sends it back for issuance."*
- **Targets (as stated 2025-06-03).** Digitised hospital-based registration points to
  rise from **97 to 600** in the next few years; school registration to continue;
  **zero unregistered births targeted by 2030**. A **[[world-bank]]**-sponsored project
  sought to identify the factors hampering civil registration in Chad.

See [[digital.rural]], [[include.access]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
[[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]].

## Inclusive enrolment

Chad issues **biometric ID cards to refugees** using **[[idemia]]** biometrics
technology, and ANATS received **biometric enrolment equipment from [[unhcr]]** to
support its inclusive-ID push (as reported **2026-03-16**). See [[include.access]].
Source:
[[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]].

## Reading it

The "single source of truth" ambition and the open-to-private-sector connection model
are **stated by ANATS**, not independently verified in the sources held. No source in
this batch reports the registry's **enrolment total**, its **technology stack**, or a
**data-protection authority** overseeing it — notable given the registry is being wired
into finance and credit decisions. The 80%-of-schoolchildren figure and the ~70%
rural-coverage figure also come from a single ID4Africa panel account. See
[[gov.protect]].
