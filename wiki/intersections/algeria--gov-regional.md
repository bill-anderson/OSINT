# Algeria × Regional & international digital cooperation

Algeria pairs its sovereignty-first domestic model with **active continental digital diplomacy** — hosting
declarations, joining DPI movements and cultivating bilateral tracks.

## Continental leadership

- **[[algiers-declaration|Algiers Declaration]]** — adopted by African telecom/ICT ministers under the
  **[[african-telecommunications-union|ATU]]**, positioning Algiers as convenor of a continental agenda
  (OTT-platform rules and telecom-sovereignty / integrated-connectivity 2026–2030).
- **[[algeria-50in5|50-in-5 DPI campaign]]** — Algeria joined as the **15th African / 33rd overall member**
  (announced at the Global Africa Tech Summit, Algiers, end-Mar 2026).
- **[[smart-africa]]** — AI-adoption / digital-integration talks (Africa AI Council, 2026-07); a **UN AI
  Governance dialogue** address in Geneva (2026-07); Algérie Télécom won an **ITU/WSIS AI award** (2026-07).
- **Benchmark.** ITU **ICT Development Index (IDI) June 2025**: Algeria **74th (up from 89th in 2024), score
  86.1** — a 15-place climb.
- **AfCFTA** — Secretariat partnership on the Africa Start-up Conference 2026 (2026-05); an Algeria–Cameroon
  startup-ecosystem framing (2025-07).

## Bilateral tracks

- **Italy** (telecom/post MoU at the 5th High-Level Summit, 2025-07; cf. the Sparkle cable),
  **Niger** (telecom/digital cooperation, 2026-03), **Tunisia** (4th Joint Technical Commission, 2026-06).
- **UNDP** support framework (2025-11) and signed agreement, extended by a **UNDP + Japan**-funded digital-
  transformation project (2026-05; 2027–2030 executive plan) supporting the HCN strategy.
