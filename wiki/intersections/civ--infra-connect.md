# Côte d'Ivoire × Connectivity, spectrum & universal access

Côte d'Ivoire runs one of West Africa's more complete connectivity programmes — **high headline coverage,
a real rural gap, an active universal-service agency, and a 2026 pivot to 5G + Starlink** — with
**[[artci|ARTCI]]** pushing traffic-localisation (peering/IXP/IPv6) underneath.

## Headline vs the rural gap

Reported figures run high — **~95% coverage, ~130% SIM penetration, ~15m users, ~38,000 km fibre** (2026) —
but the rural gap is explicit (ITU urban/rural internet **52.7% vs 24.6%**; **smartphone adoption ~41%**,
targeted to **80% by 2030**). **[[ansut|ANSUT]]** drives the fill: the **PNCR** rural programme (**575
localities / 825,675 residents** target by Dec 2025; Phase II to 30 more towns), "**Nos villages
connectés**," an **AMN/SES** upgrade of 200+ rural sites (360 targeted), and **240 radio sites** funded by
converting 2021 QoS fines into network investment. Sources:

## The 2026 pivot: 5G + Starlink + satellite

Building on a 2025 5G rollout, the minister announced (Jun 2026) a **Starlink 12-month provisional
licence** (live Jul 2026) and **5G launch** — after **US-Embassy talks** flagged Starlink entry and tech-
hub development (Mar 2026). **Starlink was live by 2026-07-17**, Côte d'Ivoire becoming SpaceX's **27th
African market**, at **XOF 148,148** (Mini dish) / **XOF 247,466** (Standard dish) hardware and
**XOF 28,746** / **XOF 40,244** per month for the two Residential tiers. *SpaceX published no launch date;
the held account is secondary (MyBroadband, 2026-07-17), so the event is dated **"live by"** that date, and
**no announcer-side launch notice is held as of 2026-07-20**.* Satellite is contested: **[[orange-sat|Orange Sat]]** (via **[[eutelsat]]**
KONNECT, Jan 2026) and **[[mtn-cote-divoire|MTN]]**'s **Eutelsat KONNECT** hybrid GEO+LEO (Apr 2026,
framed defensively against Starlink) both target rural "white areas." Sources:

## Traffic localisation & backbone

ARTCI pushed **local peering** and the third IXP (**[[civix|CIVIX]]**, colocated at Raxio Grand-Bassam),
**IPv6 adoption**, and national **broadband mapping** (**[[africa-bb-maps]]**). On the backbone: the
Orange-led **[[via-africa-cable]]** Atlantic landing (national-press outlet, May 2026), a **regional
Abidjan–Monrovia–Accra–Lagos transfrontier fibre** effort, an infrastructure-protection legal debate, and
an unusual **Plateau municipal shared-duct** for all operators. Operators: **MTN at 20 years** and its
**Ma Box** (Orange) self-service app; **Omobio × Moov Africa** took exclusive control of inbound
international **A2P SMS**. Also: exploratory postal/telecom cooperation with **Algeria**. Sources:
