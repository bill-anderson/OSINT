# Senegal × Cybersecurity

Senegal's fast public-service digitisation has run ahead of its defences: a **run of state breaches**
(tax authority, ID agency, Treasury) has made cybersecurity a first-order sovereignty problem, sharpened
by a free-internet push that widens the attack surface. (ITU GCI 2024: **67.17/100**, tier 3 of 4.)

## The breach wave

- **Why Senegal is exposed (Feb 2026).** Experts warn the question is "not if but when": the **[[daf-senegal|DAF]]**
  (biometric register / ID issuer) and **[[senegal-numerique]]** breaches reflect geopolitical, criminal
  and financial drivers, worsened by rapid digitisation, technical vulnerabilities, limited resources and
  perfectible governance — risking identity theft, extortion, state espionage, disinformation and service
  paralysis. Full ID-breach cluster: [[senegal--dpi-id]] (hub). Source:
  [[2026-02-09-seneweb-cyberattaques-senegal-expose]].
- **The state-breach run.** The **DGID** (tax authority) was hacked Oct 2025 (~1TB — tax declarations,
  IDs), the **DAF** in Feb 2026 (biometrics/ID/birth/immigration records, "Green Blood Group"), and the
  **Public Treasury** in May 2026. Source: [[2026-03-09-connectingafrica-free-internet-cyber-risks]].

## The free-internet risk

- The Universal Connectivity plan (**1m people** free internet — [[senegal--infra-connect]]) is
  commendable but, without a rigorous cybersecurity system, exposes new beneficiaries to attack; only
  **3% of rural households** had internet at end-2025. Experts (Clément Domingo) urge a **national
  cybersecurity agency**, an observatory and a task team — Senegal's geopolitical position makes
  persistent attacks likely. See [[digital.rural]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-09-connectingafrica-free-internet-cyber-risks]].

## Doctrine and cooperation

- **Armed forces bet on AI + digital sovereignty (Aug 2025).** The military framed **AI and digital
  sovereignty** as a pillar of national independence — mastery of infrastructure, cyber-expert training,
  rigorous protection of national systems — with calls (Djiby Sow, Senegalese Cybersecurity Association)
  for a **dedicated annual AI budget** and state–army–university–civil-society collaboration. See
  [[tech.ai]]. Source: [[2025-08-09-lesoleil-forces-armees-ia-souverainete]].
- **Finland cooperation (Mar 2026).** Political consultations (26 Mar, Dakar) opened cooperation on
  **cybersecurity, sovereign/resilient digital infrastructure, data-centre modernisation and AI-ready
  infrastructure** (Share Cable capacity), under the New Deal and the EU **Global Gateway**;
  misinformation and media literacy also flagged. See [[finance.mou]], [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-01-techafricanews-senegal-finland-cooperation]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the breaches hit exactly the sovereign assets the New Deal is building — the biometric
  register, the state cloud, tax and treasury systems — making the "onshore = secure" premise
  contingent on defences that don't yet exist. Cybersecurity is where Senegal's digital-sovereignty
  ambition is most visibly outrunning its capacity.
