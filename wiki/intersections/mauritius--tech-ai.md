# Mauritius × Artificial Intelligence

Mauritius is pursuing a **governance-led, sovereignty-minded** AI push — a national strategy and ethics framework
first, a state-operator compute build second, and an unusually explicit skilling target — while a domestic debate
questions the sequencing.

## Policy & governance — strategy first

- **National AI Strategy (AIM) + FAIR (Apr 2026).** The **[[mauritius-national-ai-strategy|AI for Mauritius
  (AIM)]]** strategy (launched 9 Apr 2026, AI Unit) sets six pillars and human-centred, "responsible AI regional
  leadership" ambitions (KOREK Super App, DIVA assistant, Regional AI Marketplace, AI for All). Its **FAIR
  Guidelines** are **vendor-neutral and border-agnostic** — governance by *impact, not geography* — with
  mandatory bias audits in fintech/gaming and locally-based-representative duties on foreign providers.
  Non-binding but on a legal trajectory; analysts read it as a **third model** beside Nigeria/Kenya (scale) and
  South Africa (statutory penalties). See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.standards]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-12-national-ai-strategy-fair-guidelines-launch-lexpress]],
  [[2026-04-23-ai-strategy-fair-ethics-before-execution-techcabal]].

## Skills & budget — a 50,000-trainee bet

- **Budget 2026-2027.** "AI" appears **35 times** in the budget speech. The plan: **train 50,000 Mauritians in
  AI in one year** (25,000 professionals, 12,000 Grade-9 pupils, 8,000 teachers, 5,000 civil servants); a **Rs
  25M National AI Learning Platform**; **MyT-GPT Education** scaled from 10 schools into secondary; a **15%/yr
  over 3 years (45%)** AI investment tax credit; and the **Côte-d'Or SEZ** (83 arpents for AI/digital,
  preferential data-centre power, 30-year renewable leases, 100% foreign ownership). Plus e-Gates/e-Visa,
  e-judiciary, a Cyber Forensic lab (Rs 13M), and a Healthcare Innovation & AI Unit. See [[capacity.training]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-20-budget-2026-2027-ai-training-50000-fintech-lexpress]].

## Compute & the state operator

- **MT's sovereign AI stack.** [[mauritius-telecom|MT]]'s **AI & Compute Corridor** builds out the **Rose-Belle
  Tier IV** data centre, **dedicated GPU clusters** and sovereign cloud, anchoring **[[mytgpt]]** — the
  country's first enterprise-grade sovereign AI platform — and **mytGPT Education → my.t Learn**. CEO Gungadin:
  "AI will not become an economic pillar because we declare it," pressing democratised access over specialist
  capture. The **AllMyT Summit** (16-17 Jul 2026, 1,000+ participants, PM Ramgoolam opening) framed the move
  "from ambition to visible execution." See [[infra.store]], **[[mauritius--infra-store]]**. Sources:
  [[2026-07-13-gungadin-ai-economic-pillar-interview-mauritiustimes]],
  [[2026-01-26-mytgpt-education-pilot-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-07-16-allmyt-summit-2026-techreviewafrica]].
- ⚠ **Off-source AllMyT announcements (awaiting primary).** Contemporaneous coverage *outside the sweep source
  set* reports MT announced an **AWS MoU**, **NVIDIA H200 GPUs** hosted (B300 due end-2026), a **Mauritius AI
  Registry** (airegistry.mu, open-sourced stack), Models-as-a-Service (TM Forum), an SBM Bank platform, and
  Mauritius–Seychelles cross-border payments (Eboo). These are **not yet primary-sourced** — see
  **unconfirmed as of 2026-07-20**; not asserted as fact here.

## The critique — ecosystem before infrastructure

- **The "Timing Trap" (Mar 2026, Sameer Sharma, opinion).** A pointed critique argues MT's **Rs 20bn
  infrastructure-first** bet risks obsolescence on delivery (year-long Nvidia GPU waitlists; years to commission
  Tier IV), and that Mauritius should build AI **services** now on hybrid cloud (an "Autopilot" strategy),
  locating true sovereignty in the **Logic Layer** (proprietary multi-agent systems) — and export RegTech /
  Lending-as-a-Service across Africa before its Ébène outsourcing model is displaced by autonomous AI. See
  [[tech.innovate]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-22-build-ai-ecosystem-before-infrastructure-mauritiustimes]].
