# Angola × Registries

Through 2026 Angola ran the region's most visible **civil-registry mobilisation** — a
simultaneous push to decentralise production of the **[[bilhete-de-identidade-angola|BI]]**,
register births at the point of birth, wire the civil-identity and adult-citizen databases
together, and stand up a 16.7-million-person electoral base ahead of the **2027 general
elections**. It is a state building its foundational registers under an electoral deadline
and a hard connectivity constraint — from a base where birth registration was only **63%
in 2024** ([[2025-11-26-quase-metade-dos-angolanos-sem-bilhete-de-identidade]]).

## Producing the BI: from one centre to 21 provinces

The binding bottleneck was that BIs were printed only at the national production centre in
Luanda. Over mid-2026 the **[[ministerio-da-justica-e-dos-direitos-humanos-angola|MINJUSDH]]**
decentralised printing province by province:

- **Bengo pilot (2026-06-19):** 310 BIs printed in 72 hours at the Dande post, ~350/day
  capacity, ending dependence on the Luanda centre. Source:
  [[2026-06-19-bengo-inicia-impressao-local-do-bilhete-de-identidade]].
- **2026-06-24:** only **Cabinda and Moxico** still lacked local printing; the government
  set a **24–48h issuance target**. Source: [[2026-06-24-governo-pretende-reduzir-tempo-emissao-bi-48-horas]].
- **2026-07-01: all 21 provinces print locally** (Luanda has five posts), with 2–3 printers
  per province planned and mobile brigades for remote areas. A **three-phase**
  universalisation: (1) BI to those without; (2) **BI at birth** in maternities/hospitals;
  (3) replacement of old or degraded documents. Service time is quoted at 5–8 minutes.
  Source: [[2026-07-01-impressao-do-bi-abrange-todas-as-provincias]].

Crucially, the BI is **generated from the birth-registration record "through
interoperability between the respective information systems"** — and does **not** replace
the birth certificate (*certidão*), the primary proof of juridical existence
([[2026-07-01-impressao-do-bi-abrange-todas-as-provincias]]). Uptake, not supply, is now the
constraint the government names: **120,000+ issued BIs remain uncollected** (2026-07),
prompting a civic campaign enlisting churches and traditional authorities
([[2026-07-15-mais-de-120-mil-bilhetes-aguardam-levantamento]]). The BI original was also made
mandatory for **death registration** from 2026-03 ([[2026-03-17-governo-fixa-bi-original-para-registo-de-obitos]]).

## Registering births: Nascer com Registo and Projecto Njila

Two donor-backed programmes feed the civil register upstream:

- **[[nascer-com-registo-angola|Nascer com Registo]]** — **EU-funded, UNICEF-supported**
  CRVS-in-the-maternity (MINJUSDH + Health + Social Action), placing registry posts in
  maternity wards so newborns leave registered and issued a first BI. Launched in Zaire
  (Mbanza Kongo) on 2026-03-04, running there since October 2025. Source:
  [[2026-03-04-zaire-inicia-registo-de-recem-nascidos-na-maternidade]].
- **[[projecto-njila-angola|Projecto Njila]]** — a **World Bank $250m** local-governance
  project (launched 2023) coordinated by the **[[ministerio-da-administracao-do-territorio-angola|MAT]]**
  across **8 provinces / 53 municipalities**, whose Component III rehabilitates
  birth-registration posts and runs BI drives (11 posts rehabilitated in Bié, 13 in Huambo;
  mass campaigns reaching 2,500+ and 2,800+ people, as of 2026-04). Source:
  [[2026-04-06-projecto-njila-reabilita-postos-de-registo-bie-huambo]].

## Wiring the databases: BDIC ↔ BDCM and the electoral base

The registry integration that matters analytically is the linking of the
**civil-identification database ([[bdic-angola|BDIC]])** to the **adult-citizen database
([[base-de-dados-de-cidadaos-maiores-angola|BDCM]])**:

- A **presidential decree (2026-06-08)** created an interministerial commission (chair:
  Casa Civil chief [[dionisio-da-fonseca]]) to run a **biometric proof-of-life** update —
  mandatory in pre-electoral periods since 2025 — tasked to **stabilise BDIC↔BDCM
  interoperability**, biometrically de-duplicate, update residence, and consolidate the
  BDCM as the **official electoral reference base**. Source:
  [[2026-06-08-pr-cria-comissao-actualizacao-dados-cidadaos-maiores-bdcm]].
- It carries a price tag: a **$275m supplementary credit** to MAT (more than doubling its
  budget) for the BDCM proof-of-life plan (2026-02). Source:
  [[2026-02-26-credito-suplementar-275-milhoes-usd-mat-bdcm]].

## The 2027 electoral-register update

The **[[registo-eleitoral-oficioso-angola|official electoral registration]]** / proof-of-life
operation is the deadline driving everything above:

- Runs **2026-06-15 → 2027-03-31**, in-country and diaspora, through the
  **[[balcao-unico-de-atendimento-ao-publico-angola|BUAP]]** network — **254 of a planned
  634** live in the first phase across all 21 provinces — with 1,200+ *brigadistas* rising
  to **10,747** people. **Target universe: 16,707,455 citizens.** Accepted documents: voter
  card, BI or passport (even expired); the undocumented must first obtain a BI. Source:
  [[2026-06-15-arranca-prova-de-vida-para-actualizacao-do-registo-eleitoral]].
- **BUAPs double as BI capture points**, tying the identity and electoral drives together
  ([[2026-06-12-justica-reforca-emissao-do-bi-registo-eleitoral]]).
- **The constraint is connectivity.** The government openly conceded that **weak internet
  and mobile coverage** in hard-to-reach areas is the chief risk to registration —
  the same fragility that caused four months of ID-system outages (see [[angola--dpi-id]]).
  Source: [[2026-06-20-fraca-cobertura-de-internet-pode-afectar-registo-eleitoral]].

## The land/property register

On the property side, the Executive set a target to **legalise 40,000 properties by
end-2026** via a digital platform linking **[[igca-angola|IGCA]]**, the tax authority AGT,
the *cadastro matricial* and the Conservatória do Registo Predial — the land-register
counterpart to the civil-identity integration, aimed at cutting duplication and enabling
mortgage access. Source: [[2026-04-17-plataforma-digital-legalizacao-de-40-mil-imoveis]].

## Reading it

Angola is assembling its registers **in the right order in principle** — CRVS feeding the
BI via interoperability, the BI gating the electoral base — but under two pressures that
shape the result: an **electoral clock** (which risks reducing a foundational civil-identity
project to a voter-mobilisation exercise for adults), and a **connectivity floor** that
periodically halts the very systems it depends on. That the government both monetises BI
home delivery and enlists churches to chase uncollected cards captures the gap between
production capacity and the harder problem of universal, trusted possession.
