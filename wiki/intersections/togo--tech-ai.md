# Togo × AI, Innovation & Digital Strategy

Togo pairs a **sovereign-AI** posture — treating **national-language models as public infrastructure** —
with a maturing startup layer and a new digital master plan. Its AI framing is explicitly about capability
and inclusion, not frontier ambition.

## Sovereign AI — national-language models

- **50 languages as public infrastructure (Jul 2026).** **[[togo-ai-lab]]** (a ministry initiative with
  Berkeley's CEGA, the McGovern Foundation, IDF and GIZ ProDigiT), with **Zindi** and the Togolese startup
  **Umbaji**, launched an **open-source project to build AI models for Togo's 50 national languages**
  (éwé, mina, kabyè, tem, moba, bassar…) — targeting 50h of validated recordings and 6,000 translated
  sentence pairs per language, via four Zindi competitions ($40k). Presented at **AI for Good** (Geneva).
  Cina Lawson: language models are **"essential public infrastructure"** for a **sovereign, inclusive AI**.
  See [[capacity.research]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2026-07-14-togo-langues-nationales-ia]].

## AI governance and risk

- **AI in AML/fraud — GRCRO (Jul 2026).** Lomé hosted the **3rd Grande Rencontre des Compliance & Risk
  Officers** (46 countries) on **AI in anti-money-laundering, fraud and terrorist-financing** — as
  criminals use AI for **fake documents, synthetic identities and deepfakes**, the forum sought African
  responses; **CENTIF-Togo** and **HAPLUCIA** are building the reference framework. See [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-07-08-grcro-fraude-blanchiment-ia]].
- **Togo IGF (2026).** ISOC Togo's Internet Governance Forum (+ TogoSIG school, Youth IGF) ran under
  **"governing in the era of AI, big data and geopolitics: digital sovereignty"** — framing sovereignty as
  "not isolation, but the capacities, skills and alliances to build our digital future on our own
  priorities." See [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2026-06-05-forum-gouvernance-internet-togo-igf]].

## The ecosystem and the plan

- **TogoTech (Oct 2025).** ~15 startups (Gozem, Semoa, Édolé, MiaPay, Clinicaa…) formed **[[togotech]]** to
  "speak with one voice" — claiming **CFA2bn+ cumulative revenue**, ~100 direct jobs, with Cyber Defense
  Africa and legal-firm partnerships; framed by Semoa's Adjamagbo as **"national champions on
  performance."** See [[tech.innovate]]. Source: [[2025-10-27-togotech-lancement-startups]].
- **Togo Digital 2025–2030 (2025).** The government began drafting a **new digital master plan** (updating
  the 2020 strategy) — public-service digitisation, digital entrepreneurship, marginalised access,
  cybersecurity, skills and startups. Digital was **~4% of GDP (2022)**, target **10%**. See [[gov.policy]].
  Source: [[2025-05-28-wearetech-togo-digital-strategie-2025-2030]].

## Reads

- `colonialism` / `sovereignty`: the language-model project is the sharpest expression of Togo's AI
  stance — attacking the **under-representation of African languages** in AI by building open-source data
  and models locally, and calling them *public infrastructure*. It is realistic sovereignty for a small
  state: not competing on frontier models, but owning the **data, languages and governance** that make AI
  usable and legitimate at home — matched by a governance forum, a compliance-AI framework, and a startup
  cohort pushing "national champions."
