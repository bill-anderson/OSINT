# Guinea × Connectivity & telecom sovereignty

Guinea's connectivity story in 2025–26 is **explicitly sovereignty-framed** — a telecom nationalisation, a
scramble to escape single-cable dependence, and friction over how the market is regulated.

## Telecom nationalisation

- **[[guinea-mtn-nationalisation|State took 87.5% of MTN Guinée]]** (deal official 30 Dec 2024; ~3m
  subscribers) — Minister Rose Pola Pricemou casting it, at the 2025 JMTSI "sovereignty scorecard," as reclaiming
  the telecom future (alongside a **Technopark Sonfonia**, national **CERT/SOC** and **PKI**).

## Submarine cables — from one to three

- **The single-cable problem.** Guinea has relied on **one ACE cable** for ~13–15 years, now **saturated** —
  "where the road is unique, a whole country can be plunged into digital silence" (GUILAB).
- **Second cable — MEDUSA Africa** (C&MA signed May 2026; **[[guilab-guinea|GUILAB]]** landing party).
- **Third cable — Via Africa** (co-investment, Paris, June 2026). A **2025 GUILAB/SONATEL 7-country workshop**
  had launched the feasibility work; the **Amílcar Cabral** cable was also named in the 2025 sovereignty scorecard.
- **Cross-border fibre:** a **Guinea–Sierra Leone** interconnection (SOGEB–Leoncom at Pamelap, **42.5→100 Gbps**)
  under the 2024 Digital Pact.

## Regulation & access

- **FTTH / Wifi-Zone dispute.** The **FESATEL** union threatened to strike over **FTTH restrictions**, the
  Wifi-Zone regime and Guinea's **non-recognition of Starlink** — arguing global-licence operators may run FTTH
  (law L018) and pressing **[[arpt-guinea|ARPT]]** to decongest the network. The government also **pressed Orange
  Guinée on quality/coverage/cost** (2026-07).
- **Higher-ed fibre:** **Univ Connect** (ANSUTEN) connecting **17 universities/institutes** (13 already live).
