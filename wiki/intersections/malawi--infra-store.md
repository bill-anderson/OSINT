# Malawi × Data Centres & Local Hosting

A **local-hosting market is emerging** in Malawi — government, telco and commercial — under an explicit **data
sovereignty / forex** logic: hosting abroad costs scarce foreign currency and cedes control of citizen and
corporate data.

## Public + telco capacity

- **Government National Data Centre.** The Lilongwe **National Data Centre** (built under the Digital
  Foundations Project; ~51% complete in 2024) is the local-hosting backbone for e-government, now carrying
  **Boma Lathu** and being upgraded to an **AI-ready** state under **[[dmap]]** — the anchor for the
  data-sharing regime (see [[malawi--dpi-govtech]]). The integration of immigration e-services
  (2026-07) confirms **Bomalathu runs on locally hosted server infrastructure** under [[dmap]],
  framed as data-sovereignty and reliability support. Source:
  [[2026-07-07 Malawi integrates immigration e-services]].
- **TNM's third data centre + VoLTE (2026-03).** **[[tnm-malawi|TNM]]** (CEO Michel Herbert) announced plans to
  build a **third data centre** (within ~3 months) to meet rising data/digital-service demand, alongside the
  launch of **VoLTE** (voice over LTE) on 5 Mar 2026. See [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-06-tnm-third-data-centre-volte-itweb]].

## Commercial local hosting — Sparc

- **A commercial data centre to end offshore reliance (2026-05).** **[[sparc-systems]]** launched a commercial
  data centre in **Lilongwe** to provide **affordable local hosting** — GMD **Wisely Phiri** noting most firms
  cannot afford their own infrastructure and "end up hosting data outside," raising forex costs and
  data-ownership/sovereignty concerns. The Department of E-Government (Paul Kanthambi) welcomed a "secure
  platform where data can be stored locally," aligned to Malawi 2063. (Converged Technology Networks is
  separately planning AI micro-data-centres — a growing market.) See [[sovereignty]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-01-sparc-systems-data-centre-launch-malawi24]].

## ICT-services exports as the forex case

- **Exports US$70m (2020) → US$154m (2023).** At the **ICTAM Expo 2026**, Phiri argued Malawi cannot "lament
  forex shortages while underinvesting in industries already generating external earnings," pitching a **Sparc
  export blueprint** to scale IT-services exports across Africa (locally built platforms — Comply Quest AML,
  Saris student records, Farmers Payment Systems, Sparc CRM/HR/Force, Procomos). See [[tech.industry]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-06-06-ict-services-exports-k270bn-ictam-expo-malawi24]].
