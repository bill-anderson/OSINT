# Egypt × Digital Payments

Egypt has one of Africa's deepest digital-payments stacks, pushed by the **[[central-bank-of-egypt|CBE]]** toward a cashless society under Vision 2030.

- **Inclusion:** **76.3%** (Jun 2025) → **77.6%** of adults by end-2025 (from 27.4% in 2016); a Second Financial Inclusion Strategy 2026–2030 in development.
- **Rails (as of Jun 2025):** **InstaPay** instant payments (**16m users**, **1.1bn transactions worth EGP 2.4tn**; **EGP 4.2tn** over Jul24–Jun25, inbound remittances now live), **Meeza** national cards (**43.5m**), mobile wallets (**55.5m accounts**), Apple Pay, **SoftPOS**. **InstaPay fees introduced 1 Apr 2025** (0.1%, max EGP 20) — ending free instant transfers.
- **Wallet-vs-card gap:** a **Senate proposal** (2026-06) for a free card + cashback aims to onboard **45m e-wallets** against ~6m bank cards — an inclusion play.
- **Fintech players:** **[[fawry]]** — Egypt's largest e-payment platform ($1.3bn cap, ~6m tx/day); **[[blnk]]** — PoS consumer lending ($37m raise, 16.7m credit-excluded adults); **[[onebank-egypt|onebank]]** — Egypt's first fully digital bank (CBE-approved, Banque Misr/MDI, 2025-08).
- **P2G via carrier billing:** **[[tpay]]** won Egypt's first **[[ntra-egypt|NTRA]]** Direct Carrier Billing licence to pay for government services from mobile balance — reaching the bank-less.
- **Non-bank finance:** the **[[fra-egypt|FRA]]** launched a **digital factoring portal** (with e-finance) to curb double-financing.
- **Identity link:** the CBE's **[[haweya]]** eKYC ID underpins remote account opening.
- **Consumer BNPL into travel (2026-07-20):** **Valu** (MENA consumer fintech) and **Wego** (MENA travel
  marketplace) integrated so Egyptian travellers can pay for flights and accommodation over instalment
  plans of **up to 60 months**. **No volume, take-up, pricing, interest-rate, regulator or licensing
  basis is disclosed** — the consumer-protection framing around five-year instalment credit for
  discretionary spend is **unestablished as of 2026-07-20**. Source:
  [[2026-07-20-valu-wego-travel-fintech-partnership-egypt]].

## Cross-border: PAPSS as an instrument of Egyptian influence

- **Egypt exports its PAPSS experience (2026-07-21).** The **[[central-bank-of-egypt|CBE]]** governor
  **Hassan Abdullah** met **[[central-bank-of-eswatini|Central Bank of Eswatini]]** governor **Phil
  Mnisi**, who is seeking to **leverage Egypt's [[papss|PAPSS]] experience to accelerate Eswatini's
  accession**. The CBE states the engagement "aligns with Egypt's broader strategy of strengthening
  relations with African countries and supporting continental economic and financial integration" —
  payments integration used deliberately as continental positioning, [[XNA]] into [[XSA]]. Also
  offered: the CBE's currency-circulation system and **Egyptian Currency Printing House** capability.
  Separately, a proposed **[[afreximbank|Afreximbank]]×CBE pan-African gold bank** aims to strengthen
  central-bank reserves, formalise African gold trade and **reduce reliance on foreign refining hubs**
  — **a proposal only: no structure, capital, participants or timetable established as of 2026-07-21**. **✔ Resolved same day (2026-07-21) — partner confirmed as [[afreximbank|Afreximbank]]**, the multilateral, on the CBE's own release: **MoU signed 2025-12-29**, by Governor **Hassan Abdalla** and Afreximbank President **Dr George Elombi**, at the CBE. *(A second trade-press account had rendered it "Export-Import Bank of Egypt"; that was a mis-statement of the name.)* The release adds what the wiki lacked: the initiative is at **feasibility-study stage**, sited in a **designated free zone in Egypt**, and scoped to include an **internationally accredited refinery**, **secure vaulting** and associated trading services — with Egypt named as hub **subject to the study and subsequent approvals**. Worth holding alongside the sovereignty framing: **Egypt is Afreximbank's largest shareholder and its host**. Source: [[2025-12-30-cbe-afreximbank-mou-pan-african-gold-bank]].
  **The meeting date is not given in the source; event date unestablished.** Source:
  [[2026-07-21-egypt-eswatini-deepen-banking-cooperation]].
