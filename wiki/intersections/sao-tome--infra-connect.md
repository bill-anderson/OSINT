# São Tomé and Príncipe × Connectivity

STP has **~61.5% internet penetration** (2025) on an incumbent (**[[cst-sao-tome|CST]]**) fixed/mobile
network, now supplemented by **US-brokered Starlink** — with a submarine cable to Príncipe still the
inter-island gap the World Bank project is closing (see [[sao-tome--dpi-id]], [[STP]] hub).

## The incumbent — CST

- **Network + schools (2025).** CST marked 35 years with a plan to expand fibre and mobile to more
  communities and **schools** (with the Education Ministry — connectivity for larger schools first, then
  down to 9th-grade level). See [[capacity.literacy]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-25-cst-35-anos-expansao-rede-conectividade-escolas]].
- **Água Izé antenna (Mar 2026).** CST inaugurated its **35th antenna** (Água Izé, Cantagalo), ending
  prolonged network instability for the locality — closing a rural-coverage gap flagged in early 2025.
  See [[include.divides]]. Source: [[2026-03-27-cst-inaugura-antena-agua-ize]].

## Starlink — a US soft-power pivot

- **US-brokered intent (Feb 2025).** The **US chargé d'affaires** (James Story) publicly backed Starlink's
  entry after meeting the President — notably in the same period the US **closed its 32-year-old Voice of
  America relay station** in STP. A US satellite operator stepping in as US state broadcasting steps out.
  See [[geopol.usa]]. Source: [[2025-02-24-starlink-pretende-investir-stp-eua]].
- **Live (Dec 2025).** Starlink activated service — **Standard kit STN 5,500 (~$257)**, mini STN 4,400
  (~$206); **Residential STN 1,700/mo (~$79.5)**, Lite ~$60.8 — part of its methodical African rollout
  (Somalia, Chad, Lesotho, Guinea-Bissau, Congo in the same window). See [[data.satellite]]. Source:
  [[2025-12-11-starlink-begins-service-stp]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: connectivity for a two-island micro-state is inherently partner-dependent, and the
  Starlink episode is a neat vignette of a **US pivot** — withdrawing legacy public broadcasting (VOA)
  while ushering in a private US satellite operator. It widens access (and is the enabler behind off-grid
  ID emission, [[sao-tome--dpi-id]]) but at premium prices (Residential **STN 1,700/mo**, ≈US$79.5 at
  the Dec-2025 launch price, in an economy with GDP/capita ~$2,700 (World Bank, cited 2026-07)) that
  keep it a complement to CST, not a substitute.
