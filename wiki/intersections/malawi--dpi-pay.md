# Malawi × Payments & Digital Finance

Malawi has a **large, fast-growing mobile-money economy** on mature rails (a National Switch since 2015) — but
one still stuck in **cash-substitution**, which a new central-bank strategy and active interoperability
enforcement are trying to move past.

## Scale — and the merchant-payment gap (2025 NPS Report)

- Mobile-money **transaction value rose 94% to K37.9tn** (from K19.5tn); **volumes +31.6% to 2.4bn**;
  **subscriptions 19.9m** — but only **44.8% (~8.9m) active** in a 30-day window. Usage is still basic —
  **airtime 42%**, cash-in/out **33.1%**, **merchant payments just 7.7%** ("continued dominance in
  cash-substitution … a challenge to the government's digitalisation agenda"). The gender gap narrowed to 9.7pp;
  the Ministry of Finance raised **wallet limits K1m→K5m** and transaction limits to K1m. See [[data.statistics]],
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-03-30-mobile-money-k38tn-2025-nps-report-times]].

## The central-bank roadmap

- **NPS Vision & Strategy 2026–2030.** The **[[reserve-bank-of-malawi|RBM]]** (Deputy Governor Kisu Simwaka)
  launched a payments modernisation roadmap: expand infrastructure and affordability for low-income users,
  strengthen **cybersecurity** and resilience to power/connectivity failures, modernise regulation, deepen
  **regional integration**, and (per companion coverage) run a **CBDC Proof of Concept by 2030** and migrate to
  **ISO 20022** — building on the MITASS settlement system (2002) and the **National Switch (2015)**. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[gov.regional]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16-rbm-national-payments-strategy-2026-2030-itweb]].

## Interoperability — rails and enforcement

- **The switch as aggregation layer.** Homegrown fintech **[[paychangu]]** (founded 2021, Morgan Tembo)
  launched an **Instant Bank Transfer** with **Centenary Bank** powered by the national switch **[[malswitch]]**
  — real-time transfers to any Malawian bank, up to **MWK 500m**, accessible via **\*632#** USSD (no smartphone
  needed) — consolidating **Airtel Money, TNM Mpamba and banks** in one system. PayChangu also runs an SME-focused
  aggregator (APIs, Shopify/WooCommerce plugins, payment links). See [[finance.new]], [[tech.innovate]]. Sources:
  [[2025-06-17-paychangu-centenary-instant-bank-transfer-malswitch-malawi24]],
  [[2026-04-24-paychangu-unified-payment-infrastructure-wearetech]].
- **Interoperability enforced against the duopoly (2025-07).** The **Competition and Fair Trading Commission
  (CFTC)** ordered **[[tnm-malawi|TNM]] Mpamba** to open its exclusive Super League **e-ticketing** platform to
  other providers (incl. **[[airtel-malawi|Airtel]]** Money and banks) within a year — finding exclusivity
  breached the **Payment Systems (Interoperability) Directive 2017** and **E-Money Regulations 2018** — with
  concurrent physical ticketing for six months and 90-day progress reports. A concrete pro-interoperability
  action against mobile-money lock-in. See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-16-cftc-tnm-mpamba-eticketing-interoperability-malawi24]].

## Reaching the farm

- **Airtel Money × Admarc (2026-06).** Airtel Money and the state **Agricultural Development and Marketing
  Corporation (Admarc)** launched digital agricultural-value-chain payments — farmers paid directly into mobile
  wallets, electronic commodity payments, transaction transparency — extending digital finance into **rural**
  communities. See [[digital.rural]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-24-airtel-money-admarc-farmer-payments-itweb]].

Backdrop: **[[comesa]]**'s Digital Retail Payments Platform ran a first Malawi–Zambia cross-border
local-currency payment (Oct 2025). See [[MWI]], [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]].
