# DR Congo × Digital Identity

The DRC's identity programme is the **most externalised — and most contested — national-ID build in the wiki**: a cancelled billion-dollar contract for overbilling, replaced by a **20-year exclusive e-KYC concession to a foreign Web3 firm**, running in parallel with a separate foreign card producer, over a population whose data-protection authority still doesn't exist. It is the outsourced-sovereignty extreme.

## What we know

**The reset (Aug 2024).** The DRC cancelled a **$1.2 billion** national biometric-ID contract with **[[idemia]]** (via intermediary Afritech) on 12 Aug 2024 — after a Bloomberg/Lighthouse investigation alleged overbilling — per **[[onip-drc]]** DG Richard Ilunga.

**The Trident/DRCPass pivot (2024–26).** From a December 2024 framework with the Office of the President, Singapore's **[[trident-digital-tech]]** (NASDAQ: TDTH) moved through an implementation agreement (Feb 2025), a Kinshasa validation phase (Mar 2025) and a **definitive 20-year PPP** (25 Jun 2025) making it the **exclusive e-KYC provider** for the DRC's 80m+ mobile subscribers. **[[rdc-pass|DRCPass]]** runs on Trident's **Tridentity** Web 3.0 stack (blockchain, biometrics, AI, zero-knowledge proofs); phased deployment began by Aug 2025; capex is put at **$97.1m** with financing unclear.

**A parallel card track.** Separately, the DRC is contracting Ghana's **[[margins-id-group]]** (maker of the Ghana Card) to produce **physical ID cards** via ONIP — so a digital-ID/e-KYC concession and a physical-card programme proceed at once, with the card-vendor selection process undisclosed.

**The governance gap.** An NTU-SBF analysis warned the 20-year foreign-exclusive concession risks controversy without transparency safeguards — pointed, because the DRC's **data-protection authority remains un-operationalised** (**[[drc-digital-code]]** Art. 262). Identity here is being built faster than the accountability around it.

## National-press record (2025–26): the chain dated, and RDC-PASS confirmed live

The national press dates the whole Trident chain and **confirms the launch the hub flagged as unconfirmed**:
**Dec 2024** preliminary agreement (preceded by a Singapore meeting) → **Feb 2025** signing (Trident's role
initially undetailed) → **validation workshop 15–22 Mar 2025** (Minister Kibassa; "connect each Congolese
to a reliable, secure and sovereign digital identity") → **25 Jun 2025** definitive 20-yr PPP → **RDC-PASS
launched 13 Jun 2026** in a state ceremony under Tshisekedi (confirmed independently by two outlets). The
Ministry of Planning valued it at **$97.1m**; Trident raised only **$2.6m** for rollout and phased plans
start with **technical audits and pilot testing** (Trident also holds an **$800m Ghana JV**). *Crucially,
the June-2025 ministry **denial** that "DRCPass" had launched refers to a different moment — the 2025 PPP
signature, not the 2026 activation — and should be read alongside, not merged with, the 2026 launch.*

## The ONIP physical-card track — a securitised population file

Alongside the e-KYC concession, **[[onip-drc|ONIP]]** (DG **Richard Ilunga**) is relaunching physical ID
cards, and framing itself as a **life-course civil register** ("identify each citizen from birth to their
last day"), not a card scheme. The mechanics arrived in 2026: a **CENI → ONIP handover of the 2022
electoral file** (Feb 2026; CENI's Kadima warned it needs deduplication for deaths/moves/new adults);
Interior Minister **Shabani**'s explicitly **securitised rationale** (migration control, counter-terrorism,
"impératif régalien," instruction to process the CENI data "sans délai"); a **new (unnamed) PPP partner**
for the card track (May 2026, amid a short 2026 budget — Ebuteli warns identification could be a "levier du
glissement" of the electoral calendar); and a **2026 identification bill** (Eyanga/Namazihana) mandating a
single legal framework and **interoperability across civil-registry / migration / health databases**.

## Other identity tracks — passports, IDEMIA re-dated, ENCORE

- **Passports (new track).** Germany's **[[dermalog]]** (2022, **$48m** contract) cut the biometric
  passport price to **$75** (from Semlex's $185→$99), with 50 fixed/mobile stations + 50 at missions,
  ICAO ISO/IEC 39794, 2,400/day. Source: [[2025-06-02-drc-cuts-biometric-passport-price-dermalog-biometricupdate]].
- **IDEMIA/Afritech re-dated.** The cancelled contract was a **$697m** award (held by **Afritech**, IDEMIA
  as supplier) that **ballooned to $1.2bn** — more precise than the hub's flat "$1.2bn IDEMIA/Afritech
  contract"; the World Bank refused a no-competitive-tender path. Source:
  [[2025-08-29-drc-digital-id-phased-deployment-contract-do-over-biometricupdate]].
- **ENCORE, quantified.** The World-Bank civil-servant biometric registry ([[encore-drc]]) is **$250m**,
  runs to **31 Dec 2026**, has issued **118,000+ biometric cards across all 26 provinces** (~40% of public
  spending is payroll). Source: [[2025-07-21-dr-congo-public-service-waste-biometric-id-registry-biometricupdate]].
