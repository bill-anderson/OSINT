# West Africa × Digital Identity

West Africa's foundational-ID landscape is split between two opposing dynamics: ECOWAS/WURI is building **mutual recognition** of ID credentials as a rail for regional integration, while the three Sahel secessionist states are erecting an **AES biometric counter-bloc** — sovereignty-by-integration versus sovereignty-by-secession, playing out over the same technology.

## What we know

- **[[XWA]] West Africa — WURI mutual recognition (2026-03).** The **[[wuri-programme]]** (West
  Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and Inclusion), co-secretariated by
  **[[ecowas]]** and the World Bank, held its 2nd Supervisory Committee and validated a
  **cross-border interoperability strategy** and 2026 work plan toward **mutual recognition of ID
  credentials** and harmonised technical specifications region-wide (~300m citizens, ECOWAS
  Vision 2050) — foundational ID as a rail for free movement and cross-border services. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[TGO]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 ECOWAS, World Bank Advance West Africa Digital Identity]].
- **[[NER]]/[[XWA]] Sahel — the AES biometric ID counter-bloc (2026-03).** **[[NER]] Niger** became
  the **second [[alliance-of-sahel-states]] state** (after [[BFA]] Burkina Faso, Nov 2025) to launch
  an AES-aligned biometric national ID, President Tiani the first enrollee (27 Mar). The card enables
  **passport-free travel** among Burkina Faso, [[MLI]] Mali and Niger — the three having **severed
  ECOWAS** (and WURI) ties in 2024 and launched a common AES biometric passport (Jan 2025). Framed as
  digital-sovereignty infrastructure (Niger also plans a national data centre); a Libyan firm holds
  the contract — the sovereignty-by-secession mirror image of WURI's integration model. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[colonialism]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 Focus on Sahel digital sovereignty gets clearer with Niger's new biometric ID card]].
