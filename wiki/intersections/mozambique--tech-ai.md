# Mozambique × AI

Mozambique is standing up an **AI-governance framework early** — an advisory commission, a draft national
strategy, and AI-specific safeguards folded into its next cybersecurity strategy — framed around ethical,
sovereign adoption.

## What we know

- **National Commission for AI (CNIA) advising (2026-05).** [[gov.policy]] The **[[cnia-mozambique|CNIA]]**
  (decree in force Apr 2026) began advising government on **ethical/responsible AI** — regulation,
  education, research, cybersecurity and **AI's labour-market impact**. Source:
  [[2026-05-06-cnia-begins-advising-government-on-ai-clubofmozambique]].
- **Draft National AI Strategy (2026-05).** [[gov.regional]] **[[intic-mozambique|INTIC]]** chairman
  Lourino Chemane presented Mozambique's **draft National AI Strategy** at the ITU "AI for Good" workshop
  (GITEX Kenya) — priority sectors (education, health, finance, agriculture, energy, climate, digital
  services) and **AI regulatory sandboxes** (Mozambique is in the ITU "AI for Good Sandbox"). Source:
  [[2026-05-25-presents-draft-national-ai-strategy-itu-techafricanews]].
- **AI safeguards in the next cyber strategy (2026-06).** [[infra.cybersec]] Mozambique is fast-tracking a
  **National Cyber Security Strategy 2026–2030** (replacing the 2021–2025 policy) to add **AI-specific
  risk safeguards** — announced at CPLP Cybersecurity Days (Portugal). Source:
  [[2026-06-22-fast-tracks-ai-cyber-strategy-itweb]].
