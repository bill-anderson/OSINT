# Angola × Digital Identity

Angola is building foundational identity from a **low, colonial-era base** — and doing it as a **three-way sovereignty contest** over whose stack runs the core: China devised the civil-registration system, India is bankrolling an Aadhaar-style DPI, and the UAE's G42 signed the AI/big-data programme. The result is an ID rebuild defined as much by geopolitics as by coverage.

## What we know

**A low, uneven base.** The World Bank **ID4D diagnostic** (2023) finds civil-registration and identification services began only in **2009 and 2002**, much of the CR system dates from **colonial times**, and Angola is **far from universal coverage** — with **78% of children under age 1 unregistered** (2014 baseline) and wide gaps by residence, wealth and province. The national ID is the **Bilhete de Identidade (BI)**.

**Whose stack? The three-way courtship.**
- **China** — a **Chinese-devised civil-registration system** (state-owned **CEIEC** won the contract Nov 2019) went live in Luanda in 2020, targeting 12m birth registrations by 2022. See [[geopol.china]].
- **India** — the **[[angola-india-dpi-credit-line]]** ($200m, May 2025) has Angola adopting India's DPI model and hoping to **model its digital ID on Aadhaar** (MOSIP context). See [[geopol.india]].
- **UAE/G42** — the **[[digital-angola-presight-mou]]** (2021) brought big-data/AI into the "Digital Angola" programme. See [[geopol.gulf]].

**Civil registration on the ground.** Angola began **issuing national ID cards to newborns** (2025) under the *'Born with a birth certificate'* initiative (Ministry of Health + UNICEF), starting in Zaire Province — expanding legal identity into rural/border areas (with measures to exclude non-nationals near the DRC border).

**Interoperability is the unlock.** The **[[ima-angola]]**-led Angola Digital Acceleration Project (PADA, $300m WB) targets cross-ministry interoperability so digital ID can wire into services — the precondition for the DPI vision. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[AGO]].
