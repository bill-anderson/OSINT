# Nigeria × Digital Payments

Nigeria's CBN fintech blueprint is pitched as a regional passporting template — an 18-month roadmap that positions Nigeria as the licence-recognition hub for West African and continental fintech integration.

## What we know

- **[[NGA]] Nigeria — CBN fintech policy as regional blueprint (2026-02).** The CBN's
  **[[cbn-policy-insight-series-2025]]** ("Shaping the Future of Fintech in Nigeria") sets an
  18-month, three-pillar roadmap: a **Single Regulatory Window**, **regulatory passporting**
  pilots with [[GHA]] Ghana, [[KEN]] Kenya, [[SEN]] Senegal and [[ZAF]] South Africa for mutual
  licence recognition, and a Responsible AI in Finance Hub. **87.5%** of Nigerian fintechs say
  compliance cost constrains innovation; ~20 African payment switches studied NIBSS in 2025.
  Pitched to make [[ecowas]]/[[afcfta]] financial integration real. A Kenya-angle reading: the
  bilateral pilots could let [[KEN]] Kenyan fintechs expand into West Africa without relicensing
  country-by-country — "market leader by design," a template rival markets must answer. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[XWA]].
- **[[NGA]] Nigeria on PAPSS — the H1'25 "−53%" in context (REV-002 resolved 2026-07-16).** CBN's
  Financial Stability Report (30 Jun 2025) reported Nigeria's [[papss]] flows down **53% to ₦5.6bn**
  in H1'25 (transactions **−29% to 3,246**). The decline is **real, not an FX artefact** — the count
  fell independently and the naira was flat-to-stronger, so the USD drop (~52–55%) tracks the naira
  one. But ₦5.6bn ≈ **~US$3.7m for a half-year** is implausibly small to be Nigeria's *total* PAPSS
  activity: Nigeria is the rail's largest participant (22+ banks) on a network now ~28 countries wide
  (**[[beac]]**/CEMAC joined Jul 2026). So it reads as a **narrow CBN line item**, not a proxy for
  Nigeria's throughput — local contraction on one metric and continental scaling coexist rather than
  contradict. Caveats: the base period is disputed (H1'24 vs H2'24), and no H2 2025 figure exists yet
  (watch the next CBN FSR). See [[papss]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27 Nigeria's PAPSS cross-border payments falls 53% to N5.6bn]].

## The payments transformation (2025-2026)

Nigeria runs Africa's deepest digital-payments economy, and 2025-26 saw a wave of **new rails, hard regulation
and crypto-formalisation** from the CBN, NIBSS and SEC.

### New rails and strategy

- **Open banking — first in Africa (Aug 2025).** The CBN launched **open banking**, mandating consented
  customer-data sharing (BVN-tied consent, central registry, standardised APIs) across ~**120m** bank customers
  — four years after the framework, and revised to run via independent committees rather than centralising under
  NIBSS. Lending (70% of account-holders credit-locked) is the likeliest gain. Source:
  [[2025-04-29-cbn-open-banking-august-launch]].
- **National Payment Stack (NPS).** NIBSS launched (Jun 2025) and ran the **first live NPS transaction** (7 Nov
  2025, PalmPay↔Wema, instant settlement) — next-gen infrastructure on **ISO 20022**, foundation for instant
  payments, bank/fintech integration and cross-border. Source:
  [[2025-11-07-nibss-national-payment-stack-first-transaction]].
- **Payments System Vision 2028.** The CBN unveiled **PSV 2028** (Jun 2026) — the strategic successor to
  PSV 2020/2025: oversight, inclusion, efficiency and global competitiveness. Source:
  [[2026-06-17-cbn-payments-system-vision-2028]].

### Scale

- Electronic payments reached **$256bn by Jul 2025** (from $187bn Aug 2024; 4.12bn transactions), and SIIPS 2025
  rated Nigeria's **NIP the first African instant system to achieve "mature inclusivity"** (Africa's ~$2tn instant
  total). See [[data.statistics]]. Sources: [[2025-10-09-nigeria-e-payments-256bn-cbn]],
  [[2025-12-23-siips-nigeria-instant-payments-2tn]].
- **USSD End-User Billing (Jun 2025)** ended the multi-year telco-bank USSD-debt dispute — all session costs now
  billed to airtime (₦6.98/120s), with real-time confirmation and opt-in. Source:
  [[2025-06-11-ussd-end-user-billing-model]].

### Regulation tightens — data, AML, fraud

- **Data localisation + beneficial owners (Jun 2026).** A landmark CBN circular (Dir. Rakiya Yusuf) orders banks
  and PSPs to **localise payments data**, disclose **ultimate beneficial owners**, and observe **market-share
  limits** (anti-concentration) by 2027 — a major restructuring, and the payments face of the sovereignty/
  localisation push (see [[nigeria--gov-protect]], [[infra.store]]). Source:
  [[2026-06-15-cbn-data-localisation-beneficial-owners-2027]].
- **ISO 20022 + POS geo-tagging** (Aug 2025, compliance Oct 2025); **automated AML baseline standards** (Mar
  2026, mandatory tech-driven ML/TF/PF monitoring); **AI/ML real-time fraud detection** (Jun 2026, under PSV
  2028); and a **CBN-NCC TIRMS** telecom-data feed (Apr 2026) letting banks check SIM-swap/recycled numbers
  pre-clearing (fraud fell 51% to ₦25.85bn in 2025). Sources: [[2025-08-25-cbn-pos-geotag-iso20022]],
  [[2026-03-12-cbn-automated-aml-baseline-standards]], [[2026-06-09-cbn-ai-fraud-detection-realtime]],
  [[2026-04-21-cbn-ncc-tirms-telecom-data-banks]].
- **Consumer-lending crackdown.** The FCCPC's **DEON 2025** regulations (eff. Jul 2025) rein in predatory digital
  lenders (harassment, data abuse). See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-04-fccpc-deon-digital-lending-regulations]].

### Crypto formalised; inclusion widened

- Crypto moved from ban to **regulated asset**: the **ISA 2025** brought crypto under SEC securities law; the
  **NTAA 2025** ties crypto trades to **TIN + NIN** (aligning with the OECD **CARF**, live Jan 2026); and
  SEC/CBN/EFCC now **freeze illicit wallets** (Nigeria a top-3 global crypto-adopter). A **7.5% VAT** on
  electronic-banking service fees took effect Jan 2026 (**Flutterwave** was earlier engaged by **FIRS/NRS** to
  digitise tax collection — [[2025-03-20-flutterwave-firs-tax-payments]]). Sources: [[2025-05-09-isa-2025-crypto-securities]],
  [[2026-01-12-tin-nin-crypto-tax-carf]], [[2025-11-03-sec-cbn-efcc-freeze-illicit-wallets]],
  [[2026-01-15-fg-7-5-vat-electronic-banking]].
- **Inclusion & cross-border.** The CBN launched a **diaspora Non-Resident BVN (NRBVN)** for remote onboarding
  (remittances $3.3bn→$4.73bn); **Onafriq × PAPSS** piloted Africa's first **wallet-based naira→Ghana** instant
  payments; **Visa** took a strategic stake in unicorn **Moniepoint** (300k+ POS agents); and Paystack shipped
  consumer products (Zap, then the **Paystack Index** AI checkout). Sources:
  [[2025-05-13-cbn-diaspora-nrbvn-platform]], [[2026-02-04-onafriq-papss-naira-wallet-ghana]],
  [[2025-01-23-visa-strategic-investment-moniepoint]], [[2026-06-25-paystack-index-ai-checkout]].
