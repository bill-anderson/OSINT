# Botswana × Data Protection

Botswana spent **six years with a data-protection law that never took effect** — then replaced it. The 2018 Act sat in suspended animation while its commencement date was pushed back by ministerial order after ministerial order; the 2024 Act finally switched the regime on in January 2025. It is a cautionary tale about the gap between enacting and operating a data-protection framework.

## What we know

The **[[botswana-data-protection-act-2024]]** (Act No. 18 of 2024) — published **29 October 2024**, effective **14 January 2025** — repeals the **Data Protection Act No. 32 of 2018**, which **never came into force**: its effective date was repeatedly extended by ministerial orders, and it did not bind the state or controllers outside Botswana.

The 2024 Act closes those gaps and strengthens the **[[information-data-protection-commission-botswana]]**, adding detailed obligations for controllers, processors and sub-processors: **children's-data** processing, **DPIAs**, **breach notification**, records of processing (RoPA), a **data-protection representative** and **cross-border-transfer** rules. Digital-rights context (access, inclusion, privacy) is tracked in Paradigm Initiative's Londa 2023 Botswana chapter.

The open question mirrors the region's: an authority whose real powers only began in 2025, now facing a rapidly digitising economy (national data centre, digital Omang, SIM-ID, biometric payments). See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[BWA]].
