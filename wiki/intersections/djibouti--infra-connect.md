# Djibouti × Connectivity & regional cables

Djibouti's role is the **regional cable switchboard** — and the 2025–26 record shows it extending in two
directions at once: **new cross-border corridors** outward, and **domestic fibre reaching the interior**.

## Cross-border corridors

- **[[horizon-fiber|Horizon Fiber]] — MoU → binding (Feb 2026).** The DJT/Ethio Telecom/Sudatel terrestrial
  corridor — **~2,800 km, 144 fibre pairs, ~14 ms latency**, Djibouti → Addis → Port-Sudan — moved from a
  Dec-2024 MoU to a binding agreement (signed in Djibouti, 4 Feb 2026), framed around "digital sovereignty"
  (confirmed across five outlets). Sources: [[2026-02-05-horizon-fiber-djibouti-ethio-sudatel-lanation]],
  [[2026-02-04-horizon-fiber-tripartite-deal-techafricanews]], [[2026-02-06-horizon-fiber-corridor-deal-itweb]],
  [[2026-02-05-horizon-fiber-binding-agreement-connectingafrica]],
  [[2026-02-10-horizon-fiber-binding-agreement-telecomreviewafrica]].
- **[[dare1-cable|DARE1]] extension (Kenya → South Africa).** Djibouti Telecom extended the DARE1 subsea
  system south — **36 Tbit/s, ~3,200–3,500 km Mombasa–Mtunzini, RFS 2028** — deepening the East↔Southern
  Africa link. Sources: [[2025-09-01-dare1-extension-kenya-south-africa-connectingafrica]],
  [[2025-09-01-dare1-extension-kenya-south-africa-itweb]], [[2025-09-01-dare1-extension-techafricanews]].
- **Ethio–DJT partnership.** An Addis meeting (Oct 2025) accelerated Horizon Fiber and added
  mobile-money/roaming cooperation. Source: [[2025-10-20-ethio-djibouti-telecom-partnership-techafricanews]].

## Domestic fibre reaching the interior (DFN/EARDIP)

The **World-Bank-financed [[djibouti-digital-foundation-dfn|DFN]] + [[eardip|EARDIP]]** rollout is putting
concrete connectivity into the interior: fibre reached **Ali-Sabieh regional hospital** (>200 km deployed;
**instant inter-hospital medical-record sharing** vs "a whole day" before), and **Damerjog** (Arta region —
a school with **1,200+ pupils**, a sub-prefecture and a clinic online); a grievance-mechanism/stakeholder
workshop covered Djibouti-ville, Balbala and the five interior regions. Rare, concrete rural-divide impact
evidence. Sources: [[2025-06-25-dfn-ali-sabieh-regional-hospital-fibre-lanation]],
[[2025-10-22-dfn-damerjog-school-subprefecture-fibre-lanation]],
[[2025-07-30-dfn-eardip-stakeholder-grievance-workshop-lanation]].

## The incumbent & the IXP

**[[djibouti-telecom|Djibouti Telecom]]** remains a **statutory monopoly** (loi 13/1998, loi 80/2004) — a
"new era" profile whose *cable/capacity boilerplate is stale (do not promote its "7 cables / 2 new for
2021" figures)*. And **[[wingu-africa|Wingu]]** raised **$60M (RMB)** for East-Africa expansion (DJI/ETH/
TZA), operating the **AMS-IX Djibouti IXP** amid 12 subsea cables. Sources:
[[2026-01-22-djibouti-telecom-monopoly-profile-lanation]],
[[2025-06-05-wingu-60m-rmb-funding-biometricupdate]], [[2025-06-06-wingu-60m-east-africa-expansion-connectingafrica]].
