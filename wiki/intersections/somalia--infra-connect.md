# Somalia × Connectivity

Somalia's connectivity is **private-operator-built** (Hormuud, Somtel) over multiple subsea landings,
now being widened by **Starlink** and formalised by a regulator (**[[nca-somalia]]**) and a national
digital strategy. Internet users reached **10.7m (2025, >half the population)** — but affordability and
last-mile power remain the constraints.

## Scale and the strategy

- **10.7m internet users (2025).** More than half the population, driven by Starlink and the **Digital
  Strategy 2025–2030** (one national framework for education/health/agriculture/finance) — yet Somalia
  ranks **191st** in online government services. Median fixed download **18.76 Mbps (+67% YoY**, edging
  Kenya); mobile data ~**$0.50/GB**; subsea landings at **Mogadishu, Berbera, Bosaso** (see
  [[dare1-cable]]), constrained by last-mile power outside cities. See [[gov.policy]], [[data.statistics]].
  Source: [[2025-10-16-somalia-internet-users-10-7-million]].

## Starlink — fast-tracked, foreign-owned

- **Licensed Apr 2025, live nationwide Aug 2025.** After ~2.5 years of talks the government licensed
  **[[starlink]]** (Somalia's **25th African market**); within four months the whole country — Mogadishu
  to Hargeisa to pastoral Shabelle — was "available." Cost is the barrier: **$390 hardware + $70/mo**
  ($210 mini). Notably, Somalia **fast-tracked** licensing and accommodated Starlink's **full-ownership
  model without major public debate** — a contrast with South Africa's local-ownership stall. See
  [[data.satellite]], [[include.divides]]. Sources: [[2025-04-14-starlink-license-somalia]],
  [[2025-08-05-starlink-launches-somalia-rural]].
- **AstaanConnect — first reseller (Sep 2025).** Astaan's connectivity arm became Somalia's **first
  official Starlink reseller** (local sales/install/24-7 support; plans from **$36.9/mo**), with payment
  in **SOS or USD via EVC Plus, eDahab**, bank transfer or cash — bringing local support Starlink itself
  does not provide. Source: [[2025-09-24-astaanconnect-starlink-reseller]].

## Backbone and capacity

- **IFC Submarine Cable Working Group (Jun 2026).** The **[[nca-somalia]]** and the **IFC** opened
  Somalia's first **Submarine Cable Landing Technical Working Group** (Nairobi) under the IFC **Broadband
  Infrastructure Project** — outage management, competition, and a submarine-cable **regulatory
  framework** — treating cables as "the backbone of Somalia's digital connectivity." Source:
  [[2026-06-22-somalia-ifc-submarine-cable-working-group]].
- **IPv6 migration (2026).** The NCA and a **National IPv6 Center** (Jamhuriya University) accelerated
  national **IPv6** migration under a Feb-2025 strategy — "planning for future generations." See
  [[infra.capacity]]. Source: [[2026-06-12-somalia-ipv6-migration]].

## Hormuud — from operator to digital-economy anchor

- **[[hormuud-telecom]]** is transitioning from telco to the **central pillar** of Somalia's digital
  economy: **EVC Plus** reaches ~**90%** of the population; a Humanitarian Portal lets NGOs distribute
  aid digitally; and it is **investing in local data centres** (solar-powered) to "keep more of Somalia's
  digital value within its borders." 4G covers 70%+, but ~**50%** of its base still uses feature phones —
  "the barrier is no longer the mast but the handset" (hence Get-Phone financing, see [[somalia--dpi-pay]]).
  See [[infra.store]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-06-18-hormuud-anchors-somalia-digital-economy]].

## The state National Data Center — data onshoring as declared policy

- The Federal Government is building a **National Data Center** in Mogadishu, intended as the
  central repository for all government-institution data. MoCT's stated rationale is explicitly
  sovereigntist and, in the process, an **admission of the status quo ante**: the facility will
  "eliminate dependencies of national data on international data storage solutions, **that is
  stored outside the country**" (MoCT, **28 Apr 2024**), restated as "eliminating the need to host
  Somali government data outside the country" (MoCT, **6 May 2025**). Sources:
  [[2024-04-28-moct-national-data-center-tour]],
  [[2025-05-06-moct-national-data-center-nearing-completion]].
- **Status (as of 6 May 2025): under construction, "nearing completion", not launched.** Minister
  Mohamed Adam Moalim Ali made two site inspections — 28 Apr 2024 (with NCA DG Mustafa Yasin
  Sheikh) and 6 May 2025 (with ministry DG Farah Abdinur Ahmed) — the second urging the
  implementing company toward "timely and high-quality completion." No launch is recorded in this
  wiki. (The 2026-07-20 reconcile pass separately traced the build to the World Bank **SCALED-UP**
  project, P168115, restructured 2025-10 to a **30 Jun 2026** closing date — not yet carried by a
  source page here.)
- **Separately**, **NADRA** delivered "advanced data center infrastructure" for the national ID
  system on **13 Mar 2025** — distinct from the MoCT facility. Source:
  [[2025-03-13-nadra-somalia-nid-data-centre-infrastructure]].
- Whether Somali **national-ID** data specifically is hosted on- or offshore is **unresolved** —
  MoCT's statements concern government data generally. See [[somalia--dpi-id]] and the open
  contradiction `reviews/contradictions/open/somalia-national-id-data-hosting.md`.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the state's declared data-onshoring policy (National Data Center) and Hormuud's
  commercial onshoring point the same way, but the state's own 2024-2025 wording concedes that
  Somali government data was, at the time of speaking, held **outside the country** — the sovereignty
  ambition is dated to the build, not to an achieved state.
- `sovereignty`: two opposite moves sit side by side — accommodating a **fully foreign-owned** LEO
  operator with unusual speed and no ownership conditions, while a domestic operator (Hormuud) explicitly
  onshores data centres to retain digital value. Somalia is closing the access gap fast, but on terms
  that deepen dependence on foreign satellite even as it builds local backbone.
