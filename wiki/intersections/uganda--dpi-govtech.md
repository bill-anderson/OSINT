# Uganda × E-Government & Interoperability

Uganda has strong e-government **foundations** — the **[[ughub]]** exchange, **[[ugpass]]** authentication,
a GovTech record the region rates — and is now shifting from **"infrastructure built" to "citizens actually
using it,"** decentralising service delivery, hardening transparency, and steering procurement toward local
builders. The honest baseline: only **9.2% of Ugandans** actively use e-government services.

## The strategy: from build to use (NITA-U, Jun 2026)

- **Five-year plan (FY2025/26–2029/30).** [[nita-uganda|NITA-U]]'s strategy (under Vision 2040, NDP IV)
  targets **e-government usage 9.2% → 40%**, **[[ughub]] MDA integration 37% → 73%**, National Data Centre
  utilisation **70% → 83%**, public satisfaction **22.2% → 35%**, and IT-law compliance **67% → 81%** —
  explicitly reframing success as **citizen utilisation, not infrastructure count** (with a Client Charter and
  quarterly reporting). See [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2026-06-22-nita-u-five-year-digital-strategy]].
- **End the fragmentation (May 2026).** A ministerial e-services workshop pressed for a **unified, citizen-
  centred, interoperable** system to replace fragmented platforms — "services efficient, accessible,
  transparent… data trusted, systems secure." See [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-06-govt-pushes-unified-digital-systems-eservices]].

## The architecture renewed (Feb–May 2026)

Uganda's two governing standards documents — the **Government Enterprise Architecture (GEA)** and the
**[[uganda-e-gif|e-Government Interoperability Framework]]**, both built with the Estonian
**[[e-governance-academy|eGA]]** and dated **6 May 2021** — reached their five-year mark and were
renewed, with **[[world-bank]]** support and [[nita-uganda|NITA-U]] leading.

- **Kick-off — workshop concluded 2026-02-18.** eGA experts led a **multi-agency workshop in Kampala**
  on the current state of Uganda's digital landscape, priority challenges and a shared target vision.
  The stated purpose of the GEA is a blueprint that *"ensures government systems are designed to
  collaborate together and not grow in isolation"* (Brenda Maniraguha, NITA-U project manager for the
  GEA/GIF) — the standards answer to the fragmentation the May-2026 ministerial workshop complained of
  above. Source: [[2026-02-18-ega-uganda-launches-renewal-gea-egif]].
- **Delivered — 2026-05-27.** The renewal completed: a **renewed GEA aligned to Uganda Vision 2040**,
  the Digital Uganda Vision, emerging technologies and sector digitalisation priorities; and an
  **enhanced e-GIF** for *"secure, standardised, and seamless data exchange across government
  systems."* The process ran a full assessment of the existing frameworks, cross-sector stakeholder
  consultation, and updated policy and technical recommendations, followed by awareness and engagement
  workshops for policymakers and technical teams. Claimed benefits: less duplication across systems and
  stronger evidence-based decision-making. Source:
  [[2026-05-27-ega-uganda-renewed-digital-government-frameworks]].

**Delivered is not deployed.** eGA describes the workshops as preparing institutions *"for the rollout
of the renewed frameworks"* — **as of 2026-07-20 no rollout, adoption milestone or compliance figure
for the renewed GEA/e-GIF is reported in any source held here**, and neither renewed document itself is
held. Read against the strategy targets above, this is the standards layer under the **[[ughub]] 37% →
73%** integration goal: the blueprint has been rewritten, and whether MDAs build to it is the open
question. See **ACQ-UGA-GEA**. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]].

## Service delivery, decentralised

- **Service Uganda Centres.** NITA-U + **[[posta-uganda]]** are building regional **one-stop centres** — the
  **UGX 4bn Gulu** centre (40+ workstations for URSB, URA, NIRA; UPDF Engineering Brigade; completion Jun
  2026), with Mbarara and Tororo to follow — under the UDAP-GovNet programme, bringing services physically
  closer to northern Uganda. Source: [[2026-03-13-service-uganda-centre-construction-gulu]].

## Transparency: open contracting

- **GPP → OC4IDS (Nov 2025).** The **[[ppda-uganda|PPDA]]** Government Procurement Portal — already "one of
  the strongest transparency tools in the region" with machine-readable data — is being upgraded to the
  **Open Contracting for Infrastructure Data Standard**, adding project-level and sustainability/
  environmental-safeguard data (with CoST Uganda, finance ministry). See [[data.open]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-14-govt-upgrade-procurement-portal-oc4ids]].

## Technology sovereignty: build it locally

- **National Innovator Registry (May 2026).** The Ministry of ICT issued a **public call for Ugandan
  innovators** (developers, startups, universities, hubs) to submit **working prototypes** for government
  systems — spanning tax, land, hospital records, employment and digital ID — to **reduce dependence on
  foreign technology providers** and build "a sustainable pipeline of locally developed government digital
  solutions." *(Corroborates the hub's Prototype Showcase / gazetted National Innovator Registry, 2026-05-22.)*
  See [[tech.innovate]], [[sovereignty]]. Source: [[2026-05-19-govt-turns-to-local-innovators-national-innovator-registry]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Uganda's e-government is unusually **standards-literate** — a real exchange layer, an
  authentication platform, machine-readable open contracting, a strategy that measures *use* rather than
  *spend*, and an explicit push to have **Ugandans build the state's systems**. That is a mature,
  sovereignty-minded GovTech posture. The gap is the same one that runs through the whole Uganda file: the
  plumbing is good, but only **9.2% of citizens actually use the services**, and the "citizen-centred"
  rhetoric coexists with a state that switches the network off (see [[uganda--infra-connect]]) and gates
  services behind an ID that reached barely a third of first-time registrants (see [[uganda--dpi-id]]). Build
  quality is not the bottleneck; reach and trust are.

## Acquisition note (2026-07-20)

Uganda's **renewed Government Enterprise Architecture and e-Government Interoperability Framework
(delivered by 2026-05-27) are not held as of 2026-07-20**, and one acquisition attempt confirmed that
**neither publisher publishes them**. NITA-U's own project page serves only the **2021 originals**
(e-GIF and the e-GIF Reference Architecture), and the e-Governance Academy's three project pages
announce the work and its outcomes without attaching either document. The renewal ran **November 2025
- May 2026**.

The wiki can therefore state that the frameworks were renewed and **nothing about what they now
require**: what changed against the 2021 e-GIF, the technical standards MDAs must now build to, and
any compliance or rollout schedule are all unheld. **No rollout or adoption figure is published as of
2026-07-20** either.
