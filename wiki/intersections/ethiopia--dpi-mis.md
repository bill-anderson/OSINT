# Ethiopia × Health Information Systems

Ethiopia is the wiki's best-documented **health-information-systems** case: three peer-reviewed
studies plus the [[ethiopia-moh]]'s own digital-health build show a system that has achieved near-
universal [[dhis2]] coverage but still lags on ICT infrastructure, data *use* and interoperability.

## What we know

- **Coverage vs maturity.** [[dhis2]] has been implemented nationally since **2018 (v2.30)**;
  **>95%** of facilities use it. But a SOCI maturity assessment scores the national deployment only
  at the **"defined" stage (2.81/5)** against a **"managed" (4.09) target for 2025** — with **ICT
  infrastructure the weakest domain ("repeatable", 2.14)** and interoperability/data-use only
  "emerging." Source:
  [[2024-07-26 Maturity Assessment of District Health Information System Version 2 Implementation in Ethiopia Current Status and Improvement Pathways]]
  ([[dhis2-maturity-ethiopia]]).
- **Data use is improving, from a low base.** A 10-item facility-level HMIS **data-use index**
  across **305 facilities** rose **7.2 → 7.9 (2019→2020, P<0.01)** — measurable progress in whether
  data is actually used for local decisions, not just reported. Source:
  [[2023-05-11 Data for local decision-making, not a mere reporting requirement development of an index to measure facility-level use of HMIS data  Published in Journal of Global Health Reports]]
  ([[hmis-data-use-index-ethiopia]]).
- **The community layer runs on power banks.** [[echis-ethiopia]] — the offline-first mobile app
  for **~40,000 Health Extension Workers** — is constrained by **9% rural electricity** and Ethio
  Telecom coverage of **85% 2G / 66% 3G / 4% 4G**; the MoH distributed ~29,950 power banks with
  tablets. Health workers cite the **parallel paper+electronic recording burden** and theft
  liability. Source:
  [[2023-06-07 Barriers, facilitators and motivators of electronic community health information system use among health workers in Ethiopia]]
  ([[echis-ethiopia-study]]).
- **The missing interoperability spine.** The DHIS2 maturity study explicitly names the absence of a
  national **digital ID / client registry** as an interoperability gap — a role Ethiopia's
  **[[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]** is only now beginning to fill (see [[ethiopia--dpi-id]]).
- **Sustainability risk.** After the 2025 US/USAID funding cuts, [[hisp-centre]]/DHIS2 argue for
  locally-owned, government-funded routine systems; Ethiopia is named among the countries whose
  national DHIS2 stayed online on local staffing. Source:
  [[2025-04-03 Now is the time to invest in locally-owned health data systems]].
