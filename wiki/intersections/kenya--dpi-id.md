# Kenya × Digital Identity

Kenya runs one of Africa's most scrutinised digital-ID programmes — the **[[maisha-namba|Maisha Namba]]**
UPI (successor to the litigated Huduma Namba) — while simultaneously widening statutory legal-identity
access and piloting decentralised verifiable credentials. The defining tension is **inclusion**: heavy
budget and enrolment drives against independent findings that the design risks entrenching the exclusion
of the ~5m Kenyans without IDs.

## What we know

- **[[KEN]] Maisha Namba — adoption vs exclusion (2024-26).** [[include.divides]] Kenya committed **KES
  15.13bn ($117m)** in FY2024/25 (7.5m digital ID cards, UPI rollout) and scrapped first-time-ID fees
  (Mar 2025, boosting Kakamega registrations). But the first independent Model-Governance-Framework
  analysis (Temple iLIT) found Maisha Namba "poised to exacerbate inequalities," and an HRRC case study
  documents ~5m without ID, only 34% of registered children with birth certificates (KDHS 2022), and the
  worst gaps in arid/semi-arid lands and among Nubians, ethnic Somalis and nomadic communities. Sources:
  [[2024-12-17 A Model Governance Framework Analysis of Kenya's Maisha Namba]],
  [[2025-09-18 Why Do IDs and Birth Certificates Matter - A Case Study of Kenya]],
  [[2025-09-08 Scrapping fees for first-timers boosts Kenya digital ID registration]],
  [[2024-06-17 Kenya dedicates $117M to digital ID, related projects in 2024-2025 budget]].
- **[[KEN]] Statutory CRVS access + decentralised credentials (2026).** President Ruto assented to the
  **Births and Deaths Registration (Amendment) Bill 2024**, mandating a civil-registration office in each
  of the **290 sub-counties** ([[gov.legislate]], [[digital.rural]]). Estonia's **[[cybernetica]]** and the
  **[[tony-blair-institute]]** completed a **W3C verifiable-credentials wallet** PoC with the
  **[[kenya-public-service-commission]]** (no single credential database), triggered by an audit finding
  **>2,000 fake certificates**. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.standards]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-24 Kenya streamlines access to legal and digital identity]],
  [[2026-03-09 Cybernetica, Tony Blair Institute pilot digital credential wallet in Kenya]].
