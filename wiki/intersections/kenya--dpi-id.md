# Kenya × Digital Identity

Kenya is widening statutory legal-identity access while piloting decentralised verifiable credentials — mandating civil-registration coverage across every sub-county in law, and testing a W3C-standard credential wallet with no single credential database.

## What we know

President Ruto assented to the **Births and Deaths Registration (Amendment) Bill 2024**, mandating at least one civil-registration office in each of the **290 sub-counties** (reinforcing Constitution Art. 6(3)) to reach rural and border communities — foundational CRVS access anchored in statute ([[gov.legislate]], [[digital.rural]]). Separately, Estonia's **[[cybernetica]]** and the **[[tony-blair-institute]]** completed a **verifiable-credentials wallet** proof-of-concept with the **[[kenya-public-service-commission]]** — W3C VC, decentralised (no single credential database), triggered by an audit finding **>2,000 fake certificates** in a recruitment drive, aligned to Kenya's Trust Framework. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.registry]].
