# Ghana × GovTech / e-Government

Ghana is consolidating public services onto a Ghana-Card-integrated digital stack — ambitious in scope but
scarred by a health-IT failure.

- **Ghana.gov.** The **[[ghana-gov-platform|Ghana.gov]]** platform is being expanded to **~16,000 services**
  (full Ghana Card integration), funded by the **$200m [[ghana-digital-acceleration-project|WB Digital
  Acceleration Project]]**, and re-architected in a 2026 shake-up (GOV.UK/Irembo models).
- **Interoperability ambition.** Government is weighing a **Danish "eBoks" model** to unify **NHIS, GRA (tax)
  and DVLA (licensing)** records; the Interior Ministry launched an **online digital-services portal** (Dec
  2025) integrated with NIA databases.
- **Revenue / MSME.** **Trident** (Singapore) — via a 50/50 JV with Aliska — began the **commercial rollout of
  the GRA digital tax / MSME-formalisation platform** ("Sikaflow", 530k MSMEs targeted, 2026-05).
- **Health-MIS reset.** After the failure of the **LHIMS/Lightwave** platform, the Health Ministry ordered a
  nationwide rollout of **GHIMS** (Nov 2025); Minister Akandoh exposed a **failed $77m health-data contract**
  (~15m records reconstructed; the Attorney-General to decide on recovery) — a cautionary GovTech-procurement
  case. See [[ghana-health-service]].
