# Burkina Faso × Data Centres & "Zero Data Abroad"

Burkina Faso pairs a hard localisation rule — **"zero data abroad"** — with interim capacity while a
national data centre is built, and adds a supervision layer over the whole digital estate.

## Interim capacity, ahead of a national data centre

- **Two mini data centres, Ouagadougou (Jan 2026).** Commissioned as an interim step toward a planned
  national data centre, enforcing a **"zero data abroad"** localization rule
  ([[bf-national-datacenters]]): **~3,000 TB (10× the prior capacity), 7,000+ VMs, 16bn FCFA**, and
  **~30bn FCFA of savings over five years**, run by national teams — ahead of a national **Big Data
  Centre (~2028)**. National hosting is set by a **PM circular** (health data needs a derogation).
  Sources: [[2026-01-23-burkina-faso-asserts-digital-sovereignty-mini-data-centres]],
  [[2026-03-03-tinto-hebergement-national-souverainete]].
- **The programme behind it.** **PACTDIGITAL-BF** — ~**$170m** [[world-bank]] IDA plus national
  funding, **12 flagship projects to 2030**. See [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-23 Burkina Faso steps into the future with digital investment]].

## Supervision

- **Digital Infrastructure Supervision Center (2026-01).** ~**$5.4m**, a **nine-month build**, delivery
  **~Oct 2026**, to oversee the backbone, the data centres and cyberspace. See [[infra.cybersec]].
  Source: [[2026-01-22 Burkina Faso aims for digital sovereignty with infrastructure supervision center]].

## Regional frame

Burkina Faso sits inside the **[[alliance-of-sahel-states]]** (AES) digital-sovereignty bloc, whose
"mutualised" infrastructure ambition was staged at Mali's Tier III National Data Centre launch
(2026-02) — see [[infra.store]], [[gov.regional]].
