# Senegal × AI

AI is written into the New Deal ([[senegal--gov-policy]]) as a strategic lever, and Senegal is
positioning less as a model-builder than as an advocate for **inclusive global AI governance and
capacity-building** — while standing up research labs and a data/AI use-case blueprint at home.

## Strategy and the sovereignty question

- **A framing question (Feb 2025).** Ahead of the Paris AI summit and the EU AI Act's entry into force,
  Le Soleil framed Senegal's challenge as building an **ethical AI framework** — and asked, "faced with
  the domination of **Chinese and American giants**, what strategy for Senegal and Africa?" AI as a
  lever of power the country must not simply consume. Source: [[2025-02-04-ai-strategy-for-senegal]].
- **AI as a public good (Mar 2025).** Via the **Forum Information & Démocratie** consultations (with
  Benin, Côte d'Ivoire, Mauritania) and Senegalese digital-rights org **Jonction**, seven experts
  drafted Senegal's recommendations for **inclusive AI governance** — algorithmic influence on public
  opinion as the core concern. See [[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2025-03-19-ai-as-public-good-regulation]].

## Research capacity

- **AI4D labs — ALIVE & DiCentre4AI (Jan 2025).** Cheikh Anta Diop University (UCAD) and the Cheikh
  Hamidou Kane Digital University (UN-CHK) launched two AI research labs under the **AI4D** programme —
  aimed at making Senegal a continental AI hub, with applications in agriculture, education and industry.
  See [[capacity.research]]. Source: [[2025-01-31-alive-dicentre4ai-ai-labs]].

## Data & AI governance blueprint

- **Blueprint for Value Creation Through Data (Jul 2026).** With **[[smart-africa]]** and Germany's
  **GIZ**, the digital ministry convened institutions, universities and partners (1–2 Jul) to identify
  priority **data/AI use cases** — health, agriculture, transport, education, taxation, territorial
  administration, security — feeding a national blueprint and a **secure data-sharing governance
  framework** to cut fragmented databases. Data sovereignty is the central pillar. See [[dpi.exchange]].
  Source: [[2026-07-06-wearetech-data-ai-governance-blueprint]].

## Global AI diplomacy

- **Global AI Dialogue, Geneva (Jul 2026).** Minister Samba Diouf, speaking for the **Global AI
  Capacity-Building Cooperation Network** (centres in Brazil, China, Ethiopia, Germany, Guinea, India,
  Kenya, Rwanda, Niger, Saudi Arabia, Vietnam, the EU, and others), called for "concrete, voluntary and
  inclusive" cooperation so developing countries can benefit from AI, invoking the **Global Digital
  Compact** and a proposed **global AI fund**. See [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-08-lesoleil-ia-cooperation-mondiale]].

## Inclusion

- **AI skills for the blind (Jul 2026).** The **STMicroelectronics Foundation** with the **ITU**
  (**ICB4VI** programme) ran an advanced IT/AI course for blind and low-vision Senegalese — 700 trained
  to date — advancing an inclusive digital economy. See [[capacity.training]], [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2026-07-06-seneweb-programme-employabilite-non-voyants]].

## Reads

- `colonialism` / `sovereignty`: Senegal names the dependency explicitly — AI as a power lever
  dominated by US and Chinese giants — and its answer is *governance-and-capacity diplomacy* (public-good
  framing, the capacity-building network, a global AI fund) plus local labs, rather than a national model.
  A realistic small-state posture: shape the rules and build skills where you cannot build the frontier.
