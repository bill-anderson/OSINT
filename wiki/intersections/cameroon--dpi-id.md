# Cameroon × Digital Identity

Cameroon is rebuilding identity on **two coordinated tracks** — a new foreign-run biometric ID card (DGSN/Augentic) and a legally-reformed digital civil registry (BUNEC/Law 2024/016) meant to converge on a single Unique Personal Identification Number. The ambition is real; the friction is cost, capacity and the politics of a 15-year foreign BOT dropped into an election year.

## What we know

**The biometric card (2025).** The **[[dgsn-cameroon]]** (national police) launched a new-generation biometric **CNI** under a **15-year build-operate-transfer contract with German firm [[augentic]]** (signed May 2024): e-chip, MRZ, QR, ten fingerprints; ICAO/ISO-IEC standards; Cameroon the **first African adopter of the ID-1 portrait format**. Pre-enrolment opened 17 Feb 2025 through the **[[idcam]]** portal (www.idcam.cm) for a **10,000 FCFA (~$16) fee** — a *priced gateway*, and the number opposition MPs later objected to on cost grounds. Regional enrolment began 24 Feb; Biya's decree N°2025/059 (28 Feb 2025) fixed CNI characteristics; **issuance began 3 Mar 2025**, and the **48-hour promise was met** at first hand-over (Yaoundé, 3 Mar), citizens contrasting it with cards uncollected since 2020. A **second production centre opened in Garoua** (Aug 2025, ~2,000 cards/day) after Douala, adding an IOM/UNHCR refugee-ID track for the displaced Far North. A valid CNI is required to register and vote before the **October 2025 presidential election** — which is where the second, election-side register comes in.

**The election-side register ([[elecam]]).** Distinct from the DGSN/CNI stack, **Elections Cameroon** runs its own **biometric voter register**. It took delivery of **1,000 third-generation enrolment kits** (May 2025) to replace ageing 2012/2017 kits — but not before the older kits caused real harm: **~12,000 voters were struck from the 2024 register over fingerprint-capture failures** (later reinstated after outcry; MRC's Kamto alleged deliberate exclusion). The hardest inclusion-harm evidence in the CMR corpus, and the antecedent to the kit purchase.

**The civil-registry reform (2024–26).** The **new civil-registration law** (**[[cameroon-civil-registration-law-2024]]**, Law 2024/016) was **enacted 23 Dec 2024 (130 sections)** — the wiki previously held only the Nov-2024 draft stage. It brings electronic civil-status declaration, a national database, **digital birth/marriage/death certificates** and a **Unique Personal Identification Number (UPIN)**, and lets displaced persons re-establish lost certificates in their place of residence. **[[bunec]]** (with Luxembourg's **[[liber-technologies]]**) targets a **fully digital CRVS by 2029**. The first digital birth certificate issued in the North-West/South-West (Limbe I, Jan 2026, UNDP/Denmark); councils then began routine issuance of **digital birth certificates with the same legal worth as physical ones** — against a backdrop of weak equipment, limited internet and low digital literacy.

**Consular/document layer and sectoral IDs.** Ivorian vendor **[[impact-palmares]]** holds the [[ministry-of-external-relations-cameroon|MINREX]] consular/biometric-visa contract (from 2022; **411,439 visas / FCFA 46.8bn to Sep 2025**) and, from Dec 2025, the upgrade of the **National Printing Press** — a *third* foreign identity/document vendor and a new document class. Meanwhile the CNI is becoming a spine for **sectoral IDs** increasingly run by private/municipal actors: a GIZ-backed **taxi digital ID** (Yaoundé, 2026) and an MTN-run **student ID**. On the land side, MINDCAF made a traditional-chief **non-objection letter mandatory** in land registration from 1 Jul 2025.

The through-line: a foreign-vendor-owned, multi-track ID stack (Augentic BOT, Liber CRVS, Impact Palmarès consular/printing) that is technically advanced but raises cost, capacity, exclusion and sovereignty questions. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[include.divides]], [[CMR]].
