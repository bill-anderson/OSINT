# Cameroon × Digital Identity

Cameroon is rebuilding identity on **two coordinated tracks** — a new foreign-run biometric ID card (DGSN/Augentic) and a legally-reformed digital civil registry (BUNEC/Law 2024/016) meant to converge on a single Unique Personal Identification Number. The ambition is real; the friction is cost, capacity and the politics of a 15-year foreign BOT dropped into an election year.

## What we know

**The biometric card (2025).** The **[[dgsn-cameroon]]** (national police) launched a new-generation biometric **CNI** under a **15-year build-operate-transfer contract with German firm [[augentic]]** (signed May 2024): e-chip, MRZ, QR, ten fingerprints; ICAO/ISO-IEC 19303; Cameroon the **first African adopter of the ID-1 portrait format**. Pre-enrolment opened 17 Feb 2025, with a **48-hour issuance** promise; issuance began March 2025 after a presidential decree. A **Far North rapid-enrolment** campaign (Aug 2025) targeted displaced/underserved populations. But opposition MPs criticised the system's **cost and complexity** (long biometric-capture waits) — pointed, since a valid CNI is required to register and vote before the **October 2025 presidential election**.

**The civil-registry reform (2024–26).** Parliament introduced a **new civil-registration law** (**[[cameroon-civil-registration-law-2024]]**, Law 2024/016) plus a data-protection bill (late 2024). The law brings electronic civil-status declaration, a national database, **digital birth/marriage/death certificates** and a **Unique Personal Identification Number (UPIN)**. **[[bunec]]** (with Luxembourg's **[[liber-technologies]]**) targets a **fully digital CRVS by 2029**, integrated with the biometric ID and voter kits. The first digital birth certificate issued in the North-West/South-West (Limbe I, Jan 2026, UNDP/Denmark) shows early delivery — against a backdrop of weak equipment, limited internet and low digital literacy.

The through-line: a foreign-vendor-owned ID stack (Augentic BOT, Liber CRVS) that is technically advanced but raises cost, capacity and sovereignty questions. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[CMR]].
