# Algeria × GovTech / e-Government

Algeria runs a **Presidency-steered** digitalisation drive (the **[[high-commission-digitalisation-algeria|High Commission for Digitalisation]]**, Decree 23-314) under **[[digital-algeria-2030]]**, targeting 100% of public services online by 2030.

- **Single window.** **[[dzair-services|Dzair Services]]** (Oct 2025) aggregates public services behind one access point on a national interoperability layer (46 ministries/agencies on fibre); the older **Bawabatak** portal already offers 342 services.
- **Registries.** **[[amlak|AMLAK]]** (Jan 2026) moves land titles fully electronic with end-to-end transaction traceability — the [[dpi.registry]] leg.
- **Identity.** The **[[algeria-digital-id-trust-law|digital-ID & trust-services law]]** (2025) links a national digital identity to the biometric ID card and gives e-signatures/seals legal force.
- **Sub-national.** Municipal digitalisation ("electronic municipalities") is an active reform strand ([[digital.localgov]]).
- **Infrastructure:** national data centres (El Mohammedia, Blida), a planned sovereign cloud; **67%** of procedures dematerialised by 2024 (foreign-trade clearance cut 15→3 days).
