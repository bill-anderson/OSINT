# Lesotho × AI, data policy & sovereign infrastructure

For a small, mountainous, hydropower-rich kingdom, 2026 crystallised an **AI-and-sovereignty** agenda:
a headline **$6.2bn US-backed AI-data-centre** bid, a trio of signed digital policies, a Geneva
"co-creator not consumer" pitch, and first AI-skills programmes — all against a **~50% real internet-usage
affordability gap** despite 100% broadband coverage.

## What we know

- **Project Kobong — $6.2bn AI data centre (2026-06).** [[infra.store]] [[infra.energy]] [[geopol.usa]]
  Lesotho and US firm **[[convalt-energy|Convalt Energy]]** agreed **[[project-kobong|Project Kobong]]** —
  ~$6.2bn (Lesotho's largest-ever FDI) pairing **1.2 GW hydropower** (+4.6 GW solar, 4 GWh BESS) with a
  green-powered **AI data centre in Mokhotlong** — energy security + regional export + AI hub, US exports
  possibly >$2bn, construction from 2029. **Legal status resolved (2026-07-20)**: Convalt's own release
  records a **binding Memorandum of Agreement signed 2026-06-04** (announced 2026-06-08) that is a
  **development framework only** — progression "remains subject to… feasibility studies, permitting,
  financing, regulatory approvals, and definitive project agreements". The ~$6.2bn is the **proposed
  project value, not committed capital**; no final investment decision as of 2026-07-20. Sources:
  [[2026-06-08-convalt-energy-lesotho-partnership-press-release]],
  [[2026-06-09-convalt-kobong-hydropower-ai-datacenter-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-06-30-lesotho-first-ai-data-center-connectingafrica]].
- **Three foundational digital policies signed (2026-06).** [[gov.policy]] [[gov.protect]] MICSTI Minister
  **Nthati Moorosi** signed the **Broadband Infrastructure Sharing Policy**, **Data Management Policy**
  (privacy/security/ethical-use framework) and **AI Policy** (responsible AI in health/education/public
  admin), and launched a **Digital Outreach Studio** (e-Government Infrastructure Phase II, AfDB-backed).
  See [[micsti-lesotho]]. Source: [[2026-06-12-three-digital-policies-signed-lestimes]].
- **Sovereignty pitch at Geneva (2026-07).** [[include.divides]] [[gov.regional]] MICSTI PS **Kanono
  Ramashamole** told the UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance (WSIS/AI for Good) that developing nations
  must be **"co-creators, not only consumers"** of AI — citing **100% broadband coverage but only ~50%
  regular internet use** (affordability barrier), fragmented data and weak governance; DPI + open-source
  as the readiness path. Source: [[2026-07-09-lesotho-equitable-ai-future-itweb]].
- **AI skills — US-backed (2026-06).** [[capacity.training]] An **AI Skills Development Programme** (with
  the US Embassy / American Corner) launched under PM Matekane's **[[sebabatso-initiative]]** — Lesotho's
  first digital-entrepreneurship + AI-tools training; the government has approved a **National AI Policy**
  and advanced an AI-ready data-management framework (8th African STI Forum). Source:
  [[2026-06-17-ai-skills-development-programme-publiceye]].
- **Private-sector capacity — cloud/cyber/sovereignty (2026-05).** [[infra.cybersec]] **[[dns-technologies|DNS
  Technologies]]** (Mauritius) and **[[computer-business-solutions|CBS]]** hosted an executive AI/cloud/
  cybersecurity workshop in Maseru (partners Palo Alto, Softlabs, BelCloud, Keepit) — ransomware, cloud
  security, data **sovereignty** and immutable backup as themes. Source:
  [[2026-05-30-dns-cbs-tech-workshop-lestimes]].
