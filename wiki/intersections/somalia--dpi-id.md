# Somalia × Digital Identity

After three decades without a functioning identity system, Somalia is building a
foundational digital ID from a near-zero base — ~85% of an estimated 19.7m population lack
any legal ID; fewer than 16% hold a government ID (2022 SIHBS). The [[snid]], issued by
[[nira-somalia]] since Sept 2023, is the intended backbone of the country's DPI.

## Architecture and rollout

- **Legal/institutional origin:** DADSOM (Law 41, Dec 2020) → NIRA (Identification and
  Registration Act, Mar 2023); identification and data-protection laws took effect 2023.
- **Credential:** unique lifetime **11-digit NIN** ("Tirsi Aqoonsi"), biometric
  (fingerprint 4-4-2 + facial recognition; iris planned), issued in three formats —
  physical card, [[eaqoonsi]] mobile ID, and QR-coded printable certificate. Verified via
  the HUBIYE platform.
- **Mandates driving adoption (2025-2026):** national ID made compulsory for **passport
  applications (1 Sep 2025)**, **bank-account opening** (CBS, 29 Jul 2025), **domestic
  travel (1 Jan 2026)** — enforced at **Aden Adde airport** for **inter-state travel** from
  Mar 2026 (Immigration/ICA + NIRA) — plus **driver's licences and vehicle registration**;
  PM Hamza's Apr-2025 directive requires all government and private-sector providers to
  integrate it. Border management also runs the US-backed **PISCES** biometric system.
  Source: [[2026-03-05-somalia-biometric-id-mandatory-inter-state-travel]].
- **DPI tool-set (2025).** NIRA launched three foundational systems — **HUBIYE** (consent-
  based verification, OTP), the **CDS** (Certificate Delivery System), and the **[[eaqoonsi]]**
  mobile ID (legally equivalent to the card) — **"entirely developed by Somali engineers at
  NIRA"** (DG Abdiwali Abdulle), backed by the **World Bank $75m Scaled-UP** project (biometric
  dedup, secure storage, credential issuance, grievance redress). Source:
  [[2025-05-08-somalia-dpi-tools-hubiye-eaqoonsi-cds]].
- **Mass registration:** pilot in Shangani/Boondheer from 18 Aug 2025; campaign launched
  16 Sep 2025 (ID Day). Targets 3.5m Mogadishu → Banadir → **15m nationwide by end-2029**.
  By late 2025 **>1m enrolled** with **>24 services integrated** (2nd national ID conference,
  Nov 2025); by **Apr 2026 ~2m registered** — but eAqoonsi shows **>50k downloads** only, and
  digital activation still requires an **in-person NIRA visit** first. Inclusion gaps (weak
  internet, literacy) "risk leaving women, the elderly and people with disabilities behind"
  (5A framework; SMS/USSD urged). Sources: [[2025-11-26-somalia-national-id-issuance-reaches-1m]],
  [[2026-04-24-somalia-eaqoonsi-app-dpi]].
- **Financing:** World Bank via [[somalia-spring-project]] (>$100m) and SCALED-UP; officials
  estimate **~$125m** more to complete the rollout (single-sourced, Jan 2026; funding
  sources unspecified).
- **Civil registration:** the [[somalia-crvs]] system (OpenCRVS; NADRA-built **CRMVS** handed
  over Nov 2024) is the vital-events layer beneath legal identity — NADRA cooperation dates to
  2018 under Pakistan's "Look Africa" policy (a **$10.5m grant** + technology transfer). Source:
  [[2025-01-14-somalia-civil-registration-overhaul]].

## Federal-state politics & the proclamation

The national ID has become a **federal-vs-member-state** fault line, not just a technical rollout:

- **Member states refuse.** **Puntland and Jubbaland** have declined to implement the federal ID;
  **Puntland created its own identification authority**, accusing Mogadishu of overreach on **data
  control, election influence and regional autonomy** — even as NIRA opens federal service centres
  (e.g. Boondheere, Apr 2025). Sources: [[2025-04-20-somalia-nira-id-center-boondheere]],
  [[2025-09-19-hassan-sheikh-10m-passports-id-overhaul]].
- **The "national crime" proclamation.** President Hassan Sheikh (Sep 2025) ordered every agency and
  business to use the NIRA ID over the passport, declaring it **"a national crime"** to bypass it or to
  serve someone without one — noting **10m passports** exist with no record of how many were legal. A
  legal analysis (*Politics of Proclamation*) argues this **exceeds Law No. 009/2023**, which gives
  citizens a **right** to an ID but imposes **no duty** to obtain one and criminalises only fraud/
  obstruction — "a right became an obligation ... without legislative debate." See [[gov.legislate]].
  Sources: [[2025-09-19-hassan-sheikh-10m-passports-id-overhaul]],
  [[2025-09-20-somalia-national-id-politics-of-proclamation]].

## Contested ground (lens: sovereignty)

- **Foreign build / data residency.** Pakistan's **NADRA** is the technology partner
  providing the system and hardware. NIRA's DG insists citizens' data is stored securely
  **within Somalia**; Garowe Online (2025) reports users and analysts alleging the system
  is **hosted in Pakistan and operated by foreign personnel** — an open data-residency
  contradiction (see digest). The FBI's CJIS issued an RFI (Jan 2025) to run Somalia's
  **police ABIS** at Aden Adde airport — a distinct, law-enforcement biometric system, but
  another instance of foreign operation of Somali biometric infrastructure.
- **Exclusion risk.** ~4m IDPs and large nomadic populations, missing breeder documents,
  and thin legal safeguards raise exclusion concerns (observers cite Kenya's Maisha Namba).
- **Election timing.** Rollout coincides with preparations for one-person-one-vote polls
  (2026); some fear conflation of the national ID and electoral card.
