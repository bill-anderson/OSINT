# Côte d'Ivoire × Digital identity & CRVS

Côte d'Ivoire has a coherent foundational-ID stack — **[[oneci|ONECI]]**'s biometric **RNPP** and **NNI**
feeding a growing set of services — but the national-press record adds a **counter-current the hub's
positive arc lacked**: while the *digital* layer advances, **on-time birth registration is falling**.

## The ONECI arc, filled in

- **CNI mobile app (Apr 2025).** ONECI launched a **myONECI+** application to digitise the national ID
  card — an earlier step in the same digitisation arc that later produced [[maposte|MaPoste]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-26-oneci-application-myoneci-plus-linfodrome]].
- **Identity as a KYC utility (2025).** CNI issuance gained an **e-stamp (timbre électronique)** online
  payment rail, and ONECI began **selling RNPP identity-authentication to commercial banks** — Société
  Générale CI the first — turning the register into a **KYC utility** for the financial sector. Sources:
  [[2025-10-15-cni-timbres-electroniques-en-ligne-oneci-linfodrome]],
  [[2025-11-17-societe-generale-oneci-authentification-fratmat]].
- **MaPoste super-app (Jan 2026).** With [[la-poste-cote-divoire]], the NNI/RNPP-backed digital-mailbox
  and CNI-home-delivery app; the second account adds that **digital post boxes become mandatory from
  1 Mar 2026** and CNI delivery runs through 200+ post offices. Sources:
  [[2026-01-19-maposte-app-oneci-la-poste-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-01-20-maposte-app-postal-digital-id-biometricupdate]].
- **International standing (May 2026).** ONECI presented Côte d'Ivoire's identification progress at
  **ID4Africa 2026**. Source: [[2026-05-13-id4africa-2026-oneci-identification-numerique-linfodrome]].

## The counter-current: on-time birth registration is falling

The 2024 civil-status statistical yearbook (UNICEF-funded, presented by the Interior Ministry) records
**on-time birth registration in marked decline: 66.8% (2021) → 56.6% (2023) → 55.4% (2024)** — hard,
dated evidence cutting against a uniformly positive ONECI/RNPP narrative. ONECI's response is **coverage
mechanics at the base**: local-language registration "speakers," community-leader mobilisation (Tonkpi),
and the Interior Ministry's own civil-registry-security push (CityWeb software rollout). Sources:
[[2025-07-01-annuaire-statistique-etat-civil-2024-fratmat]],
[[2026-06-09-oneci-renforce-capacites-speakers-langues-locales-linfodrome]],
[[2026-06-16-oneci-leaders-communautaires-tonkpi-linfodrome]],
[[2025-06-17-etat-civil-systeme-securise-sneci-fratmat]].

## Regional & foreign vectors

- **WURI (World Bank).** Côte d'Ivoire is a Phase-1 [[wuri-programme|WURI]] country (hosted by the
  Employment & Social Protection ministry, linked to CMU universal-health enrolment). A **social-
  evaluation study** (16 Feb–1 Mar 2025) across **10 sous-préfectures** was restituted to sub-prefects and
  community leaders (Yamoussoukro, Feb 2026) — mobile enrolment units, grievance mechanisms, anti-
  disinformation. Source: [[2026-02-27-projet-wuri-restitution-sous-prefets-linfodrome]].
- **Foreign digital-ID vectors.** Bahrain's **[[beyon-connect]]** signed a La Poste MoU for a
  **OneID/OneBox** proof-of-concept — a foreign digital-ID vector alongside the US and Gulf pushes
  elsewhere in the CIV stack. And **IOM's MIDAS** border-management system was deployed at the **Kalamon
  CIV–Burkina Faso crossing** (Japan-funded; DST + [[anssi-cote-divoire|ANSSI]] reviewed), digitising a
  paper border amid large-scale displacement from Burkina Faso. Sources:
  [[2025-11-27-beyon-connect-la-poste-mou-oneid-onebox-techafricanews]],
  [[2025-11-28-la-poste-beyon-connect-mou-itweb]],
  [[2026-07-06-iom-midas-border-management-biometricupdate]].
