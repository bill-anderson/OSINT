# Cameroon × AI

Cameroon has, over 2024–26, built a **full national-AI policy chain** — from consultation to a
2040 strategy — around the language of **digital sovereignty**. The wiki previously held a single
line ("Jul 2025 — govt taps ST Digital"); the documentary record re-dates the origin and separates
the roles.

## The policy chain

- **Origin — CONIA 25–26 June 2024** ([[conia]]): the National Consultations on AI, where Cameroon
  resolved to build a strategy. Six months on (**Jan 2025**) the strategy is still "under development,"
  with an early benchmark: the **AFD AI Investment Potential Index at 30/100**, level with Angola,
  Guinea, Ethiopia and Burkina Faso.
- **Author — Cabinet Danielle Moukouri** ([[cabinet-danielle-moukouri]]), a digital-law firm that
  **won the mandate on public tender** (Mar 2025). Not ST Digital: **[[st-digital]]** is the
  sovereign-cloud/infrastructure partner (Minpostel's 14 Jul 2025 visit to its Douala Tier-3 centre
  is the event behind the wiki's "taps ST Digital" line, and it carried an ST Digital × ANINF **Gabon**
  MoU of 9 Jul 2025).
- **Unveiling — CONIA 2, 7–8 July 2025, Yaoundé**, by Posts & Telecoms Minister Minette Libom Li Likeng
  ([[ministry-of-posts-and-telecommunications-cameroon]]). Three independent accounts corroborate the
  contents: a **2040 roadmap on seven pillars** — an **AI Authority** + **Presidential Council**;
  a government **Data Lake** + Open Data policy; a sovereign LLM **"GPT Cameroon"** for local languages;
  **15 solar-powered edge nodes**; **5 centres of excellence**, **4,000 trained/yr**, an **AI focal
  point per ministry**; a **Central Africa AI network** and a "Made in Cameroon" export label.
  Targets: **60,000 AI specialists by 2040 (40% women)**, **12,000 jobs**, **+0.8–1.2% of GDP**.
- **Newest state — Feb 2026:** a public-administration **AI-governance workshop with the ITU** (Cosmas
  Zavazava present), feeding recommendations into implementation — the wiki's prior SNIA snapshot was
  ~18 months stale. Benchmark of record: **ITU AI readiness 46.3/100, 30th of 42 African states**,
  below the 56 average. See [[snia-cameroon]] for the full pillar text and sources.

## Society, sectors and contestation

The strategy sits inside a live public debate the hub previously lacked:

- **Access framing** — opinion pieces argue AI must be *democratised* rather than captured
  ([[2025-06-07-lejour-democratise-artificial-intelligence]]); a post-Paris-summit survey asks what
  AI realistically offers **SMEs** ([[2025-02-18-leconomie-ai-applications-for-smes]]).
- **Electoral** — AI **entered the October 2025 presidential campaign** (synthetic content, messaging),
  an actor set the wiki should track ([[2025-10-08-lejour-ai-enters-2025-presidential-campaign]]).
- **Religious/civil contestation** — the **51st plenary of the Catholic Bishops' Conference (CENC)**
  voiced concern about AI's societal effects, an actor class absent from the wiki
  ([[2026-06-10-lejour-bishops-plenary-ai-concerns]]).
- **Sectoral deployment** — rail operator **Camrail** put AI at the centre of safety and logistics
  (DIBIE, Digitrack) ([[2025-11-24-leconomie-camrail-ai-rail-safety]]). *(A parallel MTN AI
  driver-behaviour/road-safety deployment is filed under the platform-economy thread.)*

The through-line is the SNIA's own tension: sovereignty and "responsible AI" as stated principles,
against foreign-run compute, a 30th/42 readiness base, and a contested political context.
