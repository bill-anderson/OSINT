# Sierra Leone × Data Centres & Data Sovereignty

Sierra Leone is building **geographic redundancy** into a digital state that until recently ran on "one
nerve centre" in flood/landslide-exposed Freetown — a commercial disaster-recovery site in Bo, and a
new **National Data Centre** for government information, both framed explicitly as **data sovereignty**.

## Orange Bo — commercial disaster recovery

- **€23m Bo backup data centre (Nov 2025).** **[[orange-sierra-leone]]** opened a Bo facility — an
  **identical replica** of its 2018 Freetown data centre, built for **disaster recovery** given
  Freetown's landslide (2017) and flooding exposure; President Bio attended. Sonatel Group CEO Brelotte
  Ba called it "a major step forward for **data sovereignty**." Concrete figures: **€23m** DC + a
  **$50m** nationwide network modernisation (now 4G/5G-ready); Orange holds **51% market share / 3.04m
  subscribers** (Dec 2024), **616 sites** (70% green-powered), extending the southern/eastern corridor
  (Makeni, Kono, Kenema). Bio pressed providers on cybersecurity duties. See [[infra.energy]],
  [[infra.cybersec]]. Sources: [[2025-11-29-orange-bo-data-centre-opening-statehouse]],
  [[2025-12-02-orange-bo-datacenter-figures-wearetech]].

## NatCA HQ — the National Data Centre

- **National Data Centre commissioned (Apr 2026).** President Bio commissioned the new **[[natca]]**
  headquarters (Southridge, Freetown — a 10-storey facility begun 2015), co-locating NatCA and MoCTI and
  housing the **National Data Centre** and Network Operations/Regulatory functions. Bio stressed it will
  **protect government information and preserve national dignity** — digital sovereignty — while
  acknowledging the rural–urban divide and high data costs (fibre now in **14 of 16 districts**). See
  [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2026-04-17-natca-hq-national-data-centre]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the sovereignty claim here is about **resilience and custody** — a second physical
  nerve centre and a state-owned National Data Centre so government data neither vanishes in a Freetown
  disaster nor sits solely in a commercial (Orange/Sonatel) facility. It is sovereignty as *continuity
  and control of location*, a step below the sovereign-cloud debates elsewhere but the right first one
  for a small, disaster-exposed state.
