# Zimbabwe × Digital Identity

Zimbabwe's identity story is the hub's "**architecture vs operation**" gap made concrete: a modernised
population registry and e-passports on one side; **1.5 million undocumented children** and a minister who
"was not aware" of a backlog on the other. The one unambiguous accomplishment is **mobile registration
reaching the hardest-to-reach**; the fastest-moving *new* thread is **sector biometric IDs for farmers**.

## The backlog the minister denied (May 2026)

- **Denial vs the record.** In the Senate, Home Affairs Minister **Kazembe Kazembe** said he "was not aware"
  of any backlog in passports, birth certificates and national IDs — hearing it "for the first time" —
  citing **e-passports** and the **[[zimbabwe-population-registry-system|ZPRS]]** and a goal of **online,
  real-time** applications, while conceding recent birth-certificate problems (funding/resources). The record
  he denied: a **300,000+ passport backlog (2022)**, ~150,000 (2021), ~80,000 (2023), ID-card backlogs
  concentrated in **rural provinces** (Matabeleland North, Masvingo, Midlands), and **~1.5m undocumented
  children** whom mobile registration has barely dented — children being **denied school enrolment** without
  birth certificates. The denial is itself the finding. See [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-14-minister-denies-id-backlog]].

## The accomplished fact: mobile registration reaches Binga (Jul 2025)

- **Identity brought home.** The **[[civil-registry-department-zimbabwe|Civil Registry Department]]**, with
  **[[unicef]]** and the **Embassy of Sweden**, ran a mobile birth-registration/national-ID drive in
  **Binga** (Tshunga-lunga), issuing IDs, birth and death certificates to villagers previously excluded —
  against national **birth-registration coverage of 57% (ZDHS 2023–24)**. A named-paper primary corroborating
  the hub's mobileidworld account; the ministry (secretary Raphael Faranisi) framed it as "leaving no one and
  no place behind." See [[digital.rural]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-14-bringing-identity-home-mobile-registration-binga]].

## The new thread: sector biometric IDs for farmers (Oct 2025)

- **Cotton follows tobacco.** The **[[agricultural-marketing-authority-zimbabwe|AMA]]** was cleared to build a
  **cotton-farmer biometric ID registry** (field teams from 15 Oct 2025) to enforce **"one farmer, one
  merchant"** and end side-marketing — explicitly emulating the **[[tobacco-industry-and-marketing-board|TIMB]]**
  tobacco-farmer registry. Sector-specific biometric identity as a **market-integrity and anti-corruption**
  instrument (Deputy Minister Haritatos: "the bedrock of a new era of transparency") — a functional digital-ID
  layer growing sector by sector, ahead of the stalled national one. See [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.rural]].
  Source: [[2025-10-10-nigeria-zimbabwe-farmer-digital-id]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Zimbabwe's identity build is running on **three tracks that don't yet meet**. The **national**
  digital ID is stuck at programme-status (hub: announced July 2025, no launch, no coverage figure). The
  **sectoral** biometric registries (tobacco, now cotton) are actually *shipping* — because they serve a
  concrete state interest (market control, revenue), not a citizen-inclusion one. And the **inclusion** work
  (Binga mobile registration) is real but donor-funded (UNICEF/Sweden) and barely scratches 1.5m undocumented
  children. The order in which these move — market-control IDs first, citizen IDs last — is the political
  economy of Zimbabwean identity in one line.
