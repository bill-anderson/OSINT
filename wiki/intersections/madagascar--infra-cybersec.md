# Madagascar × Cybersecurity

As Madagascar's digital footprint (and its biometric-ID database) grows, it is building a cybersecurity
regime from a **thin 2014-law base** — a national strategy plus a full legislative overhaul, driven by the
**[[cirt-madagascar|CIRT]]** and financed through **[[prodigy-madagascar|PRODIGY]]**.

## What we know

- **National cybersecurity strategy (2025-12).** [[gov.policy]] [[sovereignty]] The Ministry launched a
  **multisectoral national cybersecurity strategy** (via PRODIGY) — child protection, GBV, justice,
  forensic cooperation — with a three-month delivery target, youth cyber-skills training and a planned
  donor conference; MPs flagged the **2014 cyber law** as needing an update. Source:
  [[2025-12-15-cybersecurite-strategie-nationale-midi]].
- **2014-law overhaul → cybercrime bill adopted (2026-06/07).** [[gov.legislate]] At the **first
  Cybersecurity Symposium** (Antananarivo, 22–23 Jun) the Ministry previewed a new framework defining a
  **security incident** and **Critical Infrastructure Organisations (OIVs)** with strict injunctions/
  sanctions; the **Council of Ministers adopted the overhauling cybercrime bill on 8 Jul 2026** — three
  axes: protecting the vulnerable (children on social media), ending digital impunity (revised e-evidence,
  connection-data retention, suspect-asset freezing), and **mandatory operator cooperation with justice**
  (telcos, ISPs, digital-finance operators). Context: INTERPOL (2025) — cybercrime is **30% of reported
  offences** in West/East Africa. Sources:
  [[2026-06-25-madagascar-new-cybersecurity-law-wearetech]],
  [[2026-07-09-cybercriminalite-projet-loi-adopte-agenceecofin]].
