# The Gambia × Digital Payments

The Gambia built its digital-payments layer in 2025-26 around a **domestically-owned, open-source
national switch**: **[[gamswitch|BANTABA 2.0]]**, a Mojaloop-based Inclusive Instant Payment System that
lets money move in real time across banks, mobile money, MFIs and fintechs. On top, the government
digitized its own money flows — **[[yonna-wallet]]** for collecting all government-service fees (incl.
diaspora), and GRA **e-invoicing** for tax — pursuing a low-cost, sovereign DPI model in a small market.

## What we know

- **[[GMB]] BANTABA 2.0 instant payment switch (2025-12-15).** [[sovereignty]] The Central Bank +
  Ministry of Communications launched the country's **first Inclusive Instant Payment System** on
  **[[mojaloop]]**, operated by **[[gamswitch]]** (with IIDiA and the Mojaloop Foundation) — real-time
  clearing/settlement across all provider types, framed as "foundational DPI" with domestic ownership.
  Source: [[2025-12-15 Gambia launches BANTABA 2.0, first real-time interoperable payment system]].
- **[[GMB]] Yonna Wallet — government payments (2026-03-02).** [[dpi.govtech]] Yonna became the first
  Gambian fintech authorized to collect **all government-service payments** (ID cards, passports,
  business registration) via the Accountant General, with diaspora payment via the Yonna Global Wallet
  and auto-generated traceable receipts. Source: [[2026-03-02 Gambia Gov't payments now digitalised live on Yonna Wallet app]].
- **[[GMB]] GRA digital tax-invoicing (2025-07-09).** [[include.access]] The Gambia Revenue Authority
  launched e-invoicing (Avatar Technologies) with tamper-proof digital signatures to cut under-declaration.
  Source: [[2025-07-09 Gambia Gov't launches Digital Tax Invoicing System (GRA)]].
