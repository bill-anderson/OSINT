# Algeria × Connectivity

Algeria's connectivity build-out is **state-led and sovereignty-framed** (Presidency-directed, run
through the **[[ministry-of-post-and-telecommunications-algeria|Ministry of Post & Telecommunications]]**
under Minister **[[sid-ali-zerrouki|Sid Ali Zerrouki]]** and the regulator **[[arpce-algeria|ARPCE]]**),
built on the incumbent **[[algerie-telecom]]** for fixed/backbone and three mobile operators —
**[[mobilis]]** (state), **[[djezzy]]** and **[[ooredoo-algeria]]** — for 5G.

## Fibre & fixed ("All Fiber" to 2027)

- **3 million FTTH households (2026-02)** — the ministry claims **1st in Africa** on FTTH households
  (ahead of South Africa, Egypt, Mauritius, Côte d'Ivoire, Kenya). Part of the **"All Fiber"** strategy to
  **replace all legacy copper with fibre by end-2026 / early-2027 at the latest** (Zerrouki, 2026-05).
- **Fixed base (2026-05):** **>7m fixed-internet subscribers**, of which **>3.4m on fibre**; **>51m mobile
  subscribers**. Universal-service project for low-density zones reached **1,400 areas** in phase 1.
- **Backbone:** Algérie Télécom–**[[huawei]]** nationwide **400G WDM** optical network (2025-02); **WiFi 7**
  rollout at the operator's 22-year milestone (2025-05); **345 new 4G base stations** across 44 provinces
  (2026-02, toward 4,500 localities by 2027); **IPv6** generalisation launched (2026-07).

## 5G (three licences, $492m, 2025)

- **ARPCE opened the 5G tender** (announced 2025-05, launched 2025-06) for **three licences**; **Ooredoo**
  ranked 2nd and secured a provisional licence (2025-07).
- **Awards confirmed Dec 2025: $492m (DZD 63.9bn)** to **Mobilis, Djezzy, Ooredoo** — one of North Africa's
  largest telecom investments of the decade. A **six-year plan** starts with **eight pilot wilayas**,
  nationwide by **2031**; government projects the digital ecosystem adds **$9bn → $13bn to GDP by 2030**.
  Mobilis Feb-2025 trials reached **1.2 Gbps**. An **[[ericsson]]** study warns full deployment could cost
  **$3–8bn** (plus 20–35% to reach beyond profitable urban areas — a rural-gap risk).
- **Rollout raced ahead:** **Djezzy** live in **18 states** (2026-03); **Ooredoo** across **all wilayas**
  (2026-03, ahead of schedule). Ookla ranked Algiers **48th globally** for mobile speed (**163.31 Mbps**, up
  68 places, 2026-05) and flagged best-5G-network performance (2026-04).

## Satellite & subsea

- **Satellite ISPs:** ARPCE launched a tender for **two satellite-ISP (NGSO) licences** (2026-04) to reach
  remote/isolated areas and support agriculture/mining/transport IoT — a digital-divide play.
- **Subsea:** Algérie Télécom–**[[sparkle]]** MoU for a **new Italy–Algeria submarine cable**
  ([[algerie-telecom-sparkle-cable]], 2025-07), plus an Algérie Télécom **PoP in Europe**, data-centre
  support and a cloud/cybersecurity **training platform**.

## Operators

- **[[djezzy]]** — Qvantel managed-services hub (2026-03); **INATEL** industrial/sovereignty partnership
  (2026-04). **[[ooredoo-algeria]]** — Sétif **"Ganzat" smart-city** 5G+IoT pilot (2026-02); a run of
  strategy/5G-AI interviews (2025). **eSIM** adoption move (2025-10).
