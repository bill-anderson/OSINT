# Uganda × Data Centres & Cloud

Uganda's compute story is told almost entirely in the **future tense and the sovereign register**. Four
of the five projects here are **announcements, plans or launches of ecosystems rather than facilities**,
and nearly every one is sold on **data sovereignty**. The one hard datum — a **10MW sovereign
supercomputer at a hydropower dam** — is **single-sourced on cost and does not yet exist**. Read this
page as a pipeline, not an inventory.

## Aeonian / USIO at Karuma — planned, not built

At the **[[karuma-hydropower-plant]]** on the Nile, the **[[aeonian-project]]** —
**[[synectics-technologies]]** with **[[schneider-electric]]** — plans **USIO, a 10MW sovereign
supercomputer** with **[[nvidia]]** (Blackwell). The design case:

- **100MW of surplus pre-transmission power** from the **600MW** plant
- **Nile-water cooling**
- A **2,500km fibre backbone** to submarine cables via **Kenya and Tanzania**
- **Rollout begins 2026, full capacity 2028**
- Cost **$1.2 billion** — *single-sourced to Watchdog Uganda*

**Status: planned / under development as of 2025-09-15 — it does not exist.** The sovereignty argument
here is unusually literal: stranded generation at the dam, cooled by the river, is compute that never
has to leave the country or the grid.

**The "first" claim is contested and none is adopted.** *Three clips carry three different superlatives
for the same facility*, against **[[cassava-technologies]]'** competing claim to the "first AI factory
in Africa". Sources:
[[2026-01-28 Uganda to Launch Africa's First Home-Grown AI Supercomputer Center at Karuma Hydropower Plant]],
[[2025-09-15 Synectics, Schneider to build AI center in Uganda starting 2026]],
[[2025-09-11 Another 'first AI factory in Africa' - Uganda is said to have made its entry into the AI race]].
See [[tech.ai]], [[infra.capacity]].

## ABQ Cloud — a sovereign cloud launched inside someone else's data centre (2026-03-02)

**[[abq-cloud]]** launched what it calls Uganda's **"indigenous sovereign cloud ecosystem"** at the Deep
Tech Hub and the **[[raxio-group]] Data Centre, Namanve**, officiated by Science, Technology and
Innovation Minister **Monica Musenero Masanza**, with **[[sti-op-uganda]]**, Roke Cloud and AfriQloud.

Sold on **local-currency pricing, reduced latency and data sovereignty**; CTO **Eric Mugerwa** called
it *"a foundation for Uganda's digital independence."* **Note what is and isn't sovereign: the
ecosystem is Ugandan; the building is Raxio's.** Source:
[[2026-03-02 ABQ Cloud launches Ugandan sovereign cloud ecosystem]]. See [[sovereignty]].

## Centenary's green data centre — the date slipped, quietly

**[[centenary-group-data-centre]]** (Masaka), described as **Uganda's first Green Tier 3**, is to host
**critical government systems**, use **40% less power**, and run open-access to all ISPs.

- **2025-08-11** — the same publisher reported it **"on schedule for completion in late 2025"**
- **2025-10-03** — **official launch rebased to Q1 2026**

**The October piece calls the rebased schedule "on schedule" without acknowledging the earlier
commitment.** That is the finding: the slip is visible only across two clips from one publisher.
Sources: [[2025-10-03 Centenary Group set to launch its Green Tier 3 Data Centre in 2026]],
[[2025-08-11 Centenary Group affirms data centre will be ready by late 2025]].

## Liquid's Azure Stack, and the market around it (2024-08-12)

**[[cassava-technologies]]** subsidiary **Liquid** launched a **Microsoft Azure Stack** in Uganda —
local Azure **while meeting local data regulatory requirements**. **The housing data centre was not
disclosed.** Framed as supporting the **Digital Transformation Roadmap** goal to **connect 90% of
households by 2040**.

The surrounding market as that account describes it **(as of August 2024)**: **[[africa-data-centres]]
has no Uganda facility**; **[[raxio-group]]** operates colocation outside Kampala; and **MTN claims six
data centres in Uganda with capacity for 972 racks (as of August 2024)** — *operator claim; attribute*.

*Provenance note: DCD renders a bogus "14 Jul 2026" header on this piece; the true byline is
2024-08-12.* Source: [[2024-08-12 Liquid Intelligent Technologies launches Azure Stack in Uganda]]. See
[[sovereignty]].

## Nxtra — a projection, not a commitment

**Kampala is named as a future [[nxtra-africa]] Edge site (as of 2024-12-13)**. **This is a projection,
not a commitment** — no facility, date or capacity is attached. Source:
[[2024-12-13 Nxtra - Airtel's leap into Africa's growing data center market]].
