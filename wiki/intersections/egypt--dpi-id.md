# Egypt × Digital Identity

Egypt is layering **multiple biometric digital-identity channels** on top of its long-standing national ID card — split across the central bank, the interior ministry, MCIT and licensed private providers.

- **Haweya (CBE, Nov 2025).** [[haweya]] — a biometric (face + fingerprint) **digital financial ID / eKYC** app for remote bank-account opening (37 banks), SIM purchase and services; the core of Egypt's national digital financial identity, driving financial inclusion. See [[central-bank-of-egypt]], [[dpi.pay]].
- **MOIEG-PASS (Interior + MCIT).** [[moieg-pass]] — described as Egypt's **first unified biometric authentication platform** (scan national ID + face) for government services; launched Feb 2026, **selfie-biometrics pilot** widening on Android (iOS to follow, 2026-03), aligned to the ICT 2030 Strategy.
- **MCIT national digital ID (approaching, 2026-05).** A distinct **e-KYC + wider e-signature** system (face biometrics linked to civil-registry) under "Digital Egypt" for fully remote services — separate from CBE Haweya.
- **Private track — Digified.** [[digified]] — FRA-licensed **eKYC / e-signature** for non-bank financials (25m+ verifications/yr).
- **Civil-registry kiosks (2024).** Automated mall-based machines issue certificates and national-ID forms via fingerprint/face.
- **Child biometric ID (proposal).** A parliamentary proposal to issue a **biometric national-ID card for children from age 5** (amending Child Law 12/1996) — a unified child registry linked to health/education/insurance; data-protection concerns raised. See [[dpi.registry]].

Governed by the **[[egypt-pdpl|PDPL]]** regime (biometric processing, cross-border-transfer licensing). See [[EGY]], [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]], [[egypt--gov-protect]].
