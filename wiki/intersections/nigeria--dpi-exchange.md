# Nigeria — Data Exchange & Interoperability

Nigeria's identity and service data sit in **eight largely siloed government databases** (NIN,
BVN, NCC, NIS, FIRS, FRSC, CAC, INEC), forcing citizens to re-present the same details and
biometrics repeatedly and blocking the interoperability an AI/DPI economy needs. The remedy is a
central exchange backbone plus enterprise architecture, led by **[[nitda]]**.

## Developments

- **2025-08-25 — NGDX announced.** [[nitda]] unveiled the **[[ngdx]]** (Nigeria Data Exchange) at
  an Abuja stakeholders' workshop with **[[european-union]]**, Finland and Estonia — a centralised
  platform for authorised institutions to verify and share records on the back end, cutting KYC
  cost/time for fintechs and speeding service delivery. DG **[[kashifu-inuwa]]** called it a
  "unified backbone for identity and data verification"; Minister **[[bosun-tijani]]** stressed
  privacy and inclusive growth. Targeted operational by **end 2025**. Sources:
  [[2025-08-30 Nigeria to launch NGDX, unified data exchange, by end of 2025 (NITDA)]],
  [[2025-08-28 Nigeria to launch National Data Exchange (NGDX) platform to boost digital economy]].
- **2026-06-17 — Silos flagged as an AI risk.** At AI Summit Nigeria, experts warned the eight
  non-interoperable databases threaten Nigeria's AI ambitions; NITDA is building data-exchange
  frameworks via the National Cloud Policy. (See the hub.) Relates to the **[[ngea]]** enterprise
  architecture (NITDA/KOICA/[[galaxy-backbone]]).
- **DPI for tax administration (2025).** An ICTD analysis maps Nigeria's DPI-for-tax stack (digital
  IDs, merchant payments, data exchange) and finds the open question at subnational level is *which*
  system anchors the DPI **Data Exchange Architecture** — with NIN–revenue-system integration and
  e-Tax automation improving but inconsistent across states. Source:
  [[2025-07-01 Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) and Tax Administration in Nigeria The Current State of Play - ICTD]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: an exchange layer is where foundational ID becomes usable statecraft — but
  interoperability concentrates personal data, so it rides on the same [[gov.protect]] guarantees
  Nigeria's enforcement record ([[nigeria--gov-protect]]) has yet to prove. **[[ninauth]]** consent
  and the Data Protection Act 2023 are the assurance layer NGDX depends on.
