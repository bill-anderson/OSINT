# Libya × Cybersecurity

Libya's cyber governance is **fragmented along the political split**: an eastern legislative track drafts a
cybersecurity law while the GNU side runs the technical institutions (**[[gaci-libya|GACI]]** root
servers/IPv6) and absorbs live incidents — against a rapidly digitising payments base with a
**data-protection legislative vacuum**.

## What we know

- **Cybersecurity law drafting — east (2026-07).** [[gov.legislate]] [[sovereignty]] An eastern **House of
  Representatives** committee (est. by Speaker's decision 115/2025, chaired by MP Asma al-Khoja, with the
  HoR-designated communications minister) met the **National Security Council in Benghazi** to draft a
  **comprehensive cybersecurity law** — an east-side legislative initiative paralleling the GNU/Tripoli
  AI-strategy track. Source: [[2026-07-16-parliament-national-security-cybersecurity-law-alwasat]].
- **CBL cyber incident (2026-06).** [[dpi.pay]] The **[[central-bank-of-libya|CBL]]** disclosed a **cyber
  incident** affecting some technical systems; it isolated suspect systems and investigated with global
  support firms. Card services and **LYPAY** continued; no confirmed impact on customer accounts. A live
  test of the fast-growing payments backbone. Source: [[2026-06-24-cbl-cyber-incident-alwasat]] *(date
  proxied ~2026-06-24)*.
- **Cybercrime enforcement (2025 Q1).** [[gov.protect]] The Interior Ministry (GNU) handled **135
  cybercrime cases** in Q1-2025 (104 social-media, 14 mobile-phone with 22 phones seized, 8 CCTV) and
  **188 devices seized**, targeting online criminal networks, digital fraud and extortion. Source:
  [[2025-04-06-interior-cybercrime-cases-q1-2025-alwasat]] *(date proxied ~2025-04)*.
