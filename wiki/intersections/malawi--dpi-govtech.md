# Malawi × E-Government & Data Exchange

Malawi has built the *scaffolding* of a digital state — a National Data Centre, the **Boma Lathu** single
services platform, an Enterprise Service Bus, a data-protection law — under the World Bank-funded **[[dmap]]**.
The 2025–2026 story is the gap between that architecture and working service delivery, and the push to close it
with binding rules, mandatory systems and (proposed) Chinese finance.

## The data-exchange layer — Boma Lathu + a binding regulation

- **Government Data Sharing Regulation (2026-06).** Malawi proposed a **Government Data Sharing Regulation** to
  govern the **National Data Exchange Platform (Boma Lathu)** — the "missing piece," since MDAs still shared
  citizen data by email, USB and paper. It routes all data requests securely through the **National Data
  Centre**, mandates **encryption** in transit and at rest, sets a **common digital standard** for
  system-to-system exchange, and requires binding **Data Sharing Agreements** with usage limits and full **audit
  trails** — the operational safeguards atop the **[[malawi-data-protection-act-2024]]**. The National Data
  Centre is being upgraded to an **AI-ready** state. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[infra.store]], [[gov.legislate]].
  Source: [[2026-06-11-government-data-sharing-regulation-boma-lathu-itweb]].

## Mandatory e-procurement — MANePS

- **MANePS goes mandatory (2026-04-01).** The **[[ppda-malawi|PPDA]]** switched public procurement to the
  **Malawi National Electronic Procurement System (MANePS)** — mandatory for **all** public entities under a
  March-2026 government directive ("we are no longer going to use paper"), advancing the earlier World Bank
  Fiscal-Governance eGP work into a nationwide system for transparency and value-for-money (Malawi 2063). See
  [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-04-01-ppda-mandatory-e-procurement-maneps-malawi24]].

## The implementation gap (2025 review → 2026 enforcement)

- **2025 — ambition undermined by execution.** A year-in-review catalogued the failures: the **passport saga**
  (a **$29.9m** Madras Security Printers contract, 100,000+ backlog); a **[[macra-malawi|MACRA]]**
  misinformation-tracking system that drew mass-surveillance/free-expression criticism; **6,500** MEC electoral
  devices with rural offline glitches (16 Sep 2025 elections); and **e-government paralysis** from a
  non-operational **Enterprise Service Bus** leaving services siloed; forex shortages throttled 5G hardware.
  Bright spots: **[[dmap]]** fibre to **500+** government buildings, the **National e-Payment Gateway**
  (FInES/World Bank), and the **Malawi National Single Window** (May 2025, integrating MRA + Trade). See
  [[MWI]], [[dpi.id]]. Source: [[2026-01-13-ict-sector-2025-implementation-woes-times]].
- **2026 — "from a document on a shelf to a lived reality."** [[ictam|ICTAM]] voices (Bram Fudzulani, Clarence
  Gama) pressed the Department of E-Government to onboard all MDAs onto the **ESB**, make the **Digitalisation
  Policy** operational (online driver's-licence renewals, national-ID applications and payments by mid-2026),
  **zero-rate** traffic to government portals to drive uptake, tie PS appraisals to the policy, and cut
  VAT/duties on entry-level smartphones. See [[gov.policy]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27-ict-sector-eyes-policy-enforcement-times]].

## Extending the state — local government and Chinese finance

- **First ICT policy for Local Authorities (2026-05).** The **NLGFC** with the Department of E-Government rolled
  out Malawi's **first dedicated Local Authorities ICT policy** (validated 4 May 2026) plus ICT standards —
  councils had operated with no guiding framework beyond the 2023 National Digitalization Policy (a 2022 GESP
  situational analysis found weak support, absent projects and ICT excluded from procurement). See
  [[digital.localgov]], [[gov.standards]]. Source: [[2026-05-20-local-authorities-ict-policy-dowa-malawi24]].
- **Proposed US$180m Huawei ICT plan (2026-06).** In China, ICT Minister **Shadric Namalomba** presented a
  four-year **US$180m** roadmap with **[[huawei]]**: a **one-stop digital-government portal** (passports,
  business registration, tax) and a **28-district high-speed ring-protected network** to power Boma Lathu — a
  `sovereignty`/`geopol.china` dependency the funding terms of which are not yet public. See [[geopol.china]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-06-09-huawei-180m-ict-investment-plan-malawi24]].
