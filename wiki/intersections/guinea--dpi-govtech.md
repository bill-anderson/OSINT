# Guinea × GovTech / e-Government

Guinea is standing up an e-government layer under **[[simandou-2040]]**, delivered through a **merged
[[ministry-digital-economy-guinea|MCENI]] ministry** (Feb 2026) and the state digitalisation agency
**[[ande-guinea|ANDE]]**.

- **The nine-project package (2026-05).** Public-admin modernisation via the **One-Stop Shop for Public
  Services (GUSP)**, HR-system modernisation, a rehabilitated **Gouv-Lab**, e-Tax, IT-system mapping/inventory,
  digital-skills training and the **[[fugas|FUGAS]]** payroll.
- **One-stop shop — ASAN model.** A **Guinea–Azerbaijan** partnership (Mar 2026) to build the **Guichet Unique
  des Services Publics** on Azerbaijan's **ASAN** model.
- **E-procurement — TELEMO** ([[telemo]], 22 May 2026; Guinea–Rwanda) digitising the full procurement
  cycle (procurement = 11–15% of GDP).
- **Foundations.** A **GovStack workshop** under the World Bank **WARDIP** project (interoperability base);
  the **Journal Officiel went digital** (free online legal gazette, 1,100+ journals / 6,000+ texts); the
  **CNSS launched eCNSS** (online social contributions, ~60% of receipts); **LANDAYA** e-permits (industrial
  licences, 72h; successor to PLAGED); and a **national vehicle-attestation platform** (won by Ivorian startups
  SIIN + DIGITECH — South-South govtech transfer).
