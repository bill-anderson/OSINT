# Tunisia × Digital Payments & Fintech

Tunisia's payments story is **identity converting into volume**: the **[[e-houwiya]]** mobile ID wired
into the **[[paysmart]]** rail drove mobile payments **+81% in 2025**. The **[[central-bank-of-tunisia|CBT]]**
is now standardising the market (a national **TUNPAY** label) as telcos turn into fintechs and a Gulf
player enters.

## The surge (2025)

Mobile payments rose **+81% to 8.4m transactions / TND 1.769bn (~$616m)** in 2025 (CBT); active wallets
**815,000** (from 595k in 2024), licensed PSPs **435** (from 368) — the stated enabler being the
**[[e-houwiya]]–[[paysmart]] integration** (identity infrastructure into payment volume), plus **ISO
20022 (SWIFT MX)** adoption (see [[TUN]] hub). *(CBT figures reach the wiki via aggregators — GAP-TUN-001.)*

## TUNPAY — a national label

- **Unifying the ecosystem (May 2026).** The CBT, with **[[smt-tunisia|Société Monétique Tunisie]]**,
  launched **TUNPAY** — a **national label** that banks, payment institutions and the postal service must
  integrate into their wallet apps, interfaces and acceptance points (merchants/agents too) — to
  **harmonise identification**, build user confidence, improve interoperability and simplify UX. See
  [[gov.standards]]. Source: [[2026-05-07-wearetech-central-bank-tunpay-label]].

## Telcos become fintechs

- **Kashy — Tunisie Telecom (Mar 2026).** **[[tunisie-telecom]]** launched the **Kashy** wallet with
  **Viamobile** (BCT-licensed since Apr 2021) — transfers, collections, top-up and **cash withdrawal by
  phone**, phone-number as user ID, rolling out via ~30 TT commercial spaces nationwide. TT frames it as
  moving **from telco to integrated digital player** in the national cash-reduction strategy. Source:
  [[2026-03-18-assabah-tunisie-telecom-kashy-wallet]].
- **walletii — Ooredoo Fintech (Mar 2026).** **Ooredoo Fintech** won a CBT licence for **walletii**, with
  **QNB Group** and **[[smt-tunisia|SMT]]** — bills, P2P, cash-out over a **wide agent network** targeting
  the **underbanked/unbanked**. A Gulf-group fintech entry into the Tunisian market. See [[geopol.gulf]],
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-03-07-menastartupdigest-ooredoo-walletii-license]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Tunisia's payments design is unusually **state-standardised and identity-anchored** — a
  national label (TUNPAY), a national e-payments operator (SMT), and growth driven by the sovereign
  identity layer (e-Houwiya) rather than by foreign wallets. The telco pivot (Kashy) and the Gulf entry
  (Ooredoo/QNB) widen the market, but the rails and the brand stay national — a coherent, controlled
  digital-finance build atop a mature ID.
