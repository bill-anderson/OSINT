# Tunisia × Digital Identity & Registries

Tunisia's foundational ID, **[[e-houwiya]]** (Mobile ID, live since Aug 2022, legally equivalent to
face-to-face identification), is crossing from *available* to **mandatory** — the gateway to more and more
public procedures — while separate sectoral identifiers (health, business, civil registration) go digital
in parallel. The through-line is compulsion arriving before the 123-article data-protection bill that
would govern it (see [[tunisia--gov-protect|the DP bill]], [[TUN]] hub, "capability ahead of safeguards").

## Mobile ID becomes a gateway (from 1 July 2026)

- **Investment declarations (TIA).** The **[[tunisia-investment-authority|Tunisia Investment Authority]]**
  made **[[e-houwiya|Mobile ID]] mandatory for all investment-project declarations** from 1 July 2026 —
  investors must hold a digital identity before accessing investment services. Source:
  [[2026-06-26-techafricanews-mobile-id-mandatory-investors]].
- **Business register (RNE).** From the same date the **[[national-business-register-tunisia|National
  Business Register]]** goes **fully digital** — paper filing discontinued, the whole creation chain (name
  → tax ID → RNE extract) online with no physical travel — and **access requires a digital identity** for
  individuals, companies and regulated professions (lawyers, accountants). Electronic documents carry the
  **same legal value** as paper. Online tax-ID-card issuance targeted end-Sept 2026. Under the Finance
  Minister's decree of 12 Feb 2026. Source: [[2026-04-27-africanmanager-national-business-register-digital]].

Two independent public-service rails switching to compulsory digital ID on the **same 1 July 2026 date** —
a coordinated hardening of e-Houwiya into the default key for administrative and economic life.

## Sectoral identifiers and CRVS

- **National Health Identifier (Jul 2026).** The **[[ministry-of-health-tunisia|Ministry of Health]]**
  piloted an **NHI** — a unique lifetime code per citizen threading medical history across providers —
  from Habib Thameur University Hospital (Tunis), using the national ID card at registration; **9,000+
  patients** enrolled in the first weeks, nationwide by end-2026 targeted. See [[dpi.mis]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-15-tmo-national-health-identifier-pilot]].
- **Digital birth declaration (Jun 2026).** The Ministry of Health began a **first pilot of an electronic
  birth-declaration** system — a CRVS digitisation step feeding the identifier stack. *(Body not captured —
  headline/date only.)* Source: [[2026-06-11-alchourouk-digital-birth-declaration-pilot]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: this is the sharp edge of Tunisia's "**capability ahead of safeguards**" pattern. A mature
  sovereign ID (e-Houwiya) is genuinely impressive — but 2026 is the year it becomes **compulsory** to
  transact with the state (invest, register a company) and to be treated in hospital (NHI on the ID card),
  while the law meant to govern that data is *still a bill in parliament*. Mandatory identity plus a
  centralising health identifier, ahead of an enforced modern data-protection regime, is exactly the
  sequence a rights-minded reader watches: the rails are sovereign and well-built; the guardrails lag.
