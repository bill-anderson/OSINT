# Namibia × Data Centres & Sovereign Cloud

Namibia wants to **host its own data** — a **National/Public Data Centre** as a pillar of technological
sovereignty — and, more ambitiously, to become a **green-powered AI-compute host** for the region.

## The sovereign-data-centre push

- **National/Public Data Centre (cabinet, 2025-09).** Cabinet directed MICT and the Office of the Prime Minister
  to **finalise the concept note and project proposal** for a **National and Public Data Centre** — hosting
  national data locally as "technological sovereignty" — alongside a **Windhoek smart-city legal framework**
  (with Urban & Rural Development) and a **US$20m ICT venture-capital fund** for SME founders (female founders
  engaged). Cabinet also applauded MTC's **5G** launch (Aug 2025, Windhoek/Swakopmund/Walvis Bay). It is a
  pillar of the 2025-2029 digital strategy; ICT minister **[[emma-theofelus]]** later said Angola's Luanda
  sovereign DC had "encouraged" Namibia's own. Sources:
  [[2025-09-23-namibia-expedites-data-centre-smart-city-development-itweb]],
  [[2025-09-22 Namibia to build national data centre under 2025-2029 digital strategy]],
  [[2026-06-15 Namibia encouraged by Angola on datacentre establishment]].
- **Huawei's AI-ready national data centre (2026-07).** Under the **[[china-namibia-smart-city|¥98m smart-city
  pilot]]**, **[[huawei]]** is building smart-city solutions and an **AI-ready national data centre** with MHAISS
  and MICT; President **[[netumbo-nandi-ndaitwah]]** pressed Huawei (Shenzhen) to create **AI jobs** and skills,
  while Huawei's Leo Chen pitched "strengthening data sovereignty." See [[geopol.china]], [[tech.ai]],
  [[capacity.training]]. Sources: [[2026-07-08-nandi-ndaitwah-pushes-ai-jobs-huawei-talks-namibian]],
  [[2026-07-08 China funds Namibia digital infrastructure]].

## The green-AI-host thesis

- **"Grounding the cloud" (2026-02, analysis).** A widely-argued case (Peter Karon, Green Enterprise Solutions)
  positions Namibia as an **"African digital sovereign"** and ideal **AI-data-centre host**: **3,000+ hours** of
  sunshine and some of Earth's highest solar irradiation (a panel there ~2× a Central-European one), the **3GW
  green-hydrogen/solar** facility near Walvis Bay, and the **Equiano** landing — with cheap coastal ground
  cooling beating "orbital compute" (launching 1GW of space cooling could cost >US$50bn). AI workloads use ~**10×**
  the energy of a standard search, making cheap renewable power the decisive input. A **sovereignty + energy**
  pitch for terrestrial African compute. See [[infra.energy]], [[tech.ai]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-19-grounding-the-cloud-in-namibia-namibiansun]].

The tension the wiki tracks: a **sovereign-cloud** ambition being built substantially on a **Chinese
(Huawei) stack** — sovereignty by dependency. See [[NAM]], [[infra.store]], [[china-namibia-smart-city]].
