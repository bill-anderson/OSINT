# Ghana × AI, cybersecurity & the sovereignty debate

Ghana's **$270m [[ghana-national-ai-strategy|National AI Strategy]]** (Mahama, Apr 2026) triggered an unusually
rich **home-grown sovereignty debate** — a strong `sovereignty`/`colonialism`-lens cluster.

## The sovereignty debate

- **"Declaration of digital sovereignty."** Commentators read the strategy as a sovereignty statement.
- **"Sovereignty paradox / data colonialism."** A widely-cited essay frames a 21st-century "invisible scramble"
  waged through **algorithms, DPI and bilateral health compacts** — noting **Ghana rejected a US $109m
  health-data deal** — and the tension between political sovereignty and macroeconomic dependency.
- **Infrastructure over ambition.** Analysts warn Ghana's AI future turns on **compute / data-centre capacity**,
  not rhetoric; and that AI in **healthcare must deliver** rather than lean on **foreign datasets**.

## Cybersecurity

- **BoG Cyber & Information Security Directive 2026** — mandates **financial-data localisation**, expands the
  sector CSIRT (**FICSOC**), and is described as Ghana's **first AI-governance framework** for finance.
- **AI-driven security centre (2025-05)** — Ghana launched what it called **Africa's largest AI-driven security
  & digital-forensics centre** (Accra; VIA/Exabeam SOC).

## Skills

- **"One Million Coders"** — the flagship skills programme drew an **IMANI critique (Franklin Cudjoe)** for
  lacking a jobs strategy; **YEA × Ghana Digital Centres** is training **2,000 youth** in AI/cyber/data
  (Microsoft microdegrees).
