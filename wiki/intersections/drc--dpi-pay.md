# DR Congo × Digital Payments (`dpi.pay`)

Digital payment rails are deepening in the DRC as [[onafriq]] expands card–wallet
interoperability and a stablecoin settlement pilot is reported, even as a collapsed
state banking-modernisation contract has spilled into a US federal lawsuit.

## What we know

- **[[COD]] Visa settles M-Pesa top-ups in stablecoins via Onafriq / Visa Pay (2026-07).** A senior
  [[visa]] executive confirmed on record a **live DRC use case** settling **[[safaricom]] M-Pesa** wallet
  top-ups in **stablecoins in the background** via **Visa Pay**, with **[[onafriq]]** — bypassing
  correspondent banking (~**8%** of SSA remittances), building on Visa Pay DRC (Sept 2025). The core
  mechanism is corroborated; the "joint USD-stablecoin *pilot* launch" framing is downgraded — **no
  jointly-announced pilot** (Ecofin), USD peg inferred, one partnership awaiting regulatory approval amid
  BCC de-dollarisation. Deal: [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]] (REV-006 resolved 2026-07-16).
  See [[geopol.gulf]], [[finance.new]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-02 Visa tests stablecoins in DRC for cross-border payments (The EastAfrican)]],
  [[2026-07-02 DR Congo becomes Africa's live test for dollar-settled mobile money (Ecofin)]].
- **[[COD]] DR Congo — deepening rails amid a failed-deal dispute (2026-01).** [[onafriq]] is
  expanding in DRC (29m mobile-money accounts, 34.6% penetration as of Q4 2024), having
  supported the Q4-2025 **Visa Pay** launch bringing card–wallet interoperability. Against that,
  US fintech **[[payservices]]** sued the DRC in US federal court over a collapsed **$72m**
  state banking-modernisation project ([[payservices-drc-banking-deal]]), seeking up to
  **$400m** (some filings cite $4bn) and alleging obstruction after refusing bribe demands;
  Kinshasa calls the papers a non-binding MoU. See [[finance.new]], [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[geopol.usa]], [[include.access]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-26 How Onafriq is growing digital finance amid uncertainty in DRC]],
  [[2026-01-19 US fintech sues DR Congo for $400m over failed banking deal]].

## The BCC's de-dollarisation gambit (the policy anchor)

The central bank **[[banque-centrale-du-congo|BCC]]** (Gov. Wameso) set the frame the rest sits inside:
**58% financial access vs 25–30% bank-account penetration** (Apr 2026), a bet on digitalisation and youth,
policy rate cut **15% → 13.5%** — and a hard **de-dollarisation deadline: from 9 Apr 2027, no foreign-cash
transactions at all, any amount (scriptural only)**. This runs in direct tension with the **USD-denominated
rails** proliferating: FirstBank launched a USD USSD account, First Bot and a prepaid Visa card just as the
BCC pushes de-dollarisation. A **Visa × BCC MoU** (Apr 2026, tied to the BCC 2026–2030 Strategic Plan) and
the stablecoin story both sit against this backdrop. Sources:
[[2026-05-04-bcc-inclusion-financiere-58-pourcent-actualite]],
[[2025-10-31-firstbank-drc-experience-digitale-actualite]],
[[2026-04-16-visa-bcc-agreement-financial-inclusion-payments-techafricanews]].

## Interoperability forming bottom-up

Beneath the Visa/Onafriq story, a **bank-operated interoperability layer** is forming: **Ecobank's
Auto-Allocation** automated e-money creation for super-agents with **Airtel** (Nov 2025), then **Orange
Money** (Jan 2026); **FirstBank's FirstMonie** claims to be the only DRC wallet sending to all other
mobile-money services instantly; the **Transforme** project (World Bank/**Paycode**) is connecting **MFIs
and Coopecs to the BCC national switch** across 145 territories (corebanking + 10,000 biometric POS + EMV
"Mosolo" cards). Historically, **[[multipay-drc|MultiPay]]** (2015, Interswitch backend) was the DRC's
first local interbank service. Sources: [[2025-11-11-ecobank-airtel-auto-allocation-actualite]],
[[2026-01-21-ecobank-orange-money-auto-allocation-actualite]],
[[2026-01-29-firstbank-firstmonie-wallet-actualite]],
[[2026-04-16-transforme-paycode-digitalisation-imf-coopec-actualite]],
[[2025-12-17-multipay-decade-payments-evolution-drc-techcabal]].

## Biometric rails, card networks & market structure

- **Biometric payments.** **[[mainmoney|MainMoney]]** launched a **palm-vein** biometric payment terminal
  (Visa-backed; AI.Tech/Keyo), integrated into national payments infrastructure and named against the
  **2023–2028 National Financial Inclusion Strategy** (~70% by 2028). Sources:
  [[2026-04-30-mainmoney-palm-vein-lancement-actualite]],
  [[2026-05-11-mainmoney-palm-biometrics-financial-inclusion-drc-biometricupdate]].
- **Card networks reach the wallet.** **[[mastercard|Mastercard]] × [[flash-drc|Flash]]** (3m customers,
  4,000 agents) to issue prepaid cards — card↔wallet rails beyond the Visa/Onafriq story. Source:
  [[2026-07-14-mastercard-flash-digital-financial-inclusion-drc-techafricanews]].
- **Market structure.** Vodacom Congo cites ~**35m mobile-money users**, ~$120m microloans (94.5%
  repayment); Nomba's independent reporting: **>80% never held a bank account**, >90% USD-denominated,
  Rawbank $4.7bn deposits — and flags the **mobile-money count divergence** (24m/29m/30m/35m across
  sources; date and attribute individually). Sources:
  [[2026-04-03-vodacom-congo-integrated-financial-ecosystem-inclusion-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2025-11-21-nomba-enters-drc-remittance-growth-push-techcabal]].
- **Stablecoin circulation, dated.** A trade-press "joint stablecoin pilot launch" account (Jul 2026)
  documents *how the story spread* — it does **not** out-rank the Visa-exec primary (REV-006). Source:
  [[2026-07-06-visa-mpesa-onafriq-stablecoin-pilot-drc-techafricanews]].
