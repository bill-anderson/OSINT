# Niger × Critical Digital Infrastructure & Cybersecurity

A junta building **sovereign digital infrastructure from the ground up** — fibre backbone, national data
centre, IXP, PKI, a national cyber centre and a monitoring platform — explicitly framed as **digital
sovereignty** for an AES state.

## The critical-infrastructure blueprint (2025-07)

- **National committee installed (23 Jul 2025).** Minister Adji Ali Salatou installed a committee to deploy and
  operationalise Niger's **critical digital infrastructures**, enumerated as: a **national PKI**; a **National
  Cybersecurity Centre (CNAC)** with a **SOC + CERT**; the **[[niger-ixp|national IXP]]**; **government Tier III+
  reference data centres**; a **national digital forensics lab**; and the **national fibre backbone + metropolitan
  loops** — "essential to our digital sovereignty and national security." See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]].
  Source: [[2025-07-24-national-committee-critical-digital-infrastructure-lesahel]].
- **National Cybersecurity Strategy in the making (AfricaCERT, Jul 2025).** Niger hosted the **13th AfricaCERT
  Handshake symposium** (Niamey, opened by PM/Finance Minister **Zeine**): elaboration of a **National
  Cybersecurity Strategy**, accession to conventions, and **[[ansi-niger|ANSI]]**'s three pillars —
  **infrastructure, human competence, regional (AES) alliance** ("transform our handshakes into a regional
  cyber-defence alliance"). See [[capacity.training]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-25-africacert-handshake-symposium-niamey-lesahel]].

## The physical layer

- **Trans-Sahara Fibre backbone + Tier III data centre (2025-11).** Niger took provisional acceptance of
  **1,031 km of fibre on five cross-border axes** (Algeria, Chad, Nigeria, Benin, Burkina Faso) + an 88-km loop
  to the national data centre, and a **Tier III data centre in Niamey**, under the **AfDB**-financed DTS
  (~**€43m / 30bn FCFA**) — desenclavement + "sovereignty of state data." See
  **[[trans-saharan-fibre-backbone]]**, [[infra.store]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-17-trans-sahara-fibre-backbone-1031km-tier3-datacentre-lesahel]].
- **The national Data Center (2025-03).** Minister **Sidi Mohamed Raliou** (an IT specialist) said Niger was
  building a **national Data Center** — "where all Niger's data will be stored and secured" — targeted for
  delivery by **30 Sept 2025** (a date that later slipped into the DTS Tier III reception), alongside a drafted
  (then-unadopted) **national digital-development policy** and a "zéro papier" agenda with ANSI/ARCEP/HAPDP. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[infra.store]]. Source: [[2025-03-04-minister-raliou-national-data-center-interview-lesahel]].

## Regulation as sovereignty

- **ARCEP continuous-monitoring platform (2026-01).** **[[arcep-niger|ARCEP]]** inaugurated a permanent
  mobile-network QoS/regulatory supervision platform (TRAFSCAN/QUALCOP/NETFIX; Tunisian vendor INFOCOM) — a
  "lever of digital sovereignty" giving the state real-time visibility into operators' networks. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[niger--infra-connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-29-arcep-continuous-mobile-network-monitoring-platform-lesahel]].
