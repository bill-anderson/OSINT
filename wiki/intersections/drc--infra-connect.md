# DR Congo × Connectivity

DR Congo's connectivity story is unlike any other in the wiki: a country of ~**30% mobile-internet access
and <0.02% fixed** (2023, ARPTC), where **armed conflict is itself the dominant connectivity variable** in
the east, while the west and the state chase Starlink, a sovereign satellite, Chinese fibre and a
universal-service fund. This page holds the connectivity record across **conflict outages, satellite/
universal access, and towers/fibre/data centres**.

## Conflict as connectivity-destroyer (the eastern outages)

The national press documents a mechanism the wiki lacked — **the AFC/M23 war knocking out telecoms**, with
**mobile money the first-order casualty** each time:

- **Nationwide social-media restriction (Feb 2025)** — X and TikTok restricted for days; CSAC disclaims
  knowledge, PTNTIC sources call it a "technical fault," users route around via VPN. The **first shutdown
  item in the wiki**. Source: [[2025-02-03-acces-restreint-reseaux-sociaux-actualite]].
- **Walikale (Mar 2025)** — all networks failed not from battle but because **generator fuel could not
  reach tower sites** under AFC/M23 control (Airtel had abandoned Walikale ~2023): an outage *mechanism*.
  Source: [[2025-03-28-walikale-reseaux-telephonie-coupes-actualite]].
- **Rutshuru & Masisi (Jan–Feb 2026)** — a **month-long total blackout** (19 Jan–17 Feb 2026); residents
  forced onto neighbouring countries' networks; **mobile money paralysed**. Source:
  [[2026-02-18-rutshuru-masisi-reconnectes-airtel-orange-actualite]].
- **Rubaya (Feb 2026)** — shelling damaged all three operators' masts at the **coltan hub**; 24-hour
  outage, mobile money the first casualty. Source: [[2026-02-26-rubaya-reseaux-retablis-actualite]].

## Satellite, Starlink & universal access

- **Starlink licensed (May 2025).** ARPTC granted **[[starlink]] DRC S.A.** an official ISP licence —
  **reversing its own March-2024 position** that Starlink held no title and its terminals were illegal.
  Civil society (ACAJ) welcomed it as an **antitrust corrective** against incumbent monopoly abuse; retail
  channels followed (Somaf, Kindu); and Africa CDC used **150 Starlink kits as health-data infrastructure**
  in the **Ebola** response in Ituri. Sources: [[2025-05-02-starlink-licence-officielle-actualite]],
  [[2025-05-05-acaj-salue-homologation-starlink-actualite]],
  [[2026-04-03-somaf-group-kindu-starlink-actualite]], [[2026-06-04-starlink-kits-ebola-ituri-actualite]].
- **A sovereign satellite ($400m).** A **[[monacosat-drc-satellite|DRC–Monaco (Monacosat) MoU]]** (Nov
  2024) launched a **sovereign satellite programme**, cost ~**$400m** reported already mobilised. Source:
  [[2025-08-31-projet-acquisition-satellite-monacosat-actualite]].
- **The universal-service fund finally moves.** The **[[fdsu-drc|FDSU]]** (loi n°20/17, 5 Nov 2020) sat
  unfunded until it **launched its first pilot sites at Nkamba, Kongo Central (30 May 2026)** — the first
  state-financed rural telecom build. Sources: [[2025-02-19-internet-society-masterclass-fdsu-actualite]],
  [[2026-06-02-fdsu-sites-pilotes-nkamba-actualite]].

## Towers, fibre, data centres & spectrum

- **The gap, quantified.** A Tshisekedi Council directive (Jun 2025) cited ARPTC's **30.79% mobile / <0.02%
  fixed (2023)** and a ~50m-km fibre shortfall; a GSMA/DataReportal dataset (May 2026) gives **3G 68% / 4G
  57% coverage, ~65m mobile subs, smartphones ~40% of connections, internet penetration 30.5% (~35m
  users)**, entry smartphone median **$305**. A sector reset ("**états généraux** des postes et télécoms,"
  Oct 2025) named telecoms the state's **second-largest revenue source** and SCPT asset spoliation a problem
  to fix; ARPTC opened a **5G consultation** (Mar 2025). Sources:
  [[2025-06-22-tshisekedi-fracture-numerique-conseil-ministres-actualite]],
  [[2026-05-13-drc-connectivity-improves-usage-power-gaps-connectingafrica]],
  [[2025-10-27-etats-generaux-postes-telecoms-actualite]], [[2025-03-18-arptc-consultation-publique-5g-actualite]].
- **Towers — [[helios-towers-drc|Helios Towers]].** A **$100m+** ANAPI investment convention (Mar 2026);
  ~**3,000 towers** covering **32m→39m people** (both self-reported, seven weeks apart — treat as dated
  claims, not a real jump); >$0.7bn invested since 2011; >50 sites in mining zones; infrastructure-sharing
  under ARPTC/FRATEL. Sources: [[2026-03-31-anapi-helios-towers-investissement-actualite]],
  [[2026-05-08-helios-towers-fratel-partage-infrastructures-actualite]],
  [[2026-06-25-helios-towers-mining-week-connectivite-actualite]].
- **Fibre & IX.** A **$1.5bn Congo River fibre** MoU with China's **[[genew-technologies]]** (2,100km,
  $400m phase 1) — the first significant Chinese digital-infrastructure entry in the COD file; **Paratus**
  lit the **Goma→Mombasa** G2M route (tying eastern DRC to the East African corridor); and **[[acix-drc]]**
  (DE-CIX × Internet pour Tous × United SA) became the DRC's **first distributed IX**. Sources:
  [[2026-07-03-drc-plans-1-5bn-fibre-congo-river-genew-connectingafrica]],
  [[2026-02-09-paratus-goma-mombasa-fibre-highway-itweb]],
  [[2026-05-19-distributed-exchange-acix-boosts-drc-connectivity-itweb]].
- **Rural JV & data centres.** The **[[orange]]–[[vodacom]]** rural towerco JV (Jan 2025) — up to **2,000
  solar 2G/4G base stations over six years, reaching ~19m people**; and the payments-grade compliance layer
  — **Raxio Kinshasa** ($30m, Tier III, 400 racks, 1.5MW) and OADC Texaf's **ISO 27001/22301 + PCI DSS**.
  Sources: [[2025-01-14-orange-vodacom-bolster-drc-rural-network-coverage-telecomreviewafrica]],
  [[2025-10-31-mbtp-raxio-datacenter-actualite]],
  [[2025-02-03-oadc-texaf-kinshasa-iso-pci-dss-certifications-techafricanews]].
