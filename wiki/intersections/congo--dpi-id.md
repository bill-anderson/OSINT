# Republic of Congo × Digital Identity

Congo is assembling foundational identity by **upgrading a tax number into a universal biometric ID** (the NIU), while a World Bank-financed programme builds the surrounding stack — a digital-ID framework, a digital wallet, civil-status digitisation and decentralised card production — and a data-protection commission is finally stood up alongside it.

## What we know

**The NIU as foundational ID.** The **[[niu-congo]]** (Numéro d'Identification Unique) began (2004) as a mandatory taxpayer number for the DGID, was made **biometric** in 2020 (iris + fingerprints), and is now **universal**: issued to every resident and citizen **from age 6**, valid 10 years, free, and **mandatory for 8 economic transactions and 5 administrative/social formalities** (bank accounts, cross-border transfers, utilities/telecom, tax/customs, nationality/criminal-record certificates, social security). Production relaunched Sept 2025.

**The framework being built.** Under the World Bank **[[patn-congo]]** programme and "Congo Digital 2025," a July-2025 Brazzaville workshop committed the government to a legal/institutional digital-ID framework and a **Digital Wallet**, with **civil-status reform** progressing via **SIFEC** (digitising civil-status records) and the Interior Ministry pursuing a "unified, reliable, sovereign digital identity" (Thales cited as partner) — framed as national security.

**Decentralised issuance.** A new **passport + national-ID production centre in Pointe-Noire** (2025; 300 CEMAC passports + 20,000 CNI/day, French-security-firm materials) ends the need to travel to Brazzaville — administrative equity plus document security.

**Accountability, belatedly.** The **[[national-personal-data-protection-commission-congo]]** — draft law approved July 2024, members installed January 2026 — is standing up as the ID/data build accelerates. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.protect]], [[COG]].
