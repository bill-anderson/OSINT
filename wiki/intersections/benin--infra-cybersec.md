# Benin × Cybersecurity

Benin has paired one of the region's fastest e-government builds with an unusually **candid, quantified**
cyber posture — a real stack (strategy, CSIRT, PKI, forensic lab), the ambition of a West African cyber
hub, and a rare **first national vulnerability report that publishes its own weaknesses**. The tension is
explicit: a widening attack surface as services digitise, against still-uneven institutional compliance.

## What we know

- **A cyber stack since 2020.** [[gov.policy]] Benin has operated a **national digital-security strategy**
  since 2020, standing up a **national CSIRT** (bjCSIRT), a **national PKI**, a **forensic-analysis lab**
  and a **critical-infrastructure protection policy**, and is committed under the **Malabo** and
  **Budapest** conventions. Coordination runs through **[[asin-benin]]** and the **CNIN** (Centre National
  d'Investigations Numériques, DG Ouanilo Médegan Fagla), with a national data centre behind it. Source:
  [[2025-06-26-caf-5e-edition-assises-cotonou-cybersecurite]].
- **The first national vulnerability report (2021–2024) — quantified counter-evidence.** [[gov.standards]]
  Presented at CAF 2025 (25 Jun) by ASIN + CNIN: **878 recorded vulnerabilities (23% critical)**, **832
  password breaches**, and PSSIE/SSIE compliance ranging **6%–87%** across institutions — the **Public
  Service** sector worst-hit (326 cases) — attributed to outdated software, weak user awareness and
  missing patches. A deliberate act of transparency that tempers the success narrative. Sources:
  [[2025-07-03-techafricanews-benin-first-national-cybersecurity-report]],
  [[2025-06-26-cyber-africa-forum-2025-rapport-vulnerabilites]], [[2025-07-02-marc-andre-loko-dispositif-solide]].
- **Cotonou as a cyber-hub bid.** [[gov.policy]] Benin **hosted the Cyber Africa Forum** (5th edition,
  24–25 Jun 2025; 6th returning 16–17 Nov 2026), runs an **ASIN/CAF HackerLab** (500+ entrants), a
  cybersecurity-only incubator **Cyber Incub** (ASIN/ADPME/CNIN; Luxembourg LuxDev/BeDigital backing; 10
  teams, three winners at 10m FCFA), a **Benin–FIRST** table-top crisis exercise for state RSSIs, and a
  statewide **EBIOS Risk Manager** framework; ASIN trained **300 Republican Police** unit heads (2026).
  Sources: [[2025-02-26-cyber-africa-forum-2025-annonce-cotonou]], [[2025-04-16-cybersecurite-benin-start-up-innovantes]],
  [[2026-04-21-cyber-incub-abomey-calavi]], [[2025-07-10-asin-ebios-risk-manager-rssi]],
  [[2026-06-26-asin-forme-300-policiers]], [[2026-07-07-cyber-africa-forum-2026-cotonou]].
- **The widening attack surface, and where it points.** [[dpi.govtech]] ASIN's DG frames **230+
  dematerialised services** as an expanding attack surface, with ~40 priority structures under tailored
  oversight; an expert roadmap to **2033** urges a **sovereign cloud, a national cyber authority, a
  universal digital ID and AI-based intelligence fusion**, while minister **Akplogan** frames "**cognitive
  sovereignty**" ("no augmented state without protected systems"). Benin was among the states in Interpol's
  **Carton Rouge 2.0** takedown (651 arrests, 2026-02). GenAI adds new risk — a cited Smile ID finding of
  a **sevenfold rise in deepfake incidents** (H2 2024). Sources:
  [[2026-04-02-ale-abdel-farid-securite-nationale-numerique-2033]], [[2026-06-25-conference-rssi-cyberresilience]],
  [[2026-02-19-interpol-carton-rouge-651-arrestations]], [[2026-01-20-ia-generative-cybersecurite]].
