# Uganda × Civil & Business Registries

Uganda is **consolidating registration under two hubs**: **[[nira-uganda|NIRA]]** as the single civil-
registration body (births, marriages, deaths — and now resident foreigners), and **[[ursb|URSB]]** as the
single business register. The logic is a "single source of truth" per entity type, wired into the wider
DPI — the registry underpinning of the [[uganda--dpi-id|ID]] gate.

## Civil registration folds into NIRA

- **Marriage registration transferred (Apr 2025).** Under the **RAPEX** rationalisation, URSB handed
  **marriage registration to NIRA** (amended URSB Act 2024 + Registration of Persons Act 2024, Aug 2024) —
  so **births, marriages and deaths sit under one body**: *"the whole story — from birth, to marriage, to
  death — will now be stored in one place"* (Kisembo). URSB had collected UGX 1.94bn in marriage NTR in
  FY2022/23. Source: [[2025-04-25-ursb-hands-marriage-registration-to-nira]].
- **Birth-registration drive (2026).** NIRA intensified birth registration (community drives of 10+
  parents; under-9-months exempt from biometrics; certificates free under 6 months, then UGX 10k/20k/50k by
  age band; NINs free), appealing to churches to fold it into pre-baptism prep — *"every child deserves a
  legal identity from birth."* Birth registration is a **prerequisite for ID issuance**. See
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-06-01-nira-birth-registration-alien-registration]].

## Foreigners registered for the first time (Jun 2026)

- **Alien registration.** From **10 June 2026** NIRA began registering **legally resident foreign
  nationals** for the first time — issuing **Alien Identification Numbers (AINs)** and cards (Sections
  72–73, ROP Act CAP 332; Citizenship & Immigration Control Act CAP 313), **$100 fee** via URA, valid for
  the immigration facility's term. The Auditor General had repeatedly flagged the gap. Uganda hosts
  **~2 million registered foreigners** — **~1.95m refugees/asylum-seekers** (OPM/UNHCR-tracked) and
  **~120,000–150,000 conventional residents**. Sources:
  [[2026-06-01-nira-birth-registration-alien-registration]], [[2026-06-04-nira-first-registration-of-foreigners]].

## The business register — URSB's Non-Individual Register (May 2026)

- **Non-Individual Register (NIR).** URSB launched a **centralised digital register** for all
  non-individual entities — companies, partnerships, NGOs, cooperatives, CBOs — each assigned a **Unique
  Entity Registration Number (ERN)** as a universal digital identity (Tax Procedures Code Act amendment,
  2025). Framed as **"a single source of truth"** and **shared digital infrastructure for all MDAs**
  (entity authentication/verification) — the corporate counterpart to the NIN. See [[dpi.exchange]].
  Source: [[2026-05-08-ursb-non-individual-register-launch]].

## Reads

- Uganda's registry work is quietly the **plumbing beneath the ID story**: one body for every life event,
  one number per person (NIN/AIN) and per entity (ERN), all designed to be queried by MDAs, banks and URA.
  It is coherent state-building — but it inherits the same tension as [[uganda--dpi-id]]: the more
  registration becomes the single gate (to services, to legality, to a bank account), the more the
  under-registered — refugees screened at the border, children without documents — are defined *out*. The
  foreigner register in particular formalises a population (refugees) whose documentation is thinnest.
