# Cameroon × Govtech & data exchange

Cameroon is digitising the machinery of the state on several fronts at once — **revenue and customs**,
**procurement and services**, and the **local-government layer** — with donors (Korea, World Bank) and
vendors (Huawei) threaded through, and a recurring gap between ambition and delivery.

## Revenue, customs & the device registry

The sharpest thread is **[[camcis]]** — the digital customs platform behind an **IMEI-based
mobile-device import-duty regime** (Article 6 of the 2023 Finance Law, framed "not a new tax") answering
a revenue collapse from ~CFA 2bn/month to **~CFA 100m in 2025**. Enforcement was set for **25 May 2026**
but **did not land**: MTN/Orange refused on unmet prerequisites, and Motazé was still demanding compliance
in June (blocking targets IMEIs, not SIMs). A separate **digital customs-payment tool** (Mar 2026) and the
**DGI's tax-administration digitisation** ("souveraineté financière") round out the revenue push. Sources:

## Local government — big ambition, thin base

- **[[digi-cor]]** — a **CFA 92bn ($163m)** local-government digitisation programme (distinct from PATNUC),
  on a stark baseline: **68.5% of local authorities lack structured networks; 57.8% have no digitally
  trained staff**. It follows the **Jul-2025 Digital Master Plan** for the ~394 decentralised authorities.
  Ground-truth: ANTIC has reached **230 councils (~64%)** but only **48.6% of North West councils have
  functional websites** (Jul 2026). On the registry side, the **Douala City Council × Huawei MoU** (28 Apr
  2026) will digitise **200,000 land files** with a digital possession certificate — the first Huawei-CMR
  item and first `geopol.china` local-govt tag. Sources:
