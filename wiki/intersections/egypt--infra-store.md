# Egypt × Data centres, cloud & digital sovereignty

Egypt is turning its Africa–Europe–Asia crossroads position and cable concentration into a **regional
data-centre and cloud hub**, wrapped in an explicit **digital-sovereignty / data-localisation** framing —
against a continent still holding **under 2% of global data-centre capacity**.

## National strategy

- **MCIT is preparing an integrated national data-centre strategy** (2026-06, presidential directive):
  localisation, regional storage/processing hub, digital-services exports. The **Cabinet reaffirmed**
  hyperscale expansion + cloud/AI investment (2026-07).
- **[[ntra-egypt|NTRA]] has issued 10 data-centre licences in two years**; the government estimates **AI could
  add up to $42.7bn** in value (digital tech/AI **7.7% of GDP by 2030**).

## The estate

- **[[hassan-allam-data-centre|Hassan Allam Digital Infrastructure]]** — $400m first-phase DC licence (with
  **A15**), hosting/cloud/AI-ready for government, finance and business (2026-06).
- **Sinai green hyperscale DC** — talks for a **>$1bn** facility (Renergy Group) fully powered by green
  hydrogen/solar, seawater cooling, **10,000 m² → 500,000 m²**, Japanese investor interest (2026-03).
- **[[telecom-egypt]] Regional Data Hub** — RDH 1 & the Tier-III RDH 2 (existing).

## Ownership: the state keeps the estate

- **Helios majority-stake sale cancelled (2026-07-17).** Telecom Egypt's board withdrew from the
  agreement — announced **2025-09-07** — to sell **75–80%** of the unit owning the
  **[[regional-data-center-hub]]** to **[[helios-investment-partners]]**, saying the transaction's
  conditions had not been met. It will instead **carve RDH out as a wholly-owned subsidiary** with a
  dedicated technical and commercial team, citing the sector's growth and rising Egyptian demand.
  The commercial terms and the specific unmet conditions are not disclosed in the held source.
  The effect — a strategically-sited data-centre estate staying wholly under a state-controlled
  operator rather than passing to a financial sponsor — cuts with the localisation framing above,
  though the stated reason is transactional, not sovereigntist.
  See [[telecom-egypt-helios-rdh-stake-sale]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-telecom-egypt-cancels-helios-data-center-stake-sale]].

## Vendor localisation

- **[[huawei|Huawei Cloud]]** runs an **Egypt region since 2024** keeping data in-country (NTRA cert + Tier 3
  licence; adheres to **[[fra-egypt|FRA]]** localisation for non-bank financials), with a **Thndr MoU** (2026-06)
  — a `geopol.china` localisation vector. **SAP** cloud/AI/outsourcing cooperation adds an EU-vendor track
  under the same sovereignty framing (2026-03).
