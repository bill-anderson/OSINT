# South Africa × Digital Payments (`ZAF` × `dpi.pay`)

South Africa is opening its bank-only National Payment System to non-bank PSPs through activity-based licensing under [[sarb]] Vision 2025, while a broader 2026 fintech rulebook — an AI-in-finance framework, crypto licensing and the COFI Bill — builds the scaffolding beneath the payments reform.

## What we know

- **[[ZAF]] Opening the National Payment System (2026 reform).** South Africa is opening its
  bank-only NPS to **non-bank PSPs** via activity-based licensing (7 categories) under [[sarb]]
  Vision 2025 — a 2025 Exemption Notice/Draft Directive ahead of the legislative amendment.
  **PayShap** (instant payments, live 2023) crossed **>100m transactions** in year one, raised
  its per-txn limit to R50,000, and will integrate into third-party wallets; cross-border via
  SADC TCIB on ISO 20022. [[sars]] (which crossed **R2tn** annual revenue) is driving a single
  biometric digital ID and partnering [[sarb]] on a cost-effective instant-payment system to
  cut cash and widen inclusion. See [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.id]], [[finance.new]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-17 South Africa's National Payment System PSP licences and the 2026 payments reform]],
  [[2026-04-03 Say goodbye to SARS tax numbers in South Africa]].
- **[[ZAF]] South Africa — the 2026 fintech-regulation agenda.** A year-ahead outlook (Webber
  Wentzel) maps SA's pending fintech rulebook: an **AI-in-finance regulatory framework**
  expected 2026 (after the FSCA/Prudential Authority AI survey), the **[[sarb]]** Payments
  Ecosystem Modernisation opening the NPS to non-banks, **300 crypto-asset-service-provider
  licences** issued by end-2025, "**2026 the year of stablecoins**" for cross-border flows, and
  the pending **COFI Bill** consolidating conduct rules (open finance, BNPL) — the scaffolding
  under the payments reform. See [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.policy]], [[finance.new]], [[popia]].
  Source: [[2026-01-30 2026 fintech year ahead]].
