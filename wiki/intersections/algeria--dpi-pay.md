# Algeria × Digital Payments

A cash-heavy economy (credit-card penetration ~2.8%, debit ~22.9% in 2023) building electronic rails on several fronts at once, domestic and cross-border. By **end-March 2026** the market held **22m+ payment cards** (nearly **18m Edahabia** issued by **Algérie Poste**); **e-payment transactions rose 46% YoY in 2025 to DZD 939bn (~$7bn)**, with ~104k TPE terminals — and **ENIE** now produces payment terminals domestically (1.6m, 2026-05).

- **Mobile pay — DZ MOB PAY.** A QR-code smartphone payment system launched ~January 2025 (BNA, Al Salam Bank, Algeria Post), free for users and merchants, atop the June-2024 **Switch Mobile** interoperability layer; >75m smartphone transactions by Oct 2024.
- **Domestic instant switch.** The **Bank of Algeria** is standing up a domestic **instant payment system** (announced Oct 2024) to replace up-to-72-hour merchant settlement — a financial-inclusion play.
- **Government e-payments.** **Tabioucom** (Aug 2024) digitised fiscal-stamp-duty payment (Edahabia/CIB cards), with anti-fraud verification and expansion to visas, registers and consular documents.
- **Cross-border — PAPSS.** The **Bank of Algeria joined [[papss|PAPSS]]** in Aug 2025 as its 18th country (hosting IATF2025), extending PAPSS's North-African footprint (Tunisia, Egypt, Morocco, Algeria).
- **Digital banking & KYC.** The **[[bank-of-algeria|Bank of Algeria]]** (governor Mohamed Lamine Lebbou) set licensing rules for fully digital banks (**Instruction 02-2025**) and issued a **binding KYC instruction 04-2026** (30 Apr 2026) tying banks to AML/CTF and PEP scrutiny; several **digital-bank / PSP dossiers** are under review under the new monetary-and-banking law.
- **Utility & stamp-duty rails.** **Algérie Poste–Sonelgaz** "Posty Mob" utility-payment deal (2025-07); **BaridiMob** virtual card / TPE update (2025-11); **online stamp-duty** for commercial registration (Tabaakoum/Edahabia; MoF–MoT MoU, 2025-12 → 2026-01).
- **Fintech & super-apps.** **[[yassir]]** acquired **Uno hypermarkets** to extend its super-app (2026-03); **[[vaulfi]]** is building a **stablecoin-powered super-app** for North Africa (2026-02); **VOLZ** raised **DZD 600m (~$5m)** — Algeria's largest local-currency round and the first **Algerian Startup Fund** exit (2025-12).
- **CBDC exploration.** A **"digital dinar" / blockchain** track is discussed under a financial-sovereignty frame (2026-04).
