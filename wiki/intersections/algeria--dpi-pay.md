# Algeria × Digital Payments

A cash-heavy economy (credit-card penetration ~2.8%, debit ~22.9% in 2023) building electronic rails on several fronts at once, domestic and cross-border.

- **Mobile pay — DZ MOB PAY.** A QR-code smartphone payment system launched ~January 2025 (BNA, Al Salam Bank, Algeria Post), free for users and merchants, atop the June-2024 **Switch Mobile** interoperability layer; >75m smartphone transactions by Oct 2024.
- **Domestic instant switch.** The **Bank of Algeria** is standing up a domestic **instant payment system** (announced Oct 2024) to replace up-to-72-hour merchant settlement — a financial-inclusion play.
- **Government e-payments.** **Tabioucom** (Aug 2024) digitised fiscal-stamp-duty payment (Edahabia/CIB cards), with anti-fraud verification and expansion to visas, registers and consular documents.
- **Cross-border — PAPSS.** The **Bank of Algeria joined [[papss|PAPSS]]** in Aug 2025 as its 18th country (hosting IATF2025), extending PAPSS's North-African footprint (Tunisia, Egypt, Morocco, Algeria).
- **Digital banking.** Bank of Algeria **Instruction 02-2025** set licensing rules for fully digital banks.
