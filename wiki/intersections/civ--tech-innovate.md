# Côte d'Ivoire × Innovation, skills, sectoral MIS & investment

Around the ID/govtech/AI core sits a **rising innovation-and-skills economy and a growing digital budget**,
with a distinct **Chinese (Huawei) skills-and-network layer** running alongside the US/Gulf sovereignty
pushes — the wiki had **no `geopol.china` for CIV** before this.

## Innovation ecosystem & investment

The **[[ivoire-tech-forum|Ivoire Tech Forum]]** (from 2025) anchors the startup push — a **FCFA 100bn
innovation fund**, a **$178m startup-fund** call, **>250bn FCFA invested / 2,965 jobs** — extended through
**VivaTech** delegations (2025, 2026), **African Digital Week 2026** (a quantified roadmap: 7 pillars,
40 priority projects), and a **French Tech Abidjan education pact** (PIETP, 75 firms into TVET curriculum).
The private sector is arriving: **[[yango|Yango]]** made **Abidjan its largest African office / continental
HQ** (2025); Botswana agritech **Brastorne** entered via Orange; **Flutterwave×Turnkey** cross-border
(continental). Sources: [[2025-05-07-conseil-des-ministres-7-mai-fratmat]],

## Mobility fintech and the EV bet (2026)

Yango's Abidjan presence has matured from an office into an **asset-finance and vehicle-electrification
play**, and Côte d'Ivoire is now the country where it is being tested first.

- **100 electric cars handed to drivers (2026-07-18).** **[[gocab]]** — the mobility fintech that owns
  **[[yango]]'s largest CIV fleet** — delivered **100 fully electric vehicles** to driver-partners in
  Abidjan, the **first half of a 200-vehicle programme**, described at the time as one of Africa's largest
  operational fleets of **electric ride-hailing cars** and as putting CIV ahead in a race that had until
  then been fought on **two and three wheels** (cf. [[ampersand]]: **4,000+ e-motorcycles in Rwanda,
  1,300+ in Kenya, 25 swap stations**, 2026-07; [[spiro]]: **100,000+ e-motorcycles, 2,500 swap stations,
  seven markets**, 2026-07). The cars run mainly on **GOYA**, Yango's premium service.
- **The financing structure is the story.** GoCab, which **raised $45m in February 2026**, uses
  **drive-to-own**: drivers repay from ride-hailing income over **three years** and then take title — the
  model behind [[moove]]'s **$2bn valuation** (2026-07). **Over 300 drivers** had passed two years in the
  programme as of 2026-07 and are expected to **begin taking ownership from 2027**. The EV switch is what
  makes the repayment arithmetic work: GoCab's **own operating benchmarks** (2026-07, unverified) put a
  full charge at **~8,000 FCFA ($14) for up to 470 km** against **20,000–40,000 FCFA ($35–$70)** of fuel
  for the same distance — a claimed **60–80% cut in energy costs**, or **255,000–681,000 FCFA (~$444–
  $1,186)** retained per 10,000 km. Country MD **Moulaye Tabouré**: *"Reducing that cost by 60% to 80% can
  fundamentally transform a driver's economics."*
- **Why Abidjan.** Yango Africa CEO **Adeniyi Adebayo** said in **May 2026** the group would invest **at
  least $150m in African expansion in 2026** across **10 new markets**, deliberately outside Nigeria,
  Egypt, South Africa and Kenya — targeting **secondary cities in West and Central Africa** plus Namibia,
  Botswana and Mozambique, on the thesis that **cities, not countries, are the unit of analysis**.
  **Bouaké** (launched 2022, flat for three years, now among its best performers) is the cited proof, and
  CIV was the first market for **Yango Motors**, launched at the **Abidjan Auto Show, September 2025**.

**The open question is unit economics, not units.** A 100-car fleet is trivial beside the motorcycle
numbers above; what CIV is actually testing is whether **four-wheeler electric ride-hailing** — higher
capex, longer payback, dependent on a premium-fare customer base — can clear in an African market at all.
If it does, GoCab has a template for the West and Central African cities Yango is entering. Source:
[[2026-07-18-gocab-yango-abidjan-evs]]. See [[infra.energy]], [[tech.industry]], [[dpi.pay]].

## The Chinese (Huawei) layer

A **`geopol.china`** thread the hub lacked: **Huawei ICT Competition** champions (Ivorian world-winners
2025) and CIV students sent to China (2026); **Huawei runs Orange's network operations** via a GNOC (10-yr
partnership, 12+ subsidiaries, 100m+ customers); Huawei pitched a **government super-app** model for public
administration (2026) and a **Smart Agriculture** plot-mapping plan (cocoa traceability under EU

## Budget, strategy & investment framing

The **2026 digital budget is CFA 83.275bn (+37% on CFA 60.78bn), five priority pillars** (reported across
four outlets); the **[[ministry-of-digital-transition-cote-divoire|MTND]]** set a **digital 6%→15% of GDP
by 2030** target at the Africa CEO Forum; a **UNIDO/German-funded Industry 4.0** strategy and the SDSI
2026–30 IT master plan round out the instrument stack; and CIV pitched its roadmap to **ITU leadership** as
a West-Africa digital-hub bid. Sources:
