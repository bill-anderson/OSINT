# Malawi × Digital Identity and CRVS

Malawi is one of Africa's foundational-ID success stories: an identity project
started early, scaled at "breakneck speed," and now approaching **universal
coverage** — the phase where the story shifts from *enrolment* to *use*.

## Coverage — near-universal (as of 2026-07)

- **>13.5m citizens** hold a trusted national digital identity; **~99% of Malawians
  aged 16+** now have a national ID — the brink of full universal coverage, a path
  Malawi shares with Cape Verde. Over **150,000** were enrolled April–June 2026,
  **53% of them women**, narrowing the gender gap. Steered by the
  **[[national-registration-bureau-malawi]]** with **[[undp]]** support (plus the EU,
  Norway and Irish Aid). Builds on the **May–Nov 2017 mass-registration campaign**
  (~9.2m registered) and a five-year universal-legal-identity **blueprint** launched
  2025 (SDG 16.9). Source:
  [[2026-07-13 Malawi reaches 13.5M digital IDs as universal coverage comes into view]].

## From document to platform — the mobile/CRVS transition (2026-07)

- **The Nzika Wallet — a mobile ID launching September 2026.** The NRB will launch the **Nzika ("Citizen")
  Wallet** app in **September 2026**, letting smartphone users store and present their national ID digitally
  (same data as the plastic card). It **complements, not replaces** the physical ID — a person must already be
  registered before self-registering for the digital ID — and the **digital ID needs no periodic renewal**
  (unlike the expiring plastic card), cutting lost/damaged/expired-card problems and printing costs (PS
  **Patrick Machika**). The **[[malawi-data-protection-act-2024]]** and rights advocates (CHRR's Michael
  Kaiyatsa) flag the equal-access, rural-exclusion, cybersecurity and digital-literacy caveats. Sources:
  [[2026-06-11-nrb-nzika-wallet-digital-id-september-itweb]],
  [[2026-07-06-digital-id-cards-september-launch-times]].
- The move is reviewing the **National Registration Act** and the **Marriage, Divorce and Family Relations
  Act** to underpin a modern, secure civil-registration and identification system — all under the World
  Bank-funded **[[dmap]]** (Digital Malawi Acceleration Project), whose Bomalathu platform already carries NRB
  services. Source: [[2026-07-13 Malawi outlines plans to transition to digital IDs - CAJ News Africa]].

## The NRB 2025–2030 Strategic Plan (SDG 16.9)

- Launched **September 2025**, the **NRB 2025–2030 Strategic Plan** targets UN **SDG 16.9** (legal identity for
  all by 2030), aiming for **all births, marriages and deaths registered by 2030**. It rests on **six pillars**:
  universal civil registration within five years; inclusion of marginalised groups; strengthening the DPI
  ecosystem through interoperability; institutional capacity-building; civic engagement and trust; and Vision
  2063 alignment (Minister Ezekiel Ching'oma; [[undp]] co-drafted). The **2026** target announced at ID4Africa
  became the Nzika Wallet. Sources:
  [[2025-10-27-nrb-2025-2030-strategic-plan-sdg-16.9-biometricupdate]],
  [[2025-05-26-nrb-digital-id-2026-target-id4africa-techafricanews]].
- **The stated end-goal is a service gateway, not a card.** UNDP frames the ID as a
  "gateway to essential public and private services" — bank accounts, business
  registration, social protection, formal employment, elections, travel documents —
  echoing government's own line that a national ID must become "much more than an
  official document." This is the enrolment→utility pivot the wider DPI literature
  presses (see [[dpi.id]]).

## Closing the child gap — the birth-registration campaign

- The **National Birth Registration Campaign** (NRB) needs **K17.8bn** to register the **~4.6m children** still
  without a birth certificate (under-16s born before registration became mandatory or missed earlier). It runs
  in **seven phases across 25 districts**, using government primary schools as registration centres (trained
  teachers + NRB staff); **K5.4bn** was allocated in FY2025/26 for phases 1–2, with **K12.4bn** still required.
  Since the **National Registration Act 2015** (replacing a 1904 law), the NRB has registered **~3.8m children**
  and issued **>12.8m** adult IDs; Minister Peter Mukhito framed registration as protection against trafficking,
  child labour and "age manipulation" in security-service recruitment (UNICEF/UNDP/CDC support). See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-16-birth-registration-campaign-4.6m-children-malawi24]].

## ID as the financial-inclusion foundation

- **ICTAM** (ICT Association of Malawi) has pressed government to **integrate the national ID with key digital
  platforms** as a secure verification base for social welfare, health, education and banking — the NRB's
  **National Registration and Identification System (NRIS)** (with [[undp]]) is designed to expose an interface
  for compliant data-sharing to other public/private systems. Framing: Malawi targets **95% adult access to
  formal financial services by 2028 (NSFI III)** against a 2020 baseline where ~60% of adults lacked formal
  access and only 14% actively used it (RBM/UNCDF). See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2025-01-07-ictam-national-id-integration-digital-growth-times]].

## Wider Malawi ID/e-gov context

- President **Mutharika**'s Feb-2026 "digital revolution" directive named national ID
  cards among services to be digitalised "forthwith," and a **Digital Identity Wallet**
  is in testing (see [[MWI]]).
- The **[[malawi-data-protection-act-2024]]** is positioned as the safeguard basis for
  ID and e-government platforms (data privacy "no longer optional").
