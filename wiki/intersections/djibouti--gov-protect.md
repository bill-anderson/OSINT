# Djibouti × Data Protection

Djibouti built a modern data-governance stack in a single legislative push, then began erecting the institutions to run it.

- **The Digital Code (30 Jun 2025).** [[djibouti-digital-code]] — eight volumes, ~800 articles,
  drafted by Jones Day — folds data protection, cybersecurity and e-commerce into one instrument.
- **Data-protection regime.** Its **156-article** privacy law establishes the independent
  **[[cndp-djibouti|CNDP]]**, with 72-hour breach notification, mandatory DPOs, a public register,
  and a **tiered prior-authorisation** system (biometric/genetic ID data, national-identifier
  processing) echoing South Africa's POPIA; extensive extraterritorial reach.
- **Cybersecurity institutions.** The **National Cybersecurity Strategy 2024–2030** (five pillars)
  and the statutory **[[national-cybersecurity-authority-djibouti|Autorité Nationale de Cybersécurité (ANC)]]**,
  created by **Loi n° 195/AN/25** (adopted 12 Nov 2025, JO 15 Dec 2025) and **operational from Dec 2025**
  (DG Abdallah Ibrahim Yonis) — an autonomous public establishment under the Presidency whose **nucleus is
  ANSIE's DSSI** (law Art. 24). The "ANCS" credited with the 2024 strategy was a **pre-statutory label, not a
  separate body** (REV-DJI-001 resolved 2026-07-16). Djibouti ratified the AU **[[malabo-convention]]** (2023)
  and signed the UN Convention on Cybercrime (2025).
