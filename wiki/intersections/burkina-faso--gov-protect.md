# Burkina Faso × Data Protection

A weak data-protection authority elevated not by new powers but by the **sovereignty drive** —
Burkina Faso's CIL gained standing from an executive localisation campaign rather than from
statute.

## A DPA without repressive powers

The **[[cil-burkina-faso|Commission de l'Informatique et des Libertés (CIL)]]** — president
**Dr Halguièta Nassa-Trawina** — admits it has **no repressive powers**, the implementing decrees
that would supply them being missing.

## Elevated by the sovereignty campaign (2025)

- A **PM-led, ministry-by-ministry compliance campaign** raised the CIL's standing and made
  **data-protection sign-off mandatory before platform deployment**.
- Its one concrete enforcement act: a body **noticed for hosting citizens' data offshore** —
  protection fused with the **"zero data abroad"** data-localisation and counter-insurgency
  framing. See [[bf-national-datacenters]], [[infra.store]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the CIL's authority is **borrowed from the executive**, not held under statute.
  That makes it effective while the localisation drive lasts and structurally dependent on it —
  the opposite of the independence a data-protection authority is supposed to have, and a
  reminder that "the DPA is more active" and "the DPA is stronger" are not the same finding.
