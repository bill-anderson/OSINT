# Mauritius × Cybersecurity & Cybercrime

Mauritius is building out a **critical-infrastructure and cybercrime regime** in 2026 — a first for the island —
while opening a contested debate about **identity verification on social media**. (The state-surveillance side of
the cyber story lives in **[[mauritius--gov-protect]]**.)

## Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) regime

- **CII Designation Regulations 2026.** The Cabinet noted the promulgation of the **Cybersecurity and Cybercrime
  (Critical Information Infrastructure Designation) Regulations 2026** (22 May 2026), **in force 1 June 2026**,
  giving CII operators a **12-month** compliance window. The **National Cybersecurity Committee** can designate a
  system as CII under the Cybersecurity and Cybercrime Act, in consultation with regulators; owners must meet
  confidentiality and authorised-access directives. **First-phase sectors:** financial/banking, public service,
  ICT & broadcasting, energy & water, transport. Source:
  [[2026-05-22-cybersecurity-critical-infrastructure-regulations-2026-lexpress]].
- **Teeth.** Non-compliance carries penalties of **up to 25 years' imprisonment and fines up to Rs 2 million
  (~US$42,000)** — before these rules Mauritius could punish cybercriminals but lacked a uniform framework to
  **designate and regulate CII operators**. The National Cybersecurity Committee is the strategic/advisory body
  with enforcement authority alongside law enforcement (stakeholder workshop, Ébène, Minister Ramtohul). See
  [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.standards]]. Source: [[2026-06-30-cii-cyber-laws-framework-itweb]].
- **Cyber-forensics capacity.** The 2026-2027 budget funds a **Cyber Forensic laboratory (Rs 13M)** and rolls
  out an **ISMS** across the administration. Source:
  [[2026-06-20-budget-2026-2027-ai-training-50000-fintech-lexpress]].

## Social-media identity verification (the contested edge)

- **Consultations on an ID-verification mechanism (Jun 2026).** After Cabinet (5 Jun 2026), MITCI announced
  consultations toward a **social-media user identity-verification mechanism** against disinformation,
  impersonation, defamatory/hateful content, scams and CSAM. Minister Ramtohul cited **2,300 online-harm cases
  (Jan–Jun 2026)**, "verification-code" WhatsApp account takeovers, frauds up to **Rs 500,000**, and teenage
  suicides tied to intimate-image leaks — while acknowledging the **privacy / free-expression** tension, VPN
  circumvention, and the need to negotiate with platforms (invoking Art 1 of the Constitution: Mauritian law
  applies on its territory). A sovereignty-vs-rights fault line, adjacent to the surveillance debate in
  **[[mauritius--gov-protect]]**. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-07-social-media-identity-verification-consultations-lexpress]].

## Sub-national reach — Rodrigues

- **Rodrigues in the national AI/cyber strategy (May 2026).** Minister Ramtohul's mission to Rodrigues (26–28
  May) set a **cybersecurity working framework** (a Mauritius-based team + a dedicated cyber/AI representative,
  monthly meetings), the **Rod IT** youth-awareness project, and integration of **MauPass** and the **KOREK**
  super-app via Mauritius Post agents — building on the Rodrigues Regional Assembly's **"Rod-Digital Vision"**
  roadmap (launched 6 May 2026: trust, innovation, sustainability, resilience). Sub-national digital inclusion.
  See [[include.divides]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-29-rodrigues-port-mathurin-ai-cybersecurity-mission-lexpress]].
