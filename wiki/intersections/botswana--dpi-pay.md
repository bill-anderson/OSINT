# Botswana × Digital Payments & Fintech

Botswana has **a spread of rails rather than one switch (yet)** — regional corridors, wallet and card
products and a targeted grant rail, with the national switch still a funded plan.

## What we know

- **Regional corridor live.** The SADC **[[tcib]]** corridor is live via **First Capital Bank/PayInc**
  (inbound from South Africa and Zimbabwe). See [[gov.regional]], [[ZAF]], [[ZWE]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-02-payinc-fcb-botswana-tcib-corridor-live-techafricanews]].
- **Wallet and card rails.** Orange Money is onboarding taxis (**DuelaEasy**) alongside **[[visa]] 3DS**;
  **Absa** offers multi-currency virtual cards; state savings bank **BSB** is going digital.
- **Grant payments on biometrics.** **[[smartswitch-botswana]]** runs biometric **UEPS** grant cards
  (**75,000+ beneficiaries**). See [[include.access]], [[dpi.id]].
- **The switch is funded, not built.** A **national payment switch** is funded under **NDP 12**.
- **Crypto licensed but thin.** Virtual assets are licensed (**[[botswana-virtual-assets]]**) with
  **ESAAMLG gaps** outstanding. See [[gov.legislate]].
- **A thin fintech scene.** A **P2m licence gate** shapes entry; **N2 Pay / Pathetic Inc.** are the
  named players. A new **14% Remote Service VAT** applies to non-resident digital services. See
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2025-05-01-botswana-fintech-n2pay-pathetic-inc-techcabal]].
