# Rwanda × Digital Payments & Fintech

Rwanda has pushed **formal financial inclusion to 93–96%** and, in mid-2026, consolidated all
domestic retail payments onto a single interoperable national rail — **[[ekash]]** — while pivoting
policy "from access to depth" and reaching toward regional cross-border payments.

## eKash — the national interoperable rail

Full profile: **[[ekash]]** (RNDPS 2.0, Mojaloop-based, [[rswitch-rwanda]]-operated, launched Dec
2025). On **14 July 2026** the **[[national-bank-of-rwanda]]** completed migration of **all** domestic
interoperable retail payments onto eKash under **BNR Directive No. 45/2026**:

- Instant transfers across **any** bank or e-money provider — bank-to-bank, wallet-to-wallet,
  bank-to-wallet — plus merchant pay-from-any-account, over existing USSD/app/internet-banking (no new
  app). Capped at **RWF 20** per P2P transfer (institutions may waive) and **RWF 10m (~$7,000)** per
  transaction; BNR oversees availability, performance and consumer protection. Now connects **22
  financial institutions**. Built **entirely by African engineers** (AfricaNenda, Gates & Mojaloop
  foundations, RISA, AFR). Sources: [[2026-07-14-migrates-retail-payments-ekash-itweb]],
  [[2026-07-15-ekash-nationwide-interoperable-wearetech]].

## Regional — toward EAC cross-border payments

- The next eKash phase integrates more **government payment services** and **cross-border instant
  payments** — framed as a step toward seamless EAC payments. This dovetails with the **RSwitch↔TIPS**
  (Tanzania) proof-of-concept, the first corridor under the EAC Cross-Border Payment Masterplan (see
  [[RWA]] hub). Source: [[2026-07-16-ekash-regional-payments-editorial-newtimes]] *(partial body —
  editorial; context from syndicated coverage of the same migration)*.
- **Kenya–Rwanda licence passporting (Mar 2026).** The **CBK** and **[[national-bank-of-rwanda]]**
  signed an MoU to build a **payments licence-passporting framework** — a fintech licensed in one
  country could operate in the other without fresh approval, with coordinated supervision. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[finance.mou]], [[KEN]]. (Mirrors Nigeria's regional-passporting play, cf.
  [[nigeria--dpi-pay]].) Source: [[2026-03-12-kenya-rwanda-fintech-licence-passporting-techcabal]].

## Inclusion and the fintech ecosystem

- **From access to depth.** **93% of adults financially included** (May 2026, driven by mobile money;
  96% formal access reported for 2024) — but BNR deputy governor Nick Barigye says the challenge has
  shifted **from access to depth**, with innovation still concentrated in payments rather than broader
  services; a **regulatory sandbox** (2nd Demo Day) bridges innovation and regulation. Source:
  [[2026-05-14-strengthens-fintech-hub-role-itweb]].
- **Rwanda FinTech Centre (Mar 2026).** A one-stop hub (MINICT, ICT Chamber, Kigali International
  Financial Centre, Rwanda FinTech Association, Luxembourg Cooperation), inaugurated after the
  Inclusive FinTech Forum, to accelerate the **100+** fintech ecosystem and diversify beyond payments
  under the **FinTech Strategy 2024–2029**. See [[tech.innovate]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-13-fintech-centre-sets-stage-itweb]].
- **Hanga Ventures Ignite.** A MINICT startup showcase spotlighting inclusion-focused fintechs (Kayko —
  SME finance/bookkeeping; Save Directly — "Save Now, Buy Later"). Source:
  [[2026-05-29-hanga-ventures-ignite-fintech-techreview]].

## Virtual assets

- **Draft virtual-assets law (Apr 2026).** The Chamber of Deputies approved the rationale of a **draft
  law on virtual assets** (crypto) — investor protection plus a structured legal framework, now in
  parliamentary commission. See [[gov.legislate]]. Source: [[2026-04-01-virtual-assets-draft-law-techafricanews]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: eKash is a deliberately **home-built digital public good** — African-engineered, on
  open-source Mojaloop, state-owned rail — the payments counterpart to Rwanda's sovereign-by-design ID
  ([[rwanda--dpi-id]]). The open question is depth: a world-class rail still mostly carries payments,
  not the broader financial services inclusion is meant to unlock.
