# Egypt × Connectivity

Egypt is **Africa's largest submarine-cable hub** (~**15 active international subsea systems** on its eastern
coast) and is converting that transit position into **digital exports** — while a state-led 5G and rural-fibre
push runs alongside rising consumer complaints over price.

## Cable hub & exports

- **2Africa** (~45,000 km, 33+ countries, 45+ landings; Meta, Vodafone, Orange, China Mobile, MTN) —
  **Telecom Egypt + Cisco activated the 2Africa Mediterranean** capacity (2025-01). It underpins Egypt's shift
  from basic access to **monetising connectivity**: **digital exports $7.4bn** (outsourcing $4.8bn in 2025, up
  from ~$2.4bn in 2022). But **fixed broadband 12.6m (Nov 2025)** comes with rising complaints over package
  prices and data caps.

## 5G & spectrum

- **$3.5bn spectrum deal** (2026-02) with the four operators (**[[telecom-egypt]]**, Vodafone Egypt, Orange
  Egypt, e& Egypt) **doubling mobile spectrum (+410MHz)** under the **National Spectrum Strategy 2026–2030**;
  **[[ntra-egypt|NTRA]]** launched **5G in June 2025** (TE secured its 5G licence Jan 2024 for $150m). Minister
  **Amr Talaat**: fixed speeds doubled over 7.5 years (1st in Africa); **40,000 towers by 2028**.
- **$6bn total telecom investment since 2019**; +3,000 towers this year, +9,000 over three years (2026-06).

## Fibre & the rural divide

- **WE + [[huawei]] FTTR** (fibre-to-the-room) launched — a first in Egypt (2026-05; `geopol.china`).
- **"Decent Life" (Hayat Karima) rural fibre:** ~**5m fibre lines across 1,250+ of 1,450 target villages**
  (20 governorates), a dated trajectory **267 (end-2023) → 856 (end-2024) → 1,000+ (2025) → 1,250+ (mid-2026)**.
