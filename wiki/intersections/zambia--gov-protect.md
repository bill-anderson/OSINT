# Zambia × Data Protection

Zambia's sharpest live data-protection question is not the statute but a **change of custody**:
refugee registration data moving from a UN agency to the ministry that polices immigration, with
the safeguards described only in procedural terms.

## Refugee registration data changes custody

Under the **Zambia Refugees and Host Communities Project (ZRHCP)** — World Bank **P503941**,
approved **2024-09-26**:

- **UNHCR's ProGres database transfers to the Ministry of Home Affairs and Internal Security**.
- Refugees **enrol into [[inris]]** itself — the citizens' register.
- The safeguard offered is **procedural**: DNRPC operating procedures "aligned with" the **Data
  Protection Act 2021**.
- **No purpose limitation, retention limit, consent mechanism or independent oversight is
  described.**

*The **PAD** (Report No. PADHI00410, **2024-09-05**) and the **ESMF** (**2024-12**) were ingested
**2026-07-20** and are held; the transfer is the project's own **Subcomponent 1.2** line item, not
a characterisation.*

See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.mis]], [[zambia--dpi-id]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: transferring a refugee register from an international agency to a national
  interior ministry is exactly the case data-protection law exists for — a population that cannot
  refuse enrolment, a controller with an enforcement mandate, and a merge into the citizens'
  register. Procedural alignment with the Data Protection Act 2021 is what the project documents
  offer; the substantive limits are not on the page.
