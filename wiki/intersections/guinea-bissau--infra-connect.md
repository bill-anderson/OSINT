# Guinea-Bissau × Connectivity infrastructure

One of West Africa's least-connected states (**32.5% internet penetration**, 723k users as of Jan
2025), Guinea-Bissau is building a connectivity base on two tracks in parallel: a **World Bank–funded
national fibre backbone** ([[wardip|WARDIP-GB]]) to replace the absence of any domestic terrestrial
network, and **satellite broadband** ([[starlink]]) as an immediate fill — all under a telecom legal
regime being modernised away from the outdated 2010 law.

## What we know

- **[[GNB]] WARDIP-GB launched (2025-04-02).** [[gov.regional]] President Umaro Sissoco Embaló launched
  the Western Africa Regional Digital Integration Program in Bissau, structured in five components
  (connectivity, data and online markets, digital public services, human capacity). Source:
  [[2025-04-02 President Embalo launches WARDIP in Guinea-Bissau]].
- **[[GNB]] National Fibre-Optic Backbone specs (2026).** The design is **2,877 km of cable, 56 nodes**
  (50 new + 6 existing linked to international networks), **4 phases** (Phase 1: 767 km, Senegal link;
  Phase 2: 892 km, Guinea-Conakry link; Phases 3–4: resilience/redundancy), targeting **>99% population
  coverage** with a private wholesale operator under exclusive licence — **superseding the old WARCIP-era
  ~888 km figure**. Traffic projected to 870 Gbps (national) / 640 Gbps (international) by year 15.
  Source: [[2026-01-01 Guinea-Bissau National Fibre-Optic Backbone specifications (WARDIP-GB)]].
- **[[GNB]] Backbone enters safeguards phase (EIASS, 2026-03/05).** A Simplified Environmental and
  Social Impact Study (EIASS, março 2026) was published for public knowledge, under the World Bank
  Environmental and Social Framework and GNB's environmental laws — the project moving toward
  implementation. Source: [[2026-05-21 WARDIP-GB fibre backbone environmental and social impact study (EIASS)]].
- **[[GNB]] Starlink licensed then live (2025-04 → 06).** [[include.access]] Regulator [[arn]] (ARN-TIC)
  granted [[starlink]] a general provisional authorization on **2025-04-10** (after a Dec 2024
  provisional licence); commercial launch followed **2025-06-19** — GNB Starlink's 7th West African /
  20th continental market, kit ~XOF 228,000 ($400), $63/mo. Sources:
  [[2025-04-10 ARN-TIC grants Starlink Guinea-Bissau provisional authorization]],
  [[2025-06-19 Starlink enters Guinea-Bissau]].
- **[[GNB]] New Electronic Communications Law (transition-era, 2025/26).** [[gov.legislate]] The Council
  of Ministers (transitional presidency of Horta Inta-á) approved a new Electronic Communications Law
  revising the outdated 2010 telecom regime — the legal frame for networks, services and [[arn]]'s
  mandate. Also see [[guinea-bissau--gov-policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Guinea-Bissau Council of Ministers approves new Electronic Communications Law]].
