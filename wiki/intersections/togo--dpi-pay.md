# Togo — Digital Payments and Fintech

Togo's payments story runs on **someone else's rail**. The instant-payment layer is a
**regional** asset operated by the [[bceao]] for all eight WAEMU states, not a national
switch — so Togo's agency here is participation and integration, not ownership. Against
that sits [[novissi]], a domestic G2P scheme that became Togo's DPI calling card by
routing around the registers it lacked. See [[TGO]], [[dpi.pay]], [[XWA]].

## The regional instant rail: PI-SPI

The [[waemu-instant-payment-platform]] launched **30 September 2025** in Dakar, presided
over by BCEAO Governor **Jean-Claude Kassi-Brou** and broadcast to Lomé by videoconference.
It carries **instant, irrevocable 24/7 transfers** between banks, microfinance institutions,
e-money issuers and payment service providers across the **eight UEMOA states**, targeting
settlement in **under ten seconds**; implementation was financially backed by the
[[gates-foundation]] and the [[african-development-bank]]. Source:
[[2025-10-01 BCEAO Launches Instant Payments Platform in Togo with Three Banks]].

**Acronym note.** The wiki uses **PI-SPI** as canonical. The same publisher (Togo First)
renders the platform **PI-PSI** (2025-10-01), **PI** (2025-10-31) and **PI-SPI**
(2025-11-18) — one platform, three renderings. See [[waemu-instant-payment-platform]].

### Two dated participation states — not one trend

These figures describe **different things**; the later does **not** straightforwardly
supersede the earlier, and this wiki does not write "seven banks are on the platform."

- **As of 2025-10-01 — three banks OPERATIONAL in Togo:** Coris Bank, [[ecobank]] and
  [[orabank]]. Then **planning to join**: BOA, BIAT, Cofina and **Sunu Bank Togo**.
  **Guy Martial Awona** (CEO, Orabank Togo; president, UEMOA banking federation) called the
  launch a "decisive step toward an ecosystem where banks, microfinance institutions, and
  fintechs converge on a single infrastructure." Source:
  [[2025-10-01 BCEAO Launches Instant Payments Platform in Togo with Three Banks]].
- **As of 2025-11-18 — seven institutions COMMITTED / preparing interconnection:**
  [[ecobank]], [[orabank]], BOA, Coris Bank, COFINA, **Banque Atlantique** and BIAT.
  Source: [[2025-11-18 BCEAO Launches Developer Sandbox for Instant Payments]].

**Two real discrepancies.** (1) The November list **drops Sunu Bank Togo** and **adds
Banque Atlantique**; neither change is explained in the sources. (2) "Operational" is not
"committed/preparing interconnection" — and a third framing, "testing," appears in the
2025-10-31 Ecobank report. Three state-descriptions, no reconciliation on file.

### More dated states — the rollout timeline

The participation picture kept moving; each is a distinct dated snapshot, not a running total:

- **2025-08-04 (pre-launch) — seven institutions integrated:** Banque Atlantique, BOA, COFINA, BIAT,
  CORIS, [[ecobank]], [[orabank]] (real-condition tests with sample clients since **5 Jun 2025**). Source:
  [[2025-08-04-togo-7-institutions-pi-spi-bceao]].
- **2026-03-06 (public opening) — six authorised to open PI-SPI services to the public** (per the BCEAO
  list at 24 Feb 2026): BIA, BOA, Coris, Ecobank, Orabank, Cofina — up from three at launch. The remaining
  challenge is named as **public adoption** (technical integration, risk/cyber requirements, and the hit to
  traditional commissions). Source: [[2026-03-06-bceao-six-acteurs-pi-spi-togo]].
- **2026-04-03 — a hard deadline of 30 Jun 2026** to finalise connection; **80 institutions connected
  region-wide** (59 banks, 9 e-money, 11 MFIs, 1 payment institution) with 42 more testing — "a firmer
  approach" to overcome operator reluctance. Source: [[2026-04-03-bceao-adhesion-pi-spi-30-juin]].
- **2026-04-14 — Mixx (Yas Togo) joins PI-SPI**, launching **interoperable UEMOA-wide transfers** and an
  **interoperable merchant QR** — the first mobile-wallet integration, extending PI-SPI beyond banks. Mixx
  claims 5m+ users, 31,000 agents. Source: [[2026-04-14-mixx-togo-pi-spi-uemoa]].

### Developer sandbox (2025-11-18)

The BCEAO opened a **public developer sandbox** for its **API-Business**, letting Togolese
companies and IT solution providers build instant-payment products against the platform:
simulate operations, verify technical compliance, prepare integration, with technical
documentation and integration guides. Testable products include merchant payments,
cross-border transfers and aggregation linking mobile money, banks and microfinance — the
stated aim being shorter deployment times and wider adoption across the Union. A regional
central bank courting a national developer base is a notable [[tech.innovate]] /
[[gov.standards]] move. Source:
[[2025-11-18 BCEAO Launches Developer Sandbox for Instant Payments]].

## Bank-led cross-border: Ecobank × Thunes (2025-10-31)

Lomé-headquartered **[[ecobank]]** launched instant cross-border transfers with Singaporean
fintech **[[thunes]]** ([[ecobank-thunes-partnership]]), with **Togo the first market** and a
continental rollout intended to reach the group's **35 markets**. Customers can send and
receive in real time from **130+ countries** via Thunes' global Direct network. CEO **Jeremy
Awori** framed it as "borderless banking"; Thunes CEO **Peter De Caluwe** cited faster access
to liquidity. **This is a separate, bank-led product — not the BCEAO platform**, though the
same report notes Ecobank is among the Togolese banks testing PI. Source:
[[2025-10-31 Ecobank launches instant international payment service in Togo]].

## The fintech layer — licensing, wallets, market shape

Togo's mobile-money market is a **Mixx (Yas) / Flooz (Moov) duopoly** now being widened by new wallets
and hardened by BCEAO licensing:

- **Scale (BCEAO, end-2024).** **12.55m e-money accounts** (6.07m active, 48.35% activity), **+76.87% YoY**
  — one of UEMOA's fastest — on 81,000+ service points; broad financial inclusion ~**89%**. Mobile-money
  penetration **57.2%** (ARCEP, Q3 2025): **Mixx (Yas) 58%** (~2.89m active) vs **Flooz (Moov) 42%**
  (~2.06m); ~4.96m subscribers (+21% YoY), FCFA 1,398bn transacted in Q3 2025 (+33%). Sources:
  [[2026-03-18-togo-comptes-monnaie-electronique-progression]], [[2026-07-08-moov-africa-togo-mobile-money-services]].
- **New wallets.** **[[gozem]] Money** (Lomé super-app, with NSIA Banque; built on its Moneex acquisition)
  launched Oct 2025 — bills, online purchases, transfers, PFM, interoperable. **Moov** added an insurance/
  gift-card/withdrawal-card suite under its "Soyi Mavo" campaign (Jul 2026). Sources:
  [[2025-10-15-gozem-money-entre-en-service]], [[2026-07-08-moov-africa-togo-mobile-money-services]].
- **Licensing regime (BCEAO Instruction 001-01-2024).** Under the rule requiring every UEMOA payment
  fintech to be licensed or cease, **[[semoa]]** (founded 2016, Edem Adjamagbo) won a **BCEAO Level 3
  licence** (29 Jan 2026) — the most complete non-bank licence. Only ~20 payment institutions were licensed
  union-wide by Sep 2025 (deadline pushed three times) — "the payment infrastructure is being rebuilt not
  by innovation but by regulation." See [[gov.legislate]]. Source: [[2026-02-17-techcabal-semoa-bceao-licence]].

## Novissi: G2P built on the register that existed

**[[novissi]]** — Togo's fully-digital pandemic G2P cash transfer, enacted **April 2020** —
reached **500,000+ recipients, 65% of them women** (programme-published figures, reported via
the [[world-bank]] G2PX case study; not independently verified here). The load-bearing fact is
the starting position: **before COVID-19 Togo had no social registry and low national-ID
coverage, but did have a voter registry covering 93% of the population**. Targeting was solved
through the register that existed rather than the one a social-protection system would normally
use — a [[dpi.registry]] story as much as a payments one. See [[include.access]], [[dpi.id]].
Source: [[2023-01-01 Case Study - G2PX in Togo, End-to-End Digital G2P (World Bank)]].

*Reference-study note: the G2PX case study is a World Bank reference work (Brian Webster,
drawing on Boko and Akogbeto, 2022), dated 2023 at year precision, proxy. Its figures are
cited as the study's, not promoted as current state.*
