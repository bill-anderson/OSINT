# Seychelles × AI, STI & EdTech

Seychelles is building the **institutional scaffolding** for science, tech and AI — a new statutory STI
umbrella body with an explicit **AI mandate and open-data repository** — while the cyber committee and
schools begin engaging AI in practice.

## The STI umbrella — NISTI Act 2025

- **National Institute of Science, Technology and Innovation.** The **[[nisti-seychelles|NISTI Act 2025]]**
  (Act 24 of 2025, assented 22 Dec 2025) establishes NISTI as a **body corporate** to lead and coordinate
  STI research. Its objectives include **Science Diplomacy**, making R&D attractive to business, innovation
  in schools, and **"strategies to promote the use of Artificial Intelligence, other emerging technologies
  and digitisation"** across socio-economic development. Functions include a **Science Academy**, a
  **digital scientific-data repository with open access**, and incubation centres / science parks /
  maker-spaces. Commences on a date the Minister appoints. See [[tech.innovate]], [[capacity.research]],
  [[data.open]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source: [[2025-12-23-gazette-nisti-act]].

## AI in practice — media and the cyber committee

- **AI & press freedom (May 2025).** The **National Cybersecurity Coordination Committee (NCCC)** and the
  Association of Media Practitioners (AMPS) ran a World-Press-Freedom-Day workshop on **AI's impact on
  press freedom, ethics and cybersecurity** — an early governance engagement, and the item that surfaces
  the **NCCC** as the national cyber-coordination body. See [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2025-05-13-nation-ai-nccc-amps-media]].

## EdTech — SMART Education

- **SMART Education (Aug 2025).** The Ministry of Education, **[[cable-and-wireless-seychelles|CWS]]** and
  **[[huawei]]** launched Huawei's **SMART Education** solution across **four secondary schools** —
  multimedia lessons, real-time assessment, centralised management/analytics and remote learning
  (Huawei IdeaHub) — part of education reform. See [[dpi.mis]], [[geopol.china]], [[capacity.training]].
  Source: [[2025-08-30-huawei-smart-education-seychelles]] *(vendor case study — verify vs the ministry
  primary)*.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: NISTI's design is deliberately **capability-and-openness-first** — a Science Academy,
  open-access data, incubation, science diplomacy — the sensible posture for a micro-state that will not
  build frontier AI but can build **research capacity and trusted institutions**. The EdTech layer,
  though, runs on a **Chinese vendor** (Huawei via CWS), so the classroom foundation is imported even as
  the STI umbrella asserts national coordination.
