# Tunisia × Data Centres & Cloud

Tunisia is trying to convert its **connectivity position** (Med-cable landings, proximity to Europe) into a
**data-centre and AI-hosting industry** — with the state making it an explicit investment priority and a
solar-powered AI-DC flagship in train. It is early: a strategy, an MoU and an emerging-market listing, not
yet operational capacity.

## The state makes it a priority (Oct 2025)

- **Data centres as a core investment pillar.** At the **22nd Strategic Council** of the
  **[[tunisia-investment-authority|TIA]]**, data centres were named **one of two core priorities**. A TIA
  monitoring study noted global DC capital up **20× since 2016** and average project power rising **50 MW
  (2022) → a projected 450 MW (2025)**, and set out what investors want: local/regional demand, transparent
  regulation, reliable connectivity, and **data-residency compliance**. Recommendations: **modernise the
  data-protection legal framework** (ties to [[tunisia--gov-protect]]), **expand renewables** to power DCs,
  improve international connectivity via **new subsea cables**, and create a **dedicated cloud/DC special
  economic zone** via PPPs. See [[gov.policy]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-15-trendsnafrica-tia-data-centers-strategy]].

## The flagship — a solar-powered AI data centre (Feb 2026)

- **SoleCrypt × Schneider, Bizerte.** **[[solecrypt]]** (Anglo-Tunisian, est. 2022) plans a **20 MW data
  centre in Bizerte**, at the **landing point of three subsea cables including
  [[medusa-submarine-cable|Medusa]]**, powered **100% renewably** from its own **60 MW Tozeur solar site
  (T60)** (base camp operational Nov 2025). Under a **non-binding [[solecrypt-schneider-electric-mou|MoU
  with Schneider Electric]]** for **AI-dedicated, low-water, liquid-cooled** DCs using **Nvidia-co-developed
  reference architectures**; sub-millisecond latency to Europe. CEO Amir Ben Gacem: Tunisia as a
  "Mediterranean super hub for sustainable digital infrastructure." See [[infra.energy]], [[tech.ai]].
  Source: [[2026-02-06-dcd-solecrypt-schneider-bizerte-data-center]].

## The market context (reference — cite, don't absorb)

- **Africa DC market (Arizton, 2026).** Per **[[arizton-advisory|Arizton]]**'s *Africa Data Center Market
  Landscape 2026–2031*, African DC investment reaches **$8.76bn by 2031** ($3.64bn already in 2025; CAGR
  15.76%; colocation 23.74%/yr), with **Tunisia and Morocco** flagged as **emerging North-African markets**
  behind the SA/Kenya/Nigeria/Egypt leaders — Tunisia positioned as a future Europe–Africa–ME
  interconnection point on the strength of its subsea-cable links. Figures are the study's projections,
  cited not promoted. Source: [[2026-07-05-lapresse-tunisia-data-center-ai-market]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the data-centre push is where Tunisia's sovereignty ambition meets its hardest
  preconditions. The pitch is coherent — cables + European proximity + solar energy + a data-residency
  argument for keeping Tunisian (and regional) data hosted in-country — and the state is doing the sensible
  enabling work (SEZ, DP-law modernisation, renewables). But everything material here is **prospective**: a
  20 MW MoU, a solar site at base-camp stage, an "emerging market" label. The same data-protection-law delay
  flagged elsewhere is, notably, on the TIA's *own* list of things to fix to attract this investment — the
  guardrail gap is not just a rights issue but a stated commercial blocker.
