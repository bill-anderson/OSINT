# Nigeria × Innovation Ecosystem

Nigeria is building the physical and institutional scaffolding for a startup/talent economy — free
zones, hubs, and talent pipelines — aiming to lift **ICT to 22% of GDP by 2027** and train **3m tech
talents**, financed by a mix of DFI and bilateral capital.

## Free zones and hubs

- **Itana — Africa's first digital free zone (2025).** In Alaro City (Lekki Free Zone corridor),
  **Itana** creates a digital-first jurisdiction for remote incorporation and global service delivery
  (inspired by Delaware/Dubai Internet City) — $2,000 incorporation, $1,150 renewal. Its first 72,000
  m² district is under construction, backed by a **$100m Africa Finance Corporation** phase-1
  commitment (24/7 gas power, dual fibre); partnered with Future Africa (Iyinoluwa Aboyeji). Source:
  [[2025-06-10-itana-first-digital-free-zone]].
- **Katsina $10bn tech hub (Oct 2025).** One of **ten** state hubs planned; supports the 3m-talent
  programme. Katsina cites its Right-of-Way policy, State Digital Law and Digital Economic Zone. (The
  associated $500m World Bank BRIDGE financing is covered under [[nigeria--infra-connect]].) Source:
  [[2025-10-07-katsina-10bn-tech-hub-worldbank-bridge]].

## Startup and talent programmes

- **JICA Startup Hub ($51.2m, Jun 2025).** [[nitda]] began implementing a **$51.2m** JICA-funded
  programme — an **$11.2m Startup Hub** (NITDA) plus a **$40m** social-startup support environment
  (executed by the sovereign fund **[[nsia]]**), building on the iHatch initiative. Source:
  [[2025-06-19-nitda-jica-startup-hub-agreement]].
- **Doballi talent-export MoU (Apr 2025).** [[nitda]] partnered Afrovision/**Doballi** to connect
  vetted Nigerian professionals to global remote work via an AI platform (waiving the $150 assessment
  fee), routing foreign-currency earnings into Nigerian banks. See [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-03-nitda-doballi-tech-talent-mou]].

## Foreign platforms — the sovereignty framing

- **Huawei innovation centre, Lagos (May 2025).** [[huawei]] (in Nigeria since 1999) opened a Lagos
  innovation centre; Minister Bosun Tijani framed it as **"a catalyst for Nigeria's tech sovereignty"**
  favouring local co-creation over technology consumption — an unusually sovereignty-coded reception
  for a **Chinese** vendor ([[geopol.china]]; cf. the surveillance and Galaxy-Space threads in
  [[nigeria--tech-ai]], [[nigeria--infra-connect]]). Source: [[2025-05-09-huawei-innovation-centre-lagos]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the ecosystem strategy leans on **foreign capital and vendors** (AFC, JICA, Huawei)
  while branding each as capacity-building — the same "build sovereignty on imported foundations"
  tension that runs through Nigeria's AI and cloud stories.
