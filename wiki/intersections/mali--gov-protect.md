# Mali × Data Protection

Mali is the case that cuts against the "law without an authority" pattern: a **2013 statute** with
an **actively enforcing regulator**, expanding territorially, inside a junta-led AES state.

## The authority

Mali's **[[apdp-mali|APDP]]** (Autorité de Protection des Données à caractère Personnel) is an
independent authority established under **Law No. 2013-015 of 21 May 2013**.

## What it actually does (2025)

- Fined a **Bamako clinic 5m FCFA** for obstructing a verification mission.
- Issued **100+ formal notices**.
- Reviewed **~2,000 declaratory files**, including under a **mandatory-lease-declaration decree**.
- Opened a first **regional antenna in Ségou** — DPA territorial expansion.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: an *enforcing* regulator in a state under military government complicates the
  usual reading that political closure and data-protection capacity move together. Contrast
  [[caf--gov-protect]], where a newer and more comprehensive statute has no appointed authority
  at all.
