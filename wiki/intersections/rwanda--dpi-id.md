# Rwanda × Digital Identity

Rwanda is switching its entire population onto a biometric digital identity, the **e-Indangamuntu**, retiring legacy cards issued since 1962 and folding civil registration and citizen-controlled data consent into a single statutory framework.

## What we know

[[nida-rwanda]] is switching nationwide to a biometric digital ID (**e-Indangamuntu**),
adding an **iris scan** to existing fingerprint + facial biometrics and integrating
Rwanda's **CRVS**. The **Single Digital Identification Law** bars any citizen from holding two
government identities; legacy cards (issued since 1962) become invalid, replaced by
**June 2027** (sunset clause). Consent runs via a digital wallet and the **eRainbow**
platform, where citizens see which institutions accessed their data and can revoke access
(self-consent from age 16). As of 2026-03-16 NIDA had registered **2,373,854 of ~14m** target
(~40,000/day). See [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]].

## Rollout chronology (SDIS)

The scheme sits within the **Single Digital Identification System (SDIS)**, begun in 2023
by legislative amendment; bids for the core were received **May 2024** (an AMBAS/ABIS
multimodal biometric authentication system). Nationwide **registration launched 7 Aug 2025**
(from the Southern Province, 28 Oct 2025); biometric capture (face, **ten fingerprints**,
iris) from ~mid-Sept 2025; the Kigali drive opened **7 Feb 2026** (7-week window). Completed
enrolments climbed **>300,000 (Nov 2025) → ~1.49m (with ~1.69m pre-enrolled, 7 Feb 2026) →
2,373,854 (16 Mar 2026) → 4.4m (9 Jun 2026)**, moving province by province (Eastern → Northern
→ Western). After the mobile campaign, registration continues only at **sector offices**, where
biometric kits are being **permanently deployed**. Cost is reported as **~$38m** (SDIS, Aug
2025; ~$8.5m for FY25–26 enrolment, of Rwf54.2bn total) or **~$57m** as the digital-ID
component of the **Rwanda Digital Acceleration Project (RDAP)** (Feb 2026) — possibly different
scopes. Non-citizens (residents, refugees, asylum seekers, migrants, stateless persons) are
included — the **Mahama camp** (~70,000, mostly Burundian/Congolese) is being enrolled, and the
Directorate General of Immigration ran the Kigali foreigner/refugee drive (7–15 Feb 2026). See
[[include.access]]. Sources: [[2025-05-23-digital-id-85m-budget-biometricupdate]],

## Credential structure and the 2027 switchover

By the **June 2027** sunset the SDID becomes the **primary authentication tool** across banking,
healthcare, telecoms and government — online *and* offline — and those without it "could struggle
to access services" (NIDA DG Josephine Mukesha). The credential has **three components**: a
**Single Digital Identification Number (SDIN)** (unique, for life), a **transaction-specific
token** derived from it, and a **physical SDID card with a QR code** readable only after
authentication. Sources: [[2026-03-26-govt-sets-2027-deadline-digital-id-newtimes]],
[[2026-03-30-races-to-meet-2027-deadline-biometricupdate]].

## The legacy flaw the design fixes

The **2025 Auditor-General's report** found the old system **issued a new 16-digit NIN on
every correction or replacement**, so one person could accumulate multiple NINs — breaking
banking, land, SIM, voting and pension systems that assume one number per person. SDIS's
**"one unique number for life"** (with ABIS deduplication) is the intended remedy; the AG
also recommended separating the identity number from the physical card. See
[[dpi.registry]].

## SIM linkage

Under **[[rwanda-sim-card-registration-regulation-2024]]** ([[rura-rwanda]], Aug 2024, with
a Sept-2024 pilot), SIM registration is **verified against NIDA's biometric database** and
each SIM bound to the holder's unique ID number — anti-fraud (mobile-money scams) and
national security. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.legislate]].

## Privacy by design — the 1994 shadow

The design's consent-and-minimisation architecture is read locally as a deliberate answer to
history: in 1994 the ethnicity field on the Belgian-legacy paper ID card ("Tutsi") functioned, as
the *Rwanda Law Journal* (Mar 2026) put it, as "an effective death sentence." Rwanda is thus
attempting a national digital ID **with privacy and consent at its core** — data-minimisation,
citizen-controlled disclosure via the wallet/eRainbow layer — "not as an afterthought but as a
firewall against history repeating itself." The load-bearing safeguard is **DPO independence**:
the guarantee only holds if the consent layer cannot be quietly overridden by the state that
built it (see [[gov.protect]]). Source: [[2026-05-26-new-digital-id-you-control-everything-ktpress]].
