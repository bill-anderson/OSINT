# Rwanda × Digital Identity

Rwanda is switching its entire population onto a biometric digital identity, the **e-Indangamuntu**, retiring legacy cards issued since 1962 and folding civil registration and citizen-controlled data consent into a single statutory framework.

## What we know

[[nida-rwanda]] is switching nationwide to a biometric digital ID (**e-Indangamuntu**),
adding an **iris scan** to existing fingerprint + facial biometrics and integrating
Rwanda's **CRVS**. The **Single Digital Identification Law** bars any citizen from holding two
government identities; legacy cards (issued since 1962) become invalid, replaced by
**June 2027** (sunset clause). Consent runs via a digital wallet and the **eRainbow**
platform, where citizens see which institutions accessed their data and can revoke access
(self-consent from age 16). As of 2026-03-16 NIDA had registered **2,373,854 of ~14m** target
(~40,000/day). See [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]].

## Rollout chronology (SDIS)

The scheme sits within the **Single Digital Identification System (SDIS)**, begun in 2023
by legislative amendment; bids for the core were received **May 2024**. Nationwide
**registration launched 7 Aug 2025**; biometric capture (face, iris, fingerprints) from
~mid-Sept 2025. Completed enrolments climbed **>300,000 (Nov 2025) → ~1.5m (10 Feb 2026,
with ~1.69m more pending, many children) → 2,373,854 (16 Mar 2026)**. Cost is reported as
**~$38m** (SDIS, Aug 2025; ~$8.5m for FY25–26 enrolment) or **~$57m** as the digital-ID
component of the **Rwanda Digital Acceleration Project (RDAP)** (Feb 2026) — possibly
different scopes. Non-citizens (residents, refugees, asylum seekers, migrants, stateless
persons) are included, with the **Directorate General of Immigration** running the Kigali
foreigner/refugee drive (7–15 Feb 2026, eight sector offices). See [[include.access]].

## The legacy flaw the design fixes

The **2025 Auditor-General's report** found the old system **issued a new 16-digit NIN on
every correction or replacement**, so one person could accumulate multiple NINs — breaking
banking, land, SIM, voting and pension systems that assume one number per person. SDIS's
**"one unique number for life"** (with ABIS deduplication) is the intended remedy; the AG
also recommended separating the identity number from the physical card. See
[[dpi.registry]].

## SIM linkage

Under **[[rwanda-sim-card-registration-regulation-2024]]** ([[rura-rwanda]], Aug 2024, with
a Sept-2024 pilot), SIM registration is **verified against NIDA's biometric database** and
each SIM bound to the holder's unique ID number — anti-fraud (mobile-money scams) and
national security. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.legislate]].
