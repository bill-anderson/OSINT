# Kenya × Data Protection

Kenya's data-protection regime is being **stress-tested by a US health deal**: a bilateral funding
framework whose data-sharing terms collide with Kenya's own Data Protection Act — and the courts,
not the executive, have become the check.

## What we know (as of 2026-07)

- **The deal.** Kenya was the **first signatory** of the **[[america-first-global-health-strategy]]**:
  a **5-year, $2.5bn** US–Kenya Health Cooperation Framework signed by US Sec State Rubio and PCS
  Mudavadi at the State Department on **2025-12-04** ([[us-kenya-health-framework]]). Its
  **Data-Sharing Agreement** institutionalises US/contractor access to Kenyan health, genomic and
  pathogen data and national health-information systems — with the wider AFGHS reportedly seeking
  **25-year** data access in exchange for 5-year assistance.
- **The legal collision.** The framework conflicts with the **Kenya Data Protection Act 2019**, the
  **Digital Health Act 2023** (health-data localization) and the Health Act. Senator **Okiya
  Omtatah** petitioned (filed **2025-12-10**); the High Court issued an injunction **2025-12-19**,
  later **extended to the entire agreement** — freezing it. Source:
  [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]]
  (Carnegie; [[jane-munga]] & Rose Mosero).
- **Regulator and precedent.** The **[[odpc-kenya]]** (Office of the Data Protection Commissioner,
  under the DPA 2019) is the statutory guardrail; its **Worldcoin** precedent — 2023 suspension and
  a 2025 court order to delete ~300,000 Kenyans' biometric data — shows the enforcement teeth that
  the health deal now tests. Broader canvas:
  [[2026-03-19 African Digital Health Data Is a Condition of Global Health Funding]].
- **Litigation moved — Court of Appeal reversed the suspension (2026-06).** Beyond the Omtatah
  petition, the **[[consumer-federation-of-kenya]]** (COFEK) petitioned on Data Protection Act /
  Digital Health Act grounds and won a **High Court suspension** of the data-transfer component — which
  the **Court of Appeal then reversed** (case continuing). Nagadya frames the fight as data
  sovereignty in action within an African data-localisation stack ([[au-data-policy-framework]] 2022,
  Kenya Cloud Policy 2025, Ghana's Draft Data Protection Bill 2025). Munyua's structural read (Part V,
  2025-12-15) casts the framework as "the new scramble" — 25-yr data access for 5-yr funding.
  Sources:
  [[2026-06-08 Kenyan Citizens Took A $2.5 Billion Deal With America To Court To Fight For Their Health Data]],
  [[2025-12-15 THE GATEKEEPERS PART V The New Maps of Africa]].
- **The state as data-seller (Mutiso, 2026-06).** Alongside protecting health data, Kenya proposes to
  **monetise** public data: Rose Mutiso reads the **Draft National Data Governance Policy (May 2026)**
  as a plumbing/interoperability reform with a small data-marketplace ([[kenya-data-marketplace]])
  component, flagging **weak anonymisation**, non-consent via mandatory **eCitizen**, and **EU/German
  TA** whose own GDPR/Data-Governance-Act rules would bar the same revenue-maximising reuse at home.
  See [[geopol.eu]], [[data.open]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-08 Kenya Wants to Sell Your Data. But It's Complicated]].
- **Facial-recognition surveillance without safeguards (Nagadya, 2026-06).** Kenya's FR spend is put
  at **$219m** — among 11 African countries running such systems with no adequate legal framework
  (IDS, March 2026) — the biometric-surveillance flank of the same data-protection gap. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-15 If The Technology Can't See Us Accurately, We Build The Rules That Make It]].
- **Contrast — data as a tax asset.** The same rails that make health data extractable also let the
  state see more: the **[[kenya-revenue-authority]]** launched a **WhatsApp tax-filing chatbot**
  (Kenya has >22m registered taxpayers but only ~7m active filers), traceability the informal
  economy previously escaped. Source:
  [[2026-04-02 WhatsApp tax filing set to pull millions into Kenya's tax net]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: who controls Kenyan citizens' most sensitive data — a Kenyan regulator under
  Kenyan law, or a foreign funder's contractors under a bilateral MoU. The courts have, for now,
  answered "not without the DPA."
- `colonialism`: health funding conditioned on long-horizon specimen/data access reframes aid as a
  data-extraction bargain (cf. [[XAF]] AFGHS coverage and Zimbabwe's rejection).

## ODPC enforcement & the cyber-agency turn (2025-26)

Beyond the health-deal stress test, Kenya's data-governance machinery moved on enforcement and
institutionalisation through 2025-26:

- **ODPC enforcement wave.** [[gov.protect]] Commissioner **[[immaculate-kassait]]** launched a **KES
  12.6bn** ODPC five-year privacy plan (2025-07); opened **suo moto investigations into the Ray-Ban Meta
  AI glasses** (mass-surveillance / non-consensual recording / Meta-AI training data, 150+ signatories —
  Kenya joining UK/US scrutiny, 2026-03); and, with ICT CS **[[william-kabogo]]**, threatened **185
  betting/gaming firms** holding **children's data** with shutdown for non-registration (only 15 of 224
  fully registered as controllers+processors, 2026-05). A Data Privacy Conference 2026 op-ed framed
  **public trust** as decisive for Kenya's digital future. Sources:
  [[2025-07-05-odpc-sh126bn-privacy-plan-standard]], [[2026-03-31-ray-ban-meta-ai-glasses-probe-nation]],
  [[2026-05-13-kabogo-betting-firms-children-data-nation]], [[2026-01-31-public-trust-data-governance-standard]].
- **Cyber institutionalisation.** [[infra.cybersec]] After hackers **defaced president.go.ke** and
  multiple ministry sites (2025-11), Kenya stood up a central **[[national-cyber-security-agency-kenya|National
  Cyber Security Agency]]** (2026-06) — amid oversight concerns over its wide security-chief mandate. A new
  **Access to Information (Amendment) Bill** would criminalise sharing **classified information via
  personal WhatsApp/email** (four classification tiers; up to KES 1m fine / 6 months), a speech/privacy
  tension. Sources: [[2025-11-17-hackers-government-websites-presidency-nation]],
  [[2026-07-02-state-secrets-whatsapp-bill-nation]].
