# Rwanda × Data Exchange & DPI Strategy

Rwanda's decade of digitisation produced strong but **siloed** systems; the current push is to bind
them into an interoperable **data-exchange layer** under a national DPI strategy — the load-bearing
gap the country's own analysts name most often.

## The DPI strategy

- **National DPI strategy launched (Mar 2026).** At Rwanda's **first DPI Day** (9 Mar 2026, run by
  **[[rwanda-information-society-authority]]** with Access to Finance Rwanda and MINICT), the
  government unveiled the **[[rwanda-dpi-strategy]]** — a shared foundation of interoperable identity,
  payments and data-exchange layers as public goods. It marks progress since the **Centre of Digital
  Public Infrastructure** (est. 2025), explicitly to fix platforms "developed independently ... not
  designed to communicate." It also underpins Rwanda's AI ambitions ([[tech.ai]]). Source:
  [[2026-03-11-dpi-strategy-launch-newtimes]].

## The national data-sharing platform

- **Build under way, live 2026.** RISA is standing up the **national data-sharing platform** (the
  technical side of the [[rwanda-national-data-sharing-policy]]), phased for a 2025–2026 timeline. RISA
  CTO Roger Mizero described onboarding an experienced data-exchange firm **paired with internal
  experts to ensure knowledge transfer and ownership** — a sovereignty-conscious delivery model.
  Source: [[2025-09-27-data-sharing-platform-2026-newtimes]].

## RDAP — the financing spine

- **Rwanda Digital Acceleration Project ($200m, World Bank).** The **RDAP** (Rwf280bn / $200m,
  2022–2026) funds broadband, digital public services and inclusion across health, education and
  agriculture — **55% complete** (Jul 2025, per RISA CEO Innocent Muhizi) — including smart-device
  affordability, digital-literacy and connectivity for unconnected schools/hospitals/markets. It is
  the vehicle behind the ID/CRVS build ([[rwanda--dpi-id]]) and much of the exchange layer. See
  [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2025-07-09-rdap-fast-track-2026-newtimes]].

## The interoperability gap — a candid comparison

- A KT Press analysis contrasts Rwanda with **Tanzania**, whose e-GA runs **200+ government systems
  on one central backbone**. Rwanda has "strong frameworks and high ambition" (the data-governance
  unit, the 2025–2029 data-sharing platform) but faces **legal and technical silos** and uneven
  implementation across agencies — the lesson being that digital maturity is "institutional discipline"
  as much as technology. See [[gov.regional]], [[TZA]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-09-tanzania-egovernment-model-vs-rwanda-ktpress]].

## Data governance — accountability enforced

- **"Compliance is Beyond Registration" (2026).** With the Data Protection Act's grace period long
  expired (Oct 2023), the **National Cyber Security Authority (NCSA)** — Rwanda's data-protection
  supervisory authority — declared 2026 the **year of accountability**: registered controllers/
  processors must now *prove* compliance through documented policies and audits, and the DPA's
  **extraterritorial** scope reaches any global platform processing Rwandans' data. 100+ public-sector
  DPOs trained. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-26-year-of-accountability-data-controllers-newtimes]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the exchange layer is where Rwanda's DPI either coheres or fragments. The
  "pair-a-vendor-with-internal-experts" delivery rule and the open-by-default steward model are
  deliberate hedges against dependence — but the candidly-acknowledged silos show sovereignty of
  design does not yet guarantee interoperability in practice.
