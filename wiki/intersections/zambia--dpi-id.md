# Zambia × Digital Identity

Zambia's foundational-ID transition is the most developed — and most analytically
loaded — thread in the DZAP appraisal.

## What we know (as of 2025-09-11)

- **Legacy.** The paper **National Registration Card (NRC)**, introduced before
  independence in 1964, covered ~**94% of adults** by 2021. **INRIS** has since
  registered ~**15% of adults** biometrically.
- **The plan.** A **homegrown foundational ID**, built on **open standards** and
  existing investments, to close the ~85% coverage gap — explicitly designed so the
  Government can "**manage its infrastructure independently and reduce reliance on
  external vendors**," prioritising privacy, security and interoperability.
- **Data minimisation.** MoHAIS collects only name, DOB, address, gender, 10
  fingerprints and a photo — **no ethnic or sensitive data**. Enrolment is free;
  registration to expand via post offices.
- **Support.** World Bank **ID4D** technical assistance and Principles, coordinated
  with UNDP and UNHCR; credential options under evaluation (QR, plastic, polycarbonate,
  paper-based with digital features).
- **Stack fit.** The ID anchors [[dpi.exchange]] (ZamConnect), [[dpi.pay]] (ZamPay) and
  [[dpi.govtech]] (ZamPortal).

## Procuring the integrator (2026-03)

- **The procurement.** [[smart-zambia-institute]] is procuring an international **system
  integrator** for a **[[mosip]]**-based national ID integrated with civil registration —
  insisting the application layer be **built by Zambians on open standards** to avoid lock-in,
  and targeting full rollout and **80% of citizens** in the digital economy by **end-2026**.
- **Financing and South–South input.** Financed via [[dzap]] (**>$100m** World Bank IDA); an
  **Ethiopia MoU** draws on **Fayda**. This operationalises the open-standards, vendor-
  independence thread above ([[gov.standards]]). Sources:
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]],
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia seeks partner for national digital ID system]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: a deliberate move to vendor-independent, state-run identity
  infrastructure on open standards.
- `colonialism`: that sovereignty agenda is **financed and technically assisted from
  outside** ([[world-bank]] IDA grant via [[dzap]]; ID4D/UNDP/UNHCR). The interesting
  question for Data Landscapers is whether externally-scaffolded self-reliance
  actually ends dependence or reshapes it.
