# Zambia × Digital Identity

Zambia's foundational-ID transition is the most developed — and most analytically
loaded — thread in the DZAP appraisal.

## What we know (as of 2025-09-11)

- **Legacy.** The paper **National Registration Card (NRC)**, introduced before
  independence in 1964, covered ~**94% of adults** by 2021. **INRIS** has since
  registered ~**15% of adults** biometrically.
- **The plan.** A **homegrown foundational ID**, built on **open standards** and
  existing investments, to close the ~85% coverage gap — explicitly designed so the
  Government can "**manage its infrastructure independently and reduce reliance on
  external vendors**," prioritising privacy, security and interoperability.
- **Data minimisation.** MoHAIS collects only name, DOB, address, gender, 10
  fingerprints and a photo — **no ethnic or sensitive data**. Enrolment is free;
  registration to expand via post offices.
- **Support.** World Bank **ID4D** technical assistance and Principles, coordinated
  with UNDP and UNHCR; credential options under evaluation (QR, plastic, polycarbonate,
  paper-based with digital features).
- **Stack fit.** The ID anchors [[dpi.exchange]] (ZamConnect), [[dpi.pay]] (ZamPay) and
  [[dpi.govtech]] (ZamPortal).

## The MOSIP thread (2025-01 →)

*Correction, 2026-07-17: this section previously opened at the March-2026 integrator procurement.
It was missing its own prehistory — MOSIP work was already live fourteen months earlier.*

- **It starts in January 2025, not March 2026.** [[smart-zambia-institute]] e-government
  developers had **completed MOSIP training** by **2025-01-17**, delivered by **[[ihm-southern-africa]]**'s
  software team, aimed at **integrating MOSIP with the [[smartcare-pro]] EHR** to power eKYC and
  establish what SZI called a "central source of truth". Two details matter: the architecture
  **allows integration with existing biometric databases without full system replacement**, and the
  training was explicitly aimed at **letting Zambian developers independently manage and maintain the
  platform** — the vendor-independence thread in practice, not just in procurement language. Source:
  [[2025-01-17 Zambia Advances Digital ID System with MOSIP Training for Healthcare Integration]].
- **The procurement (2026-03).** [[smart-zambia-institute]] is procuring an international **system
  integrator** for a **[[mosip]]**-based national ID integrated with civil registration —
  insisting the application layer be **built by Zambians on open standards** to avoid lock-in,
  and targeting full rollout and **80% of citizens** in the digital economy by **end-2026**.
- **Financing and South–South input.** Financed via [[dzap]] (**$100m** World Bank IDA grant plus
  **$20m** unguaranteed commercial = **$120m** total, per the project documents — the figures
  reconcile and are not competing). The **Ethiopia MoU** drawing on **[[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]**
  is now dated and named: signed by **Percy Chinyama** (SZI) and **Yodahe Zemichael** (Executive
  Director, Ethiopia's NIDP), with Chinyama saying Zambia wants Ethiopia's model **because it is
  domestically developed** (as of 2025-10-03). This operationalises the open-standards,
  vendor-independence thread above ([[gov.standards]]). Sources:
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]],
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia seeks partner for national digital ID system]],
  [[2025-10-03 Zambia plans domestic digital ID development with help from international partners]],
  [[2025-01-20 PID Appraisal Stage - Digital Zambia Acceleration Project (P505094)]].
- **The rollout date is contested between agencies.** SMART Zambia's **Percy Chinyama** says digital
  ID cards by **end-2026**; the **Registrar General, [[davies-chikalanga]]** — who actually runs
  registration — said **end-2027** (2025-06-12). Held material adds that biometric NRC issuance comes
  only **after the 2026 General Elections**. Both dated and attributed; neither adopted. See
  `reviews/contradictions/open/zambia-digital-id-target`.
- **Enrolment is contested too, and it moves the wrong way.** [[inris]] enrolment is reported at
  **1.5 million** (UNDP, 2024-09), **~1.6 million** implied by the DZAP PAD (~15% of adults,
  2025-09), and **~1.3 million** (ZNBC quoting MoHAIS, 2025-12) — a later source with a lower figure,
  which is not supersession. See `reviews/contradictions/open/zambia-inris-enrolment`.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: a deliberate move to vendor-independent, state-run identity
  infrastructure on open standards.
- `colonialism`: that sovereignty agenda is **financed and technically assisted from
  outside** ([[world-bank]] IDA grant via [[dzap]]; ID4D/UNDP/UNHCR). The interesting
  question for Data Landscapers is whether externally-scaffolded self-reliance
  actually ends dependence or reshapes it.
