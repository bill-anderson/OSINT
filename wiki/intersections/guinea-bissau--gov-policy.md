# Guinea-Bissau × Digital governance & law

Through 2025–26 Guinea-Bissau assembled a **digital-governance and legislative spine** that had been
almost entirely absent — moving from a single national strategy ([[entd-gw|ENTD.GW 2025-2030]]) to a
cluster of concrete instruments: data-governance and data-protection policy, a cybersecurity strategy
and agency, an interoperability regulation, a revised telecom law, and — under the post-coup
transition — a contested digital-speech law. Much of this rests on donor scaffolding
([[wardip|WARDIP]]/[[world-bank]], UNDP) and much remains at draft-decree stage; the currency of each
instrument's status should be re-checked as it moves through the transitional institutions.

## What we know

- **[[GNB]] ENTD.GW national strategy launched (2025-01-29).** The National Digital Transformation
  Strategy 2025–2030 was launched (PM Rui Duarte Barros; Minister Marciano Silva Barbeiro; UNDP) — 10
  pillars including data, governance, trust and public services. Entity: [[entd-gw]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-29 Guinea-Bissau launches ENTD.GW national digital transformation strategy (ANG)]].
- **[[GNB]] Cybersecurity study → National Cybersecurity & Data Protection Agency (2025-01-22).**
  [[infra.cybersec]] [[gov.protect]] Government presented the results of a WARDIP/NRD cybersecurity
  feasibility study and committed to **create a National Cybersecurity and Data Protection Agency**,
  framing cybersecurity as central to "digital sovereignty" and data protection. Source:
  [[2025-01-22 Guinea-Bissau presents cybersecurity study, plans National Cybersecurity and Data Protection Agency]].
- **[[GNB]] Three digital decrees approved (2025-07-10).** [[dpi.exchange]] [[gov.protect]] The Council
  of Ministers approved draft decrees on the **Interoperability of Public/Private Digital Systems and
  Platforms**, a **National Data Governance and Protection Policy**, and a **National Cybersecurity
  Strategy** — the study above turned into instruments. These are GNB's first substantive
  data-governance/protection and interoperability instruments on file. Source:
  [[2025-07-10 Guinea-Bissau Council of Ministers approves three digital decrees (interoperability, data governance, cybersecurity)]].
- **[[GNB]] New Electronic Communications Law (transition-era, 2025/26).** [[gov.legislate]] The Council
  of Ministers approved a new Electronic Communications Law revising the outdated 2010 telecom regime.
  Connectivity detail: [[guinea-bissau--infra-connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Guinea-Bissau Council of Ministers approves new Electronic Communications Law]].
- **[[GNB]] Digital Integrity / online-falsehoods Law (2026-05-23).** [[gov.legislate]] `sovereignty`
  The transitional parliament (**Conselho Nacional de Transição, CNT** — acting after the disputed 26
  Nov 2025 events) unanimously passed a **Law on the Dissemination of False Information on the
  Internet**, framed by spokesman Fernando Vaz as targeting those who "spread hate" and act "against the
  State," with enforcement mechanisms unstated — a digital-speech law with a clear repression angle
  amid political turmoil. Source:
  [[2026-05-23 Guinea-Bissau CNT passes Digital Integrity (online falsehoods) Law]].
