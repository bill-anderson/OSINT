# Mozambique × Data centres & cloud

Mozambique is building **domestic hosting capacity and a data-localisation regime** in parallel — new
data-centre/cloud regulation with a sanctioning regime, and a five-year plan for local data centres to keep
sensitive (esp. financial) data in-country.

## What we know

- **Data-centre & cloud regulation (2025-03).** [[gov.legislate]] **[[intic-mozambique|INTIC]]** put a
  regulation for **building/operating data centres and licensing cloud services** into public consultation
  — including a **sanctioning regime**, mandatory insurance for cybersecurity, infrastructure rules and
  construction incentives — informed by Saudi Arabia, Mauritius and Egypt precedents. Source:
  [[2025-03-21-regulation-data-centres-cloud-includes-sanctions-clubofmozambique]].
- **Vodacom data centre — "17 data centres" (2025-03).** [[infra.store]] PM **Benvinda Levi** inaugurated
  **[[vodacom]]** Mozambique's new data centre, noting the country had **17 data centres (2025-03)** and a pending
  cybersecurity-legislation review — "fundamental" for centralising and securely storing data. (Complements
  the held Matola $25m Tier-3 Vodacom DC.) Source:
  [[2025-03-29-pm-opens-vodacom-data-centre-clubofmozambique]].
- **Local data centres + cloud regulation (2025-12).** [[sovereignty]] [[dpi.pay]] Minister **Muchanga**
  announced a **five-year plan to build domestic data centres** securely hosting cloud for **banks and
  institutions**, plus an updated regulatory framework replacing current restrictions — framing cloud as a
  cost/inclusion tool and encouraging bank–fintech collaboration (blockchain/digital assets), matched with
  cybersecurity/data-protection safeguards. Source:
  [[2025-12-04-build-local-data-centres-cloud-regulations-techafricanews]].
