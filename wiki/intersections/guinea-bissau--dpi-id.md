# Guinea-Bissau × Digital Identity

Guinea-Bissau is building its identity stack from a very weak base — **birth registration below 50%**
(~46% of under-fives) — largely through donor-funded projects. The civil registry is being digitized
via **[[sirec]]** and a UNDP "Building National ID" project (scanning the paper registry books), while
a functional **biometric national ID** is produced and decentralized through **[[semlex]]**'s network
of **18 production centres**. Regionally, GNB has already operationalized the **[[enbic|ECOWAS biometric
ID]]** for cross-border movement — running ahead of its domestic civil-registration completeness.

## What we know

- **[[GNB]] SIREC + civil-registry digitization (2024-25).** [[dpi.registry]] The MoJ Civil
  Registration Office (Raul Gomes) is digitizing births/deaths/marriages via SIREC, with UNDP officer
  training and a "Building National ID" project (Swiss-financed) recovering and digitizing the registry
  books; integration with health and education is being explored. Sources:
  [[2025-06-10 Guinea-Bissau deploys efforts to digitize birth registration, national ID systems]],
  [[2024-01-01 Building a National Identification System - Scanning and Digitizing the Civil Registry Books in Guinea-Bissau]].
- **[[GNB]] Semlex decentralized ID production (2026-01).** [[dpi.id]] A new biometric-ID production
  centre opened in Safim (Biombo), the third in the region and part of 18 centres nationwide — Semlex
  supplying the biometric national ID. Source: [[2026-02-11 Semlex Guinea-Bissau strengthens access to biometric identification]].
- **[[GNB]] ENBIC operationalized (2025).** [[gov.regional]] GNB is one of six ECOWAS states to have
  fully operationalized the **[[enbic]]** cross-border biometric ID — regional recognition ahead of
  domestic registration completeness. Source: [[2025-06-10 Guinea-Bissau deploys efforts to digitize birth registration, national ID systems]].
