# Algeria × AI

- **National AI Strategy.** Adopted by the **National AI Council** under Professor **Merouane Debbah** (Dec 2024); six pillars (research, talent, hardware/infrastructure, investment/ecosystem, data protection/regulation, sectoral AI). PM **Sifi Ghrieb** chaired a **government review of the strategy's deployment plan (25 May 2026)**, restating three structuring levers — **data, digital infrastructure, human skills** — ahead of Council-of-Ministers adoption. An **ethical-AI law** is in preparation (2025-11). See [[algeria-national-ai-strategy]].
- **Economic target.** **AI = 7% of GDP by 2027**; AI market projected **$498.9m (2025) → $1.69bn (2030)** (CAGR ~27.7%). A **New Lines Institute** report (2026-05) named Algeria the state **most likely to lead AI in North Africa**, citing **57,000+ students across 74 AI master's programmes in 52 universities** and researchers in the global top 2% for scientific publishing.
- **Compute base.** The state-owned **Oran AI supercomputing / data centre** (ground broken Mar 2025; GPU/HPC for researchers, universities, startups, public institutions) anchors sovereign AI capacity — reinforced by mandatory data localisation and a protected (hyperscaler-free) cloud market. **[[huawei]] AI/GPU & digital-economy cooperation** talks (2026-04); **Djezzy's sovereign AI cloud [[aventurecloudz]]** (2026-04); a first **virtual-education AI innovation centre** (2026-06); a national **AI vocational-training programme** (12-week, incubator, 2026-04).
- **Governance fit.** AI is a pillar of **[[digital-algeria-2030]]** and increasingly framed as a public-administration tool (see [[algeria--dpi-govtech]], [[algeria--tech-innovate]]).
