# Libya × AI

Libya's AI push is **strategy-and-skills-first**, led (GNU/Tripoli-side) by the **[[gia-libya|GIA]]** and a
new Minister of State for the Digital Economy (**[[ziad-al-hajjaji]]**), with a rare domestic
generative-AI milestone and foreign (India, UN) capacity partnerships — but resting on a **legislative
vacuum** (no data-protection law) the strategy itself flags.

## What we know

- **National AI Strategy 2025–2030 (2025-10).** [[gov.policy]] [[sovereignty]] The GIA published the
  **National Strategy for AI 2025–2030** (prepared with **ESCWA**), calling it the state's "highest
  reference strategy": data-science/AI training, ethical-AI legislation, AI in health/education/energy,
  and university R&D. It notably targets a **data-protection law by 2026** to fill Libya's legislative
  vacuum. *(Structural note: reportedly superseded/renamed as a 2026–2030 strategy formally adopted
  2026-06-01 under PM Dbeibeh — off-source, see gaps.)* Source:
  [[2025-10-30-national-ai-strategy-2025-2030-published-libyaherald]].
- **LIBIGPT — first domestic generative AI (2025-10).** [[tech.innovate]] Libya launched **[[libigpt|LIBIGPT]]**,
  described as the nation's first generative-AI system (pre-launch Aug 2025) — a rare domestic
  generative-AI milestone. Source: [[2025-10-28-libigpt-first-generative-ai-launched-libyaherald]]
  *(thin excerpt — full clip recommended)*.
- **Skills partnerships — India + UNDP (2025-26).** [[capacity.training]] Libya agreed **Indian
  cooperation to qualify national cadres in quantum computing and AI** (Feb 2026); and the Ministry of
  Planning launched a **professional-masters institute in AI and cybersecurity** with **[[undp]]** (tied
  to "Libya Vision 2050"). Sources: [[2026-02-01-libya-india-ai-quantum-upskilling-libyaherald]],
  [[2025-11-01-planning-ministry-ai-cybersecurity-institute-alwasat]].
