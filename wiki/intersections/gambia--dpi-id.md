# The Gambia × Digital Identity

The Gambia moved in 18 months from a foreign National-ID concession to a **locally-hosted, sovereign
identity system** — the flagship of its DPI ambition.

- **Policy origin (2025-06).** Barrow's 2025 State of the Nation set out to **unify ID cards, the eCRVS civil
  registry and the passport** into a locally-owned digital ID, **replacing the National-ID concession contract**.
- **The contract (2026-01).** The **[[gambia-national-id-contract|Margins/MIDSA DBFOT contract]]** was signed
  (Ministry of Interior) for a **secure, scalable, ICAO-compliant** national ID; new terms include **10-year
  card validity**, **free replacement** of all valid cards, an **8-year term**, a **70/30 revenue share**, and a
  **phase-2 integration of security/economic IT systems** plus a data centre.
- **The launch (2026-07).** The **[[nims-gambia|NIMS]]** went live — instant **GAM ID**, President Barrow first
  enrollee, on the new **National Data Centre (Abuko)**; nationwide enrolment from **4 Aug 2026**.
- **Controversy.** Ahead of Dec-2026 polls, the opposition **UDP alleged "unlawful" issuance of ID cards
  abroad** (Mauritania, Congo, Togo) — an election-integrity concern (reported allegation).
