# Burkina Faso × Digital Identity

Burkina Faso's identity project runs on **two tracks that meet in the CIB-AES**: a World Bank-financed foundational-ID drive (WURI, civil registration) built for regional integration and inclusion, and a **junta-era pivot to the Alliance of Sahel States** — the same biometric card reframed as a statement of post-ECOWAS sovereignty. Identity here is where donor DPI and Sahelian geopolitics collide.

## What we know

**The AES biometric card (2025–26).** **[[oni-burkina-faso]]** unveiled the **[[cib-aes]]** (Carte d'Identité Biométrique de l'AES) on **1 December 2025** (decree 6 Nov 2025) — the **5th generation** of Burkinabè ID, replacing the CNIB. It is deliverable from **age 5**, mandatory from **age 13**, costs **3,500 FCFA**, is polycarbonate and ICAO-aligned, and carries both the national flag and the **[[alliance-of-sahel-states]]** logo (bilingual "AES Identity Card"). Burkina was the **first** of the three AES states to adopt the common credential, alongside the AES common biometric passport (launched Jan 2025) — "harmonised at AES level while keeping national sovereignty."

**The CRVS foundation.** Beneath the card sits **[[veneem]]**, the digital civil-registration platform capturing births and civil-status events at their point of occurrence, tied to a **unique identifier** — the registry layer feeding foundational ID.

**The donor track.** Foundational-ID enrolment runs through the **[[wuri-programme]]** (West Africa Unique Identification, WB, with Benin/Togo/Niger) and is a named deliverable of **[[pactdigital]]** and the 2025 digital agenda (large-scale WURI population enrolment). The result is one biometric system serving two masters: World Bank inclusion goals and AES sovereignty signalling.
