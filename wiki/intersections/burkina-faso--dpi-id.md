# Burkina Faso × Digital Identity

Burkina Faso's identity project runs on **two tracks that meet in the CIB-AES**: a World Bank-financed foundational-ID drive (WURI, civil registration) built for regional integration and inclusion, and a **junta-era pivot to the Alliance of Sahel States** — the same biometric card reframed as a statement of post-ECOWAS sovereignty. Identity here is where donor DPI and Sahelian geopolitics collide.

## What we know

**The AES biometric card (2025–26).** **[[oni-burkina-faso]]** unveiled the **[[cib-aes]]** (Carte d'Identité Biométrique de l'AES) on **1 December 2025** (decree 6 Nov 2025) — the **5th generation** of Burkinabè ID, replacing the CNIB. It is deliverable from **age 5**, mandatory from **age 13**, costs **3,500 FCFA**, is polycarbonate and ICAO-aligned, and carries both the national flag and the **[[alliance-of-sahel-states]]** logo (bilingual "AES Identity Card"). Burkina was the **first** of the three AES states to adopt the common credential, alongside the AES common biometric passport (launched Jan 2025) — "harmonised at AES level while keeping national sovereignty."

**A bloc-wide credential — Niger follows.** [[NER]] Niger launched the same AES biometric ID on **27 Mar 2026** (President Tiani first enrollee), the **second** AES state after Burkina — confirming the CIB-AES is a **bloc-wide card with cross-border chip verification and passport-free AES travel**. Burkina also ran a **cross-border diaspora enrolment pilot in Bamako** (mobile kits, near-real-time production). The theme of the 2025 Journée de l'identité was explicitly **AES ID and travel-document harmonisation** — the post-ECOWAS identity architecture. Sources: [[2026-03-30-niger-biometric-id-aes-sahel-sovereignty]], [[2025-09-17-journee-internationale-identite-systeme-identification]].

**The CRVS foundation, quantified.** Beneath the card sits **[[veneem]]**, capturing births and civil-status events at their point of occurrence, tied to a **unique identifier**. The national civil-registration strategy (SNEC) reported **64.11%** physical execution in 2024 — **3,391,907 acts digitised across 54 communes**, 145,655 birth certificates issued — and by end-2025 **30 communes held fully digitised registers**, with **121,000 civil-status acts issued to internally displaced persons** (nearly double the 65,000 target). A **Registre Social Unique (RSU)** interoperating with the unique ID reached **362,698 households / 2,217,518 people (99 communes, 10 regions)**. Sources: [[2025-06-19-snec-taux-execution-physique-6411-2024]], [[2026-02-02-contrat-objectifs-121000-actes-etat-civil-deplaces]], [[2026-01-06-registre-social-unique-protection-sociale]].

**The donor track — still live post-ECOWAS.** Foundational-ID enrolment runs through the **[[wuri-programme]]** (West Africa Unique Identification, WB, with Benin/Togo/Niger). Notably, despite Burkina's **ECOWAS exit**, a **World Bank mid-term review (Feb 2026) confirmed PACTDIGITAL and WURI are still run jointly in Burkina** — WURI sensitisation continued in Bobo-Dioulasso (Aug 2025) with its data hosted **inside** Burkina Faso, complicating any thesis that the exit dropped Burkina out of WURI. The **unique electronic ID itself stalled in 2025 on a failed tender** (vendor procurement relaunched; 7m enrolments targeted for 2026). The result is one biometric system serving two masters: World Bank inclusion goals and AES sovereignty signalling. Sources: [[2026-02-09-world-bank-backs-burkina-faso-digital-infrastructure-expansion]], [[2025-08-21-wuri-sensibilisation-bobo-dioulasso-identifiant-unique]], [[2026-03-24-transformation-digitale-bilan-2025-85-pourcent]].
