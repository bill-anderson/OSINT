# Malawi × Artificial Intelligence & Innovation

Malawi's AI push is early-stage and **inclusion-framed** — a draft national strategy, a low-resource-language
data trust, responsible-AI-and-ID work with the regulator, and a skills/innovation pipeline led by banks and
telcos rather than compute build-out.

## Policy and language

- **First-ever National AI Strategy — consultation to validation (2025-09 → 2026-02).** The
  **[[malawi-ministry-of-ict]]** (Department of E-Government) launched **regional consultative workshops** in
  **Sept 2025** to develop Malawi's **first National AI Strategy** and a National Digital Transformation
  Strategy — with **[[undp]]** under the **[[idt4m|IDT4M]]** programme and the PPP Commission under
  **[[dmap]]** — reaching a **validation workshop and citizen-feedback portal in Feb 2026** (Malawi 2063);
  pre-adoption. See [[gov.policy]]. Sources: [[2025-09-25-national-ai-strategy-consultation-itweb]],
  [[2026-02-20 Malawi validates AI strategy, digital transformation policy - CAJ News Africa]].
- **Chichewa AI — a Low Resource Language Data Trust (2026-06).** Malawi launched the
  **[[malawi-language-data-trust|Malawi Low Resource Language Data Trust]]** to build AI models in **Chichewa**,
  assembling licensed local-language content for training data — **World Bank + Gates Foundation** supported,
  positioning Malawi among states building AI for underrepresented languages (digital assistants, translation,
  education). See [[data.open]], [[capacity.research]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-24-chichewa-ai-language-data-trust-techafricanews]].
- **Responsible AI + ID with the regulator (2026-07).** **[[macra-malawi|MACRA]]** and **[[undp]]** are
  exploring collaboration on **AI, digital identity and data protection** — priority on **integrating the
  National ID with SIM verification** (telecom integrity, anti-fraud), responsible AI, stronger data protection,
  and youth digital skills (DG Mayamiko Nkoloma). See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10-macra-undp-ai-digital-id-collaboration-itweb]].

## Skills and innovation pipeline

- **TNM AI labs + 30 years (2025-11).** **[[tnm-malawi|TNM]]** (CEO Michel Herbert), covering **85%** of the
  population and using **solar-powered** infrastructure for the last 15%, announced plans for **AI laboratories
  and innovation hubs** — with **forex shortages** flagged as the binding constraint. See [[infra.connect]].
  Source: [[2025-11-10-tnm-30-years-ai-labs-times]].
- **Innovation Jam — "AI for Sustainable Development" (2025-11).** **NBM** (National Bank of Malawi) gave
  **K75.5m** to the 2025 **Innovation Jam** (with **[[ictam]]**), signing a **three-year MoU** for mentorship/
  incubation and stressing AI for fraud prevention. See [[tech.innovate]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-19-nbm-ictam-innovation-jam-ai-malawi24]].
- **National ICT Innovation Awards (2026-04).** Officials framed the awards as a shift "from policy ambition to
  practical implementation" (PS Harold Msusa; Minister Namalomba) — *(PR-wire).* See [[tech.innovate]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-22-national-ict-innovation-awards-techreviewafrica]].
- **Airtel's Nzeru Fund STEM scholarships (2026-01).** The **Airtel Africa Foundation** launched the **Nzeru
  Fund** — full support for **25 low-income undergraduates** in IT, CS, software engineering, **data science, AI
  and cybersecurity** at the Malawi University of Science and Technology. See [[airtel-malawi]],
  [[capacity.training]]. Source: [[2026-01-07-airtel-nzeru-fund-stem-scholarships-malawi24]].
