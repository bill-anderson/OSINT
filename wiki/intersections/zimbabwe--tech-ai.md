# Zimbabwe × AI & Sovereign Compute

Zimbabwe's AI programme is **the most sovereignty-forward in rhetoric of any in this batch** — "our data is
our heritage," a Sovereign National Cloud, HPC as national assets — launched by the President and organised
around "computational sovereignty." The gap the material keeps exposing is between that ambition and the
base: a **0 score on responsible-AI governance**, compute that **may sit offshore**, and (per the hub) a
strategy MISA reads as centralising surveillance.

## The National AI Strategy 2026–2030

- **Launched with sovereignty at the centre (2026-03-13).** President **Mnangagwa** launched the
  **five-year [[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy|National AI Strategy]]** at the **New Parliament Building,
  Mount Hampden**, under the theme *"Harnessing AI for Inclusive National Development"* — establishing the
  **[[ai-strategy-implementation-office-zimbabwe|AISIO]]**, a **regulatory sandbox**, a **National Digital
  Regulatory Committee under [[potraz]]**, a forthcoming **National AI Act**, and an open-data push.
  *Event date fixed at 2026-03-13 by a same-day account; the held ITWeb report (published 2026-03-16) says
  "on Friday", which agrees.* Sources: [[2026-03-16-mnangagwa-launches-five-year-ai-strategy]],
  [[2026-03-13-mavetera-84-55-internet-penetration-ai-strategy]].
- **Connectivity offered as the precondition (2026-03-13).** ICT Minister **Mavetera** introduced the
  launch by presenting **84.55% internet penetration (Q4 2025)** and a **14,000 km+ fibre backbone
  (2026-03)** as what makes AI adoption feasible, framing the strategy inside **Vision 2030**'s full
  digital economy target. She also announced a planned **National AI Innovation Fund**, pursued with
  telcos, to fund and mentor AI start-ups — *an intention only; no size, capital source, governance or
  date is held.* **Caveat: the 84.55% is a subscriptions-over-population ratio, not a usage rate** — see
  [[zimbabwe--infra-connect]]; an AI-readiness argument resting on it overstates the human user base
  (**38.4%, 6.54m individuals, Oct 2025**). See [[tech.innovate]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-13-mavetera-84-55-internet-penetration-ai-strategy]].
- **"Data is our heritage" — computational sovereignty.** The strategy mandates a **Sovereign National
  Cloud** to end "digital displacement" (national/commercial data in foreign clouds), with **agricultural
  predictive models and geological/mineral maps housed on domestic servers**, **HPC centres as national
  assets**, and **Heritage-Based Education 5.0** producing specialists — the National AI Act to provide **data
  residency and algorithmic transparency**. *"To control our data is to control our future."* See
  [[infra.store]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source: [[2026-03-16-ai-strategy-mandates-local-storage]].

## The independent read: ambition vs readiness (CIPESA, May 2026)

- **Six pillars, AU-aligned, but a governance void.** CIPESA (Edrine Wanyama) reads the six pillars (talent;
  **infrastructure/computational sovereignty**; adoption; governance/ethics; R&D; international collaboration)
  as aligned with the **AU Continental AI Strategy** and Agenda 2063, and — *if implemented* — a way to
  address **"digital neo-colonialism."** But Zimbabwe **scored 0 on the 2024 Global Index on Responsible AI
  Governance** — the ambition-vs-institutional-readiness gap in one number. See [[gov.regional]],
  [[colonialism]]. Source: [[2026-05-22-cipesa-zimbabwe-ai-strategy-policy-lessons]].

## The private compute layer — and where the machines actually are

- **Econet's build (2026-01).** **[[econet-infraco]]** announced a **300-ha industrial/IT park** (100 MW
  solar, an AI-ready data centre) near Harare airport — the physical-infrastructure bet behind the AI story
  (hub). Source: [[2026-01-14-zimbabwe-ai-ready-data-center-tech-park]].
- **Econet AI (2026-04).** **[[econet-ai]]** (Yamurai/OmniContact; a stated **sovereign cloud** for
  government; **Shona local-language AI**) draws GPU compute from **[[cassava-technologies]]**'s AI factory —
  but **whether that compute is physically in Zimbabwe is unclear** (possibly offshore). The "sovereign
  cloud" pitch and the offshore-compute reality are the tension the batch can't yet resolve. See
  [[tech.innovate]]. Source: [[2026-04-17-econet-ai-launch-cassava-compute]].
- **Zimbabwean infrastructure exported (2026-06).** At the Africa Cloud Summit, **[[strive-masiyiwa]]** and
  Google SVP **[[james-manyika]]** anchored **Google's $1bn Africa push**; **[[liquid-c2]]** (Econet Group)
  opened Africa's first **Google Cloud Partner Experience Centre** in Johannesburg — framed as Zimbabwe
  flipping "from tech consumer to **regional cloud enabler**," with a **1-million-AI-jobs** target. See
  [[geopol.usa]]. Source: [[2026-06-30-masiyiwa-manyika-google-1b-africa-cloud]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty` / `colonialism`: Zimbabwe articulates AI sovereignty better than almost anyone — data
  residency, HPC as national assets, "African problems solved with African compute" (Masiyiwa). And it has,
  in the Econet/Cassava/Liquid stack, a genuinely **Zimbabwean-owned** infrastructure group exporting compute
  across the continent — a real counter to digital neo-colonialism. But three cracks run through it: the
  compute Econet AI sells **may not physically be in Zimbabwe**; the flagship **"sovereign" partner centre is
  built on Google's cloud**; and the state's own capacity to govern any of it scored **zero** on the 2024
  responsible-AI index while MISA warns the same strategy centralises surveillance ([[ZWE]] hub, Project
  Pangolin). Sovereign in ambition and ownership; dependent in compute and weak in governance — the
  distance between "our data is our heritage" and where the servers actually run.
