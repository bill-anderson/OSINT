# Central African Republic × Data Protection

CAR has a **modern data-protection law on paper and no regulator to enforce it** — the classic
law-without-authority gap. The statute is comprehensive; the supervisory body it mandates
remains unfilled two years on.

## What we know

**Law No. 24.001 (enacted Jan 2024)** — **[[car-data-protection-law-24-001]]** — is a
comprehensive regime: broad territorial scope (incl. security, defence and justice, with
personal-use exemptions); the standard principles and lawful bases (parental consent for
minors); tight controls on **sensitive data** (incl. genetic/biometric and health); a **tiered
cross-border-transfer** system with *specific conditions* for **CEMAC and [[eccas]]** members;
full data-subject rights including protection from solely-automated decisions; and a mandatory
**resident DPO** plus processing register. Sanctions run to **5% of annual turnover** plus
criminal penalties.

**But the authority is unfilled.** The independent supervisory authority the law provides for
has **not been established** — confirmed by the **[[data-protection-africa]]** factsheet (updated
28 Jan 2026: *"data protection law in place; data protection authority not appointed"*). Without
it, the sanction and complaint machinery is dormant. CAR also sits under a stack of **CEMAC-level
instruments** (e-communications 2008, payment systems 2016, consumer protection 2019–2020).

**Where the law came from: donor-driven, and now documented.** The World Bank's two P174620 project
documents bracket the law's enactment from either side. At appraisal (**7 Mar 2022**) the Bank recorded a
flat vacuum: *"because there is currently no data protection law, there are inadequate safeguards for both
ID and CR data"*, and separately that CAR *"does not have a comprehensive data protection body and lacks
cybersecurity and cybercrime legislation"*, with **no national cybersecurity strategy, no CERT and no SOC**,
scoring **3.6 on the 2018 Cybersecurity Index against 29.5 for Sub-Saharan Africa**; it urged **ratification
of the Malabo Convention**. By the additional-financing paper (**21 May 2025**) the Bank lists among
Component 2's achievements *"the enactment of two laws (cybersecurity and data protection)"*. The
law-without-authority gap is therefore best read as a **donor-financed legislative deliverable that arrived
without the institution to run it** — enactment was the indicator, appointment was not. Sources:
[[2022-03-07-worldbank-pad4661-car-public-sector-digital-governance]],
[[2025-05-21-worldbank-project-paper-af-car-public-sector-digital-governance]].

> **Attribution limit.** The World Bank documents **name neither law** — they say "two laws (cybersecurity
> and data protection)" without a number, title or date. Identifying the data-protection one as **Law No.
> 24.001** is an inference from the fact that it is the only CAR data-protection statute the wiki holds and
> that it falls inside the project window; it is **not stated by the project documents**. The paired
> cybersecurity law is **not identified at all** — the wiki holds no CAR cybersecurity statute, which is a
> live acquisition target as of **2026-07-20**.
