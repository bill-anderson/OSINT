# Rwanda × Data Centres & Cloud

Rwanda is courting **Gulf digital-FDI** to build AI-ready data-centre capacity, positioning itself as
a regional hub — while its own analysts flag an under-discussed constraint: **water**.

## AI-ready capacity — the Gulf tie-up

- **BSC × Oman Data Park (May 2026).** **[[broadband-systems-corporation]]** signed an MoU with
  **Oman Data Park** to jointly invest in an **AI-ready Tier III data centre** in Rwanda (reliability,
  redundancy, cloud and data-intensive workloads), witnessed by MINICT ministers and RISA CEO Antoine
  Sebera. A Gulf-capital DC play for AI/cloud demand. See [[finance.mou]], [[geopol.gulf]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-29-bsc-oman-data-park-mou-techafricanews]].

## Courting digital FDI

- **Digital FDI Rwanda report (Oct 2025).** MINICT, the **Digital Cooperation Organization (DCO)** and
  the **WEF** launched a Digital FDI roadmap at the **9th Future Investment Initiative (Riyadh)** —
  policy/regulatory reforms to make Rwanda a competitive destination for cloud, fintech and data-
  infrastructure investment, "strengthening trust in our data ecosystem" ([[paula-ingabire]]). The
  Gulf/DCO axis is explicit. See [[finance.new]], [[geopol.gulf]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-30-digital-fdi-report-techafricanews]].

## The water constraint

- **A water policy for data centres (Mar 2026).** New Times analysis warns Rwanda should plan early
  for the **environmental footprint** of AI infrastructure — the largest data centres can consume up
  to ~1.8bn gallons of water a year for cooling — a resource-planning gap as Rwanda scales DC/AI
  ambitions. See [[infra.energy]]. Source: [[2026-03-19-water-policy-data-centres-newtimes]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: onshore AI-ready capacity is the compute base sovereignty needs — but it is being
  built with **Gulf capital and operators** (Oman Data Park, DCO), the same "sovereignty on imported
  foundations" pattern seen elsewhere; the water-policy gap is a reminder the physical constraints are
  local even when the capital is not.
