# Uganda × Digital Identity

**[[ndaga-muntu]]**, run by **[[nira-uganda]]** under the **[[registration-of-persons-act-2015]]**, is
the clearest African case of a **gate that closed faster than the register filled**. By **June 2024**
the ID was already load-bearing for banking, tax, education and social protection. In **February 2026**
the registrar's own review reported a **first-time enrolment rate of 37.3%**. Between those two dates,
in **June 2025**, the High Court dismissed a challenge to mandatory ID on the finding that the
excluded *"appear to me to be few and isolated cases"*.

**That sequence is the argument of this page.** The dependency was built first; the coverage was
counted afterwards; the court ruled in the gap between them.

## The gate: what the ID became load-bearing for

**2024-10-14 — 74 financial institutions on the register.** Per the **Bank of Uganda 2023 annual
report** *(the primary is not held)*, **up to 74 financial institutions have NIRA database access for
digital KYC — 74 listed as of June 2024**: commercial banks, MFIs, a forex bureau, fintechs, insurers
and **one telco**; some at full operation, others in trial. Implementation has run **since 2020** under
the [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]]. In the same account, **[[bank-of-uganda]]'s April 2024
directive** requires ID verification for **all financial transactions of UGX 1 million (US$260) and
above** — a rule *"met with criticism as some citizens said it would slow down business."* Source:
[[2024-10-14 Uganda financial institutions sign on for digital KYC through national ID]]. See
[[dpi.pay]], [[uganda--dpi-pay]].

**2025-11-11 — "your NIN will be your TIN."** [[ura-uganda]] announced the merger of the Tax
Identification Number into the NIN under the **Tax Procedures Amendment Bill**, with **[[ursb]]**
numbers becoming business TINs. Source:
[[2025-11-11 URA Launches Tax Filing Season, Announces Shift from TIN to NIN]].

**Education and social protection.** The **Ministry of Education** requires a NIN for learner
registration on the **Education Management Information System (EMIS)** portal; **learners and staff
without one are excluded from enumeration, planning and budgeting**. **[[sage-uganda]]** — cash
assistance to vulnerable elderly people — benefits **only those on the National Identification
Register**. *(Both reported by [[michael-aboneka]] / DRAA — advocacy analysis, attributed.)* Source:
[[2025-09-13 Uganda's Digital ID Barrier Worsens as Court Delivers Blow to Access]].

**2026-05-28 — the platform.** A Biometric Update explainer records Uganda among the states whose
national digital ID is underpinned by **[[mosip]]** (with Ethiopia and Sierra Leone). Source:
[[2026-05-28 Why decentralized digital identity looks different in every country]].

## The reach: NIRA's own numbers (2026-02-11)

NIRA Registrar **[[claire-ollama]]**, at a press conference on **9 February 2026**, reported the full
transition to the new system: **more than 28 million records migrated**; the exercise started **May
2025** targeting **over 33 million**; total enrolments **more than 35 million**.

| Measure (as of Feb 2026) | Achieved | Target | Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Renewals | 14.3m | 15.8m | **90.5%** |
| First-time enrolments | 6.4m | 17.2m | **37.3%** |
| Cards printed → collected | 2.5m collected | 10.1m printed | **over 60% uncollected** |

**The two rates are the finding.** Renewal — re-documenting people the register already held — ran at
**90.5%**. First-time enrolment — reaching people it never held — ran at **37.3%**. The system is
efficient at serving those already inside it. And even for those enrolled, **10.1 million cards printed
against 2.5 million collected (Feb 2026)** means a NIN in the database is not a card in a hand.

**The stack.** Service provider **UAE-based [[tahaluf]]**; **five modules MOSIP-based**; other vendors
**[[tech5]]**, Technoforte, Entrust and Hyf (per NIRA ED **[[rosemary-kisembo]]**). **Gen. David
Muhoozi** cited measures against **system integration failures and QR-code scanning failures on some
new cards**, with the authentication module fully operational **by end of March [2026]**. Source:
[[2026-02-11 Uganda migrates more than 28M records to new national ID system]]. See [[geopol.gulf]].

## Mass enrolment & renewal (2025–26): the progression, the breach, the diaspora

The 2025/26 exercise now has a full documented arc, corroborating the Feb-2026 figures above from the
primary chronology:

- **Kit and card build-up (Apr 2025).** [[tahaluf]] supplied all **5,665 biometric kits** by January
  2025; two high-speed machines (~**100,000 laser-engraved cards/day**); the Uganda Security Printing
  Company delivered ID stock (1.5m of an expected 8.1m by Apr 2025) with UV/UV-Bi-fluor anti-forgery
  features. The **2014–15 cards expire end-2025** — the renewal driver. Sources:
  [[2025-04-02-nira-acquires-high-capacity-id-machines]], [[2025-05-08-uganda-targets-33m-mass-id-registration]].
- **MOSIP five modules + UPI ambition (Apr 2025).** NIRA launched **five MOSIP modules** (registration,
  renewal, correction, issuance, lost-ID replacement), planned to integrate birth/death registries, and
  flagged a **Unified Payments Interface (UPI)** giving "a free wallet for every citizen" (Kisembo). See
  [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2025-04-14-uganda-launching-5-mosip-modules]].
- **The renewal curve.** From the **27 May 2025** parish rollout: **2.5m → 3.5m (Jun) → 5.3m (Jul) → 10m+
  (Sep) → 13.37m renewed + 6.15m first-time (Dec)** → the **8 Feb 2026 milestone close** at **35m
  registered** (28,571,893 legacy records migrated; 14.31m renewals = 90.5%; 6.42m first-time = 37.3%;
  **10,152,559 cards printed, 7.26m shipped, only 2.58m collected**). Enhanced cards (laser-engraved,
  QR/barcode, ghost image, digital signature) dispatched to all districts from Oct 2025; **diaspora issuance
  opened in the UAE** (Dubai, Feb 2026). Sources: [[2025-06-23-nira-data-protection-mass-id-3-5m]],
  [[2025-07-16-5m-ugandans-renew-national-ids]], [[2025-10-27-enhanced-national-ids-dispatched-districts]],
  [[2025-12-29-over-13-3m-national-ids-renewed-extension]], [[2026-02-09-35-million-registered-national-id-milestone]].
- **The brownfield mechanics (Feb 2026).** NIRA completed **MOSIP's first brownfield migration** — legacy
  ABIS by Neurotechnology, ~**200,000 duplicates** found over 7 months, accelerating from **8,000 to a goal
  of 300,000 records/day** under electoral time pressure; **two card types** (citizen + foreigner) built to
  Ugandan law atop MOSIP. Source: [[2026-02-19-uganda-mosip-brownfield-migration-example]].
- **The database breach (Sep 2025).** A **BBC investigation** alleged NIRA staff sold National
  Identification Register data to a **criminal ring** (local reports linking it to a Dubai sex-trafficking
  network). NIRA opened an investigation with the **[[personal-data-protection-office-uganda|PDPO]]** and
  police; **[[unwanted-witness]]** demanded a fully independent inquiry. This is the concrete instance of
  the surveillance/access-risk the page flags below. See [[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-22-nira-tighten-id-security-after-breach]].
- **Biometric voter verification (Jun 2025).** Museveni insisted **no voting without biometric
  verification**; the **[[electoral-commission-uganda|EC]]** re-tendered for **~10,000 devices** after being
  refused UBOS's (glitch-prone, census-2024) kits — the procurement whose deployment **failed by 9am on
  polling day** (see [[UGA]] hub). Source: [[2025-06-16-museveni-wants-biometric-verification-2026-vote]].
- **TIN→NIN, corroborated.** A second account confirms the **NIN-as-TIN** merger under the Tax Procedures
  Amendment Bill (URSB numbers as business TINs), plus a **3-year income-tax exemption** for post-1-Jul-2025
  firms with ≤UGX 500m capital. Source: [[2025-11-12-ura-shift-from-tin-to-nin]].

## The court: *ISER v Attorney General & NIRA* (dismissed 10 June 2025)

***Initiative for Social and Economic Rights ([[iser-uganda]]) & 2 Others v Attorney General & NIRA*,
HCT-00-CV-MC-0066-2022, High Court of Uganda — dismissed 10 June 2025.** The applicants challenged the
mandatory NIN / National ID requirement under the [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]] for services
including **healthcare and social protection**, arguing disproportionate exclusion of vulnerable
populations including **older persons and women**.

**Verbatim holding:** *"Uganda's ID system is not 'digital' because it operates primarily offline. A
person cannot be denied access simply because of lack of internet connectivity."* The Court further
held that the deponents *"appear to me to be few and isolated cases"* and that the applicants had **not
adduced sufficient evidence of "actual and systemic exclusion"**. An **amicus curiae** by [[cipesa]],
**[[article-19]]** and [[access-now]], carrying expert opinions from **Prof. Tom Fisher**, **Dr Reetika
Khera** and **Prof. Philip Alston**, was unavailing.

**The timing is the point: the Court found "few and isolated cases" on 10 June 2025 — eight months
before NIRA itself published a 37.3% first-time enrolment rate (11 February 2026).** The exclusion the
Court could not see in the evidence is the exclusion the registrar later reported in the numbers.

**The rebuttal — advocacy analysis, attributed.** [[michael-aboneka]], writing for the **Digital Rights
Alliance Africa (DRAA)**, argues the ruling **legitimises** a system that denies access to those
without an ID, and that the "offline" finding is contradicted by the government's own **5-year Digital
Transformation Roadmap, launched 17 August 2023**, which shifts service provision onto DPI — the
National ID system, mobile money, **[[efris]]**, the Express Penalty Scheme, Digital Tax Stamps and
Payment Registration Numbers — **all of which require authentication**.

**The judgment itself is not held by this wiki (GAP-UGA-001)** — the account above rests on DRAA's
analysis, not the primary. Source:
[[2025-09-13 Uganda's Digital ID Barrier Worsens as Court Delivers Blow to Access]]. See
[[gov.legislate]], [[include.divides]].

## Exclusion mechanics: the bodies the biometrics could not read

**2026-03-02 — iris, because fingerprints failed.** [[iritech]] reports **more than 1 million Ugandans
had difficulty registering fingerprint biometrics** (NIRA figures cited by IriTech — **vendor-sourced;
attribute**), **disproportionately elderly people and manual labourers**. That failure is the **stated
reason** for adding **iris capture** via the **IriShield BK** binocular scanner across **more than
5,665 kits** for the **2024–2026 campaign**. The kits carry **[[mosip]] L0 compliance** and were among
the first certified to MOSIP's **MACP Framework** (assessed by BixeLab). The same account independently
corroborates **28 million biometric records migrated to MOSIP — the platform's first completed
brownfield implementation**. Source:
[[2026-03-02 IriTech provides iris biometrics for Uganda's upgraded registration kits]]. See
[[include.access]], [[tech.industry]].

**2024-07-02 — a second, different million: prints already on file that no longer match.** NIRA ED
**[[rosemary-kisembo]]**, testifying to Parliament's [[cosase-uganda|COSASE]] on the Auditor General's
2022/23 report, said **"there are currently 1 million people in the register whose fingerprints did not
satisfy the requirements"** — the stated reason NIRA would **begin incorporating iris scans**, alongside
facial recognition already in use.

**These are two distinct propositions and must not be read as two witnesses to one fact.** IriTech's
figure (2026-03-02) counts people who **could not register fingerprints at all** — a capture failure at
the door. Kisembo's counts people **already inside the register** whose **stored prints fail matching** —
an authentication failure after enrolment. Same order of magnitude, materially different populations, and
the second is the more consequential for anyone holding a NIN they cannot use. **No combined total is
computed here.**

*(Event date unknown. UBC reports the testimony but does not date the committee appearance, which
preceded publication; **2024-07-02 is the publication date only**. A separate account places a Kisembo
COSASE appearance on **1 July 2024** — the same committee, the same Auditor General's report, but the
identity of the two sittings is **not established**.) Source:
[[2024-07-02-nira-iris-scanning-1m-unmatched-fingerprints-ubc]].*

**Read against disability.** **[[asamo-hellen-grace]]**, Minister of State for Disability Affairs,
reported Uganda is **estimated to have more than 1.3 million persons living with disabilities (as of
December 2025)**, with complaints of barriers to loans, banking and essential financial services.
Source:
[[2025-12-02 Minister Asamo Calls for Stronger Financial Inclusion for Persons with Disabilities]].

## How many Ugandans lack an ID — resolved 2026-07-20 (definitional, not factual)

The three figures never conflicted; they count different things.

- **~18 million (glossed as "about 40% of the population")** — [[michael-aboneka]] / DRAA
  (**2025-09-13**). *Previously logged here as an uncited advocacy estimate. **That was wrong**: the
  number is NIRA's own, given to Parliament on 1 July 2024 — see below. The uncited element was the
  **40%-exclusion gloss**, not the count.*
- **A register of more than 35 million** uniquely identifiable people — [[nira-uganda]]
  (**2026-02-11**), which frames cumulative enrolment rather than the gap.
- NIRA's own **2024 baseline of 17.2m unregistered** minus **6.4m** first-time enrolments implies
  **~10.8m still unregistered (Feb 2026)** — which cleanly contradicts neither.

**Confirmed definitional root (resolved 2026-07-20): "registered", "holds a NIN" and "holds a usable
card" are three different populations, and the 18.1m and 35m use different denominators.**

**The 18.1m is NIRA's own figure, not an advocacy estimate** — the single most load-bearing correction
here. ED **[[rosemary-kisembo]]** told Parliament's [[cosase-uganda|COSASE]] on **1 July 2024**:
*"Based on the current census figures of 45.9 million people, we haven't registered about 18.1 million
individuals. **Most of those unregistered are children under 17 years old.**"* The denominator is the
**total** population; the balance she gave in the same testimony is **27.4m registered with NINs and
17.3m cards issued (of 20m printed) since 2015**. Source:
[[2024-07-16-nira-cosase-18-million-unregistered-staffing]].

Against the **23,154,716 adults aged 18+** the adult gap is ~**2.0m, ~9%** — a different order of
exclusion entirely. NIRA's own **15 October 2024** press statement supplies the age structure that makes
the arithmetic legible: **27.4m persons on the register, of whom 6.3m (≈22.1%) are aged 0–17**, against a
national population of **45.9m in which that bracket is 59.7%**. **The register is an adult register**;
the headline gap is overwhelmingly a child-registration gap. Source:
[[2024-10-15-nira-press-statement-mass-enrolment-status]].

The **35m is cumulative enrolments ever issued**, with no deregistration for death or emigration, so it
is not a coverage rate and cannot be subtracted from a live population. The binding constraint is
**card possession**: **10,152,559 printed, 7,261,435 shipped, 2,581,457 collected (35.5%, 2026-02-08)**.
Source: [[2026-02-09-35-million-registered-national-id-milestone]].

**[[michael-aboneka]] / DRAA's "~18 million, about 40% of the population" (2025-09-13) is therefore
numerically NIRA's own count, carried forward a year and re-framed as an exclusion rate.** The figure is
sound; the **"40% excluded" gloss is not**, because it silently converts a total-population gap composed
mostly of under-17s into an implied population of people denied services. *The wiki adopts the 18.1m as
NIRA's 2024 figure and rejects the 40%-exclusion reading.*

## Surveillance: mechanism established, causation not asserted

**2024-06-05.** Biometric Update reports **on a Bloomberg / Lighthouse Reports investigation** *(the
primary; **not held** — GAP-UGA-002)*. Human rights lawyer **[[nick-opiyo]]** believes there was an
ulterior motive to his **December 2020 imprisonment**, having "become ensnared in a surveillance
dragnet."

**The clip does not document which ID records were pulled on him.** What it establishes is the
**mechanism**: the "underlying software and datasets are easily accessed by individuals at all levels
of law enforcement." **The causal link is not asserted here.** NIRA ED **[[rosemary-kisembo]]**
**refuted on the record** that law enforcement could readily access the database. Separately, a **2023
[[acme-uganda]] study** concludes Uganda's biometric/digital identity programmes gave room for
surveillance and intrusion on journalism, with journalists targeted. Source:
[[2024-06-05 Ugandan human rights lawyer's arrest exposes use of national ID for surveillance]]. See
[[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]].

## Registration chronology: a slipping schedule against a hard deadline

Slated **1 June 2024** → rescheduled **July 2024** → **October 2024** → **actually launched 27 May
2025**. **A ~12-month slip.**

The **October 2024** plan: **5,680 kits**; targets of **17.2m** first-time registrations and **15.8m**
renewals; **13,000 personnel** across **10,717 parishes** — aligned to the Electoral Commission's **30
November** deadline for the voters' register ahead of the **2026 general elections**. *(By March 2026
IriTech reports **more than 5,665 kits** deployed — rounding against the 5,680 plan, not a conflict.)*

**Each source is accurate as of its date — the slip against a hard electoral deadline is signal, not
contradiction**, and connects to the biometric voter-verification kit failure on polling day (see
[[UGA]]).

**The slip, dated by NIRA itself.** Kisembo told [[cosase-uganda|COSASE]] (**1 July 2024**): *"We are
three months behind projections... We were supposed to begin the enrolment process on June 1, 2024. We
now hope to start by the end of July or the beginning of August."* Source:
[[2024-07-16-nira-cosase-18-million-unregistered-staffing]]. By the **15 October 2024** press statement
enrolment had **still not begun**, and NIRA's stated cause had moved to **equipment**: *"Because of the
delays in receiving the new equipment, the old equipment is being used."*

**The primary chronology (NIRA, 2024-10-15).** Cabinet approved the exercise in **August 2022** at
**UGX 666.85bn** (system UGX 293bn / blank cards UGX 183bn / administration and 13,000 registration
assistants UGX 290.95bn); Parliament appropriated **UGX 300bn** in **December 2023** — **45% of the
approved budget**. The **[[tahaluf]]** contract for the new **NSIS** was signed **8 July 2024**, kick-off
**17 July 2024** — i.e. **the technology partner was contracted five weeks after the enrolment start date
had already passed**. Recruitment ran Feb–Jun 2024: **~156,000 applicants** for **13,787** temporary
posts across 135 districts and 11 cities. Kit delivery was scheduled in three air freights (**3,000 →
1,668 → 997**, reaching 5,665 by **12 January 2025**), with go-live of the five registration modules set
for **17 December 2024**. *(Actual parish rollout began 27 May 2025.)* Source:
[[2024-10-15-nira-press-statement-mass-enrolment-status]].

**The parliamentary budget figures (2023-04-30), a year before the exercise ran.** Committee chair
Rosemary Nyakikongoro told the House the programme was **UGX 298.2bn** — *"a mass enrollment and
renewal project of Shs298.2b to develop new system and undertake mass registration of 17.2m
unregistered citizens… and renewal of 15.8m national identity cards that are due to expire"* — of
which **UGX 117.8bn** for mass enrolment, plus **UGX 12.67bn** for NIRA to recruit staff. She also
told the House the **[[electoral-commission-uganda|Electoral Commission]] expected an updated NIRA
register by November 2024**. *(The UGX 298.2bn tracks the UGX 300bn Parliament appropriated in
December 2023, against the UGX 666.85bn Cabinet approved in August 2022 — the same 45% shortfall, seen
eight months earlier from the committee's side. The staffing line reads directly against the 52%
vacancy rate below.)* Source: [[2023-04-30-monitor-fight-over-national-id-deal-splits-parliament]].

**The one-year card-validity extension, sourced to the instrument.** With **up to 3.4 million** cards
expiring between **24 August 2024 and 30 January 2025**, the Minister of Internal Affairs issued
**Statutory Instrument No. 67 of 2024 — the Registration of Persons (Amendment) Regulations 2024** on
**12 August 2024** (gazetted the same date), extending every expired card's validity by **one year from
its date of expiry** and preserving its use under **ss.65–66 of the
[[registration-of-persons-act-2015]]** for government services and financial transactions. **This is the
legal device that kept the [[uganda--dpi-pay]] KYC gate from closing on millions mid-exercise** — the
dependency was load-bearing enough that expiry had to be legislated away rather than met. See
[[gov.legislate]].

**The electoral clock, made explicit.** NIRA ran a **53-day exercise from 27 September to 23 November
2024** expressly to meet the **[[electoral-commission-uganda|Electoral Commission]]'s 30 November 2024**
deadline for submitting the voters' list — *"emphasis or focus on eligible voters"* (17+), with the
Ministry of Internal Affairs working through RDCs/RCCs, schools, **DISOs/GISOs** and LC1 chairpersons.
NIRA's own stated obstacles were **end-of-year school exams** (students being "the bulk of persons to be
registered"), **nationwide heavy rainfall** impeding kit movement and causing **power and connectivity
outages**, and the ageing equipment. **The registration drive was scheduled around an election, not
around the unregistered population** — which is the same ordering the enrolment rates later expose.

**The card-production vendor — resolved (2026-07-20).** **[[veridos]]** has held the card-printing
contract since **2018**, not a candidate awaiting selection. **Mühlbauer High Tech International** was
the incumbent card producer — it *"produced national ID cards for Ugandans between 2010 and 2018"* and
*"set up the current system that Nira uses"* — and Veridos succeeded it. Parliament's Defence and
Internal Affairs Committee tried to reverse that: it **announced by 2022-11-24** that it would probe
Veridos, after meeting Mühlbauer officials, and by **2023-04-30** its majority report recommended NIRA
re-engage Mühlbauer to upgrade the legacy system, while a **minority report** (Bashir Sempa Lubega)
backed Veridos and alleged the committee leadership had held **unsanctioned private meetings with
Mühlbauer in Uganda and in Germany**, *"in total contravention of the Public Procurement and Disposal
of Public Assets Act."* Veteran MP **Abdu Katuntu** objected that Parliament *"is not mandated to do
procurement."* Veridos told the committee its factory would not be producing until **2027**. The
recommendation had no procurement force and **no contract was awarded to Mühlbauer**.

The earlier claim that Mühlbauer was *"embroiled in a scandal concerning overcharging"* is **withdrawn
as unsupported** — the disputes on the record are **vendor lock-in** (NIRA called the legacy kit
inflexible and vendor-locked; Kohler **rebutted this publicly**) and cost objections running the
*other* way, MPs arguing the **Veridos smart ID was too expensive** against upgrading the legacy
system.

**Two layers, not one:** card production and security printing run through **USPC (Uganda Security
Printing Company)**, which supplied NIRA's blank card stock (1.5m of an expected 8.1m by April 2025);
the enrolment system is [[tahaluf]]/[[tech5]]/[[mosip]]. **So Veridos' absence from the 2026 provider
list signifies nothing** — this retires the standing "do not infer the outcome" caveat.

joint venture, a 2017-07-03 award over De La Rue, and the USPC ownership split — entered the wiki from
reconcile-pass research and are **not in any held source**. Carried as unverified on [[veridos]]; see
**ACQ-UGA-USPC**. No cost figure for USPC card production is on file.* Sources:

## Delivery capacity: the register's limit is an establishment chart

**NIRA has never been staffed to do the job it is mandated to do, and said so on the record.** To
[[cosase-uganda|COSASE]] on **1 July 2024**, ED [[rosemary-kisembo]]: *"NIRA's staffing levels have never
exceeded 50%, meaning that the ratio of unregistered Ugandans to NIRA staff is **1:80,000**. We are
present in 112 districts, leaving **24 districts and 10 cities** without our presence."* The
[[office-of-the-auditor-general-uganda|Auditor General]]'s **December 2023** report put it structurally:
of **856 approved positions only 410 were filled — 446 vacancies (52%)**, and **offices in 111 of 146
districts as of October 2023**. Approved establishment is four officers per district registration office
(one registration officer, two assistants, one IT officer); **most run two**.

**The consequence is distance.** Citizens in unserved districts — Kisembo named **Kalaki** and
**Kapelebyong** — travel **80–100 km** for a service that is the precondition for banking, tax,
education and social protection. Daily throughput where NIRA does exist: **600–1,000 people in Kampala
offices, 200–300 peri-urban, 60–150 rural**. Asked by COSASE chair **[[medard-sseggona]]** whether staff
could rotate between districts, Kisembo said rotation was **not feasible** against existing demand. The
cost of closing the gap she gave as **~UGX 6.7bn** first-year per new office, falling thereafter.

**Read this against the 37.3% first-time enrolment rate (Feb 2026) and the "few and isolated cases"
finding (Jun 2025).** The exclusion is not mysterious and was never unforeseeable: **a 52% vacancy rate
and 34 unserved districts and cities were on the parliamentary record 11 months before the Court found
insufficient evidence of systemic exclusion, and 19 months before the registrar reported the rate.**
Sources: [[2024-07-16-nira-cosase-18-million-unregistered-staffing]],
[[2024-10-15-nira-press-statement-mass-enrolment-status]]. See [[include.divides]], [[digital.rural]],
[[infra.capacity]].

## The civil-society ask, before the exercise began (2024-07-25)

**[[chrgj|CHRGJ]] (NYU School of Law), [[iser-uganda|ISER]] and [[heapi|HEAPI]]** jointly published
*"Seizing the opportunity to improve Uganda's national digital ID system"* on **25 July 2024** — three
weeks after Kisembo's COSASE testimony and three months before the October press statement. Framing the
upgrade and mass enrolment as *"a key opportunity to learn from past experiences"* given the *"many
exclusions and harms [that] have arisen from the current digital ID system"*, it made **five
recommendations**: improve communication and transparency about the new ID; proactively facilitate
participation by vulnerable communities and civil society in policy and design; conduct a
**comprehensive Human Rights Impact Assessment**; take steps to ensure marginalised groups are
proactively included in enrolment and renewal; and put in place concrete plans for the **transition
period** so no rights are violated as digital components are introduced.

**This dates the ask precisely, and that is its value.** Every one of the five was put to Government
**before** enrolment started (May 2025), before the ISER judgment (June 2025), before the database breach
(September 2025) and before the 37.3% rate (February 2026). **No HRIA is recorded in this wiki as having
been conducted.** The same coalition's [[iser-uganda|ISER]] was the lead applicant in the litigation
dismissed the following June — the submission is the pre-litigation route, and it is the one that came
first. *(Author byline: Youssef Farhat. The submission PDF itself is held as a parked lead and is not
cited; this account rests on CHRGJ's dated publication page.)* Source:
[[2024-07-25-chrgj-iser-heapi-seizing-opportunity-uganda-digital-id]]. See [[gov.protect]],
[[gov.policy]], [[include.access]].

## Launch day (2025-05-28): citizenship screening as the friction point

**Adjumani** hosts approximately **244,374 refugees** (2024 UNHCR); District Chairperson **Ben Anyama**
warned that close cultural and language ties with South Sudan risk misidentification. NIRA's **Fred
Balaba** said first-time applicants must present an **LC1 letter carrying parents' names, tribe and
clan, endorsed by the District Internal Security Officer**, to screen out non-citizens — a documentary
prerequisite that lands hardest on exactly the population least likely to hold documents. In
**Koboko**, of a **271,781** population only **217,525** were registered.

**This was formal published policy, not local improvisation.** NIRA's own **15 October 2024** press
statement sets it out as a national requirement: adult first-time applicants must present *"an
introduction letter signed by the LC 1 Chairperson, and stamped by the Internal Security Officers"*,
stating the applicant's **name, date of birth, tribe, clan and nationality** plus **both parents' names,
tribes, clans, nationality and living status**; under-18s must produce **a photocopy of a parent's
National ID** (or a blood relative's, where both parents are deceased). **The under-18 rule is
circular against the very gap NIRA identified**: most of the 18.1m unregistered are children under 17,
and the documentary route to registering a child runs through a parent's card — in a country where only
**2.58m of 10.15m printed cards had been collected (Feb 2026)**. Source:
[[2024-10-15-nira-press-statement-mass-enrolment-status]]. See [[include.access]].

*(The Tororo RDC's claim that "a survey shows more Kenyans than Ugandans currently hold Ugandan
national IDs" is attributed to an **unnamed survey** — an assertion, not a fact.)* Source:
[[2025-05-28 Huge turnout as Nira starts mass registration]]. See [[digital.rural]].

## Civil registration underneath: MVRS and the COVID gap

*(Source is an undated IDRC brief; content runs to **August 2020** — `date_precision: year`,
`date_source: proxy`.)* **[[mvrs-uganda]]** was built by the former **Uganda Registration Services
Bureau ([[ursb]]) from 2011** and moved to [[nira-uganda]] in **2015**.

During the **14-day lockdown from 31 March 2020** (extended to end-June), **civil registration was not
listed as an essential service**, so all district registration offices closed: **January–August 2020
NIRA recorded ~83,200 birth events electronically — 21% of the same period in 2019**. Coverage was
already partial: MVRS ran in **112 of 135 districts** and **222 of 5,230** birth-handling health
facilities. Birth-certificate holding: **21% (2006)**, **31% (2011)**, **32% (2016)**. Source:
[[2020-01-01 Maintaining civil registration amid COVID-19 - Uganda's mobile notification system (IDRC)]].
See [[dpi.registry]].

## Acquisition note (2026-07-20)

The **COSASE record of NIRA's UGX 18.3bn payment to USPC (July 2024) is not held as of 2026-07-20**,
and one acquisition attempt did not surface it. Parliament of Uganda publishes no report or record of
that session; the payment is reached **only through Nexus Media's 2024-07-22 account**, which gives
the figure as 30% of a projected **UGX 61bn** card cost, paid for blank cards in June 2024 after
Attorney General consultation, with the committee unsatisfied as to USPC's "capacity, ownership, and
mandate". **The contract terms behind the UGX 61bn projection are therefore unheld** - the money side
of the card-collection constraint (10,152,559 printed / 2,581,457 collected) rests on a single
trade-press report.

**The UBOS Census 2024 Final Report is still not held (as of 2026-07-20).** The report exists and its
PDF is public (`ubos.org`, *National Population and Housing Census 2024 — Final Report Volume 1
(Main)*, December 2024, 34.4 MB), but automated text extraction returns **only the preface** from both
UBOS's own host and a district mirror — the body appears to be scanned imagery. The **45.9m total** and
the **23,154,716 adults aged 18+** — the denominator under every Uganda coverage-rate claim on this
page — therefore remain **second-hand via NIRA**. This needs a manual clip. Also still unheld: NIRA's
own **2024-07-01 COSASE submission**.

**The Auditor General's NIRA report for FY2024/25 is not published (as of 2026-07-20).** OAG's own
per-agency listing for NIRA (`oag.go.ug/theagency/54`) runs only to **FY2023/24**, and its year filter
offers nothing later. This is a **dated absence with a reason**, not a fetch failure: the entity-level
audit has not been posted, though the consolidated FY2024/25 report has. The finding that the NSIS
**cannot register deaths** — which is what makes the 35m register a cumulative, un-deregistered count —
is load-bearing on this page and currently rests on a document the public record does not yet contain.

A **different** parliamentary primary was acquired instead and is staged in `new/`: Parliament of
Uganda's own record of the COSASE hearing of **30 March 2023** into the Privatisation Unit's
selection of Veridos for the UPPC joint venture. It bears on the same vendor spine but is an earlier,
separate session, and it carries no figures.
