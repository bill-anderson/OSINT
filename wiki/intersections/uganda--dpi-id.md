# Uganda × Digital Identity

**[[ndaga-muntu]]**, run by **[[nira-uganda]]** under the **[[registration-of-persons-act-2015]]**, is
the clearest African case of a **gate that closed faster than the register filled**. By **June 2024**
the ID was already load-bearing for banking, tax, education and social protection. In **February 2026**
the registrar's own review reported a **first-time enrolment rate of 37.3%**. Between those two dates,
in **June 2025**, the High Court dismissed a challenge to mandatory ID on the finding that the
excluded *"appear to me to be few and isolated cases"*.

**That sequence is the argument of this page.** The dependency was built first; the coverage was
counted afterwards; the court ruled in the gap between them.

## The gate: what the ID became load-bearing for

**2024-10-14 — 74 financial institutions on the register.** Per the **Bank of Uganda 2023 annual
report** *(the primary is not held)*, **up to 74 financial institutions have NIRA database access for
digital KYC — 74 listed as of June 2024**: commercial banks, MFIs, a forex bureau, fintechs, insurers
and **one telco**; some at full operation, others in trial. Implementation has run **since 2020** under
the [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]]. In the same account, **[[bank-of-uganda]]'s April 2024
directive** requires ID verification for **all financial transactions of UGX 1 million (US$260) and
above** — a rule *"met with criticism as some citizens said it would slow down business."* Source:
[[2024-10-14 Uganda financial institutions sign on for digital KYC through national ID]]. See
[[dpi.pay]], [[uganda--dpi-pay]].

**2025-11-11 — "your NIN will be your TIN."** [[ura-uganda]] announced the merger of the Tax
Identification Number into the NIN under the **Tax Procedures Amendment Bill**, with **[[ursb]]**
numbers becoming business TINs. Source:
[[2025-11-11 URA Launches Tax Filing Season, Announces Shift from TIN to NIN]].

**Education and social protection.** The **Ministry of Education** requires a NIN for learner
registration on the **Education Management Information System (EMIS)** portal; **learners and staff
without one are excluded from enumeration, planning and budgeting**. **[[sage-uganda]]** — cash
assistance to vulnerable elderly people — benefits **only those on the National Identification
Register**. *(Both reported by [[michael-aboneka]] / DRAA — advocacy analysis, attributed.)* Source:
[[2025-09-13 Uganda's Digital ID Barrier Worsens as Court Delivers Blow to Access]].

**2026-05-28 — the platform.** A Biometric Update explainer records Uganda among the states whose
national digital ID is underpinned by **[[mosip]]** (with Ethiopia and Sierra Leone). Source:
[[2026-05-28 Why decentralized digital identity looks different in every country]].

## The reach: NIRA's own numbers (2026-02-11)

NIRA Registrar **[[claire-ollama]]**, at a press conference on **9 February 2026**, reported the full
transition to the new system: **more than 28 million records migrated**; the exercise started **May
2025** targeting **over 33 million**; total enrolments **more than 35 million**.

| Measure (as of Feb 2026) | Achieved | Target | Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Renewals | 14.3m | 15.8m | **90.5%** |
| First-time enrolments | 6.4m | 17.2m | **37.3%** |
| Cards printed → collected | 2.5m collected | 10.1m printed | **over 60% uncollected** |

**The two rates are the finding.** Renewal — re-documenting people the register already held — ran at
**90.5%**. First-time enrolment — reaching people it never held — ran at **37.3%**. The system is
efficient at serving those already inside it. And even for those enrolled, **10.1 million cards printed
against 2.5 million collected (Feb 2026)** means a NIN in the database is not a card in a hand.

**The stack.** Service provider **UAE-based [[tahaluf]]**; **five modules MOSIP-based**; other vendors
**[[tech5]]**, Technoforte, Entrust and Hyf (per NIRA ED **[[rosemary-kisembo]]**). **Gen. David
Muhoozi** cited measures against **system integration failures and QR-code scanning failures on some
new cards**, with the authentication module fully operational **by end of March [2026]**. Source:
[[2026-02-11 Uganda migrates more than 28M records to new national ID system]]. See [[geopol.gulf]].

## Mass enrolment & renewal (2025–26): the progression, the breach, the diaspora

The 2025/26 exercise now has a full documented arc, corroborating the Feb-2026 figures above from the
primary chronology:

- **Kit and card build-up (Apr 2025).** [[tahaluf]] supplied all **5,665 biometric kits** by January
  2025; two high-speed machines (~**100,000 laser-engraved cards/day**); the Uganda Security Printing
  Company delivered ID stock (1.5m of an expected 8.1m by Apr 2025) with UV/UV-Bi-fluor anti-forgery
  features. The **2014–15 cards expire end-2025** — the renewal driver. Sources:
  [[2025-04-02-nira-acquires-high-capacity-id-machines]], [[2025-05-08-uganda-targets-33m-mass-id-registration]].
- **MOSIP five modules + UPI ambition (Apr 2025).** NIRA launched **five MOSIP modules** (registration,
  renewal, correction, issuance, lost-ID replacement), planned to integrate birth/death registries, and
  flagged a **Unified Payments Interface (UPI)** giving "a free wallet for every citizen" (Kisembo). See
  [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2025-04-14-uganda-launching-5-mosip-modules]].
- **The renewal curve.** From the **27 May 2025** parish rollout: **2.5m → 3.5m (Jun) → 5.3m (Jul) → 10m+
  (Sep) → 13.37m renewed + 6.15m first-time (Dec)** → the **8 Feb 2026 milestone close** at **35m
  registered** (28,571,893 legacy records migrated; 14.31m renewals = 90.5%; 6.42m first-time = 37.3%;
  **10,152,559 cards printed, 7.26m shipped, only 2.58m collected**). Enhanced cards (laser-engraved,
  QR/barcode, ghost image, digital signature) dispatched to all districts from Oct 2025; **diaspora issuance
  opened in the UAE** (Dubai, Feb 2026). Sources: [[2025-06-23-nira-data-protection-mass-id-3-5m]],
  [[2025-07-16-5m-ugandans-renew-national-ids]], [[2025-10-27-enhanced-national-ids-dispatched-districts]],
  [[2025-12-29-over-13-3m-national-ids-renewed-extension]], [[2026-02-09-35-million-registered-national-id-milestone]].
- **The brownfield mechanics (Feb 2026).** NIRA completed **MOSIP's first brownfield migration** — legacy
  ABIS by Neurotechnology, ~**200,000 duplicates** found over 7 months, accelerating from **8,000 to a goal
  of 300,000 records/day** under electoral time pressure; **two card types** (citizen + foreigner) built to
  Ugandan law atop MOSIP. Source: [[2026-02-19-uganda-mosip-brownfield-migration-example]].
- **The database breach (Sep 2025).** A **BBC investigation** alleged NIRA staff sold National
  Identification Register data to a **criminal ring** (local reports linking it to a Dubai sex-trafficking
  network). NIRA opened an investigation with the **[[personal-data-protection-office-uganda|PDPO]]** and
  police; **[[unwanted-witness]]** demanded a fully independent inquiry. This is the concrete instance of
  the surveillance/access-risk the page flags below. See [[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-22-nira-tighten-id-security-after-breach]].
- **Biometric voter verification (Jun 2025).** Museveni insisted **no voting without biometric
  verification**; the **[[electoral-commission-uganda|EC]]** re-tendered for **~10,000 devices** after being
  refused UBOS's (glitch-prone, census-2024) kits — the procurement whose deployment **failed by 9am on
  polling day** (see [[UGA]] hub). Source: [[2025-06-16-museveni-wants-biometric-verification-2026-vote]].
- **TIN→NIN, corroborated.** A second account confirms the **NIN-as-TIN** merger under the Tax Procedures
  Amendment Bill (URSB numbers as business TINs), plus a **3-year income-tax exemption** for post-1-Jul-2025
  firms with ≤UGX 500m capital. Source: [[2025-11-12-ura-shift-from-tin-to-nin]].

## The court: *ISER v Attorney General & NIRA* (dismissed 10 June 2025)

***Initiative for Social and Economic Rights ([[iser-uganda]]) & 2 Others v Attorney General & NIRA*,
HCT-00-CV-MC-0066-2022, High Court of Uganda — dismissed 10 June 2025.** The applicants challenged the
mandatory NIN / National ID requirement under the [[registration-of-persons-act-2015]] for services
including **healthcare and social protection**, arguing disproportionate exclusion of vulnerable
populations including **older persons and women**.

**Verbatim holding:** *"Uganda's ID system is not 'digital' because it operates primarily offline. A
person cannot be denied access simply because of lack of internet connectivity."* The Court further
held that the deponents *"appear to me to be few and isolated cases"* and that the applicants had **not
adduced sufficient evidence of "actual and systemic exclusion"**. An **amicus curiae** by [[cipesa]],
**[[article-19]]** and [[access-now]], carrying expert opinions from **Prof. Tom Fisher**, **Dr Reetika
Khera** and **Prof. Philip Alston**, was unavailing.

**The timing is the point: the Court found "few and isolated cases" on 10 June 2025 — eight months
before NIRA itself published a 37.3% first-time enrolment rate (11 February 2026).** The exclusion the
Court could not see in the evidence is the exclusion the registrar later reported in the numbers.

**The rebuttal — advocacy analysis, attributed.** [[michael-aboneka]], writing for the **Digital Rights
Alliance Africa (DRAA)**, argues the ruling **legitimises** a system that denies access to those
without an ID, and that the "offline" finding is contradicted by the government's own **5-year Digital
Transformation Roadmap, launched 17 August 2023**, which shifts service provision onto DPI — the
National ID system, mobile money, **[[efris]]**, the Express Penalty Scheme, Digital Tax Stamps and
Payment Registration Numbers — **all of which require authentication**.

**The judgment itself is not held by this wiki (GAP-UGA-001)** — the account above rests on DRAA's
analysis, not the primary. Source:
[[2025-09-13 Uganda's Digital ID Barrier Worsens as Court Delivers Blow to Access]]. See
[[gov.legislate]], [[include.divides]].

## Exclusion mechanics: the bodies the biometrics could not read

**2026-03-02 — iris, because fingerprints failed.** [[iritech]] reports **more than 1 million Ugandans
had difficulty registering fingerprint biometrics** (NIRA figures cited by IriTech — **vendor-sourced;
attribute**), **disproportionately elderly people and manual labourers**. That failure is the **stated
reason** for adding **iris capture** via the **IriShield BK** binocular scanner across **more than
5,665 kits** for the **2024–2026 campaign**. The kits carry **[[mosip]] L0 compliance** and were among
the first certified to MOSIP's **MACP Framework** (assessed by BixeLab). The same account independently
corroborates **28 million biometric records migrated to MOSIP — the platform's first completed
brownfield implementation**. Source:
[[2026-03-02 IriTech provides iris biometrics for Uganda's upgraded registration kits]]. See
[[include.access]], [[tech.industry]].

**Read against disability.** **[[asamo-hellen-grace]]**, Minister of State for Disability Affairs,
reported Uganda is **estimated to have more than 1.3 million persons living with disabilities (as of
December 2025)**, with complaints of barriers to loans, banking and essential financial services.
Source:
[[2025-12-02 Minister Asamo Calls for Stronger Financial Inclusion for Persons with Disabilities]].

## The contradiction: how many Ugandans lack an ID — unresolved

**The wiki adopts no value.**

- **~18 million (about 40% of the population)** — asserted by [[michael-aboneka]] / DRAA
  (**2025-09-13**). *The estimate carries **no cited source**; that absence is itself the finding.*
- **A register of more than 35 million** uniquely identifiable people — [[nira-uganda]]
  (**2026-02-11**), which frames coverage rather than the gap.
- NIRA's own **2024 baseline of 17.2m unregistered** minus **6.4m** first-time enrolments implies
  **~10.8m still unregistered (Feb 2026)** — which cleanly contradicts neither.

**Likely definitional root: "registered", "holds a NIN" and "holds a usable card" are three different
populations.** 10.1m printed against 2.5m collected is that same gap in another guise. See
`reviews/contradictions/open/uganda-unregistered-population`.

## Surveillance: mechanism established, causation not asserted

**2024-06-05.** Biometric Update reports **on a Bloomberg / Lighthouse Reports investigation** *(the
primary; **not held** — GAP-UGA-002)*. Human rights lawyer **[[nick-opiyo]]** believes there was an
ulterior motive to his **December 2020 imprisonment**, having "become ensnared in a surveillance
dragnet."

**The clip does not document which ID records were pulled on him.** What it establishes is the
**mechanism**: the "underlying software and datasets are easily accessed by individuals at all levels
of law enforcement." **The causal link is not asserted here.** NIRA ED **[[rosemary-kisembo]]**
**refuted on the record** that law enforcement could readily access the database. Separately, a **2023
[[acme-uganda]] study** concludes Uganda's biometric/digital identity programmes gave room for
surveillance and intrusion on journalism, with journalists targeted. Source:
[[2024-06-05 Ugandan human rights lawyer's arrest exposes use of national ID for surveillance]]. See
[[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]].

## Registration chronology: a slipping schedule against a hard deadline

Slated **1 June 2024** → rescheduled **July 2024** → **October 2024** → **actually launched 27 May
2025**. **A ~12-month slip.**

The **October 2024** plan: **5,680 kits**; targets of **17.2m** first-time registrations and **15.8m**
renewals; **13,000 personnel** across **10,717 parishes** — aligned to the Electoral Commission's **30
November** deadline for the voters' register ahead of the **2026 general elections**. *(By March 2026
IriTech reports **more than 5,665 kits** deployed — rounding against the 5,680 plan, not a conflict.)*

**Each source is accurate as of its date — the slip against a hard electoral deadline is signal, not
contradiction**, and connects to the biometric voter-verification kit failure on polling day (see
[[UGA]]).

**Vendor unresolved as of August 2024.** NIRA had announced plans to work with **[[veridos]]**, amid
controversy over the card-production contract with **Muehlbauer High Tech International**, previously
the **NSIS** supplier and *"embroiled in a scandal concerning overcharging."* **Single-sourced
(GAP-UGA-003); do not infer the outcome from Veridos' absence from the 2026 provider list.** Sources:
[[2025-05-28 Huge turnout as Nira starts mass registration]],
[[2024-08-19 Uganda Prepares Large-scale Biometric Registration for National ID]].

## Launch day (2025-05-28): citizenship screening as the friction point

**Adjumani** hosts approximately **244,374 refugees** (2024 UNHCR); District Chairperson **Ben Anyama**
warned that close cultural and language ties with South Sudan risk misidentification. NIRA's **Fred
Balaba** said first-time applicants must present an **LC1 letter carrying parents' names, tribe and
clan, endorsed by the District Internal Security Officer**, to screen out non-citizens — a documentary
prerequisite that lands hardest on exactly the population least likely to hold documents. In
**Koboko**, of a **271,781** population only **217,525** were registered.

*(The Tororo RDC's claim that "a survey shows more Kenyans than Ugandans currently hold Ugandan
national IDs" is attributed to an **unnamed survey** — an assertion, not a fact.)* Source:
[[2025-05-28 Huge turnout as Nira starts mass registration]]. See [[digital.rural]].

## Civil registration underneath: MVRS and the COVID gap

*(Source is an undated IDRC brief; content runs to **August 2020** — `date_precision: year`,
`date_source: proxy`.)* **[[mvrs-uganda]]** was built by the former **Uganda Registration Services
Bureau ([[ursb]]) from 2011** and moved to [[nira-uganda]] in **2015**.

During the **14-day lockdown from 31 March 2020** (extended to end-June), **civil registration was not
listed as an essential service**, so all district registration offices closed: **January–August 2020
NIRA recorded ~83,200 birth events electronically — 21% of the same period in 2019**. Coverage was
already partial: MVRS ran in **112 of 135 districts** and **222 of 5,230** birth-handling health
facilities. Birth-certificate holding: **21% (2006)**, **31% (2011)**, **32% (2016)**. Source:
[[2020-01-01 Maintaining civil registration amid COVID-19 - Uganda's mobile notification system (IDRC)]].
See [[dpi.registry]].
