# Seychelles × Connectivity

A well-connected archipelago now adding **redundancy and reach**: **Starlink** for the outer islands, a
**second 5G operator**, and a Chinese-vendor **emergency-comms** network — with the incumbent
**[[cable-and-wireless-seychelles|CWS]]** and Airtel both riding foreign infrastructure partners.

## Starlink — resilience for the outer islands

- **Licensed → incorporated → live (2026).** The Cabinet approved a **Starlink ISP licence** (18 Feb
  2026, subject to conditions); Starlink **incorporated as a local company** and entered final licensing
  (VP Pillay to the National Assembly, May 2026); the service **launched** (Jun 2026) across **Mahé,
  Praslin and La Digue**, with **outer-island** expansion planned. DICT framed it as **complementing**
  fixed/mobile networks and adding **capacity and resilience** for remote areas (remote work, tele-
  education, telemedicine, e-gov). See [[data.satellite]], [[geopol.usa]], [[include.access]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-06-nation-starlink-cleared-for-takeoff]], [[2026-05-28-nation-starlink-incorporated-locally]],
  [[2026-06-08-seychellestimes-starlink-launch]].

## 5G — a second operator

- **Airtel 5G (Jul 2026).** Airtel Seychelles switched on commercial **5G** (mobile + **Home Wi-Fi**, up
  to **500 Mbps**) with an **SBC** content bundle (+DStv) — the **second** 5G operator after **CWS** (5G
  on Mahé since **2020, via Huawei**). MD Eddy Kapuku: "the first to make 5G truly work for the Seychellois
  pocket and home." Source: [[2026-07-06-seychellestimes-airtel-5g]].

## Emergency-communications resilience

- **Hytera × CWS MoU (MWC 2025).** China's **Hytera** and **[[cable-and-wireless-seychelles|CWS]]** signed
  an MoU to build a **resilient emergency-communications redundant network** for uninterrupted comms
  during disasters — **push-to-talk-over-cellular (PoC)** and **mission-critical (MCX)** cores integrating
  with CWS's 4G/5G, for public safety, energy, transport and tourism. See [[geopol.china]], [[infra.cybersec]].
  Source: [[2025-03-04-developingtelecoms-hytera-cws-mou]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: for a disaster-exposed archipelago, connectivity policy is a **resilience** portfolio —
  satellite for the outer islands, a redundant emergency-comms core, dual 5G operators. The notable
  feature is **vendor pluralism across geopolitical lines**: CWS on **Huawei** (5G) and **Hytera**
  (emergency comms), Airtel on **Ericsson**, and **Starlink** (US) layered on top. Redundancy of
  suppliers *is* a form of sovereignty for a small state — but it also spreads dependence rather than
  reducing it.
