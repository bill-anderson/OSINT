# Algeria × Cybersecurity

Algeria stood up a **whole institutional cyber framework in early 2026**, framed as digital sovereignty and
anchored in the **Ministry of National Defence**. The trigger is a real threat surface: **Kaspersky recorded
70m+ cyberattacks on Algeria in 2024** (13m phishing attempts, ~750k malicious attachments blocked), while the
**ITU 2024 Global Cybersecurity Index** still places Algeria at **Tier 3 ("establishing")**.

## The institutional framework (Decree 26-07, Jan 2026)

**Presidential Decree 26-07** (7 Jan 2026) mandates **cyber / data-protection units in every public body** and
sits atop a set of specialised institutions:

- **[[assi|ASSI]]** — Information Systems Security Agency, under the **MDN**: implements national cyber policy,
  monitors cyberspace, defends critical state infrastructure.
- **National Council for Information Systems Security** — reports directly to the **President**; sets strategy
  and approves major security policies.
- **[[anpdcp|ANPDCP/ANPDP]]** — independent authority enforcing **Law 18-07** (amended by **Law 25-11**);
  president **Samir Bourehil**.
- **[[high-commission-digitalisation-algeria|HCD/HCN]]** — ensures the National Data Center & National Cloud
  meet cyber standards.
- **National Authority for the Prevention & Combating of ICT-Related Crimes** — monitors electronic
  communications.

## Strategy, CERT & capacity

- **National Strategy for Information Systems Security 2025–2029** ([[algeria-national-cybersecurity-strategy-2025-2029]])
  — unveiled by ASSI (MDN) **3 Mar 2026**, approved by President Tebboune; three objectives (protect critical
  infrastructure, secure sensitive state data, ensure service continuity).
- **[[mpt-cert|MPT-CERT]]** — sectoral post/telecom incident-response centre inaugurated **2026-06-15** by
  Minister Zerrouki with **South-Korean (KOICA)** technical support, alongside a Cyber Security Forum.
- **Training & events:** a **2nd RSSI cohort** at Sidi Abdellah (ASSI/MDN, 2026-06); the first **CYS-D 2026**
  Cyber Security Days, Algiers (2026-06-14); a **child-online-protection cyber-vigilance cell** (green line
  1111, 2026-06); the first **AI & cybersecurity start-up cluster** (2026-04-20); **NEC** next-gen firewalls
  for Ooredoo (2026-04).

## International cooperation

A **[[algeria-russia-cybersecurity-mou|Russia MoU]]** (Positive Technologies × Proxylan, a CERIST subsidiary,
2025-11) for joint research, training and tech transfer — a notable non-Western cyber tie (no `geopol.russia`
slug yet; see ISSUE-014). A cyber-policy analysis (2026-04) puts Algeria's EGDI at 0.56 with ~38m internet
users (2025).
