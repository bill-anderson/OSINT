# Namibia × Cybersecurity

Namibia's digital build-out has outrun its cyber defences: a **Tier-4 ("evolving")** posture, an **incomplete
legal framework**, a wave of high-profile breaches, and an **AI-fuelled fraud ecosystem** — the case for treating
security as infrastructure, not an afterthought.

## The threat picture (rising fast)

- **Nam-CSIRT statistics.** The **[[nam-csirt]]** (housed at **[[cran]]**) 2025 Landscape Report recorded **1.7m
  cyberattacks (+37% YoY)** and **2.23m vulnerabilities**, with **Q1-2026** alone at **103,085 threats +
  367,670 vulnerabilities**; over **1m** incidents were flagged in H1-2025, and a paywalled report cited a
  **314% spike**. Government allocated **~US$1m** to cyber for FY2026/27. ITU's 2024 Global Cybersecurity Index
  put Namibia in **Tier 4 (37.93/100)**; NDP6 admits cyber readiness of **37 vs a 2030 target of 65**. See
  [[data.statistics]]. Sources: [[2026-04-13-namibia-reports-37-surge-cyberattacks-2025-wearetech]],
  [[2026-07-14-namibia-pushes-cyber-security-reforms-itweb]],
  [[2025-07-30-cyber-threats-surge-amid-namibia-digital-growth-namibiansun]].
- **A run of major breaches (2024-2026).** **Telecom Namibia** lost **626GB / 490,000+ files** to Hunters
  International (Dec 2024, exposing 8 ministries, 5 regional councils, 10 municipalities); the **Namibia Airports
  Company** lost **~500GB** to the **INC ransomware group** (Mar 2026, dumped on the dark web — the second INC
  victim after **Otjiwarongo municipality**); the **NSFAF** published 7,000+ students' details on its own site
  (Oct 2025); **[[paratus]]** was hit in early 2025. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-21-digital-economy-needs-cybersecurity-namibian]].

## AI-fuelled fraud

- **Deepfakes of ministers (2025-09).** A deepfake video showed the **PM, Deputy PM and ICT Minister
  [[emma-theofelus]]** "endorsing" a fake investment scheme (N$35,000/week); Theofelus disavowed it. It
  overshadowed the ICT ministry's **e-signature** drive (ETA 2019; nationwide Feb 2026). Nam-CSIRT later
  catalogued an AI-driven fraud ecosystem: deepfake investment scams, **municipal double-extortion ransomware**,
  Telegram phishing and delivery-service smishing. See [[tech.ai]]. Sources:
  [[2025-09-17-ai-abuse-overshadows-namibia-e-signature-drive-itweb]],
  [[2026-04-13-namibia-reports-37-surge-cyberattacks-2025-wearetech]].
- **Financial-sector fraud.** Banks lost **N$65m (Jan–Oct 2025)**; the Bank of Namibia's 2025 report put annual
  banking-fraud losses at **N$73.9m** (up from ~N$8.7m in 2020). The government convened an inaugural **Fraud &
  Cybersecurity Summit** (MICT / **[[bank-of-namibia|BoN]]** / Bankers Association / Nam-CSIRT / Financial
  Industry Cyber Council). See [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2025-11-25-namibia-partners-with-banks-to-combat-fraud-itweb]].

## Law and institutions catching up

- **Legal framework.** The **Cybercrime Bill** and the **[[namibia-data-protection-bill|Data Protection Bill]]**
  are being finalised; in the interim, enforcement relies on the ETA, Communications Act and Penal Code. Cabinet
  approved a **government-sector CSIRT + SOCs** and cyber-skills training (Nov 2025, OPM coordinating). See
  [[gov.legislate]]. Sources: [[2025-11-21-namibia-cabinet-approves-cyber-crime-proposals-itweb]],
  [[2026-07-14-namibia-pushes-cyber-security-reforms-itweb]].
- **Capacity-building.** **NIPAM** signed an MoU with the **Global Cybersecurity Centre** (4 Jun 2026) to train
  public servants; and Namibia signed a **cyber-security MoU with India** (Nandi-Ndaitwah–Modi, State House,
  2025 — alongside UPI/digital-governance cooperation; see [[geopol.india]]). See [[capacity.training]].
  Sources: [[2026-06-09-nipam-global-cybersecurity-centre-partnership-techafricanews]],
  [[2025-07-10-namibia-india-cyber-security-alliance-itweb]].
