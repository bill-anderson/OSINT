# Congo-Brazzaville × Connectivity & divides

Congo-Brazzaville has a **regulator that enforces** ([[arpce-congo|ARPCE]]), a **World-Bank/EU-financed
rural programme that underdelivers** ([[patn-congo|PATN]]), and a **structurally expensive market** — with
the satellite thread running, unusually, to **Europe (Eutelsat/OneWeb), not Starlink**.

## The regulator bites; the cost problem is structural

- **Enforcement.** ARPCE issued a **formal mise en demeure to MTN and Airtel** (Apr 2026) after Feb-2026
  drive tests across 20 interior localities (Airtel absent from 4G in 5, blaming Congo Télécom backbone
  outages) — hard enforcement, rare in the corpus; and ordered **flawless performance + national roaming**
  ahead of **March 2026 elections**. It also drove **fibre-cable mutualisation/undergrounding** (co-
  regulation with the energy regulator; introduces GVA/CanalBox, Silicon Connect). Sources:
  [[2026-04-09-arpce-mise-en-demeure-mtn-airtel-qos-ldb]],
  [[2026-02-19-arpce-network-performance-march-elections-techafricanews]],
  [[2025-03-31-deploiement-fibre-optique-urbanisation-reseaux-arpce-ldb]].
- **The cost problem, quantified (the sweep's sharpest finding).** Backhaul **spectrum fees ≈ 5% of
  operator revenue vs a 0.5% SSA median — 10×**; rural coverage **33% 3G / 21% 4G** (~80% of rural
  Congolese have no 4G, half no coverage at all); plus delayed spectrum assignments, DRC cross-border
  interference and technology-neutrality restrictions. This is *why* connecting rural Congo "costs three
  times more than it should." Source: [[2026-07-16-congo-rural-backhaul-spectrum-fees-techafricanews]].

## PATN rural: underdelivery, dated

The World-Bank **[[patn-congo|PATN]]** rural build — with an **EU/EIB limb** (FCFA 27bn: 10bn EU grant +
17bn EIB loan, Global Gateway framing) on top of the WB $100m — was judged **"peu satisfaisant"** at
mid-term: **only 20 of 76 broadband sites operational after two years** (an ADIAC editorial), with 16
specific rural localities commissioned across six departments (>30,000 people), and an **ARPCE–INACOM
(Angola) spectrum agreement**. *(Figure to reconcile: two outlets say "66 sites," Ibombo says "76" —
20+56=76, so 66 is likely a propagated transcription error.)* Sources:

## Backbone, satellite & the incumbent

- **Single-cable dependence exposed.** Congo has relied on **WACS since 2012**; weeks of outages pushed
  ARPCE to seek a switch to another cable within three weeks (Dow Africa delayed) — nothing was held on
  COG's subsea position. Source: [[2026-01-20-congo-seeks-new-subsea-cable-wacs-failures-connectingafrica]].
- **Satellite runs to Europe, not Starlink.** ARPCE built **LEO/HAPS regulatory capacity** and met
  **[[eutelsat|Eutelsat]]/OneWeb** — the window's only satellite thread, and it is EU, not Starlink.
  Sources: [[2025-10-04-arpce-expertise-satellites-leo-eutelsat-oneweb-ldb]],
  [[2025-10-03-arpce-satellite-leo-regulatory-mission-techafricanews]].
- **China vectors.** Airtel Congo × **Wing Wah** (Southernpec subsidiary) built Congo's **first private
  industrial network** at the Banga Kayo oil field; and **BeiDou** was proposed for the Congo-Ocean Railway
  (CFCO) — Chinese satellite navigation into critical transport infrastructure. Sources:
  [[2025-08-18-airtel-congo-wing-wah-private-network-connectingafrica]],
  [[2026-03-04-congo-cfco-beidou-satellite-china-techafricanews]].
- **The incumbent — [[congo-telecom]].** First operator in COG to earn **ISO 9001:2015**, and pitching
  itself as a **Central-African digital-transit hub** (courting FAGACE/Africa50 at the AfDB Annual Meetings
  in Brazzaville; a Kinshasa delegation on interconnection/roaming). Sources:
  [[2026-03-29-congo-telecom-iso-9001-certification-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-06-21-congo-telecom-central-africa-digital-hub-ambition-techreviewafrica]].

## The GSMA verdict (re-dated to Jun 2026)

The **[[gsma|GSMA]]** Republic-of-Congo report — **launched 22 Jun 2026** at its Digital Africa Summit,
Brazzaville (the wiki previously held it proxy-dated 2026-01-01) — models **+FCFA 870bn / $1.5bn and
144,000 jobs by 2030** under policy reform, against **86% 4G coverage but only 19% mobile-internet use**,
a Digital Nations index of **26/100** and policy sub-index **33/100**. Sources:
[[2026-06-22-gsma-report-fcfa-870bn-144000-jobs-congo-techafricanews]],
