# Seychelles × E-Government

For a small, high-capacity state, 2025–26 was about **consolidating the shopfront**: a decade-overdue
government-portal relaunch, a justice-sector case system, a trade e-certificate and a business one-stop —
framed by President Herminie's ambition to put Seychelles **"among the world's leading digital economies
within five years."**

## The government portal

- **egov.sc relaunch (Jun 2026).** VP/ICT Minister **Sébastien Pillay** relaunched the **egov.sc**
  government portal ("Access Government Services, With Just One Click," DICT + Dept of Information),
  coinciding with the **50th Independence anniversary** — the portal had **not been meaningfully reviewed
  in over a decade** (little more than a public-servant email gateway). DICT PS Benjamin Choppy led
  delivery. See [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2026-06-24-techafricanews-egov-portal]].

## Sector platforms

- **Electronic Case Management System (ECMS, Jun 2026).** President Herminie launched the **ECMS** for the
  justice sector (AG's Office) — shared calendars, virtual-meeting links, case-data retrieval and secure
  chat — **now fully live**. Developed with **US State Department** funding, via **UNODC** (Global
  Maritime Crime Programme) and **UNOPS**. See [[geopol.usa]]. Source: [[2026-06-23-statehouse-ecms-launch]].
- **Electronic Certificate of Origin (e-CO, Jul 2026).** The **[[seychelles-revenue-commission|SRC]]**,
  with the **COMESA** Secretariat, launched the **e-CO** for trade facilitation and supply-chain/border
  security — traders urged to use it to claim **reduced duties** under trade agreements. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source: [[2026-07-04-comesa-seychelles-eco-launch]].
- **Ease of Doing Business portal (Nov 2025).** The Ministry of Investment, Entrepreneurship & Industry
  launched an **EODB one-stop portal** (services, document submission, payments in one place; two
  journeys — government-services and entrepreneur), with **NRD Companies** as tech partner (low-code GxP
  platform, 15+ services), full rollout targeted **30 Jun 2026**. See [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-07-nrd-seychelles-eodb-portal]] *(vendor press release — verify against the ministry primary)*.

## Reads

- Seychelles' e-gov story is **consolidation, not foundation** — unifying fragmented agency systems into
  single front doors (egov.sc, EODB) and digitising specific workflows (justice, trade). The dependency
  is on **external finance/vendors** (US/UNODC for ECMS, COMESA for e-CO, NRD for EODB), typical for a
  micro-state; the differentiator is the political ambition ("leading digital economy in five years")
  against a genuinely high institutional base.
