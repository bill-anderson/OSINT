# South Sudan × Connectivity

South Sudan has **one 200km Uganda–Juba fibre line**, the region's **most expensive data (~$23.70/GB)**,
and **~15.7% internet penetration** — but 2025–26 saw the first serious backbone push (a national fibre
programme + a private MTN/Bayobab backbone) even as an **FX crisis** and a **social-media shutdown**
exposed the fragility of the sector and of digital rights.

## The fibre backbone — first-ever

- **The national programme.** A fibre-optic implementation committee approved a **$9m design budget**
  (Jun 2025, Phase 2), targeting a first-ever network by ~Sept 2025 (World Bank-funded, tied explicitly
  to **AI/data-centre ambitions** at the Kigali AI Summit). It scaled to a **2,400km** cable from the
  **Indian Ocean via Kenya** (announced Lusaka, Oct 2025) plus a Kenya cross-border link (feasibility/EIA,
  from Aug 2025) — to cut reliance on expensive satellite. See [[finance.new]], [[tech.ai]]. Sources:
  [[2025-06-30-9m-fiber-optic-design-onecitizendaily]], [[2025-04-07-fibre-optics-boost-ai-onecitizendaily]],
  [[2025-10-09-2400km-fiber-optic-network-techafricanews]], [[2025-08-08-fibre-from-kenya-itweb]].
- **The private MTN/Bayobab backbone.** **[[bayobab-south-sudan|Bayobab Infra Solutions]]** (MTN Digital
  Infrastructure) won a **15-year NCA licence** (eff. **16 Oct 2025**) to build/operate national and
  cross-border fibre, IP transit and leased circuits (part of the **East 2 West** corridor); with **NCT
  South Sudan**, the **National Backbone Fiber Network Project** moved to **execution** (early 2026),
  linking eastern and western corridors through Juba. See [[gov.regional]]. Sources:
  [[2025-11-25-bayobab-15yr-fibre-licence-techafricanews]], [[2026-03-04-nct-bayobab-national-backbone-fiber-techreviewafrica]].

## Operators

- **[[mtn-south-sudan]]** launched Africa-first-tier **eSIM** (Jan 2025), won a new **15-year mobile
  operator licence** (Apr 2025), and cut tariffs **25%** (Dec 2025) — the ICT minister pressing for more.
  Sources: [[2025-01-22-mtn-esim-launch-telecomreviewafrica]], [[2025-04-14-mtn-15yr-mobile-licence-techafricanews]],
  [[2025-12-12-mtn-25pct-tariff-cut-laws-techafricanews]].

## Affordability and the FX crisis

- **Most expensive data in the region.** **~$23.70/GB** (Nov 2025) — vs sub-$1 in Kenya/Nigeria/Ghana —
  "shuts millions out of the digital world" (editorial). Source: [[2025-11-27-editorial-high-mobile-data-cost-onecitizendaily]].
- **The FX squeeze (Jun 2026).** The NCA approved a **phased tariff exchange-rate adjustment** to avert
  "network collapse": operators were forced to bill at an **outdated 4,526 SSP/$** while the market ran
  at **~27,000 SSP/$** — huge FX losses on a sector generating 18–25bn SSP/yr. See [[include.access]].
  Source: [[2026-06-25-nca-telecom-tariff-adjustment-radiotamazuj]].

## Scale and 2026 agenda

- **Growth.** Internet users **1.3m → 1.9m** (2024→2025), penetration **12% → 15.7%**; mobile **3.97m →
  4.47m** — via the National Broadband Roadmap, the Digital Government Strategy and a **Starlink** rural
  partnership. Source: [[2025-07-03-nca-internet-growth-figures-eyeradio]].
- **2026 agenda.** The NCA prioritised **rural connectivity**, **child online protection** and regulatory
  reform (ITU/EAC cooperation) — access still heavily concentrated in Juba. See [[include.divides]].
  Source: [[2026-01-05-rural-connectivity-child-online-safety-2026-onecitizendaily]].

## The social-media shutdown (Jan 2025)

- The NCA ordered ISPs to **block all social media** for **30–90 days** amid unrest (22 Jan 2025);
  **Zain** and **[[mtn-south-sudan]]** notified users that **TikTok and Facebook** were cut — journalists
  and rights defenders worked via VPN (CPJ). A reminder that the same regulator building connectivity also
  wields the kill switch. See [[gov.legislate]], [[south-sudan--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-24-social-media-shutdown-cpj]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the backbone build is genuine sovereignty progress (own fibre, less satellite
  dependence) — but it runs through a **foreign private operator** (MTN/Bayobab holding the national
  backbone, alongside MGI on the gateway/data centre — see [[SSD]] hub), and the FX crisis shows how a
  collapsing currency can undo it. The social-media shutdown is the counter-signal: connectivity as an
  instrument of control, not only inclusion.
