# Namibia × Connectivity

Namibia's connectivity posture cuts both ways: the regulator has refused a 100%-foreign-owned Starlink on data-sovereignty grounds, while the ICT ministry pushes a free-access counter-move to close the divide from the demand side.

## What we know

**Sovereignty vs access, both ways (2026-01/03).** [[cran]] **declined** [[starlink]]'s comprehensive licence and spectrum on **100% foreign ownership** (no Namibian shareholding), mirroring South Africa and leaving a 90-day reconsideration window (Starlink now licensed in **27** African countries). On access, ICT minister **[[emma-theofelus]]** committed **free internet for 7 years** to schools/clinics under **N$80m / nine RAN towers**, while [[cran]] proposed **tax breaks on 4G devices** after MTC reported **<50%** 4G-tower utilisation in Kunene — coverage without affordable devices is not transformation.
