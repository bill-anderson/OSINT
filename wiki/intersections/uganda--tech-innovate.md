# Uganda × Innovation, Skills & the Startup Economy

Uganda's innovation story has a distinctive **deep-tech and manufacturing** framing — "start with the hard
stuff" — backed by tax incentives and skills programmes, but shadowed by a **funding drought** and a
**scale problem**: the constraint is not talent but getting solutions past pilot stage.

## Policy support and the ecosystem's shape

- **Three-year startup tax holiday (2025).** A proposed **Income Tax Bill** amendment would give startups a
  **three-year income-tax exemption** (echoed by the post-July-2025 ≤UGX 500m-capital exemption on
  [[uganda--dpi-id]]). Uganda ranks **95th globally / 3rd in East Africa** (StartupBlink 2024; Kampala 368th),
  home to Tugende, SafeBoda, Numida, Rocket Health — but **ecosystem investment fell 60% to $10.6m (2023)**
  from $26.8m (2022), and StartupBlink flags over-concentration in Kampala. See [[gov.policy]], [[finance.new]].
  Source: [[2025-04-02-uganda-startup-income-tax-exemption]].
- **"Prepared the bride, now wants a dowry" (Jun 2025).** The 2nd **Uganda Investor Summit** was staged as a
  statement of intent — investors ferried in an **electric bus built by state-backed Kiira Motors** — framing
  Uganda's bet on **manufacturing, hardware, logistics and deep science** over startup hype, now seeking
  capital to commercialise (Minister **Monica Musenero**, [[sti-op-uganda]]). See [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-20-uganda-tech-sector-investor-summit]].

## The scale problem

- **Talent isn't the bottleneck — scaling is (Jun 2026).** At the Refactory Academy expo (National ICT
  Innovation Hub, Nakawa), **[[mtn-uganda]]** argued the biggest barrier is the **inability to move solutions
  beyond pilot** (limited infrastructure, investment, partnerships, market adoption) — calling for
  collaboration over competition and opening MoMo platforms to developers. Source:
  [[2026-06-26-mtn-uganda-calls-support-scale-home-grown-innovations]].

## Skills and inclusion

- **Mbale ICT Hub (Feb 2026).** A **Uganda–UK MoU** (UK CSO Spotlight on Africa) established a **UGX 3bn ICT
  Hub in Mbale** for young women's ICT/entrepreneurship careers — building on two existing Mbale centres that
  **graduated 800 young women (60% now employed)**, explicitly targeting the **poorest communities**, not the
  middle class. Decentralising skills beyond Kampala. See [[capacity.training]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-20-mbale-ict-hub-uk-mou-women-skills]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Uganda's "do tech differently" pitch — deep-tech, manufacturing, a home-built electric bus —
  is a genuinely sovereignty-minded stance (own the hard capacity, not just the app layer), and it dovetails
  with the National Innovator Registry's local-build push (see [[uganda--dpi-govtech]]). But the numbers puncture
  the rhetoric: **ecosystem funding fell 60%** in a year, and the sharpest voices (MTN) say the real problem is
  **scaling, not inventing**. The skills work (Mbale women, 60% employed) is the most concrete win. The gap is
  capital and market depth — the same "dowry" the Investor Summit went looking for.
