# Equatorial Guinea × Digital Payments

A **cash-heavy, oil-driven** economy trying to go cash-lite from a thin base — **CEMAC financial inclusion ~32%**
and, per a 2026 reference profile, **fewer than 5–10 fintech providers**, dominated by banks and telcos (Orange
Money the main mobile-money entry; strategy anchored in NDP 2035 and the World-Bank **ADIGE** agenda).

- **National ATM/POS plan (2025-05).** A PM-driven plan to deploy ATMs and POS at critical sites; the binding
  constraint surfaced as **connectivity** — banks warned outages compromise electronic devices, **[[gitge-gnq|GITGE]]**
  stressed last-mile fibre is essential, and **Muni Dinero** presented its CEMAC mobile-money app.
- **VP bank-sector push (2026-02).** VP Nguema Obiang Mangue pressed the **five commercial banks** (BANGE,
  CCEIBANK, Société Générale, BGFIBank, Ecobank) and **BEAC** to expand ATM/POS coverage and card issuance — a
  cash-alternative drive tied to **tourism, VAT control and anti-evasion** (not cash elimination).
- **Products.** **[[bange-gnq|BANGE MOBILE GE]]** (G-MAT Pay / Visa prepaid) and **[[getesa-gnq|GETESA]]'s Getesa
  Money** — bank- and telco-led rails widening the payments layer.
