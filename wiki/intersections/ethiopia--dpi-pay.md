# Ethiopia × Digital Payments (`dpi.pay`)

Ethiopia's cash-light push runs on two engines — [[ethio-telecom]]'s [[telebirr]] and
[[safaricom-ethiopia]]'s M-PESA — with the [[ethswitch]] national switch turning
interoperability, rather than licensing, into the growth lever.

## What we know

- **M-PESA Ethiopia's interoperability surge (2025-Q4, pub 2026-03).** [[safaricom]] M-PESA
  active users jumped **+258.5% YoY to 5.2m** (quarter to Dec 2025) after the **Oct 2025
  [[ethswitch]] integration** linked it to 30+ banks/wallets with real-time transfers and EthQR
  merchant payments (50,000+ merchants); transaction value +192%, Safaricom Ethiopia service
  revenue +54.2% to $75m. Interoperability, not a licence, is the growth lever, replicating the
  Kenyan model. See [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 M-PESA Ethiopia surges 258% as EthSwitch fuels growth]].
- **Ethiopia — Telebirr and M-PESA at national scale (2026-01/02).** Two engines anchor
  Ethiopia's cash-light push: [[ethio-telecom]]'s **[[telebirr]]** reached **72m customers** by
  mid-2025 (payments, savings, micro-loans), while [[safaricom-ethiopia]]'s M-PESA passed
  **12.2m active customers** (+71.7% YoY) — plus the [[ethswitch]] active-user surge above.
  **[[digital-ethiopia-2030]]** targets a **sevenfold** rise in digital payments via Telebirr
  and a National Payment Gateway. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[include.access]], [[infra.connect]].
  Sources:
