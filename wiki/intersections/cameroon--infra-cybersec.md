# Cameroon × Cybersecurity

Cameroon's cybersecurity story has **two faces the wiki must hold together**: a genuine
capability-and-sovereignty build led by **[[antic-cameroon|ANTIC]]**, and a **rights-side counterweight**
where the same legal apparatus underpins surveillance and shutdowns.

## The capability build (ANTIC)

- **DNS sovereignty.** ANTIC signed the national **`.cm` zone with DNSSEC on 8 Apr 2025** ("an act of
  digital sovereignty"), then set hard targets: from **56.60% national DNSSEC validation** (Dec 2025 —
  Orange Cameroun just **2.75%**, most operators **0%**) toward **98% DNSSEC / 100% RPKI** by H1 2026,
  later **100% DNSSEC by end-2026** plus an **IPv4→IPv6** push (6th National DNS Forum, Jun 2026), with
  ICANN and AFRINIC support. Operator-level figures the held wire copies lacked. Sources:
  [[2025-12-09-leconomie-antic-dnssec-rpki-cyberspace-defence]],
  [[2025-12-10-dnssec-validation-cm-domain-antic-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-06-19-antic-national-dns-forum-sovereign-internet-techafricanews]].
- **CIRT & threat surface.** The World Bank / [[patnuc]] hardened ANTIC's CIRT (**~FCFA 735m**, handover
  **20 Jan 2026**); ANTIC reports **>19m threats blocked in 2024** and **32,500 cybercrime complaints in
  2025** (+30%), and ran a **fintech cyber-risk workshop** (Nov 2025). Sources:
  [[2026-01-21-antic-cirt-cybersecurity-systems-patnuc-techafricanews]],
  [[2025-02-13-lejour-19-million-cyber-threats-blocked-2024]],
  [[2025-11-17-leconomie-antic-fintech-cyber-risk-workshop]].

## The rights-side counterweight

- **Cybercrime law as a speech instrument.** A DIPES workshop ([[paradigm-initiative]] × Civic Watch,
  Jun 2026) critiqued **[[cameroon-cybercrime-law-2010]]** (Law 2010/012) as being used against
  expression, citing the **23 Oct 2025 election-period internet restrictions** as an ICCPR / African
  Charter violation. Source: [[2026-06-16-lejour-cybercrime-laws-threaten-freedoms]].
- **State surveillance infrastructure.** Cameroon borrowed **XAF 39bn for urban video-surveillance**
  (decree 17 Dec 2025) — surveillance capacity that the wiki's capability framing had missed entirely.
  Source: [[2025-12-23-lejour-urban-videosurveillance-loan]].

The two faces are the point: cybersecurity here is simultaneously a **sovereignty asset** and a **civil-
liberties risk**.
