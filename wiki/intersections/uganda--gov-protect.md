# Uganda × Data Protection & Digital Rights

Uganda's **[[personal-data-protection-office-uganda|PDPO]]** — an independent office under
**[[nita-uganda|NITA-U]]** enforcing the **Data Protection and Privacy Act (Cap 97/97)** — has become an
unusually **assertive regulator of Big Tech**, building a jurisprudence on **extraterritorial reach and
cross-border-transfer adequacy** against Google and Meta while prosecuting local offenders. The tension with
[[UGA]]'s record is stark: the same state that ran a **selective, post-hoc** enforcement around the 2026
polls (hub) is, in the commercial sphere, holding multinationals to the letter of the law.

## The Big-Tech rulings

- **Ssekamwa v Google (Jul 2025).** The PDPO (acting director **Baker Birikujja**) found **Google in breach**
  for **operating without PDPO registration** and **transferring Ugandans' data abroad without demonstrated
  adequate safeguards** — 30 days to register, name a DPO and prove Section 19/Regulation 30 compliance. **No
  data-localisation order**, but all cross-border transfers must comply with Ugandan law, applied
  **extraterritorially** to any entity monetising Ugandan users (Google has a local tax presence). Google
  argued the rules bind only Uganda-domiciled controllers — **which the ruling rejected**. Following a major
  **2022 Uganda Securities Exchange breach**. See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-20-pdpo-google-breach-cross-border-data]].
- **AdLegal v WhatsApp/Meta (Feb 2026).** The PDPO delivered a **landmark ruling against WhatsApp LLC and
  [[meta|Meta]]** over the 2021 privacy policy (data-sharing across ~10m Ugandan users without valid
  consent). It found breaches of **data minimisation** for analytics/ecosystem integration and
  insufficiently-evidenced cross-border transfers, **joined Meta** to the case (piercing separate corporate
  personality on the factual data-sharing ecosystem), reaffirmed that **recipient countries must offer
  equivalent protection**, and newly defined **"material harm" to include autonomy, self-determination and
  dignity** — building directly on Ssekamwa v Google. *(Reported via a legal-analysis column — treat as
  analysis of the primary decision.)* Source: [[2026-02-27-pdpo-whatsapp-meta-cross-border-ruling]].

Together these establish a coherent doctrine: **the DPPA reaches any multinational monetising Ugandans, and
data leaving Uganda must land somewhere with equivalent protection.** That is a genuine data-sovereignty
assertion, made through adjudication rather than a localisation mandate.

## Enforcement at home, and the awareness gap

- **First local prosecution (Dec 2025).** A **Vuka Africa** finance manager was prosecuted (PDPO complaint)
  after the Jumia-contracted courier **circulated a client's data among delivery agents** (DPPA ss.35–36) and
  operated **unregistered** (s.5); a guilty plea to non-registration drew a lenient **UGX 100,000** fine.
  Alongside, NITA-U/PDPO launched a six-month **"Beera Ku Guard"** campaign (target: 70% awareness, 1,000
  registered controllers) against a backdrop where **only 13.6% grasp data-protection principles** and
  **cybercrime rose 93.5% in 2024** (474 incidents, UGX 72.1bn lost, UGX 420m recovered). See
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[capacity.literacy]]. Source: [[2025-12-11-vuka-africa-prosecuted-data-breach-pdpo]].
- **Civil-society dialogue (May 2026).** A **[[cipesa]]** Multi-stakeholder Dialogue on Business and Digital
  Rights (with the EU, Enabel, government) pressed for **human-centred digital transformation** and **data
  sovereignty** — strengthening **local technical capacity to control national data independently** — around
  the National AI & Emerging Technologies Strategy (ISO/IEC 42001-aligned; see [[uganda--tech-ai]]).
  *(PR-wire source; verify against CIPESA's own account.)* Source: [[2026-05-08-cipesa-uganda-digital-rights-dialogue]].

## The HRIA that was asked for and is not recorded as done

**CHRGJ (NYU)**, **ISER** and **HEAPI** jointly recommended a comprehensive **Human Rights Impact
Assessment** of the national ID system on **2024-07-25** — **before** mass enrolment began (May
2025), **before** the ISER judgment (Jun 2025), and **before** the NIRA database breach (Sep
2025). See [[dpi.id]], [[uganda--dpi-id]]. Source:
[[2024-07-25-chrgj-iser-heapi-seizing-opportunity-uganda-digital-id]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the PDPO is the most credible institution in the Ugandan digital-rights material — it took
  on Google and Meta and won on the record, inventing useful doctrine (extraterritorial monetisation,
  equivalent-protection, dignity as material harm) without resorting to a blunt localisation mandate. The
  discomfort is the **asymmetry**: the state's data-protection machinery is rigorous against foreign
  platforms and small couriers, yet (per the hub) was **absent when the state itself deployed biometric voter
  verification and NIN-linked registers** with near-zero DPIAs, and the NIRA register was **breached to a
  criminal ring** (see [[uganda--dpi-id]]). Robust against the private sector, permissive of the state — the
  familiar shape of selective enforcement.
