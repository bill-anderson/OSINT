# Nigeria × Artificial Intelligence

Nigeria is positioning as an African AI leader on four fronts at once — a **national strategy and
oversight body**, a **risk-based AI law**, **sovereign local-language models**, and **Big-Tech skills
capital** — while quietly running one of the continent's largest **AI-surveillance** estates. The
market is put at **~$434.4m by 2026** (44.2% CAGR) and a projected **$15bn in economic value by 2030**.

## Strategy and institutions

- **National AI Trust (Feb 2025).** The Federal Executive Council approved the **National Artificial
  Intelligence Trust** — billed "first of its kind globally" — to mobilise resources, provide oversight
  and guide AI development, alongside a **Universal Communication Access Project** (21m people / 4,834
  unserved communities; see [[nigeria--infra-connect]], [[digital.rural]]). It builds on the **National
  AI Strategy (NAIS, 2024 draft)** and the **National Centre for AI & Robotics ([[ncair]])**. Source:
  [[2025-02-05-ai-connectivity-government-initiatives]].

## Regulation — the risk-based turn

- **The AI Bill (2025-2026).** Nigeria is moving to become **among the first in Africa** with
  comprehensive AI legislation. The **National Digital Economy & E-Governance Bill** (expected passage
  ~Mar 2026) carries a **risk-based framework** — high-risk systems (finance, public administration,
  surveillance, automated decision-making) face stricter oversight and **annual impact assessments**,
  with fines up to **₦10m (~$7,000) or 2% of Nigerian revenue**. [[nitda]] DG Kashifu Abdullahi frames
  it as regulating early to close post-2024 gaps. Sources: [[2026-01-14-nigeria-plans-ai-regulations-bill]],
  [[2025-11-20-nigeria-ai-bill-control-innovation]].
- **The over-regulation critique.** Analysis (Datum Africa's Anda Usman) warns the bill mirrors the EU
  AI Act's language but tilts to **regulatory control**, concentrating broad powers in agencies that
  "lack proven capacity" — risking a repeat of "fintech over-regulation" that makes Nigeria "a consumer
  of other people's technology rather than a producer." See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-20-nigeria-ai-bill-control-innovation]].

## Sovereign capability — local-language models

- **N-ATLAS (Sep 2025).** At UNGA80, Minister [[bosun-tijani]] launched the **Nigerian Atlas for
  Languages & AI at Scale (N-ATLAS)** — an open-source, multilingual/multimodal LLM covering **Yoruba,
  Hausa, Igbo and Nigerian-accented English**, built on Meta's **Llama-3 8B** and fine-tuned on **400m+
  tokens**, powered by **[[awarri-technologies]]** with [[nitda]] and [[ncair]]. Nigeria has 500+ native
  languages; the goal is government services and apps usable in local tongues. Source:
  [[2025-09-20-n-atlas-nigerian-llm-launch]].

## Big-Tech capital — skills and research

- **Google.** From a Paris meeting (Feb 2025), President Tinubu opened a five-area alliance with
  **[[google]]** (cloud, skills, AI research, cloud adoption, global standing), citing local infra
  provider [[awarri-technologies]]. Google later committed **$37m continent-wide** (Jul 2025 — incl. the
  **Masakhane African-languages hub**, 40+ languages) and **₦3bn to Nigeria** (Nov 2025) across five
  partners to embed AI curricula. Sources: [[2025-02-13-nigeria-google-ai-alliance]],
  [[2025-07-24-google-37m-africa-ai-investment]], [[2025-11-28-google-n3bn-ai-training-programme]].
- **Microsoft.** A **$1m to train 1m Nigerians** in AI over two years (Feb 2025) — though the **$1/
  trainee** ratio drew feasibility scepticism. See [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2025-02-19-microsoft-pledges-1m-ai-training]].

## AI in government — deployments

- **Service-Wise GPT** — an AI assistant for the Federal Civil Service (Public Service Rules, policy
  drafting, memo generation), unveiled at the Global Government Summit (Jan 2025). Source:
  [[2025-02-03-ai-tool-public-service-efficiency]].
- **Anambra SmartGov** — an in-house AI suite flagged a **₦2.1bn** unspent education allocation and
  detected ghost workers (one account used by seven people) via NIN/BVN cross-checks, rolling across 21
  LGAs; context: NITDA says **<10%** of government operations nationwide are digitised. Source:
  [[2025-06-30-anambra-ai-smartgov-lg-spending]].
- **AI open-contracting** — a procurement-transparency platform rolled out in **Plateau, Edo and
  Ekiti** states. Source: [[2025-02-06-ai-procurement-platform-states]].
- **Lagos "Ask INU"** — a WhatsApp AI chatbot + self-reporting evidence portal for domestic/sexual-
  violence survivors (Lagos DSVA). Source: [[2025-02-25-ai-chatbot-domestic-violence-portal]].
- **Airtel AI spam alert** — a free, no-install AI spam-detection service (Mar 2025). Source:
  [[2025-03-14-airtel-ai-spam-alert]].

## Surveillance — the other AI estate

- **$470m on AI surveillance (2026).** An Institute of Development Studies report ("Smart City
  Surveillance in Africa: Mapping **Chinese** AI Surveillance across 11 Countries") puts Nigeria's spend
  on facial-recognition and number-plate tracking at **>$470m** — the continent's largest — with ~10,000
  smart cameras, ahead of Mauritius and Kenya. Researchers found little evidence it reduces crime, and
  minimal use of footage in prosecutions. See [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-24-nigeria-470m-ai-surveillance-spend]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: N-ATLAS and NAIS aim at a **producer**, not consumer, position — sovereign models,
  local languages, homegrown providers (Awarri). The AI-Bill critique warns premature regulation could
  invert that into dependence.
- `colonialism`: the **capability** layer runs on foreign stacks (Google/Microsoft capital, Meta's
  Llama-3 base) and the **surveillance** layer on Chinese hardware — the autonomy N-ATLAS asserts sits
  atop imported foundations on both the US and China sides.
