# Angola × Data Storage (data-centre estate)

Angola has one of Sub-Saharan Africa's **densest emerging data-centre clusters outside the big three** (ZAF/NGA/KEN) — a state-led sovereign build layered over a competitive commercial estate, anchored by cheap-ish power ambitions and Atlantic cable landings. The unifying theme is **data sovereignty**: a government cloud with a 30-day mandatory-migration rule, and localisation as strategy.

## What we know

**The sovereign core.** **[[infosi]]** built and, on **28 April 2026**, President João Lourenço
inaugurated the **[[data-center-e-cloud-do-governo-angola|Data Center e Cloud do Governo]]** at
Camama, Luanda: **6,225 m²**, built in **~10 months**, **Tier III** (Uptime), **208 racks / 1,040 kW**
live in a **modular 12-container** design (~336-rack capacity), with a secondary DC replicating the
primary. Cost is reported at **US$89m** (Angop) and **~US$90m** (Novo Jornal) — kept dated, not
reconciled; TechAfrica frames the build as **UAE-origin financed** (`geopol.gulf`), though its own
account does not substantiate that. It is the base for the **[[nuvem-nacional-de-angola|Nuvem
Nacional]]** (unified government cloud), onto which public services face a **progressive and
mandatory migration** (earlier reporting: a 30-day window). The framing is explicitly
**repatriation** — Angolans' **passports and education certificates**, previously held by foreign
platforms, now sit under national jurisdiction — and it fibre-links the Finance, Telecoms, Interior,
Education and Health ministries. Held up as a regional model (Namibia's minister cited it); the
**national cloud** first stone was laid Sept 2025, ICT skills built with **[[huawei]]** (5,000
trained, 10,000 by 2027).

**The commercial estate.**
- **[[raxio-group]] Angola (AG1)**, Cacuaco — the country's **largest and only Tier III** commercial DC (up to 3 MW / 800+ racks, 19,000 m², $30m, Oct 2025), sited near undersea-cable landings.
- **[[paratus]]** — a three-DC Luanda campus (ex-ITA): DC1 (2017), DC2 (2019), and **DC3**, set to be **Angola's first Tier IV** (>10 MW / 2,000 racks), financed by a $31m EAIF/Ninety One facility.
- **[[africell]]** — a cloud-based enterprise DC at its Kings Tower HQ (2021), the cornerstone of its 5G network.
- **[[angola-cables]]** — the **AngoNAP** DC and **Angonix** IXP, tied to the SACS Atlantic subsea system; **[[unitel]]** enterprise facilities (Luanda Sul, FILDA); plus MSTelcom and Multipla facilities in the wider Luanda market.

**The domestic-cloud demand play.** Beyond the state cloud, **[[angola-cables]]** is building demand
for local hosting: its **Accelera Cloud Program** (Dec 2025) offers free credits and full migration
assistance onto **Clouds2Africa** — billed as the continent's largest cloud — priced in **local
currency** to remove FX risk for SMEs and startups. Localisation cast as a commercial product, not
only a state mandate. Source: [[2025-12-03-angola-cables-unveils-accelera-cloud-programme]].

Angola ranks 156/193 on the 2024 UN EGDI — the sovereign-cloud push is as much about closing a low e-government base as about localisation. See [[infra.store]], [[infra.connect]], [[AGO]].
