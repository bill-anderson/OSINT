# Eswatini × Connectivity

Eswatini has **~95% 4G coverage** but only **~58% internet use** and **data at 3.47% of GNI/capita**
(above the affordability threshold) — the gap the World Bank frames as a **US$165m (E2.8bn) digital
opportunity**. The build combines a **WB-backed fibre rollout**, a **Mbabane smart city**, and — for a
landlocked, mountainous kingdom — **satellite**.

## Fibre and the smart city

- **Fibre backbone + capacity.** A **nationwide fibre-optic rollout** (World Bank-supported) is "the
  backbone of a modern digital economy"; international capacity rose **47 → 72 Gbps** (exceeding targets).
  The ICT ministry's **three-year roadmap** (Business Eswatini indaba, Feb 2026) anchors on fibre, service
  digitisation and local talent. See [[gov.policy]]. Sources:
  [[2025-08-04-eswatini-ramps-up-tech-reform-legislation-5g]], [[2026-02-13-optical-fibre-rollout-strengthens-business-ecosystem]].
- **Mbabane Smart City.** The capital is being fitted with a **5G network, 59 AI-enabled cameras and
  public Wi-Fi** — with forthcoming legislation on cybersecurity, critical infrastructure, e-commerce and
  **AI/robotics/space/satellite governance** (bills → see [[eswatini--gov-protect]]). See [[tech.ai]].
  Source: [[2025-08-04-eswatini-ramps-up-tech-reform-legislation-5g]].

## Satellite — landlocked resilience

- **Paratus Eswatini (Jul 2025).** A **Paratus Group × Real Image Internet** JV launched as an
  **authorised Starlink Business reseller** (managed hardware/install/24-7 support), extending
  connectivity "from remote schools and clinics to urban enterprises" (EduLINK schools). See
  [[data.satellite]], [[include.divides]]. Source: [[2025-07-30-paratus-eswatini-drives-starlink-business-adoption]].
- **Government Starlink gateway (Apr 2026).** The government commissioned a **Starlink Satellite Gateway**
  (inaugurated by King Mswati III via Prince Sicalo) — explicitly to reach mountainous/remote areas and to
  **reduce dependence on cross-border fibre**, a resilience play for a landlocked state. See
  [[data.satellite]]. Source: [[2026-04-28-eswatini-commissions-starlink-satellite-gateway]].

## The financing and the political gap

- **A "fully-funded" agenda left unspoken.** An opinion column noted PM Dlamini's 2025 year-end statement
  **omitted** the **US$165m (E2.8bn)** World Bank digital-transformation opportunity — in a country where
  **25,000 youth enter the labour market yearly against ~1,000 formal jobs** (58.2% youth unemployment) —
  a "concerning disconnect between policy priorities and economic imperatives." Source:
  [[2026-01-04-the-absent-agenda-eswatini-digital-imperative]].
- **Evidence base.** ESCCOM awarded a **~E2.8m** tender (360 Degrees Business Solutions) for the **State
  of Communications Sector Study 2025/26** to inform policy. Source:
  [[2026-01-19-e2-8m-study-signals-bright-2026-digital-outlook]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the satellite pivot is explicitly a **resilience-and-sovereignty** move — a landlocked
  kingdom hedging its cross-border-fibre dependence with a government Starlink gateway. But it deepens
  reliance on a single foreign LEO operator, and the affordability gap (data at 3.47% GNI) means the
  hard problem is still **usage among the majority**, not backbone capacity.
