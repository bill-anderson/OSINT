# Cameroon × Data centres & sovereign hosting

Cameroon talks **digital sovereignty** loudly, but the record shows a **fragile physical base**, **three
competing poles** (state, private-African, foreign hyperscaler), and a **policy tension** the country has
not resolved.

## The evidence base is damning

Before the rhetoric, **[[cenadi]]**'s three-year, **269-page** data-centre ecosystem study (restituted
May 2025, chaired by Finance Minister Motazé) found the reality weak: **uncertified small facilities,
inadequate power, thin bandwidth**. Sovereign hosting is the ambition; the base is fragile. Sources:
[[2025-05-26-cenadi-digital-independence-data-centres-itweb]],
[[2025-05-26-cenadi-data-center-ecosystem-study-sovereignty-techafricanews]],
[[2025-05-27-leconomie-cenadi-datacenter-study]].

## Three poles

- **State — [[zamengoe-data-centre]] (CAMTEL).** Pitched at SAGO 2026 as the government-data sovereignty
  asset, billed "largest in Central Africa / Tier III" (**both unverified PR-wire claims — not
  hardened**). Real and tourable; **BEAC inspected it with a view to hosting** (Dec 2025), which would
  make it a six-state CEMAC development. Camtel's Ethio Telecom MSA adds a **Sovereign Government Cloud**
  workstream and a national cloud to digitise government services. Sources:
  [[2026-06-12-lejour-camtel-zamengoe-datacenter-digital-sovereignty]],
  [[2026-06-12-camtel-zamengoe-data-centre-sovereignty-sago-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2025-12-17-beac-national-director-visits-camtel-data-center-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2025-12-05-ethio-telecom-camtel-master-service-agreement-telecomreviewafrica]],
  [[2025-12-08-ethio-telecom-camtel-connectivity-inclusion-biometricupdate]].
- **Private-African — [[st-digital]] ([[anthony-same]]).** The African-owned counterweight (500+ clients,
  three certified DCs across CMR/GAB/COG/CIV — certification unspecified), same "keep African data on the
  continent" claim from a private pan-African firm. Grassroots echo: **KMR Start-up HUB's "Pyramid"**
  local browser/mail/streaming suite hosted on Camtel + ST Digital, pitched against "<3% of world data
  stored in Africa." Sources:
  [[2026-05-13-anthony-same-st-digital-african-datacenter-network-wearetech]],
  [[2026-06-15-leconomie-kmr-pyramid-local-storage-digital-ecosystem]].
- **New financing patterns.** State pension fund **CNPS took equity in [[innovendi-data-technologies]]**
  (Mar 2026), which runs the **Douala Smart Port** edge-cloud on a vendor-financed model (Innovendi pays
  the Port Authority). Separately, a **data centre is under construction in Douala** to hold **20,000
  smart meters'** AMI data (PRSEC 2024–26, ~CFA 400bn / $710m; WB CFA 180bn + AfDB CFA 48bn) — population-
  scale automated capture as data infrastructure. Sources:
  [[2026-03-04-leconomie-cnps-innovendi-smart-port]], [[2025-11-28-douala-port-5g-innovendi-agreement-itweb]],
  [[2026-05-29-rollout-20000-smart-meters-ami-world-bank-wearetech]].

## The unresolved tension (policy, not contradiction)

At the very moment CAMTEL builds a sovereign cloud and **[[artac]]** (with CMR's regulator participating)
adopts a Central African **data-residency position** — NGSO providers (**Starlink, OneWeb, Amazon Kuiper**
named) to keep **citizens' data on the continent** — CAMTEL is in talks with **AWS** (2026-07-08, US
Embassy present) on cloud/satellite/AI. This is **not a factual contradiction but a live policy tension**,
and exactly the sovereignty material the hub exists to hold (MANIFEST Flag 14 — treated here as analysis,
not a `reviews/contradictions/` item). Sources:
[[2026-02-24-trb-artac-10th-ordinary-session-kinshasa-ngso-techreviewafrica]],
[[2026-07-08-camtel-aws-digital-transformation-talks-techafricanews]].
