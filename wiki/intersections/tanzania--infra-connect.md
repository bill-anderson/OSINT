# Tanzania × Connectivity & Telecom Infrastructure

Tanzania is building connectivity on a **state-owned spine** — the **[[nictbb|NICTBB]]** fibre backbone and
state operator **[[ttcl]]** — while private operators (Yas, Airtel, Vodacom) add towers, 5G and PPP fibre,
and the state courts foreign satellite ([[starlink|Starlink]]) and a new subsea cable. The build is real
and fast; the gap the numbers keep exposing is the **demand side** — 5G racing ahead of a **falling
smartphone base**, and a physical network thin relative to the users on it.

## The state backbone (NICTBB)

- **Doubling capacity, extending reach.** NICTBB capacity rose **tenfold, 200 → 2,000 Gbps**, reaching
  **85–109 of 139 districts** and ~**14,000 km** laid (cumulative 13,820 / 16,280 km target); connected to
  **Mombasa** via Horohoro–Tanga; **submarine cables landing rose from two to three**, and **four public
  data centres** are now commissioned — part of a **~400% sector expansion 2021–Dec 2025** (TCRA). Sources:
  [[2026-04-10-communications-sector-surges-400pc]], [[2025-05-16-tanzania-allocates-sh73-billion-ict-backbone]].
- **Budgets.** **Sh73bn (2025/26)** — DRC extension, Wi-Fi in 50 public spaces, last-mile fibre to 100
  justice/defence institutions, two national data centres (Dodoma + Zanzibar). **Sh222.5–222.6bn (2026/27
  MICIT)** — **94.1% development**; DRC link (20 stations), **7 IXPs**, Wi-Fi in 100 spaces, 100 schools; a
  digital addressing system; the cubesat educational satellite; and (committee-urged) the **ICT Bill 2026**
  plus a **Sh100bn National Data Centre** upgrade. Ministry expects **Sh160.3bn** from NICTBB capacity
  sales. Sources: [[2026-04-30-kairuki-aomba-222-bilioni-bajeti-micit]], [[2026-05-01-government-targets-telecom-expansion-budget-2026-27]].

## Subsea — Kilimanjaro One

- **KO-SCS (Nov 2025).** **[[ttcl]]** unveiled the **Kilimanjaro One Submarine Cable System** — **1,900 km**
  Dar es Salaam–**Mauritius**, branches to **Unguja and Mafia**, potential Madagascar — DG **Moremi Marwa**
  framing it as making Tanzania "**East Africa's true digital gateway**." It sits atop the NICTBB and the
  **11 international submarine cables** already terminating via Dar/Mombasa (SEACOM, EASSy, 2Africa);
  construction from 2026, completion **late 2027**; bundled with a Pan-African "Green AI & Robotics" data
  centre and a "Connectivity for All" free-Wi-Fi programme. Source:
  [[2025-11-12-tanzania-kilimanjaro-one-submarine-cable]].

## Operators, towers and PPP

- **The PPP handover (Apr 2026).** A telco consortium handed **758 towers + ~1,100 km fibre** (corridors
  Dodoma–Mwanza, Morogoro–Ifakara, Moshi–Rombo, plus metro fibre) to the government, President **Samia
  Suluhu Hassan** presiding, reinforcing the NICTBB under Vision 2050. **[[yas-tanzania|Yas]]** delivered
  **~1/3 (261 towers)** and upgraded its 2G footprint to 4G. Source:
  [[2026-04-11-yas-tanzania-hand-over-758-towers]].
- **Yas at scale (Jul 2026).** Yas reported **~4,800 towers** ("~1,000 more than any other operator"),
  **>Sh1 trillion** invested, 95%+ population 4G/5G, plus **smartphone financing** and solar charging for
  off-grid users (DITF, CEO Pierre Canton). Source: [[2026-07-04-yas-invests-sh1-trillion-digital-transformation]].
- **TTCL's fibre push.** State **[[ttcl]]** targets **1m fibre subscribers by 2028** (280k this FY);
  currently **2.78%** of mobile internet and **3rd in fixed lines** behind Airtel and Vodacom. Source:
  [[2026-05-06-ttcl-targets-1-million-fiber-subscribers-2028]].
- **Zanzibar PPPs.** **[[zictia]]** signed broadband deals with **[[airtel-tanzania|Airtel]]** (MoU, Aug
  2025) and **[[yas-tanzania|Yas Fibre]]** (**20-yr, TZS 300bn / $114.5m**, ~100k households, Jun 2026).
  Sources: [[2025-08-19-airtel-tanzania-zictia-broadband-zanzibar]], [[2026-06-25-yas-zictia-expand-broadband-zanzibar]].
- **Satellite.** Airtel Africa's **SpaceX/[[starlink]]** distribution deal (reported in TZ press, May 2025)
  covers Tanzania as one of 14 markets — the LEO layer over the terrestrial build (Starlink live in TZ
  since Nov 2025; see [[TZA]] hub, [[geopol.usa]]). Source: [[2025-05-06-airtel-spacex-starlink-afrika]].

## The numbers, and the demand-side gap

- **5G ahead of devices.** **5G population coverage 32.83%** (Q ending Mar 2026, from 30.13%), but
  **geographical coverage only ~11%** — urban-concentrated; **5G facilities +139% (754→1,804)**, all
  regions now ≥3 base stations, four new regions covered (Katavi, Lindi, Mtwara, N Pemba), Zanzibar Urban
  West +157%. Data consumption **more than doubled, 426→932 PB**. Yet **smartphone penetration is just
  42.5% and *fell*** (29.8m→28.5m users) — high device costs, mostly non-standalone 5G. See
  [[include.divides]]. Sources: [[2026-06-18-nationwide-5g-rollout-gains-momentum]], [[2026-06-10-telecom-infrastructure-5g-expansion-low-smartphone]].
- **Subscriptions.** Mobile subscriptions **52.8m (2021) → 111.9m (2026)**; mobile internet **29m → 58.9m**;
  FTTH/FTTO **+157%** (54,290 → 139,317, Mar 2024–Dec 2025). Sources:
  [[2026-06-17-tanzania-telecom-critical-infrastructure-report]], [[2026-02-21-fibre-optic-rollout-dar-compliance-concerns]].

## Two governance frictions

- **Infrastructure-sharing / visual pollution (Feb 2026).** Rapid Dar fibre rollout has left streets with
  **5+ poles from different operators**, raising **infrastructure-sharing compliance** and urban-planning
  concerns (NEMC handles large towers/EIAs; fibre poles fall to the communications sector) — operators
  prefer cheaper overhead fibre, and there is **no clear regulatory strategy**. See [[gov.standards]].
  Source: [[2026-02-21-fibre-optic-rollout-dar-compliance-concerns]].
- **"Declare telecom critical infrastructure" (Jun 2026).** A policy argument that telecom should be
  **Critical Economic Infrastructure** — **~10,000 towers** serving tens of millions strains quality, ARPU
  is low, and **taxes/levies are ~half of sector revenue**; treat it as foundational (like roads/power),
  cut regulatory friction. See [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2026-06-17-tanzania-telecom-critical-infrastructure-report]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: connectivity is where Tanzania's build-it-yourself model is most visible — a state fibre
  backbone it sells capacity from, a state operator laying its own subsea cable to Mauritius, and PPPs that
  **hand private-built towers to the government**. It is a genuinely sovereign infrastructure posture, with
  foreign inputs (Starlink, subsea consortia) layered on top rather than owning the base. The unresolved
  tension is not ownership but **reach and affordability**: 5G and towers are expanding faster than the
  smartphone base can use them, the physical network is thin per user, and the tax burden the "critical
  infrastructure" argument targets is the state's own. Sovereign rails, uneven last mile — the same shape
  as [[tanzania--dpi-pay|payments]] and [[tanzania--dpi-id|ID]].
