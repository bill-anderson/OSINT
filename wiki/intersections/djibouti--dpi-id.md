# Djibouti × Digital Identity

Djibouti moved from legal groundwork to a live foundational-ID system across 2025–26.

- **Mobile ID (launched 9 Feb 2026).** [[mobile-id-djibouti]] unifies public services around a
  single citizen digital identifier — authentication and e-signature from a smartphone; priority
  use cases in civil registry, tax, banking; planned interoperability across government databases
  (driving licences, health records). Cornerstone of **[[vision-djibouti-2035]]**.
- **Legal basis.** Built on the **[[djibouti-digital-code]]** (adopted 30 Jun 2025), whose
  156-article data-protection regime creates the **[[cndp-djibouti|CNDP]]**. Processing involving
  the **national identification number** requires **prior CNDP authorisation** — placing Mobile ID
  squarely in the regime's highest-scrutiny tier.
- **Sovereignty design.** Biometric data stored **exclusively by the Interior Ministry**; other
  institutions access only via encrypted server-to-server requests. President Guelleh cited risks
  of identity theft, cloning, espionage and "foreign interference."
- **Wallet integration.** Mobile ID is storable in the **[[smart-wallet-djibouti|Smart Wallet]]**,
  whose biometric onboarding layer is provided by **[[tech5]]** (Visa MoU).
