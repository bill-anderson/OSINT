# Chad × Digital Payments & Fintech

In a **cash-dominated** economy, Chad is digitising **from the top down** — mandating electronic public
finance and public-service payments — while a small set of fintechs (Airtel/Moov mobile money, and
home-grown **Konoom** and **Cashi**) build the retail rails. The gap is the usual one: **power, patchy
internet and thin skills**.

## The state goes cashless

- **Budget 2026 — "zéro cash, zéro papier."** From 2026 the entire expenditure chain runs through the
  integrated PFM system **SIGFIP**, with **mandatory e-invoicing** and **exclusively bank/mobile
  payments** (cash and cheques out) — for revenue security, spending control and traceability, under the
  **Public Finance Reform Strategy 2022–2027**. Analysts flag the real constraints: **limited
  electricity, uneven internet, variable digital literacy** among agents and suppliers. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2026-01-24-budget-2026-zero-cash-zero-papier-fen]].
- **Mobile payment for public services (Feb 2026).** Finance Minister Tahir Hamid Nguilin pushed a
  **convention-cadre** with **Moov Money, Airtel Money, Konoom and Cashi** to dematerialise state
  payments via mobile money — traceability, anti-fraud, faster execution, lower cost, inclusion. Source:
  [[2026-02-17-journaldutchad-paiement-mobile-services-publics]].

## The fintechs

- **Konoom.** A **COBAC-licensed** N'Djamena startup (launched 2025, Brahim Moussa Hassan) offering a
  **multichannel wallet** — app (iOS/Android), web, **USSD (no internet)** and **WhatsApp** — for
  transfers, merchant/bill payments, mass payouts and **QR** acceptance across wallets, bank accounts and
  agents. Its accessibility design deliberately reaches feature-phone users in low-connectivity zones. See
  [[include.access]], [[tech.innovate]]. Source: [[2026-06-19-wearetech-konoom-paiements-numeriques-multicanal]].
- **Cashi × IFC (Apr 2026).** The fintech **Cashi** partnered the **IFC** to deploy an **interoperable
  payments platform for Central Africa, Chad-first** — accessible by mobile, POS and SMS — to cut cash
  dependence and open formal finance to small businesses and the poorly-connected. See [[finance.new]].
  Source: [[2026-04-02-wearetech-cashi-sfi-ifc-paiements-numeriques]].

## Reads

- The sequencing mirrors Chad's wider digital story — **mandate first, capacity later**. Ordering the
  state cashless in 2026 is a strong transparency signal, but in a country with ~13% internet penetration
  and diesel-dependent networks it front-runs the rails and the users. The bright spot is that the retail
  layer includes **home-grown, COBAC-licensed** fintechs (Konoom) and USSD/WhatsApp access designed for
  the least-connected — the right shape for the market, if the agent networks can scale.
