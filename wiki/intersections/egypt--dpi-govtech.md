# Egypt × GovTech / e-Government

Egypt is a **World Bank GTMI "Group A"** GovTech state, delivering services through the **[[digital-egypt|Digital Egypt]]** platform ([[mcit-egypt|MCIT]]) — but with a rights-and-implementation gap flagged by civil society.

- **Platform.** Digital Egypt offers **175+ online services** (civil status, traffic, real-estate registration, social insurance); some in-person services are being moved **exclusively** online, toward **full automation by 2026** and a digital ID card.
- **DPI framing.** Egypt hosted the **2024 Global DPI Summit**; the DPI stack spans identity, payments and data exchange, with UNDP/ITU/World Bank support.
- **Registries.** The **[[egypt-property-id-law|Property ID Law]]** (2025) assigns a unique digital ID to every property, tied to a unified base map and required for registry/utility access — the land-registry leg.
- **Critical read.** Masaar's rights-based analysis notes Egypt's EGDI slide (95th, 2024) and argues technical indices miss legislative frameworks, equitable access and fundamental-rights protection.
