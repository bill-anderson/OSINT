# Egypt × GovTech / e-Government

Egypt is a **World Bank GTMI "Group A"** GovTech state, delivering services through the **[[digital-egypt|Digital Egypt]]** platform ([[mcit-egypt|MCIT]]) — but with a rights-and-implementation gap flagged by civil society.

- **Platform (Jan 2026).** Digital Egypt offers **210 online services** (from 170 in 2024), **~10.7m users**, **73 exclusive**; **transactions 7.8m (2024) → 25m+ (2025)**; MCIT targets **270 services in 2026**. Nine commercial-registry services made exclusive from **1 Jan 2026**.
- **Indices (2025).** **World Bank GTMI: 22nd globally** (from 69th in 2022), **score 0.911** (Group A "Extensive"; service-delivery pillar 0.962) — plus a national **Open Data Policy**; **AI-readiness 52nd** (Oxford Insights, 1st in Africa); **e-gov services 77th** (WIPO).
- **Unified investment licensing.** **GAFI's** e-licensing platform — **460 services from 41 government issuers** (built with MCIT), licence in 20 working days.
- **Unified Government Services Card.** Bundles health insurance, food subsidy, Takaful-and-Karama cash grant and fertilizer subsidy onto one card **linked to Egypt Post accounts** (financial inclusion); Port Said pilot **41,500 families**, then Ismailia/Luxor — the social-registry/[[dpi.mis]] leg (see [[egypt--dpi-mis]]).
- **DPI framing.** Egypt hosted the **2024 Global DPI Summit**; the DPI stack spans identity, payments and data exchange, with UNDP/ITU/World Bank support.
- **Registries.** The **[[egypt-property-id-law|Property ID Law]]** (2025) assigns a unique digital ID to every property, tied to a unified base map and required for registry/utility access — the land-registry leg.
- **Critical read.** Masaar's rights-based analysis notes Egypt's EGDI slide (95th, 2024) and argues technical indices miss legislative frameworks, equitable access and fundamental-rights protection.
