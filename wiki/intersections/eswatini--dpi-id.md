# Eswatini × Digital Identity & Registries

Eswatini has **not rolled out a digital ID "in earnest"** (as of 2024) — the national ID card (from 2003,
held centrally by **[[ministry-of-home-affairs-eswatini]]**) does not meet digital-ID criteria, and the
national population register is the foundation the World Bank says robust DPI needs (see [[SWZ]] hub). The
live story is less about the credential than about **fragile production and a run of rights flashpoints**.

## Even the basic card is fragile

- **ID production suspended then resumed.** National ID-card production was **suspended in late Sep 2025**
  over technical failures and **resumed 22 Feb 2026** after machinery repair/upgrades — disrupting
  banking, employment and travel for months, across service centres nationwide. A reminder that
  foundational ID infrastructure is not yet resilient. Source:
  [[2026-02-22-home-affairs-resumes-id-card-production]].
- The emerging identity-verification layer is the CBE's **national e-KYC** registry (financial inclusion +
  SADC cross-border — see [[eswatini--dpi-pay]]).

## Rights flashpoints

- **The state breaches its own citizens' data (Jun 2026).** The Ministry of Home Affairs itself
  **published unblurred national ID documents on X** (since deleted); **[[esccom]]**, as the **Eswatini
  Data Protection Authority (EDPA)**, opened an investigation — a likely **personal data breach** with no
  lawful basis, and the Ministry **failed the 72-hour breach-notification** duty under the
  [[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]]. The DPA's **first major enforcement** — against a government
  ministry. See [[gov.protect]], [[eswatini--gov-protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-24-home-affairs-investigated-for-sensitive-data-breach]].
- **Biometric school gate-passes (Jul 2025).** Some public schools rolled out **mandatory fingerprint/
  facial-recognition gate scanners** (without Education Ministry approval), charging parents **~E200/pupil**
  — sparking teacher/parent refusal over consent and child-data privacy. Biometric surveillance creeping
  into schools ahead of any governing framework. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2025-07-23-outcry-over-digital-gate-pass-in-schools]].
- **Compulsory DNA for birth certificates (Jul 2026).** A proposal to require **DNA testing before
  issuing birth certificates** would cost **~E126m/yr** (~46% of the Home Affairs budget; ~E4,300/test on
  ~29,400 births) — a cost-and-rights debate over making genetic testing a gate to legal identity. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-07-14-dna-plan-could-swallow-e126m-of-home-affairs-budget]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Eswatini's ID story inverts the usual worry — the risk here is less foreign capture than
  the **state's own handling of citizen data**: a ministry that leaks unblurred IDs and misses breach-
  notification, biometric scanners deployed on children without a framework, and DNA floated as a gate to
  birth registration. The **[[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]]** exists; the June-2026 probe is the
  first real test of whether the DPA can enforce it **against government** — the load-bearing question for
  a rights-respecting digital ID.
