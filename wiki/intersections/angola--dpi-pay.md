# Angola × Digital Payments and Fintech

Angola runs a **bank-centric, state-switched payments stack** (EMIS/Multicaixa + the fast-scaling KWiK instant rail) that a mobile-money challenger (Afrimoney) and a big digital-G2P programme (Kwenda) are now widening toward the ~50% unbanked — while the tax authority bolts on mandatory e-invoicing.

## What we know

**The rails.** **[[emis-angola]]** operates the national payment system, the **Multicaixa** card/ATM/POS network and the **KWiK** instant-payment system. KWiK scaled hard: **35m+ transactions / 590bn kwanzas in 2025**, >100% growth y/y, 22 banking and non-banking institutions, roadmap adding QR, service, state and merchant payments — a pillar of the **National Financial Inclusion Strategy (ENIF 2025-2027)**.

**Mobile money as the inclusion layer.** **[[africell]]** launched **Afrimoney** (Apr 2023) to reach the ~50% unbanked and large informal sector — the newer inclusion play beside the bank-centric rails. Africell had taken ~25% telecom share within eight months of its 2022 entry.

**Digital G2P.** **[[kwenda]]** (launched May 2020, implemented by FAS on a $320m World Bank project) delivers quarterly cash transfers digitally to most beneficiaries — 247,000+ households early on, targeting **1.6m families** (60% female-headed) — Angola's flagship social-protection payment and a financial-inclusion lever, resting on a **social registry**.

**Tax digitalisation.** **[[agt-angola]]** is phasing in **mandatory e-invoicing** (Presidential Decree 71/25): from 1 Jan 2026 for large taxpayers/government suppliers/transactions >AOA 25m, extending to all VAT-registered taxpayers by Sept 2026 (AGT-certified software, real-time transmission, SAF-T).

Regionally, Angola sits within the **SADC** financial-inclusion and cross-border settlement agenda (kwanza in the SADC settlement system — under development). See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]], [[AGO]].
