# Angola × Digital Payments and Fintech

Angola runs a **bank-centric, state-switched payments stack** ([[emis-angola|EMIS]]/Multicaixa + the fast-scaling **[[kwik-angola|KWiK]]** instant rail) that a mobile-money challenger (Afrimoney) and a big digital-G2P programme (Kwenda) are now widening toward the unbanked — while the [[bna-angola|central bank]] chases international standards and regional settlement, and the tax authority bolts on mandatory e-invoicing. The state frames the payments agenda in **monetary-sovereignty** terms.

## What we know

**The rails.** **[[emis-angola]]** operates the national payment system, the **Multicaixa** network and the **[[kwik-angola|KWiK]]** instant-payment system (built with the **[[bna-angola|BNA]]**). KWiK scaled hard: **35m transfers / 590bn kwanzas in 2025** (>100% growth y/y), **22 banking and non-banking institutions**, roadmap adding QR, service, state and merchant payments — a pillar of the **National Financial Inclusion Strategy (ENIF 2025-2027)** (as of 2026-01-29).

**A wallet-led, concentrated instant market (Feb 2026).** Per EMIS's monthly report, KWiK is dominated by non-bank wallets: **[[paypay-angola|Paypay]] 52.2%** (1,637,275 transfers, 24.5bn Kz, +142% y/y), **BAI 21.9%**, **[[unitel-money|Unitel Money]]/USPM 12.6%**, 16 banks the rest. Angola held **12.3m+ registered accounts** and ~**24.8m active payment keys** (+180% y/y); **66.4% of transfers were under 10,000 Kz** — low-value, high-frequency use by youth and informal workers.

**Central-bank standards & regional integration.** The **[[bna-angola|BNA]]** (governor Manuel Tiago Dias) **completed migration of domestic payments to ISO 20022 on 10 July 2026** (with EMIS, BODIVA, SPTR participants). The **kwanza will join the SADC-RTGS** real-time settlement system from **H2 2026** — explicitly framed as **monetary sovereignty** (less FX intermediation, regional trade in national currency) — alongside a planned **Open Banking** framework and an AI-usage diagnostic. A **2026-07-08 BNA–DRC MoU** targets payment-system interoperability and cheaper cross-border payments with Congo. See [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[COD]], [[sadc]].

**Financial inclusion — the state scorecard (2026-07-09).** Economic-coordination minister José de Lima Massano put the **financial-inclusion rate at 51.7% (Q1 2026)** against a **65% target for 2027**; **banking at 32%** (5.7m people), targeting 36% (~8m) by 2027; mobile/digital payments **+56% y/y** (H1 2026). The next ENIF phase will let payment-service providers **extend credit**. ENIF was launched Dec 2025; Angola chaired the 15th meeting of the continental AfPI regulator platform in Luanda.

**Mobile money as the inclusion layer.** **[[africell]]** launched **Afrimoney** (Apr 2023) to reach the unbanked and large informal sector — the newer inclusion play beside the bank-centric rails.

**Digital G2P.** **[[kwenda]]** (launched May 2020, implemented by FAS on a $320m World Bank project) delivers quarterly cash transfers digitally — 247,000+ households early on, targeting **1.6m families** (60% female-headed) — resting on a **social registry**.

**Tax digitalisation.** **[[agt-angola]]** made **e-invoicing mandatory from 1 Jan 2026** (Regime Jurídico de Facturas, Presidential Decree 71/25) for large taxpayers and state suppliers, extending to all VAT-registered taxpayers within 12 months (AGT-certified software — 500+ registered, 21 certified as of Dec 2025 — real-time transmission, SAF-T; e-invoices auto-reported with a verification code).
