# Tanzania × Digital Payments & Fintech

Tanzania's payments story rests on a **state-built rail** — **[[tips-tanzania|TIPS]]**, the instant
retail switch the **[[bank-of-tanzania|BoT]]** built in-house (foundations on [[TZA]] hub) — onto which the
state is now (a) **capping interoperability fees** and licensing competitors, (b) pushing a **mandatory
cash-lite economy from July 2026**, and (c) **interlinking regionally** with Rwanda. Around that public
core, private fintech (M-Pesa, NALA) is moving up the value chain into infrastructure and cross-border.

## TIPS as the national backbone, and the numbers

- **Governor's framing (2025-05).** BoT Governor **Emmanuel Tutuba** called TIPS **"the very national
  digital backbone upon which inclusive finance is being constructed,"** reaffirming a **cash-lite**
  commitment. Source: [[2025-05-15-tips-transforms-payments-financial-inclusion]].
- **Transaction growth (year to Aug 2025, BoT Oct-2025 MPR).** **TISS** (large-value) **TZS 31.1tn** (from
  29.0tn a year earlier); **mobile transactions TZS 23tn** (from 18tn); **TIPS TZS 4.9tn** (from 2.7tn) —
  the TIPS jump driven partly by **payments to government institutions**. Dated figures, not bare present.
  Source: [[2025-12-25-malipo-ya-kielektroniki-muhimu-uchumi]].

## Jamii Malipo — announced, not built; and not a BoT system

The **thinnest thread in the Jamii stack**, and the one most often mis-described. As of **2024-08-27**,
MICIT Permanent Secretary **Mohammed Khamis Abdulla** described **Jamii Malipo** as *a programme still
to be created* — an application to be built and connected to a **Jamii Kadi** (a single electronic card
consolidating bank cards, national IDs, travel cards and insurance) and, through it, to
**[[tips-tanzania|TIPS]]**. The stated ambition is transacting from a bank account, **[[mpesa|M-Pesa]]**,
Airtel Money, [[paypal]] and others via the card, a phone or a computer. Source:
[[2024-08-27-mfumo-jamii-x-change-kuunganisha-mifumo-tehama]]. [[dpi.exchange]] · [[dpi.id]]

**No launch date, no operator confirmation, and no source held gives one since.** Two guards follow:

- **Jamii Malipo is not a Bank of Tanzania system.** On the ministry's own record it sits with
  **[[micit-tanzania|MICIT]] / [[ega-tanzania|e-GA]]** under the
  **[[digital-tanzania-project|Digital Tanzania Project]]**; [[bank-of-tanzania|BoT]] owns
  **[[tips-tanzania|TIPS]]**, which Jamii Malipo would *connect to*. A widely-circulated donor case
  study asserts BoT oversight — do not absorb it (see the guard on [[tanzania--dpi-id]]).
- **Jamii Malipo ≠ TIPS.** TIPS is live and carrying volume (above); Jamii Malipo is a consumer-facing
  application announced in 2024 that no held source shows in operation.

Related: PM **Kassim Majaliwa** (event 2024-10-17) projected payment **by NIDA number** in place of
cash, as part of the same digital-economy push. Source:
[[2024-10-18-hakikisheni-mnapata-namba-ya-nida-jamii-namba-majaliwa]].

## Competition and fee caps

- **113 licensed PSPs, capped fees (2026-04).** Finance Minister **Khamis Mussa Omar** told parliament
  **113 payment-service providers were licensed as of March 2026**; interoperability transfers (bank↔wallet)
  are **fee-capped at TZS 5,000 above TZS 500,000**, and single TACH/instant transactions capped at **TZS
  2,000** — the BoT "aggressively licensing new players to break monopolies." See [[gov.legislate]].
  Source: [[2026-04-28-interoperability-costs-cut-113-agencies]].

## Private fintech moves up the stack

- **M-Pesa Fintech 2.0 (Apr 2026).** **[[vodacom-tanzania]]** launched a **$28m** **[[mpesa|M-Pesa]]** core
  upgrade — **1,000+ tps (~86m/day)**, **99.999%** target, AI monitoring. M-Pesa is **41% of ~76m
  mobile-money accounts**, **>TZS 16tn/month**, **600,000+ merchants**. Source:
  [[2026-04-03-vodacom-tanzania-mpesa-fintech-2-platform]].
- **M-Pesa × PayPal (May 2026).** [[paypal]]↔M-Pesa wallet interoperability via the Super App — a
  cross-border on/off-ramp for freelancers and online commerce. See [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-26-vodacom-mpesa-tanzania-paypal-alliance]].
- **NALA's $50m for rails (May 2026).** Tanzanian fintech **[[nala]]** raised a **$25m→$50m debt facility**
  (MUFG-backed Liquidity) for cross-border payment infrastructure and **stablecoin corridors** — investors
  treating African fintechs as **payment-systems builders**, not consumer apps. See [[finance.new]].
  Source: [[2026-05-29-nala-raises-50m-payment-rails]].

## The cash-lite mandate (from 1 July 2026)

- **Mandatory digital payments.** The 2026/27 budget makes digital payment **compulsory across key
  sectors** — public transport (BRT, ferries, rail, air, ride-hailing), parking, malls, fuel, gyms,
  cinemas, hotels, restaurants, conference/sports venues, Sabasaba/Nanenane fairs, **all education from
  pre-primary to university**, strategic-crop cooperatives (coffee, cotton, cashew, tea, tobacco, sisal),
  and **real-estate/land/vehicle transfers** (proof of e-payment required; Lands, TRA and Brela to verify).
  Tools: **Lipa Namba** and QR for small traders. Experts split — a transparency/efficiency milestone vs
  gaps in **infrastructure, cybersecurity, affordability and inclusion**. See [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-24-digital-payment-tanzania-cash-lite-economy]].

## Regional interlink

- **TIPS ↔ RSwitch (EAC, 2026-07).** A Tanzania–Rwanda technical meeting in Zanzibar (7–11 Jul 2026)
  advanced the **first EAC cross-border instant-payment corridor**, building on the inaugural Kigali
  meeting (Nov 2025), under the **[[eac-cross-border-payment-masterplan]]** — integrating TIPS with
  Rwanda's **[[rswitch-rwanda|RSwitch]]** (governance/commercial/risk frameworks; chair Nestory Maro, BoT).
  See [[gov.regional]], [[RWA]], the [[TZA]] hub, and [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10-eac-tanzania-rwanda-cross-border-payments]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Tanzania's payments architecture is unusually **state-owned at the core** — a central bank
  that built its own switch, caps the fees on it, and is now legislating cash out of the economy — with
  private players (M-Pesa, NALA, PayPal) plugging in around it rather than owning the rail. That is a
  strong sovereignty position; the risks are on the demand side the mandate ignores: a **cash-lite law
  imposed ahead of the affordability, connectivity and inclusion** the [[include.access]] and 5G/smartphone
  evidence (see [[tanzania--infra-connect]]) show are not yet universal. State-built rails, state-mandated
  usage — the open question is whether the last mile can bear the compulsion.
