# Seychelles × Digital Identity & CRVS

Seychelles runs a **launched, operating** national digital-ID platform — **[[seyid]]** —
built by a **Swiss vendor** on a proprietary product, paired with an online civil-
certificate service, **[[certexpress]]**. The distinguishing feature is a **two-tier
credential**: the virtual ID became compulsory for **new work-permit holders** while
**Seychellois citizens kept the physical-card option**.

## The platform

**[[seyid]]** is the web and mobile channel through which Seychellois citizens, the
diaspora and foreign permit holders hold and present a national ID — the wallet layer
for [[certexpress]] certificates and the authentication front door to the e-government
stack. It is run in the context of the
**[[department-of-immigration-and-civil-status-seychelles]]** (ICS) with
**[[dict-seychelles]]** (DICT).

**Origins (2014).** The Seychelles government selected **[[wisekey]]**, a Swiss
cybersecurity company, as its partner for **public-key digital-signature technology**
for electronic transactions; WISeKey International Holding subsequently developed SeyID
on its **WISeID** digital-identity platform. **Official launch: 1 November 2022**, at a
ceremony attended by Vice-President **Ahmed Afif** and President
**[[wavel-ramkalawan]]**; at launch the platform was described as in the final phases
of integration with eGovernment services and financial institutions. See
[[infra.cybersec]], [[tech.industry]]. Source:
[[2024-09-18 Seychelles Makes Digital ID Mandatory for Work Permit Holders]].

**Capabilities (as described at 2024-09).** Web- and mobile-based, with online **KYC
onboarding**, **OTP authentication**, **QR-code secure login**, a **cloud signature**
solution for legally valid document signing, and an **E-Doc wallet**. See
[[dpi.govtech]].

## The two-tier credential (from 2024-09-17)

From **17 September 2024** the ICS stopped issuing **physical** ID cards to **new
work-permit holders**: they are registered on SeyID and issued a **virtual ID card**,
valid for the **duration of the permit**. The route to a **National Identity Number
(NIN) is unchanged** — the applicant receives an **"ID letter"** carrying the assigned
NIN, then registers on the SeyID website and downloads the app. **Seychellois citizens
retain the option of a physical ID**, which expires every ten years; citizens **living
abroad** may register using their NIN card or ID letter plus passport.

PS for Immigration **Alain Volcere** framed the change as reducing administrative
burden, speeding identity verification and cutting in-person visits. The ICS framed it
as reducing waste and better allocating resources — making issuance "more sustainable
while **prioritizing Seychellois citizens**." That framing is worth reading plainly:
the digital-only tier lands on **non-citizens**, and the physical fallback is reserved
to citizens. See [[include.access]]. Sources:
[[2024-09-18 Launch of New Digital Solutions for Public Services]],
[[2024-09-18 Seychelles Makes Digital ID Mandatory for Work Permit Holders]].

## CertExpress and the civil register

**[[certexpress]]**, announced alongside the SeyID change on **2024-09-18**, lets
Seychellois citizens request **birth, marriage and death certificates** online without
an in-person visit. As announced (**2024-09-18**): delivery **within 2 working days**;
a requested own birth or marriage certificate saved to the user's **E-Doc wallet** in
the SeyID app, giving ongoing access without reapplication; **price unchanged at
SCR 50** *(the fee stated at launch in September 2024 — a time-varying figure, not a
verified-current one)*. Secondary reporting adds delivery by email and frames the
service as cutting wait times for the **Seychellois diaspora** as well as residents.

The statutory basis for civil registration is the
**[[seychelles-civil-status-act]]** (Cap 34, originally 1893; consolidated text as at
**1 November 2022**) — CertExpress is the citizen-facing delivery layer over the
register that Act constitutes. See [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.legislate]]. Sources:
[[2024-09-18 Launch of New Digital Solutions for Public Services]],
[[2022-11-01 Seychelles Civil Status Act Cap 34 (as at 1 Nov 2022)]].

## Reading it

Two sources cover the September-2024 change and are **complementary, not duplicates**:
the ICS announcement is the **primary** (the issuing department's own, carrying the
fee, turnaround and NIN mechanics), while the trade re-report uniquely carries the
**WISeKey lineage** and the **Volcere quote**. Both are cited.

A foundational national ID built on a **foreign vendor's proprietary identity
platform** is a **[[sovereignty]]** question this wiki holds open: no source held here
addresses licensing, hosting or exit terms. No source held reports SeyID **enrolment or
adoption figures**, nor the state of a data-protection regime around the platform.
