# Nigeria × Digital Identity

Nigeria's foundational-ID programme is moving from *enrolment drive* to *legal
consolidation*, with a new statute and a hard coverage target — all externally financed.

## What we know (as of 2026-07)

- **New law.** The **NIMC Act 2026** (enacted June 2026 by President Tinubu) replaces the
  19-year-old NIMC Act 2007, recognises the **NIN as the foundational credential** under a
  "**one person, one identity**" policy, and is positioned to underpin "trusted DPI."
  [[nimc]] has begun nationwide multi-stakeholder consultations to build the enabling
  environment.
- **Coverage.** NIN registrations passed **136M** by July 2026 (up from **123.9M** in
  Oct 2025 — **12M+ added**), against a **180M target by 31 Dec 2026** (~**44M** still to
  issue). The authority frames success as moving the NIN from an identity token to an
  **enabler of service access**.
- **Financing.** Scaffolded by **[[nigeria-id4d]]** — [[world-bank]], the French
  Development Agency (AFD) and the European Investment Bank (EIB) — restructured with a
  two-year extension (noted late 2024). Project total confirmed at **$430m**; timeline
  now reads **123.9M (Oct 2025) → 130M (Jun 2026) → 136M (Jul 2026)**; NIMC aims to reach
  95% of Nigerians before December. Source:
  [[2026-06-05 Nigeria looks beyond enrollment as digital ID passes 130 million]].
- **Interoperability caveat.** A Warwick/Turing study using Nigeria as its African case
  finds NIN's **inconsistent assurance** drives financial exclusion via KYC — the failure
  is at governance/assurance, not technology; it urges supranational governance via
  [[afcfta]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-03 Study finds governance, not technology, is blocking digital ID interoperability]].
- **Ward-level drive + enforcement teeth (2026-07).** Under the NIMC Act 2026, DG
  Coker-Odusote launched enrolment across all **8,809 political wards**, explicitly
  targeting rural women, undocumented children, PWDs and refugees ([[include.access]]) with
  NIN as the gate to banking/telecoms/federal programmes. The Act adds power to **arrest and
  prosecute identity thieves** via an **ICE Unit**, tethered to the 2023 Data Protection Act
  / **[[ndpc]]** ([[infra.cybersec]]). Coverage frames Nigeria's "cyber-provisions-up-front"
  approach against Kenya's litigated **Maisha Namba** rollout. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 NIMC Act 2026 Director-General Coker-Odusote Launches 8,809-Ward Digital ID Drive]].

- **NIN adopted for farmer verification (2026-07).** The Federal Ministry of Agriculture & Food
  Security will use NIMC's NIN / verification platform to authenticate beneficiaries of agri
  programmes — a centralised **farmers database** to target subsidies (phased three-year taper) at
  "genuine farmers," cut leakages and remove ghost beneficiaries. A concrete sectoral use of
  foundational ID for targeting. See [[dpi.mis]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-12 Agric Ministry adopts NIMC identity system to verify genuine farmers]].

## Access, exclusion and accountability

The coverage push has a counter-story: as the NIN becomes compulsory across banking, telecom,
tax, health, education and welfare, the burdens of a still-imperfect system fall on citizens —
by gender, by geography and by who pays to fix the state's errors.

- **NIN suspensions — who bears the cost (2026-03, op-ed).** A legal-commentary piece flags a
  trust risk: validly issued NINs are being **suspended** over enrolment-centre "bypass," with
  **reinstatement fees pushed onto citizens** even though the failure is upstream regulatory
  supervision. As NIN becomes compulsory for banking, telecom and tax, the author argues
  monetised remediation "converts regulatory failure into revenue" and erodes confidence —
  external analysis bearing on [[nimc]] accountability ([[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]],
  [[include.access]]). Source:
  [[2026-03-29 When digital identity fails the citizen Who bears responsibility for NIN suspensions]].
- **Niger State — the ID gender gap (2026-01).** A DPI Africa Journalism Fellowship report finds
  only **38.8%** of enrolled NIN holders are women vs **61.1%** men (a **22.3-point** gap,
  [[nimc]], June 2025). In Niger State, **21 NIN centres** serve ~7.5m people, an **₦3,000**
  round-trip is prohibitive, and no NIN means no access to the maternal-care **Basic Health Care
  Provision Fund** — patriarchy and rural infrastructure gaps (rural women's phone ownership
  **42%** vs 78% urban) combining to lock women out of DPI ([[include.divides]], [[digital.rural]]).
  Source: [[2026-01-28 The Gender Gap in Rural Niger State's Digital Identity]].
- **NIN as gatekeeper — the access-barrier critique (2026-03).** A THISDAY investigation: a
  functional NIN is now required for healthcare, education, banking, employment, telecoms and
  welfare, yet a 2024 [[nimc]] policy confined corrections to a **device-locked smartphone
  self-service portal** — excluding the ~**72%** of adults without smartphones
  ([[research-ict-africa]]) and enabling agent extortion (₦12,000–62,000 vs the official
  ₦2,000). ~**94m** remain unenrolled; monthly enrolment slowed to ~648,888 (2024). Advocates
  invoke the **Data Protection Act 2023** accuracy duty and Kenya's **Huduma Namba**
  unconstitutionality precedent, arguing exclusion becomes "state-caused harm": enrolment
  counted, access denied — the [[world-bank]] ID4D $430m facility runs to Dec 2026
  ([[gov.protect]], [[include.divides]]). Source:
  [[2026-03-07 Nigerians Groan Under Digital Identity Regime That Turns Basic Rights into Privileges]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: legal consolidation of a national foundational identity, coverage push,
  and statutory enforcement powers over identity data.
- `colonialism`: the system is externally financed (WB / AFD / EIB) — the same
  externally-scaffolded-sovereignty tension as [[zambia--dpi-id]]. Data Landscapers' open
  question: does externally-financed self-reliance end dependence or reshape it?
