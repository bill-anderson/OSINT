# Mauritius × Payments & Fintech

Mauritius pairs a **mature domestic instant-payment rail** with an ambition to be a **cross-border fintech
gateway** into Africa and between Africa and Asia.

## Domestic rails and adoption

- **MauCAS switch.** The **[[maucas|MauCAS]]** national instant-payment switch is a mature, interoperable rail
  (profiled in SIIPS 2024), with a QR standard now driving retail uptake. Source:
  [[2024-01-01 SIIPS 2024 Case Study - MauCAS (Mauritius)]].
- **Adoption is real and measurable (2025).** At **[[mauritius-commercial-bank|MCB]]** — the largest bank —
  **QR-code payments rose 67%** year-on-year (Juice app + MauCAS QR), contactless hit >6 in 10 card
  transactions, ~**18,000 merchants** accepted card/MauCAS QR, and the **cash share of consumer spend fell from
  30% to 27%**. See [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-01-19-mcb-qr-payments-67pc-growth-lexpress]].
- **my.t money.** [[mauritius-telecom|MT]]'s wallet (130,000+ active users) is being scaled into a cross-border
  payment product under the "Financial Corridor" of its 2026-2029 strategy.

## Strategy and regulation

- **National Fintech Strategy 2026-2030.** Unveiled 25 Jun 2026 (UN ECA support), the
  **[[mauritius-national-fintech-strategy|strategy]]** sets six pillars, a **National Fintech Governance
  Committee**, **open banking**, an AI-in-finance push and a **Business Facilitation Bill** — implemented with
  the [[bank-of-mauritius|BoM]]. See [[gov.policy]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-29-national-fintech-strategy-2026-2030-wearetech]].
- **Central-bank modernisation.** The **[[bank-of-mauritius|Bank of Mauritius]]** (Governor Muthoora Thakoor) is
  building a **KYC System** for digital onboarding, aligning to the G20 cross-border roadmap, cautioning on
  **stablecoins** and fragmentation, and exploring **AI fraud detection** (IMF support). Source:
  [[2026-02-04-bom-digital-payments-workshop-lexpress]].

## Cross-border positioning

- **UPI-RuPay (India, Feb 2024).** Mauritius's **first bilateral cross-border payments agreement**, opening
  interoperability with India's UPI rail and a path to a future UPI Global Hub — a concrete Asia link. See
  [[geopol.india]], [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-02-04-bom-digital-payments-workshop-lexpress]].
- **African payments hub.** Post-FATF-grey-list-exit, fintechs use the island as a treasury/billing gateway into
  fragmented African markets — **[[peach-payments]]**' Mauritius entity grew volume **>300% YoY**. Source:
  [[2026-05-18 Mauritius emerges as an African payments hub]].
