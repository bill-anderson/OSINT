# Ghana × Digital Payments

Ghana's interoperable mobile-money value is climbing fast, with higher-value payments migrating onto shared rails as the platform for AfCFTA integration. This page also records a retracted claim about Ghana Card payment functionality, kept so the reversed claim does not harden.

## What we know

- **[[GHA]] Ghana Card payments — retracted claim (2026-04).** A TechCabal story that the
  **[[ghana-card]]** could now make payments was **retracted** as inaccurate and removed; the
  wiki carries no Ghana-Card payment functionality. Recorded to keep the reversed claim from
  hardening. Source:
  [[2026-04-01 RETRACTION Ghana's National ID cards can now make payments]].
- **[[GHA]] Ghana — interoperable mobile money +87% (2026-02).** [[bank-of-ghana]] data show
  interoperable mobile-money **value rose from GHc3.1bn (Dec 2024) to GHc5.8bn (Dec 2025),
  +87%**; transactions ~20m→29m (+45%) — value outpacing volume, i.e. higher-value payments
  migrating onto interoperable rails since the 2018 launch. Ghana runs [[papss]], a fintech
  passporting arrangement with Rwanda and a new Virtual Asset Service Providers Act, framing
  domestic interoperability as the platform for AfCFTA integration. See [[data.statistics]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-02-05 Ghana Mobile Money Interoperability Nearly Doubles in 2025]].
