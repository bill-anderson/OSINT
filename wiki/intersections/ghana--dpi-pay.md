# Ghana × Digital Payments

Ghana runs one of Africa's deepest mobile-money markets, with interoperable value climbing fast and
migrating onto shared rails as the platform for AfCFTA integration. Around that core sit a widening set
of DPI links: cross-border settlement via **[[onafriq-papss-ghana-launch|Onafriq–PAPSS]]**, welfare
delivery on **[[leap-ghana|LEAP]]** G2P rails, the Ghana Card fixed as the mandatory KYC anchor for
banking, and a fraud problem driving biometric SIM–ID linkage. This page also records a retracted
claim about Ghana Card *payment* functionality, kept so the reversed claim does not harden.

## What we know

- **[[GHA]] Onafriq–PAPSS cross-border launch, BoG pilot (2025-06-23).** [[gov.regional]] [[dpi.exchange]]
  **[[onafriq]]** and **[[papss]]** went live in Ghana ([[onafriq-papss-ghana-launch]]) — banks,
  fintechs and MMOs transacting to wallets/accounts on PAPSS rails, [[afreximbank]] settling — under a
  **[[bank-of-ghana]]** six-month pilot; the foundational leg before the Feb-2026 Nigeria→Ghana naira
  rail. Source: [[2025-06-23 Onafriq and PAPSS launch cross-border payments in Ghana]].
- **[[GHA]] LEAP welfare on MoMo G2P rails (2025-12-22).** [[include.access]] The 99th
  **[[leap-ghana|LEAP]]** cycle paid **GH¢139.3m** to ~350,580 households (~1.5m people) on
  mobile-money-enabled government-to-person rails; **GH¢1.1bn** allocated for the year, 400,000
  households targeted for 2026 — payments DPI carrying social protection. Source:
  [[2025-12-22 Government begins 99th cycle of LEAP cash-grant payments]].
- **[[GHA]] Ghana Card mandated as sole banking ID (2026-01-11).** [[dpi.id]] A revised BoG guidance
  note makes the [[ghana-card]] the primary/sole ID for financial onboarding with full biometric +
  liveness checks — hard-wiring the ID layer into the payments stack. Source:
  [[2026-01-11 Ghana Card mandatory for banking and digital transactions - BoG]].
- **[[GHA]] Mobile-money scale and fraud (2023–2025 baselines).** BoG's Payment Systems Oversight
  Report 2023 recorded mobile money at **6.81bn transactions / GH¢1.92tn** (+34%/+79% y/y), GhIPSS
  Instant Pay at GH¢120.1bn, and Ghana's first PAPSS transaction (Mar 2023). GSMA figures cited in
  2025 put MoMo at ~200% penetration / 66m accounts (end-2023); rising fraud (~20% of 13,451 sector
  cases in 2023) drives the biometric SIM–ID linkage push. Reference figures — cited, not current
  state. Sources: [[2024-01-01 Payment Systems Oversight Annual Report 2023 (Bank of Ghana)]],
  [[2025-09-09 Ghana to Mandate Biometric SIM Links in Bid to Curb Mobile Money Fraud]].

- **[[GHA]] Ghana Card payments — retracted claim (2026-04).** A TechCabal story that the
  **[[ghana-card]]** could now make payments was **retracted** as inaccurate and removed; the
  wiki carries no Ghana-Card payment functionality. Recorded to keep the reversed claim from
  hardening. Source:
  [[2026-04-01 RETRACTION Ghana's National ID cards can now make payments]].
- **[[GHA]] Ghana — interoperable mobile money +87% (2026-02).** [[bank-of-ghana]] data show
  interoperable mobile-money **value rose from GHc3.1bn (Dec 2024) to GHc5.8bn (Dec 2025),
  +87%**; transactions ~20m→29m (+45%) — value outpacing volume, i.e. higher-value payments
  migrating onto interoperable rails since the 2018 launch. Ghana runs [[papss]], a fintech
  passporting arrangement with Rwanda and a new Virtual Asset Service Providers Act, framing
  domestic interoperability as the platform for AfCFTA integration. See [[data.statistics]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-02-05 Ghana Mobile Money Interoperability Nearly Doubles in 2025]].

## 2025–26 developments

- **Record scale (dated).** MoMo transaction value climbed **GH¢1tn → 1.9tn → 3.01tn → 4.5tn (2022 → 2025
  full-year)** — a record GH¢4.5tn in 2025 (BoG). The **GH¢518.4b** figure is the **December-2025 monthly peak**
  (not annual), consistent with the GH¢4.5tn year. Sources:
  [[2025-12-15-momo-3trn-digital-payments-surge-myjoy]], [[2026-01-29-momo-2025-4-5-trillion-myjoy]],
  [[2026-02-02-momo-transactions-518b-2025-graphic]].
- **MoMo as standalone fintech.** **[[momo-fintech-ghana|MTN MobileMoney Fintech Ltd]]** — MTN completed the
  **spin-off of its Ghana MoMo business** (Act 987 compliance; MoMo earned **$549m in 2025**). Tap-to-pay
  arrived via **Blu Penguin × Network International** (any-provider MoMo on N-Genius POS); **Nikulipe** extended
  MTN MoMo cross-border. Sources: [[2026-04-03-mtn-momo-standalone-fintech-techcabal]],
  [[2025-07-18-ghana-tap-to-pay-blu-penguin-itweb]], [[2025-09-08-mtn-momo-nikulipe-expand-telecomreview]].
- **Rails & cross-border.** **[[ghipss-ghana|GhIPSS]]** (the national switch behind GIP and MoMo
  interoperability) is pushing **cross-border expansion** (ISO 20022 migration, VASP integration, 3i Africa
  Summit). A **Nigerian PSP, Fincra**, secured an enhanced payment licence. Sources:
  [[2026-05-06-ghipss-cross-border-expansion-3i-myjoy]], [[2026-05-06-fincra-enhanced-payment-licence-techcabal]].
- **eCedi & inclusion.** BoG says the **eCedi is not abandoned** — it wants more pilots and is **pivoting to
  cross-border settlement** (AfCFTA/stablecoin talks for SME trade); on inclusion, **81% account/phone ownership**
  (2025 Findex) but "architecture" gaps remain, and BoG's fintech head argues **MoMo histories should unlock
  loans/insurance**. A proposed **0.75% MoMo-to-bank fee** (e-levy successor) drew inclusion concerns, and Prof
  Bokpin warned against a **"tax trap."** Sources: [[2026-05-20-ecedi-not-abandoned-governor-myjoy]],
  [[2026-05-25-ecedi-cross-border-pivot-graphic]], [[2026-05-12-bog-inclusion-architecture-gaps-connectingafrica]],
  [[2026-07-09-momo-histories-unlock-loans-informal-myjoy]], [[2026-05-27-momo-to-bank-0-75-fee-concerns-myjoy]],
  [[2026-07-09-digital-finance-tax-trap-bokpin-myjoy]].
- **Regulation & enforcement.** BoG revoked **[[zeepay-ghana|Zeepay]]'s DEMI licence** (14 Jul 2026; Act 987
  s.13, e-money not fully cash-backed) and is rolling out a **new digital-banking framework** (draft guidelines +
  VASP Act 2025 rules + revised cyber directive). Fraud is rising (**16,733 cases / ~GH¢99m at risk, 2024**),
  fuelling a banks-vs-fintechs regulatory-burden dispute; BoG put **fintech at the forefront** of the digital
  economy. Sources: [[2026-07-15-zeepay-licence-revocation-myjoy]], [[2026-07-16-bog-new-digital-banking-framework-myjoy]],
  [[2026-07-08-banks-fintechs-regulatory-burden-fraud-myjoy]], [[2026-03-25-ghana-fintech-forefront-digital-economy-itweb]].
