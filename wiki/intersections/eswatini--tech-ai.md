# Eswatini × AI

Eswatini's AI posture is **skills-and-governance first**: a headline **300,000-coder** ambition, an early
**deepfake/broadcasting** guidance from the regulator, and a **"human-centred AI"** pitch on the world
stage — anchored in a **National 4IR Strategy**.

## Skills — the 300,000 target

- **300,000 coders and AI practitioners (2026).** ICT Minister Maziya set a target of **300,000 digitally
  skilled citizens** (in a population of ~1.2m) to keep Eswatini "relevant in the global technological
  landscape" — "if we do not take advantage of this AI stage, we render ourselves irrelevant" (with the
  Innovators Association, RSTP, UNDP). See [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-02-300000-emaswati-needed-for-country-digital-future]].

## Regulation — deepfakes and broadcasting

- **ESCCOM AI/deepfake warning (Nov 2025).** The regulator urged broadcasters to **manage AI ethically
  and transparently** to prevent misinformation and **deepfakes** eroding public trust — an early signal
  of AI/broadcasting guidance (alongside planned AI/robotics/space legislation, see
  [[eswatini--infra-connect]], [[eswatini--gov-protect]]). See [[gov.legislate]], [[infra.cybersec]].
  Source: [[2025-11-22-esccom-issues-ai-warning-on-deep-fakes]].

## Governance — human-centred AI

- **Geneva, Global Dialogue on AI Governance (Jul 2026).** PM Dlamini (for King Mswati III) called for a
  **human-centred approach** — "technology must serve people" — warning that small/developing nations
  **"should not become mere consumers of technologies developed elsewhere,"** and urging investment in
  local innovation, research, digital literacy, computing infrastructure and **trusted data ecosystems**.
  Framed around Eswatini's **National 4IR Strategy** (coding/data-science/AI education). See [[gov.policy]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-07-06-eswatini-champions-human-centred-ai]].

## Reads

- `colonialism` / `sovereignty`: Eswatini's AI message names the dependency squarely — **don't become mere
  consumers of others' technology** — and its answer is the realistic small-state one: **skills, local
  research and trusted data** rather than frontier models. The 300k-coder target is aspirational for a
  1.2m-person kingdom, but the sequencing (literacy + governance before deployment) is sound; the risk is
  the same gap that shadows the rest of Eswatini's digital agenda — ambition ahead of delivery capacity.
