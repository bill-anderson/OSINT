# The Gambia × Connectivity, data centres & satellite sovereignty

For a small state, The Gambia is running an unusually explicit **digital-sovereignty** line across two fronts:
**local data-centre capacity** and a **contested Starlink licence**.

## Data centres — two openings in 2026

- **[[qgroup-house-datacentre|QGroup House]]** — the country's **first (private) data centre** (QGroup/QCell +
  QFiber; **[[huawei]]** the long-term vendor), inaugurated Apr 2026 as "Gambian-owned innovation."
- **National Data Centre (Abuko)** — the **state** facility co-launched with the **[[nims-gambia|NIMS]]** digital
  ID (Jul 2026) — sovereign hosting for the identity/DPI stack.

## Starlink — sovereignty vs pressure

The **[[gambia-starlink-licence|Starlink licence saga]]**: Starlink paid its fee (Dec 2024) but the licence
stayed unissued for over a year, with the minister refusing a **VSAT** category Starlink "does not qualify for"
despite **US** pressure — while experts pushed for **data-localisation / lawful-interception / AI-training-consent**
conditions and a **balanced fibre-plus-satellite** strategy.

## Backbone

- **GAMTEL–SYSROAD** (Senegal) **$50m PPP** to modernise the national broadband backbone (40→800 Gbps) — part
  of the GAMTEL/GAMCEL telecom-market restructuring.
