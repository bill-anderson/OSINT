# Malawi × Data Protection

Malawi enacted a GDPR-shaped statute in 2024 and designated its **communications regulator** as
the Data Protection Authority — the Zambian model, not a standalone body. Two years on, the live
question is operationalisation, and one of the compliance dates does not arithmetically close.

## The statute

**[[malawi-data-protection-act-2024]]** — Data Protection Act 2024 (Act No. 3 of 2024). Passed
**2023-12-07**, assented **2024-01-31**, gazetted **2024-02-02**, **in force 2024-06-03**
(Government Notice 40 of 2024).

- **s.4 designates [[macra-malawi|MACRA]]**, the communications regulator, as Data Protection
  Authority.
- GDPR-shaped: data-subject rights, breach notification, cross-border adequacy, registration of
  controllers "of significant importance", extraterritorial reach.
- Reported **s.53** grace: six months for significant-importance controllers, **all others exempt
  until 2026-06-02**. Sources: [[2024-02-02-malawi-data-protection-act-2024]],
  [[2025-10-01-dmap-tor-operationalization-data-protection-authority]].

### An arithmetic that does not close

s.53 gives non-significant controllers **24 months from commencement** (6 for significant ones).
Commencement is recorded as **2024-06-03**, which yields *early* June 2026 — but the circulating
expiry is **2 June 2026**, implying a **2 June 2024** commencement. **Government Notice 40 of 2024
is unheld**, so the day cannot be settled; the date is carried **as reported, not as sourced**.

## Operationalisation lags enactment

- An **Oct 2025 World Bank / [[dmap]] ToR** was still tendering the DPA unit's organogram,
  enforcement framework and fee schedule. Source:
  [[2025-10-01-dmap-tor-operationalization-data-protection-authority]].
- Malawi used **Data Privacy Week (Jan 2026)** to declare "Privacy by Design" and the Act "no
  longer optional." Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Malawi positions data privacy as cornerstone of digital development]].
- By mid-2026 it is **operationalising**: **[[macra-malawi|MACRA]]** drafted **registration
  regulations with turnover-based fees** (**K50,000/~$29 → K7m/>$4,000**) and, with World Bank
  technical assistance, plans to spin the **DPA and MwCERT into independent institutions**.
  Sources: [[2026-07-16-macra-data-protection-registration-compliance-malawi24]],
  [[2026-03-26-world-bank-cybersecurity-data-protection-support-techafricanews]].

## Local hosting for data control (2026-07)

Malawi's **Bomalathu** immigration-services integration ([[dmap]]) keeps immigration data on
**locally hosted servers**, letting the department **retain ownership and control** —
localisation as sovereignty and as secure public-sector data management. See [[dpi.govtech]].
Source: [[2026-07-07 Malawi integrates immigration e-services]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the regulator-as-DPA design (s.4) buys Malawi speed and an existing institution,
  at the cost of independence — the same structural objection raised of POTRAZ in [[ZWE]] and
  ESCCOM in [[SWZ]]. Enforcement capacity, not statute count, is the live question.
