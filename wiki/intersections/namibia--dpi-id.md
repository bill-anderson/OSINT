# Namibia × Digital Identity & CRVS

Namibia is rolling out a smart-card **e-ID** on a modern CRVS base — but the launch has slipped, and two very
different backlashes (a **"microchip-tracking"** panic and a **documentation-backlog exclusion** critique) have
put the politics of identity front and centre.

## The programme

- **A microchip smart-card e-ID.** The **[[namibia-digital-id|e-ID]]** is a secure contactless smart card
  (bank-card-style chip) holding **biometric and demographic** data, built on the Civil Register under the
  **[[namibia-civil-registration-identification-act-2024|CRIA 2024]]** and led by the
  **[[ministry-home-affairs-namibia|Ministry of Home Affairs]]** (ED Etienne Maritz) with **[[undp]]** (Namibia
  the first user of UNDP's Model Governance Framework). Designed for real-time verification across health,
  banking, education, elections and government, with **mobile verification units** for rural birth-registration,
  voter enrolment and grants. An **UnConference on Digital Legal Identity** (Windhoek, 26 Jun 2025) opened the
  awareness campaign. Sources: [[2025-06-09-namibia-to-launch-groundbreaking-e-id-namibiansun]],
  [[2025-06-12-namibia-roll-out-digital-id-card-july-2026-biometricupdate]].
- **An e-signature precursor (Feb 2026).** Four months before the e-ID, **[[cran]]** (CEO Emilia Nghikembua)
  planned to launch an **electronic-signatures system** (Feb 2026) enabling legally binding digital
  transactions without travel — see [[dpi.govtech]].

## The slipping timeline and 2025 volumes

- Originally targeted for **July 2026** (in the 2026/27 budget), the launch was later restated as **September
  2026**. In 2025 the government issued **117,175 national IDs**, **126,737 birth certificates**, and **682
  refugee ("African guest") IDs** (Minister Lucia Iipumbu). Sources:
  [[2026-03-16-namibia-expedites-electronic-ids-issuance-itweb]],
  [[2026-06-20-iipumbu-dismisses-e-id-microchip-tracking-namibian]].

## Two backlashes

- **"Microchip-tracking" panic (2026-06).** Residents of the **Omusati region** protested the e-ID, claiming it
  would microchip and track citizens. Minister **Iipumbu** rebutted in the National Assembly: the e-ID "does not
  involve the implantation of any device, chip, or technology into any individual" — the chip sits in the card,
  like a bank card — and implementation "will fully comply with the CRIA 2024." A case of disinformation meeting
  a biometric-ID rollout. See [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-20-iipumbu-dismisses-e-id-microchip-tracking-namibian]].
- **Documentation-backlog exclusion (2026-07).** Conversely, **thousands of undocumented Namibians** petitioned
  to **suspend** the e-ID until a **years-long backlog** of citizenship and identity documents is cleared —
  framing the documentation crisis as a **constitutional** issue: rolling out a digital layer atop an
  incomplete foundational register risks entrenching exclusion from jobs, education and services. See
  [[include.divides]], [[include.access]]. Source (paywalled lede):
  [[2026-07-01-delay-e-id-rollout-until-backlog-cleared-namibiansun]].

The tension: a well-architected e-ID whose legitimacy depends as much on **inclusion and trust** as on
technology. See [[NAM]], [[dpi.id]], [[sovereignty]].
