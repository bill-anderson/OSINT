# Ghana × Connectivity

Ghana's connectivity policy swung hard in 2026 — from a **single shared-5G wholesaler** to an **open,
competitive market** — while its fibre backbone strains under record damage.

## 5G — from NGIC monopoly to open market

- **5G-by-2027 directive** (70% population coverage, "Ghana@70"); **[[ngic-ghana|NGIC]]** (Next-Gen InfraCo)
  got **NCA clearance** and **activated Ghana's first shared 5G wholesale network** (Nokia, Mar 2026).
- **Reversal (Jul 2026):** the government **ended NGIC's exclusivity**, letting **MTN and Telecel bid** for 5G
  licences and **opening spectrum-licence applications** — a major policy U-turn (analysts had questioned the
  single-wholesaler model). See [[national-communications-authority-ghana|NCA]].

## Fibre & capacity

- **Backbone under strain:** **>8,000 fibre cuts/year** (up from ~400) — sabotage and road works disrupted
  **157 sites** (MTN); a **"Dig Once" policy** targets up to **60% lower** rollout costs. Extra spectrum to
  MTN/Telecel eased congestion; a **1,150-cell-site rollout** (MTN 800 + Telecel 350); **MTN cut fibre-broadband
  prices** (100Mbps unlimited **GH¢987 → GH¢299**).
- **Rural:** **[[gifec-ghana|GIFEC]]**'s Rural Telephony Project (Huawei RTP masts) remains the underserved-areas
  priority.

## Data centres

- **[[digital-realty-accra|Digital Realty ACR2]]** — Ghana's largest data centre (Accra, 2025-10; 1.7 MW, 500
  racks, on 2Africa) — carrier-neutral capacity for the digital-sovereignty push.
