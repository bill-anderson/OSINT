# Niger × Connectivity & Starlink

For a junta-led Sahel state with **~23% internet penetration** and **~80% rural** population, **Starlink** is the
headline connectivity story — welcomed as rural access, tightly regulated as a sovereignty/security matter, and
now folded into a telco.

## Commercial launch (US satellite in a junta Sahel state)

- **Starlink available from 13 Mar 2025.** Minister **Raliou** announced commercial availability (residential +
  business kits, online + approved sellers), following a **partnership agreement signed 29 Oct 2024 between the
  Nigerien state and SpaceX** — framed within the junta's divide-closing digital strategy but "strictly for the
  personal or professional use of the sole client." Niger was the **18th African Starlink market** at the time
  (Chad signed a licence the same week; contrast Lesotho/Namibia resistance). See [[geopol.usa]],
  [[include.divides]]. Sources: [[2025-03-13-starlink-officially-available-niger-actuniger]],
  [[2025-03-17-starlink-beams-into-niger-connectingafrica]].
- **NINETEC — a "100% Nigerien" distributor (2025-04).** **[[ninetec]]** launched its **NINE-Starlink**
  distribution campaign (30 Apr 2025) — local ownership + a global satellite leader, aiming to connect health
  centres and rural communes; the **US Embassy** economic adviser attended (the geopolitics of US connectivity in
  the Sahel). Kit sales follow **décret 2020-331** KYC (registered in the final client's name; no resale). Source:
  [[2025-05-06-ninetec-nine-starlink-kit-distribution-lesahel]].

## Regulation as sovereignty

- **ARCEP's war on pirate "Wifi Zones" (2025-09).** **[[arcep-niger|ARCEP]]** issued a formal notice against
  **unauthorised resale** of Starlink connectivity via "Wifi Zones," invoking **Loi 2018-45** (arts 16/23,
  prior authorisation): sanctions of **3 months–1 year** and fines to **60m FCFA** (plus 6–12m FCFA per
  non-homologated terminal), with a **cease-and-regularise ultimatum by 3 Oct 2025**. The tension: Starlink's
  rural-access value vs the state's regulatory/sovereignty control — the same logic behind ARCEP's continuous
  monitoring platform (see [[niger--infra-cybersec]]). See [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-09-arcep-crackdown-pirate-starlink-wifi-zones-actuniger]].

## The telco-integrated turn

- **"Starlink by Airtel" (2026-01).** **[[airtel-niger|Airtel Niger]]** (market leader, ~40%+) commercialised a
  telco-integrated satellite service from the **Bharti Airtel × SpaceX** partnership — Niger among the first
  African trials — a distinct model from direct retail (the operator, not the customer, holds the Starlink
  relationship). See [[tech.industry]], [[geopol.india]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-18-airtel-niger-starlink-by-airtel-launch-actuniger]].
