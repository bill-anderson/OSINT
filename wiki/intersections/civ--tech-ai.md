# Côte d'Ivoire × AI & the strategy stack

Côte d'Ivoire runs a **coherent, costed AI-and-data strategy stack** under a single
**technological-sovereignty** banner — and pairs it with an unusually active **international AI-governance
profile**, courting the US, the Gulf and multilateral fora at once.

## The strategy stack, costed

- **The paired strategies.** The **[[civ-ai-strategy|National AI Strategy]]** (**912bn FCFA, 15
  objectives, 109 projects / 5 yrs**) and the **[[civ-data-governance-strategy|National Data Governance
  Strategy 2030]]** were handed to the PM on **13 Mar 2025**, adopting the **UNESCO RAM** readiness
  methodology (HABG/ministry/UNESCO letter of intent, 30 Sep 2024) and launched to the private sector
  (May 2025) with a **sovereign cloud**, a **"Safe AI" label** and a National Committee. Delivery leans on
  **[[parae]]** (~49bn FCFA incl. a data centre) and PADCI. The whole is coordinated under the
  **[[ministry-of-digital-transition-cote-divoire|MTND]]** — the Yamoussoukro seminar (reported
  **8 Apr** by Linfodrome / **12 Apr** on the hub — a 4-day inter-outlet date discrepancy, unresolved)
  aligned the AI, cybersecurity, data-governance and "zero paper" strategies. Sources:
  [[2025-02-19-siade-2025-ia-defense-fratmat]],
  [[2025-03-14-bad-strategies-gouvernance-donnees-ia-fratmat]],
  [[2025-03-14-ai-data-governance-strategy-wearetech]],
  [[2025-05-15-strategies-ia-donnees-secteur-prive-fratmat]],
  [[2025-05-19-revolution-numerique-ia-moteur-developpement-fratmat]],
  [[2026-04-08-yamoussoukro-alignement-strategique-numerique-linfodrome]].
- **The "zero paper" ambition, on record.** A ministry interview set out the 2026–2030 strategy, a
  **national AI agency** and a startup law. Source: [[2025-07-01-digital-transformation-chief-interview-techcabal]].

## Sovereignty framing, and the patrons courted

- **US and Gulf, side by side.** Alongside the US/Cybastion push (see [[civ--infra-store]]), CIV signed
  **two non-binding MoUs with the UAE's [[g42]] / Presight** (Jul 2025) on public-service modernisation and
  help writing the *next* national strategy — a Gulf entry layered onto the US one. Sources:
  [[2025-07-15-g42-presight-ai-mou-wearetech]], [[2025-07-15-g42-presight-uae-public-services-mou-techafricanews]].
- **Private-sector marker.** AI consultancy **[[artefact]]** made Abidjan its Francophone Africa hub
  (May 2025). Source: [[2025-05-09-artefact-ai-office-abidjan-itweb]].
- **Sovereignty as the frame.** SIADE opened continental-sovereignty reflection (Mar 2026); the digital-law
  profession pushed a **"sovereign AI"** agenda (Jun 2026); FEMUA 18 voiced an **African-AI-hub** ambition.
  Sources: [[2026-03-12-siade-souverainete-technologique-fratmat]],
  [[2026-06-26-journees-droit-numerique-ia-souveraine-fratmat]],
  [[2026-04-30-femua-18-hub-africain-ia-fratmat]].

## Capacity and global voice

- **AI university & satellite dependency.** A public **AI university** is planned under PND 2026–2030 (with
  AI centres of excellence); ministerial framing explicitly ties AI ambitions to **satellite-driven**
  connectivity for public services. Sources: [[2026-06-02-ai-university-pnd-2026-2030-wearetech]],
  [[2026-04-30-ai-ambitions-satellite-public-services-techreviewafrica]].
- **Global AI governance.** Côte d'Ivoire took its case to Paris (2025), then to Geneva — calling at the
  **UN AI Governance Dialogue** for an **"African AI, by Africans, for Africans"** and a Regional African
  School of AI, and appearing (via ARTCI) in global AI-governance fora. Sources:
  [[2025-02-11-sommet-ia-paris-opportunite-fratmat]], [[2025-02-12-sommet-ia-ram-konate-fratmat]],
  [[2026-07-07-african-led-ai-un-governance-dialogue-techreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-07-08-gouvernance-mondiale-ia-geneve-artci-fratmat]].
