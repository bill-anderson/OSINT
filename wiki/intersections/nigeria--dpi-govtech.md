# Nigeria × GovTech & E-Government

Beneath the DPI-architecture layer ([[nigeria--dpi-exchange]]) sits a wave of **back-office
e-government systems** — treasury, payments, asset and document management — digitising the plumbing
of the federal state. Nigeria ranked **144th of 193** on the UN e-Government index (2024) and targets
**75% of public services digitised by 2027**.

## Federal financial & administrative systems (2025-2026)

- **Treasury Management & Revenue Assurance System (Mar 2025).** The Office of the Accountant-General
  launched a **TMRAS** (go-live 4 Mar 2025) to coordinate FGN revenue and payments across all MDAs and
  government-owned enterprises, with **automatic deduction/remittance** of VAT, WHT and stamp duty on
  vendor payments — revenue assurance and budget control. Source:
  [[2025-03-06-treasury-management-system-launch]].
- **CBN DocFlow + MDA Naira Payment Solution (Jan 2025).** Under the [[cbn]]'s "Digital First"
  programme, Governor Cardoso launched **DocFlow** (digitised document/approval management) and an
  **MDA Naira Payment Solution** automating MDA cash-withdrawal processing. Source:
  [[2025-01-16-cbn-mda-naira-payment-docflow]].
- **National Electronic Assets Register (Mar 2025).** **NEAR** gives real-time visibility of
  federally-owned assets — pitched (Min. of State for Finance Doris Uzoka-Anite) as improving
  Nigeria's creditor rating, debt sustainability and transparency. Source:
  [[2025-03-07-national-electronic-assets-register]].
- **National Single Window (2025).** The FG moved to implement the **National Single Window** trade-
  facilitation platform, digitally linking ports, customs and agencies to one data-exchange point
  (see [[dpi.exchange]]). Source: [[2025-01-29-national-single-window-2025]].

## Platform & certification

- **cBrain F2 e-government platform (Jan 2026).** Denmark's **cBrain** partnered Nigerian provider
  **Publica AI** to deploy its **F2** platform (case management, automated workflows, embedded AI)
  across federal ministries — with the assurance that **"all sovereign data remains in Nigeria."** See
  [[geopol.eu]], [[nigeria--infra-store]]. Source: [[2026-01-14-cbrain-denmark-f2-egovernment]].
- **Nigerian Digital Trustmark (Oct 2025).** [[nitda]] launched a **Digital Trustmark** to certify
  platforms meeting standards for data protection, cybersecurity and user trust — a benchmark for
  responsible digital-service delivery. See [[gov.standards]], [[nigeria--infra-cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-10-nitda-digital-trustmark-initiative]].
