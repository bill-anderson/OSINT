# Rwanda × Connectivity

Rwanda has **near-universal mobile broadband** but a still-modest **~43% internet penetration** and a
sharp **rural school** gap — the classic "coverage without adoption/access" shape, now attacked with
satellite resale and school-connectivity drives.

## Coverage and penetration (2025)

- **Near-universal 4G.** By end-2025, **4G coverage ~100%**, **3G 99.71%** (MWC Kigali 2025 analysis),
  on a **technology-neutral** 2022 broadband policy that abandoned the single-wholesale-network model
  and refarmed spectrum (700/900/1800/2100 MHz). Yet **internet penetration ~42.6%** (1.35m households
  with home internet), ICT-service exports **$2.52/capita** — coverage far ahead of adoption. See
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2025-10-22-digital-penetration-masterclass-telecomreview]].

## Operators

- **MTN Rwanda returns to profit (Q1 2026).** **[[mtn-rwanda]]** posted **Rwf10.8bn (~$6.8m)** profit
  after tax, reversing a year-earlier loss; service revenue **+14.7%** to Rwf295.7bn, EBITDA margin
  35.8%. Growth led by digital: **data subscribers +14.1% to 2.8m**, **MoMo MAU +17.3% to 6.2m**,
  fintech revenue +27.6% — Rwanda a fast-growing (if small) digital economy. See [[tech.industry]],
  [[dpi.pay]]. Source: [[2026-05-11-mtn-rwanda-return-to-profit-techcabal]].
- **Hayo market entry (Mar 2026).** Global provider **Hayo** launched voice, messaging, **eSIM**,
  **National Mobile Registry (NMR)** and IoT services for Rwandan operators, enterprises and
  government (also entering Senegal Q1 2026). See [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-25-hayo-expands-rwanda-techafricanews]].

## Satellite and the rural gap

- **BSC becomes a Starlink reseller (2026).** **[[broadband-systems-corporation]]** became a **Rwanda
  Space Agency-certified [[starlink]] reseller** (partnership since Dec 2025), with tailored packages
  for health/education/non-profits and planned **SME packages up to 300 Mbps** — aimed at underserved
  rural communities and small businesses. See [[data.satellite]], [[digital.rural]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-17-bsc-starlink-reseller-newtimes]] *(partial body — context from syndicated coverage;
  verify against the New Times original)*.
- **The 812-school gap.** At EdTech Mondays (Mar 2026), rural-school connectivity took centre stage:
  only **~27% of rural schools** have internet vs **~75% urban**; **62% of schools connected** by late
  2024 against an **80% target** — 812 schools remain, with Starlink+solar among proposed fixes
  (barriers: electricity, affordability, maintenance). See [[include.divides]], [[digital.rural]].
  Source: [[2026-03-31-connecting-812-schools-edtech-ktpress]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: near-universal coverage is a genuine state achievement, but the **adoption gap**
  (43% penetration, 27% rural schools) is where digital transformation stalls — and the fix increasingly
  runs through **foreign satellite** (Starlink via a certified local reseller), trading rural reach for
  a new external dependency at the access layer.
