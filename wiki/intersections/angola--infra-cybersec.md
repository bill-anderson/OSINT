# Angola × Cybersecurity

Angola is building a cybersecurity apparatus on **two parallel tracks that both stand up the same institutions**, while its *practice* — Predator spyware against a journalist, a "fake news" law the opposition calls a muzzle — runs ahead of, and against the grain of, its stated rights safeguards. The country entered this build near the **bottom of the ITU Global Cybersecurity Index (Tier 4, 2024)**, the second-lowest of five bands.

## The two-track institution build

**Legislative track — approved, then stalled.** The Executive's **[[angola-cybersecurity-law]]** (Proposta de Lei de Cibersegurança) won **general approval on 23 Jan 2026 (105–1–75)**, creating a **National Cybersecurity Council** and a **[[centro-nacional-de-ciberseguranca-angola]]**. **UNITA's 75 abstentions** were grounded in fear of **election-day/protest-day internet shutdowns**, citing 2024 precedents in Tanzania, Kenya and Uganda. Committee-stage discussion was then **suspended, unanimously, on 7–8 Jul 2026** over "inconformidades na redacção, na forma e no conteúdo" (preamble, chapters 1–2). Minister **[[mario-oliveira]]** called it strategically important; the opposition warned it could become "an instrument of abusive surveillance and digital repression" absent strong judicial guarantees. **The bill is stalled as of the window's close.**

**Decree track — a strategy in draft.** Separately, Angola unveiled a **National Cybersecurity Strategy** ([[national-cybersecurity-strategy-angola]]) in **December 2025** as a **draft Presidential Decree**, grounded in Law 7/17, with six strategic objectives and — again — a **National Cybersecurity Council + incident-response teams (CERTs)**.

> **Contradiction flagged (decree vs statute).** The Dec-2025 decree-track Strategy and the Jan-2026 legislative bill each create a National Cybersecurity Council and national centre. Whether the decree stands up the bodies pending the (now-stalled) law, or the two are rival tracks, is **not resolved** in sources held. President **[[joao-lourenco]]** listed the *Centro Nacional de Cibersegurança* among digital-sovereignty investments (2026-03-03) without clarifying which instrument creates it. Captured dated on both entity pages; raised for the orchestrator.

## The US vendor inside the institution

The national cybersecurity *agency* is being financed from outside: **[[cybastion]]**, a US technology firm, signed a **$170m** agreement with **[[angola-telecom]]** at the **US–Africa Business Summit (Luanda, 24 Jun 2025)**, a **$25m first phase** funding the **establishment of a national cybersecurity agency** and Cisco-Networking-Academy training under its "Digital Fast Track," plus a Luanda–Lobito undersea cable. A **US pole** thus sits inside Angola's cyber-institution build — a fourth external actor alongside the China/India/UAE contest the wiki reads over the ID stack.

## Content control as cybersecurity

The **[[angola-fake-news-law]]** (Lei contra Informações Falsas na Internet) **passed definitively on 21 May 2026** (105 MPLA/PRS/PHA votes; UNITA against): a base penalty of **1–5 years**, rising to **10 years** for hate incitement, violence, discrimination or **compromising electoral processes**. UNITA and civil society read it as a tool to criminalise journalists and repress expression; the government frames it as combating structured disinformation.

## Practice ahead of protections

The sharpest evidence is a **surveillance case**: Amnesty International's Security Lab gave the **first forensic confirmation** that Intellexa's **Predator** spyware ([[predator-spyware-angola]]) was **active on journalist [[teixeira-candido]]'s phone on 4 May 2024**, with the state intelligence service **[[sinse-angola]]** named as the software's end-user in a contract dated to April 2021. Cândido filed a criminal complaint; this is the adversarial counter-story (reported by *Novo Jornal* / *Expresso*) to the state's digital-sovereignty framing.

**International cooperation.** President Lourenço reported (2026-03-03) that Angola has **signed the UN Convention against Cybercrime** (ratification pending) and that ~**$20m** in cybercrime-related assets were seized in 2025 (illegal crypto-mining, bank-system attacks, comms diversion).
