# Sierra Leone × AI, Skills & Innovation

From a low base (**EGDI 172/193**, 2024), Sierra Leone is building AI and digital-skills capacity
top-down — a first **National AI Strategy**, whole-of-government cyber/AI leadership training, and a
civil-service skills pipeline — anchored by the **[[dsti]]** and MoCTI under President Bio's "Big Five
Game Changers."

## The tech-ambition frame

- **Ministerial ambition (2025).** In a wide-ranging interview, Minister Salima Bah reported **>$132m**
  invested in tech since the ministry's 2024 creation; internet users **1.84m (2023) → ~2.32m (2025)**;
  a **75%+** rise in fibre-backbone penetration since 2018; and — after privatising the state subsea
  vehicle **SALCAB** — capacity expanded **90 Gbps → 500+ Gbps**, a Freetown metro network, and
  **OneMobile**, a data-only network on **open-access 5G standalone** ("first in West Africa," Jun 2025).
  See [[sierra-leone--infra-connect]]. Source: [[2025-07-31-salima-bah-tech-ambition-interview]].

## The National AI Strategy

- **First AI Strategy + readiness diagnostic (2026).** MoCTI, with the **World Bank**, is developing
  Sierra Leone's **first National AI Strategy**, producing a **Draft AI Readiness Diagnostic** (institutional,
  technological, social readiness) and running three-day multi-stakeholder consultations — explicitly
  seeking to reflect **local languages and cultural contexts** for ethical, inclusive AI. Builds on the
  2025 Qhala AI MoU and the National AI Readiness Assessment (see [[SLE]] hub). See [[gov.policy]],
  [[gov.standards]]. Source: [[2026-05-28-national-ai-strategy-readiness]].

## Capacity — cyber, AI and civil-service skills

- **Whole-of-government AI + cyber leadership training (Mar 2026).** MoCTI, **[[dsti]]**, the National
  Cybersecurity Coordination Centre (**[[nc3]]**) and **Qhala** trained **50+ senior leaders** across
  MDAs — digital literacy, emerging-tech national-security implications, and AI foundations — so Sierra
  Leone digitises with "strong digital armor" (linked to the MTNDP Big Five). Follows the Sep-2025 Qhala
  partnership. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-03-mocti-qhala-civil-servants-ai-cyber-training]].
- **Digital Skills Programme — 1,000 civil servants (Jul 2026).** MoCTI/MoPAPA/CSTC/HRMO launched a
  civil-service digital-skills programme (**1,000** in year one; **3,000** medium-term, **5,000+** long-
  term) under the **Digital Skills Development & Gig Economy Pipeline (2025–26)** — six areas including
  AI/automation and cybersecurity — addressing skills gaps outside Freetown. See [[capacity.training]],
  [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2026-07-10-1000-civil-servants-digital-skills]].

## Geospatial & data science

- **DSTI × Catholic Relief Services (2026).** **[[dsti]]** and CRS signed a geospatial-technology MoU —
  the **Coordination & Visualization Platform (CVP)** (launched 6 Jul 2026) and an **SDS4D** ("Spatial
  Data Science for Development") workshop (60 leaders) covering GIS/remote sensing, hazard mapping, and
  **secure inter-agency data governance** for disaster management and WASH planning. See [[data.satellite]],
  [[dpi.exchange]]. Source: [[2026-06-30-dsti-crs-geospatial-data-mou]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: capacity is being built **top-down and partner-dependent** (World Bank, Qhala, CRS) but
  with deliberate sovereignty markers — local-language AI, in-house DSTI data-science, "digital armor"
  for citizen data. The bet is that public-sector capability, not private frontier compute, is the
  realistic lever for a low-EGDI state.
