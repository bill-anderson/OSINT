# Liberia × Data Protection

Liberia keeps **expanding data collection ahead of protection**: a mandatory biometric NIN, a
foreign concessionaire holding citizens' biometric records, an alleged multi-agency breach — and a
data-protection bill that is still unpassed.

## Collection first

- **EO 147** makes the biometric **NIN mandatory**.
- The **[[osd-international|OSD]] concession** hands a foreign firm the biometric records of
  **5.3m** citizens.
- A **Feb-2026 House probe** alleges a breach of ~**2m** citizens' data (LRA / LEC / NIC).

Sources: [[2026-02-12-house-probe-data-breach-2m-citizens-observer]],
[[2026-05-15-editorial-digital-sovereignty-outsourcing-spine-observer]].

## Protection later

- The comprehensive **[[liberia-data-protection-bill|Personal Information Act]]** — drafted with
  **Internews** under the **EU LMEP**, **validated Dec 2024** — only reached a **House committee
  in June 2025** and is **unpassed**. Source:
  [[2025-06-11-house-reviews-privacy-data-protection-bill-observer]].
- The bill was noted as **before the legislature** at the ID4Africa 2026 AGM. Source:
  [[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].
- The only standing guards are the **Cybercrime Act 2025** and **[[otdi-liberia|EO 163]]**'s
  **Article-16 data-sharing rules**.

## The critique

Civil society (**[[naymote]]**) and a *Liberian Observer* editorial call it building the
**"digital spine" without its legal skeleton**. See [[dpi.id]], [[liberia--dpi-id]],
[[infra.cybersec]].

## Reads

- `colonialism`: the biometric records of 5.3m citizens sit with a foreign concessionaire under an
  executive order, with no statute governing their processing and no authority to complain to —
  outsourcing the register before legislating the rights over it.
