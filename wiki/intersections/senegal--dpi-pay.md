# Senegal × Digital Payments & Fintech

Mobile money is the backbone of Senegal's economy — **>90% of adults use mobile wallets** (vs ~26% with bank
accounts) and platforms processed **CFA15.3 trillion in 2025**; GSMA (via BCEAO) puts **76% of adults** on a
mobile-money account, **14.65m active accounts (2024)**, ~2.9bn transactions worth CFA48,488bn, contributing
~$6bn / ~8.6% of GDP (2023). Competition spans Orange Money, Wave, Yas, Wizall and E-Money.

## The mobile-money tax debate (2025)

Facing a fiscal hole, the government proposed a **0.5% levy on mobile-money transfers** (plus higher merchant
charges) to raise ~**CFA220bn over three years** (2025–2028 Recovery Plan). Consumer bodies, fintechs and
some lawmakers warned it could reverse financial-inclusion gains and push users back to cash; proposed
alternatives include taxing operator revenue (~2.5%), levying only withdrawals, or progressive/high-value
taxation. The **[[gsma]]** reform study went further, recommending the **0.5% transfer tax be scrapped**
alongside broader sector-tax cuts. Sources:
[[2025-11-24 Digitizing public payments provide solution to mobile money tax reform in Senegal]],
[[2025-12-08 GSMA maps the reforms required for Senegal's digital takeoff]].

## Digital payments for universal health coverage

**[[sen-csu|SEN-CSU]]** (Universal Health Coverage agency) digitised operations and payments, helping
**coverage pass 50% of citizens in 2022** (from a decades-long <10%). Digital payments **halved enrolment
costs**, expanded nationwide reach, and empowered women (**85% of beneficiaries**); the platform, built
in-country with **[[better-than-cash-alliance]]** support, aligns to the UN 9 Principles for Responsible
Digital Payments. Source:
[[2025-10-01 How has Senegal made responsible digital payments a catalyst for universal health coverage]].

## The fintech market and its players (2025-2026)

Senegal's digital-payments market is projected at **$3.08bn (2025) → $8.15bn (2030)** (21.5% CAGR),
on a young population (74% under 40). The disruptor is **Wave** — Francophone Africa's first unicorn
($1.7bn, 2021) — whose low-fee model (free deposits/withdrawals, flat **1% transfer fee**) broke the
5–10% telco-operator pricing; Wave raised **$137m in debt** (Jun 2025; RMB/BII/Finnfund/Norfund),
serving **20m+ MAU** via 150,000 agents across eight markets. Alongside Orange Money (13m), Free Money
and Yas. A **"bank-to-wallet"** wave now lets customers move money instantly between bank accounts and
wallets — reshaping bank economics and pushing the phone to first point of access. Sources:
[[2025-06-30-techcabal-wave-137m-debt]], [[2025-07-11-itweb-flutterwave-senegal-license]],
[[2026-07-14-lesoleil-bank-to-wallet-revolution]].

## BCEAO licensing and the fintech crackdown

Under the UEMOA fintech regulation (in force **23 Jan 2024**; deposit protection, AML/CFT), **[[bceao]]**
had issued only **nine e-money/PSP approvals across UEMOA — two in Senegal (Paydunya, Mikati)** — of 100+
applicants, blocking non-compliant fintechs while pledging to speed the backlog (May 2025). **Flutterwave**
then won a **BCEAO Payment Institution Licence** for Senegal (Jul 2025), adding cross-border CFA payouts
and Shopify/WooCommerce APIs. See [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.standards]], [[gov.regional]]. Sources:
[[2025-05-10-lesoleil-bceao-fintech-agrements]], [[2025-07-11-itweb-flutterwave-senegal-license]].

## G2P, agri subsidies and startup finance

- **Agricultural-subsidy digitisation (Mar 2025).** The Agriculture Ministry partnered the **IFC** to
  digitise **agricultural subsidies** (peanuts, maize, cowpeas, rice) for transparency and efficiency —
  a G2P thread. Source: [[2025-03-14-ifc-agricultural-payments-digitization]].
- **Catalyst DER/FJ — $50m startup fund (2026).** At VivaTech, **DER/FJ** launched a **$50m** pre-seed/
  seed fund for Senegalese startups (tickets ~€50–60k) and a **DER/FJ–Wave** acceleration partnership;
  DER/FJ already disburses via Orange Money/Wave across Senegal's 552 communes. See [[tech.innovate]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-07-07-techcabal-catalyst-derfj-50m-fund]].

## Super-apps into mobility

- **Max it × Dakar BRT (Jul 2026).** [[sonatel]] integrated its **Max it** super-app into the Dakar
  **Bus Rapid Transit** (sub-Saharan Africa's first fully-electric BRT, up to 300k passengers/day) for
  ticketing, top-ups, Orange Bank financing and station Wi-Fi — digital payments on the BRT already at
  **39%** (from 10%). Source: [[2026-07-15-wearetech-sonatel-maxit-brt]].
