# Benin × Data Protection

Benin has one of Africa's more **operational** data-protection authorities — with published
numbers, a concrete enforcement act against the electoral agency, and a regional leadership role.
It is the rights-side counterweight to Benin's fast ID and e-government build.

## The authority

The **[[apdp-benin|APDP]]** (president **Luciano Hounkponou**) was created by the **Code du
numérique** (**loi 2017-20 / 2020-35**). Source: [[2025-05-05-benin-loi-protection-donnees-2021]].

## The 2025 bilan — hard numbers

- **907 files examined** (**87.4% validated**)
- **782 contentious**
- **64 control missions**
- **75 conformity certificates**
- **37 DPOs**
- **14 sanctioned cases**

Source: [[2026-06-25-apdp-bilan-2025-conformite]].

## A specific AI policy

The APDP carries a **dedicated AI policy with strict purpose-limitation** — noted at the ID4Africa
2026 AGM as one of the few African DPAs to have one. See [[tech.ai]]. Source:
[[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].

## Enforcement against the electoral agency

The APDP's clearest concrete act: making the electoral agency **strip extraneous fields from the
electoral roll before publication** — a transparency-vs-privacy question resolved by the regulator
rather than the legislature (cf. [[angola--gov-protect]], where the same conflict went to the
plenary).

## Regional leadership, and its own stated gaps

- Hounkponou is **AFAPDP vice-president** and sits on the **INPDPA executive committee**.
- Conventions with **Senegal's [[cdp-senegal|CDP]]** and **Mali's [[apdp-mali|APDP]]**. Source:
  [[2026-04-17-benin-senegal-apdp-cdp-convention]].
- The APDP flags its own gaps: **no algorithmic-audit tooling**, and budget.

See [[gov.regional]], [[senegal--gov-protect]], [[mali--gov-protect]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Benin is the counter-example to "fast DPI, absent protection" — the ID and
  e-government build ([[benin--dpi-id]]) runs alongside a regulator that publishes its caseload
  and has bitten a state body. The stated gap is technical capacity (algorithmic audit), which is
  where the next round of enforcement would have to happen.
