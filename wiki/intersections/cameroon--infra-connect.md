# Cameroon × Connectivity

Cameroon's connectivity story is one of a **Chinese-financed backbone**, a **state gatekeeper**
([[camtel]]) that is profitable only on wholesale, a regulator ([[art-cameroon]]) that has started to
bite, and a **regional dependency** running through Cameroon's coast into landlocked Chad.

## The backbone is Chinese-financed

The wiki's connectivity narrative was World-Bank-funded PATNUC; the record shows the **backbone itself
is Chinese-financed**. By decree **n°2026/084 of 6 Mar 2026**, Cameroon took a **China Exim Bank loan
of ~¥1.35bn (~108.4bn FCFA / ~US$195m)** for **Phase IV** of the national fibre backbone — **+4,000 km**
on Camtel's ~12,000 km network, Huawei-linked, the largest single China exposure in CMR holdings, amid
IMF debt-distress warnings ([[china-eximbank]], [[geopol.china]]). *(Outlet error to note: TechAfrica
headlined it "$108 Billion CFA," conflating $195m with CFA 108.4bn; ITWeb has it right.)* Sources:
[[2026-03-11-leconomie-china-eximbank-fibre-backbone-phase4-loan]],
[[2026-03-09-seeks-195-million-optic-fibre-backbone-eximbank-china-itweb]].

## Camtel: wholesale-profitable, retail-negligible, fragile at the coast

- **Market structure (2026-03):** Camtel **2.67%** mobile share vs Orange **50.08%**, MTN **47.2%** — its
  value is wholesale bandwidth and **submarine-cable landing-station** control, not retail (it tendered
  2.3m SIMs anyway). Backbone ~12,000 km; **180,000 fibre ports, target 230,000** by end-2025; 4G/LTE in
  160+ municipalities. Sources: [[2026-03-30-camtel-tender-2-3-million-sims-itweb]],
  [[2025-09-02-camtel-expands-fiber-network-fixed-telephony-techafricanews]].
- **Fragility:** a **WACS submarine-cable incident (23 Oct 2025)** disrupted national internet in a
  post-electoral context — the piece recalls the 2017 Anglophone shutdown. Source:
  [[2025-10-24-lejour-internet-disrupted-postelectoral-wacs-incident]].
- **Private capex around Camtel:** MTN worked with **Camusat/Aktivco** on network expansion
  (MTN pledged **$225m over three years**), and an **AfDB $29m** facility via CCA-Bank part-funds telecom
  equipment imports (noting CMR has "some of the slowest internet speeds on the continent"). Sources:
  [[2025-06-17-mtn-cameroon-camusat-aktivco-network-expansion-itweb]],
  [[2025-12-03-afdb-29m-cca-bank-telecoms-funding-itweb]].

## Regional dependency: Cameroon as Chad's only door

Camtel gatekeeps international bandwidth for landlocked neighbours. **Chad's Sotel renegotiated its debt
to Camtel** (May 2025); a **Cameroon–Chad digital cooperation agreement** (May 2025) promised the
Nana-Mbéré fibre section "within fourteen days" — but by **Feb 2026** it was **not built**, and the
enabling Sotel–Airtel MoU was **suspended by ARCEP Chad on digital-sovereignty grounds**. Cameroon is
Chad's only submarine-cable entry point (TCD internet penetration **13.2%**, end-2025). File the 2025
targets as dated intent, not achievement. Sources:
[[2025-05-16-lejour-sotel-tchad-renegotiates-camtel-debt]],
[[2025-05-16-cameroon-chad-digital-cooperation-agreement-techreviewafrica]],
[[2026-02-09-chad-fast-track-ndjamena-mbere-fiber-link-cameroon-connectingafrica]].

## The regulator starts to bite

**[[art-cameroon]] (ART/TRB, DG Zoo Zame)** moved from nominal to active: **fined Orange & MTN CFA 2.6bn**
(Jul 2025, off 2024 inspections); received **QoS-monitoring platforms** via PATNUC (Jan 2026) and ran
**Douala field inspections** (Apr 2026, incl. IHS Cameroon's CFA 9.3bn 2025 investment); its **2024
Annual Observatory** records **18% sector growth** (revenue CFA 1,022bn, 3.16% of GDP, 31.5m subs,
MFS +146%); and it issued device-certification guidelines (Nov 2025) presaging the IMEI regime. On the
regional side it joined the **[[artac]]** data-residency push (see [[cameroon--infra-store]]), and CAMTEL
is exploring **[[avanti-communications]]** satellite connectivity (Apr 2026). Sources:
[[2025-07-11-art-fines-orange-mtn-4-6-million-telecomreviewafrica]],
[[2026-01-07-trb-receives-qos-monitoring-platforms-patnuc-techafricanews]],
