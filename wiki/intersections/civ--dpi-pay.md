# Côte d'Ivoire × Digital payments & finance

Côte d'Ivoire's payments story has three live threads: a **state payment rail**, **state-fund fintech
equity**, and a **hard UEMOA regional interoperability deadline** — over a maturing mobile-money base.

## The rails

- **Government payment rail.** The Treasury launched **[[tresorpay-tresormoney|TrésorPay–TrésorMoney]]** to
  digitise public-expenditure and G2P/P2G flows (Aug 2025), later extended toward local authorities
  (collectivités). Sources: [[2025-08-15-tresor-public-digitalisation-tresorpay-tresormoney-fratmat]],
  [[2026-03-27-clotchor-secongo-digitalisation-collectivites-fratmat]].
- **e-money licensing.** BCEAO issued a **4th e-money establishment licence** — **[[djogana-pay]]**
  (licence **EME.CI.025/2025**), a citable regulatory primary. Mobile money continued its inflection
  (Moov Money). Sources: [[2025-09-26-monnaie-electronique-4e-etablissement-djogana-pay-fratmat]],
  [[2025-12-06-mobile-money-moov-money-fratmat]].

## State-fund fintech equity, and foreign rails

- **CDC-CI into Green-Pay.** The state investment vehicle **[[cdc-ci|CDC-CI]]** took **equity in the
  fintech [[green-pay|Green-Pay]]** (alongside Orange) to accelerate digital-payment innovation and
  financial inclusion (protocol Dec 2025 / Jan 2026). Sources: [[2026-01-06-cdc-ci-green-pay-fratmat]],
  [[2026-01-15-cdc-ci-green-pay-investment-protocol-techafricanews]].
- **Visa in discussion.** **[[visa|Visa]]** engaged CIV on postal financial services and startup financing
  (May 2026) — a foreign card rail alongside domestic/regional ones. Source:
  [[2026-05-09-visa-digital-partnership-discussions-techreviewafrica]].

## The regional hard deadline & consumer layer

- **UEMOA interoperability mandatory from 30 Jun 2026.** A **[[bceao|BCEAO]]** instruction makes
  **payment interoperability mandatory UEMOA-wide** — a regional regulatory hard deadline binding CIV's
  rails into the West African monetary union. Source:
  [[2026-05-06-uemoa-interoperabilite-paiements-obligatoire-fratmat]].
- **Consumer transparency.** An **OQSF-CI** nationwide financial-service rating system logged **2,234
  evaluations by 31 Mar 2026 (60% satisfactory / 40% not)**; payments growth is framed under
  data-protection "high vigilance." Sources:
  [[2026-04-15-oqsf-ci-financial-service-rating-system-techafricanews]],
  [[2025-03-29-paiements-numeriques-adoption-visa-fratmat]].
