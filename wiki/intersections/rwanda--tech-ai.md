# Rwanda × AI (`tech.ai`)

Rwanda is the frontier case of embedding a commercial frontier-AI vendor across
government — [[anthropic]]'s Claude spanning health, public-sector agencies and
education — alongside a Gates/OpenAI health-AI pilot. In both, the throughline is
an **accountability vacuum**: dependency on foreign AI stacks hardens before any
parliamentary, multilateral or civil-society governance defines its obligations.

## What we know

- **Anthropic as load-bearing government infrastructure — Rwanda (2026-02/03).** A three-year,
  **non-binding** MoU ([[anthropic-rwanda-mou]]) embeds [[anthropic]]'s Claude across Rwanda's
  **health ministry, public-sector agencies and education** — Claude/Claude Code + API credits for
  government developers, plus the **Chidi** learning companion (Claude-based, via a Nov-2025 ALX
  partnership and 2,000 Claude Pro licences) reaching students across **8 African countries**. Tech
  Policy Press ([[paula-ingabire]]: "an important milestone") warns of an **accountability vacuum**
  — no parliamentary, multilateral or civil-society review of commercial AI partnerships exists,
  so dependency hardens before governance defines its obligations (Huawei-4G
  precedent explicit). Domestic contrast: the US **"Department of War"**
  branded Anthropic a "supply-chain risk" and pressed it to drop military guardrails, which Amodei
  refused — the same firm winning African government trust. See [[RWA]], [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]], [[colonialism]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-17 Anthropic is Becoming the Backbone of Rwanda's Government. But Who is Accountable]],
  [[2026-03-04 U.S.-based AI firm Anthropic bags first multi-sector government deal in Africa despite policy pressures at home]].
- **Horizon1000 health-AI pilot (2026-01).** The **$50m** [[gates-foundation]]–[[openai]]
  **[[horizon1000]]** pilot embeds AI in African national health systems, beginning in **Rwanda**
  ([[paula-ingabire]]) and scaling to 1,000 primary-care clinics by 2028 — aimed at SSA's 5.6m
  health-worker gap. See [[RWA]], [[dpi.mis]], [[finance.new]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-22 Gates Foundation and OpenAI Commit $50m to AI-Powered Health Systems in Africa]].
