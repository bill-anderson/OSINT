# Malawi × Connectivity

Malawi's wholesale connectivity was transformed under the World Bank **Digital Foundations Project** — but
distribution to rural areas, schools and affordable retail access remains the binding gap.

## The wholesale gain (Digital Foundations, 2017–2024)

- The **Malawi Digital Foundations Project** brought benefits to **>8.5m** citizens — nearly **7m new internet
  users** — and collapsed the **wholesale bandwidth price from ~$460/Mbit/s to below $10**, connected ~**640
  government sites** and **83,000+** students via MAREN, and (per the World Bank brief) prepared **[[macra-malawi|
  MACRA]]** to license a new operator and enable **LEO satellite** services. Internet users per 100 rose 17.6
  (2016) → 38.3 (2022). Its successor **[[dmap]]** aims to connect 2,000 schools and 500 more offices. Source:
  [[2025-06-23 Digitalizing Malawi to improve access to education, public services, and income opportunities]].

## Operators, 5G and satellite

- **5G rollout.** **[[tnm-malawi|TNM]]** was the **first** operator to launch **5G** (Jul 2025, Lilongwe, with
  **[[huawei]]**); **[[airtel-malawi|Airtel Malawi]]** followed in **Nov 2025** and had **111 5G sites across 10
  districts** by its May-2026 AGM (MK50.8bn FY2025 capex, 58 new sites). Sources:
  [[2025-07-26-tnm-5g-launch-malawi-2063-malawi24]],
  [[2026-05-22-airtel-satellite-connectivity-agm-techafricanews]].
- **Satellite.** Airtel signalled interest in **satellite connectivity** for hard-to-reach areas (resilience +
  rural reach), on top of the LEO enablement work under the Digital Foundations TA. Source:
  [[2026-05-22-airtel-satellite-connectivity-agm-techafricanews]].

## The rural / tower / school gap

- **Towers.** Under **[[dmap]]**, MACRA is procuring **90+ (targeting ~100) new communication towers**, but a
  cited study says Malawi needs **1,000+** for nationwide coverage — the **Universal Service Fund** remains
  under-resourced (DG Daud Suleman). Source:
  [[2026-03-26-dmap-90-telecom-towers-rural-connectivity-techafricanews]].
- **Schools.** UNICEF/Giga's **School Connectivity Landscape Analysis** found **85% of Malawi's 9,000 schools**
  have limited or no internet and **~900 have none** — yet the 2026/27 budget carries **no dedicated
  school-connectivity line** (the World Bank is separately connecting 200+ schools). See [[include.divides]],
  [[capacity.training]]. Source: [[2026-04-17-school-connectivity-unicef-giga-report-malawi24]].

## Policy and the retail-price backlash

- **Spectrum fees cut 20% (announced 2025-06-23).** MACRA cut **radio-frequency spectrum licence fees
  20% across all bands** (revising its Administrative Incentive Pricing model) to lower operator costs,
  attract entrants and free capital for network expansion (MW2063). DG **Daud Suleman** announced it on
  **23 June 2025**. See [[gov.policy]], [[tech.industry]]. Sources:
  [[2025-06-23-macra-spectrum-fees-cut-20pc-malawi24]],
  [[2025-06-25-macra-spectrum-fees-cut-20pc-ecofin]],
  [[2025-06-26-macra-spectrum-fees-cut-20pc-techafricanews]].
  - **The 50% figure is the minister's, not the regulator's** (resolved 2026-07-20). On **2026-07-17**
    ICT minister **Shadric Namalomba** told parliament the reduction was **50%**. Four outlets carry
    that number, but all four report **the same single speech** — one assertion, not four
    corroborations. Against it stand **five** contemporaneous accounts of MACRA's own announcement,
    including direct quotes from the DG, all saying **20%**, and **no source of any kind records a
    second or further reduction** between June 2025 and July 2026. The wiki therefore carries **20%**
    as the figure and treats the minister's 50% as a **misstatement of the 2025 measure**, not a later
    cut. Source for the 50% claim: [[2026-07-18-namalomba-parliament-new-operators-nyasatimes]],
    [[2026-07-20-malawi-seeks-new-telecom-competition]].
  - **The fee schedule's effective date is not established (as of 2026-07-20).** MACRA's site refers to
    "a full table of prices … made available" but publishes no retrievable spectrum fee schedule, and
    no Gazette notice or statutory instrument amending the fees was found. **The wiki holds no fee
    schedule, and no MACRA press release on the cut** — every account of it is journalism. What the
    reduction applies *to*, in **MWK** or USD-pegged terms, is therefore unknown, and the date the
    reduced fees took effect is unknown. Under s.52(2) of the **Communications Act 2016** MACRA may
    review licence fees at any time; nothing requires it to publish the schedule.
  - **Read:** a minister overstating his own government's fee cut by 2.5× while defending a tariff
    rise is not a clerical slip — it is the reduction doing rhetorical work it cannot do
    substantively. The regulator's own number was already the weaker one, and it bought no
    affordability: fees fell 20% in 2025 and retail tariffs rose 22.2–26% a year later. That the
    underlying schedule is unpublished is what makes the overstatement survivable — no one outside
    MACRA can check it.

- **Consumers push back on pricing (2026-07).** An online **petition** (885+ signatures in ~a day; organiser
  Stanley Mombera) urged MACRA to review internet prices, **investigate data-billing/bundle-depletion
  practices**, publish findings and strengthen consumer protection — retail affordability and billing
  transparency as the felt edge of the connectivity gap. See [[include.access]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-macra-internet-price-petition-malawi24]].
- **MACRA orders compensation over a tariff-notice breach (2026-07-17).** Reviewing implementation of
  tariff increases **it had itself approved**, MACRA found **[[airtel-malawi|Airtel Malawi]]** and
  **[[tnm-malawi|TNM]]** in breach of **s.76 of the Communications Act 2016**, which requires approved
  tariff changes to be published in **at least two daily newspapers seven days before** they take effect —
  a transparency provision meant to give consumers time to adjust. Both operators agreed to credit
  customers who bought affected products during the notice window (**26 Jun – 2 Jul 2026**) with bundles
  worth the price difference; MACRA set a **31 July 2026** deadline plus evidence of compliance, ordered
  internal compliance procedures tightened, and said it would monitor delivery. See [[gov.legislate]],
  [[include.access]]. Source: [[2026-07-17-macra-airtel-malawi-tnm-tariff-compensation]].
- **Read:** the price rise stands — what was enforced was **notice**, not affordability. Arriving the same
  day as the 885-signature pricing petition, it is a procedural remedy answering a substantive grievance:
  MACRA polices *how* prices are announced while the *level* remains the regulator's own approved outcome.

## More licences as the answer to a duopoly (2026-07-17)

- **The proposal.** ICT minister **Shadric Namalomba**
  (**[[malawi-ministry-of-ict|Ministry of Information and Communication Technology]]**) told parliament
  on **Friday 17 July 2026** that Malawi is **considering licensing additional mobile network
  operators** to increase competition and improve affordability — answering MP Francis Belekanyama on a
  matter of urgent public importance, in a session convened after the tariff increase. (*ITWeb published
  the account on 2026-07-20 without dating the session; three Malawian outlets published 2026-07-18 and
  place it "on Friday" — the same Friday MACRA issued its compensation order.*) Measures cited alongside
  it: the spectrum-fee reduction (the minister said 50%; **the regulator's figure is 20%** — see above)
  and the **$150m [[dmap|Digital Malawi Acceleration Project]]** (*minister's figure, 2026-07-17 — the
  first USD amount the wiki holds for DMAP*), directed at tower infrastructure in underserved areas.
- **What the minister put on the record about the tariff rise.** Airtel Malawi and TNM applied for an
  average **55%** increase, citing a **~144%** rise in fuel prices and an **8%** rise in electricity
  tariffs; MACRA approved **22.2%** (Airtel) and **26%** (TNM) (all figures the minister's,
  **2026-07-17**). Source: [[2026-07-18-namalomba-parliament-new-operators-nyasatimes]].
- **The licences already issued, and unused.** **Access Communications** licensed **2007**, trading as
  **Zero2**, holds a small subscriber share concentrated in major cities. **Nyasa Mobile** licensed
  **2022** — no commercial launch, no announced date. **Malcel**, licensed as the intended **third major
  operator**, targeted a **late-2023** launch, was delayed by macroeconomic conditions, and has announced
  no new date. All three positions stand **as of 2026-07-20**.
- **Read:** the market has had a third-entrant licence outstanding for **three years** and a fourth for
  nearly **twenty**, and neither converted into a network. That points at **capital and macro
  conditions** — not licence scarcity — as the binding constraint, which a fresh licensing round does not
  touch. It also sits oddly beside the tower gap above: an entrant would need towers Malawi does not
  have, and DMAP's ~90–100 procurement is an order of magnitude short of the cited 1,000+ requirement.
  See [[include.access]], [[gov.policy]]. Source: [[2026-07-20-malawi-seeks-new-telecom-competition]].
