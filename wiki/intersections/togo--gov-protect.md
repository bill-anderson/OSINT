# Togo × Data Protection & Cybersecurity

Togo legislated data protection **early (2019)** and, unusually for the region, is now **operationalising
the authority** — public campaigns, corporate compliance officers, a cyber agency and CERT. The
watch-item is *who holds the levers*: the DPA is security-linked, and gained access to the broadcast
monitoring room.

## The framework

Data protection rests on the **2019 personal-data law** enforced by the **[[ipdcp-togo]]** (Instance de
Protection des Données à Caractère Personnel, president **Colonel Bédiani Belei**), alongside the
**[[ancy-togo]]** (National Cybersecurity Agency), the **CERT.tg** incident-response centre and the
**National Cybersecurity Strategy 2024–2028**. *(The 2019 law's text and the IPDCP's establishing act are
not held — GAP-TGO-001.)* See [[gov.legislate]].

## The regulator activates (2025-2026)

- **Operations launched (Mar 2025).** The IPDCP formally **launched its activities** with a public
  **awareness campaign** (from 1 Apr 2025) to build "a digital space of trust" and ensure ICT does not
  threaten individual liberties and privacy. Source: [[2025-03-29-ipdcp-demarre-activites]].
- **Youth campaign — 3,000 trained (Nov 2025).** IPDCP + **[[ancy-togo|ANCy]]** trained **~3,000 young
  people** in digital hygiene ("protect your info on phone and internet") — framed as **"consolidating
  Togo's digital sovereignty,"** alongside CERT.tg and the 2024–2028 strategy. See [[capacity.literacy]].
  Source: [[2025-11-03-3000-jeunes-formes-reflexes-numeriques]].
- **First corporate DPOs — 32 CPDs certified (Jun 2026).** The IPDCP certified its **first 32
  correspondants à la protection des données (CPD)** — from banking, insurance, telecoms, local
  government, health and transport — to run **processing mapping, registers, DPIAs, breach management and
  awareness** in their organisations. Belei called them "our ambassadors... essential links"; a
  professionalisation-and-certification programme toward international standards. See [[capacity.training]].
  Source: [[2026-06-25-ipdcp-forme-premiers-correspondants]].

## The concern — a security-linked DPA

The IPDCP is **headed by a colonel** and, per the hub, signed an accord giving it **access to
[[haac-togo]]'s broadcast control and monitoring room** — a data-protection authority acquiring reach into
a **broadcast-surveillance facility**, with the source (the IPDCP's own) offering no critical framing. The
"digital sovereignty" language that runs through the campaigns sits alongside this concentration of
security and privacy functions. See [[TGO]] hub, [[gov.protect]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Togo is genuinely ahead — a 2019 law *being enforced*, a professionalising DPO cadre, a
  cyber agency and CERT — the opposite of the "law without institution" pattern common elsewhere. But the
  same maturity concentrates capability in a **security-adjacent** authority (a colonel-led DPA with
  broadcast-monitoring access), so the open question is less capacity than **independence**: whether the
  body protecting citizens' data is sufficiently separated from the state's surveillance apparatus.
