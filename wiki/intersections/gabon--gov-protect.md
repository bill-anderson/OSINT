# Gabon × Data Protection

Gabon's statute binds **from publication, with no grace period** — which puts the CNIE biometric
programme in scope from its first enrolment day — while the same statute contains an **outright
biometric prohibition** whose derogation route has a drafting defect and no traceable
authorisation.

## No grace period

**Art. 219 of loi n°025/2023**: *"Les responsables de traitement sont tenus de se conformer dès
publication de la présente loi."* Obligations bind **from publication, 2023-07-15** — so the
**CNIE programme launched June 2024** was in scope from its first enrolment day. See [[dpi.id]],
[[gabon--dpi-id]]. Source: [[2023-07-15-gabon-loi-025-2023-protection-donnees-personnelles]].

## An unresolved derogation

- **Art. 75 is an outright biometric prohibition**, making the national biometric ID lawful only
  via an **art. 76 derogation**.
- **No published APDPVP authorisation covering the 2024 CNIE build has been found (as of
  2026-07-20).**
- The statute also **mis-cross-references that derogation to art. 74** (children's data) rather
  than art. 75 — a **drafting defect in the operative text**, recorded rather than read as
  intended.

Source for the statutory text throughout:
[[2023-07-15-gabon-loi-025-2023-protection-donnees-personnelles]]. The **absence** of a published
APDPVP authorisation is a search result as of **2026-07-20**, not a document on file.

## Funding: a levy on the population

Gabon's **ordonnance of 2020** levies **1,000 FCFA per person per year**, collected by
controllers, split **40% state / 60% authority** — a self-funding model with an obvious incentive
question. The comparable case is **[[AGO]] Angola's DP 263/25 art. 25**; see the comparison on
[[gov.protect]]. Source: [[2020-03-27-gabon-ordonnance-00002-pr-2020-protection-donnees]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the law is stricter on paper than most of the region — immediate effect, a
  biometric prohibition — and the state's own flagship biometric programme proceeds without a
  traceable authorisation under it. Strictness on the statute book and enforcement against the
  state are separate questions.
