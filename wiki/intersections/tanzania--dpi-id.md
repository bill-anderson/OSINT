# Tanzania × Digital Identity

**[[jamii-namba]]** (rendered "Jamii Number" in English-language accounts; the same programme) is the
national foundational ID run by **[[nida-tanzania]]** under the
**[[ministry-of-home-affairs-tanzania]]**. Adult coverage stood at **81.9% (April 2025)**, corroborated
by two independent routes. What the coverage figure does not show is the **last mile**: **300,000+
processed cards uncollected (January 2026)**, an application path that still requires a physical
**Local Chairman's stamp**, and an infant-registration pilot that **ran roughly six months ahead of its
enabling Bill**. Civil registration proper sits with **[[rita-tanzania]]** (see
[[tanzania--dpi-registry]]); independent assurance with **[[nao-tanzania]]**.

## Coverage: two independent routes that agree

Worth stating plainly — **a conference panel and a parliamentary budget speech, two independent
routes, agree closely at ~81–82% adult coverage**.

**Route 1 — [[id4africa]] 2025 (June 2025).** **[[edson-guyai]]**, NIDA Director of ID Management,
reported **over 25 million registered** against **over 31.5 million aged 18+ (2022 census)** → **~81%**;
**over 21.2m IDs personalised**; **more than 20.1m cards issued**; integration with **over 124
institutions**; ID verification generating **~$45.4 million** at **$0.18 per transaction**; and **SIM
registration boosting ID registration by ~25%**. Source:
[[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].

**Route 2 — parliament (2025-05-27).** Home Affairs Minister **Innocent Bashungwa** (DODOMA) presented
a **2.06tri/-** ministry budget for FY2025/26, up from **1.71tri/-**. NIDA registered **844,994** people
**July 2024–April 2025**, total **over 25.8 million = 81.9%** of the **31,582,976** aged 18+ (2022
census); **757,189** NINs generated in the period, total **21,514,741 = 83.2%** of those registered.
**461.4m/-** allocated to resolve **4,342,926** registered-but-no-NIN applications; **5.81bn/-** to
register **2,389,290** more in FY2025/26. Source: [[2025-05-27 Home Affairs unveils 2.06tri- budget]].

The **4,342,926 registered-but-no-NIN** figure (April 2025) is the gap between the two coverage
numbers made explicit: registration is not issuance, and issuance is not collection.

## Infant registration: the pilot ran ahead of the Bill

**2025-06-17 — the design choice.** [[edson-guyai]] told [[id4africa]] 2025 that a pilot was **expected
July 2025** with **[[unicef]]** support in selected regions, for **children below five**: *"Upon birth, a
child will receive both a birth certificate and a National ID number and document."* **Because no
biometric solution works for under-fives, NIDA opted for the interim measure of linking the child's
identity to the mother's National ID** — the most consequential design choice in this material. A
**$4.5m** minors-enrolment budget is described by the article as *seemingly separate* from the UNICEF
pilot; **that hedge is preserved**. Source:
[[2025-06-17 Tanzania launching infant biometric registration pilot with UNICEF support]].

**2026-01-04 — the legislation still pending.** NIDA DG **[[james-kaji]]** said the
**[[tanzania-infant-registration-bill]]** was **"preparing to be tabled in the next Parliament"** — if
passed, children would be registered from birth, assigned identification numbers, and issued national
IDs on the same numbers at 18. **The clip's headline says "rolls out"; the body says pending — as of
2026-01 the enabling legislation was not law.** Source:
[[2026-01-04 Tanzania rolls out infant registration for stronger citizen ID]].

Read together, **the operational pilot ran roughly six months ahead of its enabling legislation**. This
is a **finding, not a contradiction** — the two accounts are consistent; one describes an executive
pilot, the other the statute that would give it a legal basis.

## The uncollected cards

In the same January 2026 remarks, [[james-kaji]] urged **more than 300,000 citizens whose ID cards have
been processed to collect them** from district offices, noting the delays cost the government and deny
citizens use of the documents. Source:
[[2026-01-04 Tanzania rolls out infant registration for stronger citizen ID]]. [[include.access]]

## Phase II: iris and facial (2026-04-22)

Deputy Home Affairs Minister **Ayoub Mohamed Mahmoud** told parliament [[nida-tanzania]] had published
tenders to add **iris and facial recognition** to [[jamii-namba]]. Phase II also funds a NIDA
headquarters in Dodoma, an ICT-maintenance centre and registration offices in **31 districts** (~$14m
allocated FY2025/26 for Micheweni). Financed by a **~$70m Export-Import Bank of Korea** loan within the
[[world-bank]]-funded **Digital Tanzania Project** (since 2021); Phase I built the core data centre and
enrolment offices. Source:
[[2026-04-22 Tanzania seeks biometrics contractors for Phase II of national digital ID project]].
[[finance.new]]

## The hybrid process, and the Local Chairman's stamp

*(2025-01-01, `date_source: proxy`, year precision — the page carries no date.)*
**[[baker-mckenzie]]** guidance gives the structural process: eligibility is **citizens aged 18+ and
legal residents with valid permits**, and **no National ID is obtainable without a valid residence
permit**. The process is **hybrid, not digital-end-to-end**: online form on the NIDA portal → **local
government office for the official stamp and signature of the Local Chairman** → NIDA District Office →
biometric appointment. **That stamp is a concrete [[include.access]] friction point appearing in no
official account of the programme.**

*Baseline caution: this guidance's **fingerprints/photo/signature** modality set is the
**pre-Phase-II baseline** and must not be read as current-state modalities against the 2026-04
iris/facial additions.* Source:
[[2025-01-01 Tanzania - National Identification Card guidance (Baker McKenzie)]]. [[dpi.govtech]]

## Independent assurance: the state audits its own ID programme (2021-03)

The **Controller and Auditor General** (**Charles E. Kichere**, [[nao-tanzania]]) conducted a
**value-for-money audit of NIDA's registration and issuance of national ID cards** under **s.28 of the
Public Audit Act No. 11 of 2008**, submitted to the President and through her to Parliament; audited
entities were [[nida-tanzania]] and the [[ministry-of-home-affairs-tanzania]]. **An independent state
audit is a materially different kind of evidence from NIDA's own coverage claims — weigh it
accordingly.** Source:
[[2021-03-01 Performance Audit - Registration and Issuance of National ID Cards by NIDA (NAO Tanzania)]].
[[gov.policy]]

## ID at the mobile-money edge

*(2026-01-01, fieldwork 2022; CHI '26.)* A CMU-Africa 72-interview study (Kenya + Tanzania) finds
Tanzania's **biometric-fingerprint SIM verification** blocks ID-borrowing (unlike Kenya), yet
ID-required mobile money still pushes many onto agent-registered SIMs; **more than 1 in 3**
interviewees failed ID registration on the first attempt. Source:
[[2026-01-01 My Money, Your Name Challenges and Workarounds in ID-Required Mobile Money in East Africa]].
