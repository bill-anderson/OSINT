# Uganda × Artificial Intelligence

Uganda's AI posture is **strategy-and-sovereignty-forward**: a national AI policy/strategy in the making, a
locally-hosted research cloud built to escape foreign compute dependence, and a minister who frames AI as
"Africa's industrial revolution" and the continent's shot at more than **1% of global AI wealth**. The
consistent worry — echoed by civil society — is that ambition and safeguards, and homegrown vs imported
capacity, are not yet in balance.

## The policy machinery

- **Kigali signal (Apr 2025).** ICT State Minister **Joyce Nabbosa Ssebugwawo** told the Global AI Summit on
  Africa (Kigali) that Uganda was **"in the final stages" of a National AI Strategy** aligned to the Digital
  Transformation Roadmap, urging **inclusive, ethical, equitable** AI and **harmonised African frameworks**.
  Source: [[2025-04-05-uganda-calls-inclusive-ai-policies-kigali-summit]].
- **First national AI policy (Jun 2025).** Ministry of ICT PS **Dr Aminah Zawedde** confirmed Uganda's
  **first-ever AI policy** as an NDP4 priority — "not only for government but for the entire country" —
  covering innovation, **data governance**, cyber and ethics, driven by the data volumes from national IDs
  and smart agriculture. Source: [[2025-06-18-uganda-to-develop-national-ai-policy]].
- **Strategy and secretariat (Mar 2026).** A stakeholder engagement advanced the **national AI strategy**;
  **Ambrose Ruyooka** heads a **ministry AI Secretariat** laying the groundwork for a national AI ecosystem
  (agriculture, health diagnostics, education, public admin), after readiness assessments and international-
  partner engagement on rights/privacy governance. See [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12-govt-crafts-national-ai-strategy]].
- *(Rights-by-design counterweight, from the hub: **[[cipesa]]** urged mandatory HRIAs, safeguards on the
  algorithmic profiling already in digital lending, local datasets and a national AI research fund —
  warning Uganda risks becoming a "raw data supplier to foreign tech." See [[UGA]].)*

## Compute and data sovereignty

- **National AI Research Cloud (Mar 2026).** Launched at **[[makerere-university|Makerere]]** (CEDAT +
  CoCIS) with **[[renu]]** — **local compute and storage for training AI models**, explicitly to replace
  expensive foreign clouds (Amazon/Google) that "raised data-sovereignty concerns" (Dr Joyce Nakatumba
  Nabende: *"data is the new gold"*). Minister **Monica Musenero**: AI **"has arrived like a tsunami… take
  our destiny into our own hands."** The most concrete sovereignty move in the AI material. See
  [[infra.store]], [[capacity.research]]. Source: [[2026-03-16-musenero-launches-national-ai-research-cloud]].

## Skills and inclusion

- **Inclusive AI curriculum (Nov 2025).** UICT is developing an **inclusive AI curriculum** for localised,
  culturally-relevant content and **disability-inclusive** personalised learning, alongside a goal of
  **functional innovation hubs in every region by 2027** (Pillar 5 of the Digital Transformation Roadmap) —
  framed with the warning that within five years the AI-illiterate may struggle to find work. See
  [[capacity.literacy]]. Source: [[2025-11-18-govt-inclusive-ai-curriculum-learners]].

## Foreign partnerships — and their direction

- **UN Health Foundation + China (Apr 2026).** The Ministry of ICT signed an MoU with the **UN Health
  Industry Foundation** and the Prince Kimbugwe Foundation for **AI labs, youth skills and China exchange
  programmes on smart agriculture** — Chinese chairman Chunxiao Huang projecting **50,000 jobs**, drones and
  Chinese agri-investment, toward a "$500b economy by 2040." A [[geopol.china]] tilt in the AI-skills layer.
  Source: [[2026-04-01-ict-ministry-mou-un-health-china-ai-skills]].

## The framing: consumers vs contributors

- **"Africa's industrial revolution" (May 2026).** At Gitex Kenya's AI Everything Summit, **Musenero**
  argued Africa has **"only consumed AI rather than captured its economic opportunity"** and must treat it
  as "the new soil in which we are cultivating our wealth" — citing an African AI market of **$4.5bn (2025)
  → $16.5bn (2030)** (Mastercard) while Africa retains **~1% of global AI wealth**. The [[colonialism]] read
  named by the minister herself. Source: [[2026-05-20-musenero-africa-must-embrace-ai-economic-revolution]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty` / `colonialism`: Uganda says the right things and has begun to build the right thing — a
  national research cloud that keeps training data in-country is a real answer to the "raw data supplier"
  fear, not just rhetoric. But the gaps the hub already flags persist: the **strategy is still draft** while
  algorithmic profiling is *already live* in mobile lending (ungoverned — see [[uganda--dpi-pay]]), the
  **skills pipeline leans on foreign partners** (China, UN foundation) even as the sovereignty language
  sharpens, and CIPESA's warning about becoming a data supplier sits unresolved. The minister's own "1% of
  AI wealth" is the honest measure of the distance between the ambition and the base — matched by the
  locally-trained agricultural models ([[UGA]] hub, NASA-Harvest) that show what "contributor" would
  actually require.
