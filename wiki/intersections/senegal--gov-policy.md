# Senegal × The New Deal Technologique

The **[[new-deal-technologique]]** (launched 24 Feb 2025 by President Faye) is Senegal's organising
digital strategy under **Vision Sénégal 2050**. This page holds its structure, financing, targets and
first-year delivery; the entity page is the short profile.

## Four pillars

Per SENUM's official explainer, the strategy rests on four pillars:

1. **Digital sovereignty** — reduce dependence on foreign technology, especially in the sensitive
   domain of **data processing** (much of Senegal's data is currently stored abroad).
2. **Digitisation of administration & public services** — a **90% dematerialisation** target by 2034
   (cadastre, civil status, taxes online via a single digital identity).
3. **Development of the digital economy.**
4. **Positioning Senegal as a leading African digital hub.**

Sources: [[2025-03-02-senum-explains-new-deal-concepts]], [[2025-02-24-sall-interview-digital-society]].

## Financing — the ATN (1,105bn FCFA)

The **Agenda de Transformation Numérique (ATN)** carries a **1,105bn FCFA** investment across **12
priority programmes (2025–2034)**: **950bn identified budget**, **150bn private financing available**,
and a **155bn FCFA gap** still to be financed. Source: [[2025-02-24-new-deal-1105-billion-investment]].

## Targets to 2034

**95% internet connectivity** at low cost; **5,000 certified digital experts trained per year**;
**150,000 direct + 200,000 indirect jobs**; and a **15% contribution to GDP**. Source:
[[2025-02-24-new-deal-2034-indicators]].

**PAENS / World Bank (2026).** The **Digital Economy Acceleration Project (PAENS)** was aligned to the 2026
agenda: an integrated public financial-management system (**SIGIF**), a **sovereign government cloud**,
cybersecurity and AI use cases, plus a white-zones connectivity tender (groundnut basin, Lower Casamance).
The 2026 digital budget is **CFA81.06bn (+38.7%)**, ~60% investment; the **World Bank committed CFA95.05bn**.
Source: [[2026-02-17-wearetech-world-bank-paens]].

## The launch and its framing (Feb 2025)

- President Faye framed international strategy around **"three circles of alliances"**: reinforced
  **South-South** cooperation for an integrated regional digital market; **deals with tech giants** to
  implant **R&D centres** and **technology transfer in electronic-component production**; and
  **academic/technical partnerships** building AI/advanced-tech excellence centres. Source:
  [[2025-02-24-new-deal-launch-live-coverage]].
- A **20-member National Council** was named (AI specialist Moustapha Cissé, InTouch DG Oumar Cissé,
  etc.). Source: [[2025-02-24-new-deal-council-members]].
- Pre-launch analysis flagged the binding constraints: a **storage/compute-infrastructure deficit**, a
  **rural–urban divide** (grey zones while Dakar nears 5G), and the need for a **territorialised** (equity-
  of-place) approach and **land digitisation**. Source: [[2025-02-15-new-deal-stakes-and-challenges]].

## One year on (Mar 2026)

At the first anniversary, **PM Ousmane Sonko** launched the **12 structuring projects** (~1,100bn FCFA,
2025–2034), framing it as "a rupture, profound and irreversible," and created:

- **GouvNum** — a Digital Governance Committee to coordinate state digital projects and stop siloed,
  duplicative ministry initiatives; plus the installed National Digital Council. Source:
  [[2026-03-24-lesoleil-sonko-projets-structurants]].
- **e-Senegal** — the citizen single-window (guichet unique), now **operational**: passport
  applications, business creation, police-clearance (casier judiciaire) and nationality certificates
  with online tracking; unveiled alongside a **national sovereignty cloud**, a **unified government
  digital-payment system** (taxes/customs) and a consular-services platform. Targets restated as **95%
  connectivity + 90% dematerialisation by 2030**. Sources:
  [[2026-03-27-biometricupdate-e-senegal-portal]], [[2026-03-19-lesoleil-new-deal-un-an-apres]].
- Other first-year deliverables: the **National Interoperability Platform** (with a **"Once Only"**
  principle), **Sengec** electronic mail/correspondence management piloted across ministries, and
  **Startup Act** operationalisation (labelling process launched). Source:
  [[2026-03-19-lesoleil-new-deal-un-an-apres]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the New Deal makes digital sovereignty the *first* pillar and names the dependency
  explicitly — data stored abroad, foreign-tech reliance, missing compute. The design answer (onshore
  cloud, R&D-transfer deals with the same tech giants) is the recurring African tension: sovereignty
  pursued through partnerships with the very providers it seeks independence from.
