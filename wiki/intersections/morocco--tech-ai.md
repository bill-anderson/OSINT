# Morocco × AI

Morocco is building an **AI agenda framed as sovereignty** — homegrown "AI Made in Morocco" institutes and
sovereign LLMs, a framework law binding AI to data governance and digital identity, and public-private
skills partnerships — while the enabling law and sectoral safeguards are still catching up.

## What we know

- **Two legislative tracks (2025-26).** [[gov.legislate]] A parliamentary **AI-regulation bill** (Haraki
  group, **17 articles** — definitions, scope, liability, a national follow-up commission, ties to
  cybersecurity/data-protection/IP law) reached the House of Representatives in **Jan 2025** — the
  earliest-dated Moroccan AI-legislation move. Separately, the government's **[[digital-x0-law|"Digital
  X.0" framework law]]** (presented by Minister Seghrouchni, under GS-of-Government review, Nov 2025)
  binds **data governance, sector-specific digital identity and interoperability** on **traceable
  consent**, under Law 09-08. Sources: [[2025-01-09-hespress-morocco-ai-legislative-initiative]],
  [[2025-11-04-wearetech-morocco-landmark-law-ai-data-identity]].
- **Sovereign responsible-AI platform (2025-09).** [[gov.protect]] [[sovereignty]] The Ministry and the
  **[[cndp-morocco|CNDP]]** agreed to build a **national responsible-AI platform** on **sovereign Moroccan
  LLMs** (Moroccan language/culture/law/identity) — rights-respecting generative AI. Source:
  [[2025-09-11-en-hespress-morocco-responsible-ai-platform-deal]].
- **"AI Made in Morocco" / JAZARI (2026).** [[gov.policy]] The **[[jazari-institutes|JAZARI Institutes /
  "AI Made in Morocco"]]** programme (Fez seminar; Industry X.0/health) and an MTNRA–**[[orange|Orange
  Morocco]]** JAZARI-ROOT MoU (GenZ AI Summit) push a homegrown ecosystem — AI as sovereignty,
  competitiveness and human development. Sources: [[2026-03-11-techafricanews-ai-made-in-morocco-industry-x0]],
  [[2026-06-05-techafricanews-morocco-orange-ai-partnership]].
- **Skills as the binding constraint — MTNRA–Capgemini MoU (signed 2026-07-17).** [[capacity.training]]
  [[finance.mou]] The **[[ministry-of-digital-transition-morocco|MTNRA]]** signed an MoU with
  **[[capgemini]] Maroc** in Rabat (minister **Amal El Fallah Seghrouchni** / **Karine Brunet**) for AI
  skills-building and acculturation across **administrations, universities and research centres**,
  support to **[[jazari-institutes|Jazari]]**-backed start-ups, and public-service AI use cases. **No
  value, term or target disclosed.** It is the clearest statement yet that the government reads
  **skills availability**, not technology or law, as what limits [[digital-morocco-2030]] — whose
  stated targets are **240,000 digital jobs by 2030** and **3,000 start-ups** — and that the chosen
  instrument is a foreign integrator training the buyer. Reported government estimate: AI worth **>$10bn
  additional GDP** *(as reported 2026-07-20; a **$11bn** variant appears in the same publisher's
  related-headline; unresolved, no primary held)*. Source: [[2026-07-20-maroc-capgemini-ia-memorandum]].
- **Startup enablement — AI Garden (2026-04).** [[tech.innovate]] **Orange Maroc + Technopark Maroc (MITC)**
  launched the **"AI Garden"** programme (GITEX 2026) — cloud, connectivity, cybersecurity and use-cases
  for AI startups. Source: [[2026-04-09-techafricanews-orange-maroc-technopark-ai]].
- **Sectoral reality check — AI in health (2025-02).** [[dpi.mis]] [[infra.cybersec]] As Morocco embeds AI
  in hospitals, experts flag **weak medical-records security** and a workforce gap (**48,000 doctors /
  100,000 nurses** short) — AI as staffing relief and rural-access tool, but needing regulation,
  telemedicine integration and data-security fixes. Source:
  [[2025-02-20-en-hespress-morocco-ai-healthcare-cybersecurity]].
