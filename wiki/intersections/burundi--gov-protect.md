# Burundi × Data Protection

Burundi's first data-protection law was adopted **explicitly to enable the biometric ID**, not to
check it — the sequencing is stated by the government itself, and it is the whole story of this
page.

## The law (2026-01)

The National Assembly **unanimously adopted** Burundi's first
**[[burundi-data-protection-law-2026|personal data-protection law]]** on **15 January 2026**
(Ministry of Interior):

- **Eight chapters**
- An **independent authority**
- **Criminal penalties**
- **Malabo-aligned**

The framing is explicit: it was **sequenced ahead of the biometric ID** rather than as an
independent check — *"we first adopted legislation… before developing the platform."* See
[[dpi.id]], [[burundi--dpi-id]]. Source:
[[2026-01-16-burundi-personal-data-protection-law-techafricanews]].

## The data strategy it pairs with (2025-11)

A **National Data Governance Strategy (SNGD)** — seven pillars, UNECA-supported,
**[[au-data-policy-framework]]**-aligned, framing data as a **public good**. See [[gov.policy]].
Source: [[2025-11-14-burundi-adopts-national-data-governance-strategy]].

## What is untested

Whether a **brand-new authority can check a state ID drive in a dominant-party system** is
untested. Regional peers rate Burundi's data-protection regime **"nascent"**.

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: legislating protection *before* the platform is the right order on paper, and
  Burundi says so — but the stated purpose is to make the ID lawful, which is enabling rather than
  constraining. The test is the first time the new authority is asked to say no to the register.
