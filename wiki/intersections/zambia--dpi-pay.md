# Zambia × Digital Payments

Zambia's digital-payments push runs on two complementary strands: a government–industry compact with the Payments Association of Zambia to accelerate digital financial services and cut cash dependency, and the [[dzap]] Component 2 / ZamPay work building interoperable payment rails for financial inclusion.

## What we know

- **[[ZMB]] Zambia — PAYZ compact for DFS (2026-02).** The government and the **Payments
  Association of Zambia ([[payz]])** agreed to accelerate digital financial services and cut
  cash dependency, with quarterly regulatory coordination among the Ministry of Technology &
  Science, [[bank-of-zambia]] and [[zicta]]; President Hichilema hosted PAYZ at State House and
  ZedMobile was named the fourth MNO. Complements the [[dzap]] ZamPay strand. See
  [[include.access]], [[gov.policy]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-27 Zambia partners with PAYZ to boost financial inclusion]].

- **[[ZMB]] Zambia (2025).** Digital financial services are a pillar of [[dzap]] Component 2:
  **ZamPay** for interoperable digital payments, alongside a mature mobile money market (MTN
  Mobile Money, Airtel Money) used to deliver emergency cash transfers during climate shocks —
  a lever for financial inclusion and for reaching women and remote communities. Links to
  [[dpi.id]] (authentication) and [[include.divides]].

## Government as collector, and local fintech building on the rail (2026)

- **The state's own payment rail collects at scale.** The **Government Service Bus + Electronic Payment
  Gateway + [[zamportal|ZamPortal]] + [[zampay|ZamPay]]** ecosystem has collected **>K8.8bn (~$488m)** while
  onboarding **400+ services** (passports, rent, road tax), payable by bank/card/mobile money/cash, extended
  to **8 councils (of 116)** — the Ministry of Finance's Accountant General framing digital service delivery
  as a **revenue-modernisation** win. This is the payments counterpart to the govtech build on
  [[zambia--dpi-govtech]]. Source: [[2026-07-10-digital-services-strengthen-zambia-finances]].
- **Local fintech + global networks.** **[[zoyk|Zoyk]]** (Zambian fintech) partnered **[[visa]]** to bridge
  local rails and global payment networks for **SADC cross-border** trade; **[[dot-com-zambia|Dot Com
  Zambia]]** — a locally-listed fintech (LuSE; eToll/eLevy) — pitched building on the GSB and a digital-ID
  **"single source of truth"** for KYC and social protection (drawing on Ghana; regional ties with Mauritius/
  Ghana). See [[finance.mou]], [[finance.new]], [[dpi.id]]. Sources: [[2026-06-03-zoyk-visa-digital-payments-partnership]],
  [[2026-05-14-zambia-accelerate-digital-economy-ppp]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: Zambia's payments story mirrors its govtech one — a **state-run collection rail**
  (GSB/ZamPortal/ZamPay) that **local fintechs plug into** (Zoyk, Dot Com Zambia) while global networks (Visa)
  sit behind them, and the whole is explicitly tied to a national digital-ID "single source of truth." It is
  a coherent, locally-anchored design; the constraint, as everywhere in the Zambia file, is reach —
  cash-dependency, the 4th-MNO inclusion push (PAYZ), and thin connectivity mean the rail collects
  efficiently from those already on it well before it reaches the unbanked majority.
