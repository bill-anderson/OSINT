# São Tomé and Príncipe × E-Government & Interoperability

STP's e-government push is anchored by a **single citizen portal** and a **trade single-window**, sitting
on the **interoperability framework** (Law 1/2024) and the WB-built **DEX** data-exchange layer (see
[[sao-tome--dpi-id]]) — the "state closer to the citizen" made concrete for a micro-state.

## The reform frame

- **New administrative-reform strategy (2025).** Justice Minister Vera Cravid presented a **newly
  approved public-administration reform strategy** with **electronic governance as its essential pillar**
  — to rationalise processes, cut bureaucracy and costs, and promote **equity of access** to public
  services (African Public Administration Week). See [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2025-06-18-ministra-justica-digitalizacao-setor-publico]].

## The Citizen Portal

- **Portal do Cidadão — beta (Mar 2026).** The **[[inic-sao-tome|INIC]]** (Institute for Innovation and
  Knowledge) launched the **single Portal do Cidadão** (beta) at its 18th anniversary — "a structuring
  step toward a more modern, efficient and citizen-centred State" (PM Américo Ramos), with more efficient
  **inter-institutional communication**. Complements the WB/Co-Develop **Citizen Portal** (see [[STP]]
  hub) from a distinct source/angle. Source: [[2026-03-28-inic-lanca-portal-do-cidadao]].

## Trade single-window + ASYFIVE

- **Digitising external trade (2026).** With **UNDP** technical/financial support, STP is deploying an
  integrated **Port Single Window + External-Trade Single Window + ASYFIVE** — integrating **22 public
  and private institutions** (ENAPOR, ENASA, Maritime-Port Institute, Central Bank, Treasury, investment
  agency, etc.) into one traceable digital platform to cut clearance time and **boost revenue**. **ASYFIVE**
  (the UN successor to **ASYCUDA/CUDA**, used in 103+ countries) would make STP the **first African country**
  to deploy it. See [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-25-salto-tecnologico-comercio-externo-janela-unica-asyfive]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: for a ~241k-person state, e-government is about **doing more with a shared spine** — one
  citizen portal, one interoperability layer, one customs window — rather than many siloed systems. The
  reliance on UNDP/World Bank finance and international consultants is heavy, but the architecture
  (Law 1/2024 interoperability + the open-standards DEX) keeps the integration logic national.
