# Guinea × Digital Identity

Guinea is (re)building its identity stack from a very weak base — only **45-60%** of citizens held
birth certificates and **<2%** of marriages/deaths were registered (2023) — after its World Bank
**[[wuri-programme|WURI]]** foundational-ID project **closed early in 2024** with only ~8,000 IDs
issued. The pivot is toward a **domestic, sovereign** stack: a biometric civil registry and national
ID on **[[mosip]] + [[tech5]]** ([[oneci-guinea]]), the **RAVEC** administrative census, and a
functional-ID beachhead in the **[[fugas]]** biometric civil-service payroll (whose audit exposed
~$26.9m of ghost workers and prompted a call for a unique personal ID number). The pieces are not yet
integrated — civil registry, national ID, the [[rsu-guinea]] social registry and functional payroll
IDs are advancing on parallel tracks.

## What we know

- **[[GIN]] Biometric civil registry + national ID, MOSIP/TECH5 (2026-03).** [[dpi.registry]] The
  [[oneci-guinea|ONECI]] began issuing **biometric birth certificates** and enrolling the biometric
  national ID in Sanoyah commune ("one citizen, one identity"; phase 1: 2,000 certificates). Base
  documents: prior birth certificate, court supplementary judgment, or a RAVEC document; the birth
  certificate is the **foundational document** for the national ID. Source:
  [[2026-03-04 Guinea Launches Biometric Birth Certificates in Sanoyah in Civil Registry Modernization Push]].
- **[[GIN]] WURI closed early → domestic path (2025-05).** [[dpi.id]] The WB ISR records **WURI-Guinea
  closed 28 June 2024** (two years early; ~8,000 UINs), institutional arrangements unconsolidated, and
  cautions that Guinea's Oct-2023 ID/civil-registry laws "did not fully reflect the principles of
  identification for sustainable development." Côte d'Ivoire (WURI Phase 1 partner) advanced by contrast.
  Source: [[2025-05-02 WURI Implementation Status Report - Cote d'Ivoire and Guinea (P161329 Seq 11)]].
- **[[GIN]] FUGAS — functional ID via biometric payroll (2025-11).** [[dpi.govtech]] Of ~277,000
  registered civil servants, only ~130,000 confirmed biometrically — **233bn GNF (~$26.9m)** saved; PM
  Bah pressed that FUGAS "must integrate a unique personal identification number." Source:
  [[2025-11-30 Guinea Claims $26.9M Saved Since 2024 with Biometric Audit of Public Workforce (FUGAS)]].
- **[[GIN]] Civil-registry baseline (Enabel/Belgium, 2023) and social registry (RSU).** [[include.access]]
  An EU/Enabel pilot digitised the registry in 10 communes (105,000+ records; DXC Technology), and the
  **[[rsu-guinea|RSU]]** (Decree 2019) is the unified social registry awaiting a unique identifier.
  Sources: [[2023-08-03 The modernisation of Guinea's civil registry inspired by Belgium's experience (Citizenship Rights in Africa)]],
  [[2025-01-01 Registre Social Unifie (RSU) presentation - Guinea]].
