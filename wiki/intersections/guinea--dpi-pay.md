# Guinea × Digital Payments

Guinea is trying to engineer its way out of a **structural cash-preference problem** — nearly 94% of
banknotes never return to the banking system, only ~23% of adults are banked, and a 2025 liquidity
crisis had to be patched with air-freighted cash. The **[[bcrg|central bank]]**'s answer is a
coordinated shift to electronic money built on **open, state-controlled** rails: a Mojaloop-based
instant payment system (SPI), an M-Pesa-style merchant system and the national switch, plus **[[tresor-pay]]**
for public payments — a sovereignty-minded, PAPSS-ready design in one of West Africa's least-banked
markets.

## What we know

- **[[GIN]] Instant Payment System (SPI) on Mojaloop (2025-12-18).** [[sovereignty]] [[gov.regional]]
  The BCRG opened work on a national **instant, interoperable payment system** on **[[mojaloop]]** —
  chosen to limit technological dependence and favour local fintech innovation — for real-time transfers
  across banks, wallets and instruments, and to carry government payments; designed to integrate with
  **[[papss]]**/ECOWAS. Context: 23% banked (from 7% a decade ago); mobile money +8.6% to 46,795bn GNF
  (Q2 2024); WAEMU's BCEAO PI-SPI as a regional model. Sources:
  [[2025-12-22 Guinea central bank starts work on instant payment system]],
  [[2025-12-21 Guinea readies instant payment system to speed transactions and cut cash use]].
- **[[GIN]] Anti-cash strategy + 11 EMIs (2025-11-04).** [[include.access]] The BCRG presented an
  M-Pesa-inspired merchant payment system (World Bank-supported), plans to operationalise the national
  switch, and moves to ban cash in public administration — with **11 licensed EMIs** (up from 6 in
  2024) and ~26% holding an e-money account. Source:
  [[2025-11-07 Guinea Plans Mobile Payment System to Ease Cash Shortage]].
- **[[GIN]] Trésor Pay — public-payments gateway (2025-12-22).** [[dpi.govtech]] A treasury platform
  to pay state administrative fees by mobile, improving revenue mobilisation and traceability in a
  ~30%-financially-included economy. Source:
  [[2025-12-23 Guinea rolls out Tresor Pay as digital gateway for public payments]].

## 2026 fintech wave

Around the state's instant-payment build, a burst of **private e-money and cross-border** entrants arrived in
2026: **[[coris-money-guinea|CORIS Money]]** (bank-issued, Coris Bank, Feb 2026), **[[ks-wallet-guinea|KS-Wallet]]**
(BGB Solutions, non-bank, Apr 2026), **Orange Money "Accès à ma banque"** wallet↔bank interoperability with
**BNG** (Mar 2026), and **[[cauridor|Cauridor]]** — a **Guinea-founded cross-border payments infrastructure**
now in **36 countries** (ex-Flutterwave COO). **Visa** opened talks with the Finance Ministry toward a
central-bank MoU to shift Guinea off cash (Apr 2026). Sources:
[[2026-02-19-techafricanews-coris-money-launch]], [[2026-04-01-mediaguinee-bgb-solutions-ks-wallet-emoney]],
[[2026-03-13-mediaguinee-orange-money-bng-acces-a-ma-banque]], [[2026-04-14-techcabal-cauridor-appoints-coo]],
[[2026-04-12-techreviewafrica-visa-engages-guinea-digital-payments]].
