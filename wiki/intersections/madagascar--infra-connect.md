# Madagascar × Connectivity

Madagascar — a large, low-penetration island (**~20% internet, end-2025**) — is closing its divide on
three tracks: **World Bank-funded inclusion** ([[decim-madagascar|DECIM]]), **terrestrial backbone**
(fibre, subsea cable) and a fast-moving **satellite** frontier (Starlink Direct-to-Cell, Eutelsat OneWeb).

## What we know

- **DECIM + Starlink for enclaved zones (2025-03).** [[digital.rural]] Under **[[decim-madagascar|DECIM]]**
  (World Bank), **[[starlink]]** would connect **10 enclaved zones** (schools, health centres, agri-hubs)
  via a CSR arrangement (special tariffs, government-bought kits) — discussed by Minister Delmotte with
  SpaceX in Washington. Source: [[2025-03-20-starlink-decim-dix-zones-enclavees-2424]].
- **EIB $100m to AXIAN for 4G/5G (2025-04).** [[finance.new]] The EIB (Global Gateway) lent **$100m to
  [[axian-telecom]]** for 4G/5G across Madagascar and Tanzania (**$40m to Madagascar**), under the **Yas**
  brand. Source: [[2025-04-11-eib-100m-axian-telecom-4g-5g-techafricanews]].
- **DARE1 subsea cable extension (2025-09).** [[infra.connect]] The Djibouti **[[dare1-cable|DARE1]]**
  system is extending to **Mahajanga and Toliara** (3,200–3,500 km leg via Tanzania/Mozambique to South
  Africa; work from 2026, RFS **2028**) — regional resilience after the May-2024 EASSy/SEACOM cuts.
  Source: [[2025-09-10-dare1-cable-extension-madagascar-2424]].
- **Fibre backbone — Orange Tana–Mahajanga (2025-12).** [[infra.connect]] **[[orange|Orange Madagascar]]**
  inaugurated a **558 km** Antananarivo–Mahajanga fibre backbone (2,500 people, 8 months) — the country's
  third fibre operator (after Yas, Blueline) building national transmission resilience. Source:
  [[2025-12-05-orange-fibre-tana-mahajanga-2424]].
- **Tariff-cut deal & tax standoff (2025-12).** [[include.access]] [[gov.policy]] Operators agreed to
  **gradually cut mobile-internet prices** (Minister Andriamampiadana), with the state offering
  **excise-duty relief** conditioned on reinvestment in quality — resolving a state-operator standoff.
  Source: [[2025-12-02-connectivite-baisse-tarifs-internet-lexpress]].
- **Satellite frontier — Direct-to-Cell (2026).** [[data.satellite]] [[sovereignty]] SpaceX filed an FCC
  notice (13 May) for **Direct-to-Cell (SCS)** on **[[airtel-madagascar|Airtel]]**'s spectrum, then ran
  Madagascar's **first Direct-to-Cell trial** (Sadabe, 11 Jul) — the **3rd African country** (after
  Kenya, Uganda), first satellite WhatsApp video call; commercial launch pending **ARTEC**. Separately,
  Airtel + **[[eutelsat|Eutelsat OneWeb]]** deepened LEO rural broadband (100 Mbps moving-train test).
  Sources: [[2026-05-19-starlink-direct-to-cell-fcc-airtel-spaceinafrica]],
  [[2026-07-15-direct-to-cell-satellite-trial-telecomreviewafrica]],
  [[2026-06-01-airtel-eutelsat-rural-broadband-techafricanews]].
