# South Sudan × Data Centres & AI

South Sudan is building a **national data centre** and courting partners (China's Huawei, Swiss MGI) for
its data/gateway layer — but the binding constraint on every ambition, including AI, is the same:
**electricity**.

## The national data centre

- **Under construction.** A **national data centre** in Juba is **halfway complete (Apr 2026**, per
  Minister Ateny at the Connected Africa Summit) — a Juba **data hub** was reported "nearing completion"
  at the Sep-2025 China-Africa summit; intended to improve data management and position Juba as a "smart
  city." Sources: [[2026-04-30-ateny-digital-transformation-nairobi-summit-eyeradio]],
  [[2025-09-29-huawei-interest-digital-future-itweb]].
- **Foreign custody of the gateway + data centre.** The **[[national-communication-authority-south-sudan|NCA]]**'s
  Gateway Services & Data Centre Oversight Committee places the SSIGW gateway and national data centre
  under a sovereignty/security mandate — but with Swiss operator **[[mgi-communications]]** holding both,
  a notable **concentration** in foreign private hands (see [[SSD]] hub).

## Geopolitics — Huawei re-engagement

- **Huawei interest (Sep 2025).** At the **China-Africa Cooperation Summit**, Huawei shared deployment
  lessons and signalled interest in supporting South Sudan's digital architecture (national data hub,
  smart-city ambitions) — a re-engagement after the post-2014 rift. The delegation pressed Chinese and
  African partners for **more digital-infrastructure financing**, stressing **electricity** as the major
  impediment. See [[geopol.china]]. Source: [[2025-09-29-huawei-interest-digital-future-itweb]].

## AI ambitions — and the constraints

- **Three AI pillars (Apr 2025).** At the Kigali Global AI Summit, ICT undersecretary Lado Wani Kenyi
  named the prerequisites: **robust infrastructure, a quality data centre, and skilled human resources** —
  "without [these], the country will hardly benefit from AI." A candid statement of a near-zero base. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[infra.capacity]]. Source: [[2025-04-05-key-pillars-ai-development-onecitizendaily]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: the data-centre build is a real bid for onshore custody of national data — but it is
  simultaneously **foreign-operated** (MGI on the gateway/DC, Huawei courted for architecture) and
  **energy-starved**. Sovereignty of *location* without reliable *power* or domestic *operation* is
  fragile; South Sudan is assembling the boxes before it can keep the lights on.
