# EU activities (`geopol.eu`)

European digital-sovereignty policy and its lessons.

## Key material

- **Tech Sovereignty Package + the Triad (2026-06, [[the-govlab]]).** The EU's
  **Technological Sovereignty Package** (IP/26/1187, 2026-06-03) — Chips Act 2.0, Cloud &
  AI Development Act (tripling EU data-centre capacity), Open Source Strategy, AI Factories
  — is assessed by Verhulst & Gonzalez Otero against a **dependency / openness / agency**
  triad: **strong on dependency, notable on openness, weak on agency** (decisions
  relegated to national administrations; thin participation/redress/enforcement). Their
  fix: a "stewardship architecture" prioritising *agency* over ownership
  ([[triad-tech-sovereignty-verhulst-otero-2026]]). Converges with
  Eaves' commodification thesis ([[gov.standards]]). Source:
  [[2026-06-09 The Triad of Tech Sovereignty and Europe's Package Dependency, Openness, and Agency]].

- **The Package debated — three views (2026-06 batch).** Three sources now bracket the
  same **European Technological Sovereignty Package** (announced June 2026):
  (1) **pro/open-source** — [[2026-06-03 How the EU's Tech Sovereignty Package Finally Puts Open Source to the Test]] (OpenForum Europe) welcomes putting open source at the centre (CADA,
  30M-user OSS target, EUDI wallet) but flags gaps in standards-linkage, funding and
  skills; (2) **con/pro-collaboration** — [[2026-06-11 The Case Against the EU's Tech Sovereignty Package]] (ITIF) argues the EU lacks viable domestic alternatives (US firms = 70% of EU
  cloud; EU = 10% of chips) and that protectionism will backfire and benefit China; (3)
  **structural-dependence** — [[2026-06-29 How AI Keeps Europe Hooked on US Cloud]] shows even
  sovereignty-minded EU firms (DeepL → AWS) default to US hyperscalers for AI. **Why it
  matters for Africa:** Europe — far wealthier — faces the *same* US-hyperscaler dependency
  Africa does, making the EU debate a live comparator for African sovereignty strategy
  ([[gov.standards]], [[geopol.usa]], [[infra.store]]).
- **Global (2025, Eaves).** Europe's **Gaia-X** and **Data Act** are cited as a
  cautionary contrast to procurement-led standardisation: they "neither bring buying
  power to incentivize adoption" nor "recognize existing [de facto] standards," relying
  instead on voluntary or new de jure standards — and largely failed. EuroStack-style
  "sovereign stack" ambitions risk billions spent on incompatible infrastructure.
  Eaves' point: the EU is wealthy enough to attempt ownership, but even it is better
  served by interoperability ([[gov.standards]]).

- **Estonia exports DPI know-how to Africa (2026-05).** **[[estdev]]** (Estonia's development
  agency) signed an MoU with **[[smart-africa]]** in Brussels ([[estdev-smart-africa-mou]]) to
  build **interoperable, reusable DPI** across Smart Africa's 42 member states — cutting vendor
  lock-in, strengthening cybersecurity and capacity — pairing Estonia's e-government model with
  continental programme architecture. A European "digital sovereignty" partnership pitched on
  agency and reusable components rather than ownership. See [[XAF]], [[dpi.exchange]],
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-28 Estonia backs Africa digital infrastructure]].
- **Orange's Via Africa cable (2026-05).** The French-led **[[orange]]** consortium's
  **[[via-africa-cable]]** — a >20,000km Atlantic corridor linking West Africa directly to
  Europe across ~20 countries (first announced 12 May 2026 at the Africa Forward Summit,
  Nairobi, Macron present; 3–4-year build) — adds route diversity against single points of
  failure; Orange also pledged to train >3m African youth in AI/cyber/cloud by 2030 and to
  double its Digital Centres to 100. European telecom capital shaping African connectivity —
  the `colonialism` reading sits alongside genuine resilience gains. See [[NGA]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-23 Orange-backed subsea cable to connect Nigeria, 19 other countries]].
- **The EU-as-mirror series (2026-06).** Kaltheuner & Saari (Tech Policy Press / AI Now Institute)
  argue Europe's "Sovereign AI" rests on the unexamined assumption that EU firms serve EU
  strategic interests, while economic value keeps flowing to US hyperscalers — a bottom-up
  comparator for Africa's own dependency debate, extending the EU tech-sovereignty cluster. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-04 Series What Europe's AI Market Actually Looks Like]].

- **EU's €290m Nigeria package under Global Gateway (2026-03).** At the 8th EU–Nigeria Ministerial
  Dialogue (Abuja), the **[[european-union]]** announced **€290m** across seven operations
  ([[eu-nigeria-global-gateway-290m]]), including **€131m for digitalisation** — **90,000 km of
  fibre** with the [[ebrd]], secure interoperable DPI and data-exchange systems, and TVET/3MTT
  training — plus agriculture and migration. A **[[global-gateway]]** counter-offer to Chinese and
  US infrastructure financing, aligning EU capital with Nigeria's Renewed Hope agenda — European
  connectivity capital, the `colonialism`/resilience tension again. See [[NGA]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-24 Nigeria EU Announces New €290m Investments in Nigeria]].
- **Nigeria–Finland digital MoU (2026-03).** [[bosun-tijani]] and Finland signed a
  **[[nigeria-finland-digital-mou]]** on digital government, DPI, cybersecurity and capacity
  building — following Helsinki talks with Finnvera/[[finnfund]] on Nigeria's Data Exchange Platform
  and Project BRIDGE, and complementing Finland's lead in the EU **Team Europe Initiative on Digital
  Public Services** (delivered with Estonia's [[estdev]]). Another European "agency-not-ownership"
  DPI partnership. See [[NGA]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[finance.mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-01 Nigeria Signs MoU With Finland On Digital Innovation, Deepens Bilateral Ties]].
- **The Brussels Effect debate — regulation as export (2026-01).** ITIF's Center for Data
  Innovation argues the EU's **Brussels Effect** (GDPR/DSA/DMA/AI Act) functions as "regulatory
  imperialism" over the Global South — raising compliance costs, limiting experimentation and
  entrenching Western incumbents — and urges African states to reject wholesale adoption for
  flexible, regionally-grounded, interoperable rules. A pro-deregulation counter-frame to the
  POPIA-on-the-GDPR-model convergence, sitting against the EU's own Tech Sovereignty Package and
  the [[triad-tech-sovereignty-verhulst-otero-2026]] agency critique. See [[gov.standards]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[tech.ai]], [[colonialism]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-26 How the Brussels Effect Hinders Innovation in the Global South]].

- **EU-funded Kenya cyber-resilience (2026-01).** The EU financed the **Kenya Cyber Resilience
  (KCR) Project** — **$3.56M / €3M** over 36 months — implemented by **[[expertise-france]]** and
  **[[estdev]]** to harden critical infrastructure and build cyber skills, aligned to Kenya's
  National Cybersecurity Strategy. EU capacity capital via a Team-Europe delivery model. See
  [[KEN]], [[kenya-cyber-resilience-project]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27 Kenya launches project to boost cyber security]].
- **AU–EU AI-governance co-creation (2026-01).** [[ecdpm]] analysts urge the [[european-union]] and
  [[african-union]] to co-create a human-centric AI-governance framework blending the EU AI Act with
  the [[au-continental-ai-strategy]] and AU Data Policy Framework — flagging the 7th AU–EU summit
  (Nov 2025) declaration's omission of the AU frameworks and the EU's Nov-2025 "digital omnibus"
  postponement of AI Act implementation. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-21 How Africa and Europe could create global AI governance rules]].

- **The EU AI Act reaches Africa — extraterritoriality (Nagadya, 2026-05-18).** The **[[eu-ai-act]]**
  binds African businesses through its **"where the output lands" market-placement test**: scope
  follows use in the EU, not where a firm sits. Timeline: in force 2024-08-01, prohibited-practice
  bans from 2025-02-02, high-risk enforcement 2026-08-02. The Brussels Effect as direct legal reach,
  not just norm-diffusion — Nagadya urges Africa to extend rather than copy it. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-18 Europe Wrote The World's Most Advanced AI Law. The EU AI Act]].
- **"Wouldn't fly at home" — EU/German TA in Kenya's data policy (Mutiso, 2026-06-08).** Rose Mutiso
  flags that Kenya's **Draft National Data Governance Policy (May 2026)**, developed with **EU and
  German technical assistance**, mirrors the **EU Data Governance Act** yet would license aggregated
  public data for revenue — reuse GDPR and German informational self-determination would bar at home.
  European TA exporting a data-monetisation model its own law forbids: a colonialism-lens dependency
  critique. See [[KEN]], [[gov.protect]], [[colonialism]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-08 Kenya Wants to Sell Your Data. But It's Complicated]].
