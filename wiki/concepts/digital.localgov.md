# Digitalisation of sub-national government (`digital.localgov`)

Digitalisation at the city, municipal and council tier — revenue collection, civil
registration and local service delivery — distinct from national DPI programmes and often
the layer where citizens actually meet the state.

## Key material

- **South African public-sector DT: culture and capacity, not tech (2026 H1).** A cluster of SAJIM/AJIS
  studies finds the binding constraints are institutional: **organisational culture** (not digital
  maturity or leadership) is the strongest determinant of DT across Gauteng government entities; rural
  (B3/B4) Limpopo municipalities lack the **dynamic capabilities** to move beyond digitalising
  communications; Gauteng municipal clinics stall on infrastructure/funding/skills; and centralised ICT
  procurement via **[[sita]]** produces rigidity ("reform or redundancy"). Ghana's "e-government paradox"
  echoes it — adoption is mediated by governance, not user acceptance. Sources:
  [[2026-01-01 Digital transformation A model of transformational leadership in an organisation]],
  [[2025-01-01 An investigation of digital transformation initiatives in South African rural local municipalities]],
  [[2026-02-01 SITA and the Paradox of Centralized ICT Procurement in South Africas Public Sector]],
  [[2026-03-11 Examining Platform Governance and User Behavior in the Adoption of E-Government Services in a Developing Country]]. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]].

- **[[MWI]] Blantyre digitises council tax (2026-03).** **[[blantyre-city-council]]** — Malawi's
  second city — partnered NBS Bank to digitise **revenue and tax collection** and let residents pay
  council services electronically (Mayor Isaac Jomo Osman), cutting manual, in-person transactions
  and speeding financial management. It sits under the **Digital Malawi Acceleration Project** and is
  the local-government counterpart to the national **[[malawi-revenue-authority]]** e-invoicing push —
  sub-national revenue as a distinct digitalisation frontier. See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.govtech]].
  Source: [[2026-03-06 Malawi's second city digitises tax collection]].
- **[[CMR]] Council-level civil registration (2026-01).** Cameroon deployed a **digital civil-status
  system** (birth, marriage and death certificates) **at the council level**, implemented by
  **[[undp]]** with Danish funding across **ten municipalities** in the Far North, North-West and
  South-West. Officials report a birth certificate that once took three handwritten pages is now
  faster to issue, store and retrieve — digitalisation extending legal-identity access to youth,
  women and vulnerable groups, and building service continuity in crisis-affected regions. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-13 Cameroon intros digital system for civil documents]].
- **An emerging thread.** These are early instances of a pattern worth tracking: digitalisation
  landing at the **municipal/council tier** rather than the national platform — council revenue
  systems, local civil registration, and city-level administrative services. **[[CIV]]**'s
  **[[maposte]]** (postal and administrative services) extends the same logic to the last-mile
  administrative counter. Expect more sub-national cases as national DPI programmes push delivery
  downward to where citizens transact with the state. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[digital.rural]].
