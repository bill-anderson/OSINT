# Regional collaboration (`gov.regional`)

Cross-border harmonisation of digital infrastructure, rules and markets.

## Key material

- **COMESA moves onto AI-policy harmonisation (2026-07).** **[[comesa]]** — coordinating REC for the
  **[[idea-mpa]]** programme — launched (with Zambia's Ministry of Technology and Science and the
  **[[world-bank]]**) an **AI Sensitisation Workshop** in Lusaka, the first structured step toward a
  **COMESA Regional AI Strategy** and **Regional Digital Inclusion Strategy**. Explicitly framed against
  **fragmentation** ("inconsistent safeguards … missed opportunities"), it extends the regional-harmonisation
  playbook from connectivity/DPI into AI — alongside the [[eac]] AI-sovereignty bloc and the AU strategy. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[gov.policy]], [[ZMB]], [[XSA]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-14 Zambia and COMESA Launch AI Initiative to Advance Regional Digital Transformation]].
- **Africa's digital-diplomacy and integration moment (2026 H1).** As the AfCFTA Digital Trade Protocol
  is operationalised (Brookings), African states are asserting agency amid great-power rivalry: CIGI's
  Soulé maps "tech diplomacy" (Kenya's tech envoy; data-centre sovereignty), while a second CIGI brief
  argues Africa should *lead* on governing US tech giants. The **[[csep]]**/AUDA-NEPAD Africa–India
  Blueprint (RIA chapter) warns the AU Data Policy Framework and Malabo Convention "remain largely
  undomesticated," pushing DPI as an alternative to frontier-AI dependence; ODI reframes mineral fiscal
  regimes through sovereign geological-data DPI. Sources:
  [[2026-03-18 Realizing Africa’s digital trade potential under the AfCFTA]],
  [[2026-03-01 Tech Diplomacy and Avenues for Digital Development in Africa CIGI PB232]],
  [[2026-01-01 The Africa-India Blueprint for Growth]],
  [[2026-02-01 Getting Smart about Africas mineral wealth DPI and mineral fiscal regimes in West Africa]]. See [[tech.industry]], [[sovereignty]].

- **Continental data-governance instruments advance (2026 H1).** The AU is revising the
  **[[malabo-convention]]**; ECOWAS is revising its binding Supplementary Act, the EAC is drafting a
  regional data-governance framework, and SADC is revising its Model Law and floating a SADC DPA bloc.
  The [[african-commission]] circulated draft **[[african-guidelines-data-access]]** under Res.620, and
  the AfCFTA Digital Trade Protocol now imposes binding minimum data-protection standards — which Cenfri
  warns outrun DPA capacity. Sources:
  [[2026-03-12 Africa’s Data Protection Reforms A Continental Perspective on the Drivers of Change in Legal Frameworks]],
  [[2026-05-07 African Guidelines on Data Access ACHPR Res 620 draft]],
  [[2026-03-23 Reassessing the role of data protection authorities in Africa]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[afcfta-digital-trade-protocol]].

- **AfCFTA Digital Trade Forum — eight calls to action (2026-07-07, Lagos).** State parties
  pledged to expedite the **Protocol on Digital Trade** (AU Assembly, 2024): harmonized
  **e-signatures**, **mutual recognition of digital ID**, **interoperable cross-border
  payments** ([[papss]]), paperless trade, cybersecurity/consumer-protection trust, inclusion
  and PPP delivery — the route to a digital single market. SG [[wamkele-mene]]. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]].
  Source: [[2026-07-07 African nations recommit to operationalize AfCFTA digital single market framework]].
- **From negotiation to implementation (2026-07, Abuja/Lagos).** At the **18th Council of Ministers**
  Nigeria took the **Bureau chair** from Egypt, and the **2nd Digital Trade Forum** adopted **Eight
  Calls to Action** to operationalise the **[[afcfta-digital-trade-protocol]]** (DTP) — the AU-adopted
  instrument behind the "African Single Digital Market," whose **Digital Identity Annex**
  (Feb 2025) mandates harmonised ID standards and mutual assessment. Africa's digital economy:
  **~$180bn now → ~$712bn by 2050**; delivery runs through ADIEP (7,500 SMEs across 19
  countries, with Google) and **[[papss]]**. SG [[wamkele-mene]] warned the DTP needs
  **>700 data centres** (from ~220 today) to localise infrastructure ([[infra.store]]). See
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[EGY]], [[GHA]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-09 Nigeria Takes AfCFTA Council Chair as Digital Forum Charts Roadmap for Implementation]],
  [[2026-04-08 Africa's digital trade push requires 700 data centres, says AfCFTA chief]].
- **AfCFTA's governance gap (2026-07).** [[afcfta]] integrates markets faster than
  regulatory systems; the missing continental data-governance layer ([[gov.protect]],
  [[gov.legislate]]) is the binding constraint on cross-border digital business. DPI — the
  [[equity-africanenda-gates-dpi-partnership]] — is pitched as the enabler. Sources: [[2026-07-03 AfCFTA, Data Governance and the Hidden Legal Gaps Reshaping Africa's Digital Economy - P.M. News]],
  [[2026-07-07 Equity, AfricaNenda and Gates Foundation Partner to Advance Digital Public Infrastructure Across Africa]].
- **Pooling for AI (2026).** Proposed **AU Pan-African AI Compute Grid** and regional
  collective bargaining answer compute-cost exclusion ([[tech.ai]]); Rwanda's
  Kagame co-chairs the ITU [[ai-for-good-global-commission]], an African seat in global
  AI norm-setting. Sources: [[2026-06-17 Priced out of the future Confronting the rising cost of AI in Africa]],
  [[2026-05-21 AI for Good Global Commission]].

- **US–Africa investment architecture (2026-01).** The **[[us-auc-strategic-investment-working-group]]**
  (US + **[[african-union]]**) anchors investment to AfCFTA/PIDA/Agenda 2063 — regional
  frameworks recruited into US commercial diplomacy. See [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Joint Statement of the United States and African Union on the Launch of a Strategic Investment Working Group]].
- **Supranational governance as the missing layer (2026-07).** The digital-ID
  interoperability study argues the fix is **regional/continental governance** (via AfCFTA),
  not more technical standards. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-03 Study finds governance, not technology, is blocking digital ID interoperability]].
- **Continental internet-governance capacity (2026-07).** **[[smart-africa]]** and
  **[[afnic]]** signed an MoU ([[smart-africa-afnic-mou]]) to build African **ccTLD**
  capacity and DNS resilience — regional coordination of the continent's own internet.
  See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Smart Africa and AFNIC Partner to Strengthen Africa's Internet Infrastructure]].
- **DPI as the regional-integration substrate (2026, UNDP).** UNDP's continental DPI paper
  frames interoperable ID/payments/data-exchange rails as what makes **AfCFTA** ($450bn) and
  AU **Agenda 2063** operational, and urges African-led, coordinated DPI investment. See
  [[afcfta]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa - UNDP]].
- **Operationalising the DTP via peer learning (2026).** The **[[adli]]** (Africa Data Leadership
  Initiative) 2026 cohort ([[dial]]), with the AfCFTA, takes a **test-and-learn**
  approach to helping **six countries across three trade corridors** comply with the DTP's Digital
  Identity Annex against EAC/ECOWAS/SADC data-governance frameworks and
  the AU Data Policy Framework. Governance, not technology, is the binding constraint. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-27 Africa's economic future hinges on breaking down silos. This ADLI cohort advances integration]].
- **Interoperability instruments and partners (2026-04/05).** Regional plumbing: the
  **[[sadx]]** (Smart Africa Data Exchange) technical mission in Kigali advanced **PKI trust chains,
  API contracts and federated cross-border verification** with **[[RWA]]**, **[[BEN]]** and
  **[[GHA]]**; Estonia's ESTDEV signed the **[[estdev-smart-africa-mou]]** to build interoperable,
  **reusable DPI** across [[smart-africa]]'s 42 member states and cut vendor lock-in. On the rules
  side, the **[[eac]]** created a body for its **eight** national DPAs to align cross-border data
  governance. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]], [[gov.protect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-27 Smart Africa drives cross-border data interoperability through SADX Technical Mission]],

- **Continental payment and finance rails (2026-02/05).** **[[afreximbank]]** pushed its **[[papss]]**
  suite (PAPSSCARD, African Currency Marketplace) for **local-currency settlement** to cut USD
  dependence, integrating with Kenya's Pesalink. In parallel the
  **[[african-development-bank]]**'s **[[nafa]]** (New African Financial Architecture,
  Feb 2026) seeks to consolidate the continent's fragmented financial architecture via an African
  Financing Stability Mechanism and African Credit Rating Agency. See [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]],
  [[EGY]]. Sources:

- **Regional subsea backbone (2025-09).** **[[seacom]]** unveiled **Seacom 2.0**, a 48-fibre-pair
  Indian Ocean Basin cable pitched as a low-latency AI backbone and a **gateway for
  landlocked SADC and East African markets** — landing stations reframed as "AI communication nodes"
  linking sovereign AI infrastructure to global hubs. See [[XEA]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-24 Unveiling Seacom 2.0 - ​ ​A new digital renaissance and subsea network to power the Indian Ocean Basin's digital and AI future]].
- **South–South regional infrastructure (2026-06/07).** Cross-border builds assembled bilaterally,
  operator-to-operator: **[[telecom-namibia]]**–Angola satellite/subsea deals, and the
  Botswana–Zimbabwe–Zambia **[[powertel-paratus-fibre-corridor]]**. The
  **[[ZMB]]–[[MOZ]] Chanida–Cassacatiza cross-border fibre interconnector**
  (Feb 2026, [[bayobab-zambia]] + [[vodacom]]) links Zambia's backbone to Mozambique's subsea network,
  leaving Zambia — by minister Mutati's claim — fibre-linked to **eight neighbours** and the "most
  digitally connected in Southern Africa." See [[NAM]], [[AGO]], [[ZWE]], [[XSA]], [[infra.connect]].

- **[[AGO]] Angola as regional space/DPI exporter (2025-10 → 2026-06).** Angola turned its
  sovereign-infrastructure build outward. Its **[[angosat-2]]** satellite is the platform for
  outbound cooperation: a **Namibia** proof-of-concept (Oct 2025) that matured into a
  Telecom Namibia–GGPEN pilot commercial agreement plus a ministerial ICT MoU at **ANGOTIC 2026**
  ([[telecom-namibia-angola-deals]]); a **[[MOZ]] Mozambique** space & digital MoU whose
  **Boane Earth Station** already receives ANGOSAT-2 signal ([[angola-mozambique-space-digital-mou]]);
  and a **[[NGA]] Nigeria** NASRDA–GGPEN space-science agreement ([[nigeria-angola-space-cooperation]]).
  In parallel it deepened ministerial ICT ties with **[[KEN]] Kenya** at the **Luanda Financing
  Summit** (Oct 2025; [[kenya-angola-ict-mou]], with AfDB/World Bank/AUDA-NEPAD present), and had its
  **Camama national data centre** studied as a model by Namibia — regional prestige for the
  sovereignty play ([[infosi]]). The counter-current is the regulatory layer: Portugal's
  **ANACOM** (with the EU Delegation) ran an institutional-strengthening mission training
  **INACOM** ([[anacom-angola-mission]]) — the **ex-metropole shaping the rules** even as Angola
  exports infrastructure. Convened throughout by **[[ministerio-das-telecomunicacoes-tecnologias-de-informacao-e-comunicacao-social-angola|MINTTICS]]**.

- **[[ZMB]] Zambia / ESA (2025).** [[dzap]] sits inside the regional [[idea-mpa]]
  programme, coordinated by **COMESA** as Regional Economic Community. It advances the
  **Single Digital Market for East Africa** — cutting barriers to regional telecom
  infrastructure and to trade in digital services/goods via "no-stop" digital
  border facilitation — and responds to the AU Digital Transformation Strategy (universal
  access by 2030) and Agenda 2063. Cross-border infrastructure gaps still hinder seamless trade.
- **[[XEA]] EAC — toward a unified AI-sovereignty bloc (2026-03).** The 4th EAC Regional STI
  Conference (Kigali) explored a **regional AI Policy Roadmap** to harmonise rules and values
  across the eight-member bloc, so East Africa "leads as a unified bloc rather than as individual
  nations." Rwanda's Ingabire ([[paula-ingabire]]): data "stays in our hands" and AI-generated value
  "accrues to East Africans." Entity [[eac]]. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.standards]], [[gov.policy]].
  Source: [[2026-03-31 EAC moves to create unified AI Sovereignty bloc]].
- **[[XWA]] WATRA — regulatory tooling for a $216bn market (2026-04).** The **[[watra]]** (16-member
  West Africa Telecommunications Regulators Assembly) concluded its 4th Working Groups Meeting in
  Ouagadougou, finalising technical reports on **5G, subsea-cable resilience, cybersecurity, consumer
  protection and NGSO satellite regulation** — "practical instruments" to harmonise rules across
  a **$216bn** regional digital economy. See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.policy]],
  [[BFA]]. Source: [[2026-04-13 WATRA rallies Africa for region's $216b digital economy]].
- **[[GIN]] Guinea — Transform Africa Summit host and CAR chair (2025-11).** Guinea hosted the 7th
  **[[transform-africa-summit-2025|Transform Africa Summit]]** ([[smart-africa]], 42 states; 7,000+
  participants, 14 MoUs) — the first Francophone West African host — and chairs the Smart Africa
  **Council of African Regulators** via **[[arpt-guinea|ARPT]]**; TELEMO e-procurement was built through
  a **Guinea-Rwanda** partnership. See [[GIN]], [[tech.ai]], [[finance.mou]]. Sources:
  [[2025-11-13 Transform Africa Summit 2025 opens in Conakry, epicentre of digital cooperation in Africa]],

- **[[XWA]] WURI — mutual recognition of digital ID (2026-03).** The **[[wuri-programme]]**
  ([[ecowas]] + World Bank co-secretariat) held its 2nd Supervisory Committee and validated a
  **cross-border interoperability strategy** and 2026 work plan toward mutual recognition of ID
  credentials and harmonised technical specifications (~300m citizens, ECOWAS Vision 2050) — regional
  integration on interoperable foundational ID. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[TGO]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 ECOWAS, World Bank Advance West Africa Digital Identity]].
- **[[XWA]] West Africa's integration stack — single market, customs, ID, payments (2026-01→03).**
  ECOWAS is assembling the layers of a **Regional Digital Single Market** for its 400m+
  citizens ([[ecowas]], Vision 2050): a Jan-2026 Lagos Thematic Dialogue committed to "remove digital
  borders" via interoperable fintech, unified digital ID and rural broadband, and resolved to build a
  **Regional AI Governance Framework**; its **[[sigmat]]** customs-transit interoperability system
  (live since 2019, showcased at the WCO 2026 conference in Abu Dhabi) is the working cross-border
  data-exchange e-Hub beneath it; the World Bank's **$137m [[wardip]]2** (announced 2026-03-11) funds
  broadband + digital public services for **~10.6m** people in **[[BEN]]/[[LBR]]/[[SLE]]** with
  [[world-bank]]/[[smart-africa]]/[[african-union]] backing; and **[[papss]]**/[[onafriq]] began a
  CBN-approved pilot moving Nigeria→Ghana payments **fully in naira** (PAPSS: 160+ banks, 400m+
  accounts, 19 countries). See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]]. Sources:

- **[[XEA]] GovTech maturity as regional benchmark (2026-01).** The World Bank's **2025 [[gtmi]]**
  (GovTech Maturity Index; 48 indicators, 4 pillars) placed **[[TZA]] Tanzania** in Group A
  ("Extensive") alongside **[[KEN]]/[[RWA]]/[[UGA]]/[[EGY]]** — the index now weighting interoperability
  and *adoption* over launched portals, working as peer pressure across East Africa's digital
  states (Tanzania's GovESB data-exchange backbone the cited exemplar). See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-26 Tanzania's GovTech success raises bar for East Africa's digital states]].
- **A unified African voice in global AI governance (2026-07-09, Geneva).** At the inaugural **Global
  Dialogue on AI Governance** (alongside AI for Good / WSIS Forum 2026), the **[[african-union]]**
  Commission (Lerato Mataboge) pressed an Africa-centred, development-focused line anchored in Agenda
  2063, the Continental AI Strategy, the AU Data Policy Framework and the Malabo Convention. The **UN
  Independent International Scientific Panel on AI** ([[un-ai-scientific-panel]]) presented its
  Preliminary Report; Mataboge coordinated with its four African members (Marivate, Tadesse, Orji,
  El-Assady) to align science with political leadership. Regional coordination as the
  route to influence in norm-setting Africa does not control. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.standards]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 African Union pushes for unified African voice at Global AI Governance Dialogue]].
- **[[NER]]/[[XWA]] AES — the secessionist counter-bloc (2026-03).** The
  **[[alliance-of-sahel-states]]** advanced its own integration track: **[[NER]] Niger** became the
  second AES state (after [[BFA]] Burkina Faso) to launch an **AES-aligned biometric ID** enabling
  passport-free travel among Niger, Burkina Faso and [[MLI]] Mali — the trio having **quit ECOWAS/WURI
  in 2024** and issued a common AES passport (Jan 2025). It pairs this with a
  **data-sovereignty infrastructure track**: **[[BFA]] Burkina Faso** is building a ~$5.4m Digital
  Infrastructure Supervision Center (delivery ~Oct 2026) under a **"zero data abroad"** policy,
  and Mali has commissioned a Tier III data centre — sovereignty via
  self-hosting, not shared regional rails. Regional collaboration by rupture, not harmonisation. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[infra.store]], [[colonialism]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-30 Focus on Sahel digital sovereignty gets clearer with Niger's new biometric ID card]],
  [[2026-01-22 Burkina Faso aims for digital sovereignty with infrastructure supervision center]].
- **[[XAF]] UNECA — from "rail" to "train" (2026-04).** At the **58th ECA Conference of Ministers**
  roundtable, ministers (Mozambique, Tanzania, Botswana, Kenya) and industry ([[gsma]],
  [[africa-data-centres]]/[[cassava-technologies]]) called to move Africa from DPI "consumer" to
  "architect": **10× data-centre capacity** (Africa generates <1% of global data despite ~20% of
  people), duty-free smartphones, sovereign clouds/"data embassies," stronger statistics
  offices, and translation of the **AU AI Strategy** into funded local frameworks. Entity [[uneca]].
  See [[dpi.exchange]], [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]], [[data.statistics]], [[MOZ]], [[TZA]], [[BWA]].
  Source: [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].
- **Regional health-data sovereignty vs bilateral US deals (2026-03).** The US has signed **26
  bilateral health MOUs** with African governments under the
  **[[america-first-global-health-strategy]]**, each bundling data- and specimen-sharing (US access to
  national surveillance/lab databases, 10-yr post-termination) — bypassing the WHO's multilateral PABS
  pathway. **[[africa-cdc]]**'s **Health Security and Sovereignty Agenda** (Nov 2025, "non-negotiable")
  is the continental counter-position; DG Kaseya declined a US observer seat, pledging support to
  signatories and refusers alike. Regional solidarity as a bulwark against fragmenting bilateral data
  governance. See [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.mis]], [[ZWE]], [[KEN]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-19 African Digital Health Data Is a Condition of Global Health Funding]].

- **Pan-African payment interconnection deepens (2026-02/03).** Two moves knit national switches into
  the continental rail: Kenya's **[[pesalink]]** joined **[[papss]]** as a **Technical Connectivity
  Provider** — linking 80+ Kenyan banks/fintechs/SACCOs to PAPSS's 160+ institutions for instant
  local-currency settlement, the **first time PAPSS piloted a national switch for transaction
  termination**; and the **[[bank-of-ghana]]** (African Prosperity Dialogue) called for a payments
  overhaul to unlock [[afcfta]]'s ~$2.8tn market, noting intra-African payments still cost 7–10% (vs
  ~3% global) and >80% route through correspondent banks abroad (~$5.3bn/yr), while promoting PAPSS
  plus a Ghana–Rwanda fintech-passporting pilot. Payments as the circulatory system of AfCFTA. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[KEN]], [[GHA]], [[RWA]]. Sources:
