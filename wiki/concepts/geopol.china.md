# China activities (`geopol.china`)

China as the implicit or explicit counter-pole in African digital-infrastructure
financing and the global AI-capacity race.

## Key material

- **[[MWI]] Malawi — a proposed US$180m Huawei ICT plan (2026-06).** ICT Minister Namalomba presented, in China,
  a four-year **US$180m [[huawei]]** roadmap: a **one-stop digital-government portal** (passports, business
  registration, tax) and a **28-district high-speed ring-protected network** to power the Boma Lathu platform —
  extending Huawei's role beyond the TNM 5G rollout it already backs. Funding terms undisclosed; a China-finance
  dependency at the core of Malawi's e-government stack. See [[malawi--dpi-govtech]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-06-09-huawei-180m-ict-investment-plan-malawi24]].
- **DFC framed against China (2026-06, TechTrends).** The US DFC's **$300m** backing of
  Africa Data Centres ([[cassava-technologies]]) is framed *explicitly* "as a
  counterweight to Chinese infrastructure financing on the continent" — the clearest
  statement that data-centre capital is now geopolitical. See [[geopol.usa]],
  [[infra.store]]. Source: [[2026-06-23 Africa's Data Centre Boom Where The New Facilities Are Being Built]].
- **AI-capacity race (2026-06, *The Sun*).** China holds **31%** of global HPC capacity
  and invests "over £100bn annually" in AI; Africa invests **<£1bn collectively**,
  raising exclusion from AI norm-setting. See [[tech.ai]].
- **Direct financing — Namibia (2026-07).** China commits **¥98m (~US$14.4m)** to
  Namibia's Smart City pilot, with **[[huawei]]** delivering smart-city solutions, an
  AI-ready national data centre and skills under the Windhoek strategy (2026–2036) —
  Chinese capital + vendor pitched as *enabling* Namibian "data sovereignty." The clearest
  case in the wiki of the sovereignty/dependency paradox on a Chinese stack. Deal:
  [[china-namibia-smart-city]]. See [[NAM]], [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-08 China funds Namibia digital infrastructure]].

- **Direct financing — Nigeria towers (2026-06).** China Industrial Bank — in its **first
  Nigeria investment** — backs **[[nucap]]** (Nigeria Universal Communication Access Project),
  committing to at least **1,000 of 3,700** planned "green" telecom towers before end-2026 to
  reach >20m underserved Nigerians ([[ncc]] separately targets 12,000 site upgrades in 2026).
  Chinese capital as rural-connectivity financier, alongside the vendor pattern seen in
  Namibia. See [[NGA]], [[infra.connect]], [[digital.rural]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-04 Chinese bank backs Nigeria towers project]].
- **Surveillance as an "integrated governance package" (2026-04).** The IDS report *Smart
  City Surveillance in Africa* (Mar 2026) mapped Chinese-supplied surveillance across **11
  countries** — Chinese firms present in every one, and by far the largest provider ahead of
  US/EU/Israeli vendors. **[[NGA]]** is the biggest market: **China Exim Bank advanced $399m
  of the $470m** Abuja spent on a ZTE/Hikvision security network, with 10,000 cameras in
  Lagos; across the sample, states spent **>$2bn** on facial recognition and ANPR. The model
  is a **~$250m Eximbank loan bundling** thousands of cameras (often **[[hikvision]]**) with a
  build-and-transfer **national data centre** and **[[zte]]/[[huawei]]** trainers running the
  command centre — what Georgetown's Bulelani Jili calls "integrated governance packages" that
  create **institutional and technical lock-in**, not easily substituted once embedded in
  fibre/e-gov ecosystems. Marketed as public safety, systems were repurposed against
  opposition (facial-recognition fears in **[[ZWE]]**, cameras in Mozambican opposition
  strongholds) with little legal oversight. Kenya was an early adopter (a $100m Chinese grant,
  1,800 Huawei cameras post-Westgate). Lineage: China's Golden Shield. This is the sharpest
  `colonialism`/`sovereignty` case of dependency-by-infrastructure. See [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[gov.protect]], [[infra.store]], [[KEN]], [[EGY]], [[ZMB]], [[SEN]], [[UGA]], [[DZA]],
  [[RWA]], [[MOZ]]. Sources: [[2026-03-01 Smart City Surveillance in Africa (IDS 2026)]] (the IDS report by
  Wakabi & Roberts — the primary behind the SCMP coverage),
  [[2026-04-04 Chinese surveillance rolled out in Africa ZTE, Hikvision, Huawei at the helm]].
- **Vendor embedding via AI training — Ghana (2026-03).** **[[huawei]]** agreed (MWC 2026) to
  provide **free AI training to 3,000 girls** in Ghana's Girls in ICT programme and signalled
  interest in Ghana's **$250m AI Compute Centre**, 5G and a smartphone-assembly plant
  ([[ghana-national-ai-strategy]]) — the training-and-skills channel by which the Chinese stack
  seeds the next generation of the market. See [[GHA]], [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.training]].
  Source: [[2026-03-05 Ghana strikes AI training deal with Chinese multinational amid $250m tech investment push]].
- **China as the other pole of the compute duopoly (2026-04).** US and Chinese firms run
  **>90% of the world's AI data centres**; **[[NGA]]**'s NITDA DG [[kashifu-inuwa]] frames
  sovereignty as *not* "banning China" but capturing value locally, wanting hyperscalers to
  partner with Nigerian firms. Meanwhile **DeepSeek** energised the frugal-AI movement, and
  China's **open-source models** — built on its own AI-cloud and semiconductor supply chain —
  have become a foundation for developers worldwide, giving cash-constrained African builders
  a Chinese alternative to renting US compute. See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]],
  [[include.divides]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-02 Nations priced out of Big AI are building with frugal models]],
  [[2026-04-14 Nigeria Seeks Stronger Digital Sovereignty, National Software Infrastructure]].
- **China as counter-pole in the AI-sovereignty frame (2026-05).** Rest of World: African AI
  strategies voice a "warranted wariness of China and the U.S." as twin dependencies, with
  draft policies flagging reliance on both governments and their commercial actors. China's
  HPC/AI weight is the backdrop to the $60bn Africa AI Fund and the AU Continental AI
  Strategy. See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]].
- **"Payments Follow Trade" — the China corridor (analysis, 2026-04).** Samora Kariuki and Waka
  CEO April Long argue Africa's cross-border payments must be rebuilt around the **Africa–China
  trade corridor**: Africa runs a **~$62bn** goods deficit with China and an estimated
  **~$102bn/yr payment obligation**, against record **$348bn** two-way trade in 2025 (+17.7%;
  Chinese exports to Africa +25.8% to $225bn after US tariffs). Their proposed backend is
  **stablecoin rails** (global stablecoin transfers reached **$27.6tn** in 2024, exceeding
  Visa+Mastercard) plus two-sided compliance to settle into mainland China (CNH/CNY bifurcation,
  SAFE/PBOC trade-verification). Provenance: a **partner/sponsored piece** co-written with Waka's
  CEO — Waka's own traction figures are company-stated. See [[dpi.pay]], [[KEN]], [[finance.new]].
  Source: [[2026-04-20 117 - Payments Follow Trade]].

- **Huawei and Chinese state tech deepen across new markets (2026-01/02).** The vendor-embedding
  pattern spread to three more states. **[[GAB]] Gabon** signed a **[[gabon-huawei-mou]]**
  (23 Dec 2025) — a joint project committee, Gabonese-startup integration and "skills transfer for
  digital sovereignty," expanding the Connected Schools programme. **[[SSD]] South Sudan** rekindled
  ties with **[[huawei]]** a decade after a 2014 rift (officials had accused Huawei of hacking
  government email to damage a rival **[[zte]]** contract), the new CEO pledging continued presence.
  And China handed **[[NAM]] Namibia** its first **satellite data-receiving ground station** (outside
  Windhoek) — Namibia-run with Chinese-trained technicians, soft-power framing around "supporting
  African science." Each is Chinese capital/kit pitched as *enabling* national sovereignty. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[capacity.training]], [[data.satellite]], [[finance.mou]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-05 Gabon and Huawei Forge Partnership to Boost National Digital Transformation]],
  [[2026-02-09 Huawei reconnects with South Sudan]],
  [[2026-02-13 Namibia gets  Chinese satellite ground station]].
- **DeepSeek as the affordable Chinese pole (2026-03).** **[[deepseek]]** and other cheap Chinese
  open-weight models now account for **11–14%** of African chatbot usage — **~20%** in [[ETH]] and
  [[ZWE]] and rising in [[NGA]] — riding affordability and Belt-and-Road fibre/handset dominance;
  globally Chinese open-weight usage grew **1.2%→~30%** in a year. The cash-constrained African
  developer's alternative to renting US compute, and the demand-side counterpart to the
  surveillance/financing supply push. See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12 China's DeepSeek gives US tech giants a run for their money as Microsoft and others expand AI push in Africa]].

- **Skills-and-diplomacy track deepens — Rwanda & Kenya (2026-01).** Two exploratory moves extend
  the vendor-embedding pattern from kit to capacity-building. In **[[RWA]]**, China's ambassador met
  senior officials to expand ICT/AI/digital-innovation cooperation focused on **smart education** and
  school digital infrastructure (exploratory, no deal signed — no `finance.mou`). In **[[KEN]]**, the
  **[[kenya-school-of-government]]** and **[[huawei]]** Kenya opened MoU talks to train public
  servants in **AI, cybersecurity, cloud and digital governance** — the training channel by which the
  Chinese stack seeds public-sector adoption (MoU under discussion, not signed). See
  [[capacity.training]], [[tech.ai]], [[finance.mou]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-19 Rwanda, China forge technology alliance]],
  [[2026-01-12 Kenya, Huawei to boost public sector digital skills]].
- **Kenya sends officials to China on an e-commerce MoU (2026-07-17).** The
  **[[state-department-for-ict-and-the-digital-economy-kenya]]** dispatched **18 ICT officers** to the
  **2026 Silk Road E-Commerce** matching event in **Shandong**, PS **[[john-tanui]]** citing the
  **[[kenya-china-ecommerce-mou]]** as its basis and the national masterplan as its warrant. Note the
  direction of travel: unlike the Huawei training MoUs above, this is **state-to-state and outbound** —
  African officials into the Chinese e-commerce ecosystem rather than Chinese vendors into African
  ministries. **No cost or deliverable was published, and the wiki holds no text or signing date for the
  MoU itself** — a provenance gap on an instrument now functioning as a spending warrant. See
  [[capacity.training]], [[KEN]]. Source: [[2026-07-17-kenya-ict-officers-china-digital-trade]].
- **Gabon buys start-up skills from Huawei (2026-07-14).** The
  **[[gabon-huawei-kimba-connect-accord]]** makes Huawei Gabon the technical partner to
  **[[kimba-connect]]**, Gabon's first national open-innovation programme — smart rooms, training in
  networks, cybersecurity, AI and software development, and **Huawei's own certifying kits**. A second,
  narrower agreement layered on the **[[gabon-huawei-mou]]** (2025-12-23). Where the curriculum, the
  certificate and the equipment are one vendor's, capacity-building and lock-in are the same programme.
  See [[gabon--tech-innovate]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-gabon-garantie-publique-huawei-start-up]].
