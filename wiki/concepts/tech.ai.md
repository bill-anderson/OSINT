# AI (`tech.ai`)

Artificial intelligence as an axis of both opportunity and exclusion for African
states — where compute cost, data-centre siting, connectivity and governance seats
converge.

## Key material — continental & thematic

- **AI-strategy proliferation and the sovereignty/rights tension (2026 H1).** National AI strategies
  multiplied — **Zimbabwe** ([[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]]; [[project-pangolin]], an AI–Cybersecurity
  Fusion Centre; MISA warns of centralisation without safeguards), Ghana, Kenya's AI Bill, and South
  Africa's SoNA AI-in-policing agenda (RIA) — while SA's draft AI Policy was withdrawn over AI-fabricated
  citations. Two framings recur: **sovereignty/anti-extraction** — Brookings/Gabon's "sequence, not rush"
  ("once raw minerals, now raw data"), CIPIT on IP as a "standard-taker" problem, CIPESA on Uganda not
  becoming a "raw data supplier"; and **Africa–Europe asymmetry** — the Lawyers Hub report's "regulatory
  imperialism"/Adequacy Trap and a proposed **$200m AI Governance Capacity Fund**. Sources:
  [[2026-06-23 Implications of Zimbabwe’s National AI Strategy on Freedom of Expression Privacy and Access to Information]],
  [[2026-02-10 Why Africa should sequence not rush into AI]],
  [[2026-04-19 Rethinking Ownership Authorship and Value Creation in IP Policy for African AI]],
  [[2026-05-15 Africa–Europe Cooperation on AI Governance Report]],
  [[2026-05-11 CIPESA Urges Rights-Centred Approach to Uganda’s AI Strategy]],
  [[2026-02-13 From digital ID to AI in policing Turning SoNA commitments into public-interest governance]]. See [[gov.policy]], [[sovereignty]].

- **Data-protection law as de facto AI governance (2026 H1).** With no continental AI-specific statute
  yet, DPAs and legislators regulate AI *through* data-protection law: **Angola** wrote AI credit-scoring
  and anti-scraping provisions into its revised law (echoing the EU AI Act’s high-risk approach), while
  **Zimbabwe, Mauritius, Ghana, Kenya, Djibouti, Malawi and Morocco** advanced AI strategies/bills in
  early 2026. Sources:
  [[2026-03-12 Africa’s Data Protection Reforms A Continental Perspective on the Drivers of Change in Legal Frameworks]],
  [[2026-06-23 Bimonthly Update on Privacy in Africa (January & February 2026)]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.policy]].

- **The continental commitment, and the law that trails it (2025-04 → 2026).** The **[[africa-ai-declaration]]**
  (signed Kigali, 4 Apr 2025) is now anchored to its **primary text**
  ([[2025-04-04 Africa Declaration on Artificial Intelligence]]): **49 country signatories** plus the AU
  and [[smart-africa]] (settling the 49/52/54 reporting spread — see the entity and REV-001(a)). Binding
  law lags the declaration: the end-2025 data-protection roundup
  ([[2026-01-11 The Year of the Teeth Data Protection in Africa Roundup, 2025, Projections for 2026]])
  records a wave of **national AI strategies** (Côte d'Ivoire, Kenya, Libya, Nigeria adopted; Egypt
  updated; Guinea a 10-yr roadmap; Mauritius an AI Unit; Chad an AI Directorate) but projects the
  **first *comprehensive* AI laws only in Q2 2026** — Nigeria's Digital Economy & E-Governance Bill the
  frontrunner. Strategy first, statute later. See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **The cost barrier (2026-06, *The Sun* / Ademola).** Africa hosts **<1%** of global
  high-performance computing capacity (US 33%, China 31%). Cloud GPU compute rose
  **>300% globally 2023–2025**; African institutions pay **25–40% more** than Europe/N.
  America; a medium LLM training run (~£150k) can exceed an African CS department's
  annual research budget — a slide from digital marginalisation to **algorithmic
  dependency**. Remedies: AU **Pan-African AI Compute Grid**, open-source models,
  regional collective bargaining. See [[include.divides]],
  [[capacity.research]], [[infra.store]]. Source: [[2026-06-17 Priced out of the future Confronting the rising cost of AI in Africa]].
- **AI as data-centre demand driver (2026, DataCenterDynamics / TechTrends).** New
  African builds (Teraco JB7, IXAfrica, Seacom 2.0) target AI/liquid-cooling workloads;
  Kenya's **Servernah Cloud** launched as the first "sovereign AI cloud" in March 2026.
  See [[infra.store]], [[infra.connect]]. Sources:

- **AI geopolitics — the partnership scramble (2026-07 batch).** The race runs through
  **bilateral instruments and rival patrons**. Carnegie ([[jane-munga]]) notes no African
  state yet holds a US **Technology Prosperity Deal** ([[technology-prosperity-deal]]) or
  **[[pax-silica]]** seat, proposing Kenya as entry point via the
  **[[american-ai-exports-program]]**. The **[[uae]]** pledged **$1bn** for African AI
  ([[uae-ai-for-development-initiative]]), the US–Gulf–Africa bridge (via
  **[[g42]]**). **[[google]]** exceeded its $1bn Africa commitment (Ghana applied-AI lab;
  startup cohort — AfDB projects **$1.5tn** AI upside by 2035 against Africa's **~1%** of
  global DC capacity). **[[microsoft]]**'s Kenya AI data centre ([[microsoft-g42-kenya-datacenter]])
  stalled on **power capacity**. China deepens ties (Zambia). See [[geopol.usa]],
  [[geopol.china]], [[infra.capacity]].
- **The AU–Google MoU and the "sovereignty" critique (2026-02, [[african-union]]).** The Feb-2026
  **[[au-google-mou]]** — framed as advancing "Africa's sovereign AI" and implementing the
  **[[au-continental-ai-strategy]]** (Jul 2024) — puts African institutional data on US-owned
  infrastructure under the **[[cloud-act]]**, bundled with an Apolitical/Google.org-funded
  programme training **50,000 Ethiopian public officials** (continental scale-up planned) in Google's
  frameworks. Alice Munyua reads it as neo-colonial "ecosystem conditioning": the AU's 2025 budget
  was **77.5% donor-funded**, its AI strategy externally drafted (UNESCO "entry point," Global Center
  on AI Governance, a Brookings fellow) on EU-AI-Act lines not African legal traditions — echoing
  2012 data-exfiltration reports from the Chinese-built AU HQ.
  Sovereignty as press-release language, not enforceable clause. See [[ETH]], [[geopol.usa]],
  [[gov.protect]], [[colonialism]]. Source: [[2026-03-05 THE GATEKEEPERS PART XII]].
- **African-language voice AI — WAXAL (2026-02).** [[google]] released **[[waxal]]**, an
  open-source voice dataset — **>11,000 hours / ~2m audio files across 21 African languages**
  (Hausa, Yoruba, Luganda, Swahili, Igbo, Fulani…), free on Hugging Face, with contributing African
  institutions (Makerere, University of Ghana, Rwanda's Digital Umuganda, AIMS) **retaining corpus
  ownership**. It targets the data scarcity blocking voice AI across SSA's 2,000+ languages,
  following Nigeria's N-ATLAS. See [[UGA]], [[GHA]], [[RWA]], [[data.open]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-04 Google launches WAXAL, an open-source voice dataset for African languages]].
- **National AI institutions (2026-07 batch).** The agency wave: **Rwanda** created a
  **National AI Agency** ([[rwanda-national-ai-agency]], [[paula-ingabire]]); **Ethiopia**
  launched the homegrown **[[mesob-platform]]** ([[ethiopian-ai-institute]]); **Nigeria**
  has a National AI Strategy and **N-Atlas** LLM but is blocked by government **data
  silos**; **Kenya** eyes an **[[openai]]** Academy. See [[RWA]], [[ETH]], [[NGA]], [[KEN]].
- **AI-sovereignty scaffolding hardens — regional and national (2026-03/04).** From rhetoric to
  institutions. At its 4th Regional STI Conference (Kigali) the **[[eac]]** moved to explore a
  **regional AI Policy Roadmap** and act as a "unified AI Sovereignty bloc" across its eight
  members — [[paula-ingabire]]: "the economic value generated by AI in East Africa accrues to
  East Africans" ([[XEA]], [[gov.regional]]). **Ghana**'s Cabinet approved a **$250m national AI
  computing centre** plus a **National AI Strategy 2023–2033** (8 pillars, a Responsible AI Office;
  with [[smart-africa]], GIZ and The Future Society; public launch 24 Apr 2026), positioning Accra
  as West Africa's AI hub ([[ghana-national-ai-strategy]], [[GHA]], [[gov.policy]],
  [[finance.new]]). **Nigeria**'s NITDA DG **[[kashifu-inuwa]]** (GITEX Africa) is building
  **national software infrastructure** and a data-sovereignty policy — citing the EU DMA/DSA and an
  executive order making all digital infrastructure critical national infrastructure — framed as
  *"not banning China"* but "keeping the intelligence in our country" ([[NGA]], [[tech.industry]]).

- **Frugal and locally-adapted AI (2026-03/04).** Two studies sharpen the case that imported
  models are not enough. **[[nasa-harvest]]**'s **[[catherine-nakalembe]]** found Western-trained
  AI **could not recognise local crops** in western Kenya — her team collected **5m+ images**
  in two weeks to map cropland across Kenya/Uganda/Senegal — warning un-adapted systems
  "fail spectacularly" and risk **digital colonialism** ([[KEN]], [[UGA]], [[SEN]],
  [[digital.rural]]). Separately, with US/Chinese firms running **>90% of AI data centres**
  (Oxford) and adoption growing ~2x faster in rich countries, a **frugal-AI** movement builds
  smaller open-weight models on low-cost hardware for **data sovereignty**; Cambridge's Frugal
  AI Hub is in talks with **[[KEN]]/[[NGA]]** ([[tech.innovate]], [[capacity.research]]).

- **"Sovereignty = leverage, not self-sufficiency" — the analyst cluster (2026, Substack batch).**
  Named analysts converge on redefining AI sovereignty. **Adebowale** (Oxford Frontier AI Forum,
  30 May 2026): sovereignty is **leverage, not self-sufficiency** — Africa's leverage is
  data diversity, data-protection regimes and open-source models, against <1% of global DC capacity
  and ~80% of traffic on foreign servers. **Mbaya** ("controlling the keys vs owning the building"):
  the Nadella/Sunak Davos-2026 "portfolio risk-management" framing strips context, and
  54 nations without a shared definition negotiate fragmented and weak. **Nagadya**'s manifesto:
  Africa's building blocks (AU Continental AI Strategy, the Kigali **[[africa-ai-declaration]]**,
  Rwanda's Responsible AI Office, Kenya's data-sovereignty principle) all miss the **informal
  economy** and should build from mobile money and 2,000+ languages. **Okolo**: corporate/
  philanthropic **capture** (Google/Meta/Microsoft helped draft Nigeria's strategy; Brazil
  diluted its AI Act after Big-Tech lobbying), urging regional coordination via the AU. **Munyua**
  reads Kenya's **[[kenya-ai-bill-2026]]** as an **[[eu-ai-act]]** transplant regulating a stack
  Kenya did not build; **Mutiso** (VoxDev): AI is *not* a leapfrog technology since it depends
  on power, connectivity and data. **Contradiction (recorded, not reconciled):** the Kigali

- **A unified African voice in global AI governance (2026-07).** At the inaugural **Global
  Dialogue on AI Governance** (Geneva, alongside AI for Good / WSIS Forum 2026), the
  [[african-union]] Commission (Commissioner Lerato Mataboge) pressed for a coordinated,
  Africa-centred position anchored in the Continental AI Strategy, AU Data Policy Framework and

  ([[un-ai-scientific-panel]]) presented its Preliminary Report; Mataboge met the panel's African

  not just adoption. See [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]]. Source:

- **The US–China AI-diffusion contest reaches Africa (2026-03).** Two moves cast Africa as a stack
  battleground. Washington launched **[[tech-corps]]** (Kratsios, India AI Impact Summit) — thousands
  of US technical volunteers offering "last-mile" deployment of American AI in partner-nation
  health/education/agriculture, steering countries onto the US stack and off Chinese
  equivalents, extending the [[american-ai-exports-program]] and America's AI Action Plan; the US
  still draws **~93%** of global LLM site visits. Against it, China's **[[deepseek]]** and other cheap
  open-weight models (Chinese open-weight usage grew **1.2%→~30%** in a year) spread on affordability
  — **11–14%** of African chatbot use, **~20%** in [[ETH]] and [[ZWE]] — atop Belt-and-Road
  fibre/handset dominance, while [[microsoft]] races to train **3m Africans** by 2026 and push
  M365/Copilot to [[mtn-group]]'s **~300m** subscribers. See [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]],
  [[include.divides]]. Sources:

- **AI "non-alignment" as a middle-power doctrine (2026-02).** [[martin-tisne]] (Le Grand Continent,
  as the India summit opened) argues only the US and China can hold a *fully sovereign* AI stack,
  so middle powers — he names **France, Germany, Nigeria, India, Morocco** — should build a
  **non-aligned** movement around **open, resilient, shared** stacks rather than chase national
  self-sufficiency. As models commoditise, value shifts to the **data and application layers**;
  priority investments are open-source and data innovation, not raw compute. A counter-frame to
  both the US export push and Chinese lock-in. See [[data.open]], [[NGA]], [[MAR]], [[sovereignty]].
  Source: [[2026-02-16 Martin Tisné  le non-alignement à l'ère de l'IA]].
- **AU–EU AI-governance co-creation (2026-01).** [[ecdpm]] analysts propose the [[african-union]]
  and **[[european-union]]** jointly build a human-centric AI-governance framework blending the EU
  AI Act with the [[au-continental-ai-strategy]] and AU Data Policy Framework — beyond a
  "standards-setter vs standards-taker" (digital-colonialism) dynamic; the 7th AU–EU summit
  (Nov 2025) declaration notably **omitted** the AU frameworks. See [[geopol.eu]], [[gov.legislate]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-01-21 How Africa and Europe could create global AI governance rules]].
- **Voice AI to reach feature-phone users (2026-01).** Editorial analysis on voice/NLP AI to
  bring hundreds of millions of feature-phone users into digital services: Zambia's
  **Ask Viamo Anything** ([[viamo]]; 59% women users per GSMA), MTN's voice-first "Miss Baza"
  (4 languages, MWC Kigali 2025), citing the [[african-telecommunications-union]] and
  [[axian-telecom]] — AI as an inclusion bridge across SSA's 2,000+ languages. See [[ZMB]],
  [[GHA]], [[include.divides]], [[capacity.literacy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].
- **Algorithmic colonialism — the critical frame (Birhane).** The wiki's `colonialism`-lens
  reading of African AI rests on **[[abeba-birhane]]**: *The Algorithmic Colonization of
  Africa* (2020) argues Western tech monopolies ([[meta]], [[google]] et al.) reproduce colonial
  domination through control of digital infrastructure, treating Africans as "data-producing
  human natural resources"; *Narratives and Counternarratives on Data Sharing in Africa* (FAccT
  '21) shows how **non-African "deficit narratives"** distort the continent's data-sharing debate.
  See [[geopol.usa]], [[data.open]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Sources:
  [[2020-08-21 Algorithmic Colonisation of Africa]],
  [[2021-03-01 Narratives and Counternarratives on Data Sharing in Africa (Abebe, Birhane et al, FAccT 2021)]].
- **Global AI governance and the Global South (2026-07).** The UN's push — an "IPCC for AI"
  ([[un-ai-scientific-panel]]) and a leaders' "commission of the willing" — is read critically:
  the Panel's Preliminary Report is faulted for not demonstrating independence from industry and
  states, and the Global South's demand for real rule-making power (vs "a performative seat") runs
  up against 193-state consensus and US/EU/China rivalry. See [[gov.standards]], [[gov.regional]].
  Sources: [[2026-07-08 The UN Scientific Panel on AI's Preliminary Report Does Not Establish Its Independence]],
