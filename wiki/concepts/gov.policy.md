# Strategies, plans and policies (`gov.policy`)

National digital strategy, ownership and the financing conditions around it.

## Key material

- **National data policies as sovereignty instruments (2026-07).** Three tracks:
  **[[ZAF]]** South Africa's EU-adequacy **[[popia]]** vs its localization-leaning Draft
  Data & Cloud Policy ([[research-ict-africa]] warns it obstructs AfCFTA flows);
  **[[KEN]]** Kenya's **Draft Data Governance Policy (2026)**, workshopped with the US
  ([[us-department-of-state]]); and **[[NGA]]** Nigeria **harmonising** its fragmented
  digital-economy rulebook under Minister **[[bosun-tijani]]** (an NCC/NITDA/NDPC committee).
  See [[gov.protect]], [[south-africa--gov-protect]]. Sources:
  [[2025-01-01 South African Digital Sovereignty at the Crossroad of Securitization and Development (Chapter 4) - Digital Sovereignty in the BRICS Countries]],
  [[2026-07-10 Kenya and U.S. Discuss Draft Data Governance Policy to Strengthen Digital Economy]],
  [[2026-07-08 FG Halts Enforcement of New Regulations on Internet Platforms]].
- **DPI as a policy agenda, not a tech stack (2026, UNDP).** UNDP's continental paper calls
  DPI "a combination of technology, governance, standards, legal frameworks, financing and
  institutional capacity," urging African-led national-scale strategy over isolated pilots.
  See [[XAF]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa - UNDP]].
- **Financial sovereignty as the new policy frame (2026-02→05).** The **[[african-development-bank]]**
  has made "African financial sovereignty" the organising idea of continental economic policy: its
  **[[nafa]]** (New African Financial Architecture, launched Feb 2026 under president
  **[[sidi-ould-tah]]**) and **African Economic Outlook 2026** argue Africa's **$1.3tn/yr** SDG
  financing gap is an allocation problem, not a shortage — **$1.43tn** is unlockable domestically,
  including **$469bn/yr** from digitising tax administration — as the aid model frays. Policy pivots
  from external finance to domestic capital, pensions and tax. See [[finance.new]],
  [[gov.regional]], [[XSS]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-26 The AfDB's push for African financial sovereignty, and a tech investment rebound]],
  [[2026-05-26 AfDB says Africa must fund its own growth as aid model frays]].
- **AI-sovereignty draft policies (2026).** Nigeria, **[[EGY]] Egypt** and Kenya each published
  **draft AI strategies** naming dependence on US tech (Google, Microsoft, Nvidia, Meta) as a
  security threat; **South Africa's** draft was **withdrawn (Apr 2026)** after AI tools generated
  fake citations. Backdrop: Africa holds **<1%** of global data-centre capacity, a **$60bn Africa AI
  Fund** (Kigali, Apr 2025) and the **[[au-continental-ai-strategy]]** (Jul 2024). Rachel Adams
  ([[global-center-on-ai-governance]]) reframes the goal as **meaningful control** — procurement
  rules, local infrastructure/skills, African-language datasets — not autarky. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-21 Pushing back from Big Tech Africa's hard road to AI sovereignty]].
- **Continental harmonisation as policy ask (2026-04, Nairobi).** A ministerial roundtable at the
  **Connected Africa Summit** (convened by Kenya's CS William Kabogo) called for harmonised
  data-protection for cross-border flows and interoperable digital ID/payments, flagging **taxes and
  tariffs** as affordability barriers — Kenya positioning as a hub for continental digital-policy
  coordination. See [[gov.regional]], [[KEN]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-28 African Ministers call for harmonised policies and infrastructure investment at Connected Africa Summit Roundtable]].
- **National sovereignty strategies (2026-03/04).** A cluster of country roadmaps:
  - *[[SEN]] Senegal* — the **New Deal Technologique** ([[new-deal-technologique]]), founding-launched
    **Feb 2025** (Horizon 2034, ~**$1.7bn**) and marked at one year in 2026 — four pillars (digital
    sovereignty, public-service digitisation, digital economy, hub positioning), a National
    Interoperability Platform, in-country data centres/cloud, and a Universal Connectivity Project
    targeting **>1m** people with free access in 2026; the **2026 digital budget is CFA81.06bn**
    (~60% investment), against an **EGDI 135th/193 (0.5162, 2024)**. Sources:
    [[2025-02-27 Senegal unveils New Deal Technologique digital transformation strategy]],
    [[2025-12-09 Senegal allocates 60% of 2026 digital ministry budget to investment]],
    [[2026-03-25 Senegal Accelerates Digital Sovereignty with Rollout of Flagship Technology Initiatives]].
  - *[[CIV]] Côte d'Ivoire* convened a Yamoussoukro seminar to **align** its Digital
    Development, Cybersecurity, "Zero Paper by 2030", Data Governance 2030 and National AI
    strategies — coordination over proliferation (first substantive CIV material). See
    [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
    [[2026-04-12 Côte d'Ivoire convenes strategic seminar to align digital transformation agenda]].
  - *[[NGA]] Nigeria* — [[nitda]] DG [[kashifu-inuwa]] (GITEX Africa) framed **digital
    sovereignty as value capture, not exclusion**: build national software infrastructure and a
    data-sovereignty policy, inviting hyperscalers to partner with local firms ("capture that
    value here") rather than ban China, citing the EU DMA/DSA and an executive order making all
    digital infrastructure critical national infrastructure. See [[gov.standards]],
    [[tech.industry]], [[geopol.china]]. Source:
    [[2026-04-14 Nigeria Seeks Stronger Digital Sovereignty, National Software Infrastructure]].
  - *[[GHA]] Ghana* approved its **National AI Strategy 2023–2033** (8 pillars, Responsible AI
    Office) with a $250m compute centre ([[ghana-national-ai-strategy]], [[finance.new]],
    [[tech.ai]]). Source: [[2026-04-03 Ghana approves $250M AI Hub & National AI Strategy]].
- **National digital strategies — second wave (2026-01→03).** More country roadmaps, most
  flying the sovereignty flag:
  - *[[ETH]] Ethiopia* launched **[[digital-ethiopia-2030]]** as a "declaration of digital
    sovereignty": 100% 5G in 100 cities and 128m mobile subscribers, every adult on the
    **[[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]** ID and a **7×** rise in digital payments ([[telebirr]]) by 2030,
    GERD green power at **$0.03–0.05/kWh** to host a "Cloud of Africa," a 5-Million-Coders programme, a
    sovereign cloud + Amharic **Mela** model, and an East African Digital Corridor with Kenya aligned to
    the AU DTS and [[afcfta]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]], [[tech.ai]], [[infra.energy]],
    [[gov.regional]]. Source:
    [[2026-01-27 Digital Ethiopia 2030 Charting a Sovereign Path to Prosperity]].
  - *[[LBR]] Liberia* unveiled **[[digital-liberia-strategy]]** ("Equity, Sovereignty and Innovation,"
    2025–2029): eight priority areas including universal connectivity, a national digital addressing
    system, **cybersecurity & data sovereignty** and e-governance, tied to the ARREST Agenda
    ([[lta-liberia]]). Source:
    [[2026-02-03 Liberia Unveils 5-Year 'Digital Liberia' Strategy to Boost Sovereignty and Innovation]].
  - *[[BFA]] Burkina Faso* is running **PACTDIGITAL-BF** (12 flagship projects to 2030; ~**$170m**
    World Bank IDA + national funds, in force since Sept 2024), commissioning two Ouagadougou mini data
    centres (Jan 2026) under a **"zero data abroad"** localization rule ([[bf-ministry-digital-transition]],
    [[world-bank]]). See [[infra.store]], [[gov.regional]], [[XWA]]. Source:
    [[2026-03-23 Burkina Faso steps into the future with digital investment]].
  - *[[MWI]] Malawi* is validating a **Draft National Digital Transformation Strategy** and **Draft
    National AI Strategy** (UNDP-backed, Lilongwe Feb 2026, with a "Shape Malawi's Digital and AI Future"
    feedback portal) after President Mutharika ordered all MDAs to digitise services "forthwith"
    (passports, ID, licences, procurement; a Digital Identity Wallet; 80% internet by 2026) — aligned to
    Malawi 2063 ([[malawi-ministry-of-ict]], [[undp]]). See [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Sources:
    [[2026-02-20 Malawi validates AI strategy, digital transformation policy - CAJ News Africa]],
    [[2026-02-16 Malawi President orders digital revolution]].
  - *[[MOZ]] Mozambique / [[SOM]] Somalia* — two fragile-state digital-government resets: Mozambique's
    President Chapo launched an overhaul modelled on **Kenya and Rwanda** (a Multi-Sector Technical
    Commission to draft an interoperability roadmap by mid-2026; a new Ministry of Communications &
    Digital Transformation; the country ranks **177/193** on the 2024 UN e-Gov index), and Somalia
    convened its first **National DPI Summit** (Mogadishu, Feb 2026) to align CBS, NCA, NIRA and its DPA
    around a single interoperable vision. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]].
    Sources:
    [[2026-02-21 Mozambique Launches Digital Government Overhaul, Looking to Kenya and Rwanda as Models]],
    [[2026-01-29 Mogadishu to Host First National Digital Public Infrastructure Summit; Key Agencies to Map Out Digital Transformation Strategy]].
- **Fintech policy as regional architecture — Nigeria (2026-01).** The **[[cbn]]**'s **Policy Insight
  Series 2025** ([[cbn-policy-insight-series-2025]], "Shaping the Future of Fintech in Nigeria") sets an
  18-month, three-pillar roadmap — a **Single Regulatory Window**, **regulatory passporting** (bilateral
  pilots with Ghana, Kenya, Senegal and South Africa for mutual licence recognition) and a **Responsible
  AI in Finance Hub** — pitched as the infrastructure to make [[ecowas]]/[[afcfta]] fintech
  integration real (87.5% of Nigerian fintechs say compliance cost constrains innovation). National
  policy authored as a regional template. See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[GHA]],
  [[SEN]]. Source: [[2026-02-06 Nigeria just raised the bar for West African Fintech]].
- **The external counter-pressure — Washington's data-sovereignty doctrine (2026-02→03).** A State
  Department cable (18 Feb 2026, signed Rubio) directs US diplomats to **counter** data-localization and
  data-sovereignty laws worldwide — naming GDPR, promoting the US-led Global Cross-Border Privacy Rules
  Forum — reframing the "sovereignty" agenda these strategies pursue as a barrier to American
  cloud/AI, the backdrop against which African digital-sovereignty policy is now written. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[geopol.usa]], [[XGL]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-12 How Trump's Data Regulation Crackdown Undermines Digital Sovereignty]].
- **Regional AI-policy harmonisation (2026-03).** At the 4th EAC Regional STI Conference
  (Kigali) the **[[eac]]** explored a **regional AI Policy Roadmap** to act as a single "AI
  sovereignty bloc" across its 8 members; Rwanda's [[paula-ingabire]] framed data sovereignty so
  that "the economic value generated by AI in East Africa accrues to East Africans." See
  [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[tech.ai]], [[XEA]], [[RWA]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-31 EAC moves to create unified AI Sovereignty bloc]].
- **From "rail" to "train" — the UNECA sovereignty pivot (2026-04).** At the **58th [[uneca]]
  Conference of Ministers** roundtable, ministers and industry ([[african-union]] AU AI Strategy,
  GSMA, Africa Data Centres) pressed Africa to move from DPI-as-rail to owning the "intelligence":
  Africa generates **<1%** of global data despite ~20% of population and must **10x** its
  data-centre capacity; ~**1bn** Africans remain offline within mobile reach; and national
  statistical systems need funding to measure technology's GDP impact. The close: translate the
  AU AI Strategy into funded local frameworks with dedicated data-governance budgets. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[data.statistics]], [[tech.ai]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]].
- **The fiscal turn — mobile money as tax infrastructure (2026-05/06).** Under debt pressure,
  **Kenya, Nigeria and Senegal** are folding mobile-money rails into **tax collection**, trading
  financial-inclusion framing for traceability (risking cash flight among fee-sensitive users);
  **[[MWI]] Malawi's** Electronic Invoicing System now yields **>$1.2m/day** with ~**7,500 of ~9,000**
  VAT operators migrated — digital-tax-as-infrastructure in practice. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[include.access]]. Sources:
  [[2026-05-29 Africa's Mobile Money Networks Are Becoming Tax Infrastructure]],
  [[2026-06-03 Malawi reaps digital tax gains]].
- **Modernising sector policy — Nigeria (2026-05).** The **[[ncc]]** is rewriting the **National
  Telecommunications Policy** (first since 2000) to fit cloud, AI and data-centre realities —
  linking stale policy to lost hyperscaler investment ([[NGA]], [[infra.store]]).
  Source: [[2026-05-22 Why global hyperscalers are avoiding Nigeria despite Africa's biggest market]].
- **Official-statistics systems at a crossroads (2026, PARIS21).** The [[paris21]] discussion paper
  **[[2026-07-11 data-systems-crossroads]]** diagnoses a compounding crisis for national statistical offices —
  **2024–25 donor-funding cuts**, AI disruption, expanding mandates and eroding trust — and frames a
  "fork in the road" between optimisation and transformation. The evidence base for policy is itself
  a policy problem, acute for African NSOs post-aid-shock. See [[data.statistics]],
  [[capacity.research]].
- **[[ZMB]] Zambia (2025).** [[dzap]] is anchored in the **8th National Development
  Plan**, the **National e-Government Plan 2023–2026** and the AU Digital
  Transformation Strategy, and aligned to the World Bank CPF FY25–29, WB Digital
  Strategy (Jul 2025) and the IDA-21 Digital Policy Commitment. The stated intent is
  **vendor independence** — a homegrown, open-standards ID the government can run itself
  ([[dpi.id]]). The `colonialism` lens holds the tension open: that self-reliance is
  written into a **World Bank–financed** operation with donor TA (ID4D/UNDP/UNHCR) and
  Bank results frameworks.

- **Global (2025, [[david-eaves]]).** A market-shaping critique of digital-sovereignty
  policy: governments should resist "industrial policy" quick fixes (backing domestic
  telco champions that create new lock-in) and instead use **procurement + de facto
  standards** to maximise agency and drive down cost. Frames the choice as "*Can we move
  our workloads?*" not "*Can we own our stack?*" See [[gov.standards]], [[tech.industry]].
  Cross-reads with Zambia's vendor-independent approach above.

- **[[KEN]] cloud-first (2024).** Kenya's **Cloud Policy** adopts cloud-first for the
  public sector — expected to anchor sustained domestic data-centre demand
  ([[infra.store]]). Source: [[2026-06-23 Africa's Data Centre Boom Where The New Facilities Are Being Built]].
- **[[ZAF]] execution gap (2026-07).** Policy frameworks existed "on paper" at [[sita]]
  but "were not consistently implemented, enforced or monitored" — strategy without
  delivery capacity stalls digital transformation. Source:
  [[2026-07-07 South Africa's state IT agency is slowing government tech rollout]].
- **[[GHA]] Ghana — sequencing strategy (2026-07).** Ghana is using the **UNESCO Readiness
  Assessment Methodology (RAM)** to separate implementation-ready projects from those
  needing development, with [[undp]] pressing for **government co-financing** to unlock
  more donor funding — a rare case of a readiness-diagnostic driving the roadmap.
  See [[capacity.training]], [[undp]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-07 Ghana and UNDP Strengthen Partnership on Digital Transformation Projects]].
- **[[NAM]] Namibia — long-horizon strategy (2026-07).** The **Windhoek People-Centred
  Smart City Strategy (2026–2036)** anchors a decade-long, Chinese-financed modernisation
  of municipal services — sovereignty language ("secure, inclusive, knowledge-based
  economy") on external finance and a foreign vendor ([[geopol.china]],
  [[china-namibia-smart-city]]). Source:
  [[2026-07-08 China funds Namibia digital infrastructure]].

- **Rwanda's national DPI strategy (2026-03).** At its first **DPI Day** (9 Mar 2026, run by the
  **[[rwanda-information-society-authority]]** with Access to Finance Rwanda), Rwanda unveiled a
  **[[rwanda-dpi-strategy]]** to make its independently-built platforms interoperable — "roads and
  electricity for the digital economy" — positioning Rwanda as a **regional AI hub** by pooling
  reliable cross-sector data. It builds on the 2025 Rwanda Centre for DPI and flags a digital-ID
  rollout by June 2026 and the interoperable payment platform e-Kash as early steps. See
  [[RWA]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-20 Rwanda Launches Digital Public Infrastructure Strategy to Lay Groundwork for AI and Inclusive Growth]].
- **Foundational addressing as digital-economy policy — Nigeria (2026-03).** Nigeria approved a
  GIS-enabled alphanumeric **[[nigeria-digital-postcode-system]]** (Minister [[bosun-tijani]]), pitched
  as a "foundational system" for national planning, emergency response, logistics/e-commerce and
  government service delivery — addressing infrastructure treated as a digital-economy enabler. See
  [[NGA]], [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-05 Nigeria approves modern digital postcode system]].
- **De-risking the infrastructure layer (2026, DIF).** The EU/Germany/Finland-funded
  **[[digital-investment-facility]]** (implemented by GIZ + HAUS under [[global-gateway]]/D4D Hub)
  supplies technical and financial advisory to make greenfield/brownfield **data-centre and IXP**
  projects in Sub-Saharan Africa bankable (min €5m, revenue-generating, ESG-screened) and publishes
  regional data-centre market briefs — policy meeting capital where sovereignty ambitions
  need physical infrastructure. See [[infra.store]], [[infra.connect]], [[finance.new]], [[XSS]].
  Source: [[2026-01-01 Data Governance in Africa - Digital Investment Facility]].
- **DPI is a political choice, not a neutral tech project (2026, Dube).** A named-analyst commentary
  argues African DPI reshapes how power is exercised — risking function creep, surveillance and
  exclusion — and that norms exist (the [[african-union]] Malabo Convention, AU Data Policy Framework,
  ACHPR Declaration) but practice lags: under-resourced DPAs, rare human-rights impact assessments,
  and procurement shaped by vendor rather than public interest. Reclaiming DPI governance as a
  democratic project, not a technical rollout. See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.id]], [[include.divides]],
  [[XSS]]. Source: [[2026-01-01 (2) Digital Public Infrastructure and the Politics of Data in Africa]].

- **From declarations to measurable implementation — WSIS+20 (2026-07).** At the WSIS Forum 2026, an
  **[[aficta]]** + **[[nitda]]** session argued Africa has the strategies but must shift to
  **measurable** delivery: [[uneca]]'s **Africa 2035 Digital Implementation Roadmap** (9 pillars, 15
  priority indicators, unified reporting template) and a proposed WSIS Implementation Maturity Index.
  Nigeria's ICT sector cited at 13–14% of GDP; ~0.6% of global data-centre capacity continent-wide.
  See [[gov.regional]], [[capacity.training]], [[NGA]], [[XAF]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Africa urged to turn WSIS+20 commitments into measurable digital progress]].
- **AU–EU AI-governance co-creation (2026-01).** [[ecdpm]] analysts urge the [[african-union]] and
  **[[european-union]]** to co-create an AI-governance framework blending the EU AI Act with the
  [[au-continental-ai-strategy]] and **[[au-data-policy-framework]]** — the 7th AU–EU summit (Nov 2025)
  declaration omitted the AU frameworks. See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-21 How Africa and Europe could create global AI governance rules]].
- **Pan-African 2025 policy year-in-review (cited lightly).** A lead-rich year-in-review notes only
  **16 African countries** had launched national AI strategies by mid-2025, **46/54** have data-
  protection laws, and the [[afcfta-digital-trade-protocol]] gained 8 technical annexes (Feb 2025) —
  useful as a pointer to primaries, not hardened here (per intake discipline). See
  [[au-digital-transformation-strategy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-12 (3) Africa's Digital Policy Review 2025 in Perspective and Outlook for 2026]].

- **[[GAB]] Gabon — "digital sovereignty" strategy on a +156% budget.** Post-transition Gabon
  frames its digital push (the WB "Gabon Digital"/eGabon programme) as sovereignty: a **2026 digital
  budget of 82bn CFA, +156.2%** on 2025; a 2025 ordinance mandating public administrations to digitise,
  interconnect and integrate the private sector; a 3 Oct 2025 directive to digitise financial agencies.
  Benchmarks (2024-25): **EGDI 121st/193**, **ITU IDI 76.1/100 (11th in Africa)**, **GCI 2024 Tier 4**.
  Execution lags the ambition (low WB disbursement). See [[GAB]], [[infra.store]], [[dpi.govtech]].
  Source: [[2025-10-24-gabon-2026-digital-budget-156pct]].

- **National strategies — STP, SLE, SOM (2020–2030).** Three more roadmaps: **[[STP]] São Tomé and
  Príncipe**'s **2020 National Strategy for Digital Governance**; **[[SLE]] Sierra Leone**'s **National
  Data Strategy** update, aligned to the **AU Data Policy Framework** (2025); and **[[SOM]] Somalia**'s
  **Digital Transformation Strategy 2025–2030** (ITU-supported validation, Oct 2025; EGDI 191/193, 2024).
  See [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[STP]], [[SLE]], [[SOM]].
