# Training and skills (`capacity.training`)

Building the digital-skills base for citizens and the workforce.

## Key material

- **Professionalising Data Protection Officers (2026-07, Tanzania).** Tanzania's **[[pdpc-tanzania]]**
  (Personal Data Protection Commission) and the **Open University of Tanzania** launched a national
  **Certified Data Protection Officer (CDPO) Programme** — OUT trains and assesses, PDPC certifies — to
  build the compliance workforce (government, finance, telecoms, health, education) as digital services
  expand. The skills-side complement to the enforcement-capacity gap flagged in the ID4Africa DPA formula:
  statutes and regulators exist, but qualified DPOs are scarce. See [[gov.protect]], [[TZA]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-14 OUT, PDPC Launch National Data Protection Programme]].
- **Thin execution teams (2026-06, DPI export).** A recurring DPI-adoption constraint:
  "many countries do not have enough technical capacity even when they understand the
  architecture. Skilled engineers migrate quickly from places such as Fiji and Morocco,
  leaving governments with thin execution teams." [[MAR]] Morocco built **Maharat** (on
  India's open-source Sunbird) to train 26,000 preschool educators. See [[geopol.india]],
  [[mosip]]. Source: [[2026-06-17 They're taking DPI to the world - The Times of India]].

- **[[ZMB]] Zambia (2025).** [[dzap]] Component 3 (**US$5M**) funds basic and advanced
  digital skills through **Digital Transformation Centres (DTCs)**, plus a mobile
  e-learning app carrying a basic literacy curriculum. Aim: raise e-service uptake and
  build an employment-ready workforce able to use emerging tech (AI/ML). Explicitly
  linked to social protection and education, with private-sector partnerships explored.

- **This ingest (2026-07).** Skills recur as the binding constraint across new items:
  Ghana's **AI & digital-governance capacity-building for the Civil Service** (multi-cohort,
  gender-inclusion; with [[undp]] — [[GHA]]); Namibia's Chinese-financed **skills and
  workforce development** under the Smart City programme ([[NAM]], [[capacity.training]]);
  and the Sierra Leone ID MoU's emphasis on **local technical capability and knowledge
  transfer** so national institutions can run the platform themselves ([[SLE]],
  [[gov.standards]]). Sources:
  [[2026-07-07 Ghana and UNDP Strengthen Partnership on Digital Transformation Projects]],
  [[2026-07-08 China funds Namibia digital infrastructure]],
  [[2026-07-08 Sierra Leone partners with Bhutan, Sign on digital identity platform]].

- **Skilling programmes, this batch (2026-04–06).** The training layer expanded on several fronts:
  - *Community labs.* [[liquid-c2]] / [[microsoft]] launched a solar-powered **mobile digital lab**
    (Royal Bafokeng, North West SA) teaching Microsoft 365/Copilot and Power Platform, building on the
    **Mthatha** Innovation Digital Skills Centre — **515 learners trained, 96% pass rate, 149 placed**
    ([[ZAF]], [[capacity.literacy]], [[digital.rural]]). Source:
    [[2026-05-27 Liquid, Microsoft unveil mobile digital lab for North West youth]].
  - *Corporate skilling at scale.* [[microsoft-sa-2026-expansion]] ties its R5.4bn SA cloud
    investment to a pledge to **skill 1 million South Africans by 2026** and pay for **50,000** youth
    certification exams, plus a SABC+ AI-fluency channel (~1.9m registered users) ([[ZAF]]). Sources:
    [[2026-04-15 Microsoft secures land for future South Africa data centre growth]],
    [[2026-04-17 Microsoft picks South Africa for $329 million expansion in cloud infrastructure and AI training]].
  - *Telco pipeline.* [[orange]] pledged (Africa Forward Summit, Nairobi) to **train >3 million young
    people** in AI, cybersecurity, cloud and digital entrepreneurship by **2030**, doubling Orange
    Digital Centres from 50 to 100 ([[XAF]], [[geopol.eu]]). Source:
    [[2026-05-23 Orange-backed subsea cable to connect Nigeria, 19 other countries]].
  - *Peer-learning for officials.* The [[adli]] (Africa Data Leadership Initiative, run by [[dial]])
    2026 cohort — six countries across three trade corridors — is a peer-to-peer network training
    senior practitioners to implement the AfCFTA Digital Identity Annex, treating capacity as a
    *governance* skill, not just a technical one ([[NGA]], [[KEN]], [[gov.regional]]). Source:
    [[2026-05-27 Africa's economic future hinges on breaking down silos. This ADLI cohort advances integration]].
  - *Founder capacity.* Google's [[google-for-startups-accelerator-africa]] Class 10 adds mentorship
    and AI/cloud technical workshops for 15 startups (programme alumni: 106 startups, >2,800 jobs)
    ([[tech.innovate]]). Source:
    [[2026-04-21 Meet the 15 Startups Joining the Google for Startups Accelerator Africa Class 10]].

- **AI-skills programmes at population scale (2026-03/04).** A wave of AI-literacy and training
  initiatives, dominated by hyperscalers:
  - *Advanced AI education.* **[[google-deepmind]]**'s **AI Research Foundations** curriculum
    ([[google-ai-research-foundations]], co-developed with UCL, free on Google Skills) expanded
    in Africa, with Google.org funding lecturer training via Nigeria's FATE Foundation and AIMS —
    reframing Africa's **1.8% share of global AI research** as the gap to close ([[NGA]],
    [[capacity.research]]). Source: [[2026-03-17 Broadening advanced AI education across Africa]].
  - *Practical AI for MSMEs.* Google relaunched **[[hustle-academy]]** (with UpSkill Universe),
    widening from entrepreneurs to employees/students/jobseekers across SSA (**>18,000** SMEs
    trained since 2022) ([[XSS]], [[include.access]]). Source:
    [[2026-04-10 Google Expands Hustle Academy to Train Africans in Practical AI Skills -]].
  - *National skilling deals.* [[dhet-south-africa]] signed with Google for **5,000 certification
    scholarships** in AI/digital skills across universities, TVET and CET colleges (train-the-
    trainer; rural/peri-urban focus) against 43.8% youth unemployment; and **[[microsoft-elevate]]**
    — profiled through SA National AI Skills Director [[tiara-pathon]] — casts itself as the
    "connective tissue" for AI literacy as a *public good* (SABC+, Civic AI, Ikamva Digital with
    GIZ) ([[ZAF]], [[capacity.literacy]], [[include.divides]]). Sources:
    [[2026-03-30 South Africa Teams Up With Google to Train 5,000 in Digital Skills]],
    [[2026-03-27 Tiara Pathon on Turning AI Literacy From a Privilege Into a Public Good]].
  - *China and the Gulf as trainers.* [[huawei]] committed free AI training for **3,000 girls** in
    Ghana's Girls in ICT programme (feeding the One Million Coders programme; tied to the $250m
    push, [[ghana-national-ai-strategy]]) ([[GHA]], [[geopol.china]]); and Zimbabwe's flagship
    **1.5-million coding programme** with the **[[uae]]** anchors an AI-readiness drive alongside
    free public Wi-Fi and newly licensed [[starlink]] ([[ZWE]], [[infra.connect]]). Sources:
    [[2026-03-05 Ghana strikes AI training deal with Chinese multinational amid $250m tech investment push]],
    [[2026-04-07 Zimbabwe switches on free wifi to power AI future]].

- **AI-skilling deepens — Nigeria, Ethiopia, Rwanda, Gabon (2026-01–03).** Several AI-specific
  tracks landed this ingest:
  - *Corporate delivery at scale.* **[[teknowledge]]** became delivery partner for **Phase 2** of
    [[microsoft]]'s AI National Skilling Initiative in [[NGA]] Nigeria, committing to **directly
    train 10,000** (youth, women, developers) via universities and the NYSC — after Phase 1 reached
    **50,000+** and certified **3,000+** across Microsoft AI tracks, coordinated with the 3MTT
    programme ([[capacity.literacy]]). Source:
    [[2026-03-09 Over 10,000 Nigerians to Receive Advanced AI Training Through TeKnowledge-Microsoft Partnership]].
  - *National pipelines.* [[ETH]] Ethiopia's **5 Million Coders** programme and the new
    **[[ai-unipod-ethiopia]]** (AI University Innovation Pod — [[ethiopian-ai-institute]] + Addis
    Ababa University + [[undp]] + Timbuktoo) anchor the skills pillar of **[[digital-ethiopia-2030]]**,
    converting a population 70% under 30 into a digital workforce ([[tech.ai]], [[capacity.research]]).
    Sources: [[2026-02-19 Ethiopia launches AI UniPod to drive innovation]],
    [[2026-01-27 Digital Ethiopia 2030 Charting a Sovereign Path to Prosperity]].
  - *"AI for governments" as a skilling channel.* [[anthropic]]'s Rwanda MoU
    ([[anthropic-rwanda-mou]]) bundles **AI-literacy training and API credits for government
    developers** with the **Chidi** Claude-based learning companion (via the Nov-2025 ALX partnership,
    2,000 Claude Pro licences) reaching students across **8 African countries** — skilling as the
    on-ramp to a commercial stack, with the accountability caveat flagged under [[tech.ai]] ([[RWA]],
    [[geopol.usa]]). Sources:
    [[2026-03-17 Anthropic is Becoming the Backbone of Rwanda's Government. But Who is Accountable]],
    [[2026-03-04 U.S.-based AI firm Anthropic bags first multi-sector government deal in Africa despite policy pressures at home]].
  - *Vendor skills-transfer.* [[GAB]] Gabon's **[[gabon-huawei-mou]]** makes "skills transfer for
    digital sovereignty" one of three pillars (expanding the Connected Schools programme) — the
    [[huawei]] training channel by which the Chinese stack seeds the market ([[geopol.china]]).
    Source: [[2026-01-05 Gabon and Huawei Forge Partnership to Boost National Digital Transformation]].
  The US **[[tech-corps]]** volunteer scheme (see [[geopol.usa]], [[tech.ai]]) is the geopolitical
  backdrop — technical advisers as a channel for stack adoption.

- **Public-sector and cyber skilling — Kenya, Nigeria (2026-07-12 batch).** Government skilling
  recurs: Kenya's **[[kenya-school-of-government]]** opened talks with [[huawei]] on a prospective
  MoU to train public servants in AI, cybersecurity, cloud and digital governance ([[KEN]],
  [[geopol.china]]); the EU-funded **[[kenya-cyber-resilience-project]]** ([[expertise-france]] +
  [[estdev]]) pairs infrastructure hardening with cyber-skills building ([[KEN]],
  [[infra.cybersec]]); and NITDA's **[[dl4all-nitda]]** digital-literacy drive (>480,000 trained,
  70%-by-2027 target) extends the base-skills layer ([[NGA]], [[capacity.literacy]]). Sources:
  [[2026-01-12 Kenya, Huawei to boost public sector digital skills]],
  [[2026-01-27 Kenya launches project to boost cyber security]],
  [[2026-01-23 NITDA Commits to Digital Inclusion for Persons with Disabilities]].
