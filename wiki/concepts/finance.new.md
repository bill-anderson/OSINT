# New investments (`finance.new`)

Investment flowing into African digital transformation. (A *specific* transaction is a
**deal entity** — e.g. [[dzap]] — while this concept tracks the investment picture.)

## Key material

- **Raxio equity top-up — frontier data centres (2026-07).** **[[raxio-group]]** lifted committed capital
  **above US$380m** via fresh equity from **Meridiam** and **Roha** (atop a 2025 US$100m IFC package +
  Proparco/EAAIF debt), funding Tier III colocation across Uganda, Ethiopia, Mozambique, DR Congo, Côte
  d'Ivoire and Angola (Tanzania next) — infrastructure capital flowing to the markets outside the ZAF/NGA/KEN
  cluster, with H1-2026 contracted power up **6×** year-on-year on AI-workload demand. See [[infra.store]].
  Source: [[2026-07-14 Raxio raises data centre funding]].
- **Data-centre capital wave (2026-06).** Construction market ~**$1.26bn (2024) → ~$3.06bn
  (2030)**. Named flows: [[us-dfc]] **$300m** to ADC ([[cassava-technologies]]); IFC
  **$100m** to Raxio; IXAfrica up to **$200m** debt; [[mtn-group]] **>$235m** two-phase
  Lagos build; [[airtel-africa]] 38 MW hyperscale. US development finance is positioned
  against Chinese capital ([[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]]). Source:
  [[2026-06-23 Africa's Data Centre Boom Where The New Facilities Are Being Built]].
- **Subsea capex (2026-07).** [[seacom]] 2.0 (25,000 km; **$1.5bn–$2bn** build) with
  private backers (Aga Khan Fund/IPS, Remgro, Sanlam) — private capital for continental
  connectivity. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Seacom plans 25,000km subsea cable from France to Singapore and South Africa]].
- **Bilateral & donor flows (this ingest, 2026-07).** Not all capital is private:
  **China → Namibia** ¥98m (~US$14.4m) for the Smart City pilot ([[china-namibia-smart-city]],
  [[NAM]], [[geopol.china]]); **World Bank** financing behind Malawi's Bomalathu ([[dmap]],
  [[MWI]]) and Nigeria's NIN drive ([[nigeria-id4d]], with AFD/EIB, [[NGA]]); and Ghana's
  planned **national fintech fund** tied to a Startup Act, seeking private co-investment
  ([[GHA]]). The concessional-vs-sovereign tension recurs across all of them. Sources:
  [[2026-07-08 China funds Namibia digital infrastructure]],
  [[2026-07-07 Malawi integrates immigration e-services]],
  [[2026-07-09 Nigeria seeks support to operationalize new digital ID law as NIN tops 136M]],
  [[2026-07-07 Ghana and UNDP Strengthen Partnership on Digital Transformation Projects]].

- **Hyperscaler & Gulf capital at scale (2026-07 batch).** **[[google]]** said it
  **exceeded its $1bn Africa** commitment (four continental hubs, Ghana AI lab, startup
  cohort); the **[[uae]]** pledged **$1bn** for African AI ([[uae-ai-for-development-initiative]]);
  **[[ifc]]** lent **$150m** to [[airtel-africa]] ([[ifc-airtel-africa-loan]]). But capital
  meets capacity limits: the **$1bn [[microsoft-g42-kenya-datacenter]]** stalled on power.
  See [[tech.ai]], [[infra.capacity]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-01 Google has exceeded $1 billion Africa investment target]],
  [[2025-11-22 UAE announces $1 billion initiative to expand AI in Africa]],
  [[2026-07-01 IFC and Airtel Africa Expand Digital Connectivity to Strengthen Jobs and Inclusion]],
  [[2026-07-09 Microsoft and G42 data center in Kenya stalled due to lack of power capacity]].
- **Financial-sovereignty framing & the 2025 VC rebound (2026-02–05).**
  [[african-development-bank]] president [[sidi-ould-tah]] made **[[nafa]]** (New African
  Financial Architecture) the centrepiece of a "financial sovereignty" pitch as external aid
  retreats: African external debt rose from **$220bn (2009) to $685bn (end-2023)**, with **>$90bn**
  due in 2026; the AfDB argues Africa could unlock **~$1.43tn/yr** domestically, including ~$469bn
  from digitising tax administration. Against that backdrop **Partech** logged **$4.1bn** in 2025
  African tech VC (**+25%**, strongest since 2022) — but debt financing was **41%** of it, the
  "Big Four" ([[KEN]]/[[ZAF]]/[[EGY]]/[[NGA]]) took **72%**, and domestic investors supplied
  **45%** (up from ~23%); fintech remained the largest sector ($1.49bn). Naming/venue vary across
  accounts (AU summit, Addis vs Abidjan launch); both trace to the same NAFA framework. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[data.statistics]], [[tech.industry]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-26 The AfDB's push for African financial sovereignty, and a tech investment rebound]],
  [[2026-05-26 AfDB says Africa must fund its own growth as aid model frays]].
- **Data-centre, tower & subsea capital, this batch (2026-02–06).** Named new-build flows:
  **[[kasi-cloud]]** (backed by sovereign fund [[nsia]]) broke ground on a **$250m** Lekki
  hyperscale campus, though ~86MW national capacity and a <6GW grid keep it diesel-dependent
  ([[NGA]], [[infra.energy]]); **[[microsoft-sa-2026-expansion]]** added **$329m** (~R5.4bn) in
  South Africa on top of a prior $1.2bn ([[ZAF]], [[geopol.usa]]); Tanzania's Jamii Namba Phase II
  runs on a **~$70m** Export-Import Bank of Korea loan ([[nida-tanzania]], [[TZA]]); **China
  Industrial Bank** — its first Nigeria investment — is backing **≥1,000** of [[nucap]]'s 3,700
  planned rural telecom towers ([[geopol.china]], [[digital.rural]]); and [[orange]]'s
  [[via-africa-cable]] (>20,000 km Atlantic corridor, 3–4yr build) adds private European capital
  for connectivity ([[geopol.eu]]). Sources:
  [[2026-02-26 Lagos Is Getting a $250 Million Hyperscale Data Center. Here's Why Nigeria Still Can't Power the Cloud]],
  [[2026-04-17 Microsoft picks South Africa for $329 million expansion in cloud infrastructure and AI training]],
  [[2026-04-22 Tanzania seeks biometrics contractors for Phase II of national digital ID project]],
  [[2026-06-04 Chinese bank backs Nigeria towers project]],
  [[2026-05-23 Orange-backed subsea cable to connect Nigeria, 19 other countries]].
- **Payments & startup capital (2026-04).** [[peach-payments]]' Mauritius entity grew transaction
  volume **>300% YoY** (2024→2025) as the island positions itself as a cross-border payments hub
  ([[MUS]], [[dpi.pay]]); Google's [[google-for-startups-accelerator-africa]] Class 10 (15 of ~2,600
  applicants; alumni have raised **>$263m**) channels non-dilutive support into AI-first
  fintech/agritech founders ([[AGO]], [[SEN]], [[tech.innovate]]). Sources:
  [[2026-05-18 Mauritius emerges as an African payments hub]],
  [[2026-04-21 Meet the 15 Startups Joining the Google for Startups Accelerator Africa Class 10]].

- **US hyperscaler & telco capital into South Africa (2026-04).** Two large SA pledges around
  the 6th SA Investment Conference: **[[equinix]]**'s **$438m (R7.5bn)** self-funded data-centre
  expansion (Johannesburg + Cape Town; ~327,000 sqm secured incl. an **R890m** land buy, adding
  ~160MW) — the [[equinix-sa-438m-expansion]] deal — and **[[mtn-group]]**'s **R21.8bn (~$1.3bn)**
  three-year ICT-infrastructure commitment to end-2028 ([[mtn-sa-2026-investment]]), the 3rd
  largest of the conference's R113.5bn total. SA holds ~three-quarters of Africa's **~409MW**
  operational DC capacity (<1% global); its DC market ~$2.6bn (2025) → **>$5bn by 2031**. See
  [[ZAF]], [[infra.store]], [[geopol.usa]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-01 US data centre firm Equinix is investing $438 million in South Africa]],
  [[2026-04-01 US tech giant from Silicon Valley buys R890 million worth of land in South Africa]],
  [[2026-04-01 MTN to invest $1.3B in South Africa by 2028]].
- **[[GHA]] Ghana's $250m AI compute commitment (2026-04).** Ghana's Cabinet approved a **$250m
  national AI computing centre** alongside its National AI Strategy 2023–2033
  ([[ghana-national-ai-strategy]]) — a serious capital commitment for an African government,
  positioning Accra (AfCFTA Secretariat host) as a West African AI hub. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-03 Ghana approves $250M AI Hub & National AI Strategy]].
- **Satellite & cloud capital, this batch (2026-03/04).** Beyond terrestrial DCs:
  **[[amazon-web-services]]** is in advanced talks to buy **[[globalstar]]** for **~$9bn**
  ([[amazon-globalstar-acquisition]]) to accelerate Project Kuiper/Leo against Starlink —
  Globalstar's spectrum in [[ZAF]]/[[RWA]]/[[MOZ]]/[[GAB]] a regulatory shortcut into Africa
  (deal not finalised as of Apr-2026; Apple holds 20%). **[[wingu-africa]]** launched its Wingu
  Cloud Exchange in [[ETH]] Ethiopia (local-currency, in-country residency) on the back of a
  **$60m raise** for East-African expansion ([[DJI]], Ethiopia, Tanzania). And Nigeria's state
  operator **[[nigcomsat]]** reported revenue up from $650m (2023) to **>$2bn** (end-2025),
  funding two new satellites (2028/2029). See [[infra.connect]], [[NGA]]. Sources:
  [[2026-04-14 Amazon's $9bn satellite deal set to benefit Africa]],
  [[2026-04-01 Wingu Africa debuts cloud exchange platform in Ethiopia]],
  [[2026-04-01 Nigeria satellite revenue soars to $2 billion]].
- **Health-aid as contested external finance (2025-12→2026-02).** US **America First Global
  Health Strategy** ([[america-first-global-health-strategy]]) bilaterals show health funding
  operating as sovereignty-tied finance: **22 African countries** signed late-2025→early-2026.
  Kenya's **5-yr, $2.5bn** framework ([[us-kenya-health-framework]]) — first signed — was
  **halted by its High Court** over data-sharing and constitutional concerns; **[[ZWE]]
  Zimbabwe rejected** a **$367m/5-yr** deal ([[zimbabwe-us-health-mou]]) as a sovereignty threat
  (US Embassy's own figure; the earlier "$350m" a pre-announcement estimate — REV-004 resolved),
  and Zambia stalled over data clauses. Aid conditioned on up to 25-yr health-data access for
  5-yr funding — external finance that several states now decline. See [[finance.mou]],
  [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]], [[KEN]]. Sources:
  [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]],
  [[2026-02-24 ⁠Zimbabwe rejects $350 million US health funding deal warns It threatens national sovereignty]].

- **European DFI capital into North African connectivity (2026-01).** The **[[ebrd]]**
  signed an up-to-**€190m** development-linked loan (four tranches, first €50m) for
  **[[tunisie-telecom]]**'s 4G→5G upgrade, FTTH to 200,000 households and connection to the
  **[[medusa-submarine-cable]]**, plus an **€11m** EU grant — the EBRD's first
  non-sovereign-guaranteed public-sector financing in Tunisia since 2012, interest tied to
  SOE-reform milestones and aligned to EU Global Gateway. See [[TUN]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 EBRD and Tunisie Telecom Partner on €190 Million Digital Infrastructure Upgrade]].
- **EBRD's first sub-Saharan A/B loan — €270m to Yas (2026-07).** The **[[ebrd]]** extended a
  senior facility of **up to €270m** to **[[yas]]** ([[axian-telecom]]) for **4G/5G/fibre** in
  **[[SEN]] Senegal** and **[[KEN]] Kenya** ([[ebrd-yas-network-loan]]) — its **first investment
  in Senegal** and **first local-currency and A/B loan structure in sub-Saharan Africa**: **€170m
  committed** (€100m A-loan + up to €50m B-loan syndicated to institutional investors incl.
  **[[ilx-fund]]** + up to €20m in Kenyan shillings) plus **€100m uncommitted** for future Yas
  acquisitions/capex. Institutional-capital mobilisation into African connectivity; both states
  became EBRD countries of operation in 2025. See [[infra.connect]], [[geopol.eu]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 EBRD Extends €270m Financing to Yas for Kenya, Senegal Network Expansion]].
- **[[GHA]] Ghana — MTN's $1.1bn three-year commitment (2026-02).** **[[mtn-ghana]]** (part
  of [[mtn-group]]) pledged **$1.1bn over three years** for 4G/5G, hyperscale data centres and
  AI/cloud, announced during CEO [[ralph-mupita]]'s visit — echoing a 2022 >$1bn/5yr pledge.
  MTN Ghana holds ~58% share, 25m+ subscribers and 11m+ active MoMo users. See [[infra.store]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 MTN Ghana secures $1.1bn boost as AI push deepens]].
- **[[XWA]] WARDIP2 — $137m for West African DPI (2026-03).** The **[[world-bank]]**
  committed **$137m** to **[[wardip]]** phase 2 across **[[BEN]] Benin, [[LBR]] Liberia and
  [[SLE]] Sierra Leone**, targeting broadband for 5m+ and digital public services for 5m+ more
  (~10.6m total) via data-centre capacity, cross-border business rules and WAEMU-aligned
  regulatory harmonisation; [[ecowas]], the [[african-union]] and [[smart-africa]] collaborate.
  See [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.regional]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 New $137M initiative to enable digital service access for millions in three ECOWAS nations]].
- **[[SSD]] South Sudan — blended finance for green telecom power (2026-02).** **[[finnfund]]**
  committed a **$5m** follow-on (total $10m) into **[[crei-south-sudan]]**, backed by the EU's
  EFSD+/Africa Connected, to fund solar-hybrid energy across [[mtn-group]]'s 499 South Sudan
  tower sites (490 renovated, 4m+ served); coverage rose 69%→80%. Concessional capital
  de-risking connectivity in a fragile state. See [[infra.energy]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[geopol.eu]]. Source: [[2026-02-20 South Sudan green network gets $5m boost]].
- **[[GMB]] The Gambia — an African-financed national ID (2026-01).** [[margins-id-group]]
  (Ghana) won a **DBFOT** contract to design, build, finance, operate and transfer The Gambia's
  National ID and Integrated Digital ID system ([[gambia-national-id-contract]]) — the first
  African-owned firm to export a full national ID system (tender opened Jul 2024, 11 bidders).
  Africa-to-Africa capital and capability rather than donor finance. See [[GHA]], [[dpi.id]],
  [[finance.mou]]. Source: [[2026-01-20 Margins takes Ghana's ID success to The Gambia]].
- **[[COD]] PayServices–DRC — the downside of contract finance (2026-01).** US fintech
  **[[payservices]]** sued the DRC in US federal court over a collapsed **$72m** state
  banking-modernisation project ([[payservices-drc-banking-deal]]), claiming it invested >$72m
  awaiting an unpaid $20m state contribution and seeking up to **$400m** (some filings cite
  $4bn); Kinshasa calls the documents a non-binding MoU. A cautionary tale on private
  tech-infrastructure finance without enforceable terms. See [[dpi.pay]], [[geopol.usa]].
  Source: [[2026-01-19 US fintech sues DR Congo for $400m over failed banking deal]].
- **[[ZMB]] Zambia — a $1bn US health deal, then rejection (2026-02).** Extending the
  health-aid-as-contested-finance pattern above: a leaked draft **$1.012bn/5yr** US MoU
  ([[zambia-us-health-mou]]) tied funding to hiring 40,000 health workers, a $400m Zambian
  contribution, **10-year health-data access and a 25-year pathogen-data pledge**, and — per
  the US embassy — copper/cobalt mining access. Zambia requested "revisions" and effectively
  **halted** it; the health minister was fired days after denying the mining link (joining
  Zimbabwe and Kenya in pushing back). See [[finance.mou]], [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]].
  Sources: [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]],
  [[2026-02-26 Zambia rejects U.S. health aid over mining partnership ties]].

- **[[ZMB]] Zambia (2025).** [[dzap]] — a **US$100M IDA grant** + **US$20M unguaranteed
  commercial financing** — under the **US$2.48bn** [[idea-mpa]] regional envelope. The
  operation is **MFD-enabling** and **private-capital-enabling**: World Bank concessional
  finance is explicitly structured to crowd in private investment (fibre, LEO satellite,
  DFS). A test case for whether donor finance builds sovereign capacity or entrenches
  dependence ([[gov.policy]]).

- **Health-AI and cyber capital, 2026-07 batch.** Two new external flows: the **$50m**
  [[gates-foundation]]–[[openai]] **[[horizon1000]]** pilot (health-AI, beginning in **[[RWA]]**,
  scaling to 1,000 clinics by 2028); and the EU-funded **$3.56M / €3M** Kenya Cyber Resilience
  Project ([[kenya-cyber-resilience-project]], 36 months, implemented by [[expertise-france]] +
  [[estdev]]). Philanthropic and EU capacity capital rather than commercial capex. See [[tech.ai]],
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[geopol.eu]], [[KEN]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-22 Gates Foundation and OpenAI Commit $50m to AI-Powered Health Systems in Africa]],
  [[2026-01-27 Kenya launches project to boost cyber security]].

- **MNO capital reallocates to data/infrastructure (Russell Southwood, 2025-12–2026-05).**
  Southwood's state-of-market shows operator capital shifting from voice networks
  to data infrastructure: the top-three players each guide **~$1bn CAPEX**, with
  **>½ of MTN's** in Nigeria and roughly **a quarter of Airtel's** going to data
  centres, while "second division" players consolidate — [[axian-telecom]] raised
  **US$600m** and bought Wananchi. On the AI-compute side, his demand-side survey
  maps the GPU-as-a-Service investment field ([[cassava-ai-factories]] ~$720m,
  IXAfrica, the DIF-backed BCN Zadara factory). See [[tech.industry]],
  [[infra.store]]. Sources:
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]],
  [[2026-05-19 Trying to make sense of who will use Artificial Intelligence in Africa and how – Going beyond the hype]].
