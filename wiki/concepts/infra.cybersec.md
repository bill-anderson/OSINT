# Cybersecurity (`infra.cybersec`)

Security of digital platforms, data hosting and incident response — and the politics of
how "cyber threat" is defined and acted on.

## Key material

- **[[ZMB]] Zambia (2025).** [[dzap]] Component 2 is "Interoperable **and secure**
  platforms" — secure data hosting and a Cybersecurity Incident Response Team (CIRT)
  underpin the DPI stack ([[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.id]]). Security, privacy and
  interoperability are stated design priorities for the homegrown foundational ID.
- **[[ZAF]] South Africa — securitization + real exposure (2025 analysis).** The BRICS-book
  chapter reads SA cyber policy through a **securitization** lens: how threat is *framed*
  (the Cybercrimes Act, bulk-surveillance powers, apartheid-surveillance memory) can expand
  state control, while **[[research-ict-africa]]** warns draconian rules can outrun evidence.
  The exposure is real — ransomware on **Transnet** and the **DBSA** (Akira), 220m+ email
  threats (2021) — against under-resourced policing. See [[south-africa--gov-protect]],
  [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 South African Digital Sovereignty at the Crossroad of Securitization and Development (Chapter 4) - Digital Sovereignty in the BRICS Countries]].
- **[[XAF]] Continental — DNS resilience (2026-07).** The **[[smart-africa]]**–**[[afnic]]**
  MoU targets **DNS security and resilience** and hardening of ccTLD registries — securing a
  foundational layer of the continent's internet infrastructure. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Smart Africa and AFNIC Partner to Strengthen Africa's Internet Infrastructure]].
- **[[NGA]] Nigeria — identity-system enforcement (2026-07).** The NIMC Act 2026 folds
  cyber-enforcement into the ID system: **[[nimc]]**'s **ICE Unit** can arrest/prosecute
  identity thieves, tethered to the 2023 Data Protection Act — "cyber provisions up front"
  around the biometric database of 136M+ citizens. See [[dpi.id]], [[nigeria--dpi-id]].
  Source: [[2026-07-09 NIMC Act 2026 Director-General Coker-Odusote Launches 8,809-Ward Digital ID Drive]].
- **[[MOZ]] Mozambique — cyber-security package (2026-04).** Parliament **unanimously** passed a
  Cyber Security Law plus a separate Cybercrime Law. The INCM acts as technical coordinator, but
  under a state of siege/emergency its functions pass to the **Cyber Coordination Centre of the
  Defence and Security Forces**; a Cyber Security Fund is established to resource training and
  enforcement. Consolidation aims to clarify what constitutes a cybercrime, with recommendations
  to sign mutual legal-assistance agreements given cybercrime's transnational nature. See
  [[gov.legislate]], [[mozambique-cyber-security-law]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-17 Mozambique parliament unanimously approves Cyber Security Law]].
- **[[XAF]] Continental — sovereign enclaves vs the CLOUD Act (2026-04).** The
  [[smart-africa]]–[[realtyme]] "national protection framework"
  ([[smart-africa-realtyme-partnership]]) is pitched explicitly as a counter-measure to the
  **US CLOUD Act**: state-controlled communication enclaves to keep sensitive intelligence in
  national borders, tactical AI governance against deepfake disinformation, and **post-quantum**
  resilience — framed as "digital decolonisation" against both Silicon Valley and Chinese
  dominance (claim: Africa faces ~60% higher cyber-attack rate than the global average). See
  [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]], [[XSS]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 Smart Africa, RealTyme join forces for data sovereignty]].
- **Cross-border trust chains (2026-04, SADX).** A [[sadx]] Technical Assessment Mission (Kigali;
  [[RWA]], [[BEN]], [[GHA]]) worked the *security plumbing* of cross-border data exchange —
  **PKI trust chains**, API contracts, verification-request processing, certificate management
  and cross-jurisdiction incident response — toward a "functional, federated and decentralised"
  architecture that keeps national control while enabling trusted sharing. See [[dpi.exchange]],
  [[gov.standards]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-27 Smart Africa drives cross-border data interoperability through SADX Technical Mission]].
- **[[ZAF]] SA digital-ID security stack (2026-04).** Home Affairs' digital-ID build sits inside
  the **SARS hosting environment**: **PKI, a certificate authority**, and biometric-secured
  digital wallets (facial recognition primary, fingerprints secondary), with hosting to complete
  by **31 Mar 2027**. But the department concedes its cybersecurity maturity **"remains low"**
  and lacks a fully implemented disaster-recovery strategy; a security operations centre and new
  infosec posts are planned. See [[dpi.id]], [[south-africa--gov-protect]], [[sa-digital-id-regulations]],
  [[popia]]. Source: [[2026-04-21 South Africa's digital ID gets a targeted launch date]].
- **DPAs, storage risk and the [[eac]] (2026-05, ID4Africa).** A [[world-bank]] session mapped
  **six data-storage risks** — inconsistent vendor maturity, single points of failure, function
  creep, cryptographic gaps, weak insider-threat controls, and absent incident-reporting/redress
  — noting cryptographic effectiveness erodes over time and DPI's growing attack surface. Panel
  detail: Mauritius' DPO runs ~**3,000** enforcement actions/yr (99% resolved amicably), Benin's
  APDP has a dedicated AI policy, Liberia's data-protection bill is in the legislature, and the
  **[[eac]]** created a body for its **eight** member DPAs to share capacity and co-investigate.
  See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.id]], [[mosip]], [[id4africa]], [[XEA]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].
- **[[CMR]] ANTIC hardens the CIRT (2026-01).** Cameroon's **[[antic-cameroon]]** commissioned
  **~XAF735m ($1.3m)** of intrusion-detection/prevention hardware and software for its Computer
  Incident Response Team via the World-Bank-funded **[[patnuc]]** programme. ANTIC has flagged
  **≥8,502 vulnerabilities** since Jan 2024 and processed **32,500 complaints in 2025** (+30% YoY); its
  CIRT ingests **~200GB/day** against ~200 urgent requisitions, and it runs the national **PKI**.
  Cybersecurity is positioned as the trust layer under Cameroon's DPI. See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[gov.protect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-01-22 Cameroon boosts cyber defense systems]],
  [[2026-01-27 Cameroon deploys cybersecurity system to protect DPI, boost digital trust]].
- **[[BFA]] Supervising critical infrastructure (2026-01).** Burkina Faso is building a **Digital
  Infrastructure Supervision Center** (~**$5.4m**, delivery ~Oct 2026; [[bf-ministry-digital-transition]])
  to centralise oversight of the national backbone, data centres and cyberspace, paired with a
  **"zero data abroad"** localization rule — cyber-sovereignty as infrastructure control across the
  **[[alliance-of-sahel-states]]** bloc. See [[infra.store]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-22 Burkina Faso aims for digital sovereignty with infrastructure supervision center]].
- **[[MOZ]] Two tracks — breach reporting and biometric IAM (2026-02).** **[[intic-mozambique]]**
  launched a pilot **personal-data-breach reporting platform**, part of consolidating the
  data-protection regime and supplying the incident-reporting mechanism many DPAs still lack. In
  parallel, **[[bio-key-international]]** and integrator **[[runlevel]]** began deploying **biometric
  identity-and-access management** (PortalGuard, PIN:You, SSO) into **[[simo-mozambique]]**, operator of
  the **SIMOrede** national payments network — identity-first security around clearing and settlement.
  See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.id]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-06 Mozambique intros data breach platform]],
  [[2026-02-24 BIO-key and RunLevel Deploy Biometric IAM Across Mozambique's National Payments Network]].
- **[[BEN]] Securing the road-enforcement data platform (2026-01).** **[[cybastion]]** is partnering
  Benin's Ministry of Economy and Finance to extend infrastructure for **full interoperability with
  [[securoute]]**, the national platform that centralises and secures traffic/road-enforcement data
  across the police, transport and road-safety agencies — secure cross-agency data exchange as a
  govtech building block ([[asin-benin]]). See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.standards]].
  Source: [[2026-01-27 Cybastion Advances Digital Government Solutions, Supports Benin's SECUROUTE Infrastructure Upgrade]].
- **Chinese "safe city" surveillance mapped (2026-03, IDS).** An Institute of Development Studies
  report (Wakabi & Roberts) finds **[[zte]], [[hikvision]] and [[huawei]] supplied every one of 11
  African countries surveyed**, with **>$2bn** spent on facial-recognition/ANPR systems. **[[NGA]]** is
  the largest market — China Exim Bank advanced **$399m of the $470m** Abuja spent on a ZTE/Hikvision
  network, Lagos deploying ~**10,000** cameras — while **[[KEN]]** installed **1,800** Huawei cameras in
  Nairobi/Mombasa on a ~$100m Chinese grant after Westgate. The typical package — a **~$250m Eximbank
  loan bundling cameras + a national data centre + vendor training** — produces **institutional
  lock-in**, and the report documents surveillance turned on political opposition (Zimbabwe, Mozambique,
  Zambia, Senegal) absent any terror threat. The security-infrastructure counter-narrative to the
  data-sovereignty story. See [[geopol.china]], [[gov.protect]], [[infra.store]], [[EGY]], [[SEN]],
  [[ZWE]], [[DZA]], [[UGA]]. Source: [[2026-03-01 Smart City Surveillance in Africa (IDS 2026)]].

- **[[KEN]] Kenya Cyber Resilience Project (2026-01-27).** The EU-funded **€3m ($3.56m),
  36-month** **[[kenya-cyber-resilience-project]]** ([[expertise-france]] + [[estdev]],
  [[european-union]]) hardens critical infrastructure and builds cyber skills, laying groundwork
  for a future National Cybersecurity Agency — as >20,000 government services moved online in a
  year raised the attack surface. Aligned to Kenya's National Cybersecurity Strategy and Digital
  Master Plan. See [[geopol.eu]], [[gov.policy]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-27 Kenya launches project to boost cyber security]].
- **[[NGA]] The .ng ccTLD as a neglected DPI/security pillar (2026-01).** Analysis argues
  Nigeria's under-adopted **.ng** domain (~237,266 active registrations, Dec 2025, managed by
  **[[nira-nigeria]]**) is a governance and enforcement gap in digital sovereignty; the 2022 FEC
  policy mandating **.gov.ng** migration (enforced by [[nitda]]) is unevenly applied, leaving
  government identity exposed on foreign domains. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]].
  Source: [[2026-01-21 How foreign domains compromise digital identity]].
- **[[SDN]] Sudan — a national cybersecurity authority (2025-11).** Sudan created the
  **[[sudanese-cybersecurity-authority]]** (one of three agencies established by decree) to build a
  national cybersecurity framework, against an ITU GCI **Tier 4** score (48.17/100, 2024). See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[SDN]]. Source: [[2025-11-07 Sudan Establishes Three Digital Authorities to Advance E-Govt]].
- **[[NGA]] Nigeria — ITU Global Cybersecurity Index tier-3 (2024).** Nigeria placed **tier-3** in the
  **[[itu]]** Global Cybersecurity Index 2024, strongest on the legal pillar (**19.52/20**). See
  [[gov.policy]], [[NGA]]. Source:
  [[2024-09-18 Nigeria among tier-3 countries in ITU Global Cybersecurity Index 2024]].
- **[[SOM]] Somalia — a cybersecurity law and an early breach (2025–26).** Somalia approved a
  **Cybersecurity Law** (**26 Jan 2026**) establishing a national CIRT (**SOM-CIRT**), against an ITU GCI
  **Tier 4/5** score (37.38/100, 2024); a **Nov-2025 e-visa data breach** underlined the exposure. See
  [[gov.legislate]], [[SOM]].
