# MoUs and other agreements (`finance.mou`)

The agreement/partnership layer of the investment story — memoranda that signal
direction and lock in public-private roles before capital fully flows. A specific MoU
is a **deal entity**; this concept tracks the pattern.

## Key material

- **Equity × AfricaNenda × Gates Foundation (2026-07-07).** MoU to build interoperable
  DPI (payments, ID, data exchange) and scale it across **20–30 African markets**; Dr
  James Mwangi named **Continental DPI Champion**. Frames the private sector as
  implementation partner "without governments surrendering ownership." Deal:
  [[equity-africanenda-gates-dpi-partnership]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].
- **Gabon Ministry × ST DIGITAL GABON (2026-07-04).** MoU at the Tier III datacenter
  inauguration giving ministry-backed startups preferential hosting rates. See
  [[st-digital-gabon]], [[GAB]], [[tech.innovate]].
- **Sierra Leone × Bhutan NDI × Sign (2026-07-08).** Tri-party MoU to build Sierra Leone's
  national digital-ID platform on open standards, with national ownership as the design
  goal — a **South–South** transfer. Deal: [[sierra-leone-digital-id-mou]]. See [[SLE]],
  [[dpi.id]], [[gov.standards]].
- **Safaricom Ethiopia × National ID Program × Africom (2026-07-06).** Joint Venture
  Agreement to deliver Lots 3 & 4 of Ethiopia's **Fayda** registration. Deal:
  [[safaricom-ethiopia-fayda-jv]]. See [[ETH]], [[dpi.id]].
- **ESTDEV × Smart Africa (2026-05-28).** MoU (Brussels) to build **interoperable, reusable DPI**
  across Smart Africa's **42 member states**, cut vendor lock-in, and transfer Estonia's
  digital-government experience — plus STEM/girls and women-led-startup support. Framed around
  "trust, innovation and digital sovereignty." Deal: [[estdev-smart-africa-mou]] ([[estdev]],
  [[smart-africa]]). See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]], [[capacity.training]].
- **Mozambique × Ethiopia (2026-04-28).** Three-year MoU (Addis) under which Ethiopia's NIDP /
  FaydaVerse supports Mozambique's new [[atdi-mozambique]] on digital-ID platforms, DPI pilots,
  interoperability and joint cybersecurity — a **South–South** transfer pitched as "technological
  sovereignty" and reduced vendor dependence (Ethiopia has a comparable deal with Zambia). Deal:
  [[mozambique-ethiopia-dpi-mou]]. See [[MOZ]], [[dpi.id]].
- **SIN × ST Digital, Togo (2026-06-02).** Strategic partnership to run a **sovereign private
  cloud** at the Tier III Lomé Data Centre ([[lome-data-centre]]) — data hosted in-country, with
  "half-a-rack" affordability and continuing-education programmes. Deal:
  [[sin-st-digital-lome-cloud-deal]] ([[sin-togo]], [[st-digital-gabon]]). See [[TGO]],
  [[infra.store]], [[include.access]].
- **Smart Africa × RealTyme (2026-04-16).** Partnership pairing the Smart Africa Digital Academy
  with Swiss zero-trust firm [[realtyme]] on a "national protection framework" — state-controlled
  data enclaves explicitly positioned against the **US Cloud Act** and framed as "digital
  decolonisation" (claim: Africa faces a ~60% higher cyber-attack rate). Deal:
  [[smart-africa-realtyme-partnership]]. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]].
- **Gates Foundation × Anthropic (2026-05-14).** **$200m over four years** (grants + API credits +
  technical support) to build **AI public goods** — open datasets, benchmarks and tools — across
  health, education and agriculture in LMICs, with African use-cases (Kenya, Nigeria) illustrative.
  A philanthropy-plus-frontier-lab agreement, not sovereign capital. Deal:
  [[gates-anthropic-partnership]] ([[gates-foundation]], [[anthropic]]). See [[tech.ai]],
  [[capacity.research]], [[XGL]].
- **Gabon × Huawei (2025-12-23).** MoU between Gabon's Ministry of Digital Economy and
  **[[huawei]]** on three pillars — a joint project committee, integration of Gabonese
  startups, and skills transfer "for digital sovereignty" — expanding the Connected Schools
  Program. A Chinese-vendor partnership framed as sovereignty-building. Deal:
  [[gabon-huawei-mou]]. See [[GAB]], [[geopol.china]], [[tech.innovate]], [[capacity.training]].
- **Anthropic × Rwanda (2026-02).** Three-year, **non-binding** MoU embedding [[anthropic]]'s
  AI across Rwanda's health ministry, public-sector agencies and education (Claude/Claude Code
  + API credits for government developers; the Chidi learning companion across eight
  countries), extending a Nov-2025 ALX partnership. Analysts warn the terms are non-binding but
  the **dependency is not** — no parliamentary, multilateral or civil-society review exists, an
  accountability vacuum as a private firm becomes load-bearing public infrastructure. Deal:
  [[anthropic-rwanda-mou]]. See [[RWA]], [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.protect]].
- **Zambia × US health (2026-02) — an MoU declined.** A leaked **$1.012bn/5yr** draft
  ([[zambia-us-health-mou]], under the **[[america-first-global-health-strategy]]**) tied US
  health funding to a 40,000-worker hiring target, a $400m Zambian contribution, **10-yr
  health-data and 25-yr pathogen-data** access, and reported copper/cobalt mining concessions.
  Zambia requested revisions and effectively halted it (health minister fired after denying the
  mining link), joining Zimbabwe and Kenya in pushing back — MoUs as sovereignty-tests, not
  just direction-signals. See [[ZMB]], [[finance.new]], [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]].
  Sources: [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]],
  [[2026-02-26 Zambia rejects U.S. health aid over mining partnership ties]].
- **Ethio Telecom × Somcable (2026-01) — talks, not yet signed.** [[ethio-telecom]] and
  Somaliland's [[somcable]] are in **talks** on a cross-border digital/telecom partnership to
  share infrastructure and reach overseas markets — a pre-MoU stage worth tracking for a
  Horn-of-Africa corridor. See [[ETH]], [[SOM]], [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-29 Ethio Telecom, Somcable talk cross-border collaboration]].
- **Zambia × PAYZ (2026-02).** The government and the Payments Association of Zambia ([[payz]])
  agreed to accelerate digital financial services and cut cash dependency, with quarterly
  regulatory coordination ([[bank-of-zambia]], [[zicta]]) — a public-private compact on DFS.
  See [[ZMB]], [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-27 Zambia partners with PAYZ to boost financial inclusion]].

- **Nigeria × Finland — digital-innovation MoU (2026-03).** Nigeria (Minister [[bosun-tijani]]) and
  Finland signed an MoU in Abuja on digital government, DPI, cybersecurity, emerging tech and
  capacity-building ([[nigeria-finland-digital-mou]]), following Helsinki talks with Finnvera/Finnfund
  and complementing Finland's lead of the EU **Team Europe Initiative on Digital Public Services**
  (delivered with Estonia's [[estdev]] + HAUS), tied to Nigeria's Data Exchange Platform and Project
  BRIDGE. See [[NGA]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[geopol.eu]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-01 Nigeria Signs MoU With Finland On Digital Innovation, Deepens Bilateral Ties]].
- **EU × Nigeria — €290m Global Gateway package (2026-03).** At the 8th EU–Nigeria Ministerial
  Dialogue (Abuja) the EU announced **€290m across seven operations** under [[global-gateway]]
  ([[eu-nigeria-global-gateway-290m]]), including **€131m for digitalisation** — 90,000km of fibre with
  the EBRD, secure interoperable DPI and data-exchange systems, and 3MTT/TVET training — alongside
  agriculture (€86m) and migration (€16m). MoU-scale European counter-investment against the US/China
  infrastructure offer. See [[NGA]], [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[geopol.eu]],
  [[finance.new]]. Source: [[2026-03-24 Nigeria EU Announces New €290m Investments in Nigeria]].
- **AU × Google — continental AI-capacity MoU (2026-02).** The **[[african-union]]** Commission signed
  an MoU with **[[google]]** ([[au-google-mou]], 17 Feb 2026, Addis) on AI/cloud infrastructure,
  skills, R&D and responsible-AI policy, anchored in the AU Continental AI Strategy; Google pledges to
  train **3m** students/teachers by 2030 plus free Gemini Pro and Amharic support, and official
  AI-readiness training via Apolitical. Continental capacity built on a US hyperscaler — the
  sovereignty tension the CLOUD Act sharpens. See [[XAF]], [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]],
  [[gov.regional]]. Source: [[2026-02-20 AUC Signs Partnership With Google]].

- **Starlink turns rivals into partners (2026-01).** Two continent-scale partnership deals:
  **[[starlink]] × [[airtel-africa]]** ([[starlink-airtel-d2c-partnership]]) — Direct-to-Cell +
  cellular backhaul, launching 2026 across all **14 Airtel markets**; and **Starlink × [[vodacom]]**
  ([[starlink-vodacom-leo-partnership]]) — LEO backhaul + equipment resale across **25 countries**.
  A satellite operator shifting from competitor to wholesale partner of the incumbents. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[data.satellite]], [[ZAF]], [[COD]], [[LSO]], [[TZA]], [[MOZ]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]].
- **iXAfrica × Oracle — Kenya's first public cloud region (2026-01).** iXAfrica Data Centres will
  host the **Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI)** region in Nairobi ([[ixafrica-oracle-oci-nairobi]],
  [[oracle]]) — a host-partner agreement making Kenya's first public cloud region, framed as a
  "sovereign digital economy" play. See [[KEN]], [[infra.store]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 iXAfrica, Oracle to launch Kenya's first public cloud region]].
- **Prospective — Kenya School of Government × Huawei (2026-01).** KSG ([[kenya-school-of-government]])
  and [[huawei]] Kenya are **discussing** an MoU (not yet signed) to train public servants in AI,
  cybersecurity, cloud and digital governance. Tracked as a pre-signature agreement. See [[KEN]],
  [[geopol.china]], [[capacity.training]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-12 Kenya, Huawei to boost public sector digital skills]].
- **Seychelles Digital Trade Corridor MoU (2026-03-05).** The precursor MoU (AfCFTA Secretariat +
  Quest Ghana + Govt of Seychelles; [[seychelles-afcfta-mou]]) behind the
  **[[afcfta-digital-trade-corridor]]** HQ approved by Seychelles' Cabinet (8 Jul 2026). See
  [[SYC]], [[gov.regional]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Seychelles to Host AfCFTA Digital Trade Corridor Headquarters]].
