# Open data (`data.open`)

The opening, sharing and — increasingly — *monetisation* of government-held
datasets. Two 2026 developments frame the emerging African posture: data treated
as a **strategic national asset** to be licensed for revenue, and open,
vendor-neutral publication of foundational reference data.

## Key material

- **Access-as-a-right and open-by-default (2026 H1).** The draft **[[african-guidelines-data-access]]**
  (ACHPR Res.620) frames access to high-value public datasets (budgets, procurement, health,
  environment) as a component of the right to information, with proactive-disclosure duties on public
  *and* private actors; Rwanda’s data-sharing policy adopts an **open-by-default** posture for
  government data. Sources:
  [[2026-05-07 African Guidelines on Data Access ACHPR Res 620 draft]],
  [[2026-05-07 What will determine the success of Rwanda’s data governance push]]. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]].

- **Kenya's State-data marketplace (2026-06, ITWeb Africa).** Kenya's Ministry
  of ICT & the Digital Economy proposes a **State-run marketplace** to licence
  **anonymised** government datasets (from **eCitizen** — 22,000+ services across
  100+ agencies — and **KNBS**) to businesses, researchers, NGOs and innovators.
  Target **1,000+ datasets over five years**; cost up to **Sh396m (~$3.07m)**;
  free tier for public-interest use, tiered pricing otherwise; personal data
  excluded under the Data Protection Act 2019. Sits within a **National Data
  Governance Policy** designating data a strategic national asset, run by a new
  **National Data Governance & Emerging Technologies Council** and **Data
  Governance Office**. Comparators: Singapore's tiered platform; UK Ordnance
  Survey (~$263m/yr from geospatial licensing). Deal/initiative:
  [[kenya-data-marketplace]]. See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.protect]],
  [[dpi.govtech]]. Source: [[2026-06-08 Kenya proposes marketplace for State data]].
- **Zambia's open-source turn + NSDI (2026-06, ITWeb Africa).** Zambia's Cabinet
  directed ministries to **adopt open-source technology**, coinciding with the
  Lusaka launch of the **National Spatial Data Infrastructure (NSDI) Policy and
  Portal** (built on open-source software) to end fragmented geospatial data and
  vendor lock-in. Aligned with the Eighth National Development Plan and the
  **UN Integrated Geospatial Information Framework (UN-IGIF)**. See
  [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-17 Zambia's govt leans towards open-source]].

- **AI against open aid data — retrieval vs interpretation (2026-07).** [[publish-what-you-fund]]
  tested an LLM agent (Claude Code) against the **[[international-aid-transparency-initiative]]**
  open dataset (2,000+ publishing agencies): strong on retrieval (totals within 0.3% of established
  tools) but **unreliable on interpretation** — fabricating explanations and missing publisher
  idiosyncrasies (e.g. [[usaid]]'s transaction-level sector coding, UK/FCDO 2025 cuts, an Ethiopia
  food-aid worked example). A caution that open data plus AI still needs domain expertise. See
  [[ETH]], [[tech.ai]], [[data.statistics]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-02 Can AI solve open aid data's access problem]].
- **Data-for-governance scholarship (2023–2025).** Two reference captures: **[[data-for-policy]]**
  (the UK CIC and Cambridge *Data & Policy* journal; Gates-backed SSA-inclusion programme; board
  includes [[the-govlab]]'s [[stefaan-verhulst]]) and its published
  **[[digital-data-driven-transformations-landscape-review-2025|landscape review]]** on
  data-driven governance (open data among its three transformations) — reference-shelf material,
  not African-specific. See [[gov.policy]], [[XGL]], [[XSS]]. Sources:
  [[2023-09-04 Data for Policy - About Us]],
  [[2025-01-01 Digital & data-driven transformations in governance a landscape review  Data & Policy]].

- **Analyst reads on opening/selling data (2026 batch).** Rose Mutiso's close
  reading of Kenya's **Draft Final National Data Governance Policy (May 2026)**
  argues the "Kenya to sell your data" marketplace ([[kenya-data-marketplace]]) is
  a small part of a larger interoperability/plumbing reform, but flags **weak
  anonymization**, **non-consent** given mandatory eCitizen use, vague
  benefit-sharing and an implausible Jul-2026 phased rollout — and notes the EU/
  German technical assistance behind it whose own frameworks (Data Governance Act,
  GDPR) would bar the same revenue-maximizing reuse at home (a colonialism read)
  ([[2026-06-08 Kenya Wants to Sell Your Data. But It's Complicated]]). Chinasa
  Okolo presses the *supply* side of openness — African AI startups should
  open-source models and publish benchmarked results, and she maintains an open
  **Harvard Dataverse** dataset on African AI startups
  ([[2025-12-15 African AI Startups Should Publish Openly]]). Rose Mutiso (VoxDev)
  frames **data** as one of three AI fundamentals and calls for **regional
  data-standard harmonisation** ([[2026-02-10 AI in Africa Barriers, opportunities and policy]]).
  See [[KEN]], [[gov.protect]], [[capacity.research]], [[tech.innovate]].

- **[[BEN]] Benin — a budget-transparency front-runner (2025).** On the International Budget
  Partnership's **Open Budget Survey 2025**, Benin scored **77/100 transparency (5th globally, 2nd in
  sub-Saharan Africa behind South Africa), 52/100 participation (2nd globally, behind Brazil)** and
  **72/100 oversight** — a climb from **1/100 in 2012**. Its first **OGP National Action Plan** entered
  commune-level consultation (Jul 2026). Officials frame it as "active transparency" (consult, publish,
  explain, account). A signed critical read separates procedural from substantive participation. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[digital.localgov]], [[BEN]]. Sources:
  [[2026-06-19-obs-2025-benin-transparence-budgetaire]],
  [[2026-06-05-benin-2e-mondial-participation-budget-ouvert]], [[2026-07-09-pgo-communes-plan-action-national]].

## Reads

- `sovereignty`: both cases treat national data and the software that manages it
  as instruments of self-determination — Kenya monetising sovereign data as an
  asset; Zambia asserting control via open standards against vendor lock-in
  (the same open-standards thread as [[zambia--dpi-id]] and [[gov.standards]]).
