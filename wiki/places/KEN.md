- **2026-07-10** — **Cross-border identity is a governance problem, not a technical one.** A DIAL
  ([[dial]]) research-fellowship analysis notes that national ID cards are **mutually recognised for
  travel between Kenya, Rwanda and Uganda**, yet those same credentials are often **inadequate for
  cross-border financial services** (which demand passports or extra documentation) — a data
  sufficiency/adequacy/sovereignty gap that **[[afcfta]]**'s portable-identity vision must solve
  without eroding sovereignty. Kenya's eCitizen is cited as a nationally-designed identity system.
  Source: [[2026-07-10 Every country defines identity differently. Can digital public infrastructure bridge those differences]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]], [[RWA]], [[UGA]].

  strengthening data governance, **secure cross-border data flows**, and bilateral digital
  cooperation (discussions only, no MoU). Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Kenya and U.S. Discuss Draft Data Governance Policy to Strengthen Digital Economy]].
  See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[geopol.usa]],
  [[us-department-of-state]].
