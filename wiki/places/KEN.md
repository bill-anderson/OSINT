# Kenya (KEN)

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-13** — **EBRD €270m to Yas for Kenya + Senegal fibre.** The **[[ebrd]]** extended a
  senior facility of up to **€270m** to **[[yas]]** ([[axian-telecom]]) for 4G/5G/fibre, with the
  Kenya leg funding **fibre expansion on the 2025 Wananchi acquisition** ([[ebrd-yas-network-loan]];
  €170m committed + €100m uncommitted, [[ilx-fund]] in the B-loan) — the EBRD's first local-currency
  (KES) and A/B structure in sub-Saharan Africa. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 EBRD Extends €270m Financing to Yas for Kenya, Senegal Network Expansion]]. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[finance.new]], [[geopol.eu]], [[SEN]].
- **2026-07** — Kenya is **one of five sites** in Cassava's **[[cassava-ai-factories]]**
  program; the **2026-04-08** launch of Liquid C2's Google-Cloud-powered Partner Experience Centre
  (Johannesburg) confirmed Kenya as the **next AI-factory build after Cape Town** (then Nigeria,
  Morocco, Egypt) — AI infrastructure continuing to gravitate to Kenya. Sources:
  [[2026-01-01 Africa's Sovereign AI Play Cassava Technologies and Zimbabwean Strive Masiyiwa $ 720 million Bets - Ecofin Agency]],
  [[2026-04-08 Liquid C2, Google open first-for-Africa Partner Experience Centre]].
  See [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]], [[capacity.training]].
- **2026** — Kenya's **misrecognised IDs** (barring migrants / refugees) feature
  as an **exclusion example** in the Mazzucato / Eaves DPI paper. Source:
  [[2026-04-03 Digital public infrastructure and the economics of public value what is 'public' about DPI]].
- **2025** — **[[icj-kenya]]** (Kenyan Section of the ICJ) published *Mapping Global DPI: A
  Human Rights Perspective with Focus on Kenya*, assessing M-Pesa, eCitizen, Huduma Namba and
  the **Worldcoin** biometric controversy against a human-rights framework (privacy, equity,
  transparency). Source:
  [[2025-01-01 Mapping Global DPI - A Human Rights Perspective with Focus on Kenya (ICJ Kenya)]].
  See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.id]], [[kenya--gov-protect]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]].
- **2026** — **US aid-freeze** temporarily cut access to Kenyan health data —
  see [[usaid]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-26 A Year After USAID Reclaiming Control of Data Systems – Development Gateway An IREX Venture – Data and digital solutions for international development]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-07-10** — **Cross-border identity is a governance problem, not a technical one.** A DIAL
  ([[dial]]) research-fellowship analysis notes that national ID cards are **mutually recognised for
  travel between Kenya, Rwanda and Uganda**, yet those same credentials are often **inadequate for
  cross-border financial services** (which demand passports or extra documentation) — a data
  sufficiency/adequacy/sovereignty gap that **[[afcfta]]**'s portable-identity vision must solve
  without eroding sovereignty. Kenya's eCitizen is cited as a nationally-designed identity system.
  Source: [[2026-07-10 Every country defines identity differently. Can digital public infrastructure bridge those differences]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]], [[RWA]], [[UGA]].
- **2026-07-09** — **Kenya–US data-governance talks (Nairobi).** US Embassy officials met
  PS **John Tanui**, ICT Security Directorate Secretary Emmanuel Kata and Data Protection
  Commissioner **Immaculate Kassait** on Kenya's **Draft Data Governance Policy (2026)** —
  strengthening data governance, **secure cross-border data flows**, and bilateral digital
  cooperation (discussions only, no MoU). Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Kenya and U.S. Discuss Draft Data Governance Policy to Strengthen Digital Economy]].
  See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[geopol.usa]],
  [[us-department-of-state]].
- **2026-07** — **Partner2Connect ($100bn milestone).** **[[microsoft]]** pledged to connect
  **450+ rural/underserved community hubs in Kenya** via satellite-enabled infrastructure +
  **Azure Space**, through the [[itu]] [[partner2connect]] coalition. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 ITU's Partner2Connect crosses $100 billion in global connectivity pledges]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-07 (early)** — **[[visa]] settles [[safaricom]] M-Pesa top-ups in stablecoins in the DRC**
  via **Visa Pay** with pan-African network **[[onafriq]]** ([[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]])
  — blockchain settlement to bypass correspondent banking (~8% SSA remittance fees), building on Visa Pay
  in DRC (Sept 2025). The core mechanism is corroborated by an on-record Visa exec (Sullivan), though the
  "joint USD-stablecoin *pilot* launch" framing is downgraded (no jointly-announced pilot; USD peg
  inferred; one partnership awaiting regulatory approval). Still signals M-Pesa's Web3 direction and tests
  CBK/CBN crypto stances (REV-006 resolved 2026-07-16). Sources:
  [[2026-07-02 Visa tests stablecoins in DRC for cross-border payments (The EastAfrican)]],
  [[2026-07-02 DR Congo becomes Africa's live test for dollar-settled mobile money (Ecofin)]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]], [[geopol.gulf]], [[gov.protect]].

- **2026-07-09** — The **$1bn [[microsoft-g42-kenya-datacenter]]** (Olkaria) has
  **stalled**: its planned **1 GW** draw is ~a third of Kenya's **~3 GW** grid, and
  President **[[william-ruto]]** said serving it would mean cutting power to half the
  country; Treasury clearance failed and the May 2026 launch was missed. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Microsoft and G42 data center in Kenya stalled due to lack of power capacity]].
  See [[infra.capacity]], [[infra.energy]], [[microsoft]], [[g42]].
- **2026-07-07** — Kenya's PS for ICT & Digital Economy **John Tanui** discusses cloud, AI
  and **sovereign cloud infrastructure** with **[[oracle]]** (MD David Bunei) as part of
  positioning Kenya as a regional digital hub — exploratory talks, no MoU signed. Source:
  [[2026-07-07 Kenya's State Department of ICT and Digital Economy discusses digital partnership with Oracle]].
  See [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **2026-07-07** — Kenya's **[[equity-group-holdings]]** signs an MoU with
  **[[africanenda]]** and the **[[gates-foundation]]** to build interoperable DPI across
  20–30 African markets; CEO **[[james-mwangi]]** named Continental DPI Champion. Deal:
  [[equity-africanenda-gates-dpi-partnership]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-06-23** — Nairobi is East Africa's most active data-centre market: **IXAfrica**
  (up to $200m debt; second hyperscale, 53 MW, on-chip cooling) hosts **Servernah Cloud**,
  "Kenya's first sovereign AI cloud" (March 2026). Kenya's **2024 Cloud Policy** mandates
  cloud-first for the public sector. Source:
  [[2026-06-23 Africa's Data Centre Boom Where The New Facilities Are Being Built]]. See [[infra.store]], [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2026-07-03** — **Kenya's Data Protection Act 2019** cited as increasingly
  enforcement-driven under AfCFTA expansion. Source:
  [[2026-07-03 AfCFTA, Data Governance and the Hidden Legal Gaps Reshaping Africa's Digital Economy - P.M. News]]. See [[gov.protect]].
- **2026-06-17** — Kenya named (with Rwanda) as having piloted AI-enabled health
  diagnostics, but national scaling remains financially challenging. Source:
  [[2026-06-17 Priced out of the future Confronting the rising cost of AI in Africa]]. See [[tech.ai]].
- **2026-06-23** — Carnegie's **[[jane-munga]]** proposes a **US–Kenya Technology
  Prosperity Deal** ([[technology-prosperity-deal]]) as a replicable model for
  structured US–Africa AI partnership — citing Kenya's AI strategy, Silicon Savannah
  ecosystem and US ties; the stalled [[microsoft-g42-kenya-datacenter]] and UAE links
  feature as context. See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]], [[pax-silica]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-23 A Kenya Technology Prosperity Deal Could Help Washington Secure Durable AI Partnerships with Africa]].
