# Mozambique (MOZ)

## Recent developments

- **2026-04-28** — **DPI partnership with Ethiopia.** Mozambique's new Digital
  Transformation and Innovation Agency (**[[atdi-mozambique]]**, chaired by Adilson dos
  Santos Cousin Gomes, appointed March 2026) signed an **MoU** in Addis Ababa with
  Ethiopia's National ID Program (NIDP, ED Yodahe Zemichael), an initial 3-year deal.
  Ethiopia's **[[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]]** business arm, FaydaVerse, will provide
  technical support on digital-ID platforms, DPI pilot projects, government-system
  interoperability, staff training and joint cybersecurity policy; ATDI coordinates and
  leads resource mobilisation. Framed as South-South cooperation and "technological
  sovereignty" / reduced vendor dependence. Deal: [[mozambique-ethiopia-dpi-mou]].
  Source: [[2026-04-28 Mozambique taps Ethiopia's digital ID expertise in DPI partnership]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-04-17** — **Parliament unanimously passes Cyber Security + Cybercrime laws.** The
  package (**[[mozambique-cyber-security-law]]**) makes the National Institute of
  Communications (INCM) the technical coordinator for cyberspace security, with functions
  passing to the Defence & Security Forces' Cyber Coordination Centre under a state of siege
  or emergency (Art. 12); it also creates a **Cyber Security Fund**. Minister of
  Communications and Digital Transformation Américo Muchanga framed it as consolidating and
  clarifying the legal framework. (Cost figures in the source are internally inconsistent —
  ~160m MZN ≈ US$2.5m vs 167,257.94m MZN — recorded, not reconciled.) Source:
  [[2026-04-17 Mozambique parliament unanimously approves Cyber Security Law]].
  See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2026-04-14** — **Globalstar spectrum + Starlink presence.** Amazon's mooted **~$9bn
  [[globalstar]]** acquisition ([[amazon-globalstar-acquisition]]) is flagged as relevant to
  Mozambique, where Globalstar holds **spectrum authorisations / partnerships** — a potential
  Kuiper shortcut into a market where [[starlink]] is already live. Not finalised as of this
  date. Source: [[2026-04-14 Amazon's $9bn satellite deal set to benefit Africa]]. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[geopol.usa]], [[digital.rural]].
- **2026-04-13** — **UNECA: keep African data under African control.** At the 58th ECA
  Conference of Ministers roundtable on DPI, data centres and sovereignty, Mozambique's
  Communications & Digital Transformation Minister **Américo Muchanga** argued DPI is now
  "as vital as electricity" and that states must **classify their data** — deciding what
  stays on-premises or in sovereign clouds — so African data remains under African control.
  Entity: [[uneca]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]]. See
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[infra.store]], [[gov.policy]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-04-04** — **Surveillance cameras in opposition strongholds.** An IDS report (Mar
  2026) singles out Mozambique as a case where Chinese-supplied cameras
  ([[huawei]] / [[zte]] / [[hikvision]]) were **concentrated in political-opposition areas**,
  absent a genuine security threat — surveillance repurposed against dissent without legal
  oversight. Source:
  [[2026-04-04 Chinese surveillance rolled out in Africa ZTE, Hikvision, Huawei at the helm]].
  See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.china]].
- **2026-02-24** — **Biometric IAM across the national payments network.**
  **[[bio-key-international]]** and regional integrator **[[runlevel]]** signed a multi-year
  deal to deploy biometric identity-and-access management (PortalGuard, PIN:You tokenless
  auth, SSO) into **[[simo-mozambique]]**, operator of the **SIMOrede** national
  electronic-payments network (interoperability, clearing, settlement) — hardening
  authentication for participating banks. Builds on a May-2025 BIO-key/RunLevel deployment
  for a Mozambican national bank. Source:
  [[2026-02-24 BIO-key and RunLevel Deploy Biometric IAM Across Mozambique's National Payments Network]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[dpi.id]].
- **2026-02-23** — **Zambia–Mozambique fibre interconnector.** The two states commissioned
  the **Chanida–Cassacatiza** cross-border fibre link, joining Zambia's national backbone to
  Mozambique's submarine-cable network for redundancy and cross-border data exchange
  (partnership of **[[bayobab-zambia]]** (MTN) and **[[vodacom]]**); Zambia's minister claimed
  fibre links to eight neighbours. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 Zambia and Mozambique launch fibre-optic interconnector at Chanida–Cassacatiza border]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]], [[digital.rural]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[ZMB]].
- **2026-02-21** — **Digital-government overhaul, modelled on Kenya and Rwanda.** At
  Mozambique's first national digital-transformation conference (Maputo), President **Daniel
  Chapo** announced a plan to digitise **all** government services — identity, licensing, tax,
  business registration via a centralised citizen portal — modelled on **[[KEN]]** and
  **[[RWA]]**, with a **Multi-Sector Technical Commission** to draft an integration roadmap by
  **mid-2026** ("no technological islands within the state"). Led by the new Ministry of
  Communications & Digital Transformation; **[[atdi-mozambique]]** is the standing agency.
  Mozambique ranks **177/193** on the 2024 UN E-Gov Index (~25% internet access). Source:
  [[2026-02-21 Mozambique Launches Digital Government Overhaul, Looking to Kenya and Rwanda as Models]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-02-06** — **Data-breach reporting platform.** **[[intic-mozambique]]** (National
  Institute of ICT; board chair Lourino Chemane) launched a **pilot platform for reporting
  personal-data breaches**, framed as consolidating the personal-data-protection regime and
  giving data subjects a simple complaints channel. (INTIC is distinct from ATDI, the
  digital-transformation agency.) Source:
  [[2026-02-06 Mozambique intros data breach platform]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-01-01** — **Starlink–Vodacom LEO backhaul covers Mozambique.** Mozambique is among
  the 25 African countries where **[[starlink]]** is live, covered by the
  **[[starlink-vodacom-leo-partnership]]** (LEO backhaul + equipment resale). Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]]. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[data.satellite]].
- **2016–2017** *(background; publ. 2021-09-13)* — **Health-data recording burden.** A
  five-country WHO study ([[rhis-recording-burden-5country]]) found Mozambique's PHC
  facilities carried the **most reporting forms (~52 mandated, 25 in use)** and spent the
  **highest share of consultation time on recording (~53%)**, yet the **lowest per-worker
  monthly reporting time (~4h)** — a function of very high patient loads. Source:
  [[2021-09-13 The burden of recording and reporting health data in primary health care facilities in five low- and lower-middle income countries]].
  See [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]], [[gov.standards]].
