# Eritrea (ERI)

One of the **least digitally-developed states in the Horn of Africa** — a state-controlled telecom
monopoly ([[eritel|EriTel]]), among the **lowest connectivity rates on the continent**, no operational
foundational digital ID, and a largely **cash-based, under-banked** economy under a state banking
monopoly. Digital development is early and state-led, and the evidence base is unusually
provenance-constrained: the only outlets producing dated digital/telecom items are state media
(Shabait / Ministry of Information) and a diaspora/opposition outlet (Awate) — neither neutral. Hard
data comes mainly from development partners ([[undp]]/UNECA) and reference scholarship. Attribute
state figures as government claims and opposition framing plainly.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-02** — **Bank of Eritrea banking ultimatum.** The government issued a legal notice
  (~30 June 2026) ordering individuals and institutions to **deposit cash held outside banks by
  31 July 2026** or face legal action, with no definition of "excessive amount" — context for the
  near-total absence of mobile money/digital payments in a cash-dominated economy. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[bank-of-eritrea]]. Source (opposition/Awate, with embedded state notice):
  [[2026-07-02 Eritrea's banking ultimatum - deposit cash by 31 July 2026]].
- **2026-05-19** — **Telecom-expansion claims (EriTel / ministry).** Minister of Transportation and
  Communications Tesfaselasie Berhane reported (state media, unverifiable) **184 towers**, **~90%
  geographic** mobile coverage, service "reaching 4G level," and **~1,536 Mbps** total bandwidth built
  over a decade, on **35bn+ Nakfa** cumulative investment. Geographic ≠ population coverage. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[include.divides]], [[eritel]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-19 Eritrea substantial investment in communication service expansion]].
- **2026-05-08** — **EPHS 2025 / data-systems modernisation (NSO, UNDP D4D).** The National Statistics
  Office completed the **Fourth Round Eritrea Population and Health Survey** — 9,794 households across
  405 EAs at **98.4% completion**, using **CAPI** digital collection — plus 2023/24 national accounts,
  under the Joint Programme on Data for Development with [[undp]]/UNECA. Eritrea's most concrete
  data-systems development on record. See [[data.statistics]], [[capacity.research]],
  [[eritrea-national-statistics-office]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-08 Eritrea strengthens data systems with EPHS 2025 (UNDP)]].
- **2026-01-08** — **Digital customs system launched (ASYCUDA World).** Eritrea inaugurated a
  UNCTAD **ASYCUDA World** customs platform (with **[[undp]]** support), replacing 2007-era
  software — notably with a **full source-code transfer to the government** for national ownership.
  Its most concrete GovTech step to date. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-08 Eritrea launches ASYCUDA World digital customs system]].
- **2025-11-01** — **Eri-Chat: domestically built offline AI app.** A Ministry of Information software
  developer (Naod Goitom Isaac) built **Eri-Chat**, an offline, open-source-AI mobile app storing
  conversation data locally on the device; launched Apr 2025, showcased at the Asmara UN Day event
  (Oct 2025). A rare concrete Eritrean AI/innovation datapoint. See [[tech.ai]], [[tech.innovate]].
  Source: [[2025-11-01 Eri-Chat offline AI app]].
- **2025-07-26** — **Digital-identity challenges profiled.** No operational foundational digital ID;
  low connectivity, weak civil registration and a telecom monopoly are the barriers. See [[dpi.id]].
  Source: [[2025-07-26 Eritrea's population digital identity - challenges ahead]].

### Standing profile

- **Connectivity (DataReportal).** **859,000** mobile connections in late 2025 (**23.7%** of
  population, up from 22.2% in early 2025) — among Africa's lowest. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[include.divides]]. Sources: [[2025-11-08 Digital 2026 Eritrea (DataReportal)]],
  [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 Eritrea (DataReportal)]].
