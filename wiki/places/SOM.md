# Somalia (SOM)

Somalia runs one of Africa's most sophisticated **private-sector-led** digital
economies — mobile money worth ~**$8bn / 36% of GDP** built by telecom operators in
the vacuum left by the 1991 collapse of central banking. The Federal Government is
now attempting its first serious **formalisation**: a data-protection regulatory
framework, a national DPI summit, and central-bank payment interoperability
(SIPS/SOMQR). The test is whether formal institutions can keep pace with the
market's velocity.

> **Sensitivity note.** Items involving Somaliland (e.g. Somcable, Telesom) are
> mapped to SOM for the place facet only; Somaliland's political status is contested
> and this mapping is not a statement on it.

## Recent developments

- **2026-02-06** — **Data-regulatory framework adopted.** Somalia's Council of
  Ministers approved the **Regulatory Framework for the Public Data Protection Law**,
  drafted by the Ministry of Communications & Technology
  ([[somalia-ministry-communications-technology]], minister Mohamed Adam Moalim), to
  operationalise the existing Public Data Protection Law and align the legal
  structure with the national digital-transformation strategy — a step toward public
  trust in government digital services. Source:
  [[2026-02-06 Somalia adopts data regulatory framework]]. See [[gov.protect]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-01-29** — **First National DPI Summit.** The Ministry of Communications &
  Technology and the Office of the PM announced Somalia's **first National Digital
  Public Infrastructure Summit** (Mogadishu, **14-15 Feb 2026**), organised with the
  **[[central-bank-of-somalia]]** (CBS), the National Communications Authority (NCA),
  **[[nira-somalia]]** (National Identification & Registration Authority) and the
  Data Protection Authority. Objective: a **cohesive national vision rooted in
  interoperability** to synchronise the currently disparate digital efforts. Source:
  [[2026-01-29 Mogadishu to Host First National Digital Public Infrastructure Summit; Key Agencies to Map Out Digital Transformation Strategy]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.policy]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-01-29** — **Ethio Telecom–Somcable cross-border talks.**
  **[[ethio-telecom]]** (CEO Frehiwot Tamru) and Somaliland's **[[somcable]]**
  (Somaliland Cable) opened discussions on a strategic cross-border digital/telecom
  partnership to share infrastructure and reach overseas markets. Talks only, no
  signed deal. Source: [[2026-01-29 Ethio Telecom, Somcable talk cross-border collaboration]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]], [[finance.mou]], [[ETH]].
- **2026-01-16** — **Unified Digital CRVS in production (OpenCRVS).** Somalia’s [[opencrvs]]-based Unified
  Digital Civil Registration System is now production-level across **30 districts** and **160 hospitals**,
  with **28,000+ births** registered at point of occurrence — foundational legal identity rebuilt in a
  fragile state via a digital public good.
  [[2026-01-16 OpenCRVS Somalia Unified Digital Civil Registration System]] · [[dpi.registry]] · [[include.access]]
- **2026-01-07** — **Horn of Africa framing.** Feature positioning Somalia (with
  **[[ETH]]** and **[[DJI]]**) as an emerging digital force. Somalia's mobile money
  processes ~**650m transactions/yr**, worth ~**$8bn (36% of GDP)**, with **~$2bn/yr
  diaspora remittances** carried by operators like Hormuud and Telesom in the state's
  absence — a survival mechanism, not a policy choice. In early 2025 the
  **[[central-bank-of-somalia]]** launched the **Somalia Instant Payment System
  (SIPS)** with a national QR standard (**SOMQR**) to bridge isolated wallets, and
  the NCA licensed **[[starlink]]** (Apr 2025) to reach areas targeted or "taxed" by
  al-Shabaab (which also exploits the same digital rails). Source:
  [[2026-01-07 Why Ethiopia, Somalia and Djibouti are starting to matter in digital Africa]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]], [[tech.industry]], [[finance.new]], [[XEA]].
