# Somalia (SOM)

Somalia runs one of Africa's most sophisticated **private-sector-led** digital
economies — mobile money worth ~**$8bn / 36% of GDP** built by telecom operators in
the vacuum left by the 1991 collapse of central banking. The Federal Government is
now attempting its first serious **formalisation**: a data-protection regulatory
framework, a national DPI summit, and central-bank payment interoperability
(SIPS/SOMQR). The test is whether formal institutions can keep pace with the
market's velocity.

> **Sensitivity note.** Items involving Somaliland (e.g. Somcable, Telesom) are
> mapped to SOM for the place facet only; Somaliland's political status is contested
> and this mapping is not a statement on it.

## Recent developments

- **2026-02-17** — **Somalia cleared to adopt and print the EAC e-Passport.** The EAC
  authorised Somalia to adopt and print the biometric-chip **East African Community
  e-Passport**, formally handed over in Dar es Salaam by Somalia's EAC envoy Ilyaas Ali
  Hassan, alongside a Somalia–Tanzania migration-cooperation MoU (16 Feb). Somalia became a
  full EAC member on 4 Mar 2024. Source:
  [[2026-02-17 Somalia cleared to adopt and print EAC e-Passport]]. See [[gov.regional]],
  [[dpi.id]], [[TZA]], [[XEA]].
- **2026-01-26** — **Cybersecurity Law approved.** The House of the People approved the
  **[[somalia-cybersecurity-law]]**, creating **SOM-CIRT**, a nine-member cybersecurity
  committee and an emergency intervention centre — after a Nov-2025 e-visa breach and an
  ITU GCI Tier-4/5 rating (2024). Source:
  [[2026-01-29 Somali Parliament approves Cybersecurity Law amid rising threats]]. See
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2026-01-21** — **NADRA cooperation deepened.** Somalia's Interior Minister and NIRA DG
  visited Islamabad to expand cooperation with Pakistan's **NADRA** on the national ID's
  development, security and management. Source:
  [[2026-01-21 Somalia seeks to strengthen national ID system through Pakistan partnership]].
  See [[dpi.id]].
- **2025-12** — **Digital ID a pillar of the >$100m SPRING project; >1m enrolled.** By late
  2025 **[[nira-somalia]]** had enrolled **over 1 million** Somalis with **>24 public
  services integrated**; NIRA is a named institution in the World Bank
  **[[somalia-spring-project]]**. Officials separately estimate ~$125m is still needed to
  complete the rollout (Jan 2026). Sources:
  [[2025-12-17 Somalia digital ID major pillar of World Bank-funded SPRING initiative]],
  [[2026-01-12 Somalia estimates $125 million needed to complete national digital ID rollout]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[finance.new]].
- **2025-09-16** — **Mass digital-ID registration drive launched.** On International
  Identification Day, NIRA launched the foundational-ID mass-registration campaign (pilot
  from 18 Aug in Shangani/Boondheer), targeting 3.5m Mogadishu residents, then Banadir, then
  **15m nationwide by end-2029**. Fewer than 16% held any government ID (2022 SIHBS). The ID
  ([[snid]]) is now mandatory for passports (1 Sep 2025), domestic travel (1 Jan 2026) and
  bank accounts (CBS, 29 Jul 2025). Foreign-build (NADRA) and data-residency concerns
  persist. Source:
  [[2025-09-17 Somalia launches mass registration drive for its foundational digital ID]].
  See [[somalia--dpi-id]], [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]].
- **2025-08-10** — **19-district digital civil registry piloted.** On Africa CRVS Day,
  MOIFAR piloted the **[[somalia-crvs]]** Unified Digital Civil Registration Service across
  19 districts (UNICEF/Germany-backed); only 3% of under-fives have a registered birth
  (2020 DHS). Source: [[2025-08-11 Somalia pilots 19-district digital civil registry]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]].
- **2025-03-25** — **SIPS built on BPC SmartVista.** The **[[somalia-instant-payment-system]]**,
  operated by Somalia Payment Switch under CBS, launched on BPC's SmartVista platform (Jan
  2025), with 7 institutions live and the SOMQR standard (2023). Source:
  [[2025-03-25 Somalia taps BPC to launch first nationwide instant payment system]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]].
- **2025-03-18** — **Digital voter registration unveiled.** The **[[somalia-national-electoral-commission]]**
  unveiled digital voter-registration equipment for local polls then scheduled for 30 Jun
  2025 — the first direct "one person, one vote" local elections in five decades. Source:
  [[2025-03-18 Somalia's Electoral Commission unveils digital voter registration equipment]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2024-02-24** — **Data Protection Authority inaugurated.** Somalia inaugurated its
  **[[somalia-data-protection-authority]]** under the **[[somali-data-protection-act]]**
  (Law 005/2023, effective 23 Mar 2023) — foundational to the Feb-2026 regulatory framework
  below. Source: [[2024-02-26 Somalia inaugurates its Data Protection Authority]]. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2026-02-06** — **Data-regulatory framework adopted.** Somalia's Council of
  Ministers approved the **Regulatory Framework for the Public Data Protection Law**,
  drafted by the Ministry of Communications & Technology
  ([[somalia-ministry-communications-technology]], minister Mohamed Adam Moalim), to
  operationalise the existing Public Data Protection Law and align the legal
  structure with the national digital-transformation strategy — a step toward public
  trust in government digital services. Source:
  [[2026-02-06 Somalia adopts data regulatory framework]]. See [[gov.protect]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-01-29** — **First National DPI Summit.** The Ministry of Communications &
  Technology and the Office of the PM announced Somalia's **first National Digital
  Public Infrastructure Summit** (Mogadishu, **14-15 Feb 2026**), organised with the
  **[[central-bank-of-somalia]]** (CBS), the National Communications Authority (NCA),
  **[[nira-somalia]]** (National Identification & Registration Authority) and the
  Data Protection Authority. Objective: a **cohesive national vision rooted in
  interoperability** to synchronise the currently disparate digital efforts. Source:
  [[2026-01-29 Mogadishu to Host First National Digital Public Infrastructure Summit; Key Agencies to Map Out Digital Transformation Strategy]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.policy]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-01-29** — **Ethio Telecom–Somcable cross-border talks.**
  **[[ethio-telecom]]** (CEO Frehiwot Tamru) and Somaliland's **[[somcable]]**
  (Somaliland Cable) opened discussions on a strategic cross-border digital/telecom
  partnership to share infrastructure and reach overseas markets. Talks only, no
  signed deal. Source: [[2026-01-29 Ethio Telecom, Somcable talk cross-border collaboration]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]], [[finance.mou]], [[ETH]].
- **2026-01-16** — **Unified Digital CRVS in production (OpenCRVS).** Somalia’s [[opencrvs]]-based Unified
  Digital Civil Registration System is now production-level across **30 districts** and **160 hospitals**,
  with **28,000+ births** registered at point of occurrence — foundational legal identity rebuilt in a
  fragile state via a digital public good.
  [[2026-01-16 OpenCRVS Somalia Unified Digital Civil Registration System]] · [[dpi.registry]] · [[include.access]]
- **2026-01-07** — **Horn of Africa framing.** Feature positioning Somalia (with
  **[[ETH]]** and **[[DJI]]**) as an emerging digital force. Somalia's mobile money
  processes ~**650m transactions/yr**, worth ~**$8bn (36% of GDP)**, with **~$2bn/yr
  diaspora remittances** carried by operators like Hormuud and Telesom in the state's
  absence — a survival mechanism, not a policy choice. In early 2025 the
  **[[central-bank-of-somalia]]** launched the **Somalia Instant Payment System
  (SIPS)** with a national QR standard (**SOMQR**) to bridge isolated wallets, and
  the NCA licensed **[[starlink]]** (Apr 2025) to reach areas targeted or "taxed" by
  al-Shabaab (which also exploits the same digital rails). Source:
  [[2026-01-07 Why Ethiopia, Somalia and Djibouti are starting to matter in digital Africa]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]], [[tech.industry]], [[finance.new]], [[XEA]].
