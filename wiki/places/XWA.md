# West Africa (XWA)

Regional hub for West Africa (ECOWAS space plus the Sahel bloc). Country detail lives on the
member hubs — e.g. [[NGA]], [[GHA]], [[BEN]], [[GMB]], [[SEN]], [[CIV]], [[LBR]], [[BFA]],
[[MLI]], [[NER]] — this page tracks genuinely region-level developments. Political/economic
blocs are entities: [[ecowas]], [[afcfta]], [[alliance-of-sahel-states]], [[watra]], [[smart-africa]].

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-15** — **WATRA turns the global cable-resilience report into a regional agenda.** Executive
  Secretary **Aliyu Aboki** — a member of the **[[international-advisory-body-on-submarine-cable-resilience]]**
  (ITU–ICPC; final report approved 2026-07-10, co-chaired by Nigeria's **[[bosun-tijani]]**) — called for
  stronger regional cooperation on submarine-cable resilience across the **16 [[watra]] member states**,
  arguing cable protection is now an **economic** priority, not a telecoms one. He anchored the case in the
  **March 2024** multi-cable break that disrupted digital payments, communications and commerce across
  several West African countries, and picked out the report's call for **regional cooperation and regulatory
  harmonisation** — using regional/intergovernmental frameworks to cut fragmented requirements and pool
  preparedness. Read together with WATRA's April-2026 subsea-resilience technical report, this is the
  implementation hook the region's soft law has been missing. See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[NGA]]. Sources: [[2026-07-15-watra-regional-subsea-resilience-plan]],
  [[2026-07-10-itu-icpc-advisory-body-cable-resilience-report]].
- **2026-04-12** — **WATRA moves from dialogue to regulatory tools.** The **[[watra]]** (West
  Africa Telecommunications Regulators Assembly, **16 member states**) concluded its 4th Working
  Groups Meeting in Ouagadougou (hosted by Burkina Faso's ARCEP), finalising technical reports on
  **5G deployment, submarine-cable resilience, cybersecurity, consumer protection and NGSO
  (non-geostationary satellite) regulation**. Region's digital economy cited at **$216bn**.
  Source: [[2026-04-13 WATRA rallies Africa for region's $216b digital economy]] ([[BFA]]). See
  [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]], [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]].
- **2026-03-11** — **WARDIP2: $137m for a common digital market.** The **[[world-bank]]** approved
  **$137m** for phase 2 of the **Western Africa Regional Digital Integration Program**
  ([[wardip]]) in **Benin, Liberia and Sierra Leone** — broadband for **>5m** citizens and
  digital public services for **>5m** more (~10.6m total), across three pillars (infrastructure/
  data-centre capacity, business environment, cross-border operation). WARDIP (launched 2023,
  also covering The Gambia, Guinea-Bissau, Mauritania) is run with **[[ecowas]]**, the
  **[[african-union]]** and **[[smart-africa]]**, and supports WAEMU regulatory harmonisation.
  Source: [[2026-03-16 New $137M initiative to enable digital service access for millions in three ECOWAS nations]]
  ([[BEN]], [[LBR]], [[SLE]]). See [[gov.regional]], [[infra.connect]], [[finance.new]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-02-24** — **Nigeria–Equatorial Guinea subsea cable.** **[[backbone-connectivity-network-nigeria]]**
  (CEO Ibrahim Dikko) and Equatorial Guinea's telecoms/AI minister signed a **subsea fibre
  agreement** in Malabo ([[nigeria-eqguinea-subsea-cable]]), adding a direct high-capacity
  West–Central Africa link plus a commercial data centre; framed as **regional data sovereignty**
  and redundancy (Nigeria already has 7 subsea cables; Equatorial Guinea historically reliant on
  ACE). Specs/investment undisclosed. Source:
  [[2026-02-24 Nigeria, Equatorial Guinea subsea cable strengthens connectivity]] ([[NGA]], [[GNQ]],
  [[XCA]]). See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.store]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-02-06** — **Nigeria's CBN fintech blueprint as a regional test.** Commentary on the
  **[[cbn]]** **Policy Insight Series 2025** ([[cbn-policy-insight-series-2025]]) reads its
  18-month roadmap — Single Regulatory Window, **regulatory passporting** pilots with Ghana,
  Kenya, Senegal and South Africa, a Responsible AI in Finance Hub, fintech credit guarantees —
  as a template for **ECOWAS/AfCFTA financial-services integration** (mutual licence recognition,
  interoperable credit rails, shared digital-ID standards). Source:
  [[2026-02-06 Nigeria just raised the bar for West African Fintech]] ([[ecowas]], [[afcfta]],
  [[NGA]], [[GHA]], [[SEN]]). See [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]], [[gov.standards]], [[gov.regional]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2026-02-04** — **PAPSS–Onafriq wallet rail, Nigeria→Ghana in naira.** **[[onafriq]]** and
  **[[papss]]** launched a **6-month pilot** (from 1 Dec, CBN-approved) for wallet-based outbound
  payments **fully in local currency, no hard-currency conversion**, connecting bank and
  mobile-money ecosystems for individuals, merchants and SMEs. PAPSS spans **160+ banks, 400m+
  accounts, 19 countries**; supports AfCFTA operationalisation. Source:
  [[2026-02-04 PAPSS, Onafriq launch new trade rail for West Africa]] ([[cbn]], [[afcfta]], [[NGA]], [[GHA]]).
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-01-30** — **SIGMAT customs interoperability showcased at the WCO.** **[[ecowas]]** presented
  **[[sigmat]]** (Interconnected System for the Management of Goods in Transit; launched 2019) at
  the World Customs Organization 2026 Technology Conference in Abu Dhabi; roadmap adds risk
  management, mobile/field capability, a regional guarantee-management system and a secure e-Hub
  for cross-border electronic message exchange — with **non-ECOWAS countries** now interested.
  Source: [[2026-01-30 ECOWAS PRESENTS 'SIGMAT' AT THE WORLD CUSTOMS ORGANIZATION 2026 TECHNOLOGY CONFERENCE IN ABU DHABI   Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS)]].
  See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[infra.capacity]].
- **2026-01-20** — **ECOWAS digital roadmap + AI-governance framework.** A three-day Thematic
  Dialogue (Lagos, 20–22 Jan) committed **[[ecowas]]** to a **Regional Digital Single Market**
  ("remove digital borders") for its **400m+** citizens and resolved to develop a **Regional AI
  Governance Framework** — priorities: interoperable fintech, a unified digital identity layer,
  and rural broadband; also flagged information-disorder/disinformation risks. Aspirational
  communiqué tied to ECOWAS Vision 2050. Source:
  [[2026-01-24 ECOWAS Adopts Digital Roadmap; Targets Unified Single Market and AI Governance for West Africa]].
  See [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]], [[infra.connect]].
- **2024-06** *(backfill / baseline)* — **WURI foundational ID.** The World Bank-supported
  **[[wuri-programme]]** (Regional West Africa Unique Identification for Regional Integration and
  Inclusion; six countries, implemented with **[[ecowas]]**) had by 2024 helped Benin reach ~**99%**
  legal+digital ID coverage (the "Certificat NPI/fID" / "C'est moi"), enrolling 2.2m more incl.
  4,000 diaspora, with QR-code mobile authentication. Baseline for the region's foundational-ID
  agenda. Source: [[2024-06-27 WURI digital ID project improving access to public services in Benin]]
  ([[BEN]], [[world-bank]], [[anip-benin]]). See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]], [[gov.regional]].
