# Botswana (BWA)

An **upper-middle-income, comparatively strong-performing** Southern-African digital economy — 4th of 32 in Sub-Saharan Africa on the 2025 innovation index — now assembling the DPI pieces: a first national data centre, a data-protection regime that finally took effect in 2025, biometric social payments, a digital Omang ID and SADC cross-border payment rails. The through-line is a **state building foundations at last** — several long-stalled elements (the 2018 data-protection act, the national DC) came good in 2024–2026.

## Recent developments

- **2026-03-18** — **Digital Services Act + Digital Services Authority; 1Gov-1Citizen at 52 services.**
  [[dpi.govtech]] Minister **David Tshere**'s FY2026/27 plan makes government **"digital-by-default"** via a
  **[[digital-services-act-botswana|Digital Services Act]]** and a new **Digital Services Authority** —
  1Gov-1Citizen e-services **33→52**, SD-WAN across **600 sites**, a **Local Access Network** of **13,400+**
  facilities, government migration to the Digital Delta DC + a second DC planned. ICT budget ~**$300m**
  (FY2026/27). Sources: [[2026-03-18-botswana-1gov-1citizen-digital-services-act-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-03-18-botswana-ict-ministry-budget-2026-27-itweb]]. See [[gov.legislate]], [[infra.connect]].
- **2025-11-26 — Tshere roadmap; ITU IDI 6th in Africa.** [[gov.policy]] One year in, Tshere set priorities:
  cheaper internet, rural fibre, the Digital Delta DC (opened 25 Nov), expanded 1Gov, modern cyber/data
  legislation and BOTSAT-1 commercialisation. Botswana ranks **6th in Africa on the ITU IDI 2025 (82.1)**,
  **81.4% internet penetration** (2.09m users, end-2025). Source:
  [[2025-11-26-botswana-digital-priorities-minister-tshere-wearetech]]. See [[infra.connect]].
- **2025-09-17 — PEMANDU Citizen Wallet + Cryptographic Card.** [[dpi.pay]] Botswana's clearest DPI
  programme: a **[[pemandu-citizen-wallet|Citizen Wallet]]** (unified e-wallet for subsidy delivery) + a
  **Cryptographic Card** (secure smart card), with Malaysia's PEMANDU Partners. Source:
  [[2025-09-17-pemandu-digital-ecosystem-citizen-wallet-techafricanews]]. See [[dpi.id]].
- **2026-06-25 — BOTSAT-1 to the Artemis Accords.** [[data.satellite]] Botswana became the **68th Artemis
  Accords signatory** (Tshere, NASA HQ) — a US space-governance alignment ~15 months after **BOTSAT-1**, its
  first satellite (BIUST-built, hyperspectral, SpaceX-launched, in-country ground station), whose data it is
  commercialising (with SKA participation). Full case: **[[botsat-1]]**. Sources:
  [[2026-06-30-botswana-signs-artemis-accords-techreviewafrica]], [[2025-03-13-botsat-1-launch-telecomreviewafrica]].
  See [[geopol.usa]], [[tech.innovate]].
- **2025-09→2026-05 — A three-way data-centre build-out.** [[infra.store]] Beyond BoFiNet's Tier-III
  **[[digital-delta-data-centre|Digital Delta]]** (national, sovereignty-framed), Botswana has a Gulf-linked
  **Kala DC** (Tlou Energy gas-to-power + UAE's Kala Data FZCO) and a proposed **solar hyperscaler/AI campus
  at Palapye** (US ChillMine × Dutch AAAS Energy). BoFiNet also invested **P100m** in the international
  gateway (100 Gbps, citizen-owned contractors) and was shortlisted to **host the SADC IXP**. Sources:
  [[2025-09-26-kala-data-centre-tlou-energy-itweb]], [[2025-10-06-dutch-us-firms-solar-powered-data-centre-palapye]],
  [[2025-04-16-bofinet-invests-p100m-internet-gateway]]. See [[infra.energy]], [[geopol.gulf]].

  ICT Academy across five universities; Tshere's China + Austria trip, Apr 2026), while **Liquid/Cassava**

- **2025-11→2026 — Cybersecurity Act + data-sovereignty turn.** [[infra.cybersec]] Botswana passed a
  **Cybersecurity Act (Nov 2025)** creating a **National Cybersecurity Authority** over critical
  infrastructure ([[botswana-cybersecurity-act-2025]]); the first Data Protection Commissioner **Kepaletswe
  Somolekae** began enforcing on NBFIs and cross-border flows; and Minister Tshere pledged to **amend the
  DPA to compel in-country data storage** ("the data sovereignty of the people"). TransUnion put Botswana
  at **0.9% suspected fraud — lowest in Africa**. → [[botswana--gov-protect]]. Sources:

- **2025→2026 — Crypto licensed but thin; digital-services VAT arrives.** [[gov.legislate]] Botswana

  **VASP-oversight gaps** ([[botswana-virtual-assets]]); and a **Remote Service VAT Act** took effect
  **1 Jun 2026** — **14% VAT on non-resident digital services** (~P450m/yr target; Botswana joining the ATAF
  digital-tax cohort). Sources: [[2026-02-23-crypto-oversight-gaps-persist-in-botswana]],

- **2025→2026 — Connectivity: cheaper data, SADC roaming, satellite for the last mile.** [[infra.connect]]
  BOCRA approved **BTC data-price cuts** and expanded the **One Network Area** to five more SADC states
  (roaming down 10–98.6%); **[[bocra|MNP]]** began (Dec 2025). Penetration is measured variously —
  **~73% (BOCRA 2025)** vs **81.4% (DataReportal, end-2025)**. Satellite fills the gaps: Starlink is framed
  as an "existential threat" to telcos yet donated to schools (Marang JSS), Liquid×Eutelsat/OneWeb and
  Paratus×Eutelsat serve remote/mining sites, and **BTC reached 61 villages** via satellite backhaul.
