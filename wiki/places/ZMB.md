# Zambia (ZMB)

## Recent developments

- **2026-06-17** — Zambia's Cabinet directed ministries to **adopt open-source
  technology**, alongside the Lusaka launch of the **National Spatial Data
  Infrastructure (NSDI) Policy and Portal** (open-source-built) to end fragmented
  geospatial data and vendor lock-in; aligned with the 8NDP and **UN-IGIF**. Source:
  [[2026-06-17 Zambia's govt leans towards open-source]]. See [[data.open]],
  [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.govtech]], and the open-standards thread in [[zambia--dpi-id]].
- **2026-06-08** — Zambia and **China** deepened digital-cooperation talks
  (infrastructure, emerging tech, skills), building on existing **[[huawei]]/ZTE**
  investments; statements of intent, no new deal. Source:
  [[2026-06-08 Zambia, China deepen digital ties]]. See [[geopol.china]], [[infra.connect]].
- **2026-04** — **New surveillance-enabling cyber laws before the 2026 polls.** Bloggers of Zambia's 6th
  digital-rights report flags the **Cyber Security Act No. 3 / Cyber Crimes Act No. 4 of 2025** (ISP
  real-time interception; a Central Monitoring Centre) and a CCTV (Public Protection) Bill, alongside a
  now-operational Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (even as Cabinet plans to repeal the 2021
  Data Protection Act) and a post-election **digital-ID (INRIS) deferral**. Rated "Partly Free" (62/100).
  [[2026-04-01 State of Internet Freedom in Zambia 2026 6th Edition]] · [[gov.legislate]] · [[gov.protect]]
- **2026-04-04** — **Chinese surveillance.** An IDS "Smart City Surveillance in Africa"
  survey lists **Lusaka** among Chinese-supplied ([[hikvision]] / [[zte]] / [[huawei]])
  smart-city deployments, finding mass surveillance **despite no terrorism threat** and
  **without adequate legal oversight**. Source:
  [[2026-04-04 Chinese surveillance rolled out in Africa ZTE, Hikvision, Huawei at the helm]].
  See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.china]].
- **2026-03-27** — **AFGHS pushback.** Carnegie analysis reports Zambia **raised
  data-sharing objections** during **America First Global Health Strategy**
  ([[america-first-global-health-strategy]]) talks — benefit-sharing concerns delayed
  signing, with negotiators reportedly seeking **25-year data sharing for 5-year aid**
  ("Washington plays hardball with Lusaka"). Source:
  [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]].
  See [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.mis]].
- **2026-03-24** — **MOSIP-based national digital ID: SI procurement + partner search.**
  [[smart-zambia-institute]] is seeking an international **system integrator** to deploy a
  **[[mosip]]-based** ID integrated with civil registration — insisting the build be
  **home-grown and on open standards to avoid lock-in** — targeting **full rollout by
  end-2026** and 80% of citizens in the digital economy. Funded via **[[dzap]]** (>$100m
  [[world-bank]] IDA); an **MoU with Ethiopia** ([[fayda-ethiopia-national-id]], [[ETH]])
  to learn from Fayda, plus [[gates-foundation]] talks. See **[[zambia--dpi-id]]**. Sources:
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia looking for MOSIP system integrator to support digital ID project]],
  [[2026-03-24 Zambia seeks partner for national digital ID system]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[finance.new]].
- **2026-02-27** — **PAYZ financial-inclusion partnership.** President Hichilema hosted the
  **[[payz]]** (Payments Association of Zambia) at State House (~26 Feb), alongside the
  Ministry of Technology & Science, **[[bank-of-zambia]]** and **[[zicta]]**, to accelerate
  digital financial services, cut cash dependency and push digital payments across government
  — with quarterly regulatory-harmonisation talks. ZedMobile, the country's **4th MNO**,
  pledged support for the National Financial Inclusion Strategy. Source:
  [[2026-02-27 Zambia partners with PAYZ to boost financial inclusion]]. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[include.access]], [[gov.policy]], [[finance.mou]].
- **2026-02-26** — **Zambia halts the $1bn US health MOU over data-sharing + mining ties**
  (anchor reporting; cf. the 2026-03-27 Carnegie bullet below). A leaked five-year draft
  ([[zambia-us-health-mou]]) under the **[[america-first-global-health-strategy]]** offered
  **~$1.012bn** in exchange for hiring 40,000 health workers and a **$400m** Zambian
  contribution, and — worse than the 16 other African deals — **10-yr health-data access +
  a 25-yr pathogen-data pledge**, with financing reportedly tied to **copper/cobalt** access.
  Health minister Muchima denied the mining link (15 Feb) and was fired three days later;
  Hichilema requested "revisions," reframing aid cuts as a chance to "take care of our own
  affairs." **[[africa-cdc]]**'s Kaseya backed renegotiation ("we want to own our data");
  **[[zimbabwe-us-health-mou]]** ($367m) was rejected over instant pathogen-data access, and
  Kenya's High Court froze its deal. Sources:
  [[2026-02-25 US accused of 'shameless exploitation' over proposed Zambian health aid deal]],
  [[2026-02-26 Zambia rejects U.S. health aid over mining partnership ties]]
  ([[usaid]], [[us-department-of-state]]). See [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]], [[finance.mou]],
  [[dpi.mis]].
- **2026-02-23** — **Zambia–Mozambique fibre interconnector.** The two states commissioned
  the **Chanida–Cassacatiza** cross-border fibre link (a **[[bayobab-zambia]]** / [[vodacom]]
  partnership), joining Zambia's national backbone to Mozambique's submarine-cable network for
  redundancy, reliability and cross-border data exchange. Minister Felix Mutati said Zambia is
  now fibre-linked to **eight neighbours** — "the most digitally connected country in Southern
  Africa" — crediting duty removal on digital-infrastructure imports. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 Zambia and Mozambique launch fibre-optic interconnector at Chanida–Cassacatiza border]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]], [[digital.rural]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[MOZ]].
- **2026-01-23** — **e-Government Act amendment.** [[smart-zambia-institute]] presented an
  **Electronic Government (Amendment) Bill** revising Act No. 41 of 2021, to give electronic
  submissions and digital authentication clear legal standing and remove requirements for
  physical documents/handwritten signatures — targeting fragmented, vendor-locked systems that
  block data sharing (framed by officials as governance/legal, not technical, failures).
  Source: [[2026-01-23 Zambia begins amending Electronic Government Act]]. See [[gov.legislate]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]].
- **2026-01-15** — **Viamo's AI voice service (AVA) as an inclusion tool.** Zambia's **Ask
  Viamo Anything** ([[viamo]]) — dial-in AI voice responses on any phone — is cited as a
  leading feature-phone digital-inclusion example, with **59% women users** in early GSMA
  studies. Source:
  [[2026-01-15 Leveraging AI to Bypass the Smartphone Barrier and Advance Digital Inclusion in Africa]].
  See [[tech.ai]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-01-13** — **Zambia to stop accepting cheques (June 2026).** Reporting on Kenya's
  cheque persistence notes Zambia will **stop accepting cheques from June 2026**, part of an
  African trend toward cheque phase-out. Source:
  [[2026-01-13 The rise of cheques as Kenya's preferred payment method]]. See [[dpi.pay]].
- **2026-01-08** — **Online passport application system.** [[smart-zambia-institute]]'s
  e-Government Division launched an **Online Passport Application & Payment System**, with
  **>1,000** passports already processed online — pitched on transparency, reduced delays and
  data protection; part of a paperless push projected to save **>ZMW400m (~$15m)** in
  stationery. Source: [[2026-01-08 Zambia unveils online passport application system]]. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]], [[include.access]].
- **2025-09-11** — World Bank appraised the **Digital Zambia Acceleration Project
  ([[dzap]])**: a **US$100M IDA grant** (+US$20M commercial financing) to accelerate
  connectivity, DPI and digital skills, implemented by [[smart-zambia-institute]]
  under the regional [[idea-mpa]] programme. Board-approved 27 Jun 2024; closes 2030.
  Source: [[2025-09-11 DZAP World Bank PAD]].

## Country snapshot (as of the 2025 PAD)

- ~20M people; 46% under 15. Sovereign default Nov 2020; 2024 drought cut hydro power
  to blackouts >20 hrs/day (since 5–7 hrs on-grid/day) — an [[infra.connect]] and
  energy constraint on digital services.
- Internet penetration ~21 per 100 (below ESA avg 28); fixed broadband >12% of GNI
  per capita; 4 MNOs (Airtel, MTN, Zamtel, Zedmobile); ~10,000 km fibre, +≥2,000 km
  planned; Starlink live since Oct 2023; 8NDP target 80% penetration by 2026.
