# Zambia (ZMB)

Southern African ([[XSA]]) state pursuing an unusually explicit **vendor-independence** agenda — a
home-grown, open-standards [[mosip]]-based foundational ID, a Cabinet directive to adopt open source, an
Ethiopia MoU chosen precisely *because* Fayda is domestically developed — while the programme is financed
and scaffolded from outside ([[dzap]]: **$100m [[world-bank]] IDA grant + $20m unguaranteed commercial =
$120m**). That tension is worked through in **[[zambia--dpi-id]]**.

The second tension: **an identity layer built on almost no civil registration**. Just **14% of births
registered (UNICEF, 2024)**, **6% of children with a certificate** — among Africa's lowest — against a
**50%-by-end-2026** target. The state concedes the base is weak: "**the current platform is hindering
government efforts to identify duplicate social program beneficiaries**" (2025-10-03).

The third is **evidentiary**: the headline numbers disagree. Penetration spans **2.7×**; INRIS enrolment
**moves backwards**. See *Contested figures* — recorded dated and attributed, none adopted.

## Country snapshot

- Population **~20M (2025 PAD)** / **21.6M (Jan 2025)**; **46% under 15 (2025 PAD)**; **52.8% rural (Jan
  2025)**. Sovereign default **Nov 2020**. The **2024 drought** cut hydro power to blackouts **>20
  hrs/day** (since eased to **5–7 hrs on-grid/day**) — an [[infra.connect]] constraint on digital
  services. *That argument is made by the **DZAP PID/PAD**, corroborated by Ookla's 38.0% YoY speed fall
  — not by the separately-held 2019 energy diagnostic, which carries no digital payload.*
- Fixed broadband **>12% of GNI per capita (2025 PAD)**; **4 MNOs** (Airtel, MTN, Zamtel, Zedmobile);
  **~10,000 km fibre, +≥2,000 km planned (2025 PAD)**; **Starlink live since Oct 2023**; **8NDP target
  80% penetration by 2026**.

### Contested figures (dated and attributed; none adopted)

- **Internet penetration — a 2.7× spread, almost certainly three definitions.** **~21 per 100** (DZAP
  PAD, 2025-09; *below the ESA average of 28*) · **33.0%** ([[datareportal]], Jan 2025) · **56.8%**
  ([[undp]], 2024-09). See `reviews/contradictions/open/zambia-internet-penetration`.
- **[[inris]] enrolment — it goes backwards.** **1.5m** (UNDP, 2024-09) · **~1.6m** implied (PAD's ~15%
  of adults, 2025-09) · **~1.3m** (ZNBC/MoHAIS, 2025-12). See
  `reviews/contradictions/open/zambia-inris-enrolment`.
- **Digital ID target.** **end-2027** ([[davies-chikalanga]], [[dnrpc-zambia]] — the operator) ·
  **end-2026** (Percy Chinyama, [[smart-zambia-institute]] — the coordinator); ZNBC adds biometric NRC
  issuance only **after the 2026 General Elections**. See
  `reviews/contradictions/open/zambia-digital-id-target`.
- **NRC coverage.** **"over 88 percent"** and **"89 percent"** of adults 16+ — *both in the same UNDP
  article (2024-09)* — against **~94% by 2021** (DZAP PAD).
