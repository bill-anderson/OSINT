# Burkina Faso (BFA)

Under military rule and having broken from **[[ecowas]]** to help form the
**[[alliance-of-sahel-states]]** (AES), Burkina Faso frames digital transformation
as an instrument of **national and Sahel sovereignty**. The anchoring policy is
**PACTDIGITAL-BF** (12 flagship projects by 2030), paired with a **"zero data
abroad"** localization doctrine, local data-centre build-out, and a
supervision centre for critical digital infrastructure.

## Recent developments

- **2025→2026 — AI: from "no strategy" to a Roadmap 2026–2030, sovereign by language.** [[tech.ai]]
  Burkina went from having **no national AI strategy** (2025) to a **validated Roadmap 2026–2030** (RELANCE
  Plan, "AI for All"), with a distinctive **sovereign-AI-by-language** flagship — an LLM corpus in Mooré,
  Dioula, Fulfuldé and Gulmancema ([[bf-ai-roadmap]]) — plus a Maison de l'IA, CITADEL ($1m IDRC), lawmaker
  (SPIVTEN) and US youth AI training, and a data-sovereignty-themed **Grand Salon de la Data** + SIPEN-UEMOA.
  Sources: [[2026-06-17-national-ai-roadmap-2026-2030-validated-techafricanews]],

- **2026-01→02 — CRVS + social registry deepen; WURI still live post-ECOWAS.** [[dpi.registry]] The SNEC
  civil-registration strategy hit **64.11%** execution (2024; 3.39m acts, 54 communes); **121,000** acts
  went to IDPs (2×target); the **Registre Social Unique** reached **362,698 households / 2.2m people** (99
  communes). A World Bank mid-term review confirmed **PACTDIGITAL + WURI still run jointly** in Burkina
  despite the ECOWAS exit, even as the **unique eID stalled on a failed tender** (7m enrolments targeted
  2026). Sources: [[2025-06-19-snec-taux-execution-physique-6411-2024]],

- **2025→2026 — Connectivity: closing "white zones" against a huge gap.** [[infra.connect]] The FASU/
  PACTDIGITAL **"zéro zone blanche"** drive (138 covered 2024; 750- then 1,000-locality tranches) targets
  the **~2,000 localities (10–12%)** still with no coverage — completion dated **2027** or **2030** (an
  unreconciled conflict → `reviews/contradictions/open/`). Operators met **80–90%** of 2025 site
  commitments (Orange/Moov 200 each, Telecel 150→300; Omdia Q3-2025: Orange 14.2m, Moov 12.7m, Telecel
  2.8m); Moov built a ZACA HQ; but tower rollout is **blocked by local opposition** in places. Basic-plan
  cost is still **~8.8% of GNI**. Burkina/Ghana/Mali/Niger **harmonised frequencies + free roaming** across
  the ECOWAS split. Sources: [[2026-07-13-pactdigital-1000-white-zones-2026]],
  [[2026-06-21-telecommunications-taux-execution-engagements-80-90]],

- **2025→2026 — Payments: on the UEMOA rail; a spread of platforms.** [[dpi.pay]] Burkina joined the
  BCEAO **[[pi-spi]]** instant-payment rail (live-test Aug 2025 → 30 Sep launch; 9 institutions authorised
  by Feb 2026). Alongside **[[faso-arzeka]]** government payments: Orange Money (11m clients) interconnected
  bank↔wallet with BSIC; Telecel Money reached 2,500+ pharmacies; **Wave** entered ministry talks; the
  Treasury launched **LANAYA + e-BDT** (WB-backed); a **certified e-invoice** became mandatory 1 Jul 2026;
  **e-Dimaakia** digitised official-travel procurement; **PAIF-PME** (7-yr WB) closed on financial
  inclusion. Sources: [[2025-06-05-orange-money-bsic-interconnexion-bank-to-wallet]],

- **2025 — CIL: a weak DPA elevated by the sovereignty drive.** [[gov.protect]] The data-protection
  authority **[[cil-burkina-faso]]** (President Dr Halguièta Nassa-Trawina) has **no repressive powers**
  (missing decrees) but rode a **PM-led ministry-by-ministry** campaign that made DP sign-off **mandatory
  pre-deployment**; its one enforcement act noticed a body for **hosting citizens' data offshore**. A first
  national **cybercrime week** created the Galian prize. Sources:
  [[2025-07-28-cadres-ministere-administration-territoriale-outilles]],
  [[2025-09-04-burkina-faso-strengthens-data-protection-digital-transition]],
  [[2025-11-04-semaine-nationale-lutte-cybercriminalite-cloture]]. See [[gov.legislate]], [[infra.cybersec]].
- **2026-01-23** — **Two national mini data centres commissioned ("zero data abroad").** [[infra.store]]
  PM Ouédraogo commissioned two mini data centres — **~3,000 TB** (10×), **7,000+ VMs** (100–300/ministry),
  **16bn FCFA**, projected **~30bn FCFA savings/5 yrs** from ending foreign hosting, run by national teams
  (top-3 sub-region for storage) — an intermediate step toward a **national Big Data Centre** (~2028)
  repatriating public and private data. National hosting rests on a **PM circular** (DG Tinto); health
  data needs a derogation to leave. Full case: **[[bf-national-datacenters]]**. Sources:
  [[2026-01-23-burkina-faso-asserts-digital-sovereignty-mini-data-centres]],
  [[2026-03-03-tinto-hebergement-national-souverainete]]. See [[gov.policy]], [[infra.cybersec]].
- **2025-11-18** — **Traoré: "sovereignty imperatively includes the digital."** [[gov.policy]] At the
  20th **Semaine du numérique** (Ghana guest of honour; Mali/Niger special guests), President Ibrahim
  Traoré framed digital as inseparable from sovereignty — objecting to paying software licences "to those
  who are fighting us" — with datacentres "en cours d'acquisition" and a new polytechnic academy, atop the
  **twelve national chantiers** ([[bf-ministry-digital-transition]]). Sources:

- **2026-05-25** — **BFIX peering slashed the cost of access.** Internet Society data: Burkina Faso's IXP
  (BFIX, ISOC-supported since 2019) grew peak capacity 0.5→6 Gbps and cut local latency from >300ms to
  15–20ms; the income share for a basic mobile-broadband plan fell from **13% (2020) to 0.2% (2025)** —
  the largest affordability gain in a 50-country sample. [[2026-05-25 Local Infrastructure Lower Costs How Peering Is Moving the Needle on Internet Affordability]] · [[infra.connect]] · [[include.access]]
- **2026-03-23** — **PACTDIGITAL-BF anchors the strategy.** The Ministry of Digital
  Transition ([[bf-ministry-digital-transition]], minister Aminata Zerbo-Sabané)
  detailed **Fast-Tracking Digital Transformation (PACTDIGITAL-BF)** — 12 major
  digital projects by 2030, in force since Sept 2024 (5-yr), funded by a **~$170m
  (96.7bn CFA)** World Bank **[[world-bank]]** IDA loan plus national counterpart.
  It interconnects government buildings via a secure state intranet, builds a
  national data centre for data sovereignty (two **mini data centres** commissioned
  in Ouagadougou Jan 2026 under "zero data abroad"), and runs five components
  including connectivity, public-service transformation and skills. Source:
  [[2026-03-23 Burkina Faso steps into the future with digital investment]]. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[infra.store]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[capacity.training]].

  ≥R+7 building): Onatel CFA117.19bn, Orange CFA332.91bn, Telecel CFA40.44bn (ARCEP 2024). An
  economic-sovereignty counterpart to data localisation. [[arcep-burkina-faso]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Burkina Faso Orders Large Firms to Build Local Headquarters]]. See [[gov.legislate]].

- **2026-01-22** — **Infrastructure Supervision Center / "zero data abroad."** The
  government ([[bf-ministry-digital-transition]]) began work on a **Digital
  Infrastructure Supervision Center** (~**$5.4m**, 9-month build, delivery expected
  Oct 2026) to centralize supervision of the national backbone, secure cyberspace,
  and oversee data-centre operations — an "essential tool for sovereign management of
  national digital systems." Advances the "none of the country's data stored abroad"
  policy and positions BFA for AI adoption; follows a **Nov 2025 new-generation
  biometric ID system** and the AES common biometric passport. Source:
  [[2026-01-22 Burkina Faso aims for digital sovereignty with infrastructure supervision center]].
  See [[infra.cybersec]], [[infra.store]], [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]].
