# Mauritania (MRT)

A sparsely-populated West-African state that has moved fast on **foundational identity and e-government**: a
biometric national register with **~94% coverage (2025)** run by the **[[anrpts-mauritania|ANRPTS]]**, a
citizen-facing digital-ID and services app (**[[houwiyeti|Houwiyeti]]**, built on **[[tech5|TECH5]]** with
UNDP), and a growing e-gov stack (the **Ijraati** 800-procedure portal) under the **[[mtnima-mauritania|
MTNIMA]]** ministry and its **National Digital Transformation Agenda 2022–2025**. Infrastructure is thin but
improving — a new **EIB-financed Tier III data centre** in Nouakchott — while a low **EGDI (165th, 2024)** and
fourth-tier cybersecurity ranking mark the distance still to travel.

## Recent developments

- **2026-02-22** — **Ijraati e-government portal (800+ procedures).** The **[[mtnima-mauritania|MTNIMA]]**
  ministry launched **"Ijraati,"** consolidating **800+ administrative procedures** into one directory
  (documents, processing times, agencies, tracking), with a three-phase roadmap feeding the **"Khdamati"**
  remote-transactions platform. Recent additions span university enrolment, vehicle registration,
  criminal-record and investor services. Mauritania ranks **165th on the 2024 UN EGDI (0.3491)** and
  fourth-tier on the 2024 ITU cybersecurity index. Source:
  [[2026-02-22 Mauritania Centralizes 800 Administrative Procedures in New Digital Portal (Ijraati)]]. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-01-15** — **Biometric SIM enforcement.** The telecoms regulator **[[are-mauritania|ARE]]** issued
  **formal notices to Mattel, Mauritel and Chinguitel** over subscriber-identification failures, giving a
  two-month deadline before sanctions (up to 3% of turnover). Law conditions any SIM/eSIM sale on prior
  biometric identification and personal-data protection; ARE had ordered deactivation of non-biometric SIMs
  in October 2023. Source:
  [[2026-01-16 Mauritania Steps Up Pressure on Telecom Operators Over Biometric Identification]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.protect]].
- **2025-05-08** — **Nouakchott Tier III data centre inaugurated.** President Ghazaouani inaugurated the
  national data centre (8 May 2025), a **€15m EIB-financed Tier III** facility (Uptime-certified) that lets
  Mauritania host national data locally and improve data-exchange security. Part of the **West African
  Regional Communications Infrastructure Programme (WARCIP)**; interconnects with the ACE submarine cable
  (2010) and a second cable contracted to Ellalink; to be operated by **International Mauritania Telecom
  (IMT)**. Sources: [[2025-05-08 Mauritania - Nouakchott data centre inaugurated (EIB)]],
  [[2025-08-20 Construction of a Datacenter in Nouakchott, Mauritania (EEAS)]]. See [[infra.store]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-05-20** — **94% ID coverage (ID4Africa 2025).** At ID4Africa 2025, the **[[anrpts-mauritania|
  ANRPTS]]** reported **>94% of the population enrolled** in the national ID system since 2020, via the
  **SIGPTS** integrated platform; the **[[houwiyeti|Houwiyeti]]** digital-ID/e-gov platform (launched 2023)
  now carries **70% of passport requests** and processed **622,000+ document extracts** in a year. Source:
  [[2025-05-20 African nations making digital ID gains in the face of common challenges (ID4Africa 2025)]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2024-08** — **Houwiyeti digital ID launched (TECH5 / UNDP).** Building on a **[[undp|UNDP]]**-backed pilot
  (RFP 2022; **[[tech5|TECH5]]** won June 2022 with local integrator SmartMS), Mauritania launched the
  **[[houwiyeti|Houwiyeti]]** app — selfie-based identity verification giving residents access to government
  e-services, aligned with open-source DPI and privacy-by-design. Sources:
  [[2024-08-15 Mauritania Launches Digital ID for e-Services, Built on TECH5 Platform (Houwiyeti)]],
  [[2024-08-02 Mauritania launches digital ID app to boost access to services and improve governance]],
  [[2024-08-01 Mauritania pilots digital ID app (e-ID Mauritania)]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]].
- **2023-11-27** — **National Digital Transformation Agenda 2022–2025.** In an interview, digital minister
  Mohamed Abdallahi Louly set out the agenda's four axes (infrastructure, digital government, sector
  transformation, e-business/innovation), a Startup Act, the **IBTIKAR** tech park, e-gov rollout (12 of 26
  ministries digitised for mail) and ambitions to attract a first-class data centre. *(Advertorial ministerial
  interview.)* Source: [[2023-11-27 Setting the agenda for digital transformation]]. See [[gov.policy]],
  [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-03-03** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2025.** Population 5.24m; **internet penetration
  37.4%**; 6.14m mobile connections (117%); 1.57m social-media identities (29.9%). Source:
  [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - Mauritania (DataReportal)]]. See [[data.statistics]].
- **2021-06-22** *(baseline)* — **World Bank Second Private Sector, Digital & Human Capital Reform DPF.** A
  US$30m IDA grant (2nd of 3) with pillars on the business environment / financial inclusion, competitive
  broadband, and human capital / climate resilience. Cited, not absorbed. Source:
  [[2021-06-22 Mauritania Second Private Sector, Digital and Human Capital Reform DPF (P171238, World Bank)]].
