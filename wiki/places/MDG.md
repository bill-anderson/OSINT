# Madagascar (MDG)

One of Africa's poorest states, but pursuing a **World Bank-anchored digital-ID and e-government build-out**
under **[[prodigy-madagascar|PRODIGY]]** — a unified biometric national ID (NUI), civil-registry
modernisation and an X-Road-based interoperability platform ([[ugd-madagascar|UGD]]) — alongside a fast-growing
mobile-money economy ([[mvola]], Orange Money) now testing a **[[e-ariary|CBDC]]**. Connectivity is strong on
speed (private fibre; a BPO destination) but thin on reach: **internet penetration ~20% (end-2025)**, ~80%
offline, with a large rural divide.

- **2026-01-27/28** — **National biometric-ID pilot launched (PRODIGY).** Madagascar began pilot enrolment
  for its national digital ID, enrolling President Randrianirina and government first to test the
  infrastructure (biographic data + face, iris and fingerprint → a **Unique Identification Number**), mainly
  in the **Antsimondrano** district. Run by the **[[oni-madagascar|ONI]]** under the
  **[[mg-ministry-digital-development|MNDPT]]**; iris hardware from **IriTech**. Nationwide rollout targets
  **2m adults (Apr–Jun 2026)**, then ~8m rural residents; 12m+ citizens overall. Sources:
  [[2026-01-27 Madagascar Tests Biometric ID System, Aims to Register 2 Million People by Mid-2026]],
  [[2026-01-28 Madagascar Begins Pilot Biometric Enrollment for National Digital ID Program]],
  [[2026-01-28 Madagascar digital ID pilot kicks off, testing infrastructure ahead of nationwide rollout]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[prodigy-madagascar]].
- **2025-08** — **PRODIGY extended to mid-2026; interoperability platform scale-up.** The project's closing
  date was pushed to **30 June 2026**, refocusing on core ID infrastructure (biometric ID system, data
  centre, civil-registry integration); the **[[ugd-madagascar|Digital Governance Unit]]** issued an EOI for a
  firm to scale the **X-Road-based national interoperability platform**. Sources:
  [[2025-08-19 Madagascar's digital governance, ID project timeline extended to 2026]],
  [[2025-08-28 Madagascar seeks firm to expand digital interoperability platform]]. See [[dpi.exchange]],
  [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-08-04** — **Digital economy (US Country Commercial Guide).** The digital sector = **18% of growth /
  1.7% of GDP**; Madagascar claims the **fastest internet speed in Africa** and is a BPO destination (Amazon,
  Deliveroo, Air France). The **Digital Transformation Plan 2023–2028 (PSN)** targets a +6pp GDP contribution
  by 2028; regulator ARTEC; laws on cybercrime (2014-006), telecom (2005-023) and digital money (2016-056).
  Source: [[2025-08-04 Madagascar - Digital Economy (Country Commercial Guide)]]. See [[gov.policy]],
  [[infra.connect]].
- **2025-07-07** — **Civil-registry reform codified; biometric ID-card tender.** A bill on the identification
  of natural persons won Senate approval (4 Jul 2025), modernising the national ID system and recognising the
  unique-identification certificate as a legal ID; a large-scale biometric ID-card printing tender is
  imminent (likely bidders **[[in-groupe|IN Groupe]]** and Laxton, already PRODIGY contractors). Source:
  [[2025-07-07 Madagascar codifies civil registry reform, readies biometric ID card tender]]. See
  [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.id]].
- **2025-05-28** — **e-Ariary CBDC pilot.** The central bank launched a **10-month pilot** (23 May 2025) of
  its **[[e-ariary]]** digital currency — smartphone, basic-phone and offline (QR/smart-card/POS) — to cut
  cash costs and widen rural access, complementing mobile money. Source:
  [[2025-05-28 Madagascar Tests e-Ariary, Its Central Bank Digital Currency, to Modernize Payments]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].
- **2025-03** — **Mobile-money deepening.** **[[mvola]]** became **GSMA Mobile Money API compliant** (case
  study Mar 2025; 10m+ customers), and **[[orange|Orange]]** launched **Orange Money Pro** for merchants
  (18 Mar 2025). Sources:
  [[2025-03-31 MVola Madagascar - A case study for the GSMA Mobile Money API and open platforms]],
  [[2025-03-19 Madagascar - Orange Launches Mobile App (Orange Money Pro) for Business Transactions]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]].
- **2025-03-04** — **Digital Readiness Assessment (UNDP).** MNDPT + UNDP launched a five-pillar DRA (27 Feb
  2025, Antsirabe) to diagnose gaps: limited infrastructure, high connectivity cost (mobile internet ~15.5%
  of monthly GNI/capita), a ~40,000-technician skills shortage. Source:
  [[2025-03-04 Madagascar Launches Digital Readiness Assessment to Tackle Hurdles]]. See [[gov.policy]],
  [[include.access]].
- **2024-07-25** — **Inclusion-first unique identifier.** During its household census, Madagascar fast-tracked
  a **unique identifier** so families can access state services immediately, ahead of the slower biometric
  enrolment — prioritising inclusion over fraud prevention, with biometrics to follow. Source:
  [[2024-07-25 Madagascar accelerates unique identifier delivery for citizens]]. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[include.access]].
- **2025-11-08** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2026.** Population 32.9m; **internet penetration
  20.4%**; 21.8m mobile connections (66.2%); 4.15m social-media identities. ~80% offline. Source:
  [[2025-11-08 Digital 2026 - Madagascar (DataReportal)]]. See [[data.statistics]].
