# Togo (TGO)

West African ([[XWA]]) state that is, on the evidence here, **the region's digital-ID reference
implementation** — the country Senegal sent a delegation to study. Its e-ID runs on **[[mosip]]**
under an MoU signed **2021**, inside the World Bank's **[[wuri-programme]]**, on a legal base laid
unusually early: a **data protection law in September 2019**, a **foundational unique
identification law in September 2020** creating **[[anid-togo]]**, cybersecurity amendments in
**June 2022** creating ANCy, and a data protection authority, the **[[ipdcp-togo]]**. Togo also
authored **[[novissi]]**, the fully-digital pandemic cash-transfer programme that remains one of
Africa's most-cited G2P precedents.

Two caveats temper the story. First, **no cumulative e-ID enrolment figure exists in any 2025
source** — a programme repeatedly described as advancing, with no coverage number attached
(GAP-TGO-002). Second, **provenance is concentrated**: Togo First supplies nine of the nineteen
Togo clips in this batch and is the sole source for most of the current-state picture, including
the payments, AML, civil-registry and e-ID-rollout threads.

## Recent developments

- **2025-12-30** — **New AML law adopted.** The National Assembly adopted a law against money
  laundering, terrorist financing and WMD proliferation during the budget session
  ([[togo-aml-law-2025]]), **replacing a 2018 statute** and incorporating updated **[[fatf]]**
  recommendations and a **2023 [[waemu]] directive**. It introduces a **risk-based approach**,
  extends due diligence to **virtual asset service providers**, and strengthens **beneficial
  ownership** identification — the last two being the genuine data-governance content. Finance
  Minister Essowe Georges Barcola framed it as safeguarding financial-system integrity and
  reducing the opacity of flows linked to organised crime. Source:
  [[2025-12-30 Togo Adopts New Law to Strengthen Anti-Money Laundering Rules]]. See
  [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-12-15** — **Civil-status certificates go online.** Requests for birth, marriage and death
  certificates opened via the **[[services-publics-togo]]** national portal, live in **five pilot
  communes** — Agoè-Nyivé 1, Golfe 1, Avé 2, Zio 2 and Lacs 1 (Golfe 3 was already on the
  platform) — with remote submission, real-time tracking and an online chat tool. The platform
  includes "a secure interface designed to protect the confidentiality of personal data". The
  Ministry of Energy separately added **29 procedures** covering **[[ceet]]**, AT2ER and ARSE;
  other departments were expected to join from **January 2026**. Ministers Hodabalo Awate
  (Territorial Administration) and **[[cina-lawson]]** (Digital Transformation) committed to
  extending it nationwide. Sources:
  [[2025-12-16 Digitalisation - civil status act requests now possible online]] (the state portal),
  [[2025-12-17 Togo Expands Public Service Digitisation With Online Civil Registry Platform]]. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.localgov]].
- **2025-12-10** — **The data-protection authority gains access to the broadcast monitoring room.**
  **[[haac-togo]]** (the audiovisual regulator) and the **[[ipdcp-togo]]** sealed an institutional
  cooperation accord ([[haac-ipdcp-accord]]) on media regulation and digital rights. The operative
  mechanism: **HAAC authorises the IPDCP to access its control and monitoring room**, enabling
  tracking of broadcast content and coordinated response to personal-data breaches. Both presidents
  stressed that press freedom "cannot hold if the protection of digital rights remains fragile".
  *The source, which comes from the IPDCP itself, offers no critical framing of a data-protection
  authority acquiring access to a broadcast-surveillance facility.* Source:
  [[2025-12-12 Personal data and digital rights in Togo - HAAC and IPDCP seal institutional cooperation accord]].
  See [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]].
- **2025-12-18** — **Urban addressing modernised in Greater Lomé.** The government began
  modernising urban addressing in the **Autonomous District of Greater Lomé** ([[dagl-togo]]):
  databases integrating coded and named roads plus metric address points each carrying an entry
  number, signage plaques, and certification of "doors" in the database — preceded by updating the
  geographic address database of the **13 communes**. DAGL Secretary-General **Tagba Atafeyinam
  Tchalim**: "The address is a central element of domiciliation and an essential tool for access to
  public and private services." Source: [[2025-12-18 Urban addressing - modernisation effort]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.localgov]], [[gov.standards]].
- **2025-11-22** — **e-ID enrolment reaches the Maritime region.** **[[anid-togo]]** opened
  biometric enrolment in Zone 1 of the Maritime region (Vo, Yoto, Bas-Mono and Lacs prefectures),
  following rollouts in Golfe, Agoè-Nyivé and Savanes, with **[[inseed-togo]]** managing field
  personnel. Every resident is to receive a **Unique Identification Number (NIU)** as a single
  access key to public, private and social services; **children aged 0–5 are registrable using a
  parent's NIU**. Financed by the World Bank under [[wuri-programme]]. *No cumulative enrolment
  count is given (GAP-TGO-002).* Source:
  [[2025-11-19 Togo Advances Digital ID Program With New Enrollment Phase in Maritime Region]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], → [[togo--dpi-id]].
- **2025-11-19** — **Pre-2007 land titles georeferenced.** **[[otr-togo]]**, through the Direction
  du Cadastre et de la Conservation Foncière, is georeferencing land titles created **before
  2007** — often established without precise georeferencing, producing disputes and overlapping
  titles — to modernise the cadastre and secure property rights. *An official, self-promotional
  publication carrying **no parcel counts, budget or completion date**.* Source:
  [[2025-11-19 Georeferencing of land titles - a revolution for security and transparency in Togo]].
  See [[dpi.registry]], [[data.satellite]].
- **2025-10-27** — **Senegal benchmarks Togo's e-ID.** A delegation from Senegal's Ministry of
  Communication and Digital Economy visited Lomé to study the e-ID project under
  [[wuri-programme]]. ANID's Yasmine Mama: "We showed the technical architectures, infrastructures,
  and logic deployed within the context of the e-ID project. We discussed the technical solution,
  **MOSIP**, all its components, and how the country integrated it into Togo." Sources:
  [[2025-10-30 Senegal Benchmarks Togo's e-ID System for Digital Transformation]] (the origin
  account), [[2025-11-03 Senegal taps inspiration from Togo's digital ID experience]] (which adds
  the 2021 MOSIP MoU, [[novissi]] and Senegal's New Deal Technologique). See [[dpi.id]],
  [[gov.regional]], [[SEN]].
- **2025-09-30 / 2025-11-18** — **WAEMU instant payments arrive, in two dated states.** The
  **[[bceao]]** launched the interoperable **[[waemu-instant-payment-platform]]** in Dakar on
  **30 September 2025** (Governor Jean-Claude Kassi-Brou; broadcast to Lomé), covering the eight
  UEMOA states with instant, irrevocable transfers across banks, MFIs, e-money issuers and PSPs,
  backed by the **[[gates-foundation]]** and the **[[african-development-bank]]**. **At 2025-10-01:
  three banks operational in Togo** — Coris Bank, **[[ecobank]]**, **[[orabank]]** — with BOA,
  BIAT, Cofina and Sunu Bank Togo planned. **At 2025-11-18: seven committed / preparing
  interconnection** — Ecobank, Orabank, BOA, Coris Bank, COFINA, Banque Atlantique, BIAT. *These
  are different states, not a simple supersession: the November list drops Sunu Bank and adds
  Banque Atlantique, and "committed/preparing" is a weaker claim than "operational".* A **developer
  sandbox** for BCEAO's API-Business followed on **2025-11-18**. Sources:
  [[2025-10-01 BCEAO Launches Instant Payments Platform in Togo with Three Banks]],
  [[2025-11-18 BCEAO Launches Developer Sandbox for Instant Payments]]. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-10-31** — **Ecobank–Thunes cross-border transfers, Togo first.** Lomé-headquartered
  **[[ecobank]]** launched instant cross-border transfers with Singaporean fintech **[[thunes]]**
  ([[ecobank-thunes-partnership]]) — real-time send/receive to and from **130+ countries** over
  Thunes' Direct network, **Togo the first market** of an eventual rollout across the group's 35
  markets. Source: [[2025-10-31 Ecobank launches instant international payment service in Togo]].
  See [[dpi.pay]].
- **2025-09-30** — **Gest-exo: tax exemptions online.** [[otr-togo]] launched **[[gest-exo]]** in
  Lomé for exemption and waiver requests, with full traceability and **automatic integration with
  the [[sydonia]] (ASYCUDA) customs system**; **mandatory from 1 January 2026** after a three-month
  transition. Source: [[2025-10-01 Togo Launches Online Platform for Tax Exemptions and Waivers]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]].
- **2025-04-07** — **Electoral roll revised.** Revision ran **7–23 April 2025** nationwide ahead of
  the municipal elections, by presidential decree of 1 April. **[[ceni-togo]]** trained **324
  trainers**, who trained nearly **6,000 data-entry operators**. The prior electoral census for the
  April 2024 legislative and regional elections registered **more than 4.2 million voters**. *This
  is a register distinct from the ANID e-ID/NIU system — the two must not be conflated.* Source:
  [[2025-04-02 Municipal elections in Togo - revision of electoral rolls begins 7 April]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]].
- **2025-03-20** — **WAEMU consumer-protection directive: consultation, not adoption.**
  Stakeholders met in Lomé on **20–21 March 2025**, convened by the Ministry of Commerce and the
  **[[waemu]]** Commission, to discuss the bloc's **June 2023** consumer-protection directive.
  *Despite the headline, this is a stakeholder workshop on alignment — no adoption instrument, and
  the directive concerns general consumer protection rather than data or digital rights.* Source:
  [[2025-03-24 Togo Adopts WAEMU Consumer Protection Framework]]. See [[gov.regional]].
- **2024-12-18** — **$100m to accelerate digital transformation.** The World Bank approved the
  **first round** of a series of IDA operations — **$100 million (≈FCFA 62 billion)**
  ([[togo-digital-acceleration-financing]]) — to connect approximately **8,000 public institutions**
  to broadband and give **more than one million people** new or improved internet access, plus
  IFC-financed digital skills and entrepreneurship and climate-resilient connectivity.
  **[[cina-lawson]]**: "This initiative is a game-changer for Togo, as we connect our schools,
  health facilities, and public institutions to the very high-speed internet." *A separate operation
  from WURI's foundational-ID money — the two must never be summed.* Source:
  [[2024-12-18 Togo - $100 Million in Funding to Accelerate Digital Transformation]]. See
  [[finance.new]], [[infra.connect]], [[capacity.training]].
- **2024-11** — **DHIS2 education MIS, with its start date contested.** **[[hisp-wca]]**, with Norad
  and GPE KIX support, built a **[[dhis2]]**-based EMIS for **[[mepsta-togo]]**, whose **16,000**
  nursery, primary and secondary schools had collected data on paper for entry into
  **[[stateduc]]**. DHIS2 processes and visualises while StatEduc remains the collection tool;
  indicators are disaggregated by gender and disability status, with school report cards, a WhatsApp
  bot for exam results and MICS-EAGLE integration. Togo is one of **six** education ministries in
  the global DHIS2 for Education research project — alongside **[[SWZ]] Eswatini**, Mozambique, The
  Gambia, Sri Lanka and Uganda. **Resolved (2026-07-20):** 2019 = DHIS2 education-sector engagement begins; 2021 = StatEduc
  interoperability go-live. The "under review by the Ministry" line dates to the article's **English
  original of 2022-12-01** (the held French page is a translation), not 2024. DHIS2 sits as the national
  **analytical** layer above CSPro/StatEduc, which remains the census collection tool - so "national
  scale" cannot be claimed for DHIS2 as collection. In operational use as of 2026-05-04, strongest at
  regional level.
  Sources:
  [[2024-11-19 HISP WCA develops DHIS2 EMIS for education data analysis and visualization in Togo]],
  [[2024-11-05 Togo - Integrating DHIS2 with existing EMIS systems]]. See [[dpi.mis]],
  [[data.statistics]].
- **2024-03-06** — **Regional ID integration goes backwards.** The ECOWAS exits of Burkina Faso,
  Mali and Niger after military coups mean their citizens lose the **ECOWAS passport / biometric ID
  card** and drop out of [[wuri-programme]] — all three had benefited from it. Togo's own WURI test
  registrations were planned for **5–15 March 2024**, having slipped from a Q4-2023 target.
  Reported funding: **$72 million for Togo** of a **$395 million** WURI total (per Togo First).
  Source: [[2024-03-06 1 step forward, 3 steps back for West Africa's regional digital ID projects]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.regional]], [[XWA]].
- **2026-06-02** — **Private sovereign cloud at the Tier III Lomé Data Centre.** State-owned
  **Société d'Infrastructures Numériques ([[sin-togo]]**, DG Attia Byll), which operates the
  **[[lome-data-centre]]** (LDC), signed a strategic partnership with **ST Digital Togo** (DG
  Justine Adebiyi) to launch private cloud services at the facility. Togolese firms' data to be
  hosted and processed in-country to cut reliance on foreign infrastructure, with deliberately
  affordable tiers and continuing-education skills programmes. Deal:
  [[sin-st-digital-lome-cloud-deal]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-02 SIN, ST Digital to deploy cloud services at Lomé data center]]. See [[infra.store]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]].
