# Sudan (SDN)

A conflict-fragmented state (SAF–RSF civil war from April 2023) whose digital
agenda is now run from **Port Sudan** and reads as two intertwined strands: a
**Port-Sudan digital-state build** (national digital ID, e-government, digitised
revenue, sectoral MIS) and a set of **wartime survival/resilience rails**
(offline banking, mobile wallets, cross-border fibre) laid over collapsing power
and connectivity. The war's digital dimension appears mainly as infrastructure
destruction (grid/telecom) and reconstruction, plus inclusion workarounds.

## Recent developments

- **2026-05-24** — **Tax e-invoicing restored.** PM **Kamil Idris** backed the
  **[[tax-authority-sudan]]**; its **electronic-invoicing** project (advanced
  before the war) has been restored and is resuming "at pace." See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-24 PM backs Tax Authority; e-invoicing project restored after war (SUNA)]].
- **2026-05-23** — **National grid reconstruction after "systematic targeting."**
  The **[[ministry-of-energy-sudan]]** reported thermal generation collapsed to
  unprecedented lows from war damage to generation/transmission/distribution;
  priority is restoring power to water stations, hospitals and services (plus
  solar at critical sites). The upstream cause of the connectivity collapse.
  *(Government framing; capture ≠ endorsement.)* See [[infra.energy]],
  [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-23 Energy Ministry rehabilitating national grid after war destruction (SUNA)]].
- **2026-05-21** — **SUDAPASS national digital ID goes LIVE.** The
  **[[ministry-digital-transformation-sudan]]** launched **[[sudapass]]**,
  implemented via the **[[sudanese-cybersecurity-authority]]**, the
  **[[national-electronic-certification-authority-sudan]]** and the Interior
  Ministry's **[[civil-registry-sudan]]**. Activated with passport/national-number
  + face biometrics; enables e-services, e-transactions and legally-recognised
  digital signatures across government, banking, telecoms, education and health.
  **Supersedes** the earlier "imminent/preparing" status (Jan–Feb 2026). See
  [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[infra.cybersec]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-21 Sudan launches national digital identity SUDAPASS (SUNA)]].
- **2026-05-20** — **Health-sector digital strategy + Afia platform (with WHO).**
  The digital-transformation ministry met a **[[ministry-of-health-sudan]]** +
  **[[world-health-organization]]** delegation to set a health-digital strategy
  and launch the **[[afia-platform-sudan|Afia]]** health platform (health MIS).
  See [[dpi.mis]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-20 Comms Ministry and WHO discuss health-sector digital transformation; Afia platform (SUNA)]].
- **2026-04 → 05** — **Isali government e-collection (P2G) launches and spreads.**
  The **[[ministry-of-finance-sudan]]** contracted the
  **[[national-workers-bank-sudan]]** to deliver **[[isali]]**, digital
  government-revenue collection (30 Apr); by 3 May, Gadarif state was transferring
  the experience to Kassala (digitised crop-market collection, real-time revenue
  monitoring) — sub-national DPI diffusion. See [[dpi.pay]], [[digital.localgov]].
  Sources:
  [[2026-04-30 Finance Ministry-National Workers Bank contract for Isali e-collection service (SUNA)]],
  [[2026-05-03 Gadarif transfers Isali electronic-collection experience to Kassala state (SUNA)]].
- **2026-04-19** — **Unified social registry + civil-service HR system.** The
  **[[ministry-human-resources-social-welfare-sudan]]** and the digital ministry
  reviewed the **[[unified-social-registry-sudan]]** — foundational
  social-protection DPI for targeting poor/displaced groups — and a new
  civil-service system. See [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-19 Ministerial meeting on digital transformation, civil-service reform and unified social registry (SUNA)]].
- **2026-03-18** — **USSD banking launched (works without internet).** The
  digital ministry, **[[central-bank-of-sudan]]** and
  **[[communications-postal-regulatory-authority-sudan]]** rolled out USSD-based
  banking (transfers, balance/transaction queries) explicitly as a wartime
  connectivity-resilience/inclusion rail "even in areas without internet." See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-18 Sudan Launches USSD Banking Services to Expand Digital Financial Access]].
- **2026-02-23** — **Baladna e-gov expands to investment licensing.** The
  **[[national-investment-authority-sudan]]** completed integration with the
  **[[baladna-platform-sudan|Baladna]]** platform and launched fully-electronic
  investment-project licensing. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[finance.new]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-23 National Investment Authority launches electronic investment licensing via Baladna (SUNA)]].
- **2026-02-22** — **Bankak-fraud conviction.** A Dinder court convicted a network
  for forging **[[bankak|Bankak]]** payment notifications — the integrity/cyber
  dimension of the dominant wartime DFS rail (**[[bank-of-khartoum]]**). See
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-22 Fraud network convicted in Dinder for forging Bankak mobile-money notifications (SUNA)]].
- **2026-02-04 → 10** — **Horizon Fiber cross-border corridor (binding).**
  **[[sudatel]]**, **[[djibouti-telecom]]** and **[[ethio-telecom]]** signed
  (4 Feb) and converted to a **binding agreement** (10 Feb) the **[[horizon-fiber]]**
  multi-terabit terrestrial corridor (Djibouti subsea landings → Ethiopia →
  Sudan); MoU dated Dec 2024. Sudatel says it laid **~2,700 km** of new fibre
  during the war; framed as "digital sovereignty" and Sudan as a regional digital
  hub. See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]], [[finance.mou]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-04 Sudatel-Djibouti Telecom-Ethio telecom launch Horizon Fiber cross-border initiative (SUNA)]],
  [[2026-02-10 Ethio telecom, Djibouti Telecom, Sudatel Seal Binding Deal on Cross-Border Fiber Network]].
- **2026-01-19** — **Gulf/MEA processor enters Sudan.**
  **[[network-international]]** (Dubai) signed a card-processing/**[[mastercard]]**
  deal with **[[saudi-sudanese-bank]]** — external-actor involvement in rebuilding
  wartime payment infrastructure. See [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.mou]], [[geopol.gulf]].
  Source:
  [[2026-01-19 Network International Partners with Saudi Sudanese Bank to Advance Digital Payments in Sudan]].
- **2026-01-16** — **Operators framed as post-war infrastructure.** At a Port-Sudan
  business-digital event the minister praised **[[sudani-telecom]]** and
  **[[sudatel]]** for keeping services running. See [[tech.industry]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-16 Minister praises Sudani-Sudatel role in business digital transformation (SUNA)]].
- **2025-11-07** — **Three national digital authorities established by decree.**
  PM Kamil Idris's decree (5 Nov 2025) created, under the digital ministry, the
  **[[digital-transformation-authority-sudan]]**, the
  **[[sudanese-data-and-ai-authority]]** and the
  **[[sudanese-cybersecurity-authority]]**. Benchmarks: **EGDI 0.2759** (2024,
  below the 0.4247 African average); cybersecurity **Tier 4** (48.17/100); **AI
  Readiness 24.63/100**. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[tech.ai]].
  Source: [[2025-11-07 Sudan Establishes Three Digital Authorities to Advance E-Govt]].
- **2025-08-18** — **MTN Sudan "Al-Manssa" digital-skills programme** — content-
  creator training amid the war; a rare [[capacity.training]] data point. Source:
  [[2025-08-18 MTN Sudan Expands Digital Empowerment Drive with Al-Manssa]].
- **2025-06-18** — **ASYCUDA customs system reactivated (Gezira).** The
  **[[sudan-customs-authority]]** restored **[[asycuda]]** at Hantoub after war
  looting, with **[[sudani-telecom]]** connectivity — post-war digital-government
  restoration. Source:
  [[2025-06-18 ASYCUDA customs system reactivated at Gezira state customs after war damage (SUNA)]].
- **2025-04-18** — **Zain launches "Bede" digital wallet.** A telco mobile-money
  wallet (**[[bede-wallet]]**, **[[zain-sudan]]**) launched in Port Sudan under
  **[[central-bank-of-sudan]]** policy — a wartime inclusion rail complementing
  Bankak. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-04-18 Zain Launches Bede Digital Wallet to Boost Financial Inclusion in Sudan]].
- **2025-03-06** — **Zain Sudan restores service after a ~40-day war blackout**
  by migrating **>20m subscribers** to **[[totogi]]**'s AWS cloud (~$12m/week
  lost during the blackout; vendor-sourced, unverified). See [[infra.connect]],
  [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-06 How Zain Sudan restored mobile connectivity at a time of national crisis]].
- **2025-03-05** — **Rebuilding Sudan's DPI amid conflict (Magdi Amin).** The war
  collapsed digital/financial infrastructure; only **[[bank-of-khartoum]]'s
  Bankak** reliably moved money (single-provider vulnerability); **[[magdi-amin]]**
  advocates **[[mojaloop]]**-based interoperable rails. Source:
  [[2025-03-05 Rebuilding Sudan's digital infrastructure amidst conflict]].
- **2025-03-03** — **Connectivity down sharply (DataReportal, cite-not-absorb).**
  **21.6m** mobile connections (42.4%), **-20.6% YoY**; **14.6m** internet users
  (28.7%); **71.3% offline** — conflict-driven contraction. Reference snapshot,
  not current state. See [[infra.connect]], [[include.divides]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - Sudan (DataReportal)]].

## Active topics

- [[dpi.id]] · [[dpi.govtech]] — **[[sudapass]]** national digital ID **live**
  (21 May 2026) under the **[[baladna-platform-sudan|Baladna]]** e-gov programme;
  identity base in the **[[civil-registry-sudan]]**; trust/signature layer via the
  **[[national-electronic-certification-authority-sudan]]** and
  **[[sudanese-cybersecurity-authority]]**. Baladna expanding into transactional
  services (investment licensing).
- [[dpi.pay]] · [[include.access]] — wartime survival rails proliferating:
  **[[bankak|Bankak]]** (dominant), **[[bede-wallet|Bede]]** wallet, offline
  **USSD banking**, **[[isali|Isali]]** government e-collection, plus a
  **[[network-international]]**/**[[mastercard]]** processing deal — corroborating
  and extending the "single-provider concentration" thesis with multi-provider
  evidence. Interoperability (**[[mojaloop]]**) advocated as the recovery path.
- [[dpi.registry]] · [[dpi.mis]] — foundational data systems building:
  **[[unified-social-registry-sudan]]** (social protection / G2P targeting) and
  the **[[afia-platform-sudan|Afia]]** health MIS.
- [[infra.connect]] · [[gov.regional]] — conflict-driven collapse and resilience
  response; **[[horizon-fiber]]** cross-border corridor (Djibouti–Ethiopia–Sudan)
  positioning Sudan as a regional digital hub; **[[infra.energy]]** grid
  destruction as the upstream constraint.
- [[dpi.govtech]] — post-war restoration of digitised state systems: customs
  (**[[asycuda]]**), tax e-invoicing, e-collection, e-licensing — revenue
  mobilisation and transparency as the Port-Sudan government's stated aims.
- [[infra.cybersec]] · [[tech.ai]] — Nov 2025 institutional build-out (three
  authorities); payments-integrity/fraud dimension (Bankak-forgery conviction).
- [[gov.policy]] — trust, legal safeguards and interoperability flagged as
  preconditions for the ID layer; Malabo Convention and World Bank ID Principles
  cited as the governance frame.
