# Sudan (SDN)

## Recent developments

- **2026-02-02** — **SudaPass design details firm up; launch "imminent."** Reporting on the WEF
  commentary (via Biometric Update) says **[[sudapass]]** will be **free**, activated with **passport
  details and face biometrics**, with the Ministry of Digital Transformation calling the launch
  imminent. Experts warn against exclusion/surveillance failures (**Kenya** cited as a cautionary case)
  and stress trust, inclusivity and safeguards. The government also stood up the
  **[[sudanese-cybersecurity-authority]]** as part of the drive. See [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]],
  [[gov.protect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-02 Sudan's planned national digital ID could strengthen recovery push - Experts]].
- **2025-11-07** — **Three national digital authorities established by decree.** PM **Kamil Idris**'s
  decree of **5 Nov 2025** created, under the **[[ministry-digital-transformation-sudan]]**, the
  **[[digital-transformation-authority-sudan]]**, the **[[sudanese-data-and-ai-authority]]** and the
  **[[sudanese-cybersecurity-authority]]** — aiming at a unified digital system and "digital
  sovereignty" amid conflict and weak capacity. Benchmarks: **EGDI 0.2759** (2024, below the 0.4247
  African average); cybersecurity **Tier 4** (48.17/100); **AI Readiness 24.63/100**. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[tech.ai]], [[gov.policy]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-07 Sudan Establishes Three Digital Authorities to Advance E-Govt]].
- **2025-03-06** — **Zain Sudan restores service after a ~40-day war blackout.** Amid the early-2024
  civil-war connectivity blackout (three MNOs down; **[[zain-sudan]]** losing ~$12m/week), Zain migrated
  **>20m subscribers** to **[[totogi]]**'s AWS public cloud, reportedly restoring charging ~18 days after
  signing. Illustrates cloud-native resilience and payments concentration risk in fragile states
  (vendor-sourced figures, unverified). See [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.pay]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-06 How Zain Sudan restored mobile connectivity at a time of national crisis]].
- **2025-03-05** — **Rebuilding Sudan's DPI amid conflict (Magdi Amin).** VoxDevTalks interview: the
  war (Apr 2023–) collapsed digital/financial infrastructure; only **[[bank-of-khartoum]]'s Bankak**
  reliably moved money — a single-provider vulnerability. **[[magdi-amin]]** (African Renaissance
  Ventures) advocates **[[mojaloop]]**-based interoperable rails for recovery, working with the central
  bank and development partners. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]]. Source:
  [[2025-03-05 Rebuilding Sudan's digital infrastructure amidst conflict]].
- **2025-03-03** — **Connectivity down sharply (DataReportal, cite-not-absorb).** Sudan had **21.6m**
  mobile connections (42.4%) in early 2025, **-20.6% YoY**; **14.6m** internet users (28.7% penetration);
  **71.3% offline** — a conflict-driven contraction. Reference snapshot, not current state. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[include.divides]]. Source: [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - Sudan (DataReportal)]].
- **2026-01-29** — **SudaPass unified national digital ID in preparation.** Sudan's Ministry
  of Telecommunications and Digital Transformation is readying **[[sudapass]]**, a unified
  national digital identity platform under its wider **Baladna** e-government programme,
  announced as the country works to restore services and rebuild institutions amid ongoing
  conflict. Analysis (WEF, Yahya & Ibrahim) frames a credible ID layer as foundational to
  fiscal recovery, social protection, financial inclusion and eventual electoral processes —
  but warns it must be built with **trust and dialogue**, inclusive enrolment for displaced,
  nomadic and undocumented groups, and safeguards drawn from the **AU Malabo Convention** and
  the **World Bank Principles on Identification**. Humanitarian backdrop: **>30m** need aid and
  **>14m** are displaced out of a population of ~51m; no updated census since **2008**, leaving
  fragmented registries and parallel humanitarian databases. Source:
  [[2026-01-29 How to build trust alongside Sudan's digital identity system]] ([[world-bank]]).
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]], [[gov.policy]].

## Active topics

- [[dpi.id]] · [[dpi.govtech]] — **[[sudapass]]** unified digital ID under the Baladna e-gov
  programme; positioned as reconstruction infrastructure for service delivery, budgeting and
  domestic resource mobilisation in a conflict-disrupted state.
- [[include.access]] · [[dpi.pay]] — inclusion of displaced/nomadic/undocumented populations as
  the central design challenge; digital identity as an enabler of mobile money and financial
  inclusion off a low account-ownership base.
- [[gov.policy]] — trust, legal safeguards and interoperability (without unrestricted data
  pooling) flagged as preconditions; Malabo Convention and World Bank ID Principles cited as
  the governance frame.
- [[dpi.govtech]] · [[infra.cybersec]] · [[tech.ai]] — Nov 2025 institutional build-out: three new
  authorities (digital transformation, data & AI, cybersecurity) under the digital-transformation
  ministry, pursuing a unified digital system and "digital sovereignty" from a very low base.
- [[infra.connect]] — conflict-driven connectivity collapse (mobile connections -20.6% YoY, early
  2025) and the resilience response (Zain's cloud migration during the ~40-day blackout).
- [[dpi.pay]] — payments concentrated on Bank of Khartoum's Bankak during the war; Mojaloop-based
  interoperability advocated as the recovery path; DFS resilience a recurring theme.
