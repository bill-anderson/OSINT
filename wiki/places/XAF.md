# Africa (XAF)

Continental hub for genuinely Africa-wide items. Tag `XAF` only when an item is
continental in scope; country-specific detail lives on the country hubs. (`XSS`, by
rule, is all `XAF` children except `XNA`; `XGL` is for non-Africa-specific/global items.)

## Recent developments

- **2026-01→05** *(ingested 2026-07-13)* — **Data protection reaches critical mass: 44 laws, 38
  authorities.** The end-2025 continental roundup ([[digital-policy-alert]] × [[tech-hive-advisory]])
  and [[code-for-africa]] both put **≥44 African countries with data-protection laws** (~80% of AU
  members) and **≥38 operational DPAs**, with 2025 marking the shift from "paper law" to enforcement
  (Uganda's first criminal conviction; Kenya's Worldcoin deletion order; Nigeria's $220m Meta fine);
  **>50 laws** are projected by end-2026. Sources:
  [[2026-01-11 The Year of the Teeth Data Protection in Africa Roundup, 2025, Projections for 2026]],
  [[2026-05-05 Africa's data protection laws began to bite in 2025]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2025-04-04** *(primary ingested 2026-07-13)* — **Africa Declaration on AI — 49 signatories
  confirmed.** The primary Kigali Declaration text lists **49 country signatories** plus the AU and
  [[smart-africa]], settling a 49/52/54 reporting spread ([[africa-ai-declaration]]; absent: DR Congo,
  Eritrea, Libya, Seychelles, Tanzania, Western Sahara). Sources:
  [[2025-04-04 Africa Declaration on Artificial Intelligence]],
  [[2025-05-13 Optimism but uncertainty over Africa's AI Declaration]]. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-07-13** — **ITU–ICPC submarine-cable Advisory Body publishes final report.** The
  two-year International Advisory Body for Submarine Cable Resilience ([[international-cable-protection-committee]],
  [[itu]]; co-chair [[bosun-tijani]]) approved a three-pillar roadmap — faster deploy/repair, risk
  monitoring, route diversity — with SSA among the most exposed. Source:
  [[2026-07-13 ‘How Nigeria, others can secure undersea Internet cables’]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.standards]].
- **2025-12-06** *(pub 2026-07-12)* — **Algiers Declaration on OTT platforms.** African ministers,
  under the [[african-telecommunications-union]], adopted the **[[algiers-declaration]]** — a
  continental framework for OTT-platform regulation (local-reinvestment "fair share," data
  sovereignty, culturally-adapted moderation, responsible AI), DSA-inspired, heading to the AU for
  adoption in early 2026. Source: [[2026-07-12 Algiers Declaration Africa's OTT Platform Rules]].
  See [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[tech.industry]].

- **Framing (2020–2021) — algorithmic colonialism.** **[[abeba-birhane]]**'s *The Algorithmic
  Colonization of Africa* (2020) and *Narratives and Counternarratives on Data Sharing in Africa*
  (FAccT '21) are the continental theoretical anchors for the `colonialism` lens: Western tech
  monopolies' control of digital infrastructure as colonial continuation, and the non-African
  "deficit narratives" that distort Africa's data-sharing debate. See [[tech.ai]], [[data.open]],
  [[geopol.usa]]. Sources: [[2020-08-21 Algorithmic Colonisation of Africa]],
  [[2021-03-01 Narratives and Counternarratives on Data Sharing in Africa (Abebe, Birhane et al, FAccT 2021)]].
- **2026** — **UNDP flagship: *Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa*.** UNDP's Regional
  Bureau for Africa + DAI Hub published the wiki's definitive continental DPI reference:
  DPI as three layers (core ID/payments/data-exchange; sectoral apps; enabling infra),
  four barriers (exclusion, inefficiency, fiscal erosion, sovereignty), tied to **AfCFTA**
  (**$450bn**) and AU Agenda 2063, with a call for **African-led** DPI investment. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Digital Public Infrastructure in Africa - UNDP]]. See [[undp]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]], [[finance.new]].
- **2026-07-10** — **WSIS+20: from strategies to measurable implementation.** A **WSIS Forum
  2026** session convened by **[[aficta]]** and **[[nitda]]** argued Africa has the digital
  strategies but must now deliver measurable results. UNECA ([[uneca]]) presented its **Africa
  2035 Digital Implementation Roadmap** (9 thematic pillars, 15 priority indicators + unified
  reporting template) and a proposed **WSIS Implementation Maturity Index** (11 Action Lines);
  Nigeria's ICT sector was cited at **13–14% of GDP** with the **3MTT** programme (train 3m by
  2027). Continental compute-gap figures (~0.6% of global DC capacity, ~0.2% of AI compute)
  noted but not promoted here per the reference-study rule. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Africa urged to turn WSIS+20 commitments into measurable digital progress]].
  See [[gov.policy]], [[gov.regional]], [[capacity.training]], [[tech.ai]], [[include.divides]].
- **2026-07-10** — **Cross-border identity as a governance problem.** A DIAL ([[dial]])
  research-fellowship analysis argues identity is inherently local: national ID cards mutually
  recognised for travel among Kenya, Rwanda and Uganda are often **inadequate for cross-border
  financial services**, exposing three gaps — data **sufficiency**, **adequacy** and **sovereignty**
  — that **[[afcfta]]**'s portable-identity vision must resolve without eroding national sovereignty
  or data-protection regimes. Proposes standardized identity schemas + purpose-specific, locally
  compliant credentials, with cross-border finance as the mature test-bed. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Every country defines identity differently. Can digital public infrastructure bridge those differences]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.protect]].
- **2026-07-09** — **AU pushes a unified African voice on AI governance.** At the inaugural **Global
  Dialogue on AI Governance** (Geneva, alongside AI for Good + WSIS Forum 2026), AU Commissioner
  **Lerato Mataboge** ([[african-union]]) called for a coordinated, Africa-centred approach anchored
  in the Continental AI Strategy, the AU Data Policy Framework and the Malabo Convention. The UN
  **Independent International Scientific Panel on AI** ([[un-ai-scientific-panel]]) presented its
  **Preliminary Report**; Mataboge met its African members (Marivate, Tadesse, Orji, El-Assady) to
  align the continent's scientific expertise with its political positions. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 African Union pushes for unified African voice at Global AI Governance Dialogue]].
  See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-07-09** — **[[smart-africa]]–[[afnic]] MoU** (Geneva) to strengthen Africa's
  internet infrastructure: capacity of **ccTLD operators**, **DNS** security/resilience,
  registry modernisation, internet governance. Deal: [[smart-africa-afnic-mou]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-09 Smart Africa and AFNIC Partner to Strengthen Africa's Internet Infrastructure]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.standards]], [[capacity.training]].

  [[2026-07-09 ITU's Partner2Connect crosses $100 billion in global connectivity pledges]].
  See [[itu]], [[partner2connect]], [[infra.connect]], [[include.divides]].
- **2026-07-09** — [[seacom]] announces **Seacom 2.0** (25,000 km, 20 landings across 15

- **2026-07-09** — At the **18th AfCFTA Council of Ministers** (Abuja), **Nigeria** took the
  Council Bureau chair from **Egypt**; the parallel **2nd AfCFTA Digital Trade Forum** (Lagos)
  closed with **eight calls to action** to operationalize the **Protocol on Digital Trade**
  ([[afcfta-digital-trade-protocol]]): harmonized e-signatures, mutual recognition of digital
  ID, and **interoperable cross-border payments** ([[papss]]) toward a digital single market
  (digital economy ~$180bn now → ~$712bn by 2050). The **ADIEP** programme, with [[google]], is
  training 7,500 SMEs across 19 countries. SG [[wamkele-mene]]. Sources:
  [[2026-07-07 African nations recommit to operationalize AfCFTA digital single market framework]],
  [[2026-07-09 Nigeria Takes AfCFTA Council Chair as Digital Forum Charts Roadmap for Implementation]].
  See [[afcfta]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.standards]], [[dpi.exchange]].

  [[2026-07-07 Equity, AfricaNenda and Gates Foundation Partner to Advance Digital Public Infrastructure Across Africa]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].

  [[2026-07-03 AfCFTA, Data Governance and the Hidden Legal Gaps Reshaping Africa's Digital Economy - P.M. News]].

- **2025-12 → 2026-06** — **MNO state-of-market and regulator repurposing (Russell
  Southwood).** A recurring analytical thread: as **data revenue overtakes voice**,
  the continent's four "power players" ([[mtn-group]], [[airtel-africa]],
  [[orange]], [[vodacom]]) become **data/holding companies** (spinning out towers,
  data centres, wholesale/Bayobab, mobile money; MTN's **EVA/Genova** AI unit),
  with a "second division" ([[axian-telecom]] US$600m raise, [[econet-wireless]])
  and the **former fixed-line incumbents** (Telkom Kenya, Sotel, Gamtel, Camtel,
  SCPT) as "stranded assets." Southwood argues the liberalisation-era **regulators
  must repurpose** around digital infrastructure (licence-passporting, data-
  rollover, spectrum, sandboxes), rooting today's two-speed markets in the 2000s
  ICT4D/open-access history. See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.connect]],
  [[include.divides]]. Sources:
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]],
  [[2026-06-25 Regulating for Africa's future digital economy – A draft agenda to speed things up]],
  [[2026-04-16 The Long View - What ICT4D did for Africa and why that deep past matters for today]],
  [[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]].
- **2026-06** — **AI-sovereignty commentary cluster (Substack analysts).** A wave of
  named-author analysis sharpens the continental AI-sovereignty debate: Anjola
  Adebowale on a "dynamic tech stack" and sequencing localisation against
  infrastructure ([[2026-06-12 Beyond the AI Stack Why Governments Need Dynamic Digital Infrastructure]]),
  Microsoft Build 2026 as vendor vertical integration
  ([[2026-06-05 Microsoft Build 2026 What Caught My Attention and What It Means for Africa]]);
  Rebecca Mbaya on data vs digital sovereignty and the fragmentation of 54 separate
  negotiations ([[2026-06-24 Does AI Sovereignty Mean Controlling the Keys, or Owning the Building]]);
  and Chinasa Okolo warning corporate/philanthropic interests could co-opt Africa's
  AI moment ([[2026-02-17 Corporate Interests Must Not Co-Opt Africa's AI Moment]]).
  See [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-06-17** — Rising AI cost as a continental fault line; AU **Pan-African AI Compute
