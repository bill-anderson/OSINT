# São Tomé and Príncipe (STP)

One of Africa's smallest states (Gulf of Guinea; second-smallest African country by
area and population, ~241k people in late 2025; grouped under [[XWA]] in this wiki's
place authority). A Lusophone island state building foundational DPI off an unusually
strong civil-registration base (~98% birth-certificate coverage, 2023), guided by a
2020 **[[national-strategy-digital-governance-sao-tome]]** and an early move to
operationalise continental data governance at national level.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-09/10** — **National adoption of the AU Data Policy Framework.** Government
  officials and data practitioners held a **Data Governance Capacity Building Workshop**
  (9–10 Jul 2026) exploring how the **[[au-data-policy-framework]]** (AUDPF) can be adopted
  nationally to enable "trusted, secure, and inclusive data governance." Run by the
  **[[d4d-hub]]** under the EU-backed **Data Governance in Africa (#D4DataGOV)** initiative
  (Team Europe / Global Gateway), with implementing partners **[[giz]]**, **[[auda-nepad]]**
  and **[[kictanet]]**. The EU Delegation's Paula Medina framed success as translating the
  workshop into "public policies, institutional reforms, improved administrative practices."
  Source: [[2026-07-13 D4D Hub on Sao Tome and Principe]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.policy]],
  [[gov.regional]], [[capacity.training]], [[XWA]].

- **2026-04-07** — **Citizen Portal launched (verifiable-credentials pathway).** São Tomé launched its
  **Citizen Portal** (beta) on **27 Mar 2026**, funded by World Bank digitisation financing and
  **[[co-develop]]**, with technical advisory from **[[cdpi]]** — a pathway to verifiable credentialing
  (tax, police clearances; later education certificates) and a growing Lusophone-Africa DPI footprint.
  Launch coincided with the 18th anniversary of INIC (Institute for Innovation and Knowledge). Built on
  the same MOSIP/CRVS foundation as the national-ID pilot below.
  [[2026-04-07 São Tomé and Príncipe Launches Citizen Portal]] · [[2025-06-05 São Tomé and Principe pilots MOSIP-based national digital ID system]] · [[dpi.govtech]] · [[dpi.id]]

- **2025-06-05** — **MOSIP-based national digital ID pilot confirmed at ID4Africa 2025.** STP
  officials (Justice Ministry) confirmed a **[[sao-tome-national-digital-id]]** pilot built on
  **[[mosip]]**, begun in **2024** and anchored on the country's ~98% birth-registration base
  (2023, World Bank). "We started with civil registration"; nationwide rollout hoped within one
  to two years, no fixed timeline. MOSIP flagged STP as a target for cost-reduced ID solutions for
  sub-350,000-population states. [[2025-06-05 São Tomé and Principe pilots MOSIP-based national digital ID system]] ·
  [[dpi.id]] · [[dpi.registry]]

- **2025 (late) — connectivity baseline (DataReportal Digital 2026).** Internet penetration
  **61.5%** (148k users, Oct-2025) — flat vs early-2025; **154k** mobile connections (**63.9%**
  of population), of which GSMA rates **82.6%** "broadband"; social-media reach **30.3%**. The
  broadband-share drop from 93.3% (Jan-2025) reads as a GSMA methodology shift, not a real
  regression. [[2025-11-08 Digital 2026 - São Tomé & Príncipe]] · (prior: [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - São Tomé & Príncipe]]) · [[infra.connect]] · [[include.access]]

- **2024** — **Data-protection and interoperability legal framework mapped (IBA).** STP's data
  regime rests on the **[[sao-tome-data-protection-law-2016]]** (Personal Data Guarantee and
  Protection Law, Law 03/2016; modelled on EU Directive 95/46/EC), the **[[sao-tome-interoperability-framework-2024]]**
  (National Framework for Interoperability, Law 1/2024) for public-sector data sharing, and STP's
  signatory status to the **[[malabo-convention]]**. Enforced by the **[[national-data-protection-agency-sao-tome]]**
  (ANPDP). [[2024-01-01 São Tomé and Príncipe - Digital Regulations in the Metaverse Era (Data)]] ·
  [[gov.protect]] · [[gov.legislate]] · [[dpi.exchange]]
