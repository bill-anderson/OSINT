- **2026-07-10** — **Data-sovereignty stress test.** President **[[bola-tinubu]]** ordered the
  **[[fccpc]]** to investigate **[[meta]]**, Alphabet/**[[google]]** and **[[x-corp]]** (plus
  GenAI platforms) over uncompensated scraping/training on Nigerian **news content**
  (abuse-of-dominance + copyright under the FCCP Act 2018), after a Nigerian Press Organisation
  petition; EVC Tunji Bello frames it as fact-finding. Cast as a sovereignty reckoning alongside
  the **[[cbn]]** onshoring mandate (transactional data local by **2027-01-01**). Precedents
  cited: the FCCPC's **2025 $220–222m Meta penalty** (under appeal), **[[ndpc]]**'s ₦7.2bn
  privacy penalties, and South Africa's Competition Commission–Google news deal (~R688m/$40m a
  year). **[[nibss]]** logged **₦1.07 quadrillion (~$702bn)** in e-transactions in 2024, most
  still on AWS/Azure/Google Cloud (Africa's #2 DC market, ~26 facilities / 50–56 MW live).
  Sources: [[2026-07-10 Nigeria tests data sovereignty as foreign tech firms facing probe]],
  [[2026-07-10 Why Nigeria Is Investigating Meta, Google, X and AI Firms]]. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[tech.ai]], [[geopol.usa]], **[[nigeria--gov-protect]]**.
- **2026-07-10** — **DRLI sues INEC over missing privacy policy.** The
  **[[digital-rights-lawyers-initiative]]** (with lawyer Solomon Etisang; counsel Olumide
  Babalola) sued the **[[inec]]** at the Federal High Court, Yenagoa, alleging the commission —
  holder of one of Nigeria's largest personal-data registers — breached the
  **[[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]]** (ss.24/27) and the constitutional right to privacy
  (s.37) by publishing no website privacy policy. Follows an Oct 2025 FIJ report and the May
  2025 Olayinka voter-record leak; a private-enforcement data point distinct from the
  **[[ndpc-meta-settlement]]**. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 FIJ Showed That INEC Broke Data Law. Digital Rights Lawyers Sue the Commission 10 Months Later]].
  See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]], [[dpi.registry]], **[[nigeria--gov-protect]]**.

- **2026-07-07** — **[[cbn]]** data-localization directive (circular 2026-06-15) requires
  all financial institutions to host transactional data **inside Nigeria by 1 Jan 2027**,
  moving off AWS/Azure/Google/IBM. Industry says physical DC capacity (~26 facilities,
  50–56 MW live) is ready but **local cloud-compute platforms** and power are the gap;
  Africa's #2 DC market (~15% of continental capacity), projected 210–300 MW by 2030.
  Analysts (TechBooky) add that **domestication ≠ protection**: the
  **[[nigeria-data-protection-act-2023]]** already permits controlled cross-border transfer
  under safeguards (adequacy, BCRs, SCCs, certification), so encryption/key-control, a hybrid
  local-replica-plus-approved-cloud model and phased compliance matter more than pure
  geography — noting hyperscalers concentrate full-scale African DCs in South Africa. Sources:
  [[2026-07-07 CBN's local data order puts Nigeria's data centres to test]],
  [[2026-07-10 CBN's Data Localisation Directive Puts Nigerian Fintechs In A Cloud Dilemma]]. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.policy]], [[infra.store]], and the intersection **[[nigeria--gov-protect]]**.

- **2026-07-01** *(analysis)* — **CBN payments circular, deep-read.** Anjola Adebowale's two-part
  *Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure* reads the **[[cbn]]** June-2026 circular
  (**[[cbn-payments-circular-2026]]**) as four pillars — market-structure caps,
  **data localisation by 1 Jan 2027**, UBO disclosure, monthly market-share returns — judged
  strategically sound but ahead of Nigeria's data-centre/grid capacity (~75MW live across ~26
  facilities; ~6GW grid for 230m people vs South Africa's ~48GW), and argues it should have been
  *sequenced* against infrastructure milestones (cf. India's 2018–19 RBI rollback, Rwanda's phased
  DPI). Part II casts the **[[mtn-nigeria]]** Sifiso Dabengwa DC ($235m, 4.5MW, $20m naira-priced
  cloud) as the local capacity to absorb it. Payments context: **[[nibss]]** logged **~$702.6bn**
  in e-transactions in 2024 (Kariuki's "payments end-game"). Sources:
  [[2026-07-01 Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part I Inside the CBN's June 2026 Payments Circular]],
  [[2026-06-19 Strategy Ahead of Infrastructure, Part II The Opportunity and the Case for Sequencing]],
  [[2026-02-23 111 - Winning But Losing - The Payments End Game]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[infra.store]],
  **[[nigeria--gov-protect]]**.
