# Liberia (LBR)

## Recent developments

- **2026-01/2025 — National ID crisis (the batch's dominant Liberia story).** Liberia mandated biometric
  ID by **Executive Order 147** (Boakai, Apr 2025) through the **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]**
  (est. 2011; NIN/NBIS), backed by the World Bank **[[great-project-liberia|GREAT]]** project ($30m+$13m,
  enroll 2m). But it stalled: coverage only **~14-16%** of ~5.5m (740,000 by Dec 2024), a **$5 fee**
  excluding the poor (lawmakers urge free ID), bribery at overwhelmed centres, and a **procurement scandal**
  — a rejected restricted-bid award to Austria's OSD International (PPCC, Jul 2025) forced a **suspension**
  of issuance, halting EO 147. All while Liberia has **no data-protection law / DPA** despite mandating
  biometric ID and SIM-ID linkage (LTA-NIR MoU, Oct 2024). The NIR's Five-Year Strategic Plan 2026-2030
  ("No ID, No Inclusion") promises a reset. Full case: **[[liberia--dpi-id]]**. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[gov.protect]], [[include.access]].

- **2026-07-13** — **Pay Na-Na instant-payment launch.** Liberia's Inclusive Instant Payment
  System (**[[pay-na-na]]**, launched by President Boakai) makes money interoperable across
  mobile-money networks — deployed in **73 business days**, transaction costs capped **<1%**;
  52% account ownership (2024, past target), **>11m** mobile wallets; LTA granted Orange Money a
  Value-Added-Services licence. Source: [[2026-07-13 Liberia Expands Digital Finance]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]], [[infra.connect]].

- **2026-03-16** — **WARDIP2 beneficiary.** The World Bank ([[world-bank]])
  announced (2026-03-11) a **$137m** second phase of the **Western Africa Regional
  Digital Integration Program** ([[wardip]]) covering Liberia, Benin ([[BEN]]) and
  Sierra Leone ([[SLE]]): broadband for **>5m** citizens plus digital public
  services for **>5m** more (~10.6m total), across three pillars (digital
  infrastructure / data-centre capacity, business environment, cross-border
  operation). WARDIP launched 2023; collaborators include [[ecowas]],
  [[african-union]] and [[smart-africa]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 New $137M initiative to enable digital service access for millions in three ECOWAS nations]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-02-03** — **'Digital Liberia' 2025–2029 strategy.** The Ministry of Posts
  and Telecommunications unveiled **[[digital-liberia-strategy]]** — "Digital
  Liberia: Equity, Sovereignty and Innovation for a Connected Future" — with eight
  priority areas (universal connectivity, digital inclusion, national digital
  addressing + postal reform, **cybersecurity & data sovereignty**, e-governance,
  ICT-driven innovation) under four pillars, aligned to the ARREST Agenda.
  Regulator **[[lta-liberia]]** (Acting Chair Patrick R. Honnah) backed it;
  Minister Sekou M. Kromah framed it as action-oriented. Source:
  [[2026-02-03 Liberia Unveils 5-Year 'Digital Liberia' Strategy to Boost Sovereignty and Innovation]].
  See [[gov.policy]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-01-06** — **MOE Digital Learning Platform + "Liberia Learns" launch.** Liberia's
  Ministry of Education launched a **Digital Learning Platform** and the **"Liberia Learns"**
  digital radio/TV service (6 Jan 2026, Paynesville) delivering Grades 1–3 foundational
  literacy/numeracy, aligned to the Boakai ARREST Agenda. Source:
  [[2026-01-07 Digital driving licence coming to South Africa]]. See [[include.access]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[capacity.literacy]].
