# Liberia (LBR)

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-22 — Starcell revocation formalised: licence and spectrum stripped with immediate
  effect.** [[lta-liberia]] Chair **Clarence Massaquoi** announced at a media briefing that the
  **Universal Telecommunications License and Spectrum Authorization of Starcell International
  Liberia** (tagged `starcell-international-liberia`) is **revoked with immediate effect**, under a
  regulatory review conducted under the **Telecommunications Act of 2007**. The mechanics, new
  against the 07-17 FPA account below: the revocation followed a **90-day suspension (9 Feb – 10 May
  2026)** during which Starcell took no corrective action; cited grounds are failure to commence
  operations, unmet financial obligations, **non-payment of Universal Access Fund contributions**,
  regulatory violations and non-response to directives; all **spectrum, frequencies, numbering
  resources and regulatory privileges revert to the LTA for reassignment**. Market context (Omdia
  estimates, **Q2 2026**): **Orange Liberia 3.2m** mobile subscribers, **Lonestar Cell (MTN) 2.3m**,
  **Libtelco 14,000** — the duopoly the revocation was meant to loosen. The same report re-confirms
  the **Orange Liberia LRD 4m SIM-privacy fine** (see [[liberia--gov-protect]]). See
  [[infra.connect]], [[gov.legislate]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-22-liberia-strips-starcell-of-operating-license]].

- **2026-07-21 — The Technology Summit is held: Boakai puts EO 163 at the centre of a
  whole-of-government digital agenda.** [[gov.policy]] President **Joseph Nyuma Boakai** opened the
  **Liberia Technology Summit 2026** (EJS Ministerial Complex, Congo Town, theme *"Government
  Digital Transformation: Building a Smarter Liberia"*) — the summit announced 2026-07-20 (below),
  now held. Payload: Boakai disclosed he **directed the Ministry of State for Presidential Affairs
  to lead** the summit's organisation with the **Ministry of Posts and Telecommunications** and the
  **[[lta-liberia|LTA]]**; he re-anchored the agenda on **Executive Order No. 163** and the **Office
  of Technology, Digitalization and Innovation ([[otdi-liberia|OTDI]])** as the central coordinating
  authority, framing digital transformation as "a whole-of-government undertaking"; pledged support
  for digital infrastructure, cybersecurity, digital literacy, nationwide connectivity and
  investment in **AI, fintech and digital public services** ("Made in Liberia" startup framing). A
  vision statement, not a costed programme — **no budget, timeline or named deliverable was
  announced** in the held account. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[tech.ai]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-22-liberia-boakai-digital-transformation-agenda-summit]].

- **2026-07-20 — Liberia Technology Summit 2026 announced for 21–22 July, Monrovia.** [[gov.policy]]
  The government announced a two-day national technology summit in Monrovia, **presided over by the
  Ministry of State**, convening national and international experts on **artificial intelligence,
  cybersecurity and innovation** to modernise public services and stimulate the economy; **President
  Joseph Nyuma Boakai** is to deliver an address. *(Superseded: the summit was held 2026-07-21 —
  see the entry above for Boakai's address.)* It is
  noted because it is the first time AI appears as a stated Liberian government agenda item alongside
  the cybersecurity and e-governance pillars of [[digital-liberia-strategy]] and EO 163
  ([[otdi-liberia]]). See [[tech.ai]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[dpi.govtech]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-20-liberia-sommet-technologie-monrovia]].

- **2026-07-17 — LTA's sweeping telecom overhaul.** [[lta-liberia]] Board Chair Clarence Massaquoi
  unveiled the biggest reform since the 2007 Telecom Act: splitting infrastructure vs. service licences
  (with rural incentives), tower-sharing rules, Liberia's **first satellite regulations**, an **IMEI**
  anti-theft regime, and repatriating the **.lr ccTLD** to local control. It **revoked Star Cell's
  licence** (never operational since 2020) and fined **Orange Liberia** L$4m for a SIM-swap privacy
  breach; [[starlink]] is wiring **156 schools** (~90 done). Source:
  [[2026-07-17-lta-telecom-reforms-star-cell-revoked-fpa]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.protect]],
  [[include.access]].

- **2026-06 — Fibre redundancy + $50m WARDIP2 financing.** [[infra.connect]] Liberia signed
  **$125m** of World Bank agreements (of which **$50m [[wardip|WARDIP2]]** for broadband, cybersecurity,
  digital governance and digital payments) at Mount Coffee (8 Jun); and forged a regional consensus to
  commercialize spare fibre on the **TRANSCO CLSG** grid (Liberia–Sierra Leone–Guinea–Côte d'Ivoire),
  building terrestrial redundancy against sole reliance on the **ACE** cable landing in Monrovia. Sources:
  [[2026-06-08-125m-world-bank-wardip2-financing-techafricanews]],
  [[2026-06-16-transco-clsg-fibre-expansion-techafricanews]]. See [[gov.regional]], [[finance.new]].

- **2026-04-22 — EO 163 digital-government reform.** [[dpi.govtech]] Boakai signed **Executive Order
  163** creating the **National Digitalization and Modernization Initiative (NDMI)** and the **Office of
  Technology, Digitalization and Innovation ([[otdi-liberia|OTDI]])** — a whole-of-government coordinator
  for enterprise standards, interoperability, technical clearance of ICT investments, and baseline
  cybersecurity, under Article 16 privacy. Source:
  [[2026-04-29-executive-order-163-digital-government-ndmi-observer]]. See [[gov.policy]],
  [[gov.standards]], [[infra.cybersec]].

- **2026-02-12 — House probes a 2m-citizen data breach.** [[infra.cybersec]] Lawmakers ordered an
  investigation into an alleged breach exposing personal data of ~**2 million** citizens across the LRA,
  LEC and NIC, reportedly sold on the dark web (the LRA denied it) — invoking the **Cybercrime Act of
  2025** and the absence of a national cybersecurity strategy. Source:
  [[2026-02-12-house-probe-data-breach-2m-citizens-observer]]. See [[gov.protect]].

- **2025-06→2026-05 — National ID crisis: mandate → stall → debt-lock → OSD concession.** Liberia
  mandated biometric ID by **Executive Order 147** (Boakai, Apr 2025) via the
  **[[national-identification-registry-liberia|NIR]]** (est. 2011; NIN/NBIS), backed by the World Bank
  **[[great-project-liberia|GREAT]]** project. It stalled: coverage **<15%** of ~5.3m, a **$5 fee**
  excluding the poor, bribery at overwhelmed centres, and a **procurement scandal** (a rejected
  restricted-bid award to [[osd-international|OSD]], PPCC Jul 2025) forced a **suspension**. It then
  emerged the system was **locked** by Kenyan builder [[techno-brain]] over a **$1.7m debt** (a $2m WB
  tranche frozen). The fix: a **PPP concession** handing enrollment of all 5.3m to Austria's OSD — which
  a Liberian Observer editorial and [[naymote]] attack as outsourcing "the digital spine of the state"
  with still **no data-protection law** (the [[liberia-data-protection-bill|DP bill]] only reached a
  House committee in Jun 2025). Full case: **[[liberia--dpi-id]]**. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]].

- **2025-12-16 — Pay Na-Na instant-payment launch.** [[dpi.pay]] The [[central-bank-of-liberia|CBL]]
  launched the **Inclusive Instant Payment System** (**[[pay-na-na]]**), making money interoperable
  across MTN Mobile Money and Orange Money on [[mojaloop]] — deployed in **73 business days**,
  transaction costs capped **<1%**; pilot covers P2P + G2P, with P2G (LRA tax) and a National Electronic
  Payment Switch to follow. Preceded by Participation/SLA agreements (12 Dec). Sources:
  [[2025-12-17-cbl-launches-iips-mobile-money-silos-fpa]],
  [[2025-12-16-liberia-launches-iips-techafricanews]],
  [[2025-12-15-cbl-formalizes-iips-agreements-observer]], [[2026-07-13 Liberia Expands Digital Finance]].
  See [[include.access]], [[infra.connect]].

- **2025-12-16 — CSA automated LPA platform.** [[dpi.govtech]] The Civil Service Agency went live with a
  biometric (fingerprint) **Legal Power of Attorney** platform tying civil-servant purchases to POS
  verification against the CSA database — 5,000 enrolled across 16 government entities at launch. Source:
  [[2025-12-17-csa-automated-lpa-platform-observer]].

- **2026-03-16** — **WARDIP2 beneficiary.** The World Bank ([[world-bank]])
  announced (2026-03-11) a **$137m** second phase of the **Western Africa Regional
  Digital Integration Program** ([[wardip]]) covering Liberia, Benin ([[BEN]]) and
  Sierra Leone ([[SLE]]): broadband for **>5m** citizens plus digital public
  services for **>5m** more (~10.6m total), across three pillars (digital
  infrastructure / data-centre capacity, business environment, cross-border
  operation). WARDIP launched 2023; collaborators include [[ecowas]],
  [[african-union]] and [[smart-africa]]. Source:
  [[2026-03-16 New $137M initiative to enable digital service access for millions in three ECOWAS nations]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-02-03** — **'Digital Liberia' 2025–2029 strategy.** The Ministry of Posts
  and Telecommunications unveiled **[[digital-liberia-strategy]]** — "Digital
  Liberia: Equity, Sovereignty and Innovation for a Connected Future" — with eight
  priority areas (universal connectivity, digital inclusion, national digital
  addressing + postal reform, **cybersecurity & data sovereignty**, e-governance,
  ICT-driven innovation) under four pillars, aligned to the ARREST Agenda.
  Regulator **[[lta-liberia]]** (Acting Chair Patrick R. Honnah) backed it;
  Minister Sekou M. Kromah framed it as action-oriented. Source:
  [[2026-02-03 Liberia Unveils 5-Year 'Digital Liberia' Strategy to Boost Sovereignty and Innovation]].
  See [[gov.policy]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-01-06** — **MOE Digital Learning Platform + "Liberia Learns" launch.** Liberia's
  Ministry of Education launched a **Digital Learning Platform** and the **"Liberia Learns"**
  digital radio/TV service (6 Jan 2026, Paynesville) delivering Grades 1–3 foundational
  literacy/numeracy, aligned to the Boakai ARREST Agenda. Source:
  [[2026-01-07 Digital driving licence coming to South Africa]]. See [[include.access]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[capacity.literacy]].
