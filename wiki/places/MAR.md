# Morocco (MAR)

One of Africa's **most digitally advanced** states (92% internet penetration; ITU cyber Tier 1; 4th in
Africa on the 2024 UN EGDI), executing a coherent, well-funded digital agenda under **[[digital-morocco-2030]]**
(launched Sept 2024). The spine is a layered **digital-identity** stack — the **[[dgsn-morocco|DGSN]]**
electronic national ID, the **[[mon-e-id|Mon e-ID]]** app and the **[[idarati-x0|Idarati X.0]]** wallet,
plus a **[[morocco-national-population-registry|population/social registry]]** (Law 72-18, MOSIP) reaching
half the population — with a **cloud-first** infrastructure push, a forthcoming **[[digital-x0-law|Digital
X.0]]** AI/data/ID law, and a continental leadership role (its **[[add-morocco|ADD]]** chairs Smart
Africa's CAITA). Persistent gaps: institutional fragmentation, a skills deficit, and a rural digital
divide (~1,800 villages offline).

## Recent developments

- **2026-02-10** — **Idarati X.0 e-government platform (national digital wallet).** Morocco signed 8 MoUs
  in Rabat to launch **[[idarati-x0]]** — a unified entry point for public services built around a
  national digital wallet authenticated by the electronic national ID; French firms **[[idakto]]** and
  ShareID engaged on the wallet architecture (privacy/security by design, CNDP-overseen). Sources:
  [[2026-02-13 Morocco launches Idarati X.0 e-government platform (8 MoUs, national digital wallet)]],
  [[2026-02-13 Morocco signs agreements with iDakto and ShareID for national digital identity wallet]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.id]], [[morocco--dpi-id]].
- **2026-01-14** — **16.5m voters; most registrations digital.** The annual electoral-roll revision
  reached ~**16.5m** voters; **382,170 new registrants**, mostly via the online rolls (254,740 web vs
  127,430 in person); ~1.4m entries cleansed (ahead of 23 Sep 2026 elections). Source:
  [[2026-01-14 Morocco — 16.5m voters on electoral rolls; most 2026 registrations via digital channels]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[data.statistics]].
- **2025-11** — **Digital X.0 law (AI + data + digital ID).** Morocco unveiled the **[[digital-x0-law]]**
  draft — its first attempt to legislate **AI governance**, alongside data governance, sectoral digital
  IDs and interoperability (under Law 09-08). Sources:
  [[2025-11-07 Morocco introduces 'Digital X.0' law for AI governance, data governance and digital identity]],
  [[2025-11-06 Morocco prepares 'Digital X.o' draft law (AI governance, sectoral digital IDs, interoperability)]].
  See [[gov.legislate]], [[tech.ai]], [[gov.protect]].
- **2025-09-24** — **Mon e-ID fast-tracked after cyberattacks.** Morocco accelerated the **[[mon-e-id]]**
  national digital-identity app (built by **[[idakto]]** on IDEMIA/IN Group foundations; hybrid,
  privacy-preserving; **2.5m users**, 7m+ transactions since Apr 2022) after attacks on the CNSS, Justice
  Ministry and ANCFCC. Source:
  [[2025-09-24 Morocco fast-tracks 'Mon e-ID' digital identity after cyberattacks (iDAKTO)]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[infra.cybersec]].
- **2025-07-31** — **Digital Morocco 2030 (US Country Commercial Guide).** The strategy (launched 25 Sep
  2024) targets e-government, an African tech hub and startups; tech = **6.8% of GDP**; Casablanca hosts
  25+ data centres; Oracle announced two cloud regions (Casablanca + Settat). Source:
  [[2025-07-31 Morocco Digital Economy — US Country Commercial Guide (Digital Morocco 2030)]]. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[infra.store]].
- **2025-07-14** — **Smart Africa CBDE Guidelines hosted in Rabat (ADD-chaired CAITA).** Morocco's ADD,
  chairing **[[smart-africa]]**'s CAITA, convened 11 states to draft Africa's first **Cross-Border Data
  Exchange Guidelines** (SATA). Source:
  [[2025-07-14 Smart Africa co-creates Africa's first Cross-Border Data Exchange Guidelines (Rabat, ADD-chaired CAITA)]].
  See [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2025** — **Cloud First Policy.** Cloud becomes the mandatory default for public-admin IT investment
  ([[add-morocco]]; national cloud observatory from 2027), with an emerging AI First strategy. Source:
  [[2025-01-01 Morocco adopts Cloud First Policy under Digital Morocco 2030]]. See [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2025-05-05** — **DGSN–ministry digital-ID partnership.** [[dgsn-morocco|DGSN]] + digital-transition
  ministry deal to make services accessible via the national biometric digital ID (trusted-third-party
  verification). Source: [[2025-05-05 Morocco boosts digital ID for government services (DGSN–Ministry partnership)]].
  See [[dpi.id]].
- **2024-12** — **DGSN e-ID scale; WB $70m social-protection registry.** DGSN issued **4.6m** e-national
  IDs in 2024; the World Bank approved **$70m** to extend the **[[morocco-national-population-registry|RNP
  + RSU]]** (Law 72-18, ~half the population; AMO-TADAMON, Direct Social Benefit). Sources:
  [[2024-12-24 Morocco's DGSN issued 4.6 million electronic national IDs in 2024 (annual report)]],
  [[2024-12-04 World Bank approves $70M for Morocco's social protection delivery (RNP and Unified Social Registry)]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]].
- **2024-09** *(baselines)* — **Cyber Tier 1; EGDI 90th.** Morocco reached ITU GCI **Tier 1** (97.5/100)
  and rose to **90th** in the 2024 UN EGDI (4th in Africa). Sources:
  [[2024-09-14 Morocco achieves Tier 1 (top) in ITU Global Cybersecurity Index 2024]],
  [[2024-09-20 Morocco jumps 11 spots to 90th in 2024 UN E-Government ranking]]. See [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-03-03** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2025.** Population 38.3m; internet penetration
  **92.2%**; mobile 143%; social media 55.5%. Source: [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - Morocco (DataReportal)]].
  See [[data.statistics]].
- **Reference (2016 / 2020).** Long-run baselines: an OBG survey of Morocco's ICT/offshoring/e-payments
  sector (2016) and a World Bank $500m Financial & Digital Inclusion DPF (2020). Sources:
  [[2016-10-27 New plan and updated legislation provide a boost for Morocco's IT sector (Oxford Business Group)]],
  [[2020-05-26 Morocco First Financial and Digital Inclusion Development Policy Financing (World Bank)]].
- **2026-06 (held)** — **MOSIP-first adopter; 500 MW renewables AI-sovereignty project.** Morocco was the
  first MOSIP adopter (2018; ~22m registered) and is pursuing a 500 MW renewable-powered AI compute build
  outside CLOUD-Act reach ([[cassava-ai-factories]] site). Sources:
  [[2026-06-17 They're taking DPI to the world - The Times of India]],
  [[2026-06-01 Africa's Place in the AI Sovereignty Debate]]. See [[geopol.india]], [[tech.ai]], [[infra.energy]].
