# Malawi (MWI)

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-13** — **Digital ID at near-universal coverage; mobile transition begins.** Malawi
  has issued trusted national digital IDs to **>13.5m** citizens — **~99% of those aged 16+** —
  reaching the brink of universal coverage (150,000+ enrolled Apr–Jun, **53% women**), on
  **[[undp]]** / EU / Norway / Irish Aid support. Separately, the
  **[[national-registration-bureau-malawi]]** (PS Patrick Machika) outlined a transition to
  **mobile-accessible digital ID cards** and a review of the **National Registration Act** (and
  Marriage/Divorce Act), under the **[[dmap]]** — the enrolment→service-gateway pivot. Sources:
  [[2026-07-13 Malawi reaches 13.5M digital IDs as universal coverage comes into view]],
  [[2026-07-13 Malawi outlines plans to transition to digital IDs - CAJ News Africa]]. Full case:
  [[malawi--dpi-id]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.legislate]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-06-09** — **Only 3% of web content served locally.** An Internet Society Pulse content-locality
  study (weighted by bytes) put Malawi at the bottom of 12 measured countries — **3% local** — but **62%
  regional**, served largely from South Africa, keeping most traffic on-continent. Underlines the case for
  local hosting/CDNs and IXPs. [[2026-06-09 Beyond the Homepage Measuring What It Really Takes to Keep Web Traffic Local]] · [[infra.store]] · [[infra.connect]]
- **2026-06-03** — **[[malawi-revenue-authority]]**'s **Electronic Invoicing System
  (EIS)** now generates **>$1.2m/day** in tax revenue; **May collections exceeded
  ~$28m**. About **7,500 of ~9,000 registered VAT operators** have migrated. The EIS
  captures transactions in real time (VAT + income tax simultaneously), replacing the
  older Electronic Fiscal Devices and sharply improving compliance visibility —
  digital-tax-as-infrastructure, with **blackouts and connectivity** the main
  constraints. Source: [[2026-06-03 Malawi reaps digital tax gains]]. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-07-07** — Malawi integrates **immigration e-services** onto the **Bomalathu**
  single digital-services platform under the World Bank-funded **Digital Malawi
  Acceleration Project** ([[dmap]]). The public gains online access to passport, visa and
  border services while the Department of Immigration and Citizenship Services **retains
  ownership and control of its data**. **25 e-services** from various MDAs are being
  onboarded; Bomalathu already serves the Malawi Revenue Authority and the National
  Registration Bureau, on **locally hosted server infrastructure** framed as supporting
  **data sovereignty**. Source: [[2026-07-07 Malawi integrates immigration e-services]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[infra.store]].
- **2026-03-06** — **Blantyre digitises tax collection.** Malawi's second city,
  **[[blantyre-city-council]]** (Mayor Isaac Jomo Osman), signed with **NBS Bank** to
  digitise revenue and tax collection and let residents pay council services
  electronically — a sub-national counterpart to the national **[[malawi-revenue-authority]]**
  e-invoicing drive, sitting within the **[[dmap]]**. Source:
  [[2026-03-06 Malawi's second city digitises tax collection]]. See [[digital.localgov]],
  [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-02-20** — **AI + digital-transformation strategies enter validation.** Malawi
  opened a two-day validation workshop (Lilongwe) for a **Draft National Digital
  Transformation Strategy** and a **Draft National AI Strategy**, and launched a "Shape
  Malawi's Digital and AI Future" citizen-feedback portal; led by the
  **[[malawi-ministry-of-ict]]** (Department of E-Government, PS Prosper Mopiwa) with
  **[[undp]]**, aligned to Malawi 2063. Pre-adoption drafts. Source:
  [[2026-02-20 Malawi validates AI strategy, digital transformation policy - CAJ News Africa]].
  See [[gov.policy]], [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[capacity.training]].
- **2026-02-16** — **President orders a "digital revolution."** In his State of the Nation
  Address opening the 2026/27 budget, President **Arthur Peter Mutharika** directed all MDAs
  to digitalise public services "forthwith" — passports, national ID cards, driver's licences
  and public procurement — framed as anti-corruption and efficiency; a **Digital Identity
  Wallet** is being tested and the government targets **80% internet usage by 2026**, aligned
  to Malawi 2063. Source: [[2026-02-16 Malawi President orders digital revolution]]. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-01-28** — **Data privacy as cornerstone.** At Data Privacy Week (Lilongwe), ICT
  Minister **Shadrick Namalomba** declared data protection "no longer optional" and "Privacy
  by Design" essential, citing the **[[malawi-data-protection-act-2024]]** as the basis for
  curbing data misuse and building trust in e-government and digital-ID platforms. Source:
  [[2026-01-28 Malawi positions data privacy as cornerstone of digital development]]. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-10-21** *(publ.)* — **HMIS data under-used in faith-based facilities.** A
  cross-sectional study of six **Synod of Livingstonia** health facilities (Aug 2023; 103
  staff) found **70%** of facility respondents used HMIS data but only **33%** of district
  respondents did, and **31%** of facility staff had had **no data-handling training in 24
  months**; Malawi runs its HMIS on **[[dhis2]]**. Barriers: no SOPs, weak resourcing,
  supervision and incentives. Entity: [[hmis-malawi-livingstonia]]. Source:
  [[2025-10-21 Utilisation of data and factors affecting the performance of the Health Management Information System (HMIS). The case of six Synods of Livingstonia health facilities in Malawi]].
  See [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]], [[capacity.training]], [[gov.standards]].
- **2021-08-04** *(background)* — **Malawi: the national-strategy exception.** A 12-country
  African HIS review ([[his-developing-countries-review]]) found **11 of 12** countries ran
  DHIS2-based systems aligned to *donor* strategies with **no national strategy** — **Malawi
  the sole exception**, having asserted a national HIS strategy (with a procedural guide) to
  which all actors, including donors, must align. Source:
  [[2021-08-04 Health information systems in developing countries case of African countries]].
  See [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]], [[gov.standards]], [[capacity.research]].
