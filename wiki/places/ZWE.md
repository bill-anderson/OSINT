# Zimbabwe (ZWE)

Southern African ([[XSA]]) state whose digital record is defined by a **gap between
architecture and operation**. The scaffolding is real — a 2021 data protection statute with
regulations in force, a gazetted deeds-securitisation regime, a modernised population
registry — but **commencement dates, coverage figures and outcomes are repeatedly absent**:
the digital ID announced for July 2025 did not visibly start; the deeds scheme's 24-month
clock keys off a ministerial announcement the wiki does not hold; the 2024 biometric border
pilot has no recorded outcome. Set against **61.6% of the population offline and 67.1% rural
(end-2025)**, the question here is less what Zimbabwe has legislated than what has actually
been switched on. One structural feature to carry: **[[potraz]], the telecom regulator,
doubles as the data protection authority** — there is no separate DPA.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-06** — **Powertel** (ZESA's telecom arm, a Mutapa Investment Fund
  company) and **[[paratus]]** Zimbabwe switched on **phase 1 (Plumtree–Bulawayo)**
  of a cross-border fibre corridor — **DWDM >10 Tbps-capable**, initial **800
  Gbps**. Phase 2 (Bulawayo–Livingstone, Zambia) lights up **Sept 2026**,
  completing the **Botswana–Zimbabwe–Zambia** spine under a June 2025 PPP (IRU
  model, operational control retained by Powertel). Deal:
  [[powertel-paratus-fibre-corridor]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.regional]].
  Source: [[2026-07-06 Powertel, Paratus Zimbabwe switch on new digital highway]].
- **2026-06-23** — **National AI Strategy 2026–2030 — rights risks.** MISA Zimbabwe (Helen Sithole) warns
  the strategy ([[zimbabwe-national-ai-strategy]]) centralises data and verification authority without
  safeguards — **[[project-pangolin]]** (National AI & Data Platform), an AI–Cybersecurity Fusion Centre
  and a National Reality Check Platform layered on Cyber & Data Protection Act s.164C and an executive-run
  monitoring centre with no judicial oversight. "Ubuntu"/"data sovereignty" framing, but no legal force.
  [[2026-06-23 Implications of Zimbabwe’s National AI Strategy on Freedom of Expression Privacy and Access to Information]] · [[tech.ai]] · [[gov.protect]]
- **2026-06** — Zimbabwe named the **fastest-growing African Starlink market**
  (67,057 subscribers, Q4 2025, +31.6% QoQ). Source:
  [[2026-06-16 Who Owns Africa's Sky]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[starlink]].
- **2026-04-07** — **Free public Wi-Fi + AI-skills push.** ICT Minister Tatenda Mavetera
  launched free public Wi-Fi (tied to the 46th Independence Day, 18 Apr 2026): **409
  hotspots** deploying toward **1,000 by year-end**, in schools/clinics/public spaces, to
  lower the cost of access for local AI innovation. Paired with a flagship **1.5-million
  coding programme with the [[uae]]** (AI, cybersecurity, software dev) and a cyber
  programme that trained 3,000+ youths; [[starlink]] recently licensed. Source:
  [[2026-04-07 Zimbabwe switches on free wifi to power AI future]]. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[include.access]], [[capacity.training]], [[digital.rural]].
- **2026-04-04** — **Chinese surveillance.** An IDS "Smart City Surveillance in Africa"
  survey lists Zimbabwe among 11 states running Chinese-supplied ([[hikvision]] / [[zte]] /
  [[huawei]]) camera/command-centre systems **without adequate legal oversight**; critics
  fear facial-recognition targeting of opposition and activists. Source:
  [[2026-04-04 Chinese surveillance rolled out in Africa ZTE, Hikvision, Huawei at the helm]].
  See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.china]].
- **2026-02-24 → 25** — **Zimbabwe rejects the US health MoU on sovereignty grounds.**
  Harare withdrew from **America First Global Health Strategy** ([[america-first-global-health-strategy]])
  talks after President Mnangagwa (Dec 2025 memo / 23 Dec letter) called the draft
  "clearly unbalanced": objections to **long-term US access to national health data**, an
  attempt to fold in **critical minerals**, and a bilateral architecture outside the WHO.
  ~1.2m Zimbabweans rely on US-supported HIV treatment now facing wind-down. Deal:
  [[zimbabwe-us-health-mou]] (see also [[usaid]]). **Value: $367m over 5 years** — the US Embassy
  Harare's own figure (Ecofin, 25 Feb), corroborated by Africanews and Reuters/Africa CDC; the earlier
  **$350m** (Business Insider, 24 Feb) was a pre-announcement estimate off the leaked Dec-2025 memo
  (REV-004 resolved 2026-07-16). Sources:
  [[2026-02-24 ⁠Zimbabwe rejects $350 million US health funding deal warns It threatens national sovereignty]],
  [[2026-02-25 Zimbabwe Turns Down U.S. Health Funding, Citing Sovereignty Concerns]],
  [[2026-03-27 Kenya's Health Deal Is a Stress Test for the America First Global Health Strategy]].
  See [[geopol.usa]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]].
- **2025-12-19 → 22** — **Econet InfraCo: a 300-hectare industrial/IT park.**
  **[[econet-infraco]]**, the infrastructure arm of **Econet Wireless Zimbabwe**, announced a
  **300-hectare** industrial and IT park near **Robert Gabriel Mugabe International Airport**,
  Harare, with a **100 MW solar plant** and a data centre whose **capacity is not disclosed**.
  **Phased**, phase 1 apparently begun; possibly **one of the largest private-sector
  developments since independence**. To list on the **[[victoria-falls-stock-exchange]]**,
  which **accepts only US dollars** — part of a tower-unbundling following African peers. CEO
  **Douglas Mboweni** (confirmed serving Dec 2025). **Contradiction: ITWeb calls InfraCo
  "newly founded"; Developing Telecoms, citing the company, says it is "not a new business"
  but a long-standing unit newly renamed — see
  `reviews/contradictions/open/econet-infraco-founding`.** *InfraCo sits under Econet Wireless
  **Zimbabwe**, not the Group (ISSUE-008).* Sources:
  [[2025-12-19 Econet approves ambitious Harare IT park data centre]],
  [[2025-12-22 Zimbabwe's Econet InfraCo announces industrial park plans]].
  See [[infra.store]], [[infra.energy]], [[tech.industry]], [[finance.new]].

- **2025-07-18 → 08-01** — **Deeds securitisation gazetted — but not commenced.**
  **[[zimbabwe-si-76-2025]]**, gazetted **18 July 2025** by the Minister for Justice, Legal
  and Parliamentary Affairs, creates **"securitised deeds"** — digitally recorded,
  electronically authenticated, on security paper — after the **Dr Dzingai Mutumbuka** case,
  in which a deed **stolen from the Registry** and altered with an official's help was used
  to sell the former minister's home. **The 24-month validation window, after which only
  securitised deeds are valid, runs from the Minister's announcement of commencement, not
  from gazetting — and as of 1 Aug 2025 commencement had not been triggered (GAP-ZWE-003).**
  *Both sources law-firm-authored; the Nehanda piece is by a conveyancing practice paid to
  perform the validation — attribute.* **Full treatment: [[zimbabwe--dpi-registry]].**
  Sources:
  [[2025-07-28 Legal Discussions with Vengai Madzima - Digitization of the Deeds Registry in Zimbabwe]],
  [[2025-08-01 How to secure your property in Zimbabwe with the new digital deed system]].
  See [[dpi.registry]], [[gov.legislate]], [[zimbabwe-deeds-registry]].
- **2025-07-15** — **Digital ID: a missed start, not a launch.** The
  **[[ministry-of-home-affairs-zimbabwe]]** announced in **May 2025** that issuance would
  begin **July 2025** in Harare and Bulawayo; this piece, published **15 July**, records **"no
  official indications yet when exactly the process will take off"**. Minister **Kazembe
  Kazembe** framed it as curbing identity theft; Registrar-General **Henry Machiri**
  ([[registrar-general-zimbabwe]]) says personnel are trained and materials in place.
  Predicated on the **population registry modernisation begun ~2023**. **Programme status
  only: every claim is relayed second-hand and no coverage figure exists.** The same piece
  reports an **AI traffic system in Harare called "home-grown, developed by [[telone]]"**
  under the **[[safe-city-project-zimbabwe]]**, plus a smart city for a **new capital outside
  Harare** that "has faced sharp criticism from digital rights advocates". **Tension flagged,
  not resolved: "home-grown" sits awkwardly beside the 2026-04-04 entry above listing Zimbabwe
  among states running Chinese-supplied camera systems without oversight — possibly different
  systems; insufficient evidence to call.** Source:
  [[2025-07-15 Zimbabwe looks forward to digital ID rollout after population registry reform]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2025-07-07 → 11** — **Binga mobile registration — the one accomplished ID fact.** The
  **[[civil-registry-department-zimbabwe]]**, with **[[unicef]]** and the **Embassy of
  Sweden**, ran a birth-registration and national-ID drive in **Binga district**, reaching
  villages ~**160 km from Binga Centre**: journeys **up to seven hours**, poor roads,
  **wildlife risk**. **Birth registration 57% (ZDHS 2023–24** — dated to the survey, not the
  article**)**. Source:
  [[2025-07-10 Zimbabwe Launches Mobile ID Registration Program to Serve Remote Binga Communities]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[digital.rural]], [[include.divides]].
- **2025-06-30** — **The Cyber and Data Protection Act described.**
  **[[zimbabwe-cyber-and-data-protection-act]] (2021)** applies to all organisations
  regardless of size, **enforced by [[potraz]] — the telecom regulator doubling as the data
  protection authority**. Regulations in force **12 September 2024**; **DPO deadline 12
  December 2024**. **Breach notification: POTRAZ within 24 hours; individuals within 72 hours
  where risk is high.** Cross-border transfers need prior POTRAZ notification — but **the Act
  does not regulate the sale of personal data**. **No internal ROPAs; instead POTRAZ maintains
  a public register of automated data processing** — an inversion of the GDPR pattern.
  Extraterritorial: **foreign controllers must appoint a local representative**. *Law-firm
  briefing (Michalsons markets compliance services) — attribute; **the Act text is not held
  (GAP-ZWE-001)** despite two held sources relying on s.164C.* Michalsons' compliance reading
  and MISA's rights reading (2026-06-23, above) **do not conflict on facts — they weight the
  same Act differently**. Source:
  [[2025-06-30 Zimbabwe's Cyber and Data Protection Act - Overview]]. See [[gov.protect]],
  [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.standards]].
- **2024-10-23** — **Electronic payment as a consumer right.** The
  **[[consumer-protection-commission-zimbabwe]]** found **80% of the 27 service stations it
  inspected (~22) had no POS machine** — **an enforcement finding on a small fuel-retail
  sample, not a national rate**. Legal basis: **Consumer Protection Act Ch 14.44 s.51(h)**,
  the right to choose one's mode of payment. Separately the **[[reserve-bank-of-zimbabwe]]**
  **exempted electronic transactions under US$10 / ZiG equivalent from bank charges,
  effective 1 September 2024**. Source:
  [[2024-10-23 Electronic Transactions A Consumer Right - Consumer Protection Commission]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.protect]], [[include.access]].
- **2024-05-15** — **Biometric border posts — a pilot, historical baseline.** Fingerprint and
  iris biometrics to **"initially be tested"** at **Robert Gabriel Mugabe International
  Airport, Victoria Falls International Airport and Beitbridge**, plus inland stations.
  **[[obms-zimbabwe]]** is **"reportedly" the first in SADC** — a state-media claim the author
  hedges; **the wiki does not assert it**. Architecture derived from **Dubai's Smart Gates**.
  Immigration chief director **Respect Gono** describes a centralised platform letting border
  and security agencies **share information** — cross-agency biometric sharing with **no
  oversight mechanism mentioned**. **The fact the source does not argue: a single Lithuanian
  vendor ([[garsu-pasaulis]]) supplies both Zimbabwe's passports and its border database.**
  *Wholly derivative of The Sunday Mail (state-owned). **No outcome held (GAP-ZWE-002) — not
  to be written as current operation.*** Source:
  [[2024-05-15 Zimbabwe implements biometric border posts for automating immigration processes]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]], [[department-of-immigration-zimbabwe]].

## Foundational: Econet / mobile money

- **[[strive-masiyiwa]]** founded **[[econet-wireless]]** — licensed in **1997**
  only after winning a **constitutional-court battle** against the state telecom
  monopoly. Its **[[ecocash]]** mobile-money service made Zimbabwe a candidate
  for "**Africa's first cashless society**": by **2014** roughly **43% of GDP**
  moved through Econet, a shift enabled by the **2002 hyperinflation** collapse
  of trust in banks. Econet is the lineage behind [[cassava-technologies]].
  Sources: [[2014-08-18 Zimbabwe's Econet Wireless and the making of Africa's first cashless society  Working in development  The Guardian]],
  [[2023-01-01 The Rebel Behind Africa's Telecom Revolution  Stanford Graduate School of Business]],
  [[2021-05-26 Strive Masiyiwa the philanthropist who connected Africa  MoneyWeek]].
- Zimbabwe's **National AI Strategy** is referenced in the Data Landscapers
  54-country review. Source: [[2026-03-31 Africa we have a data problem]].
  See [[tech.ai]].
