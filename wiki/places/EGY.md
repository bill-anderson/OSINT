# Egypt (EGY)

North-African digital state, tracked here mainly as a **GovTech and submarine-cable** node
rather than a Data Landscapers core country (the wiki's remit is Sub-Saharan; Egypt appears
where continental infrastructure, GovTech benchmarking or AI-strategy comparisons pull it in).

## Recent developments

- **2026-05/06** — **Egypt in the AI-infrastructure batch.** Egypt is a planned site in
  the **[[cassava-ai-factories]]** ~$720m five-country GPU program (with a further ~12,000
  GPUs across EGY/NGA/KEN/MAR over 3–4 years, per Russell Southwood's demand-side survey,
  [[2026-05-19 Trying to make sense of who will use Artificial Intelligence in Africa and how – Going beyond the hype]]),
  and is counted among the "big four" African tech economies (KEN/NGA/ZAF/EGY) in Anjola
  Adebowale's sovereign-data-stack benchmarking
  ([[2026-06-12 Beyond the AI Stack Why Governments Need Dynamic Digital Infrastructure]]).
  See [[infra.store]], [[tech.ai]].
- **GovTech "Group A" (GTMI).** Egypt is scored among Africa's most mature GovTech states —
  in the "Extensive" tier alongside **[[KEN]], [[RWA]], [[UGA]]** — on the World Bank GovTech
  Maturity Index. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.regional]].
- **AI strategy drafted.** Egypt is one of the larger African tech economies — with **[[NGA]],
  [[KEN]], [[ZAF]]** — to have drafted a national **AI strategy**. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.policy]].
- **Submarine-cable landing hub.** Egypt is one of the continent's **five main cable hubs**
  ([[EGY]], [[ZAF]], [[DJI]], [[NGA]], [[KEN]]) through which most of Africa's international
  bandwidth flows — a chokepoint as well as an asset. See [[infra.connect]], [[XAF]].
