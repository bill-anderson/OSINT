# South Sudan (SSD)

South Sudan is digitising faster than the infrastructure and governance meant to
support it. A **de-facto DPI** — telecom mobile money, donor biometric registries,
humanitarian cash systems — keeps commerce and services moving, but it is
fragmented, proprietary, and vulnerable to outages, insecurity and political
interference. The two structural gaps are a **reliable connectivity/energy layer**
and, most critically, the **absence of a unified interoperable national digital ID**.

## Recent developments

- **2026-02-20** — **$5m green-network boost.** **[[finnfund]]** committed a $5m
  follow-on into **[[crei-south-sudan]]** (Communication and Renewable Energy
  Infrastructure), bringing its total to $10m, backed by the EU's EFSD+ /
  Africa Connected programme. The funds modernise solar-hybrid power at
  **[[mtn-group]]** South Sudan's **499 telecom sites** (490 renovated, serving
  4m+) under a 10-year Energy-as-a-Service contract; network coverage rose from
  **69% to 80%** of the population over three years. Reliable energy is the
  binding constraint on connectivity here. Source:
  [[2026-02-20 South Sudan green network gets $5m boost]]. See [[infra.energy]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[finance.new]].
- **2026-02-09** — **Huawei re-engagement.** **[[huawei]]**'s new CEO Wang Shibo
  (in role since Dec 2025) met ICT minister Ateny Wek Ateny in Juba, pledging to
  expand partnerships and reaffirming Huawei's presence in the ICT sector. Notable
  given the **2014 rift**, when Juba accused Huawei of hacking government emails and
  forging documents to damage a rival **[[zte]]** contract (Huawei blamed a junior
  employee). Source: [[2026-02-09 Huawei reconnects with South Sudan]]. See
  [[geopol.china]], [[infra.connect]], [[tech.industry]].
- **2026-01-30** — **"Can a digital economy exist without reliable DPI?"** Feature
  on the country's de-facto DPI: mobile money is the primary payments layer but a
  broken acceptance chain (outages, liquidity shortages) reinforces cash hoarding.
  The **[[bank-of-south-sudan]]**'s **Inter-Bank Payment & Settlement System**
  (launched Oct 2025 — instant transfers, bulk salaries, e-government payments) is
  undermined by incomplete bank integration and governance concerns that key
  tax/revenue systems are individually rather than institutionally controlled.
  Internet is among the most expensive in the region; **[[starlink]]** relieves some
  NGOs/media but is beyond most households. Humanitarian/health biometric registries
  are the most reliable systems yet remain donor-driven, proprietary and disconnected
  from national registries. Draws a comparison with **[[KEN]]**. Source:
  [[2026-01-30 Can a digital economy exist in South Sudan without reliable DPI]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[include.divides]].
- **2026-01-19** — **Cashless digital passport payments.** The
  **[[south-sudan-national-revenue-authority]]** (Commissioner-General William Anyuon
  Kuol) abolished cash at immigration offices in Juba, replacing it with an
  EU-supported bank-linked digital payment system for passports and national ID —
  invoices auto-linked to the immigration database — to curb revenue leakage and a
  black market in passport booklets. Follows a months-long booklet shortage (10,000
  received after clearing supplier debts) and a **Nov 2025 biometric registration of
  public servants** (anti-ghost-worker). Analysts stress the reform is limited absent
  a trusted national digital ID layer; the government has ordered institutions to
  digitise by January 2026. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 South Sudan introduces digital payment system for passports after supply crisis]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]].
