# Niger (NER)

A junta-led Sahelian state pursuing **foundational identity and connectivity as digital-sovereignty
projects**, tightly bound to the **[[alliance-of-sahel-states|Confederation of Sahel States (AES)]]** after
quitting ECOWAS. The spine is a **[[niger-national-eid|biometric national ID and e-passport]]** built with a
Libyan supplier (**[[al-itissal-al-jadeed]]**), backed by a formal — if young — data-protection regime
(**[[hapdp-niger|HAPDP]]** under **[[niger-data-protection-law|Loi 2017-28]]**) and a first Niamey **IXP** —
all against very low connectivity (**internet penetration ~23% (2025)**).

## Recent developments

- **2026-03-27** — **AES biometric national ID launched (Tiani first enrollee).** Niger became the **second**
  AES member (after Burkina Faso) to roll out the bloc's regional **[[niger-national-eid|biometric national
  ID]]**, President **Abdourahamane Tiani** the first to enrol — issued to citizens 15+, ICAO-standard,
  enabling **passport-free travel** within the AES and framed as a digital-sovereignty assertion. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 Focus on Sahel digital sovereignty gets clearer with Niger's new biometric ID card]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[gov.regional]], [[infra.store]].
- **2025-12-26** — **e-Passport + biometric-ID decrees approved.** The Council of Ministers approved the
  decrees creating **AES electronic passports and a biometric national ID card**, the legal step ahead of the
  March-2026 enrolment launch. Sources:
  [[2025-12-30 Niger introduces AES electronic passports and AES biometric national ID card]],
  [[2026-01-01 Niger Alliance of Sahel States' Biometric Identity Card Announced]],
  [[2026-01-01 Republic of Niger - National Electronic Identity (E-ID)]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2025-06-27** — **Two MoUs with Libyan firm to digitise ID and passports.** Niger signed **two MoUs with
  the Libyan company [[al-itissal-al-jadeed|Al Itissal Al Jadeed]]** to modernise and digitise production of
  the national ID card and passport — the choice of an African supplier over a Western vendor cast as
  technological independence and local data control. Sources:
  [[2025-06-30 Niger moves to modernize ID system with biometric e-ID and e-Passport]],
  [[2025-06-30 Signature of two MoUs between Niger and Libyan firm Al Itissal Al Jadeed - toward digitising passports and the national ID card]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2025-08-19** — **Niamey Internet Exchange Point inaugurated.** Niger inaugurated a **[[niger-ixp|national
  IXP]]** in Niamey, letting domestic networks exchange traffic locally (lower latency/cost, more data kept
  in-country) — modest but strategic in a market at **~23% internet penetration (ITU 2025)**. Source:
  [[2025-08-21 Niger inaugurates a new Internet Exchange Point (IXP)]]. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[data.statistics]].
- **2025-08-11** — **West Africa digital-ID interoperability strategy validated (WURI).** As a
  **[[wuri-programme|WURI]]** member, Niger took part in validating an ECOWAS/WURI cross-border **fID
  interoperability strategy** (Accra) for mutual recognition of foundational IDs — a regional layer that sits
  awkwardly alongside Niger's ECOWAS exit and AES pivot. Source:
  [[2025-08-11 Stakeholders validate strategy on digital ID interoperability in West Africa (WURI)]]. See
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2024-09** — **Data-protection authority (HAPDP).** Niger's **[[hapdp-niger|High Authority for the
  Protection of Personal Data]]** oversees personal-data processing under **[[niger-data-protection-law|Loi
  2017-28]]** (amended by Loi 2019-71) — the privacy backstop for a fast-moving biometric-ID programme.
  Source: [[2024-09-20 The High Authority for the Protection of Personal Data (HAPDP)]]. See [[gov.protect]],
  [[gov.legislate]].
- **2019-11** *(baseline)* — **Digital-administration reform support (Niger 2.0).** Expertise France / AFD
  backed a project to support Niger's national IT agency (ANSI) in reforming digital administration under the
  "Niger 2.0" agenda. Source:
  [[2019-11-01 Projet d'appui à la réforme de l'administration numérique au Niger]]. See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[gov.policy]].
- **Reference / baselines.** The World Bank **ID4D Country Diagnostic** (2017); the AES **common biometric
  passport** (Jan 2025, on the [[alliance-of-sahel-states|AES]] page); the **WURI** programme brief (2023);
  the national **civil-status (état civil) analysis report** (INS, 2023, computerisation from 2023); and the
  regional **STAR-UEMOA** RTGS settlement system (BCEAO) that Niger uses as a WAEMU member. Cited, not
  absorbed. Sources:
  [[2017-07-01 ID4D Country Diagnostic - Niger (World Bank)]],
  [[2024-12-01 Niger Civil Status (État Civil) Analysis Report 2023 (INS Niger)]],
  [[2004-06-25 STAR-UEMOA (BCEAO)]].
