# Niger (NER)

## Recent developments

- **2026-03-27** — **AES-aligned biometric national ID launched.** Niger became the **second**
  member of the **[[alliance-of-sahel-states]]** (AES) to roll out the bloc's new regional biometric
  national ID, with **President Abdourahamane Tiani** the first citizen to enrol at the launch
  ceremony. The card — issued to citizens aged 15+, meeting ICAO security standards — enables
  **passport-free travel** between the three AES states and is framed as a digital-sovereignty
  assertion. It follows Burkina Faso ([[BFA]]), the first adopter (Nov 2025, President Traoré first
  enrollee); Mali ([[MLI]]) is the third AES member. The bloc severed all **ECOWAS** ties in 2024 and
  launched a common AES biometric passport in January 2025. Niger contracted Libyan firm **Al Itissan
  Al-Jadeed** for its ID cards and biometric passports, and says it plans to **expand national
  data-centre capacity and cybersecurity architecture** and build domestic technical expertise to
  cut external dependence. Source:
  [[2026-03-30 Focus on Sahel digital sovereignty gets clearer with Niger's new biometric ID card]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.regional]], [[infra.store]], [[infra.cybersec]].
