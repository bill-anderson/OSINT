# Tanzania (TZA)

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-10** — **First EAC cross-border instant-payment corridor (RSwitch↔TIPS).** Rwanda
  and Tanzania concluded a week-long technical meeting in Zanzibar on a proof of concept
  interlinking Tanzania's **[[tips-tanzania]]** (TIPS) with Rwanda's **[[rswitch-rwanda]]**
  (RSwitch) — the first corridor under the **[[eac-cross-border-payment-masterplan]]**
  ([[eac]]), under the "variable geometry" principle (only RWA+TZA so far), initially for
  person-to-person transfers, with no launch date. Partners: central banks of RWA/TZA, EAC
  Secretariat, **[[world-bank]]** and **[[giz]]**; chair Nestory Nicholas Maro (Bank of
  Tanzania). Source:
  [[2026-07-10 New Rwanda-Tanzania Payment Pilot could transform trade across East Africa]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[RWA]], [[XEA]].
- **2026-05-21** — **Emerging data-centre market.** Tanzania is grouped with Morocco,
  Djibouti, Ethiopia and Ghana as second-tier African DC markets expected to attract a
  combined **>$1.3bn** in data-centre investment by 2031 (Arizton), driven by improving
  connectivity, tax incentives and Special Economic Zones. Source:
  [[2026-05-21 Africa Data Center Investment 2026 The $4.5B Race and the Power Problem]].
  See [[infra.store]].
- **2026-04-22** — **Phase II of the Jamii Namba national digital ID.** Deputy Home Affairs
  Minister Ayoub Mohamed Mahmoud told parliament that **[[nida-tanzania]]** (NIDA) has
  published tenders for contractors to add **iris and facial recognition** modalities.
  Phase II also funds a NIDA headquarters in Dodoma, an ICT-equipment maintenance centre,
  and expansion of registration offices to **31 districts** (~$14m allocated FY2025/26 for
  the Micheweni District office). Financed by a **~$70m Export-Import Bank of Korea** loan,
  within the World Bank-funded **Digital Tanzania Project** ([[world-bank]], since 2021);
  Phase I built the core data centre and enrolment offices. Source:
  [[2026-04-22 Tanzania seeks biometrics contractors for Phase II of national digital ID project]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[finance.new]].
- **2026-04-21** — **Safiri** (Dar es Salaam; transport/logistics digital infrastructure)
  is one of 15 startups in **[[google-for-startups-accelerator-africa]]** Class 10 (13 Apr–19
  Jun 2026). Source: [[2026-04-21 Meet the 15 Startups Joining the Google for Startups Accelerator Africa Class 10]].
  See [[tech.innovate]], [[finance.new]].
- **2026-04-13** — **Helios Towers completes 133 UCSAF rural sites.** [[helios-towers]]
  finished 133 telecom sites under Tanzania's **Universal Communications Service Access
  Fund** to extend coverage to rural/underserved areas (~45% of its Tanzania sites are
  rural); commemorated in Dodoma with President Samia Suluhu Hassan and ICT Minister
  Angela Kairuki. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Helios Towers completes 133 UCSAF sites to expand rural connectivity in Tanzania]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[digital.rural]], [[include.access]], [[digital.localgov]].
- **2026-04-13** — **UNECA Conference of Ministers roundtable on DPI & sovereignty.**
  At the 58th ECA session ([[uneca]]), Tanzania's Deputy Minister (President's Office –
  Planning and Investment) **Pius Stephen Chaya** argued that DPI impact requires a
  holistic public-private approach anchored in **robust cybersecurity and data-protection
  laws** to safeguard national sovereignty. Source:
  [[2026-04-13 Stakeholders urge shift in digital public infrastructure trajectory]]. See
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-04-01** — **Wingu Cloud Exchange first launched in Tanzania (Nov 2025).**
  [[wingu-africa]]'s private-cloud WCX platform — in-country data residency, local-
  currency pricing, hybrid with Azure/AWS — **debuted in Tanzania in November 2025**
  (before its Ethiopia [[ETH]] rollout), following a $60m raise for East-African
  expansion. Source: [[2026-04-01 Wingu Africa debuts cloud exchange platform in Ethiopia]].
  See [[infra.store]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.protect]].
- **2026-04-16** — **Landlocked-transit gatekeeping, historically (Russell Southwood).**
  Southwood's ICT4D long view cites Tanzania (with Cameroon) as a case of a coastal state's
  incumbent **gatekeeping international bandwidth** for landlocked neighbours in the SAT3/WACS
  era — part of the rent-seeking that open-access advocacy targeted. See [[tech.industry]],
  [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-04-16 The Long View - What ICT4D did for Africa and why that deep past matters for today]].
- **2026-03-31** — **EAC AI-sovereignty bloc.** Tanzania is one of eight [[eac]] members
  weighing a **regional AI Policy Roadmap** to harmonise rules and keep AI's value within
  East Africa; bloc ministers convened at the 4th EAC STI Conference (Kigali). Source:
  [[2026-03-31 EAC moves to create unified AI Sovereignty bloc]]. See [[gov.regional]],
  [[tech.ai]], [[gov.standards]].
- **2026-03-05** — **Information integrity in the 2025 electoral cycle (RIA).** A Research ICT
  Africa report ([[research-ict-africa]]) on multistakeholder efforts to protect information
  integrity during Tanzania's 2025 elections documents **state-led internet shutdowns**, media
  capture, digital divides and political disinformation, and argues global
  information-integrity interventions are largely ineffective in African contexts. Source:
  [[2026-03-05 Safeguarding Tanzania's information integrity]]. See [[include.divides]],
  [[infra.connect]], [[gov.policy]]. *(No dedicated disinformation/shutdown taxonomy slug —
  tagged to nearest; possible taxonomy gap flagged for human.)*
- **2026-01-01** *(fieldwork 2022; CHI ’26)* — **Third-party SIMs in mobile money.** CMU-Africa’s
  72-interview study (Kenya + Tanzania) finds Tanzania’s **biometric-fingerprint** SIM verification blocks
  ID-borrowing (unlike Kenya), yet ID-required mobile money still pushes many onto agent-registered SIMs;
  **>1 in 3** interviewees failed ID registration on the first attempt.
  [[2026-01-01 My Money, Your Name Challenges and Workarounds in ID-Required Mobile Money in East Africa]] · [[dpi.id]] · [[dpi.pay]]
- **2026-01-01** — **Starlink turns partner.** **[[starlink]]** ([[spacex]]) signed
  **[[airtel-africa]]** (Direct-to-Cell across 14 markets) and **[[vodacom]]** (LEO backhaul +
  reseller across 25 countries); Starlink was live in 25 African markets including **Tanzania**
  as of Nov 2025. Deals: [[starlink-airtel-d2c-partnership]],
  [[starlink-vodacom-leo-partnership]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]]. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[data.satellite]].
- **2025-06-12/13** *(event, needs-review)* — **Fintech Festival Tanzania 2025** (Dar es
  Salaam): a regional fintech / insurtech / identity conference (banking, SACCOs, blockchain,
  a Bank of Tanzania fintech-sandbox case study). Captured as a marginal event profile pending
  review. Source: [[2025-01-14 Conference Sessions]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[tech.innovate]].
- **2023-10-30** *(study)* — A **political-economy analysis** of Tanzania's HMIS
  ([[hmis-tanzania-pea]]) maps a **fragmented ecosystem of 18 systems**; [[dhis2]] (rolled
  out to all district councils in 2013) now carries **>90% of data flows**. Government is
  the dominant actor; **long-relationship donors** (via the Basket Fund) most influence
  investment; barriers are staff capacity, finance and **closed-source lock-in**. Entities:
  [[usaid]], [[world-bank]]. Source:
  [[2023-10-30 A political economy analysis of strengthening health information system in Tanzania]].
  See [[dpi.mis]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[gov.standards]], [[data.statistics]].
- **2021-09-13** *(study)* — A 5-country **recording/reporting-burden** study
  ([[rhis-recording-burden-5country]]; Cambodia, Ghana, Mozambique [[MOZ]], Nigeria [[NGA]],
  Tanzania) finds **Tanzania the heaviest**: **48 registers** (vs a 34-country average) and
  **~13h per health worker per month** on reporting; recording eats ~a third of consultation
  time. Calls for international standards for facility registers/forms. Source:
  [[2021-09-13 The burden of recording and reporting health data in primary health care facilities in five low- and lower-middle income countries]].
  See [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]], [[gov.standards]], [[capacity.training]].
