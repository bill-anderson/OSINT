# Guinea-Bissau (GNB)

One of West Africa's least-connected and most fragile states, now assembling the basics of a digital
state largely through **donor-driven** projects. Two threads dominate: a push to digitize a very weak
**civil registry and national ID** (birth registration below 50%) via **[[sirec]]**, a UNDP "Building
National ID" project and a **[[semlex]]** production-centre network — with the **[[enbic|ECOWAS
biometric ID]]** already operationalized; and a nascent **digital-governance strategy**, the
**[[entd-gw|ENTD.GW 2025-2030]]** (UNU-EGOV/UNDP), against an infrastructure base with no national
backbone (international connectivity confined to the ACE coast landing). ~32.5% internet penetration.

## Recent developments

- **2026-01-30** — **New biometric ID production centre (Safim).** **[[semlex]]** opened a biometric
  national-ID production centre in Safim (Biombo), inaugurated by Justice Minister Carlos Pinto Pereira
  — the third in Biombo, part of **18 production centres nationwide**, decentralizing civil
  identification. Source: [[2026-02-11 Semlex Guinea-Bissau strengthens access to biometric identification]].
  See [[dpi.id]].
- **2025-08-27** — **Seeks Chad's fibre experience for a national backbone.** GNB telecom minister
  Julio Mamadou Balde met Chad's minister in N'Djamena to learn from Chad's fibre-optic deployment.
  International connectivity is coast-confined (**ACE** cable, Mar 2023; **Starlink** approved Apr 2025);
  a national backbone is still missing. Source:
  [[2025-08-29 Fibre-optic deployment - Guinea-Bissau wants to learn from Chad]]. See [[infra.connect]].
- **2025-06-10** — **SIREC digitizes civil registration.** The Ministry of Justice's Civil
  Registration Office is digitizing births/deaths/marriages via **[[sirec]]** (UNDP-trained officers),
  with a functional biometric national ID already in place and the **[[enbic]]** operationalized (GNB is
  one of six ECOWAS states). Birth registration is below 50% (~46% of under-fives). Source:
  [[2025-06-10 Guinea-Bissau deploys efforts to digitize birth registration, national ID systems]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[guinea-bissau--dpi-id]].
- **2025-03-03** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2025.** Population 2.23m; mobile connections
  2.62m (118%, 82% broadband); internet users 723k (**32.5% penetration**); social media 19.9%. Source:
  [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - Guinea-Bissau (DataReportal)]]. See [[data.statistics]], [[include.divides]].
- **2025-01-29** — **ENTD.GW national digital strategy launched.** The **[[entd-gw]]** (10 pillars, 6
  objectives, 55 interventions, roadmap to 2030) was launched with UNU-EGOV/UNDP; a capacity-building
  event trained ~50 officials (ITMA). As of early 2025 it awaited Council of Ministers approval. Sources:
  [[2025-02-04 Digital Transformation Project in Guinea-Bissau with UNU-EGOV & UNDP]],
  [[2024-01-01 National Strategy for Digital Transformation in Guinea-Bissau (project)]]. See [[gov.policy]].
- **2024** *(project)* — **UNDP "Building National ID" — digitizing the registry books.** A UNDP/Swiss
  project set up a digitization centre and software to recover and digitize the paper civil-registry
  books as the main national platform. Source:
  [[2024-01-01 Building a National Identification System - Scanning and Digitizing the Civil Registry Books in Guinea-Bissau]].
  See [[dpi.registry]].
- **Reference (WB / IMF).** The WB **[[guinea-bissau-digital-economy-diagnostic]]** (DE4A five pillars)
  and an IMF tax-compliance TA report (May 2024) frame the digital-economy and revenue-modernization
  gaps. Sources: [[2024-01-01 Guinea-Bissau Digital Economy Diagnostic (World Bank)]],
  [[2024-05-01 Guinea-Bissau - Further Improvements in Tax Compliance (IMF technical assistance)]].
