# Guinea-Bissau (GNB)

One of West Africa's least-connected and most fragile states, now assembling the basics of a digital
state largely through **donor-driven** projects. Two threads dominate: a push to digitize a very weak
**civil registry and national ID** (birth registration below 50%) via **[[sirec]]**, a UNDP "Building
National ID" project and a **[[semlex]]** production-centre network — with the **[[enbic|ECOWAS
biometric ID]]** already operationalized; and a nascent **digital-governance strategy**, the
**[[entd-gw|ENTD.GW 2025-2030]]** (UNU-EGOV/UNDP), against an infrastructure base with no national
backbone (international connectivity confined to the ACE coast landing). ~32.5% internet penetration.

Through **2025–26** the state assembled the **governance and legislative spine** it had lacked —
data-governance/protection policy, a cybersecurity strategy and agency, an interoperability
regulation, a revised Electronic Communications Law, and (under the post-coup transition) a contested
digital-integrity/online-falsehoods law ([[guinea-bissau--gov-policy]]) — and moved on connectivity:
a **[[wardip|WARDIP-GB]]** national fibre backbone (**2,877 km**, superseding the old ~888 km design)
and **[[starlink]]** satellite entry ([[guinea-bissau--infra-connect]]).

## Recent developments

- **2026-05-23** — **CNT passes Digital Integrity / online-falsehoods Law.** The transitional
  parliament (Conselho Nacional de Transição), acting after the disputed 26 Nov 2025 events, unanimously
  passed a Law on the Dissemination of False Information on the Internet — framed against those who
  "spread hate" and act "against the State," a digital-speech law with a clear repression angle. Source:
  [[2026-05-23 Guinea-Bissau CNT passes Digital Integrity (online falsehoods) Law]]. See
  [[guinea-bissau--gov-policy]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2026-05-06** — **Telecel + Govt launch STARTOCODE skills committee.** A joint decree created a
  coordination committee for the STARTOCODE digital-skills platform (train **1,000 youth/yr over 4
  years** in programming, data analysis, cloud; emphasis on women and people with disabilities);
  **[[telecel]]** is the private partner. Source:
  [[2026-05-07 Telecel and Guinea-Bissau Government launch STARTOCODE Coordination Committee]]. See
  [[capacity.training]].
- **2026-03-16** — **262 women traders receive ECOWAS biometric ID (São Domingos).** A pilot-phase
  field issuance of the **[[enbic]]** card under a GNB–Senegal women-traders mobility project (National
  Borders Commission; ECOWAS-backed, GIZ-financed). Source:
  [[2026-03-16 Women traders receive ECOWAS biometric ID in Sao Domingos, Guinea-Bissau]]. See
  [[guinea-bissau--dpi-id]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-01-30** — **New biometric ID production centre (Safim).** **[[semlex]]** opened a biometric
  national-ID production centre in Safim (Biombo), inaugurated by Justice Minister Carlos Pinto Pereira
  — the third in Biombo, part of **18 production centres nationwide**, decentralizing civil
  identification. Source: [[2026-02-11 Semlex Guinea-Bissau strengthens access to biometric identification]].
  See [[dpi.id]].
- **2026** *(transition-era, proxy)* — **New Electronic Communications Law + national fibre backbone
  specs.** The Council of Ministers approved a new **Electronic Communications Law** revising the
  outdated 2010 telecom regime; the **[[wardip|WARDIP-GB]]** National Fibre-Optic Backbone design is
  **2,877 km, 56 nodes, 4 phases, >99% population coverage** (superseding the old ~888 km figure).
  Sources: [[2026-01-01 Guinea-Bissau Council of Ministers approves new Electronic Communications Law]],
  [[2026-01-01 Guinea-Bissau National Fibre-Optic Backbone specifications (WARDIP-GB)]]. See
  [[guinea-bissau--infra-connect]].
- **2025-08-27** — **Seeks Chad's fibre experience for a national backbone.** GNB telecom minister
  Julio Mamadou Balde met Chad's minister in N'Djamena to learn from Chad's fibre-optic deployment.
  International connectivity is coast-confined (**ACE** cable, Mar 2023; **[[starlink|Starlink]]**
  licensed Apr 2025, live Jun 2025); the national backbone now under way via **[[wardip|WARDIP-GB]]**.
  Source: [[2025-08-29 Fibre-optic deployment - Guinea-Bissau wants to learn from Chad]]. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[guinea-bissau--infra-connect]].
- **2025-07-10** — **Council of Ministers approves three digital decrees.** Interoperability of
  public/private digital systems; a **National Data Governance and Protection Policy**; and a **National
  Cybersecurity Strategy** — GNB's first substantive data-governance/interoperability instruments.
  Source: [[2025-07-10 Guinea-Bissau Council of Ministers approves three digital decrees (interoperability, data governance, cybersecurity)]]. See [[guinea-bissau--gov-policy]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.exchange]].
- **2025-06-19** — **Starlink commercial launch.** **[[starlink]]** went live (GNB its 7th West
  African / 20th continental market; kit ~XOF 228,000/$400, $63/mo) after [[arn|ARN-TIC]]'s April
  authorization. Source: [[2025-06-19 Starlink enters Guinea-Bissau]]. See [[guinea-bissau--infra-connect]].
- **2025-06-10** — **SIREC digitizes civil registration.** The Ministry of Justice's Civil
  Registration Office is digitizing births/deaths/marriages via **[[sirec]]** (UNDP-trained officers),
  with a functional biometric national ID already in place and the **[[enbic]]** operationalized (GNB is
  one of six ECOWAS states). Birth registration is below 50% (~46% of under-fives). Source:
  [[2025-06-10 Guinea-Bissau deploys efforts to digitize birth registration, national ID systems]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[guinea-bissau--dpi-id]].
- **2025-06** *(month)* — **Civil ID / registry / notary digitization workshop.** A two-day workshop
  (Justice Minister Albino Gomes; DGICRAN) launched modernisation of civil identification/registry/notary
  services — EU-backed registry digitization, birth registration lifted **23.4%→46%** (target ≥90%),
  Bissau-then-regions rollout, and CNE electoral-roll support. Source:
  [[2025-06-01 Guinea-Bissau to digitize civil identification, registry and notary services]]. See
  [[guinea-bissau--dpi-id]].
- **2025-04-10 / 04-02** — **Starlink licensed; WARDIP-GB launched.** [[arn|ARN-TIC]] granted
  **[[starlink]]** a general provisional authorization (2025-04-10); President Embaló launched the
  **[[wardip|WARDIP-GB]]** digital-integration programme in Bissau (2025-04-02, five components).
  Sources: [[2025-04-10 ARN-TIC grants Starlink Guinea-Bissau provisional authorization]],
  [[2025-04-02 President Embalo launches WARDIP in Guinea-Bissau]]. See [[guinea-bissau--infra-connect]].
- **2025-03-03** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2025.** Population 2.23m; mobile connections
  2.62m (118%, 82% broadband); internet users 723k (**32.5% penetration**); social media 19.9%. Source:
  [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - Guinea-Bissau (DataReportal)]]. See [[data.statistics]], [[include.divides]].
- **2025-01-29** — **ENTD.GW national digital strategy launched.** The **[[entd-gw]]** (10 pillars, 6
  objectives, 55 interventions, roadmap to 2030) was launched with UNU-EGOV/UNDP (PM Rui Duarte Barros;
  Minister Barbeiro; UNDP's Alessandra Casazza); a capacity-building event trained ~50 officials (ITMA).
  As of early 2025 it awaited Council of Ministers approval. Sources:
  [[2025-01-29 Guinea-Bissau launches ENTD.GW national digital transformation strategy (ANG)]],
  [[2025-02-04 Digital Transformation Project in Guinea-Bissau with UNU-EGOV & UNDP]],
  [[2024-01-01 National Strategy for Digital Transformation in Guinea-Bissau (project)]]. See [[gov.policy]].
- **2025-01-22** — **Cybersecurity study presented; National Cybersecurity & Data Protection Agency
  announced.** Government presented a WARDIP/NRD cybersecurity feasibility study and committed to create
  a national cybersecurity/data-protection agency — the institutional seed of the July 2025 decrees.
  Source: [[2025-01-22 Guinea-Bissau presents cybersecurity study, plans National Cybersecurity and Data Protection Agency]]. See [[guinea-bissau--gov-policy]], [[infra.cybersec]].
- **2024** *(project)* — **UNDP "Building National ID" — digitizing the registry books.** A UNDP/Swiss
  project set up a digitization centre and software to recover and digitize the paper civil-registry
  books as the main national platform. Source:
  [[2024-01-01 Building a National Identification System - Scanning and Digitizing the Civil Registry Books in Guinea-Bissau]].
  See [[dpi.registry]].
- **Reference (WB / IMF).** The WB **[[guinea-bissau-digital-economy-diagnostic]]** (DE4A five pillars)
  and an IMF tax-compliance TA report (May 2024) frame the digital-economy and revenue-modernization
  gaps. Sources: [[2022-09-18 Guinea-Bissau Digital Economy Diagnostic (World Bank)]],
  [[2024-05-01 Guinea-Bissau - Further Improvements in Tax Compliance (IMF technical assistance)]].

## Provenance

- The wiki now holds the **World Bank DTfA/WARDIP SOP1 Project Appraisal Document (PAD4756, Operation ID P176932, 2023-11-07)**, the financing instrument behind Guinea-Bissau's WARDIP programme. Note it is a **regional** PAD covering The Gambia, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau and Mauritania plus the AU, Smart Africa and ECOWAS — not a Guinea-Bissau-only document. Full programme financing: [[wardip]]. Source: [[2023-11-07-worldbank-dtfa-wardip-sop1-pad4756]].
- **Guinea-Bissau's allocation: US$60.00m IDA grant + US$0.50m government counterpart = US$60.50m total (2023-11-07).** The two figures in the PAD are **not an inconsistency** — they are different quantities. US$60.00m is the IDA Resources table (grant, SDR 45.7m equivalent); US$60.50m is the component-cost table, whose country columns **include** the national counterpart contribution that the IDA table excludes. The PAD's own financing block confirms it: counterpart funding US$1.50m = US$0.50m + US$1.00m, matching Guinea-Bissau (60.00→60.50) and Mauritania (60.00→61.00). Write it dated and attributed, never as a bare "US$60M grant".
- **What the PAD does *not* evidence — stated precisely.** Neither the **2,877 km backbone** figure nor the **govcloud / data-protection-regulator** specifics attributed to WARDIP-GB appear in the copy of the PAD held. **But that copy is an `excerpt`**, truncated mid-way through the results-framework tables — exactly where per-country indicator targets would sit. So the finding is that **the PAD as held does not support those claims**, *not* that it omits or contradicts them.
- **The 2,877 km figure is not unsourced.** It rests on the **WARDIP-GB project's own website** ([[2026-01-01 Guinea-Bissau National Fibre-Optic Backbone specifications (WARDIP-GB)]] — primary, but **undated**, carried `date_source: proxy` at year precision), corroborated by the **EIASS impact study (março 2026)**. The real weakness is the **undated primary**, not an absence of source. The **govcloud and data-protection-regulator** specifics, by contrast, remain **genuinely unsourced as of 2026-07-20**.
- Still **not held as of 2026-07-20**: the **RNEP-GB / WARDIP-GB Final Report (2025-04)** — which would date the backbone design — and ARN's **IDN / .GW accented-domains announcement**.
