# Lesotho (LSO)

## Recent developments

- **2026-02-11** — **RSL–Standard Lesotho Bank tax e-payment integration.** Revenue Services Lesotho
  and SLB launched integrated e-payment (USSD, internet banking, mobile app) for CIT, PAYE, VAT and
  customs duties. Source:
  [[2026-02-11 Revenue Services Lesotho and Standard Lesotho Bank launch e-payment integration for taxes]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-05-11** — **LeSwitch localizes POS (national payment switch).** The Central Bank of Lesotho's
  **[[leswitch|LeSwitch]]** national switch localized POS transactions (after ATMs, Oct 2024), so by
  early 2025 **all local card transactions are processed within the country** — cutting fees. LeSwitch
  (from 2016) also carries instant bank↔mobile-money payments (M-Pesa, Khetsi). Sources:
  [[2025-05-20 LeSwitch expands to localized POS transactions (Central Bank of Lesotho)]],
  [[2024-10-08 Lesotho National Payment Switch (LeSwitch) — Standard Lesotho Bank press release]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].
- **2025-04-02** — **Digital birth registration (Ntlafatsa Bana).** The EU, Government and UNICEF
  launched digital birth registration within the **Ntlafatsa Bana** programme (attended by King Letsie
  III) — recognising children's identity from birth; part of CASH Plus, with 20,000 CGP beneficiaries
  already on digital grant payments ([[lesotho-national-id]]). Source:
  [[2025-04-02 Lesotho launches Digital Birth Registration under Ntlafatsa Bana programme]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]].
- **2025-11-08** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2026.** Population 2.37m; internet penetration
  **48.0%**; mobile connections 92.7%; social media 27.5%. Source:
  [[2025-11-08 Digital 2026 - Lesotho (DataReportal)]]. See [[data.statistics]].
- **2024** — **50-in-5 accession; National Digital Strategy/Policy.** Lesotho joined the **50-in-5**
  DPI campaign as the 14th member (May 2024) and issued the **National Digital Transformation Strategy:
  Agenda 2030** and **National Digital Policy 2024** ([[lesotho-digital-transformation-strategy]]).
  Sources: [[2024-05-14 Lesotho joins 50-in-5 DPI campaign (14th member); CRVS-DPI integration]],
  [[2024-01-01 Lesotho National Digital Transformation Strategy - Agenda 2030]]. See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[gov.regional]].

- **2026-02-20** — **MICSTI/NUL DPI review.** The Ministry of Information,
  Communications, Science, Technology and Innovation ([[micsti-lesotho]]) met the
  National University of Lesotho's Mathematics & Computer Science department to
  review DPI progress. Three systems were presented: **X-Road** (secure
  government interoperability / information sharing), **[[mosip]]** (biometric
  digital ID), and a **Decision Support System (DSS)** for planning, budgeting and
  service delivery. Framed as government–academia collaboration on secure,
  citizen-focused systems. Source:
  [[2026-02-20 MICSTI AND NUL ADVANCED LESOTHO DIGITAL PUBLIC INFRASTRUCTURE]].
  See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.mis]].
- **2026-01-16** — **Child-centred NISSA redesign.** The **National Information
  System for Social Assistance** ([[nissa-lesotho]]) — a social registry holding
  data on **>90% of households**, categorised by vulnerability to target child
  grants, food assistance and health services — is being redesigned with UNICEF
  and the **Co-Develop Fund** to put children's rights at the centre, applying the
  **[[universal-dpi-safeguards-framework]]** (fairness, inclusion, protection of
  children's data; guarding against exclusion for missing documentation,
  disability or remoteness). Piloted in Mafeteng District; officials are
  developing a local safeguards framework. Source:
  [[2026-01-16 Lesotho's Digital Leap How Child-Centred Systems are Reshaping Social Protection]].
  See [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.mis]], [[gov.protect]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-01-01** — **Starlink live in Lesotho via Vodacom LEO backhaul.** Lesotho is among the
  25 African countries where **[[starlink]]** operates, covered by the
  **[[starlink-vodacom-leo-partnership]]** (LEO backhaul + equipment resale). Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]]. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[data.satellite]].
