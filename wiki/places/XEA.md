# East Africa (XEA)

Subregion of [[XAF]]; parent region for Kenya, Ethiopia, Rwanda, Tanzania,
Uganda, Burundi, South Sudan and others (see [countries.csv](../countries.csv)).

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-10** — **First EAC cross-border instant-payment corridor.** Rwanda and Tanzania
  concluded a Zanzibar technical meeting on a proof of concept interlinking Tanzania's
  **[[tips-tanzania]]** (TIPS) and Rwanda's **[[rswitch-rwanda]]** (RSwitch) — the first
  operational corridor under the **[[eac-cross-border-payment-masterplan]]** ([[eac]]), advanced
  under the EAC "variable geometry" principle (only RWA + TZA participating so far), initially
  person-to-person, with no launch date. Development partners: **[[world-bank]]**, **[[giz]]**.
  Source:
  [[2026-07-10 New Rwanda-Tanzania Payment Pilot could transform trade across East Africa]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[RWA]], [[TZA]].
- **2026-06-17** — **[[seacom]]** launched a high-capacity **Nairobi–Kampala**
  terrestrial route (1 Tbps → 30 Tbps, sub-50ms auto-reroute), extending toward
  Rwanda, Burundi and South Sudan. Sources:
  [[2026-06-17 Seacom deploys a new high-capacity Nairobi–Kampala route to strengthen East Africa's digital backbone]],
  [[2026-06-18 Seacom boosts East Africa connectivity]]. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[infra.capacity]].
- **2026-07-03** — **[[vodacom]]** intensified its East Africa strategy around its
  55% **[[safaricom]]** stake and mobile financial services (M-PESA), targeting
  130m financial-services customers by 2030. Source:
  [[2026-07-03 Vodacom expands East Africa strategy]]. See [[dpi.pay]].
- **2026-06** — Kenya-anchored AI moves with East-African framing: a proposed
  Nairobi **OpenAI Academy** for the region, and UAE–Kenya–East Africa "digital
  corridor" ambitions. Sources:
  [[2026-06-18 Kenya Eyes First OpenAI Academy in East Africa Following Ruto–Sam Altman Talks]],
  [[2026-06-18 UAE-Kenya Tech Partnership Gains Momentum as AI Investments Grow]].
- **2026-05-30** — **Afreximbank / PAPSS regional payments.** Afreximbank showcased its
  Pan-African Payment and Settlement System ([[papss]]) suite (PAPSSCARD, African
  Currency Marketplace) for local-currency cross-border settlement; in East Africa,
  **PAPSS has integrated with Kenya's Pesalink** network, opening dozens of banks to
  real-time cross-border payments. Entities: [[afreximbank]], [[papss]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-30 Afreximbank Banks on Digital Payments to Unlock Africa's Trillion-Dollar Trade Potential]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[finance.new]].
- **2026-05-26** — **AfDB** African Economic Outlook 2026 (Brazzaville) projects **East
  Africa** growth easing from **6.6% to 5.9%** on maritime-supply-chain and energy
  shocks — within a continental "fund your own growth" argument (SDG gap >$1.3tn/yr;
  digitising tax could raise $469bn/yr; the NAFAD financial-architecture framework).
  Entities: [[african-development-bank]], [[nafa]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-26 AfDB says Africa must fund its own growth as aid model frays]].
  See [[finance.new]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-05-16** — **EAC's cross-border DPA body.** At the ID4Africa 2026 AGM, a World
  Bank session set out a "trust formula" for digital ID/DPI; the **[[eac]]** has created
  a body to let its **eight member-state DPAs** share knowledge, align practices and
  co-investigate (Rose Mosero, EAC advisor). Entities: [[eac]], [[world-bank]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].
  See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.id]], [[infra.cybersec]].
- **2026-04-28** — **Connected Africa Summit 2026** (Nairobi): a ministerial roundtable
  convened by Kenya's ICT CS **William Kabogo** called for **harmonised data-protection
  frameworks** for cross-border flows, **interoperable digital ID and payment systems**,
  and reliable energy for data centres. Source:
  [[2026-04-28 African Ministers call for harmonised policies and infrastructure investment at Connected Africa Summit Roundtable]].
  See [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]].
- **2025-09-24** — **Seacom 2.0** subsea cable announced (Submarine Networks World,
  Singapore): a **48-fibre-pair** system for the **Indian Ocean Basin**, Middle East,
  Mediterranean and Southern Europe, engineered for low-latency AI workloads with landing
  stations as **"AI communication nodes"** linking African sovereign-AI infrastructure to
  global hubs; a gateway for landlocked East African / SADC markets (GDP and cost figures
  are marketing claims). Entity: [[seacom]]. Source:
  [[2025-09-24 Unveiling Seacom 2.0 - ​ ​A new digital renaissance and subsea network to power the Indian Ocean Basin's digital and AI future]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.capacity]], [[tech.ai]].
