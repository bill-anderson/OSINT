# Côte d'Ivoire (CIV)

Côte d'Ivoire is one of **francophone West Africa's most deliberate digital builds** — and, after this
ingest, one of the clearest cases of **"digital sovereignty" financed by competing outside powers**. The
state runs a costed strategy stack (National AI Strategy at **912bn FCFA**, Data Governance 2030,
Cybersecurity, "Zero Paper 2030") over an Estonian-model interoperability layer and an ONECI biometric ID
spine — while its flagship **National Data Centre is US-EXIM-financed** (via Cybastion, the first EXIM data
centre in sub-Saharan Africa, reportedly displacing a Chinese bidder), the **Gulf (G42)** helps write the
next strategy, **India** courts VITIB, and **Huawei** runs the incumbent's network and trains its students.
The wiki had tagged CIV `geopol.eu` only; the record now spans **US, China, Gulf and EU** at once. Minister
of Digital Transition **Djibril Ouattara** (ex-MTN CI, from Jan 2026) frames it all as technological
sovereignty; the target is digital **6% → 15% of GDP by 2030**.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-17** — **Starlink live in Côte d'Ivoire — SpaceX's 27th African market.** Reported available
  by **2026-07-17**, completing the arc from the **June 2026** 12-month provisional licence. Residential
  pricing at launch: hardware **XOF 148,148** (Mini) / **XOF 247,466** (Standard); service **XOF 28,746/mo**
  (Residential 100Mbps) and **XOF 40,244/mo** (Residential, best-effort). *Dating caveat: **SpaceX has
  published no launch date**, and the held source is a secondary (MyBroadband, 2026-07-17) — so the event is
  dated **"live by 17 July 2026"**, not to that day. As of 2026-07-20 no announcer-side commercial-launch
  notice is held.* The same tabulation puts **19 further African markets** (18 UN-recognised states plus
  Western Sahara) on a stated 2026 timeline, against a repeated pattern of slipped dates. See
  [[civ--infra-connect]], [[infra.connect]], [[starlink]], [[geopol.usa]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-starlink-launches-27th-african-country-ivory-coast]].
- **2026-07-18** — **100 electric ride-hailing cars in Abidjan — the first four-wheeler EV bet at scale.**
  **[[gocab]]**, the mobility fintech that owns **[[yango]]'s** largest CIV fleet, handed **100 fully
  electric vehicles** to driver-partners — the **first half of a 200-vehicle programme**, and one of
  Africa's largest operational fleets of **electric ride-hailing cars**, in a market where electric
  mobility has otherwise been a **two- and three-wheeler** story ([[spiro]]: 100,000+ e-motorcycles,
  2,500 swap stations, seven markets, 2026-07). GoCab, which **raised $45m in Feb 2026**, pairs the
  vehicles with **drive-to-own** finance — repayment from ride-hailing income over **three years**, then
  title — the structure behind [[moove]]'s **$2bn valuation**; **300+ drivers** pass two years in 2026 and
  **take ownership from 2027**. The economics rest on the company's **own unverified benchmarks** of a
  **60–80% cut in energy costs**. See **[[civ--tech-innovate]]**, [[infra.energy]], [[tech.industry]].
  Source: [[2026-07-18-gocab-yango-abidjan-evs]].
- **2026-07** — **US footprint expands; ARTCI's CERTINUM live.** The US EXIM/Cybastion project reached
  **$170m** (adding smart-border surveillance) with **Starlink** final approval; ARTCI's **[[certinum]]**
  data-protection-compliance platform went live (2 Jul). CIV pitched its roadmap to **ITU** leadership.
  See [[civ--infra-store]], [[artci]].
- **2026-06** — **Starlink + 5G; sectoral spread.** A **Starlink 12-month licence** (live Jul 2026) and
  **5G launch** announced; the **[[geoportail-grand-abidjan|Géoportail du Grand Abidjan]]** launched; ANSSI
  stood up a **national CISO/RSSI network**; e-Justice completed its arc — **all 39 first-instance
  tribunals connected** (from a 2-court pilot in Jan 2025). See [[civ--infra-connect]],
  [[civ--dpi-govtech]], [[anssi-cote-divoire]].
- **2026-05** — **New ARTCI Council; UEMOA payments mandate; skills.** A new **ARTCI Regulatory Council**
  (Roger Félix Adom) was seated; **BCEAO** made **payment interoperability mandatory UEMOA-wide from 30 Jun
  2026**; **Orange × UNDP** signed a digital-skills MoU. See [[artci]], [[civ--dpi-pay]].
- **2026-03** — **AI chatbot; budget +37%.** Government launched the **[[emy-101|EMY 101]]** AI assistant
  (WhatsApp/101); the 2026 digital budget confirmed at **CFA 83.275bn (+37%)**. See [[civ--tech-ai]].
- **2026-01** — **New minister; MaPoste; state fintech equity.** **Djibril Ouattara** replaced Konaté at
  the renamed **[[ministry-of-digital-transition-cote-divoire|MTND]]**; **[[maposte|MaPoste]]** super-app
  (NNI/RNPP) launched (digital post boxes mandatory 1 Mar); **[[cdc-ci|CDC-CI]]** took equity in fintech
  **[[green-pay|Green-Pay]]**. See [[civ--dpi-id]], [[civ--dpi-pay]].
- **2025-08 → 09** — **★ US EXIM anchors "digital sovereignty."** US **[[exim-bank-usa|EXIM]]** approved
  **$66m** for the **[[national-data-centre-civ|National Data Centre]]** (first EXIM DC in SSA, via
  **[[cybastion]]**) + **$47m** for Commerce, consolidated to a **$100m+ CTEP** guarantee — inside a **$550m**
  US package — explicitly framed as countering the PRC. See [[civ--infra-store]].
- **2025-09/10** — **Interoperability + sovereign-cloud cluster live.** SNDI/**[[cybernetica]]** UXP
  interoperability went live (12 pilots); **[[st-digital]]** opened a Tier III DC (with OVHcloud) at
  **[[vitib|VITIB]]**, joining Raxio CIV1. See [[civ--dpi-govtech]], [[civ--infra-store]].
- **2025-03** — **The strategy stack, costed.** The **[[civ-ai-strategy|National AI Strategy]]** (912bn
  FCFA, 15 objectives, 109 projects) and **[[civ-data-governance-strategy|Data Governance Strategy 2030]]**
  were handed to the PM; **[[parae|PARAE]]** (~49bn, incl. a data centre) launched. See [[civ--tech-ai]].
- **2025-02** — **ANSSI launched.** The **[[anssi-cote-divoire|national cybersecurity agency]]** was
  launched (created 30 Oct 2024), later taking the cyber mandate from ARTCI under Ordinance 2024-950. See
  [[anssi-cote-divoire]].
- **Counter-current** — **on-time birth registration is *falling*** (66.8% 2021 → 55.4% 2024), against the
  otherwise-positive ONECI/RNPP arc. See [[civ--dpi-id]].
