# Eswatini (SWZ)

Southern African ([[XSA]]) absolute monarchy whose digital story here is one of **legal
scaffolding well ahead of delivery**. Four ICT statutes passed in 2022 — including the
[[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]] — but the promised Cybersecurity Agency and Data
Protection Office were still not operationalised as of mid-2025, and government had, on the
record as of mid-2024, **"not started rolling out digital identity in earnest"**. The spine of
the delivery agenda is **[[government-in-your-hand]]** (GIYH), the platform ambition inside the
[[eswatini-digitalisation-strategy-2024-2028]].

The tension worth watching is adoption, not infrastructure. Eswatini has a national data centre
([[eswatini-national-data-centre]]), a live instant-payments switch ([[eswatini-payment-switch]])
and **nearly 95% 4G population coverage** — yet **over 90% of person-to-business payments were
still cash-based (2025-08-05)** and only about **58%** of people used the internet (2025-07-31).
The rails exist; usage lags them.

## Recent developments

- **2026-02-23** — **Community health services go digital.** The **[[ministry-of-health-eswatini]]**,
  with World Bank support under the **[[eswatini-health-system-strengthening-project]]**,
  introduced the electronic **Community-Based Health Information System** ([[eswatini-cbhis]]) —
  running **offline** on handheld devices and syncing when connectivity allows, integrated with
  the national electronic health record ([[eswatini-cmis]]). Modules cover NCDs, antenatal care,
  under-five nutritional screening, health education, malaria and HIV. **Rural Health Motivators**
  — nearly **22% of the national health workforce** — are the field layer. Pilot completed in
  Mpolonjeni Inkhundla, Lubombo; national rollout beginning, RHMs in training (as of 2026-02-23).
  Source:
  [[2026-02-23 From Paper to Pixels - Digital Transformation of Community Health Services in Eswatini]].
  See [[dpi.mis]], [[digital.rural]], [[dpi.exchange]].
- **2026-05-18** — **World Bank digital-transformation financing still under negotiation.**
  Eswatini was reported advancing toward World Bank funding via negotiations among the ICT,
  Finance and Economic Planning ministries, delivering services through the **GIYH** platform plus
  cybersecurity, digital skills and rural broadband; **funding amount not stated**. This
  corresponds to the World Bank's **[[digital-eswatini-project]]** (**P508948**), whose
  concept-stage PID (28 Jun 2025) proposes **US$65.16m** and gives *estimated* appraisal
  (1 Aug 2025) and *estimated* approval (1 Sep 2025) dates. **No source held confirms actual
  approval**, and the May-2026 report of live negotiations suggests those estimated dates had
  slipped. Sources:
  [[2026-05-18 Eswatini promotes digital transformation project through World Bank engagements]],
  [[2025-06-28 PID Concept Stage - Digital Eswatini (P508948)]],
  [[2025-01-01 ToR - Government in Your Hand Digital Government Implementation Roadmap (Digital Eswatini)]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[finance.new]]. *(The earlier "verify against a WB project record" flag is
  resolved to this extent: the project record is identified as P508948, but its board approval
  remains unconfirmed in the corpus.)*
- **2025-07-31** — **World Bank Economic Update: digital transformation as the growth lever.** The
  second [[eswatini-economic-update-2025]] projects ~**5% growth in 2025** against **35.4%
  unemployment**; **nearly 95% of the population covered by 4G** but only about **58% using the
  internet**; data costing **3.47% of GNI per capita**, above the regional affordability
  threshold; almost half of SMEs facing digital-adoption barriers. Five digital priorities:
  clarify MICT/regulator mandates; **operationalise the Cybersecurity Agency and Data Protection
  Office**; reform **[[eptc]]** (restructuring plus open access, rural broadband PPPs); build DPI
  (biometric digital ID for eKYC and G2P, interoperability standards across health, education and
  social protection); a National Digital Skills Action Plan; and digital entrepreneurship.
  **[[satu-kahkonen]]**: "Realizing this will require bold reforms... including the restructuring
  of Eswatini Posts and Telecommunications Corporation." Sources:
  [[2025-07-31 Eswatini's Digital Transformation Crucial to Unlocking Growth, Jobs, and Economic Resilience]]
  (the datelined primary),
  [[2025-07-31 Eswatini Economic Update - Digital Transformation Key to Unlocking Growth]],
  [[2025-08-05 Robust DPI inevitable for Eswatini's economic, jobs boom - World Bank]] — which adds
  that **over 90% of P2B payments remain cash-based** and that the **GIYH App launched in May 2025
  met data-privacy criticism** (see GAP-SWZ-002). See [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.id]], [[infra.connect]].
- **2025-05-12** — **Youth employment project brings a digital social registry.** The World Bank
  board approved the **[[eswatini-youth-employment-opportunities-project]]** — Eswatini's first
  IDA-financed project after transition to IDA/IBRD blended status. Component 2 establishes a
  **"dynamic and digital Social Registry"** for identifying and targeting vulnerable youth, framed
  as a tool for broader social protection. Targets ~**30,000** young people, at least 50% female.
  Context: only **1,000 new jobs created annually against 25,000 labour-market entrants**; over
  **53% of youth NEET**; young women's unemployment **52.4%** vs **45%** for men (as of
  2025-05-12). Source:
  [[2025-05-12 New World Bank Project Supports Job Creation for Eswatini's Youth]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]].
- **2025-02-13** — **ASCENT Eswatini: rural electrification.** [[ascent-eswatini]] launched,
  targeting the remaining **12%** of the population without electricity and universal access by
  2030: **$39m** concessional IDA + **$51m** IBRD + **$10m** performance-based grant from the
  Livable Planet Fund. Aims to reach **200,000 people** via **50,000 new household connections**
  over five years — a 20% increase on the 249,014 households connected in 2023. Source:
  [[2025-02-13 World Bank Expands Support to Electrify Rural Eswatini, Reaching 200,000 People]].
  See [[infra.energy]], [[digital.rural]].
- **2025-01-22** — **Google partnership announced.** ICT Minister **[[savannah-maziya]]** announced
  a partnership integrating Google tools into **[[government-in-your-hand]]**, focused on youth
  digital competencies, public-service accessibility and entrepreneurship
  ([[eswatini-google-partnership]]). *No MoU value, term or scope disclosed; the deal rests on a
  single secondary outlet, with no Google or Government of Eswatini primary held (GAP-SWZ-001).*
  Source:
  [[2025-01-22 Eswatini partners with Google to drive digital transformation and economic growth]].
  See [[finance.mou]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **2024-12-11** — **Eswatini Payment Switch Fast Payments goes live.** Governor **[[phil-mnisi]]**
  launched Fast Payments at Ezulwini — phase one of three (Open Banking, then POS and ATM
  switching to follow). **Three of eight** integrating participants were live at launch: Eswatini
  Bank, Swaziland Building Society and E-mali Limited. Participation is **mandated** for all
  payment service providers offering local payments. Limits: max **E50,000** per bank-account
  transaction, **E10,000** per digital-wallet transaction. Implementation service provider
  **[[paylogic]]**; technical assistance from the World Bank. *(The BIS published the speech
  2025-01-14; the launch itself is 2024-12-11.)* Source:
  [[2025-01-14 Phil Mnisi - Eswatini Payment Switch Fast Payments product launch]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.exchange]].
- **2024-11 to 2024-12** — **Digital Readiness Assessment published.** The
  [[eswatini-digital-readiness-assessment]] — MICT with [[undp]] and **[[eseparc]]**, one of the
  first African implementations of the UNDP DRA — scored Eswatini **2.6 out of 5**, the
  "systematic" stage. Strengths in people, economy and government; **DPI, connectivity and
  regulation lag**, with duplicated processes and **no interoperability framework** the key DPI
  limitation. At least **64% of stakeholders acknowledged the strategy's implementation, yet half
  perceived it as delayed** — weak design, insufficient political backing, limited institutional
  coordination. Digital exclusion of elderly, disabled and rural populations flagged. Sources:
  [[2024-12-05 Eswatini Advances Toward Digital Transformation - Key Findings from the Digital Readiness Assessment]],
  [[2024-12-10 Unveiling Eswatini's Digital Path]],
  [[2024-11-22 Eswatini unveils readiness report for digital ID, transformation push]]. See
  [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[include.divides]].
  *The release sequence is contested — validation workshop 13 Nov 2024, media unveiling ~22 Nov,
  and a handover dated 10 Dec by UNDP but reported 5 Dec by ESEPARC. See
  `reviews/contradictions/open/eswatini-dra-handover-date`.*
- **2024-07-22** — **Rights critique: legislating at speed.** APC's Melusi Matsenjwa records that
  **all four 2022 ICT Acts were tabled with certificates of urgency**, shortening stakeholder and
  public input. SIM registration under Legal Notice No. 26 of 2016 collects names, addresses,
  identity numbers and **facial images**, retained up to **five years** after contract
  termination. Biometric voter registration has run since **2013**. The National ID card
  (initiated 2003, held centrally by the **[[ministry-of-home-affairs-eswatini]]**) "does not yet
  meet the criteria for digital identification", and government "has not started rolling out
  digital identity in earnest" (as of 2024-07-22), with initiatives conceptualised via
  **[[rstp]]**. Source:
  [[2024-07-22 Risk of human rights infringement as Eswatini rushes to regulate the ICT sector]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.legislate]], [[sovereignty]].
  *Historical baseline — must not out-rank the 2025–26 material on current DPI state.*
- **2024-03-11** — **Data-protection regime and the shutdown record.** APC's Ndimphiwe Shabangu
  reports **[[esccom]]** may impose administrative fines up to **five million Emalangeni** under
  s.6(3)(b) of the [[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]], and flags **s.36** as a human-rights
  concern (grounds for non-action on filed complaints). Records that during the **June–July 2021**
  political upheaval government directed [[eptc]], **[[mtn-eswatini]]** and Eswatini Mobile to
  **suspend internet services** — the International Commission of Jurists wrote to MTN South
  Africa's CEO urging resumption — and that social-media access was restricted during October 2021
  protests. Source:
  [[2024-03-11 Eswatini strives for digital sovereignty amid technological advancements]]. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[include.access]], [[sovereignty]].

## Active topics

- [[gov.protect]] · [[gov.legislate]] — the [[data-protection-act-2022-eswatini]] (Act No. 5 of
  2022, effective **4 March 2022**), with **[[esccom]]** designated National Data Protection
  Agency. Fine ceiling **E5,000,000 or 2% of annual turnover**. Cross-border transfers permitted
  within [[sadc]] under conditions; adequacy required beyond it. Sits alongside the
  [[computer-crime-and-cyber-crime-act-2022-eswatini]] and the 2022 electronic-communications
  statutes. The World Bank notes the **Data Protection Office is not yet operationalised**
  (2025-07-31) — the law is in force; the institution is not fully stood up.
- [[dpi.id]] · [[dpi.registry]] — no digital ID rolled out in earnest as of mid-2024. The
  **national population register** is identified by the World Bank as the foundation robust DPI
  requires. ID ownership was **88% of adults**, against a **78%** average for eligible individuals
  over 15 (both 2021–2022). ID-card production was suspended (Sep 2025) then resumed (Feb 2026); rights flashpoints (school-gate biometrics, a Home Affairs data breach, compulsory-DNA-for-birth-certs). Full case: [[eswatini--dpi-id]].
- [[dpi.pay]] — [[eswatini-payment-switch]] Fast Payments live since 2024-12-11, under the National
  Payment Systems Strategy 2022–2025 ([[central-bank-of-eswatini]]); **over 90% of P2B payments
  still cash-based (2025-08-05)**; national e-KYC, National FinTech Strategy 2025-2030, SIIPS entrant.
  Full case: [[eswatini--dpi-pay]].
- [[dpi.govtech]] — [[government-in-your-hand]] (GIYH+TaxPal launched May 2025) and [[digital-eswatini-project]]
  (P508948, E1.2bn/US$65m, Change-Agent training, 2027 rollout); the Google-MoU cabinet controversy; MTN
  sector apps (Umelusi/TruMD/Content Hub). Full case: [[eswatini--dpi-govtech]].
- [[dpi.mis]] · [[digital.rural]] — [[eswatini-cbhis]] / [[eswatini-cmis]] under the
  [[eswatini-nhssp]].
- [[infra.connect]] · [[include.divides]] — **57.6% internet penetration (Jan 2025)**; **1.67m
  mobile connections = 134% of population**, 94.1% of them broadband; **74.9% rural (Jan 2025)**.
  *A World Bank feature gives ~**77% rural** as of 2026-02-23 — ~2.1pp apart, across different
  dates and near-certainly different definitions (UN/World-Bank urban-agglomeration vs Kepios).
  Both recorded dated rather than reconciled.*
  Fibre rollout (47→72 Gbps), Mbabane smart city (5G/59 AI cameras), Paratus Starlink JV + government Starlink gateway (landlocked resilience). Full case: [[eswatini--infra-connect]].
- [[infra.store]] — [[eswatini-national-data-centre]], operated by [[rstp]]; UAE (Global South Utilities) green-energy talks for the data centre (2026-02-06). RSTP hosting concentration flagged as a structural risk (Digital Landscape Assessment, [[eswatini--gov-protect]]).
- [[tech.ai]] · [[capacity.training]] — 300k-coder target, ESCCOM deepfake guidance, human-centred-AI/4IR posture. Full case: [[eswatini--tech-ai]].
- [[infra.cybersec]] — Cybersecurity Agency **not yet operationalised** (2025-07-31).
- [[gov.policy]] — [[eswatini-digitalisation-strategy-2024-2028]]; a national **data policy** was
  still being **procured** as of 2025-07-25 ([[smart-africa]] RFP, budget US$70,000, closing
  25 Aug 2025).
