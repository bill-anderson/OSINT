# Mauritius (MUS)

## Recent developments

- **2026-05-18** — **Emerging as an African cross-border payments hub.** Fintechs are using
  the island as a treasury and billing gateway into fragmented African markets. **[[peach-payments]]**
  reports its Mauritius entity grew transaction volume **>300% YoY** (2024→2025) and merchant
  count **+42%**, now serving **150+ companies**; Peach operates directly in 11 African
  markets (15 via partnerships) and is integrating Oracle/Shopify/WooCommerce and preparing
  payout services (pending approval). Mauritius' **exit from the FATF grey list** is credited
  with strengthening international confidence in the jurisdiction. Source:
  [[2026-05-18 Mauritius emerges as an African payments hub]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]],
  [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-05-16** — **Strong data-protection enforcement.** At the ID4Africa 2026 AGM,
  Mauritius data-protection commissioner Drudeisha Madhub said her office issues **~3,000
  enforcement actions a year**, with **99% resolved amicably** (avoiding prosecution);
  Mauritius mandates chief data officers and is amending its law to add administrative fines
  (faster than court). She noted weak enforcement elsewhere drives data-localisation laws.
  Source: [[2026-05-16 World Bank, African DPAs outline formula for trusted digital identity, DPI]].
  See [[gov.protect]].
