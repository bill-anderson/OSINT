# Mali (MLI)

A junta-led Sahelian state pursuing **digital sovereignty** as a political project, tightly bound to the
**[[alliance-of-sahel-states|Confederation of Sahel States (AES)]]** after quitting ECOWAS. The through-line
is **local control of critical data, identity and strategic assets**: a Tier III **National Data Centre**, an
**E-AES biometric passport** ([[emptech|Emptech]] replacing [[idemia|Idemia]]), locally-built e-government
platforms, a newly institutionalised cyber stack (**[[anssi-mali|ANSSI]]** + strategy + cybercrime chamber),
an actively-enforcing data-protection authority (**[[apdp-mali|APDP]]**), and — as economic sovereignty — the
**state's reclaimed majority of [[sotelma|SOTELMA]]**. All set against low connectivity (**internet
penetration 35.1% (2026)**) and a below-average e-government score.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-09** — **First continental AI-governance commitment (Niyel framework).** Mali joined **six
  Francophone West African states** (with Benin, Burkina Faso, Côte d'Ivoire, Guinea, Senegal) adopting shared
  AI-governance guidelines led by advocacy org **[[niyel]]**, announced at the UN's inaugural Global AI Dialogue
  on Governance — ethical/inclusive AI, stronger data protection, skills, bias mitigation and digital
  sovereignty, complementing national strategies. Source:
  [[2026-07-09-wearetech-six-francophone-ai-governance-framework]]. See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-07-08** — **National digital health portal (sante.gov.ml).** The Ministry of Health launched a national
  digital health platform centralising public-health activities and services (facility geolocation; future
  patient-record tracking), under the **National Digital Health Strategic Plan (PSNSN) 2024–2028** — flagging
  power gaps, urban-rural divide and weak interoperability as obstacles. Source:
  [[2026-07-08-techgriot-mali-national-digital-health-portal]] *(cites We Are Tech as primary)*. See
  [[dpi.mis]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-06-25** — **State reclaims SOTELMA majority ($277m).** Mali raised its stake in **[[sotelma|SOTELMA]]**
  (Moov Africa Malitel) from **49% to 56%**, cutting **[[maroc-telecom]]** to a minority via a **160bn FCFA
  (~$277m)** deal that also renewed the licence (reportedly to 2039) — **17 years after** the 2009
  privatisation. Framed as a win for **economic sovereignty**, mirroring AES asset-reclaim in mining/energy.
  Deal: [[sotelma-state-majority-2026]]. Source:
  [[2026-06-25-businessinsider-mali-regains-sotelma-majority-stake]]. See [[tech.industry]], [[gov.policy]],
  [[sovereignty]].
- **2026-06-15** — **Moov doubles fibre + launches AI assistant MIA.** Moov Africa Malitel ([[sotelma|SOTELMA]]'s
  brand) doubled fibre-plan speeds at no extra cost (up to 100 Mbps across six cities) and launched **MIA** (Moov
  Intelligence Artificielle) — intensifying broadband competition with **[[orange-mali]]** (Mali had 8.91m
  internet users, 35.1% penetration, early 2026). Source:
  [[2026-06-15-ecofin-moov-africa-doubles-fibre-ai-assistant-mia]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2026-06-10** — **ANSSI cybersecurity agency created.** The Council of Ministers adopted texts creating the
  **[[anssi-mali|Agence nationale de la Sécurité des Systèmes d'Information (ANSSI)]]** to implement the National
  Cybersecurity Strategy 2026–2030 — the institutional capstone of Mali's cyber build-out. Source:
  [[2026-07-01-maliactuel-anssi-agence-cybersecurite-creee]]. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2026-06-01** — **CIB-AES cross-border diaspora enrolment, Bamako.** Burkina Faso ran a pilot to biometrically
  enrol **Burkinabè residents in Mali** with mobile kits, producing the **[[cib-aes|CIB-AES]]** and AES passport
  near-instantly (data relayed to platforms in Burkina Faso) — the first cross-border AES ID enrolment, **hosted
  on Malian soil**. (Mali was never a WURI participant, so the AES ID is its primary foundational-ID track.)
  Source: [[2026-06-02-afriktimes-aes-cib-aes-diaspora-pilot-bamako]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-05-21** — **Cybercrime criminal chamber operational.** The criminal chamber of the **National Pole for
  the fight against Cybercrime** began hearing cases (4 cases / 8 accused — insults, threats, undermining state
  security via IT systems, ICT-enabled fraud) — operational cyber-justice alongside the strategy and ANSSI.
  Source: [[2026-05-21-lessor-cybercrime-chamber-operational]]. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2026-04-09** — **Moov Money–Mastercard prepaid card (GITEX Africa).** Moov Money Mali launched a co-branded
  prepaid Mastercard linked to the mobile wallet, targeting the unbanked (online/international payments, ATM
  withdrawals, no bank account) — the second telco-led card after Orange Money's, within BCEAO/WAEMU
  interoperability efforts. Source:
  [[2026-04-09-le360-moov-money-mali-carte-prepayee-mastercard-gitex]]. See [[dpi.pay]].
- **2026-03-03** — **Smart Africa cooperation at MWC 2026.** Mali's digital minister Alhamdou Ag Ilyène met
  **[[smart-africa]]** CEO Lacina Koné (Barcelona) — skills development, cross-border fibre interconnection, and
  Mali possibly hosting the next Transform Africa Summit — balancing AES sovereignty with continental
  cooperation. Source: [[2026-03-10-techafricanews-mali-smart-africa-mwc2026]]. See [[gov.regional]],
  [[capacity.training]].
- **2026-02-10** — **Justice e-services online (casier judiciaire + nationalité).** During the Week of Justice,
  Mali launched online **criminal-record** and **nationality-certificate** platforms — citizens apply with their
  **NINA** and civil-status details, documents returned by email (accessible to diaspora and remote areas) —
  built by **[[agetic|AGETIC]]** with an **E-verif** authenticity tool against document forgery. Source:
  [[2026-02-12-maliweb-justice-casier-nationalite-en-ligne]]. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-01-31** — **Tier III National Data Centre inaugurated.** During Mali's third annual Digital Week
  (Bamako), the Ministry of Communication, Digital Economy and Administration Modernisation (minister
  Alhamdou Ag Ilyène) launched a **Tier III "National Data Centre"** (99.982% availability) with AES
  digital-economy ministers present. It hosts the state's strategic data, e-administration and public digital
  services, will carry **"mutualised" [[alliance-of-sahel-states|AES]] infrastructure** (Niger, Burkina Faso),
  and is framed as ending dependence on foreign/extraterritorial cloud; a gradual repatriation of
  externally-hosted data is planned. Sources:
  [[2026-02-02 Mali unveils modern Tier III data centre]],
  [[2026-02-02 Mali opens Tier III data center in Bamako amid regional push to keep data at home]],
  [[2026-02-03 Mali Paves the Way for Digital Sovereign Tier III Data Centre Deployment]],
  [[2026-02-05 Government of Mali launches data center in Bamako]]. See [[infra.store]], [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-12-30** — **AGETIC government Intranet + .ml domain.** [[agetic|AGETIC]] is interconnecting all public
  and para-public services into a private, secured **government Intranet** (13 services integrated), moving a
  draft decree to make government messaging mandatory, and managing the **.ml** ccTLD (**13,441 domain names**,
  30 registrars) — a sovereign gov-network build-out. Source:
  [[2025-12-30-lessor-agetic-intranet-point-ml]]. See [[dpi.govtech]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[infra.connect]].
- **2025-12-09** — **National Cybersecurity Strategy 2026–2030 adopted.** The Council of Ministers took note of
  Mali's first coordinated cyber framework (ITU GCI Tier 4/5, "basic"), citing the Aug-2022 breach of 312,000
  taxpayers at the tax directorate (attributed to "Russian cybercriminals" — ISSUE-014,
  no Russia slug in taxonomy) and a Feb-2023 Bank of Africa Mali attack. Source:
  [[2025-12-09-wearetech-mali-strategie-cybersecurite-2026-2030]]. See [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2025-12-04** — **APDP regional antenna in Ségou.** Mali's data-protection authority **[[apdp-mali|APDP]]**
  (Law 2013-015) opened its first regional antenna — DPA territorial expansion, compliance guidance and
  awareness. Source: [[2025-12-05-lessor-apdp-segou-antenne]]. See [[gov.protect]].
- **2025-11-17** — **IFC €80m social loan to Orange Mali.** **[[ifc]]** and co-investors (BOAD; IDA PSW) lent
  **€80m (~$92.6m)** to **[[orange-mali]]** for **300 4G towers** (half rural), fibre to **~300,000
  households**, a **70%-by-2032** women's digital-training target, and diesel-to-solar conversion — the biggest
  connectivity-finance item. Deal: [[ifc-orange-mali-loan]]. Source:
  [[2025-11-17-ifc-orange-mali-80m-loan-4g-fibre]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[finance.new]], [[include.divides]],
  [[infra.energy]].
- **2025-10-28** — **"E-AES" biometric passport; Emptech replaces Idemia.** Mali launched an ICAO-compliant
  biometric passport (chip with fingerprints + photo), switching supplier from France's **[[idemia]]** to
  China's **[[emptech]]** — a change officials cast as "regained sovereignty" and local data management,
  inscribed "E-AES" for confederal integration. Source:
  [[2025-10-28 Mali Launches 'E-AES' Biometric Passport, Taps China's Emptech in Digital Sovereignty Move]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-10-06** — **APDP: 2,000-file review + lease-declaration decree + enforcement.** The **[[apdp-mali|APDP]]**
  opened a session to review ~2,000 declaratory files (incl. a draft decree for **mandatory lease declaration**)
  and, in 2025, fined a Bamako clinic **5m FCFA** and notified 100+ firms — establishing an **actively-enforcing
  DPA** and a lease-registry workstream (relevant to GAP-003). Source:
  [[2025-10-07-apanews-apdp-examine-2000-dossiers-baux]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.registry]].
- **2025-09-16** — **Locally-built e-government platforms.** Mali presented three domestically-developed
  platforms: **Sigui-Dolo** (biometric driving licences / vehicle registration, linked to TrésorPay and
  SYDONIA customs), **OpenClinic** (integrated health information system, to link to the **NINA** national ID
  and mandatory health insurance), and **SIGIP** (territorial-administration data management). Birth
  registration ~90%. Sources:
  [[2025-09-17 Mali rolls out new digital tools to modernize public administration]],
  [[2025-09-26 Mali introduces new platforms to enhance digital public service access]],
  [[2025-09-27 Mali launches new digital platforms to improve access to public services]]. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.id]], [[include.access]].
- **2025-06-05** — **Orange Money–Orabank Mastercard prepaid card.** **[[orange-mali]]** and Orabank Mali
  launched a co-branded prepaid Mastercard (virtual/physical, linked to the Orange Money wallet via Max it),
  giving the unbanked national and international card rails (caps 2m FCFA/transaction, 10m FCFA/month) — the
  first of two telco-led cards. Source:
  [[2025-06-11-maliweb-orange-money-orabank-mastercard-prepaid]]. See [[dpi.pay]].
- **2025-06-03** — **Birth-registration reform.** Mali's birth-registration rate stands at **89.5%**;
  reforms include creating the **National Agency for the Security of Civil Status Documents (ANSAEC)** in
  2025, free declarations/certificates, and 747 functional primary registration centres. Death registration
  ~20%. Source: [[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]].
- **2025-05-29** — **Cross-border remittances into Wave.** **TerraPay** partnered with **Wave Mobile Money**
  (with Orabank Mali) to route remittances from the US, Canada and Europe directly into Wave wallets in Mali
  through a single integration. Source:
  [[2025-05-29 TerraPay enables cross-border remittances for Wave Mobile Money users in Mali]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-05-07** — **Orange Mali + Intelsat 4G-over-satellite.** **[[orange-mali]]** selected **[[intelsat]]**
  to bring 3G/4G to hard-to-reach regions by satellite — "a first in Francophone West Africa" — reaching
  ~360,000 remote residents (per a Nov-2025 account), directly addressing the rural connectivity gap. Source:
  [[2025-05-07-techafricanews-orange-mali-intelsat-4g-satellite]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[data.satellite]],
  [[include.divides]].
- **2025-03-18** — **TrésorPay + SIF/GUF one-stop land office.** PM Abdoulaye Maïga launched **TrésorPay**
  (mobile-money payment of ID/passport fees and fines, built by CAISFF with the Treasury) and the **SIF/GUF**
  Land Information System / one-stop land office; Mali rose 13 places to **141st/193 on the 2024 UN EGDI**.
  Source: [[2025-03-20-wearetech-tresorpay-guichet-unique-foncier]]. See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.registry]],
  [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-03-03** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2025.** Population 24.8m; **internet penetration
  35.1%**; 23.4m mobile connections (94.2%). Mali scored **0.3005 on the 2024 UN EGDI** (below the African
  average 0.4247). Sources: [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - Mali (DataReportal)]],
  [[2025-09-17 Mali rolls out new digital tools to modernize public administration]]. See [[data.statistics]].
- **2025-02-18** — **Diaspora services platform.** Mali unveiled a platform for its diaspora to access
  administrative, identity and travel documents across consular missions — built by Malian experts,
  future-compatible with the AES **Visa Liptako**; Mali rose 13 places to 141st on the UN EGDI. Source:
  [[2025-02-21 Mali Launches Digital Platform to Streamline Services for Its Diaspora]]. See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-01-29** — **AES common biometric passport.** Mali, Burkina Faso and Niger launched a **common AES
  biometric passport**, coinciding with their definitive ECOWAS exit (old ECOWAS-logo passports valid until
  expiry) — a setback for the World Bank-supported WURI regional-ID project. Source:
  [[2025-01-27 Sahel states under military rule unveil common biometric passport Jan. 29]]. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[gov.regional]].
- **Reference (2007 / 2023).** World Bank baselines: an infoDev **ICT-in-Education Mali** report (2007) and
  the **Digital Economy for Mali Country Diagnostic** (2023). Cited, not absorbed. Sources:
  [[2007-06-01 Survey of ICT and Education in Africa - Mali Country Report (infoDev, World Bank)]],
