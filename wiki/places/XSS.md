# Sub-Saharan Africa (XSS)

Region defined by rule as **all `XAF` children except `XNA`** (North Africa). A
**tag-only** grouping: applied to genuinely pan-SSA items, never any country's
home region. Roll-up by walking parents.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-01** — **AFGHS health-data sovereignty across SSA.** Fourteen sub-Saharan states
  (Kenya, Uganda, Rwanda, Liberia, Cameroon, Eswatini, Lesotho, Mozambique, Nigeria, Côte
  d'Ivoire, Botswana, Madagascar, Sierra Leone, Ethiopia) signed
  **[[america-first-global-health-strategy]]** bilateral MOUs (~**$16bn**, five-year 2026–2030).
  A model **[[specimen-sharing-agreement]]** obliges signatories to share specimen/genetic-sequence
  data within **5 days** of a US request, onward to up to 10 non-US entities, for **25 years**,
  plus US access to national digital-health infrastructure and no reciprocity — only Kenya's text
  is public (a Kenyan court suspended part on data-privacy grounds). **[[africa-cdc]]**'s Kaseya:
  "we want to own our data." KFF maintains a standing MOU tracker/dataset
  ([[kff-afghs-mou-tracker]]). Sources:
  [[2026-07-01 KFF Tracker America First MOU Bilateral Global Health Agreements]],
  [[2026-01-01 America First, Africa Last  Data Extraction from Africa's Bodies and the Erosion of Health Sovereignty]],
  [[2025-11-21 First look Full Text of America First Global Health Strategy Specimen Sharing Agreement]].
  See [[gov.protect]], [[geopol.usa]], [[dpi.mis]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2026-06-18** — **GSMA Mobile Economy Africa 2026:** mobile contributed
  **$240bn (7.8% of GDP)** and ~13m jobs in 2025; the binding constraint has
  shifted from **coverage (9% gap)** to **usage (63% gap)**; **$76bn** network
  investment due 2025–2030. Source:
  [[2026-06-18 Africa's $240 Billion Mobile Economy A Checkpoint on Africa's Digital Transformation Journey]].
  See [[include.divides]], [[gsma]].
- **2026-07-01** — **[[ifc]]** lent **$150m** to **[[airtel-africa]]** to expand
  networks across its 14-country SSA footprint. Source:
  [[2026-07-01 IFC and Airtel Africa Expand Digital Connectivity to Strengthen Jobs and Inclusion]].
  See [[infra.connect]], [[include.access]].
- **2026-06 (Ookla Q1 2026)** — **[[starlink]]** outperforms most terrestrial ISPs
  on download across SSA; >500,000 subscribers end-2025; PoPs cut latency >80%.
  Source: [[2026-06-15 Starlink drives African broadband speeds higher]].
- **2026-06** — **[[mtn-group]]** Ambition 2030 spans its 19 SSA markets (313m
  subscribers); the usage gap and African-language AI are the frontier. Sources:
  [[2026-06-10 MTN Group goes all-in on platforms and AI]],
  [[2026-04-09 Ralph Mupita on MTN's New Frontier Connectivity, Content, and African AI]].
- **2026-03-06** — **[[cassava-technologies]] launches a [[national-sovereign-cloud]] for African
  governments** (MWC Barcelona) — locally-governed cloud + cyber-security + AI compute + local-
  language models, pitched to cut exposure to the **US CLOUD Act**; paired with a Cloud Partner
  Programme reselling NVIDIA Cloud Partner solutions and its AI Multi-Model Exchange (Anthropic
  Claude + Google Gemini). Source:
  [[2026-03-06 Cassava launches sovereign cloud for Africa's public sector]]. See [[infra.store]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[tech.ai]], [[gov.standards]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **2026-02-04** — **Google launches [[waxal]], an open-source African-language voice dataset** —
  **11,000+ hours** / ~2m audio files across **21 languages**, free on Hugging Face; built over
  three years with Makerere, the University of Ghana (13 languages), Rwanda's Digital Umuganda (5)
  and AIMS, with African partners retaining corpus ownership. Aimed at voice AI for low-literacy
  users in health/education/agriculture. Source:
  [[2026-02-04 Google launches WAXAL, an open-source voice dataset for African languages]]. See
  [[tech.ai]], [[data.open]], [[capacity.research]], [[include.access]].
- **2026** — **[[digital-investment-facility]] (DIF).** An EU/Germany/Finland-funded facility
  (implemented by GIZ + HAUS, under Global Gateway/D4D Hub) that de-risks **data-centre and IXP**
  projects across Sub-Saharan Africa (min **€5m**, revenue-generating, ESG-screened), taking them
  from early design to contract close and publishing regional DC market briefs. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Data Governance in Africa - Digital Investment Facility]]. See [[infra.store]],
  [[infra.energy]], [[finance.new]], [[infra.connect]].
- **2026-01-22** — **Gates + OpenAI commit $50m to health AI (Horizon1000).** The
  **[[gates-foundation]]** and **[[openai]]** announced **[[horizon1000]]**, a $50m pilot to
  integrate AI into African national health systems — starting in Rwanda ([[RWA]]) and
  targeting **1,000 primary-healthcare clinics by 2028** against an SSA health-workforce
  shortage estimated at ~5.6m; endorsed by Rwanda's Paula Ingabire. Source:
  [[2026-01-22 Gates Foundation and OpenAI Commit $50m to AI-Powered Health Systems in Africa]].
  See [[tech.ai]], [[dpi.mis]], [[finance.new]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **2026-01-22** — **RIA After Access: digital inequalities in platform work.** A Research ICT
  Africa ([[research-ict-africa]]) After Access report ([[after-access]]) on platform/gig work
  across Sub-Saharan Africa argues platform work **layers onto — rather than dissolves —
  existing structural inequalities**. Source:
  [[2026-01-22 After Access 2022-2023 Digital inequalities in platform work]]. See
  [[include.divides]], [[include.access]], [[capacity.training]].

- **2025-12 → 2026-04 (Russell Southwood)** — **SSA MNO state-of-market and ICT4D
  long view.** Southwood segments SSA operators as **data revenue overtakes voice**:
  four "power players" ([[mtn-group]], [[airtel-africa]], [[orange]], [[vodacom]])
  turning into data/holding companies, a "second division" ([[axian-telecom]],
  [[econet-wireless]]) and stranded former incumbents; and roots today's two-speed
  markets in the 2000s ICT4D wave (>$35bn MNO investment 2000–2007; SAT3/WACS
  open-access fights; Seacom–TENET) whose network effects re-created monopolies.
  See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.connect]]. Sources:
  [[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]],
  [[2026-04-16 The Long View - What ICT4D did for Africa and why that deep past matters for today]].
