# Chad (TCD)

Central African ([[XCA]]) state starting near the bottom of the continental tables —
**189th of 193 on the UN E-Government Development Index, score 0.1785 (2024 index)** against an
African average of 0.4247 and a global average of 0.6382, and **Tier 4** (second-lowest) of the
ITU Global Cybersecurity Index at **48.67/100 (2024)** — and attempting to buy its way up fast
through **[[tchad-connexion-2030]]**, a **US$1.5 billion** national digital investment umbrella.

Two things make Chad analytically interesting here. First, the **sovereignty framing is explicit
and ministerial**, not imputed: *"a country that does not control its data does not control its
destiny"* (Boukar Michel, 2025-12-10). Second, Chad is **courting every bloc at once** — the US,
China via Huawei, the EU, Azerbaijan, the UAE, India, Kenya, Cameroon and CEMAC neighbours — with
few signed instruments to show for it. Most of what the wiki holds for Chad is announcements,
talks and plans; the delivery record is thinner than the ambition, and the gap between the two is
the thing to watch.

## Recent developments

- **2026-03** — **The biometric registry becomes the interoperability spine.** Chad's Ministry of
  Finance, Budget, Economy, Planning and International Cooperation concluded a deal with
  **[[anats]]** to enable real-time data sharing off the **[[chad-biometric-registry]]**. ANATS DG
  **Youssouf Gorou Tchaby**: "This system, designed for interoperability, is an essential lever.
  All administrations, both public and private, will be able to connect to it to verify and
  authenticate identity." Government intends the registry as the **single source of truth** for all
  identification; private entities may integrate for identity verification and credit access. Chad
  is also issuing biometric ID cards to refugees using **[[idemia]]** technology, with enrolment
  equipment from **[[unhcr]]**. *An estimated **XAF 15 billion (~US$26.1m)** invested in the
  registry "in the last few years" is relayed second-hand from Africa 24 TV quoting government
  sources — attributed, not established.* Source:
  [[2026-03-16 Chad uses national biometric registry to boost govt systems interoperability]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.registry]].
- **2026-02-13** — **Telecom build-out, and a regulator's verdict.** Minister **[[boukar-michel]]**
  announced 50 new telecom sites in N'Djamena and 50 km of FTTH via state-owned
  **[[sotel-tchad]]** — no timeline, financing or coverage areas given. The **N'Djamena–Mberé**
  fibre link is being rehabilitated to diversify international routes via Cameroon, with links to
  Niger, Nigeria, Algeria, Libya and Egypt **planned, not underway**. The
  **[[trans-saharan-fibre-backbone]]** is **60% complete (2026-02-13)**. **[[airtel-africa]]**
  plans **50bn CFA francs (US$90.45m)** through June 2026. **[[arcep-chad]]**'s 15th
  service-quality audit (Sept–Oct 2025) found improved urban signal stability alongside faulty
  equipment, inadequate maintenance, unstable power and some sites entirely offline; authorities
  reprimanded operators again in late December 2025 over recurring disruptions. Source:
  [[2026-02-13 Chad Plans 50 Telecom Sites, 50 km of Fiber in N'Djamena]]. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[infra.energy]].
- **2026-02-05** — **National Data Center enters its final pre-launch milestone.** A tripartite
  agreement ([[adetic-ansice-techso-audit-agreement]]) was signed by **[[adetic]]** (DG Adoum
  Djimet Saboun), **[[ansice]]** (Deputy GM Nadjma Saleh Kebzabo) and **[[techso-group]]**
  (co-founder Hamza Waraki) to audit and certify the **[[chad-national-data-centre]]** against
  critical-infrastructure protection standards. Phase 1 — building construction and operational
  equipment installation — is **already complete**; audit and certification are described as the
  last step before inauguration. The agreement also covers **100 micro-data centres** across
  government institutions, interconnected with the National Data Center. Saboun: "The National Data
  Center is a strategic base for sovereign housing of state data and continuity of public digital
  services." Attributed to President Mahamat Idriss Déby Itno. Sources:
  [[2026-02-06 ADETIC, ANSICE, and TECHSO-GROUP Join Forces to Protect Chad's Digital Infrastructure]],
  [[2026-02-06 Chad Moves to Secure National Data Center Ahead of Launch]]. See [[infra.store]],
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[sovereignty]].
- **2025-12-20** — **PMICE Phase I commences: €175.1m.** The Ministry announced commencement of
  **[[pmice]]** (State Connectivity Infrastructure Modernization Project) — **€175.1 million
  (US$204.7m)** covering the National Data Center, **1,200 km** of fibre and nationwide GSM/3G/4G
  expansion; inauguration ceremony held in **Sarh, Moyen-Chari**, December 2025. Michel: "Digital
  must not stop at capitals, it must irrigate territories, connect regions, and unite the nation,"
  and "the sovereignty of a modern state is also measured by its ability to master its digital
  infrastructures." *PMICE was originally costed at **$180 million**, financed by an **Eximbank
  whose country of origin the source does not state** (GAP-TCD-003), per a 2023 World Bank
  document — a superseded estimate, recorded dated, not a contradiction.* Source:
  [[2026-01-06 Chad invests 175m in national data center and networks]]. See [[infra.store]],
  [[finance.new]], [[digital.rural]].
- **2025-12-10** — **A digital law in finalisation.** Minister **[[boukar-michel]]** announced
  (9 Dec 2025, at the third Chadian School of Internet Governance) that Chad was **finalising** —
  not enacting — a digital law incorporating international standards on cybersecurity, data
  protection and internet governance. On **22 October 2025** the National Assembly approved an
  ordinance amending the **2014 law** on electronic communications and postal services. *No text or
  enactment date is held for either (GAP-TCD-002).* Source:
  [[2025-12-10 Chad Finalizes Digital Law to Align National Framework With Global Standards]]. See
  [[gov.legislate]], [[gov.protect]], [[sovereignty]].
- **2025-12-24** — **Huawei talks.** Michel met a **[[huawei]]** North Africa delegation led by VP
  Jason Ye on telecoms infrastructure, connected services and administration digitalisation, and
  explored converting the talks into projects aligned with [[tchad-connexion-2030]] — **no
  agreement signed, no figures, no timeline**. Source:
  [[2025-12-29 Chad Steps Up Rollout of Tchad Connexion 2030 in Talks With Huawei]]. See
  [[geopol.china]], [[infra.connect]].
- **2025-12-02** — **Courting everyone.** Chad negotiated a digital partnership with **Azerbaijan**
  (national data centre, infrastructure modernisation, e-government), agreeing to formalise it by
  MoU with **no timeline** (GAP-TCD-004). On 26 Nov 2025 Michel met US chargé d'affaires William
  Flens to attract US technology companies. On 25–26 Nov Chad joined the drafting of foundational
  texts for **[[cadnac]]**, a new Central African digital-development conference framed around
  pooled expertise and **regional digital sovereignty**; and at the Cotonou regional summit
  (17–18 Nov) Michel advocated Central–West Africa fibre interconnection and digital corridors.
  Since January 2025 Chad has expanded collaboration with the [[itu]], Guinea-Bissau, the UAE,
  Greece, Cameroon, the [[undp]], Kenya, **[[cemac]]** member states (free roaming), India and
  Canada. Source:
  [[2025-12-02 Chad Accelerates Digital Cooperation to Support Its Transformation Agenda]]. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[finance.mou]], [[XCA]].
- **2025-09-09** — **Transport services to be digitised.** A meeting chaired by Transport Minister
  Fatima Goukouni Weddeye ([[ministry-of-transport-chad]]) prepared an integrated digital platform
  centralising vehicle registration cards, driver's licences, technical inspection certificates,
  transport licences and the mandatory consignment note (LVO), targeting traceability and reduced
  fraud. **Planning stage: no contract, vendor, budget or timeline named.** Source:
  [[2025-09-12 Chad to deploy integrated digital platform for transport services]]. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.registry]].
- **2025-06-03** — **Civil registration through the health system, and a colonial inheritance
  named.** At the [[id4africa]] 2025 AGM, ANATS's Coordinator of Cooperation and Civil Registration
  **Brahim Ali Seid** reported that since integrating civil registration with health facilities in
  **2023**, more than **120,000 children** have been registered via hospital-based registry
  offices, community health workers covering about **70% of Chad's rural population**; school
  campaigns have registered **180,000+** pupils since 2023, against Seid's statement that **"over
  80 percent of children in primary and secondary schools lack birth certificates."** Data moves by
  **SMS** to ANATS for validation and return; mobile phones running the ANATS application enrol
  nomadic children. Plans: **97 → 600** digitised hospital registration points; **zero unregistered
  births by 2030**. Seid: *"We inherited a colonial civil registration system governed by colonial
  laws. But progress has been made."* Source:
  [[2025-06-03 Chad, Mali, Senegal pursue birth registration reforms to close gaps]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.rural]], [[colonialism]], [[MLI]], [[SEN]].
- **2026-07-01** — Following a Chadian government mission to Washington (26–29 May 2026), the
  **US** reaffirmed support for Chad's digital transformation under its **2020–2030 National
  Digital Transformation Strategy**. Talks (State Dept, Commerce, and firms) spanned **digital
  infrastructure, satellite connectivity, cybersecurity and digital health**; goals include raising
  internet penetration **30% → 80%** and the digital economy's GDP share **1.9% → 10%**. Cybastion
  offered to help audit/certify Chad's National Datacenter; a minister flagged **US visa refusals**
  as a delivery risk. Source:
  [[2026-07-01 Chad and US Explore Partnerships on Satellite Connectivity and Cybersecurity]]. See
  [[geopol.usa]], [[infra.cybersec]].
- **2026-02-16** — **Sotel Tchad as a "stranded" former incumbent (Russell Southwood).** Chad's
  state fixed-line operator [[sotel-tchad]] — sold to Lap Green (2010) then nationalised, staff cut
  from ~10,000 to <500 — features in Southwood's former-incumbents study; recent moves include the
  SudaChad 20-year concession (EU + AfDB, 20.5bn CFA) and an **April 2025 Sotel–[[airtel-africa]]
  N'Djamena–Mberé corridor** deal. Source:
  [[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]].
  See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.connect]].

## Three financings, not one — do not sum

The wiki holds three distinct money figures for Chadian digital infrastructure. **No source states
how they relate.** They must not be added together, nor treated as versions of each other:

| Vehicle | Figure | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| [[tchad-connexion-2030]] | **US$1.5bn** | the national umbrella strategy to 2030 |
| [[pmice]] | **€175.1m / US$204.7m** | Phase I: National Data Center + 1,200 km fibre + GSM/3G/4G |
| [[chad-digital-transformation-project]] (World Bank, P180000 / PAD00070) | **US$120m** total; SDR 69.5m (**US$92.2m**) IDA grant | concept 27 Feb 2023, board 19 Dec 2023, PAD 4 Sep 2024; Ministry of Telecommunications and Digital Economy |

[[tchad-connexion-2030]] targets (fullest statement, 2026-03-16): ~$1.5bn by 2030; **at least 80%
national internet coverage**; ~**80% mobile penetration**; lifting ~**2.5 million** Chadians out of
poverty through digital-enabled services within five years. Other accounts render the horizon as
"within six years".

## Contested figures

**Internet penetration is contradicted across sources, both tracing to DataReportal:** **22.5%**
(4.18m users, January 2024) against **13.2%** at the start of 2025 — a 9.3-point fall against the
same publisher's own reported **+9.2%** user growth. Both are recorded dated and attributed;
neither is adopted. See `reviews/contradictions/open/chad-internet-penetration-2024-2025`.
Related dated figures (Jan 2024): **13.06m mobile connections = 70.4% of population**; population
**18.56m**, **75.4% rural**, median age **15.2**. Mobile *connections* are not internet *users* —
the publisher says so itself.

*Framing difference, not a contradiction:* the 2026-02-06 reporting has the data centre built and
awaiting certification, while a 2026-02-13 telecoms piece calls it "under construction" — loose
background phrasing in an off-topic article, resolved by specificity in favour of the former.
