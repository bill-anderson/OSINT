# Guinea (GIN)

Under the Doumbouya transition government, Guinea has moved from digital laggard to visible
ambition, wrapping its agenda in the **[[simandou-2040]]** development strategy (ICT as one of five
pillars, funded on projected Simandou iron-ore revenue) and staging the **[[transform-africa-summit-2025|Transform Africa Summit 2025]]**
in Conakry — the first Francophone West African host. The through-line is **digital sovereignty**: a
first **Tier III national data centre** ([[guinea-national-data-centre]]) for local hosting, sovereign
**.gn** management, a **Mojaloop-based** instant-payment system, and a **domestic MOSIP/TECH5** identity
stack after its World Bank **WURI** foundational-ID project closed early in 2024. The base is low —
26.5% internet penetration, ~23% banked, only 45-60% with birth certificates — so most of this is
early-stage build, not yet outcomes.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-16** — **Holberton Guinée officially launched in Conakry** — the software-engineering
  school's Guinean campus, a private tech-skills capacity addition.
  Source: [[2026-07-17-holberton-guinee-launch]].
- **2025–26 — biometric electoral file & free ID.** The **[[pn-ravec-guinea|PN-RAVEC]]** administrative census
  (from 15 Apr 2025) feeds the **RNPP**, now the **single official source** for a **permanent biometric electoral
  file** (**6.95m electors / 23,681 stations** for the 31 May 2026 coupled elections; **RGPH-4 population
  17,521,167**); the biometric ID card was **decreed free** (Feb 2025). See [[guinea--dpi-id]]. Sources:
  [[2025-08-28-mediaguinee-definitive-biometric-electoral-file]], [[2026-04-25-mediaguinee-fichier-electoral-7m-elections-couplees]].
- **2024–26 — telecom sovereignty & cables.** The **State took 87.5% of MTN Guinée**
  ([[guinea-mtn-nationalisation]]); with a single saturated ACE cable, **[[guilab-guinea|GUILAB]]** contracted
  a **second cable (MEDUSA Africa)** and joined a **third (Via Africa)**. See [[guinea--infra-connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-05-06-mediaguinee-medusa-africa-second-submarine-cable-cma]].
- **2026 — data-sovereignty policy & GovTech.** A **National Data Governance Strategy** (DNTIEN, Jun 2026); a
  **3% foreign-digital-services tax + Digital Sovereignty Fund** (decree 21 May 2026); nine public-admin
  projects and **TELEMO** e-procurement; the **MCENI** ministry merger (Minister **Mourana Soumah**). See
  [[guinea--dpi-govtech]]. Sources: [[2026-06-26-ecofin-guinea-national-data-governance-strategy]],
  [[2026-05-26-ecofin-guinea-3pct-tax-foreign-digital-services]].
- **2026 — fintech wave.** **[[coris-money-guinea|CORIS Money]]**, **[[ks-wallet-guinea|KS-Wallet]]**, Orange
  Money–BNG interop, Visa MoU talks, and **[[cauridor|Cauridor]]** (Guinea-founded cross-border infra, 36
  countries). See [[guinea--dpi-pay]]. Source: [[2026-02-19-techafricanews-coris-money-launch]].
- **2026-03-02** — **Biometric birth certificates in Sanoyah (civil-registry modernization).** The
  **[[oneci-guinea|ONECI]]** began issuing **biometric birth certificates** and enrolling the
  **biometric national ID card** locally in Sanoyah commune (phase 1: 2,000 certificates), on a "one
  citizen, one identity" model built on **[[mosip]]** + **[[tech5]]**. Base documents: prior birth
  certificate, court supplementary judgment, or a **RAVEC** document; private-data-centre documents
  rejected. The biometric birth certificate is the **foundational document** for the national ID.
  Source: [[2026-03-04 Guinea Launches Biometric Birth Certificates in Sanoyah in Civil Registry Modernization Push]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]].
- **2026-02-08** — **Full land-cadastre digitalization announced.** New Urbanism/Housing/Territorial
  Planning Minister Mohamed Lamine Sy Savané set the **full digitalization of the land cadastre** as a
  priority — to secure rights, fight fraud and guarantee transparency in land-title management —
  framing the ministry as "the heart of land sovereignty." Source:
  [[2026-02-08 Guinea - Urbanism Minister announces full digitalization of the land cadastre]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[digital.localgov]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-01-14** — **2025 digital-sector review; 2026 targets.** Minister **[[rose-pola-pricemou]]**
  ([[ministry-digital-economy-guinea]]) reported the national backbone **quadrupled to 200 Gbps** (from
  50), ~**12,000 km** fibre to all regions, the first Tier 3 data centre, an IXP, sovereign .gn, **39
  public-service apps**, **[[telemo|TELEMO]]** e-procurement, **[[fugas|FUGAS]]** (233bn GNF saved),
  10,000+ trained, and GIGA connecting 585 schools; telecoms hit **4.4% of GDP** and ~$220m investment,
  mobile-internet costs −25%. 2026: 600+ 'white zones', cybersecurity, local hosting. Source:
  [[2026-01-14 Guinea Reviews 2025 Digital Sector Gains, Targets Faster Rollout in 2026]]. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2025-12-22** — **Trésor Pay — digital gateway for public payments.** Guinea launched
  **[[tresor-pay]]** (22 Dec 2025) to dematerialise payment of state administrative fees via mobile,
  strengthening domestic revenue mobilisation and traceability. Source:
  [[2025-12-23 Guinea rolls out Tresor Pay as digital gateway for public payments]]. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-12-18** — **BCRG begins an instant, interoperable payment system (SPI).** The
  **[[bcrg|central bank]]** opened work on an **SPI on [[mojaloop]]** — public, open, state-controlled
  infrastructure chosen to limit technological dependence — for real-time transfers across banks,
  wallets and instruments, with future **[[papss]]**/ECOWAS integration. Context: 23% of adults banked
  (from 7% a decade ago); mobile money +8.6% to 46,795bn GNF (Q2 2024); WAEMU's BCEAO PI-SPI launched
  Sept 2025. Sources: [[2025-12-22 Guinea central bank starts work on instant payment system]],
  [[2025-12-21 Guinea readies instant payment system to speed transactions and cut cash use]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-11-30** — **FUGAS biometric payroll audit — ~$26.9m saved, ghost workers exposed.** Guinea
  put **[[fugas|FUGAS]]** into use: of ~277,000 registered civil servants only ~130,000 were confirmed
  after biometric checks, saving **233bn GNF (~$26.9m)** since 2024; PM Amadou Oury Bah pressed for a
  **unique personal ID number**. A Simandou 2040 anti-graft/digitization flagship. Sources:
  [[2025-11-30 Guinea Claims $26.9M Saved Since 2024 with Biometric Audit of Public Workforce (FUGAS)]],
  [[2025-12-03 Guinea launches biometric system to weed out 'ghost' public workers (FUGAS)]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-11-25** — **Health-information-system interoperability workshop.** Terre des Hommes with the
  Health Ministry (Enabel/Wehubit funding) ran a Conakry workshop to strengthen the **SNIS** and health
  data governance (the REC maternité electronic register piloted in 15 centres), stressing avoidance of
  tool duplication and open-source sustainability. Source:
  [[2025-11-27 Interoperability - key workshop to modernize Guinea's health information system]]. See
  [[dpi.mis]], [[dpi.exchange]].
- **2025-11-12–19** — **Transform Africa Summit 2025 (Conakry), "AI for Africa."** Guinea hosted the
  7th **[[transform-africa-summit-2025|Transform Africa Summit]]** ([[smart-africa]], 42 states;
  7,000+ participants, 14 MoUs). Guinea launched **Simandou 2040 Pillar 3** (digital → 20% of GDP) and
  **[[telemo|TELEMO]]** (Guinea-Rwanda e-procurement); Smart Africa unveiled SANIA and signed MoUs
  incl. **[[visa]]**. Guinea flagged a **new data-protection law** and the agencies **ANSSI**/**ANDE**,
  a second submarine cable landing, and interconnection with Mali/CIV/Sierra Leone. Sources:
  [[2025-11-13 Transform Africa Summit 2025 opens in Conakry, epicentre of digital cooperation in Africa]],
  [[2025-11-19 Transform Africa Summit 2025 (Conakry) - Simandou 2040 Pillar 3, TELEMO, SANIA-SADX and the closing wrap]].
  See [[gov.regional]], [[tech.ai]], [[gov.protect]].
- **2025-11-07** — **GIGA: internet to 2,200 schools; and an anti-cash payments push.** Guinea
  launched (5 Nov) a **GIGA** ([[itu]]/UNICEF) plan to connect **2,200 primary schools** by end-2026
  (~600 already equipped). Separately the **[[bcrg|BCRG]]** presented an anti-cash strategy — an
  **M-Pesa-style merchant payment system** (World Bank-supported), the national payment switch, and
  **11 licensed EMIs** (from 6 in 2024) — after a liquidity crisis (~94% of banknotes never return to
  banks). Sources: [[2025-11-07 Guinea Kicks Off Plan to Bring Internet to 2,200 Schools]],
  [[2025-11-07 Guinea Plans Mobile Payment System to Ease Cash Shortage]]. See [[infra.connect]],
  [[include.divides]], [[dpi.pay]].
- **2025-09-09** — **Guinea profiled as TAS host (Smart Africa backgrounder).** Guinea joined Smart
  Africa in 2016 and chairs the Council of African Regulators via **[[arpt-guinea|ARPT]]**; new agencies
  ANDE/ANSSI/ANSUTEN institutionalise delivery, security and inclusion. Source:
  [[2025-09-09 Get to know The Republic of Guinea Home of Transform Africa Summit 2025]]. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2025-09-04** — **First Tier III national data centre + sovereign .gn launched.** Guinea
  inaugurated its **[[guinea-national-data-centre|first Tier III data centre]]** (Uptime-certified) in
  Conakry and reinstated sovereign management of the **.gn** ccTLD; Orange Guinea cut internet tariffs
  25%. EGDI 0.4006 (29th in Africa, up from 0.2955 in 2022). Sources:
  [[2025-09-04 Guinea opens Tier 3 national data centre to strengthen digital transition]],
  [[2025-09-05 Guinea Launches National Domain .gn and Unveils First Tier III Data Center]]. See
  [[infra.store]], [[sovereignty]].
- **2025-05-02** *(WB ISR)* — **WURI-Guinea closed early; pivot to a domestic ID path.** A World Bank
  ISR records that **[[wuri-programme|WURI]]-Guinea closed early on 28 June 2024** (only ~8,000 unique
  IDs issued), with the ISR cautioning Guinea's Oct-2023 ID/civil-registry laws (underpinning **RAVEC**)
  "did not fully reflect the principles of identification for sustainable development." Guinea has since
  built its own **MOSIP/TECH5** stack ([[oneci-guinea]]). Source:
  [[2025-05-02 WURI Implementation Status Report - Cote d'Ivoire and Guinea (P161329 Seq 11)]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[gov.regional]].
- **2025-03-03** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2025: Guinea.** 14.2m mobile connections
  (95.1% of pop, 80.8% broadband); **3.96m internet users (26.5% penetration)** — 73.5% offline; 3.05m
  social-media identities; population 14.9m (61.3% rural, median age 18.3). Source:
  [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - Guinea]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[include.divides]], [[data.statistics]].
- **2025** *(profile; proxy date)* — **Registre Social Unifié (RSU).** Guinea's unified social
  registry ([[rsu-guinea]]) — single entry point for social-protection programmes, created by Decree
  D/2019/271 (1 Oct 2019). Source: [[2025-01-01 Registre Social Unifie (RSU) presentation - Guinea]].
  See [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]].
- **2023-08-03** *(baseline)* — **Civil-registry modernization pilot (Enabel/Belgium).** An EU-funded,
  Enabel-supported pilot across 10 communes digitised the civil registry (105,000+ records migrated;
  DXC Technology modules), against a stark baseline — only **45-60%** hold birth certificates, **<2%**
  of marriages/deaths registered. Source:
  [[2023-08-03 The modernisation of Guinea's civil registry inspired by Belgium's experience (Citizenship Rights in Africa)]].
  See [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]].
