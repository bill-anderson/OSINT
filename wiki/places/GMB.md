# The Gambia (GMB)

A small West-African democracy that, since the 2017 transition, has moved unusually fast on the
**building blocks of a digital state** — often with heavy donor support and a striking preference for
**foundational systems** over flashy apps. The spine is a **unique national identification number**,
generated in real time by a **World Bank-built eCRVS** during a 2022-23 mass birth-registration +
health-insurance campaign that reached ~1.17m people, now the interoperability backbone across
ministries. On top sit a Margins/MIDSA national-ID build ([[gambia-national-id-contract]]), a
**Mojaloop-based instant payment switch** (**[[gamswitch|BANTABA 2.0]]**), a **[[gambia-social-registry|social
registry]]** covering ~70% of the population, DHIS2 for health and education, and — in 2025 — a first
comprehensive **[[gambia-data-protection-act-2025|data-protection law]]**. All framed by the National
Digital Economy Master Plan 2024-2034.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-17** *(the source says the MoU was signed "on Friday" and was published Monday 2026-07-20;
  **the date is derived, not stated** — treat as 2026-07-17 ±)* — **A home-grown payment platform is
  wired into government revenue collection.** The **Office of the Accountant General** signed an MoU at
  its Banjul conference hall integrating **SUTURA** — a Gambian digital payment platform built by
  **Nifty ICT Solutions** with **GAMPOST** — into the **government's digital payment ecosystem**, so
  citizens can pay **taxes, fees and public-service charges** through additional electronic channels.
  Framed by Deputy Accountant General (Accounting and Reporting) **Clara Saine Mendy** as part of the
  **Public Financial Management reform agenda** and a **cashless-economy / domestic-revenue-mobilisation**
  drive; she says **several commercial banks and other payment channels are already integrated**.
  GAMPOST (Head of Finance **Samba Ceesay**) supplies the doorstep/postal distribution layer; Nifty MD
  **Jalamang Jobarteh** the technology. *Worth holding for one structural reason:* the revenue-collection
  rail here is **domestically built and postal-distributed**, which is a different model from the
  vendor-supplied G2P stacks that dominate the region — and it sits directly alongside the
  **[[nims-gambia|NIMS]]** ID launch below, the two together being the state's payment and identity
  spines arriving within weeks of each other. **Nothing is quantified**: no transaction volume, revenue
  figure, fee structure, user count, go-live date, integration timetable or list of the already-connected
  banks is given, and **no MoU text is held** — so this is an **announced intent, not a measured
  capability**, as of 2026-07-21. See [[dpi.pay]], [[dpi.govtech]], [[gambia--dpi-pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-20-gambia-accountant-general-sutura-digital-revenue-collection]].
- **2026-07** — **Sovereign digital ID goes live.** President Barrow launched the **[[nims-gambia|NIMS]]**
  National Identity Management System and the **National Data Centre (Abuko)** — instant **GAM ID** built by
  **[[margins-id-group]]** on a BOT model; nationwide enrolment from 4 Aug. See [[gambia--dpi-id]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-01-barrow-inaugurates-national-data-centre-nims-standard]].
- **2026 — telecom restructuring.** **YCELL acquired 80% of GAMCEL** ([[gamcel-ycell-deal]]; winning bid D6.7bn
  → final GMD 6.1bn); **[[gamtel-sysroad-ppp|GAMTEL–SYSROAD]]** $50m PPP upgrades the backbone (40→800 Gbps).
  See [[gambia--infra-connect]]. Source: [[2026-07-10-gamtel-ycell-share-investment-modernization-thepoint]].
- **2026 — data-centre & Starlink sovereignty.** The **[[qgroup-house-datacentre|QGroup House]]** (first private
  data centre; Huawei) opened; the **[[gambia-starlink-licence|Starlink licence]]** stayed stalled amid US
  pressure and data-sovereignty conditions. Sources:
  [[2026-04-30-barrow-qgroup-house-first-data-centre-thepoint]], [[2026-05-18-starlink-gambia-future-economic-fear-thepoint]],
  [[2026-05-24-gambia-starlink-licence-delayed]].
- **2025–26 — legal & governance scaffolding.** The **[[gambia-data-protection-act-2025|Data Protection Act]]**,
  the **[[gambia-communications-bill-2025|Communications Bill 2025]]**, the **[[ndasc-gambia|NDASC]]** digital
  addressing, an ITU **GovStack** national enterprise architecture, a **Senegal–Gambia** regulatory MoU, and a
  **KALP "Gambia One"** blockchain DPI pact. See [[gambia--dpi-govtech]].
- **2025–26 — skills.** A WARDIP-funded (**$3.35m**) **National Labour Digital Skill** methodology (target 280
  certified) validated; a **Gambia Digital Innovation Sprint** youth showcase (NYC / MOCDE / UNICEF). Sources:
  [[2025-12-23-validate-labour-digital-skill-report-thepoint]], [[2026-07-09-young-gambians-digital-innovations-children-thepoint]].
- **2026-03** — **Digitizing the state: government payments, elections, policing.** **[[yonna-wallet]]**
  became the first Gambian fintech authorized to collect **all government-service payments** (via the
  Accountant General), with diaspora payment via its Global Wallet (2 Mar). The **IEC** announced
  partial election digitalisation (GIS polling-station mapping, results transmission, tablets) ahead of
  December polls (12 Mar). The **IGP** (Seedy Touray) pushed police from paper to digital records (24
  Mar), and UNDP-backed **community policing** added mobile case-management apps + GIS facility mapping.
  Sources: [[2026-03-02 Gambia Gov't payments now digitalised live on Yonna Wallet app]],
  [[2026-03-12 Gambia IEC introduces partial digitalisation ahead December polls]],
  [[2026-03-24 Gambia Police must transition from paper documentation to digitalised mode - IGP]],
  [[2026-03-23 From Essau to Gambisara - Reimagining Community Policing Across The Gambia]]. See
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.pay]].
- **2026-01-30** — **DHIS2 for learner-level education data.** **MoBSE** (Basic & Secondary Education) is moving to individual
  learner tracking and School Report Cards on **[[dhis2]]** for Education (GPE/HISP) — positioned as an
  African model EMIS. Source: [[2026-01-30 The Gambia charts a path to data-driven education with DHIS2]].
  See [[dpi.mis]].
- **2026-01-22** — **Margins/MIDSA national-ID build confirmed.** A second outlet corroborated the
  **[[gambia-national-id-contract]]** — Margins subsidiary **MIDSA** to deliver an integrated National
  Identity Management System and ICAO-compliant e-ID, ending "an era of confusion." Source:
  [[2026-01-22 The Gambia picks Margins ID Group for national ID infrastructure upgrade]]. See [[dpi.id]].
- **2025-12** — **BANTABA 2.0 instant payment switch; digital addressing; data-protection review.** The
  Central Bank launched **[[gamswitch|BANTABA 2.0]]**, the first Inclusive Instant Payment System (built
  on **[[mojaloop]]**, domestically owned; 15 Dec). LoGRI documented Kanifing's **Plus Code digital
  addressing** (36,000 properties georeferenced, feeding property-tax + waste management; Dec). A legal
  review detailed the enacted **[[gambia-data-protection-act-2025|data-protection Act]]** (26 Dec).
  Sources: [[2025-12-15 Gambia launches BANTABA 2.0, first real-time interoperable payment system]],
  [[2025-12-01 Implementing a Comprehensive Digital Addressing System - Kanifing Municipality, The Gambia]],
  [[2025-12-26 Review of Gambia's Personal Data Protection and Privacy Act, 2025]],
  [[2025-12-22 Smart Paper Technology is Transforming Community Health Data Capture in The Gambia]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[digital.localgov]], [[gov.protect]], [[dpi.mis]].
- **2025-10** — **First comprehensive data-protection law passed.** The National Assembly passed the
  **[[gambia-data-protection-act-2025|Personal Data Protection and Privacy Act 2025]]**, establishing an
  independent Commission (aligned with the Open Data Strategy 2023-2026); a 2024 Presight MoU covered
  digital ID + digital government. Sources:
  [[2025-10-13 Gambia Passes Landmark Data Protection and Privacy Law]],
  [[2025-10-29 The Gambia strengthens digital trust with personal data protection & privacy bill]]. See
  [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]].
- **2025-07-09** — **GRA digital tax-invoicing (e-invoicing).** The Gambia Revenue Authority launched
  an e-invoicing platform (agreement with Avatar Technologies, USA) with tamper-proof digital
  signatures, to cut under-declaration — complementing the Digital Weigh Bridge, Fuel Integrity Marking
  and Digital Tax Stamp. Source: [[2025-07-09 Gambia Gov't launches Digital Tax Invoicing System (GRA)]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[dpi.pay]].
- **2024-05-17** — **National Digital ID + Digital Transformation Strategy validated.** A UN ECA-supported
  workshop validated the **[[gambia-national-digital-id-strategy|strategy 2023-2028]]**, with the newly
  established **[[gicta]]** among stakeholders. Source:
  [[2024-05-17 Validation of The Gambia's National Digital ID and Digital Transformation Strategy]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2024-04-23** — **GamSR social registry — ~70% coverage.** The **[[gambia-social-registry]]** reached
  203,022 households (40/43 districts) in two years, powering Nafa cash transfers and disaster response.
  Source: [[2024-04-23 The Gambia Social Registry - A Success Story in Systems Building]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]].
- **2024-03-28** — **eCRVS mass birth-registration + health insurance.** A World Bank project registered
  ~**1.17m people** (birth certificates + NHIA cards, each with a **unique national ID number + QR
  code**) via an **eCRVS**, now the foundational interoperability system (pre-2020 birth registration
  was ~59%). Source: [[2024-03-28 A Transformative Journey of Mass Birth Registration and Health Insurance in The Gambia]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.mis]].
- **2026-01-20** — **National ID exported by Ghana's Margins (DBFOT).** Foundational context: The Gambia
  signed a DBFOT contract with Ghana's **[[margins-id-group]]** ([[gambia-national-id-contract]]) — its
  third national-ID attempt. Source: [[2026-01-20 Margins takes Ghana's ID success to The Gambia]]. See
  [[dpi.id]], [[finance.new]].
- **2025-03-03** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2025.** Population 2.79m (65% urban); mobile
  connections 3.15m (113%, 89% broadband); internet users 1.28m (**45.9% penetration**); social media
  16.1%. Source: [[2025-03-03 Digital 2025 - The Gambia (DataReportal)]]. See [[data.statistics]].
- *(regional)* The Gambia is in the **[[wardip]]** cohort (WB/ECOWAS/Smart Africa); state operators
  Gamtel/Gamcel remain a "stranded" incumbent seeking rescue (Bankai judgment, 80% Y-Cell sale, Sysroad
  PPP). See [[XWA]], [[tech.industry]].
