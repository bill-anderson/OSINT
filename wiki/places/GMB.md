# The Gambia (GMB)

## Recent developments

- **2026-01-20** — **National ID system to be built by a Ghanaian firm.** The Government
  of The Gambia signed a **DBFOT** (design-build-finance-operate-transfer) contract with
  Ghana's **[[margins-id-group]]** to deliver a **National ID System + Integrated Digital
  ID System** — the deal that makes Margins the **first African-owned company to export a
  full national ID system** beyond its home market. The tender opened **22 Jul 2024**,
  drew **11 international bidders**, and Margins won after a three-firm shortlist on
  technical design and delivery record (its Ghana system: 19.2m registered, 262 integrated
  institutions). Officials called it The Gambia's **third attempt** at a national ID
  ("we got it wrong twice"), stressing knowledge transfer, diaspora access and data
  protection. Framed as Africa-to-Africa collaboration aligned with AfCFTA. Deal:
  [[gambia-national-id-contract]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-20 Margins takes Ghana's ID success to The Gambia]] ([[ghana-card]], [[GHA]]).
  See [[dpi.id]], [[finance.new]], [[tech.industry]].
- **2026-02-16** — **Gamtel/Gamcel among the "stranded" incumbents (Russell Southwood).**
  Southwood's former-incumbents study covers The Gambia's state operators: a Gambian court
  ordered **US$2.9m** paid to Bankai (Jun 2025), an **80% sale to Y Cell (Yoona)** is under
  way, and a **US$50m PPP with Sysroad** aims to lift capacity 50→800 Gbps — a repeatedly
  failing incumbent seeking rescue. See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]].
- *(regional)* The Gambia is a member of the **WARDIP** (Western Africa Regional Digital
  Integration Program) cohort — alongside Guinea-Bissau and Mauritania in phase 1 — the
  World Bank / ECOWAS / Smart Africa framework advancing broadband and a common regional
  digital market ([[wardip]], [[XWA]]).
