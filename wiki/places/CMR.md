# Cameroon (CMR)

Cameroon's digital transformation is running through two donor-backed tracks: a
**World Bank–funded cybersecurity build-out** to protect critical digital
infrastructure (via ANTIC and the PATNUC programme), and a **UNDP/Denmark-funded
digitisation of civil registration** to widen legal-identity access in
crisis-affected regions. Both are institutional-capacity plays rather than
flagship national platforms.

## Recent developments

- **2026-01** — **ANTIC cyber-defence procurement via PATNUC.** The National Agency
  for ICTs (**[[antic-cameroon]]**) commissioned intrusion-detection/prevention
  hardware and software worth **~XAF735m (~$1.3m)** — servers, storage consoles,
  investigation platforms, workstations, vulnerability-scanning/penetration-testing
  rigs — at its Computer Incident Response Team (CIRT) in Yaoundé (20 Jan 2026),
  procured through the Ministry of Posts & Telecoms under the **World Bank**–funded
  **[[patnuc]]** (Project for the Acceleration of Digital Transformation of Cameroon).
  ANTIC (DG Ebot Ebot Enaw) has identified **≥8,502 vulnerabilities** since Jan 2024;
  its CIRT collects ~**200GB/day** and handles ~**200 urgent requisitions**; it
  processed **32,500 cybercrime complaints in 2025** (+30% YoY). Sources:
  [[2026-01-27 Cameroon deploys cybersecurity system to protect DPI, boost digital trust]],
  [[2026-01-22 Cameroon boosts cyber defense systems]] ([[world-bank]]). See
  [[infra.cybersec]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2026-01-13** — **Digital civil-documents system.** Cameroon deployed a **digital
  civil-status system** (birth, marriage, death certificates; faster processing and
  secure archiving at council level), implemented by **[[undp]]** with funding from
  **Denmark** (UNDP Funding Windows), covering **10 municipalities** across the Far
  North, North-West and South-West regions (Maroua III, Koza, Mindif, Méri, Mora,
  Limbe I, Buea, Tiko, Bamenda III, Bamenda City). Aims to widen **legal-identity
  access** for youth, women and vulnerable groups and to sustain services through
  security/climate crises and displacement. Source:
  [[2026-01-13 Cameroon intros digital system for civil documents]]. See
  [[dpi.registry]], [[dpi.id]], [[digital.localgov]], [[include.access]].

- **2026-02–04 (Russell Southwood)** — **Camtel — the one profitable incumbent, via
  monopoly.** In Southwood's former-incumbents study, Cameroon's state operator **Camtel**
  (D-G Judith Yah Sunday) is the **only profitable** former fixed-line incumbent — but only
  because it holds protected **wholesale and submarine-cable landing-station monopolies**;
  recent financing includes a **Jan 2026 US$81.2m** CBC package (plus ~US$65.65m from BEAC in
  2025) and a >US$150m mobile-market bet. His ICT4D long view cites Camtel as a case of
  **landlocked-neighbour gatekeeping** on international bandwidth. See [[tech.industry]],
  [[infra.connect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]],
  [[2026-04-16 The Long View - What ICT4D did for Africa and why that deep past matters for today]].
