# DR Congo (COD)

## Recent developments

- **2026-07 (early)** — **Visa–M-Pesa–Onafriq DRC stablecoin pilot.** [[visa]],
  Safaricom's M-Pesa ([[safaricom]]) and pan-African network [[onafriq]] announced a
  **live USD-pegged stablecoin pilot** settling cross-border mobile transactions on
  blockchain, bypassing correspondent banking (which siphons ~**8%** in SSA remittance
  fees). Builds on the **Visa Pay** platform launched in DRC (Sept 2025) via an Onafriq
  API. Deal: [[visa-mpesa-onafriq-drc-stablecoin-pilot]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Visa and M-Pesa Launch Blockchain Stablecoin Pilot in DR Congo]].
  See [[dpi.pay]], [[finance.new]], [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.gulf]].
  *(⚠ needs-review: low-authority aggregator, promotional, no clear primary citation —
  corroborate against Visa/Safaricom/Onafriq releases before hardening.)*
- **2026-06-11** — DRC set to launch its national digital-ID platform
  **[[rdc-pass]]** on **13 June 2026** (Kinshasa) under President
  **[[felix-tshisekedi]]**, within the "DRC 2030 Digital Nation" strategy. Built
  on Web 3.0; supports biometric SIM auth, unified e-gov access, automated e-KYC
  and secure digital IDs. Cost ~**$97.1m**; part of a **$1bn** 2026–2030 digital
  plan. Operated under a **20-year exclusive-e-KYC PPP** with Singapore's
  **[[trident-digital-tech]]** (signed June 2025) — raising data-sovereignty
  concerns. Source:
  [[2026-06-11 DR Congo to Launch National Digital Identity System RDC-PASS on June 13]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[gov.protect]]. *(⚠ confirm launch occurred.)*
- **2026-01-30** — **Digital-government platform targeted for Q1 2026 + full 2026–2030 blueprint.**
  DRC plans to bring a **digital-government platform** online in the first quarter of 2026, alongside
  a **national interbank payment platform**, an **online tax portal** and a **customs system**
  (tax/customs expected to lift direct revenue by **1.2–1.3% of GDP**). These sit within a
  **2026–2030 digital & infrastructure blueprint** (four pillars: infrastructure/connectivity,
  human-capital/inclusion, digital public services, cybersecurity/digital trust) built on the
  **[[rdc-pass]]** ID ([[trident-digital-tech]], ~**$97.1m**). Targets: broadband to ~30m people +
  1,000 institutions (+9.7m internet users by 2029); 250,000 youth trained in AI/cyber/programming;
  a Tier-3 Kinshasa data centre; 30,000 cybersecurity jobs. The plan needs **~€8.7bn (US$10.4bn)** —
  government committing ~**$1.5bn**, the rest from partners incl. the **[[world-bank]]** IDEA
  programme ([[idea-mpa]]) and AFD — and is projected to add **$4.1bn to GDP by 2030**. Updates the
  earlier "$1bn plan" framing. Source:
  [[2026-01-30 DRC envisages a functional digital government platform by March]]. See [[dpi.govtech]],
  [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]], [[infra.store]], [[infra.connect]], [[gov.policy]], [[infra.cybersec]],
  [[capacity.training]].
- **2026-01-26** — **Onafriq deepens DRC financial-infrastructure play.** **[[onafriq]]** (CEO Dare
  Okoudjou; connects ~1bn mobile wallets + 500m bank accounts across 43 countries) reaffirmed
  expansion in DRC despite eastern-conflict risk, having supported the **[[visa]] Pay** launch in DRC
  in **Q4 2025** (card ↔ mobile-money interoperability via an Onafriq API). Frames DRC as an
  "underserved giant" — **29m mobile-money accounts / 34.6% penetration** (Q4 2024), late-cycle in
  mobile money but early-cycle in interoperability — with a supportive central bank (BCC). Source:
  [[2026-01-26 How Onafriq is growing digital finance amid uncertainty in DRC]]. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[include.access]], [[finance.new]].
- **2026-01-19** — **PayServices sues DRC for up to $400m.** Idaho fintech **[[payservices]]** filed
  in US federal court against the DRC and senior officials, alleging deliberate sabotage of a **$72m**
  project to modernise state banking/payments (incl. the Caisse Générale d'Épargne, e-gov payments,
  a national payments platform); it says it invested **>$72m** awaiting an agreed **$20m** state
  contribution never paid, and that the project collapsed after it refused **bribe demands**. Damages
  sought run to **$400m** (some filings cite **$4bn**). Kinshasa's Ministry of Public Enterprises
  calls the signed documents a **non-binding MoU** and denies the claims — a governance/contract-
  certainty irritant as DRC courts Washington on security and minerals. Deal:
  [[payservices-drc-banking-deal]]. Source:
  [[2026-01-19 US fintech sues DR Congo for $400m over failed banking deal]]. See [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.govtech]], [[finance.new]], [[geopol.usa]].
- **2025-12 / 2026-02** — **DRC in the MNO state-of-market and incumbents story (Russell
  Southwood).** [[orange]] and [[vodacom]] signed a **Jan 2025** JV to build **solar-powered
  base stations** in rural DRC — an example of the off-grid network-sharing models operators
  now adopt for rural CAPEX ([[2025-12-04 Is it a bird Is it plane No, Jim, it's an African mobile operator, but not as we know it 9]]).
  Southwood's former-incumbents study recounts DRC's state operator **SCPT**: WACS-2011
  landing-station funds vanished (D-G jailed), and **Benya** (described as **[[g42]]**-backed)
  took a 2021 shareholder deal but exited after six months
  ([[2026-02-16 Waking the dead - Slow road to extinction for Africa's former telco incumbents 11]]).
  See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.energy]], [[tech.industry]].
- **2026-01-01** — **Starlink–Vodacom LEO backhaul reaches DRC.** DR Congo is among the 25
  African countries where **[[starlink]]** is live, and is covered by the new
  **[[starlink-vodacom-leo-partnership]]** (LEO backhaul + equipment resale) — part of
  Starlink's shift from rival to telco ally (cf. [[starlink-airtel-d2c-partnership]]). Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Starlink Partners with Airtel & Vodacom From Rival to Ally]]. See
  [[infra.connect]], [[data.satellite]].
- **2026-01** — **DRC on the US critical-minerals bilateral track.** An IISS analysis of
  Washington's minerals strategy (America-First equity deals; public-finance-backed bilateral
  pacts; the Dec 2025 **[[pax-silica]]** coalition) lists **DR Congo** among bilateral
  partners, with critical-minerals diplomacy linked to **security guarantees** — geopolitical
  context for DRC's Washington courtship. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 US critical-minerals diplomacy from America-First deals to Pax Silica]]. See
  [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2025-09** — **GSMA DRC Digital Economy Report** (with Through Line Advisors) profiles
  the mobile sector: **coverage low by regional standards** and **smartphone penetration
  below peers**, reflecting high network-build costs; a **complex multi-level tax
  framework** raises the burden and crowds out investment. Modelled reforms (lower
  excise/operator taxes, simpler collection, grid expansion, regulatory stability, device
  financing) could add **+9.7m internet users by 2029** and cut the mobile-broadband
  usage gap by 8pp. Aligns with the **National Digital Plan Horizon 2025** and the
  **World Bank DRC Digital Transformation Project**. Source:
  [[2025-09-01 GSMA DRC Digital Economy Report 2025]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[tech.industry]],
  [[gov.policy]], [[include.access]].
