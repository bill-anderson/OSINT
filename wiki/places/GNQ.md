# Equatorial Guinea (GNQ)

A small, oil-dependent CEMAC state whose digital economy is **nascent and, until recently, largely
undiagnosed**. The 2024 World Bank Digital Economy Diagnostic and 2025 Country Economic Memorandum
frame digitalization as a diversification lever away from oil (≈80% of revenues), but find a **weak
regulatory framework, no licensed mobile-money operator, and a national ID system not yet implemented**
despite a 2018 digital civil-registry pilot. Identity runs through **[[cnedoge]]** (the DIP and
passports); connectivity rests on the Ceiba/ACE subsea cables and, prospectively, a Nigeria link.
Uniquely, mobile penetration is *low* (45%) while internet access is comparatively high (60%), with a
pronounced **island (Bioko/Malabo) vs continental (Bata)** and gender divide.

## Recent developments

- **2026 — telecom & cable modernisation.** **[[getesa-gnq|GETESA]]** modernisation (5G/eSIM/Wi-Fi/Getesa
  Money) with a **[[huawei|Huawei]]** contract; a new 2nd state operator **[[telgesa-gnq|Telgesa]]**; and cable
  diversification (**[[gnq-medusa-cable|Medusa]]** study, Via Africa) with **[[gitge-gnq|GITGE]]** hosting the ACE
  consortium. See [[gnq--infra-connect]]. Sources:
  [[2026-05-25-getesa-modernizacion-5g-wifi-getesa-money-reg]], [[2026-04-03-estudio-cable-submarino-medusa-analysys-mason-reg]].
- **2026 — e-government & data centres.** The **[[sigf-gnq|SIGF]]** civil-servant system (M2M Group, biometric
  attendance/payroll); **PAMFP** government data centres (Malabo/Bata); a **[[zeede-djibloho|ZEEDE-GE]]** digital
  zone; and a **[[gitge-gnq|GITGE]]** ministries digital-gap study (interop ~15%). See [[gnq--dpi-govtech]],
  [[gnq--infra-store]]. Sources: [[2026-02-20-sigf-activa-funcion-publica-18-meses-reg]],
  [[2026-06-03-pamfp-licitacion-centros-datos-malabo-bata-reg]].
- **2026 — cyber & data law.** GNQ (the host state) **ratified the [[malabo-convention|Malabo Convention]]** (25
  Mar 2026) and rolled out **[[ley-7-2024-gnq|Ley 7/2024]]** (cybercrime/social-media/privacy; creates the ANCIC
  cyber agency). See [[gnq--dpi-govtech]]. Source: [[2026-03-25-ratifican-convencion-malabo-ciberseguridad-datos-reg]].
- **2025–26 — payments cash-lite drive.** A national **ATM/POS plan**, VP bank-sector directives (5 banks + BEAC),
  **[[bange-gnq|BANGE MOBILE GE]]** and Getesa Money — bottlenecked by connectivity. See [[gnq--dpi-pay]]. Source:
  [[2026-02-25-vp-banca-cajeros-tpv-pago-electronico-reg]].
- **2025 — regional & partners.** **Gabon–GNQ** fibre/roaming/frequency accords (Mongomo); **[[cniapge-gnq|CNIAPGE]]**
  at the WB Global Digital Summit; **UNDP "Digital Fitness"**; a **China** tech-hub cooperation roadmap. See
  [[gnq--dpi-govtech]]. Source: [[2025-11-13-gabon-guinea-ecuatorial-fibra-roaming-frecuencias-techafricanews]].
- **2025-04-04** — **DIP tightening + civil-registry integrity.** The government moved to require the
  **DIP** (national ID) more strictly of over-18s and to control fraudulent "literal" (birth)
  certificates used by expatriates to obtain the DIP/passport (against the 2011 Nationality Law).
  **[[cnedoge]]** reported a national DIP tour: 7,670 dossiers obtained, 7,275 processed. Sources:
  [[2025-04-04 Government wants a rigorous control mechanism for issuing the literal (birth) certificate]],
  [[2024-03-15 The Executive invites parents to register children in the Civil Registry and adults over 18 to obtain the DIP]].
  See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.registry]].
- **2025-03-04** — **World Bank Country Economic Memorandum (first ever).** Urges a new growth model as
  oil declines; digital-relevant recommendations include improving the one-stop shop, enabling online
  business registration and digitizing the Commercial Registry, within **AGENDA 2035** / the National
  Development Plan 2035. A universal Social Protection Law is drafted but unapproved. Source:
  [[2025-03-04 Country Economic Memorandum on Equatorial Guinea 2025 - Building Foundations for Renewed Diversified and Inclusive Growth]].
  See [[gov.policy]], [[dpi.govtech]].
- **2025-11-08** *(baseline)* — **DataReportal Digital 2026.** Population 1.95m (75% urban); **mobile
  connections just 45.4%** of population; internet penetration 60.4%; social media 9.1%. Source:
  [[2025-11-08 Digital 2026 - Equatorial Guinea (DataReportal)]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[data.statistics]].
- **2024-06-25 / 2024-07-15** — **WB Digital Economy Diagnostic (DE4A).** The
  **[[equatorial-guinea-digital-economy-diagnostic]]** assesses five pillars against the **[[adige]]**
  digital agenda: broadband affordability improving but weak competition/cyber; a 2018 digital
  civil-registry pilot but **no digital ID yet**; **no licensed mobile-money operator**; recommends a
  digital-ID ecosystem, digital-payment infrastructure and an absent national **interoperability
  framework**. Sources:
  [[2024-06-25 Equatorial Guinea Digital Economy Country Diagnostic - Bridging the Gaps]],
  [[2024-06-25 Equatorial Guinea - New Report Proposes a Way Forward to Develop a Safe and Inclusive Digital Economy]],
  [[2024-07-15 World Bank identifies priority actions for DPI in Equatorial Guinea]]. See [[dpi.id]],
  [[dpi.pay]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2024** *(profile)* — **CNEDOGE document-issuance body.** [[cnedoge]] (Decree 24/2010, Ministry of
  National Security) issues the biometric DIP, passports and other official documents; ~30 days for a
  DIP, 14-17 for a passport. Source:
  [[2024-01-01 On the functioning of Equatorial Guinea's National Centre for the Issuance of Official Documents (CNEDOGE)]].
  See [[dpi.id]].
- **2022-12-08** — **DHIS2 eLMIS for malaria logistics (Bioko Island).** The BIMEP extended **[[dhis2]]**
  into an electronic logistics MIS for malaria commodities — forecasting stock, triangulating stock vs
  services data, alerting on expiries — a concrete health-data-systems bright spot. Source:
  [[2022-12-08 Deploying DHIS2 for eLMIS to improve Malaria stock management in Equatorial Guinea]]. See
  [[dpi.mis]], [[data.statistics]].
- **2026-02** — **Nigeria–Equatorial Guinea subsea cable.** A subsea-fibre agreement with **[[NGA]]**
  bundling a data centre — a prospective new international route. See [[nigeria-eqguinea-subsea-cable]],
  [[infra.store]]. Source: [[2026-02-24 Nigeria, Equatorial Guinea subsea cable strengthens connectivity]].
- **2018-06-04** *(baseline)* — **Ceiba-2 subsea cable.** Equatorial Guinea inaugurated Ceiba-2
  (400 km, 40→100 Gbps; Malabo-Bata-Kribi), its first direct international fibre link, via **GITGE** —
  complementing Ceiba-1 and ACE. Source:
  [[2018-06-06 Equatorial Guinea and Cameroon are now more connected - AFR-IX telecom]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[CMR]].

## Record not held (as of 2026-07-20)

Equatorial Guinea's government news outlet **guineaecuatorialpress.com is cookie-walled** and served no body or
publication date on repeated automated attempts. Seven GNQ developments the wiki knows of only by their existence
therefore have **no source of record as of 2026-07-20**: the **Huawei e-Government MoU**; the **e-government law**;
**BEAC joining PAPSS**; the **Starlink Guinea Ecuatorial** entity creation; the **Jul–Dec 2026 connectivity plan**
(2G/4G to 85%, "Roaming Cero", 34 towers in Annobón); the **unique person/entity identification code** (likely 2023);
and the **full text of Ley 7/2024**. This is why the GNQ page carries policy *intent* but almost no dated delivery
figures — the gap is an access problem at a single publisher, not an absence of activity. Acquisition was dropped
rather than parked: the cookie wall needs a hand-clip, which no automated pass will beat.
