# Seychelles (SYC)

Indian Ocean island state, part of East Africa ([[XEA]]). Two threads dominate its profile here.
The first is **domestic digitalisation of identity and public services**: a national digital ID
([[seyid]]) running since 2022 on Swiss public-key technology, online civil-status certificates
([[certexpress]]), a data-protection regime in force since December 2023
([[data-protection-act-2023-seychelles]]), and a central-bank programme to retire cheques outright.
The second is its emerging role as a **continental digital-trade hub** under the [[afcfta]]
framework.

Seychelles is a small, high-capacity state, and the shape of its material reflects that: less
"building foundations" than consolidating and enforcing them. Its open questions are accordingly
about commencement dates, enforcement reach and drafting gaps rather than coverage.

## Recent developments

- **2026-07-08** — **Seychelles to host the AfCFTA Digital Trade Corridor HQ.** The Cabinet
  approved hosting the **[[afcfta-digital-trade-corridor]]** headquarters in **Victoria**,
  making it the operational seat for cross-border digital commerce under the
  **[[afcfta-digital-trade-protocol]]** — sitting **alongside (not replacing)** the AfCFTA
  Secretariat in Accra. It builds on a precursor **[[seychelles-afcfta-mou]]** signed
  **2026-03-05** between the AfCFTA Secretariat, **[[quest-ghana]]** Ltd and the Government of
  Seychelles during a visit by Secretary-General **[[wamkele-mene]]**, and follows the AfCFTA
  Digital Trade Forum 2026 (Lagos, 2 July 2026). VP Sebastian Pillay announced the decision;
  FM Barry Faure credited. The corridor is intended to support cross-border commerce, the
  Africa Commodities Exchange, supply-chain connectivity and MSME integration. Source:
  [[2026-07-10 Seychelles to Host AfCFTA Digital Trade Corridor Headquarters]]. See
  [[gov.regional]], [[dpi.exchange]], [[finance.mou]], [[XAF]].
- **2025-02-07** — **President visits the Registrar General's Office.** President
  **[[wavel-ramkalawan]]** toured the Business Registration and Intellectual Property departments
  of the **[[registrar-general-office-seychelles]]**, praised digitalisation progress and
  announced upcoming legislative reforms to support modernised operations — no bill named, no date
  given. Source:
  [[2025-02-10 Seychelles Pledges to Modernize Public Services Through Digital Transformation]].
  See [[dpi.govtech]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2024-09-17** — **Digital ID becomes mandatory for new work-permit holders.** From this date
  the **[[department-of-immigration-and-civil-status-seychelles]]** stopped issuing physical ID
  cards to new permit holders, registering them on **[[seyid]]** with a virtual ID valid for the
  duration of the permit. The National Identity Number process is unchanged — applicants receive
  an "ID letter" carrying their NIN, then register on the SeyID site or app. Seychellois citizens
  **retain** the option of a physical ID (which expires every ten years), and citizens abroad may
  also register. Launched alongside **[[certexpress]]**: birth, marriage and death certificates
  online, delivered in two working days, saved to the SeyID **E-Doc wallet**, price unchanged at
  **SCR 50**. Sources: [[2024-09-18 Launch of New Digital Solutions for Public Services]] (the
  issuing department's own announcement) and
  [[2024-09-18 Seychelles Makes Digital ID Mandatory for Work Permit Holders]] (which uniquely
  carries the [[wisekey]] lineage and PS Alain Volcere's quote). See [[dpi.id]],
  [[dpi.registry]], [[include.access]].
- **2024-09-22** — **Cheques to be phased out entirely.** The **[[central-bank-of-seychelles]]**
  set a four-step schedule: banks stop **issuing** cheques to individuals from **1 Jan 2025**;
  stop **accepting** them from individuals from **1 May 2025**; stop issuing to non-residents from
  **1 Jan 2026**; stop accepting from non-residents after **1 May 2026**. CBS Director of Financial
  Inclusion **Liz Julienne** cited ~**600,000 cheques** used in transactions in 2023, against
  **over 10 million** card transactions and **[[seft-seychelles]]** electronic transfers in the
  same period. Consultations raised concerns about the **financial exclusion** of cheque-reliant
  groups and the cost of adopting digital alternatives — raised on the record, unresolved on it.
  *Provenance caveat: the canonical Seychelles News Agency URL returned HTTP 403 on two attempts;
  the text held is a same-day syndicated copy, and the date is filed `date_source: proxy`
  (GAP-SYC-002).* Source:
  [[2024-09-22 Seychelles to phase out cheques for financial transactions from 2025]]. See
  [[dpi.pay]], [[include.access]].
  - *Corroborating mention (2026-01-13):* Seychelles is named (with Ghana) among African states
    tightening cheque rules as they push digital-first payment agendas — context in an ITWeb
    Africa piece on Kenya's contrasting retention of cheques. Source:
    [[2026-01-13 The rise of cheques as Kenya's preferred payment method]]. See [[KEN]].
- **2023-12-22** — **Data Protection Act, 2023 (Act 24 of 2023) comes into operation**, repealing
  the Data Protection Act (Cap 57), which had been "enacted but never implemented in practice".
  **The gazetted primary settles the two dates that had been reported in conflict:** the Act is
  signed **"Wavel Ramkalawan, President, 19th December, 2023"** — **presidential assent** — and
  carries the gazette enacting date **"[22 December 2023]"** — **coming into operation**. They are
  two different events, not competing accounts of one. It is **Act 24 of 2023**. The Bill was
  passed by the National Assembly **5 December 2023** (Clerk's certificate). The
  **[[information-commission-seychelles]]**, created under the Access to Information Act, is
  designated the competent authority (s.5) — a designate-an-existing-body model TechHive compares
  to South Africa's. Source: [[2023-12-22-seychelles-data-protection-act-2023-act-24]] (gazetted
  text); earlier secondary accounts
  [[2024-03-25 Data Protection Law Now In Force In Seychelles]],
  [[2024-06-04 Review of Seychelles DPA]]. See [[gov.protect]], [[gov.legislate]].
  **Transition elapsed.** **s.56(1)** gives a controller or processor already processing before
  commencement **18 months from the coming into operation** to conform — i.e. **to 22 June 2025**,
  running from commencement (22 Dec 2023), not from assent. s.56(2) makes failure to comply an
  offence. As of **2026-07-20** the window is long past: Seychelles controllers and processors are
  subject to the Act in full. **No source held reports what happened at expiry** — whether
  compliance was achieved, extended, or simply not enforced.

## Active topics

- [[gov.protect]] · [[gov.legislate]] — [[data-protection-act-2023-seychelles]], enforced by the
  [[information-commission-seychelles]]; the 2025 SCB offshore-banking breach, ATIA reform + Data Protection Day. Full case: [[seychelles--gov-protect]]. GDPR-aligned on explicit consent, DPIAs for high-risk
  processing and DPO designation; **72-hour** breach notification to the authority (ss.43–44);
  cross-border transfers regulated with specific safeguards. TechHive's analysis flags a
  **territorial-scope gap** — s.3 gives no clear extraterritorial application, unlike peer laws —
  plus undefined breach-extension and subject-notification deadlines, and no age-verification
  mechanism behind the under-18 consent rule (s.23). That is one advisory firm's reading,
  attributed as such, not settled fact.

## Record not held (as of 2026-07-20)
