## Recent developments

- **2026-07-17** — **Mobile's climate ledger, and the renewables gap that hits Africa hardest (GSMA).**
  The **[[gsma-mobile-net-zero-2026|Mobile Net Zero 2026]]** report (110+ operators, 85% of global
  connections) records operational emissions of **115 Mt CO2e in 2024**, **down 5% in 2024** and **13%
  from 2019**, while connections grew 10% and traffic more than quadrupled. Renewables are the main
  lever — **24% of operator electricity in 2024, up from 10% in 2019** — but the shares sit in rich
  markets (**Europe ~70%, North America ~50%, Latin America ~45%**, 2024), and GSMA names **access to
  renewables in emerging markets** as the binding constraint. The African-relevant finding: the **100
  largest towercos** ran **~4m sites** on **>2bn litres of diesel in 2024**, with **<a quarter** holding
  validated targets. Policy asks: liberalise electricity markets, streamline clean-energy permitting,
  and treat **networks as critical infrastructure in national resilience planning**. *Reference study —
  cite, don't absorb; no Africa breakdown held.* See [[infra.energy]], [[infra.connect]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-17-gsma-mobile-net-zero-renewable-energy]].
- **2026-07-15** *(analysis)* — **"Sovereignty through collaboration" — the case for reading regionalism
  as autonomy, not fragmentation.** Writing on Geneva's Digital Week (the UN **Global Dialogue on AI
  Governance**, 6–11 July 2026, alongside the WSIS Forum and ITU's AI for Good Summit), **Emrys
  Schoemaker** (Global Digital Governance Lab, Graduate Institute; Global Policy Journal) argues that for
  capacity-constrained states — limited compute, data, technical and regulatory capacity — AI sovereignty
  is unachievable alone, so **regional and plurilateral arrangements are the practical form autonomy
  takes**, not a failure mode. The multilingualism panel (Kenyan CS for the Digital Economy, Francophone
  and Hispanophone blocs) is read as a bloc argument over who owns linguistic infrastructure. Second
  thesis: **multistakeholder participation is a "continuity mechanism"** keeping a slow institution
  connected to a fast technology between sessions (Geneva 2026 → New York May 2027), with industry and
  civil society left outside the room while standards work migrates to technical venues (e.g. the **[[itu]]**
  agentic-AI focus group, 9 July 2026). Directly bears on African digital-sovereignty framing. *Cite as
  the named analyst's argument, not as established fact.* See [[tech.ai]], [[gov.regional]], [[gov.policy]].
  Source: [[2026-07-15-sovereignty-through-collaboration-geneva-digital-week]].
- **2026-07-10** — **Submarine-cable resilience: the ITU–ICPC advisory body reports, with Nigeria in the
  chair.** The **[[international-advisory-body-on-submarine-cable-resilience]]** approved its final report
  at WSIS Forum 2026, closing a two-year programme co-chaired by Nigeria's **[[bosun-tijani]]**. Cables
  carry **>99% of global data traffic** (ITU, 2026); the report names dependence on few systems as most
  acute for **SIDS, LDCs and underserved regions**, and sets seven priorities across deployment/repair,
  risk monitoring and geographic diversity. Global standard-setting with direct African stakes — and,
  unusually, African chairmanship. See [[infra.connect]], [[gov.standards]], [[NGA]], [[XWA]]. Source:
  [[2026-07-10-itu-icpc-advisory-body-cable-resilience-report]].
- **2026-06-05** — **Microsoft Build 2026 — a vertically-owned AI platform (read for Africa).**
  Anjola Adebowale's walkthrough of Microsoft Build 2026 (Microsoft Discovery; the
  in-house **MAI** model family incl. **MAI-Thinking-1** at 35B active params; Frontier
  Tuning; the Scout agent; Project Solara hardware; the **Majorana 2** quantum chip
  pulling a commercial-quantum target to 2029) reads it as **[[microsoft]]** assembling
  a full-stack, vertically-owned AI platform — with Africa's risk being engagement on
  terms (data, workflows, learning loops) set by others. Global vendor news read for
  African implications. Source:
  [[2026-06-05 Microsoft Build 2026 What Caught My Attention and What It Means for Africa]].
  See [[tech.industry]], [[infra.store]], [[geopol.usa]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2025-12-19** — **India's monsoon AI — the lesson isn't the AI (Rose Mutiso).** India
  delivered its first **4-week-ahead monsoon forecasts** in 150 years using
  **[[google]] NeuralGCM + ECMWF's AIFS**, reaching **~38m farmers** (a quarter changed
  plans) — but Mutiso argues it worked because of **decades of meteorological data,
  institutional networks and trusted delivery infrastructure** (mKisan, 2013), not the
  model. A caution against supply-side "just add AI" leapfrogging in development.
  Source: [[2025-12-19 Why one of the year's biggest AI-for-development stories isn't actually about AI]].
  See [[data.satellite]], [[digital.rural]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2026-05-18** — **EU AI Act's extraterritorial reach (Zuria Nagadya).** An
  Africa-in-the-Room explainer argues the EU AI Act's "where the output lands" test
  already binds African businesses (prohibited-practice bans since 2025-02-02; high-risk
  enforcement 2026-08-02), and that Africa should extend, not copy, the risk-based model.
  A global-reach regulatory item relevant to the African data landscape. Source:
  [[2026-05-18 Europe Wrote The World's Most Advanced AI Law. The EU AI Act]]. See
  [[geopol.eu]], [[gov.legislate]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2026-07-11** — **Trump's American AI Exports Program underwhelms at launch.** Commerce's
  **[[american-ai-exports-program]]** drew only **78 first-round applications** (vs "hundreds"
  expected; ITA disputes the shortfall). **[[amazon-web-services]]** applied and **[[nvidia]]**
  signalled interest, but **[[openai]]**/AMD were non-committal; an abrupt, since-reversed order
  restricting **[[anthropic]]**'s foreign model access unnerved industry. Phase 2 (deal bidding)
  not yet launched — framed against Chinese open models ([[deepseek]], Alibaba) gaining in
  developing countries. Source:
  [[2026-07-11 'Not where they hoped it'd be' Launch of Trump AI promotion program underwhelms]].
  See [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]], [[tech.ai]], [[tech.industry]].
- **2026-07-02** — **Can AI open up aid data? PWYF tests an LLM agent on IATI.**
  **[[publish-what-you-fund]]** ran a Claude Code agent against the raw
  **[[international-aid-transparency-initiative]]** (IATI) dataset — strong on retrieval (ODA
  totals within 0.3% of established tools) but unreliable on interpretation (hallucinated
  causes, missed double-counting and 2025 UK/FCDO aid cuts). Ethiopia ([[ETH]]) food-aid
  worked example. Per the reference-study rule, figures are captured on the entities, not
  pushed onto concept pages. Source: [[2026-07-02 Can AI solve open aid data's access problem]].
  See [[data.open]], [[tech.ai]], [[data.statistics]].
- **2026** — **[[dpga]]: *Building Open Digital States*.** A Digital Public Goods Alliance report
  builds a four-dimension (economic/social/governance/political) value framework for DPGs-as-DPI,
  with case studies from the Philippines (MOSIP/Mojaloop; +8.3m bank accounts), Kyrgyzstan (X-Road
  Tunduk saved 21m citizen-hours + $19m in 2024) and **Rwanda** (DHIS2 lifted data completeness
  88%→95%, maternal mortality down >50%). It argues the value of open DPI is as much **political
  (sovereignty, strategic autonomy)** as economic. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 Digital Public Goods Alliance]]. See [[dpi.exchange]], [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.mis]], [[gov.policy]].
- **2026-02-02** — **[[dpi-map]] / first State of DPI Report** (UCL IIPP). A global baseline of
  **64 digital IDs, 97 digital-payment systems and 103 data-exchange systems**; of 288 launch
  participants only **6** were from Africa, and the community's top-ranked concern was **DPI
  excluding vulnerable populations**. Source:
  [[2026-02-02 Offering a Window into the DPI Community Challenges]]. See [[dpi.id]], [[dpi.pay]],
  [[dpi.exchange]], [[capacity.research]], [[gov.protect]].
- **2026-01** — **US critical-minerals diplomacy → Pax Silica.** An IISS analysis maps
  Trump's three-track state-capitalist minerals strategy (America-First equity deals;
  public-finance-backed bilateral pacts via **[[us-dfc]]**/EXIM; the Dec 2025 **[[pax-silica]]**
  coalition) to secure AI-foundational silicon and minerals against China. Africa relevance is
  narrow — the **DR Congo** ([[COD]]) bilateral track is linked to security guarantees. Source:
  [[2026-01-01 US critical-minerals diplomacy from America-First deals to Pax Silica]]. See
  [[geopol.usa]], [[geopol.china]], [[tech.ai]].
- **2025-12-15** — David Eaves ([[david-eaves]]), *The Path to a Sovereign Tech Stack
  is Via a Commodified Tech Stack* (Tech Policy Press): argues digital sovereignty is
  better pursued through **interoperability and commodified standards** (procurement +
  market-shaping, like railway gauges) than through expensive proprietary "sovereign
  stacks," which are unaffordable for most countries and can become "gilded cages."
  Source: [[2025-12-15 The Path to a Sovereign Tech Stack is Via a Commodified Tech Stack  TechPolicy.Press]].
- **2025** *(reference)* — **Submarine-cable ownership & protection baselines.** [[telegeography]]'s
  content-provider cable list has grown from 20 (2017) to 60+ systems: Google owns/part-owns ~35
  (incl. **Equiano** and **Umoja** sole), Meta part-owns 2Africa — content providers overtook
  carrier backbones as the largest users of international bandwidth in 2017. The
  **[[international-cable-protection-committee]]** (ICPC; founded 1958, 235+ members from 70+
  countries) sets install/repair best practice and co-launched the ITU/ICPC advisory body on cable
  resilience. Sources:
  [[2024-06-27 A (Refreshed) List of Content Providers' Submarine Cable Holdings]],
  [[2025-09-01 About the ICPC]]. See [[infra.connect]], [[infra.cybersec]], [[gov.standards]].
